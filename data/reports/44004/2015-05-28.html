1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ōtūpango ki te taha o Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Te Matai. Ko te nuinga o ngā tamariki ka puta i te kōhanga reo, ka whakawhiti atu ki te kura. He kaha te whānau ki te tautoko i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. He hiahia tō te whānau o tēnei hapori kia ako, kia whakawhanake hoki ā rātou tamariki mā te mātauranga e rumakina ana ki tō rātou reo, ki ngā tikanga, ki ngā mōhiotanga hoki o te ao Māori. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā tikanga Māori, tae atu ki te whakatau i ngā manuhiri, me te whai wāhi ki ngā hui i te marae. He mātātoa te whai wāhi a ngā kaumātua me te whānau ki ngā whakahaeretanga i te kōhanga reo i ia rā.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te kite i te pakari o ngā tamariki hei ākonga, i te tau hoki o tō rātou noho ki te kōhanga reo?

I te nuinga o te wā, he tau, he harikoa ngā tamariki i te kōhanga reo. I ētahi wā ka whakaatuhia e ngā tamariki tō rātou pakari i roto i ā rātou akoranga, me tā rātou kōkiri i ā rātou akoranga.

Mana Tangata

Kei te tino waia ngā tamariki ki ngā hui ōkawa Māori me ngā tikanga. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te whakatau manuhiri ki te kōhanga reo. He pai ki ngā tamariki te ārahi i ngā karakia puta noa i te rā. Ka āta whakarite te whānau me ngā kaimahi kia whiwhi ngā tamariki i ngā wheako akoranga e whai pūtake ana, e whai pānga ana, mā ngā horopaki e noho tūturu ana ki te ao Māori. Ka kitea te pakari haere o te māia, te āheinga hoki o ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā taiao o te ao Māori.

Ka kitea te tū takitahi a ngā tamariki i te wāhi tākaro o waho. Ka whakatau rātou i ā rātou ake whiringa i roto i ngā tākaro, ā, ka whai hoki i ngā āhuatanga e ngākau nuitia ana e rātou. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te whānuitanga pai o ngā tūmomo rauemi, taonga tākaro hoki mā ngā tamariki. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te tino hononga ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i te aroha me te manaaki i roto i ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga ki ngā pākeke. Ka āta kitea te hononga o te tuakana me te teina, ā, ka tautokona ngā tēina e ngā tuākana. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te tūhura me te ako ki te taha o ētahi atu.

Te Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kua taitutia te akoranga me te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. He maha ngā wā ka kōrero ngā mema o te whānau i te reo Pākehā ki a rātou anō, ki ngā tamariki hoki, tae atu ki ā rātou taunekeneke me ngā wā ako. Kāhore ngā kaimahi me te whānau e whakapūmau ana, e whakatairanga ana hoki i te reo Māori i te kōhanga reo. Kāhore te māia, te tū takitahi hoki o ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori e āta tautokona ana.

Kāhore ngā tamariki e whai wāhi atu ana ki ngā taumahi ako e whai pūtake ana. He kaha rawa te hanganga, te kōkiritanga hoki a ngā pākeke i te hōtaka akoranga. He whāiti noa te wā mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura takitahi i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga me ā rātou taumahi. He roa ngā wā ka noho ngā tamariki ki te whāriki. Ka whakariuka haere, ka aro kore haere. Kāhore ngā tamariki e ngākau nui ana ki ngā taumahi e whakaratohia ana. E whakakōroiroi ana tēnei i te āta whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki hei ākonga pakari, hei ākonga ka kōkiri i āna ake akoranga.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo matea, ngā tūmomo ngākau nuitanga hoki o ngā tamariki e āta whakaarohia ana i te wā e whakamaheretia ana te hōtaka. He whānui noa te tirohanga ki ngā kaupapa. Kāhore he mōhiohio e tohu ana i ngā tūmomo taumahi ako, i te whakarerekē haere rānei o aua taumahi mō ngā tūmomo reanga, ngā tūmomo āheinga, me ngā tūmomo ngākau nuitanga. Kāhore ngā mahere e whai whakaaro ana ki ngā huarahi ka poipoi i te māia, i te tū takitahi hoki o ngā tamariki hei ākonga. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e tino mōhio ana ki ngā mahi ka hāpai mārika i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

He maha ngā wā kāhore ngā tamariki e āta aro atu ana ki te hōtaka akoranga. Kāhore e whakatutukihia ana te arotake me te aromātai i te hōtaka. Kāhore aua whakaritenga i noho hei kaupapa matua ki ngā kaimahi. He mea nui kia aroturuki, kia arotake hoki ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā hōtaka akoranga me ngā whakaritenga, hei tāutu i ngā āhuatanga ka whai hua kia aro atu ai rātou ki ngā ākonga. Kāhore ngā matea whanaketanga o ngā tamariki e āta whakaarohia ana.

Kāhore te ahu whakamua, te ako hoki a ngā tamariki e tuhia ana, e pūrongotia ana hoki ki ngā mātua. He iti noa ngā mōhiohio aromatawai e pā ana ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā rātou wheako, me te angitu o tō rātou whakawhanaketanga hei ākonga pakari, hei ākonga tū takitahi.

Kei te pā tōraro hoki ki ngā tamariki, te kore whai huatanga o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. He ngoikore te whakahaeretanga a te whānau. Kei te tūtohinga ngā tukanga whakahaere, ā, kāhore e whakapūmautia ana te whai atu i tēnei, tae atu ki te whakatū kaimahi ki te kōhanga reo, ngā arohaehae, me te arowhai a ngā pirihimana i ngā kaimahi. He maha ngā herenga ā-ture kāhore e whakatutukihia ana i roto te hauora me te haumaru. Ko ētahi o aua āhuatanga, ko te mātakitaki i ngā tamariki, ko te tiaki i ngā tamariki i a rātou e moe ana, ko te noho wātea o ngā paitini mō ngā tamariki, ko te āheinga o ngā tamariki ki te puta i ngā papa o te kōhanga reo, me ngā āhuatanga mōrearea e noho wātea ana hoki ki ngā tamariki. Kāhore te arotake whaiaro a te whānau i āta whakawhanakehia puta noa i te kōhanga reo, tae atu ki te tuhi haere me te pūrongo i ngā whakaritenga. Kāhore e taea te whakatau te ako me te atawhai o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho);
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga kei raro iho nei, kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture. Me tahuri te whānau me te kaipupuri raihana ki te:

  1. whakarite i ngā wāhi katoa o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo, tae atu ki ngā whare me ngā rauhanga, kia arowhaingia mō ngā āhuatanga mōrearea i ia te wā, ā, kia whakahaerehia aua wāhi kia haumaru ai ngā tinana o ngā tamariki [R 45 me R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS12 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008; Te Korowai, Whāinga 10, 1995]
  2. whakarite i te waiora ā-tinana o ngā tamariki, tae atu ki ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te whāngai rongoā, me te pupuri i ngā rehita e pā ana ki ngā whakatō kano ārai mate, ā, kia aroturukitia, kia arotakengia hoki ēnei āhuatanga kia noho haumaru ai ngā tamariki [R 46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS 28-29 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  3. āta whakarite i te haumaru ā-tinana o ngā tamariki i ngā haerenga me ngā haereere, tae atu ki ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te tātari i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea, te whakamōhio i te whānau, me te whakariterite hoki mō ngā tūmomo mōrearea ka pupū ake pea [R 45 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; PF 29-38 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  4. whakaatu, te whakatinana hoki i ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te tiaki i ngā tamariki e moe ana, me te tuhi haere hoki i ngā wā moe a ngā tamariki [R 45 me R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; PF 29-38 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  5. whakamahi i Te Whāriki o Te Kōhanga Reo, hei āta whakamahere, whakatinana, aromātai anō hoki i ngā hōtaka akoranga e aro atu ana, e whai pānga nui ana ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo matea ako me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki, hei whakatairanga i tā rātou ako me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga mā te whakarato i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga hōhonu e whakaongaonga ana, e whai pūtake ana, ā, e whai kiko ana hoki [R 43 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; C1-13 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  6. whakarite i te haere tonutanga o tētahi tukanga e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro, hei hāpai i te kōhanga reo ki te whakapūmau me te whakapai ake hoki i te kounga o te mātauranga me te atawhai [R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA 6 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  7. āta whakarite i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā whānau katoa o ngā tamariki e haere ana ki te kōhanga reo, me ērā hoki e whakarato ana i te mātauranga me te atawhai ki te whai wāhi atu ki te whakawhanaketanga me te arotake o te kōhanga reo [R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA 4 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā:

kia toro wawe atu te whānau ki te tautoko me te ārahitanga o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te whakatutuki me te whakapai ake i ngā wāhanga katoa i tāutuhia ai hei whakawhanake ake ki tēnei pūrongo a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ōtūpango ki Te Puke. Kāhore i te rawaka te tautoko i ngā tamariki i roto i tā rātou ako, tō rātou atawhai hoki ki te kōhanga reo. Kāhore tō rātou noho haumaru e whakatūturuhia ana. Kei te whakatiki te pūmanawa o ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i te māia me te āheinga i roto i te reo Māori. Ka whai ngā tamariki i tētahi hōtaka e āta whakahaerehia ana, e āta kōkirihia ana hoki e ngā pākeke, ā, kāhore e whai pānga ana ki ō rātou matea, ki ō rātou ngākau nuitanga rānei.

5 Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ōtūpangoi roto i te kotahi tau.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

28 Haratua, 2015

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ōtūpango is next to Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Te Mātai. Most children who graduate from the kōhanga reo attend the kura. Whānau support is strong for the kōhanga reo kaupapa. The whānau in this community want their children to learn and develop through education that is immersed in their language, customs and knowledge of te ao Māori. Children participate in tikanga Māori practices including welcoming visitors and participating in marae events. Kaumātua and whānau are actively involved in the daily operations of the kōhanga reo.

2 The Review Findings

To what extent do children show confidence as learners, and that they are comfortable at kōhanga reo?

Children are generally comfortable and happy at kōhanga reo. At times children show some confidence and independence in their learning.

Mana Tangata

Children are very familiar with formal Māori protocols. They actively participate in welcoming manuhiri to the kōhanga reo. Children enjoy leading karakia during the day. The whānau and kaimahi make sure children regularly receive meaningful and relevant learning experiences, in traditional contexts. Children show increasing confidence and ability to participate in te ao Māori environments.

Children show independence in the outdoor play area. They make choices about their play and follow the things that interest them. Kaimahi and whānau provide a suitable range of resources and play equipment for children. Children are developing a strong sense of belonging to their kōhanga reo environment.

Children experience loving and caring interactions and relationships with adults. Tuakana, taina relationships are evident where younger children are supported by older children. Children are confident to explore and learn alongside others.

Key Next Steps

Children’s te Māori reo learning and development is hindered. Whānau members often speak English to each other and to children, including during interactions and learning times. Kaimahi and whānau do not reinforce and promote te reo Māori at the kōhanga reo. Children’s confidence and independence to use te reo Māori is not adequately supported.

Children are not engaged in meaningful learning activities. The learning programme is too structured and adult directed. There is little time provided for children to explore their interests and activities independently. Children are kept on the mat for extended periods of time. They become restless and disinterested. Children do not enjoy the activities that are provided. Children’s successful development as confident and independent learners is hampered.

Planning and Evaluation

Key Next Steps

The diverse needs and interests of children are not considered when planning the programme. There is only a broad overview of themes. There is no information to show the types of learning activities or how these are adjusted for the various ages, abilities and interests. Planning does not consider ways to foster children’s confidence and independence as learners. Kaimahi are uncertain about what to do to successfully support children’s learning.

Children are often disengaged and disinterested in the learning programmes. Programme review and evaluation does not occur. This practice has not been a priority for kaimahi. It is critical that kaimahi and whānau monitor and review learning programmes, and practices to identify how to better respond to learners. Children’s developmental needs are not recognised.

Children’s progress and learning is not documented and reported to parents. There is limited assessment information about children’s learning, their experiences and their successful development as confident and independent learners.

Children are further disadvantaged due to ineffective whānau management of the kōhanga reo. Whānau management of personnel is poor. The charter that contains management procedures are not adhered to, including the appointment of personnel in the kōhanga reo, appraisals and police vetting of employees. A number of health and safety compliance requirements are not upheld. These include supervision, monitoring sleeping children, child access to poisonous chemicals, child access off kōhanga reo grounds, hazards accessible to children. Whānau self review, including recording and reporting practices are not well developed across the kōhanga reo. Children’s learning and care at kōhanga reo cannot be assured.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements. ERO has identified the following areas of non compliance. The whānau, with their licensee must:

  1. ensure that the children’s kōhanga reo physical environment, including the premises, and facilities are regularly checked for hazards, and managed for children’s physical safety[R 45 and R 46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS 12 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008; Te Korowai, Goal 10, 1995]
  2. ensure that children’s physical wellbeing, including the practices of administering medicine and holding immunisation registers is monitored and reviewed for the safety of children [R 46 Education (Early Childhood Services)Regulations 2008; HS 28-29 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]
  3. ensure children’s physical safety on excursions and outings, including the practices of analysing risks, notifying whānau and planning for any unforeseen hazards [R 45 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; PF 29-38 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]
  4. ensure that a procedure for monitoring children’s sleep is displayed and implemented and a record of children’s sleep times is kept [R45 and R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; PF 29-38 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]
  5. use Te Kōhanga Reo Te Whāriki to effectively plan, implement, and evaluate programmes of learning that is responsive and inclusive to the diverse learning needs and interests of children, to enhance their learning and development through the provision of a wide range of stimulating, rich, relevant and meaningful learning experiences [R 43 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; C 1-13 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]
  6. ensure there is an ongoing process of self review in place to help the kōhanga reo maintain and improve the quality of its education and care [R 47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA 6 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]
  7. ensure that all whānau members of children attending the kōhanga reo, providing education and care are provided with opportunities to contribute to the development and review of the kōhanga reo [R 47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA 4 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • the whānau seek urgent support and guidance from their TKRNT to address and improve all areas for development identified in this ERO report

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ōtūpango is in Te Puke. Children do not receive adequate support for their learning and care while at kōhanga reo. Their safety cannot be assured. Children’s potential to develop in confidence and ability with te reo Māori is compromised. Children follow a highly controlled, adult determined programme that does not consider their needs or interests.

5 When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ōtūpango will be within one year.

Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori
