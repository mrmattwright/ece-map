Ko Te Reo Irirangi te kōhanga reo

E tū pā tata ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Reo Irirangi ki te takutai o Whanganui, ki Castlecliff. I tapaina te ingoa a Irirangi ki te kōhanga reo e Pinnacle Taipua, hei whakanui i te whakahirahiratanga o te whakapuaki i te reo Māori.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. He hiahia nō te whānau ki te titiro ki te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga ki te whakatutuki i ngā whāinga me ngā putanga e wawatatia ana, puta noa i Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira katoa.

He pakari ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka āta whakanui, ka āta poipoi hoki ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori ki ngā tamariki me te whānau. Ka whai hua ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā tamariki, ki waenga i ngā pākeke, ā, ki waenganui hoki i a rātou ngā pākeke me ngā tamariki. Ka whakatauira ngā pākeke i te pai o te whakawhanake i te whakaute whaiaro me te whakaaro nui ki ētahi atu. Nā aua hononga ka ako ngā tamariki i a rātou anō, me te whakaute mō ētahi atu. Ka whakanui ngā kaimahi i te aronga toi whenuatanga mā ngā huarahi e whai hua ana, e whai pūtake ana hoki ki te whānau me ngā tamariki.

Ka ako te whānau me ngā tamariki i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo karakia, hīmene hoki ka akiaki i te hononga me te ngākau nui ki waenga i a rātou. E mōhio haere ana ngā tamariki ki ā rātou taonga tuku iho.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i te ao mā te tūhura me te whakahura. Nā ngā haerenga me ngā wheako ka mārama, ka maioha hoki ngā tamariki i te ao whānui. E tupu ana te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te noho whakaute, te noho mārama hoki ki ā rātou whai wāhitanga ki te tiaki i te whenua.

He mātātoa te āta whai wāhi atu te whānau i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo me te hōtaka akoranga. Ka arotakengia ngā kaupapa here i ngā hui a te purapura, ā, ka whakakoia, ka whakamanatia hoki i ngā hui ā-whānau. Kua oti i te whānau te arotake o tā rātou mahere rautaki. He pai te tūnga pūtea a te kōhanga reo. Arotakengia ai e te whānau ngā whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga i ia te wā. I tēnei wā, kāhore anō kia oti ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi. Me whakarite te whānau kia arowhaingia ngā kaimahi katoa e ngā pirihimana.

Kāhore anō te whānau kia whakaoti tā rātou mahere rautaki, tā rātou mahere ā-tau hoki. Me arotake ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai. Kāhore te kaupapa here o te kōhanga reo mō te whakamoe, e hāngai ana ki ngā tukanga me ngā whakaritenga o ngā herenga ā-ture.

I whakaae te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau, kia tahuri te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo hei whakawhanake ake.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Reo Irirangi kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Reo Irirangi.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira hei kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. He hiahia nō te whānau ki te titiro ki te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga ki te whakatutuki i ngā whāinga me ngā putanga e wawatatia ana, puta noa i Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira katoa.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Mana Reo.E mōhio ana te whānau, ka hāngai te Mana Reo ki te whakanui i te reo Māori ki ngā tamariki me te whānau. He pakari ngā kaiako me ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki ka whakapiki ake i tō rātou mātau ki te reo Māori. Kua oti i ngā kaimahi katoa te tohu mātauranga o Te Ara Tuatahi. Tū ai ngā wā patapatai hei hāpai i tā rātou ako tonu i te reo Māori. Ka whakatauira ngā kaiako i ngā whakaritenga papai, mā te:

  • whakamahi i te reo Māori ki te taha o ngā tamariki
  • whakarite i ngā taumahi hei whiringa mā ngā tamariki, kia whai wāhi ai rātou ki ngā kaupapa ka akiaki, ka poipoi hoki i te whakamahinga o te reo Māori
  • taunekeneke i ia te wā mā te reo Māori ki ia tamaiti, ki ngā rōpū iti hoki
  • akiaki i te whānau ki te whai wāhi ki ngā karakia me ngā waiata ki te taha o ngā tamariki, hei rautaki anō hoki ki te whakatairanga i tā rātou whakamahinga o te reo Māori.

Ka āta whakanui, ka āta poipoi hoki ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori ki ngā tamariki me te whānau.

Mana Tangata.Ka arotahi ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhanake i te whakaute whaiaro o ngā tamariki, me tō rātou whakaaro nui anō hoki ki ētahi atu. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i te pai o ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā tamariki, ki waenga i ngā pākeke, ki waenga hoki i ngā tamariki me ngā pākeke, mā te:

  • whakarite i ngā hononga mauritau, whakakoia hoki ki te taha o ngā tamariki
  • tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te kōkiri i ētahi wāhanga o te hōtaka akoranga
  • whakanui i te tūturutanga o te whakahoahoa ki waenga i ngā tamariki
  • whakaako i ngā whakapapa o ngā tamariki ki a rātou
  • whakaaro nui hoki ki ngā hapū me ngā iwi o ia tamaiti.

Nā ēnei hononga, ka ako ngā tamariki i a rātou anō, me te noho whakaute ki ētahi atu.

Mana Atua.Ki tā te whānau titiro, ko Mana Atua te whakawhanaketanga o te mōhio me te maioha o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou ahurea me ā rātou taonga tuku iho. He pakari ngā tamariki i roto i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki. Ka kitea tēnei i te āhua o:

  • tā rātou harikoa i a rātou e tākaro ngātahi ana
  • tā rātou whai wāhitanga ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo karakia, hīmene hoki.

Nā ēnei wheako, e ako ana ngā tamariki i ā rātou taonga tuku iho me ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu.

Mana Aotūroa.He whānui ngā tūmomo whai wāhitanga tika a ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te ao e noho nei rātou, me te ao whānui hoki. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki ngā haerenga me ngā wheako e hāpai ana i a rātou ki te:

  • tūhonohono ki ētahi atu
  • whakatutuki i ngā kawa me ngā tikanga o ētahi momo rōpū
  • ako i te hītori o ngā wāhi whakahirahira o tō rātou iwi
  • mātakitaki i ngā tikanga whakapono, whanonga hoki o ētahi atu.

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te māramatanga me te maioha ki te ao whānui.

Mana Whenua.Ka whai hua ngā kaimahi ki te hāpai i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i te tino aronga toi whenuatanga me te whakaute mō te ao kikokiko nei. Ko tā ngā kaimahi mahi:

  • he whakakoia i ngā hononga ā-whānau o ngā tamariki mā ō rātou pepeha
  • he akiaki i te whānau ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou mōhiotanga e pā ana ki ngā hononga tuku iho, me ngā uaratanga, ngā whakapono hoki
  • he ako i te whakahirahiratanga o te whenua ki ngā tamariki.

E whakawhanake ana, e mārama ana hoki ngā tamariki ki te whai pānga o ō rātou whakapapa ki tō rātou whakaute, tō rātou manaaki hoki i te whenua.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai i ngā kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki, me te whānau. Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He tika ngā tukanga ā te whānau hei whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. He mātātoa tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo me te hōtaka akoranga.

Arotakengia ai ngā kaupapa here i ngā hui purapura, ā, ka whakapuaki ngā mema i ō rātou mōhiotanga, ō rātou mātanga hoki hei whakapai ake i ngā kaupapa here me ngā whakaritenga. Ka whakatakotohia te tuhinga whakamutunga o ngā kaupapa here ki ngā hui ā-whānau. Ka whakakoia, kia whakamana hoki te whānau i ia kaupapa here, ā, ka tuhia tētahi whakataunga ki ngā meneti o ngā hui ā-whānau. Me whakahāngai ake ētahi o ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga o te hauora me te haumaru ki ngā whakaritenga.

Kua whakaoti i te whānau te arotake o tā rātou mahere rautaki, engari kāhore anō kia whakaoti ngā whakarerekētanga. Ko ngā whāinga rautaki me ngā mahi i tīpakohia ai, ka hāpai i te whānau ki te whakawhanake me te whakarite i tā rātou ahunga whakamua. Ka arotake te whānau i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo take, kaupapa hoki. Ka hāngai nei ki te kawe tamariki, ngā māuiui mau noa, te ārai whatitata i ngā haerenga, ngā akoranga o te reo Māori, ngā haora raihana o te kōhanga reo, ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga, te ako ngaio me te whakawhanaketanga ngaio o ngā kaimahi me te whānau, me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te hōneatanga.

He pakari te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo. He mārama ngā pūrongo e pā ana ki ngā pūtea. Kei te mārama te whānau ki te whai pānga o te moni hua me ngā whakapaunga ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka whiwhi te whānau i ngā pūrongo ka whakapuakihia ki ngā hui o ia marama, ā, ka whakautu kōrero hoki mō ēnei.

Ka whakarato ngā pūrongo a ngā kaiako i ngā mōhiohio o te wā mō te hōtaka akoranga.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te whakahaeretanga a ngā kaimahi.Kāhore anō kia oti ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi. Kāhore i ngā kaimahi katoa ā rātou kape o ā rātou tuhinga tūranga mahi, ā rātou kirimana mahi rānei. Kua tīmata te whānau i te tukanga arohaehae o ia tau. Kua oti i te nuinga o ngā kaimahi te aromatawai whaiaro. Engari, tokorua anake ngā kaimahi i oti tā rātou arohaehae i ā rātou mahi. Me whakarite te whānau kia arowhaingia ngā kaimahi katoa e ngā pirihimana.

Mahi Arotake.Me arotake, me whakapai hoki i ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai. I tēnei wā, kāhore ngā pakiwaitara ako e hono ana ki te paearu aromatawai i whakatakotohia ai ki te angamahi e pā an ki ngā pakiwaitara ako. Kāhore anō ngā kaimahi kia whakamahi i ngā mōhiohio o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i whakaemihia ai, hei whakamōhio i ngā whakapaitanga ki te hōtaka.

Me arotake te kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te mātakitaki o ngā tamariki i a rātou e moe ana. Kāhore ngā mahi o tēnei wā e hāngai ana ki te kaupapa here me ngā tukanga.

Ko Te Reo Irirangi te Kōhanga Reo

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Reo Irirangi is located close to the Whanganui coast at Castlecliff. The name Irirangi was given to the kōhanga reo by Pinnacle Taipua to acknowledge the significance of sharing te reo Māori.

Whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus area for the review. The whānau were interested in how well the learning programme is achieving the objectives and desired outcomes across all of the mana strands.

Kaimahi are confident speakers of te reo Māori.Kaimahi promote and nurture te reo Māori well to children and whānau. There arepositive relationships between children, between adults, and between adults and children. Adults model the importance of developing self respect and valuing others. Through these relationships children learn about themselves and respect for others.Kaimahi promote a sense of belonging in a positive and effective way for whānau and children.

Whānau and children learn a range of karakia and hīmene that encourage bonding and commitment to each other. Children are becoming familiar with their ancestral heritage.

Children learn about the world through exploration and discovery. Trips and experiences provide children with an understanding and appreciation of the wider world. Children have a growing understanding of the need to respect and understand their roles to look after the land.

The whānau are actively involved and participate well in the operations of the kōhanga reo and the learning programme. Policies are reviewed at Purapura hui and affirmed and approved at whānau hui. Whānau have completed the review of their strategic plan. The kōhanga reo is in a strong financial position. Whānau regularly review management operations and practices. At present, personnel documentation is incomplete. Whānau must ensure that all kaimahi have been police vetted.

The whānau are still to complete their strategic and annual plan. The planning, assessment and evaluation processes in place need to be reviewed.The kōhanga reo sleep policy does not align with procedures and practices as required in the regulations.

ERO and the whānau agreed that the kōhanga reo whānau address areas identified for further development in this report.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

4 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

The whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus area for the review. Whānau were interested in how well their learning programme is achieving the objectives and desired outcomes of all of the mana strands.

Areas of good performance

Mana Reo.The whānau recognise that mana reo is about promoting te reo Māori to children and whānau. Kaiako and kaimahi are confident speakers of te reo Maori. They use a range of strategies to increase their fluency in te reo Māori. All kaimahi have completed the Te Ara Tuatahi qualification. Quiz sessions are held to support their ongoing learning of te reo Māori. Kaiako model good practice by:

  • using te reo Māori when they are with children
  • preparing activities for children to choose and participate that encourage and foster their use of te reo Māori
  • interacting consistently with individuals and small groups using te reo Māori
  • encouraging whānau to participate in karakia and waiata alongside the children as a strategy to promote their use of te reo Māori.

Kaimahi promote and nuture te reo Māori well to children and whānau.

Mana Tangata.Kaimahi focus on developing children’s sense of self respect and valuing of others. Kaimahi model positive relationships between children, between adults, and between adults and children by:

  • establishing positive and affirming relationships with children
  • supporting children to lead aspects of the learning programme
  • promoting genuine friendships among children
  • teaching children about their whakapapa
  • valuing the hapū and iwi of each child.

Through these relationships children learn about themselves and respect for others.

Mana Atua.The whānau view Mana Atua as developing children’s awareness and appreciation of their culture and ancestral heritage. Children are confident in a range of settings. This is evident in the way that they:

  • play happily together
  • participate in a range of karakia and hīmene.

Through these experiences children are learning about their ancestral heritage and connections to others.

Mana Aotūroa.Children have a range of appropriate opportunities to explore their immediate world and wider world around them. They participate in outings and experiences that support them to:

  • build new relationships
  • practice the kawa and tikanga of different groups
  • learn about the history of places that are significant to their iwi
  • observe the beliefs and behaviours of others.

Children are developing an understanding and appreciation of the wider world.

Mana Whenua.Kaimahi effectively support children to develop a strong sense of belonging and respect for the physical environment. They:

  • affirm for children their whānau links through pepeha
  • encourage whānau to share what they know about their ancestral connections, values and beliefs
  • teach children about the importance of the land.

Children are developing and understanding of how their whakapapa requires them to respect and care for the land.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau have appropriate processes in place to manage the kōhanga reo. They are actively involved and participate well in the operations of the kōhanga reo and the learning programme.

Policies are reviewed at purapura hui where members share their knowledge and experience to improve policy and practice. The final copies of the policies are presented at whānau hui. Whānau affirm and approve each policy and a resolution is recorded in whānau hui minutes. Some health and safety policies and procedures need to be more closely aligned to practice.

Whānau have completed the review of their strategic plan but have not completed amendments. The selected strategic objectives and actions should enable the whānau to develop and establish their future direction.Whānau review a range of matters and issues. These relate to transport, infectious disease, risk management for outings, te reo Māori classes, kōhanga reo licence hours, policies and procedures, professional learning and development for kaimahi and whānau, and evacuation procedures.

The kōhanga reo is in a strong financial position. Financial reporting is transparent. Whānau understand how income and expenditure impact on children’s learning and kōhanga reo operations.Whānau receives and responds to reports shared at monthly hui.

Kaiako reports provide up to date information about the learning programme.

Areas for improvement

Personnel management.Personnel documentation is incomplete.Not all kaimahi have a copy of their job description or employment contracts. Whānau have started the annual process for appraisals. Most staff have completed self assessment. However, only two kaimahi have completed their performance appraisal. Whānau must ensure that all kaimahi have been police vetted.

Review practices.The planning, assessment and evaluation processes need to be reviewed and improved. Currently learning stories do not link to the assessment criteria set in the learning stories framework. Kaimahi are yet to use information gathered about children’s learning to inform programme improvement.

The monitoring of sleeping children policy and practice requires review. Current practice is not consistent with the policy and procedures.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Reo Irirangi Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff ofcompleted an EROKōhanga Whānau Assurance StatementandSelf-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

During the course of the review ERO identified areas of non-compliance. In order to address these, the whānau must:

5.1 undertake police vetting for all staff

[s319D to s319FA, s319FE Education Act 1989]

5.2 ensure that monitoring of sleeping children policy aligns with procedures and practices.

[R46 (1) (a) Education (ECC) Regulations 2008]

6 Recommendations

ERO and the whānau agreed that:

6.1 the kōhanga reo whānau address areas identified for further development in this report:

  • complete all personnel documentation
  • complete strategic plan and annual plan
  • review planning, assessment and evaluation processes to ensure that these processes are used correctly.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
