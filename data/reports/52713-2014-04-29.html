1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngātiki ki te marae o Ngātiki, kei waho atu i te tāone o Hāwera. Nō te marae o Ngātiki ngā tamariki me te whānau, ā, nō rātou hoki te mana whenua whānui. Ka whai wāhi mātātoa tonu ngā mema kua roa nei e noho ana ki te kōhanga reo, tae atu ki ngā kuia me ngā kaumātua, ā, ka ārahi tonu, ka tautoko tonu i te whānau. He tino taonga tō rātou ngākau nui tonu mō ngā mema taiohi tonu o te whānau e whiriwhiri ana kia haere ā rātou tamariki ki tētahi kōhanga reo noho marae.

“Pono ki te kaupapa, puna ko te reo.” (Te tūtohinga a te whānau)

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Reo te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E ai ki a rātou, mā te reo me ngā tikanga Māori ka whakawhanake ngā tamariki i tō rātou māia, ā, ka ako i tō rātou tuakiri ahurea me ō rātou taonga tuku iho. I whai wāhi te whānau ki te waihanga o te pātai aromātai e ārahi ana i te arotake a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te Mana Reo, arā,

“He pēhea te whai huatanga o ā tātou tamariki ki te whakawhanake i tō rātou tuakiri ahurea, tō rātou mōhiotanga anō hoki ki ngā taonga tuku iho a ō rātou tūpuna?”

E rumakina ana ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki akoranga tūturu. Ka whakawhanake rātou i te mōhiotanga ki a rātou anō, me tō rātou hononga ki te mana whenua. Ka arotahi ngā wheako o ia rā ki te whakatairanga i ngā mōhiotanga e pā ana ki ō rātou hapū, tō rātou iwi hoki. Ka mātakitaki, ka whai wāhi atu hoki rātou ki ngā tūmomo taumahi e hāngai pū ana ki ō rātou hapū me tō rātou iwi.

Ko te kounga o ngā taunekeneke a ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki e whai hua ana ki te hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o te māia o ngā tamariki ki te tūhura me te whai wāhi atu ki te ao Māori. Ka whakapūmautia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi te whanaungatanga ki te katoa o ngā akoranga me ngā taunekeneke. Ka kite, ka wheako hoki ngā tamariki i ngā taunekeneke e whakapūmau ana i te atawhai, te aroha, te manaaki, me te whakaute.

Ahakoa ngā tūmomo wero i pā ai ki te whakarerekētanga o ngā tūnga kaimahi, ka tino whakapūmautia te haere tonutanga o ngā akoranga me te atawhai e ngā kaimahi, ngā kuia, me tō rātou whānau. Kei te ngākau nui ngātahi rātou ki te tupu tonu o te kōhanga reo, ā, ka tino kitea tēnei āhuatanga ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, te atawhai, me te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā wawata o te whānau mō ngā tamariki e pakari ana i roto i tō rātou reo, tō rātou ahurea, me ngā taonga tuku iho, e tino māramahia ana e te katoa, hei aronga matua o ngā akoranga ki te kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mokohuia o te Rautahi kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Reo

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Reo

He whakamārama

“He pēhea te whai huatanga o ā tātou tamariki ki te whakawhanake i tō rātou tuakiri ahurea, tō rātou mōhiotanga anō hoki ki ngā taonga tuku iho a ō rātou tūpuna?”

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka arongia, ka whakapuakihia hoki e ngā tamariki te reo e whakakoia ana i tō rātou tuakiri me ngā taonga tuku iho. Ko te ako i te reo Māori me ōna tikanga te kaupapa matua a te whānau mō ngā tamariki. Ka hāpaitia ngā tamariki e ngā kaikōrero papai o te reo Māori. Ka whakamahia ngā rautaki ako reo e whai hua ana, hei poipoi i te reo Māori mō ngā reanga katoa, ki ngā horopaki akoranga katoa. Whakanuia ai i ngā wā katoa ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, i ngā tamariki e tūhura ana, e whakawhanake ana i tō rātou māia, tō rātou mōhiotanga hoki ki te reo Māori.

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki hei ākonga māia, hei ākonga tākare hoki. Kei te kounga kairangi ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. Ka whakarerekē haere ngā kaimahi i ia te wā i ngā taumahi, ā, e hāpai ana tēnei i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i te māia ki te tūhura me te whakapuaki i ō rātou ake whakaaro. Kei te mōhio pai ngā kaimahi i ā rātou tamariki, ā, ka waihanga i ngā taumahi ka whakaū i te ngākau nui o ngā tamariki ki te ako. He tau, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki, ā, he pārekareka ki a rātou te tūhura i tō rātou ahurea, i ngā taonga hoki i tuku iho ai i ō rātou tūpuna.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga e pā ana ki tō rātou tuakiri me tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga. He aronga nui ki ngā wheako ka hāpai i ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi mātātoa ki ngā taiao o te ao Māori. Ka whakapūmau ngā kaimahi i ngā hononga ki ngā atua Māori, ngā taonga tuku iho, me ngā kawa, ngā tikanga hoki o te hapori, ki ngā tamariki. Ka whakapūmau ngā waiata me ngā karakia i te hītori me ngā tino kōrero tuku iho e pā ana ki ō rātou tūpuna, me te mana whenua. Ka whai wāhi tika ngā tamariki hei tangata whenua, ki te taha o tō rātou whānau.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā uaratanga o te tiaki, te manaaki tangata, me te aroha. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi katoa me te whānau i ngā taunekeneke e manaaki ana, e whakaute ana, ā, e whakaaro nui ana ki ētahi atu. He pai ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā tamariki. Ka kitea te atawhai me te whakaaro nui o ngā tuākana ki ngā tēina. E āhei ana rātou ki te mātakitaki i ngā pākeke, ngā kuia, me ngā kaumātua e taunekeneke ana ki waenga i a rātou anō ki te marae.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

Ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te whai huatanga o te whānau ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aromātai i tō rātou kōhanga reo, kia tutuki ai ā rātou whāinga, ō rātou wawata ngātahi me te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki. Ka whai whakaaro te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ki te pātai aromātai i waihangatia ai e te whānau mō tēnei arotake, i roto i te whakamahere me te aromātai, arā,

“He pēhea te āta tautoko i ā tātou tamariki mā te whai huatanga o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo, kia tutukihia ai ō tātou wawata?”

Ngāwāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He pai te ahunga whakamua a te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ō rātou wawata mō ā rātou tamariki. Tū ai ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, ā, he pai hoki te tae ā-tinana atu. E arotahi ana ngā mema ki te aroturuki i ngā kaupapa matua mō ā rātou tamariki. Ko tētahi o aua kaupapa ka hāngai ki te whakarato i ngā wheako akoranga ka whakawhanake i te tuakiri ahurea o ngā tamariki, me tō rātou mōhiotanga hoki ki ngā taonga tuku iho.

Ko ngā matea o ngā tamariki me ngā wawata o te whānau kei te pūtake o ngā mahi whakamahere, ā, ka whakamōhio hoki i te aromātai o te hōtaka. Kua āta whakawhanakehia ngā mahi whakamahere. Kei roto i ngā mahere, ko te whānuitanga o ngā rautaki whakaako, ako hoki ka whakatutuki i ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki katoa. Kua waihangatia ngā mahere hōtaka hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki i te māia me te mōhiotanga anō hoki ki ā rātou taonga tuku iho, te reo, me ngā tikanga.

Aromātaihia ai ngā hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki i ia te wā e ngā kaimahi. Ka noho matua ngā matea o ngā tamariki ki ngā kaimahi, i a rātou e whakaaro huritao ana, e aromātai ana hoki i ā rātou mahi. Ka aroturuki, ka tāutu, ka aro hoki rātou ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai hei whakapai ake.

Ka whiwhi te whānau i ngā mōhiohio papai e pā ana ki te ahunga whakamua me te katoa o te whakawhanaketanga o ā rātou tamariki. Ka pūrongo ngā kaimahi i te tū māia o ngā tamariki, me ō rātou mōhiotanga anō hoki ki ngā taonga tuku iho me te reo.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

He whāiti rawa ētahi wāhanga o ngā pūrongo. Ko ētahi o aua wāhanga, ko ngā akoranga o Te Whāriki o Te Kōhanga Reo mō ngā tamariki, te hauora me te haumaru, ngā kaupapa rautaki matua, me te ako a te whānau. Ko te aroturuki me te pūrongo i te ahunga whakamua o te hōtaka me ngā kaupapa matua o te kōhanga reo, ētahi whakaritenga ōkawa ka whakamōhio i te katoa o te whānau mō tā rātou aronga rautaki me ngā whāinga ā-tau.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakiia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o okiuh, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko ngā wāhanga e whai ake nei, i tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, ā, kāhore i te tutuki ngā herenga ā-ture:

4.1 whakarite i te arowhai a ngā pirihimana i ngā kaimahi katoa e mahi ana ki te taha o ngā tamariki, e pā tata atu ana rānei ki a rātou

[R44 Ture Mātauranga Kōhungahunga me ngā Pokapū Atawhai 2008]

4.2 whakarite i te whakatutukitanga o ngā akoako i ia te wā inā he ahi, he rū whenua rānei, me te tuhi haere hoki i aua akoako puta noa i te tau, ā, me ako hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā mahi whakangungu e pā ana ki aua akoako hōneatanga

[R46 (1) (2) Ture Mātauranga Kōhungahunga me ngā Pokapū Atawhai 2008, HS4-8]

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia:

  • whakapai ake te whānau ki te pūrongo ōkawa i ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo ki ngā hui ā-whānau o ia marama

  • whakamahi tonu te whānau i te pūrongo me te arotake whaiaro a te whānau, ki te aroturuki i te āhua o ngā mahi ki tō rātou kōhanga reo, me te pūrongo anō hoki i te tupu, te eke angitu hoki o ā rātou whāinga ki te tūtohinga, ngā whāinga rautaki i whakamaheretia ai, me ngā wawata hoki o te whānau mō ngā tamariki.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngātiki is based at Ngātiki Marae, on the outskirts of Hawera. Children and whānau affiliate to Ngātiki marae, and the wider mana whenua. Long standing members, including kuia and kaumatua, are active members of the kōhanga and continue to guide and support the whānau. Their commitment is invaluable to younger whānau members who choose their marae based kōhanga reo for their children.

“Pono ki te kaupapa, puna ko te reo.” (Whānau charter)

The whānau chose mana reo as the focus area for this Education Review. They assert that through te reo me ngā tīkanga Māori children will develop confidence and learn about their cultural identity and ancestral heritage. They participated in the design of the evaluation question which guided ERO’s review of mana reo,

“How well are our children developing their cultural identity and knowledge of ancestral heritage?”

Children are immersed in authentic and varied contexts of learning. They develop knowledge about who they are, and their connection to mana whenua. Daily experiences focus on promoting knowledge of their hapū and iwi. They observe and participate in activities that are unique to their hapū and iwi.

The quality of kaimahi interactions with children effectively supports children to develop confidence to explore and participate in te ao Māori. Whānaungatanga is reinforced in all learning and interactions by whānau and kaimahi. Children see and experience interactions that reinforce care, aroha, manaaki and respect.

Despite recent challenges with changes to staffing, continuity of learning and care is successfully upheld by kaimahi, kuia and their whānau. They have a shared commitment to continued growth of the kōhanga reo and this is strongly evident in children’s learning, care and the management of the kōhanga reo. Whānau aspirations for children who are confident in their language, culture and heritage is well understood by all as a priority for learning at the kōhanga reo.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Reo.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Mana Reo

Background

“How well are our children developing their cultural identity and knowledge of ancestral heritage?”

Areas of good performance

Children are receptive to and articulate the language that affirms their identity and ancestral heritage. Whānau have prioritised learning about te reo Māori me ona tīkanga for children. Children are supported by good speakers of te reo Māori. Effective language learning strategies are used to foster te reo Māori for all ages and in all learning situations. Conversations are promoted constantly as children explore and develop their confidence and knowledge of te reo Māori.

Children are developing as confident and enthusiastic learners. There are high quality interactions between kaimahi and children. Kaimahi frequently change activities which successfully supports children to develop the confidence to explore and express their own ideas. Kaimahi know their children well and design activities that sustain children’s interest in learning. Children are settled, happy and enjoy exploring their culture and ancestral heritage.

Children are immersed in learning about their identity and belonging. There is strong emphasis on experiences that enable children to actively participate in te ao Māori environments. Kaimahi reinforce connections of ngā atua Māori, local ancestral heritage, local customs and traditions to children. Waiata and karakia reinforce local history and significant stories about their tūpuna and mana whenua. Children participate appropriately as tangata whenua beside their whānau.

Children learn about the values of tiaki, manaaki tangata and aroha. All kaimahi and whānau model interactions that are caring, respectful and considerate. Interactions between children are positive. Tuakana show care and consideration to their teina. They are able to see how adults, including kuia and kaumatua interact with each other on the marae.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO evaluates how effectively whānau plan, manage and evaluate their kōhanga reo to achieve their objectives, shared aspirations with kōhanga management in the interests of their children. ERO considers the whānau evaluation question they designed for this review for planning and evaluation,

“How well are our children supported through effective whānau management of the kōhanga to achieve our shared aspirations?”

Areas of good performance

The whānau are working well towards realising their aspirations for their children. Whānau hui are held regularly and are well attended. Members are focused on monitoring key priorities for their children. These include providing learning experiences that develop children’s cultural identity and knowledge of their ancestral heritage.

Children’s needs and whānau aspirations are at the heart of planning and inform programme evaluation. Planning practices are well developed. Plans include a wide range of teaching and learning strategies to meet the needs and interests of all children. Programme plans are designed to support children to develop confidence and knowledge of their ancestral heritage, language and customs.

Children’s learning programmes are regularly evaluated by kaimahi. Kaimahi make children’s needs a priority when they reflect and evaluate their practice. They monitor, identify and respond to areas for improvement that are identified.

Whānau receive useful information about their children’s progress and overall development. Kaimahi report about how children’s confidence what they know about their ancestral heritage and language.

Areas for development

Some areas of reporting are limited. These includes reporting onTe Whāriki o Te Kōhanga Reolearning for children, health and safety, strategic priorities and whānau learning. Monitoring and reporting of progress about the programme and kōhanga reo priorities are important formal practices that will keep all whānau informed on their strategic direction and annual objectives.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement Self-Audit Checklistand. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

ERO identified the following areas of non-compliance:

4.1 ensure all staff who work with and around children are regularly police vetted[R44 Early Childhood Education and Care Centres 2008]4.2 ensure regular drills are carried out and recorded throughout the year for fire and earthquake, and that kaimahi are trained in the relevant emergency drills [R46 (1)(2) Early Childhood Education and Care Centres 2008, HS4-8]

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends:

  • whānau improve formal reporting on all areas of the kōhanga reo, at monthly whānau hui
  • whānau continue to use reporting and whānau self review to monitor the performance of their kōhanga, and to report on their growth and success of their charter objectives, planned strategic goals and whānau aspirations for children.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
