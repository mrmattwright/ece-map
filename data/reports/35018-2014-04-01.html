1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā tētahi ō ngā Kaiwhakahaere Matua ā-Rohe o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei, kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia e te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

  • te whakamāherehere me te aromātai; tae atu ki te whai wāhi a te whānau, ngā āwangawanga me ngā amuamu, te tiaki i ngā tuhinga, te whakahaere pūtea, te aromatawai me te aromātai, ngā arohaehae kaimahi, te whakamahere rautaki, te arotake whaiaro, me ngā tuhinga hoki e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru
  • ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture
  • ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai whai muri i te arotake o mua.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Pīpīwharauroa o Te Marae o Hukanui ki te marae o Hukanui ki te hapori tuawhenua iti o Gordonton, ki waho atu i te tāone o Kirikiriroa. Ka whakamana te whānau katoa i te iwi o Ngāti Wairere. Kua noho rangirua te kōhanga reo mō tētahi wā, whai muri i te matenga o tō rātou kaiako i whai wāhi nui ai hoki hei tino kaumātua o te marae. I mate ia, whai muri rawa mai i te arotake o mua. I roto i te tau kua pahure ake nei, kua pakari haere anō te whānau o te kōhanga reo, ā, i haere tonu ā rātou mahi ki te whakatutuki i ngā āwangawanga i te arotake o mua.

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua, me whakapakari, me whakapai hoki te whānau i ngā whakaritenga katoa e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga, hei whakatutuki i ngā tino painga mō ngā tamariki. I hāngai ēnei ki te:

  • whakapiki ake i te whai wāhi a te whānau ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga i te kōhanga reo
  • tuhi haere i ngā meneti tōtika a te whānau, kia hāngai pū ai aua tuhinga ki ngā take matua i kōrerohia ai
  • ū ki ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga, inā koa ko ērā i hāngai ai ki te hauora, te haumaru, me te waiora o ngā tamariki
  • whakapai ake i te whakahaere pūtea, te tiaki pūtea hoki, kia noho matua ai ngā tino putanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki
  • whakapakari i ngā mahi e pā ana ki te aromatawai me te aromātai i te hōtaka
  • whakaoti ngā arohaehae kaimahi
  • āta whakamahere, hei hāpai i te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ā rātou whāinga rautaki, ā rātou kaupapa rautaki hoki
  • whakawhanake i te tino māramatanga e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro
  • whakatakoto haere, te pūmau hoki i ngā tuhinga tōtika e pā ana ki ngā take hauora, take haumaru hoki.
  • I te wā o tēnei arotake, i kitea e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kua whakapau kaha te whānau, ā, kua whakatutukihia e rātou te nuinga o ngā wāhanga hei tirohanga ake. He take tonu e hāngai ana ki te aromātai hōtaka, te arohaehae kaimahi, me te whakamahere rautaki.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Te whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I tāutuhia te pūrongo o mua i te maha o ngā wāhanga hei whakatutukitanga mā te whānau. He āwangawanga i hāngai ai ki te āhua o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i ngā whakaritenga i te kōhanga reo. Ka aromātai tēnei arotake i te whānuitanga o te kōhanga reo ki te whakatutuki i aua take, me te whai pānga hoki o ēnei ki ngā painga mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

He pai te ahunga whakamua a te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā wāhanga o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, e whai ake nei:

  • Kua piki ake te whai wāhi a te whānau. Haere ai rātou ki ngā hui ā-whānau, ā, ka whakatau rātou i ngā whakaritenga hei painga mō ngā tamariki.
  • He mārama ngā meneti o ngā hui ā-whānau, ā, e hāngai pū ana ēnei ki ngā take i kōrerohia ai. He tau te whānau i roto i te mōhio, kua āta tuhia ngā whakawhitinga kōrero katoa, ā, kua whai wāhi hoki ā rātou ake whakapuakitanga.
  • E wātea ana ki ngā te whānau te katoa o ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga. Ka whakaatuhia ngā kaupapa here ki ngā pakitara, ā, kua whakaritea hoki tētahi atu wāhi hei pupuri i ngā tino tuhinga. E wātea ana ki te whānau ngā tuhinga ka hāpai i a rātou i roto i ō rātou tūranga.
  • Kua whakaritea i nāianei tētahi tukanga mō te whakahaeretanga o ngā pūtea. He mārama te pūrongo haere. Ko te tohatoha o ngā moni i roto i te pūtea, e tohu ana i te noho matua o ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki ki te whānau.
  • Ka aroturuki ngā kaimahi i ngā akoranga me te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki. Kua whakawhanakehia e te whānau tētahi anga aromatawai, ā, e whakamātauhia taua anga i tēnei wā. Ka arotakehia tēnei ā meāke nei. Ka whakamōhiotia te whānau mō te ako me te ahunga whakamua a ā rātou tamariki.
  • He pai ake te māramatanga o te whānau ki te arotake whaiaro. Ka pūrongo rātou i runga i te maha o ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kua tīmata rātou ki te tāutu i te whai pānga nui o te pūrongo e āhei te whakapai atu.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

He iti tonu te māramatanga o ngā kaimahi ki te aromātai hōtaka. Kei te rangirua tonu ngā whakaaro mō te rerekētanga o te aromatawai me te aromātai. Kāhore anō te whānau kia whakawhanake i ngā pūnaha ki te aroturuki i te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga, hei āta whakarite i te whakatutukitanga o ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki katoa.

He rangirua te pūnaha arohaehae, ā, he uaua te whai whakamāramatanga. Kāhore anō te arohaehae kaimahi kia oti. He iti noa ngā mōhiohio hei whakamahi ki te tātari i te tautoko e tika ana mō ngā kaimahi, ki te whakatairanga i te kounga o ā rātou mahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki.

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua, i whakawhanakehia tētahi mahere rautaki. Kāhore e taea tēnei te rapu i te wā o te arotake. Kāhore anō te whānau kia arotake ā rātou whāinga rautaki, kāhore anō rānei kia whakawhanakehia tētahi mahere mahi. He kaupapa matua tēnei hei āta whakarite i te whakapai tonutanga o te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki me te hapori.

Ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture

He whakamārama

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua i ngā wāhanga e rua kāhore i whakatutuki e ai ki te ture. Nā runga i aua wāhanga, me whakaatu te whānau i ngā tukanga hōneatanga o te wā, inā he ahi, he rū whenua rānei, me whakatutuki hoki i ngā akoako hōneatanga inā he ahi, he rū whenua, ā, me whakarato hoki i ngā mahi whakangungu ki ngā kaimahi. Ka arotake te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te whakatutukitanga, kāhore rānei, o te whānau i aua herenga ā-ture.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Kua whakatutukihia e te whānau ngā herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki aua wāhanga e rua.

  • Ka whakaatuhia ngā tukanga hōneatanga ki ngā wāhi putanga katoa o te kōhanga reo.
  • Whakatutukihia ai e te kōhanga reo i nāianei, ngā akoako i ia te wā, kei pā mai te ahi, te rū whenua rānei.

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ngā mahi inā ka pā mai tētahi āhuatanga ohorere.

Ngā taunakitanga

I whakawhiti kōrero te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau i ngā taunakitanga. I whakaae te whānau ki te toro atu ki te tautoko e tika ana hei:

  • whakapakari i te aromātai hōtaka
  • whakatinana i tētahi pūnaha arohaehae e tika ana mā ngā kaimahi
  • tāutu, hei tuhi, hei aroturuki hoki i ngā kaupapa matua me ngā mahi matua ki te kōhanga reo.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a ii te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu mō ngā Ratonga Arotake Māori (Te Uepū ā-Motu)

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation on the performance of the governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the previous ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • planning and evaluation including whānau participation, concerns and complaints, document control, financial management, assessment and evaluation, kaimahi appraisals, strategic planning, self review and health and safety documentation
  • areas of non-compliance
  • any issues identified since the previous review.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pīpīwharauroa o Te Marae o Hukanui is situated on Hukanui Marae in the small rural settlement of Gordonton on the outskirts of Hamilton. All whānau acknowledge Ngāti Wairere as the iwi. The kōhanga reo has been through an unsettling period with the death of their kaiako who was also a prominent kaumātua of the marae. This occurred directly after the previous review. Over the past year the kōhanga reo whānau has regained their strength and continued to address the concerns of the previous review.

The previous report identified that the whānau needed to strengthen and improve all management practices to ensure best possible outcomes for children. These included:

  • increased whānau participation in decision making for the kōhanga reo
  • keeping accurate records of whānau minutes to clearly reflect the key issues discussed
  • adherence to policy and procedure particularly where these relate to the health, safety and wellbeing of children
  • improved financial management and budgeting that gives priority to ensuring the best possible outcomes for children
  • strengthening assessment and programme evaluation practices
  • completing Kaimahi appraisals
  • clear planning to support whānau to meet their strategic goals and objectives
  • developing a strong understanding of self review
  • maintaining accurate records of health and safety issues.

During this review, ERO found that the whānau have worked hard and have achieved most of the areas that needed attention. There remain outstanding issues related to programme evaluation, kaimahi appraisals and strategic planning.

3 Findings

Planning and Evaluation

Background

The previous report identified a number of areas that the whānau needed to address. There were concerns with the ways in which the whānau managed the operations of the kōhanga reo. This review will evaluate the extent to which kōhanga reo has addressed these issues and how well these serve the best interests of the children.

Areas of progress

The whānau have made good progress in addressing the following areas of the kōhanga reo operations:

  • Whānau participation has increased. They attend whānau hui and make decisions in the best interests of the children.
  • Whānau hui minutes are clear and accurately reflect the issues that have been discussed. Whānau are satisfied that all discussions are clearly documented and their contributions are acknowledged.
  • All policies and procedure documents are available to whānau. Policies are displayed on the walls and a designated area holds other important documents. Whānau have access to documents to support them in their roles.
  • There is now a process in place for managing finances. Reporting is clear. Allocation of funds in the budget show that children’s learning needs are a priority to the whānau.
  • Kaimahi monitor children’s learning and progress. The whānau have developed an assessment framework which is currently being trialled. This will be reviewed in the near future. Whānau are informed about their children’s learning and progress.
  • Whānau have a better understanding of self review. They report against a number of aspects of kōhanga reo management and operations. They are beginning to identify the importance of reporting and how this leads to improvement.

Areas for further improvement

Kaimahi still have a limited understanding of programme evaluation. There is confusion about the difference between assessment and evaluation. Whānau are yet to develop systems for monitoring the quality of the learning programme to ensure that the learning needs of all children are provided for.

The appraisal system is confusing and difficult to understand. Kaimahi appraisals still have not been completed. There is a lack of information that can be used to determine what support kaimahi need to raise the quality of their practice with children.

The previous report identified that a strategic plan had been developed. This could not be located at the time of the review. Whānau have not reviewed their strategic goals or developed an action plan. This is a priority to ensure that the kōhanga reo continues to improve and benefit the children and the community.

Areas of non-compliance

Background

The previous report identified two areas of non-compliance. These required the whānau to display current fire and earthquake evacuation procedures, carry out regular fire and earthquake drills and provide training to staff. ERO will review whether the whānau have addressed these areas of non-compliance.

Areas of progress

The whānau have addressed both areas of non-compliance.

Evacuation plans are displayed at all evacuation points of the kōhanga reo.

The kōhanga reo now have regular fire and earthquake drills.

Children know what to do during an emergency.

Recommendations

ERO and the whānau discussed recommendations. The whānau agreed to seek support to:

  • strengthen programme evaluation
  • implement an appropriate kaimahi appraisal system
  • identify, document and monitor kōhanga reo priorities and actions.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
