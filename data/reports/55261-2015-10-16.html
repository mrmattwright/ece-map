Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pou Herenga Waka o Te Whakapono ki Mārewa, ki Ahuriri. Ka āta tautokona ngā tamariki kia pakari ai tā rātou kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao ako pai, hei tuakana, hei teina. Kua waihangatia ngā hōtaka akoranga hei aro atu ki ngā ngākau nuitanga ake, ngā pūmanawa ake hoki o ngā tamariki.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pou Herenga Waka o Te Whakapono i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pou Herenga Waka o Te Whakapono ki Mārewa, ki Ahuriri. Ka noho ngātahi te kōhanga reo me te whare karakia o ngā Mihingare Māori. Kua rua tekau mā iwa tau rātou e noho tahi ana, ā, he hononga e whai hua ana mō te katoa. Ka poipoi te wāhi nei i te kōhanga reo, ā, he marae anō hoki mō te hapori.

Kua roa te kōhanga reo e whiwhi ana i ngā pūrongo pai, mai i Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, nā runga i ngā whai wāhitanga ako a ngā tamariki, te reo Māori, me te pai o ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga. Ko te arotake whaiaro tētahi āhuatanga pakari, ā, ka ārahi tēnei i te whakahaeretanga me te whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te mārama, te kōrero hoki a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori?

E whakarato ana ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā rautaki whakawhanake reo e tautoko ana i a rātou kia mārama, kia tūmāia ai ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Mana Reo

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga o tētahi taiao reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaiako i te whānuitanga o ngā rautaki whakawhanaketanga reo ki te taha o ngā tamariki. He māhorahora te whakamahi a ngā tamariki tuākana i te reo Māori, ā, ka ākina ngā tamariki tēina ki te whakapuaki mai i ō rātou matea. Ka whakarato ngā kaiako i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te taki i ngā karakia, ngā mihimihi, ngā hīmene me ngā mōteatea. Ka whakatauira ngā kaumātua i te reo, ā, he mātātoa tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki te taha o ngā tamariki. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki ngā horopaki ōkawa me ngā horopaki ōpaki hoki ki te kōhanga reo me te marae. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ka whakaongaongatia ngā tamariki kia ako. Kua waihangatia te taiao ako hei whakatutuki i ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki. He whānui rawa ngā momo rauemi mō ngā reanga rerekē me ō rātou ngākau nuitanga rerekē hoki. Ka whakawātea ngā taumahi i ngā tamariki ki te tākaro takitahi, takirōpū rānei, me te whakatau i ā rātou whiringa ki ngā tākaro o roto, o waho hoki. Ka ākina ngā tamariki kia whai wāhi atu rātou ki ngā akoranga hei tuakana, hei teina. Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao akoranga pai.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

E whai hua ana ngā hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki. E mōhio ana ngā kaiako ki ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā pūmanawa o ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakamahi rātou i te aromatawai me te aromātai hei ārahi i te hōtaka. Ko ngā kitenga, ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, me ngā mahi a ngā tamariki ka whai pānga ki te aroturukitanga me te aromatawai o ngā tuhinga mauhanga. Hui ai ngā kaiako i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti kōrero mō te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki, ā, ki te whakapuaki whakaaro hoki hei whakapai ake i ā rātou akoranga. Ko te aro atu ki ngā taumata tonu ka whāia e ngā tamariki tētahi āhuatanga matua o ngā whakaritenga a ngā kaiako. Ka aro nui te whakamahere hōtaka ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

He āhuatanga matua te ako a ngā tamariki. Ka whai pānga te arotake whaiaro ki ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau. Ka arotahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakatutuki i ngā kaupapa matua o te kōhanga reo. Nā ngā hui ka whakapuaki te whānau i ngā āhuatanga whakahaere katoa, tae atu ki te ahu whakamua o te ako a ngā tamariki. Ko te reo Māori tētahi kaupapa matua mō ngā tamariki me te whānau. Ka tautokona te whānau me ngā kaimahi kia tae atu ki ngā hōtaka reo Māori. Ko ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki ka whai pānga ki ngā mahere i te kōhanga reo.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pou Herenga Waka o Te Whakapono ki Mārewa, ki Ahuriri. Ka āta tautokona ngā tamariki kia pakari ai tā rātou kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao ako pai, hei tuakana, hei teina. Kua waihangatia ngā hōtaka akoranga hei aro atu ki ngā ngākau nuitanga ake, ngā pūmanawa ake hoki o ngā tamariki.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pou Herenga Waka o Te Whakapono is in Marewa, Napier. Children are effectively supported to be confident speakers of te reo Māori. Children learn in a positive learning environment as tuakana, teina. Learning programmes are designed to respond to children’s individual interests and strengths.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pou Herenga Waka o Te Whakapono will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pou Herenga Waka o Te Whakapono is in Marewa, Napier. The kōhanga reo shares a site with the Anglican Māori Mission Church. The 29 year relationship between the organisations is mutually beneficial. The premise caters for the kōhanga reo and serves as a marae for the local community.

The kōhanga reo has a positive ERO reporting history about children’s learning opportunities, te reo Māori, positive interactions and relationships. Internal review is a strength and guides kōhanga reo management and operational practices.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children understand and speak te reo Māori?

Children are provided with an extensive range of language development strategies that effectively support them to understand and be confident speakers of te reo Māori.

Mana Reo

Children are immersed in a rich te reo Māori environment. Kaiako use an extensive range of language development strategies with children. Older children use te reo Māori spontaneously and younger children are encouraged to communicate their needs. Kaiako provide opportunities for children to lead karakia, mihimihi, hīmene and mōteatea. Kaumatua are language models who actively participate with children. Children hear te reo Māori in formal and informal settings in the kōhanga reo and the marae. Children are confident speakers of te reo Māori.

Children are stimulated to learn. The learning environment has been designed to meet the learning needs of children. There is an extensive range of resources different age groups and interests. Activities allow children to play independently or in a group and make choices about indoor or outdoor play. Children are encouraged to participate in learning as tuakana and teina. Children learn in a positive learning environment.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s learning programmes are effective. Kaiako know children’s interests and strengths and use assessment and evaluation to guide the programme. Observations, conversations and children’s work contributes to monitoring and assessment records. Kaiako meet regularly to discuss children’s progress and share ideas to improve their learning. Attending to children’s next steps is an integral part of kaiako practices. Programme planning is responsive to children’s learning.

Children’s learning is a priority. Internal review contributes to whānau management practices. Whānau and kaimahi are focussed on meeting kōhanga reo priorities. Meetings allow whānau to share all areas of operations including children’s progress with learning. Te reo Māori is a priority for children and whānau. Whānau and kaimahi are supported to attend te reo Māori programmes. Children’s learning needs influence kōhanga reo planning.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pou Herenga Waka o Te Whakapono is in Marewa, Napier. Children are effectively supported to be confident speakers of te reo Māori. Children learn in a positive learning environment as tuakana, teina. Learning programmes are designed to respond to children’s individual interests and strengths.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pou Herenga Waka o Te Whakapono will be in three years.
