1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

‘He kōtuku rerenga tahi’

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mokopuna o Te Matai ki tētahi marae ki Te Matai, i Te Puke. Koia nei te arotake tuatahi mō te kōhanga reo nei. E hiahia ana te whānau ki te mōhio ki te āhua o te ako me te whakawhanaketanga o ā rātou tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Ko tā rātou pātai matua e pēnei ana:

He pēhea te ako o ā tātou tamariki i tō rātou tuakiri, tō rātou whakawhanake hoki i te aronga toi whenuatanga?

Ka ako ngā tamariki i tō rātou tuakiri, ā, ka whai hua te hāpai i a rātou ki te whakawhanake i te aronga toi whenuatanga. Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e hāpai ana, e manaaki ana hoki i a rātou. He mauritau te noho a ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. Ka akiaki ngā tamariki ki te ako i tō rātou marae, ō rātou whakapapa, me tō rātou whenua. He tino tau, he tino pakari hoki ngā tamariki ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo. Ka ako ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki tētahi taiao e tino hāpai ana i a rātou. Ko te whakamahinga o te reo Māori e whakakoia ana i ngā taonga tuku iho, te ahurea, me te tuakiri hoki o ngā tamariki.

Ka whakapau kaha te whānau ki te whakahāere i te kōhanga reo. Ko tō rātou ngākau nuitanga e kōkiri ana i tā rātou whakarato i ngā painga mō ngā tamariki. He pai rawa ngā pūnaha pūtea. Ko ngā mahere rautaki e āta tāutu ana i ngā kaupapa matua o te kōhanga reo. Ka tāutu ngā mahere i te whānuitanga o ngā rautaki ka hāpai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka kitea ki te angamahi whakamahere me ngā aromatawai e tino te tirohanga nui ki te aronga toi whenuatanga me te tuakiri o ngā tamariki.

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia arotake te whānau i te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa here, me ngā tukanga. Me hāpai te whānau i ngā kaimahi ki te whakaoti ā rātou arohaehae. Kei te whakawhanake tonuhia ngā mahi arotake whaiaro, ā, kei te tīmatatanga tonu te māramatanga o te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakahāere i ngā aromātai hōtaka ka whai hua.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Whenua me Mana Tangata ngā kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Whenua, Mana Tangata

He whakamārama

He pēhea te ako o ā tātou tamariki i tō rātou tuakiri, tō rātou whakawhanake hoki i te aronga toi whenuatanga?

Ka ako ngā tamariki i tō rātou tuakiri, ā, e tino hāpaitia ana hoki kia whanake ai te aronga toi whenuatanga.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahāere

Mana Whenua. Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e hāpai ana, e manaaki ana hoki i a rātou. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te ako i tō rātou marae, tō rātou whakapapa, tō rātou whenua hoki, ā, ka kitea te tupu hāere o te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki ngā kawa me ngā tikanga. Ka whakaongaonga te hōtaka i ngā tamariki, ā, ka arotahi ki ō rātou ngākau nuitanga. He ngāwari te whakawhiti hāere a ngā tamariki ki waenga i te hōtaka akoranga me ngā mahinga. Ka kawe rātou i ngā mahi ki te tiaki i ngā rauemi me te taiao o te kōhanga reo. He ākonga pakari ngā tamariki, ā, he pārekareka ā rātou wheako ki te kōhanga reo.

Mana Tangata. Ka kitea te whakaute o ngā tamariki mō rātou anō, mō ētahi atu hoki. He tino pakari ō rātou hononga ki ō rātou hoa, ki te whānau hoki, ā, ka ahu mai tēnei i te ariā o te whanaungatanga. He manaaki tō ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā pākeke, ā, ka poipoi hoki i te tino aronga toi whenuatanga, me te whai pūtake. He whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te whakatau i ā rātou ake whakaritenga e pā ana ki tā rātou ako. Ka whakamihia ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakanuia hoki ā rātou mahi angitu. Nā ēnei hononga ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki ā rātou anō, ā, ka noho whakaute ki ētahi atu.

Te ako i te reo Māori. Ka ako ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki tētahi taiao e tino hāpai ana i a rātou. Ka whakarato te hōtaka akoranga ōkawa i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori i ngā wā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā waiata, ngā mihi, ngā whakapapa, me ngā pepeha. Ka ako rātou i te whakatakotoranga o ngā pepeha me te taki i ngā whakapapa i ia te wā. Ka whakapuakihia ngā pepeha ki ētahi atu tamariki, ā, he tino wā whakahoahoa anō hoki aua wā ako. Ka whakamahi te kaiako i ngā rautaki ako ōpaki, tae atu ki ngā waiata hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. He pārekareka ki ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki te waiata. Ka tino kitea te aronga toi whenuatanga i ngā tamariki e waiata tahi ana. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ngā pākeke hoki. Ka ākina rātou kia whakamahi i ngā mihi māmā, me te whakaute hoki i ngā pātai whānui. E whanake hāere ana te tū māia o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whakatutuki a te whānau i ngā painga mō ngā tamariki i roto i ngā mahi ki te whakahāere i te kōhanga reo?

E āta whakahāerehia ana ngā painga mō ngā tamariki e te whānau.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahāere

Te ngākau nuitanga, te āheinga hoki o te whānau. He pakari te ngākau nui o te whānau. Ka noho pūmau rātou ki te whakahāere i te kōhanga reo mā ngā huarahi pakari e whai hua ana. Kei te ū rātou ki te whakapiki ake i ā rātou tohu mātauranga, ō rātou mōhiotanga, ō rātou pūkenga anō hoki ki te whakahāere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Te whakahāere i ngā kaimahi. He tau te whakahāere i ngā kaimahi. Kua whakawhanakehia e te whānau ngā pūnaha e whai hua ana ki te hāpai ngā kaimahi i roto i ā rātou mahi. Kua oti kētia te whakarite i ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi me ngā kirimana tuku mahi.

Te whakahāere i te pūtea. He tau ngā pūnaha pūtea a te whānau. He mārama te kite i ngā pūrongo e pā ana ki ngā pūtea. Kua hokona e te whānau ngā rauemi mātauranga ka whai pānga ki te whakawero me te whakawhānui i ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou tākaro.

Te whakamahere hōtaka. Ka tāutu ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā rautaki ki ā rātou mahere, hei hāpai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He mahere mō ngā wā roa, ngā wā waenga, me te wātaka o ia rā. Ka tino kitea ki te angamahi whakamahere te aronga ki ngā whakaakoranga me ngā akoranga e pā ana ki te aronga toi whenuatanga me te tuakiri o ngā tamariki.

Te aromatawai. E whakawhanake ana ngā kaimahi i ngā tukanga aromatawai e whai hua ana ki te aroturuki me te pūrongo i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ko ngā pūkete me ētahi aromatawai ōkawa e whakamahia e rātou. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi me hāngai ngā pūkete ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Te arotake whaiaro. Me whakapakari ake te arotake whaiaro. Kāhore te whānau e aroturuki ana, e pūrongo ana rānei i ngā āhuatanga katoa o te whakahāeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ko ētahi o ēnei ko:

  • te tūtohinga me te mahere rautaki. Me āta aroturuki, me āta pūrongo ake hoki i te tūtohinga, te mahere rautaki me āna whāinga, hei āta tohu i te aronga mārama mō te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei
  • te arotake i ngā kaupapa here. Kei te whānau tētahi kohinga o ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga, tae atu ki ngā kaupapa here matua e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. Me arotake i aua kaupapa here i ia te wā
  • te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi. Kāhore anō ngā arohaehae kia oti, ā, he iti noa ngā mōhiohio hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga ngaio, te aroturuki i te āhua mahi o ngā kaimahi, me tōna whai pānga ki te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou manaaki
  • te aromātai hōtaka. He whāiti te aromātai hōtaka. Kāhore te aromātai hōtaka e tāutu ana i ngā whakapaitanga ki te hōtaka akoranga, kāhore rānei e arotahi ana ki te aronga toi whenuatanga, te tuakiri hoki o ngā tamariki.

Me matawhānui ake ngā pūrongo hei whakamōhio atu ki te whānau ngā āhuatanga katoa o te kōhanga reo.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahāerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahāerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahāerenga pūtea me ngā whakahāerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga hāerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

I tāutuhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te wāhanga kei raro iho nei, kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture:

  • me whakarite i te hāere tonutanga o tētahi tukanga e pā ana ki te arohaehae me te tautoko ngaio o ngā kaimahi i ia te wā.[R47 (1) (e) Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008]

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kia tahuri te whānau ki te:

  • arotake i te tūtohinga
  • arotake i ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga
  • tautoko i ngā kaimahi ki te whakaoti ā rātou arohaehae
  • whakapakari i te arotake whaiaro
  • rapua he tautoko hei hāpai i ngā kaimahi ki te whakapakari ake i tō rātou māramatanga, tō rātou mōhiotanga anō hoki ki te aromātai hōtaka.

6 Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

E takune ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ki te whakahāere i tētahi atu arotake i roto i ngā tau e rua.

Lynda Pura-Watson

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

‘He kōtuku rerenga tahi’

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mokopuna o Te Mataiis marae-based and located in Te Matai, Te Puke.This is the first review for the kōhanga reo.Whānau wanted to know how well their children were doing in their learning and development at kōhanga reo. They specifically asked:

How well do our children learn about their identity and develop a sense of belonging?

Children learn about their identity and are effectively supported to develop a sense of belonging. Children learn in a supportive and caring environment. Kaimahi maintain positive relationships with children. They encourage children to learn about their marae, whakapapa and whenua. Children are self assured and are confident in the kōhanga reo environment. Children learn te reo Māori in a very supportive environment. The use of te reo Māori affirms children’s heritage, culture and identity.

Whānau manage the kōhanga reo to the best of their ability. They are motivated by their commitment to providing the best for their children. Financial systems are sound. Strategic planning clearly identifies kōhanga reo priorities. Planning identifies a range of strategies to support children’s learning. The planning framework and assessments strongly reflect a focus on children’s belonging and identity.

ERO recommends that whānau review the charter and policies with procedures. Whānau need to support kaimahi to complete their appraisals. Self review practices are still developing and whānau and kaimahi are at the early stages of understanding how to undertake effective programme evaluation.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose mana whenua and mana tangata as the focus areas.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Mana Whenua, Mana Tangata

Background

How well do our children learn about their identity and develop a sense of belonging?

Children learn about their identity and are effectively supported to develop a sense of belonging.

Areas of good performance

Mana Whenua: Children learn in a supportive and caring environment. Kaimahi encourage children to learn about their marae, whakapapa and whenua and children demonstrate a growing understanding of kawa and tikanga. The programme is stimulating for children and focuses on their interests. Children transition smoothly between the learning programme and routines. They take responsibility for caring for resources and the kōhanga reo environment. Children are confident learners who enjoy their kōhanga reo experiences.

Mana Tangata. Children show respect for themselves and others. They have formed strong relationships with their peers and whānau based on the concept of whanaungatanga. Interactions among children and adults are caring and foster a positive sense of belonging and purpose. Children have opportunities to make their own choices and decisions about their learning. Children are praised and everyone celebrates their successes. Through these relationships, children learn about themselves and have respect for others.

Learning te reo Māori. Children learn te reo Māori in a very supportive environment. The formal learning programme provides varied opportunities for children to use te reo Māori. Children use te reo Māori during karakia, hīmene, waiata, mihi, whakapapa and pepeha. They learn the format for pepehā and recitation of whakapapa on a regular basis. Pepeha are shared with other children and are valuable social learning times. The kaiako uses informal learning strategies including waiata to support children’s te reo Māori development. Children sing waiata and perform waiata a ringa that highlight their identity. Kaimahi and children enjoy waiata. There is a strong sense of belonging when children sing together. Children engage in conversations with each other and adults. They are encouraged to use simple greetings and respond to routine questions. Children are developing in confidence as speakers of te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How well are children’s interests catered for when whānau manage the kōhanga reo?

The interests of children are purposefully managed by the whānau.

Areas of good performance

Whānau commitment and capability. Whānau commitment is strong. They are committed to managing the kōhanga reo in an efficient and effective way. the kōhanga reo in the best interests of children.They are determined to increase their qualifications, knowledge and skills to manage

Personnel management. Personnel management is appropriate. Whānau have developed useful systems to support kaimahi in their work. Job descriptions and employment contracts are complete.

Financial management. Whānau have sound financial systems in place. Financial reporting is clear and transparent. Whānau have purchased education resources to help challenge and extend children in their play.

Programme planning. Kaimahi identify range of strategies to support children’s learning in their planning. Planning includes long term and medium term plans and a daily timetable. The planning framework strongly reflects a focus on teaching and learning about children’s belonging and identity.

Assessment. Kaimahi are developing useful assessment processes to monitor and report on children’s learning. They use profiles books and some formal assessments. Kaimahi are aware that profile books need to focus on children’s learning.

Areas for development

Self review.Self review needs strengthening. Whānau do not regularly monitor or report all aspects of the kōhanga reo operations. These include:

  • charter and strategic plan.Closer monitoring and reporting on the charter, strategic plan and its goals is required to provide clear direction for the future of the kōhanga reo
  • policy review. Whānau have a set of policies and procedures in place that includes core health and safety policies. These need to be reviewed regularly
  • staff appraisals. Appraisals are incomplete and there is limited information to inform professional development, the monitoring of kaimahi performance and its impact on children’s learning and care
  • programme evaluation. Programme evaluation is limited. Programme evaluation does not identify improvements to the learning programme or focus on children’s belonging and identity.

More detailed reporting is needed to inform whānau on all aspects of the kōhanga reo.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO LicensedKōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

ERO identified the following areas of non-compliance:

  • ensure that an ongoing process for the regular appraisal and professional support for staff is in place.[R47 (1) (e) Education (ECC) Regulations 2008]

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends that whānau:

  • review the charter
  • review policies and procedures
  • support kaimahi to complete their appraisals
  • strengthen self review
  • seek support to assist kaimahi to strengthen their understanding and knowledge of programme evaluation.

6 When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO intends to carry out another review within two years.
