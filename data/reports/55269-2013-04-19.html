1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kua whakaingoatia Te Kōhanga Reo o Keita Ahenata Pūriri, hei tohu maumahara mō Keita Ahenata Pūriri. Nō Ngāti Porou ia, nō te whānau Goldsmith e mōhio whānuitia ana i te Tai Rāwhiti. I moe a Keita ki a Hēmi Pūriri, nō Bridge Pā. Kua huri tā rāua tamāhine a Hana Cotter ki tua o te ārai, engari nāna te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo i whakatūngia ā-kāinga ai ki tētahi marae, ā, ko Ngā Pakiaka ki te Iwi te ingoa.

Ko tā Hana Cotter tirohanga, ko te poipoi, ko te whakamana hoki i te whānau, ngā kaimahi, me ngā tamariki mā te horopaki o te mātauranga Māori, te reo Māori, me ngā tikanga Māori. I ēnei rā, nā te noho pūmau o tana tamāhine, rātou ko tōna whānau me te whānau whānui o te kōhanga reo, e ora tonu ana tana tirohanga, me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. Ka whakanuia, ka whakatōngia hoki e te whānau ngā whakapono, ngā uaratanga, me ngā whakaritenga i whakatinanahia ai i tēnei kōhanga reo.

He tokomaha ngā mema o te whānau e wawata tahi ana kia rumakina ā rātou tamariki ki te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori. Ka noho matua te reo Māori hei tino kaupapa, puta noa i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero tuatahi a te whānau me ngā kaimahi. Kāhore he hiahia tua atu i tēnā kia pakari, kia mātau hoki ngā tamariki me ngā whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Keita Ahenata Pūriri, ki te reo Māori. Nā runga i tēnei āhuatanga, i whakaritea ai ko te reo Māori te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

Tokotoru ngā kaimahi pūhou, hihiri, tākare hoki o te whānau kotahi, ā, he pai tā rātou mahi ngātahi ki te whakahaere i te hōtaka o ia rā mā ngā tamariki. Kua puta kē ngā tuāhine tokorua i Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Te Ara Hou. Ko tō rāua tungāne te kaiako whakakapi. Ka āta tautokona, ka āta arahina rātou e ngā tāngata pakeke ake ana o te whānau. He kaha rātou ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou mōhiotanga, ō rātou mātanga, ō rātou tūmanako hoki mā ngā huarahi pakari e āwhina ana, e atawhai ana hoki i a rātou ngā kaimahi. Ko te whakawhanaketanga me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā kaimahi taiohi nei, kei te pūtake o ō rātou whakaaro. Nā te whakatōpū i te taiohitanga, te kaumātuatanga, te mātau hoki, ka puta te hua o te noho ā-whakatupuranga ki te kounga o te atawhai me te ako o ngā tamariki, ā, e tino atawhaitia ana, e tino manaakitia ana hoki.

He pai ngā pūnaha whakahaere, whakarite haere ā te whānau. E tino mōhiotia ana aua pūnaha, ā, nā ēnei, e āhei ana ngā mema ki te tino whai wāhi atu, me te āta tautoko hoki i ngā taumahi. He pārekareka ki ngā mema te whai wāhi atu ki te kōhanga reo. He ātaahua te taiao mō te whānau me ngā tamariki, puta noa i te nohonga kāinga. He hāneanea te āhua, ā, ka whai pūtake hoki. Ko ngā whakaahua o rātou kua huri ki tua o te ārai, e whakapūmau tonu ana i ō rātou wairua, ō rātou tirohanga hoki ki te ao o ā rātou tamariki, ā rātou mokopuna, ā rātou mokopuna tuarua, ā, i te whānau whānui hoki. He harikoa ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ko ngā wāhanga matua hei whakawhanake ake, i hāngai ai ki te whānau me ngā kaimahi, arā, kia rautaki ake ai, kia āta whakatakoto haere ai tā rātou whakamahere me ā rātou mahi, kia rumaki, kia pakari, kia mātau hoki rātou i roto i te reo Māori. Me tautoko hoki i ngā kaimahi ki te whakaaro huritao, te aromātai hoki i ia te wā, i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki. Tāpiri atu ki tēnei, me pakari ake te aroturuki, te tuhi haere, me te pūrongo a ngā kaimahi i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Nā tēnei, ka āta whakatutukihia ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki katoa, mā ngā huarahi auaha, e whakawero ana, e whakaongaonga ana hoki i a rātou.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Keita Ahenata Pūriri kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Keita Ahenata Pūriri.

Whai muri i te hui ā-whānau tuatahi, i taunaki te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia hāngai te arotahi o te arotake ki te reo Māori.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Reo

He whakamārama

Ko te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, te reo Māori, me ngā tikanga Māori, ngā tino wawata o te whānau, ngā kaumātua me ngā kaimahi. E kore e mutu tā rātou noho pūmau ki te tirohanga me te tauāki o ō rātou kuia, ō rātou koroua hoki. Koia nei te āhuatanga e kōkiri ana i te whakatinanatanga o te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki me te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Keita Ahenata Pūriri.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te reo Māori a ngā tamariki. Ka kōrero ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ngā kaimahi, ki te whānau, ā, ki ētahi atu hoki. Ka whakapakaritia te tū me te āheinga o ngā tamariki i roto i te reo Māori, mā te taki i ngā karakia, ngā mihimihi, ngā pepeha, me ngā whakapapa. Ka kitea te tū takitahi, te pakari hoki o ngā tamariki tuākana ki te whakatauira i ngā hanganga reo e mōhiotia ana, mō ō rātou tēina. E ako ana ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te roanga o ngā rerenga kōrero i roto i ā rātou taunekeneke, taumahi hoki, puta noa i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka tautoko, ka akiaki hoki ngā kaimahi, te whānau, me ngā kaumātua i ngā tamariki, i roto i tā rātou whakamahinga o te reo Māori hei reo matua ki te whakawhitiwhiti whakaaro i te kōhanga reo.

Te ako a ngā tamariki me te taiao. Ka whakatau te taiao mahana o te kōhanga reo, ā, he ātaahua te taiao me te whakatakotoranga. Ko ngā wāhi whakahirahira, ngā taonga tuku iho, ngā whakaahua, me ētahi atu taputapu, ka kapo ake i te hiahia o te tangata i tana taenga atu ki te nohonga ā-kāinga nei. Ka whakatairanga ēnei i te wāhi akoranga tautāwhi, mārie hoki mō ngā tamariki. He pai te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi o waho, o roto hoki, me ngā rauemi, ngā hanganga hoki, kia aro atu ai ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga, kia tūhura hoki ai rātou. Ka whakapau kaha, ka harikoa hoki te whānau ki te whakarato i te taiao ātaahua e whakaongaonga ana i ngā tamariki. He pai te whakatau haere a ngā tamariki ki ngā mahinga me ngā taumahi o ia rā.

Mana Atua. He nui te aroha o ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki ngā tamariki. Ka poipoi rātou i te whakawhanaketanga o te katoa o ia tamaiti, ki tētahi taiao e hāngai pū ana ki ngā tamariki. Ka arotahi ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga ki te whakapakari i te tū pakari, te tū mōhio hoki o te tamaiti, ki roto i a ia anō. Ka kitea te ngākau māhaki o te whānau, i roto i ā rātou mahi mā ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā tino uaratanga, ngā tino whakapono anō hoki e pou here ana i ā rātou mahi. He pakari, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki.

Ngā huarahi o ngā mokopuna. Ko ngā huarahi ako, ngā huarahi whakawhanake hoki ka whāia tonuhia e ngā tamariki ā meāke nei, e noho matua ana ki te whānau. Nā tēnei, ka whakaritea ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti i te kōhanga reo, ki ō rātou huarahi ako ka whai ake. E tūmanakohia ana, kia haere ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Keita Ahenata Pūriri ki te kura kaupapa Māori me te wharekura. Mai i reira, ka noho wātea hoki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo huarahi mātauranga anō, pērā i te whare wānanga, ngā kuratini hoki e hiahiatia ana e rātou. Ko ngā tamariki ngā rangatira o āpōpō, ā, ka whakaratohia e te whānau ngā tino tūāpapa mātauranga ki a rātou, i runga i ngā tino tūmanako e wawatatia ana, me te anga whakamua o ngā whakaaro.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te whakatairanga i te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga. Kua tae ki te wā, kia mahi tahi ngā kaimahi ki te taha o tā rātou kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe me ngā tuākana o te whānau, ki te āta whai whakaaro ki ngā huarahi ka whakatairanga i te kounga me te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga o tēnei wā. Me hāngai hoki tēnei ki te āta whakatau i te whakatutukitanga o ngā matea ako me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ia tamaiti.

Te reo Māori. Kia noho rumaki ai rātou ki te reo Māori, e tika ana kia āta kōkiri, kia nahanaha hoki te whānau, ngā kaimahi, me ngā kaumātua i roto i tō rātou wawata ki te whakapiki ake i tō rātou āheinga ki te whakamahi me te kōrero i te reo Māori. Me whakarite i tētahi tino rautaki e pā ana ki te reo Māori. Kei te tari ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo te tautoko ki te whakawhanake i tēnei rautaki, hei whakapātanga atu mā te whānau.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai i ngā kōhanga reo, hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, mō ā rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka, me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. E mōhio ana te whānau ki te whai pānga o te kaupapa kōhanga reo ki te whakatōpū i ngā tāngata. E pou here ana te whanaungatanga, te manaakitanga, me te tautoko i te aronga ngātahi, kia whai wāhi ai ngā mātua me te whānau ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakamanatia e ngā mema te kōhanga reo hei tūrangawaewae e poipoi ana i te tuakiri, te ahurea, me te reo. Nō Parīhi, nō Hāmoa, nō Wanuatu, nō Īnia hoki ētahi o ngā mema o te whānau. Ka whakatōpū te whānau i te whānuitanga o ō rātou pūkenga, ō rātou pūmanawa, ō rātou wheako, ō rātou mōhiotanga anō hoki, hei whakapuakitanga mā ngā tamariki me te whānau. Tua atu i tēnei, ko te noho pūmau o ngā kaumātua, hei whakatau, hei hāpai, hei tautāwhi anō hoki i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki me te whānau.

Ngā tūranga o ngā kaimahi, me ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki i te hōtaka akoranga. He tino mārama te aratohu ā te kaiako whakakapi me āna kaiāwhina tokorua e hihiri ana, mō te whakamahere me te whakapuaki i te hōtaka. He pai tō rātou māramatanga ki te tirohanga whānui o tā rātou hōtaka, me te kiko o roto. Ka whakawhiti kōrero ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, mō ngā tūranga mahi me ngā kawenga i tohaina ai hei hāpai i te hōtaka o ia rā. Ko ngā tuākana o te whānau me te kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe e hāpai ana, e ārahi ana, e tohutohu ana hoki i ngā kaimahi pūhou, i roto i ā rātou mahi whakamahere, whakariterite, aroturuki hoki o te hōtaka akoranga. He pakari, he tākare hoki ngā kaimahi, ā, ka whakaatu i te tino ngākau nui ki te mahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki. Ko te harikoa o ngā tamariki, me ō rātou matea ako, ō rātou ngākau nuitanga hoki, e noho matua ana ki ngā kaimahi pūhou nei.

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau. E whai hua ana ngā pūnaha a te whānau mō te whakahaeretanga me te whakaritenga. E tino mōhiotia ana aua pūnaha, ā, nā tēnei, e āhei ana ngā mema ki te tino whai wāhi atu me te āta hāpai hoki i ngā taumahi. Kua whakaritea ngā tuhinga, ā, e āhei ana ki te whakapā atu. Ka noho pūmau te whānau ki ngā whakaritenga papai, te aroturukitanga i ia te wā, me te arotakenga o te whakahaere o te kōhanga reo. E mōhio ana te whānau, me arotake i ngā wā katoa, ko te rōnakitanga o te kōhanga reo mō te wā kei te heke mai. Ka whai pānga te tauatanga ki aua whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i ia te wā. He pārekareka ki te whānau tō rātou kōhanga reo, ā, he tau te whakaaro ki a rātou, e whiwhi ana ā rātou tamariki i te pai o te atawhai me ngā wheako akoranga ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te aromātai i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Me hāpai ngā kaimahi pūhou i te kōhanga reo, ki te whakaaro huritao i ia te wā, me te aromātai anō hoki i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki. Tāpiri atu ki tēnei, me auau ake, me pakari ake hoki mā rātou, te āhua o te aroturuki, te whakatakoto, me te pūrongo i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ka āta whakarite tēnei, ka āta whakatutukihia ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki katoa, mā ngā huarahi auaha, e whakawero ana, e whakaongaonga ana hoki i a rātou.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Keita Ahenata Pūriri, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātaurangame ngā whakaritengakaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo, ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakawhanakehia tētahi tino rautaki e pā ana ki te reo Māori, hei ārahi i te whānau i roto i tō rātou wawata, ā rātou mahi nui hoki ki te noho rumaki i te reo Māori

  • whakapā atu rātou ki ngā whai wāhitanga ka hāpai, ka tautoko hoki, mā te tari o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, mā ngā mātanga hoki o te hapori, me ngā ratonga wānanga, ngā whiringa ako anō hoki

  • ū tonu te kōkiri whakamua i roto i ngā mahi me ngā whakahaeretanga katoa i te kōhanga reo.

6 Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

Makere Smith

Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu mō ngā Ratonga Arotake Māori (Te Uepū ā-Motu)

19 Paenga-whâwhâ 2013

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Keita Ahenata Puriri is named after the late Keita Ahenata Puriri. She was of Ngāti Porou descent and came from the well known Goldsmith whānau of the East Coast. Keita married Hemi Puriri of Bridge Pā. Their daughter, the late Hana Cotter, established the kaupapa of kōhanga reo in a home-based marae setting which was named Ngā Pakiaka ki te Iwi.

Hana Cotter’s vision was to nurture and empower whānau, staff and children within the context of mātauranga Māori, te reo Māori and tikanga Māori. Today, her legacy continues through the commitment of her daughter, her whānau and the wider kōhanga reo whānau to her vision and to the kaupapa of kōhanga reo. The whānau aspires to and instils the beliefs, values and practices upon which this kōhanga reo was founded.

Several members of the kōhanga reo whānau shared aspirations for their children to be fully immersed in te reo Māori and tikanga Māori. The kaupapa of te reo Māori featured strongly throughout initial whānau and staff discussions. There is no greater desire than for children and whānau of Keita Ahenata Puriri to become confident and capable speakers of te reo Māori. On this basis, the focus for this review was determined to be te reo Māori.

Three youthful, energetic and enthusiastic siblings work well together to manage the children’s daily programme. The two sisters are graduates of Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Te Ara Hou. Their older brother has the role of acting kaiako. They are well supported and guided by older members of the whānau. The latter readily share their knowledge, experiences and expectations in firm, helpful and kindly ways. They have the future development and interests of their young staff at heart. The combination of youth, age and wisdom provides a strong intergenerational base for the quality care and learning of the children who are well loved and cared for.

The whānau has effective management and operational systems in place. These are known well and enable members to contribute to and support activities in the best way that they can. Members enjoy being at the kōhanga reo. The whānau and children have a beautiful environment throughout the home-based complex. It is comfortable and practical. Special portraits of those who have passed on ensure that their spirit and vision lives on in their children, grandchildren, great grandchildren and in those who may follow. Children are happy at the kōhanga reo.

Key areas for development include the need for both whānau and staff to be strategic and deliberate in planning and working towards becoming fully immersed, confident and capable in te reo Māori. 3 Findings

Mana Reo

Background

The kaupapa of kōhanga reo, te reo Māori and tikanga Māori are key aspirations for whānau, kaumātua and staff. Their commitment to the vision and mission of their late kuia and korōua is unwavering. This is a driving force behind the realisation of the kaupapa of kōhanga reo for the children and whānau of Keita Ahenata Puriri.

Areas of good performance

Children’s kōrero in te reo Māori. Children use te reo Māori with each other, with staff and whānau and with others. Children’s confidence and ability in te reo Māori is progressed through reciting karakia, mihimihi, pepeha and whakapapa. Tuakana show independence and good confidence to model familiar structures for their teina. Children are learning to use complete sentences in their interactions and activities throughout the learning programme. Staff, whānau and kaumātua support and encourage children to use te reo Māori as their main language of communication at the kōhanga reo.

Children’s learning and physical environment. The kōhanga reo environment is welcoming, homely and attractively landscaped and organised. Interesting areas, artefacts, photographs and objects capture one’s attention on entering this home-based complex. These enhance a calm and peaceful learning space for children. There is good variety of outdoor and indoor activities, resources and structures to engage children in learning and exploring. Whānau take great pride and pleasure in providing children with a stimulating attractive environment. Children settle well to their daily routines and activities.

Mana Atua. Staff and whānau have a great love for the children. Together they nurture children’s holistic development in a child-centred learning environment. Interactions and relationships focus on building children’s self-confidence and self-awareness. Whānau display a softness in the way that they do things for their children at the kōhanga reo. Strong values and beliefs underpin their practices. Children are confident and happy.

Mokopuna mapping. Children’s future learning and developmental pathways are a priority for the whānau. Mokopuna mapping involves preparing the children for transition from kōhanga reo to their next phases of learning. Graduates of Keita Ahenata Puriri are expected to attend kura kaupapa Māori and wharekura. From there they will have a range of tertiary options from whare wānanga to a career academy of choice. The children, who are tomorrow’s future, are being provided with the early foundations of learning by whānau who are aspirational and forward thinking.

Areas for development

Raising the quality of the learning programme. It is timely that the staff work closely with their district kaupapa kaimahi and senior whānau members to thoroughly consider ways to lift the quality and effectiveness of the current learning programme. This should involve providing assurance that all children’s individual learning needs and interests are being well catered for.

Te reo Māori. In order to become fully immersed in te reo Māori it is vital that whānau, staff and kaumātua are deliberate and systematic in their ambition to increase their ability to use and speak te reo Māori. A specific strategy for te reo Māori is required. Support to develop this strategy is readily accessible and available from the local office of the National Trust.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

The kaupapa of kōhanga reo. Whānau acknowledge the impact of the kaupapa of kōhanga reo in bringing people together. Whanaungatanga, manaakitanga and tautoko underpin an inclusive approach to involving parents and whānau in the kōhanga reo. Members value the kōhanga reo as a place to stand, a place where identity, culture and language is valued. Whānau include members who come from Brazil, Samoa, Vanuatu and India. Members bring a wide range of skills, talents, experiences and knowledge that are shared with the children and whānau. Above all, the constant presence of kaumātua provides security, stability and support for the kaupapa of kōhanga reo, for the children and whānau.

Staff roles and responsibilities for the learning programme. The acting kaiako and his two energetic kaiāwhina have clear guidelines for programme planning and delivery. They have a good understanding of their programme overview and content. Staff have ongoing discussions about delegated roles and responsibilities for the daily programme. Senior whānau members and the district kaupapa kaimahi support, guide and advise the young staff in their planning, organisation and monitoring of the learning programme. The staffs are confident, enthusiastic and display a genuine joy in working with the children. They are spontaneous and motivated in their care and love for the children. The children’s happiness, their learning needs and interests are important to these young tutors.

Whānau management. The whānau has effective management and operational systems in place. These are known well and enable members to contribute to and support activities in the best way that they can. Documentation is organised and accessible. Whānau attest to good practice, and regular monitoring and reviewing of kōhanga reo management. Whānau are aware of the need to ensure that the future sustainability of the kōhanga reo is continually reviewed. Succession planning is part of these ongoing discussions. Whānau enjoy their kōhanga reo and are satisfied that their children receive good care and learning experiences at the kōhanga reo.

Areas for development

Evaluating the effectiveness of the learning programme. The young staff at the kōhanga reo require support in regularly reflecting on and evaluating the effectiveness of the children’s learning programme. In addition, they require a more regular and robust way of monitoring, recording and reporting children’s learning and development. This is to ensure that the needs and interests of all children are being met in creative, challenging and stimulating ways.

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare

  • financial and property management.

  • to maintain a forward momentum in all kōhanga reo practices and operations.

These are the findings of the Education Review Office’s latest report on Te Kōhanga Reo o Keita Ahenata Pūriri.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Keita Ahenata Puriri is named after the late Keita Ahenata Puriri. She was of Ngāti Porou descent and came from the well known Goldsmith whānau of the East Coast. Keita married Hemi Puriri of Bridge Pā. Their daughter, the late Hana Cotter, established the kaupapa of kōhanga reo in a home-based marae setting which was named Ngā Pakiaka ki te Iwi.

General Information About Reviews

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
