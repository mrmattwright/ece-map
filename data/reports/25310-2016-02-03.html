Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Paa Harakeke ki Māngere, ki tua atu i te marae o Te Puea. He kaha te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou iwi, tō rātou kōhanga reo, ā, ki a rātou anō hoki. He pai te mahi ngātahi a te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga ki te kōhanga reo. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e hāpai ana, e manaaki ana hoki i a rātou.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Paa Harakeke i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Paa Harakeke ki tua atu i te marae o Te Puea, ki Māngere i Tāmaki-makau-rau ki te tonga. Ka poipoia ngā tamariki e ngā tikanga me ngā kawa o Tainui. Ka noho ngākau nui te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakarato i te hōhonutanga o tētahi taiao ako whakaongaonga mō ngā tamariki katoa, tae atu hoki ki ngā tamariki matea tautāwhi.

Ko Tainui te waka Ko Te Mānukanuka a Hoturoa te moana Ko Te Ara Pūeru te maunga Ko Te Puea te marae.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te whakahono atu a ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō, ki ō rātou iwi hoki?

He kaha te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou iwi, tō rātou kōhanga reo, ā, ki a rātou anō hoki.

Mana Whenua me te Mana Reo

Ka noho whakaute ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao. Ka tiaki rātou i ā rātou ake taputapu, me ngā rauemi hoki o te kōhanga reo. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te tiaki whaiaro me te kawenga ki ngā tamariki. Ka whakarato rātou i ngā kai hauora, ā, ka whakanui i te kai i ngā kai pai. Ka whakatairangatia ngā mahinga o ia rā e pā ana ki te hauora me te waiora. E whanake ana te tū takitahi o ngā tamariki.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i tō rātou tūnga hei tangata whenua. Ka mātakitaki rātou i ngā kuia me ngā kaumātua e tū ana hei kaikaranga, hei kaikōrero, hei kaiwaiata hoki i ngā pōwhiri me ngā kaupapa ōkawa ki te marae. Ka whakatinanahia aua tūranga ki te kōhanga reo, ā, nā tēnei he mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā tikanga me ngā kawa. Ka whakatau ngā tamariki i ngā manuhiri, ā, ka kitea tō rātou manaakitanga, tō rātou aroha hoki.

Ka tū manawa whakahī ngā tamariki hei Tainui. He tākare te whakapuaki a ngā tamariki i ō rātou hononga ā-whānau, ā-hapū, ā-iwi hoki mā ngā pepeha, ngā mōteatea, ngā karakia, ngā waiata, me ngā hīmene. Ka whakanui rātou i te Kīngitanga, ā, ka whai wāhi ki ngā huihuinga, pērā i ngā poukai, ngā koroneihana, me matariki. Ka ārahi, ka wāhi atu hoki ngā tamariki i ngā karakia o te Pai Mārire. He kaha te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki.

Ka mārama, ka whakamahi hoki ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki reo. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā kaimahi ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki te taha o ngā tamariki. Ka mārama, ka whai hoki ngā tamariki i ngā tohutohu, ā, ka whakamahi i ngā kīanga māmā me ngā mahi ā-ringa, hei whakaatu i ō rātou matea me ō rātou whakaaro. He pakari ngā tamariki tuākana ki te ārahi i ngā mahinga o ia rā. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti kōrero mā te reo Māori.

Ka rongo ngā tamariki i ngā hononga e mau nei i te atawhai me te aroha. He tākare, he hihiri hoki ngā kaimahi. Ka whakatauira rātou i te aroha me te manaakitanga i a rātou e taunekeneke ana ki ngā tamariki. Whakamihi ai ngā kaimahi i ngā whakamātau a ngā tamariki i ia te wā, ā, ka aro atu ki ō rātou matea ā-tinana, ā-whatumanawa hoki. He tino tauira ngā tuākana mō ngā tēina, ā, ka ākina ngā tuākana ki te hāpai i ngā mahinga, te reo Māori, me ngā taumahi a ngā tēina. Ka tākaro ngātahi ngā tamariki, ā, ka kitea tō rātou atawhai ki ngā kaimahi.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Ko te iti noa o ngā pokapū ako me ngā rauemi e whakawhāiti ana i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Kāhore i te rawaka ngā rauemi ka whakaongaonga i te hīraurau hopanga, te tūhura, te mahi auaha me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ia tamaiti. Kua tāutuhia e te kaiako tēnei āhuatanga hei kaupapa matua.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. Kua ahu whakamua rātou i roto i te maha o ngā wāhanga matua, tae atu ki te whakatū i ngā kaimahi hou, te whakapakari i ngā hononga, te whakapiki ake i te tatauranga tamariki, me te whakatau i ngā tamariki. Ka whakahaerehia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Kua tīmata te whānau ki te tāutu i ngā whāinga rautaki hou, ā, ka hāngai ētahi o ēnei ki te whakangungu kaimahi, te whai wāhi mai o te whānau, me te whakarato i ngā whai wāhitanga ka hāpai i te whānau. Hui ai rātou i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti kōrero, te aroturuki, me te pūrongo hoki i ngā tūmomo āhuatanga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka noho pūmau rātou ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo mā te aronga nui ki te waiora me te mātauranga o ngā tamariki.

Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te mārama o ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakamahere i te hōtaka. Kei ngā mahere o te pae tawhiti me te pae tata te tino kiko ka ārahi i ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki. Ka whai pānga ngā kaupapa ako ki te ao Māori, me ngā mōhiotanga, ngā uaratanga hoki o te iwi, tae atu ki te whakatewhatewha hoki i te ao whānui. Ka whakawerohia te ako a ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki whakahihiri e whai pūtake nui ana.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kei te tīmata te whakawhanaketanga o ngā mahi aromatawai me te aromātai hōtaka. Ahakoa kua waia ngā kaimahi ki ngā tūmomo huarahi aromatawai, kāhore ēnei e whakamahia ana i ia te wā. Nā tēnei, he uaua ki te tātari i te ahu whakamua i whakatutukihia ai e ngā tamariki. He ōpaki te nuinga o ngā hui a ngā kaimahi, ā, kāhore e tū auau ana. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e aromātai ana i te hōtaka akoranga, ngā rauemi, te reo Māori, me te taiao o te kōhanga reo.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā:

  • kia whakarite i te tāutu, te whakatakoto ā-tuhi, me te pūrongo i te ahu whakamua o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Paa Harakeke ki Māngere, ki tua atu i te marae o Te Puea. He kaha te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou iwi, tō rātou kōhanga reo, ā, ki a rātou anō hoki. He pai te mahi ngātahi a te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga ki te kōhanga reo. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e hāpai ana, e manaaki ana hoki i a rātou.

Findings

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pa Harakeke is in Mangere adjacent to Te Puea Marae. Children have a strong sense of belonging to their iwi, kōhanga reo and each other. Whānau and kaimahi work well together as they manage kōhanga reo operations. Children are happy and relaxed in a supportive and caring environment.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Paa Harakeke will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Paa Harakeke is directly opposite Te Puea Marae in Mangere, South Auckland. Children are nurtured in Tainui tikanga and kawa. Whānau and kaimahi are dedicated to providing a rich and stimulating learning environment for all children including those with special learning needs.

Ko Tainui te waka Ko Te Manukanuka a Hoturoa te moana Ko Te Ara Pueru te maunga Ko Te Puea te marae.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children connect to each other and their iwi?

Children have a strong sense of belonging to their iwi, kōhanga reo and each other.

Mana Whenua and Mana Reo

Children respect their environment. They care for their personal belongings and kōhanga reo resources. Kaimahi and whānau model personal care and responsibility for children. They provide nutritional meals and promote healthy eating habits. Daily routines such as health and wellbeing are promoted. Children are developing independence.

Children learn about their role as tangata whenua. They observe kuia and kaumātua as kaikaranga, kaikōrero and kaiwaiata during powhiri and formal events at the marae. These roles are practised at the kōhanga reo and enable children to take an active role in tikanga and kawa. Children are welcoming towards manuwhiri and show manaakitanga and aroha.

Children are proud to be Tainui. Children eagerly share their whānau, hapū and iwi links through pepeha, mōteatea, karakia, waiata and hīmene. They acknowledge the Kingitanga and participate in celebrations including poukai, koroneihana, and matariki. Children lead and participate in Pai Marire karakia. Children have a strong sense of belonging.

Children understand and use te reo Māori. Kaimahi and whānau use various language strategies. Kaimahi engage in conversations with children. Children understand and follow instructions and use simple phrases and gestures to convey their needs and thoughts. The older children confidently lead daily routines. Children confidently communicate in te reo Māori.

Children experience warm and loving relationships. Kaimahi are enthusiastic and energetic. They model aroha and manaakitanga when they interact with children. Kaimahi regularly praise children’s efforts and respond to their physical and emotional needs. Tuakana are positive role models for teina and are encouraged to assist them with routines, te reo Māori and activities. Children play cooperatively and are affectionate with kaimahi.

Key Next Steps

The lack of learning centres and resources limits children’s learning. There are insufficient resources to stimulate problem solving, exploration, creativity and individual children’s interests. The kaiako has identified this as a priority.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau manage the kōhanga reo operations well. They have made progress in several key areas including appointing new staff, restoring relationships, building roll numbers and settling the children. The kōhanga reo is managed in the best interests of children.

Whānau have begun to identify new strategic goals which include kaimahi training, engaging whānau and providing opportunities to support whānau. They meet regularly to discuss, monitor and report on different aspects of the kōhanga reo operations. They are committed to managing the kōhanga reo with children’s wellbeing and education as the focus.

Children benefit from clear programme planning processes. Long and short term plans provide clear detail to guide kaimahi in their work with children. Learning kaupapa include te ao Māori, iwi knowledge and values as well as investigating the wider world. Children are challenged to learn in a range of interesting and relevant contexts.

Key Next Steps

Assessment practices and programme evaluation are in the early stages of development. Although kaimahi are familiar with different assessment tools, they have not been used consistently. This makes it difficult to determine what progress children have made. Kaimahi meetings tend to be informal and irregular. Kaimahi do not evaluate the learning programme, resources, te reo Maori or the physical environment.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • ensure that children's learning progress is clearly identified, recorded and reported.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pa Harakeke is in Mangere adjacent to Te Puea Marae. Children have a strong sense of belonging to their iwi, kōhanga reo and each other. Whānau and kaimahi work well together as they manage kōhanga reo operations. Children are happy and relaxed in a supportive and caring environment.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Paa Harakeke will be in three years.
