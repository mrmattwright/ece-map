ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Opotiki Community Childcare Centre situated on the Opotiki College site, provides full-day education and care for children from birth to school age. This not-for-profit centre is governed by a committee consisting of teachers and parents. The centre is licensed for 55 children including 15 aged up to two years. Its roll of 62, includes 32 children of MƒÅori descent.

The centre operates two age-based areas, one for children under two and an over two area. For an hour each morning children in the over two area are separated into two groups to provide more specific age-related learning programmes.

The centre‚Äôs recently reviewed philosophy makes a commitment to providing a warm, caring, open and fun learning environment. It acknowledges the importance of working in close partnership with whƒÅnau.

Since the 2012 ERO review, leadership of the centre has remained the same and there have only been two changes in the teaching team. Teachers have participated in externally provided professional development to strengthen their understanding of te reo and tikanga MƒÅori. There has been considerable development to the centre‚Äôs facilities, including the establishment of a separate indoor area for children under the age of two. Centre leaders and teachers acknowledge the need to further develop the outdoor play areas to provide a more natural environment and increased space for children to explore and develop their physical skills.

An after-school care and school holiday programme for local primary school children continues to operate at the centre.

Opotiki Community Childcare Centre has a positive ERO reporting history.

The Review Findings

The governance committee, consisting of experienced members, provides effective centre governance. The committee is well informed about centre operations and has developed a useful plan for ongoing centre development. The centre‚Äôs finances are well managed. Committee members are very supportive of the centre manager and teachers, and generously fund them for their ongoing professional learning and development.

The centre continues to benefit from the leadership of an experienced and knowledgeable centre manager and assistant manager. They have clear expectations for centre operations and effective teaching practices. The centre manager leads useful self-review processes that contribute to ongoing centre development. Teachers value the supportive professional leadership provided by the centre manager and assistant manager.

The programme is a combination of teacher-led and child-initiated activities. Literacy and mathematics learning experiences are a feature of teacher-led sessions. Particular strengths of the programme include:

  • the meaningful inclusion of te reo and tikanga MƒÅori into all aspects of the centre
  • frequent trips into the local and wider community
  • opportunities for children to experience safe physical challenge
  • a focus on science learning
  • many opportunities for whƒÅnau and local community members to share their knowledge and expertise with children.

Individual learning profiles provide a comprehensive record of each child‚Äôs participation in the programme. They are easily accessible at the centre for children to revisit their learning. A recent initiative has been to make these accessible to whƒÅnau in an on-line format.

Teachers work well together in the best interests of children. They have established positive and affirming relationships with children and their whƒÅnau and are committed to working in partnership with whƒÅnau for the benefit of children. ERO observed teachers actively participating alongside children supporting sustained play. Children‚Äôs care needs are well met and teachers are encouraging children to develop self-care skills. Well-managed processes have been developed that support children as they transition between the age-based rooms and when they move on to school.

Children up to the age of two benefit from caring and nurturing relationships with their teachers. A particular feature of the programme in the under-two area is the incorporation of waiata and music activities. Teachers support parents care preferences and routines for their babies and toddlers.

Key Next Steps

The key next steps for the centre are to

  • access centre-wide teacher professional development to strengthen assessment, planning and evaluation processes. This should support teachers to more effectively notice, recognise and respond to children‚Äôs learning
  • review the balance between teacher-led and child-initiated activities to support teachers to provide a programme that is more responsive to children‚Äôs interests
  • review children‚Äôs access to a wide range of learning resources and equipment.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Opotiki Community Childcare Centre completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Next ERO Review

When is ERO likely to review the service again?

The next ERO review of Opotiki Community Childcare Centre will be in three years.
