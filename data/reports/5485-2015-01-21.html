1 Evaluation of Edna McCulloch Kindergarten

Requires further development

Well placed

Very well placed

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Edna McCulloch Kindergarten is one of four kindergartens governed by the Oamaru Kindergarten Association (OKA). It is open during school day hours.

Since the last review in May 2010, there has been significant change at the kindergarten. There is a new teaching team and head teacher. The head teacher had spent one term in her new role at the time of this review. She is establishing the team culture while reviewing systems, practices and aspects of the environment and programme.

The kindergarten‚Äôs philosophy is based firmly on respect for others and the environment. This is strongly evident in practice and deliberately taught.

The kindergarten has large indoor and outdoor environments. These are well used during the daily programme.

The Review Findings

Governance and management

The kindergarten is very well supported by the Oamaru Kindergarten Association (OKA). The board has a positive relationship with the general manager who effectively and efficiently manages the kindergarten. They have developed a useful strategic plan which is about to be reviewed to ensure further alignment with changing priorities.

Since ERO‚Äôs last review of the other three kindergartens and the OKA (earlier in 2014), the board and leaders have begun implementing plans for improvements identified at the time. These include a review of communication systems, and attending a workshop to build understanding of the principles of The Treaty of Waitangi and how they apply within the organisation. The general manager and education services manager (ESM) have also begun professional development for the OKA head teachers around leadership and self review.

The education services manager (ESM) travels from Christchurch to visit the kindergarten. She and the general manager support the kindergarten to implement the high expectations they have for children‚Äôs learning and wellbeing. The ESM provides valuable guidance to the newly established teaching team at Edna McCulloch. Head teachers from the other association kindergartens also provide helpful support for the new head teacher as she becomes familiar with the service and its systems.

The OKA should consider whether additional, more accessible input from the ESM or other leaders within the OKA is needed to support the teachers at the kindergarten. This would ensure current improvements are maintained and developed in teachers‚Äô daily practice.

Centre practice

The kindergarten environment is welcoming and relaxed. The large play area offers huge potential for children‚Äôs learning. The teachers support children‚Äôs wellbeing and encourage a sense of whƒÅnau and belonging. ERO observed positive, supportive relationships between adults and children. Some children have developed strong friendships. They learn and play well together. Parents are comfortable to stay and join in the programme with their children.

The teachers are beginning to form a cohesive team. They are becoming familiar with one another‚Äôs strengths and skills. The new head teacher is focused on building positive relationships. She is establishing useful centre-wide systems, routines and expectations for teaching practice and for children‚Äôs behaviour.

There have been significant improvements in developing the environment and providing learning opportunities for children within the programme. Children have more choice and better access to resources. A range of activities provide regular opportunities for children to explore, discover and create. There has been some intentional resourcing of equipment to support learning and teaching in mathematics. Group times provide good opportunities for teachers to recognise children‚Äôs interests and capabilities.

The kindergarten is using some useful self-review processes and practices. These are building teachers‚Äô capability and improving outcomes for children.

Key Next Steps

The head teacher and her team are making good progress in identifying strengths and next steps for development. It is now time to consolidate and embed the changes and develop ways to maintain and build on the improvements made. This is more likely to occur with regular ongoing guidance and support.

The team‚Äôs next steps include:

  • further developing assessment, programme planning and evaluation
  • building teachers understanding of children‚Äôs working theories
  • embedding self review and widening the scope of reviews to include the quality of interactions and the integration of MƒÅori perspectives in programmes and practices.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Edna McCulloch Kindergarten completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum

  • premises and facilities

  • health and safety practices

  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)

  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)

  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)

  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

When is ERO likely to review the service again?

The next ERO review of Edna McCulloch Kindergarten will be in three years.
