1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Toroa ki te marae o Pupuaruhe, e pā tata atu ana ki te tāone o Whakatāne. Ko Ngāti Awa te iwi. He tino hononga tō te hapū o Patuwai ki te moutere o Mōtītī.

Ka ūhia ngā tamariki ki te aroha me te whakautetanga. Ka whakanuia, ā, ka whakamihia hoki ā rātou mahi angitū. Ka ako ngā tamariki mā te mahi ā-tinana, me te whai wāhi atu ki ngā tikanga o te marae, ngā pōwhiri, me te manaaki manuhiri. E whakapūmau ana te marae i te hononga o ngā tamariki ki ngā tikanga tuku iho. Kua roa nei tētahi kuia e whai wāhi ana ki te kōhanga reo, ā, he nui āna mahi ki te whakapūmau i ngā tikanga me ngā kawa o Ngāti Awa ki te kōhanga reo. Ka ūhia ngā tamariki ki te aroha me te whakaute hoki, ā, nā wai rā, ka pērā hoki rātou ki ētahi atu.

Kua pā ki te whānau te maha o ngā take hononga ki te kōhanga reo i roto i ngā tau e rua kua pahure ake nei, ā, kua pā tōraro tēnei ki te āta whakarite i te whai huatanga o te whakamahere me te whakahaere ki te kōhanga reo i roto i ētahi wāhanga. Kua tautoko mai Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i te whānau, ā, kua whakamātau ki te whakakotahi anō i ngā wehenga ki te whānau, ā, kei te ahu whakamua ētahi āhuatanga i tēnei wā. Ahakoa he maha tonu ngā tino take hei whakatutukitanga mā te whānau, kua āta whakaritea kia kore ai aua take e whai pānga ki te whakaratonga o tētahi taiao akoranga haumaru, pai hoki mō ngā tamariki. Me āta whakarite te whānau kia wawe ai te whakatutukitanga o ngā take e pā ana ki ngā hononga i waenga i ngā pākeke.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake, ko:

  • Mana Atua.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Atua

He whakamārama

He pēhea rawa te pai o ngā tamariki ki te whakaatu i ngā uaratanga o te aroha, te manaakitanga, te atawhai, me te āwhina?

He pai ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki, ā, he mauritau hoki ngā hononga e tohu ana i te aroha, te manaaki, te atawhai, me te āwhina.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i tētahi taiao akoranga e mau nei i te aroha me te whakaute mō rātou. Ka riro i a rātou i ngā kōrero whakamihi e whakakoia ana i ā rātou mahi angitu. He tākare, he ngākau nui hoki te āhua o ngā taunekeneke a ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki. Ka whakarite rātou kia ū tonu ai te ngākau nui o ngā tamariki i roto i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i tētahi taiao akoranga e akiaki ana i a rātou ki te tū hei ākonga mātātoa.

Ka kitea te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te whanaungatanga, i roto i tā rātou taunekeneke ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ētahi atu hoki. He pakari tā rātou whai wāhi ki pōwhiri me te manaaki manuhiri. Kua roa nei tētahi kuia e whai wāhi ana ki te kōhanga reo, ā, he nui āna mahi ki te whakapūmau i ngā tikanga me ngā kawa o Ngāti Awa ki te kōhanga reo. Ka kitea te āhua o te manaakitanga me te whakaute hoki a ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu.

E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi ako ā-tinana e hāpai ana i te tūhura me te whakawero hinengaro. E whakapūmau ana te marae i te hononga o ngā tamariki ki ngā taonga tuku iho. Ka whakaongaongatia ā-tinana, ā-hinengaro hoki ngā tamariki nā ngā taiao o roto, o waho hoki. He ākonga māia, he ākonga pakari hoki ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Kei te whānau ngā tukanga ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o tō rātou kōhanga reo. He āhua rangatahi tonu te whānau, ā, he tākare, he hiahia nui hoki tō rātou ki te ako. He ngākau nuitanga, he whakapūmautanga hoki tō rātou ki te āta whakarite i te kōhanga reo hei tino wāhi noho mō ā rātou tamariki.

E mārama pai ana ki ngā kaimahi ā rātou tūranga, me ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki. He pai tā rātou mahi ngātahi ki te whakariterite i te tino taiao ako papai mō ngā tamariki. Ka whai hua rātou nā te noho pūmau o ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki, ki te kaupapa hoki o te kōhanga reo.

Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi te tika o ngā mahi e pā ana ki te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai. Ka whakamahere ngātahi ngā kaimahi i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka āta kitea ki ngā tukanga aromatawai te mōhiotanga o ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki. Ka whai pānga te aromātai hōtaka ki ngā whakapaitanga i te hōtaka akoranga. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki tētahi hōtaka akoranga he pai te whakarite, te whakahaere hoki.

Kei te tūtohinga me ngā kaupapa here ngā aratohu mārama e pā ana ki ngā whakaritenga. Kua whakamaraetia ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga o te kōhanga reo. E āta whakarite ana tēnei i te kōhanga reo hei taiao haumaru, hei taiao hauora anō hoki mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Me poipoi te whanaungatanga ki waenga i te katoa o te whānau. He taumaha ngā hononga i tēnei wā. Hei whakarite i te tino taiao e tika ana mō ngā tamariki, me mahi ngātahi te whānau hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

He mea nui kia noho mārama te whānau ki ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga. Me whai whakaaro rātou ki te āta whakarite i te tuhi, te arotake, me te aroturuki i ia te wā i ā rātou kaupapa matua ka whakawhanakehia ā meāke nei.

Ahakoa e whakatutuki ana ngā kaimahi i ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga i roto i te hōtaka akoranga, me aro tonu ki ngā whakaritenga mahi. Ko ngā kirimana tuku mahi, ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi, me ngā arohaehae, me arotake. Ka whakatairanga tēnei i ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaeretanga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora, me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia tautoko Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i te whānau ki te:

  • whakawhanake i tētahi mahere rautaki mō te whakawhanaketanga o te kōhanga reo mō meāke nei
  • whakapakari i te whanaungatanga ki waenga i ngā mema katoa o te whānau, hei painga mō ngā tamariki
  • arotake, te whakahou ake hoki i ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi, hei whakatairanga i ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e rua.

Lynda Pura Watson

Kaiurungi Whakatūruki Arotake Māori Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Toroa is located on Pupuaruhe marae, near the township of Whakatane. The iwi is Ngāti Awa. The hapū Patuwai, has strong ties to the island of Motiti.

Children are treated with love and respect. Their successes are recognised and acknowledged positively. Children learn through doing and participate in marae process, powhiri and manaaki manuhiri. The marae offers a place where children remain connected to their traditions. A long serving kuia makes significant contributions to maintaining tikanga and kawa of Ngāti Awa in the kōhanga reo. Children are treated and treat others with care and respect.

The whānau have experienced a number of internal relationship issues over the past 2 years which has detracted from ensuring effective planning and management of the kōhanga in certain areas. The whānau have received support from the kōhanga reo national trust in an attempt to unite factions within the whānau, this has had some success to date. Even though there are a number of significant issues that the whānau need to deal with, they have ensured that there has been no impact on the provision of a safe and effective learning environment for children. The whānau must ensure adult relationship issues are dealt with promptly.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Atua.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Mana Atua

Background

How effectively do children display the values of aroha, manaakitanga, atawhai and āwhina?

Children effectively interact with others and show aroha, manaaki, atawhai and āwhina

Areas of good performance

Children experience a learning environment where they are treated with love and respect. They receive positive praise that affirms their successes. Kaimahi are enthusiastic and passionate in their interactions with children. They ensure the learning programme maintains children’s interests. Children experience a learning environment where they encouraged to be active learners.

Children demonstrate an understanding of whanaungatanga in the way they interact with one another and others. They participate in processes of pohiri and manaaki manuhiri with confidence. A long serving kuia makes significant contributions to maintaining tikanga and kawa of Ngāti Awa in the kōhanga reo. Children treat others with care and respect

Children have access to a range of physical learning resources that provide opportunities for exploration and challenge. The marae offers a place where children remain connected to their traditions. The indoor and outdoor environments enable children to be stimulated both physically and mentally. Children are confident and capable learners

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

Whānau have in place processes to manage the operations and management of their kōhanga reo. They are a relatively young whānau who are keen and willing to learn. They have a passion and a commitment to ensure that the kōhanga reo is the best possible place for their children.

Kaimahi clearly understand their roles and responsibilities. They work well together to create the best possible learning environment for the children. They benefit from the commitment kaimahi have to children and the kaupapa of the kōhanga reo

Kaimahi have established appropriate practices around planning, assessment and evaluation. Kaimahi collectively plan for children’s learning. Assessment processes reflect how well the kaimahi know children. Programme evaluation lead to improvements to the learning programme. Children enjoy a learning programme that is well organised and managed.

The charter and policies provide clear guidelines for practice. Policies and procedures are in place. This ensures that the kōhanga reo is a safe and healthy environment for children.

Areas for development

Whanaungatanga amongst all whānau members need to be nurtured. Existing relationships are strained. In order to create the best possible place for children, the whānau need to work together in the best interest of the children.

It is important that whānau are clear about their roles and responsibilities. They should consider ways to ensure their priorities for future development are documented, reviewed and regularly monitored.

While kaimahi are carrying out their roles and responsibilities in terms of the learning programme, employment practices need attention. Employment contracts, job descriptions and appraisals need to be reviewed. This will enhance kaimahi practice.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends the National Te Kōhanga Reo Trust provide support for whānau to:

  • develop a strategic plan for the future development of the kōhanga reo
  • strengthen whanaungatanga amongst all whānau members to benefit the children
  • review and update personnel documentation to enhance kaimahi practice.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in two years.
