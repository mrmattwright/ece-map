Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Apanui ki Whakatāne ki te tāone o Whakatāne. Ka kitea te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te whanaungatanga i roto i ā rātou akoranga. E ako ana ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti kōrero mā te reo Māori. He tau rātou ki tō rātou kōhanga reo. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā whai wāhitanga ki te tūhura i te hapori me te whai wāhi atu ki āna kaupapa. He harikoa, he mauritau hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Apanui ki Whakatāne i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Apanui ki Whakatāne ki te tāone o Whakatāne. He hononga ā-whakapapa tō te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki Te Whānau-a-Apanui me Ngāti Awa. He tokomaha ngā mema o te whānau e whai wāhi nui ana ki ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ko te reo Māori te aronga a ngā tamariki me te whānau.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i te whanaungatanga i roto i ā rātou akoranga?

Ka kitea te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te whanaungatanga i roto i ā rātou akoranga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E ako ana ngā tamariki ki te āta taunekeneke ki ētahi atu. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā uaratanga o te manaaki me te tautoko hei whakatairanga ake i te whanonga pai. Nā ngā taumahi ako ka whakapuaki ngā tamariki i ō rātou mōhiotanga ki ngā atua Māori ki te taha o ētahi atu. Ka whakamahia ngā karakia me ngā waiata hei whakapakari i te whanaungatanga ki te hōtaka o ia rā. He tau ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka arotahi te ako a ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanaketanga o te aronga toi whenuatanga. Ka whai pānga matua ngā whakapapa me ngā pepehā ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Nā ngā taumahi ka ako ngā tamariki i ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu, ki te whenua hoki. Ko ngā wheako o ngā tamariki e hāpai ana i a rātou ki te whakatupu me te hauhake kai, me te haere ki ngā tino pou whenua o ngā iwi. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou mōhiotanga ki tō rātou tūnga ki te kōhanga reo me te hapori whānui. He mauritau ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo me te hapori.

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki te mahi ngātahi ki ētahi atu. Ka whakanuia te mahi tahi ki ā rātou taunekeneke o ia rā. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te ako hei tuakana, hei teina, mā ngā huarahi e poipoi ana i a rātou. Nā runga i te āhua o ngā taumahi, ka tohatoha, ka tuku wā hoki ngā tamariki i ā rātou e tākaro ngātahi ana, e ako ngātahi ana hoki. Ka whakatakoto ngā mahinga i tētahi tūāpapa mō ngā tamariki ki te whakatinana i ō rātou pūkenga ki te ārahi. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te whakawhanaungatanga.

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te tūmāia ki te whakawhiti whakaaro mā te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki reo ki te whakatairanga ake i te reo Māori, ā, ka whai pūtake hoki aua rautaki. Ka hāpai rātou i ngā tamariki ki te taki i ō rātou whakapapa, ō rātou pepehā, me te whakamahi anō hoki i ngā rerenga kōrero māmā. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā waiata hei rautaki ki te whakapūmau i te reo Māori mā ngā huarahi ngahau. He tākare tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki ngā taumahi reo, pērā i te tāruarua, te manawataki, me te korikori tinana. Kei te pakari haere te māia o ngā tamariki ki te ako mā te reo Māori.

E ako ana ngā tamariki ki te whakamana i ō rātou taiao tūturu, māoriori hoki. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te taiao o te kōhanga reo, i tua atu hoki. Ko te wāhi whānui ake o waho e whakawātea ana i ngā tamariki ki te tūhura, te hīraurau hopanga, me te whakahura i ngā taonga māoriori. Ka piki ake te pākiki o ngā tamariki i ngā haerenga, i a rātou e whakarongo ana ki ngā kōrero tuku iho o te wāhi nei, ā, e haere ana hoki ki ngā wāhi whakahirahira. He ākonga pākiki ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore ngā tamariki tuākana e āta tautokona ana kia whānui ake anō ai ā rātou taumata reo Māori. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nei, nā te whai wāhi atu ki te hōhonutanga ake o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki te taha o ngā kaimahi. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te ako.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā tētahi hōtaka i āta whakamaheretia ai. Ka whai ngā kaimahi i tētahi tukanga ka ārahi, ka hāpai hoki i te whakamahere me te whakapuakitanga o ngā hōtaka akoranga. Ka hono ngā mahere ki Te Whāriki, ā, ka whakarato ngā taumahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga ki ngā tamariki, mō roto tonu i te kōhanga reo, ā, ki tua atu hoki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako.

Ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā whakataunga whakahaere a te whānau. Ka ārahi te tūtohinga i ngā pūnaha whakahaere, ā, ka arotahi ēnei ki te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki ngā whakahaeretia o te kōhanga reo i ia rā. He pai te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi, ā, ka tautoko te whānau i tēnei āhuatanga i ia te wā. Ka whakamahi te whānau i Te Ara Tūāpae hei tāutu, hei whakamahere hoki i ā rātou tūmanako, ā rātou whāinga hoki mō te pae tata me te paetawhiti. Ko tā te whānau aronga, ko te whakapai ake i ngā painga mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki, tā rātou ahu whakamua hoki e āta aroturukitia ana. Kei te tūtohinga te tino aronga mō te aroturuki i ngā tūmanako, engari kāhore ngā tukanga aroturuki e āta whakahaerehia ana. Ka hāpai ngā mahi whakangungu a Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i te whakapaitanga ake a ngā kaimahi i ā rātou whakaritenga.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakapai ake ngā kaimahi i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aromatawai me te aromātai
  • whakawhanake ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki ka whakapakari ake i te reo Māori o ngā tamariki
  • whakawhanake te whānau i tētahi mahere ā-tau ka aroturuki, ka pūrongo hoki i te ahunga whakamua, i runga anō i te āhua o ā rātou whāinga rautaki.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Apanui ki Whakatāne is located in Whakatāne. Children demonstrate an understanding of whanaungatanga in their learning. Children are learning to communicate in te reo Māori. They are comfortable in their kōhanga reo environment. Children enjoy opportunities to explore the local environment and engage in community activities. Children are happy and settled at the kōhanga reo.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Apanui ki Whakatāne will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Apanui ki Whakatāne is located in Whakatāne. The whānau and kaimahi share whakapapa to Apanui and Ngāti Awa. Many whānau are involved in the kōhanga operations. Te reo Māori is a focus for children and whānau.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children demonstrate whanaungatanga in their learning?

Children demonstrate an understanding of whanaungatanga in their learning.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are learning to interact positively with others. Kaimahi use the values of care and support to promote positive behaviours. Learning activities allow children to share their knowledge of Atua Māori with others. Karakia and waiata are used to strengthen whanaungatanga in the daily programme. Children are settled in the kōhanga reo.

Children’s learning is focused on developing a sense of belonging. Whakapapa and pepehā is an integral part of children’s learning. Activities allow children to learn about their connections to others and the land. Children’s experiences support them to grow and harvest kai, and visit significant iwi landmarks. Kaimahi encourage children to share knowledge about their place in the kōhanga reo and wider community. Children are comfortable in kōhanga reo and local community.

Children learn to work co-operatively with others. Mahi tahi is emphasised in their daily interactions. Kaimahi provide opportunities for children to learn as tuakana-teina in supportive ways. Activities allow children to share and take turns as they play and learn together. Routines provide children with the platform to demonstrate their leadership ability. Children are developing a sense of whanaungatanga.

Children are developing confidence to communicate in te reo Māori. Kaimahi use a variety of language strategies to promote te reo Māori in meaningful ways. They support children to recite their whakapapa, pepehā and to use simple language structures. Kaimahi use waiata as a strategy to reinforce te reo Māori in a fun way. They eagerly participate in language activities that include repetition, rhythm and physical activity. Children learning te reo Māori with growing confidence.

Children are learning to value their physical and natural environments. Kaimahi encourage children to explore their kōhanga reo environment and beyond. The wider outdoor area offers children many opportunities to explore, problem solve and discover the wonders of the natural world. Excursions heighten children’s curiosity as they listen to local stories and visit places of interest. Children are curious learners.

Key Next Steps

Older children are not well supported to extend their levels of te reo Māori. These children would benefit from engaging in more meaningful conversations with kaimahi. Children are capable learners.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s benefit from a well-planned programme. Kaimahi follow a process to guide and support the planning and delivery of learning programmes. Planning is linked to Te Whāriki and activities provide children with a range of learning experiences at and beyond the kōhanga reo. Children enjoy learning.

Children are at the heart of whānau management decisions. The charter guides management systems that focus on children’s health and safety. Whānau actively participate and contribute to the day-to-day running of the kōhanga reo. Personnel management practices well managed with the ongoing support of the whanau. Whānau use Te Ara Tūāpae to identify and plan their short and long term objectives and goals. Whānau focus is on improving outcomes for children.

Key Next Steps

Children’s development and progress is not well monitored. The tūtohinga provides clear direction for monitoring expectations, however, monitoring processes are not well managed. TKRNT training will support kaimahi to improve their practices.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation(s):

  • Kaimahi to improve assessment and evaluation practices
  • Kaimahi to develop strategies to strengthen children’s te reo Māori
  • Whānau to develop an annual plan to monitor and report progress against their strategic objectives.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Apanui ki Whakatāne is located in Whakatāne. Children demonstrate an understanding of whanaungatanga in their learning. Children are learning to communicate in te reo Māori. They are comfortable in their kōhanga reo environment. Children enjoy opportunities to explore the local environment and engage in community activities. Children are happy and settled at the kōhanga reo.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Apanui ki Whakatāne will be in three years.
