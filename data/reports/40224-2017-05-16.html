1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Uru Karaka ki ngā papa o Te Kunenga ki Pūrehuroa, ki Te Papaioea. E poipoi ana te kōhanga reo i ngā tamariki a ngā ākonga me ngā kaimahi o te whare wānanga, me ētahi atu whānau hoki o te ope hōia.

Whai muri i te arotake o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, he tino nui te rerekē haere o ngā whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te kōhanga reo. Kāhore he kaiako e mau ana i te Tohu Whakapakari ki te kōhanga reo. I tēnei wā, kua iti rawa ake te tatauranga tamariki o te kōhanga reo i te haurua o te tatauranga ki te raihana.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakamahi i te reo me ngā tikanga Māori?

He pakari te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori, ā, ka whakatinana rātou i ngā tikanga Māori, puta noa i te rā.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E whakawhanake ana te tūmāia o ngā tamariki ki tētahi wāhi reo Māori. Whakamihia ai, whakanuia ai hoki ngā tamariki i ia te wā mō tā rātou whakamahinga i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahia e ngā kaimahi te reo Māori mā ngā huarahi poipoi e whakapakari ana i te tūmāia o ngā tamariki hei ākonga. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi e totoro ana, e whakawhānui ana hoki i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā tamariki i te reo Māori puta noa i te rā.

E rongo ana ngā tamariki i ngā hononga papai ki te taha o ētahi atu. He maha ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te mahi takitahi ki te taha o tētahi kaimahi. Ka aro atu ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. He maha ngā wā ka rangona te pārekareka o te noho tahi o ngā tamariki. He harikoa ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka tūhura ngā tamariki i tō rātou taiao mā ngā huarahi auaha e whai pūtake ana. Ka poipoi ngā kaimahi i te tū takitahi, ā, ka akiaki rātou i te tūhura haere a ngā tamariki. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te nukunuku haere ki ngā tūmomo wāhi ako. Ka ako ngā tamariki i tō rātou tuakiri, i tō rātou tūnga anō hoki ki te ao Māori, me tō rātou whai wāhi nui ki te whānau.

He pai ki ngā tamariki te whakapuaki i ō rātou mōhiotanga ki ngā tikanga me te ao Māori. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i te kōkiri me te taki a ia tamaiti i ngā karakia, ngā whakatau, ngā waiata, me ngā taumahi ako a te katoa. Ka ako rātou i ngā atua Māori me tō rātou tūnga hei tangata whenua ki te kōhanga reo. Whakanuia ai ngā tamariki i ia te wā mō ā rātou whakamātau me ā rātou whakatutukitanga. Ka tūmāia ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore i te rawaka / rahi ngā tūmomo rauemi o roto, hei hāpai i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga. Kua tāutuhia e ngā kaimahi, koia nei tētahi āhuatanga nui mō ngā tamariki, ā, e pūrongo tonuhia ana e rātou te take nei ki te whānau. Kāhore e āta whakatairangatia ana te ako me te tūhura a ngā tamariki katoa i te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

E whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i ngā wheako akoranga ā-kaupapa e whai pūtake ana, ā, i āta whakamaheretia ai hoki. E whai hua ana te whakamahere a ngā kaimahi i ngā taumahi ako e whai pānga ana ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo ngākau nuitanga me ngā tūmomo āheinga o ngā tamariki. He aronga nui ki te whakawhanake i te reo Māori mā ngā huarahi māoriori e whai pūtake ana. Whakapūmau ai ngā kaimahi i te tū o ngā hui i ia te wā, ā, ka tātari, ka tuhi haere hoki rātou i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka ki te hāpai i ngā tamariki katoa.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore te kōhanga reo e āta whakahaerehia ana. Ko ētahi o ngā wero ki te whakahaeretanga, ko te whakatinanatanga o tētahi tūtohinga mārama me te hāngaitanga o ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga o roto ki te wā, me te whakatakotoranga anō hoki i te whakahaere me te arohaehae a ngā kaimahi, te tiaki i ngā papa, me te whakamahere, te aroturuki anō hoki i te pūtea o ia tau. Ahakoa i toro atu te whānau ki te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo whai muri i te arotake o mua, kīhai i ū ki taua tautoko. He take tēnei hei tirohanga wawe mā te whānau. Kāhore ngā tamariki e whiwhi ana i ngā hua ka puta i te whakahaeretanga pai o te kōhanga reo.

Kāhore e āta whakatakotohia ana te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki i roto i Te Whāriki, ā, kāhore hoki tēnei e tino pūrongotia ana mā ngā mātua. Kāhore anō ngā pūkete kia āta whakaritea hei tohu i ngā wheako akoranga reo a ngā tamariki, me te tupu o ia tamaiti, tae atu ki te reo me ngā tikanga Māori.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

Ahakoa ngā wero, e ai ki te whānau kei te noho pūmau rātou ki tō rātou kōhanga reo. E taunaki ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia āwhina mai te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i te whānau ki te whakawhanake i tētahi mahere ka whakapakari ake i te whakahaeretanga kia whai hua ai, kia toitū ai.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Uru Karaka i roto ngā tau e rua.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

16 Haratua, 2017

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Uru Karaka is on Massey University Campus in Palmerston North. The kōhanga reo caters for children of students and staff at the campus and some whānau from the local army base.

Since the previous ERO review, turnover of whānau and staff has been significant. There is no kaiako on site who holds the Tohu Whakapakari. The kōhanga reo is currently operating well below half its licensed enrolment capacity.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children use te reo and tikanga Māori?

Children communicate confidently in te reo Māori and practice tikanga Māori throughout the day.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are developing as confidence in a te reo Māori environment. Children are frequently praised and acknowledged for their use of te reo Māori. Te reo Māori is used by kaimahi in nurturing ways to build children’s confidence as learners. Children are engaged in a variety of activities that extends and broadens their knowledge of te reo Māori. Children use te reo Māori throughout the day.

Children experience positive relationships with others. There are many opportunities for children to work individually with kaimahi. Kaimahi respond to the needs and interests of children. Children are frequently heard enjoying each other’s company. Children are happy at kōhanga reo.

Children explore their environment in creative and meaningful ways. Kaimahi foster independence and encourage children to explore. Children move with confidence between the different learning areas. Children learn about who they are, their place in te ao Māori and their connection with whānau.

Children like to share what they know about tikanga and te ao Māori. Kaimahi encourage individuals to initiate and lead karakia, whakatau, waiata and shared learning activities. They learn about atua Māori and their role as tangata whenua in their kōhanga reo. Children are regularly praised for their efforts and achievements. Children are confident in te ao Māori.

Key Next Steps

Children do not have sufficient indoor resources to support their interests. Kaimahi have identified this as a high need for children and continue to report this to whānau. Te reo Māori learning and exploration for all children is not maximised.

Planning and Evaluation

Children receive meaningful and well planned kaupapa learning experiences. Kaimahi effectively plan activities of learning that reflect the diverse interests and abilities of children. There is a strong emphasis on developing te reo Māori in natural and meaningful ways. Kaimahi maintain regular hui where they examine and record how well the programme supports all children.

Key Next Steps

The kōhanga reo is not well managed. Management challenges include the implementation of a clear charter with up to date policies and procedures, personnel management and appraisal, property maintenance and annual budget planning and monitoring. While the whānau has sought support from TKRNT since the previous review, this support has not been sustained. This is a matter of urgency for the whānau. Children do not benefit from effective kōhanga reo management.

Children’s language development across Te Whāriki is not well recorded and reported to parents. Portfolios are yet to be well established to show children’s language learning experiences and their individual growth, including te reo and tikanga Māori.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

Despite the challenges whānau report are committed to their kōhanga reo. ERO recommends Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust assist whānau to develop a plan for building effective and sustainable management. 

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Uru Karaka will be within two years.
