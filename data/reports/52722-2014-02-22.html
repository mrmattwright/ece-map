1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana te kōhanga reo o Te Wai o Turi ki te marae, ā, e tino mau ana te kōhanga reo i ngā tirohanga ataahua o ngā pamū o te taiwhenua me te moana o Te Tai o Rehua. Ka ahu mai te ingoa o Te Wai o Turi i te puna wai i kimihia e te tūpuna a Turi. E tū ana te puna wai ki te tihi o te pari, ā, kei tēnei wāhi te marae o Te Wai o Turi e tū ana i ēnei rā.

Ko Te Māhuri o Te Puna Mahara te ingoa o te wāhi e tū nei te kōhanga reo. E hāngai ana Te Māhuri ki te whakatipuranga o te tamaiti. E whakapono ana te whānau mēnā kei te ako ngā tamariki i ngā uaratanga me ngā mātāpono o Te Wai o Turi, ka puāwai ngā moemoeā o te whānau, ā, e taea ana e ngā tamariki te whai koha ki tēnei ao. Ko Te Puna Mahara te ngākau hiahia o te whānau ki te whakahaere i tētahi mātauranga kounga nui mā ā rātou tamariki, ā, kia wehe rātou i Te Wai o Turi ka mōhio ngā tamariki ko wai rātou, ā, nō hea rātou.

I kōwhiritia e te whānau te Mana Whenua hei kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E ora pai ana ngā whanaungatanga i waenganui i te marae, te whānau me ngā kaimahi, ā, e tautoko ana ēnei whanaungatanga katoa i te kaha whakapiripiri a ngā tamariki ki te whenua me te whānau, ō rātou tuakiri me ō rātou whaiora. Ko te whanaungatanga tētahi tūāpapa pakari hei ako i ngā āhuatanga o Wai o Uritanga. Tērā tētahi kaute kaimahi e taurite ana i waenganui i ngā kaiako, arā, ko ngā kaiako kua whakaako mō te wā roa me ngā kaimahi he taiohi tonu te pakeke, ā, he tiaki me te tautoko i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki ā rātou mahi. Ko Rangiharuru tētahi rauemi whai take mā ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki hei ako i te whakapapa o tēnā me tēnā me te hītori o te rohe nei.

E tautoko ana te hōtaka akoranga i ngā tamariki ki te mārama i te tino kaupapa o te whanaungatanga, te reo Māori me nga tikanga e ai ki Ngā Rauru. Ko te wharenui tētahi taiao haumaru mā ngā tamariki ki te whakamātautau me te whai haere i ngā whakahaere a te marae, ā, kua tiakina ngā tamariki e ngā whatu manaaki o ngā kuia me ngā kaumātua.

Ko te noho a te kuia i te kōhanga reo i ngā wā katoa tētahi whai hua mā te whānau ki te ako i ngā mea katoa e taea ai e rātou te mau e pā ana ki te reo Māori me ngā tikanga o Ngā Rauru. E ngākau nui ana rātou ki a ia me ōnā mātauranga, ā, e whai koha ana rātou ki a ia mō āna mahi katoa ki te kōhanga reo. Ko te nuinga o ngā kaiāwhina he mātua hou, ā, ahakoa he taiohi tonu te pakeke e tautoko ana rātou i ngā kaiako ki te whakaako me te tiaki i ā rātou tamariki. E ako tonu ana rātou i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te whakamāherehere i ngā kaupapa, te hōtaka aromātai me ngā whakahaere e pā ana ki te aromatawai, ā, kua uru rātou ki ngā wānanga ka whakahaeretia e te Poari Matua o Te Kōhanga Reo. Te pai hoki o ngā kaimahi ki te mahi ngātahi, ā, e harikoa ana rātou ki te whakawhanaunga ki a rātou anō.

I tūtohutia e te pūrongo o mua me whakatūria e te whānau tētahi māhere whakatutuki me te tāpiri kōrero hōhonu e pā ana ki ngā tāngata nō rātou te haepapa mō tēnā tūmahi me tēnā tūmahi, te utu me ngā wā hei whakatutuki i ngā tūmahi. Ahakoa kāore anō tēnei kia whakatutukitia, ka mea atu te whānau mā te whakarite i tētahi māhere whakatutuki e taea ai e rātou te aroturuki me te tuku pūrongo mō ngā paetae me ngā whanaketanga e ai ki ō rātou whāinga rautaki. E whakahaeretia ana te kōhanga raro ki raro i tētahi raihana hou.

Ko ngā whakahaere ā-whānau me ngā whakahaere hei whakatau i ngā take ka whakahaeretia e tētahi rōpū whāiti e tae atu ana ki te kōhanga reo i ia wā. Ka tino whakamōhio atu rātou i ngā whakahaere a te kōhanga reo ki te whānau, ā, e wātea ana ngā mēneti o te whānau hei pānuitanga mā rātou. E tino mōhio ana te ohu whakahaere ā-whanau i te take nui hei whakanui ake i te whai wāhitanga a ngā mātua o te whānau i roto i ngā whakahaere me ngā tukanga hei whakatau i ngā take o te kōhanga reo.

He tino kaupapa nui ngā whakahaere e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru ki te whānau. E hāngai ana ngā pūnaha me ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā ki te kaupapa nui rawa atu m ō te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki.

Ngā Mahi Ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o Te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Wai-o-Turi kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Wai-o-Turi.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko āna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Whenua

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere i te māhere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • hōtaka whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Whenua

He whakamārama

I kōwhiritia e te whānau te Mana Whenua hei kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E ai ki ō rātou whakapono ko te whanaungatanga te mea hei whakapakari ake i ngā tūhonohono i waenganui i a rātou anō, ā, ka whānui ake ngā māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te whanaungatanga me ō rātou haepapa hei kaitiaki mō te whenua.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Te Wai-o-Turitanga. Ko te marae te tino kaupapa e tautoko ana i ngā tamariki ki te ako i tō rātou Wai-o-Turitanga. Ko Te Rangiharuru te wāhi ka hui ngā tamariki me te whānau, ā, ka pōwhiritia ngā manuhiri ki tēnei wāhi. Ka whakahaere ratou i ngā kaupapa i kōnei, arā, te whakaputa me te whakanui i te reo o Ngā Rauru me ōnā tikanga, te manaakitanga me te whanaungatanga. E māia ana ngā tamariki ki te whakaputa i ngā mōteatea me te whakaputa i ō rātou whakapapa me ō rātou pepeha. Ka rongo rātou i ngā pūrākau a ō rātou tūpuna, ā, mā tēnei e tūhonohono pakari rawa ō rātou aho tuakiri ki tō rātou marae me tō rātou hapū. E tū whakahīhī ana ngā tamariki i runga i tō rātou marae.

Whanaungatanga. Ka aro kaha ngā kaimahi ki te kaupapa o te tiaki me te whakapakari ake i ngā whakapapa i waenganui i ngā tamariki. E ako ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou whanaungatanga ki ngā wāhi tapu me ngā wāhi whakahirahira o ngā tūpuna, ā, mā ēnei ka pakari ake ō rātou mōhio mō te whanaungatanga. E whai wheako ana rātou mō te whanaungatanga ki a rātou hoa me ō rātou pakeke, ā, e whai painga ana rātou mā te tiaki me te awhiawhi a ngā kaimahi e tino mōhio ana i a rātou me ō rātou whānau.

Te kuia. Ko te noho a te kuia i te kōhanga reo i ngā wā katoa te take e piki ana te ora o te reo Māori me ngā tikanga o Ngā Rauru. Kei te aroha nui rātou ki a ia, ā, he tino pakeke ia o te whānau i te kōhanga reo.

Te taiao akoranga. Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi tētahi taiao akoranga mauritau me tētahi wāhi kua whakaraupapatia ngā taputapu kia taea ai e ngā tamariki te ako me te tākaro. He mea hou, he whānui, ā, he māhana te kōhanga reo. Kua whakatūria ngā wāhi ngū me ngā wāhi kaha whakawhanaunga kia taea ai e ngā tamariki te tīmata i ngā tākaro e hiahiatia ana e rātou te whai atu. E whakaaturia ana ngā hiahia o ngā tamariki ki ngā pakitara, ā, he mea tautoko ēnei whakaaturanga mō ngā akoranga ō-mua. He manawanui me te māia te āhua o ā rātou noho ki te taiao akoranga.

Ngā whai wāhitanga a ngā tamariki ki te ako. Kua arohatia ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi, ā, he mea kaha manaaki me te poipoi te āhua o ēnei whanaungatanga. E whakahaere ana ngā kaimahi i ngā wheako akoranga hīkaka me ngā akoranga harikoa. E whakahaere ana rātou i ngā rautaki whaihua mō ngā whanonga, ngā kemū me ngā tamariki kia arotahi ai rātou ki ngā akoranga. Ko te whakamihi me ngā tohu reo ngū ētahi rautaki whakahaere ka kitea i te wā i te wā kua whakanuia ngā whai wāhitanga a ngā tamariki. Kua tautokotia ngā tamariki, ā, e harikoa ana rātou ki te ako.

Te mahitahi a ngā kaimahi. Kua poipoia te kāhui kaimahi, ā, he whai tikanga te āhua o ā rātou whakahaere. E kaha ana te kaiako ki te whakanui i ngā āheinga me ngā kaha o tēnā kaimahi me tēnā kaimahi kia riro i ngā tamariki te tautoko nui me ngā wheako akoranga whai hua. Kei te pai ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero i waenganui i a rātou anō kia riro ai ngā tamariki i ngā kōrero ōrite me ngā tohutohu whai hua mō ngā akoranga. E arotahi ana rātou ki ngā akoranga me te whaiora o ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamāherehere me te aromātai

He whakamārama

I pupū ake ai tētahi take mā te whānau hei whakapai ake anō, arā, te whakatū i tētahi māhere whakatutuki e whakaatu ai e pēhea ana ā rātou whakahaere ki te whakatutuki i ō rātou whāinga rautaki. Kāore tēnei i whakatutukitia. Ko te arotake whaiaro anō tētahi take hei whakapai ake anō. E arotahi ana te whānau ki a rātou anō ki te whakanui ake i ō rātou māramatanga kia taea ai e rātou te kaha hāpai i ō rātou haepapa.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Ngā whakamāherehere ā-kaupapa. Ka mahi ngātahi ngā kaimahi ki te whakatakoto i ngā māhere hei whakahaere i ngā wheako akoranga huhua mā ngā tamariki. E hāngai ana ngā māhere whāroa me ngā māhere whāiti ki ngā tino whāinga oTe Whāriki.He hōhonu te whai whakaaro mō ngā whakamāherehere me ngā kaupapa akoranga, ā, he mea whaihua ngā whakahaere hei whakatinana i te hōtaka akoranga. E whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā wheako hei whakanui ake i ō rātou māramatanga ki te Mana Whenua.

Ngā pūnaha me ngā whakahaere a te tari. He tōtika te āhua o ngā pūnaha whakahaere katoa i te tari, ā, e pai ana ngā whakahaere. E whai mana ana ngā tuhinga matua, ā, kua āta pupuritia ngā pūkete katoa kia māmā te tiki atu. E kaha ana ngā kaimahi ki te hāpai i ō rātou haepapa kia pai ai te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa. E tino whai mōhiohio ngā mātua o te whānau mō ngā take o te wā i te kōhanga reo me ngā whakatau a ngā kaiwhakahaere kaimahi mā te riro i ngā pānui i te wā i te wā.

Ngā whakahaere ā-pūtea. He pai ake ngā whakahaere a te whānau mō te whakahaere pūtea. E riro ana te kōhanga reo i te pūtea nui inaiānei, ā, kua whakakorengia te pūtea takarepa i pupū ake ai i ngā rā o mua. E whakaatu ana ngā pūkete whakahaere pūtea e ora pai ai te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo, ā, ka taea te whānau te tautoko i ngā kaupapa nui mō ngā rangi ā meāke nei.

Ngā whakahaere e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. He tino kaupapa nui te hauora me te haumaru ki te whānau kia tika ai ngā tākaro me ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He tōtika ngā tukanga kua whakatūria hei whakahaere me te aroturuki i ngā take e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru i te kōhanga reo.

Te kāhui tautoko. E ai ki ngā kōrero a ngā kaiwhakahaere kaimahi ka rapu rātou i te tautoko mai i ngā mātua o te whānau me ngā tāngata o te hapori e tino mātau ana ki tētahi kaupapa hei āwhina i a rātou ki te whakatutuki i ō rātou haepapa whakahaere.Ka tae anō hoki rātou ki ngā whakangungu pākihi o te rohe kia pai ake ai ō rātou mātauranga me ō rātou pūkenga whakahaere. E āhukahuka ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kaha me te manawareka o ngā kaiwhakahaere kaimahi me ō rātou hiahia nui ki te hāpai i ō rātou tūranga hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te whakatakoto i te māhere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro. Me whai wāhi atu te whānau ki te whakatū i tētahi māhere whakatutuki ki te tautoko i te ahunga whakamua o te kōhanga reo. Me tāpiritia ngā kōrero e pā ki ngā tāngata nō rātou ngā haepapa hei whakatinana i tēnā whāinga me tēnā whāinga, te utu me te wā hei whakatutuki i ngā tūmahi. Me whai wāhi anō hoki rātou ki te aroturuki me te tuku pūrongo e pā āna ki ngā putanga hua me ngā paetae e ai ki ngā tino whāinga kua whakatūria. E mea ana rātou me whai whakaaro rātou me pēhea te whakahaere i tēnei mahi.

Te whai wāhitanga a te whānau. Kāore ngā mātua katoa i te tae mai ki ngā hui ā-whanau. Tērā tētahi rōpū whāiti i te kōhanga reo e whakatau ana i ngā take. Kua tāututia e ngā kaimahi te matea nui hei whakanui i te whai wāhitanga o ngā mātua ki ngā hui ā-whānau me ngā tukanga hei whakatau i ngā take.

Te hōtaka aromātai me te aromatawai. Kāore i te ōrite ngā whakahaere aromatawai a ngā kaimahi mō ngā reanga katoa. Me pai ake ngā tūhonohono i waenganui i te hōtaka, te aromātai me ngā aromatawai. Me whakatūria e te whānau ētahi aratohu māmā mō te whakahaere i nga aromatawai hei tautoko i ngā kaimahi kia taea ai e ratou te kaha hāpai i ō rātou haepapa me ngā tino tūmahi o ia kaimahi.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

He whakamārama

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga reo o Te Wai o Turi tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga a te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Tūtohunga

I whakawhanaketia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā tūtohunga e whai ake nei, arā, i whakaae te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau kia:

5.1 Mahi ngātahi ai te komiti whakahaere, ngā kaimahi me te whanau ki te:

  • whakapakari ake i ngā tukanga whakahaere mō te aromātai me te aromatawai

  • whakawhanake me te tino whakatau i ngā tukanga whaihua e pā ana ki ngā arotake whaiaro mō te whakamāherehere kia taea ai e ratou te aroturuki me te tuku pūrongo mō ngā paetae ka puta

  • oti i ngā aromātai mō ngā kaimahi

  • whakawhanake i tētahi māhere whakatutuki hei āwhina i a rātou ki te whakapai ake i ngā take i pupū ake ai i tēnei pūrongo.

6 Ngā Mahi ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu Whakakapi mō ngā Ratonga Arotake Māori (Te Uepū ā-Motu)

22 Hui-tanguru 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Wai-o-Turi Kōhanga Reo is marae based and strategically situated to capture the picturesque views of rural farmland and the Tasman Sea. Te Wai-o-Turi takes its name from the site of the spring that was found by their ancestor Turi. The spring is on the top of a cliff where the marae Te Wai-o-Turi stands today.

The premises on which the kōhanga reo sits is called Te Māhuri o Te Puna Mahara. Te Māhuri alludes to the developmental growth of the child. The whānau believes that if children are taught about the values and principles of Te Wai-o-Turi, then whānau aspirations will blossom through children’s positive contribution to the world. Te Puna Mahara is the commitment of the whānau to provide quality education for their children so that they leave Te Wai-o-Turi knowing who they are and where they come from.

The whānau chose Mana Whenua as the focus area of this review. Positive relationships between the marae, whānau and kaimahi contribute to children’s sense of belonging, identity and wellbeing. Whanaungatanga provides a firm foundation for teaching and learning about Wai-o-Turitanga. There is a good balance of experienced kaiako and young kaimahi to care for and support children’s learning. Rangiharuru is a meaningful resource base for kaimahi and children to learn about their whakapapa connections and local history.

The learning programme supports children to understand the importance of whanaungatanga and te reo Māori and tikanga pertaining to Ngā Rauru. The wharenui is a culturally safe environment for children to practise and participate in marae activities under the watchful eyes of kuia and kaumātua.

The constant presence of kuia at the kōhanga reo provides the incentive for the whānau to learn as much as they can about te reo Māori and tikanga of Ngā Rauru. They respect and value her knowledge and contributions to the kōhanga reo. Most of the kaiāwhina are young parents who support kaiako with the education and care of their children. They are still learning about kaupapa planning, programme evaluation and assessment practices and are all enrolled onTe Kōhanga Reo National Trust(TKRNT) courses. Kaimahi work well as a team and enjoy being in one another’s company.

3 Findings

Mana Whenua

Background

The whānau chose Mana Whenua as the focus area for this review. They believe that whanaungatanga strengthens their bond with each other and will broaden children’s understanding of their relationship and responsibility as kaitiaki for the environment.

Areas of good performance

Wai-o-Turitanga. The marae is central to developing children’s sense of Wai-o-Turitanga. Te Rangiharuru is where children and whānau meet and greet visitors. It is here that they practice and reinforce Ngā Rauru reo, tikanga, manaakitanga and whanaungatanga values. Children confidently participate in mōteatea, recite whakapapa and pepeha. They hear stories about their ancestors that affirm their links to their marae and hapū. Children stand on their marae with dignity and pride.

Whanaungatanga. Kaimahi place high value on maintaining and strengthening whakapapa connections. Children learn about their relationships to their cultural and spiritual landmarks that strengthen their understanding and the place of whanaungatanga. Children experience meaningful relationships with peers and adults and benefit from the care and affection they receive from kaimahi who know them and their whānau well.

The kuia. The constant presence of the kuia at the kōhanga reo is integral to the revitalisation and maintenance of te reo Māori and tikanga of Ngā Rauru. She is a respected and valued member of the kōhanga reo whānau.

Learning environment. Kaimahi provide a calm, well organised and safe learning environment for their children to learn and play. The kōhanga reo is new, spacious, warm and welcoming. Quiet spaces and spaces for active play have been created so that children can initiate play of their choice. Children’s interests are displayed on the walls and used to revisit previous learning. They are comfortable and confident in their learning environment.

Children’s engagement in learning. Children are genuinely loved and nurtured by kaimahi. Kaimahi provide learning activities that are fun and interesting. They use positive learning behaviour strategies, games and waiata to engage children. Praise and non-verbal gestures are used to acknowledge children’s efforts regularly. Children are well supported and happy in their learning.

Team work. The kaimahi team is well organised and managed effectively. The kaiako maximises kaimahi strengths and abilities to ensure children are provided with the best possible learning opportunities and support. Kaimahi communicate well with each other so that children receive consistent messages and expectations for learning. They are focused on children’s learning and wellbeing.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

The previous report identified that the whānau needed to develop an action plan to show how they would work towards achieving their strategic goals. This has not been achieved. Self review was also identified as an area for development. Whānau are attending training to build their understanding to enable them to carry out their responsibilities effectively.

Areas of good performance

Kaupapa planning. Kaimahi plan collectively to provide a range of learning opportunities for children. Long and short-term plans are linked toTe Whārikiexpectations. Planning is detailed and provides a focus for kaupapa learning and the effective delivery of the learning programme. Children have a variety of experiences to develop their understanding of Mana Whenua.

Office systems and practices. Office systems and practices are highly organised and well managed. Key documents are kept up to date and systematically filed. Kaimahi take all reasonable steps to ensure the health and safety of children is maintained. The whānau is kept well informed about what is happening at the kōhanga reo and about management decisions through regular newsletters.

Financial management. Whānau have improved the way they manage kōhanga reo finances. The kōhanga reo is now receiving quality funding and the deficit that had accrued earlier has been cleared. Financial records show that the kōhanga reo is in a good financial position to support future kōhanga reo initiatives and developments.

Health and safety practices. Children’s health and safety is a priority for the whānau to ensure children are well protected and have a safe environment in which to play and learn. Systems are well established to manage and monitor health and safety in the kōhanga reo.

Support networks. The management staff report they seek guidance and assistance using the expertise within the whānau and community to support them with their management responsibilities. They also attend local business training courses to improve their knowledge and management skills. ERO acknowledges whānau determination and proactive attitude in their endeavour to provide the best that they can for their children.

Areas for improvement

Strategic planning and self review. The whānau needs to develop an action plan to support strategic development. This should include specific details about people responsible for various tasks, costs and timelines for when tasks will be completed. They also need to monitor and report on progress and developments against their strategic priorities. They acknowledge that further thought needs to be given to how this is managed.

Whānau engagement. Whānau meetings are not well attended. A core group of members at the kōhanga reo are the decision makers. Kaimahi have identified the need to increase whānau involvement and participation at whānau hui and in all decision making processes.

Programme evaluation and assessment. Kaimahi assessment practices are not consistent across the levels. The links between the programme, evaluation and assessment needs to be more clearly aligned. Clear assessment guidelines and a formalised process for programme evaluation needs to be developed for kaimahi to carry out their tasks consistently and effectively.

General Information About Reviews

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
