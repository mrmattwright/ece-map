Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai o Wikitoria ki Papakura, kei te tonga o Tāmaki Makaurau. E mārama ana te whānau ki ō rātou wawata mō a rātou tamariki. E whakarato ana ngā kaimahi i ngā mahere me ngā wā ako ki te whakapiki i tā ngā tamariki kuhu, noho hoki ki te reo Māori. E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo Māori. He māmā te whakapuaki i a rātou anō mā roto mai i ngā mihimihi, i ngā waiata, i ngā kēmu me ngā wā e arahi ana rātou i ngā karakia. He māia, he ngahau hoki te ako ki ngā tamariki.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai o Wikitoria i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai o Wikitoria ki Papakura, kei te tonga o Tāmaki Makaurau. E mārama ana te whānau ki ō rātou wawata mō a rātou tamariki. He mea nui ki te whānau te whakatupu i te mātauranga mō ngā tikanga me ngā kāwai whakapapa kia tū pakari ai ngā tamariki hei ākonga māia hīkaka hoki. E ako ana ngā tamariki i tētehi taiao e whai whakaaro ana ki ngā kaha o te katoa o te whānau me te whakamomori, te mahi auaha hoki a ngā kaimahi.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēwhea rawa te tautokotanga o ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo Māori?

E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo Māori.

Mana Reo

E mārama ana, e kōrero ana hoki ngā tamariki pakeke i te reo Māori, ā, he auaha, he ngāhau hoki te whakaako atu. He tokomaha tonu ngā tamariki e taea ana te whakautu pātai, te whakatinana kupu akiaki, te whai tohutohu me te whakamahi rerenga kōrero. He māia tā rātou waiata me te taki karakia, mihimihi, pepehā, waiata me ngā kaupapa ako. E whai wā ana ngā tamariki ki te arahi i ngā karakia, ngā kēmu-ā-ringa me ngā waiata. He ngāhau, he kauanuanu hoki ngā akoranga reo ki ngā tamariki.

He pai noa iho ki ngā nohinohi te kuhu ki ngā kaupapa ako reo mā te whakatinana i ngā rautaki reo. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā waiata, i ngā mahi-ā-ringa, i ngā katakata me ngā rerenga, ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ngāwari nei i a rātou e kōrero ana, e mahi tahi ana ki ngā nohinohi. Ka tīkina e ngā nohinohi ko te oro, ko ngā kupu ruarua nei me te pūkana i a rātou e mahi ana ki ngā kaimahi me o rātou hoa. Ka whakaakongia ngā kaupapa reo i runga i te ngāwari me te māmā ki te whakatenatena me te akiaki i te whai wāhitanga mai. He pai ki ngā nohinohi te whai wā ki ngā kaimahi me o rātou hoa.

Ka whakawhiwhia e te whānau ki ngā tamariki ngā tautokotanga tika mō te ako i te reo. He kōrero Māori te nuinga o te whānau, e whai mahi ana rānei e hāngai ana ki te whakaakotanga o te reo. Ka reka kē atu ngā kai o te hōtaka ako ki te whāi wāhi atu ngā mema o te whānau ki ngā wheako o te reo hei whānau awhi ki te kōhanga reo, ki te kāinga rānei. He taonga te reo Māori, ā, he nui ōna kaitautoko i te kōhanga reo, i te kāinga hoki.

He pai tā ngā kaiako mahere, ā, me ngā wheako ako i te reo ka puta noa mai mā ngā tamariki. Ka whai wā ngā tamariki ki te mahi auaha me te kuhu ki te ao pōhewa ki te whakatōmene ki roto, hurirauna hoki i te kōhanga reo. Ko te nehenehe iti kei waho he wāhi tākaro e whakatenatena ana i ngā tamariki ki te kuhu ki tō rātou taiao. He māmā noa iho te rere o ngā tikanga hātepe, ā, he wā pai hoki tēnei ki te ako i ētehi āhuatanga pai, whakawhanaunga hoki hei akiaki i te kōrerotanga o te reo Māori. E takina ana e ngā tamariki ngā karakia i ngā wā o te rā, ā, kei te whakaohongia ō rātou wairua mō te pānga o ngā atua ki a rātou ao noa, pō noa. E ako ana ngā tamariki mō rātou anō me ō rātou takenga mai i a rātou e taki ana i ngā whakapapa, i ngā pepehā me te whai wāhi atu ki ngā kaupapa mahi. E tīmata ana ngā tamariki ki te whai whakaaro ki tō rātou ao Māori, ā, e rongo ana rātou i te angitū i a rātou e tākaro ana, e ako ana hoki i tētehi hōtaka ako i tika rā te waihanga mai.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

E eke ana te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo e te whānau. He rite tonu te pūrongo i ngā wāhi nui o ngā nekenekehanga. Ka noho te tukanga whakakuhu hei tautoko i te whānau hou ki te mārama ki te kaupapa me ngā tikanga a te kōhanga reo. He mārō te noho mai o ngā aromihi, ā, e whiriwhiritia ana ngā āhuatanga hei whakawhanake mā ngā kaimahi. Kua oti i te whānau te whakatakoto i ngā tino o te whakakitenga, tae ana ki te whakarauora i te reo Māori me te rite tonu o te mātakitakitanga me te pūrongotanga. He mārama ngā whakatau a te whānau, ā, he pai tā rātou mahere hei tautoko i ngā akoranga ki ngā tamariki.

E whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki ki ētehi hōtaka ako e aro ana ki te whakatupu i te mōhio ki ngā kupu i roto i te ao Māori, puta noa hoki i te ao. He korahi te maheretanga o te hōtaka, ā, e hāngai ana ki te ārahi i ngā tikanga mahi a ngā kaimahi. Ka whakawhiwhia ki ngā tamariki te huhua o ngā kaupapa wheako ako e hāngai ana ki te wā, ki ngā whāinga ako hoki a ngā tamariki. He rite tonu tā ngā kaimahi arotake i te hōtaka e whakamahi nei i ngā kanohi hōmiromiro me ngā aratohu whai take. E tino whai hua ana ngā tamariki i te pai o te maheretanga o te hōtaka ako e tautoko ana i te whakapakaritanga o tō rātou reo. E noho ana ngā whakaāhua o ngā tamariki e whai wāhi atu ana ki ngā kaupapa huhua i roto i a rātou ake kōpaki ako, ā, ka tohaina ki te whānau. He mea whakaao mārama ēnei kōrero i te koke me te angitū o te tamaiti. E kitea ana te pūpūtanga o ngā hiahia me ngā kaha o ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai o Wikitoria is located in Papakura, South Auckland. Whānau are clear about their aspirations for their children. 1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai o Wikitoria is located in Papakura, South Auckland. Whānau are clear about their aspirations for their children. Building te reo Māori and knowledge about cultural and ancestral heritage is a priority for whānau. As part of their learning journey, whānau want their children to develop as confident, enthusiastic learners. Children learn in an environment that values the collective efforts of whānau and the commitment and creativity of kaimahi.

2 The Review Findings

How well are children supported to learn te reo Māori?

Children are well supported to learn te reo Māori.

Mana Reo

The older children understand and speak te reo Māori which is delivered in creative and fun ways. Several children can respond to questions, prompts, directions and use phrases. They confidently sing and recite karakia, mihimihi, pepeha, waiata and kaupapa ako. There are opportunities for children to lead karakia, hand games and waiata. Language learning is fun and inspiring for children.

Babies happily participate in language learning activities using verbal and non verbal techniques. Kaimahi use waiata, gestures, smiles, simple phrases and conversations when they communicate and interact with babies. Toddlers use sounds, one or two words or facial gestures when they respond to kaimahi and their peers. Language activities and games are delivered in a relaxed way to motivate and encourage participation. Babies enjoy making contact with kaimahi and their peers.

Children receive positive language support from their whānau. Most whānau members speak te reo Māori or are involved in careers that involve the teaching of te reo Māori. Children’s language experiences are enriched when whānau members are involved in the learning programme as whānau awhi at the kōhanga or at home. Te reo Māori is valued and well supported in the kōhanga reo and at home.

Kaiako provide well planned and at times spontaneous language learning experience for children. Children have opportunities to be creative and use imaginative play to explore in and around the kōhanga reo. The small bush area in the outside play area provides motivation for children to engage with their natural environment. Routines flow smoothly and are positive learning and social times for children to encourage the use te reo Māori. Children recite karakia for different parts of the day and are acquiring an awareness of how atua impact on their daily lives. Children learn about themselves and where they are from when they recite their whakapapa, pepeha and participate in kaupapa mahi. Children are beginning to make sense of their Māori world and experiencing success as they play and learn in a well designed learning programme.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau manage the kōhanga reo well. There is regular reporting on key areas of operations. The induction process is used to support new whānau members understand the kaupapa and kōhanga reo management practices. Appraisals are robust and identify the professional development needs of kaimahi. Whānau have planned for their future priorities which include raising te reo Māori and regular monitoring and reporting. Whānau make clear decisions and plan well to support children’s learning.

Children experience learning programmes that focus on building vocabulary around te ao Māori and the wider world. Programme planning is extensive and effectively guides kaimahi practice. Children receive a variety of kaupapa learning experiences that are topical and relevant to their learning needs. Kaimahi regularly evaluate these programmes using critical thinking and robust guidelines. Children benefit from well planned learning programmes that support their language learning development. Photographs of children participating in a range of kaupapa activities are contained in their individual profile folders and shared with whānau. These records highlight the child’s progress and achievements. Children’s emerging interests and strengths are becoming more evident.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai o Wikitoria is located in Papakura, South Auckland. Whānau are clear about their aspirations for their children. Kaimahi plan and provide learning opportunities to increase children’s engagement and exposure to te reo Māori. Children are well supported to learn te reo Māori. They express themselves freely through mihimihi, waiata, games and when they lead karakia. Children are confident and have fun learning.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai o Wikitoria will be in three years.
