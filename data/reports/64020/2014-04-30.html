1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Tū Roa ki te pūtahi o Ōtaki. Ka noho ngā tamariki me ngā whānau i te rohe o Ngāti Raukawa. E tino pā tata atu ana te kōhanga reo ki Te Wānanga o Raukawa, te kura o te hapori, me te wharekura. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu, te tautoko hoki o te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo. He whāinga ngātahi tā rātou ki te whakatairanga i ngā akoranga mā te ao Māori.

I whakaritea e te whānau o te kōhanga reo ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake mātauranga. E ngākau nui ana rātou ki te whakawhanaketanga o te katoa o te tamaiti. Ka whai wāhi atu te whānau ki te waihanga o te pātai aromātai e whakamōhio ana i te waihanga o te arotake me te aromātai hoki a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

“He pēhea te tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake hei ākonga māia, e noho pakari ana i roto i tō rātou tuakiri ahurea?”

E whai hua ana te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki:

  • hei ākonga māia
  • i ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki hei tangata whenua
  • i ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki hei kaitiaki o te whenua
  • i te tino māramatanga, te noho whakaute hoki ki ngā atua Māori
  • i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki ō rātou ake hononga ā-whānau, ā-iwi hoki, me ngā hononga hoki ki te whenua me te ao Māori
  • i ngā whanonga e whakaatu ana i te noho whakaute, te manaaki, me te whanaungatanga
  • i te ngākau nui, te manawa whakahī, te kaha hoki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori

Ka whai hua te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakapūmau i ā rātou kawenga whakahaere. E whakatutuki ana rātou i ngā tino putanga mō ā rātou tamariki. He nui ō rātou wawata mō ngā tamariki, kia whanake ai rātou hei ākonga māia, e noho pakari ana i roto i tō rātou tuakiri ahurea. Kua āta whakaarohia tā rātou aronga rautaki, ā, he tino huarahi mārama tēnei e whakamahia ana ki te whakatutuki i ā rātou whāinga ngātahi, ā rātou kaupapa matua ngātahi anō hoki. E tino poipoia ana, e tino tautokona ana hoki ngā tamariki e tō rātou whānau.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mokohuia o te Rautahi kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

“He pēhea te tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake hei ākonga māia, e noho pakari ana i roto i tō rātou tuakiri ahurea?”

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka ako ngā tamariki i te whai pānga nui o ngā atua Māori ki tō rātou ahurea me tō rātou tuakiri. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki ngā karakia o ia rā. Ka whai hua ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te tuitui i ngā akoranga o ngā atua Māori ki ngā āhuatanga katoa o te hōtaka.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga o ngā taiao ako o te ao Māori, e whai pānga nui ana ki a rātou. Kua āta whakawhanakehia ngā wāhi o roto, o waho hoki. Ka arotahi te hōtaka akoranga ki te tuku i ngā tamariki ki te whakatewhatewha, te tūhura, me te whakawhanake anō hoki i tō rātou hononga ki te whenua, ngā taonga māori, me ngā taonga tuku iho. Ko ētahi o ngā wheako, ko te whakamahi i ngā rākau, ko ngā māra kai, ko ngā taputapu me ngā rauemi Māori i tuku iho ai, ko ngā taonga puoro, me te poi. E tautokona ana ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i ētahi mōhiotanga ki ngā rongoā Māori me te oranga tinana.

He pakari ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, ā, e mōhio ana hoki rātou ki ā rātou tikanga, ki ngā hītori hoki. He tino āheinga tō ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakapūmau i aua akoranga. Ka whai wāhi nui ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki waenga i ngā pākeke me ngā tamariki, ā, ka hāpai ēnei i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki ako reo e tino whai hua ana, hei whakanui, hei whakawhānui, hei whakatairanga anō hoki i te reo o ngā tamariki.

He māia, he tau, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki ki te ako. He pakari rātou ki te taunekeneke me te whānau, ā, he tākare nui hoki tō rātou ki te tūhura takitahi. He māhorahora te whakapuaki a ngā tamariki i ō rātou whakaaro ki ētahi atu. Ka kitea te whanaungatanga o ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou taunekeneke ki ētahi atu. Ka ākina rātou ki te whakahono atu i a rātou anō ki ngā taonga tuku iho, mā ō rātou whakapapa. E ako ana ngā tamariki ko tō rātou waiora, ā rātou uaratanga, ā rātou whanonga anō hoki, ka whai pānga nui anō hoki ki te waiora o te whānau whānui.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

“He pēhea te tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake hei ākonga māia, e noho pakari ana i roto i tō rātou tuakiri ahurea?”

E tino poipoia ana ngā tamariki e tō rātou whānau ki te whakawhanake hei ākonga māia, ā, kia mātātoa hoki tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki te ao Māori.

Ngāwāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau.Ko ngā painga me te atawhai o ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā mema ki te aroturuki me te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. He mahere tā rātou e pā ana ki te tauatanga me te rōnakitanga. Ka whakatutukihia e te whānau katoa ngā whakataunga e pā ana ki ngā whakaritenga, te whakamahere, me te arotake. Ka ū tonu ki te whai huatanga o ngā mahi whakahaere, te noho mārama o aua mahi, me ngā kawenga takohanga.

Te aronga rautaki.Kua āta whakamaheretia te aronga whakamua o te kōhanga reo hei ngā tau kei te heke mai. Kua tino whakapuakihia ngā wawata o te whānau mō ā rātou tamariki. Ko ngā tino whāinga, me ngā kaupapa matua o ia tau, e whai pānga ana ki te tirohanga me te tauāki taketake i te tūtohinga.

Ngā mōhiohio aromatawai.Kua tino kapohia atu ki ngā pūkete te ia o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i tō rātou tuakiri ahurea. E wātea ana, ka whakapuaki hoki ki te whānau te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo mōhiohio. He pai te māramatanga o ngā kaimahi ki ngā mahi ka kapo atu i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki hei ākonga, i roto i ngā tini āhuatanga katoa o te ao Māori.

Te aromātai hōtaka.Ka noho pūmau te whakaaro huritao ki waenga i ngā kaimahi. Ka tuhi haere te kaiako i tētahi rātaka, ā, e mau ana tēnei i ngā whakaaro kaikini e pā ana ki te whai huatanga o ngā hōtaka me ngā whakaritenga mā ngā tamariki katoa. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia wā ki te whakawhiti kōrero e pā ana ki te whakatinanatanga o te hōtaka, ngā whakaritenga, me te whai pānga hoki o ēnei ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Te arotake whaiaro.Kāhore te aroturuki, te pūrongo hoki i āta whakaritea ai. Ko ngā hui ā-whānau te wāhi matua mō te arotake me te aroturuki. Me kaha ake, me āta whakatutuki hoki aua hui i te pūrongo i ngā kaupapa rautaki matua, i te hōtaka akoranga o Te Whāriki mō ngā tamariki, ā, i te reo me ngā tikanga Māori.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakiia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o okiuh, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga:

Kia whakawhanake tonu te whānau o te kōhanga reo me ngā kaimahi i ngā mahi arotake whaiaro, tae atu ki te arotake i ia te wā, i ngā whāinga ki tā rātou tūtohinga, te mahere rautaki, me Te Korowai, ā, me pūrongo hoki i aua āhuatanga i ia te wā ki ngā hui ā-whānau.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

Paenga-whāwhā 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tū Roa is located in the heart of Otaki. Children and whānau live in the Ngāti Raukawa area. The kōhanga reo is closely surrounded by Te Wananga o Raukawa, the local kura and wharekura. The whānau are actively involved and support their kōhanga reo. They share a common goal to promote learning through te ao Māori.

The kōhanga reo whānau chose ngā taumata wahakahirahira as the focus for this Education Review. They are committed to the holistic development of every child. Whānau participated in the design of the evaluation question which informed ERO’s review design and evaluation.

“How well are our children supported to develop as confident learners, strong in their cultural identity?”

Children are successfully developing:

  • as confident learners
  • their roles and responsibilities as tangata whenua
  • their roles and responsibilities as kaitiaki of the whenua
  • a strong understanding and respect for ngā atua Māori
  • knowledge about their personal connection to whānau and iwi, the land and te ao Māori
  • behaviours that display respect, manaaki and whānaungatanga
  • as passionate, proud and capable speakers of te reo Māori

The kōhanga reo whānau successfully uphold their management responsibilities. They are achieving significant outcomes for their children. They have high aspirations for children to develop as confident learners, strong in their cultural identity. Their well considered strategic direction provides a clear pathway that is used to realise shared goals and priorities. Children are well served and supported by their whānau.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata WhakahirahiraBackground

“How well are our children supported to develop as confident learners strong in their cultural identity?”

Areas of good performance

Children learn that ngā atua Māori is an integral part of their heritage and identity. They participate in daily karakia. Kaimahi successfully integrate learning of ngā atua Māori throughout the programme.

Children are immersed in rich and relevant te ao Māori learning environments. The indoor and outdoor areas are well developed. The learning programme is focused on enabling children to inquire, explore and develop an affinity to the land, natural resources and cultural taonga. Experiences include using rākau, maara kai, traditional Māori tools and implements, musical instruments and poi. Children are supported to develop some knowledge of plants for healing and healthy living.

Children confidently speak te reo Māori and are aware of their customs and history. There is strong capability amongst kaimahi and whānau to sustain this learning. Conversations between adults and children are prominent and support children’s learning. Kaimahi use highly effective language learning strategies to promote, extend and enrich children’s language.

Children are confident, settled and happy learners. They interact confidently with their whānau and are keen to explore independently. Children willingly offer their ideas to others. Children demonstrate whānaungatanga when they interact with others. They are encouraged to make personal connections to their heritage through whakapapa. Children are learning that their wellbeing, values and behaviours influence the greater whānau wellbeing.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

“How well are our children supported to develop as confident learners strong in their cultural identity?”

Children are well served by their whānau to develop as confident learners and active participants in te ao Māori.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management.Children’s interests and care is at the heart of whānau management. Members are actively involved in the monitoring and managing of the kōhanga reo. They have a plan for succession and sustainability. Decision making, planning and review is carried out by all whānau. Effective management, transparency and accountability are upheld.

Strategic direction.The future direction of the kōhanga reo is well planned. Whānau aspirations for their children are clearly articulated. Specific goals and annual priorities reflect the charter vision and mission.

Assessment information.Children’s learning about their cultural identity is captured well in portfolios. A range of information is available and is shared with whānau. Kaimahi have a good understanding about how to capture children’s development as learners in all dimensions of te ao Māori.

Programme evaluation.A reflective culture among kaimahi is maintained. The kaiako keeps a diary that contains critical reflections about the effectiveness of programmes and practices on all children. Kaimahi meet regularly to discuss programme implementation and practice and the impact on children’s learning.

Areas for development

Self review. Monitoring and reporting is not well organised. Whānau hui is the main forum for review and monitoring. These hui should have stronger and more deliberate reporting on strategic priorities,Te Whārikilearning programme for children, and te reo me ngā tikanga Māori.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement Self-Audit ChecklistBefore the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an EROand. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends:

  • The kōhanga reo whānau and kaimahi continue to develop self review practices, including regular review of their charter objectives, strategic plan and Te Korowai and report on these regularly at whānau hui.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
