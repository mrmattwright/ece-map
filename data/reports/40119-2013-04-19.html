2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū pātata ana Te Kōhanga Reo ki Pukeroa Ōruawhata ki te kura tuatahi o Rotorua, ki te tāone tonu o Rotorua. E mōhio ana ngā whānau e whakauru ana i ā rātou tamariki ki tēnei kōhanga reo, ki te aronga nui o ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori me te āta whakarite i ngā tamariki mō tā rātou whakawhitinga atu ki te kura. E whakarato ana ngā kaimahi o tēnei kōhanga reo i te tino kounga o te hōtaka mā ngā tamariki. Ka tino tautoko te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau me ngā kaimahi ko Mana Tangata me Mana Reo te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ka whai pānga nui te whanaungatanga ki te pakari o ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā tamariki, ngā kaimahi, me ngā mema o te whānau. He nui ngā tūmanako o te whānau kia mātau ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori me ngā akoranga. Ka whakapuakihia e rātou aua tūmanako ki ngā kaimahi i ngā hui ā-whānau. He pai te mahi ngātahi a ngā kaimahi ki te whakatutuki i ngā tūmanako o te whānau.

E tino whakatutukihia ana ngā matea akoranga reo o ngā tamariki, mā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo huarahi. Ka whakamahia ngā waiata tawhito, me ngā haka, ngā waiata ā-ringa hoki hei whakawhānui i te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou hītori, tō rātou tuakiri, me ngā wāhi whakahirahira o te hapori.

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. Haere ai te whānau ki ngā hui i ia te wā. He whai wāhitanga mā rātou ki te whakapuaki mōhiohio, te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga, me te whakawhiti kōrero mō te ahunga whakamua me ngā mahi angitu a ngā tamariki. Ka tino whakamōhiotia te whānau mō ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

Ka kitea te arotake whaiaro puta noa i ngā whakaritenga i te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā mōhiohio ka whakaemihia, ka pūrongotia hoki ki ngā hui ā-whānau, e whakaatu ana i te riterite o te arotake whaiaro. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki te arotake whaiaro, hei huarahi ka kōkiri tonu i tā rātou ahunga whakamua.

He matawhānui te whakamahere, ā, ka tino poipoi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo matea o ngā tamariki. Ka kitea ki ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki, tā rātou whai wāhitanga ki te hōtaka akoranga. Ka oke ngā kaimahi i ngā wā katoa, ki te whakapai ake. E arotahi ana rātou ki te whakarato i ngā wheako akoranga e tino whai hua ana ki ngā tamariki.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo ki Pukeroa Ōruawhata kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te pupuritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo ki Pukeroa Ōruawhata.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Tangata me Mana Reo.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake matauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aro matawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Tangata me Mana Reo

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Tangata me Mana Reo ā rātou kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. I whakamāramahia e rātou te whakaurutanga mai o ngā tamariki hou ki te hōtaka i te kōhanga reo, me tā rātou ako i te whanaungatanga me te reo Māori. Ki tā te whānau titiro, ka tautoko ngā tuākana i ō rātou tēina, nā runga hoki i tō rātou mōhio ki ngā mahinga.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Mana Tangata.He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi, puta noa i te rā. I ngā wā i whakaritea ai, e āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā tākaro takitahi, me te whakatau haere i ā rātou ake whiringa. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā taumahi takitahi, takirōpū iti, takirōpū nui ki ngā wāhi o roto, o waho hoki. E āhei ana rātou ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. Ka ārahi, ka whai wāhi atu hoki ngā kaimahi ki ngā tākaro a ngā tamariki, ā, ka whai pūtake hoki ā rātou taunekeneke. He pai te whakapuakitanga o ngā tohutohu ōkawa, ā, ka whakamahia ngā pūkenga o ngā kaimahi i roto i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo mahi. Ka ū tonu te ngana atu ki ngā kaupapa mā ngā rautaki e whakahihiri ana i te tamaiti, me te mātātoa hoki o ngā taunekeneke ki te taha o ngā tamariki.

Ka tino whakatauirahia te whanaungatanga ki ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi. Ka āta kitea te whakatinanatanga o ngā tamariki i te whakaaro nui, te atawhai hoki mō ō rātou hoa, me ngā tamariki hou ki te kōhanga reo. Ka tino kitea te whanonga pai i ā rātou e tākaro ana. Kua waia ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao, ā, he pakari tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga.

He pai ngā rauemi ki te kōhanga reo. Ka tiaki pai ngā tamariki i ā rātou rauemi, i tō rātou taiao ako hoki. He auaha te whakahaeretanga o ngā mahinga i whakaritea ai mō te rā, mā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki, tae atu ki ngā waiata, ngā kēmu, me ngā rotarota.

E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te noho tahi mō te roanga o te wā. He tika ngā whakawhitinga kōrero mā ngā reo e rua, ā, ka akiaki tēnei i te rere pai o ngā whakaaro. Ka ārahi, ka arahina hoki ngā tamariki i ngā wā ōkawa, i ngā wā ōpaki hoki. He harikoa ngā tamariki, ka ngana atu, ā, ka ngākau nui hoki ki te whai wāhi atu ki te hōtaka akoranga o te kounga kairangi i āta whakaritea ai.

Te reo Māori.Ka tino poipoia ngā matea ako reo o ngā tamariki mā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo huarahi. Ka tino whakamahia ngā tūmomo waiata katoa hei whakaako i te reo mā te aronga, te whai hoki i ngā tohutohu, me te whakapuaki auaha. Ko ngā pūkenga me te mātau o ngā kaiako ki ngā mahi ā te rēhia, e whakarato ana i ngā wheako ako o te kounga pai mō ngā tamariki. He pai te tū o ngā tamariki ki te waiata, ā, ka tino kitea tō rātou māramatanga nā te tika o ngā mahi ā-ringa, me ngā nekehanga o te kanohi me te tinana.

Ka ākina te hanganga reo i ngā whakamahinga.Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te roanga o ngā rerenga kōrero, ā, ka pērā hoki te āhua o ngā whakautu a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi hoki ngā kaimahi i te tāruarua, ā, ka aro nui atu ki ngā wheako e whai pūtake ana hei waihanga, hei akiaki, hei whakawhānui hoki i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamātau ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga reo, i a rātou e whakawhiti kōrero ana. Ka whakanui ngā kaimahi i ngā mahi whakamātau a ngā tamariki. He pai te nuinga o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. He pai te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori.

Te whanaungatanga.He pai te mahi ngātahi a ngā kaimahi. Kua rite rātou, kua whakatakotohia hoki e rātou ō rātou tūranga mahi, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki. Ko te whai pūtake o ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga ki te whānau me ngā tamariki e poipoi ana i te whanaungatanga me te whai pūtake o ngā mahi. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i te manaakitanga. He nui ngā tūmanako o te whānau kia mātau ā rātou tamariki i roto i te reo Māori me ngā akoranga, ā, ka whakapuakihia ēnei ki ngā kaimahi, i ngā hui ā-whānau. Nā ngā pūkenga me te mātau o ia kaimahi, ka whai hua te whānau, ā, e whakarato ana ngā kaimahi i te taiao ātaahua, harikoa hoki mō ā rātou tamariki, ki tētahi wāhi e tino tiakina ana. Ka manaaki hoki ngā tamariki, ā, e tino hāpaitia ana rātou e ngā kaimahi me te whānau.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

Ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai i te kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki, me te whānau. Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau.He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. Ko te tae ā-tinana atu ki ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, ka whakarato i ngā whai wāhitanga mō te whānau ki te whakapuaki i ngā mōhiohio, te whakatau whakaritenga, me te whakawhiti kōrero mō ahunga whakamua, te mahi angitu hoki a ngā tamariki. Nā te pūrongo i ia te wā i ngā pūtea, ngā kaimahi, te hauora, me te haumaru, ka aroturuki te whānau i aua wāhanga, me te arotake i ngā pūnaha me ngā tukanga e tika ana. Ka tino whakamōhiotia te whānau mō ngā whakahaeretanga i te kōhanga reo.

Te whakamahere rautaki.Ka whakaritea tētahi mahere rautaki, mō te roanga o ngā tau e whā. Ka arahina ngā mahi rautaki me ngā putanga e hiahia ana, e ngā tino tohu o te āhua mahi. Ko ngā tino aronga nui, ko ngā mahi rautaki e hāngai ana ki te reo Māori, Te Korowai, ngā kaupapa ā-whānau, me ngā mahi arotake whaiaro. Ko tētahi atu tino aronga, ko te whakawhanake i ngā pūkenga o ngā kaimahi. E whakatakoto ana te mahere rautaki i te tino aronga mō te whānau.

Te arotake whaiaro.Ka kitea te arotake whaiaro puta noa i ngā whakahaeretanga i te kōhanga reo. Whakaaro huritao ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, ā, ka whakaaro kaikini hoki i ā rātou mahi o ia rā. Ka kitea te arotake whaiaro o ia te wā, i ngā mōhiohio ka whakaemihia, ka pūrongotia hoki ki ngā hui ā-whānau. Ka tāutuhia ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga hei arotake. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki te arotake whaiaro, kia haere tonu ai te ahu whakamua.

Te whakamahere.Ka whakaatuhia ngā mahi whakamahere, ā, he ngāwari hoki te whai atu i tēnei. Kua whakaritea ngā tirohanga whānui mō te wā roa me te wā poto. Kua whakapuakihia e te whānau ō rātou whakaaro e pā ana ki ngā mahere. Ka hāngai te angamahi o ngā kaupapa, ki ngā kaupeka o te tau, ngā atua, me ngā tūmomo taumahi e hāngai ana ki ēnei, tae atu ki ngā kaupapa nui o te wā. Kei roto i ngā mahere ā-kaupapa ngā wāhanga matua katoa. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi mō ā rātou rōpū reanga, me ngā matea akoranga. He matawhānui ngā mahere, ā, ka tino poipoi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo matea o ngā tamariki.

Te aromatawai.Ka āta kitea ki ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki, tā rātou whai wāhitanga ki te hōtaka akoranga. Kōrerohia ai tā rātou ahunga whakamua i ia te wā. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā kōrerorero, ngā kitenga, me ngā rārangi arowhai whakawhanaketanga, ki te whakaemi i ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me tā rātou ahunga whakamua. Ka kohikohi hoki rātou i ētahi tauira o ngā mahi toi e whai pānga ana ki ngā kaupapa. E wātea ana ngā pūkete me ngā pukapuka mahi a ngā tamariki, ki te whānau i ngā wā katoa. Ko te whakawhiti pai o ngā tamariki ki te kura tētahi kaupapa matua ki te whānau me ngā kaimahi.

Te aromātai.Nā ngā whakaaro huritao e pā ana ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me te hōtaka, kua pā mai ētahi whakarerekētanga. Ka tāutu ngā kaimahi i ā rātou ake āhuatanga hei whakapai ake i roto i tā rātou whakapuakitanga, ā, ka whai i ngā mahi e tika ana. E oke ana ngā kaimahi i ngā wā katoa ki te whakapai ake, hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo ki Pukeroa o Oruawhata is located near the Rotorua Primary school in central Rotorua. Whānau, who bring their children to this kōhanga reo, are aware of the focused attention kaimahi have on te reo Māori development and the successful preparation of children for when they transition to school. The staff at this kōhanga reo provides a high quality programme for the children. Whānau fully support their kōhanga reo.

Whānau and kaimahi chose Mana Tangata and Mana Reo as the focus areas for this review. Whanaungatanga is a key element for building relationships among the children, kaimahi and whānau members. Whānau have high expectations for children to become proficient in te reo Māori and learning. They share these expectations with kaimahi at whānau hui. Kaimahi work well together to meet whānau expectations.

Children’s language learning needs are well catered for in a variety of appropriate ways. Traditional waiata, haka and waiata ā-ringa are used to extend children’s knowledge of local history, their identity and local places of significance.

The whānau manages the kōhanga reo effectively. Whānau attend hui regularly. They have opportunities to share information, make decisions, and discuss children’s progress and successes. The whānau is well informed about the kōhanga reo operations.

Self review is evident throughout kōhanga reo practice. Information gathered and reported to whānau hui shows consistent self review. The whānau actively engages in self review as a way of continuing to move forward.

Planning is comprehensive and caters well for the varying needs of children. Children’s profile books highlight their involvement in the learning programme. Kaimahi continually strive for improvement. They are focussed on providing children with highly effective learning experiences.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

4 Findings

Mana Tangata and Mana Reo

Background

The whānau chose Mana Tangata and Mana Reo as their focus areas for this review. They described how new children are introduced to the kōhanga reo programme and learn about whanaungatanga and te reo Māori. The whānau consider tuakana, who are familiar with routines, provide support for their teina.

Areas of good performance

Mana Tangata.Children enjoy a balance of activity throughout the day. During selected times children are able to participate in independent play and make individual choices. Children enjoy individual, small group and large group activity in the indoor and outdoor areas. They are able to follow their own interests. Kaimahi oversee, participate and interact purposefully with children during play. Formal instruction is delivered well using kaimahi skills in varying roles. High interest levels are maintained through animated, attention gaining strategies and active interaction with the children.

Whanaungatanga is modelled well for children by kaimahi. The children confidently practise and show concern and care for each other and any new children to the kōhanga reo. Positive behaviours are a feature of their play. Children are familiar with their surroundings and show a strong sense of belonging.

The kōhanga reo is well resourced. Children take good care of their resources and learning environment. Established routines throughout the day are managed creatively using a wide range of strategies including waiata, games and rhymes.

The children are able to sustain long periods of engagement with each other. Conversations in both languages are acceptable and encourage a flow of exchange. Children are willing to lead and be led during formal and informal activities. Children are happy, engaged and willing participants in a high quality, well organised learning programme.

Te Reo Māori.Children’s language learning needs are well catered for in a variety of ways. Waiata in all forms is used well to teach language through direction, following instructions and creative expression. Kaiako skills and expertise in performance provide quality learning experiences for the children. Children perform waiata well and their understanding is clearly demonstrated through appropriate actions, facial expression and body positioning.

Structure in use of language is encouraged.Kaimahi use whole sentences and children respond in whole sentences. They also use repetition and capitalise on meaningful experiences to create, encourage and extend children’s conversations. Children take risks with language learning as they attempt to communicate with each other. Kaimahi acknowledge children’s endeavours. The majority of children are good speakers of te reo Māori. Te reo Māori is developing well.

Whanaungatanga.Kaimahi work well as a team. They are prepared and organised for their various roles and responsibilities. Meaningful interactions and connections to whānau and children foster a sense whanaungatanga and purpose. Kaimahi model caring behaviour. Whānau have high expectations of children’s proficiency in te reo and learning and share these with kaimahi at whānau hui. The whānau benefits from the individual skills and expertise of kaimahi who provide a happy medium for their children in their attractive and well maintained environment. Children are caring and supported well by kaimahi and whānau.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent, to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management.The whānau manages the kōhanga reo effectively. Regular attendance at hui provides whānau with opportunities to share information, make decisions and discuss children’s progress and successes. Regular reporting about finances, personnel and health and safety allows whānau to monitor these areas and where necessary review systems and procedures. The whānau is well informed about kōhanga reo operations.

Strategic planning.A strategic plan is prepared and covers a four year period. Strategic actions and desired outcomes are guided by key performance indicators. Strategic actions which align to te reo Māori,Te Korowai, whānau kaupapa, and self review practices are major focus areas. A high priority includes developing kaimahi skills. The strategic plan provides clear direction for whānau.

Self review.Self review is evident throughout the kōhanga reo operations. Kaimahi regularly reflect on and critique their daily practice. Information gathered and reported to whānau hui shows consistent self review. Policies and procedures for review are identified. The whānau actively engages in self review as a way of continuing to move forward.

Planning.Planning is displayed and easy to follow. Long and short term overviews are prepared. Whānau have contributed ideas and suggestions to planning. The kaupapa framework covers seasons, atua and associated activities including special events. All the essential elements of kaupapa planning are included. Kaimahi provide for their specific age groups and learning needs. Planning is comprehensive and caters well for the varying needs of children.

Assessment.Children’s profile books highlight their involvement in the learning programme. Their progress is discussed regularly. Kaimahi use anecdotal notes, observations and developmental checklists to gather information about children’s learning and progress. They also collect kaupapa art samples. Children’s profile books and workbooks are accessible to whānau at all times. The successful transition of children to school is a priority for whānau and kaimahi.

Evaluation.Reflective practice about children’s learning and the programme has effected change. Kaimahi identify individual areas for improving their delivery and take appropriate action. Kaimahi continually strive for improvement to bring about positive changes for their children.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
