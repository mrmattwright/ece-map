Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Whanaungatanga ki te tāone o Kirikiriroa, ki tētahi wāhi kura o mua. Ka tino tautokona ngā tamariki kia ako rātou i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki a rātou anō, ā, i ētahi kē atu. Ka whakanuia, ā, ka āta tiakina hoki i ngā tamariki. Ka arotahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea o ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou mahi katoa. Tērā ka hoki mai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōhanga Reo o Whanaungatanga i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Whanaungatanga ki Kirikiriroa, ki tētahi wāhi kura o mua. He mārama te tirohanga ā te whānau, ā, e noho ngākau nui ana rātou ki te oranga tonutanga o te reo Māori me ōna tikanga, tae atu ki ngā tino uaratanga, ngā tikanga, me ngā kawa hoki o Tainui. Ka arotahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki matea o ngā tamariki, i roto i ā rātou mahi katoa.

Ka whakaratohia ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi, wheako hoki e whakawhānui ake ana i tō rātou mōhio ki a rātou anō me te hapori whānui e noho nei rātou. Ka ākina, ka tautokona hoki ngā mātua rangatahi kia ako ngātahi rātou ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki i ngā tūmomo reo, me te rerekētanga o ngā ahurea hoki. E ako ana rātou ko wai rātou, ā, kei te noho maioha rātou ki ngā āhuatanga motuhake o tēnā me tēnā.

Ka kitea te manaaki, te atawhai, me te aroha o ngā kaimahi mō ngā tamariki. Ka āta whakarite rātou i te whai pānga o ngā mahere me ngā taumahi ki ngā matea o ngā tamariki katoa. Ka noho pūmau tētahi rōpū o ngā mema rangatahi o te whānau ki te hāpai i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo i ia rā.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te mōhio o te tamaiti ki a ia anō, ki ētahi atu anō hoki?

He ākonga māia ngā tamariki, ā, e ngākau nui ana ki te ako i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki a rātou anō, ki ētahi atu hoki.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka whai, ka aro atu hoki ngā tamariki ki ngā kōrero akiaki, ngā tohutohu hoki a te whānau me ngā kaimahi mā te reo Māori. Ko te reo Māori anake e whakamahia ana ki te taha o ngā tamariki, puta noa i te rā. Kua tīmata ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā mihimihi, ngā pepeha, me ngā waiata, ā, he kaupapa pārekareka ēnei. Ka āhei ngā tamariki te whakahuahua, te whai hoki i ētahi o ngā waiata ā-ringa. Ka whakamana ngā kaimahi i ngā whakamātau a ngā tamariki i roto i te reo, ā, ka whakatauira anō hoki i ngā hanganga e tika ana. Ka kitea te koa, te hari anō hoki o ngā tamariki, ā, ka ngākau nui rātou ki te whai wāhi atu.

Ka harikoa ngā tamariki ki te āhua o te whanaungatanga me ngā taunekenekehanga hoki. Ka whakanuia ngā kaiako i te whanonga pai o ngā tamariki. He pakari te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki ngā kaimahi me ētahi atu e pā tata atu ana. He whai wāhitanga mō te whānau ki te ako me te tākaro ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki.

Ka ngahau ngā tamariki mā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi whakaongaonga o roto, o waho hoki. Ka ākina, ka hāpaitia hoki rātou e ngā kaimahi, kia tūhura, kia whakatewhatewha hoki i tō rātou taiao. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki a rātou anō, me ngā mahi a ō rātou tinana ki ngā kaupapa korikori. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te tūhura haere i tō rātou taiao.

He pakari ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo, ā, he tau rātou ki te taha o ētahi atu. Kua āta whakaritea ngā mahinga o ia rā. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ngā wāhi me te āhua o te mahi hei whāinga mā rātou. Ka pupū ake tō rātou koa, ā, he tākare rātou ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā kaupapa i te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E whakawhirinaki ana ngā tamariki i te reo Pākehā hei reo kōrero ki waenga i a rātou anō. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, me whakawhanake i ngā rautaki hei hāpai i te āheinga o ngā tamariki ki te kōrerorero me te whakawhiti whakaaro mā te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ko te tirohanga matawhānui o te tūtohinga me te mahere rautaki e ārahi ana i te whānau. He mārama ngā whāinga me ngā tūmanako, ā, he tino aronga ki ngā tamariki, hei ārahi i ngā whakahaeretanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. Ka ārahi ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga i ngā whakaritenga a ngā kaimahi me te whānau.

Ka whai pūtake ngā mahere, ā, ka whai hua ki te āta ārahi i te hōtaka akoranga. Kua āta whakaritea e ngā kaimahi i te whakamahinga o tētahi tukanga e pā ana ki te aromātai hōtaka. He mārama te tukanga a ngā kaimahi mō te aromatawai me te tuhi haere anō hoki i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i aua mōhiohio hei whakatau i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te whakapaitanga ake o te hōtaka, hei whakatutuki ake anō hoki i ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki katoa.

E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki e tētahi whānau e noho pūmau ana ki te tautoko i te kōhanga reo i ia rā. Ka kitea te whanaungatanga, ā, ka whakanuia tēnei e ngā mema o te whānau me ngā kaimahi. E mōhio ana te whānau, e tika ana kia mātātoa ai te whai wāhi mai o ngā mema katoa o te whānau, ā, e whakamātau tonu ana i ngā tūmomo huarahi ki te whakatutuki i tēnei whāinga.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kei te whanake tonu ngā mahi ki te pūrongo i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki ki te whānau. Ahakoa ka whakaemihia ngā mōhiohio e whai hua ana, kāhore aua mōhiohio e whakapuakihia ana ki te taha o te whānau i ia te wā.

Kāhore anō kia oti ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi me ngā kirimana a ngā kaimahi katoa o tēnei wā.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Hei whakapai ake i āna whakaritenga, me tahuri te whānau ki te:

  1. whakarite i te arohaehae a ngā kaimahi katoa i ia tau
     [R47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA7 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  2. whakarite i te whakatutukitanga i ia te wā o ngā akoako hōneatanga ki te taha o ngā tamariki inā he ahi, he rū whenua rānei, ā, kia tuhia aua akoako 
    [R 46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS8 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  3. whakarite i te whakatutukitanga o ngā arowhai a ngā pirihimana i ngā tāngata katoa e mahi ana ki te taha o ngā tamariki. 
    [R47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA12 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Whanaungatanga ki te tāone o Kirikiriroa, ki tētahi wāhi kura o mua. Ka tino tautokona ngā tamariki kia ako rātou i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki a rātou anō, ā, i ētahi kē atu. Ka whakanuia, ā, ka āta tiakina hoki i ngā tamariki. Ka arotahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea o ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou mahi katoa. Tērā ka hoki mai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Whanaungatanga is in Hamilton city on a vacant school. Children are well supported to learn about themselves and others. Children are respected and well cared for. Whānau and kaimahi focus on the needs of children in all of their work. ERO is likely to return in three years.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Whanaungatanga will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Whanaungatanga is located in Hamilton on a vacant school site. The whānau have a clear vision and are committed to the survival of te reo Māori me ōna tikanga including the core values, tikanga and kawa of Tainui. Whānau and kaimahi focus on the needs of children in all of their work.

Children are provided with a range of activities and experiences that broaden their sense of who they are and the wider community in which they live. Young parents are encouraged and supported to learn alongside their children. Children are exposed to different languages and cultures. They are learning about who they are, and appreciating differences in others.

Kaimahi show care, affection and aroha for the children. They ensure that planning and activities are based on the needs of all children. A committed group of younger whānau members support kōhanga operations on a daily basis.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children know about themselves and others?

Children are confident learners who enjoy learning about themselves and others.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children can follow and respond to prompts and directions from whānau and kaimahi in te reo Māori. Te reo Māori is spoken constantly throughout the day. Children enjoy and are beginning to participate in mihimihi, whakapapa, pepeha and waiata. Some children can mimic and follow waiata ā ringa. Kaimahi respect children’s attempts at language and model correct structures. Children show joy, are generally happy and keen to participate.

Children enjoy positive relationships and interactions. Kaimahi use praise to acknowledge children's good behaviours. Children confidently engage in discussions with kaimahi and others around them. Whānau have opportunities to learn and play alongside their children.

Children have fun with a range of stimulating indoor and outdoor activities. They are encouraged and supported by kaimahi to explore and investigate their environment. Children learn about themselves and how their body responds to physical activity. Children enjoy exploring their environment.

Children are confident in their kōhanga reo and comfortable around others. Daily routines are well established. Children know where to go and what to do. They express joy and are keen to participate in kōhanga reo activities.

Key Next Steps

Children often rely on English as a means of communication with each other. Kaimahi recognise the need to develop strategies to encourage children's ability to converse and communicate in te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

The whānau is guided by a well constructed and comprehensive charter and strategic plan. There are clear goals and objectives with a strong focus on children to guide all kōhanga reo operations. Policies and procedures guide kaimahi and whānau practice.

Planning is purposeful and effectively guides the learning programme. Kaimahi has established a formal programme evaluation process. They also have a clear process for assessing and recording children's learning. Kaimahi use this information to make decisions about improving the programme to better meet the learning needs of all children.

Children are well supported by a committed whānau who support the kōhanga reo on a daily basis. Whanaungatanga is evident and valued by whānau members and kaimahi. Whānau are aware of the need to actively engage all whānau members and are continuing to try different ways to achieve this aim.

Key Next Steps

Reporting to whānau about children’s learning is still developing. While useful information is gathered it is not shared regularly with whānau.

Current job descriptions and contracts for all staff are incomplete.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

In order to improve practice the whānau must:

  1. ensure that all staff are appraised annually [R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA7 Licensing criteria for kōhanga reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]
  2. ensure that fire and earthquake drills are carried out with children and recorded on a regular basis [R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS8 Licensing criteria for kōhanga reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]
  3. ensure that police vet checks are carried out on all people who work with children. [R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA12 Licensing criteria for kōhanga reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Whanaungatanga is in Hamilton city on a vacant school. Children are well supported to learn about themselves and others. Children are respected and well cared for. Whānau and kaimahi focus on the needs of children in all of their work. ERO is likely to return in three years.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Whanaungatanga will be in three years.
