1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Heti ki Castlecliff, ā, ka whakarato i tētahi taiao akoranga o te reo Māori mō te hapori whānui o Whanganui.  E whakapūmau ana te whānau ki te tupu tonu, te whakawhanaketanga tonu hoki hei painga mō ngā tamariki.  Kua roa nei ngā kaumātua e whai wāhi ana ki te kōhanga reo.  Ka hāpai rātou, ka ngākau nui hoki rātou ki te whakapūmau i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo me ngā āhuatanga motuhake e hāngai pū ana ki Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Heti.  Kua āta whakawhanakehia te whare me ngā rawa, ā, e whakarato ana mō te whānuitanga o ngā whai wāhitanga ako, wheako akoranga anō hoki. 

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Tangata te kaupapa arotahi, ā, ko te pātai aromātai e mea ana,

Ka pēhea te whai huatanga ki te hāpai i ngā tamariki, kia whanake ai tō rātou waiora, tō rātou tuakiri ahurea hoki?

Ka whai hua te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Heti ki te whakatairanga i te waiora me te tuakiri ahurea o ngā tamariki. 

Ko te oranga me te waiora o te whānau me ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o te whakahaeretanga o te whānau me ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te kōhanga reo.  Ka poipoia, ka hāpaitia hoki ngā tamariki me te whānau mā te tino whanaungatanga.  Ko ngā wheako akoranga e hāngai ana ki Te Whāriki o te Kōhanga Reo, e whakapūmau ana i te reo, ngā tikanga, ngā taonga tuku iho o te rohe, me ngā whakapono.  Ka tino whai hua ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau nā te whai wāhi atu, te kite ā-kanohi anō hoki i ia te wā ki ngā tūmomo kaupapa o te rohe.  Ko ētahi o aua kaupapa ka whakaritea e te purapura, ko ētahi hoki he haerenga ki ngā marae o te wāhi nei, hei whakapūmau i ngā taonga tuku iho.  He whānui ngā wheako e whakaratohia ana mā ngā tamariki, kia ako ai rātou i tō rātou tuakiri, me tō rātou tū hei tangata whenua.  Ka tino ākina anō hoki te hauora me te waiora. 

Ka whai hua te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ō rātou wawata e hāngai ana ki te tupu tonu me te tino waiora anō hoki o te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, ngā kaimahi, te whānau, me ngā tamariki.  Ko te whakapūmau tonu te whakapai tonutanga puta noa i te kōhanga reo, e whakatairanga ana i te waiora, te tuakiri, me ngā taonga tuku iho o ngā tamariki me te whānau.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau.  I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga reo (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo. 

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Tangata 

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga.  Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki 
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai. 

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Tangata

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whai huatanga ki te hāpai i ngā tamariki kia whanake ai tō rātou waiora, tō rātou tuakiri ahurea anō hoki?

Ka whai hua te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Heti ki te whakatairanga i te waiora me te tuakiri ahurea o ngā tamariki me tō rātou whānau. 

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whanaungatanga.  Ka whai wāhi motuhake ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo.  Ka hāpai ngā wheako akoranga i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i ō rātou hononga ki te whānau me te ao whānui.  Ka whakapūmautia te whanaungatanga puta noa i te rā, mā te karakia ngātahi me ngā tūmomo waiata o te rohe e ū ana i ō rātou hononga ā-whakapapa ki te whenua me ngā taonga tuku iho.  Ko ngā matea whatumanawa, ngā matea ā-tinana hoki o ngā tamariki e āta aroturukitia ana, e āta whakatutukihia anō hoki e ngā kaimahi.  He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e manaakitia ana, e arohaina ana rātou. 

Te taiao.  Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga e whakatairanga ana i tō rātou ahurea tuku iho me tō rātou tuakiri anō hoki hei tangata whenua.  He maha ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i ngā taonga o te taiao me ngā taonga Māori e whai hononga nui ana ki a rātou.  Ka whakatairanga, ka whakawhānui hoki ngā haerenga i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.  Kei te whanake haere te tū pakari o ngā tamariki ki te ao, tō rātou tū anō hoki hei tangata whenua. 

Te reo Māori.  Ka tino tautokona te whakawhanaketanga reo Māori o ngā tamariki.  Ko ngā rautaki e whakamahia ana e ngā kaimahi hei whakatairanga i te reo Māori, ko ngā karakia me ngā waiata o ia rā.  Ko ngā waiata o te wāhi nei e whakapūmau ana i ngā taonga tuku iho a ngā tamariki, te reo ā-iwi, ngā hononga ā-whakapapa hoki.  Nō Te Āti Haunui-a-Pāpārangi te nuinga o ngā kaimahi, ā, he tino mōhiotanga tō rātou, he mātanga hoki rātou ki te reo Māori e hāngai pū ana ki ngā hapū me ngā marae o tēnei  iwi.  Ka aro nui ngā tamariki ki te reo e whakamahia ana e ngā kaimahi, ā, e whakawhanake ana i tō rātou pakari ki te whakautu mā te reo Māori. 

Te whakahaeretanga, te whai wāhi atu hoki a te whānau.  E tino tautokona ana, e tino whakatutukihia ana ngā tamariki ki te mātauranga me te atawhai e whakaratohia ana e te whānau mātātoa, me tō rātou kaha ki te arotahi, te whai wāhi atu hoki.  Ko te waiora o ngā tamariki me te whānau te aronga i te whānau e whakatau ana i ngā whakaritenga.  He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo.  Kua āta whakaritea, kua āta māramahia hoki ngā pūnaha me ngā whakaritenga.  Ko ngā whakawhitinga kōrero i ia te wā, me te whai wāhi hoki a te whānau ki te kōhanga reo, e whakatairanga ana i te aronga ngātahi mō meāke nei, me te whakaaro ngātahi anō hoki ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga. 

Te wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te tūhura auaha, te tūhura takitahi.  E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, e tika ana kia maha ake ngā whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te tūhura me te mahi auaha.  Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga aua whakapaitanga ki te hōtaka, i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, nā tēnei ka tūhonohono ā rātou ake akoranga ki ngā kaupapa i whakamaheretia ai. 

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whai huatanga o te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Heti ki te whakatairanga i te waiora me te tuakiri ahurea o ngā tamariki mā ā rātou whakaritenga whakahaere?

E whakapiki ake ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Heti i tō rātou āheinga ki te whakamahere hōtaka, te arotake whaiaro hoki, kia pai ake ai te aroturuki, te whakatutuki hoki i ngā āhuatanga e whai pānga ana ki te waiora me te tuakiri ahurea o ngā tamariki. 

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te aronga rautaki a te whānau.  Ko ngā matea o ngā tamariki me ngā wawata o te whānau mō te waiora me te tuakiri, ka noho hei tūāpapa mō te mahere rautaki a te whānau o te kōhanga reo.  Ko ētahi o ngā whāinga rautaki i āta whakatutukihia ai e te whānau, ko:

  • te whakawhanake ake i ngā rawa kia nui ake ai ngā whai wāhitanga ako o waho me ngā whakaurutanga ki te kōhanga reo
  • te whakawhanaketanga o te māra kai me ngā rākau Māori hei hāpai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki
  • te tokomaha ake o ngā kaimahi
  • te tūhonohono atu ki ngā ratonga hauora mō te hauora me te waiora o ngā tamariki me te whānau. 

He māramatanga ngātahi tō te whānau ki ā rātou kaupapa rautaki matua, me te arotahi o ēnā ki te tupu tonu, puta noa i te kōhanga reo.  Ka whakapau kaha rātou katoa ki te whakatutuki i ā rātou whāinga mō te wā poto, me te wā roa. 

Te wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Te whakapakari i te arotake whaiaro.  E mōhio ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi, e tika ana kia whakapai ake rātou i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro, pērā i te whakaemi, te tuhi, me te pūrongo i ngā mōhiohio o te arotake whaiaro.  Ko te whakapai ake i te arotake whaiaro, ka whakarato i te whānuitanga ake o ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki te whai pānga o tēnā e mahia ana e rātou mā ngā tamariki, me ngā wāhanga anō hoki hei whakapai ake. 

Ko ētahi wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake, ko:

  • te whakamahere hōtaka me te aromātai
  • te aromatawai
  • te whakamahinga o te reo Māori
  • te whakawhanaketanga ngaio mō ngā kaiako me ngā kaimahi.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.  I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau;
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga;
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi;
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino);
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho);
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi; me
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, ā, e whakaae ana hoki te whānau, kia:

  • whakawhanake te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā mahi arotake whaiaro e pakari ake ana, ā, e whakatairanga ana hoki i te kounga o te aroturuki me te pūrongo, hei hāpai anō hoki i te whakapaitanga me ngā putanga akoranga o te kounga kairangi mā ngā tamariki. 

6 Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru. 

Lynda Pura Watson
Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu mō ngā Ratonga Arotake Māori (Te Uepū ā-Motu)

18 Pipiri 2014 

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Heti is situated in Castlecliff and provides a te reo Māori learning environment for the Whanganui community. Whānau are committed to ongoing growth and development to benefit children. Long serving kaumatua are involved in the kōhanga reo. They support and are committed to preserving the kōhanga reo kaupapa and the unique characteristics of Te Heti Kōhanga Reo. Well developed premises and facilities provide for a wide variety of learning opportunities and experiences.

The whānau focus area is mana tangata and the evaluation question is:

How effectively are children supported to develop their wellbeing and cultural identity?

The whānau and kaimahi of Te Heti Kōhanga Reo effectively promote children’s wellbeing and cultural identity.

Whānau and children’s wellness and wellbeing is at the heart of whānau management and kaimahi practice at the kōhanga. Children and whānau are nurtured and supported through strong whanaungatanga. Learning experiences based on Te Whāriki o te Kōhanga Reo reinforce language, customs, local traditions and beliefs. Children and their whānau benefit greatly from the regular involvement and exposure to activities in the area. These include events organised by the purapura and visits to local marae that reinforce ancestral heritage. A wide range of experiences are provided for children to learn about their identity and their place as tangata whenua. Health and wellbeing is actively encouraged.

The whānau successfully realise their aspirations which are focussed on the continual growth and positive wellbeing of the kōhanga kaupapa, kaimahi, whānau and children. This sustained focus on continual improvement across the kōhanga reo, enhances wellbeing, identity and the cultural heritage of children and whānau.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Tangata

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

3 Findings

Mana Tangata

Background

How effectively are children supported to develop wellbeing and their cultural identity?

The whānau and kaimahi of Te Heti Kōhanga Reo effectively promote the wellbeing and cultural identity of children and their whānau.

Areas of good performance

Whānaungatanga. Children have a special place within their kōhanga reo. Learning experiences support children to develop their connections with whānau and the wider world. Whānaungatanga is reinforced throughout the day through shared karakia and local waiata that reinforces their kinship ties and connections to the whenua and their ancestral heritage. Children’s emotional and physical needs are well monitored and responded to by kaimahi. Children are happy and settled in a caring and loving environment.

The environment. Children are immersed in learning that promotes their cultural heritage and identity as tangata whenua. There are many opportunities for children to explore the natural resources and Māori artefacts that are significant to them. Outings are used to enhance and extend children's learning. Children are developing confidence about who they are in the world and as tangata whenua.

Te reo Māori. Children’s te reo Māori development is well supported. Strategies used by kaimahi to promote te reo Māori include; daily karakia and waiata. Local waiata reinforces children’s ancestral heritage, dialect and whakapapa connections. Most kaimahi affiliate to Te Ati Haunui ā Pāparangi and bring with them knowledge of and expertise in te reo Māori that is specific to the hapū and marae of this iwi. Children are receptive to the language used by kaimahi and are developing confidence to respond in te reo Māori.

Whānau management and involvement. Children are well supported and served in the education and care provided by a proactive, focused and involved whānau. The wellbeing of children and whānau is the focus when whānau make decisions. Whānau manage their kōhanga reo well. Systems and practices are well established and understood. Regular communication and whānau involvement in the kōhanga reo promotes a shared future direction, planning, management and decision making. 

Area for improvement

Creative and independent exploration. Kaimahi recognise the need to give children more opportunities to explore and be creative. These improvements in the programme should enhance learning for children, and enable them to make their own personal learning connections to planned kaupapa.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How effectively does Te Heti Kōhanga Reo whānau promote children’s wellbeing and cultural identity through their management practices?

The whānau and kaimahi of Te Heti Kōhanga Reo are increasing their programme planning and self review capacity to better monitor and respond to factors affecting children’s sense of wellbeing and cultural identity.

Areas of good performance

Whānau strategic direction. Children’s needs and whānau aspirations about wellbeing and identity form the basis of the kōhanga reo whānau strategic plan. Strategic goals which whānau have successfully achieved include:

  • further development of property for increased outdoor learning opportunities and increased enrolments
  • the development of maara kai and native trees to support children’s learning
  • increased staffing
  • networking with health agencies for children and whānau health and wellbeing.

The whānau have a shared understanding of their strategic priorities that focus on continual growth across the kōhanga reo. They work hard together to realise their short and long term goals.

Areas for development

Strengthening self review. Whānau and kaimahi recognise the need to improve self review practices such as gathering, recording and reporting self review information. Improved self review will provide whānau with more information about the impact of what they are doing for children and where improvement needs to be made.

Areas that require further development include:

  • programme planning and evaluation
  • assessment
  • the use of te reo Māori
  • kaiako and kaimahi professional development.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Licensed Kōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends, and the whānau agrees:

  • whānau and kaimahi develop stronger self review practices that raises the quality of monitoring and reporting in order to support improvement and high quality learning outcomes for children.

6 When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
