2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Namu ki te tāone o Ōpunake, ki Taranaki. Ko Ngāti Tamarongo te hapū, ā, e tū ana te kōhanga reo ki te taha o Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Tamarongo. E ai ki te whānau, he tata te hapori o Ōpunake, ā, he pai tā rātou mahi ngātahi kia ū tonu ai te pai o te noho ā-hapori.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Aotūroa te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

Ka tino tautokona ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i tā rātou kōrero i te reo Māori. He matatau te kaiako ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, ā, ka whakapuaki ia i ōna mōhiotanga ki te reo ā-iwi o te kāinga ki te taha o ngā tamariki me te whānau. He mārama ngā tohutohu ki ngā tamariki, ā, ka aro, ka whakautu hoki rātou i ngā pātai me ngā akiaki. He māhorahora tā rātou whakamahinga i te reo Māori i a rātou e tuku mihi ana. He mea nui kia mārama hoki ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā huarahi ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori. Me whakawhānui te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga ka rongo, ka whakamahi hoki ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki te hōtaka akoranga, ā, ki ngā taumahi hoki a ngā tamariki ki tua atu i te kōhanga reo.

E whakarato ana te taiao akoranga i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura haere. Ko te whānuitanga o ngā āhuatanga motuhake i te hapori, tae atu ki tētahi whīra nui, me ngā whakairo kōhatu e whakatairanga ana i te ngākau nui me te pākiki. He pai ngā rauemi i te kōhanga reo, hei hāpai i ngā ngākau nuitanga e pupū ake ana, e tupu ake ana hoki.

Ka poipoi, ka whakakoia hoki ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā pākeke me ngā tamariki. Ka aro wawe ngā tamariki ki ngā kōrero tohutohu. He pai tā rātou tākaro ngātahi, ā, ka akiaki hoki rātou i ētahi atu ki te whai wāhi ki ngā tākaro. He pakari te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo.

He matawhānui te kohinga mahere hōtaka ā te kaiako, ā, ka whakaatuhia ēnei hei whakamōhio i te whānau. Ka aroturukitia te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Kua toro atu te kaiako ki ngā akoranga ngaio, hei whakawhanake, hei whakapakari anō hoki i āna whakaritenga aromatawai.

He pai tētahi rōpū matua o ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. Ka whai hua tā rātou whakahaeretanga i te kōhanga reo, ā, ka akiaki rātou i ētahi atu o te whānau ki te whai wāhi mai hoki. He mea nui, kia tokomaha ake ngā mema o te whānau ka whai wāhi ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He maha ngā whakaritenga nui ka tukua ki taua rōpū matua o te whānau hei whakatau. Ka pā tōraro tēnei ki te kawenga takohanga, te noho mārama, me te ū tonutanga o te kōhanga reo.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Namu kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Namu.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Aotūroa.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Aotūroa

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Aotūroa te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te whānuitanga ki te whakatutuki i te ako me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki mā ngā wheako akoranga o te Mana Aotūroa.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te reo Māori.Ka hāpai te hōtaka akoranga i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. He matatau te kaiako ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, ā, ka whakapuakihia e ia te reo ā-iwi o te hapori ki ngā tamariki me te whānau. I te hōtaka ōkawa ka whakapuaki ngā tamariki i ō rātou pepeha, ka ako i ngā rā o te wiki, ka waiata, ā, ka taki karakia. Ka patapatai, ka akiaki hoki te kaiako i ngā tamariki. Ka whakatauira ia i ngā hanganga reo tika mā ngā tamariki. Ka mārama ngā tamariki ki ngā tohutohu, ā, ka aro hoki rātou ki ngā akiaki. He māhorahora tā rātou whakamahinga i te reo Māori ki te whakamihi atu ki ngā tāngata. E tīmata ana rātou ki te whakamahi i ngā rerenga kōrero me ngā kupu māmā, hei whakapuaki i ā rātou kōrero.

Ngā kaupapa.Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā tino mōhiotanga e pā ana ki te ngahere. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi e whakamahi ana i ngā taonga i te ngahere. Ka kōrero rātou mō ngā tūmomo wāhanga o te rākau, ā, ka whakamahi rātou i aua rauemi māori ki ā rātou mahi toi. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o te ngahere, ā, ka whakapuaki i ā rātou akoranga ki ētahi atu.

Te tūhura i te taiao akoranga.Kei te taiao akoranga ngā rauemi ka hāpai i ngā tamariki ki te tūhura haere. Kua whakaritea e te whānau o te kōhanga reo tētahi uru huarākau mō ngā tamariki me tō rātou whānau. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te ako i te whakatupu rākau hua hei kai. Ka tiaki ngā mema o te whānau i ngā rākau hei akoranga, hei pātaka kai, ā, kia ako ai hoki ngā tamariki ki te hangarau. Ko te taiao tō rātou tūrangawaewae. He kōhatu hoki i whakairotia ai ki ngā tohu Māori. He whānui hoki te whīra me te pai o ngā piki, ngā heke hoki hei tūhura mā ngā tamariki. Ka whakatewhatewha ngā tamariki i te hōhonutanga o ngā momo taonga ki te taiao, huri noa i te kōhanga reo.

Ngā taunekeneke.Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi me te whānau i tētahi taiao mauritau, haumaru hoki mō ngā tamariki. Ka poipoi, ka whakakoia hoki ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā pākeke me ngā tamariki. Ka kitea te māia o te kaiako, ā, ka whai pānga hoki tēnei ki te waiora o ngā tamariki me te whakawhirinaki atu a ngā mātua ki ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo.

E noho mārama ana ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmanako i āta tāutuhia ai mō te whanonga. Ka aro wawe rātou ki ngā tohutohu. Ka atawhai, ka tautoko hoki ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā tamariki. He pai te tākaro ngātahi a ngā tamariki, ā, ka akiaki i ētahi atu ki te whai wāhi mai. He pakari te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te whakapiki ake i te whānuitanga o te reo Māori e kōrerohia ana ki ngā tamariki.Ko te kaiako te tino puna o te reo Māori e rangona ana e ngā tamariki. Ka ākina ngā tamariki kia mārama ai rātou ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki e whakamahi ana i te reo Māori. Me whakamahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga kia rongo ai, kia whakamahi ai hoki ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki te hōtaka akoranga, ki ngā taumahi hoki a ngā tamariki ki tua atu o te kōhanga reo. Me whakanui ake ngā whai wāhitanga i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero a tētahi ki tētahi, i ngā kōrerorero hoki i ngā rōpū iti. Ka taea hoki e te whānau te reo Māori te whakanui mā te whakapūmau kia reo Māori anake te taiao.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai ki ngā kōhanga reo, hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau.He pai te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā. Ko ngā tino kaiwhakahaere o te kōhanga reo, ko tētahi tino rōpū o ngā mema o te whānau kua roa ngā tau e whai wāhi atu ki te kōhanga reo. He pai tā rātou whakahaeretanga i te kōhanga reo, ā, ka akiaki hoki rātou i ētahi atu mema o te whānau ki te whai wāhi mai. Nō nā tata tonu nei te whānau i utu ai i tētahi nama nui, ā, e āhei ana rātou i nāianei ki te arotahi ki ngā whakapaitanga mō te kōhanga reo. Kua tata rātou ki te whiwhi raihana anō, i raro i ngā herenga ā-ture o te tau 2008.

Te whakamahere hōtaka.He kohinga matawhānui tā te kaiako o ngā mahere hōtaka, ā, ka whakaatuhia ēnei hei whakamōhio i te whānau. Kua tāutuhia ngā whāinga akoranga, ā, ka hono ngā mahere ki Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Ka tino hono hoki ēnei ki ngā akoranga e whakapuakihia ana ki te hōtaka.

Te aromatawai.He tika te aroturuki i te ahunga whakamua me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki. Kua toro atu te kaiako ki ngā akoranga ngaio hei whakawhanake, hei whakapakari hoki i āna mahi aromatawai. Kua tuhi haere ia i āna kitenga e pā ana ki ia tamaiti, ā, ka hono ēnei ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo whanonga, āhua hoki o ngā tamariki Māori.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te whai wāhitanga a te whānau ki te whakahaeretanga. He mea nui kia whai wāhi ake te whānau ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He maha ngā whakaritenga nui e whakataungia noatia ana e tētahi rōpū iti o te whānau, engari ko te tikanga kē, mā te whānau katoa ēnei mahi. Ka pā tōraro tēnei ki te kawenga takohanga, te māramatanga, me te noho pūmau hoki o te kōhanga reo. E tika ana, kia noho ngātahi te whānau ki te:

  • whakatau i ngā whāinga rautaki i te kōhanga reo
  • whakarite kia whakaae ngātahitia ai ngā take pūtea hei painga mō te kōhanga reo me ngā kaimahi
  • whakatutuki i ngā arohaehae kaimahi, kia tōtika ai te tautoko i ngā kaimahi
  • arotake i ia te wā ā rātou whakaritenga, hei tāutu i ngā whakapaitanga, hei whakanui i ngā āhuatanga angitu, ā, hei tautoko anō hoki i te whakawhanaketanga o tō rātou kōhanga reo ā meāke nei.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Namu is in the Taranaki town of Opunake. The local hapū is Tamarongo and the kōhanga reo is situated alongside Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Tamarongo. The whānau states that Opunake is a close knit community who work well together to maintain a healthy community spirit.

The whānau chose Mana Aotūroa as the focus area for this review.

Children are well supported as developing speakers of te reo Māori. The kaiako is a fluent speaker of te reo Māori and shares her knowledge of the local dialect with children and the whānau. Children understand instructions and respond to questions and prompts. They use te reo Māori spontaneously when greeting people. It is also important that children are encouraged to understand that te reo Maori is a language used for a wide range of purposes. Whānau and kaimahi need to extend the opportunities children have to hear and use te reo Māori in the learning programme and in children’s activities outside the kōhanga reo.

The learning environment provides opportunities for children to explore. A variety of local features including a large field and stone carvings promote interest and curiosity. The kōhanga reo is well resourced to support emerging and growing interests.

Relationships between adults and children are nurturing and affirming. Children respond quickly to instruction and direction. They play well together and encourage others to participate. Children have a strong sense of belonging to their kōhanga reo.

The kaiako has a comprehensive set of programme plans which are displayed to inform whānau. Children’s progress and development is monitored. The kaiako has sought professional learning to develop and strengthen her assessment practice.

The kōhanga reo is well managed by a core group of whānau members. They run the kōhanga reo efficiently and try to encourage other whānau to participate. It is important that more whānau become involved in the management of the kōhanga reo. Many important decisions that the whānau should make together are left to a core group of whānau members. This impacts on accountability, transparency and sustainability of the kōhanga reo.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga report is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga report again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Namu was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Namu.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Aotūroa.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

4 Findings

Mana Aotūroa

Background

The whānau chose Mana Aotūroa as the focus area for this review. ERO evaluates the extent to which children’s learning and development is catered for through Mana Aotūroa learning experiences.

Areas of good performance

Te Reo Māori.The learning programme supports children’s te reo Māori development. The kaiako is a fluent speaker of te reo Māori and shares the language of the local iwi with children and the whānau. In the formal programme, children share their pepeha, learn the days of the week, sing waiata and recite karakia. The kaiako spends time questioning and prompting children. She models correct language structures for children to follow. Children understand instructions and respond to prompts. They use te reo Māori spontaneously when greeting people. Children are developing as speakers of te reo Māori. They are beginning to use simple sentences and words to relay their wishes.

Kaupapa.Children learn appropriate knowledge about the forest and bush. Children are involved in activities that use resources from the bush. They talk about the different parts of the tree and use these natural resources in their art work. Children are knowledgeable about aspects of the bush and share their learning with others.

Exploration of the learning environment. The learning environment contains resources to support children’s exploration. Kōhanga reo whānau have developed an orchard for children and their whānau. Children have opportunities to learn about growing fruit trees for food. Whānau members take care of the trees as a learning and food resource and for children to learn about recycling. The immediate environment is their turangawaewae. It contains stone carvings that are relevant cultural symbols. There is also a wide expanse of field with interesting contours that children are able to explore. Children investigate the richly resourced environment that surrounds their kōhanga reo.

Interactions.Kaimahi and whānau provide a positive and secure environment for children. Relationships between adults and children are nurturing and affirming. The kaiako portrays confidence which influences the children’s wellbeing and parent’s trust in the kōhanga reo kaimahi.

Children understand the well defined behaviour expectations. They respond quickly to instruction and direction. Peer relationships are warm and supportive. Children play well together and encourage others to participate. Adults watch children and quickly tend to their needs as required. Children have a strong sense of belonging to their kōhanga reo.

Areas for development

Increasing the variety of te reo Māori spoken to children.The kaiako is the prime source from which children hear te reo Māori. Children are encouraged to understand that te reo Maori is a language used for a wide range of purposes. Whānau and kaimahi need to extend the opportunities children have to hear and use te reo Māori in the learning programme and in children’s activities outside the kōhanga reo. Opportunities should allow for more conversations on a one to one or small group basis. Whānau can also acknowledge the importance of te reo Maori by maintaining a te reo Maori only environment.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management. The day-to day operations are well managed. A core group of whānau members, who have been involved in the kōhanga reo for a number of years, are the key managers of the kōhanga reo. They manage the kōhanga reo efficiently and try to encourage other whānau to participate. The kōhanga reo whānau has recently paid off a large debt and is now able to focus on improvements for the kōhanga reo. They are close to being relicensed under the 2008 regulations.

Programme planning. The kaiako has a comprehensive set of programme plans which are displayed to inform whānau. Learning goals are identified and plans link to Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. They also link closely to learning delivered in the programme.

Assessment.Children’s progress and development is appropriately monitored. The kaiako has sought professional learning to develop and strengthen her assessment practice. She has noted individual observations about each child, linked to a range of behaviours and temperaments of Māori children.

Areas for development

Whānau involvement in management. It is important that more whānau become involved in the management of the kōhanga reo. Many important decisions that the whānau should make together are left to a small group of whānau members. This impacts on accountability, transparency and sustainability of the kōhanga reo. It is imperative that the whānau come together to:

  • decide on the strategic goals of the kōhanga reo
  • ensure financial matters for the good of the kōhanga reo and kaimahi are collectively agreed upon
  • carry out staff appraisals so that staff are properly supported
  • regularly review their practices to identify improvements, celebrate successes and support the future development of their kōhanga reo.

The whānau should seek the support of Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust district office to help them to understand these roles and responsibilities as a kōhanga reo whānau.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Namu completed an EROKōhanga Whānau Assurance StatementandSelf-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

6 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau recommends the whānau:

  • seeks the support of Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust district office to help them to understand their roles and responsibilities as a kōhanga reo whānau
  • supports the kaimahi and whānau to expand opportunities for children to hear and respond in te reo Maori in the learning programmes.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

Makere Smith

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū ā-Motu)

23/5/2013

To the Parents and Community of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Namu

These are the findings of the Education Review Office’s latest report on Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Namu.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Namu is in the Taranaki town of Opunake. The local hapū is Tamarongo and the kōhanga reo is situated alongside Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Tamarongo. The whānau states that Opunake is a close knit community who work well together to maintain a healthy community spirit.

The whānau chose Mana Aotūroa as the focus area for this review.

Children are well supported as developing speakers of te reo Māori. The kaiako is a fluent speaker of te reo Māori and shares her knowledge of the local dialect with children and the whānau. Children understand instructions and respond to questions and prompts. They use te reo Māori spontaneously when greeting people. It is also important that children are encouraged to understand that te reo Maori is a language used for a wide range of purposes. Whānau and kaimahi need to extend the opportunities children have to hear and use te reo Māori in the learning programme and in children’s activities outside the kōhanga reo.

The learning environment provides opportunities for children to explore. A variety of local features including a large field and stone carvings promote interest and curiosity. The kōhanga reo is well resourced to support emerging and growing interests.

Relationships between adults and children are nurturing and affirming. Children respond quickly to instruction and direction. They play well together and encourage others to participate. Children have a strong sense of belonging to their kōhanga reo.

The kaiako has a comprehensive set of programme plans which are displayed to inform whānau. Children’s progress and development is monitored. The kaiako has sought professional learning to develop and strengthen her assessment practice.

The kōhanga reo is well managed by a core group of whānau members. They run the kōhanga reo efficiently and try to encourage other whānau to participate. It is important that more whānau become involved in the management of the kōhanga reo. Many important decisions that the whānau should make together are left to a core group of whānau members. This impacts on accountability, transparency and sustainability of the kōhanga reo.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo report is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo report again as part of the regular review cycle.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to student achievement and useful to this kōhanga reo.

If you would like a copy of the full report, please contact the kōhanga reo or see the ERO web page, http://www.ero.govt.nz.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
