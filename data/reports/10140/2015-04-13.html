1 Evaluation of Amanda's Homebased ECE 2

How well placed is Amanda's Homebased ECE 2 to promote positive learning outcomes for children?

Not well placed

Requires further development

Well placed

Very well placed

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Amanda‚Äôs Homebased ECE is a well-established service with educators located across Auckland. The service includes three networks, each with different characteristics. This report focuses on a network that features a Ministry of Education (MoE) Participation Project aimed at providing education and care for children from diverse cultures. A community of Somali, Afghani and Ethiopian families uses this network. There are currently 59 children enrolled, including nine under two years of age.

The service has three Educational Team Leaders (ETLs) who are registered teachers. They provide support and guidance for educators across the three networks. The owners have responsibility for the management of the service.

Since the 2008 ERO review Amanda‚Äôs Homebased ECE has undergone significant change. Its administration has relocated from central to west Auckland, two of the ETLs are new appointments and there have been changes in MoE funding. The service was relicensed in 2013 and has needed to meet new licensing requirements and expectations.

The Review Findings

The service has been proactive in providing education and care that meets the needs of its migrant community. It has supported the families in terms of employment, financial security, socialisation and education for both children and adults. The ETLs have been sensitive in introducing Te WhƒÅriki, the early childhood curriculum, and in developing their expectations for formal learning records.

Educators provide a range of experiences for children in homes and in the community, including playgroup and group excursions. While ETLs continue to support educators to develop their play resources, children enthusiastically explore many activities at a weekly playgroup. They enjoy creative and manipulative materials as they build relationships with others and participate in physical activity and musical experiences. ETLs actively encourage children and educators to use their home languages as well as English.

Children are well supported to settle into educators' homes and to transition to school. ETLs monitor how well infants and toddlers adapt and help educators to establish daily diaries that record children‚Äôs routines and play interests. ETLs write affirming reports about children‚Äôs holistic development that provide very good information to support their entry into school.

Training opportunities have had a positive impact on educators‚Äô practice. They are developing skills to set up learning environments and use equipment to engage children in play. Educators support children‚Äôs behaviour in positive ways. ETLs model effective teaching strategies at playgroup and encourage skills for positive social interaction. The playgroups also enable ETLs to establish very good relationships with educators and families in these diverse communities.

The owners and ETLs are a collegial team committed to ongoing improvement in the service. ETLs make regular home visits to the educators and have recently improved the quality of their monthly reports about children as a result of reflective self review.

A new appraisal system for ETLs has been implemented. The owners agree this process could be developed further to ensure ETLs set measurable goals and regularly evaluate their own progress. ETLs also want to strengthen their bicultural practices.

The owners share management responsibilities with the ETLs. They engage in ongoing discussions about priorities for the service and their vision to include new opportunities for diverse communities. This informal approach does not always support a clear direction for the service. The owners and ETLs recognise that they should now redefine the leadership structure and better document expectations. This would help them to sustain the service‚Äôs operations more effectively over time.

Key Next Steps

The service owners and ETLs agree that key next steps for improving the service include:

  • increasing the support for culturally diverse educators to understand the bicultural heritage of Aotearoa New Zealand
  • improving documented systems to better support ETL practice in relation to their coaching roles, playgroup planning, parent contact and educator visit records
  • updating the strategic plan to reflect current priorities, set long-term direction and make provision for the regular evaluation of strategic goals
  • developing systems and a framework for the team to undertake planned, strategic self review.

The owners and ETLs recognise they should urgently implement documented systems for the effective monitoring of health and safety regulatory requirements.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Amanda's Homebased ECE 2 completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

In order to improve practices the service should:

  • strengthen its risk management processes for excursions
  • ensure that all teacher registrations and police vets are renewed in a timely manner
  • adjust privacy practices to reflect changes in staffing, assessment tools and documentation
  • urgently implement documented systems for the effective monitoring of health and safety regulatory requirements.

Next ERO Review

When is ERO likely to review the service again?

The next ERO review of Amanda's Homebased ECE 2 will be in three years.

ERO‚Äôs Overall Judgement and Next Review

The overall judgement that ERO makes and the timing of the next review will depend on how well placed a service is to promote positive learning outcomes for children. The categories are:
