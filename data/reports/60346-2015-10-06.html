Background

Capital City Preschool is a community-based early childhood service situated in Wellington‚Äôs central business district. The centre provides education and care for 30 children from three to five years of age. It serves a diverse community. Eleven of the children enrolled come from backgrounds where English is a second language.

A board made up of parents, provides oversight for financial management, human resources, policy development and strategic planning. Responsibility for day-to-day operation is undertaken by the centre manager.

The preschool‚Äôs philosophy emphasises the importance of supporting, affirming and challenging children and working in partnership with parents to promote each child‚Äôs learning and development. There is a strong focus on preparation for primary school.

The programme includes structured teacher-led activities linked to mathematics and literacy, and child-initiated play times where children can make choices about the timing and level of their participation.

All teachers are trained and registered. The majority have worked at the centre for some years. Two have been appointed since the September 2012 ERO review. Specialist music and gymnastic teachers are contracted by the service to provide extra support for the learning programme.

The centre has a positive reporting history with ERO.

The Review Findings

Teachers engage well with children to promote their settling and participation in activities. Interactions between teachers and children are responsive and respectful. Positive guidance is used well to promote children‚Äôs social competence. Learning conversations support their research and investigation.

The many opportunities for creative play foster children's self expression. Literacy, mathematics, science and the arts are woven throughout the programme. A calm tone is maintained.

The centre is well resourced. Materials are carefully organised to attract children‚Äôs interest and exploration. Displays are well used to support reflection on their learning. Trips are undertaken to extend the learning environment. The outdoor area provides good opportunities for challenging physical play.

Careful consideration is given to supporting children‚Äôs transitions into the centre and on to primary school. An individualised approach helps new families settle into centre life. Regular opportunities are timetabled to promote children‚Äôs readiness for school.

Leaders agree they should continue to develop purposeful links with primary schools to support the sharing of information about individual children and early childhood and primary school programmes.

  • bicultural perspective in the programme
  • play-based curriculum
  • approach to assessment for learning

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Capital City Preschool completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.
