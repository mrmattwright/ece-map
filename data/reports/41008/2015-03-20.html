1 Te Horopaki

Ko Pūtauaki te maunga tapu Ko Ōhinemataroa te awa tapu Ko Toroa te tupuna Ko Mātaatua te waka.

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Timatanga Hou ki Whakatane, ki te rohe o Mātaatua. Ko tā te whānau tirohanga, ko ‘te whai i te hiringa mā te mātauranga’. Ka ako ngā tamariki ki ngā horopaki o te ao Māori e whakakoia ana i tō rātou tuakiri, tō rātou ahurea, me ngā taonga tuku iho o tō rātou rohe. Ka tautokona, ka arahina hoki te whānau e ngā kaumātua o te hapori nei e whakarato ana i tētahi taiao e ahu mai ana i te aroha, ā, e poipoi ana, e whakahaumanu ana hoki i tō rātou reo, i ngā tikanga, ā, i ngā taonga tuku iho.

E ai ki ngā mema o te whānau, ko te kōhanga reo tō rātou wāhi mīharo mō te ako me te hui tahi, hei whakakoia i ā rātou taonga tuku iho me tō rātou reo.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te kite i te aroha o ngā tamariki ki ngā horopaki o te ao Māori?

I ngā wā katoa ka kitea te aroha o ngā tamariki ki ngā horopaki o te ao Māori.

Mana Atua

Ka whakapuakihia e ngā tamariki te aroha, te manaaki, me te tiaki anō hoki ki waenga i a rātou. He pārekareka ki a rātou ngā hononga mauritau, aroha anō hoki ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ngā kaimahi, ā, ki ētahi atu hoki. He māro ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā tuākana me ngā tēina. Ka kitea te manawa nui, te manawa popore hoki o ngā tamariki mō ētahi atu. Whakanuia ai ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, mō tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki ngā akoranga. He pai te aroturuki i ngā matea ā-tinana, ā-whatumanawa hoki o ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakatutuki wawetia e ngā kaimahi, i runga i te manaaki me te atawhai. Ko te aroha mō ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā taunekeneke me ngā akoranga katoa. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki, ā, ka whanake haere hei ākonga pakari.

Ka whakamahi ngā tamariki tuākana i te reo Māori hei reo whakawhitiwhiti kōrero, ā, ka kitea hoki te ngākau nui o ngā tēina ki te whakapuaki whakaaro mā te reo Māori. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i tō rātou whānau me ngā kaimahi e whakawhiti kōrero ana ki a rātou anō mā te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā tino rautaki ako reo e whakapakari ana i te māia me te āheinga o ngā tamariki. He aronga nui kia whakaongaongatia ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki ngā tamariki puta noa i te rā. E tino puāwai ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e mau nei i te reo Māori taketake.

He mātātoa te whai wāhi a ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga e pā ana ki te ao Māori. Kua āta waihangatia te hōtaka akoranga, ā, he pai te whakahaeretanga. Ka arotahi ngā kaimahi ki ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakarato i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi ako. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā wā ako takirōpū, takitahi anō hoki. Ko ngā taumahi e whakaongaongatia i a rātou hei whakawero, hei whakawhānui hoki i ō rātou whakaaro. Kei ngā pēpi tō rātou ake wāhi ako i whakaritea ai, ā, he pai te whakatakotoranga me te whakahaeretanga a ngā kaimahi hei whakaongaonga i te puāwaitanga o tō rātou whakawhanake ake. He hāneanea, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao.

E whanake ana ngā tamariki i te mōhiotanga me te maioha mō ngā atua Māori. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whakapuaki i tēnā e mōhiotia ana e rātou mō ngā tikanga Māori. Ko ētahi o aua tikanga, ko ngā whakatau, ngā karakia o ia rā, ngā hīmene, ngā waiata, ngā pepeha, me ngā karanga.

Ka tahuri atu ngā tamariki ki ngā wāhanga katoa o te hōtaka. He hihiri te kōhanga reo hei taiao mō te ako i te hōhonutanga o te reo Māori. Kapi katoa ana te taiao ako ki ngā tohu me ngā mahi toi e whai pānga ana ki te rohe. Ko ngā tohu me ngā mahi toi e whakaatuhia ana, huri noa i ngā tamariki, he tohu whakamahara mō rātou o ā rātou taonga tuku iho motuhake. E mōhio ana rātou ki ā rātou mahinga o ia rā, ā, he ngāwari te rere o ngā whakawhitinga, puta noa i te rā. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga mō te wā roa. He ākonga pakari ngā tamariki, ā, ka aro nui ki ngā akoranga.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Kua āta whakawhanakehia ngā mahere hei hāpai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka āta whakaaarohia e ngā kaimahi ngā take e pā ana ki ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki, ngā whāinga ako, ngā rautaki ako e tika ana me ngā rauemi hoki. Ka āta whakamaheretia te ako o ngā tamariki i te ao Māori me ā rātou taonga tuku iho. Ko ngā mahere e aratohu ana i ngā kaimahi. Ka whai wheako ngā tamariki i tētahi hōtaka akoranga kua āta maheretia.

Ka āta tuhia te eke angitu a ngā tamariki hei ākonga ki ngā horopaki o te ao Māori. He maha ngā tūmomo rautaki papai e whakamahia ana e ngā kaimahi hei whakatakoto, hei pūrongo hoki i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahia ngā pūkete hei aru i ngā ahunga o te whanonga me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Ka whiwhi te whānau i ngā tino mōhiohio e pā ana ki ngā akoranga o ā rātou tamariki i te reo Māori, i ngā tikanga, me ngā mōhiotanga anō hoki e pā ana ki te ao Māori.

Ko te painga mō ngā tamariki, kei te pūtake o ngā whakataunga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga. Kua whakaritea te nuinga o ngā pūnaha me ngā tukanga mō te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo i ia rā. He mārama ngā kaupapa here, ka whai pūtake, ā, ka whakahāngaitia e te whānau i ngā wā katoa, ki te rā. Ka whakamanahia e te whānau te whakahirahiratanga o te ao Māori mō ngā tamariki. He pai hoki ki te whānau te tautoko o te hapori me te whai wāhitanga o ngā pūmanawa o te whānau whānui. Ka tino whakatauirahia te whanaungatanga me te aroha ki waenga i a rātou anō, ā, kei te pūtake anō hoki o te eke angitu mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore he tukanga mārama hei aromātai i te hōtaka akoranga. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, ko te aromātai hōtaka tētahi āhuatanga hei whakawhanake ake. Kāhore e kitea te hononga ki ngā whāinga mahere, ngā mātāpono rānei o Te Whāriki. Kāhore he tino hāngaitanga ki waenga i te mahere, te aromatawai, me ngā aromātai.

Kāhore anō kia tuhia ki tētahi mahere rautaki ngā kaupapa matua, ngā whāinga, me ngā wawata anō hoki o te whānau. E mōhio ana te whānau, koia nei tētahi tino mahi hei whai ake mā rātou, kia puta ai te aronga mō tō rātou kōhanga reo me ā rātou tamariki.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei:

  • Kia whakatutukihia mā te ārahitanga anō hoki o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ngā whakaritenga i te pūrongo hei whai ake ā meāke nei.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Tīmatanga Hou ki Whakatāne, ki te rohe o Mātaatua. I ngā wā katoa, ka kitea te aroha o ngā tamariki, ā, he ākonga tākare, he ākonga māia anō hoki rātou. E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki kia ako ai ki te horopaki o te ao Māori. He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo, mā te tautoko anō hoki o ngā kaumātua. Tērā ka whakahaerehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Timatanga Hou ki Whakatanei roto i te toru o ngā tau.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

20 Poutū-te-rangi, 2015

1 Context

Ko Putauaki te maunga tapu Ko Ohinemataroa te awa tapu Ko Toroa te tupuna Ko Mataatua te waka.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Tīmatanga Hou ki Whakatane is in the Mataatua region. The whānau vision is to ‘pursue excellence through knowledge’. Children learn in te ao Māori contexts that affirm their identity, culture and local heritage. Whānau receive support and guidance from local kaumātua who provide an environment that is based on aroha and nurturing and the preservation of their language, traditions and heritage.

Whānau members report that the kōhanga reo is their special place for learning and coming together to affirm their heritage and language.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children show aroha within te ao Māori contexts?

Children consistently show aroha in te ao Māori contexts.

Mana Atua

Children express aroha, manaaki, tiaki towards each other. They enjoy positive and loving relationships with each other, kaimahi and others. Tuakana and teina bonds are strong. Children show patience and consideration for others. Kaimahi regularly praise children for their contributions to learning. Children’s physical and emotional needs are monitored well and responded to promptly by kaimahi with care and affection. Aroha for children is at the heart of all interactions and learning. Children are happy, settled, and develop as confident learners.

Older children use te reo Māori as their language of communication and the younger children show interest in expressing themselves through te reo Māori. Children hear their whānau and kaimahi communicate to each other in te reo Māori. Kaimahi use effective language learning strategies that build children’s confidence and ability. Strong emphasis is placed on stimulating conversations with children throughout the day. Children are thriving in a native te reo Māori environment.

Children are actively engaged in learning about te ao Māori. The learning programme is well designed and managed. Kaimahi focus on children’s interests and provide a wide range of learning activities. Children enjoy shared and independent learning times. They are stimulated by the activities that challenge and extend their thinking. Babies have their own designated learning area that is well set up and managed by kaimahi to stimulate their early development. Children are comfortable and settled in their environment.

Children are developing an awareness and appreciation for ngā atua Māori. Children enjoy sharing what they know about tikanga Māori. These include whakatau, daily karakia, hīmene, waiata, mihimihi, pepeha and karanga.

Children are engaged in all areas of the programme. The kōhanga reo is a rich and vibrant te reo Māori learning environment. The learning environment is covered in symbols and art that reflects the region. Children are reminded of their unique heritage through the symbols and art displayed around them. They are aware of their daily routines and transitions throughout the day are smooth and unrushed. Children can engage in learning for a sustained period of time. Children are confident and responsive learners.

Planning and Evaluation

Planning to support children’s learning is well developed. Kaimahi consider children’s interests, their learning goals, appropriate learning strategies and resources. Children’s learning of te ao Māori, and their heritage is well planned. Planning provides clear guidelines for kaimahi. Children experience a well planned learning programme.

Children’s success as learners in te ao Māori contexts is well documented. There is a range of effective methods kaimahi use to record and report on children’s learning. Profiles are used to track the shifts in children’s behaviour and their interests. Whānau receive valuable information about their child’s learning of te reo Māori, tikanga and knowledge of te ao Māori.

Children’s interests are at the fore front of managerial decisions. Most systems and processes are in place for the daily operations of the kōhanga reo. Policies are clear, relevant and are often up dated by whānau. Whānau value the importance of a te ao Māori environment for children. Whānau enjoy community support and skilled input from extended whānau members. Whanaungatanga and aroha for each other is modelled successfully and is the key to realising the vision of excellence for children.

Key Next Steps

There are no clear processes in place to evaluate the learning programme. Kaimahi acknowledge programme evaluation is an area that requires further development. There is no direct link to planning goals or the Te Whāriki principles. There is no clear alignment between planning, assessment and evaluations.

Whānau priorities, goals and aspirations are yet to be documented in a strategic plan. Whānau recognise this as a next important step that will provide direction for their kōhanga reo and their children.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • To address, along with the guidance of TKRNT, the next steps in the report.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Timatanga Hou ki Whakatane is in the Mataatua region. Children consistently show aroha and they are keen and confident learners. Children are well supported to learn in a te ao Māori context. Whānau, with the support of kaumātua, are managing their kōhanga reo well. ERO is likely to return in three years.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Timatanga Hou ki Whakatane will be in three years.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori

 20 March 2015
