1 Background

Introduction

A Supplementary Review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager, Review Services in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a centre has taken towards addressing issues specified in a previous education review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of the Kidz World – Brent Road governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the July 2012 ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • the safety of teachers and children through implementing effective systems to identify and remedy hazards
  • the establishment processes that ensure staff are aware of their roles and responsibilities, and supported to fulfil these to ensure quality care and education for children and their families/whānau
  • the implementation of robust appraisal systems and practices that ensure teachers receive regular and robust feedback about their teaching practice
  • provision of a wide range of good quality equipment and materials for children and teachers.

2 Evaluation Findings

Background

Kidz World Brent Road is an education and care service catering for children from birth to school age in two age related areas. The service is one of three centres that operate under the same management in Rotorua. The owner/director and managers of the three centres act as a management team for the wider Kidz World organisation.

The Education Review Report of July 2012 identified areas for improvement about aspects of health and safety, staff roles and responsibilities, staff appraisal and the range of resources and materials available to children. There were also compliance actions about police vetting, earthquake safety and planning.

Since the last review a new manager has been appointed. Staffing has remained consistent. The centre has been relicensed under the Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008. Teachers have been involved in an Education Leadership Project professional development contract. There has also been ongoing professional learning and development offered by the Kidz World leadership team.

Areas of progress

Issues regarding environmental safety identified in the last report have been fully addressed. This was confirmed in the relicensing process. Management has clarified teachers’ roles and responsibilities, particularly in relation to monitoring of the environment and hazard identification.

There has been ongoing review and refinement of the Kidz World staff appraisal process that is effectively coordinated by a member of the management team. A well-documented and coordinated process, which is based on the teacher registration criteria has been established. Key aspects of this process include:

  • goal setting that informs professional learning and development programmes
  • the maintenance of evidence folders by teachers to support their progress towards meeting goals and their involvement in the registration process
  • regular observations, mentoring and coaching of individual teachers
  • provision of appropriate written feedback to teachers about their practice.

Detailed procedures are in place to provide advice and guidance for provisionally registered teachers. Police vetting requirements are now being met for teachers who are not registered.

Self review is informing ongoing improvements to learning environments and the provision of experiences for children.

Children are actively engaged alongside teachers in interesting and meaningful learning opportunities. They have access to an appropriate range of resources and equipment to support self-initiated play and exploration. There is an increasing emphasis on providing open-ended and natural materials to enhance children’s understanding of the living world. The centre aims to provide a sustainable environment and to encourage physical challenge and problem solving.

Teacher planning has evolved and developed. Participation in a professional development contract has enhanced the documentation of how children learn and the continuity of their learning. Teachers are encouraged to reflect about their practice and the place of the environment in supporting and extending children’s learning.

Areas for further improvement

The leadership team has identified, and ERO agrees, that a useful next step to further strengthen the appraisal process and to encourage teachers to reflect on their practice, would be the development and implementation of a peer appraisal process.

3 Future Action

ERO is confident that the service is being managed in the interest of the children. Therefore ERO will review the service again as part of the regular review cycle.
