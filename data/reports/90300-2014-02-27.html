2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Nō te tau 1987 Te Kōhanga Reo o Kimihia te Mātauranga o Ngā Tūpuna i whakatūngia ai. He maha ngā wā kua hūnuku te whānau, nā te roroku o ngā whare, ā, kāhore hoki e tika ana mō rātou. I tēnei wā, e ahu whakamua ana te whānau ki te hanga i tētahi kōhanga reo hou ki runga i taua whenua. Kua tohe te whānau, ā, kua whakapā atu ki ētahi pūtea, mai i ngā tūmomo puna katoa. E whakarite ana rātou ki te whakatū i tētahi whare hou mō ā rātou tamariki ā meāke nei.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Reo te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake mātauranga. He hiahia tō te whānau kia whāngaihia ā rātou tamariki ki te mātauranga mā tētahi taiao e aro nui ana ki te whakawhanake i te tū pakari me te aronga toi whenuatanga o ia tamaiti, me te reo me ōna tikanga hoki. Ka aromātai tēnei pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te whai huatanga o te whānau ki te hāpai i te ‘Mana Reo’ o ngā tamariki.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako i te reo Māori mā tētahi hōtaka akoranga pāhekoheko e whakahihiri ana i a rātou. Ka ako rātou i tō rātou marae, i ngā hītori, ngā whakapapa, ngā pepeha, me ngā waiata mā te taunekeneke ki ngā kaimahi me ētahi atu. E wātea ana ki a rātou te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi, ā, he māmā noa te whakapā atu ki aua rauemi. Kua whakawhanakehia e ngā tamariki ngā hononga e whai pūtake ana, ki te taha o ō rātou hoa, te whānau, ngā kaimahi, te whānau whānui, me te hapori. E whakaongaongatia ana ngā tamariki, he harikoa hoki rātou, i roto i ā rātou akoranga. Ka whakatutuki te whānau i ngā mahi katoa kia noho haumaru ai ngā tamariki.

E whakawhanake tonu ana te whānau i tō rātou māramatanga ki te whakamahere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro. Me aroturuki te mahere whānui hei tāutu i ngā whakapaitanga me ngā taumata tonu ka whāia i roto i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori. Me whakapai ake te whai huatanga o te whakahaere i ngā arohaehae a ngā kaimahi, me te whakawhanaketanga ngaio.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Kimihia te Mātauranga o Ngā Tūpuna kia whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Kimihia te Mātauranga o Ngā Tūpuna.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Reo.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Reo

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Reo tā rātou kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake mātauranga. He hiahia tō te whānau kia whāngaihia ā rātou tamariki ki te mātauranga mā tētahi taiao e aro nui ana ki te whakawhanake i te tū pakari me te aronga toi whenuatanga o ia tamaiti, me te reo me ōna tikanga hoki. Ka aromātai tēnei pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te whai huatanga o te whānau ki te hāpai i te ‘Mana Reo’ o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te taiao akoranga.He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki te taiao reo Māori nei. He wāteatea, he mahana hoki te wāhi ako matua. Ka whakanuia ngā mahi a ngā tamariki, ā, he ātaahua te whakaatu o aua mahi ki ngā pātū, puta noa i te kōhanga reo. He whānui ngā tūmomo pokapū mahi, ā, he whānui anō hoki ngā rauemi e noho wātea ana ki ngā tamariki. Ka whakarato i tētahi taiao akoranga whakahihiri, ātaahua hoki ki ngā tamariki, ā, e hāpai ana tēnei i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, tō rātou whakawhanaketanga anō hoki.

Te hōtaka akoranga.He tākare te aro atu a ngā tamariki ki te hōtaka akoranga whakahihiri, pāhekoheko hoki. He pārekareka ki a rātou te waiata, te kanikani, me te tākaro, ā, he tūperepere, he korikori hoki. Ka ako ngā tamariki i tō rātou marae, me te hītori, ngā whakapapa, ngā pepeha, me ngā waiata mā te taunekeneke ki ngā kaimahi me ētahi atu. Ka whakaratohia ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako e whakawhānui ake ana i tō rātou āheinga i roto i te reo Māori.

Te whanaungatanga.Kua whakawhanakehia e ngā tamariki ngā tino hononga ki ō rātou hoa, ki te whānau, ngā kaimahi, me ngā tāngata hoki o te hapori whānui. Ka tautoko te whānau i ngā tamariki ki te ako i te marae, te whenua, te moana, me ngā maunga. Ka noho pūmau ngā kaimahi ki te āta whakapakari i ngā tino hononga e arotahi ana ki ngā matea o ā rātou tamariki. Ko ngā haerenga i whakaritea ai ki te taha o ētahi atu kōhanga reo, he whai wāhitanga anō hoki mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga, me te whakapūmau ake, te tuitui ake rānei i ngā hononga ki ētahi atu hoa.

Te reo Māori.He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako i te reo Māori. Ka whakautu rātou i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero me ngā kipakipa ki te taha o ngā kaimahi me ngā hoa. Ka whakamahi ngā tamariki i te reo kōrero me te reo tinana hoki ki te whakawhiti kōrero me te whakapuaki anō hoki i tō rātou māramatanga, i roto i ngā tūmomo horopaki ako. Haere ai ngā kaumātua ki te kōhanga reo hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki i a rātou e tākaro ana. Ka whakapuakihia ngā kupu hou ki ngā tamariki mā te tākaro, mā ngā tūmomo mahinga hoki. Ka hāpai tēnei i te whakawhānuitanga ake o ngā kupu a ngā tamariki. Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga e pā ana ki te reo me ngā tikanga Māori.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te ārahi.Me nui ake ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te ārahi i ngā kaupapa matua me ngā kawa. Me akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te ārahi i ngā karakia mō te kai, me ngā wā waiata, ngā wā pepeha hoki. Ka hāpai tēnei i te whakawhanaketanga o te tū pakari o ngā tamariki me ō rātou pūkenga reo Māori. Kātahi aua tamariki ka hāpai ake i te tū anō hoki o ō rātou hoa.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai i ngā kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga. Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka, me te aroturuki i te ahu whakamua me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakahaeretanga i ia rā.Ka whai hua tētahi rōpū iti o ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakahaere i ngā tūmomo kaupapa i te kōhanga reo i ia rā. He pai te whakaritenga, te tiaki, me te whai rauemi hoki o te kōhanga reo. Ka whai hua hoki te rōpū ki te hāpai me te ārahi i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo me te hōtaka akoranga.

Te whakahaere i te pūtea.Ka whakahaerehia e te whānau ā rātou pūtea hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki. He mārama ngā tukanga e whakamōhio ana i te whānau mō ngā pūtea me ngā utu. Ko ētahi o aua tukanga, ko:

  • te whakapaitanga ake i ngā pūnaha pūtea me te pūrongo anō hoki i ia te wā
  • te tohatoha o ngā pūtea, me te whai pānga o tēnei ki ngā kaupapa matua o te kōhanga reo.

He pai te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo. Ka hāpai tēnei i te whānau ki te tautoko anō hoki i ngā whakawhanaketanga me ngā whakapaitanga kei te heke tonu mai.

Te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.He pakari te whakamahere, ā, he tino aronga mārama o roto mā ngā kaimahi me te whānau. Ka whakamahere ngātahi ngā kaimahi, i a rātou e whakawhanake ana i ngā hōtaka akoranga mō ngā tamariki. Ka hāngai ēnei ki ngā mahere ā-tau, ā-wāhanga, o ia rā hoki. Aroturukitia ai ngā mahere ā-kaupapa i ia te wā e ngā kaimahi me te whānau. Ka hono ngā mahere ki Te Whāriki, ā, he aronga tā ngā mahere nei ki ngā atua me ngā kaupeka o te tau.

Kua whakaritea ngā mahi aromatawai hei whakatutuki i ia te wā. Ko ngā paki ako me ngā taumata tonu ka whāia e ia tamaiti, ka whakatakotohia ki ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki. He pai te aroturuki, te whakahaeretanga anō hoki o ngā mahere me ngā taumata ako ka whāia tonuhia e ia tamaiti. Ko ngā mōhiohio aromatawai e pā ana ki ngā tamariki, e noho wātea ana hei tirohanga, hei kōrerotanga hoki mā te whānau.

Aromātaihia ai te hōtaka akoranga e te whānau i ia te wā. Kei ngā pūrongo me ngā whakawhitinga kōrero tētahi pūtake mō te whakarerekē haere, hei whakaarotanga anō hoki. Ka whakamahi te whānau i aua mōhiohio ki te whakapai haere i te whakapuakitanga o te hōtaka. Aroturukitia ai ngā taumahi me ngā rauemi a ngā tamariki i ia te wā. Ka whakapuakihia hoki i ia te wā, ko ngā kupu hou me ngā taumahi e hono ana ki ngā kaupapa ako. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā tētahi hōtaka akoranga e āta tirohia ana i ia te wā, hei āta whakatau i tōna hihiri, i tōna pāhekoheko hoki.

Te haumaru me te waiora o ngā tamariki.Ko te haumaru me te waiora o ngā tamariki tētahi āhuatanga matua ki te whānau me ngā kaimahi. Whakatinanahia ai e rātou i ia te wā te whānuitanga o ngā mahi hauora, haumaru hoki. Ko ētahi o aua mahi, ko ngā akoako hōneatanga inā he ahi, ko te tāutu i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea, me te akiaki hoki i ngā tamariki ki te tiaki i ō rātou ake tinana. Ka whakapau kaha te whānau ki te whakarato i tētahi taiao haumaru, hei wāhi ako, hei wāhi tākaro anō hoki mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te mahere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro.E whakawhanake tonu ana te whānau i tō rātou māramatanga ki te whakamahere rautaki. Ahakoa ki tā te whānau me whakapai ake te ako ngaio me te āheinga i roto i te reo Māori, kāhore he mahere e tohu ana i ngā mahi ka whāia hei whakatutuki, hei aroturuki rānei i aua āhuatanga. Ko te whakawhanaketanga o te mahere rautaki, me te aroturuki hoki i ia te wā, ka hāpai i te whānau ki te whakatutuki i aua whāinga. Kāhore he aronga nahanaha ki te arotake whaiaro. I mua, he ōpaki noa ngā mahi, i ngā wā e tika ana. Me hāpai i te whānau ki te āta tuhi i ngā tukanga hei whakarite i te whakatutukitanga nahanaha o ngā whakapaitanga ki te kōhanga reo. Ko te arotake me te pūrongo i ia te wā, ka hāpai i te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ā rātou whāinga, ā rātou kaupapa matua mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā tūranga me ngā kawenga mahi.Me whakawhanake te whānau i tētahi rautaki ka tautoko i te rōnaki tonutanga o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. E whakaae ana te whānau, me toro atu ki ngā whakangungu o Te Whāriki me Te Korowai. Me whai wāhi mātātoa te whānau ki te whakawhanaketanga ngaio e whakaratohia ana e Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo. Mā tēnei, ka whakatairanga te whānau i te māramatanga ake ki ngā tūranga o te whānau, me ā rātou kawenga mahi.

Te whakahaere i te arohaehae.Ahakoa kua arohaehaengia ngā kaimahi, kāhore anō kia oti te tukanga i whakamahia ai e te whānau. Kāhore anō ngā kaimahi kia whakatakoto i ngā whāinga ngaio, i ā rātou ake whāinga rānei, hei āhuatanga tonu o te tukanga. Kāhore he mahere e pā ana ki te whakawhanaketanga ngaio, hei whakapai i ngā whakaritenga a ngā kaimahi, ahakoa kua tutuki i a rātou ētahi mahi whakangungu. Ko te āta whakahaere i te whakawhanaketanga ngaio o ngā kaimahi me ā rātou āhua mahi, ka whai wāhi nui ki te whakapai i ngā putanga ako mō ngā tamariki.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Kimihia te Mātauranga o Ngā Tūpuna was established in 1987. The whānau have moved several times due to the deterioration and unsuitability of the premises. Whānau are currently working towards building a new kōhanga reo on the existing site. Whānau have persevered and accessed funding from various sources. They plan to have a new building for their children in the near future.

The whānau chose Mana Reo as the focus for this Education Review. Whānau want their children to be educated in an environment that has a strong focus on developing each child’s confidence, belonging and te reo me ōna tikanga. This ERO report evaluates how well the whānau support children’s ‘Mana Reo’.

Children enjoy learning te reo Māori in an interesting and interactive learning programme. They learn about their marae, history, whakapapa, pepeha and waiata through interaction with kaimahi and others. They have access to a range of resources that are readily accessible. Children have developed meaningful relationships with their peers, whānau, kaimahi and the wider whānau and community. Children are happy and stimulated in their learning. The whānau takes all reasonable steps to keep children safe.

Whānau are still developing their understanding of strategic planning and self review. The overall plan needs to be monitored to gauge improvements and next steps in te reo Māori development. The effective management of kaimahi appraisals and professional development for staff needs to be improved.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

4 Findings

Mana Reo

Background

The whānau chose Mana Reo as their focus for this Education Review. Whānau want their children to be educated in an environment that has a strong focus on developing each child’s confidence, belonging and te reo me ōna tikanga. This ERO report evaluates how well the whānau support children’s ‘Mana Reo’.

Areas of good performance

Learning environment. Children are happy and relaxed in the te reo Māori environment. The main learning area is spacious and warm. Children’s work is valued and attractively displayed on walls throughout the kōhanga reo. Work stations are varied and include a range of resources that are accessible to children. Children are provided with an exciting, inviting learning environment that supports their interests and development.

Learning programme.Children respond enthusiastically to the interesting and interactive learning programme.They enjoy song, dance and play which are boisterous and physical.Children learn about their marae, history, whakapapa, pepeha and waiata through interaction with kaimahi and others. Children are provided with a variety of experiences that extends their te reo Māori capability.

Whanaungatanga.Children have developed meaningful relationships with their peers, whānau, kaimahi and people from the wider community. Whānau support children to learn about the marae, land, sea, mountains. Kaimahi are committed to building positive relationships focused on the needs of their children. Planned outings with other kōhanga reo provide opportunities for children to explore te reo Māori and tikanga and to renew or form new friendships.

Te reo Māori.Children enjoy learning te reo Māori. They respond to conversations and prompts when with kaimahi and peers. Children use verbal and non-verbal body movements to communicate and express their understanding when in different learning situations. Kaumātua regularly visit the kōhanga reo to support children’s te reo Māori development as they play. New words are introduced to children through play and routines. This helps to extend children’s vocabulary. Children are immersed in te reo me tikanga Māori learning.

Areas for development

Leadership opportunities for children. Children need more opportunities to lead key activities and protocols. Kaimahi should encourage children to lead karakia kai, waiata and pepeha sessions. This will help develop children’s confidence and te reo Māori skills. These children are likely to have a positive influence on their peers.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of the operations. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and the monitoring of children’s progress and development.

Areas of good performance

Daily management. A small group of whānau members efficiently manage the day to day running of the kōhanga reo. The kōhanga reo is well organised, maintained and resourced. The group effectively support and guide the kōhanga reo operations and learning programme.

Financial management.Whānau manage their finances in the best interest of their children. There are transparent processes to inform whānau about income and expenditure. These include:

  • improved financial systems and regular reporting
  • budget allocation that reflects kōhanga reo priorities.

The kōhanga reo is in a good financial position. This enables the whānau to support future developments and improvements.

Planning, assessment and evaluation.Planning is robust and provides clear direction for kaimahi and whānau. Kaimahi plan collectively when they develop programmes of learning for children. These include annual, term and daily plans. Kaupapa plans are monitored regularly by kaimahi and whānau. Plans link toTe Whārikiand include a focus on ngā atua and the seasons.

Regular assessment practices are in place. Individual learning stories and next steps are presented in children’s profiles. Plans and next steps for individuals are monitored and managed well. Children’s assessment information is available to whānau for viewing and comment.

The learning programme is evaluated regularly by whānau. Reports and discussions include a rationale for change and consideration. Whānau use this information to make improvements in the delivery of the programme. Children’s activities and resources consistently monitored. New vocabulary and activities linked to learning kaupapa are introduced regularly. Children benefit from a learning programme which undergoes regular scrutiny to make sure it is interesting and interactive.

Children’s safety and well being.Children’s safety and wellbeing is a priority for whānau and kaimahi. They regularly implement a range of health and safety practices. These include fire safety drills, the identification of hazards and encouraging children to take care of their personal hygiene. Whānau work hard to provide a safe environment to learn and play.

Areas for development

Strategic plan and self review. Whānau are still developing in their understanding of strategic planning. Although whānau report there is a need to improve professional learning and te reo Māori capability and capacity, there are no plans to show how this will be achieved or monitored. The development of a strategic plan along with regular monitoring will assist whānau to achieve the goals. There is no planned approach to self review. Informal practices have been used in the past when needed. Whānau need support to clearly document processes to ensure kōhanga reo improvements occur in a systematic manner. Regular review and reporting will assist the whānau to achieve goals and priorities they have for their children.

Roles and responsibilities. Whānau need to develop a strategy to support the ongoing sustainability of kōhanga reo management. Whānau agree that training in Te Whāriki and Te Korowai is needed. Whānau should actively participate in professional development made available by Te Kohanga Reo National Trust. This would make whānau promote a greater understanding of the roles and responsibilities of whānau.

Appraisal management. Although kaimahi have been appraised, the process whānau have used is incomplete. Kaimahi have not set professional or personal goals as part of the process. There is no professional development plan to improve kaimahi practices despite some training having taken place. The effective management of kaimahi professional development and performance is essential to improving learning outcomes for children.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kimihia te Mātauranga o Ngā Tūpuna Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff ofcompleted an EROKōhanga Whānau Assurance StatementandSelf-Audit Checklist. In these documents they haveattested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

6 Recommendations

ERO and the whānau developed the following recommendations that the whānau will:

  • develop an action plan to support the priorities in the strategic plan
  • provide more opportunities for children to lead different activities and protocols
  • develop clear self review processes.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

General Information About Reviews

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
