Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hohepa ki Phillipstown, ki te pokapū matua o te tāone o Ōtautahi. Ka tino poipoia, ka tino manaakitia hoki ngā tamariki. He pai ngā taunekeneke me te whanaungatanga ki waenga i ngā tamariki, ngā kaimahi, me te whānau. Ka whakahaere te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hohepa i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hohepa ki Phillipstown, ki Ōtautahi. He aumangea ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki ngā rū whenua e whai pānga tonu ana ki tō rātou noho. He manawa whakahī tō te whānau mō tō rātou kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te kite e poipoia ana, e manaakitia ana ngā tamariki?

Ka kitea te harikoa, te tūmāia hoki o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao.

Te Mana Atua 

E ako ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana i a rātou. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te rangimārie, te aroha, te manaaki me te atawhai mā ā rātou taunekeneke ki ētahi atu. Ka kitea tō rātou ngākau nui i a rātou e tākaro ana, e ako ana hoki. He whānui ngā tūmomo rauemi e noho wātea ana mā rātou. Ka āta ārahi ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki i roto i ngā mahinga me ngā tikanga. Ka mihi ngā tamariki ki a Papatūānuku, ki a Ranginui, ā, ki ētahi atu atua anō hoki, i ia rā. E ako ana ngā tamariki i ngā hononga ā-wairua me ngā hononga ā-tangata.

Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa ki te kōhanga reo. Ka taki rātou i ō rātou pepeha ki mua i ō rātou hoa me tō rātou whānau. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā waiata me ngā haka. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te ārahi i aua wā, hei whakawhanake i tō rātou tūmāia. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i te reo tika hei hāpai i te pakari o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. E pakari haere ana te māia o ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori.

Ka whai hua ngā mahi pai a ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi ki te kōhanga reo. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te tākaro me te ako ki te taha o ngā kaimahi me ō rātou hoa. He ratarata ngā tamariki, ā, he pakari tā rātou whakawhanaunga ki ētahi atu. He tau, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga i āta whakamaheretia ai. Ka whakarite ngā kaimahi i ngā mahere ā-wāhanga, ā-wiki, o ia rā hoki. Ka whai hua aua mahere ki te ārahi i ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi. Ka whakapuaki hoki te whānau i ō rātou whakaaro hei whakauru ki ngā mahere. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā tētahi hōtaka i āta whakaritea ai, i āta whakahaerehia ai hoki.

He pai te āta kapo atu i te ako a ngā tamariki. He pai ngā tukanga a ngā kaimahi ki te aroturuki i te ako a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahia ngā pūkete hei whakaemi i ngā mōhiohio, hei tohu hoki i te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga i roto i te reo Māori me ngā akoranga ā-kaupapa. Ka whakapuakihia aua mōhiohio ki ngā mātua. Ka āta aroturukitia te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki.

E whai hua ana te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He mārama ngā pūnaha e pā ana ki te pūtea. Ka tino tautokona ngā kaimahi ki te poipoi me te atawhai i ngā tamariki. Ka tāutu te arohaehae i te āhua o ngā mahi i ō rātou pūmanawa ngaio me ō rātou matea ngaio. Ko Te Ara Tūāpae te tūāpapa mō te whakawhanake tonutanga o te kōhanga reo i tā rātou aronga rautaki. Ka whakahaere te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He whāiti noa te aromātai o roto. He iti noa ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki te aromātai hōtaka. Kāhore anō ngā kaupapa here o te hauora me te haumaru kia arotakengia. Kāhore i te whānau tētahi tino pūnaha hei tāutu, hei aroturuki hoki i te ahu whakamua o ngā kaupapa rautaki matua.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā:

  • kia whakapakari tonu te whānau i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aromātai o roto, hei aromātai i te hōtaka, hei arotake i ngā kaupapa here, ā, hei aroturuki hoki i te aronga rautaki. 

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hohepa ki Phillipstown, ki te pokapū matua o te tāone o Ōtautahi. Ka tino poipoia, ka tino manaakitia hoki ngā tamariki. He pai ngā taunekeneke me te whanaungatanga ki waenga i ngā tamariki, ngā kaimahi, me te whānau. Ka whakahaere te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hohepa i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hohepa is in Phillipstown in the Christchurch CBD. Children are nurtured and well cared for. Interactions and relationships are positive among children, kaimahi and whānau. Whānau manage the kōhanga reo in the best interest of their children.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hohepa will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hohepa is in Phillipstown in Christchurch. Kaimahi and whānau have become resilient to the earthquakes that continue to impact their lives. Whānau are very proud of their kōhanga reo.  

2 The Review Findings

How well do children show they are nurtured and cared for?

Children show that they are happy and confident in their environment.

Mana Atua

Children learn in a nurturing environment. Children experience rangimārie, aroha, manaaki and atawhai through interactions with others. They show a sense of satisfaction in their play and learning. They have a variety of resources available to them. Kaimahi guide the children well through routines and tikanga. Children greet Papatūānuku, Ranginui and other atua each day. Children are learning about spiritual and cultural connections.

Children hear te reo Māori at all times at kōhanga reo. They recite their pepehā in front of their peers and whānau. Children enjoy waiata and haka. During these activities children have leadership opportunities to develop their confidence. Kaimahi model correct language to support children’s capability in te reo Māori. Children are growing in confidence in their use of te reo Māori.

Children and kaimahi have positive and rewarding experiences at kōhanga reo. Children enjoy playing and learning with kaimahi and their peers. Children are friendly and confidently engage with others. Children are settled and happy at kōhanga reo.

Planning and Evaluation

Children participate in a variety of well-planned learning experiences. Kaimahi prepare term, weekly and daily plans. These plans effectively guide kaimahi practice. Whānau contribute their ideas to planning. Children benefit from a programme that is well organised and managed.

Children’s learning is captured well. Kaimahi have good processes to monitor children’s learning. Profiles are used to gather information and show children’s progress and development in te reo Māori and kaupapa learning. This information is shared with parents. Children’s progress is monitored well.

Kōhanga reo management is effective. Financial systems are transparent. Kaimahi are well supported to nurture and care for the children. Performance appraisals identify their professional strengths and needs. Te Ara Tūāpae provides a basis for the kōhanga reo to further develop their strategic direction. Whānau manage the kōhanga reo in the best interest of their children.

Key Next Steps

Internal evaluation is limited. There is very little evidence of programme evaluation. Health and safety policies are yet to be reviewed. Whānau do not have a strong system to identify and monitor the progress of key strategic priorities.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • that whānau continue to strengthen internal evaluation practices to evaluate the programme, review policies, and monitor the strategic direction.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hohepa is in Phillipstown in the Christchurch CBD. Children are nurtured and well cared for. Interactions and relationships are positive among children, kaimahi and whānau. Whānau manage the kōhanga reo in the best interest of their children.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hohepa will be in three years.
