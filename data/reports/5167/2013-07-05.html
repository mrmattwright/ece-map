Background

Central Kids Kindergartens - Clyde Street, located in Tokoroa, provides education and care for children from two years to school age in a school-day programme. The kindergarten is licensed for 30 children, and at the time of this review had a roll of 45. There are 18 children identified as MƒÅori who whakapapa to a range of different iwi. In addition, there is a significant number of children from Pacific backgrounds including seven of Cook Island descent. The ethnicity of the current teaching staff reflects the multi-cultural nature of the kindergarten and the community. The kindergarten operates under the umbrella of the Central North Island Kindergarten Trust, (known as Central Kids). Its philosophy places strong priority on building positive relationships among people, and recognising significant places and taonga in the local community.

Since the 2010 ERO review there has been one change in the teaching staff. Central Kids have funded significant improvements to the indoor and outdoor environments, including landscaping of the playground, refurbishing the kitchen and foyer, and creating a new staff work area. Teachers have engaged in a wide range of professional learning and development opportunities that support the kindergarten‚Äôs focus on building children‚Äôs social competencies.

The kindergarten has a positive reporting history with ERO.

This review was part of a cluster of 13 reviews in the Central Kids umbrella organisation.

The Review Findings

Children benefit from participating in a programme that is responsive to their emerging interests. Teachers‚Äô recent professional development is supporting them to empower children to be independent, solve their own problems, and develop persistence and resilience. ERO observed:

  • caring relationships among teachers and children
  • teachers involving themselves in play alongside children
  • teachers commenting and conversing with children about their everyday lives
  • children who were settled, confident and secure in the kindergarten environment.

Teachers have reviewed the way the learning environment supports and sustains children‚Äôs play. They ensure children have access to a wide range of attractively presented resources and equipment. Children‚Äôs achievements are celebrated through wall displays that acknowledge their early literacy skills. The indoor areas are welcoming and inviting for parents and children, and reflect the kindergarten‚Äôs philosophy of building strong relationships with whƒÅnau. Teachers place priority on establishing a home-like environment for children and their families.

Programme priorities include the natural integration of literacy and mathematics in children‚Äôs play, environmental sustainability, and supporting children‚Äôs cultural identity. Teachers share children‚Äôs involvement in the programme in a range of ways including stories and photographs displayed on walls, and in individual portfolios. Resources and activities include the use of te reo and Pacific languages, stories and objects that reflect the cultures of the Pacific, and pepeha from whƒÅnau to share their whakapapa.

The head teacher knows the local community well and is knowledgeable about MƒÅori and Pacific cultures. She models effective teaching practice and is responsive to the needs of teachers, children and their families. The head teacher has accessed a wide range of external expertise to support teachers and the ongoing development of the kindergarten. She provides many opportunities for teachers and parents to develop their leadership skills and share their expertise with children, and leads a wide range of self-review processes to bring about improvement.

The trust is supportive of the kindergarten. The professional leader from Central Kids has a long-standing association with the kindergarten and provides the head teacher and teachers with support and guidance. Comprehensive policy guidelines assist staff to meet regulatory requirements and the Central Kids‚Äô standards and expectations.

As part of this review, ERO evaluated the effectiveness of the kindergarten‚Äôs responsibilities for the employment and management of staff. Central Kids have developed and implemented highly effective processes to manage and appoint staff.

Key Next Steps

At the time of this ERO review, the head teacher was in the early stages of reviewing assessment, planning and evaluation practices. ERO and the kindergarten leaders agree that the next step is to develop useful and manageable processes to notice, recognise and respond to children‚Äôs‚Äô learning. This development should be prioritised in the annual plan, and the new assessment processes linked to teacher professional development, appraisal and self review.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Central Kids Kindergartens - Clyde Street completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum

  • premises and facilities

  • health and safety practices

  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)

  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)

  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)

  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.
