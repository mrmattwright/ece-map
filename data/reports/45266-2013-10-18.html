1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā tētahi ō ngā Kaiwhakahaere Matua ā-Rohe o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei, kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga o Te Kōhanga Reo o Kākāriki Marae e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia e te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i t Hōngongoi 2011, me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

  • Ngā Pou e Whā, tae atu ki te whakamahere kaupapa, te aromatawai, te aromātai hōtaka, te arotake whaiaro, te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, me te whakapai ake i te whakamahinga o te reo Māori
  • ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture, tae atu ki ngā akoako hōneatanga inā he ahi, he rū whenua rānei, me te whakangungu hoki i ngā kaimahi mō ngā akoako
  • ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai whai muri i te arotake o mua.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Kākāriki Marae ki te hapori o Waikomiti, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau ki te hauāuru. I ngā rā o mua, i te tū te kōhanga reo ki ngā whenua ki te kura tuarua o Greenbay, ki te taha o te marae o Kākāriki. I te tau 2009 te kōhanga reo i hūnuku ai ki te wāhi e noho nei rātou i tēnei wā. Nō te karapū maita o ngā wāhine o Waikomiti. Kua whakahou ake ngā rauhanga me te hanganga o te kōhanga reo hei whakatutuki i ngā matea o ngā tamariki. I tēnei wā, e whakarerekē haere ana te whānau i te ingoa o te kōhanga reo, ki Te Kōhanga Reo o Kākāriki.

I tāutuhia ki te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2011, te maha o ngā wāhanga i whai pānga ai ki Ngā Pou e Whā, hei whakatutuki ake. I hāngai ēnei ki te whakamahere kaupapa, te aromatawai, te aromātai hōtaka, te arotake whaiaro, me te whakahaere a te whānau. Ko ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai ko te whakamahinga o te reo Māori puta noa i te kōhanga reo, ngā akoako hōneatanga inā he ahi, he rū whenua rānei, me ngā whakangungu a ngā kaimahi i roto i aua tukanga ohorere.

He pai te hanganga me te riterite o te hōtaka o ia rā. He whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga, te wheako i te whānuitanga o ngā kaupapa, me te whai wāhi ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi, tae atu ki ngā akoranga e pā ana ki ngā tikanga Māori.

Ka kōkiri whakamua ngā kaimahi me te whānau. Kua arotahi rātou ki te whakawhanake i ō rātou āheinga i roto i te reo Māori, hei hāpai ake i ā rātou tamariki. Kua whai wāhi ngā kaimahi ki ngā kaupapa akoranga a Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo. Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga o tētahi taiao o te reo Māori. Ka tino hāpaitia tā rātou whakawhanake haere ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Kua whakawhanakehia tētahi hōtaka o te arotake whaiaro, hei aroturuki i ngā whakawhanaketanga me ngā whakaritenga, puta noa i te kōhanga reo. Ka kōrerohia, ka pūrongotia hoki ngā tūmomo wāhanga o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo ki ngā hui ā-whānau o ia marama. He maha ngā kaupapa here i arotakehia ai, i whakamanatia ai hoki. Ka arotake tonu, ka whakahou ake hoki te whānau i tā rātou tūtohinga, kia hāngai ake ai ki ō rātou wawata, ā rātou mahi hoki.

Kua pai ake ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakamahere kaupapa me te aromātai hōtaka. Ka tāutu te mahere ā-wāhanga i ngā kaupapa o ia wiki, tae atu ki ngā taumahi, ngā whāinga akoranga, me ngā whāinga aromatawai. He mārama ngā mahere, ā, he ngāwari noa iho ki ngā kaimahi te whai atu. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia wiki hei whakawhiti kōrero, hei aroturuki hoki i ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka kōrero rātou mō te āhua o ngā taumahi, me ngā āhuatanga ka whai hua inā ka whakarerekē haere. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki, nā ngā whakaaro huritao o ngā kaimahi me ā rātou mahi ki te whakapai haere.

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i ngā take i hāngai ai ki te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, me te whai pānga o tēnā i taua wā ki te noho pūmautanga o te kōhanga reo. Kua whai wāhi atu te whānau ki te maha o ngā wānanga, tae atu ki te whakauru, ā, ka hāngai tēnei ki te tūtohinga me Te Korowai. I nāianei, he pai ake te māramatanga o te whānau ki ā rātou kawenga whakahaere. E noho ngākau nui ana te whānau ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki hei mātua, hei kaiwhakahaere hoki.

He pai ngā rauemi a te kōhanga reo. Me ātaahua ake, me wātea ake hoki te whakaatu o ngā kaimahi i ā rātou rauemi. Mā tēnei, ka taea e ngā tamariki te whiriwhiri me te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. E whakawhanake tonu ana ngā kaimahi i ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki. Me whakauru rātou i ngā mōhiohio ka tohu i te āhua o te ako a ngā tamariki me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Pou e Whā o Te Korowai te kaupapa arotahi mō te arotake i mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. Ko ngā pou e whā, ko te reo Māori, ko ngā hanganga e pā ana ki te kawenga takohanga, ko te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, ko te hauora me te haumaru.

I tāutuhia e te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga maha i raro i Ngā Pou e Whā hei whakatutuki ake. I hāngai aua wāhanga ki te whakamahere kaupapa, te aromatawai, me te aromātai hōtaka. He āwangawanga anō hoki ki te arotake whaiaro, te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, me te whakamahinga o te reo Māori, puta noa i te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Ngā whai wāhitanga ako a ngā tamariki.He pai te hanganga me te riterite o te hōtaka o ia rā, hei whakatutuki i ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. He whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga, te wheako i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo kaupapa, me te whai wāhi atu ki ngā tūmomo taumahi, tae atu ki ngā āhuatanga o ngā tikanga Māori. E āhei ana rātou ki te waihanga, te whakamahi i ō rātou pūkenga ā-tinana, te whakatū hanganga, me te ako hoki i ngā pūkenga e whai pānga ana ki te tīmatanga o te reo pāngarau me te reo matatini. Ka mārama, ka whai hoki ngā tamariki i ngā mahinga e rere ngāwari ana, puta noa i te rā.

Te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori.Ka kōkiri whakamua ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakawhanake i ō rātou ake āheinga i roto i te reo Māori, kia pai ake ai tō rātou hāpai i ā rātou tamariki. Kua uru atu ngā kaimahi ki ngā kaupapa ako a Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, tae atu ki Te Ara Tuatahi, Te Ara Tuarua, me te Tohu Whakapakari. Ka whakamahia aua kaupapa ako hei hāpai i te whakawhanake tonutanga o te reo Māori ki waenga i ngā kaimahi. Kōrero Māori ai ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa. Kua uru atu ētahi mema o te whānau ki ngā kaupapa ako e pā ana ki te reo Māori. He pakari te whānau ki te taunekeneke ki waenganui i a rātou anō, ki ngā tamariki hoki, mā te reo Māori.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga o tētahi taiao reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki hei whakaongaonga i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. Ko ētahi o aua rautaki, ko te patapatai, me te whai wāhi atu hoki ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero e whai pūtake ana. Kua pai ake te hāpai i ngā tamariki, i a rātou e whakawhanake haere ana i tō rātou reo Māori.

Te arotake whaiaro.Ka kitea te hōtaka o te arotake whaiaro. Ka aroturuki te whānau i ngā whakawhanaketanga me ngā whakaritenga, puta noa i te kōhanga reo. Ka kōrerohia, ka pūrongotia hoki ngā wāhanga whakahaere ki ngā hui ā-whānau. Ka hāngai ngā whakawhitinga kōrero a te whānau ki Te Korowai, ki te hauora me te haumaru, ki te whakatikatika me te tiaki, me te whakahaere pūtea, ngā kaimahi, me te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori. He maha ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga i arotakengia ai, i whakamanatia ai hoki e te whānau. Ka arotake tonu, ka whakahou tonu te whānau i tā rātou tūtohinga, kia hāngai ake ai ki ō rātou wawata, ā rātou whakaritenga hoki. Me whai whakaaro hoki te whānau ki te whakauru i tā rātou mahere rautaki hei kaupapa anō hoki o te rārangi take ki ngā hui ā-whānau o ia marama.

Te whakamahere kaupapa, te aromātai hōtaka.Kua toro atu ngā kaimahi ki te tautoko o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ki te whakapai ake i te whakamahere kaupapa me te aromātai hōtaka. Ka tāutu tētahi mahere ā-wāhanga i ngā kaupapa o ia wiki, tae atu ki ngā taumahi, ngā whāinga ako, me ngā whāinga aromatawai. He mārama ngā mahere, ā, he ngāwari noa iho mā ngā kaimahi ki te whai atu. Ka whakamahere ngātahi.

Ka whai hua te whakahaere o te aromātai hōtaka hei whakamōhio i ngā mahere ka whai ake, mō ngā akoranga me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia wiki ki te whakawhiti kōrero me te aroturuki hoki i ngā āhuatanga o te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka kōrero rātou mō te āhua o ngā taumahi, me ngā āhuatanga ka whakarerekēhia pea e rātou, kia pai ake ai ngā wheako akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Te whakapakari i te whakahaeretanga o te whānau.I tāutu te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2011, i ngā take i hāngai ai ki te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, me te whai pānga o tēnā i aua wā, ki te pūmau tonutanga o te kōhanga reo. He mea nui kia whakatōpū te whānau o tēnei wā i ā rātou tukanga ki te whakauru ki te kōhanga reo, hei āta whakarite i te noho mōhio o te whānau katoa ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga hoki hei mātua, hei kaiwhakahaere.

Kua whai wāhi atu te whānau ki te maha o ngā wānanga i whai pānga ai ki te hōtaka whakauru, te tūtohinga, Ngā Pou e Whā, Te Korowai, te whakawhanaungatanga me te whakamahere. He pai ake tō rātou māramatanga ki ā rātou kawenga ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. Kōrerohia ai, whakapūmautia ai hoki Te Korowai ki ia hui ā-whānau.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Ngā rauemi.Ahakoa te pai o ngā rauemi i te kōhanga reo, kāhore ngā kaimahi e whakawātea ana i ēnei mā ngā tamariki i ia te wā. Me ātaahua ake, me wātea ake hoki te whakaatu a ngā kaimahi i ngā rauemi mā ngā tamariki. Ka whai hua tēnei ki te auaha, ngā pūkenga hīraurau hopanga, te whakawhanaketanga reo, me te tupu ā-tinana hoki o ngā tamariki.

Te aromatawai.E whakawhanake tonu ana ngā kaimahi i ngā pūkete ā ngā tamariki. E hiahia ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakauru i ngā mōhiohio e tohu ana i tēnā e akohia ana e ia tamaiti, me ōna whakawhanaketanga. Ahakoa e āhei ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakaatu i tētahi tauira o ngā pūkete e hiahiatia ana, kāhore anō ēnei kia oti.

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia tahuri te whānau ki te:

  • whakapai tonu, te whakapakari tonu i ngā wāhanga e ahu whakamua ana, ā, i tāutuhia ai hoki ki tēnei pūrongo
  • rapu i te tautoko hei whakapai ake i ngā whakaritenga aromatawai, me te whakahaere, te whakarato hoki i ngā rauemi.

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kākāriki MaraeThis supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance ofgoverning body and management in relation to areas identified in the July 2011ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • Ngā Pou e Whā including kaupapa planning, assessment and programme evaluation, self review, whānau management and improving the use of te reo Māori
  • Areas of non-compliance including fire and earthquake evacuation drills and staff training in drills
  • and any issues identified since the previous review.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kakariki Marae is located in the suburb of Glen Eden, West Auckland. The kōhanga reo used to be on the grounds of Greenbay High School alongside Kakariki Marae. In 2009, the kōhanga reo moved to its current site. This site was formerly owned by the Glen Eden Women’s Bowling Club. The kōhanga reo has been refurbished and renovated to accommodate the needs of the children. The whānau is currently in the process of changing the name of the kōhanga reo to Te Kōhanga Reo o Kakariki.

The 2011 Education Review Office report identified several areas under Ngā Pou e Whā that needed to be addressed. These included kaupapa planning, assessment, programme evaluation, self review and whānau management. The use of te reo Māori across the kōhanga reo, holding fire and earthquake drills and staff training in these emergency procedures were additional concerns that were identified.

The daily programme is well structured and balanced. Children have opportunities to follow their interests, experience a range of kaupapa and participate in various activities that include learning about tikanga Māori.

The kaimahi and whānau are proactive. They have focussed on developing their te reo Māori capabilities to better support their children. Kaimahi are enrolled in Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) courses. Children are immersed in a rich te reo Māori environment. They are well supported as developing speakers of te reo Māori.

A programme of self review has been developed to monitor developments and practices across the kōhanga reo. The different areas of the kōhanga reo operations are discussed and reported at monthly whānau hui. Many policies have been reviewed and ratified. The whānau continues to review and update their charter to better reflect their aspirations and practices.

Processes for kaupapa planning and programme evaluation have improved. A term plan identifies kaupapa for each week and includes activities, learning goals and assessment objectives. Plans are clear and easy for kaimahi to follow. Kaimahi meet each week to discuss and monitor aspects of the daily programme. They discuss how activities went and what might be done differently. Children benefit from kaimahi reflection and improvements.

The previous ERO report identified issues with whānau management which had previously impacted on the viability of the kōhanga reo. The whānau has been involved in several wānanga including induction that covers the charter and Te Korowai. The whānau now has a better understanding of their management responsibilities. Whānau are committed to their roles and responsibilities as parents and managers.

The kōhanga reo is well resourced. Kaimahi should display their resources in a more attractive and accessible manner. This will enable children to make choices and follow their interests. Kaimahi are still developing children’s portfolios. They need to include information that will show how children are learning and developing.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Findings

Background

The whānau chose Ngā Pou e Whā o Te Korowai as the focus area for their previous ERO review. The four pou included te reo Māori, accountability structures, whānau management and health and safety.

The ERO report identified several areas under Ngā Pou e Whā that needed to be addressed. These included kaupapa planning, assessment and programme evaluation. There were additional concerns with self review, whānau management and the use of te reo Māori across the kōhanga reo.

Areas of progress

Children’s learning opportunities. The daily programme is well structured and balanced to meet the needs and interests of children. Children have opportunities to follow their interests, experience a range of kaupapa and participate in various activities that include aspects of tikanga Māori. They are able to create, use their physical skills, build constructions and learn early numeracy and literacy skills. Children understand and follow the routines which flow smoothly throughout the day.

Te reo Māori development. The kaimahi and whānau are proactive in developing their te reo Māori capabilities to better support their children. Kaimahi are enrolled in Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) courses including Te Ara Tuatahi, Te Ara Tuarua and Te Tohu Whakapakari. These programmes are used to support the ongoing development of te reo Māori among kaimahi. Kaimahi and whānau consistently speak te reo Māori to children. Some whānau members are enrolled in te reo Māori courses. Whānau confidently interact with each other and children in te reo Māori.

Children are immersed in a rich te reo Māori environment. Kaimahi use various strategies to stimulate children’s language development. These include questioning and engaging in meaningful conversations. The children are better supported as developing speakers of te reo Māori.

Self review. A programme of self review is evident. Whānau monitor developments and practices across the kōhanga reo. Areas of operations are discussed and reported at whānau hui. Whānau discussions involve Te Korowai, health and safety, repairs and maintenance, financial management, personnel and te reo Māori development. Many policies have been reviewed and ratified by the whānau. The whānau continues to review and update their charter to better reflect their aspirations and practices. The whānau should also consider including their strategic plan as an agenda item at monthly whānau hui.

Kaupapa planning and programme evaluation. Kaimahi have sought support from Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust to improve kaupapa planning and programme evaluation. A term plan identifies kaupapa for each week and includes activities, learning goals and assessment objectives. Plans are clear and easy for kaimahi to follow. Planning is done collaboratively.

Programme evaluation is used effectively to inform further planning for children’s learning and development. Kaimahi meet each week to discuss and monitor aspects of the daily programme. They discuss how activities went and what they might do differently to improve children’s learning experiences.

Strengthening whānau management. The 2011 ERO report identified issues with whānau management which had previously impacted on the viability of the kōhanga reo. It was imperative for the current whānau to solidify their induction process to ensure that all whānau were aware of their roles and responsibilities as parents and managers.

The whānau has been involved in many wānanga including an induction programme, the charter, Ngā Pou e Whā, Te Korowai, whakawhanaungatanga and planning. They have a better understanding of their responsibility to manage the kōhanga reo. Te Korowai is regularly discussed and reinforced at each whānau hui.

Areas for further improvement

Resources. Although the kōhanga reo is well resourced, kaimahi do not always make these available to children. Kaimahi should display resources in a more attractive and accessible manner to children. This should benefit children’s creativity, problem solving skills, language development and physical growth.

Assessment. Kaimahi are still developing their children’s portfolios. Kaimahi would like to include information that shows each child’s learning and development. While kaimahi are able to show an example of what portfolios might look like, these remain incomplete.

4 Recommendations

ERO recommends that the whānau:

  • continue to improve and strengthen the areas of progress identified in this report
  • seek support to further improve assessment practices and resource management and provision.

To the Parents and Community of Te Kōhanga Reo o Kākāriki Marae

This supplementary review evaluates the quality of education and care received by the tamariki and the performance of the whānau in relation to specific terms of reference.

The Education Review Office [ERO] evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Kākāriki Marae follows.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kakariki Marae is located in the suburb of Glen Eden, West Auckland. The kōhanga reo used to be on the grounds of Greenbay High School alongside Kakariki Marae. In 2009, the kōhanga reo moved to its current site. This site was formerly owned by the Glen Eden Women’s Bowling Club. The kōhanga reo has been refurbished and renovated to accommodate the needs of the children. The whānau is currently in the process of changing the name of the kōhanga reo to Te Kōhanga Reo o Kakariki.

The 2011 Education Review Office report identified several areas under Ngā Pou e Whā that needed to be addressed. These included kaupapa planning, assessment, programme evaluation, self review and whānau management. The use of te reo Māori across the kōhanga reo, holding fire and earthquake drills and staff training in these emergency procedures were additional concerns that were identified.

The daily programme is well structured and balanced. Children have opportunities to follow their interests, experience a range of kaupapa and participate in various activities that include learning about tikanga Māori.

The kaimahi and whānau are proactive. They have focussed on developing their te reo Māori capabilities to better support their children. Kaimahi are enrolled in Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) courses. Children are immersed in a rich te reo Māori environment. They are well supported as developing speakers of te reo Māori.

A programme of self review has been developed to monitor developments and practices across the kōhanga reo. The different areas of the kōhanga reo operations are discussed and reported at monthly whānau hui. Many policies have been reviewed and ratified. The whānau continues to review and update their charter to better reflect their aspirations and practices.

Processes for kaupapa planning and programme evaluation have improved. A term plan identifies kaupapa for each week and includes activities, learning goals and assessment objectives. Plans are clear and easy for kaimahi to follow. Kaimahi meet each week to discuss and monitor aspects of the daily programme. They discuss how activities went and what might be done differently. Children benefit from kaimahi reflection and improvements.

The previous ERO report identified issues with whānau management which had previously impacted on the viability of the kōhanga reo. The whānau has been involved in several wānanga including induction that covers the charter and Te Korowai. The whānau now has a better understanding of their management responsibilities. Whānau are committed to their roles and responsibilities as parents and managers.

The kōhanga reo is well resourced. Kaimahi should display their resources in a more attractive and accessible manner. This will enable children to make choices and follow their interests. Kaimahi are still developing children’s portfolios. They need to include information that will show how children are learning and developing.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

When ERO has reviewed a kōhanga reo we ask the whānau of the kōhanga reo to let us know how they intend to manage any difficulties set out in their report (copies of which are available from the kōhanga reo or ERO). We also encourage whānau to inform their community of any follow up action plan. You should talk to the whānau if you have any questions about this evaluation, the full ERO report or their future intentions.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
