1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā tētahi o ngā Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia ai i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia e te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i mua atu, me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

  • te Mana Whenua – te hōtaka akoranga me te whakahaeretanga o ngā tamariki
  • te whakamahere me te aromātai – te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, te whakahaeretanga i te hōtaka, te arotake me te aromatawai
  • ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutukihia ana e ai ki te ture – ngā hōtaka, te whakahaere pūtea, te hauora ngā mahi haumaru, me te arohaehae
  • ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai whai muri i te arotake o mua.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Parikino ki tētahi marae ki te awa o Whanganui, ki Parikino. Nō ngā iwi o tēnei takiwā te nuinga o ngā whānau. E noho pūmau ana rātou ki te whakatairanga i te reo Māori me ōna tikanga ki ngā tamariki me te whānau mā te kōhanga reo.

Ko te pūrongo arotake tāpiri a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori o Haratua 2013 i taunaki kia whakapai te whānau i te ako me te atawhai o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Ka hāngai hoki tēnei ki ngā āhuatanga o te whakahaeretanga o te whānau me te hōtaka akoranga. I whai wāhi te whānau ki te waihanga o tētahi pātai aromātai ka ārahi i tēnei arotake, ā, mā te whai whakaaro anō hoki ki ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga o te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori, kua toko ake te pātai aromātai e mea ana:

He pēhea rawa te whakaongaonga i ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga, te poipoi hoki i ō rātou matea?

Ka whakaongaongatia ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga, ā, he tau rātou ki te kōhanga reo.

He pai ake te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te whakarite i ngā hōtaka akoranga. Ko ō rātou manakohanga ako ka whakaaroaro. E mōhio ana, e aro atu ana hoki ngā kaimahi ki ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Ko ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki waenga i ngā tamariki mō ngā akoranga ka poipoia. Ka whakatairangatia ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori mā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi, me te pai ake hoki o ngā taunekeneke ako. He māramatanga ngātahi tō ngā mema o te whānau ki ngā whakaritenga papai i a rātou e whakatutuki ana i ngā matea o ngā tamariki katoa.

Kua whakapau kaha te whānau ki te whakapai ake i ngā wāhanga katoa o ngā mahi whakahaere o te kōhanga reo. He kaha ake te whai wāhi atu ki ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga. Ka arotahi rātou ki te haere tonutanga o te ahu whakamua me ngā whakapaitanga, hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Whenua

He whakamārama

He pēhea rawa te whakaongaonga i ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga, te poipoi hoki i ō rātou matea?

Ka whakaongaongatia ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga, ā, he tau rātou ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He hihiri ngā tamariki ki te ako. Kua whakapau kaha ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakapai ake i te hōtaka akoranga me ngā whakaritenga, whai muri i te arotake o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori. Ko te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi, rauemi hoki e hāpai ana i te ako takitahi me te ako ngātahi. Kua pai ake te kounga o ngā taunekeneke i roto i ngā akoranga. Ka poipoia e ngā kaimahi kia whai wāhi ai ngā whakawhitinga kōrero a ngā tamariki e hāngai ana ki te ako. Kei te pakari haere te māia o ngā tamariki. Ka whakatau rātou i ā rātou ake taumahi ako, ā, he auaha tā rātou tūhura ki te taha o tō rātou whānau. He harikoa, he tau hoki rātou ki te ako.

Kei te pakari haere te māia, te tākare hoki o ngā tamariki ki te taunekeneke. He māramatanga ngātahi tō te whānau ki te whakamahi i ngā tikanga pai e pā ana ki te whakahaere tamariki. He pai te taunekeneke a ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki i a rātou e tūhura haere ana i tō rātou taiao. Ko te ako reo a ngā tamariki, me ō rātou hononga ki waenga i a rātou anō, e whakatairangatia ana. He tōtika te aro a ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga e puāwai ana i ngā tamariki. He pai te atawhai i ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki. Ka tino kitea tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. E tohu ana ngā pūrongo o ia marama ki ngā hui, i te māramatanga ake puta noa i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kua pai ake te whakahaere i te pūtea. Kua tīmatahia te arohaehae. He pūmau te aronga ki te tupu me te whakawhanaketanga.

Kua waihangatia te hōtaka akoranga hei whakaongaonga i ngā tamariki, hei aro ki ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, ā, hei poipoi hoki i ō rātou matea. Ka whakarato te kaiako i te pai o te ārahitanga me te tautoko ki te whānau e hāpai ana i te hōtaka akoranga. Ko ngā manakohanga akoranga o ngā tamariki ka tino tautokona. Ka kitea te māia o ngā tamariki ki tā rātou whakatau i ā rātou ake whiringa, i ā rātou whakapuakitanga hoki ki ētahi atu mō ā rātou akoranga, tā rātou eke angitu hoki.

Kua kaha ake te whānau ki te aroturuki i te whai huatanga o ngā hōtaka akoranga mā ngā tamariki. Ka akiaki te kaiako i te whakaaro huritao ngaio i ia te wā, i waenga i ngā kaimahi me te whānau mō te whai pānga o ngā hōtaka ki te whakatutuki i te whānuitanga o ngā matea o ngā tamariki. Ka whai pānga nui tēnei mahi ki te whakawhanaketanga o ngā hōtaka. Ka āta kitea te whakaritenga o te taiao, ngā rauemi, me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki hei wāhanga i āta whakawhanakehia ai mā ngā tamariki whai muri i te arotake o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake

Kei te whakawhanake tonu ngā mahi aromatawai mā ngā tamariki. Ināianei, ka whakaemihia ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki te ako reo a ngā tamariki. Me whakapai ake tonu ngā mahi aromatawai hei āta kapo atu i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i roto i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wāhanga.

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakaae te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakamahi te whānau i te arotake whaiaro hei whakamōhio i ngā whakataunga katoa e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, te whakamahere, me te whānuitanga o te āhua o ā rātou mahi ki te kōhanga reo
  • whakawhanake tonu i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, tae atu ki ngā aromatawai e pā ana ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation on the performance of the governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the previous ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • Mana Whenua - the learning programme and child management
  • Planning and evaluation – whānau management, programme management and review and assessment
  • Areas of non compliance-programming, financial management, health, safety practices, and appraisal
  • any issues identified since the previous review.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga reo o Parikino is marae based and located on the Whanganui river in Parikino. Most whānau connect to iwi in this area. They are committed to promoting te reo Māori me ona tikanga to children and whānau through kōhanga reo.

The ERO Supplementary review report in May 2013, recommended that whānau improve learning and care for children at the kōhanga reo. This included aspects of whānau management and the learning programme. Whānau participated in the design of an evaluation question, informed by the terms of reference of the previous ERO report, to guide this review:

To what extent are children stimulated in their learning and their needs catered for?

Children are stimulated in their learning, and they are settled in the kōhanga reo.

Children receive better planned, managed and organised programmes of learning. Their preferences for learning are considered. Kaimahi recognise and respond to the interests of children. Conversations between children about learning are fostered. Children’s learning of te reo Māori is enhanced through the variety of activities, and improved learning interactions. Whānau members have a shared understanding about good practice when addressing the needs of all children.

The whānau have worked hard on improving all areas of kōhanga reo management practices. Thre is greater involvement and participation across all areas of operations. They are focussed on ongoing progress and improvement to benefit children.

3 Findings

Mana Whenua

Background

To what extent are children stimulated in their learning and their needs catered for?

Children are stimulated in their learning, and they are settled in the kōhanga reo.

Areas of progress

Children are excited about learning. Kaimahi and the whānau have worked hard since the previous ERO review to improve the learning programme and practices. A wide variety of activities and resources enable independent and shared learning. The quality of interactions for learning has improved. More learning conversations with children are fostered by kaimahi. Children are building their confidence. They determine their own learning activities and explore creatively beside their whānau. They are happy and content in their learning.

Children interact with growing confidence and enthusiasm. There is a shared understanding amongst whānau about the use of positive child management practices. Kaimahi interact positively with children as they explore. Children’s language learning and their relationships with each other are enhanced. Kaimahi respond appropriately to the emerging needs and interests of children. Children are well cared for.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of progress

Whānau are managing the kōhanga reo well for children. Their involvement and participation in all areas of the kōhanga reo is strongly evident. Monthly hui reports show greater transparency across all areas of kōhanga reo operations. Financial management has improved. Appraisal has begun. There is a continued focus on growth and development.

The learning programme is designed to stimulate children, respond to their interests and cater for their needs. The kaiako provides good leadership and support to whānau who assist with the learning programme. Children’s preferences for learning are well supported. They are happy in their learning and their environment. Children’s confidence is seen in the way they make their own choices and share with others about their learning and success.

The success of learning programmes for children are monitored more closely by whānau. The kaiako encourages regular professional reflection amongst kaimahi and whānau about how well programmes meet the diverse needs of children. This practice is having a positive impact on programme developments. The organisation of the environment, resources and children’s interests come through clearly as areas that have developed well for children since the previous ERO review.

Area for further development

Assessment practices for children are still developing. More information is now collected about children’s language learning. Further improvement in assessment practice is needed to fully capture children’s learning in a range of areas.

4 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau agreed to the following recommendations:

  • use whānau self review to inform all whānau management decisions, planning and the overall performance of their kōhanga reo
  • continue developing all areas of whānau management, including assessments of children’s learning.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
