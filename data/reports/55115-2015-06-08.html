1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Taihoa ki te marae o Taihoa, ki Wairoa. Ko Ngāti Kurupakiaka te hapū, ko Ngāti Kahungunu te iwi. Ko te kōhanga reo tētahi wāhanga o te marae whānui, ā, kei te kōhanga reo tōna ake wāhi, me ōna ake whare. Ka kīia, ko te kōhanga reo te pūtaketanga o te marae. E whakanui ana tēnei i te noho a ngā tamariki ki te pūtake o te whānau, te hapū, te marae, me te iwi. He hiahia tō te whānau kia matatau, kia whai mātauranga hoki ā rātou tamariki ki te reo me ngā tikanga Māori, te kawa o te marae, me te whenua.

I ngā wā katoa, ka noho pūmau te tokomaha o ngā tamariki ki te rārangi ingoa, me te kaha tae atu ki te kōhanga reo, ā, ka ngākau nuitia e ngā whānau. Haere ai ngā tuākana i ia te wā ki ngā akoranga whakawhiti ki te akomanga rumaki reo ki te kura o tēnei takiwā, hei whakarite i a rātou ki te haere ki te kura.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te tautoko i ngā tamariki kia mārama, kia kōrero hoki rātou i te reo Māori ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki?

E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki kia mārama, kia kōrero hoki rātou i te reo Māori ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka tino rumakina ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori me ngā tikanga o Ngāti Kurupakiaka. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo o tō rātou hapū me tō rātou iwi. Ka rongo rātou i ngā tauira reo pai ki te kōhanga reo me te marae. Ko ngā wheako ako katoa i whakamaheretia ai, i pupū ake ai rānei, ka whakapuakihia mā te reo Māori. He tokomaha ngā tamariki e āhei ana ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero me ō rātou hoa, ā, ki ngā pakeke hoki. He pakari tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki te hōhonutanga o ngā taumahi, tae atu ki ngā mōteatea, ngā mihimihi, ngā pūrākau, me ngā waiata koroua. Ka rongo, ka whakamahi rānei ngā tamariki i ngā taki kōrero e tāutu ana i tō rātou whakapapa, ā, e hāpai ana ki te whakapūmau i ngā mātāpono o te whanaungatanga. Kei te pakari haere, kei te tika haere hoki te reo Māori e whakamahia ana e ngā tamariki.

Ka āta mārama, ka āta whai atu hoki ngā tamariki i ngā mahinga. I ia te wā, ka korikori tinana rātou, ā, ka kai i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo kai hauora. I te nuinga o te wā, kei te whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi tākaro e whai pūtake ana, ā, nā te tamaiti anō i kōkiri. He harikoa ngā tamariki, ā, ka tautokona e ngā kaimahi e atawhai ana, e manaaki ana hoki.

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i te aronga toi whenuatanga me te tuakiri mā ō rātou hononga ki te whenua me te taiao. He tau te noho o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo, ki te marae, ā, ki ngā taiao māoriori hoki e karapoti ana i te kōhanga reo. Ka ākona e ngā tamariki ngā mōteatea, ngā waiata, me ngā haka e tūhono ana i a rātou ki te whenua e noho nei rātou. E ako ana ngā tamariki i te tū hei tangata whenua o te marae, mā te tautoko hoki o te whānau me ngā kaumātua. He tika te whai a ngā tamariki i ngā tikanga Māori.

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā hōtaka akoranga e whakakoia ana i tō rātou reo me ō rātou hononga ā-whatumanawa, ā-tangata, ā-wairua hoki. He tika te kawe a ngā tamariki i a rātou anō, i a rātou e whakaatu ana i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki ngā tikanga e whai pānga ana ki te mana atua. He tākare tā rātou taki i ngā karakia mō ngā tūmomo pūtake i ia te wā, puta noa i te rā. Kua korowaitia ngā tamariki ki ngā uaratanga me ngā whakapono tuku iho e poipoi ana i tō rātou waiora, ā, e hāpai ana i te whakawhanaketanga o tō rātou reo Māori.

He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te haere ki ngā wāhi whakahirahira i te hapori. Ka ako, ka whakamahia e ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā horopaki akoranga. E āhei ana rātou ki te whakamahi i ō rātou tairongoki te tūhura i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taonga Māori. Kei te waia haere ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao tūturu, ā, kei te puāwai ake te māramatanga, te noho whakaute hoki mō tō rātou ao me tō rātou reo. whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o tō rātou ao mā roto mai i te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka tautokona ngā tamariki nā te whai huatanga o te whakamahere hōtaka e tino aro tahi ana ki ngā ariā o te ao Māori. Waihoki, ko ngā mahere o te wā roa me te wā poto hei arataki, hei tautoko hoki i ngā kaimahi. He tino pai rawa atu te whakamahi a ngā kaimahi i ngā mahi arotake hei aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te hōtaka akoranga, ā tae atu hoki ki ngā mahi aromatawai. Ko te aromātai i aua wāhanga e tino whai pānga nui ana ki te pai o te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whanaketanga reo.

Ka whai hua ngā mahi aromatawai ki te kapo atu i te ahunga whakamua me ngā whakatutukitanga a ngā tamariki. He whānui ngā tūmomo aromatawai, he mārama ngā kōrerorero, ā, ka whakatakoto hoki i ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki me ngā mahi tonu ka whai ake. Ka whakawhiti whakaaro te whānau me ngā kaimahi mō te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki, puta noa i te tau. Ka whakatairangatia te wheako whakawhanaketanga o ia tamaiti, te reo Māori, me te waiora hoki mā roto i ngā mahi aromatawai mārama.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He aronga ōpaki tā te whānau ki te whakamahere i te aronga whakamua o te kōhanga reo. Ahakoa e mōhio ana te whānau ki ngā whāinga o Te Ara Tūāpae e kitea ai te taumata pūmanawa, kāhore anō rātou kia whakawhanake i tō rātou ake mahere ā-tau kia āhei te aroturuki, te tuku pūrongo e taea ai te whakatutuki i ō rātou whāinga roa me ngā whāing whāiti.

Kua tīmata noa te whakawhanaketanga i te arotake whaiaro. Ahakoa ka whakawhiti kōrero, ka pūrongo hoki te whānau mō ngā tūmomo wāhanga o ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, kāhore tēnei e whakatutukihia ana e rātou i ia te wā. Ko te āta whakatakoto i ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki aua whakawhitinga kōrero, ka whakapai mārika i ngā tukanga e pā ana ki ngā whakataunga me te pūrongo, ā, ka whai hua anō hoki tēnei mō ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakaae Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau ki te mahi ngātahi me te kaupapa kaimahi, ki te whakawhanake i ngā whakaritenga ka whai ake, kia tutuki ai ngā whakapaitanga ki te kōhanga reo.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Taihoa ki Wairoa. He pai ki ngā tamariki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana, e akiaki ana i a rātou ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori mā roto i ngā taunekeneketanga o ia rā i waenga i ngā kaimahi, ā, i waenga i a rātou anō hoki, tēnā ki tēnā. Kei te pakari haere te māia o te nuinga o ngā tamariki ki te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero e rangona ai te whānuitanga o ngā horopaki. E mōhio ana te whānau me whakawhanake a rātou tino mahi me ngā whāinga e arotahi ana ki ngā painga o te ako angitu mō ā rātou tamariki.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōhanga Reo o Taihoa i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Taihoa is at Taihoa Marae in Wairoa. Ngāti Kurupakiaka is the hapū and the iwi is Ngāti Kahungunu. The kōhanga reo is a part of the greater marae complex and has its own area and facilities. The kōhanga reo is known as the ‘pūtaketanga,’ the centre of the marae. This acknowledges that children are the central focus of whānau, hapū, marae and iwi. The whānau want their children to be fluent and knowledgeable in te reo and tikanga Māori, and kawa of the marae and whenua.

The kōhanga reo has consistently maintained a high roll and high attendances and is very popular among whānau. The tuakana regularly attend orientation classes at the local immersion unit where they are introduced to kura.

2 The Review Findings

How well are children supported to understand and speak Maori in a variety of contexts?

Children are supported well to understand and speak Maori in a variety of contexts?

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are successfully immersed in te reo Māori me ngā tikanga o Ngāti Kurupakiaka. Children are exposed to language specific to their hapū and iwi. They hear good language role models in the kōhanga reo and at the marae. All children’s planned and unplanned learning experiences are delivered in te reo Māori. Several children are able to participate in conversations with their peers and adults. They confidently participate in complex activities including mōteatea, mihimihi, pūrakau and waiata koroua. Children hear or use recitations that define their whakapapa and help reinforce the principles of whanaungatanga. Most children use te reo Māori with increasing confidence and accuracy.

Children understand and follow routines well. They regularly exercise and eat a range of healthy kai. Children spend most of their time in productive, play-based and child initiated activity. Children are happy and supported by warm and caring kaimahi.

Children experience belonging and identity through their connections with the whenua and environment. Children are comfortable being at the kōhanga reo, on the marae grounds and in natural environments that surround the kōhanga reo. The mōteatea, waiata and haka that children learn connect them to the surrounding whenua. Children are learning about the role of the tangata whenua of the marae with support from the whānau and kaumātua. Children follow tikanga Māori appropriately.

Children experience learning programmes that affirm their language and emotional, cultural and spiritual connections. Children behave appropriately when demonstrating their knowledge of tikanga specific to mana atua. They recite karakia at various times throughout the day and for different purposes. Children are cloaked by traditional values and beliefs that foster their wellbeing that support their reo Māori development. .

Children visit places of interest in the community. Children learn and use language in a range of learning contexts. They use their senses to explore the different environments and natural resources. Children are becoming familiar with their physical environment and are gaining a greater understanding and respect for their world through te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Children are supported through well planned programmes of learning that focus on te ao Māori concepts. These include long and short term plans to guide and support kaimahi. Kaimahi use evaluation effectively to monitor all aspects of the learning programme, including assessment practices. Evaluation of these areas significantly impact positively on children’s learning and language development.

Assessment practices successfully capture children’s progress and successes. Assessments are varied, clearly annotated and include children’s interests and next steps. Whānau and kaimahi discuss children’s progress throughout the year. The child’s personal development, te reo Maori and wellbeing is uplifted through clear assessment practices.

Key Next Steps

Whānau use an informal approach when planning for the future direction of the kōhanga reo. While whānau are familiar with the goals of Te Ara Tuāpae to identify potential growth, they have not yet developed their own annual plan to assist them to monitor and report against their long and short term goals.

Self review is in the early stages of development. Although whānau discuss and report on different parts of the kōhanga reo operations, they do not carry this out on a regular basis. Keeping clear records of these discussions will significantly improve decision-making and reporting procedures which have a direct impact on children.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the whānau agreed to work with the kaupapa kaimahi to develop the next steps for kōhanga reo improvement.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Taihoa is in Wairoa. Children enjoy a learning environment that nurtures and encourages their use of te reo Māori through daily interactions with kaimahi and each other. Most children are developing confidence to speak te reo Māori in a range of conversational contexts. Whānau acknowledge they have yet to develop their priorities and goals that focus on successful learning outcomes for their children.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Taihoa will be in three years.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori
