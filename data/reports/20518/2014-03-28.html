1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kei i te whanga Hauāuru o Tamaki Makaurau Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Maungarongo e tu ana pātata an ki Te Piringa Tahi Te Maungarongo Marae. He kōhanga reo iti tēnei mō ngā tamariki, whānau hoki mai i te hauāuru o Tamaki Makaurau.

Mai i te pūrongo o Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga o Pipiri 2012 i kitea ēnei wāhanga hei whakatikatika:

  • te paihere o te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te reo Māori
  • te hōtaka ako
  • te taiao o te kōhanga reo
  • ngā whakahaere o te whānau me ngā kaimahi
  • ngā mahi arotake whaiaro, me
  • te whakakaha ake i ngā mahi whakahaere

Whakamahia ai e ngā kaimahi te hōtaka hei whakaputa i te reo Māori i te roanga o te ra. Ka kōrero tahi ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki i a rātou e mahi, e tākaro ana. Katahi anō tētahi kuia o tēnei takiwā, tino mātau ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, kia uru mai ki te rōpū kaimahi. E hāngai āna mahi ki te tautoko, ki te āwhina i ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori. Kua noho tahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te wānanga iTe Whārikime ngā whakangūngū mō te reo Māori ki te hiki ake i ō rātou pūkenga, māiatanga hoki. E whai wā ana ngā tamariki ki ngā ngohe me ngā wheako ako e whakahaerehia ana i roto i te reo Māori.

Kua piki ake te kaha o te whānau i roto i ngā whakahaere o te kōhanga reo. Kua arotakehia te tukanga arataki, ā, e mārama ana te whakaatu ki ngā whānau hou me a rātou tūnga, whakataunga mahi me te kaupapa hoki o te kōhanga reo. Kei te whānau he mahere roa e hāngai ana ki Te Ara Tūāpae. E whakaatu ana tēnei mahere i ngā tino hiahia hei whakawhanake, tāpiri atu i ngā whanaketanga mō te whānau me ngā kaimahi me te whakatikatika i te papa tākaro. Kua oti te arotake i te Tūtohinga me ngā kaupapa here whakahaere hoki. Kua timata te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te arotake i ngā pūnaha katoa me ngā whakahaere.

Kāore tonu te hōtaka ako e whai hanganga, māramatanga, whānuitanga. Ruarua noa ngā whai wā mō ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou hiahia.

Ahakoa kua piki ake ngā mahi whakamahere kaupapa, kei te whanake tonu ngā aromātai hōtaka me ngā aromatawai.

Ngā Mahi ā Meake

Ko te whakaaro a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia hoki anō ki te kōhanga i roto i te tekau mā rua marama e tū mai nei ki te aromātai i te ahu whakamua a te whānau i roto i tā rātou whakautu ki ngā tūtohunga o tēnei pūrongo.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

Mai i te pūrongo o Pipiri 2012 i kitea ko ēnei ngā wāhanga hei whakatikatika. Ko te ngākaunui o te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te reo Māori, te hōtaka ako me te taiao o te kōhanga reo. He ōrite te kore mōhio o te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakatakoto rerenga kōrero māmā o te reo Māori. Kāore he rauemi e whai kounga mō ngā tamariki. Kāore i tika ngā whakaritenga me ngā whainga o te hōtaka ako

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Te whakawhanake i te reo Māori.Whakamahia ai e ngā kaimahi te hōtaka hei whakaputa i te reo Māori i te roanga o te ra. Ka kōrero tahi ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki i a rātou e mahi, e tākaro ana. Kātahi anō tētahi kuia o tēnei takiwā, tino mātau ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, kia uru mai ki te rōpū kaimahi. E hāngai āna mahi ki te tautoko, ki te āwhina i ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori. Kua uru atu ētahi o te whānau ki tēnā, ki tēnā o ngā whakangungu reo Māori hei hiki ake i ō rātou kaha ki te kōrero. E whai wā ana ngā tamariki ki ngā ngohe me ngā wheako ako e whakahaerehia ana i roto i te reo Māori.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te hōtaka ako.Kāore tonu te hōtaka ako e whai hanganga, māramatanga, whānuitanga mō ngā tamariki. Ruarua noa ngā whai wā mō ngā tamariki ki te whai i o rātou hiahia.

Ruarua noa ngā whai wā mō ngā tamariki ki te whakawhānui atu i ō rātou ake hiahia, ako hoki. He ōpaki te waiora o ngā kaimahi ki ngā whakahaere o te hōtaka ia ra. Ahakoa kei te whiwhi i ētahi rauemi, he iti noa te huhua o ngā rauemi hei aupiki, hei hopu i ngā hiahia o ngā tamariki.

Te whakamāherehere me te aromātai

He whakamārama

Mai i te pūrongo mua ake nei i kitea te ko ēnei ngā wāhanga hei whakapakari ake. Ko ngā whakahaere whānau, kaimahi, ngā mahi arotake whaiaro, me te whakapakari ake i ngā whakahaeare.

Mai i taua pūrongo i mahitahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi i te taha o te purapura me Te Tari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te whakatikatika i ngā wāhanga hei whakapiki ake i taua pūrongo.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Ngā whakahaere a te whānau.Kua piki ake te kaha o te whānau i roto i ngā whakahaere o te kōhanga reo. Kua arotakehia te tukanga arataki, ā, e mārama ana te whakaatu ki ngā whānau hou me a rātou tūnga, whakataunga mahi me te kaupapa hoki o te kōhanga reo

Kua noho tahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te wānanga iTe Whārikime ngā whakangungu mō te reo Māori ki te hiki ake i ō rātou pūkenga, māiatanga hoki. Kei te whānau he mahere roa e hāngai ana ki Te Ara Tūāpae. E whakaatu ana tēnei mahere i ngā tino hiahia hei whakawhanake, tāpiri atu i ngā whanaketanga mō te whānau me ngā kaimahi me te whakatikatika i te papa tākaro.

He kaha te kaiwhakahaere ki te whakamōhio mai i pānui, ngā kōrero ōpaki rānei i te kōhanga reo ki te whānau i ia wā. Kua piki ake te kaha o te whānau i roto i ngā whakahaere o te kōhanga reo hei tautoko i ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā whakahaere kaimahi.Kua piki ake te nuinga o ngā wāhanga o ngā whakahaere kaimahi mai i te pūrongo o mua ake nei. Kua uru mai ētahi kaimahi hou. Kua oti ngā arohaehae, e mārama ana te kite i ngā kaha, ngā aupiki me ngā whakawhanake ngaiotanga. Hui tahi ai ngā kaimahi ia wiki ka whakawhitiwhiti kōrero e pā ana ki ngā āhuatanga mahi, te hōtaka ako me ngā whakahaere o ia rangi i te kōhanga reo.

Arotake whaiaro.Mai i te pūrongo o 2012 i kitea kāore anō ngā kaupapa here, whakahaere hoki, me ngā mahi whakahaere i arotakehia kia whakaata mai i ngā mahi a te whānau me te ture. Kua oti te arotake i te Tūtohinga me ngā kaupapa here, whakahaere hoki. Kua tīmata te whānau ki te arotake i ngā pūnaha katoa me ngā whakahaere.

Ngā whakahaere.I tohua te kaiwhakahaere i te marama o Whiringa-ā-nuku 2011. Nā tōna kaha i whakaritea ngā pūnaha whai hua hei whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. E mahi tata atu ana ki ngā kaimahi o Te Tari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo arā, kei te whakangungu kia mau tonu ai ngā whakahaere tika me te whakanui i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Ngā mahi aromātai, aromatawai hōtaka.Ahakoa kua piki ake ngā mahi whakamahere kaupapa, kei te whanake tonu ngā aromātai hōtaka me ngā aromatawai. He hou tonu te nuinga o ngā kaimahi ki te kōhanga reoā, kei te ako tonu i ō rātoutūnga, whakataunga mahi.

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Maungarongo is located beside Te Piringa Tahi Te Maungarongo Marae in the urban area of West Harbour, Auckland. This small kōhanga reo caters for children and their whānau from the West Auckland area.

The June 2012 ERO report identified areas to be addressed which included:

  • whānau and kaimahi commitment to te reo Māori
  • the learning programme
  • the kōhanga reo environment
  • whānau and staff management
  • self review practices, and
  • strengthening administration practices.

Kaimahi use the learning programme to promote the use te reo Māori throughout the day. Kaimahi talk with children as they work and play. A local kuia with extensive experience in kōhanga reo has recently joined the kaimahi team. She is instrumental in supporting and guiding the kaimahi and whānau in te reo and tikanga Māori. Kaimahi and whānau members have participated inTe Whārikiwānanga and te reo Māori courses to increase their language skills and confidence. Children are exposed to activities and learning experiences where te reo Māori is promoted and shared.

Whānau members are more involved in the overall management of the kōhanga reo. The induction process has been reviewed and clearly informs new whānau about their roles and responsibilities; and the kaupapa of te kōhanga reo. The whānau now has a strategic plan which clearly links to Te Ara Tūāpae. This strategic plan shows the priorities for development including whānau and staff development and upgrading the playground. The kōhanga reo charter, including policies and procedures, has been reviewed. Kaimahi and the whānau have begun to review all systems and how these are managed.

The learning programme still lacks some structure, clarity and variety. There are few opportunities for children to follow their individual interests.

While overall kaupapa planning practices have improved, programme evaluation and assessment are still developing.

Future Action

ERO intends to return to the kōhanga reo within 12 months to evaluate the progress made in response to the recommendations in this report.

2 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

The June 2012 report identified whānau and kaimahi commitment to te reo Māori, the learning programme and the kōhanga reo environment as areas to be addressed. Both kaimahi and whānau members lacked a sound knowledge of the basic structures of te reo Māori. There was a lack of good quality resources for children. The learning programme lacked preparedness and direction.

Areas of progress

Te reo Māori development. Kaimahi use the learning programme to promote the use of te reo Māori throughout the day. Kaimahi talk with children as they work and play. A local kuia with extensive experience in kōhanga reo has recently joined the kaimahi team. She is instrumental in supporting and guiding the kaimahi and whānau in te reo and tikanga Māori. Whānau members have enrolled in various te reo Māori courses to increase their own capabilities to speak the language. Children are exposed to activities and learning experiences where te reo Māori is promoted and shared.

Areas for further improvement

The learning programme. The structure of the learning programme still lacks some clarity and variety for children. Children have few opportunities to follow their individual interests.

Children have limited opportunities to extend their interests and learning. Kaimahi have an informal attitude to managing the daily programme. While some resources are available to children there is still a limited variety of resources to challenge and interest children.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

The previous report identified that whānau and staff management, self review practices, and strengthening administration practices were areas that needed to be addressed.

Since the previous review the whānau and kaimahi have worked with local purapura members and TKRNT to address the areas for improvement identified in the last report.

Areas of progress

Whānau management.Whānau members are more involved in kōhanga reo operations. The induction process has been reviewed and clearly informs new whānau about their roles and responsibilities and the kaupapa of te kōhanga reo.

Kaimahi and whānau members have participated inTe Whārikiwānanga and te reo Māori courses to increase their language skills and confidence. The whānau now has a strategic plan that clearly links to Te Ara Tūāpae and shows priorities for development including whānau and staff development and upgrading the playground.

The kaiwhakahaere has maintained clear communication lines between the kōhanga reo and whānau members through regular pānui and informal conversations. Whānau members are now more involved in overall management of the kōhanga reo to support their children.

Personnel management. Most areas of personnel management, identified for improvement in the previous report, have improved. New staff members have recently been appointed. Appraisals have been undertaken and clearly identify strengths, challenges and professional development needs. Staff meetings are held weekly to discuss aspects of employment, the learning programme and daily kōhanga reo operations.

Self review. The 2012 report identified policies, procedures and management practices had not been reviewed to reflect current whānau dynamics or legislation. The kōhanga reo charter, including policies and procedures, has been reviewed. Kaimahi and the whānau have begun to review overall systems and how these are managed.

Administration. The current kaiwhakahaere was appointed in October 2011. She has been instrumental in establishing effective systems for managing the administration of the kōhanga reo. The kaiwhakahaere works closely with staff fromTe Kōhanga Reo National Trustand has undertaken training in order to maintain efficient administration practices and to promote the kaupapa of te kōhanga reo.

Areas for further improvement

Programme evaluation and assessment practices. While overall kaupapa planning practices have improved programme evaluation and assessment are still developing. Most kaimahi are new to the kōhanga reo and are still learning their roles and responsibilities.

3 Actions Required for Compliance

In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements they need to:

  • periodically review the programme of activities [R32, Education (ECC) Regulations 1998]

4 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  1. Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust will provide mentoring for kaimahi in the design, delivery and implementation of the learning programme, and
  2. kaimahi and whānau will continue to work closely with TKRNT to address areas for further development identified in this report.

5 Future Action

ERO intends to return to the kōhanga reo within 12 months to evaluate the progress made in response to the recommendations in this report.
