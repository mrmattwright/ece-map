Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūmanako Rāwhiti ki waho atu i te tāone o Waihōpai. He pai, he māia hoki te taunekeneke a ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu ki tētahi taiao ako ā-whānau. ka poipoi ngā hōtaka akoranga i ngā matea o ia tamaiti ki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana i te manaaki, te aroha, me te awhi. Kei te pakari haere ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūmanako Rāwhiti i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūmanako Rāwhiti ki Waihōpai, ā, he uri nō ngā mātāwaka katoa rātou. Ka kawe mai ngā whānau i ā rātou tamariki ki tēnei kōhanga reo, nā te kaha o te aroha me te atawhai e whakarato ana ki ngā tamariki. Kua paiheretia, kua whakapūmautia hoki ngā uara o te manaaki me te aroha i roto i te hōtaka ako o ia rā. Ka tino whai pānga tēnei ki te ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki ki te ako. Kei te ū te whānau ki te oranga me te waiora o ngā tamariki.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou māia hei ākonga?

He mauritau, he māia hoki ngā tamariki ki te taunekeneke ki ētahi atu, ki tētahi taiao ako ā-whānau.

Mana Aotūroa

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whakatairanga ana i te whanaungatanga. Kua tūhonohono ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō, ki ngā kaimahi, ki tō rātou kuia hoki i runga i te aroha. Whakapuakihia ai i ia te rā ngā karakia me ngā hīmene, hei poipoi i te wairua o ngā tamariki. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ō rātou tātai whakapapa, ā, he pakari tā rātou taki i ō rātou pepeha me ā rātou mihimihi. He tau, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

E whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i tētahi hōtaka e whakatutuki ana i ō rātou matea ako. He maha ā rātou whai wāhitanga ki te kōkiri i ā rātou ake tākaro. Nā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi, ka taea e ngā tamariki ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga te whai, ā, ka whakatau hoki rātou i ā rātou ake whiringa i roto i ngā akoranga. Ka akiaki, ka whakawero, ka whakawhānui hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā whakaaro o ngā tamariki i a rātou e tākaro ana. Ka ngana, ka arotahi hoki ngā tamariki mō te wā roa. He ākonga māia ngā tamariki.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao o te reo Māori. Ka akiaki te kuia i ngā tamariki me ngā pākeke ki te ū ki te reo Māori hei reo whakawhitinga kōrero, puta noa i te rā. Ka whakatauira rātou i te tika o te whakamahinga me te whakahua o te reo Māori. Ka whai pūtake ngā whakawhitinga kōrero a ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki, ā, ka hāpai, ka whakawhānui hoki ēnei i tō rātou reo. He pakari ngā tamariki tuākana ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro mā te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka arotahi ngā mahere hōtaka ki ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Ka poipoi ngā kaimahi i ngā matea o ia tamaiti me ngā rōpū tamariki hoki. Ka hono ngā rauemi ako ki ngā ariā, ā, ka hāpai hoki i ngā akoranga o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamōhio ngā kaimahi i te whānau mō te kiko o ngā āhuatanga ki te hōtaka akoranga. Ka ngana ngā tamariki hei ākonga.

Ko te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki tētahi aronga matua. He mārama ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga a te whānau hei hāpai i te waiora o ngā tamariki. Arotakengia ai ngā kaupapa here i ia te wā, ā, e noho mōhio ana te whānau ki ā rātou kawenga hei whakatutuki i ngā herenga ā-ture. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki.

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi kōhanga reo e āta whakahaerehia ana. Kua tāutuhia e te whānau ngā kaupapa matua hei whakawhanake ake. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero me ngā whakataunga e pā ana ki ngā āhuatanga matua o ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He mārama ngā tūmanako pūrongo, ā, ka whakamahia ngā mōhiohio hei whakamahere, hei aroturuki hoki i te ahu whakamua. E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki hei ākonga.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He whāiti te aromātai i te hōtaka. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e aroturuki ana i te whai pānga o ngā whakarerekētanga ki te hōtaka mā ngā tamariki. Ahakoa he tukanga aromatawai ā ngā kaimahi, kāhore ēnei e āta whakamahia ana i ia te wā. Kāhore i te riterite te pūrongo i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, kāhore he aronga ki te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki i roto i ngā akoranga.

He whāiti ngā mahi ki te aromātai o roto. Ahakoa kua tāutuhia e te whānau ngā whāinga rautaki, kāhore he mahere, he tukanga rānei ki te aroturuki i te ahu whakamua. Ko ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te aroturuki me te pūrongo, ka hāpai i te whānau ki te tāutu i ngā whakatutukitanga o ā rātou kaupapa matua ā-tau.

Kāhore te whakahaeretanga a ngā kaimahi e whakatutukihia ana. Kāhore anō te whānau kia whakatinana i te arohaehae, me te whakahāngai i tēnei ki ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi a ngā kaimahi. Me whakatutuki wawe i ngā mahi ki te arowhai a ngā pirihimana i ngā kaimahi.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. Me tahuri te whānau ki te:

  • whakatinana i tētahi tukanga aromatawai, aromātai hoki hei aroturuki i te ako me te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki
    [R43 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008 C1-14]
  • whakarite i tētahi mahere ā-tau hei whakatinana i ngā kaupapa matua o te kōhanga reo
    [R47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008 GMA6]
  • whakapau kaha ki te whakarato i te tautoko ngaio, ngā whai wāhitanga whakawhanaketanga ngaio, me ngā rauemi hoki mō ngā kaimahi, mā tētahi pūnaha arohaehae pai.
    [R47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008 GM1-12]

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakapakari i ngā mahi aromatawai, aromātai hoki
  • arotake, kia whakahou ake hoki i te whakahaeretanga me ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi
  • whakatinana, kia aroturuki hoki i tētahi mahere mahi e whai pānga ki te mahere rautaki
  • whakapakari i te arotake whaiaro, hei aroturuki i te kounga o ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūmanako Rāwhiti is located on the outskirts of Invercargill. Children interact positively and confidently with others in a whānau focused learning environment. Learning programmes cater for individual needs in an environment that fosters manaaki, aroha and awhi. Children are becoming confident speakers of te reo Māori.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūmanako Rāwhiti will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūmanako Rāwhiti is a pan-tribal kōhanga reo in Invercargill. Whānau bring their children here because children are loved and well cared for. The values of manaakitanga and aroha are embedded and reinforced in the daily learning programme. This contributes positively to children’s love of learning. Whānau are committed to children’s care and wellbeing.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children show confidence as learners?

Children interact positively and confidently with others in a whānau focused learning environment.

Mana Aotūroa

Children learn in an environment that promotes whanaungatanga. Children have formed warm, loving relationships with one another, kaimahi and their nanny. Shared karakia and hīmene are practiced daily to nurture the spiritual wellbeing of children. Children learn about their whakapapa connections and confidently recite their pepeha and mihimihi. Children are settled and happy at kōhanga reo.

Children experience a programme that meets their learning needs. They have many opportunities for independent play. A range of resources enable children to follow their interests and make their own choices about their learning. Kaimahi prompt, challenge and extend children during their play. Children are engaged and focused for long periods of time. Children are confident learners.

Children are immersed in a te reo Māori environment. The nanny encourages children and adults to maintain te reo Māori as the language of communication throughout the day. She models the correct use and pronunciation of te reo Māori. Kaimahi have meaningful conversations with children that supports and extends their language. Older children confidently express themselves in te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Programme planning focuses on children’s interests. Kaimahi cater for individual and group needs. Learning resources are linked to themes and support children’s learning. Kaimahi share with whānau detail about the learning programme. Children are engaged learners.

Children’s health and safety is a priority. Whānau have clear policies and procedures to support children’s wellbeing. Policies are regularly reviewed and whānau are aware of their legislative responsibilities. Children are happy and content.

Children learn in a well-managed kōhanga reo. Whānau have identified priorities for development. They contribute to discussions and decision making about key aspects of kōhanga reo operations. Reporting expectations are clear and information is used to plan and monitor progress. Children are well supported as learners.

Key Next Steps

Programme evaluation is limited. Kaimahi do not monitor the impact of changes in the programme for children. Although kaimahi have assessment processes in place these are not consistently practiced. Reporting about children’s learning is inconsistent and lacks a focus on children’s progress in learning.

Internal evaluation practices are limited. Although the whānau have identified strategic goals there is no plan or process to monitor progress. Monitoring and reporting processes will assist whānau to identify achievement of their annual priorities.

Personnel management is incomplete. Whānau have yet to implement appraisal and align this to kaimahi job descriptions. Police vetting is urgent and needs to be addressed immediately.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)

physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)

suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)

evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements. In order to improve practice the whānau must:

  • implement an assessment and evaluation process to monitor children’s learning and progress
    [R43ECS 2008/ C1-14)

  • establish an annual plan to action the kōhanga reo priorities
    [R47ECS 2008/ GMA6]

  • ensure all reasonable steps are taken to provide staff employed professional support, professional development opportunities and resources through an appropriate appraisal system.
    [R47ECS2008/GM1-12]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation(s):

  • Strengthen assessment and evaluation practice

  • Review and update Personnel Management and documentation

  • Implement and monitor an action plan to compliment the Strategic Plan

  • Strengthen self-review in order to monitor the quality of kōhanga reo operations

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūmanako Rāwhiti is located on the outskirts of Invercargill. Children interact positively and confidently with others in a whānau focused learning environment. Learning programmes cater for individual needs in an environment that fosters manaaki, aroha and awhi. Children are becoming confident speakers of te reo Māori.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūmanako Rāwhiti will be in three years.
