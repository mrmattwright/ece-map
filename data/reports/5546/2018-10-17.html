Background

Holborn Kindergarten provides all-day education and care for up to 40 children, aged over two years. Sessions run daily from 8:30am to 2:30pm. At the time of this ERO evaluation, nine of the 35 children enrolled identified as MƒÅori.

The kindergarten philosophy emphasise parents, whƒÅnau, caregivers and kaiako working¬† together to build a supportive, stable environment that enables tamariki to develop a sense of belonging.

Since the September 2015 report, the teaching team has experienced significant changes.

Day-to-day operation of the centre is the responsibility of a relieving head teacher. All teachers are fully qualified.

Holborn Kindergarten is one of 19 services in the Lower Hutt region, governed and managed by Hutt City Kindergarten Association (the association). Since the previous ERO review, a new senior leadership team has been appointed. This includes a general manager and two senior teachers, guided by a team leader. The governance board has also undergone significant changes.

The previous ERO report identified key next steps for this kindergarten. These included: developing good practice indicators based on the teaching philosophy; strengthening learning partnerships with whƒÅnau; and internal evaluation practice. Good progress is evident.

Practices requiring development were also identified for the association. These included: strategic planning; cultural responsiveness to MƒÅori and Pacific families; evaluation; and the appraisal process. Good progress has been made in these areas.

This review was one of a cluster of nine kindergartens in the Hutt City Kindergarten Association.¬†

The Review Findings

Children participate in a play-based programme. A home-like environment promotes exploration, interactions, experimentation and fun. Positive and warm relationships between children and with teachers are highly evident. Older children work confidently and collaboratively with younger peers.

Children with additional learning needs are well supported. Teachers work collaboratively with families, whƒÅnau and outside agencies to support their full participation in the programme.

Te Ao MƒÅori is valued in the kindergarten. Children have opportunities to share and celebrate their pepeha as well as use poi and rakau to enrich their learning. Teachers use aspects of kaupapa MƒÅori in the curriculum. Leaders and teachers should consider how to weave a place-based curriculum that promotes and celebrates local places of significant to MƒÅori. In addition, teachers should explore how best to engage with whƒÅnau MƒÅori to deepen their understanding of what educational success looks like. Consultation should also occur with the Pacific community.

A new system for planning children‚Äôs learning, based on their emerging interests is currently being implemented. The kindergarten's philosophy and Te WhƒÅriki the early childhood curriculum are used to guide this process. Leaders have identified that teachers should enhance their approach by:

  • increasing the visibility of children‚Äôs cultures, languages and identities in their assessment documentation
  • regularly measuring its impact on outcomes for individual children.

A well-considered internal evaluation framework is in place to support decision making. Leaders and teachers are very reflective and improvement focused. Teachers use evaluation to determine how well the curriculum and their practices impact on children‚Äôs learning. Enhancing the focus on outcomes for children, when developing good practice indicators, is a next step.

A comprehensive, well-considered appraisal process is in place. Teachers challenge themselves to continually improve their practice through strategic goal-setting and professional learning. Plans are in place to further strengthen teacher development by implementing an inquiry-based approach to appraisal. This should support teachers to better measure the success of improved practices in promoting outcomes for specific children and priority groups.

The governance board includes community representatives with a wide range of useful skills. The board and senior leadership team work well together, with a shared commitment to meeting strategic goals and objectives for the benefit of children, whƒÅnau and community. Leaders are successfully fostering a collective sense of responsibility for the vision, values and mission of the association.¬†

Key Next Steps

ERO and kindergarten teachers agree that their priority next steps are to:

  • develop a place-based curriculum
  • promote educational success for MƒÅori and Pacific children
  • enhance aspects of the assessment framework
  • continue to enhance aspects of internal evaluation practice.

ERO, the governance board and senior leaders agree that they should continue to build their effectiveness by:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
