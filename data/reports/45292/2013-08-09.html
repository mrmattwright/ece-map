1 Evaluation of Scenicland Pre School

How well placed is Scenicland Pre School to promote positive learning outcomes for children?

Not well placed

Requires further development

Well placed

Very well placed

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Scenicland Limited administers four early childhood centres on the West Coast. Three centres are in Greymouth and the fourth centre is in Hokitika. Scenicland Preschool is located in Greymouth.

This is the centre‚Äôs first ERO review. The centre caters for children between two years old and school age. The centre is well resourced and has a very large outdoor area. The centre employs qualified early childhood teachers or teachers in training.

The manager is highly committed to employing qualified early childhood teachers and supporting staff members to gain an early childhood teacher qualification. A major contributing factor in attracting and keeping qualified staff is the centre‚Äôs location on the West Coast.

A high level of health and safety is maintained for children, staff and families.

The Review Findings

Children are happy, confident and involved in their learning. They play well together across the age groups. Teachers have warm and caring relationships with the children. They have high expectations for learning and the development of positive social skills. They make good use of a wide range of teaching practices to help children develop their thinking and to be proud of their achievements.

Children with special needs are well integrated into the programme. Teachers ensure that children with special needs are included in all aspects of the programme and well supported in their learning and social skill development. They maintain close and positive relationships with the families and outside agencies. Individual education plan goals are carefully integrated into the planned programme for each child. Progress is well documented in ways that are meaningful for the child and those who know the child well.

Teachers are taking a range of positive steps to increase the inclusion of te reo and tikanga in the programme. They are beginning to make good use of self review, professional development and their support for each other to increase their confidence in and use of te reo MƒÅori. Te reo MƒÅori phrases are sometimes included in meaningful ways in children‚Äôs learning stories.

Children benefit from a large well-kept outdoor environment which is able to be used all year round. This provides opportunities for children to extend their physical skills and cooperative play through team games.

Teachers warmly welcome parents. They use a range of successful approaches to encourage parents to stay and play with their children. They make good use of the time parents are at the centre to share information about the child‚Äôs day. Some child assessments are beginning to make useful links between the home and centre learning experiences.

Teachers are highly motivated and involved in children‚Äôs learning. Teachers work well together to meet the needs of the children and to support each other in the programme. They also provide considerable support to each other to develop new skills to improve learning outcomes for children.

Self review is becoming established and useful as a result of external professional development. Teachers are beginning to understand and follow a self-review process.

The manager and head teachers from the four centres, with support of advisors, have put together a strategic plan that provides direction for the future development of the centre and improving teaching and learning. The plan has clear objectives that cover learning and teaching and building capability and capacity within the organisation and with staff. The plan makes particular reference to increasing the focus on New Zealand‚Äôs bicultural heritage so that all children gain appropriate knowledge and experience success in this area.

The manager is committed to growing the leadership capabilities of her head teachers and staff. She is investing significantly in their ongoing professional development and involving them in major decisions for the organisation. The head teachers from the four centres told ERO that they value the opportunity to share experiences and expertise with each other and the manager on a regular basis.

Key Next Steps

The managers and ERO agree that the next steps to improve learning outcomes for children include:

  • extending self review to evaluate how well the goals of the long-term plans for the organisation and individual centres are being met
  • continuing to establish and improve self-review practices within the centre
  • strengthening child assessment and programme planning practices to improve their consistency and usefulness for learning and teaching
  • building closer partnerships with families of MƒÅori children and the wider Maori community to promote MƒÅori children‚Äôs success as MƒÅori
  • continuing to build and strengthen educational partnerships with all families
  • continuing to use and review the effectiveness of the newly developed appraisal system.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Scenicland Pre School completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Next ERO Review

When is ERO likely to review the service again?

The next ERO review of Scenicland Pre School will be in three years.

ERO‚Äôs Overall Judgement and Next Review

The overall judgement that ERO makes and the timing of the next review will depend on how well placed a service is to promote positive learning outcomes for children. The categories are:
