1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi aromātai tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā te Toihau Arotake i Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia ai i roto i te pūrongo aromātai mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei i tāutuhia ai whai muri o taua aromātai.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei aromātai tāpiri i whakaritea i runga i te aromātai o te whakatutukitanga a te whānau i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai e te pūrongo a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i mua atu, me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua aromātai. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei aromātai, ko te titiro ki:

  • te Mana Reo – te whakarato i ngā whai wāhitanga ka whakapakari ai i te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori
  • te whakamahere me te aromātai – te whakapakari i te whakamaheretanga o te hōtaka, me te whakawhanake, te whakatinana hoki i ngā rautaki ka poipoi i te pai o ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā tamariki
  • ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture – te whakamahi i te angamahi marautanga o te kōhanga reo ki te whakatinana i te kounga o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, me
  • ētahi atu take i pupū ake ai i te wā o te arotake.

2 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Puāwai o te Aroha ki Whanganui ki te rāwhiti. Koia nei tētahi o ngā kōhanga reo tuatahi i whakatūngia ai ki tēnei takiwā. E ārahi tonu ana, e hāpai tonu ana hoki tētahi kuia i te whānau me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo.

Whai muri i te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2015, kua whakatūngia ētahi kaimahi hou, me tētahi komiti hou. E ako ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga mahi.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Reo

E ako ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e āta poipoi ana i te reo Māori. Ka whakanuia te mita o Whanganui ki te kōhanga reo. E whakamahi ana ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki, pērā i ngā waiata, ngā haka, te patapatai, ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, me ngā tohutohu, hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. Nā te taki i ngā pepeha, e whakamahi ana ngā tamariki i te reo Māori hei āhuatanga tonu o te ako i te reo. Ka whakatairanga hoki ngā kaimahi i te reo rotarota i roto i te hōtaka o ia rā, hei hāpai i ngā tamariki e mau nei i ētahi matea ako ake. Ka whakarato rātou i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi me te rongo i te reo ki ngā horopaki ōkawa, mā te whai wāhi atu ki ngā pōwhiri. Ka kitea te ngākau titikaha o ngā tamariki tuākana ki te kōkiri i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki ētahi atu, me te whakatauira i te reo ki ngā tēina. Whakanuia ai, whakamihia ai hoki e ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, te whakawhanaketanga reo o ia tamaiti i roto i te hōtaka akoranga. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

E ako ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana i a rātou. Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi ngā tikanga e whakatairanga ana i ngā tamariki ki te āta taunekeneke hei whānau, ā, hei tuakana, hei teina hoki. Kua āta whakatakotohia ngā mahinga, kia waia ai ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmanako o ia rā. Kua whakawhanakehia e ngā kaimahi ngā tino hononga e manaaki ana i ā rātou rōpū tamariki. Ka akiaki rātou i ngā tamariki ki te kōrero tahi i a rātou e tākaro ana ki waho, ka whakamihi hoki rātou i ā rātou whakamātau, ā, ka tautāwhi tētahi i tētahi i ngā wā e tika ana. He harikoa te āhua o ngā tamariki ki te ako.

Te whakamahere me te aromātai

Kei ngā kaimahi ngā whakaritenga i āta tāutuhia ai mō te whakamahere i te hōtaka, hei hāpai i te kounga o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka mahi ngātahi rātou ki te tāutu i ngā kaupapa ka whakawhanake i ngā taumahi e whakatutuki ana i ngā tūmomo reanga o ngā tamariki. E ahu mai ana ngā wheako akoranga i ngā ngākau nuitanga o ia tamaiti. Ka whakamahi ētahi o ngā kaimahi i te aronga ōpaki ki te aromātai i te hōtaka. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā kitenga o ia rā ki te whakarerekē haere me te whakapai ake i te kounga o te whakapuakitanga. Ka whakaarohia ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki.

Ka aroturukitia te ako a ngā tamariki. Ka whakaemi ngā kaimahi i ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki te ahu whakamua o ia tamaiti i roto i ngā akoranga. Ka whakatakoto ā-tuhi rātou i ngā kōrerorero e pā ana ki ngā taumahi ako, ā, ka whakaemi anō hoki rātou i ngā aromatawai e whakatakotohia ai i ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki. Ka whakapuakihia ki ngā mātua, hei whakaatu i te āhua o te ahu whakamua o tā rātou tamaiti i roto i ngā akoranga.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Ko te aromātai hōtaka tētahi āhuatanga hei whakapai ake. Ahakoa ka hui ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhiti kōrero mō te hōtaka akoranga, me tuhi tonu i ngā whakataunga ki te whakarerekē haere i ētahi āhuatanga, hei aroturuki i ngā rautaki e whakamahia ana ki te whakapai ake i ngā putanga ako ki ngā tamariki.

E whanake ana ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aromatawai. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, me whakawhānui ake i ngā whakaritenga kia whai wāhi mai ai te whānau ki ngā āhuatanga tonu e pā ana ki ngā whakapapa me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture

Ngā wāhanga e ahu whakamua ana

E whakamahi ana ngā kaimahi i te angamahi o Te Whāriki hei ārahi, hei hāpai hoki i te whakamaheretanga o te hōtaka, kia whakatinanahia ai anō hoki te kounga o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • mahi ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te taha o tō rātou tari ā-rohe o te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ki te whakapakari ake i te kounga o te whakapuakitanga.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka whakahaere anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te aromātai whai muri o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Puāwai o te Aroha i roto i ngā tau e toru.

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary evaluation is undertaken at the discretion of the Chief Review Officer of the Education Review Office (ERO).

ERO evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO evaluation and/or any additional areas identified since that evaluation.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary evaluation is based on an evaluation of the performance of the kōhanga reo governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the previous ERO report or issues identified since that evaluation. The terms of reference for this evaluation area to investigate:

  • Mana Reo – provide opportunities to strengthen children’s use of te reo Māori

  • Planning and evaluation – strengthen programme planning, develop and implement strategies to foster positive interactions amongst children

  • Areas of non-compliance – use the kōhanga reo curriculum framework to inform the quality of children’s learning

  • Any other areas that arise during the review.

2 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Puāwai o te Aroha is in Whanganui East. It was one of the original kōhanga reo to be established in the area. A kuia continues to guide and support the kōhanga reo whānau and kaimahi.

Since the last ERO report, 2015, new kaimahi have been appointed and a new committee is in place. Whānau and kaimahi are learning about their roles and responsibilities.

3 Findings

Mana Reo

Children learn in an environment where te reo Māori is fostered. The Whanganui dialect is promoted in the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi use a range of strategies such as waiata, haka, questions, conversations and instruction to support children’s language development. Reciting pepeha allows children to use te reo Māori as part of language learning. Kaimahi promote the use of sign language in the daily programme to support individuals with additional learning needs. They provide children with opportunities to use and hear the language in formal situations by participating in pōwhiri. Older children display confidence to initiate conversations with others and model the language for tēina. Kaimahi regularly acknowledge and praise individual’s language development in the learning programme. Children are developing as speakers of te reo Māori.

Children learn in a nurturing environment. Kaimahi have established tikanga that promote children to interact positively as whānau and as tuakana teina. Routines are in place to familiarise children with daily expectations. Kaimahi have developed caring relationships with the groups of children with whom they work with. They encourage children to talk with each other while they play outdoors, mihi for their efforts and comfort each other as needed. Children appear to be happy learners.

Planning and evaluation

Kaimahi have defined programme planning practices to support the quality of children’s learning. They work together to identify kaupapa to develop activities that cater for different age groups. Learning experiences are based on individuals’ interests. Some kaimahi use an informal approach to programme evaluation. They use daily observations to make changes to improve the quality of delivery. Children’s learning needs are considered.

Children’s learning is monitored. Kaimahi gather information about individuals progress in learning. They record comments about the learning activities and collect assessments, which are kept in children’s profile books. These are shared with their parents to show their child’s progress in learning.

Key Next Steps

Programme evaluation is an area for improvement. Although kaimahi meet to discuss the learning programme, decisions to make changes need to be recorded to monitor strategies used to improve children’s learning outcomes.

Assessment practises are developing. Kaimahi acknowledge that practices need to be extended to include whānau contribution specifically related to children’s whakapapa and interests.

Areas of non-compliance

Areas of progress

Kaimahi use the Te Whāriki framework to guide and support programme planning to inform the quality of children’s learning.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • that kaimahi and whanau work with their local Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust to strengthen the quality of delivery.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Puāwai o te Aroha will be in three years.
