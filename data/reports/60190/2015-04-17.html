ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Early Impressions Childcare is a well established community-based, non-profit early childhood education centre located in Johnsonville. It operates on Mondays to Fridays from 8.30am to 1.30pm.

The centre is licensed for 25 children, including up to five children up to two years of age. Children attend the centre one or two days each week. Of the current roll of 83 children, seven are MƒÅori and 12 are Asian. Good staffing ratios support children‚Äôs learning and development. Staff are qualified and registered teachers.

The Johnsonville Community Centre Management Committee is responsible for governance of the service and is supported by a centre manager.

Leaders and teachers have worked collaboratively to address the next steps identified by the March¬†2012 ERO report.

The Review Findings

The centre philosophy is focused on empowering children to learn and is evident in practice. Children are settled and take responsibility for their own learning. They learn and play in a well organised spacious indoor area and an outdoor area that promotes physical activity and exploration. Children are confident, curious and self motivated learners.

Interactions between teachers and children are warm, nurturing and respectful. Children are confident, happy, and display a sense of belonging. They participate in independent and collaborative play. Aspects of literacy, mathematics and science concepts are presented in meaningful contexts. Teachers using questioning more explicitly and modelling appropriate language should further extend children‚Äôs learning.

Infants and toddlers are nurtured and encouraged to explore their surroundings. Their needs are acknowledged and catered for in the mixed-age programme. Primary care givers support children to settle into the centre.

Staff work collaboratively to respond to children‚Äôs interests. Teachers reflect daily on programme and learning activities and make appropriate changes. They review programme focuses at the end of each term, noting children‚Äôs participation and involvement. Centre leaders acknowledge a next step is to evaluate the effectiveness of programmes on children‚Äôs learning.

The curriculum acknowledges and is responsive to children‚Äôs identity and culture. Centre leaders agree that the bicultural programme, and building on and giving significance to MƒÅori learners‚Äô culture, language and identity, are areas to continue to strengthen. Teachers should continue to make use of Ka Hikitia: Accelerating Success 2013 - 2017 and TƒÅtaiako: Cultural Competencies for Teachers of MƒÅori Learners to strengthen responsiveness to MƒÅori.

Children‚Äôs learning is documented in recently implemented electronic portfolios. They provide parents with a record of children‚Äôs participation during their time at the centre. Centre leaders have identified, and ERO's evaluation finds, that this is still an area for further development.

Parents are encouraged to be involved in their children‚Äôs learning. Teachers and leaders use a range of approaches to consult and communicate with parents and families. Regular and useful information is made available to parents about centre activities and programmes.

A flexible approach to supporting transition into the centre meets individual children and their families‚Äô needs. Centre leaders continue to investigate how to strengthen processes to transition children to kindergarten and school.

Self review has been an area of ongoing development. Clear processes have been developed that effectively guide review to make changes to the environment and improve learning opportunities for children. Staff develop a set of indicators for each review undertaken. The process should be strengthened by teachers evaluating the impact of improvements on the outcomes for children.

Appraisal processes are useful and appreciated by staff to support their ongoing growth as professionals. Appraisal includes elements of self and peer reflection and links to the registered teacher criteria. Teachers recognise the need for more ongoing, formal professional dialogue about their practice. Centre leaders have identified that revisiting and reflecting on development goals more often during the year should further improve and strengthen teacher practice.

Management provides clear expectations for centre operation through strategic planning that links to an annual plan. The teaching and management teams work well together. The centre manager and head teacher work closely to promote positive outcomes for children. They provide effective leadership for teachers.

Teachers are reflective. They are focused on improving the quality of teaching and learning for children.

Key next steps

Managers and teachers should strengthen the evaluation of programme effectiveness for children's learning.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Early Impressions Childcare completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

The next ERO review of Early Impressions Childcare will be in three years.
