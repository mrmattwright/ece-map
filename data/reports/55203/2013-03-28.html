2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

He kōhanga reo noho kāinga, Te Kōhanga Reo o Turi Pai Whenua, ki Havelock North. Haere ai ngā tamariki o tēnei kōhanga reo, i te takiwā, me ngā hapori whānui o taua wāhi. He hononga ā-whakapapa tō te nuinga o ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō, ki ngā kaimahi hoki. Nō te tau 1999 tēnei kōhanga reo i whakahaerehia ai hei kōhanga reo noho kāinga.

Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi i te kōhanga reo. Nā tēnei, ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

He tino kaha te whanaungatanga ki waenga i ngā tamariki, ngā mema o te whānau, me ngā kaimahi. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ō rātou tūranga hei tangata whenua, i runga anō i ngā kawa o Ngāti Kahungunu. Ka hāpai, ka whakapūmau hoki ngā kaimahi i te pai o te atawhai ā-waiora, ā-tinana hoki o ngā tamariki. Ka āwhina rātou i ngā tamariki ki te whakatutuki i ngā mahinga ā-whaiaro, pērā i te haere whare paku me te whakamua kākahu. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga atawhai, manaaki hoki ki te taha o ō rātou hoa me ngā pākeke.

Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i tō rātou taiao ake, i te taiao whānui hoki. Haereere ai rātou ki ngā marae, ki ētahi atu kōhanga reo, ā, ka whai wāhi rātou ki ngā huihuinga o te purapura. Ka whakapā atu ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā rauemi i te kōhanga reo. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ō rātou hononga ā-whakapapa ki te whenua, me te whai pānga nui o te whenua hei tino rauemi. Ka āta ako ngā tamariki ko wai rātou, nō hea rātou, me ō rātou hononga ki te kōhanga reo.

Kōrero Māori ai ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki, puta noa i te rā. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i ō rātou pūkenga reo mā ngā waiata, ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā pepeha, me ngā paki o te ata, te whakamahinga hoki o ngā rauemi i ngā wā taumahi. Ka whai ngā tamariki i ngā tohutohu, ā, ka whakamahi i ngā kupu me ngā kīanga mā te reo Māori.

Ka whai hua ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. E mōhio ana rātou ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi anō hoki, ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. Pūrongo ai ki te whānau i ia te wā, ā, he mārama hoki. Ko tētahi āhuatanga nui o te whakamahere rautaki ki te whānau, ko te whakapūmau i ngā hononga ki ngā kaumātua me ngā whānau o te marae o Kahurānaki. Ka whakamahere ngātahi te whānau, ā, he tino mōhiotanga hoki ki ngā whakataunga i whakaritea ai e rātou hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Kua āta whakawhanakehia, kua āta tuhia hoki te whakamahere hōtaka. Ka whakarato aua mahere i te kiko o ngā mahi ka whāia e ngā kaimahi. Ka tāutu ngā mahere i te whānuitanga o ngā whai wāhitanga ako e poipoi ana i te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui. I ngā wā katoa, ka tāutu ngā kaimahi i ngā huarahi ka whakapai ake i te whānuitanga o tā rātou hōtaka. Ka whai hua ki ngā tamariki, nā te hōtaka i āta whakamaheretia ai, ā, e arotakengia ana hoki i ia te wā.

Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo huarahi aromatawai. Me whakapai tonu rātou i te kounga o aua whakaritenga, kia mārama ake ai te kiko e pā ana ki te ahunga whakamua a ia tamaiti, me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga.

Kua whakaritea ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga hei ārahi i te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā mahi hauora, haumaru hoki. He auau ngā arowhai i te papa me ngā whenua. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i tētahi taiao ako i āta whakamaheretia ai.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Turi Pai Whenua kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Turi Pai Whenua.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

Ka hāngai Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira ki ngā wāhanga katoa o ngā akoranga me ngā whakawhanaketanga o ia tamaiti, mai i te tirohanga hoki o te ao Māori.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Mana Tangata.He atawhai, he whakakoia hoki ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā kaimahi, ngā mema o te whānau, me ngā tamariki. Whakanuia ai ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, ā, ka whakamihi rātou i ā rātou whakamātau, ā rātou whakatutukitanga hoki. Ka āwhina ngā tuākana i ngā tēina, ā, he mauritau ā rātou taunekeneke. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ō rātou tūranga hei tangata whenua i runga i ngā kawa o Ngāti Kahungunu. He tino kaha te whanaungatanga ki waenga i ngā tamariki, ngā mema o te whānau, me ngā kaimahi.

Mana Atua.Ka tino tautoko, ka tino whakapūmau hoki ngā kaimahi i te tiaki ā-waiora, ā-tinana hoki o ngā tamariki. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i te kai hauora a ngā tamariki. Ka akiaki rātou i ngā tamariki ki te ako i ngā mahinga e pā ana ki te tiaki i a rātou anō, pērā i te haere whare paku, me te whakamau kākahu. Ka kitea te manaaki, te whakaute hoki o ngā tamariki ki waenga i a rātou anō. E mōhio ana rātou ki ngā tūmanako e pā ana ki te whanonga pai. He hāneanea, he tau hoki te āhua o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Mana Aotūroa.He whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i ō rātou taiao ake, i te taiao whānui hoki. Haereere ai rātou i ia te wā ki ētahi atu kōhanga reo, ki ngā marae, ā, ki ngā wāhi whakahirahira hoki o te hapori. Nā te whānui o ngā tūmomo kaupapa, he whānui anō hoki ngā whai wāhitanga a ngā tamariki ki te ako i tō rātou taiao, i te ao Māori tonu. E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā rauemi e tino whakaatuhia ana, e whakatakoto tōtikatia ana hoki, puta noa i te kōhanga reo. Ka whakatairangatia te ako me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki, nā te marautanga i āta whakamaheretia ai.

Mana Whenua.Ka whakawhanake ngā kaimahi i ngā kaupapa e hāpai ana, e whakapūmau ana hoki i ngā akoranga e pā ana ki te Mana Whenua. Ka ako ngā tamariki i te whakahirahiratanga o te whenua, me ō rātou hononga ā-whakapapa ki te whenua, te whai pānga anō hoki o te whānau hei tino rauemi. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki ngā taumahi e whakapūmau ana i ngā ariā o te tiaki taiao. He maha ngā whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i ngā papa o te kōhanga reo. Ka ako rātou i a rātou anō, me ō rātou hononga ki te kōhanga reo nā ngā pepeha, ngā whakapapa, me ngā kōrero tuku iho. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i te atawhai, te manaaki hoki o ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga ki te taha o ō rātou hoa me ngā pākeke.

Mana Reo.Kōrero Māori ai ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki, puta noa i te rā. He whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i ō rātou pūkenga reo nā ngā waiata, ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā pakiwaitara, ngā pepeha, me te whakamahinga o ngā rauemi i ngā wā taumahi. Ka mārama, ka whai hoki rātou i ngā tohutohu i roto i te reo Māori. E āhei ana ngā tamariki tuākana ki te whakawhiti i ngā kōrero ngāwari ki ngā pākeke mā te reo Māori. Ka tino hāpaitia ngā tamariki, i a rātou e whakawhanake ana i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki te reo Māori.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai i ngā kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki, me te whānau. Kei roto hoki i te whakamahere me te aromātai ko te whakamahere hōtaka me te aroturuki i ngā whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.Ka whai hua ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. E mōhio ana ngā mema o te whānau ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. Hui tahi ai rātou i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti kōrero mō ngā whakahaeretanga i te kōhanga reo. Pūrongo ai ki te whānau i ia te wā, ā, he mārama ēnei. Ka whakamahere ngātahi te whānau, ā, ka whai mōhiotanga ngā whakataunga e aro nui ana ki ngā tamariki. Ko ngā matea me te mātauranga o ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, te whakamahere, te whakawhanake mō meāke nei, me ngā whakataunga i whakaritea ai.

Te whakamahere rautaki.He pakari te whānau ki te whakamahere hei painga mō ngā tamariki ā meāke nei. He mahere ā-tau tā rātou e arotahi ana ki te whakatairanga i te whānuitanga o te āheinga o ngā mema o te whānau i roto i te reo Māori. Kua āta whakaraupapahia ngā whāinga me ngā rautaki ki te whakatutuki whāinga. E tino mōhiotia ana e te whānau ngā pūkenga me te whakawhanaketanga ngaio e tika ana ki te whakatutuki i aua whāinga. Ka arotake i ia te wā, i te ahunga whakamua, me ngā whakawhanaketanga. Ko tētahi āhuatanga nui o te whakamahere rautaki a te whānau, ko te ū tonu ki ngā hononga ki ngā kaumātua me te whānau o te marae o Kahurānaki. He pai te aronga nui o ngā kaupapa matua a te whānau, ki ngā tamariki me te whānau.

Te whakamahere hōtaka me te aromātai.Kua āta whakawhanakehia, kua āta tuhia hoki ngā mahere. E hāngai ana ngā mahere ā-tau, ā-wāhanga, ā-marama hoki ki Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira me ngā kaupeka o te tau. Ka whakarato aua mahere i te pai o ngā kiko ka whāia e ngā kaimahi. Kei roto i ngā mahere ko ngā whāinga ako, ngā taumahi, ngā rauemi, ngā waiata, ngā pukapuka, me ngā haerenga hei whakatairanga i ngā mahi ā-kaupapa. Ka tāutu ngā mahere i te whānuitanga o ngā whai wāhitanga ako e poipoi ana i te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori me tō rātou ao whānui. He whai wāhitanga mā te whānau ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro i ngā wā whakamahere, me te hāpai i te hōtaka akoranga.

Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia wiki ki te whakawhiti kōrero mō ngā āhuatanga o te hōtaka e whai hua, me ērā hoki hei whakapai ake. I ngā wā katoa, ka tāutu ngā kaimahi i ngā huarahi ki te whakapai i te whānuitanga o tā rātou hōtaka. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te āta whakamaheretanga o tētahi hōtaka ka arotakehia, ka whakapai akehia hoki i ia te wā.

Te aromatawai.He pai te aroturuki a ngā kaimahi i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He pūkete tā ia tamaiti, ā, ka whakaemihia ngā mōhiohio i konei, hei kapo ake i te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki, me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga. E wātea ana ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki ki ō rātou whānau, ā, he pai tō rātou noho mōhio ki tā rātou ahunga whakamua, ki ō rātou whakawhanaketanga anō hoki.

Te arotake whaiaro.He tau te pūnaha arotake whaiaro. Ko te whai kiko o ngā pūrongo e whakatutukihia ana i ia te wā, me te hāngaitanga ki ngā wāhanga maha o ngā whakahaeretanga, e whakarato ana i ngā tino mōhiohio mā te whānau. Ka hāngai hoki tēnei ki ngā āhuatanga ka whakamahere ake, ka aroturuki ake, me ngā whakaritenga ka whakatau ake hoki. He mārama te whakahaere, te pūrongo hoki o ngā pūtea. Arohaehaengia ai te āhua mahi o ngā kaimahi i ia te wā. Ka arotakengia ngā kaupapa here, puta noa i te tau. E arotahi ana te kōhanga reo ki te whakapai tonu, me te tupu tonu, hei painga mō ngā tamariki me te whānau.

Te hauora me te haumaru.Ka whakapau kaha te whānau ki te tiaki i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki. Kua whakaritea ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga hei ārahi i te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā whakaritenga hauora, haumaru hoki. Ka whakatutuki pūmau te whānau i ngā arowhai o te whare me ngā papa i ia te wā, hei āta whakatau i te haumaru o te taiao mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te aromatawai.Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo huarahi aromatawai, ā, me whakamahine i ētahi o ēnei. Me rapu rātou i te tautoko o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo me te purapura, hei āwhina i a rātou ki te whakapai ake i te kounga o aua mahi aromatawai.

I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Kīhai ngā tirohanga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i tāutu i ētahi wāhanga e āwangawangatia ana.

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Turi Pai Whenua is a home-based facility located in Havelock North. Children who attend this kōhanga reo come from the local area and surrounding suburbs. The majority of children are related to each other and the kaimahi. The kōhanga reo has been operating as a home based facility since 1999.

Children participate in a diverse range of activities while they are at kōhanga reo. Consequently Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira is the focus of this review.

There is a strong sense of whanaungatanga among children, whānau members and kaimahi. Children learn about their roles as tangata whenua according to their Ngāti Kahungunu kawa. Kaimahi support and reinforce the emotional and physical care of children in positive ways. They help children with personal care routines such as toileting and clothing. Children experience warm, caring interactions and relationships with their peers and adults.

Children have opportunities to explore their immediate and wider environments. They visit marae, other kōhanga reo and participate in local purapura events. Children have easy access to a range of resources at kōhanga reo. Children learn about their ancestral links to the whenua and the importance of the whenua as a vital natural resource. Children successfully learn about who they are, where they are from and their connections to the kōhanga reo.

Kaimahi speak te reo Māori consistently to children throughout the day. Children have opportunities to develop their language skills through waiata, karakia, himene, pepeha, morning stories and the use of resources during activities. Children can follow instructions and use words and phrases in te reo Maori.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira encompass all areas of a child’s learning and development according to a Māori world view.

Areas of good performance

Mana Tangata. Interactions and relationships between kaimahi, whānau members and children are warm and affirming. Kaimahi regularly praise children and acknowledge their efforts and achievements. Tuakana help taina and relate to them in positive ways. Children learn about their roles as tangata whenua according to their Ngāti Kahungunu kawa. There is a strong sense of whanaungatanga among children, whānau members and kaimahi.

Mana Atua. Kaimahi effectively support and reinforce the emotional and physical care of children. Kaimahi encourage healthy eating habits among children. They encourage children to learn personal care routines including toileting and dressing. Children show care and respect for each other. They are aware of expectations for acceptable behaviour. Children appear comfortable and settled at kōhanga reo.

Mana Aotūroa. Children have opportunities to explore their immediate and wider environments. They regularly visit other kōhanga reo, marae and places of interest in the community. Different kaupapa provide children with a range of opportunities to learn about the environment and te ao Māori. Children have easy access to a range of resources that are well displayed and placed throughout the kōhanga. Children’s learning and development is enriched through a carefully planned curriculum.

Mana Whenua. Kaimahi develop kaupapa that support and reinforce learning about Mana Whenua. Children learn about the importance of the whenua, their ancestral links to the whenua and the whenua as a vital natural resource. They participate in activities that reinforce concepts of conservation. Children have many opportunities to explore the kōhanga grounds. They learn about who they are and their connections to the kōhanga reo through pepeha, whakapapa and stories. Children experience warm, caring interactions and relationships with their peers and adults.

Mana Reo Kaimahi speak te reo Māori consistently to children throughout the day. Children have opportunities to develop their language skills through waiata, karakia, himene, storytelling, pepeha and the use of resources during activities. They understand and follow instructions in te reo Māori. The older children are able to have basic conversations with adults in te reo Māori. Children are well supported as developing speakers of te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Kōhanga management. Whānau members effectively manage the kōhanga reo. Whanau members know their roles and responsibilities for managing the kōhanga reo. They meet regularly to discuss kōhanga reo operations. Reporting to whānau is regular and transparent. Whānau collectively plan and make well-informed decisions focused on children. Children’s needs and education are at the heart of whānau management, planning, future developments and decision making.

Strategic planning. The whānau confidently plans for the future interests of their children. They have an annual plan that focuses on raising the overall capability of whānau members in te reo Maori. Goals and strategies for achieving goals are clearly outlined. The necessary skills and professional development to achieve these goals are known by whānau. A regular review of progress and development is maintained. An important element of strategic planning for the whānau includes maintaining connections to kaumatua and whānau of Kahurānaki Marae. Whānau priorities positively focus on children and the whānau.

Programme planning and evaluation. Planning is well developed and documented. Annual, term and monthly plans are based on Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira and the seasons. These plans provide good detail for kaimahi to follow. Planning includes learning objectives, activities, resources, waiata, books and trips to enhance kaupapa mahi. Planning identifies a range of learning opportunities that fosters children’s understanding of te ao Māori and their wider world. Whānau have opportunities to share their ideas during planning and support the learning programme.

Kaimahi meet each week to discuss elements of the programme that are going well and those areas that need to be improved. Kaimahi continually identify ways to improve their overall programme. Children benefit from a well planned programme that constantly undergoes review and improvement.

Assessment. Kaimahi monitor children’s learning well. Each child has a profile book where information is collated to capture children’s progress and development. Whānau have easy access to their children’s profiles and are well informed of their progress and development.

Self review. There is a sound system of self review in place. Regular and detailed reporting against several areas of operations provides whānau with valuable information. This includes further planning, monitoring and decision making. Financial management and reporting is clear. Appraisals of kaimahi performance occur regularly. Policies are regularly reviewed throughout the year. The kōhanga is focused on continual improvement and growth to benefit children and the whānau.

Health and safety. Whānau take all reasonable steps to safeguard children’s health and safety. Policies and procedures are in place to guide the whānau to carry out health and safety practices. Whānau maintain regular checks of the premises and the grounds to ensure that the environment is safe for children.

Areas for development

Assessment. Kaimahi use several different assessment practices some of which need refinement. They should enlist the support of TKRNT and purapura to help improve the quality of these assessment practices.

General Information About Reviews

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
