Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai ki ngā papa o Te Kura o Pomare, ki Te Awakairangi. He ākonga māia, he ākonga pakari hoki ngā tamariki, ā, ka whai wāhi nui rātou ki te whānau o te kōhanga reo. E tino tautokona ana te kōhanga reo e ngā kaumātua me te whānau, ā, he pai tō rātou tūnga ki te whai tonu i ā rātou kaupapa rautaki mō te paetawhiti o te kōhanga reo.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai ki ngā papa o Te Kura o Pomare, ki Te Awakairangi. E tino tautokona ana te kōhanga reo e ngā kaumātua kua roa e whai wāhi ana ki te kōhanga reo, me te whānau e noho pūmau ana. Ka arotahi rātou ki te whakatutuki i ngā putanga ako o te kounga kairangi mō ā rātou tamariki mā ngā tikanga me te reo Māori. He mātātoa te whai wāhi a te whānau ki ngā akoranga a ā rātou tamariki. E ai ki te whānau, kua whai hua ā rātou tamariki me tā rātou ako angitu, nā te kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te āta poipoi i ngā tamariki i roto i te ao Māori me ōna tikanga?

E whai hua ana te āta poipoi i ngā tamariki i roto i te ao Māori me ōna tikanga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka mārama, ka whakamahi hoki ngā tamariki i te reo Māori i a rātou e taunekeneke ana ki waenga i a rātou anō. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero whakaongaonga e whai pūtake ana, ki te taha o ngā tamariki. Ka kōkiri, ka whakawero hoki ngā tamariki tuākana i ngā pākeke, ā, ka ū ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero e pā ana ki ngā kaupapa mahi. Ka whakanui ngā kaimahi i ngā whakaaro ka whakapuakihia e ngā tamariki. Ka tino ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou mōhiotanga e pā ana ki te whai pānga o ngā atua Māori ki tō rātou ao me te taiao. Ko ngā whakaritenga whakakotahi, ngā whakaritenga whakatōpū hoki e akiaki ana i ngā tamariki ki te whakawero i ō rātou whakaaro, me te whakawhanake i tō rātou tū ki te ārahi i ngā horopaki o te ao Māori. E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki kia ako rātou i te reo Māori.

Ka whakaaro nui ngā kaimahi ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka āta whai rātou i te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki me ā rātou whakatutukitanga. Kua āta whakatōngia ngā tikanga ki ngā whakawhitinga me ngā mahinga. He tino hua tō te whai wāhi mai o te koroua ki te poipoi i ngā kaimahi, te whānau, me ngā tamariki i roto i te mātauranga Māori, tae atu ki ngā tikanga. Ka whakamahi ia i ngā oriori, ngā mōteatea, ngā karakia, me te mirimiri hei whakatau i ngā tamariki. Kei te tino mōhio ngā tamariki ki ā rātou mahinga me ō rātou tūmanako ako. Ka kite i tā rātou whakaute, tō rātou mōhio anō hoki ki ngā tikanga e hāngai ana ki ngā tūmomo kaupapa me ngā tūmomo taumahi. He ākonga māia, he ākonga pakari anō hoki ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

He whakaute te āhua o ngā hononga me ngā taunekeneke. Ka kite i te aroha, te manaaki, me te whakaute o ngā tamariki mō rātou anō, mō tō rātou wāhi noho hoki. Ka whakatairanga ngā pākeke i te rangimārie, te manaaki, te aroha, me te tiaki puta noa i te rā me ā rātou taunekeneke. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te tū hei kaitiaki o tō rātou wāhi noho. Ka āta atawhaitia ngā pēpi, hei whakapakari ake i tō rātou māia me tā rātou kōkiri i a rātou anō. Ka toro atu ngā tama tuakana ki tō rātou koroua, mō tōna atawhai me tōna ārahitanga. E tino manaakitia ana ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Ka kitea e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te nui o ngā whakatutukitanga a ētahi o ngā tamariki i roto i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga, puta noa i Te Whāriki. E taunaki ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia mahi ngātahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhanake i tētahi aronga ka whakatakoto, ka kapo atu, ka pūrongo hoki i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo o ngā tamariki i roto i te roanga o te wā.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka whai hua te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. Tū ai ngā hui i ia te wā, ā, ka hāngai ki ngā āhuatanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga. He kaha te whakawhiti kōrero ki te whānau. Ka noho pūmau ngā taumata teitei o te hauora, te haumaru, me te atawhai. Ka mārama katoatia e te whānau ā rātou kawenga whakahaere, ā, e hāngai ana ēnei ki Te Korowai me ngā herenga ā-ture. E pou here ana te reo Māori me ōna tikanga i te āhua o ngā mahi whakahaere ki te kōhanga reo, hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā tētahi kōhanga reo i āta whakaritea ai.

Ko te ako, te whakawhanaketanga, me te waiora o te katoa o te tamaiti e pou here ana i ngā mahi ki te whakamahere i te hōtaka mā Te Whāriki. He ngana te āta aro atu ki te whakahaere i Te Whāriki me te whakatairanga i te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Ka whai hua te mahi ngātahi a te koroua me ngā kaimahi, hei āta whakarite i te whakatutukitanga o ngā matea o ngā tamariki. Ka tino mōhio ngā kaimahi ki ā rātou tamariki. Whakaaro huritao ai, arotake ai anō hoki rātou i ia te wā i ā rātou whakaritenga. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā hōtaka akoranga e arotahi ana ki ō rātou matea.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai ki ngā papa o Te Kura o Pomare, ki Te Awakairangi. He ākonga māia, he ākonga pakari hoki ngā tamariki, ā, ka whai wāhi nui rātou ki te whānau o te kōhanga reo. E tino tautokona ana te kōhanga reo e ngā kaumātua me te whānau, ā, he pai tō rātou tūnga ki te whai tonu i ā rātou kaupapa rautaki mō te paetawhiti o te kōhanga reo.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai is on the grounds of Pomare School, Lower Hutt. Children are confident and competent learners and valued members of the kōhanga reo whānau. The kōhanga reo is well supported by kaumātua and whānau who are well placed to pursue their longer term strategic priorities for the kōhanga reo.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai is on the grounds of Pomare School, Lower Hutt. The kōhanga reo is strongly supported by long serving kaumātua and a committed whānau. They are focused on achieving high quality learning outcomes for their children through tikanga and te reo Māori. Whānau are actively involved in their children’s learning. Whānau report that the kōhanga reo has positively influenced children’s lives and learning success.

2 The Review Findings

How well are children nurtured in te ao Māori me ōna tīkanga?

Children are nurtured successfully in te ao Māori me ōna tīkanga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children understand and use te reo Māori in their interactions with each other. Kaimahi engage in regular, meaningful and stimulating conversations with children. Older children initiate and challenge adults and can sustain conversations about kaupapa. Kaimahi value what children have to say. Children particularly enjoy sharing what they know about atua Māori in their physical and natural worlds. Inclusive and collaborative practices encourage children to challenge their thinking and develop their leadership in te ao Māori contexts. Children are well supported to learn te reo Māori.

Kaimahi care about their children’s learning. They closely follow children’s progress and milestones. Tīkanga is strongly embedded in transitions and routines. The koroua is invaluable in nurturing kaimahi, whānau and children in mātauranga Māori including tikanga. He uses oriori, chants, karakia and mirimiri to settle children. Children know their routines and learning expectations very well. They show respect and awareness of tīkanga associated with different kaupapa and activities. Children are confident and competent learners in their kōhanga reo environment.

Relationships and interactions are respectful. Children show love care and respect for each other, and their surroundings. Adults promote rangimārie, manaaki, aroha, and tiaki throughout the day in their daily interactions. Children enjoy being kaitiaki of their surroundings. Pēpi are monitored with care to build their confidence and develop independence. Older boys go to their koroua for assurance and guidance. Children are well cared for at kōhanga reo.

Key Next Steps

ERO recognises significant gains for individual children with te reo Māori and tīkanga across Te Whāriki. ERO recommends whānau and kaimahi work together to develop an approach that will record, capture and report children’s language development overtime.

Planning and Evaluation

The kōhanga reo is managed effectively by whānau. Hui are held regularly and cover all areas of management and operations. There is a high level of consultation with whānau. High standards of health, safety and care are maintained. Whānau fully understand their management responsibilities that are in line with Te Korowai and legislative requirements. Te reo Māori me ōna tīkanga underpins the way in which the kōhanga reo is managed for children. Children benefit from a well organised kōhanga reo.

Children’s holistic learning, development and wellbeing underpin programme planning through Te Whāriki. There is a highly responsive approach to managing Te Whāriki and promoting te reo Māori me ōna tīkanga. The koroua and kaimahi work effectively together to ensure that children’s needs are well catered for. Kaimahi know their children well. They regularly reflect on, and review their practices. Children participate in learning programmes that focus on their needs.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai is on the grounds of Pomare School, Lower Hutt. Children are confident and competent learners and valued members of the kōhanga reo whānau. The kōhanga reo is well supported by kaumātua and whānau who are well placed to pursue their longer term strategic priorities for the kōhanga reo.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai will be in three years.
