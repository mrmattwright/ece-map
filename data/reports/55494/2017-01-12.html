1 Te Aromātai i te Ratonga

He pēhea te tūnga o Te Whare Kōhungahunga o Y Tamariki ki te whakatairanga i te pai o ngā putanga ako mō ngā tamariki?

Kāhore i te pai te tūnga

Me whakawhanake ake

He pai te tūnga

He tino pai te tūnga

Me whakawhanake ake

E mōhio ana te rōpū kāwana, ngā kaiwhakahaere o te whare kōhungahunga, me ngā kaimahi, he mahi tonu hei whakapai ake, hei whakatairanga ake hoki i te āhua o ngā mahi katoa ki te whare kōhungahunga.

Ko ngā kitenga a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga e whai pānga ana ki te whānuitanga o tēnei whakataunga, kua whakarāpopotongia ki raro iho nei.

He Whakamārama

E whakarato ana Te Whare Kōhungahunga o Y Tamariki i te mātauranga me te atawhai rumaki reo Māori mō ngā tamariki, mai i te pēpi piripoho, tae atu ki ngā tamariki kua ono tau te pakeke. Nō te marama o Poutū-te-rangi, nō te tau 2008 te whare kōhungahunga i whakatūngia ai, hei whakarato i te mātauranga kōhungahunga reo rua, otirā, hei whakatutuki i te hiahia e kaha pupū ake ana i waenga i te hapori Māori o Kaitī, i Tūranga-nui-a-Kiwa. He matatau te nuinga o ngā kaiako ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, ā, kua whakangungutia, kua whiwhi tohu mātauranga hoki rātou hei kaiako.

Ko ngā whakarerekētanga ki ngā tūnga kaimahi me te whakahaeretanga – tae atu ki ngā whakataunga a ngā kaiārahi – e pā tōraro ana ki ngā āhuatanga mahi ā te nuinga. Ahakoa kāhore anō ēnei take kia pā tōraro ki te āhua o ngā mahi ā ngā tamariki, e taunaki ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ki te hoki wawe ki te whare kōhungahunga, hei hāpai i te rōpū whakahaere ki te whakatau i ngā āwangawanga i whakatakotohia ai ki tēnei pūrongo.

Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

Kei te whai pūtake ngā hononga whakawhirinaki o ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu. Kei te tino mōhio ngā kaiako ki ngā tamariki, ā, he mātātoa tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki ā rātou akoranga. E mōhio ana rātou ki te wā kāhore e tika ana kia noho tata rawa atu rātou ki ngā tamariki, kia wātea ai ngā tamariki ki te kōkiri i ō rātou ake huarahi tūhura. Ka kitea te whakaute, te whai whakaaro nui hoki o ngā tuākana mō ngā tēina, i a rātou e taunekeneke ngātahi ana. Kei te āta hono atu te nuinga o ngā kaiako ki ngā whānau. E hāpai ana tēnei i te tauutuutu ki waenga i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, ā, ka whakarato i ngā tino mōhiohio ki te whānau mō ngā akoranga a ā rātou tamariki. Ka rongo ngā pēpi me ngā tamariki kōhungahunga i ngā hononga papai e manaaki ana, e poipoi ana hoki i a rātou.

Ko te whai wā a ngā kaimahi me ngā mātua ki te whakapā i ia rā, i ngā wā ka tuku, ka tiki rānei i ā rātou tamariki, e tautāwhi ana i ngā mātua e waiho ana i ā rātou tamariki ki te whare kōhungahunga. Ka whai hua ngā rautaki e whakamahia ana e ngā kaiako ki te whakatau i ngā tamariki i ia rā. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te waiata, ā, ka hāpai tēnei i a rātou ki te whakatā me te arotahi puta noa i te rā. Ka whakatairangatia ngā wheako akoranga a ngā tamariki nā ngā hononga papai e whai pūtake ana.

Ka hāngai te taiao ako ki te whakahirahiratanga o te reo Māori me ngā tikanga i roto i te marautanga o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka āta whakatairangatia ngā whakawhitinga kōrero a ngā tamariki e ngā kaiako. Kua waia ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori, ā, kei te pakari haere tō rātou tūmāia me tō rātou āheinga ki te whakautu kōrero. Ka whakautu ētahi tamariki mā ngā kīanga māmā.

He tākare, he aronga hoki tā te tūhura a ngā tamariki i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga. He pai ki a rātou te whakatau i ā rātou ake whakaritenga, te tīpako haere i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi, me te whakatakoto i ā rātou ake whāinga ako. Ka kite, ka mōhio, ka aro atu hoki ngā kaiako ki ngā whai wāhitanga ki te whakatairanga me te whakawhānui i ngā whakaaro me ngā wheako ako o ngā tamariki. Ka kitea te pūmanawa o ngā tamariki hei ākonga pakari.

Ka whakaongaongatia ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te ao e noho nei rātou, ki tētahi taiao akoranga i āta whakaritea ai, ā, e mau ana hoki i te maha o ngā rauemi. Kua waihangatia te whare kia ngāwari ai te haereere ki roto, ki waho hoki. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te haereere puta noa i ngā tūmomo wāhi mahi. Ka whakarite ngā kaimahi i ngā wāhanga ako hei poipoi i ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā āheinga o ngā tamariki. Kei te wāhi nei ngā tūmomo wero mō ngā tamariki, ā, ka tūhura, ka whai wāhi atu hoki rātou ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi. Ka whakaongaongatia ā-tinana, ā-hinengaro hoki ngā tamariki,.

E kāwanatia ana te whare kōhungahunga e te poari o te YMCA. Ka pūrongo te Pou Whakahaere Matua ki te poari mō ngā pūmanawa me ngā wero e whai pānga ana ki ngā whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga o ngā mahi ki te whare kōhungahunga. Kātahi anō te rōpū ārahi o te whare kōhungahunga ka whakatūngia, ā, kei te āta tīmata noa rātou ki te mahi ki te taha o ngā kaimahi, ki te āta whakarite i ngā pūnaha whakahaere e whai hua ana, e noho mārama ana hoki ki te whare kōhungahunga. He pūmau te aronga ki te whakapai ake i ngā whakaritenga a ngā kaimahi. Kei te tautoko tonu te rōpū kāwana me te rōpū whakahaere i te rōnakitanga o te whakawhanaketanga ngaio o ngā kaiako, hei whakatairanga ake i ngā putanga mō ngā tamariki, tae atu ki ngā mahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai. E mōhio ana te rōpū kāwana me te rōpū whakahaere, kei te tīmatanga tonu rātou i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā whakaritenga pakari e whai hua ana i roto i te arotake whaiaro, te pūrongo, me te whakamahere.

E whakahaerehia ana te marautanga e ngā kaiako, hei whakatutuki i ngā matea o ngā tamariki. He pai te māramatanga o ngā kaimahi ki ngā āhuatanga ako o ngā tamariki. I tēnei wā, e whakatutuki haere ana ngā kaiako i ngā whakarerekētanga kua pā ki te whakaritenga me te whakahaeretanga o te marau, nā runga i te arotake nō nā tata tonu nei i whakatutukihia ai.

He whakaute ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me te whānau. Ko te noho tuwhera o te whare kōhungahunga ki ngā whānau e akiaki ana i te kaha o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero me te whai wāhi mai o ngā mātua ki te hōtaka. Kua whakaritea tētahi rōpū tohutohu a te whānau, hei whakapiki ake i te whai wāhi mai o te whānau, ā, hei whakapakari ake anō hoki i tō rātou whakapuakitanga whakaaro mō ngā whakawhanaketanga ki te whare kōhungahunga, me te whai pānga o ēnei ki ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga ka Whai Ake

Hei whakatikatika i nga nawe i waenga i nga kaimahi me te ropu whakahaere, me rapu he tangata takawaenga i waho atu i te whare kōhungahunga. E mohio ana te ropu kawana, te ropu whakahaere me nga kaimahi hoki, me matua whakarite rātou ki te whakatutuki i nga whakaritenga matua e whai ake nei. Ma te whakawhitiwhiti korero, te arotake e te mahi tahi ka puta he hua. Ara:

  • tētahi tauāki tirohanga e whakaaetia ana e te katoa
  • tetahi tikanga whakaaro mo te whare kōhungahunga, a, kia whakaaetia hoki ranei e te katoa
  • me mahi tahi te komiti whakahaere, ngā Kaiako me te whānau, ki te whakatau i ngā punaha whakahaere, ara, ki te whakarite rautaki mahere whakahaere mahi hoki ia tau, ia tau mo te painga o te whare kōhungahunga
  • me whakarite, me whakamahi e ngā Kaiako he arotake whaiaro e pa ana ki a rātou mahi ia, wa, ia, kia pai ai ta rātou whakamohio atu ki te whānau o te whare kōhungahunga mena e puawai ana ngā mahi, kaore ranei, e ahu pehea ana rātou, a, he pehea ta rātou whakamahi i ngā rauemi hei painga mo nga tamariki
  • ngā tuakana e pa ana ki te hauora me te haumaru hei whakatutuki i nga herenga a-ture, tae atu ki te noho pumau ki te tatauranga e tika ana ki waenga i te pakeke me te tamaiti
  • me whakarie, me whakamahi hoki te reo rua me te kakano rua ki roto i te marau whanui a-kura, kia ea ai ngā wawata o te whānau o te whare kōhungāhungā
  • te arotake, te whakapakari hoki i te whakamahere i te marautangā, te aromatawai me te aromatai.

Te Whakatau a te Whakahaeretangā ki ngā Wāhangā Tautukungā

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātaurangā ngā pūnaha o te ratongā ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatangā i raro iho nei e whai pāngā nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritengā whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngāi rongoā; ko ngā whakaritengā akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikangā haerengā whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātaurangā; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatangā kaiako; me te tataurangā ki waengā i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritengā, tikangā hōneatangā e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikangā, ka whakatairangā ngā ratongā mātaurangā kōhungāhungā katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitangā i ngā herengā ā-ture.

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātaurangā ngā āhuatangā kāhore e tutuki ana ngā herengā ā-ture, i roto i te kāwanatangā, te whakahaeretangā, te whakahaere tari, te whare me ngā rawa, me te hauora, te haumaru hoki. Hei whakatutuki i āna herengā, me whakapai ake te ratongā i te āhua o ngā mahi i roto i ngā wāhangā e whai ake nei, arā, ko:

  • ngā pūnaha mō te tāutu i ngā āhuatangā mōrearea, me te arowhai i ia te wā, me te whakatutuki i ngā mahi e tika ana, tae atu ki te whakamōhio i te whānau
  • te whakakore, te aukati, te whakaiti rānei i ngā āhuatangā e pā tōraro ana ki te noho haumaru o ngā tamariki
  • te whakatutuki, te tuhi haere hoki i ngā akoako hōneatangā ki te taha o ngā tamariki, inā he ahi, he rū whenua rānei
  • te ū ki te tataurangā e tika ana ki waengā i te pakeke me te tamaiti, i rungā anō i te āhua o te raihana
  • te whakamau tika i ngā rauhangā me ngā taputapu taumaha, kei taka, kei hingā rānei ētahi, me te whakamamae i ngā tamariki.
    [R 44 (1)(b), 45 & 46 (1) (2) Ture Mātaurangā (Kōhungāhungā) 2008; Paearu Raihana mō te Mātaurangā me te Atawhai ki ngā Whare Kōhungāhungā 2008]

Te Taunakitangā hei whakamahere i te whakawhanaketangā

E taunaki ana Te Tari Arotake Mātaurangā, kia whakawhanake te ratongā i tētahi mahere hei whakatutuki i ngā whakaritengā matua ka whai ake, me ngā mahi anō hoki i whakaraupapahia ai ki tēnei pūrongo.

Governance, centre management and staff recognise there is work to be done to improve and enhance the centre's overall performance.

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Te Whare Kōhungahunga o Y Tamariki provides Māori immersion education and care for children from newborn up to 6 years old. The centre was established in March 2008 to provide a bilingual early learning service in response to the growing demand of the Māori community of Kaiti, Gisborne. Most kaiako are fluent speakers of te reo Māori who are trained and qualified teachers.

Changes with staffing and management, including leadership decisions are contributing to an unhappy work environment for most. While these issues are not yet impacting on what happens for children, ERO has recommended an early return to support the centre management team to support the resolution of concerns noted in this report.

The Review Findings

Children have formed meaningful and trusting relationships with others. Kaiako know children well and actively participate in their learning. They recognise when their presence is not required by children and space for independent exploration is preferred. Tuakana show respect and consideration to teina as they interact together. Most kaiako have formed positive relationships with whānau. This encourages reciprocal communication and provides helpful information to the whānau about their children’s learning. Babies and toddlers experience positive, caring and nurturing relationships.

Daily contact between kaimahi and parents during drop off and pick up provides additional reassurance to parents leaving their children at the centre. Effective strategies are used by kaiako to settle children into the day. Waiata is enjoyed by children and helps to relax and focus them throughout the day. Children’s learning experiences are enhanced through positive and purposeful relationships.

The learning environment reflects the importance of te reo Māori and tikanga in the curriculum for children’s learning. Conversations with children are promoted well by kaiako. Children are familiar with te reo Māori and respond with growing confidence and ability. Some children respond using simple phrases.

Children explore their interests with enthusiasm and focus. They like to make their own decisions, select from a range of resources and set their own goals for learning. Kaiako notice, recognise and respond to opportunities with children to enhance and extend thinking and learning experiences. Children are viewed as confident learners.

Children are motivated to explore the world around them in a well-resourced and organised learning environment. The centre is purposely designed to provide easy flow between inside and the outdoors. Children are able to move freely between work areas. Kaimahi set up the areas to cater for children’s interests and abilities. The physical environment offers challenges for children to explore and be involved in a wide variety of activities. Children are physically and intellectually stimulated.

The centre is governed by the YMCA Board. The Chief Executive Officer reports to the Board about the strengths and challenges of the centre operations, management and practice. The newly established centre leadership team are in the very early stages of working with staff to establish centre management systems that are efficient and transparent. There is a strong commitment to improve staff practice. Governance and management continue to support sustained professional development for kaiako to enhance outcomes for children, including planning, assessment and evaluation. Governance and management recognise that they are in the early stages of developing strong and effective internal review, reporting and planning practices.

The curriculum is managed by kaiako to respond to the needs of children. Staff have a good understanding about how children learn. Kaiako are currently working through changes to curriculum organisation and management as a result of a recent review.

Relationships between kaimahi and the whānau are respectful. The centres open door policy encourages greater shared communication and parent participation in the programme. A Whānau Advisory group is in place to increase whānau involvement and strengthen their voice in centre developments for their children.

Key Next Steps

The breakdown in relationships between staff and management must be resolved with appropriate external support. Centre governance, management and staff recognise the urgency to address the following key next steps for improvement. In consultation with centre whānau review and develop:

  • review and develop a centre whānau agreed vision statement
  • review and develop a centre whānau agreed philosophy
  • establish systems to ensure whānau participation with developing the centre strategic direction and management forward planning
  • develop and implement regular self-review practices to inform the centre whānau about its growth, direction and performance of children
  • health and safety and processes to address areas of noncompliance, particularly adult to child ratios
  • develop and implement the centre curriculum based on whānau and centre aspirations of bilingual and bicultural education
  • review and strengthen curriculum planning assessment and evaluation practices.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

During the review, ERO looked at the service’s systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)

  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)

  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)

  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children’s health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements

ERO identified areas of non-compliance relating to governance, management, administration, premises, property, health and safety. To meet requirements the service needs to improve its performance in the following areas:

  • systems for the identification of hazards, and regular environment checks undertaken with appropriate action taken, including reporting to whānau

  • eliminate, isolate, or minimise hazards to the safety of children

  • carry out and record regular fire and earthquake drills with children

  • comply with licensed adult: child ratio requirements

  • secure heavy furniture, fixtures, and equipment that could fall or topple and cause serious injury or damage.
    [Regulations44 (1) (b), 45 & 46 (1) (2) Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; Licensing Criteria for Early Childhood Education and Care Centres 2008]

Development Plan Recommendation

ERO recommends that the service develops a plan to address the key next steps and actions outlined in this report.

Next Review

When is ERO likely to review the service again?

The next ERO review of Te Whare Kōhungahunga o Y Tamariki will be within two years.

3 General Information about Early Childhood Reviews

ERO’s Evaluation Framework

ERO’s overarching question for an early childhood education review is ‘How well placed is this service to promote positive learning outcomes for children?’ ERO focuses on the following factors as described in the bicultural framework Ngā Pou Here:

Pou Whakahaere – how the service determines its vision, philosophy and direction to ensure positive outcomes for children

Pou Ārahi – how leadership is enacted to enhance positive outcomes for children

Mātauranga – whose knowledge is valued and how the curriculum is designed to achieve positive outcomes for children

Tikanga whakaako – how approaches to teaching and learning respond to diversity and support positive outcomes for children.

Within these areas ERO considers the effectiveness of arotake – self review and of whānaungatanga – partnerships with parents and whānau.

ERO evaluates how well placed a service is to sustain good practice and make ongoing improvements for the benefit of all children at the service.

A focus for the government is that all children, especially priority learners, have an opportunity to benefit from quality early childhood education. ERO will report on how well each service promotes positive outcomes for all children, with a focus on children who are Māori, Pacific, have diverse needs, and are up to the age of two.

For more information about the framework and Ngā Pou Here refer to ERO’s Approach to Review in Early Childhood Services.

ERO’s Overall Judgement and Next Review

The overall judgement that ERO makes and the timing of the next review will depend on how well placed a service is to promote positive learning outcomes for children. The categories are:

  • Very well placed – The next ERO review in four years
  • Well placed – The next ERO review in three years
  • Requires further development – The next ERO review within two years
  • Not well placed - The next ERO review in consultation with the Ministry of Education

ERO has developed criteria for each category. These are available on ERO’s website.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews are tailored to each service’s context and performance, within the overarching review framework. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for children and useful to the service.
