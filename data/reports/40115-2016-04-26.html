Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Aorangi ki Western Heights, ki Rotorua. E tino whai wāhi nui ana ngā tamariki, ā, he ākonga tākare rātou. E whakawhanake ana tō rātou pakari ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i tētahi taiao ako e hāpai ana i ngā tamariki, ā, e poipoi ana i ō rātou wairua. Ka whai wāhi nui ngā tamariki ki ā rātou akoranga me ā rātou tākaro. 

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo ki Aorangi i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo ki Aorangi, ki Western Heights i Rotorua. Kua roa ngā tau e mahi ana te kaiako ki te kōhanga reo nei. Kua whakatūngia e te whānau ētahi kaimahi hou, ā, kua whakaurua e aua kaimahi ngā whānau hou ki te kōhanga reo. Mai i te arotake o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i te tau 2013, kua hūnuku te whānau ki tō rātou whare hou.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te whai wāhi nui a ngā tamariki ki ā rātou akoranga?

Kei te whai wāhi nui ngā tamariki, ā, he ākonga tākare rātou.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E rongo ana ngā tamariki i te reo Māori mā ngā huarahi e whai hua ana, e whai pūtake ana hoki. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā taumahi ka akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori hei reo whakawhitiwhiti whakaaro. Nā ngā waiata, te kori tinana, me ngā hōtaka akoranga, ka whakapuaki ngā tamariki i ō rātou whakaaro mā te reo Māori. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā hanganga reo papai mā ngā tamariki. He taumahi hoki hei whakatīmata i te reo matatini me te pāngarau, ā, ka whakawhānui ēnei i te reo a ngā tamariki. Kei te whanake te pakari o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ka hāpaitia ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana i ō rātou wairua. Ka whakauru ngā kaimahi i ngā karakia me ngā atua Māori hei poipoi i te whai wāhi nui o te manaaki i ētahi atu. Ka akiaki rātou i te taunekeneke pai a ngā tamariki hei hoa, hei tuakana, hei teina hoki, ā, ki te taha anō hoki o ngā pākeke. E wātea ana ngā kaimahi ki te tautāwhi me te tautoko i ia tamaiti, ā, he atawhai, he aroha hoki tō ō rātou hononga. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo whai wāhitanga ako. Kua āta whakaritea ngā mahinga, ā, e hāpai ana ēnei i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti haere, puta noa i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakatau whiringa ngā tamariki mō ā rātou taumahi tākaro. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te mahi ngātahi, tētahi ki tētahi. Ka whai wāhi nui ngā tamariki ki ā rātou akoranga me ā rātou tākaro.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā ngā hōtaka i āta whakamaheretia ai. E ahu mai ana ngā mahere i ngā mōhiotanga o ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea ako me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ia tamaiti, me te whakapakaritanga ake o te tūmāia o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahia ngā aromatawai hei whakatutuki i ngā tūmomo rōpū reanga, āheinga hoki, ā, hei poipoi anō hoki i ō rātou pūmanawa, ō rātou matea, me ō rātou ngākau nuitanga. Ka āta whakaatuhia ngā mahi a ngā tamariki hei tirohanga mā te whānau, ā, ka whai pānga ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo whai wāhitanga ako e wātea ana ki ngā tamariki. He ākonga tākare, he ākonga māhorahora anō hoki ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Ahakoa he matawhānui te pūnaha mahere tā ngā kaimahi, he iti noa ngā whai wāhitanga mō rātou ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro. Ko te aromātai nahanaha i te hōtaka tētahi wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakaae Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo, kia tīmata ngā kaimahi ki te whakapakari ake i te aromātai hōtaka.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Aorangi ki Western Heights, ki Rotorua. E tino whai wāhi nui ana ngā tamariki, ā, he ākonga tākare rātou. E whakawhanake ana tō rātou pakari ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i tētahi taiao ako e hāpai ana i ngā tamariki, ā, e poipoi ana i ō rātou wairua. Ka whai wāhi nui ngā tamariki ki ā rātou akoranga me ā rātou tākaro.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo ki Aorangi is in Western Heights, Rotorua. Children are highly engaged and enthusiastic learners. They are developing the confidence to speak te reo Māori. Kaimahi provide a supportive learning environment that nurtures their wairua. Children are engaged in their learning and play.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo ki Aorangi will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga reo ki Aorangi is in Western Heights, Rotorua. The kaiako has been there for many years. Whānau have appointed new staff who have brought new whānau to the kōhanga reo. Since the previous ERO review in 2013, whānau have moved into their new building.

2 The Review Findings

How well are children engaged in their learning?

Children are highly engaged and enthusiastic learners.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are exposed to te reo Māori in positive and meaningful ways. Kaimahi provide activities to encourage children to use te reo Māori as the language of communication. Waiata, kori tinana and learning programmes allow children to express themselves in te reo Māori. Kaimahi provide children with good language structures. Early literacy and numeracy activities extend children’s language. Children are developing confidence to speak te reo Māori.

Children are supported in an environment that nurtures their wairua. Kaimahi integrate karakia and atua Māori to foster the value of care for others. They encourage children to interact positively as peers, tuakana teina and with adults. Kaimahi are available to comfort and support individuals and provide positive and loving relationships. Children are happy and settled at kōhanga reo.

Children experience a variety of learning opportunities. Well established routines support children to transition throughout the learning programme. Children make choices about their play activities. Kaimahi encourage children to work co-operatively with each other. Children are engaged in their learning and play.

Planning and Evaluation

Children benefit from well-planned learning programmes. Planning is based on kaimahi knowledge about individual learning needs, interests and building children’s confidence. Assessment information is used to respond to different age groups and abilities and to cater for their strengths, needs and interests. Children’s work is proudly displayed for whānau and reflects the wide range of learning opportunities available for children. Children are enthusiastic and spontaneous learners.

Key Next Steps

Although kaimahi have a comprehensive planning system in place. There are limited opportunities for them to share ideas. Systematic programme evaluation is an area for development.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau agreed that kaimahi begin to strengthen programme evaluation.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo ki Aorangi is in Western Heights, Rotorua. Children are highly engaged and enthusiastic learners. They are developing the confidence to speak te reo Māori. Kaimahi provide a supportive learning environment that nurtures their wairua. Children are engaged in their learning and play.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo ki Aorangi will be in three years.
