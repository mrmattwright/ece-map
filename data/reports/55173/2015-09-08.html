1 Te Horopaki

E tipu e rea mō ngā rā o te tō ao Ko tō ringa ki ngā rākau a te Pākehā Hei oranga mō tō tinana Ko tō ngākau ki ngā taonga a ō tīpuna Māori Hei tikitiki mō tō māhuna Ko tō wairua ki tō Atua Nāna nei ngā mea katoa.

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakarua ki te marae o Uepōhatu, ki Ruatōrea. Kua kapohia atu ngā tirohanga ā-ahurea, ā-mātauranga hoki a te whakataukī ā Tā Apirana Ngata, e kīia nei ‘E tipu e rea’, hei tauāki taketake mō te kōhanga reo. Koia nei tētahi o ngā kōhanga reo mātāmua o Te Tai Rāwhiti.

Ko te arotahi o ngā kōhanga reo o roto o Ngāti Porou, ko te whakahaumanu i te reo ā-iwi me ngā tikanga o Ngāti Porou. He hiahia tō ngā mātua kia whiwhi ā rātou tamariki i te mātauranga papai. Ka hāpai hoki rātou i te āheinga o ngā tamariki ki te whai atu i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. He mārama ki a rātou, ko te ahu whakamua o te hapū, te marae, me te iwi, e whai pānga nui ai ki te āhua o ngā mahi ki te whakariterite i ā rātou tamariki.

Mai i te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kua whakapau kaha ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhanake i te aromātai hōtaka, ā, kua whai hua ā rātou mahi. Kua whakapai ake hoki ngā kaimahi i te hōtaka akoranga kia wheako ai ngā tamariki i tētahi taiao e whakamana ana, e whai whakaaro nui ana hoki ki ā rātou tākaro. Kei te whakawhanake haere te māia o ia tamaiti, me tō rātou whai wāhi atu ki tō rātou whānau.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te whai atu a ngā tamariki i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga, ō rātou ake matea anō hoki i roto i te hōtaka akoranga?

E tino tautokona ana ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o te nuinga o ngā tamariki i roto i te hōtaka akoranga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E tino tautokona ana ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki i roto i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whai hua ngā kaimahi ki te whakarite me te whakahaere i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka ākina ngā tamariki kia kōkiri ai rātou i ā rātou akoranga, ā, ki te ako hoki mā ā rātou tākaro. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te tākaro ki te taha o ō rātou hoa, ki te tākaro takitahi rānei. E wātea ana ki a rātou ngā rauemi o te kounga pai, me te whānuitanga hoki o ngā tūmomo taumahi whakahihiri.

He hihiri, he ātaahua hoki te taiao akoranga e ngākau nuitia ana e ngā tamariki. Ka whakaatuhia ngā pepeha me ngā whakapapa o ngā tamariki – ā rātou mahi toi hoki – puta noa i te kōhanga reo. He kuihana rahi ā rātou mō ā rātou tākaro mārie. Ka whakanui te wāhi tākaro o waho i te kori tinana. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te tūhura me te ako ki runga i tō rātou marae a Uepōhatu. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i tētahi taiao akoranga e atawhai ana, e whakaongaonga ana hoki i a rātou. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki te roanga o ngā akoranga mā ā rātou tākaro.

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā hononga e poipoi ana i a rātou ki te kōhanga reo. He manaaki, he atawhai, he mauritau hoki ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā pākeke. Whakatutukihia ai ngā matea ake o ia tamaiti e ngā kaimahi. Ka ngākau nui ngā tēina ki te noho tahi me ō rātou tuākana. He ratarata, he pākiki, he tūmāia hoki ngā tamariki. Kei te ako rātou i tō rātou whai wāhi nui ki te kōhanga reo. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki.

Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki ngā pukapuka. He maha ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te pānui, ā, mā rātou anō ngā wā pānui e kōkiri ki te taha o ngā kaimahi. Ka whakapuaki rātou i ō rātou whakaaro mō ngā pukapuka, ki te taha o ngā pākeke. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i ngā pūkenga whakarongo me ngā pūkenga whakamārama. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā wā ka noho tahi rātou ki te pānui, ā, ka hokihoki atu rātou ki ngā pukapuka i ia te wā.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E whanake tonu ana te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. Kei te mārama ngā tamariki ki ngā tohutohu me ngā kupu māmā, engari, i te nuinga o te wā ka whakautu kōrero rātou mā te reo Pākehā. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e kōrero ana i te reo Māori ki ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa. Ko te ū pūmau o te reo tētahi āhuatanga ka hāpai ake anō i te tupu tonu o te reo o ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He pai te whakamahere, te arotake nahanaha hoki i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakamahere ngātahi, ka arotake ngātahi hoki ngā kaimahi. He mahere ā-tau, ā-wāhanga, ā-marama, ā-wiki, o ia rā hoki. Ka whai wāhi ngā mātua ki te tukanga whakamahere. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te pai o te aromātai hōtaka me ngā mahi whakamahere.

Ka hāpaitia te ako a ngā tamariki nā te pai o ngā mahere rautaki. Kua whakawhanakehia e te whānau tētahi mahere rautaki mō ngā tau 2013 ki te 2018. He tahua pūtea anō hoki o roto mō ngā rauemi ako hou, ngā whakangungu o Te Whāriki mā ngā kaimahi, me ngā akoranga o te reo Māori a ngā mātua. Ka hāngai te mahere rautaki ki Te Ara Tūāpae, ki Ngā Pou e Whā, ā, ki Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira hoki. Ka ārahi ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki i ngā mahi whakamahere.

Ka whai hua te whakahaere i ngā kaimahi. He kirimana, he tuhinga tūranga mahi hoki ā ngā kaimahi. Kua whakaritea ngā tukanga arohaehae, ā, ka tāutu ēnei i ngā akoranga ngaio me ngā matea whakawhanaketanga a ia kaimahi. Kua tāutuhia hoki e te whānau ngā whāinga ako ā ngā kaimahi. Ka whakatairangatia te ako a ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi i āta whakangungu ai, e āta aro nui ana hoki.

Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo huarahi aromatawai hei whakatakoto i te āhua o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He pūkete mātai mokopuna hoki ētahi. Kei aua pūkete tētahi kohinga o ngā mahi a ngā tamariki i roto i ngā kaupapa ako. Ka tuhi ngā kaimahi i ngā kōrerorero e pā ana ki te whakawhanaketanga o ia tamaiti. Ka whakamōhiotia ngā mātua mō ā rātou tamariki mā te kōrero a-kanohi, mā ngā pātuhi, mā te Pukamata, ā, mā ngā pūkete hoki. Ka hāpai ngā aromatawai i ngā kaimahi ki te aroturuki me te whakamahere mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. E tino tautokona ana te ako a ngā tamariki mā ngā mahi aromatawai.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Ko te aromatawai i ngā tamariki e mau nei i ngā matea tauwhāiti tētahi āhuatanga hei whakapai ake. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi me whakahāngai ake ngā pūkete ki ia tamaiti ake. Kāhore e āta tāutuhia ana ngā mahi tautoko ka hāngai pū ki aua tamariki.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Ka whakapūmautia te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki nā te pai o ngā mahi kāwana. Kua whakaritea te katoa o ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. Ka whakahaerehia ngā rehita e tika ana, ā, ka hāngai hoki ēnei ki te wā. He taiāwhio auau mō te arotake i ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i tētahi taiao haumaru, hauora hoki.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • toro atu ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te tautoko o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ki te whakapakari i te reo Māori i roto i te kōhanga reo, i ngā kāinga hoki
  • toro atu ngā kaimahi ki te tautoko o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te whakawhanake ake tonu i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki ngā mātai mokopuna.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakarua ki te marae o Uepōhatu ki Ruatōrea. Kei te āta tautokona ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o te nuinga o ngā tamariki ki roto i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā hononga e āta poipoi ana i a rātou ki te kōhanga reo. Kei te mārama rātou ki ngā tohutohu me ngā kupu māmā i roto i te reo Māori, engari i te nuinga o te wā, ka whakautu rātou mā te reo Pākehā.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakarua i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

1 Context

E tipu e rea mō ngā rā o tō ao Ko tō ringa ki ngā rākau a te Pākehā Hei ora mō te tīnana Ko tō ngākau ki ngā taonga a ō tīpuna Māori Hei tikitiki mō tō māhunga Ko tō wairua ki tō atua, Nāna nei ngā mea katoa.

The Kōhanga Reo o Whakarua is based on the Uepōhatu marae in Ruatorea. The cultural and educational visions from Sir Apirana Ngata’s proverb ‘E tipu e rea’ have been adopted as the kōhanga reo vision statement. This is one of the oldest kōhanga reo in Te Tairāwhiti.

In Ngāti Porou kōhanga reo are focused on the revitalisation of the Ngāti Porou dialect and tikanga. Parents want their children to have access to good education. They also support children’s ability to follow their own interest. They are clear that the future of the hapū, marae and iwi is dependent on how well prepare their children are.

Since the previous ERO report kaimahi have worked hard to develop programme evaluation with success. The kaimahi have also improved the learning programme so children experience an environment where their play is valued and recognised. The children are developing confidence in themselves and the whānau around them.

2 The Review Findings

To what extent do children follow their own interests and needs in the learning programme?

Most children’s needs and interests are well supported in the learning programme.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children’s interests are well supported in the learning programme. Kaimahi successfully organise and manage the learning programme. Children are encouraged to learn independently and through their play. Children can play with peers or on their own. They have access to good quality resources and a range of interesting activities.

Children enjoy a vibrant and attractive learning environment. Children’s pepeha, whakapapa, artwork are displayed throughout the kōhanga reo. They have large cushions to cater for quiet play. The outdoor play area promotes physical activity. Children are able to explore and learn on their marae Uepōhatu. Children experience a warm and stimulating learning environment. Children are engaged in sustained learning through play.

Children experience nurturing relationships at kōhanga reo. Relationships between children and adults are caring, warm and positive. Kaimahi attend to children’s individual needs. Teina enjoy shared times with their tuakana. Children are friendly, curious and confident. They are learning about their place in the kōhanga reo. Children are happy and content.

Children have a strong interest in books. Children are given regular opportunities to read and will initiate reading sessions with Kaimahi. They share their ideas about books with adults. Children are developing listening and comprehension skills. Children enjoy shared reading experiences and return to books often.

Key Next Steps

Children’s te reo Māori is still developing. Children understand simple commands and words however, they usually respond in English. Kaimahi do not always speak te reo Māori to the children. A consistent language environment is required to better support children’s language growth.

Planning and Evaluation

The learning programme is well planned and regularly reviewed. Kaimahi plan and review the programme collectively. There are year, term, month, week and day plans. Parents contribute to the planning process. Children benefit from effective programme evaluation and planning.

Children’s learning is supported through good strategic planning. The whānau have developed a strategic plan for 2013 - 2018. This includes budgeting for new learning resources, Te Whāriki training for kaimahi and te reo Māori classes for parents. The strategic plan aligns with Te Ara Tūāpae, Ngā Pou e whā and Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Children’s needs and interests guide planning.

Personnel management is effective. Kaimahi have employment contracts and job descriptions. Appraisal processes are in place and identify professional learning and development needs for each kaimahi. Whānau have also identified learning objectives for kaimahi. Children’s learning is enhanced by trained and focused kaimahi.

Kaimahi use a variety of assessment methods to record children’s learning. These include a mātai mokopuna portfolio. These portfolios include a collection of children’s work about kaupapa ako. Kaimahi write comments about each child’s development. Parents receive information about their child in person, by text, Facebook and portfolios. Assessment supports kaimahi to monitor and plan for children’s learning. Children are well supported in their learning through assessment practices.

Key Next Steps

Assessment for children with special needs require improvement. Kaimahi acknowledge that their portfolios need to be more individualised. Targeted learning support for these children is not clearly defined.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Children’s health and safety are maintained through good governance. All health & safety policies and procedures are in place. The appropriate registers are operational and up to date. The policies and procedures are reviewed on a regular cycle. Children experience a safe and healthy environment.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations that:

  • kaimahi and whānau to seek support from Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) to strengthen te reo Māori within the kōhanga reo and at home
  • kaimahi seek support from TKRNT to further develop Mātai mokopuna practices.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakarua is on the Uepōhatu Marae in Ruatorea. Most children’s needs and interests are well supported in the learning programme. Children experience nurturing relationships at kōhanga reo. They understand simple commands and words in te reo Māori however, they usually respond in English.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakarua will be in three years.
