Background

Avondale Kindergarten is in West Auckland next to a primary school. It operates as a Kindergarten Day Model which enables children to attend sessions similar to school hours. The kindergarten provides education and care for up to 40 children over two years to school age. It is staffed by a head teacher, three other registered teachers, an administrator and a teacher aide.

The teaching team has remained the same since the 2012 ERO review. Teachers continue to provide the good quality practices noted in the 2012 ERO report. They have engaged in professional learning to enhance their teaching practice. The kindergarten has advanced to a Silver level in the Enviroschools programme.

Fostering child-led learning, inclusion and celebrating cultural diversity are embedded in the kindergarten‚Äôs philosophy. Teachers promote learning about environmental sustainability and healthy lifestyles.

The kindergarten is part of the Auckland Kindergarten Association, which provides leadership, a management framework, support personnel and a programme of professional development for teachers. In 2015, after extensive review and consultation, the association launched a new 10 year strategic plan. The association‚Äôs approach to rolling out a substantial change in its organisational structure has been carefully considered.

New association roles have been established to provide more targeted support for kindergartens and professional development for head teachers in their leadership roles. A Quality Improvement Process (QIP) is being implemented to monitor quality and promote ongoing improvement in kindergartens.

This review was part of a cluster of five kindergarten reviews in the Auckland Kindergarten Association.

The Review Findings

Children have fun and are confident, eager learners. They engage in sustained co-operative play, make choices and explore and learn at their own pace. Children have leadership opportunities that include sharing their cultures with others. They are developing an understanding of and a sense of responsibility and respect for themselves, others and the environment.

Children show a strong sense of belonging in the kindergarten and their cultural identities are affirmed. Teachers encourage children to share their ideas. They engage children in sustained conversations, often using their home languages. Te reo and tikanga MƒÅori are integrated in meaningful ways in consultation with whƒÅnau. Teachers are keen to continue to develop bicultural practices.

Children learn in an attractive well resourced environment that reflects the multicultural backgrounds of children attending. They have very good opportunities to explore and be physically active in the spacious outdoor area. Children are encouraged to be creative and engage in a wide variety of art, dance, and music activities.

Teachers provide a responsive curriculum that is underpinned by Te WhƒÅriki, the early childhood curriculum. Their respect for learning through play is evident in the meaningful learning opportunities they offer to children. Teachers‚Äô caring and supportive interactions help children to learn about literacy, mathematics, science and technology concepts in relevant ways.

There is a strong sense of unity, purpose and collaboration in the kindergarten. Teachers have respectful relationships with children and their families and strong connections with the local community. They encourage parent/whƒÅnau involvement in the kindergarten, inviting them to share their expertise and ideas. Good connections with local primary schools support children as they transition to school.

Teachers are culturally responsive and have a strong sense of social justice. They are committed to fostering equity and inclusion by promoting positive outcomes for all children. They actively support children‚Äôs physical and emotional wellbeing.

Collaborative working relationships contribute to the kindergarten‚Äôs culture of care and respect. The head teacher encourages teachers to take leadership roles that build on their strengths and interests. Self review is well established, however teachers are aware documentation could be strengthened.

The association provides timely, in depth support for the head teacher and the team. Its strategic plan provides a stepping stone for the kindergarten to build on to meet the needs of its local community.

The association has a strong commitment to biculturalism and in embracing diversity. Resources and personnel have been targeted to ensure that the specific needs of children and their families are met.

Key Next Steps

The teaching team, and Association Education Specialist agree that teachers could continue to:

  • offer children more opportunities to become critical thinkers
  • strengthen self review to more clearly show teachers‚Äô evaluative thinking and how review impacts positively on outcomes for children.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Avondale Kindergarten completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

The next ERO review of Avondale Kindergarten will be in three years.
