1 Te Horopaki

Ko Manaia te maunga

Ko Raumanga te awa

Ko Te Puna o te Mātauranga te marae

Ko Raumanga te kōhanga reo.

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Raumanga Kōhungahunga ki ngā papa o Tai Tokerau Wānanga, ki Whangārei. Whai muri i te arotake o mua, kua whakawhanakehia e te whānau tētahi mahere rautaki mō ia whare, arā, mō ngā tamariki kei raro i te rua tau, me ngā tamariki kua pakeke ake i te rua o ngā tau. Kua whakapai ake rātou i te āheinga o ngā kaimahi me ā rātou tohu mātauranga. Kua whakapai ake ngā whakaritenga aromatawai i te whakaemitanga o ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

I te tau kua pahure ake nei, kua pā ētahi wero ki te whānau, nā ngā whakarerekētanga nui ki ngā tūnga kaimahi. Kua whai pānga hoki tēnei ki ngā whakahaeretanga. Engari, kua tutuki haere te whānau i ngā take matua me ngā take kaimahi ki ngā whare e rua. Nā ā rātou aromātai o roto, kua pupū ake ētahi atu kaupapa matua hei whakawhanake.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou tūmāia, i tō rātou māramatanga hoki ki te reo Māori?

Ka kitea te tino tūmāia o ngā tamariki i a rātou e whakamahi ana, e mārama ana hoki i te reo Māori.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako ako reo pārekareka. He mātātoa tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki ngā akoranga e whakatairanga ana i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki te reo Māori, ā, e whakawhānui ana hoki i tō rātou māramatanga ki te reo. Ka pupū ake ngā kōrerorero māhorahora i ia te wā, ki waenga i ngā tamariki tuākana me ngā pākeke. Kua waia ngā tamariki katoa ki te reo Māori i ngā horopaki ōkawa me ngā horopaki ōpaki, ā, he pai hoki tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki te reo. He pārekareka ki a rātou ngā taumahi reo e whakawero ana i a rātou. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki ako reo pai e whakapiki ake ana i te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki, me tō rātou āheinga ki te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero. E ai ki ngā mātua, e whakamahi ana ngā tamariki i te reo Māori me ō rātou mōhiotanga ki ngā tikanga ki ō rātou kāinga. E whakatau ana ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo me ngā tikanga Māori.

E rongo ana ngā tamariki i ngā tini uaratanga o te rangimārie me te waiora i roto i ā rātou akoranga. Ka whakatauira ngā pākeke i te manawanui, te whai whakaaro, te whakaute, me te aroha mō ngā tamariki. Ka kitea hoki aua uaratanga ki waenga i ngā tamariki. Ka kitea te pakari o te whakahoahoa me ngā hononga ako ki waenga i ngā tamariki me te whānau. Ka manaaki ngā tuākana i ō rātou tēina i a rātou e noho ngātahi ana. Ka ako ngā tamariki i te hauora me te akuaku hei āhuatanga tonu o te hōtaka akoranga. Ka kitea te manaaki me te whakaaro nui o ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō, ki te taiao hoki.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i te reo Māori mā te tūhura haere i te ao māoriori me te ao tūturu. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā haerenga i ia te wā, puta noa i te tau. Ka whakamahere ngā kaimahi i ngā hōtaka akoranga e hāpai ana i te ako i te reo Māori mā te tūhura. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te kawe ake i ā rātou akoranga. E noho wātea ana ki a rātou te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi e akiaki ana i tō rātou taha auaha. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i tō rātou taiao.

Ka kitea te ngākau nui o ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te hōhonutanga o te reo. Ka whakamihi, ka whakakoia hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā whakamātau a ngā tamariki i roto i te reo Māori. E whakatairanga ana ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori mā ngā huarahi e whai hua ana ki te whakawhānui i te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te reo. Ka whakamahi rātou i te reo Māori hei whakatau, hei poipoi, hei manaaki hoki i ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahia te reo Māori hei whakaako ki ngā tamariki ō rātou whakapapa. Ka whakamahia tō rātou pepeha hei whakamana i tō rātou whai wāhi nui ki te ao Māori. Ka kitea te tino noho pūmau o te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakatairanga i te reo Māori mō rātou katoa. Ka tūmāia ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao o te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ko te ako me te atawhai i ngā tamariki e tino tautokona ana e te whānau. Ka arotahi te whānau ki ngā matea o ngā tamariki, me te whakapakaritanga ake o te tupu tonu me te whakapai tonu. Ahakoa te nui o ngā whakarerekētanga ki ngā tūnga kaimahi i roto i te tau kua pahure ake nei, kua whakapau kaha tonu te whānau ki te whakapūmau i ngā whakahaeretanga e whai hua ana ki ngā tamariki, me te whakawhanake tonutanga o ngā āheinga o ngā kaimahi. He pakari te āheinga o ngā kaimahi, ā, he tokomaha o rātou e mau ana i ngā tohu mātauranga, tae atu hoki ki Te Tohu Whakapakari. Hui ai te whānau i ia te wā, ā, he pai tā rātou whakatakoto ā-tuhi i ā rātou whakataunga. He mārama ā rātou tukanga mō te whakahaere i ngā whakapaitanga me te tutuki haere i ngā kaupapa matua. Kua kawe ake ētahi o ngā mema o te whānau i ngā mahi whakahaere matua, ā, ka āta whakarite rātou i te noho mōhio, i te arotahi tonu hoki o te whānau ki ā rātou kaupapa matua i tāutuhia ai.

He pai te whakamahere i te ako a ngā tamariki me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ka mahi ngātahi ngā kaimahi ki te whānau, hei whakamahere i ngā hōtaka akoranga e arotahi ana ki te whakapakari ake i te tūmāia me te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. Ka whai whakaaro rātou ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo matea o ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakarite i ngā wheako akoranga e whai pūtake ana, kua whakahāngaitia anō hoki ki ia tamaiti. E tino māramahia ana Te Whāriki e ngā kaimahi, ā, he pai tā rātou whakamahinga hei whakatairanga i te ako i te reo Māori, puta noa i Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Arotakengia ai e ngā kaimahi te hōtaka akoranga i ia te wā. Ka whakaemi ngā kaimahi i ngā mōhiohio e whai hua ana, ā, e whai pānga ana hoki ki te ako reo o ia tamaiti me te whakawhanaketanga i tōna katoa.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E tupu haere ana te hiahia o ngā mema o te whānau kia mātātoa ake tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. E mōhio ana rātou, e tika ana kia:

  • pakari ake te aroturuki i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru
  • piki ake te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo me te tari, me ngā whakaritenga
  • pai ake te whakatinanatanga o ngā pūnaha e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o ngā kaimahi
  • whakaritea tētahi mahere rautaki e whai pānga ana ki te tirohanga o te whānau, me ngā hiahia, te aronga anō hoki o ia whare.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture. Kia whakatutukihia e te whānau o te kōhanga reo āna herenga ā-ture, me tahuri rātou kia:

  • noho mōhio ai ngā pākeke e whakarato ana i te mātauranga me te atawhai ki ngā akoako hōneatanga ohorere e tika ana, ā, kia whakatutukihia aua akoako i ia te wā ki te taha o ngā tamariki, me te whakatakoto anō hoki i ngā tuhinga e tika ana.

[R 46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS 8 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • tautoko te tari ā-rohe o te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā whakaritenga matua me te herenga ā-ture i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo
  • whakapakarihia e te whānau tō rātou hononga ki te purapura, hei whakapuaki ngātahi i ngā whakaritenga papai me ngā mōhiotanga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga a te whānau e whai hua ai.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Raumanga Kōhungahunga i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

1 Context

Ko Manaia te maunga

Ko Raumanga te awa

Ko Te Puna o Te Matauranga te marae

Ko Raumanga te kōhanga reo.

Te Kohanga Reo o Raumanga Kōhungahunga is located on the grounds of North Tec, Whangarei. Since the previous review, whānau have developed a strategic plan for each site, the under two’s and over twos. They have improved kaimahi capability and qualifications. Assessment practices have improved the collection of information about children’s learning.

Over the past year, whānau faced challenges with significant staffing changes. This had an impact on operations. However, the whānau have worked through key matters and staffing for both sites. Additional priorities for development have emerged as a result of their internal evaluation.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children show confidence in and understand te reo Māori?

Children show great confidence when using and understanding te reo Māori.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children receive a wide range of enjoyable language learning experiences. They are actively engaged in learning that enriches their knowledge of te reo Māori, and broadens their understanding of the language. Spontaneous conversations are often heard between older children and adults. All children are familiar with and respond well to te reo Māori in formal and informal situations. They enjoy language activities that provide challenge. Kaimahi use effective language learning techniques that increase children’s knowledge and ability to communicate. Parents report that children use te reo Māori and their knowledge of tikanga in the home. Children choose to use te reo and tikanga Māori.

Children experience the values of rangimārie and holistic wellbeing in their learning. Adults’ model patience, understanding, respect and aroha for children. Children display these behaviours with each other. Strong friendships and relationships for learning are evident between children and whānau. Tuakana care for their teina when they are together. Children learn about health and hygiene as part of the learning programme. Children demonstrate care and consideration for each other and the environment.

Children learn te reo Māori through exploration of the natural and physical worlds. Children participate in regular excursions throughout the year. Kaimahi plan programmes of learning that support learning te reo Māori through exploration. Children confidently take charge of their own learning. They have easy access to a wide range of resources that encourages their creativity. Children enjoy exploring their environment.

Children show an interest in using complex language. Kaimahi praise and affirm children’s efforts to experiment with language. Kaimahi promote te reo Māori in effective ways that broaden children’s understanding of the language. They use te reo Māori to settle, calm and care for children. Te reo Māori is used to teach children about their whakapapa. Their pepeha is used to affirm their place in te ao Māori. Whānau and kaimahi demonstrate a strong commitment to promoting te reo Māori for all. Children are confident in a te reo Māori environment.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s learning and care is well-supported by the whānau. The whānau focus on the needs of children and building sustained growth and improvement. Despite significant staffing changes over the past year, whānau have worked to maintain effective operations for children and continued to grow kaimahi capability. Kaimahi capability is strong with a number who hold qualifications, including Te Tohu Whakapakari. Whānau meet regularly and keep good records of their decisions. They have clear processes for managing improvement and working on key priorities. The whānau members who have assumed key management duties make sure that other whānau are well informed and remain focussed on their identified priorities.

Children’s learning and development is well planned. Kaimahi collaborate with whānau to plan programmes of learning that focus on building children’s confidence and understanding of te reo Māori. They consider the diverse needs of children and prepare learning experiences that are meaningful and personalised for individuals. Te Whāriki is well understood by kaimahi and used well to promote te reo Maori learning across Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Kaimahi regularly review the learning programme. Kaimahi gather useful information about each child’s language learning and holistic development.

Key Next Steps

There is growing interest by more whānau members to be actively involved in managing the kōhanga reo. They recognise the need for: 

  • stronger monitoring of health and safety practices
  • increased whānau involvement in management, administration and operations
  • improved implementation of the personnel management systems
  • a strategic plan that reflects the whānau vision and the needs and direction of each site. 

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing: 

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake. 

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

ERO identified areas of non-compliance. In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements, they must:

  • ensure adults providing education and care are familiar with relevant emergency drills and regularly carry these out with the children and maintain supporting documentation.
    [R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS 8 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendations

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) District support to whānau to address the key next steps, and area of non-compliance, identified in this report
  • Whānau strengthen their connection with the local purapura to share best practice and knowledge of effective whanau management.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Raumanga Kōhungahunga will be in three years.
