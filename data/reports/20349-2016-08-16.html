Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōtuku ki ngā papa o te kura tuarua o Rutherford ki Te Atatū, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau. E whanake ana te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hononga mā te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. He pakari ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao ako e whakaongaonga ana, e poipoi ana hoki i a rātou.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōtuku i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōtuku ki ngā papa o te kura tuarua o Rutherford, ki Te Atatū, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau. I te tīmatanga, i muri te kōhanga reo i te marae o Te Kōtuku, tae rawa atu ki te tau 1996. E whai hua tonu ana ngā hononga ki waenga i te kōhanga reo, te kura tuarua o Rutherford, me Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Te Kōtuku.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te whanake haere o te mōhio o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hononga mā te reo me ngā tikanga Māori?

E whakawhanake ana te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hononga mā te reo me ngā tikanga Māori.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira 

He māhorahora te pupū ake o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. Ka poipoi ngā kaimahi i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki te taha o ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakamahi rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki reo, tae atu ki te tāruarua me te whakatauira. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā whai wāhitanga ōpaki me ngā whai wāhitanga ōkawa ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou mōhiotanga ki waenga i a rātou anō, ā, ki ngā kaimahi anō hoki. Ka kōrero ētahi o ngā whānau i te reo Māori ki te kāinga, ā, ka ako hoki rātou ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. He māori te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori.

Ka kitea te ngākaunui, te tākare hoki o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao māori, tūturu hoki. He pārekareka ki a rātou te tūhura haere o roto, o waho hoki. He whakaruruhau ki tētahi wāhi o waho, ā, he wāhi tākaro tēnei ahakoa te āhua o te rangi. Ka piki, ka peke, ka huna anō hoki ngā tamariki, huri noa i ngā taputapu i hangaia ai mā rātou. He pai te tākaro ngātahi a ngā tamariki, ā, he maha ngā wā ka whakaari rātou i ngā tūmomo kiripuaki, tae atu ki te taniwha. E ū ana te whānau ki te whakawhānui i te papa tākaro mō ā rātou tamariki, ā, i tēnei wā, kei te kohi pūtea rātou mō tētahi wāhi māra hou. He pākiki te tūhura haere o ngā tamariki.

E puāwai ana ngā tamariki ki te taiao pai e whakaute ana i a rātou. He atawhai, he manaaki hoki tō ā rātou taunekeneke me ō rātou hononga ki ō rātou hoa, ki ngā kaimahi, ā, ki te whānau tonu. Ka whai ngā pēpi i ō rātou ake ngākaunuitanga, ā, he wāhi tākaro motuhake tō rātou e mau ana i ngā taumahi me ngā rauemi e tika ana mō rātou. He maha ngā wā ka ako ngātahi te tuakana me te teina. Kua āta whakaritea ngā mahinga, ā, he ngāwari ngā whakawhitinga ki ngā tūmomo taumahi. He mauritau, he harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki.

He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i tō rātou tūmāia ki te kōhanga reo. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ō rātou pepeha, ā, ka taki rātou. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i ngā mahi ā-ringa e pā ana ki ia wāhanga o ngā pepeha, hei akiaki i ngā tamariki. Ka ārahi ngā tamariki i ngā taumahi, tae atu ki ngā karakia o ia rā me te karanga i te rārangi ingoa. E tupu ana ngā tamariki hei kaiārahi ki tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i te kounga o ngā whai wāhitanga ako e aro nui ana ki ō rātou matea. Ka āta whakamaheretia, ka āta aroturukitia, ka āta aromātaitia hoki te ako a ngā tamariki. He mārama ngā tukanga ā ngā kaimahi mō te whakamahere i te hōtaka. Whakatutukihia ai te aromatawai i ia te wā, tae atu ki ngā kōrerorero e ahu mai ana i ngā kitenga, ngā arowhai whakawhanaketanga, me ngā tirohanga. He ākonga angitu ngā tamariki.

Ka whakaaro huritao, ka aromātai hoki ngā kaimahi i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakaatu aua mōhiohio i ngā huarahi ka whai ake mā ngā kaimahi ki te whakapai ake tonu. E whai hua ana te aromātai o roto, ā, e whakarato ana tēnei i te haere tonutanga o te whakawhanaketanga me te whakapaitanga.

E noho ngākaunui ana te whānau ki te reo Māori, ā, he mārama ō rātou wawata mō ā rātou tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Kua oti i a rātou tētahi mahere rautaki i whakatinanahia ai e Te Ara Tūāpae me Te Korowai. E mōhio ana rātou ki ā rātou kaupapa matua o te wā, me ērā kei te whai ake. E whai hua ana ngā pūnaha whakahaere pūtea i whakaritea ai e te whānau, ā, e hāpai ana ēnei i te māramatanga o ngā mahi. Kua whakatutukihia ngā āhuatanga katoa e pā ana ki te whakahaere kaimahi, ā, e aro nui ana ki te whakawhanaketanga ngaio. Ka ākina ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakawhanake ake i te whakamahinga o te reo Māori. E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki hei ākonga ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore i te whānau tētehi mahere ā-tau. Ahakoa ka whakatutukihia, ka whakaotingia hoki ētahi o ngā kaupapa matua e kōkirihia ana, he uaua ki te aroturuki i te whānuitanga o te āhua o ngā mahi me ngā putanga tonu i runga i ā rātou kaupapa rautaki matua. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā ngā pūnaha papai ka whakatakotohia e te whānau mō te whakahaeretanga me te aroturukitanga.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōtuku ki ngā papa o te kura tuarua o Rutherford ki Te Atatū, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau. E whanake ana te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hononga mā te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. He pakari ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao ako e whakaongaonga ana, e poipoi ana hoki i a rātou.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōtuku is on the grounds of Rutherford College in Te Atatū, Auckland. Children are developing an awareness of their connections through te reo and tikanga Māori. Children are confident in their stimulating and nurturing learning environment. 

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōtuku will be in three years. 

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōtuku is on the grounds of Rutherford College in Te Atatū, Auckland. The kōhanga reo was originally behind Te Kōtuku Marae until 1996. Positive relationships continue between the kōhanga reo, Rutherford College and Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Te Kōtuku.

2 The Review Findings

How well are children developing an awareness of their connections through te reo and tikanga Māori?

Children are developing an awareness of their connections through te reo and tikanga Māori.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children naturally use te reo Māori on a regular basis. Kaimahi foster conversations with children and use a range of language learning strategies including repetition and modelling. Children enjoy informal and formal opportunities to share what they know with each other and kaimahi. Some whānau speak te reo Māori at home and learn alongside their children at kōhanga reo. Children use te reo Māori naturally.

Children show enjoyment and satisfaction in their natural and physical environment. They enjoy exploring inside and outdoors. A covered outdoor area provides space to play regardless of the weather. Children climb, jump and hide using the purpose built equipment. Children play well together and often role play interesting characters including taniwha. Whānau are committed to extending the playground for their tamariki and are currently fundraising for a new garden area. Children are inquisitive explorers.

Children thrive in a positive and respectful environment. Interactions and relationships with peers, kaimahi and whānau are warm and caring. Babies follow their own interests and have a separate play area with age appropriate activities and resources.  Tuakana and teina frequently learn alongside each other.  Routines are established with smooth transitions between activities. Children are relaxed, happy and settled.

Children have opportunities to develop confidence at kōhanga reo.  Children know and recite their pepeha. Kaimahi model actions for each part of the pepeha to prompt children. Children lead activities including the daily karakia and the roll call. Children are growing as leaders in their kōhanga reo.

Planning and Evaluation

Children receive quality learning opportunities that are responsive to their needs. Children’s learning is well planned, monitored and evaluated.  Kaimahi have clear processes for programme planning. Assessment takes place on a regular basis including observation anecdotes, developmental checklists and observations. Children are successful learners.

Kaimahi reflect and evaluate the effectiveness of the learning programme. This information shows how kaimahi can improve. Effective internal evaluation provides ongoing development and improvement.

Whānau are passionate about te reo Māori and have clear aspirations for their children at kōhanga reo.  They have completed a strategic plan that is informed by Te Ara Tūāpae and Te Korowai.  They know their current and future priorities. Whānau have effective financial management systems in place that support transparency. All personnel management is complete and strongly focussed on professional development.  Opportunities to further develop use of te reo Māori are encouraged for both kaimahi and whānau. Children are well supported as learners at kōhanga reo.

Key Next Steps

The whānau does not have an annual plan.  Whilst some on-going priorities are achieved and completed, it is difficult to monitor the overall performance and outcomes against their strategic priorities. Children benefit from whānau that have effective management and monitoring systems in place.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōtuku is on the grounds of Rutherford College in Te Atatū, Auckland. Children are developing an awareness of their connections through te reo and tikanga Māori. Children are confident in their stimulating and nurturing learning environment. 

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōtuku will be in three years.
