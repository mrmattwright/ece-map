1 Evaluation of the Service

How well placed is the service to promote positive outcomes for children?

This playcentre is well placed to promote positive outcomes for children.

Context

Otorohanga Playcentre is a parent-led centre that is part of the King Country Playcentre Association. The playcentre’s philosophy is based on ‘active involvement of parents/ caregivers/whānau’ with parents being first educators and learning alongside their children.

Since the 2010 ERO review there has been a considerable change in membership with most families new to the playcentre. Parents run two morning sessions from 9.30 to noon on Monday and Wednesday. There is also a ‘Big Kids’ session on Friday mornings supported by an experienced supervisor.

This review was conducted as part of a cluster approach to reviews in ten early childhood education services within the King Country Playcentre Association umbrella organisation.

The Review Findings

The New Zealand Playcentre Federation and the King Country Playcentre Association provide effective governance and management for this centre. The association provides comprehensive policy guidelines and an experienced centre support person who assists families to run the service. The playcentre is well led and effectively managed by a group of dedicated parents/caregivers. The strongly collaborative approach amongst educators ensures that each parents' particular strengths are encouraged, valued and used. More experienced members actively mentor new families and strongly promote the professional development and training programmes available through the association. This is enabling parents to become more knowledgeable and confident in their role as educators of their children.

Children benefit from a high-quality learning environment that encourages creative and active play, promotes literacy and numeracy experiences, and caters for their diverse interests and abilities. Parents carefully prepare a range of activities in response to children’s interests that provides opportunities for children to initiate and sustain learning and play. Children’s social and emotional development is supported by meaningful interactions with other children and adults. Children are increasingly confident and capable learners.

Parents develop strong supportive networks with each other. They are clearly focused on providing the best possible learning experiences for their children. There is an established culture of reflective self review which includes session evaluation and centre-specific strategic planning. These self-review practices have successfully identified areas for ongoing review and development.

Parents have identified and ERO agrees that introducing a greater bicultural presence and perspective to the playcentre will further enhance children’s learning.

2 Legal Requirements

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Otorohanga Playcentre completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service’s systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

General Information about Early Childhood Reviews

About ERO Reviews

The Education Review Office (ERO) is the New Zealand government department that reviews schools and early childhood services throughout New Zealand.

Review focus

ERO's education reviews in early childhood services focus on the factors that contribute to positive learning outcomes for children. ERO evaluates how well placed the service is to make and sustain improvements for the benefit of all children at the service. To reach these findings ERO considers:

  • Pou Whakahaere – how the service determines its vision, philosophy and direction to ensure positive outcomes for children
  • Pou Ārahi – how leadership is enacted to enhance positive outcomes for children
  • Mātauranga – whose knowledge is valued and how the curriculum is designed to achieve positive outcomes for children
  • Tikanga whakaako – how approaches to teaching and learning respond to diversity and support positive outcomes for children.

Within these areas ERO considers the effectiveness of self review and partnerships with parents and whānau.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of service performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for children and useful to the service.
