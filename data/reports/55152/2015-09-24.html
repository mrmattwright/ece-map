1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Atawhai ki te taha o te marae o Te Ahu o Te Tūranga, ki te tāone tuawhenua o Woodville. He maha ngā wāhi taiao, ngā pouwhenua motuhake hoki e karapoti ana i te kōhanga reo, tae atu ki te kapiti o Manawatū, ngā maunga o Tararua me Ruahine, me te awa o Manawatū.

He tokoiti ngā mema o te whānau, ā, e noho ngākau nui ana rātou ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. Kua āta heke iho te tatau o ngā tamariki i roto i ngā tau kua pahure ake nei, ahakoa te tokomaha o ngā tamariki i te hapori kāhore e whai wāhi atu ana ki te mātauranga kōhungahunga. E whai whakaaro ana te whānau ki ngā momo huarahi ka whai wāhi mai aua tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te maha o ngā āhuatanga papai ki te kōhanga reo. Ko ētahi o aua āhuatanga ko te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki, te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, te whakamahere rautaki, te whakamahere hōtaka, me te aromātai. I tāutu hoki te pūrongo, ko te reo Māori tētahi āhuatanga hei whakapai ake.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te kite i te oranga o ngā tamariki?

Ka kitea te tū pakari me te oranga o ngā tamariki. Ka manaaki tētahi i tētahi. Me whai wāhitanga anō hoki rātou ki te ako i te reo Māori.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka taunekeneke ngā tamariki hei tuakana, hei teina rānei. He māoriori te manaaki a ngā tuākana i ngā pēpi, ā, ka whakauru i a rātou ki ā rātou tākaro. He maha ngā wā ka kite ngā pēpi i te āhua o ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā tuākana me ngā pākeke, ā, ka tāwhai i ā rātou whanonga. Ka noho whakaute, ka whai whakaaro hoki ngā tamariki ki waenga i a rātou anō.

He ākonga pākiki ngā tamariki, ā, ka ngākau nui rātou ki te tūhura i ō rātou taiao ako. Kei ā rātou hōtaka o ia ata, ko ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, me ngā mihimihi, ā, he wā anō hoki ka whiriwhiri ngā tamariki i ā rātou ake taumahi. E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga e whai pānga ana ki ō rātou tūmomo reanga me ō rātou tūmomo āheinga. Haere ai ngā tamariki ki ngā kaupapa i te hapori, ā, ka taunekeneke ki ngā tāngata hou, ka whai wāhi atu hoki ki ngā wheako hou. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi ako whakahihiri.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako me te tākaro, ā, he tau hoki rātou. Kua āta whakaritea ngā tino mahinga, ngā taumahi, me ngā kaupapa, ā, he pai te rere o aua āhuatanga. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki, ā, kei te mōhio pai rātou ki ngā pākeke e noho ngākau nui ana ki tō rātou waiora.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Me whakapakari te whānau me ngā kaimahi i te whakamahinga o te reo Māori. Ko te kaiako anake te tangata e whakamahi ana i te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He pai te whakahaeretanga o ētahi āhuatanga o ngā whakaritenga ki te kōhanga reo. Kei te whakaraupapa te tūtohinga i ngā tukanga me ngā tikanga e tika ana mō te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. He rawaka ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. E tohu ana ngā pūnaha pūtea i te noho mārama, te whai kawenga hoki o ngā whakawhitinga katoa. He tuhinga tūranga mahi, he kirimana hoki tā ia kaimahi, ā, kua waitohungia hoki ēnei.

Ka āta mātakitaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki i a rātou e ako ana, ā, ka kohi hoki i ngā tauira mahi. Ka whakaemi rātou i aua mōhiohio, ā, ka aroturuki i te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamōhio rātou i te whānau i ia te wā, mō te ahu whakamua a ā rātou ake tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Ka whakatutuki te kaiako i te nuinga o ngā mahi e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, ā, ka whakaako hoki ia i ngā tamariki. Me whai whakaaro te whānau ki ngā huarahi hei whai ake, kia whakatutukihia e rātou ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi anō hoki. Me aro wawe ki tēnei āhuatanga nā tōna whai pānga nui ki ngā tamariki me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo.

Kāhore i te rawaka te whakamahere hōtaka me te aromātai. Ahakoa ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā mahere o ia wiki, kāhore ēnei e tino whai kiko ana. Kāhore he mahere pae tawhiti, pae waenga, o ia rā rānei hei ārahi i te whakapuakitanga o te hōtaka. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e hui tahi ana i ia te wā ki te aromātai i ngā āhuatanga i whai hua ai, me ērā hei whakapakari ake anō. Me whakauru i aua take hei kaupapa matua i te wā ka arohaehae te whānau i ngā kaimahi.

Kāhore ngā hui ā-whānau e tū auau ana i ia te wā. Nā tēnei, he uaua ki te whānau ki te whakatau i ngā tino whakaritenga me te aroturuki i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga e whai ake nei, kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture:

  • me whakarite i te whakatinanatanga o tētahi pūnaha arohaehae auau. [R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA7]

4 Te Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia tahuri te whānau ki te:

  • whakatinana i tētahi aronga nahanaha ki te whakapiki ake i te āheinga o te whānau me ngā kaimahi i roto i te reo Māori
  • whakatinana i te tautoko mō te whānau, kia āta mārama ai rātou ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki
  • whakapakari i ngā mahi ki te whakamahere i te hōtaka me te aromātai
  • whakatū auau i ngā hui o ia marama, hei hāpai i ngā mahi ki te whakapakari ake i ngā whakataunga a te whānau, ngā pūrongo, me te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo
  • whakatinana i ngā mahi ki te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Atawhai ki Woodville. E tautokona ana te ako a ngā tamariki mā ngā huarahi papai. Ahakoa kei te whakatutuki i ētahi whakawhanaketanga ki te kōhanga reo, e tika ana hoki kia toro atu Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te kōhanga reo nei, hei āta tautoko i te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakapai ake anō i āna whakaritenga.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Atawhai i roto ngā tau e rua.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Atawhai is next to Te Ahu o Te Tūranga Marae, in the rural township of Woodville. Several natural features and landmarks including the Manawatu Gorge, the Tararua and Ruahine Ranges and the Manawatu River surround the kōhanga reo.

The kōhanga whānau is a small in number and are committed to the kaupapa of te kōhanga reo. Roll numbers have slowly declined over the years even though there are many children in the community who do not attend any form of early childhood education. Whānau are looking at ways to attract these children back to the kōhanga reo.

The previous ERO report identified several positive features in the kōhanga reo. These included the children’s learning programme, whānau management, strategic planning, programme planning and evaluation. The report identified te reo Māori as an area for improvement.

2 The Review Findings

To what extent do children show they are well?

Children show personal confidence and wellness. They care for each other. They need further opportunity to learn te reo Māori.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children interact as tuakana or teina. The older children naturally care for the babies and include them in their play. The babies often observe how the older children and adults interact with each other and mimic their behaviours. Children are respectful and considerate towards each other.

Children are inquisitive learners who like to explore their learning environments. The morning programme consists of karakia, hīmene, mihimihi and time where children chose their own activities. Children have access to various learning experiences that consider their different ages and abilities. Children attend community activities and interact with new people and experiences. Children engage in a range of interesting learning activities.

Children enjoy and are comfortable as they learn and play. Clear routines, activities and events are well established and flow smoothly. Children are happy and settled with familiar adults that are committed to their wellbeing.

Key Next Steps

Whānau and kaimahi strengthen the use of te reo Māori. The kaiako is the only person who uses te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

There are some aspects of the kōhanga reo operations that are managed well. The charter outlines the processes and conditions required for whānau to manage the kōhanga reo. Health and safety practices are adequate. Financial systems show that all transactions are transparent and accountable. Kaimahi have signed job descriptions and employment contracts.

Kaimahi make observations of children as they learn and collect work samples. They collect this information and monitor children’s progress. They keep whānau informed about their child’s success.

Key Next Steps

The kaiako does most of the work with kōhanga reo operations and the teaching of children. The whānau need to consider how they can fulfil their roles and responsibilities. This is a matter of urgency because of its impact on children and the kaupapa of kōhanga reo.

Programme planning and evaluation is inadequate. Although kaimahi use weekly plans, these lack detail. There are no long term, medium term and daily plans to guide programme delivery. Kaimahi do not meet regularly to evaluate what worked and did not and why. These issues should be included as priorities when whānau appraise kaimahi.

Whānau hui are not held on a regular basis. This makes it difficult for whānau to make sound decisions and monitor the kōhanga reo operations.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

ERO identified the following areas of non-compliance:

  • ensure that a system of regular appraisal is implemented. [R47 Education (ECS) Regulations 2008 GMA7]

4 Recommendation

ERO recommends that the whānau:

  • implement a planned approach to increase te reo Māori capability of whānau and kaimahi
  • implement support for whānau so they fully understand their roles and responsibilities
  • strengthen programme planning and evaluation
  • raise the regularity of monthly meetings to help strengthen whānau decision making, reporting and kōhanga reo management
  • implement kaimahi appraisals.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Atawhai is in Woodville. Children are supported with their learning in positive ways. While there are some developments occurring in the kōhanga reo, there is a need for the Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust to provide extensive support to the whānau and kaimahi to improve its operations.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Atawhai will be within two years.
