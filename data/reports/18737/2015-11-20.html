1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā te Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori i Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia ai i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai e te pūrongo a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i mua atu, me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

  • te whakamahere me te aromātai, tae atu ki ngā tūranga o te whānau me ngā kawenga mahi, te tūtohinga me te arotake i ngā kaupapa here, ngā kaimahi, te whakahaere i ngā pūtea, te whakamahere rautaki, Te Whāriki, me te arotake whaiaro
  • ngā mahi e tika ana hei tutuki ngā herenga ā-ture, tae atu ki ngā take e pā ana ki te whare
  • ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai whai muri i te arotake o mua.

2 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Maungataniwha ki te marae o Mangamuka, ā, he hapori tuawhenua tēnei ki te tonga o Kaitāia. Nō te tau 1982 te kōhanga reo i whakatūngia ai, ā, ko te tirohanga me ngā wawata o te whānau mō ā rātou tamariki i whakaritea ai i taua wā, e ārahi tonu ana i ā rātou mahi. Ko te reo me te ao Māori e noho pūmau ana ki ngā ngākau me ngā whakaaro o te whānau.

I tāutuhia ki te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i te Pipiri o 2011, te maha o ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He pai te taunekeneke a ngā tamariki ki waenga i a rātou anō me ngā pākeke. Ko te pai o tō rātou whanonga e whakatairanga ana i ō rātou hononga ki waenga i a rātou anō. Ka tautāwhitia ngā tamariki e rangirua ana e ngā pākeke, mā ngā huarahi e āta whakatau ana i a rātou. Noho tahi ai ngā pākeke me ngā tamariki i ia te wā, ā, ka āta tautoko rātou i ngā tamariki. He mauritau ngā tamariki.

Rongo ai ngā tamariki i te reo Māori, puta noa i te rā. Ka ngākau nui rātou ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā mihimihi, me ngā pepeha. Ka whakatauira ngā kaiako i ngā hanganga reo e tika ana, ā, ka akiaki hoki rātou i ngā kōrero, hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. Ka mārama ngā tamariki i ngā kīanga me ngā pātai i roto i te reo Māori. Ka kitea tēnei nā te āhua tonu o ā rātou whakautu. He pai te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga e whai pūtake ana. Kua waia rātou ki te hōtaka o ia rā, ā, he hanganga tā tēnei e whakawātea ana i ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, tae atu ki ngā kaupapa ako, ā, he rōpū anō hoki e kōkirihia ana e ngā pākeke.

He ngāwari ngā whakawhitinga ki waenga i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me ngā mahinga. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tākaro mō te wā roa, me te whakamātau haere mā ngā huarahi haumaru. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te tūhura haere. Ka whakatutukihia ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki.

Te whakamahere me te aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Kua pai ake ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau. Kua arotakengia e te whānau te tūtohinga me te angamahi o ngā kaupapa here, ā, kua rau atu ki tēnei i nāianei ko ngā aratohu e pā ana ki ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga mahi, me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. He mārama te whakahaere i ngā pūtea. Kua oti ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi, ā, kua tāutuhia hoki ngā whāinga arohaehae mā ngā kaimahi. E tino tautokona ana ngā painga e whai hua ana mō ngā tamariki.

Kua whakatutukihia e te whānau ngā mahi katoa kia noho haumaru ai, kia whai oranga ai anō hoki te wāhi mō ngā tamariki. He noho ā rātou mahi ki te whakatika i te whare, ā, he wai mahana i nāianei mā ngā tamariki. He hāneanea te noho o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao.

He tino aronga tā te whānau ki te whakapai haere. Kua āta tautokona, kua āta arahina hoki te whānau e Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo mā tā rātou arotake whakatau o te whakahaeretanga o te tari. Kua whakapai ake te whānau i te kounga o ā rātou pūnaha, ā rātou pūrongo, me te arotake whaiaro. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te tū auau o ngā arotake.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Me arotake ētahi o ngā kaupapa here matua me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. He maha ngā kaupapa here o te hauora me te haumaru kāhore anō kia whakahāngaitia ki ngā herenga ā-ture o te tau 2008.

Kāhore anō kia oti te mahere rautaki. He whānui ngā kaupapa rautaki me ngā mahi, ā, kāhore i te mārama. Kāhore anō kia whakawhanakehia tētahi mahere ā-tau, hei whakaraupapa i ngā tāngata ka kawe i ngā mahi matua, i te āhua hoki o aua mahi matua, me te wā tonu ka whakatutukihia. Kāhore i te tino mārama ngā mahere e pā ana ki ngā whakapaitanga mā ngā tamariki.

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • arotake i ngā kaupapa here matua me ngā tukanga matua e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru, hei āta whakarite i te hāngaitanga o ēnei ki te Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008
  • arotake i te mahere rautaki hei āta whakarite i te māramatanga o ngā mahi.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Maungataniwha ki Mangamuka, e pā tata atu ana ki Kaitāia. He pai te taunekeneke a ngā tamariki ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ngā pākeke hoki. Rongo ai rātou i te reo Māori puta noa i te rā. He māramatanga ngātahi tō te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga mahi, ā, he pai tā rātou mahi ngātahi. He pai tā rātou whakahaeretanga i te nuinga o ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Maungataniwha i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of the Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of the kōhanga reo governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the previous ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • planning and evaluation including whānau roles and responsibilities, charter and policy review, personnel, financial management, strategic planning, Te Whāriki and self-review
  • actions required for compliance including building concerns
  • any issues identified since the previous review.

2 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Maungataniwha is at Mangamuka Marae in a rural community south of Kaitaia. The vision and aspirations the whānau have for their children when they first established in 1982, continue to guide their current practice. Te reo and te ao Māori remain foremost in the hearts and minds of whānau.

The previous ERO report of June 2011 identified several areas that required development.

3 Findings

Areas of progress

Children interact well with each other and adults. Their positive behaviour enhances their relationships with each other. Unsettled children are comforted in reassuring ways by adults. Adults are regularly with children and support them well. Children are relaxed.

Children hear te reo Māori throughout the day. They willingly participate in karakia, hīmene, mihimihi and pepeha. Kaiako model correct language structures and use prompts to encourage children’s te reo Māori development. Children understand phrases and questions in te reo Māori. This is evident in their responses. Children are developing well as speakers of te reo Māori.

Children engage in purposeful learning. They are familiar with the daily programme which has structure, allows them to follow their interests, includes kaupapa ako and has adult-led groups. Transitions between children’s learning and routines are smooth. There are opportunities for children to play for long periods and to take risks in safe ways. Kaimahi encourage children to explore. Children’s learning needs are met.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of progress

Whānau management practices have improved. Whānau have reviewed the charter and policy framework which now includes clear guidelines about their roles and responsibilities, the kaupapa of kōhanga reo. Financial management is transparent. Personnel documentation is complete and appraisal goals are identified for kaimahi. Children’s interests are well supported.

The whānau has taken all reasonable steps to make the physical environment safe and healthy for children. They have made significant repairs to the building and children now have access to warm water. Children are comfortable in their surroundings.

Whānau have a strong focus on improvement. Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) have supported and guided whānau well with their assurance review of office administration. Whānau have improved the quality of their systems, reporting and internal review. Children benefit from regular review.

Key next steps

Core health and safety policies with procedures require review. Several of these policies do not align to the 2008 regulations.

The strategic plan is incomplete. Strategic actions and tasks are broad and not clear. An annual plan which identifies who, what and when key tasks will be put in place each year has not been developed. Planning to improve for children is unclear.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • review core health and safety policies and procedures to ensure they align with the Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008
  • review the strategic plan to ensure the actions are clear.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Maungataniwa is in Mangamuka, near Kaitaia. Children interact well with each other and adults. They hear te reo Māori throughout the day. Whānau and kaiako have a shared understanding of their roles and responsibilities and work well together. They manage most aspects of the kōhanga reo operations well.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Maungataniwha will be in three years.
