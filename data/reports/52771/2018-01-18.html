1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi aromātai tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā te Toihau Arotake i Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia ai i roto i te pūrongo aromātai mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei i tāutuhia ai whai muri o taua aromātai.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei aromātai tāpiri i whakaritea i runga i te aromātai o te whakatutukitanga a te whānau i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai e te pūrongo a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i mua atu, me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua aromātai. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei aromātai, ko te titiro ki:

  • te Mana Reo – te whakapakari i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki
  • te Mana Tangata – ngā hōtaka ka hāpai i ngā whanonga ā-tangata
  • te whakamahere me te aromātai – te kounga o ngā whakaritenga ako, te māramatanga o te whānau ki ngā tūranga me ngā kawenga mahi, te arotake i ngā kaupapa here, me te whakamahere rautaki
  • ngā āhuatanga kāhore e tutuki ana ngā herenga ā-ture – te tuhi haere i ia te wā i ngā akoako hōneatanga inā he ahi, he rū whenua rānei, me
  • ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai whai muri i te arotake o mua.

2 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo Kautu ki te Rangi ki tētahi whare i te marae o Tauporohenui, i Hāwera.

Whai muri i te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2015, kua arotahi te whānau ki te hūnuku ki tētahi whare hou hei te mutunga tonu o tēnei tau. Nō te tīmatanga o te tau 2017 tētahi kaimahi hou i whakatūngia ai.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo Māori i roto i te hōtaka o ia rā. Kei ngā kaimahi ngā taumahi ki te whakapakari i te reo o ngā tamariki. Ko ngā waiata, te whakapuakitanga o ngā kupu hou, me te taki i ngā ingoa o ngā tae me ngā nama e hāpai ana i ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori. Engari, e kōrero ana ngā tamariki ki waenga i a rātou anō mā te reo Pākehā, ā, ka whakapuaki māhorahora i ētahi paku kupu noa o te reo Māori.

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i ngā pūkenga ki te whakahoahoa ki ētahi atu. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te tohatoha, te manaaki, me te mahi ngātahi hoki hei tuākana, hei tēina. E whakatakoto ana ngā mahinga i ngā tūmanako ka whāia e ngā tamariki i a rātou e ako ana ki te taha o ō rātou hoa. Ka kōrero ngātahi, ka tākaro ngātahi. Ka whakaaro nui ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Me whakapai tonu i ngā wheako ako a ngā tamariki. Ahakoa e mōhio ana ngā kaimahi ki ngā āhuatanga ake me ngā matea ake o ia tamaiti, kāhore te hōtaka akoranga e whakatinanahia ana ki aua tūāhuatanga.

Me tahuri ngā kaimahi ki te:

  • whakawhanake, te whakamahere, me te whakatinana i tētahi hōtaka ka whakapakari ai i te whakamahinga o ngā tamariki i te reo Māori
  • toro atu ki te tautoko o waho hei whakapiki ake i te māramatanga o ngā kaimahi ki te whakahonohono i te whakamaheretanga, te whakatinanatanga hoki o te hōtaka, me te aromatawai, te aromātai hōtaka hoki
  • mahi ngātahi ki te whakawhanake i tētahi hōtaka akoranga e poipoi ana i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo reanga me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki katoa.

Me whakatutuki te whānau i ā rātou kawenga whakahaere mā te:

  • whakahou ake i ngā kaupapa here katoa hei whakatutuki i ngā herenga ā-ture, inarā i aua herenga ā-ture e whai pānga ana ki te haumaru ā-tinana, ā-waiora hoki o ngā tamariki
  • āta whakarite i te tuhi haere o ngā akoako hōneatanga mō te ahi me te rū whenua
  • whakatinana i ngā mahi ki te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi, me te āta whakarite i tētahi hōtaka tautoko ngaio, hei whakatutuki i ā rātou ake matea whakawhanaketanga.

Ētahi atu take matua

E arotahi ana te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā tikanga ki te whiwhi raihana anō mō tō rātou whare hou. E ārahi ana, e tautoko ana hoki te tari ā-rohe o te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i te whānau.

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kia mahi te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te taha o te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te:

  • whakapakari i te āheinga o ngā kaimahi ki te whakapai ake i te kounga o ngā akoranga mā ngā tamariki
  • whakawhanake me te whakatinana i tētahi rautaki ka whakapakari ake i te whakamahinga o te reo Māori ki te kōhanga reo
  • whakapiki ake i te āheinga o te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ā rātou kawenga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o ia rā, me te whakamahere rautaki, te whakamahere ā-tau hoki.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka whakahaere anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te aromātai whai muri o Te Kōhanga Reo o Kautu ki te Rangii roto i te kotahi tau.

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary evaluation is undertaken at the discretion of the Chief Review Officer of the Education Review Office (ERO).

ERO evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO evaluation and/or any additional areas identified since that evaluation.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary evaluation is based on an evaluation of the performance of the kōhanga reo governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the previous ERO report or issues identified since that evaluation. The terms of reference for this evaluation area to investigate:

  • Mana Reo – strengthening children’s language development
  • Mana Tangata – programmes to support social behaviours
  • Planning and Evaluation – quality of learning practices, whānau understanding about roles and responsibilities, policy review, and strategic planning
  • Matters of non-compliance – record regular fire and earthquake drills
  • and any issues identified since the previous review.

2 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kautu ki te Rangi is based on the grounds of Tauporohenui Marae in Hāwera.

Since the previous ERO report in 2015, the whānau has focussed on the relocation to new premises at the end of this year. A new kaimahi was appointed at the beginning of 2017.

3 Findings

Children hear te reo Māori in the daily programme. Kaimahi have activities for children to build their language. Waiata, introducing words, reciting names for colours and numbers support children to use te reo Māori. However, children talk to each other in English and only use some words unsupported.

Children are developing skills to interact socially with others. Kaimahi encourage children to share, care and work co-operatively as tuākana tēina. Routines provide children with expectations when they learn alongside peers. They talk and play with each other. Children are considerate of others.

Key Next Steps

Children’s learning experiences need further improvement. Although kaimahi are aware of children’s differences and individual needs, the learning programme does not consider these.

Kaimahi need to:

  • develop, plan and implement a programme to strengthen children’s use of te reo Māori

  • seek external support to increase kaimahi understanding about how to link programme planning, implementation, assessment and programme evaluation

  • work collectively to develop a learning programme that caters for the range and interests of all children.

The whānau must attend to their management responsibilities by:

  • updating all policies to meet legislation requirements, particularly those related to children’s physical and emotional safety

  • ensuring regular fire and earthquake drills are recorded

  • implementing appraisal for kaimahi and ensuring there is a programme of support to meet their specific developmental needs.

Other significant issues

The whānau is focussed on meeting relicensing requirements for their new premises. The local Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) are providing guidance and support to the whānau.

4 Recommendations

ERO recommends that the TKRNT work alongside whānau and kaimahi to:

  • build the capability of kaimahi to improve the quality of learning for children
  • develop and implement a strategy to strengthen the use of te reo Māori in the kōhanga reo
  • increase whānau capability to fulfil their responsibilities with the daily operations, and strategic and annual planning.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Kautu ki te Rangi will be within one year.
