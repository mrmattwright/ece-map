1 Te Horopaki

E pā tata tonu ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Atawhai ki te moana o Taupō, ā, e tū ana ki te pūtake o te maunga o Tauhara. He tata hoki ki te kura tuatahi me te kura tuarua o Tauhara, me te kura Takawaenga o Taupō. E pā tata tonu ana hoki Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Whakarewa i te Reo ki Tūwharetoa. He kaha ngā hononga o te whānau me ngā tamariki ki ngā hapū me te iwi o Ngāti Tūwharetoa. He maha ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i te mōhiotanga, te māramatanga hoki ki tō rātou tūnga, tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga anō hoki ki te kōhanga reo me te rohe whānui o Ngāti Tūwharetoa.

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e mau nei i te reo Māori hei reo kōrerorero i ngā wā katoa. Ka tino whakamanahia, ka tino whakanuia hoki te whanaungatanga.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te hāpai i ngā matea ā-tinana, ā-tangata, ā-whatumanawa hoki o ngā tamariki?

E tino hāpaitia ana te whakawhanaketanga ā-tinana, ā-tangata, ā-whatumanawa hoki o ngā tamariki.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e manaaki ana, e poipoi ana hoki i a rātou. He pai tā rātou pāhekoheko ki te taha o ō rātou hoa me ngā kaimahi hoki. Ka kitea te atawhai me te whakaaro nui o ngā tuākana mō ngā tēina. Ka whakanuia ngā uaratanga me ngā mātāpono o te whanaungatanga. He tau te āhua o ngā tamariki, ā, ka whai oranga i ngā hononga me ngā taunekeneke e whai pūtake ana, e manaaki ana, e hāpai ana hoki i a rātou.

Kei te pakari haere te māia me te āheinga o ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori. Ka taki rātou i ngā karakia, ngā mihimihi, me ngā pepeha i ia rā. Rongo ai ngā tamariki i te reo Māori e kōrerohia ana i ngā wā katoa. Ka ākina ngā tamariki kia whakamahi i te nui o te reo e āhei ana, puta noa i ā rātou taumahi katoa. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero iti ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ngā pākeke anō hoki. E mōhio ana rātou, ko te reo Māori te reo o te kōhanga reo.

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ngā tikanga Māori me ngā uaratanga a te whānau mō ngā akoranga. Ka tino kitea tēnei āhuatanga i ngā wā kai, i ngā wā e whakawhitiwhiti taumahi ana, me ngā wā e taunekeneke ana rātou ki waenga i a rātou anō. Ka taki karakia rātou i ngā wā kai. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te kawe i ngā tūranga me ā rātou kawenga mahi e tohu nei i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki te manaaki i tō rātou taiao me tō rātou whānau.

Ka whakaongaongatia ngā tamariki hei ākonga, mā te tūhura haere me te whakatau anō hoki i ā rātou ake whiringa. He maha ā rātou whai wāhitanga ki te tūhura i ngā āhuatanga e ngākau nuitia ana e rātou. E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi, ā, ka āta whakamahi rātou i ngā wāhi katoa e wātea ana ki a rātou ki te kōhanga reo. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā kaupapa ā-tinana, pāhekoheko anō hoki e tū ana i ia te wā. Ka ngākau nui rātou ki te kōrero mō ā rātou mahi. Kei te tino mōhio ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao, ā, ka kitea tō rātou tino māia, tō rātou tākare hoki. Kei ngā tamariki i raro i te rua o ngā tau tō rātou ake wāhi tākaro. He pai te whakarato mō ngā matea ake me ngā hiahia ake o ngā rōpū tamariki. He pakari, he tākare hoki rātou ki te tūhura haere me te taunekeneke ki te ao e noho nei rātou. Ka āta manaakitia ngā tamariki, ā, he ākonga harikoa e whakaongaongatia ana.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore ngā tamariki i raro i te rua o ngā tau e whiwhi ana i ngā tino taunekeneke pai ki ētahi atu, tae atu ki ngā kaimahi. I ētahi wā, kāhore ngā kaimahi e kapo atu i ngā whai wāhitanga ki te hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo, te whakawhanaketanga ā-tangata hoki o ngā tamariki tino nohinohi.

E mōhio ana te whānau, e tika ana kia maha ake ngā mahi hei kōkiri i ngā mōhiotanga me ngā āheinga o ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He tau ngā pūnaha me ngā tukanga a te whānau ki te ārahi i te pai o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ko ētahi o ēnei, ko te tūtohinga e hāngai ana ki te rā, ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga, ko ngā mahere wā roa, wā poto hoki, ko te arotake whaiaro, me te aroturuki anō hoki i te whakaratonga o ngā akoranga me te manaaki mō ngā tamariki.

He muramura te taiao o te kōhanga reo, he pai te whakaritenga, ā, he tau hoki. Kei te āta whakarite ngā pūnaha hauora, haumaru hoki i te mahi me te tākaro a ngā tamariki, ngā kaimahi, me te whānau ki ngā wāhi haumaru, hāneanea hoki. Ko tētahi whāinga wā roa o te whānau, ko te whakatū i tētahi whare hou ka hangaia ai mō te kōhanga reo.

Ka whakakotahi ai te whanaungatanga i te whānau. Ka koha te whānau i tō rātou tautoko, ā rātou ratonga hoki ki ngā tūmomo wāhanga o te kōhanga reo. Ko te mahere ki te hanga i ngā whare hou e whakahihiri ana i te whānau ki te āta whakahaere pai i ngā whakaritenga.

He pai te whakapuaki whakaaro o te whānau mō te tautoko, te manaaki, me te whakaaro nui ka pā ki a rātou nā tā rātou whai wāhitanga ki te kōhanga reo. He aronga nui ki te whakapakari i te reo Māori, te hāpai i te whānau ki te noho mārama ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo me Te Whāriki, me te whakarato i te tino tautoko mō ngā kaimahi.

He pai te mahi ngātahi a ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere, te whakapuaki, me te arotake hoki i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka tāutu ngā mahere wā roa, wā poto hoki i ngā whāinga ako, ngā taumahi, me ngā rauemi. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā tauira hei ārahi i te aromātai me ngā whakaaro huritao ki ngā hui kaimahi i ia te wā. E tīmata ana ngā kaimahi ki te arotahi ake ki ngā taumata ako ka whāia tonuhia e ngā tamariki.

Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi e whai pūtake ana, ā, ka whakamātau, ka tūhura, ka whakatewhatewha hoki rātou. He pai te aroturuki i tā rātou ako me tā rātou ahunga whakamua. Kei ngā pūkete ngā tauira mahi me ngā whakaahua e mau ana i ngā kōrerorero, ā, e tohu ana i ngā taumahi ako me ngā wheako. Ka whakamahia tētahi tūtohi pūkete e ahu mai ana i Te Whāriki me Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira, hei aru i te ahunga whakamua a ia tamaiti. Ko ngā kitenga o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ka kapohia mā ngā matihiko.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore te whānau e whakamahi ana i ō rātou mōhiotanga, me ngā mōhiohio hoki e pā ana ki ia tamaiti, i a rātou e whakamahere ana i te hōtaka. Me whakapakari ake i te whakapuaki me te pūrongo o aua mōhiohio ki te whānau. E mōhio ana te whānau he kaupapa matua te whai hei whakakaha ake i tō rātou whai wāhitanga, tautoko hoki i te kōhanga reo.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Whakarāpopototanga

E pā tata atu ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Atawhai ki te moana o Taupō me te maunga o Tauhara. Kei te āta tautokona te whakawhanaketanga ā-tinana, ā-tangata, ā-whatumanawa hoki o ngā tamariki. Ka kitea te tino aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka ako rātou ki tētahi taiao e manaakitia ana. Ko tō rātou waiora me ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, kei te pūtake o ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau. Tērā ka whakahaerehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōhanga Reo o Atawhai i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Atawhai is near Lake Taupō, below Mount Tauhara. Close by are Tauhara School and College, and Taupō Intermediate. Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Whakarewa i te Reo ki Tūwharetoa is also nearby. Whānau and children have close connections to hapū and iwi of Ngāti Tūwharetoa. Children have many opportunities to develop an awareness and understanding of their place and sense of belonging to kōhanga reo, and to their local surrounds of Ngāti Tūwharetoa.

Children learn in an environment where te reo Māori is spoken at all times. Whanaungatanga is highly valued and promoted.

2 The Review Findings

How well are children’s physical, social and emotional needs supported?

Children are well supported in their physical, social and emotional development.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children learn in a caring and nurturing environment. They interact well with each other and with kaimahi. Tuakana show care and concern for teina. The values and principles of whānaungatanga are promoted. Children are settled, and experience meaningful, caring and supportive relationships and interactions.

Children use te reo Māori with growing confidence and ability. They recite karakia, mihimihi and pepeha daily. Children hear te reo spoken at all times. Children are encouraged to use as much te reo as possible in all their activities. Kaimahi prompt children to engage in brief conversations with each other and with adults. They know that te reo Māori is the language of the kōhanga reo.

Children are aware of tīkanga Māori and whānau values for learning. This is especially seen during kai times, in transitions between activities and during interactions between each other. At meal times they recite karakia. Children enjoy carrying out roles and responsibilities that show they know how to care for their environment, and whānau.

Children are stimulated as learners through exploration and making independent choices. They have many opportunities to explore the things that interest them. Children have ready access to a variety of resources, and make full use of the spaces available at kōhanga reo. Children enjoy the regular physical and interactive activities. They enjoy talking about what they do. Children know their surroundings well and show great confidence and enthusiasm. The under two-year-olds have their own play area. There is good provision for the specific needs and interests of groups of children. They are confident and keen to explore and interact with the world around them. Children are well-cared for, happy and stimulated as learners.

Key Next Steps

Children under two years old do not always receive adequate interactions with others, including kaimahi. At times, kaimahi underutilise opportunities to support language and social development of the very young children.

The whānau recognise that more can be done to increase children’s knowledge and ability to learn te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau have sound systems and processes in place to guide the effective management of the kōhanga reo. These include an up to date charter, policies and procedures, long and short term planning, self review and monitoring the provision of learning and care for children.

The kōhanga reo environment is bright, well-organised and tidy. Health and safety systems ensure that children, kaimahi and whānau work and play in safe and comfortable spaces. A long term goal for the whānau is to build a new all-purpose facility.

Whanaungatanga brings whānau together in positive ways. Whānau volunteer their support and services in different areas of the kōhanga reo. The plan to build new premises motivates whānau to operate in an efficient manner.

Whānau speak positively about the support, care and concern that they receive through their involvement in the kōhanga reo. There is a strong focus on strengthening te reo Māori, helping whānau understand the kaupapa of kōhanga reo and Te Whāriki, and providing good support for kaimahi.

Kaimahi work well together to plan, deliver and review the learning programme. Long and short term plans identify learning objectives, activities and resources. Kaimahi use templates to guide evaluation and reflection at regular staff meetings. Kaimahi are beginning to focus more specifically on children’s next learning steps.

Children engage in meaningful activities where they can experiment, explore and investigate. Their learning and progress is monitored well. Profile books contain work samples and annotated photographs of learning activities and experiences. A profile chart based on Te Whāriki and Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira is used to track each child’s progress. Observations of children’s learning are captured digitally.

Key Next Steps

Kaimahi do not use the knowledge and information that they have about each child to inform their programme planning. Sharing and reporting to whānau requires strengthening. Whānau acknowledge that increasing their involvement and support is a priority.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.
  • All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Atawhai is close to Lake Taupō and Mount Tauhara. Children are well supported in their physical, social and emotional development. Children show a strong sense of belonging to their kōhanga reo. They learn in a caring environment. Their wellbeing and interests are at the forefront of whānau management. ERO is likely to review the kōhanga reo in three years.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Atawhai will be in three years
