1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Kapanga ki Waiaua, ā, koia anake te kōhanga reo o tēnei takiwā. E mōhio ana te whānau, he maha ngā take hei tirohanga ake mā rātou, ā, e whai pānga ana ēnei ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. E ū ana te kaiako ki te whakarato i ngā tino painga mō ngā tamariki. E tautokona ana ia e tētahi matua e whai wāhi ana ki te āwhina i ia rā.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakahaere i ngā akoranga mā rātou anō e kōkiri?

E whai hua ana ngā tamariki ki te whakahaere i ngā akoranga mā rātou anō e kōkiri, ā, i ngā akoranga anō hoki e whai pūtake ana ki a rātou.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E wātea ana ngā tamariki ki te whai i ngā huarahi ako e whiriwhirihia ana e rātou. Ka whakawātea te kaiako i ngā rauemi katoa hei whakapā atu mā ngā tamariki. He roa ngā wā ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā kaupapa, nā te noho pūmau o ō rātou ngākau nuitanga. Ka whakarato te kaiako i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te mahi takitahi, ki te mahi takirōpū rānei. Kua āta whakaritea ngā mahinga. He ngāwari te whakawhitiwhiti haere o ngā tamariki i roto i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakatakoto te kaiako i te mauritau me te riterite hoki mō ngā tamariki. He tau ngā tamariki, ā, ka whai wāhi nui rātou ki te ako.

E ako ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e ākina ana tā rātou whakamahi i te reo Māori. Ka whai wāhi ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, me ngā mihimihi ki ngā wā ako ōkawa. Nā tēnei he whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo. Ahakoa he kaha ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Pākehā puta noa i te rā, e whāngai tonu ana te kaiako i ngā kupu kōrero o te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa. Kei te mārama ngā tamariki i te reo Māori, ā, he pai tā rātou whakahoki kōrero i ngā wā e tika ana. E āta whanake haere ana te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. 

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He pai te whakatakotoranga me te whai kiko hoki o ngā mahere hōtaka. Nō nā tata tonu nei te kaiako i whiwhi ai i ngā whakawhanaketanga ngaio e pā ana ki ngā mahi whakamahere. Ka tino kitea tēnei āhuatanga ki āna whakaritenga ki te taha o ngā tamariki. Kua tāutuhia te kiko o te hōtaka, ngā rautaki, me ngā rauemi e tika ana. He pai te whakamōhio i te whānau i tēnā e ākona ana e ā rātou tamariki. E whai wāhi nui ana ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga i āta whakamaheretia ai, ā, i āta whakaritea ai hoki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He maha ngā take hei whakaarotanga mō te whānau, hei whakatairanga ake i te kounga o te whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga i te kōhanga reo. Kāhore e āta kitea ana te tū riterite, ngā tuhinga rānei o ngā hui ā-whānau. He mea nui te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki ngā whakataunga me te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakatūngia tētahi kaiwhakahaere mō tēnei wā, ā, he tūranga hamanga tēnei. Me arotake i ngā pūnaha o te tari. He uaua te kite i ngā tuhinga matua. Ko te whakaputu me te tiaki i ngā tuhinga o mua tētahi kaupapa matua. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki mā te āta whakahaere, me te āta whakarite i ngā āhuatanga nei.

Me titiro ki te arotake whaiaro. He uaua te kite i ngā meneti o ngā hui ā-whānau. Te āhua nei, kāhore anō kia arotakengia ngā kaupapa here e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. Kāhore ngā kaupapa here matua e hāngai ana ki Te Ture Oranga Tamariki. Kāhore he rehita e whai pānga ana ki ngā kaupapa here matua o te hauora me te haumaru. E whakatakoto ana Te Ara Tūāpae i te angamahi mō te aronga rautaki. Mārakerake ana te kite atu, kāhore he mahere hei āta whakarite i te whakatutukitanga o ngā whāinga e whakatakotohia ana ki te mahere. Kāhore anō kia whakatutukihia ngā arohaehae e pā ana ki ngā kaiako. Ko ngā whai wāhitanga whakawhanaketanga ngaio i whakatutukihia ai e te kaiako, kāhore e hono pū ana ki ngā putanga arohaehae. E ako ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao kāhore e āta whakapaipai ake ana i ngā whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutukihia ana e ai ki te ture. Kia tutukihia e te whānau o te kōhanga reo āna herenga ā-ture, me tahuri rātou ki te:

  • āta whakarite i te whakatutukitanga o te arowhai, te tuhi haere, me te pūrongo i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea, hei āta whakatau i te noho haumaru o ngā tamariki
    [R 46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS 12 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • āta whakarite i te noho mōhio o ngā pākeke katoa e whakarato ana i te atawhai me te mātauranga mō ngā tamariki, ki ngā akoako hōneatanga e tika ana, ā, kia whakatutukihia aua akoako i ia te wā ki te taha o ngā tamariki, me tuhi hoki i aua whakaritenga
    [R 46 (1)(2) Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS8 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakatakoto i te kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e hāngai ana ki te tiaki i ngā tamariki e moe ana
    [R 45-46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; H29 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakarite me te tuhi haere hoki i te tukanga ki te aroturuki i te tīni kope
    [R 45 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; H25 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • āta whakarite ki te tuhi haere mā te huarahi e tika ana, i te katoa o ngā rongoā, ngā aituā, me ngā maiki
    [R 46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS25-29 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakahāngai i te katoa o ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga o te ārai tamariki ki Te Ture Oranga Tamariki 2014
    [R 46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS31-33 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • āta whakarite i te whai wāhi a te whānau ki te aromātai me te arotake o ngā whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga
    [R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA46 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakarato i te tautoko ngaio ki ngā kaimahi, hei āta whakarite i te whakatutukitanga o te kounga o ngā whakaakoranga i ngā matea o ngā tamariki katoa.
    [R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA7 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakaritea ngā tukanga ka aromātai i te hōtaka akoranga, ā, ka aroturuki hoki i te ako me te ahu whakamua o ngā tamariki katoa
  • tāutuhia ngā rautaki ka hāpai ake i te whai wāhi mai o te whānau ki ngā whakataunga, ā, i tā rātou whakahaeretanga anō hoki i ngā whakaritenga ki te kōhanga reo
  • whakaritea tētahi tukanga arotake whaiaro hei aroturuki i te kounga o ngā whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga
  • arotakengia te mahere rautaki
  • whakatutukihia te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi
  • whakarite i te whakatutukitanga o ngā herenga ā-ture katoa ki ngā wāhanga kāhore anō kia tutukihia e ai ki te ture 

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Kapanga i roto ngā tau e rua.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kapanga is the only kōhanga reo in Coromandel. The whānau acknowledge that there are a number of issues related to the operations of the kōhanga reo that require attention. The kaiako is committed to providing the best she can for the children. She is supported by a parent who helps on a daily basis.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children manage self- directed learning?

Children are successful at managing learning that is self-initiated and meaningful to them.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children have the freedom to follow learning pathways of their choice. The kaiako makes available all resources for children to access. Children are engaged for long periods of time because their interests are sustained. The kaiako provides opportunities for children to work independently or with others. Routines are well established. Children transition smoothly across the learning programme. The kaiako offers stability and consistency for the children. Children are secure, engaged learners.

Children learn in an environment where their use of te reo Māori is encouraged. The formal learning times include karakia, hīmene and mihimihi. This provides children with opportunities to learn about language. While children speak a lot of English during the day, the kaiako constantly offers te reo Māori words. Children understand te reo Māori and respond appropriately when required. Children are slowly developing as speakers of te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Programme planning is well presented and detailed. The kaiako has recently received professional development in planning. This is clearly reflected in her practice with the children. Programme content, strategies and resource requirements are identified. Whānau are well informed of what their children are learning. Children enjoy learning that is well planned and organised.

Key Next Steps

There are a number of issues that the whānau need to consider in order to lift the quality of the management and operations of the kōhanga reo. There is little evidence that whānau hui are consistent or recorded. Whānau participation in decision making and the administration of all kōhanga reo business is important. There is currently a part time kaiwhakahaere in place. The office systems should be reviewed. It is difficult to locate key documents. Filing and archiving are a priority. Children will benefit from systems that are well managed and organised.

Internal review requires attention. Whānau minutes are difficult to locate. It appears that the health and safety policies have not been reviewed. Critical policies do not align to the Vulnerable Children’s Act. There were no documented registers related to key health and safety policies. Te Ara Tūāpae provides the framework for the strategic direction. Clearly there is no plan in place to ensure the goals set in the plan are achieved. Kaiako appraisals have not been carried out. The professional development opportunities that the kaiako has participated in is not directly linked to appraisal outcomes. Children learn in an environment where improvements to operations and practices are lacking.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

ERO identified areas of non-compliance. In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements they must:

  • ensure that all hazard checking, recording and reporting requirements are carried out to ensure the safety of children
    [R46 Education (Early childhood Services) Reg.2008, HS 12 Licensing criteria for kōhanga reo affiliated with the Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • ensure that adults providing care and education for children are familiar with relevant emergency drills and regularly carry these out with children and supporting documentation
    [R46 Education (Early childhood Services) Regulations 2008, HS8 Licensing criteria for kōhanga reo affiliated with the Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • establish a policy and procedure to monitor children’s sleep
    [R45-46 Education (Early childhood Services) Regulations 2008 H29 Licensing criteria for kōhanga reo affiliated with the Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • establish and document a procedure to monitor nappy changing for young children
    [R45 Education (Early childhood Services) Regulations 2008, H25 Licensing criteria for kōhanga reo affiliated with the Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • ensure that all medication, accidents and incidences are recorded in an appropriate manner
    [R46 Education (Early childhood Services) Regulations 2008, HS25-29 Licensing criteria for kōhanga reo affiliated with the Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • align all child protection policies and procedures to the Vulnerable Children’s Act 2014
    [R46 Education (Early childhood Services) Regulations 2008, HS31-33 Licensing criteria for kōhanga reo affiliated with the Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • ensure whānau are involved in the evaluation and review of operations and practice
    [R47 Education (Early childhood Services) Regulations 2008 GMA46 Licensing criteria for kōhanga reo affiliated with the Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • whānau provide professional support for staff to ensure the quality of teaching meets the needs of all children.
    [R47 Education (Early childhood Services) Regulations 2008, GMA7 Licensing criteria for kōhanga reo affiliated with the Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendations

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • establish processes to evaluate the learning programme and monitoring the learning and progress of all children

  • identify strategies to raise the level of whānau participation in decision making and managing the operations of the kōhanga reo

  • establish an internal review process to monitor the quality of operations and practice

  • review the strategic plan

  • carry out appraisals of kaimahi

  • ensure that all areas of non-compliance are met.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Kapanga will be within two years.
