1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Pono ki te kaupapa Puna ko te reo.

Kei Waitara, Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Pekanga. Ka noho pūmau ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakatairanga i ngā tuku ihotanga, ngā tikanga, ngā mōhiotanga, me te reo Māori hoki o Te Āti Awa. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau nā aua whakaratonga.

E hiahiatia ana te whānau o te kōhanga reo, kia tū te Mana Aotūroa hei kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake, ā, i whakawhanakehia e rātou te pātai e whai ake nei, hei ārahi i te arotake, arā:

‘He pēhea te pakari o ngā tamariki ki te āta tūhura i te ao Māori?’

He pakari ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te ao Māori.

He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki, ā, e whakaongaongatia ana rātou, he pārekareka anō hoki ki a rātou te tūhura i tō rātou taiao. He pai ngā ratonga ki te hōtaka, kia āhei ai ngā tamariki ki te whiriwhiri me te whakahaere anō hoki i ā rātou ake akoranga. He pai ngā rauemi ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo i āta whakaritea ai, ā, nā tēnei, he māmā ki ngā tamariki te whakapā atu ki ngā rauemi e ngākau nuitia ana e rātou. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te ao e noho nei rātou mā te hangarau. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā taunekeneke harikoa e aro nui ana, e manaaki ana hoki i a rātou ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i te reo Māori mā ngā huarahi ngahau e whai pūtake ana. Ka ako ngā tamariki i te reo Māori mā ngā horopaki tūturu, tae atu hoki ki te marae. E mōhio ana rātou ki te whānuitanga o ngā waiata e mau nei i ngā kōrero tuku iho e pā ana ki te hapori. E ako ana ngā tamariki i ngā kōrero tuku iho me te ahurea o Taranaki.

He pai te whakahaere a te whānau i te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki. Kua āta whakawhanakehia te arotake whaiaro me te whakamahere rautaki a te whānau. Ka arotahi te whānau ki te tupu tonu me te whakapai tonu i ngā wā katoa, hei painga mō ngā tamariki me te kōhanga reo anō hoki hei ngā tau kei te heke mai. Kua tāutuhia e te whānau ētahi atu kaupapa matua hei whakawhanaketanga, nā runga i ngā putanga o te arotake o waho.

E mōhio ana te whānau, e tika ana kia whakawhanake tonu i te whakamahere hōtaka, ngā mahi aromatawai, te aromātai hōtaka, me ngā arohaehae, kia āhei ai rātou ki te whakatutuki i ā rātou kaupapa rautaki matua me ngā whāinga ā-tau.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake, ko:

  • Mana Aotūroa.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Aotūroa

He whakamārama

‘He pēhea te pakari o ngā tamariki ki te āta tūhura i te ao Māori?’

He pakari ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te ao Māori.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki, e whakaongaongatia ana rātou, ā, he pārekareka hoki ki a rātou te tūhura haere. He maha ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whiriwhiri me te whakahaere i ā rātou ake akoranga. He pai ngā rauemi me te whakaritenga o te taiao o te kōhanga reo. He whānui ngā tūmomo taputapu, rauemi hoki o te kounga pai. Ka kitea te aro nui o ngā tamariki ki aua rauemi. He pārekareka ki a rātou ngā wā akoranga ka pupū ake mō te katoa, me ērā hoki i whakamahereita ai. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao hihiri.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te tūhura i te ao e noho nei rātou mā te whakamahi i te hangarau. Kei te wātea ngā rorohiko ki ngā tamariki. He pārekareka ki a rātou te tūhura mā tēnei momo huarahi. Kei te waia haere rātou ki te ako mā te rorohiko.

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā taunekeneke e aro nui ana ki a rātou, ā, e manaaki ana hoki i a rātou ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakanui te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā mātāpono o te whanaungatanga. He hononga ā-whakapapa tō te tokomaha o te whānau. E pou herea ana aua hononga e te whakaute, te māramatanga, me te manaaki. He pārekareka ki ngā mema o te whānau te whai wāhi atu ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Ka aro nui ngā tamariki ki te reo o Taranaki. He whānui ngā whai wāhitanga mā rātou ki te kite, te rongo, te whakaatu, me te tūhura i tō rātou reo me tō rātou tino reo ā-iwi, i ōna tikanga hoki. He whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te rongo i te reo o Taranaki i ia te wā ki ngā horopaki tūturu, tae atu hoki ki te marae. E mōhio ana rātou ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo waiata e whai pānga ana ki te hītori o te hapori. E ako ana ngā tamariki i te motuhake, te mīharo anō hoki o ā rātou taonga tuku iho, ahurea hoki o Taranaki.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i te reo Māori mā ngā huarahi e ngahau ana, e whai pūtake ana hoki. He pārekareka ki a rātou te ako i tā rātou hītori me ngā kōrero tuku iho nā te kōrero paki. Ka tino whakatairangatia te reo Māori nā ngā waiata me ngā tākaro whakaari. Ka kitea te tupu haere o te ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori, ā, he pārekareka ki a rātou ngā huarahi e hāpai ana i a rātou ki te whakapuaki i ā rātou whakawhitinga kōrero, i ā rātou whakaari hoki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He pai te whakahaere a te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Kua tino whakawhanakehia ngā mahi arotake whaiaro a te whānau, tae atu ki te pūrongo me te whakatakoto tuhinga i ia te wā. Ka whakamahi te whānau i ngā mōhiohio ka puta i te arotake, hei āta whakatau i ngā whakaritenga ka whai hua mō ngā tamariki me te aronga whānui tonu o te kōhanga reo.

Ka arotahi rātou ki te tupu tonu me te whakapai tonu, hei whakatairanga i ngā putanga akoranga mō ngā tamariki me te waiora tonu o te whānau.

He pai te whakamahinga a te whānau i tētahi tūtohinga i āta whakawhanakehia ai hei ārahi i ā rātou mahi whakahaere mō ngā tamariki. Ko ngā matea o ngā tamariki me ngā wawata o te whānau kei te pūtake o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

E mōhio ana te whānau, e tika ana kia whakawhanake ake ngā āhuatanga mahi e whai ake nei, hei whakatairanga i ngā akoranga mā ngā tamariki:

  • te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai hōtaka
  • te whakatinanatanga o te arohaehae mā ngā kaimahi katoa.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaeretanga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora, me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, ā, e whakaae ana hoki te whānau, ki te whakatutuki i ngā āhuatanga katoa i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga hei whakawhanake ake, mō ngā tamariki.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Pono ki te kaupapa

Puna ko te reo.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Pekanga is in Waitara. There is a strong commitment by whānau members to promote Te Ati Awa heritage, customs, knowledge and te reo Māori. Children and their whānau benefit from these provisions.

The kōhanga reo whānau requested Mana Aoturoa as the focus for this review and they developed the following question to guide the review:

To what extent do children confidently explore te ao Māori environment?

Children confidently explore te ao Māori environment.

Children are actively engaged, stimulated and enjoy exploring their environment. There is good provision in the programme for children to choose and manage their own learning. The kōhanga reo environment is well resourced and organised so children can easily access resources of interest.Children are able to explore the world around them using technology. Children experience happy, responsive and caring interactions at kōhanga reo.

Children learn te reo Māori in fun and meaningful ways. Children learn te reo Māori in traditional contexts, including on the marae. They know a range of waiata that contain information about local history. Children are learning about their Taranaki heritage and culture.

The kōhanga reo is managed well by whānau for children. Whānau self review and strategic planning is well developed. Whānau focus on ongoing growth and improvement for children, and for the future of the kōhanga reo. The whānau have identified additional priorities for development as a result of this external review.

Whānau recognise that further development of programme planning, assessment practices, programme evaluation and appraisals is required to enable them to achieve their strategic priorities and annual objectives.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Aoturoa.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Mana Aoturoa

To what extent do children confidently explore te ao Māori?

Children confidently explore te ao Māori.

Areas of good performance

Children are actively engaged, stimulated and enjoy exploration. There are many opportunities for children to choose and manage their own learning. The kōhanga reo environment is well resourced and organised. There is an extensive range of good quality equipment and resources. Children show a strong interest in these resources. They enjoy the spontaneous and planned shared learning sessions provided. Children are happy and settled in their vibrant learning environment.

Children enjoy exploring the world around them through the use of technology. Children have access to computers. They enjoy this medium of exploration. They are becoming familiar with computers for learning.

Children experience responsive and caring interactions at kōhanga reo. The whānau and kaimahi promote the principles of whanaungatanga. Many whānau are connected to each other through whakapapa. These relationships are underpinned by respect, understanding and manaaki. Whānau members enjoy being a part of children’s learning.

Children are receptive to te reo o Taranaki. They have a range of opportunities to see, hear, perform and explore their language and its distinctive dialect and customs. Children have regular opportunities to hear te reo o Taranaki in traditional contexts, including the marae. They know a range of waiata that convey local history. Children are learning that their Taranaki heritage and culture is distinct and special.

Children learn te reo Māori in fun and meaningful ways. They enjoy learning about their history and heritage through story telling. Te reo Māori is promoted successfully through waiata and dramatic play. Children show a growing passion for te reo Māori and enjoy the expressive ways in which they can communicate and perform.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

The whānau manage the kōhanga reo in the interests of children. Whānau self review practices, including regular reporting and recording are well developed. The whānau use review information to make well informed decisions that benefit children, and the overall direction of the kōhanga reo. They are focused on ongoing growth and improvement to enhance learning outcomes for children, and for the overall wellbeing of the whānau.

A well developed whānau charter with policies and procedures is used effectively by the whānau to guide their management practice for children. Children’s needs and whānau aspirations are at the heart of whānau management

Areas for development

Whānau recognise that further development is required in the following areas of practice to enhance learning for children:

  • programme planning, assessment and programme evaluation
  • the implementation of appraisal for all kaimahi.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO LicensedKōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013, and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents, they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends and the whānau agree to address all areas for development identified in this ERO report in the interests of children.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
