Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kaahu ki Ngāruawāhia. Ka tino puāwai ngā tamariki hei ākonga ki tō rātou taiao reo Māori. E pou here ana te whanaungatanga i ngā wheako katoa e whiwhi ana ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. E whakapono ana te whānau ki te āheinga o ngā kaimahi ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kaahu i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

Ko Tainui te waka

Ko Taupiri te maunga

Ko Waikato te awa

Ko Tūrangawaewae te marae

Ko Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kaahu e tau nei.

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kaahu ki Ngāruawāhia. He tino hononga tō te kōhanga reo ki Te Kīngitanga, ā, ka whakanuia hoki tēnei āhuatanga ki tōna ingoa a Te Kaahu. He hononga ā-whakapapa tō ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki rātou ko ngā kaumātua. Ka whai wāhi nui hoki ngā kaumātua ki te whānau nei. Kua roa nei te kaiako e mahi ana ki tēnei kōhanga reo.

Ko te whanaungatanga tētahi āhuatanga nui ki te whānau, ā, ka ārahi hoki i te āhua o ā rātou mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. Ka whai whakaaro nui ki ngā painga mō ngā tamariki, mā te tirohanga a te whānau.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te whakapuakitanga o te whanaungatanga i roto i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki?

He māia ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i te whanaungatanga i roto i ā rātou akoranga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E puāwai ana ngā tamariki hei ākonga ki tō rātou taiao reo Māori. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā taumahi e whakamahia ana e ngā kaimahi ki te whakatairanga ake i tā rātou ako i te reo. Ka rongo ngā tamariki ki te reo kairangi e whakatauiratia e ngā kaumātua i roto i ā rātou taunekeneketanga. Ka hāpai ngā tuākana i ngā tēina i a rātou e tūhura ana i te reo.

Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā whakatau, me ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā waiata, ngā mihimihi, me ngā pepeha i ia rā. He pai te aro atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā wero i roto i te reo Māori.

He pakari ngā tamariki, ā, ka kawe rātou i tā rātou ake ako. He whakaahua, he tohu, he mahi toi hoki ki te taiao ako, ā, e mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki aua āhuatanga. He maha ngā rauemi e wātea ana ki ngā tamariki. He harikoa rātou ki te ako takirōpū, me te ako takitahi. He hāneanea, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka kitea te pakari haere o ngā tamariki ki te ārahi me te hāpai hoki i ētahi atu i roto i ā rātou akoranga. Ka whakanui ngā kaimahi i ngā hononga me ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i te tuakana me te teina i roto i ngā akoranga katoa. Ka kitea te manaaki, te aroha, me te manawa popore o ngā tamariki mō ō rātou hoa. Kei te whanake hoki tō rātou māramatanga ki ngā tūmomo huarahi ka whakatutuki rātou i ngā tikanga Māori. Ka whakaongaongatia ngā tamariki, kia whanake ai tō rātou āheinga ki te ārahi, ki te āta whai wāhi nui hoki ki te whānau.

Ko te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki, me tō rātou whai wāhi motuhake ki te kōhanga reo, ka whakapūmautia mā te whakapapa. Ko ngā wheako akoranga ka whakaratohia ki ngā tamariki e whakatairanga ana i ngā hononga ā-whānau. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ā rātou wā ako ki te taha o ngā kaumātua, tō rātou whānau, me ō rātou hoa. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i te aroha, te manaaki, me te tiaki, ā, e pou here nei aua ariā i ngā taunekeneke a te whānau.

Te Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E mōhio ana te whānau, kāhore te hōtaka mā ngā pēpi e āta hāpai rawa i a rātou. Ahakoa ka whai wāhi ngā pēpi ki te hōtaka a te whānau katoa, ki te taha o ō rātou tuākana i ia rā, he iti noa rawa te wāhi, te wā hoki mā rātou ki te tūhura haere mā ō rātou tairongo, i runga anō i te tikanga o tā rātou ake haere.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i tētahi hōtaka akoranga i āta whakamaheretia ai. Ka whakarato ngā mahere i ngā aratohu mārama mā ngā kaimahi, i a rātou e whakatinana ana i te hōtaka akoranga. Ko te pai o ngā putanga ako mā ngā tamariki ka āta whakaarohia.

He tino pai te kapo atu i te ako me te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. He whānui ngā tūmomo huarahi ka whakamahia e ngā kaimahi ki te whakatakoto haere i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka pūrongo rātou ki te whānau mō ngā wheako akoranga a ia tamaiti, me ā rātou ekenga angitu. Ka hāngai hoki ngā mōhiohio ki te whakawhanake haere i te tūmāia o ngā tamariki, i tō rātou hononga hoki ki tō rātou whānau.

Ko te whanaungatanga kei te pūtake o ngā whakahaeretanga ki te kōhanga reo. Kua whakamanahia ngā kaimahi e te whānau ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te nuinga o ngā wāhanga o te kōhanga reo. He māro te māramatanga tō ngā mema ki ō rātou tūranga, ā, ka āta kawe hoki i ēnei hei hāpai i ā rātou tamariki. Nā te pakari me te kaha tautoko o ngā hononga ā-whānau, ka whakawhirinaki tētahi i tētahi ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga papai mā ā rātou tamariki. Ko ngā painga mō ngā tamariki e āta whakaarohia ana e te whānau katoa.

Ka tino māramahia e te whānau ngā āhuatanga matua e pā ana ki te mātauranga me te atawhai o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo i roto i te wā kei te heke mai. Kua whai pānga nui te arotake whaiaro ki te noho marama a te whānau ki te aronga whakamua o te hōtaka akoranga me te rōnakitanga o te kōhanga reo mō meāke nei. Ka whakamahia Te Ara Tūāpae hei tāutu i ngā tūmomo wāhanga hei whakatupu ake anō. Ko ngā rautaki o te whakawhanaketanga e ahu mai ana i ngā mātāpono o te whanaungatanga me te whai wāhi nui. Ko te ara mātauranga o ngā tamariki ka āta whakaarohia, ka āta whakamaheretia.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga kia whakamahi te whānau i te arotake whaiaro hei whakapai ake i te hōtaka akoranga mā ngā pēpi.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E pou here ana te whanaungatanga i ngā wheako katoa e whiwhi ana ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. E whakapono ana te whānau ki te āheinga o ngā kaimahi ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kaahu is based in Ngāruawahia. Children thrive as learners in their te reo Māori environment. Whanaungatanga underpins all experiences children receive at kōhanga reo. Whānau trust kaimahi to operate the kōhanga in the best interest of their children.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kaahu will be in three years.

1 Context

Ko Tainui te waka Ko Taupiri te maunga Ko Waikato te awa Ko Tūrangawaewae te marae Ko Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kaahu e tau nei.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kaahu is in Ngāruawahia. The kōhanga reo has a very close association to Te Kīngitanga which is also acknowledged through their name, Te Kaahu. Kaiamahi has close whakapapa ties to tamariki and kaumātua. Kaumātua are valued members of the whānau. The kaiako has been in the kōhanga for a long period of time.

Whanaungatanga is important to the whānau and guides how they manage operations of the kōhanga reo. Children’s interests are considered through a whānau lens.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children express whanaungatanga in their learning?

Children confidently express whanaungatanga in their experiences and learning.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children thrive as learners in their te reo Māori environment. They enjoy the activities used by kaimahi to enhance their language learning skills. Children hear excellent models of te reo Māori through their interactions with kaumātua. Tuakana assist teina as they explore the language. Children participate in whakatau, daily karakia, hīmene, waiata, mihimihi, pepehā. Children respond well to challenges in te reo Māori.

Children are confident and take responsibility for their learning. The learning environment has photos, symbols and art that are familiar to children. Children have a number of resources available to them. They are happy learning together or as individuals. Children are comfortable and settled at kōhanga reo.

Children show increasing confidence leading and supporting others in their learning. Kaimahi promote tuakana-teina relationships and interactions. Children show care, concern and consideration for their peers. They are also developing an understanding about the different roles they play in tikanga Māori. Children are stimulated to develop capability as leaders and confident participants in the whānau.

Children’s belonging and special place at the kōhanga reo is reinforced through whakapapa. Learning experiences provided to children promote whānau connections. Children enjoy the learning moments they have with kaumātua, their whānau and peers. Children experience the aroha, manaaki and tiaki that underpin whānau interactions.

Key Next Steps

The whānau acknowledge that the programme for infants do not support them as well as it could. While infants are included in the programme with the entire whānau and their tuakana during the day, there is very little space and time for them to explore through their senses and at their own pace.

Planning and Evaluation

Children experience a well planned learning programme. Planning provides clear guidelines to kaimahi as they implement the learning programme. Successful learning outcomes for children are carefully considered.

Children’s learning and progress at kōhanga reo is captured well. Kaimahi record the children’s learning in a variety of ways. They report to whānau about each child’s learning experiences and their successes. Information includes how children are developing confidence and a sense of belonging to their whānau. Individual reports provided to whānau throughout the year provide an overview of children’s learning.

Whanaungatanga is fundamental to the operations of the kōhanga reo. Whānau have empowered kaimahi to make decisions pertaining to most areas of the kōhanga reo. Members have a firm understanding of their roles and carry these out well to support their children. Well established and supportive whānau relationships mean that members trust each other to make good decisions for their children. Children’s interests are considered by all of the whānau.

The future priorities for children’s education and care at kōhanga reo are well understood by whānau. Self-review has played a key role in understanding the future direction of the learning programme and sustainability of the kōhanga reo. Te Ara Tūāpae is used to identify potential areas for growth. Strategies for development are based on the principles of whanaungatanga and engagement. Children’s learning journeys are well considered and planned.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the recommendation that the whānau use self review to improve the learning programme for babies.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kaahu is based in Ngāruawahia. Children thrive as learners in their te reo Māori environment. Whanaungatanga underpins all experiences children receive at kōhanga reo. Whānau trust kaimahi to operate the kōhanga in the best interest of their children.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kaahu will be in three years.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori
