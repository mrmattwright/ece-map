1 Te Aromātai i te Ratonga

He pēhea te tūnga o Te Puna Akoranga o Ngati Whakaue ki te whakatairanga i te pai o ngā putanga ako mō ngā tamariki?

Kāhore i te pai te tūnga

Me whakawhanake ake

He pai te tūnga

He tino pai te tūnga

Me whakawhanake ake

I tēnei wā, kei te whakatutuki haere Te Puna Akoranga o Ngati Whakaue i ētahi whakarerekētanga.  He hou te kaiārahi ki tōna tūranga mahi, ā, kāhore anō kia tino mārama, kia tino whakatinana rānei i ngā pūnaha me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te puna.  Kei te rōpū kāwana ētahi take whakahaere hei whakatutuki. 

Ko ngā kitenga a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga e whai pānga ana ki tēnei whakataunga i whakarāpopotohia ai ki raro iho nei. 

He Whakamārama

“Ko te whakapakari i ngā rangatira o āpōpō, mō te ahunga whakamua tonu o Ngati Whakaue Iho Ake ā ngā rā kei te tū mai.”

Nō te tau 2009 Te Puna Akoranga o Ngati Whakaue i whakatūngia ai e te poari o Te Taumata o Ngati Whakaue Iho Ake, hei āhuatanga tonu o tā rātou rautaki mātauranga mō Ngati Whakaue. Ka whakarato te poari i te tautoko ā-kāwana, ā-whakahaere hoki ki te puna me ngā pouako. Koia nei te arotake mātauranga tuatahi o tēnei puna. He hononga ā-whakapapa tō ngā pouako, ngā tamariki, me te whānau katoa ki a Ngāti Whakaue me Te Arawa. 

Ko tā te puna whāinga, he whakarato i te hōhonutanga o tētahi taiao ako, ā, kia ako ngā tamariki i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga o Ngati Whakaue. Ki tō ngā pouako me te whānau whakaaro, ka noho matua te whanaungatanga me te manaakitanga ki te waiora me te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki. I tēnei wā, kei te rerekē haere ngā tūnga kaimahi me ngā whakaritenga. He hou hoki te kaiwhakahaere o ngā whakaritenga, ā, kei a ia te kawenga ki te whakapai ake i ngā pūnaha whakawhitiwhiti ki waenga i te puna, te whānau, me te poari. Ka noho pūmau ngā kaimahi o te pokapū, te poari, me te whānau, ki te āhuatanga motuhake o te puna, me te whakatutuki i ngā tino painga mō ngā tamariki. 

Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

Ka kitea te tūmāia o ngā tamariki me tō rātou tino tuakiri. Ka whakakoia tūturu nei ngā pouako i ngā whakatutukitanga angitu a ngā tamariki. He tino aronga ki te poipoi i ngā uaratanga me ngā tikanga e whai pānga ana ki a Ngāti Whakauetanga, i ngā wā ōkawa o te hōtaka. Ko te taki pepeha, te tautapa i ngā mōteatea, ngā karakia me ngā waiata hoki e whakamahi ana e ngā tamariki, e noho pūmau ana ki Te Arawa. Ka poipoi aua tikanga i te tino tuakiri o ngā tamariki, ā, ka hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o tō rātou reo Māori. 

Ka whai ngā tamariki i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga, ā, ka kōkiri hoki rātou i ā rātou ake akoranga, puta noa i te rā. He wāteatea te taiao ako o roto, ā, he pai hoki tōna whakaritenga, me āna rauemi.  Ka hāpai hoki tēnei wāhi i te manaaki me ngā matea mātauranga o ngā pēpi. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki tētahi taiao e whakawātea ana i a rātou ki te tākaro i ngā wā katoa, te whakamātau, me te ako ki ngā rōpū iti, rōpū nui rānei, me te kōkiri hoki i tō rātou ake akoranga. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki te ako. 

Kei te pai haere ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro. Ka arotake whaiaro i ia te wā, i te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa here, ngā mahi whakahaere pūtea, me ngā pūrongo a ngā kaiako. Kei te tīmata te pupū ake mai o ngā mahi arotake whaiaro e pā ana ki te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki.  Ka toro atu ki ngā kōrero urupare a te whānau, ā, ka tuhia hoki. E whakatairanga ake ana ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki nā te arotake nahanaha o te hōtaka akoranga i whakamaheretia ai. 

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kei te whakahaeretanga o te whare kōhungahunga ētahi āhuatanga hei whakapai ake. Kāhore he mahere rautaki, he aronga mārama rānei mō te wā kei te heke mai. He taimaha ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā kaiwhakahaere me ngā kaimahi o te whare kōhungahunga. Kāhore anō kia āta whakatakotohia ngā tūranga me ngā kawenga mahi, ā, kāhore hoki ēnei e tino māramahia ana. Me arotake te pūnaha arohaehae. Kāhore anō ngā kaimahi kia arohaehaengia, kāhore anō hoki tāutuhia ā rātou matea whakawhanaketanga ngaio. 

Kāhore i te rawaka ngā pūnaha e pā ana ki te arowhai i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea. I te wā o te arotake, he maha ngā āhuatanga mōrearea i tāutuhia ai, puta noa i te taiao ako. Kāhore te noho haumaru o ngā tamariki e whakatūturuhia ana. 

Me whakapakari ake i te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromātai, me te aromatawai. Kāhore e tino kite te kiko i roto i ngā mahere hōtaka, ā, kāore e hāngai ana rānei ki ngā aromatawai o ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Kāhore te aromātai o te hōtaka e hāngai ana ki te whai pānga o te hōtaka ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.  

Te Whakatau a te Whakahaeretanga ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te ratonga ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakatutuki e ai ki ngā ture

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ētahi wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture, mō te taiao o waho o ngā tamariki kei raro i te rua o ngā tau, me ngā arohaehae pouako. Hei whakatutuki i aua herenga ā-ture, me whakapai ake te ratonga i āna mahi i roto i ngā wāhanga e whai ake nei, arā, me:

  • whakarite i te whakatinanatanga o tētahi pūnaha arohaehae auau
    [R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA7] 
  • whakatutuki i ia te wā i ngā arowhai hauora, haumaru hoki, puta noa i te wāhi nei.
    [R 46 (1) Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008 HS12] 

Te Taunakitanga

I whakaae ngātahi Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te poari, kia whakatutukihia ngā wāhanga e whai ake nei, arā, ko te:

  • whakarite i tētahi mahere rautaki e whakatakoto ana i ngā kaupapa matua o te wā poto me te wā roa
  • arotake, te whakahou ake hoki i te pūnaha arohaehae
  • whakatutuki i tētahi tātari o te hauora me te haumaru
  • whakapai ake i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te whakamahere, te aromātai, me te aromatawai.  

Te arotake ka whai ake

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te ratonga?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Puna Akoranga o Ngati Whakaue i roto i ngā tau e rua.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

1 Evaluation of the Service

How well placed is Te Puna Akoranga o Ngati Whakaue to promote positive learning outcomes for all children in a Ngāti Whakaue context?

Not well placed

Requires further development

Well placed

Very well placed

Requires further development

Te Puna Akoranga o Ngāti Whakaue is currently going through changes. The puna leader is new to her role and has yet to fully understand and implement systems and processes related to managing the puna. The governing body has some management matters to deal with.

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

“Building future leaders for the future direction of Ngāti Whakaue Iho Ake”

Te Puna Akoranga o Ngāti Whakaue was established in 2009 by Te Taumata o Ngāti Whakaue Iho Ake Trust (Trust) as part of their education strategy for Ngāti Whakaue. The Trust provides governance and management support to the puna and pouako. This is the first education review of the puna. Pouako, children and whānau all have whakapapa connections to Ngāti Whakaue and Te Arawa.

The puna aims to provide a rich learning environment where children learn te reo Māori me ngā tikanga o Ngāti Whakaue. The pouako and whānau believe that whanaungatanga and manaakitanga are vital to children’s wellbeing and belonging. Staffing and organisational changes are in progress. The operations manager is also new and has responsibility for improving communication systems between the puna, whānau and the Trust. There is a strong commitment by centre personnel, the Trust and whānau to the special character of the puna and achieving positive outcomes for children.

The Review Findings

Children display confidence and a strong sense of identity. Pouako affirm children’s successes in genuine ways. There is a strong emphasis on nurturing values and tikanga associated with Ngāti Whakauetanga during the formal part of the programme. Children recite pepeha and chant mōteatea, karakia and waiata specific to Te Arawa. These practices nurture a strong sense of identity in children and support their te reo Māori development.

Children follow their own interests and engage in independent learning throughout the day. The indoor learning environment is spacious, well organised and resourced. The space for babies supports their care and education needs. Children enjoy an environment where they can play continuously, take risks and learn in small or large groups or independently. Children are happy, settled learners. 

Self-review processes are improving. There is ongoing self-review of the charter, policies, financial management practices and kaiako reporting. Self-review practices associated with the children’s learning programme are beginning to emerge. Whānau feedback sought and recorded. Children’s learning is enhanced as a result of planned and systematic review of the learning programme. 

Key Next Steps

There are aspects of the centre management that require improvement. There is no strategic plan or clear direction for the future. Relationships are strained among managers and centre personnel. Roles and responsibilities are not clear or well understood. The puna leader lacks vital support in her new management role. The appraisal system needs to be reviewed. Staff have not been appraised or had their professional development needs identified.

Hazard checking systems are inadequate. During the review there numerous hazards identified throughout the learning environment. Children’s physical safety is not assured.

Programme planning, evaluation and assessment need strengthening. Programme planning lacks detail and does not align to assessments of children’s needs and interests. Evaluation of the programme does not focus on the impact of the programme on children’s learning.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

During the review, ERO looked at the service’s systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

Actions for compliance

ERO identified areas of non-compliance relating to the under two year’s old outdoor environment and pouako appraisals. To meet requirements the service needs to improve its performance in the following areas:

  • ensure that a system of regular appraisal is implemented
    [R47 Education (ECS) Regulations 2008 GMA7]
  • regularly conduct health and safety checks throughout the environment.
    [R46 (1), (2)ECS, 2008/HS12) Regulations 2008 HS12]. 

Recommendations

ERO and the Trust agreed that the following areas need to be addressed:

  • establish a strategic plan with short and long term priorities
  • review and update the appraisal system
  • conduct a health and safety audit
  • improve planning, evaluation and assessment practices.

Next Review

When is ERO likely to review the service again?

The next ERO review of Te Puna Akoranga o Ngati Whakaue will be within two years.

3 General Information about Early Childhood Reviews

ERO’s Evaluation Framework

ERO’s overarching question for an early childhood education review is ‘How well placed is this service to promote positive learning outcomes for children?’ ERO focuses on the following factors as described in the bicultural framework Ngā Pou Here:

Pou Whakahaere – how the service determines its vision, philosophy and direction to ensure positive outcomes for children

Pou Ārahi – how leadership is enacted to enhance positive outcomes for children

Mātauranga – whose knowledge is valued and how the curriculum is designed to achieve positive outcomes for children

Tikanga whakaako – how approaches to teaching and learning respond to diversity and support positive outcomes for children.

Within these areas ERO considers the effectiveness of arotake – self review and of whanaungatanga – partnerships with parents and whānau. 

ERO evaluates how well placed a service is to sustain good practice and make ongoing improvements for the benefit of all children at the service.

A focus for the government is that all children, especially priority learners, have an opportunity to benefit from quality early childhood education. ERO will report on how well each service promotes positive outcomes for all children, with a focus on children who are Māori, Pacific, have diverse needs, and are up to the age of two.

For more information about the framework and Ngā Pou Here refer to ERO’s Approach to Review in Early Childhood Services.

ERO’s Overall Judgement and Next Review

The overall judgement that ERO makes and the timing of the next review will depend on how well placed a service is to promote positive learning outcomes for children. The categories are:

  • Very well placed – The next ERO review in four years
  • Well placed – The next ERO review in three years
  • Requires further development – The next ERO review within two years
  • Not well placed - The next ERO review in consultation with the Ministry of Education

ERO has developed criteria for each category. These are available on ERO’s website.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews are tailored to each service’s context and performance, within the overarching review framework. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for children and useful to the service.
