Background

Foxton Preschool and Nursery is a privately owned, all-day education and care centre. It is located at Foxton Beach and is licensed for 47 children including 18 up to two years of age. The children attending come from both the Foxton Beach settlement and the nearby town.

The centre is one of ten that are serviced by two visiting teachers. These teachers are responsible for monitoring the quality of curriculum provision and supporting teachers‚Äô ongoing professional learning and growth. A centre supervisor is responsible for the day-to-day operation of the service.

This is the first ERO review of the service.

The Review Findings

The centre curriculum, environment and resources provide children with a varied range of activities and learning experiences. Programme planning and assessment include reference to the strands and goals of Te WhƒÅriki.

Teachers support learners to follow their interests. They foster children‚Äôs language development, mathematical understanding and sustained attention to both child-initiated and adult directed learning goals. Young children welcome these opportunities to actively explore, talk about and engage in these experiences.

The centre has two distinct areas, one catering to children generally aged over two and another for those aged up two years. At the time of ERO‚Äôs visit the roll was not full and the ratio of adults to children was favourable in the up to two years old area.

Programme provision for these very young children focuses on nurturing their wellbeing through responsive care giving. Play is viewed as a vehicle for learning and opportunities are plentiful for these young learners to be active communicators and explorers.

Care routines were unhurried and are an enjoyable and integral part of children‚Äôs learning experience. As part of ongoing review and development the visiting teachers should ensure these outcomes continue as the roll increases, and as these children transition to the next learning area and programme.

Strategic planning, monitoring of progress and self review continue to evolve. Leaders and teachers have clear understandings of review for improvement.

A new appraisal process is being trialled. It is developmental and informs professional learning opportunities for staff. ERO‚Äôs evaluation confirmed that appraisal and advice and guidance programmes for beginning teachers require strengthening to improve outcomes for all children.

Key Next Steps

Centre leaders identify review of programmes of learning and the environment as next steps. ERO‚Äôs findings support review of the programme as appropriate and encourage the review be broadened to include:

  • establishing agreed teaching practice to underpin the centre philosophy and determine curriculum priorities and emphasis

ERO‚Äôs evaluation confirmed that building leadership capability of the visiting teachers and centre supervisor is necessary to strengthen the curriculum. Developing clearer expectations of and for best practice should help leaders and staff to explain and discuss their practice, improve self review and assist in building the service's evaluative capability.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Foxton Preschool and Nursery completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

The service provider delegates responsibility for management assurance to the visiting teachers and centre supervisor. ERO found that this aspect of management and leadership requires improvement. While there are policies in place and these are reviewed, there are not always procedures to guide and monitor leaders‚Äô and teachers‚Äô practice. As a priority, visiting teachers‚Äô should progress this work.
