Background

Windy Ridge Playcentre provides for up to 30 children and their families. The playcentre is cooperatively managed and families who are members implement the programmes for children. Centre practices are based on the playcentre philosophy of families learning and growing together. The centre offers three family sessions each week and a ‚ÄòBig Kids‚Äô session for older children. A paid co-ordinator is employed to lead this session.

Good quality practices identified in ERO‚Äôs 2010 report remain evident. In the past three years, a group of experienced and newer members have worked together to further improve the quality of teaching and learning, curriculum implementation and long-term planning.

The playcentre operates as part of the North Shore Playcentre Association, an umbrella organisation for twenty one playcentres situated in North Auckland. Many of these centres are semi-rural. The Association manages and distributes centres‚Äô funding and provides a training programme for parents/whƒÅnau to achieve Playcentre qualifications. It also has good systems to support centre members to manage the playcentres and to provide educational programmes for children. The association is currently reviewing many aspects of its operations to help reduce the administrative workload for its members.

This review was part of a cluster of twelve playcentre reviews in the North Shore Playcentre Association.

The Review Findings

Children are regularly involved in meaningful and sustained play. The play centre‚Äôs tone is calm and settled. Infants and toddlers are encouraged to explore and make decisions and have free access to a well-resourced environment. Older children have frequent opportunities to play cooperatively with each other and to express their thoughts and ideas. Respectful and reciprocal relationships between adults and children are a feature of the programme and provide a strong foundation for children‚Äôs learning.

Centre members use effective teaching approaches and skilfully work alongside children. Experienced members model high quality practices and support newer members to develop their understandings. The centre is effectively managed. Members work collaboratively to promote a high level of engagement and belief in the centre‚Äôs vision. They are regularly involved in playcentre training. The Association provides effective support to help Windy Ridge Playcentre remain very well placed to provide positive learning outcomes for children. Members particularly appreciate the support and guidance provided by the field officer.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Windy Ridge Playcentre completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)

  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.
