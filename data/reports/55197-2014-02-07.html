1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ko Titirangi te maunga

Ko Uawanui o Ruamatua te awa

Ko Horouta te waka

Ko Hauiti te marae

Ko Te Aitanga-a-Hauiti te iwi

Nō Te Aitanga-a-Hauiti te whānau o tēnei kōhanga reo. He tino tākare rātou mō te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo me tō rātou Hauititanga. He tokomaha ngā mema o te whānau kua whakauru atu, kua whakaoti rānei i ngā tūmomo akoranga o te reo Māori. Ko Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Whānau Whānui ki tētahi whare e tū motuhake ana, e pā tata atu ana ki te tāone o Uawa. He nui ngā whakapaitanga, ngā whakarerekētanga ki te whare o te kōhanga reo i roto i ngā tau kua pahure ake nei, kia pai ake ai tōna whakatau, tōna hanga anō hoki mō ngā tamariki.

I whakaritea e ngā kaimahi me te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ka tū manawa whakahī ngā tamariki i a rātou e taki ana i ō rātou pepeha, ō rātou whakapapa hoki. He manawa whakahī anō tō rātou mō ō rātou hononga ā-whānau, ā-hapū, ā-Hauiti, ā, ki ngā marae e whitu anō hoki o te takiwā. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te mōhiotanga ki ngā tūpuna o aua marae. Ko tēnei mōhiotanga e whakapakari ana i te aronga toi whenuatanga, te tuakiri hoki o ngā tamariki. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ngā tikanga o te marae. E whakawhanake ana tō rātou pakari ki te kōrero i te reo Māori mā te whakarongo ki ngā kaimahi e matatau ana ki te reo Māori. I nāianei, me whakawhanake ngā kaimahi i ngā huarahi ka akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti kōrero mā te reo Māori, puta noa i te rā. Ko te nuinga o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero whakahoahoa o ngā tamariki, kei roto i te reo Pākehā.

He tau ngā māramatanga o ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere i ngā kaupapa, te aromātai, me te aromatawai. Ka whai hua ngā mahere wā roa, wā poto hoki ki te ārahi i te whakapuakitanga o ngā kaimahi i ngā kaupapa ki ngā tamariki. He auau te aromātai hōtaka, ka whai pūtake, ka whai pānga anō hoki ki ngā tino whakapaitanga mā ngā tamariki. Kua whai wāhi ngā kaimahi ki te whānuitanga o ngā whakangungu e pā ana ki te aromatawai. E āhei ana rātou ki te tāutu i ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā āhuatanga kaha o ia tamaiti. I ētahi wā, kua tīmata ngā kaimahi ki te tāutu i ngā taumata ako ka whāia tonuhia e ngā tamariki.

Ka āta whakamahi te whānau i ō rātou pūkenga, ō rātou ngākaunui ki te hāpai i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kua tino whakaritea te kōhanga reo, ā, he pai anō hoki te whakahaeretanga. He mārama ngā tukanga hei hāpai i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka hono ngā kaupapa rautaki matua ki Te Ara Tūāpae, ā, nā tēnei, he aronga mārama tā te whānau mō te whakawhanake tonutanga o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei. Ka haere tonu te arotake whaiaro i ia te wā, ka whai pūtake, ā, nā tēnei ka puta mai ngā whakapaitanga ake ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, tō rātou whakawhanaketanga, me te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Whānau Whānui kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Whānau Whānui.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e ngā kaimahi me te whānau Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira hei kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira ngā whenu i tuituia ai puta noa i Te Whāriki, arā, te tuhinga e ārahi ana i ngā kaimahi me te whānau i a rātou e whakamahere ana, e whakatinana ana hoki i ngā hōtaka mā ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te waiora o ngā tamariki. Ka poipoia ngā tamariki mā ngā huarahi e manaaki ana, e hāpai ana hoki i a rātou. He aroha tō ngā kaimahi, ā, ka whakatauira i te katakata, te tākare, me te manawanui. Ka noho whakaute rātou ki ngā tamariki. Ka kitea hoki aua whanonga i roto i ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu. Ka āwhina, ka tautoko ngā tamariki tuākana i ō rātou tēina, ā, ka hāpai i a rātou ki te whakatutuki i ngā mahinga, ngā tikanga, me ngā tūmomo taumahi. Ka aro ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki ngā matea ā-whatumanawa, ā-tinana hoki o ngā pēpi, me ngā tamariki hoki hei whakatau. Whakanuia ai e rātou i ia te wā, hei whakamihi i ngā whakamātau me ngā mahi angitu a ngā tamariki. He tau ngā tamariki ki te ako.

Ngā pūkenga o ngā kaimahi me ā rātou mahi whakangungu. He pai te mahi ngātahi a te rōpū kaimahi. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā waiata, ngā haka, ngā whakapapa, me ngā pūkenga auaha, hei hāpai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te whai wāhi hihiri mai o ngā kaiāwhina tokorua, me tō rāua kaha ki te whai atu i ngā tākaro korikori, auaha hoki. He maha hoki ngā pūkenga ō te kaiako. Ko ētahi o ēnei, ko tōna mōhiotanga ki te reo Māori me te ao Māori, me tōna tino māramatanga ki Te Whāriki, mai i te tirohanga Māori.

Ka kitea te tino ngākau nui hoki o ngā kaimahi ki te ako ngaio hei hāpai i a rātou ki te whakatau i tētahi taiao mauritau o te reo Māori mō ngā tamariki. He matatau, he pakari hoki ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Tokotoru ngā kaiāwhina i tupu ake ai i roto i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, ā, kua whakaurua ki te hōtaka o te Tohu Whakapakari. Kua oti i te kaiako taua hōtaka, ā, kua uru ia ki te hōtaka whatutoto o Te Whāriki. Ka rūmakina ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao o te reo Māori.

Ngā atua. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te mōhiotanga ki ngā tūmomo whai pānga o ngā atua Māori. Whakanuia ai e ngā kaimahi ngā tūmomo atu i ngā kaupapa o te hōtaka. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ngā atua e pā ana ki te whakatō kai, te ngahere, te moana, te ahi, me ngā rū whenua. E tīmata ana ngā tamariki ki te kite i te whakahirahiratanga o ngā atua me ō rātou ao, me te whai pānga o ēnei ki te āhua o tō rātou nei noho.

I roto i ngā kaupapa o te hōtaka, ka whakanui hoki ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā tūpuna o te hapori, me tō rātou pūmanawatanga, tō rātou rangatiratanga hoki. Ka whakapuaki rātou ki ngā tamariki, ngā pūkenga me ō rātou mōhiotanga ki tō rātou Hauititanga, te whakatupu kai, me te tiaki i te whenua. He tino hononga tō ngā tamariki ki ō rātou tūpuna, ki ngā atua, ā, ki te ao kikokiko, te ao Māori anō hoki.

Te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki e whai hua ana ki te akiaki i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. Ko te waiata, ko te kōrero paki ētahi o ngā tino aronga angitu e whakamahia ana e ngā kaimahi hei whakatairanga i te whakamahinga, i te whakawhanaketanga hoki o ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. Ka taki, ka waiata hoki rātou i ngā karakia, ngā waiata, ngā hīmene, ngā whakapapa, ngā pepeha, me ngā mihi i ngā wā ako ōkawa. He whai wāhitanga hoki mō ngā tamariki e pakari ake ana ki te ārahi i ngā mahinga me ngā tikanga. He tokomaha ngā tamariki e āhei ana ki te whai i ngā tohutohu me ngā whakawhitinga kōrero. E whakawhanake ana te māia o ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori i roto i ētahi tūmomo horopaki.

Te ao whānui. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whakahura i ngā āhuatanga hou e pā ana ki te ao whānui. Haereere ai rātou i ia te wā ki ngā wāhi e whai pānga ana ki ngā kaupapa ako. I aua wā, ka taunekeneke hoki ngā tamariki ki ngā tāngata, ā, ka ako i ā rātou tūmomo mahi, ō rātou tūmomo ngākau nuitanga hoki. Ko ētahi o aua haerenga ko te marae, te taha moana, ngā kura o te hapori, me ngā whakataetae kapa haka o te rohe.

Te aronga toi whenuatanga. He tino puna mātauranga te marae, ā, i konei ka mātakitaki, ka whai wāhi atu hoki ngā tamariki ki ngā tikanga me ngā whakapono tuku iho e pā ana ki tō rātou Hauititanga. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ngā marae e whitu o te takiwā, me tūpuna o tēnā me tēnā. Haereere ai ngā tamariki ki ngā marae, ā, ka mātakitaki rātou i ngā kuia me ngā kaumātua e kawe ana i ngā tūmomo tikanga. Ka noho mōhio rātou ki ngā tikanga me ngā kawa e whai pānga ana ki te marae me te whakatau manuhiri. Ka tū manawa whakahī ngā tamariki i a rātou e taki ana i ō rātou whakapapa, ō rātou pepeha hoki.

Ngā whai wāhitanga ki te kōkiri i ā rātou ake akoranga. He angitu ngā kaimahi ki te whakapūmau i ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki nā te hōtaka i āta whakaritea ai, i āta whakatinanahia ai hoki. Ka whakarato rātou ki ngā tamariki ngā whai wāhitanga ki te mahi auaha, me te whakamahi i ngā tūmomo rauemi toi, rauemi hangarau hoki. He maha ngā whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i ō rātou pūkenga kori tinana, me te puāwaitanga o te pāngarau, te reo matatini hoki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whai i tō rātou e ngākaunui ana, te whakamātau haere, me te aro nui ki te tākaro mō te wā roa. He pakari ngā tamariki hei ākonga, ā, ka kōkiri rātou i ā rātou kaupapa ako.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. E ai ki ngā kaimahi me te whānau, kāhore ngā tamariki e tino whakamahi ana i te reo Māori i te nuinga o te wā, ki te whakawhiti kōrero ki waenga i a rātou anō. Me ako ngā kaimahi i te tupu mai o te reo kōrero o ngā tamariki nohinohi. Me whakarato aua mōhiohio i te aronga mārama ki ngā kaimahi mō ngā tūmomo rautaki, ngā tūmomo taputapu ka hāpai i te pūmautanga ake a ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai i ngā kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga hoki o te whānau ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga. Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere me te aromātai i te hōtaka, me te aromatawai i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te ngākau nui o te whānau. He mātātoa te whai wāhi a te whānau ki ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo me te hōtaka o ngā tamariki i ia rā. Ka hāpai tonu rātou i te kōhanga reo mā ngā huarahi papai. He tokomaha ngā mema o te whānau ka tae atu ki ngā kura reo, ngā huihuinga hoki o te purapura, ngā kaupapa i te hapori, me ngā huihuinga o te iwi. Ka whai wāhi te whānau ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga i a rātou ka tae atu ki ngā hui o ia marama ki te kōhanga reo. He tino pakari te aronga toi whenuatanga, te hononga hoki ki waenga i te whānau.

Ka tino ū te whānau ki te hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ā rātou tamariki. He tokomaha ngā mema o te whānau i whakauru ai ki ngā kaupapa, kua oti rānei i a rātou ētahi hōtaka, hei hāpai i a rātou ki te whakapai ake i ō rātou pūkenga reo Māori. He manawa whakahī tō te whānau mō tō rātou āheinga ki te kōrero i te reo Māori ki te kāinga, ki te kōhanga reo hoki.

Te whakamahere rautaki. He pakari te whānau ki te whakamahere mō ngā hiahia o ā rātou tamariki, mō te kōhanga reo hoki hei ngā tau kei te heke mai. Kua whakamahia e te whānau Te Ara Tūāpae hei ārahi i a rātou ki te tuhi i ā rātou ake kaupapa rautaki matua. Kei roto i te mahere, ko ngā aratohu ka whai hua ki te hāpai i te whānau ki te whakatutuki haere i ā rātou kaupapa rautaki matua i whakaritea ai. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā tētahi mahere e āta aro nui ana ki te whakawhanaketanga o te kōhanga reo me ā rātou akoranga hei ngā tau kei te tū mai.

Te arotake whaiaro. He pakari ngā whakaritenga ki te arotake whaiaro. Ka whai wāhi te katoa o te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te arotake whaiaro. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā pou e whā o Te Korowai hei ārahi i ngā whakaritenga. Kei roto i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero me ngā pūrongo ki te whānau i ia te wā, ko te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi, te hauora me te haumaru, te whakawhanaketanga o ngā rawa, te whakahaere i ngā pūtea, te whakahaere i te tari, te mahere rautaki, me ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o te hōtaka o ngā tamariki mō ia rā. He mārama ki te whānau ngā painga o te whakapai haere, ā, ka noho pūmau ki tēnei.

Ngā mahi whakahaere. Ka whakahaere, ka whakarite hoki te whānau i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mā ngā huarahi e tino whai hua ana. He pakari ā rātou tukanga ki te whakahaere i ngā pūtea, ngā kaimahi, te whakawhanaketanga o ngā rawa, te hauora me te haumaru, me te whakahaere i te tari. Ko te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa here, ngā tukanga, me ngā rehita, ka hāngai katoatia ki te rā. Ka ngākau nui te whānau ki te whakarato i tētahi kōhanga reo e āta whakahaerehia ana, ā, e hāpai ana i ngā matea o te katoa o te tamaiti.

Te whakamahere i ngā kaupapa. He pakari ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere i ngā kaupapa ako mā ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi rātou i tētahi tino tauira whakamahere e hāngai ana ki Te Whāriki. Ka hāngai ngā mahere wā roa, wā poto hoki ki ngā uaratanga me ngā tikanga Māori, ngā kōrero o te hapori, me ngā tūmomo tikanga e noho motuhake ana ki Te Aitanga-a-Hauiti. He tika te whakamahere a ngā kaimahi mō ngā tūmomo reanga. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā kaupapa e whakapūmau ana i tō rātou Hauititanga, me tō rātou mōhiotanga anō hoki ki te ao Māori.

Te aromātai i te hōtaka. Ka whai hua ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki nā te aromātai i ia te wā. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā ki te āta whakawhiti whakaaro, te kōrerorero hoki mō ngā take e whai pānga ana ki ngā tamariki i a rātou e ako ana, e tākaro ana hoki. Nā runga i aua whakawhitinga whakaaro, kua whakapai ake ngā kaimahi i te taiao, i te whakarato i ngā rauemi, me te ako i te reo Māori. Ka whai wheako ngā tamariki i ngā hōtaka e hāpai ana i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, ā, e whai pānga nui ana hoki ki ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o tēnā me tēnā.

Te aromatawai. He pai te māramatanga o ngā kaimahi ki te hua me te pūtake o te aromatawai. Kua whai wāhi rātou ki te whānuitanga o ngā mahi whakangungu e pā ana ki te aromatawai. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā tūmomo taputapu aromatawai i a rātou e aroturuki ana i te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki. Kua tāutuhia e ngā kaimahi ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā āhuatanga kaha o ia tamaiti. I ētahi wā, kua tīmata ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere mō ngā taumata akoranga ka whāia tonuhia e te tamaiti. He ātaahua te whakatakotoranga o ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki, ā, e wātea ana ēnei ki te whānau hei tirohanga, hei kōrerotanga hoki.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Whānau Whānui, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia tahuri te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te:

5.1 whakawhanake i ngā rautaki me ngā rauemi ka akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te whakanui ake i ngā wā e korero ana rātou i te reo Māori

5.2 whakamahi tonu i te arotake whaiaro hei whakapai i te whakahaeretanga o ngā whakaritenga i te kōhanga reo, me te kounga o ngā wheako akoranga a ngā tamariki.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

Kaimahi and whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus for the review. Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira are the curriculum strands woven throughout Te Whāriki, the document that guides kaimahi and whānau when they plan and implement programmes for children.

Areas of good performance

Children’s wellbeing. Children are nurtured in caring, supportive ways. Kaimahi are affectionate and model humour, enthusiasm, patience. They treat children respectfully. Children display similar behaviours in their interactions with others. The older children are helpful and supportive of their younger peers, and assist them with routines, protocols and different activities. Kaimahi and whānau are responsive to the emotional and physical needs of the babies and children who are unsettled. They regularly use praise to acknowledge children’s efforts and successes. Children are self assured learners.

Kaimahi skills and training. The kaimahi team work well together. Kaimahi use music, haka, whakapapa and creative skills to support children’s learning. Children benefit from the presence of two kaiawhina who are energetic and regularly engage in vigorous and creative play. The kaiako brings several skills to her role. These include her knowledge of te reo Māori and the Māori world and her strong understanding of Te Whāriki from a Māori perspective.

Kaimahi demonstrate a strong commitment towards professional learning that will help them to create a positive te reo Māori environment for children. Kaimahi are fluent, confident speakers. Three kaiawhina are graduates of the kōhanga reo movement and are currently enrolled in the Tohu Whakapakari programme. The kaiako has completed this programme and is enrolled in the advanced Te Whāriki programme. Children are immersed in a te reo Māori environment.

Ngā atua. Children are developing an awareness of the roles of Māori atua. Kaimahi regularly highlight the different atua during the kaupapa programme. Children are aware of the atua associated with cultivation, the bush, sea, fire and earthquakes. Children are beginning to recognise the importance of the atua and their realms and how they impact on their lives.

Kaimahi and whānau acknowledge local ancestors who were talented and able leaders in the kaupapa programme. They share with children the skills and their knowledge of Hauititanga, growing of food and caring for the land. Children have a strong sense of connection to their ancestors, ngā atua and the physical and natural worlds.

Children te reo Māori development. Kaimahi use a range of successful strategies to encourage children’s te reo Māori development. Waiata and storytelling are some highly successful approaches kaimahi use to enrich children’s te reo Māori use and development. They recite and sing karakia, waiata, hīmene, whakapapa, pepeha and mihi during formal learning times. The more able children have opportunities to lead routines and protocols. Several children are able to follow instructions and conversations. Children are developing the confidence to use te reo Māori in some contexts.

The wider world. Children enjoy making new discoveries about the wider world. They often go on outings that are linked to learning kaupapa. During this time children interact with people and learn about their occupations and interests. Recent outings include the marae, beach, local schools and the regional kapa haka competitions.

Sense of belonging. The marae is a valuable source of learning where children observe and participate in traditional practices and beliefs relating to Hauititanga. Children are familiar with the seven marae in the district and the ancestors associated with them. Children regularly go to marae where they are able to observe kuia and kaumatua in their traditional roles. They are made aware of tikanga and customs associated with the marae and hosting visitors. Children stand with pride when they recite their whakapapa and pepeha.

Independent learning opportunities. Kaimahi skilfully sustain the interests of children through the highly organised and carefully implemented programme. They provide children with opportunities to be creative and use different art resources and technologies. Children have numerous opportunities to develop their physical skills, early numeracy and literacy skills. Children enjoy following their interests, experimenting and playing uninterrupted for prolonged periods. Children are confident, independent learners.

Areas for development

Children’s use of te reo Māori. Kaimahi and whānau report their children do not often use te reo Māori in their interactions with each other. Kaimahi need to find out how young children’s oral language evolves. This information should provide kaimahi with clear direction about what strategies and tools would support children to use te reo Māori more consistently.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of the operations. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning, programme evaluation and the assessing of children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau commitment. Whānau take an active role in the kōhanga reo operations and the children’s daily programme. They continue to support the kōhanga reo in positive ways. Many whānau members attend kura reo, purapura meetings, local community events and iwi related functions. Whānau share in decision-making when they attend monthly meetings at the kōhanga reo. There is a strong sense of belonging and connectedness among whānau.

There is a clear commitment by whānau to support their children’s te reo Māori development. Several whānau members are enrolled in courses or have completed programmes to help improve their te reo Māori skills. Whānau speak proudly about their ability to speak te reo Māori at home and in the kōhanga reo.

Strategic planning. Whānau confidently plan for the future needs of their children and kōhanga reo. Whānau have used Te Ara Tūāpae to guide them in writing their own strategic priorities. The plan includes useful guidelines to support whānau in the process of addressing their planned strategic priorities. Children benefit from a plan that has a clear focus on the future development of the kōhanga reo and their learning.

Self review. Existing self review practices are rigorous. Whānau and kaimahi all contribute to self review. They use the four pou from Te Korowai to guide practice. Regular discussion and reporting to whānau includes kaimahi appraisals, health and safety, property development, financial management, office administration, the strategic plan and different aspects of the children’s daily programme. Whānau understand the benefits of, and are committed to improvement.

Management practices. Whānau manage and organise kōhanga reo operations in a highly effective way. They have robust procedures for the management of finances, staff, property development, health and safety, and office administration. The charter, policies with procedures and registers are up to date. Whānau are committed to providing a well managed kōhanga reo that supports children’s holistic needs.

Kaupapa planning. Kaimahi confidently plan learning kaupapa for children. They use a sound planning model which aligns to Te Whāriki. Long and short term plans reflect Māori values and customs and local stories and practices that are unique to Te Aitanga a Hauiti. Kaimahi plan appropriately for various age groups in their planning. Children experience kaupapa that reinforces their Hauititanga and knowledge about te ao Māori.

Programme evaluation. Regular evaluation benefits children’s learning. Kaimahi meet regularly to formally and informally discuss matters that impact on children as they learn and play. As a result of these discussions kaimahi have made improvements to the physical environment, resource provisions and the teaching of te reo Māori. Children experience programmes that support their interests and acknowledge their individual needs and interests.

Assessment. Kaimahi have a good understanding of the value and purpose of assessment. They have participated in extensive training about assessment. Kaimahi use various assessment tools when they monitor children’s progress. Kaimahi have identified each child’s interests and their strengths. In some instances, kaimahi have begun to plan the next learning steps for individuals. Children’s profile folders are attractively presented and available to whānau for viewing and comment.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Whānau Whānui completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends the whānau and kaimahi:

  • develop strategies and tools to encourage children to speak te reo Māori more consistently
  • continue to use self review to improve the management of the kōhanga reo operations and the quality of children’s learning experiences.

6 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

General Information About Reviews

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
