Background

Curious Keas Ltd is a new privately-owned childcare centre opened in April 2012, located in the small King Country town of Piopio. It provides full-day education and care for children from three months to school age. The children are predominantly from families who have strong links in some ways to the rural community. The centre facility is a converted house and its surrounding section is leased from the local council. While there are separate indoor and outdoor areas for under two‚Äôs, the programme is flexible and allows many opportunities for children of all ages to play together. The centre is licensed for 35 children, including 13 under two years of age. The roll has grown steadily since it opened and there are 57 children currently enrolled, of whom 11 identify as MƒÅori.

The centre owners provide the policy framework for personnel, financial and property management. One of the owners, who has early childhood qualifications, also fills the role as centre manager. She oversees daily operations, staff management and professional development. All current staff have an early childhood qualification and three are working towards full teacher registration.

The philosophy states the belief that children‚Äôs wellbeing and sense of belonging are very important and built within supportive relationships. It also indicates that children learn through fun-filled play.

This is the first ERO report for this centre.

The Review Findings

Children experience affirming and supportive relationships with each other and centre staff. Their social, emotional and physical needs are well met by responsive and caring staff. Familiar routines are centred around shared meal times and promote children‚Äôs social competence and confidence. Children are happy, settled and show trust in approaching adults for help or advice.

The extensive outside play environments include many natural features and allow children to engage in physical challenge and exploration. From time to time, the centre has young animals on site which children can interact with and care for. The inside play areas are well resourced. The flexible programme allows children to play independently with or alongside their peers for extended periods of time, and to make meaningful choices about activities to participate in. Children have opportunities to develop early literacy and mathematical skills, and to engage in creative and imaginative play. The local and parent community is used effectively as an additional programme resource for visits and excursions.

Infants and toddlers are well supported by primary caregivers, who take overall responsibility for each child‚Äôs care and education. These children play safely in their own areas. Many centre activities are naturally integrated and this promotes easy and relaxed transitions from the under two to the over two year old sections of the centre. Children and their parents are well informed through supportive relationships as they transition to the local primary schools.

MƒÅori children and their whƒÅnau are affirmed through the inclusive culture and supportive relationships throughout the centre. Karakia and waiata are included in daily routines as appropriate, and children have attended the local schools‚Äô kapahaka festival. Staff are aware of the need to increase their own knowledge and confidence in matters related to te reo and tikanga MƒÅori.

Teachers work as a highly collaborative team. They meet formally and informally to share information about children‚Äôs interests and strengths, and to plan activities. Teachers have made a positive start in presenting well-illustrated individual portfolios for allocated children which reflect each child‚Äôs participation in the programme. Next steps for the leadership is to include a stronger child and parent voice in assessment portfolios, and create a useful link between these portfolios and programme planning and evaluation.

The owner/centre manager provides considerable enthusiasm, energy, and extensive local networks for the service. She articulates a positive sense of direction and purpose for the centre and her staff. Parents who spoke to ERO expressed confidence in centre leadership and staff, and felt that communication with them was open and effective.

Key Next Steps

ERO and the owner/manager agree that the centre‚Äôs philosophy for learning and teaching is the priority area for review and development. This review process will include:

  • revisiting and clarifying the philosophy to develop in-depth understanding and shared ownership by staff and parents
  • the establishment of agreed expectations for staff about teaching and assessment practices, care routines and the consistent implementation of the centre‚Äôs policies and procedural guidelines
  • the development of the environment to ensure it supports the centre‚Äôs agreed approach to an emergent child-centred programme of learning
  • informed strategic decision making about staff and centre development, and resourcing.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Curious Keas Ltd completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum

  • premises and facilities

  • health and safety practices

  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)

  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)

  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)

  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

ERO‚Äôs Overall Judgement and Next Review

The overall judgement that ERO makes and the timing of the next review will depend on how well placed a service is to promote positive learning outcomes for children. The categories are:

Review Coverage

ERO reviews are tailored to each service‚Äôs context and performance, within the overarching review framework. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for children and useful to the service.
