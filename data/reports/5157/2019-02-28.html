Background

St Andrews Kindergarten located in Hamilton, is licensed for 40 children. The kindergarten offers both full and part day education and care to children from two years to school age. Children come from a diverse range of ethnic backgrounds and for many English is an additional language. At the time of this ERO review 53 children were enrolled, including 11 who identify as MƒÅori.

The kindergarten is one of 29 kindergartens that operate under the Waikato Kindergarten Association (WKA), which is a charitable trust. The strategic direction of the association is guided by the overarching statement, ‚ÄòOur children are strong in heart, head and hand‚Äô. The WKA has a commitment to providing quality, inclusive services that effectively meet the diverse educational needs of all children attending. Advice, guidance and support is provided to the organisation by a kaumƒÅtua from Tainui.

All kindergartens in the WKA are involved in a ‚ÄòCool for School‚Äô transition programme and sustainable education, environment and health programmes such as Enviroschools and Sport Waikato Under 5 Energise. The kindergartens‚Äô education, operational and administrative responsibilities are supported by experienced association personnel.

The kindergarten‚Äôs philosophy focuses on 'relationships that empower.' Priority is placed on the key components of:

  • uniqueness (culture, ethnicity and diversity)

  • community

  • environmental awareness

  • family/whƒÅnau

  • empowering children

  • learning and development

Since the last ERO review in 2015 staffing has generally remained stable. Significant improvements have been made to the outdoor learning environment. A new 'Te Ako' sustainable garden area was developed in consultation with children and parents. The kindergarten achieved Silver Enviroschools status and won the 'Keep New Zealand Beautiful' wall worthy competition. The kindergarten has responded extremely well to the areas identified in the previous report.

This review was part of a cluster of four kindergarten reviews in the WKA.

The Review Findings

Curriculum is highly responsive to children's individual learning and cultural needs. High-quality individualised assessment, planning and evaluation shows continuity of children's learning and progress towards meeting identified outcomes and clear next steps. Children have many valuable opportunities to manage themselves, solve problems, develop leadership skills and lead their own learning. The curriculum is enriched through activities, trips within the local community and visitors to the centre.

Culture and identities of MƒÅori and other diverse groups are shared and celebrated through special events and respectful practices. Whakapapa is valued and whƒÅnau are encouraged to contribute their knowledge and expertise. A positive relationship with kaumƒÅtua supports respectful tikanga practices. MƒÅori and other children develop a strong sense of belonging and their learning potential is enhanced.

Teachers use effective strategies to promote high levels of engagement. Warm, respectful and affirming relationships exist among children and teachers. Teachers capably inquire and reflect on their own practice to enhance teaching and learning. Literacy and mathematics are skilfully integrated into meaningful contexts, and there is a strong focus on extending oral language. Children are well supported to develop social skills and competencies, and tuakana-teina relationships are fostered. A key teacher approach supports effective transitions into the kindergarten for children and their families. Children with additional learning needs are well catered for through ongoing individualised planning, parent consultation and external expertise. Children benefit from an attractive and well-resourced environment that promotes valued learning outcomes.

Effective leadership is focused on high-quality, sustainable kindergarten practices. Leaders build teacher and leadership capability through shared planning, coaching, mentoring and the appraisal process. An experienced head teacher enables a positive team culture with high levels of respect, collaboration and relational trust. A strategic approach to gathering parent and whƒÅnau views and aspirations enables positive partnerships for children's learning. Parents feel welcomed, supported and their contributions valued. High-quality internal evaluation informs changes to teaching practices and improved outcomes for children.

The education services manager (ESM) works closely with the head teacher and provides well-informed professional leadership to support kindergarten operation. The WKA has recently reviewed its teacher appraisal process. Ensuring this new process is fully implemented is a priority to ESMs.

The WKA provides comprehensive systems, policies and procedures to guide kindergarten practice. Teachers have access to ongoing and targeted professional development to promote improved practice and contribute to positive outcomes for children. Effective governance by the WKA is contributing to inclusive and equitable services for children.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of St Andrews Kindergarten completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)

  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)

  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)

  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.
