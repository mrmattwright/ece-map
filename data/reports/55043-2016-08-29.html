Background

The Taikura Rudolf Steiner Kindergarten is one of three kindergartens in Hawke‚Äôs Bay, affiliated to Taikura Rudolf Steiner School, Hastings. The Rudolf Steiner philosophy underpins the programme and the learning environment.

The kindergarten caters for 40 children from the age of three years. Children going on to the Taikura Rudolf Steiner School, transition in their seventh year.

Since the April 2013 ERO report, three new teachers have been employed at the kindergarten. A new head teacher was appointed in January 2016.

The previous ERO report identified that internal evaluation and appraisal needed development. The new teaching team has made some progress in addressing these areas.

The Review Findings

The Rudolf Steiner philosophy is highly evident in practice. Children show independence and care and concern for one another. Well-established rhythms and routines support children's sense of belonging. An accessible range of open-ended resources fosters their curiosity and imagination.

Teachers model respectful relationships and positive skills. They know children well and respond effectively to their individual learning needs. Staff successfully use domestic routines as learning opportunities.¬†

Teachers notice children's emerging interests. They adapt the environment and provide a range of natural resources to support children's self-directed play. Teachers should continue to strengthen assessment practices, particularly to promote consistency across the new teaching team.

There is a deliberate and continuing focus across the Rudolf Steiner Kindergarten group to strengthen the bicultural curriculum. Te reo me ngƒÅ tikanga MƒÅori are woven throughout the daily routines. Teachers should continue to grow their knowledge of te ao MƒÅori and further develop their confidence in spontaneously using te reo MƒÅori.

A holistic approach to supporting children's wellbeing is highly evident. Parents value the Rudolf Steiner approach and support teachers in delivering a curriculum that reflects its philosophy.

There is a useful process to guide curriculum review that supports improving outcomes for children. Teachers should continue to develop and embed internal evaluation practices across the new teaching team.

An established appraisal process informs professional learning and development. The new kindergarten manager has identified a need to further develop appraisal to better support teachers' professional growth. ERO's evaluation affirms this direction. Particular attention should be given to strengthening the process for renewing teachers' Practising Certificates.

Policy documentation to guide kindergarten operations is in urgent need of review. In response to recent staff changes, teachers at Taikura should seek support from the Rudolf Steiner School, and its affiliated kindergartens, to support policy review and implementation.

Key Next Steps

The manager, teachers and ERO agree on the following key next steps for Taikura Rudolf Steiner Kindergarten. Staff should continue to:

  • strengthen assessment of children's learning
  • grow teachers' knowledge of te ao MƒÅori and spontaneous use of te reo MƒÅori
  • develop and embed internal evaluation practices
  • strengthen appraisal.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Taikura Rudolf Steiner Kindergarten completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist.¬† In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Actions for compliance

ERO identified an area of non-compliance relating to the governance, management and administration standard: general. To meet requirements the service needs to improve its performance in the following area:

  • ensure there is a process for reviewing and evaluating the service's operation (for example, learning and teaching practices, philosophy, policies and procedures) by the people involved in the service.
