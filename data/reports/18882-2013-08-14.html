2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ko Karepōnia te marae me te kōhanga reo

Ko Māmaru te waka

E noho tuawhenua ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Karepōnia, ki te marae o Karepōnia, e pā tata atu ana ki te tāone o Awanui, ki te Tai Tokerau. Nō te takiwā nei ngā tamariki o tēnei kōhanga reo. He ātaahua, he mahana hoki tēnei kōhanga reo atawhai, ā, he pai hoki te tiaki i te kōhanga reo.

Ka tautoko ngā kaumātua o tēnei takiwā i te whānau me ngā tamariki o tēnei kōhanga reo, i roto i ngā wāhanga whakahaere. Nō konei te kuia e tū ana hei kaiako, ā, neke atu i te rua tekau tau ia e whai wāhi ana ki tēnei kōhanga reo. Ko tā te whānau o te kōhanga reo tirohanga, ‘Kia puāwai i roto i te reo.’ E mea ana tēnei, ka tupu, ka puāwai, ka eke angitu hoki ā rātou tamariki mā te reo Māori. Nā tēnei, i whakaritea e te whānau ko Te Aroha o te Whānau te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

E whai hua ana ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Ka pūrongo ki te whānau i ia te wā, ā, he pai te whakatinanatanga o ngā pūnaha whakahaere. Ko ngā matea o ngā tamariki, me tā rātou mātauranga, kei te pūtake o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, te whakawhanaketanga o te whānau, ngā mahi whakamahere, me te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga.

I ngā wā katoa ka whai wāhi ngā mema o te whānau ki ngā kaupapa me ngā huihuinga ki te marae, ā, i reira, ka rongo, ka ako hoki ngā tamariki ki ngā tikanga me ngā kawa o Karepōnia. Ka hāpai ngā kaumātua i ngā kaimahi, ngā tamariki, me ngā mema o te whānau i roto i ngā wāhanga katoa o te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. E piki haere ana te pakari o ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori. E tino hāpaitia ana rātou ki te whakawhanake i tā rātou whai wāhitanga mātātoa ki tō rātou kōhanga reo, ki tō rātou hapū anō hoki.

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā whai wāhitanga ki te tūhura me te mahi auaha, puta noa i te hōtaka akoranga, ā, ka whakawerohia hoki. He pai te manaaki i ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka whai pūtake ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā pākeke me ngā tamariki, ā, he mauritau, he atawhai hoki. He kaha te aronga toi whenuatanga, te tuakiri hoki o ngā tamariki.

He tino tauira ngā kaimahi i roto i ngā akoranga. He pārekareka ki a rātou te mahi ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki, ā, he mātātoa tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki ngā tamariki i a rātou e mahi ana, e tākaro ana hoki. He hou ngā kaimahi ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki ki Te Kōhanga Reo o Karepōnia. I nāianei, me tahuri te whānau ki te tautoko i ngā kaimahi ki te whakapakari, te whakawhānui hoki i ō rātou mōhiotanga, ō rātou pūkenga hoki e pā ana ki te aromatawai me te aromātai hōtaka, kia pai ake ai te poipoi i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, hei tautoko hoki i te pūrongo tonutanga ki ngā mātua.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Karepōnia kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Karepōnia.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Te Aroha o te Whānau.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Te Aroha o te Whānau

He whakamārama

E ahu mai ana te tirohanga a te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Karepōnia i te whakapono ka tupu, ka puāwai, ka eke angitu hoki ā rātou tamariki mā te reo Māori.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te hītori.Ka ako ngā tamariki i te hītori e pā ana ki Te Kōhanga Reo o Karepōnia. He nui te kiko o ngā tuhinga hītori i te pūkete a tēnei kōhanga reo. Kei roto i te pūkete ko ngā kōrero e pā ana ki ngā tamariki tuatahi i uru ai ki te kōhanga reo, me ngā kaimahi, te whānau, ngā kaipupuri tohu mātauranga, me ngā whakaahua hoki, tae atu ki te tau 2012. E āhei ana ngā whānau hou kia kite i te nui o ngā mahi i whakatutukihia ai e ngā whānau o mua.

Ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi mā te whānau.He tino tauira ngā kaimahi i roto i ngā akoranga. Ka whakanui, ka whakamihi hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā whakamātau a ngā tamariki. He mātātoa tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki ngā tamariki i a rātou e mahi ana, e tākaro ana hoki. E tino hāpaitia ana ngā kaimahi e te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā tūmomo whakangungu ka whakapiki ake i ō rātou mōhiotanga ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, me ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te mahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki.

Ngā tamariki.Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā whai wāhitanga ki te tūhura, ki te mahi auaha, ā, ka whakawerohia hoki rātou, puta noa i te hōtaka o ia rā. He riterite ngā hanganga akoranga, me ngā whai wāhitanga anō hoki ki te kōkiri i ā rātou ake tākaro. Ka āta manaakitia ngā tamariki. E mōhio ana rātou ki ngā mahi hei tangata whenua. Ka kitea te mauritau, te atawhai, te manaaki hoki i roto i ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. He pakari te aronga toi whenuatanga me te tuakiri o ngā tamariki.

Te marae.I ngā wā katoa, ka whai wāhi atu te whānau ki ngā kaupapa me ngā huihuinga ki te marae, ā, ki reira ngā tamariki rongo ai, ako ai anō hoki i ngā tikanga me ngā kawa o Karepōnia. Ka whai pānga nui te whakapapa ki ngā taumahi katoa e whakaratohia ana mā ngā tamariki. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te whakahua i ngā ingoa me ngā āhuatanga matua e pā ana ki ngā kaupapa hītori, ngā tohu whenua matua, me ngā tāngata. E tino hāpaitia ana te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki kia mātātoa ai tā rātou whai wāhi ki tō rātou kōhanga reo, tō rātou hapū hoki.

Te reo Māori.Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. Kōrero Māori ai ngā kaimahi puta noa i te rā, hei whakamōhio, hei tohutohu, hei whakapuaki kōrero, hei whakawhitiwhiti kōrero hoki ki te taha o ngā tamariki. E pakari haere ana te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, ā, ka whakanui, ka whakamana hoki i ā rātou whakamātau. Ka tautoko ngā kaumātua i ngā kaimahi, ngā tamariki, me ngā mema o te whānau i roto i ngā āhuatanga katoa e pā ana ki te reo me ngā tikanga. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te hihiri, te whakaongaonga hoki o te taiao akoranga o te reo Māori.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te whakapakari i ngā tūranga o ngā kaimahi me ā rātou kawenga.He hou ngā kaimahi ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki ki Te Kōhanga Reo o Karepōnia. I nāianei, me hāpai te whānau i ngā kaimahi ki te whakapakari me te whakawhānui i ō rātou mōhiotanga, ō rātou pūkenga anō hoki ki te aromatawai me te aromātai i te hōtaka, kia pai ake ai te whakatutuki i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, hei hāpai anō hoki i te pūrongo tonutanga ki ngā mātua.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki me te whānau.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whānau whānui.E whai hua ana te whakahaeretanga a ngā mema o te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Pūrongo ai ki te whānau i ia te wā, ā, kua āta whakatinanahia hoki ngā pūnaha whakahaere. Ko ngā matea o ngā tamariki, me tā rātou mātauranga, kei te pūtake o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, te whakawhanaketanga o te whānau, ngā mahi whakamahere, me te whakataunga o ngā whakaritenga. Arotakengia ai ngā kaupapa here i ia te wā, puta noa i te tau. He pai te whakahaeretanga o ngā pūtea, he mārama hoki. Ka whai wāhi ngā mema o te whānau ki ngā mahi kohi pūtea, ā, ka tae ā-tinana atu hoki ki ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā. E arotahi ana te kōhanga reo ki te arotake, kia ū tonu ai te whakapai haere, te tupu haere anō hoki hei painga mō ngā tamariki, te whānau, me te hapori.

Ngā mahi whakamahere a ngā kaimahi.Ko ngā mahere o ia rā, me āna taumahi anō hoki, e hāngai ana ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. He ihupuku ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere me te whakatinana i ngā wheako ka kōkiri i ngā akoranga, ā, ka kapo atu hoki i ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Nā tēnei, i ētahi wā he rerekē te roanga o te taumahi a ngā tamariki.

Ngā rauemi.He pai ngā rauemi i te kōhanga reo hei hāpai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka whakatakotohia ngā rauemi kia wātea tonu ai ēnei ki ngā tamariki. Kua waihangatia ētahi o ngā tūtohi, ngā kāri, me ngā kēmu, ā, e hāngai pū ana ēnei ki ngā kaupapa. He aronga, he pūtake hoki ki aua rauemi. He waimārie ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo, nō te whai wāhitanga mai o ngā kaumātua tokorua, me te hōhonutanga o ō rāua puna kōrero tuku iho. Ka ngākau nui aua kaumātua ki te whakapuaki i ō rāua mōhiotanga ki a rātou i te kōhanga reo. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki ki te ako i ngā kōrero tuku iho.

Te taiao.Ka āta whakanuia te whanaungatanga. He kōwaiwai ki te taiapa, ā, nā ngā tamariki me te whānau i tā ai, hei tohu i te whakapapa o te rohe, mai i ngā rā o mua, tae noa mai ki tēnei rā. E ākina ana ngā tamariki ki te titiro ki te kōwaiwai, me te ako i ngā kōrero o roto. He pai te āta whakamahinga o ngā wāhanga katoa o tēnei whare, ahakoa tōna iti. Ka whai wāhi wātea hoki ngā tamariki inā ka hiahia.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te aromatawai.Kei ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki ētahi o ngā mōhiohio aromatawai. He hou tonu ngā kaimahi. Me whakangungu rātou hei whakapakari i ngā pūkenga e pā ana ki te aromatawai me te aromātai, kia pai ake ai te whakatutukitanga o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, me ngā pūrongo ki ngā mātua. Me tautoko, me ārahi hoki i ngā kaimahi i roto i ngā mahi aromatawai ka āwhina i a rātou ki te tāutu i te ahunga whakamua, i ngā whakatutukitanga hoki a ngā tamariki.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kareponia is a rural kōhanga reo located at Kareponia Marae, on the outskirts of Awanui in Northland. Children who attend this kōhanga reo come from the local area. The kōhanga reo is attractive, well maintained, warm and inviting.

Local kaumātua support the whānau and children of this kōhanga reo in all areas of operation. The kaiako is a local kuia who has been a part of this kōhanga reo for more than 20 years. The philosophy of the whānau of the kōhanga reo is, ‘Kia puāwai i roto i te reo.’ This means that their children will grow, blossom and succeed in te reo Māori. Consequently the whānau chose Te Aroha o te Whānau as the focus area for this review.

Whānau members effectively manage the kōhanga reo in the interests of children. Reporting to whānau is regular and management systems are implemented well. Children’s needs and education are at the heart of whānau management, whānau development, planning and decision making.

Whānau members are consistently involved in events and hui at the marae where children are exposed to and learn about tikanga and kawa of Kareponia. Kaumātua support kaimahi, children and whānau members in all aspects of te reo and tikanga Māori. Children use te reo Māori with increased confidence. They are well supported to develop as active participants within their kōhanga reo and hapū.

Children experience opportunities to explore, to be creative and to be challenged throughout the learning programme. Children are well cared for at their kōhanga reo. Interactions between adults and children are positive, warm and meaningful. Children have a strong sense of belonging and identity.

The kaimahi are good role models for learning. They enjoy working with their children and actively engage with children as they work and play. Kaimahi are new to their roles and responsibilities at Te Kōhanga Reo o Kareponia. The whānau should now support kaimahi to strengthen and extend their knowledge and skills in assessment and programme evaluation to better cater for children’s learning and to support ongoing reporting to parents.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

4 Findings

Te aroha o te whānau

Background

The philosophy of the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Kareponia is based on the premise that their children will grow, blossom and succeed in te reo Māori.

Areas of good performance

History.Children learn about the history of Te Kōhanga Reo o Kareponia. This kōhanga reo has a detailed folder of its history. The folder includes first enrolments, staff, whānau, tohu holders, and photographs up until 2012. New whānau will be able to see the amount of work that previous whānau have undertaken.

Kaimahi contribution to whānau. The kaimahi are good role models for learning. Kaimahi acknowledge and praise children’s efforts. They actively engage with children as they work and play. Kaimahi are well supported by the whānau to undertake training to increase their knowledge of the kaupapa of te kōhanga reo and working with children.

The children. Children experience opportunities to explore, to be creative and to be challenged throughout the daily programme. There is a balance of structured learning and opportunities for independent play. Children are well cared for. They know their roles as tangata whenua. Interactions between kaimahi and children are positive, warm and caring. Children have a strong sense of belonging and identity.

The marae. The whānau is consistently involved in events and hui at the marae where children are exposed to and learn about tikanga and kawa of Kareponia. Whakapapa plays a significant part in all activities provided for children. Children can confidently recall names and features of historical events, landmarks and people. Children are well supported to develop as active participants within their kōhanga reo and hapū.

Te reo Māori. The children are immersed in te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. Kaimahi use te reo Māori consistently throughout the day to inform, to instruct, to share and to have conversations with children. Children use te reo Māori with increased confidence. Kaimahi encourage children to speak te reo Māori and praise and affirm their efforts. Kaumātua support kaimahi, children and whānau members in all aspects of te reo and tikanga Māori. Children enjoy a te reo Māori learning environment that is vibrant and exciting.

Areas for development

Strengthening roles and responsibilities of kaimahi. Kaimahi are new to their roles and responsibilities at Te Kōhanga Reo o Kareponia. The whānau should now support kaimahi to strengthen and extend their knowledge and skills in assessing and evaluating the programme to better cater for children’s learning and to support ongoing reporting to parents.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau.

Areas of good performance

Whānau whānui.Whānau members manage the kōhanga reo in the interests of children effectively. Reporting to whānau is regular and management systems are implemented well. Children’s needs and education are at the heart of whānau management, whānau development, planning and decision making. Policies are regularly reviewed throughout the year. Finances are well managed and transparent. Whānau members are involved in fundraising and regularly attend whānau hui. The kōhanga reo is focused on reviewing for continual improvement and growth to benefit children, whānau and the community.

Kaimahi Planning.Daily planning and subsequentactivities are aligned to the kōhanga reo kaupapa. Kaimahi are conscientious about planning and implementing experiences that facilitate learning and hold children’s interests. As a result there are times when children’s activities vary in length from children stopping when they wish, to participating in activities that are short and sharp and suitable.

Resources.This kōhanga reo is well resourced to support children’s learning. Resources are stored yet accessible to children as required. Homemade charts and cards and games are appropriate for the kaupapa. They are focused and have purpose. The children at the kōhanga reo are fortunate to have two kaumātua at the kōhanga reo who hold rich history. These two kaumātua willingly share their knowledge with those at the kōhanga reo. Children benefit from live history lessons.

Environment.Whanaungatanga is strongly promoted. The mural on the fence, painted by children and whānau, shows the whakapapa of the area from the past up to the present day. Children are encouraged to look and learn from this mural. Very good use is made of all spaces in this small building. Children are given their own space if needed.

Areas for development

Assessment.Children’s files contain some assessment information. Kaimahi are new. They need training to strengthen assessment and evaluation skills to better cater for children’s learning and reporting to parents. Kaimahi require some support and guidance in assessment tasks that guide them to identify children’s progress and achievement.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
