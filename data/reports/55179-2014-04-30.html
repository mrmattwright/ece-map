1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana te kōhanga reo o Te Wānanga Whare Tāpere o Takitimu ki tētahi wāhi motuhake o te puna mātauranga, ā, kei tēnei wāhi te kura Kaupapa Māori o Te Wānanga Whare Tāpere o Takitimu me te Wānanga Whare Tāpere o Takitimu. E whakarato ana tēnei hapori i ngā wheako huhua mō te mātauranga kia noho rumaki ai ngā tamariki. Kua whakatūria te kōhanga reo kia anga ai ngā whare ki te maunga o Rongokako.

Ko te ahurea me te horopaki te tūāpapa o ngā hōtaka akoranga mō ngā tamariki. Ko te tautoko a ngā kaimahi me te whānau te take kei te kaha mōhio ngā tamariki ki ō rātou ake tuakiri, arā, ō rātou mana motuhake me ō rātou waiora. E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki i ngā wheako ahurea i roto i te rohe o Ngāti Kahungunu e āwhina nei i a rātou ki te whakawhanake i ō rātou māramatanga me ō rātou whakahīhī ki tō rātou Ngāti Kahungunutanga. Ko te tino whāinga o te whānau he whāngai i te reo me ngā tikanga o Ngāti Kahungunu.

Ko ngā akoranga me ngā paetae i waenganui i whakatipuranga, arā, ngā taitamariki, ngā taipakeke me ngā taikaumātua tētahi tino wāhanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka kaha kōrero te whānau mō te pai a ngā tātaki o te hunga whakahaere e tautoko nei i ngā whakatipuranga o ngā akoranga i te hapori. Ka tino whakaatu te kōhanga reo i tō rātou “whare rongonui”, ā, ka kitea ngā kaitātaki o mua, o ēnei rā me ngā rangi ā meāke nei i tae mai, kua puta rānei i te kōhanga reo.

E tino manawanui, ā, e tino whai wāhi ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga. E harikoa ana rātou ki te hōpara haere i te taiao o te kōhanga reo, ā, e whai wāhi ana rātou ki te whakapā atu i ngā rauemi kounga nui, ā, te nui me te whānui hoki o ngā rauemi. He mea ihiihi te taiao, he ataahua tōna hanga, ā, te maha hoki o ngā rauemi hei tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te ako tae noa ki ngā rauemi hei tiaki i a rātou. E harikoa ana ngā tamariki ki ngā wero ā-tinana i te taiao ō waho. Ko ngā tikanga me ngā tino uaratanga te tāhuhu o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

E tino whai painga ana ngā tamariki mā ngā taunekeneke manawanui, ngā taunekeneke whai koha me ngā whanaungatanga tiaki o tētahi ki tētahi. Ko te whanaungatanga te tūāpapa o ngā taunekeneke katoa. E whakarato ana te uepū whakahaere i tētahi anga whakahaere kaha e manaaki nei i ngā kaimahi, ngā tamariki me te whānau. He mea whakawhirinaki me te anga ki ngā hiahia o te whānau te āhua o ngā tātakitanga i te kōhanga reo. Ko te kaupapa here o te kuaha whakatuwhera te take kua piki te māia o ngā mātua o te whānau ki te whai wāhi i roto i ngā āhuatanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. E ngākaunui ana, ā, e rikarika ana te whānau mō te whai painga me te kounga o te mātauranga ka riro i ā rātou tamariki. Ko te aroha, te manaaki me te awhi te tāhuhu o te whakaako me te ako.

Kei te tino pai te mahi tahi o ngā kaimahi me ngā mātua o te whānau. Ka ngana rātou ki te whakapakari ake i a rātou anō mā te whai i ngā whakangungu, te tautoko a te purapura o te rohe me a rātou whai whakaaro i te wā mō te hōtaka akoranga me a rātou whakaritenga whakaako. E tino whakarato ana rātou ki te āta tautoko me te arataki mā te whakahaere i ngā rautaki huhua hei whakapakari ake i te māia o ngā tamariki ki te ako.

Kua tino whakaritea ngā wāhanga, ā, te pai hoki o ngā whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. Ka tuari te whānau i ō rātou haepapa hei whakahaere i ngā wāhanga rerekē o te kōhanga reo. Kua tino aroturukitia ngā whakaritenga, ā, he tino tōtika ngā whakaritenga o ia rā mō ngā take e pā ana ki te hauora, te haumaru me te kore paru. E tino arotahi ana te whānau ki te whakarato i tētahi taiao haumaru mā ngā tamariki ki te ako me te tākaro.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mokohuia o te Rautahi kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

He whakamārama

He pēhea rawa te pai o te whānau ki te poipoi me te tautoko i te akoranga whaiora o ā rātou tamariki?

Kei te tino poipoitia me te tautokotia te whānau i te akoranga whaiora o ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Mana Whenua.E tino whakaatu ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou tino mōhio mō ō rātou tuakiri me ō rātou piri pūmau ki te whānau. E tino piri ngā kaupapa akoranga ki te mana whenua, ā, mā tēnei e whakawhānui ake ana ngā māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou tūhonohono ki te whenua me tō rātou iwi. Ka tae atu ngā tamariki ki ngā hui huhua o Ngāti Kahungunu huri noa i te rohe, ā, ka whakapā atu rātou ki ngā wheako ahurea whai take. Ko ngā wāhi o ngā tūpuna i roto i ngā toi whakaataata me ngā toi peita huri noa i te kōhanga reo e tino tautoko ana i ō rātou mātauranga mō te tūrangawaewae. E mauritau ana ngā tamariki ki te kawe i ō rātou tūranga hei tangata whenua.

Mana Reo.E rumaki ana ngā tamariki i te reo Māori i te rā katoa. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori mā ngā tamariki me te whānau. E whakamahi ana rātou i ngā rautaki whai hua me ngā rauemi hei whakanui i ngā akoranga hei ako i te reo. E whakahaere ana ngā kaimahi i ngā whakawhitiwhiti kōrero whai tikanga me ngā whai wāhitanga i roto i ngā hui ahurea hei whakawhānui ake i te reo o ngā tamariki. Kei te tino tipu ngā tamariki hei kaikōrero o te reo Māori, ahakoa kātahi anō rātou ka akona te reo.

Mana Atua.E āhukahuka ana ngā kaimahi i te kaupapa nui hei tautoko i te hauora me te whaiora o ngā tamariki. Ka hāpai ngā tamariki i ngā tūranga tātaki, ā, e māia ana rātou ki te tiaki i ā rātou anō, te kai i te kai hauora me te whakapai i ā rātou anō kia paru kore ai rātou. E ako ana ngā tamariki i ngā painga o te kai hauora me te tikanga nui o te manaaki tangata. Kua tino tautoko rātou ki te kawe i ā rātou tūmahi. E whanake haere ana ngā tamariki hei ākonga māia, ā, hei ākonga takitahi anō hoki, arā, e āhei ana rātou ki te whai atu i te akoranga e hiahiatia ai e tēnā tamaiti me tēnā tamaiti.

Mana Tangata.E whai painga ana ngā tamariki mā ngā whanaungatanga whai koha o tētahi ki tētahi me ngā taunekeneke manawanui. .He mea whai koha me te ngāwari te āhua o ngā kaimahi, ā, he tino pono ō rātou manawanui ki ngā akoranga whaiora me ngā whanaketanga o ngā tamariki. Kei te tino pai ō rātou noho hei uepū, ā, ka tino tautoko tēnā i tēnā me tēnā i tēnā. Ka whakatauira rātou i ngā paerewa whanonga hei whakaputa mō ngā akoranga me ngā whanonga, ā, ko te aroha, te manaaki me te awhi te tūāpapa o ēnei paerewa whanonga. He tamariki tauawhi me te whai whakaaro te āhua o ngā tamariki pakeke. Ka taunekeneke ngātahi ai rātou ki ngā tamariki kōhungahunga, ā, i te wā ka tākaro anō hoki rātou.

Mana Aotūroa.Ka tākaro ngā tamariki i roto i tētahi taiao kua tino whakapaitia, ā, he mea kaupapa Māori te āhua o te whakahoahoa o ngā whare. He mea pōwhiri, he mea ataahua me te whānui te taiao o te kōhanga reo, ā, te nui hoki o ngā rauemi. He tino taurite te āhua o ngā wāhi ngū me ngā wāhi toritori. I te wāhi ō waho ka āhei ngā tamariki ki te tākaro pohewa me te whai haere i ngā tākaro hei wero me te whakapakari ake i te tinana. He mea ngāwari mā ngā tamariki ki te hoki ki ngā wāhi tākaro hei whai atu anō i ngā wheako harikoa kua whakapāngia kē e rātou. Kei te tino harikoa ngā tamariki i roto i tētahi taiao hīkaka e whakanui ai te pūmanawa mahira me te ngākau aroha ki te ako.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea rawa te pai o te whānau ki te whakamāherehere, te whakahaere me te arotake i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā a te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki?

E tautoko ana ngā māhere ā te whānau i ā rātou anō ki te tino whakahaere i te kōhanga reo me te whakarato i ngā wheako tino whai take kia angitū ai ā rātou tamariki.

Ngāwāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakamāherehere.E whakamahi ana ngā kaimahi i tētahi anga whai tikanga hei whakamāherehere i ngā hōtaka akoranga mā ngā tamariki. Mā tēnei tūāhua whakahaere, kua whakaratohia ngā ngohe kaupapa whānui hei whakawhanake i ngā māramatanga me te mātauranga o ngā tamariki mō te ao Māori, ā, ko te Kahungnutanga tētahi o ēnei kaupapa tino nui. E ārahi ana ngā whakamāherehere hōhonu i ngā kaimahi ki te whakatinana i tētahi hōtaka taurite e akiaki ana i ngā tamariki ki te tūhura me te noho harikoa i te wā e ako ana rātou.

Te hōtaka aromātai.E tino mārama ana ngā kaimahi ki ngā painga o te hōtaka aromātai hei whakapai ake i ngā putanga ako mō ngā tamariki. Ka whai whakaaro rātou, ka whakarerekē rātou, ā, ka tino aromātai rātou i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whiriwhiri whakaaro rātou mō ngā kaha me ngā matea o ia tamaiti, ā, ka hāngai ngā wheako ki ngā hiahia o ngā tamariki. He hūmārie te āhua o ngā kaimahi, ā, ka kaha tiaki rātou i ngā tamariki me te whakapakari ake i te māia o ia tamaiti ki te ako.

Te aromatawai.E whakamahi ana ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki aromatawai huhua hei tāutu kei te pēhea rawa te pai o ngā tamariki ki te ako me ngā paetae e whakatutukitia ana e tēnā me tēnā. Kua whakamahia ēnei mōhiohio hei tautoko i ā rātou ki te whakamāherehere me te tuku pūrongo ki ngā mātua. Ko te mahi ā meāke nei mā ngā kaimahi he tuari i ngā whakaritenga whai painga kia tōtika ai te kounga o ngā whakahaere mō ngā reanga katoa.

Te Ara Tūāpae.E whakamahi ana te whānau i te Ara Tūāpae hei whakatū i tētahi māhere whakatutuki. E ārahi ana tēnei māhere i a rātou ki te aroturuki me te whakarite pūrongo e ai ki ō rātou whāinga ā-tau. E tino arotahi ana te māhere ki ngā mahi ā meāke nei o te kōhanga reo me ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Te arotake whaiaro.Kua whakatūria e te kōhanga reo tētahi anga whakahaere whai tukanga kua whakamāhereheretia mō te arotake whaiaro. E tino mārama ana a tēnā me tēnā i ngā āhuatanga mō te arotake whaiaro, ā, ko te arotake whaiaro te tūāpapa e kaha tautoko ana i ngā tukanga hei whakatau i ngā take me ngā whakahaere o ia rā i te kōhanga reo. E whai painga ana ngā tamariki, ā, ko te whakahaere i tētahi arotake i ngā painga o ngā whare me te whenua te take e pēnei ai.

Ngā tūranga me ngā haepapa.Kei te tino pai rawa ngā whakahaere a te kōhanga i tētahi uepū o ngā mātua o te whānau, ā, ō rātou kaha hoki ki te kawe i ngā mahi tātaki. E tino whakaatu ana rātou i ō rātou whai hua ki te:

  • whakahaere pūtea hei tautoko i ngā whanaketanga o te kōhanga reo
  • whakahaere i ngā whare me te whenua me te arotahi ki ngā hiahia me ngā matea o ngā tamariki
  • whakahaere i ngā kaimahi hei tautoko i ngā whakahaere kounga nui mō te whakaako me te akoranga a ngā tamariki.
  • tautoko me te aroturuki i ngā tūmahi a ngā kaimahi me te tautokotia i ngā whakangungu o tēnā me tēnā
  • whakarato i ngā wheako mā ngā mātua o te whānau ki te ako
  • tautoko i te whānau me te tautoko i ngā kaimahi.

Te hauora me te haumaru.Tērā tētahi anga whakahaere hei aroturuki i ngā take mō te hauora me te haumaru, ā, ka āwhina tēnei anga i te whānau ki te whakarato i tētahi wāhi haumaru mā ngā tamariki, ngā kaimahi me te whānau. E ai ki ngā kōrero o te whānau ko ngā whakaritenga tōtika e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru te take e māia ana rātou, ā, e tino mōhio ana rātou mōhio kua tino tiakina ā rātou tamariki i te kōhanga reo.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakiia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o okiuh, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia:

  • kia haere tonu ai te arotahi a ngā kaimahi ki te whakapakari ake i ngā tukanga aromatawai mō ngā reanga katoa.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e whā.

Paenga-whāwhā 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Wānanga Whare Tāpere o Takitimu is situated in a unique learning environment that encompasses Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Te Wānanga Whare Tāpere o Takitimu and the Takitimu School of Performing Arts. This community based environment provides children with opportunities for a seamless education in an immersion setting. The Kōhanga Reo is strategically situated with a view of Mount Rongokako.

Culture and context are the basis of learning programmes for children. Kaimahi and whānau contribute to children’s keen sense of belonging and holistic wellbeing. Children are exposed to cultural experiences within the Ngāti Kahungunu region which helps them to develop their awareness and pride in their Ngāti Kahungunutanga. The ultimate aim for the whānau is to foster te reo and tikanga of Ngāti Kahungunu.

Intergenerational learning and achievement is a feature of the Kōhanga Reo. Whānau speak highly of strong leadership models who have contributed to shaping a community of learners. The Kōhanga Reo proudly display their ‘hall of fame’ of past, present and future leaders who have been members or graduates of the Kōhanga.

Children are interested and engaged in their learning. They enjoy exploring the Kōhanga Reo environment and have access to a wide range of good quality resources. The learning environment is vibrant, attractive and well resourced and managed to support children’s learning and care. Children enjoy the physical challenges of the outdoor environment. Tikanga and values are integral to children’s learning.

Children benefit from positive, respectful and caring relationships. Whanaungatanga forms the basis of all relationships. The management team provides a strong, yet caring approach for kaimahi, children and whānau. Leadership in the Kōhanga Reo is consultative, inclusive, and whānau oriented. The open door policy builds whānau confidence to participate in the life of the Kōhanga Reo. Whānau are enthusiastic and positive about the quality of education and care their children receive. Teaching and learning is underpinned by aroha, manaaki and awhi.

Kaimahi and whānau work well together. They strive to improve through professional development, the support of their local purapura and through regular reflection on the learning programme and their teaching practice. They consistently provide effective support and guidance in a variety of ways that build children’s confidence to learn.

The Kōhanga Reo is well organised and managed. Whānau share responsibilities for managing the Kōhanga Reo. Health, safety and hygiene practices are regularly monitored and maintained to a very high standard. Whānau focus is on providing a safe environment for children to learn and play.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata WhakahirahiraBackground

How well do the whānau nurture and support the holistic learning of their children?

Whānau effectively nurture and support the holistic learning of their children.

Areas of good performance

Mana Whenua.Children demonstrate a strong sense of identity and belonging. Kaupapa learning embraces mana whenua and extends children’s awareness of their connection to the land and to their iwi. Children attend a range of Ngāti Kahungunu events around the region and experience their culture in meaningful ways. Ancestral landmarks contained in murals and paintings around the Kōhanga Reo reinforce their knowledge of tūrangawaewae. Children are comfortable in their role as tangata whenua.

Mana Reo.Children are immersed in te reo Māori throughout the day. Kaimahi model te reo Māori for children and whānau. They use effective strategies and resources to reinforce language learning. Kaimahi use meaningful conversations and participation in cultural events to extend children’s language. Children are developing well as emerging speakers of te reo Māori.

Mana Atua.Kaimahi recognised the importance of supporting children’s health and wellbeing. Children assume leadership roles and confidently attend to aspects of their own care, nutrition and hygiene. Children learn about the benefits of healthy food and the value of manaaki tangata. They are well supported to carry out their tasks. Children are developing as confident, independent learners.

Mana Tangata.Children benefit from the positive and affirming relationships. Kaimahi are respectful, gentle and show a genuine interest in their children’s holistic learning and development. They work well as a team and are supportive of each other. They model expectations for learning and behaviour that is underpinned by aroha, manaaki and awhi. Older children are thoughtful and caring. They regularly include younger children in their interactions and play.

Mana Aotūroa.Children play in a well maintained environment where kaupapa Māori is reflected in the design of the buildings. The Kōhanga Reo environment is inviting, attractive, spacious, well resourced and organised. There is a good balance of busy and quiet areas. Outside children can combine imaginary play with physical challenges. Play areas are easily accessed when the children want to revisit the experiences they have enjoyed. Children are happy in a stimulating environment that promotes curiosity and a love for learning.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How well does the whānau plan, manage and review the operations of the Kōhanga Reo to benefit children?

Whānau plans support them to manage the operations of the Kōhanga Reo well and to provide the best possible opportunities for their children to succeed.

Areas of good performance

Planning.Kaimahi use a sound framework to plan learning programmes for children. These ensure that a wide range of kaupapa activities are provided to develop children’s understanding and knowledge about te ao Māori, and in particular, Kahungunutanga. Detailed planning guides kaimahi delivery of a well balanced programme that encourages children to explore and have fun while they learn.

Programme evaluation.Kaimahi understand the benefit of programme evaluation to improve outcomes for children. They reflect, modify and re-evaluate the learning programme. They are mindful of children’s individual strengths and needs and link planned experiences to children’s interests. Kaimahi are sensitive and respond in caring and affirming ways that build children’s confidence to learn.

Assessment.Kaimahi use a range of assessment tools to identify how well children are learning and progressing. The information is used to inform their planning and to report to parents. The next step for kaimahi is to share good practice so that assessment practices are consistent at all levels.

Te Ara Tūāpae.The whānau use Te Ara Tūāpae to develop an action plan. This guides and supports them to monitor and report against their annual priorities. The plan has a clear focus on the future development of the Kōhanga Reo and children’s learning.

Self Review.The Kōhanga Reo has established a systematic and planned approach to self review. Self review is well understood and drives decision making about operations and the daily management of the Kōhanga Reo. Property review has led to positive changes for children.

Roles and responsibilities.The Kōhanga Reo is effectively managed by a team of committed whānau members who provide ongoing support and leadership. They demonstrate effective:

  • financial management to support Kōhanga Reo developments
  • property management that focuses on children’s interests and needs
  • personnel management to support the provision of quality teaching and learning for children
  • support and monitoring of kaimahi practices which contributes to ongoing professional learning and development
  • provision of opportunities for whānau learning
  • whānau support and assistance to kaimahi.

Health and safety. A systematic approach to monitoring health and safety practices helps the whānau to provide a safe place for children, kaimahi and whānau. Whānau stated that health and safety practices engender confidence knowing that their children are well cared for at the Kōhanga Reo.
