2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Tauparanui ki Heretaunga, ki ngā papa o Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Ngāti Kahungunu ki Heretaunga. He tirohanga tō Rongokako tētahi tino pouwhenua ki Te Mātau-a-Māui - ki te kōhanga reo.

He nui whakaharahara te whakapūmautanga o te whānau ki ā rātou tamariki, ki te kōhanga reo anō hoki. He tokomaha ngā mema o te whānau i whakauru ai ki ngā tūmomo akoranga o te reo Māori. He whakahirahira hoki te tokomaha o te whānau e whai wāhi ana ki te kōhanga reo i ia rā.

I whakaritea e ngā kaimahi me te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo aronga ki te kōkiri i ngā akoranga me te waiora hoki o ngā tamariki. Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo karakia, ngā hīmene, me ngā mōteatea e arotahi ana ki ngā atua, ā, ka whakanuia te ngākau nui me te tiaki. Ka noho motuhake ētahi o ngā karakia ki a Ngāti Kahungunu.

He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te ao Māori me te ao kikokiko. Haere ai rātou ki ngā wāhi hihiri e hāngai ana ki ngā tūmomo kaupapa. Ko ētahi o aua tino wāhi, ko te whare mātai arorangi, te whare pukapuka, te whare o ngā waka patu ahi, te marae, te pāmu, me te tahamoana.

He tākare ngā tamariki mō te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo hangarau. Haereere ai rātou ki te whare pukapuka, ā, ka whiriwhiri rātou i ā rātou ake pukapuka, ka whakamahi hoki i ngā tūmomo rorohiko. Ka tuku ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i ā rātou rorohiko pōnaho, ā rātou īpapa hoki, i a rātou e noho ana ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whai pūtake ana, e hāpai ana hoki i te reo Māori. Ka whai hua ngā kaimahi hei tino tauira reo, ā, ka kōrero Māori rātou, puta noa i te rā. Ka whakamahi, ka kōrero hoki ngā tamariki tuākana i te reo Māori i roto i ā rātou taunekeneke ki ngā kaimahi, ki ō rātou hoa hoki. He pārekareka hoki ki ngā tamariki tēina ngā taumahi e hāngai ana ki te reo Māori, ā, e whakanui ana i te tāruarua, te kori tinana, me te oro.

He mārama ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromātai, me te aromatawai. He matawhānui ngā mahere hōtaka, ā, ka whai hua anō hoki ki te ārahi i te whakapuakitanga me te whakatinanatanga hoki o te hōtaka. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia wiki, ki te whakaaro huritao mō ā rātou whakaritenga. Ko ngā āhuatanga papai me ngā āhuatanga hoki hei whakapai ake e noho matua ana ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero a ngā kaimahi. Nā tēnei, ka whai hua ngā putanga mō ngā tamariki. He whānui ngā tūmomo mahi aromatawai, ā, ka āta tāutuhia ki ēnei, ko ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā kaha o ia tamaiti.

Kua āta whakatakotohia e te whānau ngā mahi e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He mārama ngā tukanga me ngā pūnaha e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o ngā pūtea, ngā take kaimahi, te whakahaere tari, me te whakatakoto haere i ngā tuhinga mauhanga. He tōtika te whakapau kaha a te whānau ki te āta whakarite i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki.

Ka kitea ētahi āhuatanga o te arotake whaiaro mārama, e arotahi ana hoki. I nāianei, me tahuri te whānau ki te aroturuki me te pūrongo hoki i ia te wā, i tā rātou mahere rautaki. Nā tēnei, ka noho mōhio te whānau ki te ahunga whakamua o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei. Me pupuri hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā pūrongo matawhānui e pā ana ki ngā mahi a ngā tamariki, engari kāhore ēnei e pūrongotia ana i ia te wā ki te whānau i ngā hui o ia marama.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Tauparanui kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Tauparanui.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e ngā kaimahi me te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E whakarato ana Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira i tētahi marautanga me tētahi horopaki, kia ako ai ngā tamariki Māori, kia wheako hoki ai rātou i te angitu.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te aronga toi whenuatanga.He pakari te tuakiri me te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki. He hononga ā-whakapapa tō te nuinga o te whānau me ngā tamariki ki a Ngāti Kahungunu. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā waiata, ngā whakataukī, ngā karakia, ngā pepeha, me ngā mōteatea hoki hei whakapuaki anō, hei whakanui hoki i ngā kōrero tuku iho e pā ana ki a Kahungunu. He maha ngā wā ka whai hua rātou o te whānau e noho kore mōhio ana ki ō rātou hononga ā-whakapapa, nā te āhua o aua taumahi i a rātou e noho ana ki te kōhanga reo. Ka tū pakari ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whakamana ana i ō rātou whakapapa ā-iwi.

Te waiora o ngā wairua.Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo aronga hei kōkiri i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me te waiora o ngā wairua. Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmomo karakia, hīmene, mōteatea hoki e arotahi ana ki ngā atua, ā, e whakanui ana hoki i te ngākau nui me te tiaki. Ka noho motuhake ētahi o ngā karakia ki a Ngāti Kahungunu. Ko te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki, me ō rātou waiora hoki, e whakanuia ana nā ēnei whakaritenga, ēnei tikanga hoki.

Te ao whānui.He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te ao Māori me te ao kikokiko. Haere ai rātou ki ngā wāhi hihiri e hāngai ana ki ngā tūmomo kaupapa. Ko ētahi o aua tino wāhi, ko te whare mātai arorangi, te whare pukapuka, te whare o ngā waka patu ahi, te marae, te pāmu, me te tahamoana. Ka whai pānga aua wheako ki te whakapakari i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki, te whakahoahoa, me ngā mōhiotanga hoki ki te ao whānui.

He tākare ngā tamariki mō te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo hangarau. Haereere ai rātou ki te whare pukapuka, ā, ka whiriwhiri rātou i ā rātou ake pukapuka, ka whakamahi hoki i ngā tūmomo rorohiko. He manawa whakahī tō rātou ki te kōrero mō tā rātou whakamahinga i ngā rorohiko, me tō rātou kore hiahia ki te tākaro ngātahi me ētahi atu tamariki ki runga i ngā rorohiko. Ka akiaki ētahi o ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te whakamahinga o ngā tūmomo hangarau ki te kōhanga reo, mā te tuku i ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i ā rātou rorohiko pōnaho, ā rātou īpapa hoki. Ka whakawero tōtikatia ngā tamariki. E pakari haere ana ō rātou pūkenga i roto i te reo matatini, te pāngarau, me te hangarau.

Te tū hei tangata whenua.He pakari ngā tamariki ki te tū hei tangata whenua. Haere ai rātou ki ngā pōwhiri, ngā huihuinga kapa haka, me ngā kaupapa whakahirahira hoki e tū ana ki te marae ki te taha o te kōhanga reo, i ia te wā. Ka kite ngā tamariki i te whānau e mātau ana ki te whaikōrero, te karanga, te waiata, me te manaaki manuhiri. Ka whakapūmau hoki ngā kaimahi i aua tikanga ki te kōhanga reo, i ngā wā hoki ka haere rātou ki ngā marae o te takiwā nei. He tau ngā tamariki ki te taha o ngā manuhiri, ki tētahi taiao hoki e āta whakatinana ana i ngā mātāpono o te manaakitanga.

Te pūmanawa o ngā tamariki ki te ārahi.Ka tino whai hua te akiaki i te pūmanawa o ngā tamariki ki te ārahi. Ka whakanui ngā kaimahi i ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki ki ngā karakia, ngā waiata, ngā hīmene, ngā mōteatea me ngā mihimihi, nā te tuku i a rātou ki te ārahi i aua kaupapa, i aua tikanga anō hoki. Ka whai oranga ngā tamariki i roto i aua taumahi e hāpai ana i a rātou ki te whakawhanake me te whakawhānui ake hoki i ō rātou āheinga me ā rātou akoranga. He hāneanea te haereere o ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmomo horopaki e whakamahia ana, e kōrerohia ana hoki te reo Māori.

Ngā hononga me ngā taunekeneke.He mauritau, he atawhai, he whakaute hoki ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. Ka tino ārahi ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki i roto i ngā tūmomo taumahi, i ā rātou hoki e whakawhiti ana i ngā mahinga. Ka whakarongo rātou ki ngā whakaaro o ngā tamariki, ā, he tika anō hoki tā rātou whakawhānui ake i ō rātou whakaaro. He tino tauira ngā tamariki pakari me ngā tamariki tuākana mō ō rātou tēina. He wawe tō rātou tautoko, tā rātou whakauru hoki i ngā tamariki tēina ki ngā tūmomo taumahi, kēmu hoki. He iti noa ngā mahi whakahaere whanonga, ārahitanga, tautoko hoki mō ngā tamariki, nō tō rātou mōhiotanga ki ngā mātāpono o te whanaungatanga. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā hononga e whai kiko ana, e tauutuutu ana hoki.

Te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki.Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao o te reo Māori e tautoko ana i a rātou, ā, e whai pūtake ana hoki. Ka whai hua ngā kaimahi ki te whakatauira i te reo, ā, ka kōrero Māori rātou puta noa i te rā. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā tūmomo aronga e akiaki ana i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te waiata me te taki i ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā waiata, ngā mōteatea, ngā mihi, ngā pepeha, me ngā whakapapa. Ka whakamahi, ka kōrero hoki ngā tamariki tuākana i te reo Māori i roto i ā rātou taunekeneke ki ngā kaimahi, ki ētahi atu hoki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki tēina ngā taumahi o te reo Māori, ā, e hāngai ana ēnei ki te tāruarua, te kori tinana, me ngā oro.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai i ngā kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā āhuatanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka hāngai te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te ngākau nui o te whānau.He nui te ngākau nuitanga o te whānau ki te kōhanga reo. He whanaunga, he hoa rānei te nuinga o ngā mema o te whānau, ā, i ahu mai i te whānau tuatahi i whakatīmata ai i te kōhanga reo, i te tau 1989. He tokomaha ngā mema o te whānau i whakauru ki ngā tūmomo kaupapa akoranga o te reo Māori. He whakataunga tēnei nā te whānau, whai muri i tētahi hui i hāngai ai ki te whakahirahiratanga o te reo Māori. E tino tautoko ana te nuinga o ngā mema o te whānau i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ā rātou tamariki ki te kāinga, ā, i te kōhanga reo hoki.

He maha ngā tūmomo mahi a te whānau e tautoko ana i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. Ahakoa te tokomaha o te whānau e mahi ana, e whai kaupapa kē atu ana rānei, he nui tonu te whai wāhitanga o te whānau ki te kōhanga reo i ia rā. Haere ai te whānau ki ngā hui me ngā akoranga i ia te wā, hei hāpai i tō rātou māramatanga ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga hoki, me tō rātou mōhiotanga hoki ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. He nui te tūmanako kia mātātoa te whai wāhi ā te whānau ki ngā akoranga a ā rātou tamariki, me te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

Te whakamahere rautaki.Kua āta tuhia te mahere rautaki, ā, ka whai hua ki te ārahi i te ahunga whakamua o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei. Ka hono te mahere ki Te Ara Tūāpae me ngā whāinga rautaki o Ngāti Kahungunu. Ko ētahi o ngā kaupapa matua, ko te reo Māori, ko Te Whāriki, ko Te Korowai, ko te whakawhanaketanga ngaio, ko te whakawhanake pakihi, me te hauora, te haumaru hoki. He mārama te aronga o ngā wawata o te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā tino putanga mō ngā tamariki.

Te arotake whaiaro.Ka kitea ētahi āhuatanga mārama, hāngaitanga hoki o te arotake whaiaro. Pūrongohia ai e te whānau te whakahaeretanga o ngā pūtea, ngā take kaimahi, te whakawhanaketanga ngaio, ngā kaupapa here, me ngā take hoki e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. He pakari te whānau ki te kawe i aua āhuatanga e āta whakahaerehia ana e rātou.

Te whakahaere tari.He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. He mārama ngā tukanga me ngā pūnaha e pā ana ki te whakahaere pūtea, te hauora me te haumaru, ngā take kaimahi, ngā arohaehae, te pupuri i ngā mauhanga, me ngā rehita. E hāngai ana aua pūnaha ki te rā, ā, kua āta whakaritea hoki.

Te whakamahere i ngā kaupapa.He matawhānui te whakamahere, ā, e whai hua ana ki te ārahi i te whakapuakitanga me te whakatinanatanga o te hōtaka. Ka hono ngā mahere ki ngā mātāpono o Te Whāriki me Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira, ā, kua whakaurua hoki ngā whāinga o te hōtaka me ngā kaupapa. Ka whakapūmautia te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori mā ngā huarahi e whai pūtake ana, e whai hua ana hoki, nā runga i te āhua o ēnei mahi whakamahere.

Te aromātai hōtaka.Tū ai te aromātai hōtaka i ia te wā, ā, ka whai pūtake, ka whai hua hoki mō ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia wiki ki te whakaaro huritao mō ā rātou whakaritenga. Ko ngā āhuatanga papai, me ngā wāhanga hoki hei whakapai ake, e kōkiri ana i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero a ngā kaimahi. Nā tēnei, ka wheako ngā tamariki i te angitu i a rātou e tūhura ana, e ako ana hoki.

Ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aromatawai.Kua arotakengia ētahi o ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aromatawai, ā, nā tēnei mahi, he whakapaitanga anō hoki ki te whakaemitanga o ngā kitenga, ngā whakaahua me ngā kōrerorero, ngā mahi ā-kaupapa, te rārangi arowhai, me ngā mahi hoki a ngā tamariki e mau kōrerorero ana. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi ki ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā kaha o ia tamaiti, ā, ka whakatutukihia ēnei i roto i te hōtaka o ia rā. E wātea ana ki ngā whānau ngā mōhiohio aromatawai e pā ana ki ngā tamariki, ā, ka aro nui ēnei ki ngā whakatutukitanga me te ahunga whakamua hoki o ia tamaiti.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te pūrongo i ia te wā.Kāhore te whānau e aroturuki ana, e pūrongo ana rānei i ia te wā, i ētahi wāhanga o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He pūrongo matawhānui ā ngā kaimahi mō ētahi o ngā tūmomo kaupapa, te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki, me ngā haerenga. He iti noa ngā wā ka whakapuakihia aua mōhiohio ki te whānau. Kāhore i te auau ngā pūrongo e pā ana ki te mahere rautaki.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Tauparanui, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhangame tētahiRārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tauparanui Ki Heretaunga is on the grounds of Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Ngāti Kahungunu ki Heretaunga, Hastings. The kōhanga reo is located within view of Rongokako, a significant landmark in Hawkes Bay.

The level of commitment shown by whānau to their children and kōhanga reo is extraordinary. There are a high number of whānau enrolled in various te reo Maori courses and classes. The large presence of whānau at kōhanga reo each day is also significant.

Kaimahi and whānau chose Nga Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus for the review.

Children are enthusiastic about a range of technology. They regularly visit the library where they are able to select their own books and use the various computers. Kaimahi and whānau allow children to use their laptops and ipads when they are at kōhanga reo.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Tauparanui was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Te Kōhanga Reo o Tauparanui.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

4 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

Kaimahi and whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus area for the review. Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira provides a curriculum and context in which Māori children can learn and experience success.

Areas of good performance

Sense of belonging. Children have a strong sense of identity and belonging. Most whānau and children have whakapapa ties to Ngāti Kahungunu. Kaimahi use waiata, proverbs, karakia, pepeha and moteatea to retell and promote stories about Ngāti Kahungunu. Whānau, who are unaware of their whakapapa connections, often benefit from these activities when they are at kōhanga reo. Children stand proudly in an environment that affirms their tribal connections.

Spiritual wellbeing. Kaimahi use a wide range of approaches to facilitate children’s learning and spiritual wellbeing. Children are immersed in various karakia, hīmene and moteatea that focus on the atua and emphasise goodwill and protection. Some karakia are unique to Ngāti Kahungunu. Children’s te reo Māori development and wellbeing are heightened through these practices and customs.

Wider world. Children have opportunities to explore the natural and physical worlds. They visit places of interest associated with different kaupapa. Highlights have included the planetarium, library, fire station, marae, farm and beach. These experiences help strengthen children’s te reo Māori development, social interactions and knowledge of the wider world.

Children are enthusiastic about different technologies. Children regularly visit the library where they are able to select their own books and use the various computers. They speak proudly about their computer use which they reluctantly share with other children. Some kaimahi and whānau encourage the use of technology at kōhanga reo by allowing children to use their ipads and laptops. Children are challenged in appropriate ways. They are gaining confidence in their literacy, numeracy and technology skills.

Tangata whenua roles. Children are confident in their roles as tangata whenua. They regularly attend powhiri, kapahaka events and special celebrations that are held at the marae sited alongside the kōhanga reo. Children observe whānau that are skilled in oratory, karanga, singing and hospitality. Kaimahi reinforce these protocols at kōhanga reo and when they visit the marae in the local area. Children are at ease with visitors and are comfortable in an environment which strongly demonstrates the principles of manaakitanga.

Children’s leadership potential. Children’s leadership potential is encouraged in positive ways. Kaimahi celebrate children’s interests in karakia, waiata, hīmene, moteatea and mihimihi by allowing them to take lead roles in these activities and protocols. Children thrive on these activities which help them to develop and extend their confidence in what they are capable of and their learning. Children move comfortably in different contexts which use and speak te reo Māori.

Relationships and interactions. Interactions and relationships between kaimahi and children are positive, gentle and respectful. Kaimahi guide children well through different activities and as they move between routines. They listen to children’s thoughts and extend their ideas in appropriate ways. The able and older children are effective role models for their younger peers. They readily support and include the younger children in different activities and games. Children require minimum behaviour management guidance and support because they are aware of the principles of whanaungatanga. Children experience meaningful and reciprocal relationships.

Children’s te reo Māori development. Children are immersed in a supportive, purposeful te reo Māori environment. Kaimahi are effective language models and speak te reo Māori throughout the day. They use various approaches to encourage children’s te reo Māori development. Children confidently sing and recite different karakia, hīmene, waiata, moteatea, mihi, pepeha and whakapapa. The older children use and speak te reo Māori in their interactions with kaimahi and each other. The younger children enjoy te reo Māori activities which include repetition, action and sound.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau commitment. There is a high level of whānau commitment to the kōhanga reo. The majority of whānau members are either related or friends that originated from the first family that began the kōhanga reo in 1989. There are a high number of whānau enrolled in various te reo Māori courses and classes. Whānau made this decision following a meeting about the importance of learning te reo Māori. The majority of whānau members willingly support their children’s te reo Māori development at home and while at kōhanga reo.

Whānau support kaimahi and children in various ways. Although there are a significant number of whānau, who have work or other commitments, there is still a large presence of whānau at kōhanga reo every day. Whānau regularly attend meetings and courses to help develop their understanding of their roles and responsibilities and knowledge of the philosophy of the kōhanga reo movement. There is a high expectation that whānau will take an active role in their children’s learning and the kōhanga reo operations.

Strategic planning. The strategic plan is well written and effectively guides the future direction of the kōhanga reo. The plan links to Te Ara Tūāpae and the Ngāti Kahungunu regional strategic goals. Priorities include te reo Māori,Te Whāriki, Te Korowai, professional development, business development and health and safety. Whānau aspirations have a clear focus on achieving positive outcomes for children.

Self review. There are elements of clear and focussed self review. Whānau regularly report on financial management, staffing matters, professional development, policies and health and safety matters. Whānau confidently oversee these areas which they manage well.

Office administration. The whānau manages the kōhanga reo well. There are clear processes and systems for financial management, health and safety, staffing matters, appraisals, record keeping and registers. These systems are up to date and highly organised.

Kaupapa planning. Planning is comprehensive and effectively guides programme delivery and implementation. Plans link to the principles ofTe Whāriki, the different mana strands and include programme objectives and kaupapa. Children’s knowledge of te ao Māori is reinforced in purposeful and effective ways through this method of planning.

Programme evaluation. Programme evaluation is regular, purposeful and benefits children in their learning. Kaimahi meet on a weekly basis to reflect on their practice. Highlights and areas for improvement guide kaimahi discussions. Consequently, children experience success as they explore and learn.

Assessment practices. Assessment practices have undergone review and have led to improvements in the collection of children’s achievement information. Practices include observations, photographs with comments, kaupapa work, a checklist and children’s work with annotations. Kaimahi are aware of each child’s interests and strengths which they address through the daily programme. Whānau have access to children’s assessment information which highlights each child’s successes and progress.

Areas for development

Regular reporting. There are some parts of the kōhanga reo operations that whānau do not regularly monitor or report on. Kaimahi keep comprehensive reports on different kaupapa, children’s te reo Māori development and outings. This information is seldom made known to whānau. There is irregular reporting on the strategic plan.

  • financial and property management.

  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
