Te Reo Rangatira

Te Mauri o te Mana Māori

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūmanako ki Kawerau. Ka ahu mai ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo i te takiwā nei. Ka mauria e ngā mātua ā rātou tamariki, ā, ka whakawhiti kōrero ngā kaimahi ki a rātou i ia rā. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā hīkoi i te hapori me ngā haerenga ki te moana me ngā tāone e pā tata atu ana.

Kua whai hua te whānau ki te whakatika i ētahi tino take nui, wero nui hoki i roto i te whakahaeretanga me ngā hononga ki waenga i te whānau. Mai i te rerekētanga ki te whakahaere i te tari, kua whakapau kaha te whānau ki te whakapakari anō i te whakapono o te whānau mō te kōhanga reo. Kua whai pānga te arotake whaiaro ngātahi, riterite hoki ki te ahunga whakamua a te whānau.

E whakarato ana te whānau o te kōhanga reo i te taiao akoranga hihiri, muramura hoki, ā, e noho pūmau ana ngā uaratanga, ngā kawa, me ngā tikanga Māori kia whakawhanakehia ai e ngā tamariki te tino tuakiri. He ātaahua te whare me ngā rawa, ā, ka āta tiakina hoki.

Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i tētahi hōtaka akoranga pai. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whiriwhiri i ā rātou taumahi, ka ngākau nui ki te tākaro auaha, ā, he wā anō hoki ki te arotahi ki ngā akoranga ā-kaupapa.

Kua whakawhanakehia e ngā kaimahi me ngā mātua ngā hononga atawhai. He mauritau te noho o ngā mātua, ā, he mātātoa tā rātou hāpai i te kōhanga reo. Ka pūrongo te heamana ki te whānau, ā, ka akiaki ia i a rātou kia whai wāhi rātou ki te noho ngātahi o te whakatau whakaritenga me te whakapuaki mōhiohio, ā, kia mātātoa tā rātou whai wāhi ki te whakamahere me te arotake i ngā mahi tari, ngā mahi whakahaere.

He harikoa ngā tamariki. He pai te tiaki o ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki. Ka whai pānga nui ngā mātāpono me ngā uaratanga o te Mana Tangata, ki te whakawhanake me te whakapūmau i te āheinga me te noho o te whānau.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūmanako kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūmanako.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Whānau.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Whānau

He whakamārama

Kua pā ki te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā wā uaua, ā, nō nā tata tonu nei rātou i whakarōpū anō ki te whakarite i te aronga pai mō te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā mātāpono o te whanaungatanga, i whai pānga nui ki tēnei whakawhanaketanga. Ko te pakari, te aronga ngātahi hoki o te arotake whaiaro i whai pānga anō hoki ki tā rātou eke angitu.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whanaungatanga.Ka whakaae tahi te whānau, ka noho matua te reo Māori me ngā tikanga ki a rātou, ki ā rātou tamariki hoki. He kaha te whanaungatanga e whanake haere ana. He pai ngā whakawhitinga whakaaro o ngā mema, ā, ka rongo rātou i te whai wāhi ki te kōhanga reo, ahakoa he iti noa te reo Māori ā ētahi. Ka whiwhi te whānau i te tautoko me te ārahitanga kia māramahia, kia whakatinanahia hoki e rātou ngā mātāpono o Te Whāriki me Te Korowai. Nā tēnei, ka ako rātou i ngā karakia, ngā waiata tawhito, me ngā waiata ngahau.

Te hōtaka akoranga.He pai te atawhai o ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki. He wā ka mahi hei rōpū kotahi, hei rōpū iti hoki. Kua waia ngā tamariki ki ā rātou mahinga. Kua reri, kua rite hoki ngā kaimahi mō ngā taumahi ā ia tamaiti, a ia rōpū, ā, hei rōpū kotahi hoki. E ākina ana ngā mātua ki te noho ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki. Ka waiata tahi ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki i a rātou e mahi ana. Ka noho pūmau ngā kaimahi ki tētahi taiao reo Māori. He nui ngā rauemi i te kōhanga reo. He mahana, he atawhai hoki te āhua o te taiao o roto. Ko ngā whakaaturanga ātaahua e tohu ana i ngā tūmomo kaupapa, ngā mahi a ngā tamariki, me ngā tūtohi hoki ka hāpai i ngā akoranga. Ko te wāhi o waho e whakarato ana i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura me te tahuri hoki ki ngā tākaro auaha. Ka ngākau nui ngā mātua ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou pūkenga, pērā i te hanga poi, te tunu kai, me te mahi māra. Ka āta haere noa ngā mātua i ngā rūma ki te āwhina me te tautoko.

Te reo me ngā tikanga Māori.He kaha te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki, ā, kei te mōhio pai rātou ki tō rātou kōhanga reo. He pakari tā rātou whakapuaki i ō rātou pepeha, ā, e mōhio ana rātou e toru ngā iwi i te takiwā whānui o Kawerau. Ka tiaki ngā tamariki tuākana i ngā tamariki tēina. Ka whakanuia ngā whakamātau pai. He pai te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. E āhei ana ngā tamariki tuākana ki te whakamahi i ngā kīanga ngāwari, me te whakautu i ngā pātai.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai i ngā kōhanga reo, hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki hoki i ngā wāhanga katoa o ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki, mō te whānau hoki. Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakahaeretanga.Ka whai hua ngā mahi a te whānau ki te whakahaere i tō rātou kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki. He pai te tae ā-tinana atu ki ngā hui ā-whānau. Ka whakarite te whakahaeretanga i te noho mōhio o te whānau ki ngā nekehanga i te kōhanga reo. Ka whai wāhi te whānau ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga, te whakamahere i te hōtaka, me ngā arotake. Nā te whakapuaki i ngā mōhiohio i ia te wā ka noho mōhio te katoa. Ko te pānui a te kōhanga reo e kīia nei koKarere, ka hāngai ki ngā take o te wā, ki ngā kaupapa, ngā rā e whai pānga nui ana, me ētahi atu take. Ko te pakari o ngā whakaritenga ki ngā arotake whaiaro ngātahi tētahi āhuatanga nui i tēnei kōhanga reo. Ka hāngai ngā tuhinga matua ki te rā. He pai te aroturukitanga o ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga. Kua whiwhi raihana anō te kōhanga reo, ā, e whakatutuki katoa ana i ngā herenga ā-ture o te tau 2008. Ka whakahaere te whānau i ngā pūtea hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Ka ahu mai ngā aronga rautaki i te arotake whaiaro. Ko ngā whakaritenga whakahaere mō te whakawhanaketanga o te kōhanga reo me te whānau, ka hāngai ki ngā whakangungu e pā ana ki ngā pūtea, ngā whare me ngā rawa, Te Whāriki, te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori, me te whakahaeretanga o ngā kaimahi. He pai te aroturuki i ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga a te whānau.

Te whakamahere me te aromatawai.He pai te whakaritenga o te whakamahere hōtaka, ā, e mau ana i ngā wāhanga matua katoa, ā, ka whakaatuhia hei ārahi i ngā kaiako, hei whakamōhio anō hoki i ngā mātua. Ka whakatakotohia ngā tauākī aromātai o ia wiki. He pai te whakariterite me te whakaatu i ngā pūkete e pā ana ki ia tamaiti. Ko ā rātou tauira mahi toi, ā rātou whakaahua hoki e tohu ana i tā rātou whai wāhitanga ki ngā akoranga. Kua tuhia hoki ngā kōrero e pā ana ki ngā mahi a ngā tamariki, ngā kitenga, me tā rātou ahunga whakamua hoki. Pūrongo ai ngā kaimahi ki ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, mō te hōtaka akoranga. He pai te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te aromatawai.I nāianei, me tahuri ngā kaiako ki te whakauru i te reo a te tamaiti ki ngā aromatawai o ā rātou mahi. Ka whai wāhi anō hoki ā rātou kōrerorero ki ngā whakataunga a ngā kaimahi mō te aronga o te hōtaka, ā, ki te whakahāngai anō hoki i te hōtaka ki ngā tino ngākau nuitanga ake o ngā tamariki.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo

Te Reo Rangatira

Te Mauri o te Mana Māori

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūmanako is located in Kawerau. Children from the local area attend the kōhanga reo. Parents bring their children and staff are able to communicate with them daily. The children enjoy local walks and outings to the beach and nearby towns.

The whānau has successfully overcome major issues and challenges with management and whānau relationships. Since the change in office administration, the whānau have worked hard to rebuild whānau confidence and belief in the kōhanga. Consistent, collaborative self review has helped the whānau to make positive moves forward.

The kōhanga whānau provide a vibrant and colourful learning environment where Māori values, customs and practices are integral to children developing a strong sense of identity. The property is attractive and well maintained.

The staff provide a good learning programme. There is opportunity for children to select their own activity, enjoy creative play and a time to focus on kaupapa learning.

Staff and parents have developed warm relationships. Parents feel welcome and actively support the kōhanga. The chairperson reports to whānau and involves them in collaborative decision-making, sharing information and actively engaging them in planning and reviewing administrative and management tasks.

Children are happy. Staff take good care of the children. Mana Tangata principles and values play an important role in developing and maintaining whānau capability and retention.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

4 Findings

Whānau

Background

The kōhanga whānau have experienced challenging times and have recently been able to regroup and establish a positive direction for the kōhanga. Principles of whanaungatanga have been integral to this development. Robust, collaborative self review has also contributed to their success.

Areas of good performance

Whanaungatanga.Whānau agree that te reo Māori me ngā tikanga will always be a priority for themselves and their children. There is a strong sense of whanaungatanga developing. Members communicate well and feel accepted despite some having limited capability in te reo Māori. Whānau receive support and guidance to understand and implement the principles of Te Whāriki and Te Korowai. Through this they learn karakia, waiata tawhito and waiata ngahau.

The learning programme. Staff take good care of the children. There are periods of whole and small group activity. Children are familiar with their routines. Staff are prepared and organised for their individual group and whole group activity. Parents are encouraged to stay with their children. Staff and children sing together while they are working. Staff maintain a te reo Māori speaking environment. The kōhanga is well resourced. The indoor environment is warm and welcoming. Attractive displays reflect various kaupapa, children’s work and charts to support learning. The outdoor area provides opportunity for children to explore and engage in creative play. Parents are willing to share skills such as poi making, cooking and gardening. Parents move quietly around the rooms helping and supporting.

Te reo and tikanga Māori. Children have a strong sense of belonging, they know their way round the kōhanga. They share their pepeha confidently and are aware that three iwi groups live in and around Kawerau. The older children take care of the younger ones. Praise is given for good effort. Children have a good understanding of te reo Māori. The older children are able to use basic phrases and respond to questions.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Management. Whānau effectively manage their kōhanga to benefit their children. There is good attendance at whānau hui. Management ensure whānau are well informed about what is happening at the kōhanga. Whānau are involved in decision making, programme planning and reviews. Consistent information sharing keeps everyone informed. The kōhanga newsletterKarerecovers current matters, kaupapa, important dates and other matters. Robust collaborative self review practices have been a feature of the kōhanga. Essential documents are kept up to date. Policies and procedures are monitored well. The kōhanga has undergone relicensing and is fully compliant with the 2008 regulatory expectations. The whānau manage finances in the best interests of children. Strategic directions come from self review. Management plans for the kōhanga and whānau development include training related to finance, property, Te Whāriki, Te reo Māori development and personnel management. Whānau management processes are well monitored.

Planning and assessment.Programme planning is well organised, contains all the essential elements and is displayed to guide kaiako and inform parents. Weekly evaluative statements are recorded. Children’s individual profiles books are well prepared and presented. Their art samples and photos provide a record of their participation in learning. Statements about children’s work, observation notes and progress are documented. Staff regularly report to whānau hui about the learning programme. Children’s learning is well planned, assessed and evaluated.

Areas for development

Assessment. Kaiako now need to include the child’s voice in the assessment of their work. Their comments can contribute to staff decisions about programme directions and tailoring the programme to the specific interest of the children.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūmanako Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff ofcompleted an EROKōhanga Whānau Assurance StatementandSelf-Audit Checklist. In these documents theyhaveattested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

6 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • continue to consolidate procedures and build whānau capability

7. Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

The chairperson reports to whānau and involves them in collaborative decision-making, sharing information and actively engaging them in planning and reviewing administrative and management tasks.

Children are happy. Staff take good care of the children. Mana Tangata principles and values play an important role in developing and maintaining whānau capability and retention.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to student achievement and useful to this kōhanga reo.

If you would like a copy of the full report, please contact the kōhanga reo or see the ERO web page, http://www.ero.govt.nz.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
