1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Raukawa i roto i a Ngāti Kapumanawawhiti, ki Ngāti Raukawa. E pātata ana Te Kōhanga Reo ki te wharenui, te urupā, me te whare karakia. Haere ai ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga i tō rātou marae.

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo, hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Ka whakatau ngā mema o te whānau i ngā whakaritenga, ki ngā hui. Tū ai ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, hei hāpai i te whakatau whakaritenga, hei āta whakarite i te noho mōhio o te whānau ki ngā mahi whakahaere.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te taiao o te marae, ā, ka ako rātou i te hapū me te iwi. E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi me te ako i te reo Māori. Ka whakarato ki a rātou ngā wheako akoranga e whakawhānui ana, e whakawero ana hoki i ā rātou akoranga.

  • He tau, he pakari hoki ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao.
  • Ka whakarite ngā tamariki i ā rātou ake akoranga, nā ngā taumahi e whai pānga nui ana ki ō rātou ngākau nuitanga.
  • Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā karakia, ngā mahinga hauora, me te tiaki i a rātou anō.
  • Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā taunekeneke e manaaki ana, e poipoi ana, e aro nui ana hoki ki a rātou, hei whakawhanake hoki i te whanaungatanga.
  • E whakawhanake ana te pakari o ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori ki te whānuitanga o ngā horopaki.
  • Ka whakaatu ngā tamariki i ngā uaratanga o te manaakitanga, te whanaungatanga, te wairua, me te ūkaipō ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ngā pākeke hoki.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Raukawa kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Raukawa.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ka hāngai Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira ki ngā whenu o te Mana Reo, te Mana Tangata, te Mana Whenua, te Mana Aotūroa, me te Mana Atua. Ko te tikanga, ka tuitui katoatia Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira ki ngā āhuatanga katoa o te hōtaka o ngā tamariki i ia rā.

Ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o ngā whai wāhitanga ako a ngā tamariki, me ngā whai pānga ki Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Mana Reo

E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki ki te ako me te whakamahi i te reo Māori, arā:

  • ka taki, ka ārahi rātou i ngā wā karakia, mō te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki
  • ka whakawhanakehia tō rātou māia ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori i roto i te whānuitanga o ngā horopaki
  • ka whakamahi rātou i te reo Māori i ngā tākaro whakaari ki te rua kirikiri, te whare tākaro, ā, i a rātou e tū ana ki te haka.

Mana Tangata

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao noho marae, ā, ka ako rātou i ō rātou whānau, tō rātou hapū, tō rātou iwi hoki, arā:

  • he hāneanea, he pakari hoki rātou ki tō rātou taiao
  • ka whakaatuhia e rātou ngā uaratanga o te manaakitanga, te whanaungatanga, te wairua, me te ūkaipō ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ngā pākeke hoki
  • ka whakatau rātou i ā rātou ake akoranga, huarahi mahi hoki, i runga i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga, me ngā akoranga o mua.

Mana Whenua

Ka whakaratohia ki ngā tamariki te hōhonutanga o ngā wheako akoranga e whai pānga nui ana ki a rātou, ā, e whakakoia ana i ngā hononga whakahirahira ki a Ngāti Kapumanawawhiti. Ka ako rātou:

  • i ngā tamariki ā Tangaroa, i ngā mahi whakatō, mahi whakatupu, mahi hauhake kai hoki, hei kaitiaki
  • ki te whakatau whiringa i roto i ā rātou mahi, mai i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wāhi ako, rauemi ako hoki
  • me pēhea te tiaki i a rātou anō, me te noho haumaru ā-wairua, ā-tangata, ā-tinana hoki.

Mana Aotūroa

E ākina ana te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou ao. Ka whakarato ki a rātou ngā wheako akoranga ka whakawhānui, ka whakawero hoki i ō rātou whakaaro. Nā tēnei:

  • e whakawhanake haere ana tō rātou mōhiotanga ki te noho whakahirahira o te rā me Matariki
  • ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā haerenga me ngā akoranga ā-kaupapa i whakaritea ai, hei hāpai i a rātou ki te whakawhānui i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui
  • ka ako rātou i ngā ariā o te pūtaiao, me te whakawhānui i tā rātou puāwaitanga o te pāngarau me te reo matatini, hei whakariterite i a rātou mō te kura.

Mana Atua

He mātātoa te kōhanga reo ki te whakatairanga me te whakawhanake i ngā tikanga Māori. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā karakia, ngā mahinga hauora, me te tiaki i a rātou anō, ā, he whai wāhitanga anō hoki ēnei ki te taunekeneke ki ō rātou hoa, ki te whānau hoki. Ka kitea tō rātou āheinga ki te tiaki i ā rātou ake taputapu. Ka wheako ngā tamariki o tēnei kōhanga reo, i ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga manaaki e poipoi ana, e aro ana hoki i a rātou. He pai tā rātou taunekeneke ki te taha o ō rātou hoa me ngā pākeke.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai ki ngā kōhanga reo, hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki, me te whānau. Ka hāngai te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka, me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakamahere hōtaka. Ka tino arahina te whānau me ngā kaimahi e te matawhānui o ngā mahere hōtaka. He mārama ngā tūmanako e pā ana ki te whakawhanaketanga o ngā mahere hōtaka, ā, he pai te whai haere i ēnei. Ka whakamahi te whānau i Te Whāriki me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki, hei pūtake mō te whakamahere i te hōtaka. He rawaka te noho ngāwari o ngā mahere hōtaka, kia āhei ai ngā kaimahi ki te aro atu ki te rerekē haere o ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā matea o ngā tamariki.

Te aromātai hōtaka. Kua āta whakaritea te aromātai hōtaka. He tau te māramatanga o ngā kaimahi ki te pūtake o te aromātai hōtaka. Nā ngā hui kaimahi o ia wiki, ka whai wā ngā kaimahi ki te aromātai i ngā āhuatanga o te hōtaka akoranga. E arotahi ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakapai tonu i te hōtaka akoranga mō ngā tamariki.

Te aromatawai. E āhei ana ngā mātua ki te whakapā atu ki ngā mōhiohio papai e pā ana ki te ahunga whakamua a ā rātou tamariki, me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ka whai wāhi nui rātou ki te whakarato i ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā kaha o ngā tamariki. Ka āwhina aua mōhiohio i ngā kaimahi, me tā rātou ako, tō rātou mōhiotanga hoki ki ngā tamariki. He pai te whakamahinga o ngā kaimahi i aua mōhiohio ki te whakahāngai i ngā hōtaka akoranga ki ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā kaha o ngā tamariki.

Te arotake whaiaro. He pai te whakaritenga o te arotake whaiaro. E whai ana tēnei whānau i tētahi huringa taiāwhio ki te whakapai tonu i ngā āhuatanga maha o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka whakamahi rātou i te maha o ngā taputapu arotake whaiaro, hei tāutu i ngā wāhanga pakari, me ngā āhuatanga hoki hei whakapai ake. Nā te aromātai i te hōtaka, ngā pūrongo ā-ohu, te aromatawai, me ngā hui ā-whānau, ka tino whakatutukihia te arotake whaiaro.

Te whakamahere rautaki. Ka whakamahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā whāinga o Te Ara Tūāpae hei tūāpapa e ārahi ana i tā rātou ahunga whakamua. Kua tuhia ngā kaupapa matua me ngā whāinga e pā ana ki te whakawhanaketanga mō meāke nei, ā, e mōhiotia ana ēnei e ētahi o ngā mema o te whānau. I nāianei, e hiahia ana te whānau ki te whakawhanake i tētahi mahere ka hāpai i te whakahaeretanga, hei āwhina i a rātou ki te whakatutuki i ā rātou whāinga me ngā wawata ā meāke nei.

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau. He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. E whai hua ana te pūnaha whakahaere ohu, ā, ka whai wāhi ngā mema katoa o te whānau nā ēnei. Ka whakatau ngā mema o te whānau i ngā whakaritenga. Tū ai ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, kia āhei ai te whānau ki te whakahaere i tō rātou kōhanga reo. He pai te tae ā-tinana atu ki aua hui.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te arotake i ia te wā, i te tūtohinga o te kōhanga reo, me ngā kaupapa here, ngā tukanga hoki.Kāhore ētahi o ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga o te kōhanga reo e hāngai ana ki te rā, kāhore rānei ēnei e tino ārahi ana i te whānau i roto i ā rātou whakaritenga o ia rā. E whakaae ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi, kua tae ki te wā ki te arotake i ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga, me te aro nui ki te hauora me te haumaru.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Raukawa, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātaurangame ngā whakaritengakaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Tūtohunga

I whakawhanakehia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • arotake te whānau me ngā kaimahi i te tūtohinga o te kōhanga reo, tae atu ki ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga.

6 Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

Makere Smith Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu mō ngā Ratonga Arotake Māori (Te Uepū ā-Motu)

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Raukawa Te Kōhanga Reo is in Ngati Kapumanawawhiti in Ngati Raukawa. The immediate environment surrounding the kōhanga reo includes a wharenui, urupa and whare karakia. Children attend kōhanga reo at their local marae.

The whānau manages the kōhanga reo well in the interests of children. Whānau members make decisions at hui. Regular whānau hui are held to support decision making and ensure management practices are known to whānau.

Children are immersed in a marae based environment that includes learning about hāpu and iwi. Children are very well supported to use and learn te reo Māori. They are provided with learning experiences that extend and challenge them.

  • Children are comfortable and confident in their environment.
  • Children determine their learning through activities based on their interests.
  • Children learn about karakia, healthy routines and other self managing practices
  • Children experience caring, nurturing and responsive interactions towards developing relationships.
  • Children are developing confidence in using te reo Māori in a range of situations.
  • Children demonstrate the values of manaakitanga, whanaungatanga, wairuatanga and ukaipotanga with each other and with adults.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Raukawa was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Te Kōhanga Reo o Raukawa.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Nga Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

The whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus area for this review. The strands of Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira include, mana reo, mana tangata, mana whenua, mana aotūroa and mana atua. Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira should be fully integrated throughout the children’s daily programme.

ERO will evaluate the quality of children’s learning opportunities as they relate to Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Mana Reo

Children are very well supported to learn and use te reo Māori. They:

  • recite and lead karakia sessions for a variety of purposes
  • are developing confidence in using te reo Māori in a range of situations
  • use te reo Māori during role play in the sandpit, play house and in kapahaka.

Mana Tangata

Children are immersed in an environment that is marae based, and learn about whānau, hapū, and iwi. They:

  • are comfortable and confident in their environment
  • demonstrate the values of manaakitanga, whanaungatanga, wairuatanga and ukaipotanga with each other and with adults
  • determine their learning emphasis and pathways based on their interests and prior learning.

Mana Whenua

Children are provided with rich and relevant learning experiences that affirm connection to iwi icons in Ngati Kapumanawawhiti. They learn about:

  • the children of Tangaroa, planting, and nurturing and harvesting food in their role as kaitiaki
  • making choices about what to do from a wide range of learning spaces and resources
  • having an awareness of how to keep themselves safe spiritually, culturally and physically.

Mana Aoturoa

Children’s knowledge of their world is encouraged. They are provided with learning experiences that extend and challenge them. Consequently they:

  • have a developing knowledge about the importance of the sun and Matariki
  • experience planned excursions and kaupapa learning that helps to extend their knowledge of the Māori world and the wider world
  • learn science concepts and extend their early numeracy and literacy learning in preparation for school.

Mana Atua

The kōhanga reo is active in promoting and developing tikanga Māori. Children learn about karakia, healthy routines and other self managing practices that provide them with opportunities to interact with peers and whānau. They show they are able to care for their own belongings. Children at this kōhanga reo, experience caring, nurturing and responsive interactions and relationships. They interact well with their peers and adults.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Programme planning. The whānau and staff are well guided by comprehensive programme planning. The expectations for developing programme plans are clear and these are followed well. The whānau usesTe Whārikiand children’s interests as the basis for programme planning. Programme planning is sufficiently flexible to allow staff to respond to the changing interests and needs of the children.

Programme evaluation. Programme evaluation is well established. The staff have a sound understanding of the purpose of programme evaluation. Weekly staff meetings provide the staff the opportunity to evaluate aspects of the learning programme. The staff is focussed on continually improving the learning programme for children.

Assessment. Parents are able to access good information about their children’s progress and development. They play a key role in providing information about their children’s interests and strengths. This information helps the staff to learn and know about the children. The staff use the information well to help tailor programmes of learning to the interests and strengths of the children.

Self review. Self review is well established. This whānau is on a cycle of continual improvement with many aspects of kōhanga reo operations. They use many self review tools to identify areas of strength and areas where improvements can be made. Programme evaluation, ohu reporting, assessment and whānau hui provide a sound foundation for self review to occur.

Strategic planning. Whānau and staff members use Te Aratūāpae goals as the basis for guiding their future direction. Priorities and goals for future development have been documented and are known by some whānau members. Whānau members now plan to develop a management support plan to help them to realise their goals and future aspirations.

Whānau management. The whānau manages the kōhanga reo well in the interests of the children. The ohu management system is working well and is inclusive of all whānau members. Decisions are made by whānau members. Regular hui are held to enable whānau to manage their kōhanga reo. These hui are well attended.

Areas for development

Regular review of the kōhanga charter and policies and procedures. Some of the kōhanga policies and procedures are out of date or do not adequately guide the whānau well in their daily practices. The whānau and staff agreed that it is now timely to review all policies and procedures with a particular emphasis on health and safety.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Te Kōhanga Reo o Raukawa Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff ofcompleted an EROKōhanga Whānau Assurance StatementandSelf-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendation

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • whānau and staff review the kōhanga reo charter including policies and procedures.

6 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

To the Parents and Community of Te Kōhanga Reo o Raukawa

These are the findings of the Education Review Office’s latest report on Te Kōhanga Reo o Raukawa.

Raukawa Te Kōhanga Reo is in Ngati Kapumanawawhiti in Ngati Raukawa. The immediate environment surrounding the kōhanga reo includes a wharenui, urupa and whare karakia. Children attend kōhanga reo at their local marae.

The whānau manages the kōhanga reo well in the interests of children. Whānau members make decisions at hui. Regular whānau hui are held to support decision making and ensure management practices are known to whānau.

Children are immersed in a marae based environment that includes learning about hāpu and iwi. Children are very well supported to use and learn te reo Māori. They are provided with learning experiences that extend and challenge them.

  • Children are comfortable and confident in their environment.
  • Children determine their learning through activities based on their interests.
  • Children learn about karakia, healthy routines and other self managing practices.
  • Children experience caring, nurturing and responsive interactions towards developing relationships.
  • Children are developing confidence in using te reo Māori in a range of situations.
  • Children demonstrate the values of manaakitanga, whanaungatanga, wairuatanga and ukaipotanga with each other and with adults.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to student achievement and useful to this kōhanga reo.

If you would like a copy of the full report, please contact the kōhanga reo or see the ERO web page, http://www.ero.govt.nz.

General Information About Reviews

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
