2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kei te Kura Tini o Poike ki Tauranga Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike e tū ana. E whakawhirinaki atu ana te kōhanga ki te Polytechnic i te mea ko ngā tamariki a ngā tauira e ako ana i reira te nuinga o rātou e whakauru mai ana. Haere mai ai ētahi o ngā whānau o te hapori hoki. I tēnei wā kaore i tino pakeke ngā tauira, arā kaore a rātou tamariki. Nā tēnei ka whakapā te nui o ngā tamariki i runga i te rārangi ingoa i te kōhanga.

Na te whānau i tohu ko Mana Reo te wāhanga arotahi mō tēnei arotake. He kaha ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. He tika te reo e rongo ana ngā tamariki. Tautokongia ai ngā tamariki hou me o rātou mātua inā koa ki te whanake i te reo Māori. He pai ki ngā tamariki ngā waiata ngā mahi-ā-ringa me te karakia i te wā kotahi. He pai te ako i te reo ki ngā tamariki mai i ngā pikitia, pūrākau, ngā whakaaturanga i runga i ngā pakitara me ngā rauemi whai kounga.

He pai te tiaki i te whenua me te whare. Ka whakarato wāhi pai mō ngā taonga tākaro me ngā karaihe hei tākaro i waho. He wātea a roto i te whare, he mā, pai hoki te āhua. E whakatenatenangia ana te whānau o te kōhanga mai i te nui o ngā tamariki pakeke, ngā tamariki hou me ngā hapa rerekē o ia tamaiti. Mā te whakarite me te tohu i ngā ngohe hei hāpai i ngā takenga o ngā tamariki ka wātea mō ngā tamariki ki te whai i o rātou ake hiahia. Mā tēnei ka taea te arotahi i ngā kōrerorero hei tautoko i te whanake o te reo o ngā tamariki.

He mārama te ārahi i ngā kaimahi me te whānau mai i ngā tuhinga o te mahere. Kua timata kē ngā kaimahi ki te kohi kōrero mō ngā ako o ngā tamariki. Kei te whakaritea a rātou pukapuka pūkete. Me timata ngā kaimahi inaianei ki te kōrero ki te arotake okawa, aro mātai i ia wā, ka whakaatu i ngā putanga ki te hui whānaui e pā ana ki hōtaka ako, te ahu whakamua o te reo o ngā tamariki, ngā whakahaere me ngā whakaritenga.

Kāore anō kia āta whanakehia ngā mahi arotake whaiaro a te whānau. Mena ka kaha ake ngā mahi arotake whaiaro me te tuhi pūrongo ki te whānau ka āta mōhio rātou mō ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa hei whakamahere hei whakaaetanga mō ngā rā e tū mai nei.

He wāhanga kei roto i ngā whakahaere hei whakatikatika. He mea nui te whakaako i ngā whānau hou ki te horopaki o te kōhanga. Kāore a rātou mahere rautaki hei whakatakoto i o rātou whakangākau, whāinga me ngā paetae.

Kei te ū ngā whakahaere o te kōhanga me ngā mahi mō ia ra. Heoi, he ruarua nō ngā tāngata kaore e kapi ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga. Nā te pūmanawa me te mātanga o ngā mema e mau mai ana ki te kōhanga ka taea te hiki ake i ngā wāhanga i kitea i roto i tēnei pūrongo hei whakapai ake.

Ngā Mahi ā Meake Nei

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te pupuritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Reo

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake matauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aro matawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Reo

He whakamārama

Na te whānau i tohu ko Mana Reo te aotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ko te reo Māori te reo o te kōhanga. Kei te tautoko te whānau i te rōpū tamariki hou kua tae mai ki te kōhanga i mua tata nei. He wero ki ngā kaimahi matatau ki te reo ki te ako i ngā tamariki iti noa te reo. Heoi, ka kōrerotia te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te reo Māori. Ko te reo Māori te reo o te kōhanga. He matatau te kaiako i roto i te reo. He kaha ia ki te tautoko i ngā kaimahi. Ka kōrero ngā pakeke i roto i te reo Māori i a rātou e whakawhiti kōrero ana ki ngā tamariki. He kaha ngā tamariki hou ki te whakarongo ki te whakauru atu. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi ki te whakauru mai i a rātou, ki te noho i te taha o ia tamaiti, rōpū rānei. Kei te waia haere rātou ki te reo Māori.

Mai i ngā rauemi whai kounga ka taea te honohono i ngā kupu ki ngā taputapu, pikitia, ngā tūtohi me ngā mahi ā-tinana. He pai ki ngā tamariki ngā waiata ngā mahi ā-ringa me ngā karakia i te wā kotahi. He pai hoki kia rātou te nui o ngā pukapuka rerekē me te pānui i o rātou ake pukapuka pūkete. He maia ngā tamariki pakeke ki te whakahua i o rātou pepeha whakapapa hoki. He pai rātou ki te kōrero i roto i te reo Māori he kaha hoki ki te kōrero ki a rātou ano.

Te taiao o te kōhanga. I āta whakatūngia te kōhanga i te whenua o Te Kura Tini o Poike.Mārama te kite mai i te huarahi. He pai te tiaki i te whare me te whenua. Ka whakarato wāhi pai mō ngā taonga tākaro me ngā karaihe hei tākaro i waho. He wātea a roto i te whare, he mā, pai hoki te āhua. Mai i ngā whakaaturanga me ngā whakaahua ka whakaongaonga i te ako. E whakaatu mai ana ngā kōrero hei āwhina i te whānau pērā i ngā karakia, kīwaha, waiata, kupu hou me ngā pukapuka whakahaere. E taunga ana ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao, ā, ka wātea ki te pā atu ki ngā rauemi.

Ngā kaupapa ako. He arotahi ngā kaupapa ako. Kei te mōhio ngā tamariki ki ngā whakahaere o te ata, tāpiri atu i te karakia himene mihimihi, i muri mai ka whai wāhi mō ngā pepeha me ngā whakapapa. Ka whai wā ngā tamariki ki te ako, ki te mātakitaki i ētahi horopaki pārekareka. He pai hoki tō rātou whakahoki pātai. Ka whakaatu mai te ngākau whakapuke me te kaingākau maioha.

He maha he rerekē he ataahua ngā rauemi. E iri ana ngā tutohi tae maha, i te teitei o te tamaiti. He pai te whakahaere a te kaiako i ana mahi, ka whakauru he waiata me ngā mahi ā-tinana i ia wā. Kei te mōhio ia ko ēhea o ngā tamariki kaore i te aro atu. I te mutunga o te wā ka noho ia ki te kōrero ki a rātou. Kei te aro atu ngā tamariki kei te whakamahi i ngā ngohe.

Ngā rauemi.Ka whai wā ngā tamariki ki te pā atu ki ngā rauemi huhua, whai kounga. Mai i ēnei ka taea te atawhai i ngā tamariki katoa arā ka pā atu mō te roānga o te rā. He kaha rātou ki te tiaki i ngā rauemi, ki te āwhina ki te whakahoki. Whakamahia ai ngā rauemi o waho me roto i ngā wā katoa.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te atawhai i ngā takenga me ngā hapa o ia tamaiti. I ētahi wāhanga o te rā me āta tika te whakarite kia ū ai ngā takenga me ngā hapa o ia tamaiti. Me kōrero te whānau mō te tohu me te whakarite ngohe ōrite ki ngā hapa o ia tamaiti. Me kaha ake ngā kaiako ki te whakauru atu ki ngā ngohe kia mau te aronga o ngā tamariki ā kia kore ai e whakapōrearea i ētahi atu. Me āta whakaaro mō ētahi wāhanga o te ra, ina koa te wā e wātea ana ngā tamariki ki te tohu ngohe mōna ake. Me mōhio ngā kaimahi kia tika te whakarite i ngā mahi.

Whakawhanaungatanga. E whakatenatenangia ana te whānau o te kōhanga mai i te nui o ngā tamariki pakeke, ngā tamariki hou, me ngā hapa rerekē o ia tamaiti. Me matatū te whānau ki te aroturuki i ngā nekenekehanga, ngā mahi me ngā whanonga i te kōhanga. Me arotahi ake ngā kōrerorero inā koa ki te tautoko i ngā tamariki e whanake ana i te reo Māori. Ko ngā kōrero i rangona i te wā kai me haere tonu i waenga o ngā mahi katoa i te kōhanga.

Te whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

E aromātai ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i ngā wā katoa, i te kounga o ngā whakamahere me ngā aro mātai i roto i ngā arotake katoa. I roto i tēnei tukanga me tāpiri atu te aromātai i te kounga o ngā whakahaere a te whānau me te mahi tahi, me te whakamahere rautaki, arotake ā-roto, whakamahere kaupapa, armātai hōtaka me ngā mahi aro matawai.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ngā whakamahere. He mārama te tuhi i ngā mahere hei whakarato ārahi mō ngā kaimahi me te whānau. E whakaatungia mai ana ngā mahere mō te wāhanga mō ia wiki. Ka tāpiri atu i te whanaketanga o te kaupapa, ngā karakia, waiata whakataukī me ngā kīwaha hei awhi i te kaupapa. Kua tuhia hoki ngā ngohe, rauemi me ngā tūnga whakahaere mō ngā kamahi. He pai te whakamahere o te hōtaka ako mō nga tamariki.

Aromatawai. Kua tīmata ngā kaimahi ki te kohi kōrero mō ngā ako o ngā tamariki. Kei te whanakehia ngā pukapuka pūkete mō ia tamaiti. Ko te nuinga o ngā tamariki he hou ki te kōhanga. E whai kōrero ana ngā mahi a ngā tamariki, kua oti te tāpiri atu i te rā i mahia, ā he whakaahua hoki o rātou e tākaro ana. Ka tarea e ngā mātua te pā atu ki ēnei. He pai ki ngā tamariki te pānui i a rātou pukapuka pūkete.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Aromātai. Kāore he tuhinga e pā ana ki te aromātai o te hōtaka ako. Me tīmata ngā kaimahi ki te hui i ia wā ki te kōrero ki te tuhi i te ako o ngā tamariki mai i te kounga o ngā ngohe, ngā whakahaere me te whanake o te reo Māori. Me tuku pūrongo ōkawa ki te whānau kia mōhio ai rātou mō te ahu whakamua, me te whanaketanga o ngā tamariki. Me whai whakaaro ki te whakamahi i ētahi atu momo rautaki aromātai pērā i ngā tirohanga me ngā kōrerorero.

Mahere rautaki. Kāore a rātou mahere rautaki hei whakatakoto whakangākau, whāinga me ngā paetae. He hou te nuinga o ngā mema. Kāore anō kia whakaritea he wānanga kia tīmata ai rātou ki te whakamahere me te tuhi whāinga mō ngā tau ki mua tāpiri atu i te whanake ako mō te whānau ki te whakarite whāinga poto whāinga roa hoki.

Te whanake arotake whaiaro mō te whānau. Kāore anō kia āta ū ngā mahi arotake whaiaro o te whānau. Ko te arotahi o ngā take i ngā hui ā-marama ko te arotake i te pūtea me te pūrongo. Kāore e kaha ana te arotake me te whai pūrongo mō ētahi atu wāhanga o te kōhanga pēra i ngā rawa, te hauora me te waiora, ngā paetae o te kōhanga, me ngā ako mō ngā tamariki. He nui tonu ngā kaupapa here, kaupapa whakahaere hoki hei whakarite e te whānau, tāpiri atu i ngā wharanga, whakahaere pūtea, me ngā wawata mō ngā whakahaere a te whānau. Me kaha ake ngā mahi me te tuku pūrongo ki te whānau kia mōhio ai rātou mō ngā wāhanga whakahaere kia kaha ai rātou ki te whakamahere ki te whakaae i ngā mahere mō ngā tau ki mua.

Ngā whakahaere kaimahi. Me whakakaha ake te tautoko mō ngā kaimahi i ētahi o ngā wāhanga o te kōhanga. Kāore e nui ngā kaupapa here, kaupapa whakahaere hei ārahi i te whānau i ngā whakahaere kaimahi tāpiri atu i ngā arohaehae, ngā tikanga whakauru kaimahi, te whanake ngaiotanga me te whakarite tuhinga mō te tūranga mahi mō ia kaimahi. E mōhio ana te whānau ko ngā whakahaere kaimahi tetahi wāhanga hei whakatikatika kia taea te tautoko i ngā kaimahi.

Ngā whakahaere a te whānau. Kei te u ngā whakahaere o te kōhanga me ngā mahi mō ia ra. Heoi, he ruarua nō ngā tāngata kaore e kapi ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga. Kāore anō kia whakahaerengia he hōtaka arataki kia whai māramatanga ngā mema hou e pā ana ki ngā whakahaere, ngā tūranga i raro i te kaupapa o te kōhanga.

5 Ngā Kupu Tūturu ā te Whānau Whakahaere mō ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakiia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro.  I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture pa ana ki: 

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau;
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga;
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi;
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Matauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino);
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho);
  • ngā tohu mātaurangame ngā whakaritengakaimahi; me
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

I te wā o te arotake i tāutuhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ētahi wāhanga tautukunga kāore i tutuki. Kia taea ai te whakatikatika i ēnei, me mātua whakarite te whānau o te kōhanga ki te:

  1. whakangungu i ngā kaimahi katoa ki ngā whakawai e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū, i roto hoki i ētahi atu ohoreretanga, kia whakahaeretia ēnei i ia wā kātahi ka tuhi. [R24 (3) Ture Mātauranga (ECE) 1998]

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike is located in the grounds of the Bay of Plenty Polytechnic Institute, Tauranga. The kōhanga relies heavily on the enrolment of children of students studying at the polytechnic. Families living in the area bring their children here too. Currently students at the polytechnic are younger and don’t have children. This has had an impact on the current number of children on the roll.

The whānau chose Mana Reo as a focus area for this review. Kaimahi speak te reo Māori well. Children hear appropriate language. New children to the kōhanga and their parents are supported by the whānau particularly in te reo Māori development. Children enjoy waiata and actions and reciting karakia together. Children’s te reo Māori learning is presented well through pictures, stories, wall displays and good quality resources.

The property and buildings are properly maintained. The outdoor areas provide good spaces for play structures and grassed areas for play. The indoors are spacious, clean and inviting.

The kōhanga whānau are challenged with the number of older and new children and their various needs. The organisation and selection of activities to cater for children’s interests should allow them to make independent choices. Interactions can then be more focused in supporting children’s te reo Māori development.

Kaimahi and the whānau are clearly guided by detailed planning. Kaimahi have begun gathering information about children’s learning. Individual profile books are being developed. Kaimahi now need to discuss, formally review and evaluate and regularly present findings at whānau hui about the learning programme, children’s progress in te reo Māori activities and routines and organisation.

Whānau self review is not fully developed. Strong review practices and reporting to whānau should keep members well informed about all areas of management for future planning and decision making.

There are areas in management and administration that require attention. The introduction to and understanding of the kōhanga context is important for new families. Whānau do not have a strategic plan that sets out their collective aspirations, goals and objectives.

The kōhanga is adequately administered and overall daily operation standards are maintained. However, management do not have enough people to consistently cover all areas of administration and management. The calibre and experience members bring to the kōhanga ensure there is ample capability to address areas identified for strengthening in this report.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.  This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Reo

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

4 Findings

Mana Reo

Background

The kōhanga whānau selected Mana Reo as the review focus. Te reo Māori is the preferred language of the kōhanga. The whānau are supporting a group of new children who have arrived recently. Kaimahi knowledge of te reo Māori and how to work with children with limited language will be a challenge. However, te reo Māori will be spoken consistently.

Areas of good performance

Te Reo Māori. Te reo Māori is the preferred language at the kōhanga. The kaiako is a fluent speaker of te reo Māori. She supports kaimahi well. Te reo Māori is used consistently by adults in conversations with children. New children listen well and try to participate. Kaimahi involve them as much as possible or plan to spend some time with individuals or groups. They are becoming more accustomed to te reo Māori.

Good quality resources help link relationship of words to items, pictures and charts as well as actions. Children enjoy waiata and actions and reciting karakia together. They also enjoy the wide variety of Māori story books and looking at their own profile books. The older children confidently share pepeha and whakapapa. They are good speakers of te reo Māori and converse freely.

The kōhanga environment. The kōhanga is placed strategically in the grounds of the Bay of Plenty Polytechnic Institute. It is clearly visible. The property and buildings are well maintained. The outdoor areas provide good spaces for play structures and grassed areas for play. The indoors are spacious, clean and inviting. Displays and photographs stimulate learning. Information for whānau support is displayed including karakia, kīwaha, waiata, new words and administrative documents. The children are familiar with their environment and have easy access to resources.

Kaupapa learning. Kaupapa learning is focused. Children are familiar with the morning routine, which includes karakia, hīmene, greetings followed by pepeha and whakapapa sharing. Children are given interesting contexts to study and observe. Children respond positively to questions. They show enthusiasm and interest.

Support resources are varied and attractive. Colourful charts are displayed at the children’s height. The kaiako paces her delivery well interspersing it with waiata and actions. She is also aware of children who are not actively involved. She spends time with these children at the end of each session. Children are engaged and participate well in activities.

Resources. Children have access to an extensive range of good quality resources. The range caters well for children and they have access to these throughout the day. Children take good care of resources and help to put them away. Resources for both the indoor and outdoor areas are used well.

Areas for improvement

Catering for individual interests and needs. There are periods during the day that should be organised to cater better for children’s individual interests and needs. Whānau must discuss the selection and preparation of activities based on children’s individual needs. Kaimahi need to be more actively involved in these activities to keep children purposefully engaged to alleviate negative behaviour. More focused attention to specific parts of the day is required, particularly when children self select an activity. Kaimahi need to ensure appropriate provision is made.

Relationships. The kōhanga whānau are challenged with the number of older and new children and their varying needs. The whānau need to be more vigilant in monitoring movement, activity and behaviour across the kōhanga. Interactions could be more focused, particularly to support children’s use of te reo Māori development. Good conversations heard at the kōhanga meal table need to occur in other activities across the kōhanga.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in all reviews. This process includes evaluating the quality of whānau management and participation, strategic planning and internal review, kaupapa planning, programme evaluations and assessment practices.

Areas of good performance

Planning. Planning is detailed and provides clear guidance for kaimahi and the whānau. Term and weekly programmes are displayed. These include the development of the kaupapa, supporting karakia, waiata, whakatauki and kīwaha. Activities, resources and kaimahi roles and responsibilities are documented. The learning programme is well planned for the children.

Assessment. Kaimahi have begun gathering information about children’s learning. Individual profile books are being developed. Many of the children are new. Children’s art samples and photographs of children participating in activities are dated with comments attached. These are accessible to parents. The children enjoy reading these.

Areas for improvement

Evaluation. There is no documented evaluation of the learning programme. It is timely for kaimahi to meet regularly, discuss and document evidence of children’s learning, quality of activities, routines and children’s te reo Māori development. Formal reporting to whānau hui is required to keep parents informed about their children’s progress and development. They should also employ observations and conversations as other assessment strategies.

Strategic planning. Whānau do not have a strategic plan that sets out their collective aspirations, goals and objectives. Most members are new. Wānanga are yet to be held to begin planning and documenting their goals for the future including whānau learning and development of long and short term goals.

Further development of whānau self review. Whānau self review has not fully developed. Monthly hui focus strongly on financial review and reporting. Regular review and formal reporting for other areas of the kōhanga is weak, including property, health and safety, kōhanga objectives and learning for children. A number of policies and procedures need to be developed by the whānau, including accidents, financial management and overall whānau management expectations. Strong review practices and reporting to whānau should keep members well informed about all areas of management for future planning and decision making.

Personnel management. Kaimahi support in the workplace in a number of areas needs to be strengthened. There are insufficient policies and procedures to successfully guide whānau to implement personnel management including appraisal, appointments, kaimahi professional learning and the development of relevant job descriptions for each kaimahi. Whānau acknowledge that personnel management is an area that needs more attention to support kaimahi.

Whānau management. The kōhanga is adequately administered and overall daily operation standards are maintained. However, management does not have sufficient whānau assistance to consistently cover all areas of administration and management. No whānau induction has occurred to help members understand their management roles and responsibilities in the context of the kōhanga.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist.  In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

During the course of the review ERO identified areas of non-compliance. In order to address these the kōhanga whānau must:

  1. ensure that all kaimahi are trained in fire and earthquake drills, and in other emergency procedures, and that regular evacuation drills are carried out and recorded [R24 (3) Education (ECC) Regulations 1998]

6 Recommendations

ERO recommends the kōhanga whānau:

  • work with the kaupapa kaimahi to address the areas for improvement identified in this report for further development.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

To the Parents and Community of Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike

These are the findings of the Education Review Office’s latest report on Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike is located in the grounds of the Bay of Plenty Polytechnic Institute, Tauranga. The kōhanga relies heavily on the enrolment of children of students studying at the polytechnic. Families living in the area bring their children here too. Currently students at the polytechnic are younger and don’t have children. This has had an impact on the current number of children on the roll.

The whānau chose Mana Reo as a focus area for this review. Kaimahi speak te reo Māori well. Children hear appropriate language. New children to the kōhanga and their parents are supported by the whānau particularly in te reo Māori development. Children enjoy waiata and actions and reciting karakia together. Children’s te reo Māori learning is presented well through pictures, stories, wall displays and good quality resources.

The property and buildings are properly maintained. The outdoor areas provide good spaces for play structures and grassed areas for play. The indoors are spacious, clean and inviting.

The kōhanga whānau are challenged with the number of older and new children and their various needs. The organisation and selection of activities to cater for children’s interests should allow them to make independent choices. Interactions can then be more focused in supporting children’s te reo Māori development.

Kaimahi and the whānau are clearly guided by detailed planning. Kaimahi have begun gathering information about children’s learning. Individual profile books are being developed. Kaimahi now need to discuss, formally review and evaluate and regularly present findings at whānau hui about the learning programme, children’s progress in te reo Māori activities and routines and organisation.

Whānau self review is not fully developed. Strong review practices and reporting to whānau should keep members well informed about all areas of management for future planning and decision making.

There are areas in management and administration that require attention. The introduction to and understanding of the kōhanga context is important for new families. Whānau do not have a strategic plan that sets out their collective aspirations, goals and objectives.

The kōhanga is adequately administered and overall daily operation standards are maintained. However, management do not have enough people to consistently cover all areas of administration and management. The calibre and experience members bring to the kōhanga ensure there is ample capability to address areas identified for strengthening in this report.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to student achievement and useful to this kōhanga reo.

If you would like a copy of the full report, please contact the kōhanga reo or see the ERO web page, http://www.ero.govt.nz.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
