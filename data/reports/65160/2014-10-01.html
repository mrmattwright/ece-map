1 Evaluation of Early Childhood on Stafford

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Early Childhood on Stafford is a privately owned and operated education and care centre. It is centrally located in Dunedin city, close to local schools and other community facilities.

The centre provides an all-day programme for up to 50 children aged from birth to five years. Children attend the centre for a minimum of 30 hours a week. Infants and toddlers are in a separate room to the over-two year olds.

The centre has twelve teachers, several of whom are males. One teacher is about to undertake training in early childhood education. All others are qualified early childhood teachers.

Teachers aim for children to be confident and competent learners. They have revisited the centre philosophy. It reflects their commitment to promote enjoyment, fun and creativity for children while at the centre. They all share responsibility for all children‚Äôs learning and care.

Centre managers are highly involved with children in the daily programme. They model their expectations for best practice for children‚Äôs learning, care and support. The centre has made good progress towards meeting the recommendations identified in the 2011 ERO report. Managers have identified the need to further develop strategic planning and aspects of self review.

The Review Findings

Children benefit from a wide range of interesting and enjoyable experiences, events and activities in and out of the centre. Children receive a rich integration of the arts, technology, mathematics and literacy, science, MƒÅori culture and social skill development during their time at the centre. These are built from the diverse strengths and interests of their teachers and the freedom each teacher has to develop the programme.

The centre‚Äôs philosophy is evident in action, in the way that:

  • children are encouraged to be creative, to self manage and pursue their interests
  • teachers‚Äô focus is to build children‚Äôs confidence, develop their practical skills, and connect them with their home and the wider world
  • a rich and diverse view of children‚Äôs learning is held and planned for by all teachers
  • all teachers interact positively and respectfully with all children.

A positive feature is the strong relationships evident between the teachers, children and parents.

  • They all get to know each other well, sharing trust, warmth and humour.
  • Teachers and children in the under and over two areas enjoy each others‚Äô company.
  • Parents are warmly greeted and made to feel at ease and welcome to join in.
  • Teachers work collegially, sharing ideas for making life at the centre fun for children.

Teachers make good use of the varied, natural outdoor environment. Children have:

  • purposeful, activities that mirror work at home and in their wider world
  • stimulating areas that promote learning and creative play
  • safe opportunities for developing their physical confidence
  • authentic experiences to develop a respect for, use of, and learning about the natural environment.

ICT is well used by children, teachers and managers at the centre. The on-line communication and sharing of children‚Äôs experiences at the centre has increased parent access and input to children‚Äôs learning.

The centre has developed detailed expectations for what five year old leavers can competently achieve. The criteria have been developed by leaders, teachers and parents. They are effectively used to set formal goals for four year old children‚Äôs learning until they leave the centre. Teachers and parents make ongoing contributions to this record over the year. Although it is in its early stage of use, parents view this positively.

Key Next Steps

The centre leaders have identified, and ERO agrees, that there is a need to:

  • develop key goals for long-term strategic planning and action plans for how they will be implemented
  • link key goals to teacher appraisal goals, where appropriate
  • build a shared understanding of what the graduate profile criteria look like in practice.

Following discussion with ERO, leaders intend to explore how their systems for evaluating the quality of children‚Äôs learning stories can be used to inform self review.

Leaders and teachers could now also consider to what extent:

  • key documents include MƒÅori dimensions, such as the philosophy and graduate profile
  • learning stories make clear what teachers have done to support the previously identified next steps in learning.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Early Childhood on Stafford completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

The centre managers need to ensure the timely police vetting of non-teaching staff. (Education Act 1989).
