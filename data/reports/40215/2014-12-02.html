1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ko Tongariro te maunga

Ko Taupō-nui-a-Tia te moana

Ko Te Heuheu te tangata

Ko Ngāti Tūwharetoa te iwi

Ko Te Arawa te waka.

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Puāwaitanga ki Taupō. E karapotia ana te kōhanga reo e ngā tino pou whenua, arā, a Ruapehu, a Ngāruahoe, a Tongariro, a Te Taupō-nui-ā-Tia. I ngā wā katoa, he tohu mahara ēnei ki ngā kaimahi, te whānau, me ngā tamariki mō te hōhonutanga o ngā taonga tuku iho o Tūwharetoa. Ka hāpai tonu ngā kaumātua i te whānau ki te whakapūmau me te whakatairanga ake anō i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo.

Ka kitea te pakari haere o te māia o ngā tamariki i a rātou e tūhura ana i ā rātou taonga tuku iho. Ka rongo rātou i ngā taunekenekeneke papai e whai pūtake ana. Ka kitea te tino ngākau nui o ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro ki ētahi atu, mō te ao e noho nei rātou. He harikoa rātou, ā, he pārekareka ki a rātou te ako ki te taha o ō rātou whānau. Ka whakaongaongatia ngā tamariki i roto i tō rātou taiao ako o te reo Māori.

Ka noho pūmau te whānau ki te mātauranga me te atawhai o te kounga kairangi mō ā rātou tamariki. He tirohanga ngātahi tā rātou, ā, ka arotahi ki te whakatairanga i ā rātou taonga tuku iho, i tō rātou reo hoki. He pai te pakari o te whānau ki te kōkiri whakamua i ā rātou kaupapa matua. Ka arotahi rātou ki te whakawhanaketanga o te whānau me te whakapūmau tonu i te pūtake o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tā rātou kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

He pēhea ngā tamariki e whakaatu i te māia, i te mauritau anō hoki o tō rātou noho?

He māia, he mauritau anō hoki ngā tamariki i te taiao o te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako i te reo Māori ki te kōhanga reo. He pai te tautoko i a rātou ki te ako i ngā waiata, ngā haka, me ngā pepeha. Ka ngākau nui ngā tuākana ki ngā whai wāhitanga ki te ārahi i te whānau i ngā wā karakia. Ka Haereere hei whakapūmau i te ako reo ki ngā horopaki tūturu. Ka tūhura ngā tamariki i te taiao Māori me te ao kikokiko hoki, ā, ki reira rātou ako ai i ā rātou taonga tuku iho, i tō rātou tuakiri hoki.

Ka manaaki, ka whakaute hoki ngā tamariki i a rātou anō. Ka pou here te whanaungatanga i ngā hononga me ngā taunekeneke e tika ana ki te kōhanga reo, hei painga mō te whānau me ā rātou tamariki. Ka whakatauira te kuia i te whakahirahiratanga o te whanaungatanga mō ngā akoranga.

He pakari te tūhura haere o ngā tamariki i te taiao. He pai te whakaritenga o te taiao o te kōhanga reo, kua āta whakaritea hoki ngā mahinga, ā, he mārama ngā tūmanako me ngā tikanga. He pārekareka ki ngā pēpi tō rātou ake wāhi, ā, he pai ngā rauemi me te whakahaeretanga o taua wāhi. Kei te whai hua ngā taunekeneke a ngā kaimahi ki ngā pēpi, ā, ka whakamahi i te reo Māori hei hāpai i te puāwaitanga mai o tō rātou reo. He harikoa, he mauritau hoki ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka whakaarohia ngā matea me te atawhai o ngā tamariki ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga. He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo, hei āta tautoko i ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi. He mārama ō rātou wawata mō ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakamahia aua wawata hei aroturuki i te whānuitanga o te aro me te tupu o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Kua āta whakawhanakehia te arotake whaiaro, ā, ka whai hua te whakamahinga hei tātari i te ahu whakamua, hei whakapūmau hoki i te pai o ngā whakaritenga, ā, hei tāutu hoki i ngā wāhanga ka whakawhanake ake. Kua whakaritea te matawhānui o tētahi mahere rautaki. He aronga mārama tāna mō te whānau.

Ka whakaemihia e ngā kaimahi ngā mōhiohio papai e pā ana ki ia tamaiti. Ka whakaatu ngā pūkete i te whakawhanaketanga haere o te māia, te mōhiotanga anō hoki o ngā tamariki ki ā rātou taonga tuku iho, ki tō rātou tuakiri, ā, ki te reo Māori hoki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake

Kāhore te whānuitanga o ngā matea me ngā hiahia ako o ngā tamariki e āta whakaarohia ana i roto i te waihanga, te kiko, me te whakaritenga whānui hoki o Te Whāriki. E mōhio ana te whānau kāhore i te rawaka te whakamaheretanga o Te Whāriki hei tohu i ngā akoranga, i te pūtake o ngā akoranga, i ngā huarahi hoki ki te whakahaere i aua akoranga mō ngā tamariki.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, ā, e whakaae ana hoki te whānau ki te:

  • whakamahi tonu i te arotake whaiaro, kia haere tonu ai te whakapaitanga ake me te tupu
  • whakapai i ngā mahi whakamahere i Te Whāriki, kia whai pānga nui ngā matea me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki ki te waihanga me te whakapuakitanga o te hōtaka.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Ko Tongariro te maunga

Ko Taupō-nui-a-Tia te moana

Ko Te Heuheu te tangata

Ko Ngāti Tūwharetoa te iwi

Ko Te Arawa te waka.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Puāwaitanga is in Taupō. Significant ancestral landmarks surround the kōhanga reo, Mount Ruapehu, Ngāruahoe, Tongariro and Lake Taupō which are constant reminders to kaimahi, whānau and children of their rich cultural heritage, Tūwharetoa. Kaumātua continue to support whānau to maintain and further promote the kōhanga reo kaupapa.

Children show growing confidence as they explore their heritage. They experience meaningful and positive interactions. Children show a strong interest in sharing with others their thinking about the world around them. They are happy and enjoy learning beside their whānau. Children are stimulated in their te reo Māori language learning environment.

The whānau are committed to high quality education and care for their children. They have a shared vision and focus on promoting their heritage and language. The whānau is well equipped to progress their priorities. They are focused on the development of whānau and maintaining the purpose of kōhanga reo for their children.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

How well do children show they are confident and content?

Children are confidence and content in the kōhanga reo environment.

Areas of good performance

Children enjoy learning te reo Māori at kōhanga reo. They are well supported to learn waiata, haka and pepeha. Tuakana look forward to opportunities to lead the whānau in karakia. Outings are used to reinforce language learning in authentic contexts. Children explore the natural and physical environments where they learn about their heritage and identity.

Children care and respect each other. Whanaungatanga underpins relationships and interactions expected at the kōhanga reo for whānau and their children. The kuia models the importance of whanaungatanga for learning.

Children confidently explore the environment. The kōhanga reo environment is well organised, routines are well established and there are clear expectations and boundaries. Babies enjoy their own designated area which is effectively resourced and managed. Kaimahi interact positively with babies using te reo Māori to support their early development of the language. Children are happy and content.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

Children’s needs and care are used to inform decision making. Whānau manage the kōhanga reo effectively so that children and kaimahi are well supported. They have clear aspirations for children and these are used to monitor the overall direction and growth of their kōhanga reo.

Self review is well developed and used effectively to determine progress, maintain good practice, and identify areas for development. A comprehensive strategic plan is in place. It provides clear direction to the whānau.

Good information is collected by kaimahi for each child. Portfolios show children’s developing confidence and knowledge about their heritage, identity and te reo Māori.

Area for development

Children’s diverse needs and preferences for learning are not carefully considered in Te Whāriki design, content and overall organisation. The whānau recognise that Te Whāriki is not adequately planned to show what, why and how learning for children will be managed.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends, and the whānau agree to:

  • continue to use self review for ongoing improvement and growth
  • improve Te Whāriki planning practices so children’s needs and development underpins programme design and delivery. When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
