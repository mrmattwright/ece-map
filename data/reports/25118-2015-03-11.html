Te Aromātai

Kei Ōtara Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Otinga ki Tāmaki e tū ana, ki te tonga o Tāmaki-makau-rau. He pai te whakawhanaketanga o te māia, te tū pakari, me te tākare o ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Otinga ki Tāmaki hei ākonga. Ka whakamanahia ngā tamariki ki tēnei kōhanga reo. Tērā ka whakahaerehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Otinga Ki Tāmaki i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

Kei Ōtara Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Otinga ki Tāmaki e tū ana ki Tāmaki-makau-rau ki te Tonga. E tū ana te kōhanga reo ki te taha o te kura tuatahi o Wymondley me tētahi atu pokapū mātauranga kōhungahunga. Nō te marama o Hakihea, i te tau 2011 te whānau i whakatūwhera ai i tō rātou whare hou. He ātaahua, he wāteatea hoki te whare me ngā wāhi o waho, ā, he pai te tiaki hoki i aua rawa. I tērā tau, i whakanui te whānau i ngā tau 30 o tāna whakahaeretanga.

Ka kawe mai ngā mema o whānau i ā rātou tamariki ki Te Otinga ki Tāmaki, nā tō rātou hononga ki Ngāpuhi. He mea nui ki te whānau, kia ako ā rātou tamariki i te reo mārika ake o tō rātou iwi, tae atu ki tōna mita, me ngā tikanga.

I whakatau te whānau me Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te māia, te tū pakari, te tākare hoki o ngā tamariki hei ākonga ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo?

He pai te whakawhanaketanga o te māia, te tū pakari, me te tākare o ngā tamariki hei ākonga ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka arotahi, ka whai wā hoki ai ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga. He tino pai te whakatutuki i ngā matea ake o ia tamaiti. E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi, puta noa i te rā, ki ngā wāhi o roto, me te wāhi tākaro anō hoki o waho. He pai te riterite o ngā taumahi ōkawa me ngā taumahi ōpaki, tae atu anō hoki ki ngā taumahi e wātea ana hei whiringa tonu mā te tamaiti. He harikoa te āhua o ngā tamariki, he tau hoki rātou i roto i tō rātou taiao.

Ka ākina, ka tautokona anō hoki ngā tamariki kia tiaki rātou i ā rātou anō, pērā i ngā wā wharepaku, ngā wā kai, me te whakarite, te whakapai anō hoki i ngā taumahi.

He hūmārie, he harikoa, he tau hoki te āhua o ngā tamariki. He pai te mahi ngātahi a te tuakana me te teina, ā, ka whai wāhi hoki rātou ki ngā taumahi takirōpū, takitahi hoki. Ka tautoko ngā tuākana i ngā tēina, ā, he pai anō hoki tā rātou tākaro ki tō rātou taha. Ka kitea te aroha me te tūturutanga o ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā tamariki, me ā rātou taunekeneke hoki ki ngā pākeke. He māro te whanaungatanga puta noa i te kōhanga reo.

Ka kitea te pakari o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou āheinga hei ākonga. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga ako e poipoi ana i te ngākau titikaha ake o ngā tamariki. Ka whakaratoa e rātou i tētahi hōtaka e whakatutuki ana i ngā matea ā-tangata, ā-ahurea hoki o ngā tamariki. Ka āta whakamahia ngā rauemi hei whakatairanga i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ka ākina ngā tamariki kia kawe i ētahi āhuatanga o te hōtaka akoranga, ā, ka tū pakari rātou ki te whakatutuki i aua tūnga.

Kei te pakari haere ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea reo Māori o ā rātou tamariki, ā, ka whakamahia ētahi rautaki hei hāpai i a rātou. Ka tino tautokona ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi me ngā kaumātua kia kōrero Māori ai i a rātou e whai wāhi atu ki ngā tūmomo kaupapa o te hapori. E noho pūmau ana te whānau ki te ako i te reo Māori. Ko te reo Māori e kōrerotia ana puta noa i te kōhanga reo.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te ārahi ngātahi a ngā kaiako me ngā kaiwhakahaere. Ka kitea tō rāua tino ngākau nui mō tō rāua mahi. Ka whakapuaki rāua i ō rāua ake pūkenga, mātanga, pūmanawa hoki, hei hāpai i ngā tamariki me te whānau, ā, hei whakatairanga hoki i te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga.

Ka whai wā ai ngā tamariki ki ngā wheako akoranga whakahihiri, huri noa i te rā. He pai te whakamaheretanga o te hōtaka, ā, ka arotakengia i ia te wā. Ka mahi ngātahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhānui i ngā akoranga a ā rātou tamariki. Ka whiwhi te whānau i ngā mōhiohio papai e pā ana ki ngā akoranga a ā rātou tamariki. Ka tino whai hua ngā tamariki nā te ngākau nui me te whai wāhi atu hoki a tō rātou whānau. He pai te mahi ngātahi a te whānau hei hāpai i ngā huihuinga me ngā kaupapa ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka whai wheako ngā tamariki i tētahi taiao ako e hāngai pū ana ki tō rātou hauora me tō rātou haumaru. Ka ārahi ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He pakari ngā pūnaha me ngā whakaritenga e whakatairanga ana i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki. Ka arotakengia ai aua āhuatanga i ia te wā.

E āta whakatakotohia ana ki ngā pūrongo te kiko o ngā kōrero urupare mā te whānau mō ngā wāhanga matua, tae atu ki te hōtaka akoranga me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā kaimahi. Ka hāpai aua pūrongo i te whānau ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga. Hui ai te whānau i ia te wā, ā, ka tū pakari ki te patapatai. E noho wātea ana ngā mōhiohio ki te whānau.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, e tika ana kia hāngai ake ngā pūkete ki ia tamaiti tonu, ā, me whakatakoto anō hoki i ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki ia tamaiti. I tēnei wā, kei roto i ngā pūkete, ko te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo kitenga, kōrerorero anō hoki e pā ana ki ngā rōpū tamariki.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau kia rapu huarahi ngā kaimahi ki te whakahāngai i ngā pūkete ki ia tamaiti ake.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

Kei Ōtara Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Otinga ki Tāmaki e tū ana, ki te tonga o Tāmaki-makau-rau. He pai te whakawhanaketanga o te māia, te tū pakari, me te tākare o ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Otinga ki Tāmaki hei ākonga. Ka whakamanahia ngā tamariki ki tēnei kōhanga reo. Tērā ka whakahaerehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Otinga Ki Tāmakii roto i te toru o ngā tau.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

11 Poutū-te-rangi, 2015

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Otinga ki Tāmaki is in Otara, South Auckland. Children at Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Otinga ki Tāmaki are developing well as confident, independent and enthusiastic learners. Children are valued members of this kōhanga reo. ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Otinga Ki Tāmaki will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Otinga ki Tāmaki is in Otara, South Auckland. The kōhanga reo is situated alongside Wymondley Primary School and another early learning centre. In December 2011, the whānau opened their new building. The building and outdoor areas are attractive, spacious and well maintained. Last year, the whānau celebrated its 30th year of operation.

Whānau members bring their children to Te Otinga ki Tamaki because many kaimahi are from Ngāpuhi. It is important to whānau that their children learn the specific language of their iwi including the mita and tikanga.

The whānau and ERO determined that the focus of the review would be Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

2 The Review Findings

To what extent are children confident, independent and enthusiastic learners in their kōhanga reo environment?

Children are developing well as confident, independent and enthusiastic learners in their kōhanga reo environment.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are focussed and engaged in their learning. Their individual needs are catered for very well. A range of activities are available to children throughout the day both indoors and in the outside play area. There is a good balance of formal and informal activities including independent activities that children can choose. Children are happy and secure in their environment.

Children are encouraged and supported to take care of their own needs including toileting, meal times, and setting up and clearing activities.

Children are calm, happy and settled. Tuakana and teina work well together and participate in group and individual activities. Tuakana support their teina and play happily alongside them. Relationships between children and their interactions with adults are loving and genuine. Whanaungatanga is strong throughout the kōhanga reo.

Children demonstrate confidence in their abilities as learners. Kaimahi provide learning opportunities that nurture children’s self confidence. They provide a programme that responds to children’s social and cultural needs. Resources are used well to enhance children’s learning and development. Children are encouraged to lead aspects of the learning programme and they undertake these roles willingly.

Children are developing confidence as speakers of te reo Māori. Kaimahi are aware of their children’s te reo Māori language needs and they determine the most appropriate strategies to support them. Children are well supported by kaimahi and kaumātua to speak te reo Māori when they participate in local events. Whānau are committed to learning te reo Māori. Te reo Māori is used well throughout the kōhanga reo.

Planning and Evaluation

Children benefit from the collaborative leadership of the kaiako and kaiwhakahaere. The kaiako and kaiwhakahaere show great passion for their roles. They share their individual skills, experience and talents to support children and the whānau and to enhance the quality of the learning programme.

Children are engaged in interesting learning experiences throughout the day. The programme is planned well and regularly reviewed. Whānau work with kaimahi to extend their children’s learning. Whānau receive good information about their children’s learning. Children benefit greatly from the interest and participation of their whānau. Whānau work well together to support kōhanga reo events and activities.

Children experience a learning environment where their health and safety is given priority. Policies and procedures guide the management the kōhanga reo. There are sound systems and practices that promote children’s health and safety. These are reviewed at regular intervals.

Reports to whānau provide detailed feedback about key areas including the learning programme and kaimahi development. These reports assist whānau with decision making. Whānau meet regularly and feel confident to ask questions. Information is accessible and available to whānau.

Key Next Steps

Kaimahi acknowledge that children’s portfolios need to be more individualised and should include information about each child. Currently, portfolios include a variety of group observations and anecdotes.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the whānau recommend that kaimahi look at ways to individualise children’s portfolios.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Otinga ki Tāmaki is in Otara, South Auckland. Children at Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Otinga ki Tāmaki are developing well as confident, independent and enthusiastic learners. Children are valued members of this kōhanga reo. ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Otinga Ki Tāmaki will be in three years.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori
