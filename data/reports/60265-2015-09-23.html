Background

Te Kainganui is a long established early childhood centre in Mt Cook, Wellington. It operates as a parent cooperative and offers all-day education and care for children aged up to five. The centre is administered by a parent committee.

Children are supported to be active learners and participants in the programme.

The centre philosophy, based on responsive and reciprocal relationships, is central to all decision-making about teaching, children‚Äôs learning and resourcing.

Families and whƒÅnau are encouraged to participate in decision-making and contribute time and expertise to the centre. Regular communication supports parent involvement. Leaders and teachers value the strong support and interest parents have in the centre.

The centre‚Äôs strategic and annual plans identify important areas to guide the future development of the programme, policies and procedures.

Leaders have responded positively to the areas for development in the October 2012 ERO report. The centre continues its good reporting history with ERO.

The Review Findings

The centre is well resourced. Children are managed in a positive way. Teachers are caring and kind in their relationships with children.

Transitions into the centre and to school are flexible and individualised. Teachers support parents‚Äô choices and provide a good range of information to them. Continuing to develop relationships with new entrant teachers should assist sharing information about children and the centre programmes when they go to school.

Teachers are responsive to children‚Äôs interests and emerging ideas. Children have choices about their learning and participation in activities. Highly inclusive practices effectively support families and whƒÅnau to participate in the life of the centre. Teachers value and include families‚Äô cultures, languages and identities in the programme.

Children and teachers work and play together in designated mixed-aged family groupings. Supporting all teachers to further develop their understanding of appropriate programmes and strategies for working with infants and toddlers in group care, is a next step.

Children with additional needs are well catered for in programmes that are responsive to their strengths and needs. Leaders use their knowledge of the assistance available through agencies and the community to support these children, their families and whƒÅnau.

Teachers‚Äô approach to planning continues to develop. They are trialling projects and planning stories to increase the visibility of children‚Äôs learning at the centre. Centre management is focused on improvement. There is a collaborative and inclusive team culture where input from teachers, children, parents and whƒÅnau is sought to guide decisions about the programme and resourcing.

A well-developed, supportive teacher appraisal process provides constructive feedback to teachers about their practices. Teachers use evidence from their teaching to identify their goals and professional learning to continue to improve outcomes for children. A new and more collaborative approach to strategic planning is being developed. Leaders are ensuring it has stronger links to the centre‚Äôs vision, philosophy and evaluation processes. Including indicators of success in the plan will assist leaders to measure progress and identify areas to change or develop further.

The current philosophy review is supporting teachers to articulate and implement agreed values that underpin teaching and learning.

The centre‚Äôs self-review process continues to be developed. Making reviews more evaluative should better support decision-making about next development steps.

Key Next Steps

ERO identified priorities for improvement. These include:

  • strengthening the evidence teachers gather about how they are responding to children‚Äôs interests and helping them progress
  • keeping planning stories visible and current so children can revisit and reflect on them
  • developing teachers‚Äô understanding of appropriate programmes and strategies for working with infants and toddlers.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Te Kainganui Early Education Centre completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.
