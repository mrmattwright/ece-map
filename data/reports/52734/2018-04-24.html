1 Te Horopaki

“Kia uiuia mai, nā wai koe?  Māu e kī atu,
E tirohia atu ki ngā ngaru e aki ana
ki Waipuna, ki te Matapihi Pūtiki Wharanui, ko Ngāti Tūpoho”

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūtiki Wharanui ki Whanganui, ki te marae o Pūtiki, e pā tata atu ana ki te awa o Whanganui. E tū pā tata ana hoki te kōhanga reo ki Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Te Āti Haunui-a-Pāpārangi. Ka tino whakamanahia te manaakitanga e te whānau. Ka noho pā tata mai te nuinga o te whānau, ā, he hononga anō hoki ō rātou ki te hapū me te iwi. Whai muri i te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2014, kua whakakotahingia ngā tūranga o te kaiako me te kaiwhakahaere.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakatinana ana i te ngākau titikaha ki tō rātou Pūtikitanga?

Ka āta whakatinanahia e ngā tamariki te ngākau titikaha ki tō rātou Pūtikitanga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira  

E whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i ngā wheako akoranga e toro atu ana ki tua atu i te kōhanga reo. Ka whakarato te whānau i ngā whai wāhitanga ako kia āhei ai ngā tamariki ki te mātakitaki i te āhua o ngā taunekeneke a ētahi atu i ngā horopaki ōkawa me ngā horopaki ōpaki. Ka whakapuaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tikanga i runga anō i tēnā e tūmanakohia ana e ngā whānau, ngā hapū, me ngā iwi o tēnei takiwā. Ka āta whakarite ngā kaimahi i te taetae atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā huihuinga katoa i te marae. I aua wā, ka kite ngā tamariki i te tū o tēnā me tēnā, hei tangata whenua. Haere ai rātou ki ngā wā karakia ki te marae i ia te wā. Ka tae atu hoki te kōhanga reo ki ngā tino hui whakamaumahara o te hapori, pērā i a Pākaitore. Ka ako ngā tamariki i aua tino pou whenua whakahirahira, me ngā kōrero anō hoki e whai pānga ana ki aua kaupapa. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā kaupapa i te hapori e whakamana ana i tō rātou Pūtikitanga.

E ako ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whakapūmau ana i te noho whakaute o ngā taunekeneke. Ka whakaatu ngā kaimahi i te aroha, te manaaki, me te āwhina, puta noa i te rā. Ka whai wāhi nui mai ngā awhiawhi. E whakarato ana te whānau i te maha o ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te taunekeneke ki ētahi atu. He mauritau, he manaakitanga hoki tō aua taunekeneke. Kua āta whakatōngia ngā mahinga ki te rere o ia rā. He ngāwari te whakawhiti haere a ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmomo kaupapa, puta noa i te rā. Nā tō rātou noho mōhio ki aua mahinga, ka āta tau mai te riterite me te mauritau. Ka taea e ngā tamariki ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o te hōtaka te kawe, pērā i ngā mihimihi me ngā karakia. Ka whakamanahia rātou, i a rātou e tū pakari ana ki te kawe i aua tūranga. He mauritau, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki.

E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi me te whānau, i a rātou e whanake haere ana i tō rātou āheinga ki te kōkiri i ō rātou ake huarahi ako. Kua āta whakaritea ō rātou wāhi ako o roto, o waho hoki ki te kōhanga reo. He wāteatea, he hihiri, he muramura hoki ngā wāhi ako. He wāteatea anō hoki, hei poipoi i ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki. He haumaru te wāhi o waho, he pai hoki te tiaki i taua wāhi, ā, he wāhi anō hoki e whakawero ana, e āta whakamātau ana i ngā tamariki. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā taumahi e whakatairanga ana i te ako tairongo me te reretahi. E whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i ngā wheako akoranga papai e whakatairanga ana i tā rātou ako. He ngākau titikaha tō rātou ki te ako.

E rumakina ana ngā tamariki ki te reo o Whanganui. I te nuinga o te rā, ka kōrero ngā tamariki i te reo Pākehā. Ahakoa taua āhuatanga, ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki e tika ana ki te hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. Ko ētahi o aua rautaki, ko te pānui pukapuka, ko te waiata, ko te tākaro hoki i ngā kēmu, tae atu hoki ki te akiaki, te patapatai, me te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero. E hāpaitia ana te taki a ngā tamariki i ō rātou pepeha. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i te takotoranga o ngā rerenga kōrero e tika ana mā ngā tamariki. Ka noho rātou ki te taha o ngā tamariki i a rātou e tākaro ana, ā, ka whakawhitiwhiti kōrero hei whāngai i ngā kupu kōrero e tika ana. E tino hāpaitia ana te ako a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. E whanake ana ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo o Whanganui.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi ngā tukanga ki te whakamahere i ngā hōtaka me te aromatawai i ngā tamariki. Ka kitea te Pūtikitanga, puta noa i ngā mahere katoa. Ka āta ārahi ngā mahere i te whakatinanatanga o te hōtaka. Kua whakaritea ngā mahi aromatawai. Ka whai aua whakaritenga i te whakatakotoranga o ngā paki ako. Ka aroturukitia, ka pūrongotia hoki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki mā te whānau.

Kua whakaritea e te whānau ngā tukanga ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo. E āta whakatakoto ana te tauāki taketake o te tūtohinga i te aronga nui ki te Pūtikitanga. Kua whakaritea te mahere rautaki. Ka āta whakaraupapa tēnei i ngā kaupapa matua mō te whakawhanake tonutanga o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei. E arotakengia ana ngā kaupapa here. Kua whakatakotohia te tukanga arohaehae. Me whakarite te whānau, kia arohaehaengia ngā kaimahi katoa. Kua whakaritea tētahi mahere ako ngaio mā ngā kaimahi, mō ngā tau e rima. Ka pūrongotia, ka arotakengia hoki ngā pūtea. Kua whakaritea ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. Ko ngā painga mō ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā whakataunga a te whānau.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He ngoikore te aromātai hōtaka. Ka hui tahi, ka whakawhiti kōrero hoki ngā kaimahi mō ngā mahere me ngā tukanga aromatawai, engari, he iti noa te whakaaro huritao mō te hōtaka akoranga. Kāhore te kounga o te hōtaka e aroturukitia ana. He uaua te aroturuki i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka mō ngā tamariki.

Kāhore te kaiako e arohaehaengia ana mō te wā roa. Kia āhei ai ia ki te whakapā atu ki te tino tautoko e tika ana mōna i roto i āna whakaritenga ki te taha o ngā tamariki, me arohaehae hei whakatinana haere i tana mahere whakawhanaketanga ngaio.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakapakarihia ngā tukanga me te āheinga, kia whai hua ai te aromātai i te hōtaka akoranga
  • āta whakaritea te whai pūtaketanga o ngā tukanga arohaehae, me te tāutu i ngā whai wāhitanga e whai pānga nui ana ki ngā kaimahi, kia haere tonu ai tō rātou whakawhanaketanga ngaio.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūtiki Wharanui i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

1 Context

“Kia uiuia mai, nā wai koe? Māu e kī atu,
E tirohia atu ki ngā ngaru e aki ana
ki Waipuna ki te Matapihi Putiki Wharanui, ko Ngāti Tūpoho.”

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūtiki Wharanui is located in Whanganui on Pūtiki Marae and close to the Whanganui river. Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Te Atihaunui-ā-Pāpārangi is also in close proximity to the kōhanga reo. Manaakitanga is highly valued by the whānau. Most of the whānau live nearby and have connections to the hapū and iwi. Since the previous ERO report in 2014, the kaiako and kaiwhakahaere roles have been combined.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children show confidence in their Pūtikitanga?

Children show confidence in their Pūtikitanga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children experience learning which extends beyond the walls of the kōhanga reo. The whānau provides learning opportunities for children to observe how others interact in formal and informal situations. Kaimahi practice tikanga in accordance with expectations of whānau, hapū and local iwi. Kaimahi ensure children attend all events on the marae. During this time, they get to observe their roles as tangata whenua. They also attend karakia on the marae regularly. The kōhanga reo attend significant local historical commemorations such as Pākaitore. Children learn about these important landmarks and the stories behind the event. Children are participants in community events that acknowledges their Pūtikitanga.

Children learn in an environment where relationships are respectful. Kaimahi display aroha, manaaki and awhina throughout the day. Cuddles and hugs are common. Whānau provide children with numerous opportunities to interact with others. These interactions are positive and caring. Routines across the day are well embedded. Children transition smoothly throughout the day. Their familiarity with these routines provides consistency and stability. Children are able to lead aspects of the programme such as mihimihi and karakia. They are affirmed as they carry out these roles with confidence. Children are settled and happy.

Children are well supported by kaimahi and whānau as they develop as independent learners. They learn in well organised kōhanga reo spaces indoors and out. Learning areas are spacious, vibrant and bright. There is plenty of room to cater for children’s learning needs. The outside area is safe, well maintained and provides opportunity for challenge and risk taking. Kaimahi provide activities that promote sensory learning and coordination. Children enjoy positive and rewarding learning experiences. They are confident learners.

Children are immersed in te reo o Whanganui. Children speak predominantly English during the day. Despite this, kaimahi use a range of appropriate strategies to support children’s language development. These include reading stories, waiata and games as well as prompts, questions and conversations. Children are supported to recite their pepeha. Kaimahi model correct sentence structures to children. They sit alongside children as they are playing and engage them in conversation providing the appropriate vocabulary as they work. Children are well supported to learn te reo Māori. Children are developing as early speakers of te reo o Whanganui.

Planning and Evaluation

Kaimahi have in place processes for programme planning and children’s assessments. Pūtikitanga features across all planning. Planning clearly guides the implementation of the programme. Assessments practices are in place. These follow the learning stories format. Children’s learning is monitored and reported to whānau.

The whānau has processes in place for managing the kōhanga reo operations. The charter’s mission statement establishes a strong focus on Pūtikitanga. The strategic plan is in place. It clearly outlines the priorities for the future development of the kōhanga reo. Policies are under review. The appraisal process is in place. Whānau need to ensure that all kaimahi are appraised. A five-year professional learning plan for kaimahi is in place. Finances are reported and reviewed. Health and safety procedures are in place. Children’s interests are at the heart of whānau decision making.

Key Next Steps

Programme evaluation is weak. Kaimahi meet and discuss planning and assessment processes, however there is very little reflection on the learning programme. The quality of the programme is not monitored. It is difficult to monitor the effectiveness of the programme for children.

The kaiako has not been through an appraisal for a long period of time. In order for her to access the best possible support to strengthen her practice with children, a professional development plan will be informed by her appraisal.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements. 

4 Recommendations

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • Strengthen processes and build capability to effectively evaluate the learning programme
  • Ensure appraisal processes are purposeful and identify meaningful opportunities for kaimahi to continue their professional development.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūtiki Wharanui will be in three years.
