Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Hamua ki Whakaoriori, ā, ka poipoi i ngā tamariki me te whānau nō te hapū o Ngāti Hāmua, nō ētahi atu hapū, ētahi atu iwi hoki. He kaha, he motuhake anō hoki te noho ā-whānau o ngā tamariki ki te taiao ako. He wāteatea, he muramura, he whakaongaonga, he hāneanea hoki ngā wāhi akoranga. He māia, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Hamua i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1  Te Horopaki

‘Ehara taku toa i te toa takitahi, engari he toa takitini’

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Hamua ki Lansdowne, i Whakaoriori. Nō te hapū o Ngāti Hāmua te nuinga o ngā tamariki. Nā ngā kaumātua i tapa te ingoa o te hapū o tēnei takiwā ki te kōhanga reo.

I te tau 2013, i haere mai ngā whānau me ngā ākonga o mua ki te whakanui i te ekenga o te kōhanga reo ki te 30 tau. I te tau 2014, i pā mai te pōuri ki te kōhanga reo, i te ngarotanga atu o tō rātou kuia, ā, nā tōna tirohanga whānui me tōna ngākau nui, i whakatū ai te kōhanga reo ki Whakaoriori. Ko te whānau o te kōhanga reo kei te whakarato tonu i te mātauranga me te atawhai e tika ana mō ā rātou  mokopuna.

2  Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te hāpai i ngā tamariki, kia riro i a rātou te mātauranga me te atawhai?

E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki kia riro i a rātou te mātauranga me te atawhai nā te mahi tahi a ngā kaimahi me te whānau.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira 

Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki te kaha o tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga, me te pakari o ō rātou hononga ki ō rātou hoa, ngā kaimahi, me te whānau. He tau, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki. E wātea ana rātou ki te tūhura haere me te whai wāhi atu ki ngā taumahi whakahihiri. He kaha, he motuhake anō hoki te noho ā-whānau o te taiao ako.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao ā-whānau e hāpai ana, e manaaki ana hoki. Haere ai ngā tamariki i ia te wā ki te noho tahi ki te taha o ngā kuia me ngā kaumātua. He maha ngā wā e noho ana te whānau ki te kōhanga reo, ki te hāpai i ngā kaupapa o ia rā, me te taunekeneke ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki.

Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga. He pai te whakaritenga me te tiaki o ngā wāhanga ako. He whānui ngā tūmomo taonga tuku iho, ngā whakaahua, ngā taonga me ngā whakaaturanga, ā, e whakatakoto ana tēnei i tētahi taiao e poipoi ana i ngā tamariki. He maha ngā rauemi, ā, e wātea ana ēnei ki ngā tamariki. He māia, he tau hoki ngā tamariki hei ākonga, ā, e kōkiri ana rātou i ā rātou ake akoranga.

He aronga nui ki te angitu mō ngā tamariki katoa. Ka tautoko te kōhanga reo i ngā hōtaka me ngā taumahi i te hapori e hāpai ana i te kai pai, te hauora, me te waiora. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā kaupapa i te hapori. Ka whakaatu rātou i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, ā, ka tautāwhitia rātou ahakoa haere ai rātou ki hea. E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki i te wā ka whakawhiti atu rātou ki te kura. Ka tūmāia ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki.

He tau, he pakari hoki ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka waiata rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo waiata, ā, ka ngākau nui ki ngā taunekeneke a ngā kaimahi me te whānau, me tō rātou aro nui. Whakawhiti haere ai ngā tamariki ki ngā wāhi o roto, o waho hoki, ki te whiriwhiri i ngā tūmomo taumahi e ngākau nuitia ana e rātou. Ka kitea te noho tahi o te tuakana me te teina i ngā tamariki e tākaro ana, e āwhina ana hoki i ētahi atu. Ka manaaki ngā tamariki, tētahi i tētahi.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kei te heke iho te māhorahora o ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i te reo Māori. Kei te mōhio te whānau me ngā kaiako, ko te āhua o te reo Māori ki te kōhanga reo i tēnei wā tētahi take. Ko te whakamahinga o te reo Pākehā ki te kōhanga reo e pā tōraro ana ki ngā mahi ki te whakapūmau i te rumakitanga o te reo Māori. Kua tae ki te wā, ki te arotake i te reo Māori, puta noa i ngā hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

Ka mahi ngātahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga ki te kōhanga reo. Ka ngākau nui rātou, ka noho pūmau tahi hoki rātou ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. He pakari ngā hononga ki te hapori whānui, ā, ka hāpai ēnei i te āheinga o te whānau ki te whakarato i te mātauranga me te atawhai e tika ana mō ngā tamariki.

Ka aroturuki, ka arotake hoki te whānau i ā rātou whakaritenga me tā rātou whakahaere. Kua whakawhanakehia e rātou ngā pūnaha matua me ngā tukanga matua, ā, he nui ō rātou tūmanako kia pūmau ai te whakawhitinga kōrero me te kawenga takohanga. E noho pūmau ana te whānau ki te tautoko i ngā kaimahi, ā, ka whakarato i ngā whai wāhitanga whakawhanaketanga ngaio i ia te wā. E pou here ana te whanaungatanga, te manaaki, me te tautoko i ō rātou hononga.

Ka whai wāhi ngā mātua me te whānau ki te whakamahere hōtaka mō ngā tamariki. Ka ahu mai ngā mahere hōtaka o te pae tata me te pae tawhiti, i Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Ko te whai pānga o ngā kaupapa ako ki tēnei hapori, e hāpai ana i te ako a ngā tamariki i ngā kōrero, ngā pakiwaitara, ngā waiata, ngā mōteatea me ngā wāhi whakahirahira o te rohe o Wairarapa. He mārama ngā aratohu a ngā kaimahi ki te whakapuaki i ngā hōtaka i whakahāngaitia ai ki ngā matea ako o ia tamaiti me ō rātou ngākau nuitanga hoki. Ka arotahi te whānau ki te whakarato i ngā tino whai wāhitanga, ngā tino painga anō hoki mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore te tūtohinga i āta whakaritea ai. Kāhore he whakapuakitanga kōrero e pā ana ki te hītori, te whakatūnga, me te pūtake o te kōhanga reo. Kua tae ki te wā kia whakarite anō te whānau i te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa here, me ngā tukanga, kia hāngai ai ēnei ki te āhuatanga motuhake, te pūmanawa motuhake anō hoki o te kōhanga reo o Ngāti Hāmua. 

3  Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Hamua ki Whakaoriori, ā, ka poipoi i ngā tamariki me te whānau nō te hapū o Ngāti Hāmua, nō ētahi atu hapū, ētahi atu iwi hoki. He kaha, he motuhake anō hoki te noho ā-whānau o ngā tamariki ki te taiao ako. He wāteatea, he muramura, he whakaongaonga, he hāneanea hoki ngā wāhi akoranga. He māia, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Hamua is in Masterton and caters for children and whānau who affiliate to the hapū of Ngāti Hamua and other hapū and iwi. Children have a strong and unique whānau-based learning environment. Spacious learning areas are bright, stimulating and comfortable. Children are confident and happy.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Hamua will be in three years.

1 Context

“Ehara taku toa i te toa takitahi, engari he toa takitini”

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Hamua is in Lansdowne, Masterton. The majority of children belong to the hapū of Ngāti Hamua. Kaumātua named the kōhanga reo after the hapū from this area.

In 2013, whānau and former graduates celebrated the 30th anniversary of the kōhanga reo. In 2014 the kōhanga reo mourned the loss of their kuia whose vision and passion led to the establishment of the kōhanga reo in Masterton. The kōhanga reo whānau continues to provide good education and care for their mokopuna.

2  The Review Findings

How well are children supported in their education and care?

Children are supported well in their education and care  through the collaborative efforts of kaimahi and whānau.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children display a sense of belonging and close relationships with peers, kaimahi and whānau. Children are relaxed and happy. They freely explore and engage in interesting activities. Children have a strong and unique whānau-based learning environment.

Children are immersed in a supportive and caring whānau environment. Children regularly visit kuia and kaumātua. Whānau are often at the kōhanga reo supporting the day to day activities and interacting with their children.

Children are engaged in learning. Learning areas are well organised and maintained. A variety of artefacts, photographs, taonga and displays create a warm environment for children. Resources are plentiful and accessible. Children are confident, secure and independent learners.

There is a strong focus on success for all children. The kōhanga reo supports community-based programmes and activities that promote nutrition, health and wellbeing. Children take part in community events. They showcase the kaupapa of kōhanga reo and are well received wherever they go. Children are well supported when they transition to school. Children are confident in a range of settings.

Children are settled and confident in their kōhanga reo environment. They sing a range of waiata and enjoy responsive interactions with kaimahi and whānau. Children move freely indoors and outdoors, choosing activities of interest. Tuakana, teina is evident in the way children play and help each other. Children care for each other.

Key Next Steps

Children’s spontaneous and natural use of te reo Māori is declining. Whānau and kaiako highlight a concern for the current state of te reo Māori in the kōhanga reo. The presence of English in the kōhanga reo hampers efforts to maintain high levels of immersion. It is timely to review te reo Māori across the children’s learning programmes.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau and kaimahi work collaboratively to manage kōhanga reo operations. The share a passion and commitment to the kaupapa of kōhanga reo. Relationships within the wider community are strong and support whānau capability to provide appropriate education and care for children.

Whānau monitor and review their practice and operations. They have developed key systems and processes and have high expectations for open communication and accountability. Whānau are committed to supporting kaimahi and provide regular professional development opportunities.  Whanaungatanga, manaaki and tautoko underpin their relationships.

Parents and whānau contribute to programme planning for children. Long and short term programme planning is based on Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Local contexts for kaupapa ako enable children to learn about stories, legends, waiata, mōteatea and important sites throughout the Wairarapa region. Kaimahi have clear guidelines to deliver programmes that are tailored to the individual learning needs and interests of children. Whānau are focused on providing the best possible opportunities and outcomes for children’s learning.

Key Next Steps

The charter is not well organised. There is no introductory section about the historical establishment and context of the kōhanga reo. It is timely that whānau re-organise the charter, policies and procedures to reflect the unique nature and character of Ngāti Hamua kōhanga reo.

3  Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements. 

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Hamua is in Masterton and caters for children and whānau who affiliate to the hapū of Ngāti Hamua and other hapū and iwi. Children have a strong and unique whānau-based learning environment. Spacious learning areas are bright, stimulating and comfortable. Children are confident and happy.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Hamua will be in three years.
