1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ruatahuna ki te mārua o Ruatahuna, ki te pito tonga o Te Urewera.

‘Ko te ngahere te whakaruruhau, te pātaka kai o Ruatahuna.’

E noho ngākau nui ana te whānau ki te whakatairanga i te motuhaketanga o te mārua o Ruatahuna. Ko te reo me te ao o Ruatahuna e noho pūmau ana ki ngā akoranga me te waiora o ngā tamariki.

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te maha o ngā whakawhanaketanga papai ki te kōhanga reo. Ko ētahi o aua āhuatanga, ko te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki. ko te tīmatanga o te reo matatini, ko te toro atu ki te taiao tūturu, māoriori hoki, ko te kaha o te ārahitanga, me te arotahi ki ngā mātauranga me te reo o Ruatahuna. Ko ētahi o ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake i taua wā, ko ngā mahi aromatawai, me te whakamahere i ngā mahi ka whai ake.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te tū pakari o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao?

He māia, he kaha hoki te aro atu a ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana i tō rātou waiora.

Mana Whenua

He pakari ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te ao māoriori, tūturu hoki. Ka haereere ngā tamariki i ia te wā, ki tō rātou hapori, ā, ka tūhura haere i te ngahere, ngā awa, me te whenua. E mōhio ana rātou ki ngā atua Māori ki ēnei taiao, me te noho o te taiao hei pātaka kai. He mauritau ngā tamariki ki tō rātou ao māoriori.

E tupu ana te mōhiotanga, te māramatanga hoki o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou tūnga hei tangata whenua. Mātakitaki ai ngā tamariki i ia te wā i ngā kaumātua e whakatutuki ana i ngā tikanga hei kaikaranga, hei kaikōrero, hei kaiwaiata hoki. He whai wāhitanga mā rātou ki te whakamahi i ēnei tūnga ki te kōhanga reo. Ka rongo i te whanaungatanga, te manaaki, te aroha, me te awhi i aua taunekeneke me ngā tikanga. He pai te aro atu a ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whakamana ana, e whakanui ana hoki i te whai wāhi mai o ētahi atu.

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e mau nei i te hōhonutanga o te reo Māori. Ka whai hua te ako a ngā tamariki nā tā rātou noho tahi me ngā kaumātua me ngā kuia e kōrero ana i te reo o Ruatahuna. Ka whakamahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki, taumahi hoki ka hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ngā hanganga reo o ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā pepeha, ngā whakapapa, me ngā waiata. He pakari te kōrero a ngā tamariki i te reo o Ruatahuna.

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hononga ā-whānau, ā-hapū, ā-iwi hoki. Ka whakamārama ngā kaumātua i ngā pou whenua me ngā kōrero tuku iho e whakakoia ana i te whakapapa o ia tamaiti. He manawa whakahī tō rātou, he tākare hoki i a rātou e whakamahara ana i ngā kaupapa me ngā wheako papai. He kaha te aronga toi whenuatanga, te manawa whakahī hoki o ngā tamariki hei uri o Ruatahuna.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka āta whakamaheretia ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Hui ai ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ia te wā ki te kawe ngātahi i ngā mahi whakamahere. Ka arotahi ngā kaupapa ki Ruatahuna, ā, ka whakatutuki i ngā tūmomo rōpū reanga. Aroturukitia ai te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki i ia te wā, ā, ka pūrongo ki te whānau. He matawhānui te aromātai i te hōtaka, ā, ka whai pānga anō hoki ki ngā whakapaitanga ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. E ako ana ngā tamariki, ā, kei te ahu whakamua hoki.

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i ngā āhuatanga maha o te kōhanga reo. Ka pou heretia te tūtohinga e Te Korowai me Te Whāriki, ā, ka whai hua anō hoki tāna ārahitanga i ngā whakahaeretanga. He matawhāiti te whakahaere a te whānau i ngā pūtea me ngā whakapaunga. Ka pūrongo i ia te wā, i ngā āhuatanga maha o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kua āta whakaritea te whakahaeretanga o te tari. He pai te mahi ngātahi a te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā tino putanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Ko ētahi wāhanga o te aromatawai me te whakamahere hōtaka, kāhore e tino mārama ana. Kāhore anō ngā kaimahi kia āta tuhia te ahu whakamua a ia tamaiti mō Mātai Mokopuna. Nā tēnei, he uaua ki te tāutu i ngā matea ako ake o ia tamaiti.

Me whakawhanake tonu i ētahi āhuatanga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga. Kāhore anō ngā kaimahi kia arohaehaengia. Ahakoa kua whakawhanakehia e te whānau ngā whāinga wā roa, kāhore he mahere ā-tau ā rātou hei hāpai i ā rātou kaupapa rautaki matua. Ko te whakatutuki i aua āhuatanga ka whakapakari ake anō hoki i ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te wāhanga kei raro iho nei, kāhore e tutuki ana ngā herenga ā-ture:

  • me whakarite i te whakatinanatanga o tētahi pūnaha arohaehae auau. [R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA7]

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakawhanake i tētahi mahere ā-tau hei hāpai i ngā kaupapa matua o te mahere rautaki
  • āta tāutu i te ahu whakamua o ngā tamariki, i a rātou e whakamahi ana i te Mātai Mokopuna.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ruatahuna ki te mārua o Ruatahuna. He pakari ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao. Ka whakamana, ka whakanui hoki te whānau i te whai wāhi mai o ētahi atu. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo o Ruatahuna, ā, he kaha anō hoki tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga. He tau ngā tukanga ā te whānau, hei ārahi i ō rātou tūranga whakahaere.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Ruatahuna i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

29 Whiringa-ā-nuku, 2015

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ruatahuna is in te mārua o Ruatahuna, the southern part of the Te Urewera ranges.

‘Ko te ngāhere te whakaruruhau, te pātaka kai o Ruatahuna’.

Whānau are committed to promoting the uniqueness of te mārua o Ruatahuna. Te reo o Ruatahuna and te ao o Ruatahuna are fundamental to children’s learning and existence.

The previous ERO report identified a number of positive developments that were happening in the kōhanga reo. These included children’s sense of belonging, early literacy, the use of the physical and natural environment, strong leadership and focusing on the knowledge and te reo o Ruatahuna. Areas for improvement included assessment practices and action planning.

2 The Review Findings

To what extent are children confident in their environment?

Children are confident and respond positively in an environment that nurtures their well-being.

Mana Whenua

Children confidently explore the natural and physical worlds. Children regularly go on outings in their local community including exploring the bush, waterways and land. They are aware of atua Māori in these contexts and how the environment provides food. Children are at ease in their natural surroundings.

Children are growing in their knowledge and understanding of their roles as tangata whenua. Children regularly observe kaumātua in their traditional roles as kaikaranga, kaikōrero and kaiwaiata. They have opportunities to practice these in the kōhanga reo. Whanaungatanga, manaaki, aroha and awhi are evident in during these interactions and formalities. Children respond positively in an environment that values and respects the contributions of others.

Children learn in a rich te reo Maori environment. Children are fortunate to learn alongside kaumātua and kuia who speak te reo o Ruatahuna. Whānau and kaimahi use a range of strategies and activities to support children’s language development. Children are familiar with language structures relating to karakia, hīmene, pepeha, whakapapa and waiata. Children are confident speakers of te reo o Ruatahuna.

Children are aware of their whānau, hapū and iwi links. Kaumātua describe the ancestral landmarks and stories that affirm each child’s whakapapa. They recall celebratory events and experiences with pride and enthusiasm. Children have a strong sense of belonging and are proud of their Ruatahuna identity.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s learning is well planned. Kaimahi and whānau meet regularly to share planning responsibilities. Kaupapa focus on Ruatahuna and cater for the different age groups. Children’s progress is regularly monitored and progress is reported to whānau. Evaluation of the programme is comprehensive and leads to improvements in children’s learning. Children are learning and making progress.

Whānau manage several aspects of the kōhanga reo well. The charter is underpinned by Te Korowai and Te Whāriki and effectively guides operations. Whānau manage income and expenditure in a prudent way. There is regular reporting on many areas of the kōhanga reo operations. Office administration is well established. Whānau work well together to achieve positive outcomes for children.

Key Next Steps

Elements of assessment and programme planning lack clarity. Kaimahi have not clearly documented each child’s progress in Matai Mokopuna. Consequently, it is difficult to identify children’s individual learning needs.

There are some management matters that need further development. Kaimahi have not been appraised. Although whānau have developed long term goals, they do not have an annual plan to support their strategic priorities. Addressing these will add rigour to kōhanga reo management processes.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

ERO identified the following areas of non-compliance:

  • ensure that a system of regular appraisal is implemented. [R47 Education (ECS) Regulations 2008 GMA7]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • develop an annual plan to support the priorities in the strategic plan
  • clearly identify children’s progress when using Matai Mokopuna.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ruatahuna is in te mārua o Ruatahuna. Children are confident in their environment. Whānau value and respect the contributions of others. Children are confident speakers of te reo o Ruatahuna and have a strong sense of belonging. Whānau have sound processes to guide them in their management roles.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ruatahuna will be in three years.
