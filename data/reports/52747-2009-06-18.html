1. Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kei te hāpori taiwhenua o Raetihi Te Kōhanga Reo o Mō te Whānau e tū ana. Nō te tau nei ka tīmata te nuinga o ngā kaimahi tae atu ana ki te kaiako, ki te kōhanga. I tāutuhia e te pūrongo arotake tāpiri o mua atu nei i te Poutū-te-rangi o 2007 i ētahi take hei whakatika mā te whānau me te āwhina o te kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe. Ko ētahi o ēnei take ko te whai wāhi atu o te whānau ki roto i ngā mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga, te arotake i ngā kaupapa here me ngā tūkanga, te aro mātai i te hōtaka akoranga tae atu ana ki te tuhi i ngā kōrero aro matawai i ngā tamariki. Ki te taha ki ngā hanganga ture e tika ana kia tahuri te whānau ki te whakarato i tētahi wāhi tākaro tōtika ki waho mā ngā tamariki me te whakarite i tētahi pūnaha arowhai pūmate. Kua tino ahu whakamua te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā āwangawanga ahakoa te tautoko takarepa a te kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe.

E ako ana ngā tamariki i roto i tētahi taiao reo Māori e ārahina ana e ngā tikanga Māori. He rite tonu ngā mahi o ia wā e whakatairanga ana i o rātou akoranga karakia, waiata, me ngā mahi ōkawa pērā i te pōwhiri. He wāhanga mātātoa kei roto i te hōtaka, ā, he wāhanga anō kia whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga mauritau. Ka kitea hoki te tino arotahi ki ngā mahi tuhituhi me ngā akoranga mō te rohe kāinga hei akoranga mā ngā tamariki pakeke ake i te rua tau. He mahana, he rata hoki te whanaungatanga i waenganui i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. Ka arotake rātou i ā rātou hōtaka i ia wā, ā, kei te tīmata hoki rātou ki te kohikohi i ngā kōrero aro matawai whai hua e pā ana ki ngā tamariki. Kātahi anō rātou ka tīmata ki te whakamahi i ēnei kōrero ki roto i ngā mahi whakamahere wheako akoranga mō ngā tamariki.

Kua tino ahu whakamua te whānau i roto i a rātou mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga. Kua whakaritea e rātou ētahi mahi kaitukumahi pūmau. Ka arotakengia ngā kaupapa here i ia wā, ā, kua whakaritea hoki he pūnaha hei whakatutuki i ngā take i roto i te taiao ā-tinana o te kōhanga pērā i te hanga i tētahi papa tākaro hou mā ngā tamariki ki waho. He tākare te whānau, e ngākaunui ana hoki rātou ki te whakapaipai ake i te kōhanga me te hāngai ake ki te whanaketanga o te reo Māori mā rātou me ā rātou tamariki. E mihi ana te whānau ki te pākahatanga tōtika o te whakatūnga o te kaiako hou me te ahu whakamua o ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā Mahi ā Meake Nei

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

2. Ngā Whakaaturanga

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I tāutuhia e te pūrongo a ERO o 2007 i ētahi wāhanga e pā ana ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga hei whakapaitanga ake mā te whānau. I te noho kōpipiri tonu te whai wāhi atu o te whānau i roto i ngā mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga, e tika ana kia arotakengia ngā kaupapa here me ngā tūkanga, ā, i te pakari haere tonu te mōhio o te whānau ki a rātou kawenga hei kaitukumahi. Kāore i te pai ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki te aro mātai i te hōtaka akoranga, ā, he mea hei whakapai ake hoki ngā mahi aro matawai.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Te whakahaere a te whānau Kua pai ake te whai wāhi atu o te whānau ki roto i ngā mahi o ia rā me ngā mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga. He hōtaka whakauru whānau hou tā te whānau. Hei te tīmatanga o ia tau ka whakamaumahara atu ki ngā whānau o rātou tūranga me a rātou kawenga. Ka whakaūngia ngā tumanakohanga mō te whai wāhi o te whānau ki roto i ngā mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga. Ka taetae atu ngā whānau ki ngā hui i ia wā, ki reira whakatau kotahi ai i ngā kaupapa. Kua aro tahi rātou ki te whakapai ake i te taiao akoranga me ngā wāhi o te kōhanga. E tū rangatira ana rātou e pā ana ki o rātou whakapaitanga ake me te pākaha takatika o a rātou mahi i runga i te ako o ngā tamariki.

Ngā kaupapa here me ngā tūkanga. Kei te whānau tētahi pūnaha pākaha mō te arotake i ngā kaupapa here. Kei roto i te pānui ā-wiki he tauira o tētahi kaupapa here hei tirohia mā te whānau kia āhei ai te arotake haere i ngā kaupapa here i ia marama. E akiakitia ana ngā mātua kia whai wāhi ki roto i ngā mahi arotake i ngā kaupapa here mā te tuhituhi i o rātou whakaaro, te tāpiri i ngā whakarerekētanga ki te papa tuhituhi a te whānau, me te taetae atu ki ngā hui. Ka whakaaetia ngā kaupapa here ki ngā hui a-marama kia oti te whakaae i ngā whakarerekētanga. E aro tahi ana te whānau ki te whakapaipai ake i te kōhanga kia pai ake te tiaki me te mātauranga e whakarato ana ki ngā tamariki.

Ngā kawenga kaitukumahi. He pai te tautoko i ngā kaimahi i ā rātou mahi i te kōhanga. Kua whakaritea e te whānau ētahi kaupapa here me ngā tukanga pūmau hei whakatutuki i ā rātou mahi hei kaitukumahi. Kei ngā kaimahi katoa he kirimana

mahi, ā, kua hainatia e rātou. Kua whakawāteatia ngā kaimahi hou e Te Tari Pirihimana, ā, kei te whānau he punaha aro haehae whai hua ināianei. Kua ngākaunuitia ngā kaimahi ki ngā whakawhanake ngaiotanga hei whakapai ake i o rātou mōhiotanga, o rātou pūkenga, me a rātou mahi. Kua whakaaetia e ngā mema o te whānau ngā mahere ako mā ia kaimahi me te haina hoki i tētahi tauakī tautoko i tēnā, i tēnā o ngā kaimahi. E whakaaro ana te kaiako ki te whakawātea i ia kaimahi mō tētahi wāhanga o te wiki kia āhei rātou ki te whakaoti i a rātou mahi whakangungu. Kua pai ake ngā hōtaka akoranga mō ngā tamariki.

Te whakamahere mō te wā roa. Kua whakaritea e te whānau he mahere mō ngā tau e rima e ārahi tōtika ana i te ahunga a mea ake nei o te kōhanga. Ko te arotahi matua ko te whakatikatika i te kōhanga, te taiao o waho me te whakahaere i ngā pūtea a te kōhanga. E whakaae ana te whānau kua tae mai te wā ki te titiro anō ki tā rātou ahunga rautaki kia āhei ai te hono o a rātou whāinga ki o rātou moemoea mō a rātou tamariki.

Te arotake whaiaro. Kua whakaritea e te whānau tētahi tūkanga arotake whaiaro. E akiakitia ana ngā mema o te whānau ki te whai wāhi ki roto i ngā mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga. Ka taea e ngā mema o te whānau te whakapuaki i o rātou whakaaro mā te tuhi ki te papa tuhituhi mā kei te tari, te whakautu ki ngā pānui me te whai wāhi ki roto i ngā mahi whakatau kaupapa i ngā hui ā-whānau. Ka tuku pūrongo ngā kaimahi i ia hui ā-whānau e pā ana ki te hōtaka akoranga me te tāutu i ngā wāhi hei whakapai ake kia tutuki i ngā matea o ngā tamariki. He pai te whakamōhio atu ki te whānau.

Te tuhi i ngā ahu whakamua o ngā tamariki. He rite tonu te kohikohi i ngā rekota o te ahu whakamua o ngā tamariki, ā, he pai hoki ēnei kōrero hei whakamōhio atu ki te whānau i te ahu whakamua o a rātou tamariki. Kua whakaritea e te kaiako he tumanakohanga mārama e pā ana ki te tuhi me te tirotiro ki te ako o ngā tamariki. Ka oti e ngā kaimahi he aromatawai i ia wiki mā te whakamahi i tētahi whakaritenga motuhake. Ka aro turuki rātou i ngā paetae a ngā tamariki ki te tuhituhi, te whakamahi i ngā tikanga, ngā tumanakohanga whanonga me te ako ā-kaupapa. Kei te mōhio pai ngā kaimahi ki a rātou tamariki. Ka pupuritia ngā kōrero mō ia tamaiti ki roto i a rātou pūkete, ā, e wātea ana ēnei ki o rātou whānau.

Te wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te whakamahere mō ngā ngākaunuitanga o ngā tamariki. He kōpipiri ngā hononga i waenganui i ngā aromatawai i te ako o ngā tamariki me te whakamahere mō a rātou ngākaunuitanga. Kāore ngā kaimahi i te whakamahi i ngā kōrero aro matawai hei tāutu i ngā akoranga ka whai ake nei mō ngā tamariki. Ko ngā kaimahi kei te whakarite i te nuinga o ngā kaupapa engari he tino kōrero e pā ana ki ngā ngākaunuitanga me ngā kaha o ia tamaiti. Tērā pea ka whānui ake te āhua o ngā hōtaka akoranga, ka nui ake hoki ngā whai wāhitanga ki te whakaongaonga me te whakawhānui ake i ngā tamariki, inā ka hāngai ngā whakamahere ki ngā kōrero aro matawai.

Mana Atua

He whakamārama

I te tau 2007, i pūrongo a ERO i te takarepa te pūnaha arowhai pūmate, te wāhi tākaro ki waho mō ngā tamariki, me te whakaako i te reo Māori.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Te reoMāori. E rūmakina ana ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori, ā, e rongo ana rātou ki te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa. Ka kōrero Māori te kaiako me ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki. Kei te kī mai te whānau kua kaha ake te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori mai i te whakatūnga o te kaiako hou. Kua whakaūngia te tikanga ki te kōrero i te reo Māori i te kōhanga mā te whakarite i ngā wāhi korero Māori, kōrero Ingarihi hoki. E whakahaeretia ana ngā hōtaka ki te reo Māori anake, ā, kua whakaritea hoki he karaehe ako reo Māori mā te whānau. Kei te kōrero Māori ngā tamariki ināianei i a rātou e tākaro ana, ki o rātou whānau hoki.

Te tākaro ki waho. He tōtika te wāhi o waho mō ngā tamariki. Kua hangaia e te whānau tētahi wāhi tākaro hou ki waho ka taea e ngā tamariki te tuhura haere me te whai wāhi ki ngā mahi ā-tinana. Hei tāpiri atu ki tēnei, ka haria ngā tamariki ki te awa, ngā papa tākaro me ngā marae hei whakarei ake i te ako o ngā tamariki ki o rātou hononga ki te rohe e ai ki o rātou pepeha, waiata, me ngā whakapapa. Ka kohi ngā tamariki i ngā taonga mai i a Papatūānuku me te whakamahi i ēnei ki roto i tō rātou hōtaka akoranga.

Te wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te arowhai pūmate. Kāore i te whakahaeretia ngā pūnaha arowhai pūmate. Kua whakaritea e te whānau tētahi pūnaha pai me te whakawhiti kōrero e pā ana ki te whakatū i tētahi kaiwhakahaere rawa hei mahi i ngā arowhai me te pūrongo ki te whānau. Heoi anō, kāore anō te whānau kia whakaūngia te tūranga nei.

  2. whakatū i tētahi kaiwhakahaere rawa kia riro i a ia te kawenga mō te arowhai hauora me te waiora i ia wā me te tuku pūrongo e pā ana ki tēnei mahi.

1. The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga reo o Mō te Whānau is located in a rural community in Raetihi. Most of the kaimahi, including the kaiako, are new to the kōhanga this year. The previous supplementary review in March 2007 identified a number of issues for the whānau to address with the support of the district kaupapa kaimahi. These included whānau participation in the management of the kōhanga, policy and procedure review and evaluation of the learning programme, including documenting children’s assessment. Actions required for compliance were to provide an adequate outdoor play area for children and to put in place a system for hazard checking. The whānau has made significant progress towards addressing these concerns with limited support from the district kaupapa kaimahi.

Children learn in a Māori language setting that is guided by tikanga Māori. They have regular routines that promote their learning of karakia, waiata and formal procedures such as pōwhiri. The programme allows for children to be active as well as give time for settled learning activities. There is a strong focus on writing and learning about the local area for children over two years old. Kaimahi have developed warm, responsive relationships with children. They regularly review their programme and are beginning to gather useful assessment information about children. They are at the early stages of using this information when planning learning experiences for children.

The whānau has made significant progress in their role as managers of the kōhanga. They have developed sound employer practices. Policy review occurs regularly and systems have been developed to address issues in the physical environment including the construction of a new outdoor play area for children. Whānau are enthusiastic and committed to improvement with a particular emphasis on the development of te reo Māori for themselves and their children. They acknowledge the positive impact that the appointment of a new kaiako has made and the progress that their children are making.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

2. Findings

Planning and Evaluation

Background

The 2007 ERO report identified several areas in kōhanga management that needed to improve. Whānau participation in the management of the kōhanga remained limited, policy and procedure was in need of review, and whānau understanding of their responsibilities as good employers was still developing. Evaluation of the learning programme was not well documented and assessment practices needed improving.

Areas of progress

Whānau management. Whānau participation and involvement in the daily operations and management of the kōhanga has significantly improved. There is an induction programme at the beginning of each year where whānau are reminded of their roles and responsibilities. The expectations for whānau participation in the management of the kōhanga are reinforced. Whānau regularly attend meetings where decisions are made collectively. They have focussed on improving the learning environment and premises. They are proud of the improvements they have made and the positive impact this has had on children’s learning.

Policies and procedures. The whānau now has a rigorous system of policy review in place. The weekly pānui includes a copy of a policy to be reviewed, ensuring that policies are reviewed each month. Parents are encouraged to have input into policy review by writing their suggestions, adding amendments to the whānau white board and attending meetings. At the monthly meeting, policies are approved following a discussion of the changes that have been suggested. The whānau is focussed on improvements in order to provide better care and education for their children.

Employer responsibilities. Employees are well supported in their work at kōhanga. The whānau has developed sound policies and procedures for fulfilling their role as a good employer. All kaimahi have signed employment contracts. New kaimahi have been police vetted and a useful appraisal system is now in place. Kaimahi have made a commitment to undertake professional development to improve their knowledge, skills and practice. Whānau members have approved study plans and have signed a statement of support for each member of staff. The kaiako plans to release each kaimahi for a period each week to enable them to fulfil their course requirements. The learning programmes for children have improved.

Long-term planning. The whānau has developed a five year plan that appropriately guides the future direction of the kōhanga. The main focus has been the maintenance of the premises, the outdoor environment and the financial management of the kōhanga. Whānau agree that it is timely to revisit their strategic direction to ensure that their goals are linked to their aspirations for their children.

Self review. The whānau has developed a process of ongoing self review. Whānau members are encouraged to have input into the operation of the kōhanga. Whānau members are able to provide their views in a number of ways including use of a whiteboard in the office that they can write on, responding to newsletters and participating in decision making at whānau meetings. Kaimahi report at each whānau meeting on the learning programme and they identify areas for improvement to meet the needs of children. The whānau is kept well informed.

Documenting children’s progress. Records of children’s progress are gathered regularly and provide good information to whānau about their child’s progress. The kaiako has established clear expectations for observing and documenting children’s learning. Kaimahi complete assessments each week using a standard format. They monitor children’s achievements in writing skills, practice of tikanga, behaviour expectations and kaupapa learning. Kaimahi know their children well. Information about individual children is stored in profiles which are available to whānau.

Area for further improvement

Planning for children’s interests. Links between assessment of children’s learning and planning for their interests are limited. Kaimahi do not use assessment information to identify the next learning steps for children. Kaupapa are largely developed by kaimahi, with little reference to the individual interests and strengths of children. Learning programmes are likely to provide more variety and opportunities for children to be stimulated and extended when planning is based on assessment information.

Mana Atua

Background

In 2007, ERO reported that the hazard checking system, the children’s outdoor play area and the teaching of te reo Māori were inadequate.

Areas of progress

Te reoMāori. Children are immersed in te reo Māori and consistently hear it spoken. The kaiako and kaimahi use te reo Māori as the language of communication with children. Whānau report that the level of te reo Māori used by kaimahi and

expectations of whānau to use te reo Māori has increased significantly since the appointment of a new kaiako. Expectations have been reinforced to whānau with the creation of Māori and English language speaking zones. Children’s programmes are now conducted entirely in te reo Māori and whānau learning classes have been planned. Children are now using te reo Māori in their play and with their whānau.

Outdoor Play. An appropriate outdoor physical environment is provided for children. The whānau has developed and constructed a new outdoor play area where children can explore and participate in physical activity. In addition, whānau make use of visits to local rivers, parks and marae to enhance children’s learning about their links to the area as expressed in their pepeha, waiata and whakapapa. Children gather natural materials and use them in their learning programme.

Area for further improvement

Hazard checks. Systematic hazard checks do not occur. The whānau has developed a good system and has discussed the appointment of a property manager to carry out checks and report to whānau. However, the whānau has not confirmed an appointment to the position.

3. Recommendations

ERO and the whānau developed the following recommendations that the whānau will:

  1. develop a strategic plan that will reflect the needs of the tamariki; and
  2. appoint a property manager to be responsible for regular health and safety checks and reporting.

4. Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.[Signed]

When ERO has reviewed a köhanga reo we ask the whänau of the köhanga reo to let us know how they intend to manage any difficulties set out in their report (copies of which are available from the köhanga reo or ERO). We also encourage whänau to inform their community of any follow up action plan. You should talk to the whänau if you have any questions about this evaluation, the full ERO report or their future intentions.[Signed]
