1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ko Whakaahurangi te marae,

Ko Kahouri te puna roimata,

Ko Taranaki te kaitiaki.

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakaahurangi ki te marae o Whakaahurangi, ki te tāone o Whakaahurangi. E tino hāpaitia ana ngā mema o te whānau e te kuia o te kōhanga reo. He mātātoa tonu tana whakanui i te whakahirahiratanga o te marae hei horopaki mō te ako i te reo me ngā tikanga o Taranaki. Ka hāpai hoki taua aronga i ngā wawata o te hapū me te iwi.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Whenua te kaupapa mō tēnei arotake. E ai ki te whānau, ko tā rātou aronga nui, ko te whakatairanga i te reo o Taranaki me ōna tikanga. He hiahia tō rātou kia mōhio ā rātou tamariki ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae, tō rātou tuakiri ahurea, tae atu ki tō rātou reo me ngā taonga tuku iho. I tonoa e te whānau kia aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i tēnei e whai ake nei, arā:

‘He pēhea te whakawhanake o ā tātou tamariki i te māia, i te mōhiotanga anō hoki ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae hoki?’

E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i te māia me te mōhiotanga ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae hoki.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi, te whānau, me te kuia, ki te whānuitanga o ngā horopaki tūturu e whai pūtake ana, e whakaongaonga ana hoki i a rātou, ā, i konei he mātātoa te tūhura haere, te pakirehua, me te ako o ngā tamariki i tō rātou tuakiri ahurea, te reo, ngā tikanga o te kāinga, me ngā taonga tuku iho. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te tino hononga ki tō rātou whenua, tō rātou marae, tō rātou whānau hoki. He pakari tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki ngā kaupapa i te marae, tae atu ki ngā pōwhiri, ā, he mōhiotanga anō hoki ki tō rātou tū hei tangata whenua. He pakari te whakamahi a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori, inā koa i ngā karakia, ngā waiata, me ngā whakawhitinga kōrero. Ka kitea tā rātou tū pakari, tō rātou manawa whakahī anō hoki mō rātou anō, mō tō rātou tūrangawaewae hoki.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga reo (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Whenua te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Whenua

He whakamārama

‘He pēhea te whakawhanake o ā tātou tamariki i te māia, i te mōhiotanga anō hoki ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae hoki?’

E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i te māia me te mōhiotanga ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae hoki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te tuakiri me te aronga toi whenuatanga. E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki i roto i tō rātou whakawhanaketanga, tuakiri hoki hei tangata whenua. Ka whakarato te whānau i te whānuitanga o ngā wheako akoranga e whakapūmau ana ki ngā tamariki tō rātou tūnga hei tangata whenua. Ka whai wāhi matua te marae hei wāhanga nui o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, i konei ka tūhura anō hoki i ngā hononga ki ētahi atu marae me ngā pou whenua o ngā tūpuna.

Te taiao. Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki ngā taiao akoranga tūturu e whai pūtake ana hoki o te ao Māori. Ka tau wawe te whakaaro ki ngā tamariki ko te taiao o te kōhanga reo kei tō rātou marae anō hoki. Ka tūhura, ka taunekeneke ngā tamariki ki te taiao i ia rā. E tino hāpaitia ana rātou kia whanake ai te hononga ki te whenua. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te mōhiotanga, te noho whakaute anō hoki ki te ao māori, te taiao hoki hei āhuatanga matua o ngā taonga tuku iho me tō rātou tuakiri.

Te waiora. Ko te waiora o ngā tamariki me te whānau e whai pānga nui ana ki te whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga ki te kōhanga reo. He mātātoa te tautoko, te aro atu hoki o te whānau ki ngā matea ā-whānau mō ngā tamariki. I ngā wā katoa ka arotahi nui ki ngā taumata teitei o te manaaki, te hauora, me te waiora ki ngā hui ā-whānau. He mārama ki te whānau te whai pānui nui o te ako a te whānau ki te kōhanga reo, ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka poipoia tō rātou waiora, tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga anō hoki.

Te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Ka tino hāpaitia ngā tamariki i a rātou e whakawhanake ana i tō rātou reo o Taranaki me ōna tikanga. Ka rongo, ka tūhura hoki ngā tamariki i te reo i ngā wā katoa, mā te whānuitanga, te hōhonutanga hoki o ngā tūmomo horopaki e whai pānga ana ki a rātou. E whakawhanake ana te pakari, tō rātou mōhiotanga anō ki tō rātou reo me te motuhake anō hoki o tōna reo ā-iwi. Ka whakatinana rātou i ngā tūmomo kawa e tika ana hei tangata whenua. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te tino mōhiotanga, te maioha anō hoki mō tō rātou reo me ngā tikanga e whai pānga matua ana ki tō rātou tūnga hei tangata whenua.

Whanaungatanga. Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao ā-whānau e poipoi ana i a rātou, ā, e pou here ana hoki i ngā uaratanga me ngā mātāpono o te whanaungatanga ki te katoa o ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga. Ka whai hua te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakanui i te manaakitanga me te aroha ki ngā tamariki me ngā pākeke. Ka whai wāhi nui te kuia ki te whakatairanga i aua uaratanga taketake ki ngā whānau hou i te kōhanga reo. Ka whakamanatia te marae o Whakaahurangi e te whānau hei wāhi whakaako, hei wāhi ako anō hoki i ngā tikanga Māori me ngā uaratanga. Ka ako rātou i tō rātou tuakiri me ngā taonga tuku iho. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i ngā tino ārahitanga hei hāpai i a rātou i roto i tā rātou ako, tō rātou whakawhanaketanga hoki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whai hua o ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau i te kōhanga reo, hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki?

Ka tino hāpaitia te waiora, te whakawhanaketanga me te ako o ngā tamariki mā te whai huatanga o ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau. E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki me tō rātou whānau mā te pai o te whakahaeretanga ki te kōhanga reo. Ahakoa he hou te nuinga o ngā whānau, ka kitea tō rātou tino tākare, tō rātou ngākau nui anō hoki ki te whai wāhi atu ki te kōhanga reo. Ka tāutu tika te whānau i ā rātou ake wāhanga hei arotake ake, hei whakawhanake ake. Ko ngā whakawhitinga kōrero a te whānau i ngā hui, e arotahi ana ki ngā matea ako me te waiora o ā rātou tamariki, me te tauatanga mō te whakahaeretanga o tō rātou kōhanga reo ā meāke nei.

Te whakamahere, te arotake, me te aromatawai i te hōtaka. He pai te whakahaeretanga a ngā kaimahi i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, hei hāpai i tā rātou ako i tō rātou tuakiri ahurea, tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga anō hoki. Ka kitea ki ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki ā rātou wheako akoranga, ā, ka whakanui ēnei i te whanake haere o te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki. He pai te māramatanga o ngā kaimahi ki te pūtake o te arotake i te hōtaka. Ka aroturuki rātou i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka o ia rā ki te whakatutuki i ngā matea o ngā tamariki.

Te wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Te arotake whaiaro. Kua tāutuhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ki te taha o te whānau, e tika ana kia whakahou ake i te maha o ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga ki te tūtohinga, te whakapai ake i te whakatinanatanga o ngā arohaehae mā ngā kaimahi katoa, me te whakatutuki haere, te tuhi haere hoki i ia te wā i ngā akoako hōneatanga mō ngā tamariki, inā he rū whenua, he ahi rānei. Me whakapakari ake ngā pūrongo ki te whānau mō te hōtaka a ngā tamariki, ngā kaupapa rautaki matua, me te katoa o ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

I tāutuhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga e whai ake nei kāhore e whakatutukihia ana e ai ki te ture:

  1. me whakatutuki, me tuhi haere hoki i ngā akoako hōneatanga ka tū i ia te wā mō ngā tamariki, inā he rū whenua, he ahi rānei. [R46 (1) (2) Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008, me ngā Paearu Raihana HS (4-8) mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • āta noho matua te tauatanga a te whānau, kia kōkirihia e te whānau te pai o ngā whakahaeretanga i ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo, tae atu ki ngā pūtea
  • whakawhanake tonu ngā mahi e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro a te whānau, tae atu ki te whakahāngaitanga o ngā pūrongo me te tuhi haere puta noa i ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura-Watson

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Ko Whakaahurangi te marae,

Ko Kahouri te puna roimata,

Ko Taranaki te kaitiaki.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakaahurangi is based at Whakaahurangi Marae, Stratford. Whānau members are well supported by the kuia of the kōhanga reo. She continues to actively promote the importance of the marae as a context for learning the language and customs of Taranaki. This focus also supports the aspirations of the hapū and iwi.

The whānau chose mana whenua as the focus for this review. The whānau report that their emphasis is on promoting te reo o Taranaki me ōna tikanga. They want their children to know who they are and where they come from, their cultural identity, language and ancestral heritage. The whānau asked ERO to evaluate:

‘How well are our children are developing confidence and knowledge of who they are and where they come from?’

Children are well supported to develop confidence and knowledge of who they are and where they come from.

Kaimahi, whānau and the kuia immerse children in a range of meaningful, stimulating and authentic settings where children can actively explore, inquire and learn about their cultural identity, language, local customs and ancestral heritage. Children are developing a strong sense of connection to their whenua, marae and whānau. They are confident participants in marae based events, including pōwhiri and they have an awareness of their special place as tangata whenua. Children use use te reo Māori confidently particularly in karakia, waiata and conversations. They show confidence and pride in who they are and where they come from.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose mana whenua as the focus area.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

whānau self review and strategic planning

programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Mana Whenua

Background

How well are our children developing confidence and knowledge of who they are and where they come from?

Children are well supported to develop in confidence and knowledge of who they are and where they come from.

Areas of good performance

Identity and belonging. Children are well supported in their development and learning as tangata whenua. Whānau provide a wide variety of learning experiences that reinforce to children their place as tangata whenua. The marae is an integral part of children’s learning and is used to explore links to other marae and ancestral landmarks.

The environment. Children are immersed in meaningful and authentic te ao Māori learning environments. Children learn quickly that the kōhanga reo environment includes their marae. Children explore and interact with the physical environment daily. They are well supported to develop an affinity to the land. Children are developing an awareness of and respect for the natural and physical worlds as a critical part of their ancestral heritage and identity.

Wellbeing. Children and whānau wellbeing influence kōhanga reo management and operations. The whānau actively support and respond to whānau needs for children. High standards of care, health and wellness are a constant focus at whānau hui. Whānau understand the importance of whānau learning at kōhanga reo with their children. Children and their whānau are happy and settled in their kōhanga reo environment. Their wellbeing and sense of belonging are nurtured.

Te reo Māori me ona tikanga. Children are well supported in their development of te reo o Taranaki me ona tikanga. Children hear and explore the language constantly, in a variety of rich and relevant settings. They are developing confidence and knowledge of their language and its distinct dialect. They practice appropriate protocols as tangata whenua. Children are developing a strong awareness of and appreciation for their language and traditions that distinguishes their special place as tangata whenua.

Whānaungatanga. Children learn in a nurturing whānau environment where the values and principles of whānaungatanga underpin all interactions and relationships. Whānau and kaimahi promote manaakitanga and aroha successfully to children and adults. The kuia plays an important role in promoting these fundamental values to new whānau at the kōhanga reo. Whakaahurangi Marae is valued by the whānau as the place for teaching and learning about tikanga Māori and values. They learn about their identity and cultural heritage. Children are developing important values to support them in their learning and development.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How well are our children supported in their development through effective whānau management of the kōhanga reo?

Children’s wellbeing, development and learning are well support through effective whānau management of the kōhanga reo.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management. Children and their whānau are well supported through good management of the kōhanga reo. While most of the whānau are reasonably new, they demonstrate strong enthusiasm and commitment to their role in the kōhanga reo. Whānau appropriately identify their own areas for further review and development. Whānau discussions at hui are focused on their children’s learning needs, wellbeing and succession planning for the future management of their kōhanga reo.

Programme planning, review and assessment. Children’s learning is well managed by kaimahi to support their learning about their cultural identity and belonging. Children’s portfolios show their learning experiences and emphasises children’s developing sense of belonging. Kaimahi have a good understanding of the purpose of reviewing the programme. They monitor how well children’s needs are met in the daily programme.

Areas for development

Self review. ERO has identified with whānau the need to update a number of charter policies and procedures, improving the implementation of appraisals for all kaimahi and the need to carry out and record earthquake and fire evacuation drills regularly with children. There should be stronger reporting to whānau about the programme for children, strategic priorities and all health and safety.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Licensed Kōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

ERO identified the following areas of non-compliance:

  1. kaimahi carry out and record regular earthquake and fire drills with children [R46 (1)(2) Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008, andLicensing CriteriaHS (4-8)for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

5 Recommendations

ERO and the whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • prioritise whānau succession for full and effective whānau management of all areas of the kōhanga reo, including finances
  • further develop whānau self review practices, including focused reporting and recording across all areas of the kōhanga reo.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
