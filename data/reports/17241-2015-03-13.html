ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Kaeo Playcentre operates as a parent cooperative. It is located in the small rural town of Kaeo beside the local primary school. It has a strong relationship with the school and this benefits children when they transition to school. The centre provides one early childhood session a week for a small group of children up to six years of age. Most of the children enrolled are of MƒÅori descent.

The centre is part of the Mid North Playcentre Association. The association provides a framework of governance and management systems. It also provides ongoing parent education programmes for members.

Since ERO‚Äôs review in 2011 there have been changes in centre membership. Parents manage the centre and are committed to Playcentre values and philosophy. However they are finding it increasingly difficult to manage the centre, maintain the needed parent education levels, and sustain and grow the centre.

The Review Findings

Children benefit from positive relationships with each other and parents who attend sessions. They are settled and have a sense of belonging in the centre. Children have good oral language skills and have warm friendly conversations with each other and the adults around them.

The centre has an attractive and purposeful environment and this supports children to become confident and capable learners. It provides opportunities for them to experience challenge and to grow in confidence and capability. Toddlers play alongside older children within the session.

The programme is responsive to children‚Äôs interests and they are provided with a good range of play choices. Children play well, both independently and cooperatively. Good centre resourcing supports children‚Äôs play and the activities they choose. Some whƒÅnau engage well with children, providing good guidance and skilfully affirming and extending their play. Through parent education programmes, adults are developing the skills to notice, recognise and respond to the needs and interests of all children.

Centre members endorse Playcentre‚Äôs commitment to te Tiriti o Waitangi and New Zealand‚Äôs bicultural heritage. They include some te reo and tikanga MƒÅori in children‚Äôs learning, but also recognise that this area of the programme would benefit from ongoing development.

More experienced members provide good leadership. They are focused on increasing centre membership and parent education training levels. These members are committed to reaching out to the community and on continuing to build strong relationships with other parents and their children.

Most administration, management and housekeeping duties are undertaken by a core group. To ensure the centre's sustainability, more members now need to become actively involved in the Playcentre and in training. While members recognise the value of strengthening their role, they also identify challenges in achieving this goal. They particularly note two issues that are negatively impacting on the centre: the geographical isolation of many families which prevents them attending centre sessions, and the distance they need to travel to attend training sessions in nearby regional centres. The Mid North Playcentre Association is aware of the significance of these issues. A key consideration for the association is how it can further support the centre to address the challenges it faces.

Key Next Steps

Centre members and ERO agree that in order to improve learning outcomes for children, they need to:

  • regularly document, plan, assess and evaluate their practices to help extend children‚Äôs learning
  • review the centre‚Äôs philosophy to help establish a greater sense of shared ownership of the centre
  • use self review to streamline management responsibilities.

In order to ensure sustainability the Mid North Association should support the centre to:

  • increase and sustain enrolments so that the centre can operate more effectively as a parent cooperative
  • develop a strategic plan specific to, and recognising the needs of, Kaeo whƒÅnau with a view to making a positive difference for children
  • provide accessible ongoing support and learning and development for parents/whƒÅnau.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Kaeo Playcentre completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

Actions for compliance

ERO identified the following areas of non-compliance relating to centre practices and performance. To meet requirements the service now needs to improve its performance by ensuring:

  • the centre‚Äôs curriculum is informed by assessment, planning and evaluation that demonstrates an understanding of children‚Äôs learning and their interests, whƒÅnau and life contexts
  • adults engage in meaningful, positive interactions to enhance children‚Äôs learning and demonstrate an understanding of children‚Äôs learning and development
  • centre documentation provides evidence of compliance with legal requirements

[Licensing Criteria for Early Childhood Education and Care Centres 2008; C1 (13), C2, C3, C4].

Development Plan Recommendation

ERO recommends that the service consult with the Ministry of Education and plan to address the key next steps and actions outlined in this report.

The next ERO review of Kaeo Playcentre will be within two years.

ERO‚Äôs Evaluation Framework

ERO‚Äôs overarching question for an early childhood education review is ‚ÄòHow well placed is this service to promote positive learning outcomes for children?‚Äô ERO focuses on the following factors as described in the bicultural framework NgƒÅ Pou Here:

ERO evaluates how well placed a service is to sustain good practice and make ongoing improvements for the benefit of all children at the service.

A focus for the government is that all children, especially priority learners, have an opportunity to benefit from quality early childhood education. ERO will report on how well each service promotes positive outcomes for all children, with a focus on children who are MƒÅori, Pacific, have diverse needs, and are up to the age of two.

ERO‚Äôs Overall Judgement and Next Review

The overall judgement that ERO makes and the timing of the next review will depend on how well placed a service is to promote positive learning outcomes for children. The categories are:

  • Well placed ‚Äì The next ERO review in three years
  • Requires further development ‚Äì The next ERO review within two years
  • Not well placed - The next ERO review in consultation with the Ministry of Education

Review Coverage

ERO reviews are tailored to each service‚Äôs context and performance, within the overarching review framework. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for children and useful to the service.
