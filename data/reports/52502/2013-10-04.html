1. Background

Introduction

A Supplementary Review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager, Review Services in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a centre has taken towards addressing issues specified in a previous education review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of the Te Timatanga Hou Kindergarten governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the May 2013 ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

the extent of progress made in developing philosophy and expectations for guiding operation, and for implementing and evaluating curriculum provision, especially a bicultural programme and programmes for children under two years.

2. Evaluation Findings

Background

In April of 2012 ERO reviewed this centre under its former governance and management, Sunshine Kids Daycare Inc. A number of issues were identified and ERO decided to carry out a supplementary review within one year.

Since April 2012 the centre has undergone a change of governance, management and staff. The Ministry of Education transferred the existing licence to the Wellington Region Free Kindergarten Association (the association) on 1 October 2012. The association’s priorities at this time were to continue to provide a service to the families who already attended, to build relationships with the community and establish appropriate routines and expectations for staff and children.

The May 2013 ERO review findings established further improvement of systems and processes was required for the association to achieve their goals. The centre’s curriculum was identified as being in the very early stages of development and needing amending, in particular, to better respond to the youngest children.

Areas of progress

Association management responded in a timely manner to the issues raised. An action plan was developed. It appropriately identifies and prioritises areas for review and development. Thoughtful consideration has been given to change management and establishing clear expectations for staff. This is being well supported by the centre’s head teacher, the senior teacher and association managers. Actions undertaken to July 2013 demonstrate steady progress. These are leading to enhanced outcomes for children, families, whānau and aiaga.

The teaching team is aware of legislative requirements and centre procedures relating to the provision of a safe emotional and physical environment. Processes are in place to ensure this is maintained. These have real potential to contribute to increased consistency in adult practice and support children’s holistic development.

The association seconded a head teacher from within their organisation to implement association expectations and guidelines, form productive relationships with the community, build a collaborative teaching team and achieve the smooth day-to-day operation of the service. The teaching team includes three permanently appointed, fully registered teachers. A culture of critical reflection is evident. This provides a strong foundation to promote positive outcomes for children.

The teaching team has been together since May 2013. They demonstrate a genuine commitment to providing a quality service to the community. Actions undertaken that have enhanced the curriculum include development of:

  • a clearly articulated philosophy that links well to the association vision, Te Whāriki and current best practice in early childhood education
  • a ‘whānau-based approach’, focusing on developing respectful relationships where all people take responsibility for their own and others' learning
  • respect for diversity, while acknowledging and celebrating Māori as tangata whenua
  • shared responsive care-giving that promotes the centre’s youngest children’s need for strong and secure attachments
  • a closer focus on children’s families, whānau and aiaga transition to the centre
  • ongoing review of the physical environment to ascertain how well equipment, furnishings and resources support children’s active involvement and stimulate their interest in learning
  • provision for experiences that support children’s developing literacy and mathematical conceptual learning.

Areas for further improvement

Teaching staff continue to increase their understandings of review for improvement. This is an appropriate focus for ongoing development.

‘Te Manawa’, the association’s document for planning and evaluating the centre’s localised curriculum is drawn from Te Whāriki and Kei Tua o te Pae. It provides explicit guidelines for effective teaching practice and assessing and sharing children’s learning. Appropriate indicators of best practice are identified. ERO suggests association leaders support teachers to better align the May 2013 professional development plan to this model, to build the centre’s evaluative capacity and further enhance outcomes for children, families, whānau and aiaga.

3. Future Action

ERO will review the service again as part of the regular review cycle.

Joyce Gebbie
