Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Aroha ki Ohākune. Ka whakanuia, ka poipoia hoki te tuakiri o ngā tamariki i te taiao o te kōhanga reo. Ka tūmāia rātou hei Māori, ā, ka ngākau nui ki tētahi hōtaka e aro ana ki ō rātou matea ako. Kei te pakari haere te māia o ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Aroha i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Aroha ki Ohākune. Ko Ruapehu te maunga, ā, e whai pānga nui ana ki tēnei rohe me te whānau. Ko ngā tamariki te whakatupuranga tuatoru o tō rātou whānau ki te noho ki tēnei kōhanga reo.

Ka whakapau kaha rātou ki te hāpai i te kaupapa o te whānau me te kōhanga reo, mō rātou, mō ā rātou tamariki hoki.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou tuakiri?

Ka whakanuia, ka whakatairangatia hoki te tuakiri o ngā tamariki ki roto i te taiao o tēnei kōhanga reo.

Mana Tangata

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga hoki. Kua waihangatia ngā hōtaka akoranga hei whakapakari ake i te tuakiri. He pakari te tū takitahi o ngā tamariki tuākana ki te taki i ngā karakia me ngā pepeha e whakapūmau ana i ō rātou whakapapa me ō rātou hononga ki tō rātou iwi. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te noho mārama ki te whakahirahiratanga o tō rātou ahurea me ō rātou tātai whakapapa. Ka tūmāia ngā tamariki.

E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whiriwhiri i ngā taumahi me ngā rauemi e hiahiatia ana e rātou. Ka whakarite ngā kaiako i te taiao akoranga hei poipoi i te ako a ngā tamariki. Kua waihangatia e rātou tētahi wāhi ako hihiri e whakaongaonga ana, e atawhai ana hoki. Ka whakanuia ngā mahi a ngā tamariki, ā, ka āta whakaatuhia puta noa i te kōhanga reo. Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi tētahi hōtaka akoranga reo matatini, pāngarau hoki, ā, ka whai wāhi nui ngā tamariki, ka ngākau nuitia hoki e rātou. Ka whakarerekē haere ngā kaimahi i te hōtaka hei whakatutuki i ngā matea ako me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Ka tino whakamihia e rātou ngā whakamātau me ngā whakatutukitanga a ngā tamariki. He ākonga harikoa ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He whāiti te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori, ā, kāhore e whakapūmautia ana te reo puta noa i te rā. He aronga nui ki ngā taumahi ako ōkawa hei whakaako i te reo Māori. Kei te mōhio haere ngā kaiako ki te whai wāhi nui o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero hei whakawhānui ake i te reo o ngā tamariki. Ahakoa he tino tauira reo ki ngā karakia, ngā pepeha, ngā mihimihi me ngā waiata mō ngā tamariki, ahakoa hoki te hōhonutanga o ngā rauemi reo e karapotia ana rātou, he whāiti tonu ngā taunekeneke ka whakapakari ake i te āheinga reo ki tua atu i ngā wā ako ōkawa. I te nuinga o te wā ka whakamahi tonutia ngā tamariki i te reo Pākehā.

Kāhore ngā tamariki i raro i te rua o ngā tau e tino hāpaitia ana hei ākonga. Kāhore anō ngā kaimahi kia whakarite i tētahi hōtaka akoranga e āta whakatutuki ana i ngā matea o ngā pēpi. Ka whakawhanakehia ngā taumahi mō ngā tamariki tuākana, engari kāhore e tino whakaarohia ana ngā matea whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki nohinohi.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

E āhei ana ki te āta aroturuki i te ako me te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki mā ngā mahere me te aromatawai. Ka hono ngā mahere ki ngā mōhiohio aromatawai e pā ana ki ngā tamariki, ā, e whakaemihia ana e ngā kaimahi. Ka tāutu rātou i ngā kaupapa ako, ngā rautaki ako, me ngā rauemi hei whakawhānui i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka whakaaro huritao ngā kaiako me ngā kaimahi ki te hōtaka, ā, he matawhānui ngā pūrongo e pā ana ki te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki. Kei te anga whakamua ngā tamariki.

He mahere rautaki tā te whānau hei hāpai i ō rātou wawata mō ngā tamariki. Ka āta tāutuhia ngā kaupapa matua o te pae tata me te pae tawhiti. He mahere mahi tā te whānau e whai hua ana ki te aroturuki i te ahu whakamua, ki te tāutu hoki i ngā wāhanga tonu hei whakawhanake. Ka kōrerohia ngā pūrongo o ia wā mō ngā kaupapa matua, ā, ka whai hua ngā mahi a te whānau ki te whakahaere i ngā take o ia rā, i te wā tonu ka pupū ake.

Kua arotakengia, kua whakahoungia hoki e te whānau tā rātou tūtohinga hei hāpai i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo i ia rā. He mārama ngā aratohu o roto mā te whānau me ērā hoki e kawe ana i ētahi o ngā tino mahi. E mōhio ana te whānau ki ā rātou kawenga kia hāngai tonu ai ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga o te hauora me te haumaru ki te rā. E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He whāiti te whakatau a te whānau i ngā whakaritenga. Kei ngā kaimahi me te heamana te kawenga ki te whakatau i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kei te āta whakatakoto te tūtohinga i te noho matua o te whakataunga a te whānau. Kāhore i te tino kaha te taetae mai o te whānau ki ngā hui.

Kāhore anō kia āta whakawhanakehia te aromatawai o ngā tamariki kei raro i te rua o ngā tau. Ahakoa ka aromatawai, ka pūrongo hoki ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, i te ahu whakamua o ngā tamariki tuākana, kāhore anō rātou kia whakawhanake i tētahi tino aromatawai mā ngā tamariki kei raro i te rua o ngā tau. Kāhore e āta whakaarohia ana he rautaki hei aromatawai I ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki kei raro i te rua o ngā tau.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. Hei whakapai ake i ngā whakaritenga o tēnei wā, me tahuri te whānau ki te:

  • whakatinana i tētahi tukanga aromatawai ka aroturuki i te ako me te ahu whakamua a ngā pēpi
    [R 43 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008/ C1-14]
  • whai wāhi ki ngā whakataunga katoa e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo.
    [R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008/GMA1-4]

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei mā te whānau, arā, kia tahuri te whānau ki te:

  • rapu i ngā rautaki ka hāpai i ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakapiki ake i ō rātou āheinga, i tā rātou whakamahinga hoki o te reo Māori ki te taha o ngā tamariki
  • whakarite i tētahi hōtaka me ngā aromatawai ka hāngai pū ki ngā tamariki kei raro i te rua o ngā tau.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Aroha ki Ohākune. Ka whakanuia, ka poipoia hoki te tuakiri o ngā tamariki i te taiao o te kōhanga reo. Ka tūmāia rātou hei Māori, ā, ka ngākau nui ki tētahi hōtaka e aro ana ki ō rātou matea ako. Kei te pakari haere te māia o ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Aroha is located in Ohakune. Children’s identity is promoted and nurtured in the kōhanga reo environment. They are confident about being Māori and enjoy a programme that responds to their learning needs. Children are gaining confidence in use te reo Māori.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Aroha will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Aroha is located in Ohakune. Mount Ruapehu is a feature of this area and significance to the whānau. Children are the third generation in their whānau who have attended this kōhanga reo.

They work to support the kaupapa of the whānau and kōhanga reo for themselves and their children.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children express their identity?

Children’s identity is expressed through whakapapa.

Mana Tangata

Children know who they are and where they belong. Learning programmes are designed to build a strong sense of identity. Older children confidently and independently recite karakia and pepeha that reinforces their whakapapa and connections to their iwi. Kaimahi help children understand the importance of their culture and whakapapa links. Children are confident.

Children are able to choose activities and resources that interest them. Kaiako organise the learning environment to cater for children’s learning. They have created a stimulating, warm and vibrant learning space. Children’s work is valued and displayed proudly throughout the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi have designed a literacy and numeracy learning programme that children find engaging and enjoyable. They adjust the programme to respond to the learning needs and interests of children. They positively acknowledge children’s efforts and achievements. Children are happy learners.

Key Next Steps

Children’s use of te reo Māori is limited and is not sustained throughout the day. There is an emphasis on formal learning activities to teach te reo Māori. Kaiako are beginning to recognise the importance of conversations to extend children’s language. While karakia, pepeha, mihimihi and waiata provide children with good language patterns and they are surrounded by language rich resources, interactions that build language capability beyond formal learning times are limited. Children still predominantly use English.

Children under two years are not well supported as learners. Kaimahi have not established a learning programme that caters specifically to the needs of babies. Activities are developed for older children with little consideration to developmental needs of the very young children.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s learning and progress can be clearly monitored through planning and assessment. Planning is linked to assessment information that kaimahi gather about children. They identify kaupapa ako, learning strategies and resources to extend children’s learning. Kaiako and kaimahi reflect on the programme and reports about children’s progress are comprehensive. Children are progressing.

Whānau have a strategic plan in place to support their aspirations for children. Short and long term priorities are clearly identified. Whānau have an action plan that is useful for monitoring progress and identifying further areas for development. Regular reporting about priority areas are discussed and whānau manage day to day operational matters effectively as they arise.

Whānau have reviewed and updated their charter to support the day to day management of the kōhanga reo. It provides clear guidelines for whānau and those with delegated tasks. Whānau are aware of their responsibilities to keep health and safety policies and practices up to date. Children are well supported in the kōhanga reo.

Key Next Steps

Whānau decision making is limited. Kaimahi and the chairperson are responsible for decisions about the kōhanga reo operations. The charter clearly states that whānau decision making is a priority. Representation and attendance at hui is currently low.

Assessment for children under two years is underdeveloped. Although kaimahi regularly assess and report on the progress of the older children they have not developed an appropriate strategy for assessing children under twos. The learning needs of children are two years are not being considered.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements. To improve current practice the whānau must:

  • implement an assessment process to monitor babies learning and progress
    (R43ECS (Early Childhood Regulations, 2008/ C1-14)]
  • engage in all decisions related to the management and operations of the kōhanga reo.
    (R47ECS (Early Childhood Regulations, 2008/GMA1-4)]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations that the whānau will:

  • seek strategies to support kaimahi and whānau to increase their capabilities and use of te reo Māori with children

  • establish a programme and assessment specifically for children under two years.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Aroha is located in Ohakune. Children’s identity is promoted and nurtured in the kōhanga reo environment. They are confident about being Māori and enjoy a programme that responds to their learning needs. Children are gaining confidence in use te reo Māori.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Aroha will be in three years.
