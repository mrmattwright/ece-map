2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Oterepo ki Ōtautahi.  Kua hūnukutia te kōhanga reo ki Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Whakapūmau ki te Reo Tūturu ki Waitaha, nā te wehenga atu i tō rātou whare, whai muri i ngā rū whenua.  Kua whakaritea e te whānau ki te Tāhuhu o te Mātauranga ki te hanga i tētahi atu whare hou hei kōhanga reo ki te papa o te kura ā meāke nei.  I tēnei wā, e whakahaerehia ana te kōhanga reo ki tētahi akomanga i te kura. 

E mahi ngātahi ana te whānau, ngā kaimahi, me ngā māngai o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te whakapakari i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.  E kawe ana te kaiwhakahaere ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i te tūnga o te heamana, hei āwhina i te whakamahine, te whakapai ake hoki o ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.  I tēnei wā e mahi ana te whānau me Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te whakatutuki i ētahi tino take nui, tae atu ki ngā mahi whakahaere, ngā take kaimahi, te waiora o ngā tamariki, me ngā tikanga hoki e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru.  He pai te āta ahu whakamua, ā, he pai ki te whānau te āhua o te wā kei te heke mai. 

Ka tino hāpaitia ngā tamariki i a rātou e ako ana, e whakamahi ana hoki i te reo Māori.  Ka ū ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau ki te kounga pai o tētahi taiao ako reo Māori, puta noa i te rā.  Ka whai pānga nui ngā tikanga Māori hei wāhanga tonu o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, he māoriori te whakatinanatanga o tēnei, puta noa i te rā. 

Ka whakapau ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakarato i te atawhai e tika ana ki ngā tamariki katoa.  Ka whakanui, ka āwhina hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te ū tonu ki ngā ritenga hauora me ngā mahinga. 

Ka kōkiri ngā tamariki i ā rātou ake akoranga, ā, ka whiriwhiri, ka whakatau haere hoki i ā rātou kaupapa mahi i ētahi wāhanga o te rā.  E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi.  E āhei ana rātou ki te tūhura, te tākaro, me te raweke i ngā rauemi e ngākau nuitia ana e rātou.  Ka whai ngā tamariki i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga, ā, ka ngana atu, ka whakaongaongatia hoki ki te ako i te kōhanga reo. 

Ka kitea ētahi āhuatanga e tohu ana i te whai huatanga o te arotake whaiaro.  Kua whakawhanakehia, kua whakatinanahia hoki tētahi hōtaka e pā ana ki te ako ngaio me te tautoko ngaio, hei āwhina i ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakapai i ā rātou whakaritenga.  Inā koa, kua tāutu te arotake whaiaro me te arotake o waho, kia āwhina i ngā kaimahi me te mema o te whānau ki te whakapai ake i te whakahaeretanga, me te ako i ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi, me tō rātou mōhiotanga hoki ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. 

Me whai hua, me tika hoki te mātakitaki a ngā pākeke i ngā tamariki.  Me ako ngā kaimahi i ngā huarahi ki te whakahaere tōtika i te whanonga o ngā tamariki.  Kua whakamaheretia ngā whakangungu e hāngai pū ana, e arotahi ana hoki ki ēnei āhuatanga. 

Kua ako te whānau me ngā kaimahi, he mea nui te whai i te aronga o ngā kaupapa here i a rātou e whakahaere ana i ngā āhuatanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, tae atu ki ngā take kaimahi me ngā amuamu.  He pai te noho mōhio o te whānau ki te kounga me te āhua o ngā āhuatanga maha o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. 

Whakaritenga Whaimuri 

E mea ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ki te hoki ki te kōhanga reo i roto i te tekau mā rua marama ki te aromātai i te ahu whakamua o te whānau i runga o tā rātou whakautu ki ngā taunakitanga o tēnei pūrongo.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Oterepo kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau.  I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Oterepo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Tangata. 

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga.  Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Tangata 

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Mana Tangata te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.  Ko Mana Tangata te ariā matua o ngā akoranga i te wā o te arotake. 

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te reo me ngā tikanga Māori.  E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki i a rātou e ako ana, e whakamahi ana hoki i te reo Māori.  Ka ū ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau ki tētahi taiao ako reo Māori o te kounga pai, puta noa i te rā.  Ka whai wāhi matua ngā tikanga Māori ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, he māoriori te whakatinanatanga o tēnei, puta noa i te rā.  Ka ākina, ka tino tautokona ngā tamariki kia whai wāhi rātou ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero māmā.  He pakari ngā tamariki ki te ako me te whakamahi i te reo Māori. 

Te waiora me te atawhai o ngā tamariki.  Ka tino whakapau kaha ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakarato i te atawhai tika ki ngā tamariki katoa.  Ka whakanui, ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te ū ki ngā ritenga hauora me ngā mahinga.  E mōhiotia ana ngā mahinga e ngā tamariki, ā, e tino whakahaerehia ana e ngā kaimahi. 

Te kōkiri a ngā tamariki i ā rātou ake akoranga.  Ka kōkiri ngā tamariki i ā rātou ake akoranga, ā, ka whiriwhiri, ka whakatau hoki rātou i ā rātou ake kaupapa mahi i ētahi wāhanga o te rā.  Ka ākina, ka tautokona hoki te kōkiri a ngā tamariki i ā rātou ake akoranga e ngā kaimahi.  Ka tūhura, ka tākaro hoki ngā tamariki ki ngā rauemi e ngākau nuitia ana e rātou.  Ka whakaratohia te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi, taumahi hoki ki ngā tamariki.  Ka haumi te whānau i te kounga pai o ngā rauemi mō ngā tamariki.  Ka whai ngā tamariki i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, ā, ka ngana atu, ka whakaongaongatia hoki i a rātou e ako ana ki te kōhanga reo. 

Te taiao akoranga.  Ka whakaratohia ki ngā tamariki te taiao akoranga whakahihiri, i āta whakaritea ai, i āta whakatakotohia ai.  He pai te whakatakotoranga o ngā wāhi ako i whakaritea ai mō ngā tamariki.  He pai hoki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi, rauemi hoki e whai pānga nui ana ki te whanaketanga me ngā kaupapa.  He māmā noa te whakapā atu ki ngā rauemi e noho wātea ana mō ngā tamariki. 

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai i ngā kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki me te whānau.  Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere i te hōtaka me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki. 

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te aronga rautaki.  Kua whakahonoa e te whānau tā rātou aronga whakamua ki ngā mahere rautaki a Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, arā, ki Te Ara Tūāpae.  Kua āta whakamaheretia, kua āta whakaarohia hoki te aronga a te whānau.  Ka hāngai tēnei ki te whakapakari i te tautoko me te whai wāhitanga o te whānau, te āheinga i roto i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori, me te ako hoki i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo.  He pai te whakamahere haere o te whānau me ngā kaimahi i te aronga o te kōhanga reo mō meāke nei. 

Te whakamahere hōtaka.  Ka mahi ngātahi ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakawhanake i ngā mahere hōtaka.  Ka āta whakamahia ngā mahere hei ārahi i te hōtaka akoranga e whakaratohia ana mā ngā tamariki i ia rā.  Ka whakaratohia ki ngā tamariki te hōtaka akoranga e whakahihiri ana, e whakaongaonga ana hoki i a rātou. 

Te arotake whaiaro.  Ka kitea ētahi āhuatanga papai i te arotake whaiaro.  Whakatakotohia ai ngā pūrongo ki te whānau i ngā hui o ia marama, mai i te kaiako, ngā kaimahi, me ngā kaiwhakahaere o te tari.  Kua ako te whānau me ngā kaimahi i te whai huatanga o te whai i te aronga o ngā kaupapa here i a rātou e whakahaere ana i ētahi āhuatanga o ngā whakaritenga i te kōhanga reo.  He pai te noho mōhio o te whānau ki te kounga me te āhua o ētahi o ngā tino āhuatanga o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.  Inā koa, kua tāutuhia nā te arotake whaiaro me te arotake o waho, kia āwhina i ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakahaere ake, me te ako i ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi. 

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te aromatawai.  E ahu whakamua ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhanake ake anō i te aromatawai hei huarahi ki te aroturuki me te whakanui i ngā akoranga.  I te rū whenua nui, i pakaru, i ngaro rānei te nuinga o ngā mōhiohio aromatawai e pā ana ki ia tamaiti.  Nā tēnei, e tīmata ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakahou ake anō i ngā pūkete, me te whakaemi i ngā mōhiohio aromatawai e whai pānga ana, e whai pūtake ana hoki ki te āhua o ngā tamariki.  

Te whakapūmau i ngā tino whakaritenga, ahakoa kāhore ētahi o ngā kaimahi matua i reira.  Kāhore te whānau me ngā kaimahi e whakaatu ana i te āheinga ki te whakapūmau i ngā whakahaeretanga o ia rā, i te wā kāhore he kaiako i reira.  Ka whakawhirinaki atu te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te kaiako, ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā ki te taumata teitei, ki te whakapūmau i te mauritau o ngā hononga ngaio, ā, ki te whakarato hoki i ngā wheako akoranga o te kounga pai mō ngā tamariki.  Me hāpai i te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakapakari ake i tō rātou ake āheinga.  Me mahi ngātahi rātou ki te āta whakahaere pai i ngā whakaritenga ki te kōhanga reo.  Me nui ake te arotahi ki te whakarato i ngā wheako akoranga o te kounga kairangi mō ngā tamariki. 

Te whakahaere i te whanonga o ngā tamariki.  Kāhore e tika ana ētahi o ngā mahi ki te whakahaere i te whanonga o ngā tamariki.  Kua whakawhanakehia, kua whakatinanahia hoki tētahi hōtaka e pā ana ki te ako ngaio, te tautoko ngaio hoki, hei hāpai i ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakapai ake i ā rātou mahi. 

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Oterepo, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro.  I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

I te wā o te arotake i tāutuhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ētahi wāhanga tautukunga kāore i tutuki.  Kia taea ai te whakatikatika i ēnei, me mātua whakarite te whānau o te kōhanga ki te:

5.1 ū ki te tuhi haere i ngā rongoā katoa ka whāngaihia ki ngā tamariki
[35 (g) Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 1998]

5.2 whakarite, kia arowhaingia ai ngā kaimahi katoa e ngā pirihimana
[Ngā wāhanga 319D ki 319FA Te Ture Mātauranga 1989]

5.3 whakamahi i ngā rautaki whakahaere whanonga e tika ana i a rātou e ārahi ana i ngā tamariki
[R33 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 1998]

5.4 ū ki ngā whakaritenga e whai hua ana mō te mātakitaki i ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa
[R21, 24, 27, 36, Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 1998]

5.5 whakawhanake i tētahi kaupapa here e pā ana ki ngā haerenga mō ngā tamariki. 
[R27 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 1998]

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Oterepo Te Kōhanga Reo is in Christchurch. The kōhanga reo has been relocated to Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Whakapumau ki te reo tuturu ki Waitaha after the earthquake left their premises uninhabitable. The whānau has negotiated with the Ministry of Education to build a new, purpose built kōhanga reo on the kura site in the near future. The kōhanga reo is currently operating from a classroom at the kura.

The whānau, staff members and representatives of Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) are working together to strengthen the kōhanga reo operations. The district manager from TKRNT has taken on the role of chairperson to help refine and improve the kōhanga reo operations. The whānau and TKRNT are currently working to address several major issues including management practices, personnel matters, children’s wellbeing and health and safety requirements. Steady progress is occurring and the whānau are positive about the future.

Children are well supported as they learn and use te reo Māori. Staff and whānau members maintain a good quality te reo Māori language learning environment throughout the day. Tikanga Māori is a vital part of children’s learning and occurs in natural ways throughout the day

Staff and whānau members make every effort to provide suitable care to all children. Staff members promote and help children to maintain healthy habits and routines.

Children are independent learners who make choices and decisions about what they will do for parts of the day. Children have access to a wide range of resources. They are able to explore, play with and manipulate resources that interest them. Children follow their interests and are engaged and stimulated to learn at kōhanga reo.

Aspects of effective self review are evident. A programme of professional learning and support has been developed and implemented to help staff and whānau members to improve their practices. In particular, self review and external review has identified the need to help the staff and whānau members to better manage and learn about their roles, responsibilities and knowledge of the kaupapa of kōhanga reo. 

There is a need for adults to provide effective and appropriate supervision of children. Kaimahi should learn how to manage children’s behaviour in an appropriate way. Specific and focussed training is planned.

The whānau and staff has learned the importance of following policy direction when managing all aspects of kōhanga reo operations including personnel matters and complaints. The whānau is well informed about the quality and performance of many aspects of kōhanga reo operations.

Future Action

ERO intends to return to the kōhanga reo within 12 months to evaluate the progress made in response to the recommendations in this report.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Oterepo was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.  This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children atTe Kōhanga Reo o Oterepo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Tangata.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education.  For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning; and
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below. 

4 Findings

Mana Tangata 

Background

The whānau and staff members selected Mana Tangata as the focus for this review. Mana Tangata was the key theme for learning during the course of the review.

Areas of good performance

Te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. Children are well supported as they learn and use te reo Māori. Staff and whānau members maintain a good quality te reo Māori language learning environment throughout the day. Tikanga Māori is a vital part of children’s learning and occurs in natural ways throughout the day. Children are encouraged and well supported to engage in simple conversations. Children confidently learn and use te reo Māori.

Children’s well being and care. Staff and whānau members make every effort to provide suitable care to all children. Staff members promote and help children to maintain healthy habits and routines. Routines are known by the children and are well managed by staff members.

Independent learners. Children are independant learners who make choices and decisions about what they will do for parts of the day. Independence is encouraged and supported by staff members. Children explore and play with resources that interest them. Children are provided with a range of resources and activities. The whānau invests in good quality resources for children. Children follow their interests and are engaged and stimulated to learn while they are at kōhanga reo.

The learning environment. Children are provided with an interesting, well prepared and organised learning environment. The learning spaces are well presented and set up for children. There is a good range of developmental and kaupapa based learning activities and resources. These resources are accessible and readily available to children.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Strategic direction. The whānau has linked their future direction to the TKRNT’s strategic plan, Te Ara Tūāpae. The whānau has a well planned and considered approach. This includes strengthening whānau support and involvement, building te reo and tikanga Māori capability and learning about the kaupapa of kōhanga reo. The whānau and staff plan well for the future direction of the kōhanga reo.

Programme planning. Staff and whānau members work collaboratively when they develop programme plans. Plans are well used to guide the daily learning programme provided for children. Children are provided with an interesting and stimulating learning programme.

Self review. Aspects of effective self review are evident. Regular reports from the kaiako, staff and administration officers are presented to whānau at monthly meetings. The whānau and staff have learned the importance of following policy direction when managing aspects of the kōhanga reo operations. The whānau is well informed about the quality and performance of key aspects of the kōhanga reo operations. In particular self review and external review has identified the need to help the staff and whānau members to better manage and learn about their roles and responsibilities.

Areas for development

Assessment. Staff members are working toward redeveloping assessment as a tool for monitoring and promoting learning. The earthquake resulted in most assessment information about individual children being damaged or destroyed. Consequently, the staff members are beginning to rebuild profiles and to gather relevant and meaningful assessment information about children.

Sustaining sound practices in the absence of key personnel. The whānau and staff do not demonstrate the capacity to sustain effective daily operations in the absence of the kaiako. The whānau and staff are reliant on the kaiako to manage daily operations to a high standard, to maintain positive professional relationships and to provide good quality learning experiences for children. Whānau and staff members need support to help build their capability. They need to work collaboratively to manage the kōhanga reo operations more effectively. There should be a greater focus on providing high quality learning experiences for children.

Managing children’s behaviour. Some practices for managing children’s behaviour are inappropriate. A programme of professional learning and support has been developed and implemented to help staff and whānau members to improve their practices.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Oterepo completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist.  In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

During the course of the review ERO identified areas of non-compliance.  In order to address these the kōhanga reo whānau must:

5.1 maintain a record of all medication given to children
[Regulation 35(g) Education (Early Childhood Regulations 1998]

5.2 ensure that all staff members are police vetted
[Sections 319D to 319FA Education Act 1989]

5.3 use effective behaviour management strategies when guiding children
[R33, Education (ECC) Regulations 1998]

5.4 maintain effective supervision practices of children at all times
[R21, 24, 27 36, Education (ECC) Regulations 1998]               

5.5 develop a policy for taking children on excursions.
[R27, Education (ECC) Regulations 1998]

ERO recommends that TKRNT assist the whānau and kaimahi to develop an action plan showing how the issues identified in this ERO report will be met. A copy of the plan should be sent to ERO.

ERO intends to return to the kōhanga reo within 12 months to evaluate the progress made in response to the recommendations in this report.

To the Parents and Community of Te Kōhanga Reo o Oterepo

These are the findings of the Education Review Office’s latest report on Te Kōhanga Reo o Oterepo.

Oterepo Te Kōhanga Reo is in Christchurch. The kōhanga reo has been relocated to Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Whakapumau ki te reo tuturu ki Waitaha after the earthquake left their premises uninhabitable. The whānau has negotiated with the Ministry of Education to build a new, purpose built kōhanga reo on the kura site in the near future. The kōhanga reo is currently operating from a classroom at the kura.

The whānau, staff members and representatives of Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) are working together to strengthen the kōhanga reo operations. The district manager from TKRNT has taken on the role of chairperson to help refine and improve the kōhanga reo operations. The whānau and TKRNT are currently working to address several major issues including management practices, personnel matters, children’s wellbeing and health and safety requirements. Steady progress is occurring and the whānau are positive about the future.

Children are well supported as they learn and use te reo Māori. Staff and whānau members maintain a good quality te reo Māori language learning environment throughout the day. Tikanga Māori is a vital part of children’s learning and occurs in natural ways throughout the day

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews.  The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation.  This area is most likely to support long term improvement.  Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based on Te Whāriki, thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues.  The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement.  A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue.  There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
