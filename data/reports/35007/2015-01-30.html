1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kei tētahi hapori iti, tuawhenua hoki Te Kōhanga Reo o Kerepehi. Ko Paeroa te taone tino tata mai. Haere ai ngā tamariki o te hapori ki tēnei kōhanga reo e tū ana ki te taha o te marae. Kua whai hua te whānau ki te whakatutuki i te maha o ngā wero i roto i ngā tau e rua kua pahure ake nei. Kua whakapiki ake rātou i te tatau o te rārangi ingoa. Ko ngā whakaritenga mahi me ngā kawenga whakahaere kei ngā tino taumata e mahi ana. Kua tuhia tētahi puka whakauru i āta whakaarohia ai, hei āwhina i te whānau ki te whakawhiti ki te kōhanga reo.

I whai wāhi te whānau ki te arotake nei a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, nā tā rātou waihanga i tētahi pātai aromātai. I whakaritea e rātou te Mana Reo hei kaupapa arotahi, ā, e hiahia ana rātou ki te mōhio

‘He pēhea te māramatanga, te whakamahinga hoki a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori?’

Kei te mārama ngā tamariki i te reo Māori, ka aro atu rātou ki ngā tohutohu me ngā kīanga māmā, engari i te nuinga o te wā ka whakawhiti kōrero mā te reo Pākehā. Ko te hāpai i te whakamahinga o te reo Māori tētahi wāhanga whakawhanaketanga mō ngā tamariki me ngā mema o te whānau. E mōhio ana te whānau, e tika ana kia whakatairangatia te reo Māori ki te kāinga. E rapu ana te whānau i ngā huarahi ki te whakawhanake i tō rātou āheinga i roto i te reo Māori. He pai ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. E whakawhanake ana rātou i ngā rautaki papai e hāpai ana i ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi me te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ka whakaaraaratia ngā tamariki ki te ako. He whai wāhitanga mā rātou ki te uru atu ki ngā akoranga takitahi, ngā mahi tikanga, ngā kaupapa, me ngā taumahi ka kōkirihia e ngā pākeke. E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi e poipoi ana i ō rātou āheinga me ō rātou ake reanga. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki tuākana ki te ārahi i ngā mahinga me ngā taumahi. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā taumahi e akiaki ana, e whakatairanga ana hoki i te auahatanga me te korikori tinana. Ka tautoko ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki mā ngā huarahi papai, i a rātou e whakahura ana i ngā āhuatanga hou mā ā rātou tākaro.

Ka tino tautokona te whānau i ngā tamariki. He hiahia tō te whānau ki te hāpai i te kōhanga reo, ā, ka tae atu te nuinga o te whānau ki ngā hui o ia marama. He mātātoa te āhua o ngā mahi arotake whaiaro, ā, he huarahi hei hāpai ake i ngā tamariki. He pai ngā tukanga mō te whakamahere rautaki me te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi, ko:

  • Mana Reo.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Reo

He whakamārama

‘He pēhea te māramatanga, te whakamahinga hoki a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori?’

Kei te mārama ngā tamariki i te reo Māori, ka aro atu ki ngā tohutohu me ngā kīanga māmā, engari i te nuinga o te wā ka whakawhiti kōrero mā te reo Pākehā.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka whakaaraaratia ngā tamariki ki te ako. He whai wāhitanga mā rātou ki te uru atu ki ngā akoranga takitahi, ngā mahi tikanga, ngā kaupapa, me ngā taumahi ka kōkirihia e ngā pākeke. E ngāwari ana mā ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti ki ngā wāhi o roto, o waho hoki. E wātea ana ki a rātou te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi e poipoi ana i ō rātou ake āheinga me ō rātou ake reanga. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki tuākana ki te ārahi i ngā mahinga me ngā tūmomo taumahi. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā momo taumahi e akiaki ana, e whakatairanga ana hoki i te auahatanga me te korikori tinana. Ka tautoko ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki mā ngā huarahi papai, i a rātou e whai ana i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, ā, e tūhura ana hoki i te ao Māori.

Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i te tino tautoko, mai i tētahi ohu e ū ana ki ngā mahi whakaako. Ka whakakoro ngā kaimahi ki te whakarato i te pai o te manaaki me te whakawhanaketanga mō ā rātou tamariki. He āhua tauhou ngā kaimahi ki tō rātou mahi. He pai tā rātou mahi ngātahi, me te tautoko o tētahi ki tētahi. Ka āwhina te whānau ki te kōhanga reo i a rātou e wātea ana. Ka tino tautoko ngā kaimahi i a rātou anō, i ngā tamariki hoki.

He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i ngā tino kaupapa nui. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te tūhura i ngā tūmomo kakano, ngā tūmomo matū hoki i a rātou e waihanga ana i ngā whakapaipaitanga mā te kōritorito me ngā tae muramura. Ka whakanuia ki ngā tamariki te whai pānga o te rākau Pōhutukawa i tēnei wā o te tau.

Ka ako, ka tākaro hoki ngā tamariki i tētahi taiao ātaahua kua āta waihangatia. Ka tino tiaki te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo. He pai te hanga o te whare, ki ngā wāhi o roto, o waho hoki. Ka whakatutuki haere te whānau ki te arowhai i ngā mōrearea i ia te wā. Ka pīataata te rūma kai me te rūma moe, nā te auaha o ngā kōwaiwai pakitara. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki tō rātou taiao, me te mea hoki, he kaha tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga.

Te wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Ka mārama ngā tamariki i te reo Māori, ka aro hoki rātou ki ngā tohutohu me ngā kīanga māmā, engari, he kaha hoki rātou ki te whakawhiti whakaaro mā te reo Pākehā. He pai ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, ā, kōrero Māori ai rātou i te katoa o te rā. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, kāhore rātou e tino akiaki ana i ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi me te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ahakoa e āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te mārama me te whai hoki i ngā tohutohu me ngā kīanga māmā, he kaha rawa rātou ki te kōrero i te reo Pākehā i a rātou e taunekeneke ana ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ngā kaimahi hoki.

Kāhore i te mātātoa te tautoko o te whānau i ngā tamariki i a rātou e ako ana i te reo Māori. E tika ana, kia whai atu te whānau ki ngā akoranga o te reo Māori, hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo Māori o ā rātou tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te auau o ngā mahi arotake whaiaro. Ko ngā mahi arotake whaiaro hoki, he tika. Arotake ai te whānau i ā rātou kaupapa here, ā rātou tukanga hoki i ia marama. Whakatutukihia ai i ia te wā ngā arowhai mō ngā āhuatanga ki te taiao e noho mōrearea ana pea. He mārama ngā tukanga pūrongo, aroturuki hoki e pā ana ki te whakahaere pūtea, te whakawhanaketanga ngaio, te whakahaeretanga i ngā kaimahi, me te kohi pūtea. Kei te noho pūmau te whānau ki te whakarato i tētahi ratonga e whai hua ana mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ko ngā painga mō ngā tamariki me tō rātou waiora, kei te pūtake o te ahunga whakamua o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakaritea te mahere wā roa. He mārama ki te whānau te whānuitanga o ngā kaupapa mahi i whakamaheretia ai e rātou mō te kōhanga reo. He kaha te noho pūmau o te whānau ki te whakarato i tētahi taiao e āta tiakina ana, e whai rauemi ana hoki mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ka whai wheako ngā tamariki i ngā hōtaka whakaaraara, ā, kua āta maheretia hoki. Ka ārahi, ka tautoko hoki ngā mahere ā-wiki i te whakapuakitanga o ngā kaimahi i ngā hōtaka mā ngā tamariki. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi ko ngā akoranga, ngā taumahi hoki e whai pūtake ana mā ngā tamariki.

Ka aroturukitia te ahunga whakamua me ngā whakatutukitanga a ngā tamariki mā te whakamahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki aromatawai. He mōhiohio ki ngā pūkete a ia tamaiti, ā, he tauira hoki o ngā mahi a ngā tamariki, ngā kitenga, ngā kōrerorero, ngā kōrero urupare a ngā mātua, me te tātari o te ahunga whakamua o ia tamaiti i runga i Te Whāriki. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, e tika ana kia noho wātea ki te whānau ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki ngā aromatawai a ngā tamariki, kia noho mōhio ai rātou ki tā rātou ahunga whakamua.

Ko tētahi tino āhuatanga, ko te aromātai i te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki. He māramatanga tō ngā kaimahi ki te pūtake me te whai huatanga o te aromātai. Kōrero ai rātou i ia te wā, i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te hōtaka, ā, ka aroturuki i te āhua, te whai wāhi atu hoki o ngā tamariki hou. Ka whakawhiti kōrero ngā kaimahi mō ā rātou mahi whakaako me te whai huatanga o ngā taumahi. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te whakaaro huritao i ia te wā me te tino aronga o tēnei ki ngā painga mō rātou.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaeretanga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora, me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia:

  • whakawhanake te whānau i ngā rautaki ka hāpai i a rātou ki te ako me te whakamahi i te reo Māori
  • toro atu ngā kaimahi ki te ārahitanga o te kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe, hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo Māori o ngā tamariki.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakatūruki Arotake Māori Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kerepehi is in the small rural community of Kerepehi. The nearest township is Paeroa. Children from the local community attend the kōhanga reo which is adjacent to the marae. The whānau have succeeded in addressing several challenges over the last two years. They have increased the roll. Organisational and management practices are kept at a high standard. A well thought out enrolment pack has been written to help whānau transition into the kōhanga reo.

Whānau contributed to this ERO review by designing an evaluation question. They chose Mana Reo as the focus and wanted to know

How well do children understand and use te reo Māori?

Children understand te reo Māori and can respond to instructions and simple phrases, however they tend to express themselves in English. The use of te reo Māori is an area for development for children and the whānau members. Whānau acknowledge there is a need for te reo Māori to be better promoted at home. Whānau are seeking ways to develop their te reo Māori capability. Kaimahi are good speakers of te reo Māori. They are developing positive strategies that support children to use and speak te reo Māori.

Children are stimulated in their learning. They have opportunities to participate in independent learning, tikanga practices, kaupapa and adult led activities. Children have easy access to a wide range of resources which cater for their specific abilities and ages. The older children have opportunities to take lead roles during routines and activities. Children enjoy activities that encourage and promote creativity and physical movement. Kaimahi support children in positive ways as they make new discoveries through their play.

Children are well supported by their whānau. The whānau are keen to support the kōhanga reo and most whānau attend regular monthly hui. Self review practices are rigorous and lead to improvements for children. There are effective processes for strategic planning and the management of the kōhanga reo.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Reo.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Mana Reo

Background

How well do children understand and use te reo Māori?

Children understand te reo Māori and can respond to instructions and simple phrases, however they tend to express themselves in English.

Areas of good performance

Children are stimulated in their learning. They have opportunities to participate in independent learning, tikanga practices, kaupapa and adult led opportunities. Children move easily between the indoor and outdoor areas. They have access to a wide range of resources which cater to their specific abilities and ages. The older children have opportunities to take lead roles during routines and different activities. Children enjoy different activities that encourage and promote creativity and physical movement. Kaimahi support children in positive ways as children follow their interests and explore te ao Māori.

Children receive effective support from a committed teaching team. Kaimahi endeavour to provide good care and development for their children. Kaimahi are new to their roles. They work together well and support each other. Whānau come in and help out when they are able. Kaimahi provide valuable support for each other and the children.

Children have opportunities to explore significant events. Children enjoy exploring different textures and materials as they create decorations using glitter and bright colours. Children are reminded of the significance of the Pohutukawa tree at this time of the year.

Children learn and play in an attractive, well designed environment. Whānau take good care of their kōhanga reo. The building is in good condition in the indoor and outdoor areas. Whānau carry out regular hazard checks. Creative mural work in the dining and sleeping rooms helps to brighten these areas. The children enjoy their environment and have a strong sense of belonging.

Area for Improvement

Children understand te reo Māori and can respond to instructions and simple phrases, however they tend to express themselves in English. Kaimahi are good speakers of te reo Māori who speak te reo Māori consistently throughout the day. Kaimahi acknowledge they do not readily encourage children to use and speak te reo Māori. Although children are able to understand and follow instructions and simple phrases, they tend to speak English when interacting with each other and kaimahi.

Children do not have the active support of their whānau when they learn te reo Māori. There is a need for whanau to undertake te reo Māori classes in order to support their children’s language development.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

Children benefit from regular self review practices. Self review practices are efficient. Whānau consistently review their policies and procedures each month. Regular checks of the environment for potential hazards are made. There are clear reporting and monitoring processes for financial management, professional development, personnel management, and fundraising. Whānau are committed to providing an effective service for their children.

Children’s interests and wellbeing are central to the future direction of the kōhanga reo. There is a long term plan in place. Whānau are clear about a range of projects they have planned for the kōhanga reo. There is a strong commitment among whānau to provide a well maintained and resource environment for their children.

Children experience programmes that are stimulating and well planned. Weekly plans provide good guidance and support when kaimahi deliver programmes to children. Kaimahi provide purposeful learning and activities for children.

Children’s progress and achievement is monitored through the use of various assessment tools. Individual portfolios are informative and include samples of children’s work, observations, anecdotal notes, parent feedback and an analysis of each child’s progress against Te Whāriki. Kaimahi acknowledge whānau should have access to children’s assessment information to keep them aware of their progress.

Evaluation of the children’s learning programme is a feature area. Kaimahi have a clear idea of the purpose and value of evaluation. They consistently discuss programme items and monitor any new children in terms of their disposition and participation. Kaimahi discuss their teaching practices and the effectiveness of activities. Children benefit from regular reflection that has a clear focus on their interests.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends:

  • whānau develop strategies to assist them to learn and use te reo Māori
  • encourage kaimahi to seek guidance from district kaupapa kaimahi to help support children’s reo Māori development.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
