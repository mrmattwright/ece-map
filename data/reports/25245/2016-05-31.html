Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūau Te Moana-nui-ā-Kiwa ki Glen Innes, i Tāmaki-makau-rau. He wāteatea te kōhanga reo, ā, e āhei ana ki te poipoi i te kotahi rau o ngā tamariki. Kua whai wāhi ētahi o te whānau ki te kōhanga reo mō ngā tau maha. E puāwai ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whakamana ana i te reo Māori.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūau Te Moana-nui-ā-Kiwa i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūau Te Moana-nui-ā-Kiwa ki ngā papa o Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Pūau Te Moana-nui-a-Kiwa, ki Glen Innes, i Tāmaki-makau-rau. He tino hononga ki waenga i te kura me te kōhanga reo. I āta tautokona te whakatūnga o te kōhanga reo e ngā tāngata matua i whai wāhi nui ai ki Te Rōpū Wāhine Māori Toko i te Ora. Ko te kōhanga reo nei tētahi o ngā kōhanga reo rahi rawa atu o Aotearoa. I te marama o Whiringa-ā-rangi, i te tau 2015, i hūnuku ai te whānau ki tō rātou whare kōhanga reo hou ki taua papa tonu.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e tohu i tō rātou tūmāia hei ākonga?

E tūhura ana ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki tētahi wāhi e tautāwhitia ana rātou mā te whanaungatanga. Ka kitea tō rātou tūmāia hei ākonga, ā, e poipoia ana rātou i tētahi tino wāhi ako.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E rumakina ana ngā tamariki, ā, e whakamanahia ana te reo Māori. He mārama ngā whakawhitinga kōrero a ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki. He pakari ētahi o ngā tamariki tuākana ki te whakautu kōrero mā te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā waiata, ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, me ngā mōteatea hei whakapakari ake i ngā mōhiotanga. Kei te piki haere te tūmāia o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ka uru atu ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo whai wāhitanga e whakamana ana i te pūtake o ā rātou mahi. E āhei ana rātou ki te tūhura i te taiao, puta noa i te kōhanga reo. Ka whakahono rātou i te ao māoriori ki ngā atua Māori. Ka āta whakawerohia ngā tamariki – ā-tinana, ā-hinengaro hoki, puta noa i te rā. He pai te whakahaeretanga me te whakaritenga a ngā kaimahi i te hōtaka ako. He ākonga māia ngā tamariki.

He tūturu, he whakawhirinakitanga hoki tō ngā hononga o ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu. Ka ahu mai ngā taunekeneke i te whanaungatanga. Ka whakanui ngā kaimahi i ngā whakamātau me ngā whakatutukitanga a ngā tamariki. He pārekareka ki ngā pēpi tētahi hōtaka e whai pānga nui ana ki te tākaro, ā, e aro ana hoki ki ō rātou matea ā-tinana, ā-waiora hoki. Nā tēnei, ka ahu whakamua rātou i runga anō i tō rātou hiahia, ā, ka tūhura anō hoki rātou i tō rātou wāhi. Ka āta mātakina ngā tamariki. Kua waia ngā tamariki ki ngā mahinga, ā, he ngāwari te whakawhiti haere ki ngā momo taumahi. He harikoa ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka whai hua ngā mahi a te whānau ki te whakahaere i tō rātou kōhanga reo. Kua tū te kaiwhakahaere ki tōna tūnga mahi mō ngā tau 25. Ka whai hua ngā pūnaha e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te tari. Kua whakaritea ngā tuhinga katoa e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi. Kua oti atu te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi. He mārama ki ngā kaimahi ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga, me ō rātou mōtika hei kaimahi. He tika, he pono hoki te whānau hei kaituku mahi.

He matawhānui te tūtohinga, ā, he mārama ngā aratohu o roto mō te whakahaeretanga me te whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki te wā o mua e tohu ana i ngā whakarerekētanga papai i whakatutukihia ai i roto i te roanga o ngā tau. Kua arotakengia, kua whakahoungia hoki ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga. Kua whakaritea ngā kaupapa here katoa e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. He tino mā te kōhanga reo. E whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whakamana ana i tō rātou hauora me tō rātou noho haumaru.

Kua āta tuhia ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai. Ka āta tohu ngā mahere i te āhua o ngā mahi ki te whakatinana i ngā taumahi. Ka whai hua te aroturukitanga o te ako me te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki. Ka whakaemi ngā kaimahi i ngā mōhiohio mā ngā tuhinga kōrerorero me ngā kitenga. Ka kitea ki ngā pūrongo aromātai te kaha o te whakaaro huritao i roto i te hōtaka akoranga, i ngā wā katoa. He pai te whakamōhio i te whānau mō te ako me te ahu whakamua a ā rātou tamariki.

He whāinga, he wawata hoki tō te whānau mō tō rātou kōhanga reo i roto i ngā tau kei te heke mai. Tū ai ngā hui ā-whānau i ia rua-marama. He pakari ngā whakawhitinga kōrero e pā ana ki te maha o ngā tūmomo take matua e whai pānga ana ki ngā whakahaeretanga me ngā whakapaitanga ki te kōhanga reo. Ka arotahi aua whakawhitinga kōrero me aua pūrongo ki te whānuitanga o ngā whakapaitanga.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Me whakamahia tonu e te whānau te aromātai o roto ki te aroturuki i te maha o ngā whakaritenga me ngā putanga hei tautoko i ā rātou whakataunga.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā:

  • me whakamahia tonutia e te whānau te aromātai o roto hei tautoko i ā rātou whakataunga.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūau o Te Moananui-ā-Kiwa is in Glen Innes, Auckland. The spacious kōhanga reo can cater for up to a hundred children. Some whānau have been involved in the kōhanga reo for many years. Children thrive in an environment where te reo Māori is used everyday.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūau Te Moana-nui-ā-Kiwa will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūau Te Moananui-ā-Kiwa is on the grounds of Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Puau Te Moananui-ā-Kiwa in Glen Innes, Auckland. There is a close relationship between the kura and the kōhanga reo. The establishment of the kōhanga reo was supported by key people who were active members of the Māori Women’s Welfare League. The kōhanga reo is one of the largest in Aotearoa. In November 2015 the whānau moved in to their new kōhanga reo premises on the site.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children show their confidence as learners?

Children are exploring te reo Māori in an environment where they are embraced by whanaungatanga. They show confidence as learners and are nurtured within a positive learning environment.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children learn in an immersion environment where te reo Māori is valued. Kaimahi are clear in their communications with children. Some of the older children are confident to respond in te reo Māori. Kaimahi use waiata, karakia, hīmene, and mōteatea to build knowledge. Children speak te reo Māori with increasing confidence.

Children experience a range of opportunities where their work is viewed as meaningful. They are able to explore the environment around their kōhanga reo. They connect the natural world to ngā atua Māori. Children are challenged physically and mentally throughout their day. The learning programme is well managed and organised by kaimahi. Children are confident learners.

Children have genuine, supportive relationships with others. Interactions are based on whanaungatanga. Kaimahi acknowledge children’s efforts and achievements. Babies enjoy a play based programme that responds to their physical and emotional needs. This enables them to move at their pace and explore the physical environment. Children are well supervised. Routines are familiar to children and transitions between activities are smooth. Children are happy.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau manage the operations of their kōhanga reo effectively. The kaiwhakahaere has been in the position for the past 25 years. Office and administration systems are efficient. All personnel documentation is in place. Kaimahi appraisals have been completed. Kaimahi are clear of their roles and responsibilities and their rights as employees. The whānau are fair and just employers.

The charter is comprehensive and provides clear guidelines for the management and operations of the kōhanga reo. Historical information clearly shows the positive changes that have occurred over many years. The policies and procedures have been reviewed and updated. All health and safety policies are in place. The kōhanga reo premises are immaculate. Children enjoy an environment where their health and safety is a priority.

Planning, assessment and evaluation practices are well documented. Planning clearly shows how activities will be implemented. Children’s learning and progress is efficiently monitored. Kaimahi gather information through anecdotal notes and observations. Evaluation reports show that there is constant reflection on the learning programme. Whānau are well informed of their children’s learning and progress.

The whānau have goals and aspirations for the future of their kōhanga reo. Whānau hui are held bi-monthly. There is robust discussion around a number of key issues that impact on the operations and improvements of the kōhanga reo. These discussions and reporting is focused on overall improvements.

Key Next Steps

Whānau continue using internal evaluation to monitor overall performance and outcomes to support them with their decision making.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO developed the following recommendation that:

  • whānau continue to use ongoing internal evaluation to support them in their decision making.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūau o Te Moananui-ā-Kiwa is in Glen Innes, Auckland. The spacious kōhanga reo can cater for up to a hundred children. Some whānau have been involved in the kōhanga reo for many years. Children thrive in an environment where te reo Māori is used everyday.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūau Te Moana-nui-ā-Kiwa will be in three years.
