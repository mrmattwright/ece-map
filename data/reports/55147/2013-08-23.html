2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Manawanui ki Pāharakeke, e pā tata atu ana ki te Kura Hapori o Kimi Ora. He hononga ā-whakapapa tō te rōpū iti o ngā mema o te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki a rātou anō. He pai tā rātou mahi ngātahi, ā, e ngākau nui ana rātou ki te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau me ngā kaimahi ko Mana Reo te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

He mātau ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. He tino tauira reo rātou mō ngā tamariki. He whānuitanga ngā tūmomo rautaki reo e whakamahia ana hei whakawhanake i te pitomata o ngā tamariki ki te reo. He whakahirahira tēnei, nō te mea, he kōhungahunga tonu te nuinga o ngā tamariki, ki waenga i te rua ki te toru o ngā tau.

He maioha ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi me ngā kēmu, ā, e whai pānga ana ēnei ki ngā nekehanga, te oro, te manawataki, me te tāruarua. He mātau te kaiako ki te whakauru mai i ngā taputapu me ngā tohu ki te hōtaka akoranga, hei akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i ō rātou ake tūmomo rongo ā-tinana. He maha ngā wā ka kanikani ngā tamariki, ka whakamahi hoki rātou i ngā kupu matua e kōrerohia ana i ia te wā. E pakari haere ana ngā tamariki ki te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero mā te reo Māori.

He ritenga, he hanganga hoki tā te hōtaka o ia rā, ā, ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki kōhungahunga. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga, me te whai wāhi hoki ki ngā tikanga me ngā tūmomo kaupapa. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi ki ngā taumahi e whakanui ana i te pāngarau, te reo matatini, te kori tinana, te taha auaha, ngā puoro, me te hanga poraka. Ka whai pānga ngā rauemi me ngā taumahi ki ngā tūmomo āhuatanga Māori, whakaritenga Māori, ā, he whānui hoki te matatini, te wero hoki mō ngā tūmomo reanga. Kua whai hua ngā kaimahi ki te waihanga i tētahi taiao kia wheako ai ngā tamariki i te angitu, me te pārekareka.

Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā angamahi whakamahere e whai hua ana. Kei roto i ngā mahere o te wā roa, wā poto hoki, ko ngā putanga, ngā kaupapa, me ngā taumahi a ngā tamariki, ā, ka hono hoki ki ngā mātāpono o Te Whāriki. Ko te reo Māori tētahi aronga matua i ngā kaimahi e whakamahere ana i ngā tūmomo kaupapa. Kua noho te kaiako ki te kōhanga reo mō tētahi wā poto. I roto i tēnei wā, kua whakaritea e ia tētahi pūkete mā ia tamaiti. He manawa whakahī tō te whānau mō ngā whakatutukitanga a ā rātou tamariki, me ā rātou wheako ki te kōhanga reo.

He ōpaki te aromātai hōtaka. Whakaaro huritao ai ngā kaimahi mō ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o te hōtaka o ngā tamariki i ia rā, engari kāhore rātou e whakatakoto tuhinga ana mō aua kōrerotanga. E wātea ana te tautoko tonu o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo mō te kōhanga reo nei. Me tahuri rātou ki ngā take hauora, haumaru hoki. Ko ētahi o aua take ko ngā papa o te kīhini me te wāhi kai, ngā whakarawe me ngā rauhanga. E noho mōrearea ana ngā tamariki nā te āhua o te pātaka mātao me te pātaka tio. E turuturu ana ki te whare paku o ngā pākeke, ā, he haunga hoki e tohu ana i ētahi raru ki ngā paipa wai.

He tika te whakahaere i te tari ki te kōhanga reo. He mārama ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakahaere pūtea, ngā take kaimahi, te whakawhanaketanga ngaio, te whakatakoto haere i ngā mauhanga, te whakamahere rautaki, me te arotake whaiaro.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Manawanui kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Manawanui.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Reo.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Reo

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e ngā kaimahi me te whānau ko Mana Reo te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. I tāutuhia e rātou, ko Mana Reo te reo whakawhitiwhiti e pou here ana i te katoa o ngā wheako akoranga o ngā tamariki i a rātou e noho ana ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ngā tauira o te reo Māori.He mātau ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whai hua rātou hei tauira reo, ā, ka kōrero rātou i te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa, puta noa i te rā. Ka whakawhanakehia te pitomata o ngā tamariki ki te reo nā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki reo. He mea nui tēnei, nō te mea, he kōhungahunga tonu te nuinga o ngā tamariki, ki waenga i te rua me te toru o ngā tau. Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao reo Māori e tino whai hua ana.

Te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki.He maioha ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi me ngā kēmu, tae atu ki ngā nekehanga, ngā oro, te manawataki, me te tāruarua. Ka whakahihiri te kaiako i te ako o te reo, nā tōna mōhio ki te whakamahi i ngā taputapu me ngā tohu ka akiaki i ngā kōhungahunga ki te whakamahi i ō rātou ake tūmomo rongo ā-tinana. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whai wāhi ki ngā kaupapa ōkawa, pērā i te karakia, te hīmene, te pepeha, te waiata, me te mōteatea. He maha ngā wā ka kanikani rātou, ā, ka whakanui hoki i ngā kupu matua e whakamahia ana i ia te wā. E pakari haere ana ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou whakawhitinga kōrero mā te reo Māori.

Ngā hononga me ngā taunekeneke.He atawhai, he whakaute, he tūturu hoki ngā hononga me ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. I ngā wā katoa, ka whakanui, ka akiaki hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā whakamātau me ngā whakatutukitanga a ngā tamariki. Ka tautoko ngā tamariki tuākana i ngā tēina, ā, ka whakauru hoki i a rātou ki ngā taumahi, ngā tikanga, me ngā mahinga mā te kōrero i te reo Māori. He iti noa ngā tūmomo rautaki whakahaere whanonga e whakamahia ana, nā te āta whai wāhi nui o ngā tamariki ki te hōtaka e whakahihiri ana i a rātou. E pou here ana te whanaungatanga i ngā taunekeneke katoa ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki.

Te taiao akoranga.He mā, he hihiri, he wāteatea hoki te taiao tākaro o roto, ā, he pai hoki te whakatakotoranga. Kua whakatūngia ngā pokapū akoranga puta noa i rūma tākaro, ā, he nui ngā rauemi ki aua pokapū. Ka hāngai ngā rauemi ako me ngā tohu ki ngā āhuatanga Māori me ngā mahi Māori. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te haereere ki waenga i ngā taumahi, ngā rauhanga, me ngā wāhi hoki o roto, o waho hoki. He hāneanea, he mauritau, he harikoa hoki te noho o ngā tamariki ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo e whakanuia ana te ao Māori.

Te hōtaka o ngā tamariki i ia rā.He ritenga, he hanganga hoki tā te hōtaka o ia rā, ā, ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki kōhungahunga. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga, me te whai wāhi hoki ki ngā tikanga me ngā tūmomo kaupapa. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi ki ngā taumahi e whakanui ana i te pāngarau, te reo matatini, te kori tinana, te taha auaha, ngā puoro, me te hanga poraka. He whai wāhitanga mā rātou ki te tūhura i ngā tūmomo taonga tuku iho, ngā raranga harakeke, me ngā taonga Māori. He whānui hoki te matatini, te wero hoki i roto i ngā rauemi me ngā taumahi, ā, ka poipoi mārika tēnei i ngā tūmomo reanga. Kua whai hua ngā kaimahi ki te waihanga i tētahi taiao kia wheako ai ngā tamariki i te angitu, me te pārekareka.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai ki ngā kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o ngā whānau o ngā kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki, me te whānau. Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te ngākau nuitanga o te whānau.He hononga ā-whakapapa tō te katoa o ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki a rātou anō. E ngākau nui ana rātou ki tō rātou kōhanga reo, me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. He pai te mahi ngātahi a tēnei rōpū whānau me ngā kaimahi, ā, kua roa tā rātou whai wāhi ki te kōhanga reo. Ka ngākau nui rātou ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā tūranga me ngā mahi whakahaere. Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whakanuia ana e te whānau ngā uaratanga me ngā tikanga Māori, ā, ka tautokona hoki ā rātou akoranga, ō rātou whakawhanaketanga hoki.

Te whakamahere rautaki.He pakari te whānau ki te whakamahere mō te aronga whakamua o te kōhanga reo. Ka hono te mahere ki Te Ara Tūāpae me ngā whāinga rautaki a Ngāti Kahungunu. Ka hāngai ngā kaupapa matua ki te reo Māori, te whakawhanaketanga ngaio, ngā arohaehae, te whai wāhitanga a te whānau, te tatauranga tamariki, me te whakamahere hōtaka. Pūrongotia ai, whakahoungia ai hoki te mahere i ia te wā. Ka ārahi ngā mahere mō meāke nei i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero e hāpai ana i te waiora o ngā tamariki me tā rātou ako.

Te arotake whaiaro.Kua āta whakaritea te arotake whaiaro. Whakawhitiwhiti kōrero ai, pūrongo ai hoki te whānau i ngā tūmomo wāhanga o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ko ētahi o ngā tino take i hāngai ai ki ngā mahi auru, me te heke iho o te tatauranga tamariki. Kua whakatinanahia e te whānau ētahi tūmomo aronga ki te whakatutuki haere i aua take. Nā te arotake whaiaro, ka whakatakotohia e te whānau te aronga whakamua o te kōhanga reo.

Te whakamahere hōtaka.Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i tētahi angamahi whakamahere e whai hua ana. Kei ngā mahere o te wā roa me te wā poto, ko ngā putanga, ngā kaupapa, me ngā taumahi a ngā tamariki, ā, ka hono hoki ki ngā mātāpono o Te Whāriki. Ko te reo Māori tētahi kaupapa matua i ngā kaimahi e whakamahere ana i ngā tūmomo kaupapa. Ka whakahihiri, ka whakaongaonga hoki ngā kaupapa i ngā tamariki.

Ngā whakaritenga aromatawai.Kua noho te kaiako ki te kōhanga reo mō tētahi wā poto noa iho. I roto i taua wā, kua whakaritea e ia ngā pūkete ā ia tamaiti. Ko ētahi o ngā mahi, ko ngā mahi ā-kaupapa, ko ngā mahi toi a ngā tamariki me ngā kōrerorero, me tētahi rārangi arowhai e hāngai ana ki te reo matatini, te pāngarau, me te whakawhanaketanga reo Māori o ia tamaiti. E tino wātea ana mōhiohio aromatawai a ngā tamariki ki ngā whānau. He manawa whakahī tō te whānau mō ngā whakatutukitanga me ngā wheako o ā rātou tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te aromātai hōtaka.He ōpaki te aromātai hōtaka. Ka whakaaro huritao ngā kaimahi i ia te wā mō ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o te hōtaka o ia rā mō ngā tamariki, engari, kāhore ēnei kōrerotanga e tuhia ana hei mauhanga. Nō nā tata tonu nei rātou i tīmata ai ki te whakawhiti kōrero mō te pūtake me te whai hua o te aromātai ki te kaipupuri raihana. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki, ka pupū ake hoki ngā putanga pai ake, mā te arotake whaiaro.

Ngā take hauora, haumaru hoki.He take hauora, he take haumaru hoki hei whakapai ake. Me horoi ngā papa, ngā rauhanga me ngā taonga o te kīhini me te wāhi kai, nā te nui o te puehu me te otaota. Ko te pātaka mātao me te pātaka tio e tū ana ki waho, he āhuatanga mōrearea mō ngā tamariki. Me tango i aua pātaka, nō te mea, kāhore he raka, ā, e pāngia ana hoki e ngā tūmomo huarere o te wā. E turuturu ana ki te whare paku o ngā pākeke, ā, he haunga hoki e tohu ana i ētahi raru ki ngā paipa wai.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Manawanui I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o, tētahiTauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhangame tētahiRārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

I te wā o te arotake i tāutuhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ētahi wāhanga tautukunga kāore i tutuki. Kia taea ai te whakatikatika i ēnei, me mātua whakarite te whānau o te kōhanga ki te:

5.1 tango i te pātaka mātao me te pātaka tio e tū ana ki te wāhi o waho, hei āta whakarite i te noho haumaru mō ngā tamariki 
[R24, 1 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 1989]

5.2 noho haumaru, te noho mā hoki o ngā whare, ngā rauhanga, ngā whakarawe, me ngā taonga, mā te horoi i ia te wā
[R24, Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 1989]

5.3 noho haumaru o ngā rauhanga ki te whare paku o ngā pākeke, mā te whakatikatika i ngā paipa wai.  
[R 19, Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 1989]

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Manawanui is in Flaxmere, a short distance from the Kimi Ora Community School. The small group of whānau members and kaimahi all have either direct or indirect whakapapa connections to each other. They work well together and are committed to the kōhanga reo.

Whānau and kaimahi chose Mana Reo as the focus for the review.

Kaimahi are competent speakers of te reo Māori. They are effective language models for children. A wide range of language strategies are used to develop children’s language potential. This is important because most children are very young and range between the ages of two to three years old.

Children have an appreciation of activities and games that include action, sound, rhythm and repetition. The kaiako skilfully includes objects and symbols that encourage children to use their different senses. Children often dance and emphasise key words that are used repeatedly. Children are becoming confident in their te reo Māori communications.

The daily programme has balance, structure and great appeal for young children. There are opportunities for children to follow their interests, participate in tikanga and engage in different kaupapa. Children are able to take part in activities that promote numeracy, literacy, physical play, creativity, music and block construction. Resources and activities reflect various Māori scenes and practices and vary in complexity and challenge for the different age groups. Kaimahi have successfully created an environment where children are able to experience success and enjoyment.

Kaimahi use an effective planning framework. Long and short-term plans include outcomes, kaupapa, children’s activities and link to the principles ofTe Whāriki. Te reo Māori is a priority when kaimahi plan different kaupapa. The kaiako has been at the kōhanga reo for a short period of time. 4 Findings

Mana Reo

Background

Kaimahi and whānau chose Mana Reo as the focus area for this review. They have defined Mana Reo as the language of communication that underpins all children’s formal and informal learning experiences while at kōhanga reo.

Areas of good performance

Te reo Māori language models. Kaimahi are competent speakers of te reo Māori. They are effective language models who continuously speak te reo Māori throughout the day. Children’s language potential is developed through the use of a wide range of language strategies. This is important because most children are very young and range between the ages of two to three years old. Children are immersed in an effective te reo Māori environment.

Children’s te reo Māori development. Children have an appreciation of activities and games that include action, sound, rhythm and repetition. The kaiako makes language learning interesting by skilfully using objects and symbols that encourage young children to use their different senses. Children enjoy participating in formalities including karakia, himene, pepeha, waiata and moteatea. They often dance and emphasise key words that are used repeatedly. Children are becoming confident in their te reo Māori communications.

Relationships and interactions. Relationships and interactions between kaimahi and children are warm, respectful and genuine. Kaimahi constantly acknowledge and encourage children’s efforts and achievements. The older children support and include their younger peers in activities, tikanga, routines and with te reo Māori. There is minimal need for behaviour management strategies because children are fully engaged in a programme that keeps them motivated. Whanaungatanga underpins all interactions between kaimahi and children.

Learning environment. The indoor playroom environment is clean, vibrant, orderly and spacious. Well resourced learning centres are positioned throughout the playroom. Learning resources and symbols reflect various Māori scenes and practices. Children are able to move freely between activities, facilities and the indoor and outdoor areas. Children are comfortable, relaxed and happy within the kōhanga reo environment that reflects te ao Maori.

The children’s daily programme. The daily programme has balance, structure and has great appeal for young children. There are opportunities for children to follow their interests, participate in tikanga and engage in different kaupapa. Children are able to take part in activities that promote numeracy, literacy, physical play, creativity, music and block construction. They have opportunities to explore different artefacts, flax work and natural resources. Resources and activities vary in complexity and challenge and cater well for the different age groups. Kaimahi have successfully created an environment where children are able to experience success and enjoyment.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau commitment. All kaimahi and whānau have direct or indirect whakapapa connections to each other. They are committed to their kōhanga reo and the philosophy of te kōhanga reo. This small group of whānau members and kaimahi work well together and have had a long association with the kōhanga reo. They willingly share roles and management functions. Children learn in an environment where whānau embrace Māori values and practices and are supportive of their learning and development.

Strategic planning. Whānau confidently plan for the future direction of the kōhanga reo. The plan links to Te Ara Tūāpae and the Kahungunu strategic goals. Priorities include te reo Māori, professional development, appraisals, whānau participation, roll numbers and programme planning. The plan is regularly reported on and updated. Future planning guides whānau discussions which supports children’s wellbeing and learning.

Self review. Self review is well established. Whānau regularly discuss and report on different parts of the kōhanga reo operations. Critical issues have included vandalism and decreasing roll numbers. Whānau have implemented various approaches in an attempt to address these concerns. Self review enables whānau to determine the future direction of the kōhanga reo.

Programme planning. Kaimahi use an effective planning framework. Long and short-term plans include outcomes, kaupapa, children’s activities and link to the principles ofTe Whāriki. Te reo Māori is a priority when kaimahi plan different kaupapa. Kaupapa generate interest and excitement among children.

Assessment practices. The kaiako has been at the kōhanga reo for a short period of time. During this time she has established profile folders for every child. Practices include kaupapa work, children’s artwork with comments and a checklist which focuses on each child’s literacy, numeracy and te reo Māori development. Whānau have easy access to children’s assessment information. Whānau are proud of their children’s achievements and the experiences they have at kōhanga reo.

Areas for development

Programme evaluation. Programme evaluation is informal. Kaimahi regularly reflect on different aspects of the children’s daily programme but do not keep records of these discussions. They have recently begun to discuss the purpose and value of evaluation with the licensee. Evaluation will benefit children and lead to improved outcomes.

Health and safety matters. There are some health and safety matters that need attention. The kitchen and dining floors, furnishings and furniture need cleaning because of the accumulation of dust and debris. The fridge and freezer units that are located outdoors are potential risks to children. These objects need to be removed as they do not have locks and are exposed to different weather conditions. The adult toilet area has a leak and musky odour indicating plumbing concerns.

  • health, safety and welfare

  • financial and property management.

5.2 ensure the premises, furniture, furnishings and fittings are kept safe and hygienic through regular cleaning
[R24, (ECC) Regulations 1989]

5.3 ensure the adult sanitary facilities are safe by addressing the plumbing concerns.
[R19, (ECC) Regulations 1989]

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
