1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pūtake Whakatupuranga ki Elgin, tētahi hapori iti ki te tāone o Tūranga-nui-a-Kiwa. He kōhanga reo nui tēnei, ā, ka taea te poipoi i ngā tamariki 70 i roto i ngā whare e rua. He tino rahi te wāhi tākaro o waho, ā, kua whakaruruhautia te ara hīkoi e whakahono ana i aua whare e rua. Mō ngā pēpi me ngā tamariki kōhungahunga te whare o mua, ā, mō ngā tamariki tuākana te whare o muri. He mā, he hihiri, he muramura hoki ngā whare, ā, he nui hoki ngā rauemi.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. I waihangatia e te whānau te pātai aromātai matua, hei ārahi i te aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, arā:

‘He pēhea te hāpai i ngā tamariki kia tū pakari, kia tū mātau hoki rātou ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui?’

E tino rumakina ana ngā tamariki o tēnei kōhanga reo ki te hōhonutanga, te whānuitanga, me te tūturutanga hoki o ngā horopaki akoranga. E tino whanake ana tō rātou māia ki te ārahi, ki te kōrero hoki i te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Ka arotahi, ka noho whakaongaonga hoki ngā tamariki ki te tūhura haere i te ao whānui. Ka āta noho mōhio rātou ki ngā mātauranga o te ao Māori. Ka kitea te manawa whakahī, te mōhiotanga hoki o ngā tamariki ki ā rātou taonga tuku iho, me te reo, ngā tikanga, me ngā whakapono. Ka kōkiri rātou i ā rātou ake akoranga, ā, he ākonga hoki e noho maioha ana ki te whakatewhatewha haere i ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o te taiao māori, tūturu hoki. Ka whai kawenga ngā tamariki, ā, he tino kaitiaki hoki o tō rātou taiao.

He whānui te puna mōhiotanga ngaio, me ngā pūkenga, te mātanga hoki o ngā kaimahi me te whānau. Ka ū rātou ki ngā tino taumata o ngā whakaritenga, ā, kua roa nei rātou e pēnei ana i nāianei. Kua whakatutukihia ngā putanga hihiri, nā te ngākau nui, te noho pūmau hoki ki te āta whakawhanaketanga o te whānau me ā rātou tamariki i roto i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. He tino mārama te whānau ki ō rātou wawata. Ka whakapūmau rātou i te tikanga o tā rātou tūtohinga i runga i te pono.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake, ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

‘He pēhea te hāpai i ngā tamariki kia tū pakari, kia tū mātau hoki rātou ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui?’

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga, te whānuitanga, me te tūturutanga hoki o ngā horopaki akoranga.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te maha o ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te ako, i runga anō i Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te:

  • ako i ngā atua
  • whai i ngā mahinga, me te kite i te whakatinanatanga o ngā tikanga Māori
  • whakawhanake i tō rātou ake māramatanga ki ngā mahi a te tangata whenua
  • whakahono i ngā atua ki ngā kaupeka o te tau me ā rātou ake wheako
  • whai wāhi ki te ārahi, puta noa i te hōtaka
  • āta tūhura haere i te taiao
  • whai wāhi ki ngā tūmomo kaupapa i te hapori whānui
  • ako ki te taha o ō rātou hoa, me te mōhio haere ki ngā whakapapa o ētahi atu tamariki.

Ka wheako, ka ako hoki ngā tamariki i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Ka whakatairanga aua wheako i te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui.

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā hononga atawhai e manaaki ana, e poipoi ana hoki i a rātou. He pakari rātou ki te taunekeneke ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ngā pakeke hoki. Tae mai ai ki te kōhanga reo, he tākare rātou ki te kite anō i ngā tamariki, me te whai wāhi atu hoki ki a rātou. Kei te tino mōhio ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki, ā, he mātau tā rātou patapatai, hei hāpai i ō rātou whakaaro, hei whakawhānui hoki.

He tino tauira ngā tamariki tuākana, ā, ka tū ki te hāpai i ō rātou tēina. Ka kitea tō rātou atawhai, tō rātou whakaute, me tō rātou manawanui mō ō rātou hoa i ngā wā o ngā taumahi, ngā mahinga, me ngā tūmomo kawa. He āhuatanga mīharo ki ngā tamariki tēina te whai wāhi atu ki ngā rōpū, ā, ka ngākau nui ki te whakahuahua i ngā kōrero a ō rātou hoa i ngā kaupapa nui me ngā mahi nui.

Ko te whanaungatanga tētahi āhuatanga matua ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whai whakaaro nui ki te whai wāhi mai o te whānau, ngā kaumātua, me te whānau whānui. He whai wāhitanga mā ngā whānau katoa ki te whakatau whakaritenga, me te whakatutuki i ngā mahi whakahaere. Tae atu ai rātou ki ngā hui me ngā haerenga o te kōhanga reo i ia te wā, ki te taha o ngā kaumātua. Ka whakapuaki ngā kaumātua i ō rātou mōhiotanga, ō rātou mātauranga hoki ki ngā tamariki, ngā kaimahi, me te whānau. Ka whai hua aua hononga ki te whakakotahi, te whakapakari hoki i te whanaungatanga, hei tino tūāpapa mō ngā tamariki i a rātou e tūhura ana, e ako ana hoki.

Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi me te whānau i tētahi taiao me tētahi hōtaka akoranga e akiaki ana i ngā tamariki ki te āta whakamātau, te whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro, te whakatau i ā rātou ake whiringa, me te tākaro takitahi, takirōpū hoki. Ka rongo, ka ako hoki ngā tamariki i ō rātou tātai whakapapa me ngā pou whenua mā ngā waiata, ngā mōteatea, ngā kōrero tuku iho, ngā haka, ngā whakaari, ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, ngā haerenga, me ngā pepeha. Whakaari ai rātou i ia te wā, ā, ka kitea hoki te whakatinanatanga o ngā tikanga i a rātou e tākaro ana, e waiata ana hoki.

He hihiri te taiao akoranga i āta whakatakotohia ai, ā, e whai hua ana ki te whakatutuki i ngā tūmomo rōpū reanga, ngā tama, ngā tamariki pakari, me ia tamaiti. E noho wātea ana ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā rauemi, tae atu ki ngā taonga Māori tuku iho. Ka tino whakawhānui ngā kaimahi i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki mā ngā taumahi whakawhānui, ngā wheako pāngarau, reo matatini hoki, ngā haerenga, me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whakahura i ngā āhuatanga hou i a rātou e tākaro ana, e whakamātau ana hoki.

He kaha ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti whakaaro, ā, e tino rumakina ana rātou ki tētahi taiao o te reo Māori e hāpai ana i a rātou. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i ngā tino tauira o te reo Māori i a rātou e noho ana ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo aronga hei whakaongaonga i ngā akoranga reo mō ngā tamariki. E tino tautokona ana, e tino arahina ana te reo me ngā tikanga Māori o ngā tamariki e ngā kuia me ngā mema o te whānau. He manawanui tō ngā kaimahi, ā, he mātau tā rātou whakapuaki i ngā kīanga, ngā tohutohu, me ngā whakawhitinga kōrero e whai pūtake ana ki te taha o ngā tamariki. Ka āta kōrero te nuinga o ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki ngā tūmomo horopaki whakahoahoa, ā, e āhei ana ki te āta whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro me ō rātou hiahia. Ka ākina, ka hāpaitia hoki ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakawhanake i ō rātou ake pūkenga reo Māori. E puāwai ana te mātau, te māia hoki o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i te pūmanawa o ngā tamariki ki te ārahi, mā ngā huarahi papai. He whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te ārahi i ngā karakia, ngā waiata, ngā taumahi ako, me ngā mahinga, tae atu ki te karanga i te wā kai. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te ārahi i aua taumahi. Ko te kuia te kaiwhakahaere matua o te kōhanga reo, ā, e whakatauira ana tōna āhua i te ārahitanga ki ngā tamariki. Ka kitea e ngā tamariki tōna ū ki te tika, ā, ka whakapūmau ia i ngā tamariki ki te pūtake o ngā whakataunga katoa ki te kōhanga reo. E mōhio ana, e whakatauira ana hoki ngā tamariki i ngā tikanga Māori. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki hei ākonga pakari, hei kaiārahi tākare hoki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He pakari te mōhiotanga o ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere kaupapa mā te rangahau me te āhua o ngā whakaritenga papai ki te ao Māori me te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā mahi whakamahere tētahi āhuatanga pakari. Nā te āhua o aua mahi, ka taea e ngā kaimahi ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā matea o ngā tamariki te whakatutuki mā ngā huarahi ka whai hua. Nā te whakamahere kaupapa ka whai wāhitanga ngā tamariki i ia te wā ki te tūhura i ngā aria e pā ana ki te ao Māori, tae atu ki ngā kōrero tuku iho me ngā hononga o te Tai Rāwhiti. He ngāwari ngā mahere mā ngā tamariki tēina, ā, ko te whāinga o aua mahere, ko te whakatutuki i ngā matea o ia tamaiti. He matawhānui te kiko o ngā mahere. Ka whakamahere ngātahi ngā kaimahi, ā, nā tēnei ka whai wāhi atu hoki ngā mema o te whānau.

Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā pakiwaitara ako, ngā kōrerorero, me ngā kitenga hei whakaemi i ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki ngā akoranga me te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki. Tāpiri atu ki tēnā, ka whakaemi ngā kaimahi i ngā tauira mahi a ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakamahi hoki i ngā whakaahua hei tohu i ngā huihuinga, ngā taumahi, me ngā taumata. He ātaahua, he hihiri hoki ngā pūkete e whakatakoto ana i te noho o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Ka kōrerohia, ka tuhia hoki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga i ia te wā.

He mātātoa ngā kaimahi i te wā ka tāutu rātou i ngā wāhanga o ā rātou mahi, o te hōtaka rānei, hei whakapai ake. E tika ana ā rātou mahi ki te whakatika i aua wāhanga. I ngā wā katoa ka oke ngā kaimahi ki te whakapai haere, hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ko te whakahaeretanga me ngā mahere a te whānau ka waihangatia, ka ahu mai hoki i runga anō i tēnā ka whai hua ki ngā tamariki me te whānau, ā, ko te reo Māori hoki tētahi aronga matua. Ka kitea mā ngā āhuatanga e whai ake nei, arā:

  • e hāngai ana ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga ki te wā
  • he mārama te pūrongo i ngā pūtea, ā, ka whakatakoto ki te whānau i ngā hui o ia marama
  • kua āta whakaritea ngā take kaimahi. He tuhinga tūranga mahi ā ngā kaimahi, ā, ka whakatutuki hoki ā rātou arohaehae
  • nā te pai o ngā pūrongo ki ngā hui ā-whānau o ia marama, ka whakatutukihia te arotake o roto i ia te wā
  • kua tāutuhia e te whānau ētahi kaupapa matua mō te whakawhanake tonutanga o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Ko te kuia te kaiwhakahaere matua o te kōhanga reo, ā, ka whai hua tana pūnaha hei āta whakarite i te rere pai o ngā whakahaeretanga ki te kōhanga reo. He tino kaitautoko ia mō ngā tamariki me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, ā, ka tūwhana ia i te kounga kairangi o ngā tukanga me ngā putanga.

Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi, te whakahaeretanga, me te whānau i te arotake whaiaro hei huarahi ki te whakatutuki mahi, ki te whakaaro huritao hoki, ā, ka arotahi ki te whakapai tonutanga. Ko tā rātou arotake whaiaro nahanaha e whai pūtake ana, ka whai pānga ki te arotake o waho me ngā kaupapa matua o roto, ngā whāinga rautaki hoki. Ka whakahāngaitia te ako ngaio, te whakawhanaketanga ngaio hoki hei hāpai i te whakatutukitanga o ngā whāinga o te kōhanga reo.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaeretanga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora, me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia whakamahi tonu te whānau i te arotake whaiaro hei rauemi mō te whakatutuki mahi, hei huarahi hoki ki te whakaaro huritao, ā, kia aro tēnei ki te whakapai tonutanga.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

27 Whiringa-ā-Rangi 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pūtake Whakatupuranga is in Elgin, a small suburb of Gisborne. This is a large kōhanga reo which can cater for up to 70 children and spans two buildings. The kōhanga reo has an enormous outside play area and a covered walkway that joins the two houses. The front house accommodates the infants and toddlers while the other building caters for the older children. The buildings are clean, well resourced, vibrant and colourful.

The whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as their focus area for this review. The whānau designed the key evaluative question to guide ERO’s evaluation:

‘How effectively are children supported to be confident and competent in te ao Māori and the wider world?”

Children who attend the kōhanga reo are immersed in rich, varied and authentic contexts of learning. They are successfully developing as confident, developing leaders and speakers of te reo Māori me ona tīkanga. Children are focused and stimulated explorers of the wider world. They have a heightened awareness and knowledge of te ao Māori. Children show they are proud and knowledgeable of their cultural heritage, language, customs and beliefs. They are independent and appreciative learners who inquire into aspects of the natural and physical environments. Children act responsibly and are caring kaitiaki of their environment.

Kaimahi and whānau have vast professional knowledge, skills and expertise. They maintain high standards of practice that they are able to sustain over time. The commitment and dedication to the successful development of whānau and their children in the kōhanga reo kaupapa has resulted in exciting outcomes. The whānau understand their aspirations well. They uphold their charter obligations with integrity.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

How effectively are children supported to be confident and competent in te ao Māori and the wider world?

Children are immersed in rich, varied and authentic contexts of learning.

Areas of good performance

The kaimahi and whānau provide many opportunities for the children to learn according to Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Children are able to:

  • learn about ngā atua
  • follow routines and observe tikanga Māori
  • develop their understanding of the tangata whenua role
  • link ngā atua to seasons and their experiences
  • experience leadership opportunities throughout the programme
  • explore their environment confidently
  • participate in experiences in the wider community
  • learn alongside their peers and become familiar with other children’s whakapapa.

Children experience and learn about many different aspects of Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. These experiences enrich children’s knowledge about te ao Māori and the wider world.

Children experience warm, caring and nurturing relationships. They are confident in their interactions with each other and adults. They arrive at the kōhanga reo eager to meet and join in with other children. Kaimahi know the children well and are skilful in asking them questions as a way of helping them with their thought processes and extending their ideas.

The older children are effective models and take a lead role in supporting their younger peers. They show affection, respect and patience towards their peers during activities, routines and different protocols. The younger children value being a member of the group and enjoy mimicking their peers during key events and activities.

Whanaungatanga is a significant feature of the kōhanga reo. High importance is placed on the role and contributions of whanau, kaumatua and wider whānau. All whanau have opportunities to share in decision making and carry out management functions. They regularly attend kōhanga reo events and outings along with kaumatua. Kaumatua share their knowledge and wisdom with children, kaimahi and whanau. These connections help unify and strengthen relationships and provide a sound foundation for children as they explore and learn.

Kaimahi and whānau provide an environment and a learning programme that encourages children to take risks, share their ideas, make their own choices and to play independently or in groups. Children hear and learn about their ancestral ties and landmarks through waiata, moteatea, stories, haka, drama, discussions, trips and pepeha. They regularly role play and include these customary practices as they play and sing.

The learning environment is vibrant, carefully planned and effectively caters for the different age groups, boys, able children and individuals. Children have access to a wealth of resources including traditional Māori items. Kaimahi effectively extend children’s learning through extension activities, numeracy and literacy experiences, outings and through children’s interests. Children enjoy making new discoveries as they play and experiment.

Children are confident communicators and are successfully immersed in a supportive te reo Māori environment. Children hear effective te reo Māori models when they are at the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi use a range of approaches to make language learning stimulating for children. Kaimahi and children are well supported and guided in te reo and tikanga Māori by local kuia and members of the whānau. Kaimahi are patient and skilfully deliver meaningful te reo Māori phrases, instructions and conversations to children. Most children successfully speak te reo Māori in different social settings and are able to clearly express their ideas and needs. Whānau members are encouraged and supported to develop their own te reo Māori skills. Children are growing as competent and confident speakers of te reo Māori.

Kaimahi encourage children’s leadership potential in positive ways. Children have opportunities to lead karakia, waiata, learning activities and routines such as karanga for meals. Children confidently lead these activities. The kuia, who is also the overall manager of the kōhanga reo is a role model of leadership for the children. She demonstrates to children a determination to do what is right and holds children at the forefront of all decisions made at the kōhanga reo. Children know about and model tikanga Māori. Children are developing as confident learners and enthusiastic leaders.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

Kaimahi have a sound knowledge of kaupapa planning based on research and best practice in te ao Māori and kōhanga reo. Planning and is an area of strength. This enables kaimahi to cater for children’s interests and needs in successful ways. Kaupapa planning provides children with regular opportunities to explore concepts about te ao Māori including the local Tai Rāwhiti histories and connections. Planning for the younger children is flexible and aims to cater for their individual needs. Plans are comprehensive in detail. Planning is a collaborative effort by kaimahi and allows whānau members to have input to planning.

Kaimahi use learning stories, anecdotal notes and observations to gather information about children’s learning and progress. In addition, kaimahi collect samples of children’s work and use photographs to highlight events, activities and milestones. Profile books are attractive and exciting records of children’s time at kōhanga reo. Children’s learning and development is discussed and documented regularly.

Kaimahi are proactive when they identify areas for improvement in their practice or the programme. They take appropriate action to amend these areas. Kaimahi continually strive for improvement to bring about positive changes for their children.

Whānau management and planning is designed and based on what is best for the children and the whānau with te reo Māori as a priority.This is evident in the following ways:

  • all policies and procedures are current and up to date
  • financial reporting is transparent and submitted to whānau at monthly hui
  • personnel matters are well established. Kaimahi have job descriptions and undertake appraisals
  • a high level of reporting at whānau monthly hui ensures that internal review is carried out constantly
  • whānau have identified some priorities for the ongoing development of their kōhanga reo.

The kuia, who is also the overall manager of the kōhanga reo has an effective system to ensure the operations of the kōhanga reo progress smoothly. She is a strong advocate for children and the kaupapa of kōhanga reo and insists on high quality processes and outcomes.

Kaimahi, management and the whānau use self review as an action and reflection tool which focuses on continuous improvement. Their systematic and purposeful self review is responsive to external review and internal priorities and strategic goals. Professional learning and development is targeted to support the achievement of the kōhanga reo goals.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends the whānau continue to use self review as an action and reflection tool which focuses on continuous improvement.

When is ERO likely to reciew the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
