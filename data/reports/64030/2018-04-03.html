1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kaahui ki Newton, ki Te Whanganui-a-Tara. Mai i tōna tīmatanga i te tau 1984, kua tino whai wāhi atu ngā mema o te whānau ki te whanake tonutanga o te whare me ngā rawa o tō rātou kōhanga reo, hei painga mō ngā tamariki. He kaha te noho pūmau o ngā kaimahi, tae atu anō hoki ki tō rātou kuia kua whai wāhi nui mai ki te kōhanga reo. He pakari te whānau ki te whakapiri mai ki te kōhanga reo, me te āta tautoko.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i ō rātou ngākau titikaha hei Māori?

Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki ō rātou ngākau titikaha hei Māori, puta noa i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga, me te āhua tonu o ngā hononga ki waenga i a rātou anō.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira  

E hāpaitia ana te tūhura o ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana i a rātou. Ka tūmāia ngā tamariki ki te waiata i ngā tini waiata e mōhio whānuitia ana i te motu. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki e whai hua ana ki te akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka mahi rātou ki te taha o ngā rōpū tuākana ki te whakawhānui i tō rātou whakawhanaketanga reo mā te pānui ngātahi me ngā pātai e whakaongaonga ana i ngā whakaaro. Ka whakawhitiwhiti kōrero ngā tamariki tuākana ki ngā pākeke, mō ngā tūmomo āhuatanga e whai pūtake ana ki a rātou. Ka waiata, ka kōrero hoki ngā kaimahi me ngā tuākana ki ngā pēpi, mā ngā huarahi e poipoi ana i te āhua o tā rātou ako. E whanake ana ngā tamariki ki te āta whakawhitiwhiti whakaaro mā te reo Māori.

E ako ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana i tō rātou tuakiri. Ka noho mārama ki ngā mahi me te āhua e tika ana mō te whakatau manuhiri. Ka ārahi ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā tikanga, ā, ka whakatauira hoki rātou i te āhua o ngā pōwhiri. He maha ngā tūmomo wā e ako ana ngā tamariki i tō rātou tū hei tangata whenua. Ka rangona, ka kitea hoki ētahi tamariki e whakatinana ana i ngā mahi i te marae, pērā i te karanga me te waiata. Whakatakotohia ai e ngā kaimahi ngā wā kia tū ētahi o ngā rōpū tamariki ki te mihimihi me te taki i ō rātou pepeha. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te kawe atu i ngā mahi ārahi, pērā i te tātaki i ngā karakia me ngā waiata. Ka kitea te pūmau o ngā tamariki ki te ako.

He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te uru atu ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i tētahi taiao ako haumaru e hāpai ana i te tūhura me te tākaro. He whānui ngā tūmomo rauemi, taumahi hoki, kia āta whai wāhi mai ai ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga. He pukumahi ngā tamariki i a rātou e mahi ana ki te taha o ō rātou hoa, i a rātou rānei e noho takitahi ana. Ka whakapakarihia te noho o te tuakana me te teina i aua wā. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te tūhura haere me te whai anō hoki i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. He whiringa ā ngā tamariki ki te wāhi o roto, me te wāhi o waho, ā, nā tēnei ka whakawerohia rātou – ā-tinana, ā-hinengaro hoki. Ka tūmāia ngā tamariki ki te tūhura haere i tō rātou taiao.

E whai wāhi nui ana ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga i tētahi wāhi e aro matua ana ki tō rātou waiora. Ka ū pūmau ngā kaimahi ki ngā taumata papai o te atawhai i a rātou e tiaki ana i ngā pēpi, ngā tēina, me ngā tuākana. Ka āta mātakina ngā tamariki katoa hei poipoi wawe i ō rātou matea. Ka hāngai aua mahi ki ngā wā e tika ana mō te whakatā, te inu wai, te kai, me ngā mahi akuaku. He ngāwari ngā whakawhitinga ki ngā tūmomo mahinga, ā, he pai hoki te whakahaere i aua wā. Ka whai pānga nui te manaakitanga ki ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi me te whānau. E whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i ngā painga ka puta i ngā taunekeneke whakaute, manaakitanga hoki ki waenga i te katoa. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki tuākana ki te whakahua ake i ō rātou matea ki ngā pākeke i ngā wā katoa. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te aro ki ō rātou ake matea, me ērā anō hoki o ētahi atu.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

Ko te waiora o ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga hei Māori, kei te tūāpapa o ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki te whakahaeretanga, ngā whakaritenga, me te aronga anō hoki o te kōhanga reo. Ka aroturukitia, ka kōrerohia, ka pūrongohia hoki ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo ki ngā hui o ia marama. Ka whakatata wawe atu te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā mahi whakahaere o ia rā i ngā wā e tika ana, hei hāpai i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. He pai ngā tukanga me ngā pūnaha i whakatakotohia ai hei ārahi i te whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga i te kōhanga reo. Arotakengia ai, whakahoungia ai hoki te tūtohinga i ia te wā. E pou here ana te whanaungatanga i ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau.

Ko ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki e whakamōhio ana i ngā mahi ki te whakamahere i te hōtaka, me ngā whakaritenga anō hoki a ngā kaimahi. E pou here ana Te Whāriki i ngā mahere katoa a ngā kaimahi. He aronga ngātahi ki te whakamahere, ā, e akiaki ana i te whai wāhi mai o ngā kaimahi katoa me te whānau. E arotahi ana ngā mahere ki ngā rautaki e kōkiri ai i te reo o ngā tamariki, mā ngā huarahi e whai kiko ana, ā, e whai pūtake ana hoki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kua tīmata ngā kaimahi ki te whakatinana i ngā mahi aromātai hōtaka e whai hua ai ki te whakarato i ngā tino mōhiohio ka tohu i te āhua o ngā hōtaka ki te poipoi i ngā matea ako o ā rātou tamariki. Tāpiri atu ki tēnei, e mōhio ana te whānau, ko ētahi āhuatanga o te whakatakoto haere me te pūrongo ka whakatairanga ake i te aromātai o roto. Mā tēnei, ka puta ngā tino mōhiohio mō te āhua o ngā mahi ki te whakatutuki i ngā mahere rautaki, te arotake kaupapa here, me ngā whāinga o te tūtohinga. E whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i ngā painga o ngā mahi ki te āta whakamahere me te āta whakariterite i te hōtaka me ngā whakahaeretanga.

Me whakapakari ake te aromatawai i ngā tamariki. Ko te nui o ngā tino akoranga e whakapuakihia ana mā ngā tamariki, kāhore e kapohia ana, kāhore hoki e pūrongohia ana ki te taumata e tika ana. Kei te tīmata te whānau ki te whakawhanake i te aronga o ā rātou mahi aromatawai. He hiahia tō rātou ki te āta whakatakoto haere i ngā tino whakaritenga o te aromatawai ka kapo ai i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori me te mātauranga Māori o ā rātou tamariki, i roto i te roanga o te wā.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo, te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakapakarihia ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aromatawai me te aromātai hōtaka.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kaahui i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kaahui is located in Newtown, Wellington. Since its establishment in the early 1980s, whānau members have contributed well to the ongoing development of their kōhanga reo property and premises for children.  There is good retention of kaimahi, including the involvement of their kuia who has made a significant contribution to the kōhanga reo. Whānau participation and involvement in the kōhanga reo is strong.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children show they are confident in themselves as Māori?

Children appear confident in themselves as Māori across a range of learning experiences and in their relationships with one another.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are supported to explore te reo Māori in a safe environment. Children sing with confidence a range of waiata that are familiar across the country. Kaimahi draw on a range of effective strategies that encourage children to speak te reo Māori. They work with groups of older children to extend their language development through shared reading and questions to prompt thinking.  Older children have conversations with adults about things that are meaningful to them.  Kaimahi and tuākana sing and talk to babies in ways that are appropriate to their ways of learning. Children are developing as confident communicators of te reo Māori. 

Children learn in an environment where their identity is nurtured. They understand and behave appropriately when welcoming manuhiri. Kaimahi and whānau guide tikanga and model pōwhiri. They are learning about their role as tangata whenua on different occasions. Individual children are heard and seen to act out roles specific to the marae such as karanga and waiata. Kaimahi set aside regular times for groups of children to stand to mihimihi and recite their pepeha. Children are confident to take on leadership roles such as leading karakia and waiata. Children show they are secure learners.

Children have opportunities to participate in a range of learning experiences. Kaimahi provide a safe learning environment that encourages exploration and play. There are a wide range of resources and activities to engage children in learning. Children are busy as they work alongside their peers or as individuals. During this time tuakana and teina relationships are strengthened.  Kaimahi encourage children to explore and to follow their own interests. Indoor and outdoor areas provide children with choice which enables them to be challenged physically and intellectually.  Children explore their environment with confidence.

Children enjoy learning in an environment where their wellbeing is a priority. Kaimahi maintain very good standards of care for pēpi, tēina and tūakana. All children are closely monitored to ensure their immediate needs are catered for. These include rest, water, kai and hygiene. Transitions across routines are smooth and well managed. Manaakitanga is integral to kaimahi and whānau practices. Children enjoy respectful and caring interactions with everyone. Kaimahi encourage older children to communicate their needs to adults at all times. Children are able to monitor their needs and that of others.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s wellbeing and their development as Māori is the basis of whānau whakahaere. The whānau are actively involved in the management, operations and direction of the kōhanga reo. All areas of the kōhanga reo are monitored, discussed and reported at monthly hui. The whānau are on hand to respond to daily operational needs that support kaimahi and children. There are good processes and systems in place to guide kōhanga reo management and operations. The tūtohinga is regularly reviewed and updated. Whanaungatanga underpins whānau whakahaere.

Children’s needs and interests inform programme planning and kaimahi practice. Te Whāriki underpins all kaimahi planning. There is a shared and collaborative approach to planning, that encourages all kaimahi and whānau input. Planning is focused on strategies that advance children’s language in meaningful and purposeful ways.

Key Next Steps

Kaimahi are in the early stages of implementing effective programme evaluation practice that will provide clear information about how well programmes are catering for the learning needs of their children. In addition, whānau recognise that aspects of recording and reporting can raise the level of internal evaluation. This will provide important information on how well strategic planning, policy review and charter goals are being achieved. Children benefit from a programme and operations that is well planned and organised.

Children’s assessments require strengthening. The significant learning children are exposed to at the kōhanga reo is not as well captured and reported as it could be. The whānau are in the early stages of developing their approach to assessment. They are keen to embed good assessment practice that will capture their children’s development of te reo Māori and Māori knowledge over time. 

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements. 

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • strengthen assessment and programme evaluation practices.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kaahui will be in three years.
