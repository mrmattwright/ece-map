1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi aromātai tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā te Toihau Arotake i Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia ai i roto i te pūrongo aromātai mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei i tāutuhia ai whai muri o taua aromātai.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei aromātai tāpiri i whakaritea i runga i te aromātai o te whakatutukitanga a te whānau i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai e te pūrongo a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i mua atu, me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua aromātai. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei aromātai, ko te titiro ki:

  • te hōtaka akoranga, inarā, ki te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori mō ngā tamariki
  • te whakamahere me te aromātai, arā, ko ngā āhuatanga e whai pānga ana ki ngā whakaritenga whakahaere a te whānau
  • ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture, arā, ki ngā āhuatanga e whai pānga ana ki te aromātai o roto, te whakahaere i te pūtea, te hauora me te haumaru, te whakahaere i ngā kaimahi, te whakamahere ā-tau, me te whakamahere rautaki
  • ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai whai muri i te arotake o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

2 Te Horopaki

E tū pā tata ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Mawake ki te pokapū o te tāone o Tūrangi. E ono wiki noa i mua i tēnei arotake i whakatuwheratia ai te kōhanga reo. Whai muri i te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2016, kua pā te maha o ngā whakarerekētanga ki te kōhanga reo, tae atu ki te whakahoutanga ake anō o ngā hanganga i te kōhanga reo, he komiti matua hou a te whānau, me te whakatū i ētahi atu kaimahi me ētahi atu kaiwhakahaere.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā wāhanga e ahu whakamua ana

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i ngā pūkenga ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka tūmāia ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, he tākare hoki rātou, ā, ka whakatauira anō hoki i ngā hanganga reo papai. Ka whakamahi rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki e whai pānga ana ki ngā horopaki ōkawa me ngā horopaki ōpaki, hei whakawhanake i te reo o ngā tamariki. Ko ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā mihimihi, me ngā waiata e hāpai ana i ngā tamariki ki te rongo i ngā hanganga reo ōkawa. He whai wāhitanga anō hoki mō ngā tamariki ki te uru atu ki ngā pōwhiri, me te rongo i te reo o te karanga, te whaikōrero, me ngā waiata mōteatea. Ka noho ngā kaimahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki i ngā wā tākaro me ngā wā kai, kia rongo ai rātou i ngā tamariki e kōrero ana mō ā rātou taumahi. Ka patapatai hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki, hei akiaki i ō rātou whakaaro, ā, hei whakapuaki anō hoki i ngā kupu hou e whakatairanga ai i ō rātou reo. E ākina ana ngā tamariki nohinohi, ngā pēpi hoki kia whakawhiti kōrero mā tō rātou reo ā-tinana, hei tohu i te whakawhanaketanga o tō rātou reo me tō rātou māramatanga. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā kaupapa. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka aromatawaihia, ka aroturukitia hoki te ako a ia tamaiti. Kua whakawhanakehia, kua whakatinanahia hoki e ngā kaimahi ngā tukanga e ārahi ana i ngā mahi aromatawai. Ka kapo atu rātou i te ahu whakamua o ngā akoranga a ia tamaiti mā ā rātou tirohanga me ngā whakaahua hoki e hono ana ki ngā whāinga ako. Ka whakatakotohia aua tūāhuatanga ki ngā pūkete, ā, ka whakapuakihia hei tirohanga hoki mā ngā mātua.

Kua pai ake ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau. E tautoko ana, e ārahi ana hoki tētahi kuia me tētahi tumuaki o te hapori i ngā kaupapa whakahaere a te whānau. Kua tae atu te komiti hou ki ngā kaupapa whakangungu e whakapiki ake ana i tō rātou māramatanga ki ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga mahi. Kua whakatakotohia tētahi mahere ā-tau hei hāpai i te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā mahi o ia tau. Hui ai te whānau i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti kōrero mō te whakahaeretanga o ngā kaimahi, te tiaki i ngā rawa, me ngā take katoa e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru, hei whakatutuki i ngā herenga ā-ture. Kua toro atu rātou ki te tautoko o waho, ki te whakawhanake me te whakatinana i ngā pūnaha pakari ki te whakahaere i ngā pūtea. Kua āta hāpai tēnei i te pūnaha pūtea me te aroturukitanga. He kawenga ngātahi tā te whānau ki te arotake i te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa here, me ngā mahi ki te arowhai me te tiaki i ngā rawa. Ko te waiora o ngā tamariki tētahi aronga matua.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake tonu

E whanake ana te aromātai o roto. Ahakoa kua whakatutukihia e te whānau ētahi whakapaitanga, me whakatō tonu te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā whakaritenga aromātai o roto ki ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga, hei hāpai ake i ngā putanga ki ngā tamariki.

Kua whakaraupapahia e te whānau kia noho matua te tautoko auau o ngā kaimahi. Kāhore anō rātou kia whakatakotohia ngā whai wāhitanga ki te ako ngaio, te whakawhanake ngaio anō hoki, hei whakapakari ake i ngā āheinga me te tūmāia o ngā kaimahi. Ka whakatairanga ake te kounga o te ako a ngā tamariki, i te wā ka tautokona ngā kaimahi mā te whakawhanaketanga ngaio e tika ana.

Ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture

Ngā wāhanga e ahu whakamua ana

Kua whakapeto ngoi te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā herenga ā-ture kāhore i te tutuki i mua, arā, ko ngā herenga ā-ture i roto i ngā āhuatanga e whai pānga ana ki te mahi arowhai a ngā pirihimana, te whakatinana me te tuhi haere i ngā akoako hōneatanga, me te whakatikatika haere i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā:

  • kia rapu āwhina ki te whakapakari i te arotake whaiaro me ngā āheinga o ngā kaimahi, hei āta whakatau i te rōnakitanga me ngā tino painga mō ngā tamariki.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka whakahaere anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te aromātai whai muri o Te Kōhanga Reo o Mawake i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary evaluation is undertaken at the discretion of the Chief Review Officer of the Education Review Office (ERO).

ERO evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO evaluation and/or any additional areas identified since that evaluation.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary evaluation is based on an evaluation of the performance of the kōhanga reo governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the previous ERO report or issues identified since that evaluation. The terms of reference for this evaluation area to investigate:

  • the learning programme specifically te reo Māori development for children

  • Planning and evaluation: related to whānau management practices

  • Areas of non-compliance: related to internal evaluation, financial management, health and safety, personnel management, annual planning, strategic planning

  • Any other issues identified since the previous ERO review.

2 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Mawake is located near the town centre in Tūrangi. The kōhanga reo was opened six weeks prior to this review. Since the previous ERO report in 2016, there have been several changes that include a kōhanga reo restructure, new whānau executive and the appointments of kaimahi and kaiwhakahaere.

3 Findings

Areas of progress

Children are developing the skills to communicate in te reo Māori. Kaimahi are confident speakers of te reo Māori who are enthusiastic and model good language structures. They use a range of strategies for formal and informal settings to develop children’s language. Karakia, hīmene, mihimihi and waiata provide children with opportunities to hear language structures for formal situations. There are opportunities for children to participate in pōwhiri to hear the language of karanga, whaikōrero and waiata mōteatea. Kaimahi sit with children during play and kai times to hear children talk about their activities, use questions to prompt their thinking and introduce new words to enrich their language. Younger children and babies are encouraged to use body language to communicate their language development and understanding. Kaimahi support children to participate. Children are happy to share in te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s individual learning is assessed and monitored. Kaimahi have developed and implemented processes to guide assessment practices. They capture children’s individual learning progress through observations and photos linked to learning objectives. These are recorded in profile books and shared with parents.

Whānau management practices have improved. A kuia and tumuaki from the local community provide support and guidance related to operational matters for the whānau. The new committee has attended training to increase their understanding of their roles and responsibilities. An annual plan is in place to help assist whānau with meeting yearly deadlines. Whānau regularly meet to discuss matters related to personnel management, property maintenance and all health and safety matters to fulfil legislative requirements. They have accessed external support to develop and implement sound financial management reporting systems. This has impacted positively on budget controls and monitoring. The whānau has shared responsibility to review the charter and policies, property checks and maintenance. Children’s wellbeing is a priority.

Areas for further progress

Internal evaluation is developing. Although the whānau have made improvements, the whānau and kaimahi need to embed internal evaluation practices across all operational areas to improve outcomes for children.

The whānau has prioritised regular support for kaimahi. They are yet to put in place professional learning and development opportunities to build kaimahi capability and confidence. The quality of learning for children will improve as kaimahi gain support through appropriate professional development.

Areas of non-compliance

Areas of progress

The whānau has worked to meet areas of non-compliance related to police vetting, implementing and recording emergency drills, and attending to hazards.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • To seek assistance to strengthen self-review and build kaimahi capability to ensure sustainability and positive outcomes for children.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Mawake will be in three years.
