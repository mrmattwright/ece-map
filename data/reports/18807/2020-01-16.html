1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ōhākī ki te marae o Roma, ki Ahipara, i Muriwhenua. Whai muri i te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2016, kua tīni haere ngā kaimahi me te whānau. I kōkirihia te arotake i te whānau e tīmata ana ki te whakahaere i aua rerekētanga. Kua tahuri mai te tari ā-rohe o te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te tautoko me te ārahi i te whānau, i a rātou e whakatutuki haere ana i ngā āhuatanga matua e tika ana kia tupu tonu.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou whai hononga ki te hapori o Ahipara?

Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tō rātou ngākau nui ki te ako i ō rātou hononga ki te hapori o Ahipara.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He hōhonu ngā wheako ako ā-marae a ngā tamariki. E mōhio ana rātou ki ngā tikanga me ngā kawa o te marae. Whai wāhi atu ai ngā tamariki ki ngā kaupapa i te marae ki te taha o tō rātou whānau. Ka kite rātou i ngā mahi a te tangata whenua e whakatutukihia ana e tō rātou whānau. Ka whakatairanga te whānau i ngā waiata me ngā karakia, tae atu hoki ki ngā whakapapa me ngā hītori e pā ana ki te whenua. Kua whakamaheretia ngā wheako ako ki Ahipara, tae atu ki ērā e whai pānga nui ana ki a Tangaroa. He tino hononga tō ngā tamariki ki te marae.

E ako ana ngā tamariki i te reo Māori mā te tūhura haere. Ko ngā taumahi i whakamaheretia ai, e ahu mai ana i ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Ka kitea tō rātou aronga nui ki te tūhura i ngā wāhi o waho me te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. Ka kōrero ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori ki te taha o ngā tamariki, i ngā tamariki e tūhura haere ana. Ka kite ngā kaimahi i ngā kaupapa e aro nuitia ana e ngā tamariki, ā, ka kapo atu rātou i aua wā hei whakawhānui, hei wero hoki i te reo e whakamahia ana, e mōhiotia ana hoki e ngā tamariki, tae atu ki ngā wā kai me ngā tākaro e kōkirihia ana e te tamaiti. E rongo ana ngā tamariki i te reo o te rohe i roto i ngā kaupapa a te marae. E whakaongaongatia ana te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki.

E ako ana ngā tamariki i te whakahirahiratanga o te manaaki me te tiaki. Ko ngā mihimihi me ngā pepeha i ia rā, e whakamana ana i te tū o ngā tamariki i roto i te whānau, te hapū, me te iwi. Ka tautoko ngā tamariki i ō rātou hoa mā ngā waiata kīnaki. He whakaute, he manaakitanga hoki tō ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā tamariki. Ka whakapuakihia te aroha me te manaaki ki waenga i tēnā me tēnā, puta noa i te rā. Ka kōkiri ngā kaimahi i ngā taumahi ako katoa kia puāwai ai te tohatoha, te whakaaro nui, me te rangimārie o tētahi ki tētahi. E manaaki ana, e aroha ana hoki ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ko te ako a ngā tamariki, ko te atawhai hoki i a rātou, tētahi aronga nui o te whānau. Ahakoa te nui o ngā rerekētanga, kua tahuri ngā mātua ki te whakatutuki i ngā tūranga matua me ngā kawenga mahi. E tino arahina ana ngā mema ki tā rātou tūtohinga e āta whakatakoto ana i ngā whakaritenga mō te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakatakotohia ngā pūnaha hei ārahi i ngā whakahaeretanga o ia rā. Kua āta whakaritea ngā mauhanga whakahaere i te tari. Tū ai ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, ā, he pai te taetae atu a te whānau. Ko ngā tūmomo pūrongo e whakatakotohia ai, ko ērā mō te whakahaere i te tari, i te waka, i ngā pūtea, me te hauora me te haumaru, me ērā hoki a te heamana, ngā kaiako, me te purapura. He pai te aroturuki i te nuinga o ngā wāhanga o te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. E whakapūmau ana te whānau i ngā uara o te tūtohinga, me ngā tikanga, ngā kawa hoki, i roto i te āhua o tā rātou whakahaere i te kōhanga reo i tēnei wā hurihuri. E arotahi ana rātou kia whai kounga te atawhai i ngā tamariki, me tā rātou ako.

E whakahaere ana te whānau i te marautanga o Te Whāriki mā ngā tamariki. E arotahi ana ngā mahere o Te Whāriki ki te ako mā te tūhura me te whakahonohono ki te taiao whānui tonu o te kōhanga reo. Ka whakaritea ngā haerenga i ia te wā hei whakawhānui ake i te ako a ngā tamariki i ngā kaupapa i whakamaheretia ai. Kua whakatakotohia ngā whakaritenga o ia rā me ngā mahere ā-wiki, ā-wāhanga, ā-tau hoki hei ārahi i te whakapuakitanga. Ka whakamaheretia te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo kaupapa whai hononga mā ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kua tāutuhia e te whānau ngā kaupapa matua kia whakahaerehia ai ngā rerekētanga me te tupu tonu o te kōhanga reo. Kua mahi te kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe o te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te taha o te whānau ki te whakatutuki haere i aua kaupapa matua. Ko ētahi o aua kaupapa matua, ko te whakatū i tētahi atu kaiako whai tohu mātauranga, me te whakapakari ake i ngā āheinga ngaio o ngā kaimahi. Hei te tīmatanga o te tau e whai ake nei aua whakaritenga whakapuakihia ai.

I tētahi hui ā-whānau nō nā noa nei i tū ai, i whakatakoto te whānau i ngā kaupapa matua hei whakawhanake ā tērā tau. Ko ētahi o aua kaupapa, ko te arotake i ngā mahere rautaki, i te tūtohinga, ā, i Te Korowai hoki. Ka āta hāpai tēnei i te whānau ki te kawe atu i ngā tūranga hou me ngā mahi hou.

Kua tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga tētahi kaupapa rautaki matua mā te whānau, arā, kia whakapakari te whānau i ō rātou āheinga i roto i te reo Māori, hei whakatairanga i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture. Kia tutuki i te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā herenga ā-ture, me tahuri rātou ki te:

  • whakatau i a rātou ngā pakeke e whāngai ana i te mātauranga me te atawhai, tō rātou mōhio ki ngā akoako ohorere e tika ana, me te whakatutuki haere i aua akoako ki te taha o ngā tamariki, te tuhi hoki i aua wā, i ia toru marama (koni atu rānei), ā, me whai whakaaturanga e tohu ana i te whai pānga o te aromātai i ngā akoako ki te arotakenga ā-tau o te mahere ohorere a te ratonga.
    [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS8 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • mahi te whānau ki te taha o ngā kaimahi ā-rohe o te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā whakaritenga matua i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo ā Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia whai ake.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ōhaki is based on Roma marae, Ahipara, in the far north. Since the previous ERO report, 2016, there has been changes in kaimahi and whānau. The review was conducted when the whānau were in the early stages of managing these changes. The local Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) has responded with support and guidance to whānau to work through key priorities for growth.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children show their connection to Ahipara community?

Children show enjoyment in learning their connections to the community of Ahipara.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children have rich marae-based learning experiences. They are familiar with tikanga and kawa of the marae. Children frequently participate with whānau at events held on the marae. They see the tangata whenua roles carried out by their whānau. They promote waiata and karakia that includes whakapapa and history about the whenua. There are planned learning experiences in Ahipara, including to Tangaroa. Children have strong connections to the marae.

Children learn te reo Māori through exploration. Planned activities are based on the needs and interests of children. They show a strong preference to explore the outdoors and follow their interests. Kaimahi use te reo Māori alongside children as they explore. Kaimahi recognise moments of high interest to children and use these times to extend and challenge children’s use and knowledge of the language including wā kai and self-directed play. Activities on the marae expose children to hear the language of the rohe. Children’s language development is stimulated.

Children learn about the importance of manaaki and tiaki. Daily mihimihi and pepeha affirm children’s place within the whānau, hapū and iwi. Children support their peers with waiata tautoko. Respectful and caring relationships are formed amongst children. They show aroha and manaaki towards each other throughout the day. Kaimahi facilitate all learning activities in ways that foster sharing, consideration and rangimārie with each other. Children are caring and loving.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s learning and care is an ongoing focus of whānau. Despite significant change, parents have stepped into key roles and responsibilities. Members are well guided by their tūtohinga that sets out clear practices for managing the kōhanga reo. Systems are in place to guide daily operations. Administration records are well organised. Regular whānau hui are held with good whānau attendance. Tabled reports include administration, chairperson, kaiako, waka, finance, purapura, and health and safety. Most areas of the kōhanga reo are monitored well for children. Whānau uphold the charter values, tikanga and kawa in the way they manage the kōhanga reo through this time of change. They are focused on quality care and learning for children.

Whānau are managing the curriculum of Te Whāriki for children. Te Whāriki plans focus on learning through exploration and making connections to the local and wider kōhanga reo environment. Regular haerenga are planned to extend children’s learning of planned kaupapa. Weekly, daily organisation, term plans and a mahere a tau are in place to guide delivery. Connections for children are planned and varied.

Key Next Steps

The whānau has identified priorities to manage change and grow the kōhanga reo. TKRNT district has been working with the whānau on these priorities. These include the recruitment of another qualified kaiako and building the professional capacity of existing kaimahi. Plans are in place for the beginning of next year.

A recent whānau hui has highlighted key priorities for development next year. These include the review of strategic planning, the tūtohinga and te korowai. This will support the whānau well as they take on new roles and responsibilities.

ERO has identified a strategic priority for whānau to increase their te reo Māori capacity to enhance language development for children.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

ERO identified areas of non-compliance. In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements they need to:

  • adults providing education and care are familiar with relevant emergency drills, carry these out with children and maintain records of these, on an at least three-monthly basis and have evidence of how evaluation of the drills has informed the annual review of the service’s emergency plan.
    [R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS8 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • the whānau to work with TKRNT district representatives to address the key next steps identified in this ERO report.

5 When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ōhaki will be in three years.
