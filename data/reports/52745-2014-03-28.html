2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ko Ratana te marae Ko Manuao te pou herenga waka Ko Temepara te puna wai ora Ko te Iwi Morehu te iwi Ko te maramatanga te kaupapa.

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Wharemarama ki te pā o Rātana. Kua kaha tautokotia tēnei kōhanga reo e te hau kāinga. E noho ana te nuinga o ngā mātua ki te pā o Rātana. Ia rā, ia rā ka whakawhitiwhiti ētahi o ngā mātua me ngā tamariki mai i Whanganui ki te kōhanga reo mā te waka kawe o te kōhanga reo.

I kōwhiritia e te whānau te Mana Atua me te Mana Reo hei kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E hāngai ana ēnei kaupapa ki tō rātou kaupapa nui ki te whakaako i ngā tamariki me ngā mātua o te whānau i ngā āhuatanga o te tino kaupapa o te hāhi Rātana me te reo Māori.

E ako ana ngā tamariki me ngā mātua o te whānau i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te kaupapa o te whakapono o te hāhi Rātana, ā, he ōrite ngā whai tikanga a ngā kaimahi ki te hāpai i ngā uaratanga me ngā whakapono o Rātana. E tino whai wāhi ana rātou ki te whai i ngā tino takunetanga o te hapori me ngā huihuinga ahurea. Kia tau iho te mauritau i waenganui i tēnā me tēnā kua whakahaeretia ngā karakia ia rā, ia rā. He rawe te āhua o te tiaki tamariki. He mea māhana me te tiaki tē āhua o ngā taunekeneke i waenganui i a rātou me ngā pakeke i te kōhanga reo. E noho mauritau, ā, e māia ana ngā tamariki i roto i te taiao o te kōhanga reo.

Kua tino tautokotia ngā tamariki me ngā pakeke ki te ako i te reo Māori. E tino whai wāhi ana ngā kaimahi me ngā mātua o te whānau ki te whai i ngā whakangungu hei whakapiki i ō rātou mātau ki te reo Māori. Heoi anō, me whai wāhi ngā kaimahi ki te whakapai ake i te whakahaere i te hōtaka akoranga me te kaha akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te whakaputa i te reo Māori. Me pai ake ā rātou whakaritenga ki te whakarato i ngā ngohe hīkaka me ngā wheako pārekareka.

Kua whakahaere paitia te kōhanga reo e ngā mātua o te whānau, ā, ko ngā hiahia o ā rātou tamariki te kaupapa tuatahi mō ngā mea katoa. Me pai ake te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā kia hāngai ai ēnei tuhinga ki te tino kaupapa o te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Mahi Ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o Te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Wharemarama kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Wharemarama.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko āna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • te Mana Atua me te Mana Reo.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere i te māhere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • hōtaka whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Atua

He whakamārama

I kōwhiritia e te whānau te Mana Atua hei kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Tō rātou kamakama hoki ki te whakaako i ā rātou tamariki me ngā mātua o te whānau i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te hāhi Rātana, arā, te whakapono, ngā uaratanga me ngā tino tikanga whai take ki te hāhi. E tino hiahia ana tēnei aromātai ki te whakautu i te pātai e whai ake nei:

E pēhea ana te whai hua o te tautoko a ngā tamariki me ngā mātua o te whānau ki te ako i te kaupapa me ngā whakapono o te hāhi Rātana?

Kua whakatauria e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kua tino tautoko ngā tamariki me te whānau ki te ako i ngā āhuatanga o te kaupapa me ngā whakapono o te hāhi Rātana.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Ngā akoranga e pā ana ki te hāhi Rātana E ako ana ngā tamariki me ngā mātua o te whānau i te kaupapa o te hāhi Rātana, ā, e hāngai tōtika ana ngā whakahaere a ngā kaimahi ki ngā uaratanga me te kaupapa o Rātana. E tino whai wāhi ana rātou ki ngā tino kaupapa o te hapori i te wā i te wā mō ngā hui whakanui, ngā hui kua whakatūria tae noa ki ngā hui ahurea. Kua whakapuaki ngā mātua o te whānau i ō rātou tūmanakotanga kia piri pūmau ngā āhuatanga ako katoa ki te kaupapa me te whakapono o te hāhi Rātana. E whakanui ana ngā tamariki me ngā mātua o te whānau i te tino kaupapa me ngā whakapono o Rātana me ōnā tikanga katoa.

Te whanaungatanga. He rawe te āhua o te tiaki tamariki. He mea aroha me te tiaki te āhua o ngā taunekeneke i waenganui i a rātou anō me ngā pakeke. He pakari rawa ngā here whanaungatanga i waenganui i te whānau, ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki.

He pono ngā hiahia o tēnā me tēnā ki te whakawhanake i ngā taunekeneke me ngā whanaungatanga. Kua whakanuia ngā uara o te manaakitanga me te kaitiakitanga, ā, kua whakatinanatia e ngā matua o te whānau me ngā tamariki ēnei uaratanga. E harikoa ana te whānau, ā, e māia ana ngā tamariki i roto i te taiao o te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Ngā tuhinga a te kōhanga reo. Kāore te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā i te hāngai ki te tino kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. Me tino whai wāhi atu te whānau ki te:

  • arotake me te whakatika i te tūtohinga kia pai ake ai tōna hāngai ki te tino kaupapa me ngā moemoeā o te whānau
  • arotake me te whakapai ake i ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga o ia rā kia hāngai ai ngā kōrero ki te tino kaupapa o te kōhanga reo.

Mana Reo

He whakamārama

I kōwhiritia e te whānau te Mana Reo hei kaupapa arotake mō tā rātou arotake. E arotahi ana rātou ki te whakapiki i te reo Māori o ngā tamariki me ngā mātua o te whānau. Ko te reo Māori tētahi kaupapa hei whakapai ake anō. E hiahia ana tēnei aromātai ki te whakautu i te pātai e whai ake nei:

E pēhea ana te whai painga o te tautoko a ngā tamariki me ngā mātua o te whānau ki te ako me te whakaputa i te reo Māori?

E whai tautoko ana ngā mātua o te whānau ki te ako me te whakaputa i te reo Māori.

Me pai ake te tautoko mā ngā tamariki ki te ako me te whakaputa i te reo Māori i a rātou e whai haere ana i te hōtaka akoranga.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Ngā akoranga hei ako i te reo Māori. He nui rawa ngā moemoeā o te whānau mō ā rātou tamariki kia tū ai rātou hei kaikōrero o te reo Māori. E ako ana ngā tamariki me te whānau ki te whakaputa i te reo e tino hāngai ana ki te kaupapa o te whakapono o te hāhi Rātana. Kei te whakahaere ngā akoranga ki ngā wāhi whai take me ngā horopaki pārekareka, arā, ki te pā o Rātana, ki Manuao me te marae. He wheako haumako ēnei hei ako i te reo. E whai wāhi ana ngā kaimahi me ngā mātua o te whānau ki ngā whakangungu hei āwhina i ā rātou ki te whakapiki i ō rātou mātau ki te reo Māori. Kua tautokotia ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi me ngā pakeke te ako i te reo Māori, ā, e whai wāhi ana ngā pakeke ki te uru ki roto i ngā akoranga ki te ako i te reo.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te hōtaka akoranga. Kāore ngā tamariki i te kōrero i te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa. Me tino arotahi ngā kaimahi ki te akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te whakaputa i te reo Māori. Me whakarato i ngā ngohe pārekareka hei tautoko i ā rātou ki te kōrero i te reo Māori i te wā e taunekeneke ana rātou ki a rātou anō me ngā pakeke. Me pai ake ngā whakahaere me ngā ritenga mō te hōtaka. Kāore anō kia whakaritea ngā whāinga ako mō te reo Māori i roto i ngā whakamāherehere.

Me whai wāhi ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, i ia te wā ki te aromātai e pēhea ana te whai painga o te hōtaka akoranga me tāna tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te tū hei kaikōrero o te reo Māori. Me whakahaeretia ngā aromatawai mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki ki te ako me te whakawhanake i te reo Māori i ia te wā, i ia te wā.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamāherehere me te aromātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo hei whakatau i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamāherehere, ngā whakahaere me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga reo mā a rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka āta hāngai te aromātai nei ki te hōtaka whakamāherehere me te aroturuki i te tipuranga ako o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Ngā whakahaere a te whānau. Kei te whakahaere paitia te kōhanga reo e ngā mātua o te whānau, ā, ko ngā tino hiahia o ngā tamariki te kaupapa matua mō ngā whakahaere katoa.

Tū ai ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, ā, he pai te tae ā tinana atu.

Ka whakatau ngātahi te whānau i ngā take mō ngā whakahaere a te kōhanga reo me ngā take e pā ana ki te ahunga whakamua o te kōhanga reo.

E tino mārama ana te whānau mō ngā uaratanga o Rātana, ā, he tāhuhu ēnei uara mō ā rātou whakahaere.

Ka whakawhanake te whānau i ngā māhere rautaki, ā, kei roto i ngā māhere nei ngā whāinga matua hei arahi i tō rātou ahunga whakamua.

E ako ana te whānau e pēhea ana te arotake whaiaro e tautokotia ai ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te aromātai i ngā kaimahi. Kāore te whānau i tētahi tukanga ōkawa hei whakahaere me te whakapai ake i te whai hua o ngā putanga mahi a ngā kaimahi. Kāore te aromātai mō ngā kaimahi katoa i te whakahaeretia ia tau. Kāore anō ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā kia whakatūria hei arahi i te whānau ki te whakahaere me te whakatinana i ngā aromātai mō ngā kaimahi. Me whai wāhi te whānau ki te whakawhanake me te whakatinana i ngā aromātai mā ngā kaimahi katoa ia tau i te mea he rautaki tēnei hei aroturuki, te ine me te whakahaere i te whai hua o ngā putanga mahi a ngā kaimahi.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

He whakamārama

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Wharemarama tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga a te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

6 Ngā Tūtohunga

E tūtohu ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ki te:

  • whānau ki te whanake me te whakatinana i tētahi pūnaha hei aromātai i ngā kaimahi katoa
  • whānau ki te arotake i ngā tino tuhinga kia hāngai ai ki tō rātou kaupapa motuhake
  • kaiako me ngā kaimahi ki te arotahi ki ngā wāhanga o te hōtaka akoranga hei whakapai ake anō mō ngā tamariki
  • kaiako me ngā kaimahi ki te whakapai ake i ngā whakahaere mō te aromatawai kia hāngai ai ngā whakahaere ki ngā paetae me te whakatipuranga o ngā tamariki mō tētahi wā kua tohungia.

7 Ngā Mahi Ā Meāke Nei

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Ko Ratana te marae Ko Manuao te pou herenga waka Ko Temepara te puna wai ora Ko te Iwi Morehu te iwi Ko te Maramatanga te kaupapa.

Wharemarama Te Kōhanga Reo is in Ratana Paa. This kōhanga reo is well supported by the local community. Most whānau members live in Ratana Paa. Some whānau members travel to and from the kōhanga reo daily from Whanganui in the kōhanga reo van.

The whānau chose Mana Atua and Mana Reo as the focus areas for the review. These reflect their focus on teaching children and whānau members about the Ratana faith and te reo Māori.

Children and whānau members learn about the Ratana faith at the kōhanga reo where kaimahi practice is consistent with the values and beliefs of Ratana. They are also involved in regular community events and cultural activities. Daily karakia sets a positive tone for the day. Children are nurtured. They have warm and caring interactions with each other and adults in the kōhanga reo. Children are confident and comfortable in the kōhanga reo environment.

Children and adults are well supported to learn te reo Māori. Kaimahi and whānau members are involved in training to improve their capability in te reo Māori. However, kaimahi need to better manage the learning programme and focus on encouraging children to use te reo Māori. They need to be better organised and prepared to provide interesting and engaging activities and learning experiences.

The kōhanga reo is well managed by whānau members who put the interests of their children first. The charter, policies and procedures should be improved to reflect the special character of the kōhanga reo.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Wharemarama was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Te Kōhanga Reo o Wharemarama.

The whānau chose as its focus areas:

  • Mana Atua and Mana Reo.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning; and
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

4 Findings

Mana Atua

Background

The whānau selected Mana Atua as a focus area for this review. They are motivated to teach their children and whānau members about the Ratana faith, values and icons of significance. This evaluation seeks to answer the following question:

How well are children and whānau members supported to learn about the Ratana faith?

ERO found that children and whānau are well supported to learn about the Ratana faith.

Areas of good performance

Learning about the Ratana faith. Children and whānau members learn about the Ratana faith at the kōhanga reo where kaimahi practice is consistent with the values and beliefs of Ratana. They are also involved in regular community celebrations, events and cultural activities. Whānau members have articulated a clear expectation that learning is based on the Ratana faith. Children and whānau members uphold, promote and celebrate Ratana practices, rituals and tikanga.

Whanaungatanga, interactions and relationships. Children are nurtured. They have loving and caring interactions with each other and adults. There are strong bonds between whānau, staff members and children.

There is a genuine commitment to developing positive interactions and relationships. The values of manaakitanga and kaitiakitanga are promoted and practiced by whānau members and by children. Whānau are happy and content and children are confident and comfortable in the kōhanga reo environment.

Areas for development

Kōhanga reo documents. The charter, policies and procedures do not reflect the special character of the kōhanga reo. The whānau needs to:

  • Review and refine the charter to better reflect their unique focus and whānau aspirations
  • review and improve the policies and procedures to reflect the special character of the kōhanga reo.

Mana Reo

Background

The whānau also chose Mana Reo as a focus area for their review. They are focussed on improving the te reo Māori capability of both children and whānau members. Te reo Māori is a priority area for development. This evaluation seeks to answer the following question:

How well are children and whānau members supported to learn and use te reo Māori?

Whānau members are adequately supported to learn and to use te reo Māori.

Support for children to learn and use te reo Māori in the learning programme needs to be improved.

Areas of good performance

Learning te reo Māori. Whānau have high aspirations for children to become speakers of te reo Māori. Children and whānau learn and use language that is specific to the Ratana faith. Learning takes place in real and relevant contexts including Ratana Paa, the Temepara, Manuao and marae. These are meaningful stimulus for language learning. Staff and whānau members are involved in training to help improve their capability in te reo Māori. Children are supported to learn te reo Māori by staff and adults who are also involved in the learning process.

Areas for development

The learning programme. Children do not use te reo Māori consistently. Kaimahi need to focus more on encouraging children to use te reo Māori. They should provide stimulating and interesting activities that engage children and encourage them to use te reo Māori in interactions with each other and with adults. The programme needs to be better organised. There are no learning objectives for te reo Māori included in programme planning.

Kaimahi should regularly evaluate how effectively the learning programme supports children to become te reo Māori speakers. Assessment of children’s learning and development in te reo Māori should also be conducted regularly.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management. The kōhanga reo is well managed by whānau members who put the interests of their children first.

Regular whānau meetings occur that are well attended.

Whānau members make decisions collectively about kōhanga operations and the future direction of the kōhanga.

The whānau is clear about the Ratana values that underpin their practices.

The whānau develop strategic plans with priorities to guide their future direction.

The whānau is learning about how self review can support improvement.

Areas for development

Appraisal. The whānau does not have a formal process for managing and improving staff performance. Appraisal is not carried out annually for all staff. There are no policies or procedures to guide the whānau in the management and implementation of staff appraisal. The whānau needs to develop and implement appraisal for all staff annually as a way of monitoring, tracking and managing staff performance.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Wharemarama completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

6 Recommendations

ERO recommends that the:

  • whānau develop and implement a system for appraising all staff
  • whānau review all key documentation to reflect their special character
  • kaiako and kaimahi focus on improving the learning programme for children
  • kaiako and kaimahi improve assessment practices to better reflect children’s progress and development over time.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.
