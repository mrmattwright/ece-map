2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Pihi o Te Purapura Pai ki ngā papa o te kura tuarua o Birkenhead, ki Te Raki Paewhenua.

He tokomaha ngā mema o te whānau kua whai hononga ki Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Pihi o Te Purapura Pai mō te wā roa. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu o te whānau ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakatutuki ngātahi rātou i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He pai te tautoko o te whānau i ngā kaupapa huihuinga o te kōhanga reo, me te hōtaka o ia rā, ngā haerenga hoki. Ka whakatau ngātahi i ngā whakaritenga, ā, ka tohaina ngā kawenga mahi ki ngā mema e whai pūkenga ana, he mātanga hoki ki aua tūmomo kawenga mahi. Ka hāpai, ka manaaki hoki ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā pākeke me ngā tamariki. Ka kitea te tino tautoko o tētahi ki tētahi.

E kawe ana tētahi tino rōpū o ngā mema o te whānau, i ngā āhuatanga katoa e pā ana ki te kōhanga reo hou e whakawhanakehia ana. He tino pai te tautoko o te whānau whānui i tēnei kaupapa, ā, koia nei te tino aronga matua ki te whānau mō te wā kei te heke mai.

Aroturukitia ai e te whānau ngā wāhanga matua katoa o ngā whakahaeretanga ki te kōhanga reo. Ka pūrongo ki te whānau i ia te wā, ki ngā hui ā-whānau o ia marama. E ngākau nui ana te whānau ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, me ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, tō rātou atawhai hoki.

Kōrerohia ai te reo Māori ki te kōhanga reo i ngā wā katoa. He māhorahora te kōrero a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori, ā, he pakari hoki. He māori hoki te pupū ake o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, ngā pātai hoki ki waenganui i ngā tamariki me ngā pākeke.

Ka whāia ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki mā tētahi hōtaka ngāwari i āta whakaritea ai. He pai te riterite o ngā taumahi o roto, o waho hoki. He whānui aua tūmomo taumahi, ā, he pārekareka, he wero hoki mō ngā tamariki. Ko te tākaro whakaari tētahi āhuatanga nui o te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka ako ngā tamariki i te hītori o te hapori mā ngā mahi kaupapa me te whakaari. Ka ako ngā tamariki i tō rātou ao Māori me te ao whānui. He pai ngā kaimahi ki te mahi hei rōpū. Ka manaaki, ka akiaki hoki ngā tamariki. Ka ako ngā tamariki tuākana ki te tiaki i ngā tēina. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, he mea nui te whai pūtake o ngā taunekeneke ki ngā tamariki. Kei te mōhio pai ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

He pai te tiaki o ngā wāhi mahi, ngā wāhi tākaro hoki ki roto, ki waho, ā, he ātaahua hoki. Ka whakaatuhia ngā mahi a ngā tamariki, puta noa i te kōhanga reo. E mahi ngātahi ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo ki te whakatū i tētahi whare hou kua hangaia hei kōhanga reo, ki taua papa tonu. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

He pakari ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere ngātahi i ngā hōtaka kaupapa o ngā tamariki. Kua whakawhanakehia e rātou tētahi tauira whakamahere e wāhi hua ana hei ārahi i a rātou ki te whakapuaki i ngā hōtaka ā-kaupapa ki ngā tamariki. He pai te whakaemi i ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki, ki ā rātou ake pūkete. Ka hono aua mōhiohio i ngā taumahi me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki ki Te Whāriki, ā, ka whakaatuhia te ahunga whakamua i roto i te roanga o te wā. Ka noho mōhio ngā mātua mā ngā pūrongo ā-tau.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Pihi o Te Purapura Pai kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Pihi o Te Purapura Pai.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

E ai ki titiro a te ao Māori, e hāngai ana ngā Taumata Whakahirahira ki ngā wāhanga katoa o ngā akoranga ā te tamaiti me tōna whakawhanaketanga.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te hōtaka akoranga. He tino ngāwari te whakaritenga o te hōtaka, ā, ka hāngai ki ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. He pai te riterite o ngā taumahi o roto, o waho hoki. He whānui ngā tūmomo taumahi, ā, he pārekareka, he wero hoki mō ngā tamariki. He whai wāhitanga mō rātou ki te noho tahi hei rōpū. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te tūhura i tō rātou taiao. Ka ako rātou i ngā hītori o te hapori mā ngā mahi kaupapa me te whakaari.

Ko te tākaro whakaari tētahi āhuatanga o te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka ako ngā tamariki i a rātou anō, me ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu. Ka ākina te reo matatini puta noa i te hōtaka, tae atu ki te pānui ngātahi, te whakatinana i ngā pūrākau o ngā pukapuka, me te whakakī i te taiao ki te reo tuhituhi. Ka ako ngā tamariki i tō rātou ao Māori me te ao whānui. Kei te tino mōhio ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Te reo Māori. Kōrerohia ai te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa ki te kōhanga reo. He māhorahora ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, ā, he pakari hoki. He māori te pupū ake o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, ngā pātai hoki ki waenganui i ngā tamariki me ngā pākeke. Ka akiaki, ka tautoko, ka whakawhānui hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki ngā tamariki. He pai te whakamahi i ngā kupu e hāngai pū ana ki ngā kaupapa. Ko ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā waiata, ngā whakaari, me ngā haka, ka whai wāhi matua ki ngā whakaritenga o te reo Māori me ngā tikanga. He tokomaha ngā mema o te whānau ka whai wāhi ki ngā whakangungu a Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te whakapiki i ō rātou ake pūkenga reo, hei hāpai ake i ā rātou tamariki, i ngā kaimahi hoki.

Te whanaungatanga. He pakari ngā hononga o ngā kaimahi ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ngā tamariki e mahi ana ki ō rātou taha, ā, ki ngā mema hoki o te whānau. Ka tautoko ngā mema o te whānau i ngā kaimahi, i a rātou anō hoki, ki ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. Ka whakatutuki ngātahi rātou i ngā tūranga ārahi. He mārama ngā tūranga me ngā kawenga mahi. He pai te mahi ngātahi a ngā kaimahi hei rōpū. Ka manaaki, ka akiaki hoki rātou i ngā tamariki. Ka ako ngā tamariki tuākana ki te tiaki i ngā tēina. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, he mea nui te whai pūtake o ngā taunekeneke ki ngā tamariki.

Te taiao o te kōhanga reo. He pai te tiaki o ngā wāhi mahi, ngā wāhi tākaro hoki ki roto, ki waho, ā, he ātaahua hoki. Ka whakaatuhia ngā mahi a ngā tamariki, puta noa i te kōhanga reo. E mahi ngātahi ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo ki te whakatū i tētahi whare hou kua hangaia hei kōhanga reo, ki taua papa tonu. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai i ngā kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo i te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki, me te whānau. E hāngai ana te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakahaeretanga o te whānau. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, ka kawe ngātahi i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He pai te tautoko o te whānau i ngā kaupapa huihuinga o te kōhanga reo, i te hōtaka o ia rā, ā, i ngā haerenga hoki. Tū ai ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, ā, he pai te tae ā-tinana atu ki aua hui. He kaha te whakawhitiwhiti whakaaro i ngā wā katoa ki ngā mātua, mā te whakamahi i ngā pānui ki ngā pakitara, ngā pānui mō te kāinga, me tētahi pukapuka hei tuhi haere, hei whakaatu hoki i ngā mōhiohio e whai pānga ana ki ngā āhuatanga o ia rā.

Ka tautoko te whānau i ngā kaimahi i roto i te whakawhanaketanga ngaio me te whakapuakitanga o te hōtaka. Ko te tukanga ki te whakauru i ngā whānau hou me ngā kaimahi, ka whai pānga ki te whakatau i ngā tūranga me ngā kawenga mahi. Ka whakatau ngātahi i ngā whakaritenga, ā, ka tohaina ngā tūranga mahi ki aua mema e mau ana i ngā pūkenga, i te mātanga rānei e whai pānga nui ana ki aua tūmomo tūranga. He pakari, he mārama hoki ngā pūnaha e pā ana ki te whakahaere pūtea. E kawe ana tētahi tino rōpū o ngā mema o te whānau i ngā āhuatanga katoa e pā ana ki te whakatūnga o te kōhanga reo hou. E tautoko ana te whānau whānui i taua kaupapa. E ngākau nui ana te whānau ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga re, me ngā akoranga, te atawhai hoki o ngā tamariki.

Te arotake whaiaro. Aroturuki ai te whānau i te katoa o ngā āhuatanga matua o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Pūrongo ai ki te whānau i ngā hui ā-whānau o ia marama, ā, ka hāngai ēnei ki ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka kitea te arotakenga o ngā kaupapa here me ngā whakaritenga, ki ngā meneti me ētahi atu tuhinga. Ka aro te whānau ki te whakapai tonutanga. Ka whakatutuki rātou i tēnei āhuatanga, mā te arotake i ia te wā i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

Te whakamahere hōtaka. He pakari te whakamahere ngātahi a ngā kaimahi i ngā hōtaka ā-kaupapa mō ngā tamariki. Kua whakawhanakehia e rātou tētahi tino tauira whakamahere hei ārahi i a rātou ki te whakapuaki i ngā hōtaka ā-kaupapa ki ngā tamariki. Kei roto i te whakatakotoranga o ngā mahere, ko ngā putanga, ngā taumahi, ngā pukapuka, me ngā waiata, e hāngai ana ki te kaupapa. Ko ngā mahi e pā ana ki te kaupapa o te wā e whakaatuhia ana hei tirohanga mā ngā mātua, ā, he tino mārama. Ka tautoko ngā kaiako i ngā kaiāwhina, mā te whakamahere, te whakapuaki hoki o ngā hōtaka, me te whakarato i ētahi atu whakaaro, i ngā kōrero urupare hoki. Ka whakapuakihia hoki ngā mahere ki ngā mātua. Ko te whakamahere mō te takitahi, mō te takirōpū hoki, e hāngai ana ki ngā ngākau nuitanga. Ka whakaratohia ki ngā tamariki ngā hōtaka ā-kaupapa i āta whakamaheretia ai.

Te aromatawai. He pai te whakaemi i ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki ki ā rātou ake pūkete. He ātaahua te whakaatu i ngā tauira mahi toi a ngā tamariki, tae atu ki ngā kōrero, me ētahi atu taumahi. Ko ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki ngā whakatutukitanga, ka whakahono i ngā taumahi me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki ki Te Whāriki, ā, ka kitea te ahunga whakamua i roto i te roanga o te wā. Ka noho mōhio ngā mātua nā ngā pūrongo ā-tau. Ka āta aroturukitia te ako, te whakawhanaketanga hoki o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Ngā taumata akoranga ka whāia tonuhia. Ka whakatakoto haere ngā kaimahi i ngā mōhiohio mārama, whai kiko hoki, mō ngā āhuatanga e akohia ana e ngā tamariki, me ngā huarahi hoki e whāia ana. E mōhio ana rātou, ko te mahi aromatawai hei whai atu i nāianei, ko te whakawhānui i ā rātou kitenga o ngā tamariki e mahi ana, e tākaro ana hoki. E mārama ana ki ngā kaimahi, ka whai hua te tāutu i ngā ngākau nuitanga ake me ngā kaha o ia tamaiti, me ngā momo huarahi pea ka hāpai ake, ka whakawhānui ake i ēnei i roto i te hōtaka akoranga.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Pihi o Te Purapura Pai I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātaurangame ngā whakaritengakaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

6 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo kia:

  • mahi tahi tonu te whānau, puta noa i ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo
  • whakawhanake tonu te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā mahi aromatawai, hei hāpai tonu i te ako me te whakawhanaketanga o ā rātou tamariki.

7 Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Ngā Pihi o Te Purapura Pai is located on the grounds of Birkenhead College, North Shore.

Many whānau members of Ngā Pihi o Te Purapura Pai have a long association with the kōhanga reo. The whānau is actively involved in children’s learning and collectively share in the management of the kōhanga reo. There is good whānau support for kōhanga reo events, the daily programme and for trips. Decision making is shared and responsibilities are delegated to those members who have the skills or experience to undertake specific roles. The relationship between adults and children is supportive and caring. They display genuine support for each other.

A core group of whānau members is responsible for all aspects of the new kōhanga reo that is under development. This project is well supported by the wider whānau and is the major future focus for the whānau at this time.

The whānau regularly monitor all key areas of operations in the kōhanga reo. Regular reporting to whānau occurs at monthly whānau hui. The whānau are committed to the kaupapa of kōhanga reo and children’s learning and care.

Te reo Māori is spoken consistently at the kōhanga reo. Children speak te reo Māori spontaneously and with confidence. Conversations and questions occur naturally between children and with adults.

Children’s interests are followed through an organised and flexible programme. There is a good balance of indoor and outdoor activity. These activities are varied and provide interest and challenge for children. Dramatic play is a feature of the daily programme. Children learn about local history through kaupapa mahi and drama. Children learn about their Māori world and the wider world. Kaimahi work well as a team. They are caring and encouraging of children. Older children learn to look after younger children. Kaimahi are aware of the importance of purposeful interactions with the children. The children know their kōhanga reo environment well.

The indoor and outdoor work and play areas are well maintained and attractive. Children’s work is displayed throughout the kōhanga reo. The whānau and kaimahi of the kōhanga reo are working together to establish a new, purpose built kōhanga reo on the present site. Children enjoy their kōhanga reo environment.

Kaimahi confidently and collectively plan kaupapa programmes for children. They have developed an effective planning model to guide them when they deliver kaupapa programmes to children. Information regarding children’s progress is collated well in their individual folders. Information links activities and children’s development toTeWhārikiand shows progress over time. Parents are kept informed through annual reports.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Pihi o Te Purapura Pai was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.  This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Pihi o Te Purapura Pai.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

4 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

According to a Māori world view ngā Taumata Whakahirahira encompass all areas of a child’s learning and development.

Areas of good performance

The learning programme. The organisation of the programme is very flexible and follows children’s interests. There is a good balance of indoor and outdoor activity. Children’s activities are varied and provide interest and challenge for children. They have opportunities to come together as a whole group. Children enjoy exploring their environment. They learn about local history through kaupapa mahi and drama.

Dramatic play is a feature of the daily programme. Children learn about who they are and their relationships to others. Literacy is encouraged throughout the programme including shared reading, use of books to re-enact stories and a print saturated environment. Children learn about their Māori world and the wider world. The children know their kōhanga reo environment well.

Te reo Māori. Te reo Māori is spoken consistently at the kōhanga reo. Children speak te reo Māori spontaneously and with confidence. Conversations and questions occur naturally between children and with adults. Kaimahi encourage, support and extend conversations with children. Kaupapa vocabulary is used well. Karakia, himene, waiata, drama and haka are an integral part of te reo Māori and tikanga practices. Many whānau members are involved in TKRNT training to increase their own language skills to better support their children and kaimahi.

Whanaungatanga. Kaimahi have well established relationships with each other, the children they work with and with whānau members. Whānau members support kaimahi and each other in all areas of the kōhanga reo. Leadership roles are shared. Roles and responsibilities are clear. Kaimahi work well as a team. They are caring and encouraging of children. Older children learn to look after younger children. Kaimahi are aware of the importance of purposeful interactions with the children.

Kōhanga reo environment. The indoor and outdoor work and play areas are well maintained and attractive. Children’s work is displayed throughout the kōhanga reo. The whānau and kaimahi of the kōhanga reo are working together to establish a new, purpose built kōhanga reo on the present site. Children enjoy their kōhanga reo environment.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management. The whānau is actively involved in children’s learning and collectively shares in the management of the kōhanga reo. There is good whānau support for kōhanga reo events, the daily programme and for trips. Whānau hui are held regularly and are well attended. Ongoing communication with parents includes the use of board notices, newsletters and a daybook for recording and sharing relevant daily information.

The whānau support kaimahi with professional development and programme delivery. An induction process for new whānau and kaimahi helps to clarify roles and responsibilities. Decision making is shared and responsibilities are delegated to those members who have the skills or experience to undertake specific roles. Financial management systems are robust and transparent. A core group of whānau members is responsible for all aspects of the new kōhanga reo that is under development. The wider whānau support this project well. The whānau are committed to the kaupapa of kōhanga reo and children’s learning and care.

Self review. The whānau regularly monitor all key areas of operations in the kōhanga reo. Regular reporting to whānau occurs at monthly whānau hui and includes all areas of kōhanga reo operations. Reviews of policies and practices are evident through minutes and other documents. The whānau are responsive to the need for continual improvement. They accomplish this through the regular review of kōhanga reo operations.

Programme planning. Kaimahi confidently and collectively plan kaupapa programmes for children. They have developed an effective planning model to guide them when they deliver kaupapa programmes to children. Planning format includes outcomes, activities, books, and songs relevant to the kaupapa. The current kaupapa activities are displayed for parents to see and easily understand. Kaiako support kaiāwhina through planning and delivery of programmes and providing additional ideas and feedback. Planning is shared with parents. Individuals and group planning is interest based. Children are provided with well planned kaupapa programmes.

Assessment. Information about children’s progress is collated well in their individual folders. Children’s artwork samples, including comments and other activities, are attractively displayed. Achievement information links activities and children’s development toTe Whārikiand shows progress over time. Parents are kept informed through annual reports. Children’s learning and development is well monitored.

Areas for development

Next steps for learning. Kaimahi keep clear and concise information on what and how children are learning. They acknowledge that a next step in their assessment process is to extend their observations of children at work and at play. Kaimahi understand the valueof identifying individual children’s particular areas of interest and strengths and how these might be better supported and extended within the learning programme.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Pihi o Te Purapura Pai completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist.  In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

6 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau recommend that:

  • the whānau continue to work together across all areas of the kōhanga reo
  • the whānau and kaimahi further develop assessment practices to continue to support the learning and development of their children.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
