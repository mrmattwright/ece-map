Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rākau Kōwhai o Ngā Tamariki ki Waihōpai. He māia te āhua o ngā tamariki ki ngā taiao o te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. Ka whakahaere te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rākau Kowhai o Ngā Tamariki i roto i te toru o ngā tau. 

1  Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rākau Kōwhai o Ngā Tamariki ki Waihōpai. E whakahaerehia ana te kōhanga reo i tētahi whare hāhi wāteatea. Nō ngā hau e whā ngā whānau. Ka ahu mai te ingoa o Te Rākau Kōwhai i te whakatō o tētahi rākau kōwhai i roto i te temepara Rātana. Ko ngā whakaakoranga me te whakapono o te hāhi Rātana, e whai pānga nui ana ki tēnei kōhanga reo.

2  Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te kite ki ngā tamariki tō rātou tūmāia, tō rātou āheinga hoki i roto i ngā taiao o te reo me ngā tikanga Māori?

He māia te āhua o ngā tamariki ki ngā taiao o te reo me ngā tikanga Māori.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira 

Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā uaratanga ā-wairua, ā-tangata hoki. Ko ngā uaratanga o te hāhi Rātana me ngā mātāpono hoki o te whānau, pērā i te aroha me te manaaki, kua āta whakatōngia ki ngā taunekeneke o ia rā. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i ngā taunekeneke e manaaki ana, e whai hua ana hoki ki te taha o ētahi atu. Ka tino manaakitia ngā tamariki, ā, ka tautokona hoki tā rātou ako ki te kōhanga reo. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki.

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao ako o te kounga kairangi. Kua āta waihangatia te hōtaka akoranga me te taiao mō ngā tamariki. He hāneanea te noho o ngā tamariki ki ngā wāhi ako o roto, o waho hoki. Kua āta whakaritea aua wāhi hei poipoi i ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Kua āta whakatōngia ngā mahinga, ā, he ngāwari te rere o ngā whakawhitinga mai i tētahi taumahi, ki tētahi atu. Ka tino ngākau nuitia ngā whakaaturanga me ngā rauemi, ā, ka akiaki ēnei i te tūhura o ngā tamariki. Whai wāhi ai te whānau ki te hōtaka akoranga i ia te wā. E whai wāhi nui ana ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga e whai pūtake ana ki a rātou.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore ngā tamariki e rumakina ana ki te reo Māori mō te katoa o te rā. Ahakoa ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki, ka whakanui hoki ngā kaimahi i ā rātou whakamātau ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, he maha tonu ngā wā ka kōrero ngā pākeke i te reo Pākehā. Kāhore he wāhi i whakaritea ai mō te reo Māori anake. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, e tika ana kia whakawhānuihia te reo o ngā tamariki, mai i te reo māmā, ki te hōhonutanga ake o te reo. E whakawhāitihia ana te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

He pai te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Mai i te arotake o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kua whakapau kaha te whānau ki te whakatupu ake i tō rātou mōhiotanga, ō rātou pūkenga, me tā rātou whai wāhi mātātoa ki te kōhanga reo. He tau ngā pūnaha whakahaere. Tū ai ngā hui i ia te wā, ā, ka whakatutukihia ngā kaupapa matua, tae atu ki te pūtea, te hauora, te haumaru, te ako a te whānau, te whakahaere i ngā kaimahi, me ngā putanga mō ngā tamariki. Ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā whakataunga katoa a te whānau.

Kua āta whakamaheretia, kua āta whakaritea hoki ngā hōtaka akoranga. E hāngai ana ngā aratohu whakamahere, aratohu whakapuaki hoki ki Te Whāriki. Kei ngā mahere te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo kaupapa ako whakahihiri mā ngā tamariki. Ka tīni, ka āta whakarerekē haere rānei ngā kaimahi hei hāpai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te ako.

E tino tautokona ana te ako a ngā tamariki. Ka kitea ki ngā mōhiohio e whakaemihia ana, ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. He whakatakotoranga aromatawai hou anō hoki kei aua mōhiohio. Kei ngā pūkete o mua ngā tauira mahi a ngā tamariki. Ka hāngai ēnei ki ngā kaupapa ako, ā, he kōrerorero anō hoki e whakamārama ana i ngā akoranga me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Kei te tino mōhio ngā kaimahi ki ia tamaiti, ā, he kaha tā rātou whakapuaki i aua kitenga ki ngā mātua me te whānau.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E mōhio ana te whānau, e tika ana kia whakapā atu ngā kaimahi ki ngā akoranga ngaio me te whakawhanaketanga ngaio e whai pānga nui ana ki a rātou i ia te wā, ā, kia arotahi hoki tēnei ki te whakapiki ake i te mātau ki te reo Māori.

3  Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rākau Kōwhai o Ngā Tamariki ki Waihōpai. He māia te āhua o ngā tamariki ki ngā taiao o te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. Ka whakahaere te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rākau Kowhai o Ngā Tamariki is in Invercargill. Children appear confident in te reo and tikanga Māori environments.  The whānau manage the kōhanga reo in the best interests of children.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rākau Kowhai o Ngā Tamariki will be in three years. 

1  Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rākau Kowhai o Ngā Tamariki is in Invercargill. The kōhanga reo operates from a spacious church building. Whānau come from many different iwi. Te Rākau Kōwhai represents the commemorative planting of a kōwhai tree in the Rātana temple. The teachings and beliefs of the Rātana faith are important at this kōhanga reo. 

2  The Review Findings

How well do children demonstrate confidence and capability in te reo and tikanga Māori environments?

Children appear confident in te reo and tikanga Māori environments. 

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children learn spiritual and cultural values. Rātana values and whānau principles of aroha and manaaki are embedded in daily interactions. Children experience caring and positive interactions with others. Children are well cared for and supported to learn at kōhanga reo. Children are happy and settled.

Children learn in a high quality learning environment. The learning programme and environment is well designed for children. Children are comfortable inside and in the outdoor learning areas. These are organised to cater for children’s needs and interests. Routines are well established and transitions between activities flow smoothly. Interesting displays and resources encourage children to explore. Whānau have regular input to the learning programme. Children are engaged in learning that is meaningful to them.

Key Next Steps

Children are not immersed in te reo Māori throughout the day. While children are receptive and kaimahi praise children’s efforts to speak te reo Māori, adults often speak English. There are no designated areas for te reo Māori only. Kaimahi acknowledge the need to extend children’s language from basic to more complex language.  Children’s development as te reo Māori speakers is limited.

Planning and Evaluation

The kōhanga reo is managed well. Whānau have worked hard since the previous ERO review to grow their knowledge base, skills and active involvement in the kōhanga reo. Systems for management are sound. Hui are held regularly and key areas are covered, including finance, health and safety, whānau learning, personnel management and outcomes for children. Children are at the centre of whānau decision making.

Programmes of learning are well planned and organised. Guidelines for planning and delivery are based on Te Whāriki. Planning includes a range of interesting kaupapa ako for children. Kaimahi adjust or make useful changes to support children’s learning. Children enjoy learning.

Children are well supported in their learning. Information is collected to show children’s learning and development. This includes a new assessment format. Previous profiles contain samples of children’s work. These align to kaupapa ako with annotations that describe learning and children’s interests. Kaimahi know individual children well and are able to readily share these observations with parents and whānau.

Key Next Steps

The whānau recognise the need to ensure kaimahi access regular and targeted professional learning and development that includes a focus on improving proficiency and fluency in te reo Māori.

3  Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rākau Kowhai o Ngā Tamariki is in Invercargill. Children appear confident in te reo and tikanga Māori environments.  The whānau manage the kōhanga reo in the best interests of children.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rākau Kowhai o Ngā Tamariki will be in three years. 

Lynda Pura-Watson

Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori
