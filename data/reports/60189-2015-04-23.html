1 Evaluation of Polyhigh Community Childcare Centre

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Polyhigh Community Childcare Centre is located on the campus of Wellington High School and Massey University of Wellington. The centre operates 10 hours a day and is licensed for 50 children, including 20 aged up to 2 years. It is governed by a parent committee.

The ethnic diversity of staff contributes to meeting the needs of the multicultural community, including the 10 MƒÅori children on the roll. Children come from the wider Wellington region.

Most of the teachers are fully registered and qualified. Many are long serving. The high ratio of teachers to children supports opportunities for individual attention to be given to children.

All food is provided. The centre supports healthy eating and is working towards a Healthy Heart Award.

The philosophy has recently been reviewed and is clearly evident in practice. The Resources in Infant Education (RIE) and the Reggio Emilia approach to early childhood education are promoted within the programme.

Polyhigh Community Childcare Centre has a positive ERO reporting history. It has achieved the next steps identified in the July 2012 ERO report.

The Review Findings

Children benefit from a very well planned and implemented programme. The centre‚Äôs three learning areas support children to develop at their own pace. The thoughtful and well-managed transition programme enables smooth and effective progress from one room to the next. Children have opportunities to play with siblings. Teachers across all areas communicate very well.

Children are actively engaged in a wide range of learning experiences. Interactions among them and with the teachers are very positive. Conversations are constant and authentic, supporting the growth of children's literacy skills. Children are seen as capable learners and they are treated with respect. They are encouraged to be independent and self managing.

Teachers worked closely together and involved the parent community in the recent review of the centre philosophy. This review, which emphasised the importance of place-based learning, has impacted positively on the programme. It has led to teachers and children researching their physical and historical environment, with community support.

A strong bicultural programme is supported by a positive relationship with the on-campus marae, Kuratini. The children have tangata whenua status and now actively welcome children from other centres on to the marae. The bicultural approach is highly evident in the environment with displays, extensive use of natural materials and resources. Te reo MƒÅori is used by the teachers and children, in waiata, karakia and pepeha. Children have visited other marae, including Te Papa Tongawera marae, and various local landmarks.

Programme planning effectively supports group needs and interests. Teachers prepare plans for each child in consultation with families. The impact of the programme is evaluated and documented leading to informed decisions to maximise learning. Teachers strongly support children to grow their literacy, mathematics and science knowledge. Children with high needs are very well integrated into the programme and involved in all activities. All children settle well in an environment that recognises their needs. They explore, create and play in an unhurried, respectful atmosphere.

Parent and whƒÅnau involvement in planning, assessment and review encourages partnerships for learning. Parents access children's learning stories through a secure electronic portal with their families. This facilitates considerable response and conversation.

Children leave the centre to go to schools across the wider Wellington region. The centre programme enables children to be very well prepared to enter the school environment. Increasingly close relations are being developed with local schools.

The parent committee and manager provide very effective governance for and administration of the centre. The manager provides strong and knowledgeable leadership that effectively supports three assistant supervisors and staff to capably undertake their work.

Sound governance and leadership has assisted in establishing a collaborative staff culture. Staff receive suitable professional development opportunities. The teaching team is enthusiastic about providing high-quality care and education for all children at the centre.

Practices are in place to support sustained practice. Key elements of centre operations and the programme are regularly reviewed. The information from this self-review programme is gathered from a range of sources including research and community views. It is a well-established model that is evidence based. Outcomes are effectively used to inform decisions about improvements in practice and organisation.

Key Next Steps

This is an improvement-focused centre. Leaders are constantly identifying development steps to provide better quality experiences for each child.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Polyhigh Community Childcare Centre completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Next ERO Review

When is ERO likely to review the service again?

The next ERO review of Polyhigh Community Childcare Centre will be in four years.
