2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ko te Poari Kaitiaki o Kokiri Marae Keriana Olsen te rōpū mana whakahaere o Te Kōhanga Reo o Kokiri Marae me Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hono Ki Kokiri ki Wainuiomata me Te Kōhanga Reo o Paparākau ki Whakatiki.

E whakarato ana te kaiwhakahaere i nga pūkenga tātaki mō ngā whakahaere katoa o te Poari Kaitiaki, ā, ko te whakahaere i ngā kōhanga reo ōnā haepapa tātaki. Ka mahi tahi tētahi kaihāpai ki te taha o te kaiwhakahaere kia tōtika ai ngā whakahaere a ngā kōhanga reo e ai ki te tirohanga whakamua o te Poari Kaitiaki, tō rātou tino kaupapa, ō rātou kaupapa here me ngā whakaritenga o ia rā a te Poari Kaitiaki.

Kua whakaputaina e te whānau i ō rātou manawanui ki te kōhanga reo. E ngākau nui ana, ā, e tino ū ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakarato i ngā akoranga whai painga mō ā rātou tamariki. Ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake, arā, ko te whakaako, te whakanui, te akiaki, te arahi me te tautoko i ngā mokopuna me te whānau ki te pupuri i ō rātou oranga ā-wairua, oranga ā-tinana, oranga ā- pāpori me ō rātou oraranga ā-ahurea te whānuitanga o tēnei kaupapa arotake.

E hāngai ana te hōtaka akoranga ki ngā matea akoranga o ngā tamariki. Kei te pai ngā kaimahi ki te whakarite me te whakahaere i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka kaha arotahi rātou ki te whakawhanake i ngā pūkenga o te mātauranga ā-pāngarau me te mātauranga ā-pānui o ngā tamariki. Ka āhei ngā tamariki ki te noho me te tino arotahi mō te wā roa.

He mea tiaki me te aroha te āhua o ngā taunekeneke a ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. He mea pono te āhua o te whai koha a ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki, ā, e whai wheako ana ngā tamariki ki te wero i ō rātou whakaaro. Kua akiaki ngā tamariki ki te tū hei ākonga mahira mā te tūhura i ngā ariā me ngā whakaaro e hāngai ana ki ngā ngohe kua whakamāheretia me ngā tākaro tene. Kua tautoko ngā tamariki ki te whai māramatanga me te whakaputa i te reo Māori. Ka whakahaere ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki whai hua hei tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i tō rātou reo.

Kei te Poari Kaitiaki o Kokiri Marae ngā pūnaha whai painga hei whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā o te kōhanga reo. Kātahi anō ka arotakengia te tūtohinga. He mea aratohu te āhua o te tūtohinga mō ā rātou kaupapa here me ā rātou tukanga whakahaere o ia rā. Kua whakatūria e te whānau ngā tukanga pakari rawa mō te arotake whaiaro. E hāngai tōtika ana te arotake whaiaro ki ngā whakahaere hei whakapai ake anō.

Kei te Poari Kaitiaki o Kokiri Marae te mahi whakahaere pūtea. E ora pai ana te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakatūria ngā tukanga tōtika mō te tono me te whakatau i ngā kaimahi. He matawhānui te tukanga aromihi.

Kua whakaritea te hōtaka whakamāherehere me ngā pūkete akoranga mā ngā tamariki. He tirohanga matawhānui, he āmiki hoki ngā mea e rua nei. Ka āta arahi te hōtaka whakamāherehere i te hōtaka akoranga. Kua whakaritea ngā pūkete akoranga mā ngā tamariki. E tino mārama ana ngā mātua ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me ō rātou putanga paetae.

Kua tae te wā mā te Kaitiaki Poari ki te whakatū i tētahi tukanga hei aromātai i te whai painga o te hōtaka akoranga.

Ngā Mahi Ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o Te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Khanga Reo o Kokiri Marae (Nui ake i te rua o ng tau) kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.ōā

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Khanga Reo o Kokiri Marae (Nui ake i te rua o ng tau).ōā

I whakaritea e te whānau ko āna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere i te māhere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • hōtaka whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

Ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E hāngai ana tēnei ki te kaupapa o Te Kōhanga Reo o Kokiri Marae, arā, te whakaako, te whakanui, te akiaki, te arahi me te tautoko i ngā mokopuna me te whānau ki te pupuri i ō rātou oraranga ā-wairua, oranga ā-tinana, oranga ā-noho me ō rātou oranga ā-ahurea.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

E whakatutuki ana te hōtaka akoranga i ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki katoa. E whai ake nei ētahi huarahi e kitea ai tēnei āhuatanga:

He mea ngāwari ngā whakawhitiwhiti a ngā tamariki i waenganui i ngā ngohe akoranga me te takamahi o ia rā. E waia ana ngā tamariki te rere o te hōtaka, ā, ka tino mōhio rātou he aha te mahi whai muri i tēnā ngohe me tēnā ngohe. Kei te tino pai ngā kaimahi ki te whakahaere me te whakarite i ngā āhuatanga mō te hōtaka, ā, e tino mārama ana rātou he aha ō rātou tūranga me ngā haepapa mō te rā katoa.

He kaupapa nui te mahi hei whakapakari ake i ngā pūkenga o ngā tamariki mō te pāngarau me te pānui. Ka harikoa ngā tamariki ki te mahi i aua mahi. Ka kaha arotahi rātou ki ēnei wheako mō te wā roa.

He wā anō ki ngā tamariki hei whakaohooho i te hinengaro. E āhei ana rātou ki te whakaputa i o rātou pūkenga whiriwhiri pānga i rātou e whai haere ana i ngā ngohe. Ka akiaki ngā tamariki ki te tū hei ākonga mahira mā te tūhura i ngā ariā me ngā whakaaro o ngā ngohe kua whakamāhereheretia me ngā tākaro tene.

Kua tautoko ngā tamariki ki te whai māramatanga me te whakaputa i te reo Māori. E whakahaere ana ngā kaimahi i ngā tohu akiaki, ngā tīwhiri me ngā whakawhitiwhiti kōrero ki te whakanui ake i ngā āheinga o ngā tamariki ki te whakaputa i te reo Māori. Ko ngā waiata me te kōrero i ngā pūrākau ētahi rautaki e tautoko ana i ngā tamariki ki te whakapiki i ō rātou mātau ki te reo.

He mea tiaki, he aroha te āhua o ngā taunekeneke a ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki. He pono te whai koha o tēnā me tēnā ki ngā tamariki. E āwhina ana ēnei whanaungatanga ki te whakatau i ngā tamariki me te tautoko i a rātou ki te whakapakari ake i ō rātou māiatanga ki te ako.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamāherehere me te aromātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo hei whakatau i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamāherehere, ngā whakahaere me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga reo mā a rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka āta hāngai te aromātai nei ki te hōtaka whakamāherehere me te aroturuki i te tipuranga ako o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Kei te Poari Kaitiaki ngā pūnaha whai painga hei whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā o te kōhanga reo.

Kātahi anō kia arotakengia te tūtohinga. E mārama ana te tirohanga whakamua me te tino kaupapa te Poari i ngā hiahia o ngā kaitiaki mā ngā mokopuna me te whānau e tae mai ana ki te kōhanga reo. He mea aratohu te āhua o te tūtohinga mō ā rātou kaupapa here me te tukanga whakahaere o ia rā. Kei te hāngai tōtika ēnei ki ngā rekureihana mō ngā ratonga whare kōhungahunga 2008.

Kātahi anō kia oti te mahi hei whakahou me te arotake i ngā kaupapa here me ngā whakaritenga o ia rā mō te hauora me te haumaru. He aratohu whai hua ēnei mō ngā whakahaere o ia rā. E rata ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao i te mea ko tō rātou hauora me tō rātou oranga te kaupapa nui.

Kua whakatūria e te whānau ngā tukanga pakari rawa mō te arotake whaiaro. E hāngai tōtika ana te arotake whaiaro ki ngā whakahaere hei whakapai ake anō.

Kei te Poari Kaitiaki o Kokiri Marae te mahi whakahaere pūtea. He kōataata tē āhua o ngā pūnaha. Kua tāutuhia ngā rauemi hei tautoko i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i roto i te māhere whāroa tāhua pūtea. E ora pai ana te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo.

Kua whakatūria ngā tukanga tōtika mō te tono me te whakatau i ngā kaimahi. He matawhānui te tukanga aromihi. E hāngai ana ēnei ki te tino kaupapa me ngā tino hiahia o te Poari Kaitiaki o Kokiri Marae. Mā tēnei kua whakaritea ngā wheako whakangungu kia pakari ake ai ngā putanga mahi a ngā kaimahi hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Kua whakaritea te hōtaka whakamāherehere me ngā pūkete akoranga mā ngā tamariki. Ka āta ārahi te hōtaka whakamāherehere i te hōtaka akoranga. He mārama te kite atu i ngā kaupapa ako me ngā rautaki hei tautoko i te akoranga ā ngā tamariki me ngā rauemi e hiahiatia ana. E hāngai tōtika ana ngā pūkete akoranga a ngā mokopuna ki ngā whakamāherehere. E tino mārama ana te whānau ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me ō rātou putanga paetae.He matawhānui, he āmiki hoki ngā mea e rua nei.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te hōtaka aromātai. Me kaha te arotahi ki te hōtaka aromātai. He iti noa ngā taunakitanga e tāututia ai, ā hea, ka pēhea, ā, he aha i whakarerekētia ai ngā whakahaere a te hōtaka akoranga. Me whai wāhi atu te whānau inaiānei ki te whai whakaaro mō tētahi tukanga hei aromātai i te whai hua o te hōtaka.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

He whakamārama

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Khanga Reo o Kokiri Marae (Nui ake i te rua o ng tau) tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:ōā

  • ngā whakahaerenga a te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

The Kokiri Marae, Keriana Olsen Trust (Trust), is the governing body for the Kokiri Marae Te Kōhanga Reo, Te Hono Ki Kokiri Te Kōhanga Reo in Wainuiomata and Paparākau Te Kōhanga Reo, Upper Hutt.

The manager provides leadership in all aspects of the Trust’s operations which include the kōhanga reo. A supervisor works alongside the manager to ensure that the kōhanga reo operates in conjunction with the Trust’s mission, philosophy, policies and procedures.

The whānau has expressed complete satisfaction with the kōhanga reo. The kaimahi are passionate and committed to providing the best possible outcomes for their children. Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira is the focus of this review which is to teach, promote, encourage, guide, support and assist mokopuna and whānau in the conservation of their spiritual, physical, social and cultural wellbeing.

The learning programme appropriately caters for the learning needs of children. Kaimahi manage and organise the learning programme well. There is a strong focus on developing children’s literacy and numeracy skills. Children remain engaged for long periods of time.

Kaimahi interactions with children are caring and loving. There is a genuine respect for children who have opportunities to be challenged in their thinking. Children are encouraged to be curious learners while they explore concepts and ideas through planned activities as well as spontaneous play. They are supported in their understanding and use of te reo Māori. Kaimahi use appropriate strategies to support children’s language development.

The Trust has effective systems to manage kōhanga reo operations. The charter has recently been reviewed. It provides clear direction in the policies and procedures. The whānau has a robust self review process. This leads to improvement in practice.

Finances are managed by the finance manager of the Kokiri Marae Trust. The kōhanga reo is in a financially viable position. There are clear processes for appointing kaimahi to work in the kohanga reo. The appraisal process is comprehensive.

Programme planning and records of mokopuna learning are in place. Both are comprehensive and detailed. The programme plan guides the learning programme clearly. Whānau responses clearly show that they are clear about what children are learning and their progress.

It is now time for the Trust to establish a process to evaluate the effectiveness of the learning programme.

Future Action

ERO is confident the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Khanga Reo o Kokiri Marae (Over 2 Years) was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.ō

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Te Khanga Reo o Kokiri Marae (Over 2 Years).ō

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

4 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira is the focus of this review. This links to the philosophy of Kokiri Marae Te Kōhanga Reo which is to teach, promote, encourage, guide, support and assist mokopuna and whānau in the conservation of their spiritual, physical, social and cultural wellbeing.

Areas of good performance

The learning programme appropriately caters for the learning needs of all children. This is apparent in a number of ways.

Children transition between learning activities and routines easily. The flow of the programme is familiar and predictable. Kaimahi manage and organise the programme well and they are clear about their roles and responsibilities throughout the day.

There is a strong focus on developing children’s literacy and numeracy skills. Children enjoy the activities associated with these. They are engaged for long periods of time.

Children have opportunities to be challenged in their thinking. They are able to apply problem solving skills in activities. Children are encouraged to be curious learners by exploring concepts and ideas through planned activities and spontaneous play.

Children are supported in their understanding and use of te reo Māori. Kaimahi use prompts and clues as well as conversations to extend children’s use of te reo Māori. Waiata and storytelling are also used to support children’s language development.

Kaimahi interactions with children are caring and loving. There is a genuine respect for children. These relationships help to settle children and support them as they become more confident as learners.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

The Trust uses effective systems to manage the operations of the kōhanga reo.

The charter has recently been reviewed. The mission and philosophy clearly outlines the intent the Trustees have for mokopuna and whānau who attend the kōhanga reo. The charter provides clear direction for their policies and procedures. These are aligned to the 2008 Early Childhood Regulations.

All health and safety policies and procedures have recently been reviewed and updated. They provide clear guidelines for practice. Children enjoy an environment where their health and wellbeing is nurtured.

The whānau has a robust self review process. This clearly highlights key aspects of practice and leads to actions for improvement.

Finances are managed by the finance manager of the Kokiri Marae Trust. The systems are transparent. Resources to support children’s learning are clearly identified in the budget forecast. The kōhanga reo is in a financially viable position.

There are clear processes for appointing kaimahi to work in the kōhanga reo. This clearly reflects the philosophy and intent of the Kokiri Marae Trust. There is a comprehensive appraisal process. Kaimahi professional development enhances their practice for the benefit of the children.

Programme planning and records of mokopuna learning are in place. Both are comprehensive and detailed. The programme plan clearly guides the learning programme. This identifies kaupapa ako, strategies for supporting children’s learning and required resources. Mokopuna learning records are directly linked to planning. Whānau are clear about what children are learning and their progress.

Areas for development

Programme Evaluation. Programme evaluation needs attention. There is little evidence to identify when, how and why changes are made to the learning programme. It is time for whānau to consider a process to evaluate the effectiveness of the programme

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Khanga Reo o Kokiri Marae (Over 2 Years) completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:ō

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

To the Parents and Community of Te Kōhanga Reo o Kokiri Marae (Nui ake i te rua o nga tau)

These are the findings of the Education Review Office’s latest report onTe Khanga Reo o Kokiri Marae (Over 2 Years).ō

The Kokiri Marae, Keriana Olsen Trust (Trust), is the governing body for the Kokiri Marae Te Kōhanga Reo, Te Hono Ki Kokiri Te Kōhanga Reo in Wainuiomata and Paparākau Te Kōhanga Reo, Upper Hutt.

The manager provides leadership in all aspects of the Trust’s operations which include the kōhanga reo. A supervisor works alongside the manager to ensure that the kōhanga reo operates in conjunction with the Trust’s mission, philosophy, policies and procedures.

The whānau has expressed complete satisfaction with the kōhanga reo. The kaimahi are passionate and committed to providing the best possible outcomes for their children. Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira is the focus of this review which is to teach, promote, encourage, guide, support and assist mokopuna and whānau in the conservation of their spiritual, physical, social and cultural wellbeing.

The learning programme appropriately caters for the learning needs of children. Kaimahi manage and organise the learning programme well. There is a strong focus on developing children’s literacy and numeracy skills. Children remain engaged for long periods of time.

Kaimahi interactions with children are caring and loving. There is a genuine respect for children who have opportunities to be challenged in their thinking. Children are encouraged to be curious learners while they explore concepts and ideas through planned activities as well as spontaneous play. They are supported in their understanding and use of te reo Māori. Kaimahi use appropriate strategies to support children’s language development.

The Trust has effective systems to manage kōhanga reo operations. The charter has recently been reviewed. It provides clear direction in the policies and procedures. The whānau has a robust self review process. This leads to improvement in practice.

Finances are managed by the finance manager of the Kokiri Marae Trust. The kōhanga reo is in a financially viable position. There are clear processes for appointing kaimahi to work in the kohanga reo. The appraisal process is comprehensive.

Programme planning and records of mokopuna learning are in place. Both are comprehensive and detailed. The programme plan guides the learning programme clearly. Whānau responses clearly show that they are clear about what children are learning and their progress.

It is now time for the Trust to establish a process to evaluate the effectiveness of the learning programme.

Future Action

ERO is confident the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to student achievement and useful to this kōhanga reo.

If you would like a copy of the full report, please contact the kōhanga reo or see the ERO web page, http://www.ero.govt.nz.

General Information About Reviews

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based on Te Whāriki, the whānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
