Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Papatoetoe Whānau, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau. He pai te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori, ā, ka aro atu rātou ki tēnā e mōhiotia ana. Ko te whanaungatanga kei te pūtake o ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga ki te kōhanga reo. He huarahi anō hei whai atu ki te whakawhānui ake i ngā mōhiotanga me ngā āheinga o ngā tamariki, mā ngā whai wāhitanga ako ka āta whakamaheretia. Ko te whakapakari ake i ngā pūnaha whakahaere tētahi āhuatanga hei whakawhanake ake.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo ki Papatoetoe Whānau i roto ngā tau e rua.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo ki Papatoetoe Whānau ki Papatoetoe, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau. Nā te whakahoutanga o ngā whare nō nā tata tonu nei, kua pai ake te wāhi ako o ngā tamariki. He hiahia tō te whānau kia matatau ā rātou tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, ko te arotake whaiaro tētahi āhuatanga pakari, ā, ko te mātakitaki tamariki tētahi āhuatanga hei whakawhanake tonu. Kua whakapai ake te whānau i ā rātou whakaritenga e pā ana ki te mātakitaki tamariki.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakamahi, e whakaatu hoki i tō rātou māramatanga ki te reo Māori?

Ka mārama ngā tamariki i te reo Māori, ā, ka taea te reo Māori te whakamahi ki te kōhanga reo.

Mana Reo

Ka mārama ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki ngā tūmomo horopaki. He pai ngā kaimahi ki te whakatauira i te reo, ā, ka whakamahi rātou i te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa. Ka patapatai rātou i ia te wā hei whakatairanga i te reo. Ka whakamahia ngā waiata hei whakawhānui i ngā kupu a ngā tamariki, hei whakaako hoki i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo hanganga rerenga kōrero. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whakautu mā te reo Māori.

He pai ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki. He atawhai, he manaaki hoki ki ō rātou hononga ki ngā kaimahi. Ka kite wawe, ka aro wawe hoki ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea ā-whatumanawa, ā-tinana hoki o ngā tamariki. He pai te mahi ngātahi a ngā tamariki ki te taha o ō rātou hoa, ki te whakaoti i ngā taumahi ka whakaratohia. He kaha rātou ki te pātai ki ngā kaimahi me te whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro e pā ana ki ā rātou akoranga. Ka kitea te tūmāia o ngā tamariki.

Kua waia ngā tamariki ki ā rātou mahinga. Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi tētahi wātaka mō ia rā, ā, ka āta whāia. Ka whai ngā tamariki i ngā tohutohu me ngā whakawhitinga ki waenga i ngā mahinga, ā, ka rere pai ngā taumahi. Ka mōhio, ka whai hoki ngā tamariki i ngā tikanga pērā i ngā karakia, te whakarite mō te wā kai, me te tango hū mō roto i te whare. He tau ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He iti noa ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga, ki te kōkiri rānei i ā rātou ake tākaro. Kāhore ngā rauemi e tino wātea ana ki ngā tamariki. Ahakoa te muramura o te wāhi, kāhore e tino whakamahia ana hei wāhi ako. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, he iti noa ā rātou whakawhitinga kōrero mō te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga me te whai pānga o tēnei ki ngā tamariki. Kei te whakawhāitihia te whakawhanaketanga o te tū takitahi o te tamaiti.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

He aronga tō ngā mahere hōtaka. Ka whakahono ngā kaimahi i ngā mahere o te tau, ngā wāhanga, me ngā wiki. Ka tāutu rātou i te kiko noa o ā rātou mahere, pērā i ngā rauemi, ngā taumahi, me ētahi rautaki hei whakaako i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo kaupapa ki ngā tamariki. Ka whai ngā tamariki i ngā tohutohu e pā ana ki ngā taumahi.

Ka mahi ngātahi ngā kaimahi ki ētahi atu, hei whakamahere i ngā taumahi ako. He hononga tō te kaiwhakahaere ki tētahi atu kōhanga reo o te hapori nei. E āhei ana ia ki te kōkiri i te whakamahere ngātahi me ngā kaupapa. I whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i tētahi whakaari, ā, he kaupapa tēnei i kawea ngātahi ai e ngā kōhanga reo e rua. He pakari te taunekeneke a ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu, ki waho atu i tō rātou wāhi ako.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore i te tino mārama te whānuitanga o te whai wāhi a te whānau ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kāhore i te whānau he whāinga mārama, he mahere rānei e arotahi ana ki ngā tamariki, e hāngai ana rānei ki Te Ara Tūāpae. Kāhore ngā kaupapa here e arotakengia ana i ia te wā. Kāhore i te rawaka ngā mōhiohio ki ngā pūrongo, hei tāutu i ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki, hei whakamōhio rānei i ngā whakapaitanga. He whāiti te arotake whaiaro. Kāhore ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki e tino tautokona ana.

Kāhore te aromatawai o te ako me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā pēpi e tino whakaarohia ana. Ka whiwhi te whānau i ētahi mōhiohio e pā ana ki te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki  Ka whakaemi ngā kaimahi i ngā whakaahua me ngā tauira mahi hei tohu i te ahu whakamua. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā tauira ki te whakatakoto i ngā kōrerorero e pā ana ki te ahu whakamua o ngā tamariki tuākana. Engari, kāhore aua tauira e tika ana mā ngā pēpi. Kāhore i te mārama te ahu whakamua a ngā pēpi.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whai aronga te whai wāhi a te whānau
  • whai whakaaro te whānau ki te whakarato i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te kōkiri i ā rātou ake tākaro
  • whai whakaaro te whānau ki te whakarite i tētahi tukanga pai ake mō te aroturuki i te ahu whakamua a ngā pēpi
  • toro atu te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki ētahi atu tautoko o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, hei whakapai ake i te arotake whaiaro.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Papatoetoe Whānau, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau. He pai te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori, ā, ka aro atu rātou ki tēnā e mōhiotia ana. Ko te whanaungatanga kei te pūtake o ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga ki te kōhanga reo. He huarahi anō hei whai atu ki te whakawhānui ake i ngā mōhiotanga me ngā āheinga o ngā tamariki, mā ngā whai wāhitanga ako ka āta whakamaheretia. Ko te whakapakari ake i ngā pūnaha whakahaere tētahi āhuatanga hei whakawhanake ake.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo ki Papatoetoe Whānau i roto ngā tau e rua.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo ki Papatoetoe Whānau is in Papatoetoe, Auckland. Children have a good understanding of te reo Māori and respond to what they know. Whanaungatanga is at the heart of interactions and relationships at the kōhanga reo. There is potential to extend children’s thinking and capability through well planned learning opportunities. Strengthening management systems is an area for development.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo ki Papatoetoe Whānau will be within two years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo Papatoetoe Whānau is in Papatoetoe, Auckland. As a result of recent renovations the physical learning environment for children has improved. Whānau want their children to be speakers of te reo Māori.

The previous ERO report identified self-review as a strength and supervision as an area for development. The kōhanga reo whānau have improved their supervision practices.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children use and demonstrate their understanding of te reo Māori?

Children understand te reo Māori and are able to use it in the kōhanga reo.

Mana Reo

Children understand te reo Māori in their context. Kaimahi are good models of language and they use te reo Māori consistently. They use questions frequently to promote language. Waiata is used to increase children’s vocabulary and teach sentence structures that vary in complexity. Children are able to respond to te reo Māori.

Children interact positively. They have warm and caring relationships with kaimahi. Kaimahi notice and respond to children’s emotional and physical needs immediately. Children work well alongside each other to complete activities as they are provided. They happily ask kaimahi questions and share their ideas about their learning. Children show that they are confident.

Children are familiar with their routines. Kaimahi have established a timetable for each day which is followed carefully. Children follow instructions and transitions between routines and learning activities are smooth. Children know and follow tikanga such as karakia, preparing for kai, removing shoes while inside. Children are settled.

Key Next Steps

Children have few opportunities to follow their own interests or engage in independent play. Resources are not easily accessible to children. Despite the vibrant space this is underutilised as a learning environment. Kaimahi acknowledge that they have few discussions about the quality of the learning programme and the impact on children. Children’s developing independence is inhibited.

Planning and Evaluation

Programme planning is provides some direction. Kaimahi make links between annual, term and weekly plans. They identify the basic content in their planning like, resources, activities and some strategies to teach children about a range of topics. Children follow the instructions for doing activities.

Kaimahi collaborate with others to plan learning activities. The kaiwhakahaere has an affiliation to another local kōhanga reo. She is able to facilitate shared planning and projects. Children participated in producing a play which was a combined activity between the two kōhanga reo. Children confidently interact with others outside of their environment.

Key Next Steps

The extent of whānau participation in managing the kōhanga reo is unclear. The whānau do not have clear goals or a plan that focuses on children or aligns to Te Ara Tūāpae. Policies are inconsistently reviewed. Reports do not provide sufficient information to identify children’s needs and interests or inform ongoing improvements. Self-review is limited. Children’s learning needs are not fully supported.

Assessment of learning and development for babies is not well considered. Whānau receive some information about how children are progressing. Kaimahi collect photographs and work samples to show progress. Kaimahi use templates to comment on the progress of older children. However, these templates are not suitable for the babies. It is unclear how babies are progressing.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • that whānau participation is a focus
  • that the whānau consider how to provide opportunities for independent play
  • that the whānau consider establishing a better process to monitor the progress of babies
  • that the kōhanga reo whānau seek further support from the TKRNT to improve self-review. 

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo ki Papatoetoe Whānau is in Papatoetoe, Auckland. Children have a good understanding of te reo Māori and respond to what they know. Whanaungatanga is at the heart of interactions and relationships at the kōhanga reo. There is potential to extend children’s thinking and capability through well planned learning opportunities. Strengthening management systems is an area for development.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo ki Papatoetoe Whānau will be within two years. 

Lynda Pura-Watson

Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori
