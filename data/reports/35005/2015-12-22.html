Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Kauriki ki waho atu i Taumarunui, ki te marae o Kauriki. He harikoa ngā tamariki, he ākonga pakari hoki rātou ki tētahi taiao akoranga ā-whānau pai. He tākare te whānau ki te kawe i ō rātou tūranga whakahaere, ā rātou mahi whakahaere hoki, hei hāpai i te ako a ā rātou tamariki me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Kauriki i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Kauriki ki te marae o Kauriki, ki te whāruarua o Ngāpuke, ki Taumarunui. Kua whakahaerehia te kōhanga reo mai i te takiwā o te tau 1980. Ko tā te whānau tirohanga, kia rumakina ngā tamariki ki te mātauranga kōhungahunga e tino whai pūtake ana, ā, e whakamana ana i tō rātou reo, i ā rātou taonga tuku iho, me ngā tikanga. Koia nei hoki te hiahia o te whānau o te marae me te hapū hoki o tēnei takiwā, arā, o Ngāti Hinemihi. He hiahia tō ngā mātua kia haere tonu te mātauranga rumaki reo Māori o ā rātou tamariki, ki tua atu i te kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga, i tō rātou tuakiri hoki?

He ākonga māia ngā tamariki, ā, he mātātoa tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki tō rātou whānau.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao ako, ā, he māia, he tau hoki rātou i konei. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i ngā taunekeneke e hāpai ana, e manaaki ana hoki i a rātou. Ka whakaatu ngā tuākana i te aroha, te manaaki, me te whakaaro nui mō ō rātou tēina. Ka whakamihi, ka tautoko hoki ngā pākeke i ngā tamariki ki te ārahi me te whakawhanake hoki i tō rātou tū takitahi. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā taumahi e whai pānga ana ki ngā momo reanga me ngā momo ngākau nuitanga. Ka tohu ngā whakaaturanga mahi a ngā tamariki i tēnā e ākona ana e rātou, i tēnā hoki i whakatutukihia ai e rātou. He ngana ngā tamariki ki te ako.

He pārekareka, ka whai pūtake hoki ngā huarahi e whāia ana e ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo Māori. Ka whakatairangatia te reo Māori me ngā tikanga, puta noa i te hōtaka. Ko ētahi o ngā rautaki ako reo e whai hua ana, ko te tāruarua mā ngā waiata, ko te patapatai, ko te akiaki, ā, ko te whakanui hoki. Ka whakakoia ngā tamariki mō tā rātou whakamahinga i te reo Māori. Ka tautokona te nuinga o ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo, hei whakapiki ake i tō rātou ake āheinga ki te tautoko i ngā tamariki. Ka mārama, ka kōrero hoki ngā tamariki i te reo Māori.

Ka whakapūmautia ngā hononga ahurea, ngā hononga tuku iho hoki o ngā tamariki. Ka whai wāhi matua te marae hei puna akoranga ki te kōhanga reo. Ka ako ngā mātua i ā rātou taonga tuku iho, ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki. Tūhura ai ngā tamariki i tō rātou hapori i ia te wā. Ka whakapūmautia te tuakiri o ngā tamariki nā ngā taumahi i whakaritea ai, ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, me te whakapuaki ngātahi i ngā kōrero tuku iho, ngā waiata, ngā karakia, me ngā pepehā. He tino hononga tō ngā tamariki ki te whenua, te marae, me te whānau.

Ka poipoia ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whakanui ana i ngā whānau katoa. Ka whakamanatia te whanaungatanga ki te kōhanga reo. Kua whakaritea ngā hononga ā-whānau papai e tautokona ana. Ka tautoko te whānau i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo i ia rā, tae atu ki tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki te hōtaka o ia rā, te whakawhanaketanga o ngā rawa, me ngā haerenga. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whai wāhi mai o ō rātou pāpā ki te kōhanga reo, ā, he tino tauira rātou o ngā pūmanawa o te tāne, mō ngā tamariki katoa. He pai ngā hononga o ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. Kei te mārama te whānau ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga hoki mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, tō rātou atawhai, me ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo i ia rā. Ko ngā kaupapa here, ngā tukanga, me ngā mahere pae tawhiti, pae tata hoki e ārahi ana i ngā whakahaeretanga. Arotakengia ai aua tuhinga i ia te wā ki te taha o te whānau. Nā te tautoko pū, te āwhina hoki o ngā kaimahi o te tari ā-rohe o Tūwharetoa o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, kua whakatutuki ngā tino whakapaitanga mō te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā painga mō ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau.

Ka āta whakamaheretia ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka hāngai pū a Te Whāriki ki ngā mahere i whakawhanakehia ai e ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau. Ka whakarite i mua noa atu ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā taumahi ngahau e whai pūtake ana. Ka waihanga, ka hanga hoki rātou i ā rātou ake rauemi e whakatairanga ana i te ako i ngā kaupapa. Ka noho matua ki ngā kaimahi te ako i ngā kōrero e pā ana ki Kauriki, nā te whai pānga nui o tēnei ki ngā tamariki me te whānau. He pai te māramatanga o te whānau ki ngā mahi whakamahere hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā kōrero e pā ana ki a rātou anō.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, e tika ana kia whakawhanakehia ngā aromatawai ka tohu i ngā akoranga me ngā whakatutukitanga a ngā tamariki i roto i te ao Māori, me tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga, tō rātou tuakiri hoki ki Kauriki.

Kua kitea hoki e te whānau, e tika ana kia arotakengia tā rātou mahere rautaki hei whakapai ake i te māramatanga o ngā whāinga ā-tau, hei aroturuki hoki i te āhua o tā rātou ahu whakamua.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakamahi tonu i ngā mōhiohio arotake whaiaro i ia te wā hei aroturuki i te whānuitanga tonu o te āhua mahi, me ngā putanga ka whai hua anō hoki ki te tūtohinga a te whānau me te aronga rautaki.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Kauriki ki waho atu i Taumarunui, ki te marae o Kauriki. He harikoa ngā tamariki, he ākonga pakari hoki rātou ki tētahi taiao akoranga ā-whānau pai. He tākare te whānau ki te kawe i ō rātou tūranga whakahaere, ā rātou mahi whakahaere hoki, hei hāpai i te ako a ā rātou tamariki me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kauriki is on the outskirts of Taumaranui, at Kauriki marae. Children are happy and confident learners in a positive whānau learning environment. Whānau undertake their management roles and responsibilities with enthusiasm to support their children’s learning and the kaupapa of kōhanga reo.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Kauriki will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kauriki is based at Kauriki Marae, in the Ngāpuke valley, Taumarunui. The kōhanga reo has been operating since the early 1980’s. The whānau vision for children is to be immersed in meaningful early childhood education that affirms their language, heritage and customs. This is shared by the marae whānau and local hapū, Ngāti Hinemihi. Parents are keen for their children to continue in Māori immersion education beyond kōhanga reo. 

2 The Review Findings

How well do children demonstrate a sense of belonging and identity?

Children are confident learners and active members of their whānau.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are familiar, confident and settled in their learning environment. Children experience supportive and caring interactions. Tuakana show love, care and concern for their teina. Adults praise and support children to lead and develop independence. Kaimahi provide a range of activities for the various ages and interests. Displays of children’s work show what they are learning and have achieved. Children are engaged learners.

Children learn te reo Māori in enjoyable and meaningful ways. Te reo Māori and tikanga are promoted throughout the programme. Effective language learning strategies include repetition through waiata, questioning, prompting and praise. Children are affirmed for their use of te reo Māori. Most whānau receive language learning support to increase their ability to support children. Children understand and speak te reo Māori. 

Children’s cultural and ancestral connections are reinforced. The marae is an integral part of learning at the kōhanga reo. Parents learn about their heritage with their children. Children regularly explore their local community. Children’s identity is reinforced to in planned activities, conversations, shared stories, waiata, karakia and pepehā. Children have a strong affinity to the land, marae and whānau.

Children experience an environment where all whānau are valued. Whanaungatanga is valued in the kōhanga reo. Positive and supportive whānau relationships are established. Whānau support the daily management of the kōhanga reo including participating in daily programme, property developments and outings. Children enjoy having their fathers at the kōhanga reo where they are positive male role models for all children. Children have positive relationships. 

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau manage the kōhanga reo well. Whānau understand their roles and responsibilities for children’s learning, care and daily kōhanga reo management. Policies and procedures and long and short term plans guide operations. These are regularly reviewed with whānau. Targeted support and advice from the Tūwharetoa district Kōhanga Reo staff has brought about positive improvements for the kōhanga reo. Children’s interests are central to whānau management.

Learning for children is well planned. Te Whāriki is integral to plans developed by kaimahi and whānau members. Kaimahi and whānau prepare well in advance meaningful and fun activities. They design and make their own resources that promote learning kaupapa. Kaimahi give priority to learning about Kauriki as this is meaningful to children and whānau. Whānau have a good understanding of planning for children’s. Children learn about who they are.

Key Next Steps

Kaimahi acknowledge the need to develop assessments that show children’s learning and success in te ao Māori and their sense of belonging and identity to Kauriki.

Whānau have recognised the need to review their strategic plan to improve the clarity of annual goals and monitor how well they are progressing.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • continue to use regular self-review information to monitor overall performance and outcomes in line with the whānau charter and strategic direction.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kauriki is on the outskirts of Taumaranui, at Kauriki marae. Children are happy and confident learners in a positive whānau learning environment. Whānau undertake their management roles and responsibilities with enthusiasm to support their children’s learning and the kaupapa of kōhanga reo. 

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Kauriki will be in three years.
