1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Marae o Hoani Waititi ki te marae o Hoani Waititi. Koia nei te arotake tuatahi o te kōhanga reo, whai muri i tōna tū takitahi, motuhake anō hoki i te Tarahiti o te marae o Hoani Waititi, me te komiti o Hoani Waititi. He pakari te tūnga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakatau i ngā take e toe ana. Ka hāngai aua take ki te kirimini rīhi me ētahi o ōna tikanga. Kei te noho tonu ētahi mema o te whānau ki te tarahiti me te komiti o te marae. E pakari haere ana te māia o te whānau ki te whakatau i ā rātou ake whakaritenga, me te whakariterite haere mō meāke nei.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Aotūroa te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ko tā rātou kaupapa matua i tēnei wā, ko te whakauru mai o ētahi tamariki hou ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo, me te whakawhanake i tō rātou tino waiora, tō rātou mauritau anō hoki ki te kōhanga reo te kaupapa tuarua, ko te whakapūmau tonu i ngā hononga ki te whānau whānui. E mōhio ana te whānau, ko te ao Māori, ngā tikanga, te whanaungatanga, me te ao whānui, e pou here ana i tō rātou māramatanga ki te Mana Aotūroa.

He tau, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki i a rātou e ako ana, e tūhura ana hoki. Ka whakatauira ngā tamariki tuākana i ngā whanonga mō ō rātou tēina, ā, ka āta hāpai anō hoki i a rātou ki te whakatutuki i ngā mahinga. He rite ngā kaimahi, ā, e mōhio ana rātou ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki, ā, he mārie tā rātou taunekeneke haere, tō rātou tautoko anō hoki i ngā tamariki, i a rātou anō.

Ka āta whakamahia ngā wāhi puta noa i te kōhanga reo, hei hāpai i ngā tamariki i a rātou e ako ana, e tākaro ana hoki. Ka tino whai hua te wāteatea o ngā parani, ā, kua āta whakaritea ngā rauemi e noho wātea ana ki ngā tamariki. He māmā te whakawhiti ki roto, ki waho. Taunekeneke ai ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi i ia wā, ki ētahi atu o ngā kōhanga reo, ā, he whai wāhitanga tēnei mō ngā tamariki ki te tākaro ngātahi.

Ka whakaritea ngā tirohanga whānui me ngā mahere ā-wāhanga, ā, he pai te whakatakotoranga o ngā kiko kōrero. Nō nā tata tonu nei ngā kaimahi i haere ai ki ngā whakangungu e pā ana ki Te Whāriki. I hāpai tēnei i ngā mōhiotanga o ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere i ētahi tino kaupapa. He mahere ā-wiki tā ia kaimahi i roto i ā rātou rātaka, mō ā rātou ake rōpū. Kōrerorerohia ai ngā aromatawai me ngā whakaaro aromātai i ia te wā i ngā hui o ia wiki. Kua tāutuhia ngā whakapaitanga ake i roto i te aromatawai me te aromātai hōtaka.

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. E ngākau nui ana rātou ki te whakarato i te mātauranga o te kounga kairangi mō ā rātou tamariki. He mārama ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki te whānau mō ngā whakahaeretanga o ia rā, me te whakatakoto haere i ngā whāinga, ngā mahere hoki mō meāke nei. I tēnei wā e whakawhiti kōrero ana te whānau ki te whakatū i tētahi papa tākaro taunekeneke.

He tākare te whānau mō ā rātou tūmomo whakawhanaketanga ka pupū ake hei te tau 2014.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Marae o Hoani Waititi kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Aotūroa.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

He whakamārama

Te Pātai Aromātai

He pēhea te āta hāpai i ngā tamariki kia taunekeneke ai, kia tūhura ai hoki rātou i tō rātou ake taiao, i te taiao whānui anō hoki?

He tino pakari ngā hononga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao, me te whānau ki te marae.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Mana Aotūroa.He tino maioha ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao ake, ki te taiao whānui anō hoki. Ka whakarato te whānau me ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki, kia tūhura ai, kia taunekeneke ai hoki rātou ki tō rātou taiao. Kei te mōhio pai ngā tamariki ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo. Ka āta whakamahia ngā wāhi tākaro katoa e ngā tamariki. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmomo kaupapa ki te kōhanga reo e pā tata atu ana ki a rātou. Hīkoi haere ai rātou i te taiao o te marae, ā, he haerenga pārekareka ēnei ki a rātou. I aua wā hīkoi, ka aroturuki ngā tamariki i te tupu o ngā rākau hua ki tētahi uru huarākau e pā tata atu ana. Ko ngā whai wāhitanga ki te rongo i te taiao – tae atu ki te rongo ā-ihu, ā-māngai hoki, me ōna tae, ōna kakano e hāpai ana ki te whakawero me te whakaongaonga i ngā whakaaro o ngā tamariki.

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki te whakamihi i ētahi atu tāngata, ā, e ākina ana ki te taunekeneke ki te tini o ngā mema o te whānau ka taetae mai ki te marae. Ka whakanui te whānau i te ‘mihi atu’ me te ‘mihi mai’. Kua waia ngā tamariki ki te tūmomo āhuatanga o te ao māori me te ao kikokiko, nā tō rātou taiao ake, nā te taiao whānui hoki.

Ngā hononga me ngā taunekeneke.Ko ngā mihi a ngā tamariki ka tino whakamihi mārikatia e te whānau whānui i te marae. Ka whakanui ngā tamariki i ngā ākonga i te kura. Ka arohaina, ka whakautengia hoki ngā kuia e ngā tamariki. I te huihuinga tuatahi o te āta, ka tino whakamihia ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi katoa. He pakari ngā tamariki, ā, ka tino mōhio rātou ki tō rātou hononga nui ki te kōhanga reo nei, me te marae.

Te ako a ngā tamariki.Ka aro nui ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga. Ka whai aronga ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, ka āta whakamaheretia. Ka whakarite ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi, rauemi hoki e whakatutuki ana i ngā reanga me ngā āheinga o ngā tamariki. Ka akiaki rātou i ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu, te whakanui atu hoki i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga. Ka whakamihia ngā tamariki. Ka whakawhiti kōrero ngā tamariki mō te hauora me te haumaru, pērā i te mau pōtae, te noho ngātahi ki waho, te mau hū i a rātou hīkoi, me te tiaki i ngā tamariki tēina. Ko te haumaru me te waiora o ngā tamariki tētahi aronga matua.

Te reo Māori. He pai te whanake haere o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. Ko te reo Māori tētahi kaupapa matua ki te whānau. Ka tino tautoko ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki, hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o tō rātou reo. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā mahi ā-ringa, kia auaha ai, kia whai pūtake ai hoki ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, me ngā waiata, ki ngā tamariki. Tū harikoa ai ngā tamariki ki te taki i ō rātou pepeha, ō rātou whakapapa hoki. He maha ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki tuākana ki te taki i ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, me ngā tikanga. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero e whai pūtake ana, ki te taha o ngā tamariki. Ka tuituia ngā kupu hou e whai pānga ana ki te Mana Aotūroa, ki ngā taumahi.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

Te Pātai Aromātai

Ka pēhea te whai huatanga o te whānau ki te whakahaere, te whakarite, me te whakamahere mō te kōhanga reo mō meāke nei?

Ka whakahaerehia, ka whakaritea hoki te kōhanga reo ki te taumata teitei o te whai huatanga.

Ngāwāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te hanganga whakahaere hou.Kei te noho tonu ētahi o ngā mema o te whānau ki te tarahiti me te komiti o te marae o Hoani Waititi. E pakari haere ana te kōhanga reo ki te whakatau i ā rātou ake whakaritenga mō meāke nei. Kei te poipoi tonu rātou i ngā hononga papai ki ētahi atu ratonga i te marae.

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau.E whakapūmau ana te whānau ki te whakarato i te mātauranga o te kounga kairangi, me te āta whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga ki te kōhanga reo. Hui ai rātou i ia te wā, ā, ka āta whakawhitiwhiti whakaaro ki te whānau. E pakari haere ana ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga, me te whakamahere mō meāke nei. I tēnei wā, kei te whakawhiti kōrero rātou me te tarahiti o te marae, ki te whakatau i ngā take e pā ana ki te kirimini rīhi, me ōna tūmomo tikanga. He āheinga tō te whānau ki te whakatau i aua take, mā te tautoko hoki o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo.

Te arotake whaiaro.Pūrongotia ai ngā whakahaeretanga i te kōhanga reo, e te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ia te wā. He pakari ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te tiaki rawa, me te hauora, te haumaru hoki. Kei te whakapai tonu te whakahaere pūtea, i te whānau e ako ana ki te whakahaere i tēnei o ngā kawenga. Ka whai whakaaro nui te whānau ki te āta whakatau i ngā whakaritenga hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Te whakamahere mō ngā tau kei te heke mai.Ko ngā mahere ka whakawhanakehia e te whānau mō meāke nei, e whakarato ana i te tino aronga mārama, ā, ka tuitui hoki i ngā whāinga o Te Ara Tūāpae. Ko ētahi o ngā kaupapa matua, ko ngā wānanga, me te waihanga i tētahi papa tākaro taunekeneke mō ā rātou tamariki. Kua whakamaheretia hoki te reo Māori, te whakangungu kaimahi, me te whakapakari i te whakahaeretanga o te whānau i roto i ngā tau e toru ka whai ake. I tāutuhia e te whānau ko te reo Māori tētahi aronga mā ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau. He mātau te waru tekau ōrau - neke atu rānei - o te whānau ki te reo Māori. E wātea ana ki te whānau ētahi akoranga o Te Ātaarangi i ngā pō. E putaputa mai ana ētahi kupu hou, mai i te kura. He hiahia tō te whānau ki te noho mōhio ki ngā whakawhanaketanga o te wā.

Te whakamahere.He matawhānui ngā anga whakamahere, ā, kua āta tuhia hoki. Ka whai ngā mahere ā-tau, ā-wāhanga hoki i tētahi anga e whai pānga ana ki ngā atua Māori, Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira, ngā kaupeka o te tau, me te tautoko i ngā tūmomo kaupapa. Ka tuhi, ka whakaatu hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā whāinga, ngā taumahi, ngā pukapuka, ngā haerenga, ngā waiata, me ngā kupu hou mā te whānau. He pai te ārahi o ngā mahere i ngā kaimahi. Ka whakatakoto haere ia kaimahi i ngā kaupapa o ia rā, mā ā rātou ake rōpū reanga. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti kōrero mō ngā mahere, me te tautoko i tētahi ki tētahi. Ka whai pūtake ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, ka āta whakaritea anō hoki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Te aromatawai me te aromātai.E whai whakaaro ana ngā kaimahi ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo huarahi ki te aromatawai me te aromātai. Kua whakaritea e rātou ngā pūkete mā ia tamaiti. E whakaae ana ngā kaimahi, ka hāngai aua pūkete ki ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki, ā, ka hāngai pū ki ia tamaiti.

E whakarite ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakatakoto haere i tētahi kōwaiwai pakitara e whakatōpū ana i te Mana Aotūroa. E ai ki ngā kaimahi, ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te aromātai i te kaupapa. Ko te whakapiki ake i te whakamahinga o te reo a te tamaiti, ka whakatairanga ake anō i ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te aromatawai me te aromātai i te hōtaka.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakiia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia:

  • tūhura te whānau i ētahi atu rautaki anō e pā ana ki te aromatawai i ngā tamariki me te aromātai i te hōtaka
  • whakawhanake tonu te whānau i ō rātou pūkenga ki te whakahaere ā-whānau, hei whakapakari tonu ake i tō rātou āheinga.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

15 Paenga-whāwhā 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Hoani Waititi is located at the Hoani Waititi marae complex. This is the first review of the kōhanga reo since it became autonomous and separate from the Hoani Waititi Marae Trust and the Hoani Waititi committee. The whānau at the kōhanga reo are in a strong position to resolve the remaining issues. These pertain to the lease agreement and some conditions. Some whānau members remain on the marae trust and the committee. The whānau are growing in confidence to make independent decisions and to plan for the future.

The whānau chose Mana Aotūroa as the focus for this review. Their current priority has been the introduction of new children to the kōhanga reo environment and to develop their strong sense of wellbeing and security. The second priority is to maintain links with the wider whānau. Whānau recognise that te ao Māori, tikanga, whanaungatanga and te ao whānui underpin their understanding of Mana Aotūroa.

Children are settled and happy as they learn and explore. The older children model behaviours for their younger peers and support them well with routines. Kaimahi are prepared, familiar with their roles and responsibilities and quietly interact and support the children and each other well.

Spaces across the kōhanga reo are used effectively to support children as they learn and play. The expansive decks are particularly useful and well set up with resources which are accessible to children. There is an easy indoor outdoor flow. Children and kaimahi regularly interact with the neighbouring kōhanga reo where children have opportunities to play together.

Planning overviews and term plans are prepared and well written. Kaimahi recently attended training about Te Whāriki. This helped reinforce kaimahi knowledge of planning for specific topics. Kaimahi have weekly planning for their respective groups in their individual diaries. Assessment and evaluation ideas are discussed regularly during weekly hui. Further improvement in assessment and programme evaluation has been identified.

The whānau manage the kōhanga reo operations well. They are committed to providing high quality education for their children. There is clear communication with whānau regarding daily operations, the setting of goals and future planning. The whānau are currently discussing building an interactive playground.

The whānau are looking forward to the emerging developments they have for 2014.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Aotūroa.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Mana Aotūroa Background

Evaluative Question

How well are children supported to interact with and explore the immediate and local environment?

Children have strong connections to their environment and the whānau at the marae.

Areas of good performance

Mana Aotūroa.Children have a sound appreciation of their immediate and wider environment. The whānau and kaimahi provide a range of opportunities for children to explore and interact with their environment. Children are familiar with the kōhanga reo surroundings. All the play spaces are used well by children. Children engage in regular activity with the kōhanga reo next door. They enjoy regular walks around the marae complex. During these walks children monitor and discuss the growth of the fruit trees at a nearby orchard. Opportunities to observe nature including the smells, colours, textures and tastes help challenge and stimulate children’s thinking.

Children know how to greet people and are encouraged to interact with the various whānau members that frequent the marae complex. Whānau reinforce ‘mihi mai’ ‘mihi atu’. Children are familiar with different aspects of the natural and physical worlds within the immediate and wider environment.

Relationships and interactions.Children’s greetings are reciprocated heartily by the wider whānau on the marae. Children acknowledge the students at the kura. The nannies are loved and respected by the children. During the initial gathering in the morning, children are greeted warmly by all kaimahi. Children are confident and know they belong to this kōhanga reo and are important members of the marae.

Children’s learning.Children are receptive to learning.Children’s learning is focused and deliberately planned. Kaimahi prepare a range of activities and resources that cater for children’s ages and abilities. They encourage children to participate and follow their interests. Children are praised. Children discuss health and safety such as wearing hats, keeping together outside, wearing shoes during walks and looking after the younger children. The safety and wellbeing of children is a priority.

Te Reo Māori. Children’s te reo Māori is developing well. Te reo Māori is a priority for the whānau. Kaimahi provide valuable support to assist children’s language development. They use actions to make karakia, hīmene and waiata meaningful for children. Children happily volunteer to recite their pepeha and whakapapa. The older children are provided with several opportunities to lead karakia, hīmene and protocols. Kaimahi encourage purposeful conversation with children. New vocabulary associated with Mana Aotūroa is integrated into activities.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

Evaluative Question

How well do whānau manage, organise and plan for the future of the kōhanga reo?

The kōhanga reo is managed and organised to a high level of efficiency.

Areas of good performance

New management structure. Some whānau members remain on the Hoani Waititi Marae Trust and the Hoani Waititi marae committee. The kōhanga reo whānau are growing in confidence to make independent decisions for the future. They continue to foster good relationships with other agencies at the marae.

Whānau management.Whānau are committed to providing high quality education and manage the kōhanga reo operations well. They meet regularly and there is open communication with the whānau. Whānau members are becoming more confident in making decisions and future planning. They are currently negotiating with the marae trust to finalise matters that relate to the lease agreement and its conditions. The whānau are capable of resolving these issues with support from Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust.

Self review.Whānau and kaimahi regularly report on kōhanga reo operations. There are robust policy and procedures for property maintenance and health and safety. Financial management continues to improve as whānau learn how to manage this responsibility. The whānau are conscientious about making sound decisions to support the interests of their children.

Future planning.Future plans developed by the whānau provide clear direction and incorporate Te Ara Tūāpae goals. Some priorities include wānanga and creating an interactive playground for their children. Te reo Māori, kaimahi training and strengthening whānau management over the next three year period is also planned. The whānau identified te reo Māori as a focus for kaimahi and whānau members. At least eighty per cent of whānau are competent speakers of te reo Māori. Te Ātaarangi night classes are available to the whānau. The whānau want to be kept up to date with current developments.

Programme Planning.Planning frameworks are comprehensive and well written. Annual and term plans follow a template that includes atua Māori, taumata whakahirahira, the seasons and supporting kaupapa. Kaimahi document and display their plans for the whānau. Planning guides kaimahi well. Each kaimahi records the daily activities for their specific age groups. Kaimahi meet regularly to discuss planning and to support each other. Children’s learning is purposeful and well co-ordinated.

Areas for improvement

Assessment and evaluation.Kaimahi are considering a range of assessment and evaluation ideas. They have prepared portfolio folders for each child. Kaimahi agree these portfolios will reflect children’s interests and development and will be uniquely individual.

Kaimahi plan to compile a mural to summarise Mana Aotūroa. Kaimahi acknowledge children will be given a voice in evaluating the kaupapa. Increasing the use of the child’s voice will add value to the assessment and programme evaluation processes.

  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends:

  • the whānau explore further techniques for assessment of children and programme evaluation
  • the whānau continue to develop their whānau management skills to build their capability and capacity.
