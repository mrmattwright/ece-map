Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Manu Kōrero ki Ōhākune. He tau, he harikoa, he māia hoki ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo. E tino tautokona ana te ako a ngā tamariki. E whakawhanakehia ana e ngā tamariki tā rātou whakamahinga i te reo Māori.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Manu Korero i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Manu Kōrero ki te marae o Maungārongo, ki Ōhākune, ki raro i te maunga o Ruapehu. Kua āta kapohia ake te reo Māori, ngā tikanga, ngā kawa, me ngā whakapono o Ngāti Rangi ki tēnei kōhanga reo noho marae. I ngā wā katoa, e tautoko ana ngā kuia me ngā kaumātua o te hapori nei, ā, ka whai wāhi nui rātou ki te kōhanga reo. He kaha hoki ngā hononga ki Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Ngāti Rangi, me te Whare Hauora o Ngāti Rangi.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou māia hei ākonga ki te kōhanga reo?

Ka whakaatu ngā tamariki i tō rātou māia hei ākonga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka kite i te tūmāia o ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki. E hāpai ana ngā karakia, ngā waiata, ngā pepehā me ngā whakapapa i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki ngā whakatakotoranga reo, me ngā tūmomo kōrero ka pupū ake ki ngā momo horopaki. Ko ētahi atu horopaki ako, ko te Hui Aranga me ngā haerenga, ā, ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo Māori e whakamahia ana ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki reo e whai hua ana, hei hāpai i te ako i te reo. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakawhānui i tō rātou mōhiotanga, tā rātou whakamahinga anō hoki i te reo Māori. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā wheako ako reo e whakaratohia ana.

Kei te tino mōhio ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ko ngā wā ako ngātahi ki te taha o te whānau e hāpai ana i te whakahonohono o ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo. He pārekareka ki ngā tuākana, te whakatauira haere mā ngā tēina ō rātou mōhiotanga ki ngā tikanga me te whai pānga nui o ngā tikanga o ia rā ki ngā akoranga. E hāpai ana ngā kaupapa ako i te whakahonotanga atu o ngā tamariki ki ngā atua Māori. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whakatau i ā rātou ake whiringa ako i a rātou e tūhura ana i tō rātou taiao ako.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga. He whai wāhitanga mō rātou ki te tākaro ngātahi, te tākaro takitahi, me te whai wāhi atu hoki ki ngā kaupapa e kōkirihia ana e ngā pākeke. Kua whakaritea tētahi wāhi motuhake mō ngā tamariki tēina i ngā wā e tika ana, ā, he rūma anō tō ngā tamariki tuākana mō ā rātou kaupapa e āta whakawero ake ana i a rātou tonu. Kua āta whakaritea ngā mahinga. He tau ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ko ngā tamariki me te whānau kei te pūtake o te kōhanga reo. Tū ai ngā hui i ia te wā, hei aroturuki i te āhua o ngā mahi whānui ki te kōhanga reo. Ka āta tuhia ngā whakataunga a te whānau. Kua whakaritea ngā tukanga arotake mārama, ā, ka whakamahia ēnei hei hāpai i te ū tonu o te whānau ki te arotake whaiaro pai. Ko ngā painga mō ngā tamariki me ngā wawata o te whānau e whakatakotohia ana ki ngā mahere mō meāke nei.

Ka āta whakamaheretia ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, ka whai pānga. Ka whakarite ngā kaimahi i ngā taumahi, tae atu ki ngā kēmu me ngā taumahi e hāpai ana i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori. Ka hāngai ngā mahere ki ngā kaupapa e hono ana ki Te Whāriki. Ko te taiao o tēnei hapori – pērā i ngā maunga, ngā awa, me te hukarere – e whakatūturu ana i te hononga o ngā tamariki. Ko te noho marae o te kōhanga reo e whakatairanga ana i te taiao ako me ngā mahi ki te hōtaka. Ka whai pūtake ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Ka tāutuhia, ka whakapuakihia hoki te ako a ia tamaiti, ki te taha o te whānau. He pūkete tā ia tamaiti, ā, e whakanui ana ēnei i tā rātou ako ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakamahia ngā mahi toi a ngā tamariki hei whakapaipai i te wāhi ako. He manawa whakahī tō ngā tamariki mō ā rātou mahi, ā, ka taea e rātou te whakapuaki ngā hononga ki ngā kaupapa o te wā.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E mōhio ana te whānau, me whakapakari ake i te arohaehae a ngā kaimahi.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Manu Kōrero ki Ōhākune. He tau, he harikoa, he māia hoki ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo. E tino tautokona ana te ako a ngā tamariki. E whakawhanakehia ana e ngā tamariki tā rātou whakamahinga i te reo Māori.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Manu Kōrero is in Ōhākune.  Children are settled, happy and confident in their kōhanga reo environment. Children are well-supported in their learning. They are developing in their use of te reo Māori.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Manu Korero will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Manu Kōrero is situated at Maungarongo Marae in Ōhakune, beneath Maunga Ruapehu. Te reo Māori, culture, customs and beliefs of Ngāti Rangi are captured well within this marae based kōhanga. There is ongoing support from local kuia and kaumātua who play a significant role in the kōhanga reo. There are strong relationships with Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Ngāti Rangi and Ngāti Rangi Community Health Centre.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children show that they are confident learners at the kōhanga reo?

Children show that they are confident learners.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children show confidence to use te reo Māori for a variety of purposes. Karakia, waiata, pepeha and whakapapa help children to develop their knowledge of language patterns and make sense of what to say in different situations. Other learning contexts including Hui Aranga and haerenga expose children to te reo Māori used for a range of situations. Kaimahi use effective language strategies that support language learning. There are opportunities for whānau members to extend their knowledge and use of te reo Māori. Children enjoy the language learning experiences provided.

Children know their kōhanga reo environment well. Shared learning times with whānau help children make connections to their kōhanga reo environment. Tuakana enjoy modelling to teina what they know about tīkanga and the importance of these daily protocols for learning. Kaupapa learning supports children to make connections to ngā atua Māori. Children enjoy making their own learning choices as they explore their learning environment.

Children enjoy a range of learning experiences. They have opportunities to play cooperatively, independently and in adult led activities. Younger children have a defined safe space when required and older children have a separate room to pursue more challenging activities. Routines are well established. Children are settled in their kōhanga reo environment.

Planning and Evaluation

Children and whānau are the focus of the kōhanga reo. Regular hui are held to monitor the overall performance of the kōhanga reo. Whānau decision making is well documented. Clear review processes are in place and used to help whānau maintain good internal review. Children’s interests and whanau aspirations inform future planning.

Children’s learning is well planned and relevant. Kaimahi prepare activities including games and activities which support the development of te reo Māori. Planning reflects kaupapa that link to Te Whāriki. Local contexts such as maunga, awa and hukarere provide a personalised connection for children. Being marae-based enhances the learning environment and programme practices. Children have relevant learning.

Children’s individual learning is defined and shared with whānau. Each child has an observation book to celebrate their learning at kōhanga reo. Examples of children’s artwork are used to decorate the learning environment. Children are proud of their work and can articulate the links to the current kaupapa.

Key Next Steps

Whānau acknowledge that there is a need to strengthen appraisal for kaimahi.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Manu Kōrero is in Ōhākune. Children are settled, happy and confident in their kōhanga reo environment. Children are well-supported in their learning. They are developing in their use of te reo Māori.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Manu Korero will be in three years.
