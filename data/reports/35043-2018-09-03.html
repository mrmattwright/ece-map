1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangimārie ki te hapori o Melville, ki Kirikiriroa. Kua mahi te whānau ki te taha o te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo me Tainui Waikato ki te whakarato i tētahi wāhi hou e mau ana i ngā rauhanga tika mō te kōhanga reo. Kua roa nei ngā kaimahi e whai wāhi mai ana ki te kōhanga reo nei.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i ō rātou ngākau titikaha ki te ako?

Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki ō rātou ngākau titikaha ki te ako, mā tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki ngā wheako ako ōkawa me ngā wheako ako ōpaki.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He wairua ngāwari tō te taiao e nohoia ana e ngā tamariki, hei hāpai i tā rātou ako i te reo Māori. Ka kōrero ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā tūmomo rauemi me ngā tūmomo rautaki ki te whakapakari me te hāpai i te ako reo a ngā tamariki. Ko ētahi o aua tū āhuatanga, ko te patapatai, te whāngai kupu, me te tāruarua kōrero. Ka kite hoki i te reo Māori ki ngā tūmomo whakaaturanga, puta noa i te kōhanga reo. E whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi ako reo e whakawero ana i ō rātou whakaaro. Ahakoa ka kōrero ngā tamariki i te reo Pākehā i te nuinga o te wā, ka kite anō hoki mā tō rātou reo ā-tinana, mā ā rātou whakautu kōrero, tō rātou māramatanga ki te reo Māori. E whanake ana ngā tamariki i tō rātou māramatanga ki te reo Māori.

Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga e whakatairanga ana i tō rātou tuakiri. He hononga ā-whakapapa tō te nuinga o ngā tamariki ki a Waikato Tainui. Whakanuia ai Te Kīngitanga e te whānau i ia te wā. Ka kawe atu ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmomo kaupapa, wāhi whakahirahira anō hoki, pērā i ngā poukai me te Koroneihana, tae atu hoki ki a Taupiri maunga, ki te awa o Waikato, ā, ki ngā marae anō hoki o te takiwā. Nā tēnei tūāhuatanga, e whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki ki te taunekeneke tūturu nei ki ō rātou tūrangawaewae. Ka āta whakarite ngā kaimahi kia riro i ngā tamariki ngā whai wāhitanga i ia te rā ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou pepeha me ā rātou mihimihi ki te taha o ētahi atu. Kei te mārama haere ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hononga tangata me ō rātou tūrangawaewae.

E rongo ana ngā tamariki i te whakawhanaungatanga. Ka ahu mai ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki i te aroha me te manaakitanga. Ko te waiora o ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā whakaritenga a ngā kaimahi. Ka whakatauira rātou i ngā hononga whakaute me te ārahitanga papai. He ngāwari te reo kōrero o ngā kaimahi, ā, ka whakamihi i ngā tamariki mā ō rātou ringa awhi. Ka poipoi rātou i te mārie me te mahi ngātahi i roto i te tū, i ngā whakaaro, ā, i te noho tahi anō hoki. He mauritau ngā tamariki ki te whakatata atu me te whakawhanaunga ki te tangata hou. Ka kitea te manaaki o ngā tuākana i ngā tēina. E hāpaitia ana te āta whai wāhi nui o ngā pēpi ki ngā taumahi katoa. Ka ārahi ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki nohinohi, puta noa i te rā. Tohatoha ai ngā tamariki i ngā taonga i ia te wā, ā, he pai tō rātou noho tahi i te nuinga o te wā. He whakaute tō ngā hononga o ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu.

He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura haere i ō rātou taiao. Ka āta tiakina te wāhi wāteatea o waho e āta whakawero ā-tinana ai i ngā tamariki. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whiriwhiri i ā rātou taumahi me te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi, taumahi pārekareka anō hoki, ā, he poto noa te wā ki ia taumahi kia ū tonu ai te noho tākare tonu o ngā tamariki. Ka aro rātou ki ngā matea o ngā tamariki. Ka kawe atu ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā tamariki i ngā tū haerenga e whakahonohono tūturu ai i ngā tamariki ki te ao e noho nei rātou. E whai wāhi atu ana aua mahi ki ngā wheako ako e whakawhānui ana i ngā whakaaro o ngā tamariki ki tua atu i te kōhanga reo. Kei te harikoa, kei te mātātoa hoki ngā tamariki ki te ako.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ko te ako a ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te whakamahere me te aromatawai. Ka toro atu ngā kaimahi ki ngā whakaaro o ngā whānau, kia whai wāhi mai ai aua whakaaro ki ngā mahere. Ka āta whakarite rātou kia ārahi ai ngā mahere i te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka āta kitea ki ngā aromatawai a ngā tamariki, ko ngā tū akoranga e whai pānga nui ana ki ngā tamariki. He pai te whakamōhio i te whānau ki tēnā e ākona ana e ā rātou tamariki, me te āhua anō hoki o tā rātou ahu whakamua.

E whakahaere ana te whānau i ētahi wāhanga o te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki. E whakahaere ana te kaiwhakahaere i ngā pūnaha whakahaere o te tari. Ka tahuri ia ki te mahere ā-tau hei ārahi i ngā whakahaeretanga o ia rā. He mātātoa anō hoki te tautoko o te kaiwhakahaere i ngā kaimahi i roto i ā rātou mahi. Ko Te Ara Tūāpae te angamahi e whakamahia ana e te whānau i roto i ā rātou mahi ki te whakamahere rautaki. Ka āta tāutu taua angamahi i te tirohanga me ngā whāinga, ngā kaupapa matua hoki a te whānau mō te paetawhiti. Nō nā tata tonu nei te tūtohinga i arotakengia ai. He rawaka ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga o te hauora me te haumaru, hei ārahi i te hauora me te haumaru tonu o ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He whāiti noa te whai wāhi mai o te whānau. Te āhua nei, kāhore anō kia tū tētahi hui ā-whānau i te tau 2018 nei. E whakawhāiti ana taua tūāhuatanga i ngā tukanga a te whānau ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga, ā, e pā tōraro ana anō hoki ki te aromātai o roto. I tēnei wā, kāhore i te rawaka te aromātai o roto. Kāhore he pūrongo, kāhore rānei he mahi e aroturuki ana i ngā āhuatanga matua o ngā whakahaeretanga. Ahakoa kua whakatutukihia ētahi arotake, kāhore anō te whānau kia whakamana i ngā whakarerekētanga. Me hāngai ngā kaupapa here o te hauora me te haumaru ki Te Ture Oranga Tamariki 2014. Kāhore anō taua mahi kia whakatutukihia. Hei hāpai i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki, he mea nui kia toro atu te whānau ki ngā rautaki ka whakatairanga ake i te whai wāhi mai o ngā mema o te whānau.

Kei te haere tonu tētahi take e pā ana ki te atawhai o ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi. Kāhore i te rawaka te kaupare mātao hei āta whakarite i te noho mahana, i te hā ora hoki o te taiao ako puta noa i te rā, ā, he āhuatanga tēnei hei āta whakatika. He āwangawanga ā te whānau me Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga mō te iti noa o ngā kaupare e puritia ana te mahana ki roto i te whare. E rua ngā papu mahana e whakamahia ana puta noa i te rā, engari kāhore tonu te pā mahana e eke ana ki te 16 tohurau, hei whakatutuki i ngā taumata itinga mō te hauora me te haumaru. Kei te pā tōraro tēnei tū āhuatanga ki te hauora me te waiora o ngā tamariki.

Me whakapakari ake i te aromātai hōtaka. Ahakoa ka whakawhiti kōrero ngā kaimahi mō tēnā e mahia ana ki te hōtaka, he uaua ki te aroturuki i ngā āhuatanga i panoni ai kia whakatutukihia ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki katoa. He mea nui, kia tuhi haere ngā kaimahi i ngā tū rerekētanga ki te hōtaka, me te pūtake i mahi pērā ai. Kei te pā tōraro tēnei ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture. Kia tutukihia e te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā herenga ā-ture, me tahuri rātou ki te:

  • āta whakarite i te whakatutukitanga o ngā mahi katoa e whakatairanga ake ai i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki i whakaurua ai ki te ratonga nei.
    [R46 Ngā Ture Mātauranga (Ratonga Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS24 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei:

  • kia mārama te whānau ki ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga mahi e whai pānga ana ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo
  • kia mahi te whānau ki te taha o te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā wāhanga e whai pānga ana ki ngā herenga ā-ture
  • kia whakapakarihia te aromātai hōtaka.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangimārie i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangimārie is in Melville, Hamilton. Whānau have been working alongside the Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust and Tainui Waikato to provide a new site with suitable facilities for the kōhanga reo. Existing kaimahi have been at the kōhanga reo for a long period of time.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children show they are confident learners?

Children show they are confident learners through their participation in both formal and informal learning experiences.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children learn te reo Māori in a non-threatening environment. Kaimahi are speakers of te reo Māori. They use different resources and strategies to build and support children’s language learning. These include questions, prompts and repetition. Te reo Māori is visible in a variety of displays around the kōhanga reo. Children engage in language learning activities that challenge their thinking. While children speak English most of the time, their body language and responses reflect their understanding of te reo Māori. Children are developing an understanding of te reo Māori.

Children participate in learning where their identity is promoted. Most children have whakapapa links to Waikato Tainui. The whānau regularly acknowledge Te Kīngitanga. Kaimahi take the children to significant events and places, such as poukai, Koroneihana, Taupiri maunga, the Waikato river and the local marae. This enables children to physically interact with where they come from. Kaimahi ensure children have daily opportunities to share their pepeha and mihimihi with others. Children are coming to understand who they belong to and where they come from.

Children experience positive relationships. Interactions between kaimahi and children are based on aroha and manaakitanga. Children’s well-being is at the forefront of kaimahi practices. They model respectful relationships and positive guidance. Kaimahi speak softly and welcome children with hugs and cuddles. They foster calm and cooperative ways of acting, thinking and being with others. Children feel safe to approach and engage with new people. Tuakana demonstrate care for the teina. Pēpi are supported to confidently join in all activities. Kaimahi guide younger children throughout the day. Children often share taonga and generally get along very well together. Children have respectful relationships with others.

Children have opportunities to explore their environments. A well-kept, spacious outdoor area enables opportunities for children to be challenged physically. Children have opportunities to choose their activities and follow their interests. Kaimahi provide a range of resources and fun activities that are kept short to maintain children’s enthusiasm. They are responsive to children’s needs. Kaimahi and whānau take the children on outings to make relevant connections with the world around them. This contributes to learning experiences that extends children’s thinking beyond the kōhanga reo. Children are happy, active learners.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s learning is at the heart of planning and assessment practices. Kaimahi seek whānau input into planning. They ensure that the planning guides the daily programme. Children’s assessment clearly show what learning is important to the children. Whānau are well informed of what their children are learning and how well they are progressing.

The whānau manage parts of the kōhanga reo in the best interest of their children. The kaiwhakahaere manages the administration systems. She refers to the annual plan to guide day to day operations. The kaiwhakahaere also actively supports kaimahi in their work. Te Ara Tūāpae is the framework whānau use for strategic planning. This clearly identifies the vision and long-term goals and priorities for the whānau. The charter has been recently reviewed. Health and safety policies and procedures are sufficient to guide the health and safety of children.

Key Next Steps

Whānau participation is limited. It appears that there has been no whānau hui for 2018. This restricts whānau decision making processes and inhibits internal evaluation. Currently internal evaluation is insufficient. There is no reporting or monitoring of key aspects of operations. While some reviews have been carried out, the whānau have not ratified any changes. Health and safety polices must align to the Vulnerable Children’s Act 2014. This still has not happened. In order to support practices for the wellbeing of the children, it is important that whānau seek strategies to raise participation of existing whānau members.

There is an ongoing concern relating to the care of children and kaimahi. The lack of adequate insulation to ensure that the learning environment is well heated and ventilated throughout the day needs to be addressed. Whānau and ERO have concerns about the lack of insulation to retain heat. Two continuously running heat pumps fail to raise the temperature above 16 degrees to meet minimum health and safety standards. Children’s physical health and wellbeing is compromised.

Programme evaluation requires strengthening. While kaimahi discuss what is happening in the programme, it is difficult to monitor what changes have been occurred to meet the learning needs of all children. It is important that kaimahi record any changes to the programme and why. Children’s learning is compromised.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

ERO identified areas of non-compliance. In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements they need to:

  • ensure that all reasonable steps are taken to promote the health and safety of the children enrolled in the service.
    [R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations; HS24 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendations

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • the whānau understand their roles and responsibilities to the kaupapa of kōhanga reo

  • the whānau work alongside the Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust to address area of non-compliance

  • Programme evaluation is strengthened.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangimārie will be in three years.
