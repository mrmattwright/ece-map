Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo ki Tāmaki Rāwhiti i Ōtara. Ahakoa he tokoiti ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo, ka kitea tō rātou mauritau i roto i ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu. He ākonga pākiki, he ākonga harikoa hoki rātou. He ngoikore te whakahaeretanga a te whānau me tō rātou tautoko i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo ki Tāmaki Rāwhiti i roto ngā tau e rua.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo ki Tāmaki Rāwhiti ki te taha o te kura tuatahi o Tāmaki ki te Rāwhiti, i Ōtara, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau. E whakahaerehia ana te kōhanga reo e tētahi kuia me ngā kaiako e noho pūmau ana ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, ā, e whakapau kaha ana kia noho tuwhera tonu ai te kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou mauritau, tō rātou harikoa anō hoki hei ākonga?

Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tō rātou mauritau i roto i ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu. He ākonga pākiki, he ākonga harikoa hoki rātou.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao o te reo Māori. Nō te Tai Tokerau ngā kaiako, nō reira ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo ā-iwi o te Tai Tokerau. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā waiata, ngā haka, ngā pānui, me ngā mahi a Rēhia, hei whakawhānui atu i ō rātou mōhiotanga me tō rātou māramatanga ki te reo Māori. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā pūkenga whakarongo o ngā tamariki i roto i ngā taumahi me ngā kaupapa ako. Ka kōrero ngā tamariki i te reo Pākehā, engari he pai ō rātou māramatanga ki te reo Māori. E āhei ana rātou ki te whakautu i ngā pātai me ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki te taha o ngā kaiako. E whakawhanake ana te māia o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te atawhai me te manaaki ki ō rātou hononga i te kōhanga reo. Ka whai whakaaro nui rātou ki ētahi atu, ā, he māia ki te pātai i te wā e hiahia āwhina ana rātou. He pai ngā tamariki ki te mahi takitahi me te mahi ā-rōpū. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i te aroha, te awhi, me te manaaki. Ka whakamihi, ka whakakoia hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā whakamātau me ngā mahi angitu a ngā tamariki. Kotahi anake te pēpi i te kōhanga reo, ā, e manaakitia ana ia e te kuia. E wātea ana te pēpi ki te haereere i te kōhanga reo, ā, he maha ngā wā ka whai wāhi atu hoki ia ki ngā tūmomo taumahi ki te taha o ngā tuākana. He mauritau, he pakari hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana i te ako. He iti noa ngā rauemi i te kōhanga reo, engari he kaha tonu ngā kaiako ki te āta whakamahi i aua rauemi. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi, ā, ka patapatai ngā kaiako hei whakawhānui ake i ngā whakaaro. Ka arotahi ngā tamariki mō ngā wā roa. He maha ngā wā e āhei ana rātou ki te whiriwhiri i ā rātou ake rauemi hei whakamahinga. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura me te mahi auaha. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki tētahi hōtaka e mau nei i ngā taumahi me ngā mahinga riterite, e āta mōhiotia ana. He ākonga harikoa, he ākonga tūmāia hoki ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

E whai hua ana ngā tamariki nā te hōtaka e āta whakamaheretia ana. He mārama ngā mahere, ā, ka hāngai ki tēnei e whakaakona ana, ngā rautaki e whakamahia ana, me ngā rauemi e tika ana. He pai te whakaritenga me te whakahaeretanga a ngā kaiako. Ka whai wāhi nui ngā tamariki, ā, he harikoa rātou.

E whai hua ana te ārahi i te hōtaka. Ka whakapau kaha ngā kaimahi me te heamana kia noho tuwhera tonu ai te kōhanga reo, hei whakarato anō hoki i te kounga o te atawhai ki ngā tamariki, i te kounga anō hoki o ngā akoranga mā rātou. Ka ū ngā kaiako ki ngā mahinga me ngā tikanga ahakoa te tokoiti o ngā tamariki me ngā whānau. He tākare, he ngana hoki ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Me whakapakari ake i te aromātai hōtaka me te aromatawai i ngā tamariki. Ahakoa kua whakaritea ētahi tukanga, he uaua te aroturuki i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga me te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki. Ko ngā aromatawai, he tuhinga o ia rā o tēnā i mahia ai e ngā tamariki, ehara kē i tēnā i ako ai rātou. Kei ngā pūkete a ia tamaiti āna tauira mahi, ā, e hāngai ana ēnei ki ngā kaupapa i whakamaheretia ai.

He whāiti ngā mahere me te aromātai o roto. Kāhore anō te whānau kia whakaoti i tā rātou mahere rautaki, kāhore anō hoki kia tāutuhia e rātou ā rātou kaupapa matua mō te paetawhiti. Ko te whakapiki ake i te tatauranga tamariki ki te kōhanga reo tētahi aronga nui mō te ahu nahanaha a te whānau. Me whakapakari ake i te aromātai o roto. Ahakoa ka pūrongo i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te hōtaka akoranga me te whakahaere tari, he poto noa. Me hōhonu ake te pūrongo i ngā āhuatanga matua o ngā whakahaeretanga. Kāhore anō ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga kia kōrerohia e te whānau.

Kāhore ngā pūnaha whakahaere tari e āta whakaritea ana. Kua roa nei te whānau e whakahaere ana, engari kāhore he kaiwhakahaere. He ngoikore te whakariterite i ngā tuhinga, tae atu ki ngā mōhiohio matua e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi me te whakahaere tari. Ka whakatutuki ngā kaimahi i ngā mahi whakahaere tari, i runga anō i ā rātou mahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki. Kāhore anō kia whakawhanakehia ētahi o ngā kaupapa here e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru, tae atu ki te whāngai rongoā me te tāutu i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea i ngā haerenga.

Kāhore anō kia oti ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi. Kua arowhaingia ngā kaimahi katoa e ngā pirihimana. Kāhore ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi e hāngai ana ki te wā. Kāhore he kirimana mahi ā ngā kaimahi. Nō nā tata tonu nei ngā arohaehae i whakaotia ai, engari kāhore anō ngā whāinga a ngā kaimahi kia tuhia. Kāhore anō ngā kaimahi kia whai wāhi atu ki te whakawhanaketanga ngaio mō tētahi wā roa. He whāiti noa ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā kaimahi ki te whakapai ake i ō rātou pūkenga me ā rātou whakaritenga. 

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te ratonga ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakawhanakehia tētahi mahere rautaki mō te paetawhiti
  • whakapai ake i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aromātai o roto
  • whakapakarihia te tautoko ngaio mō ngā kaimahi
  • toro atu ki te tautoko o te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo me te purapura, ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo ki Tāmaki Rāwhiti i Ōtara. Ahakoa he tokoiti ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo, ka kitea tō rātou mauritau i roto i ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu. He ākonga pākiki, he ākonga harikoa hoki rātou. He ngoikore te whakahaeretanga a te whānau me tō rātou tautoko i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā ki te kōhanga reo.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo ki Tāmaki Rāwhiti i roto ngā tau e rua.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ki Tāmaki Rāwhiti is in Otara. While the roll is low, children show they are settled and secure in their relationships with others. They are inquisitive and happy learners. Whānau management and support for the day to day operation of the kōhanga reo is poor.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo ki Tāmaki Rāwhiti will be within two years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ki Tāmaki Rāwhiti is beside East Tamaki School, in Otara, Auckland. The kōhanga reo is managed by a kuia and kaiako who are committed to the kaupapa of te kōhanga reo and continue to do all they can to keep the kōhanga reo open.

2 The Review Findings

To what extent do children show that they are settled and happy learners?

Children show they are settled and secure in their relationships with others. They are inquisitive and happy learners.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are immersed in a te reo Māori environment. The kaiako are from Te Taitokerau, therefore children are exposed to the dialect of the northern tribes. Children participate in waiata, haka, reading and ngā mahi a Rehia to extend their knowledge and understanding of te reo Māori. Kaimahi support children’s listening skills during activities and kaupapa ako. Children speak English but have a good understanding of te reo Māori. They are able to respond to questions and conversations with kaiako. Children are developing confidence to speak te reo Māori.

Children experience warm and caring relationships at kōhanga reo. They are considerate of others and are confident to ask for help when they need it. Children work well on their own and in groups. Kaimahi model aroha, awhi and manaaki. Kaimahi praise and affirm children’s efforts and successes. There is only one baby on the roll who is cared for by the kuia. The baby moves freely around the kōhanga reo and often joins activities with the tuakana. Children are settled and secure at kōhanga reo.

Children participate in an environment that fosters learning. The kōhanga reo has very few resources but the kaiako use what they have effectively. Children engage in activities where kaiako ask questions to extend thinking. Children are focussed for long periods of time. They are often able to work with resources of their choice. Kaimahi provide opportunities for children to explore and to be creative. Children enjoy a programme where activities and routines are consistent and familiar. Children are happy and confident learners.

Planning and Evaluation

Children benefit from a programme that is well planned. Planning is clear and reflects what is being taught, what strategies are being used and what resources are required. The programme is well organised and managed by the kaiako. Children are engaged and happy.

Leadership in the programme is positive. The kaimahi and chairperson work hard to keep the kōhanga reo open and provide quality care and learning for the children. Kaiako maintain routines and tikanga despite low numbers of children and whānau. Children are enthusiastic and attentive.

Key Next Steps

Programme evaluation and children’s assessment requires strengthening. While there are processes in place it is difficult to monitor the effectiveness of the learning programme and children’s progress. Assessments are daily notes of what the children have done, rather than what they have learned. Individual profiles contain samples of children’s work aligned to the planned kaupapa.

Planning and internal evaluation is limited. The whānau have not completed their strategic plan or identified their long term priorities. Increasing the roll is an important focus for the strategic direction of the whānau. Internal evaluation requires strengthening. While there is reporting about the learning programme and administration these are brief. Reporting wider on key areas of operations of required. Policies and procedures have not been discussed by the whānau.

Administrative systems are not well organised. The whānau have operated without a kaiwhakahaere for a long time. Document are poorly organised including important personnel and administration information. Kaimahi complete administrative tasks in addition to working with the children. Some health and safety policies including administering medicines and risk management on trips have not been developed.

Personnel documentation is incomplete. All staff have been police vetted. Job descriptions are out of date. There are no employment contracts for kaimahi. Appraisals have been completed recently but kaimahi goals are not recorded. Kaimahi have not participated in any professional development for some time. Kaimahi have limited opportunities to improve their skills and practice.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendations

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations to:

  • develop a long term strategic plan

  • improve internal evaluation practices

  • strengthen professional support for kaimahi

  • seek support from the TKRNT and the purapura to address the issues identified in this report.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ki Tāmaki Rāwhiti is in Otara. While the roll is low, children show they are settled and secure in their relationships with others. They are inquisitive and happy learners. Whānau management and support for the day to day operation of the kōhanga reo is poor.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo ki Tāmaki Rāwhiti will be within two years.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori
