2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana te Kōhanga Reo o Ruātoki i runga i te whenua o te kura ki Ruātoki. E tū tata ana te hapori iti o Ruātoki ki te tāone o Whakatāne. Kei roto i te kīanga kōrero e whai ake nei ko ngā tūmanakotanga o te whānau mō ā rātou tamariki, “Kia noho Māori tonu, kia noho Tūhoe tonu.” E kaha ū ana, ā, e kaha tautoko ana te whānau me te hapori i te kōhanga reo.

He reo māhorahora te āhua o te reo Māori e rere ana i te kōhanga reo. Ko te mātau o te katoa o ngā kaiako, ngā kaimahi me te tokomaha o ngā mātua o te whānau ki te reo Māori te take e whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori i te kōhanga reo, heoi anō i te kāinga me te hapori whānui hoki. Mā te noho tahi a te kuia me te koro i te kōhanga reo e whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki te whai wheako a ngā tūpuna me te mātauranga tuku iho i a rātou e ako ana tae noa ki ngā wheako whakaako.

Kua whakahaeretia pai te hōtaka ako a ngā tamariki, ā, kua āta whakaraupapatia ngā whakahaere. E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki te tīmata me te whai atu i ā rātou ake akoranga. He ākonga manawanui rātou i roto i tētahi taiao e tiaki ai ō rātou matea ā-tinana me ō rātou matea ā-hinengaro. Mā ngā tukanga whakahaere e pā ana ki te whakamāherehere me te aromatawai e tino mōhio ana te whānau he aha ngā akoranga o ā rātou tamariki me ngā tukanga e aroturuki ai ō rātou tipuranga. Me whai wāhi ināianei te whānau ki te whiriwhiri i ngā rautaki hei aromātai i te whaihua o te hōtaka akoranga.

Kua whakatūria e te whānau ngā tukanga hei whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā mō tō rātou kōhanga reo. E ora pai ana te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo. Kua tiakina pai ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā take a ngā kaimahi, ā, he tōtika ngā whakahaere e pā ana ki te whakahaere kaimahi. Mā ngā tukanga arotake ā-roto e tino mōhio ana te whānau i ngā take o te wā i te kōhanga reo. Me whakarite ināianei i ngā aromātai mō ngā kaimahi hei tāutu i ō rātou matea ngaio. Tēra anō tētahi matea nui, arā, te whakatū i tētahi māhere rautaki hei arahi i ngā tipuranga whakamua mō ngā rangi ā meāke nei o te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Mahi Ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o Te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ruātoki kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ruātoki.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko āna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Atua.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere i te māhere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • hōtaka whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Atua

He whakamārama

I kōwhiritia e te whānau te Mana Atua hei kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E ai ki ō rātou whakapono e noho piri tahi ana te Mana Atua ki te Mana Whenua, te Mana Tangata, te Mana Reo me te Mana Aotūroa, ā, e tūhono ana ngā kaupapa nei ki a rātou anō. E whakaatu ana ēnei whenu i ngā tūmanakotanga o te whānau mō ā rātou tamariki. Anō nei ko ngā kīanga kōrero e tautoko ai ngā tūmanakotanga, arā, “Kia noho Māori tonu, kia noho Tūhoe tonu” me te kīanga kōrero e whai nei, “Te matemate ao nei”. E arotake ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te whānui me te whai painga o te kōhanga reo ki te tiaki i te whaiora ā-wairua, te whaiora ā-hinengaro me te whaiora ā-pāpori o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Mana Atua.Kua whakahaeretia e te whānau tētahi taiao e tiaki nei te whaiora ā-hinengaro me te whaiora ā-wairua o ngā tamariki. Ka kitea ēnei putanga mā ngā tauira e whai ake nei:

  • He mea hauora te kai o ngā tamariki, ā, e taea ana e ngā tamariki te kai i te rā katoa. Mā reira, e tino rite ā-hinengaro, ā-tinana rānei ana ngā tamariki.
  • He mea pono te whanaungatanga a ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō me ngā pakeke. Kei te pakari ake ngā whanaungatanga i te mea he ōrite te whakapapa o ngā tamariki. He mea aroha tētahi ki tētahi, ā, he ngākau tauawhi te āhua o ngā taunekeneke i waenganui i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki.
  • Ko te reo Māori te kaupapa mātua. E rere ana te reo o Tūhoe ki ngā wāhi ako katoa tae noa ki ngā ngohe ako huri noa i te kōhanga reo. E tino mātau ana ngā kaimahi me te nuinga o ngā matua o te whānau ki te reo Māori. Ko te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero i ngā wā katoa i waenganui i ngā kaimahi te take kei te riro i ngā tamariki ngā tauira tōtika ki te reo Māori. He reo māhorahora te āhua o te reo Māori e rere ana i te kōhanga reo.
  • E tino tūwhera ana ngā ngākau o ngā tamariki ki te ako, arā, he ākonga hiahia ako ngā tamariki. Ko te whānui me te tini o ngā wheako te take e taea ai e ngā tamariki te mahi ki a rātou anō, te mahi rānei i tōna kōtahi. E manawareka ana ngā tamariki, ā, te kaha hoki o ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanaunga.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamāherehere me te aromātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo hei whakatau i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamāherehere, te whakahaeretanga me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga reo mā a rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka āta hāngai te aromātai nei ki te hōtaka whakamāherehere me te aroturuki i te tipuranga a ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Kua whakatūria e te whānau ngā tukanga whai painga hei whakahaere me te aroturuki i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā i te kōhanga reo. E whai ake nei ngā tauira whai painga, arā:

  • Tēra tētahi pūnaha whakahaere pūtea tōtika, ā, he whai tikanga ngā whakahaere. E ora rawa atu ana te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo
  • Kua āta tiakina ngā pūkete a ngā kaimahi, ā, kua tiakina pai ngā take e pā ana ki ngā take a ngā kaimahi. E mārama ana ngā kaimahi mō ā rātou tūranga me ō rātou haepapa ki te whānau, ngā tamariki tae noa ki ngā haepapa ki a rātou anō
  • E arotakengia ana ngā kaupapa here me ngā whakaritenga o ia rā i tēnei wā tonu. Kei te tōtika ngā kaupapa here e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru kei a rātou i tēnei wā e whai mana ai, ā, he aratohu whai tikanga ēnei tuhinga mā te whānau
  • He nui ngā pūrongo kua whakatakotoria i te hui ā-whānau. E hāngai ana ēnei pūrongo ki te whakahaere pūtea, te whare, te harihari tamariki, te kohikohi pūtea, ngā arotake e pā ana ki ngā kaupapa here, ngā pūrongo a ngā kaimahi me ngā pānui ā-whānau. E tino mōhio ana te whānau i ngā take o te wā i te kōhanga
  • Kua āta whakatūria te hōtaka whakamāherehere me ngā aromatawai a ngā tamariki. Kua tuhia pai ngā kaupapa ako, ā, ka kitea ēnei tuhituhi i roto i ngā hōtaka whakamāherehere me ngā aromatawai. E tino mōhio ana te whānau i ngā kaupapa e ako ana i ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te hōtaka aromātai.E hāngai ana ngā whakawhitiwhiti kōrero i ngā hui kaimahi ki ngā take me kōrerotia i ngā hui ā-whānau. Me hāngai ngā kōrero i ngā hui kaimahi ki te hōtaka akoranga me te tāutu i ngā wāhanga kāore e whai hua ana me te mahi anō hei whakarerekē kia pai ake ai te hōtaka ako mā ngā tamariki. Mā reira ka noho ko ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki ki te pūtake o ā rātou mahi.

Ngā aromātai a ngā kaimahi.Kāore anō ngā aromātai a ngā kaimahi kia oti. He kaupapa tino nui tēnei mā te whānau ki te whakatutuki. Mā tēnei e taea ai e ngā kaimahi te tāutu i ā rātou whāinga ngaio me te riro whakangungu hei tautoko i a rātou mahi ki ngā tamariki.

Te whakatakoto i te māhere rautaki.Me whakatū te whānau i tētahi māhere whāroa hei āwhina i a rātou ki te tāutu i ngā whāinga matua o te kōhanga reo. Mā reira e taea ai e te whānau te āta arotahi me te mahi tahi ki te whakatutuki i ngā whāinga matua. Mā te whakatū i tētahi māhere whakatutuki e taea ai e te whānau te whakatinana i ngā whāinga matua o te kōhanga reo me ngā wāhanga anō hei whakapai ake mō ngā rangi ā meāke nei.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

He whakamārama

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ruātoki tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga a te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

6 Ngā Tūtohunga

E tūtohu ana te Tari Arotake o te Mātauranga ki te whānau ki te rapu tautoko mai i te kaimahi ā-kaupapa ki te:

  • whakapakari ake i te hōtaka aromātai
  • whakahaere i ngā aromātai a ngā kaimahi
  • whakatū i te māhere rautaki e tāutu ai ngā whāinga matua o te kōhanga reo.

7 Ngā Mahi Ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ruātoki is on the grounds of the local kura in Ruātoki. The small community of Ruātoki is located near Whakatane. The aspirations the whānau have for their children are evident in the following phrase,“Kia noho Māori tonu, kia noho Tuhoe tonu.”The whānau and community are very supportive and committed to the kōhanga reo.

Te reo Māori is normalised within the kōhanga reo. The fluency of all kaiako, kaimahi and many of the whānau ensures the children are exposed to te reo Māori not only in kōhanga reo but in the home and wider community. The presence of nanny and koro in the kōhanga reo enables children to experience intergenerational learning and teaching.

The children’s learning programme is well organised and managed. Children have opportunities to initiate their own learning. They are active learners in an environment where they are respected and cared for both physically and intellectually. The planning and assessment process allows whānau to be informed of what their children are learning and how their progress is monitored. It is now time for kaimahi to consider ways to evaluate the effectiveness of the learning programme.

Whānau have established systems to manage the operations of their kōhanga reo. The kōhanga reo is financially viable. Personnel issues are well documented and managed. Internal review processes keep whānau well aware of what is happening in the kōhanga reo on a regular basis. Whānau now have to organise the appraisal of kaimahi in order to identify their professional development needs. There is a need to put in place a strategic plan to guide the future development of kōhanga reo.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

4 Findings

Mana Atua

Background

Whānau chose Mana Atua as a focus for this review. They believe Mana Atua sits alongside Mana Whenua, Mana Tangata, Mana Reo, Mana Aotūroa and all are connected. These strands reflect the aspirations whānau have for their children. These include“Ka noho Māori tonu, kia noho Tuhoe tonu”and“Te matemate ao nei”.ERO will review the extent to which the kōhanga reo nurtures children’s spiritual, intellectual and social wellbeing.

Areas of good performance

Mana Atua. Whānau provide a learning environment where children’s spiritual and intellectual wellbeing are nurtured. This is evident in the following ways:

  • Children’s meals are nutritional and food is available throughout the day. Children are physically and intellectually prepared for learning as a consequence of these practices
  • Children experience familiar relationships with one another and adults. These relationships are strengthened through common whakapapa. Interactions between kaimahi and children are loving and respectful.
  • Te reo Māori is a priority. Te reo o Tuhoe permeates all learning activities and teaching practices that occur in the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi and most whānau are fluent speakers of te reo Māori. Continuous conversations between kaimahi provide children with authentic language models. Te reo Māori is used in natural ways in the kōhanga reo
  • Children are active learners. A variety of learning experiences ensures that children have opportunities to engage with one another or work on their own. Children are curious and very sociable. Children are happy as they work and play.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent, to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

The whānau have effective systems to manage and monitor the operations of the kōhanga reo. This includes the following:

  • a financial system that is transparent and well managed. A budget for resourcing is allocated. The kōhanga reo is in a sound financial position
  • personnel matters are organised and records well maintained. Kaimahi are clear about their roles and responsibilities to the whānau, children and each other
  • policies and procedures are currently being reviewed. Existing health and safety policies are adequate and provide clear guidelines and practices for whānau to follow
  • a number of reports are tabled at whānau hui. These relate to financial management, the building, transport, fundraising, policy reviews, kaiako reports and newsletters. The whānau are well aware of what is happening at the kōhanga reo
  • programme planning and children’s assessments are well organised. Kaupapa ako are clearly written in programme plans and assessments. Whānau are made aware of what their children learn.

Areas for development

Programme evaluation. Discussions at kaimahi hui are based around issues that should be discussed at whānau hui. Kaimahi hui should be focussed on the learning programme in order to identify what has not worked for children in the programme and what would need to be changed. This will ensure that the learning needs of the children remain the focus of their work.

Kaimahi appraisals.Kaimahi appraisals have yet to be completed. This is an important step whānau need to take. This will assist kaimahi to identify their performance goals and receive appropriate professional development to support their practice with children.

Strategic planning. Whānau need to establish a long term plan to help identify kōhanga reo priorities. This will enable whānau to work toward major goals in a systematic manner. The development of an action plan will assist the whānau with the implementation of kōhanga reo priorities and ongoing improvements.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ruātoki Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff ofcompleted an EROKōhanga Whānau Assurance StatementandSelf-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

6 Recommendations

ERO recommends that the whānau seek support from the district kaupapa kaimahi to:

  • strengthen programme evaluation
  • implement kaimahi appraisals
  • establish a strategic plan that clearly identifies kōhanga reo priorities.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

Whānau have established systems to manage the operations of their kōhanga reo. The kōhanga reo is financially viable. Personnel issues are well documented and managed. Internal review processes keep whānau well aware of what is happening in the kōhanga reo on a regular basis. Whānau now have to organise the appraisal of kaimahi in order to identify their professional development needs. There is a need to put in place a strategic plan to guide the future development of kōhanga reo.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to student achievement and useful to this kōhanga reo.

If you would like a copy of the full report, please contact the kōhanga reo or see the ERO web page, http://www.ero.govt.nz.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
