Te Aromātai

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia whakatewhatewha i ngā taumahi ka whakawero ā-tinana, ā-hinengaro hoki i te tokomaha o ngā tama, kia whai wāhi nui ai hoki rātou. 

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Ōtara Marae ki Ōtara, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau ki te Tonga. Kei te marae o Ngāti Ōtara te kōhanga reo, e pā tata atu ana i Te Whare Takiura o Manukau. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whakatairanga ana i te whanaungatanga, mā te aroha, te tiaki, me te manaaki. Ka kitea te manaaki, te whai whakaaro nui hoki o ngā tamariki mō rātou anō, mō ētahi atu hoki.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Ōtara Marae ki te tāone o Ōtara, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau ki te Tonga. Kei te marae o Ngāti Ōtara te kōhanga reo, e pā tata atu ana ki Te Whare Takiura o Manukau. E whakahaerehia ana te kōhanga reo i tētahi whare e tino tiakina ana, i hangaia ai hei kōhanga reo, ā, he wāteatea ngā wāhi ako me ngā wāhi tākaro o roto, o waho hoki.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i te whanaungatanga?

Ka kitea te manaaki, te whai whakaaro nui hoki o ngā tamariki mō rātou anō, mō ētahi atu hoki.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira  

He pai ngā hononga o ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu. He atawhai, he manaakitanga hoki tō ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. Ka mihia ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau ki te kōhanga reo i ia rā. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i te aroha, te tiaki, me te manaaki. E ai ki te whānau, kei te ako hoki rātou ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki. Ka noho matua te atawhai i ngā tamariki me ō rātou ake matea.

He tau ngā tamariki, ā, e mōhio ana rātou ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga anō hoki. Kua āta whakaritea ngā mahinga me ngā kaupapa mahi o ia rā, ā, he pai te rere ngāwari o aua mahi. Ka kawe ngā tamariki i a rātou anō, ka tiaki hoki rātou i tō rātou wāhi.

Ka tūmāia ngā tamariki i roto i te hōtaka akoranga. Kua āta whakaritea ngā taumahi mā ngā tamariki. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako e hono ana ki ngā kaupapa mahi. Ka tiaki ngā kaimahi i tētahi rōpū puta noa i te rā, engari he maha tonu ngā whai wāhitanga a ngā tamariki ki te mahi ngātahi me te tākaro ngātahi. He nui ngā rauemi ki te kōhanga reo. He ātaahua, he wāteatea, he mā hoki te taiao akoranga, ā, ka āta tiakina.

Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo Māori e kōrerohia ana e ngā kaimahi, ā, he matatau ngā kaimahi ki te reo. Ka whai wāhi nui tētahi kuia ki te hōtaka o ia rā, ā, ka hāpai ia i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori mō ngā tamariki, ngā kaimahi, me te whānau. Ka whakamahi ngā tamariki i te reo kōrero me te reo ā-tinana ki te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero me te whakapuaki whakaaro. E tupu ana te āheinga o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, ā, e tino tautokona ana rātou e ngā kaimahi me te whānau.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

Hui ai ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti kōrero mō te hōtaka me ngā mahere. Ka whakaaro huritao rātou ki te hōtaka, ā, ka tāutu i ngā taumahi ka ngākau nuitia pea e ngā tamariki. Ka whai wāhi te nuinga o ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga e whai pūtake ana ki a rātou.

He pai te whakahaeretanga o ngā mahi ki te aromatawai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā pūkete me ngā pūrongo whakawhanaketanga hei tāutu i tēnā e ākona ana e ngā tamariki. Ka kōrero rātou ki ngā mātua mō te ahu whakamua o ā rātou tamariki. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā ngā rautaki e aro nui ana ki ā rātou whakatutukitanga.

He mārama ngā tūmanako o te tūtohinga me ngā aratohu e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga me te whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo. He māramatanga ngātahi tō te whānau me ngā kaimahi mō ō rātou tūranga, me ā rātou kawenga mahi. Kua whakaritea ngā kaupapa here matua me ngā tukanga matua hoki e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. Ka whakamahia ngā pūtea hei whakatutuki i ngā matea o ngā tamariki. Ka whakahaerehia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore te whānuitanga o te hōtaka akoranga e āta whakawero ana, e āta whakahihiko ana hoki i ngā tama. Kua tāutuhia e ngā kaimahi, e tika ana kia whakatewhatewha i te whānuitanga o ngā rautaki whakaako ka hāpai ake i ngā tama me ā rātou akoranga.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia whakatewhatewha i ngā taumahi ka whakawero ā-tinana, ā-hinengaro hoki i te tokomaha o ngā tama, kia whai wāhi nui ai hoki rātou. 

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Ōtara Marae ki Ōtara, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau ki te Tonga. Kei te marae o Ngāti Ōtara te kōhanga reo, e pā tata atu ana i Te Whare Takiura o Manukau. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whakatairanga ana i te whanaungatanga, mā te aroha, te tiaki, me te manaaki. Ka kitea te manaaki, te whai whakaaro nui hoki o ngā tamariki mō rātou anō, mō ētahi atu hoki.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Ōtara Marae i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Ōtara is in Ōtara, South Auckland. The kōhanga reo is on the grounds of Ngāti Ōtara Marae and is close to Manukau Institute of Technology. Children experience an environment that promotes whanaungatanga through aroha, tiaki and manaaki. Children show care and concern for themselves and others.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Ōtara Marae will be in three years. 

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Ōtara is in Ōtara, South Auckland. The kōhanga reo is on the grounds of Ngāti Ōtara Marae and is close to Manukau Institute of Technology. The kōhanga reo operates from a well maintained, purpose-built complex with spacious indoor and outdoor learning and play areas.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children demonstrate whanaungatanga?

Children show care and concern for themselves and others.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children have positive relationships with others. Interactions between kaimahi and children are warm and caring. Children and their whānau are welcomed to kōhanga reo each day. Kaimahi model aroha, care and manaaki. Whānau report that they are learning alongside their children. Children’s care and individual needs are important.

Children are settled and know who they are and where they belong. Daily routines and activities are well established and flow smoothly. Children take responsibility for themselves and their surroundings.

Children are confident in the learning programme. Activities for children are well organised. Kaimahi provide a variety of experiences that link to kaupapa mahi. Kaimahi take responsibility for a group throughout the day but children have many opportunities to work and play together. The kōhanga reo is well resourced. The learning environment is attractive, spacious, clean and well maintained.

Children hear te reo Māori spoken by kaimahi who are competent speakers. A kuia, who plays a prominent role in the daily programme, provides te reo Māori and tikanga support to children, kaimahi and whānau. Children use verbal and non-verbal ways to communicate and express themselves. Children are growing in their ability to speak te reo Māori and are well supported by kaimahi and whānau.

Planning and Evaluation

Kaimahi and whānau meet regularly to discuss the programme and plan. They reflect on the programme and identify activities that children may enjoy. Most children are engaged in learning that is meaningful to them.

Assessment of children’s learning is well managed. Kaimahi use profiles and progression reports to identify what children are learning. They speak with parents about their children’s progress. Children benefit from strategies that focus on their achievements.

The charter expectations and the guidelines for the management and operations of the kōhanga reo are clear. Whānau and kaimahi have a shared understanding of their roles and responsibilities. Core health and safety policies and procedures are in place. Finances are utilised to meet the needs of children. The kōhanga reo is managed in the best interests of children.

Key Next Steps

The overall learning programme does not provide sufficient challenge and interest for the boys. Kaimahi have identified the need to investigate a range of strategies for teaching that will support boys and their learning.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation to investigate activities that will physically and mentally challenge and engage the large number of boys.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Ōtara is in Ōtara, South Auckland. The kōhanga reo is on the grounds of Ngāti Ōtara Marae and is close to Manukau Institute of Technology. Children experience an environment that promotes whanaungatanga through aroha, tiaki and manaaki. Children show care and concern for themselves and others.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Ōtara Marae will be in three years.
