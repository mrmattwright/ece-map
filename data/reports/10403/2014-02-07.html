1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

‘Ko Puputahitanga te maunga

Wairoro te awa

Tokareireia te puke

Te Kōtahitanga te marae

Ngāti Whakaeke te hapū

Ngāpuhi te iwi.’

(Te tūtohinga a te whānau )

Kei te marae o Te Kotahitanga ki Kaikohe Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kotahitanga. Ko Ngāti Whakaeke te iwi. He whānau kotahi ngā tamariki, ngā whānau me ngā kaimahi, ā, nō tēnei rohe tonu rātou. He tino āhuatanga tēnei ka whakamōhio te whānau ki a rātou tamariki i te kōhanga reo.

I kōwhiri te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira hei wāhi arotahi mō tēnei arotake mātauranga. Kua kīi mai rātou ko te reo me ōna tikanga kei te pūtake o te ako me te whanake o ngā tamariki. Ko te tuakiri o ngā tamariki, ō rātou hitori tuku iho, ngā tikanga me ngā kawa, ngā whāinga matua mō ō rātou akoranga. I whai wāhi ngā mema o te whānau ki te waihanga i te pātai aromātai e whai ake nei i arahi i te aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

‘He pēhea te kaha o te angitū o te poipoi i ngā tamariki kia puāwai tō rātou māiatanga me tō rātou mōhio ki te ao Māori?’

He angitū te rūmaki o ngā tamariki ki ngā horopaki whai rawa, whai hua hoki hei whakawhanake i ō rātou āheinga me ō rātou mōhio ki te ao Māori. Ka ngākaunui ngā tamariki pakeke ki te kōrero Māori. Ka kitea ngā tikanga Māori ki te hōtaka o ia rā. He rangatira te mōhio o ngā tamariki ki ēnei tikanga. Ka tū rangatira rātou ki te whakatauira i ngā tikanga e tika ana, ngā karakia me ngā waiata. He kaha te tuitui o ngā whakapapa me ngā hitori tuku iho ki ngā wheako akoranga katoa o ngā tamariki. He māia te whakatauira o ngā tuakana ki ngā teina i ngā tikanga me ngā whanonga e ai ki te hāhi Rātana.

He whakaute, he atawhai, he poipoi hoki ngā taunekeneke me ngā whanaungatanga i waenganui i ngā tamariki me ngā whānau. He angitū te poipoi i ngā tamariki kia puāwai hei akonga māia, akonga tākare hoki. Ka kitea te puāwai o te mārama me te tū rangatira o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou ao Māori.

Kei te angitū tonu te whakahaere o te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo me te arotahi ki te whakarewa ake i o rātou whāinga rautaki me o rātou wawata mō a rātou tamariki. Ko tō rātou tirohanga roa, o rātou wawata me o rātou whāinga kei te aroaro o ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau i te kōhanga reo, te whakamahere rautaki, te arotake whaiaro me te whakatau kaupapa. Kei te whakarato tonu te whakatinana tōtika iTe Whārikime te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo ngā putanga akoranga angitū mō ngā tamariki me te whānau.

Ngā Mahi ā Meake Nei

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kohanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Titiro Hāngai a te Arotake

I mua atu i te whakahaerenga o te arotake, i ïnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kotahitanga kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake mā te whai i ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ki a rātou.

Ï muri mai i ngā whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenganui i te rōpū arotake o Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau, ka whakaritea e rātou ngā tino kaupapa motuhake hei arotakehanga. I titiro ēnei whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero ki runga i ngā kōrero i te pupuritia e te kōhanga reo (e whai wāhia anangā kōrero arotake whaiaro),ā, me te whānuitanga o ngā take pūmanawa hei arotakehanga i runga i ngā putanga e whai hua ana ki ngā mokopuna oTe Kōhanga Reo o Te Kotahitanga.

Ko te kaupapa i whakaritea e te whānau hei titirohanga mā tēnei arotake ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

E titiro ana te katoa o ngā arotake a ERO i roto i ngā kōhanga, ki runga i te kounga o te mātauranga. E ai ki a ERO, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o:

  • ngā arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me ngā whakamāhere rautaki; me
  • ngā whakamāhere o ngā hōtaka akoranga, ngā aro matawai me ngā aro mātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

I kōwhiri te whānau o te kōhanga reo ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira hei wāhi arotahi mō tēnei arotake. I whai wāhi rātou ki te waihanga i te pātai aromātai e whai ake nei ka arahi i te arotake a te Tari Arotake Matauranga:

“He pēhea te kaha o te angitū o te whanake o te māiatanga me te mōhio o ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui?”

Kua kii mai ngā kuia, ngā kaimahi me te whānau ko te tiaki me te oranga o a rātou tamariki me te whānau kei te puku o ngā akoranga katoa tae atu ana ki te reo me ōna tikanga.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te Ao Māori. He angitū te rūmaki o ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga whai rawa, pono hoki ki te ao Māori. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi me ngā kuia i ngā wheako akoranga whai hua ki reira ka tuhura ngā tamariki, ka whakamatau, ka whakawhānui i o rātou mōhio ki te ao Māori. Ka tae atu ngā wheako ki te whai wāhi ki te marae me te tuhura i ngā taiao tuku iho me ngā taiao a tinana. Kei te puāwai te mōhio me te marama o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou tūranga motuhake hei tangata whenua.

Whānaungatanga. Ka poipoi ngā tamariki ki tētahi whānau aroha, atawhai, whakaute hoki. Ka noho tahi ngā tamariki me o rātou kuia, ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ia rā. Ka mahi angitū te katoa o ngā kaimahi me ngā kaumātua ki te whakautu ki ngā matea o tēnā, o tēnā o ngā tamariki. Ka hāngai ngā āhuatanga motuhake me ngā mātāpono kei ngā taunekeneke katoa me ngā tamariki ki te hāhi Rātana.

Te reo me ona tīkanga Māori. Kei te whanake te mōhio o ngā tamariki ki te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. Ka whakarato te whānau i ngā wheako whai hua ka whakawhanake i te kōrero o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. Ka whakatutuki angitū tēnei mā te hōtaka akoranga whānui, whakautu hoki e whakanui ana i te reo, e whakaihiihi ana. Kei te puta mai ngā āheinga me ngā māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki ngā tikanga ki ngā wā rerekē.

He taonga te akonga. Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i te whānui o ngā akoranga whai hua huhua. Tae atu ana ki te tūhura mātātoa ki te taiao, te whai wāhi ki ngā wā ako a rōpū me te tākaro ki waho, ki rō whare hoki. Ka whakarei ake, ka whakawhānui hoki ngā kaimahi i te mōhio o ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga ā-kaupapa. Ka poipoi rātou i ngā ngākaunui me ngā kaha o tēnā, o tēnā o ngā tamariki mā ngā mahi takatika. Ka āwhina tēnei ki te whakawhanake i te māia o ngā tamariki ki te uiui me te whakaatu i tō rātou rangatiratanga ki te ako. He pai te tautoko i ngā tamariki kia mōhio ai rātou ki ō rātou ake kaha i a rātou e tūhura ana me te whai whakaaro ki te ao Māori.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

E aro mātai ana a ERO i ngā wā katoa, i te kounga o ngā whakamahere me ngā aro mātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo ki te titiro ki te kaha o te whānau o te kōhanga ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere me te aro turuki i te katoa o ngā wāhanga whakahaere o te kōhanga mō aratou tamariki me te whānau. Kei roto i te wāhanga o te whakamahere me te aro mātai ko te whakamahere hōtaka me te aro turuki i te whanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. He angitū te whakahaere a te whānau e tino ngākaunui ana ki a rātou tamariki i te kōhanga reo. He pai te whakamahine, te mārama me te whakatinana i ngā pūnaha whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. Nō reira ka tōtika te tuku pūrongo, te aroturuki me te whakahaere i ngā wāhi katoa o te kōhanga reo. Ka whakamāramatia te whānau ki ngā mahi whakahaere me ngā whanaketanga. Ka mahi ngātahi rātou ki te whakaū i te whanaungatanga me te arotake i te ahunga o te kōhanga reo. He kaha te arotahi ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo i runga i te whakaaro tuatahi ki ngā tamariki.

Te ahunga a rautaki. Kei te whānau tētahi mahere rautaki me ngā whāinga matua e hāngai ana ki te tipu a meāke nei o te kōhanga reo me a rātou tamariki. Ka kitea ēnei whāinga ki te kōhanga reo hei maumahara i te whānau i a rātou e mahi ana ki te whakatutuki i ō rātou whāinga ngātahi.

Te arotake whaiaro o te whānau. He pai ngā mahi arotake whaiaro. Ka mau ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā wāhi katoa o te kōhanga reo. Ka whakamahi ēnei kōrero hei aroturuki i ngā mahi me te tuku pūrongo ki te whānau e pā ana ki ngā whakahaere me ngā mahi o te kōhanga reo. Ka tāutu ngā whāinga, ā, ka whakamahere ngā whanaketanga ki ngā hui ā-whānau. Kei te pūtake o ngā mahi arotake whaiaro ko te oranga katoa me ngā matea o ngā tamariki me te whānau.

Ngā mahere a hōtaka me ngā aromatai. Ko te ako me te whanake o ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā mahi whakamahere hōtaka. He tōtōpu, he whai whakaaro hoki ngā mahi whakamahere. Kei reira ngā hōtaka kua pai te whakamahere, he whānui, ā, he kaha te whakatairanga i te ako. Ka hāngai ngā mahere ki ngā kōeke, ngā āheinga me ngā ngākaunui katoa. Kei te rūmaki ngā tamariki ki ngā horopaki te ao Māori pono ki te rohe.

Ka whakamahia tōtika e ngā kaimahi te aromātai i te hōtaka hei aroturuki i te kaha o ngā hōtaka akoranga ki te hāpai ake i ngā ngākaunui o ngā tamariki. He whai hua, he whakaihiihi, he pai te whakamahere me te aroturuki i te whanake me te ako o ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori.

Te hopu i te tipu me te whanake o ngā tamariki. He tino tōtika, ā, he tino whai kōrero ngā mahi aromatawai. Ka hopu ngā kaimahi i te tipu me te angitū o ngā tamariki ki te reo me ōna tikanga me era atu wāhi katoa o te hōtaka i ia wā. Ka taea e te whānau te pānui i ēnei kōrero. Ka aroturuki ngā kaimahi i te tipu me te whanake o ngā tamariki ki ngā horopaki te ao Māori.

  • me ngā whakaritenga mō ngā puta tere wawe atu ki waho, inā ka puta he ahi, he rū ranei.

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

‘Ko Puputahitanga te maunga

Wairoro te awa

Tokareireia te puke

Te Kōtahitanga te marae

Ngāti Whakaeke te hapū

Ngāpuhi te iwi.’

(Whānau charter)

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kotahitanga is located on the grounds of Te Kōtahitanga Marae, in Kaikohe. The iwi is Ngāti Whakaeke. Children, whānau and kaimahi have strong whakapapa links to each other and the local area. This is a special characteristic which whānau pass on to children at kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus for this education review. They report te reo me ona tīkanga Māori is central to children’s learning and development. Children’s identity, ancestral heritage, customs and traditions are a priority for their learning. Whānau members participated in the design of the evaluation question which guided ERO’s evaluation of Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

‘How successfully are our children nurtured to develop confidence in and knowledge of te ao Māori?’

Children are successfully immersed in a range of rich and meaningful contexts to develop their language capabilities and knowledge of te ao Māori. Older children choose to communicate in te reo Māori. Tīkanga Māori is evident in the daily programme. Children know these well. They proudly model appropriate protocols, karakia and waiata. Whakapapa and ancestral heritage is strongly woven through all learning experiences for children. Older children confidently model to younger children Ratana protocols and behaviours.

Interactions and relationships among children and whānau are respectful, caring and supportive. Children are successfully nurtured to develop as confident, enthusiastic learners. Children show a growing awareness, understanding and pride in their world Māori.

The whānau continue to successfully manage their kōhanga reo with a sustained focus on upholding their strategic objectives and the aspirations they have for children. Their vision, aspirations and objectives are at the forefront of whānau management, strategic planning, self review and decision making. The effective implementation ofTe Whārikiand kōhanga management continues to provide successful learning outcomes for children and whānau.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kotahitanga was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.  This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kotahitanga.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

The kōhanga reo whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus area for this review. They participated in the design of the evaluation question which is used to guide ERO’s review:

“How successfully are our children developing confidence in and knowledge of Te Ao Māori and the wider world?”

Kuia, kaimahi and the whānau report that at the heart of all learning, including te reo me ona tīkanga, is the care and well being of their children and whānau.

Te Ao Māori. Children are successfully immersed in rich, authentic te ao Māori learning. Kaimahi and kuia provide meaningful learning experiences where children investigate, explore and extend their knowledge of te ao Māori. Experiences include participating on the marae and exploring the wider ancestral and physical environments. Children’s awareness and understanding of their unique place as tangata whenua is developing.

Whānaungatanga. Children are nurtured within a loving, caring and respectful whānau. Each day, children are surrounded by and interact with their kuia, kaimahi and whānau. All kaimahi and kaumatua work successfully together to respond to the individual needs of children. The unique characteristics and principles that are fostered throughout all interactions with children strongly align to the Ratana faith.

Te reo me ona tīkanga Māori. Children are developing knowledge of te reo me ngā tīkanga Māori. The whānau provide meaningful experiences that develop children’s use of te reo Māori. This is successfully achieved through a varied and responsive learning programme that is language rich and vibrant. Children’s language capabilities and understanding of tikanga in different situations are emerging.

Valued learners. Children are provided with a range of diverse and meaningful learning activities. These include actively exploring the environment, participating in group learning sessions and outdoor and indoor play. Kaimahi enhance and extend children’s knowledge of kaupapa topics. They nurture children’s individual interests and strengths in positive ways. This helps to develop children’s confidence as they ask questions and show independence in their learning. Children are well supported to realise their strengths as they explore and make sense of te ao Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Kōhanga management. The kōhanga reo is successfully managed by whānau who are strongly committed to their children. Kōhanga reo management systems are well defined, understood and implemented. This allows for effective reporting, monitoring and management across all areas of the kōhanga. Whānau are made aware of the operations and overall developments. They work together to reinforce whanaungatanga and review the direction of the kōhanga reo. There is a strong focus on managing the kōhanga reo in the best interests of children.

Strategic direction. The whānau have in place a strategic plan with key priorities that are relevant to the future growth of the kōhanga reo and their children. These priorities are displayed at the kōhanga reo as a reminder to whānau as they work towards meeting their shared goals.

Whānau self review. Self review is well established. Records across all areas of the kōhanga reo are maintained well. This information is used to monitor practices, and report to whānau about kōhanga reo management and operation. Whānau hui are used to identify priorities and plan developments. The overall wellbeing and needs of children and whānau are central to self review.

Programme plans and evaluation. Children’s learning and development is at the heart of programme planning. Planning is thorough and well considered. There are comprehensive, well planned programmes in place that strongly promote learning. Plans cater for all ages, abilities and interests. Children are immersed in local and authentic te ao Māori contexts.

Programme evaluation is used effectively by kaimahi to monitor how well programmes of learning support children’s interests. Children’s development and learning about te ao Māori is meaningful, stimulating, well planned and monitored.

Capturing children’s growth and development. Assessment practices are highly effective and informative. Children’s growth and success in te reo me ona tīkanga, and all other areas of the programme are captured on a regular basis by kaimahi. This well collated information is available for whānau to view. Kaimahi monitor children’s growth and development in te ao Māori contexts.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kotahitanga completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist.  In these documents they haveattested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends the whānau continue to use self review to record, monitor and report progress of their strategic priorities and those in Te Ara Tūāpae.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

To the Parents and Community of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kotahitanga

These are the findings of the Education Review Office’s latest report on Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kotahitanga.

‘Ko Puputahitanga te maunga

Wairoro te awa

Tokareireia te puke

Te Kōtahitanga te marae

Ngāti Whakaeke te hapū

Ngāpuhi te iwi.’

(Whānau charter)

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kotahitanga is located on the grounds of Te Kōtahitanga Marae, in Kaikohe. The iwi is Ngāti Whakaeke. Children, whānau and kaimahi have strong whakapapa links to each other and the local area. This is a special characteristic which whānau pass on to children at kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus for this education review. They report te reo me ona tīkanga Māori is central to children’s learning and development. Children’s identity, ancestral heritage, customs and traditions are a priority for their learning. Whānau members participated in the design of the evaluation question which guided ERO’s evaluation of Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

‘How successfully are our children nurtured to develop confidence in and knowledge of te ao Māori?’

Children are successfully immersed in a range of rich and meaningful contexts to develop their language capabilities and knowledge of te ao Māori. Older children choose to communicate in te reo Māori. Tīkanga Māori is evident in the daily programme. Children know these well. They proudly model appropriate protocols, karakia and waiata. Whakapapa and ancestral heritage is strongly woven through all learning experiences for children. Older children confidently model to younger children Ratana protocols and behaviours.

Interactions and relationships among children and whānau are respectful, caring and supportive. Children are successfully nurtured to develop as confident, enthusiastic learners. Children show a growing awareness, understanding and pride in their world Māori.

The whānau continue to successfully manage their kōhanga reo with a sustained focus on upholding their strategic objectives and the aspirations they have for children. Their vision, aspirations and objectives are at the forefront of whānau management, strategic planning, self review and decision making. The effective implementation ofTe Whārikiand kōhanga management continues to provide successful learning outcomes for children and whānau.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
