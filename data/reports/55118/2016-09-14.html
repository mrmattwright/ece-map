Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongowhakaata ki runga i te marae o Whakatō, ki Manutuke, i te tapa tonu o Tūranga-nui-a-Kiwa. E rumakina ana ngā tamariki ki ngā horopaki tuku iho o te ako, ā, e whakatairanga ana tēnei i te reo me ngā tikanga o Ngāti Maru. E whanake ana te tūmāia o ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro mā te reo o Ngāti Maru.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongowhakaata i roto ngā tau e rua.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongowhakaata ki runga i te marae o Whakatō, ki Manutuke, i te tapa tonu o Tūranga-nui-a-Kiwa. Nō tēnei marae te whānau o te kōhanga reo, nō te iwi tonu o Rongowhakaata. He tokomaha ngā mātua e kawe mai ana i ā rātou tamariki ki te kōhanga reo i Tūranga-nui-a-Kiwa, kia ako ai rātou i te mita, i ngā tikanga, me ngā kawa o tō rātou marae. Ka whakapūmau i ō rātou hononga ā-hapori ki tō rātou purapura me te iwi whānui.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa tā ngā tamariki whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro mā te reo o Ngāti Maru?

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i ngā pūkenga reo ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro mā te reo o Ngāti Maru.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E ahu mai ana ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i ngā tikanga me ngā kawa o tō rātou marae. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i te tūhura a ngā tamariki i ngā wāhi katoa o te marae, tae atu ki te urupā, ki te whare tipuna, me ētahi atu o ngā tino pou whenua i tuku iho ai ki a rātou. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te ako i ngā tikanga hei tangata whenua ki runga i te marae. E mōhio ana rātou ki tō rātou tūnga i runga i te marae, ā, ki ā rātou kawenga hoki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki tā rātou noho ki te marae.

Ka ngākaunui ngā tamariki ki tō rātou wāhi ako me te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whiriwhiri i ngā taumahi me ngā rauemi e hiahiatia ana e rātou. Ko ngā karakia, ngā mihimihi, me ngā pepeha te aronga matua o ngā akoranga ōkawa. Kua waia ngā tamariki ki ngā mahinga me ngā tumanakotanga o ia rā. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i ngā tūmomo kaupapa hei whakaongaonga i ō rātou whakaaro. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki tō rātou noho ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka whai pūtake, he whakaute hoki ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu. Ka ākina ngā hononga papai i ngā wā katoa. Ka whakamihia, ka whakanuia ngā whakamātau a ngā tamariki i ia te wā. Ka toro atu rātou ki te tauāwhi, ki te atawhai anō hoki i ngā kaimahi i ngā wā e tika ana. Ka ākina ngā tuākana ki te tautoko i ō rātou tēina. He tūturu, he whakaute ngā taunekeneke e rangona ana e ngā tamariki i a rātou e ako ana.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i te reo o Ngāti Maru me ngā tikanga, ngā kawa hoki o te marae. Ka rongo rātou i ngā waiata me ngā mōteatea o Ngāti Maru, me ngā hīmene, ā, ka hāpai ēnei i te whakapakaritanga ake o tō rātou whanaketanga reo. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā pātai me ngā kōrero akiaki hei hāpai i ngā tamariki ki te kōrero. Mā ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki ngā tamariki, ka whai wā rātou ki te kōrero me te whakapuaki anō hoki i ō rātou whakaaro. E tīmata ana te whānau ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā tūmomo taumahi ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki, hei whakapai ake hoki i ō rātou ake pūkenga reo. Ka whakamahi ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki ō rātou ake kāinga. E whakawhanake ana te māia ō ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro mā te reo Māori.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E mōhio ana te whānau, e tika ana kia whakakaha ake rātou i te whakamahinga o te reo Māori. He tokomaha ngā mātua e kī ana kei te whakamahi rātou i te reo Pākehā ki te kāinga, ā, ki te kōhanga reo anō hoki. He tākare tō rātou ki te whakawhanake i tētahi rautaki e pā ana ki te reo Māori.

Kāhore ngā wāhi ako e āta whakaritea ana, e āta whakahaerehia ana hoki. Kāhore he wāhi i whakaritea ai mō ngā taputapu me ngā rauhanga o te tari. He uaua ki te whakapā atu ki ngā tuhinga o te kōhanga reo nā te whāiti noa o ngā taputapu putu. Kāhore e taea e ngā tamariki te āta toro atu ki ngā pukapuka pānui nā te teitei rawa o te pae pukapuka. E whakaae ana hoki te whānau, kia whai whakaaro ki ngā tūmomo huarahi ka hāpai i te whakaritenga me te whakahaeretanga o ngā wāhi ako, hei painga mō te whakahaeretanga me te ako.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He tākare te whānau ki te ako i ō rātou tūranga hei mātua, hei kaiwhakahaere anō hoki o tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka āwhina rātou i ngā mahi i te kīhini, ka mātakitaki i ngā tamariki, ā, ka whai wāhi hoki ki ētahi āhuatanga o te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka hiki ake tēnei tautoko i te nui o ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi, ā, ka whakawātea i a rātou ki te aro nui ki te hōtaka akoranga mā ngā tamariki. Ka tino tautoko hoki ngā kuia i te whānau me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. Ka whai wāhi mai te hapū me te iwi mā te tautoko i ngā hōtaka o te reo me ngā tikanga Māori ki te kōhanga reo, me te whakatairanga ake anō hoki i tēnei ki te hapori whānui. E arotahi ana te whānau ki te whakapūmau i te pai o ngā hononga mō te whakawhanake tonutanga ā meāke nei. Ka poipoia ngā tamariki ki te mauritau me te atawhai i te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore i te rawaka te arotake whaiaro. E mōhio ana te whānau, kāhore anō te tūtohinga kia arotakengia. He maha ngā tino aratohu o te whakahaeretanga kāhore anō kia tuhia hei hāpai i te whakahaeretanga whānui a te whānau i te kōhanga reo, tae atu ki te whakahaere i ngā kaimahi. Kāhore i ngā kaimahi ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi mārama i āta whakawhanakehia ai, ā, kāhore anō hoki kia whakatinanahia ngā mahi arohaehae. Kāhore ngā aratohu e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru e hāngai ana i te Ture Mātauranga Kōhungahunga me ngā Paearu Raihana o te tau 2008. Ko ētahi o ēnei, ko ngā akoako hōneatanga inā he ahi, he rū whenua rānei, me ngā tikanga e pā ana ki te tīni kope, te mātakitaki i ngā tamariki e moe ana, te arowhai i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea, ngā aituā, ngā tūmomo mate pāwera, me te akiaki i te oranga kai.

Kāhore anō te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromātai, me te aromatawai kia āta whakaritea. E mōhio ana te kaiako me ngā kaimahi, e tika ana te whakapai ake i aua tū āhuatanga.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

3.1 whakapūmau i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te arowhai a ngā pirihimana, kia haumaru ai te noho o ngā tamariki
[319D ki te 319FA o te Ture Mātauranga 1989]

3.2 whakapūmau i ngā tuhinga mauhanga katoa me te pūrongo anō hoki o ēnei e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru, tae atu ki ngā aituā, te whāngai i ngā rongoā, ngā haerenga, te tū auau o ngā akoako hōneatanga ki te taha o ngā tamariki, ngā āhuatanga mōrearea o ngā rawa, te moe o ngā tamariki, me te tīni kope
[R 45 & 46 (1)(2) Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS & PF Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

3.3 whakarite i te āta whakatutukitanga, te āta whakapūmautanga o ngā taumata mahi katoa e pā ana ki te kāwana, te whakahaere, me te whakarite tari, hei painga mō ngā tamariki me te whānau.
[R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA 1-12 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • arotake, kia whakapai ake hoki i te whakaritenga o roto o tēnei wā, mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki
  • whakawhanake, kia whakatinana hoki i ngā mahi arohaehae pakari nei, tae atu hoki ki ngā tūmanako nui kia ōkawa te pūrongo me te arotake i te hōtaka akoranga, ā, kia
  • mahi hoki ki te taha o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo me te purapura, ki te whakatairanga tonu i ia te wā i te kounga o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, hei whakatairanga i te mātauranga me te atawhai anō hoki o ngā tamariki.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongowhakaata ki runga i te marae o Whakatō, ki Manutuke, i te tapa tonu o Tūranga-nui-a-Kiwa. E rumakina ana ngā tamariki ki ngā horopaki tuku iho o te ako, ā, e whakatairanga ana tēnei i te reo me ngā tikanga o Ngāti Maru. E whanake ana te tūmāia o ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro mā te reo o Ngāti Maru.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongowhakaata is on the grounds of Whakatō Marae in Manutuke, on the outskirts of Gisborne. Children are immersed in traditional contexts of learning that promote te reo me ngā tikanga o Ngati Maru. Children are developing confidence to express themselves in te reo o Ngāti Maru.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongowhakaata will be within two years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongowhakaata is the grounds of Whakatō Marae in Manutuke, on the outskirts of Gisborne. All whānau members affiliate to the marae and iwi of Rongowhakaata. Many parents bring their children from Gisborne to the kōhanga reo to learn their dialect, customs and traditions of their marae. Strong community networks are maintained with their local purapura and wider iwi whānau.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children express themselves in te reo o Ngāti Maru?

Children are developing the language skills to express themselves in te reo o Ngāti Maru.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children’s learning is based on tikanga and kawa of their marae. Kaimahi encourage children to explore the marae environment including the urupa, whare tipuna and other local significant ancestral landmarks. Children have opportunities to learn about the tikanga on the marae as tangata whenua. They are familiar with where they connect on the marae and the things they need to do. Children enjoy their time at the marae.

Children enjoy their learning environment and the variety of learning experiences. There are opportunities for children to choose activities and resources of interest to them. Karakia, mihimihi and pepeha is the focus of the formal learning. Children are familiar with daily routines and expectations. Kaimahi encourage children to explore different kaupapa to stimulate their thinking. Children enjoy their time at kōhanga reo.

Children have meaningful and respectful interactions with others. Positive relationships are encouraged at all times. Children receive regular praise and acknowledgement for their efforts. They will seek comfort and reassurance as the need arises from kaimahi. Tuakana are encouraged to support their teina. Children learn in an environment where interactions are genuine and respectful.

Children learn te reo o Ngāti Maru, and tikanga and marae kawa. They hear Ngāti Maru waiata, mōteatea and himene that help build their language development. Kaimahi use questions and prompts to encourage children to speak. Conversations with children provide them with ways to speak and share their ideas. Whānau are beginning to participate in activities alongside their children to improve their language skills. Children use te reo Māori at home. Children are developing confidence to express themselves in te reo Māori.

Key Next Steps

Whānau acknowledge that they need to further grow the use of te reo Māori. Many parents acknowledge they use English at home and in the kōhanga reo. They are keen to develop a te reo Māori strategy.

Learning spaces are not well organised and managed. There is no designated space for administration equipment and furniture. It is difficult to access kōhanga reo documentation due to the limited storage space available. Children cannot readily access reading books because the bookshelf is too high. Whānau agree much more consideration needs to be given to how best to organise and manage learning spaces for operations and learning.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau members are keen to learn about their roles as parents and managers of their kōhanga reo. They help with kitchen duties, supervision and aspects of the daily programme. This support alleviates work for kaimahi who can focus on the learning programme for children. Kuia strongly support the whānau and kaupapa of the kōhanga reo. The hapū and iwi interest is to support kōhanga reo te reo me ngā tikanga Māori programmes to promote this in the wider community. The whānau are focused on maintaining positive relationships for the future development. Children experience a positive, caring atmosphere at the kōhanga reo.

Key Next Steps

Internal review is inadequate. Whānau acknowledge the tutohinga has not been reviewed. There are important management guidelines that have not been documented to assist whānau with overall kōhanga reo operations including personnel management. Kaimahi do not have clear and well developed job descriptions neither has appraisal been implemented. Health and safety guidelines do not reflect the Early Childhood Regulations and Licencing 2008 criteria. These include fire and earthquake drills, nappy changing, monitoring sleeping children, hazard checks, accidents, allergies and promoting healthy kai.

Programme planning, evaluation and assessment are not well established. The kaiako and kaimahi acknowledge that improvements are needed in these areas.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

3.1 maintain police vetting requirements for the safety and protection of children
[319D to 319FA of the Education Act 1989]

3.2 maintain all records and reporting requirements for health and safety, including-accidents, administering of medications, excursions, regular evacuation drills with children, property hazards, sleep and nappy changing
[R45 & 46(1)(2) Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS & PF Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

3.3 ensure all governance, management and administration practice standards are fully met and maintained for children and whānau.
[R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA 1-12 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendations

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • review and improve the current indoor organisation for children’s learning,

  • develop and implement robust appraisal practices that includes high expectations for formal reporting and review of the learning programme, and

  • work closely with TKRNT and purapura to continue raising the quality of whānau management to enhance education and care for children.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongowhakaata is on the grounds of Whakatō Marae in Manutuke, on the outskirts of Gisborne. Children are immersed in traditional contexts of learning that promote te reo me ngā tikanga o Ngati Maru. Children are developing confidence to express themselves in te reo o Ngāti Maru.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongowhakaata will be within two years.
