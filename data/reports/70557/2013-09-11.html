1 Evaluation of Lincoln University Early Childhood Centre

Lincoln University Early Childhood Centre is well placed to promote positive learning outcomes for children.

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Lincoln University Early Childhood Centre is situated in the grounds of Lincoln University. It is one of two centres managed by Lincoln Hospitality Limited, a subsidiary of the university. The centre provides education and care for the children of students and teaching staff from Lincoln University as well as children from the local community. The centre is licensed for 33 children aged from birth to school age. Families from many cultures use the centre. The centre has a focus on promoting environmental sustainability and is part of enviroschools and Lincoln Envirotown Trust. An experienced centre coordinator oversees the day-to-day operation of the centre. During 2012, there were some changes to the teaching team. Teaching staff have qualifications in Early Childhood Education and two staff are in training.

Children and teachers share warm, caring relationships. Teachers know children and their families well and work together to support children‚Äôs wellbeing and development. Particular attention is given to supporting babies and toddlers to develop close relationships with key teachers.

Teachers use a wide range of high-quality teaching practices in the nursery and preschool. These practices help children‚Äôs language development. Teachers work together in a strong team culture with a shared philosophy. They regularly reflect on their teaching practices and share information about individual children‚Äôs interests and learning. Teachers support each other well within the programme. The centre coordinator ensures that good use is made of individual teacher strengths and passions to support the whole teaching team. Teachers are given many opportunities to share and develop their leadership skills.

Key Next Steps

The centre coordinator and teachers have made good use of their professional discussions and self review to reliably identify the next steps for the centre's development.

They are in the early stages of trialling some new systems to improve the areas they have identified. They now need to make sure these new systems are monitored for effectiveness and successfully become a regular part of centre practices. These include:

  • appraisal practices for individual teachers
  • recent initiatives to strengthen assessment
  • new processes for guiding centre self review.

They have also identified a next step is to extend the role of children in assessing their own learning and contributing to centre self review.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Lincoln University Early Childhood Centre completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.
