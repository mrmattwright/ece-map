1 Background 

Introduction

A Supplementary Review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager, Review Services in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a centre has taken towards addressing issues specified in a previous education review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of the Turua Playcentre governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the April 2013 ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • annual planning and self review
  • assessment, planning and evaluation
  • members’ responses to incidental learning opportunities and children’s emerging interests
  • opportunities for children to learn about te reo and tikanga Māori and New Zealand’s bicultural heritage
  • the learning and physical environment.

2 Evaluation Findings

Background

Turua Playcentre is located close to the local primary school in the rural township of Turua, on the Hauraki plains. Members provide education and care for children from birth to school age for two mornings a week. They strongly believe in parents as first teachers and supporting their children’s learning through child-initiated play. Since March 2013, the centre’s roll has increased to 12, including seven boys and five children of Māori descent.

The centre operates under the umbrella of the Thames Valley Coromandel Playcentre Association. Since the April 2013 ERO review the association has effectively supported members to improve the quality of education. This has been achieved through targeted workshops and liaison officer support. A support group for Māori members, Puriri Whakamaru o Hauraki, has also provided centre and marae-based programmes to increase members’ awareness and understanding of te reo and tikanga Māori.

The centre was fully licensed in September 2013 after addressing the areas for improvement identified on the transitional licence. The specified areas included the quality of self review, planning, assessment and evaluation practices, and incorporating te reo and tikanga Māori in the programme. Members have also made significant progress in responding to the interests of boys and older children and upgrading the learning environment. They have recently commenced professional development with the New Zealand Playcentre Federation on assessment practices.

Areas of progress

Relationships between members are based on mutual respect. They highly value individual parent’s contributions, strengths and ideas, and delegate leadership roles and responsibilities accordingly. Parents who belong to Puriri Whakamaru o Hauraki have worked collaboratively with other members and the wider community to increase bicultural awareness. A core group of members has provided continuity of centre leadership since the 2013 ERO review. Having several members with association-wide responsibilities has improved centre access to association support.

Members now use self review more effectively to identify centre strengths and areas for improvement. They are currently developing a new strategic plan. Members could consider more clearly defining the desired outcomes and delegated tasks for each goal. This should make it easier for them to monitor progress and evaluate the effectiveness of their efforts.

Members have significantly improved the quality of programme planning, assessment practices, and parent participation in end-of-session evaluations. Profile book nights have increased the confidence and motivation of members to complete assessments of their own and other children.

The programme strongly reflects playcentre philosophy and the goals of Te Whāriki, the early childhood curriculum. Parents respond more promptly and effectively to children’s emerging language, interests and ideas. They integrate literacy and mathematical concepts in meaningful play, and skilfully encourage children to think for themselves and solve problems. Adult interactions with children are consistently warm and respectful. Parents also foster the development of social skills and positive interactions between older and younger children.

Children are well supported to become capable and competent learners. They confidently communicate with a wide variety of adults and children and demonstrate a strong sense of belonging. Children are consistently well engaged in play of their choice, and play happily with and alongside others. Adult interactions with babies are particularly gentle and caring. Babies have good opportunities to practice their developing physical and social skills.

Areas for further improvement

Centre members and the liaison officer identified, and ERO agrees, that the areas for further improvement are to:

  • focus strategic planning and self review on improving teaching practices and learning outcomes for children
  • continue to strengthen planning, assessment and evaluation practices
  • further develop members’ confidence in implementing Māori language and culture in the programme.

3 Future Action

ERO is confident that the service is being managed in the interest of the children. Therefore ERO will review the service again as part of the regular review cycle.

Dale Bailey

National Manager Review Services Northern Region
