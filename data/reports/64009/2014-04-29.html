1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kei te taha o te whare karakia o Rangiātea ki Ōtaki Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kākano o Te Kura e tū ana. E tū ana te kōhanga ki te poka pū o te mātauranga o Ōtaki ki te taha o te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Te Rito, te Wānanga o Raukawa me ngā kōhanga reo e whā o te rohe.

Ko te reo Māori te kaupapa matua mō te mātauranga ki tēnei kōhanga reo. E whakaatu ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi i tō rātou ū ki te kaha tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te whakapiki i tō rātou mātau hei kaikōrero e tino mōhio ana ki te reo Māori. E whakapiki ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou māia hei kaikōrero ki te reo Māori.

E māia ana, ā, e noho mauritau ana ngā tamariki ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo. He maha ngā wheako akoranga me ngā ngohe kua whakaritea mā ngā tamariki. Ka kōwhiritia ngā momo ngohe e ngā tamariki hei whai atu mō te rā. Ka tino whai wāhi ngā kaimahi ki te āta whakawhitiwhiti kōrero ki ngā tamariki. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whai atu i ō rātou hiahia me te whai wāhi ki ngā ngohe huhua. He mea whai tikanga ngā taunekeneke, ā, he mea pono, he mea mahana te whanaungatanga i waenganui i tēnā me tēnā.

Ko ngā tikanga Māori te tino tūāpapa o ngā whakahaere katoa o ia rā me te takamahi o ia rā i te kōhanga reo. E whakatauira ana ngā pakeke i ngā tikanga pērā i te pōwhiri ki ngā tamariki. I ētahi wā, ka whakahaere ngā tamariki i ngā tūranga tātaki mō te whakahaere karakia me ētahi atu wāhanga o te hōtaka o ia rā. E tino mōhio ana ngā tamariki i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā, ā, e manawanui ana rātou ki ēnei whakaritenga.

E whakahaeretia pai ana te kōhanga reo e ngā mātua o te whānau me ngā kaimahi. Ka whakatakoto rātou i ngā māhere mō ngā rangi ā meāke nei, heoi anō, ka tino arotahi rātou ki ngā wāhanga whakahaere o te wā. Ka whakahaeretia ngā arotake whaiaro me te whai whakaaro mō ngā take huhua ia wā, ia wā. Mā ēnei mōhiohio e riro ana i ngā mātua i te mātauranga me ngā whakamārama hei whakapai ake i ngā mahi hei tautoko i ngā akoranga o ā rātou tamariki.

E tino whai māramatanga ana ngā mātua o te whānau mō ngā paetae o ā rātou tamariki. Ka kohikohi ngā kaimahi, ā, ka whakatakotoria ēnei mōhiohio aromatawai kounga nui e whakaaturia ai te huarahi akoranga o ia tamariki mō te wā kua whakaritea. E whai wāhi ana te whānau ki te whakaputa i ā rātou mōhiohio mō ngā paetae me ngā whanaketanga o ā rātou tamariki. Kua whakatutukitia ngā hiahia me ngā matea o ia tamaiti i tēnei kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mokohuia o te Rautahi kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Reo.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

He whakamārama

I kōrero te whānau ki te Tari Arotake Mātauranga mō ngā take e pā ana ki tō rātou kōhanga reo mō ngā āheinga me ngā wheako akoranga kua whakatakotoria hei tautoko i ā rātou tamariki. I whakanui rātou i te kaupapa nui me te ariā matua o te reo Māori. Nā tēnei, i whakawhanaketia tētahi pātai aromātai hei arahi i te arotake.

Kei te pēhea rawa te pai o te tautoko a ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā tamariki ki te ako me te whakaputa i te reo Māori?

Kei te tino tautokotia ngā tamariki ki te ako me te whakaputa i te reo Māori.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te ako i te reo Māori me ōnā tikanga.Kua tino tautokotia ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi te ako me te whakaputa i te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. E whai painga ana ngā tamariki mā ngā wheako akoranga whai take me ngā ngohe hīkaka me ngā whakaritenga whakahirahira kua whakaritea. E whakahaere ana ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki whakapiki i te reo whai hua hei tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te ako me te whakaputa i te reo Māori. E tino pai ana ngā kaimahi ki te kimi i ngā rauemi hei tautoko i ngā tamariki i ā rātou e ako ana, ā, kei te pai ngā whakawhitiwhiti kōrero o tēnā me tēnā kia whai wāhi ai ngā tamariki katoa i te koanga ngākau. Mā ngā akoranga pānui pukapuka ki te katoa ka manawanui ngā tamariki ki te whakapā ki ēnei wheako kia whai wāhi ai rātou ki te kōrero i ngā pūrākau me te tūhura haere i ngā pukapuka me ngā whakaahua. E whakatauira ana ngā kaimahi i te reo me ōnā tikanga, ā, ka whakahaere rātou i ngā wheako akoranga kounga nui. E whakaputa ana ngā tamariki i te reo Māori me te hāpai i ngā tikanga Māori, ā, e tino whai wāhi ana rātou ki ngā akoranga katoa.

Ngā whanaungatanga.E noho pūmau ana ngā tamariki ki tō rātou whānau, ā, kua tiakina ngā tamariki e te whānau. Kua kīnakitia ō rātou paetae me ō rātou whanaketanga e ngā whanaungatanga me ngā taunekeneke whai koha o ngā kaimahi. Ka tino kitea ēnei taunekeneke tata me ngā whanaungatanga whai koha me te aroha i waenganui i ngā tamariki, ngā kaimahi me ngā mātua o te whānau. Kua poipoia, ā, kua whakanuia ngā uara o te whanaungatanga. E ako ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou tūhonohono ki te whānau e noho tata ana ki tō rātou taiao tae noa ki ngā whānau o te hapori whānui tonu. Ka tae atu ngā mātua o te whānau ki te kōhanga reo ia wā, ia wā, ā, ka mahi ngātahi ngā mātua me ngā kaimahi ki te whakarato i tētahi hōtaka akoranga kounga nui e tautoko ana i ngā tamariki ki te ako me te whakaputa i te reo Māori. Ka toro atu ngā tamariki ki te wānanga me te whai wāhi ki ngā whakahaere i te taha o te whānau, ā, i te wā e tika ana e taea ana e ngā tamariki te whai atu i ngā kaupapa i te wānanga.

Te whakawhanake i tōna tuakiri me tōna mana motuhake.E noho rumaki ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi hōtaka e āhukahukatia ai, e whakahaumakotia ai, e whakanuitia i ō rātou ahurea, ō rātou tuakiri me ngā mahi ā ō rātou tūpuna. E ako ana ngā tamariki ko wai ratou, ā, nō hea anō rātou. E ako ana rātou i ngā waiata e ai ki ō rātou tūhonohono ki tō rātou hapū, te iwi me ō rātou pūrākau rongonui. E whakatauira ana ngā tikanga Māori e ngā kaimahi me ngā mātua o te whānau. Ka hāpai ngā tamariki i ngā tūranga tātaki i te rā katoa. E ako ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou tuakiri ahurea i roto i tētahi taiao mauritau, ā, kua poipoia ō rātou māia hoki me tō rātou noho pūmau ki te kōhanga reo me te whānau.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea rawa te pai o ngā whakahaere a te whānau me ngā kaimahi i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki?

Kei te tino pai ngā whakahaere a te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Ngāwāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ngā whakahaere a te whānau.He tino whai hua te āhua o te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Kua whakatūria ngā hui ia wā, ia wā, ā, ka tae atu te tini o ngā mātua ki ēnei hui. He haepapa tō ia ohu, ā, kua tino whakatutukitia ō rātou haepapa. Ka whai atu ngā kaimahi me te whānau i tētahi māhere hei whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. E riro ana i ngā tamariki tētahi taiao reo Māori, ā, kua tino tautokotia ngā tamariki e te whānau.

Te arotake whaiaro.E pai ana ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakahaere i te arotake whaiaro hei whakapai ake i ngā wāhanga whakahaere o te kōhanga reo. E rere whai hua ana ngā whakaritenga o ia rā, ngā takamahi o ia rā me ngā hōtaka, ā, mā te whai whakaaro me te arotahi ki ngā whakahaere kounga nui te take e pēnei ai. Ka whakatakoto ngā ohu i ngā māhere me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga e hāngai ana ki ō rātou haepapa. E tino mārama ana te whānau e pēhea ana te whai painga me ngā whakahaere a te kōhanga reo.

Te whakatakoto i te māhere rautaki.Kua tuhia e te whānau ngā whāinga me ngā māhere mō ngā rangi ā meāke nei. E tino arotahi ana te whānau ki te whakapiki i te kounga o te reo Māori. Kua whakatakotoria te māhere whakangungu mō ngā kaimahi me ngā mātua o te whānau ia tau, ia tau. He kaupapa motuhake e haere ake nei mō te whakapai ake i te whenua me ngā whare, ā, ko te whakanui ake te rahi o te tari, te whare tākaro ō-waho me te whare moe. Ka arotake te whānau me ngā kaimahi ia wā, ia wā i ngā paetae kua pīkautia e rātou. E kaha ana te whānau ki te tino whakarato i ngā rawa hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Te hōtaka whakamāherehere.He tino whai hua te āhua o te hōtaka whakamāherehere. Ka mahi ngātahi ngā mātua me ngā kaimahi ki te whakahoahoa me te whakatinana i te hōtaka akoranga. Ko te tauira “tapawhā” te tūāpapa o te hōtaka whakamāherehere me ngā whakahaere hei whakatinana, arā, ko te taha tinana, te taha wairua, te taha hinengaro me te taha whānau ngā wāhanga o te tauira. Kua tuhia ngā whāinga paetae, ā, he mea ine ngā putanga akoranga mō ngā tamariki. E tino whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki i tētahi hōtaka akoranga kounga nui.

Te aromatawai.E whakahaeretia whai hua ana e ngā kaimahi ngā tukanga aromatawai hei mau ā-tuhi i ngā akoranga ā ngā tamariki.E tino whakaatu ana ngā pūkete aromatawai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ō rātou harikoa me ō rātou ngākau nui ki ngā mahi e whai atu ana rātou. He whānui me te hōhonu ngā mōhiohio i roto i ngā pūkete e whakaatu ana i ngā wheako ā ngā tamariki, ō rātou paetae me ō rātou whanaketanga mō te wā kua whakaritea. E tino whai māramatanga ana ngā mātua o te whānau mō ngā paetae o ā rātou tamariki. Kua whakamaua ngā wheako akoranga mā ngā whakaahua, ngā tuhituhi e hāngai ana ki ngā āheinga o ngā tamariki me ngā kōhinga mahi toi kua āta whakaaturia i roto i ngā pūkete paetae nui. He tino pai ngā mōhiohio mō ngā whanaketanga o ngā tamariki me ā rātou whai wāhitanga i a rātou e ako ana.

Te hōtaka aromātai.E whakahaere ana ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te hōtaka aromātai ki te whakapai ake i ngā wāhanga o te hōtaka akoranga mā nga tamariki i ngā wā katoa. He tino kaimahi whai whakaaro ngā kaimahi katoa, ā, i ngā wā katoa ka rapu haere rātou i ngā rautaki whai hua hei tautoko i ngā tamariki. E riro ana i ngā tamariki tētahi hōtaka akoranga kounga nui me tētahi taiao kounga nui.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakiia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o okiuh, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Tūtohunga

E tūtohu ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ki te whānau kia haere tonu ai ā rātou whakamāherehere ki te whakapai ake anō i te whenua me ngā whare o te kōhanga reo hei whakarato i tētahi taiao kounga nui mā ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

29 Paenga-whāwhā 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kākano o Te Kura Te Kōhanga Reo is in Ōtaki located next to the Rangiātea church. The kōhanga reo is situated in the educational hub of Ōtaki alongside Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Te Rito, Te Wānanga o Raukawa and four other kōhanga reo.

Te reo Māori is the primary focus for education at this kōhanga reo. The whānau and staff demonstrate a total commitment to support children to become competent, fluent speakers of te reo Māori. Children develop very well as speakers of te reo Māori.

Children are confident and comfortable in the kōhanga reo environment. There are many learning opportunities and experiences available to children. Children choose the types of learning activities they will undertake during the day. Staff members engage children in meaningful conversations. Staff encourage children follow their own interests and to participate in a range of activities. Interactions are purposeful and relationships are warm and genuine.

Tikanga Māori underpins the everyday practices and routines that occur at the kōhanga reo. Adults model tikanga such as pōwhiri, to children. At times, children take lead roles in delivering karakia and leading other aspects of the daily programme. Children know the routines of the day well and are comfortable with these.

The kōhanga reo is very well managed by the whānau and staff. They plan for the future while maintaining a focus on current events. Self review and reflection is regular. This information provides the whānau with the knowledge and background needed to refine and improve what they are doing to support their children’s learning.

Whānau members are well informed about the progress of their children. Staff gather and present high quality assessment information that provides an in-depth insight into each child’s learning journey, over time. Whānau contribute information about the progress and development of their children. Children’s interests and needs are catered for well at this kōhanga reo.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Reo.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning; and
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Mana Reo

Background

The whānau talked to ERO about their kōhanga reo and the opportunities and learning experiences presented to their children. They emphasised the importance and significance of te reo Māori. Consequently, an evaluative question was developed to guide the review.

How well are children supported by kaimahi and whānau to learn and use te reo Māori?

Children are very well supported to learn and to use te reo Māori.

Areas of good performance

Learning te reo me ngā tikanga Māori.Children are successfully supported by staff to learn and use te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. Children benefit from exciting, memorable and meaningful language learning experiences. Staff members use effective language acquisition strategies to support children to learn and use te reo Māori. Resources to support children in their learning and conversations are used well by staff and foster positive engagement. Shared reading sessions are well attended by children who choose to be a part of storytelling and the investigations of books and pictures. Staff model te reo and tikanga Māori for children and facilitate high quality learning experiences and interactions. Children use te reo Māori, practise tikanga Māori and are fully engaged in learning.

Relationships.Children are surrounded and nurtured by whānau. Their progress and development is enhanced by the significant and meaningful relationships that are established with staff. These close, loving and caring relationships exist between children, kaimahi and whānau members. The values of whanaungatanga are promoted and fostered. Children learn about their connections to whānau in their immediate environment and wider community. Whānau attend the kōhanga reo regularly and, with staff are committed to providing a high quality learning programme that supports children to learn and use te reo Māori. Children visit the wānanga and participate with their whānau when appropriate.

Developing a sense of belonging and identity.Children are immersed in a learning programme that acknowledges, promotes and celebrates their culture, identity and heritage. Children learn about who they are, where they are from. They learn waiata that reflect their connections to their hapū and iwi and their special stories.Tikanga Māori is modelled by staff and whānau. Children take lead roles throughout the day. Children learn about their cultural identity in a comfortable environment where they are confident and have a strong sense of belonging.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How well does the whānau and staff manage the kōhanga reo in the interests of children?

The kōhanga reo is very well managed in the interests of children.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management.The whānau and staff are highly effective in managing the kōhanga reo in the interests of children. There are regular meetings that are well attended. Whānau and staff work together to make decisions. Ohu groups have areas of responsibility that they address well. Staff and whānau have a planned approach for managing the kōhanga reo. Children are provided with a te reo Māori learning environment where they are well supported by whānau.

Self-review.Staff and whānau use self review well to improve the kōhanga reo operations. The day to day operations, routines and programmes operate smoothly and efficiently due to regular reflection that is focussed on high quality practice. Ohu plan and monitor their areas of responsibility. The whānau has a clear understanding of how well the kōhanga reo operates.

Strategic planning.The whānau have documented goals and plans for their future direction. The whānau is focused on further developing the level of te reo Māori. Professional development for staff and whānau members is planned annually. Property development is an ongoing project and includes increasing the size of the office, outdoor play room and the sleeping room. The whānau and staff regularly review the progress they have made toward meeting their goals. The whānau makes every effort to provide the best for their children.

Programme planning.Programme planning is highly effective. Parents and staff members work together to design and implement the learning programme. The “tapawha” model including te taha tinana, te taha wairua, te taha hinengaro and te taha whānau is the basis for programme planning and implementation. Achievement objectives are documented as are measureable learning outcomes for children. Children experience a high quality learning programme.

Assessment.Kaimahi use assessment practices effectively to record children’s learning. Assessment profiles clearly show that children are learning, happy and enjoy what they do. There is a comprehensive range of information included in profiles about children’s experiences, progress and development over time. Whānau are kept well informed about their child’s progress. Learning moments are captured through photos, narratives and collections of art work that are well presented in large profile books. There is good information available about children’s development, progress and engagement.

Programme evaluation.Staff and whānau useprogramme evaluation to continually improve the learning programme for children. Staff members are reflective practitioners who seek ways to provide the best they can for children. Children receive a high quality learning programme and environment.

Overview

  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommendsthat the whānau continue their plans to redevelop the kōhanga reo buildings and premises in order to provide a high quality physical environment for children and staff.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
