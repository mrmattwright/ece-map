Background

ABC Rotorua East operates under the umbrella of Kidicorp Ltd, which provides overall strategic direction and comprehensive systems to manage centre operations. The centre is an all-day, mixed age education and care service that is licensed for 36 children, including up to 10 children under the age of two years. Currently 28 of the 37 children attending are identified as MƒÅori.

The centre is located in a converted house. Parents and teachers have worked together to develop the outdoor playgrounds to feature edible plants and interesting areas where children play in a natural environment. Recently Kidicorp Ltd has introduced a transport service designed to maintain children‚Äôs regular attendance.

Since the last ERO review the centre has experienced a change in ownership, as new manager has been appointed as the centre leader, and there has been a change in teaching staff. The centre has improved its self review processes as recommended in the 2011 ERO review.

The recently reviewed centre philosophy aspires for children to ‚Äòbe valued as unique individual learners capable of taking a risk and being independent‚Äô. They aspire for children to learn in a ‚Äònurturing, stimulating programme and environment‚Äô where their learning and care needs are met.

The Review Findings

Children experience respectful, responsive relationships in an interesting, well-resourced environment. Their identity is recognised through their family stories which are prominently displayed. Children also hear and use te reo MƒÅori in conversations, waiata, and karakia at kai time. They know that they are free to make choices and follow their interests. Children are taking responsibility for their learning and using resources in creative ways.

Babies and toddlers are cared for with individual routines that are familiar from home. Teachers nurture and support children to explore freely, encouraging their independence and self-help skills. These younger children enjoy playing with their older siblings who include them and look after their wellbeing. Babies and toddlers are learning to be confident, competent learners who communicate knowing that their teachers, and also their peers, will respond to their words and non-verbal signals.

Teachers welcome children and their families into the centre‚Äôs warm, inclusive environment. They know children well and interact with them through many conversations, asking open questions to prompt them to share ideas and develop their thinking. Literacy and numeracy are a natural part of the programme. Teachers read many stories, including local myths and legends. They foster children‚Äôs enjoyment of books, and encourage their interest in experimenting creatively with art and writing materials.

Teachers are increasingly including parent‚Äôs aspirations for their children in their programme planning. Parents are well-informed about their children‚Äôs learning through displays, individual profiles, and conversations with teachers. The centre is currently reviewing the planning cycle, and teachers are making changes to increase their capacity to respond to children‚Äôs changing learning and development needs and abilities.

Teachers‚Äô professional knowledge has benefited from ongoing learning and development and guidance from Kidicorp Ltd managers. Teachers engage in discussion and reflection about their teaching practice to share successes and seek new ideas.

The centre manager places priority on maintaining a centre climate that welcomes children and parents, and promotes a sense of belonging. She has led an in-depth review that established a collaborative staff team with a strong sense of commitment to children‚Äôs learning and wellbeing. With the support of the teaching team and external specialists, the manager has also ensured that children with additional learning needs have been well catered for and included.

Area leaders know the centre well. Kidicorp managers have assisted the centre through a period of change. They provide appropriate leadership for centre leaders and teachers, with a strong focus on improving the quality of education and care. This close professional oversight has supported programme development, relationships with parents, self review, and the capability of the teaching team.

Key Next Steps

ERO and centre managers agree that the next steps for the centre are to:

  • continue to strengthen the links between planning and assessment, so that children‚Äôs learning and progress can be identified and supported.

The centre has identified, and ERO agrees, that this initiative is valuable for its potential to strengthen the links between planning and assessment, and the evaluation of outcomes for children.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of ABC Rotorua East completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.
