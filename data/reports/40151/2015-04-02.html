Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāpeke ki te marae o Te Whetū o te Rangi ki Welcome Bay, i Tauranga. He pakari, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo. He pai tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki ngā kaimahi, te whānau, me ngā kaumātua mā te reo Māori, ā, he māoriori tā rātou whai atu i ngā tikanga Māori. I ngā wā katoa, ka tautoko ngā kaumātua me te poari ā-iwi o Ngāti Pūkenga i ngā tamariki me te whānau. Tērā ka whakahaerehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri i roto i ngā tau e toru.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāpeke ki te marae o Te Whetū o te Rangi, ki Welcome Bay i Tauranga, ā, kei roto hoki i te whare kotahi e whakahaerehia ana te poari ā-iwi o Ngāti Pūkenga. He tino hononga tō ngā tamariki me te whānau ki te marae, ki a Ngāti Pūkenga, ā, ki Tauranga Moana hoki. Ko ngā kaumātua e noho ana ki te marae, e whai wāhi atu ana ki ngā akoranga a te whānau me ngā mokopuna, ā, ka tino whakanuia aua whai wāhitanga. He tino aronga ki te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori o Ngāti Pūkenga me Tauranga Moana. Ko te whakawhanake i ngā tamariki ki tēnei horopaki, tētahi kaupapa matua.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te whakawhiti whakaaro a ngā tamariki mā te reo Māori, me te whai hoki i ngā tikanga Māori?

He pai te whakawhiti whakaaro a ngā tamariki i a rātou e whakarongo ana, e ako ana, e kōrero ana hoki i te reo Māori. He māoriori te whai atu a ngā tamariki i ngā tikanga Māori.

Mana Reo

Ka mārama ngā tamariki i te reo Māori, ā, ka whakamahi hoki rātou i te reo ki ētahi atu. He maha ā rātou whai wāhitanga i ngā wā ako ōkawa, ōpaki hoki, i ngā mahinga hoki, ki te kōrerorero ki ngā kaimahi me te whānau. Ka taea e ngā tamariki ngā pātai, ngā akiaki, me ngā tohutohu te whai atu. Ka hāngai hoki ēnei ki ngā karakia, ngā mihimihi, ngā pepeha, ngā waiata, me ngā kaupapa ako. Ka tohu āwhina, ka akiaki, ka ārahi hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki puta noa i te rā, ā, he nui te tūmanako kia whakamahi ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā tamariki i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori ki ā rātou mahinga, ā rātou taumahi hoki o ia rā.

He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao. He hononga ā-whakapapa tō te nuinga o ngā tamariki me te whānau, ā, ka whakamanahia ia tamaiti hei taonga. He manawanui, he atawhai hoki ngā kaimahi, ā, ka aro nui atu ki ngā tamariki. He riterite te tū o ngā mahinga mō ngā tamariki, puta noa i te rā. He mārama ki ngā tamariki ngā tūmanako e pā ana ki te whanonga pai, ā, he mauritau hoki te ū tonu a ngā kaimahi ki aua āhuatanga. He whānui ngā tūmomo pūmanawa, āhuatanga auaha hoki o ngā kaimahi, ā, ka whakamahia hei whakatairanga ake i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka tino ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki ngā waiata, te kapa haka, me te kori tinana. He pai ngā hononga o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hoa, ki ngā pākeke hoki ki te kōhanga reo.

He pakari, he tau hoki te āhua o ngā tamariki ki ngā horopaki Māori. Ka tino whakamahia te marae hei wāhi ako. Ka whai wāhi nui ngā kaumātua me te whānau ki te hāpai i te ako, te whakawhanaketanga hoki o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. Ka tino hāpaitia ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi, i tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki ngā kaupapa i te marae. Ka āta rongo atu ngā tamariki i te reo ōkawa me te reo ōpaki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore i te tino nui ngā rerekētanga, ngā āhuatanga auaha, ngā whiringa hoki ki te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki. He iti noa ngā taumahi e whai pānga ana ki ngā tūmomo reanga, kia āhei ai ngā tamariki ki te tūhura, te whakamātau, me te whai wāhi atu hoki ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero whakahihiri e whai pānga nui ana ki ā rātou mahi me ā rātou tākaro.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka kite ngā tamariki i ngā tino tauira o te whanaungatanga ki te kōhanga reo. E pou here ana te whanaungatanga i te āhua o ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau, ā, he aronga whakakotahi tā rātou ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga ki te kōhanga reo. Pūrongo ai i ngā āhuatanga matua o te whakahaeretanga i ngā hui o ia marama. Ka whāia ngā tukanga a te whānau mō te aroturuki, te arotake whaiaro, me te whakatau whakaritenga.

Ka whai hanganga ngā mahere hōtaka, ā, ka ārahi i ngā whakaritenga a ngā kaimahi. He tirohanga whānui o Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Ko Te Matatini te kaupapa o te hōtaka akoranga i tēnei wā. Ka tāutu te tirohanga whānui i ngā kaupapa whakaako, ā, ka whakamārama ngā mahere ā-wiki i ngā taumahi a ngā tamariki i ia rā.

Ka whakatakotohia, ka whakapuakihia anō hoki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki ki te taha o te whānau. Kei ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki tētahi kohinga o ngā taumahi me ngā whakaahua. He pārongo kōrero anō hoki mō te pepeha o ia tamaiti, me ētahi kōrerorero a ngā kaimahi.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Ko te whakawhanaketanga ngaio me te ako ngaio o ngā kaimahi, tētahi kaupapa matua. Ko ētahi wāhanga matua, ko ngā rautaki whakaako, ako hoki, ko te whakamahere i ngā hōtaka e hāngai ana ki ngā tūmomo reanga tamariki, ko te whakapai ake i ngā mahi aromatawai, ko te aromātai i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga, me te whakatinana i ngā pūmanawa me te taha auaha o ia kaimahi i roto i ā rātou mahi katoa ki te taha o ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga e whai ake nei, kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture, arā:

  • me āta whakarite i te mōhio o ngā pākeke e whakarato ana i te mātauranga me te atawhai, ki ngā akoako ohorere e tika ana, ā, kia whakatutuki hoki i aua mahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki i ia te wā [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008 HS8]
  • me āta whakarite i te whakatinanatanga o tētahi pūnaha arohaehae ka tū i ia te wā [R47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008 GMA7]
  • me āta whakarite i te noho haumaru o te wāhi mahi o waho, kia tika te rere atu o te wai me te āhua o te papa mō te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi. [R45 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008 PF13]

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, te whānau, me te poari ā-iwi ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakawhanake te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā hōtaka akoranga whakahihiri, hei whakawero, hei kapo atu, hei whakawhānui hoki i ngā whakaaro o ngā tamariki me ā rātou whakamahinga i te reo Māori
  • whakatinana te whānau me te rōpū tautoko i ngā rauemi me ngā kaupapa e tika ana mō tētahi hōtaka whakawhanaketanga ngaio ka āta whakamaheretia mā ngā kaimahi.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāpeke ki te marae o Te Whetū o te Rangi ki Welcome Bay, i Tauranga. He pakari, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo. He pai tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki ngā kaimahi, te whānau, me ngā kaumātua mā te reo Māori, ā, he māoriori tā rātou whai atu i ngā tikanga Māori. I ngā wā katoa, ka tautoko ngā kaumātua me te poari ā-iwi o Ngāti Pūkenga i ngā tamariki me te whānau. Tērā ka whakahaerehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāpeke i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

02 Paenga-whāwhā, 2015

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāpeke is located on Te Whetū o te Rangi Marae in Welcome Bay, Tauranga. Children are confident and comfortable in their kōhanga reo environment. They engage well with kaimahi, whānau and kaumātua through te reo Māori and follow tikanga Māori naturally. Ngāti Pūkenga Iwi Trust and kaumātua provide ongoing support for the children and whānau. ERO is likely to return in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāpeke is located on Te Whetū o te Rangi Marae in Welcome Bay, Tauranga and operates in the same building as Ngāti Pūkenga Iwi Trust. Children and whānau have close links to the marae, Ngāti Pūkenga and Tauranga Moana. Kaumātua, who reside on the marae contribute to whānau and mokopuna learning which is highly valued. There is a clear focus on te reo Māori and tikanga Māori pertaining to Ngāti Pūkenga and Tauranga Moana. Children’s development in this setting is a priority.

2 The Review Findings

How effectively do children communicate using te reo Māori and follow tikanga Māori?

Children communicate well as they listen, learn and speak te reo Māori. Children follow tikanga Māori naturally.

Mana Reo

Children understand and use te reo Māori with others. They have many opportunities in formal and informal learning times and during routines to talk with kaimahi and whānau. Children can respond to questions, prompts and directions. These include karakia, mihimihi, pepeha, waiata and kaupapa ako. Kaimahi prompt, encourage and guide children in their use of te reo Māori. Kaimahi maintain the use of te reo Māori throughout the day and have a high expectation of children to use te reo Māori. Children use te reo Maori and tīkanga Maori in their daily routines and activities.

Children are happy and secure in their environment. Many children and whānau are connected through whakapapa and each child is valued as a ‘taonga’. Kaimahi are patient, caring and responsive. Routines throughout the day are consistent and predictable for children. Expectations for behaviour are clear to children and positively maintained by kaimahi. Kaimahi have a range of talents and creativity that is used to enrich children’s learning. Children particularly enjoy waiata, kapa haka and kori tinana. Children have good relationships with their peers and adults at kōhanga reo.

Children appear confident and secure in Māori contexts. The marae is used effectively as a place for learning. Kaumātua and whānau have a valuable role in supporting children’s learning and development in te reo. Children are well supported by kaimahi when they participate in events at the marae. Children are exposed to the formal and informal language.

Key Next Steps

The children’s learning programme lacks variety, creativity and choice. There are limited age-appropriate activities where children can explore, experiment and engage in meaningful and interesting conversations about their work and play.

Planning and Evaluation

Children observe positive models of whanaungatanga at the kōhanga reo. Whanaungatanga underpins the management style of whānau who value an inclusive approach to managing kōhanga reo operations. There is regular reporting on key areas of operations at monthly hui. Whānau processes for monitoring, self review and decision-making are followed.

Programme planning is structured and guides kaimahi practice. There is an overview of Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Te Matatini is the current theme for the learning programme. The overview identifies teaching topics while weekly plans describe children’s daily activities.

Children’s learning is recorded and shared with whānau. Children’s profiles contain a collection of activities and photographs. These include information about each child’s pepeha and some anecdotal comments by kaimahi.

Key Next Steps

Professional development and learning for kaimahi is a priority. Key areas include teaching and learning techniques, planning age-appropriate programmes, improving assessment practices, evaluating the effectiveness of the learning programme, applying the individual talents and creativity of kaimahi in all their work with children.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

ERO identified the following areas of non-compliance:

  • ensure that adults providing education and care are familiar with relevant emergency drills and regularly carry this out with the children [R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008 HS8]
  • ensure that a system of regular appraisal is implemented [R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008 GMA7]
  • ensure that outdoor activity space is safe, well-drained and suitably surfaced for a variety of activities [R45 Education (ECS) Regulations 2008; Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008 PF13]

4 Recommendation

ERO, the kōhanga reo whānau and Iwi Trust developed the following recommendations:

  • whānau and kaimahi develop exciting learning programmes that challenge, engage and extend children’s thinking and use of te reo Māori
  • whānau and the support team resource and implement a well-planned professional development programme for kaimahi.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāpeke is located on Te Whetū o te Rangi Marae in Welcome Bay, Tauranga. Children are confident and comfortable in their kōhanga reo environment. They engage well with kaimahi, whānau and kaumātua through te reo Māori and follow tikanga Māori naturally. Ngāti Pūkenga Iwi Trust and kaumātua provide ongoing support for the children and whānau. ERO is likely to return in three years.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāpeke will be in three years.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori
