1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā te Kaiurungi Whakatūruki Arotake Māori i Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia ai i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai e te pūrongo a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i mua atu, me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

  • te Mana Tangata, tae atu ki te hōtaka akoranga, ngā hononga, me te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori
  • te whakamahere me te aromātai, tae atu ki te whakamahere hōtaka, te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, me te whakamahere rautaki
  • ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai i te wā o te arotake.

2 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Taringamotu ki te taha o te marae o Hia Kaitūpeka, ki waho noa i Taumarunui. I mua tata i te arotake o mua, i huri te kaiako o te kōhanga reo ki tua o te ārai, ā, kua roa nei tōna whai wāhi nui ki te kōhanga reo. I pā nui tōna ngarotanga atu ki te whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo. Ko te tautoko o te whānau, te purapura, ngā kuia, ngā koroua, me ngā ākonga o mua kua whai pānga nui ki te whakapakaritanga ake anō o ngā whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga ki te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā wāhanga katoa kāhore i te tutuki ngā herenga ā-ture, ā, i tāutuhia ai ki te pūrongo o mua, kua whakatutukihia i nāianei e te kōhanga reo.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Tangata

He Whakamārama

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i te poipoitanga o ō rātou matea ā-whatumanawa me ō rātou matea ako?

Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki e tino poipoia ana ō rātou matea ā-whatumanawa me ō rātou matea ako.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

E whai hua ana ngā tamariki nā te hōtaka akoranga i āta whakamaheretia ai. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki ngā tākaro e whakawero ana i a rātou ā-tinana, ā-hinengaro hoki. E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki, ā, ka whai wāhi atu ki ngā akoranga.

Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te aroha me te manaaki ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakamihi, ka akiaki hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā whakamātau a ngā tamariki. E whakapakari ana, e whakapūmau ana hoki ngā tātai whakapapa i ngā tino hononga. He tau te noho a ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

He pai te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. Ko te reo Māori te reo matua ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki hei akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whai ngā tamariki i ngā tohutohu, ā, ka pātai i ngā pātai māmā mā te reo Māori. E whakawhanake ana te pakari o ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti whakaaro mā te reo Māori.

Te whakamahere me te aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka whai hua ngā tukanga ā te whānau ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga ki te kōhanga reo. Kua arotakengia te tūtohinga. Kua whakahoungia te nuinga o ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga, ā, kei te arotake tonuhia ētahi atu. Kua mahi te whānau ki te taha o te purapura, ki te whakawhanake i tētahi mahere rautaki e mau nei i tētahi rārangi o ngā kaupapa matua a te katoa. He angamahi tēnei mahere mā te whānau, hei whakauruuru i ā rātou ake kaupapa matua mō te paetawhiti. Ka whakamātauhia, ka aroturukitia hoki te mahere rautaki e te whānau me te purapura.

He tukanga tā ngā kaimahi mō te whakamahere hōtaka me te aromatawai i te ako a ngā tamariki. Ka whai kiko ngā mahere ā-tau, ā-wāhanga hoki. He pūkete tā ia tamaiti, ā, he riterite te whakatakoto haere i ngā tohu ako. He pai te whakamōhio i te whānau mō te ako me te ahu whakamua o ā rātou tamariki. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā tētahi hōtaka i āta whakamaheretia ai, i āta whakaritea ai hoki.

He maha ngā whiringa mā te whānau, mō te ako i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo me te reo Māori. He māia ake, he pūmau ake hoki te whānau ki te hāpai i te kōhanga reo. Ka āta whakarite tētahi rōpū matua o ngā mema o te whānau i te whakatutukitanga o ngā take whakahaere tari, whakaritenga hoki. Ko te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki te aronga matua o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki ngā hui ā-whānau o ia marama. He pai te whakahaeretanga o ngā pūtea ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakahaere te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei tino painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore he tukanga e pā ana ki te aromātai i te hōtaka akoranga. Nā tēnei, he uaua ki te aroturuki i te whakatutukitanga o te hōtaka i ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki katoa.

Kāhore he tuhinga tūranga mahi ā te kaiwhakahaere me ngā kaiako. Ahakoa ka whakatutuki ngā kaimahi i ā rāua ake mahi, kāhore he whakamāramatanga hei ārahi i ā rāua mahi.

Kāhore anō ngā arohaehae kaimahi kia whakatutukihia. Nā tēnei, kāhore he tukanga hei tāutu i te whakawhanaketanga ngaio e tika ana, hei tautoko i ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki.

4 Ngā wāhanga kāore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture

He whakamārama

Kia tutuki ai te whānau o te kōhanga reo i ā rātou herenga ā-ture, me tahuri rātou ki te:

  • whakapau kaha ki te whakarato i te whakawhanaketanga ngaio, ngā whai wāhitanga whakawhanaketanga ngaio, me ngā rauemi ki ngā kaimahi i whakatūngia ai, me ngā tāngata e whai wāhi nui ana ki te kōhanga reo.
    (R47ECS2008/GM1-12)

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakawhanakehia tētahi tukanga ka aromātai i te hōtaka akoranga
  • whakaritea kia oti ai ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi a te kaiwhakahaere me ngā kaiako
  • whakatutukihia te arohaehae a ngā kaimahi katoa.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Taringamotu ki Taumarunui. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te aroha me te manaaki ki te kōhanga reo. Kua pai ake ētahi āhuatanga matua o ngā whakahaeretanga ki te kōhanga reo. E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki, e tino poipoia ana hoki rātou hei ākonga.

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of the Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of the kōhanga reo governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the previous ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • mana tangata including the learning programme, relationships and te reo Māori development
  • planning and evaluation including programme planning, whānau management and strategic planning
  • any other issues identified during the course of the review.

2 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Taringamotu sits alongside Hia Kaitūpeka Marae, just beyond Taumaranui. Prior to the previous review, the long serving kaiako of the kōhanga reo had passed away. This had a major affect on the management and operations of the kōhanga reo. Support from the whānau, local purapura, kuia, koroua and past students has contributed significantly to improving the management and operations of the kōhanga reo. The kōhanga reo has addressed all areas of non-compliance identified in the previous report.

3 Findings

Mana Tangata

Background

How well do children show that their emotional and learning needs are being nurtured?

Children demonstrate that their emotional and learning needs are well nurtured.

Areas of progress

Children benefit from a well-planned learning programme. They participate in play where they are challenged physically and mentally. Children have access to a variety of resources. Children are happy, settled and engaged in learning.

Children experience aroha and maanaki at kōhanga reo. Kaimahi praise and encourage children’s efforts. Whakapapa connections help to build and maintain close relationships. Children feel secure at kōhanga reo.

Children have a good understanding of te reo Māori. Te reo Māori is the dominant language at kōhanga reo. Kaimahi use a range of strategies to encourage the children to speak Maori. Children follow instructions and can ask simple questions in te reo Māori. Children are developing confidence to communicate in te reo Maori.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of progress

Whānau have useful processes to manage kōhanga reo operations. The charter has been reviewed. Most policies and procedures have been updated while others are under review. The whānau have worked with the purapura to develop a strategic plan with a list of collective priorities. This plan provides a framework for the whānau to include their specific long term priorities. The strategic plan will be trialled and monitored by whānau and purapura.

Kaimahi have a process for programme planning and assessing children’s learning. Annual and term plans are detailed. Children have individual profiles and evidence of learning is consistent. Whānau are well informed of their children’s learning and progress. Children benefit from a programme that is well planned and organised.

There are a number of options for whānau to learn about the kaupapa of Te Kōhanga Reo and te reo Māori. Whānau are more confident and committed to supporting the kōhanga reo. A core group of whānau members ensure that administration and operation matters are addressed. Children’s health and safety is the main focus of discussion at monthly whānau hui. Kōhanga reo finances are well managed. Whānau manage the kōhanga reo in the best interests of their children.

Key Next Steps

There is no process for evaluating the learning programme. Consequently it is difficult to monitor whether the programme is meeting the learning needs of all children.

There are no job descriptions for the kaiwhakahaere and kaiako. While both kaimahi carry out their respective duties, there are no reference points to guide their work.

Kaimahi appraisals have not been carried out. Consequently there is no process to identify what professional development is required to support kaimahi in their work with children.

4 Actions of non-compliance

Overview

In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements they need to:

  • ensure all reasonable steps are taken to provide staff employed or engaged in the service with adequate professional support, professional development opportunities, and resources.
    (R47ECS2008/GM1-12)

5 Recommendation

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendations that the whānau will:

  • develop a process to evaluate the learning programme
  • ensure job descriptions for kaiwhakahaere and kaiako are completed
  • carry out the appraisal of all kaimahi.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Taringamotu is in Taumarunui. Children experience aroha and maanaki at kōhanga reo. Key aspects of kōhanga reo operations have improved. Children are well supported and nurtured as learners.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Taringamotu will be in three years.
