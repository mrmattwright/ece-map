1 Te Aromātai i te Ratonga

He pēhea te tūnga o Te Pā Harakeke o Te Awahou ki te whakatairanga i te pai o ngā putanga ako mō ngā tamariki?

Kāhore i te pai te tūnga

Me whakawhanake ake

He pai te tūnga

He tino pai te tūnga

Ko ngā kitenga a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga e hāpai ana i te whānuitanga o tēnei whakataunga, kua whakarāpopotongia ki raro iho nei.

He Whakamārama

E te mokopuna, ko koe te manawanui o tōku ao

Ko Te Pā Harakeke o Te Awahou te whare kōhungahunga rumaki reo Māori anake ki Te Awahou. He ratonga rā katoa tēnei whare kōhungahunga, ā, kua raihanatia mō ngā tamariki 30, tae atu ki ngā tamariki 10 ki raro i te rua o ngā tau. He rōpū whakahaere pakari e ngākau nui ana ki te tirohanga, te tauāki taketake, me te tirohanga o Te Pā Harakeke. Kei te whare kōhungahunga nei tētahi kaiako mātauranga kōhungahunga i rehita tūturuhia ai, tētahi kaiāwhina wā-kikī, tētahi kaiwhakahaere tari, me tētahi kaitiaki papa ka whakapai hoki i te whare. He mātanga ā-pakihi, ā-mātauranga, ngaio hoki tēnei rōpū, ā, he mema hoki o te whānau, te hapū, me te iwi kei tēnei rōpū. Kei a rātou te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo mōhiotanga, mātauranga, pūmanawa hoki e kawe nei rātou ki te kaupapa. Ko te iti noa o te whai wāhi o te tuakiri Māori, te reo Māori, me te ahurea Māori ki tēnei hapori te tino pūtake o te whakahaumanutanga o te reo Māori.

Ka whakarato Te Pā Harakeke i tētahi taiao e poipoi ana, e manaaki ana i ngā tamariki, ‘hei kāinga mō ngā mokopuna Māori’. Ko tā Te Pā Harakeke whāinga, ko te whakahaumanu i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga mā te whakapuaki i ngā hōtaka akoranga rumaki reo Māori o te kounga kairangi. He wāteatea ngā taiao hihiri o roto, o waho hoki, ā, ka toro ngātahi hoki ki ngā ahurea. He whakahihiri, he manawa whakahī hoki tō ngā kaumātua ki te whai wāhi atu ki te whakawhanake tonutanga o Te Pā Harakeke.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

Ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o te kaupapa o Te Pā Harakeke. Ka āta whakaraupapa te tirohanga o te whare kōhungahunga i ngā putanga e wawatatia ana mō ngā tamariki. Ka hāngai ēnei ki te rumaki i ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga o tētahi taiao hihiri, e poipoi ana i a rātou, ā, kia noho matua ko te reo Māori hei reo whakawhiti whakaaro. Ko te mauritau, te manaaki, me te whakaute i roto i ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga e whai pānga anō hoki ki te whakawhanaketanga i te katoa o ia tamaiti. Ka ako wawe ngā tamariki i te whakahirahiratanga o ngā uaratanga matua, ngā waiaro matua, me ngā tikanga Māori. Ka whakatakoto aua āhuatanga i te tūāpapa mō ngā tamariki ki te mahi tahi, te tākaro tahi hoki mā ngā huarahi ngahau e whakakotahi ana i a rātou.

Ka whai wāhi atu, ka whakahihiritia, ka whakawerohia hoki ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao akoranga whakaongaonga. He pai te whakaritenga o te hōtaka o ia rā, ā, ka whakatutuki i te katoa o ngā rōpū reanga, me ngā ngākau nuitanga, ngā matea hoki o ngā tamariki. Ka whai pūtake ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā kaiako, ā, ka whai pānga anō hoki ki te ao o te tamaiti. Kei te tino mōhio ngā tamariki ki ā rātou mahinga. Ka ākina ngā tamariki ki te kawe i ā rātou akoranga, me te whakaaro nui hoki ki tō rātou waiora, ki te waiora hoki o ētahi atu. He harikoa ngā tamariki, ā, kei te tino whakawhanake hei ākonga māia, hei ākonga pākiki hoki.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. He pai tō rātou māramatanga ki te reo Māori. Ahakoa ka whakamahi ngā tamariki i te reo Pākehā, ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te reo Māori hei reo tohutohu, hei reo whakawhiti whakaaro hoki. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki ngā waiata me ngā mahi kori tinana. Ka ako rātou i ngā kupu mahi me ngā kīanga māmā i a rātou e tākaro ana, e taunekeneke ana hoki. Kei ngā tamariki ngā tūtohi me ngā kāri nui, muramura hoki hei hāpai i te whakapūmautanga o tā rātou whakamahi i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaiako i ngā whai wāhitanga katoa ki te whakawhiti kōrero, te patapatai, te akiaki, me te whai wāhi atu ki ngā tamariki mā te reo Māori. Ka whakamahia e ngā kaimahi te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki e whai hua ana ki te hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki.

Ka puāwai ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao ako e poipoi ana i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga o Ngāti Raukawa. He aronga ā-kaupapa ki ngā whakaakoranga me ngā akoranga, ā, ka ārahi tēnei i ngā mahere me te whakapuakitanga o ngā hōtaka akoranga. Kei te whakamahine haere ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau i tētahi marautanga i tuituia ai, ā, e ahu mai ana hoki i ngā angamahi kaupapa Māori e toru, pērā i Te Whāriki, te Whare Tapawhā, me te Kaupapa Matua. Ko te tuakiri, te reo, me te ahurea e hāngai mārika ana ki ngā hapū me te iwi o konei, i whakatinanahia ai ki aua tuhinga. Kua tino whakaritea, kua tino hangahangatia hoki te whare kōhungahunga kaupapa Māori nei, ā, ko tōna whāinga kia tū a Te Pā Harakeke o Te Awahou hei tino whare kōhungahunga rumaki reo Māori e hiahiatia ana ki tēnei takiwā.

Ka ako ngā tamariki mā te ngātahi me te tautoko, ā, e mōhio ana rātou ki ngā tūmanako kia pai tō rātou whanonga. He mātātoa te whakatairanga a ngā kaiako i ngā taunekeneke a te tuakana me te teina hei hāpai ake ai i ngā akoranga. Ka whakatauira ngā kaiako i aua uaratanga, ā, he maha hoki ngā wā ka whakaatu rātou ki ngā tamariki te whakaako a te teina i te tuakana. Ko te whakaaro nui ki te whanaungatanga e whai pānga nui ana hoki ki te kiritau, te waiora, me te tūmāia hoki o ngā tamariki.

Ko te kaha o te whanaungatanga me te ū pūmau ki te Kaupapa Matua e pou here ana i ngā mahi a te rōpū whakahaere me ngā kaimahi. Ka whakapuaki rātou i te ngākau nuitanga ki te whakawhanake tonutanga o te whare kōhungahunga. Ka ārahi te mātāpono, te tirohanga, me ngā wawata mō ngā tamariki i te ārahitanga rautaki, ngā whakaaro, me te whakatutukitanga o ngā mahi.

Kua whakapau kaha ngā kaiwhakahaere matua, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi ki te whakatakoto i ngā hononga ka whai hua i roto i te hapori o Te Awahou, ā, ki tua atu hoki. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi ki te hapori whānui. Ka kōkiri rātou i te whakatairanga ake o te reo, ngā tikanga, me te kaupapa Māori, ā, kua whai pānga pai tēnei i nāianei ki te mōhiotanga me te whai whakaaro o te hapori ki ngā āhuatanga Māori.

Ko te auau o te arotake whaiaro rautaki e whakamōhio ana i ngā mahi a te whare kōhungahunga, ki ngā wāhanga katoa o āna āhua mahi. He mārama ngā whakaaro huritao, ngā aromātai, me ngā mōhiohio arotake, ā, ka whai pūtake, kua āta tuhia hoki. He aronga nui ki te whai pānga me te whai huatanga o ngā hōtaka akoranga, me te ako me te ahu whakamua o ngā tamariki, tae atu ki tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ahakoa he hou te whare kōhungahunga, ka kitea ki ngā kaimahi me te whānau te āheinga ki te rōnaki i ngā whakaritenga o te kounga kairangi. E mōhio ana te whānau, ngā kaiwhakahaere matua me ngā kaimahi, nā te arotake whaiaro kaikini ka pupū ake hoki te paitanga ake o ngā putanga ki ngā tamariki, o ngā kawenga takohanga hoki ki te hunga whai pānga matua.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He maha ngā wā ka whakamahi ngā tamariki i te reo Pākehā i roto i ā rātou taunekeneke me ā rātou tākaro. Kua tāutu kētia e te whare kōhungahunga tēnei āhuatanga hei whakawhanake ake. Nō reira, he mea nui kia whai whakaaro ngā kaimahi ki ngā huarahi auaha ake, ka akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi mārika i te reo Māori.

He angamahi marautanga whakahihiri tā te whare kōhungahunga hei whakawhanake ake. E mōhio ana ngā kaiako, e tika ana kia whakamahine i te angamahi, kia tau ai te māramatanga me ngā hononga ki te tuitui haere o te marautanga. Ka hāpai tēnei i ngā kaiako ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga papai, me te whakariterite i ngā āhuatanga e whai pānga nui ana ki te ako me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

E mōhio ana ngā kaiako me tūhura tonu rātou i ngā hononga ki waenga i te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai. Kua tae ki te wā kia titiro rātou ki te pūtake me te tukanga e pā ana ki te tuhi i te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ko te whakamahi i ngā pūkete hei kapo i te huarahi ako o ia tamaiti tētahi āhuatanga hoki hei arotake.

Te Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia toro atu tonu te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Pā Harakeke ki ngā whakawhitinga ngaio me ngā ratonga mātauranga, hei whakatairanga i ngā whakaritenga, ā, hei whakakoia anō i te whanaungatanga hei tino āhuatanga motuhake o te whakawhanaketanga ā-kaupapa.

Te Whakatau a te Whakahaeretanga ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te ratonga ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā ratonga mātauranga kōhungahunga katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Background

E te mokopuna, ko koe te manawanui o tōku ao

Te Pā Harakeke o Te Awahou (Te Pā Harakeke) is the only te reo Māori immersion early childhood centre in Foxton. The centre offers a full day service and is licensed for 30 children including 10 under two years old. There is a strong management team dedicated to the vision, mission and philosophy of Te Pā Harakeke. The centre has one fully registered early childhood educator and another educator who is provisionally registered. Additional staff includes a registered primary teacher, a full time kaiāwhina, an administrator and a grounds person who is also the cleaner. The team are highly experienced business, educational and professional people who consist of whānau, hapū and iwi members. They each bring a vast range of knowledge, expertise and credibility to the kaupapa. The low profile of Māori identity, language and culture in the local community has been a key driver for the revitalisation of te reo Māori.

Te Pā Harakeke provides a nurturing and caring environment for children, “hei kāinga mō ngā mokopuna Māori – a place of learning for future generations”. Te Pā Harakeke aims to revitalise te reo Māori and tikanga through the delivery of high quality total immersion teaching and learning programmes. The indoor and outdoor environments are spacious, vibrant and culturally inclusive. Kaumātua are excited and proud to be part of the ongoing development of Te Pā Harakeke.

2 The Review Findings

Children are at the heart of the kaupapa of Te Pā Harakeke. The centre philosophy clearly outlines desired outcomes for children. These include immersing children in a rich, vibrant and nurturing environment where te reo Māori is the main language of communication. Positive, caring and respectful interactions and relationships contribute to the holistic development of each child. Children learn very early about the importance of key values, attitudes and tikanga Māori. These practices provide the foundations for children to work and play together in fun and inclusive ways.

Children are engaged, excited and challenged in a stimulating learning environment. The daily programme is well organised and caters for all age groups, children’s interests and needs. Conversations among children and teachers are meaningful and relevant to the child’s world. Children know their routines well. Children are encouraged to take responsibility for their learning and for the wellbeing of self and others. Children are happy and are developing well as confident, curious learners.

Children are immersed in te reo Māori. They have a good understanding of te reo Māori. While children may use English, staff and whānau consistently model te reo Māori as the language of instruction and communication. Children love waiata and kori tinana activities. They learn action words and simple phrases in their play and interactions. Children have large, bright charts and cards to help reinforce the use of te reo Māori. Teachers use every opportunity to converse, question, prompt and engage with children in te reo Māori. A variety of effective strategies are used by staff to support the development of children’s language.

Children thrive in a learning environment that fosters te reo Māori and tikanga pertaining to Ngāti Raukawa. A kaupapa-based approach to teaching and learning guides the planning and delivery of learning programmes. Staff and whānau members are refining an integrated and cohesive curriculum based on three kaupapa Māori frameworks which include Te Whāriki, Whare Tapawhā and Kaupapa Matua. These documents encapsulate the tribal identity, language and culture specific to the local hapū and iwi. The highly organised and structured kaupapa Māori centre aims to establish Te Pā Harakeke o Te Awahou as the preferred Māori immersion centre in the area.

Children learn in a cooperative, supportive setting and are aware of expectations for positive behaviour. Teachers actively promote tuakana, teina interactions to support learning. Teachers model these values and often reverse roles with children to show how tuakana can learn from teina. The significance of whanaungatanga impacts positively on children’s self esteem, wellbeing and confidence.

A strong sense of whanaungatanga and the belief in the Kaupapa Matua underpin the work of the management team and staff. They articulate a commitment to the ongoing development of the centre. The philosophy, vision and aspirations for children guide strategic leadership, thinking and action.

Managers, whānau and staff have worked hard to establish constructive relationships within and beyond the community of Foxton. Children take part in a variety of activities in the wider community. They lead the way in promoting te reo, tikanga and kaupapa Māori which has now begun to positively impact and influence community awareness and interest in things Māori.

Regular and strategic self review informs centre practices at all levels of performance. Reflections, evaluations and review information are clear, meaningful and well documented. There is a strong focus on the impact and effectiveness of learning programmes, and children’s learning, progress and development. While the centre is new, staff and whānau demonstrate the capacity to sustain high quality practice. Whānau, managers and staff recognise that critical self review leads to improved outcomes for children and accountability to key stakeholders.

Key Next Steps

Children often use English in their interactions and play. This is a self-identified area for development. Therefore, it is important that staff consider further innovative and creative ways to persuade and encourage children to use te reo Māori more readily.

The centre has an interesting curriculum framework that requires further development. Teaching staff are aware of the need to refine the framework to bring clarity and cohesion to the integrated nature of the curriculum. This should enable teachers to make good decisions and bring balance to what’s important for children’s learning and development.

Teachers acknowledge they need to continue to explore the connections between programme planning, assessment and evaluation. It is timely that they look at the purpose and process for documenting children’s learning and development. The use of profiles to capture the learning journey of the individual child is also an area for review.

Recommendation

ERO recommends that whānau and staff at Te Pa Harakeke continue to engage in professional exchanges with educational providers and services to enhance practice and to re-affirm whanaungatanga as a valued dimension of kaupapa-based development.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

During the review, ERO looked at the service’s systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Next Review

When is ERO likely to review the service again?

The next ERO review of Te Pā Harakeke o Te Awahou will be in three years.

3 General Information about Early Childhood Reviews

ERO’s Evaluation Framework

ERO’s overarching question for an early childhood education review is ‘How well placed is this service to promote positive learning outcomes for children?’ ERO focuses on the following factors as described in the bicultural framework Ngā Pou Here:

  • Pou Whakahaere – how the service determines its vision, philosophy and direction to ensure positive outcomes for children
  • Pou Ārahi – how leadership is enacted to enhance positive outcomes for children
  • Mātauranga – whose knowledge is valued and how the curriculum is designed to achieve positive outcomes for children
  • Tikanga whakaako – how approaches to teaching and learning respond to diversity and support positive outcomes for children.

Within these areas ERO considers the effectiveness of arotake – self review and of whanaungatanga – partnerships with parents and whānau.

ERO evaluates how well placed a service is to sustain good practice and make ongoing improvements for the benefit of all children at the service.

A focus for the government is that all children, especially priority learners, have an opportunity to benefit from quality early childhood education. ERO will report on how well each service promotes positive outcomes for all children, with a focus on children who are Māori, Pacific, have diverse needs, and are up to the age of two.

For more information about the framework and Ngā Pou Here refer to ERO’s Approach to Review in Early Childhood Services.

ERO’s Overall Judgement and Next Review

The overall judgement that ERO makes and the timing of the next review will depend on how well placed a service is to promote positive learning outcomes for children. The categories are:

  • Very well placed – The next ERO review in four years
  • Well placed – The next ERO review in three years
  • Requires further development – The next ERO review within two years
  • Not well placed - The next ERO review in consultation with the Ministry of Education

ERO has developed criteria for each category. These are available on ERO’s website.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews are tailored to each service’s context and performance, within the overarching review framework. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for children and useful to the service.
