Background

Little Wonders is a new, purpose-built centre that opened in 2011. It is one of 8 centres of the same name nationwide and the first to open in Timaru. The centre is licensed to have 75 children of mixed ages. Teachers provide education and care for children within programmes delivered from four separate rooms ‚Äì Matai, Tawa, Rimu and Kauri. This has helped build the children‚Äôs identity and sense of belonging. Teachers actively work to establish partnerships with parents to help understand children‚Äôs needs and preferences.

Children across the service have the opportunity to experience a range of activities within the four programmes. Teachers have had a focus on understanding children‚Äôs attitudes to learning and to establish the centres routines and expectations for behaviour.

The infants in the Matai room are well cared for. Teachers have a flexible programme so that they can provide care that fits in with each child‚Äôs home routines. When children reach the stage of needing to move between rooms they are supported by a well-planned process that helps them settle easily.

Teachers have been investigating ways to improve how their programmes and teaching practices support and extend children‚Äôs learning. They are aware that this is work in progress and that they need to establish useful processes to ensure all children have the opportunity to learn and be challenged by the programmes they provide. Teachers need to revisit the centre philosophy and build a shared understanding across the service about how this will be best demonstrated in practice.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Little Wonders Childcare (Timaru) completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities

  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.
