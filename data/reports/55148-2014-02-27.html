2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kia whāia ko te Ara-Poutama-a-Tane

Kia uhia mai nga taonga, tuku iho a nga tipuna

E tū ana te kōhanga reo o Te Whakaruruhau i Kaiti ki Tūranga-nui-a-kiwa. He taonga tino nui te kōhanga reo ki te hapori whānui tonu mō ōnā painga. Ko te nuinga o te whānau he uri rātou nō Ngāti Porou me Ngāti Oneone. E mārama ana te whanau ki ō rātou tūranga hei tangata whenua. Ka rangona te kaha whanaungatanga i waenganui i te whānau, ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki.

I kōwhiritia e te whānau Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira hei kaupapa arotake mā tēnei arotake. Kei te hiahia te whānau ki te mōhio e pēhea ana ā rātou tamariki ki te hāpai i ngā wāhanga rerekē o Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

E kaha tiaki ana te whānau i tētahi taiao ko te reo Māori anake te reo ka rangona. Kua tipu ake te kaiako me te kuia i roto i te reo Māori, ā, e mātau ana ngā kaimahi ki te reo Māori. E whakapiki ana te māia me te mātau o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. Mā ngā rautaki huhua ka kitea e tino ngākau nui ana ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo Māori.

E piki ana te manawareka o ngā tamariki, ā, ko ngā wheako hīkaka te take e pēnei ai. I ngā wā katoa ka torotoro atu rātou ki ngā wāhi i te hapori me te rohe, ā, e hāngai ana ēnei torotoro ki ngā kaupapa rerekē. Mā te whakarite i ēnei haerenga, kua whakanikotia te mātauranga o ngā tamariki mō ngā atua, te ao ō-mataora, te ao ō-ahupungao

E tū whakahīhī ana ngā tamariki ki te whakaputa i ō rātou pepeha me ō rātou whakapapa. He mea āwhina ēnei whakahaere hei whakanui i ngā tūhonohono o te whānau, ngā hapū me ngā iwi o ngā tamariki. Ka toro atu ngā tamariki ki ngā marae rerekē ki reira mātakitaki ai rātou i ngā tūranga rerekē o ngā kuia me ngā kaumātua. Ka kitea e rātou ngā kawa me ngā tikanga e hāngai ana ki tēnā marae me tēnā marae. He mea whakahirahira ēnei wheako e manaaki nei i te tuakiri me te tūrangawaewae o ngā tamariki.

Kua tautokotia pai ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi te ako, ā, kia tipu ora pai ai rātou. Ko te tamaiti te tino pūtake o ngā akoranga katoa. Kua āwhinatia e ngā tamariki te whakahaere i ā rātou anō me te mahi i tō rātou kōtahi. E whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki pakeke ki te whai atu i ngā wheako wero hinengaro, ā, he mea hīkaka ēnei wheako. E tino whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki ki ngā ngohe huhua hei whakanui ake i ā rātou akoranga me ō rātou mātauranga. Mā ēnei ngohe huhua e tino tere ana ngā tamariki ki te noho mauritau i te wā ka tae atu rātou ki te kura. Ka kōrero whakahīhī te whānau me ngā kaimahi mō ngā painga ka riro i ngā tamariki i te wā e ako ana rātou tae noa ki te wā e tākaro ana rātou.

Kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo e te whānau hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā Mahi Ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o Te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whakaruruhau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whakaruruhau. I whakaritea e te whānau ko āna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere i te māhere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • hōtaka whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

I kōwhiritia e te whānau Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira hei kaupapa arotahi mā tēnei arotake. E hiahia ana te whānau ki te mōhio e pēhea ana te whai painga o ā rātou tamariki ki te ako i ngā wāhanga rerekē o Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira i te wā e ako ana rātou tae noa ki te wā e whakatipu ana rātou.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Te whakapiki i te reo Māori o ngā tamariki.E piki ana te māia o ngā tamariki hei kaikōrero ki te reo Māori. E kamakama ana rātou ki te whai atu i ngā ngohe ōkawa mō te karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā mihi, ngā whakapapa me ngā pepeha. E whai wāhi ana ētahi o ngā tamariki ki te tātaki i ngā tikanga, ngā whakaritenga o te wā me te tīmata i ngā ngohe. E āhei ana rātou ki te whai i ngā tohutohu, ngā kōrero wero hinengaro me te whakahoki kōrero ki ngā kaimahi mā te whakaputa i ngā rerenga kōrero māmā noa. E tino whai wāhi ana ngā kōhungahunga ki te whai atu i ngā ngohe e pā ana ki te nekeneke, ngā mahi ā-ringa, te whai i ngā rangi o te oro me te ngē.

Ngā tauira o te reo:E noho rumaki ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao reo Māori e whai hua ana. Ka whakapā atu rātou i te wā i te wā ki ngā kaikōrero e whakatauira ana i te reo Māori. Tokotoru ngā kaimahi i tipu ake ai i roto i te reo Māori. Ko ēra atu o ngā kaimahi e mātau ana rātou ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. E whakahaere ana ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki whānui hei tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te mau i te reo Māori. Kua ākina ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi te ako i te reo Māori.

Ngā whakangungu a ngā kaimahi:E tino manawanui ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakanui ake i ō rātou ngaiotanga. Tokorua ngā kaimahi he Tohu Whakapakari ā rāua. E whai atu ana ēra atu o ngā kaimahi i te hōtaka whakangungu ka whakahaeretia e te Poari Matua o te Kōhanga Reo. Ka tae atu ngā kaimahi i te wā i te wā ki ngā hui o te purapura, ngā kura reo me ngā whakangungu ka whakahaeretia e te hapori. E whai hua ana ngā tamariki i te mea e whai wāhi atu ana ngā kaimahi ki ngā whakangungu hei whakanui ake i ō rātou pūkenga.

Te ao whānui:Kua piki ngā pūmanawa me ngā āheinga o ngā tamariki ki te whai whakaaro, ā, ko ngā wheako whakaihiihi te take e pēnei ai. I ngā wā katoa ka torotoro atu rātou ki ngā wāhi i te hapori me te rohe, ā, e hāngai ana ēnei torotoro ki ngā kaupapa rerekē. E whai hua ana ngā tamariki mā ēnei haerenga me ngā wheako, ā, he mea āwhina ēnei kaupapa kia taea e rātou te taunekeneke ki ētahi tāngata rerekē me te ako i ō rātou tūranga me ō rātou haepapa. Ko ngā wāhi whakahirahira kua tae atu rātou ko te taha moana, te pāmu, te whare pupuri taonga, ngā marae me ētahi atu o ngā kōhanga reo. Mā te whakahaere me te whakarite i ēnei taunekeneke me ēnei haerenga, kua whakanikotia te mātauranga o ngā tamariki mō ngā atua, te ao ō-mataora, te ao ō-ahupungao.

Te hōtaka mā ngā tuakana:E wero ana ngā kaimahi i te hinengaro o ngā tamariki pakeke, ā, he hīkaka ngā rautaki ka whakahaeretia. E whai wāhi ana ēnei tamariki ki ngā ngohe huhua hei āwhina i a rātou ki te tuhi i ō rātou ingoa, te tāutu i ngā pū me te whiriwhiri i ngā ariā mō te tau me ngā ariā e pā ana ki te pūtaiao. Mā ēnei ngohe huhua e tino tere ana ngā tamariki ki te noho mauritau ki te kura. Ka kōrero whakahīhī te whānau me ngā kaimahi mō ngā painga ka riro i ngā tamariki i ngā wā ka ako rātou me ngā wā ka tākaro rātou.

Te hōtaka akoranga mā ngā tamariki:He mea tōtika te hōtaka whakahaere o ia rā, ā, e rere ngāwari ana ngā whakahaere. E whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki ki te uru ki ngā kaupapa rerekē, arā, ngā akoranga mō te tamaiti ki te mahi i tōna kōtahi, ngā tākaro mō ngā rōpū nui me ngā rōpū iti, tae noa ki ngā kemū ka whakahaeretia e ngā pakeke. E wātea ana rātou ki te whakawhiti mai i ngā wāhi ō-waho, ngā wāhi ō-roto me ngā akoranga rerekē kua whakaritea. Ka tautoko ngā tamariki pakeke i ā rātou hoa kōhungahunga kia mōhio ai rātou i te takamahi o ia rā, ā, e whai wāhi atu ana ngā kōhungahunga ki te tākaro ki te taha o ngā pakeke. E rere ngāwari ana ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā, ā, e waia ana ngā tamariki ki ēnei whakahaere. E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki te uru ki ngā ngohe e whakanui ai i ō rātou auahatanga, ō rātou tipuranga ā-tinana, ō rātou mātauranga mō te pāngarau me te pānui, me ō rātou pūkenga e pā ana ki te pūtaiao me te hāngarau. E whai wheako angitū rātou i te wā ka ako rātou me te whai atu i ō rātou hiahia.

Te whaiora o ngā tamariki:E whakatauira ana ngā kaimahi i ngā uara whaitake, arā, te aroha, te manaaki, te tiaki me te whai koha o tētahi ki tētahi. E ōrite ana te whanonga o ngā tamariki pakeke i te wā e mahi tahi ana rātou ki ā rātou hoa. He mea tautoko te āhua o ngā kaimahi i te wā e arahi ana rātou i ngā tamariki. Mā ngā tamariki e noho āwangawanga kua awhiawhitia rātou. E noho mauritau ngā tamariki, ā, e māia ana rātou ki te whakapiri ki a rātou anō i te koanga ngākau i te wā e tākaro ana rātou. E whakahaere ana ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā rautaki huhua hei tautoko i te whaiora ā-tinana me te whaiora ā-whatumanawa o ngā tamariki.

Te tuakiri me te mana motuhake o tēnā me tēnā.E tū whakahīhī ana ngā tamariki i te wā ka whakaputa rātou i ō rātou pepeha me ō rātou whakapapa. Mā ēnei wheako kua whakanuia ngā tūhonohono i waenganui i te whānau, ngā hapū me ngā iwi o ngā tamariki. Ka toro atu ngā tamariki ki ngā marae ki reira kite atu ai ngā kuia me ngā kaumātua e hāpai ana i ō rātou tūranga. Ka mātakitaki rātou i te kawa me ngā tikanga e hāngai ana ki tēnā marae me tēnā marae. E tino waia ana ngā tamariki ki ngā mahi o ngā mātua tūpuna, ō rātou tuakiri me ō rātou tūrangawaewae.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamāherehere me te aromātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo hei whakatau i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamāherehere, ngā whakahaere me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga reo mā a rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka āta hāngai te aromātai nei ki te hōtaka whakamāherehere me te aroturuki i te tipuranga ako o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Te ū o te whānau:E whai hua ana ngā tamariki mā te tautoko ā ngā mātua. E tino tautoko ana te whānau i te kōhanga reo me tōna tino kaupapa. Ka kaha tautoko rātou i te kōhanga reo, ā, he whai hua te āhua o tā rātou tautoko. Ka tautoko te whānau i ngā haerenga, ngā hui nui me ngā whakahaere rerekē i te kōhanga reo. E tino ngākau nui ana ngā kaimahi ki ngā mātua o te whānau me ā rātou āwhina, ā, e tino harikoa ana rātou i te wā ka noho ngā mātua hei kaiāwhina.

E tīmata ana ngā mātua o te whānau ki te mārama i te hua me te kaupapa nui ki te ako me te whakaputa i te reo Māori. Kātahi ka tīmata ētahi o ngā mātua o te whānau ki te haere ki ngā akoranga mō te reo Māori, ā, kātahi ētahi atu o ngā mātua ka oti i ngā akoranga mō te reo Māori. He tokoiti noa ngā mātua o te whānau e tino mātau ana ki te reo Māori. Ka kōrerotia te reo Māori e ēnei mātua. He tauira whaihua rātou ki te whakatauira i te reo Māori ki te whānau whānui.

E whakapapa ana te nuinga o ngā mātua o te whānau ki Ngāti Porou me Ngāti Oneone. E mārama ana rātou ki ō rātou tūranga hei tangata whenua. Ka pōwhiri te whānau i ngā manuhiri ki te kōhanga reo. Ka rangona te kaha whanaungatanga i waenganui i te whānau, ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki.

Ngā tukanga whakahaere:E whakahaere ana te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Kua whakatūria te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā. He whai tikanga me te whai hua te āhua o ngā whakahaere pūtea. Kua hokona e rātou ngā rauemi mātauranga kia kounga nui ai te āhua o ngā rauemi mā ngā tamariki.E ora pai ana te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo.

E whai tikanga ana te whānau mō ngā take e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi, ā, e pai ana ngā whakahaere. Kua whakatūria e te whānau ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā hei tautoko i a rātou ki te hāpai i ō rātou haepapa mō te whakahaere i nga kaimahi. Kua whakaritea ngā pūkete whakawhiwhi mahi mō ngā kaimahi katoa. E ū ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakapakari ake i ō rātou mātauranga ā- ngaio. Ka tae atu rātou ki ngā wānanga hei tautoko i a rātou anō ki te pīkau i ō rātou tūranga whakaako. Kua whakahaeretia ngā aromātai mō ngā kaimahi, ā, kei roto i ngā aromātai tētahi wāhanga e pā ana ki ngā whāinga whakangungu mō tēnā kaimahi me tēnā kaimahi. E hāngai ana ēnei whāinga ki te reo Māori, te whakahaere aromatawai me te hōtaka aromātai.

Ka kaha aro te whānau ki ngā take katoa e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru kia pai ai te noho a ngā tamariki. He whānui, ā, he hōhonu ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā ā rātou hei arahi i a rātou ki te hāpai i ō rātou haepapa e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. E whakahaere ana ngā kaimahi me te whānau i tētahi taiao whaiora ā-tinana me tētahi taiao whaiora ā-whatumanawa mā ngā tamariki.

Te arotake whaiaro:He tōtika, ā, he whai hua te āhua o ngā tukanga mō te arotake whaiaro.Ka riro i a rātou ngā pūrongo i te wā i te wā i ngā hui ā-whānau kia mōhio ai rātou i ngā whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. He hōhonu rawa ngā arotake whaiaro ka whakahaeretia i ngā wā katoa hei painga mō te whakatipuranga me ngā akoranga mā ngā tamariki.

Te whakamāherehere:E whakahaeretia ana e ngā kaimahi tētahi anga tōtika i te wā ka whakatakoto rātou i ngā māhere mā ngā tamariki. Kei roto i ngā māhere whāroa me ngā māhere poto ngā kōrero hōhonu hei arahi i ngā kaimahi ki te hāpai i ngā kaupapa ki ngā tamariki. E whakatutuki ana ngā māhere i ngā matea rerekē me ngā tau rerekē o ngā tamariki. E hāngai ana ngā kaupapa ki te reo Māori, ā, e hono tōtika ana ngā kaupapa ki Te Whāriki. E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki ngā wheako ā-kaupapa kua whakamāhereheretia.

Te aromatawai:E tino mārama ana ngā kaimahi ki te pūtake me ngā painga o te aromatawai. E whakahaeretia ana e rātou ngā rautaki aromatawai huhua ka hāpaitia e tēnā wāhi mātauranga me tēnā wāhi mātauranga. He whakarongo ki ngā kōrero a ngā mātua o te whānau, te mātakitaki me tētahi rārangi whakatutuki e hāngai ana ki Te Whāriki ētahi o ēnei rautaki aromatawai.

E tīmata ana ngā kaimahi inaiānei ki te whakahaere i tētahi tukanga e tāutu ai ngā akoranga mō ngā rangi ā meāke nei. E āhei ana ngā mātua o te whānau ki te tiki atu i ngā mōhiohio mō tā rātou tamaiti i te wā e hiahiatia ana e rātou.

Te aromātai:E ū ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakarerekē i ngā wahanga kāore i te whai hua kia pai ake ai ngā painga mā ā rātou tamariki. Ka aromātai rātou i te wā i te wā i ngā wahanga rerekē o te hōtaka o ia rā. Mā tēnei ka whakarerekē ngā kaimahi i ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā, ngā kaupapa, te reo Māori, ngā haerenga me ngā rauemi mā ngā tamariki. Ko te aromātai te take e pai ake ana ngā putanga hua mā ngā tamariki.

Ngā wahanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te whakatakoto i te māhere rautaki:Kāore e kitea tētahi māhere whakatutuki kua tuhia e whakaatu ai e pēhea ana te whānau whakatutuki i ō rātou whāinga rautaki. Me tāutu, ā, me tuhi te whānau i ngā tūmahi hei whakatutuki mō ia whāinga, ā, me kitea ‘ko wai’, ‘he aha’, me te ‘ā hea’ mō ia tūmahi. Mā te aroturuki me te tuku pūrongo mō ngā paetae ka puta me ngā paetae angitū e hāngai ana ki ngā whāinga ā-tau ka whai take ngā mōhiohio arotake whaiaro mā ngā whakamāherehere mō ngā rangi e tū mai nei.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

He whakamārama

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whakaruruhau tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga a te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

6 Ngā Tūtohunga

E tūtohu ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ki te whānau ki te whakatakoto ā-tuhi i tā rātou māhere whakatutuki kia whai mana ai tō rātou māhere rautaki.

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whakaruruhau is in Kaiti, Gisborne. The kōhanga reo is valued for its contribution to the local community. Most of the whānau affiliate to Ngāti Porou and Ngāti Oneone. Whānau understand their roles as tangata whenua. There is a strong sense of whanaungatanga among whānau, kaimahi and children.

The whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus area for the review. Whānau are interested in how well their children reflect the different dimensions of ngā taumata whakahirahira.

The whānau maintains a high quality Māori immersion environment. Kaiako and kuia are native speakers of te reo Māori and kaimahi are competent speakers of te reo Māori. Children are developing well as confident speakers of te reo Māori. Children are motivated to learn te reo Māori.

Children’s natural curiosities are developed and extended in stimulating ways. They often go to places in the local and wider community associated with different kaupapa. Children’s knowledge of ngā atua and the natural and physical worlds are heightened through these interactions and outings.

Children stand proudly when they recite their pepeha and whakapapa. These practices help affirm children’s whānau, hapū and iwi links. Children visit different marae where they observe kuia and kaumatua in their traditional roles. They observe the kawa and tikanga specific to that marae. Children’s sense of identity and turangawawae is affirmed in positive ways.

Kaimahi support children well in their learning and development. The programme is child focused. Children are encouraged to be self managing and independent. There are opportunities for the older children to be challenged and extended in stimulating ways. Children are able to participate in various activities that advance their education. Several of these activities help children transition into kura in a smooth way. Whānau and kaimahi speak proudly about the learning gains children make as they learn and play.

Whānau manage the kōhanga reo in the best interests of children.

4 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

The whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus area for the review. Whānau are interested in how well their children reflect the different dimensions of ngā taumata whakahirahira in their learning and development.

Areas of good performance

Children’s te reo Māoridevelopment. Children are developing confidence as speakers of te reo Māori. They eagerly participate in formal activities that include karakia, himene, waiata, mihi, whakapapa and pepeha. Some children have opportunities to lead rituals and protocols or initiate activities. They are able to follow complex instructions and conversations and respond to kaimahi in simple phrases. The younger children confidently participate in activities that include movement, action, rhythm and noise.

Language models: Children are immersed in a purposeful te reo Māori environment. They have regular exposure to a range of language models. Three kaimahi are native speakers of te reo Māori. The remaining kaimahi are fluent and confidently speak te reo Māori. Kaimahi use various language strategies to support children’s te reo Māori development. Children are motivated to learn te reo Māori.

Kaimahi learning: There is a strong commitment by kaimahi to improve their professional knowledge. Two kaimahi have Tohu Whakapakari. The remaining kaimahi are in TKRNT training programmes. Kaimahi regularly attend purapura meetings, kura reo and locally organised community courses. Children benefit from ongoing training provided for kaimahi.

Wider world: Children’s natural curiosities are developed and extended in stimulating ways. They often go to places in the local and wider community associated with different kaupapa. Children benefit from these outings and opportunities which enable them to interact with various people and learn about their roles and responsibilities. Some places of interest have included the beach, farm, museum, marae and other kōhanga reo. Children’s knowledge of ngā atua and the natural and physical worlds are heightened through these interactions and outings.

Tuakana programme: Kaimahi challenge and extend the older children in stimulating ways. These children have opportunities to participate in various activities that promote name writing, letter identification, and number and science concepts. Several of these activities help children transition into kura in a smooth way. Whānau and kaimahi speak proudly about the learning gains children make as the play and learn.

Children’s learning programme: The daily programme has structure, balance and flows smoothly. Children have opportunities to participate in different kaupapa, independent learning, large and small group play and adult-led games. They can move freely between the indoor and outdoor areas and different learning activities. The older children support their younger peers with routines and include them in their activities. Routines and transitions flow smoothly and are familiar to children. Children have opportunities to participate in activities that promote creativity, physical growth, numeracy and literacy, scientific skills and technology. They experience positive outcomes as they learn and follow their interests.

Children’s wellbeing: Kaimahi model positive values including aroha, manaaki, tiaki, kindness and respect. The older children model similar behaviours when they are with their peers. Kaimahi guide and manage children in supportive ways. Unsettled children are comforted and reassured in positive ways. Children are self assured and confidently approach each other as they play. Kaimahi and whānau implement a number of measures to support children’s health and emotional wellbeing.

Sense of identity and belonging: Children stand proudly when they recite their pepeha and whakapapa. These practices help affirm children’s whānau, hapū and iwi links. Children visit different marae where they observe kuia and kaumatua in their traditional roles. They observe the kawa and tikanga specific to that marae. Children are familiar with their heritage, identity and turangawaewae.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of the kōhanga reo operations. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau commitment: Children benefit from whānau input. Whānau are fully supportive of the kōhanga reo and its unique philosophy. They assist at the kōhanga reo in a variety of purposeful ways. Whānau help with outings, special events and different activities at the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi appreciate the help whānau give especially when they act as parent helpers.

Whānau are beginning to understand the value and importance of learning and using te reo Māori. Some whānau members have begun to attend te reo Māori classes while others have just completed similar programmes. There are a small number of whānau members who are fluent speakers of te reo Māori. These whānau members readily speak te reo Māori. They are effective role models for the wider whānau.

Most of the whānau affiliate to Ngāti Porou and Ngāti Oneone. They understand their roles as tangata whenua. Whānau welcome visitors to the kōhanga reo. There is a strong sense of whanaungatanga among whānau, kaimahi and children.

Management practices: Whānau manage the kōhanga reo in the best interests of children. The charter and policies with procedures are in order.The whānau manage their finances effectively. They have purchased education resources to help make sure children have high quality resources. The kōhanga reo is in a financially stable position.

Whānau manage staffing matters well. Whānau have developed several polices with procedures to assist them with their staffing responsibilities. All personnel records are in place. Kaimahi are committed to improving their professional knowledge. They are involved in various courses to assist them in their teaching roles. Appraisals have been undertaken and include kaimahi professional development goals. These goals include te reo Māori, assessment and programme evaluation.

Whānau take all reasonable steps to make sure the kōhanga reo is a healthy and safe place for children. They have a comprehensive set of policies with procedures to guide them with their health and safety responsibilities. Whānau and kaimahi are committed to providing children with a safe physical and emotional environment.

Self review: Whānau have an efficient process for self review.They receive regular reports at whānau hui that inform them of kōhanga reo operations. Regular self review is thorough and benefits children’s learning and development.

Planning: Kaimahi use a sound framework when they plan kaupapa for children. Long and short term plans include the necessary detail to guide kaimahi when they deliver kaupapa to children. Plans cater for the special characteristics of the various age groups of children. Kaupapa focus on te reo Māori and have clear links to Te Whariki. Children experience well planned kaupapa that motive them to learn.

Assessment: Kaimahi understand the value and purpose of assessment. They use various assessment tools which are widely used in different educational settings. Some of these tools include whānau feedback, observations and a Te Whariki checklist. Children’s interest and strengths have emerged from these different approaches.

Kaimahi are now in the process of identifying children’s next learning steps. Whānau can access assessment information about their child when required.

Evaluation: Kaimahi are committed to bringing about positive change for their children. They regularly evaluate different aspects of the children’s daily programme. This often leads to kaimahi making modifications to children’s routines, the physical environment, kaupapa, te reo Māori, outings and resource provisions. Evaluation leads to improved outcomes for children.

Areas for development

Strategic planning: There is no documented action plan that shows how whānau will achieve their strategic goals. The whānau should identify and document the actions required for each goal and include ‘who’, ‘what’, and ‘when’ for each task. Monitoring and reporting progress and successes against annual priorities should provide useful self review information for future planning.

Overview

  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

General Information About Reviews

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
