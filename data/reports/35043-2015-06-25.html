1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangimarie ki Melville, ki Kirikiriroa. Koia nei te kōhanga reo tuatahi i whakatūngia ai ki te rohe o Tainui. Ka whakahaerehia te kōhanga reo ki tētahi whare mahana, he iti anō hoki, ā, e āta tiakina ana, kua whakahoungia hoki hei whakatutuki i ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki. He mātua rangatahi ngā kaimahi o tēnei kōhanga reo, ā, ka ngākau nui rātou ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, me te manaaki i ā rātou tamariki ki tētahi tino taiao akoranga.

I te tau 2012, i tāutu te pūrongo a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te maha o ngā whakawhanaketanga papai ki te kōhanga reo. I hāngai aua āhuatanga ki te reo Māori, ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga, me te waiora o ngā tamariki. Ko te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, te whakamahere rautaki, ngā mahi aromatawai, te whakamahere hōtaka me ngā aromātai ētahi atu āhuatanga pakari. I taunaki te pūrongo kia whakapai ake te whānau i ā rātou whakaritenga e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro.

E pou here ana te whanaungatanga i te katoa o ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga mō ngā akoranga ki te kōhanga reo. He mārama ki te whānau te tirohanga me ngā kaupapa matua mō meāke nei. E arotahi ana rātou ki te tupu tonu me te whakapai tonu. He pārekareka te ako a ngā tamariki, ā, e whakawhanake ana hoki tō rātou tūmāia ki te kōrero ake i te reo Māori i tā rātou noho ki te kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te poipoi, te tautoko hoki i ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo?

E tino tautokona ana, e tino poipoia ana hoki ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo. E pou here ana te whanaungatanga i ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga katoa i roto i ngā akoranga. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā taunekeneke e atawhai ana, e whakakoia ana hoki. He nui ngā mihi me ngā kōrero akiaki ki a rātou. Ka tautokona ngā tamariki hei whakawhanake i tā rātou ake kōkiri i ngā mahi pērā i te haere ki te whare paku me te whakamau kākahu. Whiwhi ai rātou i ngā tohutohu mārama puta noa i te hōtaka. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā kōrero mārie, ngāwari hoki i a rātou e kōrero ana ki ngā tamariki. Ka āta whakaatuhia ngā mahi a ngā tamariki, ā, he ātaahua hoki te whakatakotoranga ki ngā pūkete hei pānui mā ngā mātua. He mārama ngā mahinga, ā, he māmā ki ngā tamariki te whai atu. Ka whakatutukihia ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ia tamaiti.

Kei te pakari haere te āheinga o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. E āhei ana rātou ki te whakautu i ngā mihi me ngā tohutohu māmā. Ka mārama rātou ki tēnā e kōrerohia ana e ngā pākeke. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā waiata me ngā kēmu i roto i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa, puta noa i te rā. E mōhio ana rātou, me whakapā atu rātou ki ētahi atu mahi whakangungu, hei whakawhānui, hei whakatairanga hoki i ō rātou ake mōhiotanga.

Te Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

I ētahi wā he taimaha ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā tamariki. Kāhore ngā tamariki e tino mōhio ana ki ngā āhuatanga ka puta ake inā kāhore i te pai tō rātou whanonga. Kāhore anō ngā kaimahi kia whakarite i ngā rautaki ka whakahaere tōtika i te whanonga o ngā tamariki.

Ka whai hua ngā kaimahi mā ētahi atu whakawhanaketanga, me ētahi atu whakangungu i roto i te reo Māori. Me whiwhi tautoko hei tāutu i ngā wā e whakamahi ana ngā kaimahi i ngā hanganga reo e hē ana, hei āta whakarite i te whiwhinga o ngā tamariki i te whakatakotoranga reo me ngā kupu e tika ana i roto i te reo Māori. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te tika ake o ngā kaimahi me te whānau i roto i te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka āta whakamaheretia, ka āta aroturukitia te ako me te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki. Ka whai pūtake ngā taumahi ako i whakamaheretia ai, ā, he whakaongaonga, e ahu mai ana hoki i ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. He māramatanga ngātahi tō ngā kaimahi ki ngā mahere, te whakapuakitanga, me te whakahaeretanga whānui i te hōtaka. Ka whakamahere ngātahi rātou, ā, ka whai wā anō hoki ki te whakaaro huritao mō te hōtaka. Ka whai whakaaro rātou ki tēnā e ngākau nuitia ana e ngā tamariki, me ngā āhuatanga i whai hua ai i mua. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki aromatawai papai hei kapo atu i te ako a ngā tamariki me ā rātou ekenga angitu. Kei te noho mōhio, kei te noho mārama hoki ngā kaimahi ki ā rātou tamariki. He pai te whakamōhio i te whānau mō ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki ngā akoranga a ā rātou tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Ka poipoia, ka hāpaitia hoki ngā pūmanawa ake o ia tamaiti.

He mārama ki te whānau te tirohanga me ngā kaupapa matua mō te whakawhanaketanga o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei. Kua tāutuhia e rātou te whānuitanga o ngā whāinga, mā te whakamahi i Te Ara Tūāpae. Ka hāngai ēnā ki tētahi whare hou me te whakawhanake i te āheinga o te whānau.

Ka tāutu te tūtohinga i te māramatanga o ngā wawata me ngā tūmanako o te whānau. He mōhiohio papai hoki kei roto hei ārahi i te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. Kei te mārama te whānau ki te take ka whai pānga nui te tū auau o ngā hui, te haere tonutanga o te arotake whaiaro, me te whakamahere hoki mō meāke nei. Kua āta whakaritea ngā mahi arotake whaiaro, ā, he pai te pūrongo atu ki te whānau. He pai te whakamōhio i te whānau mō ngā āhuatanga hei aroturuki ake, hei whakapai ake. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te whai pānga o te whakahaeretanga ki ō rātou matea.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangimarie ki te hapori o Melville, ki Kirikiriroa. Ka whakahaerehia te kōhanga reo ki tētahi whare mahana, he iti anō hoki, ā, e āta tiakina ana. Ka tino tautokona, ka tino poipoia hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakatutukihia ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ia tamaiti, ā, kei te pakari haere tō rātou tūmāia ki tētahi taiao reo Māori.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangimarie i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Rangimarie is in Melville, Hamilton. This was the first kōhanga reo to be established in the Tainui district. The kōhanga reo operates from a small, cosy, well maintained house that has undergone modification to cater for the learning needs of children. The kaimahi at this kōhanga reo are young parents who are committed to the kaupapa of Te Kōhanga Reo and caring for their children in a positive learning environment.

The ERO report of 2012 identified a number positive development in the kōhanga reo. These included te reo Māori, interactions and relationships and children’s wellbeing. Whānau management, strategic planning, assessment practices, programme planning and evaluations were additional areas of strength. The report recommended that whānau improve their self review practices.

Whanaungatanga underpins all interactions and relationships for learning at the kōhanga reo. Whānau are clear about the vision and priorities for the future. They are focused on continual growth and improvement. Children enjoy learning and are developing confidence to speak te reo Māori more during their time in the kōhanga reo.

2 The Review Findings

How well are children nurtured and supported in their kōhanga reo?

Children are well supported and nurtured in their kōhanga reo.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are happy and settled in their kōhanga reo environment. Whanaungatanga underpins all interactions and relationships for learning. Children experience warm and affirming interactions. They receive lots of praise and encouragement. Children are supported to develop independence in activities such as toileting and getting dressed. They regularly receive clear instructions throughout the programme. Kaimahi use calm and soft tones when speaking to children. Children’s work is prominently displayed and presented attractively in folders for parents to read. Routines are clear and children follow these easily. Children’s individual needs and interests are catered for.

Children are becoming confident in their ability to speak te reo Māori. They are able to respond to basic greetings and instructions. They understand what adults are saying to them. Children enjoy waiata and games in te reo Māori. Kaimahi use te reo Māori consistently throughout the day. They acknowledge that they need to access further training to extend and enrich their own knowledge.

Key Next Steps

At times interactions between children are strained. Children are not aware of the consequences of their negative behaviour. Kaimahi have not established strategies for managing children’s behaviour appropriately.

Kaimahi would benefit from additional development and training in te reo Māori. Support to identify when kaimahi are using incorrect language structures is necessary to ensure that children receive correct te reo Māori grammar and vocabulary. Children will benefit from increased kaimahi and whānau accuracy in te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s learning and progress is well planned and monitored. Planned learning activities are meaningful, stimulating and based on children’s interests. Kaimahi have a shared understanding about programme plans, its delivery and overall management. They plan together and spend time reflecting on the programme. They consider what children are interested in and what has worked well in the past. Kaimahi use good assessment methods to capture children’s learning and their successes. Kaimahi know and understand their children. Whānau are well informed of their children’s learning while at kōhanga reo. Children’s individual talents are nurtured and supported.

Whānau are clear about the vision and priorities for the future development of the kōhanga reo. They have identified a range of goals using Te Ara Tūāpae. These include a new building and developing whānau capability.

The charter identifies clear aspirations and expectations of whānau. It includes good information to guide whānau management of the kōhanga reo. Whānau members understand the need for regular meetings, ongoing self review and planning for the future. Self review practices are well established, with good reporting to whānau. Whānau are well informed about areas that require monitoring and improvement. Children benefit from management that is based on their needs.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Rangimarie is based in the suburb of Melville, Hamilton. The kōhanga reo operates from a small, cosy, well maintained house. Children are well supported and nurtured at kōhanga reo. Children’s individual needs and interests are catered for and they are becoming confident in a te reo Māori environment.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangimarie will be in three years.
