1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangiora ki te tāone o Inglewood, ki Taranaki.

I whakawhanakehia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau te pātai aromātai e whai ake nei, hei hāpai i te aronga o te arotake, arā:

‘He pēhea te whakaatu a ngā tamariki i tō rātou mauritau, tō rātou harikoa hoki?’

He tino mauritau, he tino harikoa hoki ngā tamariki ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo. Ka kitea tō rātou ngākau nui ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako ako reo hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o tō rātou reo. I ngā wā katoa, ka tautoko, ka ārahi hoki ngā kuia me ngā kaiako i ngā tamariki i roto i te reo Māori. Ka iti haere te ako a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori i te wā ko ētahi noa o te whānau e whakamahia ana te reo Māori. E mōhio ana te whānau, koia nei tētahi āhuatanga hei whakapakari ake.

Kua āta whakawhanakehia te whakamahere hōtaka, ā, ka whakahāngaitia anō hoki ki te whakapuakitanga o te hōtaka me ngā whakaritenga. He pai te noho rite o ngā kaimahi, ā, ka kitea ki ā rātou whakaritenga Ka āta tohu te aromatawai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Kua oti kētia ngā pūkete te whakarite, ā, ka whai hua hoki ngā mātua. E āhei ana rātou ki te titiro ki ngā mōhiohio me te kōrero ki ngā kaimahi mō ngā akoranga a ā rātou tamariki.

E tino tautokona ana ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki nā te pai o te whakahaere i ngā pūtea. E mōhio ana te whānau, he maha ngā āhuatanga o te kōhanga reo hei arotake ake, hei pūrongo ake. Ko ētahi o aua wāhanga, ko ngā arohaehae, te whakamahere hōtaka, me te aromātai hōtaka.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko āna kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake, ko:

  • te Mana Tangata.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Tangata

He whakamārama

‘He pēhea te whakaatu a ngā tamariki i tō rātou mauritau, tō rātou harikoa hoki?’

He tino mauritau, he tino harikoa hoki ngā tamariki ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He pārekareka te āhua o ngā tamariki i a rātou e noho ana ki te kōhanga reo. Ka manaaki te whānau i te waiora ā-tinana, ā-tangata, ā-wairua, ā-whatumanawa hoki o ngā tamariki. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i tētahi taiao i āta whakaritea ai, ā, e hāpai ana hoki i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhitiwhiti haere ki ngā momo taumahi puta noa i te rā. He mārama ngā mahinga me ngā tūmanako hoki e pā ana ki te whanonga. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki papai ki te whakapūmau i aua kaupapa ki ngā tamariki.

He pakari ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki ki ngā pākeke. Ka whakamihi ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki i ia rā, ā, ka āta whakarite kia tau ai ngā tamariki katoa. Ka whakarato rātou i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te ārahi. Ka tīmata ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki ki te taunekeneke tētahi ki tētahi. Ka kitea tō rātou tino ngākau nui mō ia tamaiti.

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae, ki tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga hoki. Ka whakamahi te whānau i te marae me ngā tino pouwhenua ki ngā akoranga, kia āhei ai ngā tamariki ki te ako i aua āhuatanga, me te noho mārama ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae. Ka whakapuaki te kuia i ōna mōhiotanga, me ōna wheako. Nā runga i te mōhio o ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō, ka tū māia rātou.

He harikoa ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako ako reo e hāpai ana i te whakawhanaketanga o tō rātou reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kuia me ngā kaiako i te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa ki te taha o ngā tamariki. I ngā wā katoa ka tautoko, ka ārahi hoki rātou i ngā tamariki, ā, ka akiaki i a rātou ki te whakamātau i ngā hanganga reo hou.

Te wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Ka iti haere te ako a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori i te wā ko ētahi noa o te whānau e whakamahia ana te reo Māori. E mōhio ana te whānau, koia nei tētahi āhuatanga hei whakapakari ake.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Kua āta whakawhanakehia te whakamahere hōtaka, ā, ka whakahāngaitia anō hoki ki te whakapuakitanga o te hōtaka me ngā whakaritenga. E tino ārahi ana ngā mahere i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te hōtaka akoranga i āta whakamaheretia ai.

Ka āta tohu te aromatawai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Kua oti kētia ngā pūkete te whakarite, ā, ka whai hua hoki ngā mātua. E āhei ana rātou ki te titiro ki ngā mōhiohio me te kōrero ki ngā kaimahi mō ngā akoranga a ā rātou tamariki.

E tino tautokona ana ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki nā te matawhāiti o te whakahaere i ngā pūtea. He pūnaha ā te whānau hei ārahi i tā rātou whakahaere i ngā pūtea. Pūrongo ai ki te whānau i ia te wā. Ko te toha pūtea mō te hōtaka i ngā rauemi ako a ngā tamariki tētahi kaupapa matua. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te pai o ngā mahi ki te whakahaere i ngā pūtea.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Kāhore ngā tamariki e tino whai hua ana nā ngā mahi arotake whaiaro i tēnei wā. E mōhio ana te whānau, he maha ngā āhuatanga o te kōhanga reo hei arotake ake, hei pūrongo ake. Ko ētahi o aua wāhanga, ko ngā arohaehae, te whakamahere hōtaka, me te aromātai hōtaka.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaeretanga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora, me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia:

  • whakapakari tonu ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo hei whakawhanake ake.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangiora is in the Taranaki township of Inglewood.

ERO and the whānau developed the following evaluative question to support the focus of the review:

How well do children show they are comfortable and happy?

Children are comfortable and happy in the kōhanga reo environment.They appear to enjoy speaking te reo Māori. Children receive a range of language learning experiences to support their language development. Kuia and kaiako constantly support and guide children in te reo Māori. Children’s language learning is lessened when only some members of the whānau use te reo Māori. The whānau acknowledge that this is an area that needs strengthening.

Programme planning is well developed and aligned with programme delivery and practices. Kaimahi are well organised and it shows in practice. Assessment clearly reflects what children are learning. Profile books are developed and useful for parents. They are able to view information and talk with kaimahi about their children’s learning.

Children’s learning is well supported by good financial management. Whānau recognise that there are a number of areas in the kōhanga reo that need stronger review and reporting. These include appraisals, strategic planning and programme evaluation.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Tangata.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Mana Tangata

Background

How well do children show they are comfortable and happy?

Children are comfortable and happy in the kōhanga reo environment.

Areas of good performance

Children appear to enjoy going to kōhanga reo. Whānau care for the physical, social, spiritual and emotional wellbeing of children. Kaimahi provide an environment that is well organised and supports children to easily transition through activities during the day. There are clear routines and expectations for behaviour. Kaimahi use positive strategies to reinforce these for children.

Children interact confidently with adults. Kaimahi welcome children warmly each day and ensure all children are settled. They provide opportunities for children to be leaders. Kaimahi initiate one to one interactions with children. They show a genuine interest in each child.

Children are developing knowledge about who they are, where they come from and where they belong. Whānau use the marae and significant iwi landmarks in learning so children can learn about these things and understand where they come from. The kuia shares her knowledge and experience. Children’s confidence benefits from knowing who they are.

Children are happy speaking te reo Māori. Children receive a range of language learning experiences that support their development of te reo Māori. Kuia and kaiako use te reo Māori consistently with children. They constantly support and guide children and encourage them to attempt new language structures.

Area for development

Children’s language learning is lessened when only some members of the whānau use te reo Māori. The whānau acknowledge that this is an area that needs strengthening. Planning and Evaluation

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

Programme planning is well developed and aligned with programme delivery and practices. Kaimahi are well organised and this shows in practice. Planning effectively guides the learning programme. Children benefit from a well planned learning programme.

Assessment clearly reflects what children are learning. Profile books are developed and useful for parents. Parents are able to view information and talk with kaimahi about their children’s learning.

Children’s learning is well supported by prudent financial management. Whānau have systems in place to guide them with the management of their finances. Reporting to whānau is regular. Budgeting for the purchase of children’s learning resources is a priority. Children benefit from well managed finances.

Area for development

Children do not benefit fully from current self review practices. Whānau recognise that there are a number of areas in the kōhanga reo that need stronger review and reporting. These include appraisals, strategic planning and programme evaluation.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends

kaimahi and whānau continue to strengthen the areas for development identified in this report.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
