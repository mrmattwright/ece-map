1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Kauri ki Taitoko. He tino hononga ā-whakapapa tō te whānau o te kōhanga reo nei ki a Ngāti Raukawa, ā, he kaha tā rātou mahi ngātahi ki te iwi. Whai muri i te arotake o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2014, he nui ngā whakarerekētanga ki te kōhanga reo, tae atu ki ētahi kaiako hou me ētahi kaimahi hou, ā, he matatau rātou ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i ō rātou hononga?

He tino hononga tō ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau ki te kōhanga reo me te hapori.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori, puta noa i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki ako. Ko te ako, te mārama, me te kōrero i te reo Māori tētahi wawata o te whānau mō ā rātou tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo mahi ako reo e whai hua ana, hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo o ngā tamariki. Ko ētahi o ēnei, ko te whakatauira i te whakamahinga tika o te reo, me te tāruarua, te patapatai, ngā karakia, me ngā waiata hoki. Nā ngā haerenga ki ngā marae o te hapori nei, ka kite, ka rongo hoki ngā tamariki i te karanga, ngā whaikōrero, me ngā mōteatea. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i tō rātou ngākau titikaha ki te whakawhiti whakaaro mā te reo Māori.

Ka āta whakahono atu ngā tamariki tuākana ki ētahi atu. He pai te mahi ngātahi a ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakatinana rātou i te whanaungatanga. He whakaute, he mauritau anō hoki ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga o ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu tamariki, ā, ki ngā pākeke hoki. E ākina ana ngā tuākana kia hāpai, kia manaaki hoki rātou i ngā tēina. E atawhaitia ana, e aro nuitia ana ngā pēpi, hei āta poipoi i a rātou. Ka āta whakarite ngā kaimahi kia maha ngā whai wāhitanga o ngā tamariki i ia te wā, ki te taki i ō rātou ake whakapapa. He mauritau ngā tamariki i roto i ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu.

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou ake tuakiri. He tino hononga tō te whānau ki a Ngāti Tukorehe, ā, he tino tūāpapa tēnei mō tō rātou hāpai i te ako a ngā tamariki i tō rātou toi whenuatanga. E whakatau haere ana te whānau i te huarahi, mai i te kōhanga reo ki te kura kaupapa Māori. Ka āta whakarite rātou i te ako a ngā tamariki i ngā tikanga me ngā hītori tuku iho o te hapori, mā te taetae atu i ia te wā ki ngā marae o te takiwā. E tautoko ana ngā kaimahi i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki ō rātou whakapapa, mā ngā whakaaturanga i te wāhi ako. Ka akiaki rātou i te mahi ngātahi a ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana anō hoki i a rātou. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō, ā, ki ō rātou tūrangawaewae hoki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

E whai hua ana ngā tamariki nā te pai o ngā mahi ki te whakamahere i te hōtaka me te aromatawai. Ka whai pūtake ngā mahere i āta waihangatia ai e ngā kaimahi, ā, he ngohengohe tonu te noho o te hōtaka akoranga. He pai te whakamōhio i ngā whānau mō te ako a ngā tamariki. He pai te whakamahinga i ngā pūkete hei whakaatu i ngā tauira mahi a ngā tamariki, tae atu hoki ki ngā paki ako e whakatakoto haere ana i tā rātou ahu whakamua. He pai anō hoki te whakamahinga a ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te pae pāpāho pāpori, me ngā hui ōpaki, ngā hui ōkawa, ngā whakawhitinga whakaaro, me ngā kōrerorero, hei whakapuaki i ngā whakaaro e pā ana ki te ako me te āhua o ngā whakawhanaketanga.

Ko te aromātai i te hōtaka tētahi tino mahi a ngā kaimahi e whai hua ana. Ko te tū auau, te hāngaitanga anō hoki o ngā whakaaro huritao e hāpai ana i a rātou ki te tāutu i ngā mahi ka whai ake, hei whakapai ake i tā rātou whakapuakitanga akoranga, me tō rātou hāpai i te ako a ngā tamariki. Ko te ārahitanga ngaio e whakaratohia ana e te kaiako matua, e whai pānga nui ana ki te whakatairanga i te kounga o ngā mahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai. Ko ngā tamariki te aronga matua i te kōhanga reo.

He tukanga tā te whānau e whai pūtake ana ki te āta whakarite i te rere pai o ngā whakahaeretanga i te kōhanga reo. Kei tētahi rōpū matua o te whānau te kawenga ki te whakahaere i te tari ki te kōhanga reo. E āta whakatutukihia ana e te whānau ngā tātari pūtea. He auaha te arotake i ia te wā i ngā kaupapa here, he mahi whakatōpū anō hoki, ā, e tino ākina ana te whai wāhi mai o te whānau.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Me whakapakari i ētahi wāhanga o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau. Me whakarite i tētahi tukanga ki te arotake whaiaro, hei aroturuki i ngā whakapaitanga ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Me whakawhanake, me whakatinana hoki i tētahi mahere rautaki ka whakaraupapa i ngā kaupapa matua a te whānau hei te pae tawhiti. Ka hāpai tēnei i a rātou ki te whakatau tonu i ngā whakaritenga papai, mō te whakawhanake tonutanga o tō rātou kōhanga reo ā meāke nei.

Ko tētahi tino pūnaha arohaehae kaimahi, hei hāpai i ngā kaimahi ki te tāutu i ō rātou pūmanawa me ngā wāhanga hoki hei whakawhanake ake. Ka hāpai ake anō tēnei i a rātou ki te arotahi ki tēnā e tino whai hua ai ki ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā:

  • ka whakapakari ake te arotake whaiaro i te aroturukitanga me te pūrongo, hei hāpai anō hoki i te whakatinanatanga o ngā āhuatanga e whai pānga ana ki te whakapai tonutanga.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Kauri i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kauri is located in Levin. This kōhanga reo has close whakapapa and working relationships with Ngāti Raukawa. Since the 2014, ERO review, there have been a number of changes, including new kaiako and new kaimahi, who are fluent speakers of te reo Māori.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children show that they have connections?

Children and their whānau have strong connection to the kōhanga reo and the community.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are exposed to te reo Māori across a range of different learning settings. Learning, understanding and speaking te reo Maori is a whānau aspiration for their children. Kaimahi use a wide range of effective language learning practices to support children with their language development. These include modelling the correct use of the language, repetition, questions, karakia and waiata. Visits to local marae allow children to see and hear karanga, whaikōrero and mōteatea. Children are developing their confidence to communicate in te reo Māori.

Older children engage in meaningful relationships with others. Kaimahi work well together and model whanaungatanga. Children’s interactions and relationships with each other and adults are respectful and positive. Tuākana are encouraged to support and care for tēina. Babies receive focused and nurturing care and attention. Kaimahi ensure children have regular opportunities to recite and recall their own whakapapa. Children are secure in their relationships with others.

Children are developing their own sense of identity. Whānau have close connections to Ngāti Tukorehe which in turn provide them with a strong foundation to support children to learn about who they are. Whānau are working towards creating a pathway from kōhanga reo to kura kaupapa. They ensure children learn about tikanga and local histories through regular visits to the local marae. Kaimahi support children as they develop knowledge of their whakapapa through displays in the learning environment. They encourage children to work cooperatively with each other in a nurturing environment. Children know who they are and where they are from.

Planning and Evaluation

Children benefit from effective programme planning and assessment. Kaimahi planning is purposeful, well designed and also provides for a flexible learning programme. Whānau are well informed about the children’s learning. Scrapbooks are used well to display samples of children’s work including learning stories that track their progress. Social media, informal and formal meetings and discussions are well used by kaimahi and whānau to share about learning and development.

Programme evaluation is a strength of kaimahi. Regular focused reflections are helping them to identify key next steps for improving their delivery and support children’s learning. The professional leadership of the kaiako matua has contributed to lifting the quality of planning, assessment and evaluation. Children are the focus on the kōhanga reo.

The whānau has a practical process in place to ensure the smooth running of the kōhanga reo. A core group of whānau take responsibility for the administration of the kōhanga reo. Financial audits are competently met by the whānau. Regular policy review is innovative and inclusive with whānau participation encouraged.

Key Next Steps

Aspects of whānau management need strengthening. In order to monitor improvements to the management of the kōhanga reo a process of internal review should be established. The development and implementation of a strategic plan should outline the long term priorities for the whānau. This will help them to continue to make good decisions for the future development of their kōhanga reo.

A robust kaimahi appraisal system will support kaimahi to identify their strengths and areas for development. This will further support them to focus on what most benefits children.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation that

  • Internal evaluation will strengthen monitoring and reporting to inform ongoing improvement.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Kauri will be in three years.
