1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Kauri ki Taitoko. Mā te whakawhiti kōrero me te whānau, i whakawhanake Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te pātai aromātai e whai ake nei, arā:

‘He pēhea te pai o ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori?’

He tika te whakamahinga, te mārama, me te whakautu a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori.

E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki ki te ako me te whakamahi i te reo Māori. He hihiri, he tākare hoki ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā taumahi i te kōhanga reo. He pai te tākaro ngātahi a ngā tamariki, ā, he harikoa ō rātou āhua. He pai te wairua ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka whai hua ngā mahi mahere a ngā kaimahi, hei whakarato i tētahi hōtaka e whakahihiri ana, e whakaongaonga ana hoki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te aromātai hōtaka ki te whakapai tonu i te hōtaka akoranga i ia te wā. He pai te whakamōhio i ngā matua mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga. He pai te mōhio o ngā kaimahi ki ā rātou tamariki, ā, e āhei ana ki te whakatutuki i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, ō rātou pūmanawa, me ō rātou matea hoki.

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Kei te haere tonu ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakapakari ake i ētahi āhuatanga o te pupuri tuhinga me ngā pūrongo e pā ana ki te pūtea. Ko te pai o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau e hāpai ana i ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakarato i tētahi taiao ako o te kounga pai mō ngā tamariki.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake, ko:

  • te reo Māori.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Reo

He whakamārama

‘He pēhea te pai o ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori?’

He tika te whakamahinga, te mārama, me te whakautu a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki kia ako, kia whakamahi hoki rātou i te reo Māori. Ka hāngai pū te titiro o ngā kaimahi ki te whakaako i ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori. Ka whai hua ā rātou mahi ki te whakatauira i ngā kupu hou, ngā kīanga hou, me ngā rerenga kōrero. Ka whakarato rātou i te pai o tētahi taiao o te reo Māori e akiaki ana i ngā tamariki. He pai te whakamahinga a ngā kaimahi i ngā rauemi ako. Ka whakatauira ngā tuākana i te reo Māori mō ngā tēina. He kaha ngā tamariki ki te taki karakia me ngā mōteatea hoki. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti kōrero mā te reo Māori.

E whanake ana te tū takitahi a ngā tamariki. He hihiri, he tākare hoki rātou ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā taumahi i te kōhanga reo. He pai te tākaro ngātahi a ngā tamariki, ā, he harikoa ō rātou āhua. Kua waia ngā tamariki ki ngā mahinga, ā, he pai tā rātou whakatutuki haere i ngā whakawhitinga i te hōtaka. E tīmata ana ngā tamariki tuākana ki te tiaki i ō rātou ake matea ā-tinana.

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i te pai o te whanaungatanga. Ka whakarato e te whānau tētahi taiao e atawhai ana, e poipoi ana i ngā tamariki. He pai te whakawhitinga ki ngā wāhi o roto, o waho hoki, ā, nā tēnei e āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whakatau i tēnā e hiahiatia ana e rātou hei mahi. E tautokona ana ngā tamariki kia whiriwhiri rātou, mai i te whānuitanga pai o ngā tūmomo rauemi, taumahi hoki e hono atu ana ki te kaupapa ako. Ka ako ngā tamariki i te taiao o te hapori. Ka āta whakarato ngā kaimahi hei painga mō ngā tamariki. He hihiri, he pai hoki te wairua ki te kōhanga reo.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki kia ako rātou i te ao Māori me te ao whānui. He pai te whakamahere a ngā kaimahi, hei whakarato i tētahi hōtaka whakahihiri e whakatutuki ana i ngā tūmomo matea o ngā tamariki katoa. Ka whai wāhi ngā mātua ki te whakamahere i te hōtaka. Ka āta hono ngā mahere kaupapa ki Te Whāriki o Te Kōhanga Reo, ā, ka kitea hoki te aronga ki te ako i te reo me ngā tikanga Māori.

Whakawhiti kōrero ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, mō te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga. Ka aromātai rātou i te hōtaka, ā, ka tāutu i ngā āhuatanga ka whakapai ake, hei hāpai ake anō i ngā tamariki ki te ako me te whakamahi i te reo Māori. Ka arotahi ngā kaimahi ki te whakapai tonutanga.

Ka whai hua ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te aroturuki i te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ko ngā mōhiohio ki ngā pūkete a ia tamaiti e hāngai ana ki te whai wāhitanga a ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga. He pai te whakamōhio i ngā mātua mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga. He pai te mōhiotanga o ngā kaimahi ki ā rātou tamariki, ā, e āhei ana rātou ki te poipoi i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, ō rātou pūmanawa, me ō rātou matea.

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Kua mahi tahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te taha o te kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe, ki te whakapai ake i tō rātou mōhiotanga, tō rātou māramatanga hoki ki te whakahaeretanga o ngā whakaritenga ki te kōhanga reo. Tū ai ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, ā, he pai te pūrongo i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te whakahaere i te tari o te kōhanga reo, me te hōtaka akoranga. Kua whakatakotohia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi ngā whāinga mō ngā tau kei te heke mai, ā, kei te ahu whakamua rātou ki te whakatutuki haere. Ka hāngai aua whāinga ki ngā tūmomo whakangungu mā ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau, me te whakapai tonutanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Kei te haere tonu ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te whakapakari i ētahi āhuatanga o te pupuri tuhinga me te pūrongo i te pūtea. Ko te pai o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau e hāpai ana i ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakarato i ngā tino painga mō ngā tamariki.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaeretanga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora, me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia whakamahi tonu te whānau me ngā kaimahi i te arotake whaiaro, hei whakapai ake anō i te hōtaka akoranga mā ngā tamariki.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

22 Hakihea 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kauri is in Levin. In consultation with the whānau ERO developed the following evaluation question:

How well do children use te reo Māori?

Children use, understand and respond appropriately to te reo Māori.

Children are well supported to learn and use te reo Māori. Children are excited and eager to participate in activities at kōhanga reo. Children play well together and appear to be happy. There is a positive feeling at kōhanga reo.

Staff plans effectively to provide children with an interesting and stimulating programme. Kaimahi use programme evaluation to continually improve the learning programme. Parents are well informed about children’s learning and development. Kaimahi know their children well and are able to respond to their interests, strengths and needs.

The whānau manages the kōhanga reo well in the interests of children. Kaimahi and whānau continue to work on strengthening some aspects of record keeping and financial reporting. Effective whānau management supports the staff and whānau to provide a good quality learning environment for children.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • te reo Māori.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Mana Reo

Background

How well do children use te reo Māori?

Children use, understand and respond appropriately to te reo Māori.

Areas of good performance

Children are well supported to learn and use te reo Māori. Kaimahi maintains a strong focus on teaching children to use te reo Māori. They effectively model new vocabulary, phrases and sentences. They provide a positive and encouraging te reo Māori environment. Kaimahi uses learning resources well. Older children model te reo Māori for the younger children. Children confidently recite karakia and waiata-mōteatea. Children use te reo Māori to communicate.

Children are developing independence. They are excited and eager to participate in activities at kōhanga reo. Children play well together and appear to be happy. Children are familiar with the routines and manage the transitions in the programme well. Older children are beginning to care for their own physical needs.

Children experience positive relationships. Kaimahi provide a warm, nurturing environment for children. There is a good flow from the indoor to outdoor areas that allows children to make choices about what they want to do. Children are supported to select from a good range of resources and activities that link to learning kaupapa. Children learn about the local environment. Kaimahi provide the best they can for children. There is a good energy and a positive feeling at the kōhanga reo.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

Children are well supported to learn about the Māori world and the wider world. Kaimahi plan effectively to provide an interesting programme that meets the various needs of all children. Parents contribute to programme planning. Kaupapa planning is clearly linked to Te Whāriki o te Kōhanga Reo and reflects a focus on learning te reo and tikanga Māori.

Kaimahi regularly discuss the quality of the learning programme. They evaluate the programme and identify improvements that will further support children to learn and to use te reo Māori. Kaimahi are focussed on continual improvement.

Kaimahi effectively monitor children’s progress and development. Information in individual profiles highlights children’s involvement in learning. Parents are well informed about children’s learning and development. Kaimahi know their children well and are able to respond to their interests, strengths and needs.

The whānau manages the kōhanga reo well in the interests of children. The whānau and staff have worked with the district kaupapa kaimahi to improve their knowledge and understanding of managing kōhanga reo operations. Whānau meetings occur regularly and there is good reporting about kōhanga reo administration and the learning programme. The whānau and staff have set goals for the future which they are working toward. Goals include training for staff and whānau members and continuing to improve the learning programme. Kaimahi continue to work on strengthening some aspects of record keeping and financial reporting. Effective whānau management supports the staff and whānau to provide the best they can for children.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends that the whānau and staff continue to use self review to further improve the learning programme for children.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)
