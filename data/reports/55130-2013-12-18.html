1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Waiapu ki te hapori tuawhenua o Tikitiki ki te Tai Rāwhiti, ā, e tū pā tata atu ana ki te marae o Rāhui. Ka tupu te mōhiotanga, te māramatanga hoki o ngā tamariki ki ngā tikanga e whai pānga ana ki ngā huihuinga ōkawa, ōpaki hoki, nā tā rātou mātakitaki i te whakatutukitanga o ngā pākeke i ngā tūranga me ngā kawenga mahi. He tino hononga tō te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki a Ngāi Tāne me Karuwai. Ka whai painga ngā tamariki nā te tū o te kōhanga reo ki te marae, me tā rātou tino whai wāhi atu ki ngā kaupapa i te marae.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Whenua te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E whakapono ana rātou, mā te Mana Whenua ka ako ngā tamariki ki te āta whakahono atu ki te whenua me ngā tāngata, mā te whakapapa. Ka hāpai aua whai wāhitanga i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā ariā o te whanaungatanga me te kaitiakitanga mō ngā tamariki.

Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā hōtaka akoranga e hāngai ana ki ngā taonga tuku iho, ngā tikanga, me te reo ā-iwi o Ngāti Porou. Ka whakapakaritia, ka whakakoia hoki te tuakiri me te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki, nā tā rātou ako i ngā kōrero tuku iho, ngā waiata, ā, nā te haere hoki ki ngā tino wāhi whakahirahira, tāngata whakahirahira hoki o Ngāti Porou. Ka whakatairangatia te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki nā ā rātou taunekeneke, ā rātou whakawhitinga kōrero o ia rā ki ngā pākeke, ki ētahi atu. Ka rongo rātou i ngā tauira pai o te reo ā-iwi o Ngāti Porou, mai i ngā kaumātua me ngā kaimahi.

Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā whiringa mā ngā tamariki, hei whakawhanake, hei hāpai hoki i te kōkiri a ia tamaiti i āna ake akoranga. Nā ngā whakaritenga i āta whakatakotohia ai, ka whakaatu ngā tamariki tuākana i ō rātou pūkenga ārahi, i a rātou e hāpai ana i ngā tamariki tēina. He whakaute, he tauutuutu hoki ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā pākeke me ngā tamariki.

Ka aromātai ngā kaimahi i ā rātou mahi me tāna whai pānga ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ko ngā mōhiohio ka whakaemihia, ka hāpai i ngā kaimahi ki te whakahāngai i ngā hōtaka akoranga hei whakatutuki, hei hāpai anō hoki i te rerekē haere o ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. He tau ngā tamariki, ā, he pārekareka hoki ki a rātou te noho ki te kōhanga reo.

Kua pai ake te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki ngā kaupapa i te kōhanga reo. He nui te noho pūmau i ngā kaimahi me te whānau e whai wāhi atu ana ki ngā hōtaka whakangungu ki te whakapai i ō rātou pūkenga, tō rātou māramatanga hoki ki Te Whāriki me Te Korowai. Kua whakarato hoki te purapura me ngā kaupapa kaimahi i te tino tautoko i roto i te whakahaere tari me ngā mahi i te kōhanga reo. Ka noho maioha te whānau ki te haere tonutanga o taua tautoko.

Kua arotahi te whānau ki te whakapai i ngā taiao o roto, o waho hoki, tae atu ki te whakahou ake i te whare me āna rawa. He pai te whakahaeretanga o ngā pūtea hei kōkiri i ngā whakapaitanga i te kōhanga reo. He harikoa te whānau mō te ahunga whakamua i whakatutukihia ai, tae noa mai ki tēnei wā. E mōhio ana rātou me whakawhānui ake ā rātou tukanga arotake whaiaro, hei whakapai ake i te āhua o tā rātou arotake i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Me tahuri hoki te whānau ki te whakapai ake hoki i te pūrongo. Ko tētahi aronga arotake whaiaro e nahanaha ake ana ka hāpai i te whānau ki te whakahāngai i āna whakaritenga ki tā rātou mahere wā roa, me ngā tūmanako hoki o Te Whāriki me Te Korowai.

Whakatutukihia ai ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. Ka tākaro, ka ako hoki ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e āta whakahaere tōtikatia ana.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Waiapu kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Waiapu.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Whenua.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Whenua

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Whenua te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ki tā rātou titiro, ka hāngai te Mana Whenua ki te āta tūhono atu ki te whenua me ngā tāngata, mā te whakapapa.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te reo Māori me ngā tikanga. Ka āta whakarongo, ka āta aro atu hoki ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori i roto i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki. Whakamahia ai te reo Māori me ngā tikanga i ngā wā katoa, puta noa i te rā. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki hei hāpai i te ako reo a ngā tamariki. Ka whakawhānui ake rātou i ngā kōrero a ngā tamariki hei whakauru i a rātou ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero e whai pūtake ana. Ka tino hāpaitia ngā tamariki, ā, ka ngahau rātou i a rātou e ako ana i te reo Māori.

Te tūrangawaewae. Kua waia ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao. E whakawhanake ana rātou i te māramatanga ki te whai pānga nui o te marae ki te whakatairanga i ō rātou hononga ā-hapū, ā-iwi hoki. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā hui me ngā kaupapa i te marae.

Ka whakawhānui ake te aronga o te tūrangawaewae i ngā tamariki e ako ana ki te taki mai i ō rātou tuakiri, mā ngā tino tohu whenua, ngā tino tīpuna hoki. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i ngā mahi rangatira a ō rātou tīpuna, me te hītori o te hapori, o te rohe whānui hoki o Ngāti Porou. Ka wheako rātou i ngā taunekeneke e whai pūtake ana, ā, e whakakoia ana i tō rātou tūnga ki te whānau me te kōhanga reo.

Te whanaungatanga. He pakari te aronga o te whanaungatanga puta noa i te kōhanga reo. Ko te whanaungatanga te tūāpapa mō ngā whakawhitinga kōrero me ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā pākeke. Ko te whai wāhi mai o ngā kaumātua me ngā pākeke ka harikoa te wairua ā-whānau ki te kōhanga reo. He pārekareka ki ngā kaumātua te noho mai me te kōrerorero ki ngā tamariki. Ka ako ngā tamariki i te whakahirahiratanga o te manaaki, te aroha, me te awhi mā ā rātou taunekeneke ki ngā pākeke, ki ētahi atu hoki. He tau, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki.

Te kaitiakitanga. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki kite ako i te kawe ngātahi, me te manaaki ngātahi mā ngā kaupapa pērā i te whakatupu i tētahi māra huawhenua, me te koha i ngā hua ki tō rātou whānau. Ka manaaki ngā tamariki i te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Te mahi tahi. Ka whai hua ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te mahi tahi, me te whakakotahi i ō rātou ake pūkenga, ō rātou ake pūmanawa mō te katoa. He mārama ō rātou tūnga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki, ā, he pai tā rātou whakawhiti kōrero ki waenga i a rātou anō. Ka hāpai tēnei i te riterite o ngā kōrero me ngā tūmanako ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i roto i ngā akoranga. He pai te tatauranga ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me te whānau, kia pai ai tiaki me te manaaki i ngā tamariki.

Ngā mahinga. Kua waia ngā tamariki ki ngā mahinga o ia rā. He pai te mātakitaki i a rātou, ā, ka whakatauira ngā tamariki tuākana i te ārahitanga. Ka hāpai rātou i ngā tamariki tēina ki te whakatutuki i ā rātou mahinga o ia rā. Ka tino mōhio ngā tamariki ki ā rātou kawenga hei tuakana.

Te taiao ako. Ka āta kitea ngā uaratanga o te whānau ki te mārie me te noho whakariterite anō hoki o te taiao akoranga. Ka āta whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rauemi māori, hou hoki e akiaki ana i ngā tamariki ki te mahi auaha, me te mahi pohewa. Ka whakanuia ngā mahi a ngā tamariki, ā, he ātaahua hoki te whakaatu.

Te hōtaka akoranga. Ka aro pai ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki, mā te whakarato i tētahi hōtaka akoranga e noho ngāwari ana. Ka āta whai pūtake te whakauru o ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā kēmu me ngā taumahi e ngahau ana, e whakahihiri ana, hei whakaongaonga, hei whakawero hoki i a rātou. Ka whakakipakipa rātou i a rātou anō, he pākiki hoki rātou, ā, e wātea ana rātou ki te whiriwhiri i ngā taumahi e ngākau nuitia ana e rātou i te hōtaka akoranga. He harikoa, he pakari hoki ngā tamariki, ā, e whakawhanake ana i ngā pūkenga ki te kōkiri i ā rātou ake akoranga.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai i ngā kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki, mō te whānau hoki. Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka me te aroturuki hoki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakamahere me te aromātai. E whakatakoto ana ngā mahere hōtaka i ngā aratohu mō te whakatinanatanga o ngā hōtaka akoranga. Ka hāngai te whakamahere hōtaka ki tētahi marautanga e aro nui ana ki te hītori, ngā kōrero tuku iho, me ngā tīpuna o Ngāti Porou. Ka aromātai ngā kaimahi i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga i te mutunga o ia wiki. Ka whakahāngai ngā kaiako i ngā hōtaka akoranga hei hāpai i ngā matea akoranga o ia tamaiti.

Te whai i te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki. E āhei ana ngā kaimahi ki te aru i te ahunga whakamua me ngā whakatutukitanga a ngā tamariki, mā te whakamahi i te whānuitanga pai o ngā tūmomo rautaki aromatawai. Ko te aromatawai i ia tamaiti e tino hono ana ki te hōtaka akoranga, ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi, me te whai wāhitanga a ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi. Ka kite ngā kaimahi i ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā āheinga o ia tamaiti, o ngā tūmomo rōpū tamariki hoki. Ka whakamahi rātou i aua mōhiohio ki te whakatinana i te hōtaka. Ko te mahi mā ngā kaimahi i nāianei, ko te whakapuaki i aua mōhiohio ki te whānau mā te pūrongo i ia te wā ki ngā hui ā-whānau.

Te whakamahere mō te wā roa. Ka whakamahere nahanaha te whānau mō te whakawhanake tonutanga o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakawhanakehia e rātou tētahi tirohanga, ā, kua tuhia hoki ā rātou whāinga wā roa mō ngā tau e rima ka whai ake. Kua whakaraupapahia aua whāinga, i raro i ngā akoranga mātauranga a ngā tamariki me te whānau, me te whakapai ake i ngā taiao o roto, o waho.

Kua whakawhanakehia tētahi mahere mahi, ā, e hāngai ana tēnei ki te mahere wā roa. E mōhio ana te whānau ki te whakahirahiratanga o te whakamahere ki te whakapā atu ki ngā pūtea i whakaritea ai.

Te whai wāhi, te ngākau nui hoki o te whānau. Kua piki ake te whai wāhi a te whānau ki ngā hui o ia marama, ngā kaupapa kohi pūtea, me te hōtaka akoranga o ia rā. He tākare rātou ki te whai wāhi ki ngā tukanga whakataunga hei kōkiri i ngā whakapaitanga me te whakawhanake tonutanga o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei. Ka āwhina te whānau i ngā kaimahi ki te whakatutuki i ngā mahi o ia rā ki te kōhanga reo.

Ko te pai o ngā pūnaha whakawhiti whakaaro e whakamōhio tonu ana i ngā mātua mō ngā taumahi me ngā kaupapa ka hiahiatia tō rātou āwhina. He tākare te whānau mō ngā whakawhanaketanga o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei, me te mātauranga e whiwhi ana ngā tamariki.

Te whakahaere i ngā pūtea. He pai te tūnga pūtea a te kōhanga reo, hei āta whakahaere i ngā whakapaitanga me ngā whakawhanaketanga ka whai ake. Ka whakaatuhia ngā pūrongo pūtea ki ngā hui o ia marama, hei āta whakarite i te noho pūmau o ngā kawenga takohanga. Ka tuhia ēnei ki ngā meneti a te whānau. Kua whakaritea tētahi tahua hei whakahaere i ngā whakapaitanga ki te kōhanga reo, ā, hei hāpai hoki i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga.

Te whakapai i te taiao o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakapai te whānau i ngā taiao o roto, o waho hoki. Ko ētahi o ēnei, ko:

  • te whakahou ake, te whakawhānui ake hoki i te whare
  • te whakatakoto i ngā whāriki hou me te kahupeka hou
  • te whakatū i tētahi taiepa hou, me tētahi kēti pūmau
  • te arowhai i ngā tukanga me ngā taputapu e pā ana ki te hauora
  • te hoko i ngā rawa hou.

E whakatutukihia ana ngā matea o ngā tamariki e te whānau. Kei te tino whakapau kaha te whānau ki te whakarato i tētahi taiao haumaru mō ā rātou tamariki.

Te whakangungu i ngā kaimahi me te whānau. He tākare ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whai wāhi ki ngā tūmomo kohinga whakangungu e whakaratohia ana e Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo. Tokotoru ngā kaimahi e ahu whakamua pai ana i roto i ngā mahi whakangungu a te Tohu Whakapakari. Kua oti i ngā mema tokorua o te whānau, te Pae rangatiratanga me Te Ara Hiko. Kua noho pūmau ētahi atu mema o te whānau ki te ako tonu, hei whakapakari i tō rātou māramatanga, tō rātou mōhiotanga hoki ki Te Whāriki me Te Korowai. Ka āta toro atu te whānau ki tō rātou purapura, mō te tautoko me te whakapuaki anō hoki i ngā pūkenga me ngā mōhiotanga.

Ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. Ko te noho haumaru o ngā tamariki tētahi aronga matua ki te whānau i ngā wā katoa. E mōhio ana rātou ki te whakahirahiratanga o te aroturuki me te tuhi haere i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. Ka aroturukihia, ka tuhia hoki ngā pūnaha ki te whakatakoto haere i ia rā i ngā mahinga me ngā arowhai haumaru. Ka mahi ngātahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi kia noho haumaru ai te taiao o te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te arotake whaiaro. Kāhore te whānau e āta aro ana ki te kōkiri i te arotake whaiaro, he mahi kē ka whai atu i te wā ka pupū ake tētahi take. Me whakawhanake rātou i ngā tūmanako riterite e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro, me te whakawhānui i te hōkai ki tēnā e tirohia ana e rātou. Me whakarite i tētahi taiāwhiao mō te arotake i ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga. Ko ngā arotake o ia te wā ka hāpai i te whānau ki te aroturuki nahanaha me te pūrongo nahanaha i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. E ai ki te whānau, kei te whakawhanake rātou i ngā pūnaha ki te āta whakatinana i ngā arotake whaiaro ka whai hua.

Te pūrongo ki te whānau. Ka taea e te whānau ā rātou pūnaha pūrongo te whakapai ake, mā te whakaemi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo mōhiohio ka whai pānga ki te whakamōhio i ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapaitanga. Me whakaatu ēnei ki ngā hui ā-whānau, kia noho mōhio tonu ai ngā mema ki ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, me te āhua o te ako, te whakawhanaketanga, me te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki.

Te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi. Ahakoa i arohaehaengia ngā kaimahi, ka taea te whakapai ake tonu te whakahaeretanga i te āhua mahi, hei āta whakarite i te tino whakatinanatanga, te tino whakatutukitanga hoki o ngā tukanga. Ko tētahi whakarāpopototanga o ngā take whakawhiti kōrero matua, ngā kōrero urupare, me ngā kōrero kōkiri, ka hāpai i ngā kaimahi ki te whakapai i tā rātou āhua mahi.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Waiapu, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Waiapu Kōhanga Reo is located in the rural east coast community of Tikitiki and is situated adjacent to the Rāhui Marae. Children gain some awareness and understanding about tikanga associated to formal and informal events as they see the roles and responsibilities carried out by adults. The kōhanga reo whānau has strong links to Ngāi Tāne and Karuwai. Children benefit from a marae based kōhanga reo where they fully participate in marae activities.

The whānau chose Mana Whenua as the focus for the review. They believe that through Mana Whenua children learn about making meaningful links to the land and with people through whakapapa. These opportunities help to develop concepts of whanaungatanga and kaitiakitanga for children.

Kaimahi provide learning programmes that reflect the traditions, tikanga and dialect of Ngāti Porou. Children’s sense of identity and belonging is strengthened and affirmed through learning about their local stories, waiata and visiting significant places and people in Ngāti Porou. Children’s language development is enhanced through their daily interactions and communication with adults and each other. They hear good models of Ngāti Porou dialect used by kaumātua and kaimahi.

Kaimahi provide choices for children to develop and support independent learning. Well established routines enable older children to demonstrate their leadership skills as they support the younger children. Relationships are respectful and reciprocal among adults and children.

Kaimahi evaluate their practices and the impact it has on children’s learning. The information gathered help kaimahi to tailor learning programmes to meet and support children’s changing needs and interests. Children are settled and enjoy being at the kōhanga reo.

Whānau involvement and participation in kōhanga reo activities has improved. There is a high level of commitment as kaimahi and whānau participate in training programmes to improve their skills and understanding ofTe WhārikiandTe Korowai. The local purapura and kaupapa kaimahi have also provided valuable administrative and collegial support. Whānau are appreciative of their ongoing support.

The whānau focus has been on improving the indoor and outdoor environments, including building renovations and refurbishments. Finances are well managed to progress kōhanga reo improvements. The whānau is pleased with the progress achieved to date. They acknowledge that their self review processes need to be extended to improve the way they review kōhanga reo operations. Reporting to whānau is another area that requires attention. A more structured approach to self review should assist the whānau to align its practices to their long term plan,Te WhārikiandTe Korowaiexpectations.

Health and safety procedures are regularly carried out. Children play and learn in a well managed environment.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Waiapu was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Waiapu Kōhanga Reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Whenua.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Mana Whenua

Background

The whānau chose Mana Whenua as the focus for the review. They define Mana Whenua as making meaningful connections to the land and people through whakapapa.

Areas of good performance

Te reo Māori and tikanga. Children listen to and respond confidently to te reo Māori in various contexts. Te reo Māori and tikanga is practiced consistently throughout the day. Kaimahi use a range of strategies to support children’s language learning. They build on children’s responses to engage them in meaningful conversations. Children are well supported and have fun learning te reo Māori.

Tūrangawaewae. Children are familiar with their surroundings. They are developing an understanding of the importance of the marae to enhance their hapū and iwi connections. Children participate in marae events and activities.

The sense of tūrangawaewae is broadened as children learn to identify themselves in terms of their significant landmarks and local ancestors. Children hear stories about the exploits of their ancestors and history of the local and wider Ngāti Porou region. They experience meaningful interactions that affirm their place in the whānau and the kōhanga reo.

Whanaungatanga. There is a strong sense of whanaungatanga throughout the kōhanga reo.Whanaungatanga provides the foundation for communication and interactions among children and adults. The presence of kaumātua and pakeke contributes to a happy whānau atmosphere. Kaumātua enjoy being with, and talking to the children. Children learn about the importance of manaaki, aroha and awhi though their interactions with adults and each other. Children are settled and happy.

Kaitiakitanga. Kaimahi provide opportunities for children to learn about communal responsibility and hospitality through activities such as growing a vegetable garden and sharing the products with their whānau. Children take care of their kōhanga reo environment.

Mahi tahi. Kaimahi and whānau work effectively together using the individual strengths and qualities of the team. They have clear roles and responsibilities and communicate well with each other. This helps ensure that children receive consistent messages and expectations for learning. A good ratio of kaimahi and whānau help to provide good supervision and car for children.

Routines. Children are familiar with the daily routines. They are well supervised and the older children model leadership. They support the younger children to manage their daily routines. Children have a strong awareness of their responsibilities as tuakana.

Learning environment. The whānau values are clearly evident in the calm and well organised learning environment. Kaimahi make good use of natural and contemporary resources that encourage children to be creative and imaginative. Children’s work is valued and attractively displayed.

Learning programme. Kaimahi respond appropriately to children’s needs and interests by providing a learning programme that is flexible. Children are purposefully engaged in a range of fun and interesting games and activities that stimulate and challenge them. They are self motivated and curious and have the freedom to choose activities that interest them in the learning programme. Children are happy, confident and developing skills to be independent learners.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans manages and monitors all areas of kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Planning and evaluation. Programme plans provide clear guidelines for the implementation of learning programmes. Programmeplanning reflects a curriculum that has a strong focus on the history, stories and ancestors of Ngāti Porou. Kaimahi evaluate the effectiveness of the learning programme at the end of each week. Kaiako tailor learning programmes to support children’s individual learning needs.

Tracking children’s progress. Kaimahi are able to track children’s progress and achievement using a good range of assessment tools. Children’s individual assessment is closely linked to the learning programme, kaimahi practice and children’s involvement in activities. Kaimahi notice individual and group interests and abilities. They use this information to inform the programme. The next step for kaimahi is to share this information with whānau through regular reporting at whānau hui.

Long term planning. Whānau strategically plan for ongoing kōhanga reo developments. They have developed a vision and documented their long term goals for the next five years. These goals are prioritised under educational learning for children and whānau, and, improving the indoor and outdoor environments.

An action plan is developed and aligns to the long term plan. The whānau recognise the importance of planning to access targeted funding.

Whānau participation and commitment. Whānau participation has increased at monthly hui, fundraising events and in the daily learning programme. They are keen to share in decision-making processes to progress improvements and future kōhanga reo developments. Whānau help kaimahi in the day-to-day running of the kōhanga reo.

Effective communication systems keep parents informed about activities and events where their support is needed. Whānau are enthusiastic about future kōhanga reo developments and the education that their children receive.

Financial management. The kōhanga reo is in a good financial position to effectively manage future improvements and developments. Financial reports are presented at monthly hui to ensure accountability practices are maintained. These are documented in whānau minutes. A budget is in place to manage kōhanga reo improvements and to support children’s learning and development.

Improving the kōhanga environment. The whānau has made improvements to the indoor and outdoor environments. These include:

  • building renovations and extensions
  • laying new carpets and linoleum
  • fitting a new fence and a secure gate
  • checking health procedures and equipment
  • purchasing new furniture.

Children’s needs are being met by the whānau. Whānau are doing the best that they can to provide a safe environment for their children.

Staff and whānau training. Staff and whānau are enthusiastic about participating in training packages offered by Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT). Three kaimahi are progressing well on theWhakapakaritraining course. Two whānau members have completedPaerangātiratangaandTe Ara Hiko. Other whānau members have committed to further study to strengthen their understanding and knowledge ofTe WharikiandTe Korowai. Whānau take advantage of their local purapura for support and to share skills and knowledge.

Health and safety practices. Children’s safety is an ongoing priority for whānau. They acknowledge the importance of monitoring and recording health and safety practices. Daily recording systems for routines and safety checks are monitored and documented. Whānau and kaimahi work together to keep the kōhanga reo environment safe for children.

Areas for improvement

Self review. Whānau approach to self review is reactive rather than proactive. They need to develop consistent expectations for self review and broaden the scope of what they are looking at. A regular cycle of review for policies and procedures need to be established. Regular reviews would support the whānau to monitor and report on all areas of kōhanga reo operations in a planned way. Whānau acknowledge that they are developing systems to implement effective self review.

Reporting to whānau. The whānau could improve their reporting systems by gathering a wide range of information to help inform decisions for improvement. These should be presented at whānau hui to keep members well informed about kōhanga reo operations and how well children are learning, developing and progressing.

Kaimahi appraisals. While kaimahi have been appraised, performance management could be further improved to ensure procedures are rigorously implemented and followed up. A summary of key discussion points and constructive feedback and feed-forward would support kaimahi to improve their performance.

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Waiapu completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

ERO recommend that whānau and staff continue to work alongside the kaupapa kaimahi to guide and support them in addressing the key areas for development that are identified in this report.

General Information About Reviews

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
