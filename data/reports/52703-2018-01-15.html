1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Pekanga ki te tāone o Waitara, i Taranaki. Nō Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo te whare matua. I te roanga o ngā tau, kua whakahoungia e te whānau te whare me ngā rawa kia wāteatea, kia ātaahua hoki te wāhi ako ki ngā tamariki. Ko te reo ā-iwi me ngā kupu o Taranaki tētahi aronga matua a te whānau. Ko ngā uaratanga o te tū takitahi, te ngākau titikaha, me te whanaungatanga e noho whakahirahira ana ki te whānau.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa te kitea ki ngā tamariki tō rātou ngākau titikaha hei ākonga?

Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tō rātou ngākau titikaha hei ākonga i roto i ā rātou mahi katoa.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E ako ana ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki tētahi wāhi e tautoko ana i a rātou. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i te reo ā-iwi o Taranaki i roto i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakamahi rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki e akiaki ana i ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, mā ngā kōrero kipakipa, te patapatai, me te tāruarua. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te rongo i te reo ki ngā horopaki ōkawa me ngā horopaki ōpaki. Ka whakamahi ngā tamariki tuākana i te reo e mōhiotia ana e rātou. Ka whakawhiti kōrero ngā pēpi mā te reo ā-tinana me te āhua o ō rātou kanohi. Ka ako te whānau i te reo Māori ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki, mā te hōtaka o ia rā. E whanake ana ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te pai o te whanaungatanga. E whakatairanga ana ngā kaimahi i ngā uaratanga o te manaaki, te tiaki, me te aroha i roto i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakapuakihia anō hoki tēnei tūāhuatanga e ngā tamariki, ā, he tūmāia rātou ki te taunekeneke ki ō rātou hoa me te whānau. Ka toro atu ngā tamariki ki te tautāwhi o ngā pākeke i ngā wā e hiahiatia ana. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te ako hei tuakana, hei teina. Whakamihi ai rātou i ngā tamariki mō ā rātou whakamātau ki te mahi ngātahi me te tohatoha taonga ki te taha o ētahi atu. Kei te harikoa, kei te mauritau hoki ngā tamariki.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i tō rātou tuakiri. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou pepeha. Ka whakarato rātou i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te ako i ō rātou whakapapa, kia mōhio ai rātou ki ō rātou hononga ki te whānau, te hapū, me te iwi. Ka whakamahia hoki ngā waiata ki te taha o ngā tamariki, ki te whakawhanake i tō rātou māramatanga ki ngā hītori o tō rātou hapori e whai pānga ana ki ō rātou tūpuna me ngā pou whenua matua. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te taiao o te hapori, me te haereere ki ngā wāhi whakahirahira. E ngākau nui ana ngā tamariki ki te ako i ō rātou hononga ki te whenua.

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki tō rātou taiao ako. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, me te whakatau hoki i ā rātou akoranga. He whānui ngā tūmomo rauemi hei whakamahinga mā ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te wāhi o waho hei whakawhānui ake i te wāhi ako o ngā tamariki. He auaha tā rātou whakamahi i ngā rauemi, ā, ka whakatairanga tēnei i te ngahau i roto i ngā akoranga. Pānui ai ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki i ia te wā, ā, ka akiaki rātou ki te pānui ki ētahi atu. Ko ngā kākahu whakaari me ngā kaupapa mahi toi ka hāpai i te taha auaha o ngā tamariki. Kei te harikoa ngā tamariki hei ākonga.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He mārama ngā tukanga ā ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere me te aromātai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka mahi ngātahi rātou hei whakapuaki i te whakawhanaketanga me te whakatinanatanga o ngā kaupapa mahi. Ka arotahi ki ngā tūmomo taumahi ka āta tahuri i ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga e whai pānga nui ana ki te ao Māori. E ākina ana te whakapuakitanga o te whānau i ō rātou whakaaro, me ā rātou rauemi. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā ki te aromātai i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga ki te aro atu ki ngā matea ako o ngā rōpū tamariki, o ia tamaiti hoki. Ka whakamahia ngā pūkete hei whakatakoto haere, hei aroturuki haere anō hoki i te ahu whakamua o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ko ngā tamariki e mau ana i ngā matea ako ake, ka tautokona kia tupu, kia ako ngātahi tonu rātou ki te taha o ō rātou hoa. Whakapuaki kōrero ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā ki te taha o ngā whānau mō ā rātou tamariki, tae atu hoki ki ā rātou aromatawai e pā ana ki te ako a ngā tamariki, me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ko te ako o ngā tamariki tētahi aronga matua ki te kōhanga reo.

Kua whakawhanakehia e te whānau tētahi tūtohinga e āta whakatakoto ana i te ahunga whakamua. Ka tautuhi te tūtohinga i ngā tukanga ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga i te kōhanga reo. He mārama ngā tūmanako i roto i ngā mahi whakataunga, te whakahaeretanga, ngā kawenga mahi, me te kawenga takohanga a te whānau. Ka whai te whānau i tētahi huringa taiāwhio ki te arotake i ngā kaupapa here. Ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te hauora, te haumaru, me te whakahaeretanga o ngā kaimahi, e whakatutuki katoa ana i ngā herenga ā-ture. Tū ai ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, ā, he pai te taetae atu. Ka tuhia ngā meneti a ngā hui ā-whānau, kia noho mōhio ai ngā mātua ki ngā āhuatanga e āwangawangatia ana, me ngā mahi hoki ka whai ake hei hāpai i te whakapaitanga atu. Ka toro atu ki ngā ratonga o waho ki te whakatairanga i ngā matea whakangungu o ngā kaimahi me te whānau. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu me te tautoko o tētahi rōpū o te whānau i ngā whakahaeretanga o ia rā. E whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i ngā painga ka puta i tētahi kōhanga reo e āta whakahaerehia ana.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore anō ngā mahi arohaehae kia whakatinanahia, kia tuhia rānei, hei āta whakarite i te hāngaitanga o te whakawhanaketanga ngaio o ngā kaimahi ki te whakapaitanga ake i te kounga o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te wāhanga e whai ake nei, kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture. Hei whakapai ake i āna whakaritenga, me tahuri te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te:

  • āta whakarite i te whakatinanatanga i ia te wā, i te arohaehae a ngā kaimahi.
    [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA7 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • arohaehae te whānau i ngā kaimahi i ia tau, hei hāpai i tō rātou whakawhanaketanga ngaio.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Pekanga i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Pekanga is in the Taranaki township of Waitara. The main building is owned by Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust. Over the years the whānau have renovated and refurbished the building to create a spacious, attractive learning environment for children. Taranaki dialect and kupu is a whānau focus. Values of independence, confidence and whanaungatanga are important to whānau.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children show that they are confident learners?

Children show confidence as learners in all they do.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children learn te reo Māori in a supportive environment. Kaimahi model the Taranaki dialect in the learning programme. They use a range of strategies to encourage children to speak te reo Māori by using prompts, questions and repetition. Kaimahi provide opportunities for children to hear language in formal and informal settings. Older children use the language they know. Babies communicate using body language and facial expressions. Whānau learn te reo Māori alongside their children in the daily programme. Children are developing as speakers of te reo Māori.

Children experience positive relationships. Kaimahi promote the values of manaaki, tiaki and aroha in the learning programme. This reflected by the children who confident to interact with peers and whānau. Children will seek comfort as needed from adults. Kaimahi provide children with opportunities learn as tuakana teina. They regularly praise children for their efforts for being co-operative and sharing with others. Children are happy and content.

Children learn about their identity. Kaimahi support children to share pepeha. They provide children with opportunities to learn their whakapapa to learn about their connections to the whānau, hapū and iwi. Waiata are used with children to develop their understanding about the local histories related to their ancestors and landmarks. Kaimahi provide children with opportunities to explore the local environment and visit areas of significance. Children enjoy learning about their links to the whenua.

Children are developing an awareness of their learning environment. Kaimahi encourage children to follow their interests and make choices in their learning. There is a wide range of resources for children to use in the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi use the outdoor area to extend children’s learning space. They are creative with resources to help promote fun in learning. Kaimahi regularly read to children and encourage them to read with others. Dress up and art activities support children to be creative. Children are happy learners.

Planning and Evaluation

Kaimahi have clear processes for planning and evaluation of children’s learning. They work together to contribute to the development and implementation of kaupapa mahi. There is a focus on activities to engage children in learning that incorporates te ao Māori. Whānau are encouraged to contribute ideas and resources. Kaimahi regularly meet to evaluate how the learning programme responds to group and individual children’s learning needs. Profile books are used to record and monitor children’s learning progress. Children with additional learning needs are supported to grow and learn alongside their peers. Kaimahi regularly share with whānau including their assessment of children’s learning and development. Children’s learning is a priority at the kōhanga reo.

Whānau have developed a charter that provides clear direction. It defines processes to manage kōhanga reo operations. There are clear expectations for whānau decision-making, management, responsibility and accountability. The whānau follow a cycle of policy review. Policies and procedures for health and safety, and personnel management meet legislative requirements. There are regular whānau hui that are well attended. Whānau minutes are documented to keep parents informed about areas of concern, and next steps to support improvement. External agencies are contacted to support kaimahi and whānau training needs. A group of whānau actively participate and support the daily operations. Children benefit from a well-managed kōhanga reo.

Key Next Steps

Appraisal has not been implemented or documented to ensure that kaimahi professional development is focussed on improving the quality of children’s learning.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

ERO identified an area of non-compliance. In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements they need to:

  • ensure appraisal for all employed staff is regularly implemented.
    [R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008, GMA7 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • the whānau appraise kaimahi annually to support their professional development.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Pekanga will be in three years.
