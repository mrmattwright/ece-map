Background

Highland Park Kindergarten has a history of positive ERO reports. The kindergarten in East Auckland, provides education and care for children over two years of age, and operates under the Kindergarten Day Model. Some children stay for the whole day and others for the morning session. There are four fully qualified teachers, a teacher assistant, an administrator and a teacher aide. Specialist teachers in technology and yoga contribute to the richness of the programme.

The Kindergarten operates as part of the Auckland Kindergarten Association (AKA). The association provides policy, procedural and operating guidelines. It also provides teachers with regular, topical professional development and on-going support from professional managers.

ERO‚Äôs 2010 report commented on teachers' high quality interactions and noted how programmes were driven by children‚Äôs interests. Very good teaching practices have been maintained and teachers have further strengthened partnerships with families and their community. Teachers have continued to review and enhance the learning environment with children.

This review was part of a cluster of ten kindergarten reviews in the Auckland Kindergarten Association

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Highland Park Kindergarten completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.
