Background

Central City Quality Edu-care (Pukaki St) is one of two privately owned Education and Care services in central Rotorua. The owner director is also the licensee. She is a fully qualified ECE teacher and works directly with the staff, children and parents. She employs two supervisors to oversee the daily running of the centres and they report directly to her.

Pukaki Street operates on Monday to Friday from 7.30 am until 5.30 pm and is licensed for a maximum of 35 children, including up to 8 under two years of age. Currently 36 children attend the centre and 12 are of MƒÅori descent. It has a culturally diverse roll and teaching team. The teaching team is well qualified and have remained constant with only a small number of changes since the last ERO review in 2013. This has resulted in a strong team culture and close and positive relationships with families and children.

The centre operates a mixed-aged approach where all age groups interact, learn and play together. The centre philosophy encourages children to develop and explore at their own pace through a variety of activities and play-based learning opportunities. The centre aims to create a warm and secure environment that fosters self-confidence and encourages social skill development. Relationships are seen as the key in enhancing children's learning outcomes with strong connections between the centre, children, home environment and extended whƒÅnau.

The centre has made good progress in addressing the recommendations from the previous ERO report in 2013. Self review has been strengthened by encouraging staff to take leadership responsibility in identifying areas for review, and this is leading to continuous improvement in learning opportunities for children, and enhanced teaching practice.

The Review Findings

Children feel included and enjoy trusting relationships with teachers and each other. They demonstrate a strong sense of belonging and a willingness to share their ideas and interests. The challenging but safe environment is well prepared to provide a rich variety of experiences. Children play and explore in both the indoor and outdoor areas using their imagination and creativity in pursuing their interests. Oral language, literacy, mathematics, science and nature are naturally integrated into the programme.

A feature of the programme is a mixed-age approach where children of all ages are able to interact and play with each other in all parts of the centre. The environment promotes children's safety, physical and emotional wellbeing, intellectual stimulation and effectively supports very young children. Infants and toddlers receive responsive caregiving which helps their needs for strong and secure attachments. Older children demonstrate responsible attitudes and care for younger children.

Children learn through play and are encouraged to follow their interests, solve problems and take responsible risks by teachers who know them well. Teachers spend time with children supporting their engagement in self-initiated play. They notice, recognise and respond intentionally to extend learning opportunities for children. Open-ended questions are used to prompt children's thinking. Children are developing independence and are being encouraged to take increasing responsibility for their own learning and wellbeing. Teachers are particularly skilled in helping children with additional learning needs. They work with parents and external agencies to identify areas for development and share useful skills and approaches in supporting these children.

All children and their families are welcomed into the centre. Children are understood as unique learners, and accepted and celebrated for who they are. Teachers are culturally responsive and promote the identity and culture of each child. Te reo MƒÅori, tikanga and bicultural practices are integrated into the programme in authentic contexts. All parents interviewed by ERO were pleased with the way the individuality and identity of their children's culture are celebrated and fostered.

Programme planning uses ideas from children's individual interests as well as a balance of group interests, which respond to children's fascination with a chosen topic. An important part of planning is the knowledge that children bring with them to the centre and share with others. Children's interests, learning and progress are recorded in attractive individual portfolio books and they enjoy reading and sharing these books.

Children benefit from effective transition procedures to support them on entering the centre or on entry to school. Procedures include visits, liaison with schools, sharing of information and support for parents. A transition programme operates for older children who choose to be involved and this provides a fun and interesting way of developing skills and knowledge that is likely to benefit them on school entry. Children demonstrate high levels of enthusiasm and interest in this programme.

The knowledgeable and experienced director and head teacher provide strong collaborative leadership for the teaching team. They empower teachers to use their strengths and interests to take on leadership roles. The team shares a commitment to beliefs and values about children's learning and wellbeing. Teachers are reflective practitioners who use self review to continually enhance their teaching practice.

Teachers have been involved in effective professional development and learning that has promoted enthusiasm, and increased their confidence and engagement in learning and teaching. Currently teachers are working on two main areas identified through self-review. These relate to enhancing the links between centre and home thereby creating a more holistic learning environment, and encouraging communication through enhanced displays of planning and learning stories.

Key Next Steps

Senior Management and ERO agreed that it would be timely to:

  • review strategic planning to manage ongoing changes and identify goals for future centre development
  • continue developing effective appraisal systems for both teaching and non-teaching staff.
