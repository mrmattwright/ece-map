1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E whakahaerehia ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Manawa Tapu o Te Kupu i tētahi whare i hangaia ai hei kōhanga reo, ki te taha o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Reo Rangatira ki Whaiora. Kei raro aua kōhanga reo e rua i te maru o te marae o Whaiora, ki Ōtara, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau ki te tonga. Kua raihanatia te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki kei raro i te rua o ngā tau, ā, ka poipoi Te Manawa Tapu o Te Kupu i ngā tamariki kua pakeke ake i te rua o ngā tau.

Ka whakarato te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Manawa Tapu o Te Kupu i tētahi taiao e wāteatea ana, e noho ātaahua ana hoki mō ngā pēpi. He muramura ngā kara ki te rūma tākaro o ngā pēpi. Kua whakaritea ki te wāhi ako wāteatea te maha o ngā wāhi tākaro, wāhi ako hoki, me ngā rauemi, ngā taumahi hoki e whakawero ana, e whakahihiri ana i ngā pēpi. Kei te wāhi tākaro o waho ētahi atu wāhi tākaro, wāhi ako anō hoki.

I whakaritea e ngā kaimahi ko Mana Whenua te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ka āta hāpai ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ā rātou tamariki kia tau ai rātou ki ngā hononga, ngā uaratanga, me ngā whakapono i tuku iho ai. Ka akiaki rātou i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i te tino aronga toi whenuatanga. Ko te noho mōhio ki te taiao me te tiaki hoki i te taiao ētahi atu tino āria matua ka whakapuakihia e te whānau mā ā rātou tamariki. I ngā wā katoa ka whakatauira, ka whakapūmau hoki te kaiako i te reo Māori, puta noa i te hōtaka o ia rā.

He pai te mahi ngātahi a ngā kaiwhakahaere me te whānau ki te whakahaere i ō rātou kōhanga reo e rua. Pūrongo ai te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā tūmomo wāhanga o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka mōhio mārika rātou ki ngā āhuatanga hei whakapai ake, me ngā ekenga angitu hoki. Me arotake, me whakahou ake hoki te whakamahere rautaki.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga reo (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Whenua.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Whenua

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whakawhanaketanga o te māia me te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou tuakiri, ā rātou taonga tuku iho, me tō rātou reo hoki?

He tino pai te whakawhanaketanga o te māia me te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou tuakiri, ā rātou taonga tuku iho, me tō rātou reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te ako i te Mana Whenua. He kaha te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i te maha o ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te ako i te Mana Whenua. Ka whai wāhi ngā pēpi ki ngā mihimihi ki te taha o ō rātou tuākana, ā, ka rongo rātou i ngā tuākana e taki ana i ō rātou pepeha, ā, e mihi ana ki te whānau. Ka whakarato te whānau i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te taunekeneke me ētahi atu o te hapori whānui. Ka whakapuakihia ngā kōrero tuku iho e pā ana ki a Matariki me Tangaroa, mā ngā pēpi.

Te reo Māori. E tino tautokona ana te puāwaitanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. I ngā wā katoa, ka whakatauira, ka whakapūmau hoki te kaiako i te reo Māori puta noa i te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā āhuatanga katoa o te tikanga me te kawa, tae atu ki ngā pōhiri me ngā whakatau. Ka whai hua ngā mahi a ngā pākeke ki te akiaki i te puāwaitanga o te reo.

Te hōtaka. Kua waihangatia e ngā kaimahi tētahi taiao e whakaongaonga ana i ngā tamariki ki te ako. He whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga, te tākaro mō te wā roa, me te tūhura haere. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā tūmomo taumahi, rauemi anō hoki ki ngā tamariki, puta noa i te rā. Ka hāpai aua taumahi i ngā tamariki ki te waihanga, te ako i te tīmatanga o ngā āria pāngarau, reo matatini hoki, me te whakawhanake i ō rātou pūkenga ā-tinana, whakaaro pohewa hoki. Kua tau hoki ki ngā tamariki ngā mahinga e pā ana ki te kai, te moe, me te haere ki te whare paku.

E pou here ana te whanaungatanga i ngā taunekeneke katoa. He atawhai, he manaaki hoki ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā pākeke. Ka tino whakatutukihia ngā matea ā-tinana, ā-whatumanawa hoki o ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whai hua ki te whakatutuki i te ako a ngā tamariki me ngā wawata o te whānau, mā te āta whakahaeretanga a te whānau?

E tino whakatutukihia ana te ako a ngā tamariki, ngā wawata hoki o te whānau mā te pai o te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga i te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau. He pai te mahi ngātahi a ngā kaiwhakahaere me te whānau ki te whakahaere i ngā kōhanga reo e rua. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā hononga e tautoko ana, mā te noho mārama tonu o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero. Ka hāngai ngā kaupapa here me ngā aratohu ki ngā whāinga me ngā tūmanako o Te Korowai. Kua tohaina ngā tūmomo kawenga mahi ki ngā mema o te whānau, tae atu ki te kohi pūtea, te arotake, te whakahou ake hoki o te tūtohinga me ngā kaupapa here, me te whakahaere i te whare me ngā rawa.

Te whakamahere me te aromātai. He matawhānui te kiko o ngā mahere a ngā kaimahi mō te tau, me ia marama, ia wiki anō hoki. Ka āta kitea ki ngā mahere ngā hononga ki Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira me Te Whāriki. Ka aro nui ia kaupapa ako ki ngā tino āria, ngā taumahi, me ngā tūmomo rauemi e whai pānga ana. E ākina ana te whānau kia whakapuaki ō rātou whakaaro, ā rātou rauemi hoki mō ngā mahere. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti kōrero mō te whai huatanga o ngā kaupapa, me ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā hōtaka i āta whakamaheretia ai.

Te arotake whaiaro. Ka whai pūtake te arotake whaiaro, ā, ka whakatutukihia i ia te wā. Pūrongo ai te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. E mōhio ana rātou ki tēnā hei whakapai ake, me ā rātou whakatutukitanga. Ka arotahi te whānau ki te whakapai tonu i ngā wā katoa.

Te aromatawai. He pai te māramatanga o ngā kaimahi ki te aromatawai. Ka whakamahi rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki hei aroturuki, hei aromatawai hoki i te ahunga whakamua o ia tamaiti. Ko ētahi o aua āhuatanga ko ngā kōrero urupare a te whānau, ngā kitenga o ia rā, me ngā aromatawai o ia wiki. Kua waia ngā kaimahi ki ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā pūmanawa o ia tamaiti, me ngā taumata ako anō hoki ka whāia tonuhia. Ka tuhi haere ngā kaimahi i ngā ekenga angitu, ngā wheako hoki a ngā tamariki, ki ā rātou pūkete, ā rātou puka hoki. E noho wātea ana aua mōhiohio ki ngā whānau hei kōrerotanga anō hoki mā rātou.

Te wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Te whakawhanaketanga i te mahere rautaki. Ahakoa he mahere rautaki, kei te mōhio hoki te whānau he āhuatanga tēnei hei whakawhanake ake. He whānui rawa ētahi whāinga, ā, kāhore he tino kiko kia āhei ai te whānau ki te whakariterite ake.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia tahuri te whānau o Te Manawa Tapu o Te Kupu me Te Reo Rangatira ki Whaiora ki te arotake, te whakahou ake hoki i tā rātou mahere rautaki.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura-Watson Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu mō ngā Ratonga Arotake

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Manawa Tapu o Te Kupu operates out of a purpose built building alongside Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Reo Rangatira ki Whaiora. These kōhanga reo are part of the Whaiora Marae complex in Otara, South Auckland. This kōhanga is licensed for children under two years old only and Te Reo Rangatira ki Whaiora provides for children over two years old.

The whānau and kaimahi of Te Manawa Tapu o Te Kupu provide a spacious and inviting environment for babies. The babies’ playroom is colourful and bright. The open plan learning area has several designated play and learning areas with resources and activities that provide challenge and interest for babies. The outdoor play area includes further play and learning spaces.

Kaimahi chose mana whenua as the focus for this review. Kaimahi and whānau support their children well to become familiar with ancestral connections, values and beliefs. They encourage children to develop a strong sense of belonging. Environmental awareness and care are also critical concepts whānau impart to their children. The kaiako consistently model and reinforce te reo Māori throughout the daily programme.

The administrator and whānau work well together to jointly manage and organise their two kōhanga reo. Whānau and kaimahi regularly report on different parts of the kōhanga reo operations. They are highly aware of what needs to be improved and their successes. Strategic planning needs to be reviewed and updated.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana whenua.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Mana Whenua

Background

How well are children developing confidence and knowledge in their identity, heritage and language?

Children are developing very well in their confidence and knowledge of their identity, heritage and language.

Areas of good performance

Mana whenua learning. Children have a strong sense of belonging. Kaimahi provide children with many opportunities to learn about mana whenua. Babies participate in mihimihi with their tuakana where they hear tuakana recite their pepeha and mihi to the whānau. The whānau provides opportunities for children to interact with others in the local and wider community. Stories about Matariki and Tangaroa are shared with babies.

Te reo Māori. Children are well supported as developing speakers of te reo Māori. The kaiako consistently model and reinforce te reo Māori throughout the day. Children participate in all aspects of tikanga and kawa including pōhiri and whakatau. Adults encourage children’s early language development in positive ways.

Programme. Kaimahi have created an environment that stimulates children to learn. Children have opportunities to follow their interests, play for extended periods and explore. Kaimahi provide children with various activities and resources throughout the day. These activities enable children to create, learn early numeracy and literacy concepts and develop their physical and imaginative skills. Children have set routines for eating, sleeping and toileting.

Whanaungatanga underpins all interactions. Interactions between children and adults are warm and caring. Children’s physical and emotional needs are met promptly.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How well are children’s learning and whānau aspirations being met through whānau management?

Children’s learning and whānau aspirations are very well met through the effective management of the kōhanga reo operations.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management.The kaiwhakahaere and whānau work well together to manage the two kōhanga reo. Kaimahi and whānau encourage supportive relationships through clear and open communication. Policies and guidelines reflect the goals and objectives ofTe Korowai.Whānau have allocated specific areas of responsibilities including fundraising, charter and policy reviews and updates and property management.

Planningand evaluation. Kaimahi have comprehensive and detailed plans for the year and each month and week. Planning shows clear links to Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira and Te Whāriki. Each learning topic highlights major themes, activities and the resources that are required. Whānau are encouraged to contribute their ideas and resources to planning. Kaimahi meet regularly to discuss the effectiveness of kaupapa and different aspects of the daily programme. Children experience well planned programmes.

Self review. Self review is purposeful and regular. Whānau and kaimahi regularly report on different parts of the kōhanga reo operations. They are aware of what needs to be improved and their successes. Whānau are focussed on continual improvement.

Assessment. Kaimahi have a good understanding of assessment. They use a range of strategies to monitor and assess each child’s progress. Some of these include whānau feedback, daily observations and weekly assessments. Kaimahi are familiar with each child’s interests, strengths and next learning steps. Kaimahi report children’s successes and experiences in their portfolio folders and notebooks. This information is readily available to whānau for comment.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO LicensedKōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends the whānau of Te Manawa Tapu o Te Kupu and Te Reo Rangatira ki Whaiora review and update their strategic plan.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
