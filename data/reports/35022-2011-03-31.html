2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Waikato taniwharau

he piko he taniwha, he piko he taniwha

Kei te Pā o Waahi, Te Kōhanga Reo o Rāhui Pōkeka. Ko te nuinga o ngā tamariki, he uri nō te Kīngitanga. E ū ana a Waahi ki tōna tūnga hei kāinga o te Kāhui Ariki. Kei konei a Whatumoana e noho ana, ā, ko ia te hoa rangatira o te Ariki Tapairu, a Te Atairangikaahu, kua huri kē ki tua o te ārai. E noho wātea ana te pā ki ngā tamariki. Ka whai wāhi atu te kōhanga ki ngā huihuinga pērā i te poukai, te koroneihana, te riketa, me ētahi atu huihuinga nui o te marae. He māramatanga tō ngā tamariki ki te whai pānga o te Kīngitanga ki a rātou. He pakari ngā hononga o te kōhanga ki Te Wharekura o Rākaumangamanga. Tokomaha ngā mema o whānau o te kōhanga, e mahi ana ki te kura hei kaiako, hei kaiāwhina rānei.

E whakarato ana ngā kaimahi i tētahi hōtaka akoranga e whakatutuki ana i ngā matea o ngā tamariki katoa. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki tētahi hōtaka e whakapuaki tika ana i ngā akoranga kua whakaritea, me ngā akoranga takitahi. Nā te whānuitanga o ngā taumahi, e āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i tō rātou taiao, te hīraurau hopanga, me te mahi auaha, ā, kei te whakawerohia ā-tinana, ā-hinengaro hoki.

He kaha ngā kaimahi ki te mātakitaki i ngā tamariki. E wātea ana rātou ki te whakawhānui i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, i te wā e tika ana. Kua tae ki te wā, kia whai whakaaro ngā kaimahi ki te whakatakotoranga o te wāhi o-roto. Me whai whakaaro rātou ki ngā huarahi kia wātea ai ngā tamariki ki te tākaro, me te kore pōrearea o ētahi atu āhuatanga ki ā rātou tākaro. Ko te āta whakamahi i ngā wāhi kia pai ake ai te rere haere ki tēnā, me tēnā o ngā rauemi.

Ki tō te whānau titiro, ko te reo Māori te kaupapa matua. He mātau ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whakaū ki te reo ā-iwi o Waikato, puta noa i te rā. Ka whakaū ngā kaimahi ki tētahi taiao reo Māori, mō te roanga o te rā. Ka whakamahi rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā rautaki, hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki hei kaikōrero pakari o te reo Māori.

He tukanga ā ngā kaimahi ki te whakamāhere i te hōtaka akoranga, me te aroturuki i ngā akoranga o ā rātou tamariki. E noho mōhio ana te whānau ki ngā āhuatanga e akohia ana e ā rātou tamariki, me tā rātou ahunga whakamua ki te kōhanga. Engari, ko te aromātai i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka, he āhuatanga hei whakapakari.

E whakaū ana te whānau ki ngā pūnaha papai e hāngai ana ki te whakahaeretanga me te whakaritenga o tō rātou kōhanga. E noho mārama ana rātou ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki. Kei te whānau te whakamāhere rautaki, me ngā kaupapa here, ngā tukanga hoki e pā ana ki te hauora, te haumaru, te pūtea, me ngā take hoki e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi. He mea nui i nāianei, kia whai whakaaro te whānau ki te aroturuki me te pūrongo i te māhere rautaki, me te aroturuki i te pūtea, i runga i ngā utunga.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Rāhui Pōkeka kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Rāhui Pōkeka.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Atua

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Atua

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Atua te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E whakapono ana te whānau, mā ngā tamariki o tēnei hāpori e kōkiri ā meāke nei, ā, nā tēnei, ka poipoia tō rātou waiora, tō rātou aronga toiwhenuatanga hoki. Ko Tangaroa me te Kirihimete ngā kaupapa o te wā. E āta hono ana te kaupeka o Raumati ki ngā whakanuitanga o te Kirihimete. E aromātai ana tēnei arotake i te whānuitanga o te kōhanga ki te poipoi i te waiora o ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te hōtaka akoranga.Kua whakawhanakehia e ngā kaimahi tētahi hōtaka, kia āhei ai ngā tamariki ki te kite i a rātou anō hei ākonga pakari. He maha ngā huarahi e whakamahia ana, hei whakatutuki i tēnei āhuatanga.

Ka wheako ngā tamariki ki tētahi hōtaka e whakarato ana ki te a rātou, te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi akoranga. E hāpai ana ngā taumahi nei i ngā tamariki, me ta rātou whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. Ka whakaratohia ki ngā tamariki ngā whai wāhitanga ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga, te whiriwhiri i ā rātou ake taputapu, me te kawe i ā rātou ake akoranga.

Ka whakarōpū ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki, i runga i tō rātou reanga tau. Ka whakatutuki ngā tamariki tuākana i ngā taumahi e pā ana ki te kaupapa. He tika ngā taumahi nei. Ka noho rātou mō ngā wā roa, e aro atu ana ki ā rātou mahi. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki nei ki te mahi auaha, me te tūhura i ngā rauemi toi. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki tēina i ngā taumahi e wātea ake ana mō rātou. E āhei ana rātou ki te tūhura, te whakanao, te hanga, me te hīraurau hopanga. E wheako ana ngā tamariki katoa i tētahi taiao e whakanuia ana i ā rātou tākaro, hei akoranga e whai tikanga ana.

He pai te mātakitaki a ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki, i ngā wā katoa. Kāhore rātou e whakapōrearea i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka hāpai, ka whakawhānui hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā whakaaro o ngā tamariki, i ngā wā e tika ana. Ka whai wāhi atu rātou ki ngā tamariki i ngā wā tika ka pupū ake. I ngā wā katoa, ka āta whakarite ngā kaimahi i te ū tonutanga ki ngā tatauranga tika ki waenga i te pakeke me ngā tamariki.

He whanaungatanga ki waenga i ngā tamariki, i runga i tō rātou whakapapa. Ko ō rātou hononga ki te marae me te Wharekura o Rākaumangamanga, e whakarite ana i tō rātou māramatanga ki tō rātou tūnga, puta atu i te kōhanga. Nā te hononga ki waenga i te kura me te kōhanga, he ngāwari te whakawhiti atu o ngā tamariki ki te kura. Tokomaha ngā whānau i noho ai ki konei, mai i te whakatūnga o te kōhanga. Nā te pakari o ō rātou hononga ā-hapū, ā-iwi hoki, e whakakoia ana ngā tamariki, hei taonga o tō rātou hāpori.

Ka āta whakarite ngā kaimahi, kia rere ngāwari ai ngā whakawhitinga ki waenga i te hōtaka me ngā mahinga. Ka whakamōhiotia ngā tamariki, i mua i te mahinga, mō ngā mahi kei mua i a rātou. Nā tēnei, ka tīmata ngā tamariki ki te whakapaipai i te wā e tika ana. Kua āta whakaritea ngā mahinga. Ka whakarite ngā kaimahi i ngā taumahi, i mua tonu i ngā mahi, kia kore ai tēnei e taupatupatu i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, i a rātou e whakawhiti ana, mai i ngā mahinga, ki te hōtaka. Ka whakawhanake ngā tamariki i te noho māoriori, i te wā he riterite, he wāia hoki ngā tukanga.

Te reo Māori.E mātau ana ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā rautaki e tika ana, hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, hei whakamārama, hei patapatai, hei whakamōhiohio, hei horopaki hoki mō ngā tamariki, i a rātou e mahi ana, e tākaro ana hoki. Ka whakamahi hoki i te waiata me te pānui pukapuka, hei whakapakari i te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori, me tā rātou whakamahinga i te reo. Ka whakamahia te reo ā-iwi o Waikato i ngā wā katoa. Ka wheako ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga, i tētahi taiao e whakatū ana i te reo Māori hei reo matua.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te taiao akoranga o-roto.He tino wāteatea te taiao o te kōhanga. He nui ngā whai wāhitanga ki te whakariterite i ngā wāhi akoranga, hei whakatairanga i ngā whai wāhitanga akoranga o ngā tamariki. I tēnei wā, kua whakaritea ngā taumahi, kia kore ai e pōrearea i ā rātou akoranga. He tata rawa ngā taumahi wāhanga ki ngā wāhi tākaro wāteatea o ngā tamariki. He maha ngā wā ka pōreareatia ngā wāhi tākaro o ngā tamariki, nā ngā tamariki e hīkoi atu ana ki te whakapā atu ki ētahi atu taumahi. Ka tino āwangawanga ngā tamariki i aua wā. Kāhore e āta whakamahi ana i ētahi wāhi, hei āta whakanui i te whakamahinga tōtika o tēnei taiao.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He mātātoa te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga. Ko te kaiwhakahaere tari e noho pūmau ana ki ngā whakahaeretanga. Ka āta whakarite ia i te whakatūnga me te whakapūmautanga o ngā pūnaha ki te kōhanga. He pūkenga, he mātanga hoki tō te tokomaha o ngā mema o te whānau, i roto i te whakahaere tari, ā, ka āta whakamahia ngā pūmanawa nei. E arotake ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te whai huatanga o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakamāhere me te aromatawai.He tukanga ā ngā kaimahi, ki te whakamāhere hōtaka me te aroturuki i te ahunga whakamua me ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka kitea ngā āhuatanga matua o ngā māhere mō te wā roa, me te wā waenga. Kua āta whakatakotohia te kiko o ngā putanga akoranga, o te horopaki o ngā akoranga, o ngā rautaki a ngā kaimahi ki te ako me te whakaako, me tētahi rārangi o ngā kupu hou e hono ana ki te kaupapa. He tino matawhānui te whakamāhere. E āhei ana te whānau ki te āta aroturuki i ngā āhuatanga e akohia ana e ā rātou tamariki.

Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi ngā huarahi ki te aroturuki i te ahunga whakamua o ngā tamariki. Kei roto i ngā pūkete ngā mōhiohio tōtika. Kua tuhia ngā whakapapa me ngā pepeha o ngā tamariki. Kei roto hoki i ngā pūkete, ko tētahi māhere hōtaka mō taua wāhanga. E hono ana ngā tauira mahi toi a ngā tamariki, ki te kaupapa. He whakaahua hoki e whakaatu ana i ngā tamariki e aro atu ana ki ngā akoranga. Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi ngā mōhiohio nei ki ngā pūkete, hei āta tāutu i ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā māhere wā-roa, me ngā māhere wā-waenga. E whakarato ana ngā pakiwaitara akoranga i ngā mōhiohio e whai hua ana ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He pai te noho mōhio o te whānau ki te ahunga whakamua o ā rātou tamariki.

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau.E ū ana te whānau ki ngā pūnaha e whai hua ana ki te whānuitanga o ngā wāhanga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga me te whakaritenga o tō rātou kōhanga. E noho mārama ana rātou ki ō rātou tūranga, me ā rātou kawenga mahi. Tū ai ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, ā, he pai te tae ā-tinana atu o te whānau. He tino matawhānui ngā pūrongo a ngā kaimahi. Kei te rawaka te whakahaeretanga me te pūrongo i te pūtea. He pai te whakawhitinga kōrero ki waenga i te kōhanga me te whānau, ā, e whai hua ana hoki. Ka tautoko te whānau, ā, ka whai wāhi ki te maha o ngā tūmomo mahi, ki te whakahaere i tō rātou kōhanga. E tohu ana ngā kaupapa here me ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi, i te tū pai o te whānau hei kaituku mahi. He pai te tiaki o te taiao o te kōhanga. E whakahaere ana te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga, hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Te whakamāhere rautaki.Ka āta whakapuaki te whānau i ā rātou kaupapa matua mō te kōhanga reo. E arotahi ana te tauāki taketake me te tirohanga, ki ngā akoranga o te kounga pai, me te whakahaeretanga o te whānau. Kua tāutuhia te whānuitanga o ngā wāhanga rautaki matua. E hāngai ana ēnei ki te kounga o ngā pūnaha whakahaere, te kounga o te whakatū kaimahi, te kounga o ngā hōtaka, me te kounga o ngā putanga mō ā rātou tamariki. He whāinga mārama hoki ki te whakatutuki i ēnei kaupapa matua.

Te hauora me te haumaru.E ārahi ana ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga a te whānau, i ngā whakaritenga a ngā kaimahi, hei āta whakarite i te noho haumaru, te noho hauora hoki o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga. Kua arotakengia ngā kaupapa here katoa e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru, ā, kua tuhia ki te rā. Ka whakatutuki haeretia ngā arowhai rawa, ka tiaki pai i ngā whare, mō tēnei wā tonu te rēhita tikanga o ngā whare, ā, ka whakatutukihia ngā tikanga hōneatanga i ia te wā. E noho ngākau nui ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whakanui matua ana i tō rātou hauora, tō rātou haumaru hoki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te aromātai hōtaka.Me whakapakari te aromātai hōtaka. Nō nā tata tonu nei noa ngā kaimahi i tīmata ai ki te tuhi haere i ā rātou whakawhitinga kōrero ki ngā hui kaimahi. Ko te nuinga o ngā kaupapa e kōrerohia ana, he take hei whakatutuki kē ki ngā hui ā-whānau. He mea nui kia arotahi ngā kaimahi ki te hōtaka akoranga, me te whakawhiti kōrero mō ngā huarahi ki te whakapai haere, hei whakatairanga i te kounga o ngā akoranga mō ngā tamariki.

Te pūrongo i runga i te māhere rautaki.Ahakoa he māhere rautaki tā te whānau, he iti noa te aroturuki i te ahunga whakamua ki te whakatutuki i ngā whāinga. He mea nui kia aroturuki te whānau i tā rātou ahunga whakamua, hei tāutu i ngā āhuatanga e whakahaere pai ana, me ngā wāhanga hoki hei whakarerekē, hei whakapai rānei.

Te aroturuki i te pūtea.He rawaka ngā pūnaha a te whānau mō te pūtea, engari kāhore rātou e aroturuki ana i ngā utunga, i runga i te tahua. Me whakatū te whānau i ngā tukanga mārama, hei āta whakarite i te rawaka o ngā pūtea ki te whakatutuki i ngā whāinga i whakaraupapahia ki ngā māhere rautaki, ngā māhere whakahaere hoki.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

Te Kōhanga Reo o Rāhui Pōkeka I mua o te arotake, i whakakiia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau me ngā kaimahi o, tētahiTauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhangame tētahiRārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau;
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga;
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi;
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino);
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho);
  • ngā tohu mātaurangame ngā whakaritengakaimahi; me
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Kīhai ngā tirohanga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i tāutu i ētahi āhuatanga e āwangawangatia ana.

6 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakaae te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo kia:

6.1 arotake te whānau i ā rātou whakaritenga e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i whakaraupapahia ai ki tēnei pūrongo hei whakapai ake.

7 Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

Waikato taniwharau

he piko he taniwha, he piko he taniwha

Rāhui Pōkeka Kōhanga Reo is situated on Waahi Pā. Many of the children are descendants of the Kīngitanga. Waahi maintains an important spiritual role as the home of the Kāhui Ariki, the paramount family. It is where Whatumoana, the husband of the late Ariki Tapairu Te Atairangikaahu resides. The children have open access to the pā. This kōhanga participates in events related to poukai, koroneihana, regatta and any significant events that the marae hosts. Children have an understanding of the influence the Kingitanga has on their lives. The kōhanga has also established close relationships with Te Wharekura o Rākaumangamanga. Many of the whānau of the kōhanga are kaiako or kaiāwhina at the kura.

Kaimahi provide a learning programme that caters for the needs of all children. Children participate in a programme that offers a balance of structured and independent learning. The range of activities enables children to explore their environment, problem solve, be creative and be challenged both physically and intellectually.

Kaimahi provide good supervision for children. They are available to extend on children’s learning when appropriate. It is timely for kaimahi to now consider the layout of the indoor area. They need to think of ways in which children’s play can occur without interruptions. Optimum use of the areas would enable a smoother flow between the activities.

The whānau consider te reo Māori is a priority. The kaimahi are competent speakers of te reo Māori. Waikato dialect is maintained at all times. Kaimahi maintain a Māori speaking environment throughout the day. They use a range of strategies to support children’s language development. Children are developing as confident speakers of te reo Māori.

Kaimahi have processes for planning the learning programme and monitoring their children’s learning. Whānau are well informed of what their children are learning and the progress they are making at kōhanga. However, evaluating the effectiveness of the programme needs to be strengthened.

Whānau maintain effective systems related to the operations and management of their kōhanga. They are clear about their roles and responsibilities. Whānau have a strategic plan and policies and procedures for health and safety, finance and personnel matters. It is now important for whānau to consider monitoring and reporting on the strategic plan and monitoring the budget against expenditure.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

4 Findings

Mana Atua

Background

The whānau choose Mana Atua as the focus for this review. Whānau believe children are the future of this community and because of this their wellbeing and belonging is nurtured. The kaupapa is currently Tangaroa and Christmas. The season of summer is closely linked to the celebration of Christmas. This review evaluates the extent to which the kōhanga caters for the well-being of their children.

Areas of good performance

Learning programme.The kaimahi have developed a programme that enables children to recognise themselves as confident learners. This happens in a variety of ways.

Children experience a programme that provides them with a range of learning activities. These activities support children to follow their own interests. Children are provided with opportunities to make decisions, choose their own materials and take responsibility for their own learning.

Kaimahi organise children into groups based on their ages. The older children undertake activities based on the kaupapa. The activities are appropriate. They sit for long periods of time engaged in their work. These children have the opportunity to be creative and explore art media. The younger children participate in activities that are more open ended. They are able to explore, manipulate, construct and problem solve. All children experience an environment where their play is viewed as meaningful learning.

Kaimahi provide effective supervision for children at all times. They do not disrupt children’s learning. Kaimahi provide support and extend children’s thinking when the time is right. They become involved in the play if the opportunity arises. Kaimahi ensure that adult to child ratios are maintained at all times.

Children have established relationships based on whakapapa. Their links with the local marae and Rākaumangamanga Wharekura ensure that they understand their place beyond the walls of the kōhanga. Because of the relationship between the kura and kōhanga, the transition for children to kura is smooth. Many whānau have been here since the establishment of the kōhanga. Because of their strong hapū and iwi links, the children are affirmed as valued members of their community.

Kaimahi ensure that transitions between programme and routines are smooth. Children are informed ahead of time about what is going to happen. Because of this children begin to tidy up when required. Routines are well established. Kaimahi prepare activities in advance to ensure there are no disruptions to the children’s learning as they transition from routines back into the programme. Children develop a sense of security when processes are consistent and familiar.

Te reo Māori.Kaimahi are competent speakers of te reo Māori. They use appropriate strategies to support children’s te reo Māori development. The kaimahi use conversations to describe, question, provide information and content for children as they work and play. Waiata and pānui pukapuka are also used to build on children’s knowledge and use of te reo Māori. Waikato dialect is used consistently. Children experience an environment where te reo Māori is the only language used in kōhanga reo.

Areas for improvement

Indoor learning environment.The kōhanga environment is very spacious. It presents wonderful opportunities for organising learning areas to optimise children’s learning opportunities. As it is, activities are organised in ways that disrupt their learning. Quiet activities are too close to areas of play where children need a lot of space. There are constant interruptions of play as children walk where others are playing to access other activities. Children get very upset. Some areas have wasted space which could be used to enable maximum use of the environment.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

The whānau are active in the management of their kōhanga. The administrator is a key member of the operations. She ensures that kōhanga systems are established and maintained. A number of whānau members have skills and expertise in areas of administration which are well used. ERO reviews the effectiveness of the management of the kōhanga.

Areas of good performance

Planning and assessment.The kaimahi have processes for programme planning and monitoring children’s progress and learning. Essential elements of long term and medium term plans are evident. Learning outcomes, content of learning, kaimahi strategies for learning and teaching and a list of new words linked to the kaupapa are clearly detailed. Planning is very comprehensive. Whānau are able to monitor clearly what their children are learning.

Kaimahi have established methods for monitoring children’s progress. Profiles include relevant information. Children’s whakapapa and pepeha are listed. A programme plan for the term is included. Samples of children’s art work link to kaupapa mahi. There are photos of children engaged in learning. Kaimahi have organised the information in the profiles to clearly identify the links between the long term and medium term plans. Learning stories provide some useful information about children’s learning. Whānau are well informed of their children’s progress.

Whānau management.The whānau maintains effective systems in a number of areas related to the operations and management of their kōhanga. They are clear about their roles and responsibilities. Whānau hui are held regularly and well attended. Kaiako reporting is very comprehensive. Whānau are actively involved in all decisions related to their kōhanga. Financial management and reporting is adequate. Communications between the kōhanga and whānau are open and effective. Whānau are supportive and participate in various ways to the operations of their kōhanga. The personnel policies and practices indicate the whānau are good employers. The physical environment is well maintained. Whānau manage their kōhanga in the best interests of their children.

Strategic planning.Whānau clearly articulate their priorities for the kōhanga reo. The mission statement and vision focus on good quality learning and whānau management. A number of key strategic areas have been identified. These focus on quality management systems, quality staffing, quality programmes and quality outcomes for their children. There is a set of clear objectives to achieve these priorities.

Health and safety.Whānau policies and procedures guide kaimahi practice to ensure that the children have a safe and healthy kōhanga. All health and safety policies have been reviewed and are up to date. Property inspections are carried out, buildings are well maintained, the building warrant of fitness is current and evacuation drills are regularly carried out. The children enjoy an environment where their health and safety is paramount.

Areas for improvement

Programme evaluation.Programme evaluation needs strengthening. Kaimahi have only recently begun recording their discussions during staff hui. Most of the matters discussed are issues that need to be addressed at whānau hui. It is important that kaimahi focus on the learning programme with discussions about how improvements can be made to raise the quality of learning for children.

Reporting against strategic plan.While the whānau has a strategic plan, there is very little monitoring of progress towards achieving the objectives. It is important that whānau monitor their progress to identify what is going well and what areas may need to be adapted or improved.

Monitoring the budget.Whānau have adequate finance systems but they do not monitor expenditure against the budget. Whānau should establish clear procedures for monitoring outgoing payments against priorities set in the budget. This will ensure that funding is adequate to achieve the goals outlined in the strategic and management plans.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Te Kōhanga Reo o Rāhui Pōkeka Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff ofcompleted an EROKōhanga Whānau Assurance StatementandSelf-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration;
  • health, safety and welfare;
  • personnel management; and
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse);
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures);
  • staff qualifications and organisation; and
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

ERO’s investigations did not identify any areas of concern.

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
