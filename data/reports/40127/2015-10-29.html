1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hikuwai i Rotoiti. He hononga-ā-whakapapa ō ngā tamariki katoa me te whānau ki a Ngāti Pikiao me Te Arawa.

Ka tono ngā mātua i a rātou tamariki ki tēnei kōhanga kia ako ai rātou i ngā kōrero mō Ngāti Pikiao, mō ō rātou whanaunga ake, mō te reo, mō te ahurea Māori me te tuakiritanga. Ia te rā ka kuhu atu ngā koroua me ngā kuia ki te kōhanga reo ki te whakatauira i te āhua o te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. Ka whakatairanga rātou i te Ngāti Pikiaotanga mō ngā tamariki.

He tokomaha tonu ngā whānau me ngā kaimahi kua roa e piri ana ki te kōhanga reo nei. He nui tā rātou whakapau kaha ki te whakarato i ngā teiteitanga o te mātauranga ki a rātou tamariki mokopuna.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēwhea rawa te pai o te mātau o ngā tamariki ki ngā kōrero mō Ngāti Pikiao?

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ko wai rātou, ā, e pakari haere ana tō rātou mōhio ki ngā kōrero mō Ngāti Pikiao.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E mātau ana ngā tamariki ki ngā wāhi o Ngāti Pikiao, ki ngā tāngata, ki te reo me ngā tikanga. E hāngai pū ana te taiao ako o ngā tamariki ki te whanaungatanga. Ia te rā ka kuhu atu ngā kuia me ngā koroua o te takiwā ki te whakarato i te tautoko i roto i te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. E mātau ana ngā tamariki ki ngā marae o te rohe me ngā āhuatanga whakahirahira, arā, ki te moana me ngā maunga. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki ngā pōwhiri, ka taki karakia, ka manaaki manuwhiri hoki. Ka ako ngā tamariki i te pepehā o Ngāti Pikiao. Kei te mōhio ngā tamariki ko wai rātou, nō whea hoki rātou.

He māia ngā tamariki, e hāneanea ana hoki te noho i roto i te kōhanga reo. He aroha pono, he wairua tiaki ō ngā kaimahi me te whānau mō ngā tamariki. He māia tā ngā tamariki wero i a rātou anō i roto i ngā akoranga i te taha o ngā mātua e whakapono nei rātou. He āio, he tau, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki.

Ka whai haere ngā tamariki i o rātou e hiahia ana, ā, ka kuhu ki ngā kaupapa mahi whai take. Kei te whakarato ngā kaimahi i tētehi hōtaka ako kounga nei. He kipakipa, he ngahau hoki te taiao mā ngā tamariki. He pai ki ngā tamariki ngā kaupapa mahi o ia rā, ā, e whakaratohia ana ngā wheako ako. Kei te wātea ki ngā tamariki ngā rauemi huhua. He wāhi ngahau mā ngā tamariki ā-waho, ā, he tino pai hoki te āhua o te hoahoa. Kuhu ai ngā tamariki i roto i ngā akoranga.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kei te kaha ū te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te ako mō Ngāti Pikiao. Ahakoa kei te whakamahi rātou i te marau-ā-iwi a Ngāti Pikiao, kāore i waihangatia mai mā ngā tamariki nohinohi, kāore hoki i te hāngai ki Te Kōhanga reo o Te Hikuwai. E whakaae ana ngā kaimahi ko te mahi ka whai ake ko te tautohu i ngā tino akoranga mō Ngāti Pikiao mā ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He pai te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo e te whānau. Ka whakatau ngā mātua, ngā kaumātua me ngā kaimahi i te huarahi whakamua mō te kōhanga reo. Ka manaakitia, ka tautokona hoki te tū a ngā kaimahi mā roto mai i ngā aromhi o ia tau. Kei te tomo ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki roto i ngā mahi whakangungu hei whakamomona i tō rātou mōhio ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo me te whakawhānui i tō rātou mātau ki te reo Māori. Kei te tautoko ngā aromātai i ngā whakanikotanga o ngā āhuatanga maha o ngā nekenekehanga a te kōhanga reo. He rite tonu te pūrongotanga o ngā kaupapa a te kōhanga reo. He pai te manaakitanga o ngā tamariki, ā, e wheakotia ana te angitū hei ākonga.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He ngoikore ngā tikanga aromatawai. Ahakoa e mōhio pai ana ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki, kāore i te kitea i roto i ngā kōnae ako a ngā tamariki. E mārama ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakahirahiratanga o te whakaniko i te kounga o ngā tikanga aromatawai hei whakawhakawhānui ake i ngā tikanga whakaako, ako hoki.

E rehurehu ana te rautaki whakamua a te kōhanga reo. Ahakoa he whakakitenga ō te whānau me ngā kaimahi mō anamata kāore anō rātou kia whakawhanaketia hei rautaki-ā-tau, hei mahere-ā-tau e whakaāhua mai ana i ngā tino kaupapa o te wā.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hikuwai i Rotoiti. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ko wai rātou, ā, e tupu ana te mōhio ki ngā kōrero a Ngāti Pikiao. E whakaratohia ana ki ngā tamariki tētehi hōtaka ako kounga nei. Hei tino pai te whakahaeretia o te kōhanga reo e te whānau.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hikuwai i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hikuwai is in Rotoiti. Children and whānau all have whakapapa connections to Ngāti Pikiao and Te Arawa.

Parents enrol their children at this kōhanga reo so they learn about Ngāti Pikiao, their extended whānau, language, culture and identity. Koroua and kuia attend the kōhanga reo daily and model te reo and tikanga Māori. They promote Ngāti Pikiaotanga for children.

Many whānau and staff have been part of the kōhanga reo for a long time. They are committed to providing the best education they can for their children and mokopuna.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children demonstrate knowledge of Ngāti Pikiao?

Children know who they are and are developing knowledge of Ngāti Pikiao.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children know about Ngāti Pikiao places, people, language and tikanga. Children learn in an environment where whanaungatanga is central. Local kuia and koroua are at kōhanga reo each day to provide support in te reo and tikanga Māori. Children know local marae and significant features including the moana and maunga. They participate in pōwhiri, recite karakia and care for visitors. Children learn Ngāti Pikiao pepeha. Children know who they are and where they come from.

Children are confident and comfortable at kōhanga reo. Kaimahi and whānau genuinely love and care for children. Children confidently take risks in their learning with adults that they trust. Children are calm, settled and happy.

Children follow their interests and engage in meaningful learning activities. Kaimahi provide a high quality learning programme. The environment is stimulating and exciting for children. Children enjoy the daily activities and learning experiences are provided. Children have access to a range of resources. The well-designed outdoor area is an exciting place for children. Children are engaged in learning.

Key Next Steps

The whānau and kaimahi are determined to support children to learn about Ngāti Pikiao. While they currently use the Ngāti Pikiao iwi curriculum document this is not designed for young children or tailored to Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hikuwai. Kaimahi agree that the next step is to identify the key aspects of Ngāti Pikiao learning for kōhanga reo children.

Planning and Evaluation

The whānau manage their kōhanga reo very well. Parents, kaumātua and kaimahi make decisions about the future direction of the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi performance is managed and supported through annual appraisals. Kaimahi and whānau are enrolled in training to enhance their knowledge of kōhanga reo and to extend their capability in te reo Māori. Self-review supports improvement in many aspects of kōhanga reo operations. There is regular reporting about kōhanga reo matters. Children are well cared for and experience success as learners.

Key Next Steps

Assessment practices are inadequate. While kaimahi know their children well this is not reflected in children’s profiles. Kaimahi understand the importance of improving the quality of assessments to inform teaching and learning practices.

The strategic direction of the kōhanga reo is unclear. Although the whānau and kaimahi have a vision for their future they have not developed this into a strategic or annual plan that reflects their immediate priorities.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hikuwai is in Rotoiti. Children know who they are and have a growing knowledge of Ngāti Pikiao. Children are provided with a high quality learning programme. The whānau manages their kōhanga reo very well.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hikuwai will be in three years.
