1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hikuwai ki Rotoiti. E tino pā tata atu ana ki Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Rotoiti me te marae o Tapuaeharuru. He hononga ā-whakapapa tō ngā tamariki me te whānau ki a Ngāti Pikiao rāua ko Te Arawa. E ākina ana te whai wāhi mātātoa mai a te whānau ki te whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga i te kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa te mātātoa o ngā tamariki hei ākonga?

Ka whai wāhi mātātoa ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga, puta noa i ngā wāhanga katoa o te hōtaka akoranga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira  

E hāpaitia ana te ako a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaiāwhina i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki hei whakatōpū, hei whakawhānui hoki i ngā mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki, ā, i tā rātou whakamahinga anō hoki o te reo Māori. Ko ētahi o aua huarahi ako, ko ngā waiata, ngā mōteatea, ngā rotarota, te pānui pukapuka, te patapatai, te whāngai kupu, me te tohutohu. Ka taea e ngā tamariki tuākana ngā rerenga kōrero poto me ngā whakautu kōrero te whakaputa. Ka taea e ngā tamariki te taki ngā karakia, me te kore whai ārahitanga. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou pepeha. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā tauira reo e hāngai ana ki ngā horopaki ōkawa. E whanake ana ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

E ako ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana i ō rātou tuakiri. Ka whakatinana ngā kaimahi i tētahi hōtaka e whakanui ana i ngā mātauranga o te iwi hei aronga matua. Ka hāpai ngā kaiāwhina i ngā tamariki ki te ako i ngā whakapapa o ō rātou tūpuna, ā, i ō rātou hononga anō hoki ki ētahi atu i te kōhanga reo. Ka haereere rātou ki ngā marae e whai hononga ana ki a Ngāti Pikiao, ā, e tū ana ki ngā tahataha tonu o ngā roto. Ka āta whakarite ngā kaimahi i te taetae atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmomo kaupapa i ngā marae. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā pōhiri, kia ako ai rātou i ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi anō hoki hei tangata whenua. E ako ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou hononga ki te iwi, ngā hapū, me ngā marae hoki o Ngāti Pikiao.

He tino hononga ō ngā tamariki e ahu mai ai i te whanaungatanga. Ka whai whakaaro nui ngā kaimahi i a rātou e mahi ana ki te taha o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaiāwhina me te whānau i ngā rautaki papai ki te whakatairanga ake i te ngākau titikaha me te whakawhirinakitanga o ngā tamariki. Whakawhanaunga atu ai ngā tamariki ki ngā kaumātua i ia te wā. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki tuākana ki te tū mātātoa hei tuakana mō ngā tamariki tēina. Nā tēnei tūāhua ka pupū ake ngā whai wāhitanga mō rātou ki te tū hei kaiārahi. Ka aro nui ngā pēpi i te wā e noho pā tata mai ana ngā tamariki tuākana. He aroha, he mārie hoki te āhua o ngā taunekeneke a ngā kaimahi ki ngā pēpi. He mauritau te āhua o ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou taunekeneke ki ētahi atu.

Kei te tākare ngā tamariki ki te ako. Ka āta whakarite ngā kaimahi kia wātea ai ki ngā tamariki, ko te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako ako. Ka tūhura haere ngā tamariki i ngā ariā me ngā mātauranga e whai pānga ana ki te ao o ō rātou tūpuna. Ka whakahono ngā kaimahi i ngā mahi a ngā tamariki ki ngā atua Māori. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te rongo i tō rātou whai pānga ki te taiao māoriori. E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rawa o te taiao, taonga tūturu hoki. Ko te āhua o te whakatakotoranga o te taiao me ngā rauemi e hāpai ana i ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga, me te toro atu ki ngā taumahi e whakatairanga ake ana i tā rātou ako me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga ā-tinana. He pai te whai wāhi mai o ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi ki te hōtaka akoranga. He ngākau titikaha ō ngā tamariki hei ākonga.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

He tukanga ā ngā kaimahi hei whakamahere, hei aromātai hoki i te hōtaka akoranga. Kua whakaritea anō hoki ngā mahi e aromatawai ana i ngā tamariki. Ka āta tāutu ngā mahere i tēnā e ākona ana e ngā tamariki, me te āhua anō hoki o te whakatinanatanga o aua akoranga. He pai te whakahaeretanga me te whakaritenga o te ako a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i tētahi pūkete mā ia tamaiti, ā, koia nei te huarahi aromatawai matua. Kei roto i aua pūkete, ko ngā tauira mahi a ngā tamariki e whakaatu ana i tēnā e ākona ana e rātou. Ka āta whakamōhiotia te whānau ki tēnā e ākona ana e ngā tamariki. Kua whakaritea te aromātai hōtaka. Aromātai ai te kaiako me ngā kaimahi i te hōtaka akoranga i ia wiki. Ka arotahi ā rātou aromātai ki ngā whakaritenga, ki tēnā i whai hua ai me tēnā anō hoki kīhai i whai hua ai, ki ngā take i pērā ai, ā, ki ngā whakaritenga ka whai ake. Ka riro i ngā tamariki ngā painga i te wā e arotahi ana te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me ngā mahi aromātai ki ō rātou matea ako.

E whakahaere ana te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki. E tāutu ana te tūtohinga i te aronga rautaki a te kōhanga reo. Ka āta tāutu ngā whāinga i ngā kaupapa matua o te pae tawhiti mō te whanake tonutanga o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei. E ākina ana te whai wāhi mātātoa mai a te whānau ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka kitea ō rātou pūmanawa hei kaituku mahi e whai kawenga ana. Kua whakaritea ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi katoa, ā, e hāngai ana hoki ki tēnei wā. Kua whakatakotohia ngā tukanga arohaehae. E kawe tonuhia ana i ia te wā, ko te aronga ki te whakarato i te ako ngaio me te whakawhanaketanga ngaio.

E whai hua ana te arotake whaiaro. Kua whakaritea tētahi rārangi arotake e tāutu ana i ngā wāhanga hei arotake i ia marama, ā, i ia wāhanga hoki. Kua arotakengia ngā kaupapa here e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru, ā, kua whakatakotohia anō hoki ngā tukanga. Whiwhi ai te whānau i ngā pūrongo i ia te wā ki ā rātou hui. Ka riro i ngā tamariki ngā painga i te wā e whakaaro nuitia ana ō rātou matea mō meāke nei.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Me titiro ki ngā tukanga e hāpai ana i ngā tamariki ki te whakatau tautohetohe. He mea nui kia kaua ngā tamariki e noho whakaita ana mō ngā wā roa. Ehara te noho whakaita o ngā tamariki ki ngā tūru teitei i tētahi rautaki e tika ana ki te whakahaere i te whanonga o ngā tamariki. Kua tae ki te wā kia arotake te whānau i tā rātou kaupapa here me tā rātou tukanga e pā ana ki te whakatau tautohetohe, inarā, i te hāngaitanga o aua tūāhuatanga ki Te Ture Oranga Tamariki o te tau 2014. Tē taea te whakatūturu te noho haumaru ā-whatumanawa nei o ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga kāhore e whakatutuki ana i ngā herenga ā-ture. Kia tutuki i te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā herenga ā-ture, me tahuri rātou ki te: 

  • arotake me te whakahou ake i ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga hei āta whakarite i te whakatutukitanga me te whakapūmautanga o te ārai i te tamaiti me ngā rautaki ki te whakahaere i te whanonga, hei painga mō te waiora ā-whatumanawa, ā-tinana hoki o ngā tamariki katoa.

[R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS1-33 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]  

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • arotakengia e te whānau ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki ngā herenga ā-ture mō te noho whakaita, me te āta whakarite kia whāia aua tūāhuatanga i ngā wā katoa.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hikuwai i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hikuwai is in Rotoiti. It is in close proximity to Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Rotoiti and Tapuaeharuru Marae. Children and whānau have whakapapa connections to Ngāti Pikiao and Te Arawa. Whānau are encouraged to be active in the management and operations of the kōhanga reo.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How active are children as learners?

Children are actively involved in learning across all areas of the learning programme.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are supported to learn te reo Māori. Kaiāwhina use a range of strategies to consolidate and extend children’s knowledge and use of te reo Māori. These include waiata, mōteatea, rotarota reading stories, questions, prompts and instructions. Older children are able to use short sentences and responses. Children are able to recite karakia without guidance. Kaimahi support children to express their pepeha. Children learn about language patterns that are specific to formal occasions. Children are developing as speakers of te reo Māori.

Children learn in an environment where their identity is nurtured. Kaimahi implement a programme where local iwi knowledge is a priority. Kaiāwhina support children to learn the whakapapa of their tūpuna and their connections to others at the kōhanga reo. They visit the different marae connected to Ngāti Pikiao that are located around the lakes. Kaimahi ensure children attend occasions on the marae. Children are involved with pōhiri to learn about their roles as responsibilities as tangata whenua. Children are learning about how they belong to the iwi, hapū and marae within Ngāti Pikiao.

Children have established relationships based on whanaungatanga. Kaimahi are very thoughtful when working with children. Kaiāwhina and whānau use positive strategies to promote confidence and trust with the children. Children have regular interactions with kaumātua. Kaimahi encourage older children to be active as tuakana to the younger children. This gives them opportunities to take on leadership roles. The babies are very responsive when older children are near. Kaimahi interactions with babies are loving and calm. Children appear secure in their interactions with others.

Children are eager learners. Kaimahi ensure children have access to a range of learning experiences. Children explore concepts and knowledge related to the world of their ancestors. Kaimahi link children’s work to atua Maori. Children are able to experience the impact they have on the natural environment. Children have access to a range of natural and physical resources. The way in which the environment and resources are organised allows children to follow their interests and find activities that enhance their learning and physical development. There is positive involvement by both children and kaimahi in the learning programme. Children are confident learners.

Planning and Evaluation

Kaimahi have processes in place to plan and evaluate the learning programme. Children’s assessments are also in place. Planning clearly identifies what children will be learning and how this knowledge will be implemented. Children’s learning is well managed and organised. Kaimahi use individual profiles as the main method of assessment. These include samples of children’s work to reflect what they are learning. The whānau is well informed of what children are learning. Programme evaluation is in place. The kaiako and kaimahi evaluate the learning programme each week. Their evaluations focus on preparation, what went well, what did not go well and why, and where to next. Children benefit when planning, assessment and evaluation practices focus on their learning needs.

The whānau manage their kōhanga reo in the best interests of their children. The charter identifies the strategic direction for the kōhanga reo. The goals clearly identify long term priorities for the future development of the kōhanga reo. Whānau are encouraged to take an active role in the management of the kōhanga reo. They reflect the characteristics of responsible employers. All employee documentation is current and in place. Appraisal processes are in place. There is an ongoing focus of providing professional learning and development.

Self review is effective. There is a review schedule in place which identifies the areas for review each month and term. Health and safety policies have been reviewed and procedures are in place. The whānau receive regular reporting at their hui. Children benefit when their future needs are considered.

Key Next Steps

Processes of conflict resolution for children requires attention. It is important that children are not restrained for long periods of time. Harnessing children in high chairs is not an appropriate strategy for managing children’s behaviour. It is time for whānau to review their conflict resolution policy and procedure and in particular how this aligns to the Vulnerable Children’s Act 2014. Children’s emotional safety cannot be guaranteed.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements. 

ERO identified areas of non-compliance. In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements they need to:

  • review and update policies and procedures to ensure that appropriate child protection and behaviour management strategies are met and maintained for the emotional and physical wellbeing of all children.
    [Regulation 46 (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS1-33 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • The whānau review the policies and procedures regarding restraint regulations and ensure these are followed consistently.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hikuwai will be in three years.
