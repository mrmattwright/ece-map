1 Evaluation of the Service

How well placed is the service to promote positive outcomes for children?

Rainbow Mangorei Road is well placed to promote positive outcomes for children.

Context

Rainbow Mangorei Rd provides full day education and care for infants, toddlers and preschool children in three specific rooms. The majority of teachers are qualified with the remainder in training. This is the centre’s first ERO report.

Kidicorp owns and manages the centre. Their Taranaki-based professional services and business managers provide advice, guidance and support to the centre manager and staff. Kidicorp’s policies, procedures and review systems provide clear expectations and direction for daily organisation.

Management and staff reviewed the centre philosophy during 2012. This comprehensive process, involving consultation, decision-making and bicultural partnership, has led to the development of their new philosophy ‘With unity and respect, together we learn and grow’.

Areas of strength

The centre’s philosophy is highly evident in teacher practice. Children are supported to lead and direct their own play. Teachers are responsive and respectful and involve children in decisions that affect them. A calm, positive learning environment prevails.

Children’s independence is well supported. Routines are established and flexibly implemented. Resources are appropriate to age and developmental stages. Teachers working with infants and toddlers up to 2 years of age, respond to their needs and rhythms and undertake care procedures with respect. Their environment includes safe spaces for exploration.

Programme planning is appropriately based on evaluation of observations of children and aims to support and extend their interests and learning. Ways of including parents in planning and assessment have been developed.

A well-planned, strategic approach guides centre development. Professional development for staff is planned to meet areas identified through teacher reflection, appraisal and self review. Self review is improvement focused. Management and teachers have a commitment to achieving desirable outcomes for children and their families through examining their own teaching practice.

Parents have regular opportunities to share information about their children and their aspirations. Communication occurs in ways that suit parents’ preferences and family situations. Key teacher relationships, where each infant and toddler has a primary caregiver, contribute to confident partnerships with children and their families.

Areas for development and review

ERO and centre leaders agree that next steps include:

  • continuing to embed understanding and use of evidence-based self review
  • strengthening and fully implementing the appraisal process to enhance teaching capability and team leaders’ professional growth
  • developing a shared understanding of effective assessment practice
  • increasing the use of deliberate teaching strategies to enhance literacy and numeracy learning
  • further developing teachers’ use of te reo Māori to strengthen bicultural practice.

2 Legal Requirements

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Rainbow Mangorei Rd completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service’s systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

3 Next Review

When is ERO likely to review the early childhood service again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.

General Information about Early Childhood Reviews

About ERO Reviews

The Education Review Office (ERO) is the New Zealand government department that reviews schools and early childhood services throughout New Zealand.

Review focus

ERO's education reviews in early childhood services focus on the factors that contribute to positive learning outcomes for children. ERO evaluates how well placed the service is to make and sustain improvements for the benefit of all children at the service. To reach these findings ERO considers:

  • Pou Whakahaere – how the service determines its vision, philosophy and direction to ensure positive outcomes for children
  • Pou Ārahi – how leadership is enacted to enhance positive outcomes for children
  • Mātauranga – whose knowledge is valued and how the curriculum is designed to achieve positive outcomes for children
  • Tikanga whakaako – how approaches to teaching and learning respond to diversity and support positive outcomes for children.

Within these areas ERO considers the effectiveness of self review and partnerships with parents and whānau.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of service performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for children and useful to the service.
