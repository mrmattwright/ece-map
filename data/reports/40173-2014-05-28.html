1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Opopoti ki ngā papa o te marae o Opopoti, ki Maungatapu i Tauranga. E tū ana te marae me te kōhanga reo ki te wāhi ātaahua o te whanga o Opopoti. He āhuatanga nui, he āhuatanga motuhake hoki tēnei ki te whānau o tēnei wāhi, me te hapū, te iwi hoki. Ka whakarauika mai te tokomaha i ngā huihuinga o te whānau me te hapori ka tū ki te marae. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te kaha hihiri o te marae.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori, ki tētahi taiao e hāpai ana i a rātou. He pakari te kaiako ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, ā, ka whai hua ia ki te whakatauira i te reo Māori mō ngā tamariki me te whānau. Ka whai pūkenga tana whakapuaki, tana whakatauira anō hoki i ngā tino kīanga, tohutohu, whakawhitinga kōrero anō hoki o te reo Māori, i a ia e whakawhiti kōrero ana ki ngā tamariki.

He kaha te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. He pakari, he nahanaha hoki ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga. He mārama ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakamahere rautaki, te whakahaeretanga a ngā kaimahi, ngā take pūtea, te hauora me te haumaru, te whakawhanaketanga o ngā rawa, me te whakahaere i te tari. He auau te arotake whaiaro, ā, ka whai pūtake hoki. He maha ngā tūmomo huarahi e whakamahia ana hei aroturuki, hei pūrongo hoki i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

Ka tino tautoko te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo, i ngā kaimahi, i ngā tamariki hoki. He pai tā rātou mahi ngātahi, ā, ka āwhina i te kōhanga reo i ia te wā. He tokomaha o te whānau e whai wāhi ana ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga, tae atu ki te whakamahere hōtaka me te aromātai. E tupu haere ana te ngākau nui o te whānau ki te whakapai ake i ō rātou pūkenga reo Māori. He tokomaha o te whānau kua uru atu ki ngā akoranga reo Māori. Me whakapai ake te aromātai i te hōtaka.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga reo (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • te arotake whaiaro a te whānau, me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

He pēhea te pai o te tautoki i ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo Māori?

He pai te tautokohia o ngā tamariki e te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te ako i te reo Māori.

Te wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He rawe te titiro atu ki ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i te atawhai, te manawanui, te whakaute, me te ngākau nui, i a rātou e taunekeneke ana ki ngā tamariki, ki waenga hoki i a rātou anō. He pērā anō hoki te āhua o te whanonga o ngā tamariki tuākana i a rātou e taunekeneke ana. Mā ngā huarahi whakamanawa ka taea ai te whakatau, te atawhai hoki i ngā tamariki e rangirua ana. Ka whakamahia hoki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki papai hei ārahi, hei whakahaere hoki i ngā tamariki. He tau ngā tamariki, ā, he ākonga e māia ana ki te kōrerorero i te reo Māori.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao o te reo Māori e tautoko ana, e whai hua ana hoki. He pakari te kaiako ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whai hua ia ki te whakatauira i te reo Māori ki ngā tamariki, ngā kaimahi, me te whānau. He pakari ngā tamariki tuākana ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori, ā, ka whakatauira rātou i te reo tika ki ō rātou tēina. He pākiki ngā tamariki, ā, he pārekareka ki a rātou te patapatai me te whakapuaki whakaaro. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki tēina te whakahuahua i ngā kaimahi me ō rātou hoa. Ka tino ngākau nui rātou ki ngā taumahi me ngā kēmu e mau nei i te manawataki, te tāruarua, me ngā mahi-ā-ringa. Ka whakamahi te kaiako i ngā nekehanga, te whakamahi, me te patapatai i a ia e taunekeneke ana me ngā tamariki. He pai te whakawhanake ake a ngā tamariki ki te tino kōrero mārika i te reo Māori.

He pai te whakariterite o ngā kaimahi hei āta whakatau anō hoki i te whakaratonga o te hōtaka i ngā wero me ngā whiringa e tika ana ki ngā tamariki. Kua whakaritea ngā pokapū taumahi mō ngā tamariki, i a rātou e tatari ana kia tīmata ai te hōtaka ōkawa. Ka āta whakaarohia ngā wā ako kia aro pai ai ngā tamariki. Ka kawe ngā tamariki i ā rātou akoranga i ngā wā e tākaro takitahi ana, ā, ka āta tautokona e ngā kaimahi. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi i āta whakaritea ai kia kōkirihia e rātou, ā, ka ngākau nui rātou ki ā rātou akoranga.

Kua waia ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao, ā, he pai te manaaki i a rātou. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi me te whānau i tētahi taiao akoranga mā, ā, ka āta mātakina ngā tamariki. He tere rātou ki te huri ki penapena ki ngā matea ā-tinana, ā-whatumanawa anō hoki o ngā tamariki. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ngā hononga ā-whakapapa ki ō rātou marae, ō rātou hapū me ō rātou iwi hoki. Ka harikoa ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki ki te tākaro tahi, te ngahau tahi. Ka whakanuia, ka tohu ā-tinana hoki, hei whakamihi i te whai wāhi mai o ngā tamariki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whai whakaaro o ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki a rātou.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea ngā mahi a te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki?

He tino pai ngā mahi a te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Te whai wāhi mai, te ngākau nuitanga hoki o te whānau. He pai te mahi ngātahi a te whānau, ā, e noho pūmau ana ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. Ka tino tautoko te whānau i ā rātou tamariki, ngā kaimahi, me te kōhanga reo. He tokomaha ngā mema o te whānau ka whai wāhi atu ki te whakamahere i te hōtaka me te aromātai. Whai wāhi ai te whānau ki te āwhina i te kōhanga reo i ia te wā. Ka haere rātou ki ngā wānanga, ā, ka whai wāhi ki ngā tūmomo huihuinga o te hapori. E tupu haere ana te whakapūmautanga o ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakapai ake i ō rātou pūkenga reo Māori. He tokomaha o te whānau i whakauru atu ki ngā akoranga o te reo Māori. E ai ki te whānau, e āhei ana rātou i nāianei ki te hāpai ake i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ā rātou tamariki.

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau. He pai te whakahaeretanga a ngā mema o te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Hui ai ngā mema o te whānau i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti kōrero mō ngā take e pā ana ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakatau ngātahi ai rātou i ngā take. He pai te tae ā-tinana atu ki ngā hui. Ka tautoko te whānau i a rātou anō, i ngā kaimahi hoki, ki te taetae atu me te whai wāhi ki ngā tūmomo whakangungu.

Te whakamahere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro. Kua āta whakaritea ngā mahi arotake whaiaro. He mārama ki te whānau ngā āhuatanga o te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo e whai hua ana, me ngā wāhanga hoki hei whakapai ake. He aronga tō te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki ngā mahi whakapai haere. I ia te wā, ko ngā pūrongo a te kaiako, ngā kaimahi, te kaiwhakahaere, me ā rātou komiti, ka whakatakotohia ki te whānau i ngā hui o ia marama. He pai te noho mōhio o te whānau ki te kounga me te āhua o ngā mahi i ngā wāhanga matua o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

Ka ārahi te anga mahi o Te Ara Tūāpae i te whānau, mā te whakarato i tētahi aronga nahanaha e ahu mai ana i te whakarauora o te reo Māori. Ka aroturuki, ka pūrongo hoki i ia te wā, i te ahunga whakamua ki te whakatutuki i ngā whāinga wā roa i tāutuhia ai. Ko ētahi o ngā whāinga wā roa, ko te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori mō ngā mema o te whānau, ko te tautoko tonu i ngā kaimahi mā te whakawhanaketanga ngaio, me te whakapūmau hoki i te ātaahua, te mā hoki o te taiao e wātea ana ki ngā tamariki. Ka whakatau whakaritenga te whānau i runga i tō rātou tino mōhio ki ngā whakawhanaketanga o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei.

Te whakamahere kaupapa. He mātau ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere i ngā kaupapa ako mā ngā tamariki. Ka akiaki rātou i ngā mema o te whānau ki te whiriwhiri i ngā kaupapa ka ngākau nui ki ā rātou tamariki, ka hāngai rātou ki ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, ō rātou matea. Ko ētahi o ngā mahere, ko ngā whāinga, ngā taumahi, ngā haerenga, me ngā rārangi kupu. Ko te arotahi o ngā kaupapa ako e hāpai ana i ngā tamariki ki te ako i te ao Māori me te ao whānui.

Te aromātai hōtaka. He tika te whakamahinga o te aromātai hōtaka hei whakapai i ngā mahinga me ngā āhuatanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti kōrero, te whakaaro huritao hoki mō ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o te hōtaka a ngā tamariki i ia rā. Nā tēnei, kua pai ake ngā mahinga a ngā tamariki, ngā wā kai, te taiao, te whakamahi i te reo Māori, me ngā tukanga hoki e pā ana ki te whakamahere.

Te aromatawai. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi ki ngā whakaritenga papai o te aromatawai. Ka whakamahi rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā whakaritenga hei aroturuki, hei aromatawai hoki i te ahunga whakamua o ia tamaiti. Ko ētahi o aua whakaritenga, ko ngā kōrero urupare a te whānau, ko te mātakitaki i ia rā, me ngā aromatawai o ia wiki. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi ki ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā pūkenga o ia tamaiti, me ngā taumata ako ka whāia tonuhia. Ka whai hua ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te kapo atu i ngā āhuatanga angitu, i ngā wheako hoki a ngā tamariki, ki ā rātou pūkete, ā rātou puka hoki.

Te wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Ahakoa ka whai hua te aromātai hōtaka, he āhuatanga anō hoki hei whakapai ake. Kua tae ki te wā kia whai whakaaro ngā kaimahi ki ngā huarahi e tika ana mō te aroturuki i te hōtaka akoranga, hei āta whakarite i te whakanuitanga o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. I tēnei wā, ka hāngai ngā tuhinga katoa ki ngā mahere. He tino māramatanga tō te kaiako i nāianei mō te pūtake o te aromātai hōtaka.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua..

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia:

  • arotake te whānau i te kounga me te pūtake o te aromātai hōtaka.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu mō ngā Ratonga Arotake Māori (Te Uepū ā-Motu) Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

28 Haratua 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Opopoti is located on the grounds of Opopoti Marae, Maungatapu in Tauranga. The marae and kōhanga reo are situated in a picturesque setting in Opopoti Bay. This is an important and special feature to the whānau of this area, hapū and iwi. This marae is alive with many local whānau and community events. Children benefit from a vibrant and thriving marae environment.

Children are immersed in a supportive te reo Māori environment. The kaiako is a confident speaker of te reo Māori and effectively models te reo Māori for children and whānau. when she communicates with children.She skilfully delivers and models meaningful te reo Māori; instructions and conversations

Whānau confidently manage the kōhanga reo operations. Management practices are robust and systematic. There are clear processes for strategic planning, personnel management, financial matters, health and safety, property development and office administration. Self review is regular and purposeful. Various strategies are in use to monitor and report on the kōhanga reo operations.

Whānau are highly supportive of their kōhanga reo, kaimahi and children. They work well together and regularly help out in the kōhanga reo. Several whānau members have input into decision making including programme planning and evaluation. There is a growing commitment among whānau to improve their te reo Māori skills. Several whānau are enrolled in te reo Māori courses and classes. Programme evaluation requires improvement.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

How well are children supported to learn te reo Māori?

Children are well supported by whānau and kaimahi to learn te reo Māori.

Interactions and relationships between kaimahi and children are positive. Kaimahi model patience, respect and kindness when they interact with children and each other. Tuakana display similar behaviours in their interactions. Children are settled and comforted in reassuring ways. A range of positive strategies are used to guide and manage children. Children are secure, highly communicative learners.

Children are immersed in a supportive, successful te reo Maori environment. The kaiako is a confident speaker of te reo Māori. She effectively models te reo Māori for children, kaimahi and whānau. The older children confidently use te reo Māori and they model appropriate language for their younger peers. Children are inquisitive and enjoy asking questions and sharing ideas. The younger children enjoy mimicking kaimahi and their peers. They have a particular interest in activities and games that include rhythm, repetition and action. The kaiako uses gestures, praise and questions when she interacts with children. Children are developing well as emergent and confident speakers of te reo Māori.

Kaimahi are well organised and prepared to ensure the programme provides children with appropriate challenge and choices. Activity stations are set up for children as they wait for the formal programme to begin. Learning times are carefully considered to keep children interested. Children take responsibility for their learning during independent play and are well supported by kaimahi. Children engage in organised and self directed activities and enjoy their learning.

Children are familiar with their environment and are well cared for. Kaimahi and whānau provide a clean, well supervised, learning environment. They promptly attend to children’s physical and emotional needs. Children are aware of their whakapapa connections to their marae, hapū and iwi. Kaimahi and children play and have fun together. Praise and nonverbal gestures are used to acknowledge children’s contributions. Children enjoy the attention they receive from kaimahi and whānau.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How well does the whānau manage the kōhanga reo in the best interest of the children?

The whānau manages the kōhanga reo very well in the best interest of the children.

Areas of good performance

Whānau participation and commitment. Whānau work well together and are committed to the kaupapa of kōhanga reo. Whānau are highly supportive of their children, kaimahi and the kōhanga reo. Several whānau members have input into programme planning and evaluation. Whānau regularly help out at the kōhanga reo. They attend wānanga and participate in various community events. There is a growing commitment among whānau members to improve their te reo Māori skills. Several whānau members are enrolled in te reo Māori courses. Whānau report they are now able to support their children’s language development.

Whānau management.The whānau manage the kōhanga reo well in the interest of children. Whānau meet regularly to discuss kōhanga reo matters. They make decisions collaboratively and meetings are well attended. The whānau supports each other and the staff to attend and participate in training.

Strategic planning and self review. Self review practice is well established. The whānau is clear about the aspects of kōhanga reo operations that are working well and the areas where improvements can be made. The staff and whānau are on a journey of continual improvement. Regular reports from the kaiako, staff, the kaiwhakahaere and internal committees are presented to whānau at monthly meetings. The whānau is well informed about the quality and performance of key aspects of the kōhanga reo operations.

Te Ara Tūāpae framework guides the whānau by providing a strategic direction based on the revitalisation of te reo Māori. There is regular monitoring and reporting about progress made toward achieving the defined long term goals. Long term goals include te reo Māori development for whānau members, ongoing support for staff through professional development and maintaining the attractive and safe environment available to children. Whānau make well informed decisions about future kōhanga reo developments.

Kaupapa planning. Kaimahi skilfully plan learning kaupapa for children. They encourage whānau members to select kaupapa that appeal to their children or match their interests and needs. Plans include objectives, activities, outings and word lists. Focussed learning kaupapa support children to learn about te ao Māori and the wider world.

Programme evaluation. Programme evaluation is used appropriately to improve routinesandaspects of the learning programme. Kaimahi meet frequently to discuss and reflect on various aspects of the children’s daily programme.This has seen improvements to children’s routines, meal times, the physical environment, te reo Māori use and planning procedures.

Assessment. Kaimahi know about good assessment practice. They use a range of practices to monitor and assess each child’s progress. Some of these include whānau feedback, daily observations and weekly assessments. Kaimahi are familiar with each child’s interests, strengths and next learning steps. Kaimahi successfully capture children’s successes and experiences in their portfolio folders and notebooks.

Area for development

While programme evaluation is useful there are some aspects that could be improved. It is time for kaimahi to consider ways to monitor the learning programme to ensure children’s learning is maximised. Currently all documentation focuses on planning. The kaiako now has a clear understanding about the purpose of programme evaluation.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends:

  • the whānau review the quality and purpose of programme evaluation.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?
