1 Te Aromātai i te Ratonga

He pēhea te tūnga o Ngā Pūkeikura o Maniapoto – Te Pukeiti Early Childhood Centre ki Te Kūiti, ki te whakatairanga i te pai o ngā putanga ako mō ngā tamariki?

Kāhore i te pai te tūnga

Me whakawhanake ake

He pai te tūnga

He tino pai te tūnga

Ko ngā kitenga a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga e hāpai ana i te whānuitanga o tēnei whakataunga, kua whakarāpopotongia ki raro iho nei.

Ko Ngā Pūkeikura o Maniapoto – Te Pukeiti Early Childhood Centre tētahi pokapū mātauranga kōhungahunga ka whakahaerehia e ngā kaiako mō te rā katoa, ā, koia nei tētahi o ngā tūmomo ratonga e toru i raro i te maru o Poari ā-Iwi o Maniapoto. Kei Te Kūiti te whare kōhungahunga nei, ā, e whakahaerehia ana e te aronga rautaki a te Poari ā-Iwi o Maniapoto.

Ko tā te whare kōhungahunga whāinga, ko te poipoi i ngā matea o te whānau me ngā tamariki ō Maniapoto. Ko te wawata nei, arā, kia noho ngākau nui te iwi hihiri o Maniapoto ki te waiora ā-ahurea, ā-tangata hoki, ki te rōnakitanga o te taiao me te tupu’.

Tokowhitu ngā kaimahi kua whai tohu mātauranga kōhungahunga ki te pokapū nei, tokowhā o rātou he kaiako kua rehitatia, ā, tokorua anō hoki ngā kaiako tiwhikete whaiaro. Koia nei te arotake tuatahi a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i tēnei ratonga.

Kua whakawhanakehia e ngā kaimahi te marautanga a te pokapū e kīia nei, ko ‘Ngā Pou o Rereahu’, ā, kua tuituia tēnei ki Te Whāriki. Kei roto i te marautanga ko ngā mātauranga Māori, me te hītori, ngā mōhiotanga, me te tuakiri o Maniapoto.

Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pai te aro atu a ngā tamariki ki ā wheako reo Māori. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whai me te āta mārama i ngā kīanga māmā me ngā tohutohu māmā. Ka ngākau nui rātou ki ngā karakia, ngā mihi, ngā waiata, me ngā waiata ā-ringa, ā, ka whai pānga nui aua āhuatanga ki te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka whai pūtake ngā taunekeneke me ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ako a ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. Ka whakatairangatia te ako reo a ngā tamariki nā aua whakawhitinga ōkawa, ōpaki anō hoki.

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e āta whakanui ana i ngā wawata o Maniapoto. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā hītori o Maniapoto mā ngā waiata, ngā kōrero, me ngā whakawhitinga whakaaro. Ka whakakoia aua taumahi i te tuakiri ahurea o ngā tamariki me ngā taonga tuku iho. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i ngā tino hononga ki a Maniapoto.

E puāwai ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao akoranga e akiaki ana i a rātou ki te tūhura, ā, e mau nei hoki i te maha o ngā rauemi. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te uru atu ki ngā tākaro whakaari, te whakamahi hoki i ngā īpapa, me te mahi i ngā taumahi auaha. He pai te rere o ngā mahinga me ngā whakawhitinga, ā, he tino wā ako ēnei mō ngā tamariki. He mātātoa te hāpai a ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki, me tā rātou whakawhānui anō hoki i ā rātou akoranga, me te whakaawe i a rātou ki te ako. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tākaro i roto i ngā rōpū nui, i ngā rōpū iti rānei, me te tākaro takitahi. Ka ngahau ngā tamariki i a rātou e ako ana ki ngā horopaki o te ao Māori.

He pai, he aronga nui hoki ki ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi, te whānau, ngā kaumātua, me ngā kuia. He whanaunga te nuinga o te whānau me ngā kaimahi, ā, ka whai pānga pai tēnei ki te waiora o ngā tamariki me tā rātou ako. Ka taunekeneke ngā tamariki ki te taha o ngā kuia me ngā kaumātua i ngā wā ōkawa, pērā i ngā pōwhiri me te tiaki i ngā manuhiri. Nā te whai pānga nui o te whanaungatanga ki tēnei pokapū, ka ngākau nuitia ai tēnei pokapū e ngā tamariki katoa me ō rātou whānau. Ko te kaha me te whai pūtaketanga o ngā hononga e toro atu ana anō hoki ki te hapori whānui. He kaha te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki, ā, he pārekareka ki a rātou ā rātou taunekeneke whakahoahoa ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ngā kaimahi hoki.

He whai wāhitanga mō ngā pēpi ki te tūhura haere i te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo. He ngāwari te hōtaka ka whakaratohia e ngā kaimahi, ā, ka hāngai nui tēnei ki ngā matea o ngā pēpi me ā rātou mahinga. Kei ngā pēpi ō rātou ake wāhi ako, ā, he muramura, he ātaahua hoki te āhua. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki te hōtaka akoranga, ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki. Ko te koa me te mauritau o te tamaiti tētahi āhuatanga nui ki te whānau.

Ka whakanui ngā kaiārahi i ngā putanga mātauranga papai mō ngā tamariki me te whānau. Ka tohaina te ārahitanga, ā, ka hāngai ki ngā pūkenga me ngā mōhiotanga o ngā kaimahi, te whānau, me ngā kaumātua. Ka whai wāhi aua rōpū ki te pai o ngā whakataunga me ngā mahere mā ngā tamariki. Ka whakamaheretia, ka pupū ake anō hoki te arotake whaiaro, ā, e hono ana tēnei ki te mahere rautaki, te tirohanga, te tauāki taketake, te ako a ngā tamariki, me ngā hanganga e pā ana ki ngā kawenga takohanga. Ko te whakawhanaketanga i te katoa o te tamaiti, kei te pūtake o ngā whakataunga me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakamahere.

Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā hōtaka akoranga e aro ana ki ō rātou matea, ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, me ō rātou āhua ake. He auau te aromātai hōtaka, ka whai wāhi hoki ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki tēnei mahi, ā, ka whai pānga anō hoki ki ngā whakapaitanga e whakatutukihia ana mā ngā tamariki. Ka whakatutuki ngā tamariki i ngā wheako akoranga i āta whakaaro nuitia ai, i āta whakamaheretia ai.

Ka whai wāhi nui te whānau ki te ako me te waiora o ā rātou tamariki. He whai wāhitanga mā te whānau ki te whakaputa i ō rātou whakaaro, ā rātou mahi hoki ki ngā āhuatanga o te whakahaeretanga o te whare kōhungahunga me te mātauranga o ā rātou tamariki. Ka noho whakaute, ka whakanui hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā tirohanga me ngā whakaaro o te whānau, a, he tino aronga mārama tō ēnā ki te ako o ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi me te whānau, kei te whanake tonu tā rātou marautanga e kīia nei, ko Ngā Pou o Rereahu. E ai ki a rātou, he wāhanga tonu o te marautanga hei whakapaitanga ake. He hiahia tō ngā kaimahi me te whānau kia nui ake te whai pānga mai o Maniapoto ki ā rātou mahi whakamahere, mahi aromatawai anō hoki.

Te Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia whakawhanake tonu, kia whakamahine tonu te whānau i te marautanga e kīia nei, ko Ngā Pou o Rereahu, kia hāngai ake ai tēnei ki ngā āhuatanga motuhake o Maniapoto.

Te Whakatau a te Whakahaeretanga ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te ratonga ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā ratonga mātauranga kōhungahunga katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

1 Evaluation of the Service

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Ngā Pūkeikura o Maniapoto – Te Pukeiti Early Childhood Education is an all day, teacher led facility and is one of three service types under the Maniapoto Māori Trust Board (MMTB). The centre is in Te Kuiti and operates under the strategic direction of the MMTB.

The centre aims to foster the needs of whānau and their children from Maniapoto. The aspiration of a ‘vibrant Maniapoto iwi enjoying cultural and social wellbeing, environmental sustainability and economic growth ’guides the MMTB’s approach.

The centre has seven qualified early childhood educators of which four are registered teachers and two provisionally registered. This is the first Education Review Office (ERO) review of this service.

The kaimahi have developed the centre’s curriculum “Ngā Pou o Rereahu” which is interwoven with Te Whāriki. The curriculum incorporates mātauranga Māori and the history, knowledge and identity of Maniapoto.

The Review Findings

Children respond positively to their te reo Māori experiences. Children are able to follow and understand basic phrases and instructions. They enjoy karakia, mihi, waiata and waiata-ā-ringa which are strong elements of the daily programme. Kaimahi have meaningful interactions and learning conversations with children. Children’s language learning is enhanced through these formal and informal exchanges.

Children learn in an environment that actively promotes the aspirations of Maniapoto. Children learn about the history of Maniapoto through waiata, stories and discussions. These activities affirm children’s cultural identity and ancestral heritage. Children are developing strong links to Maniapoto.

Children thrive in a learning environment that encourages exploration and is well resourced. They have opportunities to participate in dramatic play, use ipads and creative activities. Routines and transitions flow smoothly and are positive learning times for children. Kaimahi actively support and extend children in their learning and inspire them to learn. Children have opportunities to play in large or small groups or independently. Children have fun as they learn in te ao Māori contexts.

Children have positive and responsive interactions with kaimahi, whānau, kaumātua and kuia. Most whānau and kaimahi are related and this has a positive impact on children’s wellbeing and learning. Children interact with kuia and kaumātua during formal occasions including pōwhiri and the hosting of visitors. The importance of whanaungatanga makes this centre a preferred place for all children and their whānau. Strong and meaningful relationships have extended to include the local community. Children have a strong sense of belonging and enjoy their social interactions with each other and kaimahi.

Babies have opportunities to explore their kōhanga reo environment. Kaimahi provide an unhurried programme based on babies needs and their routines. Babies have their own learning spaces which are colourful and inviting. Whānau are actively involved in the learning programme alongside their children. The joy and contentment of the child is important for whānau.

Leaders promote positive education outcomes for children and whānau. Leadership is shared and includes the skills and knowledge of kaimahi, whānau and kaumātua. These groups contribute to sound decision making and planning for children. Self review that is planned and spontaneous occurs and is linked to the strategic plan, vision, mission statement, children’s learning and accountability structures. Children’s holistic development is central to decision making and planning processes.

Children participate in learning programmes that respond to their needs, interests and dispositions. Programme evaluation is regular, involves kaimahi and whānau and leads to improvement for children. Children experience thoughtfully planned learning experiences.

Whānau make valuable contributions to their children’s learning and wellbeing. Opportunities are available for whānau input into aspects of the centre operations and their children’s education. Kaimahi respect and value whānau views and opinions which have a clear focus on their children’s learning.

Key Next Steps

Kaimahi and whānau acknowledge their curriculum document, Ngā Pou o Rereahu is still evolving. They report there are some parts of the curriculum that need improvement. Kaimahi and whānau would like to include a stronger Maniapoto influence in their planning and assessment practices.

Recommendation

ERO recommends the whānau continue to develop and refine the curriculum document, Ngā Pou o Rereahu to reflect the unique features of Maniapoto.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

During the review, ERO looked at the service’s systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Next Review

When is ERO likely to review the service again?

The next ERO review of Ngā Pūkeikura o Maniapoto - Te Pukeiti Early Childhood Centre will be in three years.

3 General Information about Early Childhood Reviews

ERO’s Evaluation Framework

ERO’s overarching question for an early childhood education review is ‘How well placed is this service to promote positive learning outcomes for children?’ ERO focuses on the following factors as described in the bicultural framework Ngā Pou Here:

  • Pou Whakahaere – how the service determines its vision, philosophy and direction to ensure positive outcomes for children
  • Pou Ārahi – how leadership is enacted to enhance positive outcomes for children
  • Mātauranga – whose knowledge is valued and how the curriculum is designed to achieve positive outcomes for children
  • Tikanga whakaako – how approaches to teaching and learning respond to diversity and support positive outcomes for children.

Within these areas ERO considers the effectiveness of arotake – self review and of whanaungatanga – partnerships with parents and whānau.

ERO evaluates how well placed a service is to sustain good practice and make ongoing improvements for the benefit of all children at the service.

A focus for the government is that all children, especially priority learners, have an opportunity to benefit from quality early childhood education. ERO will report on how well each service promotes positive outcomes for all children, with a focus on children who are Māori, Pacific, have diverse needs, and are up to the age of two.

For more information about the framework and Ngā Pou Here refer to ERO’s Approach to Review in Early Childhood Services.

ERO’s Overall Judgement and Next Review

The overall judgement that ERO makes and the timing of the next review will depend on how well placed a service is to promote positive learning outcomes for children. The categories are:

  • Very well placed – The next ERO review in four years
  • Well placed – The next ERO review in three years
  • Requires further development – The next ERO review within two years
  • Not well placed - The next ERO review in consultation with the Ministry of Education

ERO has developed criteria for each category. These are available on ERO’s website.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews are tailored to each service’s context and performance, within the overarching review framework. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for children and useful to the service.
