1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Huinga o Te Ao ki te marae o Kutarere, ki te hapori iti o Kutarere. Nō Upokorehe te whānau me te nuinga o ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo. Ko te kaumātua o te marae te heamana hoki o te kōhanga reo. Ka whai pānga nui ia ki ngā whakaritenga me ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou māramatanga ki tō rātou tuakiri?

Ka kitea te māramatanga pai o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou tuakiri hei tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Huinga o Te Ao.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E whakatairangatia ana te tuakiri o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hononga ā-tāngata, ā-whenua anō hoki. Ka whai wāhi atu rātou ki ngā kaupapa katoa i te marae. Ka noho tahi ngā kaumātua me ngā tamariki i ia te wā. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te mātakitaki i ngā tikanga e pā ana ki te karanga, te whaikōrero, me ngā waiata hoki o Upokorehe. Ka kitea tō rātou tino māramatanga ki te whanonga e tika ana mō aua wā. Ko ngā pouwhenua o te hapori e tohu ana ki ngā tamariki i tēnā e whai pūtake matua ana ki te iwi, te hapū, me te whānau. Ka whai wāhi nui ngā karakia Ringatū ki tēnei takiwā. E tino hāpaitia ana e ngā kaimahi te taki a ngā tamariki tuākana i ngā karakia. E whakawhanake ana te tino tūnga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou hapori.

E ako ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi wāhi e whakamana ana i te reo Māori. Ka tohutohu, ka ārahi, ka hāpai anō hoki ngā kaimahi i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. Ka aro nui, ka aro tika hoki ngā tamariki i te wā e kōrero ana tētahi ki a rātou. Kei te mārama rātou i ngā tauira reo, i a rātou e taki ana i ngā karakia me ō rātou pepeha. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki kāhore e tūmāia ana. Kei ngā whakaaturanga i ngā pakitara ngā pikitia e whakatairanga ana i ngā kupu Māori, hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki i ā rātou kupu Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā tamariki i ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā mōteatea, me ngā waiata ki te whakapuaki haere i tō rātou māramatanga ki te reo Māori. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Kei te whakatau haere ngā tamariki i te ao e noho nei rātou mā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo whai wāhitanga ako e whakaratohia ana e ngā kaimahi. Ko te marae, te hapori, me te pā tata atu hoki ki te moana e āta whakatakoto ana i tētahi tino wāhi tākaro mō ngā tamariki. Ka tūhura ngā tamariki i te taiao māoriori mā te kohi kaimoana, te whakatō putiputi, huawhenua hoki, me te rongo i ngā kōrero e pā ana ki ngā atua Māori me ngā rākau rangatira o tēnei takiwā. Nā ēnei kōrero tuku iho ka ako ngā tamariki i tō rātou hononga ki te taiao. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i aua whakaaro mā te whakautu pātai, te waiata, ā, mā te tuitui anō hoki i aua āhuatanga ki ā rātou tākaro, i a rātou e whai ana i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga. He ākonga tūmāia, he ākonga mauritau anō hoki ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore i te tino pakari te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki ngā whakataunga. I tēnei wā, ko te heamana me ngā kaimahi te hunga e taetae atu ana ki ngā hui ā-whānau. He mea nui kia whakaritea ngā tukanga ka āta hāpai i te māramatanga o te whānau katoa ki ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga mahi i roto i te whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo.

Kua whakaritea e te whānau ngā tuhinga maha. Ko ētahi o aua tuhinga, ko te tūtohinga, ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga, ā, ko tētahi mahere rautaki hoki. Ahakoa tēnei, he maha tonu ngā wāhanga ka taea te whakatutuki ake, nā te iti noa o te whai wāhi mai a te whānau, me tā rātou whakatau whakaritenga. Nā tēnei, e tika ana kia āta tirohia hoki te aromātai o roto.

E whanake ana te arotake kaupapa here. I tēnei wā, kāhore te whānau me ngā kaimahi e tino noho mārama ana ki ngā huarahi ka whakatinana i ngā kaupapa here ki ngā mahi. Ko te whai wāhi mai o te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te arotake i ngā kaupapa here, ka āta whakarite i te noho mōhio o ngā pākeke, ā, ka whakapūmau anō hoki i te noho o ngā tamariki ki te pūtake o ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru.

Kua whakatakotohia ngā whāinga mō te whakawhanake tonutanga o te kōhanga reo. Kāhore he mahere ā-tau hei āta whakarite i te ahu whakamua o ngā whāinga rautaki me te whakatutukitanga o ngā wawata. Ko te pūrongo i te ahu whakamua i runga anō i te mahere ā-tau, ka hāpai i te whānau ki te aroturuki i te ahu whakamua o ngā whakawhanaketanga me ngā kaupapa matua ā meāke nei.

I tēnei wā e waitohungia ana ngā arohaehae e te heamana. Engari, kāhore tēnei e hāpai ana i ngā whakataunga mō tēnā ka āwhina i ngā kaimahi ki te whakatairanga ake i ā rātou mahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki. He mea nui, kia whakamahia ngā mōhiohio arohaehae hei whakatinana haere i te tautoko ngaio tonu o ngā kaimahi.

Me hāpai i te whakahaeretanga o te tari, hei āta whakarite i te whakatakotoranga me te whakapūmautanga o ngā pūnaha. I tēnei wā, e āwhina ana ngā kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe o te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i te whakahaeretanga o ngā pūnaha me ngā tuhinga i te tari. Ko te whakatakoto i ngā tukanga pakari, ka āta whakatau i te rōnakitanga o te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga ki te kōhanga reo.

Me whakapakari ake te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai. Me nui ake te kiko o ngā mahere, me ngā rautaki, ngā rauemi hoki e tika ana ki te whakatinana haere i ngā mahi. Kāhore e tuhia ana ngā whakawhitinga kōrero e pā ana ki te hōtaka akoranga. He uaua te whakatau i te huarahi e whāia ana hei aroturuki i te hōtaka. Me whakawhanake ake i te aromatawai i ngā tamariki. He uaua te aroturuki i te ako a ngā tamariki, me tā rātou ahu whakamua i roto i te roanga o te wā. 

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutukihia ana e ai ki te ture. Kia tutukihia e te whānau o te kōhanga reo āna herenga ā-ture, me tahuri rātou ki te:

  • whakapiki ake i te whai wāhi atu, te whakatau whakaritenga hoki a te whānau i roto i ngā take katoa i te kōhanga reo
    [R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA 1-4 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • āta whakarite i ngā whai wāhitanga mō te whānau ki te hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tuhinga whakahaere o te kōhanga reo
    [R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA 4 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • arotake i ngā whakahaeretanga katoa o te kōhanga reo
    [R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA 5-7 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • āta whakarite ki te whakarato i te tautoko ngaio, i ngā whai wāhitanga ki te whakawhanake ngaio, ā, i ngā rauemi ngaio hoki mā tētahi pūnaha arohaehae e tika ana
    [R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA 1-12 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakarite me te whakatinana i tētahi mahere ā-tau
    [R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA 8 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakarite i te whakatinanatanga o te marautanga mā ngā mahere, te aromātai, me te aromatawai.
    [R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; C1 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakatutukihia e te whānau ngā take katoa i whakaraupapahia ai ki tēnei pūrongo

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Huinga o Te Ao is located on the Marae o Kutarere in the small township of Kutarere. The whānau and most of the children of the kōhanga reo affiliate to the iwi of Upokorehe. The kaumātua of the marae is the chairperson of the kōhanga reo. He has a major influence on the operations and management of the kōhanga reo.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children demonstrate an understanding of their identity?

Children demonstrate a good understanding of their identity as tamariki of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Huinga o Te Ao.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children’s identity is enhanced by their connections with people and places. They are involved in all activities of the marae. The kaumātua have regular contact with the children. Children have opportunities to observe the rituals of karanga, whaikōrero and waiata specific to Upokorehe. They demonstrate a good understanding of appropriate behaviour during these times. The local landmarks provide children with visual reminders of what is fundamentally important to the iwi, hapū and whānau. Ringatū karakia is prominent in the area. The older children are well supported by kaimahi to lead karakia. Children are developing a strong sense of their place in the community.

Children learn in an environment where te reo Māori is a priority. Kaimahi provide instruction, guidance and support for children’s language development. Children pay attention and respond appropriately when being spoken to. They understand language patterns as they recite karakia and their pepeha. Kaimahi provide prompts for those children who are not as confident. Wall displays provide pictorial images supported by kupu Māori to help children develop their vocabulary of Māori words. Children use karakia, himene, mōteatea and waiata to communicate their understanding of te reo Māori. Children are developing as speakers of te reo Māori.

Children make sense of their world through a range of learning opportunities provided by kaimahi. The marae, the community and the close proximity to the sea creates a wonderful playground for the children. Children explore the natural environment through gathering seafood, planting flowers and vegetables and hearing stories about ngā atua Māori and rākau rangatira from the area. These stories enable children to learn the relationship between themselves and nature. Children are able to articulate these ideas by answering questions, through waiata and by integrating in to their play as they follow their interests. Children are confident, secure learners.

Planning and Evaluation

Key Next Steps

Whānau participation in decision making is not strong. Currently the chairperson and kaimahi attend whānau hui. It is important that processes are put in place to ensure that all whānau understand their roles and responsibilities in the management and the operations of the kōhanga reo.

The whānau have in place a number of documents. These include the charter, policies and procedures and a strategic plan. Despite this, there are a number of areas which cannot be addressed further because of very little whānau participation and decision making. As a result internal evaluation requires attention

Policy review is developing. At the moment whānau and kaimahi are not really clear about how policies translate in to practice. Whānau and kaimahi input into policy review will ensure that adults are informed and will keep children at the centre of healthy and safe practices.

Goals have been set for the future development of the kōhanga reo. There is no annual plan in place to ensure that the strategic goals are progressed and aspirations are achieved. Reporting on progress against an annual plan will enable whānau to monitor progress toward future development and priorities.

Currently appraisals are signed off by the chairperson. However this does not influence decisions about what kaimahi need to assist them to lift their practices with children. It is important that appraisal information be used to inform further professional support for staff.

Administration support is needed to ensure that systems are established and maintained. At the moment the District Te Kōhanga Reo Kaupapa Kaimahi are assisting with the management of the office systems and documentation. Having robust processes in place will help ensure that the management of the operations of the kōhanga reo is sustainable.

Planning, assessment and evaluation require strengthening. Planning requires more content detail, strategies for implementation and resource requirement. Discussions around the learning programme are not recorded. It is difficult to gauge how the programme is monitored. Children’s assessments require further development. It is difficult to monitor children’s learning and progress over time.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements. 

ERO identified areas of non-compliance. In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements they need to:

  • increase whānau participation and decision making in all matters of the kōhanga reo
    [R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008: GMA 1-4 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • provide whānau opportunities to contribute to the development of kōhanga reo operational documents
    [R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008: GMA 4 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • review all operations of the kōhanga reo
    [R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008: GMA 5-7 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • ensure all reasonable steps are taken to provide staff employed professional support, professional development opportunities and resources through an appropriate appraisal system
    [R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008: GMA 1-12 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • establish and implement an annual plan
    [R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008: GMA8 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • ensure that the curriculum is informed by planning, evaluation and assessment.
    [R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008: C1 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • That whānau address all areas of concern outlined in this report.

5 When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Huinga o Te Ao will be within two years.
