1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Purapura Hou ki Kawerau. He tātai whakapapa tō te nuinga o ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau ki a Tūwharetoa ki Kawerau. E āta toro atu ana te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te hōhonutanga o te mātauranga me te mātanga o tō rātou kuia e ārahi ana i a rātou. Whai muri i ngā take kaimahi nō nā tata tonu nei i pupū ake ai, e kawe ana ngā kaimahi i ētahi tūranga hou. E noho pūmau ana te whānau ki te whakapai ake i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo me ngā putanga mō ngā tamariki.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga?

Ka kitea te tino aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Mana Tangata

Ka rongo ngā tamariki i ngā hononga papai. He tau, he tūmāia hoki rātou i roto i ā rātou taunekeneke ki ngā pākeke me ō rātou hoa. Ko te whai wāhi mai o ngā pāpā me ngā karani pāpā/koroua tētahi āhuatanga motuhake o te noho mai o te whānau. Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te aroha, te manaaki, me te tiaki. Ka toro wawe atu ngā tamariki ki te tautāwhi a ngā pākeke. Ka ako rātou i ō rātou whakapapa hei whakamana i tō rātou tuakiri me tō rātou whai wāhi nui ki te whānau, te hapū, me te iwi. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā waiata e whakapakari ana i tō rātou māramatanga ki te pepeha o te hapori nei. Ka manaaki, ka whakaute ngā tamariki, tētahi ki tētahi.

Mana Reo

E tūhura ana ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki tētahi wāhi e āta tautoko ana i a rātou. He whānui ngā tūmomo taumata māramatanga. Ka kōrero ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori i a rātou e mahi ana ki te taha o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi ngahau, tae atu ki ngā kōrero akiaki, te patapatai, me te tāruarua hei hāpai i ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka aro atu ngā tamariki tuākana ki ngā tohutohu me ngā pātai whāiti mā te reo Māori. Ka whakawhiti kōrero ngā pēpi mā ngā whakapuakitanga ā-kanohi me te reo ā-tinana. Ka ako te whānau i te reo Māori ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki i ngā wā whakaako, ā, i ngā taumahi ako hoki. Ka whakatauira te kuia i te kounga kairangi o te reo Māori mā ngā kōrero, ngā karakia, me ngā waiata. E whakawhanake ana te puāwai mai o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka whai ake

Me whakapakari ake i te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai hōtaka. Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi ngā mahere mō te paetawhiti. Kāhore anō rātou kia whakawhanake i ngā mahere mō te pae tata. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, kāhore rātou e mārama ana i ngā āhuatanga katoa e pā ana ki te whakamahere. He pūkete mokopuna ā ngā kaimahi. Engari, kua tae ki te wā kia arotakengia e ngā kaimahi tēnei tukanga kia mārama ai ngā tino tūhonohonotanga ki te hōtaka akoranga me te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki. Me aro atu ki te aromātai hōtaka. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e hui ana i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti kōrero mō tēnā i whai hua ai, mō tēnā kīhai i whai hua ai, ā, mō ngā āhuatanga hoki o te hōtaka akoranga hei whakapai ake. Kua tae ki te wā kia toro atu te whānau ki te tautoko ngaio mō ngā kaimahi, kia āhei ai rātou ki te whakatairanga ake i ā rātou whakaritenga ki te taha o ngā tamariki. Ko te kounga ngoikore o te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me ngā mahi aromātai hōtaka e pā tōraro ana ki te ako a ngā tamariki.

Kāhore i te pai te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ahakoa he pai te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki ngā taumahi o ia rā, kāhore te whānau e whakatū ana i ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti kōrero me te aroturuki i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kei te tūtohinga te tino ārahitanga mō te whakahaeretanga, engari kei te whakatō tonuhia tēnei ki ngā whakahaeretanga o ia rā. Kāhore te whānau e whakatutuki ana i ā rātou kawenga whakahaere mō te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. Kāhore te whānau e aroturuki ana i ngā āhuatanga katoa o ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, tae atu ki te whakamahere rautaki, te whakahaere kaimahi, te arotake i ngā kaupapa here, ngā pūrongo a ngā kaimahi, me ngā take e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. Kāhore i te rawaka te arotake o roto. Kāhore ngā tamariki e whiwhi ana i ngā painga ka ahu mai i tētahi kōhanga reo e āta whakaritea ana, e āta whakahaerehia ana hoki.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakatūngia ngā wānanga ka hāpai i te māramatanga o te whānau ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo me ā rātou kawenga e whai pānga ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo
  • whakatūngia, kia tuhia hoki ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, ki te whakawhiti kōrero me te aroturuki i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo
  • toro atu ki te tautoko ngaio ka whakapakari i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai
  • whakapakarihia te arotake o roto.

5 Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Purapura Hou i roto ngā tau e rua.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Purapura Hou is in Kawerau. Most children and their whānau have whakapapa connections to Tūwharetoa ki Kawerau. The kōhanga reo whānau use the extensive knowledge and experience of their kuia for leadership and guidance. Following recent personnel issues kaimahi have taken on new roles. The whānau is committed to improving management of the kōhanga reo and outcomes for children.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children show a sense of belonging?

Children show a strong sense of belonging to the kōhanga reo.

Mana Tangata

Children experience positive relationships. They are comfortable and confident in their interactions with adults and their peers. Participation of fathers and grandfathers is a special feature of whānau involvement. Children are immersed in aroha, manaaki and tiaki. Children readily seek assurance from adults. They learn about their whakapapa to affirm who they are and their place within their whānau, hapū and iwi. Children learn waiata to strengthen their understanding of the local pepeha. Children treat each other with care and respect.

Mana Reo

Children explore te reo Māori in a supportive environment. They are at varying levels of understanding. Kaimahi speak te reo Māori when working with children. They use a range of fun activities including prompts, questions and repetition to encourage children to speak te reo Māori. Older children respond to commands and closed questions in te reo Māori. Babies communicate through facial expressions and body language. Whānau learn te reo Māori alongside their children during teaching and learning activities. The kuia models high quality te reo Māori through kōrero, karakia and waiata. Children are developing as emergent speakers of te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Key Next Steps

Planning, assessment and programme evaluation needs strengthening. Kaimahi have long term planning in place. They have yet to develop short term plans. Kaimahi acknowledge there are gaps in their understanding of planning. Kaimahi have in place mokopuna portfolios. However, it is time for kaimahi to review this process in order to make clear links between the learning programme and children’s progress. Programme evaluation requires some attention. Kaimahi do not meet regularly to discuss what worked, what didn’t work and any improvements to the learning programme. It is time for whānau to seek professional support for kaimahi in order for them to enhance their practices with children. Children’s learning is hindered by a lack of quality planning, assessment and programme evaluation practices.

The kōhanga reo is not well managed. While there is good whānau involvement in daily activities whānau do not hold regular whānau hui to discuss and monitor kōhanga reo operations. The charter provides clear guidance for management however this is still being embedded in day to day operations. Whānau do not fulfil their management responsibilities to the kōhanga reo kaupapa. Whānau do not monitor all areas of kōhanga reo operations including strategic planning, personnel management, policy review, kaimahi reporting and health and safety issues. Internal review is inadequate. Children do not benefit from a kōhanga reo that is well organised and managed.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendations

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • Provide wānanga to support whānau understanding of the kaupapa of te kōhanga reo and their obligations to the management of the kōhanga reo
  • Hold and record regular whānau hui to discuss and monitor kōhanga reo operations
  • Seek professional support to strengthen planning, assessment and evaluation practice
  • Strengthen internal review.

5 When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Purapura Hou will be within two years.
