1 Te Horopaki

Ko Rātana te marae

Ko Manuao te pou herenga waka

Ko Temepara te puna wai ora

Ko te Iwi Mōrehu te iwi

Ko te māramatanga te kaupapa.

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Wharemārama ki Rātana Pā. E tino tautokona ana te kōhanga reo nei e te hapori. E noho ana te nuinga o ngā mema o te whānau ki te takiwā o Rātana Pā. Kua hokihoki mai ētahi whānau ki te takiwā nei, nā runga i ō rātou hononga ki Rātana Pā, ā, e ako ana rātou i te māramatanga me te Mōrehutanga. Whai muri i te arotake o mua, kua whakatūngia tētahi heamana hou me te katoa o ngā kaimahi hou.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakamahi i te reo Mōrehu?

Ka kitea te tūmāia o ngā tamariki i a rātou e whakamahi ana i te reo Mōrehu ki ngā horopaki ōkawa.

Te Mana Reo me te Mana Atua

E ako ana ngā tamariki i te reo Mōrehu i a rātou e whai ana i te hāhi Rātana. Ka ako rātou ki te taki i ō rātou pepeha, ā, ka tau haere rātou ki ō rātou ake hononga ki ētahi atu mā te Mōrehutanga. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ngā kawa e ahu mai ana i te Mōrehutanga. Ka rongo rātou i te reo e whakamahia ana e ngā kaumātua, ngā kaimahi me ō rātou whānau i ngā wā ōkawa me ngā wā ōpaki. Nō tata tonu nei Te Whāriki i arotakengia ai kia whai wāhi mai ai te reo Māori me ngā tikanga o te Mōrehutanga me te hāhi Rātana. Ka whakapuakihia te ao me ngā whakapono o te Mōrehutanga ki ngā tamariki, mā tō rātou reo.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā hononga me tō rātou tūrangawaewae. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā horopaki e whai pānga ana ki te Mōrehutanga me te hāhi Rātana. He hiahia tō te whānau kia ako ā rātou tamariki i te reo me ngā tikanga Māori mā te Mōrehutanga, hei whakakoi i tō rātou tuakiri. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te haere ki ngā kaupapa o te hapori. E mōhio ana rātou ki ngā tikanga, ngā waiata, me ngā hīmene o Rātana. Ka whakanuia aua whakaritenga ki te kōhanga reo, puta noa i te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka ako ngā tamariki i tō rātou whai pānga nui ki te Mōrehutanga.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore ngā ngākau nuitanga, ngā matea me ngā hiahia ako hoki o ngā tamariki e tino kitea ana, e aro nuitia ana hoki. Ka āta whakatakotohia te hōtaka akoranga e ngā pākeke. He iti noa ngā taumahi me ngā rauemi e wātea ana ki ngā tamariki. Ki tē taea e rātou te uru atu ki ngā taumahi katoa, ā, ka tauwhāinga rātou mō te iti noa o ngā rauemi e noho wātea ana. He maha ngā wā ka kitea te pōkaikaha o ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i ētahi āhuatanga o tō rātou kōhanga reo. He mārama te tūtohinga, ā, ka tāutuhia ko te reo Māori te kaupapa matua i te kōhanga reo. Arotakengia ai ngā kaupapa here i ia te wā. Kua whakaritea ngā kaupapa here matua me ngā tukanga matua e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru, ā, e hāngai ana hoki ēnei ki te wā. He pai te whakatakoto haere i ngā rehita a te whānau mō te hauora, te haumaru, te waiora, me te atawhai. Ko ētahi o ngā kaupapa matua a te whānau, ko te whakawhanaketanga i ngā kaimahi, ko te reo Māori, me te māramatanga. Kua whakatutukihia ētahi o ngā whāinga o te mahere rautaki. Ka whakaaro huritao te whānau mō te nuinga o ā rātou whakahaeretanga o ia rā, hei tāutu i ngā wāhanga ki te whakapai ake.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore ngā kaimahi e āta whai whakaaro ana ki ngā matea ako me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki i a rātou e whakamahere ana. He whānui rawa te whai pānga o te nuinga o ngā taumahi, ki te katoa o ngā reanga me ngā āheinga o ngā tamariki. Kāhore he taumahi e hāngai pū ana ki ngā tamariki i raro i te rua o ngā tau, hei āta hāpai ake i tā rātou ako me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ko te tūmanako ki te kōhanga reo, kia hāngai ngā tamariki katoa ki te taumahi ā-rōpū kotahi, ki ngā taumahi ā-rōpū e rua rānei, mō te roanga o te wā e noho ana rātou ki te wāhi kotahi. Ka takeo haere ngā tamariki, ā, kāhore rātou e aro atu ana ki ētahi o ngā kaimahi.

Etahi wa, ka haumaru ngā tamariki inā kāhore i te pai o rātou whanonga. Ko te tikanga, me hoki te whānau ki ngā kaupapa here ki te kimi rautaki hai ārahi i ngā mahi whanonga o te tamaiiti ki runga i te tika.

Kāhore i te rawaka te aromatawai. Kāhore ngā pūmanawa ake, ngā hiahia ako, me ngā wheako reo Māori o ia tamaiti e āta kapohia ana, e āta tuhia ana rānei mā Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

He ngoikore te aromātai hōtaka. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e kōrero ana, e arotake ana hoki i ngā hōtaka, i ngā whakaritenga rānei hei tātari i tēnā i whai hua ai, me tēnā kāhore i whai hua ai ki ngā tamariki. Kāhore ngā hōtaka akoranga e aro atu ana ki te rerekē haere o ngā matea o ngā tamariki. 

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ētahi wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture. Kia whakatutukihia e te whānau āna herenga ā-ture, me tahuri rātou ki te:

  • whakarite i te whakaratonga o ngā pākeke i te mātauranga me te atawhai ka whakatairanga ake i te ako a ngā mokopuna mā ngā taunekeneke papai e whai pūtake ana
    [R41 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; C1-4 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • arotake i ia te wā i te hōtaka o ngā taumahi e whakatutukihia ana ki te kōhanga reo
    [R47 (1) (b) (i) (ii) Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008]
  • whakarite i te whakapuakitanga o te marautanga o te kōhanga reo mā te whakamahere, te whakatinana, me te aromātai e whakaatu ana i te māramatanga ki te ako a ngā mokopuna, ki ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, ā, ki te āhua anō hoki o ō rātou whānau me tō rātou ao.
    [Paearu C2 – Whakaritenga Ngaio, Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; R41 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; C1-4 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Te Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia whakarato te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i te tautoko rautaki me te whakangungu i whakahāngaitia ai ki te whānau, kia tutuki ai, kia ū ai hoki ngā whakapaitanga matua e tika ana mō ngā tamariki.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Wharemārama i roto ngā tau e rua.

1 Context

Ko Rātana te marae

Ko Manuao te pou herenga waka

Ko Temepara te puna wai ora

Ko te Iwi Mōrehu te iwi

Ko te māramatanga te kaupapa.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Wharemārama is in Rātana Pā. This kōhanga reo is well supported by the local community. Most whānau members live in the Rātana Pā area. Some whānau who have connections to Rātana have returned to live in the area and are learning about māramatanga and Mōrehutanga. Since the previous review a new chairperson and all kaimahi have been appointed.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children use te reo Morehu?

Children demonstrate confidence as they use te reo Mōrehu in formal contexts.

Mana Reo and Mana Atua

Children learn te reo Mōrehu as they practice te hāhi Rātana. They learn to recite their pepeha and become familiar with their personal connections to others through Mōrehutanga. Children are familiar with protocols based on Mōrehutanga. They hear language used in formal and informal situations by elders, kaimahi and their whānau. Te Whāriki has recently been revised to include te reo Māori me ngā tikanga o te Mōrehutanga, and te hāhi o Rātana. Children are introduced to the Mōrehu way of life and beliefs through their language.

Children learn about connections and their tūrangawaewae. Children actively participate in the wider Mōrehu and Rātana contexts. Whānau want their children to learn te reo me ngā tikanga Māori according to Mōrehutanga to affirm who they are. Kaimahi provide lots of opportunity for children to attend local events. They are familiar with tikanga, waiata and hīmene specific to Rātana. These practices are promoted at the kōhanga reo throughout the daily programme. Children learn that they are a special part of te Mōrehutanga.

Key Next Steps

Children’s interests, needs and their learning preferences are not recognised and responded to. The learning programme is determined by adults. There are limited activities and resources available to children and they compete for the few available resources. Children frequently appear frustrated.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau manage aspects of their kōhanga reo well. The charter is clear and identifies that te reo Māori is the kaupapa for the kōhanga reo. Policy review is ongoing. Core health and safety policies and procedures are current and in place. Whānau health and safety and wellbeing and care registers are recorded well. Whānau priorities include kaimahi development, te reo Māori and māramatanga. Some goals in the strategic plan have been achieved. Whānau reflect on most of their day to day operations to identify areas for improvement.

Key Next Steps

Kaimahi do not adequately consider children’s learning needs and interests when planning. Most activities are generic to all ages and abilities at one time. Children under two years of age have no specific activities to support their learning and development. All children are expected to conform to one or two whole group activities in one space for extended periods of time. Children lose interest and do not respond to some kaimahi.

At times, management of children's behaviour is inappropriate. Children are confined if they misbehave. It is important that the whānau review their behaviour management practices against their policy and procedures.

Assessment is inadequate. Children’s individual strengths, learning preferences and te reo Māori experiences are not captured and recorded against Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Programme evaluation is poor. Kaimahi do not adequately discuss and review programmes or practices to determine what went well and what didn’t go well for children. Learning programmes do not respond to the changing needs of children.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

ERO identified areas of non-compliance. In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements they need to:

  • ensure adults provide education and care to enhance mokopuna learning through meaningful and positive interactions
    [R41 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; C1-4 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]
  • periodically review the programme of activities undertaken at the kōhanga reo
    [R47 (1) (b) (i) (ii) Education (ECC) Regulations 2008]
  • whānau need to ensure that the curriculum of the kōhanga reo is informed by planning, implementation and evaluation that demonstrates an understanding of mokopuna learning, their interests, whānau and life contexts.
    [Criterion C2-Professional Practice, Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008] [R41 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; C1-4 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendation

ERO recommends that Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust provide strategic support and targeted training to whānau to achieve and sustain the necessary improvements needed for children.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Wharemārama will be within two years.
