Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Waihīrere ki te marae o Waihīrere, e pā tata atu ana ki Tūranga-nui-a-Kiwa. Kei te whanake haere te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki a Waihīrere, me tā rātou whakamahinga o te reo me ngā tikanga Māori ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo pūtake. Ko ētahi āhuatanga hei whakapai ake, ko te hauora me te haumaru, te whakamahere hōtaka, me ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aromātai me te aromatawai.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Waihīrere i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Waihīrere ki te marae o Waihīrere, e pā tata atu ana ki Tūranga-nui-a-Kiwa. Kei tuawhenua te kōhanga reo, ki tētahi hapori whānau iti, ā, e pā tata atu ana ki tētahi papa rēhia e mau nei i ngā rākau māori, ngā manga, me ngā wāhi wāteatea.

Kua toru tekau tau te kōhanga reo e whakahaerehia ana. He tokomaha ngā mema o te whānau kua roa nei e whai wāhi ana ki te kōhanga reo, taka rawa mai ki tēnei rā.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu ana i tō rātou mōhiotanga mō Waihīrere, te reo, me ngā tikanga Māori?

Kei te whakawhanake haere te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki mō Waihīrere, ā, e ako ana rātou ki te whakamahi i te reo me ngā tikanga Māori mō te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo take.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki kia ako i a rātou anō, i tō rātou tūrangawaewae hoki. Ka ako rātou i te marae, i ngā pou whenua me ngā hītori o Waihīrere me tōna hapori. Ka whai wā ngā tamariki i ia rā ki te whare nui, kōrero ai mō ō rātou whānau me ō rātou hononga ki te marae. Ka tautoko ngā kaiako i ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā kaupapa i te marae, tae atu ki te pōwhiri, me ngā whakanuitanga, ngā hui hoki a te whānau me te iwi. He kaha te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao o te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaiako i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki ako reo e hāpai ana i te whakamahinga o te reo Māori. Ka tāwhai ngā tamariki nohinohi i ngā oro, ā, ka whakawhiti kōrero hoki mā ngā kupu iti me ngā rerenga kōrero māmā. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki tuākana ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero māmā, ā, ka whai hoki i ngā tohutohu mā te reo Māori. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te taki i ngā pepeha me te waiata i ngā waiata e pā ana ki Waihīrere. Ka tautokona tā rātou pānui pukapuka, ka whakapuaki rātou i ngā whakamāramatanga ki ngā pikitia, ā, ka ako hoki i ngā kupu hou. Ka akiaki ngā kaiako i ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori ki te kāinga. E mōhio ana ngā mātua, kei te pakari haere ā rātou tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori ki te kāinga. Kei te ahu whakamua ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e manaaki ana, e poipoi ana hoki. Ka poipoi ngā kaimahi i te pai o ngā hononga me ngā taunekeneke ki te taha o ngā tamariki. Kua āta whakaritea ngā taumahi ako, ā, ka akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i tō rātou taiao ako. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tiaki i ngā kararehe, ā, ka kawe atu ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te kohikohi kai. He harikoa ngā tamariki, ā, he pārekareka ki a rātou te noho ki te kōhanga reo.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He mea nui ki te whānau te ako a ngā tamariki. Kua tāutuhia e rātou ngā whāinga ako mā ia tamaiti. Ka whakarite rātou i tētahi hōtaka hei aro ki ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Ka mahi ngātahi te whānau ki te taha o ngā kaimahi ki te whakarite i ngā kai, me te whakatutuki i ngā matea i ngā wā e tika ana. Ka tautokona ngā tamariki i te hōtaka o ia rā.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore anō te whānau kia tāutu ngā kaupapa rautaki matua, ngā kaupapa ā-tau matua hoki. Kāhore ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki e āta aroturukitia ana. He whāiti noa ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki te aromātai hōtaka. Ahakoa ka hui rātou i ia te wā hei whakatutuki haere i ngā taumahi o ia rā, he iti noa te aro atu o aua āhuatanga e whai pānga ana ki ngā akoranga hei te rā ka whai ake. Me āta arotahi atu ki te hāpai i taua āhuatanga.

Kāhore anō kia oti ngā tuhinga ōkawa e pā ana ki te haumaru o ngā tamariki. Me whakawhanake, me whai atu hoki i ngā kaupapa here, ngā tukanga, me ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Hei whakapai ake i āna whakaritenga, me tahuri te whānau ki te:

  • tāutu i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea puta noa i te whare kōhungahunga, o roto, o waho hoki, me te whakatutuki haere i ngā mahi e tika ana ki te whakakore i aua āhuatanga [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; Ngā Paearu Raihana HS12]
  • whakatinana i tētahi tukanga mō te aroturuki i te moe o ngā tamariki, me te whakaatu i taua tukanga, te tuhi haere hoki i ngā wā moe o ngā tamariki [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; Ngā Paearu Raihana HS 9]
  • tuhi haere i ngā whara katoa ka pupū ake ki te kōhanga reo [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; Ngā Paearu Raihana HS25]
  • pupuri i ngā whakaaetanga i tuhia ai e ngā mātua mō te whāngai rongoā, me te whakatakoto ā-tuhi hoki i ngā rongoā katoa (ērā i tūtohungia e te rata, ērā hoki i tiki noa atu) i whāngai ai ki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; Ngā Paearu Raihana HS28]
  • āta whai atu i ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te haumaru o ngā tamariki ki ngā haerenga i whakaritea ai. [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; Ngā Paearu Raihana HS17]

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakaae ngātahi Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo, kia toro atu ki te tautoko e tika ana ki te whakatutuki i ngā mahi ka whai ake, nā runga i tēnā i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Waihīrere ki te marae o Waihīrere, e pā tata atu ana ki Tūranga-nui-a-Kiwa. Kei te whanake haere te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki a Waihīrere, me tā rātou whakamahinga o te reo me ngā tikanga Māori ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo pūtake. Ko ētahi āhuatanga hei whakapai ake, ko te hauora me te haumaru, te whakamahere hōtaka, me ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aromātai me te aromatawai.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Waihīrere is based on Waihīrere Marae near Gisborne. Children have a developing knowledge about Waihīrere, learning to use te reo and tikanga Māori for a variety of purposes. Areas for improvement include health and safety; programme planning, evaluation and assessment practice.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Waihīrere will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Waihīrere is located at Waihīrere Marae near Gisborne. This kōhanga reo is in a rural setting that includes a small whānau based settlement and a local park with native trees, small streams and wide open spaces.

The kōhanga reo has been operating for thirty years. Many whānau members have had a long association with the kōhanga reo and remain involved today.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children show that they know about Waihīrere, te reo and tikanga Māori?

Children have a developing knowledge about Waihīrere and are learning to use te reo and tikanga Māori for a variety of purposes.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are supported to learn about who they are and where they are from. They learn about the marae, important landmarks and the history of Waihīrere and its community. Children spend time each day in the meeting house talking about their whānau and connections to the marae. Kaiako support children to participate in marae activities including pōwhiri, whānau and iwi celebrations and hui. Children have a strong sense of belonging.

Children are immersed in a te reo Māori environment. Kaiako use a range of language learning strategies that support the use of te reo Māori. Younger children mimic sounds, use single words and simple sentences to communicate. Older children engage in simple conversations and follow te reo Māori instructions. Children enjoy singing waiata and recite pepeha about Waihīrere. They are supported to read books, share the meaning of pictures and learn new words. Kaiako encourage children to speak Māori at home. Parents acknowledge their children are growing in confidence to use te reo Māori at home. Children are progressing as speakers of te reo Māori.

Children learn in a caring and nurturing environment. Kaimahi foster positive relationships and interactions with children. Learning activities are well organised and encourage children to explore their learning environment. Children have opportunities to care for animals and kaimahi take children to gather kai. Children are happy and enjoy being at the kōhanga reo.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s learning is important to whānau. They have identified learning goals for individual children. They prepare a programme with the intention to respond to children’s needs and interests. Whānau work alongside kaimahi preparing kai and attending to children’s needs as required. Children are supported in the daily programme.

Key Next Steps

Whānau have not identified strategic or annual priorities. Children’s learning is not well monitored. Kaimahi have limited programme evaluation information. While they meet regularly to follow up the day to day activities there is little focus on how this will influence learning the next day. This area requires focused support.

Formal records about children’s safety are not completed. Health and safety policies, procedures and practices need to be developed and followed.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

In order to improve practice the whānau must:

  • identify hazards throughout the centre both inside and outdoors and then take practical steps to eliminate [R46, Early Childhood and Care Centres 2008, Licensing Criteria, HS 12]
  • implement a procedure for monitoring children’s sleep and ensure it is displayed and a record of children’s sleep times is kept [R46, Early Childhood Education and Care Centres 2008, Licensing Criteria, HS 9]
  • keep a record of all injuries that occur in the kōhanga reo [R46, Early Childhood Education and Care Centres 2008, Licensing Criteria, HS 25]
  • keep a record of the written authority from parents for the administering of medicine and a record of all medicine (prescription and non prescription) given to children left in the care of the kōhanga reo [R 46, Early Childhood Education and Care Centres 2008, Licensing Criteria, HS 28]
  • that policies and procedures about children’s physical safety are rigorously followed for planned excursions. [R46, Early Childhood Education and Care Centres2008, Licensing Criteria, HS 17].

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau agreed to seek appropriate support to address the next steps identified in this report.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Waihīrere is based on Waihīrere Marae near Gisborne. Children have a developing knowledge about Waihīrere, learning to use te reo and tikanga Māori for a variety of purposes. Areas for improvement include health and safety; programme planning, evaluation and assessment practice.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Waihīrere will be in three years.
