Te Aromātai

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hiku o te Ika ki Te Hāpua, ā, e whakakaurera ana i te whanga o Pārengarenga, ki Te Tai Tokerau. Koia nei te kōhanga reo whakateraki rawa atu o Aotearoa. Ka whai wāhi nui tēnei kōhanga reo ki te hapori motuhake nei.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te kite i te tūmāia o ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo?

Ka tūmāia ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakatinana rātou i ngā uaratanga o te kōhanga reo, ā, ka kite i tō rātou whakaaro nui ki te taiao whānui.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira 

E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo Māori ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te tāruarua me te whakatauira hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo ō ngā tamariki mā te ōkawa me te ōpaki o ngā horopaki. Ka whakapūmautia te reo ā-iwi me ngā kupu motuhake a Ngāti Kurī ki waenganui i ngā tamariki me ngā pākeke. Ka ako ngā mema o te whānau i ngā waiata a te kōhanga reo, ngā hīmene, ngā pepeha, me ngā whakamoemiti ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki. E āhei ana te nuinga o ngā tamariki tuākana ki te whakawhiti whakaaro mā te reo Māori. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori.

Ka whakamanahia te tuakiri me te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki. Ka whakanui ngā tamariki i ō rātou tuakiri me ō rātou tūrangawaewae mā te whakahua ake i ō rātou pepeha. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te ako i ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu. Ka kitea te hononga pakari o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo, ā, ki tō rātou whānau hoki.

E wheako ana ngā tamariki i te mārō o te taura hono tāngata. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i ngā uaratanga o te whanaungatanga, o te aroha, o te manaakitanga, me te tautoko. He pai ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā tuākana me ngā teina, ā, ka ngākaunui ngā tuākana ki te hāpai i ō rātou tēina. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga ki te tākaro takitahi me te tākaro takirōpū. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā wā ki te whakatangi i ngā taonga puoro, ā, ka whakatau rātou i ngā mema o te pēne Rātana. He pakari te taunekeneke o ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu i tō rātou wāhi noho.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

E whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i ngā akoranga e whai pānga ana ki te wāhi e tū nei tō rātou kōhanga reo. Kua whakawhanakehia e ngā kaimahi tētahi mahere ā-tau e whai pānga ana ki ngā āhuatanga motuhake o Te Hāpua me Ngāti Kurī. Kua tuituia ngā ngākaunuitanga o ngā tamariki ki te hōtaka o ia rā. Ko ngā āhuatanga o te noho taiwhenua, pērā i te whakangau poaka me te hī ika e whai pānga ana ki te ao o ngā tamariki. He pakari tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki te taiao tūturu, māoriori hoki. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te pūtaketanga me te kiko hoki o ngā mahere e whai whakaaro ana hoki ki ō rātou ngākaunuitanga me tō rātou wāhi noho.

Kua āta tuhia ngā mahere me ngā aromatawai o te ako a ngā tamariki. E whakamōhiotia ana te whānau mō te koke o ā rātou tamariki, ā, he rite tonu te tuhia, te aroturukitia hoki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā kitenga me ngā kōnae ako hei kapo atu i aua mōhiohio. Ka whakarato te whānau i ō rātou whakaaro me ngā kōrero urupare hei hāpai i ngā mahere me ngā aromatawai. E tino tautokona ana te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga ki te kōhanga reo.

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. Kua āta whakaritea te taiao ako, he ātaahua, he muramura hoki o roto, o waho, ā, he nui ngā rauemi. He tino tirohanga tā te whānau mō te aronga o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakamahia Te Ara Tūāpae hei tāutu i ngā whāinga rautaki me te whakawhanake i tētahi mahere mahi ā-tau. Kua oti te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi katoa. He tau ngā pūrongo pūtea. Ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā whakataunga katoa ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E mōhio ana te whānau o te kōhanga reo, e tika ana kia whakapakari ake te āheinga ki te hāpai i te whakamaheretanga o tangata kē atu. He iti noa ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhanake i tō rātou ārahitanga, me te whakangungu tonu kia riro ai i a rātou te Tohu Whakapakari.

Kāhore ētahi o ngā whakaritenga o te hauora me te haumaru e āta whakapūmautia ana. Kāhore te tukanga mō te whakamoe tamariki i te whakatinanatia e ai ki te kaupapa here whakamoe. Ahakoa he rite tonu te whakamātauria o ngā tamariki e moe ana, kāhore ēnei e waitohungia ana e tētahi pakeke e mau nei i taua kawenga.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakawhiti kōrero, kia whakatinana hoki i ngā rautaki hei whakamahere i a tangata kē atu
  • āta whakarite i te pūmautanga ki ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te āta mātakitaki i ngā tamariki e moe ana.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hiku o te Ika ki Te Hāpua, i te Tai Tokerau. He tūmāia ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakatinana rātou i ngā uaratanga o te kōhanga reo, ā, ka kitea hoki tō rātou whakaaro nui ki te taiao whānui. He ākonga harikoa, he ākonga ngana hoki ngā tamariki, e āta poipoia ana, e āta hāpaitia ana hoki e ngā kaimahi me te whānau.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hiku o te Ika i roto i te toru o ngā tau. 

Lynda Pura-Watson
Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori 

16 Here-turi-kōkā, 2016

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hiku o te Ika is in Te Hāpua, Northland.  Children are confident at kōhanga reo. They demonstrate the kōhanga reo values and show respect for the wider environment. Children are happy and engaged learners who are surrounded by supportive kaimahi and whānau.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hiku o te Ika will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hiku o Te Ika is in Te Hāpua, and overlooks the Parengarenga Harbour, Northland. It is the most northern kōhanga reo in Aotearoa. The kōhanga reo is an important part of this remote community.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children demonstrate they are confident in their kōhanga reo environment?

Children are confident at kōhanga reo. They demonstrate the kōhanga reo values and show respect for the wider environment.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are well supported to learn te reo Māori at kōhanga reo. Kaimahi use repetition and modelling to support children’s language development in both formal and informal settings. The unique dialect and vocabulary of Ngāti Kurī is reinforced with children and adults. Whānau members learn kōhanga reo waiata, hīmene, pepehā and whakamoemiti alongside their children. Most of the older children can communicate in te reo Māori. Children confidently use te reo Māori.

Children’s identity and belonging is valued. Children acknowledge who they are and where they are from in their pepeha. Kaimahi support children to learn about their connections to others. Children display a strong sense of belonging to their kōhanga reo and whānau.

Children experience strong relationships. Kaimahi model the values of whanaungatanga, aroha, manaakitanga and tautoko. Relationships between tuakana and teina are positive and tuakana willingly support their teina. Kaimahi provide opportunities for independent and group play. Children enjoy sessions with musical instruments and imitate the Rātana band members. Children interact confidently with others in their environment.

Planning and Evaluation

Children experience learning that reflects the location of their kōhanga reo. Kaimahi have developed an annual plan that acknowledges significant features of Te Hāpua and Ngāti Kurī. Children’s interests are incorporated into the daily programme. The rural lifestyle including hunting and fishing is a natural way of life for the children. They interact confidently in the physical and natural environment. Children benefit from purposeful and relevant planning which considers their interests and location.

Planning and assessment of children’s learning is well documented. Whānau are informed of what children are learning and progress is recorded and monitored regularly. Kaimahi use observations and profile books to capture this information. Whānau provide ideas and feedback to support planning and assessment. Children’s learning and development is well supported at kōhanga reo.

Whānau members manage the kōhanga reo well. The learning environment is well organised, well resourced, attractive and colourful both indoors and outdoors. The whānau has a clear vision for the direction of the kōhanga reo. Te Ara Tūāpae has been used to identify strategic goals and to develop an annual action plan. Appraisals for all staff have been completed. Financial reporting is sound. Children are at the heart of all kōhanga reo decision making.

Key Next Steps

The kōhanga reo whānau recognise the need to build capability to support succession planning. Kaimahi have few opportunities to develop leadership roles and continue with training towards the tohu whakapakari.

Some health and safety practices are not well maintained. The procedure for monitoring children while they sleep is not implemented in line with the sleeping policy. While regular checks of sleeping children are made these are not signed off by a responsible adult.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • discuss and implement strategies for succession planning
  • ensure that procedures for monitoring sleeping children are maintained.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hiku o te Ika is in Te Hāpua, Northland.  Children are confident at kōhanga reo. They demonstrate the kōhanga reo values and show respect for the wider environment. Children are happy and engaged learners who are surrounded by supportive kaimahi and whānau. 

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hiku o te Ika will be in three years. 

Lynda Pura-Watson

Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori
