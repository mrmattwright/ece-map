Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Iti Noa ki Elgin, i Tūranga-nui-a-Kiwa. E whanake ana te āheinga o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. E whakawhanake ana rātou i te māramatanga ki ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga hei kaiārahi ki waenga i ō rātou hoa.

E noho pūmau ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakarato i te kounga o ngā akoranga me ngā whakatutukitanga mō ngā tamariki. He pai te noho mōhio o te whānau ki tēnā e ākona ana e ā rātou tamariki. Ka mātua noho ngā wheako akoranga a ngā tamariki hei tahū. .

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Iti Noa i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1  Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Iti Noa ki Elgin, ki Tūranga-nui-a-Kiwa. Kua whai wāhi ētahi whānau hou ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

E noho pūmau ana ngā kaimahi ki te kounga o ngā akoranga me ngā whakatutukitanga mō ngā tamariki. E mōhio ana te whānau, ko te reo Māori tētahi aronga matua mō ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

2  Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa tā ngā tamariki whakaatu i te tūmāia hei rangatira, i te whakamahinga o te reo Māori?

E whanake ana te āheinga o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. E whakawhanake ana rātou i te māramatanga ki ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga hei kaiārahi ki waenga i ō rātou hoa.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira 

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te māramatanga ki tā rātou ake whai wāhi atu ki ā rātou akoranga. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki tuākana ki te ārahi i ētahi āhuatanga o te hōtaka akoranga ōkawa, tae atu ki ngā karakia. He māhorahora te whakapuaki a ngā tamariki i ō rātou whakapapa, ā, ka ako i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki ētahi atu. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te ako i ngā atua Māori me ō rātou whakapapa. Ko te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi e hāpai ana i ngā tamariki ki te whakatau i ā rātou ake whiringa i roto i ā rātou akoranga. Kua waihangatia ngā taumahi hei akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te mahi ngātahi ki te taha o ō rātou hoa me ngā pākeke. He pai te taunekeneke a ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki, ka whakapuaki i ngā kōrero whakakatakata, ā, ka ngākaunui hoki ki te noho ngātahi. Ka whai wāhi nui te whakamihi, te akiaki, me te whakanui i ngā whakamātau a ia tamaiti ki te hōtaka. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te āta taunekeneke me te whakaute i ngā kaumātua, te whānau, me ō rātou hoa. He ākonga ngana, he ākonga tākare hoki ngā tamariki.

Kei ngā tamariki tētahi taiao e whakatūturu ana i te whai wāhi nui o te reo Māori. Kōrero Māori ai ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori, ā, ka whakamahi rātou i ngā akoranga kē ā ngā tamariki ki te whakatairanga ake i tō rātou reo. Ka aro nui ngā tamariki ki ngā akiaki a ngā kaimahi, ka whai wāhi ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, ā, ka whakautu hoki i ngā pātai e pā ana ki ā rātou taumahi. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te tāruarua, ngā waiata, ngā kēmu ā-ringa, me ngā rākau hei whakatairanga i te reo o ngā tamariki. Ko ngā haerenga me ngā mahi ā-ringa e poipoi ana i te ngahau i a rātou e ako ana i te reo Māori. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i te whakamahinga a ngā pēpi i te reo ā-tinana ki te whakatinana i tō rātou māramatanga. Ko te tīmatanga o te reo matatini me te pāngarau e whai wāhi nui ana ki te hōtaka akoranga o te reo. Ka ngākaunui ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

He pai te whakarite me te whakamahere i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Kua āta tuhia ngā mahere hōtaka, ā, e tino hono ana ki ngā putanga mō ngā tamariki i runga anō i te āhua o ō rātou reanga, ā, ka whai pānga anō hoki ki ngā pūmanawa me ngā ngākaunuitanga o ia tamaiti. Ka hāpai te whakamahere hōtaka i te whakapuakitanga, ā, ka whakamahi i te aromātai hei tāutu i ngā matea me ngā whakapaitanga. Aromatawaihia ngā tamariki i ia te wā hei tautuhi i tā rātou ahu whakamua. Ka noho mōhio te whānau ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka tino kuhu ngā tamariki ki te ako.

Ko ngā ngākaunuitanga o ngā tamariki e ārahi ana i ngā whakataunga a te whānau i roto i ngā whakahaeretanga. Ka kitea te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo ki te whānuitanga o te whakahaeretanga me ngā mahere mō meāke nei. He pai te āta arotake whaiaro hei whakamōhio i ngā whakawhanaketanga. Ka whai wāhi te whānau ki ngā whakataunga me te arotake i te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa here, me ngā tukanga. Arotakengia ai i ia te wā ngā kaupapa here me ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te hauora, te haumaru me ngā kaimahi. He pai te kaiwhakahaere ki te whakariterite i ngā tuhinga o te kōhanga reo, ā, ka noho wātea ēnei ki te whānau, ka hāngai hoki ki te rā. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā ngā pūnaha whakahaere e poipoi ana i ō rātou waiora me ā rātou akoranga.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He mea nui ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki. Ahakoa kua whakarerekē haere ngā kaimahi i te hōtaka me ngā mahinga hei whakatutuki i ngā matea o ngā tamariki, kāhore aua whakarerekētanga e tuhia ana i ngā wā katoa. E whakaae ana ngā kaimahi, ko te aromātai hōtaka tētahi wāhanga hei whakapakari ake.

E whanake ana te whakamahere rautaki. Ko te whakawhanaketanga i tētahi mahere rautaki paetawhiti me tētahi mahere mahi ā-tau ka hāpai i te whānau ki te tāutu i ngā kaupapa matua ā-tau, ā, ki te aroturuki hoki i te ahu whakamua. Ko te whare me ngā rawa tētahi āhuatanga kia āta aroturukitia e te whānau.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakawhanake i tētahi mahere rautaki
  • whakapakari i te aromātai hōtaka
  • whakatutuki, kia tuhi hoki i te arowhai o ngā āhuatanga mōrearea i ia te wā.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga reo o Iti noa is in Elgin, Gisborne. Children are developing as speakers of te reo Māori. They are developing an understanding their roles and responsibilities as leaders amoung their peers.

Kaimahi are committed to the provision of quality learning and success for children. Whānau are kept well informed of what their children are learning. Children’s experiences in learning are a priority. 

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Iti Noa will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Iti Noa is in Elgin, Gisborne. New whānau are involved in managing the kōhanga.

Kaimahi are committed to quality learning and success for children. Whānau recognise te reo Māori is a priority for children in the kōhanga reo.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children demonstrate confidence as leaders and using te reo Māori?

Children are developing as speakers of te reo Māori. They are developing an understanding their roles and responsibilities as leaders among their peers.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are developing an understanding of their role in learning. Kaimahi encourage the older children lead aspects of the formal learning programme including karakia. Children willingly share their whakapapa and learn about others. Kamahi support children to learn about ngā atua Māori and their whakapapa. A range of activities enable children to make choices in their learning. Activities are designed to encourage children to work co-operatively with their peers and adults. Kaimahi interact positively with children, sharing funny stories and enjoying each other’s company. Praise, encouragement and acknowledging individuals efforts is a natural part of the programme. Kaimahi provide opportunities for children to interact positively and respectfully with kaumātua, whānau and peers. Children are engaged and enthusiastic learners.

Children experience an environment where te reo Māori is normal. Kaimahi are speakers of te reo Māori and use children’s prior learning to enhance their language. Children respond to kaimahi prompts, engage in conversations and answer questions about their activities. Kaimahi use repetition, waiata, hand games and rākau to enhance children’s language. Excursions and hands on activities foster fun while learning te reo Māori. Kaimahi encourage babies to use body language to convey their understanding. Early literacy and numeracy are an important part of the language learning programme. Children enjoy learning te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s learning is well organised and planned. Programme planning is well documented and clearly linked to outcomes for children that are age appropriate and reflect individual strengths and interest. Programme planning supports delivery and evaluation is used to identify the needs and improvements. Children are regularly assessed to determine their progress. Whānau are kept informed about children’s learning. Children are engaged learners.

Children’s interests guides whānau management decisions. The kaupapa of kōhanga reo is reflected in the overall operation and forward planning. Internal review is used well to inform developments. Whānau contribute to decision making, reviewing the charter, policies and procedures. Health and safety and personnel policies and practices are regularly reviewed. The kaiwhakahaere keeps kōhanga reo documents organised, accessible and up to date. Children benefit from management systems that nurtures their wellbeing and learning.

Key Next Steps

Children’s learning needs are important. Although kaimahi have made some changes to the programme and routines in response to children’s needs these are not always documented. Kaimahi agree that programme evaluation is an area to be strengthened.  

Strategic planning is developing. Developing a long term strategic plan and an annual action plan will assist whānau to identify annual priorities and monitor progress. Property is an area whānau need to monitor closely.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • develop a strategic plan
  • strengthen programme evaluation
  • conduct and record regular hazard checks.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga reo o Iti noa is in Elgin, Gisborne. Children are developing as speakers of te reo Māori. They are developing an understanding their roles and responsibilities as leaders amoung their peers.

Kaimahi are committed to the provision of quality learning and success for children. Whānau are kept well informed of what their children are learning. Children’s experiences in learning are a priority. 

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Iti Noa will be in three years.
