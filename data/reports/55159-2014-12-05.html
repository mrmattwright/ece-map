1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

‘Ko tā te kōhanga reo, ko te reo kia tika, ko te reo kia rere, ko te reo kia Māori.’ (Te tūtohinga a te whānau)

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Kimihia te kupu ki Kaiti, ki te Tūranga-nui-a-Kiwa. E ngākau nui Te Kōhanga Reo o Kimihia te Kupu ki te whakatairanga i te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Mai i tōna tīmatanga, kua whai hua ngā mahi a ngā kaumātua me te whānau ki te whakawhanake i te taiao o te kōhanga reo, kia whānui ai ngā rauemi a ā rātou tamariki, ā, kia pai ai hoki te āta waihanga i te whare me ngā rawa mō te ako, me te manaaki.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. He tino hiahia tō te whānau ki te mōhio:

‘He pēhea te whakawhanake o ngā tamariki i te tū māia me te mōhiotanga anō hoki ki a rātou anō?’

He māia, he tākare hoki ngā tamariki, ā, kei te mōhio rātou ki a rātou anō.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki a rātou anō ki tētahi taiao e manaaki ana, e aroha ana hoki i a rātou. Ko te reo Māori me ōna tikanga tētahi aronga ki te whānau i ngā wā katoa. Ka whai wāhi atu te whānau ki ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo, ā, ka ngākau nui hoki ki te whai i ngā akoranga a ā rātou tamariki, i ō rātou whakawhanaketanga anō hoki. Ko te tupu me te whakawhanaketanga o te whānau mō meāke nei tētahi aronga ki te whānau i ngā wā katoa.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

‘He pēhea te whakawhanake o ngā tamariki i te tū māia me te mōhiotanga anō hoki ki a rātou anō?’

He māia, he tākare hoki ngā tamariki, ā, kei te mōhio rātou ki a rātou anō.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako i tō rātou reo. Ka tautoko ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te tūhura me te whakamahi i te reo i roto i ngā nekehanga katoa o ō rātou rā. He whānui ngā tūmomo rauemi e whakaongaonga ana i te pākiki o ngā tamariki me te whanake haere o tō rātou tirohanga ki tō rātou ao. He tau, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki, ā, e whakaongaongatia ana kia ako.

Ka whai pūtake, ka whai kiko hoki ngā wheako akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka whakapakari aua wheako i te māia me te mōhiotanga ki te tuakiri o ngā tamariki me ngā tuku ihotanga. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi e hāpai ana i ngā tamariki ki te whakahonohono ki a rātou anō, ki te whānau whānui hoki. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki ki ētahi atu, ngā mōhiotanga e pā ana ki a rātou anō.

He kaha ngā hononga, ka whai pūtake hoki te whanaungatanga ki waenga i ngā tamariki me tō rātou whānau. Ka whakanui ngā kaimahi i ngā taunekeneke papai e hāngai ana ki te aroha, te manaaki, me te awhi. He manaaki, he whakaute hoki ki ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki ki waenga i a rātou anō. He tau, he harikoa hoki rātou.

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki, he mea whakahirahira te tiaki i a rātou anō, i ētahi atu, i te taiao hoki. Ka aroturuki, ka aro atu hoki ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki ngā matea o ngā tamariki. Ka whakarite te whānau kia noho te ‘hauora o te tangata’ hei kaupapa matua i ngā wā katoa. He aronga nui tā rātou ki te hauora o te tangata me te kai i ngā kai pai.

Ka tāutuhia, ka poipoi tōtikatia hoki ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki, me te whakawhanaketanga hoki o ō rātou pūmanawa. He mātanga ngā kaimahi ki te aro ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo matea, ngā tūmomo āheinga hoki o ngā tamariki. Kua whakawhanakehia e rātou te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi ako, rautaki ako hoki hei hāpai i ngā tamariki e mau nei i ngā tino matea, tae atu ki ngā matea tauwhāiti. Ko tētahi kaupapa matua, ko te tū māia o ngā tamariki me tō rātou āheinga ki te whai wāhi hei ākonga.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka āta whakamaheretia ngā kaupapa matua a te whānau. Kei te whānau tētahi mahere rautaki i āta whakawhanakehia ai, ā, e whakatakoto ana tēnei i ngā kaupapa matua o te wā roa. Ka hāpaitia tēnei nā te kiko o ngā mahere whakahaere ā-tau, ā, i whai wāhi atu ngā mema ki aua mahere. Ka whai wāhi atu te arotake rautaki me ngā pūrongo ki ā rātou mahi arotake whaiaro. Ko te angitu o ngā tamariki me te tupu o te whānau tētahi kaupapa rautaki matua.

Ko ngā matea o ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau ka whakatutukihia. Kei te tino whakahaerehia, kei te tino aroturukitia hoki ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. Kua tino whakawhanakehia, ā, e āta whakatinanahia hoki e te whānau ngā pūnaha whakahaere me ngā whakaritenga. He pai te whakawhanaketanga o te arotake whaiaro, ā, e whakamahia ana e te whānau hei whakariterite mō ngā tau kei te heke mai. Ko ngā matea o ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau e ārahi ana i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te whakahaere a te whānau.

Kei ngā tamariki te maringanui kua āta maheretia ngā hōtaka hei hāpai i a rātou akoranga.

He māramatanga ngātahi ki te whakamahere, te whakarite, me te whakapuaki hoki i te hōtaka mō ngā tamariki. ko te kaha o ngā mahi ki te aromātai i te hōtaka, me ngā whakaaro huritao ngaio, e hāpai ana i te aroturukitanga o te whai hua o te hōtaka mō ngā tamariki katoa.

He pai te tuhi haere, te aroturuki hoki i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka pūrongo ngā kaimahi i te tupuranga me te whakawhanaketanga o ia tamaiti ki te whānau. He pūrongo whakamōhiohio e tohu ana i ngā whakatutukitanga a ngā tamariki.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaeretanga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora, me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Hei a hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Ko tā te Kōhanga Reo, Ko te reo kia tika, ko te reo kia rere, ko te reo kia Māori.’ (Whānau charter)

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kimihia te Kupu is in Kaiti, Gisborne. Te Kōhanga Reo o Kimiha te kupu is committed to promoting te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Since its establishment, kaumatua and whānau have worked successfully to develop the kōhanga reo environment so that their children have extensive resources and well designed facilities for learning and care.

The whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus for this review. The whānau were particularly interested in:

‘How well are children developing confidence and knowledge about who they are?

Children are confident, enthusiastic and know about who they are.

Children learn about who they are in a caring and loving environment. Te reo Māori me ōna tikanga is an ongoing focus for whānau. Whānau are involved in all areas of the kōhanga reo, and follow their children’s learning and development with interest. The growth and development of whānau is an ongoing focus for the future.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose ngā taumata whakahirahira as its focus area.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

‘How well are children developing confidence and knowledge about who they are?

Children are confident, enthusiastic learners, who know about who they are.

Areas of good performance

Children enjoy learning their language. Kaimahi support children to explore and use the language in their everyday lives. There are a range of resources that stimulate children’s curiosity and their developing views about their world. Children are settled, happy and stimulated to learn.

Children have relevant and meaningful learning experiences. These experiences build confidence and knowledge about children’s identity and heritage. Kaimahi and whānau use a range of activities that help children make connections with each other and the wider whānau. Children confidently share with others what they know about who they are.

Children have strong connections and meaningful relationships with their whānau. Kaimahi emphasise positive interactions that reflect aroha, manaaki and awhi. Children interact with each other in caring and respectful ways. They are settled and happy.

Children are aware of the importance of looking after themselves, others and the environment. Kaimahi and whānau monitor and respond to the needs of the children. The whānau ensure that health and well-being is of paramount importance at all times. They have a strong emphasis on healthy living and eating.

Children’s individual learning needs and developing talents are recognised and appropriately catered for. Kaimahi are skilled at responding to the diverse needs and abilities of children. They have developed an extensive range of learning resources and strategies to support children with high needs, including disabilities. Children’s confidence and their ability to contribute as learners is a priority.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

Whānau priorities are well planned. Whānau have a well developed strategic plan that sets out long term priorities. This is supported by a detailed annual management plans that members have contributed to. Strategic review and reporting are part their self review practice. Children’s success, and whānau growth is a strategic priority.

The needs of children and their whānau are well served. All areas of the kōhanga reo are effectively managed and monitored. Management systems and practices are highly developed and rigorously implemented by the whānau. Self review is well developed and used by the whānau for future planning. Children and their whānau needs guide whānau management practice.

Children receive well planned programmes to support their learning. There is a shared understanding about programme planning, organisation and delivery for children. Strong programme evaluation practices and professional reflection assists in the monitoring of programme effectiveness for all children.

Children’s learning is well recorded and monitored. Kaimahi regularly report on children’s growth and development to their whānau. These reports are informative and indicate what children have achieved.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Licensed Kōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
