2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

‘Nona te ao, no tātou katoa. No tātou te ao, nona ano.’

Ka whakahaeretia Te Kōhanga Reo o Matawera ki te taha o Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Ruamata, i te marae o Ruamata. Ka mahi tahi ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakatutuki o rātou wawata me o rātou whāinga kua puta mai i Te Aho Matua, ā, e whakareia ana e Te Korowai. He pai te whakatakoto o ngā whenua me ngā rawa o te kōhanga reo, ā, he pai hoki te tāpiri o ēnei ki te marae whānui me te taiao o te kura. Ka whakaūtia ngā tohu whenua o neherā huri noa i te kōhanga reo i roto i ngā kaupapa mātauranga ka tukuna ki ngā tamariki.

I kōwhiri te whānau ko Te Aho Matua hei wāhi arotahi mō tēnei Arotake Mātauranga. Kei te pūtake o ngā whāinga me ngā ratonga akoranga ko te whakatairanga i te reo Māori me ōna tikanga, te hauora me te oranga katoa o te whānau me ngā tamariki me te noho pūmau o te angitū o te mātauranga o te whānau. Ka rongo ngā tamariki ki ngā hōtaka akoranga whai rawa, tōtika, whakautu hoki e whakatairanga ana i tō rātou māia me tō rātou kaha hei kaikōrero i te reo Māori me te whakatenatena i a rātou ki te ako me te whai wāhi ki ngā horopaki whānui o Te Ao Māori.

Ka whakaatu ngā tamariki i tō rātou tino maioha me tō rātou piripono ki tō rātou taiao. Kua waihangatia ngā hōtaka akoranga kia whakawhānui te mārama o ngā tamariki ki Te Ao Māori. He mea whai hua ngā taunekeneke akoranga, ā, he mea atawhai. He pai te tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te puāwai hei kaikōrero māia ki te reo Māori.

E whakanui ana ngā mātua hei tino kaitautoko i te tipu me te oranga o ngā tamariki. Nā tō rātou ngākaunui me tō rātou whai wāhi mātātoa i tino whakaawe te ako o te whānau i te kāinga, te wāhi kei te rere noa te reo i ia rā. Ko te reo Māori te reo tuatahi o te nuinga o ngā tamariki. Kei ngā kaimahi me te whānau whānui ngā momo mātauranga Māori whānui, ngā tohu mātauranga, ngā pūkenga, me ngā pūmanawa hei whakarei ake i te tipu o ngā tamariki. Kei te whai wāhi ngā kaumātua ki te kōhanga reo, ā, e harikoa ana rātou ki ngā angitū o ngā whānau o te kōhanga reo, te kura me te marae ki te whakawhanake i ngā taumata o te reo o a rātou mokopuna.

He tōtika te whakahaere me te aroturuki i te kōhanga reo i runga i te ngākaunui ki ngā mokopuna me te whānau. Ka whakarewaina, ka aroturukihia aTe Korowaime ngā kawenga hanganga ture me ngā whāinga o Te Aho Matua. Ka tauoko Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te whāinga matua o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamātau anō i ngā huarahi mā rātou ki te whakawhānui me te whakarei ake i ngā taiao akoranga e whakanikoniko ana i ngā wheako akoranga o te whānau me ngā tamariki ki Te Ao Māori.

Ngā Mahi ā Meake Nei

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kohanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i ïnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Matawera kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te pupuritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Matawera.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Te Aho Matua.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake matauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aro matawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Te Ira Tangata

He whakamārama

‘Ahakoa iti, he iti mapihi pounamu. He kakano i ruia mai i Rangiatea, e kore ia e ngaro.’

E whakapono ana te whānau ki te puna angitū te oranga katoa o ngā tamariki, o rātou wairua, hinengaro, tinana me o rātou whatumanawa me pono ko te whanaungatanga. Kua kii mai rātou kei te mōhio, kei te whakaute a rātou tamariki ki ngā āhua motuhake o tēna, o tēna, ā, he mea nui kia whakamanatia ngā tāngata katoa.

Ngā wāhi e pai ana te haere

Te Ira Tangata.He pai te hāpai i te whanaketanga a ahurea, a tinana, a wairua, a whatumanawa hoki o ngā tamariki. He maia te mahi tahi o ngā tamariki me o rātou hoa i ngā momo horopaki. He akonga tau, he akonga harikoa ngā tamariki. Ko tētahi tino āhua o te hōtaka o ia rā ko te ako tahi a te whānau, ā, ka ako ngā tamariki, ka tuhura haere i te taha o o rātou whānau me ērā atu tamariki. E whakanui ana te whānau katoa tae atu ana ki ngā kaumātua mō o rātou tohungatanga, o rātou mōhio, o rātou pūkenga me o rātou pūmanawa. Ka whakamaua o rātou pūkenga me o rātou pūmanawa katoa hei waihanga me te tuku i ngā hōtaka akoranga whai hua, tōtika, pono hoki. Ka rongo ngā tamariki ki ngā taunekeneke takatika, whakaute, whai hua hoki e hāpai ana i tō rātou whanaketanga me tō rātou ako. Kei te whanake te māiatanga o ngā tamariki ki te arataki me te whakatau i ngā huarahi ako ka whakaihiihi i o rātou whakaaro me te whakatenatena i a rātou ki te tuhura.

Te Ao

He whakamārama

‘Ka pu te ruha, ka hao te rangatahi.’

Kua kii mai te whānau o te kōhanga reo ka whiwhi o rātou tamariki i ngā wheako akoranga whai hua, tōtika hoki kua waihanga hei whakawhanake i te toi ahurea, te tū rangatira me te mārama ki ngā kōrero tuku iho mō tō rātou iwi o Te Arawa. Kua kii mai hoki te whānau ka hāpaitia tō rātou tirohanga roa tahi e te tino ngākaunui ki te whakarato me te whakatairanga i ngā wheako akoranga angitū i ngā horopaki whai hua tae atu ana ki te ako i te kainga.

Ngā wāhi e pai ana te haere

Te Ao.Kei te kaha haere te mārama o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou ao o Te Arawa, ngā hononga, ngā whakapapa me te ahurea tuku iho. Ka puku mahi ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakarato i ngā wheako akoranga whai hua mai i Te Ao Māori me Te Aotūroa kua āta whakaarohia, kua maheretia, ā, he mea whakautu. Tāpiri ko te whanake a reo ka āwhina ēnei wheako i te toi kaitiakitanga o ngā tamariki me tō rātou mōhio ki te pūtaiao. Ka whakatutuki angitū ngā horopaki akoranga whānui i ngā ngākaunuitanga me ngā matea huhua o ngā tamariki. No reira ka taea e ngā tamariki te tuhura haere kia mārama tō rātou ao ki a rātou. Ka whakarei ake, ka whakawhānuitia te whanaketanga katoa me te mārama o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori me ngā tikanga, ngā kōrero tuku iho, me ngā whakapono o te iwi ki te noho rūmaki rātou ki ēnei horopaki pono me o rātou whānau.

Ngā Iwi

He whakamārama

‘Te piko o te mahuri, tera te tupu o te rakau.’

Kua kii mai te whānau ko te whānau tonu kei te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo kia tutuki ko te tirohanga ngātahi, ngā matea me ngā wawata o te whānau, te marae, te hapū me te iwi. Kei te hiahia rātou ki ngā taumata o te pai mō a rātou tamariki hei Māori nō Te Arawa, ā, kia noho hoki rātou hei kaiwhaiwāhi angitu ki te ao whānui. Ka whakahaere, ka arotake, ka aroturuki tōtika rātou i o rātou ahunga whakamua, o rātou angitū me tō rātou ahunga e ai ki ngā mātāpono o Te Korowai.

Ngā wāhi e pai ana te haere

Ngā Iwi.E whakatairanga angitū ana te oranga katoa o ngā tamariki mā te whakarato i tētahi taiao akoranga kaha, mātātoa hoki a whānau, ā hapū, ā iwi. Ka whai wāhi te whānau me te āwhina i tō rātou kōhanga reo ki te whakarato i ngā wheako ā iwi, ā hapū, ā whānau hoki mō a rātou tamariki. Ka rūmakina ngā tamariki ki ngā horopaki te reo Māori o mua ki reira rātou e rongo i ia wā, e kite i ia wā, e ako i ia wā ki ēnei pūtaketanga tae atu ana ki ngā kaupapa, ngā waka, ngā pūrākau, ngā pakiwaitara, ngā tikanga, ngā waiata me ngā karakia. Ka mōhio pai te whānau ki ngā mahi katoa i te kōhanga reo mai i ngā pānui i ia wā, ngā hui me ngā pūrongo e pā ana ki o rātou ahunga whakamua me o rātou angitū e pā ana ki tō rātou tirohanga ngātahi, o rātou whāinga me tō rātou ahunga. He pai te tipu o ngā tamariki hei kaiwhaiwāhi māia, pakari hoki ki ngā horopaki Te Ao Māori puta mai i te iwi.

Te Reo Māori

‘Toku reo, toku ohooho. Toku reo, toku mapihi maurea. Toku reo, toku whakakai marihi.’

He whakamārama

Kua kii mai te whānau ko te reo Māori te mea nui ki te angitū mō te wā roa ngā putanga mō a rātou tamariki. Ko te pakari me te āhei o a rātou tamariki ki te whai wāhi mātātoa a Māori nei ki tō rātou taiao me te ao whānui ngā tino ngākaunuitanga o tō rātou whānau, hapū me tō rātou iwi.

Ngā wāhi e pai ana te haere

Te Reo Māori. Ko te reo Māori te reo anake i te kōhanga reo. Ko te reo Māori te reo tuatahi o te nuinga o ngā tamariki. He mea whai hua ngā wheako akoranga, he tōtika, kua āta whakaarohia, ā, kua āta whakamaheretia. Ka whānui ake te mārama o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori ki te noho rūmaki rātou ki ngā horopaki Māori whānui e pono ana. Ka whakaūtia hoki ko te reo Māori te reo i e kainga o te nuinga o ngā tamariki. Kei te puāwai ngā tamariki hei kaikōrero maia, kaikōrero pakari ki te reo Māori, he kaiwhakaaro kaikini ki te reo, ā, he kaipānui pūkākā ana ki te reo. E tino ngākaunui ana te whānau ki te tautoko i te kōhanga reo ki te hāpai i o rātou tamariki ki te tipu hei kaikōrero me te whakamana i te reo i ia rā.

Āhuatanga Ako

He whakamārama

‘Tamariki wawahi taha. Aratakina ki te matapuna o te mohio, o te ora, o te maungarongo. Whaia te iti kahurangi, te tuohu koe, me he maunga teitei.’

Kua kii mai te whānau he tōtika te whakahaere i te ako o ngā tamariki, ā, ka whiwhi rātou i te whānui o ngā wheako kaupapa Māori whai hua e whakamana ana i o rātou tuakiri, o rato mātauranga me o rātou tikanga ā iwi. He huarahi kotahi tō te whānau ki te waihanga, te whakamahere, te whakahaere me te tuku i ngā wheako akoranga kia tutuki te whānui o ngā putanga angitū mō ngā tamariki katoa.

Ngā wāhi e pai ana te haere

He pai te whakatutuki i te ako, te whanake me te tiaki i ngā tamariki. Ka whiwhi rātou i ngā wheako akoranga whai hua, tōtika, pono hoki kua pai te whakamahere me te whakahaere hei tutuki i ngā matea me ngā ngākaunuitanga huhua o ngā tamariki. Ko ētahi o ngā mahi ko te hakinakina, te kapa haka, te waka ama, te maara kai, te hauora, te panui, te hangahanga, ngā mahi auaha, te tā kōrero, te taunekeneke, me te tuhura ki te whānui o ngā taio.

Ka āta aroturuki, kātahi ka tuhi me te tuku kōrero ki ngā mātua i ia wā mō te whanake, te ahunga me ngā putanga angitū o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kōrero aromatawai hei kīnaki i te āhua o te hōtaka, tōna whakatinana, me tōna whakahaere. Ka tuku pūrongo ngā kaimahi i ia wā e pā ana ki ngā hōtaka akoranga ka tuku ki a rātou tamariki me te tino arotahi ki te tuku pūrongo e ai ki ngā whāinga me te tirohanga roa o te kōhanga reo.

Te Tino Uaratanga

He whakamārama

‘Kia tu pakari, tu rangatira ia hei raukura mo tona iwi.’

‘Kei te tiaho mai o ratou wairua.’

Kei roto i te tirohanga roa o te whānau whānui, te hapū me te iwi ko te tirohanga roa o te kōhanga reo. Kei te pūtake o te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo ko te rūmaki tonu, te angitū ā mātauranga me ngā paetae puta noa i ngā wahanga katoa o te oranga o ngā tamariki. He nui ngā kōrero angitū kua puta mai e pā ana ki ngā tamariki i te kōhanga reo i tēnei wā me ngā tamariki kua puta i te kōhanga reo, o rātou whanaketanga katoa me te whakaumutanga o te whānau. Tae atu ana ki:

  • te whai wāhi whānui o ngā paetahi o te kōhanga reo ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo tae atu ana ki te hoki ki te kōhanga reo hei kaiako a tōna wā
  • te matatau ake o te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te reo Māori me te mātauranga Māori
  • te whiwhi tohu mai i ngā whare wānanga, whare takiura
  • te whai wāhi mātātoa o ngā kaumātua hei tūranga kaikini i tō rātou rautaki mātauranga
  • te angitū ā motu, angitū ā ao whānui tae atu ana ki ngā mahi toi me te mātauranga.

Ngā wāhi e pai ana te haere

Kei ngā putanga angitū mō ngā tamariki ko:

  • ngā akonga maia, harikoa, whakaute
  • ngā kaikōrero matatau ana ki te reo Māori
  • ngā akonga whakaihiihi ā whaiaro, ngā kaiwhaiwāhi hiahia hoki ki ngā horopaki Te Ao Māori
  • ngā kaiwhakaaro kaikini
  • ngā akonga whakahaere ia rātou anō, otirā ko ngā akonga pūkeke.

5 Ngā Kupu Tūturu ā te Whānau Whakahaere mō ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakiia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Matawera, tētahiTauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhangame tētahiRārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture pa ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Matauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātaurangame ngā whakaritengakaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

6 Ngā Tūtohunga

I whakaritea e Te Tari Arotake me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te tūtohutanga e whai ake nei:

  • kia whakamahi tonu te whānau i te arotake whaiaro hei hāpai i ngā whāinga rautaki, ngā matea o te whānau me te ahunga rautaki o te kōhanga reo.

7 Nga Mahi Hei ā Meake Nei

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kohanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

‘Nona te ao, no tātou katoa. No tātou te ao, nona ano.’

Te Kōhanga Reo o Matawera operates beside Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Ruamata, at Ruamata Marae. Whānau members work in partnership to achieve their aspirations and objectives which stem from their philosophy of Te Aho Matua, complemented byTe Korowai. The kōhanga reo premises and facilities are well developed and integrated into the wider marae and kura environment. Ancestral landmarks surrounding the kōhanga reo are emphasised in the provision of education to children.

Whānau chose Te Aho Matua as the focus area for this Education Review. Learning priorities and provision centre on the promotion of te reo Māori me ona tīkanga, the holistic health and wellbeing of whānau and children, and the sustained educational success of their members. Children receive rich, relevant and responsive programmes of learning that promote their confidence and competence as speakers of te reo Māori, and motivate them to learn and participate in wider Te Ao Māori contexts.

Children show a strong appreciation of and an affinity to their local natural and physical environment. Programmes of learning are designed to extend children’s understanding of Te Ao Māori. Interactions for learning are rich, meaningful and caring. Children are well supported to develop as confident speakers of te reo Māori.

Whānau are valued as key contributors to children’s development and wellbeing. Their shared commitment and active involvement has strongly influenced whānau learning at home, where te reo Māori is a strong part of their everyday lives. For most children te reo Māori is their first language. Kaimahi and the wider whānau collectively bring an extensive range of matauranga Māori, skills, qualifications and talents to benefit children’s growth and development. Kaumātua are involved at all levels, and are very pleased with the success of the kōhanga reo, kura and marae whānau in developing te reo Māori competence of their mokopuna.

The kōhanga reo is effectively managed and monitored in the interests of mokopuna and whānau.Te Korowaiand compliance obligations are upheld and monitored alongside their Te Aho Matua goals. ERO supports the kōhanga reo whānau priority to further examine ways they can extend and enhance existing learning environments that enrich whānau and children’s Te Ao Māori learning experiences.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

4 Findings

Te Ira Tangata

Background

‘Ahakoa iti, he iti mapihi pounamu. He kakano i ruia mai i Rangiatea, e kore ia e ngaro.’

The whānau believes that successful development of their children’s holistic wellbeing, wairua, hinengaro, tinana and whatumanawa is strongly dependant on whanaungatanga. They report that their children are aware of and respect individual uniqueness and that it is important that they show respect for themselves and others.

Areas of good performance

Te Ira Tangata.Children’s cultural, physical, spiritual and emotional development is well catered for. They confidently interact with each other, in various contexts and alongside others. Children are settled and happy learners. A strong feature of the daily programme is whānau shared learning, where children learn and explore beside their whānau and other children. All members, including kaumātua, are valued and recognised for their individual expertise, knowledge, skills and talents. Their combined skills and talents are harnessed to design and deliver rich, relevant and authentic learning programmes. Children experience positive, respectful and meaningful interactions that effectively support their development and learning. Children are developing the confidence to lead and determine ways of learning that stimulate their thinking and motivate them to explore.

Te Ao

Background

‘Ka pu te ruha, ka hao te rangatahi.’

The kōhanga reo whānau and whānau whānui report that their children are provided with rich and relevant learning experiences designed to develop a strong sense of cultural identity, pride and understanding of their Te Arawa ancestral heritage. Whānau members report that their shared vision is supported through a strong commitment to provide and promote successful learning experiences in meaningful contexts, including learning at home.

Areas of good performance

Te Ao.Children are developing a strong understanding of their Te Arawa world, connections, affiliation and cultural heritage. Kaimahi and whānau work hard to provide rich Te Ao Māori and Te Aotūroa learning experiences that are well considered, planned and responsive. Along with language development, these experiences help to develop children’s sense of kaitiakitanga and knowledge of science. The wide range of learning contexts, successfully caters for the diverse interests and needs of children. Consequently, children are able to explore in many ways to make sense of the world around them. Children’s holistic development and understanding of te reo Māori and local traditions, knowledge and beliefs is enhanced and extended when immersed in these authentic contexts with their whānau.

Ngā Iwi

Background

‘Te piko o te mahuri, tera te tupu o te rakau.’

Whānau members report that the kōhanga reo is managed by its whānau to meet the shared vision, needs and aspirations of whānau, marae, hapū and iwi. They want the best for their children as Te Arawa Māori and to be successful contributors to the wider world. They effectively manage, review, and monitor their progress, success and direction under the principles of Te Korowai.

Areas of good performance

Ngā Iwi.Children’s holistic wellbeing is successfully promoted through the provision of a strong and active whānau, hapū and iwi learning environment. Whānau participate and help their kōhanga reo to provide iwi, hapū and whānau based experiences for children. Children are immersed in traditional te reo Māori contexts where they can regularly hear, see and learn about these foundations, including kaupapa, waka, purakau, pakiwaitara, tīkanga, waiata and karakia. Whānau are well informed through regular panui, hui and reports about their progress and success with their shared vision, goals and direction. Children are developing well as confident and capable participants in Te Ao Māori, iwi based contexts.

Te Reo Māori

‘Toku reo, toku ohooho. Toku reo, toku mapihi maurea. Toku reo, toku whakakai marihi.’

Background

Members report that all aspects of te reo Māori are fundamental to long term successful outcomes for their children. Their children’s competence and capability to actively contribute and participate, as Māori, in their environment and the wider world are strong interests of their whānau, hapū and iwi.

Areas of good performance

Te Reo Māori. Te reo Māori is the only language used in and around the kōhanga reo. Most children use te reo Māori as their first language. Language learning experiences are rich, meaningful and relevant, carefully considered and planned for. Children’s depth of understanding of te reo Māori is extended when immersed in the various authentic Māori contexts. Te reo Māori is reinforced as the language of communication in a significant number of children’s homes. Children are developing as confident and capable speakers of te reo Māori, critical thinkers of the language and avid readers who are passionate about the language. Whānau are highly committed to supporting the kōhanga reo to develop their children as confident and competent speakers of te reo Maori and affirm the language as a normal part of their everyday lives.

Āhuatanga Ako

Background

‘Tamariki wawahi taha. Aratakina ki te matapuna o te mohio, o te ora, o te maungarongo. Whaia te iti kahurangi, te tuohu koe, me he maunga teitei.’

Whānau report that children’s learning is effectively managed and children are provided with a rich range of kaupapa Māori experiences that affirm their identity, knowledge and traditions. There is a shared whānau approach to designing, planning, managing and delivering learning experiences to achieve a range of successful outcomes for all children.

Areas of good performance

Children are well catered for in their learning, development and care. They receive rich, relevant and authentic learning experiences that are well planned and managed to respond to the diverse needs and interests of children. Activities include hakinakina, kapa haka, waka ama, maara kai, hauora, reading, construction, creating, publishing, interacting and exploring the vast natural and physical ancestral surrounds.

Children’s development, progression and outcomes for success are closely monitored, recorded and shared with their whānau on a regular basis. Assessment information is used by kaimahi to inform programme design, delivery, organisation and overall management. Kaimahi report regularly about the outcomes of learning programmes provided to their children, with a strong focus on reporting against kōhanga reo objectives and vision.

Te Tino Uaratanga

Background

‘Kia tu pakari, tu rangatira ia hei raukura mo tona iwi.’

‘Kei te tiaho mai o ratou wairua.’

The kōhanga reo vision is part of the wider vision of the whānau whānui, hapū and iwi. Continued immersion, educational success and achievement across children’s phases of life are at the heart of this kōhanga kaupapa. Success stories abound about existing and former kōhanga reo children, their holistic development and whānau transformation. These include:

  • kōhanga reo graduates contributing widely to the kaupapa in many ways, including returning to become kaiako
  • increased whānau and kaimahi te reo Māori and matauranga Māori competence
  • gaining qualifications from tertiary institutions
  • active involvement of kaumātua as a critical role in their education strategy
  • national and international success including the arts and education.

Areas of good performance

Success outcomes for children include:

  • confident, happy and respectful learners
  • competent speakers of te reo Māori
  • self motivated learners and enthusiastic participants in Te Ao Māori contexts
  • critical thinkers
  • self managing and determined learners.

26/03/2013

To the Parents and Community of Te Kōhanga Reo o Matawera

These are the findings of the Education Review Office’s latest report on Te Kōhanga Reo o Matawera.

‘Nona te ao, no tātou katoa. No tātou te ao, nona ano.’

Te Kōhanga Reo o Matawera operates beside Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Ruamata, at Ruamata Marae. Whānau members work in partnership to achieve their aspirations and objectives which stem from their philosophy of Te Aho Matua, complemented byTe Korowai. The kōhanga reo premises and facilities are well developed and integrated into the wider marae and kura environment. Ancestral landmarks surrounding the kōhanga reo are emphasised in the provision of education to children.

Whānau chose Te Aho Matua as the focus area for this Education Review. Learning priorities and provision centre on the promotion of te reo Māori me ona tīkanga, the holistic health and wellbeing of whānau and children, and the sustained educational success of their members. Children receive rich, relevant and responsive programmes of learning that promote their confidence and competence as speakers of te reo Māori, and motivate them to learn and participate in wider Te Ao Māori contexts.

Children show a strong appreciation of and an affinity to their local natural and physical environment. Programmes of learning are designed to extend children’s understanding of Te Ao Māori. Interactions for learning are rich, meaningful and caring. Children are well supported to develop as confident speakers of te reo Māori.

Whānau are valued as key contributors to children’s development and wellbeing. Their shared commitment and active involvement has strongly influenced whānau learning at home, where te reo Māori is a strong part of their everyday lives. For most children te reo Māori is their first language. Kaimahi and the wider whānau collectively bring an extensive range of matauranga Māori, skills, qualifications and talents to benefit children’s growth and development. Kaumātua are involved at all levels, and are very pleased with the success of the kōhanga reo, kura and marae whānau in developing te reo Māori competence of their mokopuna.

The kōhanga reo is effectively managed and monitored in the interests of mokopuna and whānau.Te Korowaiand compliance obligations are upheld and monitored alongside their Te Aho Matua goals. ERO supports the kōhanga reo whānau priority to further examine ways they can extend and enhance existing learning environments that enrich whānau and children’s Te Ao Māori learning experiences.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to student achievement and useful to this kōhanga reo.

If you would like a copy of the full report, please contact the kōhanga reo or see the ERO web page, http://www.ero.govt.nz.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
