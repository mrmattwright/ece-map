1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Whāngai i te reo kia puāwai te wairua

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Puawairua i Puke Waetaratara, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau ki te Hauāuru. Neke atu i te rua tekau tau te kōhanga reo e whakahaerehia ana. Nā ētahi o ngā mahi ki te whakahou ake i ngā whare, i whakarato i ētahi atu wāhi akoranga, wāhi tākaro hoki, me tētahi wharenui mō ngā karakia, ngā mihimihi, ngā hui ā-whānau, me ngā kaupapa motuhake.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Whenua te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E ai ki a rātou, ka hāngai te Mana Whenua ki te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō, ō rātou tūrangawaewae, me tō rātou mātau, tō rātou tū pakari hoki ki te ao Māori.

Nā tēnei, ka whakawhanakehia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau te pātai aromātai e whai ake nei, hei ārahi i te whai pānga o arotake ki te Mana Whenua:

He pēhea te hāpai o ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te tamaiti kia mōhio ai ia ki a ia anō?

Ka whakaratohia ki ngā tamariki ngā wheako akoranga o ia rā e whakapūmau ana i te whai wāhitanga, te whakahirahiratanga hoki o tō rātou tuakiri, ā rātou taonga tuku iho, me ngā tikanga. Nā tēnei, ka riro i ngā tamariki te tino aronga toi whenuatanga me te tino hononga ki tō rātou whānau, ki te kōhanga reo hoki.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki ngā taiao hihiri i āta whakawhanakehia ai, ā, e mau ana i te maha o ngā rauemi. Kua whakawhanake anōtia te katoa o ngā whare me ngā rawa, ā, kua āta whakarite anōtia hei whakapiki ake i ngā whai wāhitanga ako me ngā wheako mā ngā tamariki.

He whakaute, he manaaki, he tautoko hoki i roto i ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā tamariki me te whānau. E tino poipoia ana ngā tamariki kia whanake ai hei ākonga māia, tākare hoki. Ka kitea te tupu o te mōhiotanga, te māramatanga, me te manawa whakahī o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou ao Māori.

E whai hua tonu ana te whānau ki te whakahaere i tō rātou kōhanga reo, ā, he tino aronga pūmau ki te ū tonu ki ā rātou whāinga rautaki me ō rātou wawata mō ā rātou tamariki. Ko tā rātou tirohanga, ō rātou wawata, ā rātou whāinga hoki kei te pūtake o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, te whakamahere rautaki, te arotake whaiaro, me te whakatau whakaritenga. Ko te tino whakatinanatanga pai o Te Whāriki me te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, e whakarato tonu ana ki te whakarato i ngā putanga ako e whai hua ana ki ngā tamariki me te whānau.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mokohuia o te Rautahi kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

Mana Whenua

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki a te whānau me te arotake whaiaro
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Whenua

He whakamārama

He pēhea te hāpai o ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te tamaiti kia mōhio ai ia ki a ia anō?

E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi me te whānau, kia mōhio ai ia tamaiti ki a ia anō.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki ngā taiao hihiri i āta whakawhanakehia ai, e mau ana hoki i te maha o ngā rauemi, ā, e hāngai ana ki te ao Māori. Ko te katoa o te whare me ngā rawa i whakawhanake anōtia, ā, i āta whakaritea anō hoki, hei whakapiki ake i ngā whai wāhitanga ako me ngā wheako a ngā tamariki. Ko te taiao o ngā pēpi e whakawātea ana i a rātou ki te āta ngaoki haere, te hīkoi haere hoki, me te tākaro ki te whānuitanga o ngā rauemi. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā pēpi ki tō rātou taiao.

Ka whai hua te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei āta whakarite i te poipoi o ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao akoranga e aroha atu ana, e manaaki ana, ā, e aro nui ana hoki ki a rātou E whai hua ana te whakawhanake haere o ngā tamariki i:

  • te mōhiotanga me te āheinga i roto i te reo me ōna tikanga Māori
  • ō rātou hononga hei tangata whenua, me tō rātou whai wāhi nui atu ki tō rātou whānau
  • ngā taunekeneke manaaki, whakaute hoki
  • te pakari ki te tūhura haere me te mārama ki tā rātou whai wāhi ki te ao e noho nei rātou, ki ngā mahi katoa hoki e whakatutukihia ana e rātou
  • te āheinga ki te whakatau i ā rātou ake whakaritenga mō ā rātou akoranga.

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i tō rātou tū pakari, tō rātou mōhiotanga hoki ki a rātou anō hei Māori.

Ka poipoia, ka tino hāpaitia hoki te tuakiri Māori o ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi me te whānau. Nā tēnei, he kaha te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki, me tā rātou hononga ki tō rātou whānau, ki tō rātou kōhanga reo hoki. Ka hāpai ngā pepeha, ngā tikanga, me ngā waiata i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki i tō rātou māramatanga ki tō rātou whai wāhi nui ki tō rātou whānau, ā, hei tangata whenua hoki. E whakanui ana aua taumahi, aua mahinga anō hoki i ngā tikanga tuku iho, ngā kawa, me ngā whakapapa.

Ka whakaratohia ki ngā tamariki ngā wheako akoranga o ia rā e whakapūmau ana i te whai wāhi nui, te whakahirahiratanga hoki o te Māori me tōna tuakiri, āna taonga tuku iho, me ōna tikanga.

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e rangona ana i te aroha me te manaaki. Ka kitea te mauritau, te tautoko, te whakaute, te manaaki hoki o ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā mema o te whānau me ngā kaimahi. Ka whakaatu ngā tamariki i aua whanonga me aua uaratanga ki waenga i a rātou anō. Kei te kounga kairangi ngā taunekeneke, ā, ka whai hua ki te whakatairanga i ngā akoranga a ia tamaiti, me tōna whakawhanaketanga. Ka ako ngā tamariki, he tino taonga ō rātou hononga, ā rātou taunekeneke, te whānaungatanga hoki ki waenga i a rātou ko te whānau, me te whai pānga nui o tēnei ki ā rātou taonga tuku iho, tō rātou tuakiri hoki.

E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki ki te ako me te whakamahi i te reo Māori. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi me te whānau i tētahi taiao e whakatairanga ana, e akiaki ana hoki i te reo Māori. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo Māori o te kounga pai e whakamahia ana Ka whakaongaonga te ako reo i ngā tamariki, ā, he pārekareka hoki. He ngahau ngā pakiwaitara, ngā waiata, me ngā wā e ako ana rātou i ngā kaupapa. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki tēina, nā ngā taumahi e hāngai ana ki te manawataki, te tāruarua, me ngā mahi ā-ringa. He pai te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai i ngā kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngāwāhanga hei whakawhanake

E tino whakahaerehia ana te kōhanga reo e te whānau, ā, e tino noho pūmau ana rātou hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki. Kua āta tāutuhia, kua tino māramahia, kua tino whakatinanahia hoki ngā pūnaha whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. Nā tēnei, ka whai hua ngā pūrongo, te aroturuki me te whakahaeretanga puta noa i ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. E mōhio ana te whānau ki ngā whakahaeretanga me te whānuitanga anō hoki o ngā whakawhanaketanga. Ka mahi ngātahi rātou ki te whakapūmau i te whanaungatanga, ā, ka arotake i te aronga o te kōhanga reo. He tino aronga ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

He mahere rautaki tā te whānau, ā, e tohu ana tēnei i ngā kaupapa matua e whai pānga ana ki te tupu tonu o te kōhanga reo me ngā tamariki ā meāke nei. Ka whakaatuhia aua kaupapa matua i te kōhanga reo, hei whakamaumahara i te whānau i a rātou e ahu whakamua ana ki te whakatutuki i ā rātou whāinga ngātahi.

Kua āta whakaritea te arotake whaiaro. He pai te whakapūmau i ngā tuhinga, puta noa i ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. Ka whakamahia aua mōhiohio hei aroturuki i ngā whakaritenga, hei pūrongo hoki ki te whānau. Ka whai wā i ngā hui ā-whānau ki te tāutu i ngā kaupapa matua me te whakamahere i ngā whakawhanaketanga ka whai ake. Ko te whānuitanga o te waiora me ngā matea o ngā tamariki me te whānau, kei te pūtake o ngā whakataunga.

Ko te ako me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā mahi whakamahere hōtaka. Ka āta whakatakoto haere i te katoa o ngā mahere, ā, ka āta whakaarohia hoki. He matawhānui ngā hōtaka i āta whakamaheretia ai, ā, ka tino whakatairanga nui i te ako. Ka whakatutuki ngā mahere i te katoa o ngā reanga, ngā āheinga, me ngā ngākau nuitanga. a rumakina ngā tamariki ki ngā horopaki tūturu o te ao Māori.

Ka whai hua te whakamahinga a ngā kaimahi i te aromātai hōtaka hei aroturuki i te whai huatanga o ngā hōtaka ako hei hāpai i ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Ko te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki, me te ako i te ao Māori, e whai pūtake ana, e whakaongaonga ana, i āta whakamaheretia ai, ā, ka āta aroturukitia hoki.

Ka whai hua, ka whai kiko anō hoki ngā mahi aromatawai. Ko te tupu me te angitu o ngā tamariki i roto i te reo me ōna tikanga, me ngā wāhanga katoa hoki o te hōtaka, ka kapohia ake i ia te wā e ngā kaimahi. E wātea ana aua mōhiohio i āta whakaemihia ai, hei tirohanga mā te whānau. Ka aroturuki ngā kaimahi i te tupu me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki i roto i ngā horopaki o te ao Māori.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakiia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o okiuh, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia:

  • whakarato tonu te whānau i tētahi taiao akoranga tautoko kia ākona paitia ngā tamariki.

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Whāngai i te reo kia puāwai te wairua

Te Kōhanga Reo o Puawairua is located in Puke Waetaratara(Massey), West Auckland. The kōhanga reo has been operating for over 20 years. Recent renovations and upgrades to buildings provide additional learning and play areas, and a wharenui or meeting space for karakia, mihimihi, whānau hui and other special occasions.

Whānau chose mana whenua as the focus for this review. They assert that mana whenua is about children knowing who they are, where they are from and being competent and confident in te ao Māori.

Consequently ERO and the whānau developed the following evaluation question as a guide for the review based on mana whenua,

How well do kaimahi and whānau support children to know who they are?

Children are provided with daily learning experiences that reinforce the place and importance of their identity, heritage and customs. As a result children acquire a strong sense of belonging and connectedness to their whānau and kōhanga reo.

Children are immersed in vibrant, well developed and resourced environments. The entire building and property has been redeveloped and successfully organised to increase learning opportunities and experiences for children.

Interactions and relationships among children and whānau are respectful, caring and supportive. Children are successfully nurtured to develop as confident, enthusiastic learners. Children show a growing awareness, understanding and pride in their world as Māori.

The whānau continue to successfully manage their kōhanga reo with a sustained focus on upholding their strategic objectives and the aspirations they have for children. Their vision, aspirations and objectives are at the forefront of whānau management, strategic planning, self review and decision making. The effective implementation ofTe Whāriki o Te Kōhanga Reoand kōhanga reo management continues to provide successful learning outcomes for children and whānau.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children.

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Whenua.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Mana Whenua

Background

How well do kaimahi and whānau support children to know who they are?

Children are well supported by kaimahi and whānau to know who they are.

Areas of good performance

Children are immersed in vibrant, well developed and resourced environments which reflect te ao Māori. The entire building and property has been redeveloped and successfully organised to increase learning opportunities and experiences for children. The babies’ environment allows them to safely crawl or walk and play with a range of resources. The babies are happy and settled in their environment.

The whānau and kaimahi effectively manage the kōhanga reo to ensure children are nurtured in a loving, caring and highly responsive learning environment. Children are successfully developing:

  • knowledge and capability with te reo me ōna tīkanga Māori
  • their special connection as tangata whenua, and their place within their whānau
  • caring and respectful interactions
  • confidence to explore and understand their place in the world around them, and in everything that they do
  • the ability to make choices about their own learning.

Children are developing confidence and knowledge of who they are as Māori.

Children’s identity as Māori is nurtured and well supported by kaimahi and whānau. As a result children have a strong sense of belonging and connectedness to their whānau and kōhanga reo. Pepeha, tīkanga practices and waiata assist children to develop their understanding of their special place within their whānau and as tangata whenua. These activities and routines celebrate traditional practices, protocols and whakapapa.

Children are provided with daily learning experiences that reinforce the place and importance of Māori and their identity, heritage and customs.

Children learn in a loving and caring environment. There are positive, supportive, respectful and caring relationships between whānau members and kaimahi. Children display these behaviours and values with each other. Interactions are of high quality and successfully enhance each child’s learning and development. Children learn that their connections, interactions and relationships with whānau are precious, and an important part of their heritage and identity.

Children are well supported to learn and to use te reo Māori. Kaimahi and whānau provide an environment where te reo Māori is promoted and encouraged. Children hear good quality te reo Māori being used. Language learning for children is stimulating and enjoyable. Storytelling, waiata and kaupapa learning sessions are fun. The younger children benefit from activities that include rhythm, repetition and actions. Children are developing well as speakers of te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

The kōhanga reo is successfully managed by whānau who are strongly committed to their children. Kōhanga reo management systems are well defined, understood and implemented. This allows for effective reporting, monitoring and management across all areas of the kōhanga. Whānau are aware of the operations and overall developments. They work together to reinforce whānaungatanga and review the direction of the kōhanga reo. There is a strong focus on managing the kōhanga reo in the best interests of children.

The whānau have in place a strategic plan with key priorities that are relevant to the future growth of the kōhanga reo and their children. These priorities are displayed at the kōhanga reo as a reminder to whānau as they work towards meeting their shared goals.

Self review is well established. Records across all areas of the kōhanga reo are maintained well. This information is used to monitor practices, and report to whānau. Whānau hui are used to identify priorities and plan developments. The overall wellbeing and needs of children and whānau are central to decision making.

Children’s learning and development is at the heart of programme planning. Planning is thorough and well considered. The comprehensive, well planned programmes strongly promote learning. Plans cater for all ages, abilities and interests. Children are immersed in local and authentic te ao Māori contexts.

Programme evaluation is used effectively by kaimahi to monitor how well programmes of learning support children’s interests. Children’s development and learning about te ao Māori is meaningful, stimulating, well planned and monitored.

Assessment practices are highly effective and informative. Children’s growth and success in te reo me ōna tīkanga, and all other areas of the programme are captured on a regular basis by kaimahi. This well collated information is available for whānau to view. Kaimahi monitor children’s growth and development in te ao Māori contexts.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends:

  • that the whānau continue to provide a supportive and positive learning environment for children.

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
