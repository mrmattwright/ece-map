Te Aromātai

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Tārewa Pounamu ki Rotorua. Nō Te Arawa te nuinga o ngā tamariki ki tēnei kōhanga reo. Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i ngā tino hononga e whai pūtake ana, ki te taha o te whānau me ētahi atu i te hapori whānui.

He mārama ngā wawata o te whānau mō ā rātou tamariki, ā, e noho ngākau nui ana ki te hāpai i ā rātou akoranga. Ko ngā akoranga, ngā taunekeneke, me te atawhai o ngā tamariki e pou herea ana e te whanaungatanga. Ka tūmāia ngā tamariki, e mōhio ana rātou ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae hoki.

Ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā whakataunga me ngā mahi whakamahere a te whānau o te kōhanga reo. Ka arotahi te whānau ki te pūmautanga o te tupu me te whakapai tonutanga. Kua whakaritea te arotake whaiaro, ā, e whakamahia ana hei aroturuki i te āhua o ngā whakahaeretanga whānui.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

Mana Tangata

He pēhea rawa te whakaatu a ngā tamariki i tō rātou māramatanga ki te whanaungatanga?

He harikoa, he tau, he māia hoki ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao ako ā-whānau.

Ka rongo ngā tamariki i ngā hononga whakaute e whai pūtake ana. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā uaratanga o te aroha, te awhi, te tautoko me te whanaungatanga. He pakari te taunekeneke a ngā tamariki ki te taha o ō rātou hoa me ngā pākeke.

Ka whakanuia te whai wāhi mai o ngā tamariki ki te whānau o te kōhanga reo, ā, ka whakamanahia, ka whakapūmautia hoki tō rātou tuakiri hei uri o Te Arawa. He nui ngā wawata o te whānau kia tū manawa whakahī ā rātou tamariki nā runga i te mōhio ki a rātou anō me tō rātou tūrangawaewae.

Ka whakanuia te reo Māori mā ngā huarahi papai e whai pūtake ana. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki e whai hua ana ki te kōkiri i te mōhiotanga me te āheinga o ngā tamariki i roto i te reo Māori. Ka rongo, ka whakamahi hoki rātou i te reo Māori mā ngā horopaki ōkawa me ngā horopaki ōpaki. Ka ākina ngā tamariki ki te tū ki te ārahi i te whānau i roto i ngā karakia me ngā waiata. Ka tū takitahi ngā tamariki tuākana ki te taki i ō rātou ake pepeha. Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi kaupapa ako e whakawhānui ana, e whakatairanga ana hoki i te whakawhanaketanga o tō rātou reo. Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki te piki haere o tō rātou mōhiotanga ki te reo Māori hei reo whakawhitiwhiti kōrero.

He maha ngā whai wāhitanga a ngā tamariki ki te whakatewhatewha i ngā taiao o roto, o waho hoki ki te kōhanga reo. Ka ākina rātou ki te tūhura i ō rātou whakaaro me ā rātou tirohanga mō te ao māoriori me te ao tūturu. Ko tētahi āhuatanga nui o te tūhura ki waho, ko te māra kai, ā, ka hāpai tēnei wāhi i ngā akoranga e pā ana ki te kaitiakitanga me te manaakitanga. Ka tautokona ngā tamariki kia hīraurau hopanga, kia whakaaro pohewa hoki. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki ngā taumahi ka whakapakari ake i tō rātou tūmāia, ā, ka whakawero anō hoki i ō rātou whakaaro. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te kōkiri i tā rātou ake tūhura haere. Ka kitea te tūmāia, te tākare hoki o ngā tamariki hei ākonga.

E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki kia whanake ai te māia, te harikoa, me te mātātoa hoki o tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki ako.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He pai te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. He tau ngā pūnaha e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o ngā kaimahi, ngā pūtea, me ngā pūrongo, puta noa i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga. Ka tino aro nui ngā hui ā-whānau ki te waiora me te atawhai o ngā tamariki. He pai te noho mōhio o te whānau ki ngā whakahaeretanga me ngā whakataunga. Kua āta whakawhanakehia tētahi mahere rautaki, ā, ka whakatakoto tēnei i ngā kaupapa mahi e arotahi ana ki ngā whakapaitanga me te tupu tonutanga.

Ko ngā mahere ako mā ngā tamariki ka whakahāngaitia ki Te Whāriki. Kua waihangatia ngā taumahi a ngā tamariki mō ngā tūmomo reanga me ngā tūmomo āheinga. Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i ngā hōtaka akoranga e whai pūtake ana.

Ka āta tuhia te ako a ngā tamariki me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ko ā rātou wheako me ā rātou wā ako motuhake, ka kapohia atu ki ngā pūkete e whakatinanahia ana e ngā kaimahi. He pai ngā mōhiohio e tohu ana i te āhua o te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki. E wātea ana aua pūkete ki ngā mātua, ā, e āhei ana rātou ki te aroturuki i te whanaketanga o ā rātou tamariki. E ai ki ngā kaimahi, e whakaaro tonu ana rātou i ngā wā katoa ki ngā tūmomo huarahi ka whakapai ake i ā rātou mahi aromatawai.

Kua āta whakaritea te arotake whaiaro. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti kōrero, ki te aroturuki hoki i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka me te kounga o te atawhai e whakaratohia ana e rātou mō ā rātou tamariki. Ka whakamahia aua mōhiohio hei hāpai i te waihanga, te whakapuakitanga, me ngā whakaritenga o te hōtaka. Ka whai hua ngā mahi a te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Ko te whakamahere, te pūrongo me te aroturuki i te tau tētahi āhuatanga hei whakawhanake ake. E mōhio ana te whānau, koia nei tētahi tino taumata anō ka whai hua ki te whakapūmau i tā rātou tirohanga ki te ahunga whakamua nahanaha me ngā whakapaitanga mō ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

whakamahia ngā mōhiohio o te arotake whaiaro hei hāpai i te whakamahere ā-tau me te pūrongo ā-tau i ngā kaupapa rautaki matua a te whānau.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Tārewa Pounamu ki Rotorua. E tino tautokona ana te whakawhanaketanga o te reo me ngā tikanga Māori o ngā tamariki. Ka ako ngā tamariki, ka whai pānga matua te whanaungatanga ki tō rātou tuakiri me tō rātou waiora. Ka aro nui te whakahaeretanga a te whānau ki ngā painga mō ngā tamariki me te tupu tonu o te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Tārewa Pounamu i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tārewa Pounamu is in Rotorua. Most children who attend the kōhanga reo affiliate to Te Arawa. Children experience strong and meaningful connections with whānau and others in the wider community.

Whānau have clear aspirations for their children and are committed to supporting their learning. Children’s learning, interactions and care are underpinned by whanaungatanga. Children are confident, know who they are and where they belong.

The kōhanga reo whānau have children at the centre of decision making and planning. They are focused on sustained growth and continual improvement. Internal review is established and used to monitor the overall performance of operations.

2 The Review Findings

Mana Tangata

How well do children show they understand whanaungatanga?

Children are happy, settled and confident in their whānau learning environment.

Children experience meaningful and respectful relationships. Kaimahi and whānau model the values of aroha, awhi, tautoko and whanaungatanga. Children interact confidently with peers and adults.

Children are valued members of the kōhanga reo whānau and their identity as Te Arawa descendants is acknowledged and affirmed. Whānau have high aspirations that their children will be proud of who they are and where they come from.

Te reo Māori is promoted in positive and meaningful ways. Kaimahi use effective strategies to advance children’s knowledge and capability in te reo Māori. They hear and use te reo Māori in formal and informal contexts. Children are encouraged to take on lead roles for the whānau, including karakia and waiata. Older children recite their pepehā without assistance. Children receive a range of kaupapa learning activities that extend and enrich their language development. Children show increasing awareness of te reo Māori as the language of communication.

Children have many opportunities to investigate the indoor and outdoor environments at the kōhanga reo. They are encouraged to explore their ideas and views of the natural and physical worlds. Maara kai is a prominent feature of outdoor exploration to support learning about kaitiakitanga and manaakitanga. Children are supported to problem solve and use their imagination. They participate in activities that build their confidence and challenge their thinking. Children enjoy independent exploration. Children show confidence and enthusiasm as learners.

Children are well supported to develop as confident, happy and active participants in a range of learning contexts.

Planning and Evaluation

The kōhanga reo is managed well in the interests of children. Sound systems for managing personnel, finance and reporting across all areas of operations are in place. Whānau hui are strongly focussed on the wellbeing and care of children. Whānau are kept well informed of operations and decisions. A well-developed strategic plan sets out priorities that focus on improvement and continual growth.

Planning for children’s learning is aligned to Te Whāriki. Children’s activities are designed for the various ages and levels of ability. Children experience meaningful programmes of learning.

Children’s learning and development is well documented. Their experiences and special learning moments are captured in profiles that are maintained by kaimahi. There is good information to show how well children are progressing. Parents have access to these profiles and can monitor their child’s development. Kaimahi report that they are continually looking at ways to improve their assessment practices.

Self-review is well established. Kaimahi meet regularly to discuss and monitor programme effectiveness and the quality of care they provide for their children. This information is used to inform programme design, delivery and practice. Whānau manage the kōhanga reo in an efficient way.

Key Next Steps

Annual planning, reporting and monitoring is an area for development. Whānau recognise that this is a valuable next step to maintain their focus on strategic progress and improvements for children.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)

physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)

suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)

evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

use internal review information to support annual planning and reporting on whānau strategic priorities.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tārewa Pounamu is in Rotorua. Children are well supported in their development of te reo and tikanga Māori. Children learn that whanaungatanga is a critical part of their identity and their wellbeing. Whānau management is centred on success for children and continual growth of the kōhanga reo kaupapa.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Tārewa Pounamu will be in three years.
