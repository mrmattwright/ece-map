2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kei Wainuiomata Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ngahere o te Rangimarie. Kei te taha o te marae o Wainuiomata tēnei kōhanga reo. He tino wāhanga o te marae me te hapori o ngā kōhanga reo ngā tamariki.

I kōwhiri te kaiako me te whānau i Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira hei wāhi arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ka whakaata tēnei i te huarahi kotahi ka whakamārama ēnei ariā ki ngā tamariki.

Ka rongo ngā tamariki ki ngā whanaungatanga takatika, mahana, tautoko hoki. He whakaute, ka āwhina hoki ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngarautaki pūmau hei arahi me te whakahaere i ngā tamariki. He māia, ā, he tau ngā tamariki ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo.

He pai te tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te ako me te kōrero i te reo Māori. He mea whakaihiihi, harikoa hoki ki ngā tamariki te ako i te reo. Kei te whanake ngā tamariki hei kaikōrero i te reo Māori.

Ka waihanga, ka tuhura, ka whai hoki ngā tamariki i o rātou ngākaunui. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki ngā mahi whakatairanga i te reo matatini me ngā ariā pūtaiao. Ka haere ngā tamariki ki ngā haerenga hei hāpai i te ako.

Ka whakapau kaha ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakarato i ngā hōtaka akoranga whakaihiihi, whakaongaonga hoki ki ngā tamariki. Ka ako ngā tamariki ko wai rātou, ngā tino tāngata kei o rātou ao me o rātou hononga ki te kōhanga reo me te marae. He pai te tiaki i a rātou. He kaha te toiwhenua o ngā tamariki.

Ka whakautu ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea a tinana me ngā matea a whatumanawa ngā tamariki. Ka whakarato rātou i ngā kai hauora ki ngā tamariki. Ka akina ngā tamariki ki te whakatā nō reira ka tau rātou i mua i te hoki ki te kainga.

Ka whakarato te kaitiaki o te kaupapa, kua roa hoki ia hei kaimahi ki te kōhanga reo, i ngā tātaki kairangi me te tautoko i te whānau me ngā kaimahi o tēnei kōhanga reo. Ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā whakataunga kaupapa e pā ana ki te ahunga o nāianei me te ahunga a meāke nei o te kōhanga reo.

Ka whakahaere te whānau i te kōhanga reo i runga i te ngākaunui ki ngā tamariki. Ko ngā mema o te whānau ngā tino kaiwhakatau kaupapa i te kōhanga reo. Ka mahi, ka whakamahere rātou a rautaki. Ka arotake ngā wāhi katoa o ngā mahi o te kōhanga reo ki ngā hui a whānau ka tū i ia marama. Ka whakapau kaha rātou ki te āhei ka whakaata ngā mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga reo i ngā tumanako o Te Korowai. He pai te kounga o ngā pūrongo ka whiwhi ki ngā hui a whānau.

He pai te whakahaere i ngā take hauora me te haumaru, ngā pūtea, ngā kaimahi me te tiaki i te kōhanga reo. Ka whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo ki ngā mahi ngātahi me ngā mahi mārama.

Ngā Mahi ā Meake Nei

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kohanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Titiro Hāngai a te Arotake

I mua atu i te whakahaerenga o te arotake, i ïnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Tāwerawera ki te Kūao o Taupiri kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake mā te whai i ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ki a rātou.

Ï muri mai i ngā whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenganui i te rōpū arotake o Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau, ka whakaritea e rātou ngā tino kaupapa motuhake hei arotakehanga. I titiro ēnei whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero ki runga i ngā kōrero i te pupuritia e te kōhanga reo (e whai wāhia anangā kōrero arotake whaiaro),ā, me te whānuitanga o ngā take pūmanawa hei arotakehanga i runga i ngā putanga e whai hua ana ki ngā mokopuna oTe Kōhanga Reo o Tāwerawera ki te Kūao o Taupiri.

Ko te kaupapa i whakaritea e te whānau hei titirohanga mā tēnei arotake ko:

  • Mana Atua
  • Mana Tangata
  • Mana Whenua.

E titiro ana te katoa o ngā arotake a ERO i roto i ngā kōhanga, ki runga i te kounga o te mātauranga. E ai ki a ERO, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o:

  • ngā arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me ngā whakamāhere rautaki
  • ngā whakamāhere o ngā hōtaka akoranga, ngā aro matawai me ngā aro mātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

I kōwhiri te kaiako me te whānau i Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira hei wāhi arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ka whakaata tēnei i te huarahi kotahi ka whakamārama ēnei ariā ki ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te reo Māori. He pai te tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te ako me te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka noho rūmaki rātou ki tētahi taiao reo Māori. Ka rongo ngā tamariki ki te kounga o te reo Māori ka kōrerotia e ngā kaikōrero matatau ana ki te reo Māori. Ka kōrero rātou i te reo Māori ki ngā karakia, ngā waiata, ngā pepeha me ngā wā mihimihi.

Kei ngā kaimahi te whānui o ngā rautaki hopu reo tōtika hei āwhina i ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. He mea whakaihiihi, he mea harikoa te ako ki te reo. He pai hoki ki ngā tamariki te kōrero pūrākau, ngā waiata me te ako a kaupapa.

Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki whakaako reo tōtika. Tae atu ana ki te whakamihi, te whakatauira, te uiui, te tatari me te mahi a ringa. He pai ki ngā tamariki nohinohi, ā, ka rere, ka whai hua rātou i ngā mahi tāruarua kupu, tohutohu, mahi hoki hei whakaatu i te tino take o ngā mahi.

Ka rongo ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori e kōrerotia ana ki ngā horopaki pono. He pai kia rātou te haere ki te marae kei te taha o te kōhanga reo. Kei te puāwai ngā tamariki hei kaikōrero i te reo Māori.

Ngā whai wāhitanga akoranga e whakarato ana ki ngā tamariki. Kei te hōtaka akoranga o ia rā me ngā wheako akoranga he anga e tōtika ana, he taurite, ā,he tino whakamere mā ngā tamariki. Ka taea e ngā tamariki te whai wāhi ki ngā mahi takitahi me ngā mahi ko ngā pakeke kei te whakahaere. Ka waihanga, ka tuhura haere, ka whai hoki rātou i o rātou ngākaunui. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā mahi whakatairanga i te reo matatini me ngā ariā pūtaiao. Ka haereere ngā tamariki i ia wā hei wāhanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakaapau kaha ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakarato i te hōtaka akoranga whakaongaonga, whakaihiihi hoki ki ngā tamariki. Ka rongo ngā tamariki ki te angitū ki tētahi taiao e whakanui ana i te ao Māori me te ao whānui.

Ngā taunekeneke me ngā whanaungatanga.Ka rongo ngā tamariki ki ngā whanaungatanga takatika, mahana, tautoko hoki. He kaha ngā kaimahi ki te tatari, he aroha rātou tētahi ki tētahi me te mahi whakaute, āwhina hoki me ngā tamariki. Ka whakatauira ngā tuakana i ngā whanonga penei ki te mahi tahi rātou, ā, te mahi me ngā taina hoki. Ka toro atu ngā tuakana ki ngā taina ki te uru ki ngā mahi whānui me te āwhina ia rātou ki ngā mahi whai hua.

Ka tautoko ngā pakeke i ngā tamariki ki te whakaoti mahi me te whakahaere pai i ngā tākaro me ngā taunekeneke. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki pūmau hei arahi me te whakahaere i ngā tamariki. He māia, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo.

Te tuakiri me te toi whenua. Ka whakaatu ngā tamariki i tō rātou mōhio pai ki tō rātou ake tuakiri me tō rātou toi whenua ki te kōhanga reo me te marae. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā whakaahua, ngā pikitia, ngā whakapapa, me ngā whakaaturanga hei whakatairanga i te ako o ngā tamariki ki o rātou whānau, o rātou hapū me o rātou iwi me ngā hononga ki te kōhanga reo me te marae. Ka ako ngā tamariki ko wai rātou, ko wai hoki ngā tino tāngata kei o rātou ao. He pūtake ngā tamariki ki te marae me te hapori o te kōhanga reo.

Te oranga o ngā tamariki. He pai te tiaki i ngā tamariki puta noa i te rā. Ka whakautu ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea a tinana me ngā matea a whatumanawa o ngā tamariki. Ka whakarato rātou i ngā kai hauora ki ngā tamariki. Ka akina ngā tamariki ki te whakatā nō reira ka tau rātou i mua i te hoki ki te kainga. Ka whakamahi te karakia mō te whānui o ngā take tae atu ana ki te whakatau tamariki. He kaha te toiwhenua o ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

E aro mātai ana te Tari Arotake Matauranga i ngā wā katoa, i te kounga o ngā whakamahere me ngā aro mātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo ki te titiro ki te kaha o te whānau o te kōhanga ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere me te aro turuki i te katoa o ngā wāhanga whakahaere o te kōhanga reo mō a rātou tamariki me te whānau.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ngā whakahaere a te whānau.Ka whakahaere te whānau i te kōhanga reo i runga i te ngākaunui ki ngā tamariki. He pai te taetae atu o te whānau ki ngā hui a whānau ka tū i ia marama. Ko ngā mema o te whānau ngā tino kaiwhakatau kaupapa i tēnei kōhanga reo. Ka mahi, ka whakamahere rātou a rautaki.

Kua nui ake te wāriu o te kōhanga reo a pūtea nei i ngā whakahoutanga ake ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo.

Ka arotake ngā wāhi katoa o ngā mahi o te kōhanga reo ki ngā hui a whānau ka tū i ia marama. Ka whakapau kaha rātou ki te āhei ka whakaata ngā mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga reo i ngā tumanako o Te Korowai. Kei ngā kaimahi katoa ngā tohu e tika ana, ā, kua uru te nuinga ki ētahi whakangungu inā tata nei.

He pai te kounga o ngā pūrongo ka whiwhi ki ngā hui a whānau.Kei ngā pūrongo ngā kōrero e pā ana ki te hōtaka akoranga me te kounga o ngā wheako akoranga e whakarato ana ki ngā tamariki.

He pai te whakahaere i ngā take hauora me te haumaru, ngā pūtea, ngā kaimahi me te tiaki i te kōhanga reo. Ka whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo ki ngā mahi ngātahi me ngā mahi mārama.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te aro turuki i ngā tamariki e moe ana. Kua whakauru ngā kaimahi i ngā tukanga me ngā mahi hou mō te tuhi i ngā arowhai i ia tekau miniti i ngā tamariki e moe ana. He mea nui kia whakatinanatia ēnei mahi i ia rā me te artake me te aroturuki i ēnei i ia wā kia āhei ai te whakamahi i ēnei mahi e ai ki ngā tumanakohanga me te whakamahere i ēnei mahi.

Te whai wāhi o ngā kaimahi ki ngā akoranga me ngā whakawhanake ngaiotanga.Kāore i te pai te whai wāhi o ngā kaimahi ki ngā whakangungu. Me whakarato te whānau i ētahi atu whai wāhitanga mō ngā kaimahi ki te uru ki ngā whakangungu e tika ana, e whai hua ana kia rātou.

Te aromātai i te hōtaka. Kāore ngā kaimahi i te aromātai ōkawa i te hōtaka akoranga kia oti ko tēna, ko tēna o ngā kaupapa. He mea nui kia ako ngā kaimahi ki te whai whakaaro ki ngā āhua o te hōtaka akoranga i pai te haere, me ngā āhua ka taea te whakapai ake. Me whai ngā kaimahi i tētahi huarahi whakapai ake i ngā wā katoa.

5 Ngā Kupu Tūturu ā te Whānau Whakahaere mō ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua atu i te whakahaerenga o te arotake, i whakatutukitia e te kaipupuri o te raihana, te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ngahere o te Rangimarie tētahi

Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhangame tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhituhinga i ōati rātou i kaha rātou ki te whakatutuki i ngā hanganga ture pa ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaere ā te whānau i to rātou kōhanga
  • te hauora, te waiora me te oranga tinana
  • ngā whakahaere o ngā kaiako me nga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaere o te pūtea, ngā rawa me ngā taonga.

I te wā i whakahaeretia ai te arotake, i āta titirohia e te Tari Arotake Matauranga ēnei take e whai ake nei, notemea, e whai wāhi nui ana rātou i runga i ngā hua e puta ana ki ngā mokopuna:

  • te āhuru aronganui o ngā mokopuna (tāpiri atu ki ngā whakahaere o ngā whanonga o ngā mokopuna, te ārai i ngā mahi whakawetiweti me ngā mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru-ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ki te whakatau tamariki) ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, te tiaki i ngā tamariki; ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā ki ngā mokopuna, ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ngā kaupapa here me ngā kawa mo ngā haere a nga mokopuna i waho atu o to rātou kōhanga)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga o ngā kaiako me ngā kaimahi, ā,me ngā whakaritengae pā ana ki a rātou
  • me ngā whakaritenga mō ngā puta tere wawe atu ki waho, inā ka puta he ahi, he rū ranei.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Ngahere o te Rangimarie Te Kōhanga Reo is in Wainuiomata. This marae based kōhanga reo is next to Wainuiomata Marae. Children are a central part of the marae and kōhanga reo community.

The kaiako and whānau selected all of the Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus area for this review. This reflects the holistic way that these concepts are introduced to children.

Children experience positive, warm and supportive relationships. Staff members treat children in respectful and helpful ways. They use sound strategies to guide and manage children. Children are confident and comfortable in the kōhanga reo environment.

Children are well supported to learn and to use te reo Māori. Language learning is stimulating and enjoyable for children. Children are developing well as emergent speakers of te reo Māori.

Children create, explore and follow their interests. They participate in activities that promote numeracy, literacy and science concepts. Children often go on excursions to support learning.

Staff and whānau members make every effort to provide children with stimulating and exciting learning programmes. Children learn about each other, significant others in their lives and their connections to the kōhanga and marae. They are well cared for. Children have a strong sense of well being.

Staff members are responsive to children’s physical and emotional needs. They provide healthy and nutritional snacks for children. Rest and sleep is encouraged and children are well revitalised before going home.

The kaitiaki o te kaupapa, a long serving staff member, provides high quality leadership and support to the whānau and staff members of this kōhanga reo. Children are central to decisions made about the current and future direction of the kōhanga reo.

Whānau members manage the kōhanga reo in the interests of children. Whānau members make the key decisions at this kōhanga reo. They act and plan strategically. Regular review of all key aspects of kōhanga reo operations occurs at whānau meetings. Every effort is made to ensure that kōhanga reo management practices reflect the expectations of Te Korowai. There is good quality reporting at whānau meetings.

Health and safety, finance, personnel and maintenance issues are well managed. The kōhanga is operated in a collaborative, transparent way.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ngahere o te Rangimarie was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ngahere o te Rangimarie.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

4 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

The kaiako and whānau selected all of the taumata whakahirahira as the focus area for this review. This reflects the holistic way that these concepts are introduced to children.

Areas of good performance

Te reoMāori. Children are well supported to learn and to use te reo Māori. They are immersed in a te reo Māori environment. Children hear good quality te reo Māori being used by many competent speakers. They use te reo Māori during karakia, waiata, pepeha and mihi sessions.

Staff members have an effective range of language acquisition techniques to help children communicate in te reo Māori. Language learning is stimulating and enjoyable. Storytelling, waiata sessions and kaupapa learning is fun for children.

Staff members use effective language teaching strategies well. These include praise, modelling, questioning, pausing and the use of gestures. The younger children enjoy and benefit from activities that include rhythm, repetition of words and commands and actions to show meaning.

Children hear te reo Māori being used in authentic contexts. They enjoy visiting the marae next to the kōhanga reo. Children are developing well as emergent speakers of te reo Māori.

Learning opportunities provided for children.The daily learning programme of activities and learning experiences has suitable structure, balance and is highly interesting for children. Children are able to participate in independent and adult led activities. They create, explore and follow their interests. Children participate in activities that promote numeracy, literacy and science concepts. Children often go on excursions as part of the learning programme. Staff and whānau members make every effort to provide children with a stimulating and exciting learning programme. Children experience success in an environment that acknowledges the Māori world and the wider world.

Interactions and relationships.Children experience positive, warm and supportive relationships. Staff members are patient, are affectionate and they treat children in respectful and helpful ways. The older children model similar behaviours when they are with each other and with the younger children. The older children willingly include younger children in different activities and assist them in various meaningful ways.

Adults support children to complete tasks and to manage play and interactions well. Staff members use sound strategies to guide and manage children. Children are confident and comfortable in the kōhanga reo environment.

Identity and belonging.Children demonstrate good knowledge about their personal identity and a strong sense of belonging to the kōhanga reo and marae. Staff members use photographs, murals and whakapapa charts and displays to promote learning about children’s whānau, hapū, iwi links and connections to the kōhanga reo and marae. Children learn about each other and significant others in their lives. Children are a central part of the marae and kōhanga reo community.

Children’s well being.Children are well cared for during the day. Staff members are responsive to children’s physical and emotional needs. Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management.Whānau members manage the kōhanga reo in the interests of children. There is good attendance by whānau members at monthly meetings. Whānau members make the key decisions at this kōhanga reo. They act and plan strategically.

Recent property developments add value to the physical environment of the kōhanga reo.

Regular review of all key aspects of kōhanga reo operations occurs at whānau meetings. Every effort is made to ensure that kōhanga reo management practices reflect the expectations ofTe Korowai. All staff members hold suitable qualifications and many are or have recently been involved in training courses.

Reporting at whānau meetings is of good quality. Reports include information about the learning programme and the quality of the learning experiences that are provided for children.

Health and safety, finance, personnel and maintenance issues are well managed. The kōhanga reo is well managed in a collaborative, transparent way.

Areas for development

Monitoring sleeping children.Staff members have introduced new procedures and practices for recording ten minute checks of sleeping children. It is important that these practices are implemented daily and are regularly reviewed and monitored to ensure that they are occurring as expected and as planned.

Staff involvement in professional learning and development.Staff members are not adequately involved in training. The whānau should provide more opportunities for staff members to attend suitable and relevant training.

Programme evaluation.The staff do not formally evaluate the learning programme after each kaupapa has been completed. It is important that staff learn to reflect about aspects of the learning programme that have gone well and aspects where improvements can be made. The staff need to be on a journey of continual improvement.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
