1 Evaluation of Montessori Herne Bay

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Montessori Herne Bay provides education and care for up to 41 children from 12 months to school age. The centre layout comprises of two areas: Tui providing for younger children, and Kea for older children. Younger children transition to the older age group around three years of age. The centre occupies well maintained premises with good facilities for staff. Nutritious meals are prepared on site by the centre‚Äôs cook.

The centre operates within the policies and management framework of the BestStart Education and Care Centres organisation, which provides an overarching governance and management framework as well as personnel to support individual centres. BestStart managers visit regularly and provide professional advice for the centre manager and teachers.

The 2013 ERO report identified good practices that have been maintained. Areas for development focused on improvements to the outdoor area and strengthening assessment and programme planning.

Since ERO‚Äôs 2013 review, a centre manager has been appointed and there have been changes in staffing. The outdoor area has been redeveloped and attractively landscaped. Most teachers are qualified and registered and continue to be involved in professional development.

The centre‚Äôs strong Montessori method and Te WhƒÅriki, the early childhood curriculum, guide the programme and teaching practices. Respect and independence are core features of the centre philosophy. School readiness is promoted through everyday learning experiences and the 'Be School Ready' programme.

The Review Findings

Children have fun, and are innovative, engaged learners. They are self managing, caring and respectful of others. The centre is extensively resourced with Montessori and general early childhood resources. The large outdoor area has wide variety of learning areas that invite children to investigate and explore. Teachers encourage children's physical activity and opportunities for problem solving and creativity. They skilfully support the development of children's ability to lead and join in group activities, and to use literacy, mathematics, and science in meaningful ways.

Teachers understand and provide well for the special characteristics and interests of very young children. They provide nurturing individualised care with good learning opportunities. Infants and toddlers are confident, explore freely and are trusting of adults and older children. They play and learn at a relaxed pace in a calm, supportive environment.

Teachers plan and implement a rich, responsive curriculum with a strong Montessori approach. Planning is influenced by current theories and research and builds on children‚Äôs interests. Children‚Äôs assessment portfolios are highly valued records of learning and development. Parents are provided with good information about the curriculum and encouraged to contribute their ideas and aspirations for their child's learning.

Skilful and supportive teaching practices affirm and build on the strengths children bring. Teachers model the Montessori philosophy and interact with respect. They acknowledge children‚Äôs cultural backgrounds, are inclusive, and adapt the environment to meet children's needs. Teachers manage children‚Äôs transitions through the centre carefully and are responsive to individual children's and family needs. Teachers encourage environmental sustainability and healthy lifestyles. Bicultural practices and use of te reo MƒÅori are woven through aspects of the programme.

Teachers work collaboratively and benefit from the centre manager‚Äôs professional leadership. Their teaching capabilities are fostered through sound appraisal processes and ongoing professional development. They are enthusiastic, improvement focused and work in partnership with parents.

A strong focus on children and families with a clear shared vision underpins centre practices. Self review is developing, guiding decision making and contributing to centre improvement.

The centre manager is very well supported by BestStart personnel. BestStart has useful systems for monitoring centre operations. These include regular management meetings and an internal audit process (QEC) which is used to identify challenges and develop plans for improvement. In addition, the centre has a strategic plan that is linked to the BestStart vision and strategic plan, as well as to the centre's philosophy statement, an annual management plan and QEC action plans.

Key Next Steps

The centre manager and ERO agree that internal evaluation is an area to develop further, and that teachers could:

  • more consistently document how they reflect on and evaluate their teaching practices

  • deepen their bicultural knowledge and practice.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Montessori Herne Bay completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)

  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)

  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)

  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.
