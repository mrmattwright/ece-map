Background

Mountain View Preschool Limited is located in Rotorua, adjacent to Western Heights Primary School. It provides all-day education and care for children from birth to school age. The centre is licensed for up to 42 children, including 15 up to two years of age. At the time of this ERO review, 42 children were enrolled, 30 of whom are identified as being of MƒÅori descent. The centre, previously known as R.E.A.L Kids Preschool, was purchased by Provincial Childcare Holdings in November 2013. It is one of three centres in Rotorua, owned and operated by this private childcare company.

ERO‚Äôs previous review in March 2013 identified the need for many aspects of centre operations to be improved. These included professional leadership, staff appraisal, self review, resourcing, learning environments, assessment, planning, evaluation, and teaching practices. Since purchasing the centre, the new owners and local management r≈çpu have undertaken significant review of centre operations and practices. There have been considerable staff changes, including leadership positions. In addition there has been immediate upgrading of the environment for children under two years of age, and equipment and resources for all age groups. At the time of this review, the area for children over 2 was being significantly upgraded. There has been steady roll growth, with many new children and families joining the centre community.

The centre philosophy celebrates diversity through practices that are inclusive. It recognises the potential of all children, as well as the skills, knowledge and abilities they already have, and endeavours to protect the mana of each child.

The Review Findings

Centre owners and the local management r≈çpu have high expectations for developing effective education and care for children and families. Since November 2013, continual review has resulted in many positive changes for children. These include improved routines, nutrition, health and safety, and positive guidance. The management r≈çpu contributes a range of highly relevant and complementary knowledge and skills to the centre.

The centre philosophy is underpinned by the principles inherent in Te Tiriti o Waitangi. It has a strong focus on children‚Äôs holistic development and maintaining reciprocal partnerships with parents. Centre owners are committed to ensuring that barriers to participation and attendance are minimised for children and families.

Management and centre leaders have made solid progress in a very short time towards developing a positive and collaborative team culture. There is a strong focus on shared responsibility and valuing the contribution that staff and parents make with decisions that enhance outcomes for children. Teachers have benefited from the planned approach to developing expected practices. This has involved employing staff with recognised strengths, deliberate role modelling, mentoring, and providing constructive feedback to teachers about their practice. There is an effective appraisal process that informs planned professional learning and development for teachers.

The centre‚Äôs curriculum prioritises children‚Äôs development of social awareness, competence and confidence, and dispositions for life-long learning. The uniqueness of each person, their culture, background and ability is celebrated. There is a strong emphasis on providing a bicultural curriculum that fosters links and connections with local people and places of significance to MƒÅori. Recent upgrades have enhanced teaching and learning environments and equipment for children and staff.

Whanaungatanga and manaakitanga are evident through teachers‚Äô affirming and responsive interactions with children and families. Many teachers demonstrate confidence to integrate conversational te reo MƒÅori. This provides good modelling for children and encouragement for staff who are less confident users of the language.

Teachers implement effective strategies that empower children to lead their play and learning. Children enjoy teacher-led experiences designed to challenge thinking and extend interests. There is a focus on providing positive guidance that fosters children‚Äôs confidence and social skills. Teachers value parents as the first teachers of their children and respond flexibly to their ideas and wishes. Learning is becoming increasingly visible to parents and children through assessment profiles and planning displays.

Infants and toddlers are very well nurtured and cared for. Teachers value each child as a capable communicator and explorer. Interactions are respectful and responsive to the personal routines, interests and abilities of each child. Teachers foster tuakana/teina relationships that encourage communication and learning with older peers and whƒÅnau members. There is strong evidence of collaborative planning with parents to ensure positive outcomes for children up to 2 years of age. Teachers confidently model language in both te reo MƒÅori and English, that is well understood by children.

Key Next Steps

Centre management has identified, and ERO strongly agrees that the following next steps are important for the ongoing development of the service:

  • continuing to develop expected teaching practices and learning environments
  • sustaining community partnerships
  • providing opportunities for children to develop literacy, mathematics and investigation skills in the context of their play.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Mountain View Preschool Limited completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Next ERO Review

When is ERO likely to review the service again?

The next ERO review of Mountain View Preschool Limited will be in three years.
