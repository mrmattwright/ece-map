Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Mere Te Aroha ki te hapori o te marae o Kaiwhaiki ki te awa o Whanganui. Haereere ai ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo i Whanganui i ia rā. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki, ā, kei te mōhio ki ō rātou hononga ki tō rātou marae me tō rātou iwi. Kei te arotakengia te tūtohinga, ā, me whakapai ake i ētahi āhuatanga o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Mere Te Aroha i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

‘E rere kau mai te awa nui, mai i te kāhui maunga ki Tangaroa, ko au te awa, ko te awa ko au. Ko te awa e kīia ana, ko Whanganui.’

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Mere Te Aroha ki te hapori o te marae o Kaiwhaiki, ki te awa o Whanganui. Kua ahu mai te ingoa o te kōhanga reo i tētahi tipuna. He hononga ā-whakapapa tō te nuinga o te whānau ki te iwi o tēnei takiwā. Haereere ai ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo i Whanganui, i ia rā. E ai ki te whānau, he hiahia tō rātou kia ako, kia mōhio hoki ngā tamariki ki ngā taonga tuku iho me te reo o te awa.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou tūmāia, i tō rātou mōhiotanga hoki ki tō rātou taiao?

He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki, ā, kei te mōhio rātou ki ō rātou hononga ki te marae me te iwi.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao. Hīkoi haere ai ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki i ia te wā ki te marae, ā, i reira ngā tamariki ako ai i ngā kōrero tuku iho me ō rātou tīpuna. Kua waia ngā tamariki ki ngā mahinga i āta whakaritea ai, tae atu ki ngā karakia, ngā mihimihi, ngā wā mō te kai me te tākaro, me ngā akoranga ōkawa, ōpaki hoki.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i te reo Māori mā te whakamahinga o te reo ā-iwi o konei. Ka rongo, ka whakamahi hoki rātou i te reo Māori ki ngā horopaki ōkawa, ōpaki hoki. Ka whakatauira ngā kaumātua me ngā kaimahi i te reo e tika ana mō ngā tamariki, ā, ka akiaki i a rātou ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori puta noa i te rā. Ka mauminamina, ka aro nui hoki ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori.

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ngā tikanga Māori. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā tamariki ki te kawe i ngā karakia me ngā tikanga o ia rā. Kei te mārama ngā tamariki ki ngā tikanga e pā ana ki te whakatau me te manaaki manuhiri. Ka whakatinana hoki rātou i ngā tikanga e pā ana ki te akuaku me te whakarite kai. He tika te āhua o ngā tamariki ki ngā horopaki Māori.

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki te taiao māoriori, tūturu hoki. Ka manaaki rātou i te māra kai, ā, ka whakarite rātou i ngā kai i whakatupu ai rātou. Ka whai wā ngā tamariki puta noa i te rā ki te tūhura me whakatewhatewha i ngā taonga māoriori ki tō rātou taiao. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmomo mahi kori tinana. He ākonga tūmāia ngā tamariki.

He pai te taunekeneke o ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo tāngata. Ka whai wāhi mai ngā kaumātua me ngā kuia, ā, he pai ō rātou hononga ki ngā tamariki. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i ngā taunekeneke pai, ngā taunekeneke atawhai hoki ki waenga i a rātou anō me ngā pākeke. Ka whakawhanaunga, ka whakahoahoa hoki ngā tamariki ki ngā manuhiri.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He mārama te tirohanga ā te whānau mō ngā tamariki. Ka noho matua ki a rātou, ko te rumaki ki te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. Ka noho pūmau rātou ki te whakarato ki ngā tamariki te hōhonutanga o ngā māramatanga e pā ana ki ngā kōrero tuku iho me tō rātou hononga ki te awa me tō rātou iwi. Ka arotahi te hōtaka ki te whakarato i te reo Māori mā te noho ā-whānau. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hononga ki te awa me tō rātou iwi.

Ka mahi ngātahi te whānau ki te hāpai i ngā whakahaeretanga o ia rā ki te kōhanga reo. Kua whakatūngia e rātou tētahi komiti matua, ā, he mārama ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga hoki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore te hōtaka akoranga e whakarato ana i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi, e poipoi ana rānei i ngā tūmomo matea ako me ngā ngākau nuitanga. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, e tika ana kia nui ake ngā mahi ki te whakatutuki i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo matea o ngā tamariki, ā, i te whakaratonga anō hoki o tētahi hōtaka akoranga whakahihiri.

He whāiti te arotake o roto. E mōhio ana te whānau, me arotake i te tūtohinga, ā, kāhore tēnei e āta whakamahia ana hei ārahi i te whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga ki te kōhanga reo. Kāhore ētahi o ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e hāngai ana ki te rā, ā, kāhore e whai pānga ana ki te horopaki o te kōhanga reo. He ngoikore te kounga o ngā pūrongo ki ngā hui ā-whānau o ia marama, ā, kāhore e arotahi ana ki ngā kaupapa rautaki matua.

He iti noa te aronga ki te whakawhanaketanga o ngā kaimahi. Kāhore te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi kia whakatutukihia i roto i te roanga o te tau. Kāhore ngā kirimana mahi e hāngai ana ki te wā. He hākirikiri ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi a ngā kaimahi, ā, kāhore he kiko o roto e hāngai ana ki te āhua o ngā mahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. Hei whakapai ake i ngā whakaritenga o tēnei wā, me tahuri te whānau ki te:

3.1 whakarite i te whakatutukitanga o te arowhai a ngā pirihimana i ngā kaimahi katoa e mahi ana ki te taha o ngā tamariki
[Ture Mātauranga 1989]

3.2 whakatutuki, te tuhi haere hoki i ngā akoako hōneatanga ki te taha o ngā tamariki inā he rū whenua, he ahi rānei
[R 46 (1)(2) Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008, HS4-8 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

3.3 whakarite i te whakatutukitanga o tētahi pūnaha ka tāutu i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea, ā, me whakatutuki, me tuhi hoki i ngā arowhai i ia te wā
[R 46 (1)(2) Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008, HS12 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

3.4 whakapau kaha ki te āta hāpai i ngā kaimahi, me te whakarato i te whakawhanaketanga ngaio, te aroturuki hoki.
[R 47(1e) Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008, GMA5 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā:

  • kia whakarato Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i te ārahitanga me te tautoko e tika ana kia tutukihia ai e te whānau ngā whakaritenga matua katoa e whai ake ana, me ngā wāhanga hoki i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kāhore e tutuki ana i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki, ā, kei te mōhio ki ō rātou hononga ki tō rātou marae me tō rātou iwi. Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Mere Te Aroha is a part of the Kaiwhaiki Marae community on the Whanganui river. Children travel to the kōhanga reo from Whanganui each day. Children are happy and settled, and are aware of their connections to their marae and iwi. The charter is under review and some aspects of whānau management need to be improved.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Mere Te Aroha will be in three years.

1 Context

‘E rerekau mai te awa nui mai te kāhui maunga ki Tangaroa, Ko au te awa, ko te awa ko au. Ko te awa e kii ana, ko Whanganui.’

Te Kōhanga Reo o Mere Te Aroha is a part of the Kaiwhaiki Marae community on the Whanganui River. The kōhanga reo is named after an ancestor. Most whānau whakapapa to the local iwi. Children travel to the kōhanga reo from Whanganui each day. Whānau report that they want their children to learn and know the traditions and language of the awa.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children show they are confident and have knowledge of their local environment?

Children are happy and settled, and are aware of their connections to their marae and iwi.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are happy and settled in their environment. Kaimahi and children take regular walks to the marae where children learn about their history and ancestors. Children are familiar with the routines that are well established and include karakia, mihimihi, time for meals and play, formal and informal learning.

Children learn te reo Māori using the local dialect. They hear and use te reo Māori in formal and informal settings. Kaumātua and kaimahi model correct language for children and encourage them to use te reo Māori throughout the day. Children are receptive and respond to te reo Māori.

Children are aware of tikanga Māori. Kaimahi and whānau support children to lead karakia and other daily rituals. Children understand the protocols for welcoming and caring for visitors. They practice the tikanga for hygiene and food preparation. Children act appropriately in Māori settings.

Children are aware if the natural and physical environment. They care for the maara kai and prepare food they have grown for eating. Children have time throughout the day to explore and investigate the natural resources in their environment. Children enjoy exercise and physical activities. Children are confident learners.

Children interact well with a range of people. Kaumātua and kuia are present and have good relationships with children. Children experience positive and caring interactions with each other and adults. Children are warm and sociable with visitors.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau have a clear vision for children. Immersion in a te reo and tikanga Māori context is important to them. They are committed to providing children with a deep understanding of their history and connection to the awa and their iwi. The programme is focused on providing ‘Māori language in a whānau setting.’ Children know about their connections to the awa and their iwi.

Whānau work together to support the day to day operation of the kōhanga reo. They have elected an executive committee who have clear roles and responsibilities.

Key Next Steps

The learning programme does not provide a range of activities or cater for different learning needs and interests. Kaimahi acknowledge that more needs to be done to meet the diverse needs of children and provide a vibrant learning programme.

Internal review is limited. The whānau acknowledge the need to review the charter and it is not used well to guide kōhanga reo management and operation. Some policy and procedure are out of date and do not reflect the kōhanga reo context. The quality of reporting at monthly whānau hui is weak, and not focused on strategic priorities.

There has been little attention placed on kaimahi development. Kaimahi appraisals have not been carried out for over a year. Employment contracts are out of date. Kaimahi job descriptions are vague and lack detail regarding working with children.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

3.1 ensure that police vetting for all employees working with children is up to date
[Education Act 1989]

3.2 carry out and record regular earthquake and fire drills with children
[R46(1)(2) The Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS4-8 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

3.3 ensure a hazard identification system is maintained and regular checks are carried out and documented
[R46(1)(2) The Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS12 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

3.4 ensure all steps are taken to adequately support employees, and all necessary professional support and monitoring is provided.
[R47(1e) The Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA5 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • TKRNT provide the necessary guidance and support to whānau to address all key next steps, and areas of non-compliance in this ERO report.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Mere Te Aroha is a part of the Kaiwhaiki Marae community on the Whanganui river. Children travel to the kōhanga reo from Whanganui each day. Children are happy and settled, and are aware of their connections to their marae and iwi. The charter is under review and some aspects of whānau management need to be improved.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Mere Te Aroha will be in three years.
