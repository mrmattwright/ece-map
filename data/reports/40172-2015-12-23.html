Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Rereatukāhia ki waho atu i Katikati, ki Te Moana-a-Toitehuatahi. He pai te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. Ka noho pūmau ngā kaimahi ki te mahi ngātahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki. He tokoiti noa ngā mema o te whānau e tautoko mātātoa ana i ngā whakahaeretanga o ia rā. Me aro atu ki ngā mahi aromatawai mā ngā pēpi. He harikoa ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Rereatukāhia i roto ngā tau e rua.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Rereatukāhia ki Katikati. Nō nā tata tonu nei te tatauranga o ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi i piki ake ai. He pai te tūnga o te kōhanga reo ki te hapori whānui. He hononga ā-whakapapa tō ngā whānau katoa, ā, ka noho pā tata rātou ki tō rātou pā, ki te pā tonu rānei. He hiahia tō te whānau kia matatau ā rātou tamariki ki te reo Māori, me te tū pakari hei Māori. Ko te nuinga o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo i tēnei wā, kei raro i te rua o ngā tau.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea te mōhio o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori, he pēhea hoki tā rātou whakamahinga?

Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tō rātou māramatanga ki te reo Māori, ā, ka taea e rātou te whakautu mā te reo Māori.

Mana Reo

Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo Māori puta noa i te rā. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki, pērā i te akiaki, te tāruarua, me te waiata hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. Ka toro atu ngā kaimahi ki ngā whai wāhitanga katoa, hei rumaki i ngā tamariki ki te reo. He pai te aro atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā pātai me ngā tohutohu. Ka whakamātau ngā tamariki i te reo.

Ka mārama ngā tamariki katoa i te reo Māori, ā, e āhei ana te nuinga ki te whakawhiti kōrero ki te kōhanga reo mā te taumata o te reo e mōhiotia ana e rātou. E āhei ana rātou ki te kawe i tā rātou ake whakapuaki i ngā kōrero, ngā mihimihi, ngā pepehā, ngā mōteatea me ngā waiata. E āhei ana ētahi o ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i ngā tauira reo māmā, hei whakawhiti kōrero ki ētahi atu, ki ngā pākeke hoki. He pakari te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori e mōhiotia ana e rātou.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana i a rātou. Ko te whanaungatanga te pūtake o ngā taunekeneke katoa ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi. He ngāwari te reo o ngā kaimahi, ā, ka whai wāhi atu rātou ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero āhuareka ki te taha o ngā tamariki. He tau ngā tamariki tuākana ki te mahi mō ngā wā roa. He mātātoa ngā pēpi, ā, he nui te aro atu ki a rātou. Ka whai atu ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga ki te taha o ō rātou pākeke, ō rātou hoa hoki e whakatau ana i te wāhi ako. He harikoa ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore ngā tamariki tuākana e āta tautokona ana ki te whakawhanake, ki te ahu whakamua i te taumata o tō rātou reo Māori. Ka tukua aua tamariki ki te mahi takitahi i ngā pākeke e tiaki ana i ngā tamariki tēina. Kāhore anō ngā kaimahi kia kite i ngā matea reo ake o ngā tamariki tuākana, kāhore hoki e mōhio ana me pēhea te tāutu i aua āhuatanga.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

Ka noho ngākau nui ngā kaimahi ki te mahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahere ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā kaupapa ako whakahihiri mō te hōtaka, ā, ka tāutu i ngā taumahi me ngā rauemi ka ārahi i tēnā ka whāia e ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki i ia rā. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te hanganga, te riterite, te whakatakotoranga ōrite hoki o te hōtaka.

E ngākau nui ana te whānau ki te reo Māori, ā, he tino wawata ō rātou mō ā rātou tamariki. E hāngai ana te tūtohinga ki te rā, ā, he tino aratohu mārama o roto mō te whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakaritea hoki ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. Ka āta whakaraupapa ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi i ngā tūranga o ngā kaimahi, me ā rātou kawenga mahi. Kua whakatutukihia ngā mahi arowhai e ngā pirihimana. Ka tāutu ngā kirimana mahi o ia kaimahi i ngā tūmanako o te whānau me ngā whiwhinga o ngā kaimahi.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He whāiti ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau. Kāhore i te tino mārama te rōnakitanga o ngā whakapaitanga nō nā tata tonu nei i whakatutukihia ai, nā te mea kei te tīmata noa te whānau ki te whakapakari ake i tō rātou āheinga. He tokoiti noa ngā mema o te whānau e tautoko mātātoa ana i ngā whakahaeretanga o ia rā. Kāhore he mahere rautaki, kāhore hoki ētahi tino kaupapa matua mō meāke nei. Kāhore ngā tamariki e taetae mai ana ki te kōhanga reo i ia rā.

Kāhore anō kia oti te tukanga arohaehae a ngā kaimahi. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e whiwhi ana i te whakawhanaketanga ngaio e hāngai pū ana ki ngā mahi matua me ngā putanga e tika ana mā rātou. Kāhore ngā kaimahi hou e tino hāpaitia ana i a rātou i roto i ā rātou kawenga mahi.

Me aro atu ki ngā whakaritenga aromatawai e pā ana ki ngā pēpi. He nui rawa te aro atu ki tēnā e mahia ana e ngā pēpi, kāhore i te aro kē ki tēnā e ākona ana rātou. He mea nui kia hāngai pū, kia tika hoki ngā aromatawai mō tēnei reanga.

He whāiti te aromātai hōtaka. Kāhore he tukanga hei aromātai i te whānuitanga o te ako a ngā tamariki i a rātou e whai wāhi atu ana ki ngā taumahi ako. He uaua ki te mōhio ki te whai huatanga o ngā whakapaitanga i te hōtaka akoranga, hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Kāhore ngā tamariki e whakawhānuihia ana, kāhore rānei e whakawerohia ana.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

Hei whakapai ake i ngā whakaritenga, me tahuri te whānau ki te:

  • tāutu i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea puta noa i te kōhanga reo, o roto, o waho hoki, me te whai i te huarahi e tika ana ki te whakakore atu i aua āhuatanga 
    [R 46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; Paearu Raihana HS12] 
  • whakapau kaha ki te whakarato i te tautoko ngaio, ngā whai wāhitanga whakawhanaketanga ngaio, ngā rauemi hoki mā ngā kaimahi me rātou e whai wāhi atu ana ki te ratonga. 
    [R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; Paearu Raihana GM1-12] 

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • āta mahi ngātahi ki te taha o ngā kaimahi ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ki te whakapakari i te āheinga o te whānau ki te whakahaere me te ārahi i te kōhanga reo.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Rereatukāhia ki waho atu i Katikati, ki Te Moana-a-Toitehuatahi. He pai te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. Ka noho pūmau ngā kaimahi ki te mahi ngātahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki. He tokoiti noa ngā mema o te whānau e tautoko mātātoa ana i ngā whakahaeretanga o ia rā. Me aro atu ki ngā mahi aromatawai mā ngā pēpi. He harikoa ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Rereatukāhia is on the outskirts of Katikati in the Bay of Plenty. Children have a good understanding of te reo Māori.  Kaimahi are dedicated to working with the children. Only a few whānau members actively support the day to day operation. Assessment practices for babies requires attention. Children are happy at kōhanga reo.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Rereatukāhia will be within two years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Rereatukāhia is in Katikati. There has been a recent increase in the roll and number of staff. The kōhanga reo has a positive profile in the local community. All whānau are connected through whakapapa and they live in or nearby their pā (community). The whānau want their children to be fluent in te reo Māori and live confidently as Māori. Most of the children currently enrolled are under two years of age.

2 The Review Findings

How confident are children in their knowledge and use of te reo Māori?

Children show that they understand and are able to respond to te reo Māori.

Mana Reo

All children hear te reo Māori throughout the day. Kaimahi use strategies such as prompts, repetition and waiata to support children’s language development. Kaimahi use every opportunity to immerse children in language. Children respond positively to questions and instructions. All children experiment with language.

All children understand and most are able to communicate at their level of te reo Māori at kōhanga reo. They are able to recite karakia, mihimihi, pepehā, mōteatea and waiata without prompting. Some children can use simple language patterns to communicate with each other and adults. Children are comfortable using te reo Māori that is familiar to them.

Children learn te reo Māori in a nurturing environment. Whanaungatanga is the basis of all interactions between children and kaimahi. Kaimahi are quietly spoken and engage in pleasant conversations with children. The older children work quietly for sustained periods of time. The babies are active and require a lot of attention. Children experience learning where familiar adults and peers provide a stable and secure environment. Children are happy.

Key Next Steps

Older children are not well supported to develop and progress their levels of te reo Māori. These children are left to work on their own as adults care for the younger children. Kaimahi do not yet recognise or know how to identify the specific language needs of the older children.

Planning and Evaluation

Kaimahi are dedicated to working with the children. Kaimahi and whānau plan interesting kaupapa ako for the programme and identify the activities and resources to guide what kaimahi and children do each day. Children enjoy the structured, consistent and predictable programme.

Whānau are passionate about te reo Māori and have clear aspirations for their children. The charter is up to date and provides clear guidelines for the management and operations of the kōhanga reo. Health and safety policies and procedures are in place. Job descriptions clearly outline kaimahi roles and responsibilities. Police vetting has been carried out. Individual employment contracts identify whānau expectations and kaimahi entitlements.

Key Next Steps

Whānau management is limited. The sustainability of recent improvements is unclear as whānau are at the early stages of building their capability. Only a few whānau members actively support the day to day operation. There is no strategic plan or clear priorities for the future. Daily attendance of children is inconsistent.

The current kaimahi appraisal process is incomplete. Kaimahi do not receive professional development that is relevant to the key tasks and outcomes that is required of them. New staff have little support as they undertake their roles and responsibilities.

Assessment practices for babies require attention. There is a great deal of emphasis on what babies are doing as opposed to what they are learning. It is important that assessments are relevant and appropriate for this age group.

Programme evaluation is limited. There is no process in place to evaluate to what extent children learn when they participate in learning activities. It is difficult to know how improvements to the learning programme have benefited children.  Children are not extended or challenged.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

The following areas require attention:

  • identify hazards throughout the centre both inside and outdoors and then take practical steps to eliminate these
    [R46 (1)(2)ECS2008/HS12]
  • all reasonable steps are taken to provide staff employed or engaged in the service with adequate professional support, professional development opportunities, and resources.
    (R47ECS2008/GM1-12)

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • work closely with district kōhanga reo staff to build whānau capability to manage and lead the kōhanga reo.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Rereatukāhia is on the outskirts of Katikati in the Bay of Plenty. Children have a good understanding of te reo Māori.  Kaimahi are dedicated to working with the children. Only a few whānau members actively support the day to day operation. Assessment practices for babies requires attention. Children are happy at kōhanga reo.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Rereatukāhia will be within two years.
