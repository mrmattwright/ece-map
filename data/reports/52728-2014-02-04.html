1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā tētahi ō ngā Kaiwhakahaere Matua ā-Rohe o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei, kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

I whakaritea tēnei pūrongo arotake tāpiri i runga o ngā tikanga whakahaere paerewa kua whakamanatia e te Āpiha Kaiarotake Matua.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga O Te Kōhanga Reo o Kaiwhaiki, e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia e te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i te Mahuru 2011 me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

  • Mana Atua – ngā whai wāhitanga ako mō ngā tamariki, te whakapakari i te āheinga o te whānau ki te kōkiri tonu i te whakahaeretanga
  • te whakamahere me te aromātai – te whakapakari i te whakahaeretanga o te whānau, te arotake whaiaro a te whānau, te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromātai me te aromatawai
  • ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture – te whakatakoto haere me te pūrongo i ngā whara, ngā māuiui me ngā rongoā i whāngaihia ai, me te ū tonu ki ngā akoako i ia te wā inā he ahi, he rū whenua rānei
  • ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai whai muri i te arotake o mua.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

‘Ko au te awa, ko te awa ko au’ (te tūtohinga a te whānau)

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Kaiwhaiki ki te marae o Kaiwhaiki, ki te taha o te awa o Whanganui. Ko Ngā Paerangi te hapū, ko Te Āti Haunui-a-Pāpārangi te iwi. Ko te awa o Whanganui me ngā whakapono, ngā kōrero tuku iho hoki o te papakāinga o Kaiwhaiki ka whai wāhi matua ki ngā wheako akoranga ka whakaratohia e ngā kaimahi me te whānau.

Ka aromātai te arotake tāpiri a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te ahunga whakamua a te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia ai ki te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te Mahuru o te tau 2011. Ko te tikanga i taua wā, kia whakapai te whānau me ngā kaimahi i te hōtaka akoranga, hei āta hāpai i ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga, ō rātou whakawhanaketanga anō hoki. Ko te whai wāhi mātātoa anō hoki o te whānau ka noho pūmau ki te whakapai ake i ngā wāhanga o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

I nāianei, ka āta whakamaheretia, ka āta whakahaerehia hoki ngā hōtaka e whakaratohia ana mō ngā tamariki, hei āta hāpai tōtika i ā rātou akoranga, me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga, ō rātou ngākau nuitanga hoki. Mai i te arotake o mua, kua whakapā atu ngā kaimahi ki ngā whakangungu a Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i roto i Te Whāriki, ā, kua whakapakari anō rātou i tā rātou toro atu ki te purapura. I ngā wā katoa ka whakaaro huritao rātou ki te whai hua o te waihanga me te whakapuaki o ngā hōtaka, me te whai pānga o ēnei ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ko te whānau o te kōhanga reo – tae atu ki te whānau o te marae o Kaiwhaiki – ka whai wāhi mātātoa, ka hāpai mārika anō hoki i ngā whakahaeretanga o ia wiki.

Ka tautokona anō hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo nā te whakapaitanga ake o te whakahaeretanga o te whānau. I nāianei, he pakari ake te aronga ngātahi ki te tupu, te rōnakitanga, te arotake, me te whakapai tonutanga o te kōhanga reo. Ahakoa ka arotake te whānau i ia te wā i ā rātou whakaritenga, me āta aroturuki anō hoki i te whakatakotoranga me te pūrongo o ngā pūnaha. I nāianei, e whakahaere ana, e aroturuki ana hoki te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo, i runga anō i ngā whāinga, ngā wawata hoki ki tā rātou tūtohinga.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Atua

He whakamārama

I tāutuhia te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te Mahuru o 2011 ngā wāhanga matua e rua hei whakapai ake. Kāhore i te rawaka te tautoko e whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga, ō rātou whakawhanaketanga hoki, tae atu ki te reo Māori. He iti noa te whai wāhi a te whānau ki ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, ā, i whai pānga anō hoki ki ngā take e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga. I te wā o tēnei arotake, i aromātaihia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te ahunga whakamua a te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā whakapaitanga mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Ngā whai wāhitanga ako o ngā tamariki. Ko te whānuitanga o ngā matea ako, ngā ngākau nuitanga hoki o ngā tamariki katoa e tino hāpaitia ana i nāianei, nā ngā hōtaka akoranga i āta whakamaheretia ai, i āta whakahaerehia ai hoki. Kua tino whakapiki ake ngā kaimahi i ō rātou māramatanga ki te whakahaere i ngā hōtaka akoranga. Ko te rerekē haere o ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki ka kitea, ka mōhiotia, ā, ka āta whakatutuki wawe hoki i ēnei kia whai hua ai.

Kei te kounga pai ngā taunekeneke me ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi. I ngā wā katoa, ka kōrerorero ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakapuaki hoki rātou i ō rātou whakaaro. Ka toro atu ngā kaimahi ki ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakawhānui i ēnei mā ngā whakawhitinga kōrero. Akiaki ai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whakawero i a rātou anō, me te ū tonu ki ngā taumahi i whiriwhirihia ai. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero mā te reo Māori i a rātou e ako ana.

Te whakapakari i te āheinga o te whānau ki te kōkiri tonu. Ko tētahi kaupapa matua, ko te whakapakari i te āheinga o ngā kaimahi. Kua roa nei tētahi kuia e whai wāhi ana ki te kōhanga reo, ā, kua riro i a ia tana Tohu Whakapakari, kei te whakatutuki haere tētahi o ngā kaimahi i tana Tohu Whakapakari, ā, kei tētahi atu tana Pae Rangatiratanga. Kua whakamanatia ētahi atu o ngā mema o te whānau mā Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo. Ko ngā hua o te whakawhanaketanga ngaio me te whakangungu ngaio, e kitea ana ki te whakahaeretanga me te whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo.

Te whakamāherehere me te aromātai

He whakamārama

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i ngā wāhanga ngoikore o te whakahaeretanga o te whānau me te hōtaka. Me whai tautoko te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo i te whānau. He iti noa te ako ngaio, te whakawhanaketanga ngaio anō hoki e whakaratohia ana ki ngā kaimahi, hei whakawhanake i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te hōtaka. Ko te tupu, te whakapai ake, me te rōnakitanga ngā wāhanga matua hei whakatutuki mā te whānau, kia pakari ake ai anō hoki te whakahaeretanga.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Te whakapakari i te whakahaeretanga a te whānau. He mātātoa te whai wāhi a te whānau ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Haere ai rātou ki ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, ā, ka arotahi ki te tupu me te rōnaki o te kōhanga reo, me te whakapai ake i ngā whakaritenga me ngā putanga mō ngā tamariki.

Ka whai hua ngā huihuinga ā-whānau i whakaritea ai ki te whakakotahi i ngā mema, hei whakatairanga i ngā hononga, hei whakapūmau tonu hoki i te ngākau nui o te whānau ki ngā whakawhanaketanga i te kōhanga reo, hei whai wāhi ki ngā wā ki te ako i te kaupapa, ā, hei whakaaro huritao ki te aronga whānui o te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki.

Kua kaha ake te ngākau nuitanga, te whai wāhitanga hoki a te whānau o te marae o Kaiwhaiki. He mātātoa tā rātou whai wāhi, tā rātou tautoko hoki ki te aronga, te whakahaeretanga, me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo mō te whānau me ngā tamariki.

Te arotake whaiaro a te whānau. Kua whakaritea e te whānau te whakaaro nui ki ngā mahi whakaaro huritao me te arotake. Nā te piki ake i te whai wāhitanga, te ngākau nuitanga, me te māramatanga o ngā kaimahi, he tokomaha ake ngā mema e hāpai mātātoa ana i ngā whakahaeretanga o ia wiki, ā, e whai wāhi ana hoki ki te whakamahere, te arotake, me te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga ki ngā hui o ia marama. E puta mai ana te whakaritenga o ngā pūrongo ki ngā hui ā-whānau o ia marama.

Te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromātai hoki. I nāianei, e whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i ngā tino painga katoa ka ahu mai i ngā mahi whakamahere, arotake hoki i whakamaheretia ai. He pai te māramatanga o ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere hōtaka mā te aro nui. Ka whai kiko ngā mahere hōtaka katoa e tika ana, ā, he tika te whakatinanatanga i ēnei. E mau ana te marae me tōna taiao i ngā taonga Māori tuku iho. Ka āta whai hua te whakamahinga o aua rauemi ki te hōtaka akoranga. Ko te pupuri me te whakangungu i ngā kaimahi ka whai pānga ki te whakapaitanga ake o te whakaaro huritao me te arotake a ngā kaimahi. Ka whakapai tonu te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ā rātou tamariki hei painga mā ngā tamariki.

Te aromatawai. He pai ngā mōhiohio aromatawai e wātea ana ki ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau i nāianei. Ka whai hua aua mōhiohio ki te kapo ake i te whakawhanaketanga o te katoa o te tamaiti, tae atu ki te whakawhanaketanga reo me ngā putanga ako.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te pūrongo ki te whānau. Kāhore te whānau e whiwhi ana i ngā pūrongo i whakahāngaitia ai ki ngā hōtaka akoranga o Te Whāriki. Me arotake ngā kaimahi i tēnā e pūrongohia ana e rātou i tēnei wā ki te whānau i ngā hui o ia marama, ā, me arotahi ā rātou pūrongo ki te whai pānga o ngā hōtaka ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, me ngā tūmomo huarahi ka whai hua ki te whakapai ake i te hōtaka mā ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kāhore i te tutuki e ai ki te ture

He whakamārama

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i ngā wāhanga e rua o ngā herenga ā-ture, hei whakatutukitanga mā te whānau. Ko aua wāhanga, ko te ū ki te whakatakoto me te pūrongo i ngā tikanga e pā ana ki ngā whara, ngā māuiui, me te whāngai i ngā rongoā, ā, kāhore i te whakatutukihia, i te tuhia rānei hoki ngā akoako i ia te wā, inā he rū whenua, he ahi rānei.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Ngā akoako me ngā hōneatanga ohorere. Nō nā tata tonu nei te whānau i whakaoti ngā akoako hōneatanga inā he ahi, he wā ohorere rānei. Kua whakawhanakehia e rātou tētahi tauira ka whāia hei pūrongo i aua wā i ngā hui ā-whānau.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te tuhi haere me te pūrongo i te hauora me te haumaru. He iti noa te whakatakoto haere i ngā whara, ngā māuiui, me te whāngai rongoā. Nō nā tata tonu nei tētahi tauira i whakawhanakehia ai mō te whakatakoto haere i ngā whara. Hei āhuatanga tonu o te arotake whaiaro a te whānau, e tika ana kia whakapakari te whānau me ngā kaimahi i te whakatakoto haere, te pūrongo hoki i te katoa o ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru, i runga anō i ngā herenga ā-ture, ā-raihana hoki.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

  • whakatutuki wawe i ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga hei wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana i ngā herenga ā-ture
  • whakawhanake tonu i ngā tukanga arotake whaiaro katoa a te whānau, tae atu ki te whakatakoto haere, te pūrongo hoki, kia ngāwari ai te whakapā atu a te whānau i aua mōhiohio, hei whakamahinga ki te aroturuki i te whakahaeretanga whānui o te kōhanga reo me te whakatutukitanga o ā rātou kaupapa matua rautaki.

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of Te Kōhanga Reo o Kaiwhaikigoverning body and management in relation to areas identified in the September 2011 ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • Mana Atua-learning opportunities for children, and building whānau capability for sustainable management
  • Planning and Evaluation-strengthening whānau management, whānau self review, programme planning, evaluation and assessment
  • Areas of non-compliance-recording and reporting of accidents, illness, and medicines administered, and maintain regular drills for fire and earthquake
  • Any issues identified since the previous review.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

‘Ko au te awa, ko te awa ko au’ (whānau charter)

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kaiwhaiki is located at Kaiwhaiki Marae, alongside the Whanganui River. The hapu is Ngā Paerangi of Te Ati Haunui a Paparangi. The Whanganui River and Kaiwhaiki papakainga beliefs and traditions are an integral part of learning experiences provided by kaimahi and whānau.

This ERO supplementary review evaluates progress the whānau has made with the matters raised in the ERO report of September 2011. Whānau and kaimahi were to improve the learning programme to successfully support children in their learning and development. Whānau active involvement and participation was also critical to improving areas of kōhanga reo management.

Children now receive well planned and managed programmes that successfully support their learning, development and interests. Since the last review, kaimahi have accessed Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) training for Te Whāriki, and strengthened participation with their purapura. They continually reflect on how well programmes are designed and delivered and their impact on children’s learning. Kōhanga reo whānau, including the whānau from Kaiwhaiki Marae are actively involved and support the weekly operations.

Children are further supported at kōhanga reo through improved whānau management. There is now a stronger shared focus on growth, sustainability, review and ongoing improvement of the kōhanga reo. While whānau regularly review their practices, recording and reporting systems need to be closely monitored. The kōhanga reo whānau are now managing and monitoring their kōhanga reo in line with their charter objectives and aspirations.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Findings

Mana Atua

Background

The September 2011 ERO report identified two major areas for improvement. Children did not receive sufficient support in their learning and development, including te reo Māori. Whānau involvement in kōhanga reo management was limited and this contributed to the management concerns. During this review ERO evaluated the progress whānau have made to bring about improved outcomes for children.

Areas of progress

Learning opportunities of children. The diverse learning needs and interests of all children are now successfully supported through well planned and managed programmes of learning. Kaimahi have significantly increased their understanding about managing programmes for learning. Children’s changing needs and interests are noticed, recognised and responded to promptly and effectively.

Interactions and conversations between children and kaimahi are of good quality. Children talk constantly, share conversations and communicate their ideas and opinions. Kaimahi harness children’s interests and extend these through conversations. Kaimahi regularly encourage children to challenge themselves, and persevere with chosen activities. Children are confident about communicating in te reo Māori during learning.

Building whānau capability for sustainability. Building kaimahi capability has been a priority. The long standing kuia has Tohu Whakapakari, one kaimahi is working towards completing Tohu Whakapakari and another has an administration qualification (Pae Rangatira). More whānau members have been attested through Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT).The benefits of professional development and training are reflected in the management and operation of the kōhanga reo.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

The last ERO report identified areas of whānau management and programming that were weak. The management of the kōhanga reo needed whānau support. There was little professional learning and development provided to kaimahi to develop programme practices. Growth, improvement and sustainability were key areas whānau needed to address for stronger management.

Areas of progress

Strengthening whānau management. Whānau are actively involved in the management of the kōhanga reo. They regularly attend whānau hui where the focus is on kōhanga reo growth and sustainability and improving practices and outcomes for children.

Planned whānau gatherings successfully bring members together to enhance relationships, maintain whānau interest in kōhanga reo developments, take part in kaupapa learning sessions and reflect on the overall kōhanga reo direction for children.

There is also more interest and involvement from the whānau of Kaiwhaiki Marae. They actively contribute and support the direction, management and the kaupapa of the kōhanga reo for whānau and children.

Whānau self review. The whānau has established a culture of self reflection and review. Increased involvement, interest and understanding by whānau has resulted in more members actively assisting with weekly operations and contributing to monthly hui planning, review and decision making. Organised reporting at monthly whānau hui is emerging.

Programme planning, evaluation. Children are now receiving the full benefits of organised planning and review. Kaimahi have a good understanding about responsive programme planning. All necessary programme plans are detailed and appropriately implemented. The local marae and its surrounds are rich in Māori history. These resources are well used in the learning programme. Retention and training of kaimahi has contributed to improved kaimahi reflection and review. Whānau and kaimahi are continually improving their practices for children.

Assessment. Good assessment information is now available for children and their whānau. The information successfully captures children’s holistic development including language development and learning outcomes.

Area for further improvement

Reporting to whānau. Whānau do not receive focused reports about Te Whāriki learning programmes. Kaimahi should review what they are currently reporting to the whānau at monthly hui and focus their reporting on the impact of programmes on children’s learning and ways in which to improve the programme for children.

Areas of non-compliance

Background

The previous ERO report identified two areas of non compliance the whānau needed to address. These were maintaining adequate records and reporting requirements for accidents, illness and administration of medicines. Earthquake and fire drills were not regularly carried out, and documented.

Areas of progress

Emergency drills and evacuation. Whānau have recently completed fire and emergency evacuation drills. They have developed a template for reporting on these at whānau hui.

Area for further improvement

Recording and reporting of health and safety. There is limited recording of accidents, illness and administration of medicines. A template for recording accidents has recently been developed. As part of whānau self review, it is important that the whānau and kaimahi, strengthen all health and safety recording and reporting practices as required under the regulatory and licence obligations.

4 Actions Required for Compliance

In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements they need to:

  1. ensure that while at the kōhanga reo there are kept for each child clear and detailed records of every accident, illness and medicine administered to the child, and parents are informed of these. [R35(e-g) Education (ECC) Regulations 1998]

  • immediately address the areas of non compliance identified in this ERO report
  • further develop all whānau self review procedures, including recording and reporting, so that information is readily available for whānau to use to monitor the overall performance of the kōhanga reo in meeting their strategic priorities.

To the Parents and Community of Te Kōhanga Reo o Kaiwhaiki

This supplementary review evaluates the quality of education and care received by the tamariki and the performance of the whānau in relation to specific terms of reference.

The Education Review Office [ERO] evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Kaiwhaiki follows.

‘Ko au te awa, ko te awa ko au’ (whānau charter)

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kaiwhaiki is located at Kaiwhaiki Marae, alongside the Whanganui River. The hapu is Ngā Paerangi of Te Ati Haunui a Paparangi. The Whanganui River and Kaiwhaiki papakainga beliefs and traditions are an integral part of learning experiences provided by kaimahi and whānau.

This ERO supplementary review evaluates progress the whānau has made with the matters raised in the ERO report of September 2011. Whānau and kaimahi were to improve the learning programme to successfully support children in their learning and development. Whānau active involvement and participation was also critical to improving areas of kōhanga reo management.

Children now receive well planned and managed programmes that successfully support their learning, development and interests. Since the last review, kaimahi have accessed Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) training for Te Whāriki, and strengthened participation with their purapura. They continually reflect on how well programmes are designed and delivered and their impact on children’s learning. Kōhanga reo whānau, including the whānau from Kaiwhaiki Marae are actively involved and support the weekly operations.

Children are further supported at kōhanga reo through improved whānau management. There is now a stronger shared focus on growth, sustainability, review and ongoing improvement of the kōhanga reo. While whānau regularly review their practices, recording and reporting systems need to be closely monitored. The kōhanga reo whānau are now managing and monitoring their kōhanga reo in line with their charter objectives and aspirations.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

When ERO has reviewed a kōhanga reo we ask the whānau of the kōhanga reo to let us know how they intend to manage any difficulties set out in their report (copies of which are available from the kōhanga reo or ERO). We also encourage whānau to inform their community of any follow up action plan. You should talk to the whānau if you have any questions about this evaluation, the full ERO report or their future intentions.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based on Te Whāriki, the whānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
