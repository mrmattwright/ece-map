1 Evaluation of Little Hands Childcare & Early Learning Centre

How well placed is Little Hands Childcare & Early Learning Centre to promote positive learning outcomes for children?

Not well placed

Requires further development

Well placed

Very well placed

Little Hands Childcare & Early Learning Centre is in the early stages of developing an effective management and leadership framework. Leaders and teachers have yet to put in place management and teaching systems and practices that promote ongoing improvement.

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Little Hands Childcare & Early Learning Centre is privately owned. Leaders and teachers provide care and education for children from birth to school age. The centre is licensed for 38 children, including up to 10 under two years of age. The largest group of children at this centre is MƒÅori. The owner takes responsibility for the day-to-day operation of the centre. This is the centre's first ERO review.

Children learn and play in two separate rooms. One is for children up to the age of two years and the other room is for the older group of children. The centre's philosophy emphasises the belief that children learn through play.

Families from a variety of cultural backgrounds attend the centre and staffing reflects this diversity. The majority of children are learning English as an additional language.

Since the centre opened in 2016, there has been a significant number of staff changes. An existing team leader was appointed to the role of centre supervisor. Most staff are qualified early childhood teachers. A cook provides nutritious snacks and meals for children.

The Review Findings

Children are settled and happily engaged in their learning. They have many opportunities to choose their interests from an appropriate range of resources and equipment in attractive, well-presented indoor and outdoor environments.

Children experience respectful relationships with each other, their teachers and other staff. Teachers view children as independent, active, diverse, confident learners. They show care and concern for children's wellbeing and build children's sense of belonging while they are at the centre.

Teachers foster children's creativity through readily available art resources. They skilfully incorporate mathematics concepts and language development opportunities in meaningful play experiences.

Infants and toddlers are cared for and learn in a nurturing, calm environment. Teachers gently support children. They express aroha openly within their caring interactions with children. Toddlers have regular access to the older children's play areas. This strongly supports their transition into the older group.

Transition into the centre is family-led and well managed. Teachers support children's successful transition to school by helping them develop independence and self-help skills.

Parents are well informed about their children's learning and participation in the programme. The centre has introduced an online digital platform so parents are able to receive real-time information.

Teachers meet regularly to discuss centre operations, children's assessment and their planning and evaluation processes. There are some useful examples of learning stories that assess children's learning and development. Leaders now need to place more emphasis on planning for children's next learning steps. This planning should identify intentional teaching strategies to extend children's learning.

The owner and supervisor work closely together and share centre responsibilities. They are yet to strongly link strategic planning to positive learning outcomes for children. Leaders support teachers to increase their capability and ensure they have access to a range of professional learning and development opportunities. Leaders should ensure that teachers and staff are involved in whole-centre professional learning. This would help to build a shared understanding for staff about good quality systems and practices for sustainability and improvement.

Key Next Steps

Key next steps include:

  • establishing clear, shared expectations for assessment, planning and evaluation, to ensure a strong emphasis on learning and teaching

  • strengthen teachers' understanding of bicultural practices in the context of Aotearoa New Zealand and the Treaty of Waitangi

  • further develop and implement the appraisal process to build teacher capability

  • redesign the strategic plan and develop annual action plans to ensure a stronger focus on positive learning outcomes for children

  • strengthen internal evaluation by evaluating the impact of changes on outcomes for children.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Little Hands Childcare & Early Learning Centre completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)

  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)

  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)

  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children‚Äôs health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Actions for compliance

ERO identified non-compliance relating to appraisal. The service needs to implement a regular system of appraisal in order to meet Education Council requirements.

Licensing Criteria for Early Childhood Education and Care Centres 2008, GMA7.

Development Plan Recommendation

ERO recommends that the service, in consultation with the Ministry of Education, develops a plan to address the key next steps and actions outlined in this report.

Next ERO Review

When is ERO likely to review the service again?

The next ERO review of Little Hands Childcare & Early Learning Centre will be within two years.
