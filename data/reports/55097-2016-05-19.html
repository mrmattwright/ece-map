Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Taniwaka ki te tāone o Taniwaka. Ka kitea te tūmāia o ngā tamariki i roto i te reo Māori me te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki ako. Ka whiwhi rātou i te kounga o ngā wheako akoranga o te reo Māori, ā, he manawa whakahī tō rātou ki te whakapuaki i tō rātou Māoritanga. He tau, he pakari hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao whakaongaonga.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Taniwaka i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Taniwaka ki te tāone o Taniwaka. Nā tētahi kuia tēnei kōhanga reo i whakatū, i whakahaere anō hoki. Ka maumahara te whānau ki āna takoha nui ki te kaupapa me te kōhanga reo. Nā tēnei āhuatanga, kei te kawe te whakatupuranga tuawhā o te whānau i ngā mahi ki te whāngai i te mātauranga rumaki reo ki ā rātou tamariki i ēnei rā, ā, mō meāke nei hoki. Kua whakahāngaitia e te whānau tā rātou aronga rautaki hei āta whakarite i te noho o te reo Māori ki te pūtake o te kōhanga reo.

Mai i te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i te tau 2013, kua whakaae Te Kōhanga Reo o Taniwaka me tētahi atu kōhanga reo o tēnei tāone ki te whakahaeretanga ngātahi a ngā kōhanga reo hei whānau kotahi.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te whakaatu a ngā tamariki i te pakari haere o tō rātou tūmāia i roto i te reo Māori?

Ka whakaatu ngā tamariki i te tūmāia i roto i te reo Māori, ā, i te whānuitanga hoki o ngā tūmomo horopaki ako.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i te kounga o ngā wheako akoranga mā te reo Māori. Ka whakapakari, ka aroturuki hoki ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. Ko te tūmanako, ka whakatauirahia te reo Māori mā ngā tamariki, i te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka hāpai ngā taumahi reo i ngā tamariki ki te kōrero me te whakapuaki i ā rātou akoranga ki waenga i a rātou anō. Ka whakatairanga ngā rauemi reo, ngā waiata, me ngā mihimihi i ngā akoranga ki ngā horopaki ōkawa me ngā horopaki ōpaki. He māhorahora te kōrero a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori.

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hononga ā-whakapapa. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki tēina ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou pepehā, ā, he pakari te tū o ngā tamariki tuākana ki te taki i ō rātou ake pepehā. Ka poipoia te whanaungatanga ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga i te hapori, ngā haerenga ki te marae, me ngā kaupapa hoki ki te taha o ētahi atu kōhanga reo. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, ā rātou akoranga hoki ki te taha o ō rātou hoa me te whānau. He manawa whakahī tō ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i tō rātou Māoritanga.

E tupu haere ana te mōhio o ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō. Ka whai pānga matua ngā uaratanga o te whakaute, te atawhai, me te manaaki i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whakatinana i te whakaute me te manaaki ki ētahi atu. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki papai e hāpai ana, e akiaki ana, ā, e whakawhānui ana hoki i te whanonga o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahia ngā karakia hei whakatau, hei whakarite, hei whakapakari hoki i ngā hononga ā-wairua o ngā tamariki. He tau, he pakari hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

E ako ana ngā tamariki ki te noho maioha me te whakamana anō hoki i ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whakanui i ētahi atu ahurea me ētahi atu reo i te wāhanga mihimihi o te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka hāngai ngā mahinga ki ngā tikanga, hei hāpai i ngā tamariki ki te taunekeneke mā ngā huarahi e whai hua ana, ā, ki te whānuitanga anō hoki o ngā horopaki. Ka tuituia ngā uaratanga o te manaaki, te aroha, me te tautoko ki ā rātou akoranga, hei whakawhanake i te tūmāia ki te taha o ngā manuhiri. He pai ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu.

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao whakaongaonga. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi kia whai wāhi ai ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga. Ko ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki, me te pārekareka, te tākaro pohewa hoki tētahi aronga o ā rātou akoranga. E whakaritea e ngā kaimahi kia wātea ai te whakapā atu a ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi. Kua whakaritea tētahi wāhi haumaru mō ngā tamariki tēina i ngā wā e tika ana. Nā ngā mahinga i te kōhanga reo ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā kawenga e tiaki ana i te kōhanga reo. He pārekareka te ako ki ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ko ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki e whai pānga ana ki ngā mahi ki te whakamahere i te hōtaka. Ka whakamahere ngātahi ngā kaimahi ki te taha o tētahi atu kōhanga reo. Ka whai rātou i tētahi tukanga whakamahere mārama, pakari hoki, ā, kua whakahāngaitia tēnei ki Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Ka tuhia te hōtaka o ia rā ki runga i tētahi papa mā hei whakaatu i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki ki te whānau. Ka mātakitaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki i roto i te hōtaka, hei tāutu i tā rātou aro atu ki ngā taumahi ako. Ka whai wāhi atu te whānau ki te hōtaka o ia rā, ā, ka ako rātou ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki. He harikoa ngā tamariki ki roto i ā rātou akoranga.

Ka āta aroturukitia te ako a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo aromatawai ōkawa, ōpaki hoki, kia whakaemihia ai ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki ia tamaiti. Ko ngā kitenga o ia rā me ngā tauira mahi a ngā tamariki, ka tuhia ki ngā pūkete, ā, ka hono ki ngā kaupapa ako. Ka noho mōhio ngā whānau ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki nā ngā pūrongo ahu whakamua i ia te wā. He pūrongo ōkawa anō hoki ka whakaratohia mā te whānau, mō te whakawhanaketanga ako o ia tamaiti. Ko te ahu whakamua a ia tamaiti tētahi kaupapa matua.

Ko te whanaketanga o ngā tamariki e ārahi ana i ngā whakaritenga whakahaere. Ko ngā whānau o ngā kōhanga reo e rua e whai wāhi atu ana ki ngā whakahaeretanga o aua kōhanga reo. Kua whakatūngia tētahi rōpū matua o te whānau, ā, ko tā rātou mahi he whakawhiti kōrero mō ngā kaupapa matua e pā ana ki ngā take whakahaere. Ka aro nui ki te whakatupu ake i te āheinga reo Māori, me te whakatutukitanga o ngā whakaritenga a te kaituku mahi pai. Ka noho matua ko ngā putanga ako a ngā tamariki.

Ko ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki e whakamōhio ana i ngā mahere rautaki. He whāinga rautaki ā te whānau e hāngai ana ki tō rātou horopaki, ā rātou kaupapa matua hoki mō ngā tamariki. Ka whakawhanakehia aua mahere e te whānau me ngā kaimahi. Ka aroturuki te whānau i te ahu whakamua mā ngā wānanga, ā, ka whakawhānui ake, ka whakarerekē ake hoki i runga anō i ā rātou mahere rautaki. Ko te reo Māori kei te pūtake o te kōhanga reo, ā, ka noho matua anō hoki puta noa i Te Ara Tūāpae. Ka noho matua te ahu whakamua o ngā tamariki hei ākonga, mā Te Ara Tūāpae.

Ka whai pānga te whakawhanaketanga o te katoa o te tamaiti ki te aromātai o roto. Ka whāia ngā tūmanako i āta tāutuhia ai i roto i ngā pūrongo, te aroturuki, me te arotake. Ka kōrerohia ngā whāinga rautaki, ka arotakengia ngā kaupapa here e pā ana ki te hauora me ngā haumaru, ā, ka hāpai te aromātai hōtaka a ngā kaimahi me te ahu whakamua a ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aromātai o roto. Ka āta whakaarohia te atawhai o ngā tamariki, me tō rātou tupu i roto i te reo Māori me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo.

He pakari tonu te arotake, te pūrongo, me te aroturuki. Ka hāngai ngā hui ā-whānau ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero e pā ana ki ā rātou whāinga rautaki. Kua whakaritea ngā kaupapa here mō te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki. He pai te āta tiaki i ngā rawa i ia te wā. Ka aromātai ngā kaimahi i te hōtaka akoranga i ia wiki, ā, ka whakawhiti kōrero rātou mō ngā āhuatanga i tutuki pai, me ngā āhuatanga hoki hei whakapai ake. Ka tātari ngā kaimahi i te ahu whakamua o te reo o ngā tamariki me te puāwai mai o tō rātou tūmāia. Kei ngā aromātai o roto ngā wawata kia kaha ake ai te uru mai o te hangarau ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ko te atawhai o ngā tamariki, te whakawhanaketanga i te reo Māori, me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo e āta whakahaerehia ana.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • toro atu tonu te whānau ki te tautoko o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ki te kōkiri i ā rātou hanganga whakahaere e noho ngātahi ana, hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Taniwaka is based in Dannevirke. Children demonstrate confidence in te reo Māori and in a variety of learning settings. They receive quality learning experiences in te reo Māori and are proud to express their Māoritanga. Children are settled and secure in the kōhanga reo. Children learn in a stimulating environment.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Taniwaka will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Taniwaka is based in Dannevirke. A kuia established and managed the kōhanga reo. Whānau remember her contributions to the kaupapa and the kōhanga reo. As a result the fourth generation of whānau have taken on the responsibility to educate their children in immersion educatin now and into the future. Whānau have aligned their strategic direction to ensure that te reo Māori is the cornerstone of the kōhanga reo.

Since the last ERO Report 2013 Te Kōhanga Reo o Taniwaka and another kōhanga reo in Dannevirke have agreed to be managed as one whānau.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children show their developing confidence in te reo Māori?

Children demonstrate confidence in te reo Māori and in a variety of learning settings.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children receive quality learning experiences in te reo Māori. Kaimahi and whānau strengthen and monitor children’s language development. It is expected that te reo Māori is the language modelled for children in the daily programme. Language activities enable children to speak and share their learning with each other. Language resources, waiata and mihimihi promote learning in formal and informal contexts. Children are spontaneous speakers of te reo Māori.

Children know their whakapapa links and connections. Kaimahi support younger children to share their pepehā, while older children confidently share without support. A range of learning experiences in the community, visits to marae and activities with other kōhanga reo foster whanaungatanga. Kaimahi encourage children to share their interests and learning with their peers and whānau. Children are proud to express their Māoritanga.

Children are growing in their knowledge of who they are. Values of respect, nurture and care are an integral part of the learning programme. Kaimahi encourage children to show respect and care for each other. Kaimahi use a range of positive strategies to support, encourage and extend children’s behaviour. Karakia is used to settle, prepare and reaffirm children’s spiritual connections. Children are settled and secure in the kōhanga reo.

Children are learning to appreciate and value relationships with others. Kaimahi encourage children to acknowledge other cultures and languages in daily programme of mihimihi. Routines are based on tikanga to support children to interact in meaningful ways with a range of settings. Values of manaaki, aroha and tautoko is integrated into their learning to develop confidence with manuhiri. Children interact positively with others.

Children learn in a stimulating environment. Kaimahi provide a range of activities to engage children in learning. Children’s interests, enjoyment and imaginary play is a focus in their learning. Kaimahi provide children with easy access to a variety of resources. Younger children have a defined safe space when required. Routines established in the kōhanga reo provide children to share responsibility to care of their kōhanga reo environment. Children enjoy learning.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s interests informs programme planning. Kaimahi plan collaboratively with another kōhanga reo. They follow a clear and robust planning process aligned to Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. The daily programme is written on a whiteboard to share children’s learning with whānau. Kaimahi observe children in the programme to identify their response to learning activities. Whānau participate daily programme to learn alongside their children. Children are happy in their learning.

Children’s learning is rigorously monitored. Kaimahi use a range of formal and informal assessments practices to gather information about individual children. Daily observations, samples of children’s work are recorded in profile books linked to kaupapa ako. Regular progress reports keep whānau informed about children’s learning. Formal reports are provided for whānau about individuals learning development. Children’s individual progress is a priority.

Children’s development guide management practices. Whānau from two kōhanga contribute to kōhanga reo operations for two sites. A core group of whānau have been delegated with responsibilities to meet and discuss priorities for operational matters. Emphasis is placed on growing te reo Māori capability and fulfilling good employee practices. Children’s learning outcomes is important.

Children’s learning informs strategic planning. Whānau have strategic goals that align to their context and their priorities for children. These plans are developed by both whānau and kaimahi. Whānau monitor progress through wānanga and make additions and amendments accordingly to their strategic plans. Te Reo Māori is a cornerstone of the kōhanga reo and is a common theme throughout Te Ara Tūāpae. Children’s future direction as learners is prioritised through Te Ara Tūāpae.

Children’s holistic development contributes to internal evaluation. There are defined reporting, monitoring and review expectations followed. Strategic goals are discussed, health and safety policies reviewed and kaimahi programme evaluation and children’s progress in learning supports internal evaluation practices. Children’s care and holistic growth in their te reo Māori and their kōhanga reo kaupapa are considered well.

Review, reporting and monitoring overall is reasonably strong. Whānau hui highlight discussions around their strategic goals. Policies are in place for the health and safety of children. Good maintenance of the property is a regular practice. Kaimahi evaluate the learning programme weekly and discuss what went well and how to improve. Kaimahi analyse the progress of children’s reo and their growing confidence. Internal evaluation includes aspirations to include technology more in children’s learning. Children’s care , te reo Māori development and the kōhanga reo kaupapa are well managed.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • that the whānau continue to seek support from the TKRNT to advance their combined managerial structures in the best interest of children.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Taniwaka is based in Dannevirke. Children demonstrate confidence in te reo Māori and in a variety of learning settings. They receive quality learning experiences in te reo Māori and are proud to express their Māoritanga. Children are settled and secure in the kōhanga reo. Children learn in a stimulating environment.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Taniwaka will be in three years.
