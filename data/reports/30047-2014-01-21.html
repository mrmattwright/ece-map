Background

Lil Pumpkins Preschool Centre Limited is a privately owned centre located in the Glenview suburb of Hamilton. The centre provides education and care for children from three months to school age in two age-based rooms. It is licensed for 30 children, including five under the age of two years. At the time of this ERO review the centre‚Äôs roll was 30, including 11 children who identify as MƒÅori.

Since the 2011 ERO review there has been a change in centre leadership and some changes in the teaching staff. The centre owner provides overall governance for the centre. The company manager provides professional guidance for the centre supervisor and teachers, and oversees centre administration. Four of the seven teachers at the centre are fully qualified early childhood teachers. The centre has a positive ERO reporting history and centre leaders have responded positively to the areas for review and development in the 2011 ERO review about strengthening partnerships with families. At the beginning of 2013 the centre was relicensed by the Ministry of Education under the Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008.

The centre philosophy makes a commitment to providing learning experiences that allow children to become confident and competent learners with a positive sense of identity and mana. Its curriculum is intended to reflect the principles of the early childhood curriculum, Te WhƒÅriki, and the Reggio Emilia philosophy.

The Review Findings

The centre owner promotes positive outcomes for children by maintaining very good teacher-to-child ratios and a well-resourced learning environment. In consultation with staff and the company manager, she has developed a comprehensive strategic plan to guide centre development. A useful policy and procedures framework informs centre operations. These are regularly reviewed in consultation with parents and teachers.

The recently appointed centre supervisor is providing good quality leadership for teachers and children. She models effective teaching practice and has already established a collaborative team culture among teachers. The knowledgeable and experienced company manager provides high-quality support and professional guidance for the supervisor and teachers. The strong emphasis placed on building and supporting effective teaching practices, has been enhanced by implementing more robust teacher appraisal processes.

Children participate in a programme that provides them with many opportunities to explore and follow their interests. Teachers effectively integrate literacy and mathematics learning into children‚Äôs play and at set-group times. Children enjoy regular excursions into the local community. The programme is enhanced by parents and members of the wider community frequently sharing their knowledge and expertise with children. An increased emphasis on te reo, tikanga and te ao MƒÅori has strengthened the identity and sense of belonging of MƒÅori children and their whƒÅnau at the centre. A specific programme for older children supports their readiness for school by focussing on small group skills, number recognition and aspects of literacy.

Teachers have established strong partnerships with parents and whƒÅnau. They keep parents well informed about their children‚Äôs participation in the programme and learning through regular conversations and attractively presented individual portfolios. Centre celebrations and special events are well attended by parents and members of the wider community.

Toddlers benefit from respectful, responsive and nurturing care. Consistent care giving supports their need to form strong and secure attachments. Toddlers also have good opportunities to interact with, and learn alongside their older siblings and peers.

Teachers‚Äô interactions and relationships with children are positive and affirming. They skilfully foster development of children‚Äôs social skills and oral language. Children with diverse needs experience an environment that caters well for their emotional and learning needs. The spacious outdoor play areas offer a wide variety of physical challenge and many opportunities for imaginative play. Attractively presented wall displays celebrate children‚Äôs creativity and learning.

Key Next Steps

ERO and centre leaders agree that the key next steps are to:

  • review and strengthen current assessment and planning processes to support teachers to more consistently notice, recognise and respond to children‚Äôs emerging interests and learning
  • access centre-wide professional learning for teachers on the Reggio Emilia philosophy to build a shared understanding of how to implement this in the centre.

In these documents they attested that they select ‚Äòhave‚Äô or ‚Äòhave not‚Äô taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:
