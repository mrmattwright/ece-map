1. He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā tētahi ō ngā Kaiwhakahaere Matua ā-Rohe o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei, kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

I whakaritea tēnei arotake tāpiri i runga i te aro mātai i ngā whakahaere a te rōpu whakahaere o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ngakau Mahana e pa ana ki ngā wāhanga i kitea i roto i te ripoata a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te marama o Poutū-te-rangi o te tau 2011, i ēra atu take rānei kua pupū ake mai i taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mo ngā whakapuakitanga o tēnei arotake, ko te āta titiro ki:

  • Mana Whenua, tāpiri atu ki ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki, te hauora o ngā tamariki kei raro iho i te rua tau e pā ana ki te hinengaro me te whatumanawa.
  • te whakamāherehere me te aromātai - tae atu ki te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa here, ngā tukanga whakahaere, te whakatakoto māhere rautaki, te arotake whaiaro, te whai wāhitanga a te whānau, te whakahaere kaimahi, te mahi whakamāherehere, te aromatawai me te aromātai me te wāhi i waho.
  • ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture.
  • ēra atu tino take ka pupū ake i te wā e whakahaeretia ana tēnei arotake.

2. Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kātahi anō ngā tamariki me te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ngakau Mahana kia hūnuku ki tētahi whare hou ki te kura tuarua o Henderson ki Tamaki Makaurau.

E kōrero paitia ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi mō te āhua o ngā whare hou, ā, he whakapaipai tonu te āhua o ngā whare. E whai wāhi ana te tokomaha o ngā tamariki ki te tae mai ki te kōhanga reo ia rā. Kua tutuki paitia ngā take i pupū ake ai i te pūrongo o mua o Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga e pā ana ki te hauora, te haumaru me te tiaki.

E hāngai ana tēnei pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ki ngā take huhua i tāututia i te pūrongo tāpiri i te Poutū te rangi, 2011. I hāngai ngā take ki ngā rauemi a ngā tamariki, te reo Māori, te hauora, te haumaru me te tiaki tamariki. I puta anō ētahi atu take i te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, arā, te whakatakotoranga o te māhere rautaki, te arotake whaiaro, te whakamāherehere i ngā hōtaka, te aromātai tae noa ki ngā tūranga me ngā haepapa a ngā kaimahi.

He kaimahi hou te nuinga o ngā kaimahi, ā, he kaimahi hou ngā kaiako. Ko te kuia anake te kaimahi mai i tērā atu kāhui kiawhakaako. E whai hua ana te katoa o pūkenga me ngā pūmanawa o ngā kaimahi, ngā kaimahi me te kuia, ā, e whai wāhi atu ana rātou ki te whakamāherehere me te aromātai i ngā kaupapa rerekē. E whanake ana rātou i ngā pūkenga reo Māori o ngā tamariki, ā, e whai wāhi ana tamariki ki ngā akoranga hihiri. E maia ana ngā kaiako, ā, kua roa rātou e mahi ana. He whai hua o rātou mahi, ā, e āta tātaki ana rātou i ngā kaimahi.

He kamakama, he tōtika, ā, he whānui te hōtaka akoranga mā ngā tamariki. E tino whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki ngā rauemi maha me ngā ngohe huhua. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whai i a rātou e hiahiatia, te hōpara haere, me te whai wāhi ki ngā wheako akoranga e hāngai ai ki te ako ā-pānui me te ako ā-tau mā ngā kōhungahunga. E whai wāhi ana rātou ki te tākaro pōhewa me te tākaro auaha, ā, e whakamahi ana rātou i ngā hangarau rerekē. Ko te reo Māori te reo whakaputa mā ngā taunekeneke me ngā ngohe.

Ka rangona te reo Māori e ngā tamariki i te rā katoa. Ko ngā kaimahi te take he hihiri ngā akoranga reo Maori, ā, e whakahaeretia ana e rātou ngā kēmu, ngā ngohe me ngā rautaki whai hua. E maia ana ngā tuakana ki te kōrero i te reo i ngā wheako akoranga ō-kawa, ā, i te wā e tātaki ana rātou i ngā tikanga rerekē me ngā whakaritenga o ia rā. E āhei ana rātou ki te whai i ngā tohutohu māmā tae noa ki ngā whakawhitiwhiti kōrero uaua. E hari koa ana ngā kōhungahunga i te wā kei te kōrero ā-rotarota rātou tae noa ki te wā kei te kōrero tāruarua rātou.

E whai hua ana te mahi tahi a te whānau, ā, e rikarika ana rātou ki tō rātou kōhanga reo. Te tokomaha hoki ka tae mai ki ngā hui ia wā ia wā, ā, ka whai wāhi rātou ki te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero me te whakatau i ngā take.

Kua āta whakaritea te māhere rautaki, ā, e aro nui ana te māhere rautaki ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, te reo Māori me te whakangungu i ngā kaimahi. E piki ake ana te maia o te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te arotake whaiaro. E tīmata ana rātou ki te aroturuki me te whakarite pūrongo e pā ana ki ngā whakahaere rerekē o te kōhanga reo. E mārama ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki ngā wāhanga o te kōhanga e tika ana hei whakapai ake anō.

Ngā Mahi ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3. Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Atua

He Whakamārama

I tāututia ngā take huhua e pā ana ki te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki i te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake o te Mātauranga Kāore ngā rauemi me ngā ngohe i te tautoko i ngā tamariki kia whai wāhi ai rātou ki te mahi pōhewa me te mahi auaha. Me whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā wheako huhua hei wero hinengaro.

Ahakoa he ōrite te kōrero Māori a ngā kaimahi i te rā, me whai wāhi anō rātou ki te whakapiki i te reo a ngā tamariki mā te whakahua i ngā kupu hou me te whakatauira i ngā rerenga kōrero hou.

I tāututia ētahi take e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru hei whakapai ake. Ko ngā ngā take e whai ake nei ngā take i tāututia, arā, ngā turuturu i te parani, ngā whakaritenga tiaki tamariki, te whakamau tōtika i ngā taputapu o waho, te tiaki me te whakapai i te wāhi oneone me te wāhi tākaro mō ngā tamariki kei raro iho i te rua ngā tau.

E noho ana ngā tamariki me te whānau i tētahi wāhi hou. He whare tiaki tamariki kōhungahunga i ngā rā o mua te wāhi hou o te kōhanga. Heoi anō, kei te tū tonu te kōhanga reo ki te kura tuarua o Henderson.

He maha ngā kaimahi hou i te kōhanga. E mātau ana ngā kaiako tokorua ki ngā mahi tika o te kōhanga, ā, kātahi anō rāua ka tīmata. E whai wāhi ana rātou ko te kuia ki te āta tātaki me te whakarite i ngā kaupapa ngaio hei arahi i ngā kaimahi.

Ngā Wāhanga Kua Pai Ake

Ngā rauemi whai take me ngā ngohe. Kua whakatūria e ngā kaimahi tētahi hōtaka whakahirahira mā ngā tamariki. E noho harikoa ana ngā tamariki. Kua whakaritea ngā wāhi whai rauemi i ngā wāhi i waho me ngā wāhi i roto. E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki te hāpai i ngā hangarau rerekē, te tākaro pohewa me te tākaro auaha, te whai weako ā-tau me ngā wheako ā-pānui mā te hunga kōhungahunga, te tākaro whakahanga me te puoro. He maha ngā wheako wero hinengaro kua whakaritea hei whakaihiihi i ngā tamatāne, ngā tamariki e āhei ana me ngā rōpū reanga rerekē. Ka kitea te whai whakaaro pohewa i te wā e tākaro ana ngā tamariki. He ākonga maia ngā tamariki.

Te tautoko i te reo Māori. E mātau ana te kuia me ngā kaiako ki te reo Māori. E whai wāhi atu ana rātou ki te whakatauira i te reo Māori ki ngā kaimahi, te whānau me ngā tamariki. E maia ana ngā tamariki ki te whakaputa i te reo Māori i ngā wā o te waiata, te karakia, te hīmene me te whakapapa. E whai wāhi ana ngā tuakana ki te tū hei kaitātaki i ngā wā ka whakahaeretia te takamahi o ia rā me ngā wā ka hāpaitia ngā tīkanga. E mārama ana rātou ki te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero, te reo tohutohu, te reo whakahaere me te kōrero i ngā rerenga āhua māmā tae noa ki ngā rerenga uaua. He maha ngā rautaki a ngā kaimahi hei tautoko me te whakanui ake i ngā kupu me ngā pūkenga ā-reo o ngā tamariki. E noho ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi wāhi angitū e rere ana te reo Māori anake i ngā wā katoa.

Te tiaki, te hauora me te haumaru. Kāore te whānau e aroaro ana ki ngā take haumaru me te hauora i pupū ake ai i te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. Kua hūnuku ngā tamariki me te whānau ki tētahi wāhi rerekē i runga i te whenua o te kura tuarua o Henderson. E pai ana te āhua o ngā whare me te whenua o te kōhanga reo hou, ā, kua āta tiakina e te whānau me te kura. E tino whai wāhi ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te hāpai i ngā whakaritenga tiaki tamariki i ngā wāhi i roto me ngā wāhi i waho. Nā tēnei mahi e noho haumaru ana ngā tamariki i te wā kei te kōhanga reo rātou.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He Whakamārama

I tāututia ētahi take e pā ana ki te whakahaere o te kōhanga reo i te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. Kāore te whānau i te whakarite ā-tuhi a rātou whāinga rautaki mō ia tau, kāore rānei rātou i te aro atu ki Te Ara Tuapae. Me pai ake anō hoki ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro. Ahakoa i whakakohia ngā mōhiohio i ngā wāhi rerekē kāore ngā mōhiohio i te tātari.

Ko te whakamāherehere me te aromātai ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake. Kāore ngā kaimahi i te whakamahi i ngā māhere ā-tau, ngā māhere ā-wahanga ako me ngā māhere ā-wiki hei whakahaere i te hōtaka ako. Kāore e kitea ngā whāinga o te hōtaka me ngā ngohe. He whāiti he pakupaku te āhua o te aromātai i te hōtaka. He iti noa te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero e pā ana ki te te taiao ā-tinana, te kaupapa, ngā whakaritenga o ia rā, ngā ngohe a ngā tamariki me ngā take e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi.

Kāore nga kaimahi i te āta mōhio ki a rātou haepapa me te tūranga mahi a tēnā me tēnā. Kāore i ngā kaiako ngā pūkenga tātaki hei whakahaere i ngā kaimahi.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Ngā whakaritenga o te māhere rautaki. He tōtika ngā whāinga me ngā wawata a te whānau hei tautoko i ngā painga mō a rātou tamariki me te kōhanga reo mō ngā rā kei te heke mai nei. Ka nui a rātou whai whakaaro me te whakapai ake i ngā take huhua i pupū ake ai i te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. Ko ngā mahi nui mō te whānau he whakatau i ngā kaimahi hou me te hūnuku ki tētahi wāhi hou hei whakatū i tō rātou kōhanga reo hou. Kua tāututia ētahi whāinga hou e te whānau. E hāngai ana ēnei whāinga ki te Te Ara Tuapae, tāpiri atu ki te reo Māori, te whakangungu, te whakarite rauemi, te akoranga a te whānau, te ako ā-tau me te ako ā-pānui mā ngā kōhungahunga. E āta hāngai ana ngā whāinga rautaki ki te ako a ngā tamariki, te reo Māori me te whakangungu kaimahi.

Te arotake whaiaro. E pakari haere ana te maia o te whānau ki te aroturuki me te whakarite pūrongo e hāngai ai ki ngā whakahaere rerekē o te kōhanga reo. E whakamahia ana e te whānau me ngā kaimahi tētahi whārangi whakarite pūrongo hei arahi i a rātou i te wā e arotake whaiaro ana rātou i a rātou anō. E whai ake nei ko ngā wāhanga matua o te pūrongo arotake whaiaro, arā, te whakahaere pūtea, ngā take e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru, ngā whakaritenga whakangungu, ngā āwangawanga o te whānau, te arotake i ngā kaupapa here, te reo Māori me te mahi hei tiaki i ngā rawa o te kōhanga reo. E mārama ana te whānau ki ērā wahanga anō hei whakapai ake. E whai ana rātou i tētahi māhere ki te whakapai ake i ērā wāhanga.

Te whakatakoto māhere ā-kaupapa. Te maia hoki o ngā kaimahi ki te whakatakoto māhere mā ngā tamariki. He whānui he hōhonu ngā māhere whāroa me ngā māhere poto, ā, he tōtika te mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te hāpai i ngā kaupapa ki ngā tamariki. E āta hāngai ngā māhere ki Te Whāriki, Te Korowai me ngā atua Māori. Ka rata anō hoki te whānau ki te whāngai korerō hei whakarite i ngā māhere. Ko te whakatakotoranga a ngā māhere me ngā putanga wheako akoranga te take he hōhonu te akoranga a ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori me te reo Māori.

Te hōtaka aromātai. E whakamahi ana ngā kaimahi i te hōtaka aromātai ki te whanake me te whakapai ake i te akoranga a ngā tamariki. E whakamahi ana rātou i ngā hui whakawhitiwhiti kōrero ō-kawa me ngā hui whakawhitiwhiti kōrero ōpaki ki te aromātai i ngā painga o te taiao ā-tinana, te kaupapa, ngā haerenga, te tūranga a ngā tuakana, ngā whakaritenga o ia rā, me te whai wāhi ki ngā rauemi. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi ki ngā aukatinga e pā ana ki te wāhi o te tari, te wāhi tiaki pepī me ētahi atu wāhi tākaro. E whakawhitiwhiti kōrero ana rātou ki te whakapai ake i ēnei take.

Te rārangi mahi me ngā haepapa a ngā kaimahi. He mahana, he rikarika ngā kaimahi, ā, he roopu angitu rātou. He hou te nuinga o ngā kaimahi me ngā kaiako. E whakamahi ana rātou i te katoa o a rātou pūkenga ki te kōrero i ngā pakiwaitara, te whakarite i ngā whakaari, te kōrero i te reo me te whakarite pūoro hei whanake i te tipuranga me ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. E whai hua ana te mahi tātaki a ngā kaiako ki ngā kaimahi ki te whakamāherehere, te aromātai me te mahi hei hāpai i te reo Māori. He kirimana whakawhiwhi mahi a ngā kaimahi, ā, he rārangi mahi a rātou e āta whakaatu mai ana a rātou haepapa me a rātou tūranga. Kua āta whakaritea ngā kaimahi, ā, e maia ana rātou ki te kawe i a rātou tūranga i te wā e mahi tahi ana rātou ko ngā tamariki.

Te whai wāhitanga me te whakahaeretanga o te whānau. E maia ana te whānau ki te hāpai ō rātou pūkenga ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo me te tautoko i te wā e ako ana, e tākaro rānei ana ngā tamariki. E whai hua ana te mahi tahi a te whānau, ā, e rikarika ana rātou ki tō rātou kōhanga reo. Te tokomaha hoki ka tae atu ki ngā hui ia wā ia wā, ā, ka whai wāhi rātou ki te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero me te whakatau i ngā take e hāngai ai ki a rātou tamariki. Ko te whakarite ngohe me te mahi tahi ki ngā tamariki te haepapa o ētahi mātua o te whānau. E whai take ana te mahi a tēnā matua me tēnā matua, ā, e whai koha ana tētahi ki tētahi te āhua o te mahi tahi a te whānau. Kua ūhia ngā kaimahi me te whānau e te wairua kōtahitanga me te mauritau. E ako ana ngā tamariki katoa ki tētahi taiao ako, ā, he whānui he hōhonu ngā wheako akoranga.

Ngā Wāhanga Hei Whakapai Ake Anō

Te whakapakari i ngā tukanga arotake whaiaro. E whai wāhi atu ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakarite i tētahi taiao kaupapa Māori he kounga nui te mātauranga, ā, kua āta tiakina ngā tamariki. Me haere tonu te mahi tahi a ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakawhanake, te whakatika me te whakawhānui i ngā tukanga arotake whaiaro hei whakapai ake i ngā whakaritenga me ngā āhuatanga rerekē ka puta mai.

4. Ngā Tūtohunga

E tūtohu ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia:

  • Haere tonu te whakawhanake, te whakatika me te whakawhānui i ngā tukanga arotake whaiaro hei whakapai ake i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga me ngā rerekētanga rānei i te hōtaka ako a ngā tamariki o ia rā.

5. Ngā Mahi ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

1. Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ngakau MahanaThis supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance ofgoverning body and management in relation to areas identified in the March 2011 ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • Mana Atua, including children’s resources, te reo Māori and health, safety and care
  • planning and evaluation, including strategic planning, self review, programme planning, programme evaluation and kaimahi roles and responsibilities
  • areas of non-compliance, including the kōhanga premises
  • any issues identified since the previous review.

2. The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

The children and whānau at Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ngakau Mahana have recently moved into a new building at Henderson High School, Auckland. Whānau and kaimahi speak positively about the new building which is in good repair and caters well for the large number of children who attend kōhanga reo each day. The issues that were raised in the previous ERO report in relation to health, safety and care, no longer apply.

This ERO report follows through on a number of issues that were identified in the March 2011 review report. These matters included children’s resources, te reo Māori and health, safety and care. Strategic planning, self review, programme planning, evaluation and kaimahi roles and responsibilities were raised as additional issues in the previous ERO report.

A number of staffing changes have taken place at the kōhanga reo. Most kaimahi including the two kaiako are new appointments. The kuia is the sole person who remains from the previous teaching team. The combined skills and interests of kaimahi, kaiako and kuia are used in effective ways to plan and evaluate different kaupapa, develop children’s te reo Māori skills and make learning exciting for children. Kaiako are confident, experienced staff. They provide clear direction and leadership to kaimahi.

The learning programme has structure, balance and is highly stimulating for children. Children have easy access to a wide range of resources and activities. Children are able to follow their interests, explore and participate in a range of activities that promote early literacy and numeracy. They participate in imaginative and creative play, and use different technologies. Te reo Māori underpins all these interactions and activities.

Children hear te reo Māori throughout the day. Kaimahi make language learning exciting for children through games, activities and the use of effective language strategies. The older children confidently use te reo in a range of formal learning contexts and when they lead different tikanga and routines. They are able to follow instructions and participate in simple to complex conversations. The younger children enjoy activities that include repetition and rhythm.

Whānau work well together and are highly supportive of kaimahi. Whānau use their skills to help manage the kōhanga reo and to assist with the daily programme. They regularly attend hui and share in decision making. The strategic plan is well written and has a strong focus on children’s learning and development, te reo Māori and staff professional development. Whānau and kaimahi are gaining confidence in self review. They have begun to monitor and report on various parts of the kōhanga reo operations. Whānau and kaimahi are aware that some areas of the kōhanga reo operations need still nee refinement.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

3. Findings

Mana Atua

Background

The previous ERO review report identified several issues relating to the children’s learning programme. Resourcing and activities did not adequately support children to be creative or imaginative. Children needed to be provided with greater challenges.

While kaimahi consistently used te reo Māori throughout the day, they needed to consider how they could extend children’s language through using more vocabulary and modelling phrases.

There were some health and safety practices that needed addressing. These included leakages on the verandah, supervision practices, the secure storage of outdoor equipment, the maintenance of the sandpit areas and the condition of the play area for the under two year olds.

The children and whānau now occupy new premises. The new kōhanga reo was once used as an early childhood centre. The kōhanga reo is still located at the Henderson High School.

There have been significant staffing changes at the kōhanga reo. The two new kaiako are experienced practitioners. They, along with the kuia, provide strong professional leadership and direction to kaimahi.

Areas of progress

Provision of suitable resources and activities. Kaimahi have created a highly effective and stimulating learning programme for children. Well resourced centres are established throughout the indoor and outdoor areas. There are opportunities for children to participate using different technologies, imaginative and creative play, early numeracy and literacy experiences, construction play and music. There are sufficient challenges to motivate boys, the able children and the different age groups. Children are highly creative in their play and are confident learners.

Te reo Māori support. The kaiako and kuia are competent and fluent speakers of te reo Māori. They are effective language models for kaimahi, whānau and children. Children confidently use te reo Māori during waiata, karakia, himene, pepeha, mihi and whakapapa. The older children have opportunities to take lead roles during routines and tikanga. They are able to understand instructions, directions and medium to complex phrases and conversations. Kaimahi use a range of strategies to help develop children’s language skills and vocabulary. Children are successfully immersed in a te reo Māori environment.

Health, safety and care. Whānau and kaimahi no longer face the health and safety issues that were raised in the previous ERO report. The children and whānau have moved to new premises which are still located at the Henderson High School. The new kōhanga reo premises are in good repair, spacious and well maintained by the school and whānau. Whānau and kaimahi maintain effective supervision practices in the indoor and outdoor areas. This helps safeguard children while they are at kōhanga reo.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

The previous ERO report identified issues with the management of the kōhanga reo. The whānau had not documented its annual strategic priorities or considered how they would respond to Te Ara Tūāpae. Further development of self review was required. While information was gathered and recorded from a number of sources, it was not analysed.

Programme planning and evaluation were areas that needed addressing. Kaimahi did not use annual, term or weekly plans to guide the learning programme. Plans lacked programme objectives and children’s activities. Programme evaluation was narrow in scope. There was limited discussion about the physical environment, kaupapa, routines, children’s activities and staffing matters.

Kaimahi did not have a clear understanding of their individual roles and responsibilities. The kaiako did not have the necessary leadership skills to guide kaimahi.

Areas of progress

Strategic planning. Whānau have clear expectations and aspirations to support the future interests of their children and kōhanga reo. They have worked in a strategic way to address several of the issues raised in the previous ERO report. Major priorities for whānau have included appointing new kaimahi and moving to a new kōhanga reo site. Whānau have identified new strategic priorities. These link to Te Ara Tūāpae and include te reo Māori, professional development, resource provisions, whānau learning and early numeracy and literacy. Strategic goals have a direct link to children’s learning, te reo Māori and the upskilling of kaimahi.

Self review. Whānau are gaining in confidence as they monitor and report on different parts of the kōhanga reo operations. Whānau and kaimahi use a reporting sheet to guide their reviews. Key areas of reporting include financial management, health and safety matters, different kaupapa, professional development, whānau concerns, policy reviews, te reo Māori and property management. Whānau are aware of what areas still need further development. They are working in a planned way to develop these areas.

Kaupapa planning. Kaimahi confidently plan kaupapa for children. Long and short term plans are comprehensive and provide clear direction to kaimahi when they deliver kaupapa to children. Plans have clear links to Te Whāriki, Te Korowai and the atua Māori. Whānau willingly contribute to planning. Children’s knowledge of the Maori world and te reo Māori is greatly enriched through well planned learning experiences.

Programme evaluation. Kaimahi use programme evaluation to bring about positive changes to children’s learning and development. They use informal and formal discussions to evaluate the effectiveness of the physical environment, kaupapa, outings, tuakana roles, routines and resource provisions. Kaimahi are aware there are space restrictions in relation to the office, nursery and some play areas. They are holding discussions to address these issues.

Kaimahi roles and responsibilities. Kaimahi are warm, enthusiastic and work well as a team. The majority of kaimahi and kaiako are new. They use their collective strengths in storytelling, drama, te reo Māori and music to develop and extend children’s learning and development. The kaiako provides effective leadership to kaimahi in planning, evaluation and te reo Māori. Kaimahi have employment contracts with job descriptions that outline their roles and responsibilities. Kaimahi are well organised and confidently carry out their roles when they are with children.

Whānau commitment and management. Whānau confidently use their skills to manage the kōhanga reo and support children as they play and learn. Whānau work well together and are highly enthusiastic about their new kōhanga reo. They regularly attend hui where they share in decisions that affect their children. Several whānau take responsibility for setting up activities and working alongside children. Whānau contributions are valued as meaningful and purposeful. There is a strong sense of unity and positivity among kaimahi and whānau. Children learn in an environment where their interests and learning is maximised.

Areas for further improvement

Strengthening self review processes. Whānau and kaimahi are committed to providing children with highly effective care and education in a kaupapa Māori environment. Whānau and kaimahi should continue to develop, refine and extend their self review processes to improve existing practices or as changes occur.

4. Recommendation

ERO and the whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • continue to develop, refine and extend self review processes to improve existing practices or any changes that may occur to the children’s daily programme or the kōhanga reo operations.

5. Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
