1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Ngā Oho Mata Kamo Kamo Te Kōhanga Reo o Korongata ki te hapori iti o Bridge Pā, ki te tonga o Heretaunga. Kei te marae o Korongata te kōhanga reo e tū ana, ā, he tino wāhi akoranga tēnei.

Mai i te arotake o mua, kua arotakengia, kua whakahoungia hoki e te whānau te katoa o ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. He whakaongaonga te taiao ako o te kōhanga reo, ā, he pai te whakahaeretanga. Ka manaakitia ngā tamariki ki te tino horopaki o te whānau.

Kua tāutuhia e te whānau ko te whakapakari i te reo Māori tētahi āhuatanga hei whakapai ake. He mahere rautaki tā rātou kia whai wāhi ai ngā mātua ki te ako i te reo Māori, kia piki ake hoki te mātau o ngā kaimahi.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ko whea rātou?

He māia ngā tamariki, ā, he kaha tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga, tō rātou tuakiri anō hoki.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka ako ngā tamariki ko whea rātou, ā, ka ako hoki rātou i ō rātou hononga ki te marae. Ka taki rātou i ngā pepeha, ngā waiata, me ngā mōteatea, ā, ka whai wāhi atu ki ngā kaupapa i te marae. Ka hāpai aua tikanga i te akoranga a ngā tamariki i ō rātou hononga ki ō rātou whānau, ō rātou hapū, tō rātou iwi, me te hapori. Ka tū manawa whakahī ngā tamariki i a rātou e whakapuaki ana ko whea rātou. Ka whakawhanakehia te māia o ngā tamariki mā te whakahono atu ki tō rātou marae. He kaha te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki.

Ko ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki ki te whānau me ngā kaumātua e poipoi ana i tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga me tō rātou tuakiri. He whakaute ngā hononga, ā, ka ako ngā tamariki i te whakahirahiratanga o te whanaungatanga. He mauritau, he māia anō hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo, ki te marae, ā, ki te hapori tonu.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao o te reo Māori e tino tautokona ana. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā pūkenga reo o ngā tamariki. Kei te māia haere ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori. Ka whakamanahia te tuakiri o ngā tamariki mā te reo Māori.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te tūhura i tō rātou taiao. He whai wāhitanga mā rātou ki te ako i te tū hei kaitiaki o te taiao. He muramura, he hihiri, he mahana hoki te kōhanga reo. Ka whai ngā tamariki i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga i roto tonu i te kōhanga reo, i ngā wāhi tākaro o waho anō hoki, ā, he nui ngā rauemi ki aua wāhi. He whai wāhitanga anō ngā haerenga mō ngā tamariki ki te whakawhānui ake i ō rātou mōhiotanga ki te ao. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki tētahi hōtaka akoranga e āta whakahaerehia ana, ā, e whakamana ana anō hoki i tō rātou ahurea, ā rātou taonga tuku iho, ā, e hāpai ana i te puāwaitanga o ō rātou ngākau nuitanga.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kua rite ngā tamariki kia whakawhānuihia, kia whakawerohia anō hoki tā rātou whakamahinga i te reo Māori. E mōhio ana te whānau, e tika ana kia hāngai ake ai te titiro ki te ako reo o ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

E tautokona ana ngā tamariki e ngā tukanga me ngā pūnaha tino pakari i roto i ngā whakahaeretanga. Kei te whānau te whānui o ngā tūmomo kaupapa here e hāpai ana i te waiora ā-whatumanawa, ā-tinana hoki o ngā tamariki. Aroturukitia ai ngā rehita hauora, haumaru hoki i ia te wā. He mahere rautaki tā te whānau, ā, e hāngai ana tēnei ki Te Ara Tūāpae, e whai pānga ana hoki tae atu ki te tau 2019. Kua whakawhanakehia tētahi mahere ā-tau e hono ana ki ngā whāinga rautaki, ā, e mau nei i te kiko o ngā whakamārama e pā ana ki ngā kaupapa matua a te whānau, tae atu ki te huarahi ka whāia, te wā e tika ana mō aua kaupapa, me rātou ka kawe i aua āhuatanga. Kua waihangatia ngā tukanga a te whānau hei whakapai ake i ngā putanga ako mō ngā tamariki. Ka kitea te arotake whaiaro puta noa i te kōhanga reo.

Ka tino tautokona te ako a ngā tamariki, nā te pai o te whakamahere me te aromātai hoki i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whai hua ngā mahere hōtaka ki te ārahi i te whakapuakitanga o te hōtaka. Ka tuhia te aromātai hōtaka ki ngā meneti a ngā kaimahi, ā, ka pūrongohia ki ngā hui ā te whānau. Ka whakamahia ngā whakaaturanga ka puta i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, hei whakatairanga i te hōtaka akoranga.

Ko ngā mōhiohio aromatawai e whakatakoto ana i te pai o ngā mōhiohio mā te whānau, mō te ako a ngā tamariki, tā rātou ahunga whakamua, me ngā whakatutukitanga.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He whāiti te whai wāhi a te whānau ki ngā āhuatanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. Kāhore e tino māramatia ana ngā kawenga a te whānau i whakaraupapa ai ki Te Korowai.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Ngā Oho Mata Kamo Kamo Te Kōhanga Reo o Korongata ki tētahi hapori iti o Bridge Pā, ki te tonga o Heretaunga. E manaakitia ana ngā tamariki o Ngā Oho Mata Kamo Kamo Te Kōhanga Reo o Korongata ki tētahi taiao e aroha ana, e tautoko ana hoki i a rātou. He māia ngā tamariki, ā, he kaha tō rātou tuakiri me tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga. Tērā ka whakahaerehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Oho Mata Kamo Kamo Te Kōhanga Reo o Korongata i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

26 Poutū-te-rangi, 2015

1 Context

Ngā Oho Mata Kamo Kamo Te Kōhanga Reo o Korongata is in the small settlement of Bridge Pā, south of Hastings. The kōhanga reo is on the Korongata Marae which provides a valuable context for learning.

Since the previous review whānau have reviewed and updated all health and safety policies and procedures. The kōhanga reo learning environment is stimulating and well managed. Children are cared for in a strong whānau setting.

The whānau has identified strengthening te reo Māori as an area for improvement. They have a strategic plan in place to engage the parents in learning te reo Māori and increase the competency of kaimahi.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children know about who they are?

Children are confident and have a strong sense of belonging and identity.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children learn about where they come from and their marae connections. They recite pepeha, waiata and mōteatea and they participate in marae activities. These protocols help children to learn about their connections to their whānau, hapū, iwi and community. Children stand with pride when they share where they come from. Children’s confidence is developed through interaction with their marae. Children have a strong sense of belonging.

Children’s interactions with whānau and kaumātua foster their sense of belonging and identity. Relationships are respectful and children learn the importance of whanaungatanga. Children are self assured and content in the kōhanga reo, marae and local community.

The children are immersed in a supportive te reo Māori environment. Kaimahi use various strategies to help develop children’s language skills. Children use te reo Māori with increasing confidence. Children’s identity is affirmed through te reo Māori.

Children enjoy exploring their environment. They have opportunities to learn about being kaitīaki of the environment. The kōhanga reo is a bright, vibrant and inviting place. Children follow their own interests inside and in the outdoor play areas which are well resourced. Excursions are further opportunities for children to increase their knowledge of the world. Children participate in a well managed learning programme that affirms their culture, heritage and supports their emerging interests.

Key Next Steps

The children are ready to be extended and challenged in their use of te reo Māori. The whānau acknowledge the need to provide greater focus on language learning for children.

Planning and Evaluation

Children are supported by robust management processes and systems. Whānau have a range of policies that support children’s emotional and physical wellbeing. Health and safety registers are regularly monitored. The whānau has a strategic plan which aligns to Te Ara Tūāpae and extends to 2019. An annual plan which links to the strategic goals has been developed and includes what, how, when and who will have responsibility for the whānau priorities. Whānau processes are designed to improve learning outcomes for children. Self review is evident throughout the kōhanga reo.

Children’s learning is well supported by good planning and evaluation of the learning programme. Programme planning successfully guides the delivery of the programme. Programme evaluation is recorded in the kaimahi minutes and reported in the whānau meetings. Findings from the discussions are used to enhance the learning programme.

Assessment information provides whānau with good information about children’s learning, progress and achievement.

Key Next Steps

Whānau participation in all aspects of the kōhanga reo is limited. Whānau responsibilities as outlined in Te Korowai are not well understood.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Conclusion

Ngā Oho Mata Kamo Kamo Te Kōhanga Reo o Korongata is in the small settlement of Bridge Pā, south of Hastings. The children of Ngā Oho Mata kamo Kamo Te Kōhanga Reo o Korongata are cared for in a loving and supportive environment. Children are confident and have a strong sense of identity and belonging. ERO is likely to return in three years.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Ngā Oho Mata Kamo Kamo Te Kōhanga Reo o Korongata will be be in three years

Lynda Pura-Watson

Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori
