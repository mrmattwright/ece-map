1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kei Whaingaroa te Kōhanga Reo o Whaingaroa e tū ana. Kei te whare hapori o Kōkiri te kōhanga reo. E whakamahia ana e ngā kaimahi me te whanau ngā rawa o mataora me ō rātou mātauranga mō ngā atua Māori hei whakamanawa i te whakaaro kawe haepapa ki ngā tamariki me ō rātou mōhio me pēhea rawa te tū hei kaitiaki o te whenua.

He tōtika rawa ngā moemoeā ō te whānau me ngā kaimahi mō ā rātou tamariki. Ko te whakaako i ngā tamariki ō rātou tūranga hei kaitiaki mō te whenua, ō rātou tū pakari rawa hei Māori me ō rātou tū maia mō ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki ō rātou tuakiri. Ko tētahi whāinga anō tō te whānau me ngā kaimahi he whakapakari ake i te ngākau hiahia o ngā tamariki ki te ako. Kei te whakanui ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ō rātou tūranga hei kaitiaki me ō rātou mātauranga e pā ana ki a rātou anō, arā, ko wai rātou, ā, nō hea anō hoki rātou.

He hōtaka akoranga hīkaka tō ngā tamariki, ā, kua whakautetia rātou hei ākonga mātau. He mea whai take te āhua o te hōtaka, ā, kua tino whakahaeretia te hōtaka e ngā kaimahi me ngā mātua o te whānau. E ako ana ngā tamariki ki te whai koha ki ngā tāngata katoa me te ao o mataora. E whakapā ana ki ā rātou ngā whanaungatanga māhana, ngā taunekeneke aroha o tētahi ki tētahi me te tiaki. E tino mōhio ana, ā, ka tino whai haere rātou i te takamahi o ia rā. E ako ana ngā tamariki i ngā āhuatanga o tō rātou tuakiri hei Māori me ō rātou mana motuhake i roto i tō rātou whānau me te hapori. E harikoa ana ngā tamariki, ā, e noho mauritau ana rātou i te kōhanga reo.

Kua tino tautokotia ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi te whakamahi me te ako i te reo Māori. Ka whakaputa ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki i te reo Māori i te rā katoa. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi, ā, ka whakamahi i te reo ā-tinana hei tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te mau i te reo, ā, he mea whai hua ēnei rautaki.

Kei te tino whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Kua arahina te whānau e ngā pūnaha whai hua, ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā. He maha ō rātou whāinga hei ārahi i te ahunga whakamua o te kōhanga reo. Kua tautokotia ēnei whāinga me ētahi tūmahi hei āwhina i a rātou ki te whakatutuki i ō rātou whāinga.

Kua tino whakatūria ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro. Ka kitea ngā pūrongo me ngā whakahaere aroturuki ia wā, ia wā mō ngā take huhua. He whakangungu i ngā kaimahi me ngā akoranga a ngā kaimahi tētahi wāhanga o ngā whakahaere arotake. Ka aroturuki te whānau i ō rātou paetae mō te whakatutuki i ngā whāinga i roto i te māhere rautaki. Kei te arotahi te whānau ki ngā tino wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mokohuia o te Rautahi kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

He whakamārama

He pēhea rawa te pai o te tautoko ā ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te ako i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te kaitiakitanga me ō rātou tuakiri?

Kei te tautokotia ngā tamariki mā ngā rautaki whai tikanga kua whārikitia e ngā kaimahi hei ako i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki ō rātou tuakiri me ō rātou tūranga hei kaitiaki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te hōtaka akoranga. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou mātauranga mō ō rātou tūranga hei kaitiaki. He hōtaka akoranga hīkaka tō ngā tamariki, ā, he ākonga māia rātou i te mea e whai koha ana ngā kaimahi ki a rātou i ngā wā katoa. He tino pai ngā whakaritenga me te whakahaere o te hōtaka e ngā kaimahi me ngā mātua o te whānau. E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki te torotoro atu ki te taiao o mataora, ā, he wāhi akoranga tēnei mā rātou. Ko ngā atua Māori tētahi tino wāhanga o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. E ako ana ngā tamariki i ngā kaupapa whai take o ngā atua Māori me ā rātou tūrangi hei tautoko me te poipoi i ā rātou anō ia rā, ia rā. E ako ana rātou ki te whai koha ki ngā tāngata me te whai koha anō hoki ki te taiao o mataora. Kua whakatipua te manawanui o ngā tamariki ki te ao o mataora, ā, ka noho rātou ki ēnei wāhi ki te whakapau wā me te tākaro. E whakapā ana ki ā rātou ngā whanaungatanga māhana, ngā taunekeneke aroha o tētahi ki tētahi me te tiaki. E mōhio ana, ā, ka whai atu ngā tamariki i te takamahi o ia rā, ā, kua tino whakatutukitia ngā matea o ngā tamariki. E noho harikoa ana, ā, e mauritau ana ngā tamariki i te kōhanga reo.

Te tuakiri. Kua tino tautokotia ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi ki te ako i ngā āhuatanga mō ō rātou tuakiri hei Māori me ō rātou tūranga mana motuhake i roto i tō rātou whānau me te hapori. E ako ana ngā tamariki i ngā kōrero o neherā mō te whenua mā te whakaputa i ngā karakia, ngā pātere me ngā mōteatea. E ako ana rātou i ngā āhuatanga o te whakapapa me ō rātou tūhonohono ki ngā tāngata huri noa i a rātou. E piki haere ana te mātauranga o ngā tamariki e pā ana ki ō rātou tuakiri, arā, ko wai rātou, ā, nō hea rātou.

Te ako i te reo Māori.Kua tino tautokotia ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi te ako me te whakaputa i te reo Māori i te rā katoa. E whakahaere ana ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki whai hua hei tautoko i te whanaketanga o ngā tamariki ki te mau i te reo. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi, ā, ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te reo ā-tinana hei tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te mau i te reo, ā, he mea tino whai hua ēnei rautaki. He whakarite i ngā akoranga harikoa te mahi a ngā kaimahi. Kua whakamihia, ā, kua akiakitia ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi kia tipu ai rātou hei kaikōrero o te reo Māori.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea rawa te pai o ngā whakahaere a te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki?

He tino pai ngā whakahaere a te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Ngāwāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ngā whakahaere a te whānau.He whai painga te āhua o ngā whakahaere a te whanau nā tō rātou ū ki te tiaki i ngā tamariki. E arahina ana e te whanau ngā kaupapa here whai tikanga me ngā tukanga o ia rā mō ngā tino wāhanga whakahaere o te kōhanga reo.

He whai tikanga teāhua o ngā whakahaere a ngā mātua o te whanau i ngā tūranga mahi o ngā kaimahi.Kua whakatūria tētahi pūnaha hei aromātai i ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi. Kua aromātaia ngā kaimahi katoa, ā, he tino pai ngā āhuatanga o te pūnaha aromātai kaimahi.

He whāinga maha ō te whānau hei arahi i te kōhanga reo mō ngā rangi ā meāke nei. Kua tāututia e rātou ngā tūmahi hei whakahaere ki te whakatutuki i ō rātou whāinga. Ahakoa he mahi ā rātou anō ki te whakatū i tētahi māhere whakatutuki kua whai whakaaro te whānau ki te whiriwhiri i ngā rautaki hei tautoko i a rātou mō ngā rangi ā meāke nei.

Kei te tino matawhāiti te whānau i te pūtea whakahaere o te kōhanga reo. He tōtika te āhua o ngā whakahaere pūtea, ā, kei te ora rawa te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo. Kua oti ngā pūrongo o te kaitātaki pūtea, ā, e whai wāhi ana te whānau ki te tirotiro i ēnei pūrongo. Kua whakawhanaketia ngā tahua pūtea, ā, e tino hāngai ana te tahua pūtea ki te whakarato rauemi me ngā taputapu mā ngā tamariki. Ka mahi ngātahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakatau take hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Te arotake whaiaro.Kua tino whakatūria te arotake whaiaro. Ka whakaputa ngā pūrongo ia wā, ia wā, ā, ka whakahaeretia ngā tukanga aroturuki ia wā, ia wā mō ngā whakaritenga o ia rā i te kōhanga reo. Ka arotahi ngā whakaritenga arotake ki ngā whakangungu me ngā akoranga mā ngā kaimahi. Ka aroturuki te whānau i ngā paetae hei whakatutuki i ngā whāinga nui o te māhere rautaki. Te kaha hoki o te whānau ki te arotahi ki ngā mahi hei whakapai ake anō.

Te aromatawai.He tino pai ngā kaimahi ki te ine i te huarahi paetae me ngā whanaketanga o ngā tamariki. Ka tāutu ngā pukapuka paetae he aha ngā akoranga mō te wā kua whakaritea. Ka kitea ngā paetae o ia tamariki mā te whānui o ngā mōhiohio kua kohia, ā, me ngā mōhiohio e whakaaturia ai ō rātou tipuranga ako. E hāngai ana ēnei ki ngā whakaahua o ngā tamariki i te wā e tākaro ana rātou me ngā kōrero whakamārama kua tuhia. E tino whai māramatanga ngā mātua o te whānau mō ngā paetae me ngā whanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te hōtaka whakamāherehere.Kāore anō te hōtaka whakamāherehere kia tino whakatūria. He iti noa ngā kōrero i roto i ngā whakamāherehere, ā, kāore ngā whakamāherehere i te tino ārahi i ngā kaimahi ki te whakatinana i te hōtaka. Me nui ake ngā kōrero whai take i roto i ngā māhere.

Te hōtaka aromātai.Kāore te hōtaka aromātai i te whai hua me te āwhina i ngā kaimahi ki te whakapai ake i ngā wāhanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Kāore ngā kaimahi i te arotake i te hōtaka ki te tāutu i ngā mahi e whai hua ana, i ngā mahi rānei hei whakapai ake anō kia pai ake te whai wāhitanga, te whakaihiihi me te whakahihiko o ngā tamariki ki te ako. Me nui ake te arotahi a ngā kaimahi ki ngā rautaki hei whakapai ake i te whai painga o te hōtaka akoranga.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakiia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o okiuh, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Tūtohunga

E tūtohu ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga:

  • ki te whānau ki te whakawhanake i tētahi māhere whakatutuki hei ārahi i a rātou ki te tohatoha i ngā rawa, te whakatau i ngā wā aukati, te tāutu i ngā haepapa me te whakatau i ngā putanga aroturuki hei whakatutuki i ngā whāinga o te māhere rautaki

  • ki te whānau ki te whakapai ake anō i te kounga o te hōtaka whakamāherehere me te hōtaka aromātai.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

Paenga-whāwhā 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Whaingaroa is in Raglan. The kōhanga reo is located in the Kōkiri Centre. The staff and whānau use the natural environment and their knowledge about ngā atua Māori to instil in children a sense of responsibility including how to act as kaitiaki of the land.

The whānau and staff members have clear aspirations for their children. These include teaching children their roles as kaitiaki of the land, standing strong as Māori and being confident about their identity. Whānau and staff members also aim to build children’s enthusiasm for learning. Staff promote and reinforce to children their role as kaitiaki and knowledge about who they are and where they come from.

Children have a stimulating learning programme where they are respected as competent learners. The programme is purposeful and well managed by staff and whānau members. Children learn to respect people and the natural environment. Children experience warm, loving and caring interactions and relationships. They know and follow routines well. Children learn about their identity as Māori and as members of their whānau and community. Children are happy and settled at the kōhanga reo.

Children are effectively supported to use and learn te reo Māori. Staff members and children consistently use te reo Māori throughout the day. Staff members model te reo Māori for children, make learning fun and use body language to good effect.

The kōhanga reo is well managed in the interests of children. The whānau is guided by sound systems, policies and procedures. They have a range of goals to guide the future direction of the kōhanga reo. These are supported with well defined actions to help achieve their goals.

Self review is well established. There is regular reporting and monitoring for a variety of purposes. Review practices include a focus on professional development and learning for staff. The whānau monitors their progress toward meeting the goals in the strategic plan. The whānau is focussed on improvements that will have positive impact on their children.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning; and
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

How effectively are children supported by staff to learn about kaitiakitanga and their identity?

Children are supported in positive ways by staff to learn about their identity and their role as kaitiaki.

Areas of good performance

The learning programme.Children have a developing knowledge about their role as kaitiaki. Children have a stimulating learning programme where they are respected as competent learners. The programme is well managed and organised by staff and whānau members. Children have access to the natural environment which they use as their learning space. Ngā atua Māori feature significantly in children’s learning. Children learn about the importance of atua Māori in their daily lives. They learn to respect people and the natural environment. Children have developed a close affinity with the natural world where they spend time and play. They experience warm, loving and caring interactions and relationships. Children know and follow routines and their needs are well cared for. Children are happy and settled at the kōhanga reo.

Identity.Children are supported in positive ways to learn about their identity as Māori and as members of their whānau and community. Children learn about the history of the land through karakia, pātere and mōteatea. They learn about whakapapa and how they are connected to people around them. Children have a developing knowledge of who they are and where they come from.

Te reo Māori learning.Children are effectively supported to learn and to use te reo Māori throughout the day. Staff members use a range of effective language learning techniques to support children’s language development. Staff model and use body language to good effect. They make learning fun. Children are praised and encouraged to develop as speakers of te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How well is the kōhanga reo managed in the interests of children?

The kōhanga is well managed in the interests of children.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management.Whānau manage the kōhanga reo in positive ways because of their commitment to children. The whānau is guided by sound policies and procedures for key areas of the kōhanga reo operations.

Whānau members manage staff performance appropriately. An appraisal system is in place. All staff have been appraised and the system is working well.

The whānau has a range of goals to guide the future direction of the kōhanga reo. They have defined the actions they will take to accomplish their goals. While there remains work to be done to complete an action plan the whānau takes a considered approach to their future direction.

The whānau manages the kōhanga reo funds in a prudent manner. Financial management is transparent and the kōhanga reo is in a sustainable position. Audit reports are complete and made known to whānau. Budgets are developed that focus on the provision of resources and equipment for children. Whānau and staff members make decisions based on what is best for their children.

Self review.Self review is well established. There is regular reporting and monitoring of kōhanga operations. Review practices focus on professional development and learning for staff. The whānau monitors the progress toward meeting the goals of the strategic plan. The whānau is focused on improvement.

Assessment.Staff members effectively track children’s progress and development. Profile books clearly identify what learning has occurred for children, over time. Progress for each child is made evident through the variety of information collected and presented. These include photos of children at play and written narratives. Whānau members are well informed about children’s progress and development.

Areas for development

Programme planning.Programme planning is not well developed or understood. Planning lacks detail and does not effectively guide staff members when they implement the programme. More specific detail is required in plans.

Programme evaluation.Programme evaluation is not effective in helping staff to make improvements to the learning programme. Staff do not critique the programme to define what is working well or what needs to be improved to better engage, stimulate and motivate children to learn. Staff need to focus more on how they can improve the effectiveness of the learning programme.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement Self-Audit ChecklistBefore the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an EROand. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends that:

  • the whānau develop an action plan to help them to allocate resources, set timeframes, define responsibilities, and set monitoring expectations for achieving the goals of the strategic plan
  • improve the quality of programme planning and evaluation.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
