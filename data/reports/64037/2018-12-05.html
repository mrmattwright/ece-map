1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Toka Āhuru ki Porirua, ki Te Whanganui-a-Tara. He Māori, he tangata rānei nō te Moana-nui-a-Kiwa te nuinga o ngā tāngata o te hapori nei. Ko te whai hononga ki te hapori tētahi āhuatanga whakaharahara ki te whānau. Kua roa nei te nuinga o ngā kaimahi, ngā kuia, me ngā koroua e whai wāhi atu ana ki te kōhanga reo. Mai i te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2015, kua aro atu te whānau ki te whakatutuki haere i ngā take i hāngai ai ki te whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo. I roto i te tau kua pahure ake nei, kua āwhina te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te whakatinana i ngā pūnaha me ngā tukanga e whakapai ake ai i ngā whakaritenga o ngā pūtea me te whakahaeretanga o te tari. E mōhio ana te whānau, ko te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga me ngā rawa ētahi āhuatanga hei whakawhanake ake.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga?

E whakaatu ana ētahi o ngā tamariki i tō rātou hiahia ki te tūhono, ki te tūhura, me te whakatūturu i tō rātou whai wāhi atu hei ākonga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E whanake ana te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou whai wāhi nui ki te whānau. Kua whakatakotohia ngā tikanga o ia rā, pērā i ngā karakia, i ngā mihimihi, me ngā pepeha. Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tuākana te pakari haere o ō rātou ngākau titikaha, ō rātou āheinga hoki ki te whakatutuki takitahi i aua tikanga, me te iti noa anō hoki o ngā tohutohu me te tautoko o ngā kaimahi. E ākina ana, e whakanuia ana hoki ngā tamariki tēina mō ā rātou whakamātau. Ka hora ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te parakuihi mā ngā tamariki e hiahia ana, hei whakariterite i a rātou ki te ako. Ka ngākau nui ia tamaiti ki taua wā ki te taha o tō rātou whānau. He pārekareka ngā wāhanga ōkawa o te hōtaka akoranga ki te nuinga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki e āta hāpaitia ana. Kāhore ngā taumahi reo e poipoi ana i ngā tūmomo reanga katoa o ngā tamariki. Ahakoa ka hui tahi te katoa mō ngā akoranga ōkawa pērā i ngā mihimihi me ngā pepeha, kāhore ngā tamariki katoa e whai wāhi mātātoa ana i aua wā. Kāhore anō kia pūmau ngā tūmanako kia kōrerohia te reo Māori i te hōtaka o ia rā, o ia rā. Me noho matua ki te kōhanga reo, ko te aronga ake a te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakapūmau i te whakamahinga o te reo Māori, puta noa i te hōtaka akoranga.

Kāhore te taiao e āta whakatakotohia ana hei whakatairanga i te kounga o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He iti noa ngā wāhi ako ā-kaupapa ka whakaritea mā ngā tamariki. Kāhore te nuinga o ngā rauemi ako e noho wātea ana ki ngā tamariki. He iti noa ngā mahi a ngā tamariki e whakaatuhia ana ki te kōhanga reo, hei hāpai i te rongo o ngā tamariki i te whai huatanga o ā rātou akoranga, i tā rātou whai hononga hoki ki a rātou akoranga. He iti rawa, he tīwekaweka hoki te rūma moe i ngā takapau, i taonga ā-whare tawhito, i ngā rauemi e raua ki reira. He tini rawa anō hoki ngā taonga ā-whare me ngā taputapu e whakawhāiti ana i ngā wāhi o roto, o waho tonu o ngā whare o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakapuakihia e te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, e tika ana kia kounga te taiao ako o ngā tamariki, engari kāhore anō taua āhuatanga kia whakatutukihia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Ko te kounga o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki tētahi āhuatanga hei whakawhanake ake. E whakamahi ana ngā kaimahi i Te Whāriki a Te Kōhanga Reo hei hāpai i te whakamaheretanga o te hōtaka, heoi, e mōhio ana hoki rātou, tērā anō ētahi atu mahi tonu mā rātou ki tēnei āhuatanga, kia tupu ake ai ā rātou whakaritenga. E aro atu ana te titiro a ngā kaimahi ki te whakapiki ake i ō rātou māramatanga e pā ana ki ngā hononga o ngā mahere, ki te pūtake o te aromātai hōtaka, ā, ki ngā huarahi kia whāia ki te whakaemi me te whakamahi i ngā mōhiohio ka whakapai ake i te ako a ngā tamariki. Kāhore ngā matea ako o ia tamaiti e whakaarohia ana.

Kāhore ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo e āta whakahaerehia ana e te whānau. Kua tautoko tonu, kua ārahi tonu te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i te whānau, hei whakapiki ake i tō rātou māramatanga ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. Me whai wāhi mātātoa ngā mema o te whānau, kia pakari ai te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. E hāngai ana tēnei ki te taetae mai i ia te wā ki ngā hui ā-whānau, te whai wāhi atu ki ngā whakataunga, ki te arotake me te whakahou ake i ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga o te tūtohinga, ki te whakamahere rautaki, me te aroturuki i te ahu whakamua o ngā kaupapa matua a te whānau. Kāhore anō ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga arohaehae kia whakatinanahia mā ngā kaimahi katoa. Kāhore te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga e āta tautokona ana e te whānau, i runga anō i tēnā e whai pānga nui ana ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

I te wā o te arotake, i tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture.

Kia tutuki i te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā herenga ā-ture, me tahuri rātou ki te:

  • whakapau kaha ki te whakatika haere, te tiaki hoki i ia te wā i ngā whare, ngā rauhanga, me ngā taputapu i te kōhanga reo, ā, me whai pānga anō hoki aua taonga ki ā rātou whakaritenga, me noho mōrearea kore anō hoki.
    [R45 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS1, PF4, 5, 8, 12 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • āta whakarite i te hāngaitanga o ngā taonga ā-whare moe me ngā taputapu moe o ngā tamariki ki te rahi e tika ana kia toropapa te takoto, ā, kua hangaia anō hoki kia haumaru mō rātou
    [R45 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS10, PF29-31 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • āta whakarite i te noho mōhio o ngā pakeke i te kōhanga reo ki ngā akoako hōneatanga e tika ana, ā, kia whakatutukihia aua akoako i ia te wā ki te taha o ngā tamariki, ā, kia tuhia anō hoki aua wā akoako
    [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS8 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakapau kaha ki te whakarato ki ngā kaimahi katoa e mahi ana, e whai wāhi ana rānei ki te kōhanga reo, te rawaka o te tautoko mā te tū auau o ngā mahi arohaehae i ia te wā
    [R47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA7 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • āta whakarite i te hāngaitanga o ngā mahi marautanga ki te marautanga i whakatakoto kētia ai mō ngā kōhanga reo, ā, kia whakamōhiotia hoki ki ngā mahere, te whakatinanatanga, me ngā mahi aromātai e tohu ana i te māramatanga ki te ako a te mokopuna, ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, ō rātou whānau, me te āhua anō hoki o ō rātou ake ao.
    [R43 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; C2-4 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • toro atu tonu te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki ngā tohutohu me te ārahitanga o te tari ā-rohe o te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, kia pakari ai te whakatutuki haere i ngā whakaritenga whai muri i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Toka Āhuru i roto ngā tau e rua.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Toka Āhuru is in Porirua, Wellington, in a largely Māori and Pacific community. This community connection is important to the whānau. Most kaimahi, kuia and koroua have been involved with the kōhanga reo for a long period of time. Since the previous ERO report in 2015, the whānau have been attending to issues related to the management and operations of the kōhanga reo. In the past year, Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) have helped to implement systems and processes to improve financial and administrative practices. The whānau acknowledge that the quality of learning programme and property remain areas for development.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children show a strong sense of belonging?

Some children show an interest to connect, explore and affirm their place as learners.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are developing an understanding about their place in the whānau. Daily routines such as karakia, mihimihi and pepeha are in place. Older children show emerging confidence and ability to carry out these routines independently and with minimal direction and support from kaimahi. Younger children are encouraged and praised for their efforts. Kaimahi and whānau offer breakfast for children as needed to help them prepare for learning. Individual children enjoy this time with their whānau. Most children enjoy the formal learning programme.

Key Next Steps

Children’s language development is not well supported. Language activities do not cater for the different age groups of children. Although whole group learning is used for formal times for mihimihi and pepeha, not all children actively participate in these activities. There is a lack of consistent expectations to speak te reo Māori in the daily programme. A stronger focus for whānau and kaimahi to maintain the use of te reo Māori throughout the learning programme must be a priority in the kōhanga reo.

The physical environment is not well organised to enhance the quality of children’s learning. There are few kaupapa learning areas set up for children. Most learning resources are not easily accessed by children. There is little work by children displayed in the kōhanga reo so that children feel valued and connected to their learning. The sleeping room is small and untidy with mattresses, old furniture and resources stored in the same space. There is also a significant amount of unnecessary furniture and items that take up space both inside and outside of the kōhanga reo buildings. TKRNT have highlighted the need to create a quality learning environment for children, however this has not yet been addressed by the whānau and kaimahi.

Planning and Evaluation

Key Next Steps

Children’s quality of learning is an area of development. Kaimahi use Te Whāriki ā Te Kōhanga Reo to support programme planning and acknowledge that further work is needed to grow their practice in this area. Kaimahi are working towards increasing their understanding about the links of planning, the purpose of programme evaluation, and how to gather and use assessment information to improve children’s learning. Children’s individual learning needs are not considered.

The operations of the kōhanga reo are not well managed by the whānau. TKRNT have provided ongoing support and guidance for whānau to increase their understanding of the kaupapa of kōhanga reo. Active participation by whānau members is needed to ensure the management of the kōhanga reo is robust. This includes regular attendance at whānau hui, contributing to decision making, reviewing and updating charter policies and procedures, strategic planning and monitoring progress towards whānau priorities. Appraisal policies and procedures have not been implemented for all kaimahi. Children’s learning and development is not effectively supported by whānau in line with the kaupapa of kōhanga reo.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

ERO identified areas of non-compliance.

In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements, they need to:

  • take all reasonable steps to ensure that the premises, facilities and other equipment on those premises are kept in good repair, maintained regularly, suitable for the intended purposes, and kept free from hazards
    [R45 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS1, PF4,5,8,12 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • furniture and items intended for children to sleep on are of a size that allows children using them to lie flat, and are of a design to ensure their safety
    [R45 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS10, PF29-31 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • adults providing education and care are familiar with relevant emergency drills, that these are carried out regular with children and records kept of these drills
    [R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS8 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • all reasonable steps are taken to provide staff employed or engaged in the service have adequate support through a regular cycle of appraisal
    [R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA7 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • ensure that curriculum practice is consistent with the prescribed curriculum for kōhanga reo and is informed by planning, implementation, and evaluation that demonstrates an understanding of mokopuna learning, their interests, whānau, and life contexts.
    [R43 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; C2-4 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

the whānau and kaimahi continue to seek advice and guidance from the local TKRNT to actively address the key next steps identified in this report.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Toka Āhuru will be within two years.
