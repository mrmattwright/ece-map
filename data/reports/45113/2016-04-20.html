1 Evaluation of Dorie Community Preschool

Very well placed

Dorie Community Preschool is well placed to promote positive outcomes for children.

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Dorie Community Preschool is owned and operated by the community. The centre provides good quality education and care for infants, toddlers and children to school age. It is located next to the primary school.

Children attend the centre from a wide rural area. An increasing number of children are from a diverse range of cultures.

There has been a number of staff changes since the last ERO review. Most staff are qualified early childhood teachers. The centre is governed by a skilled, elected parent board.

The centre has made good progress in addressing most areas for further development identified in the 2013 ERO report. This includes increasing the visibility of bicultural practice in the learning programmes, strengthening self review and improving assessment and planning processes.

The Review Findings

Children are settled and confidently make choices about their play. Teachers are strongly focused on fostering children's wellbeing and social skills.

Children have good access to a broad range of well-presented resources to support their play. Centre leaders and teachers have designed the outdoor area to promote a range of physical challenges for children. Teachers make good use of the centre vegetable garden, fruit trees and plantings to encourage children to make healthy choices about their eating.

Teachers support children to find their own solutions to challenges. They regularly assess children's learning and plan a learning programme to support their emerging interests. Children have many opportunities to develop their ideas and extend their thinking.

Children experience caring, positive relationships with each other and their teachers. Children under two years of age, experience peaceful, unhurried routines with familiar teachers. They have many opportunities to explore their well-resourced indoor and outdoor environments. Teachers readily share information with parents in a range of effective ways. They are sensitive to children's needs and interests. Teachers show a deep understanding of their teaching philosophy in their practice.

Teachers have positive relationships with local schools. They provide many opportunities for children to be well prepared for school as part of the learning programme.

Children with diverse needs, including English as a second language, are very well supported by teachers and the board. Centre leaders and teachers provide effective support to families and parents with English as another language as they become part of the centre.

Transitions into and within the centre are well planned to meet the individual needs of children and families.

Centre leaders and teachers create strong partnerships with parents through regular communication. Parents are well informed about their children's learning and development. They have a range of opportunities to contribute to children's learning and the operation of the centre. The annual parent survey provides centre leaders with good information about parent satisfaction.

The centre manager provides clear direction and supports staff to improve their teaching practice. Self review is becoming well established and promotes positive changes to teaching and centre practices.

Governance and management roles and responsibilities are well defined. The board is currently updating its strategic and annual action plans. It is strongly committed to the success of the centre and provides appropriate support to the centre manager.

Key Next Steps

Centre leaders, and ERO agree, that the next key steps for the centre include:

  • extending on bicultural practices to ensure MƒÅori children have a greater sense of belonging, identity and language

  • reviewing teacher led practices in the programme to increase opportunities for children to make more decisions in their learning

  • seeking external expertise to better support teachers' practice and communication with children and families with English as a second language

  • strengthening self review

  • monitoring implementation of the new strategic and annual plans

  • ensuring teachers' appraisal goals are robust and linked to the centre's strategic direction and personal professional development.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Dorie Community Preschool completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum

  • premises and facilities

  • health and safety practices

  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)

  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)

  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)

  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Next ERO Review

When is ERO likely to review the service again?

The next ERO review of Dorie Community Preschool will be in three years.
