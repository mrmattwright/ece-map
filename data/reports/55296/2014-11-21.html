1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ko Kauaheihei te maunga

Ko Kaikoura te awa

Ko Pukehou te marae

Ko Ngāti Whatuiāpiti te hapū

Ko Ngāti Kahungunu te iwi

Ko Tākitimu te waka

Ko Te Otane te kōhanga reo.

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ōtane ki te tāone iti, tuawhenua hoki o Otane, ki te Matau-ā-Māui. He hapori tūhono piri tō rātou me te mea hoki he taiohi tonu te nuinga o te whānau i te kōhanga reo. Ū te wā o tēnei aromātai i hiahia te whānau ki te mōhio ki te āhua o te ako, te whakawhanaketanga hoki o ā rātou tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Ko tā rātou tino pātai e pēnei ana:

He pēhea te ako o ā rātou tamariki i tō rātou tuakiri, ā rātou taonga tuku iho hoki?

He pai te mōhio haere o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou tuakiri me ā rātou taonga tuku iho. He harikoa rātou, ā, he pārekareka ki a rātou te noho ki te kōhanga reo. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki ngā wāhanga ōkawa o te hōtaka akoranga ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori mā ngā tūmomo horopaki whānui. Ka tino whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga e pā ana ki ō rātou ahurea.

He pai te hononga ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. He tūāpapa tēnei mō ngā akoranga e pā ana ki te whanaungatanga. Nā te whai wāhi mai o te kuia ka tau mai te mārie, te mauritau anō hoki mō ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. He āhuatanga nui tēnei, nō te mea, nō nā tata tonu nei te kaiako me ngā kaimahi i tīmata ai.

He muramura, he karakara, he hihiri hoki te āhua o te wāhi o roto mō ngā tamariki. Ka whakatairanga ake anō te wāhi nei nā ngā mahi a ngā tamariki e whakaatuhia ana, puta noa i te kōhanga reo. He nui rawa ngā rauemi, he haumaru hoki ngā taiao akoranga ki rō whare, ki waho hoki. He maha ngā tūmomo wāhi mahi o te kōhanga reo. He wā tā ngā tamariki ki te kōkiri i ā rātou ake mahi, ā, he wāteatea hoki ngā wāhi mō ā rātou ake kaupapa.

Kāhore te whānau e tino whai wāhi mai ana, ā, me whakapakari ake hoki te arotake whaiaro. Me whakawhanake i ētahi wāhanga o ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau. Ko ētahi o aua wāhanga, ko te aromātai hōtaka, te whakahaere pūtea, me ngā arohaehae.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Tangata te kaupapa arotahi.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Tangata

He whakamārama

He pēhea te ako o ā rātou tamariki i tō rātou tuakiri, ā rātou taonga tuku iho hoki?

He pai te mōhio haere o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou tuakiri me ā rātou taonga tuku iho.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka whakamanatia te tuakiri o ngā tamariki mā ngā huarahi papai. Ka noho te tuakiri o ngā tamariki me ā rātou taonga tuku iho hei kaupapa matua ki te whānau. Ko ngā hononga ki te whenua, ngā hapū, ngā marae, me ngā iwi e whakanuia ana e ngā kaiako. Ka poipoi ngā kaiako i ngā tamariki nō iwi kē mā te whakaako i ō rātou ake pepeha ki a rātou, me ngā mōhiotanga anō hoki e pā ana ki ō rātou ake marae, ō rātou ake hapū, ō rātou ake iwi hoki. Kei te mōhio haere ngā tamariki ki te whakahirahiratanga o ō rātou pou whenua tuku iho. He kaha te aronga toi whenuatanga me te waiora o ngā tamariki, ā, he māia hoki.

E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki kia ako, kia whakamahi hoki rātou i te reo Māori. Kua whakaritea e te kaiako ko te reo Māori te reo matua o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero puta noa i te rā. He pakari ngā tamariki tuākana ki te hāpai tonu i taua kaupapa mā te ū ki te reo Māori i a rātou e whai wāhi atu ana ki ngā manuhiri, ngā kaimahi, ā, ki waenga hoki i a rātou anō. Ko te toenga o ngā tamariki e tīmata ana ki te ako me te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahia e rātou ngā whakawhitinga reo ā-waha, ā-tinana hoki. He hāneanea, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki te taiao reo Māori.

Ka whakakoia te aronga toi whenuatanga, te tuakiri hoki o ngā tamariki, nā te pakari o ā rātou hononga ki waenganui i a rātou anō, ki ngā pākeke hoki. He atawhai, he manaaki, he whakaute hoki ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā kaiako me ngā tamariki. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whakatauira i te whanaungatanga, te manaakitanga, me te aroha i roto i ā rātou hononga, ā rātou taunekeneke anō hoki ki ētahi atu tamariki, ki ngā kaiako hoki. Ka whakauru ngā tuākana i ngā tēina ki ā rātou tākaro, ā, ka tautoko i a rātou ki te whakatutuki i ngā mahinga. Ka whai hua ngā mahinga hei wā ako i a rātou e whakahoahoa ana.

Ko te pai o ngā rauemi me te whai pūtake o te taiao akoranga e hāpai ana i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He ngāwari te whakawhitiwhiti haere ki waenga i ngā wāhi ki roto, ki waho. Kei ngā pakitara ngā whakaaturanga o ngā mahi a ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakamahia hei tino rauemi whakaako. Haereere ai ngā tamariki i ia te wā i te hapori whānui. Ka kite, ka wheako hoki rātou i tō rātou tuakiri tuku iho e tohua ana ki te taiao whānui. Kua whakawhanakehia e te whānau ngā mahere ki te whakahou ake i te whare, hei whakatairanga ake anō i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He pēhea te whai hua a te whakahaere a te whānau ki te te tautoko i ngā tamariki kia ākona i tō rātou tuakiri, ā rātou taonga tuku iho hoki?

He pai te whakahaere a te whānau i ētahi wāhanga mahi o te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Kei te tūtohinga me ngā kaupapa here te tino tirohanga, te tino aronga anō hoki hei ārahi i te whakahaeretanga me ngā mahi i te kōhanga reo. Ka hāngai ngā tuhinga ki te waiora o te katoa o te tamaiti, ā, kua whakahāngaitia anō hoki ki te tirohanga oTe Korowai. Arotake ai, whakawhiti kōrero ai hoki te whānau ki ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, mō te pūtake o te kōhanga reo me ngā kaupapa here.

He tika te whai pānga o te mahere rautaki ki ngā wawata o te whānau mō ā rātou tamariki, me te āhua o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei. He mārama ngā hononga ki Te Ara Tūāpae me ngā kaupapa matua ā-tau i tāutuhia ai e te whānau. Ko ētahi kaupapa matua, ko te reo Māori me te whakapai ake i te whakahaeretanga o te whānau. Ka arotahi ngā whāinga rautaki ki te whakapakari tonu i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki e pā ana ki tō rātou ahurea me ngā taonga tuku iho.

Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i tētahi tino angamahi i a rātou e whakamahere ana i ngā hōtaka mō ngā tamariki. Kei ngā mahere ngā aratohu ā-wiki, ā-wāhanga, ā-tau hoki e hāngai ana kiTe Whāriki. Ka whai kiko ngā mahere, ā, ka whai hua ki te ārahi i ngā kaimahi. Ka whiwhi ngā kaimahi i ngā wheako i āta whakamaheretia ai, ā, e whakapūmau ana hoki i ā rātou taonga tuku iho, i tō rātou tuakiri hoki i roto i te ao Māori.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake

He iti noa te whai wāhi mai o te whānau. Kāhore te whānau e tae ā-tinana atu ana i ia te wā ki ngā hui o ia marama, kāhore rānei e whai wāhi mātātoa ana ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga. Kei te whanake tonu tō rātou mōhiotanga, tō rātou māramatanga anō hoki ki te tirohanga o te kōhanga reo.

Kei te tīmata noa te whakawhanaketanga o te arotake whaiaro. He iti noa te aroturuki, te pūrongo hoki i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka hāngai ngā pūrongo a ngā kaiako ki ngā kaupapa e whai ake ana, engari he iti noa te aronga ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Kua roa nei ngā raru i pā ki te whakahaeretanga o ngā pūtea, ā, kāhore anō kia whakatutukihia. Kāhore te whānau e pūrongo ana i runga i ā rātou whāinga rautaki, ā rātou mahi rautaki hoki. Kāhore ngā tukanga arotake whaiaro e tino whakatinana rawatia ana hei painga mō ngā tamariki me te kōhanga reo.

Ko te whakahaeretanga o ngā pūtea tētahi āwangawanga. I taunaki te kaitātari kaute kia āta penapena pūtea te whānau. Me whakapakari ake i te whakahaere pūtea, nō te mea ka whai pānga mārika tēnei ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Kāhore anō ngā arohaehae kia oti. Kua whakaoti te kaiako i tāna ake arohaehae e tāutu ana i ōna pūmanawa me ngā āhuatanga hoki hei whakapai ake. Kāhore anō taua tukanga kia whakamanatia. Kāhore te arohaehae kaimahi e whakamahia ana hei whakamōhio i ngā matea me ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te whakawhanaketanga ngaio.

Kei te whakawhanake tonu i ngā mahi aromatawai. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i tētahi rārangi arowhai, ā, ka kohikohi i ngā tauira mahi a ngā tamariki hei tohu i tā rātou ahunga whakamua. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e tohu ana i te rā, e tuku kōrero ana rānei ki ngā mahi a ngā tamariki hei āta whakapuaki whakaaro mō ngā akoranga me te ahunga whakamua ka kitea.

Ahakoa he rawaka te kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te aromātai hōtaka, he ngoikore te whakatinanatanga o aua āhuatanga. He iti noa ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, ngā whakaaro huritao hoki ki waenga i ngā kaimahi mō te āhua o te whakatinanatanga o ngā kaupapa me ngā wāhanga hoki o te hōtaka akoranga.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kia toro atu te whānau ki te tautoko o te kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe, hei tāutu i te pūtake me ngā tukanga o te arotake whaiaro, hei whakatutuki i ngā wāhanga āwangawanga i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e rua.

Ko Te Otane te kōhanga reo.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ōtane is in the small rural town of Otane, Hawke’s Bay. During this evaluation whānau wanted to know how well their children were learning and developing at kōhanga reo. They specifically asked:

How well do our children learn about their identity and heritage?

Children’s knowledge of their identity and heritage is developing well. They are happy and enjoy being at the kōhanga reo. The formal aspects of the learning programme provide opportunities for children to use te reo Māori in different contexts. The children are fully engaged in learning.

The kaimahi have positive relationships with children. This provides the basis for learning about whānaungatanga. The kuia is a calming influence and provides stability for kaimahi and children. This is important as the kaiako and kaimahi have only recently started.

The indoor environment is bright, colourful and inviting for children. The environment is further enhanced by the children’s work which is displayed throughout the kōhanga reo. The indoor and outdoor learning environments are well resourced and secure. There are multiple work stations for children to choose. Children have time and space to work independently.

Whānau involvement is limited and self review needs to be strengthened. There are some aspects of whānau management that need developing. These include programme evaluation, financial management and appraisals.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review. The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between those present.

The whānau chose mana tangata as its focus area.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Mana Tangata

Background

How well do children learn about their identity and heritage?

Children’s knowledge of their identity and heritage is developing well.

Areas of good performance

Children’s identity is affirmed in positive ways. Whānau make children’s identity and heritage a priority. The links to the local whenua, hapū, marae and iwi are emphasised by kaiako. The kaiako cater for children from other iwi by teaching them their own pepeha and knowledge of their own marae, hapū and iwi. Children are becoming aware of the importance of their ancestral landmarks. Children have a strong sense of belonging, wellbeing and they are confident.

Children are well supported to learn and use te reo Māori. The kaiako has established te reo Māori as the normal language of communication throughout the day. The older children confidently respond in kind by maintaining te reo Māori when engaging with manuhiri, kaimahi and each other. The remaining children are emergent learners and speakers of te reo Māori. They use verbal and non verbal communication. Children are comfortable and confident in a Māori speaking environment.

Children’s sense of belonging and identity is affirmed by the strong relationships they have with each other and adults. Interactions between kaiako and children are warm, welcoming and respectful. Kaimahi encourage children to practice whanaungatanga, manaakitanga and aroha in their relationships and interactions with other children and kaiako. Tuakana include the younger children in their play and support them with routines. Routines are positive social learning times.

The well resourced and purposeful learning environment supports children’s learning. There is easy flow between the indoor and outdoor areas. Wall displays that children contribute to adorn the walls and are used as valuable teaching aids. Children often go on outings into the local and wider community. They see and experience their ancestral identity reflected in the wider environment. Whānau have developed plans to renovate the building to further enhance children’s learning.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

The charter and policies provide a clear vision and direction to guide the operations and practices of the kōhanga reo. Documents focus on the children’s holistic wellbeing and align with the philosophy of Te Korowai. Whānau regularly review and discuss the purpose of kōhanga reo and policies at whānau hui.

The strategic plan appropriately reflects the whānau aspirations for their children and the future of the kōhanga reo. There are clear links to Te Ara Tūāpae and the annual priorities identified by whānau. Priorities include te reo Māori and improving whānau management. The strategic goals focus on building children’s learning about their culture and heritage.

Kaimahi use a sound framework when they plan programmes for children. Plans include weekly, term and annual guidelines that align to Te Whāriki. Planning is detailed and effectively guides kaimahi. Children receive well planned experiences which reinforce their cultural heritage and identity in te ao Māori.

Areas for development

Whānau involvement is limited. Whānau do not regularly attend monthly hui or take an active role in decision making. They are still developing in their knowledge and understanding of the philosophy of kōhanga reo.

Self review is in the early stages of development. There is limited monitoring and reporting on the kōhanga reo operations. Kaiako reports cover upcoming events but have a limited focus on children’s learning. There have been ongoing difficulties with financial management that have not been addressed. Whānau do not report against their strategic goals and tasks. Self review processes are not being initiated to full affect for the benefit of the children and the kōhanga reo.

The management of finances is of concern. The auditor recommended that whānau budget carefully. Financial management practices need strengthening as they have a direct impact on children’s learning.

Appraisals are incomplete. The kaiako has completed a self appraisal which identifies her strengths and areas for improvement. This process has not been formalised. Appraisal of kaimahi is not used to inform professional development needs and planning.

Assessment practices are still developing. Kaimahi use a checklist and collect samples of children’s work to show children’s progress. Kaimahi do not date or comment on children’s work to clearly articulate the learning and progress that is evident.

While programme evaluation policy and procedures are adequate implementation of these is weak. Discussion and reflection among kaimahi about how well kaupapa and aspects of the learning programme are being implemented is limited.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Licensed Kōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends that whānau seek support from the district kaupapa kaimahi to determine the purpose and processes of self review in order to address the areas of concerns in this report.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The recommendation is that ERO returns in two years.
