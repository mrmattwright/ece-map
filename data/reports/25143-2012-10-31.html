2. Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū tata anaTe Kōhanga Reo o Te Wiri ki te taone nui o Manukau. Mai i te tau 1991, e whai wāhi ana te whānau ki te whakapai ake i te whenua me ngā whare me te whakarite i ngā wāhi ako i roto me ngā wāhi ako i waho mā ngā tamariki. E kaha ana ngā mātua o te whānau ki te whakahua i te whakatauki o tō rātou tūtohinga, arā,‘Ko ngā tamariki o tēnei rā, ngā kaiarahi mō apōpō.’

E tino whakapono ana rātou, kia pakari rawa te whakangungu o te whānau ka puta mai ngā whakaritenga whai hua me ngā māramatanga e pā ana ki ngā whakahaere o te kōhanga e ai ki ngā mātāpono oTe Korowaimā a rātou tamariki.

I kōwhiritia Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira oTe Whārikie te whānau o te kōhanga reo hei kaupapa arotahi mā tēnei arotake mātauranga. E whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki ki ngā wheako akoranga huhua mai i te wā ka tīmata rātou i te kōhanga tae noa ki te wā ka puta atu rātou. E whanake ana rātou hei ākonaga maia, hei ākonga e āhei ana, ā, hei ākonga pākiki e mōhio ana ki te reo Māori.

E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki o ngā reanga katoa ki ngā wheako whakaihiihi me te whakawhānui i o rātou māramatanga e pā ana ki te ao huri noa i a rātou. E whai māramatanga ana rātou, ā, e whai koha ana rātou ki ngā tīkanga o te kōhanga reo. Mā te taunekeneke whai take i waenganui i ngā pakeke me ngā tamariki e tino whai wāhi ana ngā akoranga. I te wā e ako ana rātou e whai koha ana ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō. E arahi paitia ngā mātua hou e te kuia, ngā kaimahi kua roa nei e mahi ana me ngā mātua. Nā tēnei manaaki e whai wāhi atu ana rātou ki te hāpai i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā, te whakahaere me te ako i te taha o ā rātou tamariki.

He maha ngā pūkenga, ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā wheako mātau o ngā kaimahi katoa. Ka tautoko ēnei pūmanawa i a rātou mahi. Ko ēnei rautaki te take e rere pai ana ngā whakahaere me te whakatinana i ngā hōtaka akoranga e ai ki Te Whāriki mā a rātou tamariki. Ko te reo rotarota (tētahi reo ā-ture i Aotearoa) tētahi o ngā reo ki te taha o te reo Māori e whai mana mā ngā tamariki me te whānau. Tokotoru o ngā kaimahi tokoono e pupuri ana i teTohu Whakapakarihei hāpai iTe Whārikii roto i te kōhanga reo, ā, kua tata oti tētahi atu o ngā kaimahi i tōnā tohu. E whakawhiwhia ana e ētahi atu o ngā kaimahi ngā tohu ā-motu. Ka kite atu i ō rātou mātauraga hōhonu oTe Whārikime te kaupapa o te kōhanga i roto i te whakahaere angitū me ngā hōtaka ako e whai hua ana mā ngā tamariki.

E whakahaere paitia ana te kōhanga reo e te whānau e ai ki ngā whāinga o tō rātou tūtohinga me ngā tūmanako o te whānau. E mahi ngātahi ngā mātua hou me ngā mātua he roa te noho i te kōhanga ki te whakahaere i ngā akoranga me te whakahaere i ngā tūāhua whakaritenga tiaki tamariki i te kōhanga hei painga mā a rātou tamariki.

Tokorua ngā mātua o te whānau e pupuri ana i ngā tohu mātauranga o Te Pōari Matua o te Te Kōhanga Reo e pā ana ki te whaekahaere tari me te tohu e pā ana ki te whakahaere, arā, te Pae Rangatiratanga, ā, kua tata oti tētahi atu o ngā matua i tōnā tohu. Kua whakaurutia e te tokomaha anō o ngā mātua ngā whakangungu whai tohu a Te Pōari Matua o te Te Kōhanga Reo. E whai wāhi atu ana rātou ki ngā hui whakangungu o te purapura ā-rohe. He whai hua, ā, he whai whakaaro rautaki ngā whakaritenga o te kōhganga reo, ā, e mārama rawa ngā mātua ki ngā whakaritenga tae noa ki ngā whakahaere ahu whakamua.

Ko te haere tonu o te whakapakari i ngā tamariki me te whakangungu i ngā mātua o te whānau kia haere tonu ai te whakahaere whai hua ngā tino wāhanga whakahaere o te kōhanga reo. E akiaki ana Te Tari Arotake i te whānau ki te whakatakoto ā-tuhi me te tuku pūrongo i a wā o te tau e hāngai ai ki ngā tino whāinga rautaki. Mā tēnei ka kite atu i ngā tipuranga me te whai hua o te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā whāinga e ai ki a rātou whāinga rautaki.

Ngā Mahi ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

​3. Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Wiri kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Wiri.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko āna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4. Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

E ai ki ngā mātua o te whānau e haere tonu ana te pukumahi ki te āta whakarite i ngā kaupapa whai hua hei whakanui i te reo Māori,Te Whārikime ngā wheako akoranga hei whakatutuki i ngā matea whānui me ngā hiahia o ngā tamariki. Ka kī anō ngā

mātua ko te Mana Reo te tino kaupapa hei whakapakari me te tāhuhu o ngā wheako akoranga katoa oTe Whārikie riro ana i ngā tamariki. Ko ngā tīkanga me te tiaki i te wairua ngā whenu e hāpai ana i ngā kaimahi hei whakaihiihi i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. E ai ki ngā kōrero o ngā mātua, kua āta tiakina rātou e te whānau i te wā e ako ana rātou i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo i te taha o ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā wahanga kua pai ake

Ngā horopaki ako.He kaupapa nui ngā horopaki ako i tēnei kōhanga. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te hōpara haere me te whakawhānui i o rātou mātauranga e pā ana ki te ao huri noa i a rātou. Ko te āta whakatakoto o ngā horopaki me ngā kaupapa ngā take e pēnei ai te āhua. Kua āta tiakina ngā tamariki, ā, e whai wāhi atu ana rātou ki ngā ngohe whai hua me ngā ngohe hōpara i roto i ngā whare tae noa ki ngā ngohe hōpara i waho ia rā. He wheako whakaihiihi ēnei e hāngai ai ki ngā hiahia me ngā matea o tēnā tamaiti me tēnā tamaiti. Te pai hoki o ngā kaimahi ki te hāpai i ngā uaratanga o te manaaki me te tiaki hei tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te taunekeneke me te whakawhanaunga i waenganui i a rātou anō.

E whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā rauemi ako, ā, he māmā te tiki atu i ngā rauemi kia āhei ai te tamaiti ki te whiriwhiri i te kaupapa e hiahiatia ana te whai atu. Kua āta whakaritea ngā wāhi ako me ngā rauemi whai take i te kōhanga, ā, hei whakanui i te reo Māori, teWhāriki, ngā akoranga o Te Ao Māori ngā take e pēnei ai ngā whakaritenga. Ko te hua anō ka puta mai ko te tipu haere o ngā tamariki hei ākonga maia, hei ākona takitahi, arā, ko te tamaiti anō te kaitātaki o ia anō.

Te whānui o te ako me ngā putanga angitū.Ka nui ngā putanga angitū, ā, ka whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki i ngā akoranga i te kōhanga i te nuinga o te wā. E āta hāngai ana ngā whakaritenga ki ngā matea whānui o tēnā tamaiti me tēnā tamaiti. Kua āta whakatakotoria ngā māhere me ngā whakaritenga mā ngā tamariki katoa, ngā rōpū tokoiti tae noa ki ngā rōpū rerekē. E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga whai take me ngā akoranga hei wero hinengaro me te whakaihiihi i ō rātou whai whakaaro. He akoranga nui ēnei kia haere tonu ai te tipuranga papai o ngā tamariki.

E rere pai ana ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, kua tiutiua ngā kēmu poto me ngā waiata ki roto i te hōtaka hei hīnaki i ngā akoranga. E mārama ana ngā kaiako ki ngā matea whānui o ngā tamariki te kaupapa nui hei whiriwhiri i te wā ka whakatakotoria ngā māhere tae noa ki te wa ka whakahaeretia te hōtaka ako. Kua tiaki paitia te maia o tēnā tamaiti me tēnā tamaiti e te whānau.

Te kōrero i te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa.Ko te reo Māori te reo whakaputa i te kōhanga reo. Ko te reo Māori te reo o ia rā, ā, mā te reo Māori ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero ki tēnā ki tēnā. He ihiihi, he whai take ngā horopaki ako, ā, mā ēnei akoranga papai e ako ana ngā tamariki i te reo Maori me ōnā tikanga. Ka whakarongo rātou ki te reo papai o ngā pakeke, ā, ka akiaki ngā pakeke i ngā tuakana ki te kawe i ō rātou haepapa, arā, te āta tautoko i ā rātou teina. He tūāpapa tēnei hei whakapakari i te ako a te whānau. Kei te kōhanga ētahi tamariki e tino mātau ana ētahi tamariki ki te reo Māori.

E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki te tere whakautu i ngā pātai me te patapātai atu ki a rātou anō i a rātou e tūhura ana i te ao huri noa i a rātou. E whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki ki te taunekeneke me te kōrero ngākau nui i te wā ka pānui ngātahi rātou i te pukapuka. Ka āhei rātou ki te whai maramatanga, ā, e harikoa ana rātou ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ko ngā waiata maha, ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā whakatauki me te karanga ngā take e tino whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo Māori.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamāherehere me te aromātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo hei whakatau i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamāherehere, te whakahaeretanga me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga reo mā a rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka āta hāngai te aromātai nei ki te hōtaka whakamāherehere, te aromātai me te aroturuki i te tipuranga a ngā tamariki.

Ngā wahanga kua pai ake

Te whakahaere me te whakatinana iTe Whāriki. He pai rawa te whakamāherehere me te mahi hei whakatinana iTe Whāriki. He hōhonu, he whai take ngā māhere hei whakatutuki i ngā matea ako o ā rātou tamariki. Ia wā ia wā, ka whai wāhi ngā kaimahi ki te arotake whaiaro i te whai hua o ngā māhere me te hōtaka ako hei whakatutuki i ngā huringa hiahia me ngā huringa matea o ngā tamariki. E tino mōhio ana ngā kaimahi ki te āta whakarite i ngā hōtaka me te āta hāpai i ngā akoranga me te tipuranga o ngā tamariki kia angitū ai te mahi kua whakaritea.

Te tipuranga ako o ngā tamariki.E āhei ana ngā tamariki me te whānau ki te tiki atu i ngā pūkete ako a ngā tamariki e whakaatu ai i te tipuranga ako o te tamaiti mai i te wā ka tīmata ia ki te kōhanga reo. Kua āta whakatakotoria ngā mōhiohio, ā, he tino mōhiohio whai take e whakaatu ai i te tipuranga ako. Kua whakamahia ngā rautaki maha hei kapo i te tipuranga me te whanaketanga mō te wā roa. MāTe Whārikie ako ana ngā tamariki, ā, e whakapiki ana te reo Māori o ngā tamariki.

Te whakangungu kaimahi.Kua oti ngā kaimahi ki te whiwhi tohu mā te Poari Matua o te Kōhanga Reo me ngā hōtaka whakangungu whai mana. Kua roa te wā e mahi ana ētahi kaimahi, ā, e ai ki a rātou e whai koha ana te whānau ki a rātou. Kua hoki mai ētahi tamariki tāwhito o te kōhanga reo o Te Wiri ki te āta hāpai i ngā tūranga me ngā haepapa hei whakahaere i te kōhanga. E waimarie ana ngā tamariki nō te mea te tokomaha o ngā mātua e tino mōhio ana i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, te tini hoki o ngā pūkengā me ngā pūmanawa kei ngā mātua, ā, te tokomaha hoki o ngā mātua e mōhio ana ki te reo Māori.

Ngā whakahaere i te kōhanga.E angitū ana te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. E hāngai hoki ngā whakahaere ki ngā tūmanako o te whānau. E whai wāhi atu ngā mātua, ā, e mōhio ana rātou ki ngā kōrero o te wā e pā ana ki ngā whakaritenga me ngā whakahaere o te kōhanga reo. Ka whakaputa ngā pānui o te whānau ia wā ia wā, ā, he rautaki papai anō hoki te pānui ā-whānau hei whakamarama atu ki ngā mātua i ngā kaupapa e rewa ana i te kōhanga reo. Mā te tuku pānui hoki ka pakari ake te whanaungatanga i waenganui i ngā mātua me ngā tamariki. He whai take, he whai ture ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaere tari. Ka āta aro ngā mātua ki ngā tukanga angitū kia haere tonu ai te whakahaere papai. E whai

whakangungu ana ngā mātua katoa hei tautoko i a rātou ki te whai hua me te tū maia hei hāpai i ō rātou tūranga. Ko te manaaki me te whanaungatanga te anga whakahaere i tēnei kōhanga.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te arotake whaiaro me te tuku pūrongo.Ka arotake whaiaro te whānau ia wā ia wā, ā, kua āta whakaritea ngā tukanga whakamau mōhiohio. Ināianei, e āhei ana rātou ki te tātari, te whakarite tuhinga me te whakarite i ngā pūrongo. Mā te whakarite pūrongo i ngā wā o te tau e hāngai ai ki ngā whāinga matua mē ngā whāinga rāutaki kua whakaritea ka āhei ngā mātua o mua, ngā mātua o tēnei wā tonu, me ngā mātua kei te heke mai nei ki te mōhio e pēhea ana te tipuranga ako, ngā hua rānei e puta mai ana e ai ki ngā whāinga o te kōhanga reo.

5. Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Wiri, tētahiTauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga a te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Kāore ngā tirohanga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i tāutu i ētahi wāhanga e āwangawangatia ana.

6. Ngā Tūtohunga

I whakawhanakehia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Wiri te taunakitanga e whai ake nei:

  • Kia haere tonu te whakapai ake i ngā whakaritenga hei arotake whaiaro, te whakatakoto ā-tuhi me te tuku pūrongo e pā ana i te tipuranga me te mahi e whai hua ana me te whiriwhiri i ētahi atu whāinga rautaki me ngā whāinga.

7. Ngā Mahi ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

2. The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Wiri is located near Manukau City. Since 1991, whānau have contributed to developing their property to provide a range of indoor and outdoor learning experiences for children. Whānau members often refer to their charter whakatauki,‘Ko nga tamariki o tenei ra, nga kaiarahi mo apopo.’They strongly believe that investing in continued whānau learning and development maintains quality whānau practices and a firm understanding of kōhanga reo management aligned toTe Korowai, for their children.

The kōhanga whānau chose for this Education Review, Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira ofTe Whārikias their focus area. Children experience a wide variety of learning opportunities that supports them to develop well from the time they enrol to graduation. They are developing as confident, capable and inquisitive te reo Māori language learners.

Children of all ages are stimulated to develop and extend their understandings of the world around them. They understand and show respect for tikanga of the kōhanga reo. Learning is enhanced through the meaningful and responsive interactions between children and adults. Children show respect for each other as they learn. 4. Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

Whānau members report that they continue to work hard to provide rich te reo Maori,Te Whāriki, learning experiences that cater for the diverse needs and interests of their children. Kaimahi report that Mana Reo is a strength and underpins all dimensions ofTe Whārikilearning experiences provided to children. Tikanga and spiritual development are essential elements integrated by kaimahi to enrich children’s learning. Parents report that they are valued and nurtured in their learning of the kōhanga reo kaupapa alongside their children.

Areas of good performance

Contexts for learning.Contexts for learning are a significant feature of the kōhanga. Children are able to freely explore and extend their knowledge about the world around them within these well considered and organised contexts. They are provided with meaningful and well supervised outdoor and indoor exploration each day. These opportunities stimulate the various interests and specific needs of children. Kaimahi model and reinforce in nurturing ways values of manaaki and tiaki for interactions and developing relationships among children.

Learning resources are located for children’s easy access and independent choices. All resources and the layout of the kōhanga reo learning environment are carefully chosen to promote te reo Maori,Te Whārikiand Te Ao Maori learning that develops confident and independent learners.

Learning diversity and success.Children frequently experience success in their learning at kōhanga reo. Their diverse learning needs are well catered for. Whole, small and group learning is well planned and managed. Children receive regular learning enrichment and extension to challenge and continue to stimulate their thinking and development. Children’s pace and engagement in learning is maximised through focused delivery blended with short games or waiata. Learning diversity is understood by kaimahi to be a key consideration in programme design, management and delivery. Their confidence as individuals among their whānau is successfully nurtured.

Normalising te reo Maori.Te reo Māori is normalised in the kōhanga reo for children. Te reo Māori has become an everyday part of their way of doing and communicating with each other. Children learn te reo Māori in meaningful contexts that are stimulating. They also learn about tikanga associated with these. They hear good language use from adults who remind tuakana of their responsibility to support their teina. This provides solid whānau learning foundations. There are children who have reached a high level of fluency in te reo Māori.

Children respond to questions readily and question each other as they explore and make sense of the world around them. Interactions and open conversations by children during shared book reading show their depth of understanding and enjoyment of using te reo Māori. Numerous waiata, karakia, himene, whakatauki and karanga add to children’s learning dimensions for te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Te Whāriki management and implementation. Te Whāriki is planned and implemented well. It has structure and purpose to meet the needs of their children. Kaimahi regularly reflect on how well plans and delivery meet their children’s changing needs and interests. Planned programmes are successfully managed by kaimahi for children’s learning and development.

Children’s learning and development. Children and whānau have easy access to well documented and maintained learning portfolios that capture well children’s learning, development and progress while at kōhanga reo. A number of methods are used to capture progress and development over the long term. Children’s learning throughTeWhārikiincludes their development of te reo Maori.

Kaimahi professional learning.Kaimahi have successfully attained qualifications among themselves through Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) approved training programmes. A number of kaimahi are long standing and report they are highly appreciated and valued by their whānau for the work they do with children. Te Wiri kōhanga reo graduates have also returned and effectively carry out their roles and responsibilities for managing the kōhanga reo. Children are well served with a range of skills and expertise in the kōhanga reo kaupapa, including te reo Maori speakers.

Kōhanga reo management.Whānau successfully manage the kōhanga reo in the interests of children, and in line with whānau expectations. Members are actively involved and well informed about most areas of kōhanga reo operations and management. Whānau pānui are regular and provide an additional way to keep whānau members informed, and contribute to building whānau relationships. All areas of administration are well organised for transparency and accountability. Whānau members continue to focus on sustained successful management. All whānau members are provided with training and mentoring that helps them to develop confidence to carry out their roles successfully. Manaaki and whanaungatanga underpin the way in which the kōhanga reo is managed.

Areas for development

Strategic review and reporting.Whānau regular review and recording practices are well developed. This places them in a good position to collate, document and report against strategic priorities and objectives throughout each year. This should enable former, present and future members of the kōhanga to easily track progress and success to date against kōhanga reo objectives.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
