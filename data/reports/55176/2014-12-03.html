1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whānau Aroha ki Tūranga-nui-a-Kiwa. Ka tino ngākau nui te kaiako, ngā kaimahi, me te whānau ki te whakatairanga i te reo Māori me ōna tikanga ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki. Kua whai hua te whānau ki te whakawhanake i tētahi taiao akoranga hihiri e whakaongaongatia ana. Ka rongo te whānau me ā rātou tamariki i ngā hononga e manaaki ana i a rātou ki te kōhanga reo. Kei reira e kitea ana te whanaungatanga puta noa i ngā hononga me ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā tamariki me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. I whai wāhi rātou ki te waihanga i te pātai aromātai i ārahi ai i te arotake a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori, arā:

He pēhea te pai o te whakawhanaketanga o te manawa whakahī, te mōhiotanga anō hoki o ā tātou tamariki mō ā rātou taonga tuku iho?

E tino poipoia ana ngā tamariki kia whanake ai te manawa whakahī, te mōhiotanga anō hoki mō ā rātou taonga tuku iho.

Ka whai hua ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakawhanake i ngā mōhiotanga, te manawa whakahī, me te ngākau nuitanga hoki o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori me ōna tikanga, i a rātou e ako ana, e tupu haere ana hoki i ia rā. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te tūhura, te ako hoki i ngā taonga i tuku iho ai i ō rātou tūpuna. Kei te whanake te mōhiotanga pai ki te tiaki i a rātou anō, i ētahi atu hoki. Ka kōkiri rātou i tā rātou ake ako, ā, he pai tō rātou māramatanga, tō rātou noho maioha ki te ao Māori i roto i ā rātou nekehanga o ia rā.

Kei te tino whakahaerehia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. E hāpai ana ngā mahi whakahaere i te whakatutukitanga a te whānau i ō rātou wawata.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

He pēhea te pai o te whakawhanaketanga o te manawa whakahī, te mōhiotanga anō hoki o ā tātou tamariki mō ā rātou taonga tuku iho?

E tino poipoia ana ngā tamariki kia whanake ai te manawa whakahī, te mōhiotanga anō hoki mō ā rātou taonga tuku iho.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki kia whanake ai te mōhiotanga me te pakari anō hoki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori. Kua rumakihia ngā tamariki ki ngā wheako akoranga e tākirikiri ana, e whai pūtake ana hoki ki a rātou. Ka whai pānga tēnei ki te whakapakari i te reo a ngā tamariki, ā, e āhei ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakahāngai i ngā hanganga reo Māori me ngā kīanga ki ia tamaiti. Ka kitea te tino ngākau nui o te whānau ki te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki i tō rātou reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Ka kitea te tino ngākau nui o ngā tamariki ki te whakaaro, te whakatutuki kaupapa, te whakaari, me te whakawhiti kōrero mā te reo Māori.

E hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki kia whanake ai tō rātou mōhiotanga ki ā rātou taonga tuku iho, tō rātou tuakiri hoki mā ngā huarahi e tino whai pūtake ana. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo kaupapa hihiri, whai wāhitanga hihiri hoki, kia ako ai ngā tamariki i ō rātou hononga ake ki te ao Māori. Ka tuhura ngā tamariki i ā rātou taonga tuku iho mā te huarahi tikanga ahurea, auaha hoki. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou mōhiotanga ki ētahi atu.

E tino poipoia te whakawhanaketanga o te katoa o te tamaiti e ngā kaimahi me te whānau. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te whanaungatanga i ia rā. Kua māro kētia te whanaungatanga ki waenga ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. Ka ngahau tahi, a, he pārekareka ki a rātou katoa hoki te ako mā te whakahura. Ka whakapau kaha te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakapūmau i te whakaute me te manaaki ki ā rātou taunekeneke katoa me ngā tamariki. Ka kitea te tupu haere o te māia, te mōhiotanga anō hoki o ngā tamariki ki te manaaki i a rātou anō, i ētahi atu hoki.

Ka kitea te whakaute me te aronga nui o ngā tamariki ki ā rātou taonga tuku iho me tō rātou ahurea. Kua āta waihangatia te hōtaka akoranga e whakapuakihia ana ki ngā tamariki. Ka ākina ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te ao Māori, tae atu ki te hōhonutanga o ngā taonga tuku iho o ō rātou tūpuna. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā tino rautaki e whai hua ana ki te whakapuaki me te whakapūmau i ngā akoranga ā-kaupapa, kia āhei ai ngā tamariki ki te whakamātau i ō rātou whakaaro, me te tūhura, te whakatau hoki i te ao Māori. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te mōhiotanga whānui ki ngā atua Māori i roto i te ao e noho nei rātou.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga o tētahi taiao hihiri e mau nei i te maha o ngā rauemi, ā, e hāpai ana i ā rātou akoranga. He pai te whakatakotoranga o ngā wāhi ako o roto, o waho hoki, ā, he nui ngā rauemi hei akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te whakatewhatewha me te whakatau haere i tō rātou ao. E tino tautoko ana ngā tamariki e tō rātou whānau, ā, ka kitea tō rātou tino hiahia, tō rātou tino ngākau nuitanga hoki ki te tūhura i te taiao me te ao tūturu.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

E tino whakahaerehia ana te kōhanga reo i runga i ngā wawata o te whānau. Kua āta tāutuhia, kua āta tuhia, kua āta whakatinanahia hoki ngā pūnaha katoa e pa ana ki te whakahaeretanga. E arotahi ana ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā whāinga o tā rātou tūtohinga, me te whai i ā rātou whāinga, ā rātou kaupapa matua hoki. Kei te ngākau nui tahitia e te whānau te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, te rumaki hoki i ā rātou tamariki ki te ao Māori. Ko ngā painga mō ngā tamariki me te whānau te aronga o te whakahaeretanga pai o te kōhanga reo.

Ka āta whakaarohia, ka āta whakamaheretia, ka āta whakahaerehia e ngā kaimahi te ako me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki i ā rātou taonga tuku iho ki te ao Māori. He māramatanga ngātahi tō ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere me te whakatinana i te hōtaka kia whai hua ai. Ko te reo Māori me ōna tikanga kei te pūtake o ngā mahere mā ia tamaiti. He pai te whakamōhio i te whānau ki ngā mahi e pā ana ki te whakamahere hōtaka.

He pai te tuhi haere, te pūrongo hoki i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi ngā rautaki aromatawai e whai hua ana ki te whakatakoto haere i huarahi ako a ia tamaiti. Ka kitea Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira me te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki ki ngā tuhinga aromatawai. He pai ngā mōhiotanga o te whānau ki te angitu, te ako, me ngā mōhiotanga o ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

E mōhio ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kua arotahi te whānau ki te maha o ngā kaupapa matua hei whakatutuki wawe i te tau kua pahure ake nei. He pai tā rātou whakatutuki haere i aua take. Kua tae ki te wā, kia whakawhanake te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā kaupapa rautaki matua a te katoa.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaeretanga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora, me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia whakawhanake ake te whānau me ngā kaimahi i tā rātou mahere rautaki, me te whakamahi tonu i te arotake whaiaro a te whānau ki te aroturuki i te āhua o ngā mahi ki ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

3 Hakihea 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whānau Aroha is in Gisborne. The kaiako, kaimahi and whānau have a strong commitment to promoting te reo Māori me ōna tikanga with their children. The whānau have successfully developed a vibrant and stimulating learning environment. Whānau and their children experience caring relationships while at the kōhanga reo. Whanaungatanga permeates all relationships and interactions between children and whānau.

The whānau chose ngā taumata whakahirahira as the focus for this review. They participated in the design of the evaluation question which guided ERO’s review:

How well do our children develop pride and knowledge about their cultural heritage?’

Children are successfully nurtured to develop pride and knowledge about their cultural heritage.

Kaimahi and whānau successfully develop children’s knowledge, pride and passion for te reo Māori me ōna tikanga as they learn and live their daily lives. Children confidently explore and learn about their ancestral and cultural heritage. They develop an awareness of self care and care for others. They are self motivated to learn and have a good understanding and appreciation of te ao Māori in their everyday lives.

The kōhanga reo is successfully managed in the interests of children. Management practices support whānau to achieve their aspirations.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus area.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

How well do our children develop pride and knowledge about their cultural heritage?

Children are successfully nurtured to develop pride and knowledge about their cultural heritage.

Areas of good performance

Children are well supported to develop knowledge and the confidence to use te reo Māori. Children are immersed in stimulating and meaningful learning experiences. This helps build children’s language and enables kaimahi to personalise te reo Māori structures and phrases for each child. Whānau show a strong interest in their children’s development in te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Children show a strong preference to think, act, perform and communicate through te reo Māori.

Children are supported to develop knowledge of their cultural heritage and identity in highly purposeful ways. Kaimahi provide a wide range of exciting activities and opportunities for children to learn about their connections to te ao Māori. Children explore their cultural heritage in traditional and creative ways. Children confidently share their knowledge with others.

Children’s holistic development is effectively nurtured by kaimahi and whānau. Children regularly experience whanaungatanga on a daily basis. Kaimahi have established close and trusting bonds with children. Together they have fun and enjoy learning through discovery. Whānau and kaimahi work hard to reinforce respect and care in all of their interactions with children. Children show a growing confidence and awareness of how they care for themselves and others.

Children show respect and strong interest in their heritage and culture. The learning programme is carefully designed and delivered to children. Children are encouraged to explore te ao Māori including their rich cultural and ancestral heritage. Kaimahi use highly effective strategies to introduce and reinforce kaupapa based learning so children can test their ideas, explore and make sense of te ao Māori. Children are developing a broad knowledge about ngā atua Māori in their everyday lives.

Children are successfully immersed in a rich, vibrant and well resourced environment that supports their learning. The indoor and outdoor learning areas are well designed and extensively resourced to encourage children to investigate and make sense of their world. Children are well supported by their whānau and show a strong desire and passion to explore the natural and physical world.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

The kōhanga reo is effectively managed in accordance with whānau aspirations. All management systems are well defined, documented and implemented. Kaimahi and whānau are focused on reaching their charter objectives and pursuing their goals and priorities. There is a shared interest and commitment by whānau to the purpose of Te Kōhanga Reo and to immersing their children in te ao Māori. Children and whānau interests are the focus of the effective management of the kōhanga reo.

Children’s learning and development of their cultural heritage in te ao Māori is well considered, planned and managed by kaimahi. Kaimahi have a shared understanding about effective programme planning and implementation. Te reo Māori me ōna tikanga is at the heart of planning for each child. Whānau are well informed about programme planning.

Children’s learning and development is well recorded and reported. Kaimahi have established effective assessment methods that record individual learning journeys. Ngā taumata whakahirahira strands and children’s language development are evident in assessment documents. Whānau have good information about their children’s success, learning and knowledge.

Areas for development

ERO recognises that the whānau have focused on a number of urgent priorities over the past year. They have successfully worked through these matters. It is now timely that the whānau and kaimahi develop shared strategic priorities.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO LicensedKōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends the whānau including kaimahi further develop their strategic plan and continue to use whānau self-review to monitor the performance of all areas of the kōhanga reo.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
