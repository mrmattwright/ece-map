1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā tētahi o ngā Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia ai i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai e te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i mua atu, me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira, tae atu ki te āheinga reo Māori o ngā mema o te whānau

  • te Whakamahere me te Aromātai, tae atu ki te whakamahere rautaki, ngā arohaehae kaimahi, me te arotake whaiaro
  • ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture, tae atu ki te hauora me te haumaru
  • ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai whai muri i te arotake o mua.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kei Tāmaki-nui-a-Rua Te Kōhanga Reo o Kaitoke. E hono ana te kōhanga reo nei ki a Rangitāne me Ngāti Kahungunu o Te Wairarapa. Kei runga marae te kōhanga reo nei.

E aromātai ana tēnei pūrongo a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te ahunga whakamua a ngā mātua, whai muri i te arotake o mua, i te Whiringa-ā-nuku o 2012.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te noho ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whai wāhi atu rātou ki ngā tikanga, te kori takitahi, me ngā taumahi ako. He pai ngā rauemi ki te kōhanga reo. He maha ngā taumahi e hāpai ana i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki. He ngāwari te rere o ngā mahinga, he pai te whakahaeretanga, ā, he tino wā ako whakahoahoa. He pai te whakawhanaunga o ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō me ngā kaimahi.

He tau ngā tamariki ki te taiao o te reo Māori. He mātanga reo te kaiako. Ka whakamahi rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā rautaki e hāpai ana i te ako a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. Ko ētahi o ēnei, ko te whakarato i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo hanganga reo. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā wā waiata. Ka whai wāhi ngā mātua ki ngā akoranga o te reo Māori. Ka arotahi rātou ki te whakatairanga ake i te whakamahinga o te reo Māori ki ō rātou kāinga.

E tino pūmau ana, e tino ngākau nui ana te whānau mō te whakawhanaketanga o ā rātou tamariki. Ka whakamahia ngā mōhiohio hei whakamōhio i te whakawhanaketanga ngaio o ia kaiako. Ka whakarato rātou i te tino ārahitanga o te aronga o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakahāngai te whānau i ā rātou mahi ki ngā whāinga rautaki me ngā tūmanako o Te Ara Tūāpae. Arotake ai rātou i te mahere mahi i ia te wā. Kua aromātai, kua whakaaro huritao hoki te whānau mō ā rātou whakaritenga me te whakahaere o te kōhanga reo.

Kua whakatinanahia e te whānau te maha o ngā rautaki e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. I nāianei, e arotahi ana te whānau ki ngā mahi kei mua tonu i a rātou.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i ngā take i hāngai ai ki te āheinga reo Māori o te whānau. I taunaki taua pūrongo, kia whakawhanake te whānau i tētahi rautaki ki te whakapai ake i ō rātou pūkenga reo Māori.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te noho ki te kōhanga reo. He whānui ngā tūmomo taumahi ako a ngā tamariki. Ka taea e rātou te tākaro ā-rōpū iti, ā-rōpū nui rānei, te tākaro takitahi rānei. He whakaute, he atawhai hoki te āhua o ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki te hōtaka akoranga.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te tūhura i tō rātou taiao akoranga whakaongaonga. Ka whakaatuhia ngā mahi toi a ngā tamariki puta noa i te kōhanga reo. He maha ngā tūmomo rauemi ako kei a rātou. He pai te whakatakotoranga, te whakaritenga hoki o ngā taiao o roto, o waho hoki.

Ka whakatairangatia te āheinga o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori e te whakaaro nui o te whānau ki te reo i ngā kāinga. Kua whakawhanakehia, kua whakatinanahia e te whānau tētahi rautaki reo Māori, ki te whakapakari i tō rātou āheinga reo. Haere ai rātou ki ngā akoranga o Te Ataarangi me ngā akoranga ā-wiki e whakahaerehia ana e tētahi matua e matatau ana ki te reo Māori. Kua whakaritea ētahi wāhanga reo ki te whakapakari i te āheinga o te whānau i roto i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga. Ko te whakamahinga a ngā mema o te whānau i te reo Māori e hāpai ana i te tino hopu reo a ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I tāutu te arotake o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i ngā take i hāngai ai ki te whakamahere rautaki, ngā arohaehae kaimahi, me te arotake whaiaro. I reira ētahi take hauora, take haumaru hoki e noho ana hei āhuatanga mōrearea pea ki ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

E whakarato ana te whakamahere rautaki i te tino pūtaketanga, te tino aronga hoki ki te whānau mō te ahunga whakamua o te kōhanga reo me ā rātou tamariki. Kua tīmata te whānau ki te whakamahere i ngā whāinga mō te aronga whakamua o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei. Ka hāngai aua whāinga ki ngā matea o ā rātou tamariki me te whānau. Aroturukitia ai te mahere i ia te wā.

Ka whakatairangatia ngā wheako akoranga a ngā tamariki mā te whakawhanaketanga ngaio i ia te wā. Kua oti kētia te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi katoa. Kua tāutuhia ā rātou whāinga mō te whakawhanaketanga ngaio. Ko te reo Māori tētahi kaupapa matua mō ngā kaimahi.

He pai te whakawhanaketanga o te arotake whaiaro. Ināianei, ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga tētahi āhuatanga ōkawa o te taiāwhio arotake whaiaro. Arowhai ai i ia te wā i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea, te hauora, me te haumaru, ā, ka aroturuki, ka whakatakoto haere te whānau i aua arowhai i ia te wā. He maha ngā wā ka whakawhiti kōrero, ka pūrongo hoki te whānau mō te whakamahere pūtea, te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki, ngā rauemi, me ngā tukanga whakahaere tari.

Ko ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā hiahia o ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā mahi whakamahere a ngā kaimahi. Ka ārahi ngā kaiako i ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere me te aromātai i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakapuaki whakaaro te whānau hei hāpai i ngā mahi whakamahere, mā te whai wāhi atu ki ngā huihuinga. He riterite ngā tukanga whakamahere ki te āhua o ngā mahi whakaako. Ka aromātai i ia te wā i ngā kaupapa ako me te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka tomo atu ngā tamariki ki tētahi hōtaka i whakamahere tikatia, i aromātai tikatia hoki.

Kei ngā aromatawai a ia tamaiti ngā tino mōhiohio e pā ana ki tāna ahunga whakamua me ngā whakawhanaketanga. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā tūmomo huarahi aromatawai ki te aroturuki i te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki. Ko ngā whakaaturanga o ngā wheako akoranga tūturu a ngā tamariki, ka whakaemihia. E tino wātea ki te whānau ngā aromatawai e pā ana ki ngā tamariki, ā, he whakatakotoranga mārama ēnei o ā rātou wheako, me tā rātou ahunga whakamua.

Kua whakatinanahia e te whānau ngā rautaki ki te aroturuki i te hauora me te haumaru. Kua piki ake te haumaru o ngā tamariki nā te whakatinanatanga o te tuhi haere i ia te wā o ngā arowhai mōrearea me te tango hoki o ngā papa mārō ki te wāhi tākaro. He mata ngohengohe kei te papa tākaro ināianei. Ko te haumaru o ngā tamariki tētahi kaupapa matua ki te whānau.

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia haere tonu te whānau ki te whakapakari, te arotake, me te aroturuki i ā rātou whakaritenga kāwanatanga me te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

Kaiurungi Whakatūruki Arotake Māori Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

30 Kohi-tātea 2015

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of the kōhanga reo governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the previous ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira including the reo Māori capacity of whānau members
  • Planning and Evaluation including strategic planning, kaimahi appraisals and self review
  • Areas of non-compliance including health and safety
  • and any issues identified since the previous review.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kaitoke is in Dannevirke. The kōhanga reo affiliates to Rangitane and Ngāti Kahungunu o Te Wairarapa. It is marae-based.

This ERO report evaluates the progress parents have made since the previous review of October 2012.

Children enjoy being at kōhanga reo. They participate in tikanga, independent play and learning activities. The kōhanga reo is well resourced. There are many activities that support children’s development. Routines flow smoothly, are well managed and are valuable social learning times. Children relate to each other and kaimahi in positive ways.

The children are comfortable in a te reo Maori environment. The kaiako are positive language models. They use a range of strategies to support children’s te reo Māori learning. These include providing a range of language structures. Children enjoy waiata time. Parents are engaged in te reo Māori studies. They are focused on enhancing the use of te reo Māori in their homes.

Whānau are highly committed and passionate about their children’s development. Appraisal information is being used to inform professional develop for individual kaiako. They provide strong leadership for the direction of the kōhanga reo. Whānau have aligned their actions to the strategic goals and objectives of Te Ara Tūāpae. They regularly review the action plan. Whānau have been evaluating and reflecting on their practices and the management of the kōhanga reo.

Whānau have implemented a number of health and safety measures. The whānau are now focussed on their next steps.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

The previous ERO report identified issues with the reo Māori capacity of whānau. The report recommended that whānau develop a strategy to improve their te reo Māori skills.

Areas of progress

Children enjoy being at kōhanga reo. Children have a range of learning activities. They can choose to play in small or large groups or independently. Interactions and relationships between kaimahi and children are respectful and welcoming. Children confidently participate in the learning programme.

Children enjoy exploring their stimulating learning environment. Children’s artwork is displayed throughout the kōhanga reo. They have a range of learning resources. The indoor and outdoor environments are tidy and well organised.

Children’s te reo Māori capacity is enriched by whanau placing importance on the language in homes. The whanau have developed and implemented a te reo Māori strategy to build their language capacity. They attend Te Ataarangi classes and weekly lessons held by a parent who is fluent in te reo Maori. Wānanga reo have been organised to build the reo Māori and cultural capacity of whanau. The use of te reo Māori by whānau members supports children’s language acquisition in positive ways.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

The previous ERO review identified issues associated with strategic planning, kaimahi appraisals and self review. There were some health and safety concerns that were potential risks to children.

Areas of progress

Strategic planning provides whānau with a clear sense of purpose and direction for the future of the kōhanga reo and their children. The whānau have begun to plan goals for the future direction of the kōhanga reo. These goals are relevant to the needs of their children and whānau. The plan is monitored regularly.

Children’s learning experiences are enriched through regular professional development. All kaimahi have been appraised. They have had their professional development goals identified. Te reo Māori is a priority for kaimahi.

Self review practices are developing well. Policies with procedures are now a part of a formal self review cycle. There are regular hazard, health and safety checks which whānau regularly monitor and record. Whānau often discuss and report on financial management, the children’s learning programme, resources and administration processes.

Children’s interests and preferences are at the centre of kaimahi planning. The kaiako leads kaimahi in planning and evaluating the learning programme. Whānau contribute ideas towards planning by being involved in meetings. Planning processes are consistent with teaching practice. There is regular evaluation of learning topics and the children’s learning programme. Children are engaged in a well planned and evaluated programme.

Children’s assessments provide valuable information about their progress and development. Kaimahi use different assessment tools to monitor children’s progress. Evidence of children’s practical learning experiences is collated. Whānau have easy access to children’s assessments which provide a clear record of their experiences and progress.

Whānau have implemented measures for health and safety monitoring. The safety of the children has increased through the implementation of regular recording of hazard checks and the removal of hard surfaces in the play area. A soft fall surface is now in place. The safety of the children is a priority for whānau.

4 Recommendations

ERO recommends the whānau continue to strengthen, review and monitor their governance practices and the children’s learning programme.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.

Lynda Pura Watson

Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)
