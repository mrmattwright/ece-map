Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Au o Waikato ki Mōrena. He hiahia tō te whānau ki te mōhio ki te āhua o te mauritau me te harikoa o ā rātou tamariki hei ākonga. Ki tā Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga whakatau, he tino harikoa, he tino mauritau anō hoki ngā tamariki hei ākonga.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Au o Waikato i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Au o Waikato ki runga i te marae o Kai a te Mata, ki Mōrena. Ko te noho marae e āta whakamana ana i ngā hononga ki waenga i te whānau, tae atu hoki ki ngā kaumātua. Nā tēnei, e noho ngākaunui ana te whānau ki te whakapūmau i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo me ngā tikanga, ngā kawa hoki o te marae. Ka noho pūmau hoki te kōhanga reo ki te Kīngitanga.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa tā ngā tamariki whakaatu he ākonga mauritau, he ākonga harikoa anō hoki rātou?

Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tō rātou mauritau, tō rātou harikoa anō hoki hei ākonga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whakapūmau ana i te reo Māori hei kaupapa matua. Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i ngā whaiwāhitanga ako ōkawa me ngā whaiwāhitanga ako ōpaki. Ka hāpaitia, ka ākina hoki kia taki rātou i ō rātou pepeha me ngā mihi ki ngā tamariki me ngā pākeke i ia rā. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā taumahi ngahau ka hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki mā ngā waiata me ngā kēmu. E tupu ana te tūmāia o ngā tamariki i a rātou e puāwai ana ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i ngā akoranga e poipoi ana i tō rātou tuakiri. Ka whakarite ngā kaimahi i te taetae ā-tinana atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā pōwhiri katoa i te marae. Nā tēnei, ka āta kite rātou i ngā tikanga me ngā kawa ki te marae. I ētahi atu wā, e wātea ana te marae ātea ki ngā tamariki hei wāhi tūhura, hei wāhi tākaro hoki. Ka hāpai ngā kaumātua i te ako a ngā tamariki me te whānau i te whakahirahiratanga o te mana whenua, o tō rātou marae, o tō rātou hapū, me tō rātou iwi. Kei te mōhio ngā tamariki ki ngā wāhi tapu me ngā tino pou whenua hoki o tēnei takiwā. Ka kitea te kaha o te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki.

He harikoa, he mauritau hoki ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka rongo rātou i te aroha me te manaaki i ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu. Ka tino whai hua te noho mai a ngā kaumātua ki te taha o ngā tamariki. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki papai ka whakatairanga ake i te tūmāia me te whakawhirinaki o ngā tamariki. Ko ō rātou matea tētahi aronga matua. Ka ū ngā kaimahi ki te kounga kairangi o ngā mahi i whakatakotohia kētia ai e te kaiako o mua. Ko te whanaungatanga te tūāpapa o ngā taunekeneke katoa. Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao mauritau, taiao pūmau hoki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Me hāpai ake ngā tamariki ki te whakawhānui ake i tō rātou māramatanga, i tā rātou whakamahi hoki i te reo Māori. He pakari ngā hanganga reo a ngā tamariki mō ngā karakia, ngā pepeha, me ngā mihimihi. E āhei ana rātou ki te taki i ēnei mā te iti noa tonu o te tautoko. Kei te mōhio rātou ki ngā pātai me ngā tohutohu, ā, ka whakautu hoki rātou i ēnei. Engari, he iti noa ngā tūmomo horopaki e āta whakamahia ana e rātou te reo Māori. He mea nui, kia rapu ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki ka hāpai i te hopu a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori hei reo tuarua.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He pai te whakamahere i te ako a ngā tamariki. Ka whai wāhi te whānau ki te whakamahere i ngā hōtaka, ā, ka whakarato hoki rātou i ngā kōrero urupare. Ka tautoko ngā kaumātua i ngā wānanga e pā ana ki a Ngāti Hauā, ā, ka whai wāhi anō hoki ēnei ki ngā mahere. Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i te kounga o ngā whaiwāhitanga ako e aro nui ana ki ō rātou matea.

He pai te whakahaere i te aromatawai o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā pūkete hei kapo atu i ngā tauira mahi, ā, hei aroturuki hoki i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka kōrero hoki ngā kaimahi ki ngā whānau hei te mutunga o ia wāhanga, mō te ahu whakamua a ā rātou ake tamariki. He pai te whakamōhio i te whānau mō te ahu whakamua a ā rātou ake tamariki, me tō rātou whanaketanga.

Ka whai wāhi mātātoa nei te whānau ki ngā whakahaere o te kōhanga reo. Kua oti i te whānau tētahi mahere rautaki me tētahi mahere ā-tau i whakamōhiotia ai e Te Ara Tūāpae me Te Korowai. Kua whakatakotohia e te whānau a rātou kaupapa matua mō tēnei wā, ā, mō meāke nei hoki. Kei te mahere ā-tau ngā mahi, ngā putanga, me rātou e kawe ana i aua kaupapa, hei aroturuki i te ahu whakamua. Ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā whakataunga katoa ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Me āta titiro ki te whakahaeretanga o ngā kaimahi. Ka whakaoti ngā kaimahi i ngā arohaehae whaiaro, engari kāhore i te tino mārama te tukanga ka whai ake. Kei te āhua pēnei nā te mea, kei te ngaro ētahi tuhinga. E mōhio ana te whānau, he mea nui te whai huatanga o ngā pūnaha ki te whakahaere i te tari.

He ōpaki te aromātai a ngā kaimahi i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Kāhore rātou e tāutu ana i te āhua o te aromātai hōtaka ki te whakapakari i ngā hōtaka akoranga, i ngā whakaritenga rānei a ngā kaimahi. Me whai whakaaro ngā kaimahi ki tētahi tukanga ka ine i te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā tētahi hōtaka ka rite tonu te āta aroturukitanga, te āta whakatauriteatanga hoki, hei whakatutuki i ō rātou matea ako.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • mahi ngātahi tonu ki te taha o ngā kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ki te whakapakari ake i te āheinga o te whānau ki te whakahaere me te ārahi i te kōhanga reo.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Au o Waikato ki Mōrena. He hiahia tō te whānau ki te mōhio ki te āhua o te mauritau me te harikoa o ā rātou tamariki hei ākonga. Ki tā Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga whakatau, he tino harikoa, he tino mauritau anō hoki ngā tamariki hei ākonga.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Au o Waikato is in Morrinsville. Whānau were eager to know how settled and happy their children are as learners. ERO is pleased to advise that the children are happy and well settled learners.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Au o Waikato will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Au o Waikato is on Kai a te Mata marae, Morrinsville. The marae based setting reaffirms whānau relationships including kaumātua. This ensures that whānau are committed to maintaining the kaupapa of kōhanga reo and tikanga and kawa of the marae. The kōhanga reo are loyal to the Kīngitanga.

2 The Review Findings

How do children show that they are settled and happy learners?

Children show that they well settled and happy learners.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children learn in an environment where te reo Māori is a priority. Children experience both formal and informal learning opportunities. They are prompted and encouraged to recite their pepeha and mihi to children and adults on a daily basis. Kaimahi provide fun activities to support children’s language development through waiata and games .Children are growing in confidence as beginning speakers of te reo Māori.

Children experience learning where their identity is nurtured. Kaimahi ensure children attend all pōwhiri at the marae. This ensures they observe tikanga and kawa o te marae. At other times children have access to the marae ātea to explore and play. Kaumātua support children and whānau to learn about the significance of mana whenua, their marae, hapū and iwi. Children are familiar with local wāhi tapu and other significant landmarks. Children demonstrate a strong sense of belonging.

Children are happy and settled in their kōhanga reo environment. They experience aroha and manaaki in their relationships with others. Kaumātua presence has a positive influence on children. Kaimahi provide positive strategies to promote confidence and trust in children. Their needs are a priority. Kaimahi maintain high standards originally set by the previous kaiako. Whanaungatanga is the foundation for all interactions. Children learn in a stable and secure environment.

Key Next Steps

Children require support to extend their understanding and use of te reo Māori. Children are confident with language structures such as karakia, pepeha and mihimihi. They are able to recite these with very little support. Children are familiar with and respond to questions and instructions. However, the range of situations where they confidently use te reo Māori is limited. It is important that kaimahi seek a range of strategies to support children’s acquisition of te reo Māori as a second language.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s learning is well planned. Whānau have input and provide feedback to programme planning. Kaumātua support wānanga about Ngāti Hauā which also contributes to planning. Children receive quality learning opportunities that are responsive to their needs.

Assessment of children’s learning is well managed. Kaimahi use profile books to capture samples of work and monitor children’s learning. Kaimahi also kōrero with whānau at the end of each term about their children’s progress. Whānau are well informed of their child’s progress and development.

Whānau play an active role in the management of the kōhanga reo. Whānau have completed strategic and annual plans that are informed by Te Ara Tūāpae and Te Korowai. Whānau have set their current and future priorities. The annual plan includes actions, outcomes and who is responsible to monitor progress. Children are at the heart of all kōhanga reo decision making.

Key Next Steps

Personnel management needs attention. Kaimahi complete self-appraisals but the next stage of the process is unclear. This is partly due to missing documentation. Whānau are aware of the importance of efficient administration systems.

Kaimahi informally evaluate the effectiveness of the learning programme. They do not identify how programme evaluation might strengthen learning programmes or kaimahi practice. Kaimahi should consider a process to gauge the quality of the learning programme. Children will benefit from a programme that is regularly monitored and moderated to meet their learning needs.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • continue to work closely with district kōhanga reo staff to build whānau capability to manage and lead the kōhanga reo.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Au o Waikato is in Morrinsville. Whānau were eager to know how settled and happy their children are as learners. ERO is pleased to advise that the children are happy and well settled learners.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Au o Waikato will be in three years.
