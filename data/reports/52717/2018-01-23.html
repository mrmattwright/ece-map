1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakaahurangi ki te taha o te marae, ki te tāone anō hoki e kīia nei ko Whakaahurangi. Nō te tau 1994 tēnei o ngā whare kōhanga reo i whakatuwheratia ai, whai muri i te roanga o ngā tau e hui ana, e kohi pūtea ana, ā, e whakapau kaha ana te whānau ki te whakatutuki i tā rātou tino whāinga nei. I āwhina anō hoki te hapori i te whānau i taua wā.

He hou te kaiwhakahaere ki tōna tūranga me āna kawenga mahi. He kuia nō Whakaahurangi te kaiako o mua, ā, ka tautoko tonu ia i ngā tamariki me te whānau i roto i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori. Haere mai ai ngā mātua i te hapori whānui tonu ki te kawe mai i ā rātou tamariki ki Whakaahurangi.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa te whakatinana a ngā tamariki i te whanaungatanga?

He pai te whakatinana a ngā tamariki i te whanaungatanga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Rongo ai ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki te kōhanga reo i ngā wā katoa. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te taki i ō rātou pepeha. He pakari ngā tamariki tuākana ki te whakapuaki whakaaro ki te taha o ētahi atu. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki e whakatairanga ana i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki, pērā i te kōrerorero ki ngā tamariki, te patapatai mō ā rātou taumahi, me te hāpai i ngā tamariki ki te tāwhai i te reo ka rangona e rātou. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā kēmu, ka tāutu hoki i ngā ingoa o ngā tae me ngā kākahu, hei whakawhānui i ā rātou kupu kōrero. E tīmata ana te puāwaitanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā hua o te whakawhanaungatanga ki ētahi atu. Ka whai wāhi matua te whanaungatanga ki ngā mahinga a ngā tamariki i ia rā. Ka whakamihi nui ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki i ā rātou taenga mai ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakatauira, ka hāpai hoki i ngā taunekeneke o te whakaute, te whakaaro nui, me te aroha ki ētahi atu. Ka kitea anō hoki aua tūāhuatanga ki ngā tamariki hei tangata whenua i ngā pōwhiri. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, me ngā waiata. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te whakawhanaungatanga ki waenga i a rātou anō, ā, ki ngā pākeke hoki e noho ana ki ō rātou taha.

E noho mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo me te marae hei taiao akoranga mō rātou. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i ngā taiao māoriori, tūturu hoki, tae atu ki te marae, me ngā ara hikoi o te hapori. Ko ngā tikanga o ngā wā ōkawa me ngā wā ōpaki e tuituia ana ki roto i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ko te haereere i ia te wā ki te marae, e whakapūmau ana i ngā akoranga o te ao Māori. He mauritau ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga.

Ka aro nui te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki ki ō rātou matea. Ka poipoi ngā kaimahi i ngā ngākau nuitanga, ngā matea, me te atawhai o ia tamaiti, mā te akiaki i a rātou ki te whakatau i ā rātou ake whiringa i roto i ngā akoranga. E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te waia haere ki ngā tikanga me ngā tūmanako e noho mārama ana. Ka whakarato rātou i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te kōkiri i ā rātou ake akoranga. He wāhi ako motuhake tō ngā pēpi, hei whakatutuki anō hoki i tō rātou atawhai, tae atu ki ō rātou wā moe, me tā rātou kai. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki tā rātou ako.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

E arotahi ana te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi whakaongaonga e whai pūtake ana. Ka whakamahia ngā mahere hōtaka hei ārahi i te whakapuakitanga. Ka whakarerekē haere ngā kaimahi i te hōtaka akoranga i runga anō i ngā ngākau nuitanga ake o ngā tamariki i ia rā. Ka whakawhanake rātou i ngā taumahi e whai pānga ana ki ngā reanga, ngā ngākau nuitanga, ngā tama, me ngā kōtiro. E ākina ana te whakapuaki a te whānau i ō rātou whakaaro mō ngā mahere. He tika te whakatakoto haere o ngā aromatawai i te ako a ngā tamariki. Whakapuakihia ai aua mōhiohio i ia te wā ki te taha o ngā whānau. Kei te tino mārama ngā kaimahi ki te pūtake me te whai huatanga o te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai. Ka noho matua te ako a ngā tamariki.

Ko te ako a ngā tamariki e ārahi ana i ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau. E ākina ana te tautoko mai o ngā mātua i ngā whakahaeretanga o ia rā. He hou te kaiwhakahaere ki tōna tūranga, nō reira e ako tonu ana ia i ngā pūnaha me ngā tukanga ka ārahi i ngā mahi whakahaere. He mahere rautaki tā te whānau e hono ana ki ngā whāinga me ngā tūmanako o Te Ara Tūāpae. E arotahi ana ki te whakapakari i te āheinga reo o te whānau. He auau te pūrongo i te mahere rautaki, ngā pūtea o te kōhanga reo, ngā whakawhanaketanga rawa, me ngā take haumaru. E whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i ngā painga ka puta i tētahi kōhanga reo e tino whakahaerehia ana.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore anō kia whakatinanahia te arohaehae. He mea nui, kia arohaehaengia e te whānau ngā kaimahi katoa, hei āta whakarite i tētahi hōtaka tautoko e hāngai pū ana ki ō rātou matea ngaio. Tāpiri atu ki tēnā, me aroturuki, me tuhi hoki te whānau i ngā mahi arohaehae, i runga anō i ngā tikanga e whakatakotohia ai ki te kaupapa here.

Me whakahou ake ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga, inarā, ko ērā e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru, otirā e whai pānga matua ana ki te waiora ā-tinana, ā-whatumanawa hoki o ngā tamariki. Me whakatinana i ngā tukanga ka kawe tonuhia i ia te wā, i ngā mahi ki te aroturuki, te tuhi haere, me te pūrongo.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te wāhanga e whai ake nei, kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture. Kia whakatutukihia ai e te whānau o te kōhanga reo āna herenga ā-ture, me tahuri rātou ki te:

  • āta whakarite i te whakatinanatanga i te arohaehae auau o ngā kaimahi.
    [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA7 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakaoti i te whānau te arohaehae o ngā kaimahi katoa, me te whakawhanake i tētahi hōtaka tautoko e hāngai pū ana ki ō rātou ake matea ngaio.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakaahurangi i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakaahurangi is beside Whakaahurangi Marae in Stratford. The current kōhanga reo premises opened in 1994 after years of hui, fundraising and whānau dedication to fulfil their goal. The local community helped the whānau during this time.

The kaiwhakahaere is new to his role and responsibilities. The previous kaiako is a local kuia who still supports the children and whānau with te reo Māori and tikanga Māori. Parents travel from the wider community to bring their children to Whakaahurangi.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children demonstrate whanaungatanga?

Children demonstrate whanaungatanga well.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children hear te reo Māori consistently at kōhanga reo. Kaimahi support children to recite their pepeha. The older children share confidently with others. Kaimahi use strategies to enhance children’s language development talking with children, asking questions about their activities, allowing children to mimic the language they hear. They use games, identifying names for colours and clothing to extend their vocabulary. Children are emergent speakers of te reo Māori.

Children learn about the positive aspects of good relationships with others. Whanaungatanga is an important part of children’s daily routines. Kaimahi greet children with warmth on arrival to the kōhanga reo. They model and support interactions of respect, consideration and aroha for others. This is reflected in children’s behaviour for pōwhiri as tangata whenua. Kaimahi support children to participate in karakia, hīmene and waiata. Children enjoy warm relationships with each other and the adults around them.

Children are familiar with their kōhanga reo and marae-based learning environment. Kaimahi encourage children to explore the natural and physical environments, including the marae, and local walkways. Tikanga for formal and informal occasions are integrated into children’s learning. Regular visits to the marae reinforce learning in a te ao Māori setting. Children are settled in their learning.

Children’s learning programme responds to their needs. Kaimahi cater for individual’s interests needs and care by encouraging them to make choices in their learning. A range of resources are accessible for children. Kaimahi support children to familiarise themselves with clear boundaries and expectations. They provide children with opportunities to take responsibility for learning independently. The babies have a separate learning space to respond to their care routines including sleeping and eating. Children enjoy their learning.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s learning programme is focused on meaningful and stimulating activities. Programme planning is used to guide delivery. Kaimahi adjust the learning programme according to children’s interests each day. They develop activities based on ages, interests and gender. Whānau are encouraged to share their ideas to planning. Assessment practices appropriately record children’s learning. This information is regularly shared with whānau. Kaimahi have a clear understanding of the purpose and value of planning, assessment and evaluation. Children’s learning is a priority.

Children’s learning guides whānau management practices. Parents are encouraged to support the daily operations. The kaiwhakahaere is new to their role, therefore is learning about systems and processes to guide management practices. The whānau a strategic plan linked to the goals and objectives of Te Ara Tūāpae. The focus is building whānau language capability. There is regular reporting about the strategic plan, kōhanga reo finances, property developments and safety matters. Children benefit from a well-managed kōhanga reo.

Key Next Steps

Appraisal has not been implemented. It is important for the whānau to appraise all staff to ensure a programme of support is in place specific to their professional needs. Furthermore whānau need to monitor and record appraisal according to the policy.

Policies and procedures need to be updated, particularly health and safety that impacts on children’s physical and emotional wellbeing. Ongoing monitoring, recording and reporting processes need to be implemented.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

ERO identified areas of non-compliance. In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements they need to:

  • ensure appraisal for all employed staff is regularly implemented.
    [R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008, GMA7 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • the whānau complete appraisal of all staff and develop a programme of support specific to their professional needs.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakaahurangi will be in three years.
