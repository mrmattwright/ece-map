Te Aromātai

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Puāwai o Te Aroha ki Whanganui ki te Rāwhiti. I te tau 2014 te whānau i whakanui ai i te ekenga o te kōhanga reo ki ngā tau 30. He wāteatea ngā whare me ngā rauhanga, ā, e tino tiakina ana hoki.

Ka tūmāia ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka taunekeneke rātou ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki. Ka hāngai te titiro o te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea ako me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Ko te ao Māori, ngā tikanga Māori, me te reo Māori ngā ariā e whakamanahia ana i roto i ngā akoranga.

E kōkiri ana tētahi rōpū matua o te whānau me ngā kaimahi i tētahi tino arotake o ngā pūnaha, ngā tukanga me ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga. Ko tā rātou whāinga, ko te whakapakari ake anō i te āheinga o te whānau ki te āta whakahaere i te kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakamahi i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori i a rātou e ako ana?

He pai te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori i a rātou e tūhura ana, e whakatewhatewha ana hoki i tō rātou taiao māoriori, tūturu hoki.

Mana Aotūroa

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā akoranga e pā ana ki tō rātou taiao. E mōhio ana rātou ki te taiao māoriori, tūturu hoki, i a rātou e whakatewhatewha ana i te taiao, ngā kīrehe, me ngā taonga o waho. He pai te whakaritenga o te taiao, ā, ka poipoi hoki i te kori tinana o ngā tamariki. Ki tā ngā tamariki, he wāhi whakahihiri, he wāhi mārie hoki te wāhi o waho, ā, e ākina ana ki te whakatau whakaritenga i a rātou e tākaro ana, e ako ana hoki. Ka tūmāia ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao māoriori, tūturu hoki.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori e hāngai ana ki te taiao māoriori. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa. Ka whakamahi ngā tamariki i te reo ā-waha, ā-tinana hoki ki te whakawhiti kōrero me te whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea ako reo o ngā tamariki. He whai wāhitanga ki te tautoko me te ārahi i te ako a ia tamaiti, me te ako ā-rōpū hoki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamarki te ako i te reo mē ngā tikanga Māori.

Ka kōkiri ngā tamariki tēina i te rere o tā rātou ako. He pārekareka ki a rātou te tūhura me te raweke i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taputapu, hanganga hoki. Ka tūmāia rātou, ā, ka aro nui hoki ki ō rātou hoa me ō rātou pākeke. Ka kaha tautoko ngā kaimahi i ō rātou matea ako me ō rātou matea whakawhanaketanga, ā, ka kitea hoki te pai o ā rātou whakaritenga ki te whakarato i tētahi hōtaka e tika ana. He ākonga pākiki ngā tamariki nei.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He kaha ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Pākehā ki te taha o ō rātou hoa, ngā kaimahi, me ngā pākeke. Kua kitea ngā kaimahi, me aro tā rātou titiro ki te hōtaka akoranga kia whai wāhi ai te whakakaha i te reo Māori a ngā tamariki mā roto i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero whai take me ngā mahi pāhekoheko hoki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka whakapau kaha te whānau, ngā kaimahi, me ngā kuia ki te whakarite anō, ki te whakatōpū anō hoki i tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka kitea ki ngā hui ā-whānau te pai o te tae ā-tinana mai, te pūrongo i ia te wā, me te kaha hoki o ngā whakataunga. Ka kitea ki ngā meneti, he rawaka te aroturuki me te arotake o ngā kaupapa mahi me ngā whakahaeretanga i tāutuhia ai. He pakari te whakahaere i te pūtea, ā, ka whai hua hoki ngā tohutohu tātari kaute hei whakapai ake i ngā whakahaeretanga o roto. Ka whakanuia e te whānau te haere tonutanga o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero me ngā mōhiohio. Kua whakaritea e te kōhanga reo ngā hononga ki ngā ratonga me ngā umanga ngaio, mahi ā-rehe hoki. Kei te whakatōpū i ngā pūnaha, ngā tukanga, me ngā whakaritenga hoki e pā ana ki ngā mahi whakahaere.

Kua tāutuhia e te whānau ngā tino kaupapa matua, ngā mahi matua, ngā wā mahi, ngā tūranga, me ngā kawenga hoki ki te whakatutuki i ō rātou whāinga. Kei ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga o te tūtohinga te tino ārahitanga mō te whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga mō te pae tawhiti me te pae tata. Ka tāutu ngā mahere rautaki me ngā mahere ā-tau i ngā taumata e whai pūtake ana, e tika ana hoki mō te whakawhanaketanga o tēnei wā tonu, te wā waenga, me te wā roa. Kei te pūtake o ngā whakawhanaketanga, ko ngā tamariki me ō rātou matea, ō rātou ngākau nuitanga hoki e rerekē haere ana.

He tino hononga ā-whānau, ā-hapū, ā-iwi hoki tō ngā kaimahi, ā, kua roa nei hoki rātou e whai wāhi ana ki te kōhanga reo. Ka tautoko ngā kaimahi i a rātou anō, ā, he pai tā rātou mahi ngātahi. Ka aro nui, ka whai wāhi nui hoki ki ngā tamariki, ā, ka manaaki hoki. Ka haere tonu te mahi arohaehae. He āhuatanga tēnei e whai hua ana ki ngā kaiako.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore i te rawaka te whānuitanga o te hōtaka akoranga ki te āta whakawero me te whakatutuki hoki i ngā matea ako me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki katoa. Me arotake i te hōtaka, puta noa i ngā wāhanga katoa o ngā whakaritenga. Me hāngai hoki tēnei ki te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromātai, me ngā mahi aromatawai.

Kāhore ngā mahi ki te whakahaere whanonga e whai hua ana. Me tahuri te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakatutuki i te maha rawa o ngā maiki me ngā aituā, me te whakatinana i ngā rautaki papai ka hāpai i te taunekeneke mauritau a ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga e whai ake nei, kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture:

  • kia āta whakarite i te whiwhinga o ngā tamariki i te hōhonutanga o ngā hōtaka akoranga whakaongaonga e hāngai pū ana ki te angamahi marautanga o te kōhanga reo, ā, e whakatinanahia ana ki ngā mahere, te whakapuakitanga, me te aromātai e tohu ana i te māramatanga ki te ako a te mokopuna.[R43 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; C1-4 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia tahuri te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te:

  • whakapā atu ki te tautoko me te ārahitanga o te purapura me ngā kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo
  • arotake i te kaupapa here, ngā tukanga, me ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te whakahaere whanonga.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Puāwai o Te Aroha ki Whanganui ki te Rāwhiti. I te tau 2014 te whānau i whakanui ai i te ekenga o te kōhanga reo ki ngā tau 30. Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki te aronga toi whenuatanga me tō rātou tūmāia ki tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka taunekeneke, ka tūhono hoki rātou ki ngā tāngata me ngā wāhi e whai pānga nui ana ki ā rātou akoranga.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Puāwai o Te Aroha is in Wanganui East. In 2014 whānau celebrated the 30th anniversary of the kōhanga reo. Premises and facilities are spacious and well maintained.

Children are confident in their kōhanga reo. They interact in various contexts. Whānau and kaimahi maintain a clear focus on the learning needs and interests of children. Te ao Māori, tikanga Māori and te reo Māori are valued concepts for learning.

A core group of whānau and kaimahi have been undertaking a thorough review of management systems, processes and practices. They aim to re-build and strengthen whānau capacity to better manage the kōhanga reo.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children use te reo Māori and tikanga Māori in their learning?

Children use te reo Māori and tikanga Māori well while exploring and investigating their natural and physical environment.

Mana Aotūroa

Children enjoy learning about their environment. They are aware of the natural and physical environment where they investigate nature, living creatures and physical elements. The environment is well organised and caters for children’s physical activity. Children find the outdoors an exciting and calming place to be and they are encouraged to make choices while they play and learn. Children are confident in their natural and physical environment.

Children learn te reo Māori and tikanga Māori that is particular to the natural environment. Kaimahi model te reo Māori consistently. Children use verbal and non-verbal ways to communicate and express themselves. Kaimahi are aware of children’s language learning needs. There are opportunities for individual learning support and guidance as well as group learning. Children happily enjoy learning te reo and tikanga Māori.

Young children learn at their own pace. They enjoy exploring and manipulating a range of equipment and structures. They are confident and responsive to their peers and adults. Kaimahi support their learning and developmental needs well, demonstrating good practice in providing an appropriate programme. These children are curious learners.

Key Next Steps

Children frequently speak English with peers, kaimahi and adults. Kaimahi recognise the need to review the learning programme that will provide opportunities to strengthen children’s use of te reo Māori through meaningful conversations and interactions.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau, kaimahi and kuia dedicate time and effort to re-establish and consolidate their kōhanga reo. Regular whānau hui show good attendance, consistent reporting and decision making. Minutes reflect satisfactory monitoring and reviewing of identified action points and operations. Financial management is sound with useful audit advice to improve internal controls. Whānau value ongoing communication and information. The kōhanga reo has an established network of professional and trades services and support. Management systems, processes and practices are being consolidated.

Whānau have identified clear priorities, key tasks, timelines and roles and responsibilities to achieve their goals. Charter policies and procedures give clear guidance for long and short term management and operations. Strategic and annual planning define practical and realistic targets for immediate, medium and long term development. Central to developments are children and their ongoing needs and interests.

Kaimahi have close whānau, hapū and iwi connections and a long association with the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi support each other and work well together. They are responsive, engaging and have caring ways with children. Appraisal has been on-going. This has been a positive experience for kaiako.

Key Next Steps

The overall learning programme does not sufficiently challenge and meet the learning needs, interests of all children. A review of the programme across all areas of practice is required. This should include programme planning, evaluation and assessment practices.

Behaviour management practices are ineffective. It is imperative whānau and kaimahi address the high level of incidents and accidents, and implement positive strategies to support positive interactions amongst children.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

  • ensure that children receive rich and stimulating programmes of learning that are consistent with the kōhanga reo curriculum framework that is informed by planning, implementation, and evaluation that demonstrates an understanding of mokopuna learning.[R43 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; C1-4 Licensing Criteria for Te Kōhanga Reo affiliated to Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendation

ERO developed the following recommendations that whānau and kaimahi:

  • access support and guidance from purapura and district Kōhanga Reo staff
  • review policy, procedures and practice for behaviour management.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Puāwai o Te Aroha is in Wanganui East. In November 2014 whānau celebrated the 30th anniversary of the kōhanga reo. Children display a sense of belonging and confidence in their kōhanga reo. They interact and connect with people and places that are important for their learning.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Puāwai o Te Aroha will be within two years.
