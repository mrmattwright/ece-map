1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūporo Tuhikiterangi ki te pokapū o Rotorua. Ko te reo Māori me ngā tikanga te aronga matua o te whānau. Ka tū tangata rātou hei uri o Ngāti Whakaue me Te Arawa. He poto noa te haerenga mā raro atu ki te marae o Ōhinemutu i te kōhanga reo. Na te kore e taea e te kōhanga reo ki te kawe tonu i ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga, ā, nā tēnei tūāhuatanga kua heke iho te tatauranga tamariki. E mahi ngātahi tonu ana te whānau iti hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae hoki?

Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki te kaha o tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga ki tō rātou kōhanga reo. E whanake ana hoki tō rātou māramatanga ki te whānuitanga o ō rātou hononga ā-iwi.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira  

He māia te tū o ngā tamariki, ā-whatumanawa, ā-tinana hoki. Ka whakahaere ngā tamariki i ētahi āhuatanga o tō rātou atawhai. Ka moe, ka kai hoki rātou i ngā wā e tika ana mō rātou. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te aroha, te manaaki, me te āwhina. Ka awhiawhi ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki nohinohi i ngā wā karakia, ā, he ngākau titikaha, he manawa whakahī hoki tō ngā tamariki tuākana i a rātou e ārahi ana i ngā karakia. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā karakia me ngā waiata. Ka whakatauira ngā tuākana i te āhua o te tākaro me te ako ki ngā tēina. He mauritau, he māia hoki ngā pēpi ki te tākaro ki te taha o ō rātou tuākana. Ka whakatauira, ka akiaki hoki ngā kaimahi i te āhua o te whakawhanaunga ki ētahi atu. Ka whakanuia ngā whakautu me ngā whakamātau katoa a ngā tamariki. He tino mauritau, he tino hāneanea hoki te noho o ngā tamariki.

Ko te waiora o te katoa o te tamaiti tētahi aronga nui o te whānau. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te toro atu ki te hapori me te taiao whānui. Ka kawe atu ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te hikoi haere ki te marae. Ka tūmāia ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki. E whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i ngā whai wāhitanga ako e hāngai pū ana ki ō rātou reanga. Ka taea e ngā tamariki te tākaro takirōpū, takitahi hoki mō ngā wā roa. He manawa reka tō ngā tamariki i a rātou e tākaro ana, e ako ana. Kei te harikoa ngā tamariki.

E tautāwhitia ana ngā tamariki ki te whanaungatanga. Ka aro nui ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea o ngā tamariki. Tērā ngā wā ka rongo ngā tamariki i te harikoa me te rangimārie. He mauritau te āhua o ngā tamariki, ā, he pakari rātou ki te tūhura haere i te whānuitanga o tō rātou wāhi. Ka katakata, ka tākaro hoki ngā tamariki ki te taha o ētahi atu. He mātātoa tā rātou aro nui ki ā rātou kēmu. Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tō rātou whai wāhi nui ki te kōhanga reo. E whakarato ana te whānau i tētahi wāhi atawhai mō ngā tamariki. He haumaru, he mauritau hoki ngā tamariki.

Ka whakahihiritia, ka whakaohoohotia ngā tamariki ki te taiao ako reo. Ka āta aro atu, ka āta whakarongo atu hoki ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. Ka whakatō ngā kaimahi i ngā tikanga me te reo o Te Arawa ki te hōtaka akoranga. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki tuākana ki te whakawhitiwhiti whakaaro ki ētahi atu. Ka tāwhai ngā tamariki hou me ngā pēpi i ngā kupu me ngā kīanga māmā, ā, ka whakamahi hoki rātou i te reo ā-tinana ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro. Ka āta whakawhānui te kaiako i te reo o ngā tamariki mā te ngahau me ngā huarahi whakahihiri. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo Māori. E whanake ana ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

E whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i ngā hua o ngā mahere e whai pūtake ana me tētahi hōtaka akoranga i āta whakaritea ai. E hāngai ana ngā mahere ā-tau ki ngā kaupeka, ā, he mārama ngā hononga ki Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira me te rākau. E hāngai ana ngā hōtaka akoranga ki te rerekē haere o te rākau i roto i te roanga o te tau. Ka whakamahere ngātahi ngā kaimahi, ā, kua āta whakaritea anō hoki ā rātou mahi aromatawai. Ko ētahi o aua whakaritenga, ko ngā pūkete a ia tamaiti, ko ngā pūrongo ahu whakamua, me te mātai tamaiti. Ka whakamahia hoki te pae pāpāho pāpori kia whiwhi wawe te whānau i ngā kōrero urupare e pā ana ki tēnā e ākona ana e ngā tamariki. Kei te tino mōhio ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ā rātou taumahi ako.

E whanake ana te arotake whaiaro. Ko te pakari o te whakahaere pūtea tētahi āhuatanga i noho matua ai. Kua whai kawenga, kia āta aro atu hoki te whānau i roto i ā rātou mahi ki te tohatoha pūtea. Nā tēnei āhuatanga, i heke te tatauranga o ngā kaimahi, me ngā haora mahi anō hoki o ētahi. Ka whakatutuki katoatia ngā take e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. E hāngai ana ngā whāinga rautaki ki Te Ara Tūāpae. E ārahi ana ngā mahere ā-tau i te whānau. E whai whakaaro ana te whānau ki ngā huarahi ka whakapiki ake i te tatauranga tamariki, kia ngāwari ake ai te taumahatanga o ngā pūtea. E noho ngākau nui ana te whānau ki te whakahaere i tō rātou kōhanga reo kia puāwai mai ai ngā tino painga mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore anō te haere tonutanga o te aromātai hōtaka kia whakaōkawatia. Tū ai ngā kōrerorerotanga i ia te wā mō tēnā i whai hua ai, me tēnā hei whakapai ake pea. Kāhore anō ngā kaimahi kia tuhi i aua kōrero. E mōhio ana rātou, mā te tū auau o ngā hui me te tuhi i ngā whakawhiti whakaaro, ka whakatakotohia te tūāpapa o tētahi pūrongo hei whakatakoto ki ia hui ā-whānau.

Kei te tīmatanga tonu te whakawhanaketanga o te arotake whaiaro. Ahakoa e mōhio ana te whānau ki te hua o te arotake whaiaro i ia te wā, kāhore anō kia āta whakatūturuhia taua mahi ki ngā whakaritenga. Kua kōrerohia te tūtohinga me ngā kaupapa here, engari kāhore anō aua kōrero kia whakaōkawatia. 

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture. Kia tutuki i te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā herenga ā-ture, me tahuri rātou ki te: 

  • whakarite i tētahi tukanga ki te aromātai i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga 
    [R43 (1)(a)(i)(ii) Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; C1-4 me te C7-10 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]  

  • āta whakarite i te haere tonutanga o tētahi tukanga ki te arotake whaiaro, hei hāpai i te kōhanga reo ki te whakapūmau me te whakapai tonu i te kounga o te mātauranga me te atawhai.
    [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA6 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Te Taunakitanga

Kia toro atu te whānau ki te tautoko o te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā whakaritenga i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo, hei whai ake.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūporo Tuhikiterangi i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūporo Tuhikiterangi is based in central Rotorua. Te reo Māori and tikanga is a priority for the whānau. They identify strongly as Ngāti Whakaue, Te Arawa. Ohinemutu marae is within walking distance of the kōhanga reo. An inability to continue transporting children has resulted in a roll decrease for the kōhanga reo. The small whānau continue to work together in the interests of the children.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children show their understanding of who they are and where they come from?

Children show a strong sense of belonging to their Kōhanga reo. They are developing understanding of their wider tribal links.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are emotionally and physically confident. Children manage aspects of their own care. They sleep and eat according to their needs. Children experience aroha, manaaki and awhina. Kaimahi cuddle younger children at karakia time, and older lead the karakia with confidence and pride. Children actively participate in karakia and waiata.  Tuakana show their teina how to play and learn. Babies are comfortable and confident to play with their tuākana. Kaimahi model and encourage positive relationships with others. All children’s responses or attempts to respond are affirmed. Children are very settled and comfortable.

Children’s holistic well-being is a priority for whānau. Children are provided with opportunities to interact in the local and wider environment. Kaimahi take children for walks to the local marae. Children are confident in a wide range of contexts. Children experience age appropriate learning opportunities. Children can play in groups and alone for long periods. They have a sense of satisfaction in their play and learning. Children are happy.

Children are embraced by whanaungatanga. Kaimahi are responsive to children’s needs. There are times when children experience times of happiness and calm. Children appear secure and explore their environment confidently. Children laugh and play with others.  They are actively engaged in their play. Children show they have a place in the kōhanga reo. Whānau provide a loving environment for children. Children are safe and secure.

Children are interested and motivated by the language learning environment. Children pay attention to and listen to te reo Māori. Kaimahi embed Te Arawa tikanga and reo into the learning programme.  Older children enjoy talking with others. New children and babies mimic words and simple phrases and use body language to express their ideas. The kaiako deliberately extend children’s language in fun and interesting ways. Children enjoy learning te reo Maori. Children are developing as speakers of te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Children benefit from purposeful planning and a well organised learning programme. Annual plans are based on the seasons with clear links to ngā taumata whakahirahira and the rākau. Learning programmes are based on the changes of the rākau over the year. The kaimahi plan collaboratively and have established assessment practices. This includes individual profiles, progression reporting, and mātai te tamaiti.  Social media is utilised to provide instant feedback to whānau about what children are learning. The kaimahi know the children well. Children enjoy their learning activities.

Self-review is developing.  Sound financial management has been a priority. Whānau have been responsible and responsive to careful use of resourcing. This has created the need to reduce the number of kaimahi and the hours of others. Health and safety matters are all attended to.  Strategic goals align to Te Ara Tūāpae. Annual planning guides the whānau. Whānau are considering ways to increase the roll to alleviate financial pressures. The whānau are committed to managing their kōhanga reo to ensure positive outcomes for children.

Key Next Steps

Ongoing programme evaluation is not formalised. Regular informal discussions are held on what went well and what could be better. Kaimahi are yet to record these. They acknowledge that regular meetings and recording of discussions will provide the basis of a written report for each whanau hui.

Self-review is at the early stages of development. Whilst the whānau acknowledge the value of regular self-review, this is not embedded into practise. Some discussions have been held on the Charter and policies but are yet to be formalised.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

ERO identified areas of non-compliance. In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements they need to:

  • establish a process for evaluating the effectiveness of the learning programme
    [R43 (1) (a) (i) (ii) Education (Early Childhood Service) Regulations 2008; C1-4 and C7-10]
  • ensure there is an ongoing process of self-review to help the kōhanga maintain and improve the  quality of its education and care.
    [R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA6 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4  Recommendation

The whānau seek support for Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust to address the next steps of this report.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūporo Tuhikiterangi will be in three years.
