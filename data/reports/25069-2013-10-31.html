2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kei Otara i Tamaki Makaurau Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kupenga. Kei tētahi whare paku nei te kōhanga reo. He taiao karakara, maioha, whakaihiihi tō rātou mō ngā tamariki nohinohi.

I kōwhiria e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira hei wāhi arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te ako mō ngā mea e ora ana i tō rātou taiao. He pai ki ngā tamariki ēnei wā ka taea e rātou te tūtaki ki ngā tāngata hou me te haere ki ngā taiao hou. Ka waiata ngā tamariki i ngā waiata e whakaū ana i a Mana Whenua me te āwhina i a rātou ki te ako mō Papatuanuku, mō Ranginui me ngā atua Māori. He pai ki ngā tamariki ngā mahi ka whai wāhi rātou ki tō rātou taiao o Papatuanuku.

He maha ngā āhua takatika kei te hōtaka akoranga o ngā tamariki mō ia rā. He pai te rere o ngā tikanga, ngā kaupapa mahi me ngā wā waiata, ā, he wā whai take hoki ēnei mō ngā tamariki. Ka hapai angitū ēnei mahi i te whanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki me te poipoi i te ngākaunui me te auahatanga o tēna, o tēna o ngā tamariki. He takatika hoki te pāhekoheko o ngā tamariki me o rātou hoa, te whānau me ngā kaumātua. Ka whakaatu ngā tamariki i te aroha, te manaaki me te awhi i a rātou e mahi ana i ngā mahi o ia rā. I ia te wā ka whakamahi rātou i Te Reo Māori i ringa o te tautoko me te whakahau ā nga kaimahi. Te ahua nei he harikoa, he tau ngā tamariki.

He pai te whakahaere o te whānau i te kōhanga reo i runga i tō rātou ngākaunui ki a rātou tamariki. He kaha hoki te whai wāhi o te whānau ki te kōhanga reo. He nui hoki ngā mema o te whānau ka tae atu ki ngā hui. Kei te mārama ki te whānau me ngā kaimahi ngā wahanga o ngā mahi o te kōhanga reo e pai ana te haere me ngā wāhi hei whakapai ake. I tēnei wā kei te whai te whānau i tētahi huarahi whakapai i ngā wā katoa.

He pai te whanake o ngā mahere hōtaka. Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i ngā hōtaka akoranga pūmau whakaihiihi, whakaongaonga hoki. He kaiwhakaaro whakamuri ngā kaimahi. Ka aromātai rātou i ia wā i te tōtika o ngā kaupapa me ngā ahuatanga o te hōtaka o ia rā mā ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki whānui hei tuhi i te ako me te ahunga whakamua o ngā tamariki.

Ka mahi tahi ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakarato i tētahi taiao haumaru, ahuru hoki mā ngā tamariki.

Ngā Mahi ā Meake Nei

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kohanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Titiro Hāngai a te Arotake

I mua atu i te whakahaerenga o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kupenga kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake mā te whai i ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ki a rātou.

Ī muri mai i ngā whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenganui i te rōpū arotake o Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau, ka whakaritea e rātou ngā tino kaupapa motuhake hei arotakehanga. I titiro ēnei whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero ki runga i ngā kōrero i te pupuritia e te kōhanga reo (e whai wāhia anangā kōrero arotake whaiaro),ā, me te whānuitanga o ngā take pūmanawa hei arotakehanga i runga i ngā putanga e whai hua ana ki ngā mokopuna oTe Kūpenga.

Ko te kaupapa i whakaritea e te whānau hei titirohanga mā tēnei arotake ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

E titiro ana te katoa o ngā arotake a ERO i roto i ngā kōhanga, ki runga i te kounga o te mātauranga. E ai ki a ERO, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o:

  • ngā arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me ngā whakamāhere rautaki; me
  • ngā whakamāhere o ngā hōtaka akoranga, ngā aro matawai me ngā aro mātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

Ka hāngai Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira ki ngā wāhi katoa o te ako me te whanaketanga o te tamaiti e ai ki tā te Māori titiro ki te ao.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Mana Reo.Ka kōrero Māori ngā tamariki puta noa i te hōtaka akoranga. He maia rātou ki te mihi, te karakia me te waiata. Ka rongo ngā tamariki ki te reo me ngā tikanga nō ō rātou ake hapū. Kei te mōhio rātou ki ngā kupu me ngā kianga māmā ki te reo Māori. Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i ngā tautoko tōtika ki te taki whakapapa me te kōrero i ngā reo māmā, ngā reo e mōhio ana rātou. Kei te whanake te mōhio o ngā tamariki me ō rātou kaha ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Mana Atua. Kei te maia ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo. He takatika tō rātou pāhekoheko me o rātou hoa, o rātou whānau me ngā kaumātua. He mātātoa te arahi me te whai wāhi o ngā tamariki ki te karakia me te waiata. Kei te mōhio pai rātou ki o rātou mahi mō ia rā. Ka whai wāhi noa ngā tamariki ki ngā mahi whānui. Ka whakawhiti ngā kaimahi i o rātou mōhio ki ngā atua Māori mā ngā kaupapa me ngā whakawhitinga kōrero whānui. Te āhua nei he harikoa, he tau ngā tamariki.

Mana Aotūroa. He maha ngā whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te tuhura ki tō rātou taiao a tinana, a rohe, whānui hoki.Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te ako mō ngā mea e ora ana i tō rātou taiao. He pai ki ngā tamariki ēnei wā ka taea e rātou te tūtaki ki ngā tāngata hou me te haere ki ngā taiao hou. Ka whakaatu ngā tamariki i tō rātou whakaute me tō rātou kaha tiaki i tō rātou taiao.

Mana Tangata. Kei te ako ngā tamariki ki te taki o rātou whakapapa. Kei te nui haere tō rātou mōhio ko wai rātou, nō hea rātou, nō wai hoki rātou. Nā te tuakiri me te mōhio o ngā tamariki ko wai rātou, ka māia rātou ki te mahi me ētahi atu tāngata i te whānui o ngā āhuatanga. Kei te mārama ngā tamariki ki te wāriu me te hiranga o te manaaki, te aroha me te awhi i o rātou hoa, ngā kaimahi me te whānau whānui.

Mana Whenua. Ka rongo ngā tamariki ki te whānui o ngā mahi a mana whenua mā te whakapapa me ētahi atu kaupapa ako e pa ana ki mana whenua. Ka taki pepeha, taki whakapapa rātou. Ka waiata rātou me te whakaū i a mana whenua hei āwhina i a rātou ki te ako ō Papatuanuku. He pai ki ngā tamariki ngā mahi ka whai wāhi rātou ki a Papatuanuku.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

E aro mātai ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i ngā wā katoa, i te kounga o ngā whakamahere me ngā aro mātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo ki te titiro ki te kaha o te whānau o te kōhanga ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere me te aro turuki i te katoa o ngā wāhanga whakahaere o te kōhanga reo mō a rātou tamariki me te whānau. Kei roto i te wāhanga o te whakamahere me te aro mātai ko te whakamahere hōtaka me te aro turuki i te whanaketanga o te akoranga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakamahere hōtaka. He pai te whakatakoto o ngā mahere hōtaka. Ka arahi ngā mahi whakamahere hōtaka i ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau ki te tuku rātou i ngā hōtaka akoranga ki ngā tamariki. He pīngawingawi ngā whakamahere mō ngā taina, ā, ko te whāinga ko te whakatutuki i ngā matea me ngā ngākaunui o tēnā, o tēnā o rātou. He pai te whakatakoto a tuhi ngā mahere mō ngā tuakana, ā, he whānui te amiki o ēnei. Ka whakaatu i ngā ngākaunui o ngā tamariki.

Ka whakarato te mahere a tau i te tirohanga roa ō te tau. He ngātahi te whakarite a ngā mahere hōtaka nā ngā mema o te whānau me ngā kaimahi. Ka hāngai hoki ngā mahi whakamahere ki ngā kaupapa e pā ana ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui. Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i ngā hōtaka akoranga kua pai te whakamahere.

Te whakahaere o te whānau.He pai te whakahaere o te whānau i te kōhanga reo i runga i tō rātou ngākaunui ki a rātou tamariki. Ka tū ngā hui a whānau i ia wā, ā, ka tae atu ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau. He whānui ngā pūrongo ka tuku ki te hui a whānau. Ko ētahi o ēnei ko ngā pūtea, te whakahaere kaimahi, te hauora me te haumaru me te paerangatiratanga. Mā ēnei pūrongo ka mōhio pai te katoa ki ngā mahi o te kōhanga reo. Nō reira ka taea e te whānau te whakatau kaupapa i runga i te mōhio.

Te arotake whaiaro. He pai te whakatau o ngā mahi arotake whaiaro.Kei te mārama ki te whānau me ngā kaimahi ngā wahanga o ngā mahi o te kōhanga reo e pai ana te haere me ngā wāhi hei whakapai ake. I tēnei wā kei te whai te whānau i tētahi huarahi whakapai i ngā wā katoa.

Te whakamahere rautaki. Ka whakamahi te whānau i Te Ara Tūāpae hei arahi i tō rātou ahunga rautaki. Kua whakamāramatia ngā whāinga me ngā rautaki hei tutuki i ngā whāinga a meāke nei. Ko ētahi o ngā whāinga ko te whakawhānui ake i te whare kōhanga, te whakakaha i ngā whanaungatanga me te kura kaupapa Māori a rohe, te ako a whānau me te reo Māori. Ko tētahi whāinga matua ko te whakawhanake i ngā pukenga o ngā kaimahi. Nā te arotake i te mahere rautaki i ia wā ka āhei ai te aroturuki i ngā mahinga rautaki. Ka whakarato te mahere rautaki i te ahunga mārama ki te whānau.

Te aromatawai. Ka whakamārama ngā pūkete mahi a ngā tamariki i o rātou whai wāhitanga ki te hōtaka akoranga. Ka kōrerotia te ahunga o ngā tamariki i ia wā. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā kōrero paki, ngā tirohanga me ngā arowhai a whanake hei kohikohi kōrero e pā ana ki te ako me te ahunga whakamua o ngā tamariki. Ka kohi hoki rātou i ngā tauira mahi toi a ngā tamariki. Ka whai wāhi te whānau ki ngā pūkete mahi me ngā puka mahi o a rātou tamariki i ngā wā katoa.

Te aromātai. Nā te whai whakaaro whakamuri ki te ako o ngā tamariki me te hōtaka ko te mutunga mai ko ngā rerekētanga takatika. Ka tāutu ngā kaimahi i tēnā, i tēnā wāhi hei whakapai ake i o rātou mahi me te whakatutuki i ēnei. Ka ū ngā kaimahi ki te whakapai ake kia takatika ngā whakarerekētanga ki ngā mahi mō a rātou tamariki.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kupenga is situated in Otara, South Auckland. The kōhanga reo operates from a small, compact building. The environment is colourful, welcoming and stimulating for young children.

The whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus area for this review. Children have regular opportunities to learn about living things within their immediate and local surroundings. Children enjoy these occasions where they are able to meet new people and be in new environments. Children sing waiata that affirm mana whenua and help them to learn about Papatūānuku, Ranginui and the atua Māori. Childrenenjoy activities that expose them to their natural physical environment.

The children’s daily learning programme has many positive features. Tikanga practices, kaupapa and waiata sessions flow smoothly and are purposeful learning times for children. These practices successfully support children’s te reo Māori development and help nurture their individual interests and creativity. Children interact and relate to each other, whānau and kaumātua in positive ways. Children display aroha, manaaki and awhi as they go about their daily routines and activities. They regularly use te reo Māori with support and prompts from kaimahi. Children appear happy and settled.

The whānau manages the kōhanga reo well because of their commitment to their children. There are high levels of whānau participation in the kōhanga reo. Whānau maintain high attendances at meetings. The whānau and staff members are clear about those parts of the kōhanga reo operations that work well and the areas where improvements could be made. The staff and whānau are on a journey of continual improvement.

Programme plans are well developed. Children are provided with sound learning programmes that are exciting and stimulating. Kaimahi are reflective thinkers. They regularly evaluate the effectiveness of kaupapa and aspects of the children’s daily programme. Kaimahi use various methods to record children’s learning and progress.

The kaimahi and whānau members work together and make every effort to provide children with a safe, secure and comfortable environment.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

4 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira encompass all areas of a child’s learning and development according to a Māori world view.

Areas of good performance

Mana Reo.Children use te reo Māori consistently throughout the learning programme. They are confident in their recitation and singing of mihimihi, karakia and waiata. Children are exposed to language and tikanga specific to their hapū. They know basic words and phrases in te reo Māori. Children receive effective support when they recite whakapapa and use simple and familiar language. Children are developing their knowledge and use of te reo Māori.

Mana Atua.Children are confident in their kōhanga reo environment. They interact and relate to each other, whānau and kaumātua in positive ways. Children actively lead and participate in karakia and waiata. They know their daily routines well and readily take part in various activities. Kaimahi transfer their knowledge of ngā atua to children through various kaupapa and discussions. Children appear happy and settled.

Mana Aoturoa.Children have regular opportunities to explore their local and wider physical environment. They learn about living things within their immediate and local surroundings. Children enjoy these occasions where they are able to meet new people and be in new environments. Children show respect and care for their environment.

Mana Tangata.Children are learning to recite their whakapapa. They are becoming more aware of who they are, where they come from and to whom they belong. Children’s sense of identity and belonging gives them the confidence to interact with others in a range of situations. Children understand the value and the importance of manaaki, aroha and awhi when they are with their peers, kaimahi and the wider whānau.

Mana Whenua.Children experience a range of mana whenua activities through whakapapa and other relevant kaupapa ako. They recite pepeha and whakapapa. They sing waiata that affirm mana whenua and help them to learn about Papatūānuku, Ranginui and the atua Māori. Childrenenjoy activities that expose them to their natural physical environment.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of the operations. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Programme planning.Programme plans are well developed. Programme planning guides the staff and whānau members when they deliver learning programmes to children. Planning for the younger children is flexible and aims to cater for their individual needs and interests. Plans for the older children are well documented and comprehensive in detail. They reflect what children are interested in.

An annual plan provides an overview of the programme for the year. Programme plans are developed collaboratively by whānau and staff members. Planning incorporates te ao Māori kaupapa and the wider world. Children are provided with well planned programmes of learning.

Whānau management.The whānau manages the kōhanga reo well because of their commitment to their children. Regular whānau hui occur and include both staff and whānau members. A wide variety of reports are presented at whānau hui. These include finance, personnel management, health and safety and administration. These reports help to keep everyone up to date about kōhanga reo operations. This enables whānau to make informed decisions.

Self review.Self review practice is well established. The whānau is clear about aspects of kōhanga reo operations that are working well and the areas where improvements can be made. The staff and whānau are on a journey of continual improvement.

Strategic planning.The whānau use Te Ara Tuāpae to guide their strategic direction. Objectives and strategies for achievement of future goals are outlined. Goals include extending the kōhanga reo building, strengthening partnerships with local kura kaupapa Māori, whānau learning and te reo Māori. A high priority includes developing kaimahi skills. Regular review of the strategic plan ensures ongoing monitoring of strategic actions. The strategic plan provides clear direction for whānau.

Assessment.Profile books highlight children’s involvement in the learning programme. Children’s progress is discussed regularly. GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
