1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

‘Pono ki te kaupapa Puna ko te reo’

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo Tuatahi o Tokoroa ki te tāone o Tokoroa. He hononga tōna ki ngā rohe o Te Waiariki me Raukawa. Nō ngā iwi maha o te Ika-a-Māui o Aotearoa ngā mema o te whānau o tēnei kōhanga reo. Ka whakarato te kōhanga reo nei i tētahi wāhi e hāpai ana i te whānau me ngā tamariki, me tō rātou whakakoia i tō rātou tuakiri, tō rātou reo, me ngā taonga tuku iho.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ki tā te whānau titiro, e manaakitia ana, e whakamanatia ana hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakamahere rātou mō ngā āhuatanga katoa o ngā akoranga a ā rātou tamariki, ā, ka āta hāpai, āta whakapakari hoki i a rātou hei Māori. Ka arotahi te arotake nei i te whai huatanga o te whānau ki te poipoi i te tuakiri, te aronga toi whenuatanga, me te tū pakari o ngā tamariki.

Kei te hōtaka akoranga te whānuitanga o ngā whai wāhitanga ako mā ngā tamariki. Ka kitea te tūnga pai o te whānau ki te poipoi i te tuakiri, te aronga toi whenuatanga, me te tū pakari o ngā tamariki. Kei te hōtaka akoranga te aronga nui ki ngā atua Māori. Ka ako ngā tamariki ki te whakamana i te taiao. Ka whakapūmautia ngā whakapapa o ngā tamariki, puta noa i te hōtaka. Ka kitea te kaha o te tū tangata o ngā tamariki, me tō rātou hononga ki tō rātou kōhanga reo, ō rātou hapū, ō rātou iwi, ō rātou marae, me tō rātou hapori. E pou here ana te whanaungatanga i ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā pākeke. Kua āta tuituia te reo me ngā tikanga Māori puta noa i te hōtaka akoranga. He māoriori te whakamahinga o te reo Māori hei reo kōrerorero i te kōhanga reo. E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi. Ko ngā wāhi o waho, o roto hoki e whakawātea ana i ngā tamariki ki te tūhura me te mahi auaha i roto i ā rātou akoranga. Ka whai hua te whānau ki te poipoi i te katoa o te waiora, te whakawhanaketanga hoki o ia tamaiti.

Ka noho pūmau te whānau ki te kōhanga reo. He rawaka ā rātou pūnaha e whakarite ana i te whai huatanga o ngā whakahaeretanga mō ngā tamariki. Kua whakaritea ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakamahere me te aromatawai. Ka whakamōhiotia te whānau i ia te wā mō tēnā ka ākona e ngā tamariki, me te whai huatanga o tā rātou ahu whakamua. Ko te ngātahi o ngā mahi whakahaere, whakatau whakaritenga hoki e hāpai ana i te whānau ki te whakarato i tētahi taiao e whakamana ana i te waiora me te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki.

Kei ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga ētahi āhuatanga hei whakapakari ake. Ka hāngai ēnei ki te whakamahere rautaki, te arotake whaiaro, me te aromātai hōtaka. He mea nui kia toro atu te whānau ki te tautoko me ngā tohutohu āwhina.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga reo (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

He pēhea te poipoi i te tuakiri, te aronga toi whenuatanga, me te tū pakari o ngā tamariki?

Ka whai hua te poipoi i te waiora me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Kei te hōtaka akoranga te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo whai wāhitanga ako mā ngā tamariki. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā akoranga ōkawa, akoranga ōpaki hoki. Ka kitea tō rātou ngākau nui ki ngā tūmomo taumahi katoa e wātea ana ki a rātou.

Ko te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou tūnga ki te ao whānui, e hāpaitia ana nā te aronga nui o te hōtaka ki ngā atua Māori. Ka ako ngā tamariki ki te whakamana i te taiao. E tupu ana tō rātou māramatanga ki te whai pānga nui o te taiao ki te āhua o tō rātou noho i ia rā.

Ka whakapūmautia te whakapapa o ngā tamariki nā te hōtaka akoranga. Ka kitea te kaha o te tū tangata o ngā tamariki, me tā rātou hononga ki tō rātou kōhanga reo, ō rātou hapū, ō rātou iwi, ō rātou marae, me tō rātou hapori.

E pou here ana te whanaungatanga i ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā pākeke. Ka whai hua ngā taunekeneke, ā, ka kitea hoki te aroha. Ko te pai o ngā hononga te tino tūāpapa mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamanatia ngā tamariki hei ākonga pakari.

Ka whai hua te āta tuitui i te reo me ngā tikanga Māori puta noa i te hōtaka akoranga. He pakari ngā tamariki tuākana ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori. Ka kitea te mōhiotanga o ngā pēpi ki te reo Māori mā ā rātou whakautu, ā rātou neke ā-tinana hoki. He māoriori te whakamahinga i te reo Māori hei reo kōrerorero ki te kōhanga reo.

E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā rauemi. Kei ngā wāhi o roto, o waho hoki ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura me te mahi auaha i roto i ā rātou akoranga. He maha ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te kōkiri i ā rātou ake akoranga, te kawe i ngā wero, me te whakatutuki i ngā whāinga ako nā rātou anō i whakarite ai.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

Ka pēhea te hāpai o ngā pūnaha i te kōhanga reo ki te hāpai i te tuakiri, te aronga toi whenuatanga, me te tū pakari o ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao haumaru?

He rawaka ngā pūnaha ā te whānau hei āta whakarite i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō te painga o te whakawhanaketanga o ā rātou tamariki – ā-ahurea, ā-tangata, ā-mātauranga hoki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He matawhānui ngā tukanga whakamahere, aromatawai hoki, ā, kua āta tuhia hoki. Ka āta tāutu te kiko o te hōtaka i te ahurea me te tuakiri o ngā tamariki, ā, ka noho matua aua āhuatanga. Ko ngā mahi aromatawai e whakanui ana i ngā akoranga o ngā tamariki, ā, ka whai pūtake hoki. Ka noho mōhio te whānau ki tēnā e ākona ana e ngā tamariki, me tā rātou ahu whakamua.

Ko te ngātahi o te whakahaere me te whakatau whakaritenga e hāpai ana i te whānau ki te whakarato i tētahi taiao e whakamana ana i te waiora me te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki. Kua āta whakatakotohia te aroturuki pūtea, ngā pūnaha pūrongo, me ngā kaupapa here me ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. Ko te ako, te hauora, me te haumaru o ngā tamariki e noho matua ana ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka whakamanatia te whānau kia mātātoa ai tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki te ako o ā rātou tamariki, ā, ka noho pūmau hoki rātou ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. Ka whakamahia te tūtohinga hei ārahi i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kua arotakengia taua tuhinga, ā, e whakahou ake anōtia e te whānau. E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki e te whānau me ō rātou wawata e arotahi ana ki tō rātou whakawhanaketanga me tō rātou waiora.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake

Te whakamahere rautaki. Kāhore i te mārama te mahere rautaki. Kāhore i te tino mārama te hononga ki waenga i ngā mahere hōtaka mā ngā tamariki me ngā kaupapa matua, ngā wawata hoki o te wā roa e wawatatia ana mō te kōhanga reo. Kua tae ki te wā kia toro atu te whānau ki te tautoko e tika ana mō te whakawhanaketanga o tētahi mahere rautaki mō te wā roa.

Te arotake whaiaro. He whāiti te arotake whaiaro. Kāhore i te rawaka te kiko o ngā pūrongo ki ngā hui ā-whānau hei āta whakarite i te aroturuki o ngā whakahaeretanga i te kōhanga reo i ia te wā.

Te aromātai hōtaka. Kāhore te aromātai hōtaka e arotahi ana ki ngā whakapaitanga mō akoranga a ngā tamariki. Me tuhi i tēnā i whai hua ai me ngā whakarerekētanga hei whakatutuki, hei hāpai i ngā kaimahi ki te aroturuki i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia toro atu te whānau ki Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te

  • whakawhanake i tētahi mahere rautaki mō te whakawhanake tonutanga o te kōhanga reo
  • whakapakari i te arotake whaiaro hei aroturuki i ngā āhuatanga katoa o ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo
  • whakawhanake i tētahi tukanga e pā ana ki te aroturuki i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga mō ngā tamariki.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu mō ngā Ratonga Arotake Māori (Te Uepū ā-Motu)

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

‘Pono ki te kaupapa

Puna ko te reo’

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tuatahi o Tokoroa is located in the Tokoroa town. It has links to the Waiariki and Raukawa regions. Whānau members at the kōhanga reo affiliate to a number of iwi across the north island of New Zealand. This kōhanga reo provides a supportive place where whānau and children are able to affirm their identity, language and traditions.

The whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus for this review. Whānau believe the kōhanga reo is a place where children are well cared for and valued. They plan for all aspects of their children’s learning and strongly support and strengthen who they are as Māori. This review focuses on how well the whānau nurtures the identity, belonging and confidence children.

The learning programme includes a variety of learning opportunities for children. It is evident that the whānau is well placed to nurture children’s identity, belonging and confidence. The learning programme has a strong focus on ngā atua Māori. Children learn to respect the environment. Children’s whakapapa is reinforced throughout the programme. Children display a strong sense of self and connection to their kōhanga reo, hapū, iwi, marae and community. Whanaungatanga underpins relationships between children and adults. Te reo and tikanga Māori are successfully woven throughout the learning programme. Speaking te reo Māori is the normal language of communication in the kōhanga reo. Children have access to a range of resources. The indoor and outdoor areas allow children to explore and be creative in their learning. The whānau successfully nurtures the holistic wellbeing and development of their children.

Whānau are committed to the kōhanga reo. They have adequate systems to ensure the operations of the kōhanga reo benefit children. Planning and assessment processes are in place. Whānau are kept informed about what children learn and how well they are progressing. Collective management and decision making supports whānau to provide an environment where children’s sense of wellbeing and belonging is valued.

There are some aspects of the kōhanga reo operations that whānau need to strengthen. These include strategic planning, self review and programme evaluation. It is important that whānau seek support and advice.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

How well is the children’s identity, belonging and confidence nurtured?

Children’s wellbeing and development is successfully nurtured.

Areas of good performance

The learning programme provides a variety of learning opportunities for children. Children willingly engage in both formal and informal learning. They display a great deal of interest in all activities that are available.

Children’s understanding of their place in the wider world is supported through a programme that has a strong focus on ngā atua Māori. Children learn to respect the environment. They have a growing understanding of how important the physical environment is to their daily lives.

Children’s whakapapa is reinforced throughout the learning programme. Children display a strong sense of self and connection to their kōhanga reo, hapū, iwi, marae and community.

Whanaungatanga underpins relationships between children and adults. Interactions are meaningful and loving. Positive relationships provide a firm foundation for children’s learning. Children are viewed as confident learners.

Te reo and tikanga Māori are successfully woven throughout the learning programme. The older children use te reo Māori confidently. Babies demonstrate their understanding of te reo Māori through their responses and body language. Te reo Māori is the normal language of communication throughout the kōhanga reo.

Children have access to a range of resources. The indoor and outdoor areas include opportunities for children to explore and be creative in their learning. There are many opportunities for children to direct their own learning, undertake challenges and achieve learning goals they have set for themselves.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How well do kōhanga reo systems support children’s identity, belonging and confidence in an environment that is safe and secure?

The whānau have adequate systems to ensure the operations of the kōhanga reo benefit the cultural, social and educational development of their children.

Areas of good performance

Planning and assessment processes are comprehensive and well documented. Programme content clearly identifies children’s culture and identity, both of which are priorities. Assessment practice acknowledges children’s learning and is meaningful. Whānau are well informed about what children learn and how well they are progressing.

Collective management and decision making supports whānau to provide an environment where children’s sense of wellbeing and belonging is valued. The financial monitoring and reporting systems and health and safety policies and practice are clearly set out. Children’s learning, health and safety are priorities in the kōhanga reo.

Whānau are empowered to be active participants in their children’s learning and they are committed to the philosophy of the Kōhanga Reo. The charter is used to guide the operation of the kōhanga reo. This document has undergone review and is being updated by the whānau. Children are well supported by a whānau who have aspirations that focus on their development and wellbeing.

Areas for development

Strategic planning. The strategic plan lacks clarity. There is confusion between programme planning for children and desired long term priorities and aspirations for the kōhanga reo. It is time for whānau to seek support for the development of a long term strategic plan.

Self review. Self review is limited. Reporting at whānau hui lacks sufficient detail to ensure that all aspects of the kōhanga reo operations are regularly monitored.

Programme evaluation. Programme evaluation does not focus on improvements for children’s learning. Documenting what worked and what changes should occur will assist kaimahi to monitor the effectiveness of the learning programme.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO LicensedKōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends the whānau seek support from the Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust to:

  • develop a strategic plan for the ongoing development of the kōhanga reo
  • strengthen self review in order to monitor all aspects of the kōhanga reo operations
  • develop a process for monitoring the effectiveness of the learning programme for children.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
