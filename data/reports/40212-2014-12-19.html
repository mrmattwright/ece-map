1. Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

He kōhanga reo noho marae a Te Kōhanga Reo o Tanatana, ā, e pā tata atu ana ki Waimana. Ka āta whakarite ai te whānau o tēnei kōhanga reo kia atawhaitia, kia arohaina hoki ngā tamariki, ā, kia whiwhi hoki rātou i ngā whai wāhitanga ako o te kounga kairangi ki tētahi taiao reo Māori.

I whakaritea e te whānau o te kōhanga reo ko Mana Reo te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. I whai wāhi rātou ki te waihanga i te pātai aromātai.

He pēhea te whai huatanga o ngā mahi ki te poipoi i te mōhio o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori me tō rātou tuakiri?

E tino whai hua ana ngā mahi ki te poipoi i te mōhio o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori me tō rātou tuakiri.

Ka kitea te tino pārekareka, te ngākau nui hoki o ngā tamariki ki te ako. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā ngā akoranga e pā ana ki tō rātou marae me ōna whenua. He harikoa ngā tamariki, ā, he pārekareka ki a rātou te ako. Kei te hōtaka o ia rā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi whakahihiri. Ka mahi ngātahi ngā kaimahi ki te hāpai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He manaakitanga, he atawhai anō hoki tō te whakawhanaunga atu a ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu.

E tino tautokona ana te whānau e ngā kaumātua e tae atu ana ki te kōhanga reo i ia rā. He pai ngā mahi a te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. Ka aro nui ki te whakarato i ngā whai wāhitanga ako ka whai pānga ki te ako ngātahi a ngā mātua rangatahi me ā rātou tamariki. Nā tēnei, he nui ngā tūmanako o te whānau mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ka whakanuia, ka whakatauirahia anō hoki te whanaungatanga mō ngā tamariki. Ka whakapuaki ngā mātua i te manawa whakahī mō te āhua o ngā akoranga me ngā mahi a ā rātou tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Ka hāpai te whānau ki te whakarato i tētahi taiao aroha, manaaki hoki, kia āhei ai ngā tamariki ki te ako, te tākaro me te tūhura haere i reira.

2. Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Reo te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3. Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Reo

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whai huatanga o ngā mahi ki te poipoi i te mōhio o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori me tō rātou tuakiri?

E tino whai hua ana ngā mahi ki te poipoi i te mōhio o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori me tō rātou tuakiri.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He ākonga pākiki ngā tamariki, ā, he pai tā rātou taunekeneke ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ngā kaimahi hoki. Ka mahi ngā kaimahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i ngā wāhi o roto, o waho hoki o te kōhanga reo, me te hapori whānui hoki. Haere ai rātou ki ngā hui i te marae, ngā tangihanga, me ētahi atu tūmomo huihuinga. Ka kitea te hihiri, te pārekareka hoki o ngā tamariki ki te ako.

Ka mahi ngātahi ngā kaimahi ki te hāpai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka whakarite ngā kaimahi i te whakamahinga o ngā mōhiohio aromatawai, hei whakamahere i ngā wheako akoranga e tika ana mō ngā tamariki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te hihiri anō hoki o te papa tākaro. Nā te aronga nahanaha, he ngāwari, he māmā noa te whakawhiti a ngā tamariki ki te kura ā muri i te kōhanga reo. Nā runga i taua āhuatanga, ko ō rātou mōhiotanga me tō rātou tuakiri ahurea, e whakamanahia ana, ā, e whakamaiohatia ana hoki.

Ka kitea te whakaaro nui, te maioha hoki o ngā tamariki mō ētahi atu. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā taumahi e whāngai ana, e poipoi ana hoki i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga me ō rātou matea. Ka tautoko ngā tamariki i ētahi atu i roto i ā rātou akoranga, ā, ka whakaatu anō hoki i te tino whakaaro nui mō ō rātou hoa, me ngā kaimahi.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa. Ka ako, ka katakata, ka tākaro hoki ngā tamariki ki ngā taiao e whakamana ana i ngā tikanga me te reo o Tūhoe. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te kairangi o te reo e kōrerohia ana e ngā kaumātua e mau nei i te reo Māori hei reo taketake. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ngā tikanga me ngā whanonga e tika ana me te mōhio hoki ki te whakamahi i aua āhuatanga. He ākonga pakari ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao rumaki reo.

Te wāhanga hei whakawhanake

He iti noa ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whakawhānui me te whakawhanake i tō rātou reo kōrero. Me whakawhanake ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki ka whakatairanga ake, ka whakawhanake ake hoki i te āheinga o ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti kōrero me te taunekeneke hoki mā te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka āta hāpai te whānau i te kōhanga reo me ā rātou tamariki. He māramatanga papai tō te whānau ki tēnā e hiahiatia ana mō ā rātou tamariki, me te huarahi hoki ka hāpai i a rātou. Ka pūrongo i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo ki ngā hui ā-whānau. Kua whakaritea hoki ngā tino pūnaha hei whakahaere i ngā pūtea.

He mārama, he koi, he matawhānui hoki ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi a ngā kaimahi katoa. Kua whakatakotohia ngā whāinga o ngā kaimahi katoa, ā, e arotahi ana ēnei ki te whakapai i ngā putanga mō ngā tamariki me te whakapai ake i te āhua o ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi. Ka whai hua te aromātai i te hōtaka. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti kōrero mō te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakaaro huritao rātou i ia te wā, mō ngā āhuatanga i whai hua ai, ngā āhuatanga hei whakapai ake, me ngā huarahi ka whāia tonuhia. Ka whakarerekē haere i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, nā runga i te āhua o ngā mōhiohio ka puta i te arotake whaiaro.

E ngākau nuitia ana ngā tamariki ki ngā wāhi tākaro me ngā wāhi ako e haumarutia, e whakahiamo i a rātou. He pai te whakahaeretanga, te noho pūmau hoki o tētahi mahere hei whakahaere i te whare me ngā papa. Nō nā tata tonu nei te whānau i hanga ai i ngā mahau hou, ngā taiepa e karapoti ana i te kōhanga reo, me ngā whakaruruhau. Ka whakapau kaha te whānau hei āta whakarite i te noho haumaru, te noho tau, me te hāneanea hoki o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Ka whakaritea te pūrongo ki ngā whānau i ia te wā mō te ahunga whakamua o ngā tamariki, ā, ahakoa he pūrongo ā-waha e whakatutukihia ana e ngā kaimahi, he iti noa ngā tuhinga o aua whakawhitinga kōrero.

Ahakoa he mahere rautaki mārama tā rātou e tohu ana i ngā whāinga e hāngai ana ki te whakapaitanga me te whakatairanga ake o ngā putanga mō ngā tamariki, kāhore anō ngā mahi kia whakatakotohia, ā, tē taea te kite te whakatutukitanga o aua mahi. Me whakarite ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te tuhi i te whakatutukitanga o ngā mahi i tāutuhia ai.

4. Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaeretanga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora, me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5. Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia toro atu te whānau ki te tautoko me te ārahitanga o tō rātou tari ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo me te purapura, ki te whakatutuki i ngā wāhanga katoa i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo.

6. Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

19 Hakihea 2014

1. The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tanatana is a marae based kōhanga near Waimana. Whānau at this kōhanga reo ensures that children are well cared for, loved and receive high quality learning opportunities in a te reo Māori environment.

The kōhanga reo whānau chose Mana Reo as the focus for this review. They participated in the design of the evaluation question.

How successfully is children’s knowledge of te reo Māori and sense of identity nurtured?

Children’s knowledge of te reo Māori and sense of identity is successfully nurtured

Children show high levels of enjoyment and interest in learning. Children benefit from learning on and around their local marae. Children are happy and enjoy learning. The daily programme provides a range of interesting activities. Kaimahi work collaboratively to support children’s learning. Children relate to others in a caring and positive manner.

Whānau are well supported by kaumatua who attend the kōhanga reo daily. Whānau manage the kōhanga reo operations effectively. Emphasis is on providing learning opportunities where young parents learn alongside their children. As a result, whānau have high expectations for their children.

Relationships are valued and modelled for children. Parents express pride in what their children learn and do in the kōhanga reo. Whānau support the provision of a loving and caring environment for children to learn, play and explore in.

2. Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose Mana Reo as its focus areas.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3. Findings

Mana Reo

Background

How successfully is children’s knowledge of te reo Māori and sense of identity nurtured?

Children’s knowledge of te reo Māori and sense of identity is successfully nurtured

Areas of good performance

Children are curious learners and they interact in positive ways with each other and kaimahi. Kaimahi work alongside children. Children have opportunities to explore the indoor and outdoor areas and wider community. They attend hui at local marae, tangihanga and other cultural events. Children show interest and enjoyment in learning.

Kaimahi work collaboratively to support children’s learning. Kaimahi ensure that assessment information is used to plan appropriate learning experiences for children. Children enjoy an exciting playground. A planned approach ensures that children have a smooth and effortless transition into kura after they leave the kōhanga. As a result their prior learning and cultural identity is valued and appreciated.

Children show a sensitivity and appreciation of others. Children experience activities that foster and nurture their interests and needs. Children support others in their learning and show genuine concern for their friends and for kaimahi.

Children are immersed in te reo Māori at all times. Children learn, laugh and play in environments that value the tikanga and te reo o Tūhoe. Children are exposed to high quality language models by kaumātua who are native speakers of te reo Māori. Children recognise tikanga practices and desired behaviours and respond appropriately. Children are confident learners in a total immersion environment.

Area for development

There is limited opportunity for children to extend and develop their spoken language. Kaimahi should develop strategies to further promote and develop children's ability to communicate and interact in te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

Whānau fully support the kōhanga reo and their children. Whānau have a clear understanding about what they want for their tamariki and how to support them. Reporting about kōhanga reo operation takes place at whānau hui. Strong financial management systems are in place.

All kaimahi have clear, concise and comprehensive job descriptions. There are objectives set for all kaimahi that are focused on improving outcomes for children and on improving kaimahi performance. Programme evaluation is effective. Staff meet regularly to discuss the quality of the learning programme. They regularly reflect on what went well, what can be done better and what should happen next. Changes are made to kōhanga operations based on self review information.

Children enjoy safe and exciting playing and learning areas. There is a well managed and maintained property management plan. The whānau have recently completed the construction of new decks, perimeter fencing and covered areas. Whānau do all they can to ensure children are safe, secure and comfortable.

Areas for development

Regular reporting to whānau about children's progress is planned for and while staff are making verbal reports there is a limited record of discussions.

Whilst there is a clear strategic plan with goals that are directly focused on improving and enhancing outcomes for children, tasks have not been recorded and there is no way of knowing if they have been achieved. Kaimahi and whānau need to ensure that identified task outcomes are recorded.

4. Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO LicensedKōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5. Recommendations

ERO recommends the whānau seek support and guidance from their district TKRNT and purapura to address all areas for development identified in this report

6. When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)
