1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Atawhai ki te taha o te kura o Tauhara. E noho pūmau ana te whānau ki te āta whakatau i te whai tonu a ā rātou tamariki i te mātauranga rumaki reo Māori i tā rātou wehe atu i te kōhanga reo. Ka uru atu te nuinga o ngā tamariki ki te kura kaupapa Māori o te hapori nei. He tino hononga tō te whānau me ngā tamariki ki ngā hapū o te iwi o Ngāti Tūwharetoa.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa te ngākau titikaha o ngā tamariki hei ākonga?

Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki te whakawhanaketanga o ō rātou ngākau titikaha hei ākonga, i a rātou e taunekeneke ana ki ētahi atu i roto i te hōtaka o ia rā.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e āta poipoi ana i te rangimārie me te harikoa. Ka ahu mai ngā hononga o ngā tamariki i te whanaungatanga. Ka rongo rātou i te aroha, te manaaki, te atawhai, te ngākau nui, me te harikoa i roto i ā rātou taunekeneke ki te taha o ngā kaimahi. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i te whakaute o te tangata mōna ake, ā, mō ētahi atu hoki. He pai te whakahaere i ngā tamariki. He riterite, he mārama hoki ngā tūmanako me ngā mahinga e hāpai ana i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki i te whanonga me ngā hononga e whai hua ana. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whai i te huarahi tika i roto i ā rātou whanonga, ā rātou taunekeneke i ngā wā ōkawa o te hōtaka akoranga. He mauritau, he harikoa hoki te āhua o ngā tamariki.

E whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmomo akoranga e poipoi ana i ō rātou tuakiri. E whakatauira ana ngā kaimahi i ngā wawata o te whānau, tae atu hoki ki ērā e hāngai ana ki te noho mōhio o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou tuakiri me ō rātou tūrangawaewae. Kei te tino mōhio ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te ako i ō rātou pepeha me ō rātou marae. He tūmāia ngā tamariki tuākana ki te taki i ō rātou pepeha, me te iti noa tonu o te whai wāhi mai o ngā kaimahi hei hāpai i a rātou. E hāpaitia ana te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki i tō rātou māramatanga ki te tū o te tangata whenua. E ākina ana tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki ngā whakatau me ngā pōwhiri mā ngā huarahi e tika ana. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga.

He nui ngā puna rauemi e hāpai ana i te ako a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. He pakari ētahi mema o te whānau ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka tū rātou hei tauira mō ngā tamariki me ētahi o atu o te whānau. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā waiata, ngā karakia, me ngā mōteatea o Tūwharetoa, hei rautaki e hāpai ana i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā kēmu ā-ringa kia āta areare mai ngā taringa o ngā tamariki ki te whakarongo. He pakari ngā tamariki tuākana ki te uiui me te whakautu i ngā pātai māmā. He tika te aro atu a ngā tamariki katoa ki ngā tohutohu me ngā pātai. Ka tohutohu, ka kōrerorero hoki ngā pēpi i a rātou e taunekeneke ana ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ō rātou tuākana, ā, ki ngā pākeke hoki. Ka aro atu ngā kaimahi ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero a ngā tamariki mā te atawhai me te aroha. Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tō rātou māramatanga ki te reo Māori.

He tūmāia te tūhura haere a ngā tamariki i tō rātou taiao. Ka whakaratohia ki ngā pēpi ngā wheako e aro nui ana ki ō rātou matea ako. He hihiri, he tākare hoki te kaiako. He māhorahora ake te whakapuaki a te kaiako me ngā tamariki i ō rātou ake āhua, i a rātou e tākaro ngātahi ana. Ka whakarato te whānau i te taiao haumaru, pūmau hoki, ā, e hāpai ana aua āhuatanga i te tūhura haere me te āta whakamātau. Ka akiaki, ka hāpai hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. Ka tuitui ki te hōtaka, ko ngā whai wāhitanga ki te tākaro me te ako i roto i te taiao māoriori o te hapori. E whanake ana te ngākau titikaha o ngā tamariki hei ākonga.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Kua whakarite e te whānau ngā tukanga ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o tō rātou kōhanga reo. E tāutu ana ngā mahere rautaki me ngā mahere ā-tau i ngā kaupapa matua o te pae tawhiti. Ka whakatutukihia ngā take e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi, ngā pūtea, te hauora, me te haumaru. Hui ai te whānau i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti whakaaro mō ngā whakahaeretanga i te kōhanga reo. He pūnaha ā rātou e hāpai ai i te rōnakitanga, ā, ko ētahi o ēnei, ko te whiwhi tohu mātauranga a te whānau, me te mahi ngātahi ki te whakawhānui ake i tō rātou whare. E mahi ngātahi ana te whānau hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi ngā tukanga ki te whakamahere i te hōtaka me te aromatawai i ngā tamariki. Ka hāngai hoki ēnei ki ngā rauemi e tika ana, ngā whāinga, me ngā tino hononga ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. E ākina ana te whai wāhi mai o te whānau ki te waihanga o te hōtaka akoranga. He pai te māramatanga o ngā kaimahi ki ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga mahi. Ka whakatakotohia ngā akoranga me te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki ki ngā Pukapuka Kitenga. E noho pūmau ana ki te whakapai tonu me te āta whakarite i te whakatutukitanga o ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki. He pai te whakariterite me te aroturuki i te ako a ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore te whānau e aroturuki ana i te ahu whakamua ki te whakatutuki haere i ngā whāinga ā-tau. E mōhio ana rātou ki te hua o te arowhai i ia te wā i te ahu whakamua o aua whāinga. E noho pūmau ana te whānau ki te whakapakari ake me te whakaōkawa ake i ā rātou whakaritenga. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki mā te whakatinanatanga o ngā wawata o te whānau kia puta ai ngā painga mō ā rātou akoranga.

Ko te aromātai hōtaka tētahi wāhanga hei whakapai ake. Me whai whakaaro ngā kaimahi ki te āhua o te ahu whakamua o te hōtaka akoranga, ki tēnā e whai hua ana, ā, ki tēnā hoki hei whakapai ake. Me tuhi rātou i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, ā, me whakarerekē haere i te wā e tika ana. Ka riro i ngā tamariki ngā painga ka ahu mai i ngā whakataunga i āta whakaarohia ai.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakawhanakehia, kia whakatinanahia hoki ngā whakaritenga pūrongo e tāutu ana i ngā kaupapa matua, ā, e whakaōkawa ana hoki i te aromātai hōtaka.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Atawhai i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga reo o Atawhai is situated beside Tauhara school. Whānau are committed to ensuring their children continue in Māori medium education after leaving kōhanga reo. Most children enrol at the local kura kaupapa. Whānau and children have close connections to the hapū and iwi of Ngāti Tūwharetoa.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How confident are children as learners?

Children show they are developing as confident learners as they interact with otheres in the daily programme.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children experience an environment where peace and happiness is nurtured. Children’s relationships are based on whanaungatanga. They experience aroha, manaaki, atawhai, ngakanui and harikoa in their interactions with kaimahi. Kaimahi model respect of self and others. Children are managed positively. Consistent and clear expectations and routines support children to develop positive behaviour and relationships. Kaimahi support children to behave and respond appropriately during the formal parts of the learning programme. Children appear settled and happy.

Children participate in learning where their identity is nurtured. Kaimahi model the aspirations of the whānau which includes children knowing who they are and where they come from. They know the children very well. Kaimahi support children to learn their pepeha and marae. Older children confidently recite their pepeha with very little support from kaimahi. Children are supported to develop their understanding of their role as tangata whenua. They are encouraged to participate in whakatau and pōwhiri in appropriate ways. Children are developing a sense belonging.

Children are well resourced to learn te reo Māori. Some whanau are good speakers of te reo Māori. They are role models for children and other whānau members. Kaimahi use waiata, karakia and mōteatea specific to Tūwharetoa as strategies to support children’s language development. The kaimahi use hand games to focus listening. Older children confidently ask and answer simple questions. All children respond appropriately to instructions and questions. Babies point and babble when interacting with each other, tuākana and adults. Kaimahi respond to children’s communication with warmth and love. Children demonstrate an understanding of te reo Māori.

Children explore their environment with confidence. Babies are provided with experiences that are responsive to their learning needs. The kaiako is animated and enthusiastic. Both kaiako and children express themselves more freely when they are engaged in play with one another. Whānau provide a safe and secure environment that encourages exploration and risk taking. Kaimahi encourage and support children to pursue their interests. Opportunities to play and learn in the local natural environment are integrated into the programme. Children are developing as confident learners.

Planning and Evaluation

Whanau have in place processes to manage the operations of their kōhanga reo. Strategic and annual plans identify long term priorities. Personnel, finances and health and safety matters are attended to. Whānau meet regularly to discuss kōhanga reo operations. They have systems in place to support the sustainability that includes whānau gaining qualifications and working towards extending their building. Whānau work together in the interests of their children.

Kaimahi have established process for programme planning and children’s assessments. It includes appropriate resources, whainga and clear links to the kaupapa of kōhanga reo. The whanau are encouraged to contribute to the design of the learning programme. Kaimahi have good understanding of their roles and responsibilities. Children’s learning and progress is recorded in Pukapuka Kitenga. There is a commitment to improvement and ensuring children’s learning needs are catered for. Children’s learning is well organised and monitored.

Key Next Steps

Whānau do not monitor progress about annual goals. They do acknowledge the benefits of consistently checking the progress of these goals. Whānau are committed to strengthening and formalising their practices. Children benefit when whānau aspirations will result in positive outcomes for their learning.

Programme evaluation is an area for improvement. Kaimahi need to consider how the learning programme is progressing, what is working well and what needs to be improved. They should record discussions and implement changes as needed. Children benefit from well considered decisions.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluate, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • Develop and implement reporting expectations that identifies priorities and formalise programme evaluation.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Atawhai will be in three years.
