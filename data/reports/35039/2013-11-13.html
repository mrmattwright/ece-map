2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ka tū tangata ai tātou katoa

i raro i te marū o te mana tangata

E tū ana te kōhanga reo tuarua o Tokoroa ki te tonga o te tāone o Tokoroa. E tino tautoko ana ngā kuia o te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, i te whānau. He wāhi hei tino tautoko i ngā tamariki te kōhanga reo, ā, kua tiakina pai ō rātou tuakiri.

E tino ū ana te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki ngā mahi hei whakapai ake i ngā wā katoa me ngā hua angitū mā te whānau me ngā tamariki. E whakanui ana te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo i te reo Māori, ā, kua ākina e tēnā me tēnā te kōrero i te reo Māori. Kua tautokotia ngā tamariki ki te tū hei ākonga maia, ā, hei ākonga e taea ai te mahi i tāna kōtahi.

E whakanui ana te hōtaka akoranga i te whānui me te hōhonu o te reo Māori. E noho rumaki ana ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. E maia ana rātou ki te whakaputa i te reo Māori. E tautoko ana ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te ako i ō rātou whakapapa me ngā mahi a ngā tūpuna. He mea mauritau me te pōturi te āhua o te hōtaka mā ngā pēpi. Ka toro atu ngā tamariki i ngā wāhi whakahirahira. E āhei ana rātou ki te tūhura i te ao whānui me te taunekeneke ki ngā tāngata rerekē.

E kaha ū ana te whānau ki te whakatinana i ō rātou whāinga. E hāngai ana ēnei whāinga ki te whānui o te reo Māori ka kōrerotia ngā mātua me te whakapiki i ō rātou maia ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ko te whakapaipai me te whakatika i ngā wāhi e raru ai ngā whare me te whenua tētahi anō o ngā tino whāinga.

Kua whakatūria pai ngā tukanga whakahaere e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro. Kua whakatūria e te whānau ngā tukanga hei aroturuki i ngā wāhanga kua whakapaitia. Mā te arotake whaiaro e taea ai e te whānau te whiriwhiri i te ahunga whakamua o te kōhanga reo.

He mea āta whiriwhiri, ā, he mea hōhonu ngā whakamāherehere. E riro ana i ngā tamariki te mātauranga me te maia mā te ako i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui. He mea aratohu ngā whakamāherehere hei tautoko i ngā whakahaere a te hōtaka.

Ko te aromatawai i ngā matea o ngā tamariki tētahi wāhanga hei whakapai ake. He iti noa ake ngā mōhiohio mō ngā tamariki e whakaatu pēhea ai ngā paetae me ā rātou akoranga. Me whai wāhi ngā kaimahi ki te whakapakari ake i te hōtaka aromātai. He iti noa ngā mōhiohio e whakaatu pēhea ai te whaihua o ngā aroturuki i te hōtaka akoranga. Me arotahi ngā whakawhitiwhiti kōrero i ngā hui kaimahi ki ngā wāhanga e whai hua ana, ngā wāhanga kāore i te whai hua me ngā wāhanga hei whakarerekē kia piki ake ai te kounga o te hōtaka mā ngā tamariki.

Heoi anō, kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo e te whānau hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā Mahi Ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o Te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo Tuarua o Tokoroa kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o o Te Kōhanga Reo Tuarua o Tokoroa.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko āna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Tangata.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere i te māhere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • hōtaka whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Tangata

He whakamārama

I kōwhiritia e te whānau teMana Tangatahei kaupapa arotahi mā te hōtaka akoranga. Ko ngā putanga hua o te Mana Tangata e hiahiatia ana e ratou kia tipu pakari ake ai te mōhio o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou ake mana anō me te whai koha ki ētahi atu. E pīrangi ana te whānau ki te mōhio e pēhea ana te whaihua o ā rātou whakahaere ki te whakatipu i ā rātou tamariki ki te tū māia me te tū mōhio ki te ao Māori me te Ao Tūroa.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Mana Tangata. He kaha rawa te kōhanga reo ki te arotahi kia pai rawa ai ngā putanga hua me ngā wheako angitū mā te whānau me ngā tamariki i tēnei wā me ngā rangi ā meāke nei. E tautoko ana te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo i ēnei whakahaere mā te whai i ēnei rautaki e whai ake nei:

  • E piki ana te mātau o ngā mātua o te whānau ki te reo Māori me ōnā tikanga
  • Ko ngā uara Māori te tūāpapa o ngā whakahaere katoa
  • Kua tiakina te whakapapa me te tuakiri o ngā tamariki
  • Ka kitea te noho manawa hari i waenganui i te whānau
  • He ākonga māia ngā tamariki, ā, e taea ana e tēnā me tēnā te whai atu i tāna e hiahiatia ana.
  • E māia ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakaputa i te reo Māori
  • He mea tautoko te wairua o te kōhanga reo, ā, e tautoko ana te wairua i te noho ngātahi o te whānau, ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi.

Te reo Māori. E rumaki ana ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. E maia ana rātou ki te kōrero i te reo Māori i te wā o te karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā waiata, ngā mihi, ngā whakapapa me ngā pepeha. E piki ana te maia o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

E kōrero ana ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori ki ngā tamariki mō te rā katoa. Kei ngā reanga rerekē o te reo Māori ratou. E mātau ana ngā kuia ki te reo Māori, ā, kei te kōhanga reo rātou i te wā i te wā. E āhei ana rātou ki te tautoko me te whakanui ake i ngā pūkenga reo o ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki.

Te hōtaka akoranga. He mea whaihua te hōtaka akoranga hei tautoko i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He mea pōwhiri, ā, he mea māhana te taiao mā ngā tamariki. E tino whai wāhi atu ana rātou, ā, e harikoa ana rātou ki ngā ngohe o te ata. E tino kamakama ana ngā tamariki ki te hāpai i ngā tūranga tātaki. Ka kitea tēnei mā te tirotiro i a rātou e whakaputa ana i ō rātou pepeha. Ka karakia ngātahi ngā tamariki, ngā mātua me ngā kaimahi, ā, ka waiata rātou i ngā waiata me ngā waiata ā-ringa. E whai wāhi atu ana rātou ki te whiriwhiri i ā rātou ake ngohe me tākaro i ā rātou kōtahi, E whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki ki te toro atu ki ngā wāhi ō- waho me ngā wāhi ō-roto i a rātou e whai ana i te hōtaka o te rā. E tino mōhio i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā, ā, he māmā ki a rātou ki te whai atu rātou i ēnei whakaritenga mō te rā katoa.

Te ao whānui. Ka haere atu ngā tamariki ki ngā wāhi hihiri. E āhei ana rātou ki te tūhura i te ao whānui me te taunekeneke ki ngā tāngata rerekē. E hāngai ana ēnei haerenga ki ngā kaupapa rerekē, ā, he mea whakahirahira ēnei haerenga mā ngā tamariki me te whānau. E whanake ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou mātauranga me ō rātou māramatanga ki ngā tohu, ngā taonga me ngā uaratanga i te wā ka toro atu rātou ki ngā wāhi rerekē. E tino mōhio ana rātou ki te ao wairua me te ao ō ahupungao me ngā whakapā rerekē o ngā atua ki a rātou anō.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamāherehere me te aromātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo hei whakatau i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamāherehere, ngā whakahaere me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga reo mā a rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka āta hāngai te aromātai nei ki te hōtaka whakamāherehere me te aroturuki i te tipuranga a ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

E whakahaere whaitake ana te whānau i te kōhanga reo. Ko tō rātou hiahia ngātahi he whakarite i tētahi wāhi mātauranga whaihua me te tiaki i ā rātou tamariki mā te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. E piri pūmau ana ngā kuia ki te kaupapa, ā, ō rātou kaha hoki ki te tātaki, ki te hāpai me te whakanui i te kaupapa. E kaha ū ana te whānau ki te whakanui ake i ō rātou pūkenga me ō rātou āheinga kia tipu pakari ai te kōhanga reo mō ngā rangi ā meāke nei.

Ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā. Kua whakahoutia ēnei mea, ā, e haere ake ana ngā mahi ki te whakapai ake me ngā arotakenga a te whānau. E hāngai ana ngā kaupapa here ki ngā wāhanga katoa e pā ana ki te hauora, te haumaru, ngā tohu mātauranga, ngāwhakaritenga tae noa ki ngā tikanga e pā ana ki te hōneatanga.

Te whakahaere i ngā kaimahi. Kua whakatūria e te whānau ngā tukanga hei tautoko i te mahi a ngā kaimahi. Kua oti i a rātou te whakarite i ngā rārangi mahi me ngā kirimana whakawhiwhi mahi. He tika me te whai koha tō rātou āhua ki te whakahaere i ngā kaimahi. Kua ākina e te whānau ngā kaimahi ki te whai i ngā tohu mātauranga me te whakanui ake i ō rātou pūkenga. E hanga ana rātou ki te mātau o tēnā me tēnā mā ngā rangi ā meāke nei.

Te whakahaere pūtea. He whaitake ngā whakahaere pūtea o te kōhanga reo. He tahua pūtea tā rātou mā ngā rauemi mātauranga hei tautoko i te hōtaka akoranga.

Māhere Rautaki. E mārama ana ngā mātua o te whānau i ngā tino whāinga o inaiānei me ngā rangi ā meāke nei. Ko ngā tino whāinga rautaki ko te:

  • whakanui i te whānui me te hōhonu o te reo Māori
  • tuku mōhiohio ki te whānau mō ngā āhuatanga whaitake e pā ana ki te kōhanga reo
  • tautoko i ngā mātua he taiohi tonu te pakeke hei whakapakari ake i ō rātou māia me ō rātou mātau ki te reo Māori
  • te whakapaipai i ngā whare me te whakatika i ngā wāhanga e raru ana kia tika rawa atu te mahi whakapaipai.

Kua rārangitia ngā wāhanga hei whakatutuki mā ia whāinga, ā, he tukanga tōtika a rātou ki te arotake i ō rātou paetae me ngā wāhanga kua whakatutukitia.

Te arotake whaiaro. He tōtika ngā pūnaha mō te arotake whaiaro. E whai wāhi ana te whānau i te wā i te wā ki te whiriwhiri me te tuku pūrongo mō ngā wāhanga whakahaere o te kōhanga reo. E aroturuki ana, ā, e tuku pūrongo ana te whānau mō ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki tā rātou māhere rautaki. Kua arotaketia ngā whāinga whāiti me ngā whāinga whāroa. E tautoko whai hua ana ngā arotake whaiaro i te ahunga whakamua o te kōhanga reo.

Te whakamāherehere. He mea whiriwhiri whakaaro me te hōhonu ngā whakamāherehere. E riro ana i ngā tamariki te mātauranga me te maia mā te ako i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui. He mea aratohu ngā whakamāherehere hei tautoko i ngā whakahaere a te hōtaka.

E whakahaeretia ana e te whānau te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te aromatawai. Kāore ngā aromatawai i te tino hāngai ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He iti noa ake ngā mōhiohio e whakaatu ai i ngā paetae o ngā tamariki. Hei mau i te tipuranga o ngā tamariki, me arotahi te whānau ki ngā tukanga hei kohikohi me te momo mōhiohio kua kohia mā ngā pūkete paetae a ngā tamariki. Kāore i a rātou ngā mōhiohio whai take e pā ana ki te aromatawai hei tuari ki te whānau.

Te hōtaka aromātai. Me whakapakari ake ngā kaimahi i te hōtaka aromātai. He iti noa nei ngā mōhiohio e whakaatu ai te whaihua o te hōtaka me ngā pānga ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Me arotahi ngā whakawhiti kōrero i ngā hui a ngā kaimahi ki ngā wāhanga e whai hua ana, ngā wāhanga kāore i te whai hua me ngā whakahaere hei whakarerekē kia piki ake ai te kounga o te hōtaka mā ngā tamariki.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga s

He whakamārama

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo Tuarua o Tokoroa tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga a te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

6 Ngā Tūtohunga

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Ka tū tangata ai tātou katoa

i raro i te marū o te mana tangata

Te Kōhanga Reo Tuarua o Tokoroa is located on the southern side of the Tokoroa township. Prominent kuia of the kōhanga reo movement are very supportive of the whānau . The kōhanga reo is a place where children are cared for and their sense of belonging is nurtured.

The kōhanga reo are committed and focused on continual growth and success for whānau and children. The kaupapa of the kōhanga reo promotes and encourages the use of te reo Māori. Children are supported to be confident and independent learners.

The learning programme promotes the richness of te reo Māori. Children are immersed in te reo Māori. They confidently use te reo Māori. Kaimahi support children to learn their whakapapa and heritage. Babies experience a programme that is calm and unhurried. Children regularly go to places of interest. They are able to make discoveries about the wider world and interact with many different people.

Whānau are committed to achieving their goals. These goals focus on promoting te reo Māori amongst parents to increase their confidence and use of te reo Māori. Maintaining and repairing facilities is another goal.

Self review is well established. Whānau have processes to monitor improvements. Self review enables whānau to determine the future direction of the kōhanga reo.

Planning is well considered and comprehensive. Children gain knowledge and confidence learning about Te Aō Māori and Te Aō Tūroa. Planning provides clear guidelines for programme implementation.

Assessing children’s learning needs attention. There is little information to show how children have progressed with their learning. Kaimahi need to strengthen programme evaluation. There is little information to indicate how effectively the learning programme is being monitored. Kaimahi hui discussions need to focus on what worked, what did not work and what changes need to happen to increase the quality of the programme for children.

Overall the whānau manage the kōhanga reo in the best interests of their children.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo Tuarua o Tokoroa was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Te Kōhanga Reo Tuarua o Tokoroa.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Tangata.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

4 Findings

Mana Tangata

Background

The whānau chose mana tangata as the focus for the learning programme. The desired outcomes of mana tangata are that children develop a sense of self respect and value others. Whānau want to know how effective they are in developing their children to become confident and capable in Te Aō Māori and Te Aō Tūroa.

Areas of good performance

Mana Tangata. The kōhanga reo is committed and focused on continual growth and success for the whānau and children. The kaupapa of the kōhanga reo promotes and supports these in the following ways:

  • te reo Māori me ōna tikanga is strengthened within whānau
  • Māori values underpin whānau practices
  • children’s heritage and identity are nurtured
  • positive relationships are evident within the whānau
  • children are confident and independent learners
  • kaimahi are confident in their use of te reo Māori
  • the wairua within the kōhanga reo affirms a sense of belonging for whānau, children and kaimahi.

Te reo Māori. Children are immersed in te reo Māori. They confidently use te reo Māori during karakia, hīmene, waiata, mihi, whakapapa and pepeha. Children are gaining confidence in using te reo Māori.

Kaimahi speak te reo Māori to children throughout the day. They are at varying stages of competency in te reo Māori. Kuia, who are fluent speakers of te reo Māori are frequently at the kōhanga reo. They are able to support and extend the language skills of kaimahi and children.

Learning programme. The learning programme is effective in supporting children’s learning. Children are welcomed into a warm, nurturing environment. They are willing participants and enjoy the morning activities. Children willingly take leadership roles. They are confident in who they are. This is evident as they recite their pepeha. Children, parents and kaimahi recite karakia, sing songs and perform action songs. Children have opportunities to select their own activities and participate in independent play. Children have access to both indoor and outdoor areas during the programme. They know the daily routines and follow these smoothly throughout the day.

Wider world. Children regularly go to places of interest. They are able to make new discoveries about the wider world and interact with many different people. These outings link to different kaupapa and are highlights for children and whānau. Children are developing their knowledge and understanding of symbols, artefacts and values when they visit different sites and places. They are highly aware of the physical and spiritual worlds and the impact the different atua have on their daily lives.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent, to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau manage the kōhanga reo well. Their collective interest is in providing the best education provision and care to their children through the operations of the kōhanga reo. Kuia are committed and provide active leadership in maintaining and promoting of the kaupapa. The whānau are strongly committed to growing their skills and competencies for the future development of the kōhanga reo

Policies and procedures. These have been updated and continue to be edited and reviewed by the whānau. Policies cover all areas required for health, safety, qualifications, drills and evacuations.

Personnel management. Whānau have systems in place to support kaimahi in their work. Job descriptions and employment contracts are complete. They demonstrate that they are good and reasonable employers. Whānau have encouraged kaimahi to gain qualifications and skills. They are building capability for the future.

Financial management. The kōhanga reo manage their finances effectively. They budget for education resources to ensure that the learning programme has high quality resources to support the programme.

Strategic Plan. Whānau members understand their priorities for now and to the future. The key strategic priorities are to:

  • promote the richness of te reo Māori
  • inform whānau with information relevant to the kōhanga reo
  • support young parents to increase their confidence and fluency in te reo Māori
  • ensure that facilities are maintained and repaired to the best standards.

The whānau have listed the actions required for each priority area and have systematic reviews of their progress.

Self review. Self review is well established. Whānau regularly discuss and report on different parts of the kōhanga reo operations. Whānau monitor and report on their strategic plan. Both short-term and long term goals are reviewed. Self review contributes to the overall direction of the kōhanga reo.

Planning. Planning is well considered and comprehensive. Te Whāriki is the basis of the learning programme. Children gain knowledge and confidence learning about the Māori and the wider worlds. Planning provides clear guidelines for programme implementation.

The whānau manages the kōhanga reo in the best interests of their children.

Areas for development

Assessment. Assessment does not reflect children’s learning. There is little information to show how children have progressed. To capture children’s development, whānau should focus on how and what they record in children’s port folios. There is no good quality assessment information to share with whānau.

Programme evaluation. Kaimahi need to strengthen programme evaluation. There is little information to indicate how effectively the programme impacts on children’s learning. Kaimahi hui discussions need to focus on what worked, what did not work and what changes need to happen to increase the quality of the programme for children.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo Tuarua o Tokoroa completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

6 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • whānau seek support to strengthen understanding and practice of assessment and evaluation.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
