2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

“I ahu mai i te Whakarehu

Te tū ai ko hea”

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Wharehoa ki Waiwhetū, ki te taha o te tari ā-rohe o Ikaroa, o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo. I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Reo te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E hiahia ana ngā mātua kia pakari ā rātou tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka aromātai tēnei arotake i te whai huatanga o te kōhanga reo ki te whakatairanga i te reo Māori.

Ka whakatau te rōpū whakahaere o te kōhanga reo i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kei roto i tēnei rōpū ko ngā kuia, ngā kaimahi, me ētahi o ngā mātua. Ka whakahaere rātou i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Kua whakaritea ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga katoa e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. Ka whakatau te whānau kia noho matua te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki.

Ka noho pūmau te whānau ki tētahi taiao rumaki reo Māori o te kounga kairangi. He matatau ngā kuia ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka tino hāpai te hōtaka akoranga i te ako o ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki whakawhanake reo e tika ana. Ka whakahihiritia, ka kipakipaina hoki ngā tamariki kia ako i te reo Māori.

He atawhai, he whakaute, he tūturu hoki ngā hononga me ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. I ngā wā katoa, ka whakanui, ka akiaki hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā whakamātau me ngā whakatutukitanga hoki a ngā tamariki. E pou here ana te whanaungatanga i ngā taunekeneke katoa ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki.

Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i tētahi wātaka ā-wiki, o ia rā hoki, hei whakatutuki i ngā matea o ngā tamariki, hei ārahi hoki i te whakatinanatanga o te hōtaka. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā taumahi, ā, ka whakarato ki a rātou te pai hoki o ngā rauemi. Ka riro i ngā tamariki te mōhiotanga, te māia hoki, nā te ako i te ao Māori me te ao whānui.

He mārama ngā tukanga i whakaritea ai e ngā kaimahi ki te aromatawai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Kei roto i ngā pūkete, ko te whānuitanga o ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki tēnā i ākona ai e ngā tamariki, ā rātou wheako, me ngā tauira o ngā mahi toi. Me whakapai ngā kaimahi i ā rātou mahi ki te aromātai i te hōtaka, hei hāpai ake anō hoki i te whakapaitanga o te hōtaka.

Ka whakamōhio Te Ara Tūāpae i ngā whāinga rautaki me ngā mahi rautaki i whakawhanakehia ai e te whānau. Ka whakatutuki te rōpū whakahaere i te arotake whaiaro mā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo huarahi, hei tātari i te ahunga whakamua o te kōhanga reo i roto i te wā kei te heke mai. Me whai kiko ake ngā tuhinga a te whānau mō te arotake i te mahere rautaki me te mahere ā-tau.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Reo

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e te kōhanga reo ko Mana Reo te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E hiahia ana ngā mātua kia tū pakari ā rātou tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka aromātai te arotake nei i te whai huatanga o te kōhanga reo ki te whakatairanga i te reo Māori.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te reo Māori.He tika te hāpai o te hōtaka akoranga i ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo Māori. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, ā, ka kōrero ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki mō ā rātou mahi. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki whakawhanaketanga reo e tika ana, pērā i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero māmā ki ngā tamariki, te tohutohu, me te patapatai. Ka tautoko ngā kuia i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki ki te whakapakari i tō rātou āheinga i roto i te reo Māori, ā, ka hāpai anō hoki i a rātou ki te whakawhānui ake i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki ngā tikanga Māori. Ka whakatauirahia te reo ōkawa ki ngā tamariki i ngā wā ōkawa o te hōtaka.

Ka aro pai ngā tamariki ki ngā kipakipa me ngā pātai. He māhorahora tā rātou whakamahinga i ngā kīanga me ngā kupu māmā i a rātou e kōrero ana ki ngā kaimahi, ki ō rātou hoa anō hoki. Ka kitea te tino māramatanga o ngā tamariki i a rātou e whakawhiti kōrero ana ki ngā pākeke.

Ngā tauira o te reo Māori. I ngā wā katoa ka noho pūmau ngā kaimahi ki tētahi taiao rumaki reo Māori o te kounga kairangi. He matatau ngā kuia ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whakahihiritia, ka kipakipaina hoki ngā tamariki kia ako i te reo Māori.

Ngā hononga me ngā taunekeneke. Ko te whanaungatanga e pou here ana i ngā taunekeneke katoa ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. He atawhai, he whakaute, he tūturu hoki ngā hononga me ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. I ngā wā katoa ka whakanui, ka akiaki anō hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā whakamātau me ngā whakatutukitanga a ngā tamariki. He iti noa ngā rautaki e whakamahia ana ki te whakahaere i te whanonga, nā te kaha whai wāhi o ngā tamariki ki tētahi hōtaka e whakahihiri ana i a rātou.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai ki ngā kōhanga reo, hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te Ara Tūāpae.Ka hāpai, ka whakamōhio hoki te tauāki taketake me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo i ngā whāinga rautaki mō te kōhanga reo. Kua whakamahia e te whānau te angamahi o Te Ara Tūāpae hei whakawhanake i ngā wāhanga e arotahi rautakihia ana e rātou.

Te arotake whaiaro. Ka whakatutukihia e te rōpū whakahaere o te kōhanga reo te arotake whaiaro mā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo huarahi, hei tātari i te ahunga whakamua o te kōhanga reo i roto i te wā kei te heke mai. Ka whiwhi rātou i ngā pūrongo i ia te wā, ka kōkiri hoki rātou i te arotake whaiaro, i te wā ka pupū ake ngā kaupapa me ngā take.

Te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga. He pai ngā whakataunga a te rōpū whakahaere o te kōhanga reo, hei hāpai i te rere pai o ngā mahi i te kōhanga reo. Kei roto i taua rōpū ko ngā kuia, ngā kaimahi, me ētahi o ngā mātua. Ka whakahaere rātou i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga. He kohinga kaupapa here tā te whānau, ā, kua whakaritea ēnei i raro i ngā wāhanga matua e whā. Ko ngā kaupapa here e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru e whakamōhio ana i ngā tukanga, ā, e ārahi ana i ngā whakaritenga hei whakatutuki i runga anō i ngā herenga ā-ture. Kua whakaritea te katoa o ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. Ka noho matua te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Te whakamahere hōtaka. Ka whakamahere ngā kaimahi i ngā taumahi e tika ana hei hāpai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka ārahi ngā mahere i te whakatinanatanga o te hōtaka. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi, ā, ka whakaratohia ngā rauemi pai. Ka riro i a rātou ngā mōhiotanga me te māia hoki, nā te ako i te ao Māori me te ao whānui.

Te aromatawai. He mārama ngā tukanga i whakaritea ai e ngā kaimahi ki te aromatawai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka mātakitaki, ka tuhi hoki rātou i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me tā rātou ahunga whakamua. Kei roto i ngā pūkete, ko te whānuitanga o ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki tēnā i ākona ai e ngā tamariki, ā rātou wheako, me ngā tauira o ngā mahi toi.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te mahere rautaki.Kāhore he mahere mahi i tuhia ai hei tohu i te huarahi ka whāia e te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ā rātou whāinga rautaki. Me tāutu, me tuhi hoki te whānau i ngā mahi e tika ana mō ia whāinga, tae atu ki te whakarite i ngā kaikawenga, te whakamāramatanga o ia whāinga, me te wātaka ki te whakatinana i ia mahi. Ko te aroturuki me te pūrongo i te ahunga whakamua me ngā āhuatanga angitu, i runga i ngā kaupapa matua o ia tau, ka tohu i ngā mōhiohio arotake whaiaro ka whai hua ki te whakamahere mō ngā tau kei te heke mai.

Te aromātai hōtaka. Me whakapai ake ngā kaimahi i te aromātai o te hōtaka. He iti noa ngā mōhiohio ka tohu i te āhua o te whai pānga o te hōtaka ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Me arotahi ngā whakawhitinga whakaaro o ngā kaimahi ki tēnā i whai hua ai, ki ngā āhuatanga anō hoki kāhore i whai hua ai, me ngā āhuatanga hoki ki te whakarerekē kia pai ake ai te whai huatanga o te hōtaka mō ngā tamariki.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

“I ahu ma i te Whakarehu

Te tū ai ko hea”

Te Kōhanga Reo o Wharehoa is situated in Waiwhetu, alongside the Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust, Ikaroa district office. The whānau chose Mana Reo as the focus for this review. Parents want their children to be confident speakers of te reo Māori. The review will evaluate how effectively the kōhanga reo promotes te reo Māori.

The kōhanga reo management team make decisions for the running of the kōhanga reo. The team includes kuia, kaimahi and some parents. They manage the kōhanga reo in the best interests of the children. All health and safety policies and procedures are in place. The whānau make children’s health and safety a priority.

The whānau maintains a high quality Māori immersion environment. Kuia are competent and fluent speakers of te reo Māori. The learning programme provides good support for children to learn te reo Māori. Kaimahi use appropriate language development strategies. Children are interested and motivated to learn te reo Māori.

Relationships and interactions between kaimahi and children are warm, respectful and genuine. Kaimahi constantly acknowledge and encourage children’s efforts and achievements. Whanaungatanga underpins all interactions between kaimahi and children.

Kaimahi use a weekly and daily planner to meet the needs of children and guide the implementation of the programme. The children enjoy a range of activities and are provided with good resources. Children gain knowledge and confidence learning about te ao Māori and the wider world.

Kaimahi have clear processes in place to assess children’s learning. Profiles include a range of information about what children have learnt, their experiences and art samples. Kaimahi need to improve their programme evaluation practices to support programme improvement.

Te Ara Tūāpae informs the strategic objectives and actions developed by the whānau. The management team carries out self review in various ways to determine the future direction of the kōhanga reo. The whānau needs to document the review of the strategic and annual plans in more detail.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Wharehoa was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Te Kōhanga Reo o Wharehoa.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Reo.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning; and
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

4 Findings

Mana Reo

Background

The kōhanga reo chose Mana Reo as a focus for this review. Parents want their children to be confident speakers of te reo Māori. The review will evaluate how effectively the kōhanga reo promotes te reo Māori.

Areas of good performance

Te Reo Māori. The learning programme appropriately supports children to learn te reo Māori. Children are able to follow their interests and during this time kaimahi talk with children about what they are doing. Kaimahi use appropriate language development strategies such as having simple conversations with children, giving directions and asking questions. Kuia support kaimahi and children to strengthen their te reo Māori capability and help expand their knowledge about tikanga Māori. Formal language is modelled for children during formal parts of the programme.

Children respond well to prompts and questions. They use simple phrases and words spontaneously when talking with kaimahi and to their peers. Children demonstrate a high level of understanding when responding to adults.

Te reo Māori language models. Kaimahi consistently maintain a high quality Māori immersion environment. Kuia are competent and fluent speakers of te reo Māori. Children are interested and motivated to learn te reo Māori.

Relationships and interactions. Whanaungatanga underpins all interactions between kaimahi and children. Relationships and interactions between kaimahi and children are warm, respectful and genuine. Kaimahi constantly acknowledge and encourage children’s efforts and achievements. There is minimal need for behaviour management strategies because children are fully engaged in a programme that keeps them motivated.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Te Ara Tūāpae. The mission statement and kaupapa of the kōhanga reo supports and informs the strategic goals for the kōhanga reo. The whānau has used the Te Ara Tūāpae framework to develop their strategic focus areas.

Self review. The kōhanga reo management team carries out self review in various ways to determine the future direction of the kōhanga reo. They receive regular reports and conduct self review when unplanned events and issues emerge.

Decision making. The kōhanga reo management team make good decisions to support the smooth running of the kōhanga reo. The team includes kuia, kaimahi and some parents. They manage the kōhanga reo in the best interests of the children.

Policies and procedures. The whānau have a set of policies organised under four specific areas. Health and safety policies inform procedures and guide practice to comply with appropriate regulations. All health and safety policies and procedures are in place. The kōhanga reo is a place where children’s health and safety is a priority.

Programme planning. Kaimahi plan appropriate activities to support children’s learning. Planning guides the implementation of the programme. Children enjoy a range of activities and are provided with good resources. They gain knowledge and confidence learning about te aō Māori and the wider world.

Assessment. Kaimahi have clear processes in place to assess children’s learning. They observe and document children’s learning and progress. Children’s profile books include a range of information about what children have learnt, their experiences and art samples.

Areas for development

Strategic Plan. There is no documented action plan that shows how whānau will achieve their strategic goals. The whānau should identify and document the actions required for each goal and include ‘who’, ‘what’, and ‘when’ each task will be implemented. Monitoring and reporting progress and successes against their annual priorities should provide useful self review information for future planning.

Programme evaluation. Kaimahi need to improve the evaluation of the programme. There is very little information to indicate how well the programme impacts on children’s learning. Kaimahi discussions need to focus on what worked, what did not work and what changes need to happen to improve the positive impact of the programme on children.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Wharehoa completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

6 Recommendations

ERO and kōhanga reo management developed the following recommendations for the whānau to:

  • improve programme evaluation practices to support programme improvement
  • document the strategic goals and annual plans.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

To the Parents and Community of Te Kōhanga Reo o Wharehoa

These are the findings of the Education Review Office’s latest report onTe Kōhanga Reo o Wharehoa.

“I ahu ma i te Whakarehu

Te tū ai ko hea”

Te Kōhanga Reo o Wharehoa is situated in Waiwhetu, alongside the Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust, Ikaroa district office. The whānau chose Mana Reo as the focus for this review. Parents want their children to be confident speakers of te reo Māori. The review will evaluate how effectively the kōhanga reo promotes te reo Māori.

The kōhanga reo management team make decisions for the running of the kōhanga reo. The team includes kuia, kaimahi and some parents. They manage the kōhanga reo in the best interests of the children. All health and safety policies and procedures are in place. The whānau make children’s health and safety a priority.

The whānau maintains a high quality Māori immersion environment. Kuia are competent and fluent speakers of te reo Māori. The learning programme provides good support for children to learn te reo Māori. Kaimahi use appropriate language development strategies. Children are interested and motivated to learn te reo Māori.

Relationships and interactions between kaimahi and children are warm, respectful and genuine. Kaimahi constantly acknowledge and encourage children’s efforts and achievements. Whanaungatanga underpins all interactions between kaimahi and children.

Kaimahi use a weekly and daily planner to meet the needs of children and guide the implementation of the programme. The children enjoy a range of activities and are provided with good resources. Children gain knowledge and confidence learning about te ao Māori and the wider world.

Kaimahi have clear processes in place to assess children’s learning. Profiles include a range of information about what children have learnt, their experiences and art samples. Kaimahi need to improve their programme evaluation practices to support programme improvement.

Te Ara Tūāpae informs the strategic objectives and actions developed by the whānau. The management team carries out self review in various ways to determine the future direction of the kōhanga reo. The whānau needs to document the review of the strategic and annual plans in more detail.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based on Te Whāriki, the whānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
