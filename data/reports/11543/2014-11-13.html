1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Okaihau ki te uru-mā-raki o Kerikeri. Kua āta whakaritea te kōhanga reo nei, ā, he maha ngā tūmomo rauemi ki ngā taiao akoranga whānui e hāngai mārika ana ki te ao Māori. He tino tirohanga ngātahi tā te whānau me ngā kaimahi, ā, e arotahi ana tēnei ki te whakatairanga i te reo Māori me ōna tikanga mā ngā huarahi e whakaongaonga ana, e whai pūtake ana hoki.

E tino poipoia ana ngā tamariki e tō rātou whānau, e ngā kaimahi hoki hei whakawhanake i tō rātou pakari, i tō rātou mōhiotanga hoki ki te reo Māori me ōna tikanga.

I ngā wā katoa ka wheako ngā tamariki i te angitu i roto i ā rātou akoranga. Ka tautoko te whānau i ngā kaimahi, ā, ka akiaki rātou i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i te pakari me te āheinga ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka ako rātou i te ao Māori mā te tūhura haere. He ākonga māia, he ākonga mōhio hoki rātou, ā, ka mōhio rātou ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae, me ō rātou tātai hononga. Ka whai whakaaro nui ngā tamariki, ka noho whakaute, ā, ka kawe hoki i a rātou anō i a rātou e ako ana. Ka whai wāhi atu rātou ki te taha o tō rātou whānau, ā, ka whakamanatia e te whānau ngā taonga motuhake me ngā pūmanawa ake o ia tamaiti. He tākare, he harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki hei ākonga, ā, he mātātoa tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki te ao Māori.

Ka āta whakamaheretia, ka āta whakahaerehia, ka āta arotakengia, ka āta pūrongohia anō hoki te nuinga o ngā wāhanga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

He pēhea te poipoi i ā tātou tamariki kia whanake ai te pakari me te mōhiotanga ki te reo Māori me ōna tikanga?

He tika te poipoi me te tautoko a te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ā rātou tamariki, kia whanake ai te pakari, te mōhiotanga anō hoki ki te reo Māori me ōna tikanga.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki i roto i te whakawhanaketanga o tō rātou reo Māori me ōna tikanga. He pai te waihanga, te whakapuaki hoki i ngā wheako akoranga o Te Whāriki, hei āta whakatairanga ake i te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. Ka kōrero ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori. I ngā wā katoa, ka whakamahi rātou i te reo Māori ki te taha o ngā tamariki, mā te whakawhiti kōrero, te waiata, te karakia, ā, i ngā wā tākaro me ngā wā taumahi hoki i whakamaheretia ai. He tākare te whakamahi a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki te whakawhiti whakaaro, te whakapuaki whakaaro anō hoki ki te taha o ētahi atu. E whakawhanake ana tō rātou pakari, tō rātou mōhiotanga hoki ki te reo Māori me ōna tikanga i roto i ngā āhuatanga o ia rā.

E tino ākina ana ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi kia whanake ai te tino aronga toi whenuatanga me te tuakiri. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako whakaongaonga o te kounga kairangi e whai pānga ana, ā, e hāpai ana i ngā tamariki ki te whakahono mārika ki te ao Māori. Ka āta whai wāhi atu ngā kaimahi ki ngā whai wāhitanga ako e whakapūmau ana i te tuakiri o ngā tamariki me ā rātou taonga tuku iho. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te tino mōhiotanga ki tō rātou tūnga ake hei tangata whenua, hei mema hoki o tō rātou whānau.

E whai hua ana te akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i ngā hononga whakaute, papai hoki ki tō rātou whānau. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā taunekeneke mauritau, whakaongaonga hoki e manaaki ana i a rātou e ako ana. Ka whakarite ngā kaimahi i te maha o ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te katakata ngātahi, me te ngahau hoki i roto i ā rātou akoranga. Ka whakamanatia te whakapapa ki te ao o ngā tamariki mā te taki i ia rā, i ngā pepeha, ngā karakia, me ngā waiata, me te āhua hoki o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero. He tino tau ngā tamariki i a rātou e ako ana ki te taha o tō rātou whānau. Ka kitea te tino mōhio o ngā tamariki ki te whai pānga nui o te whanaungatanga.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki ngā taiao akoranga whakaongaonga o te ao Māori e whai pānga ana ki a rātou, ā, e hāpai ana hoki i tā rātou ako i te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Kua whai hua te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhanake i ngā taiao akoranga katoa o te kōhanga reo, hei akiaki i te tūhura, te whakahura, te taha auaha, me te pakirehua i roto i te ako. He whānui ngā tūmomo rauemi a te kōhanga reo, mō ngā wāhi o roto, o waho hoki. Ka āta kitea ki ngā taiao katoa te hāngaitanga nui ki ngā akoranga o te ao Māori. E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki ngā tūmomo rauemi māori i ia rā. Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi tētahi taiao e whakatairanga ana i te hōhonutanga o te ako reo. Ka āta whakamahia te taiao hei whakaongaonga, hei whakawero hoki i ngā whakaaro o ngā tamariki me ngā pūkenga reo Māori.

E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki kia whanake ai te pakari me te mōhiotanga ki te reo Māori me ōna tikanga, i a rātou e tūhura haere ana, ā, e whakahura haere ana hoki i ngā āhuatanga hou. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga o te ao Māori, ā, e whakatairanga ana ēnei i ngā mōhiotanga me te reo o ngā tamariki. Ka āta whakamahere, ka āta waihanga hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā taumahi. Ka whakamahi rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo aronga, tae atu ki te whakawhiti whakaaro ngātahi, te hīraurau hopanga, me te auaha kia hihiri ai ngā akoranga mā ngā tamariki.

E whai hua ana ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te tuitui i ngā atua Māori puta noa i ngā āhuatanga katoa o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka hāpai tēnei i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki e pā ana ki te whakaute, te atawhai, te manaaki, te aroha me te tiaki. Ka ako ngā tamariki mā ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, ngā waiata, ngā karakia, me te whakatinanatanga anō hoki o ngā tūmomo tikanga Māori. He māia, he tākare hoki ngā tamariki. Ka kitea tō rātou mōhiotanga ki ngā tikanga Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka whai hua ngā mahi a te whānau ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga i te kōhanga reo. Kua āta tāutuhia, kua āta tuhia hoki te katoa o ngā pūnaha whakahaere, ā, e whakapūmautia ana i ngā wā katoa. He pai te arotake, te pūrongo hoki a te whānau puta noa i ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. I ngā wā katoa, ka aro ki te tupu o te kōhanga reo, te ako a te whānau me ngā kaimahi, me te waiora o te katoa o te tamaiti. Ko te aronga o te whakahaere a te whānau, ko ngā wawata nui e pā ana ki te reo Māori me ōna tikanga.

Ko ngā matea me ngā wawata o ngā tamariki me tō rātou whānau e āta whakamaheretia ana, e āta whakahaerehia ana hoki. Ka whakatakoto te mahere rautaki i ngā kaupapa matua a te whānau mō te tupu me te whakawhanaketanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka hāngai aua kaupapa matua ki te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Ko ngā mahere mahi a te whakahaeretanga e whakarato ana i te kiko o ngā kōrero mō te whakatinanatanga me te arotake. Kua whakatutukihia e te whānau te maha o ngā painga rautaki, tae atu ki te whakawhanaketanga o ngā rawa me te whakapakari i te āheinga o ngā kaimahi i roto i Te Whāriki.

Ko ngā matea ako me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki, me te reo Māori me ōna tikanga kei te pūtake o te waihanga, te whakahaeretanga, me te whakatinanatanga o Te Whāriki. Ka whai hua ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakamahere mō ngā tamariki katoa. Ka kitea ki ngā mahere te aronga nui ki te whakawhanake i te mōhiotanga me te pakari o ngā tamariki i roto i tō rātou ahurea, tae atu ki te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. I ngā wā katoa, ka wheako ngā tamariki i te angitu i roto i ā rātou akoranga.

He pai te tuhi haere i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. E noho wātea mārika ana ki te whānau ngā mōhiohio e whai pūtake ana, ā, e tohu ana i te aronga atu a ā rātou tamariki ki ngā akoranga mā te ao Māori, me te eke angitu anō hoki i ia te wā. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā wheako akoranga hōhonu o te kounga kairangi, hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o tō rātou pakari, tō rātou mōhiotanga hoki ki te reo Māori me ōna tikanga.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake

E mōhio ana te whānau, me arotake, me whakapai ake hoki i ngā rauemi arohaehae o tēnei wā, kia hāngai pū ai ngā kōrero urupare e whakapuakihia ana ki ngā kaimahi katoa. Kāhore te pūnaha o tēnei wā e tāutu ana i ngā whāinga e tika ana mō te tupu ngaio, te whakawhanake ngaio hoki o ngā kaimahi, hei painga ake mō ngā tamariki.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, ā, e whakaae ana hoki te whānau o te kōhanga reo, kia toro atu rātou ki te āwhina o ngā māngai ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ki te whakatutuki i te wāhanga i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo hei whakawhanake ake.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

13 Whiringa-ā-Rangi 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Okaihau is north west of Kerikeri. The kōhanga reo is well established and has extensively resourced learning environments that strongly reflect te ao Māori. Whānau and kaimahi share a strong vision that focuses on promoting te reo Māori me ōna tīkanga in stimulating and meaningful ways.

Children are successfully nurtured by their whānau and kaimahi to develop in confidence and knowledge of te reo Māori me ōna tikanga.

Children consistently experience success in their learning. Kaimahi with the support of whānau, encourage their children to develop as confident and capable speakers of te reo Māori. They learn about te ao Māori through exploration. They are confident and knowledgeable learners who know who they are, where they come from and are aware of their ancestral links. Children are considerate, respectful and responsible learners. They participate alongside their whānau who acknowledge each child’s special and unique gifts and talents. Children are passionate, happy and settled learners who are active participants in te ao Māori.

Most areas of kōhanga reo management are well planned, managed, reviewed and reported.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose ngā taumata whakahirahira as its focus area.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

How well are our children nurtured to develop confidence and knowledge about te reo Māori me ōna tikanga?

Children are appropriately nurtured and supported by their whānau and kaimahi to develop confidence and knowledge of te reo Māori me ōna tikanga.

Areas of good performance

Children are very well supported in their development of te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Te Whāriki learning experiences are well designed and delivered to effectively promote the language and tikanga Māori. Kaimahi are speakers of te reo Māori. They consistently use te reo Māori with children in conversations, waiata, karakia, play time and during planned activities. Children passionately use te reo Māori to communicate their ideas and what they know to others. They are developing confidence and knowledge in te reo Māori me ōna tikanga in everyday life.

Children are effectively encouraged by kaimahi to develop a strong sense of belonging and identity. Kaimahi provide a wide range of high quality, relevant and stimulating experiences that help children to make personal connections to te ao Māori. Kaimahi make the most of learning opportunities that reinforce children’s identity and cultural heritage. Children are developing a strong awareness of their special place and role as tangata whenua and as members of their whānau.

Children are successfully encouraged to develop respectful and positive relationships with their whānau. Children experience positive, stimulating and caring interactions as they learn. Kaimahi create many opportunities for children to laugh together and have fun as they learn. The importance of whakapapa in children’s lives is affirmed through daily recitation of pepeha, karakia, waiata and conversations. Children are very settled as they learn beside their whānau. Children show a strong awareness of the importance of whānaungatanga.

Children are immersed in relevant and stimulating te ao Māori learning environments that support their learning of te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. The whānau and kaimahi have successfully developed all kōhanga reo learning environments to encourage exploration, discovery, creativity and inquiry for learning. The kōhanga reo has an extensive range of resources in the indoor and outside areas. All environments strongly reflect te ao Māori learning. Children have daily access to natural materials. Kaimahi have created an environment that promotes rich language learning. The environment is used effectively to stimulate and challenge children’s thinking and te reo Māori skills.

Children are successfully supported to develop confidence and knowledge of te reo Māori me ōna tikanga as they explore and make new discoveries. Kaimahi and whānau provide a wide variety of te ao Māori learning experiences that enriches children’s knowledge and language. Kaimahi carefully plan and design activities. They use a range of approaches including shared discussion, problem solving and creativity to make learning stimulating for children.

Kaimahi effectively integrate ngā atua Māori throughout all aspects of children’s learning. This helps support children’s learning about respect, care, manaaki, aroha and tiaki. Children learn through conversations, waiata, karakia and the practice of various tikanga Māori. Children are confident and enthusiastic. They show an awareness of tikanga Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

Whānau effectively manage the operations of the kōhanga reo. All management systems are well defined, documented and consistently upheld. Whānau review and report well across all areas of the kōhanga reo. There is constant focus on the growth of the kōhanga reo, whānau and kaimahi learning and the holistic wellbeing of children. High aspirations for te reo Māori me ōna tikanga is the focus of whānau management.

Children and their whānau needs and aspirations are well planned for and managed. The strategic plan sets out whānau priorities for the growth and development of the kōhanga reo. These priorities focus on te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Management action plans provide the detail for implementation and review. The whānau have achieved many strategic successes, including property developments and building kaimahi capacity and capability in relation to Te Whāriki.

Children’s learning needs, interests and te reo Māori me ōna tikanga is the basis of Te Whāriki design, management and implementation. Kaimahi and whānau effectively plan for all children. Plans show a strong emphasis on developing children’s knowledge and confidence in their cultural heritage including te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Children consistently experience success in their learning.

Children’s learning is well documented. Whānau have readily available meaningful information that shows their children’s engagement and regular success in learning through te ao Māori. Children receive rich, high quality learning experiences to help support their developing confidence and knowledge in te reo Māori me ōna tikanga.

Areas for development

Whānau recognise there is a need to review and improve existing appraisal tools to ensure that relevant and targeted feedback is provided to all kaimahi. The current system does not identify relevant goals for the professional growth and development of kaimahi which will ultimately benefit children.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO LicensedKōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends and the kōhanga reo whānau agree that they seek assistance from the District Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust representatives to address the area for development identified in this report.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
