Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Tītahi Bay ki te hapori o Tītahi Bay, ki Te Whanganui-a-Tara. Kāhore ngā tamariki e āta tautokona ana kia whai wāhi pai rātou ki te ao Māori mā te reo Māori. Kāhore te hōtaka a ngā tamariki e āta whakahaerehia ana, e āta whakaritea ana hoki. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e āta tautokona ana i roto i tā rātou whakaritenga ngaio.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Tītahi Bay i roto i te kotahi tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Tītahi Bay ki te hapori o Tītahi Bay, i Porirua. Kua roa nei ētahi o ngā mema o te whānau e whai wāhi ana ki te kōhanga reo. I ngā tau kua pahure ake nei, he nui te taetae mai o ngā whānau hou, me te rerekē haere o te whānau. Neke atu i te 28 ngā tau kua whai wāhi atu te kaiako o tēnei wā ki te kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori mā te reo Māori?

Kāhore ngā tamariki e tautokona mārika ana kia āta whai wāhi atu ai rātou ki te ao Māori mā te reo Māori.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Kāhore ngā tamariki e tautokona mārika ana kia whakawhanake ai tā rātou kōrero i te reo Māori. Ahakoa he mātau ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, kāhore ngā tamariki e whiwhi ana i ngā hua o tō rātou mōhiotanga. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e whai wāhi atu ana ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki te taha o ngā tamariki i roto i ngā taumahi, i a rātou e tākaro ana rānei. He iti noa ngā whai wāhitanga e whakaratohia ana mō ngā tamariki hei whakaongaonga i ngā wheako ako reo. I te nuinga o te wā, ka whakamahi kē ngā tamariki i te reo Pākehā.

Kāhore ngā tamariki e noho mārama ana ki ā rātou mahi, ki te whanonga rānei e tika ana i ngā kaupapa ōkawa me ngā kaupapa ōpaki. Ko ētahi o aua wā, ko te wā mihimihi me te wā karakia, ko te kai paramanawa o te ata, me ētahi o ngā mahinga, ngā whakawhitinga hoki. He maha hoki ngā wā puta noa i te rā ka whakapōrearea ngā tamariki i ētahi atu, ā, kāhore e whai wāhi atu ana ki ngā taumahi. Kei te rangirua ngā tamariki.

Kāhore ngā tamariki e tino tautokona hei ākonga tūmāia, hei ākonga mātau hoki ki te kōhanga reo. Kāhore i te rawaka te āta kite, te āta aro atu hoki ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo matea o ngā tamariki. Ahakoa ka waihanga ngā tamariki i ā rātou ake momo tākaro, kāhore ngā kaimahi e kapo atu ana i ngā whai wāhitanga ki te whakawhānui i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Kei te pā tōraro tēnei ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He take āwangawanga te āhua o te mātakitaki i ngā tamariki. I te wā o te arotake, i kite Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i ngā tamariki e omaoma haere ana me te kore tiaki o tētahi i a rātou, te noho takitahi o ngā tamariki tēina ki te rūma horoi, me te kore whakatika a ngā kaimahi i te whanonga kāhore i te pai. E ai ki ngā kaimahi, he uaua rawa ki a rātou ētahi āhuatanga o te whakaritenga me te whakahaeretanga o te hōtaka.

E ai ki te whānau, he hiahia tō rātou kia whakaongaongatia ā rātou tamariki mā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako reo Māori e tino whai hua ana. Kāhore ngā kaimahi i arohaehaengia i roto i ngā tau e toru kua pahure ake nei. Kāhore te whānau e āta rite ana ki te aroturuki i te whai pānga o ngā whakaritenga ngaio ki ā rātou tamariki. Kāhore i te rawaka te tautoko i te whakawhanaketanga ngaio o ngā kaimahi. Kāhore ngā matea ako o ia tamaiti e whakatutukihia ana.

He ngoikore ngā mahi aromātai o roto. Kāhore ngā pūrongo a ngā kaiako mō te hōtaka e arotahi ana ki ngā āhuatanga e whai hua ana, me ngā āhuatanga hei whakapai ake. Kāhore ngā rehita maiki e tātarihia ana, e pūrongotia ana hoki i ia te wā. Kāhore ngā akoako hōneatanga inā he rū whenua kia whakatutukihia i tēnei tau. Kāhore he arotake rautaki, kāhore hoki he aroturuki rautaki. E hāngai ana ngā meneti o ngā hui ā-whānau ki ngā take a ngā pākeke, kāhore kē e whai pānga ana ki ngā putanga mō ngā tamariki.

He whāiti te māramatanga o te whānau ki ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga. Kāhore anō ngā mema hou o te whānau kia āta whakaurua mai ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. Kāhore i te tino hōhonu te māramatanga o aua mema o te whānau ki ngā huarahi ka hāpai i te whai huatanga o te whakahaeretanga me te tupu o te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore te whakamahere mō meāke nei e whai hua ana. Kāhore anō te whānau kia whakawhanake i tētahi mahere rautaki. Kāhore he angamahi tā rātou hei aroturuki, hei ine rānei i te tupu me ngā putanga paetawhiti, mā ngā tamariki. Kei te pā tōraro tēnei ki ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

 

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Hei whakatutuki te whānau o te kōhanga reo i ā rātou herenga ā-ture, me tahuri rātou ki te:

3.1 whakatutuki i ngā akoako hōneatanga ohorere e tika ana ki te taha o ngā tamariki[R 46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS8 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

3.2 whakatinana i te tū auau o tētahi pūnaha arohaehae me te ratonga o te whakawhanaketanga ngaio me te ako ngaio[R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA7 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

3.3 whakarite i te tuhinga o tētahi tauāki tikanga whakaaro ka ārahi i te kōhanga reo, ā, me whakarite i te tū auau o tētahi tukanga arotake whaiaro, hei hāpai i te kōhanga reo ki te whakapūmau me te whakapai ake hoki i te kounga o te mātauranga me te atawhai[R 46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA5, GMA6 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

3.4 whakarato i tētahi marautanga o te kōhanga reo e whakamōhiotia ana ki ngā mahere, te whakatinanatanga, me te aromātai, ā, e tohu ana i te māramatanga ki te ako a ngā mokopuna, ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, me te horopaki o ō rātou whānau me tō rātou ao[R 43 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; Paearu C2 o Ngā Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

3.5 whakarite, kia mātakina ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa ki te kōhanga reo, ā, kia mātakina anō hoki ngā pākeke e whakarato ana i te mātauranga me te atawhai ki a rātou, e tētahi e noho haepapa ana.[R 44 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; Paearu C2 o Ngā Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā:

Kia toro atu te whānau ki te tautoko o te tari ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ki te whakatutuki i ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo kāhore e tutuki ana i ngā herenga ā-ture, tae atu ki te:

  • whakapai ake i te whakamahere, te whakaritenga, me te whakahaeretanga o te hōtaka
  • whakapiki ake i te aromātai whaiaro, tae atu ki te whakamahere rautaki me te aroturuki.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Tītahi Bay ki te hapori o Tītahi Bay, ki Te Whanganui-a-Tara. Kāhore ngā tamariki e āta tautokona ana kia whai wāhi pai rātou ki te ao Māori mā te reo Māori. Kāhore te hōtaka a ngā tamariki e āta whakahaerehia ana, e āta whakaritea ana hoki. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e āta tautokona ana i roto i tā rātou whakaritenga ngaio.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Tītahi Bay i roto i te kotahi tau.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tītahi Bay is in Tītahi Bay, Wellington. Children are not adequately supported to engage well with te ao Māori through te reo Māori. The programme for children is not well managed and organised. Kaimahi are not well supported in their professional practice.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Tītahi Bay will be within one year.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tītahi Bay is in Tītahi Bay, Porirua. Some whānau members have been involved with the kōhanga reo for a long time. In recent years there has been a regular turnover of new whānau. The current kaiako has been a part of the kōhanga reo for over 28 years.

2 The Review Findings

To what extent do children engage in te ao Māori through te reo Māori?

Children are not adequately supported to engage well with te ao Māori through te reo Māori.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are not well supported as developing speakers of te reo Māori. While kaimahi are competent speakers of te reo Māori children do not benefit from their knowledge. Kaimahi do not engage in conversations with children during activities or free play. There are limited opportunities provided for children to stimulate language learning experiences. Children use English most of the time.

Children do not understand their roles or expected behaviours during formal and informal activities. These times include mihimihi and karakia, morning tea and other routines and transitions. There are often times throughout the day when children are disruptive towards others and not engaged in activities. Children are unsettled.

Children are not well supported as confident and competent learners at kōhanga reo. The diverse needs of children are not adequately noticed, recognised or responded to. While children create their own play, kaimahi miss opportunities to extend children’s learning. Children’s learning is disadvantaged.

Planning and Evaluation

Supervision of children is a concern. During the review ERO observed children running around unsupervised, younger children on their own in the bathroom and kaimahi not addressing unacceptable behaviour. Kaimahi acknowledge they are struggling with aspects of programme organisation and management.

The whānau report that they want their children stimulated through a range of highly effective te reo Māori experiences. Kaimahi have not been appraised for three years. Whānau are not well placed to monitor the impact of professional practice on their children. Kaimahi are not adequately supported to develop professionally. Children’s individual learning needs are not met.

Internal evaluation practices are weak. Kaiako reporting about the programme does not focus on what works well and what can be done better. Accident registers are not analysed and regularly reported. Earthquake drills have not been carried out this year. There is no strategic review and monitoring. Whānau hui minutes are focussed on adult matters rather than on outcomes for children.

Whānau understanding of their roles and responsibilities is limited. New whānau members have not received a thorough induction to the kaupapa of kōhanga reo. These whānau members lack a deep understanding of how to support the effective management and growth of the kōhanga reo.

Key Next Steps

Future planning is ineffective. The whānau have not developed a strategic plan. They have no framework to monitor or measure growth and long term outcomes for children. Children are disadvantaged.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing: 

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake. 

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements they need to:

3.1 Carry out relevant evacuation and emergency drills regularly with children[R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS8 Licensing criteria for kōhanga reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

3.2 Implement a system of regular appraisal and provision for professional development and learning[R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA7 Licensing criteria for kōhanga reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

3.3 Ensure that there is a philosophy statement that guides the kōhanga and that there is an ongoing process of self-review to help the kōhanga maintain and improve the quality of its education and care[R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA5, GMA6 Licensing criteria for kōhanga reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

3.4 Provide a Kōhanga reo curriculum informed by planning, implementation, and evaluation that demonstrates an understanding of mokopuna learning, their interests, whānau and life contexts[R43 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; Criterion c2 Licensing criteria for kōhanga reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

3.5 Ensure that at all times while children attend the kōhanga reo, those children, and the adults providing education and care who supervise them, are supervised by a person responsible.[R44 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; Criterion c2 Licensing criteria for kōhanga reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008].

4 Recommendations

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

That the whānau seek support from the local Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust District Office to address the areas of non-compliance identified in this report including:

  • improve programme planning, organisation and management
  • increase internal evaluation, including strategic planning and monitoring.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tītahi Bay is in Tītahi Bay, Wellington. Children are not adequately supported to engage well with te ao Māori through te reo Māori. The programme for children is not well managed and organised. Kaimahi are not well supported in their professional practice.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Tītahi Bay will be within one year.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori
