2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana a Kaitumutumu te kōhanga reo i te marae o Kaitumutumu ki Rāhui Pōkeka. Kua whakarerekētia ngā whakahaere a te kōhanga reo mai i te tīmatatanga o te tau. I te mutunga o te tau 2012, i hūnuku te whānau, ā, ka haria e rātou ā rātou tamariki. I hūnuku hoki ngā kaimahi. I whakatauria e te whānau hou me ngā kaimahi ki te whakarerekē me te whakapai ake i ngā whakahaere a te kōhanga reo. Te nui hoki o tā rātou whakapau kaha ki te whakaora me te whakapai ake i ngā whakahaere a te kōhanga reo mā te tautoko a te Poari Matua o te Kōhanga Reo.

E ako ana ngā tamariki i te hītori me ngā pūrākau o tō rātou marae me rohe o Rāhui Pōkeka.

Ko te kīngitanga me te Paimārire ētahi mātāpono nui mā ngā akoranga me ngā taunekeneke. He whaitake te āhua o ngā kaimahi ki te whakahaere i te whanonga a ngā tamariki. Ko ngā whakaritenga e whakahaeretia ana e rātou ka ahu mai i ngā uaratanga o te whanaungatanga me te manaakitanga. He ngākau nui me te harikoa te āhua o te whanaungatanga a ngā tamariki ki ngā kaimahi.

Tēra tētahi arotahi nui ki te reo Māori mā ngā tāngata katoa. E whakahaere ana ngā kaimahi i a rātou taunekeneke ki ngā tamariki hei whakanui me te whakawhānui i tō rātou reo Māori. He māia ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ko ngā uaratanga o te whanaungatanga me te manaakitanga te tūāpapa o te hōtaka akoranga. E whakapā ana ēnei uaratanga ki ngā whanaungatanga a ngā tamariki. Ko te Mana Tangata me te Mana Whenua ngā tino wāhanga o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. E ako ana ngā tamariki i ngā ariā whaiaro, arā, ko wai rātou, ā, nō hea anō rātou. E whai wāhi ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakarite i ngā wheako mā ngā rōpū iti o ngā tamariki. E whakahaere ana rātou i ngā rautaki huhua hei whakaako, ā, e arotahi ana rātou ki ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki.

He tino pai ngā kaimahi ki te whakahaere i te hōtaka. Te tini hoki o ngā rauemi mā te hōtaka akoranga.E kaha ū ana ngā kaimahi ki te arotahi ki ngā hiahia whaiaro a ngā tamariki o te ao whānui. E ngākau nui ana ngā tama tāne pakeke ki te tū ki te haka i te haka o te kapa o Pango.

He wāhi whakahirahira, ā, e pīata ana ngā tae i roto i te kōhanga reo. He mea harikoa me te pōwhiri ki te tangata te āhua o ngā wāhi ō-roto. E manawanui ana ngā tamariki ki tēnei taiao. Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi tētahi hōtaka māmā, ā, he whai tikanga te āhua o ngā whakaritenga. Kua āta tiakina ngā pēpi. Me whai whakaaro ngā kamahi a ngā rangi tata e tū mai nei ki te āta whakarite i tētahi māhere motuhake e hāngai tōtika ana ki ngā matea ako mō ngā pēpi.

E tino ora ana te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakatūria e te whānau ngā tukanga whai tikanga i ngā marama e whā kua pahure ake nei.Kua whakatūria ngā kaupapa here me ngā whakaritenga o ia rā.Kua whakaritea e te whānau ngā tukanga whai hua hei whakamāherehere me te aromātai i te hōtaka ako.

E whakahaere ana te whānau i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā a te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki. Ahakoa he iti noa te wā, kua whakatūria e rātou ngā tukanga whai tikanga hei arahi me te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā o te kōhanga reo. He angitu te mahi a te whānau ki te whakanui ake i te tokomaha o ngā tamariki mai i te tokorima o ngā tamariki ki te rua tekau mā tahi o ngā tamariki. Kua eke rātou ki te kaute mōrahi o tō rātou rārangi kaute. E tino whakaatu ana te whānau i tā rātou mahi ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Tēra tētahi māhere rautaki e whakaatu ana i ētahi whāinga nui. E ai ki nga pūrongo a te whānau, he whāinga anō ō rātou e hāngai ana ki te whakapaipai i te whenua me ngā whare, ā, kāore anō ēnei whāinga kia uru ki roto i te māhere. E whakaae ana rātou me haere tonu mahi ki te whakapai ake i tā rātou māhere rautaki, ā, mā tēnei ka pai ake te māramatanga o te whānau ki te whakahaere me te āta whakatutuki i ngā tino whāinga kua whakatūria.

Ngā Mahi ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o Te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Kaitumutumu Te Kōhanga Reo kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Kaitumutumu Te Kōhanga Reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko āna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Tangata.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere i te māhere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • hōtaka whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Tangata

He whakamārama

I kōwhiritia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi teMana Tangatahei kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E ai ki a rātou whakatau me whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā wheako whaitake kia ōrite ai ngā wheako mō te ako, ā, e whai mana ana tō ia tamaiti ki te ako i tāna kōtahi me te ako hoki ki te taha o ā rātou hoa.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Mana Tangata.E ako ana ngā tamariki i te hītori me ngā pūrākau o tō rātou marae me te rohe o Rāhui Pōkeka. E ako hoki ana rātou i ō rātou pepeha. E whakaako ana ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te ako i ngā karakia, ngā hīmene me ngā waiata. Ko te kīngitanga e tautokotia ai e ngā iwi o Tainui, te whakapono karaitiana e kī nei ko te Paimārire ētahi mātāpono nui e hāngai ana ki ngā akoranga me ngā taunekeneke. He whaitake te āhua o ngā kaimahi ki te whakahaere i ngā whanonga a ngā tamariki. Ko ngā whakaritenga e whakahaeretia ana e rātou e ahu mai ana i ngā uaratanga o te whanaungatanga me te manaakitanga. He ngākau nui me te harikoa te āhua o te whanaungatanga a ngā tamariki ki ngā kaimahi.

E harikoa ana ngā tamariki, ā, he manawanui ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki i waenganui i a rātou anō. E whakaatu ana ngā tamariki tuakana i ō rātou ngākau nui me ō rātou hiahia ki te āwhina i ngā tamariki teina. Kua ākina e ngā pakeke ngā tamariki te hāpai i ngā tūranga tātaki, ā, mā tēnei e pakari ake ana ō rātou tū māia me ō rātou āhei ki te mahi i ā rātou kōtahi. E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whakanui ai i te aroha ki te tangata me te ngākau iti o tētahi ki tētahi.

Te reo Māori.Tēra tētahi arotahi nui ki te reo Māori mā ngā tāngata katoa. E whakahaere ana ngā kaimahi i a rātou taunekeneke ki ngā tamariki hei whakanui me te whakawhānui i tō rātou reo Māori. E whakatauira ana rātou i te reo Māori ki ngā tamariki kōhungahunga i te wā e whakaputa ana rātou i tō rātou pepeha. I ngā wā katoa e whakamana ana, e whakamihi ana, e whakanui ana ngā kaimahi i ia tamaiti me ngā rōpū e kōrerotia ana te reo Māori.

Ngā hōtaka akoranga.Ko ngā uaratanga o te whanaungatanga me te manaakitanga te tūāpapa o te hōtaka akoranga. E whakapā ana ēnei uaratanga ki ngā whanaungatanga a ngā tamariki. Ko te Mana Tangata me te Mana Whenua ngā tino wāhanga o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. E ako ana ngā tamariki ko wai rātou, ā, nō hea anō rātou. Mā te whakatakotoranga a te hōtaka o ia rā e whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki te taunekeneke ki ētahi atu me te whakapakari ake i ngā here whanaungatanga i waenganui i a rātou hoa i te wa e ako ana rātou.

Kei ia kaiako te haepapa mō tētahi rōpu iti. E āhei ana rātou ki te arotahi ki te whanake i ngā pūkenga motuhake o ia tamaiti. E whakahaere ana ngā kaiako i ngā rautaki whānui hei tautoko i ngā tamariki. E hāngai pū ana a rātou mahi whakaako ki ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki.

He tino pai ngā kaimahi ki te whakahaere i te hōtaka. E āhei ana rātou ki te whakatutuki i ngā matea me ngā hiahia o ia tamaiti me ngā rōpū ahakoa ka panoni haere aua matea. Ka āhei ngā kaimahi ki te aroturuki, te tautoko me te whakahoki kōrero whaitake i te wā ka hūnuku rātou i waenganui i ngā ngohe me ngā wāhi rerekē. E harikoa ana ngā tamariki ki ngā ngohe huhua, ā, ko ngā taputapu hanganga ētahi o ngā ngohe harikoa.

E kaha ū ana ngā kaimahi ki te arotahi ki ngā hiahia whaiaro a ngā tamariki e pā ana ki te ao whānui. E ngākau nui ana ngā tama tāne pakeke ki te tū ki te haka i te haka o te kapa o Pango. Koi anei te haka motuhake o te tira Kapa Pango i ngā tino tākaro whutupaoro o te motu. E tautoko ana ngā kaimahi i ngā akoranga whakawhitiwhiti kōrero a ngā tamariki. He mea ihiihi, ā, he mea whakahirahira ēnei wheako mā ngā tamariki i a rātou e whakapakari ake i ō rātou mātau ki te reo Māori me ō rātou mātau ki ngā āhuatanga o te ao whānui.

Te taiao ako.He wāhi whakahirahira, ā, e pīata ana ngā tae i roto i te kōhanga reo. He wāhi ngākau māhana, ā, he wāhi harikoa te kōhanga reo mā ngā tamariki. Kua whakanuia e te whānau a rātou rauemi mai i te arotake o mua. He mea harikoa ngā taputapu hanganga mā ngā tamariki katoa. E whakamahi ana ngā kaimahi i ngā rauemi ō-mataora mō ngā papa ā-toi me ngā mahi toi a ngā tamariki. Ko ngā pānui nui kei ngā pakitara e whakamōhio ana i ngā mātua o te whānau ngā kaupapa o mua, te kīngitanga, ngā karakia, ngā waiata me ngā whakaritenga o ia rā e pā ana ki te hauora. E noho ngākau nui, ā, e harikoa ana ngā tamariki ki tēnei taiao.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te hōtaka mā ngā pēpi.E whakahaeretia ana e ngā kaimahi tētahi hōtaka whai take mō ngā pēpi. Kua whāia ngā whakaritenga o ia rā mō ngā pēpi. Ka karakia ngā pēpi ki ētahi atu o ngā tamariki. E whakahaere ana te kaiako i tētahi hōtaka whakangungu ki te kaimahi kei a ia te haepapa hei whakahaere i te hōtaka mā ngā pēpi. Me whai whakaaro ngā kamahi a ngā rangi tata e tū mai nei ki te āta whakarite i tētahi māhere motuhake e hāngai tōtika ana ki ngā matea ako mō ngā pēpi.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamāherehere me te aromātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo hei whakatau i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamāherehere, te whakahaeretanga me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga reo mā a rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka āta hāngai te aromātai nei ki te hōtaka whakamāherehere me te aroturuki i te tipuranga a ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Te mahi tahi me te whakapono a te whānau ki te kaupapa.Kātahi te whānau hou me ngā kaimahi ka whakapā ki ngā whakahaere rerekē. I te tīmatanga o te tau i tino whai whakaaro rātou ki te āta whakarerekē i ngā whakahaere kia tōtika ai ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā. He mahi ngātahi tā rātou mahi kia tōtika ai te whai hua. He ringa raupā te āhua a ngā mahi a te whānau me ngā kaimahi hei whakaora me te whakarerekē i ngā whakahaere katoa a te kōhanga reo mā te tautoko a te Poari Matua o te Kōhanga Reo.

Kua whakaritea ngā pūkete kaimahi mō ia kaimahi. E tino mārama ana ngā kaimahi i ā rātou tūranga me ā rātou haepapa. E mōhio hoki ana rātou i ngā ritenga tika e taea ai e rātou te whiwhi. E whai wāhi atu ana ngā kaimahi i ngā whakangungu huhua hei whakapakari ake i a rātou anō. E taea ana e te whānau ināianei te whakahaere i ngā tukanga aromātai kaimahi mā ngā kaimahi hou.

E tino whakahaere ana te whānau i tā rātou pūtea. He māmā te pānui, ā, he tōtika ngā tukanga hei whakarite i ngā pūrongo pūtea. E tino ora ana te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo. Mā te titiro whakamuri i ngā mēneti a ngā hui ā-whānau ka kitea ngā rerekē me ngā wāhanga kua pai ake. Ka āta whiriwhiri te whānau i ngā take huhua e hāngai ana ki ngā tipuranga me ngā tukanga anō hei whakapai ake. Kua tautokotia ngā tukanga aromātai e ngā pūrongo whānui i ngā hui ā-whānau. E whakamōhio ana ēnei pūrongo i ngā paetae kua whakatutukitia e pā ana ki ngā whakahaere katoa a te kōhanga reo tae noa ki ngā wāhanga anō kua tāututia hei whakapai ake anō. Kua whakatūria ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā.

Te hōtaka whakamāherehere, te aromātai me te aromatawai.Kua whakaritea e te whānau ngā tukanga whai hua hei whakamāherehere me te aromātai i te hōtaka ako. E arahi ana te whakamāherehere i ngā whakahaere o ia rā hei aroturuki i te kounga o te hōtaka. He ōrite te whai whakaaro a ngā kaimahi mō te aromatawai. Ko ā rātou aromatawai mō ia tamaiti te take e riro ai ngā mātua i ngā mōhiohio e pā ana i ngā akoranga o ā rātou tamariki. E tino mōhio ana ngā mātua o te whānau i ngā paetae o ā rātou tamariki.

E whakahaere ana te whānau i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā a te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki. Kua whakatūria ngā pūnaha hou me ngā tūnga hou. Mai i te tīmatanga o te tau kua arotakengia e te whānau ngā tino tūranga whakahaere, ā, kua tāututia ngā tino tūranga me te tuku i ēnei tūranga ki ngā mātua hou o te whānau.

Kua riro i ngā mātua o te whānau ētahi tūnga o te komiti whakahaere me ētahi atu tino tūranga. Mā te tautoko a te kaimahi ā-kaupapa o te rohe kua whakatūria e te whānau tētahi māhere whakatutuki hei whakapaipai i te whenua me ngā whare mā reira e taea ai e rātou te whakahaere, te aroturuki me te whakaputa pūrongo mō ngā mahi kua whakatutukitia i ngā hui ā-whānau.

He angitu te mahi a te whānau ki te whakanui ake i te tokomaha o ngā tamariki i ngā marama e whā mai i te tokorima o ngā tamariki ki te rua tekau mā tahi o ngā tamariki. Kua eke rātou ki te kaute mōrahi o tō rātou rārangi kaute. E tino whakaatu ana te whānau i tā rātou mahi ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te māhere rautaki.Tēra tētahi māhere rautaki e whakaatu ai ētahi whāinga nui. E ai ki ngā pūrongo a te whānau, he whāinga anō ō rātou e hāngai ana ki te whakapaipai i te whenua me ngā whare, ā, kāore anō ēnei whāinga kia uru ki roto i te māhere. E whakaae ana rātou me haere tonu te mahi ki te whakapai ake i tā rātou māhere rautaki, ā, mā tēnei ka pai ake te māramatanga a te whānau ki te whakahaere me te āta whakatutuki i ngā tino whāinga kua whakatūria.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

He whakamārama

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Kaitumutumu Te Kōhanga Reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga a te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

6 Ngā Tūtohunga

E tūtohu ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te:

6.1 te whānau mā te tautoko o te kaimahi ā-kaupapa o te reo ki te:

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Kaitumutumu Te Kōhanga Reo is based on Kaitumutumu Marae located in Huntly. The kōhanga reo had a change in management at the beginning of this year. At the end of 2012 the previous whānau moved on and took their children with them. Kaimahi also moved on. The new whānau and kaimahi resolved to turn around the management of the kōhanga reo. They have worked tirelessly to restore and rejuvenate the management of the kōhanga reo with the support of Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT).

Children learn about the history and stories of their marae and the area of Huntly. The Kingitanga and Paimarire play a significant role in principles for learning and interaction. Kaimahi manage children’s behaviour well. Their practices are underpinned, enhanced and enriched by the values of both whanaungatanga and manaakitanga. Children enjoy the warm relationship they have with kaimahi.

There is a strong focus on te reo Māori for all. Kaimahi use their interactions with children to promote and extend their te reo Māori. Children are confident speakers of te reo Māori.

The values of whanaungatanga and manaakitanga are the basis of the learning programme. Mana Tangata and Mana Whenua are an important part of children’s learning. Children learn about who they are and where they come from. Kaiako provide small group learning opportunities for children.They use a range of learning strategies and are responsive to children’s learning needs.

Kaimahi manage the programme well. The learning programme is well resourced. Kaimahi take a keen interest in children’s personal interests of the wider world. Older boys enjoy performing the hakaKapa o Pango.

The interior of the kōhanga reo is vibrant and colourful. It has a welcoming and happy feel to it. Children are comfortable in this environment. Kaimahi provide a good basic programme. Babies are well cared for. Kaimahi should consider planning a learning programme for the near future, specifically for babies.

The kōhanga reo is in a financially viable position. Whānau have developed good self review practices within the past four months. All necessary policies and procedures are in place. Whānau have effective processes for planning, assessing and evaluating the learning programme.

Whānau manage the operations of the kōhanga reo to ensure best possible outcomes for the children. They have, in a short time, developed appropriate processes to guide and manage kōhanga reo operations. The whānau has successfully grown the kōhanga reo roll in a period of four months from five to twenty one children. The whānau has demonstrated they are managing the kōhanga reo in the best interests of the children.

There is a strategic plan with some priorities noted. The whānau reports other priorities, including property maintenance which is yet to be included in the plan. They agree that further work on their strategic plan should give whānau a clearer picture about how to manage and implement specific priorities.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

4 Findings

Mana Tangata

Background

The whānau and staff selected Mana Tangata as the focus area for this review. They are determined that children experience an environment where there are equitable opportunities for learning, children are affirmed as individuals and are encouraged to learn with and alongside others.

Areas of good performance

Mana Tangata. Children learn about the history and stories of their marae and the area of Huntly. They also learn their pepeha. Kaimahi teach children to learn karakia, himene and waiata. The Kingitanga movement, strongly supported by Tainui tribes, and the Christian faith called Paimarire play a significant role in influencing principles for learning and interaction. Kaimahi manage the behaviour of the children well. Their practices are underpinned, enhanced and enriched by the values of both whānaungatanga and manaakitanga. Children enjoy the warm relationship they have with kaimahi.

Children are happy and enjoy positive social interactions with each other. Older children show they care and want to help the younger ones. They are encouraged to take the lead which builds their confidence and independence. Children enjoy an environment that promotes respectful and caring relationships.

Te reo Māori. There is a strong focus on te reo Māori for all. Kaimahi use their interactions with children to promote and extend their te reo Māori. They model te reo Maori to younger children when they recite their pepeha. Kaimahi constantly affirm, praise and celebrate individuals and groups using te reo Māori. Children are confident speakers of te reo Māori.

Learning programme.The values of whanaungatanga and manaakitanga are the basis of the learning programme. These influence all relationships experienced by children. Mana Tangata and Mana Whenua are important parts of children’s learning. Children learn about who they are and where they come from. The daily programme provides many opportunities for children to interact with each other and develop strong bonds between peers as they learn.

Kaiako have responsibility for a small group of children.They are able to concentrate on developing eachchild’s unique skills. Kaiako use a range of learning strategies. They are responsive to children’s learning needs.

Kaimahi manage the programme well. They are able to respond to the changing needs and interests of individuals and groups. Kaimahi monitor, support and respond to children as they move between activities and spaces. Children enjoy activities that include construction toys. The learning programme is well resourced.

Kaimahi take a keen interest in children’s personal interests of the wider world. Older boys enjoy performing the hakaKapa o Pango. This haka is one that the All Blacks perform on special occasions at rugby tests. Kaimahi support children’s learning conversations. These teaching moments are rich and exciting for children as they progress their te reo Māori capacity and their understanding of the wider world.

Learning environment. The kōhanga reo is vibrant and colourful inside. It is a welcoming and happy place for children. Whānau have increased their resources since last review. Construction toys are enjoyed by all children. Kaimahi use natural resources gathered from outings to contribute to collage and children’s artwork. The posters on the walls inform whānau about past kaupapa, Kingitanga, karakia, waiata, and health routines. Children enjoy and are comfortable in this environment.

Areas for further development

Programme for babies.Kaimahi provide a good basic programme for babies. Routines for babies are followed. They have karakia with other children. A kaiako provides ongoing mentoring to kaimahi in charge of the babies’ programmes. Kaimahi should consider in the near future developing a planned and specific learning programme for babies.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent, to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau commitment and support.The new whānau and kaimahi have recently experienced significant change. At the beginning of the year they set about in an orderly, controlled and systematic fashion to make changes. They worked together so that the envisaged benefits of change were actually realised. Whānau and kaimahi have worked tirelessly to restore and rejuvenate the management of the kōhanga reo with the support of TKRNT.

Personnel documents are in place. Kaimahi are clear about their roles and responsibilities. They are also aware of their entitlements. Kaimahi are exposed to a wide range of personal development options. Whānau are well place to implement appraisals for new kaimahi.

The whānau manages their finances effectively. Financial reporting is clear and transparent. The kōhanga reo is in a financially viable position. Whānau minutes track developments and improvements. Whānau discuss a range of business matters that focus on continual growth and improved management practices. Self review is supported by a range of reporting at whānau hui. These reports inform the whānau of progress in all aspects of kōhanga reo operations and identify areas for further development. All necessary policies and procedures are in place.

Programme planning, evaluation and assessment. Whānau have effective processes for planning and evaluating the learning programme. Planning guides implementation and evaluation is used to monitor the quality of the programme. Kaimahi have a shared understanding about assessment. Their assessments of each child provide parents with information about what children are learning. Whānau are well informed of their children’s progress.

Whānau manage the operations of the kōhanga reo to ensure best possible outcomes for children. They have, in a short time, developed appropriate processes to guide and manage the kōhanga reo operations. New management systems and practices are in place. Since the beginning of the year, whānau have reviewed and identified essential management roles and responsibilities and allocated these roles to new members.

Committee positions and other key roles have also been assigned to whānau members. The whānau, with the support of the District Kaupapa Kaimahi, has developed a property maintenance action plan to implement, monitor and report completed actions to the whānau at their hui.

Whānau have successfully grown the kōhanga reo roll in a period of four months from five to twenty one children. They have almost reached their maximum roll. Whānau have demonstrated that they are managing the kōhanga reo in the best interests of the children.

Area for Development

Strategic plan. There is a strategic plan with some priorities noted. The whānau reports other priorities including property maintenance which are yet to be included in plan. They agree that further work on their strategic plan will give whānau a clear picture about how to manage and implement specific priorities.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
