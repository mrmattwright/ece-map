Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Kiwitoa ki Papakura. E tino poipoia ana, e tino atawhaitia ana hoki ngā tamariki. Kei te tupu haere tā rātou whakamahinga i te reo Māori. He māia, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki, ā, ka ngākau nui rātou ki te whai wāhi atu ki te taha o ō rātou hoa me ngā kaimahi. Ka noho maioha ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga o ngā wheako akoranga.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Kiwitoa i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Kiwitoa ki Papakura, i Tāmaki-makau-rau ki te tonga. E poipoi tonu ana te whānau i te pūmautanga ki te reo Māori, i runga i ngā mahi nui a ngā kaumātua me ngā kuia i tīmata ai i te kōhanga reo, ā, e rumakina ana ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. Ko ngā kuia i kōkiri ai i te whakapuakitanga o te kōhanga reo e whai wāhi nui tonu ana i ēnei rā.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te poipoi me te atawhai i ngā tamariki?

He pai te poipoi me te atawhai i ngā tamariki.

Mana Atua

Ka tautāwhitia ngā tamariki ki te aroha me te manaaki. Ka rongo rātou i te rangimārie, te aroha, te manaaki, te atawhai, me te pārekareka. Ka ngākau nui rātou ki ngā taumahi ki te taha o ō rātou hoa, me ngā kaimahi. Ka kite i te tūmāia me harikoa o ngā tamariki i a rātou e tākaro ana, ā, e ako ana hoki. Ka ngākau nui rātou ki ngā wā waiata ki te taha o ō rātou hoa me ngā pākeke. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te taiao o te reo Māori. Ka rongo, ka whakamahi hoki rātou i te reo Māori mā ngā horopaki ōkawa me ngā horopaki ōpaki. Ka taki ngā tamariki i ō rātou pepehā, ā, he whai wāhitanga anō hoki mō rātou ki te kawe i ngā karakia me ngā mihimihi. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i te whakamahinga tika o te reo Māori, hei hāpai i te āheinga o ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā waiata me ngā waiata ā-ringa, hei whakawhānui, hei whakatairanga anō hoki i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori. Kei te pakari haere ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori.

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi wāhi ako i āta whakaritea ai. He wāteatea, he hihiri, he muramura hoki ngā wāhi ako. He pai te wāteatea hei poipoi i ngā matea ako ngā tamariki. He haumaru te wāhi tākaro o ngā tamariki ki waho, ā, he whāriki ngohengohe kei raro iho. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā taumahi ka whakanui i te ako ā-tairongo, me te haratau. E ākina ana te whai wāhi a ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi e whai pānga ana ki te tākaro. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā wheako akoranga papai e whai hua ana.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā hōtaka akoranga i āta whakamaheretia ai. Ka whai ngā pēpi i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. Ka whai wāhi te whānau ki ngā mahi whakamahere. Ka whai hua ngā mahere, kua āta whakawhanakehia, ā, ka ārahi hoki i ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te whai pūtaketanga o ngā mahere.

He pai te whakahaere i ngā aromatawai e pā ana ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā pūkete me ngā pūrongo ahu whakamua hei tāutu i tēnā e ākona ana e ngā tamariki. Ka mātakitaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tākaro a ngā tamariki, hei hāpai i ā rātou whakataunga aromatawai. Ka whakaatu ngā mōhiohio i te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga i roto i te reo Māori me ngā kaupapa ako. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whakapā atu ki aua mōhiohio me te kōrero ki ngā kaimahi mō ngā akoranga a ā rātou tamariki. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā ngā rautaki e arotahi ana ki ā rātou whakatutukitanga.

Ka whakaaro nui te whānau ki ā rātou tamariki i a rātou e whakahaere ana i te kōhanga reo. Ka ārahi te tūtohinga i te whānau, i roto i ngā āhuatanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kei ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga ngā aratohu mārama e pā ana ki te hauora, te haumaru, me te waiora o ngā tamariki. Ka whai hua ngā mahi a Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo me tā rātou tuhinga e kīia nei ko Tiaki Mokopuna, hei ārahi i te arotake o ngā kaupapa here e pā ana ki te hauora te haumaru, te waiora, me te atawhai. Kua whakaritea ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā tūranga kaimahi. Kua oti ngā arohaehae a ngā kaimahi. Ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā whakataunga.

Kua oti i te whānau tētahi mahere rautaki i whakatinanahia ai e Te Ara Tūāpae me Te Korowai. E mōhio ana rātou ki ā rātou kaupapa matua mō tēnei wā, ā, mō meāke hoki. He pai te whakawhanaketanga o te aromātai o roto, ā, he hua o roto mō ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore ngā kaimahi e aromātai ana i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga. He iti noa ngā mōhiohio e tohu ana i ngā āhuatanga ka whakapai ake i te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga mō ngā tamariki. Ahakoa ka kōrerorero ngā kaimahi hei whakapai ake i te whakapuakitanga o ngā hōtaka akoranga, kāhore ngā whakataunga e tuhia ana hei whakatutuki mārika i ngā whakarerekētanga. Ka pai ake ngā putanga ako a ngā tamariki, mā te whakapai ake i ngā mahi aromātai a ngā kaimahi.

Kāhore he mahere ā-tau ā te whānau. Ahakoa ka whakatutukihia, ka whakaotia hoki ētahi o ngā kaupapa matua o tēnei wā, kāhore e taea e rātou te whakamahere me te aroturuki te āhua o ngā mahi whānui me ngā putanga, i runga anō i ā rātou kaupapa rautaki matua.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakawhanake, kia whakatinana hoki te whānau i ngā tukanga ka whakapai ake i te aromātai hōtaka
  • whakaōkawa te whānau i tētahi mahere ā-tau hei aroturuki i te āhua o ngā mahi whānui me ngā putanga, i runga anō i ā rātou kaupapa matua rautaki.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Kiwitoa ki Papakura. E tino poipoia ana, e tino atawhaitia ana hoki ngā tamariki. Kei te tupu haere tā rātou whakamahinga i te reo Māori. He māia, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki, ā, ka ngākau nui rātou ki te whai wāhi atu ki te taha o ō rātou hoa me ngā kaimahi. Ka noho maioha ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga o ngā wheako akoranga.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kiwitoa is in Papakura. Children are nurtured and well cared for. They are growing in confidence in their use of te reo Māori. Children are confident and happy and enjoy the company of their peers and kaimahi. Children appreciate rewarding learning experiences.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Kiwitoa will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kiwitoa is in Papakura, South Auckland. Whānau continue to foster the legacy of founding kaumātua and kuia where the kōhanga reo is totally committed to te reo Māori and children are immersed in a reo Māori environment. Kuia who were instrumental in the foundations of the kōhanga reo still play an important role today.

2 The Review Findings

How well are children nurtured and cared for?

Children are nurtured and well cared for.

Mana Atua

Children are nurtured in a loving and caring environment. They experience rangimārie, aroha, manaaki, atawhai and pārekareka. They enjoy activities with their peers and kaimahi. Children show confidence and enjoyment in their play and learning. They enjoy waiata sessions with peers and adults. Children are happy and settled at kōhanga reo.

Children are immersed in a reo Maori environment. They hear and use te reo Māori in both formal and informal settings. Children recite their pepehā and have opportunities to lead karakia and mihimihi. Kaimahi model correct use of te reo Māori to support children’s language learning capability. They use waiata and waiata-ā-ringa to extend and enrich te reo Māori development. Children are growing in confidence in their use of te reo Māori.

Children learn in a well organised learning environment. Learning areas are spacious, vibrant and bright. There is plenty of room to cater for children’s learning needs. Children’s outside play area is safe and covered with soft padding underfoot. Kaimahi provide activities that promote sensory learning and coordination. Children are encouraged to participate in play-based activities. Children enjoy positive and rewarding learning experiences.

Planning and Evaluation

Children participate in well planned learning programmes. Babies follow their own interests. Whānau are involved in planning. Plans are effective, well developed and useful for guiding kaimahi practice. Children benefit from purposeful planning.

Assessment of children’s learning is well managed. Kaimahi use profiles and progression reports to identify what children are learning. Kaimahi observe children at play to support their assessment judgements. Information shows children’s progress and development in te reo Māori and kaupapa learning. Parents are able to view this information and talk with kaimahi about their children’s learning. Children benefit from strategies that focus on their achievements.

Whānau manage their kōhanga reo with children in mind. The charter guides the whānau in all aspects of kōhanga reo operations. Policies and procedures provide clear guidelines for the health and safety and wellbeing of children. Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) and their document Tiaki Mokopuna, is a helpful guide when reviewing health, safety, wellbeing and care policies. Kaimahi employment documentation is in place. Kaimahi have completed appraisals. Children are at the heart of decision making.

Whānau have completed a strategic plan that is informed by Te Ara Tūāpae and Te Korowai. They know their priorities now and into the future. Internal evaluation is developing well and benefits children and their whānau.

Key Next Steps

Kaimahi do not evaluate the effectiveness of the learning programme. There is very little information to show areas for improving the quality of the learning programme for children. Whilst kaimahi informally discuss learning programmes to improve delivery, they do not record decisions to bring about change. Children’s learning can lead to better outcomes by improving kaimahi evaluation practices.

Whānau do not have an annual plan. Whilst some ongoing priorities are achieved and completed they are unable to plan and monitor the overall performance and outcomes against their strategic priorities.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendations

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • whānau develop and implement processes to improve programme evaluation
  • whānau formalise an annual plan to monitor the overall performance and outcomes against their strategic priorities.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kiwitoa is in Papakura. Children are nurtured and well cared for. They are growing in confidence in their use of te reo Māori. Children are confident and happy and enjoy the company of their peers and kaimahi. Children appreciate rewarding learning experiences.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Kiwitoa will be in three years.
