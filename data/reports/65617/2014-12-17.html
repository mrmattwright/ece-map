1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kei Stoke, ki Whakatū Te Kōhanga Reo o Kia Tau Te Rangimārie e tū ana. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā mema o te whānau ki ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. He tirohanga ngātahi tā rātou kia ako ngā tamariki i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga mā ngā horopaki o te ao Māori. He hiahia tō rātou kia mōhio ā rātou tamariki ki a rātou anō, me ā rātou taonga tuku iho. Kua roa nei te kuia ka whai wāhi ki te kōhanga reo hei kaiako, ā, ka whakatairanga ia i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo ki ngā whānau, i a rātou e kawe ana i ō rātou tūranga whakahaere, ā rātou mahi hoki.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. He hiahia tō rātou kia arotake te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te pātai aromātai e whai ake nei, arā:

‘He pēhea te kite i te pakari o ngā tamariki hei ākonga i te ao Māori?’

Ka kitea te tino pakari o ngā tamariki i a rātou e tūhura ana, e ako ana hoki ki ngā horopaki o te ao Māori.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te tūhura i te ao Māori. Kua whakaongaongatia,ā, kua tau hoki rātou ki ngā taumahi e hāpai ana i tō rātou māramatanga ki tō rātou ao. I ngā tamariki e tupu haere ana i roto i te kōhanga reo, ka whakapuaki haere rātou i ō rātou mōhiotanga e pā ana ki ngā atua Māori. Ka kitea tō rātou tino maioha, tō rātou tino whakaute hoki mō te whai pānga o ngā atua Māori ki tō rātou ao.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake, ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

‘He pēhea te kite i te pakari o ngā tamariki hei ākonga i te ao Māori?’

Ka kitea te tino pakari o ngā tamariki i a rātou e tūhura ana, e ako ana hoki ki ngā horopaki o te ao Māori.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te maha o ā rātou whai wāhitanga ki te tūhura me te whakahura i te ao Māori. Ka whai hua ngā haerenga i ia te wā, hei whakawhānui, hei whakatairanga hoki i ō rātou mōhiotanga ki te ao Māori. Kei te mōhio ngā tamariki ki ngā atua Māori, ā, he pai ki a rātou te whakapuaki i tēnā e mōhiotia ana e rātou ki waenga i a rātou anō.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i te ao e noho nei rātou, mā te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi rātou i te reo Māori ki te whakawhitiwhiti i ō rātou whakaaro, ā rātou kōrero hoki. Ka taki rātou i ngā waiata, tae atu hoki ki ērā e whakapūmau ana i ō rātou tātai whakapapa ki te rohe. Ka ngākau nui hoki ngā tamariki ki ngā wā karakia.

He tau te noho a ngā tamariki. Ka rumakina rātou ki tētahi tino taiao ako o te reo Māori. Kei te tino mōhio hoki ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao, ā, ka whakaongaongatia hoki rātou e ngā taumahi e hāpai ana i a rātou ki te tūhura takitahi i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga. Kua tino whakawhanakehia ngā māra kai me ngā tūmomo wāhanga o waho, ā, ka whakamahia aua wāhi hei akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te hono atu ki a Papatūānuku. Ka tino kitea te māia o ngā tamariki ia rātou e tūhura ana i tō rātou taiao ki te taha o tō rātou whānau. He pārekareka ki a rātou te whakawhiti kōrero, me ngā kōrero tuku iho. Ka whakatairangatia te hononga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou ao Māori, ā, ka whakapakaritia anō hoki tō rātou tuakiri, me tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga.

I te nuinga o te wā ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā taunekeneke papai, e whakakoia ana i a rātou. He manaakitanga tō ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. E ako ana ngā tamariki i te manaakitanga ki ngā taunekeneke katoa. Ka aro pai rātou ki ngā kōrero whakamihi, ngā kōrero akiaki hoki. Ka arahina ngā tamariki nā ngā mahinga i āta whakaritea ai. He tino tauira te kuia mō te whānau me ngā kaimahi. Ka whakatauira ia i ngā huarahi papai ki te manaaki i ngā tamariki, te whakahaere i te whanonga, te taunekeneke me te whanaungatanga i roto i ngā akoranga. He tau, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao ako ā-whānau e aroha ana, e whai whakaaro nui ana, e whakamana hoki i ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo, mā te tautoko hoki o tō rātou kuia. Ka hāngai ngā hui ā-whānau ki te tupu tonu o ngā kaimahi me te whai wāhi o te whānau. Ka hāngai ngā whakawhitinga kōrero me ngā whakataunga a te whānau, ki ngā kaupapa matua o te wā hei whakawhanake ake, tae atu ki te whakatairanga i te kōhanga reo ki te hāpai, me te whakawhanake anō hoki i ngā rawa. E aro nui ana te whānau ki te whakapai tonu me te tupu tonu, kia whai hua ai ā rātou tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga, me te atawhai.

Ka tino whakamaheretia, ka tino whakahaerehia hoki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ka whakamahere te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā hōtaka akoranga e whakanui ana i ngā akoranga ki te ao Māori, me te reo Māori me ngā tikanga. Ka āta whakaarohia ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. He pai te whakamōhio i ngā mātua mō ngā akoranga a ā rātou tamariki. Kua whakapūmautia te arotake i te hōtaka akoranga ki te tukanga whakamahere, ā, kua tuhia hoki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i aua mōhiohio hei tūāpapa mō ngā pūrongo ki ngā hui ā-whānau o ia marama.

He pai te kapo atu i ngā huarahi ako a ngā tamariki. He pai te whakamōhio i ngā mātua, mō te whakawhanaketanga o ā rātou tamariki, me ā rātou ekenga angitu hei ākonga ki te ao Māori. Ka arotahi ngā kaimahi ki te whakapai tonutanga hei whakatairanga ake i ngā putanga ki ngā tamariki.

Ka whai hua te arotake whaiaro hei whakapai ake i ngā whakaritenga. Kua whakapau kaha ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakawhanake i ngā mahi arotake whaiaro, kia noho mōhio ake ai rātou ki te āhua o ngā mahi ki ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. Nō nā tata tonu nei te whānau i whakatutuki ai i ngā arotake e pā ana ki ngā kaupapa matua o te kōhanga reo, tae atu ki te reo Māori, te hōtaka mō ngā tamariki, me te whai wāhi mai o te whānau.

Ka whakarato aua whakaaturanga i ngā wāhanga matua hei whakawhanake, ā, kua tīmata kē ngā mahi a te whānau. Ka whakamahia tētahi mahere whakahaere hei ārahi i ngā whakawhanaketanga puta noa i te tau. Ka hāpaitia tēnei e tētahi mahere rautaki e whai hua ana ki te whakatakoto haere i ngā kaupapa matua o te wā roa.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Kāhore i te riterite ngā kōrero ki ngā tamariki mō te āhua o ā rātou whanonga. E whakaae ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi, kua tae ki te wā, ā, e tika ana hoki, kia hoki anō rātou ki ngā tukanga me ngā kaupapa here o tā rātou tūtohinga mō te whakahaere tamariki, me te arotake, te whakahou ake hoki.

Kāhore ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki te hauora, te haumaru, me te waiora o ngā tamariki, e tino whakamahia ana. Kua whakapau kaha te whānau ki te whakawhanake me te whakapūmau hoki i ngā rehita e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki. He tino mōhiohio ki aua rehita, ā, ka taea te whakamahi hei whakamōhio i te whakawhanaketanga ngaio o ngā kaimahi me te whakamahere hōtaka o ngā tamariki.

Ka kōrero ngā mema o te whānau mō te whai pānga nui o ngā pou e whā me ngā taonga tuku iho o te rohe nei, hei whakamōhio i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo me ngā akoranga a ā rātou tamariki. Kāhore aua pou here e tino kitea ana ki tā rātou tūtohinga. E mōhio ana te whānau ki tēnei āhuatanga, ā, he hiahia hoki tō rātou ki te mahi ngātahi me ngā kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe, hei whakarite i te hāngaitanga o tā rātou tūtohinga ki tō rātou tuakiri, ngā taonga tuku iho, te hītori, ngā uaratanga, ngā tikanga, ngā whakapono, me ngā mahi hoki e whai pānga ana ki te whakahaeretanga me te manaaki o ā rātou tamariki.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaeretanga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora, me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, ā, e whakaae ana hoki te whānau, ki te whakawhanake tonu i ngā mahi e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro, me te whakatutuki haere i ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo hei whakawhanake ake.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

17 Hakihea 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kia Tau Te Rangimarie is in Stoke, Nelson. Whānau members are actively involved in all areas of the kōhanga reo. They have a shared vision that children will learn te reo Māori me ngā tīkanga in te ao Māori contexts. They want their children to know about who they are and their cultural heritage. The long serving kuia-kaiako promotes the philosophy of Te Kōhanga Reo to whānau as they take on their management roles and responsibilities.

The kōhanga reo whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as their focus area for this review. They wanted ERO to review:

‘How well do our children show confidence as learners in te ao Māori?’

Children show great confidence as they explore and learn in te ao Māori contexts.

Children enjoy exploring te ao Māori. They are stimulated and settled in activities where they can make sense of their world. As children progress through kōhanga reo, they share what they know about atua Māori. They show a strong appreciation and respect for atua Māori in their lives.

Children know about and practice tīkanga Māori at kōhanga reo. They often apply what they know at home with their whānau. Older children confidently use te reo Māori when learning. Children experience positive and caring relationships and interactions. Their sense of belonging is enhanced.

The whānau manage their kōhanga reo well, in the interests of children. They are guided by a well documented strategic plan that is focused on growth for children. New whānau and kaimahi should continue to draw on this expertise as they learn to manage the kōhanga reo for children.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

‘How well do our children show confidence as learners in te ao Māori?’

Children show great confidence as they actively explore and learn in te ao Māori contexts.

Areas of good performance

Children enjoy the many opportunities they have to explore and discover in te ao Māori. The regular outings are used successfully to broaden and enrich their knowledge of te ao Māori. Children are familiar with atua Māori and like to share what they know with others.

Children learn about the world around them through te reo Māori. They use te reo Māori to communicate their thoughts and ideas. They can recite waiata, including those that reinforce their local whakapapa connections to the area. Children appreciate karakia time.

Children are settled. They are immersed in a strong te ao Māori learning environment. Children know their surrounds well, and are stimulated by activities that enable them to explore their interests independently. Well developed maara kai and designated areas outside are used to encourage children to interact with papatūānuku. Children’s confidence is obvious as they explore their surrounds with their whānau. They enjoy the shared conversations and stories about their heritage. Children’s affinity to their world Māori is enhanced and their sense of identity and belonging is strengthened.

Children generally experience positive and affirming interactions. Relationships between kaimahi and children are caring. Children are learning about manaakitanga in all interactions. They respond well to positive affirmation and words of encouragement. Children are guided by well established routines. The kuia is an invaluable model to whānau and kaimahi. She models positive ways to care for children, manage behaviour, interact and establish relationships for learning. Children are settled and happy in a loving, considerate and respectful whānau learning environment.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

The kōhanga reo is managed well by the whānau, with the support of their kuia. Whānau hui are focused on growth of kaimahi and whānau involvement and participation. Discussions and decision making by whānau are focused on current priorities for development which include promoting the kōhanga reo to the community and property developments. Whānau are strongly focused on continual improvement and growth so their children benefit in their learning and care.

Children’s learning is well planned and managed. Whānau and kaimahi plan programmes of learning that promotes learning in te ao Māori contexts, te reo Māori and tīkanga. Children’s interests are well considered. Parents are well informed about what their children are learning. Review of the learning programme is embedded in the planning process and documented. Kaimahi use this information as their basis for reporting at monthly whānau hui.

Records of children’s learning journeys at kōhanga reo are captured well. There is good information for parents about their children’s development and success as learners in te ao Māori. Kaimahi are focused on ongoing improvement to enhance outcomes for children.

Self review is used effectively to improve practice. Whānau members have worked hard on developing self review practices so that they are better informed about the performance of all areas of the kōhanga reo. Recent reviews have been carried out by the whānau on key kōhanga reo priorities, including te reo Māori, the programme for children and whānau participation.

These findings provide key areas for development which whānau have begun to work on. A management plan is used to guide developments across the year. This is supported by a useful strategic plan that sets out long term priorities.

Areas for development

Children receive inconsistent messages about their behaviour. The whānau and kaimahi agree that it is timely and necessary to revisit, review and update the child management procedures and policies in their Charter.

Information about health and safety and children’s wellbeing is underutilised. Whānau have worked hard to develop and maintain health and safety registers for children. There is valuable information on these registers which would be used to inform professional development for kaimahi and programme planning for children..

Members speak of ngā pou e whā and their local cultural heritage as essential elements that inform the management of the kōhanga, and learning for their children. These underpinnings are not strongly reflected in their charter. The whānau recognise this and are keen to work with the district kōhanga reo staff to ensure their charter reflects their identity, heritage, history, values, tīkanga, beliefs and practices for the management and care of their children.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO LicensedKōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013, and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends and the whānau agree to continue developing self review practices, and work through the areas for development identified in this report.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
