2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kei te marae o Papakai te kōhanga reo o Papakai i Tūrangi ki te rohe o Ngāti Tūwharetoa. Ko Ngāti Hikairo o Ngāti Tūwharetoa te hapū. E whakapapa ana te nuinga o ngā tamariki me ngā mātua ki tēnei rohe.

I kōwhiritia e te whānau o te kōhanga reo ko Mana Whenua hei kaupapa arotahi mō a rātou arotake mātauranga. I whai wāhi ai rātou ki te whakahoahoa i te pātai aromātai hei tautoko i te aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga mō te Mana Whenua;

“E pēhea ana te tautoko o ā mātou tamariki ki te whakawhanake: i te mātauranga hōhonu o ngā tūpuna e pā ana ki ō rātou tuakiri me ō rātou tūrangawaewae, ō rātou tikanga, te reo Māori tae noa ki ngā mahi o neherā?”

E hāngai pū ana ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki ki ngā mahi a ngā tūpuna, ngā uara, te reo Māori me ngā tikanga o Ngāti Tūwharetoa me te hapū o Hikairo. E noho rūmaki ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi wāhi whai rawa o ngā mātua tūpuna, ā, kua whakaritea pai te hōtaka hei whakanui i ngā rawa o te kāinga. He mea whakahirahira te mahi a ngā kaimahi me ngā mātua o te whānau ki te whakanui i te reo Māori. E piki ake ana te mātauranga o ngā tamariki me ō rātou māia ki tō rātou tuakiri me tō rātou tūrangawaewae. E whakaatu ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou whai koha me ō rātou māramatanga hei tangata whenua, hei kaitiaki anō hoki ki tēnei rohe. Ko ngā ngohe me ngā wheako ngā tino kawenga e whai wāhi ai ngā tamariki ki te ako i ō rātou tūranga. Ko te whakapakari ake i ō rātou pūmanawa ki te whai koha me te tiaki i te whenua me ngā rawa ō mataora hei whakaora i te whānau ētahi wheako a ngā tamariki. E tautoko ana ngā akoranga katoa i te kōhanga reo ki te ako i ngā tikanga, te kawa o te kāinga, ngā uara me ngā mahi a ngā tūpuna.

E whai hua ana te whānau ki te hāpai i ō rātou tūranga hei kaitiaki o tō rātou kōhanga reo. Kua whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo e tētahi whānau tautoko e hiahiatia ai te whakanui i te marae, te whānau me ngā kuia. He kaha rawa o rātou mahi ngātahi ki ētahi atu o ngā kōhanga reo o te rohe. Ko te tiaki, te haumaru me te whaiora o ngā tamariki te tino pūtake o ngā kaupapa katoa mō te whakatau take, te whakamāherehere me ngā whakahaere katoa. Kua tiakina pai te whānau me ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao akoranga e whakanui ai tō rātou reo, ngā tikanga me ngā mahi a ngā mātua tūpuna.

E wātea ana ngā waka kawe o te kōhanga reo ki te harihari i ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo me te whakahoki i a rātou ki te kāinga.

Ngā Mahi Ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o Te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Papakai kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Papakai.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko āna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Whenua

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere i te māhere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • hōtaka whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Whenua

He whakamārama

I kōwhiritia e te whānau o te kōhanga reo te Mana Whenua hei kaupapa arotahi mō a rātou arotake mātauranga. I whai wāhi ai rātou ki te whakahoahoa i te pātai aromātai hei tautoko i te aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ki tēnei kaupapa arotahi;

“E pēhea ana te tautoko o ā mātou tamariki ki te whakawhanake: te mātauranga hōhonu o ngā tūpuna e pā ana ki ō rātou tuakiri me ō rātou tūrangawaewae, ō rātou tikanga, te reo Māori tae noa ki ngā mahi o neherā?”

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Te tuakiri, tōna tūrangawaewae me ngā mahi o neherā. Kua tiakina pai ngā tamariki i a rātou e ako ana, ā, he kaihāpai angitū rātou ki tō rātou rohe ā-iwi me tō rātou tūrangawaewae. E whai hua ana ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakarite me te whakahaere i te hōtaka o ia rā mō ngā akoranga, ā, e noho rūmaki ana ngā tamariki ki ngā horopaki akoranga whai take. E kaha tautoko ana ngā hōtaka akoranga i ngā mahi a ngā mātua tūpuna, te whānau, ngā marae, ngā hapū me te tūhonohono o ngā tamariki ki te iwi. E whai wheako ana ngā tamariki i te wā i te wā kia whai wāhi ai rātou ki te mahi tahi ki te whānau o tō rātou marae. E māia ana ngā tamariki, ā, e noho mauritau ana rātou i a rātou whakapiki ana ō rātou māramatanga mō ngā mahi a ngā tūpuna tae noa ki ngā tikanga, ngā kawa me ngā uara.

Te whanaungatanga. E ako ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi wāhi e whakatauira ana ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā uara o te whai koha, te tiaki, te aroha me te manaaki tētahi ki tētahi. E kaha ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakanui i ēnei whanonga ki ngā tamariki. Ka kite ēnei tūāhua i te rā katoa mō ngā horopaki akoranga o ia tamaiti i a rātou e ako ana, i ngā akoranga a ngā rōpū tae noa ki te wā ka haere atu ngā tamariki ki tō rātou marae. E ako ana ngā tamariki ko te tūāpapa o ngā taunekeneke katoa ki tō rātou whānau, ko te aroha, te manaaki, te whai koha me te tiaki tētahi ki tētahi.

Ngā taunekeneke i te wā ako. E whai tautoko ana ngā tamariki ki te whakaputa kōrero hei hiki me te whakanui i ā rātou wheako akoranga. Ka kitea ngā whakawhitiwhiti kōrero i waenganui i ngā mātua o te whānau me ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa. E whakahaeretia ana e ngā kaimahi ngā rautaki whai hua i a rātou e whakawhitiwhiti kōrero ana hei whakatutuki i ngā putanga akoranga mā ngā tamariki. Ko ngā rautaki ka whakahaeretia ko te whakapiki i te reo, te whakapakari ake i te māia o ngā tamariki me ā rātou āheinga ki te whakaputa i te reo Māori ā, tae noa ki ngā putanga hei whakapakari ake i ngā tamariki hei ākonga māia e taea ai te mahi i tōna kōtahi.

Te reo me ōnā tikanga. E noho rūmaki ana ngā tamariki ki ngā wāhi ka rangona te reo Māori, ā, he whakahirahira te kounga o te reo ka rangona. Ko te hōtaka o ia rā te take e whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki ki ngā wheako hīkaka, te tūhura i te reo Māori me te ako i te kaupapa nui o ngā tikanga. E tīmata ana ia tamaiti ki te whakaputa i te reo Māori me te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero ki a rātou anō. E ngākau nui ana ngā tamariki pakeke ki te reo ka kōrerotia e ngā kaimahi me ngā mātua o te whānau. E whai tautoko ana ngā tamariki ki te whakapiki i ō rātou mātau ki te reo Māori i te wā he tamariki kōhungahunga tonu rātou.

Te whai koha me te tiaki i te tūrangawaewae. E whakaatu ana ngā tamariki i tō rātou ngākau nui me tō rātou whai koha ki tō rātou whenua. E whakahaeretia ana e ngā kaimahi me ngā matua o te whānau ngā whakapapa hei āwhina i ngā tamariki ki te mārama i ō rātou whakapapa ki te whenua me ngā mahi ā ngā mātua tūpuna. He tūāpapa tēnei hei tautoko i te ngākau whakapiri o ngā tamariki me ō rātou māramatanga ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae. E whai tautoko ana ngā tamariki ki te whakapakari ake i ō rātou ngākau nui ki ngā wāhi katoa huri noa i a rātou. E whanake ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou pūmanawa me ō rātou haepapa ki te manaaki me te tiaki i ngā taonga tuku iho.

Te tautoko me te ū o te whānau. E tino whai tautoko ana ngā tamariki ki te whakapakari ake i a rātou anō, ā, ko te whai wāhi me te ngākau tautoko a ō rātou mātua te take e pēnei ai. E whakaatu ana te whānau i tō rātou ngākau nui ki ngā akoranga o ā rātou tamariki. E tautoko ana rātou i ngā whakahaere o ia rā, ā, e ngākau nui ana rātou ki te ako i ngā mahi a ngā tūpuna ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki. E kamakama ana ngā mātua o te whānau ki te ako; i ngā tūranga katoa o te kōhanga hei tautoko i a rātou ki te hāpai i ngā whakahaere, ngā rautaki hei whakamāherehere tae noa ki ngā āhuatanga hei whakatinana i ngā māhere kua whakatūria. Ka kitea te kaha ū o te whānau ki te whakatinana i ō rātou moemoeā mā a rātou tamariki.

Te Whakamāherehere me te aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamāherehere me te aromātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo hei whakatau i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamāherehere, ngā whakahaere me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga reo mā a rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka āta hāngai te aromātai nei ki te hōtaka whakamāherehere me te aroturuki i te tipuranga ako o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Ngā whakahaere o te kōhanga reo. Ko te putanga ako kounga nui me te tiaki i ngā tamariki te pūtake o ngā whakahaere a te whānau. E tino whai wāhi atu ana ngā mātua o te whānau i ngā whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga reo. E whakahaeretia ana e rātou; ngā whakaaro whaiaro o tēnā me tēnā, te arotake, ngā tukanga hei tuku pūrongo ki te aroturuki me te hāpai i ngā tukanga whakahaere whai take. E whakahaeretia ana e te whānau ngā rautaki whānui e whai take ai hei whakapiri i te whanaungatanga i waenganui i ngā mātua me te whānau whānui. E riro ana rātou i ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki ngā kaupapa whai take o te kōhanga reo me te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Ngā whakahaere e pā ana ki te ahunga whakamua. Ko te ahunga whakamua o te kōhanga reo me tōna whakatipuranga te pūtake o ngā whakaaro o te whānau, te whakamāherehere, te aroturuki me ngā tukanga whakahaere katoa. Mai i tērā pūrongo arotake a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kua whakatutukitia e te whānau ngā whāinga nui hei whakapai ake anō. Kua oti i a rātou te whakamāherehere, te aroturuki me te tuku pūrongo mō ēnei whāinga i ngā hui ā-whānau. Ko te kaha arotahi ki te mahi hei whakapai ake anō i ngā wā katoa me te tipuranga e piki ake ai ngā hua mā ngā tamariki me ngā mātua o te whānau.

Te arotake whaiaro a te whānau. Kua tino whakatūria ngā pūnaha e pā ana ki arotake whaiaro a te whānau, ā, he mea tōtika ēnei whakahaere. Ko te tuku pūrongo i ngā hui ā-whānau ia marama, ngā whakawhitiwhiti kōrero me ngā pūkete kōrero kua whakaritea e whai wāhi ai ngā mātua o te whānau ki te whakatau i ngā take, te whakamāherehere me te tāutu i ngā whāinga nui mō ngā rangi ā meāke nei e pā ana ki te tipuranga tae noa ki ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō. Kua aroturukitia te nuinga o ngā whakahaere i te kōhanga hei tautoko i te tipuranga angitū o ngā tamariki, a, he whai take ēnei whakahaere aroturuki.

Te hōtaka aromātai me te tuku pūrongo. E whakaputa ana ngā kaimahi i ngā mōhiohio kounga nui ki te whanau ia marama mō ngā hōtaka akoranga o te Whāriki mā ngā tamariki. E arotahi ana ēnei pūrongo ki ngā wāhanga e whai hua ana me ngā wāhanga o te hōtaka hei whakapai ake anō kia pai ake ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. E riro ana te whānau i ngā mōhiohio whai take hei tautoko i a rātou ki te aroturuki i ō rātou paetae ki te whakapai ake anō i te hōtaka akoranga.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te māhere rautaki. Ka tino pai ake te hua o te māhere rautaki mā te whānau mēnā kua whakatakotoria ā-tuhi, ka whakaaturia ngā whāinga nui katoa o te māhere rautaki, ā, tuhia ō rātou paetae tae noa ki ngā hua ka putaina.

Te whakapai ake anō i te hōtaka whakamāherehere. E piki tonu ana te kounga o te hōtaka whakamāherehere. He mea tīmatanga noa ngā whakangungu a ngā kaimahi ki te whakatakoto i ngā māhere mō te Whāriki. Kāore anō rātou kia tāpiri i ngā whāinga ako roa me ngā whāinga whāiti ki ngā hōtaka whakamāherehere. Me tuhia ngā ngohe huhua e hāngai ana ki ngā akoranga ā-kaupapa mā ngā tau rerekē o ngā tamariki.

Te whakapai ake anō i ngā pūkete e pā ana ki te akoranga. Kāore ngā pūkete paetae mā ia tamaiti e mau ana i te whānui o ā rātou huarahi ako me ngā putanga ako o tēnā me tēnā. Ka tino pai ake te hua mā te whānau mēnā ka riro i ia tamaiti tētahi pūkete e whakaatu ai i ā rātou huarahi ako i te wā e piki ake ana ō ratou mātauranga e pā ana ki ngā mahi a ngā tūpuna, te reo Māori me ōnā tikanga tae noa ki ngā uara.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

He whakamārama

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Papakai tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga a te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

I te wā o te arotake i tāututia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga kāore i whakatutukitia e ai ki te ture. Hei whakatutuki i ēnei wāhanga me tino whai wāhi atu te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te:

  1. te whakahaere i te wā tika i ngā whakaritenga me ngā tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua ki te taha o ngā tamariki me te mau i tētahi pūkete ā tuhi mō ēnei whakahaere. [R46 Ngā rekureihana mō ngā ratonga kōhungahunga 2008 (HS8) Ngā paearu whai raihana mā ngā kōhanga reo e whai mana ai i raro i ngā paerewa o te Poari Matua o te Kōhanga Reo 2008; 2009; Te Korowai 1995, Whāinga 6 &10].

6 Ngā Tūtohunga

I whakaritea e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā tūtohunga e whai ake nei: Me whai wāhi te whanau o te kōhanga reo kia:

  • haere tonu ai ngā rautaki arotake whaiaro ki te whakarite pūrongo hōhonu mō ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo
  • haere tonu ai ngā mahi hei whakapai ake anō i te hōtaka whakamāherehere me te tāpiri atu i ngā whāinga whānui, ngā whāinga whāiti me ngā ngohe e whakatutukitia ai ngā matea me ngā tau rerekē o ngā tamariki.
  • whānui ake ai ngā pūkete mau kōrero ā ngā tamariki me te tāpiri i ngā mōhiohio mō ā ratou huarahi akoranga me ngā putanga ako.

7 Ngā Mahi ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

Lynda Pura Watson

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Papakai is at Papakai Marae and is set in the heart of the National Park in Ngati Tuwharetoa. The hapū is Ngāti Hikairo of Ngāti Tuwharetoa. Most children and whānau affiliate to this area.

The kōhanga reo whānau chose Mana Whenua as their focus area for this Education Review. They participated in the design of their evaluation question which informed ERO’s review of Mana Whenua:

“How well are our children supported to develop: strong ancestral and cultural knowledge of who they are, their customs, language and heritage?”

Learning for children is set around Ngati Tuwharetoa and Hikairo ancestral heritage, customs, values and language. Children are immersed in rich, natural ancestral surroundings which are meaningfully incorporated into the learning programme. Te reo Māori is successfully promoted by kaimahi and whānau. Children are developing well in their knowledge and confidence of who they are. Children show a growing respect and awareness of their role as tangata whenua and guardians of their area. Activities and experiences enable children to learn about their important roles. These include developing respect and care for the land and valuing rich natural resources that assist whānau to live and survive. All learning at kōhanga reo assists children to learn about tīkanga and local kawa, beliefs and customs.

The whānau effectively uphold their responsibilities as kaitiaki of their kōhanga reo. The kōhanga reo is managed by a supportive whānau who use a shared approach that encourages the involvement of their marae whānau and kuia. They have strong partnerships with other kohanga reo in the area. Care, safety and children’s holistic wellbeing is at the heart of decision making, planning and management. The whānau and children are successfully nurtured in a learning environment that reflects and reinforces their language, customs, heritage and traditions.

Kōhanga reo vans are available to whānau to transport children to and from the kōhanga reo.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Papakai was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Te Kōhanga Reo o Papakai.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Whenua.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

4 Findings

Mana Whenua

Background

The kōhanga reo whānau chose Mana Whenua as their focus area for this Education Review. They participated in the design of the evaluation question which informed EROs evaluation of this focus area:

“How well are our children supported to develop: strong ancestral and cultural knowledge of who they are, their customs, language and heritage?”

Areas of good performance

Identity, belonging and ancestral heritage. Children are successfully nurtured in their learning and development as active participants in their tribal area and on their turangawaewae. Kaimahi and whānau effectively organise and manage daily programmes of learning where children are immersed in a variety of meaningful and relevant contexts for learning. Learning programmes reinforce children’s ancestral heritage, whānau, marae, hapu and iwi connections. Regular experiences provided enable children to participate with their marae whānau. Children are confident, settled and developing well in their awareness and understanding about their heritage, customs, traditions and values.

Whānaungatanga. Children learn in an environment where kaimahi and whānau model and reinforce values including respect, care, aroha and manaaki. Whānau and kaimahi promote these behaviours well to children. This is evident throughout the day in learning interactions with individual children, groups and when children go to their marae. Children are learning that all interactions with their whānau are nurtured through aroha, manaaki, respect and care.

Learning interactions. Children are effectively supported to communicate well to enhance and enrich their learning experiences. Conversations occur constantly between whānau and children. Kaimahi use effective strategies during conversations to achieve a number of outcomes for children. These include building language, developing children’s confidence and ability to use te reo Māori and developing confident and independent learners.

Te reo me ona tīkanga. Children are immersed in rich and relevant language learning environments. The daily programme provides for a wide variety of stimulating ways children can explore te reo Māori and learn about the importance of tīkanga. Individual children are beginning to use te reo Māori to communicate with each other. The older children are receptive to the language used by kaimahi and whānau. Children are well supported in their early years of te reo Māori development.

Respect and care for turangawaewae. Children show a strong affinity to and respect for their whenua. Kaimahi and whānau use whakapapa to help children understand their kinship to the land and their ancestral heritage. This forms the foundation for developing children’s broader appreciation and understanding of their turangawaewae. Children are supported well to develop a strong sense of connectedness to their surroundings. Children are developing a keen sense of responsibility for caring and protecting their ancestral heritage.

Whānau commitment and support. Children are well supported in their development through the active participation and support of their whānau. Whānau show a strong interest in their children’s learning. They assist in the daily operations and are very keen to learn about their heritage and language alongside their children. Whānau are eager to learn all kōhanga reo roles to help support them with management practices and planning and implementation. Whānau demonstrate a strong commitment to realising their aspirations for their children.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Kōhanga reo management. Quality learning outcomes and care for children are at the heart of whānau management. Whānau members are actively involved in all areas of kōhanga reo management. They use ongoing shared reflection, review and reporting to monitor and maintain effective systems and practices. The whānau use a range of useful strategies to foster close partnerships with its members and the wider whānau. They are kept aware of kōhanga reo developments and the children’s learning programme.

Strategic management. The kōhanga reo direction and its continued growth is at the heart of whānau thinking, planning, monitoring and practice. Since the last ERO review, whānau have worked through and completed key priorities areas for development. The planning, monitoring and reporting of these goals have occurred at whānau hui. The constant focus on continued improvement and growth enhances outcomes for children and whānau members.

Whānau self review. Whānau self review systems are well implemented and maintained. Regular reporting at monthly whānau hui, discussions and records are often used by members to inform decision making, planning and identifying future priorities for growth and improvement. Most aspects of the kōhanga reo are well monitored to support the successful development of children.

Programme evaluation and reporting. Kaimahi report good quality information to whānau each month about Te Whāriki learning programmes for children. These reports focus on areas of success and those aspects of the programme that may need attention to better support children’s learning. Whānau have available to them good information which they can draw on to monitor their progress in improving and developing the learning programme.

Areas for development

Strategic plan. It would be of value to the whānau to document and display all long term strategic goals and to record progress, achievements and success.

Further development of programme plans. Programme planning is still developing. Kaimahi are in the early stages of their formal training for Te Whāriki planning. They are yet to add Te Whāriki learning goals and objectives to programme plans. The variety of activities intended with kaupapa learning for the various ages need to be documented.

Ongoing development of learning and development records. Children’s individual portfolios do not yet capture a good amount of their learning journeys and outcomes. It would be of great value to whānau, kaimahi and children to have portfolios which shows each child’s learning journeys as they develop in their knowledge of their ancestral heritage, language, values and customs.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Papakai completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

During the course of the review ERO identified areas of non-compliance. In order to address these the kōhanga reo whānau must:

  1. ensure that regular fire and earthquake drills are carried out with children, and ensure that a record of these practices are kept. [R46 Early Childhood Centres Regulations 2008 (HS8) Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with TKRNT Criteria 2008; 2009; Te Korowai 1995, Goal 6 &10].

6 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendation(s): That the kōhanga reo whānau would:

  • continue to use self review across all areas of the kōhanga reo to report on the overall performance
  • further develop programme plans to include learning goals, objectives and activities that cater for the different ages and abilities of children
  • extend children’s portfolios to include information about their learning journeys and learning outcomes.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based on Te Whāriki, the whānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
