2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Wairoa ki te tāone o Wairoa o roto o Ngāti Kahungunu. Nō Ngāti Kahungunu te tokomaha o ngā tamariki o tēnei kōhanga reo, he Pākehā tētahi, ā, nō iwi kē ētahi atu. He hiahia tō ngā mema katoa o te whānau kia mātau ai ā rātou tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Nō nā tata tonu nei te whānau i whakanui ai i tō rātou ekenga ki te 25 tau. Kua roa te kaiako e whai wāhi ana ki te kōhanga reo. Kua heke iho te tatauranga o ngā tamariki, ā, nā tēnei kua whakakorengia e te whānau ētahi o ngā tūnga kaimahi. Ko tētahi aronga matua a te whānau, ko te whakapai i te pūmautanga o te kōhanga reo, mā te tautoko anō hoki o te purapura.

Ko te arotahi o tēnei arotake, ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. He pai te atawhai me te manaaki hoki i ngā tamariki. Nā te pai o ngā taunekeneke puta noa i te kōhanga reo, he tau, he harikoa hoki te taiao akoranga. Ka tautoko ngā ngā whāea i ā rātou tamariki me ngā kaimahi hoki.

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō, me tō rātou tūrangawaewae mā te taki me te whakapuaki hoki i ngā whakapapa me ngā pepeha. Ko ngā whai wāhitanga ki te ārahi e hāpai ana i te whakawhanaketanga o te tū pakari o ngā tamariki. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whakatau i ētahi o ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki tā rātou akoranga.

Ka whakapuakihia ngā karakia, ngā waiata, me ngā rotarota hei hāpai i ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. Ka whakapau kaha ngā kaimahi ki te ū ki te hihiri me te pārekareka o te taiao ako o te reo Māori, engari i te wā o te arotake, ko te reo Pākehā e kōrerotia ana e ngā pakeke ki ngā tamariki. He mea nui, kia whakarite te whānau i ngā wāhi reo Māori, me ngā tino tūmanako e pā ana ki te whakamahinga o te reo Māori.

Mā roto i ngā taumahi e whakaratohia ana e ngā kaimahi e taea ai ngā tamariki te torotoro haere. He maha rawa ngā rauemi i te kōhanga reo. He mea nui kia whakamahi ngā kaimahi i aua rauemi hei whakawhānui ake i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te kōkiri i tā rātou ake huarahi ako.

Mā te tautoko o te kaiako, he pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i ngā nekehanga o te kōhanga reo i ia rā. He tino kaha te tiaki tika i ngā rawa o te kōhanga reo. Ka whakarato te whānau i tētahi taiao akoranga ātaahua mō ngā tamariki. Me tahuri te whānau ināianei ki te whakawhanake i tētahi mahere rautaki, te arotake i ā rātou kaupapa here me ā rātou tukanga, me te āta whakarite i te tika me te whai hua o te tukanga arohaehae. Me whakatau wawe e rātou te toenga pūtea e tārewa ana.

Me whakapai ake te arotake whaiaro hei hāpai i te whānau ki te tāutu me te whakapai tonu i ā rātou whakaritenga, ā rātou whakahaeretanga anō hoki o te kōhanga reo.

Kua whakaritea ngā mahi e pā ana ki te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai, engari, me whakapakari ake tonu aua wāhanga katoa.

Ngā Mahi ā Meake Nei

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Titiro Hāngai a te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Wairoa kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Wairoa.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake, hei aromātai i ngā wāhanga katoa o ngā akoranga i te kōhanga reo, whai muri i te arotake o mua.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Mana Atua.Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i tētahi taiao akoranga mārie, e hāngai pū ana ki te tamaiti. Ka ākina, ka tautokona hoki ngā tamariki kia whai wāhi mātātoa ki te hōtaka akoranga. He pai te noho tahi a ngā tamariki, me te noho tahi hoki a ngā pakeke me ngā tamariki. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki me pēhea te tohatoha, me te manaaki hoki i ētahi atu. Ka kitea te hononga ki waenga i ngā tuākana me ngā tēina, ā, ka whakamanatia ngā pēpi. Ka tiaki te kaiako i ngā pēpi me ngā tamariki e moe ana. E kawea ana e ia te aroturukitanga o te ahu whakamua me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki. He harikoa ngā tamariki i te kōhanga reo.

Mana Reo.Ka tino tautoko ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki kia whanake haere ai tō rātou mātau ki te reo Māori. Ka whakapau kaha ngā kaimahi ki te whakarato he taiao e kōrerohia ana i te reo Māori. Ko ngā tino taumahi e hāpai ana i te ako o te reo Māori, ko ngā karakia, ngā rotarota, me ngā waiata. Ka whakamahi ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki te tākaro i ngā kēmu reo, me te whakautu anō hoki i ngā pātai. Ko ngā mihi me ngā whakapapa ka tuari. Ka mārama ngā tamariki ki te tikanga kia whakautu ngā kōrero ki te reo Māori i a rātou e whai wāhi ana ki ngā wāhanga ōkawa o te hōtaka.

Mana Tangata.Ka ako ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō, ki ētahi atu hoki mā te whakataki me te whakapuaki hoki i ngā whakapapa. Ko te whakapapa o ngā tamariki ka tuhia ki tā rātou ake pūkete. Ka ākina ngā tamariki ki te whakatau i ētahi o ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki tā rātou akoranga. Ka poipoia te āheinga a ngā tuākana i a rātou e ārahi ana i te karakia. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te torotoro haere i tō rātou taiao.

Mana Whenua.E whakawhanake ana te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki Te Wairoa, ki ō rātou ake hononga ā-iwi hoki. Ka whakapuaki rātou i ō rātou pepeha ki ētahi atu, ā, ka ako i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te tikanga me ngā tohu ahurea o Ngāti Kahungunu ki Te Wairoa, me ō rātou ake hononga ā-iwi. Ka whakawhanaunga atu ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hoa i ētahi atu o ngā kōhanga reo, i a rātou e taetae atu ana ki ngā huihuinga o te purapura.

Mana Aotūroa.He maha ngā wā ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te torotoro haere. I te wā o te arotake, ko te tākaro wai tētahi aronga nui, ā, he pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whakamātau me te torotoro haere anō hoki i ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o te wai. Ko ngā taputapu nui o waho, me ngā tūmomo taonga eke, ka whakawātea i ngā tamariki ki te whakamātau i te koi o tō rātou kori tinana.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te whakapiki ake i te pūmanawa o te hōtaka akoranga.Kua rite te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakapiki ake i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki te whakatinana i tētahi hōtaka o te kounga kairangi, whai hua anō hoki, mā te whakamahi i:

  • Te Whāriki hei hāpai i te whakamahere i te hōtaka
  • ngā rauemi, kia whānui ake ngā tūmomo whiringa e wātea ana ki ngā tamariki
  • ngā rauemi Māori, ngā rauemi tūturu tonu hei whakatairanga i ngā kaupapa ako.

He taiao rumaki. Ko te taiao ako ō ngā tamariki, kāhore anō kia rumakihia. Ahakoa ka whakapau kaha ngā kaimahi ki te whakarato i tētahi taiao e ū ana ki te reo Māori, kāhore anō kia tino whakamanahia e ētahi atu o ngā pakeke. Kāhore anō kia whakaritea ngā tikanga e pā ana ki ngā wāhi motuhake mō te kōrero i te reo Māori. Me whakawhanake i ētahi atu rautaki ki te whakatairanga i te āheinga o te whānau ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te arotake whaiaro ki ngā kōhanga reo. Ka tātari tēnei i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te arotake i ngā wāhanga katoa o ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka hāngai hoki tēnei wāhanga ki te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromātai, me te aromatawai.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau.Mā te tautoko o te kaiako, he pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i ngā nekehanga o te kōhanga reo i ia rā. Ka whakaoti te kaiako i ngā mahi e pā ana ki te whakahaere tari. Hui ai te whānau i ia te wā, ā, ka whakatau ngātahi rātou i ngā whakaritenga. He pai te tiaki i ngā rawa o te kōhanga reo. Ka whakakoro te whānau ki te whakarato i tētahi taiao ako ātaahua, haumaru hoki mō ngā tamariki.

Te aromatawai.He pai te whakatakoto haere i ngā tauira aromatawai i tēnei wā. Ka whakapau kaha ngā kaimahi kia ātaahua ai ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki. Ka whakaurua ki ngā pūkete ngā tauira mahi, ngā whakaahua, me ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki ia tamaiti. Ka whakahokia ngā pūkete ki ngā whānau, hei te mutunga o ia tau.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te whakapiki ake i te rōnakitanga mō te roanga o te wā.Ahakoa he pai te whakahaere a te whānau i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo i ia rā, me tahuri rātou ināianei ki te whakawhanake i ngā pūnaha ka hāpai i te rōnaki tonutanga o te kōhanga reo. Me mahi ngātahi te whānau ki te:

  • whakawhanake i tā rātou ake mahere rautaki e hono ana ki Te Ara Tūāpae
  • arotake i ia te wā, ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga
  • āta whakarite i te whai huatanga o te tukanga arohaehae, me te whai pānga o tēnei hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga ngaio o ngā kaimahi
  • whakapiki ake i tō rātou mōhiotanga, tā rātou whakamahinga anō hoki i te arotake whaiaro
  • whakatau i te take pūtea i tāutuhia ai e te kaitātari kaute.

Te whakapiki ake i ngā mahi ngaio.Ahakoa ka whakatutukihia te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromatawai i ia te wā, me whakawhanake ake, me whakapakari ake hoki i aua whakaritenga. I tēnei wā:

  • kāhore anō ngā hononga ki Te Whāriki kia āta tuituia ki ngā mahere hōtaka
  • he iti noa ngā mōhiohio ki ngā tuhinga aromatawai, mō te ahunga whakamua me ngā whakatutukitanga hoki a ngā tamariki
  • he whāiti te aromātai i te hōtaka, nā te kawe ōpaki noa i tēnei mahi.

E mōhio ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi, e tika ana kia toro atu rātou ki te tautoko o te purapura me Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, hei whakawhanake ake i ā rātou whakaritenga ngaio.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Wairoa is located in the Ngāti Kahungunu township of Wairoa. Some of the children who attend the kōhanga reo have Ngāti Kahungunu links and others are from other iwi or are Pākehā. All whānau members want their children to be speakers of te reo Māori.

The whānau recently celebrated their 25th anniversary. The kaiako has had a long association to the kōhanga reo. Roll numbers have declined and whānau have had to reduce staff. The priority for whānau is to improve the sustainability of the kōhanga reo with the support of the local purapura.

The focus for the review is Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Children are well looked after and cared for. Positive interactions throughout the kōhanga reo result in a settled and happy learning environment. Mothers support their children and kaimahi.

Children learn about themselves, and where they belong through reciting and sharing whakapapa and pepeha. Leadership opportunities help develop children’s confidence. Children are able to make some choices about their learning.

Karakia, waiata and rotarota are used to support children’s use of te reo and tikanga Māori. Kaimahi try hard to maintain an interesting and enjoyable te reo Māori learning environment however, during the review, English was spoken to and around children by other adults. It is important whānau establish designated te reo Māori areas and high expectations for the use of te reo Māori.

Children explore through activities provided by the kaimahi. There are many resources in the kōhanga reo. It is important that kaimahi use these resources to extend children’s opportunities for more self directed learning.

Whānau, with the support of the kaiako manage the day-to-day running of the kōhanga well. The kōhanga reo property is maintained to a high standard. The whānau provide an attractive learning environment for children. The whānau now need to develop a strategic plan, review their policies and procedures and make sure that the appraisal process is appropriate and useful. They need to resolve quickly the remaining financial issue.

Self review needs to be improved to assist the whānau to identify and make ongoing improvement to their management practices and the operation of the kōhanga reo.

Programme planning, assessment and evaluation practices are in place however, all of these areas need strengthening.

Future Action

ERO is confident the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Wairoa was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children atTe Kōhanga Reo o Te Wairoa.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning; and
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

4 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

ERO chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus of this review to evaluate progress in all areas of kōhanga learning since the previous review.

Areas of good performance

Mana Atua. The kaimahi provide a calm and child centred learning environment. Children are encouraged and supported to be active participants in the learning programme. Interactions among children and between adults and children are positive. Children know how to share and care for others. Tuakana teina relationships are evident and babies are valued. The kaiako oversees the babies and sleeping children. She has responsibility for monitoring children’s progress and development. Children are happy at kōhanga reo.

Mana Reo.Children are well supported by to develop competence in te reo Māori. Kaimahi work hard to provide a te reo Māori speaking environment. Formal activities to support te reo Māori learning include karakia, rotarota and waiata. Children use te reo Māori to play language games and respond to questions. Mihi and whakapapa are shared. Children understand the expectation to respond in te reo Māori and when participating in the formal part of the programme.

Mana Tangata.Children learn about themselves and others by reciting and sharing whakapapa. Children’s whakapapa is documented in their portfolios. Children are encouraged to make some decisions about their learning. Leadership capability is fostered when tuakana lead karakia. Children are interested in exploring their environment.

Mana Whenua.Children are developing knowledge about Te Wairoa and their own iwi links. They share their pepeha with others and they learn about aspects of Ngāti Kahungunu ki Te Wairoa tikanga and cultural symbols and their own iwi links. Children build relationships with their peers from other kōhanga reo when they attend purapura events.

Mana Aoturoa. Children are given many opportunities to explore. During the review, water play was a major focus where children enjoyed experimenting with and exploring the properties of water. The large outdoor equipment and ride-on toys enabled children to test their physical dexterity.

Areas for development

Increasing the potential of the learning programme.The whānau and kaimahi are ready to increase their knowledge of how to implement a high quality and highly effective programme that includes using:

  • Te Whāriki to support programme planning
  • resources to provide greater choice for children
  • natural and physical resources to enhance learning kaupapa.

Immersion environment. Children do not learn in a te reo Māori immersion environment. While the kaimahi work hard to provide a te reo Māori speaking environment, the importance of te reo Māori is not always recognised by other adults. Protocols are yet to be established about where te reo Māori must be spoken. English only speaking areas have not been identified. Further strategies to enhance the capability of whānau to speak te reo Māori should be developed.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and self review in kōhanga reo. This determines the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and reviews all areas of the kōhanga reo operations. This section includes programme planning and evaluation and assessment.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management.Whānau, with the support of the kaiako manage the day-to-day operation of the kōhanga reo well. The kaiako completes office administration work. Whānau meet regularly and make decisions collectively. The kōhanga reo property is well maintained. Whānau endeavour to provide an attractive and safe learning environment for children.

Assessment.Existing assessment samples are well presented. Kaimahi work hard to make children’s portfolios attractive. Work samples, photos and information about individual children are included in the portfolios. Portfolios are given to whanau at the end of the year.

Areas for development

Increasing long term sustainability.While whānau manage the day-to-day operation of the kōhanga reo well, they now need to develop systems to support the ongoing sustainability of the kōhanga. Whānau collectively need to:

  • develop their own strategic plan that links to Te Ara Tūāpae
  • regularly review policies and procedures
  • make sure the appraisal process is effective and supports kaimahi professional development
  • increase their knowledge and use of self review
  • resolve the financial issue identified by the auditor.

Increasing professional practice.Although programme planning, assessment and evaluation are undertaken on a regular basis further development and strengthening of these practices are needed. Currently:

  • programme planning does not fully incorporate the links toTe Whāriki
  • assessment records provide limited information about children’s progress and achievement
  • evaluation of the programme is limited because it is carried out informally.

The whānau and kaimahi acknowledge the need to seek support from the purapura and the National Kōhanga Reo Trust to further develop their professional practice.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Wairoa completed an EROKōhanga Whānau Assurance StatementandSelf-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

6 Recommendations

ERO recommends the whānau seek the support of the purapura to address the areas of concern identified in the report.

Future Action

ERO is confident the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

General Information About Reviews

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and

provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
