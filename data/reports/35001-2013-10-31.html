2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kia mōhio te tamaiti ki tona ake ao Māori ki ngā tikanga Māori me tōna ake reo Māori. Ka tū pakari ia i roto i te ao hurihuri. He tangata, he tipua, he tangata. Tihei mauri ora.’(te tauākī tūmanako a te whānau)

Kua whakaratohia e te kōhanga reo o Ata Mārie te hapori o Tokoroa mai i te tau 1991. Ko tō rātou kaupapa nui ko te whakatipu me te whakanui i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. E ai ki ngā pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kua kitea te tipuranga me te whanake o tēnei kōhanga reo mai i taua wā. E whai painga ana ngā tamariki me te whānau. Kua kaha tipu ngā taiao e pā ana ki te whenua me ngā whare katoa. He mea kaha ngā āheinga me ngā pūmanawa o ngā kaimahi me te whānau. E noho ana ngā whānau o tēnei kōhanga reo mō te wā roa, ā, e tino whai wāhi ana ngā mātua o te whānau ahakoa tamariki mai, taiohi mai, pakeke mai, kaumātua mai te reanga e hāpai ana i te mahi.

I kōwhiritia e te whānau o te kōhanga reo teMana Reohei kaupapa arotahi mā tēnei arotake mātauranga. I te katoa o te arotake i kaha ai ngā mātua o te whānau tae noa ki te kuia ki te whakaputa ki te Tari Arotake o te Mātauranga, ‘Ko te reo te mauri o te tamaiti.’ I whakahoahoatia e te whānau tō rātou pātai aromātai hei arahi i te aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga:

‘E pēhea ana tā mātou, tā te whānau whai hua ki te whakanui i te reo Māori me ōnā tikanga ki te whakareri i a tātou tamariki mā te ao whānui?’

E noho rumaki ana ngā tamariki ki ngā horopaki akoranga whai take, ngā horopaki whānui me ngā horopaki whai tikanga. Ewhakatipuangitū ana rātou hei:

  • kaitātaki māia ahakoa e tipu tonu ana, ā, hei kaikōrero o te reo Māori me ōnā tikanga
  • kaituhura hihiko e arotahi ana ki te ao whānui
  • ākonga e tino arotahi ana ki ngā māramatanga me te mātauranga o te ao Māori
  • ākonga e whakahīhī ana, ā, e mātau ana ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae, tō rātou reo Māori me ōnā tikanga tae noa ki ngā tikanga me ngā whakapono o ngā mātua tūpuna
  • ākonga mahi takitahi e ngākau nui ana ki te āta ako i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te ao ō-mataora me te ao ō-ahupungao
  • kaitaki e taea ai te hiki haepapa me te tiaki i ō rātou taiao
  • tamaiti motuhake e maia ana ki te whakatutuki i tōna e hiahiatia ana.

He whānui, he hōhonu te mātauranga ngaio, ngā pūkenga me ngā pūmanawa whai painga o ngā kaimahi me te whānau. E roa ana te wa e hāpai ana rātou i ngā paerewa whakahaere tino teitei. Ko tō rātou kaha hoki ki te hāpai me te whakapiki i te mātau o te whānau me ā rātou tamariki i roto i te kaupapa ka kitea nga putanga hua mā rātou. E whakatutuki ana te whānau i ō rātou moemoeā, ā, he mea pono tō rātou pūmau i ngā haepapa hei kawe e pā ana ki tō rātou tūtohinga.

Ngā Mahi Ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o Te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ata Mārie kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga reo (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ata Mārie.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko āna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Reo.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere i te māhere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • hōtaka whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Reo

He whakamārama

I kōwhiritia e te whānau o te kōhanga reo teMana Reohei kaupapa arotahi mā tēnei arotake mātauranga. I whai wāhi ai rātou ki te whakahoahoa i te pātai matua o te aromātai hei arahi i te aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga mō teMāna Reo:

‘E pēhea ana tā mātou, tā te whānau whai hua ki te whakanui i te reo Māori me ōnā tikanga ki te whakareri i a tātou tamariki mā te ao whānui?’

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Te Ao Whānui.He mea tino whai hua me te whai painga ngā taiao o te kōhanga reo, ā, e kaha tautoko ana ēnei taiao i te tipuranga ā-hinengaro tae noa ki te tipuranga ā-pāpori o ngā tamariki. E tino whai wāhi ana rātou ki te āta tūhura i ngā āhuatanga o te mātauranga Māori, ā, he mea auaha me te hihiri ēnei momo wheako. E tino tautoko ana ēnei taiao i te pāhekoheko o tēnā me tēnā, ā, kua whakatūria ngā whakaritenga hei tautoko i te mahi takitahi a ngā tamariki. E whakahaeretia ana ngā wāhi katoa me ngā rauemi e ngā kaimahi me te whānau, ā, he mea hei tautoko i a rātou ki te whānui i te whai wāhi o tēnā me tēnā ki te ako me te whakanui i te tipuranga ako o tēnā me tēnā. E maia ana ngā tamariki ki te āta tūhura i tō rātou kōtahi, ā, te mātau hoki o ngā tamariki ki te tūhura haere. E whakaatu ana rātou i ō rātou whakahīhī me ō rātou mōhio ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui tonu.

Mauri tau.Ka kitea te aroha nui o ngā tamariki ki te ako. E maia ana rātou ki te tūhura haere me te taunekeneke tētahi ki tētahi. E noho mauritau ana ngā tamariki, ā, kua tiakina pai ō rātou hinengaro mō te rā katoa. E harikoa ana rātou ki te tātaki me te whai koha tētahi ki tētahi. E whakahaere ana ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki huhua hei tiaki i ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau. E whanake kaha rawa ana ngā tamariki ki te whai koha ki ngā whatumanawa o tēnā me tēnā.

Oranga tamaiti.E arotahi ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakarite i ngā wheako whai take mō ngā matea ako o tēnā tamaiti me tēnā tamaiti o ngā tamariki katoa. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi i ngā kaha, i ngā tūmanako, i ngā āheinga me ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki kia tōtika ai ngā akoranga mā ngā tamariki. He mea angitū ā rātou hāpai me ā rātou whakahaere ki te whakatutuki i ngā matea o tēnā me tēnā. E tere ana ngā tamariki ki te noho mauritau ki roto i te taiao o te kōhanga reo, ā, e tere ana rātou ki te whakawhanaunga me te whai koha ki ā rātou hoa me ngā pakeke. Kua poipoitia te whaiora o ngā tamariki hei whakareri i ā rātou mō te ao whānui.

Whanaungatanga.E whakatauira ana te whānau i te ngākau whai koha tētahi ki tētahi mā ngā tamariki. E whakatauira ana rātou i ngā whakawhitiwhiti kōrero ngākau iti i tētahi taiao e manaaki ana, e tiaki ana i ngā tāngata katoa ahakoa ko wai. E whakaputa ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou herenga ki te whānau o tō rātou kōhanga reo tae noa ki te whānau whānui.

Te reo Māori me ona tikanga.E noho rumaki ana ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori me ōnā tikanga, ā, e whai take ana ngā horopaki whai hua me ngā horopaki whānui tonu ka riro i a rātou. Ko te reo Māori te reo whakaputa ka whiriwhiritia e ngā tuakana i te wā ka whakawhitiwhiti kōrero rātou. He mea whai take te reo ka whakaputa i a rātou mā ngā horopaki whānui. E ngākau nui ana rātou ki te katakata i te wā e whakawhitiwhiti ana rātou ki te reo Māori. E patapātai ana rātou i ngā pātai me te tuari i ō rātou whakaaro me ā rātou kōrero ā-ngākau mā te reo Māori. E whanake ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou mātau ki te reo.

E whakaputa ana ngā pēpi i ō rātou mōhio ki te reo ka rangona e rātou. Ka kitea ēnei mōhio mā te nuku tinana, te whakaputa oro me ngā whakahoki kōrero i ngā wā e noho tahi ana rātou i ngā wā ako. E tino whakaputa ana ngā kōhungahunga i ngā oro whāroa me ngā oro whāiti i a rātou e waiata ana tae noa ki te wā e whakawhiti kōrero ana rātou. E whai mārama ana rātou ki ngā karakia me ngā pōwhiri. E noho rumaki ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whakanui ana i tētahi tirohanga o te ao Māori, ā, i tētahi wāhi ko te reo Māori me ōna tikanga te kaupapa nui.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamāherehere me te aromātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo hei whakatau i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamāherehere, ngā whakahaere me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga reo mā a rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka āta hāngai te aromātai nei ki te hōtaka whakamāherehere me te aroturuki i te tipuranga a ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Ngā kaitiaki o te kōhanga reo.E whai hua ana ngā whakahaere a te whānau o te kōhanga reo e ai ki ngā tūmanako me ngā whāinga o te kaupapa. Ko te arotahi ki te mahi mā ngā rangi ā meāke nei, te whiriwhiri i ngā kaimahi hou me te noho mauritau o tēnā me tēnā ngā take nui e whai hua ana ngā whakahaere angitū a te kōhanga reo. E toru ngā reanga whakatipurana o te whānau e whakahaeretia ana ngā tūranga kaitiaki o te kōhanga reo. E aro tōtika ana te whānau i ngā wā katoa ki ngā putanga hua he kounga nui te āhua mā ā rātou tamariki me ō rātou whānau.

He whāinga nui teMāna Reo, ā, ka arotake me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te tau. Tērā anō ngā pūnaha kounga nui mō te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero me te tuku pūrongo. E kaha ū ana te whānau ki te mahi ngātahi ki te whakatutuki i ō rātou whāinga me ō rātou tūmanakotanga kua whakatauria e rātou katoa. Tērā anō tētahi whai whakaaro ngātahi o te whānau ki te arotake whaiaro e pā ana ki te tipuranga me ngā hua whai take mā ngā rangi ā meāke nei.

Te aromatawai i te reo Māori mā ngā tamariki.Kua whakatakototia pai ā-tuhi te tipuranga me ngā putanga hua o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori me ōnā tikanga. E āhei ana te whānau ki te pānui i ngā putanga hua o ā rātou tamariki ki te reo Māori me ōnā tikanga mā te titiro ki a rātou pukapuka aromatawai. He tirohanga whai take ēnei pukapuka kia mōhio ai rātou i te tipuranga ako o ā rātou tamariki ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui tonu.

Te aromātai mō te whai painga.Kua whakatūria e ngā kaimahi tētahi taiao whai hua e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro i tipuranga me te whai painga o te hōtaka. Mā reira, kua āta aroturukitia ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki, ā, kua whakatutukitia ngā matea. E arotahi ana ngā kaimahi i ā rātou mahi aroturuki ki te whai painga o ngā tikanga whakahaere o ia rā me ngā matea e pā ana ki te reo Māori me ōnā tikanga o ngā tamariki ki te whakareri i a rātou mā te ao whānui tonu.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

He whakamārama

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Atamarie tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga a te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

‘ Kia mohio te tamaiti ki tona ake ao Māori ki ngā tīkanga Māori me tona ake reo Māori. Ka tū pakari ia i roto i te ao hurihuri. He tangata, he tipua, he tangata. Tihei mauri ora.’(Whānau mission statement)

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ata Mārie has serviced the Tokoroa community since 1991. Their sustained focus is on the growth of the kōhanga reo kaupapa. The ERO reporting history of this kōhanga reo shows continued growth and development. Children and whānau benefit from this. The physical environments are well developed. Kaimahi and whānau capacity is strong. They have strong retention of whānau and active participation of the different generations.

The kōhanga whānau chose Mana Reo as their focus area for this Education Review. Throughout the review whānau, including kuia, reinforced to ERO,‘Ko te reo te mauri o te tamaiti.’The whānau designed their key evaluative question to guide ERO’s evaluation:

‘How effectively do we, whānau, promote te reo Māori me ona tīkanga to prepare our children for the wider world?’

Children are immersed in rich, varied and authentic contexts of learning. They are successfully developing as:

  • confident and growing leaders and speakers of te reo Māori me ona tīkanga
  • focused and stimulated explorers of the wider world
  • learners with a heightened awareness and knowledge of te ao Māori
  • proud and knowledgeable of their cultural heritage, language, customs and beliefs
  • independent and appreciative learners who inquire into aspects of the natural and physical environments
  • responsible and caring kaitiaki of their environments
  • unique individuals who confidently realise their own success.

Kaimahi and whānau have vast professional knowledge, skills and expertise. They maintain consistently high standards of practice that are sustained over time. Their commitment and dedication to the successful development of whānau and their children in the kōhanga reo kaupapa has resulted in exciting outcomes for them. The whānau are realising their aspirations and uphold their charter obligations with integrity.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

4 Findings

Mana Reo

Background

The kōhanga reo whānau chose Mana Reo as their focus area for this Education Review. They participated in the design of the evaluation question which guided ERO’s evaluation of Mana Reo:

‘How effectively do we, whānau, promote te reo Māori me ona tīkanga to prepare our children for the wider world?’

Areas of good performance

Te Ao Whānui.The kōhanga reo learning environments are highly developed and effectively support children to develop intellectually and socially. They have opportunities to explore matauranga Māori in exciting and creative ways. These environments are highly interactive and set up to foster children’s independence. Kaimahi and whānau make full use of the space and resources to promote optimum learning and development. Children are confident, competent and independent explorers in their world. They show a strong sense of pride in knowing their place in te ao Māori and the wider world.

Mauri tau.Children demonstrate a strong love for learning. They confidently explore and interact. Children are settled and mentally stimulated throughout the day. They enjoy leading and show care and consideration to others. Kaimahi use a range of strategies to successfully nurture children and their whānau. Children are developing high levels of sensitivity to the emotional needs of others.

Oranga tamaiti.Whānau and kaimahi focus on providing for the individual needs of children. Kaimahi know children’s strengths, interests, capabilities and needs for learning. They respond to and manage their diverse needs successfully. Children quickly settle into the kōhanga reo environment, and form strong and trusting bonds with their peers and adults. Children’s holistic development is successfully nurtured to prepare them for the wider world.

Whanaungatanga.Whānau model positive relationships for children. They model positive communication in an environment that is caring and respectful. Children frequently share conversations about their connections to their kōhanga reo whānau and whānau whānui.

Te reo Māori me ona tikanga.Children are immersed in rich and varied contexts of te reo Māori me ona tikanga. Older children choose to speak te reo Māori when communicating. They use language appropriately for a range of settings. They enjoy humour when communicating in te reo Māori. They ask questions and share their ideas and opinions in te reo Māori. Older children are developing fluency in the language.

Babies respond appropriately to the language that they are exposed to. This is reflected in their gestures, sounds and responses during shared learning times. Toddlers are making more refined verbal sounds when singing and communicating. They show an awareness of karakia and pōwhiri. Children are immersed in an environment that promotes a Māori world view where te reo Māori and tīkanga are important.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Guardians of the kōhanga reo.The whānau manage the kaupapa of the kōhanga reo successfully, in line with their aspirations and objectives. Sustainability, succession and stability contribute to the successful management of the kōhanga reo. There are currently three generations of whānau that are actively involved as guardians of the kōhanga reo. Whānau are consistently focused on high quality outcomes for their children, and their whānau.

Mana Reo is an ongoing strategic priority which is reviewed and monitored throughout the year. High levels of communication and reporting is maintained. The whānau are committed and work together to achieve their shared goals and aspirations. There is a strong culture and practice of reflection for growth and success.

Assessing te reo Māori for children. Children’s development and growth in te reo Māori me ona tikanga is well documented. Whānau can read about their child’s success in te reo Māori me ona tīkanga in their assessment books. These also provide insight into how well their child is developing knowledge about te ao Māori and the wider world.

Evaluating for effectiveness.Kaimahi have a well established culture of critical reflection that is focused on programme development and growth. As a result, children’s needs are closely monitored and responded to. Programmes for children are continually reflected on and kaimahi training priorities are planned and provided. Kaimahi focus their monitoring how well practices and programmes cater for all children’s needs for te reo Māori me ona tīkanga, and to prepare them for the wider world.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
