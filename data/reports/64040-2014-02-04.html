2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Kāwai ki Porirua. Ka tino whai hua ngā tamariki nā te whai wāhi nui mai o ngā kuia me ngā kaumātua i ia rā. Ka āta manaakitia ngā tamariki e ngā kaumātua me ngā kaimahi rangatahi. Ka rongo i te mauritau me te manaaki ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te atawhai me te manaaki o ngā pākeke ki a rātou.

Ka whakaratohia ngā tamariki ki tētahi hōtaka akoranga e whakaongaonga ana, e whakahihiri ana hoki i a rātou. He ākonga harikoa ngā tamariki, ā, he tau, he pakari anō hoki rātou ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi ki ngā taumahi i te kōhanga reo. He pai ngā rauemi i te kōhanga reo, ā, kua āta whakaritea tēnei wāhi mō ngā tamariki. He pukumahi, he ngana hoki rātou ki ngā akoranga, puta noa i te rā.

Ka whakahihiri ngā kaimahi i te whakaako me te ako i te reo Māori. E mārama haere ana ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori, ā, e tīmata ana hoki rātou ki te whakamahi i te reo. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whai i ngā tohutohu māmā. Ka mōhio, ka whakamahi hoki ngā tamariki tuākana i ngā kīanga māmā o te reo Māori.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i ō rātou hononga ā-whānau, ā-hapū, ā-iwi hoki. Ka ako rātou i te hītori o ō rātou whānau, me te whai wāhi o ō rātou whānau ki te kōhanga reo me te hapori. He kaha ngā hononga o ngā whānau ki te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā ākonga o mua o te kōhanga reo, he mātua, he kaimahi anō hoki i nāianei ki te kōhanga reo. Ka kitea te tino aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo me tōna whānau.

Ka aru ngā kaimahi i te ahunga whakamua me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki. He pai te whakamahinga a ngā kaimahi i ngā mōhiohio aromatawai, ā, e tīmata ana rātou ki te whakahāngai i te hōtaka akoranga ki ia tamaiti.

E tino whakahaerehia ana tēnei kōhanga reo e te whānau hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Ka noho mōhio te whānau ki ngā whakahaeretanga i te kōhanga reo, ā, ka whakatau rātou i te katoa o ngā whakaritenga matua. He mārama ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, me ngā mahi ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Kawai kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Kawai.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e te whānau me ngā kaimahi ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira tā rātou kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ko tā rātou aronga ki te whakaako me te ako ka hāngai ki te katoa o te tamaiti, ā, ka whakamahi ngātahi rātou i te katoa o Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te hōtaka o ia rā.Ka whakaratohia ki ngā tamariki tētahi hōtaka akoranga e whakaongaonga ana, e whakahihiri ana hoki. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ngā mahinga o ia rā, ā, he pai te whakahaere i aua mahinga. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā wheako akoranga o roto, o waho hoki. E āhei ana rātou ki te whai wāhi ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi e whakawhanake ana i te kori tinana. E noho wātea ana ki ngā tamariki ngā rauemi akoranga o te kounga pai. Ko ngā akoranga ā-kaupapa, tae atu ki ngā mahi tikanga, te kōkiri a ngā tamariki i ā rātou ake tākaro, me te ako i ngā mahinga, e whakarato ana i te pai o ngā wheako akoranga ōkawa, ōpaki hoki ki ngā tamariki. He ākonga harikoa ngā tamariki, ā, he tau, he pakari hoki rātou ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo.

  • Ka whiriwhiri ngā tamariki i ā rātou taumahi, ā, ka kōkiri rātou i ā rātou tākaro
  • Ka tū ngā tamariki ki te ārahi
  • He pai ngā tamariki ki te tākaro takitahi, takirōpū anō hoki mō te wā roa
  • Ka whakamahi ngā tamariki i ngā rautaki hīraurau hopanga ki te whakahaere i a rātou anō, ki te whakatutuki i ngā mahi ka whakawero i a rātou
  • E ako ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou hononga ā-whānau, ā-hapū, ā-iwi hoki
  • Ka kitea te tino aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki.

Te reo Māori.E mārama haere ana, e tīmata ana hoki ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaumātua me ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori, puta noa i te rā. Ka whakahihiri ngā kaimahi i te whakaako me te ako i te reo Māori. Ka whai wāhi nui, ka noho whakahihiri, ka whakaongaongatia hoki ngā tamariki ki te ako i ngā wā waiata, ā, ka ngākau nui rātou ki te kanikani. He pārekareka hoki ki a rātou ngā wā karakia, te taki pepeha, me ngā mihi, ā, he pukumahi tā rātou whai wāhi ki ngā taumahi, puta noa i te rā. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whai i ngā tohutohu māmā. E mōhio ana, e whakamahi ana hoki ngā tamariki tuākana i ngā kīanga māmā o te reo Māori. Ka tino whai wāhi nui ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi i te kōhanga reo.

Te whakamahere hōtaka.Ka whakaratohia ngā tamariki ki ngā hōtaka akoranga i āta whakamaheretia ai. Ka whakawhanake ngā kaimahi i ngā mahere hōtaka mā te whakawhiti kōrero ki te whānau. Ka āta whakawhanakehia ngā mahere ā-tau, ā-wāhanga, ā-wiki, o ia rā hoki. Ka whai hua aua mahere hei ārahi i ngā kaimahi ki whakatinana i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi ako, wheako ako hoki e whai pūtake ana ki ngā tamariki. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao akoranga e hāpai ana i a rātou ki te ako i te ao Māori me te ao whānui.

Te aromātai hōtaka.Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti kōrero mō te hōtaka akoranga me te waiora o ngā rōpū tamariki e tiakina ana e rātou. Ahakoa kāhore aua hui e tuhia ana, e āhei ana ngā kaimahi ki te āta whakawhiti whakaaro mō tēnā e whai hua ana ki te hōtaka, mō ngā āhuatanga hei whakapai ake, me ngā mahi hoki ki te whakapai ake i te whakapuakitanga me te whakatinanatanga o te hōtaka. He nahanaha te aromātai a ngā kaimahi i te whai huatanga o ngā rauemi e whakaratohia ana mā ngā tamariki katoa, tae atu ki ngā tamariki i raro i te rua o ngā tau, te taiao, ngā mahinga a ngā tamariki, me ngā taumahi ako i whakaritea ai. Ka whakaratohia ki ngā tamariki tētahi hōtaka akoranga e aro nui ana ki ō rātou matea me ō rātou ngākau nuitanga.

Te aromatawai.Ka whai ngā kaimahi i te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. He pūkete tā ia tamaiti e tohu ana i ngā whakaahua me ngā tuhinga kōrero e pā ana ki te huarahi ako o ia tamaiti. He tino taonga ēnei pukaiti i āta waihangatia ai e ngā tamariki me te whānau. Kua kapo ake ngā kaimahi i ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā āhuatanga kaha o ia tamaiti. He pai te whakamahinga a ngā kaimahi i ngā mōhiohio aromatawai, ā, e tīmata ana rātou ki te whakahāngai i te hōtaka akoranga ki ia tamaiti.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai i ngā kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki me te whānau.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo.He pai te mahi ngātahi a ngā kaumātua me ngā mātua ki te whakahaere i tēnei kōhanga reo. Ka whakamōhiotia ngā mema o te whānau mō ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, ā, ka whakatau rātou i te katoa o ngā whakaritenga matua. Hui ai te whānau i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti kōrero, te pūrongo hoki i ngā āhuatanga whakahirahira o te kōhanga reo, pērā i te arotake whaiaro, te whakamahere mō ngā tau kei te heke mai, te whakahaeretanga o ia rā, ngā pūtea, te tiaki i te whare me ngā rawa, te hauora me te haumaru, me ngā take kaimahi. Ka ngākau nui hoki ngā kaimahi ki te hōtaka akoranga e whakaratohia ana mā ngā tamariki. Ka tuku pūrongo te kaiako i ia te wā, ā, he tino tirohanga tēnei ki ngā mātua, mō ngā mahi mā ngā tamariki. He pai te whakahaeretanga, he mārama hoki ngā whakaritenga i te kōhanga reo.

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Kāwai , tētahiTauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhangame tētahiRārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

I te wā o te arotake i tāutuhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ētahi wāhanga tautukunga kāore i tutuki. Hei whakapai ake i ngā whakaritenga o tēnei wā, me tahuri te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te:

  • whakakoro hei āta whakarite i whakatika haere, i te noho haumaru anō hoki o ngā wāhi tākaro o waho mō ngā tamariki, i ngā wā katoa.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Ngā Kawai Te Kōhanga Reo is in Porirua. Children are very fortunate to have kuia and kaumātua involvement and participation on a daily basis. Children are well cared for by kaumātua and younger staff members. A warm and welcoming tone is evident. Children benefit from the warmth and care shown to them by the adults.

Children are provided with a stimulating and interesting learning programme. Children are happy learners who are comfortable and confident in the kōhanga reo environment. Children are willing participants in kōhanga reo activities. The kōhanga reo is well resourced and set up for children. They are busy and engaged in learning throughout the day.

Staff members make te reo Māori teaching and learning interesting. Children are beginning to understand and use te reo Māori. Children are able to follow basic commands and instructions. Older children know and use simple te reo Māori phrases.

Children learn about their whānau, hapū and iwi connections. They also learn about their whānau history and their whānau association to the kōhanga reo and local area. Whānau connectedness to the kōhanga reo is strong. Past students of the kōhanga reo are now parents and staff members. Children demonstrate a strong sense of belonging to the kōhanga reo and the people involved.

Staff members track children’s progress and development. Assessment information is used well by the staff members who are beginning to tailor the learning programme to each child.

This kōhanga reo is well managed by the whānau in the interests of the children. Whānau members are well informed about kōhanga reo operations and they make all key decisions. Kōhanga reo operations and decision making is transparent.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Kāwai was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Kāwai.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning; and
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

4 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

The whānau and staff selected Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as their focus for this review. They follow a holistic approach to teaching and learning and use all of the Taumata Whakahirahira collectively.

Areas of good performance

The daily programme.Children are provided with a stimulating and interesting learning programme. Children know the daily routines and these are well managed. Children enjoy both indoor and outdoor learning experiences. They are able to participate in a range of physical developmental activities. Good quality learning resources are readily available and accessible for children. Kaupapa learning including tikanga practices, independant play and learning about routines provide children with a good variety of formal and informal learning experiences. Children are happy learners who are comfortable and confident in the kōhanga reo environment.

  • Children choose their activities and initiate play
  • Children take lead roles
  • Children play well independently and in groups for sustained periods of time
  • Children use problem solving techniques to manage themselves and to approach challenging tasks
  • Children are learning about their whānau, hapū and iwi connections
  • Children demonstrate a strong sense of belonging.

Te reo Māori.Children are beginning to understand and to use te reo Māori. Kaumātua and staff members use te reo Māori throughout the day with children. Staff members make te reo Māori teaching and learning interesting. Children are fully engaged, excited and stimulated to learn during waiata sessions where they dance joyfully. They also enjoy karakia times, reciting pepeha and mihi and are busily involved in activities during the day. Children are able to follow basic commands and instructions. Older children know and use simple te reo Māori phrases. Children are willing participants in kōhanga reo activities.

Programme planning.Children are provided with well planned learning programmes. Staff members develop programme plans in consultation with whānau members. Annual, term, weekly and daily plans are well developed. These plans provide staff members with a useful guide to help them implement a range of meaningful learning activities and experiences for the children. Children participate in a learning environment that supports them to learn about the Māori world and the wider world.

Programme evaluation.Staff meet regularly to discuss the learning programme and the well being of the group of children that they have responsibility for. While these meetings are not documented, staff are able to confidently discuss what is going well in the programme, the areas that require improvement and the actions taken to improve the delivery and implementation of the programme. Staff systematically evaluate the effectiveness of resource provision for all children including children under two years old, the physical environment, children’s routines and organised learning activities. Children are provided with a learning programme that is responsive to their needs and interests.

Assessment.Staff members track children’s progress and development. There are individual books that include photos and captions about each child’s learning journey. These small, well designed books are treasured by both the children and whānau members. Staff members have captured information about individual children’s interests and strengths. Assessment information is used well by the staff members who are beginning to tailor the learning programme to each child.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau.

Areas of good performance

Kōhanga reo management. Kaumātua and parents work well together to manage this kōhanga reo. Whānau members are informed about kōhanga reo operations and they make all key decisions. Whānau members meet regularly to discuss and report on important aspects of the kōhanga reo such as self review, planning for the future, day to day management, finance, maintenance, health and safety and personnel matters. Whānau members also take a keen interest in the learning programme provided for children. The kaiako provides regular reports that give the parents a clear insight into what is happening for children. Kōhanga reo operations are well managed and transparent.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Kāwai Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff ofcompleted an EROKōhanga Whānau Assurance StatementandSelf-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

In order to improve current practice the kōhanga reo whānau should:

  • endeavour to ensure that the outdoor play areas are kept in good repair and are safe at all times for children.

6 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendation(s):

6.1 the whānau ensure that all kōhanga reo records are kept at kōhanga reo.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

To the Parents and Community of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Kāwai

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā KāwaiThese are the findings of the Education Review Office’s latest report on.

Ngā Kawai Te Kōhanga Reo is in Porirua. Children are very fortunate to have kuia and kaumātua involvement and participation on a daily basis. Children are well cared for by kaumātua and younger staff members. A warm and welcoming tone is evident. Children benefit from the warmth and care shown to them by the adults.

Children are provided with a stimulating and interesting learning programme. Children are happy learners who are comfortable and confident in the kōhanga reo environment. Children are willing participants in kōhanga reo activities. The kōhanga reo is well resourced and set up for children. They are busy and engaged in learning throughout the day.

Staff members make te reo Māori teaching and learning interesting. Children are beginning to understand and use te reo Māori. Children are able to follow basic commands and instructions. Older children know and use simple te reo Māori phrases.

Children learn about their whānau, hapū and iwi connections. They also learn about their whānau history and their whānau association to the kōhanga reo and local area. Whānau connectedness to the kōhanga reo is strong. Past students of the kōhanga reo are now parents and staff members. Children demonstrate a strong sense of belonging to the kōhanga reo and the people involved.

Staff members track children’s progress and development. Assessment information is used well by the staff members who are beginning to tailor the learning programme to each child.

This kōhanga reo is well managed by the whānau in the interests of the children. Whānau members are well informed about kōhanga reo operations and they make all key decisions. Kōhanga reo operations and decision making is transparent.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to student achievement and useful to this kōhanga reo.

If you would like a copy of the full report, please contact the kōhanga reo or see the ERO web page, http://www.ero.govt.nz.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū ā-Motu)

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
