1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kei Camberley, ki Heretaunga Te Kōhanga Reo o Heretaunga e tū ana. Ka ahu mai ngā tamariki o tēnei kōhanga reo i te takiwā nei.

I whakaae te whānau, ko Mana Reo te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. I ngā wā katoa, ka noho pūmau te whānau ki tētahi tino taiao rumaki reo Māori mō ngā tamariki. He mātau ngā kuia me ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i ngā tauira papai o te reo Māori e kōrerohia ana.

Ka whakamaheretia, ka āta whakaritea anō hoki te hōtaka akoranga. He pai te riterite o ngā akoranga ōkawa me ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura haere me te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao, ā, me te kaha aronga anō hoki ki tō rātou toi whenuatanga.

He pukumahi te whānau, ā, ka whai hua tā rātou tino whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga i te kōhanga reo. Nā te pūrongo i ia te wā, ka noho mōhio te whānau, ā, ka whakatau ngātahi anō hoki i ngā whakaritenga. Ka ū te whānau ki ngā whakaritenga a te kaituku mahi pai. Ka whakamahia te arotake whaiaro hei tātari i ngā wāhanga ka whakapai ake i te kōhanga reo. Ka whiwhi, ka whakatutuki haere hoki i ngā pūrongo ka whakapuakihia i ia te wā ki ngā hui o ia marama. Ka whai hua te arotake whaiaro i ia te wā, hei painga mā ngā tamariki.

He matawhānui, he pai hoki te whakaritenga o te whakamahere hōtaka. Ka whai hua ai ngā mahi aromatawai. Engari, ko te aromātai hōtaka tētahi wāhanga hei whakapai ake. Me whakaoti hoki te whānau i tā rātou mahere rautaki me tā rātou mahere ā-tau.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga reo (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Reo

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki

  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Reo

He whakamārama

He pēhea te tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo Māori?

Ka tino tautokona ngā tamariki ki te ako, kia whakamahi hoki i te reo Māori.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Mana Reo. Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. I ngā wā katoa ka noho pūmau te whānau ki tētahi taiao rumaki reo Māori. He mātau ngā kuia me ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, ā, ka taunekeneke rātou ki ngā tamariki mā te reo Māori, puta noa i te rā. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā rautaki hei whakatairanga i te reo Māori. Ko ētahi o aua rautaki, ko ngā pukapuka, ngā pikitia, ngā tohu, me ngā taunekeneke ka tino whai pūtake. He hihiri, he whakaongaonga hoki ngā tamariki nā te taiao ako reo. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki kia mātau ai ki te reo Māori.

Ka rongo ngā tamariki i ngā tauira papai o te reo Māori e kōrerohia ana. Ka whakatauira te kuia i te kairangi o te reo Māori. He pakari te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā waiata, ngā mihimihi, ngā pepeha, me te whakapapa māmā. Ka pou here aua taumahi i te Mana Tangata me te Mana Whenua. E ako ana ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i ngā hanganga reo e tika ana i a rātou e whakautu pātai ana. Kei te pakari haere ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori.

Te hōtaka akoranga. Kua āta whakamaheretia, kua āta whakaritea anō hoki te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki. He ritenga tā te ako ōkawa me ngā whai wāhitanga anō hoki mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura me te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao, ā, me te kaha aronga anō hoki ki tō rātou toi whenuatanga. Ka whai pānga nui te noho mai o ngā kuia ki te mauritau o ngā tamariki. Ka tino kitea hoki te noho ngātahi a te tuakana me te teina. Ka āta whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi e ngākau nuitia ana e rātou, ā, ka noho pūmau ki ā rātou kaupapa mahi mō te roanga o te wā. Ka whai huanga ngā tēina ki ngā taumahi kua āta whakamahertia, ā, ka poipoi anō hoki i tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ka whakaihiihi ai ngā tamariki, ka harikoa hoki rātou.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whakahaere a te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki?

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau. He pukumahi te whānau, ā, ka whai hua tā rātou whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo. He pai te tae ā-tinana atu ki ngā hui ā-whānau. Ko ngā pūrongo o ia te wā e whakamōhio ana i te whānau, ā, ka whakatau ngātahi hoki rātou i ngā whakaritenga. He pakari ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga ā rātou mō te hauora me te haumaru, ā, ka ārahi ēnei i ngā whakaritenga. He mārama te pūrongo i ngā pūtea me ngā take moni. Ka tautoko mārika te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Ngā kaimahi. Ka kitea ki te whānau ngā whakaritenga a te kaituku mahi pai. He pūnaha ā rātou hei hāpai i ngā mahi a ngā kaimah. Kua whakaritea ngā tuhinga a ngā kaimahi. Ka ārahi ngā arohaehae i te āhua mahi o ngā kaimahi me te whakawhanaketanga. Ko te āhua mahi a ngā kaimahi ka whai pānga pai ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Te Ara Tūāpae. He mārama ki ngā mema o te whānau ā rātou kaupapa matua mō tēnei wā, me ērā hoki ka whai ake. Ka whai pānga Te Ara Tūāpae ki ngā whāinga rautaki me ngā mahinga e whai atu ana. Kāhore anō te whānau kia whakawhanake i tā rātou mahere rautaki me te mahere ā-tau hoki.

Te arotake whaiaro. Ka whakamahia te arotake whaiaro hei tātari i ngā wāhanga ki te whakapai ake i te kōhanga reo. Ka whiwhi, ka whakatutuki hoki te whānau i ngā pūrongo e whakapuaki ana i ia te wā ki ngā hui o ia marama. Ka whai hua te arotake whaiaro i ia te wā, ā, he painga mō ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi te whānau i te tukanga o te arotake whaiaro hei ārahi i a rātou i te wā ka pupū ake ngā tūmomo huihuinga me ngā tūmomo take.

Te whakamahere hōtaka. He matawhānui te whakamahere hōtaka, ā, he pai te whakarite i te ako a ngā tamariki. He mārama te kiko o te hōtaka. Kua tāutuhia ngā rauemi e hiahiatia ana. Kua whakaritea hoki ngā rautaki hei whakawhanake i ngā mōhiotanga, ngā pūkenga hoki o ngā tamariki.

Te aromatawai. Ka whakaemihia ngā mahi a ia tamaiti ki tāna ake pūkete. Ko te kiko ka whakaemihia e āta tāutu ana i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i runga anō i tēnā e mōhio ana rātou. Ka whai hua ngā kōrerorero e pā ana ki ngā whakatutukitanga, i runga anō i ngā whāinga ā-kaupapa, hei hāpai i ngā mātua ki te kite i te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki.

Te wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Te aromātai. Kāhore he kitenga i tuhia ai, e tohu ana i te aromātai hōtaka. Me whakarite ngā kaimahi i tētahi tukanga ki te aromātai i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga, tae atu ki ngā mahinga, te whakaritenga, me ā rātou ake mahi. Ka hāpai tēnei i te whakapai tonutanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Te mahere rautaki, te mahere ā-tau. Ahakoa ka whakamōhio, ka ārahi hoki Te Ara Tūāpae i te kōhanga reo, me tuhi te whānau i ngā mahi e tika ana mō ia whāinga, tae atu ki tēnā ka kawe i ngā mahi, te āhua o ngā mahi matua, me te wā e tika ana ki te kawe i aua mahi matua i ia tau. Ko te aroturuki me te pūrongo a te whānau i te ahunga whakamua me ngā whakatutukitanga i runga i ngā kaupapa matua ā-tau, ka whakarato i te ahunga whakamua mō meāke nei.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki: 

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa. 

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki: 

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia:

  • whakaoti te whānau i tā rātou mahere rautaki me tā rātou mahere ā-tau

  • rapu tautoko te whānau ki te whakarite i tētahi tukanga ka aromātai i te whai huatanga o ā rātou mahi ki te hōtaka akoranga.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu mō ngā Ratonga Arotake Māori (Te Uepū ā-Motu)

19 Pipiri 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Heretaunga is located in Camberley, Hastings. Children from the local area attend the kōhanga reo.

The whānau agreed that mana reo would be the focus for this review. The whānau consistently maintain a high Māori immersion environment for children. Kuia and staff are competent speakers of te reo Māori. Children hear good models of te reo Māori spoken.

The learning programme is planned and organised well. There is a balance between more formal learning and opportunities for children to explore and follow their own interests. Children are familiar with their environment and have a strong sense of belonging.

Whānau are efficient and effectively manage the kōhanga reo operations well. Regular reporting keeps the whānau informed and there is collective decision making. Whānau use good employer practices. Self review is used to determine areas for improvement in the kōhanga reo. Whānau receive and act on reports that are shared regularly at monthly hui. Regular self review is effective and benefits children.

Programme planning is comprehensive and well organised. Assessment practices are effective. However, programme evaluation is an area for improvement. The whānau also need to complete their strategic and annual plans.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Reo

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Mana Reo

Background

How well are children supported to learn te reo Māori?

Children are well supported to learn and use te reo Māori.

Areas of good performance

Mana Reo. Children are immersed in te reo Māori. The whānau consistently maintain a Māori immersion environment. Kuia and staff are competent speakers of te reo Māori and they interact with children in te reo Māori throughout the day. Staff use a range of strategies to promote te reo Māori. These include books, pictures, labels and purposeful interaction. Children are interested and motivated by the language learning environment. Children are developing competency in te reo Māori.

Children hear good models of te reo Māori spoken. The kuia models high levels of te reo Māori. Children confidently engage in karakia, hīmene, waiata, greetings, pepeha and basic whakapapa. These activities underpin mana tangata and mana whenua. Children are learning to use correct structures when responding to questions. Children are becoming confident users of te reo Māori.

The learning programme. The children’s learning programme is well planned and organised. There is balance between formal learning and opportunities for children to explore and follow their own interests. Children are familiar with their environment and have a strong sense of belonging. Kuia presence affects and influences children’s conduct in a positive way. Tuakana teina practices are highly visible. Children take advantage of activities they enjoy and remain engaged for a long period of time. The younger children enjoy planned activities that cater well for their development. Children are stimulated and happy.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How well does the whānau manage their kōhanga reo in the interests of their children?

The whānau manage their kōhanga reo well in the interests of their children.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management. Whānau are efficient and effectively manage the kōhanga reo operations. There is good attendance at whānau hui. Regular reporting keeps the whānau informed and decision making is shared. They have important health and safety policies and procedures in place to guide practice. Financial reporting is clear, transactions are transparent. Whānau provide good support for their kōhanga reo.

Personnel. Whānau reflect good employer practice. They have systems to support staff in their work. Personnel documentation is in place. Appraisals guide staff performance and development. Staff performance impacts positively on children’s learning.

Te Ara Tūāpae. Whānau members understand their current priorities and those of the future. Te Ara Tūāpae has informed strategic objectives and actions. Whānau are yet to develop their strategic and annual plan.

Self review. Self review is used to determine areas for improvement in the kōhanga reo. Whānau receive and act on reports that are shared regularly at monthly hui. Regular self review is effective and benefits children. Whānau use a process of self review to guide them when unplanned events and issues arise.

Programme planning. Programme planning is comprehensive and well organised for children’s learning. Programme content is clear. Resource requirements are identified. Strategies are in place to develop children’s knowledge and skills.

Assessment. Children’s work is collated in individual profile books. The material collated clearly identifies children’s learning in terms of what they know. Useful comments about achievement against kaupapa goals help parents to see children’s progress.

Area for development

Evaluation. There is no documented evidence of programme evaluation. Staff need to establish a process to evaluate the effectiveness of the learning programme including routines, organisation and their own practices. This will assist with ongoing improvements that benefit children.

Strategic and annual plan. While Te Ara Tūāpae informs and guides the kōhanga reo, the whānau need to document the actions required for each objective including who, what and when key tasks are to be undertaken each year. Whānau monitoring and reporting of progress and successes against annual priorities need to provide direction for the future.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Licensed Kōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake. 

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends:

  • the whānau complete their strategic and annual plans
  • seek support to establish a process to evaluate the effectiveness of their learning programme practices.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
