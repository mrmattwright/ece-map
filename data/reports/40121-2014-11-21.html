1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongopai ki te pūtake o te maunga o Ngongotahā, ki Rotorua, ā, nō te tau 1985 i whakatūngia ai e ētahi tino kuia, nā runga i te karanga o te Hāhi Perehipitiriana Māori. Kei te whakanui tonu te whānau i te nui o tana whai wāhi mai, i a rātou e whakapakari tonu ana i ngā tūāpapa i whakatakotohia ai e ia mō ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongopai.

Nō Tūhoe te nuinga o ngā kaimahi, ā, ko te reo Māori te reo taketake o te kaiako. He hōhonu te māramatanga o ngā kaimahi katoa ki ngā tikanga Māori, ā, he nui hoki tō rātou āheinga ki te whakawhiti kōrero me te hāpai i ngā akoranga reo Māori a ngā tamariki. I whakaritea e ngā kaimahi me te whānau ko Mana Atua te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ka hāngai te Mana Atua ki ngā aria o te waiora me te aronga toi whenuatanga. Ko tō te whānau hiahia i roto i te arotake, ko te mōhio ki tēnei pātai, arā:

‘He pēhea te whai huatanga o ngā tamariki ki te whakaatu i tō rātou waiora, me te pakari anō hoki o tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga me tō rātou tuakiri hoki?’

He tōtika te whakahaere me te whakarite a ngā kaimahi i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakaatu ngā tamariki i te hōhonutanga o tō rātou māramatanga ki ngā kōrero tuku iho o Te Arawa e whakapapa ana ki a rātou. He aronga nui tā te hōtaka akoranga ki ngā atua Māori. Ko ngā hononga ā-wairua i waenga i ngā tamariki me te ao tūturu kua whakarewa ake i runga anō i te whai whakaaro. Ka whai hua ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā tamariki, ō rātou hoa, me ngā pākeke, ā, e ahu mai ana i te whanaungatanga. E rongo ana ngā tamariki i tētahi taiao e hāpai ana i tā rātou ako i ngā wā katoa, ā, he haumaru, he tau hoki tō rātou noho.

He mātau katoa ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahia te reo Māori hei wero, hei whakawhānui ake i ngā whakaaro o ngā tamariki. He māia ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. He ngākau whakapuke, he kaimātauranga hihiri hoki ngā kaimahi. Ka whakatūria e ngā tamariki tō rātou ngākau nui me te hiahia ki te ako. He ākonga māia,, he mātau hoki, ā, he mauri tau ki roto i te taiao o te kōhanga reo.

Ka tino whai hua te whānau i roto i tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki ngā mahi hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Ka kitea tēnei āhuatanga i roto i ngā tino mahi whakahaere me ngā pūnaha whakariterite. Ko ngā tukanga arotake whaiaro puta noa i te kōhanga reo e whai pānga ana ki te whakapai tonutanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Atua te kaupapa arotahi.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Atua

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whai huatanga o ngā tamariki ki te whakaatu i tō rātou waiora, me te pakari anō o tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga me tō rātou tuakiri hoki?

He whakaritenga taumata pai e whakaatu ana e ngā tamariki, me te pakari anō hoki o tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga me tō rātou tuakiri hoki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka āta poipoia te tino aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā kōrero tuku iho o Te Arawa e akona e rātou. Ka whakatinanahi ngā kōrero tawhito e ngā kaimahi i roto anō i ngā tāruatanga. Ka taea e ngā tamariki te iho o ngā kōrero te whakahāngai anō hoki ki tō rātou ake ao.

He kaha anō hoki ngā hononga ā-wairua o ngā tamariki ki te ao tuturu mā roto i ngā ngohe ako me te karakia . He aronga nui tā te hōtaka akoranga ki ngā atua Māori. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki ki te tuituia i ngā mōhiotanga Māori tuku iho ki ngā horopaki o tēnei ao hou.

Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te tino hononga o te aroha ki waenga i ngā pākeke me ō rātou hoa. He manaaki, he tūturu hoki ngā taunekeneketanga. Ka kitea te tino whakaaro nui o ngā kaimahi ki te waiora o ngā tamariki katoa. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i tētahi taiao e hāpai ana i tō rātou haumaru, tō rātou noho tau ki a rātou anō hei ākonga.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki tētahi taiao reo e whakawhānui ana i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga mā ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, mā te waitohu, me ngā pātai whānui. He kaha ngā kaimahi katoa ki te whakawhiti whakaaro me te hāpai i ngā akoranga o ngā tamariki i roto i te reo Māori. Ka tino whakamahia te reo Māori hei wero, hei whakawhānui hoki i ngā whakaaro o ngā tamariki. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

He hihiri ngā tamariki ki te ako. He ngākau whakapuke, he kaimātauranga hihiri hoki ngā kaimahi. Ka wero, ka whakawhānui hoki rātou i ngā whakaaro o ngā tamariki. Ka whakarere kētia ngā mahi e ngā kaimahi kia aro atu ngā matea ako o tēnā, o tēnā o ngā tamariki. He ākonga māia, he ākonga mātau hoki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka tino whai hua te arotake whaiaro, ā, nā aua mahi ka haere tonu ngā whakapaitanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. He mārama, i āta kitea ngā pūnaha pūtea. He pakari ngā pūnaha e pā ana ki te whakahaere i ngā kaimahi. Ka tāutu te arohaehae a ngā kaimahi i tēnā e tika ana kia ū tonu ai te kounga o ngā mahi whakaako. Ko ngā mahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai e āta hāngai ana ki te mātauranga Māori, te aroturuki i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me tā rātou ahunga whakamua. Whakaaro huritao ai ngā kaimahi mō ā rātou mahi, ā, ka whai hoki i ngā huarahi ka hāpai ake anō i a rātou.

Kei te tūtohinga te māramatanga o te tirohanga, ngā mātāpono, me ngā whāinga. E whakarato ana te tūtohinga i ngā aratohu mārama ki te whānau mō ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga mahi. Ka tāutu ngā mahere rautaki i ngā kaupapa matua mō te whakawhanake tonutanga o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei. E arahina ana ngā mahi e ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru.

He pūnaha whakahaere papai tā te whānau, he kaha hoki te ārahitanga, hei hāpai i ngā painga mō ngā tamariki. He nui ngā pūkenga, ngā mōhiotanga hoki o te kaiwhakahaere, ā, noho pūmau, ka whakawhirinaki hoki te whānau ki a ia. Ka āta whakaritea ia i te ū tonutanga ki ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo. He komiti pai e āta tirohia ana ngā whakahaeretanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. He hihiri te whānau, ā, ka noho pūmau, ka noho ngākau nui hoki rātou mō te kōhanga reo me te mātauranga o ā rātou tamariki. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo komiti e kawe ana i ngā mahi ki te aroturuki, ā, ka pūrongo hoki ki te whānau mō ngā āhuatanga katoa e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te pakari, nā te pūmau hoki o ngā pūnaha whakahaere i whakaritea ai.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Whakaritenga whaimuri

He kaha te whānau ki te kōkiri whakamua i ā rātou anō, ā, mā rātou anō e whakatau ngā whakaritenga ka whai ake.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e whā.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

21 Whiringa-ā-Rangi 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongopai sits at the base of the Ngongotaha Maunga in Rotorua and was established in 1985 by a well respected kuia following a request from the Māori Presbyterian church. The whānau continue to recognise her contribution as they continue to strengthen the foundations she laid for the children of Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongopai.

The kaimahi are predominantly of Tūhoe and the kaiako is a native speaker of te reo Māori. All kaimahi have a deep understanding of tikanga Māori, a strong ability to communicate and support children’s learning of te reo Māori. The kaimahi and whānau chose mana atua as the focus for the review. Mana atua embraces the concepts of wellbeing and belonging. During the review the whānau wanted to know:

“How effectively do children show that they are happy and confident with a strong sense of identity?”

Children are confident, competent learners and are happy in the kōhanga reo environment. They are effectively supported to achieve success and they are excited about learning. Children are confident speakers of te reo Māori. They demonstrate high levels of wellbeing and a strong sense of belonging and identity. Relationships between children, their peers and adults are positive and based on whanaungatanga.

Whānau are highly effective in their contribution to positive outcomes for children. Self review processes throughout the kōhanga reo result in ongoing improvements that benefit children. Children experience an environment where they are constantly learning, they feel safe and secure.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose mana atua as its focus area.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Mana Atua

Background

How effectively do children show that they are happy and confident with a strong sense of identity?

Children are happy and confident learners with a strong sense of belonging and identity.

Areas of good performance

Children’s strong sense of belonging is effectively nurtured in the kōhanga reo. They learn about the traditional stories of Te Arawa. Kaimahi bring these stories to life. Children can apply the messages from the stories to their own lives.

Children enjoy a language environment where their interests are extended through conversations, the use of predictions and open ended questions. All kaimahi are strong communicators and support children’s learning of te reo Māori. Te reo Māori is successfully used to challenge and extend children’s thinking. Children are confident speakers of te reo Māori.

Children’s spiritual connections to the natural world are strong. The learning programme has a strong emphasis on ngā atua Māori. Kaupapa learning activities and karakia allow children to successfully integrate knowledge from Māori traditions into contemporary contexts.

Children experience strong, loving relationships with adults and their peers. The interactions are respectful and genuine. Kaimahi show a deep concern for the wellbeing of all children. Children experience an environment where they feel safe and secure in who they are as learners.

Children are excited about learning. Kaimahi are enthusiastic and motivated educators who challenge and extend children’s thinking. Kaimahi respect children’s mana and they recognise and respond to their differences. Kaimahi practices are highly effective in developing children’s knowledge and understanding of te ao Māori and the wider world. Te reo and tikanga Māori are deeply embedded in the programme. Children are confident and competent learners.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

Self review is highly effective and results in ongoing improvements that benefit children. Financial systems are clear and transparent. Personnel management systems are robust. Kaimahi appraisals identify what is required to ensure the quality of teaching for children is maintained. Planning, assessment and evaluation clearly focuses on mātauranga Māori, monitoring children’s learning and their progress. Kaimahi regularly reflect on their practice and seek ways to improve.

The charter provides a clear vision, principles and goals. It provides the whānau with clear guidelines about their roles and responsibilities. Strategic plans identify priorities for the future development of the kōhanga reo. Health and safety policies and procedures guide practice.

Whānau have effective operational systems and strong leadership in place to support positive outcomes for children. The kaiwhakahaere is skilled and knowledgeable and has the respect and confidence of whānau. She ensures that the kōhanga reo systems are maintained to a high standard. There is an effective committee who oversee the operations of the kōhanga reo. Whānau are motivated, committed and passionate about the kōhanga reo and the education of their children. They participate in various committees who take responsibility for monitoring and reporting back to the whānau on all aspects of kōhanga reo operations. Children benefit from the strong and sustainable management systems that are in place.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO LicensedKōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Key Next Steps

The whānau are forward thinkers and they will determine their next steps.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in four years.

Lynda Pura Watson National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)
