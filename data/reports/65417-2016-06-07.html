Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Kōpūāwai ki te marae o Whakatū, ki te tāone o Whakatū. He ākonga pakari ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo, ki tō rātou marae hoki. Ka whakamanahia hoki ngā tamariki, me tō rātou tūnga hei tangata whenua. E noho pūmau ana te whānau ki te mātauranga o ā rātou tamariki me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. He hiahia tō te whānau ki te mōhio ki te whai huatanga o ngā mahi a te kōhanga reo ki te poipoi i te tuakiri o ā rātou tamariki hei Māori.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Kōpūāwai i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Kōpūāwai ki te marae o Whakatū, ki te tāone o Whakatū. E rua ngā whare, ko Rangiruhia rāua ko Rangianewa, ā, koia nei ngā ingoa o ngā wahine i whakatū ai i te kōhanga reo nei. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki me te whānau nā te tautoko me ārahitanga o ngā kaumātua. E noho pūmau ana te whānau ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo me te mātauranga o ā rātou tamariki. He pārekareka ki ngā kaimahi te mahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te tūmāia o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou wāhi akoranga?

He ākonga pakari ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wāhi ako.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E poipoia ana te whakaaro pohewa, te auaha hoki o ngā tamariki. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā taumahi ki ngā tamariki hei whakatutuki i ngā matea ake me ngā ngākau nuitanga ake o ia tamaiti. Ka aroturukitia ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki, ā, nā runga i aua āhuatanga ka whakarerekēhia te hōtaka. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whakatau i ā rātou whakaritenga ako. Nā ngā rauemi ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i ō rātou taiao māoriori, tūturu anō hoki. He tau ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

He ākonga pakari ngā tamariki. Ka hāpai ngā taumahi i ngā tamariki ki te whakahono i te ako pūtaiao kia hono anō hoki ki ngā tino atua Māori. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te ako mā te whakahura me te tūhura. Nā ngā hīkoi ki ngā pouwhenua matua o te hapori, ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā kōrero me ngā taonga tuku iho. Ka tautoko ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te taunekeneke me te whakapuaki i ā rātou akoranga ki te taha o ētahi atu. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo āhuatanga ki te hōtaka akoranga.

Ko te ako a ngā tamariki ki runga i te marae e whai pānga matua ana ki te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā kaupapa i te marae. E ako ana ngā tamariki i tō rātou tūnga hei tangata whenua. Ka whakatairangatia te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou tuakiri.

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki ki te tū tangata whakarawe. Ka mahi ngātahi ngā kaumātua me ngā kaimahi, hei akiaki i te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā mahi māra kai. Ka whai wāhi ai ngā tamariki ki ngā mahi whakatupu, te hauhake, te tunu, ā, ka whakapounamu hoki rātou i ngā huarākau me ngā huawhenua, mā te tautoko mai o ngā kaumātua me te whānau. Ko te wā hauhake te wā ka whakanui ngā tamariki i ā rātou mahi ki te taha o ngā kaumātua ki te marae. E whakawhanake ana te manaakitanga o ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Kua āta tuhia ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakamahere, te aromatawai me te aromātai. Ka whai kiko ngā mahere, ā, he tino aratohu anō hoki ēnei mā ngā kaimahi, mō te whakapuakitanga o ngā hōtaka akoranga. E rua ngā whare, tētahi mō ngā tuākana, tētahi hoki mō ngā tēina, ā, nā runga i taua āhuatanga, he rerekē anō hoki ngā mahere hei āta whakatutuki i ngā matea o ia reanga. Aromātai ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, i te whai huatanga o ā rātou whakaritenga, ā, ka whakahāngai ake i te hōtaka hei whakatutuki i ngā hiahia whakatupu a ngā tamariki.

E aroturukitia ana te ako a ngā tamariki. Ka whakaemi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo mōhiohio e pā ana ki ngā akoranga a ia tamaiti, ki te whānuitanga tonu o ngā tūmomo horopaki. Whakaemihia ai ngā mōhiohio i ia te wā, tae atu ki te whakahoutanga ake o ngā pūkete e pā ana ki ngā tamariki, te whakatakotoranga haere o ngā akoranga a ia tamaiti, me te aromātai ā-wiki. Pūrongo ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā i ngā taumahi me ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki ki ngā mātua i ngā hui ā-whānau. Ka noho matua te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki i roto i ngā akoranga.

E atawhaitia ana ngā tamariki i tētahi taiao e noho haumaru ana, e āta tiakina ana, ā, kua taiapatia anō hoki. E whakarato ana te tūtohinga i te aronga me te māramatanga o ngā tino tūmanako kia noho haumaru ai ngā tamariki me te whānau. Arotake ai te whānau i ngā kaupapa here i ia marama, ā, ka whakahou ake i ngā kaupapa here e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. E whai hua ana ngā mahi a te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. Ka noho matua te haumaru me te waiora o ngā tamariki.

Ka arotahi ngā mahere rautaki ki te rōnakitanga o te kōhanga reo hei te paetawhiti. E whakahaerehia ana tētahi komiti whakahaere, ā, ko te tikanga ka whai wāhi atu ngā whānau katoa ki ngā ohu mahi. Ka ngākau nui te whānau ki ngā ohu mahi, ngā kaupapa kohi pūtea, me ngā haerenga. He tau, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kei te whakawhanakehia ngā mahi aromatawai e pā ana ki ngā tamariki kei raro i te rua o ngā tau. Ka whai pānga nui te whakawhanaketanga ngaio ki te whakapakaritanga ake o ngā pūmanawa o ngā kaimahi ki te kōkiri i ngā aromatawai e whai pānga ana ki ngā matea ako, ngā matea whakawhanaketanga anō hoki o ngā pēpi.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei:

  • Kia whakarato ngā kaimahi i tētahi whakatakotoranga aromatawai e hāngai ana ki te tupu, te whanaketanga hoki i te katoa o te tamaiti kōhungahunga.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

Ka whakamanahia hoki ngā tamariki, me tō rātou tūnga hei tangata whenua. E noho pūmau ana te whānau ki te mātauranga o ā rātou tamariki me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. He hiahia tō te whānau ki te mōhio ki te whai huatanga o ngā mahi a te kōhanga reo ki te poipoi i te tuakiri o ā rātou tamariki hei Māori.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kōpūāwai is situated on the Whakatū marae in Nelson. Children are confident learners in their kōhanga reo environment and on the marae. Children’s mana also is enhanced and their roles as tangata whenua affirmed. The whānau are committed to their children’s education and the kaupapa of the kōhanga reo. The whānau were interested in knowing just how well their children’s identity as Māori is nurtured through the kōhanga reo.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Kōpūāwai will be in three years. 

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kōpūāwai is based on Whakatū Marae, Nelson. There are two buildings, Rangiruhia and Rangianewa, named after women who established the kōhanga reo. Children and whānau benefit from the support and guidance provided by kaumātua. Whānau are committed to the kaupapa of the kōhanga reo and education of their children. Kaimahi enjoy working alongside children.

2 The Review Findings

How confident are children in their learning environments?

Children are confident learners in a range of environments.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children’s imagination and creativity is fostered. Kaimahi provide children with activities to respond to individual needs and interests. Children’s interests are monitored and programme changes made accordingly. Kaimahi encourage children to make choices in their learning. Resources provide children with opportunities to explore their natural and physical environments. Children are comfortable in their kōhanga reo environment.

Children are confident learners. Activities support children to link science learning to relevant atua Māori. Kaimahi encourage children to learn through discovery and exploration. Walks to local landmarks allows children to learn their history and cultural heritage. Kaimahi support children to interact and share their learning with others. Children benefit from a varied learning programme.

Children’s learning on the marae is an integral part of the daily programme. Kaimahi encourage children to participate in marae activities. Children are learning about their role as tangata whenua. Children’s knowledge about their identity is enhanced.

Children are developing skills to be self-sufficient. Kaumātua and kaimahi work together to encourage children to participate in māra kai activities. Children participate in growing, harvesting, cooking and preserving fruits and vegetables with the support of kaumātua and whānau. Harvesting is a time for children to celebrate their efforts with kaumātua on the marae. Children are developing a sense of manaakitanga.

Planning and Evaluation

Planning, assessment and evaluation processes are well documented. The planning is detailed and provides kaimahi with clear guidelines for the delivery of learning programmes. Because there are two whare, one for tuakana, and one for teina, there are two different plans which contribute to meeting the needs of each group. Kaimahi regularly evaluate the effectiveness of their practice and modify programmes to cater to children’s growing needs.

Children’s learning is monitored. Kaimahi gather an extensive range of information about individuals learning in a range of contexts. Information is regularly collected including updating children’s profiles, recording individuals learning and weekly evaluation. Kaimahi regularly report children’s activities and learning to parents at hui whānau. Children progress in learning is important.

Children’s are cared for in a safe, secure, well maintained environment. The charter provides direction and clear expectations to keep children and whānau safe. Whānau regularly review policies each month and update polices around health and safety. Whānau manage kōhanga reo operations effectively. Children’s safety and wellbeing is a priority.

Strategic planning is focused on the long term sustainability of the kōhanga reo. A komiti whakahaere is operational and all whānau are expected to participate in ohu mahi. Working bees, fund raising ventures and excursions are always popular with the whanau. Children’s are settled and happy at the kōhanga reo.

Key Next Steps

Assessment practices for children under two is developing. Professional development is vital to build kaimahi capability to manage assessment that is responsive to the learning and developmental needs of babies.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • That kaimahi provide a model of assessment that reflects the holistic nature and development of babies.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kōpūāwai is situated on the Whakatū marae in Nelson. Children are confident learners in their kōhanga reo environment and on the marae. Children’s mana also is enhanced and their roles as tangata whenua affirmed. The whānau are committed to their children’s education and the kaupapa of the kōhanga reo. The whānau were interested in knowing just how well their children’s identity as Māori is nurtured through the kōhanga reo.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Kōpūāwai will be in three years.
