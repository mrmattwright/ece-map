Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Taneatua ki te tāone o Tāneatua. Kāhore te whānau e whakatairanga ana i te kounga o ngā putanga ako mō ngā tamariki, kāhore hoki rātou e whakapūmau ana i ngā mātāpono o Te Korowai. Kāhore ngā tamariki e tino tautokona ana kia ako ai rātou i te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. He kaumingomingo ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Taneatua i roto i te kotahi tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Taneatua, ki te tāone o Tāneatua. Nō tēnei hapori tonu te whānau katoa, ā, nō Tūhoe hoki te nuinga o rātou. E hāpaitia ana te kōhanga reo e ngā kuia me ngā kaumātua. E ai ki te whānau, he hiahia tō rātou kia tū pakari ā rātou tamariki i roto i tō rātou reo me ngā kawa me ngā tikanga kua tuku iho mai.

Nā te nui o ngā tino take āwangawanga i roto i te whakahaeretanga, te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, i whakaaetia e Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo me te whānau o te kōhanga reo kia whakahāngai Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te aronga o tēnei arotake mātauranga ki Te Korowai.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te whānau e whakatinana ana i ngā mātāpono o Te Korowai, ā, e whakatairanga ana hoki i te kounga o ngā putanga mō ngā tamariki?

Kāhore te whānau e whakatairanga ana i te kounga o ngā putanga ako mō ngā tamariki, kāhore hoki rātou e whakatinana ana i ngā mātāpono o Te Korowai i ngā wā katoa.

Te whakahaeretanga me te kawenga takohanga a te whānau

He ngoikore te whakahaeretanga me te kawenga takohanga a te whānau.

Kāhore i te kaha te ngākau whakapuke o te whānau, ā, e whai pānga ana tēnei ki ngā whakapae o te hīanga, te matakana, me te whakahēnga ki waenga i te whānau. Kua tino heke iho te taetae atu ki ngā hui ā-whānau, ā, kei te whakarite tūpono noa i ngā whakataunga. I roto i te roanga o ngā tau e rua kua pahure ake nei, he iti noa te aronga ki ngā putanga mō ngā tamariki.

Kāhore he aronga, he ārahitanga hoki ā te whānau. Kāhore te tukanga whakauruuru mā ngā whānau hou kia whakatinanahia, ā, he iti noa tō rātou māramatanga ki ā rātou kawenga i whakatakotohia ai ki Te Korowai. Kāhore ngā tūranga o ngā kaimahi me ā rātou kawenga mahi e hāngai ana ki ā rātou tuhinga tūranga mahi, ki ā rātou kirimana rānei.

Kāhore te whakamahere rautaki me te whakamahere ā-tau e whai hua ana. Kāhore he mahere mō te kōhanga reo mō meāke nei. Ko te kaha kanukanu o ngā rawa, me te whakamahinga anō hoki o tētahi wāhanga o ngā papa nei kāhore e whai raihana ana ētahi take āwangawanga. Kāhore ngā tamariki e noho ana ki te pūtake o ngā whakataunga ki te kōhanga reo.

Kāhore i te rawaka ngā mahi ki te whakahaere i ngā kaimahi. Kāhore te pūkete a ia kaimahi e tino tiakina ana, e whakaritea ana hoki, ā, kāhore hoki e hāngai ana ki te wā. Kāhore anō te arowhai a ngā pirihimana i ngā kaimahi kia whakatutukihia, ā, kāhore anō ngā kaimahi katoa kia whiwhi i ngā kirimana mahi i waitohungia ia. Neke atu i te kotahi tau, ā, kāhore anō ngā arohaehae kia whakatutukingia. Kāhore e whakaarohia ana ngā whai wāhitanga ki te ako ngaio me te whakawhanake ngaio. Kāhore he hononga mahi ki waenga i ngā kaimahi. Kāhore ngā tamariki e whiwhi ana i ngā whai wāhitanga ako o te kounga pai.

Te reo me ōna tikanga

Kāhore te ako a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori me ōna tikanga e tino hāpaitia ana. Kāhore i te wātea ki ngā tamariki ngā rauemi, ngā taputapu, me ngā matū ka hāpai i ā rātou akoranga. Ko te iti noa o ngā rerekētanga, ngā wero, me ngā āhuatanga whakakipakipa e whakawhāiti ana i te ako a ngā tamariki. Kei te pā tōraro ki te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga.

Whaioranga

Kāhore e taea te waiora o ngā tamariki te āta whakatau.

Kāhore e taea e te whānau te noho tau ki te whakaaro kei te manaakitia ngā tamariki ki tētahi wāhi haumaru. Kāhore ngā tamariki e mātakina ana ki waho, ā, e noho wātea ana ki a rātou te maha o ngā āhuatanga mōrearea ki taua wāhi. Nō nā tata tonu nei i whakatika ai te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki, me te kore hiko. Kāhore he pūnaha ki te arowhai i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea. Kāhore i te mā, kāhore hoki i te pai te tiaki me te āhua o ngā whare paku me ngā wāhi horoi. He taumaha ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā mema o te whānau. Kei te noho mōrea te haumaru, te atawhai, me te āhuru o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. I te wā o te arotake, i tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga kāhore e whakatutuki ana i ngā herenga ā-ture. Me tahuri te whānau ki te:

3.1 whakarite i te whakamahere, te whakatinana, me te aromātai a ngā kaimahi i te marautanga i whakatakotohia ai mō ngā kōhanga reo, ā, i waihangatia ai hoki hei whakatairanga i te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga, mā te whakarato i ngā wheako akoranga e poipoi ana i ngā ngākau nuitanga ako, ngā pūmanawa, me ngā āheinga hoki o ngā tamariki; me te whakarato i tētahi taiao ako mō aua tamariki[R 43 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; C1-13 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

3.2 whakapau kaha ki te whakatairanga i te hauora pai me te haumaru pai o ngā tamariki i whakauruhia ai ki te ratonga, me te whakapau anō hoki ki te whakakore i ngā maiki, ā, me whakatika, me tiaki hoki i ia te wā i ngā papa, ngā rawa me ngā taputapu, hei whakakore atu i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea[R 46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS1-33 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

3.3 whai whakaaro ki te tokomaha me te āhua anō hoki o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo, me te āta whakarite i te whare me ngā rawa o te kōhanga reo kia rawaka ai, kia tika ai anō hoki te wāhi mō te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi, me te whakataka kai, te kai, te moe, te putu, te haere ki te whare paku me te horoi, ā, me tika anō hoki te whakamahana, te kā o ngā raiti, te hoihoi, te hā ora, me ngā taputapu ka hāpai i te whakatinanatanga tika o te marautanga me ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te haumaru me te hauora[R 45 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; PF 1-38 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

3.4 whakarite i te whai huatanga o ngā mahi kāwana, mahi whakahaere hoki ki te kōhanga reo, i runga anō i te pai o ngā tikanga whakahaere, tae atu ki Te Korowai.[R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA 1-12 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā:

Kia kawe ake Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i te whakahaeretanga me te whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo, hei whakahou ake anō, hei whakapakari ake anō i te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, ā, kia mahi ngātahi hoki rātou ki te taha o te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā take katoa i whakatakotohia ai ki tēnei pūrongo a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Taneatua ki te tāone o Tāneatua. Kāhore te whānau e whakatairanga ana i te kounga o ngā putanga ako mō ngā tamariki, kāhore hoki rātou e whakapūmau ana i ngā mātāpono o Te Korowai. Kāhore ngā tamariki e tino tautokona ana kia ako ai rātou i te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. He kaumingomingo ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Taneatua is in Taneatua. Whānau do not promote quality learning outcomes for children or consistently adhere to the principles of Te Korowai. Children are not well supported to learn te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Management of the kōhanga reo is in disarray.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Taneatua will be within one year.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Taneatua is in Taneatua. All whānau are from the local community and most are of Tūhoe descent. The kōhanga reo is supported by kuia and kaumātua. Whānau members report that they want their children to be confident in their language, customs and local traditions.

Due to significant management and health and safety concerns to children, it was agreed by Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) and the whānau of the kōhanga reo that ERO focus this Education Review on Te Korowai.

2 The Review Findings

To what extent do whānau practice to the principles of Te Korowai and promote quality outcomes for children?

Whānau do not promote quality learning outcomes for children or practice the principles of Te Korowai consistently.

Whānau management and accountability

Whānau management and accountability is poor.

Whānau morale is low and symptomatic of allegations of misconduct, distrust and disagreement that permeates the whānau. Attendance at whānau hui has declined significantly and decisions are made in an ad hoc manner. Over the past two years there has been minimal focus on outcomes for children.

Whānau lack direction and leadership. Induction for new whānau has not been implemented and they have limited understanding of their responsibilities as prescribed in Te Korowai. Kaimahi roles and responsibilities do not align to their job descriptions or employment contracts.

Strategic and annual planning are ineffective. There is no kōhanga reo plan for the future. The extensive deterioration of the facilities and the use of unlicensed space on the property are cause for concern. Children are not at the heart of decision making at the kōhanga reo.

Personnel management is inadequate. Individual personnel files are poorly maintained, disorganised and outdated. Police vetting of kaimahi is incomplete and not all kaimahi have signed employment contracts. Appraisals have not occurred for over a year. Professional learning and development opportunities are not considered. Working relationships amongst kaimahi have broken down. Children do not receive quality learning opportunities.

Te reo me ona tikanga

Children are not well supported to learn te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Children do not have access to resources, equipment or materials to support their learning. The lack of variety, challenge and motivation limits children’s learning. Children are disadvantaged in their learning and development.

Whaioranga

Children’s wellbeing cannot be assured.

Whānau cannot be assured that children are cared for in a safe and secure environment. Children are left unsupervised while outside where they have easy access to a number of property hazards. Until recently, the kōhanga reo was operating without electricity while children were in attendance. There is no system for regularly checking for hazards. The overall cleanliness, maintenance and state of toilets and washing areas is poor. Relationships between whānau members are tense. There is a potential risk to children’s safety, care and protection while at kōhanga reo.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements. During the course of the review, ERO identified areas of non compliance. The whānau must:

3.1 ensure that kaimahi plan, implement, and evaluate the Te Kōhanga Reo prescribed curriculum that is designed to enhance children’s learning and development through the provision of learning experiences that responds to the learning interests, strengths, and capabilities of enrolled children; and provide a positive learning environment for those children[Regulations 43 (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; C1-13 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

3.2 ensure that all reasonable steps are taken to promote the good health and safety of children enrolled in the service; and take all reasonable precautions to prevent accidents and that the premises, facilities, and other equipment on those premises are kept in good repair, maintained regularly, used safely and kept free from hazards[Regulations 46 (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS 1-33 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

3.3 ensure that the kōhanga reo premises and facilities used, having regard to the number and age range of the children attending the premises, provide sufficient and suitable space for a range of activities, facilities for food preparation, eating, sleeping, storage, toileting, and washing, and sufficient and suitable heating, lighting, noise control, ventilation, and equipment to support appropriate curriculum implementation, and safe and healthy practices[Regulations 45 (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; PF 1-38 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

3.4 ensure that the kōhanga reo is effectively governed and managed in accordance with good management practices, including Te Korowai.[Regulations 47 (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA 1-12 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

TKRNT assume management and administration of the kōhanga reo for the purpose of working to restore and rebuild effective whānau management, including working alongside whānau to address all the matters raised in this ERO report.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Taneatua is in Taneatua. Whānau do not promote quality learning outcomes for children or consistently adhere to the principles of Te Korowai. Children are not well supported to learn te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Management of the kōhanga reo is in disarray.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Taneatua will be within one year.
