1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūnanga Te Wao ki te marae o Tangoio, e pā tata atu ana ki Ahuriri. Ko te nuinga o ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo, he uri nō Ngāti Kahungunu me te hapū o konei, arā, ko Marangatuhetaua. Ko Pūnanga Te Wao te ingoa o te wharenui me te kōhanga reo, ā, e hāngai ana tēnei ki tētahi putiputi o tēnei takiwā, me tōna whai pānga ki ngā whare wānanga o mua. E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki ngā wāhi māhorahora, huri noa i te marae.

He tokomaha ngā whānau o te kōhanga reo kei roto i te hāhi Rātana. Ka kitea aua whakaakoranga ki ngā whakaritenga i te kōhanga reo.

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i te tau 2012, i te maha o ngā whakaritenga papai, tae atu ki te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki, te whanaungatanga, te reo Māori, te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, me te whakawhanaketanga ngaio. I tāutuhia te arotake whaiaro hei wāhanga ki te whakapai ake.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori?

Ka āta whakamahi ngā tamariki tuākana i te reo Māori i a rātou e whai wāhi atu ana ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero māmā. Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tēina, tō rātou māramatanga haere ki te reo Māori, ā, kua waia rātou ki ngā tohutohu māmā.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He pai te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whakapūmautia e ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau tētahi taiao rumaki reo Māori, puta noa i te rā. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo ōkawa i a rātou e whakatau ana i ngā manuhiri ki runga i te marae. Ka ngākau nui te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki tuākana ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero māmā i roto i te reo Māori. He pārekareka ki a rātou te whakautu pātai me te kōrerorero mō tēnā e mahia ana e rātou. Kei te waia haere ngā tamariki tēina ki ngā oro me ngā tauira o te reo Māori, ā, e āhei ana rātou ki te aro tika atu ki ngā tūmomo tohutohu ka rangona i ia te wā. He tau ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao reo Māori.

Ka whakaongaongatia, ka whakahihiritia ngā tamariki ki te ako. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā taumahi whakahihiri e hāpai ana i ngā tamariki ki te kōkiri i ā rātou ake akoranga, te whakatau i ā rātou ake whakaritenga, me te whai i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga. He pai te waihanga, te whakatakotoranga anō hoki o te wāhi o waho mō ngā tamariki. Ka āta whakamahia, ka ngākau nuitia hoki e ngā tamariki te rua onepū, te neti poirewa, me te koura poitarawhiti. He harikoa ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo, ā, ka puāwai hoki rātou ki tētahi taiao e whakapuakihia ana ngā wheako akoranga i whakamaheretia ai me ērā i pupū ake ai mā te reo Māori.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i te ao Māori. Ka ako rātou i ngā tūmomo atua Māori, ā, e āhei ana rātou ki te taki i te maha o ngā tūmomo karakia. Nā ngā karakia o te ata ka tau mai te wairua o te rangimārie mō ngā āhuatanga o te rā. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā tūranga me ngā kawenga mahi a ngā atua Māori, me tō rātou whai pānga ki ā rātou mahi ā-kaupapa, puta noa i tō rātou rā. Ka whakaemi, ka whakamahi hoki rātou i ngā rauemi māori hei hāpai i ā rātou akoranga. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te noho ki waho, ā, ka rongo rātou i ngā huānga o ngā atua Māori.

He tau ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Kua whakaritea ngā mahinga, ā, ka tino whakahaerehia ēnei e ngā kaimahi me te whānau. He ngāwari te āta whakatutuki i ngā mahinga, ā, he tino wā ako, he tino wā whakahoahoa hoki ēnei mō ngā tamariki. Ka whakatauira ngā tamariki tuākana i ngā tikanga e tika ana mō ō rātou tēina, i a rātou e kai ana, e whakatā ana, ā, e horoi ana hoki i ō rātou ringaringa. Ka tiaki ngā tamariki i ō rātou ake matea ā-tinana.

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga atawhai, aroha hoki. Ka whakanui ngā kaimahi i ngā whakamātau me ngā whakatutukitanga o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki papai hei ārahi, hei whakahaere hoki i ngā tamariki, i a rātou e taunekeneke ana ki ētahi atu, ā, ka ako hoki ki te tohatoha me te tuku wā ki ētahi atu. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki i a rātou e tūhura ana, e hīraurau hopanga ana, ā, e tau haere ana ki te ao e noho nei rātou. He pakari te waiora o ngā tamariki.

He tau ngā tamariki ki te marae. Ka ako rātou i ngā huihuinga me ngā tikanga tuku iho i a rātou e whai wāhi atu ana ki ngā kaupapa i te marae. He tino puna mātauranga te marae mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka mahi ngātahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te waihanga i ngā mahere hōtaka. Ka whai hua aua mahere ki te āta ārahi i te whakapuakitanga o ngā kaimahi i ngā hōtaka akoranga whakahihiri, ngana hoki mā ngā tamariki. Ka hāngai ngā mahere ki ngā kaupeka o te tau, Te Whāriki, me ngā wāhi ako matua, tae atu ki te tīmatanga o te pāngarau me te pūtaiao. Ka whakaurua ngā kaupapa motuhake ki te hōtaka. Ka tūhonohono ngā mahere ā-tau, ā-marama, ā-wiki hoki, ā, ka āta tāutu i te aronga o ngā akoranga i roto i te roanga o ngā wā. Kei te whai wheako ngā tamariki i ngā hōtaka akoranga kua āta whakamaheretia, ā, kua āta whakaritea hoki.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki tētahi hōtaka e aro nui ana ki ō rātou matea. Arotake ai, whakaaro huritao ai hoki ngā kaimahi ki te kounga o ngā wheako akoranga ka whakaratohia e rātou mā ngā tamariki. Ka whakawhiti kōrero, ka whakatakoto hoki rātou i ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki ngā āhuatanga e whai hua ana, me ngā āhuatanga hoki hei whakapai ake. I ngā wā katoa, kei te whakapai tonu ngā kaimahi i a rātou anō, ā, ka whakarato hoki rātou i ia te wā i ngā tino wheako akoranga mā ngā tamariki e tiakina ana e rātou.

He mārama te tirohanga me te mahere a te whānau mō ā rātou whāinga me ō rātou wawata mō meāke nei. Kua tāutuhia e rātou ngā mahi ka whakatutuki i ā rātou whāinga. He mārama te arotahi a te whānau ki te whakapakari i te āheinga o ngā kaimahi, me te whakatairanga ake tonu i te reo Māori o ngā kaimahi me te whānau. E noho pūmau ana te whānau ki te whakapai tonutanga i roto i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me tō rātou waiora.

Kei te tino mōhio ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki. Ka aru rātou i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i roto i te roanga o te wā, ā, ka whakamahi rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā rautaki aromatawai hei ārahi i ā rātou whakataunga. E āhei ana ngā mātua ki te kite i te ahunga whakamua me te whakawhanaketanga o ā rātou tamariki i roto i ngā pūkete, ā, he ātaahua te whakatakoto. Ka harikoa ngā tamariki i a rātou e pānui ana i ā rātou pūkete.

Ka whai hua te tukanga arohaehae a te whānau. He mārama ngā aratohu e pā ana ki te whakaaro huritao, te whakatakoto whāinga, me te whai wāhi atu ki te whakahaeretanga, ngā kōrero urupare hoki. He pai te noho mōhio o te whānau ki te āhua mahi o ngā kaimahi me ngā taumata tonu ka whāia tonuhia, hei whakapai ake i te whānuitanga o te āhua mahi. Ko te āta whakahaere i ngā arohaehae kaimahi ka whai pānga ki te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga.

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. Ka ako tonu rātou i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te kaituku mahi pai, me ngā huarahi ki te whakahaere i ā rātou pūtea. Kei te whakangungu te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā akoranga a Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, hei whakapai i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki ngā mahi whakahaere. Hui ai te whānau i ia te wā. He pai tā rātou mahi tahi me ngā kaimahi ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga papai mā ngā tamariki. Haere ai ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki ngā hui purapura kia noho mōhio ai rātou ki ngā kōkiritanga hou, ā, ki te whakapuaki tahi, te ako tahi hoki ki te taha o ētahi atu kōhanga reo o te rohe. Ka noho ngā tamariki ki te pūtake o ngā whakataunga a te whānau.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore ngā kaimahi e tuhi ana i ngā whāinga whakawhanaketanga e pā ana ki ia tamaiti. Ahakoa ka whakatakotohia ngā whāinga mā ētahi o ngā tamariki, kāhore i te riterite tēnei mahi.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūnanga Te Wao ki te marae o Tangoio, e pā tata atu ana ki Ahuriri. He pai te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki tuākana i te reo Māori. Kei te whanake haere te māramatanga o ngā tamariki tēina ki te reo Māori. Ka whakahaere pai te whānau i te nuinga o ngā āhuatanga ki te kōhanga reo.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūnanga Te Wao i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

Lynda Pura-Watson

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūnanga Te Wao is based at Tangoio Marae near Napier. Most children who attend the kōhanga reo are descendants of Ngāti Kahungunu and the local hapū of Marangatuhetaua. Pūnanga Te Wao is the name of the wharenui and kōhanga reo and refers to a local flower that was associated with the traditional whare wānanga of learning. Children have access to wide open spaces around the marae.

Many of the whānau members of the kōhanga reo are part of the Rātana faith. These teachings are evident in kōhanga reo practices.

The previous ERO report of 2012 highlighted several positive practices including the children’s learning programme, whanaungatanga, te reo Māori, kōhanga management and professional development. Self review was identified as an area that needed to be improved.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children use te reo Māori?

The older children use te reo Māori well when they participate in simple conversations. The younger children show they have a growing understanding of te reo Māori and are familiar with simple commands and instructions.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are developing well as speakers of te reo Māori. A te reo Māori immersion environment is maintained by staff and whānau members throughout the day. Children are exposed to formal language when welcoming visitors on the marae. The older children engage willingly in simple te reo Māori conversations. They enjoy responding to questions and talking about what they are doing. The younger children are becoming familiar with the sounds and patterns of te reo Māori and are able to respond appropriately to regular commands and instructions. Children are comfortable in a te reo Māori environment.

Children are stimulated and motivated to learn. The staff provides interesting activities that support children to learn independently, make decisions and to follow their interests. The outdoor area is well designed and presented for children. The sand pit, volley ball net and netball hoop are well used and enjoyed by the children. Children are happy at kōhanga reo and thrive in an environment where all planned and unplanned learning experiences are delivered through te reo Maori.

Children learn about te ao Māori. They learn about different atua Māori and are able to recite a range of karakia. The morning karakia routine sets a positive tone for the day. Children learn about the roles and responsibilities of the atua Māori in their daily kaupapa activities. They gather and use natural resources to support their learning. Children enjoy being outdoors where they are able to observe elements of atua Māori.

Children are settled at kōhanga reo. Routines are established and well managed by staff and whānau. Routines are unhurried, flexible and are valuable learning and social times for children. The older children model appropriate etiquette and behaviours for their younger peers when they eat, rest and wash their hands. Children look after their own physical needs.

Children experience warm and loving interactions and relationships. The staff acknowledges children’s efforts and achievements. They use positive strategies to guide and manage children as they interact with others and learn how to share and take turns. Staff support children as they explore, problem solve and make sense of their world. Children have a strong sense of wellbeing.

Children are comfortable in a marae setting. They learn about traditional ceremonies and protocols when they participate in marae events. The marae is a valuable context for children’s learning.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau and staff work collaboratively to design programme plans. These plans effectively guide staff to deliver interesting and engaging learning programmes for children. Plans reflect the seasons of the year, Te Whāriki and key learning areas including early maths and science. Special events are included in the programme. Annual, monthly and weekly plans are cohesive and clearly indentify the focus of learning over specific time periods. Children experience well planned and prepared learning programmes.

Children enjoy a programme responds to their needs. Staff regularly review and reflect on the quality of learning experiences they provide for children. They discuss and record information about what is going well and what needs improvement. Staff members are on a journey of continual improvement and regularly provide the best learning experiences they can for the children in their care.

The whānau have a clear vision and plan for their future goals and aspirations. They have identified actions to achieve their goals. The whānau has a clear focus on building staff capability and on the further enhancement of the reo Māori capacity of staff and whānau. The whānau is committed to ongoing improvements for children’s learning and wellbeing.

Staff know the children well. They track children’s learning over time and use a wide range of assessment strategies to guide their decisions. Parents are able to see the progress and development of their children in profile books which are presented in an attractive manner. Children gain pleasure when they look through their profile folders.

The whānau has an effective appraisal process. There are clear guidelines for self reflection, goal setting and management input and feedback. The whānau is well informed about staff performance and the next steps for improving overall performance. Carefully managed staff appraisals have a positive impact on children’s learning and development.

The whānau manages the kōhanga reo well. They continue to learn about being a good employer and how to manage their finances. Whānau and staff members are undergoing training through the Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust to improve their knowledge of management practices. The whānau meets regularly. They work well with staff to make good decisions for children. Staff and whānau attend purapura meetings to keep abreast of new initiatives and to share and learn from other kōhanga reo in the area. Children are central to whānau decision making.

Key Next Steps

Staff do not document individual development goals for all children. While goals are set for some children, this practice is not consistent.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūnanga Te Wao is at Tangoio Marae near Napier. The older children use te reo Māori well. The younger children are developing in their understanding of te reo Māori. The whānau manages most aspects of the kōhanga reo well.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūnanga Te Wao will be in three years.
