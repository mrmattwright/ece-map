2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Waimeha ki te hapori o Richmond, ki Whakatū. Tokorua ngā kaumātua i whakaingoa i te kōhanga reo i tōna whakatūnga. I tū te kōhanga reo ki ngā wāhi maha i te tīmatanga, tae noa ki te wā i tau ki tōna wāhi i nāianei, e pā tata tonu atu ki te kura tuatahi o Victory. Nō ngā iwi katoa ngā tamariki me te whānau o te kōhanga reo, ā, ka whai wāhi rātou katoa ki te kōhanga reo.

Mai i te arotake a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2009, kua pā ngā whakarerekētanga papai ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakatūngia tētahi tangata hei kaitautoko, hei kaiwhakahaere, ā, hei kaiārahi anō hoki i te marautanga. Ka tino hāpaitia ia e tētahi rōpū matua o ngā mema pūhou o te whānau e noho pūmau ana ki te kōhanga reo, ā, he maioha anō hoki ki tōna ārahitanga ngaio. Kua kōrerohia, kua whakatutukihia hoki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai ki te pūrongo o mua hei whakapai ake. Kua tino whakaritea, e tino whakahaerehia ana hoki ngā pūnaha me ngā mahi e pā ana ki te whakahaere tari.

He mārama te tirohanga o te whānau mō te atawhai, te waiora, me te mātauranga o ā rātou tamariki. E whakawhanake haere ana rātou i roto i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki te arotake whaiaro. Hui ai te whānau i ia te wā, ki te whakawhiti kōrero, te aroturuki, me te pūrongo hoki i ngā āhuatanga o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. E hiahia ana rātou ki ngā tino painga mō ā rātou tamariki. Ko ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā whakataunga katoa.

Ka kōkiri ngā kaimahi me te whānau i tā rātou aronga ki te ako ngaio, kia pai ake ai tō rātou māramatanga ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki. Ka whai atu, ka tautoko hoki i ngā kōkiritanga e pā ana ki te whakapuakitanga o ngā pūkenga me ngā mōhiotanga ki waenga i a rātou anō, hei whakapiki ake i te āheinga o te whānau, me te whai wāhi mai.

E mōhio ana te whānau ki te hua o ngā tino hononga, hei hāpai i ngā tamariki me te whānau. Kua āta whakaritea ngā hononga pakari ki ngā rōpū o te hapori, ngā ratonga o waho, me te purapura hoki, hei hāpai i te whānuitanga o ngā matea o ā rātou tamariki. Ko te pai o ngā hononga ki waenga i te hapori me te whānau o te kōhanga reo, ka whai pānga anō hoki ki te waiora o ngā tamariki.

Ka rongo i te mauritau ki te kōhanga reo, e pou herea ana e ngā hononga atawhai, manaaki e aro nui ana ki te tangata. Kua āta whakaritea ngā mahere mō ngā tamariki kei raro i te rua o ngā tau, ā, ka whai hua te whakahaeretanga o tēnei. Ko te mahi ka whai ake, ko te whakarite i ngā tūmanako kia ū ai ngā mahere me te aromatawai, puta noa i ngā pokapū akoranga katoa.

Ka ako, ka tākaro hoki ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao haumaru, hauora hoki e manaaki ana i a rātou.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Waimeha kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Waimeha.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Waimeha, ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ki tō rātou whakapono, kua tuituia, e tū honohono ana hoki Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira, puta noa i te hōtaka akoranga.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whai wāhi, te tautoko hoki o ngā kuia. Ko te whai wāhi mai, te tautoko hoki o ngā kuia, tētahi tino āhuatanga ki te kōhanga reo. He poutokomanawa rātou mō te whānau me te hapori. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki te:

  • whakapūmau i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga ki te kōhanga reo
  • whakapuaki i ngā hītori me ngā kōrero tuku iho e pā ana ki te rohe, ki ngā tamariki me te whānau
  • whakatauira i ngā uaratanga o te kōhanga reo, arā, te noho whakaute a tētahi ki tētahi, te ngākau māhaki, me te manaaki tangata
  • whakapakari, me te whakapūmau i te whanaungatanga
  • tino whakatauira i te tautoko mō ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki.

Ko te whai wāhi mai o ngā kuia ka whakatau i ngā tamariki, ā, ka āwhina i ngā kaimahi ki te whakatakoto i te noho ā-whānau hei tūāpapa mō te tupu me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki. Ka noho maioha te whānau ki tō rātou whai wāhi nui mai ki te kōhanga reo.

Te taiao akoranga. Ka whakarato te whānau i tētahi taiao atawhai, ātaahua hoki mō ngā tamariki, ā, ka hāpai tēnei i te tākaro kakama, te tākaro māhaki hoki o ngā tamariki. He pai te whakatakotoranga o roto, o waho hoki, ā, e whakawātea ana tēnei i ngā tamariki ki te tūhura haere, me te whakawhanake hei ākonga pākiki, takitahi hoki. Kua whakaritea ētahi tino wāhi akoranga e whakanui ana i ngā taumahi e hāngai pū ana ki te whakawero, me te whakahihiri i te whai wāhitanga a ngā tamariki.

He wāteatea, he mā, he haumaru, he hihiri hoki ngā wāhi akoranga ātaahua, kia hiahia ai ngā tamariki ki te tākaro me te ako. Ka āta mātakina ngā tamariki, ā, he pārekareka ki a rātou te ako me te tākaro ki te taiao mauritau nei.

Te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā hōtaka ngāwari e whakatutuki ana i ngā ngākau nuitanga, me ngā tūmomo whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki tēina. Ka poipoi, ka manaaki ngā kaimahi i roto i ā rātou taunekeneke ki ngā pēpi me ngā tamariki kōhungahunga. I kitea ngā tauira pai o te whakamahere me te whakatinanatanga o te hōtaka ki te wāhanga mō ngā tēina.

Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki tuākana ki ngā hōtaka akoranga e poipoi ana i tō rātou pākiki, tō rātou tākare hoki ki te ako. Ka ākina te tū takitahi a ngā tamariki mā te whakawātea i a rātou ki te whiriwhiri me te whakatau i ā rātou ake whakaritenga i roto i ā rātou akoranga, ā rātou tākaro. He pakari tā rātou whakahaeretanga i ngā mahinga o ia rā, ā, he pārekareka hoki ki a rātou te whai wā ki te taha o ngā tēina. He mātau ngā kaimahi i roto i ā rātou taunekeneke e whakakoia ana, e tautoko ana, e whakapūmau ana i ngā tākaro me ngā whakawhitinga kōrero a ngā tamariki.

Ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka whakanuia ngā whakaahua me ngā mahi toi a ngā tamariki, ā, he ātaahua ngā whakaaturanga ki ia pokapū. Ka whakamahia ēnei hei rauemi anō, ā, ka akiaki i ngā tamariki, ngā mātua, me ngā kaimahi ki te whakapuaki whakaaro me te kōrero mō ngā akoranga ā-kaupapa a ngā tamariki, me ngā kaupapa i te kōhanga reo.

Ka kitea i roto i ngā whakaahua te kaha o te whanaungatanga ki te kōhanga reo me te hapori. Ko te mauritau o ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā tamariki me te whānau e whakakoia ana i ngā uaratanga me ngā tikanga o te kōhanga reo, e kōkiri ana i ngā akoranga ā-kaupapa.

Ngā hononga tautoko. E whakarite ana te whānau i ngā hononga papai ki te hapori me te takiwā. E whakarato ana ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i ngā tino wāhi whakahirahira, me te ako i ngā mahi rangatira a ō rātou tīpuna. Ko ngā haerenga ki ngā kōhanga reo o te hapori, ka whai pānga ki te whakawhānui ake i ngā hononga ā-whānau, ā-tangata hoki. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te tino mōhio ki tō rātou kōhanga reo, ki te taiao whānui hei tino puna mātauranga e whakapakari ana i te whanaungatanga.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa ka arotake te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te tautoko i te whānau. Kua whakatūngia e te whānau tētahi kaitautoko mātanga, ā, e whakarato ana ia i te tino ārahitanga ngaio, te tino aronga ngaio hoki. Ka kōkiri ia i āna ake kawenga tautoko. Ko tōna whakapūmau ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo i whai pānga ki te whakapaitanga ake o ngā putanga ki ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi. He nui ngā tūmanako, kia whiwhi ai ngā tamariki i te kounga o te atawhai me ngā whai wāhitanga mātauranga, ki tētahi taiao akoranga e haumaru ana, ā, e hāpai ana hoki.

He pai te tautoko o te whānau i ngā kaimahi. E kawe ana te heamana i ngā mahi e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi, hei whakarite i te tino whai mārika i ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi. Ka hono ngā arohaehae ki ngā whai wāhitanga tōtika o te whakawhanaketanga ngaio.

Te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te aromātai tonutanga o te hōtaka me ngā mōhiohio aromatawai hei whakamōhio i ngā mahere. Ka whai kiko ngā mahere, ā, he mārama ngā hononga ki Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Ka whakaatuhia ēnei ki ngā pakitara hei whakamōhio i te whānau, kia āhei ai rātou ki te tautoko i te whakatinanatanga o ngā hōtaka akoranga.

Ka whakatakotohia ngā taumahi ā-kaupapa a ngā tamariki ki ā rātou pūkete. He pai ki ngā kaimahi te whai wāhi mai o te whānau. Ka whakanui te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā akoranga me ngā whakatutukitanga a ngā tamariki. Ko ngā hononga ki waenga i te kāinga me te kōhanga reo e whakanui ana i ngā putanga papai mō ngā tamariki.

Te arotake whaiaro me te whakamahere rautaki. Ko te arotake whaiaro te tūāpapa o te aroturuki me ngā pūrongo e pā ana ki te whakahaere o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakawhanakehia e te whānau te mahere rautaki mō ngā tau e toru, ā, he mahere mahi anō hoki o roto, hei aroturuki i te āhua o tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā whāinga. Kua pai anō te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo. Kua pai ake hoki te whakahaeretanga me ngā tikanga e pā ana ki ngā utu o te kōhanga reo. Ka whakaatuhia ngā pūrongo pūtea ki ngā hui ā-whānau kia pai ai tō rātou noho mōhio, ā, hei āta whakarite hoki i te ū tonu o ngā utu ki tō rātou pūtea. He pai te noho mōhio o te whānau ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga e hāpai ana i ngā kōkiritanga me ngā whakawhanaketanga ki te kōhanga reo.

Te ako a te whānau. He mārama ki te whānau ngā painga e pā ana ki te whakarato i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakangungu, hei whakawhānui i tō rātou mōhiotanga ngaio, ō rātou pūkenga hoki e pā ana ki Te Whāriki me Te Korowai. He aronga mātātoa tā te whānau ki te ako, ā, ka whai wāhi nui rātou ki ngā tūmomo hōtaka whakangungu ka whakaratohia e Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo me ngā ratonga whakangungu o waho. Ka whai pānga anō hoki tēnei ki te whakapiki ake i te āheinga i roto i te reo Māori.

Ngā whakaritenga haumaru. Ko te hauora me te waiora o ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi tētahi kaupapa matua ki te whānau. Ka whakarato rātou i tētahi angamahi i tuhia ai hei aroturuki tonu i ngā herenga ture ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whai hua ngā kaimahi me te whānau, nā te taumata teitei o ngā whakaritenga hauora, haumaru hoki e whakatutukihia ana ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te whakamahere i te hōtaka. I kitea e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, he rerekē ngā mahi a tēnā me tēnā o ngā kaimahi, ki te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, me aro ake rātou ki te:

  • whakamahere i ngā taumahi mō ngā tama e pakeke ake ana, kia ngākau nui tonu ai, kia ū tonu ai hoki rātou ki ngā mahi
  • whakawhanake i tētahi pūnaha e whakariterite ana i ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi, puta noa i te pokapū akoranga, me te
  • whakarato i te tino kiko o ngā pūrongo ki ngā mātua, e tohu ana i te whai wāhitanga me te ahunga whakamua hoki a ngā tamariki i roto i te roanga o te wā, tae atu hoki ki te reo Māori.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Waimeha, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro.  I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki: 

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

6 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakaae te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo kia tahuri ngā kaimahi ki te:

  1. whakapai ake i ā rātou tukanga whakamahere, kia whai wāhi ai hoki ngā hōtaka akoranga me ngā taumahi e hāngai ana ki ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā matea o ngā tama e pakeke ake ana
  2. whakaōrite i te whakamahinga o ngā kitenga me ngā mōhiohio aromatawai hei whakamōhio i te whakamahere
  3. pūrongo ki te whānau mō te whai wāhitanga me te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki.

7 Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Waimeha is in the suburb of Richmond in Nelson. It was named by two kaumātua at the time the kōhanga reo was established. In its early beginnings, the kōhanga reo was located at a number of different sites before settling in at its current location adjacent to Victory School. Children and whānau, who attend the kōhanga reo, affiliate to various iwi and are all welcomed at the kōhanga reo.

Since ERO’s 2009 review, positive changes have occurred in the management of the kōhanga reo. An experienced mentor, manager and curriculum leader has been appointed. He is well supported by a core group of young committed whānau members who appreciate his professional leadership and guidance. Areas of improvement identified in the previous report have been discussed and addressed. Administration systems and practices are highly organised and effectively managed.

The whānau has a clear vision for the care, wellbeing and education of their children. They are developing in their knowledge of self review. Whānau regularly meet to discuss, monitor and report on aspects of kōhanga reo operations. They want the best possible outcomes for their children. Children’s needs and interests are at the heart of all decision making.

Kaimahi and whānau are proactive in their approach to professional learning to better understand their roles and responsibilities. They take advantage of and support initiatives to share skills and knowledge with each other to increase whānau capability and participation.

The whānau recognises the value of strong support networks to benefit children and whānau. Strong links with community groups, outside agencies and local purapura are firmly established to support the diverse needs of their children. Positive relationships between the community and the kōhanga reo whānau contribute to children’s sense of wellbeing.

The kōhanga reo is characterised by a welcoming atmosphere underpinned by warm, caring, responsive relationships. Planning for the children under two years old is well organised and effectively managed. The next step is to ensure expectations for planning and assessment is maintained across all learning centres.

Children learn and play in a safe, healthy and caring environment.

Future Action

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Waimeha was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. 4 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

The whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Waimeha chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus for this review. They believe that Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira is integrated and interconnected throughout the learning programme.

Areas of good performance

Kuia presence and support. Kuia presence and support is a feature at the kōhanga reo. They are well respected members of the whānau and community. Their contributions include:

  • maintaining te reo Māori and tikanga at the kōhanga reo
  • sharing the history and stories of the area with children and whānau
  • modelling kōhanga reo values of respect for one another, ngākau māhaki and manaaki tangata
  • strengthening and reinforcing whanaungatanga ties
  • providing strong models of support for kaimahi and children.

Kuia have a calming effect on children and help kaimahi to provide a stable whānau base for children to grow and develop. They are appreciated members of the kōhanga reo whānau.

Learning environment. Whānau provide a warm, welcoming environment for children that supports children’s active and quiet play. There is good indoor/outdoor flow that allows children to explore and develop as curious, independent learners. Specific learning areas promote focused activities that challenge and motivate children’s engagement and participation.

The learning areas are spacious, clean, safe, vibrant and, attractively presented to invite children to play and learn. Children are well supervised and enjoy learning and playing in a relaxed atmosphere.

Learning programme. Kaimahi provide flexible programmes that meet younger children’s interests and stages of development. Kaimahi are nurturing and caring in their interactions with infants and toddlers. Good models of programme planning and practise were evident in the teina area.

The older children participate in learning programmes that foster their curiosity and enthusiasm for learning. Children’s independence is encouraged through freedom of choice and independent decisions in their learning and play. They manage daily routines confidently and enjoy time with their teina. Kaimahi skilfully interact with children in ways that affirm, support and sustain their play and conversations.

Children’s learning. Children’s photographs and artwork is valued and attractively displayed in each learning centre. These are used as added resources to encourage children, parents and kaimahi to share and talk about children’s kaupapa learning and kōhanga reo events.

Photographs reflect the strength of whanaungatanga at the kōhanga reo and in the community. The way children and whānau interact positively together affirms that kōhanga reo values and tikanga drives kaupapa learning.

Support networks. The whānau is establishing good connections with the local and wider community. Kaimahi provide opportunities for children to explore significant places of interest and to learn about the exploits of their tīpuna. Excursions to local kōhanga reo help to extend whānau and cultural ties. Children are developing a keen sense of awareness of their kōhanga reo and wider community environments as a valuable learning source for consolidating whanaungatanga links.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of kōhanga operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau support. The whānau has appointed an experienced mentor who provides strong professional leadership and direction. He takes a proactive approach to his mentoring responsibilities. His commitment to the kōhanga reo kaupapa has led to improved outcomes for children and staff. Expectations are high to ensure children receive quality care and education opportunities in a safe, supportive learning environment.

The whānau provides good support for their kaimahi. The responsibility for staff is now undertaken by the chairperson to ensure personnel policy and procedures are rigorously followed. Appraisals are linked to appropriate professional development opportunities.

Planning, assessment and evaluation. Kaimahi use ongoing programme evaluation and assessment information to inform planning. Plans are detailed and clearly linked to Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. These are displayed on the walls to inform whānau so they can support the implementation of learning programmes.

Children’s kaupapa activities are recorded in children’s portfolios. Kaimahi welcome whānau input. Whānau and kaimahi celebrate children’s learning and achievements. These partnerships between the kōhanga reo and home promote positive outcomes for children.

Self review and strategic planning. Self review forms the foundation for monitoring and reporting on kōhanga reo operations. The whānau has developed a three year strategic planwithan action plan to monitor how their goals will be achieved. The financial position of the kōhanga reo is restored. There is improved management and control around kōhanga reo expenditure. Information gathering is used to assist the targeting of financial resources. Financial reports are presented at whānau hui to keep them well informed and to ensure that spending is within their budget. Whānau make well informed decisions to support kōhanga reo initiatives and developments.

Whānau learning. The whānau understand the benefits of providing training opportunities for kaimahi and whānau to extend their professional knowledge and skills about Te Whariki and Te Korowai. Whānau are proactive in their approach to learning and take advantage of training programmes provided by Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust and external training providers. This includes increasing te reo Māori capability and capacity.

Safety practices. The health and well-being of the children and staff is a priority for the whānau. They provide a documented framework for on-going monitoring of compliance in the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi and whānau benefit from the high standard of health and safety practices carried out at the kōhanga reo.

Areas for improvement

Programme planning. ERO found variable practice in the way kaimahi plan, assess and evaluate children’s learning. Kaimahi are aware that they need to focus more attention on:

  • planning activities for older boys to keep them interested and on-task
  • developing a system to ensure kaimahi practices are consistent across learning centres, and
  • providing meaningful reports to parents that show children’s engagement and progress over time including te reo Māori.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Waimeha completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

6 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau agreed that kaimahi:

  • improve their planning processes to include programmes of learning and activities to cater to the interests and needs of older boys
  • develop consistency in the use of observations and assessment information to inform planning
  • report to whānau about children’s engagement and progress.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based on Te Whāriki, the whānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
