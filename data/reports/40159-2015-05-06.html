Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Teko ki te hapori iti o Te Teko. Ka tino whakaatuhia e ngā tamariki te aroha. He māia rātou, ā, ka manaaki hoki tētahi i tētahi. He māhorahora ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki tuākana, ā, ka hāpai rātou i ngā tamariki tēina.

1 Te Horopaki

Ki a koutou e ngā tamariki,mokopuna

tangihia, waiatatia, kōrerotia

tō koutou reo rangatira.

Ka āta whakatakotohia ki te tūtohinga te wawata o ngā kaumātua me te whānau i whai wāhi nui ai ki te whakawhanaketanga o te kōhanga reo, i te tīmatanga o te ngahurutanga o ngā tau 1980. E ora tonu ana tō rātou wawata i roto i ngā mokopuna, te whānau, ngā kaimahi, me te hapori whānui o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Teko.

He nui te pokapū o te kōhanga reo, ā, e tū ana ki te hapori iti, āhua tuawhenua hoki o Te Teko. E pā tata an ako Te Kōhanga Reo o Taahuna, Te Kura o Te Teko, ngā marae e toru me tētahi hapori e mau nei i ētahi toa hokohoko. Haere ai ngā tamariki i ia te wā ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo kaupapa ki te hapori me te marae, ā, ka tino whai wāhi nui, ka tautokona hoki rātou i aua wā. Ka ako, ka whakamahi hoki ngā tamariki i te reo Māori, ngā tikanga Māori, ngā tino uaratanga me ngā whakaritenga ki ngā horopaki tūturu.

E kōpani ana te tauāki tirohanga a te whānau i te noho pūmau ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā moemoeā, ngā tūmanako, me ngā wawata hoki o rātou mā kua huri atu ki tua o te ārai, e noho pūmau tonu ana ki te pūtake o ngā mahi katoa a te whānau me ngā kaimahi hei painga mō te ako, te mātauranga, me te waiora o ngā tamariki. Ka āta kitea te tino hononga, te tino aroha anō hoki mō ngā whakaakoranga me ngā akoranga whakahirahira i tuku iho ai. Ko ngā tamariki ngā kaitango o ngā hua o aua whakaakoranga.

Ka ako, ka tupu hoki ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana, e whakapakari ana hoki i tō rātou reo, tō rātou tuakiri me tō rātou ahurea hoki. Ko te pakari o te ārahitanga, me te hōhonutanga o ngā mōhiotanga ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, e ārahi ana i te whai huatanga o ngā mahi whakahaere ki te kōhanga reo. Ka pou here te whānuitanga o te whanaungatanga i ngā tūranga o te whānau me ngā kaimahi, me ā rātou kawenga mahi.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te whakaatu o ngā tamariki i te aroha?

Ka whakaatu ngā tamariki i te tino aronga nui o te aroha.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He māia ngā tamariki, ā, ka manaaki hoki tētahi i tētahi. Ka kitea te aronga nui o tō rātou whanaungatanga. He māhorahora ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki tuākana ki ngā tēina, ā, ka tautoko hoki i a rātou. He maha ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te mahi me te tākaro takitahi, takirōpū rānei. Ka tino mōhio ngā tamariki ki ā rātou mahinga, ā, ka ngākau nui hoki ki te ārahi i ngā kaupapa a te katoa, a ngā rōpū iti rānei. Ka aro nui, ka whai wāhi nui hoki ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao akoranga whakahihiri.

Whakanuia ai ngā tamariki i ia te wā mō te pai o te whanonga, ngā whakamātau, me ngā whakatutukitanga. Ka toro rātou ki te whakaahuru, ngā taunaki hoki a ngā kaimahi, te whānau, me ō rātou hoa. Ahakoa ka mahi ngā kaimahi ki te taha o ētahi tino rōpū reanga, ka aro atu, ka tahuri atu hoki ki ngā matea o ngā tamariki katoa. He tau ngā tamariki, ā, ka tino manaakitia rātou ki te taiao o tō rātou whānau.

E ako ana ngā tamariki i ngā tino uaratanga, ngā whakapono, me ngā whakaritenga. Ka rumakina rātou ki te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori o tō rātou hapū me tō rātou iwi. He māhorahora te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti kōrero, ā, ka whakamahia ia taumahi hei whakatairanga i te whakawhanaketanga reo Māori o ngā tamariki. Mā ngā huarahi me ngā horopaki e whai pūtake ana e āhei ai ngā tamariki ki te ako i ngā tikanga. Ko te reo Māori, te tuakiri, me te aronga toi whenuatanga hoki o ngā tamariki e tino whakatairangatia ana ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki ako.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā taumahi me ngā hangarau whakahihiri e whai pānga ana ki a rātou, e hāngai ana hoki ki ō rātou reanga. I te wā o te arotake, i tunu tētahi rōpū iti o ngā tamariki i te pīkara, ā, i whai wāhi atu tētahi atu rōpū ki tētahi wero ā-tinana. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te waihanga i ngā mahi toi. He wā motuhake ngā wā kai ki ngā tamariki. Mā ngā tuākana ngā kai oranga, ngā kai reka hoki e hora. Ka whakatauira ngā tamariki i te kai tahi ki te marae i a rātou e kai ana. Ka whakapūmautia te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi pokapū nui e mau nei i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wāhi taumahi whakahihiri. Kua āta whakaritea ia wāhi. He whānui ngā tūmomo rauemi i hokona ai me ngā rauemi māoriori, ngā whakaaturanga me ngā matū toi, ngā taputapu me ngā hanganga hoki e tika ana mō ngā tūmomo reanga o ngā tamariki. He mīharo te maha o ngā wāhi tākaro whakahihiri ki waho hei tūhura, hei whakahura, hei whakamātau hoki ngā tamariki. Ka āta tiakina te whānuitanga o aua wāhi ako. He maha ngā whiringa a ngā tamariki, hei poipoi i ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga ake o ia tamaiti.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore te reo Māori e whakamahia ana e ngā tamariki katoa i ia te wā. Kei te whiriwhiri ētahi tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Pākehā ki ō rātou hoa, ki ētahi atu rānei. Nā te tokomaha o ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi, e āhei ana ki te tautoko me te whai wā hoki ki te hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ia tamaiti. He tino āhuatanga nui tēnei i ngā kaupapa a ngā rōpū iti, i ngā tamariki e whakawhiti ana i ngā whakaaro hōhonu ake ki ngā kaimahi, ki te whānau rānei. He mea nui, kia āta kapo atu ngā kaimahi i aua whai wāhitanga ki te whakapakari ake, te whakatairanga ake hoki i te reo Māori o ia tamaiti.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o te tirohanga ngātahi o te kōhanga reo. Ka whai pānga matua te whānau, te hapū, me te iwi ki te kaupapa. He nui ngā tūmanako mō te manaaki me te waiora i te katoa o te tamaiti. Ko te reo Māori, ngā tikanga Māori, me tētahi aronga nui ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki e noho matua ana. He whānui ngā tūmomo pūmanawa, pūkenga, wheako hoki o ngā kaimahi, ā, ka whakapuakihia e rātou ki te hōtaka akoranga. Ko te mātanga ngātahi o te whānau me ngā kaimahi e whakatairanga ana i ngā whai wāhitanga ako a ngā tamariki.

Ko te pakari o te ārahitanga e hāpai ana i te whai huatanga o te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. He nui ngā mihi a te whānau mō ngā pūkenga ārahi, te ngākau nuitanga hoki o te kaiako matua. Ka whai whakaaro nui ia ki ngā tikanga tuku iho, ngā uaratanga, me ngā whakapono. Ka noho pūmau ia, ka whakaaro huritao hoki, ā, ka whakanui ia i te tū pakari i roto i ngā mahi. Ka hāngai tētahi mahere tauatanga ki te ārahi a ia kaimahi i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo i ia rā. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te pakari o te whakaritenga, te whai pūkenga hoki o ngā mahi a ngā kaiako me ngā kaimahi.

Ka tino hāpaitia ngā mema o te whānau ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki. Ka whai hua ngā pūnaha whakahaere tari me ērā o te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te whakaaro huritao me te arotake whaiaro, e hāpai ana i te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te aroturuki i te āhua o ngā mahi puta noa i ngā wāhanga matua o te whakahaeretanga. He nui te kawenga takohanga o ngā pūrongo ki ngā hui o ia marama, me ngā hui ā-tau. Ka whakatairanga te whānau i te tino whanaungatanga, i ngā hononga hoki i roto i te atawhai me te mātauranga o ngā tamariki. Ko te waiora me te whakawhanaketanga o te katoa o ia tamaiti, me te aronga ki te whakapai tonu, e noho matua ana ki te whakahaeretanga me te kawenga takohanga anō hoki o te whānau.

Ka haere tonu te ako ngaio, te whakawhanaketanga ngaio hoki hei kaupapa matua. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki ngā huihuinga o te purapura, me ngā kaupapa whakangungu ā-rohe, ā-motu hoki. Ka noho pūmau rātou ki te hāpai i te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga anō hoki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Ka whai pānga nui te kaiako matua ki te whakahaeretanga me te kāwanatanga o te kōhanga reo. ahakoa he mahere tauatanga mō ngā kawenga whakahaere a ngā kaimahi mō te wā poto, he mea nui anō hoki kia whai rautaki pērā mō te whānuitanga o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Teko ki te hapori iti o Te Teko. Ka tino whakaatuhia e ngā tamariki te aroha. He māia rātou, ā, ka manaaki hoki tētahi i tētahi. He māhorahora ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki tuākana, ā, ka hāpai rātou i ngā tamariki tēina.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Teko i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

06 Haratua, 2015

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Teko is located in the small community of Te Teko. Children display a strong sense of aroha. They are confident and caring towards each other. Older children are spontaneous in their interactions and are supportive of the younger children.

1 Context

Ki a koutou e ngā tamariki, mokopuna

tangihia, waiatatia, kōrerotia

tō koutou reo rangatira.

‘To you our children and mokopuna, exalt your language with profound humility, rhythm and verse to achieve the ultimate oratory’.

The charter clearly states the aspiration of elders and whānau who were instrumental in the development of the kōhanga reo in the early 1980s. The legacy lives on in mokopuna, whānau, kaimahi and the wider community of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Teko.

The kōhanga reo is a large complex located in the small semi-rural community of Te Teko. Close by is Te Kōhanga Reo o Taahuna, Te Kura o Te Teko, three of the local marae and a residential area with a small number of retailers. Children regularly attend a range of community and marae events and are well received and supported. Children learn and use te reo Māori, tikanga Māori and important values and practices, in real life settings.

The whānau vision statement encapsulates a strong commitment to the kaupapa of kōhanga reo. The dreams, hopes and aspirations of elders who have long passed remain at the heart of all that the whānau and kaimahi do to provide for the children’s learning, care and wellbeing. A deep sense of connection and aroha for important teachings and learning that have been passed down is clearly evident. Children are the recipients of these teachings.

Children learn and grow in an environment that nurtures and strengthens their language, identity and culture. Strong leadership and a deep knowledge of the kaupapa of kōhanga reo guide the effective management of the kōhanga reo. Whanaungatanga, in its widest sense, underpin the roles and responsibilities of whānau and kaimahi.

2 The Review Findings

To what extent do children display aroha?

Children display a strong sense of aroha.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are confident and caring towards each other. They display a strong sense of whanaungatanga. Older children are spontaneous in their interactions and are supportive of the younger children. There are many opportunities for children to work and play on their own and with their peers. Children know their routines well and willingly lead whole or small group activities. Children are responsive and engaged in a vibrant learning environment.

Children regularly receive praise for positive behaviours, effort and achievement. They seek and receive comfort and reassurance from kaimahi, whānau and their peers. Although kaimahi work with particular age groups, they are attentive and responsive to the needs of all children. Children are settled and well cared for in their whānau environment.

Children learn about important values, beliefs and practices. They are immersed in te reo Māori and tikanga Māori of their local hapū and iwi. Children readily engage in conversation and every activity is used to promote children’s te reo Māori development. Children learn tikanga in meaningful ways and settings. Children’s te reo Māori and sense of identity and belonging is successfully enhanced through a range of learning contexts.

Children enjoy a wide range of high interest, practical and age appropriate activities and technologies. During the review, a small group of children made pickle while another group took part in a physical challenge. Children are able to create art pieces. Meal times are special times for children. Tuakana are responsible for serving nutritious and tasty dishes. Children replicate the typical setting of dining at the marae. Te reo Māori and tikanga Māori are reinforced in many of the children’s learning experiences.

Children learn in a large complex which has a wide range of vibrant activity areas. Every space is well planned. There is a variety of manufactured and natural resources, art and craft displays and materials, equipment and structures that are appropriate for children’s different ages. The outdoors has an amazing array of interesting play areas where children explore, discover and experiment. These extensive learning areas are well maintained. Children have many choices to cater for their individual needs and interests.

Key Next Steps

Te reo Māori is not used consistently by all children. Some children choose to speak English with their peers or with others. Given the large number of children and kaimahi, there is sufficient support and time to dedicate to children’s individual te reo Māori development. This is especially important during small group activities where children have more complex conversations with kaimahi or whānau. It is important that kaimahi maximise these opportunities to further strengthen and enhance every child’s te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Children are at the heart of a shared vision for the kōhanga reo. Whānau, hapū and iwi are integral to the kaupapa. There are high expectations for children’s holistic care and wellbeing. Te reo Māori, tikanga Māori and a kaupapa-based approach to children’s learning is a priority. Kaimahi bring a range of strengths, skills and experience to the learning programme. The collective expertise of whānau and kaimahi enhance learning opportunities for children.

Strong leadership guides the effective management of the kōhanga reo. Whānau speak highly of the leadership skills and dedication of the lead kaiako. She has a strong sense of traditional customs, values and beliefs. She is firm, reflective and promotes a high trust working environment. A succession plan involves individual kaimahi leading the daily management of the kōhanga reo. Children benefit from the highly organised and skilful way in which the kaiako and kaimahi work.

Whānau members are well-supported in their roles and responsibilities. Administration and management systems are efficient. Reflective and self review practices help whānau and kaimahi monitor performance across key areas of operations. There is a high level of accountability reporting at monthly and annual meetings. Whānau promote a strong sense of whanaungatanga and partnership in the care and education of children. The holistic wellbeing and development of children, and a focus on continuous improvement, are central to whānau management and accountability.

Professional learning and development is an ongoing priority. Whānau and kaimahi are actively involved in purapura forums, local and national training courses. They are committed to supporting children’s learning and development.

Key Next Steps

The lead kaiako is a central figure in the management and governance of the kōhanga reo. While there is a succession plan in place for short term management responsibilities by kaimahi, it is important that a similar strategy for the overall operation of the kōhanga reo be put in place for whānau.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Teko is located in the small community of Te Teko. Children display a strong sense of aroha. They are confident and caring towards each other. Older children are spontaneous in their interactions and are supportive of the younger children.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Teko will be in three years.
