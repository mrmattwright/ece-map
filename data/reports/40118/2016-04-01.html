Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Pipiwharauroa ki te taha o te marae o Mātaatua, i Rotorua. Ka kitea te tino māramatanga, te tino mōhiotanga hoki o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako mā te whakatōmene me te tūhura. E tupu haere ana tō rātou pakari ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Pipiwharauroa i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Pipiwharauroa ki Rotorua. E tū ana ki te taha o te marae o Mātaatua. Ka whakarato te whānau i te mātauranga rumaki reo Māori me te atawhai mō ngā tamariki. He tātai whakapapa tō ngā kuia, ngā kaimahi, me te whānau ki a Ngāi Tūhoe. Ko te reo o Tūhoe tētahi āhuatanga motuhake o tēnei kōhanga reo.

Mai i te tīmatanga o te kōhanga reo, kua whai wāhi nui te kuia ki reira. Kua kawe ia i ngā uaratanga, ngā whakapono, me ngā tikanga tuku iho. E arotahi ana ia ki ngā tino painga mō ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau.

2  Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu?

Ka kitea te tino māramatanga, te tino mōhiotanga hoki o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira 

Ka kitea te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te whakahirahiratanga o ngā hononga mā roto mai i ngā tikanga Māori. Ko te whai wāhi atu ki ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā waiata koroua me ngā pepeha, me te taki anō hoki i ngā whakapapa, e hāpai ana i te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu, ki tō rātou iwi hoki. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te taunekeneke, te whakapuaki kōrero, me te ako i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki ō rātou hoa, ngā kaimahi, me te whānau. Ka mihimihi, ka whakanui hoki ngā kaimahi, hei akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te ako. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao.

Ka hāpaitia ngā tamariki kia tūhura ai rātou i tō rātou taiao ako. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi hei ārahi i ngā tamariki ki te whakatewhatewha haere i tō rātou ao. Kua waihangatia ngā taumahi hei akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te hīraurau hopanga. Nā ngā haerenga, ka whai wā ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te ao māoriori, te ao tūturu hoki. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā atua Māori, hei kaitiaki o te taiao māoriori. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako mā te whakatōmene me te tūhura.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga o te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori. Nā ngā pepeha, ngā karakia me ngā whakapapa, ka rongo ngā tamariki i ngā hanganga o te reo ōkawa. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi, kia rongo, kia ako hoki ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki ngā horopaki ōkawa me ngā horopaki ōpaki. Ka whakamahia ngā pakiwaitara me ngā pukapuka hei whakawhānui i te reo me te māramatanga o ngā tamariki. Ka aroturuki ngā kaimahi i te ako a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori, hei āhuatanga tonu o ā rātou mahi aromatawai. E tupu haere ana te pakari, te āheinga anō hoki o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

Ka āta whakamaheretia ngā hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki. He mārama ngā tukanga whakamahere e ārahi ana i te whakapuakitanga, ā, e arotahi ana ki ngā matea o ia reanga. Ka hāngai ngā pūkete me ngā pūrongo ahu whakamua ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ka whakaemi ngā kaimahi i ngā mōhiohio e whai hua ana, e whai pūtake ana hoki ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, mā ngā kitenga me ā rātou taunekeneke. Ka noho mōhio ngā mātua ki te ahu whakamua a ā rātou tamariki, me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako.

Aromātaitia ai ngā hōtaka a ngā tamariki i ia te wā. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, ā, ka whakawhiti kōrero mō te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ka whakamahi rātou i aua mōhiohio ki te whakapai ake i te kounga o te ako. Ka tautoko te whānau i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i ngā wā e tika ana. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te kounga o te hōtaka ako i āta whakaritea ai.

Ko te ako a ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā mahi whakamahere a te whānau. He pakari ngā pūnaha whakahaere me ngā kaupapa here e ārahi ana i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakatakotohia e te whānau ngā whāinga mō te pae tata me te pae tawhiti, hei hāpai i te whakaritenga o ngā kaupapa matua ā-tau. Ka pūrongo ngā kaimahi mō te ako a ngā tamariki, ngā rauemi, ngā haerenga, me ngā matea, ā, ka whai pānga nui ēnei ki ngā whakataunga. Ko ngā tamariki te tino pūtake o ngā whakataunga ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Nā ngā mōhiohio i te aromātai o roto, ka kitea ngā take whakahaere hei tirohanga ake. Ko ētahi o aua āhuatanga, ko te whakapakari i te whai wāhi a te whānau ki ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo i ia rā, me te arotake i te tūtohinga kia hāngai ai tēnei ki ngā āhuatanga motuhake o tēnei kōhanga reo. Ko te pūrongo me te aroturuki o roto i ia te wā ka hāpai i te whānau ki te kōkiri tonu i ngā whakapaitanga, me te ahunga whakamua o te aronga rautaki.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā:

  • kia toro atu te whānau ki te ārahitanga me te tautoko ki te whakapai ake i te aromātai o roto me te noho pūmau, te whai wāhi nui hoki o te whānau.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Pipiwharauroa ki te taha o te marae o Mātaatua, i Rotorua. Ka kitea te tino māramatanga, te tino mōhiotanga hoki o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako mā te whakatōmene me te tūhura. E tupu haere ana tō rātou pakari ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pipiwharauroa is next to Mataatua Marae in Rotorua. Children show a good understanding and awareness of their connections to others. Children enjoy learning through discovery and exploration. They are growing in confidence to speak te reo Māori.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Pipiwharauroa will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pipiwharauroa is in Rotorua. It is next to Mātaatua Marae. Whānau provide immersion education and care for children. Kuia, kaimahi and whānau have links to Ngāi Tūhoe. Tūhoe dialect is a feature of the kōhanga reo.

The kuia has been involved in the kōhanga reo since its establishment. She has been responsible for the traditional cultural values, beliefs and practices. She is focused on positive outcomes for children and their whānau.

2  The Review Findings

How well do children show an awareness of their connections to others?

Children show a good understanding and awareness of their connections to others.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children show an understanding of the importance of connections through tikanga Māori. Participation in karakia, hīmene, waiata koroua, pepeha and recitation of whakapapa supports children to understand their connections to each other and their iwi. There are opportunities for children to interact, share and learn about their peers, kaimahi and whānau. Kaimahi use mihimihi and praise to encourage children to learn. Children are happy and settled in their environment.

Children are supported to explore in their learning environment. Kaimahi use a variety of resources to guide children to investigate their world. Activities are designed to encourage children to solve problems. Excursions allow children to explore the natural and physical worlds. Children learn about atua Māori as kaitiaki of the natural environment. Children enjoy learning through discovery and exploration.

Children are immersed in a rich te reo Māori and tikanga Māori environment. Pepeha, karakia and whakapapa expose children to examples of formal language structures. Kaimahi use a range of activities where children hear and learn te reo Māori in formal and informal situations. Storytelling and reading books are used to extend children’s language and understanding. Kaimahi monitor children’s te reo Māori learning as part of their assessment practices. Children’s confidence and ability to speak te reo Māori is growing.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s learning programmes are well planned. There are clear planning processes that guide delivery that focus on the needs of each age group. Profile and progression reports focus on children’s learning and development. Kaimahi gather useful and meaningful information about children learning through observations and their interactions. Parents are well-informed about their children’s progress and growth. Children enjoy learning.

Children’s programmes are regularly evaluated. Kaimahi meet regularly and discuss children’s learning and development. They use this information to improve the quality of learning. Whānau provide support for children’s learning as these arise. Children benefit from quality learning programmes that are well organised.

Children’s learning is a priority in whānau planning. Sound management systems and policies are in place to guide kōhanga reo operations. Whānau have set long and short term goals to assist with determining annual priorities. Kaimahi reporting about children’s learning, resourcing, excursions and needs informs decision making. Children are at the heart of decision making processes at the kōhanga reo.

Key Next Steps

Internal evaluation information has highlighted operational matters that need attention. These include strengthening whānau participation in kōhanga reo daily operations and reviewing the charter to reflect the kōhanga reo uniqueness.  Regular internal reporting and monitoring will assist the whānau with ongoing improvement and progress of the strategic direction.

3  Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4  Recommendation

ERO developed the following recommendation:

  • that the whānau seek guidance and support to improve internal evaluation and whānau commitment and participation. 

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pipiwharauroa is next to Mataatua Marae in Rotorua. Children show a good understanding and awareness of their connections to others. Children enjoy learning through discovery and exploration. They are growing in confidence to speak te reo Māori.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Pipiwharauroa will be in three years.
