1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ko Te Kōhanga Reo o Kokohinau tētahi kōhanga reo noho marae kei Te Teko. Ka whakarato te whānau i ngā wheako akoranga o te ao Māori ki ngā tamariki, hei āta whakatau mai, hei āta whakamana hoki i ngā tikanga me ngā kawa.

‘He pēhea rawa te pai o te whakariterite i ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui?’

Ka tino whakariterite i ngā tamariki me te whānau ki te whai wāhi atu ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui.

E tino manaakitia, e tino aroha nui ana hoki te āhua o ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā hua o te ako mā te noho marae. Whai wāhi ai ngā tamariki i ia te wā ki ngā tikanga me ngā kawa ki te marae, ā, i reira rātou ako ai i ā rātou taonga tuku iho. E tīmata ana rātou ki te whakawhanake i tētahi hononga ā-wairua ki tō rātou marae. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo Māori e kōrerohia ana puta noa i te rā. Ka tāwhai, ka tāruarua hoki ngā tamariki tēina i te reo. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti i ngā kōrero māmā ki ngā kaiako.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te taiao akoranga whakaongaonga i āta whakaritea ai. Ka taea e rātou te whiriwhiri mai i te whānuitanga o ngā taumahi ka ngākau nuitia e rātou. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te kōrero, me te whakawhiti whakaaro mō ā rātou mahi, me ā rātou akoranga. He pai te mahi ngātahi a ngā kaimahi ki te whakarato i tētahi aronga mauritau, riterite hoki.

Kei te whanake tonu te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ahakoa ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa, kei te kōrero tonu ngā tamariki i te reo Pākehā. E mōhio ana te whānau, me whakawhanake i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki ka hāpai i te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake, ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

‘He pēhea rawa te pai o te whakariterite i ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui?’

Ka tino whakariterite i ngā tamariki me te whānau ki te whai wāhi atu ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Kei te ako ngā tamariki i tō rātou tuakiri ā-iwi, ā-ahurea hoki. Nō te marae me te hapū o tēnei wāhi te nuinga o ngā tamariki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako i te reo Māori, tō rātou ahurea, tō rātou tuakiri, me tō rātou marae. E tīmata ana ngā tamariki ki te taki haere i ō rātou pepeha me ō rātou whakapapa. E tīmata ana rātou ki te whakawhanake i te hononga ā-wairua ki tō rātou marae.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whakarongo ki te reo Māori, ā, e tīmata ana rātou ki te taunekeneke me te whakawhiti whakaaro i te reo Māori. Ko te reo Māori te reo taketake o ētahi i reira, ā, ka whakatauira rātou i te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa, puta noa i te rā. Ka taea e ngā tamariki tēina te whakautu mā ngā ringa, ngā nekehanga, me ō rātou āhua. E tīmata ana ngā tamariki tuākana ki te whakamahi i ngā kupu taunga, ngā kīanga māmā, me ngā rerenga kōrero. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti kōrero ki ngā kaimahi. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā waiata, te kapa haka, me te whakaari.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whai wāhi ki ngā taumahi ako i āta whakamaheretia ai. Ka whakarite ngā kaimahi kia hui tahi ai rātou i ia te wā ki te whakamahere. Kei ngā kaimahi ngā mahi whakamahere mārama, ā, he ngāwari te whai haere, he riterite hoki, kia āta tāutu ai ngā putanga ako mārama mō ngā tamariki. Ka whakaratohia ki ngā tamariki ngā pokapū mahi me ngā taumahi, tae atu ki tētahi wāhi pānui, te whakakarakara, ngā poraka hangahanga, ngā mahi kutikuti me te kāpia, ngā taonga puoro, me ngā kākahu whakaari. E whakamātau ana ngā kaimahi i tētahi huarahi hou ki te aromātai i te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki. Ka āta whakarite ngā kaimahi kia whakaaro huritao rātou, kia aromātai hoki rātou i ngā kaupapa ako. Ka whakawhiti whakaaro, ka tātari hoki ngā kaimahi i te whai huatanga o ngā whai wāhitanga ako mā ngā tamariki, tae atu ki ngā mahi ka whāia tonuhia. Ka āta whakarite ngā kaimahi kia mauritau ai ngā taunekeneke ki ngā tamariki, ā, kia hāngai pū anō hoki ki te tamaiti i ia te wā. He pai te wāhi o waho i āta whakaritea ai, e mau nei i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wero kori tinana, me ngā whiringa. He atawhai te wāhi nei, he hihiri hoki, ā, ka āta tiakina. He harikoa ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakaaro pai te tamaiti mōna ake, mō ōna hoa hoki. He māia ngā tamariki, ā, e ngākau nui ana hoki ki ngā akoranga.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Kei te whanake tonu te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ahakoa e whakamahi ana ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa, kei te kōrero tonu ngā tamariki i te reo Pākehā. E mōhio ana te whānau, me whakawhanake i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki ka hāpai i ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi me te kaiwhakahaere i ngā pūrongo e whai hua ana ki te whānau i ia te wā. He pai ki ngā mātua ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te pūrongo kanohi ki te kanohi, i ngā akoranga me te ahunga whakamua a ā rātou tamariki. Ka āta kitea ngā putanga ako mō ngā tamariki i roto i ngā pūrongo. He pai te whakamōhio i te whānau mō te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki.

He pai te takatū a ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakatau ngātahi ai ngā kaimahi i ngā kaupapa ako, ngā whāinga ako, ngā rauemi, me ngā haerenga. Whakawhiti whakaaro ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, ā, e hāngai pū ana aua kōrero ki te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakawhiti kōrero ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki e pā ana ki ngā putanga ako, me ngā huarahi pea hei whāinga mā rātou ki te whakapai ake. E whakamātau ana ngā kaimahi i tētahi puka aromātai hou, kia whakarato ai rātou i ngā hōtaka akoranga e whai hua ake ana, e ngākau nui ake ana hoki mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ka kitea te manawa whakahī, te ngākau nui hoki o ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki whakaaro me te kōrero hoki mō ā rātou pūkete. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki whakaaro mō ā rātou ake wheako. Ka whakarite ngā kaimahi i te tiaki o ngā pūkete, kia hāngai hoki ēnei ki te wā, ā, kia pai hoki te whakatakotoranga. Whakamōhiotia ai ngā mātua i ia te wā, mō ngā āheinga o ā rātou tamariki, me tā rātou ahunga whakamua.

He pai te whakahaere a te whānau me ngā kaimahi i te kōhanga reo. He pai te whakahaere i ngā pūtea. Ka hāngai ngā kirimana me ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi a ngā kaimahi katoa ki te rā, ā, e whakatakoto ana hoki ēnei i ngā kawenga e tūmanakohia ana mō te whakamahere, te whakaako, me te aromātai. He mā ngā papa me ngā wāhi hoki o te papa tākaro o te kōhanga reo, ā, he pai hoki te waihanga o aua wāhi. Ko te tiaki i ia te wā i te wāhi o waho nei, e hāpai ana i te ako me te tākaro o ngā tamariki.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaeretanga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora, me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau kia mahi ngātahi ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakawhanake i ngā rautaki ka whakapiki ake i te āheinga o ngā tamariki ki te kōrerorero mā te reo Māori.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakatūruki Arotake Māori Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kokohinau is a marae based kōhanga in Te Teko. The whānau provide children with te ao Māori learning experiences that normalises and values tikanga and kawa practices.

How well are children prepared to participate in te ao Māori and the wider world?

Children and whānau are well prepared to participate in te ao Māori and the wider world.

Children are well cared for and loved by kaimahi. Children enjoy the benefits of marae based learning. Children participate regularly in the tikanga and kawa practices of the marae where they learn about their heritage. They are beginning to develop a spiritual connection to their marae. Children hear te reo Māori being spoken throughout the day. Younger children mimic and imitate language. Children are able to have simple conversations with kaiako.

Children enjoy a well organised and stimulating learning environment. They can choose from a range of activities that interest them. Children are able to talk about and discuss what they have done and what they have learnt. Kaimahi work well together to provide a positive and consistent approach.

Children's progress in speaking te reo Māori is still developing. While kaimahi use te reo Māori consistently, children continue to speak English. Whānau recognise the need to develop a range of strategies that support children to use te reo Māori.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children atthe kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā taumata whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Ngā taumata whakahirahira

Background

How well are children prepared to participate in te ao Māori and the wider world?

Children and whānau are well prepared to participate in te ao Māori and the wider world.

Areas of good performance

Children learn about their tribal and cultural identity. The majority of children affiliate to the local hapū and marae. Children enjoy learning about te reo Māori, their culture and their identity and their marae. Children are beginning to recite their own pepeha and whakapapa. They are beginning to develop a spiritual connection to their marae.

Children enjoy listening to te reo Māori and are at the beginning stages of being able to interact and communicate in te reo Māori. There are native speakers who model te reo Māori constantly throughout the day. Younger children can respond using gestures, actions and expressions. Older children are beginning to use familiar words, simple phrases and sentences. Children are able to have simple conversations with kaimahi. Children participate in and enjoy music, kapa haka and performing.

Children enjoy taking part in well planned learning activities. Kaimahi ensure that they meet regularly to plan. Kaimahi have a clear, easy to follow and consistent planning practice in place that ensures clear learning outcomes for children are identified. Work stations and activities are provided for children including a reading area, colouring in, building blocks, cutting and pasting activities, musical instruments and dress up. Kaimahi are trialing a new way to evaluate children's progress. Kaimahi ensure that they reflect on and evaluate kaupapa ako. Kaimahi discuss and assess how successful learning opportunities for children have been including what steps are required next. Kaimahi ensure that engagement with children is positive and child centred at all times. Children have a well organised outdoor area that provides a wide range of physical challenges and choices. The setting is inviting, interesting and well maintained. Children are happy and positive about themselves and towards each other. Children are confident and willing learners.

Areas for development

Children's progress in speaking te reo Māori is still developing. While kaimahi use te reo Māori consistently, children continue to speak English. Whānau recognise the need to develop a range of strategies that support children to use te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

Kaimahi and the kaiwhakahaere provide regular and useful reports to the whānau. Parents appreciate how kaimahi report to them ‘kanohi ki te kanohi’ about their child’s learning and progress. Reporting highlights outcomes for children. Whānau are well informed about children’s progress

Kaimahi provide well planned programmes of learning for children. Kaimahi collectively make decisions about learning topics, learning goals, resources and excursions. Kaimahi have regular focused discussions about the learning programme. Kaimahi discuss outcomes for children and how they may improve next time. Kaimahi are trailing a new evaluation form to enable them to provide more effective and enjoyable programmes of learning for their children.

Children show pride and pleasure in sharing and talking about their profile books. Children are able to talk about their own experiences. Kaimahi ensure that profile books are well kept, up to date and well presented .Parents are informed regularly about what their children can do and their children's progress.

The kōhanga is well managed by the whānau and kaimahi. Finances are effectively managed. All staff have up to date contracts and job descriptions which have expectations of their responsibilities for planning, teaching and evaluating. The kōhanga grounds and playground areas are clean and well designed. Regular maintenance of the physical environment supports children to learn and play.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO and the whānau agree that kaimahi and whānau work together to develop strategies to increase the spoken language ability of their children in te reo Māori.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.

Lynda Pura-Watson
