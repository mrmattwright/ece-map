2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Au o Waikato ki te taha o te marae o Kai-a-te-Mata ki Mōrena.  E whakahaerehia ana te kōhanga reo ki tētahi whare taupua.  E whai whakaaro ana te whānau ki te tawhito o te whare o te kōhanga reo.  Ka whakapau kaha rātou ki te tiaki i te whare nei. 

Ka arotahi nui te kōhanga reo nei ki te marae.  Ko ōna hītori, ōna kawa, ōna tikanga hoki e ārahi ana i ngā kaupapa, tae atu ki te whakaako, te ako hoki i te reo Māori.  He hononga ā-whakapapa tō te nuinga o ngā tamariki me te whānau, ā, nō Ngāti Haua rātou. 

Ia rā, ka wheako ngā tamariki i te hōhonutanga o te whanaungatanga e whai pūtake ana.  Ka arahina, ka hāpaitia hoki ngā tamariki e ngā kuia, ngā kaumātua, me te whānau.  He hou te nuinga o te whānau.  E tīmata ana rātou ki te waia haere ki ngā kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, me te kawe i ngā tūranga hou, me ngā mahi hou.  He mauritau, he whakakoia hoki ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā pākeke me ngā tamariki.  E hāpai tōtikatia ana ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou tākaro, ā rātou akoranga hoki. 

Ko te whakaako me te ako i te reo Māori tētahi āhuatanga tino pakari.  Ka whakatairangatia te taiao reo nā te whakamahinga, te whakapūmautanga hoki i te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa, ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki.  Ka hāngai nui ngā akoranga ā-kaupapa ki te whakahirahiratanga o ngā kawenga mahi hei kaitiaki.  Ko te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te marae, te whenua, me te moana, e whakawhānuihia ana nā ngā haerenga i ia te wā. 

Ko te tika o te tautoko me te ārahitanga a ngā kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, i āwhina i te whānau hou ki te whakawhanake i ngā pūnaha e whai hua ake ana ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. 

Ko ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai hei whakapai ake, ka hāngai ki te whakawhanake me te whakatinana i ngā pūnaha mō te arotake whaiaro me ngā arohaehae kaimahi. 

Whakaritenga Whaimuri 

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki.  Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Au o Waikato kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau.  I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Au o Waikato.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Atua. 

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga.  Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Atua – tae atu ki te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, te whakamahere rautaki, te arotake whaiaro, me te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai 

He whakamārama

He mātanga te kaiako, ā, kua mahi ia ki te kōhanga reo mō te maha o ngā tau.  Kua whakatūngia ngā kaimahi hou.  He tokomaha hoki ngā mema o te whānau e tauhou ana ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo.  Ko ētahi o ngā whakawhanaketanga rautaki mō meāke nei, e hāngai ana ki te whakatūnga o tētahi whare kōhanga reo hou. 

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Mana Atua.  Ka whakanuia e te katoa ngā tukanga o te whakawhanaungatanga.  He whakaute te whanaungatanga, ngā hononga hoki, ki waenga i te whānau.  Ko ngā wheako ki te marae e whakarato ana i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te ako mā ngā taunekeneke mauritau, mā ngā horopaki tūturu o ngā tikanga Māori.  He pakari tā rātou whai wāhi ki te pōwhiri, te whakatau, te karakia, me ngā pepeha.  Ka atawhaitia ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao haumaru, e tautoko ana i a rātou. 

Te reo Māori.  Kua āta whakatakotohia ngā akoranga reo ki tēnei kōhanga reo.  Ka whai pūtake, ā, he pārekareka hoki.  Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te reo me ngā tikanga Māori.  Ka tino ārahi, ka tino hautū hoki te kaiako i tana rōpū, me te whānau.  Ka āta whakatakoto ia i ngā tino tūmanako mō te ritenga me te whakahaeretanga o ngā wheako akoranga o te reo Māori.  Ko ngā whakaaturanga me ngā tūtohi ki ngā pakitara, e whai pānga ana, e whakaatu ana hoki i ngā akoranga ā-kaupapa.  Ka āta whakamahia ēnei mōhiohio hei whakapūmau i ngā akoranga o te reo Māori, me te whakawhanaketanga.  Ka whakaratohia ki ngā tuākana ngā whai wāhitanga ki te taki i ngā karakia, ngā kōrero, me ngā waiata.  He tino tauira rātou mō ngā tēina.  He tākare ngā tamariki, ā, ka āta whai wāhi atu ki ngā kaupapa. 

Te kounga o ngā hononga.  He mauritau, he whakakoia hoki te whanaungatanga ki waenga i ngā pākeke me ngā tamariki.  He pai te whakawhitinga kōrero a ngā kaiako ki ngā tamariki, ā, ka akiaki i te whai wāhitanga mātātoa ki ngā wāhanga katoa o te hōtaka.  He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whai wāhitanga ki te tūhura me te whakamahi i ngā rauemi katoa nā tēnei āhuatanga.  He ākonga māia, he ākonga pakari hoki. 

Te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.  Kua whakawhanakehia, kua whakaatuhia hoki tētahi angamahi mārama e pā ana ki ngā mahi whakamahere.  Ka kitea ki te rātaka me ngā mahinga.  Ka rere pai ngā whakawhitinga i waenga i ngā mahinga me ngā taumahi.  E hāngai ana te kaupapa ki Te Whāriki, Te Korowai, me te reo Māori, ā, ka ārahi, ka tuku aronga hoki ēnei mō ngā kaimahi me te whānau. 

Kua whakawhanakehia e te kaiako ngā tukanga aromatawai e whai hua ana.  E hāngai ana ngā aromatawai ki te whakamahinga me te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori, me ngā tikanga, ngā kawa hoki.  Kua whakatakotohia ngā paearu, ā, ko ngā aromatawai o ia te wā e whai pānga ana ki te āheinga o ia tamaiti ki te whakamahi, me te mārama hoki i te reo Māori.  Ka kōrerohia ngā pūrongo ki te taha o ia whānau.  Nā ēnei pūrongo, ka noho mōhio te whānau ki te ahunga whakamua o tā rātou tamaiti, me tōna whakawhanaketanga. 

He ōpaki ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te aromātai hōtaka.  Tū ai ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me te whānau, i te mutunga o ia wāhanga.  Ka whakapuakihia ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki ngā āhuatanga i whai hua ai, me ērā hoki hei whakapai ake.  I tēnei wā, ka whakawhiti kōrero hoki mō ngā kaupapa ako, ngā waiata, me ngā rauemi hoki ka whai ake i te wāhanga whai muri mai. 

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau.  Kua whakapau kaha te whānau ki te whakawhanake me te whakatinana i te whai huatanga ake o ngā pūnaha whakarite, whakahaere hoki o te kōhanga reo.  Kua whakatutukihia tēnei nā te tautoko me te ārahitanga o te kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo.  Ka hāngai aua pūnaha ki:

  • te whakahou ake i te pūnaha whakahaere pūtea, e hāngai atu ana ki te pūrongo ā-motu, te aroturuki, me tētahi mahere mahi ki te whakatutuki i ngā kōhikohiko i tāutuhia ai ki te pūrongo tātari pūtea o mua
  • ngā tukanga whakahaere kaimahi e whai pānga ana ki te whakawhanake ngaio, me ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi, ngā kirimana tuku mahi hoki i waitohungia ai
  • tētahi mahere rautaki o te wā roa e whakaraupapa ana i ngā kaupapa matua i whakaaetia ai mō Te Ara Tūāpae, me tētahi mahere mahi, tirohanga hoki mō te hanganga o tētahi whare hou mō te kōhanga reo
  • tētahi hōtaka hauora, haumaru hoki hei āta whakarite i te whakatutukitanga o te arowhai me te whakapai i ia te wā o ngā rawa me te whare
  • tētahi rārangi mahi mā te whānau i ia te rā, hei hāpai i te ako ā-kaupapa me te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori
  • tētahi aronga ka āta tautoko i te whakaurutanga mai o ngā whānau, me te tohaina haere o ngā tūranga mahi, ngā kawenga mahi hoki. 

He pakari te tūnga o te whānau ki te whakapai i ngā pūnaha mō te whakawhanake tonutanga. 

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi.  Kāhore te arohaehae auau o ngā kaimahi e whakatutukihia ana.  Ko te whai huatanga o ngā kōrero urupare, ka whakarato i te aronga mārama ki ngā kaimahi mō meāke nei, me ngā rauemi hoki hei hāpai i te hōtaka akoranga o te reo Māori. 

Te arotake whaiaro.  He ōpaki ngā tukanga arotake whaiaro i tēnei wā.  Ko tētahi aronga ōkawa ka āta whakarite i te tuhi haere o ngā whakataunga, me te whakamahinga o ēnā hei hāpai i ngā whakaritenga, ngā whakawhanaketanga hoki o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei. 

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga 

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Au o Waikato, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro.  I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

6 Ngā Taunakitanga

I taunaki te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo kia tahuri te whānau ki te:

  • whakawhiti kōrero ki ngā kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, me te whakawhanake, te whakatinana i te ōkawa ake o tētahi hōtaka arotake whaiaro
  • whakawhanake i tētahi tukanga arohaehae kaimahi. 

7 Whakaritenga Whaimuri 

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki.  Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Au o Waikato is situated alongside Kai-a-Te Mata Marae in Morrinsville.  The kōhanga reo operates out of a prefabricated building.  The whānau are mindful of the age of the kōhanga reo building. They work hard to maintain the condition of these premises. 

The marae is the focal point for this kōhanga reo.  Its history, kawa and tikanga guide the kaupapa, including te reo Māori teaching and learning.  Most children and whānau are related through whakapapa and affiliate to Ngāti Haua. 

Each day, children experience rich and meaningful relationships.  They receive mentoring and support from kuia, kaumātua and whānau mentor and support the children.  Most whānau are new.  They are beginning to familiarise themselves with the kaupapa of kōhanga reo and undertake new roles and responsibilities.  Interactions between adults and children are positive and affirming.  Children are well supported in their play and learning.

Te reo Māori teaching and learning is a major strength.  The language environment is enriched through the constant use and reinforcement of te reo Māori in a range of contexts.  Kaupapa learning emphasises the importance of kaitiaki responsibilities. Children’s understanding of marae, the land and the sea is extended through regular trips. 

Appropriate support and advice by Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) district kaupapa kaimahi, has assisted the new whānau to develop more efficient systems for managing the kōhanga reo. 

Areas identified for improvement include developing and implementing systems for self review and kaimahi appraisals. 

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children.  Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Au o Waikato Te Kōhanga Reo was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.  This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children atTe Kōhanga Reo o Te Au o Waikato Te Kōhanga Reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Atua.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education.  For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below. 

4 Findings

Mana Atua - including whānau management, strategic planning, self review and planning, assessment and evaluation

Background

The experienced kaiako has been with the kōhanga reo for many years.  New kaimahi have been appointed. A high number of whānau members are also new to the kaupapa of kōhanga reo. Future strategic developments include the construction of a new kōhanga reo building. 

Areas of good performance

Mana Atua. Whakawhanaungatanga processes are valued by all.  Relationships and connections within the whānau are respectful. Marae-based experiences provide opportunities for children to learn through positive interactions in traditional contexts and tikanga Māori.  They confidently participate in pōwhiri, whakatau, karakia and pepeha.  Children are well cared for in a safe and supportive environment. 

Te Reo Māori. Language learning in this kōhanga reo is carefully constructed.  It is meaningful and fun.  Children are totally immersed in te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. The kaiako provides strong leadership and direction to her team and whānau.  She sets high expectations for the organisation and management of te reo Māori learning experiences.  Wall displays and charts reflect and illustrate kaupapa learning. This information is well used to reinforce te reo Māori learning and development.  Tuakana are provided with opportunities to lead karakia, kōrero and waiata. They are positive role models for teina.  Children are enthusiastic, fully engaged and involved. 

Quality of relationships. Relationships between adults and children are positive and affirming.  Kaiako communicate well with children and they encourage active participation in all aspects of the programme.  Children enjoy the opportunity to explore and use all resources as a result of this.  They are confident and competent learners.

Programme planning, assessment and evaluation. A clear framework for planning is developed and displayed. It is reflected in the daily timetable and routines. Transition between routines and activities occur without upheaval. The kaupapa is based on Te Whāriki, Te Korowai and te reo Māori which guide and give direction for kaimahi and whānau. 

The kaiako has developed effective assessment procedures. Assessment is based on children’s use and understanding of te reo Māori, tikanga and kawa.  Criteria are set and regular assessments recognise each child’s ability to use and understand te reo Māori.  Reports are discussed with each whānau.  These reports keep whānau well informed of their child’s progress and development. 

Procedures for programme evaluation are informal. Regular discussions among kaimahi and whānau are held at the end of each term. Information is shared about what has been successful and what needs improving. At this time, decisions are made about the next kaupapa ako, waiata and resources required for the next term.  

Whānau management. The whānau has worked hard to develop and implement more efficient and effective kōhanga reo organisation and management systems.  This has been achieved with the support and advice of Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) district kaupapa kaimahi. These systems include a:

  • restructured financial management system that includes national reporting and monitoring and an action plan to address anomalies identified in the previous financial audit report
  • personnel management processes that encompass professional development, signed job descriptions and employment contracts
  • long-term strategic plan that includes agreed priorities for Te Ara Tūāpae and an action plan and vision for the construction of a new kōhanga reo building
  • health and safety programme to ensure regular property inspections and maintenance
  • daily whānau roster to support kaupapa learning and te reo Māori development
  • supportive approach to whānau induction and delegation of roles and responsibilities.

The whānau is in a sound position to improve systems for continued development.

Areas for development

Kaimahi appraisals.  Regular kaimahi appraisals do not occur. Constructive feedback should provide kaimahi with a clear future direction and resources to support te reo Māori learning programme. 

Self review. Current self review processes are informal. A more formal approach should ensure decisions are documented and used to support future planning and kōhanga reo developments. 

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Au o Waikato Te Kōhanga Reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist.  In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

6 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau recommend the whānau:

  • works in consultation with the (TKRNT) district kaupapa kaimahi to develop and implement a more formal self review programme
  • develops a kaimahi appraisal process. 

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children.  Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle. 

Ani Rolleston
National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū ā-Motu)

Te Au o Waikato is situated alongside Kai-a-Te Mata Marae in Morrinsville.  The kōhanga reo operates out of a prefabricated building.  The whānau are mindful of the age of the kōhanga reo building. They work hard to maintain the condition of these premises. 

The marae is the focal point for this kōhanga reo.  Its history, kawa and tikanga guide the kaupapa, including te reo Māori teaching and learning.  Most children and whānau are related through whakapapa and affiliate to Ngāti Haua. 

Each day, children experience rich and meaningful relationships.  They receive mentoring and support from kuia, kaumātua and whānau mentor and support the children.  Most whānau are new.  They are beginning to familiarise themselves with the kaupapa of kōhanga reo and undertake new roles and responsibilities.  Interactions between adults and children are positive and affirming.  Children are well supported in their play and learning.

Te reo Māori teaching and learning is a major strength.  The language environment is enriched through the constant use and reinforcement of te reo Māori in a range of contexts.  Kaupapa learning emphasises the importance of kaitiaki responsibilities. Children’s understanding of marae, the land and the sea is extended through regular trips. 

Appropriate support and advice by Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) district kaupapa kaimahi, has assisted the new whānau to develop more efficient systems for managing the kōhanga reo. 

Areas identified for improvement include developing and implementing systems for self review and kaimahi appraisals. 

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children.  Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues.  The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to student achievement and useful to this kōhanga reo.

If you would like a copy of the full report, please contact the kōhanga reo or see the ERO web page, http://www.ero.govt.nz.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews.  The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation.  This area is most likely to support long term improvement.  Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based on Te Whāriki, thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues.  The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement.  A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue.  There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
