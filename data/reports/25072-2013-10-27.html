ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Little Wonders Early Childhood Centre provides full day education and care for children from three months to six years of age. There are five separate areas within the centre and children move from room to room according to their ages and stages of development. All teachers are either qualified or in training for qualifications.

The service is a privately owned company and is the first of eight Little Wonders Early Childhood Centres operating across New Zealand. The centre is managed by a centre manager together with team leaders in each of the five rooms. Company policies and procedures are regularly reviewed and amended as necessary through consultation with staff and families.

At the time of the 2010 ERO review, the centre had increased its roll and catered for up to 50 children. Since that review the centre has continued to grow rapidly and is now licensed for 110 children. Three new rooms have been built, new play areas have been established, and many new staff have been appointed. The cultural backgrounds of teachers reflect the centre and community‚Äôs multicultural diversity.

The company philosophy has been personalised for this centre and further reviewed by teachers and parents to reflect the culture of each individual room. These philosophies are very evident in centre programmes, relationships and culture of the centre.

The 2010 ERO report commented on the overall positive relationships between adults and children, the inviting learning environments and the wide provision of literacy opportunities for babies and children. These good practices remain features of the centre. The report also recommended the need to strengthen self review practices, and to continue to develop staff confidence to use te reo MƒÅori in the programme. Managers and staff have worked together well to address these areas for improvement.

The Review Findings

Little Wonders Early Childhood Centre provides a calm, welcoming environment for children and their families. Respectful, positive relationships are evident between teachers, children and parents. Children settle quickly to play on arrival and confidently make choices about their play.

Teachers are caring and focused on providing positive outcomes for children of all ages. They work well together and frequently reflect on ways to improve what they do.

Good provision is made for babies and toddlers. The adoption of the Resources for Infant Educarers (RIE) philosophy of respect for children and freedom of movement is very evident in teachers‚Äô interactions and relationships with infants and toddlers. Young children are well supported through a flexible curriculum that caters for individual routines and preferences.

In response to parent aspirations for children, the preschool room has a very strong focus on school readiness. Two primary trained teachers run the programme for 4 ¬Ω year old children. Reading, writing and mathematics is taught during mat times and during the twice weekly formal preschool sessions. Some of this learning is linked to children‚Äôs interests. The centre ensures all children have visited a school and are familiar with school expectations of them.

Robust self-review practices are in place. Review is systematic, improvement focused and includes many perspectives. Formal and informal review is well documented and is personalised for each age group. Self-review and professional development has strengthened teachers‚Äô understanding of biculturalism and increased their confidence to include te reo and tikanga MƒÅori during the programme.

The centre has good systems to promote children‚Äôs safety and well being. Parents‚Äô views are regularly sought and appreciated. Teachers are valued and provided with regular professional development and support. The teacher appraisal process is improvement focussed. Centre leaders agree they could appoint an external appraiser for the centre manager.

Key Next Steps

ERO discussed key next steps with the owner and team leaders. These included:

  • further promoting open-ended questions to foster the development of children‚Äôs critical thinking and problem solving during play
  • using examples of well written learning stories in children‚Äôs portfolios as models to promote greater consistency of good teacher practice across the centre
  • reviewing the school readiness programme to better align it with Te WhƒÅriki principles and best practice theory of early childhood learning.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Little Wonders Early Childhood Centre completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

In order to improve practice, centre managers should ensure that adult-to-child ratios for trips away from the centre are written on parent permission forms. Teachers should also better identify and document how they will respond to any emergency arising while on trips.

Next ERO Review

When is ERO likely to review the service again?

The next ERO review of Little Wonders Early Childhood Centre will be in three years.
