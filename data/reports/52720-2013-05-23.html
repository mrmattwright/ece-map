1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā tētahi ō ngā Kaiwhakahaere Matua ā-Rohe o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei, kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Pātea, e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia e te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i te Here-turi-kōkā 2010, me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

  • te whakamāherehere me te aromātai, tae atu ki te whai wāhi atu a te whānau, te arotake whaiaro, te whakamahere hōtaka, me te aromatawai;
  • te hōtaka akoranga, tae atu ki te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori
  • ngā wāhanga kāhore e whakatutuki ana e ai ki te ture, tae atu ki te tatauranga ki waenga i ngā pākeke me ngā tamariki
  • ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai whai muri i te arotake o mua.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

I koha tētahi rata o te hapori i te whare o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Pātea ki te whānau o te kōhanga reo. Nō Pātea te nuinga o ngā tamariki i tēnei kōhanga reo.

E whai muri ana tēnei arotake tāpiri i tētahi atu arotake tāpiri i tū ai i te Pipiri o 2010. I tāutu te arotake a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te Pipiri o 2010, i te ahunga whakamua i whakatutukihia ai whai muri i te arotake tāpiri i mua atu, me ngā wāhanga hoki hei whakawhanake ake. Ko ētahi o aua wāhanga, ko te whakamahere me te aromātai, te whai wāhi a te whānau, te arotake whaiaro, te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, te hōtaka akoranga, tae atu hoki ki te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori me ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ā-ture ana, pērā ki te tatauranga ki waenga i te pakeke me te tamaiti.

Kua waihangatia e ngā kaimahi tētahi taiao akoranga ātaahua ake, tōtikatia ake hoki mō ngā tamariki. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whiriwhiri haere, mai i te whānuitanga o ngā rauemi me ngā taumahi hihiri. He muramura, he ātaahua hoki te whakatakotoranga o ngā wāhi akoranga. He harikoa ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Ka kōkiri ngā kaimahi me te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Pātea i te whakawhanaketanga o ō rātou āheinga i roto i te reo Māori, kia hāpai ake ai rātou i ā rātou tamariki. Kua uru atu ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau ki ngā tūmomo kaupapa whakangungu ka hāpai i te whakawhanake tonutanga o tō rātou reo Māori. Kōrero Māori ai ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa. Kua pai ake te tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i tō rātou reo Māori.

Kua pai ake te whakahaere ngātahi a te whānau, whai muri i te arotake a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2010. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakahaeretanga o tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ko te haere tonutanga o ngā akoranga e pā ana ki Ngā Pou e Whā, e hāpai ana i ngā mema o te whānau ki te noho mārama ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga hoki hei mātua, hei kaituku mahi anō hoki ki te kōhanga reo. Kua pai ake ngā whakaritenga arotake whaiaro. Ka whai hua ki ngā tamariki te pai o te whakaritenga, te whakahaeretanga hoki i te kōhanga reo.

Me whakawhanake ake, me tautoko ake hoki i ētahi wāhanga o te whakahaeretanga o te whānau, tae atu ki te whakamahere rautaki, te whakamahere hōtaka, me te aromatawai.

I tāutuhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, ko ngā akoako inā he ahi, he rū whenua rānei, tētahi herenga ā-ture hei whakatutuki tonu.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Te hōtaka akoranga

He whakamārama

I tāutu te arotake tāpiri i te tau 2010, i ngā āhuatanga o te hōtaka akoranga hei whakawhanake ake. Ka hāngai ēnei ki te waihanga i tētahi taiao akoranga ātaahua, tōtika hoki mō ngā tamariki, me te whakawhanaketanga i te reo Māori mō te whānau me ngā kaimahi.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Te hōtaka akoranga.He ātaahua ake, he tōtika ake hoki te taiao akoranga i waihangatia ai e ngā kaimahi mō ngā tamariki. E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki, ko te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi, taumahi hoki e hihiri ana. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whiriwhiri i ngā tūmomo rauemi hei whakamahi, me ngā taumahi hoki ka whāia e rātou. He muramura, he ātaahua hoki ngā wāhi akoranga. He harikoa ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Te whakawhanake i te reo Māori.Ka kōkiri ngā kaimahi me te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Pātea i te whakawhanaketanga o ō rātou āheinga ki te reo Māori, kia pai ake ai tō rātou tautoko i ā rātou tamariki. Kua uru atu ngā kaimahi ki ngā kaupapa whakangungu a Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, tae atu hoki ki Te Ara Tuatahi, Te Ara Tuarua, me te Tohu Whakapakari, hei hāpai i te whakawhanake tonutanga o tō rātou reo Māori. Kōrero Māori ai rātou ki ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa. Kua uru atu hoki ngā mema o te whānau ki ngā kaupapa ako reo Māori. Kei te hāpai ake i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i tā rātou kōrero i te reo Māori.

Te whakamahere me te aromātai

He whakamārama

I tāutu te pūrongo arotake tāpiri o mua i ngā āhuatanga hei whakawhanake ake, pērā i te whai wāhitanga a te whānau, te arotake whaiaro, te whakamahere rautaki, te whakamahere hōtaka, me te aromatawai.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau.Kua pai ake te whakahaere ngātahi a te whānau, whai muri i te arotake tāpiri a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2010. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu o ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakahaeretanga o tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ko te ako i ia te wā, i Ngā Pou e Whā, e hāpai ana i ngā mema o te whānau ki te noho mārama ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki hei mātua, hei kaituku mahi anō hoki o te kōhanga reo. Kua pai ake te whānuitanga o ngā whakaritenga arotake whaiaro, tae atu ki te pūrongo i ngā hui ā-whānau hei whakamōhio i ngā whakataunga, me te arotake i ngā kaupapa here, te whakamahere anō hoki mō meāke nei. E tino tautokona ana ngā kaimahi me te whānau e ngā kaumātua o te hapori me te purapura.

Ka tautoko ngā mema o te whānau i te hōtaka o ia rā, mā ngā haerenga ā-whānau, me te tae ā-tinana atu i ia te wā ki ngā hui ā-whānau. Ka whai hua ki ngā tamariki te noho whakarite, te whakahaeretanga pai hoki o te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te whakamahere rautaki.He mahere rautaki tā te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Pātea mō tō rātou kōhanga reo i nāianei. Kei roto hoki i tēnei mahere, ko ngā kaupapa matua e pā ana ki tō rātou whare, ki te reo Māori, ā, ki te whakawhanaketanga ngaio hoki o ngā kaimahi. Me whai whakaaro te whānau i nāianei ki te whakawhanake i tētahi hōtaka akoranga e aro ake ana ki ngā matea o ngā tamariki.

Te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai.Kua whakawhanakehia e ngā kaimahi ētahi āhuatanga o te whakamahere hōtaka me te aromatawai. He āhuatanga tonu ēnei hei whakawhanake ake. Kāhore anō kia tāutuhia ngā putanga akoranga e whai pānga ana ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. Me whai whakaaro hoki ngā kaimahi ki te whakaemi i ētahi atu mōhiohio anō, kia āhei ai rātou ki te aroturuki i te ako a ngā tamariki, me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ko ētahi whakaahua o tūmomo mahi a ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo, ka tohu i te whakatutukitanga o ngā putanga me ngā whāinga.

Ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture

He whakamārama

I tāutu te pūrongo o te tau 2010, i ngā wāhanga e ture kāhore e tutuki ana i ngā herenga ā-ture, arā, ko ngā wāhanga hoki hei whakatutukihia e te whānau. Ko aua wāhanga ko te whakarato i tētahi papa e tika ana ki raro i ngā taputapu tākaro o waho, ko te whakarite i te whakatutukitanga o ngā kaimahi katoa i ngā whakangungu e pā ana ki ngā akoako inā he ahi, he rū whenua rānei, me te whakahaere i aua akoako i ia te wā, ā, ko te noho pūmau hoki ki te tatauranga tika ki waenga i ngā pākeke me ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Ngā herenga ā-ture hei whakatutuki.Ka noho pūmau ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te tatauranga e tika ana ki waenga i ngā pākeke me ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa. Ka tango, ka whakahou ake hoki i ngā whāriki tawhito i raro i ngā hanganga tākaro o waho.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Ngā akoako inā hei rū whenua, he ahi rānei.Ko ngā akoako inā he ahi, he rū whenua rānei, tētahi herenga ā-ture tonu hei whakatutuki.

4 Ngā Kawenga Takohanga

Kia tutuki ai i te whānau ngā kawenga e pā ana ki ngā hanganga ture, me tahuri rātou ki te:

4.1 whakarite i te whakangungutanga o ngā kaimahi katoa, ki ngā akoako inā he ahi, he rū whenua rānei, ki ētahi atu tukanga ohorere, ā, kia tutukihia hoki i ia te wā ngā akoako hōneatanga. [R24 (3) Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 1998]

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia noho pūmau tonu te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te tautoko o te purapura me Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, hei āwhina i a rātou ki te:

  • whakatutuki i ngā herenga ā-ture i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo hei āhuatanga ki te whakatutuki tonu, tae atu ki te whakangungu me te whakahaere hoki i ia te wā, i ngā akoako inā hei ahi, he rū whenua rānei 
  • whakawhanake tonu, whakapakari tonu hoki i te whakamahere rautaki, te whakamahere hōtaka, me te aromatawai.   

6 Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti PāteaThis supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance ofgoverning body and management in relation to areas identified in the August 2010ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • planning and evaluation including whānau involvement and participation, self review, and programme planning and assessment
  • the learning programme including te reo Māori development
  • areas of non-compliance including adult to child ratios
  • any issues identified since the previous review.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Pātea building was gifted to whānau of the kōhanga reo by a local doctor. Children who attend this kōhanga reo are mostly from Pātea.

This supplementary review follows a supplementary review carried out in June 2010. The June 2010 ERO review identified progress made since the previous supplementary review and areas identified for further development. These included planning and evaluation, whānau involvement and participation, self review, programme planning, assessment, the learning programme including te reo Māori development and areas of non-compliance including adult to child ratios.

Kaimahi have created a more attractive and suitable learning environment for children. Children are able to make choices from a range of interesting resources and activities. Learning areas are bright and attractively displayed. Children are happy in their kōhanga reo environment.

The kaimahi and whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Pātea are proactive about developing their te reo Māori capabilities to better support their children. Kaimahi and whānau members are enrolled on various courses to support their ongoing te reo Māori development. Kaimahi consistently speak te reo Māori to children. The children are better supported as developing speakers of te reo Māori.

Collective whānau management has improved since the 2010 ERO supplementary review. Whānau members are actively involved in the management of their kōhanga reo. Ongoing learning about Ngā Pou e Whā helps whānau members to understand their roles and responsibilities as parents and employers in the kōhanga reo. Self review practices have improved. Children benefit from an organised and well managed kōhanga reo.

Some aspects of whānau management require further development and support including strategic planning, programme planning and assessment.

ERO identified regular fire and earthquake drills as an area of non-compliance that still needs to be addressed.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Findings

The learning programme

Background

The 2010 Supplementary review identified aspects of the learning programme that needed further development. These included creating an attractive and suitable learning environment for children and te reo Māori development for whānau and kaimahi.

Areas of progress

The learning programme. Kaimahi have created a more attractive and suitable learning environment for children. A variety of interesting resources and activities are accessible to children. Children are able to make choices about what resources they use and activities they want to participate in. Learning areas are bright and attractive. Children are happy in their kōhanga reo.

Te reo Māori development. The kaimahi and whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Pātea are proactive about developing their te reo Māori capabilities to better support their children. Kaimahi are enrolled on Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) courses including Te Ara Tuatahi, Te Ara Tuarua and Te Tohu Whakapakari to support their ongoing te reo Māori development. They speak te reo Māori to children continually. Whānau members are also enrolled on te reo Māori courses. Children are better supported as developing speakers of te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

The previous supplementary review report identified whānau involvement and participation, self review, strategic planning, and programme planning and assessment as areas for further development.

Areas of progress

Whānau management. Collective whānau management has improved since the 2010 ERO supplementary review. Whānau members are actively involved in the management of their kōhanga reo. Ongoing learning about Ngā Pou e Whā helps whānau members to understand their roles and responsibilities as parents and employers in the kōhanga reo. Overall, self review practices have improved including reporting at whānau hui to inform decision making, policy review and future planning. Kaimahi and the whānau are well supported by local kaumātua and the purapura.

Whānau members support the daily programme through whānau trips and regular attendance at whānau hui. Children benefit from an organised and well managed kōhanga reo.

Areas for further improvement

Strategic planning. The whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāti Pātea now has a strategic plan for their kōhanga reo. This plan includes priorities relating to their building, te reo Māori and kaimahi professional development. The whānau should now consider developing a learning programme that is more responsive to children’s needs.

Programme planning and assessment. Kaimahi have developed some aspects of programme planning and assessment. This remains an area for further development. Learning outcomes relevant to kōhanga reo kaupapa are not identified. Kaimahi should also consider gathering more information in order to monitor children’s learning and development. Photographs of children at work in the kōhanga reo would show that outcomes and goals have been achieved.

Areas of non-compliance

Background

The 2010 report identified three areas of non compliance that the whānau needed to address. These included providing suitable surfacing under outdoor play equipment, ensuring all kaimahi are trained in fire and earthquake drills and carry these out regularly and maintaining correct adult to child ratios at all time.

Areas of progress

Actions for compliance. The kaimahi and whānau ensure that correct adult to child ratios are maintained at all times. Old safety matting beneath outdoor play structures is to be removed and replaced.

Area for Further Improvement

Earthquake and fire drills.Regular fire and earthquake drills is an area of non-compliance that still needs to be addressed.

4 Actions Required for Compliance

In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements they need to:

  1. all staff are trained in fire and earthquake drills, and in other emergency procedures, and that regular evacuation drills are carried out.

[R24(3) Education (ECC) Regulations 1998]

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends that the kōhanga reo whānau maintains support from local purapura and TKRNT to help them to:

  • address remaining areas of non-compliance identified in this report including regular training and practice of fire and earthquake drills
  • continue to develop and strengthen strategic planning, programme planning and assessment. 

6 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
