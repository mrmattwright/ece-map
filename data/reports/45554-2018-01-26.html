1 Te Aromātai i Te Puna Akoranga o Ngāti Whakaue

He pēhea te tūnga o Te Puna Akoranga o Ngāti Whakaue ki te whakatairanga i te pai o ngā putanga ako mō ngā tamariki?

Kāhore i te pai te tūnga

Me whakawhanake ake

He pai te tūnga

He tino pai te tūnga

Ko ngā kitenga a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga e whai pānga ana ki tēnei whakataunga i whakarāpopotohia ai ki raro iho nei.

He Whakamārama

“Ngāti Whakaue Iho, Ngāti Whakaue Ake”

Koia nei te tirohanga e pou here ana i ngā whāinga mō ngā tamariki me ngā whānau ki Te Puna Akoranga o Ngāti Whakaue. Nō te tau 2009 te puna akoranga i whakatūngia ai e te tarahiti e kīia nei, ko Te Taumata o Ngāti Whakaue Iho Ake, hei āhuatanga tonu o te rautaki mātauranga a Ngāti Whakaue.

E whakarato ana te puna akoranga i te mātauranga kōhungahunga rumaki reo Māori mō ngā tamariki o ngā tau 0 ki te 5. He hononga ā-whakapapa tō te nuinga o ngā tamariki me ngā whānau o te puna akoranga ki a Ngāti Whakaue.

E whai pānga nui ana te pūrongo nei ki te ahunga whakamua o te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā taunakitanga i whakatakotohia ai ki te pūrongo a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2015. Whai muri i te arotake o mua, ā, nō te Haratua o 2017 tētahi kaiwhakahaere hou o te puna me tētahi pouārahi hou i whakatūngia ai. Ko te aronga a te kaiwhakahaere, ngā poumahi, me te whānau, ko te whakatutuki i ngā whakaaturanga i whakatakotohia ai ki te pūrongo o mua.

Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou ngākau titikaha hei ākonga?

Ahakoa e piki ana te kawe ake a ngā tamariki i ā rātou akoranga, he maha ngā take e tāwēwē tonu hei whakatutukitanga mā te whānau.

Te Whakahaeretanga me ngā Whakaritenga

I tāutuhia ai ki te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, te maha o ngā take i te pā tōraro ki te puna akoranga. Ko ētahi o aua take i hāngai ai ki ngā hononga, ki te kore māramatanga o ngā tūranga me kawenga mahi, me ngā pūnaha ki te hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga ngaio o ngā poumahi, tae atu hoki ki ētahi take herenga ā-ture.

Ko te whakatū i tētahi kaiwhakahaere o te puna me tētahi pouārahi i whai pūtake ai ki te whakapakari haere i ngā hononga papai ki ngā taumata katoa o te ratonga. He āhua hou tonu ngā kaiwhakahaere ki ō rāua tūranga. E āwhina ana tētahi kuia o te hapori nei i a rāua ki te whakatinana i ngā whakarerekētanga e tika ana hei hāpai i ngā tamariki, ngā whānau, me ngā poumahi, engari kāhore anō tonu tētahi tukanga arohaehae kia whakaritea hei hāpai i te tupu ngaio tonu o ngā kaiwhakahaere.

Whai muri i te arotake o mua, kua whakahoungia ngā pūnaha e pā ana ki te arowhai i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea.

Kua whakawhanakehia te mahere rautaki me te mahere ā-tau, engari kāhore anō kia whakamanatia e Te Taumata o Ngāti Whakaue Iho Ake.

I tāutuhia ai ki te pūrongo a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te Whiringa-ā-rangi o 2015, kāhore he tino kiko ki ngā hōtaka i whakamaheretia ai, hei hāpai i ngā mahi aromatawai. I te aromātai i te kounga o ngā mahi whakaako, engari he iti noa te aro atu ki te whai pānga o te hōtaka ki te ako a ngā tamariki.

Ko ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki me te Ngāti Whakauetanga e ārahi ana i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā mahere a ngā pouako. Ko ngā whakaritenga aromatawai a ngā pouako, he mahinga ngātahi, ā, ka whai wāhi anō hoki te reo o ngā tamariki. Ka arotahi ngā paki ako ki ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki, ki tēnā e mahia ana, e ākona ana hoki e rātou, ā, ka whai wāhi mai anō hoki te whānau. E whanake tonu ana ngā mahi ki te aromātai ōkawa i te hōtaka. E whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i ngā painga e ahu mai ai i te mahi ngātahi ki te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Te Hōtaka Akoranga

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki tō rātou tūnga hei tangata whenua. Ka āta whakarite te whānau i te whai wāhi nui o ngā tamariki ki te Ngāti Whakauetanga, hei āhuatanga matua tonu o te marautanga. Ko te wawata matua o te whānau, kia ako ngā tamariki i te reo Māori, ā, kia whakawhanake hoki rātou i ngā pūkenga ki te āta kōrero i te reo Māori. Ko ngā whakaaturanga muramura o ngā mahi toi a ngā tamariki ki ngā wāhi ako, e āta whakaatu ana i te toi motuhake o Ngāti Whakaue, i ngā tūpuna rongonui, me ngā waiata, ngā mōteatea hoki i ākona ai e ngā tamariki. Ka āta whakarite te whānau kia whai wāhi nui ai ngā tamariki i ia te wā ki ngā huihuinga i te marae me ngā kaupapa a te iwi. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i tō rātou mōhiotanga me tō rātou māramatanga ki a rātou anō.

Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tō rātou whakaute mō ētahi atu. E poipoi ana, e manaaki ana hoki ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā pākeke me ngā tamariki. Ka karakia ngā poumahi, ā, ka waiata, ka awhiawhi, ka tuku hoki i ngā kōrero atawhai hei tautāwhi i ngā tamariki e āwangawanga ana. Ka tautoko rātou i ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou tākaro, ā, ka whakamihia ngā mahi a ngā tamariki i ia te wā. He mauritau ngā tamariki.

He tūmāia ngā tamariki ki te ako. Ka hāpai ngā poumahi me te whānau i te whakawhanaketanga o ā rātou tamariki i ō rātou ake huarahi ako. Ka whakarato ngā poumahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi ako, taumahi ako hoki hei akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki e mau ana i ngā matea tauwhāiti kia whanake ai tō rātou mana āhua ake. Kei te harikoa ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka whai ake

I tāutuhia, i kōrerohia hoki e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ki te taha o te whānau, ko ngā whakaritenga matua ka whai ake hei whakapai tonu i ngā whakaritenga. Ka hāngai ēnei ki:

  • te whakapakari i ngā tukanga me te whakatinanatanga o ngā mahi ki te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai
  • te whakaraupapa i ngā mahi matua me ngā whāinga matua ka whakapakari ake ai i te āheinga o ngā poumahi me ngā kaiwhakahaere ki te whakapai ake i ngā putanga ako mō ngā tamariki
  • te whakaoti i te mahere rautaki me te mahere ā-tau.

Te Whakatau a te Whakahaeretanga ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te ratonga ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā ratonga mātauranga kōhungahunga katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Background

“Ngāti Whakaue Iho, Ngāti Whakaue Ake”

This is the vision that underpins the goals for tamariki and whānau at Te Puna Akoranga o Ngāti Whakaue. The puna akoranga was established in 2009 by Te Taumata o Ngāti Whakaue Iho Ake Trust as part of the education strategy for Ngāti Whakaue.

The puna akoranga is a Māori immersion provider of early childhood education for tamariki ranging in age from 0 – 5 years old. Most tamariki and whānau who attend the puna akoranga have whakapapa links to Ngāti Whakaue.

This report highlights the progress made by the whānau to meet the recommendations from the 2015 ERO report. Since the last review a new puna manager and pouārahi were appointed in May of this year. The focus for the manager, poumahi and whānau has been to address the findings from the last report.

The Review Findings

To what extent do tamariki show confidence as learners?

Although tamariki are taking increasing responsibility for their learning, there are a number of outstanding matters for the whānau to address.

Management and Operation

A number of issues were identified in the previous ERO report that were having a negative impact on the puna akoranga. These included relationships, lack of clarity about roles and responsibilities, systems for supporting the professional development for poumahi and some compliance matters.

The employment of a puna manager and a pouarahi has been a purposeful step towards building positive relationships at all levels of the organisation. The managers are relatively new to their roles. A local kuia is helping them to implement necessary changes to support tamariki, whānau and poumahi however there is still no appraisal process in place to support managers in their professional growth.

Hazard checking systems have been updated since the previous review.

Strategic and annual plans have been developed but have not yet been endorsed by the Te Taumata o Ngāti Whakaue Iho Ake Trust.

The November 2015 ERO report identified that planned programmes lacked the detail to support assessment. Evaluation of the quality of teaching practice was occurring but there was little focus on the impact of the programme on children’s learning.

Tamariki interests and Ngāti Whakauetanga guide the development of pouako planning. Pouako assessment practices are collaborative and inclusive of children’s’ voice. Learning stories focus on the children’s’ interests, what they are doing and learning and includes whānau input. Formal programme evaluation is still developing. Children benefit from collaborative planning, assessment and evaluation.

Learning Programme

Tamariki are aware of their role as tangata whenua. The whānau ensure tamariki experience Ngāti Whakauetanga as an essential part of the curriculum. The key aspiration of the whānau is that tamariki are learning te reo Māori and developing skills as capable speakers of te reo Māori. The colourful displays of tamariki artwork in the learning environments reflect the unique art of Ngāti Whakaue, the tupuna rongonui, waiata and mōteatea tamariki have learned. Whānau ensure tamariki have regular opportunities to participate in marae and iwi events. Tamariki are developing a knowledge and understanding about who they are.

Tamariki demonstrate respect for others. Relationships between adults and with tamariki are nurturing and caring. Poumahi use karakia, waiata, cuddles and kind words to reassure upset tamariki. They support tamariki with their play and praise their work often. Tamariki are settled.

Tamariki are confident learners. Poumahi and whānau support their tamariki to develop their own learning pathways. Poumahi offer a variety of learning resources and activities to encourage tamariki to follow their interests. Tamariki with special learning needs are well supported to develop as unique individuals. Tamariki are happy.

Key Next Steps

ERO identified and discussed the key next steps for improving practice with the whānau. These include:

  • Strengthen planning, assessment and evaluation processes and implementation

  • Prioritise key actions and goals for building capability of staff including managers to improve learning outcomes for tamariki

  • Complete strategic and annual planning.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

During the review, ERO looked at the service’s systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)

  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)

  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)

  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Next Evaluation

When is ERO likely to evaluate the immersion centre again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Puna Akoranga o Ngāti Whakaue will be within three years.

Purpose

The Education Review Office (ERO) is the government department that, as part of its work, reviews early childhood services throughout Aotearoa New Zealand. ERO’s reports provide information for whānau, hapū, iwi and communities about strengths and next steps for development. ERO’s evaluation framework Ngā Pou Here is described in SECTION 3 of this report. Immersion early childhood services are partners in the review process and are expected to make use of the review findings to enhance children's wellbeing and learning.

3 General Information about Immersion Early Childhood Reviews

ERO’s Evaluation Framework

ERO’s overarching question for an immersion early childhood education review is ‘How well placed is this service to promote positive learning outcomes for children?’ ERO focuses on the following factors as described in the framework Ngā Pou Here:

  • Pou Whakahaere – how the service determines its vision, philosophy and direction to ensure positive outcomes for children.
  • Pou Ārahi – how leadership is enacted to enhance positive outcomes for children.
  • Mātauranga – whose knowledge is valued and how the curriculum is designed to achieve positive outcomes for children.
  • Tikanga whakaako – how approaches to teaching and learning respond to diversity and support positive outcomes for children.

Within these areas ERO considers the effectiveness of arotake – self review and of whanaungatanga – partnerships with parents and whānau.

ERO evaluates how well placed a service is to sustain good practice and make ongoing improvements for the benefit of all children at the service.

A focus for the government is that all children, especially priority learners, have an opportunity to benefit from quality early childhood education. ERO will report on how well each service promotes positive outcomes for all children, with a focus on children who are Māori, Pacific, have diverse needs, and are up to the age of two.

For more information about the framework and Ngā Pou Here refer to ERO’s Approach to Review in Early Childhood Services.

ERO’s Overall Judgement and Next Review

The overall judgement that ERO makes and the timing of the next review will depend on how well placed a service is to promote positive learning outcomes for children. The categories are:

  • Very well placed – The next ERO review in four years

  • Well placed – The next ERO review in three years

  • Requires further development – The next ERO review within two years

  • Not well placed - The next ERO review in consultation with the Ministry of Education

ERO has developed criteria for each category. These are available on ERO’s website.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews are tailored to each service’s context and performance, within the overarching review framework. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for children and useful to the service.
