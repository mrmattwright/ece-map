1  Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Whare Maha ki te taha o Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Te Raki Pae Whenua, ki te whanga o Mairangi, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau. Kei waenganui i te kura me te kōhanga reo, ko te marae me te whare tupuna a Ohomairangi. Ka hāpai tēnei wāhi i ngā wawata o te whānau mō te mātauranga rumaki reo Māori mō ā rātou tamariki. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā mema o te whānau ki te kōhanga reo i ia te wā.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako i te reo Māori puta noa i te rā. Ka ngākau nui ngā tuākana ki ngā whai wāhitanga mā rātou ki te ārahi me te tautoko i ngā tēina. He whakaute, he manaaki hoki ki ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā tamariki. Ka whai hua ngā mahi whakahaere a ngā mema o te whānau, kia tutukihia ai ā rātou kaupapa matua e whai pānga ana ki te whakapai tonutanga puta noa i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka tino tautokona ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

2  Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā horopaki o te ao Māori?

Ka whakaongaongatia ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whakakoia ana i te ao Māori.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira 

Ka whakaongaongatia ngā tamariki ki te ako me te whakamahi i te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. Ka kōrero ngā kaimahi me ngā pākeke i te reo Māori ki ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā pakiwaitara, me te whakarongo anō hoki ki ngā waiata mā te reo Māori. Ka whakatairangatia te reo Māori e ngā kaimahi ki ngā horopaki o te ao Māori. Ka whakapūmautia ngā tikanga e ngā kaimahi i ngā mahinga o ia rā, ā, i te hōtaka akoranga hoki. E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki kia whanake ai rātou hei ākonga tūmāia ki te ao Māori, ā, kia pakari hoki tō rātou reo Māori.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te tūhura i te ao māoriori, te ao tūturu hoki. Ka whakarato te whānau i ngā whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi, o roto, o waho hoki. Kua whakaritea te wāhi o roto kia āhei ai ngā pēpi ki te tūhura i tō rātou ake wāhi inā e tika ana. He tākare, he pākiki hoki te tūhura haere o ngā tamariki.

E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Ka ako rātou ki tētahi taiao ā-whānau e aroha ana, e manaaki ana hoki i a rātou. Ko te whanaungatanga te tūāpapa o ngā taunekeneke katoa. He pārekareka ki ngā tuākana te noho tahi me ngā tēina. Ka poipoi te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā hononga papai, ngā hononga manaaki hoki. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki, ā, ka tino rongo rātou i te aroha.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E mōhio ana te whānau ki ngā hua ka pupū ake nā te whakakaha ake i te whai wāhi atu ki te purapura me ngā kura o tēnei takiwā. E mōhio ana rātou, ka whakatairanga tēnei i tā rātou whakaritenga ngaio, ka hāpai hoki i ngā wawata o te whānau mō te mātauranga rumaki reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

Ka whakahaere te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. He whānui ngā tūmomo pūmanawa, ngā tūmomo pūkenga hoki o te whānau. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga, me te whakamahere mō te kōhanga reo. Ka haere tonu i ia te wā, ko te aroturuki me te arotake i ngā rawa, ngā rauemi, te reo Māori, te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te ako a te whānau. He tokomaha ngā mema o te whānau e whakangungutia ana i raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo me ētahi atu ratonga o waho, hei whakapakari ake i ngā āheinga o te whānau. Ka aroturuki te whānau i ā rātou whāinga ki ngā hui e tū ai i ia te wā. Ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau.

Ka whai wāhi matua ngā matea o ngā tamariki ki te arotake whaiaro a te whānau. Ka whiwhi te whānau i te pai o ngā mōhiohio. Ka whakamahi rātou i aua mōhiohio hei tāutu i ngā wāhanga ka whakawhanake tonuhia. Ka hāngai te arotake whaiaro ki te arotake o te tūtohinga, Te Whāriki, te whakaritenga o ngā kaimahi, ngā tikanga, me te whakatutukitanga o ngā herenga ā-ture. Ka arotahi hoki te mahere rautaki ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo me ngā whakapai tonutanga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He rerekē te āhua o ngā mahi e pā ana ki te whakamahere, te aromātai, me te aromatawai. Kāhore te tūtohinga e whakatakoto ana i ngā tino tūmanako ka ārahi i ngā kaimahi katoa. E mōhio ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi, me whakawhanake tonu hei whakapai ake i te kounga o te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromātai, me ngā pūrongo.

3  Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

4  Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • haere tonu ngā mahi e whakapai ake ana i te kounga o te arotake whaiaro me te pūrongo.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Whare Maha ki te whanga o Mairangi, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau. Ka whakaongaongatia ngā tamariki hei ākonga, ki te taiao ako o tō rātou kōhanga reo. E tino tautokona ana rātou ki te ako i te reo Māori. He nui te whai wāhi mai, te tautoko hoki o te whānau i te kōhanga reo.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Whare Maha i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

1  Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Whare Maha is located next to Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Te Raki Pae Whenua in Mairangi Bay, Auckland. The marae and whare tupuna, Ohomairangi is between the kura and kōhanga reo. This location supports whānau aspirations for Māori immersion education for their children. Whānau members are actively and regularly involved at the kōhanga reo. 

Children enjoy learning te reo Māori throughout the day. Tuakana enjoy opportunities to lead and support teina. Relationships between children are respectful and caring. Whānau members provide effective management to realise their priorities for continual improvement across kōhanga reo operations. Children are well supported at kōhanga reo.

2  The Review Findings

How well do children engage in te ao Māori contexts?

Children are stimulated in an environment that affirms te ao Māori.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are stimulated to learn and use te reo and tikanga Māori. Kaimahi and adults speak te reo Māori to children at all times.  Children enjoy stories and listening to music in te reo Māori.  Kaimahi promote te reo Māori in te ao Māori contexts. Tikanga is reinforced by kaimahi during daily routines and the learning programme. Children are well supported to develop as confident learners in te ao Māori, and capable speakers of te reo Māori.

Children enjoy exploring the natural and physical worlds. The whānau provides a variety of indoor and outdoor activities. Children are stimulated by the choices they have to explore. The indoor environment provides opportunities for babies to explore in their own designated space if necessary. Children are keen and curious explorers.

Children are very well supported at kōhanga reo. They learn in a loving and caring whānau environment. Whanaungatanga is the foundation of all interactions. Tuakana enjoy time with teina. Whānau and kaimahi foster positive and caring relationships. Children are happy, settled and know they are loved.

Key Next Steps

The whānau recognise the benefits of increasing participation with purapura and local kura. They acknowledge that this will enhance their professional practice, and support whānau aspirations for Māori immersion education.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau manage the kōhanga reo in the interests of children. Whānau have a wide range of strengths and skills. They contribute to decision making and planning for the kōhanga reo.  There is ongoing monitoring and review of property, resources, te reo Māori, children’s safety and whānau learning. A significant number of whānau members are in training through Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT), and other external providers to build whānau capacity. Whānau monitor their goals at regular meetings. Children are at the heart of whānau management practice.

Children’s needs are integral to whānau self-review. The whānau receive good information. They use this information to identify areas for further development. Self-review includes the review of the charter, Te Whāriki, staff organisation, tikanga and compliance with legislation. The strategic plan also focuses on the kōhanga reo kaupapa and ongoing improvements for their children.

Key Next Steps

Planning, evaluation and assessment practices vary. The charter does not set out clear expectations to guide all kaimahi. Whānau and kaimahi recognise that further development is needed to improve the quality of programme planning, evaluation and reporting.

3  Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4  Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau agreed to the following recommendation:

  • continue to improve the quality of self-review and reporting.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Whare Maha is in Mairangi Bay, Auckland. Children are stimulated learners in their kōhanga reo learning environment. They are well supported to learn te reo Māori. There are high levels of whānau involvement and support at the kōhanga reo. 

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Whare Maha will be in three years.

Lynda Pura-Watson
