1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

‘E kore e ngaro te kākano rangatira’

Kei Matatā te kōhanga reo o Rangitihi e tū ana ki te taha o Rangitihi Marae. I kōwhiritia e te whānau Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira hei kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.Ka tino kite Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira i te hōtaka whakamāherehere, ngā aromatawai me ngā whakahaere e pā ana ki te aromātai me te hōtaka whakahaere o ia rā.

E whakawhānui ake ana, ā, e wero ana ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki i a rātou e ako ana. Ka wero ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā tamariki ki te āta whakaaro, ā, ka whakawhānui ake rātou i ō rātou hiahia. Ka whakanui, ka whakaako, ā, ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā whanonga whai tikanga hei tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te tiaki me te whai koha ki ētahi atu, ki a rātou anō me te taiao. Mā ēnei wheako e ako ngā tamariki i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki ngā mahi a ō rātou tūpuna me ō rātou tūhonohono ki ētahi atu.

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou māia me ō rātou mātauranga e pā ana kia ko wai rātou me ā rātou tūhonohono ki a Ngāti Rangitihi. E whakarato ana ngā kaimahi i ngā wheako huhua me ngā wheako whai hua mā ngā tamariki hei āwhina i ā rātou ki te whakanui ake i ngā mahi ō neherā a ngā tūpuna, ngā whakapapa, ngā kawa me te reo Māori me ōnā tikanga.

E harikoa ana, ā, e noho mauritau ana ngā tamariki. E harikoa ana rātou ki te tuku whakaari i a rātou e ako ana. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou mātau ki te reo Māori. To rātou pai hoki ki te whakahoki kōrero i te wā e rere ana te reo Māori huri noa i a rātou, ā, e mārama ana rātou ki ā rātou tūhonohono ki te whenua. E tāutu ana te hōtaka whakamāherehere i ngā matea o ngā tamariki, ā, kua arahina ngā whakahaere o ia rā e te hōtaka whakamāherehere. E piki ana te mātauranga me te māia o ngā tamariki i te wā e ako ana rātou i ngā āhuatanga mō te ao Māori me te ao whānui.

E whakahaere ana te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. E arahina ana te whānau e ngā whāinga mai i Te Ara Tūāpae, kia tuhia tētahi māhere ā-tau mā tō rātou kōhanga reo. Kei te whai hua ngā whakahaere a te whānau mō te whakahaere pūtea me ngā take e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi. Ka āwhina ngā tūmahi o te arotake whaiaro i te whānau ki te aroturuki me te whakapai ake i ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā. Kua whakatūria ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā mō te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki. He kaupapa nui rawa te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki ki tēnei kōhanga reo.

Ngā Mahi Ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o Te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau Te Kōhanga Reo o Rangitihi kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Rangitihi.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko āna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere i te māhere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • hōtaka whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

I kōwhiritia e te whānauNgā Taumata Whakahirahirahei kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Kua whakauru ngā wāhanga katoa o Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira ki te hōtaka whakamāherehere, ngā aromatawai, ngā aromātai me te hōtaka o ia rā a ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Mana Reo

E piki ana te mātau o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. E whakanui ake ana, ā, e tautoko ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki te whakawhanake i tō rātou mārama ki te reo Māori. Ka torotoro ngā tamariki i te marae me te tae atu ki ngā hui ka rangona te reo Māori, ā, e kōrerotia ana i ngā horopaki whānui. Kua whakanuia te reo Māori kia Māori ai ngā whatumanawa ō ngā tamariki ki te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero ki ētahi atu. Ko ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki ki te whānau, ngā kaimahi me ētahi atu i te kōhanga reo te kaupapa nui hei tautoko i ā rātou ki te whakapiki i ō rātou mātau ki te reo Māori.

Mana Aotūroa

Kua whakawhānuitia, ā, kua werohia ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi i te wā e ako ana rātou. E whakarato ana ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā ngohe huhua mō ia rā hei:

  • whakahoi i te whakatipuranga o ngā tamariki
  • wero i ō rātou whai whakaaro
  • whakawhānui ake i ō rātou hiahia me te whakawhānui ake i ō rātou mātauranga.

E harikoa ana, ā, e noho mauritau ana ngā tamariki i te wā e ako ana rātou.

Mana Atua

E whakaatu ana ngā tamariki ī ngā uara me ngā whanonga o te Mana Atua i ā rātou e taunekeneke ana. Tērā te manawanui o ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakanui, te whakaako me te whakatauira i ngā whanonga whai tikanga hei akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te whai koha ki a rātou anō, ki ētahi atu me te taiao.

Mana Tangata

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou māia me ō rātou mātauranga e pā ana kia ko wai rātou me ā rātou tūhonohono ki a Ngāti Rangitihi. E whakarato ana ngā kaimahi i ngā wheako huhua me ngā wheako whai hua mā ngā tamariki hei āwhina i ā rātou ki te whakanui i ngā mahi ō neherā a ngā tūpuna, ngā whakapapa, ngā kawa me te reo Māori me ōnā tikanga o ngā tamariki. Ka mahi ngātahi te whānau ki ngā kaimahi, ngā kuia me ngā kaumātua ki te whakahaumako i ngā wheako akoranga a ngā tamariki. He nui ngā wheako a ngā tamariki ki te whai atu, te tātaki me te whakaatu i ō rātou mātauranga o te kawa me ngā tikanga o te marae.

Ngā tamariki me ō rātou matea motuhake.E tiakina ana, ā, e whakaako ana te whānau i ngā tamariki wāwāhī taha, ā, he mea whai tikanga te āhua o ā rātou tautoko i ngā tamariki wāwāhī taha. Kua whakaritea tētahi māhere mātauranga mō ia tamaiti e whakaatu ai e pēhea ana te hōtaka a te kōhanga reo e hāngai ana ki ō rātou matea. E harikoa ana, ā, e noho mauritau ana rātou i roto i te taiao o te kōhanga reo.

Mana Whenua

E rumaki ana ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga e whakaako ai te mātauranga e pā ana ki ō rātou whenua, ō rātou tikanga me ngā mahi ō-neherā a ō rātou tūpuna. Ka tino whakanui te hōtaka o ia rā me ngā wheako i ngā tamariki ki te whakapā, te rongo, te tūhura, te pata pātai me te taunekeneke ki te ao ō-mataora. E tuari ana te whānau i ō rātou mātauranga me ō rātou āheinga matatau ki ngā tamariki. E whai ake nei ētahi tauira, arā:

  • ngā mōhiohio mō te hītori Māori o te hau kāinga
  • ngā tikanga mō te hī ika
  • te whakatō i ngā tipu, te mahi māra me te hauhake
  • te hangahanga me te waihanga.

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou tūhonohono ki te whenua.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te hōtaka mō ngā pēpi.Kāore te hōtaka i te whakatutuki ngā tino matea o ia pēpi. Me whai wāhi ngā kaimahi ki te arotake i te hōtaka me te whai whakaaro ki ngā matea me ngā hiahia o ngā pēpi.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamāherehere me te aromātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo hei whakatau i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamāherehere, ngā whakahaere me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga reo mā a rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka āta hāngai te aromātai nei ki te hōtaka whakamāherehere me te aroturuki i te tipuranga ako o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Ngā whakahaere a te whānau.Kei te pai ngā whakahaere a te whānau ki te kōhanga reo. E tautoko ana ngā tukanga kia rere ngāwari ai ngā whakaritenga o ia rā me ngā whakahaere a te kōhanga reo. E tino whai wāhi ana te whānau ki te whakatau i ngā take, te whakatau i ngā whāinga, te whakamāherehere mō ngā rangi ā meāke nei me te aroturuki i te whai painga o te kōhanga reo me ngā putanga hua. Tērā anō te manawanui me te ū o te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā.He aratohu tā te whānau hei tautoko i a rātou ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. Kua whakatauria e te whānau ngā kaupapa here e hāngai tōtika ana kiNgā Pou e WhāoTe Korowai. E tautoko ana ngā kaupapa here e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru i ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā, ā, ka hāngai tōtika ki ngā ture whakahaere whare kōhungahunga.

Ngā Kaimahi.Tērā tētahi pūnaha aromātai kaimahi whai hua. Ka tautoko te whānau i ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi. Kua whakaritea ngā rārangi mahi, ā, kua waitohutia ngā kirimana whakawhiwhi mahi e ngā kaimahi. Tērā anō tētahi aromātai mō ngā kaimahi ia tau, ā, ka whakatau rātou i ō rātou whāinga whaiaro me ō rātou whāinga whakangungu.

Te whakahaere pūtea.He mea whai painga ngā whakahaere pūtea a te whānau. Kātahi anō rātou ka hokona ētahi rauemi mātauranga hei tautoko i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Te arotake whaiaro.E whakahaere ana te whānau i ngā mōhiohio arotake whaiaro hei tautoko i a rātou ki te whakamāherehere mō ngā rangi ā meāke nei o te kōhanga reo. Ka riro i a rātou ngā pūrongo i ia wā hei āwhina i a rātou ki te whiriwhiri me te whakatau i ngā take e pā ana ki ngā whakahaere a te kōhanga reo. E aroturuki ana te whānau, ā, e tuku pūrongo ana te whānau mō ō rātou whāinga rautaki me ngā tūmahi e hāngai ana ki ngā whāinga o Te Ara Tūāpae. Ko te whakanui ake i te tokomaha o nga tamariki me ngā whai painga o te kōhanga reo mō ngā rangi ā meāke nei te kaupapa nui ki tēnei kōhanga reo.

Te hōtaka whakamāherehere.E pai ana te āhua o ngā whakamāramatanga a ngā kaimahi ki te whakamāherehere. Kua whakawhanaketia ngā māhere hōhonu e ngā kaimahi mā te kōrero ki ngā mātua o te whānau. E whakatutuki ana ngā māhere i ngā matea o ngā tamariki, ā, ka arahi ngā māhere i ngā whakahaere a te hōtaka. E piki ana te mātauranga me te māia o ngā tamariki mā te ako i ngā āhuatanga o te ao Maori me te ao whānui.

Te aromatawai mō ngā tamariki nui ake i te rua ngā tau.E whakahaere ana ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki aromatawai whai painga ki te whakaatu i ngā akoranga me te tipuranga o ngā tamariki. Kei roto i ngā pūkete paetae ngā mōhiohio whānui mō ngā kaupapa kua akona e ngā tamariki, arā, ā rātou wheako me ngā tauira mahi toi. I te wa, i te wa, kua whakamōhiotia ngā mātua o te whānau e ngā kaimahi mō ngā putanga paetae e pā ana ki ngā akoranga me ngā paetae o ā rātou tamariki.

Te aromātai.E ū ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakarerekē i ngā āhuatanga whakahaere hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki. Ka aromātai rātou i ngā wāhanga o te hōtaka i te wā i te wā. Mā tēnei ka whakarerekē rātou i ngā wāhanga o te hōtaka akoranga hei whakapai ake anō. Ko te aromātai te take ka pai ake ngā putanga ako mō ngā tamariki.

E whakahaeretia ana e te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te whakamāherehere me te aromatawai mō ngā tamariki ki raro iho i te rua ngā tau.Kāore anō ngā kaimahi kia whai wāhi ki te whiriwhiri i ngā māhere e tāutu ai ngā tino wheako akoranga mō ngā tamariki ki raro iho i te rua ngā tau. E tipu haere ana te rārangi kaute mō ngā pēpi. Me tautokotia ngā wheako akoranga i ngā whakatipuranga o ngā pēpi. Kāore anō ngā tukanga aromatawai kia whakatūria mō ngā tamariki ki raro iho i te rua ngā tau.

Ngā tuku pūrongo a ngā kaiako.Ahakoa ka tuku ngā kaiako i tētahi pūrongo ki te whānau ia marama, me kaha ake tō rātou arotahi ki ngā wheako a ngā tamariki me ō rātou whanaketanga, ā, me iti iho ngā kōrero mō ngā mahi ā-tari me ngā take whakahaere.

Te whare o te kōhanga reo.Me tere rawa te tautoko i te whānau ki te whakapai ake i ngā take e pā ana ki te whenua me te hūnuku a tō rātou whare.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

He whakamārama

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Rangitihi Kōhanga Reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga a te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

4 Ngā Tūtohunga

E tūtohu ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ki te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā tūtohunga e whai ake nei:

  • te whakamāherehere mō ngā matea rerekē o tēnā me tēnā ki raro iho i te rua ngā tau me te aroturuki me te aromatawai i te wā i te wā i ō rātou paetae
  • te whakatakoto ā-tuhi i ngā tūmahi mō ia whāinga i roto i te māhere rautaki, te aroturuki me te tuku pūrongo i ia wā mō ngā paetae o ēnei whāinga
  • te rapu tautoko mai i te Poari Matua o Te Kōhanga Reo ki te whakatau inā he wāhi tika te whare o te kōhanga reo mā ngā tamariki.

5 Ngā Mahi ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

27 Hui-tanguru 2014

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu Whakakapi mō ngā Ratonga Arotake Māori (Te Uepū ā-Motu)

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

‘E kore e ngaro te kakano rangatira’

Our ancestors will never die

They live on in each one of us.

Rangitihi Kōhanga Reo is in Matata and is next to Rangitihi Marae.The whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus for this review. Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira are fully integrated throughout programme planning, assessment, evaluation practices and the daily programme.

Kaimahi extend and challenge children in their learning. Kaimahi and whānau challenge children’s thinking and extend their interests. Kaimahi and whānau reinforce, teach and model high standards of behaviour that encourage children to show respect and care for others, themselves and the environment. Through these experiences children learn about their ancestral heritage and connections to others.

Children are developing confidence and knowledge about who they are and their connections to Ngāti Rangitihi. Kaimahi provide a range of meaningful learning experiences that affirm children’s whakapapa, customs, traditions and language. Children are happy and settled. They enjoy role play as they learn. Children are developing competence in te reo Māori. They respond appropriately to te reo Māori around them and they are aware of their connections to the whenua. Programme planning identifies the needs of children and guides the implementation of the programme. Children gain knowledge and confidence when they learn about te ao Māori and the wider world.

Whānau manage the kōhanga reo in the best interests of the children. The whānau use the goals from Te Ara Tūāpae in their annual plan for their kōhanga reo. Whānau manage their finances and personnel effectively. Useful self review activities help the whānau to monitor and improve the operations and practice. All health and safety policies and procedures are in place. Children’s health and safety is a priority at this kōhanga reo.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

The whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus for this review. Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira is fully integrated throughout programme planning, assessment, evaluation and the children’s daily programme.

Areas of good performance

Mana Reo

Children are developing competence in te reo Māori. Whānau and kaimahi encourage and support children’s te reo Māori development. Children visit the marae and attend events where te reo Māori can be heard and used in a range of contexts. Te Reo Māori is positively reinforced so that it is a natural way for children to communicate with others. The interactions children have at kōhanga reo with whānau, kaimahi and others are valued as an important part of developing their te reo Māori.

Mana Aoturoa

Kaimahi extend and challenge children in their learning. Kaimahi and whānau provide a wide range of daily activities that:

  • simulate children’s development
  • challenge their thinking
  • extend their interests and broaden their knowledge.

Children are settled and happy in their learning.

Mana Atua

Children demonstrate the values and behaviours of mana atua in their interactions. There is a collective effort by kaimahi and whānau to reinforce, teach and model the expected behaviours that encourage children to show respect and care for others, themselves and the environment.

Mana Tangata

Children are developing confidence and knowledge of who they are and their connections to Ngāti Rangitihi. Kaimahi provide a range of meaningful learning experiences for children that help affirm children’s ancestral heritage, whakapapa, customs, traditions and language. Whānau work with kaimahi, kuia, kaumatua to enrich children’s learning experiences. Children have many opportunities to participate in, lead and to demonstrate their knowledge of marae kawa and tikanga.

Children with special needs. Whānau care for and educate children with special needs in appropriate ways. Children have an individual education plan that shows how the kōhanga reo programme will be adapted to fit their needs. They are happy and settled in the kōhanga reo environment.

Mana Whenua

Children are immersed in learning that teaches them about their land, customs and traditions. There is a strong emphasis in the daily programme on experiences that encourage children to touch, feel, explore, discover, inquire and interact with the natural environment. Whānau share their knowledge and expertise with children. These include:

  • information about local Māori history
  • fishing practices
  • planting, gardening and harvesting
  • building and construction.

Children are developing an understanding of their connections to the land.

Area for review and development

Babies programme.The programme does not meet the specific developmental needs of babies. Kaimahi need to review the programme and give consideration to the needs and interests of babies.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of the operations. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and the monitoring of children’s progress and development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management.The whānau manage the kōhanga reo well. The systems support the smooth operation and management of the kōhanga reo. Whānau are actively involved in decision making, the setting of goals, planning future direction and monitoring the performance of kōhanga reo growth and outcomes. There is a collective commitment and responsibility by whānau to manage the kōhanga reo in the interests of children.

Policies with procedures.The whānau has suitable guidelines to assist them in managing the kōhanga reo. Whānau have a set of policies that align withNgā Pou e WhāfromTe Korowai. Health and safety policies inform procedures and guide practice to comply with appropriate regulations.

Personnel. There is an effective appraisal system in place. Whānau support kaimahi in their work. Job descriptions are in place and kaimahi have signed employment contracts. All kaimahi have an annual appraisal and they set personal and professional development goals.

Financial management. Whānau manage their finances effectively. They have recently purchased education resources to support children’s learning.

Self review.Whānau use self review information appropriately to plan for the future of the kōhanga reo. They receive regular reports that help them make decisions about the kōhanga reo operations. Whānau monitor and report on their strategic objectives and actions which are linked to the goals inTe Ara Tūāpae. There is a strong focus on continuing growth and sustainability.

Programme planning.Kaimahi have a good understanding of planning. Kaimahi in consultation with whānau develop clear and comprehensive plans. Plans meet the needs of children and guide the implementation of the programme. Children gain knowledge and confidence learning about te aō Māori and the wider world.

Assessment for children over two years old. Kaimahi use an effective range of assessment tools to show children’s learning and development. Profiles include a range of information about what children have learned, their experiences and art samples. Kaimahi regularly inform whānau about the progress of their child’s learning and achievements.

Evaluation.Kaimahi are committed to bringing about positive change for their children. They regularly evaluate aspects of the programme. This often leads to modifications to the learning programme. Evaluation leads to improved outcomes for children.

The whānau manages the kōhanga reo to ensure best possible outcomes occur for children.

Areas for development

Planning and assessment for children under two years old.Kaimahi are yet to consider plans that provide specific learning experiences for children under two years old. The kōhanga reo has a growing roll of infants.Learning experiences should help guide their development.Assessment for children under two years old is not yet fully established.

Kaiako reporting.While kaiako report to whānau each month, they should focus more on children’s experiences and developments and less on administration and operational matters.

Kōhanga reo building.Whānau need urgent support to address property issues and relocation of their building.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Rangitihi completed an EROKōhanga Whānau Assurance StatementandSelf-Audit Checklist. In these documents they haveattested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends the whānau address the following:

  • plan for the specific needs of the under two year olds and regularly monitor and assess their progress
  • document the actions for each objective in the strategic plan and regularly monitor and report on the achievement of these goals
  • seek support from the NKRT to ensure the kōhanga reo building is suitable for children.

6 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
