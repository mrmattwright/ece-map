1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Tōmairangi ki Mangapapa, ki Tūranga-nui-a-Kiwa. Kua toru tau ngā tamariki me te whānau e noho ana ki tō rātou whare hou. He manawa whakahī tō te whānau mō tēnei whakatutukitanga. Ka whai pānga nui te ingoa Tōmairangi ki te whānau. He mauritau, he rangimārie hoki te wairua ki waenga i te whānau me ngā kaimahi, ā, kua whai pānga nui, kua whai hua anō hoki tēnei te kōhanga reo me ngā tamariki. Ko te reo Māori te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

Ka rongo ngā tamariki i ngā tino tauira hiranga o te reo Māori, mai i ngā kaimahi, ngā pākeke, ngā kuia me ngā kaumātua. Ka aro nui, ka whai wāhi atu, ka āta whakarongo hoki ngā tamariki ki te whānau me ngā kaimahi. He hakune ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, ka whai pūtake hoki, ā, he maha ngā wā ka kōkirihia e ngā tamariki. E tino puāwai ana te reo Māori. Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga o tētahi taiao o te reo Māori e whai pūtake ana.

He pakari ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere i ngā kaupapa hihiri mā ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi rātou i tētahi tino angamahi hei ārahi i ngā tukanga whakamahere. He matawhānui ngā mahere, ā, ka whai hua ki te ārahi i te whakapuakitanga o ngā kaimahi i ngā kaupapa ki ngā tamariki. Ka aromātai, ka whakaaro huritao hoki i ia te wā mō ngā āhuatanga o te hōtaka. Nā tēnei, he nui ngā hua ka puta ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. He pakari ngā mahi aromatawai, ā, e tohu matawhānui ana i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki, me ā rātou akoranga.

Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i tētahi hōtaka akoranga e whakatairanga ana i te āta ngana atu, te āta whai wāhi atu hoki a ngā tamariki. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whakatau whakaritenga e pā ana ki ā rātou ake akoranga, te tūhura i ngā tūmomo ariā, me te mahi takitahi, ki ngā rōpū iti rānei, ngā rōpū nui rānei. He pai ngā rauemi ki ngā taiao akoranga o roto, o waho hoki, ā, he tino whānui ngā tūmomo rauemi e wātea ana ki ngā tamariki. He pai te riterite o ngā taumahi ā-kaupapa e ārahina ana e ngā pākeke, me ngā kaupapa hoki ka kōkiri kētia e ngā tamariki nā runga i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. He pai ngā tauneketanga ki waenga i ngā tamariki rātou ko ngā kaimahi. Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki te tau, te mārie, te māhaki hoki e whakatauirahia e ngā kaimahi me te whānau.

Ka noho pūmau ngā kaumātua, ngā kuia, ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki ngā mahi o te kōhanga reo. Ka whai hua ngā tukanga me ngā pūnaha e whakahaere ana i ngā whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga me ngā whakapuaki whakaaro mō ngā mahere ā-kaupapa. He manawa whakahī tō te whānau mō te kōhanga reo me ā rātou akoranga i roto i te reo Māori. He kaha te whanaungatanga ki waenga i te whānau, ngā kaimahi, me ngā tamariki.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga reo (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Reo

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Reo

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whai huatanga o ngā mahi hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki?

Ka tino whai hua te tautoko i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki, ā, he whakawhanake ana te māia me te mātau o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka noho pūmau te whānau ki te whakarato i ngā tino whai wāhitanga ako mō ā rātou tamariki, mā tētahi aronga nui hoki ki te reo Māori.

Mana Reo. Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao o te reo Māori e atawhai ana, e tautoko ana hoki. He ngāwari te āta tohutohu, te āta ārahi hoki o ngā kuia, ngā kaumātua, me ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki kia whakamahi rātou i ngā hanganga rerenga kōrero e tika ana, me te whānuitanga anō hoki o ngā kupu. He ngāwari tā rātou poipoi i ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki. E āhei ana ngā kaimahi me ngā kaumātua ki te whakawhānui ake i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero a ngā tamariki, kia noho hoki ngā akoranga hei wheako ngahau, hei wheako pārekareka hoki. Ka whakamahia te tākaro hei huarahi mō te ako i te reo. Ka mātakitaki te whānau i te āhua o te whakapakari haere o ngā tamariki, i roto i ō rātou mōhiotanga ki te reo Māori, me tā rātou whakamahinga anō hoki i te reo.

Ka whakahihiri ngā kaimahi i te ako reo me ngā mahi ōkawa mā ngā tamariki. He māmā te whakawhiti kōrero a ngā tamariki ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki te whānau hoki mā ngā mahi ā-ringa, ngā waiata, me ngā whakawhitinga kōrero. Ka tahuri, ka aro nui hoki ngā tamariki ki ngā kaimahi i aua tauneketanga, i aua taumahi hoki. He tau te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā huihuinga ōkawa, tae atu ki te karanga, te whaikōrero, te hongi, me te harirū. Kua tau ai ngā tamariki hei tangata whenua. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, ā, he pai hoki te māramatanga o ngā tamariki kōhungahunga.

Te hōtaka akoranga. E tino puāwai ana ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, ka whai pūtake hoki. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. Ka ako, ka tākaro hoki rātou ki ngā wāhi o roto, o waho, me te nui hoki a ngā rauemi. He pukumahi ngā kaimahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki, ā, he whakamōwai hoki. Ka whai pūtake ā rātou whakawhitinga kōrero ki ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa. Kua waihangatia te hōtaka hei poipoi i ngā āheinga me ngā reanga o ngā tamariki katoa. He pai te riterite o ngā taumahi ā-kaupapa e arahina ana e ngā pākeke, me ngā taumahi hoki e kōkirihia ana e te tamaiti, nā runga i ōna ngākau nuitanga. He pai ngā tauneketanga ki waenga i ngā tamariki rātou ko ngā kaimahi.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whai hua o te whānau ki te whakahaere i tō rātou kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki?

Ka whai hua te whānau ki te whakahaere i tō rātou kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo me te tari. Ka whai hua te tuhi me te aroturuki i ngā pūnaha me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga a te whānau me te whakahaere tari. Arotakengia ai aua tukanga, aua tuhinga hoki i ia te wā. Ka kitea ki te āta pūrongo puta noa i ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga reo, te kaha o te arotake whaiaro me te whakamōhio i te whānau ki tēnā e kōkirihia ana. Ka whai wāhi mārika te whānau ki ngā mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. He tākare tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki te whakamahere i te hōtaka, ā, he maha ngā wā ka haere rātou ki ngā akoranga o te reo Māori. He pai te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Te whakamahere. He ihupuku te whakamahere a ngā kaimahi i ngā hōtaka e tino whai hua ana mā ā rātou tamariki. He pai te tuhituhi me te whai kiko o ngā mahere o te wā roa me te wā poto. He rawaka ngā aratohu o roto i ngā mahere hei hāpai i te whakapuakitanga o ngā kaimahi i ngā kaupapa ki ngā tamariki. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā kaupapa e whakatairanga ana, e whakamana ana hoki i te ao Māori.

Te aromatawai. He matawhānui ngā whakaritenga aromatawai, ā, he tika te whakatakoto haere i te ahunga whakamua me ngā mahi angitu a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā rautaki ka aroturuki i te ahunga whakamua me ngā whakatutukitanga a ngā tamariki. Ka whakatakotohia ngā kitenga o ia rā, inā koa, ko te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ia tamaiti. Kei ngā pūkete a ia tamaiti ngā whakaahua me ngā tauira o ngā mahi toi o ia kaupapa. Kua āta tāpirihia ngā kōrerorero e whai hua ana, e whai mōhiotanga anō hoki. Ko tētahi whakamārama o te ahunga whakamua me ngā whakatutukitanga a ia tamaiti i āta tuhia ai, ā, e noho wātea ana hei tirohanga mā te whānau.

Te aromātai hōtaka. He matawhānui te aromātai hōtaka, ā, he painga hoki mā ngā akoranga me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia wiki ki te whakawhiti kōrero, te aromātai, me te tuhi haere i ō rātou whakaaro huritao mō te hōtaka. Ko te ahunga whakamua me ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki tētahi aronga nui i ngā kaimahi e whakaaro huritao ana ki ā rātou mahi whakaako me te whai huatanga o te hōtaka o ia rā.

Te wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Me arotake te whānau i ngā kawenga a te kaiwhakahaere tari. I tēnei wā, e whakatutuki ana te kaiako i te tūranga o te kaiwhakahaere tari, me āna kawenga tonu hei kaiako. Ko te whakakaha ake i te tautoko o te whānau ki te whakahaere i te tari, ka whakawātea anō hoki i te kaiako ki te arotahi mārika ki āna mahi ki te taha o ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa
  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga:

Kia whakawhiti kōrero, kia arotake anō hoki te whānau i te tūnga o te kaiako me te whānui ake o āna mahi hei kaiwhakahaere tari.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e whā.

Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu mō ngā Ratonga Arotake Māori (Te Uepū ā-Motu)

19 Pipiri 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tomairangi is located in Mangapapa, Gisborne. The children and whānau have been in their new premises for three years. The whānau are proud of this achievement. The name Tomairangi is of major significance to whānau. There is an aura of peace and calm among whānau and kaimahi which has a significant and positive effect on the kōhanga reo and children. Te reo Māori was the focus for this review.

Children are exposed to excellent te reo Māori models including kaimahi, pakeke, kuia and kaumatua. Children are responsive, attentive and listen carefully to whānau and kaimahi. Conversations are deliberate, meaningful and often lead by the children. Te reo Māori is flourishing. Children are immersed in a rich, purposeful te reo Māori environment.

Kaimahi confidently plan exciting kaupapa for children. They use a sound framework to guide planning processes. Plans are comprehensive and effectively guide kaimahi when they deliver kaupapa to children. There is regular evaluation and reflection of the programme. This leads to significant benefits to children’s learning and development. Assessment practices are robust and provide a comprehensive account of children’s te reo Māori development and learning.

Kaimahi provide a learning programme that promotes purposeful engagement and participation by children. Children are able to make decisions about their own learning, explore different concepts and work in small or large groups or independently. The indoor and outdoor learning environments are well resourced with a rich selection of equipment that are readily available to children. There is a good balance of kaupapa activities that are guided by adults and child initiated activities that are based on their interests. Interactions among children and kaimahi are positive. Children reflect the settled, calm and quiet manner of kaimahi and whānau.

Kaumātua, kuia, kaimahi and the whānau are committed to the work of the kōhanga reo. Procedures and systems for the management of the kōhanga reo operations are effective. Whānau take an active role in decision making and regularly contribute to kaupapa planning. Whānau speak proudly about the kōhanga reo and their te reo Māori classes. There is a strong sense of whanaungatanga among whānau, kaimahi and children.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children atthe kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Reo

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.
  • ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Mana Reo

Background

How effectively is children’s te reo Māori development supported?

Support for children’s te reo Māori development is highly effective and children are developing as confident, competent speakers of te reo Māori.

Areas of good performance

The whānau arecommittedto providing the best learning opportunities for their children with a strong focus on te reo Māori.

Mana Reo. Children learn te reo Māori in a warm, supportive environment. Kuia, kaumātua and kaimahi gently advise and guide children to use correct sentence structures and a wide range of vocabulary. They support children’s interactions in a non invasive way. Kaimahi and kaumatua are able to build on children’s conversations and make learning a fun and enjoyable experience. Play is used as a vehicle for language learning. Whānau observe how children are gaining confidence in their knowledge and use of te reo Māori.

Kaimahi make language learning and formalities exciting for children. Children communicate easily with each other and whānau through actions, drama, waiata and discussions. Children are attentive and responsive to kaimahi during these encounters and activities. Children participate comfortably in formal ceremonies including karanga, whaikōrero, hongi and hariru. Children are comfortable in their roles as tangata whenua. Children are confident speakers of te reo Māori while younger children have a good understanding.

Learning programme. Children’s learning is flourishing and purposeful. Children are able to follow their own interests. They learn and play in well resourced indoor and outdoor areas. Kaimahi are hard workers who work with children in an unassuming way. Their conversations with children are always purposeful. The programme is designed to cater for the abilities and ages of all children. There is a good balance of kaupapa activities that are guided by adults and child initiated activities that are based on their interests. Interactions among children and kaimahi are positive.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How well does the whānau manage their kōhanga reo to benefit of their children?

The whānau effectively manage their kōhanga reo in the best of their children.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management and administration. Whānau management and administration systems and procedures are effectively documented and monitored. These processes and documents are regularly reviewed. Full reporting across all areas of kōhanga reo operations reflect strong self review and keep whānau informed of what is happening. Whānau participate fully in the running of the kōhanga reo. They eagerly share in programme planning and often attend te reo Māori classes. The kōhanga reo is managed well in the interests of children.

Planning. Kaimahi conscientiously plan highly effective programmes for their children. Long and short term plans are well written and detailed. Plans contain sufficient guidelines to assist kaimahi when they deliver kaupapa to children. Children experience kaupapa that promote and value te ao Māori.

Assessment. Assessment practices are comprehensive and appropriately record children’s progress and successes. Kaimahi use a range of strategies to monitor children’s progress and achievement. Daily observations, particularly of each child’s te reo Māori development are recorded. Individual profiles contain photos and kaupapa art samples. These are carefully annotated with valuable and informative comments. A personal account of each child’s progress and achievement is successfully documented and made available for whānau.

Programme evaluation. Programme evaluation is comprehensive and benefits children’s learning and development. Kaimahi meet on a weekly basis to discuss, evaluate and document their programme reflections. Children’s progress and learning is a strong focus when kaimahi reflect on their teaching practice and the effectiveness of the daily programme.

Area for Development

Whānau need to review the role of the administrator. Currently the kaiako fulfils the role as administrator along with her duties as kaiako. Greater whānau support in administration is likely to allow the kaiako to focus on her work with kaimahi and children.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an EROLicensed Kōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013andSelf-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.
  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends:

the whānau discuss and review the role of the kaiako and her additional role as administrator.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in four years.
