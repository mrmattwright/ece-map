1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pāhou ki te marae o Te Pāhou, ki Manutuke. Ko Rongowhakaata te iwi, ko Ngāti Maru rāua ko Ngāi Tāwhiri ngā hapū. He nui ngā akoranga ka whakapuakihia e te whānau o te kōhanga reo e hāngai ana ki te taiao Māori, tae atu ki te whenua me te moana. Ka arotahi te whānau ki ngā mateā akoranga o ā rātou tamariki.

He pai te atawhai o ngā kaimahi ki ā rātou tamariki. Nō nā tata tonu te whānau i whakapakari ake anō ai i ō rātou kaimahi whakaako, me ana mema. Kātahi anō rātou ka whakatū i tētahi kaiako e mau ana i te Tohu Whakapakari. He nui ngā wawata o ngā kaimahi me te whānau mō ā rātou tamariki.

Nā ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki waenga i te whānau me te rōpū a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori, i whanakehia te pātai aromātai e whai ake nei.

He pēhea te whai hua o te kōhanga reo ki te poipoi i ngā tamariki hei Māori?

Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i te hōhonutanga o ngā wheako ako reo. Haere ai ngā tamariki ki te marae, ā, ka taunekeneke rātou ki ētahi atu i reira, ka whai wāhi anō hoki ki ngā kaupapa. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i ngā tūmomo reo ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki.

Kua pakari ake te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, whai muri i te pūrongo arotake o mua. Kua noho pūmau te whakahaere i ngā pūtea ki te kōhanga reo. He kaha te whakahaeretanga o ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. He pai te whakahaere i ngā kaimahi, ā, he pakari hoki tō rātou ngākau whakapuke. Kua whai pānga pai tēnei ki te ngā mahi whakamahere me te aromātai.

E toro atu ana ngā kaimahi ki te tautoko tonu o tō rātou kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe, kia whanake ake ai tō rātou mōhiotanga ki te aromatawai. He rangatahi tonu te whānau, ā, me whai tautoko rātou kia pakari ake tā rātou whakahaere i te kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi, ko:

  • Mana Whenua.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Whenua

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whai hua o te whānau ki te poipoi i ngā tamariki hei Māori?

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo Māori e kōrerohia ana ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki. Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori mā ngā karakia, ngā pepeha, ngā whakapapa, me ngā kōrero o te hapori. Ka aro pai ngā tamariki ki ngā wā waiata, ā, he hihiri aua wā e whakanui ana i tō rātou tuakiri, e hāpai ana hoki i tō rātou whakawhanaketanga reo. He rerekē ngā taumata reo a ngā tamariki, kei te mārama ētahi i te reo Māori, ā, kei te kōrero ētahi i te reo Māori.

Whai wāhi atu ai ngā tamariki ki ngā kaupapa i te marae i ia te wā. Ka kite, ka taunekeneke hoki rātou ki ngā kaumātua o te marae i ngā pōwhiri me ngā whakatau. Ka kite ngā tamariki i ngā tūmomo mahi ā tēnā me tēnā i ngā wā ōkawa. Ka tautoko rātou i ō rātou kaumātua mā te waiata i ngā waiata kīnaki. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā tūmomo horopaki ako ki ngā huihuinga i te marae.

He whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga me te mahi auaha. He maha ngā wā ka whakamau rātou i ngā kākahu pohewa, me te whakaari i ā rātou tino kiripuaki. Ka tautoko ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki i a rātou e tākaro ana mā te whakarato i ngā kākahu pohewa me te tuku pātai ki a rātou. He whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti haere ki waenga i ngā wāhi o roto, o waho hoki, me te whakapā atu ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi e tika ana mō ō rātou reanga, ō rātou āheinga hoki. He pārekareka ki a rātou te tūhura i ngā tūmomo aria me te ako, mai i ō rātou hoa.

Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmomo karakia puta noa i te rā. Nā ngā karakia, ka tau te mārie ki ngā āhuatanga o te rā, ā, ka whai pānga anō hoki ki te whakatau i ngā tamariki. Ka whakanuia ngā atua Māori i ngā tūmomo wā ako, wā tākaro hoki. Kua waihangatia e ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki tētahi taiao moana me tētahi māra hoki ki roto i tō rātou kōhanga reo, hei hāpai i ngā tamariki ki te ako i a Tangaroa rāua ko Rongomātāne. Ko tētahi aronga nui o te hōtaka, ko ‘Ko au’, ā, ka kitea ki ngā mahi a ngā tamariki e whakaatuhia ana ki ngā pakitara. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako.

He tau ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki ā rātou mahi o ia rā. He pai te whakahaere a ngā kaimahi i ngā mahinga. Ko ngā mahinga tētahi tino wā ako mō ngā tamariki. He kaha ngā kaimahi ki te tautoko i ngā pēpi i roto i ngā tūmomo mahinga. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te whanaungatanga me te manaakitanga.

He tino kaha te tautoko i ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga. Ahakoa he iti te kōhanga reo, he atawhai, he muramura, he ratarata hoki te āhua o tōna taiao. He maha ngā momo wāhi ako hei whiringa mā ngā tamariki. He rūma moe mō ngā tamariki nohinohi. He wāhi anō hoki mō ngā tākaro whakamārie. He taiao haumaru te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He tākare te ako a ngā tamariki i te ao Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i tētahi tino tauira whakamahere e mau nei i ngā mahere o te wā roa me te wā poto, hei ārahi i te whakapuakitanga o te hōtaka. Kei roto hoki i ngā mahere, ko ngā pou e whā o Te Korowai me ngā kaupapa. Ka aromātaitia, ka whakahāngaitia hoki ētahi tūmomo wāhanga o te hōtaka akoranga i ia te wā, hei whakatutuki i ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Ka hui tahi ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakawhiti kōrero mō ētahi o ngā tūmomo wāhanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Nā tēnei, ka puta ake ngā putanga hua ki ngā tamariki.

He tino aronga tā te arotake whaiaro me ngā mahere rautaki ki ngā tamariki. Whakawhiti kōrero ai ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ia te wā, mō ngā tūmomo wāhanga o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. E āhei ana rātou ki te tāutu i ngā wāhanga kaha, me te whakatutuki i ngā momo take ka pupū ake pea i ā rātou hui ā-whānau. Ka āta whakatakotohia ki te mahere rautaki ngā tino kaupapa matua e hāngai ana ki Te Ara Tūāpae. Aroturuki ai, pūrongo ai hoki te whānau i ia te wā, i te whakatutukitanga o ā rātou kaupapa matua rautaki. Ka hāngai te arotake whaiaro me ngā mahere rautaki ki te whakapai ake mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake

Kāhore ngā wheako akoranga a ngā tamariki e tino aroturuki rawatia ana e ngā kaimahi. E mōhio ana ngā kaiako, he mahi tonu hei whai atu i roto i ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki. Kāhore ngā mahi aromatawai o tēnei wā e tohu ana i ngā taumata ka whāia tonuhia e ngā tamariki, i ngā kōrerorero rānei e whakamārama ana i tēnā e ākona ana, i te ahunga whakamua rānei. He iti noa ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori.

Kāhore i te pakari te whakahaere a te whānau. Kei te whakatutuki te kaiwhakahaere tari i āna kawenga me āna mahi. Ko ētahi whaea rangatahi anake o te whānau e whai wāhi atu.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Kīhai ngā tirohanga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i tāutu i ētahi wāhanga e āwangawangatia ana.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori kia toro atu whānau ki te tautoko o te kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe, hei āwhina i a rātou ki te:

  • whakawhanake, te whakatinana hoki i ngā mahi aromatawai, hei aroturuki, hei aromatawai hoki i te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki
  • whakakaha ake i te whai wāhi atu a te whānau, me te akiaki hoki i ngā mema ki te whai wāhi mātātoa ki ngā tūranga whakahaere, ki te whakatau hoki i ngā whakaritenga.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pāhou is a part of Te Pāhou marae, Manutuke. The tribe is Rongowhakaata and the hapū are Ngāti Maru and Ngāti Tawhiri. The kōhanga whānau include lost of learning about the natural environment including the land and sea. The whānau focus on the learning needs of their children.

Kaimahi are caring towards their children. The whānau have been through a period of rebuilding their teaching staff and its members. They have recently appointed a kaiako who is a holder of Whakapakari. The kaimahi and whānau have great aspirations for their children.

Discussions between whānau and the ERO team resulted in the development of the following evaluative question.

How well does the kōhanga reo nurture the children as Māori?

Children receive rich, language learning experiences. Children go to the marae where they interact with others and are a part of what is happening there. Children are being exposed to different forms of language and in different settings.

The kōhanga reo management has strengthened since the last review report. Financial management of the Kōhanga reo has been consistent. The health and safety policies & procedures are being managed strongly. The personnel management has been administered well with the staff moral being strong. This has impacted positively on the planning and evaluation.

The kaimahi are seeking further support from their district kaupapa kaimahi to develop their assessment knowledge. The whānau is young and will need support to get better at managing the kōhanga reo.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Whenua.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Mana Whenua

Background

How well do whānau nurture the children as Māori?

Areas of good performance

Children hear te reo Māori being spoken in different contexts. Children are immersed in te reo Māori through karakia, pepeha, whakapapa and local stories. Children respond well to waiata sessions which are exciting, acknowledge their identity and assist their language development. Children are at different levels with some understanding te reo Māori while others are speaking te reo Māori.

Children regularly join in on the marae. They observe and regularly interact with the elders of the marae during pōwhiri and whakatau. Children see others doing different things during formal events. They support their elders by singing waiata. Children experience different learning contexts at marae events.

Children have opportunities to follow their interests and be creative. They often dress up and act out the roles of their favourite characters. Kaimahi support children while they play by providing them with costumes and questioning them. Children have opportunities to move between the indoor and outdoor areas where they have access to a range of resources suitable for their ages and abilities. They enjoy exploring different concepts and learning from each other.

Children participate in different karakia throughout the day. Karakia provide a positive tone for the day and help settle children. Atua Māori are acknowledged through the various learning and play times. Kaimahi and children have created an indoor beach environment and garden to enable children to learn about Tangaroa and Rongomaitane. ‘Ko au’ is a major programme focus and is evident in the children’s work which is displayed on walls. Children enjoy learning.

Children comfortably participate in their daily routines. Kaimahi manage routines well. Routines are valuable learning times for children. Kaimahi support babies well during different routines. Children benefit from whanaungatanga and manaakitanga.

Children are well supported in their learning. Although the kōhanga reo is small the environment is warm, colourful, and friendly. There are a number of different learning areas for the children to choose from. There is a room for the young to sleep. It also provides a place for some to play quietly. The kōhanga reo is a safe environment for the children.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

Children eagerly learn about te ao Māori. Kaimahi use a strong planning model comprising of long and short term plans to guide programme delivery. The four ‘pou’ of Te Korowai are included in plans along with kaupapa. Different parts of the learning programme are regularly evaluated and modified to meet children needs and interests. Kaimahi meet with whānau to discuss different parts of the learning programme. This produces positive outcomes for children.

Self review and strategic planning has a clear focus on children. Kaimahi and whānau regularly discuss different parts of the kōhanga reo operations. They are able to identify areas of strength and address any issues that may arise at their whānau meetings. The strategic plan clearly sets out specific priorities that align to Te Ara Tuapae. Whānau regularly monitor and report on the achievement of their strategic priorities. Self Review and strategic planning are focused on improvement for children.

Areas for development

Children learning experiences are not monitored well by kaimahi. Kaiako acknowledge there is still a need to work to on children’s profiles. Existing assessment practices do not include children’s next steps or annotations that describe what learning or progress is taking place. There is limited information about te reo Māori development.

Whānau management is not strong. The administrator is fulfilling her responsibilities and duties. Whānau involvement is limited to a few young mothers.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends the whānau seek support from the district kaupapa kaimahi with the following:

  • develop and implement assessment practices to monitor and assess children’s progress
  • improve the level of whānau participation and encourage members to take an active role in management roles and decision making.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
