Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāhauranga ki Tawa. He tau ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao reo Māori. He whakaritenga whai ake mā te whānau te mahi tika i ngā mahere me ngā arotakenga.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāhauranga i roto i te rua tau. 

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāhauranga ki Tawa. He tauhou te nuinga o ngā mema o te whānau ki te kōhanga reo me tōna kaupapa. Kātahi anō ka whakanohohia ētahi o ngā kaimahi ki ngā tūranga i te kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori?

He tau ngā tamariki ki tētahi wāhi reo Māori, engari kāhore e taea e rātou te āta whakapuaki whakaaro mā te reo Māori. Ko te kanukanu haere o te whare me ngā rawa e pā tōraro ana ki te ako a ngā tamariki.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira 

He tau ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao reo Māori. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i te whakamahinga o te reo Māori me ōna tikanga i ngā taumahi ako ōkawa. Ka taki ngā tamariki i ngā karakia me ngā pepeha, ā, he pārekareka ki a rātou te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo waiata e hāpai ana i tā rātou ako i te reo. He whai wāhitanga mō rātou ki te ārahi me te whakapuaki i ō rātou mōhiotanga. Ka whakamahi ngā tamariki i te reo e mōhio ana rātou.

Kua waia ngā tamariki ki ngā mahinga o ia rā. He pai te whakahaeretanga o ngā whakawhitinga i ngā tamariki e nuku ana ki ia taumahi. He pai te aro atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā kupu whakamihi me ngā kōrero akiaki. He ngahau, he pārekareka hoki ō rātou hononga ki ō rātou hoa, ngā kaimahi, me te whānau. He tau, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i te ao whānui. Ka haere rātou ki ngā wāhi e noho whakahirahira ana ki a rātou, ko ō rātou whānau. Ka ngākaunui ngā tamariki ki te rongo i ngā kōrero e pā ana ki te ao e noho nei rātou. Ka hoki atu anō rātou ki ō rātou wheako, ā, ka kōrero i ngā āhuatanga i noho whakahihiri ai ki a rātou. E whakawhanake ana te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E tīmata ana ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo. Kāhore ngā tamariki e rumakina ana ki te reo Māori. E mōhio ana te whānau, e tika ana kia whakapiki ake rātou i te whakamahinga a ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te reo Māori, hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. Kāhore e taea e ngā tamariki te āta whakapuaki whakaaro mā te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

Ka mātua whai whakaaro te whānau ki te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki. Ka kitea ki ngā meneti o ngā hui ā-whānau, kua āta tuhia ngā whakawhitinga kōrero e pā ana ki ngā take ki ngā rawa, ā, kua whakapuakihia ngā āwangawanga mō te haumaru me te waiora o ngā tamariki. Kāhore e taea te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki te āta whakatau.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

I tēnei wā, e whakahaere ana ngā kaimahi i ngā whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga o ia rā, tae atu anō hoki ki ngā mahi hei whakatinana i te hōtaka akoranga mō ngā tamariki. Me mātua aro te whānau ki te waha anō i ngā kawenga me ngā haepapa rā.

He ngoikore ngā mahi aromatawai me te aromātai hōtaka. He iti noa te māramatanga o ngā kaimahi ki te pūtake o te aromatawai me te aromātai. Kāhore ngā aromatawai e tohu ana i te ahu whakamua o ia tamaiti, kāhore hoki e whakamahia ana hei whakatinana i ngā mahere hōtaka. Kāhore te ako a ngā tamariki e āta tautokona ana.

Kāhore ngā kaimahi me te whānau e aromātai ana i te whai huatanga o ā rātou whakaritenga. Kāhore ngā mōhiohio arotake whaiaro e hono ana ki ngā putanga mō ngā tamariki. Kāhore anō kia oti ngā taumahi auau o te arotake whaiaro e mātua whai pānga ana ki ngā tamariki, ā, kua hapa rānei te wā. Ko ētahi o aua tuhinga ko ngā kirimana mahi a ngā kaimahi, me ā rātou arohaehae. Kāhore i te rawaka ngā mōhiohio hei tautuhi i te whakawhanaketanga ngaio e tika ana mō ngā kaimahi. Kāhore ngā matea o ngā tamariki e poipoia ana.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te ratonga ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

He nui ngā āhuatanga hanganga o te whare me ngā rawa o te kōhanga reo hei whakatika ake. He nui rawa ēnei nama mā te whānau. 

Me tahuri te whānau ki te:

3.1 whakapau kaha ki te whakatairanga i te hauora pai me te haumaru i ngā tamariki, me te whakapau kaha anō hoki ki te whakakore i ngā maiki, ā, me whakatika, me tiaki hoki i ia te wā i ngā papa, ngā rawa me ngā taputapu  
[R 46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS1-33 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008] 

3.2 whai whakaaro ki te tokomaha me te āhua anō hoki o ngā reanga o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo, me te āta whakarite i te whare me ngā rawa o te kōhanga reo kia rawaka ai, kia tika ai anō hoki te wāhi mō te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi, me te whakataka kai, te kai, te moe, te putu, te haere ki te whare paku me te horoi, ā, me rawaka, me tika anō hoki te whakamahana, te kā o ngā raiti, te hoihoi, te hā ora, me ngā taputapu ka hāpai i te whakatinanatanga tika o te marautanga me ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te haumaru me te hauora 
[R 45 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; PF 1-38 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008] 

3.3 whakarite i te whakamahere, te whakatinana, me te aromātai a ngā kaimahi i te marautanga i whakatakotohia ai mō ngā kōhanga reo, ā, i waihangatia ai hoki hei whakatairanga i te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga, mā te whakarato i ngā wheako akoranga e poipoi ana i ngā ngākaunuitanga ako, ngā pūmanawa, me ngā āheinga hoki o ngā tamariki; me te whakarato i tētahi taiao ako pai mō aua tamariki.
[R 43 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; C1-13 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008] 

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā:

Kia mahi ngātahi te whānau me te kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe ki te whakawhanake me te whakatinana i tētahi mahere mahi ka ārahi i te whakatutukitanga a te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā take i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāhauranga is in Tawa, Wellington. Children are comfortable in a te reo Māori environment. Whānau planning and evaluation is an area for further development.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāhauranga will be within two years

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāhauranga is in Tawa, Wellington. There are a number of whānau new to the kōhanga reo and its kaupapa. Some kaimahi are newly appointed to their positions in the kōhanga reo.

2 The Review Findings

To what extent do children use te reo Māori? 

Children are comfortable in a te reo Māori environment nevertheless they are not able to express themselves fully in te reo Māori.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are comfortable when exposed to te reo Māori. Kaimahi encourage the use of te reo me ōna tikanga during formal learning activities. Children recite karakia, pepeha and enjoy a range of waiata that supports their language learning. They have opportunities to lead and share what they know. Children use the language that they are familiar with.

Children are familiar with the daily routines. Transitions are managed well as children move from one activity to the next. Children respond well to praise and encouragement. They have fun and enjoy meaningful relationships with peers, kaimahi and whānau. Children are settled and happy.

Children learn about the wider world. They visit places of interest that are important to them and their whānau. Children enjoy hearing stories about the world around them. They revisit their experiences and talk about what was interesting to them. Children are developing an awareness of their environment.

Key Next Steps

Children are at the early stages of language learning. Children are not immersed in te reo Māori. Whānau recognise the need to increase te reo Māori use by kaimahi and whānau to support children’s language development. Children are not able to express themselves fully in te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau give priority to the health and safety of children. Whānau minutes show a well-documented communication trail relating to property issues that express concerns about the safety and wellbeing of children. Whānau understand the importance of children’s health and safety.

Key Next Steps

Kaimahi currently manage the day to day operations and administration tasks as well as implementing the learning programme for children. It is important that whānau assume some of these responsibilities.

Assessment and programme evaluation practices are weak. Kaimahi have a limited understanding of the purpose of assessment and evaluation. Assessment information does not show how each child is progressing and it is not used to inform programme planning. Children’s learning is not well supported.

Kaimahi and whānau do not evaluate the effectiveness of their practices. Self-review information is not linked to outcomes for children. Regular self-review activities that have are direct impact on children are incomplete or overdue. These include employment contracts and kaimahi appraisal. There is insufficient information to determine appropriate professional development for kaimahi. Children’s needs are not catered for.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

The kōhanga reo building and premises require major structural repairs. These are beyond the financial capability of the whānau.

The whānau must ensure that:

3.1 all reasonable steps are taken to promote the health and safety of children including all reasonable precautions to prevent accidents, and that ensuring the premises, facilities, and other equipment on those premises are kept in good repair and are maintained regularly
[Regulations 46 (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS 1-33 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

3.2 the kōhanga reo premises and facilities, having regard to the number and age range of the children attending the premises, provide sufficient and suitable space for a range of activities, facilities for food preparation, eating, sleeping, storage, toileting, and washing, and sufficient and suitable heating, lighting, noise control, ventilation, and equipment to support appropriate curriculum implementation, and safe and healthy practices
[Regulations 45 (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; PF 1-38 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

3.3 kaimahi plan, implement, and evaluate the prescribed curriculum for kōhanga reo that is designed to enhance children’s learning and development through the provision of learning experiences that responds to the learning interests, strengths, and capabilities of enrolled children; and provides a positive learning environment for those children.
[Regulations 43 (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; C1-13 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

That the whānau work closely with district kaupapa kaimahi to develop and implement an action plan to address issues identified in this report.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāhauranga is in Tawa, Wellington. Children are comfortable in a te reo Māori environment. Whānau planning and evaluation is an area for further development.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngāhauranga will be within two years
