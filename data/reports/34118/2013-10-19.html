The Review Findings

Children are involved in interesting and active play. Their exploration of the environment is well supported by teachers. Children participate confidently, relate well together and have fun. Older children are very aware of the younger children and are generally protective and loving. Te reo MƒÅori and tikanga are integrated, particularly through waiata and karakia, and children are learning to share their pepeha with teachers and their peers. Self review could also be used to develop a strategic plan to guide the long-term development of the service to meet changing needs and circumstances.

Centre leaders show a strong commitment to the goals and philosophy of the service. The team leader has established professional support networks and uses guidance and mentoring processes to build the capacity of other staff. He also works with staff to develop their confidence and encourage emergent leadership.

The Waahi Whaanui organisation provides sound policy and procedural guidelines in the areas of finance, property and employment. The performance management process has supported teacher development. Consideration is being given to including aspects of the Registered Teacher Criteria to enhance the emphasis on best practice in teaching.

Key Next Steps

ERO and centre management agree that outcomes for children can be further enhanced by:

  • re establishing a programme planning process that displays and documents intended teaching strategies to support children‚Äôs interests and extending learning
  • completing a strategic plan that identifies centre development priorities. This needs to be linked to the outcomes of curriculum self review, centre operations and teacher development
  • involving centre leadership in setting the annual budget to allow for planned expenditure that clearly reflects centre needs and priorities
  • establishing an appropriate process for appraising the team leader.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Whanui Early Learning Centre completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.
