1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakaari ki te hapori o Brockville, ki Ōtepoti. Ka tautoko ngā kaumātua o tēnei tāone i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga ki te kōhanga reo. He hononga tō te nuinga o ngā mema o te whānau ki te kōhanga reo.

He nui ngā wero kua pā ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He nui hoki te rerekē haere o ngā kaimahi me te whānau i tēnei wā. E tautoko ana Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i te whānau me ngā kaiako i roto i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga, me ngā mahi e tika ana i roto i te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki. Mai i te whai wāhi mai me te tautoko mai o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo. Kua piki ake te tatauranga tamariki, ā, kei te pai ake hoki te whai wāhi mai o te whānau. Kei te kite te whānau i ngā painga ka hua ake ki te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te kite i te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo?

Ahakoa kei reira ētahi wheako mā ngā tamariki kia mōhio ai rātou ki a rātou anō me ngā taunekeneke e tika ana ki te kōhanga reo, kāhore anō rātou kia whakaatu i te whakawhanaketanga o tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga ki tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao ako ātaahua i āta whakaritea ai. Ka whakamahia ngā whakaaturanga o ngā whakapapa hei whakapakari ake i te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou whānau me ō rātou iwi. Ka ako ngā tamariki i a Matariki, ā, he pai te whakaatu i ā rātou mahi. E whakawhanake ana te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

He pakari te tūhura o ngā tamariki i te ao māoriori, tūturu hoki e noho nei rātou. Ka hāpai te hōtaka ngāwari i ngā tamariki me tā rātou whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga ki roto, ki waho hoki i te kōhanga reo. Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi ngā tino whakaritenga e pā ana ki te karakia, te wā kai, te horoi ringa, me ngā whakawhitinga atu ki ngā tūmomo taumahi. He tau ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

I te wā o te arotake i tāutu Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te maha o ngā āwangawanga e pā ana ki te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakaae ngātahi te whānau me ngā māngai o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, me tahuri wawe ki te whakapai ake i ngā āhuatanga ka whai ake:

  • Kāhore i te wātea ki ngā tamariki te rawaka o ngā tūmomo rauemi ako. Ka noho kē te nuinga o ngā rauemi ki tētahi kāpata
  • Kāhore ngā tamariki e taunekeneke ana ki ngā pākeke. Ahakoa he wā ka noho tahi ngā pākeke ki ngā tamariki, pērā i te wā kai, kāhore ngā pākeke e whakapuaki ana i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero hei rautaki ako, hei huarahi rānei ki te whakawhanake i te reo
  • Kāhore i ngā tamariki ngā taumahi ako e whai pūtake ana. Kāhore ngā hiahia ako o ia tamaiti e kitea ana e ngā kaiako, ā, kāhore e whakamahia ana hei whakamōhio i tētahi hōtaka akoranga ka whai pūtake. He ngoikore te ārahitanga i te hōtaka akoranga, me te arotahi ki te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga
  • Kāhore ngā tamariki e kai ana i ngā kai hauora, i ngā inu hauora rānei. Me aro atu ki te hauora i roto i ngā kai me ngā inu a ngā tamariki
  • He iti noa te tautoko mō ngā tamariki kia whanake ai tō rātou āheinga ki te kōrero i te reo Māori
  • Kāhore ngā tamariki e whakaongaongatia ana hei ākonga
  • Kāhore ngā tamariki e whakawhanake ana i ngā hononga papai ki waenga i a rātou anō.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

E ai ki te whānau, kei te noho pūmau rātou ki te whakatairanga ake i te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga me ngā whakahaeretanga. Mā te tautoko o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, kua whakatūngia e rātou tētahi mātanga kaiako, hei hāpai i a rātou ki te whakatutuki i ngā whakapaitanga e tika ana. E tīmata ana te whānau ki te hāpai i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo i ia rā.

Kei te tīmata te whānau ki te whakawhanake me te whakatinana i ngā pūnaha me ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga. I tēnei wā, e tautoko ana, e ārahi ana hoki Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i ngā mema hou o te whānau. Ko te arotake whaiaro a te whānau me ngā mahere rautaki kei te tīmatanga noa o ngā whakawhanaketanga. Ka whakamōhiotia ngā mema mō ngā kaupapa me ngā whakanuitanga i whakamaheretia ai, me ētahi o ngā āhuatanga ka rerekē haere pea. Ka āta whakaatuhia ngā meneti o ngā hui. E mōhio ana te whānau, e tika ana kia nui ngā whakapaitanga hei tika ake anō hoki te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E mōhio ana te whānau, kei te tīmatanga noa rātou o tā rātou whakapakaritanga ake. He mea nui kia:

  • arotahi rātou ki te whakapakari ake i tō rātou āheinga
  • ū tonu te tautoko o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo me ā rātou whakangungu mā ngā mema katoa o te whānau, i roto i ngā āhuatanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, kia whai hua ai ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau
  • whakatinana rātou i ngā mahi whakahaere, tae atu ki te arotake whaiaro a te whānau, i runga anō i te tūtohinga a tō rātou whānau me Te Korowai.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. I te wā o te arotake i tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga e whai ake nei, kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture.

Hei whakapai ake i ā rātou whakaritenga i tēnei wā, me tahuri te whānau ki te:

  • whakamahere, te whakatinana, me te aromātai i Te Whāriki o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, hei whakatutuki i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo matea, ngā tūmomo ngākau nuitanga hoki o te ako o ngā mokopuna, me ngā matea o te whānau, ngā horopaki anō hoki o te ao [R 43 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; (C1-4) Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakarite i te whakatairanga ake o ngā whai wāhitanga ako katoa o ngā tamariki me tō rātou atawhai, mā te whai pūtake, te poipoi, me te kounga kairangi o ngā taunekeneke katoa, kia whai kounga anō ai hoki ngā hononga ako [R 43 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; (C1-4) Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakarite i te whiwhinga o ngā tamariki hei ākonga i ngā hōtaka akoranga o Te Whāriki e whai pānga ana ki a rātou me te katoa o ngā matea, ngā āheinga, me ngā ngākau nuitanga, me te whakamana i te whai wāhi mai o te whānau ki te ako a ngā mokopuna [R 43 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; (C7-10) Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakawhanake, te whakatinana, me te whakapūmau i te haere tonutanga o tētahi tukanga arotake whaiaro, hei hāpai i te kōhanga reo ki te whakapūmau me te whakapai ake i te kounga o te mātauranga me te atawhai [R 43 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; (GMA6) Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakatinana me te whakapūmau i ngā mahi pūmanawa tāngata e tika ana [R 43 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; (GMA7) Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakarite i te whakapūmautanga ki ngā mahere ā-tau e tika ana mō te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. [R 43 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; (GMA8) Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakaari ki te hapori o Brockville, ki Ōtepoti. Ahakoa kei reira ētahi wheako mā ngā tamariki kia mōhio ai rātou ki a rātou anō me ngā taunekeneke e tika ana ki te kōhanga reo, kāhore anō rātou kia whakaatu i te whakawhanaketanga o tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga ki tō rātou kōhanga reo.

E mōhio ana te whānau, he nui tonu ngā mahi ki te whakatairanga ake i te kounga o ngā putanga ako mā ngā tamariki. E whakarato ana Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i te tautoko ngaio me te ārahitanga ngaio.

4 Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakaari i roto i te kotahi tau.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

21 Mahuru, 2015

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakaari is in the Brockville suburb of Dunedin. Local kaumātua support te reo Māori and tikanga in the kōhanga reo. Most whānau members have connections to the kōhanga reo.

The kōhanga reo management has been challenging. There have been significant changes in staff and whānau during this time. Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) is supporting the whānau and kaiako in all areas of management, and with what should be happening in the learning programme for children. Since TKRNT intervention and support, the roll has increased and whānau participation is improving. Whānau are feeling positive about the future of the kōhanga reo.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children show a sense of belonging in their kōhanga reo?

Although children have some experiences to help them understand who they are and how they should interact in the kōhanga they do not yet show that they have developed a sense of belonging to their kōhanga reo.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children learn in a well-organised and attractive learning environment. Displays about whakapapa are used to build children’s knowledge of their whānau and iwi. Children learn about Matariki and their work is nicely displayed. Children are developing a sense of belonging to the kōhanga reo.

Children confidently explore the natural and physical worlds around them. The flexible programme supports children to follow their own interests both indoors and outside. Kaimahi have established clear routines for karakia, kai time, hand washing and transitions between activities. Children are secure in their environment.

Key Next Steps

During the review ERO identified a number of concerns about the learning programme. The whānau and TKRNT representatives, agree that the following require urgent attention:

  • Children do not have easy access to a reasonable selection of learning resources. Most resources are stored away in a cupboard
  • Children do not interact with adults. While there are times when adults are with children, including kai time, adults do not promote conversations as a strategy for learning or language development
  • Children do not have purposeful learning activities. The individual learning preferences of children go unnoticed by kaiako and are not used to inform a meaningful learning programme.
  • There is a lack of leadership in the learning programme to focus on children’s learning and development
  • Children do not eat healthy food and drink. A focus on healthy eating and drinks for all children
  • Children have limited support to develop as speakers of te reo Māori
  • Children are not stimulated learners
  • Children are not developing positive relationships with each other.

Planning and Evaluation

The whānau state that they are committed to raising the quality of the learning programme and operations. With the support of TKRNT, they have appointed an experienced kaiako to help them bring about the necessary improvements. Whānau are beginning to support the daily operation of the kōhanga reo.

Whānau are in the early stages of developing and implementing management systems and practices. TKRNT currently provides support and guidance to the new whānau members. Whānau self review and strategic planning are in the early stages of development. Members are informed about planned events, celebrations and possible changes that may occur. Minutes for meetings are clearly displayed. Whānau acknowledge the significant improvements that are required to improve the kōhanga reo for their children.

Key Next Steps

The whānau recognise that they are at the very early stages of rebuilding. It is important that:

  • They focus on building their capability.

  • TKRNT provide ongoing sustained support and training to all whānau members across all areas of kōhanga reo management for effective whānau management

  • They implement management practices, including whānau self review, in line with their whānau charter, and Te Korowai.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements. During ERO’s onsite review, the following areas of non-compliance were identified.

To improve current practice the whānau must:

  • plan, implement, and evaluate the prescribed Te Kōhanga Reo Early Childhood Curriculum Framework, Te Whāriki, for mokopuna learning informed by their diverse needs and interests, whānau needs and life contexts [R43 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008, (C1-4) Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]
  • ensure that all children’s learning opportunities and their care is enhanced through meaningful, nurturing and high quality interactions for quality relationships for learning [R43 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008, (C1-4) Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]
  • ensure that children as learners receive Te Whāriki learning programmes that are inclusive, responsive to all needs, abilities and interests, and empowering whānau participation in mokopuna learning [R43 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008, (C7-10) Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]
  • develop, implement and maintain an ongoing process of self-review to help the kōhanga reo maintain and improve the quality of its education and care [R43 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008, (GMA6) Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]
  • implement and maintain suitable human resource management practices [R43 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008, (GMA7) Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]
  • ensure all the required annual planning is maintained for the management of the kōhanga reo for children. [R43 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008, (GMA8) Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakaari is in the Brockvale suburb of Dunedin. Although children have some experiences to help them understand who they are and how they should interact in the kōhanga they do not yet show that they have developed a sense of belonging to their kōhanga reo.

The whānau recognise that there is still much more work to do to raise the quality of learning outcomes for children. The Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust is providing professional support and guidance.

4 When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakaari will be within one year.
