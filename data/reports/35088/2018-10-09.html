1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rau Aroha ki Matamata, i Waikato. Ko te ingoa o te kōhanga reo e tohu ana i te aroha mutunga kore o te Atua. Ko Ngāti Hauā te iwi, ā, ka whakamanahia, ka whakanuia hoki e te whānau ngā hononga ki Te Kīngitanga. He tino hononga anō hoki tō te kōhanga reo ki Te Kōhanga Reo o Kutia, ā, ka tautoko tētahi i tētahi. Ka whakawhiti atu te nuinga o ngā tamariki ki Te Kura o Waharoa me Te Wharekura o Te Rau Aroha.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakapuaki i ō rātou ake whakaaro, ō rātou tuakiri, me ō rātou tūrangawaewae?

He pakari ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou ake whakaaro, ō rātou tuakiri, me ō rātou tūrangawaewae.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E manaakitia ana ngā tamariki, ā, he pai ā rātou taunekeneke ki te taha o ētahi atu. Ka whakapuaki ngā kaimahi i te aroha, te awhi, me te manaaki i a rātou e tiaki ana i ngā tamariki. Ka āta whakarite rātou kia tau ngā tamariki i te tīmatanga tonu o ia rā. Ka akiaki rātou i ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou kare ā-roto. Ka rongo rātou i ō rātou matea, ā, ka aro pū ki a rātou. He pārekareka ki ngā pēpi te noho tahi me ngā tuākana i ngā wā whāriki. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tēina me ngā tuākana ki te ārahi i ngā wāhanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakamihi ngā tamariki i tēnā me tēnā o rātou, mō ā rātou mahi angitu me ā rātou mahi ki te whakamātau i ngā āhuatanga hou. Ka hāpai tētahi kaimahi mātanga i ngā tamariki hei tautoko ake i roto i ngā akoranga kia pakari ai tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki te hōtaka akoranga. Kei te mauritau ngā tamariki, ā, ka noho whakaute hoki ki te taha o ētahi atu.

E rumakina ana ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori me ngā tikanga o Waikato Tainui. Ka kōrero anake ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori ki ngā tamariki, ā, ki waenganui hoki i a rātou anō. Ka whakatauira rātou i ngā hanganga reo me ngā rerenga kōrero e tika ana, ā, ka whakamahi i ngā rautaki -pērā i te tāruarua, te waiata, me te reo ā-ringa – ki te hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. Ko ngā mahi ā te rēhia - inarā tonu ko te haka - e whakamahia ana hei whakawhānui ake i ngā puna kupu me te māramatanga ki te reo ā-iwi me ngā hītori o Waikato Tainui. Ka taea e ngā tamariki tuākana te tū māia ki te ārahi i ngā wā whāriki mā te reo Māori. He mauritau ngā tamariki ki te mihi me te whakautu pātai mā te reo Māori. Ka whakaatu ngā pēpi i tō rātou māramatanga ki tēnā e kōrerohia ana mā ō rātou reo ā-tinana. E whanake ana ngā tamariki hei ākonga tūmāia i a rātou e tīmata ana ki te ako i te reo Māori.

E whakamanahia ana te ahurea me te tuakiri o ngā tamariki. Ka āta whakarite ngā kaimahi i te whakaurutanga mai o ngā mātauranga o te hapori ki te hōtaka akoranga. Ka taea e ngā tamariki te taki ngā karakia o te Pai Mārire e whai pānga nui ana ki a Waikato Tainui. Ka ako rātou i ngā whakataukī me ngā tongi o Te Kīngitanga. Ka taea e ngā tamariki ngā pūmanawa o ia ariki te whakahua ake. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou pepeha me tēnā anō hoki o te kōhanga reo. Ka poipoi ngā kuia i te ako a ngā tamariki i a rātou e whakapuaki ana i ngā kōrero tuku iho e pā ana ki te hapori me ngā whenua. Ka āta whakarite ngā kaimahi i te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmomo kaupapa i te marae o tō rātou hapori. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki tō rātou hapori, ā, ka haere ki te noho tahi me ngā kaumātua. Ka tae atu rātou ki ngā huihuinga katoa, pērā i ngā poukai me te Koroneihana. E ako ana ngā tamariki i a rātou anō, hei uri o Waikato Tainui.

E whai pūtake ana te āhua o ngā mahi taunekeneke a ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao ako. Ka āta whakarite ngā kaimahi kia noho wātea ai ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi. He wāteatea te wāhi o roto, ā, he ātaahua anō hoki ngā whakaaturanga e tohu ana i ngā akoranga me ngā mahi toi a ngā tamariki. He nui ngā taputapu ki te papa tākaro rahi, ā, he wāteatea anō hoki ngā tū wāhanga hei whakawero i te whakawhanaketanga ā-tinana, ā-tangata tonu o ia tamaiti. Ka āta tiakina ngā māra huawhenua, ā, nā tēnei tūāhuatanga ngā māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te taiao e whakapuaki ake ai. Ka whakahonohono ngā kaimahi i ngā akoranga ki ngā atua Māori, kia kite ai ngā tamariki i ō rātou ake whai hononga anō hoki ki te taiao māoriori. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te tūhura haere i te wāhi o waho e whakatairangatia ana, e whakapuakihia ana hoki ō rātou whakaaro pohewa ki te taha o ētahi atu. He ngākau titikaha ō ngā tamariki ki te ako.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Kei te whānau ētahi tukanga ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o tō rātou kōhanga reo. Kei te mahere rautaki ngā whāinga, ngā mahinga, me ngā putanga. Hui ai te whānau i ia te wā. He auau te arotake i ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga i ia te wā. Kua whakatakotohia ngā rehita me ngā akoako hōneatanga, ā, ka tuhia hoki. Kua whakapakari anōtia e te whānau tō rātou whakawhirinaki, tō rātou tūmāia hoki ki te whakapūmau i te kounga o tētahi taiao e hāpai ana i te ako a ngā tamariki i ō rātou tuakiri me ō rātou tūrangawaewae.

He tukanga ā ngā kaimahi mō te whakamahere hōtaka me te aromatawai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. E hono ana ngā mahere ki Te Whāriki, ā, e whai pānga ana hoki ki ngā uaratanga me ngā whakapono o te kōhanga reo. Kua whai wāhi atu ngā kaimahi ki te ako ngaio e whakaratohia ana e ngā kaimahi ā-rohe o te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, kia pakari ake ai tō rātou tūmāia me tō rātou āheinga i roto i ngā mahi whakamahere. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā pūkete mā ia tamaiti, mā tōna whānau anō hoki. E whakaatu ana aua pukapuka aromatawai i tēnā e ākona ana e ngā tamariki, me ngā kōrero ake e pā ana ki ō rātou tuakiri me ō tūrangawaewae. Ka āta tuhia te ako me te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Me whakapakari ake i te aromātai hōtaka. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e kapo atu ana, e tuhi ana hoki i ā rātou whakawhitinga kōrero, hei whakatakoto i te āhua o te hōtaka ki te whakatutuki i ngā matea o ngā tamariki katoa. Kua tae ki te wā, kia tuhi, kia whakapakari hoki ngā kaimahi i ā rātou tuhinga e pā ana ki te aromātai hōtaka. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā tētahi hōtaka akoranga e aroturukitia ana, e tuhia ana hoki i ia te wā.

He mea nui kia noho mārama te whānau ki ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga mahi. Kua whakapā atu rātou ki te tautoko o te kaimahi ā-rohe hei hāpai i a rātou ki te whakahāngai anō me te whakatakoto ake anō hoki i ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga mahi. Engari, kāhore anō te whānau kia whakatū i ngā tāngata ki aua tūranga matua hei āta whakarite i te whakahaeretanga o ngā pūnaha katoa. Tē taea te kounga o te atawhai me te mātauranga te whakapūmau mō ngā tamariki katoa, ina kāhore anō aua tūranga matua kia whakakīia.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakapakarihia te aromātai i te hōtaka
  • whakapakarihia tō rātou āheinga ki te whakapūmau i te kounga o te atawhai me te taiao ako mō ngā tamariki.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rau Aroha i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rau Aroha is situated in Matamata, Waikato. The name of kōhanga reo translates to the never-ending love of God. Ngāti Hauā is the local iwi and the whānau value and celebrate the links to Te Kīngitanga. The kōhanga reo has a strong relationship with Te Kōhanga Reo o Kutia and they support for each other. Most children will attend Te Kura o Waharoa and Te Wharekura o Te Rau Aroha.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children express themselves, who they are and where they come from?

Children confidently express themselves, who they are and where they come from.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are cared for and have positive interactions with others. Kaimahi express aroha, awhi, manaaki and tiaki towards children. They ensure that children are settled as they begin each day. Kaimahi encourage children to share their feelings. They listen and respond to their needs. Pēpi enjoy being included alongside teina and tuakana during mat times. Kaimahi encourage teina and tuākana to lead parts of the learning programme. Children praise each other for their successes and for trying new things. A specialist kaimahi supports children who require learning support to confidently participate in the learning programme. Children are settled and are respectful of others.

Children are immersed in te reo Māori and tikanga of Waikato Tainui. Kaimahi only speak te reo Māori to children and each other. They model correct language structures and sentences, and use strategies such as repetition, waiata and reo ā-ringa to support children’s language development. Kapa haka, in particular haka, is used to build vocabulary and understanding relating to Waikato Tainui dialect and history. Older children are able to confidently lead mat times in te reo Māori. Children are comfortable to mihi and respond to questions in te reo Māori. Babies communicate their understanding of what is being said through their body language. Children are developing as confident early learners of te reo Maori.

Children’s culture and identity is affirmed. Kaimahi ensure the learning programme incorporates local knowledge. Children are able to recite Pai Mārire karakia that are specific to Waikato Tainui. They learn whakataukī and tongi of Te Kīngitanga. Children can recall attributes of each of ariki. Kaimahi support children to share their pepeha and that of the kōhanga reo. Kuia nurture children’s learning as they tell stories of the local area and land. Kaimahi ensure children participate in events at their local marae. Children are active in their community and visit the elderly. They attend all celebrations such as poukai and Koroneihana. Children are learning about who they are as descendants of Waikato Tainui.

Children interact with their learning environment in meaningful ways. Kaimahi ensure children have access to a range of resources. A spacious indoor area attractively displays children’s learning and art works. The large playground is well equipped with generous spaces to challenge children’s physical and personal development. Well maintained vegetable gardens grow children’s understanding of nature. Kaimahi make links between learning and atua Māori so that children can see how they connect to the natural environment. Children enjoy exploring outdoors where their imagination is enhanced and shared with each other. Children are confident learners.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau have some processes in place to manage kōhanga reo operations. The strategic plan includes goals, actions and outcomes. Whānau meet regularly. Policies and procedures are regularly reviewed. Registers and drills are in place and recorded. Whānau have rebuilt their trust and confidence to maintain a quality environment where children learn who they are and where they come from.

Kaimahi have processes for programme planning and assessment of children’s learning. Planning is linked to Te Whāriki and reflects the values and beliefs of the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi have participated in professional learning provided by district kaimahi of Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust to build their confidence and capability in planning. Kaimahi provide individual profile books for children and their whānau. These assessment books show what children learn, who they are and where they come from. Children’s learning and progress is well documented.

Key Next Steps

Programme evaluation requires strengthening. Kaimahi do not capture and document their discussions to show how well the programme is meeting the needs of all children. It is timely for kaimahi to record and strengthen their documentation around programme evaluation. Children benefit from a learning programme that is regularly monitored and recorded.

It is important for whānau to be clear about their roles and responsibilities. They have accessed support from the district kaimahi to assist them to refocus and restructure their roles and responsibilities. However, whānau have not yet appointed key people to these roles to ensure all systems are managed. Maintaining quality care and education for all children cannot be assured while key roles are not filled.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • strengthen programme evaluation

  • build their capability to sustain a quality care and learning environment for children.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rau Aroha will be in three years.
