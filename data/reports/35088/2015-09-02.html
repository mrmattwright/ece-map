Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rau Aroha ki Matamata. Ko Ngāti Hauā te iwi. Ka whakawhiti whakaaro ngā tamariki mā te reo Māori. Ka whai rātou i ngā tikanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka ako, ka whai wāhi atu hoki ngā tamariki ki ngā tikanga Māori. Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e manaaki ana, e poipoi ana hoki i a rātou.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rau Aroha i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rau aroha ki Matamata, ki te rohe o Waikato. Ko Ngāti Hauā te iwi o taua takiwā.

E noho ngākau nui ana te whānau ki te tautoko me te whakanui i Te Kīngitanga. Ka ako ngā tamariki i Te Kīngitanga me tōna whai pānga ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou iwi hoki. Ka whai wāhi aua tikanga ki te whakamana i te tuakiri me te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki.

Ka tino whai wāhi atu ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, me ngā whakaritenga o ia rā. Noho mai ai hoki ngā mātua i ētahi wā ki te tautoko i ā rātou tamariki me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te whakamahi a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga o te kōhanga reo ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo?

Ka whakamahi ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki te whakawhiti whakaaro, ā, kei te mōhio hoki rātou ki ngā tūmomo tikanga o te kōhanga reo mā roto i ngā whakamahinga o ia rā.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka harikoa ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana, e whakaongaonga ana hoki i a rātou. Kua whakaritea e te whānau me ngā kaimahi tētahi taiao hei wāhi ako mō ngā tamariki, ā, he pārekareka ki a rātou te whai wāhi atu ki te whānau. Haere ai ngā whānau ki te kōhanga reo i ia te wā, ā, ka whai pūtake te maha o ā rātou mahi āwhina. Ka tino mātakina ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakaratohia rātou ki ngā taumahi ako me ngā wheako e whakahihiri ana. He tau, he hāneanea hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka whakamahi, ka rongo hoki ngā tamariki i te reo Māori, puta noa i te rā. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki ako reo papai ki te taha o ngā tamariki. Ka pānui pukapuka rātou ki ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakamahi rātou i ngā pikitia ki te whakapuaki i ngā patapatai me ngā whakawhitinga kōrero. Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao reo Māori e tautoko ana i a rātou.

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ngā kawa o te kōhanga reo, me ētahi āhuatanga o ngā tikanga Māori, pērā i te whakatau manuhiri. Ka āta whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā mahinga o ia rā, tae atu ki te taki i ngā karakia i te tīmatanga me te mutunga o te rā me ngā wā kai. He pakari te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā tikanga Māori tuku iho.

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i te pai o ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga. He atawhai, he aroha hoki tō ngā hononga o ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki ngā tamariki. Ka kitea te pakari, te mauritau hoki o ngā tamariki. Ka whai hua te whakamahinga a ngā kaimahi i ngā kōrero whakamihi, hei whakanui i te whanonga pai o ngā tamariki. Ka akiaki rātou i te āta whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi ako me ngā wheako. He pakari ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā tuākana me ngā tēina. Ka whakatauira ngā tuākana i te whakatutuki haere o ngā mahinga o ia rā, me te āhua hoki e tūmanakohia ana. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te noho ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore i te rawaka te maha o ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura takitahi. Kāhore ngā tamariki e tautokona ana ki te whakatau i ā rātou whiringa mahi, ā, he maha ngā tūmomo taumahi, ngā akoranga hoki a ngā tamariki ka kōkirihia e ngā kaimahi. Me whai whakaaro ake ki te waihanga i tētahi hōtaka akoranga e hāngai pū ake ana ki te tamaiti.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. Ka tino whai wāhi te whānau ki ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo i ia rā. Haereere ai rātou ki ngā hui i ia te wā, ā, ka āta whai wāhi atu. Ka whai hua te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te mahi ngātahi, hei whakatau i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kei te ako tonu, kei te whakatinana tonu te whānau i ngā pūnaha e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro. Ka tino whakahaerehia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Ka mahi ngātahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere i ngā hōtaka akoranga. Ka tino mōhiotia aua mahere te whānau, ā, ka hāpai i ngā kaimahi ki te whakapuaki i ngā whai wāhitanga ako whakahihiri mō ngā tamariki. Ko ngā kaupeka o te tau me Te Whāriki o te Kōhanga Reo kei te tūāpapa o ngā mahi ki te whakamahere hōtaka. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā hōtaka ā-kaupapa i āta whakamaheretia ai.

Kua whakawhanakehia e te whānau tētahi mahere rautaki hei hāpai i tā rātou ahunga whakamua. Ka arotahi Te Ara Tūāpae me ngā whāinga o te tūtohinga ki te whakapakari i te āheinga reo Māori o te whānau me ngā kaimahi. Kei te whakahāngai tonu te whānau i tā rātou aronga rautaki ki Ngā Pou e Whā. He aronga nahanaha tā te whānau ki te whakatutuki i tā rātou ahunga whakamua. Ko te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou waiora kei te pūtake o ngā whakataunga a te whānau.

Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te maha o ngā tūmomo rautaki ki te aru i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Kei ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki nohinohi, te whānuitanga o ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori. Ka whakaatuhia ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki te whanake haere o ngā mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki ngā tikanga Māori, ā, he kohinga whakaahua, he tauira mahi toi anō hoki e tohu ana i ngā wheako akoranga. He pai te noho mōhio o ētahi o ngā mātua ki ngā wheako akoranga a ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore ngā mahi aromatawai me ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki katoa e whakawhanakehia ana ki te taumata e tika ana. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e tino arahina ana i roto i te pūtake o ngā aromatawai me ngā pūkete a ia tamaiti.

Kāhore te aromātai hōtaka i āta whakaritea ai. Ahakoa ka hui ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhiti whakaaro mō te whakahaeretanga o tamariki kōhanga reo, kāhore rātou e āta arotahi atu ana ki te arotake i te kounga me te whānuitanga o ngā taumahi ako me ngā wheako akoranga a ngā tamariki. He iti noa ngā kōrerorero e pā ana ki te angitu o te hōtaka akoranga, ki ngā wāhanga rānei hei whakapai ake, hei whakatairanga ake i te hōtaka akoranga me tōna hāpai i te kōkiri takitahi a ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rau Aroha is in Matamata, in the Waikato region. Ngāti Haua is the local iwi.

The whānau is committed to supporting and celebrating the Kīngitanga movement. Children learn about the Kīngitanga and how it relates to them and their iwi. These practices help affirm children’s identity and sense of belonging.

Whānau members are highly involved in kōhanga reo management and day-to-day operations. Parents attend the kōhanga reo from time to time to support their children and the kaupapa of the kōhanga reo.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children use te reo Māori and tikanga in their kōhanga environment?

Children use te reo Māori to communicate and familiar with kōhanga reo tikanga through their daily routines.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are happy in a nurturing and stimulating environment. Whānau and staff have established an environment where children learn and enjoy being part of the whānau. Whānau attend kōhanga reo regularly and help out in a variety of meaningful ways. Children are well supervised and they are provided with interesting learning activities and experiences. Children are settled and comfortable in the kōhanga reo.

Children use and hear te reo Māori throughout the day. Staff use effective language learning strategies with children. They read books to children and use the pictures to ask questions and to prompt discussion. Children are immersed in a supportive te reo Māori environment.

Children know about protocols of the kōhanga reo and about some aspects of tikanga Māori including welcoming visitors. Children participate fully in daily routines including reciting karakia at the start and end of the day and at kai time. Children confidently participate in traditional Māori protocols.

Children experience positive interactions and relationships. Staff and whānau have warm, loving relationships with children. Children show they are confident and comfortable. Staff use praise to good effect to acknowledge positive behaviour in children. They encourage children to participate fully in learning activities and experiences. Tuakana, taina relationships are well established. Tuakana model how to manage daily routines and what is expected of them. Children enjoy being at kōhanga reo.

Key Next Steps

Children are not provided with enough opportunities to explore independently. Children are not supported to make choices about what they will do and activities often rely on the staff members taking the lead and directing children’s learning. Further consideration should be given to creating a learning programme that is more child-centred.

Planning and Evaluation

The whānau manages the kōhanga reo well. Whānau are highly involved in the day to day operation of the kōhanga reo. They attend meetings regularly and participate fully. Whānau and staff work effectively together to make decisions about the management of the kōhanga reo. The whānau continues to learn about and implement self review systems. Children are provided with a well managed kōhanga reo.

The whānau and staff work collaboratively to plan learning programmes. These plans are well known by the whānau and help to guide staff to deliver interesting learning opportunities for children. Seasons of the year and Te Whāriki o te Kōhanga Reo are the basis for programme planning. Children experience well planned kaupapa based programmes.

The whānau has developed a strategic plan to help guide their future direction. Te Ara Tūāpae and charter goals focus on building te reo Māori capability of whānau and staff. The whānau continues to align their strategic direction to Ngā Pou e Wha. The whānau has a planned approach to realising their future direction. Children’s learning and wellbeing is central to whānau decision making.

Staff use a variety of methods to track children’s learning. There is a range of information about children’s progress and development in using te reo Māori in the younger children’s profile books. Information is presented about children’s developing knowledge of tikanga Māori and there are a selection of photos and art samples which show learning experiences. Some parents are well informed about children’s learning experiences at kōhanga reo.

Key Next Steps

Assessment practices and children’s profile books are not consistently developed to a similar standard. There is little guidance for staff members about the purpose of assessment and individual profiles.

Programme evaluation is not well established. While staff members meet to discuss kōhanga reo operations, they do not adequately focus on reviewing the quality and range of children’s learning activities and experiences. There is limited discussion about the successes of the learning programme and or the areas where improvements can occur to enhance the learning programme to better support independence.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rau Aroha is in Matamata. Ngāti Haua is the local iwi. Children use te reo Māori to communicate. They follow the tikanga of the kōhanga reo. Children learn about and participate in tikanga Māori. Children are immersed in a caring, nurturing environment.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rau Aroha will be in three years.
