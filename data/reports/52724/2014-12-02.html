1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kua tapaina te ingoa o Te Kōhanga Reo o Kura Mahiao, hei tohu maumahara mō tōna kaiwhakaū, arā, ko tōna kuia, kaiako hoki i whakapapa atu ai ki Kaiwhāiki me Taranaki. Ko te kōhanga reo ā-kāinga nei e tautokona e ngā kaumātua. Ka noho pūmau te reo Māori me ōna tikanga hei tino aronga ki te whānau. He tokomaha ngā mema o te whānau e hono atu ana ki te marae o Kaiwhāiki, ki te awa o Whanganui. I te wā o te arotake, he hiahia tō te whānau kia mōhio:

‘He pēhea rawa te whakaatu a ngā tamariki i tō rātou māia, tō rātou mōhiotanga anō hoki ki tō rātou ake tuakiri ahurea, ā rātou ake taonga tuku iho hoki?’

He pai te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki, ā, ka kitea tō rātou māia, tō rātou mōhiotanga anō hoki ki tō rātou ake tuakiri ahurea me ā rātou ake taonga tuku iho.

Ka whakarato te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā wheako akoranga e whakaongaonga ana, e whai pūtake ana hoki, ā, ka āta tohua hoki te ao Māori. Kua roa nei ngā kaumātua e whai wāhi mātātoa ana ki te kōhanga reo, ā, e whakatairanga ana tēnei i ngā painga mō ngā tamariki me te whānau. He pai hoki te whānau ki te whakahaere i ō rātou wawata me ngā kaupapa rautaki matua e ahu mai ana i te tino tirohanga ngātahi.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi, ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

‘He pēhea rawa te whakaatu a ngā tamariki i tō rātou māia, tō rātou mōhiotanga anō hoki ki tō rātou ake tuakiri ahurea, ā rātou ake taonga tuku iho hoki?’

He pai te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki, ā, ka kitea tō rātou māia, tō rātou mōhiotanga anō hoki ki tō rātou ake tuakiri ahurea me ā rātou ake taonga tuku iho.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako i tō rātou reo, ngā tikanga, me ngā taonga tuku iho. Ka whai take, whakaongaonga hoki ngā wheako ako reo. He tau, he pakari hoki rātou ki te taki i ngā karakia, ngā waiata, ngā pātere me ngā pepeha. Ka ngākau nui, ka hihiri hoki ngā tamariki i a rātou e whakamahi ana i te reo Māori, ā, ka kitea tō rātou manawa whakahī.

Ka kitea te tino hononga o ngā tamariki ki te whānau o tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki te whānuitanga o ngā wheako ako e akiaki ana i tā rātou tūhura, tā rātou hononga anō hoki ki ā rātou taonga tuku iho. Ko te hitori, te whakapapa, ngā tikanga me ngā whakapono, kua āta whakatōngia ki ngā akoranga mā ngā tamariki i ia rā. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whakapuaki ki ētahi atu ko wai rātou, nō hea rātou.

Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te manaaki me te aroha i roto i ngā taunekeneketanga. Ko te whai wāhi mai o ngā koroua e whakamaumahara ana i ngā uaratanga o te whakaute, te atawhai, te aroha, me te manaaki ki te whānau me ngā tamariki. Ka kite ngā tamariki i te whai pānga nui o ngā kaumātua ki tō rātou ao me te ao Māori. Ka tino aro ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki. Ka poipoi rātou i te māia me te ngākau nui o ngā tamariki ki te ako. Ka kite i te whakaute, te manaaki, me te whakaaro nui o ngā tamariki mō ō rātou hoa, te whānau, me ngā kaumātua.

Ka whakaongaongatia ngā tamariki hei tūhura i te ao e noho nei rātou. He pai ngā rauemi, he pai hoki te whakaritenga o ngā taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo. He kāinga te kōhanga reo, ā, ka āta kitea ngā taonga tuku iho a ngā tamariki. He maha hoki ngā whakaahua o te kuia i tū ai hei kaiako, o ētahi atu kaumātua, o ngā taonga tuku iho hoki e karapoti ana i ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka āta whakaarohia ngā matea ako, ngā ngākau nuitanga, me ngā āheinga o ngā tamariki ki te whakamaheretanga, te whakaritenga, me te whakapuakitanga hoki o te hōtaka. Ka whai hua ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te whakarerekē haere, te tīni haere hoki i ā rātou mahere, i runga i te rerekē haere o ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. He auau te whakaaro huritao, te arotake anō hoki o ngā kaimahi me te whānau mō te whai pānga o te hōtaka ki ngā tamariki me ā rātou akorang. Ka tino whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga, ā, ka kitea tō rātou māia ki tamariki tūhura i te ao e noho nei rātou.

Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā huarahi māmā, papai hoki ki te whakatakoto haere i ngā akoranga me ngā wheako a ngā tamariki i a rātou e noho ana ki te kōhanga reo. Ka kite ngā mātua i ngā tūmomo wheako me ngā putanga ako a ā rātou tamariki.

E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki e tō rātou whānau, ki te ako i ā rātou taonga tuku iho me tō rātou tuakiri. Ko te waiora me ngā matea o ngā tamariki me te whānau te aronga o ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau. He whānau tata, he kaha hoki rātou ki te tautoko tētahi i tētahi. He pai te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo.

He mārama te tirohanga, te aronga rautaki hoki a te whānau. Arotakengia ai te whānau tā rātou ahu whakamua i ia tau. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā mōhiohio o te arotake whaiaro, hei whakamahere mō te tupu me te eke angitu. He pai te noho mōhio o ngā mema ki ngā whakawhanaketanga o mua a te kōhanga reo, ā, he pai tā rātou tūnga ki te kōkiri hoki i ngā kaupapa matua ka whai ake ā meāke nei.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake

Kua tāutuhia e te whānau, me whakapiki ake rātou i tō rātou āheinga i roto i te reo Māori. Kua tāutuhia taua kaupapa matua ki te mahere rautaki a te whānau. E akiaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i te katoa o te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te āta aro atu ki tēnei āhuatanga, hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki, me te whakawhanaketanga anō hoki o te whānau.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori, ā, e whakaae ana hoki te whānau ki te āta whakamahi i te arotake whaiaro, hei aroturuki i ā rātou kaupapa rautaki matua me te whānuitanga hoki o te āhua o ngā mahi me te tupu o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kura Mahiao is named after its founder, the late kuia-kaiako who had links to Kaiwhaiki and Taranaki. This home based kōhanga reo, has the full support of kaumātua. Te reo Māori me ōna tīkanga has always been a focus for whānau. Many whānau members affiliate to Kaiwhaiki Marae, on the Whanganui River. During the review whānau wanted to know:

‘How well do children show they are confident and know about their cultural identity and heritage?’

Children are developing well, and show, confidence and knowledge in their unique cultural identity and heritage.

The whānau and kaimahi provide stimulating and meaningful learning experiences that strongly reflect te ao Māori. The presence and active involvement of long standing kaumatua in the kōhanga reo enhances outcomes for children and whānau. Whānau effectively manage their aspirations and strategic priorities which stem from a strong shared vision.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

‘How well do children show they are confident and know about their cultural identity and heritage?’

Children are developing well, and show, confidence and knowledge in their unique cultural identity and heritage.

Areas of good performance

Children enjoy learning their language, customs and traditions. They have meaningful and stimulating language learning experiences. They are comfortable and confident carrying out karakia, waiata, pātere and pepeha. Children use te reo Māori language with passion, excitement and a sense of pride.

Children show a strong sense of belonging to their kōhanga reo whānau. They participate in a wide range of learning experiences that encourage exploration and connection to their cultural heritage. History, whakapapa, customs and beliefs are embedded well into daily learning for children. Children enjoy sharing with others about who they are and where they come from.

Children experience caring and loving interactions. The presence of the koroua reminds whānau and children of the values of respect, care, aroha and manaaki. Children see the important role kaumātua play in their lives, and te ao Māori. Kaimahi are highly responsive to children. They nurture children’s confidence and passion for learning. Children show respect, care and concern for their peers, whānau and kaumātua.

Children are stimulated to explore the world around them. Their kōhanga reo environments are well resourced and organised. The kōhanga reo is homely and strongly reflects children’s ancestral heritage. Numerous photos of the kuia-kaiako, other kaumātua, taonga tuku iho surround the children.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

Children’s learning needs, interests and abilities are well considered in programme planning, organisation and delivery. Kaimahi effectively adapt and modify their plans to respond to the changing needs and interests of children. There is regular reflection and review among kaimahi and whānau about the impact of programmes for children and their learning. Children are highly engaged in learning, and show confidence to explore the world around them.

Kaimahi use simple and effective methods to record children’s learning and experiences while at kōhanga reo. Parents can see the various experiences and learning outcomes for their children.

Children are well supported by their whānau to learn about their cultural heritage and identity. The wellbeing and needs of children and whanau are at the focus of whānau management practices. Whānau members are close and supportive of each other. There is good whānau involvement and participation in all areas of the kōhanga reo.

Whānau have a clear vision and strategic direction. Whānau review their progress annually. They use the self review information to plan for growth and success. Members are well informed about past kōhanga reo developments and are positioned well to pursue their future priorities.

Area for development

Whānau have identified the need to increase their te reo Māori capacity. This priority is identified in the whānau strategic plan. ERO encourages all whānau and kaimahi to give this significant attention to support the language development of children and whānau development.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends and the whānau agree to use self review well, to monitor their strategic priorities, and the overall performance and growth of their kōhanga reo.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)
