Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whare Aroha o Te Hūmārie ki Ōpōtiki. Ka whai wāhi nui Te Hāhi Ringatū ki te hōtaka o te kōhanga reo. Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tuākana tō rātou kaha ki te ārahi i ngā tikanga Ringatū i ia rā. E whakahaere ana te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei tino painga mō ngā tamariki.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whare Aroha o Te Hūmārie i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whare Aroha o Te Hūmārie ki Ōpōtiki. Nā te āhua o ngā whare, e wātea ana ki te tūhura o roto, o waho hoki. Mai i te arotake o mua, kua arotahi ki te whakahaumanu i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo me te whakatinanatanga o Te Whāriki.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e tohu i te tūmāia, i te mōhiotanga hoki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo?

He pakari ngā tamariki i runga i te mōhio ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae, ā, ki te taiao anō hoki o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka tino tūhonohono ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hoa me ngā pākeke. Taunekeneke ai rātou i ia te wā ki ngā mātua me ngā kaumātua e taetae atu ana ki te kōhanga reo i ia rā. Ko te whakapapa me te whanaungatanga kei te pūtake o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka tino mōhio ngā kaimahi ki ā rātou tamariki. Nā tēnei, ka toro atu ngā tamariki ki tō rātou noho āhuru, tō rātou tautāwhi anō hoki. He harikoa ngā tamariki.

Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te ako me te tākaro. Ka ako rātou i te reo me ngā tikanga Māori mā te Hāhi Ringatū. He pakari ngā tamariki tuākana ki te taki i te whānau i roto i ngā tikanga Ringatū, puta noa i te rā. He pakari te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki te taha o ō rātou hoa me ngā pākeke. Ka whakapūmau te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā wāhi akoranga o ngā tamariki, hei wāhi mō te reo Māori anake. E tino tautokona ana te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki.

E whakawhanake ana te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou tūnga ki te kōhanga reo me te hapori whānui. Ka whai wāhi atu rātou ki ngā kaupapa i te hapori me te rohe. He ngāwari ngā mahinga me ngā whakawhitinga ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakarato ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi ako. Ka whakatutuki rātou i ētahi o ngā āhuatanga e whai pānga ana ki tō rātou atawhai me tō rātou waiora. He ākonga māia, he ākonga pākiki hoki ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka whai kiko ngā mahere hōtaka, ā, ka hāngai hoki ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo me ngā mātauranga o te hapori. Ka whakamahere ngātahi ngā kaimahi ki te taha o te whānau. E tino māramahia ana Te Whāriki e te whānau me ngā kaimahi. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā tētahi hōtaka e āta whakamaheretia ana, ā, e āta whakaritea ana hoki.

Kei te tūtohinga ngā aratohu e tika ana mō te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He tino hononga o roto ki Ngā Pou e Whā me Te Ara Tūāpae. Kua āta tāutuhia ngā tūranga o te whānau me ā rātou kawenga mahi. Kua whakaritea ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. Ka whai hua ēnei hei aratohu mō ngā whakaritenga. E whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana i tō rātou waiora.

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i ngā whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo. He mahere rautaki tā te whānau, ā, kua whakahāngaitia tēnei ki Te Ara Tūāpae. Kua āta tāutuhia ngā whāinga me ngā tūmanako i whakatakotohia ai e te whānau. He mārama te mahere, ā, ka arotahi ki te rōnakitanga. Ka hāngai te arotake whaiaro ki ngā whakapaitanga. He riterite ngā hui ā-whānau, ā, ka hāngai ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki ngā whakahaeretanga i te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā whakataunga ki Te Whare Aroha o Te Hūmārie.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He ōpaki ngā mahi ki te aromātai i te hōtaka, engari he uaua ki te aroturuki i ngā tūmomo whakapaitanga ka whakatutukihia ki ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi, ki te hōtaka rānei.

E whanake tonu ana te aromatawai. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e arotahi ana ki te kapo atu i tēnā e mōhiotia ana e ngā tamariki, ki ngā mahi e āhei ana rātou ki te whakatutuki, ā, ki tēnā hoki e ākona ana e rātou. Kāhore te ako me te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki e aroturukitia ana i roto i te roanga o te wā.

Kāhore he mahere mahi tā te whānau hei tohu i te huarahi ka whāia kia tutuki ai ngā whāinga me ngā wawata o te mahere rautaki. Ka whakatakoto anō hoki tēnei i tētahi aronga mō te pūrongo i te ahu whakamua ki te whānau i ia te wā.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Ka whakahaere te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. E mōhio ana rātou, mā te whakatairanga ake i te kounga o te arotake whaiaro puta noa i te kōhanga reo, ka whai hua anō hoki te tupu me te kounga i roto i ngā tau kei te heke mai.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakatinana i te aromātai hōtaka hei tāutu i ngā āhuatanga e whai hua ana me ērā hoki e ngoikore ana ki te hōtaka akoranga, ā, i ngā matea whakawhanaketanga ngaio hoki o ngā kaimahi.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whare Aroha o Te Hūmārie ki Ōpōtiki. Ka whai wāhi nui Te Hāhi Ringatū ki te hōtaka o te kōhanga reo. Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tuākana tō rātou kaha ki te ārahi i ngā tikanga Ringatū i ia rā. E whakahaere ana te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei tino painga mō ngā tamariki.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whare Aroha o Te Hūmārie is in Ōpōtiki. Te Hāhi Ringatū is a significant part of the kōhanga reo programme. Older children demonstrate leadership in Ringatu tikanga on a daily basis. Whānau manage the kōhanga in the best interests of the children.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whare Aroha o Te Hūmārie will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whare Aroha o Te Hūmārie is in Ōpōtiki. The facilities provide for indoor and outdoor exploration. Since the last review there has been a focus on rejuvenating kōhanga management and te implementation of Te Whariki.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children show confidence and knowledge in their kōhanga reo?

Children are confident about who they are, where they are from and their kōhanga reo environment.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children form strong bonds with their peers and adults. They regularly interact with parents and kaumātua who attend kōhanga reo every day. Whakapapa and whanaungatanga are central to tamariki learning. Kaimahi know their children well. Consequently children seek comfort and reassurance from them. Children are happy.

Children love to learn and play. They learn te reo and tikanga Māori through Te Hāhi Ringatū. Older children are confident to lead the whānau in Ringatū customs and traditions throughout the day. Children confidently use te reo Māori with their peers and with adults. Whānau and kaimahi reinforce children’s learning areas as te reo Māori only speaking areas. Children are well supported as developing speakers of te reo Māori.

Children are developing an awareness of their place in the kōhanga reo and the local community. They participate in local and regional events. Routines and transitions at kōhanga reo are smooth. Children are provided with a variety of learning resources. They manage aspects of their own care and wellbeing. Children are confident and inquisitive learners.

Planning and Evaluation

Programme planning is detailed and reflects the kaupapa of kōhanga reo and local knowledge. Kaimahi plan collaboratively with whānau. Te Whāriki is well understood by whānau and kaimahi. Children benefit from a programme that is well planned and organised.

The charter provides appropriate guidance for the operations of the kōhanga reo. There are specific links to Ngā Pou E Whā and Te Ara Tūāpae. Whānau roles and responsibilities are clearly defined. Health and safety policies and procedures are in place. These provide effective guidelines for practice. Children enjoy an environment that caters for their wellbeing.

The whānau manage the operations of the kōhanga reo well. The whānau has a strategic plan aligned to Te Ara Tūāpae. The goals and objectives set by the whānau are clearly identified. The plan is clear and focuses on sustainability. Internal review is based on improvements. Whānau hui are consistent and discussions focus on kōhanga reo operations. Children are at the heart of decision making at Te Whare Aroha o Te Hūmārie.

Key Next Steps

Programme evaluations are carried out informally, however it is difficult to monitor any improvements made to kaimahi practice or the programme.

Assessment is still developing. Kaimahi do not focus on capturing what children know, what they can do and what they are learning. Children’s learning and progress is not monitored over time.

The whānau do not have an action plan to show how the goals and aspirations identified in the strategic plan will be realised. This will also provide a focus for regular reporting back to whānau around progress.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

The whānau manage the kōhanga reo in the best interests of the children. They acknowledge that raising the quality of internal review across the kōhanga reo will benefit future growth and enhancement.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • Implement programme evaluation to identify strengths and weaknesses in the learning programme and professional development needs of kaimahi.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whare Aroha o Te Hūmārie is in Ōpōtiki. Te Hāhi Ringatū is a significant part of the kōhanga reo programme. Older children demonstrate leadership in Ringatu tikanga on a daily basis. Whānau manage the kōhanga in the best interests of the children.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whare Aroha o Te Hūmārie will be in three years.
