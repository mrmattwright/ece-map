1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Nō te tau 1986 Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongomai i whakatuwherahia ai, ā, e tū ana ki ngā papa o te kura o Rongomai, ki Ōtara. He pai te tiaki, te whai taputapu, te whai rauemi hoki o ngā rawa me ngā whare, hei whakatutuki i ngā whānuitanga o ngā matea o ngā tamariki. I whiriwhiri ngā mātua i tēnei ratonga mō ā rātou tamariki, nā te kounga o te atawhai, te reo Māori, me ngā tikanga, me te āhua o ngā mahi kia tau mai ai ki a rātou te aronga toi whenuatanga ki te kōhanga reo.

Mai i te tau 2012, kua whakahaerehia Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongomai e Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo. Kua pēnei nā te ngoikore o te whakahaeretanga o mua, tae atu ki ngā pūtea, me ngā tino take taupatupatu i pupū ake ai ki waenga i te kaiako o mua, ngā kaimahi, me ngā mema o te whānau. E ai ki ngā kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe, ngā mema o te whānau, me ngā kaimahi, he nui ngā whakapaitanga ake i whakatutukihia ai, puta noa i ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakapau kaha te whānau o Rongomai ki te whakapai i ngā wāhanga katoa o tō rātou kōhanga reo, ā, ka ū tonu ki te nuinga o ngā tūranga whakahaere i te Hōngongoi o 2013, mā te tautoko tonu o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

Ka whakatauirahia te reo Māori mō ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga katoa ki te patapatai, te whakawero, me te akiaki i te whakamahinga o te reo. Ka aro tōtika ngā tamariki ki ngā kōrero mā te reo Māori, ā, ka whai wāhi atu ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero. Ka tino tautokona ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ko ngā hononga me ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā tamariki, ngā mema o te whānau, me ngā kaimahi e ahu mai ana i te whanaungatanga. Ka kōkiri, ka whakapūmau, ka ngākau nui hoki ngā tamariki ki ngā hononga ki ētahi atu. Ka mahi ngātahi ngā pākeke, ā, ka whakapau kaha rātou ki te whakarato i te taiao haumaru, tau, hāneanea hoki mō ngā tamariki.

Ka mahi ngātahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te hāpai i tō rātou kōhanga reo. He whāinga mārama ā rātou mō ngā tamariki me te kōhanga reo. Ko ētahi o ngā kaupapa matua, ko te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori, me te whakapiki ake i te tatauranga tamariki. Ka whakanuia, ka tautāwhitia hoki te whānau me ngā tamariki, ā, he kaha tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga ki te kōhanga reo.

Kei ngā mahere hōtaka te tino kiko o ngā whāinga me ngā rauemi e pā ana ki te hōtaka. Ka manawareka ki ngā kaupapa, ā, ka hāngai ēnei ki te haumaru, ki ngā atua Māori, me te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki. Ko te ao Māori me te ao whānui ngā tino āhuatanga nui o ngā akoranga me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

E whakawhanake ana te whānau i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki te arotake whaiaro. Me whakapakari rātou i ā rātou whakaritenga e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro, ā, me whakaōkawa i te tukanga e pā ana ki te pūrongo i te whakahaere pūtea, ngā kaupapa here, te mahere rautaki, te hauora, te haumaru, me te whakahaere i ngā rawa.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongomai kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongomai.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga a ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

Ka hāngai Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira ki ngā wāhanga katoa o ngā akoranga a te tamaiti, me tōna whakawhanaketanga, i runga i te tirohanga Māori ki te ao.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Mana Reo

Ka whakatauirahia te reo Māori mō ngā tamariki. Ka tino hāpaitia ngā kaimahi me te whānau e tētahi kuia kua roa nei e whai wāhi ana ki tēnei kōhanga reo.

  • Ka āta whai atu ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga katoa ki te patapatai, te whakawero, me te akiaki hoki i te whakamahinga o te reo.
  • Haere ai ngā mātua ki ngā akoranga o te reo Māori e tū ana ki te kōhanga reo i ia wiki.
  • Ka whakautu tika ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori, ā, ka whai wāhi atu ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero.
  • Ka whakautu, ka whai hoki ngā tamariki tēina i ngā tohutohu ngāwari.
  • Ka tautoko ngā tamariki tuākana i ngā tamariki tēina ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori.
  • Taunekeneke ai, whakawhiti kōrero ai hoki ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa, ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki akoranga, puta noa i te rā.

Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā kīwaha, ngā whakataukī, ngā kīrehu hoki o te reo Māori, mai i ngā iwi katoa, i tō rātou ake iwi hoki o Tainui. Ka kitea te ngāwari ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, ā, nā tēnei, he rerekē, he pukukata hoki ngā tūmomo whakawhitinga kōrero.

Ka tino tautokona ngā tamariki ki te tupu i tō rātou āheinga ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Mana Atua

Ka whakapau kaha ngā pākeke ki te whakatau i ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. He pai ngā pākeke ki te whakaako, te whakapūmau, me te whakahaere hoki i ngā mahinga mā ngā tamariki. Ko ngā tamariki:

  • ka tino mōhio i ngā mahinga o ia rā, ā, he pakari anō hoki rātou ki te whakatutuki i ēnei
  • ka whakaatu i te whai whakaaro nui ki te taha wairua, te taha tangata hoki, i tā rātou whakamahinga i ngā karakia me ngā mōteatea
  • ka tautoko tētahi i tētahi ki te taki i ngā pepeha, he pai tā rātou mahi ngātahi, ā, ka tautoko anō hoki rātou i a rātou i ngā wā kai
  • ka kitea tō rātou harikoa ki te kōhanga reo
  • ka whakaatu i te tiaki whaiaro, me ngā whanonga ki te hāpai i a rātou anō
  • ka kitea tō rātou whakaute ki tō rātou taiao, ā, ka whakapai rātou i ia te wā
  • ka akiaki i te taha hauora, te taha waiora hoki i ia te wā.

Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā tūmomo karakia, hīmene, waiata hoki hei whakatau i ngā tamariki i ētahi tūmomo wāhanga o te rā.

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki te whakahirahiratanga o ngā atua ki tō rātou hauora, tō rātou waiora hoki.

Mana Tangata

Ko ngā hononga me ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā tamariki, ngā mema o te whānau, me ngā kaimahi, ka ahu mai i ngā ariā o te whanaungatanga. Ka whai wāhi ngā pākeke ki ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo i ia rā. Ka mahi ngātahi rātou, ka whakapau kaha rātou, ki te whakarato i te taiao haumaru, pūmau, hāneanea hoki mō ngā tamariki. He tokomaha ngā mātua me ngā kaitiaki e haere ana ki te kōhanga reo i ia rā. Ko ngā tamariki:

  • ka whakanui, ka mihi hoki i ngā manuhiri
  • e āhei ana ki te taki me te maumahara anō hoki i tō rātou ake whakapapa
  • ka kitea tō rātou noho haumaru, noho tau hoki ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo
  • ka kitea tō rātou manaaki, tō rātou whakaaro nui anō hoki ki a rātou anō, ki ētahi atu
  • ka kōkirihia, ka whakapūmauhia, ka ngākau nuitia hoki e rātou ngā hononga ki ētahi atu
  • ka whakaatu, ka wheako hoki rātou i te aroha, te manaaki, me te āwhina.

Whakanuia ai, whakakoia ai hoki e ngā kaimahi ngā whakamātau me ngā mahi angitu a ngā tamariki. Ka akiaki tēnei i ngā tamariki ki te arotahi tonu, me te tākaro ngātahi.

Ka āwhina ngā tamariki tuākana i ngā kaimahi ki te ārahi me te hāpai i ō rātou tēina. Ka whakauru ngā tamariki tuākana i ngā tēina ki ā rātou tākaro, ā, ka tautoko i a rātou i roto i ngā mahinga me te kōrero i te reo Māori.

He pakari, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo.

Mana Aotūroa

Ka whakarite, ka whakarato hoki ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau i te pai o ngā taumahi i āta whakaritea ai, ā, e whakahihiri ana, e whakaongaonga ana hoki i ngā tamariki. Ko ngā tamariki:

  • e āhei ana ki te whiriwhiri haere, me te tīpako hoki, mai i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi, taumahi hoki
  • ka tūhura i te taiao māori e karapoti ana i te kōhanga reo, tae atu ki te taiao atawhai e pā tata atu ana ki a rātou
  • ka haere ki te moana, ki te kauranga ika o Kelly Tarlton, ki ngā pāmu o te takiwā, ā, ki ētahi atu kōhanga reo hoki
  • ka ako i te taiao o te takiwā
  • ka whai wāhi atu, ka ako hoki i ngā tūmomo ahurea.

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te pākiki mō te ao e noho nei rātou.

Mana Whenua

He pai te tautoko o ngā pākeke i ngā tamariki ki te ako i tō rātou ake taiao, ki roto, ki tua atu hoki i tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ko ngā tamariki:

  • ka noho pakari rātou ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo
  • ka wheako i ngā whai wāhitanga ki te ārahi
  • noho mōhio ki ngā manuhiri me ngā uaratanga hoki o te manaaki, te whakatau, me te tiaki.

He pai ngā wheako ka whakatutukihia e ngā tamariki ki te hapori tonu.

Te hōtaka o ia rā.He riterite, he hanganga, he tino whakaongaonga hoki te hōtaka o ia rā, mō ngā tamariki nohinohi. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga, me te whakahura hoki i ngā āhuatanga hou. He whai wāhitanga anō hoki ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā tūmomo kaupapa, te kōkiri i ā rātou ake tākaro, me ngā kaupapa hoki e kōkirihia ana e ngā pākeke. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te waihanga, me te tūhura i ngā tūmomo ariā e pā ana ki te pāngarau me te reo matatini, te whai wāhi atu hoki ki ngā tākaro kori tinana, me te mahi pohewa. Ka whakatutukihia ngā matea o ngā tama me ngā tamariki pakari, nā ngā wero e āta whakaratohia ana e ngā kaimahi mā rātou. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki tētahi hōtaka e whakaongaonga ana, e whakahihiri ana hoki i a rātou.

Te taiao akoranga.He wāteatea, he mahana, he mā, he muramura hoki te taiao o te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i ō rātou pūmanawa auaha, te harataunga ā-tinana, te taha whakaari, te taha pohewa hoki, me ngā pūkenga reo matatini, pāngarau hoki. Ka āta whakatutuki ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo reanga, āheinga hoki o ngā tamariki, nā te whakarato i ngā tūmomo taumahi, rauemi hoki e whai pānga ana ki ā rātou reanga ako. Nā runga i te āhua o ngā whakaaturanga, he ātaahua, he hihiri hoki te taiao mō ngā tamariki. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i tō rātou taiao muramura, whakaongaonga hoki.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

Mai i te tau 2012, kua whakahaerehia Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongomai e Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo. Kua pēnei nā te ngoikore o te whakahaeretanga, tae atu ki ngā pūtea, me te nui hoki o ngā take taupatupatu ki waenga i te kaiako o mua, me ngā kaimahi, ngā mema o te whānau hoki. E mōhio ana ngā kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe, he nui ngā whakapaitanga i whakatutukihia ai, puta noa i ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. Ahakoa kua whakapau kaha te whānau o Rongomai ki te whakapai i ngā wāhanga katoa o tō rātou kōhanga reo, ā, ka tū hoki rātou ki te nuinga o ngā tūranga whakahaere hei te Hōngongoi 2013, ka noho tonu te tautoko o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ngā whakaritenga whakahaere. I nāianei, ka whai hua, he riterite hoki te āhua o te whakahaeretanga ki te kōhanga reo. He pai ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru, me te whakahaeretanga o te tari. Kua āta whakaritea, e noho wātea ana hoki, ngā tuhinga matua pērā i te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa here, ngā rehita, me ngā mauhanga hoki e pā ana ki ngā tamariki, ā, ka hāngai katoa ēnei ki te rā.

Te whakamahere rautaki. He mārama ngā whāinga a te whānau mō ngā tamariki me te kōhanga reo. He mahere ā-tau e whakaraupapa ana i ngā whāinga a te whānau mō taua tau. Ko ētahi o ngā aua kaupapa matua, ko te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori mō te whānau, ngā tamariki, me ngā kaimahi, me te whakapiki ake hoki i te tatauranga tamariki. Ko te whakamahere mō te wā kei te heke mai, e whai hua ana ki ngā tamariki.

Te whai wāhitanga a te whānau me tō rātou ngākau nuitanga. Ka mahi ngātahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te hāpai i tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka whakarato rātou i te tino tautoko mō ā rātou tamariki me ngā kaimahi. Ka whai wāhi te whānau ki te āwhina i ngā haerenga, ki te whakamahere i ngā kaupapa, ki te tū hei kaiāwhina, me te uru mātātoa hoki ki ngā kaupapa me ngā hui ki te kōhanga reo. Ka kitea te hiahia o ngā mema o te whānau ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā hōtaka whakangungu a Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo me ngā kaupapa mahi a te purapura. Ka hāpai tēnei i a rātou ki te ako i ā rātou kawenga mahi, ā, ki te tautoko hoki i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka ū tonu te kaha tae ā-tinana atu ki ngā hui o ia marama, ā, ka whai wāhi atu te whānau ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, me te whakatau ngātahi i ngā whakaritenga. Ka whakamanatia, ka tautāwhitia hoki te whānau me ngā tamariki, ā, he tino pakari tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga ki te kōhanga reo.

Te whakamahere kaupapa. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i tētahi tauira whakamahere e whai hua ana. He tino kiko i runga i ngā mahere e hāngai ana ki ngā whāinga o te hōtaka, me ngā rauemi, ngā rārangi kupu, ngā kaupapa, me ngā tūmomo whenu mana. He rerehua ngā kaupapa, ā, ka arotahi ki te haumaru, ki ngā atua Māori, me te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki. Ka te ao Māori me te ao whānui hei āhuatanga nui ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te pūrongo i ia te wā. E whakawhanake ana te whānau i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki te arotake whaiaro. He ōpaki tā rātou whakawhiti kōrero mō te ahunga whakamua o te kōhanga reo. Me whakapakari te whānau i ā rātou whakaritenga e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro, ā, me whakaōkawa i te tukanga e pā ana ki te pūrongo i te whakahaeretanga o ngā pūtea, ngā kaupapa here, te mahere rautaki, te hauora me te haumaru, me te whakahaeretanga o ngā rawa.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongomai, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro.  I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki: 

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo kia:

5.1 mahi ngātahi tonu te whānau – mā te tautoko hoki o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo – ki te whakapakari i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

6 Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu mō ngā Ratonga Arotake Māori (Te Uepū ā-Motu)

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongomai opened in 1986 and is situated on the grounds of Rongomai School, Otara. The premises and facilities are well maintained, equipped and resourced to provide for the diverse needs of children. Parents choose this service for their children for the quality of care, te reo Māori me ngā tikanga and the way in which they are supported to feel a sense of belonging to the kōhanga reo.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongomai has been under the management of Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) since 2012. This was as a result of previous poor management including finances and serious conflict issues between the previous kaiako, staff and whānau members. 3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira encompass all areas of a child’s learning and development according to a Māori world view.

Areas of good performance

Mana Reo

Te reo Māori is modelled for children. Kaimahi and the whānau are well supported by a kuia who has a long association with this kōhanga reo.

  • Kamahi use every opportunity to question, challenge and encourage language use.
  • Parents attend weekly te reo Māori classes held at the kōhanga reo.
  • Children can respond accurately to te reo Māori and participate in conversations.
  • Younger children respond to and follow simple commands and instructions.
  • Older children support younger children to use te reo Maori.
  • Kaimahi constantly interact and communicate with children in various learning contexts throughout the day.

Kaimahi use idioms, sayings and expressions in te reo Māori from different iwi including their own Tainui iwi. This reflects an ease for speaking te reo Māori and provides variety and a sense of humour to conversations.

Children are well supported as growing speakers of te reo Māori.

Mana Atua

Adults make every effort to settle children at kōhanga reo. Adults work well to teach, reinforce and manage routines for children. Children:

  • know daily routines well and are confident in them
  • demonstrate spiritual and cultural respect through their use of karakia and mōteatea
  • support each other to recite pepeha, to play well together and support each other during kai time
  • appear happy in their kōhanga reo
  • display self care and self help behaviours
  • show respect for their environment and they regularly tidy up after themselves
  • encourage health and well being regularly

Kaimahi use different karakia, himene and waiata to calm and settle children at different intervals throughout the day.

Children know the importance of ngā atua to their health and wellbeing.

Mana Tangata

Relationships and interactions among children, whānau members and kaimahi are based on the concepts of whanaungatanga. Adults contribute to kōhanga reo operations on a daily basis. They work together and make every effort to provide children with a safe, secure and comfortable environment. Many parents and caregivers attend kōhanga reo daily. Children:

  • acknowledge and greet visitors
  • are able to recite and recall their own whakapapa
  • demonstrate that they feel safe and secure in the kōhanga reo environment
  • show care and concern for themselves and others
  • initiate, maintain and enjoy relationships with others
  • display and experience aroha, manaaki and awhina.

Kaimahi regularly acknowledge and affirm children’s efforts and successes. This encourages children to stay focussed and to play cooperatively.

The older children assist kaimahi in guiding and supporting their younger peers. The older children include the younger children in their play and support them with routines and te reo Māori.

Children are confident and comfortable in the kōhanga reo environment.

Mana Aotūroa

The staff and whānau members prepare and provide well organised activities that are interesting and stimulating for children who:

  • are able to make choices and select from a wide range of resources and activities
  • explore the natural environment surrounding the kōhanga reo including the reserve next door
  • go on excursions to the beach, Kelly Tarltons, local farms and other kōhanga reo
  • learn about the local environment
  • interact with and learn about different cultures

Children are developing a curiosity about the world around them.

Mana Whenua

Adults support children well to learn about the immediate environment in and around their kōhanga reo. Children:

  • are confident in their kōhanga reo environment
  • experience leadership opportunities
  • become aware of visitors and values like manaaki, whakatau and tiaki.

Children have many positive experiences in in the local community.

The daily programme. The daily programme has balance, structure and is highly stimulating for young children. Children are able to follow their interests and make new discoveries. They have opportunities to participate in different kaupapa, independent play and adult-led activities. Children are able to create, explore different numeracy and literacy concepts, engage in physical play and be imaginative. The needs of boys and able children are met through the challenges that kaimahi provide for them effectively. Children participate in a stimulating and exciting programme.

Learning environment. The kōhanga reo provides a spacious, warm, clean, bright environment for children. Children have opportunities to develop their creative potential, physical proficiency, dramatic and imaginative interests and literacy and numeracy skills. Kaimahi successfully cater for the different levels and abilities of children by providing them with activities and resources suitable to their learning levels. Displays make the environment attractive and inviting for children. Children confidently investigate their colourful, stimulating environment.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongomai has been under the management of Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust since 2012. This was as a result of poor management including finances and serious conflict issues between the previous kaiako, staff and whānau members. Local kaupapa kaimahi acknowledge that significant improvements have been made across all areas of the kōhanga reo. While the whānau of Rongomai have worked hard to improve all areas of their kōhanga reo and should retain most management roles in July 2013 TKRNT support will continue.

Areas of good performance

Management practices. The kōhanga reo is now managed in an efficient and organised manner. There are good processes for health and safety and office administration. Key documents including the charter, policies, registers and children’s records are well organised, up to date and accessible.

Strategic planning. Whānau have clear goals for children and the kōhanga reo. There is an annual plan which lists whānau goals for this year. Priorities include te reo Māori development for whānau, children and kaimahi, and increasing the roll. Future planning has positive results for children.

Whānau participation and commitment. Whānau and kaimahi work cooperatively to support their kōhanga reo. They provide valuable support for their children and kaimahi. Whānau help with trips, contribute to kaupapa planning, parent help and take an active role in kōhanga reo activities and events. Whānau members display an interest to participate in Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust training programmes and local purapura workshops. This should help them to learn about their responsibilities and support children’s learning. Positive attendance is maintained at monthly hui where whānau participate in discussions and shared decision-making. Whānau and children are valued, fully embraced and have a strong sense of belonging to the kōhanga reo.

Kaupapa planning. Kaimahi use an effective planning model. Plans contain clear detail about programme objectives, resources, wordlists, kaupapa and the different mana strands. Kaupapa are appealing and focus on safety, atua Māori and children’s sense of belonging. Te ao Māori and the wider world are important aspects of children’s learning and development.

Areas for development

Regular reporting. Whānau are developing in their knowledge of self review. They discuss informally how the kōhanga reo is progressing. Whānau need to strengthen their self review practices and formalise the process for reporting on financial management, policies, the strategic plan, health and safety and property management.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Rongomai completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist.  In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

General Information About Reviews

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
