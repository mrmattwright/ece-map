1. Te Aro Mātai a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

He köhanga kāinga Te Köhanga Reo o Te Hau Ora kei te taihauāuru o te moka taone o Tamaki Makaurau kei Kelston. Kei muri ngā whare o te köhanga i te whare o te whānau, ā, no te kaipupuri o te raihana ēnei whare e rua. E tötika ana te tiaki a te whānau i ngā rawa ahu mahi o roto i te whare, ā, me ēra o waho o te whare, ā, e ātaahua ana ēnei, ā, e whakaihiihi ana i ngā mokopuna. E whakaāhei ana te whare i te ngāwari o te hūnuku haere mai i roto i te whare ki waho, ā, mai hoki i waho ki roto i te whare.

E pono ana te whānau ki te whakahaere i to rātou köhanga i runga i ngā taumata e tika ana mō tēnei. Haeremai ai ētahi o ngā mātua ki te āwhina i roto i ngā whakahaere a to rātou mō te kore utu, ā, e whakapono ana rātou ki to rātou köhanga. E mahi ana hoki te whānau o te köhanga i runga i tā rātou whai whakaaro nui ki a rātou mokopuna.

He kawa whakahaere tötika tā ngā kaimahi mō te whakamahere me te aro mātai. E whakaatu mai ana ngā whakamahere o ngā hōtaka akoranga me ngā kaupapa mahi, i roto i ngā aro matawai me ngā aro mātai o ngā hōtaka akoranga.

He mārie te reo kōrero o te kaiako, ā, e matatau ana hoki a ia ki te körero i te reo Māori. Körero ai ngā kaimahi i roto i te reo Māori puta noa i te roanga ake o te ra. Whakamahi ai hoki rātou i ngā hanganga tötika o te reo Māori, ā, he tauira pai rātou hei whai mā ngā mokopuna ki te whanake hei mokopuna e körero ana i te reo Māori.

E whakaratohia ana e ngā kaimahi he hötaka akoranga e whiwhi ana i ngā wā motuhake mō ngā akoranga ōkawa. E tuwhera ana ngā huarahi ki ngā mokopuna ki te tohu i ngā mahi e hiahia ana rātou ki te mahi, mai i te maha atu o ngā mahi kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi hei mahi mā rātou i roto i to rātou whare, ā, i waho hoki o to rātou whare. E whakamātautau ana, ā, e whakaara ana ēnei mahi i a rātou ki te ako, ā, ki te torotoro haere. He maha ngā rauemi a te köhanga, ā, e whakarato ana i ngā tuāhuatanga whakamātautautanga ki tēna, ki tēna, reanga o ngā mokopuna.

Ngā Mahi Hei Mahi a Meake Nei

E whakapono ana a ERO kei te whakahaeretia e te whānau to rātou köhanga i runga i tā rātou whai whakaaro nui ki a rātou mokopuna, ā, na tēnei tu-āhua, ko te arotake anō a ERO i te köhanga a meake nei, ko tēra ka whai i tā rātou kaupapa arotake e whakahaeretia ana e rātou i ia toru tau.

2. Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Whakamahere me Ngā Aro Mātai

He Kupu Whakamārama

E aro mātai ana a ERO i ētahi o ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki ngā whakariterite me ngā whakahaere o ngā köhanga reo, pēnei i te whai wāhi mai a ngā mema o te whānau i roto i ēnei, ā, i roto hoki i ngā whakamahere, ngā aro matawai me ngā aro mātai o ngā hōtaka akoranga.

Ngā wāhanga e ahu whakamua ana.

Te whai wāhi mai a ngā mema o te whānau. E pono ana te whānau ki te whakahaere i to rātou köhanga i runga anō i ngā taumata e tika ana mō tēnei. E maha ana rātou e haeremai ana ki a rātou hui i ngā wā katoa. E whai wāhi ana ngā mātua i roto i ngā arotake o ngā kaupapa here me ngā whakahaere mō ēnei, ā, e maha ana hoki rātou e āwhina ana i to rātou köhanga i runga i ngā haerere a a rātou mokopuna i waho atu o to rātou köhanga. E whakaatu mai ana te kaupapa arataki a te köhanga i ngā tumanakohanga a ngā mātua. Haeremai ai ētahi o ngā mātua ki te köhanga ki te āwhina i ngā kaimahi me ngā mokopuna. E whakapono ana ngā mema o te whānau ki te hōtaka akoranga a te köhanga, ā, tāpiri atu ki ngā tūranga me ngā kawenga a ngā kaimahi. E mahi ngātahi ana ngā mema o te whānau i runga i tā rātou whai whakaaro nui ki a rātou mokopuna.

Ngā whakamahere o ngā hötaka akoranga, ngā aro matawai me ngā aro mātai. He kaupapa whakahaere tā ngā kaimahi mō te whakamahere me te aro mātai. He maha ngā tu-āhua huarahi e whāia ana e te whānau mō te rēkōta i ngā aro matawai o ngā mokopuna, arā, ki roto i a rātou pukohi körero, ki roto i a rātou ripoata takitahi, ā, ki roto hoki i te pukapuka a te kaiako. Titiro hāngai ai ngā hui a ngā kaimahi ki runga i ngā akoranga a tēna, a tēna, o a rātou mokopuna, ā, i runga hoki i o rātou ahu whakamuatanga i roto i te mātauranga. E möhio ana ngā kaimahi ki ngā rautaki mō te hāpai i te haere tonu o ngā whanaketanga o a rātou mokopuna. E titiro hāngai ana a rātou aro mātaitanga ki runga i ngā tukunga iho a te hōtaka akoranga, ā, i runga hoki i ngā paetae o te mātauranga i taea e tēna, e tēna, o ngā mokopuna. E whakaatu mai ana ngā tohu o ngā whakamaheretanga o ngā hōtaka akoranga me ngā kaupapa mahi i roto i ngā aro matawai o ngā mokopuna, ā, i roto hoki i te aro mātai o te hōtaka akoranga.

Mana Whenua

He Whakamārama

Ko te kaupapa e titirohia ana e tēnei arotake, ko Mana Whenua. E aro mātai ana a ERO e ētahi o ngā āhuatanga o te hōtaka akoranga e titiro hāngai ana ki runga i ngā rauemi, te whanaketanga o te reo Māori, ā, i ngā huarahi e whāia ana e ngā kaiako me ngā kaimahi mō te whakamātautau me te whakaara i a rātou mokopuna ki te ako.

Ngā wāhanga e ahu whakamua ana

Te Reo Māori. E mārie ana te kaiako ki te körero, ā, e matatau ana hoki a ia i te reo

Māori. Körero ai hoki ngā kaimahi i roto i te reo Māori puta noa i te roanga ake o te ra. Ahakoa e körero tonu ana ētahi o ngā mokopuna i roto i te reo o tauiwi, e ū tonu ana te körero a ngā kaimahi i ngā haurārangi tika o te reo Māori, ā,me ngā hanganga tika o te reo Māori ki a rātou mokopuna. Whakamahi ai ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki pēnei i ngā akiakinga, ngā patapatai, ngā körerorero anō, ngā tuku mihi me ngā whakatauiraira mō te akiaki i a rātou mokopuna ki te körero i te reo Māori. Ka taea e ngā mokopuna te körero ngā kupu ngāwari me ngā haurārangi ngāwari o te reo Māori, inā ka körero rātou ki ēra atu o rātou, ā, ki ngā pakeke hoki. Akiaki ai ngā kaimahi i a rātou ki te körerorero ngātahi, ā, kua pai ake hoki ngā tautokotanga ki ngā mokopuna i a rātou e pūāwai haere nei hei kai-korero o te reo Māori.

Te hötaka akoranga. E whakaratohia ana e ngā kaimahi he hötaka akoranga e whiwhi wā motuhake ana mō ngā mokopuna ki te ako i ngā karakia, ngā mihimihi, ngā pepeha me ngā waiata. E tuwhera ana ngā huarahi ki ngā mokopuna ki te tohu i ngā mahi e hiahia ana rātou ki te mahi, mai i te maha atu o ngā tu-āhua mahi kua whakaritea mō roto i te whare, ā, mō waho hoki o te whare e akiaki ana, ā, e whakangākau nui ana i a rātou ki te ako, ā, ki te torotoro haere. E maha ana ngā rauemi a te köhanga, ā, e whakarato ana i ngā tu-āhua whakamātautautanga ki tēna, ki tēna, reanga o ngā mokopuna. E whakarite ana e ngā kaimahi ngā mahi hei mahi mā a rātou mokopuna, ā, me ngā rauemi hoki mō ēnei, ā, e taea ana e te katoa o ngā mokopuna te hūnuku māhorahora haere mai i tēna wāhi akoranga, ki tētahi atu wāhi akoranga. He wā motuhake e whakaritea ana mō ēnei i roto i ngā kawa whakahaere mō ia ra, ā, i roto hoki i te wātaka a te köhanga. E akiakitia ana ngā mokopuna ki te whakareri i ngā mahi me ngā rauemi hei mahi mā rātou, ā, ki te whakahoki i ēnei ki te wāhi e tika ana mō ēnei i muri mai i a rātou mahi. E whai wāhi ana ngā mokopuna ki te hōtaka akoranga e whakaāhei ana i a rātou ki te whai wāhi i roto i ngā wheako akoranga e whai hua ana ki a rātou.

1. The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hau Ora is a home based kōhanga in the west Auckland suburb of Kelston. The kōhanga buildings are situated behind a house that is a family home. The licensee of the kōhanga owns both these properties. The indoor and outdoor facilities are well maintained, attractive and stimulating for children. The premises allow for easy movement between the indoor and outdoor areas.

The whānau is committed to operating and managing of its kōhanga. A number of parents attend kōhanga on a voluntary basis. Whānau members are positive about the kōhanga reo. The kōhanga whānau works in the best interest of their children.

The kaimahi have an appropriate process for planning and evaluation. Programme planning and kaupapa mahi is reflected in children’s assessments and programme evaluation.

The kaiako is gently spoken and is a competent speaker of te reo Māori. Kaimahi consistently speak Māori throughout the day. They use appropriate language structures and are good role models for the children, who are better supported to develop as speakers of te reo Māori.

The kaimahi provide a programme where there are set times for formal learning. Children have opportunities to choose from a range of activities, both indoors and outdoors, that challenge and motivate them to learn and explore. The kōhanga is well resourced, providing variety and challenge for the different age groups of children.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

2. Findings

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO evaluates aspects of the organisation and management of kōhanga reo such as whānau participation and programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

Areas of progress

Whānau participation and involvement. The whānau is committed to operating and managing its kōhanga. There is constantly high attendance at whānau hui. Parents are involved in policy review. There is always excellent attendance to assist kōhanga during excursions. The induction process clearly identifies expectations of parents. A number of parents attend kōhanga on a voluntary basis. Whānau members are positive about the kōhanga programme and the roles and responsibilities of the kaimahi. The kōhanga whānau works in the best interest of their children.

Programme planning, assessment and evaluation. The kaimahi have a process for planning and evaluating. Children’s assessments are recorded in a number of ways including portfolios, individual reports and in a kaiako diary. Kaimahi hui focus on individual children’s learning and progress. Kaimahi identify strategies to support children’s ongoing development. Evaluations focus on the results of the learning programme and children’s achievement. Programme planning and kaupapa mahi is reflected in children’s assessments and programme evaluation.

Mana Whenua

Background

The focus for the review is Mana Whenua. ERO evaluates aspects of the learning programme that focuses on resourcing, development of te reo Māori and how children are challenged and motivated to learn.

Areas of progress

Te reo Māori. The kaiako is gently spoken and a very competent speaker of te reo Māori. Kaimahi consistently speak Māori throughout the day. While some children continue to speak English, kaimahi persist with correct phrases and language structures in te reo Māori. Kaimahi use strategies including prompting, questioning, repeating, praising and modelling to encourage children to speak te reo Māori. Children can use simple words and phrases in te reo Māori when speaking with their peers and adults. Kaimahi encourage conversation. Children are better supported to develop as speakers of te reo Māori.

The learning programme. The kaimahi provide a programme where there is set time for formal learning of karakia, mihimihi, pepeha and waiata. Children have opportunities to choose from a wide variety of activities, both indoors and outoors that

challenge and motivate them to learn and explore. The kōhanga is well resourced providing variety and challenge for the different age groups of children. Kaimahi set up activities and resources where all children are able to move freely between areas of learning. Set activity times are part of the daily routines and timetable. Children are encouraged to help set up and clear activities when finished. They experience a programme, which enables them to engage in meaningful learning experiences.

Recommendations

ERO recommends that:

  1. the whānau continues to work with the district kaupapa kaimahi to further strengthen the kaupapa of te kōhanga reo and positive learning experiences for their children

3. Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.
