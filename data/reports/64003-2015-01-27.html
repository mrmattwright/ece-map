1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā tētahi o ngā Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia ai i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai e te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i mua atu, me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

  • te kounga o te mātauranga, tae atu ki te whakamahere hōtaka, te whakatinanatanga, te aromatawai, te aromātai, te whakahaere whanonga, te ārahi, me te tautoko i ngā kaimahi
  • te whakahaere a te whānau, tae atu ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo, ngā hononga, te arohaehae, me te arotake whaiaro
  • ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture
  • me ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai whai muri i te arotake o mua.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ōrongomai Marae ki te marae o Ōrongomai, ki Te Awakairangi, i Te Whanganui-a-Tara. Ka aromātai tēnei arotake tāpiri i te whai pānga whānui o te ahu whakamua a te whānau ki te kounga o te ako me te atawhai mō ngā tamariki. Ko ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake e hāngai ana ki te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai. Me whakapai ake ngā mahi e pā ana ki te whakahaere whanonga. Ko ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai ko te arohaehae kaimahi me te arotake whaiaro.

Ko tā Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga pātai aromātai e pēnei ana, arā:

He pēhea rawa te whakaongaonga, te whai wāhi mātātoa hoki o ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga e whai tikanga ana, e whai pūtake ana hoki?

Ka whakaongaongatia ngā tamariki, ā, ka whai wāhi mātātoa rātou ki ngā akoranga i te kōhanga reo. Kua whakapau kaha te whānau ki te whakawhanake i ngā mahi whakamahere e whai hua ake ana. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga e whai tikanga ana, e whai pūtake ana. Ināianei, kua āta whakaarohia ō rātou matea, ō rātou ngākau nuitanga ki ngā mahere o te hōtaka akoranga. E tino māramahia ana, e tino whakatinanahia ana hoki te aromatawai me te aromātai hōtaka i nāianei. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā taunekeneke e whai hua ana, e manaaki ana hoki i a rātou. He pārekareka ki a rātou te ako me te whakamahi i te reo Māori. He tau, he pakari, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki.

He aronga ngātahi tā te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakarato i ngā putanga papai mō ngā tamariki. He pai te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Ka whakahaerehia ngā whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo e te komiti o te marae o Ōrongomai, mā te tautoko nui hoki o te whānau. E mōhio ana ngā mema kei reira hoki ētahi wāhanga hei whakawhanake, ā, ka whakatairanga hoki tēnei i te whakahaere o te kōhanga reo me te whakatinanatanga o ā rātou whāinga rautaki.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Te kounga o te mātauranga

He whakamārama

He pēhea rawa te whakaongaonga, te whai wāhi mātātoa hoki o ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga e whai tikanga ana, e whai pūtake ana hoki?

Ka whakaongaongatia ngā tamariki, ā, ka whai wāhi mātātoa rātou ki ngā akoranga i te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka whakaongaongatia ngā tamariki, ā, ka whai wāhi mātātoa rātou ki ngā akoranga i te kōhanga reo. Kua whakapau kaha te whānau ki te whakawhanake i ngā mahi whakamahere e whai hua ake ana. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga e whai tikanga ana, e whai pūtake ana. Ināianei, kua āta whakaarohia ō rātou matea, ō rātou ngākau nuitanga ki ngā mahere o te hōtaka akoranga. Ka āta kitea Te Whāriki ki ngā mahere e whakatairanga ana i te ako i te reo Māori. Ka aromātai tōtika ngā kaimahi i te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka hāngai te aromatawai me ngā mōhiohio aromātai ki te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki me te whai huatanga o ngā taumahi me ngā whakaritenga i te kaupapa hei hāpai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Ka kitea te tupu haere o te māia me te āheinga o ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori. Ko te whakamahinga me te ako i te reo Māori e whakatauirahia ana, e whakatairangatia ana hoki e ngā kaimahi. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te taki i ngā karakia me te whakapuaki i ō rātou pepeha ki ētahi atu. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki ōkawa, ōpaki hoki hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki.

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā taunekeneke pai, manaaki hoki ki te kōhanga reo. Ka hāpai te taiao akoranga i te whakawhanaketanga ā-tinana, ā-tangata, ā-ahurea hoki o ngā tamariki. Kua āta whakatūria ngā whakaritengao ia rā, me ngā whāinga. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki, ā, ka kitea tō rātou tino hononga ki tō rātou whānau, te kōhanga reo, me te marae.

He mauri tau te āhua o ngā tamariki, he tino hihiwa hoki. Kua pai ake te ārahitanga me te aronga a ngā kaimahi. He pai tā rātou mahi ngātahi ki te whakarato i tētahi hōtaka ki ngā tamariki e whai hanganga ana, ā, e āhei ana hoki ki te whakarerekē haere hei whakatutuki i ngā matea o ngā tamariki.

Te whakahaere a te whānau

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He pai te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Kei te whakahaerehia te komiti o te marae o Ōrongomai i ngā whakahaeretanga whānui o te kōhanga reo ināianei. Ka āta kitea ngā whakapaitanga ki te whakahaeretanga. Ka arotake whaiaro i ia te wā. Ka whiwhi te whānau i ngā pūrongo i ia te wā, mō ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka tino arotahi te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā putanga papai mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake

Kāhore anō kia oti ngā tukanga arohaehae mō tēnei wā. E mōhio ana te whānau he mea nui te whakamahi i ngā arohaehae a ngā kaimahi hei whakamōhio i te whakawhanaketanga ngaio o ngā kaimahi, ā, ka whai pānga anō hoki ki te whakapai i te hōtaka. I tēnei wā, kei te whakapakari rātou i te pūnaha arohaehae o tēnei wā kia whai hua ai.

He whāiti te pūrongo i ngā putanga ki ngā tamariki i runga i ngā kaupapa rautaki matua. Kāhore ngā pūrongo a te whānau e whakamōhio ana i te āhua o te ahunga whakamua i ia te wā. Ka whai hua tēnei ki te whānuitanga o te arotake whaiaro o te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i ngā hōtaka akoranga e arotakengia ana i ia te wā hei tātari i te whai huatanga mō ngā tamariki katoa. Ko te arotake hōtaka e whakamahia e ngā kaimahi hei aroturuki i ngā whakaritenga me ngā akoranga i whakamahereita ai mā ngā tamariki. Kua mahi ngātahi te whānau me tō rātou marae ki te āta tiaki i ngā whare me ngā rawa o te kōhanga reo, kia haumaru ai te noho o ngā tamariki.

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia whakapai ake te whānau i ngā mahi ki te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakatūruki Arotake Māori Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

Supplementary Review Report: Te Kōhanga Reo o Ōrongomai Marae

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of the kōhanga reo governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the previous ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • the quality of education including programme planning, implementation, assessment, evaluation, behaviour management practices and leading and supporting staff
  • whānau management including kōhanga reo operations, relationships, appraisals and self review
  • areas of non-compliance
  • and any issues identified since the previous review.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ōrongomai Marae is based at Ōrongomai marae in Upper Hutt, Wellington. This supplementary review evaluates the extent to which progress by the whānau is positively impacting on the quality of learning and care for children. Areas for development included programme planning, assessment and evaluation. Behaviour management practices needed to be improved. Other issues identified included staff appraisal and self review.

ERO’s guiding evaluation question was:

To what extent are children stimulated and actively engaged in learning that is meaningful and purposeful?

Children are stimulated and actively engaged in learning at kōhanga reo. Kaimahi and whānau have worked hard to develop more effective planning practices. Children enjoy a wide range of meaningful and purposeful learning experiences. Their needs and interests are now well considered in the planning of the learning programme. Assessment and programme evaluation is now well understood and practiced. Children experience positive and caring interactions. They enjoy learning and using te reo Māori. Children are settled; confident and happy.

Whānau and kaimahi have a shared focus on providing positive outcomes for children. The kōhanga reo is managed well in the interests of children. The Ōrongomai Marae committee has oversight of kōhanga reo operations with strong support from whānau. Members recognise that there are areas for development that will enhance management of the kōhanga reo and they realisation of their strategic goals.

3 Findings

Quality of Education

Background

To what extent are children stimulated and actively engaged in learning that is meaningful and purposeful?

Children are stimulated and actively engaged in learning at kōhanga reo.

Areas of progress

Children are stimulated and actively engaged in learning at kōhanga reo. Kaimahi and whānau have worked hard to develop more effective planning practices. Children enjoy a wide range of meaningful and purposeful learning experiences. Their needs and interests are now well considered in the planning of the learning programme. Te Whāriki is well reflected in plans which promote learning te reo Māori. Kaimahi appropriately evaluate the daily programme. Assessment and evaluation information focus on children’s development and the effectiveness of programme activities and practices to support children’s learning.

Children show growing confidence and ability to use te reo Māori. The use and learning of te reo Māori is modelled and promoted by kaimahi. Children confidently lead karakia and share their pepeha with others. Kaimahi use a range of strategies that are both formal and informal to support children’s language development.

Children experience positive and caring interactions at kōhanga reo. The learning environment supports children’s physical, social, and cultural development. Routines and expectations are well established. Children are happy, settled and show a strong sense of belonging to their whānau, the kōhanga reo and the marae.

Children are settle and attentive. There is improved leadership and direction amoung kaimahi. They work well together to provide children with a programme that is structured and flexible to respond to children’s needs.

Whānau Management

Areas of progress

The kōhanga reo is managed well, in the interests of children. The Ōrongomai Marae committee now has management oversight of kōhanga reo operations. Improvements with management are strongly evident. Regular self review is in place. Whānau receive regular reporting on all areas of kōhanga reo operations. Whānau are strongly focused on achieving positive outcomes for their children.

Areas for further development

Current appraisal processes are incomplete. The whānau recognise the importance of using kaimahi appraisals to inform kaimahi professional development and support improvements in the programme. They are currently strengthening the existing appraisal system for effectiveness.

Reporting outcomes for children in relation to strategic priorities are limited. Whānau reports do not include regular updates about progress. This should contribute to the overall self review of the kōhanga reo for children.

Areas of non-compliance

Areas of progress

Children receive programmes of learning that are periodically reviewed to determine the effectiveness for all children. Kaimahi use programme review to monitor practices and planned learning for children. The whānau have worked closely with their marae whānau to ensure the kōhanga reo property and premises are maintained for children’s safety.

4 Recommendations

ERO recommends that whānau improve kaimahi appraisal practice.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.

Lynda Pura Watson

Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)
