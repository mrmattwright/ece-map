1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā tētahi ō ngā Kaiwhakahaere Matua ā-Rohe o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei, kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai e te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i mua atu, me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

  • te Mana Reo, tae atu ki te whakawhānui i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero e pā ana ki ngā akoranga, te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga o te noho ā-rōpū, me ngā whakawhitinga mai i tētahi mahi ki tētahi atu
  • te whakapakari i te whai wāhi a te whānau
  • te whakamahere mō meāke nei, me te arotake whaiaro, tae atu ki te tūtohinga me te arotake i ngā kaupapa here
  • te whakawhānui i ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki
  • ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutukihia ana e ai ki te ture
  • ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai whai muri i te arotake o mua.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Mātaatua ki Māngere ki te taha o te marae o Mātaatua, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau. Ka ahu mai ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo i te takiwā nei.

Kua whakatutukihia e te whānau te nuinga o ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai ki te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, hei whakawhanake ake. He pai te whakatutukitanga o ngā rerekētanga pai ki te maha o ngā wāhanga o te kōhanga reo. He kaha te ārahi, te aronga hoki a te whānau. Ka tino hāpaitia ngā kaimahi e te purapura me Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo. He ātaahua, he atawhai hoki ngā taiao o roto, o waho hoki mō ngā tamariki, ā, ka āta tiakina hoki. Ka tino manaakitia ngā tamariki, ā, he harikoa, he ratarata hoki.

He pai te whakahaeretanga o te hōtaka akoranga. He pai ngā rauemi e whakamahia ana hei hāpai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ka kitea te hōhonutanga o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. Ko te nui ake o te tautoko ki ngā rūma o ngā tuākana me ngā tēina e hāpai ana i te whakahaeretanga ake o ngā tamariki. Ka āta mātakina ngā whakawhitinga, mai i tētahi mahi ki tētahi atu.

Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā mahi aromatawai hei aroturuki, hei aromatawai hoki i te ahunga whakamua o ia tamaiti. Ka whai hua ā rātou kōrerorero e pā ana ki ngā paetae a ngā tamariki me tā rātou ahunga whakamua. E noho wātea ana aua mōhiohio ki te whānau.

He mātātoa te whai wāhi a ngā mema o te whānau ki ngā āhuatanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. He pakari te tae ā-tinana atu ki ngā hui o ia marama, ā, ka whakatau ngātahi i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te tiaki i ngā rawa, te whakahaere i ngā pūtea, te whakahaere i te tari, me te whakamahere mō meāke nei. Whakamahia ai i ia te wā ngā pūrongo e whai hua ana, me ngā mōhiohio, hei whakapai ake i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, me tō rātou haumaru, tō rātou waiora hoki. Whakawhiti kōrero ai rātou i ia te wā i te whakawhanaketanga me te arotake anō hoki o te tūtohinga me ngā kaupapa here.

Kua whakatutukihia e te whānau ngā wāhanga hei whakatutuki i ngā herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki ngā akoako hōneatanga me ngā whakangungu inā he ahi, he rū whenua rānei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Reo

He whakamārama

I tāutuhia ki te pūrongo arotake tāpiri o mua, ko ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ako tētahi āhuatanga hei whakawhanake ake. Ko te akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro i roto i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki, ko te whakahaere hoki i ngā rōpū, me te roa rawa o ngā tamariki e tatari ana, ētahi atu āhuatanga hei whakapai ake.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te reo Māori. E tino hāpaitia te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i ngā tāngata e matatau ana ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, puta noa i te rā. Ka ākina ngā kaimahi me te whānau kia whai wāhi ki ngā kaupapa whakangungu o te reo Māori. Ka whakaatuhia ngā karakia, ngā waiata, ngā tūtohi me ngā tohu puta noa i te kōhanga reo, hei tino tirohanga mā te whānau me ngā kaimahi i a rātou e whakaako ana.

Ngā whakawhitinga kōrero e whai pūtake ana. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te pai o ngā pūkenga kōrero i a rātou e whakawhiti ana i ō rātou whakaaro ki ētahi atu. I ngā kaimahi e mahi ana me ngā tamariki, ka pātai, ka akiaki hoki rātou i ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro. He tākare te kōrero a ngā tamariki ki waenga i a rātou anō, ā, ka ngākau nui ki te whakapuaki whakaaro ki te taha o ō rātou hoa. Ka whakarongo ngā kaimahi ki ngā kōrerorero a ngā tamariki i a rātou e tā pikitia ana. Ka kapo atu ngā kaimahi i aua kōrero mā te whakatakoto i aua kōrero ki ngā pikitia a ngā tamariki. Ka ākina ngā tamariki kia whakamahi i te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa. He pakari te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ngā kaimahi hoki.

Te hāpai i te ako reo. He pai te whakamātau a ngā tamariki i raro i te rua o ngā tau, i te reo. He kaha tā rātou whakamahi i ngā kupu māmā. Ka tuhi ngā kaimahi i ngā tūmomo kōrero ka maringi mai i ngā tamariki, ā, ka whai ake i aua kupu i a rātou e taunekeneke ana. I ngā wā katoa, ka whakatahuri ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki i raro i te rua o ngā tau, ki te whakawhiti kōrero, ka akiaki hoki i a rātou ki te whakautu, me te whakamahi anō hoki i ngā kupu hou ki ngā kīanga. He pai ki ngā tamariki te tāruarua i aua kīanga, me te whakahuahua i ngā momo oro. Ka ngākau nui ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhanake i te reo o ngā tamariki.

Ngā whakawhitinga. He pai te rere o ngā whakawhitinga, me te mātakitaki i aua wā. Kāhore ngā tamariki e tatari ana mō ngā wā roa i nāianei, i a rātou e whakawhiti ana mai i tētahi taumahi ki tētahi atu. Ka āwhinatia hoki rātou inā e tika ana, ā, he pai hoki te tohatoha a ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā mahi. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā kēmu, ngā rotarota, me ngā pātai kia ū tonu ai te aro nui o ngā tamariki ki ngā mahinga. Ko ngā taumahi a ngā rōpū iti e hāpai ana i ngā kaimahi ki te mahi ki te taha o ētahi tamariki takitahi, me te aroturuki hoki i te whai wāhitanga atu a ngā tamariki. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā kēmu me ngā rotarota.

Ngā mahinga. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te āwhina, ā, he kaha tā rātou whai i ngā tūmomo kawa, ngā tūmomo mahinga hoki ki te kōhanga reo. Kua āta whakaritea ngā mahinga mā ngā tamariki. E mōhio ana rātou ki ngā tūmanako, ā, ka kawe rātou i ngā mahi ki te whakatika haere, te manaaki o tētahi ki tētahi, me te rere pai o ngā mahinga ki te rūma horoi tinana, ngā wā kai anō hoki.

Te taiao. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te taiao o te kōhanga reo. He wāhi e whakatau ana i ngā tamariki, ā, he muramura, he ātaahua hoki. Kua roa te whānau e aro nui ana ki te whakahou ake me te tiaki i ngā wāhi o roto, o waho hoki i te kōhanga reo. Ka whakaongaongatia ngā tamariki i a rātou e tūhura ana i ngā tūmomo āhuatanga, taumahi hoki ki te kōhanga reo.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I tāutu te pūrongo o te arotake tāpiri o mua i te hiahia kia tahuri ake te whānau ki te whai wāhi atu ki te kōhanga reo. I ākina te whānau kia whakapai ake i ngā mahi e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro. Tāpiri atu ki tēnei, kāhore te mahere rautaki i hāpai i te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ā rātou whāinga. I taua wā, me whakawhanake ake ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whai wāhi atu a te whānau. He pai te mahi ngātahi a te whānau. He ngākau nuitanga tō rātou ki te whakarato i ngā painga mō ā rātou tamariki. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te whai wāhi mai o te whānau ki te kōhanga reo. Tae atu ai te whānau katoa ki ngā hui i ia te wā. Ka whakatau ngātahi rātou i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te tuku mahi, te whakahaere pūtea, te hauora me te haumaru, te whakangungu me ngā amuamu. Kua mahi ngātahi te whānau ki te whakapai ake i te whare. Kua whai wāhi te whānau ki ngā wānanga o ngā rā whakatā, mō ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki. Kua hāpai tētahi tukanga whakauru i te whānau ki te noho mārama ki ō rātou tūranga me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. Ka whakatinana te whānau i te mana whakahaere, ā, ka whakatau i te aronga o te kōhanga reo.

Ka whakanui te whānau i te wairua mauritau ki waenga i a rātou. He wāhi harikoa te kōhanga reo, ā, ka noho kotahi te whānau. Ka kitea tēnei ki te whanonga o ngā tamariki. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki.

Te arotake whaiaro. Ko te aronga a te whānau ki ngā whakaaturanga o te arotake whaiaro me ngā take whakahaere e whakatairanga ana anō hoki i te waiora o ngā tamariki. Kua āta tirohia e te whānau tō rātou tūnga, ā, kua arotakengia ngā wāhanga e tika ana. Ka whakatutuki te whānau i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea, ā, ka whai i ngā tukanga e tika ana inā he maiki. He rārangi arowhai e pā ana ki te papa tākaro o waho, hei ārahi i te arowhai i te haumaru o ngā taputapu. Kua arotakengia hoki ētahi o ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga.

Te whakamahere rautaki. Kei te whakawhanakehia te whakamahere rautaki i tēnei wā. He kaupapa ā te whānau, pērā i te whakawhanake ake i te papa tākaro o waho, te ārai, me te tiaki i te whare. Kua whakaritea hoki tētahi wānanga reo Māori mō te whānau. Ka hono te mahere rautaki ki ngā mōhiohio i te arotake whaiaro. E tīmata ana te whānau ki te whakamahi i tētahi aronga nahanaha ki te whakapai ake.

Te whakawhānui i ngā pūkete a ia tamaiti. Kei ngā pūkete ā ia tamaiti te hōhonutanga o ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki tā rātou ahunga whakamua, ā rātou mahi angitu hoki. He mōhiohio ā ngā pūkete. Kei ngā tauira mahi me ngā whakaahua o ngā tamariki ngā kōrerorero matawhānui e pā ana ki ā rātou mahi angitu me tā rātou ahunga whakamua. Ka whai pūtake ngā whakaahua me ngā pakiwaitara a ngā tamariki, ā, he auaha hoki. Kei ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki ngā mōhiohio e whai hua ana, hei tirohanga anō hoki mā te whānau.

Ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture

He whakamārama

I te pūrongo o mua o te arotake tāpiri, me whakapai ake ngā tikanga ohorere, tae atu ki te arotake i ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Kua whakatutukihia e te whānau ā rātou tikanga ohorere me ngā tūmomo tuhinga e whai pānga ana ki tēnei. Kua whakatutukihia e te kōhanga reo aua tikanga herenga ā-ture.

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia whakawhanake tonu te whānau i ō rātou mōhiotanga, ō rātou pūkenga hoki hei whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga i te kōhanga reo, ā, kia whakaoti hoki rātou i te mahere rautaki.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

13 Whiringa-ā-Rangi 2014

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of the kōhanga reo governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the previous ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • mana reo including extending learning conversations, managing group situations and transitions
  • strengthening whānau involvement and participation
  • future planning and self review including charter and policy review
  • extending children’s profiles
  • areas of non-compliance
  • any issues identified since the previous review.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Mataatua ki Mangere is next to Mataatua Marae, Auckland. Children from the local area attend the kōhanga reo.

The whānau have addressed the majority of areas for development that were identified in the previous ERO report. Positive changes have occurred in numerous parts of the kōhanga reo. There is strong leadership and direction among the whānau. Kaimahi receive valuable support from the purapura and Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT). The indoor and outdoor environments are attractive and welcoming for children and are in good repair. Children are well cared for, happy and friendly.

The learning programme is managed well. Good resources are used to support children’s learning and development. Rich conversations between kaimahi and children are evident. Extra support in the tuakana and teina rooms contributes to better management of children. Transitions are well supervised.

Kaimahi use a range of assessment practices to monitor and assess each child’s progress. They make constructive comments about children’s achievements and progress. This information is available to whānau.

Whānau members are actively involved in all aspects of the kōhanga reo. Regular attendance at monthly meetings is strong and there is collective decision making about property maintenance, financial management, administration and future planning. Regular, constructive reporting and information sharing is used to improve children’s learning, safety and wellbeing. Charter and policy development and review is discussed regularly.

The whānau have addressed the areas of compliance relating to fire and earthquake drills and training.

3 Findings

Mana Reo

Background

The previous supplementary review report identified learning conversations as an area for development. Encouraging children to express their ideas in various situations, group management and making children wait for long periods were other areas requiring improvement.

Areas of progress

Te reo Māori. Children are supported well in their language development. Children hear confident speakers of te reo Māori throughout the day. Kaimahi and whānau are encouraged to participate in te reo Māori training courses. Karakia, waiata, charts and signs are displayed around the kōhanga reo and are highly visible for whānau and kaimahi to refer to as a learning aid.

Meaningful conversations. Children are developing good oral skills in communicating their thinking to others. When kaimahi work with children they ask questions and encourage children to share their ideas. Children eagerly talk to each other and are keen to share with their peers. Kaimahi listen to children’s stories as they create pictures. Kaimahi capture these comments by recording children’s responses on their drawings. Children are encouraged to use te reo Māori at all times. Children engage confidently in conversations with each other and with kaimahi.

Supporting language learning. The under two year olds appropriately take risks with language. They use simple words confidently. Kaimahi note any words children utter and follow these up when they interact with them. Kaimahi consistently engage the under two year olds in conversation, encourage them to respond and incorporate new words in phrases. Children are happy to repeat these phrases and enjoy mimicking sounds. Kaimahi are committed to developing children’s language.

Transitions. Transitions are smooth and effectively supervised. Children no longer wait for long periods of time as they transition from one activity to the next. Extra help is available and tasks are shared effectively among kaimahi and whānau. Kaimahi use games, rhymes and questions to capture children’s attention during routines. Small group activities enable kaimahi to work alongside individuals and monitor children’s levels of participation. Children are willing participants in games and rhymes.

Routines. Children are willing helpers and confidently follow the different protocols and routines in the kōhanga reo. Children have well established routines. They are aware of expectations and they take responsibility for tidying up, caring for each other and for maintaining orderly bathroom and mealtime routines.

Environment. Children enjoy the kōhanga reo environment. It is a welcoming place for children and is colourful and attractive. The whānau have spent a considerable amount of time renovating and maintaining the indoor and outdoor areas of the kōhanga reo. Children are stimulated as they explore the different features and activities in the kōhanga reo.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

The previous supplementary review report identified the need for more whānau involvement and participation. The whānau were encouraged to improve self review practices. In addition, strategic planning did not support whānau to achieve their goals. Further development of children’s profiles was required.

Areas of progress

Whānau involvement and participation. The whānau work well together. They have a shared commitment providing the best for their children. Children benefit from the presence of whānau in the kōhanga reo. All whānau attend hui regularly. They make collective decisions regarding employment, financial management, health and safety, training and complaints. Whānau have worked together to improve the building. Whānau have participated in weekend wānanga relating to their roles and responsibilities. An induction process has supported whānau to understand their roles and the kōhanga reo philosophy. The whānau practice mana whakahaere and set the direction for the kōhanga reo.

The whānau acknowledge there is a positive wairua amongst its members. The kōhanga reo is a happy place and there is unity among whānau. This is reflected in the children’s behaviour. Children are content and settled.

Self review. Whānau responsiveness to the findings of self review and operational matters promotes children’s wellbeing. The whānau have critically examined where they are at and have reviewed areas that require attention. The whānau address hazards and they follow appropriate procedures for accidents. There is an outdoor playground checklist to guide safety inspections of equipment. There has been some policy and procedure review.

Strategic planning. Strategic planning is under development. Whānau have projects such as the outdoor playground development, shading and building maintenance. A te reo Māori wānanga for the whānau is planned. The strategic plan is linked to self review information. The whānau are beginning to use a planned approach to improvements.

Extending children’s individual profiles. Children’s individual profiles contain valuable information about their progress and achievements. Profiles are informative. Children’s work samples and photos have constructive comments about their successes and progress. Children’s pictures and stories are meaningful and creative. Children’s profiles contain useful information which are available to whānau.

Areas of non-compliance

Background

In the previous supplementary review report emergency practices needed to be improved including the review of policies and procedures.

Areas of progress

The whānau have addressed their emergency practices and the related documentation is in place. The kōhanga reo have met these compliance requirements.

4 Recommendations

ERO recommends the whanau continue to develop their knowledge and skills in managing the kōhanga reo operations and complete the strategic plan.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)
