1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā tētahi o ngā Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia ai i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai e te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i mua atu, me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira, tae atu ki te wāhi o waho mō ngā tamariki
  • te whakamahere me te aromātai, tae atu ki te hauora, te haumaru, me ngā take rawa, ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau, ngā tūranga kaimahi, te arotake me te pūrongo i te hōtaka, ngā aromatawai a ngā tamariki, te arotake whaiaro, te toha pūtea, me te whakamahere rautaki
  • ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture
  • me ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai whai muri i te arotake o mua.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūporo Tuhikiterangi ki Rotorua. He maha ngā take i tāutuhia ai hei whakapai ake ki te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i te tau 2013. I hāngai aua take ki te hauora me te haumaru, te whakahaere rawa, ngā tukanga hōneatanga, ngā take kaimahi, me ngā rauemi. He take anō i hāngai ai ki te whakahaeretanga me te whakaritenga a te whānau, ngā mahi aromātai hōtaka, me ngā aromatawai. Ka aromātai tēnei arotake tāpiri i te ahunga whakamua a te whānau ki te whakatutuki i aua take.

Ināianei, kei te wheako ngā tamariki i te haumaru o tētahi taiao tākaro, ā, kua whakapau kaha te whānau kia noho haumaru ai ā rātou tamariki. Kua whakatutukihia e te whānau ngā tūmomo take i hāngai ai ki te hauora me te haumaru, me ngā āwangawanga hoki ki ngā rawa i tāutuhia ai hei āhuatanga mōrearea. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i ngā tino hononga ki te kaiako matua hou. E ārahi ana te kaiako matua i ngā kaimahi i roto i ā rātou mahi whakaako. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, kei te whakawhanake tonu rātou i tō rātou māramatanga ki te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te ngākau nui o ngā mema o te whānau ki tō rātou waiora me ā rātou akoranga. Kua tino pai ake ngā mahi e pā ana ki te whakamahere rautaki, te arotake whaiaro, te arohaehae, me te whakahaere pūtea. Ināianei, he pakari ake te whai wāhi a te whānau ki ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He mārama te pūtake, te aronga hoki a te whānau mō ā rātou tamariki.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

I tāutuhia ki te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ētahi take i hāngai ai ki te wāhi o waho mō ngā tamariki. He iti rawa te onepū mā ngā tamariki ki te rua kirikiri. Kāhore i te nui ngā wāhi mō ngā tamariki i a rātou e whakamahi ana i ō rātou paihikara. Me whakatika, me tīni rānei i ngā hanganga tākaro mō ngā tamariki kōhungahunga me ngā pēpi. Kāhore he papa e tika ana kia haumaru ai te nuku haere o ngā pēpi. I kitea hoki te kaha noho mai o ngā pōpokorua ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Kua whakatinanahia e te whānau te maha o ngā tukanga hauora, haumaru hoki hei hāpai i te noho haumaru o ā rātou tamariki i a rātou e ako ana, e tākaro ana hoki. Kua whakatinanahia e te whānau tētahi huringa ki te arotake i te tiaki o ngā rawa, me tētahi rehita hoki, ā, e whakamahia ana ēnei. Kua whakakīia te rua kirikiri ki te onepū. Kua whai pānga tēnei ki te haumaru o ngā tamariki ki waho. Kua tango te whānau i ngā tūmomo hanganga tākaro, tae atu ki te papa, ā, kua tohua hoki tētahi pūtea mō te tīni haere i aua āhuatanga. I toro atu rātou ki tētahi ratonga ngaio, ā, kāhore rātou i tāutu i tētahi pokipokitanga pōpokorua. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te wāhi o waho, ā, he whakahihiri, he ātaahua hoki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te maha o ngā wāhanga hei tirohanga ake. Kāhore te whānau i tuhi, i pūrongo rānei i ngā wā katoa, ngā take i hāngai ai ki ngā tiaki o ngā rawa, tae atu ki ngā āhuatanga mōrearea. Kāhore i te rawaka te whakatutuki haere i ngā mauhanga e pā ana ki ngā akoako hōneatanga, ngā aituā, me ngā maiki.

E ai ki taua pūrongo, kāhore te whānau i whai wāhi atu ki te kōhanga reo. He take anō hoki i hāngai ai ki ngā kaimahi, te arohaehae kaimahi, me te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo me te tari. I taua wā, me arotake te whānau i tā rātou pūtea, ā, me aro ake hoki rātou ki te ako a ngā tamariki. Me whakawhanake te arotake whaiaro hei whakatutuki i ngā take i tāutuhia ai ki te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. Ahakoa he mahere rautaki tā te whānau, kāhore he mahere mahi hei hāpai i te whakatinanatanga o tēnei.

Kāhore ngā kaimahi i arotake, i pūrongo rānei ki te whānau mō te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki. He whāiti ngā mahi aromatawai.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā mahi a te whānau ki te whakahaere me te whakarite i te kōhanga reo. Kua tīmata te whānau ki te haere ki ngā wānanga me ngā hui e hāngai ana ki Te Korowai me Te Whāriki. Kei te mōhio haere rātou ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo me ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi anō hoki. Kua āta tuhia tētahi mahere mahi hei hāpai i ngā kaupapa rautaki matua o te whānau. E noho pūmau ana te whānau ki te whakapai ake i ngā putanga akoranga mō ngā tamariki.

Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā ngā tūmomo mahi haumaru i whakatinana ai i te whānau nō nā tata tonu nei. He whānui ngā tūmomo kaupapa here, tukanga hoki hei ārahi i te whānau me ngā take hauora, haumaru hoki. E hāngai ana ki te rā ngā tukanga me ngā rehita e pā ana ki ngā aituā, ngā maiki, ngā hōneatanga, ngā āhuatanga mōrearea, ngā tamariki māuiui, me ngā tikanga rongoā, ā, e tino whakamahia ana hoki. Mā aua mahi hauora, haumaru hoki ka āhei ai te kawhakaheke ngā tūmomo āhuatanga mōrearea mō ngā tamariki.

Kua pai ake ngā whai wāhitanga a ngā tamariki ki te ako me te whakawhanake, whai muri tonu mai i te rerekētanga o ngā tūnga kaimahi. Kua whakatūngia e te whānau tētahi kaiako hoki, ā, he matatau ia, e whakarato ana hoki i te ārahitanga ngaio ki ngā kaimahi. Kua arohaehaengia ngā kaimahi katoa, ā, kua tāutuhia anō hoki ā rātou whāinga ngaio.

Te wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake

Kāhore tonu te ahunga whakamua, ngā wheako hoki a ngā tamariki e tino aroturukitia ana, e tino aromātaitia ana rānei. Ahakoa he pūkete tā ia tamaiti, me āta arotahi ake anō tonu ngā kaimahi ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, kaua noa ki ngā take whānui. Me whakapai ake tonu i te aromātai. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e hui ana i ia te wā, ki te aromātai i te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki, i ā rātou ake mahi whakaako rānei.

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

whakapai ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aroturuki i te ahunga whakamua me te aromātai i te hōtaka

whakapakari tonu ngā mahi e pā ana ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo me tari.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of the kōhanga reo governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the previous ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira including the children’s outdoor area
  • Planning and Evaluation including health, safety and property matters, whānau management practices, staff roles, programme review and reporting, children’s assessments, self review, budget allocations and strategic planning
  • Areas of non-compliance
  • and any issues identified during this review.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūporo Tuhikiterangi is in Rotorua. A number of issues that needed attention were identified in the previous ERO report of 2013. These issues included health and safety, property management, evacuation processes, personnel matters and resources. There were further issues with whānau management and administration, programme evaluation practices and assessments. This supplementary review evaluates the progress the whānau has made towards addressing these matters.

Children now experience a safe play environment where whānau have taken all reasonable actions to safeguard their children. Whānau have addressed the various health and safety matters and property concerns that were identified as risks. Children are developing strong relationships with the new senior kaiako. The lead kaiako is guiding staff with their teaching practices. Staff acknowledge they are still developing their understanding of assessment and evaluation.

Children benefit from whānau members who are committed to their wellbeing and learning. Practices for strategic planning, self review, appraisal and financial management have improved significantly. There is now stronger whānau engagement in the kōhanga reo operations. Whānau have a clear sense of purpose and direction for their children.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

The previous ERO report identified issues with the children’s outdoor area. The sandpit had very little sand for children. There were insufficient surfaces for children to use when they rode their bikes. The play structures for toddlers and infants needed repairing or replacing. Babies needed an appropriate surface for safe movement. There was evidence of ant infestation.

Areas of progress

Whānau have implemented a number of health and safety measures to help safeguard their children as they learn and play. The whānau have implemented a maintenance review cycle and register both of which are operational. The sandpit has been filled with sand. This has added to the safety of the children whilst outdoors. The whānau have removed various play structures including the surfacing and have set aside a budget to replace these. They hired a professional agency who found no evidence of ant infestations. Children confidently explore the outdoor area which is welcoming and attractive.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

The previous ERO report identified several areas that required attention. The whānau did not always record or report property maintenance matters including hazards. Records of drills, accidents and incidents were not adequately addressed.

The report stated that whānau did not play an active role in the kōhanga reo. There were particular issues with personnel, staff appraisals and general management and administration. The whānau needed to review their budget and have a stronger focus on children’s learning. Self review needed to be developed to address the issues in the previous ERO report. Although whānau had a strategic plan, there was no action plan to support its implementation.

Kaimahi did not review and report to whānau about the children’s learning programme. Assessment practices were limited.

Areas of progress

Children are the central focus for whānau when they manage and organise the kōhanga reo. Whānau have begun to attend wānanga and hui associated with Te Korowai and Te Whāriki. They are becoming more aware of the kaupapa of the kōhanga reo and their roles and responsibilities. There is a well written action plan to support whānau strategic priorities. Whānau are committed to improving children’s learning outcomes.

Children benefit from the safety measures whānau have recently implemented. There are a range of policies and procedures to guide whānau with health and safety matters. Procedures and registers for accidents, incidents, evacuations, hazards, unwell children and medication requirements are up to date and are well applied. These health and safety practices help minimise potential risks to children.

Children’s learning and development opportunities have improved since recent staffing changes. The whānau have appointed a new kaiako who is an experienced practioner and provides professional guidance and direction to kaimahi. All kaimahi have been appraised and have had their professional goals identified.

Areas for further improvement

Children’s progress and experiences are still not adequately monitored or evaluated. Although children have individual profiles, kaimahi still need to focus more on children’s learning rather than general matters. Evaluation continues to need improvement. Kaimahi do not meet regularly to evaluate the children’s learning programme or their teaching practices.

4 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • improve practices for monitoring children’s progress and programme evaluation
  • continue to strengthen management and administration practices.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)
