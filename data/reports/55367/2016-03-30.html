Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Rēwai ki Pāharakeke, ki Heretaunga. Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao o te reo Māori. He tākare te ako a ngā tamariki, ā, he tau hoki rātou ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakahaere te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Rēwai i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Rēwai ki Pāharakeke, ki Heretaunga. Ko te kaiako tētahi ākonga o mua o te kōhanga reo, ā, kei te kawe ia i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whai pānga nui te whanaungatanga ki ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka noho matua te reo Māori ki ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i te manaakitanga?

He māoriori, he māhorahora hoki te whakatinanatanga o ngā tamariki i te manaakitanga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira 

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e hāpai ana i a rātou. Ka whai pānga nui ngā uaratanga o te manaaki, te whakaute, me te aroha ki ngā akoranga. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whakatinana i aua uaratanga. Nā ngā taumahi ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i ngā whai wāhitanga ki te āta taunekeneke ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ō rātou tēina hoki. Kua whakaritea ngā rautaki hei āta whakatau pai i ngā take ki waenga i ngā hoa. He tākare te ako a ngā tamariki.

Ko te whakawhanaketanga i te katoa o te tamaiti tētahi aronga matua. E arotahi ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakatutuki i ngā matea o ia tamaiti. Ko tō rātou mōhio ki ia tamaiti ka hāpai i tā rātou aro nui ki ō rātou ake matea. Ko te rere ngāwari o te hōtaka o ia rā ka hāpai i ngā kaimahi ki te mahi ki te taha o ia tamaiti, ki ngā rōpū tamariki rānei. Nā ngā taumahi ka whakaatu ngā tamariki i te āhua o tō rātou manaaki, tō rātou tautoko hoki i ō rātou hoa. Ka ākina ngā tuākana kia whanake ai ngā pūkenga o te ārahitanga. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga ako, i runga i te āhua o ō rātou ngākau nuitanga. He tau ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka tautokona ngā tamariki kia ako i te reo Māori. Ka rumakina rātou ki te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Ka arotahi ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhanake i te reo o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā pātai, ngā pukapuka, me ngā kupu māmā hei hāpai i te kōrero a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro mā te reo ā-tinana, te reo ā-kanohi, me ngā oro e tika ana mō tō rātou reanga. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā kaimahi ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero me ngā tamariki, ā, ka aroturuki hoki i tā rātou ahu whakamua ki te ako i te reo Māori. Ka tūmāia ngā tamariki ki ngā horopaki o te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

Ka āta whakamaheretia ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He mārama ngā tukanga ā ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere i ngā hōtaka. Ka whai whakaaro ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea o ia tamaiti, o ngā tūmomo reanga anō hoki. Ka hāngai ngā taumahi ki te kaupapa o te wā me te ao Māori. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi ako e whai pūtake ana ki a rātou.

Aroturukitia ai te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki i ia te wā. Ka whai hua te whakamahinga o ngā pūkete hei whakaemi i ngā mōhiohio, ā, hei pūrongo hoki ki te whānau. Ka whakamahia ngā tuhinga mātakitaki hei aru i ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahia te angamahi o Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira hei ārahi i te aromatawai. He pai ngā mōhiohio ka whakaratohia mā ia whānau mō te ako o tā rātou tamaiti.

Ka whakahaere te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei tino painga mō ā rātou tamariki. Kua whakawhanakehia e rātou tētahi mahere rautaki i ahu mai ai i ā rātou whāinga, ā, i whakahāngaitia ai hoki ki te angamahi o Te Ara Tūāpae. Ko ngā pūrongo auau ka whakapuakihia ki te whānau mō te ahu whakamua. Ko ngā pūrongo a ngā kaimahi mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, e arotahi ana ki te reo Māori me tō rātou waiora. Ka whai pānga nui hoki te whakamahere mō te tauatanga ki te whakapakaritanga o ngā mahi whakahaere ki te kōhanga reo me te āhua o tōna aronga mō meāke nei. Ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā whakawhanaketanga ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore te aromātai hōtaka e whakamōhio ana i te whakapaitanga ake o ngā whakaritenga. Kua tāutuhia e te kaiako, e tika ana kia tū auau ngā hui hei aromātai i te āhua o te hōtaka ki te whakatutuki i ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki katoa.

Kei te whanake haere te whakahaeretanga o ngā kaimahi. Ahakoa he tūmanako i āta tāutuhia ai mō ngā arohaehae, kāhore ēnei e hono ana ki ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi. E mōhio ana te whānau, me whai wāhi hoki te whakaaro huritao, ngā kōrero urupare hoki o ngā kaimahi hei āhuatanga tonu o te tukanga arohaehae.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.  

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia tahuri te whānau ki te:

  • whakarite i te hononga o ngā arohaehae katoa a ngā kaimahi ki ā rātou tuhinga tūranga mahi
  • mahi ngātahi ki ngā kaimahi, hei aromātai i te hōtaka akoranga.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Rēwai ki Pāharakeke, ki Heretaunga. Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao o te reo Māori. He tākare te ako a ngā tamariki, ā, he tau hoki rātou ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakahaere te whānau i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Rēwai is in Flaxmere, Hastings. Children are immersed in a te reo Māori environment. Children are enthusiastic learners and settled at kōhanga reo. Whānau manage the kōhanga reo in the best interests of their children. 

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Rēwai will be in three years. 

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Rēwai is in Flaxmere, Hastings. The kaiako is a kōhanga reo graduate and is responsible for the learning programme. Whanaungatanga is an integral part of kōhanga reo operations. Te reo Māori is a priority for children and their whānau.

2  The Review Findings

How well do children show manaakitanga?

Children show manaakitanga naturally and spontaneously.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children learn in a supportive environment. Values of care, respect and aroha are an integral part of learning. Kaimahi encourage children to practice these values. Activities provide children with opportunities to interact positively with each other and their teina. Strategies to resolve issues between peers in a positive way are in place. Children are enthusiastic learners.

Children’s holistic development is a priority. Kaimahi are focused on catering for individual children’s needs. Their knowledge about individuals helps them to respond to specific needs. Flexibility in the daily programme enables kaimahi to work alongside individuals and, or groups of children. Activities enable children to show how they care and support their peers. Tuakana are encouraged to develop leadership skills. Kaimahi encourage children to make decisions about learning based on their interests. Children are settled in the kōhanga reo.

Children are supported to learn te reo Māori. They are immersed in te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Kaimahi focus on developing children’s language. They use questions, books and basic words to support children with speaking te reo Māori. Kaimahi encourage children to express themselves using body language, facial expressions and sounds appropriate for their age. Kaimahi engage in conversations with children and monitor their progress in learning te reo Māori. Children are confident in te reo Māori environments.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s learning is well planned. Kaimahi have clear programme planning processes. Kaimahi consider the needs of individuals and ages groups. Activities are based on the current kaupapa and te ao Māori. Children are engaged in learning activities that are meaningful to them.

Children’s progress is regularly monitored. Profiles are used effectively to gather information and report to whānau. Observation sheets are used to track children’s interests. The Taumata Whakahirahira framework is used to guide assessment. Whānau have good information about their child’s learning.

Whānau manage the kōhanga reo in the best interests of their children. They have developed a strategic plan based on their goals and aligned to the Te Ara Tūāpae framework. Regular reporting about progress is shared with whānau. Kaimahi reports about children’s learning focus on te reo Māori and their wellbeing. Succession planning is an integral part of strengthening kōhanga operations and its future direction. Children are the focus of the kōhanga reo developments.

Key Next Steps

Programme evaluation does not inform improved practice. The kaiako has identified the need for regular meetings to evaluate how well the programme meets the learning needs of all children.

Personnel management is developing. Although there are clearly defined expectations about appraisal, these do not link to job descriptions. Whānau recognise the need to include self-reflection and feedback to kaimahi as part of the appraisal process.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations for whānau to:

  • ensure appraisals for all staff link to job descriptions
  • work collaboratively with kaimahi to evaluate the learning programme.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Rēwai is in Flaxmere, Hastings. Children are immersed in a te reo Māori environment. Children are enthusiastic learners and settled at kōhanga reo. Whānau manage the kōhanga reo in the best interests of their children. 

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Rēwai will be in three years.
