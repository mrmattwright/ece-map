1 Te Horopaki

E whakahaerehia ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rapa ki tētahi whare o te kura tawhito o Richmond Park, ki te taha tonu o Te Kōhanga Reo o Whanaungatanga. Kei te rīhi tonu te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i ngā whare e rua, mai i Te Tāhuhu o te Mātauranga. Whai muri i te arotake o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kua tino heke te kounga o ngā akoranga, te tatauranga tamariki, me te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kei te whakatāuteute rawa te whānau ki te whakapakari ake anō i te kōhanga reo, kia rōnaki tonu ai, kia whai hua ai, ā, kia whakahaere tōtikatia ai hoki.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i te whanaungatanga?

Kei te rongo ngā tamariki i ētahi āhuatanga o te whanaungatanga i tō rātou noho ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He pai ki ngā tamariki te tūhonohono ki ētahi tāngata me ētahi wāhi. Ka ngākau nui ia tamaiti ki te whakawhanaunga atu ki ngā manuhiri me te whānau ki te kōhanga reo. Ka kitea tō rātou kaha ki te manaaki i a rātou anō, ā, i ētahi atu hoki. Kei te māramahia ngā mahinga o ia rā e ngā tamariki. Kei te whakawhanake ngā tamariki i ō rātou ngākau titikaha.

E whakatairangatia ana e ngā kaimahi te reo Māori mā ngā karakia, ngā waiata, ngā tākaro, te pānui pukapuka, me ngā kaupapa mahi toi. Ko ētahi o ngā rautaki reo e whakamahia ana e ngā kaiako, ko te whāngai kupu, te tāruarua, me te whakakapi kōrerorero. Ka tāwhai ngā tamariki tēina i te reo Māori ki te taha o ō rātou hoa. Ka tūhonohono ngā tamariki ki te reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore e taea e ngā tamariki tō rātou taiao ako te āta whakapuaki rawa. Kāhore ngā wāhi ako e āta whakamahia ana e ngā kaimahi hei whakawhānui ake i ngā mahi tūhura a ngā tamariki. He mea nui kia āta hāpaitia ngā wheako ako o ngā tamariki mā te taiao me ngā rauemi e āta whakaritea ai.

Kāhore anō te whānau kia tango i te whare tākaro e takoto pakaru ana ki te wāhi tākaro o waho mō ngā tamariki. Kāhore anō ngā papa o waho kia whakawhanakehia hei wāhi ako, hei wāhi tūhura hoki e whai pūtake ana.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore ngā tamariki e āta tautokona ana e tō rātou whānau. Me tahuri wawe ki te whakapakari ake i ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau, ā, ki te whakapai ake hoki i te āhua o ngā mahi whakahaere, mahi whakarite anō hoki ki te tari me te kōhanga reo. Ko te iti noa o te whai wāhi mai o te whānau, ko te tokoiti tonu o te whānau e toro atu ana ki aua wāhanga whakahaere, me te tino heke o te tatauranga tamariki ki te kōhanga reo, kua pā tōraro katoa ki te whai huatanga o ngā mahi whakahaere hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Kua whakatūngia ētahi kaimahi ki te whakatinana me te aroturuki i te marautanga, engari ko rātou anō hoki e kawe atu ana i ētahi atu mahi, tae atu ki te whakahaeretanga o te tari, te taraiwa i te wēne, me te tiaki i ngā rawa. Ko te whakahaeretanga i ngā kaimahi tētahi take hei whakatutuki. Nā aua tūāhuatanga, kāhore ngā whakaritenga katoa o te hauora me te haumaru e whakapūmautia ana hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Kāhore ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki e āta whakamaheretia ana, e āta whakaarohia ana hoki. Tērā ētahi wāhanga o ngā mahere kāhore e tuhia ana, tae atu hoki ki ngā mahi e whakapuaki ai i ngā kaupapa ako ki ngā tūmomo rōpū ākonga. Kāhore e āta toro atu ana ki ngā whakaaro o te whānau i roto i te tukanga whakamahere. Kua tata te oti i te kaiako tana Tohu Whakapakari.

Kāhore te ako me te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki e āta tuhia ana, e āta pūrongotia ana hoki. Kāhore he tuhinga, he kōrero tāpiri rānei a ngā kaimahi ki te taha o ngā whakaahua, o ngā tauira mahi rānei hei whakamārama i te ako me te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki. E ai ki ngā kaimahi, kua tīni rātou i ngā kitenga ki tētahi tirohanga rārangi arowhai. Kāhore taua huarahi mahi e āta whakarato ana i te kiko o ngā mōhiohio hei tirohanga mā te whānau mō te āhua o ngā akoranga i whakatutukihia ai e ā rātou tamariki, i runga tonu anō hoki i te āhua o Te Whāriki o Te Kōhanga Reo.

Kāhore te haumaru me te atawhai o ngā tamariki e āta aroturukitia ana e te whānau. He iti noa ngā pūrongo me ngā mahi arotake puta noa i ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo, tae atu ki te hauora, te haumaru, me te atawhai. He maha ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga kāhore e arotakengia ana, e whakahoungia ana hoki i ia te wā. Kāhore i te rawaka te ako a te whānau me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā kaimahi. He whāiti noa ngā pūrongo a ngā kaiako ki te whānau mō te hōtaka akoranga. Kāhore ngā rehita katoa o te hauora me te haumaru e whakatutuki ana i ngā paearu raihana o tēnei wā. Kāhore ngā maiki e tuhia ana. Kāhore he pūnaha pakari e aroturuki ana, e arotake ana hoki hei āta whakatau i te kounga o te atawhai me te waiora mō ngā tamariki, ā, hei hāpai anō hoki i te tupu me te whakapai tonu o te kōhanga reo.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture. Kia tutuki i te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā herenga ā-ture, me tahuri rātou ki te:

  • whakamahere, te whakatinana, me te aromātai hoki i te marautanga o Te Whāriki o Te Kōhanga Reo, hei whakatairanga ake i te ako a ngā tamariki me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga mā te whakarato i ngā wheako akoranga e aro pū ana ki ngā ngākau nuitanga ako, ngā pūmanawa ako, me ngā āheinga ako o ngā tamariki, me tohu hoki ēnei i te māramatanga ki te ako me te whakawhanaketanga e hāngai ana ki ngā rangahau, ngā ariā, me ngā whakaritenga o te mātauranga kōhungahunga o tēnei wā
    [R43 (1)(a)(b), (2) Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; C2-4, 7-10 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • āta whakarite i te arowhaingia te mahana, te hā, te oranga hoki o ngā tamariki e moe ana i ia 5 ki te 10 meneti, i mua noa atu rānei hei whakatutuki i ngā matea o ētahi, ā, me tuhi hoki i ngā wā moe o ngā tamariki
    [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS9 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • āta tuhia te katoa o ngā aituā me ngā maiki i pā ai ki ngā tamariki i te ratonga, ā, me whai wāhi ki tēnei ko te ingoa o te tamaiti, te wā, te taima, te whakamāramatanga o te maiki, ngā mahi i whāia – i a wai hoki, me ngā whakaaturanga e tohu ana i whakamōhiotia ngā mātua
    [R 46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS25 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakarite kia arowhaingia ngā taputapu, ngā rawa, me ngā rauhanga i ia rā whakahaere mō ngā āhuatanga mōrearea pea ki ngā tamariki. Me tātari i ngā tuhinga aituā, tuhinga maiki hoki hei tāutu i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea, me ngā mahi e tika ana hei whai atu. Me whakakore, me whakataha rānei, me whakaheke rānei i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea e pā tōraro ana ki te haumaru o ngā tamariki.
    [R 46 (1)(c) Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS1, 12 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakarite i te noho mōhio o ngā pakeke e whakarato ana i te mātauranga me te atawhai, ki ngā akoako hōneatanga ohorere e tika ana - tae atu ki ērā o te rū whenua – a, kia whakatutukihia aua hōneatanga ki te taha o ngā tamariki i ia toru marama, i mua noa atu rānei
    [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS8 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakarite i te whakatinanatanga o ngā mahi pūmanawa tāngata e tika ana, tae atu ki ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te pūmanawa tāngata, me tētahi pūnaha ki te arohaehae i ia te wā, me te whakarato i te whakawhanaketanga ngaio
    [R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA7 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakarite i tētahi mahere ā-tau ka ārahi i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, ā, kia tāutu hoki tēnei i ngā tāngata e kawe ana i ngā mahi matua o ia tau, i te āhua o aua mahi, ā, i te wā hoki e tika ana ki te whakatutuki i aua mahi
    [R47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA8 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakarite i tētahi tukanga arotake whaiaro ka haere tonu i ia te wā, hei hāpai i te kōhanga reo ki te whakapūmau me te whakapai ake i te kounga o te mātauranga me te atawhai e tika ana mō ngā tamariki, ā, me whai wāhi anō hoki tētahi tukanga e arotake ana, e aromātai ana hoki i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, heoi, e whakatakoto ana hoki i ngā rārangi mahi e pā ana ki ngā mahi arotake i whakamaheretia ai mō ngā tūmomo wāhanga whakahaere, ā, me tuhi hoki i ngā putanga e hiahiatia ana i te tukanga arotake.
    [R47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA4,6 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • riro wawe i te whānau te wawaotanga o waho, hei hāpai i a rātou ki te whakatutuki i ngā take katoa i whakaraupapahia ai ki tēnei pūrongo
  • tuku te whānau i tētahi mahere mahi ki Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, ā, kia whakaraupapahia tēnei i te huarahi mahi me ngā rārangi wā ka whāia e rātou hei whakapai ake, hei tupu hoki i te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rapa operates out of a building at the former Richmond Park School site, alongside Te Kōhanga Reo o Whanaungatanga. Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) continue their lease with the Ministry of Education (MOE) for both centres. Since the last ERO review, the quality of learning, roll and the management of the kōhanga reo has significantly weakened. The whānau struggle to rebuild the kōhanga reo so that it is viable, productive and operating effectively.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children express whanaungatanga?

Children experience aspects of whanaungatanga during their time at kōhanga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children like to make connections with people and places. Individuals willingly interact with manuhiri and whānau at the kōhanga reo. They show care for self and others. Children understand daily routines. Children are developing confidence.

Kaimahi promote te reo Māori through karakia, waiata, play, panui pukapuka and art activities. Language strategies used by the kaiako include prompting, repetition and substitution. Younger children mimic te reo Māori beside their peers. Children make connections with language.

Key Next Steps

Children are not able to use their learning environment to its fullest. Existing learning spaces are not fully utilised by kaimahi to extend children’s exploration. It is important that children’s learning experiences are well supported by a well prepared environment and resources.

The whānau has not removed the broken playhouse in the children’s outdoor play area. The outdoor grounds have not been developed for purposeful outdoor learning and exploration.

Planning and Evaluation

Key Next Steps

Children are not actively supported by their whānau. Whānau management needs to strengthen with urgency, to improve the performance of administration, operations and management. The lack of whānau participation and involvement by few whānau in these areas’ and the significant drop in roll numbers has compromised effective management for children. Kaimahi employed to implement and monitor areas of curriculum have been left with additional responsibilities including administration, van driver and property management. Personnel management is an issue that requires addressing. Consequently, not all health and safety practices are upheld for children.

Children’s learning needs are not adequately planned and considered. There are parts to plans that are not documented, including how kaupapa learning will be delivered to the different groups of learners. Whānau are not adequately consulted in the planning process. The kaiako is nearing completion of her Tohu Whakapakari.

Children’s learning and progress is not adequately documented and reported. There are no kaimahi comments or annotations alongside photos or work samples that explain children’s learning and progress. Kaimahi report that they have replaced observations with a tick box overview. This method does not provide whānau with detailed information about the learning that has occurred for their children based on Te Whāriki o Te Kōhanga Reo.

Children’s safety and care at kōhanga reo is not carefully monitored by the whānau. There is limited reporting and review across all areas of the kōhanga reo, including health, safety and care. Many policies and procedures have not been regularly reviewed and updated. Whānau learning, and personnel development is inadequate. Kaiako reports to whānau about the learning programme are limited. Not all health and safety registers meet the current licensing criteria. The recording of accidents does not occur. Children are without robust monitoring and review systems that ensure their total care and wellbeing and inform kōhanga reo growth and improvement.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

ERO identified areas of non-compliance. In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements they need to:

  • plan, implement, and evaluate Te Whāriki o Te Kōhanga Reo curriculum to enhance children’s learning and development through the provision of learning experiences that responds to the learning interests, strengths, and capabilities of enrolled children, and reflects an understanding of learning and development that is consistent with current research, theory, and practices in early childhood education
    [R43 (1)(a)(b), (2) Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; C2-4, 7-10 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • ensure that children sleeping are checked for warmth, breathing, and general well-being at least every 5-10 minutes, or more frequently according to individual needs, and a record of children’s sleep times is kept
    [R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS9 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • maintain a record of all injuries of children that occur at the service that includes the child‘s name, the date, time, and description of the incident, actions taken and by whom, and evidence of parental knowledge of the incident
    [R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS25 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • ensure equipment, premises and facilities are checked on every day of operation for hazards to children. Accident/incident records are analysed to identify hazards and appropriate action is taken. Hazards to the safety of children are eliminated, isolated or minimised
    [R46 (1)(c) Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS1,12 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • ensure adults providing education and care are familiar with relevant, including earthquake, emergency drills and carry these out with children on an at least three-monthly basis
    [R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS8 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • ensure suitable human resource management practices are implemented that includes processes for human resource management, including a system of regular appraisal and provision for professional development
    [R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA7 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • ensure an annual plan is in place to guide the kōhanga operation that identifies who, what, and, when in relation to key tasks undertaken each year
    [R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA8 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • ensure an ongoing process of self-review is in place to help the kōhanga maintain and improve the quality of its education and care and includes a process of reviewing and evaluating the kōhanga reo operation showing timelines for planned review of different areas of operation with recorded outcomes from the review process.
    [R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA4,6 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendations

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • whānau receive immediate external intervention to support whānau to address all the concerns outlined in this report

  • whānau submit an action plan to ERO that outlines steps and timelines for how they will improve and grow the kōhanga reo for children.

5 When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rapa will be within one year.
