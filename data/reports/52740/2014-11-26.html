1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā tētahi o ngā Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia ai i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai e te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i mua atu, me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira, tae atu ki te hōtaka akoranga me te whakatairanga i te whakamahinga o ngā tamariki i te reo Māori
  • te whakamahere, te aromātai hoki, tae atu ki te whakahaeretanga o ngā kōhanga reo, te whai wāhi a te whānau, te whakahaeretanga me te whakatinanatanga hoki i Te Whāriki
  • ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture, tae atu ki ngā akoako hōneatanga inā he ahi, he rū whenua rānei, ngā mahi arowhai a ngā pirihimana, te tiaki i ngā rawa, te ū ki ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakahaere tamariki, ngā kawenga a te kaituku mahi pai, me ngā pūnaha ki te whakatakoto haere i ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā aituā, ngā māuiui, me te whāngai rongoā
  • ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai whai muri i te arotake o mua.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Nō te tau 1986 Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūtaramoana i whakatūngia ai. He tino motuhake tōna ingoa, ā, e tohu ana i ngā tātai hononga ki a Ngā Rauru ki te tonga o Taranaki. Ka whakanui hoki te whānau o te kōhanga reo i ō rātou hononga ki Te Āti Haunui-a-Pāpārangi me te awa o Whanganui. Ka noho pūmau ngā kaiako me te whānau ki te whakauka, te whakatairanga hoki i te reo Māori me ōna tikanga, me te ao Māori hoki.

I tāutuhia i te pūrongo o mua te maha o ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake. Mai i taua wā, kua whakapau kaha te whānau, ngā kuia, me ngā kaimahi ki te whakatairanga ake i te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga me te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

E tino tautokona ana, e tino poipoia ana hoki ngā tamariki i roto i tā rātou ako, te atawhai hoki a te whānau i a rātou ki te kōhanga reo. He harikoa, he tau, he pakari hoki rātou i te ao Māori. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i ngā tino hononga e whai pūtake ana, e tautoko ana hoki i a rātou, mai i ngā kaimahi me tō rātou whānau. Ka tino tautokona rātou kia tūhura i te reo Māori me ōna tikanga mā ngā huarahi e whakaongaonga ana, e whakahihiri ana hoki i a rātou.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

‘He pēhea te pai o te tautoko, te poipoi hoki i ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga, me te atawhai hoki i a rātou ki te kōhanga reo?’

E tino tautokona ana, e tino poipoia ana hoki ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga, me te atawhai hoki a te whānau i a rātou ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga, i te atawhai hoki o te whānau me ngā kaimahi. Kua whakapau kaha ngā mema katoa o te whānau ki te whakapakari ake i ngā hononga e whai pūtake ana ki ngā tamariki. He nui te whakatauanga ki te katakata ngātahi me te ngahau ngātahi, ā, he harikoa ngātahi hoki ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki te hōtaka o ia rā. Kua whai pānga pai tēnei ki te kounga o ngā taunekeneke ki ngā tamariki. Ko ngā mahi ki te whakahaere i ngā tamariki e poipoi ana, ā, e hāngai ana hoki ki te tūtohinga. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki, ā, kei te whakawhanake ake rātou i ngā tino hononga ki ngā kaimahi me tō rātou whānau.

He maha ngā whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te reo Māori, mā ngā huarahi e whakaongaongatia ana, e whai pūtake ana hoki. E haere tonu ana ngā mahi a te whānau, ngā kuia, ngā kaumātua, me ngā kaimahi ki te whakapakari i te āheinga me ngā māia ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ko tētahi kuia nō te rohe e hāpai ana, e akiaki ana hoki i ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau ki te whakamahi i te reo ā-iwi o Whanganui. Ka kitea te tino ngākau nuitanga, te maioha hoki o ngā tamariki mō te reo Māori me ōna tikanga.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I whakarewahia e te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2012, ngā āwangawanga i hāngai ai ki te āhua o ngā mahi whakahaere ki te kōhanga reo. I taunaki te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia arotahi te whānau ki ngā putanga ako mā ngā tamariki, te ako me te whai wāhi a te whānau, ngā kawenga a te kaituku mahi, me te whakahaeretanga, te whakatinanatanga hoki o Te Whāriki.

‘He pēhea te whānuitanga o ngā tamariki ki te wheako i te angitu, i te atawhai hoki o te kounga pai, nā runga i te paitanga ake o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo?’

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo mahi angitu, ā, he pai hoki te kounga o te atawhai i a rātou.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ko ngā matea, ngā ngākau nuitanga, me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki e tino hāpai ana i te hōtaka. Kua pai ake te mahi whakamahere. Kua tino whakapai ake ngā mema o te whānau, ngā kaimahi, me te kuia i tō rātou māramatanga, tā rātou whakamahinga hoki i ngā aromatawai a ngā tamariki. Ko ngā tino mōhiohio i whakaemihia ai e whakatakoto ana hoki i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i roto i te ao Māori me te reo Māori. Ka aromātai, ka pūrongo hoki ngā kaimahi i te whakatinanatanga o ngā akoranga o Te Whāriki mā ngā tamariki.

He pai te whakahaeretanga, te whakatinanatanga hoki o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, me ō rātou matea, te atawhai i a rātou, me ngā wawata o te whānau. Ka tino kitea te whai wāhi ake o te whānau ki te tiaki i ngā rawa, ngā mahi hauora, haumaru hoki, te whakamahere mō ngā tamariki, me ngā hui ā-whānau. E tino tautokona ana e te whānau te kuia e tū ana hei kaiako ki te kōhanga reo. Mārakerake ana te kite atu, ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā mahi whakahaere ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture

He whakamārama

He mātātoa te whakatutuki haere a te whānau i te nuinga o ngā wāhanga kāhore i te tutuki ngā herenga ā-ture i te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I hāngai aua take ki te hauora me te haumaru, me te waiora o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

E whai hua ana ngā mahi a te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te manaaki me te tiaki i ngā tamariki. Kei te mārama ngātahi, kei te whakapūmau ngātahi hoki ngā mema hou o te whānau ki te ū ki ngā tikanga herenga ā-ture katoa, hei hāpai i te noho haumaru o ngā tamariki.

Te wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake

Kāhore anō kia oti ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te arowhai a ngā pirihimana i ngā kaimahi. Nā te rerekē haere i tēnei wā o ngā kaimahi, rātou e whiwhi utu ana, rātou hoki e mahi tūao ana, me tahuri te whānau ki te whakatutuki i te arowhai a ngā pirihimana i mua i te tīmatanga o ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki.

3 Ngā Kawenga Takohanga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

Kia tutuki ai te whānau o te kōhanga reo i āna tikanga herenga ā-ture, me tahuri rātou ki te:

  • whakarite, kia tutuki ai te arowhai a ngā pirihimana i ngā kaimahi me ngā tāngata katoa e mahi ana ki te taha o ngā tamariki.

[Ngā wāhanga 315/315AA Ture Mātauranga]

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakaoti ngā whakaritenga kia arowhaingia ngā kaimahi hou e ngā pirihimana
  • whakamahi te arotake whaiaro hei aroturuki i te whānuitanga o te āhua o ngā mahi ki te kōhanga reo.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of the kōhanga reo governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the previous ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • ngā taumata whakahirahira including the learning programme and promoting children’s use of te reo Māori
  • planning and evaluation including kōhanga reo management, whānau involvement and Te Whāriki management and implementation
  • areas of non-compliance including fire and earthquake drills, police vetting, property maintenance, adherence to the child management policies with procedures, good employer responsibilities and the record keeping systems for accidents, illnesses and the administration of medicines
  • and any issues identified since the previous review.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūtaramoana was first established in 1986. The name of the kōhanga reo has special significance and identifies whakapapa links to Ngā Rauru in South Taranaki. The kōhanga reo whānau acknowledges their links to Te Ati Haunui-ā-Pāpārangi and the Whanganui River. Kaiako and whānau are committed to the preservation and promotion of te reo Māori me ona tīkanga and te ao Māori.

The previous review identified the need for improvements in a number of areas. Since then, the whānau, kuia and kaimahi have worked hard to raise the quality of the learning programme, kōhanga reo management.

Children are well supported and nurtured in their learning and care by whānau at kōhanga reo. They are happy, settled and confident in te ao Māori. Children experience close, meaningful and supportive interactions with kaimahi and their whānau. They are successfully supported to explore te reo Māori me ona tīkanga in stimulating and interesting ways.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

To what extent are children supported and nurtured in their learning and care at kōhanga reo?

Children are well supported and nurtured in their learning and care by whānau at kōhanga reo.

Areas of progress

Children are well supported in their learning and care by whānau and kaimahi. All whānau members have worked hard to build more meaningful relationships with children. Children and kaimahi share many moments of laughter, happiness and fun. Whānau are actively involved in the daily programme. This has had a positive impact on the quality of interactions with children. Child management practices are nurturing and consistent with the charter. Children are happy, settled and are developing strong bonds with kaimahi and their whānau.

Children have many opportunities to explore te reo Māori in stimulating and meaningful ways. The whānau, kuia, kaumātua and kaimahi continue to build te reo Māori capability and confidence. A kuia from the rohe supports and encourages children and their whānau to use the whanganui dialect. Children show a strong interest and appreciation for te reo Māori me ona tīkanga.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

The 2012 ERO report raised concerns about the way in which the kōhanga reo was being managed. ERO recommended that whānau focus on learning outcomes for children, whānau learning and involvement, employer responsibilities and Te Whāriki management and implementation.

‘To what extent do children experience success and good quality care as a result of improved whānau management of the kōhanga reo?’

Children experience a range of successes and they receive good quality care.

Areas of progress

Children’s needs, interests and development are well supported in the programme. Planning practices have improved. Whānau members, kaimahi and kuia have significantly improved their understanding and use of children’s assessments. Valuable information that is gathered records children’s learning in te ao Māori and te reo Māori. Kaimahi evaluate and report on the implementation of Te Whāriki learning for children.

Children’s learning needs and care and whānau aspirations are managed and monitored effectively. Increased whānau involvement is strongly evident in property maintenance, health and safety practices, planning for children and whānau hui. The kuia-kaiako is well supported by the whānau. Children are clearly at the centre of kōhanga reo management.

Areas of non-compliance

Background

The kōhanga reo whānau have actively addressed most areas of non compliance raised in the last ERO report. These issues related to health and safety and children’s wellbeing.

Areas of progress

Whānau and kaimahi manage the care and protection of children effectively. There is a shared understanding and commitment by new members to uphold all compliance requirements to help keep children safe.

Area for further improvement

Police vetting processes are incomplete. With recent staffing changes, including paid and voluntary, whānau need to make sure the police vetting process occurs prior to staff working with children.

4 Actions of non-compliance

Overview

In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements they need to:

4.1 ensure police vetting of employees and all persons working with children are carried out.[Reference: Sections 315/315AA Education Act]

5 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendations to:

  • complete police vetting of all new staff
  • use self review to monitor the overall performance of the kōhanga reo.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
