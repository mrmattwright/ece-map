1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

‘Ko te reo kia tika, ko te reo kia rere, ko te reo kia Māori.’

Ko tā te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūraki Ao tirohanga, kia rumakina ā rātou tamariki ki te hōhonutanga o ngā horopaki ako reo. Kua whakawhanakehia ngā taiao ako i āta waihangatia ai, e āta whakahaerehia ana hei poipoi i ngā matea o ngā tamariki, kia whai hua ai anō hoki te āta tautoko i tā rātou ako i te ao Māori.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. I whai wāhi atu te whānau ki te waihanga i te pātai aromātai i ārahi ai i te arotake a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, arā:

He pēhea te whai huatanga o ā tātou tamariki ki te whakawhanake i te tino mōhiotanga ki a rātou anō i roto i te ao Māori?

E whai hua ana ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i te tino mōhiotanga ki a rātou anō i roto i te ao Māori.

He pai ngā wheako akoranga ā ngā tamariki. Ka whai hua te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te āta whakahaere iTe Whāriki. Ka kitea te tino aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki. He māia tā rātou whakamahi i te reo Māori mā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo huarahi, ngā tūmomo horopaki hoki. Ko te whakahirahiratanga o te whanaungatanga e pou here ana i te katoa o ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga e hāpai ana i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Ka āta hāpaitia ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau mā te whai huatanga anō hoki o ngā mahi whakahaere. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā matea o ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau e whakamahia ana hei ārahi i ā rātou whakataunga. Ka arotahi tonu te whānau ki tā rātou tirohanga wā roa mō te kōhanga reo me tōna pūtake.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whai huatanga o ā tātou tamariki ki te whakawhanake i te tino mōhiotanga ki a rātou anō i roto i te ao Māori?

E whai hua ana ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i te tino mōhiotanga ki a rātou anō i roto i te ao Māori.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki ngā horopaki e poipoi ana i a rātou me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga i te mōhiotanga, te tū pakari hoki mā te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Ka whakapūmau ngātahi te whānau ki te whakatairanga i te reo Māori me ōna tikanga mā ngā huarahi e whakaongaongatia ana. Ka kitea te pakari haere o te whakaute me te manawa whakahī o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou mōhiotanga ki tō rātou ao. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te whakawhitiwhiti whakaaro mā te reo Māori.

Ka whakaatu ngā tamariki i tō rātou hononga ki te kōhanga reo, te whānau, me te ao Māori. He rautaki tōtika e whakamahi ana e rātou hei whakawhānui ake i te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou taonga tuku iho, me tō rātou tuakiri. He māia ngā tamariki, he manawa whakahī hoki tō rātou ki tēnā e mōhiotia ana. He tākare rātou ki te whakamōhio i ētahi atu ko wai rātou, nō hea rātou. E mārama pai ana te tūnga motuhake a ngā tamariki hei tangata whenua.

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki kei te arohaina rātou e tō rātou whānau. He aroha, he manaaki, he whakaute hoki ki te katoa o ā rātou taunekeneke, hononga hoki. Ka hāpai te whanaungatanga i ngā tino akoranga papai mā ngā tamariki. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā ngā taunekeneke me te whānau, me te maha hoki o ngā whakatupuranga, tae atu ki ngā kuia. Ka poipoi ngā kaimahi katoa i ngā hononga papai ki waenga i ngā tamariki me tamariki me te whānau. Ka tau wawe ki ngā tamariki te aronga toi whenuatanga ki te kōhanga reo.

He nui te whakaute ka kitea ki ngā tamariki mō ngā taiao māori, tūturu hoki. Tū ai ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura haere i ia te wā. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo haerenga ki waho atu i te kōhanga reo. Ka whakarato te whānau i ngā wheako ki ngā tamariki hei whakatairanga, hei whakawhānui hoki i te mōhiotanga o ia tamaiti. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ka tino whai pānga te taiao ako o ngā tamariki, ā rātou taonga tuku iho me tō rātou tuakiri hoki. E ako ana ngā tamariki, ka toro atu ō rātou hononga ki tua atu i te kōhanga reo.

He tau, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki, ā, ka tūhura i te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo. He pai te waihanga o te taiao o te kōhanga reo, he whakaongaonga hoki, ā, ka āta tohu i te ao Māori. Huri noa i ngā tamariki ko ngā tohu, ngā tauira, ngā taonga, me te reo hei whakapūmau anō i te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō me tō rātou tūnga motuhake ki te ao Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki me tō rātou whānau mā te whai huatanga o ngā mahi whakahaere. He mātātoa te whai wāhi a ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. He auau te pūrongo puta noa i ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo, ā, ka whakamahia ngā pūrongo hei aroturuki i te whai huatanga o ngā mahi whakahaere.

Ko ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki e āta whakaarohia ana ki te whakahaeretanga, te whakatinanatanga hoki o te hōtaka. Kua āta whakamaheretia ngā wheako akoranga aTe Whārikiki ngā tūmomo matea o ngā tamariki. Ka whai wāhi ngā kaimahi katoa ki te katoa o te waihanga me te aronga o te hōtaka akoranga. Ka kitea ki ngā mahere te aronga nui ki te whakatairanga i te tuakiri ā-tangata me ngā taonga tuku iho mā te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Mā tēnei e mātau ana te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori.

He pai te kapo atu a ngā kaimahi i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga mā te ao Māori. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā huarahi e tika ana hei kapo atu i ngā akoranga me ngā mahi angitu a ia tamaiti mā te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. He pai te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki pua noa i te whānuitanga o ngā horopaki ako e whakapūmau ana i ā rātou taonga tuku iho.

Ko ngā wawata o te whānau e tino kitea ana ki tā rātou mahere rautaki, ā, kua whakahāngaitia tēnei ki Te Ara Tūāpae. Ko te whakapakari i te mōhiotanga, te māia, me te āheinga o te whānau puta noa i ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo tētahi kaupapa matua i roto i ngā tau kua pahure ake nei. Kua āta whakatakotohia ngā putanga e whai hua ana. Ko te angitu o te whānau me ā rātou tamariki, kei te pūtake o ngā mahi whakamahere, arotake hoki mō ngā tau kei te heke mai.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake

E mōhio ana te whānau o te kōhanga reo, e tika ana kia arotake i ngā tūmomo huarahi arohaehae i tēnei wā. Me hāngai ngā mahi arohaehae ki te katoa o ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi a ngā kaimahi.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kia tūhura tonu te whānau i te whakamahinga o te arotake whaiaro hei kapo, hei pūrongo hoki i ngā putanga hua a ngā tamariki i roto i te roanga o te wā, i runga anō i te whai pānga o te reo Māori me ōna tikanga, ngā tāonga tuku iho, me te aronga toi whenuatanga.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

‘Ko te reo kia tika, ko te reo kia rere, ko te reo kia Māori.’

The vision of Te Kōhanga Reo o Tūraki Ao whānau is to immerse their children in rich language learning contexts. Well designed and managed learning environments have been developed to cater for children’s needs and successfully support their learning about te ao Māori.

The whānau chose ngā taumata whakahirahira as the focus for this review. Whānau participated in the design of the evaluation question which guided ERO’s review:

How well do our children develop a strong sense of who they are in te ao Māori?

Children are successfully developing a sense of who they are in te ao Māori.

Children have positive learning experiences. The whānau and kaimahi manage Te Whāriki successfully. Children display a strong sense of belonging. They use te reo Māori with confidence, in a variety of ways and contexts. The importance of whanaungatanga underpins all interactions and relationships that support children’s learning. Te reo Maori is the only language that children use.

Children and their whānau are well supported through effective management practices. Whānau are actively involved in the kōhanga reo. They use the interests and needs of children and their whānau to guide their decisions. The whānau continue to focus on their long term vision for the kōhanga reo and its purpose.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

How well do children develop a strong sense of who they are in te ao Māori?

Children are successfully developing a sense of who they are in te ao Māori.

Areas of good performance

Children are immersed in contexts where they are nurtured to develop knowledge and confidence with te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. There is a collective commitment by the whānau to promote te reo Māori me ōna tikanga in stimulating ways. Children show a growing respect for and pride in what they know about their world. Children confidently use te reo Māori to communicate.

Children display their connection to the kōhanga reo, whānau and the Māori world. They use highly effective strategies to extend children’s knowledge about their cultural heritage and identity. Children are confident and proud of what they know. They willingly tell others who they are and where they come from. Children have a strong understanding of their special place as tangata whenua.

Children know they are loved by their whānau. They are surrounded by loving, caring and respectful interactions and relationships. Whanaungatanga supports successful learning for children. Children benefit from interactions with whānau from multiple generations including kuia. All kaimahi foster positive connections between children and whānau. Children quickly develop a strong sense of belonging at kōhanga reo.

Children show great respect for the natural and physical environments. Children have regular opportunities to explore. They participate in a range of outings outside of the kōhanga reo. The whānau provide children with experiences that help enrich and broaden each child’s knowledge. Children know that their learning environment is significant to their cultural heritage and identity. Children are learning that their connections extend beyond the kōhanga reo.

Children are settled, happy and explore their kōhanga reo environment. The kōhanga reo surroundings are well designed, stimulating and strongly reflect te ao Māori. Children are surrounded by symbols, images, tāonga and language that remind them of who they are, and their special place in te ao Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

Children and their whānau are well supported through effective management practices. Whānau members are actively involved in managing the kōhanga reo. Reporting across all areas of the kōhanga reo is regular, and reports are used to monitor the success of management practice.

Children’s needs, interests are well considered in management and implementation of the programme. Te Whāriki learning experiences are well planned and consider the various needs of children. All kaimahi contribute to the overall design and direction of the learning programme. Planning reflects a strong emphasis on promoting cultural identity and ancestral heritage through te reo Māori me ōna tikanga.

Children’s learning and development in te ao Māori is captured well by kaimahi. They use appropriate methods to capture each child’s learning and success with te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Children are developing well across a wide range of learning contexts that reinforces their heritage.

The aspirations of whānau are strongly reflected in their strategic plan which is aligned to Te Ara Tūāpae. Building whānau knowledge, confidence and capacity across all areas of the kōhanga reo has been a priority over the years. Indicators of successful outcomes are clearly stated. Success for whānau and their children is at the heart of future planning and review.

Areas for development

The kōhanga reo whānau acknowledge the need to review current appraisal tools. Appraisal practices need to reflect all kaimahi job descriptions.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Licensed Kōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends and whānau continue to explore the use of self review to capture and report children’s success outcomes overtime in relation to te reo Māori me ōna tikanga, heritage and belonging.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)
