1 Evaluation of Garden Grove Montessori Preschool Ltd

Not well placed

Well placed

Very well placed

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

The Garden Grove Montessori offers Montessori education for children over the age of two. Teachers provide a warm and welcoming environment for children and their parents. Children play and learn in a positive and caring environment. The culture and ethnicity of those attending the centre are valued and celebrated.

Since the last ERO review in 2010, the centre has a new owner/manager and a number of new staff. The manager has made many significant improvements and is continuing to foster effective teaching and leadership. The centre's processes for self review are now established. As a result, children benefit from an enriched bicultural programme, a more consistent focus on literacy and mathematics learning, and improved provisions for health and safety. The quality of teachers‚Äô assessment and the recording of children‚Äôs learning have also improved. Children and staff have a clear and shared understanding of the centre programme, routines and expectations.

The Review Findings

Children at Garden Grove Montessori learn and play in a caring and supportive environment. This helps them to quickly settle on arrival and to feel that they belong. Parents are made to feel welcome and involved in their child‚Äôs learning and in the life of the centre.

Teachers know the children well and are responsive to their learning, interests and individual abilities. The children are well supported to develop skills and independence in their learning. ERO observed children interacting positively and respectfully with each other and with adults.

Children benefit from the Montessori programme and have time to explore their interests. The centre‚Äôs resources for play and learning are attractive, engaging and accessible. Teachers use them well to build on children‚Äôs learning.

Teachers have a strong focus on children‚Äôs learning. They have improved the quality of their planning and assessment. They regularly share their observations and experiences with each other. Children‚Äôs profiles more consistently show what they have learnt and the future focus for their learning. Parents are increasingly being involved in their child‚Äôs learning.

Children benefit from well-planned individual and group experiences that cater for their emerging learning, needs and interests. Their teachers model positive ways of relating with others. They intentionally support children‚Äôs literacy and mathematics learning through their interactions. Children are well-supported with transitions within the centre and when they move on to school.

The centre has a strengthened focus on self improvement. Teachers are becoming active participants in a culture of self reflection and evaluation. Teachers are reflecting on their own practices and sharing their experiences of children‚Äôs learning. The manager has introduced a useful framework to guide self review. She is building capacity within the teaching staff. Teachers have opportunities to lead the emerging focus areas for improving the centre programmes.

Key Next Steps

The centre manager and ERO agree the next steps are to:

  • better link some aspects of the strategic planning and assessment with the centre philosophy
  • review the bicultural programme.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Garden Grove Montessori Preschool Ltd completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Next ERO Review

When is ERO likely to review the service again?

The next ERO review of Garden Grove Montessori Preschool Ltd will be in three years.
