1. Evaluation of the Service

How well placed is the service to promote positive outcomes for children?

AUT Early Childhood Centre is very well placed to promote positive outcomes for children through effective leadership, management, curriculum and teaching practices. Context

AUT Early Childhood Centre is a well-established centre on the Wellesley campus of Auckland University of Technology (AUT). The centre caters for the children of staff and students at the university. The centre’s philosophy reflects its context as part of a university community, valuing ‘excellence, equity and ethics in learning, teaching and scholarship’.

The centre caters for infants and toddlers and children up to school age. Programmes are based on Te Whāriki, the New Zealand early childhood curriculum, and reflect the centre philosophy of respectful relationships and children’s developing independence.

The centre has a history of positive ERO reports and they have responded well to ERO’s recommendations for ongoing improvement. Centre management are about to begin an extensive redevelopment of the outdoor environment. They are also planning the refurbishment and upgrade of indoor spaces to provide more opportunities for children’s complex and extended play. The Review Findings

Children are confident and stand tall in their culture, identity and language. Teachers actively promote and respect children’s independence and self-care skills. Positive relationships between children and adults are evident. Te reo Māori is widely used by teachers and the home languages of children are valued. Teachers’ diverse backgrounds support children’s cultural competence.

Teachers work collaboratively to support and promote the care and learning of all children. They provide high quality programmes based on a culture of respect for children and their whānau. Teaching practices reflect the centre philosophy and current research.

The 2009 ERO report noted the high quality of care and education for children up to two years of age. This quality of provision is now evident for children of all ages. Teachers recognise and respond to children’s emerging interests, involving them in discussions and decisions. Some children play with others or play independently for sustained periods of time. Teachers are considering ways to further extend older children’s interests and learning.

Partnerships with parents and whānau are strong. A review of transition practices has resulted in well-considered processes for children to move into, within and from the centre. Transition to school is well managed, benefiting children, families and building links between the centre and schools.

Centre programmes reflect Māori perspectives and the cultural heritage that all children and their families bring. Teachers take full advantage of the centre’s central location. Excursions to local parks, community amenities and the AUT marae are a regular component of programmes.

Centre self review is very effective. Systematic planned self review informs decision-making at all levels and focuses on improved outcomes for children. Effective leadership ensures that parents, staff, centre leaders and the governance group are consulted and have a voice in decisions.

Leaders model good practices in teaching and learning, building a professional learning culture amongst staff in the centre. They are committed to ongoing progress and are open to feedback. Teachers are motivated and are actively involved in planning for the pending redevelopment of the environment.

2. Legal Requirements

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of AUT Early Childhood Centre completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service’s systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

3. Next Review

When is ERO likely to review the early childhood service again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
