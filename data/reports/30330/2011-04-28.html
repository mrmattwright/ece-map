1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

E whakahaeretia ana te Arotake Tāpiri i runga i te whai whakaaro a tētahi o Ngā Kaiwhakahaere-a-Rohe o Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kia whakahaeretia tēnei.

E arotake ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānui me te whai hua o ngā mahi kua mahia e te whānau o te kōhanga, ki te whakatutuki i ngā take motuhake i pupü ake i roto i ta rātou arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, i ēra atu take rānei kua kitea mai i taua arotake.

I whakaritea tēnei ripoata o te arotake tāpiri, i runga i ngā kawa whakahaere mo ēnei kua whakamanatia e Te Āpiha Arotake Matua.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

I whakaritea tēnei arotake tāpiri i runga i te aro mātai i ngā whakahaere a te rōpu whakahaere o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ao Hou e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i kitea i roto i te pūrongo a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te marama o Hakihea o te tau 2009, i ēra atu take rānei kua pupü ake mai i taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mo ngā whakapuakitanga o tēnei arotake, ko te āta titiro ki:

  • ngā whakamahere me ngā aro mātai tae atu ana ki te arotake i te tūtohinga, te whakawhanake kaupapa here, te whakamahere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro, te whakahaere kaimahi, me te aromātai i te hōtaka
  • Mana Tangata – te reo Māori me ngā akonga tū maia
  • ngā wāhanga kāore i te tutuki ki ngā hanganga ture
  • ēra atu tino take tēra ka pupū ake i te wā e whakahaeretia ana tēnei arotake.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ko te Paneariki te tangata

Ko Mana Ariki te marae

Ko Taumarunui te rohe pōtae

Ko Te Ao Hou te kōhanga reo

Hurihia te ngākau o ngā tamariki ki ngā mātua

Hurihia te ngākau o ngā mātua ki ngā tamariki

Hurihia te ngākau o tētahi ki tētahi

Hurihia te ngākau o te katoa ki te Atua

Kei te hono tata Te Kōhanga Reo O Te Ao Hou ki te marae o Mana Ariki me Te Paneariki i raro i te maru o Te Kotahitanga Incorporated Society.  He wāhanga nui o Mana Ariki te kōhanga reo, ā, kāore e tawhiti i te marae i Otangarue, ki Taumarunui. 

He takatika ētahi o ngā rerekētanga me ngā whakapaitanga ki te kōhanga mai i te arotake o mua atu i tēnei.  Kua pai ake te whakarite o te hōtaka akoranga, ā, kua pai ake tona aronga me tona ahunga.  Kua tau ake ngā tamariki otirā kua tau ake te whānau me tō rātou harikoa ki te mahi tahi me ngā tamariki.  Kua rahi ake te rārangi ingoa o te kōhanga. 

He pai ki ngā tamariki ngā waiata ātaahua me ngā waiata whakanui ka whai i te karakia me ngā mihimihi i ia rā.  Ka tau rātou mō te tīmatanga o tō rātou rangi ki te kōhanga.  Kei te mārama pai ngā tamariki ki ngā mahi o ia rā, ngā kawa me ngā tumanakohanga e pā ana ki ngā whanonga pai, ngā taunekeneke me te ako.  E kitea ana te hiranga o Te Paneariki ki ngā akoranga o ngā tamariki i ngā whakaaturanga me ngā pikitia. 

He tino tauira te kaiako i te reo Māori.  He mārama ia, he mauritau, ā, he māmā te whai i aia.  He mea nui ki te whānau me ngā kaimahi te whai wāhi ki te whakawhanake i o rātou reo i te taha o a rātou tamariki me te kaiako. 

E kitea ana ngā hītori, te ahurea me te kaupapa a wairua o Te Paneariki i ngā horopaki o te hōtaka akoranga.  Kua whakaritea e te kaiako ētahi mahi me ētahi rauemi e hāngai ana ki te kaupapa o Te Paneariki.  Heoi anō, he tino wāhi tēnei hei whanaketanga mā rātou.  Me arotake hōhonutia te hōtaka, tōna whakarite me tōna kawenga. 

He kōpipiri ngā kōwhiringa akoranga mā ngā tamariki.  Kāore i te wātea māmā ngā rauemi, kāore hoki ēnei e whakamahi tōtika ana hei hāpai i ngā matea akoranga me ngā ngākaunuitanga o ngā tamariki.  Me whakaaro hoki ki te whakanui i ngā wāhi ako ki rō whare, ki waho hoki. 

Kua arotakehia, kua whakahoungia, kua whakaaengia hoki te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga.  Ka arotahi ngā whāinga rautaki ki te haumaru, te orange me te ako o ngā tamariki i roto i te horopaki o Te Paneariki.  Ka hāpai tēnei korowai kaupapa i te whānau i a rātou e mahi ana ki te whakatutuki i te tirohanga mō Te Ao Hou. 

Ka mahi tata te kaiako me ētahi mema matua o Te Paneariki ki te whakawhanake i ngā mahi whai hua mō te arotake i ngā mahi o te kōhanga tae atu ana ki te hōtaka akoranga.  Kua tīmata te kaiako ki te whai whakaaro ki te pai o te hōtaka akoranga ki te whakatutuki i ngā matea o ngā tamariki katoa.  Me hāngai ngā whanaketanga arotake whaiaro ki te whakatakoto i te tōtika o ngā mahi o tēnei whānau puta noa i ngā wāhi katoa o te kōhanga.  Tae atu ana tēnei ki te arotake me te aromātai i ia wā i te hōtaka akoranga.  He tūranga matua tō ngā kaimahi me ngā mema katoa o te whānau i roto i ēnei mahi. 

Kua whakatikahia ētahi take kāore i te tutuki ki ngā hanganga ture i tāutuhia ki te pūrongo o mua i tēnei.  Me matua mōhio te whānau inaia nei ki te take o ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga katoa e pā ana ki te hauora me te waiora.  Me āhei hoki te whānau kia tika te owehenga o ngā pakeke ki ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Te whakamāherehere me te aromātai

He whakamārama

Kua puku mahi te whānau me ētahi mema matua o Te Paneariki me Te Kotahitanga Incorporated Society ki te whakatika i ngā wāhi hei whakapai ake i tāutuhia ki te pūrongo o mua.  I te hui tuatahi i tukuna te whānau me ngā kaitautoko i tētahi mahere mahi me ngā kōrero tautoko e whakamārama ana i te ahunga whakamua ki te whakatutuki i ngā mahi matua. 

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Te arotake me te whanake i te tūtohinga me ngā kaupapa here.  Kua arotakehia, kua whakahoungia, kua whakaaengia hoki te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga.  He aratohu mārama ēnei mā te whānau kia tōtika tā rātou whakahaere i ngā mahi me ngā wāhi katoa o te kōhanga.  Ko te mahi mā te whānau inaia nei ko te matua mōhio ki te take o ngā kaupapa here tae atu ana ki ērā ka pā kaha ki te hauora me te waiora o ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi. 

Te whakamahere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro.  E arotahi ana ngā whāinga rautaki o te whānau ki te haumaru, te orange me te ako o ngā tamariki.  Kei te hono te mahere rautaki a te kōhanga ki te rohe o Aotea, te purapura a rohe me Te Ara Tūāpae.  E whai ake nei ko te tirohanga tauāki, “To tātou rohe, tō tātou ao a mea ake nei, tō tātou mahere.”.  Ki raro i tēnei tauāki ka mahi tahi ngā rōpū katoa ki te tautoko i tēna, i tēna o ngā kōhanga i raro i o rātou ake kaupapa, horopaki, ahunga whakmua. 

Ngā kirimana whai mahi.  Kei te whānau ngā whakaahuatanga mahi me ngā kirimana whai mahi kua hainatia e ngā kaimahi katoa. 

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te arotake whaiaro.  Ka noho tonu te arotake whaiaro hei wāhi me whanake tonu.  Me matua mōhio ngā mema katoa o te whānau ki te hiranga me te wariu o te arotake whaiaro ki ngā mahi whakahaere i ngā wāhi katoa o te kōhanga.  Ka hāpai ake tēnei i te tuku pūrongo tika me te whakatau tōtika i ngā kaupapa e hāngai ana ki ngā matea o ngā tamariki me te kōhanga. 

Te whakahaere i ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi.  He mea nui ki te whānau ngā mahi me te ngākaunuitanga o ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga.  Heoi anō he mea nui anō kia whakwhanake te whānau me te whakatinana i tētahi hōtaka aromihi tōtika mō te kaiako me ngā kaiāwhina.  Mā tēnei pea e āhei ai te whānau ki te whakarato whai wāhitanga mā ngā kaimahi ki te tae a tinana atu ki ngā whakangungu me ngā whanaketanga e tika ana. 

Te aromātai i te hōtaka.  He kōpipiri ngā kōrero e pā ana ki te aromataitanga whai hua i te hōtaka.  Ka tuku te kaiako i ia wa i ngā pūrongo ki te hui a whānau me ngā hui o Te Paneariki.  Heoi anō kaore i te rawaka ngā kōrero nei ki te whakamārama ake i te tōtika o te hōtaka akoranga.  Kāore hoki ēnei kōrero i te tāutu ka pēhea te whakatutuki a ngā rerekētanga me ngā whakapaitanga i ngā matea me ngā ngākaunuitanga o ngā tamariki takitahi me ngā reanga tamariki. 

Te whakahaere kaimahi.  Me whakaritea tētahi pūnaha pai ake mō te whakataha me te pupuri i ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi.  Me whakatūria hoki tētahi āpiha punanga hei whakatōpu me te whakawhanake i tētahi tukanga hei hāpai ake i ngā tikanga muna me te punanga o ngā tuhinga a kaimahi. 

ManaTangata

He whakamārama

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua i te take kia kimi ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā mahi

tōtika hei whanake i te reo Māori o ngā tamariki.  Kāore ngā tamariki i te kōrero i te reo Māori hei reo whakawhiti kōrero me o rātou hoa i te kōhanga.  Hei tāpiri atu ki tēnei, i te kōpipiri ngā whai wāhitanga ki te tākaro me te mahi takitahi.  Kāore ngā tamariki i te werohia, i te whakawhānuitia rānei, ā, kāore hoki i te wātea kia rātou ngā momo rauemi. 

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Te reo Māori.  Kei te rūmaki ngā tamariki ki ētahi taiao kōrero i te reo Māori.  He tino tauira te kaiako mō te reo Māori.  Ka whakamahi ia i te whānui o ngā rautaki hei kipakipa, hei uiui, hei kōrero, hei mihi, hei akiaki hoki i ngā tamariki.  Ka hāpai hoki ngā waiata, ngā waiata whakanui, ngā karakia me ngā mihimihi i te whanaketanga o te reo o ngā tamariki mā te whakangahau me ētahi atu āhuatanga.  Ka whakautu ngā tamariki mā te reo Māori i ēnei wā.  Kei te mōhio rātou he mea tino nui te reo Māori i o rātou akoranga me tō rātou taiao.  Ka kōrero rātou i ngā kupu me ngā kianga māma, taunga hoki.  Ka tautoko te kaiako, ngā kaiāwhina me te whānau i ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo i ngā wā katoa. 

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Ngā akonga takitahi.  Kāore tonu i te rawaka ngā whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te tākaro me te mahi takitahi.  Kāore anō hoki i te wātea kia rātou te whānui o ngā raumi, ngā taputapu me ngā rawa hei whakamere me te whakawero i ngā tamariki.  Ahakoa kei te hōtaka he anga, kāore tonu tēnei i te whakatutuki i ngā matea whanaketanga o ngā tamariki takitahi, ranei ngā reanga pakeke puta noa i te rā. 

E whakamārama ana tēnei pūrongo i te take ki te aromātai tūturu, i ia wā me te arotake i te htaka akoranga.  Mā te aroturuki tata i ngā tamariki i roto i te hōtaka akoranga e āwhina ki te whakarite i ngā momo wheako, ngā mahi me ngā rauemi hei whakarei ake i o rātou akoranga.  Me whai whakaaro anō hoki rātou ki te whakanui i ngā wāhi ako o rō whare, o waho hoki.  Ka whakakōroiroi ngā kōpipiritanga o te hōtaka akoranga i te whanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki.

1 Background

Introduction

A Supplementary Review is undertaken at the discretion of an Area Manager in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in a previous education review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

This supplementary review report was prepared in accordance with standard procedures approved by the Chief Review Officer.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ao Hougoverning body and management in relation to areas identified in the December 2009ERO report or issues identified since that review.  The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • planning and evaluation including charter review, policy development, strategic planning and self review; personnel and performance management and programme evaluation
  • Mana Tangata - te reo Māori and independent learners
  • actions of non-compliance
  • any other significant issues identified during the course of the review.

Te Ao Hou Te Kōhanga Reo maintains a close relationship and connection with Mana Ariki Marae and Te Paneariki under the mantle of Te Kotahitanga Society Incorporated.  The kōhanga is an important part of Mana Ariki and is located a short distance from the marae in Ongarue, Taumarunui. 

Positive changes and improvements have occurred since the previous review.  The learning programme is more organised with better direction and focus.  Children are more content while whānau are settled and enjoy working alongside the children.  The kōhanga roll has also increased.

Children enjoy beautiful waiata and songs of praise that follow their daily karakia and mihimihi.  They settle well for the start of their day.  Children understand and are aware of routines, protocols and expectations for positive behaviour, interaction and learning.  A number of displays and murals highlight the significance of Te Paneariki in children’s learning.

The kaiako is a strong role model for te reo Māori.  He is articulate, patient and easy to follow and understand.  Whānau and kaimahi value the opportunity to develop their te reo Māori alongside the children and the kaiako. 

The contexts for the learning programme reflect the historical, cultural and spiritual kaupapa of Te Paneariki.  The kaiako has prepared several units of work and resources in relation to the kaupapa of Te Paneariki.  However, this is a key area for development.  An in-depth review of the current programme content, organisation and delivery is required. 

There are limited learning activity choices for children.  Resources are not readily accessible nor are they used effectively to support children’s learning needs and interests.  Further thought should also be given to maximising the use of the indoor and outdoor learning spaces.

The charter and policies with procedures have undergone review and been updated and ratified.  Strategic priorities focus on children’s safety, wellbeing and learning within the context of Te Paneariki.  This overarching framework supports whānau as they work towards achieving the vision for Te Ao Hou.

The kaiako works closely with key members of Te Paneariki to develop useful ways to review kōhanga practices, including the learning programme.  The kaiako has begun to reflect on how well the learning programme is meeting the needs of all children.  Further self review development must focus on determining the effectiveness of current whānau practices across all areas of kōhanga operations.  This includes the regular review and evaluation of the learning programme.  All kamahi and whānau members have an important role in this process. 

Several non-compliance areas identified in the previous report have been addressed.  The whānau now need to ensure that they clearly understand the intent and purpose of all health and safety policies and procedures.  The whānau must also maintain the correct ratio of adults to children at all times.  

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children.  Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Findings

Planning and Evaluation

Background

Whānau have worked closely with key members of Te Paneariki and Te Kotahitanga Society Incorporated to address the areas for improvement identified in the previous report.  During the initial hui the whānau and kaitautoko presented an action plan and supporting evidence to highlight the progress made towards achieving key tasks and actions. 

Areas of progress

Charter and Policy Review and Development.  The charter and key policies with procedures have undergone review and been updated and ratified.  These provide clear guidelines for whānau to efficiently manage kōhanga operations and practices.  The next step for whānau is to fully understand the intent and purpose of important policies including those that impact on the health and safety of children and staff.

Strategic Planning and Self Review.  Whānau strategic priorities focus on children’s safety, wellbeing and learning.  The kōhanga strategic plan has links to the Aotea District, local purapura and Te Ara Tūāpae.  The vision statement, ‘Our rohe, our future, our plan’, brings each group together to work in a collaborative way to support each kōhanga within their own particular kaupapa, context and future direction. 

Employment contracts.  Whānau have current and signed job descriptions and individual employment agreements for staff.  

Areas for further improvement

Self review.  Self review continues to be an area for further development.  All whānau members need to fully understand the importance and value of self review in the management of all kōhanga operations.  This supports accurate reporting and effective decision-making relating to the needs of children and the kōhanga.

Performance management.  Whānau value the work and commitment of kōhanga staff.  However, it is important that the whānau develops and implements an appropriate appraisal programme for the kaiako and kaiāwhina.  This should enable whānau to provide opportunities for staff to attend targeted training and development.

Programme evaluation.  There is limited evidence of useful programme evaluation.  The kaiako presents regular reports to whānau and Te Paneariki meetings.  However, these do not sufficiently highlight the effectiveness of the learning programme.  Nor do they identify how changes and improvements to the programme can readily meet the individual and age group learning needs and interests of children.

Personnel management.  A more efficient filing and storage system for all personnel documents is required.  Also a privacy officer should be appointed to centralise and develop a process to support maintaining confidentiality and privacy of personnel files. 

Mana Tangata

Background

The previous reported identified the need for kaimahi and whānau to find effective ways of developing children’s te reo Māori.  Children did not use te reo Māori as the first language of communication for interaction in the kōhanga.  Furthermore, there was a lack of opportunity for independent play and work.  Children were not challenged and extended and did not have ready access to resources.

Areas of progress

Te Reo Māori.  Children are immersed in a te reo Māori speaking environment.  The kaiako is a strong model for te reo Māori.  He uses a range of strategies to prompt, question, converse, praise and encourage children.  Waiata, songs of praise, karakia and mihimihi, support children’s te reo Māori development through having fun and in a variety of other ways.  Children are responsive in te reo Māori during these activities.  They know that te reo Māori is an important part of their learning and environment.  They use simple, familiar phrases and words.  The kaiako, kaiāwhina and whānau support children to use te reo Māori as often as possible.

Area for further improvement

Independent Learners.  Children still do not have sufficient opportunities for independent play and work.  Also, they do not have ready access to a variety of resources, equipment and materials which interest and challenge them.  While the programme has some structure and organisation, it still does not cater for the learning and developmental needs of individual children or the different age groups throughout the day. 

This report highlights the need for regular, in-depth evaluation and review of the learning programme.  Close monitoring of children within the learning programme should help determine the type of experiences, activities and resources that are required to enhance their learning.  Further consideration should also be given to maximising the use of the indoor and outdoor learning areas.  The limitations of the learning programme hinder the development of children’s te reo Māori.

4 Any other Significant Issues Identified During the Course of the Review

Whānau Management and Relationship with Te Paneariki

Background

The kōhanga has a close relationship and connection to Te Paneariki and Te Kotahitanga Society Incorporated.  There is a significant focus on the kaupapa and context of Te Paneariki within kōhanga programmes and practices. 

Area for further improvement

Whānau management roles and responsibilities.  The management and operational structure for the kōhanga reo under the mantle of Te Paneariki is not clear.  There are well defined expectations and systems for communicating, consulting and reporting to Te Paneariki and Te Kotahitanga.  This provides a level of accountability to Te Paneariki and kōhanga whānau.  However, within this arrangement, it is critical that whānau maintain a key role and responsibility for the ongoing management, decision-making and operation of the kōhanga.  Whānau, as primary carers, must ensure that the needs and interests of children remain central to all decision-making within the kōhanga and inclusive of Te Paneariki.

6 Recommendations

ERO recommends that the kaupapa kaimahi assist whānau to:

  • provide children with more learning resources to enrich and extend their play and cater for the learning and developmental needs of individual children and the different age groups;
  • maintain a key role and responsibility for the ongoing management, decision-making and operation of the kōhanga
  • develop and implement self review procedures to monitor and report on the kōhanga operations and carry out staff appraisals; and
  • evaluate the effectiveness of the children’s learning programme.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children.  Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

Te Ao Hou Te Kōhanga Reo maintains a close relationship and connection with Mana Ariki Marae and Te Paneariki under the mantle of Te Kotahitanga Society Incorporated.  The kōhanga is an important part of Mana Ariki and is located a short distance from the marae in Ongarue, Taumarunui. 

Positive changes and improvements have occurred since the previous review.  The learning programme is more organised with better direction and focus.  Children are more content while whānau are settled and enjoy working alongside the children.  The kōhanga roll has also increased.

Children enjoy beautiful waiata and songs of praise that follow their daily karakia and mihimihi.  They settle well for the start of their day.  Children understand and are aware of routines, protocols and expectations for positive behaviour, interaction and learning.  A number of displays and murals highlight the significance of Te Paneariki in children’s learning.

The kaiako is a strong role model for te reo Māori.  He is articulate, patient and easy to follow and understand.  Whānau and kaimahi value the opportunity to develop their te reo Māori alongside the children and the kaiako. 

The contexts for the learning programme reflect the historical, cultural and spiritual kaupapa of Te Paneariki.  The kaiako has prepared several units of work and resources in relation to the kaupapa of Te Paneariki.  However, this is a key area for development.  An in-depth review of the current programme content, organisation and delivery is required. 

There are limited learning activity choices for children.  Resources are not readily accessible nor are they used effectively to support children’s learning needs and interests.  Further thought should also be given to maximising the use of the indoor and outdoor learning spaces.

The charter and policies with procedures have undergone review and been updated and ratified.  Strategic priorities focus on children’s safety, wellbeing and learning within the context of Te Paneariki.  This overarching framework supports whānau as they work towards achieving the vision for Te Ao Hou.

The kaiako works closely with key members of Te Paneariki to develop useful ways to review kōhanga practices, including the learning programme.  The kaiako has begun to reflect on how well the learning programme is meeting the needs of all children.  Further self review development must focus on determining the effectiveness of current whānau practices across all areas of kōhanga operations.  This includes the regular review and evaluation of the learning programme.  All kamahi and whānau members have an important role in this process. 

Several non-compliance areas identified in the previous report have been addressed.  The whānau now need to ensure that they clearly understand the intent and purpose of all health and safety policies and procedures.  The whānau must also maintain the correct ratio of adults to children at all times.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews.  The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation.  This area is most likely to support long term improvement.  Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based on Te Whāriki, thewhānau through their  management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues.  The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement.  A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue.  There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
