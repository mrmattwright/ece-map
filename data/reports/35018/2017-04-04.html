1 Te Horopaki

Kei te marae o Hukanui ‘Te Kohanga Reo o Pīpiwharauroa o Te Marae o Hukanui’ e tū ana, a, kei waho atu te hāpori o Hukanui i Kirikiriroa. I whakaritea e te whanau he waka hei hari i ngā tamariki ki te kohanga reo ia rā, ia rā. I pēnei ai, n ate mea, kua rahi haere ake ngā akonga kei runga i te tatauranga ingoa. Kei roto ngā kaupapa o te Kīngitanga i ngā hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ko ngā tikanga me ngā uaratanga o Waikato-Tainui e whai pānga matua ana ki ngā whakaritenga a te whānau me ngā kaimahi. He tino hononga tō te whānau ki te marae me te iwi.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakatinana i te whanaungatanga?

Ka kitea te māramatanga o ngā tamariki he aha te whanaungatanga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka tūhura ngā tamariki i ō rātou hononga ā-whānau mā ngā taumahi ako reo. Ka taki rātou i ngā pepeha me ngā mihimihi. Ka tū pakari ngā tamariki ki te waiata ki te taha o te whānau. He pai ki ia tamaiti te pānui i ngā pukapuka ki te taha o ngā kaimahi, me te rongo i te reo Māori ki te kōhanga reo. Ka aro nui rātou ki te reo Māori e whakamahia ana ki te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakamahi ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki te whakamārama i ō rātou hononga ā-whānau.

E ako ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou hononga ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki te marae, ā, ki te whenua hoki. Ka hāpai ngā waiata me ngā hīmene i te ako a ngā tamariki i ā rātou kōrero tuku iho me ō rātou whakapapa. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā kaupapa o te hapū me te iwi, hei whakawhanake i tō rātou māramatanga ki ngā tikanga e hāngai pū ana ki te Kīngitanga, pērā i te whakatū o te haki, me ngā poukai. Kei te pakari haere te tū o ngā tamariki hei tangata whenua.

E whakawhanake ana te tūmāia o ngā tamariki ki te tūhura me te whakatewhatewha i tō rātou taiao ako. Ka whakatau whiringa ngā tamariki i roto i ngā taumahi ako. E wātea ana ki a rātou te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi hei whakamahi. He pai te taunekeneke a ngā tamariki ki waenga i a rātou anō, ā, ka rapu huarahi hoki rātou ki te hīraurau hopanga mā te mahi ngātahi. E ākina ana ō rātou whakaaro pohewa. E whakawātea ana te wāhi i ngā tamariki ki te tūhura me te whakatewhatewha i tō rātou taiao. He tau ngā tamariki ki te taiao ako.

Ka āta whakaarohia te waiora o ngā tamariki. Ka whakatairangatia ngā mahinga hauora ki te hōtaka akoranga. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te tiaki i tō rātou wāhi. Ka whakapai haere rātou, ā, ka tiaki anō hoki i ngā rauemi i te kōhanga reo. Kua whakaritea ngā mahinga e pā ana ki te kai, ngā tikanga akuaku, me te moe. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā kaumātua ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakapuaki anō hoki rātou i ō rātou mōhiotanga. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te waiata, te kanikani, me te korikori haere ki ngā waiata. Ka kitea te whakaute me te manaaki o ngā tamariki mō rātou anō, ā, mō ētahi atu anō hoki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Kua āta whakamaheretia te ako a ngā tamariki. Kei ngā mahere a ngā kaimahi te māramatanga o te aronga me te whakapuakitanga. E whai pānga matua ana te ako i ngā atua Māori ki te hōtaka. Ka whai whakaaro ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea ako me ngā matea whakawhanaketanga o ngā reanga tamariki katoa, tae atu hoki ki ngā pēpi. Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki matea tauwhāiti i te tautoko o ngā mātanga e tika ana. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā pūkete me ngā kitenga ki te pūrongo i te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki. Ka whai wāhi nui ngā tamariki ki ngā wheako akoranga e whai pūtake ana.

Ko ngā putanga ako o ngā tamariki e whakamōhio ana i ngā mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. Ka ārahi tētahi mahere rautaki i te aronga o te kōhanga reo mō meāke nei. Ka whakaaro huritao te whānau mō te āhua o ā rātou mahi ki te whakatutuki i ā rātou kaupapa matua. Ka whakarato rātou i te ako ngaio me te whakawhanaketanga ngaio ki ngā kaimahi, hei whakapai ake i te ako a ngā tamariki. He mātau te kuia ki ngā mahi i te kōhanga reo, ā, ka hāpai ia i te whakaurutanga mai o ngā whānau hou. Ko ngā matea ako me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki te aronga matua.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore anō te aromātai hōtaka kia whakaritea. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e tuhi ana, e tāutu ana rānei i te huarahi mahi hei whakapai ake i te kounga o te hōtaka.

Kāhore i te rawaka ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki te ako a ngā tamariki ki ngā tuhinga mauhanga. He whāiti noa ngā mahi aromatawai, ā, kāhore e whakamahia ana hei whakatinana i ngā mahere hōtaka.

He ngoikore te aromātai o roto. Me pūrongo i ia te wā i te ahu whakamua ki te whakatutuki i ngā kaupapa matua i te kōhanga reo, hei whakatinana ake i ngā whakataunga a te whānau.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho);
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • mahi te whānau ki te taha o te tari ā-rohe o te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ki te whakatutuki i ngā whakaritenga matua katoa i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo, hei whai ake.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōhanga Reo o Pīpīwharauroa o Te Marae o Hukanui i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pipiwharauroa o Te Marae o Hukanui is a marae based in Gordonton on the outskirts of Hamilton. The whānau provide daily transportation for children. This supports the growing roll. Kingitanga underpins children’s learning programmes. Waikato - Tainui values and practices are an integral part of whānau and kaimahi practices. Whānau have strong connections to the marae and iwi.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children demonstrate whanaungatanga?

Children demonstrate an understanding about whanaungatanga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children’s explore their whānau connections through language learning activities. They recite pepeha and mihimihi. Children confidently perform waiata alongside whānau. Individual children enjoy reading books with kaimahi and hearing te reo Māori in the kōhanga reo. They respond to te reo Māori that is used in the learning programme. Children use te reo Māori to describe their whānau connections.

Children are learning about their connections to each other, the marae and whenua. Waiata and hīmene help children learn their history and whakapapa. Children participate in hapū and iwi events to develop their understanding of tikanga specific to the kingitanga such as raising the flag and poukai. Children are developing confidence in their role as tangata whenua.

Children are developing confidence to explore and investigate their learning environment. Children make choices about learning activities. There are range of resources available for them to use. Children interact positively with each other and find ways to work co-operatively to solve problems. They are encouraged to use their imagination. The physical space allows children to explore and investigate in their environment. Children are settled in the learning environment.

Children’s wellbeing is carefully considered. Healthy habits are promoted in the learning programme. Kaimahi encourage children to be responsible for their physical environment. They clean up after themselves and care for of resources in the kōhanga reo. Routines are in place for eating, hygienic practices and sleeping. Kaumātua are actively involved in children’s learning and they share their knowledge. Children enjoy singing, dancing and moving to music. Children show respect and care for self and others. 

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s learning is well planned. Kaimahi planning provides a clear direction and delivery. Learning about atua Maori is an integral part of the programme. Kaimahi consider the learning and development needs for all age groups including babies. Children with special learning needs receive appropriate specialist support. Kaimahi use profile books and observations to report children’s progress. Children are engaged in meaningful learning experiences.

Children’s learning outcomes informs kōhanga reo management practices. A strategic plan guides the future direction of the kōhanga reo. Whānau reflective about how well they are meeting their priorities. They provide professional learning and development to kaimahi to improve children’s learning. A kuia with kōhanga reo knowledge supports the induction for new whānau. Children’s learning needs and development is a priority.

Key Next Steps

Programme evaluation is not in place. Kaimahi do not record or identify steps to improve the quality of the programme.

Records of children’s learning do not provide sufficient information about children’s learning. Assessment practices are limited and not used to inform programme planning.

Internal evaluation practice is weak. Regular reporting about the progress towards meeting kōhanga reo priorities is needed to inform whānau decision making.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendations

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • that whānau work with the District Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) to address all key next steps identified in this report.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Pīpīwharauroa o Te Marae o Hukanui will be in three years.
