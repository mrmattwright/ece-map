1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Āwhina i roto o Te Papaioea, ā, e tū māro hītararī ana hei whare ako mō ngā tamariki. He hononga kaha ō te whānau ki ngā kura kaupapa Māori e rua o Te Aho Matua. He māmā te whakawhiti a ngā tamariki mai i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo ki te kaupapa o te kura.

He tina ki runga He tamore ki raro

E whakahīhī pai ana te kōhanga reo i te hītori, te whakahirahiratanga ōna, me te tuakiritanga. Nā te tapatahi o te ngākau ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo i mau ai te aro o ngā mātua me ngā kaimahi ki te poipoi i te pitomata i roto i ia tamaiti kia ako kia tupu hoki. E haere ana ngā mihi a te whānau ki te mōhio, ki te matatau, me te whakapau kaha ō te rōpū kaimahi. Ko te whakamārama a ētehi mema mō ngā kaimahi, ‘he mōhio ki te whakaaro mō te tamaiti’. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki i te whakamomori o te whānau me ngā kaimahi.

He pai te pūrongotanga o Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kōhanga reo i roto i ngā tau. I puta i te pūrongo o mohoa nei a Te Tari Arotake, i te tau 2012, ngā whakawhanaketanga pai o te kōhanga reo. Hei tāpiri ki tēnei ko te hōtaka ako a ngā tamariki, ko te rūmakitanga o te taiao Māori, ko ngā pūkenga mahi o ngā kaimahi me te mahi tahi. Waihoki, ko ngā tukanga me ngā tikanga i whai hua ki te whakamomori a te whānau, ki te rangatiratanga, ki te whakahaere i te marau, ki ngā mahere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēwhea rawa te tākirikiritanga, te akiakitanga me te whakakuhutanga o ngā tamariki i roto i te whakaaro arohaehae me ngā whakatinanatanga mā roto mai i te reo Māori?

E whakamanatia ana te tākirikiritanga, te akiakitanga me te whakakuhutanga o ngā tamariki i roto i te whakaaro arohaehae me ngā whakatinanatanga mā roto mai i te reo Māori.

Mana Reo

He māia he mōhio hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōrero Māori. He wahapū, he mārama hoki ngā tuakana i roto i ngā whakaaro me te kōrero mō ngā kaupapa me ngā mahi pai ki a rātou. He māori te whakatinanatanga o te reo e ngā teina ki ngā kaimahi me ō rātou hoa, mā roto mai i ngā mahi-ā-ringa, i ngā whakapuakitanga me te reo o te kanohi. E hiri ana te mahara o ngā tamariki, ā, e ātamai ana ki te whakapakari i ō rātou mōhiotanga. E puta ana i ngā tuākana ngā pātai me te tātari i ngā whakaaro me ngā kōrero a o rātou hoa. Ko te reka o ngā kai o te hōtaka ako, ko te pai o te whakarite kaimahi, me te ātaahua o ngā wāhi ako te kaiwhakakori, te kaiakiaki hoki i ngā tamariki ki te ako mā roto mai i te reo Māori. E rongo ana ngā tamariki i ngā tauira kairangi o te reo Māori, ā, he taiao haumako ō rātou hoki.

He ngangahau, he nui hoki ngā rauemi kei ngā taiao ako puta noa i te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā wāhi motuhake mō ia rōpū-ā-tau nei e hāngai pū ana ki te tamaiti, he pūahoaho, he reka hoki ki te karu. He hīkaka tā ngā tamariki arahi i ngā momo akoranga. E kawe hoki ana rātou i ngā tikanga mahi o ia rā, arā, te tohatoha kai me te whakapai i te tēpu. E ako ana ngā tamariki i ngā tino uara me ngā tikanga mā roto mai i te mahi tahi me ngā hononga ki ō rātou hoa. He māia rātou, ā, e mōhio pai ana rātou ki ō rātou kaimahi, ki te whānau hoki. He rawe ki ngā tamariki te haere ki te kōhanga reo e kaha aro ana ki te ako i te ao Māori me te whakawhanake i a rātou.

He tino māro te whakarite mai o te hōtaka ako engari he ngāwari tonu te whakarerekē ki te hopu i ngā akoranga moroiti nei me ngā whiwhinga ako. E kuhu ana ngā tamariki i roto i ngā momo kaupapa ako tūhonohono nei e āhei ai te ako takitahi, te ako ngātahi me te tākaro. He māia he tau hoki ngā nohinohi, ā, e wātea ana ki te whakatōmene i ō rātou ake akoranga. He wāhi tō te hōtaka Ōkura i roto i te hōtaka ako. He wā motuhake ō ngā tuākana e kuhu atu ai rātou ki te matatini o ngā mahi tuhituhi, nama hoki mō te whakarite i te whakawhitinga ki te kura. Kei te āta mātakitaki ngā kaimahi i te ngāwari o te whakawhitinga o ngā tamariki nohinohi e koke nei ki rōpū kē atu. E eke ana te whakatutukitanga o ngā hiahia o ngā tamariki mā roto mai i te hōtaka ako e whakaratotia ana e ngā kaimahi.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

E eke ana te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, o ngā rautaki me te pai o te whakahaere. Ko ngā tino uara me ngā whāinga mō te ako te tiaki tamariki, te tūāpapa o ngā pūnaha whakahaere, arataki hoki. E ngana ana te ohu ki te mau tonu ki ngā tikanga rite tonu, mārama hoki hōrapa i ngā nekenekehanga. E haere ana te pūrongotanga o ngā kōrero, te arotake whaiaro me te aro ki te whakaniko me te whakamanatanga o te kaupapa, ā, e mau ana i ngā pukapuka matua. Nā te hāngai o ngā pānui me ngā wānanga i nui ai te mōhio o te whānau, ngā kaimahi me te hapori. E tino whai wāhi atu ana ngā whānau nō mua, nō mohoa nei hoki ki te kōhanga reo. E tino manaakitia ana ngā hiahia me ngā koingo o ngā tamariki.

Ko ngā kaimahi he tāngata whai whakaaro, aro hoki ki te whakaniko i a rātou tikanga mahi. E whakatauira ana rātou i te auahatanga i roto i a rātou arotake i ngā kaupapa ako me te pānga ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He pai te whakarite mai, te mahere mai hoki o ngā hōtaka ako hōrapa i ngā tau o ngā tamariki. Kei ia wāhi ako, e kuhu ana ngā tamariki ki tētehi hōtaka e taea ai e rātou te whakatōmene, te matapaki, te taunaha, ā, me te whakamahi i te huhua o ngā rauemi kounga i roto i a rātou akoranga. E mōhio pai ana nga kaimahi ki ngā tamariki, ā, e manaakitia ana o rātou hiahia koingo hoki i roto i ngā āhuatanga huhua. He toa ngā kaimahi me ō rātou mātauranga me ngā whakawhanaketanga e whai hua ana ki ngā tamariki.

E mahi tahi ana, e tautoko tahi ana hoki te ohu, te whānau me ngā kaimahi. Ko ngā whāinga nui me ngā taumata mā ngā tamariki me te whakamomori ki te reo Māori me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo te whakaihuwaka o ngā tikanga whakahaere me ngā nekenekehanga. He pai ngā pūnaha whakahaere whakawhiti kōrero hoki. He mārama ngā mahi me ngā kawenga a te whānau, ā, he pai hoki te whakahaeretanga. He māro te noho mai a ngā kaimahi. E whakatitinatia ana te whānau i ngā pūkenga me ngā mātauranga o ngā kaimahi me te whakamomori ki te whakarato i tētehi taiao manaaki tautoko hoki i te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kua roa te tūtohinga e tatari ana kia tino arotakengia te katoa. He tika te wā, he mea whai mana hoki mō te ohu, ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakahou ngātahi nei i ngā paearu o te tūtohinga, ngā kawa me ngā tikanga e tika ana.

Ahakoa e whakapono ana te whānau ki te tū a te kōhanga reo, e whakamana ana rātou i te whakaaro ki te whakamau ki tētehi whakahaere arohaehae a roto, ngā arotakenga me ngā hokinga whakaaro ki ngā tino o ngā tikanga mahi.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau kia rite tonu tā te ohu, tā te whānau, tā ngā kaimahi whakamomona, whakakaha hoki i te arotake whaiaro, te arohaehae whaiaro me ngā tikanga whai whakaaro hōrapa o ngā rautaki me ngā āhuatanga pūrongo.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Āwhina i roto o Te Papaioea, ā, e tū māro hītararī ana hei whare ako e e tākirikiri ana, e akiaki me te kuhutanga o ngā tamariki i roto i te whakaaro arohaehae me ngā whakatinanatanga mā roto mai i te reo Māori. He hononga kaha ō te whānau ki ngā kura kaupapa Māori e rua o Te Aho Matua. E mahi tahi ana, e tautoko tahi ana te ohu, te whānau me ngā kaimahi. He nui ō rātou hiahia mō ngā tamariki, ā, he kaha tā rātou whakamomori ki te reo Māori me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Āwhina i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Āwhina is in Palmerston North and is a well established place of learning for children. The whānau have a close relationship with two Te Aho Matua kura kaupapa Māori. Children are able to make a smooth transition from the kaupapa of kōhanga reo to that of the kura.

He tina ki runga contentment above He tamore ki raro firmly rooted below

The kōhanga reo is proud of its history, special character and identity. An unwavering belief in the kaupapa of kōhanga reo keeps whānau and kaimahi focused on nurturing each child’s potential to learn and grow. Whānau acknowledge the capability and capacity of a dedicated team of kaimahi. Members describe kaimahi as being ‘really good at thinking about the child’. Children benefit from the commitment of whānau and kaimahi.

The kōhanga reo has had a positive history of ERO reporting. The most recent ERO report of 2012 identified several positive developments in the kōhanga reo. These included the children’s learning programme, Māori immersion environment, the professional capability of kaimahi and interactions. In addition, processes and practices were effective for whānau commitment, leadership, curriculum management and strategic planning and self review.

2 The Review Findings

How effectively are children stimulated, motivated and engaged in critical thinking and expression through te reo Māori?

Children are effectively stimulated, motivated and engaged in critical thinking and expression in te reo Māori.

Mana Reo

Children are confident and very capable speakers of te reo Māori. Tuakana are articulate and clear in their thinking and expression about topics and activities of interest. Teina communicate naturally in non-verbal ways with kaimahi and peers through gesture, utterances and facial expressions. Children are curious and readily ask questions to help develop their understanding. Tuakana often question and check each other’s thinking and kōrero. High interest learning programmes, well organised kaimahi and attractive spaces stimulate and motivate children to learn in and through te reo Māori. Children hear excellent models of te reo Māori and have a rich language learning environment.

Children have vibrant and well resourced learning environments throughout the kōhanga reo. The special areas for each age group are child-centred, bright and attractive. Children eagerly lead different learning activities. They also take responsibility for daily routines such as serving snacks and clearing tables. Children learn important values and practices through their interactions and relationships with their peers. They are confident and know their kaimahi and whānau well. Children love being at kōhanga reo where their holistic learning and development is a priority.

The learning programme is highly organised yet flexible enough to capture incidental teaching and learning opportunities. Children engage in a variety of interactive kaupapa ako activities that allow for independent and shared learning and play. Toddlers are confident and settled as they freely explore their own learning area. The Ōkura programme is a feature of the overall learning programme. Tuakana have a special time where they have more complex literacy and numeracy activities in preparation for transition to kura. Kaimahi closely monitor the smooth transition of younger children as they move to the next age group. The needs of all children are effectively catered for through various learning programmes that kaimahi provide.

Planning and Evaluation

The management of the kōhanga reo is highly effective, strategic and well organised. Administration and management systems are underpinned by strong values and expectations for children’s learning and care. The ohu strive to maintain consistent and transparent practices across all areas of operations. Reporting, self review and a focus on improvement and accountability is ongoing and evident in key documentation. Effective communication and consultation keep the wider whānau, kaimahi and the community well informed. Past and present whānau members are actively involved in the kōhanga reo. Children’s needs and interests are well catered for.

Kaimahi are reflective and focused on improving their practice. They demonstrate creativity in their evaluations of kaupapa ako and the impact on children’s learning. Age group programmes are well planned and well organised. In each learning space, children are engaged in high interest programmes where they can explore, investigate, discover and use a range of quality resources in their learning. Kaimahi know individual children well and cater for their needs and interests in a variety of ways. Kaimahi are effective practitioners whose professional knowledge and development benefits children.

The ohu, whānau and kaimahi work in collaborative and supportive ways. High expectations and standards for children, and a strong commitment to te reo Māori and the kaupapa of kōhanga reo are key drivers in management practices and the operations. Administration and communication systems are efficient. Kaimahi and whānau roles and responsibilities are explicit and well managed. The kōhanga reo has a stable staff. Whānau are reassured of the capability and capacity of kaimahi and of their commitment to providing a welcoming and supportive kōhanga reo environment.

Key Next Steps

The kōhanga reo charter has not been fully reviewed for some time. It is timely and important for ohu, kaimahi and whānau members to collectively work on updating charter guidelines, policies and procedures where required.

While whānau are confident with the performance of the kōhanga reo, they acknowledge the need to sustain an ongoing approach to internal review, evaluation and reflection in key areas of practice.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the whānau agreed that the ohu, whānau and kaimahi continue to enhance and strengthen self review, self evaluation and reflective practices across all strategic and reporting areas.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Āwhina is in Palmerston North and is a well established place of learning where children are stimulated, motivated and engaged in critical thinking and expression in te reo Māori. Whānau and kaimahi have a close relationship with the two local kura kaupapa. The ohu, whānau and kaimahi work in collaborative and supportive ways. They have high expectations for children, and a strong commitment to te reo Māori and the kaupapa of kōhanga reo.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Āwhina will be in three years.
