1 Te Aromātai i Te Puna Reo Ōkahukura

He pēhea te tūnga o Te Puna Reo Ōkahukura ki te whakatairanga i te pai o ngā putanga ako mō ngā tamariki?

Kāhore i te pai te tūnga

Me whakawhanake ake

He pai te tūnga

He tino pai te tūnga

He pēhea rawa te whakaatu a ngā tamariki i tō rātou ngākau titikaha hei ākonga?

Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tō rātou tino ngākau titikaha hei ākonga, i roto i ā rātou mahi me ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu.

Ko ngā kitenga a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga e whai pānga ana ki tēnei whakataunga i whakarāpopotohia ai ki raro iho nei.

He Whakamārama

E tū ana Te Puna Reo Ōkahukura ki Ōrākei, i Tāmaki-makau-rau. Nō te tau 2007 tēnei puna reo i whakatuwheratia ai, ā, e whakarato ana i te atawhai mō te rā katoa, mō ngā tamariki 50, ā, kia 15 hoki o rātou ki raro i te rua o ngā tau. I tēnei wā e whakahaerehia ana te puna reo ki te taumata 80% mō te reo Māori. Ko tētahi kaupapa matua paetawhiti, kia tū te puna reo hei ratonga rumaki reo Māori, mā te aronga nui anō hoki ki te reo me ngā tikanga o Ngāti Whātua.

E ahu mai ana te ingoa o te puna reo i tētahi wāhi i te whanga o Kaipara i pā tata ai ki te whare wānanga whakamutunga o nehe i roto i te iwi o Ngāti Whātua. Ko tā te puna reo tirohanga, kia tū hei whare hiringa e mau nei i te reo hei taonga tuku iho. E tino whai pānga ana tēnei ki te aronga anō hoki ki te tuakiri, te reo, me te ahurea o te iwi. E kawea ana ngā whakahaeretanga o ia rā e te tumuaki. E whakatutukihia ana te kāwana o te puna reo e Whai Māia Ltd, ā, koia nei te peka whakawhanaketanga ā-iwi o Ngāti Whātua ki Ōrākei.

Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

E whai wāhi nui ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao ako e ū ana ki te ngahau me te pārekareka i roto i ngā akoranga. E hāngai ana te hōtaka akoranga ki ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga e panoni haere ana ki ngā tamariki. He nui ngā tūmomo rauemi i te puna reo, ā, he pai hoki tōna whakatakotoranga. He ātaahua, he muramura, he rerehua hoki te wāhi māhorahora o roto. He wāteatea, he hihiri hoki te wāhi o waho, ā, e āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te tūhura haere. Ka kōkiri ngā pākeke i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, ka tautoko hoki rātou i ia te wā, ā, ka whakamihi, ka akiaki hoki i ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou mahi. Ka whakatauira rātou i tētahi tino taumata o te whakaute mō te whaiaro o te tamaiti, tōna mana āhua ake, me tōna aronga toi whenuatanga anō hoki. E wātea ana ngā tamariki ki te whai me te kōkiri anō hoki i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. Kua whakarite ngā kaiako i ngā kawa me ngā tikanga ka whāia e ngā tamariki i roto i ngā mahinga o ia rā. E poipoia ana te whakawhiti atu o ngā tamariki i te ratonga nei ki te kura. Ka poipoia hoki te whakahoahoa me ngā taumahi ā-rōpū, kia rongo ai ngā tamariki i te mauritau me te ngākau titikaha ki te puna reo. He ākonga tūmāia ngā tamariki.

E ako ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whakatairanga ana i ngā wawata o Ngāti Whātua ki Ōrākei. Ka ako rātou i ō rātou pepeha, i ngā kōrero e pā ana ki te hapori, i ngā kōrero tuku iho a te iwi, ā, i te whakahirahiratanga anō hoki o ngā pou whenua o te takiwā nei. Ka tae atu hoki rātou ki ngā kaupapa i te marae o Ōrākei. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā kaiako, te whānau, me ngā tamariki ki te ako ngātahi mā ngā huarahi e whai pūtake ana. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i ngā hononga e ahu mai ana i te whanaungatanga me te whakaaro aroha. Ka whai pūtake ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā pākeke me ngā tamariki, ā, he whakaute hoki. He mātātoa te hāpai me te ārahi o ngā tuākana i ō rātou tēina. Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga ki tō rātou puna reo me tō rātou hapori.

E tino tautokona ana te ako a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. Kōrero Māori ai ngā kaiako i ngā wā katoa, puta noa i te hōtaka o ia rā, ā, ka patapatai, ka whakawhiti kōrero hoki ki ngā tamariki, hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o tō rātou reo. He tino hōhonu te māramatanga o ngā tamariki tuākana ki te reo Māori, ā, e āhei ana rātou ki te whakahoki kōrero, te whakapuaki whakaaro anō hoki mā te whakamahi i ngā rerenga kōrero māmā. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te pānui ngātahi ki te taha o ngā kaiako, ā, he whai wāhitanga anō hoki mō rātou ki te whakaputa kōrero mō ngā pakiwaitara e rangona ana e rātou. E pakari haere ana te ngākau titikaha o ngā tamariki, i tō rātou reo Māori e puāwai mai ana.

He tau, he harikoa hoki ngā pēpi, ā, e tino atawhaitia ana hoki. Ka ngākau nui, ka whai wāhi nui hoki ngā pēpi ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi e whakawhānui ana i ā rātou wheako akoranga, ā, e hāpai ana hoki i tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. He pakari tā rātou tūhura haere ki tō rātou taiao e noho hihiri ana, ā, he hāneanea, he mārie hoki. Ka whai ngā kaiako i ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā pēpi i a rātou e whakamahere ana i te hōtaka akoranga. Kei te tino mārama ngā mātua ki tēnā e ākona ana e ā rātou pēpi, ā, ka aroturuki rātou i tā rātou ahu whakamua. He pai te whakawhanaketanga o ngā pēpi hei ākonga e puāwai mai ana.

Ko ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, me ō rātou mōhiotanga hoki e whakatinana haere ana i ngā mahi ki te whakamahere i te hōtaka. E ārahi ana Te Whāriki i ngā mahere a ngā kaiako. Ko ngā paki ako me ngā taumata ka whāia tonuhia e ngā tamariki, e tūhonohono ana ki Te Whāriki. E whai mōhiohio ana ngā tuhinga aromatawai. He pai te whakatakotoranga o ngā pūkete a ia tamaiti, ā, e mau ana hoki i ngā whakaaturanga o ā rātou akoranga me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te whakaatu i ā rātou pūkete ki ō rātou whānau. E whai hua ana ngā huarahi mahi a ngā kaiako, ā, ka mahi ngātahi hoki rātou hei whakatairanga i te ako a ngā tamariki. Aromātaihia ai e rātou i ia te wā, te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga. E tino hāpaitia ana te ako a ngā tamariki.

Ko te ako, te whakawhanaketanga, me te waiora o ngā tamariki e whakatinana ana i ngā whakataunga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te puna reo. Kua kōkiri, kua whai hua anō hoki te tumuaki me ngā kaimahi i te waihanga o tētahi taiao e whakatairanga ana i te whanaungatanga, te manaakitanga, me te kaitiakitanga. He pai te whakahaere i ngā mahi o ia rā. Arotakengia ai, whakahoungia ai hoki ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga i ia te wā. Ka whakarato te tumuaki i ngā pūrongo o ia marama, hei whakamōhio i ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te kāwana. E whai kounga ana ngā mahi arotake whaiaro, ā, e kitea ana ngā hua pai mō ngā tamariki.

Ko te ahunga whakamua o te puna reo, e ahu mai ana i te tirohanga me ngā kaupapa matua o te paetawhiti o Ngāti Whātua ki Ōrākei, hei painga mō ō rātou hapū me tō rātou iwi. Ko ētahi o ā rātou kaupapa matua, ko te whakarauoratanga o te reo i Ngāti Whātua ki Ōrākei, me te rōnakitanga mā te aro nui ki ngā uri o te iwi. Ka whakamanahia ngā whānau, ā, e piki ake ana tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki te hōtaka akoranga me ngā whakahaeretanga i te puna reo. E whai hua ana ngā tamariki, nā tētahi mātauranga e aroturukitia ana i ia te wā hei whakapai ake.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kia whai whakaaro tonu i ngā mahinga me ngā pūnaha auaha hei whakawhānui ake i ngā āhuatanga pakari e kitea ana ki roto i tēnei puna reo.

Te Whakatau a te Whakahaeretanga ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te ratonga ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho);
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā ratonga mātauranga kōhungahunga katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

How well do children show that they are confident learners?

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Te Puna Reo Ōkahukura is in Ōrākei, Auckland. This puna reo was opened in 2007 and provides a full day service for up to 50 children including 15 under two years old. The puna reo is currently operating at 80% in te reo Māori. It is a long term priority of the puna reo to become a total immersion service with an emphasis on the language and tikanga of Ngāti Whātua.

The puna reo takes its name from a place in the Kaipara Harbour that was close to the last ancient whare wānanga of Ngāti Whātua. The vision for the puna reo is to be centre of excellence where their language is their legacy. This aligns strongly to the focus on iwi identity, language and culture. Day to day operations are the responsibility of the tumuaki (centre manager). Governance of the puna reo is undertaken by Whai Māia Ltd, the tribal development arm of Ngāti Whātua ki Ōrākei.

The Review Findings

Children enjoy an environment where learning is fun and enjoyable. The learning programme is based on children’s changing needs and interests. The puna reo is extensively resourced and well designed. The open plan indoor environment is attractive, colourful and inviting. The outdoors is spacious and exciting and allows children to explore freely. Adults initiate conversations, provide regular support and praise and encourage children as they work. They model a high degree of respect for children’s personal space, individuality and sense of belonging. Children are free to follow and initiate their own interests. Teachers have established kawa and tikanga for children to follow in the daily routines. Children are nurtured to transition into the service and out to school. Friendships and group activities are fostered to give children a sense of security and confidence in the puna reo. Children are confident learners.

Children learn in an environment that promotes the aspirations of Ngāti Whātua ki Ōrākei. They learn their pepeha, local stories and iwi histories, and the significance of local landmarks. They also attend events at Ōrākei marae. Teachers, whānau and children have opportunities to learn together in purposeful ways. Children experience relationships that are based on whanaungatanga and aroha. Interactions are meaningful and respectful between adults and children. Tuākana actively support and guide their tēina. Children demonstrate that they belong to their puna reo and the local area.

Children are well supported to learn te reo Māori. Teachers speak te reo Māori consistently throughout the daily programme, ask questions and have conversations with children to support their language development. Tuākana have a high level of understanding of te reo Māori and are able to respond and convey messages using simple sentence structures. Children enjoy shared reading with teachers and have input to the stories they hear. Children are gaining confidence as early speakers of te reo Maori.

Babies are settled, happy and well cared for. Babies enjoy and participate in a variety of activities to extend their learning experiences and to support their development. They confidently explore in a vibrant, comfortable and calming environment. Teachers follow babies’ interests when planning the learning programme. Parents have a clear understanding of what their babies are learning and monitor their learning progress. Babies are developing well as early learners.

Children’s learning interests and knowledge inform programme planning. Te Whāriki guides teacher planning. Learning stories and next steps for children are linked to Te Whāriki. Assessment records are informative. Well-presented individual portfolio books contain evidence of their learning and development. Children enjoy sharing their portfolio books with their whānau. Teachers work in positive and collaborative ways to enhance children’s learning. They regularly evaluate the effectiveness of the learning programme. Children’s learning is supported well.

Children’s learning, development and well-being inform decisions about the operation of the puna reo. The tumuaki and staff have been proactive and effective in creating an environment that promotes whanaungatanga, manaakitanga and kaitiakitanga. The daily operations are managed well. Policies and procedures are reviewed and updated regularly. The tumuaki provides monthly reports to inform governance processes. Internal evaluation is robust and supports positive outcomes for children.

The future direction of the puna reo is based on the long term vision and priorities of Ngāti Whātua ki Ōrākei for their hapū and iwi. Their priorities include language revitalisation particular to Ngāti Whātua ki Ōrakei and sustainability through investment in their own people. Whānau are valued and are increasingly involved in the learning programme and operations of the puna reo. Children benefit from an education that is constantly monitored for improvements.

Key Next Steps

Continue to explore innovative practices and systems that build on current good practices in the puna reo.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the service’s systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's well-being:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)

  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)

  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)

  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Next Evaluation

When is ERO likely to evaluate the immersion centre again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Puna Reo Ōkahukura will be within three years.

3 General Information about Immersion Early Childhood Reviews

ERO’s Evaluation Framework

ERO’s overarching question for an immersion early childhood education review is ‘How well placed is this service to promote positive learning outcomes for children?’ ERO focuses on the following factors as described in the framework Ngā Pou Here:

  • Pou Whakahaere – how the service determines its vision, philosophy and direction to ensure positive outcomes for children.
  • Pou Ārahi – how leadership is enacted to enhance positive outcomes for children.
  • Mātauranga – whose knowledge is valued and how the curriculum is designed to achieve positive outcomes for children.
  • Tikanga whakaako – how approaches to teaching and learning respond to diversity and support positive outcomes for children.

Within these areas ERO considers the effectiveness of arotake – self review and of whanaungatanga – partnerships with parents and whānau.

ERO evaluates how well placed a service is to sustain good practice and make ongoing improvements for the benefit of all children at the service.

A focus for the government is that all children, especially priority learners, have an opportunity to benefit from quality early childhood education. ERO will report on how well each service promotes positive outcomes for all children, with a focus on children who are Māori, Pacific, have diverse needs, and are up to the age of two.

For more information about the framework and Ngā Pou Here refer to ERO’s Approach to Review in Early Childhood Services.

ERO’s Overall Judgement and Next Review

The overall judgement that ERO makes and the timing of the next review will depend on how well placed a service is to promote positive learning outcomes for children. The categories are:

  • Very well placed – The next ERO review in four years

  • Well placed – The next ERO review in three years

  • Requires further development – The next ERO review within two years

  • Not well placed - The next ERO review in consultation with the Ministry of Education

ERO has developed criteria for each category. These are available on ERO’s website.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews are tailored to each service’s context and performance, within the overarching review framework. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for children and useful to the service.
