Te Aromātai

Ko Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai anake te ratonga o te mātauranga me te atawhai rumaki reo Māori ki te hapori whānui o Te Waiharaheke. He tākare, he tūmāia hoki te ako me te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori. Ka ngākau nui rātou ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo hōtaka akoranga e whakaongaongatia ana. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki, ā, he ākonga tūmāia anō hoki rātou ki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana i a rātou.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai ki te taha o Te Whare Wānanga o te Tauihu o Te Waka a Māui. Koia nei anake te ratonga e whakarato ana i te mātauranga rumaki reo Māori mō ngā tamariki kōhungahunga. Ko tā te whānau tirohanga paetawhiti, kia whakaratohia te mātauranga rumaki reo Māori, te atawhai anō hoki mō ngā tamariki i raro i te maru o te kaupapa o ngā kōhanga reo. He pakari ngā hononga ā-hapori o te whānau hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga me te tupu o te kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou tūmāia hei ākonga mātātoa e whai wāhi nui ana ki te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori?

He tākare, he tūmāia hoki te ako me te whai wāhi atu o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo, ā, e whakaongaongatia ana rātou. He maha ā rātou whai wāhitanga ki te tūhura i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga. E āta aroturukitia ana ngā tamariki, hei āta whakarite i te whakatinanatanga o tō rātou māia ki te whakawhanake i tō rātou tū takitahi hei ākonga. E whai hua ana ngā kaimahi ki te āta whakapakari i ngā hononga ki ngā akoranga, kia māia ake anō ai ngā tamariki, ā, hei whakatairanga hoki i te ako mātātoa. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā wā e noho ā-whānau ana rātou, ā, e whakakoia ana i tō rātou tuakiri, i tō rātou tūrangawaewae hoki. He mātātoa tā rātou tūhura i te taiao māoriori, tūturu hoki, tae atu ki te māra kai, te rua onepū, me ngā māra o te hapori. E tupu haere ana te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki te tiaki i te whenua, te hauhake i ngā hua o te whenua, me te noho whakaute ki a Papatūānuku rāua ko Ranginui. Ko ētahi o ngā mahi a ngā tamariki hei hāpai i tēnei whakawhanaketanga, ko te hangarua, ko ngā māra o te hapori, me ngā haerenga, ngā hīkoi hoki i whakaritea ai. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o tō rātou taiao māoriori, tūturu hoki.

Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tō rātou tākare me tō rātou manawa whakahī ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori i ia rā. Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i te whānuitanga o ngā taumahi kaupapa ako auaha, tūhura hoki, ā, e whakaongaonga ana ēnei i ō rātou tairongo. Nā aua taumahi, ka whakatairanga ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori mā ngā huarahi ngahau, me ngā huarahi e whai pūtake ana ki ngā tamariki. E ai ki te whānau, he tūmanako kia hāpai ngā mātua i te ako o ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki te kāinga. He pai ki ngā tamariki te rongo me te whakapuaki i ngā kōrero e pā ana ki ngā atua Māori ki te ao māoriori, tūturu hoki, me te whakahonohono mā ngā karakia me ngā waiata. He maha ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro mō ngā kaupapa mā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi toi. Ka whakaatuhia aua mahi ki ā rātou pūkete, ki runga rānei i ngā pakitara.

E tūmāia ana ngā tamariki ki te taiao ako nei e poipoi ana, e whakaute ana hoki i a rātou. Ka kite i te whakaute, te manaaki, te whakaaro nui, me te atawhai o ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō. Ka ngākau nui ngā tuākana ki te aroha, te manaaki, me te atawhai i ō rātou tēina. He pakari ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā pākeke me ngā tamariki. He maha ngā wā ka rongo i ngā tamariki e kata ana ki te taha o ngā kaimahi. Ka kite i te whakawhirinaki o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou kaimahi. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te ārahi.

E āta manaakitia ana ngā tamariki. Ko te hauora o ō rātou tinana me ō rātou waiora kei te pūtake o ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi. Ka whakapūmau te whānau i ngā tino taumata o te hauora, te haumaru, me te tiaki i ngā rawa me ngā rauhanga. Ko te tū auau o te arotake whaiaro e hāpai ana i te whānau ki te whakapai ake me te whakatupu ake.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. Mai i te arotake o mua, kua whakatutukihia e te whānau te maha o ngā whāinga me ngā kaupapa matua i whakaritea ai. I hāngai ēnei ki te whakapakaritanga ake o ngā pūmanawa o te whānau. Kua riro i tētahi o ngā kaimahi tana Tohu Whakapakari. Kua riro i ētahi atu o ngā kaimahi te whānuitanga o ngā tohu mā Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo me ngā ratonga o waho. E whai hua tonu ana ngā tamariki nā te tau, nā te whai mōhiotanga anō hoki o ngā akoranga ngaio me te whakawhanaketanga ngaio.

Ka whakaatuhia ngā kaupapa rautaki matua i whakamaheretia ai, ā, he tirohanga ā-tau anō hoki e hāpaitia ana, hei ārahi i te whakatinanatanga. Ko te arotake o roto me te whakaaro huritao ka whakatōngia ki ngā whakaritenga, i te whānau e whakamahi ana i te arotake o waho hei whakamōhio i ngā mahi ka whāia tonuhia i roto i te whakawhanaketanga, te tupu, me ngā whakapaitanga.

He mahinga ngātahi te whakamahere, ā, ka ākina te katoa o ngā kaimahi me te whānau te whai wāhi atu ki te waihanga me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā hōtaka. Ka aro nui ngā mahere ki te whakatairanga i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori mā Te Whāriki. He ngāwari te whakahaere a ngā kaimahi i aua mahere kia hāngai ai ngā hōtaka ki te rerekē haere o ngā matea o ngā tamariki. Ka āta whakahaerehia, ka āta aroturukitia ngā mahere hei whakanui i ngā whai wāhitanga ako mō ngā tamariki.

He pai te whakawhanaketanga o te arotake hōtaka, ā, ka whakamahia hei aroturuki, hei whakamōhio hoki i ngā mahere kia tutuki ai ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Ka whakaemi ngā kaimahi i te pai o ngā mōhiohio e tohu ana i te āhua o te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki hei ākonga tūmāia, hei ākonga mātātoa hoki. Kei te tino mōhio ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kei te whānau me ngā kaimahi tētahi tino huarahi ki te ārahi ngātahi i te kōhanga reo. Engari, kāhore ngā tuhinga kaimahi, ngā tauāki kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e whakaatu ana i te āta tohatoha o ngā mahi e pā ana ki te ārahitanga ngātahi. Kua tae ki te wā kia arotake, kia whakahou ake te whānau i ngā tuhinga kaimahi, tae atu ki ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga o te tūtohinga, hei whakaaro huritao ki ngā tūmanako ārahitanga. He mea nui anō hoki ki te whakanui i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo pūkenga, tohu mātauranga hoki e whakapuakihia ana e ngā kaimahi ki ō rātou tūnga me ā rātou kawenga mahi. Me whai wāhi hoki ngā mōhiohio o te arohaehae kaimahi ki taua arotake.

Ko tētahi atu whakaritenga hoki hei whai ake, ko te āta whakarite i te aronga mārama ki te pūrongo i te ahu whakamua a te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā whāinga rautaki me ngā kaupapa rautaki matua i tāutuhia ai. He tino āhuatanga matua tēnei o te arotake whaiaro, te aroturuki, me te pūrongo.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

Ko Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai anake te ratonga o te mātauranga me te atawhai rumaki reo Māori ki te hapori whānui o Te Waiharaheke. He tākare, he tūmāia hoki te ako me te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori. Ka ngākau nui rātou ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo hōtaka akoranga e whakaongaongatia ana. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki, ā, he ākonga tūmāia anō hoki rātou ki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana i a rātou.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai is the only provider of total immersion education and care within the wider community of Blenheim. Children are enthusiastic and confident learners and participants of te reo Māori and tikanga Māori. They enjoy varied and stimulating learning programmes. Children are actively engaged and confident learners in a nurturing environment.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai is located next to Nelson-Marlborough Educational Institute of Technology. It is the only service that provides total immersion education for pre-school. The long term vision for whānau is to provide pipeline immersion education and care for children within the kaupapa of kōhanga reo. Whānau have strong community networks to support the development and growth of the kōhanga reo.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children show confidence as active learners and participants of te reo and tikanga Māori?

Children are enthusiastic and confident learrners and participants of te reo Māori and tikanga Māori.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are happy, stimulated and settled in their kōhanga reo environment. They have many opportunities to explore their interests. Children are closely monitored to make sure their confidence to develop independence as learners is realised. Kaimahi are effective in building relationships for learning that bring children’s confidence out and promote active learning. Children enjoy shared whānau sessions that affrim who they are and where they come from. They actively explore their natural and physcial surroundings that include maara kai, the sand pit and community gardens. Children are developing an early awareness of caring for the whenua, gaining sustenance from the land and showing respect for Papatūānuku and Ranginui. Some of the experiences children receive to support this development include recycling, community gardens, planned haerenga and hīkoi. Children are aware of their wider natural and physical surroundings.

Children show enthusiasm and pride to use te reo Māori in every day life. Children experience a range of creative and exploratory kaupapa ako activities that stimulate their senses. These enable kaimahi to promote te reo Māori in fun, exciting, meangingful ways for children. Whānau report that there is an expectation that parents will support children’s te reo Māori learning at home. Children like to hear about and share what they know about atua Māori in the natural and physcial world, and make links through karakia and waiata. Children have many opportunities to express their ideas of kaupapa using a variety of art materials. These are displayed in their portfolios or on the walls.

Children are confident in a nurturing and respectful learning environment. Children show respect, care, concern and consideration towards each other. Tuakana like to show aroha, manaaki and affection for teina. Relationships between adults and children are strong. Children are often heard laughing with their kaimahi. Children show trust in their kaimahi. Children love leading.

Children are well cared for. Their physical health and wellbeing is at the heart of kaimahi practice. Whānau maintain high standards of health, safety, and montoring of property and facilities. Regular internal review practices support whānau improvement and growth.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau manage the kōhanga reo well. Since the last review, whānau have achieved a number of planned goals and priorties. These include building whānau capability. One kaimahi has graduated with Tohu Whakapakari. Other kaimahi have attained a range of qualifications through TKRNT and external providers. Children continue to benefit from sound and informed professional learning and development.

Planned strategic priorities are displayed with a supporting annual overview to guide implementation. Internal review and professional reflection is embedded practice while whānau use external review to inform next steps for development, growth and improvement. Reporting occurs regularly and focus on the needs and care of children, whānau growth and involvement.

Planning is collaborative, encouraging all kaimahi and whānau to contribute to the design and development of programmes. Emphases in plannning focus on promoting te reo Māori and tikanga Māori based on Te Whāriki. Kaimahi manage these plans in a flexible way so that programmes are responsive to the changing needs of children. Plans are effectively managed and monitored to maximise learning opportunities for children.

Programme review is well developed and used to monitor and inform planning to meet children’s needs and interests. Kaimahi collect good information that show how well children are developing as confident and active learners. Kaimahi know the needs and interests of children well.

Key Next Steps

Whānau and kaimahi have a particular approach for shared leadership in the kōhanga reo. However, personnel files, policy statements and procedures do not reflect an equally shared approach to leadership. It is timely for whānau to review and update personnel documentation, including charter policies and procedures, to reflect leadership expectations. It is also important to acknowledge the diverse skills and qualifications kaimahi bring to their roles and responsibilities. Kaimahi appraisal information should also be included in this review.

A further next step involves ensuring that there is a clear focus on reporting whānau progress towards defined strategies goals and priorities. This is a key part of internal review, monitoring and reporting.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai is the only provider of total immersion education and care within the wider community of Blenheim. Children are enthusiastic and confident learners and participants of te reo Māori and tikanga Māori. They enjoy varied and stimulating learning programmes. Children are actively engaged and confident learners in a nurturing environment.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Puāwai will be in three years.
