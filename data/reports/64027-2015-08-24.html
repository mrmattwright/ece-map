Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Horouta ki Porirua, e pā tata atu ana ki te marae o Horouta ki Pōneke. Ka ako ngā tamariki i te whakawhanaungatanga i a rātou e taunekeneke ki waenga i a rātou anō. He hiahia tō te whānau kia tū pakari ā rātou tamariki i roto i tō rātou reo, tō rātou ahurea me tō rātou tuakiri hoki i raro i te korowai o te whanaungatanga.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Horouta is in Porirua on the Horouta ki Poneke Marae complex. Children learn about whanaungatanga through their daily interactions with one another. Children have a good understanding of their language, culture and identity that is underpinned by whanaungatanga.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Horouta ki Porirua, ki te taha o te marae o Horouta ki Pōneke. He hiahia tō te whānau kia tū pakari ā rātou tamariki i roto i tō rātou reo, tō rātou ahurea me tō rātou tuakiri hoki. Ka tino whakapono rātou ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, ā, ka hāpai tēnei i te whakatutukitanga o aua wawata. E noho wātea ana te kōhanga reo ki ngā whānau katoa o te hapori nei. Ka whakamanahia, ka whakautehia, ā, ka tino manaakitia hoki i te oranga o ngā tamariki.

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te maha o ngā whakawhanaketanga e whai hua ana ki te kōhanga reo. Ko ētahi o aua whakawhanaketanga, ko te tae ā-tinana mai o ngā kaumātua, ko ngā hononga, ko te reo Māori, ko te hōtaka a ngā tamariki, me te taiao ako. Ko te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, te arotake whaiaro, te whakamahere hōtaka, me te aromātai ētahi atu āhuatanga pakari. Ko tētahi āhuatanga i tāutuhia ai hei whakawhanake ake, ko te whakamahine i ngā mahi e pā ana ki te aromatawai.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te whakaatu a ngā tamariki i te whanaungatanga?

He māia ngā tamariki ki te whakaatu i te whanaungatanga ki waenganui i a rātou me ngā pakeke hoki.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E puāwai ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e arohaina ana rātou, e poipoia ana rātou, ā, e akiaki ana i te whanaungatanga. E tautāwhitia ana rātou e ngā kaimahi e atawhai ana, e manaaki ana hoki i a rātou. He mauritau, he aroha hoki tō ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi. Ka whakanui ngā kaimahi i ngā whakamātau me ngā whakatutukitanga a ngā tamariki. He pai te mahi ngātahi a ngā tamariki, ā, ka ākina hoki rātou kia noho whakaute, kia āwhina hoki tētahi i tētahi. He tau, he pakari hoki ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

He pārekareka ki ngā pēpi ngā taunekeneke e whai pūtake ana, e hāpai ana hoki i a rātou, ā, e whakatairanga ana aua taunekeneke i tō rātou waiora me tō rātou tūhonohono ki ētahi atu. He tere te aro nui a ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea o ngā pēpi. Ka aro wawe, ka aro tōtika atu rātou hei āta whakarite i te whakatutukitanga o ō rātou matea ā-tinana, ā-tangata, ā-whatumanawa hoki. He ngāwari te rere o te hōtaka, ā, ka rongo i te mauritau. He tau te āhua o ngā pēpi i tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga.

Ka tino poipoia te ahurea me te tuakiri o ngā tamariki nā tā rātou hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakamanahia ō rātou hononga ā-whakapapa i ia te wā, mā ngā huarahi papai. E ako ana ngā tamariki ki tō rātou tūnga i roto i tō rātou hapori. Ka whakapuakihia te ako i te ao Māori mā ngā kaupapa ako. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te noho ki tō rātou kōhanga reo.

He pai te whanake haere o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. Ka rumakina rātou ki te reo Māori mā te kapa haka, te karakia, te hīmene, me ngā waiata Māori. Ka noho mārama, ka aro atu hoki te nuinga o ngā tamariki ki ngā tohutohu, ngā pātai, me ngā kīanga. Ka whakamahia te reo Māori puta noa i ngā taunekeneke katoa ki waenga i ngā tamariki, ngā kaimahi, me te whānau.

Ka whakatau, ka manaaki hoki ngā tamariki i te manuhiri ka whai wāhi atu ki tō rātou taiao. He mātau te kuia ka taetae mai ki te kōhanga reo i ia te wā, ā, ka whakatau ia i te rangimārie ki te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki me te whānau. He ngākau nui ki ngā tamariki te ako i te whanaungatanga e whai take ana.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā hōtaka whakahihiri e arotahi ana ki te ao Māori. Ka hono ngā mahere o te wā roa me te wā poto ki Te Whāriki o te Kōhanga Reo me Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi e poipoi ana i ō rātou matea takirōpū, takitahi hoki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā taumahi ako hei hāpai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i tō rātou ahurea me tō rātou tuakiri.

He mārama te aronga a te whānau ki te tautoko, hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki. He rōpū matua o ngā mema o te whānau e āta tautoko pūmau ana. Hui ai te whānau i ia te wā, ā, ka mahi ngātahi. Ka whakawhiti kōrero rātou mō te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo take e whai pānga ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He mārama ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakahaere i te tari me te kōhanga reo. He pakari te aroturuki, te pūrongo hoki a te whānau i ngā pūtea hei whakapai ake ngā putanga mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He whāiti te arotake whaiaro ki te nuinga o ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He tawhito rawa te tūtohinga me ngā mahere rautaki, ā, kāhore e hāngai ana ki ngā wawata me te aronga o te whānau i tēnei wā. Kāhore te pukapuka o ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e āta arotakengia ana i ia te wā. Kāhore anō kia oti ētahi o ngā rehita e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru, ā, kāhore ngā akoako hōneatanga mō ngā rū whenua me ngā ahi e whakatutukihia ana i ia te wā. Kua whakawhanakehia e te whānau tētahi mahere mahi ki te whakaoti i aua mahi, me te āta whakatutuki haere i ia te wā.

He take kaimahi anō hoki hei tirohanga ake. Ahakoa kua oti i ngā kaimahi ā rātou puka arohaehae whaiaro, kāore anō aua puka i te hāngai ki ā rātou tuhinga tūranga mahi, ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki.

Kāhore te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki e āta aroturukitia ana. Kāhore he kiko, kāhore hoki i te tino mārama ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki te aromātai me te aromatawai. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e tuhi kōrerorero ana i ia te wā ki ngā mōhiohio i whakaemihia ai hei tāpiri atu ki ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Hei whakapai ake i ā rātou whakaritenga, me tahuri te whānau ki te whakarite i:

  • te whakatutukitanga o ngā akoako hōneatanga ki te taha o ngā tamariki inā he rū whenua, ā, kia tuhia aua akoako i ia te wā
    [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS8 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • te arowhai o ngā taputapu, ngā whare, me ngā rauhanga i ia te wā mō ngā āhuatanga ka noho mōrearea pea ki ngā tamariki.
    [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS12 ki te HS18 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakaae ngātahi Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo, kia mahi te whānau ki te taha o ngā kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ki te whakaoti i ngā mahi ka whakatutuki i ngā herenga ā-ture i tohua ai ki te mahere mahi ā-tau.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Horouta ki Porirua, e pā tata atu ana ki te marae o Horouta ki Pōneke. Ka ako ngā tamariki i te whakawhanaungatanga i a rātou e taunekeneke ki waenga i a rātou anō. He hiahia tō te whānau kia tū pakari ā rātou tamariki i roto i tō rātou reo, tō rātou ahurea me tō rātou tuakiri hoki i raro i te korowai o te whanaungatanga.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Horouta is in Porirua beside Horouta ki Poneke Marae. Whānau want their children to be confident in their language, culture and identity. They have a strong belief in the kaupapa of kōhanga reo which will help achieve these aspirations. The kōhanga reo is open to all whānau from the local community. Children are valued, respected and well cared for.

The previous ERO report of 2012 identified a number of positive developments in the kōhanga reo. These included kaumātua presence, relationships, te reo Māori, the children’s programme and the learning environment. Whānau management, self review, programme planning and evaluation were other areas of strength. Refining assessment practices was raised as an area for further development.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children demonstrate whanaungatanga?

Children confidently demonstrate whanaungatanga with each other and adults.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children thrive in a loving and nurturing environment where whanaungatanga is encouraged. They are surrounded by kaimahi who are welcoming and caring. Interactions between children and kaimahi are positive and loving. Kaimahi praise children for their efforts and achievements. Children work well together and are encouraged to respect and help each other. Children are comfortable and confident in their kōhanga reo environment.

Babies enjoy meaningful and supportive interactions which enhance their wellbeing and sense of connection to others. Kaimahi attend to the needs of babies. They respond quickly and appropriately to ensure their physical, social and emotional needs are met. The programme is unrushed and there is sense of calm and ease. Babies are settled in a nurturing environment.

Children’s culture and identity is well nurtured through their learning programme. Their whakapapa links are regularly affirmed in positive ways. Children learn about their place in their local community. Learning about te ao Māori is introduced through kaupapa ako. Children enjoy being at their kōhanga reo.

Children are developing well as speakers of te reo Māori. They are immersed in te reo Māori through kapa haka, karakia, hīmene and waiata Māori. Most children understand and respond to instructions, questions and phrases. Te reo Māori is used in all interactions between children, kaimahi and whānau.

Children are welcoming and manaaki visitors at their kōhanga reo. The kuia who is regularly at the kōhanga reo provides a positive role model for children and whānau. Children appreciate learning about whanaungatanga in meaningful ways.

Planning and Evaluation

Children experience programmes that are interesting and focus on te ao Māori. Long and short term plans link to Te Whāriki o te Kōhanga Reo and Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Children receive a range of learning activities that caters for their collective and individual needs. Kaimahi use a range of learning activities to support children to understand their language, culture and identity.

Whānau have a clear focus on supporting the interests of their children. There is a committed core group of whānau members who support the kōhanga reo kaupapa. Whānau meet on a regular basis to discuss a range of matters that affect the operation of the kōhanga reo. There are clear processes for administration and management tasks. Whānau have a sound financial monitoring and reporting system to make informed decisions to improve outcomes for children.

Key Next Steps

Self review across most areas of kōhanga reo operations is limited. The charter and strategic plans are outdated and do not reflect the current aspirations and direction of the whānau. The policy with procedures manual is not reviewed regularly. Some health and safety registers are incomplete and earthquake and fire drills are not conducted on a regular basis. Whānau have developed an action plan to complete these tasks and ensure that they are carried out on a regular basis.

There are some personnel matters that require attention. Although kaimahi have completed self appraisal forms, these do not always link to their job descriptions or roles and responsibilities.

Children’s progress is not monitored well. Evaluation and assessment information lacks detail and clarity. Kaimahi do not consistently annotate the information they gather for children's profile.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

In order to improve practice the whānau must:

  • ensure that earthquake drills are carried out with children and are recorded on a regular basis
    [R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS8 Licensing criteria for kōhanga reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]
  • ensure that equipment, premises, and facilities are regularly checked for hazards to children.
    [R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS12 to HS18 Licensing criteria for kōhanga reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau agreed that the whānau work with Te Kōhanga Reo district staff to complete actions for compliance as indicated in the annual action plan.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Horouta is in Porirua on the Horouta ki Poneke Marae complex. Children learn about whanaungatanga through their daily interactions with one another. Children have a good understanding of their language, culture and identity that is underpinned by whanaungatanga.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Horouta will be within two years.
