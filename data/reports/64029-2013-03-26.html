1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā tētahi ō ngā Kaiwhakahaere Matua ā-Rohe o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei, kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngaio, e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia e te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i te Whiringa-ā-nuku 2011, me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

  • te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori, tae atu ki te aromatawai me te aromātai hōtaka
  • te whakahaere a te whānau, tae atu ki te arotake whaiaro, te whakamahere rautaki, te whakahaere kaimahi, me ngā kawenga mahi a te whānau
  • te whaioranga
  • me ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai whai muri i te arotake o mua.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngaio, ki Ngaio. Kua whakaritea te wāhi hei wāhi tuku iho. E mōhio whānuitia ana mō te matomato o ngā ngahere māori, tae atu ki ngā tūmomo papa. He tino puna mātauranga tēnei taiao māori mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Koia nei anake te kōhanga reo ki tēnei takiwā. E haere tonu ana te ngākau nui o te whānau ki te whakarato i te atawhai me te mātauranga o te kounga kairangi, ki tētahi taiao akoranga mauritau, whakaongaonga hoki mō ngā tamariki.

E aromātai ana tēnei arotake tāpiri a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te ahunga whakamua a te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i tāutuhia ai ki te pūrongo o mua, i te Whiringa-ā-nuku o 2011. I hāngai aua take ki te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, te aromātai hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te hauora, te haumaru hoki. Kei runga noa atu te ahunga whakamua a te whānau. Ko tō rātou ngākau nui, tā rātou whakapūmau hoki ki te whakapai me te tupu tonu, i whai pānga nui ki tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā wāhanga katoa i tāutuhia ai ki te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2011, hei whakapai ake.

Ka hāpai, ka aro, ka poipoi hoki te whakawhanaungatanga ki waenga i te whānau. Ko te pai o te waihanga me ngā mōhiohio ki tā rātou whārangi ipurangi, e akiaki ana i te whai wāhitanga me ngā whakawhitinga kōrero a te whānau. He māramatanga ngātahi tō ngā mema mō ngā mahi ki te āta whakahaere i te kōhanga reo i runga i ngā tūmanako o tā rātou tūtohinga me ngā mātāpono o Te Korowai. Kua āta whakaritea ngā pūnaha arotake whaiaro me ngā whakaritenga, ā, ka whakamahia hei aroturuki, hei tātari hoki i te āhua mahi o te kōhanga reo. Ka whakamahi te whānau i te arotake whaiaro hei pūtake mō ngā whakamaheretanga ā meāke nei, me te pūrongo i ngā whakawhanaketanga, ngā putanga, me ngā āhuatanga angitu. Ka whakamahia tētahi mahere rautaki i āta whakawhanakehia ai hei pūtake mō te whakahaeretanga me te aroturukitanga anō hoki i ngā whakatutukitanga i te kōhanga reo. Kei roto i te mahere, ko te tirohanga, ngā whāinga, ngā kaupapa matua hoki o tā rātou tūtohinga, me te tino whakatakotoranga o ngā mahi whakahaere.

Kua whakahou ake, kua whakapai ake hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā mahi aromatawai. Ka whakaemihia ngā tino mōhiohio, ā, e wātea ana ki ngā mātua. Ka hāngai nui ngā mōhiohio aromatawai ki ngā akoranga a ā rātou tamariki, me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga, mā te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. E ai ki ngā kaimahi, e tino hāpaitia ana rātou e te whakawhanaketanga ngaio, i roto i ō rātou tūranga ngaio, me ngā huarahi mahi i whakaritea ai.

Ka whakanuia e ngā mema o te whānau te ārahi tonutanga, te tautoko tonutanga ka whiwhi rātou i te tari ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo me te purapura, kia tutuki ai ngā whakapaitanga. Ko tā rātou mahere mahi i tuku ai ki te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, e tino whai kiko ana, ā, i āta ārahi i te whānau ki te whakatutuki haere i ngā whakapaitanga katoa. Ko te arotake o roto, whai muri i te arotake o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i tāutu i ētahi atu take i hāngai ai ki te haere tonutanga o te tupu me te whanaketanga. Ko ngā matea o ngā tamariki, ngā ngākau nuitanga o te whānau, me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, kei te pūtake tonu o te arotake whaiaro a te whānau, me tā rātou aronga rautaki anō hoki.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Te reo me ngā tikanga Māori

He whakamārama

I taunaki te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kia whakapai ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te aromātai hōtaka, kia pai ake anō ai hoki te aroturuki me te pūrongo i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka mā ngā tamariki. I taunaki te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia ngātahi ake, kia riterite ake hoki te aronga ki te aromatawai, hei whakapai ake i te tuhi haere i ngā aromatawai me te pūrongo anō hoki ki ngā mātua, mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Te aromatawai i ngā akoranga.He pai ake te whakamōhio i ngā mātua mō ngā akoranga a ā rātou tamariki, me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga, tā rātou ahunga whakamua anō hoki. He pai ngā mōhiohio ka whakaemihia, ka tuhia, ka pūrongotia hoki ki ngā mātua. E wātea ana ngā pūkete ki ngā mātua me ā rātou tamariki. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, ki te whakaaro huritao mō ngā whakaritenga papai, me te aroturuki hoki i te whai huatanga o tā rātou whakamahinga o ngā rautaki aromatawai hou, ngā huarahi aromatawai hou hoki. Ko te whakaaro huritao a ngā kaimahi, me te tātari i ia te wā o ngā mōhiohio hou, e hāpai ana i a rātou ki te ū ki tētahi marautanga e aro ana ki te tamaiti, ā, e whai pānga ana, e whai hōhonutanga hoki mā rātou. Ko te whakaaro huritao me te whakapuaki whakaaro i ia te wā, e hāpai ana i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā mahi aromatawai, ā, e whai hua ana tēnei ki ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau.

Te aromātai i te hōtaka.Whakatutukihia ai te aromātai hōtaka i ia te wā, ā, ka whakamahia hei aroturuki i ngā hōtaka me te pūrongo ki ngā whānau. Ka rapu ngā kaimahi i ngā kōrero urupare a ngā mātua, mō ngā hōtaka a ā rātou tamariki. Ka arotahi rātou ki te whakapai, te whakapakari hoki i ngā wā katoa, i roto i te aromātai hōtaka, te arotake, me te pūrongo, hei painga mō ngā matea me te atawhai o ngā tamariki.

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau

He whakamārama

I te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2011, i puta te taunaki kia whakawhanake te whānau i ngā pūnaha arotake whaiaro me tētahi mahere rautaki, ā, me kaha ake te tautoko e whakaratohia ana ki ngā kaimahi i roto i ā rātou mahi ngaio. Kāhore i ngā ohu whakahaere ngā aratohu i tāutuhia ai, hei hāpai i ia rōpū ki te whakahaere i ā rātou kaupapa ohu. Engari, e ai ki ngā mema o te whānau, i te wā o taua pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i te rerekē haere ngā tūnga mahi, i te whakapakaritia ngā hanganga whakahaere, ā, i te whakawhanake anō hoki te māramatanga o te whānau ki aua tukanga.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Te arotake whaiaro mō te whakapaitanga ake.He nui te ahu whakamua a te whānau o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakaritea e rātou ngā tukanga arotake whaiaro me ngā whakaritenga e tau ana, e whai mōhiohio ana hoki. Ka whakaatu ngā mema katoa i te ngākau nui tahi, ki te whakapūmau i ngā tūmanako o tā rātou tūtohinga i āta whakahoungia ai. Ka tohu te tūtohinga i te whai pānga nui o te arotake ōkawa i ia te wā, puta noa i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, hei hāpai i te whakapai tonutanga, i te tupu tonutanga.

Te whakahaere rautaki.Kua āta whakaarohia tētahi mahere mahi matawhānui e pā ana ki te whakahaere rautaki, ā, e ārahi ana tēnei i te whakatinanatanga. Kua whakahou ake hoki te whakarite hui, kia pai ake ai te tuhi haere me te aru i ngā kaupapa rautaki matua me ngā mahi i whakatutukihia ai i ia marama.

Te whai huatanga o ngā ohu.He pai ake te whakahaeretanga o ngā kaupapa ohu. Ko ngā tūranga me ngā kawenga mahi ki ia tohu, ngā whakawhitinga kōrero me te pūrongo, ka āta tāutuhia ki tā rātou tūtohinga. Ka whai hua ngā mahi a ngā mema o ia ohu ki te hāpai i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka kōkiri kaupapa ngā ohu, ā, ka whakarato hoki i ngā tino pūrongo ōkawa ki te whānau i ngā hui o ia marama. He pai te noho mōhio o te whānau ki te ahu whakamua o ia ohu ki te whakatutuki i ngā kaupapa rautaki matua. I whakatutukihia e te ohu hauora, haumaru hoki tētahi tātari whaiaro matawhānui e hāngai ana ki ngā wāhanga katoa o ngā whakahaeretanga, tae atu ki ngā kaupapa here me whakaritenga i roto i te hauora me te haumaru. Ka tino whakatairangatia te arotake whaiaro a te kōhanga reo, nā ngā mahi o ia ohu.

Te whakahaere i ngā kaimahi.Kua whakatutukihia e te whānau ngā take katoa i tāutuhia ai ki te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, mō te whakahaere kaimahi. He pai ake te tautoko mō ngā kaimahi, ki tētahi taiao mahi e whakaute ana, e atawhai ana hoki. Ka whakakipakipa i a rātou ki te eke angitu i roto i ngā mahi, ā, nā tēnei he mātātoa tā rātou tautoko ngaio, whakangungu ngaio i ia te wā. I whakatinanahia te katoa o tā rātou huringa arohaehae i te tau 2012, ā, i whakatau ngā whāinga whakawhanaketanga ngaio mō te tīmatanga o te tau 2013. Ka haere tonu ngā kaimahi i ā rātou mahi whakangungu ōkawa, ki te whakawhānui i ō rātou pūkenga, ō rātou mōhiotanga hoki, hei whakatairanga i ngā akoranga mō ngā tamariki.

Whaioranga

He whakamārama

I tāutuhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, e tika ana kia arotake, kia whakapai ake hoki te whānau i ngā taumata o te hauora me te haumaru, ngā whakaritenga me ngā pūnaha, inā koa, ko te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea, me ngā hōneatanga. I taunaki te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia whakahou ake te whānau i tā rātou tūtohinga mō te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Te hauora, te haumaru, me te waiora.Kua whakatinanahia ngā huarahi papai ki te tāutu i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea. Kua whiwhi raihana anō tēnei whānau i te Tāhuhu o te Mātauranga, i raro i te paearu raihana o te tau 2008. Whakatutukihia ai ngā akoako ahi, rū whenua hoki i ia te wā. Ka āta aroturuki te whānau i te whakatinanatanga o te katoa o ngā pūnaha hauora, pūnaha haumaru hoki, mā ngā mauhanga i tuhia ai. Ko te tātari whaiaro matawhānui o te hauora me te haumaru i tāutu i te maha o ngā taumata me ngā tūmanako hauora, haumaru hoki i whakatakotohia ai ki tā rātou ake tūtohinga.

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei:

  • kia ū ki ngā pūnaha whakahaere pai, ngā mahi whakahaere pai hoki, me te aro nui tonu ki ngā kaupapa rautaki matua hei tupu tonu anō i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, ā, hei whakatairanga hoki i ngā putanga ki ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau.

5 Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

Te Kōhanga Reo o NgaioThis supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance ofgoverning body and management in relation to areas identified in the October 2011ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori including assessment and programme evaluation
  • whānau management including self review, strategic planning, personnel management, whānau responsibilities
  • whaioranga
  • any issues identified since the previous review.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Ngaio Te Kōhanga Reo is situated in Ngaio. The local area is classified as a heritage site. It is well known for its dense native bush surrounds, including parks and reserves. This environment provides a rich natural context for children’s learning. They are the only kōhanga reo in the local area. Whānau continue in their commitment to provide high quality care and education in a positive and stimulating learning environment for children.

This ERO supplementary review evaluates the progress whānau have made to address matters raised in the last ERO report, October 2011. These matters related to the whānau management, programme evaluation, assessment and health, and safety. Whānau have made outstanding progress. Their dedication and commitment to improvement and continual growth has been pivotal in addressing all areas for improvement identified in the 2011 ERO report.

Whānau relationships are supportive, responsive and nurturing. Their well-designed and informative website encourages whānau engagement and communication. Members have a shared understanding about how to effectively manage the kōhanga reo in line with their charter expectations and Te Korowai principles. Self-review systems and practices are well established and used to monitor and determine kōhanga reo performance.Whānauuse self review as a basis for future planning and reporting developments, outcomes and successes. A well developed strategic plan is used as the basis for managing and monitoring what is happening at the kōhanga reo. The plan includes their charter vision, goals, priorities and clear management actions.

Kaimahihave revised and improved assessment practices. Valuable information is collected and is available to parents. Assessment information highlights their children’s learning and development through thekaupapaof kōhanga reo. Kaimahi report they are supported well in their professional roles and planned career pathways through professional development.

Whānau members acknowledge the ongoing guidance and support they receive from their regional Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) and purapura to bring about improvements. Their detailed action plan, submitted to ERO, gave clear guidance to whānau to work through all improvements. Internal review carried out after the last ERO review, identified additional matters for further growth and development. Children’s needs, whānau interests, and the kaupapa of kōhanga reo are at the heart of whānau self review and strategic direction.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Findings

Te Reo me ngā tikanga Māori

Background

The last ERO report recommended that kaimahi and whānau improve programme evaluation to better monitor and report on programme effectiveness for children. ERO recommended a more coordinated and consistent approach to assessment to improve the recording of, and reporting to, parents about children’s learning and development at kōhanga reo.

Areas of progress

Assessment for learning.Parents are better informed about their children’s learning, development and progress. Good information is gathered, recorded and reported to parents. Portfolios are easily available to parents and their children. Kaimahi meet regularly to reflect on good practice and monitor the effectiveness of how they use new assessment strategies and tools. Shared kaimahi reflection and regular analysis of assessment information assists them to maintain a responsive curriculum that is relevant and rich for their children. Regular reflection and sharing supports the development of assessment practice which benefits children and their whānau.

Programme evaluation.Programme evaluation is carried out regularly and used to monitor programmes and report to whānau. Kaimahi canvas parent’s feedback about their children’s programmes. They focus on continually improving and strengthening programme evaluation, review and reporting in the interests of children’s needs and care.

Whānau management

Background

In the 2011 ERO report it recommended that whānau develop self review systems, a strategic plan and provide better support for personnel in their professional roles. Theohumanagement groups had no defined guidelines to support each group to manage their portfolios. However, whānau members report that at the time of the previous ERO review, they were experiencing staffing changes, strengthening management structures and developing whānau understanding of these processes

Areas of progress

Self review for self improvement.The kōhanga reo whānau have made significant progress. They have established sound and informative self-review processes and practices. All members demonstrate a shared commitment to upholding the expectations of their well-revised charter. The charter highlights the importance of regular and formal review across all areas of kōhanga reo management to support continual improvement and growth.

Strategic management.A well considered and comprehensive strategic management action plan guides implementation. Hui organisation has also been revised to better record and track strategic priorities and actions addressed each month.

Ohu effectiveness.Ohu portfolios are better managed. Ohu roles and responsibilities, communication and reporting, are clearly defined in their charter. Members of each ohu work effectively to support kōhanga reo management. Ohu are proactive and provide valuable formal reports to the whānau at monthly hui. Whānau are informed about the progress each ohu is making toward achieving key priorities. The health and safety ohu carried out a comprehensive self audit on all areas of operations, including health and safety policies and practices. Kōhanga self review is greatly enhanced through the work of each ohu.

Personnel management. The whānau has addressed all personnel management matters raised in the previous ERO report. Kaimahi are better supported in a respectful and caring work environment. They are motivated to perform at their best and actively seek regular professional support and training because of this. The full appraisal cycle was implemented in 2012 with professional development goals set for the beginning of 2013. Kaimahi continue formal training to extend their skills and knowledge to enhance learning for children.

Whaioranga.

Background

ERO identified the need for whānau to review and improve health and safety standards, practices and systems, particularly hazard management and evacuations. ERO recommended that whānau revise and update their kōhanga charter.

Area of progress

Health, safety and wellbeing.Effective methods for identifying hazards are implemented. The Ministry of Education (MOE) has relicensed this whānau under the 2008 licensing criteria. Regular fire and earthquake drills and reviews are carried out. Whānau closely monitor the implementation of all health and safety systems through written records. The comprehensive health and safety self audit identified a number of health and safety standards and expectations set out in their own charter.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • sustain good management systems and practices, and continue to invest in strategic priorities to further grow the kaupapa of the kōhanga reo and enhance outcomes for children and their whānau.

5 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

To the Parents and Community of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngaio

This supplementary review evaluates the quality of education and care received by the tamariki and the performance of the whānau in relation to specific terms of reference.

The Education Review Office [ERO] evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngaio follows.

Ngaio Te Kōhanga Reo is situated in Ngaio. The local area is classified as a heritage site. It is well known for its dense native bush surrounds, including parks and reserves. This environment provides a rich natural context for children’s learning. They are the only kōhanga reo in the local area. Whānau continue in their commitment to provide high quality care and education in a positive and stimulating learning environment for children.

This ERO supplementary review evaluates the progress whānau have made to address matters raised in the last ERO report, October 2011. These matters related to the whānau management, programme evaluation, assessment and health, and safety. Whānau have made outstanding progress. Their dedication and commitment to improvement and continual growth has been pivotal in addressing all areas for improvement identified in the 2011 ERO report.

Whānau relationships are supportive, responsive and nurturing. Their well-designed and informative website encourages whānau engagement and communication. Members have a shared understanding about how to effectively manage the kōhanga reo in line with their charter expectations and Te Korowai principles. Self-review systems and practices are well established and used to monitor and determine kōhanga reo performance.Whānauuse self review as a basis for future planning and reporting developments, outcomes and successes. A well developed strategic plan is used as the basis for managing and monitoring what is happening at the kōhanga reo. The plan includes their charter vision, goals, priorities and clear management actions.

Kaimahihave revised and improved assessment practices. Valuable information is collected and is available to parents. Assessment information highlights their children’s learning and development through thekaupapaof kōhanga reo. Kaimahi report they are supported well in their professional roles and planned career pathways through professional development.

Whānau members acknowledge the ongoing guidance and support they receive from their regional Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) and purapura to bring about improvements. Their detailed action plan, submitted to ERO, gave clear guidance to whānau to work through all improvements. Internal review carried out after the last ERO review, identified additional matters for further growth and development. Children’s needs, whānau interests, and the kaupapa of kōhanga reo are at the heart of whānau self review and strategic direction.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

When ERO has reviewed a kōhanga reo we ask the whānau of the kōhanga reo to let us know how they intend to manage any difficulties set out in their report (copies of which are available from the kōhanga reo or ERO). We also encourage whānau to inform their community of any follow up action plan. You should talk to the whānau if you have any questions about this evaluation, the full ERO report or their future intentions.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
