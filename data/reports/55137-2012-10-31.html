1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā tētahi ō ngā Kaiwhakahaere Matua ā-Rohe o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei, kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāututia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te Kohi-tātea 2011, me ngā take rānei kua tāututia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

  • Te Mana Whenua - tae atu ki te tiaki me ngā whakaritenga tiaki tamariki te taiao ako me te reo Māori Mana Whenua, tāpiri atu ki ngā whakahaere mo ngā whakamahere o ngā hotaka akoranga, ngā aro matawai, ngā aro mātai me ngā mahi akoranga
  • Te whakamāherehere me te Aromātai - tae atu ki te aronga whakamua me te toitū kaihautu te hōtaka whakamāherehere, te hōtaka aromātai, me ngā whakaritenga aromatawai, te whakahaeretanga o te whānau, te aromātai kaimahi
  • te arotake whaiaro me ngā whakaritenga o te mahere rautaki
  • ētahi atu take i tāutuhia whai muri i te arotake o mua atu.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E hāngai ana tēnei Arotake Tāpiri ki te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i puta mai i te Kohi-tātea 2011. I tāututia e te pūrongo o mua ētahi wāhanga hei whakapai ake, arā, ko te mana whenua, te whakamāherehere me te aromātai. Ko te kōrero kei tēnei pūrongo e hāngai ai ki te tipuranga me ngā whanaketanga kua tutukitia mai i taua wā. 

Kei runga tēnei kōhanga reo i te huarahi whakapakari me te huarahi arotake whaiaro. E rata ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te ariā o te arotake ā-roto hei whakapakari i ā rātou anō. Kei te tauihu o te waka te whānau, ā, ko tā rātou arotahi he whakarerekē me te whakapakari ake i ngā āhuatanga o te kōhanga reo. E āwhina ana ngā mātua o te whānau ki te whakatau me te whakarite i te ahunga whakamua o te kōhanga reo.

E tino tiaki ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ā rātou tamariki. Kua arotakehia ngā kaupapa here mai i te wā o te pūrongo o mua. Kua tae atu ētahi kaimahi me ētahi mātua o te whānau ki ngā wānanga mō ngā kaupapa huhua. He whakahoki kōrero pai a ngā mātua mō te noho tiaki pai a ngā tamariki. Kei te tautoko nuitia, kei te poipoitia kei te tiaki paitia ngā tamariki i te rā katoa.

He rauemi whai hua kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi, ā, kua whai whakaaro mō te whakatakotoranga o te wāhi ako mō ngā kōhungahunga. E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga whakaihiihi ā-rōpū i runga i te whāriki, hei tīmata i te rā, ā, he ihiihi te noho tahi ā ngā tamariki ki te whāriki.

E tautoko ana ngā kaimahi i ā rātou tamariki ki te karakia. He kamakama he harikoa ngā koranga waiata, ā, ka waiata tahi, ka kanikani tahi ngā tamariki me ngā pakeke. Kei ngā kaimahi ngā pūmanawa whakahirahira hei whakataki i ngā mihimihi me ngā pepeha ki ngā tamariki. He whai whakaaro ngā kaimahi te take e noho ai ngā tamariki i te koanga ngākau i roto i te kōhanga reo i te rā katoa. E rite ana ngā kaimahi i ngā wā katoa. He ihiihi, ā, he whai take ngā wheako akoranga ā ngā tamariki.

E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao ako he reo Māori te kawenga reo. E tautoko nuitia ana ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo Māori.

E mōhio ana ngā mātua i ā rātou rārangi mahi me ā rātou haepapa hei whakatīnana i ngā hōtaka ako. Ko ngā tāngata kei te kōhanga reo he tautoko i te rere waiora o te takamahi o ia rā me te tuku i ngā wheako akoranga ki ngā tamariki. E noho maia ana ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao.

He hōhonu he whai hua ngā māhere, ā, ka ārahi ngā māhere i ngā kaimahi ki te aro me te whakatinana i te hōtaka ako. Tērā tētahi pūnaha whai take hei whakatakoto māhere ako. Kua whakaritea paitia ngā mahere waiora hei whakatinana i Te Whariki. Ka arotakengia e rātou ngā hōtaka hei āwhina i ā rātou ki te whakapai ake i ngā wāhanga e tika ana. He hōhonu a rātou arotakenga mō te kounga o te hōtaka ako me ngā wheako akoranga e tukuna atu ana ki ngā tamariki.

E whai māramatanga ana ngā mātua ki te tipuranga ako o a rātou tamariki. Tērā tētahi pūnaha aromatawai whai take hei arahi i te whanaketanga me te tipuranga ako a ngā tamariki.

Ngā Mahi ā Meake Nei

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anotia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana whenua

He whakamārama

I te pūrongo o mua i tāututia ētahi āhuatanga o te Mana Whenua hei whakapai ake. Ko te tiaki me ngā whakaritenga tiaki tamariki, ko te taiao akorangan me te reo Māori ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō.

Ngā Wāhanga Kua Pai Ake

Te tiaki me ngā whakaritenga tiaki tamariki. Kei te aro nui te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki ngā whakaritenga hei tiaki i ngā tamariki. Kua oti i ā rātou te arotake i ngā kaupapa here mai i te pūrongo o mua. Kua tae atu ētahi o ngā kaimahi me ētahi o ngā mātua o te whānau ki ngā wānanga me ngā akoranga whakangungu. He whānui, ā, he huhua ngā kaupapa whakangungu i aua wānanga. E ai ki ngā mātua kei te manaaki paitia a rātou tamariki. E kaha ana ngā mātua ki te āta tautoko i te kōhanga reo i te rā hei āwhina i ngā kaimahi ki te tiaki i ngā tamariki i ngā wāhi ako i te kōhanga reo. E tino tiaki ana ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki i te kōhanga reo. He whai hua te takamahi o ia rā. Nā reira, kei te tautoko paitia, kei te tiakina, ā, kei te manaakitia ngā tamariki.

Te taiao ako. He maha ngā rauemi me ngā wāhi ako whai hua kua wharitea e ngā kaimahi me te whānau. I te tīmatatanga o te rā ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te noho tahi i te koanga ngākau. E noho waiora pai ana te kōhanga reo mō te rā katoa nā te mea e whai hua ana ngā pukenga whakahaere a ngā kaimahi. Kua whakaritea ngā rauemi me ngā maramataka e ngā kaimahi, ā, ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te āta kōwhiri i ngā ngohe. He rikarika, ā, he hihiri te hōtaka ako. Ka taea e te tamaiti te whiriwhiri i tāna e hiahiatia ana. E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga whai hua me ngā akoranga rikarika.

Te reo Māori. E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao reo Māori. Ka kōrerotia te reo Māori e ngā kaimahi me ngā mātua, ā, ka kōrerotia te reo ki ngā tamariki i te kōhanga reo. He maha ngā horopaki hopu i te reo Māori ka whakamahia e ngā kaimahi. Kua tautoko nuitia ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka tae atu te hāpori o te kōhanga reo ki ngā wānanga. Nā ēnei wānanga kua pakari ake o rātou mōhiotanga ki te waiata, me te reo Māori. Ka tae atu ngā tamariki ki te marae kei te taha o te kōhanga reo. He horopaki ako kanohi ki te kanohi tēnei rautaki hei ako i te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. Kei te tautoko nuitia ngā tamariki ki te hopu i te reo Māori.

Te whakamāherehere me te aromātai

He whakamārama

I tāututia e te pūrongo o mua ētahi wāhanga o te whakamāherehere me te aromātai hei whakapai ake. E whai ake nei ngā tino wāhanga hei whakapai ake, arā, ko te ahu whakamua me ngā mahi tātaki, te hōtaka e hāngai ai ki te whakamāherehere, te hōtaka e pā ana ki te aromātai me te aromatawai, ngā whakahaeretanga ā te whānau, te aromātai kaimahi, te arotake whaiaro me ngā whakaritenga o te māhere rautaki.

Ngā Wāhanga Kua Pai Ake

Te whakahaeretanga me te anga ahu whakamua. E mōhio ana ngā mātua me ngā kaimahi i ā rātou haepapa me ā rātou rārangi mahi hei whakatinana i ngā hōtaka ako. Ka āwhina ētahi mātua o te whānau i ngā whakahaeretanga o ia rā, pērā i te whakarite kai, te tuku kai me te mahi hei whakapaipai i te kōhanga. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi i ā rātou mahi hei whakatūtuki mō te rā, ā, ka whai wāhi atu ngā kaiako ki ngā akoranga ā ngā tamariki. Ka tautoko nuitia ngā tangata katoa ki te kōhanga reo. He pai rawa ngā whakahaeretanga o ia rā, ā, he whai hua te āhua o ngā akoranga. E noho rangimarie ngā tamariki, ā, he maia te tū a ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao.

Te hōtaka whakamāherehere. He hōhonu, ā, he whai hua ngā māhere hei arahi i ngā kaimahi, nā tēnei tūāhua whakaritea ka aro nui rātou ki te hōtaka ako. Tērā tētahi pūnaha whai hua. Ko te māhere te take ka kitea te rautaki e whakaatu ai i te whakatinanatanga o Te Whāriki. He kaupapa kōrerorero ēnei rautaki ki ngā hui ā te whānau, ā, ki reira whai wāhi ai ngā mātua ki te whakawhitiwhiti whakaaro me te tautoko i te whanaketanga o ngā hōtaka. Kei ia kaimahi te haepapa ki te whakarite i tētahi māhere ako o ia rā me te māhere ako o ia wiki mō tāna rōpū anō. Ko te arotahi ki ngā matea kaha me ngā tūmanakotanga o ngā tamariki te tūāpapa o te hōtaka ako. Kua tāututia e ngā kaimahi te huarahi whai muri, arā, ko te whakarite hōtaka whanonga takitahi mō ētahi tamariki. E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi hōtaka ako, ā, ko te whakamāherehere te take e pēnei ai.

Te hōtaka aromātai. Ka arotakengia ngā hōtaka ako e ngā kaimahi i ngā wā katoa, hei kimi i nga wahanga hei whakapai ake te take o ngā arotake. He hōhonu, he whānui ā rātou whai whakaaro mō te kounga o ngā hōtaka me ngā wheako whai hua a ngā tamariki. Ko te tuhinga me te kōrero ōpaki ētahi rautaki aromātai. Kua whakaritea ngā ngohe whai take mā ngā tamariki, ā, e hāngai ana ki o rātou hiahia me te reanga ako o tēna me tēna.

Te aromatawai. E mōhio hōhonu ana ngā mātua i te paetaetanga me te whanaketanga o ā rātou tamariki. Tērā tētahi pūnaha e whai hua ana e mau ai ngā mōhiohio o ngā tamariki mō ā rātou paetaetanga me o rātou whanaketanga. Kua mau ngā tirohanga o ngā tamariki i ā rātou e takaro ana, ā, kei te kōnae paetae takitahi he mōhiohio whai take mā ngā mātua me ngā kaimahi. Kei roto hoki i ngā kōpaki paetaetanga he tauira mahi nā ngā tamariki, ā, kua tātaringia ngā mōhiohio. Kua tāututia ngā matea ako whai muri. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi me ngā mātua ki ngā wheako akoranga a ngā tamariki i te kōhanga reo.

Ngā whakahaere a te whānau. Nā te tū kaha me te tū tahi o te whānau e rerekē ana, e pai ake ana te kōhanga reo. Ka āwhina ngā mātua o te whānau ki te whakatinana i ngā whakatau me te whakarite i te ahunga whakamua o te kōhanga reo. Kua tae atu rātou ki ngā wānanga whakangungu ki te whakapakari i a rātou maramatanga ki te kaupapa o Te Korowai me Te Whariki o te kōhanga reo. He pai rawa te tae mai o ngā mātua ki ngā hui a te whānau. He pai rawa hoki te whai wāhitanga o ngā mātua ki ngā mahi huhua o te kōhanga reo ia ra. Ka āwhina ngā mātua o te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo.

Te aromātai kaimahi. Ia tau kua aromātaia ngā kaimahi e ngā mātua o te whānau. Kua whakaritea e te whānau tētathi pūnaha hei aromātai i te mahi o ngā kaimahi. Ko te tukanga o te aromātai kaimahi he arotake whaiaro takitahi, te whai whakaaro, te tirotiro i te kaimahi e mahi ana, me te rapu whakaaro o te tētahi atu i waho o te whānau. Ka whakaputahia he purongo e pa ana ki te aromatai mahi mō te whānau me ia kaimahi. Ahakoa me pakari ake ngā wāhanga e pā ana ki ngā wānanga whakangungu, kei te tuatoko te punaha hou i nga kaimahi ki te tāutu i o rātou matea ngaio. E whai whakaaro ana, ā, e whakahaere ana te whānau i te mahi a tēna kaimahi me tēna kaimahi.

Te arotake whaiaro. Kei runga tēnei kōhanga reo i te huarahi hei whakapakari me te huarahi hei arotake i a ratou anō mō ngā rā whai muri. E rata ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te ariā o te arotake-ā-roto. He maha ngā rautaki whakahaere kua whakaritea hei whakapakari ake i te pūnaha arotake whaiaro, arā, ko te āta tiaki i ngā tamariki, ngā whakaritenga hei mātakitaki tamariki i ngā wā katoa, te taiao ako, te aromatawai, te hōtaka aromātai me te whakamāherehere, te aromātai i ngā kaimahi me ngā whakahaeretanga a te whānau.

Ngā Wāhanga Hei Whakapai Ake Anō

Te whakarite māhere rautaki. Kāore i te whānau tā rātou ake māhere rautaki. Tērā tētahi māhere e hāngai ai ki ngā kōhanga reo o te rohe engari kāore i te hāngai ki ngā whāinga me ngā tūmanakotanga o tēnei whānau. Me waihanga te whānau i tētahi māhere rautaki hei tautoko i te ahunga whakamua mō te kōhanga reo.

Te whakahaere pūtea. Ko te Poari Whakahaere Matua o ngā Kōhanga Reo kei te whakahaere i te pūtea a te whānau. E ai ki te pūrongo a te kaitātari pūtea he maha ngā pūnaha whakahaere pūtea kua tāututia hei whakapai ake. Hei tautoko i te whānau, kua whakangungua tētahi tangata hei tiaki pai i te pūtea a te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga kāhore i tūtuki e ai ki ngā ture

He whakamārama

I tāututia e te pūrongo o mua ko te tiaki i ngā tamariki me ngā whakaritenga hei āta mātakitaki i ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa tētahi wāhanga ā-ture kāore i te tutuki. Ka hāngai tēnei wāhanga o te pūrongo ki ngā mahi kua whakatutukitia hei whakapai ake i ēnei wāhanga.

Ngā Wāhanga Kua Pai Ake

Ngā whakaritenga hei tiaki tamariki me ngā whakaritenga mātakitaki tamariki. Kua whakatutukitia e te whānau ngā haepapa ā-ture e pā ana ki te tiaki i ngā tamariki me ngā whakaritenga mātaki tamariki.

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of Te Kōhanga Reo o Poukawa governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the January 2011 ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • Mana Whenua - including care and supervision, the learning environment and te reo Māori
  • planning and evaluation - including direction and leadership, programme planning, programme evaluation and assessment, whānau management, appraisal, self review and strategic planning
  • areas of non-compliance - including supervision
  • any issues identified since the previous review.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

This supplementary review is based on the Education Review of January 2011. That report identified areas for development in mana whenua and planning and evaluation. This report comments on the progress and development that has occurred since that time.

This kōhanga is on a journey of self improvement and continual reflection. The whānau and staff embrace the concept of self review and use it for improvement. The whānau has been instrumental in bringing about change and improvement in the kōhanga. Whānau members help to make decisions and to set the future direction of the kōhanga.

The whānau and staff make every effort to care for and supervise their children. Policy review has occurred since the previous report. Some members of the staff and whānau have attended wānanga and training for a variety of purposes. Parents report positively about the way that their children are cared for. Children are well supported, cared for and supervised throughout the day.

The kaimahi and whānau provide a well resourced, well set out learning space for young children. Children are involved in stimulating whole group mat sessions to start the day. Kaimahi support children to carry out karakia. Energised waiata sessions are fun for children and adults who sing and dance. The kaimahi also use innovative ways of introducing mihimihi and pepeha to children. Kaimahi practices help to set a positive tone in the kōhanga for the day ahead. The kaimahi are well organised and prepared. Children experience stimulating and relevant learning opportunities.

Children are provided with a te reo Māori learning environment. Children are well supported to acquire te reo Māori.

Whānau members and kaimahi know their various roles and responsibilities for implementing the learning programme. People at the kōhanga reo actively support the smooth running of daily routines and the provision of learning experiences to children. Children are comfortable and confident in their environment.

Programme plans are well developed and help to guide kaimahi with the focus and implementation of the learning programme. A sound planning system is in place. Plans provide for a holistic approach to implement Te Whāriki. The kaimahi evaluate their programmes of learning to consider areas for improvement. They have good reflective practices about the quality of the learning programme and learning experiences provided for children.

Parents are well informed about the progress and development of their children. Children’s progress and development is captured by a sound assessment system.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Findings

Mana Whenua

Background

The previous report identified that there were aspects of Mana Whenua that required improvement. In particular care and supervision, the learning environment and te reo Māori were areas for further development.

Areas of progress

Care and supervision. The whānau and staff make every effort to care for and supervise their children. Policy review has occurred since the previous report. Some members of the staff and whānau have attended wānanga and training for a variety of purposes. Parents report positively about the way that their children are cared for. They are active helpers at kōhanga reo during the day and assist kaimahi to supervise children in the various learning spaces at the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi actively care for and supervise children at the kōhanga reo. Daily routines are well managed. Children are well supported, cared for and supervised throughout the day.

The learning environment. The kaimahi and whānau provide a well resourced, well set out learning space for young children. Children are involved in stimulating whole group mat sessions to start the day. Kaimahi support children to carry out karakia as part of these daily sessions. Children and adults sing and dance at energised waiata sessions that are fun. The kaimahi also use innovative ways of introducing mihimihi and pepeha to children. Their practices help to set a positive tone in the kōhanga for the day ahead. The kaimahi are well organised and prepared as they set up activities for children to choose from. They provide an interest-based learning programme where the child sets their own learning direction. Children are provided with stimulating and relevant learning experiences.

Te reo Māori. Children are provided with a te reo Māori learning environment. Kaimahi and whānau members use te reo Māori with each other and with children at kōhanga reo. Kaimahi use a range of effective language acquisition techniques to help children to use te reo Māori. The kōhanga reo community attend wānanga. These wānanga have helped them to improve knowledge and use of waiata and te reo Māori at the kōhanga reo. Children attend events held at the marae next to the kōhanga reo. This provides a real life context to their learning about te reo and tikanga Māori. Children are well supported to acquire te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

The previous report identified that there were aspects of planning and evaluation that required improvement. In particular direction and leadership, programme planning, programme evaluation and assessment, whānau management, appraisal, self review and strategic planning were areas for further improvement.

Areas of progress

Programme direction and leadership. Whānau members and kaimahi know their various roles and responsibilities for implementing the learning programme. Some whānau members help out regularly with daily tasks such as preparing and serving food and by cleaning. Kaimahi know what to do during the day and how to be involved in children’s learning. People at the kōhanga actively support the smooth running of daily routines and the provision of learning experiences to children. Children are comfortable and confident in their environment.

Programme planning. Programme plans are well developed and help guide kaimahi with the focus and implementation of the learning programme. A sound planning system is in place. Plans provide for a holistic approach to implementing Te Whāriki. These are discussed at whānau hui where parents contribute ideas to support programme development. Individual kaimahi take responsibility for weekly and daily planning and preparation for their respective groups. Supporting and developing children’s interests and strengths is a central focus of the learning programme. Kaimahi have defined their next step as creating individual development plans, relation to behaviour, for some children. Children are provided with a well planned learning programme.

Programme evaluation. The kaimahi regularly evaluate their programmes of learning to consider areas for improvement. They have good reflective practices about the quality of the learning programme and learning experiences provided for children. Kaimahi meetings are used to talk about the quality of the learning programme. Both informal conversations and documentation are used as tools for reflection. When appropriate, kaimahi change aspects of the learning programme based on their collective reflections. Children’s activities cater well for their current interests and learning levels.

Assessment. Parents are well informed about the progress and development of their children. A sound assessment system captures information about children’s progress and development. Observations of children at play and individual profiles contain useful information for both kaimahi and parents. Individual profiles contain analysed work samples. Comments made by kaimahi in these profile books focus on the individual child and talk about his or her accomplishments. Next steps for learning are also identified. Kaimahi and parents know about the learning experiences provided to children at kōhanga reo.

Whānau management. Whānau have been instrumental in bringing about change and improvement in the kōhanga reo. Whānau members help to make decisions and to set the future direction of the kōhanga reo. They have participated in wānanga to improve their understanding of the kaupapa of kōhanga reo through learning about Te Korowai and Te Whāriki. Whānau meetings are well attended. There is good participation and involvement by the whānau in the day to day life of the kōhanga reo. Whānau members continually help to manage the kōhanga reo.

Appraisal. Whānau members appraise kaimahi annually. Whānau have established a system for appraising kaimahi. The process for kamahi appraisal includes self reflection and external comment and observation. Reports are generated about the appraisal for both whānau and the individual kaimahi. While the links to professional development and training can be further strengthened the new system supports kaimahi to identify their areas for development. The whānau acknowledges and manages kaimahi performance.

Self review. This kōhanga reo is on a journey of self improvement and continual reflection. The whānau and staff embrace the concept of self review. Many practices including care and supervision, the environment for learning, assessment, programme evaluation and planning, appraisal and whānau management have improved because of enhanced self review.

Areas for further improvement

Strategic planning. The whānau do not have their own strategic plan. There is a local, generic plan available for the kōhanga to use but this does not include many of the goals of this whānau. Whānau should create their own strategic plan for the long term direction of the kōhanga reo. They should consider their own goals, self review, the four pou of kōhanga reo and Te Ara Tūāpae when developing their plan for the future.

Financial management. TKRNT manages finances for the whānau. The most recent auditor’s report identified many aspects of financial processing systems that required improvement. A new finance person is being trained to manage kōhanga reo finances on behalf of the whānau.

Areas of non-compliance

Background

The previous report identified that care and supervision was an area of compliance that was not being met fully. This section reports about progress in this area.

Areas of progress

Care and supervision. The whānau meets their compliance responsibilities for care and supervision.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • the whānau should continue to work with the local kaupapa kaimahi to improve financial management and to develop a strategic plan.

5 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based on Te Whāriki, the whānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
