Background

Terrace End Playcentre is one of 19 administered by the Central Districts Playcentre Association (the association). This review is one of ten undertaken by ERO in the association‚Äôs playcentres during Term 4, 2014.

The centre is open for three mornings per week and caters for children from birth to six years of age. Responsibility for day-to-day operations is undertaken by centre-elected office holders. A paid facilitator supports parents and whƒÅnau to develop and implement the daily programme. Professional advice and feedback to strengthen members‚Äô practice is provided by a liaison officer employed by the association.

Over the past year a complete change of parent committee members has occurred. The new team are working to establish processes to guide centre operations. At the time of this review, all parents at this centre are undertaking Playcentre training.

Playcentres' philosophy statement, 'whƒÅnau tupu ngƒÅtahi ‚Äì families growing together', encapsulates the value this organisation places on families and whƒÅnau working collectively to support children‚Äôs learning. This playcentre has a positive reporting history with ERO.

The New Zealand Playcentre Federation is currently reviewing the organisational structure of Playcentre across New Zealand. The outcomes of this review may result in changes to operation at centre level.

The Review Findings

Adults engage responsively with children. Members have a flexible approach to planning daily sessions linked to children's emerging interests and Playcentre philosophy. Creativity is encouraged. Literacy, numeracy and mathematics are well integrated into the programme in the context of play and everyday happenings. Infants and toddlers are active participants in all aspects of the programme. They confidently explore the playcentre environment.

Members provide a wide variety of high quality resources and learning materials. These are freely available for children. The outdoor area provides good opportunities for physical play and adventurous activities. Children enjoy the environment with its many opportunities for investigation and independent learning.

Through the use of information and communication technologies adults share information about children‚Äôs developing interests which may be used to plan suitable activities for subsequent sessions. They agree that review and development of their approach to assessment, planning and evaluation should continue to be a priority.

Te ao MƒÅori is reflected through waiata, signage, and some use of te reo MƒÅori. Adults are working to ensure children have experiences that assist them to become confident in Aotearoa New¬†Zealand‚Äôs dual cultural heritage. Acknowledged next steps are to continue to develop strategies and approaches to effectively promote MƒÅori children‚Äôs success as MƒÅori.

Children with diverse learning needs are well supported in an inclusive environment.

Centre members work collaboratively to put systems in place to guide centre practices and operations. Self review is in the very early stages of development. It is responsive and beginning to be understood by parents. They should continue to develop self review practice to enable ongoing improvement.

Useful support is provided by the association. This includes written guidelines and systems for managing finance and legislative obligations. Work is being completed to improve employment practices. Regular visits from the liaison officer assist members in their management and teaching roles. A review of the liaison officer role is being undertaken to support an improved approach.

The appraisal process, particularly for those employed as team leaders and liaison officers, needs further development. The provision of ongoing professional, constructive feedback based on observations of practice, and linked to identified needs and goals, should add rigour.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Terrace End Playcentre completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.
