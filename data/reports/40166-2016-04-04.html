Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Tane nui a Rangi ki Murupara. Ka kitea te whanaungatanga o ngā tamariki i a rātou e whakawhanaunga atu ki ō rātou hoa, ki ētahi atu hoki. Ka tino mōhio ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki. He harikoa, he tākare hoki te ako a ngā tamariki.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Tane nui a Rangi i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo Te Tane nui a Rangi ki Murupara. Koia nei te kōhanga reo tuatahi i whakatūngia ai ki Murupara.

Ka whakarato te kaiako me ngā kaimahi i tētahi taiao whakahihiri mō ngā tamariki. He pārekareka ki ngā kaimahi te mahi ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whai wāhi hoki te whānau ki te whakawhanaketanga o te hōtaka me ngā rauemi.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i te whanaungatanga?

Ka kitea te whanaungatanga ki ngā tamariki i a rātou e whakawhanaunga ana ki ō rātou hoa, ki ētahi atu hoki.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira 

E ako ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e tautoko ana i a rātou. Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi ngā mahinga papai e hāpai ana i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti haere ki ngā tūmomo taumahi. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā taumahi e poipoi ana i ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki, ā, e whakanui ana hoki i te ngana nui ki te ako. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te āta whakawhanaunga hei hoa, ā, hei tuakana, hei teina hoki. He hāneanea, he tau hoki ngā tamariki.

He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura me te hīraurau hopanga. E wātea ana ki a rātou te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi. Kua whakawhanakehia e te whānau tētahi mahere ki te whakapai ake i te wāhi nui o waho. He pai te māramatanga o ngā kaimahi ki te āhua o te ako a ngā tamariki. Ka whakarato ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi e hāpai ana i ā rātou akoranga me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. He pakari ngā tamariki hei ākonga.

E whakawhanake ana te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo Māori o tō rātou iwi. Ka whai ngā tamariki i ngā tohutohu, ā, ka aro atu hoki ki ngā tono. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki e tika ana hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. Ka tautokona, ka ākina hoki te whakamahinga a te whānau i ngā kupu me ngā kīanga māmā e rongo ana ngā tamariki i ia rā. E tupu haere ana te māia o ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori.

Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki ngā hononga e whai pūtake ana. He hononga ā-whakapapa tō rātou ki ngā kaimahi me te whānau. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā kaupapa i te hapori, ā, he pakari tā rātou taunekeneke atu ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo tāngata. Ka whakarato ki ngā tamariki ngā whai wāhitanga ki te haere ki ngā kaupapa nui o te hapū. Ka whakanui ngā kaimahi i te āheinga o ngā tamariki ki te tūhono atu ki ētahi atu. He tau te noho tahi o ngā tamariki ki te taha o ētahi atu.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He whāiti te tupu o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. Ki tō te whānau whakaaro, he mea nui te ako a ā rātou tamariki i te reo Māori. E mōhio ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi, me whakapai ake rātou i te taumata o tō rātou reo Māori. Ka akiaki tētahi i tētahi ki te toro atu ki ngā huarahi ka whakapakari ake i tō rātou mātau ki te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

Ka whai hua ngā mahere, ā, ka ahu mai i ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Ka whakapuaki te whānau i ō rātou whakaaro ki ngā mahi whakamahere. Ka ārahi ngā mahere i te whakatinanatanga o te hōtaka. Ka kitea ngā mahi whakamahere mō ngā tūmomo reanga tamariki. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te hōtaka akoranga i āta whakaritea ai.

He mārama ki ngā kaimahi te pūtake o te aromatawai. Kua whakaritea ngā tukanga aromatawai. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā pūkete hei aroturuki i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahia ngā kitenga me ngā tauira o ngā mahi a ngā tamariki hei whakamōhio i ngā kaimahi mō te ahu whakamua me ngā matea ako o ia tamaiti. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā ki te whakaaro huritao me te whakawhiti kōrero mō te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ka āta whakamōhiotia te whānau mō te ahu whakamua o ā rātou tamariki.

He tau ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau. Kua whakaritea ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. He mārama ngā pūnaha pūtea. Kua whakaritea ngā tukanga me ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o ngā kaimahi, tae atu ki ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi, te kirimana ā ia tangata, me te arohaehae whaiaro. He mārama ki ngā kaimahi ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga i roto i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He whāiti ngā mahi ki te aromātai i te hōtaka. He ōpaki kē te aromātai a ngā kaimahi i ngā hōtaka akoranga. Ahakoa ka whakawhiti kōrero rātou mō te hōtaka, kāhore ngā whakarerekētanga ka pupū ake i ā rātou whakawhitinga kōrero e tuhia ana, ā, kāhore rānei ka whakapuakihia ki te taha o te whānau. Kāhore ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki e whakawhānuihia ana.

Kāhore he kiko ki te mahere ā-tau. He mārama ngā whāinga rautaki ā te whānau, engari kāhore he mahere e tohu ana i te huarahi ka whai atu ki te whakatutuki i aua whāinga. 

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

Hei whakapai ake i āna whakaritenga, me tahuri te whānau ki te:

  • whakarite i te arowhai i ia te wā, i ngā taputapu, te wāhi, me ngā rauhanga mō ngā āhuatanga ka noho mōrearea mō ngā tamariki.

[R 46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS4-8 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008] 

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakapakari i te aromātai hōtaka 
  • whakawhanake, kia whakatinana hoki i tētahi pūnaha arohaehae
  • whakahaere, whakatakoto ā-tuhi hoki i ngā arowhai mō ngā āhuatanga mōrearea
  • whakawhanake i tētahi mahere rautaki e tino whai kiko ana.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Tane nui a Rangi ki Murupara. Ka kitea te whanaungatanga o ngā tamariki i a rātou e whakawhanaunga atu ki ō rātou hoa, ki ētahi atu hoki. Ka tino mōhio ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki. He harikoa, he tākare hoki te ako a ngā tamariki.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Tāne-nui-a-Rangi is located in Murupara.  Children demonstrate whanaungatanga as they build positive relationships with their peers and others. Kaimahi know the children well. Children are happy and enthusiastic in their learning.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Tane nui a Rangi will be in three years. 

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Tane nui a Rangi is in Murupara. It was the first kōhanga reo established in Murupara.

The kaiako and kaimahi provide an interesting environment for children. Kaimahi enjoy working in the kōhanga reo. Whānau contribute to programme and resource development.

2  The Review Findings

How well do children demonstrate whanaungatanga?

Children demonstrate whanaungatanga as they build positive relationships with their peers and others.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children learn in a supportive environment. Kaimahi have established good routines that enable children to transition between activities. Kaimahi provide activities that cater for children’s interests and promote engagement in learning. Kaimahi encourage children to interact positively as peers and as tuakana, teina. Children are comfortable and content.

Children have opportunities to explore and problem solve. They have access to a variety of resources. Whānau have developed a plan to improve the large outdoor area. Kaimahi have a good understanding of how children learn. Children are provided with a range of activities that support their learning and development. Children are confident learners.

Children are developing an understanding of te reo Māori. Children hear te reo Māori specific to their iwi. Children follow instructions and respond to requests. Kaimahi use appropriate strategies to support children’s language development. Whānau are supported and encouraged to use the words and simple phrases that the children hear on a daily basis. Children are growing in confidence to use te reo Māori.

Children enjoy meaningful relationships. They have strong whakapapa links to kaimahi and whānau. Children participate in community events and confidently interact with a range of people. Children are provided with opportunities to attend important hapū events. Kaimahi promote children’s ability to build trusting relationships. Children are comfortable with others.

Key Next Steps

Children’s growth in te reo Maori is limited. Whānau consider it important that their children learn te reo Māori. Whānau and kaimahi recognise the need to improve their level of te reo Māori. They encourage each other to seek support to strengthen their te reo Maori competency. 

Planning and Evaluation

Planning is effective and based on the needs and interests of the children. Whānau contribute their ideas to planning. Planning guides programme implementation. Planning for different age groups is evident. Children benefit from a well organised learning programme.

Kaimahi are clear about the purpose of assessment. Assessment processes are in place. They use profiles to monitor children’s learning. Observations and samples of children’s work are used to inform kaimahi about their progress and individual learning needs. Kaimahi regularly meet to reflect and discuss children’s learning and development. Whānau are well informed of their children’s progress.

Whānau have sound management practices. Health and safety policies and procedures are in place. Financial systems are transparent.  Personnel management processes and documentation are in place including job descriptions, individual employment contracts and self-appraisal. Kaimahi are clear about their roles and responsibilities for children’s learning.

Key Next Steps

Programme evaluation practices are limited. Kaimahi informally evaluate learning programmes. While they discuss the programme they do not document changes that they make as a result of their discussions or share these with whānau. Children’s learning is not extended.

The annual plan lacks detail. The whānau have clear strategic goals however there is no plan of how they will achieve these.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

To improve current practice the whānau must:

  • ensure that equipment, premises and facilities are regularly checked for hazards to children.

[r46 Education( Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008, HS4-8, Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated to Te Kōhanga National Trust 2008]. 

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • strengthen programme evaluation
  • develop and implement an appraisal system
  • conduct and record regular hazard checks
  • develop a detailed strategic plan.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Tāne-nui-a-Rangi is located in Murupara.  Children demonstrate whanaungatanga as they build positive relationships with their peers and others. Kaimahi know the children well. Children are happy and enthusiastic in their learning.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Tane nui a Rangi will be in three years.
