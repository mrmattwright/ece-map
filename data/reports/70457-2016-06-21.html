1 Evaluation of Cotswold Preschool and Nursery

The centre is Well placed to promote positive learning outcomes for children.

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Cotswold Preschool and Nursery is a small community-based early childhood education (ECE) centre that has been operating for almost 50 years. Children and families experience a welcoming and caring service that encourages close relationships between the centre, the child and the family. An elected committee of parents is responsible for the governance of the centre.

An increasing number of children come from diverse cultural backgrounds. This is reflected in the profile of the centre staffing.

The centre is involved with a group of local ECE centres and schools working together to improve educational outcomes for children. Transitions into, within the centre and to school have benefitted from the collaborative work.

The centre has made some progress in addressing the recommendations from the 2013 ERO Report in self review, planning and assessment. Further development is required in strategic planning and appraisal. These are identified within the report.

The Review Findings

Children learn in spacious, well-presented and well-resourced environments. They have an opportunity to direct their own learning by asking teachers to set up activities and resources that match their interests, needs and passions. The outdoor environment provides for fun, physical challenge and creativity.

Infants and toddlers learn through uninterrupted play in an attractive environment. Routines are unhurried and in line with routines at home. Children are acknowledged as individuals through close responsive relationships with the nursery teachers.

Relationships are positive and respectful among children and with adults. Children play well together and support each other in their learning. There is an inclusive culture with the many ethnicities of children and staff reflected in the environment and celebrations. Children with special and educational needs are well supported by teachers and parents in conjunction with outside agencies.

The centre philosophy of increased children‚Äôs self choice in their learning, a focus on strengthening and extending children‚Äôs interests is embedded in centre practice. There is a broad range of curriculum opportunities to capture and extend on children‚Äôs interests. Children have many opportunities to learn about healthy eating and sustainable practices and to extend their physical capabilities through play.

Teachers make purposeful use of team observations of children at play to plan interesting programmes that build on children‚Äôs knowledge and understanding. Parents receive useful information in learning stories and assessments to show the progress children are making in their learning. Links to home experiences are reflected in the learning opportunities in the centre.

Aspects of the environment reflect bicultural New Zealand. The centre has engaged with the local marae to further their understanding and knowledge of te ao MƒÅori.

Managers are increasingly encouraging teachers to take responsibility for aspects of the operation of the centre. The centre is part of a collaboration of community-based ECEs that provide support to leaders and teachers to its improve their practice. Self review is supporting the centre to make well considered improvements to centre and teacher practice.

Key Next Steps

The centre has identified, and ERO agrees, the next steps to further improve outcomes for children include:

  • seeking support to assist with the development of robust policies and procedures related to new legislation that has an important impact on appointments, child protection and hazard remediation

  • improving evaluation of whole-centre planning to include what impact the programme has had on children‚Äôs learning, and what teachers could do to improve practice

  • clarifying centre expectations to ensure staff and children have opportunities to learn about and through the unique language, culture and identity of Aotearoa New Zealand

  • implementing and embedding the newly adopted appraisal system

  • strengthening strategic and annual planning.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Cotswold Preschool and Nursery completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum

  • premises and facilities

  • health and safety practices

  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)

  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)

  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)

  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.
