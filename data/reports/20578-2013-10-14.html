2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana te kōhanga reo o Pomaria ki te kura o Pomaria ki tāone nui o Henderson ki Waitakere. E tū ana te kōhanga reo o Pomaria ki tēnei wāhi mai i te tau 1986. Kua whakaritea e te whānau tētahi taiao ataahua, tētahi taiao kore paru me tētahi taiao manaaki mā ā rātou tamariki. Mai i te wā i tīmata ai te kōhanga reo kua piri pūmau te kaiwhakahaere ki tēnei kōhanga reo. He mema o te poari kaitiaki o te kura o Pomaria ia mō ngā tau tekau mā whitu.

E whakahaere whaihua ana ngā kaimahi i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā, arā, te wātaka hei whakahaere i ngā tamariki, ngā ngohe akoranga me te haepapa hei mātakitaki me te tiaki i ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa. Kua arotakengia ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru mā te kōrero ngātahi ki te whānau. He manaaki i te whaiora o ngā tamariki te tino pūtake o ngā whakahaere katoa ki te kōhanga reo.

I kōwhiritia e te whānau teMana Whenuahei kaupapa arotahi mā tēnei arotake. E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki te whai atu i ngā wheako hei tākaro ki a rātou anō. E āhei ana rātou ki te whiriwhiri i ngā ngohe e hiahiatia ana e rātou te whai atu i ngā ata. He whānui rawa ngā rauemi kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi, ā, he rauemi whakaihiihi ēnei rawa hei tautoko i hōtaka mā ngā tamariki. E whakanui ana rātou i te reo Māori me te aroturuki i ngā taunekeneke pāpori i waenganui i ngā tamariki. Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi ngā wheako hei tautoko i tēnā tamaiti me tēnā tamaiti ki te kōrero i tōna kōtahi ki ngā kaimahi. E whai wāhi atu ana te whānau ki te ako i te reo Maori. Me haere tonu ngā whakaritenga mā ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakapakari ake me te whakapiki i ō rātou mātau ki te reo Māori.

He mea whaitake ngā whakahaere a te kōhanga reo. He mea whaihua ngā pūnaha whakahaere pūtea. E kaha nei te nuinga o ngā mātua o te whānau ki te tautoko i ngā haerenga, ngā mahi ngākau nui hei whakapaipai i te kōhanga, te hōtaka o ia rā me ngā hui ki te kōhanga reo. Kua whakatūria e te whānau tētahi māhere rautaki hei tautoko i tā rātou ahunga whakamua. Ka kitea ngā putanga hua kua hāpaitia hei arotake whaiaro i ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga i te wā i te wā. Ko ngā whakahaere e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru, te māhere rautaki, ngā whakahaere e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi tae noa ki te hōtaka ngā arotake whaiaro ka kitea. E whai painga ngā tamariki mā ngā whakahaere kua whakatūria i te kōhanga reo.

Ahakoa kua whakatakotoria ngā māhere ā-kaupapa e ai ki ngā huarere o te tau me ngā atua Māori, mō te tukanga e arataki ai ngā māhere kāore e kitea ngā kōrero hōhonu. He whāiti noa ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki te aromatawai mō tēnā tamaiti me tēnā tamaiti. Me tautokotia ngā kaimahi me te whānau e te Poari Matua o te Kōhanga Reo ki te whakapai ake i ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakatakoto māhere me te whai māramatanga e pā ana ki te pūtake o te aromatawai.

Ngā Mahi Ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o Te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Pomaria kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Te Kōhanga Reo o PomariaKātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko āna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Whenua.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere i te māhere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • hōtaka whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Whenua

He whakamārama

I kōwhiria e te whānau teMana Whenuahei kaupapa arotahi mā tēnei arotake. E ai ki teMana Whenuaka waia haere te tamaiti ki te whakapapa ki ōna tūpuna, ki ōna uaratanga tae noa ki ngā whakapono. E tipu ake te mana tuakiri o ia tamaiti me māramatanga o ia tamaiti ki te tiaki i te taiao. E hāngai ana te mana tuakiri o te tamaiti ki te ao ō mataora tae noa ki te ao ō ahupungao.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Te tākaro takitahi o tēnā me tēnā.E whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki ki te whai i ngā wheako kia āhei rātou ki te tākaro i ō rātou kōtahi. Kua whakaritea ngā wā kia taea ai e ngā tamariki te whiriwhiri i ā rātou ake ngohe i ngā ata. E whakahaere ana ngā kaimahi i ngā rauemi huhua hei whakanui i te reo Māori, hei aroturuki i ngā whanonga ā-pāpori a nga tamariki me ngā wheako whakawhitiwhiti kōrero a tēnā me tēnā. E mahi tahi ana ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki, ā, e whakaputa ana rātou i ngā pātai hei wero i ngā whai whakaaro o ngā tamariki. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te oti, te tātaki, te whai atu me te whakarite i tētahi tūranga motuhake mā rātou anō e ai ki te reanga ako o tēnā me tēnā. E ngākau nui ana ngā tamariki ki te tākaro tahi ki a rātou anō.

Te tiaki me te whaiora.He whaihua te āhua a ngā kaimahi ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā, arā, te wātaka whakahaere a ngā tamariki, ngā ngohe akoranga me te haepapa nui hei mātakitaki i ngā tamariki.

E tino marama ana ngā tamariki i ngā take e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru tae noa ki ngā āhuatanga whakahaere e pā ana ki te paru kore, ā, e whakahaeretia ana ēnei tūkanga e ngā kaimahi. Ka nui te kai mā ngā tamariki, ā, he māhana te taiao i te wā e noho ana rātou ki te kōhanga reo. Kua arotakengia ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru mā te kōrero ngātahi ki te whānau. He manaaki i te whaiora o ngā tamariki te tino pūtake o ngā whakahaere katoa ki te kōhanga reo.

Te taiao akoranga.Kua whakatūria e ngā kaimahi tētahi taiao paru kore me tētahi taiao kia rere pai ai ngā takamahi mā ngā tamariki. Kua whakaritea pai ngā wāhi akoranga ō-roto me ngā wāhi akoranga ō-waho kia whai wāhi ai ngā tamariki ki te hōpara haere i ō rātou matea ā-tinana me ō rātou matea ā-pāpori. He rauemi whānui rawa a ngā kaimahi hei tautoko i a rātou ki te whakatinana i tētahi hōtaka pārekareka.

Ngā wheako akoranga mō te reo Māori.Ka kōrerotia te reo Māori i ngā wā me ngā wāhanga katoa o te hotaka. E whakaputa ana ngā tamariki i ngā kupu māmā noa me ngā rerenga māmā noa e mōhio ana i a rātou i te wā e ako ana rātou i te kaupapa tae noa ki te wā e tākaro ana rātou. E whakaputa ana ngā kaimahi i ngā kīanga māmā noa, ngā rerenga kōrero me ngā kupu e hāngai ana ki te kaupapa o te wā. E whakamahi ana ngā tamariki i ngā pakiwaitara me ngā waiata hei tautoko i a rātou ki te whakapiki i tō rātou mātau ki te reo Māori.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te whakapiki i te reo Māori.Me tino whai wāhi ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whai i ngā wheako ki te whakapiki i ō rātou mātau ki te reo Māori. Kia haere tonu te tautoko mai i ngā mātua o te whānau, ngā kaumātua, te purapura o te rohe me te Poari Matua o te Kōhanga Reo ka pai ake te whai hua o tēnā me tēnā. Mā tēnei e taea ai, ā, kua tautokotia ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakaputa i ngā rerenga whai tikanga ki ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamāherehere me te aromātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo hei whakatau i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamāherehere, ngā whakahaere me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga reo mā a rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka āta hāngai te aromātai nei ki te hōtaka whakamāherehere me te aroturuki i te tipuranga a ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Ngā whakahaere a te whānau.E whaihua ana ngā whakahaere o te kōhanga reo. E taetae mai ana ngā mātua o te whānau ki ngā hui ā-whānau ia marama. E whai hua ana ngā pūnaha whakahaere pūtea.E kaha ana te nuinga o ngā mātua o te whānau ki te tautoko i ngā haerenga, ngā mahi ngākau nui hei whakapaipai i te kōhanga, te hōtaka o ia rā me ngā hui ki te kōhanga reo.Kua whakatūria e te kaiwhakahaere ngā tukanga whaitake hei whakahaere me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. E ako ana ngā mātua o te whānau i te reo Māori ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki tae noa ki a rātou haere ki ngā kura ā-rohe hei ako i te reo. Kua whakatūria e te whānau tētahi māhere rautaki hei tautoko i ngā mahi ā meāke nei. Ka kitea ngā arotake whaiaro. Ko ngā take e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru, te māhere rautaki, ngā take e pā ana ki te whakahaere i ngā kaimahi me te hōtaka o ia rā ētahi tauira o ngā aromātai kua oti i a rātou.E whai painga ngā tamariki mā ngā āhuatanga whakahaere kua whakatūria i te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te hōtaka aromātai.Kāore anō te hōtaka aromātai kia ata tuhia. Mā te mau i ngā mēneti o ngā whakwhitiwhiti kōrero e pā ana ki te hōtaka aromātai ka riro i ngā kaimahi:

  • ngā tuhinga ā-tuhi o ngā whakatau e pā ana ki ngā āhuatanga o te hōtaka o ia rā
  • ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga e whai hua ana me ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō
  • tētahi tirohanga whānui o ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi tae noa ki tētahi tirohanga o ngā whakahaere whānui tonu.

Te hōtaka whakamāherehere. Ahakoa kua whakatakotoria ngā māhere ā-kaupapa e ai ki ngā huarere o te tau me ngā atua Māori engari mō te tukanga e arataki ai ngā māhere kāore e kitea ngā kōrero hōhonu. Kua whakatakotoria ngā māhere ā-wiki me ngā māhere mō ia wāhanga ako, ā, kei roto i ngā māhere ngā kaupapa me ētahi whāinga ako, ngā tukanga whakahaere mō ia rā me ngā ngohe e hāngai ana kiNgā Taumata Whakahirahira. Mā te tāpiri anō i ētahi kōrero ka pai ake ngā whakahaere hei arataki i ngā kaimahi ki te whakahaere i ngā kaupapa.

Te aromatawai.Kei te reanga māmā noa ngā āhuatanga whakahaere mō te aromatawai. Ahakoa kua whakatūria ngā tauira toi i roto i ngā pukapuka nui mā ngā tamariki katoa, he whāiti noa ngā mōhiohio mō ia tamaiti. Me tautokotia ngā kaimahi e te Poari Matua o te Kōhanga Reo ki te āwhina i a rātou ki:

  • te mārama i te pūtake o te aromatawai
  • te whiriwhiri me te whakatau i ngā mōhiohio e whai hua ana hei arahi i a rātou anō ki te tāutu i ngā akoranga whakamua mō ia tamaiti
  • te aromatawai i ngā paetae o ngā tamariki e hāngai ana ki ngā akoranga ā-kaupapa.

Kāore ngā kaimahi me te whānau e tino mōhio ana i ngā paetae me te tipuranga ako o ā rātou tamariki.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pomaria is situated at Pomaria School in Henderson, West Auckland. The kōhanga reo has been on this site since 1986. The whānau maintains an attractive, clean and comfortable kōhanga reo environment for their children. The kaiwhakahaere has been associated with this kōhanga reo since it was established. She has been a member of the Pomaria School Board of Trustees for seventeen years.

Kaimahi effectively manage the daily operations which include managing children’s routines and learning activities and supervision practices. Health and safety policies and procedures are regularly reviewed in consultation with whānau. Children’s well being is at the heart of kōhanga reo management.

The whānau chose Mana Whenua as the focus area for this review. Children have opportunities for independent play. They are able to choose activities throughout the morning. Kaimahi have an extensive range of resources which they use to provide a vibrant programme for children. They promote te reo Māori and monitor social behaviour. Kaimahi provide opportunities for individual conversations with children. While whānau have opportunities to learn te reo Māori, kaimahi and whānau should continue to improve their capability in te reo Māori.

The kōhanga reo is well managed. Financial management systems are effective. Whānau often support trips, working bees, the daily programme and events at the kōhanga reo. The whānau has a strategic plan in place to support their future direction. Regular self review of the kōhanga reo is evident. This includes review of health and safety practices, the strategic plan, personnel management and the programme. Children benefit from a well established and well managed kōhanga reo.

While kaupapa planning is based on seasons and ngā atua Māori, the overall processes for planning lack vital detail. There is limited assessment information available about individual children. TKRNT needs to support the kaimahi and whānau to refine planning processes and to understand the purpose of assessment.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

4 Findings

Mana Whenua

Background

The whānau chose Mana Whenua as the focus area for this review. Mana Whenua asserts that the child will become familiar with ancestral connections, values and beliefs. Each child will develop a strong sense of belonging and become environmentally aware. This will include the natural (living) world and the physical (non-living) environment.

Areas of good performance

Independent play. Children have opportunities for independent play. There are periods where children are able to choose their activities throughout the morning. Kaimahi use a variety of resources to promote te reo Māori, monitor children’s social behaviour and for individual conversations. Kaimahi engage well with children and use questions to challenge their thinking. Children are able to compete, lead, be guided and establish a place for themselves within their age groups. Children enjoy playing together.

Care and wellbeing.Kaimahi effectively manage the daily operation which includes managing routines, learning activities and the supervision of children. Health, safety and hygiene practices are understood by children and managed well by kaimahi. Children are well fed and kept warm while they are at the kōhanga reo. Health and safety policies and procedures are regularly reviewed in consultation with whānau. Children’s wellbeing is at the heart of kōhanga reo management.

The learning environment. Kaimahi have established a tidy and easy flowing environment for children. Learning centres in the indoor and outdoor areas are well organised for children’s physical and social exploration. Kaimahi have an extensive range of resources to help them implement a vibrant programme for children. Children have numerous opportunities to move from one activity to the next.

Te reo Māori learning opportunities. Te reo Māori is used consistently throughout the programme. Children use simple words and phrases they are familiar with during kaupapa learning and while they play. Kaimahi use simple phrases and sentences and vocabulary associated with the current kaupapa. Children use stories and songs to support their language development.

Area for development

Te reo Māori development. Kaimahi and whānau should take all opportunities to improve their abilities in te reo Māori. Ongoing support from whānau members, kaumatua, local purapura and Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust is valuable. This will support kaimahi and whānau to deliver correct and appropriate language structures to children.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management.The kōhanga reo is well managed. Whānau hui are well attended each month. Financial management systems are effective. Whānau often support trips, working bees, the daily programme and events at kōhanga reo.The kaiwhakahaere has effective systems and procedures to manage and monitor all aspects the kōhanga reo. Whānaulearn te reo Māori alongside their children at kōhanga reo and through local courses. The whānau has a strategic plan in place to support their future direction. Regular self review of kōhanga reo operation is evident. This includes health and safety, the strategic plan, personnel management and the daily programme. This results in a safe, efficient and fun environment for children and adults. Children benefit from a well established and managed kōhanga reo.

Areas for development

Programme evaluation. Programme evaluation is not well documented. Keeping minutes of programme evaluation discussions will provide kaimahi with:

  • a record of decisions about aspects of the daily programme
  • information about what is working well and what might be improved
  • an overall view of kaimahi practice and overall operations.

Programme planning. While kaupapa planning is based on seasons and ngā atua Māori, the overall processes for planning lack vital detail. There are term and weekly plans which include kaupapa with some learning objectives, routines and activities that link to Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Additional detail would more effectively guide kaimahi in the delivery of kaupapa.

Assessment.Assessment is in the early stages of development. While children’s scrapbooks contain some artwork, there is limited assessment information available about each child. TKRNT needs to support the staff and whānau to help them to:

  • understand the purpose of assessment
  • determine what information would be useful to assist them to determine each child’s next steps for learning
  • assess children’s progress and development in kaupapa learning.

The kaimahi and whānau are not well informed about the progress and development of their children.

6 Recommendations

ERO recommends the TKRNT assist kaimahi to:

  • improve their individual skills and abilities in te reo Māori
  • improve their knowledge and skills in planning, programme evaluation and assessment practices.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
