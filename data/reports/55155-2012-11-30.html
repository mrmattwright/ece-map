1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā tētahi ō ngā Kaiwhakahaere Matua ā-Rohe o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei, kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Maara a Tāwhao, e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia e te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i te Here-turi-kōkā 2011 me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

  • te reo me ngā tikanga Māori – te hōtaka akoranga, ngā whai wāhitanga ako reo, te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromātai, me te aromatawai
  • te kawenga takohanga a te whānau – te arotake whaiaro mō te pūrongo, te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga, me te whakahaere rautaki
  • te whakahaeretanga a te whānau – ngā tūranga o te whānau me ā rātou kawenga mō te whakahaere pai
  • ētahi take i tāutuhia ai whai muri i te arotake o mua.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Maara a Tāwhao ki tētahi marae i Greenmeadows, ki te tapa o Ahuriri. E aromātai ana tēnei pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te whānuitanga o te ahu whakamua me ngā whakapaitanga kua whakatutukihia e te whānau, mai i te arotake mātauranga o te tau 2011. E ai ki te whānau o te kōhanga reo, kei te whakapau kaha tonu rātou ki te whakapai i ngā whakaritenga whakahaere me te hōtaka akoranga mō ā rātou tamariki.

I tāutu te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2011, i te maha o ngā wāhanga ki te hōtaka akoranga kāhore i te whakawhanakehia hei āta whakatutuki i ngā matea akoranga o ngā tamariki. Me whakahaere ake ngā mema o te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki, mō te kawenga takohanga, te māramatanga hoki.

Kua mahi ngā māngai ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te taha o te whānau, ki te whakatutuki i ngā wāhanga matua, me te whakapakari i te māramatanga o te whānau, te pai hoki o tā rātou whakarite pai ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. E pakari tonu ana te mōhiotanga o te whānau ki te whakahaere. He nui ngā whakapaitanga kua whakatutukihia e te whānau.

He pai ake te whakahaere i ngā hōtaka akoranga, ā, e whakarato ana i te pikitanga ake o ngā akoranga, kia ako ngā tamariki i te reo Māori mā ngā huarahi e hihiri ana, e whai pūtake ana hoki. He nui ake te whakaongaonga o ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga. Ko te ako ngaio me te whakawhanaketanga ngaio o ngā kaimahi, i whai pānga ki te whakarerekē i tō rātou māramatanga ki ngā mahi aromatawai. Kua whakaritea ngā mahi aromatawai papai. Ko te roanga o ngā akoranga me te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki, ka kitea ki ngā mōhiohio aromatawai.

Kua tino pai ake te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kua whai wāhi nui te tautoko, te ārahitanga hoki o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki ēnei whakapaitanga ki te whānau. Kua whakatūngia ētahi ki ngā tūranga matua mō te whakahaere tari, whakahaere hoki. Ko ngā tāngata kua whakatūngia ki aua tūranga, he mātau, ā, e kawe ana hoki i ngā pūkenga matua ki ēnei mahi. Kua whakapakari anō tēnei i te whakahaere tari, ā, kua whakahoki anōtia te whakahaeretanga ki te papai o ngā whakaritenga a te whānau mō te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo.

Kua pai ake te whakamōhio i ngā mema o te whānau, mō te āhua o ngā mahi me te whakahaeretanga o tō rātou kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. He pai te whakawhanaketanga o te arotake whaiaro, ā, e whakarato ana tēnei i ngā mōhiohio papai mō te whānau, i roto i te whakatau whakaritenga me te whakamahere. E arotahi ana ngā hui ā-whānau ki ngā whāinga o te kōhanga reo. He pai te tae ā-tinana atu o te whānau ki ngā hui, me tā rātou whai wāhi atu hoki. He pai kē atu tā rātou tūnga i nāianei ki te arotahi ki te whakatutuki i ā rātou whāinga rautaki, tūmanako hoki. E ai ki Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ka haere tonu te whakarato i te aroturuki, i ngā akoranga ā-whānau hoki i whakamaheretia ai.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Te reo me ngā tikanga Māori

He whakamārama

I tāutu te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2011, kāhore i te āta whakawhanakehia ngā wāhanga matua e tika ana hei whakahaere, hei aroturuki, hei whakatinana hoki i ngā hōtaka akoranga mō ngā tamariki. Nā tēnei, kāhore i te rawaka te whai whakaarotanga ki ngā matea ako, ngā ngākau nuitanga hoki o ngā tamariki, ā, kāhore e whakatutukihia ana.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Te reo Māori. Ka whakaaro nui ngā kaimahi ki te whakanui i te reo Māori ki ngā tamariki i ngā wā ako. He matatau te kuia ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, ā, ko ia hoki te kaiako. Ka whakatauira ia i te whakamahinga o te reo Māori. Ka whai pānga nui tēnei ki te whakapakaritanga ake o te māia me te āheinga o ētahi atu o ngā kaimahi, ki te āta whakamahi ake i te reo Māori i roto i ā rātou taunekeneke ki ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki ka hāpai i te whakanuitanga o te reo Māori, tae atu ki te kipakipa me ngā kīanga. He hihiri ngā tamariki, ā, he kaha hoki ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā pukapuka o te reo Māori ka whakaatuhia. Kua arotahi te whānau ki te whakapakari i te āheinga o ngā kaimahi me te whānau i roto i te reo Māori, hei hāpai ake anō i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki.

Te whakamahere hōtaka. Kei te mārama ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere hōtaka. He aronga ngātahi ki te whakamahere mā te whakamahi i Te Whāriki i ngā akoranga. E whakarato ana ngā mahere i te kiko e tika ana hei āwhina i ngā kaimahi ki te whakapuaki me te whakahaere i ngā akoranga mō ngā tamariki katoa. Ka whakaatu ngā kaimahi i te pakari ake o te whakaritenga mō te hōtaka o ia rā. E whakaongaongatia ana ngā tamariki e te hōtaka akoranga o ia rā, ā, ka āta tahuri atu ki ngā mahi.

Te aromatawai. Kua whakapau kaha ngā kaimahi katoa i roto i te tau kua pahure ake nei, hei āta whakarite i ngā mahi aromatawai papai e whakarato ana i te tino tūāpapa mō te pūrongo i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki. Kua rapu rātou i te ārahitanga ngaio, mai i tō rātou purapura. He pai ngā mōhiohio e whakaemihia ana mā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo huarahi e pā ana ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki mā Te Whāriki. E tīmata ana ngā kaimahi ki te noho marama ki ngā huarahi ki te whakamahi i aua mōhiohio kia whai pānga ai ki te waihanga me te whakapuakitanga o te hōtaka, hei whakatutuki i te rerekē haere o ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki.

Te hōtaka akoranga. Ka kitea ngā tino whakapaitanga ki te hōtaka akoranga o ngā tamariki. I nāianei, ka whiwhi rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā wheako akoranga o Te Whāriki, o ō rātou ngākau nuitanga hoki, puta noa i te rā. Ka whakawhānuihia te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori, mā ngā taumahi e whai pūtake ana, e whakaongaonga ana, ā, kua whakahāngaitia hoki. Ka whakaatu ngā kaimahi i te mōhiotanga ki te whakarato i tētahi taiao akoranga e āta whai rauemi ana, e whakaongaongatia ana hoki mō ngā tamariki. Ka whakarato ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā rauemi hei whiriwhiri, ā, e whakaongaonga ana ēnei i ngā akoranga me te whakawhanaketanga, puta noa i te rā.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te aromātai hōtaka. Kua mahi ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhanake i tō rātou māramatanga ki te aromātai hōtaka, kia haere tonu ai te whakapai i te hōtaka, i roto i te tau kua pahure ake nei. E mōhio ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi, ko te tuhi haere me te pūrongo i ngā whakaritenga ki te aromātai hōtaka, tētahi āhuatanga hei whakawhanake ake. Ka whai hua ki te whānau te whakanui a ngā kaimahi i te kiko o ngā mōhiohio e tuhia ana, e pūrongotia ana hoki mō ngā akoranga o Te Whāriki a ngā tamariki. Ka whai hua hoki tēnei ki ngā kaimahi, i roto i te aroturuki i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga mō ngā tamariki katoa, inā koa mō ngā tama tuākana me ngā pēpi.

Te kawenga takohanga a te whānau

He whakamārama

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kīhai te whānau i āta whakamōhiotia i te āhua o ngā mahi i roto i te maha o ngā wāhanga o te kōhanga reo. Kāhore i te āta whakawhanakehia te arotake whaiaro, hei aroturuki ōkawa i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, me tana aronga, i runga i ngā whāinga o tā rātou tūtohinga i te kōhanga reo, me Te Korowai.

I te ngoikore te whakahaere i ngā pūtea. I taunaki te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia aroturuki tonu, kia hāpai tonu Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i te whānau, hei whakapai ake i te kawenga takohanga a te whānau. Kua whakatūngia ētahi ki ngā tūranga whakahaere matua, hei whakahoki anō i te kawenga takohanga a te whānau. E ai ki Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo me te whānau, he nui ngā whakapaitanga kua whakatutukihia.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Te pai ake o te arotake whaiaro. Kua whakapau kaha ngā mema ki te whakapai i te arotake whaiaro a te whānau. Pūrongotia ai te nuinga o ngā wāhanga o te kōhanga reo i ia te wā, tae atu ki ngā rawa, ngā kaimahi, te hauora, te haumaru, ngā pūtea, te ako a te whānau, me ngā mahi whakangungu a ngā kaimahi.

Ka whakarato ngā meneti a te whānau i ngā mōhiohio papai e pā ana ki te whānuitanga o te ahunga whakamua me te āhua mahi o te kōhanga reo. E ai ki te whānau, kua pai ake tō rātou noho mōhio ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki. I nāianei, ka whakamahia te arotake whaiaro e te whānau hei aroturuki i ngā whakapaitanga, hei whakamahere hoki mō ngā matea o te whānau ā meāke nei, tae atu ki ngā wānanga a te whānau i ia te wā mō Te Korowai. Ka arotahi ngātahi te whānau o te kōhanga reo kia hāngai ai te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo ki ā rātou whāinga i te tūtohinga.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te pūrongo rautaki, te arotake hoki. Kua whakamaheretia te hao nui o ngā whāinga rautaki wā roa, tae noa atu ki te tau 2033. E mōhio ana te whānau ko te pūrongo auau ki ngā hui o ia marama i ētahi kaupapa rautaki i whiriwhirihia ai, ka hāpai ake i a rātou ki te āta aroturuki ake i te ahunga whakamua me te angitu hoki ki te whakatutuki i ā rātou whāinga rautaki.

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau

He whakamārama

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kāhore i te rawaka te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki. Kāhore i te pakari ngā mema o te whānau, ā, kīhai hoki i whakaatu i te māramatanga pai ki ō rātou tūranga whakahaere, ā rātou kawenga whakahaere hoki.

I taunaki te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia mahi te whānau ki te taha o ngā māngai o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, hei whakapai ake i tō rātou māramatanga ki ngā tūmanako, ngā tūranga, me ngā kawenga mahi hoki i hāngai ai ki te whakahaeretanga.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau. Kua pai ake te whakahaere me te whakahaere tari o te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki. Kua mahi Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te taha o te whānau ki te whakatairanga i ngā taumata whakahaere mō ngā tamariki me āna mema. Kua whai pānga nui ngā wānanga i whakaritea ai, me te whakatū tāngata ki tūranga whakahaere kōhanga reo, tūranga whakahaere tari hoki.

Kua āwhina hoki aua whakatūnga i te whānau, ki te whakapai ake i tō rātou māramatanga, tō rātou pakari anō hoki mō ngā tohu o te angitu ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. I nāianei ka aroturukitia, ka pūrongotia hoki ngā wāhanga matua o te whakahaeretanga i ia te wā. Ko ngā wāhanga ka pūrongotia, ko ngā rawa, ngā kaimahi, te hauora me te haumaru, te ako a te whānau, me te whakahaere rautaki.

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo kia tahuri te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te:

  • whakamahi tonu, te whakawhānui tonu hoki i ngā whakaritenga arotake whaiaro, hei aroturuki, hei pūrongo hoki i te ahunga whakamua me te angitu mā ngā whāinga rautaki
  • whakapakari i te arotake me te pūrongo ōkawa o te hōtaka akoranga mā ngā tamariki.

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Maara a Tāwhao governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the August 2011 ERO report or issues identified since that review.  The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • Te Reo me Ngā Tikanga Māori-learning programme, language learning opportunities, programme planning, evaluation and assessment
  • whānau accountability-self review for reporting, decision making and strategic management
  • whānau management-whānau roles and responsibilities for effective management
  • any issues identified since the previous review.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Maara a Tawhao is based on a marae in Greenmeadows, on the outskirts of Napier. This ERO Supplementary Review report evaluates the extent of progress and improvements whānau has made since the 2011 Education Review. Kōhanga reo whānau members report that they continue to work hard on improving management practices and the learning programme for their children.

The 2011 ERO report identified a number of areas in the learning programme that were not well developed to cater sufficiently for children’s learning needs. Whānau members and kaimahi also needed to better manage all areas of the kōhanga reo for their children and to ensure accountability and transparency.

Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) district representatives have worked closely with the whānau to address priority areas and build whānau understanding and good practice for managing the kōhanga reo. Whānau knowledge about kōhanga reo management continues to improve. The whānau has made significant improvements.

Programmes of learning are better managed and provide increased learning for children to learn te reo Māori in interesting and purposeful ways. Children are more stimulated in their learning. Kaimahi professional learning and development has helped to transform their understanding about assessment practice. Good assessment practices are established. Children’s learning and progress overtime is reflected in assessment information.

Kōhanga reo management has significantly improved. TKRNT support and guidance has been instrumental in these improvements with the whānau. Key appointments in administration and management roles have been made. The people appointed to these roles bring knowledge and necessary skills to these jobs. This has restored administration, and focused management back on best whānau practice for the kōhanga reo kaupapa.

Whānau members are better informed about how well their kōhanga reo is performing and is managed in the interests of their children. Self review is developing well and provides good information to whānau for future decision making and planning. Whānau hui are focused on kōhanga reo objectives. Whānau attendance and involvement at hui is good. They are now better placed to focus on achieving their strategic goals and objectives. TKRNT reports that continued monitoring and planned whānau learning will be provided.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Findings

Te Reo me Ngā Tikanga Maori

Background

The 2011 ERO report identified that key components necessary for managing, monitoring and implementing learning programmes for children were not well developed. Consequently, children’s learning needs and interests were not adequately considered and catered for.

Areas of progress

Te Reo Māori. Kaimahi make a conscious effort to promote te reo Māori to children during learning. The kuia/kaiako is a fluent speaker of te reo Māori. She models the use of te reo Māori. This impacts positively on other kaimahi who are building their confidence and capability to use more te reo Māori during interactions with children. Kaimahi use strategies to help promote te reo Māori, including prompts and phrases. Children are excited and engaged when te reo Māori books are shared with them. Whānau have focused on building kaimahi and whānau capability in te reo Māori to further support children’s language development.

Programme planning. Kaimahi understand programme planning. There is a shared approach to planning using Te Whāriki for learning. Plans provide necessary detail to assist kaimahi with delivery and management of learning for all children. Kaimahi demonstrate a greater level of organisation and preparation for the daily programme. Children are stimulated by the daily programme for learning and are engaged.

Assessment. Kaimahi have collectively worked hard over the past year to establish good assessment practices that provide a solid foundation for reporting on children’s development. They have sought professional guidance from their purapura. Good information is collected in a range of useful ways about aspects of children’s learning through Te Whāriki. Kaimahi are beginning to understand how this information can be used to inform programme design and delivery to meet the changing needs and interests of children.

Learning programme. Children’s learning programmes reflect significant improvements. They now receive a range of Te Whāriki and interest-based learning experiences throughout the day. Children’s understanding of te ao Māori is extended through meaningful, stimulating and focused activities. Kaimahi demonstrate an awareness of the need to deliver a well resourced, stimulating learning environment for children. Children are provided with resources to choose from that stimulate learning and development throughout the day.

Areas for further development

Programme evaluation. Kaimahi have worked on developing their understanding of programme evaluation for continual programme improvement over the past year. Whānau and kaimahi acknowledge that recording and reporting practices for programme evaluation needs further development. If kaimahi increased the detail of information recorded and reported about Te Whāriki learning for children it would be useful to whānau. This would also be valuable to kaimahi for monitoring the effectiveness of the learning programme for all children, particularly tuakana boys and babies.

Whānau Accountability

Background

The previous ERO report identified that whānau were not well informed about how many areas of the kōhanga reo were performing. Self review was not well developed to formally monitor kōhanga reo management and its direction against their kōhanga reo charter objectives and Te Korowai.

Finances were poorly managed. ERO recommended TKRNT continues to monitor and support the whānau to improve whānau accountability. New appointments have been made to key delegated management positions to help restore whānau accountability. TKRNT and whānau report significant improvements.

Areas of progress

Improved self review. Members have worked hard to improve whānau self review. Regular reporting occurs across most areas of the kōhanga reo, including property, personnel, health and safety, finances, whānau learning and kaimahi training.

Whānau minutes provide good information about overall progress and performance of the kōhanga reo. Whānau report that they are better informed about kōhanga reo management of their children. Self review is now used by whānau to monitor improvements and plan for future whānau needs, including regular whānau wānanga on Te Korowai. The kōhanga reo whānau collectively focuses on managing the kōhanga reo in line with their charter objectives.

Areas for further development

Strategic reporting and review. Ambitious long term strategic objectives are planned to year 2033. Whānau acknowledge that regular reporting at monthly hui on selected strategic priorities would support them to monitor progress and success towards meeting their strategic objectives more closely.

Whānau Management

Background

The previous ERO report identified that the kōhanga reo was not adequately managed by its whānau for children. Whānau members were not confident and did not demonstrate a good understanding about their management roles and responsibilities. ERO recommended whānau work with TKRNT representatives to improve their understanding about management expectations, roles and responsibilities.

Areas of progress

Whānau management. Management and administration of the kōhanga reo for children has improved. TKRNT has worked closely with whānau to raise management standards for children and its members. Planned wānanga and key appointments in management and administration positions have been significant.

These appointments have helped whānau to improve their understanding and confidence about what is successful kōhanga reo management. Key management areas are now regularly monitored and reported on. The areas reported on include property, personnel, health and safety, whānau learning and strategic management.

4 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau recommend the kōhanga reo whānau:

  • continues to use and extend self review practices to monitor and report on progress and success with strategic objectives
  • strengthens review and formal reporting of the learning programme for children.

5 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based on Te Whāriki, the whānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
