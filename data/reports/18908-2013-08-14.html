2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana te Kōhanga Reo o Te Piri o Waimirirangi ki te tāone taiwhenua ki te rohe o te Tai Tokerau. Ko te marae tētahi wāhi nui mā ngā tamariki me te hapori, ā, ki reira ako ai ngā tamariki. E tino whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā hui ka tū ki te marae me te tirotiro i ngā kaumātua me ngā kuia e hāpai ana i ngā mahi a ngā tūpuna. Ko te noho piri tata a ngā kaumātua i te roto i te kōhanga reo tētahi take e whai hua ana ngā tamariki.

Ko te reo Māori me ōna tikanga tētahi kaupapa matua o te kōhanga reo. Kei ngā reanga ako rerekē ngā tamariki mō te hopu i te reo Māori. E āhei ana ētahi o ngā tamariki pakeke ki te whakahoki kōrero mā ngā pātai me te kaha whakaputa i ō rātou pepeha. E whai wāhi atu ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero ki ngā tamariki, ā, he whai painga tēnei tūmomo wheako mā ngā tamariki. E taea ana e ngā mātua o te whānau ki te whakapā ki ngā akoranga hei ako i te reo Māori kia whai wāhi ai rātou ki te ako ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki i te koanga ngākau.

He kaihao ētahi o ngā mātua o te whānau. He kai whakahirahira te ika i te wā e manaaki ana rātou i ngā manuhiri ki te kōhanga reo. Ko Tangaroa tētahi tino horopaki ako mā ngā kaupapa ako. Ko ngā wāhi hī ika me ngā tikanga haumaru tētahi kaupapa matua i te wā e whakawhitiwhiti kōrero ana ngā mātua i ngā pūkenga me te mātauranga o Tangaroa. E tino mōhio ana te whānau me kaha mōhio ā rātou tamariki i ēnei pūkenga me ngā mahi a ngā tūpuna kia ora ai rātou ki tō rātou taiao. Ka ahu mai anō tō rātou whaiora i te whenua me te mahi a Rongamatane, ā, ko tōna haepapa he tautoko me te manaaki i ngā matea o te whānau. Kei te whānau tētahi māra hua whenua kua hangaia e ngā wira rapa o te motokā. Ko ēnei māra tētahi wāhanga o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki e pā ana ki a Papatūānuku.

Te pai hoki o ngā mātua o te whānau ki te tautoko i tō rātou kōhanga reo. He tōtika tā te mahi a ngā mātua o te whānau ki te aroturuki i ngā mahi, ngā whakahaere me ngā whakaritenga o ia rā i te kōhanga reo. Ka whiriwhiri ngā mātua o te whānau i ngā kaupapa huhua e pā ana ki ngā whakahaere, arā, tā rātou tūtohinga me ngā kaupapa here, ngā whakangungu mā ngā kaimahi, te kohikohi pūtea me ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te whakahaere pūtea. E whakaae ana rātou e hāngai ana te utu o te mahi hei whakapaipai i te kōhanga ki ngā whakahaere pūtea i te mea kei tētahi wāhi taiwhenua te kōhanga reo e tū ana. E whai wāhi atu ana te whānau ki te whiriwhiri me te whakatau ngātahi i ngā take hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ko te marae, te hāhi, te whānau me te reo Māori te tūāpapa o ngā tūmanakotanga katoa o tēnei hapori mā ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā Mahi Ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Whanaungatanga

He whakamārama

Kei tētahi wāhi taiwhenua a Te Kao ki te rohe o te Tai Tokerau. E tū ana te kōhanga reo ki te marae me te toa, te whare ratonga mō te hauora, te kura me te tari ā-iwi o Te Aupouri. He kaimahi ngā mātua o te whānau ki roto i ēnei wāhi tae noa ki te kōhanga reo. E tino mōhio ana ngā tamariki ko Te Kao tō rātou tūrangawaewae.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Te whai wāhi a te whānau.Ko te whanaungatanga tētahi tino wahanga o te kōhanga reo. E tautoko ana ngā mātua o te whānau, ā, he rerekē te tautoko whaihua a tēna me tēna. Ko te noho ngātahi a ngā kaumātua i te wā i te wā tētahi whakaritenga harikoa mā ngā tamariki. Kua akiaki ngā mātua taiohi o te hapori ki te whai mahi i roto i te kōhanga reo. Kātahi ka hoki mai ētahi o ngā mātua ki te kāinga, ā, e kimi tonu ana rātou i ō rātou tūranga i roto i te hapori. Kua whakaritea tētahi wātaka whai mahi tā ngā mātua hei tautoko i ngā kaimahi. E whai mārama ana ngā mātua o te whānau mō ngā kaupapa, nga haerenga me paetae e whakatutuki ana i nga tamariki. He ōrite ngā whāinga a ngā tamariki mō ngā wāhanga ako. E mārama ana ngā tamariki i te kaupapa nui o te whānau i roto i te kōhanga me tō rātou haepapa nui atawhai i a rātou ia rā ia rā.

Ngā whakapā ki te marae.Ko te marae tētahi tino wāhi akoranga mā ngā tamariki. Ka kite ngā tamariki i ngā karanga me ngā whaikōrero a ngā kaumātua me ngā kuia. Ka rangona e rātou te reo Māori o tēna me tēna e kōrerotia ana i te marae. Ka kite ngā tamariki i te mahitahi a te whānau hei tiaki me te manaaki i ngā manuhiri. E tino mōhio ana ngā tamariki i te kaupapa nui o te marae me te mōhio anō hoki nō te marae anō hoki rātou. E noho mauritau ngā tamariki, ā, e mārama ana rātou ko te marae anō tētahi wāhanga o tō rātou kōhanga reo me te kāinga.

Te reo Māori.Ko te reo Māori me ōna tikanga tētahi kaupapa matua mā te kōhanga reo. Kei ngā reanga ako rerekē ngā tamariki mō te hopu i te reo Māori. E āhei ana ētahi o ngā tamariki pakeke ki te whakahoki kōrero mā ngā pātai me te kaha whakaputa i ō rātou pepeha. Kua tautokotia ēra atu o ngā tamariki ki te whakapiki i ā rātou pūkenga ki te reo Māori. Ko ngā kemu tētahi rautaki hei akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te whai pānga me ngā tukanga whai whakaaro. He mea harikoa me te whaihua ngā akoranga reo Māori a ngā tamariki.

Ka kōrerotia te reo Māori e ngā kaimahi me ētahi atu o ngā pakeke i te rā katoa. E whai wāhi ana rātou ki te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero ki ngā tamariki me te akiaki i a rātou ki te whakahoki kōrero i te koanga ngākau. Kua whakaritea ngā akoranga hei tautoko i ngā mātua ki te whakapiki i ō rātou mātau ki te reo Māori kia whai wāhi ai rātou ki te ako ngātahi i te taha o ngā tamariki i te koanga ngākau. E wātea anō hoki ana ngā pakeke o te hapori ki te uru ki ēnei akoranga. E piki ana te mātau o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori i te koanga ngākau.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamāherehere me te aromātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo hei whakatau i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamāherehere, te whakahaeretanga me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga reo mā a rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka āta hāngai te aromātai nei ki te hōtaka whakamāherehere me te aroturuki i te tipuranga a ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Ngā whakahaere a te whānau.E tino hāpai ana te whānau i ō rātou haepapa ki te whakahaere i tā rātou kōhanga reo. Ka hui rātou i te wā i te wā me te arotahi ki ngā take kua rārangitia. Kua whakatakotoria ngā pūrongo e ngā kaiako, te kaiwhakahaere pūtea, te kaiwhakahaere me te rōpū kohikohi pūtea. E whai mōhiohio ngā mātua o te whānau, ā, e āhei ana rātou ki te whiriwhiri i ngā take me te whakatau ngātahi i ngā take. E whai haere ana ngā kaimahi i ngā whakangungu. E tino whai painga ana ngā tamariki i te mea e hiahiatia ai e te whānau te whakatū me te pupuri i ngā tukanga whakahaere whai take i te kōhanga reo.

Te whakamāherehere me te aromatawai.E rite ana ngā māhere, ā, e whakaatu ana ngā māhere mā te whānau me ngā kaimahi. Tēra anō ētahi kōrero hōhonu i roto i ngā māhere, ā, kua tāututia ngā wāhanga katoa o ngā māhere ki ngā whakaritenga hei whakahaere i te hōtaka. E whai wāhi ana ngā whai whakaaro o ngā māhere ki roto i ngā māhere, ā, e ngākau ana ngā kaimahi ki ngā whai whakaaro o tēna me tēna. Ko Tangaroa te tino kaupapa i ngā marama o te raumati me te ngahuru. E harikoa ana ngā tamariki me te whānau i ngā ngohe ō-waho, ā, e āhei ana rātou ki te torotoro i ngā wāhi whakahirahira me te ako i ngā kōrero e hāngai pū ana ki te takiwā.

Kua whakaritea pai ngā pukapuka paetae takitahi e pā ana ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Kei roto i ngā pukapuka paetae ngā pikitia, ngā whakaahua, ngā kōrero tae noa ki ngā tuhinga paetae e hāngai ana ki ngā whāinga ako. Me whai whakaaro ināianei ngā kaimahi ki te whakarerekē i ēnei whāinga kia hāngai tōtika ai ngā whāinga ki ngā hiahia me ngā ngohe motuhake o tēna tamaiti me tēna tamaiti.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te aromātai.Ka hui ōpaki ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhitiwhiti i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki a rātou hōtaka i te mutunga o ia kaupapa. Me whai whakaaro rātou ināianei ki te whakatakoto ā-tuhi i a rātou whakawhitiwhiti kōrero kia taea ai e ratou te whiriwhiri me te whakatau i ngā take hei whakapai ake i ngā hōtaka. Me whakatau rātou i ngā wā mā ngā hui a ngā kaimahi. Mā reira, ka pai ake ngā mōhiohio me ngā pūnaha whakahaere ki ngā hui ā whānau.

Te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā.Me tirotiro haere te whānau i a rātou kaupapa here me ngā whakaritenga o ia rā kia kite ai ae rānei, kāore rānei ngā tuhinga e whai mana ana. Ka āhei rātou ki te tuari i ēnei mahi me te whakatakoto i ēnei tuhinga ki ngā hui whānau i te wā i te wā hei whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero me te whakamana. Mā reira, ka pai ake te māramatanga a ngā mātua o te whānau ki ngā kōrero, te pūtake o ngā kaupapa me ngā tukanga whakahaere o ia rā.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

He whakamārama

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Piri o Waimirirangi tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga a te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Piri o Waimirirangi is situated at Te Kao a small community in the Northland region. The marae is integral to children’s learning and is important to the community. Children participate in marae events where they are able to observe kaumatua and kuia in their traditional roles. The presence of kaumatua in the kōhanga reo has a positive influence on children.

Te reo Māori me ōna tikanga is a priority for the kōhanga reo. Children are at varying stages in their te reo Māori development. Some of the older children are able to respond to questions and share their pepeha with confidence. Kaimahi engage in conversations with children in positive ways. Te reo Māori lessons are available to whānau members so they can enjoy learning alongside their children.

Several whānau members are fisherman. Fish is a specialty on the menu particularly when catering for visitors. Tangaroa features in kaupapa learning. Fishing grounds and safety practices are a priority for whānau members when the skills and knowledge of Tangaroa are discussed. Whānau realise their children will need to learn these skills and traditions to help them survive in their environment. Another source of sustenance comes from the land and how Rongomatane provides for the needs of whānau. The whānau have tyre gardens with vegetable plants. These gardens are part of children’s learning about Papatūānuku.

The whānau support their kōhanga reo well. They are active members in monitoring the work, management and operation of the kōhanga reo. Whānau discuss a range of items including their charter and policies, kaimahi training, fund raising and finance. They agree the cost of maintenance work at the kohanga reo impacts on finances because of its isolation. Whānau make collective decisions about how best to provide for their children.

The marae, church, whānau and te reo Māori underpin the collective aspirations of this community for their children.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

4 Findings

Whanaungatanga

Background

Te Kao is a small rural community, in the Northland region. The kōhanga reo is located next to the marae and is close to the local store, health centre, kura and Aupouri Iwi office. Whānau are active members within these facilities as well as the kōhanga reo. Children know this is their tūrangawaewae.

Areas of good performance

Whānau involvement.Whanaungatanga is a feature of the kōhanga reo. Whānau members support the kōhanga reo in effective ways. Regular kaumātua presence is enjoyed by the children. Young parents in the community are encouraged to participate in the kōhanga reo. Several parents have moved back to the area and are adjusting to a different lifestyle. Parent helpers have rostered days to assist kaimahi. Whānau are kept informed about kaupapa, outings and how children are progressing. Term goals for children’s learning are shared. Children understand the importance of whānau in the kōhanga reo and their daily lives.

Contact with the marae.The marae is integral to children’s learning. Children see how kaumātua and kuia participate in karanga and whaikōrero. They hear the language of the marae being spoken and shared. Children see how the whānau work together to care and support visitors. Children know how important their marae is to their community. They often play at the marae and know this is where they belong. Children are content and accept the marae as an extension of their kōhanga reo and home.

Te reo Māori.Te reo Māori me ōna tikanga is a priority for the kōhanga reo. Children are at varying stages in their te reo Māori development. Some of the older children are able to respond to questions and share their pepeha with confidence. Other children are well supported and encouraged with their language skills. Games are used to encourage children’s problem solving skills and thinking processes. Children find language learning to be fun and rewarding.

Kaimahi and other adults consistently use te reo Māori throughout the day. They engage in conversations with children and encourage them to respond in positive ways. Te reo lessons are available to whānau members so they can enjoy learning alongside their children. These lessons are available to other members of the community. Children’s te reo Māori is developing in positive ways.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management.The whānau take responsibility for the management of their kōhanga reo. They meet regularly with an agenda of items. There are regular reports from the kaiako, treasurer, kaiwhakahaere and fund raising group. Whānau members are kept informed and are able to discuss items and make decisions together. Kaimahi are enrolled in training programmes. Children benefit from the efforts of the whānau who actively seek to establish and maintain good management practices at kōhanga reo.

Planning and assessment. Planning is prepared and displayed for the whānau and kaimahi. There is good detail in plans and all the elements for focused delivery of the programme are identified. Whānau contributions to planning are valued. Tangaroa is a prominent kaupapa during the summer months and autumn. The children and whānau enjoy outdoor activities where they are able to visit special places and learn about stories that are unique to the area.

Individual profile books are a well prepared perspective of children’s learning. Profile books include pictures, photographs and comments and documented achievements against learning goals. Kaimahi should now consider individualising these goals based on children’s unique interests and activities. Children are progressing well.

Areas for development

Evaluation.Kaimahi meet informally and generally discuss their programmes at the end of each kaupapa. They must now consider documenting their discussions where they can make informed decisions for improvement. Specific times need to be set for kaimahi meetings to allow this to happen. Information for sharing at whānau hui will be more structured and purposeful as a consequence of these changes.

Charter, policies and procedures.The whānau need to check their policies and procedures to ensure they are up to date. They can share this task and present these documents regularly at whānau meetings for discussion and approval. Whānau members are more likely to gain a better understanding of the content and purpose of relevant policies and procedures by adopting this process.

To the Parents and Community of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Piri o Waimirirangi

These are the findings of the Education Review Office’s latest report on Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Piri o Waimirirangi.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Piri o Waimirirangi is situated at Te Kao a small community in the Northland region. The marae is integral to children’s learning and is important to the community. Children participate in marae events where they are able to observe kaumatua and kuia in their traditional roles. The presence of kaumatua in the kōhanga reo has a positive influence on children.

Te reo Māori me ōna tikanga is a priority for the kōhanga reo. Children are at varying stages in their te reo Māori development. Some of the older children are able to respond to questions and share their pepeha with confidence. Kaimahi engage in conversations with children in positive ways. Te reo Māori lessons are available to whānau members so they can enjoy learning alongside their children.

Several whānau members are fisherman. Fish is a specialty on the menu particularly when catering for visitors. Tangaroa features in kaupapa learning. Fishing grounds and safety practices are a priority for whānau members when the skills and knowledge of Tangaroa are discussed. Whānau realise their children will need to learn these skills and traditions to help them survive in their environment. Another source of sustenance comes from the land and how Rongomatane provides for the needs of whānau. The whānau have tyre gardens with vegetable plants. These gardens are part of children’s learning about Papatūānuku.

The whānau support their kōhanga reo well. They are active members in monitoring the work, management and operation of the kōhanga reo. Whānau discuss a range of items including their charter and policies, kaimahi training, fund raising and finance. They agree the cost of maintenance work at the kohanga reo impacts on finances because of its isolation. Whānau make collective decisions about how best to provide for their children.

The marae, church, whānau and te reo Māori underpin the collective aspirations of this community for their children.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
