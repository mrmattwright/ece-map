1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ko Tākitimu te waka

Ko Kahurānaki te maunga

Ko Waipawa te awa

Ko Tapairu te marae

Ko Ngāti Kahungunu te iwi

Ko Ngāti Marau te hapū

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Waipawa ki tētahi whare e pā tata atu ana i te awa o Waipawa. He hononga ā-whakapapa tō ngā tamariki katoa. He kaha te whanaungatanga ki waenga i ngā tamariki me te whānau i te kōhanga reo. Ka tino tautoko te whānau i te katoa o ngā whakahaeretanga me ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, tō rātou whakawhanaketanga anō hoki.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Whenua te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E tupu haere ana te maioha, te whakaute anō hoki o ngā tamariki mō te ao Māori, te ao tūturu, me te ao wairua, me te whai pānga anō hoki o ngā atua Māori. Tūhura haere ai ngā tamariki i tō rātou ake taiao, ā, i te taiao whānui hoki. Ka tūhura hoki ngā tamariki i ō rātou hononga ā-whānau, ā-hapū, ā-iwi hoki, mā ngā pepeha, ngā mōteatea, me ngā waiata, ā, mā tā rātou whai wāhi atu hoki ki ngā hui i te marae. He ākonga pakari, he ākonga pākiki hoki rātou, ā, e whanake haere ana tō rātou mōhio ki a rātou anō, ki te ao whānui tonu.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te reo me ngā tikanga Māori. He mātau, he pakari hoki ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki e whai hua ana ki te whakawhanake i te whakamahinga me ngā pūkenga o ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. Ka tino hāpaitia ngā tamariki ki te ako i te reo Māori i a rātou e noho ana ki te kōhanga reo, i ō rātou ake kāinga anō hoki.

Ka whakahaere te whānau i te maha o ngā tūmomo wāhanga o ngā whakahaeretanga i te kōhanga reo, ā, ka tino whai hua ā rātou mahi. He pakari ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakahaere pūtea, te whakamahere rautaki, te hauora me te haumaru, ngā take e pā ana ki ngā rawa, te whakahaere tari, te aromātai hōtaka, me te aromatawai. Ka whakatutukihia te arotake whaiaro i ia te wā, ā, ka āta whakaarohia tēnei mahi, ka hāngai pū anō hoki ki te tikanga matua o te kōhanga reo, me ngā whakaritenga papai o te mātauranga mō ngā tamariki kōhungahunga.

Ko te whakatakoto i ngā whāinga ako, me te pūrongo i ngā wheako akoranga, ētahi āhuatanga hei whakapai ake. He whānui rawa ngā whāinga ako, ā, kāhore anō kia tīmata ngā pūrongo ōkawa. Kāhore te pūnaha arohaehae e whai kiko ana, ā, kāhore ngā whakaritenga e tino hāngai ana ki ngā aratohu i te kaupapa here.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mokohuia o te Rautahi kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Whenua

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Whenua

He whakamārama

He pēhea te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki ngā ariā e pā ana ki te mana whenua?

He tino pai te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki i tō rātou mōhiotanga, tō rātou māramatanga hoki ki te mana whenua.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He tākare te tūhura haere o ngā tamariki i te ao Māori. Ka taki rātou i ngā karakia, ā, nā tēnei ka mauritau te āhua o te rā. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā hononga ki waenga i a Ranginui rāua ko Papatūānuku me ngā atua Māori. Ka kitea te whakaute o ngā tamariki ki te mana whenua.

He tino pakari te aronga toi whenuatanga, te tuakiri hoki o ngā tamariki ki a Ngāi Te Whatu-i-āpiti. Ka whakapakari tēnei i te whanaungatanga. Ka hono rātou ki ngā marae e toru o te hapori. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae anō hoki.

Ka noho whakaute ngā tamariki ki te taiao māoriori. Ka ako rātou i ngā otaota Māori me ngā tūmomo hua o aua otaota. Whakarite taonga ai rātou i ia te wā, ā, ka whakatakoto tohutaka hoki rātou hei whakamahinga i te kōhanga reo, hei hoatu rānei ki te whānau. Ka whakamihi, ka whakanui hoki te whānau i ngā painga o te mana whenua.

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te māramatanga ki te ao Māori. Ka whai wāhi atu rātou ki ngā whakanuitanga o Matariki, ā, ka taea e rātou ngā kāhui whetū o Matariki te tāutu. Ka whakarato te whānau i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te uru atu ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo kaupapa e pā ana ki a Matariki.

He tau te āhua o ngā tamariki, ā, he pakari tā rātou tūhura haere i tō rātou taiao. Haereere ai ngā tamariki i ia te wā ki te ngahere, te moana, te awa, me te marae. Kohikohi ai ngā tamariki i ngā taonga Māori i ia wā, ā, ka whakamahi i aua taonga i roto i ā rātou mahi toi. Ka kohi hoki rātou i te kai.

He pai ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā kaimahi, ngā tamariki, me te whānau. Ka kitea te manawanui, te atawhai, me te aroha o ngā kaimahi i a rātou e taunekeneke ana ki ngā tamariki, ki te whānau, ki waenga i a rātou anō hoki. Ka kitea aua uaratanga i ngā tamariki e taunekeneke ana ki waenganui i a rātou anō. Whakanui ai, whakamihi ai hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā whakapuakitanga me ngā mahi angitu a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā rautaki papai hei whakatau, hei whakahaere hoki i ngā tamariki. He kaha te waiora o ngā tamariki. He harikoa, he pakari, he tau hoki rātou ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te reo me ngā tikanga Māori, puta noa i te rā. He mātau, he pakari hoki ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whakatauira rātou i te reo e tika ana mō ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakamahi i te whānuitanga o ngā rautaki hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. He whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te taki i ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā waiata, me ngā mahi katoa. He māhorahora te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki tuākana i te reo Māori i aua wā. Ka noho mārama rātou ki ngā tohutohu hōhonu. Ka aro pai ngā tamariki tēina ki ngā kōrero tāruarua me ngā mahi ā-ringa, i a rātou e whakawhanake ana i ō rātou reo. He pai te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ka tino tautokona ngā tamariki kia whanake ai tā rātou kōrero i te reo Māori. Haere ai ngā kaimahi katoa me ētahi o ngā mema o te whānau i ia te w, ki ngā kura reo, me ngā hui o te purapura, i te rohe hoki, ki te hāpai ake i te ako i te reo Māori. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā kīanga, ngā waiata, ngā karakia, me ngā hīmene ki te whānau, hei ako. He tokomaha ngā mema o te whānau e pakari ana ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whai huatanga o te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakahaere me te whakarite i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo me ōna whakawhanaketanga mō meāke nei?

He pakari te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakahaere me te whakarite i te maha o ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, ā, he pai hoki tā rātou whakariterite mō meāke nei.

Ngāwāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka tino whai hua te whakahaere a te whānau me ngā kaimahi i te maha o ngā wāhanga o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakaritea e rātou ngā tino pūnaha pakari, ngā tino tukanga pakari hoki e pā ana ki te:

  • whakahaere pūtea
  • whakahaere rawa
  • te hauora me te haumaru
  • te whakahaere tari
  • te whakamahere rautaki
  • te aromatawai, me te
  • aromātai hōtaka.

He tino tau te māramatanga o te whānau ki te arotake whaiaro. Ka tāutu te whānau i ngā wāhanga matua hei arotake. Ko ētahi o aua wāhanga e hāngai ana ki te reo me ngā tikanga Māori, te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki, te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromātai hōtaka, te waiora o ngā tamariki, me te whakahaeretanga a te whānau. Ka whakaae te whānau, ka hāngai ā rātou whakaritenga ki te tikanga whānui o te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Kāhore ngā tukanga arohaehae o tēnei wā e whai kiko ana. E mōhio ana te whānau, me whakapakari rātou i te whakatinanatanga o ngā arohaehae me te mārama ake o ngā tukanga e hāngai ana ki te whakatakoto whāinga, te whakawhanake i ngā mahere e pā ana ki te whakawhanaketanga ngaio, me te pūrongo.

He ōpaki rawa te pūrongo ki te whānau mō te hōtaka akoranga. Pūrongo ā-waha ai te kaiako ki te whānau. Ka hāngai ake aua pūrongo ki ngā whakawhanaketanga a ngā kaimahi, kāhore e tino hāngai ana ki ngā wheako ako a ngā tamariki.

He whāiti te whakamahere mō ngā āheinga o ia tamaiti. Kāhore anō ngā kaimahi kia whakawhanake i ngā tino whāinga e pā ana ki ngā tūmomo reanga o ngā tamariki. I tēnei wā, he whānui rawa ngā whāinga ki te hōtaka mō ngā rōpū e whā o ngā tamariki.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakiia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o okiuh, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia tahuri te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te:

  • whakawhanake i ngā whāinga hōtaka e hāngai pū ana ki ngā tūmomo reanga, ngā tūmomo āheinga hoki o ngā tamariki
  • whakarato i ngā pūrongo ōkawa e pā ana ki te hōtaka me ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki
  • arotake i te whakatinanatanga o te pūnaha arohaehae.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

Ko Ngāti Marau te hapū

Te Kōhanga Reo o Waipawa is in a standalone building, a short distance from Waipawa. Children are connected to each other through whakapapa. There is a strong sense of whānaungatanga among children and whānau in the kōhanga reo. Whānau fully support all aspects of the operations and children’s learning and development.

The whānau chose Mana Whenua as the focus for the review. Children have a growing appreciation and respect for the natural, physical and spiritual worlds and the role of ngā atua Māori. Children regularly explore their immediate and wider environments. Children explore their links to whānau, hapū and iwi through pepehā, mōteatea, waiata and when they participate in marae events. Children are self-assured, curious learners who have a developing sense of who they are and the wider world.

Children are immersed in te reo and tikanga Māori. Kaimahi are competent, confident speakers of te reo Māori. They use a range of effective strategies to develop children’s te reo Māori use and skills. There is a growing commitment among whānau and kaimahi to learn and use te reo Māori. Children are well supported to learn te reo Māori while they are at the kōhanga reo and in their homes.

The whānau manage several parts of the operations of the kōhanga reo in a highly efficient way. There are robust processes for financial management, strategic planning, health and safety, property matters, office administration, programme evaluation and assessment. Self review is regular, well considered and focuses on the philosophy of kōhanga reo and good educational practice for young children.

Setting learning objectives and reporting about children’s learning experiences are areas for improvement. Learning objectives are too general and formal reporting is yet to occur. The appraisal system lacks detail and practice is not consistent with policy guidelines.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Whenua

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning; and
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Mana WhenuaBackground

How well do children understand concepts that relate to mana whenua?

Children are developing very well in their knowledge and understanding of mana whenua.

Areas of good performance

Children eagerly explore the Māori world. They recite karakia which sets a positive tone for the day. Children learn about the connections between Ranginui, Papatuanuku and ngā atua Māori. Children demonstrate respect for mana whenua.

Children have a strong sense of belonging and identity to Ngāi Te Whatuiāpititanga. This strengthens whanaungatanga. They all affiliate to the three local marae. Children know who they are and where they belong.

Children have respect for the natural environment. They learn about Māori plants and their uses. They regularly prepare gifts and make recipes for use in the kōhanga reo or for whānau. Whānau acknowledge and celebrate the benefits of mana whenua.

Children are developing an understanding of the Māori world. They are involved in annual Matariki celebrations and can identify the Matariki constellation. Whānau provide opportunities for children to participate in a wide range of activities about Matariki.

Children appear secure and explore their environment confidently. Children regularly go to the local bush, sea, river and marae. Children often gather natural resources which they use for their artwork. They also gather food.

Interactions between kaimahi, children and whānau are positive. Kaimahi display patience, kindness and affection when they interact with children, whānau and each other. These values are evident when children interact with each other. Kaimahi regularly praise and acknowledge children’s contributions and successes. They use positive strategies to settle and manage children. Children have a strong sense of wellbeing. They are happy, self assured and content at kōhanga reo.

Children are immersed in te reo and tikanga Māori throughout the day. Kaimahi are competent, confident speakers of te reo Māori. They model appropriate language for children and use a range of strategies to support children’s language development. Children have opportunities to lead karakia, hīmene, waiata and routines. The older children spontaneously use te reo Māori during these situations. They can comprehend complex instructions. The younger children respond well to repetition and actions as they develop language. Children are developing well as emerging speakers of te reo Māori.

Children are well supported to develop as te reo Māori speakers. All kaimahi and some whānau members regularly attend kura reo, purapura and regional meetings where te reo Māori learning is supported. Kaimahi provide whānau with phrases, waiata, karakia and hīmene to learn. Several whānau members are confident speakers of te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How effectively do the whānau and kaimahi manage and organise the Kōhanga Reo operations and its future development?

The whānau and kaimahi confidently manage and organise several aspects of the Kōhanga Reo operations and plan well for its future.

Areas of good performance

Whānau and kaimahi manage several parts of the kōhanga reo operations in a highly efficient way. They have established robust systems and processes for:

  • financial management
  • property management
  • health and safety
  • office administration
  • strategic planning
  • assessment and
  • programme evaluation.

Whānau have a sound understanding of self review. Whānau identify key areas for review. These included te reo and tikanga Māori, the children’s learning programme, programme planning, programme evaluation, children’s wellbeing and whānau management. Whānau affirm their practices are consistent with the philosophy of kōhanga reo.

Areas for development

Current appraisal processes lack detail. Whānau acknowledge they need to strengthen the implementation of appraisals with clearer procedures for goal setting, developing professional development plans and reporting.

Reporting to whānau about the learning programme is informal. The kaiako reports verbally to whānau. These reports tend to focus more on kaimahi developments rather than on children’s learning experiences.

Planning for children’s individual abilities is limited. Kaimahi are yet to develop specific objectives for the different ages and levels of children. Presently, the programme objectives are too generic for the four groups of children.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends that whānau and kaimahi:

  • develop specific programme objectives for the different ages and abilities of children
  • provide formal reports about the programme and children’s learning
  • review the implementation of the appraisal system.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
