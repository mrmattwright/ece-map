1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Pukerau ki ngā papa o te kura o Russell, ki Porirua ki te rāwhiti. Ka ahu mai te ingoa o te kōhanga reo i ngā puke kotahi rau e karapoti ana i te takiwā o te kōhanga reo. I tēnei wā, e whā ngā whakatupuranga o te whānau kotahi e whai wāhi atu ana ki te whānau me te rōpū kaimahi o te kōhanga reo. Ka hāpai, ka ārahi hoki ngā kuia i ngā kaimahi i ia rā.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i te whanaungatanga?

He hononga ā-whakapapa ki waenga i ngā tamariki, ā, ka kitea tō rātou māramatanga ki te whanaungatanga mā ngā huarahi maha.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka āta rongo ngā tamariki i te aronga toi whenuatanga. Ka rongo rātou i ngā taunekeneke papai, whakawhanaunga hoki ki ētahi atu. Ka āta whakarongo, ka āta tautoko, ka āta ārahi hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki, puta noa i te hōtaka. He pai te tākaro ngātahi a ngā tamariki, ā, he pakari tā rātou whakawhitiwhiti haere i te kōhanga reo. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i te whanaungatanga i roto i te āhua o tō rātou tautoko i a rātou anō. He tau, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki, ā, e tino atawhaitia ana rātou i te kōhanga reo.

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi wāhi e whakawhanake ana i te reo Māori. Kei raro te nuinga o ngā tamariki i te rua o ngā tau. He kaha ngā kaimahi ki te kōkiri me te whakauru i ngā tamariki ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero māmā. E mārama ana ngā tuākana ki te reo, ā, he tika tā rātou whakautu kōrero i ngā wā e ākina ana rātou. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā karakia, ngā waiata, ngā rotarota, me te rere tonu o te reo waiata hei hāpai i te ako reo. Ka kitea te pai o te aro atu a ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori.

Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te hōtaka akoranga e whakahihiri ana i a rātou. E āhei ana rātou ki te whiriwhiri i ngā taumahi e ngākau nuitia ana e rātou, ā, e whakahihiri ana hoki i a rātou. He mā te wāhi, he pai hoki te whakatakotoranga. Ka āta whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā kaupapa e ngākau nuitia ana hei waihanga i ngā hanganga matatini mā ngā paraka, me ngā toi auaha mā te parāoa pokepoke. Ka kōrerorero ngā kaimahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki i a rātou e mahi ana. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te mahi takitahi, ā-rōpū hoki i ngā taumahi. Ka tino ngākau nui ngā tēina ki ngā mahi kori tinana, tae atu ki te kanikani me ngā puoro. Ka tūhura ngā tamariki i tō rātou taiao, ā, ka whakamātau rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo kaupapa hou me ngā rauemi hou. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi ako e whakaongaongatia ana.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Ko te whakawhanaketanga i te reo Māori te kaupapa matua puta noa i te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā wawata ō te whānau kia ako rātou i te reo Māori ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki. Ka hāpaitia ake te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki inā e rumakina ana rātou ki tētahi taiao Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka whakatakotohia ki ngā mahere o Te Whāriki ngā ara ako o ngā tamariki. Ka āta whai kiko ngā mahere ā-tau, ā-wāhanga, ā-wiki, o ia rā hoki. Ka āta whakaatuhia aua mahere hei tirohanga mā te whānau me ngā kaimahi. Whai muri i te whakauru nui mai o ngā tamariki, kua whakahāngaitia ake ngā mahere hei whakatutuki ake i ō rātou matea. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te pakari o ngā mahi whakamahere.

E tino tautokona ana ngā mahi a te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Ka tautoko ngā kuia me ngā kaimahi i a rātou, i roto i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga. Kua piki ake te tatauranga tamariki ki te kōhanga reo i roto i te tau kua pahure ake nei. Ka ako ngā whānau hou i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, me ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki. He wawata ō rātou mō ā rātou tamariki, mō rātou anō hoki, kia ako ai rātou i te reo Māori. He mahere rautaki tā te whānau, ā, e hāngai ana tēnei ki Te Ara Tūāpae me ngā whakawhanaketanga i whakaritea ai hei whakapiki ake i te reo Māori me te āheinga o te whānau. Ka whakarato te tari ā-rohe o te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i te ārahitanga me ngā whakangungu mō te whakahaere i te tari, hei hāpai i te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ō rātou tūranga whakahaere. E whai hua ana ngā tamariki nā tētahi kōhanga reo e āta whakahaerehia ana.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E whanake tonu ana te aromatawai. Kua tīmata ngā kaimahi ki te whakaemi i ngā tūmomo rauemi hei whakauru ki ngā pūkete a ia tamaiti. Me hāngai ake ngā pūkete ki tēnā e mōhiotia ana e ngā tamariki, me tēnā e ākona ana e rātou. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te kite i ā rātou whakamātau e whakanuia ana, e whakatakotohia ana hoki ki ā rātou ake pūkete. Kāhore ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki e tino aroturukitia ana.

Ka aromātai ōpaki ngā kaimahi i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Kāhore rātou e tino whakawhiti kōrero ana, e tuhi ana hoki i ngā āhuatanga papai o te hōtaka, me ngā wāhanga hoki hei whakawhanake ake, pērā i ngā mahinga, ngā tūnga kaimahi, me te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki. Kāhore ngā tamariki e whiwhi ana i ngā hua ka pupū ake i ngā whakapaitanga ki te hōtaka akoranga.

E whanake tonu ana ngā āhuatanga o te arotake o roto. Kāhore te whānau e tino arotake ana ia wa i ā rātou whāinga rautaki. Kāhore a rātou mahere ā-tau tā rātou, hei whakatakoto i ngā mahi hei hāpai i tā rātou aroturuki i te ahu whakamua, puta noa i ngā wāhanga matua o ngā whakahaeretanga. 

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Pukerau ki te rāwhiti o Porirua. He hononga ā-whakapapa ki waenga i ngā tamariki katoa, ā, ka whakaatuhia e rātou te whanaungatanga mā ngā huarahi maha. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i te manaakitanga ki ā rātou taunekeneke, hei hāpai i te harikoa me te mauritau o ngā tamariki. He tākare ngā whānau hou ki te ako tonu i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, me te tautoko i ā rātou tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Pukerau i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pukerau is on the grounds of Russell School in Porirua East. Pukerau refers to one hundred hills that surround the area where the kōhanga reo is located. There are currently four generations from one family who are part of the whānau and kaimahi team. Kuia provide support and guidance to the kaimahi on a daily basis.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children demonstrate whanaungatanga?

Children are connected by whakapapa and demonstrate their understanding of whanaungatanga in a variety of ways.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children experience a strong sense of belonging. They experience positive and affirming interactions with others. Kaimahi take the time to listen, support and guide the children throughout the programme. Children play well together and move confidently about the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi model whanaungatanga in the way they support each other. Children are settled, happy and well cared for at kōhanga reo.

Children learn in an environment where te reo Māori is developing. The majority of children are under two years old. Kaimahi endeavour to initiate and engage children in basic conversations. The older children have a good understanding of the language and respond appropriately when prompted. Kaimahi use karakia, waiata, simple rhymes and background music to support language learning. Children show a positive response to te reo Māori.

Children enjoy a learning programme that is vibrant and exciting. They can choose activities that interest and excite them. The environment is clean and well organised. Children engage in areas of interest long enough to create complex structures with blocks, and creative moulding with dough. Kaimahi have informal conversations with children as they work. Children enjoy activities on their own and with others. Younger children particularly like physical activity including dancing and music. Children explore their environment and try new and different activities and resources. Children engage in stimulating learning activities.

Key Next Steps

Te reo Māori development is a key priority across the kōhanga reo. Whānau have aspirations to learn te reo Māori alongside their children. Children’s language development is better supported if they are immersed in a te reo Māori environment.

Planning and Evaluation

Te Whāriki planning provides learning pathways for children. Annual, term, weekly and daily plans are detailed. These are clearly displayed for whānau and kaimahi. Since the recent influx of children, some focussed planning has occurred particularly to better cater for their needs. Children benefit from sound planning.

Whānau are well supported to manage the kōhanga reo in the best interests of children. Kuia and kaimahi support them in all areas of operations. There has been an increase of children on the kōhanga reo roll throughout the year. New whānau learn about the kaupapa of kōhanga reo and their roles and responsibilities. They have aspirations for their tamariki and themselves as whānau to learn te reo Māori. The whānau have a strategic plan aligned to Te Ara Tūāpae with developments planned for Te Reo Māori and building whānau capability. The Kōhanga Reo National Trust district office provides administrative guidance and training for whānau to fulfil their management roles. Children benefit from a well-managed kōhanga reo.

Key Next Steps

Assessment is still developing. Kaimahi have begun to collate material for children’s individual profile books. The profiles need to reflect more on what children know and what they are learning. Children enjoy seeing their efforts valued and documented in their own books. Children’s learning is not well monitored.

Kaimahi informally evaluate the effectiveness of the learning programme. They do not regularly discuss and document positive aspects of the programme and areas for development including routines, staffing and children’s progress. Children do not benefit from improvements to the learning programme.

Aspects of internal review are still developing. Whānau do not regularly review their strategic goals. They do not have an annual plan with targeted actions to help them monitor progress across key areas of operations.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pukerau is in Porirua East. Children are connected by whakapapa and show whanaungatanga in a variety of ways. Kaimahi provide caring relationships to support children to be happy and settled. New whānau are enthusiastic to learn more about the kaupapa of kōhanga reo and to support their children at kōhanga reo.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Pukerau will be in three years.
