1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Tāwerawera ki te Kūao o Taupiri ki te marae o Taupiri, e pātata atu ana ki Rāhui Pōkeka. He tino hononga tō te kōhanga reo ki Te Kīngitanga. Whai muri i te arotake o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kua tū ētahi atu ki ngā tūranga o te kaiako me te kaiwhakahaere, me ngā tūnga mahi o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau. I tēnei wā e whakahaerehia ana te kōhanga reo e te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, hei ārahi i tā rātou tukanga whakahoutanga.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou harikoa hei ākonga?

Kei te harikoa ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou taunekeneke ki te taha o ētahi atu, ā, i ngā wā hoki e whai wāhi atu ana rātou ki ngā akoranga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki ki ngā hononga e whai pūtake ana. Ka waihanga ngā kaimahi i tētahi taiao e whakaahuru ana, e whakahei ana hoki. He ngāwari ō rātou reo kōrero, ā, he whakaute ō rātou hononga ki ngā tamariki. Kei te mōhio ngā tamariki ki ā rātou mahinga, me ngā tūmanako hoki mō rātou. Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tō rātou mauritau ki tētahi taiao e noho riterite ana, e noho mauritau ana hoki mō rātou. Ka atawhai ngā tamariki, tētahi i tētahi. Ka whakatinana ngā kaimahi i tētahi hōtaka akoranga kua āta tuituia. Ka harikoa ngā pēpi i a rātou e whai wāhi atu ana ki ngā taumahi o ngā rōpū nunui. Nā tēnei tūāhuatanga e puāwai mai ana ngā hononga ki waenga i te tuakana me te teina. He mauritau te āhua o ngā tamariki.

Ka whakamanahia ngā tamariki hei uri o Waikato. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te ārahi i ētahi wāhanga matua o te hōtaka, pērā i te Pai Mārire, ngā mōteatea, me te karanga mō te kai. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te waiata i ngā waiata o Waikato. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whai māramatanga ki ngā hītori o Te Kīngitanga. Ka taetae atu rātou ki ngā huihuinga whakahirahira o Waikato, pērā i ngā poukai, te K160, ngā rīketa, te Koroneihana, te waka ama, ngā Manu Kōrero, me ngā whakataetae kapa haka. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te māramatanga ki tō rātou tuakiri hei uri o Waikato.

E tautokona ana te ako a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. He matatau ngā kaiako ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, ā, ka whakatauira rātou i te reo me ōna tikanga. Ko te mita o Waikato te aronga matua. Ka whakamahi ngā kaiako i ngā waiata hei rautaki e hāpai ai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. Ko te tohutohu, te whāngai kupu, me te patapatai ētahi rautaki e kaha whakamahia ana e ngā kaimahi. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whakawhitiwhiti whakaaro ki ngā kaimahi. Ka mārama haere ngā pēpi ki te reo Māori mā te mātakitaki me te taunekeneke tētahi ki tētahi ki te taha o ngā kaimahi. E tīmata ana ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

He pakari ngā tamariki ki te tūhura haere i tō rātou taiao. Ka whakamahere ngā kaiako i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi kia whai wāhi mai ai ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga. E ākina ana te noho tahi o ngā pēpi ki ō rātou tuākana, ā, ka whakauru ngā tuākana i ngā pēpi ki ngā taumahi katoa. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā taumahi o ngā rōpū nui, ā, ka akiaki hoki i ngā mahi auaha me te tūhura haere i ngā taonga o waho. Ahakoa ngā wero nā te iti o te wāhi ako o roto, he pai te whakahaeretanga a ngā kaiako me te whānau i taua wāhi. He rahi te wāhi o waho kua taiapatia, ā, e whai hua ana hei wāhi tūhura mō ngā tamariki. E whakarite ana te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i ngā huarahi ki te whakatutuki i ngā āhuatanga o te wāhi ako e noho pakeke ana, hei hāpai ake anō i te ako a ngā tamariki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako mā te tākaro.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He tukanga tā ngā kaimahi mō te whakamahere i te hōtaka. He matawhānui ngā mahere, ā, he tino aronga mārama o roto mō te whakatinanatanga o te hōtaka. Ka āta whakamōhio i te whānau ki tēnā e ākona ana e ā rātou tamariki.

E whakapau kaha ana te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. He hou te heamana me te kaiwhakahaere ki ō rāua tūranga me ā rāua kawenga mahi. Kei Te Ara Tūāpae te tirohanga me te aronga mō te whānau. Kua whakahoungia te tūtohinga. I tēnei wā e whakatōpū ana te whānau i ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi me ngā kirimana mā ngā kaimahi. E mōhio ana te whānau, ka whai hua tonu ā rātou tamariki mā te āta whakarite i ngā pūnaha ki te whakahaere i te tari.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Me whakapakari i te arotake whaiaro. Ahakoa kua tāutuhia e te whānau ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake, kāhore anō tētahi paku mahi kia whakatutukihia. Hei āta whakarite i te whakapai tonutanga, puta noa i ngā whakahaeretanga katoa o te kōhanga reo, me kōkiri whakamua te whānau i ngā mahi katoa.

He maha ngā āwangawanga mō te āhua o te whare o te kōhanga reo. Kua kopū te tuanui o roto nā te taumaha o te wai e papī mai ana i ngā hikuhiku. Nā tēnei tūāhuatanga, kāhore ētahi wāhanga o te tuanui o roto i reira. Ko te waea hiko e tautau mai ana i te tuanui o roto tētahi āhuatanga mōrearea i tēnei wā tonu. Kua kopū ngā pātū, nā te roanga o ngā tau kua turuturu mai te ua ki te papa. Koia nei hoki te take he pāhiwihiwi hoki ngā papa. Tē taea ētahi o ngā kūaha te kati, nā te torehapehape o ngā pakitara. He makariri te wāhi wharepaku, kei te māturuturu hoki te wai, ā, kotahi anake te heketua e mahi ana mō ngā tamariki me ngā pākeke. E pā tōraro ana aua tūāhuatanga ki te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi.

Kāhore anō kia whakatakotohia te aromātai hōtaka. Kua tae ki te wā kia whai whakaaro ngā kaimahi ki ngā huarahi ki te aroturuki i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Kāhore e taea te whakatau te whakatutukitanga o ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki i te kounga o te hōtaka.

Kāhore i te whānau tētahi mahere rautaki. Kāhore anō kia tāutuhia ngā kaupapa matua o te pae tawhiti. Kāhore he mahere hei āta whakakoia i te rōnaki tonutanga o te kōhanga reo mō meāke nei. Kei te rangirua te angaanga mō ngā tamariki me te whānau.

Kāhore he mahere ā-tau. Hei whakatutuki i ngā tino whāinga matua me ngā wātaka matua e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, he mea nui kia whakatakotohia tētahi mahere hei ārahi i te whakatinanatanga o ngā mahi matua i te tari.

Kāhore anō kia oti te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e āta tautokona ana e te whānau ki te whakapā atu ki te whakawhanaketanga ngaio ka whakapai ake i ā rātou whakaritenga. Ka whakatairangatia ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki, hei te whakaratonga o te tautoko e tika ana.

Kāhore anō kia whakatutukihia te arowhai a ngā pirihimana i ngā kaimahi. He kaupapa tēnei hei tirohanga wawe, kia tutuki ngā herenga ā-ture i whakaraupapahia ai ki Te Ture Oranga Tamariki 2014. Kāhore te noho haumaru o ngā tamariki e whakatūturuhia ana.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

I te wā o te arotake, i tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture. Kia tutuki i te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā herenga ā-ture, me tahuri rātou ki te:

  • whakarite i te hauora, te haumaru, me te waiora o ngā tamariki, ā, kia whakatikaina, kia tiakina i ia te wā, kia noho mōrearea kore hoki ngā rawa me ngā taputapu
    [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS12-HS18 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • āta whakarite i te haere tonutanga o te tukanga arotake whaiaro, hei hāpai i te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakapūmau me te whakapai ake i te kounga o te mātauranga me te atawhai
    [R47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA6 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakatakoto i tētahi tukanga mō te aromātai i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga
    [R43 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; C1-4 me C7-10 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakawhanake i tētahi mahere ā-tau e ārahi ana i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo
    [R47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA8 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakarite i te whakatinanatanga i ia te wā i te arohaehae o ngā kaimahi katoa, ā, me whakatutuki anō hoki ngā tukanga kia arowhaingia ngā kaimahi me ngā kaikirimana e ngā pirihimana.
    [R47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA7 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Te Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kia mahi tonu te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te taha o te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te:

  • aro matua ki te whakatutuki i ngā take hauora me ngā take haumaru i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo
  • whakatinana i tētahi mahere mahi matawhānui hei hāpai i te whakahoutanga o te kōhanga reo.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Tāwerawera ki te Kūao o Taupirii roto i te kotahi tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Tāwerawera ki Te Kūao o Taupiri is located on Taupiri Marae, near Huntly. The kōhanga reo is closely associated to Te Kīngitanga. Since the previous ERO review, there have been changes in the positions of kaiako and kaiwhakahaere, as well as within whānau management. The kōhanga reo is currently under the management of Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) to guide them through a restructuring process.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children show they are happy learners?

Children are happy in their interactions with others and when involved in learning.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children engage in meaningful relationships. Kaimahi create an environment that is warm and welcoming. They are gently spoken and have a very respectful relationships with children. Children know their routines and what is expected of them. Children show they are settled in an environment that is consistent and predictable. Children are affectionate with each other. Kaimahi implement an integrated learning programme. Babies happily participate in large group activities. This provides opportunity for tuakana teina relationships. Children appear to be settled.

Children are affirmed as uri of Waikato. Kaimahi encourage children to lead significant parts of the programme such as Pai Mārire, mōteatea and karanga kai. Children enjoy singing waiata specific to Waikato. Kaimahi support children to understand the history of Te Kīngitanga. They attend significant events pertaining to Waikato such as Poukai, K160, regatta, Koroneihana, waka ama, Manu Kōrero, and kapa haka competitions. Children are developing an understanding of their identity as descendants of Waikato.

Children are supported to learn te reo Māori. The kaiako are fluent speakers, and model te reo me ōna tikanga. Waikato dialect is the priority. Kaiako use waiata as a strategy to support children’s language development. Instructions, prompts and questions are strategies that are used often by kaimahi. There are opportunities for children to engage in conversations with kaimahi. Babies gain an understanding of te reo Māori though observations and one to one interactions with kaimahi. Children are emergent speakers of te reo Māori.

Children confidently explore their environment. Kaiako plan a range of activities to engage children in learning. Babies are encouraged to sit alongside tuakana who include them in all activities. Kaimahi support children to participate in large group activities, and encourage creativity and exploration of physical resources. Despite the challenges of the small indoor learning environment, this is managed well by kaiako and whānau. A large outdoor fenced area gives children opportunities to explore. The TKRNT is planning ways to address challenges in the physical learning environment to further support children in learning. Children enjoy learning through play.

Planning and Evaluation

Kaimahi have a process in place for programme planning. Planning is comprehensive and provides clear direction for the implementation of the programme. The whānau is well informed of what their children are learning.

The whānau is managing the kōhanga reo to the best of their ability. The chairperson and kaiwhakahaere are new to their roles and responsibilities. Te Ara Tūāpae provides vision and direction for the whānau. The charter has been updated. The whānau is currently consolidating job descriptions and employment contracts for kaimahi. The whānau acknowledge that well established administration systems will benefit their children.

Key Next Steps

Self-review requires strengthening. While the whānau has identified areas for development, no actions have been carried out. In order to ensure improvements are ongoing across all operations of the kōhanga reo, the whānau should progress all actions.

There are numerous concerns about the condition of the kōhanga reo building. The ceiling is warped from the weight of water that seeps under the eaves. As a result, there are missing tiles in the ceiling. The exposed electric wiring hanging from the ceiling poses an immediate threat. The walls are warped from years of rain leaking on to the floor. As a result the floors are uneven. Some of the doors cannot shut because of the uneven nature of the walls. The toilet area is cold and leaking, and there is only one functional toilet for children and adults. The health and safety of children and kaimahi is compromised.

Programme evaluation is not in place. It is time for whānau to consider ways to monitor the effectiveness of the learning programme. Children cannot be assured that the quality of the programme will meet their learning needs.

The whānau do not have a strategic plan in place. Long term priorities have not been identified. There is no plan in place to assure the future sustainability of the kōhanga reo. The outlook for children and whānau is in doubt.

There is no annual plan in place. In order to meet critical goals and dates related to the operations of the kōhanga reo, it is important that a plan be put in place to guide the implementation of key administration tasks.

Appraisals of kaimahi have not been completed. Kaimahi are not well supported by the whānau to access professional development to improve their practice. The learning needs of children will be enhanced when the appropriate support is provided.

Kaimahi police vetting has not been carried out. In order to meet the requirements outlined in the Vulnerable Children’s Act 2014, this matter is urgent. Children’s safety cannot be assured.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

ERO identified areas of non-compliance. In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements, they need to:

  • ensure children’s health, safety and wellbeing, and kōhanga reo premises, facilities and equipment are kept in good repair, maintained regularly and are free from hazards
    [R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS12-HS18 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • ensure there is an ongoing process of self-review to help the kōhanga reo whānau maintain and improve the quality of its education and care
    [R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA6 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • establish a process for evaluating the effectiveness of the learning programme
    [R43 Education (Early Childhood Service) Regulations 2008; C1-4 and C7-10 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • develop an annual plan that guides the operations of the kōhanga reo
    R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA8 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • ensure the appraisal for all employed staff is regularly implemented, and procedures for police vetting of employees and contractors are carried out.
    [R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008, GMA7 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendations

ERO recommends Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust continue to work alongside the whānau and kaimahi to:

  • prioritise addressing the health and safety issues identified in this report

  • implement a comprehensive action plan to support the restructuring of the kōhanga reo.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Tāwerawera ki te Kūao o Taupiri will be within one year.
