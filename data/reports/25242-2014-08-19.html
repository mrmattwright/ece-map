1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E whakahaerehia ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Reo Rangatira ki Whaiora i tētahi whare i hangaia ai hei kōhanga reo, ki te taha o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Manawa Tapu o Te Kupu. Kei raro aua kōhanga reo e rua i te maru o te marae o Whaiora, ki Ōtara, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau ki te Tonga. Kua raihanatia te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki kua pakeke ake i te rua o ngā tau, ā, ka poipoi Te Manawa Tapu o Te Kupu i ngā tamariki kei raro i te rua o ngā tau.

Ka whakarato te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Reo Rangatira ki Whaiora i tētahi taiao e wāteatea ana, e noho ātaahua ana hoki mō ngā tamariki. Kua whakaritea ki te wāhi ako wāteatea te maha o ngā wāhi tākaro, wāhi ako hoki, me ngā rauemi, ngā taumahi hoki e whakawero ana, e whakahihiri ana i ngā tamariki. I whakaritea e te whānau me ngā kaimahi ko Mana Aotūroa te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

He tamariki auaha, ā, e mōhio ana hoki ki te ako takitahi. Ka haereere ki te marae o Whaiora hei whakawhānui ake i te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki ā rātou taonga tuku iho, ki te reo Māori, ā, ki ō rātou hononga anō hoki ki te ao Māori. Ka tino hāpaitia te puāwaitanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki.

He pai te mahi ngātahi a ngā kaiwhakahaere me te whānau ki te whakahaere i ō rātou kōhanga reo e rua, ā, e noho tahi ana ngā mahi whakahaere tari me ngā whakaritenga hoki. Ka noho mōhio mārika te whānau ki ngā āhuatanga hei whakapai ake, me ngā ekenga angitu hoki. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā hononga tautoko mā te noho mārama o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero.

E mōhio ana te whānau katoa, e tika ana kia tahuri rātou ki te whakapakari ake i ngā hononga ki ngā kura kaupapa Māori, hei hāpai ake i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga reo (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Aotūroa.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Aotūroa

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whakawhanaketanga o te māia me te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou tuakiri, ngā tuku ihotanga me tō rātou reo hoki?

He tino pai te whakawhanaketanga o te tuakiri, ngā tuku ihotanga me te reo o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake ko Mana Aotūroa. Ko ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki, te oranga me te whanaungatanga ngā tino wāhanga e whakamahi ana i roto i te hōtaka o ia rā. Hei tāpiri atu, ka whakarite te whānau kia whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te taiao tūturu, Māori hoki.

Te tūhura me te whakawhānui haere.E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga, te tūhura haere, me te hīraurau hopanga. He pakari ngā kaimahi ki te hāpai i te taha pākiki o ngā tamariki me ō rātou ngākau nuitanga. Kua whakaritea e rātou tētahi taiao ako e kitea ana i te maha o ngā rauemi mā ngā tamariki. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i ō rātou pūkenga e pā ana ki te taha auaha, te pāngarau, me te reo matatini, te nekeneke tinana, me te pūtaiao. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te tākaro hei rōpū nui, hei rōpū iti, hei tamaiti takitahi rānei, ā, ki te taha rānei o tētahi kaimahi. Ka haereere ki te marae o Whaiora hei whakawhānui ake i ngā mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki ā rātou taonga tuku iho, te reo Māori, me te whakahonohono ki te ao Māori. Nā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako e whakaratohia ana e ngā kaimahi, ka whanake ngā tamariki i te whānuitanga ake o te titiro ki tō rātou ao, ki tā rātou whai wāhi atu hoki.

Te hōtaka.He whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te tākaro mō te wā roa. Kua waihangatia e ngā kaimahi tētahi taiao kia hihiri ai ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā tino akoranga. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā tūmomo kēmu, ngā tūmomo rauemi anō hoki ki ngā tamariki, puta noa i te rā. Ka hāpai aua taumahi i ngā tamariki ki te tohatoha, te mahi ngātahi, me te manaaki hoki i ngā tamariki tēina. Ka rere pai ngā whakaritenga me ngā tikanga Māori, ā, ka whai hua anō ngā wā ako me he wā whakahoahoa mō ngā tamariki. He ākonga auaha ngā tamariki, ā, e mōhio ana hoki ki te ako takitahi.

E pou here ana te whanaungatanga i ngā taunekeneke katoa. I te nuinga o te wā he mauritau ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā pākeke, ā, he tino whakaute hoki. Koia nei tētahi tino tūāpapa mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Te reo Māori.E tino tautokona ana te puāwaitanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. I ngā wā katoa, ka whakatauira, ka whakapūmau hoki te kaiako i te reo Māori puta noa i te hōtaka o ia rā. E whakawhanake ana te mātau o ētahi tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. E āhei ana rātou ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori hei reo matua mō ā rātou whakawhitinga kōrero ki ētahi atu. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā āhuatanga katoa o te tikanga me te kawa, tae atu ki ngā pōhiri me ngā whakatau. He tākare hoki ngā mema o te whānau ki te ako i te reo Māori ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whai hua ki te whakatutuki i te ako a ngā tamariki me ngā wawata o te whānau, mā te āta whakahaeretanga a te whānau?

E tino whakatutukihia ana te ako a ngā tamariki, ngā wawata hoki o te whānau mā te pai o ngā tukanga me ngā hanganga anō hoki e pā ana ki te whakahaere.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau.He pai te mahi ngātahi a ngā kaiwhakahaere me te whānau ki te whakahaere i ngā kōhanga reo e rua. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā hononga e tautoko ana, mā te noho mārama tonu o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero. Ka hāngai pū ana ngā kaupapa here me ngā aratohu ki ngā whāinga me ngā tūmanako o Te Korowai. Kua tohaina ngā tūmomo kawenga mahi ki ngā mema o te whānau, tae atu ki te kohi pūtea, te arotake, te whakahou ake hoki o te tūtohinga me ngā kaupapa here, me te whakahaere i te whare me ngā rawa.

Te whakamahere me te aromātai.He matawhānui te kiko o ngā mahere a ngā kaimahi mō te tau, me ia marama, ia wiki anō hoki. Ka āta kitea ngā hononga o te mahere ki Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira me Te Whariki hoki. Ka aro nui ia kaupapa ako ki ngā tino ariā, ngā taumahi, me ngā tūmomo rauemi e whai pānga ana. E ākina ana te whānau kia whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro, ā rātou rauemi hoki mō ngā mahere. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti kōrero mō te whai huatanga o ngā kaupapa, me ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka whai wheako ngā tamariki i ngā hōtaka i āta whakamaheretia ai.

Te arotake whaiaro.Ka whai pūtake te arotake whaiaro, ā, ka whakatutukihia i ia te wā. Pūrongo ai te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. E tino mōhio ana rātou ki ēnā hei whakapai ake, me ā rātou whakatutukitanga. Ka arotahi te whānau ki te whakapai tonu i ngā wā katoa.

Te aromatawai.He pai te māramatanga o ngā kaimahi ki te aromatawai me tōna whakatinanatanga. Ka whakamahi rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki hei aroturuki, hei aromatawai hoki i te ahunga whakamua o ia tamaiti. Ko ētahi o aua āhuatanga ko ngā kōrero urupare a te whānau, ngā kitenga o ia rā, me ngā aromatawai o ia wiki. Kua waia ngā kaimahi ki ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā pūmanawa o ia tamaiti, me ngā taumata ako anō hoki ka whāia tonuhia. Ka tuhi haere ngā kaimahi i ngā ekenga angitu, ngā wheako hoki a ngā tamariki, ki ā rātou pūkete, ā, rātou puka hoki. E noho wātea ana ki te whānau aua mōhiohio hei kōrerotanga anō hoki mā rātou.

Te wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Te whakawhanaketanga i te mahere rautaki.Ahakoa he mahere rautaki, kei te mōhio hoki te whānau he āhuatanga tēnei hei whakawhanake ake. He whānui rawa ētahi whāinga, ā, kāhore he tino kiko kia āhei ai te whānau ki te whakariterite ake.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahiTauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013,me tētahiRārangi Tātari Whaiaromā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • ra, te waiora me te whai orangate hauo
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Reo Rangatira ki Whaiora operates out of a purpose built building alongside Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Manawa Tapu o Te Kupu. These kōhanga reo are part of the Whaiora Marae complex which is in Ōtara, South Auckland. This kōhanga reo is licensed for children over two years old and Te Manawa Tapu o Te Kupu caters for children under two years old.

The whānau and kaimahi of Te Reo Rangatira ki Whaiora provide an environment that is spacious and inviting for children. The open plan learning area has several designated play and learning areas with resources and activities that provide challenge and interest for children. The whānau and kaimahi chose mana aotūroa as the focus for this review.

Children are creative, independent learners. Visits to Whaiora Marae are used to extend children’s knowledge of their cultural heritage, te reo Māori and their links to te ao Māori. Children are well supported as developing speakers of te reo Māori.

The kaiwhakahaere and whānau work well together to manage their two kōhanga reo which are jointly administered and organised. Whānau are highly aware of what needs to be improved and their successesKaimahi and whānau encourage supportive relationships through clear and open communication.

The collective whānau acknowledge that they need to look at relationships with local kura kaupapa Māori in order to further support the development of te reo Māori.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Aotūroa.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Mana Aotūroa

Background

How well are children developing confidence and knowledge in their identity and language?

Children are developing very well in their identity and language through a range of opportunities to explore the local physical and natural environments.

Areas of good performance

Exploration and extension. Children are able to follow their interests, explore and problem solve. Kaimahi confidently support children’s natural curiosities and interests. They have created a well resourced learning environment for children. There are opportunities for children to develop their creativity, numeracy and literacy skills, motor development and science skills. Children can play in large or small groups, independently or accompanied by kaimahi. Visits to Whaiora Marae are used to extend children’s knowledge of their cultural heritage, te reo Māori and make their links to te ao Māori. The wide range of experiences made available by kaimahi enable children to develop a broader view of their world and their place in it.

Programme. Children have opportunities to play for extended periods. Kaimahi have created an environment that invites children to participate in meaningful learning. Kaimahi provide children with various games and resources throughout the day. These activities help children to take turns, work cooperatively together and to care for the younger children. Routines and tikanga Māori flow smoothly and are positive learning and social times for children. Children are creative, independent learners.

Whanaungatanga underpins all interactions. Interactions between children and with adults are generally positive and genuinely respectful. This provides an important foundation for children’s learning.

Te reo Māori. Children are well supported as developing speakers of te reo Māori. The kaiako consistently model and reinforce te reo Māori throughout the daily programme. Some children are developing fluency in te reo Māori. They are able to use te reo Māori as their main form of communication with each other. Children participate in all aspects of tikanga and kawa including pōhiri and whakatau. Whānau members are keen to learn te reo Māori alongside their children.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How well are children’s learning and whānau aspirations being met through whānau management?

Children’s learning and whānau aspirations are being very well met through effective management processes and structures.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management.The kaiwhakahaere and whānau work well together to manage the two kōhanga reo. Kaimahi and whānau encourage supportive relationships through clear and open communication. Policies and guidelines reflect the goals and objectives ofTe Korowai.Whānau have allocated specific areas of responsibilities including fundraising, charter and policy reviews and updates and property management.

Planningand evaluation. Kaimahi have comprehensive and detailed plans for the year and each month and week. Planning shows clear links to Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira and Te Whāriki. Each learning topic highlights major themes, activities and the supporting resources that are required. Whānau are encouraged to contribute their ideas and resources to planning. Kaimahi meet regularly to discuss the effectiveness of kaupapa and different aspects of the daily programme. Children experience well planned programmes.

Self review. Self review is purposeful and regular. Whānau and kaimahi regularly report on different parts of the kōhanga reo operations. They are highly aware of what needs to be improved and their successes. Whānau are focussed on continual improvement.

Assessment. Kaimahi have a good understanding of assessment and what this looks like in practice. They use a range of strategies to monitor and assess each child’s progress. Some of these include whānau feedback, daily observations and weekly assessments. Kaimahi are familiar with each child’s interests, strengths and next learning steps. Kaimahi record children’s successes and experiences in their portfolio folders and notebooks. This information is readily available to whānau for comment.

Areas for development

Strategic plan development. While there is a strategic plan in place, whānau acknowledge it requires further development. Some goals are too broad and lack the detail necessary to enable the whānau to plan further.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO LicensedKōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends that the whānau of Te Reo Rangatira ki Whaiora and Manawa Tapu o Te Kupu review and update their strategic plan.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
