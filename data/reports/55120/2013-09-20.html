1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

‘Ko Puketawai te maunga, ko Mangapeka te waiū, ko Ngāti Kuranui te hapū, ko Te Aitanga-a-Hauiti te iwi, ko Te Rangipureora te tipuna, ko Puketawai te marae, ko E Tipu te kōhanga reo.’

I tonoa e te whānau o te kōhanga reo, kia hāngai te arotahi mō tēnei arotake mātauranga ki Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Koia nei pātai aromātai i whakaritea ai e te whānau, hei ārahi i te arotake, arā:

‘Pēhea te whai huatanga o ā tātou mahi hei whānau, ki te whakawhanake i ā tātou tamariki ki te tū māia, te tū pakari hoki hei uri o Te Aitanga-a-Hauiti?’

I tāutuhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga:

  • e whakawhanake ana te pakari, te āheinga hoki o ngā tamariki i roto i te reo me ngā tikanga o Te Aitanga-a-Hauiti
  • e whakaatu ana ngā tamariki i te manawa whakahī mō ō rātou hononga ā-whakapapa ki ō rātou whānau, tō rātou kōhanga reo, me te marae, te hapū, me te iwi
  • e mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki te mihi ki te whenua, ki ngā tīpuna, ki ngā atua Māori, ki ngā marae, ngā hapū, ngā iwi, ngā awa, ngā maunga, ngā whānau, me te kōhanga reo
  • he pakari ngā tamariki ki te waiata me te taki i ngā waiata me ngā karakia o Te Aitanga-a-Hauiti, i roto i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo kaupapa, horopaki, huihuinga hoki
  • e mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmanako me ngā uaratanga o te whanaungatanga, me te whakahirahiratanga anō hoki o te whakapapa; ā,
  • he pakari ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā kaupapa o te marae, o te hapū, o te iwi hoki.

He hōhonu, he whānui hoki ngā mōhiotanga o ngā kaiako ki ngā hapū me ngā marae o Te Aitanga-a-Hauiti. He kaumātua rātou nō tēnei iwi. Nā tēnei, ka tino whai hua te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki, me ā rātou akoranga. Engari, e mōhio ana te whānau, he āhuatanga anō hoki o tā rātou hōtaka, o ā rātou whakaritenga hei whakawhanake ake, kia nui ai ngā wheako akoranga a ngā tamariki mā ō rātou ringaringa, mā te tūhura, mā te whakatewhatewha hoki. Ka hāpai tēnei i te whānau ki te arotahi ki te whakawhanake i ngā tamariki ki te tū hei uri māia, hei uri pakari anō hoki o Te Aitanga-a-Hauiti.

Ko te whai wāhi mātātoa o ngā mema o te whānau ki tō rātou marae, ka whai pānga ki te whakapūmau i te tino aronga ki ngā uaratanga o Te Aitanga-a-Hauiti ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o E Tipu kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o E Tipu.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

I whakaritea te whānau o te kōhanga reo ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira tā rātou kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake mātauranga. I waihangatia e te whānau – mā te tautoko hoki o ngā māngai o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo – tā rātou ake pātai aromātai hei ārahi i te arotake a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

‘Pēhea te whai huatanga o ā tātou mahi hei whānau, ki te whakawhanake i ā tātou tamariki ki te tū māia, te tū pakari hoki hei uri o Te Aitanga-a-Hauiti?’

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te noho rumaki, te tūturu hoki o ngā horopaki. Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga me te tūturutanga o ngā horopaki ka whakapūmau i tō rātou tuakiri, ō rātou hononga anō hoki ki tō rātou taiao me ngā taonga tuku iho. Koia rā te taiao e whakaratohia ana e te marae o Puketawai mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Hīkoi haere ai rātou i ia te wā hei whakapūmau i ngā pou whenua me ngā taonga tuku iho o te rohe nei e noho whakahirahira ana ki tō rātou whānau, tō rātou hapū, tō rātou iwi hoki. Ka whakamahia, ka whakatinanahia hoki te reo Māori, ngā tikanga, me ngā kawa hoki o Te Aitanga-a-Hauiti nā tēnei horopaki. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ō rātou tohu pou whenua hei uri o Te Aitanga-a-Hauiti.

Te reo Māori me ngā tikanga o Te Aitanga-a-Hauiti. Ka ngākau nui ngā kaiako ki te whakatairanga i te reo, ngā tikanga, me ngā mātauranga o Te Aitanga-a-Hauiti. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ngā waiata, ngā karakia, me ngā hītori hoki o tēnei takiwā. He mātātoa tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki tō rātou hapū, ki ngā huihuinga me ngā kaupapa i te marae. Whakapūmautia ai ngā kīwaha me ngā whakataukī o tēnei wāhi e ngā kaiako, i ngā wā ako. Ka noho tuwhera ngā tamariki ki te reo, ā, he mōhiotanga anō hoki tō rātou ki ngā taonga tuku iho o tō rātou iwi.

Te whanaungatanga. E pou herea ana te whanaungatanga ki te kōhanga reo e ngā hononga o te whānau ki tō rātou marae, tō rātou hapū, tō rātou iwi hoki. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki ngā marae o tēnei takiwā. Ka arahina ngā tamariki e tō rātou whānau, ki te whakawhanake i tō rātou mōhiotanga, tō rātou māramatanga hoki ki te whanaungatanga, mā te whakapapa, ngā taunekeneke, ngā uaratanga, me te whai wāhi atu ki te marae.

Ngā tātai whakapapa. Ka kitea te kaha mārama, te tū pakari hoki o ngā tamariki i roto i ō rātou mōhiotanga ki ō rātou hononga ā-whānau, ā-hapū, ā-iwi hoki. Ka whai hua te hōtaka o ia rā ki te whakanui i te whakapapa. Ka mārama, ka whakapuaki hoki ngā tamariki i ngā mihi ki te whenua, ki ngā atua, ki ngā tīpuna, ki te whānau, me ngā marae. Ko ngā waiata me ngā pepeha o te wāhi nei e whakapūmau ana ki ngā tamariki ō rātou tātai whakapapa, ō rātou hītori, tō rātou reo, me te aronga toi whenuatanga anō hoki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki ngā kaiako mō ngā atua Māori. Ka kitea te manawa whakahī, te pakari hoki o ngā tamariki i a rātou e whakapuaki ana i ō rātou whakapapa.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te āta whakarato hei āta whakawhanake. E mōhio ana ngā kaiako me te whānau, e tika ana kia paku rerekē i ētahi o ngā taumahi o ia rā, kia pupū ake ai ki ngā tamariki te wheako ako mā ngā ringaringa, mā te tūhura, mā te whakatewhatewha hoki. Ka whai hua tēnei whakarerekētanga ki te hāpai i te whakawhanake o ngā tamariki hei uri māia, hei uri pakari hoki o tō rātou marae, tō rātou hapū, tō rātou iwi hoki.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai ki ngā kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki, mō te whānau hoki. Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka, me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakahaeretanga, te aronga hoki o te kōhanga reo. Ka ngākau nui ngātahi ngā mema o te whānau kia whai hua ai ā rātou mahi hei kaiwhakahaere o te kōhanga reo. Nō nā tata tonu nei i arotake ai i ngā kaupapa matua, ā, kua whakapakari tēnei i tā rātou aronga ki te whakamahere mō te tauatanga a te whānau me te arotake whaiaro. Kua whakauru ētahi o ngā mema ki ngā kaupapa whakangungu a Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo. Ko te arotake me te pūrongo i ia te wā e whakarato ana i te pai o ngā mōhiohio mā te whānau, mō ngā whakawhanaketanga o te kōhanga reo, ngā whakatutukitanga, me te āhua o ngā mahi whānui. Ko ngā mema o te whānau, tae atu hoki ki ngā kaiako, ngā tino kaiārahi matua o roto i Te Aitanga-a-Hauiti. Ka whakahaere te whānau i te kōhanga reo mā ngā huarahi e tino whakaatu ana, e tino whakatairanga ana hoki i ngā mātāpono, ngā tūmanako rautaki, ngā uaratanga, me ngā taonga tuku iho o tō rātou marae, tō rātou hapū, tō rātou iwi hoki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te whakamahere, te aromātai, me te aromatawai. Kāhore anō ngā mahere hōtaka, ngā pūrongo kaimahi, me ngā aromatawai e tino tohu ana i te whānuitanga o ngā taumahi ka whakamaheretia ai hei hāpai tōtika i ngā tamariki, kia tū māia, kia tū pakari hoki rātou i roto i tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki Te Aitanga-a-Hauiti.

E ai ki ngā kaiako, ko tā rātou kaupapa i tēnei wā, e aro ana ki ngā tūmomo marae ki tō rātou iwi. He rangahau e pā ana ki ia marae. Engari, hei whakatairanga i ngā wheako akoranga a ngā tamariki, me whakamahere mō te ako mā ngā ringaringa, mā te taha auaha, me te taha tinana, mā te tūhura hoki, ā, me whai wāhi hoki aua āhuatanga ki te kaupapa o te hōtaka e whiwhia ana e ngā tamariki.

Kāhore anō ngā kaiako kia pūrongo mō te whai huatanga o te hōtaka ki te whakatutuki i ā rātou whāinga, me te āhua o ngā mahi ki te whakahaere, te whakatutuki hoki i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo matea ako o ngā tamariki.

Me whai hoki ngā tauira mahi ki ngā pūkete, i ngā kōrerorero e hono ana ki ngā putanga ako, ā, me whakamārama i ngā kaupapa e whakaakona ana, me te āhua o ngā mahi i ākona ai e ngā tamariki.

Me whakamahi hoki i Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira hei ārahi i te whakamahere, te aromātai, me te aromatawai.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o E Tipu, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

‘Ko Puketawai te maunga, ko Mangapeka te waiu, ko Ngāti Kuranui te hapū, ko Te Aitanga ā Hauiti te iwi, ko Te Rangipureora te tipuna, ko Puketawai te marae, ko E Tipu te kōhanga reo.’

The kōhanga reo whānau requested Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as their focus for this Education Review. This is the evaluative question the whānau determined to guide the review.

‘How effectively do we as whānau develop our children as confident and capable members of Te Aitanga ā Hauiti?’

ERO identified that:

  • children are developing confidence and capability in te reo me ona tīkanga o Te Aitanga ā Hauiti
  • children show a strong pride in their whakapapa links to their whānau, kōhanga reo, marae, hapū and iwi
  • children know mihi that acknowledges whenua, tīpuna, ngā atua Māori, marae, hapū, iwi, awa, maunga, whānau and kōhanga reo
  • children confidently sing and recite Te Aitanga ā Hauiti waiata and karakia for different purposes, situations and settings
  • children know the expectations and values of whanaungatanga and the importance of whakapapa and
  • children are confident participants in marae, hapū and iwi events.

Kaiako hold extensive knowledge of the hapū and marae of Te Aitanga ā Hauiti. They are descendents and kaumatua of the iwi. Children greatly benefit in their development and learning from this. However, whānau now recognise that aspects of their programme and practice could be developed to provide more hands on, exploratory and investigative learning experiences for children. This would support the whānau focus on developing children to be confident and capable members of Te Aitanga a Hauiti.

The active involvement and participation of whānau members on their marae helps to preserve a strong focus on Te Aitanga ā Hauiti values in the management of the kōhanga reo.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o E Tipu was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Te Kōhanga Reo o E Tipu.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning; and
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

The kōhanga whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as their focus for this Education Review. The whānau, with the support of Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) representatives, designed their own evaluative question to guide ERO’s review of Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

‘How effectively do we as whānau develop our children as confident and capable participants of Te Aitanga ā Hauiti?’

Areas of good performance

Immersion and authentic contexts. Children are immersed in rich and authentic settings that reinforce their identity, connectedness to their surrounds and ancestral heritage. Puketawai Marae provides such an environment for children’s learning. Regular hīkoi are used to reinforce to children, their ancestral landmarks and natural resources of significance to their whānau, hapū and iwi. Te reo Māori, tīkanga and kawa of Te Aitanga ā Hauiti are used and practised in this context. Children know about their cultural landmarks as descendants of Te Aitanga ā Hauiti.

Te Reo Māori me ngā tīkanga o Te Aitanga ā Hauiti. Kaiako are dedicated to promoting Te Aitanga ā Hauiti language, customs and knowledge. Children know local waiata, karakia and history of the area. They actively participate with their hapū during marae events and activities. Local kīwaha and whakatauki are regularly reinforced by kaiako during learning. Children are receptive to the language and knowledgeable about the traditions of their iwi.

Whānaungatanga. Whanaungatanga at the kōhanga reo is underpinned by whānau connections to their marae, hapū, and iwi. Whānau members are actively involved with the marae in the district. Children are guided by their whānau to develop their awareness and understanding about whanaungatanga through whakapapa, interactions values and marae involvement.

Belonging through whakapapa. Children show a strong understanding and confidence about their whānau, hapū and iwi connections. The daily programme effectively emphasises whakapapa. Children understand and perform mihi to the whenua, ngā atua, tīpuna, whānau and marae. Local waiata and pepeha reinforce to children their ancestral links, history, language and belonging. Children enjoy conversations with kaiako, about ngā atua Māori. Children show an inner pride and confidence when they are able to share their whakapapa.

Area for development

Full provision for maximum development. Kaiako and whānau recognise the need to modify some daily activities to make them more hands on, exploratory and investigative learning experiences for children. This modification should effectively support children’s development as confident and capable members of their marae, hapū and iwi.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Kōhanga reo management and direction. There is a shared commitment by members of the whānau to be effective managers of the kōhanga reo. A recent review of key priorities has strengthened their focus on whānau succession planning and self review. Some members have been enrolled on TKRNT training courses. Review and regular reporting provides good information for whānau about kōhanga reo developments, achievements and overall performance. Members of the whānau, including kaiako, are also key leaders within Te Aitanga ā Hauiti. Whānau manage the kōhanga reo in ways that successfully reflect and uphold their marae, hapū and iwi principles, strategic expectations, values and traditions.

Areas for development

Planning, evaluating and assessment. Programme plans, kaimahi reports and assessment are yet to show a range of planned activities that directly support children to become confident and capable participants of Te Aitanga ā Hauiti.

Kaiako report that their current kaupapa focus is on the various marae within the iwi. There is a study for each marae. However, to enhance children’s learning experiences, hands on, creative, physical and exploratory activities should be planned for and included in the kaupapa programme that children receive.

Kaiako are yet to report on how effectively the programme meets their objectives, and how children’s diverse needs for learning are being managed and met.

Work samples in portfolios should include annotations that link to the learning outcomes and state what, and how well children are learning.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira should also be used to guide planning, evaluation and assessment.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o E Tipu completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

To the Parents and Community of Te Kōhanga Reo o E Tipu

These are the findings of the Education Review Office’s latest report on Te Kōhanga Reo o E Tipu.

‘Ko Puketawai te maunga, ko Mangapeka te waiu, ko Ngāti Kuranui te hapū, ko Te Aitanga ā Hauiti te iwi, ko Te Rangipureora te tipuna, ko Puketawai te marae, ko E Tipu te kōhanga reo.’

The kōhanga reo whānau requested Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as their focus for this Education Review. This is the evaluative question the whānau determined to guide the review.

‘How effectively do we as whānau develop our children as confident and capable members of Te Aitanga ā Hauiti?’

ERO identified that:

  • children are developing confidence and capability in te reo me ona tīkanga o Te Aitanga ā Hauiti
  • children show a strong pride in their whakapapa links to their whānau, kōhanga reo, marae, hapū and iwi
  • children know mihi that acknowledges whenua, tīpuna, ngā atua Māori, marae, hapū, iwi, awa, maunga, whānau and kōhanga reo
  • children confidently sing and recite Te Aitanga ā Hauiti waiata and karakia for different purposes, situations and settings
  • children know the expectations and values of whanaungatanga and the importance of whakapapa and
  • children are confident participants in marae, hapū and iwi events.

Kaiako hold extensive knowledge of the hapū and marae of Te Aitanga ā Hauiti. They are descendents and kaumatua of the iwi. Children greatly benefit in their development and learning from this. However, whānau now recognise that aspects of their programme and practice could be developed to provide more hands on, exploratory and investigative learning experiences for children. This would support the whānau focus on developing children to be confident and capable members of Te Aitanga a Hauiti.

The active involvement and participation of whānau members on their marae helps to preserve a strong focus on Te Aitanga ā Hauiti values in the management of the kōhanga reo.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

General Information About Reviews

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
