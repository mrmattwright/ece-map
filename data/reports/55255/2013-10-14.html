2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Waihīrere ki te marae o Tākitimu, ki Wairoa.  I tēnei wā e whakatika ana te whānau, te purapura, me te kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe i te raihana, kia hāngai ai tēnei ki ngā rerekētanga hou i whakatutukihia ai ki te kōhanga reo.  He whakataunga ōkawa ki waenga i ngā kōhanga reo o Te Waihīrere me Māramahātea.  I nāianei, ko Te Kōhanga Reo o Māramahātea mō ngā tuākana e pakeke ake ana i te toru o ngā tau, ā, ka poipoi a Te Waihīrere i ngā tēina kei raro i te toru o ngā tau.  Arā anō ētahi atu āhuatanga papai o tēnei hononga.  Ka whakamahi rātou i te wēne hei kawe i ngā rōpū tamariki e rua ki ō rātou kōhanga reo.  Ka hui ngātahi hoki ngā whānau o ngā kōhanga reo e rua i ia rua marama.  Kei ia kōhanga reo hoki ō rātou ake hui.  Ka mahi te kuia i roto i ngā kōhanga reo e rua. 

I whakaritea e te whānau me ngā kaimahi ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.  Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga o tētahi taiao reo Māori.  Ka rongo, ka kite hoki rātou i te whakamahinga o te reo Māori ki ngā horopaki tūturu me ngā wheako reo hoki o ia rā, ki te kōhanga reo me te marae.  Ka kawe ngā tamariki i ētahi o ngā tikanga, pērā i ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā mihi, me ngā waiata.  He tokomaha ngā tamariki e āhei ana ki te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero ki ō rātou hoa, ki ngā kuia, ki ngā kaimahi hoki.  He mauritau, he whakaute hoki te hononga o ngā kuia me ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki.  He mīharo te whai wāhitanga a ngā kuia ki te whakamana i te mana āhua ake, te whakahirahiratanga hoki o ngā tamariki.  Nā tēnei, he pakari, he tau, he hāneanea hoki ngā tamariki. 

He ākonga pākiki ngā tamariki, ā, he hihiri tā rātou whai atu i ngā nekehanga o te ao whānui.  Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā tūmomo kaupapa e hāngai nui ana ki te ao tukupū, te ahuwhenua, ngā atua Māori, te ngahere, me te moana.  Haereere ai ngā tamariki i ia te wā ki ngā wāhi whakahirahira e whai pānga ana ki ngā kaupapa ako.  He pakari rātou ki te kawe i ngā mahi hei tangata whenua.  Haere ai ngā tamariki ki ngā huihuinga i te marae i ia te wā.  E āhei ana rātou ki te mātakitaki i ngā kuia me ngā koroua e kawe ana i ngā tikanga.  He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga, me te tūhura haere.  E āhei ana rātou ki te mahi auaha, te ako i ngā ariā e whakatīmata ana i te pāngarau me te reo matatini, me te whakawhanake hoki i ō rātou pūkenga ā-tinana.  Ka wheako ngā tamariki i te angitu i a rātou e tākaro ana, e ako ana hoki mā tētahi hōtaka i āta waihangatia ai. 

He pai te whakahaeretanga o te whānau i te kōhanga reo.  He mārama ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakamahere rautaki, ngā āhuatanga o te arotake whaiaro, te whakatutuki i ngā tukanga, te hauora me te haumaru, ngā arohaehae me te whakawhanaketanga ngaio hoki o ngā kaimahi.  I nāianei, me tahuri te whānau ki te hāpai i ngā kaimahi i roto i ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te pūrongo i te hōtaka, me te whakatutuki haere hoki i ngā taumata akoranga ka whāia tonuhia e ia tamaiti. 

Ka tino tautoko te kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe me te purapura i tēnei kōhanga reo, i ētahi atu hoki i te takiwā.  Koia nei tētahi tino āhuatanga o te rohe nei. 

Whakaritenga Whaimuri 

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki.  Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Waihīrere kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau.  I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Waihīrere.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. 

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga.  Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira 

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e te whānau me ngā kaimahi ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.  I tāutuhia ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te marautanga me te horopaki e hāpai ana i te ako a ngā tamariki Māori, i tā rātou wheako hoki i te angitu. 

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ngā tauira reo.  Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga o tētahi taiao reo Māori.  Rongo ai ngā tamariki i ia te wā i ngā tāngata e matatau ana ki te reo Māori hei reo taketake.  Nā te whakapuakitanga o te kuia i te reo Māori, ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ōkawa me te reo Māori o ia rā.  Ka whakamahi te kuia me ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo aronga ki te whakawhanake i ngā pūkenga reo Māori o ngā tamariki.  Ko te manawa reka, te katakata, te whakanui, me te haere tonutanga o ngā pātai me ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, ētahi tauira o ngā tūmomo rautaki e whakamahia ana e ngā kaimahi me te kuia hei āta whakatairanga ake i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. 

Te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo.  E whakawhanake ana te pakari, te mātau hoki o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.  Ka ārahi rātou i ngā karakia, ngā karanga, ngā hīmene, me ngā waiata.  Kua waia ngā tamariki ki ngā mahinga.  E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whai i te matatini o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero me ngā tohutohu.  E āhei ana ētahi o ngā tamariki ki te ū ki te whakawhiti kōrero me ō rātou hoa, ngā kaimahi hoki mā te reo Māori.  

Te waiora o ngā tamariki.  He tino kaha te waiora ā-wairua, ā-whatumanawa hoki o ngā tamariki.  Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā waiata, me ngā nekehanga ā-tinana hei whakatau, hei whakaahuru hoki i ngā tamariki.  Ka tino whai pānga nui te kuia ki te whakakoia i te mana āhua ake, te whakahirahiratanga hoki o ngā tamariki.  Ka akiaki rātou i ngā tamariki ki te āwhina, ā, ka whakanui rātou i ngā whakamātau ā ētahi atu.  Nā tēnei, he pakari, he tau, he hāneanea hoki ngā tamariki.  Ka tino rongo i te kotahitanga me te whanaungatanga ki waenga i ngā tamariki, ngā kaimahi, me te kuia. 

Ngā mahi hei tangata whenua.  He pakari ngā tamariki hei tangata whenua.  He whai wāhitanga mā rātou ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā huihuinga ōkawa ki te marae.  Mātakitaki ai ngā tamariki i ia te wā, i ngā kuia me ngā koroua e tū ana hei kaikōrero, hei kaiwaiata, hei kaikaranga hoki.  Ka whakapūmau, ka whakatinana hoki ngā kaimahi i aua tikanga ki te kōhanga reo.  Kua waia ngā tamariki ki ngā ariā e hāngai ana ki te pōwhiri me te manaaki manuhiri.  He maha ngā wā ka ārahi rātou i ngā manuhiri i roto i ēnei wheako ōkawa. 

Te tuakiri, te aronga toi whenuatanga hoki o ngā tamariki.  He tino pakari te tuakiri me te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki.  Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te taki i ō rātou pepeha, me ō rātou whakapapa.  Ka whakamana tēnei i ngā whānau, ngā hapū, me ngā iwi o ngā tamariki, me ngā pouwhenua whakahirahira.  Ko ngā waiata, ngā haerenga, ngā pakiwaitara, me ngā haerenga ki te marae me ngā wāhi whakahirahira e whakapūmau ana i te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki.  He manawa whakahī tō ngā tamariki i a rātou e kōrero ana mō ō rātou hononga ā-whakapapa. 

Te ao māoriori, te ao kikokiko hoki.  He ākonga pākiki ngā tamariki, ā, he hihiri tā rātou whai atu i ngā nekehanga o te ao whānui.  Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā tūmomo kaupapa e hāngai nui ana ki te ao tukupū, te ahuwhenua, ngā whetū, ngā atua Māori, te ngahere, me te moana.  Haereere ai ngā tamariki i ia te wā ki ngā wāhi whakahirahira e whai pānga ana ki ngā kaupapa ako.  He pārekareka ki a rātou te taunekeneke ki ngā tāngata hou me te ako i ngā whakaaro hou.  Kua āta whakawhanakehia te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui. 

Te kōkiri i ā rātou ake akoranga.  He ākonga auaha ngā tamariki e kōkiri ana i ā rātou ake akoranga.  He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whakatau i ā rātou ake akoranga, te tākaro mō ngā wā roa, me te tūhura haere.  Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā taumahi ki ngā tamariki hei hāpai i tā rātou whakapuaki i ngā akoranga e pā ana ki te tīmatanga o ngā ariā pāngarau, reo matatini hoki, ā, e whakawhanake ana hoki i ō rātou pūkenga ā-tinana, pūkenga auaha hoki.  He pai te rere o ngā mahinga, ā, he tino wā ako, wā whakahoahoa anō hoki ēnei mō ngā tamariki.  Ka wheako ngā tamariki i te angitu i a rātou e tākaro ana, e ako ana hoki mā tētahi hōtaka i āta waihangatia ai. 

Ngā taunekeneke.  He whakaute, he manaaki hoki kei ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā kaimahi, ngā kuia, me ngā tamariki.  Ka whakaaro nui ngā kaimahi me ngā kuia ki ngā tamariki, ā, ka whai hua tā rātou hāpai, i ngā tamariki e tākaro ana, e ako ana hoki.  Ka ārahi, ka āwhina hoki te whānau i ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, mā ngā huarahi e whai pūtake ana, e whai kiko ana hoki.  Ka tino rongo i te whanaungatanga ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi. 

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai ki ngā kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki me te whānau.  Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka, me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki. 

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whanaketanga hou.  I tēnei wā, e whakatika ana te whānau, te purapura, me te kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe i te raihana o te kōhanga reo, kia hāngai ai tēnei ki ngā rerekētanga hou i whakatutukihia ai ki te kōhanga reo.  He whakataunga ōkawa ki waenga i ngā kōhanga reo o Te Waihīrere me Māramahātea.  I nāianei, ko Te Kōhanga Reo o Māramahātea mō ngā tuākana e pakeke ake ana i te toru o ngā tau, ā, ka poipoi a Te Waihīrere i ngā tēina kei raro i te toru o ngā tau. 

Anei ētahi atu āhuatanga papai o tēnei hononga: 

  • Ka hui tahi hoki ngā whānau o ngā kōhanga reo e rua i ia rua marama.  Kei ia kōhanga reo hoki ō rātou ake hui. 
  • Ka mahi te kuia i roto i ngā kōhanga reo e rua. 
  • Ka kawe, ka whakahoki anō te wēne i ngā tamariki ki ngā kōhanga reo e rua. 

Te whakamahere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro.  He tino aronga mārama tā te whānau mō te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei.  Kei te mahere rautaki tētahi tirohanga ā-purapura, me ngā hononga ki Te Ara Tūāpae.  Nō nā tata tonu nei i pā mai ai ngā rerekētanga ki ngā tūnga kaimahi i te kōhanga reo.  Ka kitea ēnei ki te mahere ā-tau.  Ko ētahi atu kaupapa matua mō te whakapiki ake i te tatauranga tamariki, te whai wāhitanga a te whānau, te whakawhanaketanga ngaio, ngā kaimahi, te reo Māori, me Te Whāriki.  Kua whakatutukihia e te whānau te maha o aua kaupapa matua. 

Pūrongo ai te whānau i ia te wā i ngā take e pā ana ki te whakahaere pūtea, te whakamahere rautaki, ngā herenga ā-ture, me te hauora, te haumaru hoki.  Kua whakatutukihia te arotake o ngā kaupapa here.  I nāianei, ka hāngai aua kaupapa here ki ngā tikanga raihana a te Tāhuhu o te Mātauranga. 

Te whakamahere hōtaka.  Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i tētahi tino angamahi ki te whakamahere i ngā kaupapa mā ngā tamariki.  He matawhānui ngā mahere, ā, ka whai hua ki te ārahi i ngā kaimahi i a rātou e whakapuaki ana i ngā akoranga ā-kaupapa ki ngā tamariki.  Ka wheako ngā tamariki i te angitu i a rātou e tūhura ana i te ao Māori, me te ao whānui. 

Te aromātai hōtaka.  He matawhānui te aromātai hōtaka, ā, ka whai pānga anō hoki ki ngā whakapaitanga mā ngā tamariki.  Ka whakaaro huritao te kaimahi.  Hui ai ia i ia wiki, ki te kuia i Te Kōhanga Reo o Māramahātea.  He matawhānui ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ka whakatutukihia mō te whai huatanga o ngā tūmomo kaupapa, te whakawhanaketanga reo Māori o ngā tamariki, ngā rauemi, ngā take hauora, haumaru hoki, me ngā matea anō hoki o ngā tūmomo rōpū reanga.  Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā hōtaka e hāngai ana ki ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, ā, e poipoi ana hoki i ēnei. 

Te aromatawai.  Ka whakamahi te kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo whakaritenga hei aroturuki, hei aromatawai hoki i te ahunga whakamua o ia tamaiti.  Kei ia tamaiti tētahi pūkete e mau ana i:

  • ngā mahi kaupapa me ngā kōrerorero
  • ngā whakaahua me ngā tuhinga kōrero
  • ngā rārangi arowhai o Te Whāriki
  • ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā whakaaro o ngā tamariki. 

E kite haere ana i te puāwaitanga o ngā ngākau nuitanga, ngā pūmanawa hoki o ngā tamariki. 

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te pūrongo ki te whānau.  Kāhore te kaimahi e whakarato ana i ia te wā ngā pūrongo ki te whānau mō ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o te hōtaka o ia rā mō ngā tamariki.  He iti noa ngā mōhiotanga o te whānau ki te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki, ngā putanga mā ngā tamariki i roto i ngā akoranga ā-kaupapa, ngā haerenga, me te whakaratonga o ngā rauemi.  He āhuatanga nui te pūrongo kia noho mōhio ai te whānau ki tēnā e akohia ana, e mahia ana hoki e ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. 

Ngā taumata akoranga ka whāia tonuhia e ngā tamariki.  Me whakamahere te kaimahi mō ngā taumata akoranga ka whāia tonuhia e ia tamaiti.  He āhuatanga nui tēnei ka āta hāpai i ngā tamariki ki te kōkiri whakamua i roto i ā rātou akoranga, ō rātou whakawhanaketanga hoki. 

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga 

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Waihīrere, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro.  I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi; me
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

6 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kia tautoko tonu te kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe me te purapura i te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te:

6.1 pūrongo i ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o te hōtaka o ia rā o ngā tamariki

6.2 whakamahere mō ngā taumata akoranga ka whāia tonuhia e ia tamaiti. 

7 Whakaritenga Whaimuri 

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki.  Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Waihirere is at Takitimu Marae, Wairoa. Whānau, the purapura and the district kaupapa kaimahi are in the process of amending the licence to reflect the new changes that have occurred at the kōhanga reo. A formal arrangement has been reached between Te Waihirere and Maramahatea Te Kōhanga Reo. Te Kōhanga Reo o Maramahatea now acts as the tuakana and Te Waihirere, the teina. The tuakana now caters for children over three years old while the teina provides for children under three. There are other positive features to this relationship. These include the use of the van to transport the two groups of children to their kōhanga reo. The whānau from the two kōhanga reo have shared meetings on a bi-monthly basis. Whānau of the respective kōhanga reo also have their own individual meetings. The kuia work in both kōhanga reo.

The whānau and kaimahi chose ngā taumata whakahirahira as the focus area for the review. Children are immersed in a rich te reo Māori environment. They are exposed to traditional forms of te reo Māori and everyday language experiences at the kōhanga reo and on the marae. Children take lead roles during karakia, karanga, hīmene, mihi and waiata. Several children are able to maintain ongoing conversations with their peers, kuia and the kaimahi. The kuia and kaimahi relate in warm and respectful ways with children. The kuia play a special role in affirming children’s uniqueness and importance. Consequently, children are self assured, secure and relaxed.

Children are inquisitive learners and are intrigued about what happens in the wider world. The kaimahi use different kaupapa to highlight concepts about the universe, cultivation, ngā atua Māori, the bush and sea. Children regularly go to places of interest associated with the learning kaupapa. They are confident in their roles as tangata whenua. Children frequently attend events at the marae. They are able to observe kuia and koroua in their traditional roles. There are opportunities for children to follow their interests and explore. Children are able to create, learn early numeracy and literacy concepts and develop their physical skills. Children experience success as they play and learn in a well designed programme.

Whānau manage the kōhanga reo well. There are clear processes for strategic planning, aspects of self review, meeting procedures, health and safety, appraisals and kaimahi professional development. Whānau now need to support the kaimahi with processes for reporting on the programme and catering for each child’s next steps for learning.

The district kaupapa kaimahi and purapura provide valuable support to this kōhanga reo and others in the district. This is a strong feature of this region.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children.  Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Waihīrere was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.  This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children atTe Kōhanga Reo o Te Waihīrere.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Nga Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education.  For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below. 

4 Findings

Nga Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

Whānau and kaimahi chose ngā taumata whakahirahira as the focus for this review. Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira has been defined as the curriculum and context in which Māori children learn and experience success.

Areas of good performance

Language models. Children are immersed in a rich te reo Māori environment. The children hear fluent and native speakers of te reo Māori on a regular basis. The delivery of te reo Māori by kuia exposes children to traditional forms of te reo Māori and everyday language. The kuia and kaimahi use a range of approaches to develop children’s te reo Māori skills. Humour, laughter, praise and ongoing questioning and conversations are some examples kaimahi and kuia use to successfully promote children’s language development.

Children’s language use. Children are developing as confident, competent speakers of te reo Māori. They take lead roles during karakia, karanga, hīmene and waiata. Children are familiar with routines. Children are able to follow complex conversations and instructions. Some children are able to maintain conversations with their peers and kaimahi in te reo Māori.

Children’s wellbeing. Children have a strong sense of spiritual and emotional wellbeing. The kaimahi uses karakia, hīmene, waiata and physical gestures to calm and reassure children. The kuia play a special role in affirming children’s uniqueness and importance. They encourage children to help and praise each other’s efforts. Consequently, children are self-assured, secure and relaxed. There is a strong sense of unity and whanaungatanga among children, kaimahi and the kuia.

Tangata whenua roles. Children are confident in their roles as tangata whenua. They have opportunities to participate in formal events at the marae. Children regularly observe kuia and koroua in their traditional roles as orators, kaiwaiata and kaikaranga. Kaimahi reinforce and practise these protocols at the kōhanga reo. Children are familiar with concepts associated with pōwhiri and hospitality. They often guide visitors in their roles during these formal experiences.

Children’s identity and sense of belonging. Children have a strong sense of identity and belonging. Kaimahi encourage children to recite their pepeha and whakapapa. These recitations affirm children’s whānau, hapū and iwi connections along with landmarks of significance. Waiata, outings, stories and visits to the marae and places of interest reinforce children’s sense of belonging. Children stand proudly when they speak about their whakapapa connections.

The natural and physical worlds. Children are inquisitive learners and are intrigued about what happens in the wider world. The kaimahi uses different kaupapa to highlight concepts about the universe, cultivation, constellations, ngā atua Māori, the bush and sea. Children regularly go to places of interest associated with kaupapa. They enjoy interacting with new people and learning new ideas. Children have a well developed awareness of te ao Māori and the wider world.

Independent learners. Children are creative, independent learners. Children have opportunities to make decisions about their own learning, play for extended periods and explore. The kaimahi provides children with activities enabling them to create, learn early numeracy and literacy concepts and develop their physical and creative skills. Routines flow smoothly and are positive learning and social times for children. Children experience success as they play and learn in a well designed programme.

Interactions. Interactions and relationships between kaimahi, the kuia and children are respectful and caring. The kaimahi and kuia are attentive to children and effectively support them as they play and learn. Whānau regularly guide and assist children and kaimahi throughout the daily programme. This assistance allows kaimahi to work with children in a deliberate and purposeful way. There is a strong sense of whanaungatanga among children and kaimahi.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau.  Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

New development. Whānau, the purapura and the district kaupapa kaimahi are in the process of amending the kōhanga licence to reflect the new changes that have occurred at the kōhanga reo. A formal arrangement has been reached between Te Waihirere and Maramahatea Te Kōhanga Reo. Te Kōhanga Reo o Maramahatea now acts as the tuakana and Te Waihirere, the teina. The tuakana caters for children over three years old while the teina provides for children under three.

Other features include:

  • Whānau from the two kōhanga reo meet on a bi-monthly basis. They also have their own separate meetings.
  • The kuia works in both kōhanga reo.
  • The van picks up and drops off children from the two kōhanga reo.

Strategic planning and self review. Whānau have a clear sense of direction for the future of the kōhanga reo. The strategic plan includes a purapura perspective and links to Te Ara Tūāpae. The kōhanga reo has recently undergone staffing changes. These are evident in the annual plan. Other priorities include improving roll numbers, whānau participation, professional development, staffing, te reo Māori and Te Whāriki. Whānau have addressed several of these priorities.

Whānau regularly report on financial management, strategic planning, compliance issues and health and safety matters. Policies have undergone review. These policies now reflect the Ministry of Education’s licensing requirements.

Programme planning. The kaimahi use a sound framework when planning kaupapa for children. Plans are comprehensive and effectively guide the kaimahi to deliver kaupapa learning to children. Children experience success when they explore te ao Māori and the wider world.

Programme evaluation. Programme evaluation is thorough and leads to improvements for children. The kaimahi is a reflective thinker. She meets on a weekly basis with the kuia from Maramahatea Kōhanga Reo. Detailed discussions are held about the effectiveness of different kaupapa, children’s te reo Māori development, resources, health and safety matters and the needs of the different age groups. Children experience programmes that are appropriate and cater for their interests.

Assessment. The kaimahi uses a variety of practices to monitor and assess each child’s progress. Each child has a portfolio folder which contains:

  • kaupapa work with annotations
  • photographs with comments
  • Te Whāriki checklists
  • notes about children’s thinking.

Children’s emerging interests and strengths are becoming more evident.

Areas for development

Reporting to whānau. The kaimahi does not provide regular reports to whānau about different aspects of the children’s daily programme. Whānau have limited knowledge of children’s te reo Māori development, outcomes for children in kaupapa learning, outings and resource provision. Reporting is an important step in keeping whānau aware of what children learn and do at kōhanga reo.

Children’s next learning steps. The kaimahi needs to plan for each child’s next learning steps. This is an important stage that will help children advance in their learning and development.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Waihīrere completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist.  In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

6 Recommendations

ERO recommends the district kaupapa kaimahi and purapura continue to support whānau and the kaimahi to:

6.1 report on different aspects of the children’s daily programme
6.2  plan for each child’s next learning steps.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children.  Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews.  The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation.  This area is most likely to support long term improvement.  Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based on Te Whāriki, thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues.  The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement.  A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue.  There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
