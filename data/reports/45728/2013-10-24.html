2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kātahi anō Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Taura Here i te Ao ka whakatūngia, hei ratonga mō ngā hapori o Ngā Ruahinerangi rāua ko Ngāti Ruanui. Nō tērā tau rātou i whakatūwhera ai i tō rātou whare, ā, kua tata eke kē rātou ki te taumata o tā rātou tatauranga tamariki. I whakapau kaha te hapori nei ki te whakatū i tētahi kōhanga reo hei ea i te hiahia nui o ngā whānau. Ko te āhua o te hanganga o ngā whare me te papa i waihangatia ai kia whai pānga hoki ki a Taranaki. E anga atu ana te tomokanga o te kōhanga reo ki te maunga. Ko ngā tauira māheahea ki te pakitara e tohu ana i a Taranaki Maunga, ā, ka hāngai ngā tauira o te papa ki te awa. Ko ngā rama i te hōro e tohu ana i a Matariki. E toru noa iho ngā kōhanga reo hei poipoi i ngā uri o te iwi o Ngā Ruahinerangi.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Tangata te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake mātauranga. Nā rātou te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i āwhina ki te waihanga i te pātai aromātai i ārahi ai i te aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

“Ka pēhea te whai hua o tātou hei whānau, ki te whakawhanake i ā tātou tamariki kia tū pakari, kia tū mōhio hoki ki te ao Māori, ā, hei whakarite i a rātou mō te ao whānui?”

E hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki mā te whānuitanga o ngā rautaki, ki te whakawhanake i te tū pakari, te tū mōhio hoki ki te ao Māori. Ka ako rātou i a rātou anō, me tō rātou whai wāhitanga anō hoki ki te ao whānui. He whai wāhitanga mā rātou ki te haereere, ā, nā tēnei, ka taunekeneke, ka tūhura, ka tūhono atu hoki ki te whenua o te hapori. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te mōhiotanga ki ō rātou hononga hei tangata whenua, ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae. Ka toro atu ngā tamariki ki tō rātou mōhiotanga ki te reo Māori. E tīmata ana rātou ki te whakamahi i tēnā e mōhiotia ana e rātou, ki te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero ki ētahi atu i a rātou e tūhura ana i tō rātou taiao.

Ki tā ngā kaimahi pūrongo, e whakamahine tonu ana rātou i ngā hōtaka akoranga ka whakawhānui i ngā wheako akoranga mā ngā tamariki. Ka arotahi rātou ki ngā huarahi ka whakapai i ngā hōtaka me ngā whakaritenga ka hāpai i ngā tamariki ki te tū pakari me te tū mōhio ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui.

E whakapūmau ana, e arotahi ana hoki te whānau ki te āta whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahia ngā pūkenga me ngā mātanga o te whānau hei whakatairanga i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo me te tari. He pai te tae ā-tinana atu, te whai wāhi atu hoki o te whānau ki ngā hui o ia marama. Ka tino whakamōhiotia te whānau mō te āhua o ngā mahi i te kōhanga reo mā ngā tamariki. Ko te whakapakari i ngā mōhiotanga ki te reo Māori me te māramatanga ki ā rātou taonga tuku iho, tētahi aronga rautaki mō te whānau i ngā wā katoa. Ka whakahaerehia te kōhanga reo e te whānau mā te arotahi ki te whakapai tonu, te tupu tonu o te whānau hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Taura Here i te Ao.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Tangata.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Tangata

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e te whānau o te kōhanga reo ko Mana Tangata tā rātou kaupapa arotahi mō te arotake. I hāngai nui rātou ki te tū pakari, te harikoa hoki o ngā tamariki. E whakapono ana ngā mema katoa, ka noho matua te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori ki a rātou, ki ā rātou tamariki hoki. Ka whai pānga nui te tuakiri, ngā taonga tuku iho, te whakapapa, me te whanaungatanga ki te ako. I whai wāhi te nuinga o ngā mema o te kōhanga reo ki te waihanga i te pātai aromātai i arahina ai te aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

“Ka pēhea te whai hua o tātou hei whānau, ki te whakawhanake i ā tātou tamariki kia tū pakari, kia tū mōhio hoki ki te ao Māori, ā, hei whakarite i a rātou mō te ao whānui?”

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te reo me ngā tikanga Māori.E tīmata ana ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori i a rātou e whakawhiti kōrero ana, e tūhura haere ana, e whakapuaki kōrero ana hoki i ngā wā e ako ngātahi ana rātou. Ko ngā waiata o ia rā e whakapūmau ana i te whakawhanaketanga o tō rātou mōhiotanga, i tō rātou tū pakari anō hoki, nā runga i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki ō rātou tuakiri me ā rātou taonga tuku iho. Ka whakanuia ngā tikanga Māori puta noa i te rā. Ka noho mārama ngā tamariki ki ngā tino tikanga e hāngai ana ki ngā karakia. Ka whakapūmau ēnei i ngā tikanga tuku iho a te whānau. Ko ngā haerenga me te whānuitanga hoki o ngā wheako akoranga o roto, o waho tonu, e hāpai ana i ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te reo mā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki.

Te ao Māori.Ka hāpaitia te ako o ngā tamariki i te ao Māori, nā te tūhura, te whakahura, me te ako ngātahi ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki. He maha ngā horopaki nei ka whai pānga ki ngā hononga o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou whenua, ki a Ngā Ruahinerangi, ā, ki Taranaki whānui hoki. Ka āta whakamahia te wāhi o waho, puta noa i te rā. Kua whakaritea ngā māra kai. Ka whakamahia te mahau o waho e ngā kaimahi, mō ngā taumahi akoranga e whai pānga ana ki te tūhura, me ngā kaupapa e ngākau nuitia ana. He wāteatea te nuinga o te wāhi o roto, ā, e whakatairanga ana tēnei i te ako ngātahi, me te kōkiri a te tamaiti i āna ake mahi tūhura. Ka whakaratohia hoki ngā haerenga, kia āhei ai ngā tamariki ki te taunekeneke me te tūhura i te whānuitanga o ō rātou taiao māori, tūturu hoki.

Te whanaungatanga.He pakari ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu ki tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka whakapūmautia tō rātou whai wāhitanga, tō rātou hononga anō hoki ki te kōhanga reo e ō rātou hoa, e te whānau hoki i ia rā, mā ngā pepeha me ngā mihimihi. Ko te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi ā-rōpū, ā-tamaiti hoki, ka whai hua ki te poipoi i ngā taunekeneke whakaute ki waenga i ngā tamariki. E mārama ana ngā tamariki, he mīharo ngā mema katoa o tō rātou whānau, ā, ka whakamanatia hoki.

Te whaioranga.He pai te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki i ngā uaratanga pai o te whakaute me te manaaki i a rātou anō, i tō rātou taiao, i ētahi atu hoki. Ka whai hua ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakapūmau i ngā pūnaha katoa o te hauora, te haumaru, te akuaku, me te atawhai, mō te waiora o ngā tamariki. Ka whakanuia te whānau mō tō rātou tautoko i ngā mahinga o ia rā. Ko ētahi o aua mahinga, ko te whakareri i ngā kai, ko te mātakitaki i ngā tamariki, ko te āwhina hoki i te akuaku me te whakamoe. I ngā wā katoa, ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i ngā uaratanga o te whakaute me te manaaki i a koe anō, i ētahi atu hoki, puta noa i te rā. He hou te nuinga o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. He harikoa rātou, ā, e tau haere ana ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Te noho pūmau o te whānau.E tino tautokona ngā tamariki e tō rātou whānau ki te whakawhanake hei kaiārahi pakari, mātau hoki ki te ao Māori, ki te ao whānui tonu. Kua whakapau kaha te whānau ki te whakatū i te kōhanga reo. He tirohanga ngātahi, he wawata ngātahi hoki ō rātou, tae atu hoki ki te whakapakari ake i te āheinga o te whānau ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Kua tata te eke ki te rahi o te tatauranga tamariki, ā, he kaha te ngākau nui o te whānau me tā rātou whai wāhitanga. Ko te noho ngātahi o ō rātou mātanga, ō rātou mōhiotanga anō hoki ki te ao Māori, e hāpai ana i a rātou ki te whakatutuki i ā rātou kaupapa matua. E noho pūmau ana ngā kaimahi ki te tupu tonu, te whakawhanake tonu hoki, hei āta hāpai anō hoki i ngā wawata o te whānau.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te whakaritenga o ngā wāhi o roto.E mōhio ana te whānau, e tika ana kia arotake rātou i te whakatakotoranga o ngā wāhi akoranga o roto. I tēnei wā, kāhore taua wāhi e āta whakaritea ana hei hāpai i te whakawhiti haere noa o ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmomo taumahi me ngā wāhi e ngākau nuitia ana e rātou. Ko te whakariterite anō i te wāhi ka hāpai hoki i ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te arotahi ake ki āta taunekeneke, te āta whakawhiti kōrero ki ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai ki ngā kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki, me te whānau. Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakamahere rautaki.He mahere rautaki tā te whānau i āta whakaarohia ai, i āta whakawhanakehia ai hoki. Ko tā rātou kaupapa rautaki matua, ko te whakawhanake ake i te māramatanga o te whānau ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, tae atu ki te whai pānga nui o ngā akoranga a te whānau me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ka arotakengia, ka aroturukitia, ā, ka pūrongotia ki te whānau, ētahi tino whāinga.

Te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.Ka whai hua te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. He aronga nui ki te whakapūmau i te noho mārama mā ngā pūnaha i āta tāutuhia ai, mō te pūrongo ki te whānau i ngā hui i ia te wā. Kua āta whakawhanakehia te tūtohinga, ā, ka aroturukitia ngā whāinga i ia te wā. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga me te whakamahere. Ko te mauritau o ngā hononga ā-whānau me ngā whakawhitinga whakaaro e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga, tētahi tino tūāpapa mō te tupu tonu, te whakawhanaketanga tonu hoki o te kōhanga reo.

Te kapo i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga.Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā rautaki aromatawai hei whakatakoto haere i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka tohu ngā pūkete i te pakari haere, te mātau haere hoki o ngā tamariki, me te puāwaitanga o te mōhio ki tō rātou tuakiri, tō rātou reo, ā rātou taonga tuku iho hoki. E noho wātea ana aua pūkete hei tirohanga mā te whānau, kia kite ai rātou i te whakawhanaketanga pai o ā rātou tamariki hei kaiārahi pakari, mātau hoki, i roto i tō rātou ao.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te aromātai me te pūrongo i Te Whāriki.E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, me whakapakari ake i te aromātai hōtaka me te pūrongo ki ngā whānau. Me tū ngā hui ā-kaimahi i ia te wā, hei aromātai i te hōtaka, ā, me tuhi hoki i ngā kōrerorero hei pūrongo atu, hei aroturuki hoki. Me hāngai ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki te whai huatanga o ngā hōtaka me ngā whakaritenga ki te whakatutuki i te whānuitanga o ngā matea o ngā tamariki. Me tāutu hoki rātou i ngā āhuatanga hei whakawhanake ake. He rautaki pai hoki ki te whakamahi i aua mōhiohio hei tūāpapa mō ngā pūrongo ki te whānau. Me whakamōhio ake i te whānau mō ngā whakapaitanga ki te hōtaka, te tupu, me te whai angitu hoki o ngā hōtaka mā ngā tamariki.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Taura Here i te Ao I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o, tētahiTauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhangame tētahiRārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga

  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Taura Here i Te Ao is a newly established kōhanga reo that serves the communities of Ngā Ruahinerangi and Ngāti Ruanui. They officially opened their premises last year and have almost reached their roll capacity. The local people worked hard to establish the kōhanga reo in response to the high demand from whānau. Architectural features of the property and premises are specifically designed to reflect Taranaki. The kōhanga reo entry faces the mountain. The subtle wall patterns represent Mt Taranaki and the floor patterns acknowledge the river. The lighting in the hallway signifies matariki. The kōhanga reo is one of only three kōhanga reo that serve the iwi of Ngā Ruahinerangi.

The whānau chose Mana Tangata as the focus for this Education Review. They assisted ERO in the design of the evaluation question that guided ERO’s evaluation:

“How well do we as whānau develop our children as confident and capable in te ao Māori and prepare them for the wider world?”

Children are supported in a variety of ways to develop confidence and capability in te ao Māori. They learn about who they are and their place in the wider world. They have opportunities for outings which enable them to interact, explore and connect with the local whenua. Children are developing a sense of connection, as tangata whenua, to their turangawaewae. Children draw on their knowledge of te reo Māori. They are beginning to use what they know to communicate with others while they explore their environment.

Kaimahi report that they are continuing to refine programmes of learning that will broaden learning experiences for children. They are focused on how they can improve programmes and practices to support children to be confident and capable in te ao Māori and the wider world.

The whānau are committed and focused on managing the kōhanga reo effectively to benefit children. The skills and expertise of whānau are used to enhance all areas of kōhanga reo management and administration. Whānau attendance and participation at monthly meetings is good. Kaumatua are active supporters of the kōhanga reo and whānau. Whānau are well informed about how the kōhanga reo is performing for children. Building knowledge of te reo Māori and understanding their cultural heritage is an ongoing strategic focus for the whānau. Whānau manage the kōhanga reo with a focus on continual improvement and growth of the whānau to benefit their children.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Taura Here i te AoBefore the review, the whānau ofwas invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

4 Findings

Mana Tangata

Background

The kōhanga reo whānau chose Mana Tangata as their focus for the review. They highlighted that children are confident and happy. All members assert that te reo and tīkanga Māori is a high priority for them and their children. Identity, cultural heritage, whakapapa and whānaungatanga are implicit in learning. Most members of the kōhanga reo took part in the design of the evaluation question which guided ERO’s evaluation:

“How well do we as whānau develop our children as confident and capable in te ao Māori and prepare them for the wider world?”

Areas of good performance

Te reo me ngā tikanga Māori.Children are beginning to use te reo Māori in conversations when they independently explore and during shared learning sessions. Their developing knowledge and confidence in their identity and heritage is reinforced through daily waiata. Tikanga Māori is promoted throughout the day. Children understand that there are important protocols in karakia. These reaffirm the traditional practices of whānau. Outings and the variety of indoor and outdoor learning experiences support children to explore the language in different contexts.

Te ao Māori.Children’s learning of te ao Māori is supported through exploration, discovery and shared learning in a variety of contexts. Many of these contexts remind children about their connections to their whenua, Ngā Ruahinerangi and the wider Taranaki area. The outdoors is used well throughout the day. Maara kai are established. The outdoor veranda is used by kaimahi to provide exploratory and interest based learning activities. The indoors is largely open plan which promotes shared learning and independent exploration. Outings are also provided so children are able to interact and explore their wider natural and physical environments.

Whānaungatanga.Children interact confidently with others in their kōhanga reo environment. Their place and connection to the kōhanga reo is affirmed by their peers and whānau each day through pepeha and mihimihi. The various shared and independent activities effectively foster respectful interactions between children. Children understand that all members of their whānau are valued and special.

Whaioranga.Children are developing well those positive behaviours of respect and care for themselves, their environment and others. Whānau members effectively maintain all health, safety, hygiene and care systems for children’s wellbeing. Whānau are valued for the support they provide in the daily routines. These include preparing kai, supervision of children, helping with hygiene and sleeping. Kaimahi consistently model values of respect and care for oneself and others throughout the day. Most children are new to the kōhanga reo. They are happy and settling into their kōhanga reo environment.

Whānau commitment.Children are well supported by their whānau to develop as confident and capable leaders in te ao Māori, and the wider world. The whānau worked hard to establish the kōhanga reo. They have a shared vision and aspirations, including building whānau te reo Māori capacity. The current roll is almost full and whānau interest and involvement is strong. Their combined expertise and knowledge of te ao Māori supports them to achieve their priorities. Kaimahi are committed to ongoing growth and development to successfully support whānau aspirations.

Area for development

Organisation of indoor environments. Whānau members recognise the need to review the organisation of indoor learning areas. Currently the space is not well organised to support children to move easily between activities and areas of interest to them. Reorganisation of the space should also support kaimahi and whānau to focus on more intimate interactions and conversations with children.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Strategic planning.The whānau have a well considered and developed strategic plan. Their strategic priority is to further develop whānau understanding of the Kōhanga Reo kaupapa, including the importance of whānau learning and development. Specific goals are reviewed, monitored and reported on to the whānau.

Kōhanga management.The kōhanga reo is managed effectively by whānau. There is a strong focus on maintaining transparency through well defined systems for reporting to whānau at regular hui. The charter is well developed and the goals are regularly monitored. Whānau members are actively involved in decision making and planning. Healthy whānau relationships and communication for management sets a strong foundation for continued growth and development of the kōhanga reo.

Capturing children’s learning and development. Kaimahi use a range of assessment methods to record children’s learning. Portfolios show children’s developing confidence and capability and their growing knowledge of their identity, language and heritage. Portfolios are easily accessible to whānau so they can see how well their children are developing as confident and capable leaders in their world.

Area for development

Te Whāriki evaluation and reporting.Kaimahi recognise that programme evaluation and reporting to whānau needs to be stronger. Kaimahi meetings to evaluate the programme need to be held regularly, and recorded for the purposes of reporting and monitoring. Discussions should focus on how well programmes and practices are meeting the diverse needs of children. They should also identify aspects that may need further development. It is good practice to use this information as a basis for reporting to whānau. Whānau should be better informed about the necessary programme improvements, growth and overall success of programmes for children.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Taura Here i te Ao Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff ofcompleted an EROKōhanga Whānau Assurance StatementandSelf-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

6 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendations for the whānau to:

6.1 maintain high quality self review and reporting of all areas of the kōhanga reo kaupapa to monitor growth and success

6.2 monitor the areas for development identified in this report to benefit their children’s learning at the kōhanga reo.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

To the Parents and Community of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Taura Here i te Ao

These are the findings of the Education Review Office’s latest report on Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Taura Here i te Ao.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Taura Here i Te Ao is a newly established kōhanga reo that serves the communities of Ngā Ruahinerangi and Ngāti Ruanui. They officially opened their premises last year and have almost reached their roll capacity. The local people worked hard to establish the kōhanga reo in response to the high demand from whānau. Architectural features of the property and premises are specifically designed to reflect Taranaki. The kōhanga reo entry faces the mountain. The subtle wall patterns represent Mt Taranaki and the floor patterns acknowledge the river. The lighting in the hallway signifies matariki. The kōhanga reo is one of only three kōhanga reo that serve the iwi of Ngā Ruahinerangi.

The whānau chose Mana Tangata as the focus for this Education Review. They assisted ERO in the design of the evaluation question that guided ERO’s evaluation:

“How well do we as whānau develop our children as confident and capable in te ao Māori and prepare them for the wider world?”

Children are supported in a variety of ways to develop confidence and capability in te ao Māori. They learn about who they are and their place in the wider world. They have opportunities for outings which enable them to interact, explore and connect with the local whenua. Children are developing a sense of connection, as tangata whenua, to their turangawaewae. Children draw on their knowledge of te reo Māori. They are beginning to use what they know to communicate with others while they explore their environment.

Kaimahi report that they are continuing to refine programmes of learning that will broaden learning experiences for children. They are focused on how they can improve programmes and practices to support children to be confident and capable in te ao Māori and the wider world.

The whānau are committed and focused on managing the kōhanga reo effectively to benefit children. The skills and expertise of whānau are used to enhance all areas of kōhanga reo management and administration. Whānau attendance and participation at monthly meetings is good. Kaumatua are active supporters of the kōhanga reo and whānau. Whānau are well informed about how the kōhanga reo is performing for children. Building knowledge of te reo Māori and understanding their cultural heritage is an ongoing strategic focus for the whānau. Whānau manage the kōhanga reo with a focus on continual improvement and growth of the whānau to benefit their children.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to student achievement and useful to this kōhanga reo.

If you would like a copy of the full report, please contact the kōhanga reo or see the ERO web page, http://www.ero.govt.nz.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
