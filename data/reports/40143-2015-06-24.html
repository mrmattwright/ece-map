Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Hairini ki te marae o Hairini, ki Tauranga. E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake hei ākonga pakari ka kōkiri i ā rātou ake akoranga. He pai te hono atu a ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hoa, me ngā kaimahi. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo Māori e whakamahia ana i ngā wā katoa, puta noa i te rā. He pai te atawhai i ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōhanga Reo o Hairini i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Hairini is located at Hairini Marae, Tauranga. Children are well supported to develop as confident, independent learners. Children have formed positive relationships with their peers and kaimahi. Children hear te reo Māori used consistently throughout the day. Children are well cared for in their kōhanga environment.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Hairini will be in three years.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Hairini ki te marae o Hairini, ki Tauranga. Nō Ngāti Ranginui ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau. Haere ai ngā tamariki o te takiwā nei ki tēnei kōhanga reo. Ko te marae tētahi tino horopaki mō ngā akoranga e pā ana ki ngā tikanga me ngā whakapapa, ā, ka whai pānga nui ki te poipoi i te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki. Nō te tau 1982 te kōhanga reo i whakatūngia ai, ā, koia nei te kōhanga reo tuatahi ki Tauranga Moana.

Ko te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te Paenga-whāwhā o 2012 i tāutu i ngā tino whakawhanaketanga ki ngā wāhanga e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi, ngā pūtea, te whakamahere rautaki, te arotake whaiaro, me te whakamahere hōtaka. I taunaki Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kia tautokona te whānau me ngā kaimahi i roto i ngā mahi aromatawai, me ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aromātai hōtaka.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te tautoko i ngā tamariki kia tū ai rātou hei ākonga pakari ka kōkiri i ā rātou ake akoranga?

Ka tino tautokona ngā tamariki kia whanake ai rātou hei ākonga pakari ka kōkiri i ā rātou ake akoranga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He pakari te whai atu a ngā tamariki i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga me te mahi ngahau me ō rātou hoa. E wātea ana ki a rātou te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi kia āhei ai rātou ki te waihanga, te hīraurau hopanga, te tūhura me te whakamātau. Ka kitea ki te taiao ako ngā tohu me ngā āhuatanga o te ao Māori. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te ārahi i ētahi o ā rātou ake akoranga mō ngā wā roa. Kā āhei rātou te ako hei rōpū iti, hei rōpū nui hoki. Ka akiaki, ka tautoko, ka taunekeneke hoki ngā pakeke ki te taha o ngā tamariki. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te ako i ngā tikanga, i roto i ngā mahinga me ngā wā ōkawa ki te kōhanga reo me te marae. Ka whakamanahia rātou hei ākonga pakari, hei ākonga pākiki.

He pai ngā hononga o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hoa, ngā kaimahi, me te whānau. He tākare ngā kaimahi, ā, ka atawhai, ka ārahi hoki i ngā tamariki mā ngā huarahi ka whakakoia i a rātou. Whakanuia ai e rātou i ia te wā, ngā whakamātau me ngā whakatutukitanga a ngā tamariki, mā te whakamihi me te menemene. Ka whakaatuhia e ngā tamariki te whanonga pai ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ngā kaimahi hoki. He manaakitanga tō ngā hononga o te tuakana me te teina, ā, ka whakanuia te whanaungatanga. E ako ana ngā tamariki ki te whakahono atu, te whakawhirinaki atu hoki ki tētahi taiao e hāpai ana, e atawhai ana hoki i a rātou.

He tokomaha ngā tamariki e tīmata ana ki te kawe i a rātou anō, me te tiaki i tō rātou taiao. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi kia whai wāhi ai ngā tamariki ki ngā ārahitanga. Ka ākina ngā tamariki tuākana ki te whakapai haere i ō rātou wāhi mahi. Ka tiaki, ka āwhina hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā pēpi i te wā moe, te wā kai me ngā wā taumahi o ia rā. Ka whakamanahia, ka whakanuia hoki ngā whakamātau a ngā tamariki.

Ka whakakoia te reo o ngā tamariki, ā, he pai tōna whakawhanaketanga. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo Māori e kōrerohia ana i ngā wā katoa, puta noa i te rā. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā tūmomo aronga kōrerorero, aronga ā-tinana hoki ki te whakawhiti whakaaro me te whakatauira i te reo Māori ki ngā tamariki. Tokomaha ngā tamariki e tohu ana i tō rātou māramatanga, tā rātou waia hoki ki te reo Māori, nā tā rātou tāwhai i ngā oro me te whakamahi i ētahi paku kupu, i ngā kīanga poto rānei. Ka aro pai ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi reo me ngā kēmu reo, tae atu anō hoki ki ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, me ngā waiata.

Ka kōkiri ngā tēina i ā rātou ake tākaro, ā, e āhei ana ki te whiriwhiri, mai i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi. He pai tā rātou whakariterite i tō rātou taiao ako, he mārie, ā, e tika ana hoki ngā rauemi o roto. E āhei ana ngā tēina ki te ako ki te taha o ngā tuākana, ki ā rātou ake rōpū rānei. E ako ana ngā tēina i te ao whānui ki tētahi taiao e hāpai nui ana i a rātou.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

E whai hua ana ngā tukanga a te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. He pakari ā rātou pūnaha mō te whakahaere i ngā pūtea, i te nuinga o ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru, me ngā tukanga hoki e pā ana ki te whakahaere tari. Kua āta tuhia ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga hei ārahi i te whānau i roto i ngā wāhanga whakahaere o te kōhanga reo. He tukanga arotake o roto tā te whānau, ā, e arotahi ana tēnei ki te whakapai ake me te whakatupu ake i te kōhanga reo. He pai tonu te nui o ngā pūrongo ki ngā hui ā-whānau, mō ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o ngā whakahaeretanga ki te kōhanga reo. Ka noho pūmau te whānau ki te whakarato i te tino tautoko, te tino mātauranga hoki e tika ana mō ā rātou tamariki.

He pakari ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere i ngā hōtaka mā ngā tamariki, me te aroturuki, te aromatawai hoki i ia te wā kia eke angitu ai rātou. He pai te kiko o roto i ngā mahere ā-wāhanga, ā-wiki hoki mā ngā kaimahi. Ka whakapuaki whakaaro hoki te whānau ki te hōtake akoranga. Kei ngā mahere hoki ngā rauemi me ngā taumahi a ngā tamariki. Kei ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki ētahi tino mōhiohio e hāngai ana ki ā rātou akoranga me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga hoki, ā, e wātea ana ki ngā mātua. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā tūmomo rauemi aromatawai hei kapo i ngā hua o te ako o ngā tamariki. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te whai pūtaketanga o ngā mahere, me te whai huatanga o ngā aromatawai, ā, e āta whakatakoto ā-tuhi nei ēnā i tā rātou ahu whakamua.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He tawhito rawa ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā āhuatanga mahi a ngā kaimahi. Me arotake i ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi, ngā kirimana tuku mahi, me ngā tukanga arohaehae kia hāngai ai ēnei ki te Ture Whiwhinga Mahi. Ka hāpai tēnei i ngā kaimahi ki te noho mārama ki ngā tūmanako o te whānau, me ā rātou tikanga ake hei kaimahi. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te noho mōhio ake a ngā kaimahi ki ā rātou ake tika, ō rātou ake tūranga, me ā rātou ake kawenga mahi.

Kāhore he kaupapa here, he tukanga hoki ā te whānau mō ngā whara me ngā maiki. He mea nui, kia whanakehia, kia whakatinanahia hoki taua kaupapa here, hei hāpai i ngā kaimahi ki te whakahaere tika i ngā whara, i ngā maiki rānei hei ārai i ngā tamariki.

Me whakawhanake ake i te whakamahere rautaki. Kāhore anō te whānau kia tāutu i ā rātou kaupapa matua mō meāke nei. E mōhio ana rātou, kāhore anō rātou kia whakawhanake i tētahi mahere mahi ka whakaraupapa haere i tā rātou huarahi ki te whakatinana, te aroturuki, me te pūrongo i ā rātou whāinga rautaki. Ka hāpai aua whakawhanaketanga i te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ō rātou wawata mō ā rātou tamariki.

He whāiti noa te aromātai hōtaka. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, ka whai hua te aromātai hōtaka, ā, ka whakapuaki hoki rātou i tēnei. Ko te tuhi haere i aua whakawhitinga kōrero me aua whakaaro huritao ka whai pānga nui ki te whai huatanga o te aromātai hōtaka. Kia kapo atu ngā kaimahi i tēnei aronga kē atu, ka āta piki ake te ako a ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

  • Me whakarite i te tuhi matawhānui o ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki ngā whara katoa a ngā tamariki, ā, me ōkawa te whakamōhio hoki i aua maiki ki ngā mātua.
    [R 46 (1)(2) Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; Hauora me te Haumaru 25, Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo]

4 Te Taunakitanga

E whakaae tahi ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau kia whakatutukihia e te whānau ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, mō ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo me te hōtaka a ngā tamariki.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Hairini is located at Hairini Marae, Tauranga. The children and their whānau are affiliated to Ngāti Ranginui. Children from the surrounding area attend the kōhanga reo. The marae provides an important context for learning about tikanga and whakapapa and is vital in fostering children’s sense of belonging. The kōhanga reo was established in 1982 and was the first to open in Tauranga Moana.

The previous ERO report of April 2012 identified positive developments in the areas of personnel, finances, strategic planning, self review and programme planning. ERO recommended that whānau and kaimahi needed support with assessment and programme evaluation practices.

2 The Review Findings

How well are children supported to be confident, independent learners?

Children are well supported to develop as confident, independent learners.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Areas of good performance

Children confidently follow their interests and have fun with their peers. They have access to a wide range of resources which enable them to create, problem solve, explore and experiment. The learning environment reflects te ao Māori symbols and signs. Children are able to lead some of their own learning for extended periods. They also learn in small and large groups. Adults encourage, support and interact with children. Children have opportunities to learn about tikanga during routines and during formal occasions at the kōhanga reo and marae. Children are valued as confident, curious learners.

Children have positive relationships with their peers, kaimahi and whānau. Kaimahi are enthusiastic, welcoming and guide children in affirming ways. They regularly acknowledge children’s efforts and achievements through praise and smiles. Children display positive behaviours with each other and kaimahi. Tuakana teina relationships are caring and promote whanaungatanga. Children are learning to form trusting relationships in a supportive, warm environment.

Several children are beginning to take responsibility for themselves and their environment. Kaimahi provide leadership opportunities for children. The older children are encouraged to tidy up after themselves. Pēpi are well supervised and supported when they are sleeping, at meal times and during their daily activities. Children’s efforts are valued and recognised.

Children’s language is affirmed and is developing well. Children hear te reo Māori being used consistently throughout the day. Kaimahi use various verbal and non verbal approaches to communicate and model te reo Māori to and with children. Many children show understanding and familiarity with te reo Māori by imitating sounds and using single words or short phrases. Children respond positively to language activities and games including karakia, hīmene, waiata.

The teina play independently and are able to choose from a range of resources. Their learning environment is well organised, peaceful and is appropriately resourced. Teina are able to learn alongside the older children or in their own groups. Teina are learning about the wider world in a supportive environment.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau have effective processes to manage the kōhanga reo. They have robust systems for financial management, most aspects of health and safety and administration procedures. Whānau management practices are guided by well written policies with procedures. Whānau have an internal review process that focuses on improving and growing the kōhanga reo. There is a reasonable level of reporting on different aspects of the kōhanga reo operations at whānau hui. Whānau are committed to providing the best possible support and education for their children.

Kaimahi confidently plan programmes for children and regularly monitor and assess their successes. Term and weekly planning provide good detail for kaimahi. Whānau contribute to the learning programme. Planning includes resources and children’s activities. Children’s profile books provide valuable information about their learning and development and are available to parents. Kaimahi use various assessment tools to capture student’s achievements. Children benefit from purposeful planning and assessments practices.

Key Next Steps

Kaimahi employment documents are outdated. Job descriptions, employment contracts and appraisal processes should undergo review to align them with the current Employment Act. This will help kaimahi to understand whānau expectations and what their entitlements include. Children will benefit from kaimahi who are more informed and aware of their rights, roles and responsibilities.

The whānau do not have an accident and incident policy with procedures. It is important this policy is developed and implemented to assist kaimahi manage any injuries or incidents keep children safe.

Strategic planning requires further development. Whānau have not identified their future priorities. They acknowledge they have been remiss in developing an action plan to show how they will implement, monitor and report on their strategic goals. These developments will help whānau achieve the aspirations they have for their children.

Programme evaluation is limited. Kaimahi are aware of the value of programme evaluation and are able to articulate this. The documentation of conversations and reflections is the key to effective programme evaluation. Children’s learning will improve in significant ways when kaimahi take advantage of this refined approach.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

  • ensure detailed records are kept of all injuries to children and that parents be formally informed of these incidents.
    [R46, (1),(2), Early Childhood Regulations 2008, Health and Safety 25, Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the whānau agreed to address the areas highlighted in this ERO report pertaining to the kōhanga reo operations and children’s programme.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Hairini is located at Hairini Marae, Tauranga. Children are well supported to develop as confident, independent learners. Children have formed positive relationships with their peers and kaimahi. Children hear te reo Māori used consistently throughout the day. Children are well cared for in their kōhanga environment.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Hairini will be in three years.
