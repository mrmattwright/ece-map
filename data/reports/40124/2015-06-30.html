1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whakarewarewa ki Rotorua, ā, ko ngā tikanga me te kawa o Te Arawa tōna tūāpapa. He tino hononga ā-whakapapa tō ngā tamariki ki ngā hapū maha o Te Arawa. He āhua rangatahi tonu te whānau, ā, he tino wawata ō rātou mō ā rātou tamariki, tae atu hoki ki tō rātou tū pakari hei Māori, kia eke angitu hoki rātou hei pākeke.

Ka whai wāhi matua te kuia ki te kōhanga reo. He matatau ia ki te reo Māori, ā, he tino puna mātauranga anō hoki ia o te kāinga nei kia whai pānga nui ai ki te whakawhanaketanga o te māramatanga, te mōhiotanga anō hoki o ngā tamariki hei uri o Te Arawa.

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere, pērā i te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki, ngā tikanga me te reo ā-iwi o Te Arawa, te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, me te arotake whaiaro. I taunaki taua pūrongo kia whakapai te whānau i te whakamahere rautaki.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te kite i te whanaungatanga o ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou tākaro, ā rātou taunekeneke hoki ki te kōhanga reo?

Ka kitea te whakatinanatanga o te mōhiotanga me te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te whanaungatanga i a rātou e noho ana ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka tino hāpaitia ngā tamariki ā-whatumanawa, ā-wairua hoki i a rātou e ako ana, e tākaro ana hoki. Ka hono ngā wheako akoranga katoa o ngā tamariki ki ngā atua Māori me ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga hoki. E ora ana ngā atua ki te ao o ngā tamariki. Ka whakarato te whānau i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi e mau nei i te hōhonutanga o ngā tohu Māori, ā, e hāpai ana hoki i te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori. Ka tautāwhi, ka mauritau hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e mau nei i te reo Māori hei reo whakawhitiwhiti kōrero. Ka mārama ngā tamariki i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo whakatakotoranga reo, ā, ka kitea ki ngā wāhanga ōkawa o te hōtaka akoranga. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i te māramatanga me te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo, mā ngā rautaki ako reo e tika ana. Kei te māia haere te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori.

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i te whānuitanga o ngā hononga papai e hāpai ana i a rātou hei ākonga. Kua roa nei te kuia e whai wāhi ana ki te kōhanga reo, ā, ka whakatauira ia i ngā rautaki ka āta hāpai i te poipoi o te whanonga pai o ngā tamariki. He mātau ia ki ngā mahi tuku iho e ahu mai ana i ngā kawa me ngā tikanga o Te Arawa, ā, ka āta whakapuakihia aua mātauranga ki ngā tamariki me ngā pākeke. He tūturu, he whakaute hoki ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki.

Ka manaaki, ka poipoi hoki ngā tamariki i a rātou anō. He māia te kawe a ngā tamariki i ā rātou mahi hei tuakana. He māhorahora tā rātou atawhai, tā rātou manaaki hoki i ngā pēpi, puta noa i te rā. Ka whai hua ngā kaimahi ki te whakauru i ngā pēpi ki te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whai pānga te whanaungatanga ki te katoa o ngā hononga me ngā taunekeneke puta noa i te kōhanga reo. Ka tautokona ngā pēpi i roto i ā rātou akoranga, me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga mā ngā hononga e aroha ana, e manaaki ana hoki i a rātou.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka whakahaere te whānau i ngā whakaritenga o tō rātou kōhanga reo, hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō nā tata tonu nei te tūtohinga i arotakengia ai, i whakahoungia ai hoki. Kua tāutuhia e te whānau ngā tino whāinga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga a te whānau me te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori. Ka pūrongo i ngā pūtea i ia te wā. Kua whakaritea te katoa o ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi. Kua oti ngā arohaehae, ā, ka whai pānga ēnei ki te whakapaitanga ake o ngā whakaritenga a ngā kaimahi.

Ko ngā painga mō ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā mahi whakataunga katoa a te whānau. Kei te whānau tētahi tukanga arotake whaiaro e arotahi ana ki te whakarite i ngā pūnaha katoa, hei hāpai i ngā putanga whai hua mō ngā tamariki. He kaha te tae ā-tinana atu ki ngā hui ā-whānau. Ka noho maioha te whānau ki ā rātou āta whakawhitinga kōrero ki ngā kaimahi. Ki tō te whānau whakaaro, ka ākina, ka whakanuia hoki tā rātou whai wāhi atu.

He kiko, he matawhānui hoki te whakamahere i te hōtaka. Kua āta tāutuhia te kaupapa ako me te kiko o te hōtaka. Kua tāutuhia hoki ngā tino rautaki whakaako. E tika ana ēnei ki ngā reanga me ngā taumata ako o ngā tamariki katoa. He tika ngā rauemi mō te hōtaka akoranga, ā, kua whakatakotohia tētahi tahua pūtea hei āta whakarite i te kounga pai o ngā rauemi mā ngā tamariki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te hōtaka akoranga i āta whakaritea ai, i āta whakahaerehia ai hoki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Me whakapakari ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aromatawai. Kāhore he tukanga mārama i āta whakaritea ai hei aroturuki i te ako me te ahu whakamua a ia tamaiti. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā pūkete mō te whakaemi me te putu i ngā tauira mahi a ngā tamariki. He mea nui, kia noho mārama ngā kaimahi ki te pūtake o ngā pūkete, ā, me āta tohu hoki aua pūkete i te ahu whakamua me te ako a ngā tamariki. Kāhore i ngā whānau ngā mōhiohio mārama e pā ana ki ngā akoranga a ā rātou ake tamariki.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakaae ngātahi Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau kia whakarite ngā kaimahi i ngā tukanga ka whakapakari ake i ngā mahi aromatawai.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whakarewarewa ki Rotorua. Ko ngā mātāpono o te whanaungatanga e pou here ana i ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki me ō rātou hononga. Ka whakatinanahia e ngā tamariki tō rātou mōhiotanga, tō rātou māramatanga hoki ki te whanaungatanga i a rātou e noho ana ki te kōhanga reo. Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e mau nei i te reo Māori hei reo whakawhitiwhiti kōrero.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakarewarewa is in Rotorua and is based on ngā tikanga and kawa o Te Arawa. Children have strong whakapapa links to the many hapū of Te Arawa. Whānau are relatively young and have clear aspirations for their children including being able to stand tall as Māori and being successful as adults.

The kuia plays a key role in the kōhanga reo. Her fluency in te reo Māori, as well as the wealth of local knowledge she has, is critical to the development of children’s understanding and knowledge of themselves as descendants of Te Arawa.

The previous ERO report identified areas of good performance for programme planning, assessment, the children’s learning programme, Te Arawa traditions and dialect, whānau management and self review. The report recommended that whānau improve strategic planning.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children reflect whanaungatanga in their play and interactions at the kōhanga reo?

Children display a working knowledge and understanding of whanaungatanga at kōhanga reo.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children experience strong emotional and spiritual support as they learn and play. All children’s learning experiences are linked to atua Māori and their roles and responsibilities. Children refer to these entities as living beings. Whānau provide a range of resources that are rich in Māori symbolism and support children’s awareness of te ao Māori. Children feel comforted and settled at kōhanga reo.

Children learn in an environment where te reo Māori is the language of communication. Children understand a range of language patterns which is evident in the formal parts of the learning programme. Kaimahi support children’s understanding and use of te reo with appropriate language learning strategies. Children are becoming confident in their use of te reo Māori.

Children experience a range of positive relationships that support them as learners. A long standing kuia models how to positively influence children’s behaviour. She is knowledgeable about traditions based on Te Arawa kawa and tikanga and shares this openly with children and adults. Kaimahi interactions with children are genuine and respectful.

Children are caring and nurturing with each other. The older children carry out their role as tuakana confidently. They are naturally warm and caring with the babies throughout the day. Kaimahi successfully integrate the babies in to the learning programme. Whanaungatanga influences all relationships and interactions in the kōhanga reo. Babies are supported in their learning and development through loving and caring relationships.

Planning and Evaluation

The whānau manages the operations of their kōhanga reo in the best interest of the children. The charter has recently been reviewed and updated. Whānau have identified clear goals related to whānau management and the development of te reo Māori. There is regular financial reporting. All personnel documents are in place. Appraisals are complete and contribute to improving kaimahi practice.

Children’s interests are central to whanau decision-making. Whānau have a self review process that focuses on ensuring all systems support positive outcomes for the children. Attendance at whānau hui is consistent. Whānau appreciate the clear communication they have with kaimahi. Whānau feel their input is encouraged and valued.

Programme planning is detailed and comprehensive. Kaupapa ako and programme content is clearly identified. Specific teaching strategies are identified. These are appropriate to the ages and level of learning for all children. Resources for the learning programme are appropriate and a budget is established to ensure there are good quality resources for children. Children enjoy a learning programme that is well organised and managed.

Key Next Steps

Assessment practices require strengthening. There is no clear, established process for monitoring individual children’s learning and progress. Kaimahi use profiles to gather and store samples of children’s work. It is important that kaimahi understand the purpose of profiles and how these should clearly reflect children’s progress and learning. Whānau do not have clear information about their child’s learning.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the whānau agreed that kaimahi establish processes strengthen assessment practices.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Whakarewarewa is in Rotorua. The principles of whanaungatanga underpin children’s interactions and relationships. Children display a working knowledge and understanding of whanaungatanga at kōhanga reo. Children learn in an environment where te reo Māori is the language of communication.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo ki Whakarewarewa will be in three years.
