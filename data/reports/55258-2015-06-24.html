1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētehi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga a te Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori i Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia ai i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētehi atu wāhanga rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai e te pūrongo a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i mua atu, me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

  • te mana whenua, arā, te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki te whakatōmene, te āhuatanga ki te tuku rauemi, te whakahaeretanga o te hōtaka, te mārama o ngā kaimahi ki ā ratou mahi me ā rātou kawenga
  • te mahere me te arotake, tatū atu ki ngā pūrongo kaiako, te arataki i te hōtaka, ngā aromatawai me te manaaki kaimahi, arā, ngā aromihi
  • ngā here ki te ture, arā, ko te whakahaere o te marau, te whakaūnga o te marau me te manaaki kaimahi
  • ngā take e tārewa tonu ana nō te pūrongo o mua.

2 Te Horopaki

E tū ana te kōhanga reo i tētehi hapori taiwhenua iti nei ki Nūhaka, pātata ki Wairoa. He nui, he pūrangiaho te wāhi e tū nei te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, ā, he whenua rahi ōna mō te tākaro.

I whakaao māramatia e te pūrongo arotake a Te Tari Arotake o te tau 2014 ngā take nui o te hōtaka ako. He torutoru noa iho ngā rauemi me ngā kaupapa i wātea ki ngā tamariki. Kāore ngā tamariki i whai wā ki te ako motukahe nei. Kāore i tika te whakahaeretanga o te hōtaka ako, ā, nā tēnei i kore ai te whānau me ngā kaimahi i mātau ki te hōtaka ako. Kāore ngā kaimahi i mārama pū ki a rātou mahi me a rātou kawenga.

Tērā ētehi āhuatanga o te manaaki kaimahi me whakapakari ake. Kāore i oti tika ngā aromihi. Kāore i maheretia ngā whakataunga a ngā kaimahi, ā, i pūpū ake te raru i kōnei ki te whānau.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Whenua

Ngā wāhanga e koke ana

E whai wā ana ngā tamariki ki te whaiwhai haere i ō rātou hiahia me te whakatōmene i tō rātou taiao. E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā rauemi me ngā kaupapa mahi, ā, he ātaahua te noho. E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki te whakatau i ngā mea hei ako mā rātou. He raupapatanga tō te hōtaka, arā, he kaupapa mahi ōna, he kaupapa ako-ā-rōpū ōna, he takaro takitahi hoki tētehi wāhanga ōna. E mātau ana ngā kaimahi ki tō rātou tūranga me a rātou kawenga puta noa i te rā, ā, he pānga pai tōna ki te ako me te whakanake haere o ngā tamariki. E ngaruru ana, e hikaka ana ngā tamariki ki te ako.

Ngā whakaritenga matua ka whai ake

Ahakoa he nui tonu ngā whakanikotanga kua whakaūngia e ngā kaimahi ki te hōtaka ako, me mātakitaki me whakakaha hoki e rātou ngā tikanga mahi i te wā e tautokongia ana ngā tamariki ki te ako. He mahi whai hua te aro ki ngā tikanga mahi a kōhanga reo kē atu me te whai tautoko i te Pōari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo (TKRNT) e nui ai ngā putanga mā ngā tamariki.

Te whakamahere me te aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e koke tonu ana

He mea whakahāngai ngā hiahia o ngā tamariki i ngā wānangatanga me ngā whakataunga a te whānau me ngā kaimahi. He rite tonu te tuku i ngā pūrongo kaiako ki ngā hui whānau, ā, he māramatanga o roto. E whakahīhī pai ana te whānau i ngā whakanikotanga kua puta i roto i ngā marama kua taha ake. He pai, he whai hua hoki te whakamārotanga o te hononga ki ngā kaimahi, ā, he nui kē atu te aro inaianei ki ngā hiahia ō ngā tamariki.

He pai kē atu te mōhio ō ngā kaimahi ki tēnei hanga o te whakahaere aromatawai. He whānui ā rātou tikanga mahi ki te mātakitaki me te aromatawai i te kokenga o te tamaiti. Tāpiri atu ki tēnei ko ngā kōrero a te whānau, ko ngā kitenga o ia rā me ngā aromatawai-ā-wiki. Kua waia ngā kaimahi ki ngā hiahia, ki ngā kaha me ngā mahi ka whai ake mā te tamaiti. E hopu ana ngā kaimahi i ngā angitūtanga, i ngā wheako hoki ō ngā tamariki i roto i ngā kōpaki me ngā pukapuka.

Ko te whakangungu kaimahi tētehi kaupapa nui ki te whānau. Kua whakaūngia e rātou te pūnaha aromihi, ā, me te tohu i ngā wāhi hei whakapakari mā ngā kaimahi. Kua arotakengia e rātou tā rātou pūnaha manaaki kaimahi, ā, kua pakari ake ētehi wāhanga. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki i ngā pūnaha manaaki kaimahi e aro ana ki ngā whakanikotanga, ki ō rātou hiahia hoki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E whakaae ana te whānau me whakarato e rātou he wā e taea ai e ngā kaimahi te whakapakari i te pitomata ki te arahi.

4 Te Taunakitanga

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau kia whakapakari tonu ngā kaimahi ō rātou mōhiotanga me ō rātou māramatanga mō ngā āhuatanga me te huarahi e ako ai ngā tamariki i te horopaki o te ao Māori.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana te kōhanga reo i tētehi hapori taiwhenua iti nei ki Nūhaka, pātata ki Wairoa. E whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki ki tētehi hōtaka ako kua panonitia i runga i te pai. Mā te koke i roto i ngā kaupapa whakangungu kaimahi ngā kaimahi e tautoko kia pūmau tonu, kia pai kē atu ā rātou tikanga whakaako.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōhanga Reo o Nūhaka i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of the Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of the kōhanga reo governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the previous ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • mana whenua including opportunities for children to explore , resource provisions, programme management and staff knowledge of their roles and responsibilities
  • planning and evaluation including kaiako reporting, programme leadership, assessment, and personnel management including staff appraisals
  • areas of non compliance including curriculum management and implementation and personnel management
  • any issues identified since the previous review.

2 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Nūhaka is in the small rural community of Nūhaka, near Wairoa. The kōhanga reo operates from a spacious, bright building which has a large outdoor play area.

The ERO report of 2014, identified several issues with the learning programme. Children had access to only a few resources and activities. Children did not have opportunities to direct their own learning. The learning programme was disorganised resulting in whānau and staff not knowing what was happening. The staff is unfamiliar with their roles and responsibilities.

There were aspects of personnel management that needed improvement. Staff appraisals were incomplete. Staffing decisions were unplanned and caused confusion and tension among whanau.

3 Findings

Mana Whenua

Areas of progress

Children have opportunities to follow their interests and explore their environment. They have easy access to a wide range of resources and activities which are set out attractively. Children are able to make decisions about their own learning. The programme has structure and includes kaupapa mahi, group learning times and independent play. The staff knows about their roles and responsibilities throughout the day and this has a positive influence on children’s learning and development. Children are stimulated and self motivated to learn.

Key Next Steps

Although the staff has implemented a number of improvements to the learning programme, they still need to monitor and strengthen their practice when supporting children to learn. Observing good practice in other kōhanga reo and seeking guidance from Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) is a highly valuable exercise that should produce positive results for children.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of progress

Children’s interests help focus whānau and staff discussions and decision making. Kaiako reporting at whānau hui is regular and informative. Whānau are proud of the improvements that have been made over recent months. They acknowledge relationships with kaimahi are more positive and productive and there is now a greater focus on children and their needs.

Staff knowledge about what constitutes good assessment practice has improved. They use a range of practices to monitor and assess each child’s progress. Some of these include whānau feedback, daily observations and weekly assessments. Staff are familiar with each child’s interests, strengths and next learning steps. Staff record children’s successes and experiences in their portfolio folders and notebooks.

The professional development of staff is a priority for whānau. They have implemented the appraisal system and identified staff professional development needs. They have reviewed their personnel management systems and refined some areas. Children benefit from personnel systems that are focussed on improvement and their interests.

Key Next Steps

Whānau acknowledge they should provide opportunities for staff to develop their leadership potential.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the whānau agreed that staff continue to strengthen their knowledge and understanding of what and how young children learn in a te ao Māori context.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Nūhaka is located in the small rural community of Nūhaka, near Wairoa. Children experience a learning programme that has undergone positive changes. Ongoing staff professional development should assist staff to sustain and improve their teaching practice.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Nūhaka will be in three years.
