Te Whare Punanga Takawaenga Atawhai O Aorere, Aorere College Childcare Centre - 05/03/2014

1 Background

Introduction

A Supplementary Review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager, Review Services in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a centre has taken towards addressing issues specified in a previous education review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of the Te Whare Punanga Takawaenga Atawhai O Aorere governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the November 2012 ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • teaching practices that respond to children’s interests and extend their learning
  • processes for assessing each child’s learning
  • personnel systems to provide effective guidance and support for staff
  • documented self-review processes focused on improving outcomes for children
  • systems for monitoring sleeping children
  • any other issues relevant to the management and operation of the centre that arise in the course of the review.

2 Evaluation Findings

Background

Te Whare Punanga Takawaenga Atawhai O Aorere/Aorere College Childcare Centre provides all day care and education for up to 40 children between three months and five years of age. It is located within the grounds of Aorere College and operates under a management committee that report to the college’s Board of Trustees.

A centre manager is responsible for the daily management of the centre and the learning programme provided for children. The input of students from Aorere College enriches the centre curriculum and enables children to be part of a wider community.

The 2012 ERO report noted the positive tone of the programme provided for children. Children were supported by a learning environment and centre routines that promoted strong tuakana/teina relationships. They were confident, articulate, and persevered in their play. Adult interactions with children focused on modelling good language development in English and children’s home languages. These positive elements remain evident in this 2013 ERO report.

The 2012 ERO report also identified concerns about quality of curriculum implementation, leadership and management practices, and self review. This 2013 ERO review finds good progress has been made in all these areas. A new centre manager was appointed in April 2012. The centre is now staffed above minimum ratios with a high number of qualified staff. The centre is now much better placed to promote positive outcomes for children.

Areas of progress

Effective leadership is a significant factor in contributing to the improvements in the centre. The centre manager and assistant manager foster collaborative ways of working. They are building an environment of trust where staff work as a team and contribute to decisions made in the centre. Centre managers provide teachers with clear direction and expectations for their professional practice. They have developed processes for performance management in consultation with staff, and these are ready for implementation. These processes are designed to support teachers to reflect on their practice, and continue to grow their capacity to meet the diverse needs of children in the centre.

Changes made to the indoor learning environment are supporting children’s interests and have the potential to extend their learning. The development of different areas of play allows more resources to be readily available to children and supports them to make independent decisions about their play. Teachers are trialling planning and assessment processes to enable the programme to be more responsive to individual children.

The centre manager and staff are developing good administration and self-review systems that focus on improving outcomes for children. The annual plan shows clear links to the outcomes of self review and is supporting future directions for the management committee and staff. Good systems are now in place for monitoring sleeping children.

Areas for further improvement

The centre manager and ERO agree that areas for further improvement include the centre manager and staff:

  • continuing to develop teachers’ planning, assessment and evaluation practices that promote individual children’s learning
  • building on teacher’s knowledge and current learning theories to use the environment as the third teacher
  • considering ways to support the ongoing leadership development of centre managers.

Next ERO Review

When is ERO likely to review the service again?

The next ERO review of Te Whare Punanga Takawaenga Atawhai O Aorere will be in three years.
