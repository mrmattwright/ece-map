Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mōrehu o Uenuku ki Raetihi. He ākonga pākiki, he ākonga tūmāia hoki ngā tamariki. Ka arotahi te whānau ki ngā whakapaitanga ka hāpai ake i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i tētahi taiao akoranga e poipoi ana i ngā tamariki katoa. He harikoa ngā tamariki i a rātou e noho ana ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mōrehu o Uenuku i roto i te toru o ngā tau. 

1 Te Horopaki

Ko Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mōrehu o Uenuku tētahi o ngā kōhanga reo e rua ki Raetihi. He hononga ā-whakapapa tō te katoa o ngā kaimahi me te whānau. I tēnei wā, he nui ngā mahi whakahou ake ki ngā whare o te kōhanga reo, ā, kei te whakahou ake anō hoki i te wāhi o waho.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mōrehu o Uenuku will be in three years.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te kite i te tūmāia o ngā tamariki hei ākonga?

He ākonga tūmāia, he ākonga pākiki hoki ngā tamariki.

Mana Tangata

Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga e whai pūtake ana ki a rātou. Ka hāpai, ka akiaki hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te kōkiri i ā rātou ake akoranga. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā rautaki e pā ana ki te whakahikohiko whakaaro me te hīraurau hopanga. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te whānuitanga o te wāhi o waho. He ākonga harikoa, he ākonga mauritau anō hoki ngā tamariki.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana i a rātou. Ka kitea tō rātou māramatanga ki ngā tūmomo āheinga reo i ngā wā karakia me te wā mihimihi. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki ka hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i te mōhiotanga me ngā māramatanga ki te reo Māori.

E whai pūtake ana ngā tino hononga o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hoa me ō rātou pākeke. He pai ā rātou taunekeneke ki ētahi atu. Kei te tino mōhio ngā kaimahi ki ā rātou tamariki, ā, ka whakanui rātou i ngā whakamātau me ngā whakatutukitanga a ngā tamariki. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te aroha me te manaaki ki te kōhanga reo. He aronga toi whenuatanga tō ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore te reo Māori i te reo matua o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki te kōhanga reo. Kōrero ai ngā tamariki me te whānau i te reo Pākehā i ia te wā ki a rātou anō. E mōhio ana te whānau, me whakatupu ake rātou i tō rātou māramatanga, tā rātou whakamahinga anō hoki i te reo Māori, hei hāpai ake i ā rātou tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. E hāngai ana te tūtohinga ki te rā, ā, e mau ana hoki i ngā aratohu mārama e pā ana ki ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga, tae atu hoki ki te hauora me te haumaru. Ka whakawhiti kōrero mō ngā āhuatanga matua o ngā whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo ki ngā hui ā-whānau. Ka hāngai ngā pūrongo a ngā kaimahi ki te hōtaka akoranga. He mārama ki ngā kaimahi ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga, me ā rātou whiwhinga. He tirohanga tā te mahere rautaki ki ngā kaupapa matua o te pae tata me te pae tawhiti ki te kōhanga reo. Ko te whakahou ake i te whare me te wāhi tākaro o waho tētahi kaupapa matua ki te whānau. Ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā whakataunga katoa ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He ngoikore ngā mahi whakamahere, te aromatawai, me te aromātai. Kāhore anō ngā arohaehae kia whakatutukihia, hei tāutu i ngā matea whakawhanaketanga ngaio o ngā kaimahi. He iti noa ngā mōhiohio e tohu ana i te āhua o ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakapai ake i te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga mō ngā tamariki.

Kāhore anō ētahi āhuatanga o te hauora me te haumaru kia whakaritea, tae atu ki ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te whanonga o ngā tamariki, me te arowhai auau i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. Hei whakapai ake i te āhua o āna whakaritenga, me tahuri te whānau ki te:

  • arotake me te whakatinana i tētahi pūnaha e pā ana ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea, me tētahi kaupapa here, tukanga hoki mō te whakahaere i te whanonga o ngā tamariki, mō te haumaru me te ārai i ngā tamariki 

[R 46 HS1, 8, 12, 25, 28 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008] 

  • āta whakapau kaha ki te whakarato i te tautoko ngaio, i ngā whai wāhitanga whakawhanaketanga ngaio me ngā rauemi ki ngā kaimahi, mā tētahi pūnaha arohaehae e tika ana.

[R 47 GM1-12 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008 GMA8]  

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā:

  • kia tāutu te whānau i ngā rautaki ka whakapai ake i te kounga o te reo Māori mō ngā tamariki
  • kia whakarite i tētahi pūnaha ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea me ngā kaupapa here, ngā tukanga hoki e pā ana ki te whakahaere i te whanonga o ngā tamariki
  • kia whakatutuki ngā arohaehae a ngā kaimahi.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mōrehu o Uenuku ki Raetihi. He ākonga pākiki, he ākonga tūmāia hoki ngā tamariki. Ka arotahi te whānau ki ngā whakapaitanga ka hāpai ake i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i tētahi taiao akoranga e poipoi ana i ngā tamariki katoa. He harikoa ngā tamariki i a rātou e noho ana ki te kōhanga reo.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mōrehu o Uenuku is in Raetihi. Children are inquisitive, confident learners. The whānau focus on improvements that support children’s learning. The kaimahi provide a learning environment that caters for all children. The children are happy during their time at kōhanga reo. 

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mōrehu o Uenuku will be in three years. 

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mōrehu o Uenuku is one of two kōhanga reo in Raetihi. The kaimahi and whānau are all connected through whakapapa. The kōhanga reo is undergoing some major renovation work to the buildings as well as upgrading the outdoor area.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children show that they are confident learners?

Children are confident, inquisitive learners.

Mana Tangata

Children engage in learning that is meaningful to them. Kaimahi support and encourage children to initiate their own learning. Children learn strategies for active thinking and problem solving. Children are able to explore the extensive outdoor area. Children are happy and contented learners. 

Children learn te reo Māori in a supportive environment. They demonstrate an understanding of language functions during karakia and mihimihi.  Kaimahi use a range of strategies to support children’s language development. Children are developing knowledge and understanding of te reo Māori. 

Children have established meaningful relationships with their peers and adults. They have positive interactions with others. Kaimahi know their children well and acknowledge children’s efforts and achievements.   Children experience aroha and manaaki at the kōhanga reo. Children have a sense of belonging at the kōhanga reo.

Key Next Steps

Te reo Māori is not the main language of communication at the kōhanga reo. Children and whānau regularly speak English to each other. Whānau acknowledge that they need to grow their understanding and use of te reo Māori to better support their children. 

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau manage the kōhanga reo well. The charter is up to date and includes clear guidelines for policies and procedures including health and safety. Discussion around key aspects of the operations and management of the kōhanga reo occur at whānau hui. Kaimahi reports focus on the learning programme. Kaimahi are clear about their roles, responsibilities and entitlements. The strategic plan provides insights in to the short and long term priorities of the kōhanga reo. Building renovations and the outdoor play area are priorities for the whānau. Children are the focus of decisions made at the kōhanga reo.

Key Next Steps

Planning, assessment and evaluation are weak. Appraisals have not been carried out to identify kaimahi professional development needs. There is very little information to show how kaimahi and whānau are improving the quality of the learning programme for children.

Some aspects of health and safety are not in place including child behaviour management procedures and regular hazard management checks.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements. To improve current practice the whānau must:

  • review and implement a hazard management system and child behaviour management policy and procedure for the safety and protection of children
    [R46 HS1, 8, 12, 25, 28 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008] 
  • ensure all reasonable steps are taken to provide staff employed professional support, professional development opportunities and resources through an appropriate appraisal system.

[R47 GM1-12 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008 GMA8]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • whānau to identify strategies to improve the quality of te reo Māori for the children
  • establish a hazard management system and child behaviour management policy and procedures
  • carry out kaimahi appraisals.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mōrehu o Uenuku is in Raetihi. Children are inquisitive, confident learners. The whānau focus on improvements that support children’s learning. The kaimahi provide a learning environment that caters for all children. The children are happy during their time at kōhanga reo. 

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Mōrehu o Uenuku will be in three years.
