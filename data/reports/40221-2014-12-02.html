1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Pirimai ki te pū o Whanganui, e pā tata atu ana ki te awa o Whanganui. Ka whakarato te whānau i tētahi taiao haumaru, atawhai hoki e manaaki ana, e whakanui ana hoki i ngā tamariki.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko te tuakiri me te aronga toi whenuatanga te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. I whai wāhi atu rātou ki te waihanga o tā rātou pātai aromātai e ārahi ana i te arotake a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i tēnei āhuatanga, arā:

‘He pēhea te whai hua ki te poipoi i ā tātou tamariki kia whanake ai te tino tuakiri, te tino aronga toi whenuatanga anō hoki?’

E tino poipoia ana ngā tamariki, ā, e whanake ana i te tino tuakiri, te tino aronga toi whenuatanga hoki.

He atawhai, he manaaki hoki ki ngā hononga i waenga i ngā tamariki me te whānau. Kei ngā tuākana ki te tiaki i ō rātou tēina. Ka noho tahi ngā whakatupuranga hei hāpai i ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo nei. Ko ngā hononga ā-whakapapa ki waenga i te whānau me ngā tamariki e whai pānga ana ki te kaha o te aronga toi whenuatanga me te whanaungatanga. Ka kitea te whakaute o ngā tamariki mō rātou anō, mō ētahi atu, ā, mō ngā kaimahi hoki. Ka kitea hoki te māia o ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i a rātou ake anō, me tō rātou tūrangawaewae.

Ko te whānau – tae atu ki ētahi tino kuia – ka whakarato i te tūāpapa e poipoi ana, e whakarauora ana hoki i te reo, ngā tikanga, ngā mātauranga Māori, me ngā kōrero tuku iho. Ka whakanuia te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki, ki te kōrero me te whakawhiti whakaaro mā te reo Māori. Ko te nuinga o ngā tamariki ka whakamahi, ka kōrero hoki i te reo Māori ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo horopaki ōkawa, ōpaki hoki.

Ko ngā ngākau nuitanga me te waiora o ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā mahi whakahaere, ngā whakaritenga hoki a te whānau. He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. He matawhānui ā rātou tukanga, ā rātou hanganga hoki mō ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. Ka arotake whaiaro i ia te wā, ā, ka whai pānga tēnei ki te whakapai tonutanga. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki te mātauranga o ā rātou tamariki. Ka noho ngākau nui rātou ki te whakapūmau i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo.

Hei aha koa, kāhore ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki e aromātai ana i ia te wā, kāhore rānei i te rawaka te aroturuki a ngā kaimahi i tā rātou ahu whakamua. Me whakamahine ngā whakaritenga o tēnei wā, hei whakapai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

‘He pēhea te whai hua ki te poipoi i ā tātou tamariki kia whanake ai te tino tuakiri, te tino aronga toi whenuatanga anō hoki?’

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā taumahi i waihangatia ai hei whakawhanake i tō rātou āheinga ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Kōrero Māori ai ngā kaimahi i ngā wā katoa. Ka whakamahi rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo kēmu, taumahi hoki kia hihiri ai ngā akoranga mā ngā tamariki. He tākare te taki a ngā tamariki i ngā karakia, ngā waiata, me ō rātou ake pepeha. He pakari hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi rātou i te reo Māori ki te whānuitanga o ngā horopaki ōkawa, ōpaki hoki.

Ka taea e ngā tamariki tuākana te whakapuaki ō rātou whakaaro e pā ana ki a rātou anō, ki te whānau, ā, ki te kōhanga reo hoki. Ka whakarato te whānau i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo whai wāhitanga, taumahi hoki kia ako ai ngā tamariki i ngā kōrero e pā ana ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae hoki. E whakanui ngā tamariki i ō rātou hononga ā-whānau, ā-hapū, ā-iwi hoki mā ngā pepeha me te whakapapa. Ka waiata rātou i ngā waiata e whakapūmau ana i aua hononga. He tino kaha te tuakiri, te aronga toi whenuatanga hoki o ngā tamariki.

E ākina ana ngā tamariki kia tūhura takitahi, ki te taha hoki o ō rātou hoa. He maha ā rātou whai wāhitanga ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga, te mahi auaha, me te hīraurau hopanga. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi ki ngā tūmomo horopaki ako, tae atu ki ngā akoranga ā-kaupapa, ngā taumahi e kōkirihia ana e ngā pākeke, te tākaro takitahi, me ngā taumahi e pā ana ki ngā tikanga. He pai te whakatutuki a ngā kaimahi i ngā tūmomo matea ake o ngā momo rōpū reanga. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki te hōtaka akoranga, ā, e tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo, ki waenga hoki i tō rātou whānau.

E ako ana ngā tamariki ki te tiaki i a rātou anō, i ētahi atu hoki. Kei ngā tuakana ki te tiaki i ō rātou tēina. He tautoko ā-whakatupuranga anō hoki, ā, ka tiaki ngā kuia i ngā tamariki, nā runga i te noho pūmau ki te waiora, te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki mō meāke nei. Ko te kaha o ngā hononga ā-whakapapa o te whānau me ngā tamariki e hāpai ana i te aronga toi whenuatanga me te whanaungatanga. Ka kitea te whakaute o ngā tamariki mō ō rātou hoa, mō ngā kaimahi hoki.

He pai ngā taiao akoranga kua whakaritea, ā, e tākirikiri nei hoki i ngā tamariki ki te ako. Ka ākina ngā tamariki kia tūhura tō rātou taiao, tae atu ki te kōhanga reo, te marae, te awa, me ngā wāhi motuhake o te hapori. E noho wātea ana ngā ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā rauemi o roto, o waho hoki, ā, e tika ana aua rānei mō ō rātou tūmomo reanga, tūmomo āheinga hoki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te tūhura, te ako hoki i te taiao o tō rātou hapori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ko ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā matea o ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o te whakatau a te whānau i ngā whakaritenga, me ngā whakahaeretanga. Ka mahi nahanaha te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. He kaha te aronga rautaki ki te whakawhanake i te āheinga o te whānau ki te whakahaere i tō rātou kōhanga reo i roto i te roanga o te wā. He matawhānui ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga a te whānau, kia whai hua ai te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

He pai rawa ngā pūnaha kua whakaritea hei ārahi i te taha pūtea. He whānui te aroturuki, te pūrongo hoki e whakatutukihia ana puta noa i ngā wāhanga maha o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka whai hua te whānau me ngā kaimahi nā te tautoko, ngā kaupapa whakangungu hoki o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. E tino whakahaerehia ana ngā mahi o ia rā ki te kōhanga reo.

E whakawhānuihia ana ngā mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori, nā te āta whakaritenga o ngā tukanga whakamahere. Nā te whai huatanga o ngā mahere, he mārama te aronga mā ngā kaimahi. Ka tautoko, ka whai wāhi hoki te whānau ki te whakamahere ngātahi, me te arotahi atu ki ngā matea o ngā tamariki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako, ā, ka wheako i ngā hōtaka i āta whakamaheretia ai.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Me whakawhiti kōrero ngaio ngā kaiako mō ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki, me te hōtaka akoranga. Me whakarite ngā kaimahi i te tino aroturukitanga o te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki i ia te wā. He ōpaki ngā mahi aromātai hōtaka i tēnei wā.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakiia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o okiuh, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

Me toro atu te whānau ki te āwhina o te rōpū tautoko o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo hei:

  • ako i te aroturuki me te aromātai i te hōtaka.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pirimai is based in central Whanganui a short distance from the Whanganui river. Whanau provide a safe and warm environment where children are welcomed and valued.

The kōhanga reo whānau chose identity and belonging as the focus area for this review. They participated in the design of their evaluation question which guides ERO’s review of this area:

‘How well are our children nurtured to develop a strong sense of identity and belonging?’

Children are well nurtured and are developing a strong sense of identity and belonging

Children and whānau share warm, caring relationships. The tuakana look after their teina. There is intergenerational support for children in the kōhanga reo. Close kinship ties amongst the whānau and children help support a strong sense of belonging and whanaungatanga. Children show respect for themselves, each other and kaimahi. Children show confidence in being able to express who they are and where they are from.

Whānau, including a group of kuia, provide a strong base where te reo, tikanga, cultural knowledge and history is nurtured and thrives. Te reo Māori me ōna tikanga is valued. Whānau are active participants alongside their children in speaking and communicating in te reo Māori. Most children use and speak te reo Māori in various formal and informal settings.

Children’s future interests and wellbeing are central to whānau management and practices. Whānau manage the kōhanga reo well. They have comprehensive processes and structures for different aspects of the kōhanga reo operations. Self review is constant and leads to ongoing improvement. Whānau are actively involved in their children’s education. They are committed to upholding the kaupapa of kōhanga reo.

Nevertheless children’s learning is not regularly evaluated and neither do kaimahi adequately monitor their progress. Existing practices need refinement to improve children’s learning and development.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose ngā taumata whakahirahira as its focus area.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

‘How well are our children nurtured to develop a strong sense of identity and belonging?’

Areas of good performance

Children participate in a range of activities designed to develop their ability to communicate in te reo Māori. Kaimahi speak Māori at all times. They use a variety of games and activities to make language learning exciting for children. Children eagerly recite karakia, waiata and their own pepeha. Children are confident speakers of te reo Māori. They use te reo Māori in a range of formal and informal settings.

Older children can share their views and thoughts about themselves, whānau and the kōhanga reo. Whānau provide a range of opportunities and activities for children to learn about who they are and where they are from. Children acknowledge their whānau, hapu and iwi connections through pepeha and whakapapa. They sing waiata which endorse these links. Children have a strong sense of identity and belonging.

Children are encouraged to explore independently and with their friends. They have several opportunities to follow their interests, be creative and problem solve. Children are able to participate in various learning contexts including kaupapa learning, adult led activities, independent play and tikanga activities. Kaimahi cater well for the specific needs of the different age groups. Whānau take an active role in the learning programme and are fully supportive of the children. Children are happy and content in the kōhanga reo and amongst their whānau.

Children are learning to take care of themselves and others. The tuakana look after their teina. There is intergenerational support for children from kuia who are committed to their future wellbeing and development. Close kinship ties amongst the whānau and children help support a strong sense of belonging and whanaungatanga. Children show respect toward each other and kaimahi.

Children learn in well organised, stimulating learning environments. Children are encouraged to explore their local environment including the kōhanga reo, marae, river and special places in the local area. Children have easy access to a range of indoor and outdoor resources which are suitable for their varying ages and abilities. Children enjoy exploring and learning about their local environment.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

Children’s interests and needs are central to whānau decision making and organisation. Whānau work in a planned way to manage the kōhanga reo. There is a strong strategic focus on developing the ability of whānau to manage their kōhanga over a long term.Whanau have sound systems in place to guide them with the management of their finances. Extensive monitoring and reporting occurs across several areas of the kōhanga reo operations. Whānau and kaimahi benefit from the Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust support and training. Whānau are actively involved in the management of the kōhanga reo. The kōhanga reo is successfully managed on a day to day basis.have comprehensive policies and procedures to effectively manage the kōhanga reo. They

Children’s knowledge of te ao Māori extended through deliberate planning procedures. Effective planning gives clear direction to kaimahi. Whānau support and participate in shared planning that focuses on children’s needs. Children enjoy learning and experience well planned programmes.

Areas for development

Kaiako should hold professional discussions about children’s needs, interests and the learning programme. Kaimahi should ensure that children’s progress is effectively monitored on a regular basis. Existing practices for programme evaluation is informal.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO LicensedKōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

The whānau should seek further assistance from the national trust support team to:

  • Learn about programme monitoring and evaluation.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
