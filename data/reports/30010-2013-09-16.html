ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Walton Playcentre is located in a rural setting, near Matamata, in the grounds of Walton School. It provides two sessions a week of education and care for a maximum of 26 children, including up to 10 under two years. The centre continues to maintain close links with the neighbouring school.

The centre is a parent cooperative and operates under the umbrella of the East Waikato Playcentre Association. It has been relicensed by the Ministry of Education under the Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008. After a period of significant membership change, there is now a small core group of enthusiastic parents who share responsibility for centre roles and have completed initial playcentre training. The association employs a session coordinator who has higher levels of playcentre training to enable both sessions to meet funding requirements.

The centre has a positive reporting history. Since the 2010 ERO review, parents have made some progress in clarifying the purpose of individual children‚Äôs portfolios and documenting children‚Äôs participation in the programme. As identified in the 2010 ER0 report, there continues to be a need for the association to review the extent and availability of support for centre members.

The centre‚Äôs philosophy is for parents to facilitate and extend their children‚Äôs learning and play.

This review was part of a cluster of 10 playcentre reviews in the East Waikato Playcentre Association.

The Review Findings

A core group of enthusiastic parents takes collective responsibility for centre management. Parent members have benefited from association training about their roles, and association support with the relicensing process. These parents have made good use of their own knowledge and experience to develop a strategic plan and determine centre priorities. They use an effective combination of formal and informal review to make decisions about ongoing improvement and development.

Members place a strong emphasis on welcoming new parents and fostering emergent leadership. They respond with flexibility, empathy and understanding to the unique needs of rural families. A next step for centre leaders is to identify desired outcomes for self reviews and how they might evaluate their effectiveness. ERO agrees with centre members about the need to further review and upgrade learning environments, including the outdoor environment.

The centre programme is responsive to children‚Äôs interests and includes a wide variety of adult-initiated activities designed to extend children‚Äôs learning. These activities include trips to places of interest, celebrations and seasonal activities. Children‚Äôs learning and participation in the programme is well documented in individual learning books, wall displays and centre activity books. Children enjoy revisiting their learning and sharing this with other children and adults. They also have many opportunities to develop early literacy and mathematical concepts and a love of books. Adults also encourage children to explore aspects of natural science and to develop observation skills. Centre members and ERO agree that a next step would be to further incorporate aspects of MƒÅori language and culture. This should extend children‚Äôs understanding and appreciation of Aotearoa/New Zealand as a bicultural society.

Adults‚Äô interactions and relationships with children are respectful, responsive and affirming. Parents use rich language in conversations to extend children‚Äôs thinking. These conversations support children to become capable and confident communicators. Parents work alongside children at their level and make effective use of humour, singing and modelling as teaching strategies. The curriculum and parent interactions foster children‚Äôs independence and promote exploration and experimentation. Children bring their knowledge of farming life to their play and adults make strong links to home, integrating this into the life of the centre.

Policies and procedures developed by the association contribute to the effective operation of the service. Members are keen to continue their training when the association re-establishes training and courses in their area. As part of this review, ERO evaluated the effectiveness of the centre‚Äôs responsibilities for employment and management of staff.

Key Next Steps

The association implemented effective procedures when employing the session coordinator. A next step is to strengthen appraisal processes by including clear indicators for quality practice for playcentre education. Regular feedback and guidance from a suitably experienced and knowledgeable appraiser will assist the implementation of these processes. Parents have also identified the need to review the session coordinator‚Äôs job description to reflect the centre‚Äôs changing needs and priorities.

Centre sustainability would be enhanced with a strengthening of association support for members, such as regular visits and constructive feedback and advice from well-informed association personnel.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Walton Playcentre completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.
