1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kei te taha o Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Te Ara Hou ki Ahuriri Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ara Hou. Nō tēnei rohe tonu ngā tamariki o tēnei kōhanga reo. He kaha te aro o te whānau ki te ao Māori tae atu ana ki te reo Māori. Kei te hiahia te whānau kia ako a rātou tamariki ki tētahi taiao kaupapa Māori e whakanui ana i tō rātou tipuranga katoa me to rātou motuhaketanga. Kei te kaha tonu ngā mema katoa o te whānau ki te whakapai ake i ō rātou pūkenga ki te reo Māori hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

I kōwhiri te whānau kia noho ko Mana Tangata hei wāhi arotahi mō tēnei arotake.He mea nui ki te whānau te mōhio ko wai koe, nō whea hoki koe. Nā te angitū o ngā noho marae o mua i āhei ai ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi ki ngā tikanga Māori, te ao Māori me te whanaungatanga. Koinei hoki ētahi tino āhua o te toi whenua.

He nui te atawhai o ngā tamariki ki tēnā, ki tēnā o rātouHe pai te whakarite i ngā mahi o ia rā. Kei te mōhio ngā tamariki ki ngā wāhi me noho rātou kia haumaru ai rātou i a rātou e tuhura haere ana ki tō rātou taiao. He pai te tiaki i ngā tamariki nohinohi, ā, he tōtika hoki te tautoko i a rātou ki te ako. He pai te tākaro me te mahi tahi a ngā tamariki.. Ka whakahaere ngā tuakana i ngā mahi o ia rā me ngā kaupapa mahi. Ka tautoko rātou i a rātou taina.

He pai te tiaki me te whakarite i ngā rawa me te taiao o waho, o roto hoki. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki kei te whakawhiti ki te kura ki ngā mahi i te kura e tika ana mō rātou. He whānau, he tuakana, taina tō ētahi o ngā tamariki i te kura. Ka āwhina tēnei i tō rātou whakawhiti maheni ki te kura.

He ahurea tino mōhiohio, he āhuatanga whai whakaaro hoki kei ngā kaimahi. Ka whai wāhi hoki rātou ki ngā whakangungu e hiahia ana rātou te whai. Kua tūtuki pai ai ngā tohu e tika ana mō ētahi o rātou. Kua pūmau tēnei tīma mahi i runga i ngā tautoko me ngā ārahi ā-roto, te pai o te whakarite i ngā tūranga me ngā kawenga, me ngā tikanga mahi i āta kite.

Ka kitea te pai o ngā mahi whakamahere ki ngā tuhinga ā-tau, ā-wahanga o te tau, ā- wiki. He pai te whakarite o ngā pūkete, ā, kei ēnei ngā kōrero nui e pā ana ki ngā tino akoranga o ngā tamariki. Ka whakawhiti kōrero ngā kaimahi i ia wā me te tuhi i ngā kōrero aromātai i ngā kaupapa ako.

Kei te whakaae mai te whānau me whai whakaaro anō, me mahi anō i ngā whakamahere rautaki. Me tahuri tonu te whānau ki te whakaōkawa i tō rātou tirohanga roa me o rātou wawata mō a rātou tamariki me te kōhanga reo. Kei te whanake tonu ngā mahi arotake whaiaro. Kei reira ētahi mahi aroturuki me te tuku pūrongo i runga i ngā mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga reo kāore i te maheretia, kāore rānei i te mahi i ia wā. Me kaha tonu te whānau ki te whakapai ake i ō rātou mōhio me ō rātou mahi arotake whaiaro.

Ngā Mahi ā Meake Nei

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Titiro Hāngai a te Arotake

I mua atu i te whakahaerenga o te arotake, i ïnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ara Hou kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake mā te whai i ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ki a rātou.

Ï muri mai i ngā whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenganui i te rōpū arotake o Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau, ka whakaritea e rātou ngā tino kaupapa motuhake hei arotakehanga. I titiro ēnei whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero ki runga i ngā kōrero i te pupuritia e te kōhanga reo (e whai wāhia anangā kōrero arotake whaiaro),ā, me te whānuitanga o ngā take pūmanawa hei arotakehanga i runga i ngā putanga e whai hua ana ki ngā mokopuna oTe Kōhanga Reo o Te Ara Hou.

Ko te kaupapa i whakaritea e te whānau hei titirohanga mā tēnei arotake ko:

  • Mana Tangata.

E titiro ana te katoa o ngā arotake a ERO i roto i ngā kōhanga reo, ki runga i te kounga o te mātauranga. E ai ki a ERO, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o:

  • ngā arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me ngā whakamāhere rautaki
  • ngā whakamāhere o ngā hōtaka akoranga, ngā aro matawai me ngā aro mātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Tangata

He whakamārama

He mea nui ki te whānau te mōhio ko wai koe, nō whea hoki koe. Ka whakatairanga te whānau i ngā wāriu o te whakapapa me te whanaungatanga me ngā tamariki i te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Mana Tangata. He mea nui te whanaungatanga ki te akoranga o ngā tamariki. Ko ētahi o ngā mahi o ia rā ko te taki pepeha me te whakapapa me te ako ki ngā hononga ki te whānau whānui me te hapori. Nā te angitū o ngā noho marae o mua i āhei ai ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi ki ngā tikanga Māori, te ao Māori me te whanaungatanga. Koinei hoki ētahi tino āhua o te toi whenua. Na te mau i ngā tamariki ki te marae kua tū rangatira rātou me te mōhio ko wai rātou. He pai te whanake o te mōhio o ngā tamariki ko wai rātou, nō whea hoki rātou.

Te reo Māori. He kaha te aro o te whānau ki te ao Māori tae atu ana ki te reo Māori. Kei te kaha tonu ngā mema katoa o te whānau ki te whakapai ake i ō rātou pūkenga ki te reo Māori hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki. Ka whai wāhi ngā kaimahi me tō rātou rata ki te ako i te reo Māori mā ētahi whakangungu. He wāhi reo Māori anake kua tohua ki te kōhanga reo. Ka tuhia ngā pānui ki te reo Māori. Hei tāpiri atu ki tēnei, ka whakahaeretia ētahi wāhanga o te hui ā-whānau ki te reo Māori. Ka kōrero, ka waiata, ka pānui, ka tohutohu, ka whakahaere, ka uiui, ka whakawhiti kōrero hoki ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. He māia ngā tamariki nohinohi ki te whai wāhi ki ngā mahi ahakoa te aha me te āhei ki te mahi me te whakawhiti kōrero me ō rātou tuakana. Ka whakanui rātou mō te tuhura me te whakamātau i ngā oro me te reo. Ka kīnaki ngā kupu hou ka whakamahi ki te horopaki tika kia puāwai te reo o ngā tamariki. He tauira pai ngā tuakana mō ā rātou taina. He pai te whanake o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki.

Te mahi takitahi. He pai te whakarite i ngā mahi o ia rā. Kei te mōhio ngā tamariki ki ngā wāhi me noho rātou kia haumaru ai rātou i a rātou e tuhura haere ana ki tō rātou taiao. He pai te tiaki i ngā tamariki, ā, he tōtika hoki te tautoko i a rātou ki te ako. He pai te tākaro me te mahi tahi ā ngā tamariki. Ka āta mātaki ngā kaimahi i a rātou. He totika te rahi me te whānui o ngā rauemi mā ngā tamariki pakeke. He kaha ngā tamariki te tiaki i a rātou anō, ā, he pai hoki ki a rātou tō rātou taiao mahana, tau, whakaihiihi hoki. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki te whakatau i ō rātou ake kaupapa. Ka whakahaere ngā tuakana i ētahi o ngā kaupapa nui o te rā me ngā mahi o ia rā. Ka tautoko ēnei tamariki i a rātou taina me ngā mahi takatika. He kaimahi whakarite ēnei me te whakutu ki ngā tono me ngā matea o ngā tamariki. He pai ki ngā tamariki tō rātou taiao me ngā whai wāhitanga ki te tuhura me te whai i ō rātou e ngākaunui ana.

Te taiao o te kōhanga reo. He pai te tiaki me te whakarite i ngā rawa me te taiao o waho, o roto hoki. E rima tau te pakeke o ngā tamariki, ā ka puta i te kōhanga reo, kātahi, ka haere ki te kura. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki ngā mahi e tika ana me ngā mahi whakanui ki te kura. He whānau tō ētahi o ngā tamariki ki te kura. Ka āwhina ēnei āhua i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti maheni ki te kura. Kei te kōhanga reo ngā wāhi tauwhāiti mō tēnā, mō tēnā o ngā rōpū pakeke ki reira rātou e puku mahi ai. He taiao ātaahua, whakaihiihi hoki te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki ki te ako me te tipu.

Te ārahi me te tautoko i ngā kaimahi. He ahurea tino mōhiohio, he āhuatanga whai whakaaro hoki kei ngā kaimahi. Ka whai wāhi hoki rātou ki ngā whakangungu e hiahia ana rātou te whai. Kua tūtuki pai ai ngā tohu e tika ana mō ētahi o rātou. Kua pūmau tēnei tīma mahi i runga i ngā tautoko me ngā ārahi ā-roto, te pai o te whakarite i ngā tūranga me ngā kawenga, me ngā tikanga mahi i āta kite. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki i ngā kaimahi e ngākaunui ana ki te noho ki te aroaro o ngā mea hou e puta mai ana ki te ao mātauranga.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te whakatutuki i ngā matea akoranga o ngā tuakana.Me tahuri ngā kaimahi ināianei ki te whakarato i ētahi atu wero ki ngā tuakana. Ko te hiraurau hopanga, te mahi takitahi i ngā kaupapa mahi rānei ko ngā whakaaturanga a rōpū ētahi mahi ka taea te mahi hei āwhina ki te whakaihiihi i ngā taumata whakaaro ki roto i ngā tamariki. He pākiki ngā tamariki, ā, he pai ki a rātou te wero.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

E aro mātai ana a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i ngā wā katoa, i te kounga o ngā whakamahere me ngā aro mātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo ki te titiro ki te kaha o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere me te aro turuki i te katoa o ngā wāhanga whakahaere o te kōhanga mō a ratou tamariki me te whānau. Kei roto i te wāhanga o te whakamahere me te aro mātai ko te whakamahere hōtaka me te aro turuki i te whanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakamahere. Ka kitea te pai o ngā mahi whakamahere ki ngā tuhinga ā-tau, ā- wahanga o te tau, ā-wiki. He mārama ngā hononga i waenganui i ngā kaupapa puta noa i ngā wāhanga e whā o te tau. Ka whai wāhi te whānau ki te tuku whakaaro. Nā te pai o te whakamahere o ngā kaupapa ako i āhei ai ngā tamariki ki te tuhura me te whakamātau i te maha o ngā āhua o te ao Māori.

Te aromatawai. Kei te mārama ki ngā kaimahi te nui o te aromatawai me tōnā take. Ka āta mātaki rātou, ka whakawhiti kōrero kātahi, ka whakatau kaupapa e pā ana ki ngā āhua o ngā tamariki. He pai te whakarite a ngā pūkete, ā, kei ēnei ngā kōrero nui e pā ana ki ngā tino akoranga o ngā tamariki. He tino kaupapa ngā kōrero paki o ngā kaimahi e pā ana ki te akoranga o ngā tamariki ki ngā tamariki me o rātou whānau. He pai ki ngā tamariki me ō rātou whānau te pānui i ngā pūkete.

Ngā mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. He pai te whakahaere o te whānau i ētahi o ngā wāhanga o te kōhanga reo. He mārama ngā tukanga mō te whakahaere kaimahi, ngā aromhi, ngā tukanga whakahaere hui, te paerangatiratanga, ngā take ā-pūtea, me te haumaru me te hauora. Ka hāngai ki te wā tonu ngā rehita, te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa here me ngā kōrero e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi, ā, he pai te whakataha i ēnei.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te aromatai. Ka whakawhiti kōrero ngā kaimahi i ia wā me te tuhi i ngā kōrero aromātai i ngā kaupapa ako. Ka taea e rātou ināianei te whakawhānui ake i tēnei mahi kia tae atu ki te aromātai i ngā mahi o ia rā, ngā mahi āta mātaki i ngā tamariki, me ngā matea whanake o ngā tamariki. Mā te whakawhānui i te tirotiro ki ngā mahi aromātai e whai hua ai te ako me te whanake o ngā tamariki.

Te whakamahere rautaki. Ka whakaae mai te whānau me mahi tonu rātou ki te whakapai ake i ngā mahi whakamahere rautaki. Kua tāutu rātou i ētahi whāinga rautaki e hāngai ana ki Te Ara Tūāpae. He whānui rawa ētahi o ēnei whāinga. Kāore he mahere hei whakaatu ka pēhea, āhea ranei ēnei whāinga e whakatutuki. Me tahuri tonu te whānau ki te whakaōkawa i tō rātou tirohanga me o rātou wawata mō te wā roa, mō ā rātou tamariki me te kōhanga reo.

Te arotake whaiaro. Kei te whanake tonu ngā mahi arotake whaiaro. Me kaha tonu te whānau ki te whakapai ake i ō rātou mōhio me ō rātou mahi arotake whaiaro. Ka aroturuki, ka tuku pūrongo te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ia wā i runga i ō rātou mahi whakahaere pūtea me ngā whai wāhitanga whakawhanake ngaiotanga. Ka arotake rātou i te tūtohinga i ētahi wā me ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga. Kei reira ētahi mahi aroturuki me te tuku pūrongo i runga i ngā mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga reo kāore i te maheretia, kāore rānei i te mahi i ia wā. Me tahuri tonu te whānau ki te whakapai ake i ō rātou mōhio ki te arotake whaiaro. Ka āwhina tēnei i te whānau ki te aroturuki me te whakapai ake i te tōtika o ngā mahi o te kōhanga reo.

4 Ngā Kupu Tūturu ā te Whānau Whakahaere mō ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua atu i te whakahaerenga o te arotake, i whakatutukitia e te kaipupuri o te raihana, te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ara Hou tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhituhinga i ōati rātou i kaha rātou ki te whakatutuki i ngā hanganga ture pa ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaere ā te whānau i to rātou kōhanga
  • te hauora, te waiora me te oranga tinana
  • ngā whakahaere o ngā kaiako me nga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaere o te pūtea, ngā rawa me ngā taonga.

I te wā i whakahaeretia ai te arotake, i āta titirohia e te Tari Arotake Matauranga ēnei take e whai ake nei, notemea, e whai wāhi nui ana rātou i runga i ngā hua e puta ana ki ngā mokopuna:

  • te āhuru aronganui o ngā mokopuna (tāpiri atu ki ngā whakahaere o ngā whanonga o ngā mokopuna, te ārai i ngā mahi whakawetiweti me ngā mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru-ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ki te whakatau tamariki) ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, te tiaki i ngā tamariki; ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā ki ngā mokopuna, ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ngā kaupapa here me ngā kawa mo ngā haere a nga mokopuna i waho atu o to rātou kōhanga)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga o ngā kaiako me ngā kaimahi, ā,me ngā whakaritengae pā ana ki a rātou
  • me ngā whakaritenga mō ngā puta tere wawe atu ki waho, inā ka puta he ahi, he rū ranei.

5 Ngā Tūtohutanga

E tūohu ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia tahuri te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te:

  • whakawhānui ake i ngā mahi aromātai i te hōtaka kia tae atu ki ngā mahi o ngā tamariki o ia rā, ngā mahi āta mātaki i ngā tamariki me ngā matea whanake o tēna, o tēna o ngā rōpū pakeke

  • whakarato i ētahi atu were ki ngā tuakana

  • whakaoti i te mahere rautaki me te aroturuki i o rātou whāinga i ia wā

  • whakamahere i tētahi huarahi ki te arotake whaiaro.

6 Ngā Mahi Hei Mahi a Meake Nei

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

7 Hui-tanguru 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ara Hou is located next to Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Te Ara Hou in Napier. Children from the local area attend the kōhanga reo. The whānau has a strong focus on te ao Māori including the language. Whānau want their children to learn in a kaupapa Māori environment which acknowledges their holistic development and uniqueness. All members of the whānau are continuing to improve their skills in te reo Māori for the benefit of their children.

The whānau chose Mana Tangata as the focus for this review. Knowing who you are and where you come from an important to the whānau. Highly successful noho marae has allowed children to experience tikanga Māori, te ao Māori and the practice of whanaungatanga which are important aspects of belonging.

Daily routines and activities are well established. Children know the boundaries which help keep them safe whilst allowing them to freely explore their environment. The younger children are well cared for and are effectively supported in their learning. Children play and interact well together and are caring towards each other. The older children take lead roles during routines and activities. They are supportive of their teina.

The outdoor and indoor environments are well maintained, organised and resourced. Children who are transitioning to the kura participate in appropriate kura activities and celebrations. Some of the children have whānau and siblings at the kura. This assists their smooth transition in to the kura.

The staff shares a well informed and thinking culture. Training opportunities are available to all staff who take advantage of these courses. Several have successfully completed a number of essential qualifications. Internal support and guidance, well organised roles and responsibilities and transparent working conditions has brought stability to the teaching team.

Well developed planning is evident in annual, term and weekly documents. Individual profile books are well presented and contain important information about children’s learning milestones. Staff regularly discuss and record evaluation of learning topics.

Whānau acknowledge strategic planning requires more thought and effort. Whānau still need to formalise their vision and aspirations for their children and kōhanga reo. Self review is in the early stages of development. There is some monitoring and reporting on aspects of the kōhanga reo operations but this is not planned or regular. Whānau need to continue to improve their knowledge and practice of self review.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ara Hou was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the children at Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ara Hou.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Tangata.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Mana Tangata

Background

The importance of knowing who you are and where you come from is important to the whānau. The whānau promote the values of whakapapa and whanaungatanga with children at kōhanga reo.

Areas of good performance

Mana Tangata. Whanaungatanga is integral to children’s learning. Regular activities include recitation of individual pepeha and whakapapa and making links to extended whānau in the community. Highly successful noho marae allows children to experience tikanga Māori, te ao Māori and whanaungatanga. These are important aspects of developing children’s sense of belonging. Taking children to their marae has instilled a sense of pride about knowing themselves. Children are developing well in their knowledge about who they are and where they come from.

Te reo Māori. The whānau has a strong focus and belief in the value of te reo Māori for their children. All members of the whānau continue to improve their skills in te reo Māori for the benefit of their children. Staff participate in and enjoy learning te reo Māori through various courses. Designated areas of the kōhanga reo are dedicated to te reo Māori only. Newsletters are written in te reo Māori. In addition, parts of whānau hui are also conducted in te reo Māori. Staff consistently speak, sing, read, instruct, direct, question and converse with children. The younger children confidently take risks and attempt to interact and communicate with their older peers. They are acknowledged for experimenting with sounds and language. New words and kīwaha that are used in context contribute to children’s growing vocabulary. The older children are effective language models for their teina. Children’s te reo Māori is developing well.

Independent activity. Daily routines and activities are well established. Children know the boundaries which help keep them safe whilst allowing them to freely explore their environment. The children are well cared for and supported in their learning. Staff supervise them closely. Age appropriate resourcing is effective and varied. Children are self sufficient and enjoy a warm, comfortable and stimulating environment. There is opportunity to make independent choices. The older children take lead roles during important activities and routines throughout the day. These children support their teina in positive ways. Kaimahi are prepared, organised and responsive to children’s requests and needs. Children enjoy their surroundings and the opportunities they have to explore and follow their interests.

The kōhanga reo environment. The outdoor and indoor environments are well maintained, highly organised and effectively resourced. Most children who graduate from kōhanga reo attend the kura as five year olds. They participate in appropriate kura activities and celebrations. Some of the children have whānau and siblings at the kura. These factors assist children to transition smoothly to kura. The kōhanga reo has age specific areas where sustained activity can take place. The kōhanga is an attractive and stimulating environment for children to learn and grow in.

Staff guidance and support. Staff have developed a well informed and thinking culture. Training opportunities are readily available to staff who take advantage of these courses and have successfully completed a number of essential qualifications. Internal support and guidance, well organised roles and responsibilities and transparent working conditions has brought stability to the teaching team. Children benefit from staff who are committed to keeping ahead of emerging trends in education.

Areas for development

Catering for older children’s learning needs.Staff now need to think about providing more challenges for the older children. Problem solving, individual projects or group performances or presentations are possible activities that could help stimulate higher level thinking in children. Children are naturally inquisitive and enjoy a challenge.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent, to which the whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of operations. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s progress and development.

Areas of good performance

Planning. Well thought through planning is evident in annual, term and weekly documents. There are clear links between kaupapa across the four terms. The whānau have opportunities to contribute ideas. Well planned learning topics allow children to make numerous discoveries about te ao Māori.

Assessment. Kaimahi understand the importance of assessment and its purpose. They consistently observe, discuss and make decisions about children’s dispositions. Individual profile books are presented well and contain information about important learning milestones. Staff stories about children’s learning are a highlight for children and whānau. Children and whānau enjoy reading their profiles.

Management practices. Whānau manage parts of the kōhanga operations well. There are clear processes for personnel management, appraisals, meeting procedures, office administration, financial matters and health and safety. Registers, the charter document, policies and personnel records are kept up to date and systematically filed.

Areas for development

Evaluation. Kaimahi discuss and record evaluation of kaupapa regularly. They can now extend this activity to include the evaluation of routines, supervision practices and the developmental needs of children. A broader view of evaluation practices should benefit children’s learning and development.

Strategic planning. Whānau acknowledge strategic planning requires more work. They have identified several strategic goals which align to Te Ara Tūāpae. Many of these goals are of a general nature. There is no plan to show how and when these goals will be achieved. Whānau still need to formalise their vision and aspirations for their children and kōhanga reo.

Self review. Self review is in the early stages of development. Whānau and staff regularly monitor and report on their financial management practices and professional development opportunities. They often review the charter document and policies with procedures. There is some monitoring and reporting on other parts of the kōhanga reo operations but this is not planned or regular. Whānau still need to improve their knowledge about self review. This will help whānau monitor and improve the effectiveness of the kōhanga reo operations.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ara Hou completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends that the whānau and kaimahi:

  • extend programme evaluation practices to include children’s routines, supervisions practices and the developmental needs of the different groups

  • provide more challenges for the older children

  • complete the strategic plan and regularly monitor their goals

  • establish a planned approach to self review.

6 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

To the Parents and Community of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ara Hou

These are the findings of the Education Review Office’s latest report on Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ara Hou.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ara Hou is located next to Te Kura Kaupapa Māori o Te Ara Hou in Napier. Children from the local area attend the kōhanga reo. The whānau has a strong focus on te ao Māori including the language. Whānau want their children to learn in a kaupapa Māori environment which acknowledges their holistic development and uniqueness. All members of the whānau are continuing to improve their skills in te reo Māori for the benefit of their children.

The whānau chose Mana Tangata as the focus for this review. Knowing who you are and where you come from an important to the whānau. Highly successful noho marae has allowed children to experience tikanga Māori, te ao Māori and the practice of whanaungatanga which are important aspects of belonging.

Daily routines and activities are well established. Children know the boundaries which help keep them safe whilst allowing them to freely explore their environment. The younger children are well cared for and are effectively supported in their learning. Children play and interact well together and are caring towards each other. The older children take lead roles during routines and activities. They are supportive of their teina.

The outdoor and indoor environments are well maintained, organised and resourced. Children who are transitioning to the kura participate in appropriate kura activities and celebrations. Some of the children have whānau and siblings at the kura. This assists their smooth transition in to the kura.

The staff shares a well informed and thinking culture. Training opportunities are available to all staff who take advantage of these courses. Several have successfully completed a number of essential qualifications. Internal support and guidance, well organised roles and responsibilities and transparent working conditions has brought stability to the teaching team.

Whānau acknowledge strategic planning requires more thought and effort. Whānau still need to formalise their vision and aspirations for their children and kōhanga reo. General Information About Reviews

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
