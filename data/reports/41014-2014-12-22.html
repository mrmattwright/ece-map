1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o He Maungarongo ki te tāone o Murupara. Tata ki te rua tekau tau te kōhanga reo e whakahaerehia ana, ā, ka tū te whakanuitanga o tōna rua tekau tau hei te Whiringa-ā-rangi o 2015. Mai i tōna whakatūnga, kua hāpaitia te kōhanga reo e tōna kuia me tōna koroua, ā, he mātātoa tonu tā rāua whai wāhi ki ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo i ia rā.

Ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi o tēnei arotake. I whakataungia tēnei kaupapa whai muri i te hui tuatahi ki waenga i ngā kaimahi, ngā mema o te whānau, me Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

‘He pēhea te whakawhanaketanga ake o ngā akoranga me ngā pūkenga reo o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo?’

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako, ā, kei te pakari haere tō rātou kaha ki te kōrero ake i te reo Māori i a rātou e noho ana ki te kōhanga reo. Ka rongo rātou i ngā tauira papai o te reo Māori puta noa i te rā. Ka poipoia te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki. He harikoa, he māia hoki rātou i tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Ka hautū, ka ārahi hoki te kuia i ngā kaimahi ki te whakapuaki i tētahi hōtaka akoranga. Ka aro nui ngā kaimahi, ā, ka arotahi ki te whakarite i te harikoa o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, e tika ana kia whakawhanake tonu rātou i te hōtaka akoranga, hei āta whakarite i te whakaongaonga me te whakahihiri o ngā tamariki ki te ako.

Me whakawhanake ake i ētahi āhuatanga o te whakahaere a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. Ka āta whakarite tēnei i te ahu whakamua, te whakapūmau hoki o ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau i te kounga kairangi, me te riterite anō hoki puta noa i ā rātou mahi katoa mā ngā tamariki.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake, ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

‘He pēhea te whakawhanaketanga ake o ngā akoranga me ngā pūkenga reo o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo?’

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako, ā, kei te pakari haere tō rātou kaha ki te kōrero ake i te reo Māori i a rātou e noho ana ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka poipoia te hononga o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo me te hapori. He manaakitanga tō tō rātou hononga ki tō rātou kuia, ngā kaimahi, me te whānau. He pai te āhua o ngā kōrero a ngā mātua me te whānau mō te ako me te whakawhanaketanga o ā rātou tamariki. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki, ā, ka arohaina ki a rātou.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā wheako akoranga e whai pūtake ana ki a rātou. E ako ana ngā tamariki i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki Ngā Kararehe o te Pāmu. Ia wiki, ka hāngai ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki ki tētahi kararehe o te pāmu, tae atu ki te āhua, ngā tangi, me ngā kai a taua kararehe. He kōwaiwai pakitara ki te kōhanga reo, e kapo ana i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i te wāhanga kua pahure ake nei. Ka whakarato, ka whakamahi hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā kupu e whai pānga ana, hei whakatairanga i te ako a ngā tamariki i te kaupapa, me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga reo.

E pakari haere ana ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i ngā tauira papai o tō rātou reo ā-iwi puta noa i te rā. E āhei ana rātou ki te taki i ngā karakia me ō rātou pepeha. Ahakoa ka mārama ngā tamariki ki tēnā e kōrerohia ana ki a rātou i roto i te reo Māori, e mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, me hāpai ake i ngā tamariki ki te whakautu me te kōrero mā te reo Māori i ia te wā, puta noa i te rā.

He māia ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo. He mā te kōhanga reo, he pai hoki te whakatakotoranga. E rua ngā wāhi o roto mō ngā akoranga, tae atu ki tētahi wāhi motuhake mō ngā tamariki i raro i te rua o ngā tau. Ka āta whakamahi ngā tamariki i te ruarua o ngā rauemi e wātea ana ki a rātou, hei hāpai i ā rātou akoranga me tā rātou tākaro. He tino wāteatea te wāhi o waho hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga me te korikori o ngā tinana o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Me nui ake te aronga, te tautoko hoki hei whakatairanga, hei whakawhānui i te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga. Me hāngai hoki tēnei ki ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā kaimahi i te whakauru ki ngā whakangungu o Te Whāriki. Kāhore anō ngā kaimahi kia whakarite i tētahi wā i ia wiki ki te kōrero ngātahi mō tēnā i whai hua ai, me ā rātou rautaki anō hoki ka whakapakari i te reo Māori o ngā tamariki me te whakapai i te kounga o ngā akoranga.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whai huatanga o ngā mahi ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki?

Me whakawhanake ake i ētahi wāhanga o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka whai ngā kaimahi i tētahi angamahi riterite ki te whakamahere i ngā kaupapa mō ngā tamariki. Ka whakaatuhia ngā mahere ki te pūtake tonu o te wāhi ako matua, hei ārahi i ngā kaimahi me te whānau. Ahakoa he māmā noa te kiko o taua mahere, ka hono tonu ki te katoa o Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira, ā, ka mau hoki i ngā mahere ā-tau, ā-wāhanga, ā-wiki hoki. Ka ū tonu ngā pūkete mahi toi a ngā tamariki hei mauhanga o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

He māro ngā hononga atu ā te whānau ki tēnei kōhanga reo. Ka noho matua te kuia me te koroua ki te whakahaeretanga i te katoa o te kōhanga reo. Kua whai wāhi rāua ki te kōhanga reo, mai i tōna tīmatanga, ā, he tokomaha anō hoki o ā rāua tamariki, ā rāua mokopuna hoki i noho ai ki te kōhanga reo ki tō rāua taha. Ko te kuia te kaiako matua ki te kōhanga reo. Tua atu i āna mahi hei heamana, ka whai wāhi hoki te koroua ki te tiaki i ngā rawa me te whare.

Ka hautū, ka ārahi hoki te kuia i ngā kaimahi ki te whakapuaki i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka aro nui, ka arotahi anō hoki ngā kaimahi, hei āta whakarite i te harikoa o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, me whakawhanake tonu rātou i te hōtaka akoranga hei āta whakarite i te whakaongaonga, te whakahihiri hoki o ngā tamariki katoa ki te ako. Ki te whakatutuki i tēnei o ngā whāinga, e tautoko ana te kuia i tētahi o ngā kaimahi ki te whakaoti i te Tohu Whakapakari. Ka whai pānga tēnei ki te tauatanga wā roa o te kōhanga reo.

Kua oti te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi katoa i tēnei tau. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi he huarahi tā rātou ki te whakapakari i tō rātou ake whakawhanaketanga mā te whakangungu tonu.

Te wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Ahakoa e kawe ana ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ō rātou ake tūranga ki te whakarite me te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo, me hāpai ake, me whakawhanake ake hoki hei hiki ake i te kounga me te ritenga o aua mahi. Ka hāngai hoki tēnei ki te āta whakarite ki te whakapūmau me te rokiroki tika i ngā tuhinga me ngā mauhanga, te whakatutuki i ngā take e pā ana ki ngā pūtea, me te noho matua o te whakawhanake i ngā kaimahi. E mahi ana te tari ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te taha o te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā take pūtea i tāutuhia ai ki tā rātou pūrongo tātari pūtea ā-tau o 2013.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaeretanga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora, me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, ā, e whakaae ana hoki te whānau, kia mahi ngātahi te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki tō rātou kaupapa kaimahi ā-rohe ki te hiki i te kounga o ngā whakaritenga o tēnei wā i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e rua.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o He Maungarongo is in the township of Murupara. The kōhanga reo has been operating for nearly two decades and will celebrate its 20th anniversary in November 2015. Since its establishment, the kōhanga reo has been supported by its kuia and koroua who continue to play an active role in the daily operations of the kōhanga reo.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira is the focus of this review. This was determined following the initial meeting between kaimahi, whānau members and ERO.

To what extent are children’s learning and language skills developed in the kōhanga reo?

Children enjoy learning and are developing confidence to speak te reo Māori more during their time in the kōhanga reo. They hear good models of te reo Māori throughout the day. Children’s sense of belonging to the kōhanga reo is nurtured. They are happy and confident in their kōhanga reo.

The kuia provides leadership and guidance to kaimahi to deliver the learning programme. Kaimahi are attentive and focussed on ensuring children are happy in the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi know they need to continue to develop the learning programme to ensure all children are stimulated and excited to learn.

Some aspects of whānau management in the kōhanga reo require further development. This will ensure kaimahi and whānau members continue to work towards and maintain high levels of quality and consistency across all of their work for children.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

To what extent are children’s learning and language skills developed in the kōhanga reo?

Children enjoy learning and are developing confidence to speak te reo Māori more during their time in the kōhanga reo.

Areas of good performance

Children’s sense of belonging to the kōhanga reo and the local area is nurtured. They have caring relationships with their kuia, kaimahi and whānau. Parents and whānau talk positively about their children’s learning and development. Children are happy, settled and loved.

Children enjoy learning experiences that are meaningful to them. Children have been learning about Ngā Kararehe o te Pāmu. Each week children’s learning focuses on one farm animal including its appearance, sounds and food. A large mural on the wall of the kōhanga reo captures children’s learning over this last term. Kaimahi provide and use relevant words to enhance children’s kaupapa learning and language development.

Children are developing confidence to speak te reo Māori. Children hear good models of their local dialect throughout the day. They can recite karakia and their pepeha. While children can understand what is being said to them in te reo Māori, kaimahi recognise that children require further support to respond and speak te reo Māori more consistently throughout the day.

Children are confident in the physical environment of their kōhanga reo. The kōhanga reo is clean, tidy and well presented. There are two indoor spaces for learning, including a separate area for children under the age of two years. Children make good use of the few resources that are available to support their learning and play. The outdoors area has lots of open space to support children’s physical development and activity.

Area for development

Further direction and support is required to enhance and extend the quality of the learning programme. This should include opportunities for kaimahi to participate in Te Whāriki training. Kaimahi are yet to schedule time each week to talk about what has worked well and what they can do to strengthen children’s te reo Māori and improve the quality of learning.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

To what extent is the kōhanga reo managed effectively for the benefit of children’s learning?

Some aspects of whānau management in the kōhanga reo require further development.

Areas of good performance

Kaimahi follow a consistent framework to plan kaupapa for the children. Planning is displayed centrally in the main learning area to guide kaimahi and whānau. While the content of this planning is basic, it links to all of the Taumata Whakahirahira and includes annual, term and weekly plans. Portfolios of children’s art work are maintained as records of children’s learning.

There are strong whānau links within this kōhanga reo. The kuia and koroua are central to the overall operation of the kōhanga reo. Both have been part of the kōhanga reo since it was established and many of their own children and mokopuna have been at the kōhanga reo with them. The kuia is the head kaiako in the kōhanga reo. As well as being the chairperson, the koroua helps with property and building maintenance.

The kuia provides leadership and guidance to the kaimahi to deliver the learning programme. Kaimahi are attentive and focussed on ensuring children are happy in the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi know they need to continue to develop the learning programme to ensure all children are stimulated and excited to learn. To achieve this, the kuia is supporting one of the kaimahi to complete the Tohu Whakapakari course. This is part of a longer term succession plan for the kōhanga reo.

Appraisals for all kaimahi have been completed this year. Kaimahi acknowledge there is scope to strengthen their individual development through ongoing training.

Area for development

While the kaimahi and whānau are attending to their respective roles in terms of the operations and management of the kōhanga reo, further support and development is required to lift the overall quality and consistency of those practices. This includes ensuring documents and records are maintained and stored appropriately, financial issues are fully resolved and staff development is prioritised. The local Kōhanga Reo Trust office is working with whānau to address the financial issues identified in their 2013 annual audit report.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends and the whānau agree that whānau and kaimahi work closely with their kaupapa kaimahi to lift the quality of current practices as identified in this report.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in two years.
