2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ko Tongariro te maunga

Ko Ngāti Tūwharetoa te iwi

Ko Te Arawa te waka

Ko Waihī te kōhanga reo

I te tīmatanga, i te tū tata Te Kōhanga Reo o Waihī ki te pā o Waihī, ki te taha hoki o Taupō-nui-a-Tia. I te tau 2009 te whenua o Waihī i rū ai. He nui ngā āhuatanga i pakaru, ā, he mōrearea hoki taua papa. I taua wā, i hūnukutia Te Kōhanga Reo o ki te marae o Korohe, ā, kei reira tonu rātou i tēnei wā. Ko ngā hononga ā-whakapapa ki waenga i ngā hapū o Waihī rāua ko Korohe, i hāpai i te tau pai o te whānau me ngā tamariki ki konei.

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i te whānuitanga o ngā whai wāhitanga akoranga e whakaongaonga ana i a rātou – ā-tangata, ā-whatumanawa, ā-hinengaro, ā-tinana hoki. Ka tino rongo i te rere o te reo Māori ki tēnei kōhanga reo. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti kōrero ki ō rātou hoa, ki ngā kaimahi hoki.

He mātātoa ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi ki ngā āhuatanga katoa o te hōtaka. Ko te tū hei uri o Ngāti Tūwharetoa, e noho pūmau ana ki te hōtaka akoranga. He pai te whakaritenga, te whakahaeretanga hoki o te hōtaka mā ngā pēpi. Ka whakatutukihia ō rātou matea ako. Ka whai hua, ka hāpai hoki ngā hononga o ngā kaimahi ki ngā pēpi. Ka whai hua anō hoki ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki katoa. Ki tō rātou titiro, he pakari ngā tamariki ki te ako.

He pakari ngā pūnaha a te whānau ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o tō rātou kōhanga reo. Kua arotakengia, kua whakahou akehia hoki te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa here, me ngā tukanga. Ko te whakamahi i aua tukanga e hāpai ana i tēnei kōhanga reo ki te noho haumaru, ora hoki mō ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa.

He tukanga ā te whānau ki te arotake i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka hāpai ēnei i te whānau ki te oke i ngā wā katoa, hei whakatairanga i te kounga o ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He pai te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo. He pakari ā rātou pūnaha whakahaere pūtea, ā, ka whai hua hoki. Ka āta whakaraupapa te mahere rautaki i te whakawhanaketanga o te kōhanga reo mō meāke nei.

Ko ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi, e hāngai ana ki te rā. He mārama ki ngā kaimahi ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki i a rātou e mahi ana ki te taha o ngā tamariki. He tika, he matawhānui hoki te whakamahere me te aromatawai. He pai te whakamōhio i te whānau mō ngā akoranga, te ahunga whakamua hoki a ā rātou tamariki.

Me whakarite, me whakatutuki hoki te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi. Ka āwhina ngā arohaehae i te whakarato o te tautoko tika ki ngā kaimahi, hei whakapakari i ā rātou whakaritenga mahi.

Me whakatinana te aromātai hōtaka hei aroturuki i te whai hua tonutanga o te hōtaka akoranga.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

E whakapono ana te whānau, ko te kōhanga reo tētahi wāhi ka hāpai, ka poipoi hoki i te whakawhanaketanga o te katoa o te tamaiti. Ko te whakarato i te tino mātauranga, te kaupapa matua ki te whānau. E whakapono ana hoki rātou, ka penapena, ka whakapūmau hoki te kōhanga reo i te mana o Ngāti Tūwharetoa. Ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te whānuitanga o te whakatairanga i te waiora me te tuakiri o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i te whānuitanga o ngā whai wāhitanga akoranga e whakaongaonga ana i a rātou – ā-tangata, ā-whatumanawa, ā-hinengaro, ā-tinana hoki. Ka tino rongo i te rere o te reo Māori ki tēnei kōhanga reo. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki tika kia tahuri ai ngā tamariki ki te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā pātai whānui hei akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te whai whakaaro ki ā rātou whakautu. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti kōrero ki a rātou anō mā te reo Māori. I ngā wā katoa, ka whakamahara tētahi i tētahi, he wāhi kōrero Māori anake te kōhanga reo.

Ka whakarato te hōtaka akoranga i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te mahi ā-rōpū, me te kōkiri hoki i ā rātou ake akoranga. He maha ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te aro atu ki ā rātou kaupapa mahi mō te roanga o te wā. Nā te ngāwari noa ki te whakapā atu ki ngā rauemi, ka whakawerohia ā-hinengaro, ā-tinana hoki ngā tamariki.

He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā āhuatanga katoa o te hōtaka. Ka ako rātou i te whakahirahiratanga o ngā atua Māori mā ngā karakia me ngā waiata. Ko te tū hei uri o Ngāti Tūwharetoa, e noho pūmau ana ki te hōtaka akoranga.

He pai te whakaritenga, te whakahaeretanga hoki o te hōtaka mā ngā pēpi. Ka whakatutukihia ō rātou matea ako. He rawaka, he tika hoki te taiao akoranga me ngā rauemi. He ngāwari te rere o te hōtaka me ngā taunekeneke whakaute. Ka whai hua, ka hāpai hoki ngā hononga ki waenganui i ngā kaimahi me ngā pēpi.

Ka whai hua ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki. He mātātoa, he hihiri hoki rātou, ā, ka kapo ake hoki rātou i ngā wā ako ka pupū ake. He māhorahora ngā kaimahi, ā, ka aro atu, ka āta whakamahi hoki i ngā whai wāhitanga ki te tahuri i ngā tamariki ki te ako.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te taiao akoranga e whakamana ana i ō rātou matea akoranga. Ki tō te kōhanga reo titiro, he pakari ngā tamariki ki te ako.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai i te kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aroturuki ki te whakamahere hōtaka me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He pakari ngā pūnaha a te whānau ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o tō rātou kōhanga reo. Kua arotakengia, kua whakahou akehia hoki te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa here, me ngā tukanga. He tino mōhiohio ki te tūtohinga e hāngai ana ki te hītori o te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā mātāpono e pou here ana, e whakapūmau ana hoki i te ia o ngā wawata o te whānau. Kua whakaritea ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga katoa. Ka whakarato ēnei i ngā aratohu mō ngā whakaritenga, ā, e hāpai ana ēnei i tēnei kōhanga reo ki te noho haumaru, ora hoki mō ngā tamariki.Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau.

Whakatutukihia ai e te whānau ngā arotake i ia te wā. Ko ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero i ngā hui ā-whānau, e tohu ana i te kaha o te whakawhitiwhiti whakaaro mō ngā āhuatanga o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga, hei whakapai ake. He mārama ki te whānau ngā āhuatanga o te kōhanga reo, nā ngā pūrongo e whakatakotohia ana ki ngā hui. Ka hāpai ake aua tukanga i te kaha o te whānau ki te oke i ngā wā katoa, hei whakatairanga ake i te kounga o te whakahaere tari ki te kōhanga reo.

He mahere rautaki ā te whānau e whakaraupapa ana i ngā kaupapa matua o te whakawhanake tonutanga o te kōhanga reo. He maha ngā kaupapa i whakatakotohia ai. Ko te ū tonu ki te aroturuki me te pūrongo i runga i aua whāinga, ka āta whakarite i te ahu whakamua o aua kaupapa matua.

He pai ngā kōrero urupare a te kaitātari kaute, mō ngā pūnaha whakahaere pūtea a te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā pūrongo pūtea ki ngā hui o ia marama, e āta whakarite ana i te noho mārama o ngā take katoa e pā ana ki ngā pūtea. He pai te tūnga pūtea o tēnei kōhanga reo.

Ko ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi, e hāngai ana ki te rā. Kei ngā kaimahi katoa ā rātou tuhinga tūranga mahi, ā rātou kirimana hoki, ā, kua waitohungia. He mārama ki ngā kaimahi ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki i a rātou e mahi ana ki te taha o ngā tamariki.

He tika, he matawhānui hoki te whakamahere me te aromatawai. Ka ārahi ngā mahere i te whakatinanatanga o te hōtaka. E āhei ana te whānau ki te whai wāhi ki te whakamahere. He rautaki ā ngā kaimahi hei kapo ake, hei whakamārama hoki i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He pai te noho mōhio o te whānau ki te ahunga whakamua o ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Me whakarite, me whakatutuki hoki te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi. Mā te whakatutukitanga o tēnei mahi, ka noho mōhio te whānau ki ngā tūmomo kaupapa whakawhanaketanga ngaio hei whai ake.

Me whakatinana te aromātai hōtaka. Kāhore e aromātaihia ana i tēnei wā. He whakawhitinga kōrero ōpaki, engari he uaua ki te aroturuki i ngā āhuatanga i whakarerekēhia ai nā aua kōrero. Me whakarite ngā kaimahi ki te tuhi haere i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero katoa e pā ana ki te hōtaka akoranga, hei arotahi i te aroturuki tonutanga o te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Ko Waihi Te Kōhanga Reo

Waihi Te Kōhanga Reo was originally located close to Waihi Pā beside Taupo-nui-a-Tia. In 2009 there was an earthquake in Waihi. The damage was considerable and the site deemed dangerous. The kōhanga reo was then relocated to Korohe Marae where they remain at this stage. Whakapapa links between the hapū of Waihi and Korohe have supported the whānau and children settle in well.

Children experience a range of learning opportunities that offer social, emotional, intellectual and physical stimulation. Te reo Māori is alive and well in this kōhanga reo. Children confidently converse with their peers and kaimahi.

Children are active participants in all aspects of the programme. Being Ngāti Tūwharetoa is central to the learning programme. The baby programme is well organised and managed. Their learning needs are catered for. Kaimahi relationships with babies are meaningful and supportive. Kaimahi work with all children effectively. They view children as confident learners.

Whānau have sound systems to manage the operations of their kōhanga reo. The charter, policies and procedures have been reviewed and updated. The use of these procedures supports this kōhanga reo to be a safe and healthy place for children at any given time.

The whānau has processes to review kōhanga reo operations. These support the whānau to constantly strive to raise the quality of kōhanga reo operations. The kōhanga reo is in a sound financial position. Their financial management systems are robust and effective. The strategic plan clearly outlines priorities for the future development of the kōhanga reo.

Personnel documentation is up to date. Kaimahi are clear about their roles and responsibilities when working with children. Planning and assessment is appropriate and comprehensive. Whānau are well informed of their children’s learning and progress.

Kaimahi appraisals need to be established and undertaken. Appraisals should assist kaimahi to be provided with appropriate support to strengthen their practices.

Programme evaluation needs to be implemented to monitor the ongoing effectiveness of the learning programme.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Waihī was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Te Kōhanga Reo o Waihī.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

4 Findings

Nga Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

The whānau believes the kōhanga reo is a place where children’s holistic development is supported and nurtured. Providing the best education possible is a priority for the whānau. They also trust that the kōhanga reo is a place where the mana of Ngāti Tūwharetoa is preserved and maintained. ERO will evaluate the extent to which children’s wellbeing and identity is promoted

Areas of good performance

Children experience a variety of learning opportunities that offer social, emotional, intellectual and physical stimulation. Te reo Māori is alive and well in this kōhanga reo. Kaimahi use appropriate strategies to engage children in conversations. They use open ended questions to encourage children to think about their responses. Children speak to one another confidently in te reo Māori. They consistently remind others that the kōhanga reo is a place where te reo Māori only is spoken.

The learning programme provides opportunities for children to work together in groups as well as initiate their own learning. There are many opportunities where children are able to engage in tasks for sustained periods of time. The availability of resources enables children to be challenged both mentally and physically.

Children are active participants in all aspects of the programme. They learn about the significance of atua Māori through karakia and waiata. Being Ngāti Tūwharetoa is central to the learning programme.

The baby programme is well organised and managed. Their learning needs are catered for. The learning environment and resources are adequate and appropriate. The programme is unrushed and interactions with babies are gentle and respectful. Kaimahi relationships with babies are meaningful and supportive.

Kaimahi work with children effectively. They are energetic and vibrant and capture teachable moments. Kaimahi are spontaneous and responsive and use every opportunity to engage children in learning.

Children enjoy a learning environment where their learning needs are respected. They are viewed as confident learners.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management. Whānau have sound systems to manage the operations of their kōhanga reo. Charter policies and procedures have been reviewed and updated. The charter provides valuable information about the history of the kōhanga reo. The principles underpin and uphold the intent of whānau aspirations. All policies and procedures are in place. These provide guidelines for practice, which support the kōhanga reo to be a safe and healthy place for children.

The whānau carries out reviews regularly. Records of discussion at whānau hui reveal a high level of robust debate about aspects of kōhanga reo operations that require attention. The whānau is clear about what is happening for the kōhanga reo through the reports tabled at hui. These processes give support to the fact that whānau are constantly striving to raise the quality of kōhanga administration.

The whānau has a strategic plan which outlines priorities for the future development of the kōhanga reo. There are a number of projects recorded. Ongoing monitoring and reporting against the goals should ensure that these priorities are progressed.

The kōhanga reo financial management systems received positive feedback from the Auditor. Financial reports to monthly hui ensure that all matters related to funding are clear and transparent. This kōhanga reo is in a sound financial position.

Personnel documentation is up to date. All kaimahi have signed job descriptions and employments contracts. Kaimahi are clear about their roles and responsibilities when working with children.

Planning and assessment is appropriate and comprehensive. Planning guides programme implementation. Whānau are able to contribute ideas to the planning. Kaimahi have methods to capture and comment on children’s learning. Whānau are well informed of their children’s progress.

Areas for development

Kaimahi appraisals need to be established. Whānau will have a good idea about what professional development is required to strengthen the practices of kaimahi once these are completed.

Programme evaluation needs to be implemented. At the moment there is no evaluation happening. Informal conversations occur but it is difficult to monitor what changes have occurred as a result. Kaimahi need to ensure that all discussions about the learning programme are recorded as a focus for ongoing monitoring of the quality of the learning programme.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Waihī completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement andSelf-Audit Checklist. In these documents they haveattested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based on Te Whāriki, the whānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
