1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ko Manaia te maunga

Ko Raumanga te awa

Ko te Puna o te Mātauranga te marae

Ko Raumanga te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. He mārama ngā wawata o te whānau, kia mātau ai, kia tū manawa whakahī ai hoki ā rātou tamariki hei Māori. Ka hāpai ngātahi rātou i te whakawhanaketanga o te kōhanga reo, hei whakapiki ake i tā rātou whai huatanga, ā, hei whakarato i tētahi taiao ako o te reo Māori e tino whakahaerehia ana mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ka tino manaakitia ngā tamariki e ngā tāngata e whakanui ana, e whakamana ana hoki i tō rātou oranga ā-wairua, ā-whatumanawa, ā-tinana hoki. Ka whakapūmautia te kaupapa Māori mā ngā taumahi e akiaki ana i ngā tamariki ki te ako i ngā atua Māori, ō rātou ao, me tō rātou whai pānga anō hoki ki te ao e noho nei rātou ngā tamariki. Ka hāngai pū te hōtaka ki te tamaiti, ā, ka tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te tūhura, te mahi auaha, me te whakaaro pohewa. Ka kōkiri ia tamaiti i āna ake akoranga, ā, ka whakatau anō hoki ngā tamariki i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki ā rātou akoranga.

Ka tautokona ngā tamariki ki te ako i tō rātou tū hei uri o Ngāpuhi-nui-tonu. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki ngā pōwhiri, ka mōhio ki ngā kawa o Ngāpuhi, ā, he mārama hoki ki a rātou tō rātou tūnga hei tangata whenua. Noho tahi ai rātou ki ngā tamariki o ētahi atu kōhanga reo o te Tai Tokerau, ā, ka whakanuia te whānaungatanga.

He aronga rautaki tā te whānau, ki te whakapiki i te āheinga i roto i te reo Māori. He mātātoa tō rātou tautoko i ngā mema o te whānau me ngā kaimahi, ki te whakawhanake i tō rātou mātau ki te reo Māori. Ka akiaki rātou i tētahi taiao reo Māori ki te kōhanga reo. E whanake haere ana te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. He pai tō rātou māramatanga, ā, ka whakamahi rātou i ngā kupu me ngā kīanga māmā.

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka hāngai ngā mahi arotake whaiaro ki ngā tūmanako me ngā tikanga a te tari ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o ngā Kōhanga Reo. Whakaratohia ai ngā pūrongo e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, ki ngā hui ā-whānau o ia te wā. Ka haere tonu ngā whakawhitinga kōrero kia piki taumata anō ai te whai huatanga o ngā mahi, ā, nā runga i tēnei āhuatanga, ka pai ake ngā whakaritenga. Ināianei, me tahuri te whānau ki te whakapakari i ā rātou mahi whakamahere rautaki, hei hāpai i te whakatutukitanga o ngā tino whāinga i whakaritea ai.

He matawhānui te whakamahere hōtaka, ā, ka whakatutukihia te aromātai i te hōtaka i ia te wā. Ka arotahi ngā tukanga aromatawai ki ngā kaupapa. Ka taea te whakapai ake, mā te tuhi haere i te ahunga whakamua me te whakawhanaketanga o ia tamaiti, i ngā wā ka tāutuhia.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Raumanga Kōhungahunga kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Raumanga Kōhungahunga.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

He whakamārama

Ka pēhea te whai huatanga ki te poipoi me te whakawhanake i te kiritau o ngā tamariki hei Māori?

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, ā, e tino hāpaitia ana kia pakari, kia tākare hoki tā rātou kōrero, tā rātou whakatinana anō hoki i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori.

Mana Atua

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka poipoia te oranga ā-tinana, ā-whatumanawa, ā-wairua hoki o ngā tamariki.

He hononga ngātahi, he tautoko hoki ki waenga i ngā mema katoa o te whānau o te kōhanga reo. Ka ngākau nui te whānau kia eke angitu ai ā rātou tamariki, ā, ka whakaongaonga tēnei, ka ārahi anō hoki tēnei i ā rātou mahi. He pai te tiaki i ngā tamariki. Kua tuituia ngā uaratanga me ngā tikanga Māori ki te atawhai o ngā tamariki. Taunekeneke ai ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa. Ka kōrerorero, ka patapatai, ā, ka whai wāhi anō hoki ki te atawhai, te tākaro, me ngā akoranga o ngā tamariki. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā atua Māori. He tino wheako akoranga ngā haerenga ki te moana me te ngahere. Ka whakahonoa ngā akoranga e pā ana ki ngā atua, ki te ao e noho nei rātou ngā tamariki. He harikoa, he pakari hoki te ako o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Mana Reo

Rongo ai ngā tamariki i te reo Māori huri noa i te roanga o te rā.

Ka ngākau nui te whānau kia mātau ā rātou tamariki ki te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori. He tino aronga rautaki ki te whakapiki i te mātau o te whānau me ngā kaimahi i roto i te reo Māori. Ka kōrerohia te reo Māori ki mua i ngā tamariki, ā, ka kōrerohia anō hoki ki ia tamaiti, ki ngā rōpū tamariki anō hoki. Ka whakamahia te whānuitanga o ngā rautaki ki te whakatairanga i te reo o Ngāpuhi i roto i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka tāwhai ngā pēpi, ā, ka mārama hoki ki ngā kīanga māmā me ngā kupu māmā. Ka mārama, ka whakamahi hoki ō rātou tuākana i ngā kupu me ngā kīanga māmā. E whakawhanake ana te reo Māori o ngā tamariki.

Mana Tangata

Ka whai hua nui ngā tamariki nā te ngākau nui o ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakarato i ngā tino painga mō rātou.

He hononga ā-whakapapa tō te katoa o ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi ki a Ngāpuhi-nui-tonu. Ka ako, ka whakataki hoki ngā tamariki i ō rātou whakapapa me ō rātou pepeha, ki te taha o ō rātou hoa. Ka kōkiri ngā tamariki i ā rātou ake akoranga, ā, he pakari rātou ki te tūhura me te whakatau hoki i ā rātou ake whakaritenga ako. Ka whakakoro te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakatutuki i ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā matea o ia tamaiti, nā te whakarato i tētahi hōtaka akoranga e hāngai pū ana ki ngā tamariki.

Ka ākina ngā tamariki ki te tiaki i a rātou anō, me te kawe i ā rātou ake akoranga.

Mana Aotūroa

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te tūhura i te taiao Māori, tūturu hoki.

Ka whakamahi ngā tamariki i ō rātou taha pohewa, taha auaha hoki i a rātou e tūhura haere ana i te taiao tūturu, Māori hoki. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā wheako akoranga ka akiaki i a rātou ki te whakamahi i ō rātou tūmomo rongo ā-tinana. Nā ngā haerenga e hono ana ki ngā kaupapa, ka tūhura ngā tamariki i ngā taonga o te taiao Māori. Ko te tū ki te kapa haka me te mahi whakaari hoki, ka whai wāhi nui ki ngā akoranga, ā, ka hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o te māia o ngā tamariki. Ko te ako mā ngā taumahi e pā ana ki te pānui me te tatau nama, i hāpai ake anō i te nui haere o ō rātou mōhiotanga, ō rātou pūkenga hoki i roto i te tīmatanga o te reo matatini me te pāngarau.

Mana Whenua

Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki ngā hapū me ngā iwi o te Tai Tokerau.

E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki kia whanake ai te tuakiri e hono ana i a rātou ki te whenua me ngā tāngata whenua o te Tai Tokerau. I a rātou e whai wāhi ana ki ngā pōwhiri, he pakari tō rātou tū hei tangata whenua, me tā rātou whakatinanatanga i ngā tikanga me ngā kawa o Ngāpuhi. Ko ngā haerenga ki tua atu i te kōhanga reo e hāpai ana i ngā akoranga ā-kaupapa, ā, ka akiaki anō hoki i ngā akoranga e pā ana ki ngā tino pouwhenua, ngā tino taonga anō hoki o Ngāpuhi tonu. Ko te whai wāhi ki ngā taumahi o te purapura me ngā kaupapa a ngā kōhanga reo o te Tai Tokerau, e whakapakari ana i te hononga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou whānau whānui.

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ō rātou taonga tuku iho, me te ahurea o Ngāpuhi-nui-tonu.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

Ka pēhea te kiritau o ngā tamariki e poipoia, e whakawhanakehia hoki hei Māori, mā te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo?

Ka whai hua te whānau hei kaiwhakahaere o te kōhanga reo, ā, nā tēnei, e tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki kia pakari, kia tākare anō hoki tā rātou kōrero, tā rātou whakatinanatanga hoki i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori.

Ngāwāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka whai hua te whānau hei kaiwhakahaere o te kōhanga reo. Nā te tū o ngā hui ā-whānau o ia te wā, ka āta whakarite i te whai huatanga, te hāngaitanga anō hoki o ngā pūtea, ngā kaimahi, me ngā mahi hauora me te haumaru. Ka arotake te whānau i ā rātou whakaritenga, ā, ka tuku pūrongo rātou ki te whānau me te tari ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o ngā Kōhanga Reo.

He pai te whakawhanake haere o ngā mahi e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro. Ka whakamahia ngā mōhiohio arotake whaiaro e te whānau, hei whakatau i ngā whakaritenga ka whakapiki ake i te whai huatanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka taea pea e te whānau ināianei, te whai whakaaro ki te arotake i te whai huatanga o ā rātou whakataunga.

He matawhānui ngā mahi ngaio a ngā kaimahi, ā, ka hāpai tēnei i te whakapuakitanga, te aroturukitanga hoki o te hōtaka akoranga. Ka āta whakatinanahia ngā mahere hōtaka, ā, ka tino ārahi tēnei i te whakatinanatanga o ngā kaupapa. Ka aromatawaihia ngā putanga ako, ā, ka pūrongotia te ahunga whakamua ki ngā mātua. Ka āta aromātaihia te hōtaka, ā, ka whakamahia aua mōhiohio hei whakapiki ake i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka ka tahi, hei whakapuakitanga anō hoki mā te whānau ka rua.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Ināianei, me tahuri te whānau ki te whakawhanake i tā rātou ake mahere rautaki, kia whai pānga ai tēnei ki ā rātou kaupapa matua. Kua whakatinanahia e te whānau te mahere rautaki a te purapura, ā, ka whai hua rātou ki te kōrero me te whakatinana i te huarahi ki te whakatutuki i aua whāinga. Engari, me whakatau i tētahi mahere ā-tau hei āwhina i te whānau ki te āta whakaaro huritao me te āta whakamahere mō ngā wawata o te whānau me ngā kaupapa matua i roto i te whānuitanga o ngā whāinga a te purapura.

Ahakoa he pai te tukanga e pā ana ki te aromatawai i te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki i roto i te hōtaka akoranga, me whai whakaaro hoki ngā kaimahi ki te whakatakoto haere i ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki ngā whakatutukitanga ako, me te whakawhanaketanga hoki o ia tamaiti, i te wā ka tāutuhia. Ka taea hoki e ngā mātua me ngā tamariki te whai wāhi ki tēnei tukanga.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakiia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o okiuh, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia:

  • whakawhanake te whānau i tētahi mahere ā-tau, hei hāpai i a rātou ki te whakatutuki i ā rātou whāinga me te whakapiki ake i te whai huatanga o ngā mahi e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

Paenga-whāwhā 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Ko Mania te maunga

Ko Raumanga te awa

Ko te Puna o Te Matauranga te marae

Ko Raumanga te kōhanga Reo.

Whānau selected Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus for the review. Whānau have clear aspirations for their children to be knowledgeable about, and proud to be Māori. They collaboratively support kōhanga reo development, to increase their effectiveness and to provide a well functioning te reo Māori learning environment for their children.

Children are well cared for by people who acknowledge and value their spiritual, emotional and physical wellbeing. Kaupapa Māori is reinforced through activities which encourage children to learn about ngā atua Māori, their realms and the everyday things that relate to them. The programme is child-centred and supports children to explore, be creative and imaginative. Children are independent learners who make decisions about their learning.

Children are supported to learn about themselves as children of Ngāpuhi-Nui-Tonu. They participate in pōwhiri, know Ngāpuhi kawa and are clear about their role as tangata whenua. They meet with children from other kōhanga reo in the north and whānaungatanga is reinforced.

Whānau have a strategic approach to increasing te reo Māori capability. They actively support whānau members and kaimahi to develop te reo Māori competence. They encourage a te reo Māori speaking environment in the kōhanga. Children are developing as speakers of te reo Māori. They have a good understanding and use words and simple phrases.

Whānau manage their kōhanga reo well. Self review practices reflect District Trust Office expectations and requirements. Regular reports about kōhanga reo operations are provided at whānau hui. Discussions to increase effectiveness are ongoing and result in improved practice. Whānau now need to strengthen their strategic planning practices to support the achievement of specific goals.

Programme planning is comprehensive and programme evaluation is carried out regularly. Assessment processes focus on the kaupapa. Further improvement could be made by documenting the progress and development of individual children when it occurs.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning; and
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

How effectively is children’s self esteem as Māori nurtured and developed?

Children are immersed in the kaupapa of kōhanga reo and are very well supported to become confident, enthusiastic speakers and practitioners of te reo Māori and tikanga Māori.

Areas of good performance

Mana Atua

Children’s physical, emotional and spiritual wellbeing is nurtured.

Relationships between all members of the kōhanga reo whānau are collaborative and supportive. Whānau are passionate about their children’s success and this motivates and leads them to action. Children are looked after well. Appropriate Māori values and practices are incorporated into their care. Kaimahi constantly interact with children. They discuss, question and participate in children’s care, play and learning. Children learn about ngā atua Māori. Visits to the beach and bush are important learning experiences. Learning about ngā atua is linked to their daily lives. Children happily and confidently go about their learning at kōhanga reo.

Mana Reo

Children hear te reo Māori throughout the day.

Whānau are passionate about their children becoming steeped in te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori. There is a strong strategic focus to increase te reo Māori competency of both whānau and kaimahi. Te reo Māori is spoken around and to individuals and groups of children. A range of strategies are used to promote te reo o Ngāpuhi in the learning programme. Babies mimic and understand simple phrases and words. Their tuakana understand and use words and simple phrases. Children are developing as speakers of te reo Māori.

Mana Tangata

Children benefit greatly from the commitment of kaimahi and whānau to provide the best for them.

All children and kaimahi have whakapapa links to Ngāpuhi-Nui-Tonu. Children learn and share their whakapapa and pepeha with their peers. Children are independent learners who feel secure to explore and make decisions about their learning. Whānau and kaimahi endeavour to meet the individual interests and needs of children by providing a child focused learning programme.

Children are encouraged to take responsibility for themselves and their learning.

Mana Aotūroa

Children enjoy exploring the natural and physical environment.

Children use their imagination and creativity when they explore the physical and natural environment. Kaimahi provide learning experiences that encourage them to use their senses. Excursions linked to the kaupapa enable children to explore resources in the natural environment. Performance in kapa haka and whakaari has been an important part of learning and has helped to develop children’s confidence. Learning through reading and number activities has helped to increase their knowledge and skills in early literacy and numeracy.

Mana Whenua

Children learn about the hapū and iwi of Te Tai Tokerau.

Children are well supported to develop a sense of identity that links them to the land and people of Te Tai Tokerau. When they take part in pōwhiri, they confidently carry out their role as tangata whenua using Ngāpuhi tikanga and kawa. Excursions outside the kōhanga reo support kaupapa learning and encourage learning about cultural landmarks and resources particular to Ngāpuhi. Participation in purapura activities and kōhanga activities of Te Tai Tokerau strengthens children’s relationships with their wider whānau.

Children are aware of their Ngāpuhi-Nui-Tonu heritage and culture.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How effectively is children’s self esteem as Māori nurtured and developed through the management of the kōhanga?

Whānau are effective managers of the kōhanga and through this, children are well supported to become confident, enthusiastic speakers and practitioners of te reo Māori and tikanga Māori.

Areas of good performance

Whānau are effective managers of the kōhanga reo. Regular whānau hui help ensure that financial, personnel and health and safety practices are efficient and up-to-date. The whānau reviews their practices and reports to the whānau and the District Kōhanga Reo Trust office.

Self review practice is developing well. Self review information is used by whānau to make decisions about how to increase the effectiveness of the kōhanga reo. Whānau could now consider reviewing the effectiveness of their decisions.

Kaimahi professional practice is comprehensive and supports the delivery and monitoring of the learning programme. Programme planning is thorough and provides a useful guide for kaupapa implementation. Learning outcomes are assessed and progress is reported to parents. The programme is evaluated well and this information is used to increase programme effectiveness as well as being shared with whānau.

Areas for development

Whānau now need to develop their own strategic plan to reflect their priorities. The whānau has adopted the purapura strategic plan and they do well to discuss and implement steps to achieve these goals. However, an annual plan is needed to help whānau better reflect and plan for whānau aspirations and priorities within the broader purapura goals.

While there is a good process for assessing children’s progress in the learning programme, kaimahi should also consider recording information about the learning achievements and development of individual children as they occur. Parents and children could also contribute to this process.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends:

  • The whānau develop an annual plan to support them to achieve their goals and increase the effectiveness of the self review practices.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
