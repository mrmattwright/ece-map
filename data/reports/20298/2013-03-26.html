2. Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

He kōhanga reo a Te Hau Ora kua whakatū ki te kainga o tētahi kuia i Kelston, i Tāmaki Makaurau. Kei muri ngā whare kōhanga i te kāinga o te kuia nei. Nō te kuia, koia hoki te kaipupuri i te raihana o te kōhanga reo, ngā whare e rua. He pai te tiaki i ngā wāhi o ro whare, o waho hoki i te kōhanga reo, he ataahua, he wāhi whakaihiihi hoki mō ngā tamariki. Nā te āhua o te whakatakoto o te whare kōhanga he māmā noa iho te whakawhiti mai i rō whare ki waho.

Ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira ngā wāhi arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

He takatika, he atawhai hoki ngā taunekeneke me te whanaungatanga. He pai te mātaki i ngā tamariki puta noa i te rā. He pai te mōhio o ngā kaimahi ki a rātou tamariki, ā, ka kohikohi rātou i ngā kōrero e tika ana e pā ana ki te oranga o a rātou tamariki. Kātahi ka whakamahi ēnei kōrero kia tāutu i te ahunga whakamua me te whanake o ngā tamariki. Kei te wātea noa ēnei kōrero aromatawai ki ngā mātua.

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki ngā atua i ngā kaupapa mahi. Ka tīmata, ka oti ngā mahi nui katoa i te kōhanga reo ki te karakia. He tau ngā tamariki, ā, he māia tō rātou whakawhiti haere i tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Ka haereere ngā tamariki ki ngā wāhi whakamere i te hapori. Ka whakaū ēnei haerenga i ngā akoranga me ngā ariā e hono ana ki tēna, ki tēna o ngā kaupapa mahi. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te mahi auaha, te tuhura ki ngā ariā o te reo matatini me te whai wāhi ki ngā tākaro tokonga whakaaro, ngā tākaro a tinana hoki.

Ka rongo ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa i te kōhanga reo. Ka kōrero rātou i ngā kianga reo Māori māmā i ngā wā whāriki me ngā wā tuhura takitahi me ngā kaimahi me o rātou hoa. Ka tukuna ngā tamariki ki te arahi i ngā mahi ki te reo Māori pērā i te karakia, te waiata me te mihimihi. Tāpiri atu ki te whānau, he pai ngā tautoko i ngā tamariki kia tipu hei kaikōrero i te reo Māori. Ki te āta whakaaro ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhiti kōrero me ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori, ka hāpai ake anō tēnei whanaketanga.

He harikoa ngā tamariki, ā, he kaha hoki tō rātou toioranga. Ka whakatau ētahi o ngā karakia, ngā himene me ngā waiata i ngā tamariki puta noa i te rā. He hangore, he whakatakohe hoki ngā mahi mō ia rā me ngā whakawhitinga o ngā tamariki. Ka ako ngā tamariki ko wai rātou, nō whea hoki rātou. Ka kitea te atawhai me te kaha tiaki o ngā tuakana i ngā taina.

Ka tū ngā hui ā whānau i ia wā, ā, he pai te taetae atu o te whānau ki ngā hui nei. He mātātoa te whai wāhi o te whānau ki te ako o ngā tamariki me te tiri i ngā mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. He pai te tautoko o te whānau i ngā mahi o te kōhanga reo, te hōtaka o ia rā me ngā haerenga. Ka whiwhi te whānau i ngā pūrongo i ngā hui ā marama e pā ana ki ngā whakahaere i te kōhanga reo.

Ka kitea ki ngā miniti o ngā hui me ētahi atu tuhinga kua arotake ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga. He mārama ngā pūnaha whakahaere pūtea. E ngākaunui ana te whānau ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, me te ako me te tiaki o ngā tamariki.

Ngā Mahi ā Meake Nei

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kohanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

I mua o te arotake, i ïnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hau Ora kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te pupuritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hau Ora.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.
  • Ko te katoa o ngā arotake matauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:
  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aro matawai me te aromātai.

4. Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

Koia ko te katoa o Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira ngā wāhi katoa o te ako me te whanake o te tamaiti e ai ki tō te Māori titiro ki te ao.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Mana Atua. He takatika, he atawhai hoki ngā taunekeneke me te whanaungatanga. He tere te whakautu o ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea o ngā tamariki. He pai te mātaki i a rātou puta noa i te rā. Ka tākaro ngātahi ngā tamariki. He pai te mōhio o ngā kaimahi ki a rātou tamariki, ā, ka kohikohi rātou i ngā kōrero e tika ana e pā ana ki te oranga o a rātou tamariki. Kātahi ka whakamahi ēnei kōrero kia tāutu i te ahunga whakamua me te whanake o ngā tamariki. Ka tīmata, ka oti ngā mahi nui katoa i te kōhanga reo mā te karakia. Ka ako ngā tamariki ki ngā atua i ngā kaupapa mahi. He tau rātou, ā, he māia tō rātou whakawhiti haere i tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Te hōtaka akoranga. Kua whakamahere, kua waihangatia te hōtaka akoranga hei whakaihiihi i te pakiki o ngā tamariki me te hāpai i o rātou ngākaunuitanga. Ka haereere ngā tamariki ki ngā wāhi whakamere i te hapori. Ka whakaū ēnei haerenga i ngā akoranga me ngā ariā e hono ana ki tēna, ki tēna o ngā kaupapa mahi. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te mahi auaha, te tuhura ki ngā ariā o te reo matatini me te whai wāhi ki ngā tākaro tokonga whakaaro, ngā tākaro a tinana hoki. He māia rātou, ka uru hoki rātou ki te hōtaka akoranga.

Mana Reo. Ka rongo ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa i te kōhanga reo. Ka kōrero rātou i ngā kianga reo Māori māmā i ngā wā whāriki me ngā wā tuhura takitahi me ngā kaimahi me o rātou hoa. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki whānui i ngā wā katoa hei whakatairanga i te whanaketanga o te reo. Tae atu ana ki te tuku pātai puare hei akiaki whakautu, te whakatauira tukurua i ngā kianga tōtika me ngā hanganga reo, te whai i ngā mahi pānui me te whakarongo me te mahi ā ringa, me te waiata. Ka whakanui ngā kaimahi i ngā kaha me ngā paetae. Ka tuku ngā tamariki ki te arahi i ngā mahi ki te reo Māori pērā i te karakia, te waiata me te mihimihi. Tāpiri atu ki te whānau, he pai ngā tautoko i ngā tamariki kia tipu hei kaikōrero i te reo Māori.

Mana Whenua. He harikoa ngā tamariki, ā, he kaha hoki tō rātou toioranga. Ka whakatau ētahi o ngā karakia, ngā himene me ngā waiata i ngā tamariki puta noa i te rā. He hangore, he whakatakohe hoki ngā mahi mō ia rā me ngā whakawhitinga o ngā tamariki. Ka taea e rātou te taki o rātou pepeha me o rātou hononga ā whānau. He māia ngā tamariki ki te tuhura i te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka whakautu ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea whatumanawa me ngā matea-ā-tinana o ngā tamariki.

Mana Tangata. Ka ako ngā tamariki ko wai rātou, nō whea hoki rātou. He pai o rātou taunekeneke me ētahi atu tāngata tae atu ana ki ngā kaumātua, te whānau me o rātou hoa. Ka kitea te atawhai me te kaha tiaki o ngā tuakana i ngā taina. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te tātaki i ngā āhua o te hōtaka tae atu ana ki te karakia me te mihimihi.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki te reo Māori.Ahakoa ka kōrero ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori i te hōtaka puta noa i te rā me whai whakaaro rātou ki ngā huarahi whakanui ake i ngā whai wāhitanga ki te whakawhiti kōrero me ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

E aro mātai ana a ERO i ngā wā katoa, i te kounga o ngā whakamahere me ngā aro mātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo ki te titiro ki te kaha o te whānau o te kōhanga ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere me te aro turuki i te katoa o ngā wāhanga whakahaere o tō rātou kōhanga mō a rātou mokopuna me o rātou whānau. Kei roto i te wāhanga o te whakamahere me te aro mātai ko te whakamahere hōtaka me te aro turuki i te whanaketanga o te akoranga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakamahere kaupapa me te aromatawai. Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi tētahi tauira whakamahere kaupapa mārama mō te wā roa. Ka whai wāhi ngā kaimahi katoa ki ngā mahi whakamahere-ā-tau. He mārama ngā hono o te whakamahere ki ngā atua, ngā wāhanga o te tau me Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Mā te āta mātaki i ngā tamariki i ia wā me te tuhi amiki i ēnei e whiwhi ngā kaimahi i ngā kōrero whai hua. Ka wetewete ōpaki ngā kaimahi i ngā kōrero nei hei whakatau i ngā huarahi ako. Kei te wātea noa ngā kōrero aromatawai ki ngā mātua.

Te whakahaere o te whānau. He mātātoa te whai wāhi o te whānau ki te ako o ngā tamariki me te tiri i ngā mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. He pai te tautoko o te whānau i ngā mahi o te kōhanga, te hōtaka o ia rā me ngā haerenga. Ka tū ngā hui ā whānau i ia wā, ā, he pai te taetae atu o te whānau ki ngā hui nei. Ka whakamōhiotia te whānau ki ngā mahi o te kōhanga reo mā ngā pānui kite papa pānui, ngā pānui ka tuku kia rātou me te kōrero ōpaki mō ngā kōrero o te rā. Ka whakamārama tētahi tukanga whakauru whānau hou i ngā tūranga me ngā kawenga ki ngā whānau hou. He mārama ngā pūnaha whakahaere pūtea. E ngākaunui ana te whānau ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, me te ako me te tiaki o ngā tamariki.

Te arotake whaiaro. Ka aroturuki te whānau i ngā wāhanga matua o te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. Ka whiwhi pūrongo te whānau i ngā hui ā marama. Kei te hāngai ēnei pūrongo ki ngā pūtea, te pūrongo a te heamana, te kaiwhakahaere me te kaiako me te tāutu i ngā take hauora me te haumaru. Ka kitea ki ngā miniti o ngā hui me ētahi atu tuhinga kua arotake ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga.He kaha whakautu te whānau ki te take ki te whakapai ake i ngā wā katoa me te tutuki i tēnei mā te arotake i ngā mahi o te kōhanga reo i ia wā.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te aromatawai.Ahakoa kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi ngā mahi aromatawai e whakarato ai rātou i ngā kōrero whai hua e pā ana ki ngā tamariki me whai whakaaro hoki rātou ki te whakamahine i ngā whāinga akoranga kia hāngai ki ngā meā ka ako ngā tamariki.

2. The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hau Ora is a home based kōhanga reo in the West Auckland suburb of Kelston. The kōhanga reo buildings are situated behind a house that is a family home. The licensee of the kōhanga reo owns both these properties. The indoor and outdoor facilities are well maintained, attractive and stimulating for children. The premises allow for easy movement between indoor and outdoor areas.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira are the focus areas for this review.

Interactions and relationships are positive and caring. Children are well supervised during the day. Kaimahi know their children well and collect appropriate information about children’s well being. This information is then used to identify children’s’ progress and development. Assessment information is readily available to parents.

Children learn about ngā atua through kaupapa mahi. Karakia is used to start and end all important events at kōhanga reo. Children are settled and move confidently in their kōhanga reo environment.

Children go on outings to places of interest in the community. These outings reinforce learning and concepts that link to different kaupapa mahi. Children have opportunities to be creative, explore early numeracy and literacy concepts and participate in imaginative and physical play.

Children consistently hear te reo Māori at kōhanga reo. They use simple te reo Māori phrases during group mat time and independent exploration with kaimahi and their peers. Children are given opportunities to lead activities in te reo Māori including karakia, waiata and mihimihi. Along with the whānau they are well supported as developing speakers of te reo Māori. Kaimahi-led conversations in te reo Māori would further support this development.

Children are content and have a strong sense of wellbeing. Different karakia, himene and waiata help calm and settle children throughout the day. Their routines and transitions are flexible and leisurely. Children learn about who they are and where they are from. Older children show care and support for younger children.

Whānau hui are held regularly and are well attended. The whānau is actively involved in children’s learning and shares in the management of the kōhanga reo. There is good whānau support for kōhanga reo events, the daily programme and for trips. Whānau receive regular reports at monthly hui about kōhanga reo management.

Policy and practices reviews are evident through minutes and other documents. Financial management systems are transparent. The whānau is committed to the kaupapa of kōhanga reo and children’s learning and care.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

4. Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira encompasses all areas of a child’s learning and development according to a Māori world view.

Areas of good performance

Mana Atua.Interactions and relationships are positive and caring. Kaimahi respond promptly to the needs of children. They are well supervised during the day. Children play cooperatively. Kaimahi know their children well and collect appropriate information about children’s well being. This information is then used to identify children’s progress and development. Karakia is used to start and end all important events at kōhanga reo. Children learn about ngā atua through kaupapa mahi. They are settled and move confidently in their kōhanga reo environment.

Learning programme.The learning programme is planned and designed to stimulate children’s curiosity and support their interests. Children go on outings to places of interest in the community. These outings reinforce learning and concepts that link to different kaupapa mahi. Children have opportunities to be creative, explore early numeracy and literacy concepts and participate in imaginative and physical play. They are confident and engaged throughout the learning programme.

Mana Reo.Children consistently hear te reo Māori at kōhanga reo. They use simple te reo Māori phrases during group mat time and independent exploration with kaimahi and their peers. Kaimahi consistently use a range of strategies to promote language development. These include using open ended questions to encourage responses, modelling appropriate phrases and language structures in a repetitive way, undertaking reading and listening activities and using gestures and waiata. Kaimahi praise effort and achievement. Children are given opportunities to lead activities in te reo Māori including karakia, waiata and mihimihi. Along with the whānau they are well supported as developing speakers of te reo Māori.

Mana WhenuaChildren are content and have a strong sense of wellbeing. Different karakia, himene and waiata help calm and settle children throughout the day. Children’s routines and transitions are flexible and leisurely. They can talk about their pepeha and their whānau connections. Children are confident to explore their kōhanga reo environment. Kaimahi are responsive to children’s emotional and physical needs.

Mana Tangata.Children learn about who they are and where they are from. They interact well with others including kaumātua, whānau and their peers. Older children show care and support to younger children. Children have opportunities to lead aspects of the programme including karakia and mihimihi.

Areas for development

Conversations in te reo Māori.While kaimahi consistently use te reo Māori throughout the programme they should consider ways to increase opportunities to have conversations with children in te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Kaupapa planning and assessment.The kaimahi have developed a clear long term kaupapa planning template. All kaimahi are involved in annual planning. Planning clearly links to ngā atua, the seasons and to Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Regular and detailed observations of children at work and at play provide kaimahi with useful information. Kaimahi informally analyse information to determine what direction learning might take. Assessment information is readily available to parents.

Whānau management. The whānau is actively involved in children’s learning and share in the management of the kōhanga reo. There is good whānau support for kōhanga reo events, the daily programme and for trips. Whānau hui are held regularly and are well attended. Ongoing communication with parents includes the use of board notices, newsletters and informal sharing of relevant daily information. An induction process for new whānau and kaimahi helps to clarify roles and responsibilities. Financial management systems are transparent. The whānau is committed to the kaupapa of kōhanga reo and children’s learning and care.

Self review. The whānau regularly monitors all key areas of operations in the kōhanga reo. Whanau are reported to at monthly hui. These reports include finances, chairperson’s reports, kaiwhakahaere and kaiako reports and identifying any health and safety issues. Review of policies and practices is evident through minutes and other documents. The whānau is responsive to the need for continual improvement and accomplish this through the regular review of kōhanga reo operations.

Areas for development

Assessment.While kaimahi have developed assessment practices that provide them with useful information about children they should also consider refining learning objectives to focus on what children might actually learn.

5. Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hau Ora Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff ofcompleted an EROKōhanga Whānau Assurance StatementandSelf-Audit Checklist. In these documents theyhaveattested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

6. Recommendation

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • the kaimahi and whānau seek ongoing support from TKRNT, including future wananga, to support them to further develop their kaupapa for children.

7. Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

To the Parents and Community of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hau Ora

These are the findings of the Education Review Office’s latest report on Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hau Ora.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hau Ora is a home based kōhanga reo in the West Auckland suburb of Kelston. The kōhanga reo buildings are situated behind a house that is a family home. The licensee of the kōhanga reo owns both these properties. The indoor and outdoor facilities are well maintained, attractive and stimulating for children. The premises allow for easy movement between indoor and outdoor areas.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira are the focus areas for this review.

Interactions and relationships are positive and caring. Children are well supervised during the day. Kaimahi know their children well and collect appropriate information about children’s well being. This information is then used to identify children’s’ progress and development. Assessment information is readily available to parents.

Children learn about ngā atua through kaupapa mahi. Karakia is used to start and end all important events at kōhanga reo. Children are settled and move confidently in their kōhanga reo environment.

Children go on outings to places of interest in the community. These outings reinforce learning and concepts that link to different kaupapa mahi. Children have opportunities to be creative, explore early numeracy and literacy concepts and participate in imaginative and physical play.

Children consistently hear te reo Māori at kōhanga reo. They use simple te reo Māori phrases during group mat time and independent exploration with kaimahi and their peers. Children are given opportunities to lead activities in te reo Māori including karakia, waiata and mihimihi. Along with the whānau they are well supported as developing speakers of te reo Māori. Kaimahi-led conversations in te reo Māori would further support this development.

Children are content and have a strong sense of wellbeing. Different karakia, himene and waiata help calm and settle children throughout the day. Their routines and transitions are flexible and leisurely. Children learn about who they are and where they are from. Older children show care and support for younger children.

Whānau hui are held regularly and are well attended. The whānau is actively involved in children’s learning and shares in the management of the kōhanga reo. There is good whānau support for kōhanga reo events, the daily programme and for trips. Whānau receive regular reports at monthly hui about kōhanga reo management.

Policy and practices reviews are evident through minutes and other documents. Financial management systems are transparent. The whānau is committed to the kaupapa of kōhanga reo and children’s learning and care.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to student achievement and useful to this kōhanga reo.

If you would like a copy of the full report, please contact the kōhanga reo or see the ERO web page, http://www.ero.govt.nz.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
