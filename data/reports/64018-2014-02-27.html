2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ko te Poari Kaitiaki o Kokiri Marae Keriana Olsen te mana whakahaere o Kokiri Marae Te Kōhanga Reo me Te Hono Ki Kokiri Te Kōhanga Reo ki Wainuiomata me Te Paparākau Te Kōhanga Reo ki Whakatiki.

E whakarato ana te kaiwhakahaere i nga tātakitanga mō ngā whakahaere katoa o te Poari Kaitiaki, ā, ko te whakahaere i ngā kōhanga reo ōnā haepapa tātaki. Ka mahi tahi tētahi kaihāpai ki te taha o te kaiwhakahaere kia tōtika ai ngā whakahaere a ngā kōhanga reo e ai ki te tirohanga whakamua o te Poari Kaitiaki, te tino kaupapa, ngā kaupapa here me ngā whakaritenga o ia rā a te Poari Kaitiaki.

Kua whakaputaina e te whānau ō rātou ngākau nui mō ngā āhuatanga katoa i te kōhanga reo. E manawanui ana, ā, e ū ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakarato i ngā āhuatanga katoa hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki. Ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake ā, ko te whakaako, te whakanui, te akiaki, te arahi me te tautoko i ngā mokopuna me te whānau ki te pupuri i ō rātou whaiora ā-wairua, whaiora ā-tinana, whaiora ā- pāpori me ō rātou whaiora ā-ahurea.

Kei te Poari Kaitiaki o Kokiri Marae ngā pūnaha whai painga hei whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā o te kōhanga reo. Kātahi anō ka arotakengia te tūtohinga. He mea aratohu te āhua o te tūtohinga mō ā rātou kaupapa here me ā rātou tukanga whakahaere o ia rā. Kua whakatūria e te whānau ngā tukanga pakari rawa mō te arotake whaiaro. E hāngai tōtika ana te arotake whaiaro ki ngā whakahaere hei whakapai ake anō. Kua whakahaeretia ngā whakahaere pūtea e te kaiwhakahaere pūtea o te Poari Kaitiaki o Kokiri Marae. He tōtika me te whai tikanga te āhua o ngā pūnaha. E ora pai ana te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo. Tērā ngā tukanga tōtika mō te tono me te whakatau i ngā kaimahi. Tērā anō tētahi tukanga whānui hei aromātai i ngā kaimahi.

E noho rumaki ana ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori, ā, e whakarato ana ngā kaimahi i tētahi taiao hūmārie me tētahi taiao mauritau, ā, kua tino whakaritea ngā whakahaere katoa mō te hōtaka akoranga.

Kua whakaritea te hōtaka whakamāherehere me ngā pūkete akoranga mā ngā tamariki. He mea hōhonu me whānui ngā mea e rua. Ka āta arahi te hōtaka whakamāherehere i te hōtaka akoranga. Kua āta whakatūria ngā pūkete akoranga mā ngā tamariki. E tino mārama ana ngā mātua o te whānau he aha ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me ō rātou putanga paetae.

Kua tae mai ki te wā mā te Kaitiaki Poari ki te whakatū i tētahi tukanga hei aromātai i te whai painga o te hōtaka akoranga.

Ngā Mahi Ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o Te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau Te Kōhanga Reo o Paparākau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Paparākau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko āna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere i te māhere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • hōtaka whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

KoNgā Taumata Whakahirahirate kaupapa mō tēnei arotake. Ko te tino kaupapa o ngā mea katoa o Te Kōhanga Reo o Kokiri Marae, ko te whakaako, te whakanui, te akiaki, te arahi me te tautoko i ngā mokopuna me te whānau ki te pupuri i ō rātou whaiora ā-wairua, whaiora ā-tinana, whaiora ā- pāpori me ō rātou whaiora ā-ahurea.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Te hōtaka akoranga.E whakarato ana ngā kaimahi i tētahi taiao hūmārie me tētahi taiao mauritau, ā, kua tino whakaritea ngā whakahaere katoa mō te hōtaka akoranga. Ka nui te arotahi ki te mātauranga ā-pānui me te mātauranga ā-pāngarau. E harikoa ana ngā tamariki ki ngā ngohe katoa e hāngai ana ki ēnei ariā. He tino pai te māramatanga o ngā kaimahi ki ngā tūranga me ngā haepapa o ngā atua Māori. E whakahaeretia ana ngā rautaki whai hua hei tuari i te mātauranga o ngā atua. E whakaatu ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou māramatanga hōhonu mō ngā atua me ō rātou haepapa rerekē ki tēnā wāhi me tēnā wāhi. He whānui rawa ngā rauemi akoranga kua whakaritea hei tautoko i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Mā tēnei e taea e ngā tamariki te whiriwhiri me te whakatau i ngā ngohe akoranga hei whai atu. E whai tautoko ana rātou ki te tūhura haere i te ao ō-ahupungao, ā, he mea e wero ana i ō rātou matea ā-hinengaro me ō rātou matea ā-tinana ēnei tautoko.

Te whakamau i te reo Māori.E noho rumaki ana ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. E māia ana ngā tuakana ki te whakaputa i te reo Māori. E āhei ana ngā pēpi ki te tūhono i ngā kupu ki ngā pikitia me ngā taputapu. E whakahaeretia ana e ngā kaimahi ngā rautaki whai hua hei tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te whakapiki i ō rātou mātau ki te reo Māori.

Te hōtaka mā ngā tamariki ki raro iho i te rua ngā tau.E whakarato ana ngā kaimahi i tētahi hōtaka me ngā rauemi whai hua hei āta tautoko i ngā akoranga a ngā pēpi. He hūmārie, ā, te aroha hoki o ngā kaimahi e mahi ngātahi ana ki ngā pēpi. Kua āta whakatūria te takamahi o ia rā. Mā tēnei he ōrite ngā whakahaere, a, he mauritau te āhua o te taiao mō ngā pēpi i ngā wā katoa.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamāherehere me te aromātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo hei whakatau i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamāherehere, ngā whakahaere me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga reo mā a rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka āta hāngai te aromātai nei ki te hōtaka whakamāherehere me te aroturuki i te tipuranga ako o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Kei te Poari Kaitiaki o Kokiri Marae ngā pūnaha whai painga hei whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā o te kōhanga reo.

Kātahi anō ka arotakengia te tūtohinga. E whakamārama ana te tirohanga whakamua me te kaupapa i te ngā hiahia o ngā kaitiaki mā ngā mokopuna me te whānau e tae atu ana ki te kōhanga reo. He mea aratohu te āhua o te tūtohinga mō ā rātou kaupapa here me ā rātou tukanga whakahaere o ia rā. E hāngai tōtika ēnei ki ngā rekureihana mō ngā ratonga whare kōhungahunga 2008.

Kātahi ka oti te mahi hei whakahou me te arotake i ngā kaupapa here me ngā whakaritenga o ia rā mō te hauora me te haumaru. He aratohu whai hua ēnei mō ngā whakahaere o ia rā. E rata ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao ko tō rātou hauora me tō rātou whaiora te kaupapa nui.

Kua whakatūria e te whānau ngā tukanga pakari rawa mō te arotake whaiaro. Ka whakamārama ēnei tukanga i nga tino wāhanga whakahaere mō te kōhanga reo, ā, mā ēnei tukanga ka whakahaeretia ngā tūmahi hei whakapai ake i ngā wahanga hei whakapai ake anō.

Kua whakahaeretia ngā whakahaere pūtea e te kaiwhakahaere pūtea o te Poari Kaitiaki o Kokiri Marae. He tōtika me te whai tikanga te āhua o ngā pūnaha. Kua tāututia ngā rauemi hei tautoko i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i roto i te māhere whāroa tāhua pūtea. E ora pai ana te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo.

Tērā ngā tukanga tōtika mō te tono me te whakatau i ngā kaimahi. E hāngai ana ēnei ki te tino kaupapa me ngā tino hiahia o te Poari Kaitiaki o Kokiri Marae. Tērā anō tētahi tukanga whānui hei aromātai i ngā kaimahi. Mā tēnei kua whakaritea ngā wheako whakangungu kia pakari ake ai ngā whakahaere a ngā kaimahi hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Kua whakaritea te hōtaka whakamāherehere me ngā pūkete akoranga mā ngā tamariki. He mea hōhonu, ā, he whānui ngā mea e rua. Ka āta arahi te hōtaka whakamāherehere i te hōtaka akoranga. Kua tāututia e ngā kaupapa ako ngā rautaki hei tautoko i ngā akoranga ā ngā tamariki me ngā rauemi hei whakarite. E hāngai tōtika ana ngā pūkete akoranga a ngā mokopuna ki ngā whakamāherehere. E ai ki ngā whakahoki kōrero a ngā mātua o te whānau e tino mārama ana rātou he aha ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me ō rātou putanga paetae.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Me kaha te arotahi ki te hōtaka aromātai. He iti noa ngā taunakitanga e tāututia ai, ā hea, ka pēhea ā, he aha te take i whakarerekē ai ngā whakahaere a te hōtaka akoranga. Me whai wāhi atu te whānau inaiānei ki te whai whakaaro mō tētahi tukanga hei aromātai i te whai hua o te hōtaka.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

He whakamārama

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Paparākau tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga a te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

The Kokiri Marae Keriana Olsen Trust (Trust) is the governing body for the Kokiri Marae Te Kōhanga Reo, Te Hono Ki Kokiri Te Kōhanga Reo in Wainuiomata and Paparākau Te Kōhanga Reo, Upper Hutt.

The Manager provides leadership in all aspects of the trusts operations which includes the kōhanga reo. A supervisor works alongside the manager to ensure that the kōhanga reo operates in conjunction with the trust mission, philosophy, policies and procedures.

The whānau have expressed complete satisfaction with the kōhanga reo. The kaimahi are passionate and committed to providing the best possible outcomes for their children. Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira is the focus of this review which is to teach, promote, encourage, guide, support and assist mokopuna and whānau in the preservation of their spiritual, physical, social and cultural wellbeing.

The Kokiri Marae Trust have in place effective systems to manage the operations of the kōhanga reo. The Tūtohinga has recently been reviewed. It provides clear directions for their policies and procedures. The whānau have in place a robust self review process. This is focused on improvement in practice. Finances are managed by the Finance Manager of the Kokiri Marae Trust. The kōhanga reo is in a financially viable position. There are clear processes in place for the application and appointment of kaimahi. There is a comprehensive appraisal process in place.

Children are immersed in te reo Māori and kaimahi provide a peaceful, calm learning environment and the learning programme is well organised and managed.

Programme planning and records of mokopuna learning are in place. Both are comprehensive and detailed. The programme plan clearly guides the learning programme. The records for mokopuna learning are well established. Whānau have a good understanding about what children are learning and their progress.

It is now time for the Trust to establish a process to evaluate the effectiveness of the learning programme.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

4 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira is the focus of this review. The underpinning philosophy of Kokiri Marae Te Kōhanga Reo which is to teach, promote, encourage, guide, support and assist mokopuna and whānau in the preservation of their spiritual, physical, social and cultural wellbeing.

Areas of good performance

The learning programme.The kaimahi provide a peaceful, calm learning environment and the learning programme is well organised and managed. There is strong emphasis on literacy and numeracy. The children enjoy all activities associated with these concepts. Kaimahi have a good understanding of ngā atua Māori. Relevant strategies are used to transfer knowledge of ngā atua. Children demonstrate a good understanding of ngā atua and their domains. There is a wide range of learning resources available to support children’s learning. This enables children to make decisions about their learning. They are supported to explore the physical environment which provides mental and physical challenge.

Te reo Māori acquisition.Children are immersed in te reo Māori. The older children confidently use te reo Māori. The babies are able to associate words with pictures and objects. Kaimahi use appropriate strategies to support children’s language development.

Programme for under twos. Kaimahi provide a programme and resources that effectively support babies learning. Kaimahi who work with babies are patient and loving. Routines are well established. This provides babies with consistency and stability.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

The Kokiri Marae Trust has in place effective systems to manage the operations of the kōhanga reo.

The charter has recently been reviewed. The mission and philosophy clearly outlines the intent the trustees have for the mokopuna and whānau that attend the kōhanga reo. The charter provides clear directions for their policies and procedures. These are aligned to the 2008 Early Childhood Regulations.

All health and safety policies and procedures have recently been reviewed and updated. These provide clear guidelines for practice. Children enjoy an environment where their health and wellbeing is nurtured.

The whānau have in place a robust self review process. This clearly outlines key aspects of practice for the kōhanga reo and leads to actions for improvement.

Finances are managed by the Finance Manager of the Kokiri Marae Trust. The systems are transparent. Resources to support children’s learning are clearly identified in the budget forecast. The kōhanga reo is in a financially viable position.

There are clear processes in place for the application and appointment of kaimahi. This clearly reflects the philosophy and intent of the Kokiri Marae Trust. There is a comprehensive appraisal process in place. This results in professional development to enhance kaimahi practice for the benefit of the children.

Programme planning and records of mokopuna learning are in place. Both are comprehensive and detailed. The programme plan clearly guides the learning programme. This identifies kaupapa ako, strategies for supporting children’s learning and required resources. The records for mokopuna learning are directly linked to planning. Whānau responses clearly identify they are clear about what children are learning and their progress.

Areas for development

Programme evaluation needs attention. There is little evidence to identify when, how and why changes are made to the learning programme. It is time for whānau to consider a process to evaluate the effectiveness of the programme.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Te Kōhanga Reo o Paparākau Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff ofcompleted an EROKōhanga Whānau Assurance StatementandSelf-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

6 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • to establish a strategies for increasing the effectiveness of the learning programme.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

The Manager provides leadership in all aspects of the trusts operations which includes the kōhanga reo. A supervisor works alongside the manager to ensure that the kōhanga reo operates in conjunction with the trust mission, philosophy, policies and procedures.

The whānau have expressed complete satisfaction with the kōhanga reo. The kaimahi are passionate and committed to providing the best possible outcomes for their children. Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira is the focus of this review which is to teach, promote, encourage, guide, support and assist mokopuna and whānau in the preservation of their spiritual, physical, social and cultural wellbeing.

The Kokiri Marae Trust have in place effective systems to manage the operations of the kōhanga reo. The Tūtohinga has recently been reviewed. It provides clear directions for their policies and procedures. The whānau have in place a robust self review process. This is focused on improvement in practice. Finances are managed by the Finance Manager of the Kokiri Marae Trust. The kōhanga reo is in a financially viable position. There are clear processes in place for the application and appointment of kaimahi. There is a comprehensive appraisal process in place.

Children are immersed in te reo Māori and kaimahi provide a peaceful, calm learning environment and the learning programme is well organised and managed.

Programme planning and records of mokopuna learning are in place. Both are comprehensive and detailed. The programme plan clearly guides the learning programme. The records for mokopuna learning are well established. Whānau have a good understanding about what children are learning and their progress.

It is now time for the Trust to establish a process to evaluate the effectiveness of the learning programme.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to student achievement and useful to this kōhanga reo.

General Information About Reviews

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
