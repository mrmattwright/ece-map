1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Motutaiko ki Taupō, ki te rohe o Ngāti Tūwharetoa. Ko te ingoa o te kōhanga reo i ahu mai i tētahi motu tapu i te moana o Taupō.

Ko te ako i te reo Māori te aronga matua a te whānau. Ko ngā kaimahi, te whānau, me ngā tamariki ka whai wāhi atu ki te tākaro i ngā kēmu reo, te waiata, te taki i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo karakia, me te whakatutuki haere i ngā tikanga Māori. He pai te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki.

E whanake ana te ngākau nui o ngā tamariki ki te pukapuka, ngā ariā pāngarau, me te ako reo. Ka tino hāpaitia rātou kia tūhura i te ao e noho nei rātou. Ka whai wāhi anō hoki ngā kōrero pakiwaitara, ngā pukapuka, me ngā whakaaturanga hei akiaki i te ngākau nui o ngā tamariki ki te ako.

He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i te pai o te whanaungatanga ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ngā pākeke hoki. Ka whai pūtake ngā tākaro a ngā tamariki, ka tohatoha hoki i ngā taputapu, ka noho mārama ki te whanonga pai, ā, ka tiaki tētahi i tētahi.

Ka whakanui, ka whakatairanga hoki ngā kaimahi i te taki haere o ngā pepeha. Ka hāpai aua mahinga o ia rā i te whakahono o ngā tamariki i a rātou anō ki te whenua, ki tō rātou iwi hoki. Ka ākina ngā tamariki tuākana kia kōrero mō ā rātou pepeha, tae atu hoki ki ō rātou hononga ā-whakapapa me te whānau. Kei te puāwai te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae hoki.

Kua whakahoungia, kua whakamahinetia hoki te tūtohinga o te kōhanga reo, kia mārama ai, kia whai pānga ai anō hoki. I nāianei, he mārama ngā tūmanako me ngā aratohu o taua tuhinga, hei hāpai i te whānau ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga ki te kōhanga reo.

He pai te whakamahere a te whānau me ngā kaimahi i tā rātou ahunga whakamua. Ka arotahi te whānau ki te whakapai i te āheinga o ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, me te whakapakari anō hoki i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He mahere tā te whānau kia whakatutukihia e rātou ngā whāinga.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga reo (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

He pēhea te poipoi i ngā tamariki ki te ako i Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira?

He pai te poipoi i ngā tamariki kia ako ai rātou i ngā ariā e whai pānga ana ki Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Mana Whenua, Mana Tangata. E whanake haere ana te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae anō hoki. Ka whakatairanga ngā kaimahi me te whānau i te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki. Ka whakanui, ka whakatairanga anō hoki rātou i te whakapuakitanga o ngā pepeha. Ka hāpai tēnei i ngā tamariki ki te whakahonohono i a rātou anō ki te whenua, ki tō rātou iwi hoki. Ka ākina ngā tamariki tuākana ki te kōrero mō ō rātou pepeha, tae atu ki ō rātou hononga ā-whakapapa, me te whānau. He pai te whakapuaki o ngā tuākana i ō rātou pepeha, i ngā mihi hoki, ā, he tino tauira rātou mō ngā tamariki tēina.

Mana Reo. He pai te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. Ko te ako i te reo Māori te aronga matua mō tēnei whānau. He tino tauira ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki ako reo e whai hua ana ki te hāpai me te whakatairanga i te whakamahinga o te reo Māori mā ngā tamariki me te whānau. Ko ngā kaimahi, te whānau, me ngā tamariki ka whai wāhi atu ki te tākaro i ngā kēmu reo, te waiata, te taki i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo karakia, me te whakatutuki haere i ngā tikanga Māori. Ka akiaki, ka whakanui hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki i a rātou e whakamahi ana i te reo Māori.

Mana Aotūroa. E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki kia tūhura haere i te ao e noho nei rātou. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te tāutu i ngā tae me ngā āhua, me te kaute hoki. Ka whakamahia ngā pakiwaitara, ngā pukapuka, me ngā whakaaturanga hei akiaki i te ngākau nui o ngā tamariki ki te pānui. Ka akiaki hoki i ngā tamariki ki te ako i ngā kupu hou. E whanake haere ana te ngākau nui o ngā tamariki ki ngā pukapuka, ngā ariā pāngarau, me te ako reo.

Mana Atua. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i te whanaungatanga e manaaki ana, e poipoi ana hoki i waenga i a rātou anō, i ngā pākeke hoki. Ka ākina e ngā kaimahi te mauritau, te atawhai hoki o ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki. Ka whai pūtake ngā tākaro a ngā tamariki, ka tohatoha hoki i ngā taputapu, ka noho mārama ki ngā ture me te whanonga pai, ā, ka tiaki tētahi i tētahi. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Te wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake

Te aromātai i te hōtaka akoranga. Kāhore anō te aromātai hōtaka kia āta whakaritea. I tēnei wā, kāhore ngā kaimahi e aromātai ana i tēnā e whai hua ana, ngā āhuatanga hoki ki te whakapai ake hei akiaki i te ako a ngā tamariki, me ngā whakapaitanga hoki ki te hōtaka. Me whakamahi i te aromātai hōtaka i whakapai ake ai, hei whakamōhio i ngā mahere hōtaka, ā, hei whakatutuki hoki i ngā matea me ngā pūmanawa o ia tamaiti.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki?

E whanake haere ana te āheinga o te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te arotake i te tūtohinga. Kua whakahoungia, kua whakamahinetia hoki te tūtohinga o te kōhanga reo, kia mārama ai, kia whai pānga ai anō hoki. Ka hāngai te tūtohinga ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, ā, he mārama ngā aratohu me ngā tūmanako, hei hāpai i te whakahaeretanga o ngā whakaritenga o ia rā.

Te arotake i ngā kaupapa here. He pai te whakahaeretanga o te whānau i te arotake o ngā kaupapa here. Kua arotakengia, kua whakamahinetia hoki e rātou ngā kaupapa here matua me ngā tukanga, tae atu ki te hauora me te haumaru, ngā kaimahi, te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me ngā whakawhanaketanga. He tika ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e ārahi ana i te whānau.

Te whakamahere rautaki. He pai te whakamahere a te whānau me ngā kaimahi i tā rātou ahunga whakamua. He mahere rautaki tā rātou e whakatakoto ana i ā rātou kaupapa matua hei whakawhanake i roto i te roanga o te wā. Ka arotahi te whānau ki te whakapai i te āheinga o ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, me te whakapakari anō hoki i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He mahere tā te whānau kia whakatutukihia e rātou ngā whāinga.

Te wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Te arotake whaiaro. Kāhore anō ngā pūnaha me ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro kia āta whakaritea. Me whakapai ake te pūrongo i te maha o ngā tūranga whakahaere matua, me ngā kawenga mahi matua. Kāhore i te rawaka ngā pūrongo o tēnei wā, ā, he iti noa te kiko o roto hei āta whakamōhio i te whānau mō ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia tahuri te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te:

  • whakapakari i ngā mahi arotake whaiaro hei whakapai ake i te kounga o ngā mōhiohio e whakamahia ana e te whānau ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga
  • whakapai i te aromātai hōtaka, kia āhei ai rātou ki te whakapai haere i ngā wā katoa, i te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga mā ngā tamariki.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu mō ngā Ratonga Arotake Māori (Te Uepū ā-Motu)

5 Mahuru 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Motutaiko is in Taupō, in the Ngāti Tuwharetoa region. The kōhanga reo takes its name from a sacred island on Lake Taupō.

Learning te reo Māori is the primary focus for the whānau. The staff, whānau and children play language games, sing waiata, recite a range of karakia and participate in tikanga Māori. Children are developing well as speakers of te reo Māori.

Children have a developing interest in books, mathematical concepts and language learning. They are well supported to explore the world around them. Oral stories, books and displays are used to encourage children’s interest in reading.

Children are happy and settled at the kōhanga reo. Children experience positive relationships with each other and adults. Children play purposefully together, take turns with equipment, understand boundaries and look after one another.

Staff celebrate and promote the recitation of pepeha. These daily rituals help children to make connections for themselves to the land and their people. The older children are encouraged to talk about their pepeha including their whakapapa connections and whānau members. Children have a developing knowledge of who they are and where they come from.

The kōhanga reo charter has been updated and refined so that it is clear and relevant. The document now provides clear expectations and guidelines to assist the whānau to manage the kōhanga reo operations.

The whānau and staff plan well for their future direction. The whānau is focussed on improving the te reo Māori capability of staff and whānau and strengthening the kōhanga reo operations. The whānau has a plan to achieve their goals.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • ngā taumata whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

How well are children supported to learn concepts about ngā taumata whakahirahira?

Children are well supported to learn about concepts associated with ngā taumata whakahirahira.

Areas of good performance

Mana Whenua, Mana Tangata. Children have a developing knowledge of who they are and where they come from. Kaimahi and whānau promote children’s sense of belonging. They celebrate and promote the use of pepeha. This helps children to make connections for themselves to the land and their people. The older children are encouraged to talk about their pepeha including their whakapapa connections and whānau members. Tuakana present their pepeha and mihi well and are good role models for the younger children.

Mana Reo. Children are developing well as speakers of te reo Māori. Learning te reo Māori is the primary focus for this whānau. The staff are positive language models for children. They use a wide range of effective language learning strategies to support and promote the use of te reo Māori for children and whānau. The staff, whānau and children play language games, sing waiata and participate in daily karakia and tikanga Māori. Kaimahi encourage and praise children when they use te reo Māori.

Mana Aotūroa. Children are well supported to explore the world around them. The staff encourage children to identify colours and shapes and to count. Stories, books and displays are used to encourage children’s interest in reading. Children are encouraged to learn new vocabulary. Children have a developing interest in books, mathematical concepts and language learning.

Mana Atua. Children experience caring and nurturing relationships with each other and adults. Positive and warm interactions among children are encouraged by kaimahi. Children play together in purposeful ways, take turns with equipment, share learning experiences, understand rules and boundaries and look after one another well. Children are happy and settled at the kōhanga reo.

Area for development

Evaluation of the learning programme. Programme evaluation is not well established. Staff currently do not evaluate what is working well, what can be done to improve and encourage children’s learning or what improvements can be made in the programme. Improved programme evaluation should be used to inform programme planning and to meet the needs and strengths of individual children.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How well is the kōhanga reo managed in the interests of children?

The whānau and staff are developing in their capability to manage the kōhanga reo in the interest of children.

Areas of good performance

The charter reflects the kaupapa of Kōhanga Reo and provides clear guidelines and expectations to manage daily operations.

Policy review. The whānau manages policy review well. They have reviewed and refined some key policies with procedures including health and safety, personnel, planning, assessment and development. The whānau is guided by suitable policies and procedures.

Strategic planning. The whānau and staff plan well for their future direction. They have a strategic plan that documents their long term priorities for development. The whānau is focussed on improving the te reo Māori capability of staff and whānau and strengthening the kōhanga reo operations. The whānau has a suitable plan to achieve their goals.

Area for development

Self review. Self review systems and practices are not well established. Reporting about many key management roles and responsibilities requires improvement. Current reports are inadequate and have insufficient detail to keep the whānau well informed about the kōhanga reo operations.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO LicensedKōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends the whānau and staff

  • strengthen self review practices to improve the quality of information used by whānau to make decisions
  • improve programme evaluation to enable them to continually improve the quality of the learning programme for children.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
