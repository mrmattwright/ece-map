1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rāhuitanga ki ngā whenua nō te Tarahiti o Kōkiri, ki Ōtara, i Tāmaki-makau-rau ki te tonga. Kua roa nei ngā tau e whakahaerehia ana te kōhanga reo noho tāone nei. He wawata ō te whānau, kia ako ā rātou tamariki i te reo Māori.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i ō rātou ngākau titikaha hei ākonga?

He harikoa, he tākare hoki ngā tamariki hei ākonga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He hononga whakaute ō ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i te whakaute me te manaaki mō ētahi atu. E ākina ana te whakapuaki mai o ngā tamariki i ō rātou whakaaro i ngā wā ako ā-rōpū. Ka whakaatuhia e ngā kaimahi tō rātou aroha ki ngā tamariki, ā, e poipoia ana rātou ki ngā kupu whakamihi. Ko te wā karakia te wā e whakataungia ana ngā tamariki hei whakatīmata i ngā mahi o te rā. Ka āta whakarite ngā kaimahi i te noho mārama o ngā tamariki tuākana ki tā rātou kawenga, kia manaakitia ngā pēpi. He atawhai tō te hononga ki waenga i ngā tuākana me ngā tēina. Ka ako ngā pēpi ki tētahi taiao e mau nei i ngā hononga e whai pūtake ana, ā, koia nei te tūāpapa o ā rātou akoranga. Ka tautāwhi ngā kaimahi i ngā pēpi. He mauritau te āhua o ngā tamariki.

Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga e hāpai ana i ō rātou tūmomo matea ake. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i tētahi taiao muramura o te atawhai me te aroha mō ngā tamariki. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whiriwhiri me te taki i ngā waiata. He tākare tā rātou kōkiri i te kaupapa nei. I ngā wā whāriki, ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te tū me te whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro ki ētahi atu. Ka tūmāia ngā tamariki tuākana i aua wā. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te patapatai me te whakawero i ngā whakaaro i whakapuakihia ai e ētahi atu. Ko ngā tamariki e whaikaha ako ana, e tino hāpaitia ana kia whai wāhi atu, kia whakapuaki anō hoki i ō rātou whakaaro. Ka whiwhi ngā pēpi i ngā taunekeneke o tētahi ki tētahi. Kei te tino mōhio ngā kaimahi ki ngā pēpi. Ka aro nui rātou ki ō rātou hiahia ā-whatumanawa, ā-tinana hoki. Kei te harikoa, kei te mauritau hoki ngā tamariki.

He pakari ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te reo Māori mā tētahi taiao e hāpai ana i a rātou. Ka āta kapo atu ngā kaimahi ki ngā tūmomo horopaki ka pupū ake, kia puāwai ai ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki te taha o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā tuākana i te reo Māori ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro. He tākare te whakapuaki a ngā tamariki i ō rātou mōhiotanga ki ngā tau, ngā tūāhua, me ngā tae. He pārekareka ki a rātou te whakarongo ki ētahi atu e pānui ana i ngā pukapuka reo Māori, ā, ka taea e rātou ngā tuhinga me ngā pikitia te tūhonohono ki ngā kupu. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā whakaaturanga i te pakitara me ngā rauemi ki te whakapakari tonu i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. Kei te mārama ngā pēpi ki tēnā e kōrerohia ana e ētahi atu, ā, ka tāwhai anō hoki rātou i aua kōrero. E puāwai ana te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

E ākina ana te tūhura a ngā tamariki i te ao e noho nei rātou. Kei ngā wāhi o roto, o waho hoki ngā whai wāhitanga e āta whakawero ana i te kori tinana o ngā tamariki. Haere ai ngā tamariki ki te takaporepore i te pokapū o te hapori, ā, ka pakari haere tā rātou kōkiri i ō rātou ake tinana. Ka whai wāhi atu hoki ngā tamariki ki te pakitara pikipiki o roto. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tama ki te āta whai wāhi nui ki te tākaro matawhānui e korikori ana i ō rātou tinana. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te hangarau, te pūtaiao, me te pāngarau ki te kapo atu me te whakawhānui ake i ngā whakaaro o ngā tamariki. Ka whakangahau ngā tamariki i a rātou e whakawero ana i ō rātou whakaaro, e hīraurau hopanga ana, ā, e whakapuaki ana hoki i ō rātou whakaaro ki ētahi atu. He ākonga mātātoa ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He tukanga ā ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere i te hōtaka, ki te aromatawai i ngā mahi a ngā tamariki, ā, ki te aromātai hoki i te hōtaka. He ātaahua te whakatakotoranga o ngā mahere ki ngā pakitara, ā, ka kitea te aronga nui ki te whakawhanake i ngā pūkenga e pā ana ki te reo matatini me te pāngarau. Ka whakamahere ngātahi ngā kaimahi, ā, ka āta whakarite rātou i te whakatutukitanga o aua mahere i ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Ka aroturukitia te aromatawai i te ako a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamōhio i te whānau mō te ako a ngā tamariki, mā ngā pūrongo whai kiko a ngā kaiako e whakatakotohia ana ki ngā hui ā-whānau. Ka arotahi te aromātai hōtaka ki te aroturuki i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakaaro huritao ngā kaimahi ki tēnā hei huarahi tonu mō rātou, kia pai ake ai ngā painga mō ngā tamariki. E āta poipoia ana te ako a ngā tamariki.

Ka noho matua ki te whakahaeretanga a te whānau, ko te hauora me te waiora o ngā tamariki. He mahere ā-tau tā te whānau. E āta whakarite ana taua mahere i te whakatutukitanga o ngā mahi matua i roto i te whakahaeretanga o te tari. Kua arotakengia, kua whakahoungia hoki ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga. Kua whakaritea ngā rehita e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru, ā, e āta aroturukitia ana hoki. Kua whakatakotohia anō hoki ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā tūranga mahi a ngā kaimahi. Kua whakaritea ngā mahi arohaehae. E haere tonu ana te arotake whaiaro i ia te wā, ā, e arotahi ana ki te whakapai ake i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka whai pānga matua te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kua whakatakotohia e te whānau tētahi mahere rautaki. Hei whakapakari ake i te aronga rautaki a te kōhanga reo, me mahi tonu te whānau ki te taha o te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te whakatōpū i ngā whāinga me te mahere mahi. Tē taea e te whānau, e ā rātou tamariki hoki te whakatūturu te noho pūmau o te kōhanga reo mō meāke nei.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakarite i ngā whāinga rautaki, i tētahi mahere mahi hoki, hei whakatinana i ngā wawata o te whānau.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rāhuitanga i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rāhuitanga is situated on land that is owned by the Kōkiri Trust in Ōtara, South Auckland. This urban kōhanga reo has been operating for many years. The whānau has aspirations for their children to learn te reo Māori.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children show confidence as learners?

Children are happy and enthusiastic learners.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children have respectful relationships with others. Kaimahi model respect and care for others. Children are welcomed to contribute their ideas during group learning. Kaimahi display love for children who are nurtured with praise. Karakia is a time when children are settled to begin their day. Kaimahi ensure that the older children understand their responsibility to care for the babies. Tuakana teina relationships are affectionate. The babies learn in an environment where meaningful interactions are the basis for their learning. Kaimahi keep babies close. Children appear secure and settled.

Children engage in learning that provides for their different needs. Kaimahi provide a warm, bright and loving environment for children. Children are given opportunities to choose and lead waiata. They do this with enthusiasm. During wā whāriki, kaimahi offer children time to stand and share their ideas with others. Older children are able to do this with confidence. Kaimahi encourage children to ask questions and challenge the ideas that others have offered. Children with learning challenges are well supported to participate and share their thoughts. Babies receive one to one interactions. Kaimahi know the babies really well. They are responsive to their emotional and physical demands. Children are happy and settled.

Children confidently explore te reo Māori in a supportive environment. Kaimahi make the most of situations to stimulate conversations with children. Tuakana use te reo Māori to express themselves. Children enthusiastically share their knowledge of numbers, shapes and colours. They enjoy hearing others read te reo Māori books, and can link text and pictures to words. Kaimahi utilise the wall displays and resources to build on children’s language development. Babies understand what is being said by others and mimic what they hear. Children are developing as early speakers of te reo Māori.

Children are encouraged to explore their world around them. Both the indoor and outdoor areas provide opportunities for children to be challenged physically. Children attend gymnastics at the local centre where they can gain confidence in the control of their bodies. Children take advantage of the indoor climbing wall. Kaimahi enable boys to engage confidently in constructive physical play. Kaimahi utilise technology, science and maths to engage and extend children’s learning. Children have fun as they challenge their own thinking, solve problems, and share their thoughts with others. Children are active learners.

Planning and Evaluation

Kaimahi have processes in place for programme planning, children’s assessments and programme evaluation. Planning is attractively displayed on walls and shows a strong focus on developing literacy and numeracy skills. Kaimahi plan collaboratively and ensure these plans cater for children’s interests. Assessment of children’s learning is monitored. The whānau is informed of children’s learning through the detailed kaiako reports presented at whānau hui. Programme evaluation focuses on monitoring the effectiveness of the learning programme. Kaimahi reflect on what they need to do to improve outcomes for children. Children’s learning is well catered for.

Whānau management prioritises the health and wellbeing for children. The whānau has an annual plan in place. This ensures critical timelines for key administration tasks are met. Policies and procedures have been reviewed and updated. Health and safety registers are in place and well monitored. Kaimahi employment documentation is in place. Appraisals are in place. Self-review is ongoing and is focussed on improvements for the management of kōhanga reo. Children’s health and safety is important.

Key Next Steps

The whānau has set a strategic plan. In order to strengthen the strategic direction of the kōhanga reo, the whānau will need to continue to work alongside the Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust to consolidate the goals and action plan. The whānau and their children cannot be assured that the future of their kōhanga reo is secure.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • Establish strategic goals and an action plan to realise the aspirations of whānau.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rāhuitanga will be in three years.
