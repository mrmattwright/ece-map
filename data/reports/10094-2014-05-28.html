1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kei Tāmaki-makau-rau ki te Tonga, i te tāone o Ōtara, Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Huinga Reo. E tū anganui ana te kōhanga reo ki Te Whare Takiura o Manukau. Kua whakawhanakehia e te whānau tētahi taiao akoranga o roto, o waho hoki e whakaongaongatia ana. He nui ngā rauemi me ngā rauhanga ki te taiao nei, ā, e poipoi ana tēnei i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo matea, ngākau nuitanga hoki o ngā tamariki.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga o te taiao ako reo. Ka rongo rātou i te reo Māori e whakamahia ana ki te whānuitanga o ngā horopaki. Ka whai hua te whakahaeretanga a ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā rauemi a ngā tamariki me te taiao akoranga. Ka whai hua rātou ki te whakamahere i ngā hōtaka kia puāwai ai te aroha ki te ako reo, me te whakawhanake i te māia me te āheinga o ngā tamariki i roto i te reo Māori.

Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi tētahi horopaki ako e whakawātea ana i ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te katoa o te taiao. Whakapuakihia ai e ngā tamariki ngā waiata i ia te wā, ā, ka ako ngātahi rātou hei tuakana, hei teina. Ka poipoi tēnei aronga i te tino whakawhanaketanga o te whakahoahoa me ngā taunekeneke whakaute. Ko ngā mātāpono o te whanaungatanga, tae atu ki te manaaki me te tiaki, e pou here ana i te āhua ka poipoia te waiora o te katoa o te tamaiti. He māia, he harikoa, he mauritau hoki ngā tamariki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo.

He pai te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki me te whānau. Ka ruruku ngā mema o te whānau ki te whakatupuranga, te whakawhanaketanga anō hoki i tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka arotahi rātou ki te whakapūmau i ngā putanga hua mō ngā tamariki, ngā kaimahi, ā, mō rātou anō hoki. Kua āta whakaritea ngā mahi e pā ana ki te whakahaere, me te whakamahere, te pūrongo, me ngā tukanga arotake hoki, ā, ka hāpai ēnei i te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga takohanga. Ko ngā whakaritenga hauora, haumaru hoki, tae atu ki te arowhai i ia te wā, e whai pānga ana ki te tāutu i ngā āhuatanga e noho mōrearea ana pea mō ngā tamariki.

Ka whai hua te whakamahere hōtaka. Ka tāutuhia ngā mahere i ngā putanga ako whānui nō Te Whāriki. Ka whakamahia aua putanga hei pūtake mō ngā mahi aromatawai me te aromātai hoki. Kei te mōhio pai ngā kaimahi ki ā rātou tamariki, tae atu ki ō rātou matea whakawhanaketanga. He ngāwari noa mā ngā tamariki me te whānau te whakapā atu ki ngā pūkete ako e kapo ake ana i ngā wheako me ngā akoranga a ia tamaiti. He pai ngā mōhiohio ka whakaemihia e ngā kaimahi mō te tupuranga me te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki i tō rātou noho ki te kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga reo (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau, me te whakamahere rautaki

  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

E pēhea ana te kaha o te tautoko mō ngā tamariki me te whānau ki te ako me te kōrero i te reo Māori?

E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki me te whānau kia ako, kia kōrero hoki i te reo Māori.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Mana Reo. Ka whai hua te whakatauira i te reo Māori ki ngā tamariki. Ka tino tautokona ngā kaimahi me te whānau e te kaiwhakahaere kua roa nei i whai wāhi ai ki tēnei kōhanga reo.

Ka whakapau kaha ngā kaimahi ki te patapātai, te wero, me te akiaki i te whakamahinga o te reo. Haere ai ngā mātua ki ngā akoranga o te reo Māori ki te kōhanga reo, ki te hapori hoki. E āhei ana ngā tamariki tuākana ki te aro atu ki te reo Māori me te whai wāhi ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero. Ka aro atu, ka whai atu hoki ngā tamariki tēina i ngā tohutohu ngāwari. Ka tautoko ngā tamariki tuākana i ngā tamariki tēina ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori. Taunekeneke ai, whakawhiti kōrero ai hoki ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa, i te whānuitanga hoki o te horopaki ako hoki, puta noa i te rā.

Ka tino tautoko i ngā tamariki e whanake haere ana ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Mana Atua. Ka whakapau kaha ngā pākeke ki te āta whakatau i ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. He pai ngā mahi a ngā pākeke ki te whakaako, te whakapūmau, me te whakahaere i ngā mahinga mā ngā tamariki.

  • Ka tino mōhio, ka tino whakatutuki hoki ngā tamariki i ngā mahinga o ia rā

  • Ka kitea te whakaaro nui o ngā tamariki ki te taha wairua me te taha tangata, mā te āhua o tā rātou kawe i ngā karakia me ngā mōteatea

  • Ka tautoko ngā tamariki i a rātou anō ki te taki i ngā pepeha, te āta tākaro ngātahi, ā, ka tautoko hoki tētahi i tētahi i ngā wā kai

  • Ka kitea te tamaiti e tiaki ana i a ia anō, e hāpai ana hoki i a ia anō

  • Ka kitea te noho whakaute o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao, ā, ka whakatika haere rātou i ō rātou ake wāhi i ia te wā.

He harikoa, he mauritau hoki te āhua o ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Mana Tangata. He tau, he atawhai hoki te āhua o ngā hononga me ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā tamariki, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi. Ka mahi ngātahi ngā pākeke, ā, ka whakapau kaha ki te whakarato i tētahi taiao hāneanea e noho haumaru ana mō ngā tamariki.

  • E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te maumahara me te taki i ō rātou ake pepeha.

  • He haumaru, he tau hoki te āhua o ngā tamariki ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo.

  • Ka kōkiri, ka whakapūmau hoki ngā tamariki i ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu.

Whakamana ai, whakanui ai hoki ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, ngā whakamātau me ngā whakatutukitanga a ngā tamariki. Ka hāpai tēnei i te aro tonu o ngā tamariki me tā rātou tākaro ngātahi. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā whakaahua, ngā kōwaiwai pakitara, me ngā whakaaturanga hei whakanui i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hononga ā-whānau.

Ka whakauru ngā tamariki tuākana i ō rātou teina ki ā rātou tūmomo tākaro, ā, ka tautoko hoki i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā mahinga, i te reo Māori hoki.

He pakari, he hāneanea hoki ngā tamariki ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo.

Mana Aotūroa. Ka whakarite, ka whakarato hoki ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau ngā taumahi i āta whakaritea ai, hei whakahihiri, hei whakaongaonga hoki i ngā tamariki.

  • E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whakatau i ā rātou whiringa, me te tīpako anō hoki mai i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi, taumahi hoki

  • Ka tūhura ngā tamariki i te taiao Māori e karapoti ana i te kōhanga reo, tae atu ki ngā māra huawhenua

  • Haereere ai ngā tamariki i te hapori, ki tua atu hoki i te takiwā, hei hāpai i ngā hōtaka akoranga

  • Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te taiao o te hapori.

E whanake ana te pākiki o ngā tamariki ki te ao e noho nei rātou.

Mana Whenua. He pai te tautoko o ngā pākeke i ngā tamariki, kia ako ai rātou i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te taiao tonu, puta noa anō hoki i tō rātou kōhanga reo.

  • He pakari ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo

  • Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā whai wāhitanga ki te ārahi

  • Ka whai whakaaro ngā tamariki ki ngā manuhiri me ngā uaratanga, pērā i te manaaki, te tiaki, me te whakatau.

He maha ngā wheako papai a ngā tamariki ki te hapori.

Te taiao akoranga. He wāteatea, he mahana, he mā hoki te taiao pīataata o roto, o waho hoki e whakaratohia ana e te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i tō rātou pūmanawa auaha, te whakapakari tinana, te taha whakaari, te whakaaro pohewa hoki, me ngā pūkenga e pā ana ki te reo matatini me te pāngarau. E whai hua ana ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te poipoi i ngā tūmomo reanga, āheinga hoki o ngā tamariki. Ka whakarato ki a rātou ngā tūmomo taumahi, rauemi hoki e whai pānga nui ana ki ō rātou ake tūmomo reanga ako. He ātaahua, he hihiri hoki te taiao ki ngā tamariki nā ngā tūmomo whakaaturanga. He pakari te whakatewhatewha haere a ngā tamariki i tō rātou taiao muramura, whakaongaonga hoki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Te whai wāhi a ngā tuākana ki te kōkiri i a rātou anō. Ahakoa e ai ki ngā kaimahi ka tautoko rātou i ngā tuākana ki te kōkiri ake, te kawe ake hoki i a rātou anō puta noa i te rā, he maha tonu ngā kawenga kia ako ngā tuākana. Ko ētahi o ēnei ka whai pānga hoki ki te whakahaere me te tiaki a te tamaiti i a ia anō.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whai hua o te whānau ki te whakamahere mō te ahunga whakamua o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei?

He pai te whānau ki te whakamahere mō te ahunga whakamua o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Te whakamahere mō te wā roa.Kei te whānau ngā whāinga mō te wā roa me ngā mahere anō hoki e whakaraupapa ana i te ahunga whakamua o tō rātou kōhanga reo ā meāke nei. Kei taua anga mahi ngā hoaketanga rautaki, ngā mahi rautaki me ngā whāinga hoki. Ka hāngai anō hoki aua āhuatanga ki tō Te Ara Tūāpae. Ka whai pūtake ngā whāinga, ka taea te whakatutuki, ā, he aronga anō hoki ki ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki, me te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori. Kua tāutuhia ngā mahinga rautaki me ngā whakatutukitanga ki ngā meneti o ngā hui ā-whānau. Ko tētahi āhuatanga nui o te aronga rautaki, ko te whakarite i tētahi wāhi ki te kōhanga reo, mō ngā akoranga a te whānau i te reo Māori. He mārama te tirohanga o te whānau mō te ahunga whakamua o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Te whakamahere i ngā kaupapa, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.He mārama ki ngā kaimahi ngā mahi ki te whakamahere i ngā hōtaka ā-kaupapa mō ā rātou tamariki. Ka whakamahere ia kaimahi mō ā rātou ake rōpū reanga. Haere ai ngā kaimahi ki ngā hui ā-purapura o te hapori, ā, i reira, ka tautokona rātou, ā, ka whakapuaki anō hoki i ō rātou whakaaro e pā ana ki te whakamahere me te aromatawai. Ka ākina ngā mātua kia whai wāhi ki ngā mahi whakamahere.

He tino hononga tō ngā mahere ā-kaupapa ki Te Whāriki. Ka whakawhiti kōrero ngā kaimahi mō te hōtaka akoranga, kia tau ai ngā whakapaitanga ake ki te hōtaka me ā rātou ake mahi. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā kaupapa e whakapūmau ana i te ao Māori me te ao whānui.

Whakaaro huritao ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, ā, ka whakawhiti kōrero anō hoki mō ngā hōtaka i ngā hui o ngā kaimahi. Ka tāutu rātou i ngā wāhanga i whai hua ai, me ērā hoki hei whakapai ake.

Ka whai pānga nui te pūkete a ia tamaiti ki āna whai wāhitanga ki ngā akoranga. He ātaahua aua pūkete, ā, he mōhiohio anō hoki mō te āhua o ngā mahi me ngā tākaro o ngā tamariki. Ka āta aroturukitia ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga.

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau.Ka whai hua te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. Tū ai ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, ā, he pai te tae ā-tinana ana a ngā mema o te whānau. Nā ngā pānui o ia te wā ka noho mōhio ngā mātua ki ngā kaupapa i te kōhanga reo me ngā mōhiohio whānui. He mārama, he pakari hoki te whakahaeretanga o ngā pūtea. Ka kitea te arotake whaiaro puta noa i te whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo. Tautoko ai ngā mātua i te kōhanga reo i ia te wā, mā te whānau awhi, ngā haerenga, me te tiaki tamariki i te wā o te hōtaka akoranga. He mārama, he wātea hoki ngā pūnaha whakahaere a te tari, ā, kua āta whakaritea hoki. Ko ngā painga mō ngā tamariki, ka noho matua ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhangame tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki: 

  • te whakahaere tari;
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga;
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi;
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa. 

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki: 

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino);
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho);
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi; me
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia:

  • whakarato ngā kaimahi i te maha ake o ngā kawenga, o ngā whai wāhitanga anō hoki ki te ārahi, hei āhuatanga hoki o tā rātou whakawhiti atu ki te kura.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Huinga Reo is an urban kōhanga reo in Ōtara, South Auckland. The kōhanga reo is located opposite the Manukau Institute of Technology campus. Whānau have developed a stimulating indoor and outdoor learning environment. This is well resourced and furnished and caters for the diverse needs and interests of children.

The whānau chose Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira as the focus for the review

Children are immersed in a rich language learning environment. They hear te reo Māori used in a variety of contexts. Kaimahi and whānau effectively manage children’s resources and the environment for learning. They plan programmes to successfully foster a love of language learning and to develop children’s confidence and capability in te reo Māori.

The kaimahi have created a learning setting that allows children to use the environment to its full capacity. Children frequently share waiata and learn beside each other as tuakana and taina. This approach fosters positive social development and respectful interactions. The principles of whanaungatanga, including manaaki and tiaki underpin the way in which children’s holistic wellbeing is nurtured. Children are confident, happy and settled in their kōhanga reo.

The kōhanga reo is well managed in the interests of children and whānau. Whānau members are committed to continual growth and development of their kōhanga reo. They focus on sustaining successful outcomes for children, kaimahi and themselves. Management practices, planning, reporting and review processes are well established and assist the whānau to meet their accountability obligations. Health and safety practices including regular maintenance checks help identify potential risks to children.

Programme planning is effective. Plans identify general learning outcomes from Te Whāriki. These outcomes are used as a basis for assessment and evaluation. Kaimahi know their children well including their developmental needs. Children and whānau have easy access to learning portfolios that capture each child’s experiences and learning. Kaimahi gather good information about children’s growth and development during their time at kōhanga reo.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning; and
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

How well are tamariki and whānau supported to learn and speak te reo Māori?

Tamariki and whānau are well supported to learn and speak te reo Māori.

Areas of good performance

Mana Reo. Te reo Māori is modelled successfully for children. Kaimahi and the whānau are well supported by the kaiwhakahaere who has a long association with this kōhanga reo.

Kaimahi use every opportunity to question, challenge and encourage language use.Parents attend te reo Māori classes held at the kōhanga reo and in the community. Older children can respond to te reo Māori and participate in conversations. Younger children respond to and follow simple commands and instructions. Older children support younger children to use te reo Māori. Kaimahi constantly interact and communicate with children in various learning contexts throughout the day. Children are well supported as growing speakers of te reo Māori.

Mana Atua. Adults make every effort to ensure children are settled at the kōhanga reo. Adults work well to teach, reinforce and manage routines for children. Children:

  • know daily routines well and are confident in them

  • demonstrate spiritual and cultural respect through their use of karakia and mōteatea

  • support each other to recite pepeha, to play well together and support each other during kai time

  • display self care and self help behaviours

  • show respect for their environment and they regularly tidy up after themselves.

Children appear happy and settled in their kōhanga reo environment.

Mana Tangata. Relationships and interactions among children, whānau members and kaimahi are positive and caring. Adults work together and make every effort to provide children with a safe, secure and comfortable environment. Children:

  • are able to recall and recite their own pepeha
  • demonstrate that they feel safe and secure in the kōhanga reo environment
  • initiate, maintain and enjoy relationships with others.

Kaimahi regularly acknowledge and affirm children’s efforts and successes. This encourages children to stay focussed and to play cooperatively. They use photos, murals and displays to promote learning about children’s whānau connections.

The older children include the younger children in their play and support them with routines and te reo Māori.

Children are confident and comfortable in the kōhanga reo environment.

Mana Aotūroa. The staff and whānau members prepare and provide well organised activities that are interesting and stimulating for children who:

  • are able to make choices and select from a wide range of resources and activities
  • explore the natural environment surrounding the kōhanga reo including box vegetable gardens
  • go on trips in the community and outside the local area to support learning programmes
  • learn about the local environment.

Children are developing a curiosity about the world around them.

Mana Whenua. Adults support children well to learn about the immediate environment in and around their kōhanga reo. Children:

  • are confident in their kōhanga reo environment
  • experience leadership opportunities
  • are aware of visitors and values like manaaki tiaki, and whakatau.

Children have many positive experiences in the local community.

Learning environment. The kōhanga reo provides a spacious, warm, clean, bright environment for children both indoors and outdoors. Children have opportunities to develop their creative potential, physical proficiency, dramatic and imaginative interests and literacy and numeracy skills. Kaimahi successfully cater for the different levels and abilities of children. They provide them with activities and resources suitable to their learning levels. Displays make the environment attractive and inviting for children. Children confidently investigate their colourful, stimulating environment.

Areas for development

Independence for tuakana. While kaimahi state that they support tuakana to be more independent and responsible throughout the day, there are still many areas of responsibility that tuakana could learn. These include self management and self care.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How well does the whānau plan for the future of the kōhanga reo?

The whānau plans well for the future of the kōhanga reo.

Areas of good performance

Long term planning. Whānau have long term goals and plans outlining the future direction for their kōhanga reo. The framework includes strategic objectives, actions and goals. These align to Te Ara Tūāpae. Goals are practical, achievable and there is a focus on children’s needs and interests and te reo Māori development. Strategic actions and achievements are identified in whānau hui minutes. A major focus of the strategic direction is to establish a te reo Māori learning facility for whānau members onsite. The whānau has a clear vision for the future of their kōhanga reo.

Kaupapa planning, assessment and evaluation. The kaimahi understand how to plan kaupapa programmes for their children. Each kaimahi plans for their individual age groups. Kaimahi attend local purapura hui where they are supported with, and share planning and assessment ideas. Parents are encouraged to have input into planning.

Kaupapa plans are clearly linked to Te Whāriki. Kaimahi discuss the learning programme in order to make improvements to the programme and to their own practice. Children participate in kaupapa that reinforce te ao Māori and the wider world.

Kaimahi regularly reflect on and discuss programmes at kaimahi hui. They identify areas that went well and areas that might be improved.

Individual profile books highlight children’s involvement in learning. These are attractive and informative displays of children at work and at play. Childrens’ learning and development is well monitored.

Whānau management. The kōhanga reo is effectively managed by the whānau. There are regular whānau hui that are well attended by whānau members. Regular pānui keep parents informed about kōhanga reo events and general information. Financial management is transparent and robust. Self review is evident across all areas of kōhanga reo management and operation. Parents regularly support the kōhanga reo through whānau awhi, trips and supervision during the learning programme. Office systems are clear, accessible and well organised. Children’s interests are a priority in the management of the kōhanga reo.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake. 

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends:

  • that kaimahi provide older children with more responsibility and leadership opportunities as part of their transition to school.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
