2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ko te Poari Kaitiaki o Kokiri Marae Keriana Olsen te rōpū mana whakahaere o Te Kōhanga Reo o Kokiri Marae me Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hono Ki Kokiri ki Wainuiomata me Te Kōhanga Reo o Paparākau ki Whakatiki.

E whakarato ana te kaiwhakahaere i nga pūkenga tātaki mō ngā whakahaerenga katoa o te Poari Kaitiaki, ā, ko te whakahaere i ngā kōhanga reo ōnā haepapa tātaki. Ka mahi tahi tētahi kaihāpai ki te taha o te kaiwhakahaere kia tōtika ai ngā whakahaere a ngā kōhanga reo e ai ki te tirohanga whakamua o te Poari Kaitiaki, tō rātou tino kaupapa, ō rātou kaupapa here me ngā whakaritenga o ia rā a te Poari Kaitiaki.

I whakatūria e Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hono Ki Kokiri i raro i ngā whakahaere o Keriana Olsen i te tau 1989. He whakamāmā i te taumaha o te whānau mai i Wainuiomata e hiahiatia ai te whakauru ā rātou tamariki ki Te Kōhanga Reo o Kokiri Marae. Kei te tino tautoko te whānau o te kōhanga reo, ā, e tino manawanui ana rātou ki tō rātou kaupapa. Ka tae mai ētahi o ngā matua ki te kōhanga reo ia te wā, ia te wā. E tino manawanui ana ngā kaimahi ki ā rātou whai wāhitanga ki te kōhanga reo.

Kua whakaputaina e te whānau ō rātou manawanui ki te kōhanga reo. E ngākau nui ana, ā, e tino ū ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakarato i ngā akoranga whai painga mō ā rātou tamariki. Ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

Ko te whakaako, te whakanui, te akiaki, te arahi me te tautoko i ngā mokopuna me te whānau ki te pupuri i ō rātou oranga ā-wairua, oranga ā-tinana, oranga ā-noho me ō rātou oranga ā-ahurea te whānuitanga o te kaupapa arotahi.

Kua ākina ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi ki te hāpai i ngā tūranga kaihautū mō ngā wāhanga ōkawa o te hōtaka. E whakarato ana ngā kaimahi i ngā wheako akoranga e taea e ngā tamariki te kaha arotahi mō te wā roa. Ka puku mahi ngā tamariki, ā, i te nuinga o te wā ka pīkau ētahi tamariki i ō rātou ake akoranga. Ka tino whai wāhi atu ngā kaimahi ki te tautoko i ngā tamariki i te wā e ako ana rātou.

E whakatutuki ana te hōtaka mō ngā pēpi i ō rātou matea ā-tinana me ō rātou matea ā-whatumanawa. E whakarato ana te kōhanga reo me ngā kaimahi i tētahi wāhi mauritau me tētahi wāhi e rere ai te aroha. He tino whānui te taiao ō-roto, ā, kua āta whakaritea ngā wāhanga katoa.

He mea pono te āhua o te whai koha me ngā whanaungatanga i waenganui i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. E whakapono ana ngā tamariki ki ngā kaimahi i te wā e mahi tahi ana rātou. Ka kōrero ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa. E whakahaere ana rātou i ngā rautaki whai hua hei tautoko i te whakatipuranga ako o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. E harikoa ana ngā tamariki ki te hōtaka akoranga i te mea ko ngā tamariki te tino kaupapa, ā, he ākonga māia rātou e tino hiahia ana ki te ako.

E whakahaere ana te Poari Kaitiaki o Kokiri Marae i ngā pūnaha whai painga hei whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā o te kōhanga reo. Kātahi anō ka arotakengia te tūtohinga. He mea aratohu te āhua o te tūtohinga mō ā rātou kaupapa here me ā rātou tukanga whakahaere o ia rā. Kua whakatūria e te whānau ngā tukanga pakari rawa mō te arotake whaiaro. E hāngai tōtika ana te arotake whaiaro ki ngā whakahaere hei whakapai ake anō. Kua whakahaeretia ngā whakahaere pūtea e te kaiwhakahaere pūtea o te Poari Kaitiaki o Kokiri Marae. He tōtika te whai tikanga me te āhua o ngā pūnaha. E ora pai ana te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakatūria ngā tukanga tōtika mō te tono me te whakatau i ngā kaimahi hou. He tukanga whānui anō tō rātou hei aromātai i ngā kaimahi.

Kua whakaritea te hōtaka whakamāherehere me ngā pūkete akoranga mā ngā tamariki. He tirohanga matawhānui, āmiki hoki ngā mea e rua nei. Ka āta arahi te hōtaka whakamāherehere i te hōtaka akoranga. Kua whakaritea ngā pūkete akoranga mā ngā tamariki. E tino mārama ana ngā mātua ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me ō rātou putanga paetae.

Kua tae mai ki te wā mā te Kaitiaki Poari e whakatū i tētahi tukanga hei aromātai i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga.

Ngā Mahi Ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o Te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hono Ki Kokiri kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hono Ki Kokiri.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko āna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere i te māhere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • hōtaka whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

KoNgā Taumata Whakahirahirate kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E hāngai ana tēnei ki te kaupapa o Te Kōhanga Reo o Kokiri Marae, arā, te whakaako, te whakanui, te akiaki, te arahi me te tautoko i ngā mokopuna me te whānau ki te pupuri i ō rātou oranga ā-wairua, oranga ā-tinana, oranga ā-noho me ō rātou ioranga ā-ahurea.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

E tino whakarato ana te hōtaka akoranga i ngā wheako huhua mā ngā tamariki ki te tū hei ākonga māia.

E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki te whakapā ki ngā akoranga wheako mō te wā roa. E whakapā ana rātou ki ngā wheako wero hinengaro ki te whakanui ake i ō rātou whakaaro mō ngā ngohe e pā ana ki te mātauranga ā-pāngarau me te mātauranga ā-pānui. Ka pukumahi ngā tamariki, ā, i te nuinga o te wā e pīkau ana rātou i ā rātou ake akoranga.

Kua ākina ngā tamariki pakeke e ngā kaimahi te hāpai i ngā tūranga tātaki mō ngā wāhanga ōkawa o te hōtaka. He ākonga rātou, ā, e āhei ana rātou ki te ako i tō rātou kōtahi.

E whakatutuki ana te hōtaka i ngā tino matea o ngā pēpi, arā, ō rātou matea ā-tinana me ō rātou matea ā-whatumanawa. E tautoko ana ngā rauemi me ngā wāhi ako kua whakaritea mā rātou i ā rātou akoranga. E whakarato ana te kōhanga reo me ngā kaimahi i tētahi wāhi mauritau, me tētahi taiao e rere ai te aroha mā ngā pēpi.

E tino whai wāhi ngā kaimahi ki te mahi tahi ki ngā tamariki i a rātou e ako ana. E wātea ana rātou i ngā wā katoa, ā, ka āwhina rātou i te wā e hiahiatia ana e ngā tamariki te āwhina. He pono te āhua o te whai koha i waenganui i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. He whakapono te āhua o ngā tamariki i te wā ka taunekeneke rātou ki ngā pakeke.

E kōrero ana ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa. E whakahaere ana rātou i ngā rautaki whai tikanga hei tautoko i ngā tamariki ki te mau i te reo. E māia ana ētahi o ngā tamariki ki te whakaputa i ngā rerenga kōrero māmā noa mā te reo Māori.

Te whānui hoki o te taiao akoranga ō-roto, ā, kua tino whakaritea ngā wāhanga katoa o tēnei taiao akoranga. Mā tēnei, he iti noa ngā pōraru ka whakapā ki ngā akoranga me te takamahi o ia rā. He ngāwari ngā whakawhitiwhiti i waenganui i ngā tūmahi rerekē.

E harikoa ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi hōtaka akoranga, i te mea ko ō rātou matea te kaupapa nui, ā, e ai ki ō rātou mātua he māia te āhua o tō rātou ako.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamāherehere me te aromātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo hei whakatau i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamāherehere, ngā whakahaere me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga reo mā a rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka āta hāngai te aromātai nei ki te hōtaka whakamāherehere me te aroturuki i te tipuranga ako o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Kei te Poari Kaitiaki ngā pūnaha whai painga hei whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o ia rā o te kōhanga reo.

Kātahi anō ka arotakengia te tūtohinga. E whakamārama ana te tirohanga whakamua me te kaupapa i te ngā hiahia o ngā kaitiaki mā ngā mokopuna me te whānau e tae mai ana ki te kōhanga reo. He mea aratohu te āhua o te tūtohinga mō ā rātou kaupapa here me ā rātou tukanga whakahaere o ia rā. Ka hāngai tōtika ēnei ki ngā reku reihana mō ngā ratonga whare kōhungahunga 2008.

Kua oti kētia te mahi hei whakahou, hei arotake hoki i ngā kaupapa here me ngā whakaritenga o ia rā mō te hauora me te haumaru. He aratohu whai hua ēnei mō a rātou mahi. E rata ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao i te mea ko tō rātou hauora me tō rātou oranga te tino kaupapa.

Kua whakaritea e te whānau he tukanga whai oranga rawa mō te arotake whaiaro. Ka tīpakohia nga tino wāhanga hei whakahaere mā te kōhanga reo me ngā wāhanga hoki hei whakapai ake.

Kua whakahaeretia te taha pūtea e te kaitiaki pūtea o te Poari Kaitiaki o Kokiri Marae. He tōtika te āhua o ngā pūnaha. Kua tāututia ngā rauemi hei tautoko i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i roto i te māhere whāroa toha pūtea. E ora pai ana te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo.

Kua whakatūria ngā tukanga tōtika mō te tono me te whakatau i ngā kaimahi hou. E hāngai ana ēnei tukanga ki te tino kaupapa me ngā tino hiahia o te Poari Kaitiaki o Kokiri Marae. He tukanga whānui anō tā rātou ki te aromātai i ngā kaimahi. Mā tēnei kua whakaritea ngā wheako whakangungu hei hiki i ngā putanga mahi a ngā kaimahi hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

Kua whakataungia te hōtaka whakamāherehere me ngā pūkete akoranga mā ngā tamariki. He tirohanga matawhānui, āmiki hoki ngā mea e rua nei. Ka āta arahi te hōtaka whakamāherehere i te hōtaka akoranga. Mā tēnei, ka āhei te tāutu i ngā kaupapa me ngā rautaki hei tautoko i ngā akoranga ā ngā tamariki me ngā rauemi hoki. E hāngai tōtika ana ngā pūkete akoranga a ngā mokopuna ki ngā whakamāherehere. E tino mārama ana ngā mātua ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me ō rātou putanga paetae.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te hōtaka Aromātai.Me kaha ake te arotahi i te hōtaka aromātai. He iti noa ngā taunakitanga e tāututia ai, ā hea, ka pēhea, ā, he aha te take i whakarerekētia ai ngā whakahaere a te hōtaka akoranga. Me whai wāhi atu te whānau ināianei ki te whai whakaaro mō tētahi tukanga hei aromātai i te whai hua o te hōtaka.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

He whakamārama

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hono Ki Kokiri tētahiTauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga a te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

The Kokiri Marae Keriana Olsen Trust is the governing body for the Kokiri Marae Te Kōhanga Reo, Te Hono Ki Kokiri Te Kōhanga Reo in Wainuiomata and Paparākau Te Kōhanga Reo, Upper Hutt.

The Manager provides leadership in all aspects of the trust’s operations which includes the kōhanga reo. A supervisor works alongside the manager to ensure the kōhanga reo operates in conjunction with the trust’s mission, philosophy, policies and procedures.

Te Hono Ki Kokiri Te Kōhanga Reo was established under the leadership of Keriana Olsen in 1989. This was to ease the pressure of whānau from Wainuiomata wanting their children to attend Kokiri Marae Te Kōhanga Reo. The whānau of the kōhanga reo are very supportive and passionate about the kaupapa. There are parents who attend kōhanga reo regularly. Their input is valued by kaimahi.

The whānau has expressed complete satisfaction with the kōhanga reo. The kaimahi are passionate and committed to providing the best possible outcomes for their children. Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira is the focus of this review. It is to teach, promote, encourage, guide, support and assist mokopuna and whānau in the conservation of their spiritual, physical, social and cultural wellbeing.

Children are encouraged to take leadership roles during the formal part of the programme where appropriate. Kaimahi provide learning opportunities where many children are engaged for sustained periods of time. Children are busy and in many instances take responsibility for their own learning. Kaimahi actively support children when they are learning.

The programme for babies is responsive to their physical and emotional needs. The kōhanga reo and kaimahi provide a secure and loving place for the babies. The indoor learning environment is spacious and well organised.

The relationships between kaimahi and children are genuinely respectful. Children respond to kaimahi in a trusting manner. Kaimahi maintain te reo Māori at all times. They use appropriate strategies to support children’s language development. Children enjoy a learning programme where they are valued as confident and capable learners.

The Kokiri Marae Trust use effective systems to manage the operations of the kōhanga reo. The charter has recently been reviewed. The charter provides clear direction for policy development and procedures. The whānau has a robust self review process. This leads to improvement in practice. Finances are managed by the finance manager of the Kokiri Marae Trust. The kōhanga reo is in a financially viable position. There are clear process for appointing kaimahi to work in the kohanga reo The appraisal process is comprehensive.

Programme planning and records of mokopuna learning are in place. Both are comprehensive and detailed. The programme plan clearly guides the learning programme. Whānau are clear about what children are learning and their progress.

It is now time for the trust to establish a process to evaluate the effectiveness of the learning programme.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hono Ki Kokiri was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children atTe Kōhanga Reo o Te Hono Ki Kokiri.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Wahakhirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

4 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira is the focus of this review. This links to the philosophy of Kokiri Marae which is to teach, promote, encourage, guide, support and assist mokopuna and whānau in the conservation of their spiritual, physical, social and cultural wellbeing.

Areas of good performance

The learning programmes provide many opportunities for children to engage as confident, active learners.

Children are provided with learning opportunities where they are engaged for sustained periods of time. They are challenged to extend their thinking through numeracy and literacy activities. The children are busy and in many instances take responsibility for their own learning.

Older children are encouraged to take leadership roles during the formal part of the programme. They are very independent learners.

The programme for the babies is responsive to their physical and emotional needs. The resources and allocated space supports their learning. The kōhanga reo and kaimahi provide a place that is secure and loving for the babies.

Kaimahi actively participate with children in their learning. They are available at all times and provide assistance when needed. The relationships between kaimahi and children are genuinely respectful. Children respond to kaimahi in a trusting manner.

Kaimahi use te reo Māori at all times. They appropriate strategies to support children’s language development. Some children confidently use simple sentence structures in te reo Māori.

The indoors learning environment is spacious and well organised. This allows for minimal disruption between learning and routines. Transitions are smooth.

Children enjoy a learning programme where they are valued as confident, capable learners.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

The Kokiri Marae Trust has effective systems to manage the operations of the kōhanga reo

The charter has been reviewed recently. The mission and philosophy clearly outlines trustee’s intent for mokopuna and whānau who attend the kōhanga reo. The charter provides clear directions for their policies and procedures. These are aligned to the 2008 Early Childhood Regulations.

All health and safety policies and procedures have been reviewed and updated recently. These provide clear guidelines for practice. Children enjoy an environment where their health and wellbeing is nurtured.

The whānau have a robust self review process. This clearly highlights key aspects of practice for the kōhanga reo and leads to actions for improvement in practice.

Finances are managed by the finance manager of the Kokiri Marae Trust. The systems are transparent. Resources to support children’s learning are clearly identified in the budget forecast. The kōhanga reo is in a financially viable position

There are clear processes for appointing kaimahi to work in the kohanga reo. This clearly reflects the philosophy and intent of the Kokiri Marae Trust. Appraisal is comprehensive. This results in professional development to enhance kaimahi practice for the benefit of the children.

Programme planning and records of mokopuna learning are in place. Both are comprehensive and detailed. The programme plan clearly guides the learning programme. This identifies kaupapa ako, strategies for supporting children’s learning and required resources. The records for mokopuna learning are directly linked to planning. Whānau are clear about what children are learning and their progress.

Areas for development

Programme Evaluation.Programme evaluation needs attention. There is little evidence to identify when, how and why changes are made to the learning programme. It is time for whānau to consider a process to evaluate the effectiveness of the programme

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hono Ki Kokiri completed an EROKōhanga Whānau Assurance StatementandSelf-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

6 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

to establish a process for evaluating the effectiveness of the learning programme.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.
