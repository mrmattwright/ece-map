1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Whetū o Tāwera ki roto i Tūhoe, ki te hapori o Rūātoki. Ko tā te whānau tirohanga, kia whakatōngia te reo o Tūhoe me ngā uaratanga o Tūhoe, ki te katoa o ngā kaupapa me ngā mahinga i te kōhanga reo. Ka tino hāpai, ka tino ārahi hoki ngā kuia me ngā koroua i te whānau me ngā tamariki.

I whakaritea e ngā kaimahi ko Mana Whenua te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ka tau ki ngā tamariki ngā mōhiotanga e pā ana ki ngā waiata tuku iho me ngā kōrero a Tūhoe. Ka ako ngā tamariki i a rātou anō, me ō rātou hononga ki te kōhanga reo, tō rātou whānau, tō rātou hapū, tō rātou iwi hoki. E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki ki te ako i tō rātou tūrangawaewae.

I tēnei wā, kua tahuri te aronga rautaki a te whānau ki te reo Māori. E ākina ana ngā tamariki kia whai wāhi ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero. Ka āta whai wā ngā tamariki tuākana ki te whakawhiti whakaaro, te whakatau whakaritenga, me te tūhura haere. E pakari haere ana ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo. He pai te tae ā-tinana atu ki ngā hui ā-whānau, ā, ka whai hua hoki ngā pūrongo. He pai te kaiwhakahaere ki te whakahaere i ngā mahi e pā ana ki te tari me ngā kaimahi. Kei te mōhio te whānau ka whai hua ā rātou tamariki nā te pai o ngā mahi whakahaere.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga reo (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Whenua

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Whenua

He whakamārama

He pēhea te tino ako a ngā tamariki i ngā ariā me ngā uaratanga e pā ana ki te Mana Whenua?

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki te whakahirahiratanga o te Mana Whenua.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ako i te Mana Whenua. He whai wāhitanga mā rātou ki te tūhura i tō rātou taiao me te ao whānui. Ka whakarato te whānau i ngā whai wāhitanga ki ngā tamariki, hei hāpai i a rātou ki te ako i a rātou anō, i tō rātou tūrangawaewae hoki. Ka whakapakaritia te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki mā aua taumahi.

Te reo Māori. Ko te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki tētahi kaupapa matua. I tēnei wā ko te reo Māori te aronga rautaki. Ka whakatauira ngā pākeke i ngā hanganga reo e tika ana mō ngā tamariki. Ka whakawhānui ake anō te reo Māori mā ētahi atu taumahi, tae atu ki ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, me te maha o ngā tūmomo waiata. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā waiata me ngā kōrero tuku iho o Tūhoe. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ngā taunekeneke. E whanake haere ana te tino māia o ngā tamariki i a rātou e taunekeneke ana me ētahi atu. Ka whakanui ngā pākeke i ngā taunekeneke mauritau ki waenga i a rātou me ngā tamariki, ki waenga tonu i ngā tamariki. E ākina ana ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti kōrero ki ngā pākeke, ki ō rātou hoa hoki. He pai te whai wā o ngā tamariki tuākana ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga me te tūhura. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga e atawhai ana, e manaaki ana hoki i a rātou, ki te taha o ō rātou hoa me ngā pākeke, tae atu ki ngā kuia, ngā koroua, me te whānau whānui. He harikoa ngā tamariki ki te taunekeneke me ngā pākeke, tae atu hoki ki ngā manuhiri.

Te hōtaka i ngā ata. Ka āta hāpaitia ngā tamariki kia whanake ai te tino aronga toi whenuatanga ki te kōhanga reo. Ka kitea ki te hōtaka o ngā ata, te atawhai o te noho ā-whānau. He wā kua whakaritea mō ngā akoranga i whakaraupapahia ai, ā, he wā anō hoki mō ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki māhorahora i ō rātou ake whakaaro. Ka tiaki ngā tuākana i ngā tāina. He pakari ētahi o ngā tamariki ki te kawe i ngā karakia me ngā waiata. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te tohatoha i ngā taumahi me ngā rauemi ki ō rātou hoa.

Mana Whenua. Ka tino hāpaitia ngā tamariki kia ako rātou i a rātou anō, i tō rātou tūrangawaewae hoki. Ka whakawhanake ngā kaimahi i ngā wheako ako e hāpai ana, e whakapūmau ana hoki i te ako i te Mana Whenua. Ka ako ngā tamariki i te whai pānga o te whenua ki te whakatupu kai, me ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te tiaki i te whenua. Ka ako rātou i ō rātou tātai whakapapa ki te whenua. Ka whakapūmau ngā kaimahi i ngā ariā e pā ana ki te tiaki taiao. He maha ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te papa o te kōhanga reo me te hapori whānui, tae atu ki te Kura o Tāwera. Ka noho pūmau ngā tikanga o te marae ki te kōhanga reo. He pakari te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki te Mana Whenua ki tō rātou hapori.

Te wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake

Te whakarato mō ngā tamariki kei raro i te rua o ngā tau. Me aro ki ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki nohinohi. Me aro nahanaha ki te whakapai ake i te kounga o ngā wheako akoranga mō ngā tamariki kei raro i te rua o ngā tau. Ko te āta whakapuaki i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ka whai pānga ki te puāwaitanga o te reo, ā, e tika ana kia maha ake te whakamahinga o taua rautaki.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whai hua a te whānau ki te whakahaere me te whakarite i te kōhanga reo, me te whakariterite anō hoki hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki ā meāke nei?

Ka whai hua te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo me te whakariterite anō hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki ā meāke nei.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakamahere. Ka tino tautokona te ako a ngā tamariki mā te tika, te hāngai pū hoki o ngā mahere. Ka whakarato ngā mahere i te tino ārahitanga ki ngā kaimahi i a rātou e whakapuaki kaupapa ana ki ngā tamariki. Ka whakaritea hoki ngā tirohanga ā-tau, ā-wāhanga, ā-wiki hoki, ā, ka whai wāhi ngā mema o te whānau ki aua tirohanga. He matawhānui aua mahere, ā, he nui te kiko o roto. Kei roto hoki i ngā mahere o te hōtaka, ko ngā whāinga, ngā rauemi, ngā kaupapa hou, ngā taumahi, me ngā waiata.

Te aromatawai. He pai te aroturuki i te ahunga whakamua me ngā whakatutukitanga a ngā tamariki. Kei ngā pūkete a ia tamaiti ngā tauira o ngā tūmomo taumahi. He kōrerorero anō hoki o roto, nā runga i te tirohanga a ia tamaiti. Ka kapo atu hoki ngā whakaahua i ētahi o aua taumahi me te whai wāhi atu o ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmomo wheako. He rārangi arowhai anō hoki e pā ana ki ngā whakawhanaketanga mō ia wāhanga, ā, he tino mōhiohio o roto e pā ana ki te eke angitu o ngā tamariki. E wātea ana ki te whānau ngā mōhiohio aromatawai e pā ana ki ā rātou ake tamariki. Ka hāpaitia ngā tamariki kia eke angitu.

Te aromātai. Arotakengia ai te ako, te hauora, me te waiora o ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa. Ka āta whai whakaaro ngā kaimahi ki ngā mahinga, te āhua o ngā tamariki, me te whai huatanga o ā rātou mahi ki e whakatutuki i ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā matea o ia tamaiti. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki, nā te kaha aro o ngā kaimahi ki te katoa o te tamaiti i roto i āna akoranga, i tōna waiora hoki.

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau. He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka whakatau ngātahi rātou ki ngā hui ā-whānau o ia marama. Ko te kiko o ngā pūrongo ka whakaputaina i ia te wā, e whakamōhio ana i te whānau mō ngā tūmomo taumahi i te kōhanga reo. Kei te mārama te whānau ki ngā painga mō ā rātou tamariki, mai i te tōtika, te whai huatanga anō hoki o ngā mahi whakahaere.

Te arotake whaiaro. Ka kitea te pai o te arotake whaiaro puta noa i te katoa o ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, nā te āhua o ngā mahi a te whānau ki te whakariterite me te whakatutuki anō hoki i ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki. Kua roa nei te pai o te whānau ki te whakapai tonu, me te whakapūmau hoki i ngā pūnaha me ngā tukanga mārama. Aroturukitia ai te tino aronga o ngā mahere rautaki i ia te wā. Ko te waiora o ngā tamariki tētahi kaupapa matua.

Te whakahaere i te tari. He pai te whakahaere a te kaiwhakahaere i ngā āhuatanga mahi e pā ana ki te tari me ngā kaimahi. Kua whakarite katoatia ngā tuhinga. Ka noho mōhio te whānau ki ngā take katoa, tae atu ki te kōhanga reo me ā rātou tamariki.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia tahuri te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakarato hei painga mō ngā matea ako o ngā tamariki kei raro i te rua o ngā tau.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Whetū o Tawera is located in the rural area of the Tūhoe community of Rūātoki. The whānau vision is for te reo o Tūhoe and Tūhoe values to be embedded in the kaupapa and all practices in the kōhanga reo. The kuia and koroua provide strong support and guidance for the whānau and children.

The whānau chose mana whenua as the focus for this review. Children acquire knowledge about traditional waiata and sayings of Tūhoe. reo, their whānau, hapū and iwi. Children are well supported to learn about where they are from.Children learn about themselves and their connections to the kōhanga

The strategic focus of the whānau is currently on te reo Māori. Children are encouraged to engage in conversations. The older children spend quality time communicating, making decisions and exploring. Children are growing in confidence as speakers of te reo Māori.

The whānau manage their kōhanga reo well. Regular whānau hui are well attended and reporting is useful. The administrator manages administrative tasks and personnel efficiently. The whānau understand that their children benefit from effective management.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Whenua.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Mana Whenua

Background

How well do children learn the concepts and values about mana whenua?

Children are aware of the importance of Mana Whenua.

Areas of good performance

Children enjoy learning about Mana Whenua. They have opportunities to explore their immediate environment and wider world. The whānau provide children with opportunities to learn about themselves and where they are from. Children’s sense of belonging is strengthened through these activities.

Te reo Māori. The development of children’s te reo Māori is a priority. The strategic focus is currently on te reo Māori. Adults model appropriate and accurate language structures for children. Te reo Māori is further extended through other activities including karakia, hīmene and a wide variety of waiata. Children learn traditional waiata and sayings of Tūhoe. Children are willing speakers of te reo Māori.

Interactions. Children are developing high levels of confidence when interacting with others. Adults promote positive interactions with and among children. Children are encouraged to engage in conversations with adults and with each other. Older children spend quality time making decisions and exploring. Children experience warm, caring interactions and relationships with their peers and adults including kuia, koroua and other whānau members. Children happily engage with adults including visitors.

Morning programme. Children are well supported to develop a strong sense of belonging in the kōhanga reo. The morning programme reflects a warm whānau environment. There are periods of structured learning and times when children have freedom to express themselves. Tuakana care for their teina. Some children confidently take lead roles in karakia and waiata. Children willingly share activities and resources with each other.

Mana Whenua. Children are well supported to learn about themselves and where they are from. Kaimahi develop learning experiences that support and reinforce learning about mana whenua. Children learn about the whenua as a vital natural food source and its care. They are made aware of their ancestral links to the whenua. Kaimahi reinforce concepts of conservation. Children have many opportunities to explore the kōhanga reo grounds and the local community including Te Kura o Tawera. Tikanga that applies to the marae are adhered to at the kōhanga reo. Children have a strong sense of mana whenua in their community.

Area for development

Provision for children under two years old. The learning needs of the younger children need focused attention. A planned approach is required to improve the quality of learning experiences for the children under two years old. Animated conversations contribute to early language development and should be used more frequently.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How effectively do whānau manage and organise the kōhanga reo operations and plan for their children’s future interest?

The whānau effectively manage the kōhanga reo operations and plan for their children’s future interests.

Areas of good performance

Planning. Children’s learning is supported well through appropriate and targeted planning. Planning provide kaimahi with clear guidance when they deliver kaupapa to children. Annual, term, and weekly overviews are prepared with input from whānau members. These plans are comprehensive and detailed. Goals, resources, new vocabulary, activities and waiata are included in programme plans.

Assessment. Children’s progress and achievement is monitored well. Individual profiles contain samples of activities. These include comments from each child’s perspective. Photographs capture some of these activities and children’s involvement in different experiences. A term developmental checklist is included and provides valuable information about children’s successes. Whānau have access to their child’s assessment information. Children are supported to succeed.

Evaluation. Children’s learning, health and wellbeing is reviewed consistently. Kaimahi conscientiously consider routines, children’s dispositions and how well they provide for each child’s interests and needs. Children benefit from kaimahi who are focussed on children’s holistic learning and wellbeing.

Whānau management. The whānau manage their kōhanga reo well. Monthly whānau hui include collective decision making. Regular and detailed reporting informs the whānau about the kōhanga reo activities. The whānau understand that their children benefit from efficient and effective management.

Self review. Positive self review evidence across all kōhanga reo operations is evident in the way whānau organise and fulfil their roles and responsibilities. The whānau have a good history of ongoing improvement and sustaining clear transparent systems and procedures. Focused strategic planning is monitored regularly. Children’s wellbeing is a priority.

Administration. The administrator manages administrative tasks and personnel management well. Documents are organised. The whānau are kept well informed of all matters including the kōhanga reo and their children.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO LicensedKōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends the whānau and kaimahi provide for the learning needs of the children that are under two years old.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
