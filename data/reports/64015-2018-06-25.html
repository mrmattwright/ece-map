1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Āwhina Te Kaahui Nohinohi ki ngā papa o tētahi kura tuatahi i Naenae. He huarahi rumaki reo Māori mō ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo i tā rātou whakawhitinga atu ki te kura tuatahi. Kua hangaia te whare o te whānau hei kōhanga reo, ā, nō nā tata tonu nei rātou i hoko ai i tētahi wēne hei kawe i ngā tamariki.

Whai muri i te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2015, kua panoni haere ētahi tūāhuatanga, pērā i te tupu o te tatauranga tamariki, te uru mai o ngā whānau hou, me te whakatūnga o tētahi kaiwhakahaere me tētahi heamana. Tua atu i aua rerekētanga, nō nā tata tonu nei te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i whakahoki atu ai i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo ki te whānau.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whai wāhi atu ki ngā akoranga?

Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki te iti noa o tā rātou aro atu ki te hōtaka akoranga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore ngā tamariki e rongo ana, e kōrero ana rānei i te reo Māori i roto i te hōtaka akoranga. Mō rātou o te whānau e mau ana i te iti noa o te reo Māori, kua whakaritea ētahi wāhi mō te kōrero i te reo Pākehā, engari kāhore te noho anake ki aua wāhi kōrero Pākehā ai e whāia ana. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori i tā rātou whakapuaki i te hōtaka akoranga, engari kāhore i a rātou ngā rautaki ki te hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. He whāiti te whakawhanaketanga reo Māori o ngā tamariki.

He whāiti ngā wheako ako a ngā tamariki. Kāhore anō ngā kaimahi kia whakarite i ngā mahinga e ārahi ai, e hāpai ai hoki i te whakapuakitanga o te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki. Kāhore ngā taumahi e whakapuaki ana i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whakatau i ā rātou ake whiringa i runga anō i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga, ā, kāhore hoki aua taumahi e whakaongaonga ana i ngā tamariki, e hāpai ana rānei i tā rātou tūhura haere. Ahakoa kua tuitui i ngā tikanga Māori ki te hōtaka o ia rā, kāhore i te riterite te whakatauira, te whakatinana hoki a ngā kaimahi i aua whakaritenga. Nā tēnei, kāhore i te tika te whanonga a ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E whanake ana ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau. E ako ana ngā whānau hou i ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga kia mātātoa ai tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki ngā mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. He auau ngā hui kaimahi, ā, ka whakamōhiotia e ngā mema o te komiti ngā whakahoutanga i te hōtaka akoranga, i ngā take whakahaere, ā, i te hauora me te haumaru hoki.

Ko te arotake whaiaro tētahi āhuatanga hei whakawhanake ake. Ahakoa kua arotakengia, kua whakahoungia hoki e te whānau ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga i te tūtohinga, he mahi anō ki te whakatutuki haere i ia te wā i te arotake whaiaro o ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, tae atu ki te whakahaere i ngā kaimahi, me te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi i ia te wā.

He ngoikore te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga. He iti noa te māramatanga o ngā kaimahi ki ngā hononga ki waenga i te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai hōtaka. Ahakoa ngā mōhiotanga ngātahi o ngā kaimahi, kāhore i te tino mārama te ārahitanga me te ahu whakamua ka whakapai ake i te kounga o te ako mō ngā tamariki. He mea nui kia toro atu ngā kaimahi ki te āwhina ka whakapai ake ai i te whakapuakitanga o te hōtaka akoranga mā ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture. Kia tutuki i te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā herenga ā-ture, me tahuri rātou ki te:

  • whakarite i te aro nui o te marautanga ki ngā ngākau nuitanga ako o ngā tamariki, i te whai pānga hoki o te marau ki te kounga o te whakapuakitanga, ā, me whakatinana hoki i te marau mā te aromatawai me te aromātai hōtaka
    [R43 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; C2-4 me te C7-8 & 10 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakarite kia rumakina ngā wāhi ako o te kōhanga reo ki te reo Māori me ngā tikanga Māori
    [R43 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; C9 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakarite i te whakatinanatanga o ngā mahi ki te whakahaere tika i ngā pūmanawa tangata
    [R47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA7 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]
  • whakarite i te haere tonutanga o tētahi tukanga arotake whaiaro e hāpai ai i te kōhanga reo ki te whakapūmau me te whakapai ake i te kounga o te mātauranga me te atawhai.
    [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA6 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakawhanake te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i tētahi hōtaka tautoko matawhānui mā te whānau me ngā kaimahi, ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Āwhina Te Kaahui Nohinohi is located on the site of a primary school in Naenae. There is a Māori immersion pathway for kōhanga reo graduates into the primary school. The whānau has a purpose-built kōhanga reo building, and has recently purchased a van to transport children.

Since the last ERO report in 2015, there have been some changes related to a roll growth, new whānau, an appointment of a kaiwhakahaere and chairperson. In addition, Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) have recently given management back to the kōhanga reo whānau.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well are children engaged in learning?

Children display limited interest in the learning programme.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Key Next Steps

Children do not hear or speak te reo Māori in the learning programme. While there are designated areas where whānau with limited te reo Māori can speak English, this is not followed. Kaimahi use te reo Māori in the delivery of the learning programme, however they do not appear to have strategies to support children’s language development. Children’s te reo Māori development is limited.

Children’s learning experiences are limited. Kaimahi have not established routines to guide and support the delivery of children’s learning programme. Activities do not provide opportunities for children to make choices based on their interests, and to stimulate and support exploration. Although tikanga Māori are integrated into the daily programme, these is no consistency in how kaimahi model and implement those practices. Consequently, children’s behaviour towards others is inappropriate.

Planning and Evaluation

Key Next Steps

Whānau management practices are developing. New whānau are learning about their roles and responsibilities to actively participate in managing kōhanga reo operations. There are regular meetings for kaimahi, and committee members provide updates about the learning programme, operational matters, and health and safety.

Self review is an area for development. Although the whānau have reviewed and updated the charter policies and procedures, further work is required to maintain regular self review of all kōhanga reo operations, including personnel management and regular appraisal of kaimahi.

The quality of the learning programme is lacking. Kaimahi have a limited understanding about the connections between programme planning, assessment and programme evaluation. Despite the collective knowledge of kaimahi, there is no clear leadership and direction to improve the quality of learning for children. It is imperative that kaimahi seek assistance to improve the delivery of learning programmes for children.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

ERO identified areas of non-compliance. In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements, they need to:

  • ensure the curriculum is responsive to children’s learning interests inform the quality of delivery, and is informed by assessment and programme evaluation
    R43 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; C2-4 and C7-8 &10 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • ensure the environment of the kōhanga reo is rich in te reo Māori and tikanga Māori
    [R43 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; C9 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • ensure that suitable human resource management practices are implemented
    [R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA7 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

  • ensure there is an ongoing process of self-review to help the kōhanga maintain and improve the quality of its education and care.
    [R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA6 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • That Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust develop a comprehensive programme of support for whanau and kaimahi to address the areas of concern identified in this report.

5 When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Āwhina Te Kaahui Nohinohi will be within one year.
