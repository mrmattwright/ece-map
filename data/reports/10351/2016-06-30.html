1 Evaluation of Mt Royal Early Learning Centre

How well placed is Mt Royal Early Learning Centre to promote positive learning outcomes for children?

Not well placed

Requires further development

Well placed

Very well placed

The service requires external support to further develop its internal evaluation practices and the quality of the learning environment for children.

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Mt Royal Learning Centre is located in Mt Albert, Auckland. The centre provides full day education and care for children from six months to five years of age. The owner is the centre manager and she leads four qualified teachers. Centre practices are inclusive and children of all ages share a large room and an outdoor area. Parents appreciate the nurturing care that staff provide for children.

The centre has not had a positive ERO reporting history. However, ERO's 2013 report noted that some progress had been made since the 2011 review. At that time ERO identified the need for the owners to develop systems for long and short term management planning and for internal evaluation.

The Review Findings

Relationships and interactions between children and teachers in the centre are positive. Teachers are affirming in their practice and work to build children's confidence. They ensure all children have an opportunity to participate in the programme. Although areas of play are well defined, teachers should review the learning environments. They should aim to make them more inviting for children and to have them better reflect current learning and play interests.

The condition of the outdoor learning environment somewhat limits children's opportunities to learn and play. A review of the outdoor space in 2013 has not resulted in sufficient improvement. A more deliberate approach to the management of this environment is necessary to encourage children to explore, investigate and engage in self-directed learning.

The centre has implemented a new planning process which is more responsive to children's developing interests. Portfolios contain a record of children's participation and progress. The centre manager agrees that developing more individual learning stories with greater complexity is a next step for teachers.

Recently established strategic plans guide the centre and outline the areas of practice that need strengthening. The addition of some underpinning health and safety procedures will help to make centre policies more effective. A review of key documentation including the philosophy, strategic and annual plans, and policies should be undertaken with the aim of better aligning these documents.

The centre manager supports teachers' professional practice. She agrees that the centre's existing appraisal system requires strengthening to ensure it meets the legal requirements of New Zealand Education Council. A shared approach to leadership could help with the development of a more professional learning community within the centre.

Key Next Steps

To improve provision for children's care and education the owners should engage external professional support to help them to:

  • upgrade the learning environments

  • improve assessment and programme planning with a view to improving provision for children's learning.

  • develop and implement robust internal evaluation to contribute to ongoing improvement

  • review health and safety practices to align them with recent changes to legislation

  • review teacher appraisal policy and practices so that they are compliant with Education Council of New Zealand requirements.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Mt Royal Early Learning Centre completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum

  • premises and facilities

  • health and safety practices

  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)

  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)

  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)

  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children‚Äôs health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements

Actions for compliance

At the time of the review ERO identified areas of non-compliance relating to Health and Safety. One area of concern relates to the installation of safety glass. ERO notes that this matter is now being promptly rectified.

In addition, ERO identified areas of non-compliance relating to curriculum, management and provision for children's health and safety. To meet requirements, the owners must:

  • take all measurable steps to promote children's health and safety and ensure that the environment is free from hazards

  • review and improve the learning environments so that there is good quality, variety and quality of educational equipment and resources to support children's learning

  • ensure that teachers' performance appraisal is regular and meets the requirements of the Education Council of New Zealand

  • establish an ongoing, systematic process of improvement focussed internal evaluation that considers all aspects of the services operation

Licensing Criteria for Early Childhood Education and Care Services 2008, PF4, GMA 6, 7 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008, 46 (1).

To improve current practice the owners should ensure that:

  • household cleaning products are not accessible by children

  • hazards in the environment are promptly identified and removed or minimised.

Development Plan Recommendation

ERO recommends that the service consult with the Ministry of Education and plan to address the key next steps and actions outlined in this report.

Next ERO Review

When is ERO likely to review the service again?

The next ERO review of Mt Royal Early Learning Centre will be within two years.
