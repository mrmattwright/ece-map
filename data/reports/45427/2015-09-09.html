1 Evaluation of Selwyn Kids

How well placed is Selwyn Kids to promote positive learning outcomes for children?

Not well placed

Very well placed

Selwyn Kids is becoming well placed to promote positive learning outcomes for children.

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Selwyn Kids Limited commenced operation in April 2015 under new ownership and management. Selwyn Kids purchased the service which was previously operated under the name Selwyn Toddlers Network. The service operates a before and after school care programme from the same premises. It also operates a home-based education and care service licensed under Selwyn Kids Limited. This report covers a review of the kindergarten.

An additional building now provides the kindergarten with two separate home-like environments to cater for the specific learning needs of children aged 2 to 3%2 years old and 3%2 to 5 years old.

Staffing has remained stable through these changes. Leaders and teachers have made good progress with strengthening self-review systems and practices.

The Review Findings

The centre's philosophy places a strong focus on providing a child-centred programme that is well supported by teachers who are responsive to the strengths, interests and capabilities of children. Leaders and teachers use the philosophy as a basis for self review to ensure that their expectations match practice in the service.

Leaders and teachers foster inclusive and positive relationships to support children's learning and wellbeing. They provide flexible routines and supportive transition processes into the service, between learning areas and on to school.

Children benefit from a curriculum that makes good use of the principles of Te Whariki, theNew Zealand Early Childhood Curriculum, to drive the learning programme. Children are empowered to be confident to express their opinions and be involved in decision making about their learning interests. Literacy, mathematics and the use of a good range of technologies are well integrated into the programme. Teachers actively listen to children and help them to investigate and extend on their ideas and thinking.

Children are encouraged to be independent and to take responsibility for the environment. There is an appropriate emphasis on children's creativity and enterprise. Regular excursions into the community and parent contributions further enhance the learning programme offered to children.

Leaders and teachers are developing a bicultural curriculum. They make good use of natural resources and have a strong focus on sustainable practices. The new owners have developed a useful strategic plan that identifies key priorities for the service in the next three years. They recognise strong leadership as a key driver for change and improvement. ERO agrees and encourages the owners to continue to build the leadership capacity and capability of all staff.

Self review is aligned to the philosophy and is well led and managed. The systematic approach to self review includes good use of research and results in positive outcomes for children's learning and wellbeing. The support and guidance manager helps teachers to use self review to inform the learning programme and to reflect on teaching practices.

The owners and leaders continue to place a strong focus on providing professional development (PD) to enhance teaching and learning practices. The curriculum leader effectively leads PD and draws on external support where appropriate. The planning of whole-service professional development fosters a sense of community for all staff and parents.

Key Next Steps

The new owners have identified long-term planning goals. Priority should now be given to:

  • reviewing and implementing health and safety policies and procedures, and consistent management systems and processes
  • developing and implementing annual action planning, and evaluating the impact of these actions on meeting the long-term goals
  • placing a greater emphasis on bicultural perspectives in key governance and management documentation and practices
  • continuing to improve appraisal to provide a more evidence-based inquiry process to extend teaching practices and leadership skills.

ERO has identified that assessment practices should continue to be strengthened to better reflect the richness of what is happening in the programme. This includes giving prominence to:

  • children's language, culture and identity
  • children's learning and parent aspirations
  • making better use of individual assessment to inform programme planning and children's progress in learning over time.
