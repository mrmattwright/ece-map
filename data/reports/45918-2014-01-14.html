1 Evaluation of Scuola Montessori

How well placed is Scuola Montessori to promote positive learning outcomes for children?

Not well placed

Requires further development

Well placed

Very well placed

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Scuola Montessori is a privately owned service, located in Waihi Road Tauranga. This is the first ERO review of the centre, which has been operating for eighteen months. The centre is fully licensed under the Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008, to provide sessional and all-day care for 30 children, including eight under the age of two.

Centre owners/managers have systematically employed qualified early childhood teachers and a high proportion are trained in the Montessori approach. The centre owners take an active role in day-to-day operations. A vision statement based on Montessori principles is at the heart of centre practices and is strongly represented in the programme and the environment.

Since opening in May 2012, owner/managers have developed sound systems and policy guidelines for key operations. An ongoing process of policy review is being implemented. Developing and resourcing the environment has been an early priority, and development is continuing. There has been a change to the leadership team, with a new head directress being appointed in January 2013.

The centre is beginning to develop positive relationships with its parent community. Regular newsletters to families and surveys seeking their viewpoints are assisting management to build this partnership. Supporting children and families during the period when they prepare to transition to school is a focal area for development. A network is already established with a nearby primary school which has Montessori classes.

The Review Findings

Owner/managers are providing sound management structures and clear direction for the staff. Good strategic and financial management has been instrumental in the provision of a well-resourced environment for a growing roll. Centre owners are continuing to develop aspects of the physical layout of the building to improve access and available space, especially for babies and toddlers.

Leadership of the curriculum (learning and teaching) by the head directress is an area for continuing development. Expectations for this role have recently been clarified and now need to be communicated to all members of the teaching team. This is necessary to ensure a collective understanding of, and commitment to, the agreed philosophical approach to teaching, and the curriculum in action. The head directress is well supported by management and has undertaken some leadership training.

The curriculum strongly reflects the Montessori approach and is being implemented by a team of teachers who are knowledgeable about this approach to learning and teaching. The environment has been arranged and resourced to support this curriculum design. Teachers' model learning using equipment and the values of grace and gratitude are integral to the programme. Assessment planning and evaluation practices are still developing under the leadership of the head directress. Teachers record their observations of children‚Äôs learning in individual profiles and these are shared with parents. It is important that teachers develop assessment practices that effectively document children‚Äôs interests, and then the intentional planning as a result of this learning. Expectations for teachers about assessment and planning need to be clear and well documented for shared and ongoing reference by the team.

Teachers work hard to prepare and present an environment that engages children with materials that promote sensorial learning, life skills, independence and self management for sustained periods of time. Children interact freely with their environment and are familiar with routines. They play happily with and alongside their peers. Teachers have undertaken professional development about positive guidance strategies. Children, toddlers and babies are well supported to develop social competencies in a caring and nurturing environment.

Key Next Steps

Agreed priorities for review and development are:

  • leadership roles and responsibilities for the head directress, to ensure that these are made explicit and are communicated to all teaching staff
  • the philosophy for learning and teaching, to ensure that this is agreed and well understood, and that expectations for teachers are clear
  • the curriculum design to ensure consistent implementation of a programme that is strong in Montessori principles and practices, but which also takes account of Te Whariki, the early childhood curriculum, and bicultural perspectives
  • provision for babies and toddlers in the environment.

Recommendation

That centre managers seek and engage an external facilitator for ongoing centre-wide professional development for all teaching staff in relation to assessment, planning and evaluation. This should occur in conjunction with the review and development of the philosophy and curriculum.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Scuola Montessori completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

In order to improve the sleep provisions for children:

  • The centre owners and head directress will undertake a review of the sleep room for children in order to ensure a safe, designated place for rest and sleep.

Next ERO Review

When is ERO likely to review the service again?

The next ERO review of Scuola Montessori will be in three years.
