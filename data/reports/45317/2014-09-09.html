Background

Paula‚Äôs Tiny Tots Tahunanui provides education and care for infants and toddlers. The centre is owner operated. One of the co-owners is the licensee and centre manager. She is a qualified early childhood teacher and carries out a supportive, mentoring role in the programme.

Almost all the teachers are qualified. The small roll allows children to enjoy close, nurturing relationships with teachers in a family-like environment. Positive relationships exist between teachers, children and their families.

The centre managers have used ERO‚Äôs April 2011 report to strengthen their self-review process, their assessment procedures and the usefulness of their strategic plan and annual plan. Although progress is evident, the updating of the centre's long-term planning needs to be completed. The use of evaluative questions are likely to achieve the quality the centre desires.

The Review Findings

During the review, ERO observed children happily settled and enthusiastic about their play. They have many activities linked to mathematics, literacy, the arts and physical activity. They have good opportunities to hear te reo MƒÅori. Teachers enjoy the support they receive from MƒÅori parents in using te reo and tikanga MƒÅori.

The centre staff use the MƒÅori values of manaakitanga (caring and nurturing) and whanaungatanga (family-like relationships) to enhance the positive culture in the centre. The manager strongly supports the teachers‚Äô focus on MƒÅori values and the way these add to the positive relationships in the centre.

Teachers have developed strong two-way relationships with parents and families. Parents regularly make useful contributions to the centre programme. Centre staff are respectfully supportive of the children and their families. Parents‚Äô wishes for their children are effectively used by teachers to complement their planning.

Most teachers provide useful information to parents about their children‚Äôs progress and learning. In the best examples, teachers:

  • identify children‚Äôs learning and extend on their interests
  • include feedback from parents and children
  • identify next steps in learning.

Centre managers have a useful self-review process that focuses on ongoing improvement. This could be further strengthened by staff focusing more on how effectively the programme is meeting outcomes for children.

The centre is very well led by the supervisor. The centre manager includes the supervisor and assistant supervisor in many decisions about how the centre is run. This includes developing the centre‚Äôs long-term plans and managing self review. This approach is building staff capability and maintaining and building on current good practice.

Key Next Steps

The managers and ERO agree that the next key steps for promoting high-quality learning and teaching include extending self-review practices and completing the update of the centre's long term plan.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Paula's Tiny Tots Tahunanui completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

The next ERO review of Paula's Tiny Tots Tahunanui will be in three years.
