1 Te Horopaki

Ko Horouta me Tākitimu ngā waka,
Ko Manawawaru te maunga,
Ko Te Ārai te Uru te awa,
Ko Ngāti Kaipono me Ngāi Te Aweawe ngā hapū,
Ko Manutuke te marae,
Ko Rongowhakaata te iwi,
Ko Manutuke te kōhanga reo.

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Manutuke ki tētahi marae ki te tonga o Tūranga-nui-a-Kiwa. Kua toru tekau tau te kōhanga reo e whakahaerehia ana. E noho ngākau nui ana te whānau ki te whakatinana i te tirohanga ngātahi o te hapū me te iwi, arā, ki te whakarauora i te reo Māori me te whakatupu ngātahi i ā rātou tamariki. He mauritau te hononga ki waenga i te marae me te kōhanga reo, e ahu mai ana i ngā mātāpono o te whanaungatanga. I tēnei wā kei te whakariterite te whakahoutanga ake o te rūma tākaro whakamutunga, me te whakahoahoa ake i te taiao o waho. I ia tau, ka tuku te kōhanga reo i tētahi koha iti ki te marae mō te whakamahinga o ngā whare me ngā papa.

Nō nā tata tonu nei ētahi o ngā kuia i ngaro atu ai, tae atu hoki ki te kaiako matua, ā, kua pā nui tēnei ki te kōhanga reo. Nā tēnei, kei te whakakikī tonu te whānau i ngā tūranga ārahi kua mahue ake.

I taunaki te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kia whakamahine tonu, kia whakamahi tonu hoki te whānau i ngā mōhiohio o te arotake whaiaro, hei aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu ana i tō rātou tūmāia, tō rātou pākiki hoki hei ākonga?

He pai te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki hei ākonga tūmāia, hei ākonga pākiki hoki.

Mana Whenua me te Mana Atua

Ka rongo ngā tamariki i tētahi taiao mahana e poipoi ana i a rātou. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i ngā uaratanga o te aroha, te manaaki, me te rangimārie i a rātou e taunekeneke ana, e whakawhiti kōrero ana hoki ki ngā tamariki. Ka manaaki, ka āwhina hoki ngā tuākana i ngā tēina, ā, he tino tauira hoki mō rātou. Ka tino hāpai ngā kaumātua me te whānau i ngā tamariki i a rātou e whai wāhi atu ana ki ngā kaupapa i te hapori me te marae. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo me te marae.

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi hōtaka akoranga e tino whakaongaongatia ana. E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo pokapū akoranga o roto, o waho hoki e mau rauemi ana. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga, te tākaro mō te roanga o te wā, te mahi auaha, me te whakamātau haere mā ngā huarahi haumaru. Kua waia ngā tamariki ki ngā mahinga, ā, he tino wā ako, wā whakahoahoa hoki te riterite o ēnei. He wā anō e wātea ana ngā tamariki ki te tākaro ngātahi hei rōpū nui, hei rōpū iti rānei, ki ngā taumahi rānei e kōkirihia ana e ngā pākeke. Ka tino hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki i a rātou e tūhura ana, e hīraurau hopanga ana, e whakahura ana hoki i ngā āhuatanga hou. Kei te whanake ngā tamariki hei ākonga tūmāia, hei ākonga pākiki hoki.

He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te ako i ā rātou mahi hei tangata whenua. Ka tautoko ngā tamariki i ngā huihuinga ki te marae, nā te taki me te waiata i ngā hīmene, ngā waiata, ngā haka me ngā mōteatea. He maha ngā wā ka tū rātou hei kanohi mō te kōhanga reo, te hapū, me te iwi ki te Ahurei Kapa Haka o Ngā Kōhanga Reo o Te Tai Rāwhiti, me ngā tūmomo kaupapa ki ngā kura o tēnei takiwā. He pakari te aronga toi whenuatanga me te tuakiri o ngā tamariki.

E ako ana ngā tamariki ki te tū takitahi me te tiaki i a rātou anō. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki me pēhea te whakahoki atu i ā rātou taonga ake ki te wāhi tika, me te whakapai haere whai muri i ā rātou mahi. Ka whakatau rātou i ā rātou ake whiringa, ā rātou ake whakataunga i a rātou e tākaro ana, e ako ana hoki. He tākare te kawe atu a ngā tamariki i ngā tūranga me ngā kawenga mahi i ngā mahinga me ngā huihuinga, ngā taumahi hoki i ia te wā. Ka aro pai ngā tamariki ki ngā wheako e mōhiotia ana e rātou.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga o te taiao reo Māori. He pakari te taki a ngā tamariki i ngā karakia, ngā mihimihi, ngā whakapapa, ngā pepeha, ngā waiata, me ngā mōteatea. Ka rongo rātou i te reo Māori ōpaki, te reo Māori tuku iho hoki e whakamahia ana e ngā kaumātua i te marae. Ko ētahi o ngā mātua o te whānau e tino whakatauira ana i te reo, ā, he ngana tā rātou whakawhitiwhiti kōrero ki ngā tamariki. He pai te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki me te puāwai haere o tō rātou reo Māori.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore i ngā kaimahi te āheinga ki te whakawhānui i te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. E whakaae ana ngā kaimahi, kāhore i a rātou i nāianei ngā pūkenga reo e tika ana hei whakawhānui ake i ngā pūkenga reo Māori o ngā tamariki. E noho pūmau ana te whānau ki te whakatū i ētahi atu kaimahi e mau nei i Te Tohu Whakapakari.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka whai hua ngā mahi a te whānau ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. He tau te angamahi kaupapa here a te whānau, hei ārahi i tā rātou whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. He mārama ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga pūtea, ngā take kaimahi, te hauora me te haumaru, me te whakahaere tari. Ka whakaraupapa ngā mahere rautaki i ngā kaupapa matua e arotahi ana ki te reo Māori i roto i ngā tau kei te haere mai. Hui ai te whānau i ia te wā ki te tāutu, te whakatutuki, me te pūrongo hoki i ngā take e whai pānga ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā painga mō ngā tamariki e noho pūmau matua ana ki ngā whakataunga me ngā whakaritenga a te whānau.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Me whakapai ake te whakamahere hōtaka me ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aromatawai. Kāhore ngā kaimahi i ū pūmau ki ā rātou whakaritenga mō te aromatawai me te whakamahere i roto i ngā marama kua pahure ake nei. E mōhio ana rātou, ka pā tōraro aua āhuatanga ki ngā tamariki me ā rātou akoranga.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakaae ngātahi Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau, ka toro atu rātou ki te tautoko o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ki te whakapai ake i te whakamahere hōtaka me ngā whakaritenga aromatawai, me te whakawhānui ake hoki i te reo Māori o ngā tamariki.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Manutuke ki te marae o Manutuke, ki te tonga o Tūranga-nui-a-Kiwa. He pai te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki hei ākonga tūmāia, hei ākonga pākiki hoki. He pakari tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga ki te marae, te hapū, me te iwi. Ka whai hua ngā mahi a te whānau ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo. Ko te whakamahere, te aromatawai, me te whakawhānui i te reo Māori o ngā tamariki, ētahi āhuatanga hei whakapai ake.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Manutuke i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

1 Context

Ko Horouta me Tākitimu ngā waka,
Ko Manawawaru te maunga,
Ko Te Ārai te Uru te awa,
Ko Ngāti Kaipono me Ngāi Te Aweawe ngā hapū,
Ko Manutuke te marae,
Ko Rongowhakaata te iwi,
Ko Manutuke te kōhanga reo.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Manutuke is a part of the local marae and is south of Gisborne. The kōhanga reo has been operating for thirty years. Whānau are committed to implementing the shared vision of the hapū and iwi, which is to revitalise the Māori language and to raise their children together. The relationship between the marae and kōhanga reo is harmonious and based on the principles of whanaungatanga. Plans are underway to refurbish the last playroom and landscape the outdoor environment. The kōhanga reo pays a small yearly koha to the marae for the use of the buildings and grounds.

The recent loss of a number of kuia including the senior kaiako has impacted on the kōhanga reo. As a consequence, whānau are still filling in the leadership positions created by these losses.

The previous ERO report recommended that the whānau continue to refine and use self-review information to monitor all areas of the kōhanga reo operations.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children show they are confident, curious learners?

Children are developing well as confident, curious learners.

Mana Whenua and Mana Atua

Children experience a warm, nurturing environment. Kaimahi model the values of aroha, manaaki and rangimarie when they interact and communicate with children. Tuakana are caring, helpful and are positive role models for teina. Elders and whānau warmly support children when they participate in community and marae events. Children are confident in the kōhanga reo and on the marae.

Children learn in a highly stimulating learning programme. Children have access to a wide range of well-resourced indoor and outdoor learning centres. There are opportunities for children to follow their interests, play uninterrupted, be creative and take risks in safe ways. Children are familiar with routines which are valuable learning, social times and predictable. There are times when children are able to play cooperatively in large and small groups or in adult-led activities. Kaimahi provide valuable support to children as they explore, problem solve and make new discoveries. Children are developing well as confident, curious learners.

Children have opportunities to learn about their roles as tangata whenua. Children support formalities at the marae by reciting and singing hīmene, waiata, haka and mōteatea. They often represent the kōhanga reo, hapū and iwi when they perform at the Tairāwhiti Kōhanga Reo Kapa Haka Festival and attend activities at the local school. Children have a strong sense of belonging and identity.

Children are learning about independence and how to take care of themselves. Some children know how to pack away their personal belongings and clean up after themselves. They make independent choices and decisions about their play and their learning. Children eagerly take on roles and responsibilities during routines and regular events and activities. Children respond well to experiences which are familiar.

Children are immersed in a rich te reo Māori environment. Children confidently recite karakia, mihimihi, whakapapa, pepeha, waiata and mōteatea. They hear te reo Māori being used in formal and traditional ways by kaumātua on the marae. Some whānau parents are positive language models and readily engage children in dialogue. Children are developing well as emerging speakers of te reo Māori.

Key Next Steps

Kaimahi do not have the capacity to extend children’s te reo Māori. Kaimahi accept they no longer have the language skills that are required to extend children’s te reo Māori skill. Whanau are committed to recruiting kaimahi with Te Tohu Whakapakari.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau manage the operations of the kōhanga reo in an efficient manner. Whānau have a sound policy framework to guide them with the management of the kōhanga reo. There are clear processes for financial management, personnel matters, health and safety and office administration. Strategic planning outlines future priorities which focus on te reo Māori. Whanau meet regularly to identify, address and report on matters that affect the kōhanga reo operations. Children’s interests are critical to whānau decision making and planning.

Key Next Steps

Programme planning and assessment practices require improvement. Kaimahi have not consistently maintained their practices for assessment and planning in recent months. They acknowledge these inadequacies disadvantage children in their learning.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the whānau agreed that they would seek support from the local Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust to improve programme planning and assessment practices and extend the children’s te reo Māori.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Manutuke is at Manutuke Marae, south of Gisborne. Children are developing well as confident, curious learners. They have a strong sense of belonging to the marae, hapū and iwi. Whānau manage the kōhanga reo operations in a highly efficient way. Planning, assessment and extending children’s te reo Māori are areas requiring improvement.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Manutuke will be in three years.
