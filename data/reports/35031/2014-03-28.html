2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Awhi Mai ki te marae o Te Papa o Te Aroha ki Tokoroa. I kōwhiritia e te whānau ko te Mana Tangata hei kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E ai ki ngā pūrongo o te whānau e hāngai ana te Mana Tangata ki tō rātou manawanui ki te ahurea, te reo Māori me tō rātou tuakiri. E whakarato ana ngā kaimahi i tētahi hōtaka e ākina nei i a rātou ki te tū hei ākonga kamakama e hiahiatia ai te ako. Kua tautokotia ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi ki te ārahi i ētahi wāhanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakatairangatia ngā kaimahi i ngā putanga ako a ngā tamariki.

He mauritau, he aroha te āhua o ngā taunekeneke a ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki. He whai tikanga, ā, ka kaha kitea te whānaungatanga i waenganui i ngā tamariki, ā rātou hoa me ngā pakeke. Ko te reo Māori me ōnā tikanga te tino kaupapa i roto i te kōhanga reo. Ka tino arotahi ngā kaimahi ki te whakamana me te whakanui i te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa. E noho ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao ko tō rātou tuakiri me tō rātou taiao te kaupapa nui.

Kei te whānau ngā tukanga whai hua hei whakahaere i ngā wāhanga o tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka aroturuki te kaiwhakahaere i ngā tukanga katoa kia hāngai ai ngā whakahaere a te kōhanga reo ki ngā ture me ngā rekureihana. He tōtika, ā, e whai mana ana ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga mō te hauora me te haumaru. Ka tino hāpai te whānau i ngā rautaki katoa hei whakahaere i tētahi kōhanga reo haumaru me tētahi kōhanga reo paru kore mā ngā tamariki.

Kei te pai ngā pūnaha whakahaere mō ngā kaimahi. Kua whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa kōrero katoa. Kua whakritea hoki tētahi tukanga pakari mō te arotake whaiaro. E tino whai māramatanga te whānau mō ngā whakahaere me ngā whakaritenga o ia rā o te kōhanga reo. Kua tāututia e rātou ngā whāinga matua hei whakawhanake ake i te kōhanga reo. E hāngai ana ēnei whāinga nui ki ngā whāinga rautaki o Te Ara Tūāpae.

He whai tikanga te āhua o ngā tukanga mō te whakamāherehere me te aromatawai i ngā paetae o ngā tamariki. E ārahi ana te whakamāherehere i ngā āhuatanga hei whakatinana i te hōtaka. E tino mārama ana te nuinga o ngā kaimahi ki te pūtake o te aromatawai, ā, kei te tautoko i ērā o ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhanake i ō rātou māramatanga mō ngā āhuatanga hōhonu e pā ana ki te aromatawai. Kua tae mai ki te wā ināianei mā te whānau ki te whakatū i tētahi tukanga ki te aromātai i te whai hua o te hōtaka akoranga. Mā tēnei e taea ai e ngā kaimahi te whakarerekē i ngā wāhanga o te hōtaka kia pai ake ai ngā painga mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Ngā Mahi Ā Meāke Nei

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki.  Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o Te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Awhi Mai kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Awhi Mai.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko āna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Tangata.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere  i te māhere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • hōtaka whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Tangata

He whakamārama

I kōwhiritia e te whānau te Mana Tangata hei kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E ai ki a rātou, e hāngai ana te Mana Tangata ki te whakatipuranga o ngā tamariki hei ākonga māia, ā, hei ākonga mahira. E whai tautoko ana rātou kia manawanui ai rātou ki tō rātou ahurea, tō rātou reo me tō rātou tuakiri. I aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau ki te whakanui i te mana motuhake a ngā tamariki me ō rātou hiahia ki te tū hei ākonga mātau.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Ngā wheako akoranga. E whakarato ana ngā kaimahi i tētahi hōtaka e ākina i ngā tamariki ki te tū hei ākonga e ngākau nui ana ki te ako. Kei roto i te hōtaka akoranga ngā ngohe akoranga huhua, ā, e hāngai ana ēnei ngohe ki ngā tau rerekē o ngā tamariki me ō rātou ake matea. E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te taiao ō-ahupungao me te whakamātautau i ngā rauemi kua whakaritea mā rātou. He mahira te āhua o ngā tamariki, ā, ka kaha aro rātou ki ngā akoranga whai take mō te wā roa. E ai ki ngā pakeke, he ākonga mātau ēnei.

Ngā whai wāhitanga ā ngā tamariki ki te hōtaka. Kua whakanui ngā whai wāhitanga a ngā tamariki ki te hōtaka. Kua ākina ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi te whakahaere i ētahi wāhanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Kua riro i ngā tamariki ētahi tūranga whakahaere mō te karakia, te wāhanga mō te tuku pepeha me te karanga ingoa, ā, tō rātou hīkaka hoki ki te kawe i ēnei haepapa. He rawe ngā whakahoki kōrero a ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki, me te whakanui hoki i ā rātou whakatutukitanga.

Te whānaungatanga. Ko te whānaungatanga te tūāpapa mō ngā tauneketanga katoa. He mauritau, he aroha te āhua o ngā tauneketanga i waenganui i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. Te

 angitū hoki o ngā whakahaere ki te whakauru i ngā pēpi ki roto i te hōtaka ki te taha o ngā tamariki pakeke. Ka whakatauiratia te āhua o te ako e ngā tamariki pakeke, ā, ka whai haere ngā tamariki kōhungahunga i ēnei tauira. Ko ngā taunekeneke pono me ngā whānaungatanga whai tikanga te tāhuhu mō ngā akoranga ā ngā tamariki.

Te reo Māori. Ko te reo Māori me ōnā tikanga tētahi kaupapa whai take i te kōhanga reo. Ka nui te aro a ngā kamahi kia reo Māori te taiao i ngā wā katoa. He mōteatea te karawhiu a ngā kaimahi. Mā tēnei ka waia haere ngā tamariki ki ngā oro me ngā whakahī o ngā mōteatea ka rangona e rātou i runga i te marae. E whai wāhi ana ngā tamariki ki te hāpai i tētahi o ngā wāhanga whakahaere i te marae. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te whai haere i ngā tikanga me te uru atu ki roto i ngā ngohe katoa i te marae. He tōtika ō rātou whai i ngā tikanga i te wā e hāpai ana rātou i ngā tukanga ōkawa mō te pōwhiri i ngā manuhiri, te tuku karakia tae noa ki te wā ka whakataki rātou i ngā whakapapa. E tino whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao ko ō rātou tuakiri me ō rātou tikanga te kaupapa nui.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamāherehere me te aromātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo hei whakatau i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamāherehere, ngā whakahaere me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga whakahaere katoa o te kōhanga reo mā a rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka āta hāngai te aromātai nei ki te hōtaka whakamāherehere me te aroturuki i te tipuranga ako o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga kei te pai te whakahaere

Ngā whakahaere a te whānau. Kei te whānau ngā tukanga whai hua hei whakahaere i ngā wāhanga o tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka aroturuki te kaiwhakahaere i ngā tukanga katoa kia hāngai ai ngā whakahaere a te kōhanga reo ki ngā ture me ngā rekureihana.

Ngā kaupapa here me te whakahaere. He tōtika, ā, e whai mana ana ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga mō te hauora me te haumaru. Ka tino hāpai te whānau i ngā rautaki katoa hei whakahaere i tētahi kōhanga reo haumaru me tētahi kōhanga reo paru kore mā ngā tamariki. 

Ngā take e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi. He tino pai ngā tukanga mō te whakahaere kaimahi. Kua whakaritea kētia ngā rārangi mahi, ngā kirimana whakawhiwhi mahi, ngā whakatutuki ā-ture e pā ana ki ngā aromātai ā-Pirihimana me ngā tukanga aromātai i ngā kaimahi. E āhei ana ngā kaimahi ki te whai atu i ngā whakangungu hei whakapai ake i ā rātou putanga mahi.

Te Arotake Whaiaro. He tukanga tōtika tā te whānau mō te arotake whaiaro. Ka aromātai tētahi kaiarotake ō-waho i ngā whakahaere katoa o te whānau ia rua tau. E whakarato ana āna pūrongo i ngā kōrero whakatau mō ngā whakahaere a te whānau, ā, ka tuku anō hoki ia i ngā tūtohinga mō ngā mahi hei whakapai ake anō. Kua whakatinanahia te nuinga o ngā tūtohinga. Ka tino whai māramatanga te whānau mō ngā whakahaere me ngā whakaritenga o ia rā o te kōhanga reo.

Ngā whāinga matua o te māhere rautaki. Kua tāututia e te whānau ngā whāinga matua hei whakawhanake anō. Ko te reo Māori te kaupapa matua. Kua āta whakaritea te māhere rautaki, ā, kei roto i te māhere rautaki ngā whāinga, ngā putanga rautaki me ngā tūmahi hei whakatutuki. Ka hāngai pū ana ēnei mahi ki ngā whāinga o Te Ara Tūāpae.

Te whakamāherehere me te aromatawai. He whai tikanga tō te āhua o ngā tukanga mō te whakamāherehere me te aromatawai i ngā paetae o ngā tamariki. E whai wāhi ana te whānau ki te whakauru i ō rātou whakaaro mō te whakamāhere hōtaka. He mea hōhonu te āhua o ngā māhere a ia kaiako, ā, kei roto i ngā māhere ngā kōrero e pā ana ki te kaupapa ako, ngā rautaki me ngā rauemi. Kua āta whakaritea ngā māhere, ā, e arahi ana ngā māhere i ngā whakahaere ki te whakatinana i te hōtaka.

E tino mārama ana te nuinga o ngā kaimahi ki te pūtake o te aromatawai. E whakaatu ana nga pūkete paetae a ngā tamariki i ō rātou māramatanga ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. E whai tautoko ana ēra atu o ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhanake i ō rātou māramatanga ki te āta mau me te pupuri i ngā kōrero mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te hōtaka aromātai. Me whakatūria tētahi tukanga hei aromātai i te whai hua o te hōtaka akoranga.He mea uaua ki te mōhio i te whānui o ngā mahi kua whakapai ake anō, ā, he aha anō hoki te take e pērā ai. He kaupapa nui te hōtaka aromātai, i te mea he tautoko tēnei mā nga kaimahi kia taea ai rātou te whakarerekē i ngā wāhanga o te hōtaka hei painga mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

He whakamārama

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Awhi Mai tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga a te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Awhi Mai Te Kōhanga Reo is situated on the grounds of Te Papa o Te Aroha Marae in Tokoroa.

The whānau chose Mana Tangata as a focus for this review. Whānau report Mana Tangata relates to children feeling passionate about their culture, language and identity. The kaimahi provide a programme that encourages them to be active and eager learners. Children are supported to lead aspects of the learning programme. Children’s achievements are affirmed by kaimahi.

Kaimahi interactions with children are calm and loving. Relationships between children, their peers and adults are purposeful where whānaungatanga is strongly reflected. Te reo Māori and tikanga Māori are fundamental in the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi make sure that te reo Māori is maintained at all times. Children experience an environment where their identity and culture is valued.

The whānau have effective systems for managing the operations of the kōhanga reo. The kaiwhakahaere monitors all processes to make sure practices are consistent with current legislation and regulations. Health and safety policies and practice are up to date and include the new licensing requirements. The whānau take all reasonable steps to ensure the kōhanga reo is a safe and hygienic place for children.

Personnel systems are well managed. All required documentation is in place. There is a robust self review process. Whānau are well informed of the management and operations of the kōhanga reo. They have identified priorities for the further development of the kōhanga reo. These are clearly aligned with Te Ara Tūāpae strategic objectives.

There are clear processes for planning and assessing children’s progress. Planning guides programme implementation. Most kaimahi have a good understanding about the purpose of assessment and others are being supported to develop a deeper understanding. It is now time for the whānau to establish a process for evaluating the effectiveness of the learning programme. This should support kaimahi to make relevant changes to the programme to benefit children’s learning.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children.  Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Awhi Mai was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.  This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children atTe Kōhanga Reo o Te Awhi Mai.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Tangata.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education.  For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning; and
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below. 

4 Findings

Mana Tangata

Background

The whānau chose Mana Tangata as a focus for this review. They report Mana Tangata relates to the promotion and development of children who are confident, curious learners. They are supported to be passionate about their culture, language and identity. ERO evaluated the extent to which the whānau promotes children’s belonging and encourages them to be competent learners

Areas of good performance

Learning opportunities. Kaimahi provide a programme that encourages children to be active and eager learners. The learning programme includes a range of learning activities that cater for the ages and needs of all children. Children have opportunities to explore the physical environment and the different resources that are available. Children are curious and they engage in learning that is meaningful to them for sustained periods of time. Children are viewed as competent learners.

Children’s contribution to the programme. The contributions children make to the programme are valued. Children are encouraged to lead aspects of the learning programme. Karakia, pepeha and roll call responsibilities are delegated to children who are keen to take on these roles. Children receive affirming feedback from kaimahi. Their achievements are celebrated.

Relationship. Whānaungatanga underpins all interactions. Kaimahi interactions with children are calm and loving. The babies are integrated successfully in to the programme alongside the older children. The older children model learning that the younger children imitate. Meaningful interactions and positive relationships create a firm foundation for children’s learning.

Te reo Māori. Te reo Māori and tikanga Māori are fundamental and valued in the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi make sure te reo Māori is maintained at all times. Kaiako chant mōteatea. This allows children to become accustomed to the sounds and pitches of mōteatea that are commonly heard on the marae. Children have opportunities to be part of all marae activities. They demonstrate appropriate practice when engaging in formal processes such as welcoming manuhiri, karakia and reciting whakapapa. Children experience an environment where their identity and cultural practices are valued.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent, to which the whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas of the operations.  Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and the monitoring of children’s progress and development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management. The whānau have effective systems to manage the operations of their kōhanga reo. The kaiwhakahaere monitors all processes to ensure practices are consistent with current legislation and regulations.

Policies and procedures. Health and safety policies and practices are up to date. The whānau takes all reasonable steps to make sure the kōhanga reo is a safe and hygienic place for children.

Personnel matters. Personnel systems are well managed. Job descriptions, employment contracts, police vetting requirements and appraisals systems are in place. Kaimahi have access to professional development to enhance their practice.

Self review. The whānau have a robust self review process. An external reviewer evaluates all aspects of whānau management every two years. Her reports provide clear judgements about whānau practices and offers recommendations for improvement. Most of the recommendations are acted on. Whānau are well informed of the management and operations of the kōhanga reo.

Strategic priorities. The whānau have identified priorities for further development. Te reo Māori is the major focus. The strategic plan is organised, well written and includes goals, outcomes strategies and actions. These are clearly aligned with Te Ara Tūāpae strategic objectives.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews.  The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation.  This area is most likely to support long term improvement.  Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based on Te Whāriki, thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues.  The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement.  A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue.  There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
