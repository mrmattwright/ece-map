1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kei Pukenui Te Kōhanga Reo o Houhora e tū ana, pātata tonu ki Kaitāia. Nō taua takiwā tonu ngā tamariki o tēnei kōhanga reo. I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ki tā te whānau titiro, ko te kōhanga reo tētahi wāhi ka whakapakari i te tino tupu ā-Māori nei o ā rātou tamariki. Ka whakamanatia te whakapapa o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo, ā, e āhei ana rātou ki te ako ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ka whai pānga nui te hōtaka akoranga me ngā mahi whakahāere a te whānau ki te tuakiri, te tū pakari, me te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki. E pou here ana te whanaungatanga i ngā whakaritenga katoa ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā wheako akoranga ōkawa, ōpaki anō hoki mā ngā tamariki, ā, ka rumakina ki te reo Māori. Kei te pakari hāere te māiatanga o ngā tamariki pakeke ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te tūhura, ki te whakaputa i tō rātou wairua auaha hoki nā te pai o te whakariterite i ngā taiao akoranga. Ka whakamahia tonutia e ngā tamariki i ngā wāhi wātea me ngā taputapu hoki. Kua tipu te whai whakaarotanga a ngā tamariki ki tā rātou tū i roto i te ao whānui.

Ka whai hua ngā tukanga a ngā kaimahi hei whakarite hōtaka, te whakamātautau i te akoranga a ngā tamariki me te whakatinana i te hōtaka. E tukuna tonutia ana ngā mōhiohio ki te whānau i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki mā roto i ngā mahi whakariterite me ngā whakamātautau. Kua arotakengia, kua whakahoungia anō hoki te tūtohinga.He mārama te aronga o tēnei tuhinga mō te whakahāeretanga me te whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga o te hauora me te haumaru e ārahi ana i ngā whakaritenga a ngā kaimahi. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te noho ki tētahi taiao akoranga e whai whakaaro nui ana ki tō rātou hauora, tā rātou noho haumaru hoki. E āta mārama ana te whakatakoto me te whakamahi i ngā pepa whai mana mā te katoa o te whānau o te kōhanga reo. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki tētahi taiao akoranga e whakapūmautia ana ngā mahi e ngā kaimahi, ā, kua āta whakamaheretia, kua āta whakatinanahia anō hoki te hōtaka akoranga.

He tūkaha ngā tukanga tahua, ā, he pūataata ngā whakatakotoranga. Ka whakahāere paitia te pūtea hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Whakahāngaitia te mahere rautaki a te whānau ki ngā whāinga o Te Ara Tūāpae. Ka whakaatu mārama mai ai te mahere rautaki i ngā tino mahi hei whakawhanake ake ā kō ake nei. He matawhānui ngā tukanga arotake whaiaro. Ka aroturukihia e te whānau i tā rātou ahunga whakamua me te whakatau i ngā take e hiahiatia ana hei whakatikatika mā rātou.

Me whakapakari ake i te aromātai hōtaka. Nā te aromātai i te hōtaka akoranga ia wā, ia wā, e āhei ana ngā kamahi te aro turuki i te pakaritanga o te hōtaka akoranga. He take tō te whānau ki te ū ki te whakawhanake i tō rātou reo Māori kia āhei ai te tautoko i ā rātou tamariki ki te kāinga. Ahakoa he pūmau te ngākau nui o te whānau ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori, kua tae ki te wā kia whakatinana rātou i ō rātou wawata hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga reo (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

He pēhea te poipoi i te tuakiri, te aronga toi whenuatanga, me te tū pakari o ngā tamariki?

E whai hua ana te poipoi i te tuakiri, te aronga toi whenuatanga, me te tū pakari hoki o ngā tamariki puta noa i te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahāere

E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki mā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo huarahi, kia whanake ai te tino tuakiri, te aronga toi whenuatanga, me te tū pakari. Ka whai pānga nui te waihanga o te hōtaka akoranga ki te poipoi i te tuakiri, te tū pakari, me te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki. Ka kitea mā ngā āhuatanga e whai ake nei, arā:

  • kua whakawhanaunga atu ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki mā ngā huarahi whakaute. Ka whakanuia ngā mahi angitu a ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakamanahia tēnā me tēnā o rātou. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā taunekeneke e whai pūtake ana, e poipoi ana hoki i ngākau titikaha o te tamaiti
  • e pou here ana te whanaungatanga i ngā whakaritenga katoa ki te kōhanga reo. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā whai wāhitanga ako e whakamana ana i tō rātou whakapapa me tō rātou tuakiri. Ka kitea te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki
  • ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā wheako akoranga ōkawa, ōpaki anō hoki ki ngā tamariki. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te kawe i ā rātou ake akoranga. I ētahi wā, ka tino whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga mō ngā wā roa. He ākonga pakari ngā tamariki
  • ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. He mātau te kaiako ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā rautaki hei hāpai i te māramatanga me te whakamahinga o ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. E pakari hāere ana te māia o ngā tamariki tuākana ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori
  • kua whakaritea ngā wāhi ako hei hāpai i ngā tamariki ki te tūhura me te mahi auaha. Ka āta whakamahi ngā tamariki i ngā wāhi wāteatea me ngā rauemi. He pai te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki ngā atua Māori me tō rātou whai pānga ki te āhua o tō rātou noho ki te kōhanga reo i ia rā. He mōhiotanga tō ngā tamariki ki tō rātou whai wāhi ki te ao whānui.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whakahāere a te whānau i ngā whakaritenga o tō rātou kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki?

Ka whakahāere te whānau i ngā whakaritenga o tō rātou kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahāere

He pakari ngā tukanga ā ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, me te whakatinanatanga o te hōtaka. He matawhānui te whakamahere. Ka āta tāutuhia te kiko o te hōtaka, ā, ka hāngai ki Te Whāriki. He pai te whakahāeretanga me te whakaritenga o te hōtaka. Ka āta whakamāramahia ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki ki ngā pūrongo e whakaratohia e ngā kaimahi mā te whānau. Kua tīmata ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhanake hāere i ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki. He pai te noho mōhio o te whānau ki tēnā e akohia ana e ngā tamariki mā ngā mahere me ngā mōhiohio aromatawai.

Kua arotakengia, kua whakahoungia hoki te tūtohinga. He mārama te aronga o tēnei tuhinga mō te whakahāeretanga me te whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo. Ka āta whakaraupapa te tūtohinga i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo me ngā kawenga a te whānau. Ka whakapau kaha te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā wawata i tāutuhia ai ki tā rātou tūtohinga.

Ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga o te hauora me te haumaru e ārahi ana i ngā whakaritenga a ngā kaimahi. He rārangi hei āta whakarite i te arotakenga o ngā kaupapa here i ia te wā. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki tētahi taiao akoranga e whakaaro nui ana ki tō rātou hauora, tō rātou noho haumaru hoki.

Kua āta whakatakotohia ngā tuhinga katoa e pā ana ki ngā kaimahi. Ka kitea te mahi tika a te whānau hei kaituku mahi. He mārama ki ngā kaimahi ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga hoki, tae atu ki te mātauranga o ā rātou tamariki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki tētahi taiao akoranga e noho ihupuku ana ngā kaimahi, ā, kua āta whakamaheretia, kua āta whakatinanahia anō hoki te hōtaka akoranga.

He pai te whakahāeretanga o ngā pūnaha pūtea, ā, he mārama hoki ngā mahi pūrongo. He pai ngā rauemi ki te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakahāerehia ngā pūtea hei painga mō ngā tamariki.

He mahere rautaki tā te whānau e hāngai ana ki ngā whāinga whānui o Te Ara Tūāpae. He mahere mahi hoki, me tētahi rautaki mō te aroturukitanga o ngā putanga. Ka āta whakatakoto te mahere rautaki i ngā kaupapa matua o te kōhanga reo hei whakawhanaketanga ake ā meāke nei.

He matawhānui ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro. Pūrongotia ai ki te whānau i ia te wā, mō ngā āhuatanga o te whakahāeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Nā tēnei, e āhei ana te whānau ki te aroturuki i te ahunga whakamua, me te whakatutuki i ngā take e tika ana.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Me whakapakari te aromātai hōtaka, ā, me tuhi anō hoki i ngā āhuatanga pakari o te hōtaka, ngā tūmomo wero pea anō hoki mā rātou, me ngā āhuatanga hoki hei whakapai ake. Ko te aromātai i ia te wā ka hāpai i ngā kaimahi ki te aroturuki i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga me te whai pānga o tēnei ki ngā tamariki.

Ka arotahi te tūtohinga me te mahere rautaki ki te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori mō te whānau. He iti noa te ahunga whakamua i roto i tēnei āhuatanga. Ahakoa te pūmau o te ngākau nui o te whānau ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori, kua tae ki te wā kia whakatinana rātou i ō rātou wawata, hei hāpai anō hoki i ā rātou tamariki.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahāerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahāerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahāerenga pūtea me ngā whakahāerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga hāerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia tahuri te whānau ki te:

  • rapu tautoko mō ngā kaimahi, hei hāpai i te whakapakaritanga ake o te aromātai hōtaka
  • āta rapu i ngā huarahi ki te whakapakari ake i tō rātou āheinga ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, hei hāpai ake anō i te whakawhanaketanga o te reo o ā rātou tamariki, i roto i ō rātou ake kāinga.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahāeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Houhora is located at Pukenuinear Kaitaia. The whānau chose ngā taumata whakahirahira as the focus for this review. They see the kōhanga reo as a place where their children can grow strong as Māori. The kōhanga reo is a place where the children’s whakapapa is valued and they are able to learn to speak te reo Māori.

Children are confident learners and demonstrate a strong sense of belonging. The learning programme and whānau management practices contribute considerably to children’s identity, confidence and belonging. Kaimahi have established respectful relationships with children. Whanaungatanga underpins all practices in the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi provide a range of formal and informal learning experiences for children that are immersed in te reo Māori.

Whānau manage the kōhanga reo in the interests of their children. The charter has been reviewed and updated. This document provides clear direction for the management and operations of the kōhanga reo. Health and safety policies and procedures guide kaimahi practice. Children enjoy a learning environment where their health and safety is a priority. Children enjoy a learning environment where the kaimahi are consistent and the learning programme is well planned and implemented.

Programme evaluation requires strengthening. While whānau are sincere about their commitment to use te reo Māori, it is now timely for them to action their aspirations to benefit their children.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose ngā taumata whakahirahira as the focus of the review.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

How well is the identity, sense of belonging and confidence of children nurtured?

Children’s sense of identity and belonging and their confidence is effectively nurtured throughout the kōhanga reo.

Areas of good performance

Children are effectively supported in a range of ways to develop a strong sense of identity, belonging and confidence. The design of the learning programme contributes considerably to fostering children’s identity, confidence and belonging. This is evident in the following ways:

  • kaimahi have established respectful relationships with children. Children’s successes are celebrated and they are affirmed as individuals. Children enjoy interactions that are meaningful and foster self confidence
  • whanaungatanga underpins all practices in the kōhanga reo. Children experience learning opportunities where their whakapapa and identity is a priority and meaningful. Children demonstrate a strong sense of belonging
  • kaimahi provide children with a range of formal and informal learning experiences. There are opportunities for children to take responsibility for their own learning. These are times when children become highly engaged in learning for a sustained period of time. Children are confident learners
  • children are immersed in te reo Māori. The kaiako is a competent speaker of Māori. The kaimahi use a range of strategies to support children’s understanding and use of te reo Māori. The older children use te reo Māori with increasing confidence
  • the learning environments are organised to support children to explore and be creative. Children make maximum use of the spaces and resources. Children have a good understanding of the atua Māori and the impact they have on their daily lives at kōhanga reo. Children have an awareness of their place in the wider world.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How well does the whānau manage the operations of their kōhanga reo to benefit children?

The whānau manages the operations of their kōhanga reo well to benefit their children.

Areas of good performance

The kaimahi have robust processes for programme planning, assessing children’s learning and programme implementation. Planning is comprehensive. Programme content is clearly identified and aligns to Te Whāriki. The programme is well managed and organised. Children’s learning is clearly described in reports that kaimahi provide for whānau. Kaimahi are in the early stages of developing children’s profiles. Whānau are well informed of what children learn through planning and assessment information.

The charter has been reviewed and updated. This document provides clear direction for the management and operations of the kōhanga reo. The charter clearly outlines the kaupapa of the kōhanga reo and whānau responsibilities. Whānau work tirelessly to fulfil the aspirations identified in their charter.

Health and safety policies and procedures guide kaimahi practice. There is a schedule in place to ensure policies are regularly reviewed. Children enjoy a learning environment where their health and safety is a priority.

All personnel documentation is clearly set out. The whānau demonstrate they are fair employers. Kaimahi are clear about their roles and responsibilities including the education of their children. Children enjoy a learning environment where the kaimahi are conscientious and the learning programme is well planned and implemented.

Financial systems are well managed and reporting is transparent. The learning programme is well resourced. The finances are managed in the best interest of the children.

The whānau have a strategic plan which aligns to the broad goals of Te Ara Tūāpae. There is an action plan and a strategy for monitoring outcomes. The strategic plan clearly outlines the kōhanga reo priorities for future development.

Self review processes are comprehensive. There is regular reporting to whānau on aspects of the kōhanga reo operations. This ensures that whānau are able to monitor progress and address any matters that require attention.

Areas for development

Programme evaluation requires strengthening to include documenting the strengths of the programme, any challenges they face and where improvements can be made. Regular evaluation will enable kaimahi to monitor the effectiveness of the learning programme and the impact it has on children.

The charter and strategic plan focus on the development of te reo Māori for the whānau. There has been very little progress in this direction. While whānau are sincere about their commitment to use te reo Māori, it is now timely for them to action their aspirations in support of their children.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO LicensedKōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends that whānau:

  • seek support for kaimahi to help strengthen programme evaluation practice
  • actively seek ways to build their own capacity to speak Māori in order to support their children’s language development in their homes.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
