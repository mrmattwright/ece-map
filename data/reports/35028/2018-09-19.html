1 Te Horopaki

Ko te awa ko au, ko au ko te awa.

He tino hononga tō Te Kōhanga Reo o Tātou Katoa Rā ki te marae o Ngāpūwaiwaha ki Taumarunui. E ahu mai ana ngā whakaakoranga o te kōhanga reo i ngā whakapono me ngā tikanga whakaaro o Te Kotahitanga. Ko te aronga o te whānau i tēnei wā, ko te whakapakari ake i te whanaungatanga me ngā whakaakoranga i tuku iho mai i ō rātou tīpuna.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whai wāhi atu ana ki ngā akoranga?

He ngākau titikaha ō ngā tamariki i a rātou e whai wāhi atu ana ki ngā akoranga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E ako ana ngā tamariki ki te whakawhitiwhiti kōrero mā te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki reo Māori hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. Ko ētahi o aua rautaki, ko te tautoko i ngā tamariki i roto i ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā mihi, ngā rotarota, ngā wā mirimiri, ngā pepeha, me ngā whakataukī. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whakautu tika i ngā tohutohu a ngā kaimahi. Ka taea e rātou ngā kupu me ngā pikitia te whakahāngai. Ka taea e ngā tamariki tō rātou māramatanga te whakaatu mā te kupu kotahi. Ka whakamahi ngā pēpi i te reo ā-tinana hei whakapuaki i tō rātou māramatanga ki ngā waiata. E āhei ana rātou ki te nekeneke mā te manawataki tonu o ngā waiata i ngā wā o te kanikani, te hurahura, me te pekepeke. E whanake ana te puāwai mai o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki.

E tautāwhitia ana ngā tamariki ki te whanaungatanga. He tino hononga ā-whakapapa ki waenga i te whānau. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i te aroha, te awhi, te manaaki, te tiaki, me te harikoa. Ka whakaute, ka atawhai hoki ngā tamariki i ō rātou hoa me ngā pakeke. He mātātoa te poipoi o ngā tuākana i ō rātou tēina. Ka noho mataara rātou i a rātou e mahi ana ki te taha o ngā pēpi. Ka kitea te aroha o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou kuia. Kua whai wāhi mai te kuia ki te kōhanga reo nei, mai i tōna tīmatanga. Kua whai wāhi mai ngā whakatupuranga e rima o tōna whānau ki te kōhanga reo nei, ā, e whai wāhi tonu ana. Ka whakamihi ngā kaimahi i ngā whakatutukitanga o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamanahia ngā tamariki i roto i ō rātou herenga tangata. E whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i ngā tino hua o te whakawhanaungatanga ki ētahi atu.

E ako ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e poipoi ana i tō rātou tuakiri. He tino hononga ō te whānau ki a Whanganui, ki a Ngāti Tūwharetoa, ā, ki a Ngāti Maniapoto hoki. Taetae atu ai ngā tamariki ki ngā marae i ia te wā, hei whakapakari ake i ō rātou hononga ki te whenua. E ako ana rātou i ngā tikanga me ngā kawa o ō rātou iwi. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te ako i ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga hoki hei tangata whenua. Hīkoi haere ai ngā tamariki i tō rātou hapori i ia te wā, kia ako ai rātou i ngā tini āhuatanga o tō rātou wāhi noho. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi ki te hōtaka akoranga, ko ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanaunga atu ki ētahi atu o ngā tamariki o tō rātou purapura. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te manaaki i tō rātou kōhanga reo. He pakari te āhua o ngā tamariki hei ākonga.

Ka whai wāhi mātātoa atu ngā tamariki ki te whānuitanga o ngā kaupapa tākaro, kaupapa ako hoki. Ka ako ngā tamariki i a Matariki, i ngā whetūao, ā, i ngā tini pūmanawa anō hoki o ngā atua Māori. Ka whakaatuhia ngā mahi a ngā tamariki puta noa i te kōhanga reo. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā wā mō ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. Ka whakarato anō hoki ngā kaimahi i tētahi hōtaka o te ata e whakaongaonga ana, e whakahihiri ana hoki i ngā tamariki. Ka whakapuaki ngā kaimahi i ngā wheako papai, ngā wheako hoki e hāpai ana i ngā tamariki i roto i ngā akoranga me ngā tū tākaro. Ka whakapuaki ngā kaimahi i ngā whakaritenga i tuku iho ai, me ērā anō hoki o te ao hurihuri nei mā ngā tamariki. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō, ki ētahi atu anō hoki i tō rātou wāhi noho.

E rumakina ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e āta tuitui ana i a Ranginui rātou ko Papatūānuku, ko ngā atua Māori hoki ki te taiao ako. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā karakia o te Hāhi Ringatū me Te Kotahitanga. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te ārahi i ngā karakia e tū ai i ia ata i te 11 karaka. Ka āta whai pūtake ngā hononga tangata e whakapūmautia ana e ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He tukanga ā te whānau ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o tō rātou kōhanga reo. He tuhinga e tohu ana i ngā mahi ki te arotake i te tūtohinga. Kua tāutuhia e te whānau ngā kaupapa matua o te paetawhiti, mō ngā whakawhanaketanga o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei. Kua whakatakotohia te katoa o ngā kaupapa here e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. I tēnei wā, kei te rawaka aua kaupapa here hei āta whakarite i te whai whakaarotanga ake ki te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki. Kua arowhaingia ngā kaimahi e ngā pirihimana. Kua whakatakotohia anō hoki ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi me ngā kirimana mahi. I tēnei wā, e whakahaerehia ana ngā pūtea e te tari ā-rohe o te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo. Tū ai ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, ā, he pai te taetae atu ki ēnei. Ka whakaaro nuitia ngā tamariki i roto i ngā mahi whakahaere i te tari o te kōhanga reo.

He matawhānui ngā mahere hōtaka, ā, e whai kiko ana hoki. He tino aronga ki ngā hononga ā-iwi i roto i ngā mahere. E hāngai ana ngā mahere ki ngā wawata mō ngā tamariki e whakatakotohia ana ki Te Whāriki. Ka whakamōhiotia ngā aromatawai ki ngā pūrongo ā-tamaiti, ā, ka whakatau te whānau i te āhua o te ahu whakamua me te whakawhanaketanga ā-hinengaro, ā-tinana, ā-wairua, ā-whatumanawa hoki o ngā tamariki. He pai te whakariterite i te ako a ngā tamariki me ā rātou aromatawai.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Me whai whakaaro ki ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro. Kāhore te aromātai e āta tāutu ana i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Kua tae ki te wā kia whakarite te whānau i tētahi tukanga ka aroturuki i te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga. Me whakapakari ake te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi. Kua tae ki te wā kia whakaōkawa te whānau i te tukanga e arohaehae ana i ngā kaimahi. Kāhore i te auau te arowhai i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea. Ko ngā aratohu e pā ana ki te aroturuki moe, kāhore e hāngai ana ki ngā herenga ā-ture o tēnei wā. Kāhore e whakatutukihia ana ngā akoako hōneatanga inā he rū whenua. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki i te wā ka riterite te whakatutuki auau o ngā mahi ki te arotake, te aroturuki, me te pūrongo i ngā āhuatanga matua o ngā whakahaeretanga.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Hei whakatutuki i ngā herenga ā-ture, me tahuri te whānau ki te:

  • arotake me te whakahou ake i ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga hei āta whakarite i te whakatakotoranga o ngā herenga katoa e whai pānga ana ki ngā akoako hōneatanga ohorere inā he rū whenua, ā, ki ngā wā moe hoki.
    [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS1-33 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā:

  • kia whakapakarihia e te whānau te arotake whaiaro, hei āta whakatika i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki te aromātai hōtaka, ngā mahi arohaehae, te arowhai i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea, me ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana ngā herenga ā-ture.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Tātou Katoa Rā i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Ko te awa ko au, ko au ko te awa.

Te Kōhanga reo o Tātou Katoa Rā has close links to Ngāpūwaiwaha marae in Taumarunui. The teachings of the kōhanga reo are based the beliefs and philosophy of Te Kotahitanga. The current focus for the whānau is to strengthen whanaungatanga and the teachings of their tīpuna.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children engage in learning?

Children engage in learning with confidence.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are learning to communicate in te reo Māori. Kaimahi use a range of te reo Māori strategies to support children’s language development. These include supporting children during karakia, hīmene, mihi, rotarota, mirimiri, pepeha and whakataukī. Children are able to respond to instructions from kaimahi. They can associate words with pictures. Children can use single words to convey their understanding. Babies use body language to express their understanding of waiata. They are able to move in time with the rhythm of the music during kanikani, hurahura and pekepeke. Children are developing as early learners of te reo Māori.

Children are embraced by whanaungatanga. Whānau have strong whakapapa connections. Kaimahi role model aroha, awhi, manaaki, tiaki and harikoa. Children are respectful and warm towards their peers and adults. The older children play an active role as tuākana to the teina. They are careful as they work alongside the babies. Children interact lovingly with their kuia. She has been with the kōhanga reo since it began. Five generations of her whānau have attended and are still involved with the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi praise children’s successes. Children are affirmed in their relationships. Children enjoy meaningful contact with others.

Children learn in an environment that nurtures their identity. The whānau have strong links to Whanganui, Ngāti Tūwharetoa and Ngāti Maniapoto. Children frequently visit marae to strengthen their connections with the land. They learn about tikanga and kawa of their iwi. Kaimahi support children to learn about their roles and responsibilities as tangata whenua. Children take regular walks around their community to learn about their neighbourhood. Kaimahi provide opportunity in the learning programme for children to enjoy interacting with children from their purapura. Kaimahi encourage children care for their kōhanga reo. Children appear secure as learners.

Children actively participate in a range of play and learning situations. Children learn about Matariki, the planets, and the different realms of atua Māori. Children’s mahi is displayed throughout the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi provide time for children to follow their own interests. Kaimahi provide a morning programme that is stimulating and energising. Kaimahi expose children to positive and supportive experiences in learning and in play. Kaimahi introduce traditional and contemporary practises for children to encounter. Children are aware of themselves and others in their environment.

Children are immersed in a setting that infuses Ranginui, Papatūānuku and atua Māori into the learning environment. Children learn karakia from Te Hāhi Rātana and Te Kotahitanga. Kaimahi provide children with an opportunity to lead the 11.00am karakia. Children build meaningful relationships.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau have processes in place to manage the operations of their kōhanga reo. There is evidence of charter review. The whānau have identified long term priorities for the future development of the kōhanga reo. All health and safety policies are in place. At this time these are sufficient to ensure children’s health and safety are considered. Kaimahi have been police vetted. There are job descriptions and employment contracts in place. The regional office of Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust currently manages the finances. There are regular whānau hui and these are well attended. Children are considered in the administration of kōhanga reo business.

Programme planning is comprehensive and detailed. Planning has a strong focus on iwi connections. Planning aligns with Te Whāriki aspirations for children. Assessment is informed through pūrongo ā tamaiti where whānau gauge children’s progress and development in terms of hinengaro, tinana, wairua and whatumanawa. Children’s learning and assessments are well organised.

Key Next Steps

Self-review processes require attention. Evaluation does not clearly identify the effectiveness of the learning programme. It is time for whānau to establish a process to monitor the quality of the learning programme. Kaimahi appraisals require strengthening. It is time for whānau to formalise the process of kaimahi appraisal. Hazard checks are irregular. Guidelines for sleep checks do not align to current legislation. Earthquake emergency drills are not carried out. Children will benefit when regular reviewing, monitoring and reporting against key aspects of operations are consistent.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

To meet legislative requirements, the whānau must:

  • review and update policies and procedures to ensure all requirements for earthquake emergency evacuation drills and sleeping are in place.
    [Regulation 46 (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS1-33 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • That the whānau strengthen self-review to ensure that programme evaluation, appraisals, hazard checks and areas of non-compliance are rectified.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Tātou Katoa Rā will be in three years.
