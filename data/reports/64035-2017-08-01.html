1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hono ki Kōkiri ki Wainuiomata. Koia nei tētahi o ngā kōhanga reo e toru o te rohe whānui o Ōrongomai, Te Awakairangi, me Wainuiomata e kāwanatia ana e te Kōkiri Marae Keriana Olsen Trust. Kua tekau mā tahi tau te kaiako e mahi ana ki ngā kōhanga reo nei. Ka whai wāhi nui tōna mōhiotanga me ōna pūkenga ki te whakawhanaketanga me te whakatinanatanga o te marau e āta whai hua ana.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga me tō rātou hononga anō hoki ki ngā tāngata me te wāhi e karapoti ana i a rātou?

Ka kitea te tino aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki ki ngā tāngata me ngā wāhi e karapoti ana i a rātou.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E ākina ana ngā tamariki kia whakawhiti kōrero rātou mā te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā pakiwaitara me ngā rauemi ataata hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. Ka ako ngā tamariki ka whakatakotohia te pakiwaitara mā te reo tuhituhi me ngā pikitia. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te taki i ngā karakia me ngā whakapapa. Ka whakapūmautia ngā pūkenga whakawhiti whakaaro o ngā tamariki, me tō rātou tuakiri. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori me ngā waiata hei whakatau, hei tautāwhi hoki i ngā pēpi. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā kupu me ngā nekehanga hei akiaki i te whai wāhi mai o ngā pēpi. Whakaratohia ai e ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te waiata, te mōteatea, me te mihimihi. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā tauira reo mā te tāruarua. He aronga nahanaha tā ngā kaimahi i roto i ā rātou mahi ki te whakaako mā te reo Māori. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i ā rātou kawenga ki ētahi atu. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i te mahi ngātahi a ngā tamariki. Ka āta whakarite rātou i te tautoko o ngā tuākana i ngā tēina, i ngā wā e tika ana. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i te noho mārama o ngā tamariki ki te whai pānga nui o te rangimārie me te mauritau ki te wairua o te whare. Ka āta whakaaro nui ngā tamariki ki ō rātou hoa me ngā kaimahi. Ka whakamihia rātou e ngā kaimahi mō ā rātou whakamātau me ā rātou whakatutukitanga. Ka tino mōhio ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā rautaki kōkiri e akiaki ana i te mahi ngātahi a ngā tamariki. Ka whakapau kaha ngā kaimahi ki te whakarato i ngā whai wāhitanga maha mō ngā pēpi ki te āta taunekeneke atu ki ngā tamariki tuākana. He pakari ngā hononga o ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu.

E whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e āta poipoi ana i tō rātou tuakiri. Ka āta whakatau ngā kaimahi i te noho riterite o te ōkawa me te ōpaki o ngā akoranga. Mā ngā akoranga ōkawa ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki te ako i ō rātou tuakiri me ō rātou tūrangawaewae. Ka whakapūmau ngā kaiako i ngā hononga ā-whakapapa hei poipoi i te tūmāia o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahere ngā kaimahi i te hōtaka kia āta waia haere ai ngā tamariki ki ngā mahinga me te whakatakotoranga o ngā akoranga. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te āta whakawhiti haere ki ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga. Nā tēnei, e whakahaere ana ngā tamariki i tō rātou wāhi ake. E mōhio ana rātou ki tō rātou hononga pū ki te kōhanga reo me tō rātou marae. Ka kitea te tino aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki.

Ka āta whakatau ngā tamariki i te ao e noho nei rātou, mā te tūhura me te tākaro. Ka āta whakarite ngā kaimahi i te whiwhinga o ngā tamariki i tētahi marautanga riterite e whakamahi ana i ngā rauemi māoriori me ngā rauemi hou. E arotahi ana ngā mahere hōtaka ki ngā akoranga e hāngai matua ana ki ngā atua Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā pūrākau, ngā karakia, me ngā mōteatea hei whakapuaki ake i te ao o ngā atua Māori ki ngā tamariki. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te kōkiri i ā rātou ake akoranga, me te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. Ka poipoia tō rātou pākiki. He tūmāia ngā tamariki hei ākonga.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ko ngā tamariki te aronga o ngā whakahaeretanga katoa ki te kōhanga reo. E whai hua ana, he tōtika anō hoki ngā whakaritenga whakahaere a te tarahiti o te marae. He pakari, he mārama hoki ngā pūnaha e pā ana ki te whakahaere tari. He nui ngā tūmanako o te kaiwhakahaere, ā, ka arotahi ēnei ki te hiranga i ngā taumata katoa. Kua whakaritea tētahi rārangi mahi hei ārahi i te aromātai o roto. Pūrongo ai te kaiwhakahaere ki ngā matapopore i ngā wā e whā o te tau, mō ngā take katoa e whai pānga ana ki ngā whakahaeretanga. E whakatutuki ana ngā pūrongo i ngā āhuatanga katoa, ā, he matawhānui anō hoki. Ka āta tāutuhia ngā take katoa. Ka whakatakotohia ngā whakataunga, ā, ka aroturukitia ngā putanga. Ko te pakari o te aromātai o roto me te whakamahere anō hoki mō te tauatanga e hāpai ana i te rōnakitanga o te kōhanga reo mō meāke nei. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā ngā pūnaha e arotahi ana ki te whakapai tonu i ia te wā i ngā whakaritenga.

E hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki e te tino ārahitanga i ngā taumata katoa o te kōhanga reo. Ka mahi ngātahi te kaiwhakahaere me te kaiako matua ki te ārahi whānui i te hōtaka me ngā whakahaeretanga o ia rā. Kei ia kōhanga reo tētahi kaiako e ārahi ana i te whakawhanaketanga o te hōtaka. Kei te hāpai te kaiako i te whakawhanaketanga ngaio o ngā kaiāwhina, ā, ka ārahi, ka aroturuki hoki i te whakapai tonutanga o te hōtaka. He pai tōna māramatanga ki ngā pūnaha whakahaere tari, ā, ka poipoi ia i te ārahitanga ngātahi o ngā kaimahi katoa. He mauritau ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao ako.

Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki tētahi hōtaka akoranga e whakatutuki ana i ō rātou matea ako. Ka whai hua ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te whakarite me te whakahaere i te whakatinanatanga o te hōtaka akoranga. He matawhānui ngā mahere, te aromātai, me ngā mōhiohio aromatawai, e tino whai kiko ana hoki, ā, e whakamahia ana ki te whakatinana haere i ngā whakapaitanga. Ka aro ngā aromatawai o ngā tamariki ki ō rātou ngākau nuitanga me ā rātou akoranga. Ka whakatakotohia ā-tuhi nei te ahu whakamua me ngā whakatutukitanga o ngā tamariki i roto i te roanga o te wā. He harikoa te ako a ngā tamariki, ā, e whai wāhi nui ana rātou ki ngā akoranga.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

 

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hono ki Kōkiri i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hono ki Kōkiri Marae is in Wainuiomata. The kōhanga reo is one of three in the Hutt Valley and Wainuiomata that is governed by the Kōkiri Marae Keriana Olsen Trust. The kaiako has worked between these kōhanga reo for eleven years. Her knowledge and skills contribute to the development and implementation of highly effective curriculum.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children demonstrate a sense of belonging and connection to the people and places around them?

Children demonstrate a strong sense of belonging to the people and places around them.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are encouraged to communicate in te reo Māori. Kaimahi use storytelling and visual aids to support children’s language development. Children learn that text and illustrations create a story. Kaimahi support children to recite karakia and whakapapa. Children’s communication abilities and sense of identity are reinforced. Kaimahi use te reo Māori and waiata to soothe and comfort babies. They use words and gestures to invite babies to engage. Kaimahi provide regular opportunities for children to participate in waiata, mōteatea and mihimihi. Children learn language patterns through repetition. Kaimahi are deliberate in their approach to teach through te reo Māori. Children are developing as speakers of te reo Māori.

Children learn about their responsibilities to others. Kaimahi encourage children to work together. They ensure that older siblings support their teina when needed. Kaimahi support children to understand that times of peace and calm are important to the wairua of the whare. Children are very respectful to their peers and kaimahi. They receive positive feedback from kaimahi for their efforts and achievements. Kaimahi know the children well. They use proactive strategies to encourage children to work together. Kaimahi provide as many opportunities as possible for babies to have meaningful interactions with the older children. Children are secure in their relationships with others.

Children experience an environment where their identity is fostered. Kaimahi ensure there is a balance between formal and informal learning. Formal learning provides children opportunities to learn about who they are and where they come from. Kaiako reinforce whakapapa connections to foster children’s confidence. Kaimahi plan the programme to ensure that children are familiar with routines and sequences of learning. Children are able to transition successfully between learning experiences. This enables children to have control over their immediate environment. They know they belong to their kōhanga reo and marae. Children demonstrate a strong sense of belonging.

Children make sense of their world through exploration and play. Kaimahi ensure children receive a balanced curriculum based on both natural and contemporary resources. Programme planning focuses on learning that is based predominantly on ngā atua Māori. Kaimahi use storytelling, karakia and mōteatea to bring the world of atua Māori alive for children. Children also have opportunities to initiate their own learning and pursue their own interests. Their curiosity is fostered. Children are confident learners.

Planning and Evaluation

Children are the focus of all kōhanga reo operations. The Marae Trust have effective and efficient management structures in place. The administration systems are robust and rigorous. The manager has high expectations which focuses on excellence at all levels. There is a schedule in place to guide internal evaluation. The manager reports quarterly to the trustees on all matters of operations. Reporting is thorough and comprehensive. All issues are clearly identified. Resolutions are proposed and outcomes are monitored. The combination of strong internal evaluation and succession planning ensures that the future of the kōhanga reo is sustainable. Children benefit from systems that focus on continuous improvements to practice.

Children are supported by strong leadership at all levels of the kōhanga reo. The kōhanga reo manager and the head kaiako work together to oversee the daily programme and operations. Each kōhanga reo have a kaiako who leads programme development. The kaiako supports the professional development of the kaiāwhina as well as leading and monitoring ongoing programme improvement. She has a good understanding of administration systems and fosters collective and collaborative leadership amongst all kaimahi. Children are secure in their learning environment.

Children participate in a learning programme that meets their learning needs. Kaimahi effectively organise and manage the implementation of the learning programme. Planning, evaluation and assessment are comprehensive, detailed and used to inform improvements. Assessment information highlight children’s individual interests, their strengths and achievements. Children’s learning progress and achievements over time are recorded. Children are happy and engaged learners.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements. 

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hono ki Kōkiri will be in three years.
