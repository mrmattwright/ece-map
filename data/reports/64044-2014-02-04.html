2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Muturangi ki Moera, ki Te Whanganui-ā-Tara. Ka whai hua te kōhanga reo nā te roanga o te wā kua ū pūmau ngā kaimahi. E whakarato ana te kuia me te kaiako matua i te tino tūāpapa mō te whānau me ngā tamariki. Ka whakamanatia, ka manaakitia, ka māramahia hoki ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e mau pūmau ana i ngā uaratanga me ngā whakapono tuku iho. He mātātoa ngā tamariki, ā, ka ngākau nui ki te whai wāhi ki te hōtaka.

Ko te arotahi a te whānau, ko te reo Māori me Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Kua tuituia te reo Māori puta noa i Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira, ā, e kōkiri ana hoki i ngā akoranga i te kōhanga reo. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo Māori e kōrerohia ana puta noa i te rā. Ka whakahihiri ngā kaimahi i te ako reo kia pārekareka ai. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whai i ngā tohutohu me ngā kīanga.

Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki te pakari o ngā hononga mahi. Ka tūhura ngā tamariki i ngā huarahi auaha ki te aro ki ngā patapatai a ngā kaimahi. He kaha te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki. Ka patapatai, ka āwhina hoki i ētahi atu i roto i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, i ngā wā e tika ana. E noho mārama ana, e noho mōhio ana hoki ngā tamariki ki tō rātou ao māori. Ka kitea te whakaute, te manaaki hoki o ngā tamariki mō te taiao, mō rātou hoki e pā tata ana ki a rātou.

Ka whakarato te whakamahere kaupapa i te tino aronga mō ngā kaimahi me te whānau. Ka whakaaro huritao ngā kaimahi ki te whai pānga me te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga. E mārama ana ngā kaiako ki te pūtake o te aromatawai, ā, kua whakaemihia ngā tino mōhiohio e hāngai pū ana ki tēnā e mōhiotia ana e ngā tamariki. Whakapuakihia ai aua mōhiohio ki te whānau i ia te wā.

He nui te whai wāhi a te whānau ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He pakari te āheinga o te whānau ki te whakahaere i tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Ka pūrongotia tētahi tirohanga o te hōtaka akoranga ki ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā. Engari, kāhore anō kia āta tuhia te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki me ngā mahi ka whai ake.

Kua rua tekau ngā tau e whai wāhi ana te kuia ki tēnei kōhanga reo. Kua 18 ngā tau te roanga o te wā e mahi ana te kaiako matua. Ko te arotake i ā rāua kawenga mahi ka hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga me te tauatanga o ētahi atu ki ngā wāhanga matua o ngā whakaritenga i te kōhanga reo. Ka whai hua ngā mema o te whānau nā tō rāua mōhiotanga, tō rāua mātanga hoki.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Muturangi kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Muturangi.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

E whakapono ana te whānau, ko te reo Māori e whai pānga matua ana ki Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira, ā, e kōkiri ana hoki i ngā akoranga katoa i te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te reo Māori.Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo Māori e kōrerohia ana, puta noa i te rā. Ka whakahihiri, ka whakaongaonga ngā kaimahi i te ako reo. Ka whakamahia ngā rauemi hei whakatairanga, hei whakawhānui hoki i te whakamahinga o te reo Māori. Nā te pāhekoheko o ngā kēmu, ngā waiata, me ngā taumahi, ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whai i ngā tohutohu me ngā kīanga.

Te hāpai i te ahurea.Ka noho whakaute, ka manaakitia, ka māramahia hoki ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whakapūmau ana i ngā uaratanga me ngā whakapono tuku iho. He mātātoa, he ngākau nui hoki ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu. Ka manaaki ngā tamariki, tētahi i tētahi. Ka kitea tō rātou whakaute mō ngā kaimahi, mō ō rātou hoa hoki. He harikoa rātou, ā, ka whai wāhi nui ki ā rātou akoranga, ā rātou tākaro anō hoki.

Ngā hononga.Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki ngā tino hononga pakari. He pai te taunekeneke a ngā tamariki ki waenga i a rātou anō, ā, ka whai wāhi ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero e whai pūtake ana. Ka whakapuaki ngā tamariki i ō rātou whakaaro ki ngā kaimahi. Ka tūhura ngā tamariki i ngā huarahi auaha ki te whakatutuki i ngā patapatai a ngā kaimahi.

Te aronga toi whenuatanga.He tino aronga toi whenuatanga tō ngā tamariki. Ka ako rātou i ngā hononga ā-whānau, ā-hapū, ā-iwi hoki mā ngā pepeha, ngā whakapapa, me ngā waiata. He pārekareka ki a rātou ngā whakawhitinga kōrero e whakamana ana i tō rātou tuakiri, tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga anō hoki. Ka patapatai rātou, ā, ka hāpai hoki i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero a ētahi atu i ngā wā e tika ana. Whakamanahia ai e ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki, mā ngā huarahi e tino whai hua ana.

Mana Aotūroa.Kei te noho mārama, kei te noho mōhio hoki ngā tamariki ki tō rātou ao māori. Ka noho mōhio rātou ki a rātou anō, ki ētahi atu hoki ki tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka taunekeneke rātou ki ngā taonga tuku iho o te ao māori, o te ao kikokiko hoki. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i te tikanga e whai pānga ana ki te taiao māori, kikokiko hoki. Ka kitea te noho whakaute, te whakaaro nui hoki o ngā tamariki mō tō rātou taiao, mō ngā tāngata e noho pā tata ana hoki ki a rātou.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa, ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai ki ngā kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki hoki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakamahere hōtaka.Ka whakarato te whakamahere hōtaka i te tino aronga mārama mō ngā kaimahi me te whānau. Ka aro ngā mahere ki ngā wawata o te whānau mō ā rātou tamariki. Kua tāutuhia ngā whāinga ako, ā, ka whakamahia hei whakahāngai i ngā taumahi ako. Ka whakaaro huritao ngā kaimahi ki te aronga me te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga.

Te aromatawai.Ka noho mārama ngā kaiako ki te pūtake o te aromatawai, ā, kua whakaemihia ngā tino mōhiohio e pā ana ki ngā āheinga o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki ki te whakaemi i ngā mōhiohio, tae atu ki ngā pūkete, ngā tauira mahi a ngā tamariki, ngā kōrerorero a ngā mātua, ngā tuhinga i ngā wātaka, me ngā pūrongo aromatawai. Whakapuakihia ai ngā mōhiohio ki te whānau, i ia te wā. Ka whai wā anō hoki ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero mō ngā matea o ngā tamariki, tae atu ki ngā taumata ako ka whāia tonuhia e rātou.

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau.He nui te whai wāhitanga a te whānau ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He rawaka te ako ngaio e whakaratohia ana, e hāpaitia ana hoki e Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo. Ka hāngai nui ngā mahere rautaki mō te ahunga whakamua, ki te reo Māori. Ka whakatutukihia te arotake whaiaro ki ngā hui ā-whānau, ā, he wā anō tēnei ki te arotake me te whakaaro huritao i ia te wā, ki te mahere rautaki me ngā kaupapa matua. Ka whakaoti te arohaehae o ngā kaimahi katoa. Ka aroturukitia ngā take pūtea e Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo. He pakari te whānau i roto i tō rātou āheinga ki te whakahaere i tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Ngā arohaehae.Ahakoa ka whakaoti ngā arohaehae kaimahi, me whai whakaaro hoki te whānau ki ngā mahi whakaaro huritao a ngā kaimahi, me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga ka whai ake. Ka whakapakari tēnei i ngā hononga ki te whakawhanaketanga ngaio me ngā matea whakangungu a ngā kaimahi.

Te whakariterite i te tauatanga.Kua roa te kuia me te kaiako matua i whai wāhi ai ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, ā, he tino puna mātauranga tō rāua hei whakapuaki. Ko te arotake i ngā kawenga mahi ka hāpai i ētahi atu o ngā kaimahi ki te whakapakari ake i ō rātou ake āheinga, puta noa i ngā wāhanga matua katoa o te kōhanga reo.

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Muturangi , tētahiTauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhangame tētahiRārangi Tātari Whaiaro.   • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga

  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Muturangi is located in Moera, Wellington. The kōhanga reo benefits from the long term commitment demonstrated by kaimahi. The kuia and lead kaiako provide a sound foundation for whānau and children. Children are respected, cared for and understood in an environment with strong cultural values and beliefs. Children are active and willing participants in the programme.

The whānau focus is on te reo Māori and Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Te reo Māori is integrated across Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira and is the driving force for learning in kōhanga reo. Children hear te reo Māori being spoken throughout the day. Kaimahi make language learning exciting and interesting. Children are able to follow instructions and phrases.

Kaimahi and children have established sound working relationships. Children explore creative ways to respond to kaimahi enquiries. Children have a strong sense of belonging. They enquire and assist others in conversations as required. Kaimahi regularly affirm children’s sense of belonging in positive ways. Children understand and are aware of their natural world. Children show respect and care for their environment and those around them.

Kaupapa planning provides clear direction for kaimahi and whānau. Kaimahi reflect on the responsiveness and effectiveness of the learning programme. Kaiako understand the purpose of assessment and have compiled specific information about what children can do. The information is regularly shared with whānau.

There is strong whānau involvement and participation in the management of the kōhanga reo. Whānau are confident in their ability to manage their kōhanga reo.

An overview of the learning programme is reported regularly at whānau hui. However, the monitoring of children’s development and next steps are not clearly stated.

The kuia has 20 years experience in this kōhanga reo. The lead kaiako has 18 years experience. A review of their responsibilities will assist the development and succession planning of others into key areas of kōhanga reo arrangements. Whānau members benefit from their knowledge and experience.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Muturangi was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Te Kōhanga Reo o Muturangi.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning; and
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

4 Findings

Nga Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

The whānau believe that te reo Māori is an integral part of Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira and is the driving force for all learning in kōhanga reo.

Areas of good performance

Te reo Māori. Children hear te reo Māori being spoken throughout the day. Kaimahi make language learning exciting and interesting. Resources are used to promote and extend the use of te reo Māori. Interactive games, songs and activities engage children’s interest. Children are able to follow instructions and phrases.

Cultural support. Children are respected, cared for and understood in an environment with strong cultural values and beliefs. Children are active and willing participants. Children care for one another. They display respect for kaimahi and each other. They are happy and involved in their learning and play

Relationships.Kaimahi and children have established sound relationships. Children interact well with one another and engage in meaningful discussions. Children communicate their ideas with kaimahi. Children explore creative ways to respond to kaimahi enquiries.

Sense of belonging. Children have a strong sense of belonging. They learn about whānau, hapū, iwi connections through pepeha, whakapapa and waiata. They enjoy conversations that affirm their identity and belonging. They ask questions and assist others in conversations as needed. Kaimahi regularly affirm children’s sense of belonging in positive ways.

Mana Aoturoa.Children understand and are aware of their natural world. They are aware of themselves and others in their kōhanga reo. They interact with natural and physical resources. Children experience tikanga associated with the natural and physical environment. Children show respect and care for their environment and those around them.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Programme planning.Programme planning provides clear direction for kaimahi and whānau. Planning responds to the aspirations of the whānau for their children. Learning goals are identified and used to focus learning activities. Kaimahi reflect on the responsiveness and effectiveness of the learning programme.

Assessment. Kaiako understand the purpose of assessment and have compiled specific information about what children can do. They use a variety of techniques to collect information including profile books, samples of children’s work, parent commentary, diary notations and assessment reports. The information is regularly shared with whānau. Kaimahi take time to talk about children’s needs including their next steps for learning.

Whānau management.There is strong whānau involvement and participation in the management of the kōhanga reo. Professional learning is adequately provided for and supported by TKRNT. Strategic plans for future direction are based largely on te reo Māori. Self review occurs in whānau hui and includes regular reviews and reflections of the strategic plan and priorities. Appraisals for all staff are completed. Financial matters are monitored by TKRNT. Whānau are confident in their ability to manage their kōhanga reo.

Areas for development

Appraisals. While staff appraisals are completed the whānau need to consider staff self reflection practices and their next steps for development. This will strengthen the links to professional development and the training needs of staff.

Succession planning.The kuia and lead kaiako have many years experience in kōhanga reo and have a wealth of knowledge to share. A review of responsibilities will help other staff to build their capability across key areas of the kōhanga reo.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Te Kōhanga Reo o Muturangi Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff ofcompleted an EROKōhanga Whānau Assurance StatementandSelf-Audit Checklist. In these documents they havetested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

6 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • link self reflection practices with staff professional development and training
  • develop succession planning to ensure the long term stability of the kōhanga.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

To the Parents and Community of Te Kōhanga Reo o Muturangi

Te Kōhanga Reo o MuturangiThese are the findings of the Education Review Office’s latest report on.

Ngā Mokohuia o te Rautahi Te Kōhanga Reo is located in Kawerau. The staff and whānau members of this kōhanga provide high quality education and care to the children who attend the kōhanga.

Children are provided with a high quality learning programme. They are provided with numerous opportunities to explore their kōhanga environment. Children are also provided with a wide range of good quality natural and manmade resources. They are very well supported to be creative, imaginative learners who are immersed in a high quality te reo Māori environment.

Te reo Māori is a strong feature of the learning programme. Children experience high quality learning experiences and warm, caring and nurturing relationships. They are confident and comfortable in their kōhanga environment.

This whānau is totally focussed on the revitalisation, retention and use of te reo Māori. Staff demonstrate that they are highly skilled at supporting children to learn and to use te reo Māori. The staff at Mokohuia are dedicated to making a positive difference in the children’s lives. They work diligently to support the children in their care in the best that they can. Children are developing as competent speakers of te reo Māori.

Children sing and comprehend complex and important traditional songs. They clearly enjoy singing and performing traditional waiata Māori including waiata tangi. Children know these songs well and perform them with confidence and enthusiasm. The staff work deliberately to match appropriate hand and body actions to the meaning of the songs to help children to comprehend them. Children are developing a love for waiata koroua.

The staff and whānau are well informed about the progress and development of their children. Profile books have been created for all of the children at the kōhanga. Most of these books are very well presented and contain a good range of assessment information.

Staff and whānau understand and implement the self review process. They systematically review many of the key aspects of kōhanga operations. Most kōhanga operations run very smoothly because of the regular review that occurs. When issues are identified, they are dealt with quickly by the whānau and staff. This whānau is on a cycle of continual improvement.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to student achievement and useful to this kōhanga reo.

If you would like a copy of the full report, please contact the kōhanga reo or see the ERO web page, http://www.ero.govt.nz.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
