Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pūaha ki te marae o Ōmaeroa, ki Te Pūaha o Waikato. Ko te tuakiri me te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki e whakapūmautia ana, e whakakoia ana hoki i ia rā. He pārekareka ki ngā kaimahi tūao te mahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki e atawhaitia ana e rātou. He harikoa ngā tamariki, ā, ka ngahau i a rātou e ako ana. Kua whakaae te whānau me te hapū, he take whakahaere a te whānau hei tirohanga wawe.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pūaha i roto i te kotahi tau.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pūaha ki te marae o Ōmaeroa, ki Te Pūaha o Waikato. I whakatā te kōhanga reo mō ngā marama 18, tae noa ki te marama o Hōngongoi, i te tau 2015. Nā ngā kaumātua e tātai whakapapa ana ki te marae me te hapū i tono kia tūwhera anō te kōhanga reo. Tokorua ngā kaimahi tūao e mahi ana ki te kōhanga reo, ā, he whaea hou, he whaea rangatahi hoki rāua, ā, he iti noa ā rāua mahi whakangungu, whakawhanaketanga ngaio hoki mō tēnei tūmomo mahi. Tokowhitu ngā tamariki e atawhaitia ana e rāua. I tēnei wā e whakahaere ana te komiti o te marae i te kōhanga reo. E mōhio ana te whānau, e tika ana kia whiwhi ngā kaimahi i ngā whakangungu ka whakapai ake i ā rāua whakaritenga, ā, me whakapiki ake anō hoki i ngā tatauranga tamariki, kia pūmau ai te tū o te kōhanga reo hei te wā kei te heke mai.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e ako ana i a rātou anō, i tō rātou tūrangawaewae hoki?

I ia rā, he whai wāhitanga e whakapūmautia ai te tuakiri me te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E ako ana ngā tamariki i tō rātou tuakiri. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki ki ngā kaupapa i te marae. Ka whakapuaki ngā tuākana i ō rātou pepehā ki te taha o ō rātou hoa me ō rātou tēina. Ka tāruarua ngā kaimahi hei hāpai, hei ārahi hoki i te whakapuakitanga o ngā tēina i ō rātou pepehā ki ētahi atu. Ko ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo ki te tākaro ki te taha o ētahi atu tuakana, e hāpai ana i te whakawhānuitanga o tō rātou māramatanga ki te whanaungatanga. Ka whai pānga nui ngā kaumātua ki te kōhanga reo, mā te whakapuaki i ngā kōrero tuku iho o te hapori nei, ā, i te whakahirahiratanga o ngā poukai me te Kīngitanga. He tau, he pakari hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki i ō rātou pūkenga ki te kōrero mā te reo Māori. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou ake whakaaro mā te waiata, te mihimihi, me te taki i ō rātou pepeha. Ka mārama, ka whai hoki ngā tamariki i ngā tohutohu māmā i te reo Māori, ā, ka tāwhai hoki i ngā rerenga kōrero māmā. Ka rongo, ka ako hoki rātou i te reo me ngā tikanga o Tainui. Kei te pakari haere ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ka āta whakawhanaunga ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu. Ka poipoia, ka whakapūmautia hoki ngā tino hononga ki te whānau whānui. Ka ākina, ka tautokona hoki ngā tuākana, kia tākaro, kia manaaki hoki rātou i ō rātou tēina. Ko te ako i ngā hononga whakaute ka tuituia anō hoki ki te hōtaka o ia rā. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā kaimahi ki ngā tākaro a ngā tamariki. Ka kawe ngātahi ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki i ngā whakaritenga, puta noa i ngā mahinga o ia rā. He mauritau te whakawhanaunga a ngā tamariki ki ētahi atu.

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao māoriori motuhake. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te haere ki ngā wāhi whakahirahira i te hapori. Ka tae atu, ka whai wāhi atu hoki ngā tamariki ki ngā kaupapa i ngā marae me te hapū. Ka tautoko te whānau i ngā kaupapa ā-hapori o te hōtaka akoranga. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki, he tino whanaunga rātou. He pakari, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmomo taiao akoranga.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E tīmata ana te reo Māori o ngā tamariki ki te puāwai. Ahakoa ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori e mōhio ana rāua, ko te whakangungu ake tonu i te reo Māori ka whakapiki ake i tō rāua mōhiotanga, tō rāua āheinga hoki ki te whakatairanga i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Kāhore ngā pūmanawa, ngā ngākau nuitanga, me ngā matea o ia tamaiti e āta whakaatuhia ana ki te hōtaka akoranga. He āhua hou tonu ngā kaimahi ki ō rāua tūranga. He iti noa ō rāua mōhiotanga, ō rāua māramatanga hoki ki ngā tikanga o Te Whāriki, ki te huarahi rānei e tika ana ki te āta whakamahere me te āta whakapuaki i te kounga o ngā hōtaka akoranga mā ngā tamariki. He tohu Whakapakari tā te kaiako o mua, ā, kua toro atu ki te tautoko i ngā mahi ki te whakamahere i te hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai. E ako tonu ana ngā kaimahi i ō rāua tūranga me ā rāua kawenga mahi.

Me aro atu ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. He ōpaki ngā hui a te whānau me ngā kaimahi, ā, kāhore e tū auau ana. I tēnei wā, kāhore he mahere rautaki hei whakarato i ngā whakangungu ki ngā kaimahi kia pakari ake ai ō rāua pūkenga ngaio me ō rāua mōhiotanga. I tēnei wā, kāhore te tupu me te ahu whakamua o ngā tamariki i roto i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo e aroturukitia ana.

Kāhore anō te tūtohinga o te kōhanga reo me ngā kaupapa here kia arotakengia, kia whakahoungia hoki. Kāhore he tuhinga mōhiohio whaiaro, tae atu ki ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi, ngā kirimana tuku mahi, me ngā arohaehae. Kei te pā tōraro tēnei ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me tō rātou hauora, tō rātou waiora hoki.

He nui ngā rauemi ki te kōhanga reo, ā, he mā te āhua o te taiao. Kua whakapau kaha te whānau hei āta whakarite i te haumaru me te mā o ngā wāhi o roto, o waho hoki mō ngā tamariki. E mōhio ana te whānau, me whakapiki wawe i te tatauranga tamariki hei whakapūmau i te tū o te kōhanga reo mō meāke nei. He harikoa ngā tamariki ki te taetae mai ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Ka whakapau kaha ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te hāpai i ngā tamariki e atawhaitia ana e rātou. Ko tō rātou āheinga ki te āta whakahaere i te kōhanga reo e whakakōroiroitia ana nā tō rātou kore māramatanga ki Te Whāriki me Te Korowai. Ko te whakangungu i te whānau me ngā kaimahi ka hāpai ake i a rātou ki te āta whakatutuki ake i ngā ako, te haumaru, me te waiora o ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. Me tahuri ki te:

3.1 whakarite i te whakamahere, te whakatinana me te aromātai a ngā kaimahi i tētahi marautanga e whai pānga ana ki Te Kōhanga Reo, ā, i waihangatia ai hei whakatairanga ake i te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga mā te whakarato i ngā wheako akoranga ka aro nui ki ngā ngākau nuitanga ako, ngā pūmanawa, me ngā āheinga o ngā tamariki, ā, e whakarato ana hoki i tētahi taiao ako pai mō aua tamariki
[R43 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; C1-13 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

3.2 whakarite i te āta kāwana, te āta whakahaere i te kōhanga reo, i runga i ngā whakaritenga papai o te whakahaeretanga, tae atu ki Te Korowai
[R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA 1-12 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

3.3 whakarite i te arowhai o ngā taputapu, ngā rawa me ngā rauhanga, mō ngā āhuatanga e noho mōrearea ana pea ki ngā tamariki
[R 46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS 12 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

3.4 whakarite i te whakaatu o ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te tīni (te whakakore rānei) o ngā kope ki te taha o te wāhi tīni kope, ā, me whakatutuki i aua tukanga i ia wā.
[R 46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS3 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • toro atu te whānau ki te tautoko o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te whakarato i ngā mahi whakangungu e tika ana mā ngā kaimahi
  • whakatū i tētahi kaiako e mau ana i ngā tohu e tika ana, ā, hei ārahi i ngā hōtaka o Te Whāriki
  • tautokona te whānau kia whakatutuki ngā take i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo, ā, kia whakawhanake hoki i tētahi mahere mahi ka aroturuki i te ahu whakamua.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pūaha ki te marae o Ōmaeroa, ki Te Pūaha o Waikato. Ko te tuakiri me te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki e whakapūmautia ana, e whakakoia ana hoki i ia rā. He pārekareka ki ngā kaimahi tūao te mahi ki te taha o ngā tamariki e atawhaitia ana e rātou. He harikoa ngā tamariki, ā, ka ngahau i a rātou e ako ana. Kua whakaae te whānau me te hapū, he take whakahaere a te whānau hei tirohanga wawe.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pūaha is based on Ōmaeroa Marae in Port Waikato. Children’s identity and sense of belonging is reinforced and affirmed on a daily basis to have their. Voluntary kaimahi enjoy working alongside children in their care. Children are happy and have fun while they learn. Whānau and hapū have agreed that there are some urgent whānau management issues that need to be addressed.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pūaha will be within one year.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pūaha is based on Ōmaeroa Marae in Port Waikato. The kōhanga reo was in recess for 18 months until July 2015. Kaumātua who have strong links to the marae and hapū requested that the kōhanga reo be reopened. The kōhanga reo is staffed by two voluntary kaimahi who are new, young mothers with limited training and experience. They have seven children in their care. The marae committee are currently managing the kōhanga reo. The whānau recognises the need to provide appropriate training to improve kaimahi practices, and to increase roll numbers to sustain the kōhanga reo into the future.

2 The Review Findings

To what extent do children learn about who they are and where they come from?

Children have opportunities on a daily basis to have their identity and sense of belonging reinforced.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are learning about their identity. Kaimahi and children actively participate in marae activities. Tuakana share their pepehā with their peers and teina. Kaimahi use repetition to support and guide teina to share their pepehā with others. Opportunities for kōhanga reo children to interact with tuakana helps to extend their understanding of whanaungatanga. Kaumātua play an integral part in the kōhanga reo by sharing stories about the local area, the importance of poukai and Kingitanga. Children are settled and confident at kōhanga reo.

Children are developing their skills to communicate in te reo Māori. Kaimahi provide opportunities for children to express themselves through singing, mihimihi and reciting their pepehā. Children understand and follow simple instructions in te reo Māori and can mimic simple sentences. They are exposed to, and are learning Tainui reo and tikanga. Children are developing confidence in te reo Māori.

Children build positive relationships with others. Strong relationships with the extended whānau are fostered and maintained. Tuakana are encouraged and supported to play and care for their teina. Learning about respectful relationships is integrated in the daily programme. Kaimahi actively participate in children’s play. Kaimahi and children share responsibilities during their daily routines. Children engage positively with others.

Children are familiar with their unique natural environment. Kaimahi provide regular learning opportunities for children to visit places of historical importance to the community. Children also travel to, and participate in marae events and hapū activities. Whānau support the localised content of the learning programme. Children know that they are all closely related. Children are secure and comfortable in different learning environments.

Key Next Steps

Children’s reo Māori is beginning to emerge. While kaimahi use reo Māori, they are aware that further reo training will increase their knowledge and capability to enhance children’s language development.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s individual strengths, interests and needs are not reflected in the learning programme. The kaimahi are relatively new to their roles. They have limited knowledge and understanding about the requirements of Te Whāriki or how to effectively plan and deliver quality programmes of learning for children. The previous kaiako, who has a Whakapakari certification, has offered to provide support with programme planning, assessment and evaluation. Kaimahi are still learning about their roles and responsibilities.

The management of the kōhanga reo requires attention. Whānau and kaimahi hui are informal and irregular. There is currently no strategic plan in place to provide training for kaimahi to advance their professional skills and knowledge. Children’s growth and progress in the kaupapa of the kōhanga reo is currently not being monitored.

The kōhanga reo charter and policies have not been reviewed and updated. Personnel information including job descriptions, employment contracts and appraisals is non-existent. Children’s learning, health and wellbeing are being compromised.

The kōhanga reo is well resourced and the environment is clean and tidy. The whānau have worked hard to ensure that the indoor and outdoor environment is safe and hygienic for the children. The whānau acknowledge that there is an urgent need to increase roll numbers to sustain the kōhanga into the future. Children are happy to come to kōhanga reo.

Key Next Steps

Kaimahi and whānau do their best to support children in their care. Their ability to manage the kōhanga reo effectively is hampered by their lack of understanding of Te Whāriki and Te Korowai. Whānau and kaimahi training would better prepare them to better cater to children’s learning, safety and wellbeing.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

3.1 ensure that kaimahi plan, implement, and evaluate Te Kōhanga Reo prescribed curriculum that is designed to enhance children’s learning and development through the provision of learning experiences that responds to the learning interests, strengths, and capabilities of enrolled children; and provides a positive learning environment for those children
[Regulations 43 (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; C1-13 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

3.2 ensure that the kohanga reo is effectively governed and is managed in accordance with good management practices, including Te Korowai
[Regulations 47 (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA 1-12 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

3.3 ensure that equipment, premises, and facilities are regularly checked for hazards to children
[Regulations 46 (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS12 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

3.4 ensure that a procedure for the changing (and disposal, if appropriate) of nappies is displayed near the nappy changing facilities and consistently implemented.
[Regulations 46 (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS3 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation(s that):

  • whānau seek the support of TKRNT to provide appropriate training for kaimahi

  • appoint a suitably qualified kaiako to lead Te Whāriki programmes

  • whānau are supported to address the concerns that are identified in the report and develop an action plan to monitor progress.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pūaha is based on Ōmaeroa Marae in Port Waikato. Children’s identity and sense of belonging is reinforced and affirmed on a daily basis to have their. Voluntary kaimahi enjoy working alongside children in their care. Children are happy and have fun while they learn. Whānau and hapū have agreed that there are some urgent whānau management issues that need to be addressed.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Pūaha will be within one year.
