1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Tīmatatanga ki Manurewa. Nō te marama o Mahuru, i te tau 1988 i whakatuwheratia ai te kōhanga reo, ā, i tino aro tō rātou ingoa ki te motuhaketanga o te tīmatatanga hou mō te whānau e noho ana ki Manurewa. He mea hou te kaupapa o Te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā mātua, ā, he wā tuatahi hoki te whakamōhio atu te kaupapa ki a rātou. Ka hono hoki te ingoa o tō rātou kōhanga reo ki Te Orokohanga me Te Tīmatatanga o te Ao. Ka ārahi Te Orokohanga i te āhua o ngā mahi a te whānau hei painga mō ngā tamariki e noho ana ki Manurewa.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou māramatanga ki te whanaungatanga?

Ka āta whakaatu ngā tamariki i tō rātou māramatanga ki te whanaungatanga mā ā rātou taunekeneke me ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga. He hihiri ngā tamariki, ka kawe atu i ngā wero, ā, ka ngākau nui ki ngā hīraurau hopanga. Kua waihangatia te hōtaka hei hāpai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga me ā rātou ake whiringa. Ka poipoi ngā taumahi i te katoa o ngā tūmomo rōpū reanga me ō rātou ngākau nuitanga. Ka kōkiri ngā kaimahi i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ako ki te taha o ngā tamariki, ka akiaki, ka ārahi hoki rātou, ā, ka mātakitaki rātou i ā rātou e tākaro ana. Kua waia ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao, ā, he ākonga harikoa, he ākonga māia hoki rātou.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga o tētahi taiao reo Māori. Ka noho te reo Māori hei kaupapa matua ki te whānau. Ka tautoko ngā kaimahi i te ārahi a ngā tamariki i ngā mahinga me ngā taumahi. Ka whakamahia ngā karakia, ngā waiata, me ngā mihimihi hei whakaaweawe, hei akiaki hoki i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero mā te reo Māori ki te taha o ngā kaimahi me ō rātou hoa. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te whakawhiti kōrero mā te reo Māori.

He tau ngā tamariki, ā, he pakari hoki tō rātou whakahoahoa. He rarata, ā, ka tiaki tētahi i tētahi. He mahira, he pākiki hoki ngā tamariki, ā, he pai ki a rātou te whakapuaki kōrero mō tēnā e ngākau nuitia ana, e whakaarohia ana hoki e rātou. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā taunekeneke papai, ā, ka hāpai hoki rātou i ngā tamariki ki te tūhonohono mā ngā hononga whakawhirinaki. Ko te kaha o te whanaungatanga e poipoi ana i te ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki ki te ako. He harikoa, he tau hoki ngā tamariki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka arotahi ngā kaimahi ki te katoa o te tamaiti, ā, he pai te whai huatanga o tā rātou whakamahi i te angamahi hei whakamahere i te hōtaka. Ka hāpai, ka ārahi hoki ngā mahere i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He whai wāhitanga mā te whānau ki te whakamahere ki te taha o ngā kaimahi, me te mahi auaha. He matawhānui te aromātai hōtaka, ā, ka puta ake ngā whakapaitanga ki ngā whakaritenga. Kei te tino mōhio ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki, ā, e āhei ana rātou ki te āta whakamahere mō ā rātou akoranga. Kua āta tuhia ngā whakapaitanga me te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki. Ka pūrongo ngā pūkete i te ahu whakamua o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā tūmomo taumahi e whakaratohia ana mā rātou.

He mea nui te waiora o ngā pēpi. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā pēpi, mā te noho riterite o ngā mahinga. Ka whakamahere rātou mō ngā akoranga, ngā wā tākaro, me te tūhura haere hoki o ngā pēpi. He whakaongaonga, he ātaahua hoki te taiao, ā, ka hāngai pū ki te ako. He whānui ngā tūmomo rauemi, ā, kua āta whakaritea. Ka whai hua ngā pēpi nā tētahi hōtaka akoranga e aro nui ana ki ā rātou akoranga. He harikoa ngā pēpi, ā, e tino atawhaitia ana hoki.

Ko te ārahi ngātahi me te whai huatanga hoki o ngā pūkenga whakahaere me ngā whakaritenga whakahaere a tēnā me tēnā, ka whai pānga ki ngā tino putanga mō ngā tamariki. E ākina ana ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou pūkenga, tō rātou mātanga hoki hei hāpai, hei whakapūmau hoki i te kounga kairangi o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka whai wāhi te katoa ki ngā whakataunga, te whakamahere, me te aroturuki. He pai te tukanga whakahaere kaimahi tā te whānau, tae atu ki tētahi pūnaha arohaehae e whai hua ana, ā, e whakatutuki ana hoki i ō rātou matea. Kei te whai wāhi atu te nuinga o ngā kaimahi – kua oti kē rānei i a rātou – ngā hōtaka whakangungu a Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo. He whakawhanaketanga ngaio tā ngā kaimahi, ā, ka haere tonu tēnei i ngā wā katoa. E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki hei ākonga.

He pakari ngā whakaritenga aromātai o roto. He mārama ngā whāinga rautaki ā te whānau mō te ako me te waiora o ngā tamariki. Pūrongo ai te kaiwhakahaere i ia te wā, mō te tiaki me te whakapai haere i ngā rawa. Ka whakarato te kaiako i tētahi pūrongo matawhānui e pā ana ki te hōtaka a ngā tamariki mō ia rā. He mārama te aroturuki a te whānau i ngā pūtea. Ko te tū auau o ngā pūrongo e whai pānga ana ki ngā whakapaitanga.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore anō te whānau kia whakawhanake i tētahi mahere ā-tau e ārahi ana i te whakatinanatanga me te aroturukitanga o ā rātou whāinga, i te tāutu rānei o ngā kaupapa matua mō meāke nei. Ka whakawhiti kōrero rātou i ia te wā mō ā rātou whāinga rautaki, ā rātou mahi rautaki hoki, engari kāhore he mahere ki te whakatutuki nahanaha i aua whakaritenga.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā:

  • kia whakawhanake, kia aroturuki hoki te whānau i tā rātou mahere ā-tau.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Tīmatatanga ki Manurewa. Ka kitea te noho mārama o ngā tamariki ki te whanaungatanga, nā ā rātou taunekeneke, ō rātou hononga hoki ki ētahi atu. Ka ārahi ngātahi, ka whai hua hoki ngā pūkenga me ngā whakaritenga whakahaere o ia tāngata, ā, ka whai pānga ēnei ki ngā tino putanga mō ngā tamariki. E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki i roto i ngā akoranga, ā, ka whai wāhi nui.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Tīmatatanga i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

Lynda Pura-Watson

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Tīmatatanga is in Manurewa. The Kōhanga Reo opened in September 1988 and their name recognised the importance of new beginnings for whānau living in Manurewa. Te Kōhanga Reo was a new kaupapa and this was the first time parents had been introduced to the kaupapa. The name of the kōhanga reo also links to Te Orokohanga me Te Tīmatatanga o te ao. Te Orokohanga, guides how whānau work for the benefit of children who live in Manurewa.

2 The Review Findings

How effectively do children demonstrate their understanding of whanaungatanga?

Children effectively show that they understand whanaungatanga through their interactions and relationships with others.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are engaged in learning. Children are energetic, accept challenges and enjoy problem solving. The programme is designed to support their individual interests and choices. Activities cater for all age groups and interests. Kaimahi initiate learning conversations with children, encourage, guide and supervise their play. Children are familiar with their environment and are happy confident learners.

Children are immersed in a rich te reo Māori environment. Te reo Māori is a priority for whānau. Kaimahi support children to lead routines and activities. Karakia, waiata, mihimihi are used to inspire and encourage children’s language development. Children enjoy conversations in te reo Māori with kaimahi and each other. Children are confident communicators in te reo Māori.

Children are settled and socially aware. They are friendly and take care of each other. Children are inquisitive, curious and willing to talk about what they like and think. Kaimahi encourage positive interactions and support children to build trusting relationships. The strong sense of whanaungatanga fosters children’s willingness to learn. Children are happy and settled.

Planning and Evaluation

Kaimahi focus on the whole child and use their programme planning framework effectively. Planning supports and guides children’s learning. Whānau have opportunities to plan with kaimahi and to be innovative. Programme evaluation is thorough and leads to improved practice. Kaimahi know the children well and are able to plan for their learning effectively. Children’s achievements and progress is well documented. Profiles report progress in children’s learning. Children enjoy the activities provided for them.

Babies’ wellbeing is important. Kaimahi support babies by ensuring routines are consistent. They plan for babies learning, play and exploration. The environment is stimulating, attractive and conducive to learning. Resources are varied and well organised. Babies benefit from a learning programme that is responsive to their learning. Babies are happy and well cared for.

Shared leadership and effective individual management skills and practices contribute to positive outcomes for children. Whānau are encouraged to share their skills and expertise to support and maintain high quality learning for children. All contribute to decision making, planning and monitoring. Whānau have an efficient personnel management process including an effective appraisal system that meets their needs. Most kaimahi are involved or have completed Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust training programmes. There is ongoing professional development for kaimahi. Children are well supported learners.

Internal evaluation practices are sound. Whānau have clear strategic goals for children’s learning and wellbeing. The kaiwhakahaere reports regularly on property maintenance and repairs. The kaiako provides a comprehensive report about the children’s daily programme. Whānau financial monitoring is transparent. Regular reporting leads to improvement.

Key Next Steps

Whānau have yet to develop an annual plan that guides the implementation and monitoring of their strategic goals or the identification of future priorities. They regularly discuss their strategic goals and actions but do not have a systematic plan to achieve these.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation that:

  • whānau develop and monitor their annual plan.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Tīmatatanga is in Manurewa. Children show their understanding of whanaungatanga through their interactions and relationships with others. Shared leadership and effective individual management skills and practices contribute to positive outcomes for children. Children are well supported and engaged in learning.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Tīmatatanga will be in three years.
