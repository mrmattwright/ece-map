1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā tētahi ō ngā Kaiwhakahaere Matua ā-Rohe o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei, kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga o Te Kōhanga Reo o Kapanga, e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia e te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i te Hōngongoi 2012, me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

  • te Mana Atua
  • te whakamāherehere me te aromātai
  • ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture
  • ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai whai muri i te arotake o mua.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Kapanga ki te tāone o Coromandel. Koia nei anake te kōhanga reo ki tēnei takiwā. I tāutu te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te maha o ngā take hei whakatutuki i ngā wāhanga o te whakaako, te ako, me te whakatinana i ngā kaupapa here. Ka ngākau nui katoa te whānau ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. Ka whai pānga nui te kaiwhakahaere ki te whakapūmautanga o ngā whakahaeretanga ki te kōhanga reo.

Ko ngā take i tāutuhia ai ki te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2012, i hāngai ai ki te whakaritenga me te whakahaeretanga o te hōtaka akoranga. I tuhia hoki, kāhore i te tika ngā rautaki whakaako i whakamahia ai hei kōkiri i ngā akoranga. Mai i taua wā, tokorua ngā kaiako hou i whakatūngia ai e te whānau. He nui ngā whakarerekētanga papai i whakatutukihia ai e rāua, i roto i te whakaritenga me te whakatinanatanga o te hōtaka.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki tētahi hōtaka e whakawātea ake ana i a rātou ki te mahi ki te taha o ētahi atu, me te whai hoki i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. I nāianei, me whai whakaaro te whānau ki te whakamahere me te whakatinana i tētahi hōtaka e whakatutuki ana i ngā matea o ngā pēpi.

Kua whakaritea e te whānau ngā tukanga ki te whakahaere i ngā whakaritenga o tō rātou kōhanga reo. I mua, he āwangawanga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga mō te whakatinanatanga o ngā kaupapa here. I nāianei, he tuhinga ā te whānau i whakahāngaitia ai ki ngā tikanga kaupapa here. Kua whakatutukihia hoki ngā take i hāngai ai ki te whakamahere me te aromatawai.

He take tonu te aromātai hōtaka me ngā arowhai i ia te rā mō ngā āhuatanga mōrearea. Kua tae ki te wā, ki te whai whakaaro ki ngā tukanga ka aroturuki i te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga, me tēnei tino āhuatanga e whai pānga ana ki te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Atua

He whakamārama

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kia tautoko ake te whānau i te kaiako ki te whakahaere me te whakarite i te hōtaka akoranga. He mea nui anō hoki kia whai pānga ngā akoranga e kōkirihia ana e te kaiako, mō ngā tamariki nohinohi.

Ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te whānuitanga o te hōtaka akoranga ki te whakatutuki i aua take i whakatakotohia ai ki te pūrongo o mua.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Te hōtaka akoranga.Mai i te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2012, kua pā ētahi whakapaitanga ki te hōtaka akoranga. Kua whakatūngia e te whānau ngā kaiako hou e rua, ā, e āta ahu whakamua ana rāua ki te whakarato i tētahi hōtaka e tika ana mō ngā tamariki katoa.

I nāianei, he pai te whakahaeretanga, te whakaritenga hoki o te hōtaka akoranga. Kua waia ngā tamariki ki ngā mahinga o te rā. Ka tino whakamahi ngā kaiako i ngā rauemi e wātea ana ki a rāua, ahakoa he iti tonu ngā rauemi mō ētahi kaupapa. Ka whai wā ngā tamariki ki te āta tākaro i ētahi wā, ā, ka ngana hoki rātou ki ngā akoranga ka whai pūtake ki a rātou.

E wheako ana ngā tamariki i te whānuitanga o tētahi hōtaka i nāianei. He take tonu te kaha kōkiri o ngā pākeke. Engari, kua iti ake tēnei. He tākare ngā kaiako ki te whakapai i ā rāua whakaritenga mā te whai wāhi atu ki te Whakapakari. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki tētahi hōtaka e whakawātea ana i a rātou ki te whiriwhiri me te kōkiri i ā rātou ake akoranga.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te whakamahere me te whakatinana i ngā hōtaka mā ngā pēpi.Me tahuri tonu te whānau ki te whakamahere me te whakatinana i tētahi hōtaka akoranga e hāngai mārika ana ki ngā matea o ngā pēpi. Kua whakaurua ngā pēpi ki te hōtaka mā te katoa, ā, i ētahi wā kei te makihoi noa rātou. Kua tae ki te wā, kia whai whakaaro te whānau ki ngā huarahi ki te whakariterite i tētahi hōtaka e whakatutuki mārika ana i ngā matea ā-hinengaro, ā-tinana hoki o ngā pēpi.

Te whakamāherehere me te aromātai

He whakamārama

I whakapuakihia ki te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, ngā āwangawanga i hāngai ai ki te whakatinanatanga o ngā kaupapa here. He maha ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake. I hāngai ēnei ki ngā herenga ā-ture hei whakatutuki ki te kōhanga reo. Ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te hauora me te haumaru.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Ngā akoako hōneatanga.Whakatūngia e ngā kaimahi ngā akoako hōneatanga i ia te wā. Kua tino waia ngā tamariki ki tēnei mahi. He mahere mahi tā ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhānui i ngā akoako ohorere ki ētahi atu tūmomo maiki, pērā i te rū whenua, me te tai āniwhaniwha. Nā te tū o te kōhanga reo ki runga rawa i tētahi maunga, kāhore te tai āniwhaniwha e tino whai pānga ana hei āhuatanga mōrearea, pērā i te rū whenua.

Te arowhai i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea.He pūnaha tā te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā arowhai mōrearea i ia toru marama. Ka hono aua arowhai ki te aroturuki tonutanga o te tiaki o ngā whare. Pūrongotia ai ki ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā.

Te rehita mō ngā aituā me ngā maiki.He āwangawanga anō hoki, nā te mea, kāhore he rehita mō ngā aituā me ngā maiki. Kua whakatinanahia tēnei i nāianei, ā, he mārama ngā mōhiohio mā te whānau, mō ngā tūmomo whara ka pā pea ki ngā tamariki.

Ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki te tīni kope.Kua whakaritea ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki te tīni kope. Ka āta tuhia e ngā kaimahi kia hāngai ēnei ki te wā. He ngāwari hoki mō te whānau ki te whakapā atu ki aua tuhinga.

Te arohaehae kaimahi.Kua whakaritea te tukanga arohaehae kaimahi. Nō te mea he hou ngā kaiako tokorua, ka whakatutukihia ā rāua arohaehae ki waenganui tonu i te tau.

Te whakamahere me te aroturuki i te hōtaka.Kua whakaritea ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakamahere hōtaka me te aroturuki hoki i te ahunga whakamua a ngā tamariki. He tino matawhānui ngā mahere hōtaka, ā, he aratohu anō hoki ki roto mō te whakatinanatanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Ka āta tāutu ngā aromatawai o ngā tamariki i ngā tūmomo āhuatanga e akohia ana e rātou, ā, i roto i te roanga o te wā, ka aroturuki hoki ēnei i tā rātou ahunga whakamua.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te arowhai i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea i ia rā.Me tāutu te whānau i te pūtake ki te arowhai i ngā āhuatanga mōrearea i ia rā, me te rerekētanga o ēnei ki ngā arowhai o ngā whare i ia toru marama. He mea nui kia whai whakaaro te whānau ki tētahi tukanga kia tutukihia ai ngā arowhai mōrearea o ia rā. Ka āta whakarite tēnei i te noho hauora, noho haumaru hoki o te kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki.

Te aromātai hōtaka.Me āta tirohia te aromātai hōtaka. Ka hui ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhiti kōrero mō ngā mahi i roto i te roanga o ngā wiki o mua, engari kāhore he tuhinga e pā ana ki aua kōrerorero. E tika ana, kia tuhia aua kōrero, hei āta whakatakoto haere i ngā tūmomo whakapaitanga.

Ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture

He whakamārama

I mua, i te āwangawanga te Tari Arotake Mātauranga mō ngā tukanga kaupapa here hei whakapai ake, mō ngā take hauora, take haumaru hoki ki te kōhanga reo. Mai i taua wā, kua whakapau kaha te whānau ki te whakatutuki i aua wāhanga.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Mai i te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2012:

  • e whakatutukihia ana ngā akoako hōneatanga i ia te wā
  • kua whakatinanahia tētahi pūnaha ki te whakakore i ngā tūmomo āhuatanga mōrearea i roto i ngā marama e toru
  • kua whakaritea e te whānau tētahi tukanga ki te whakatakoto haere i ngā aituā me maiki ka pā pea ki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo
  • kua whakawhanakehia tētahi pūnaha arohaehae kaimahi.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te rārangi arowhai mō ngā āhuatanga mōrearea o ia rā.He mea nui kia whakatinana te whānau i tētahi rārangi arowhai mō ngā āhuatanga mōrearea o ia rā. Me arowhai i ia rā, i mua i te taenga mai o ngā tamariki. Mā tēnei, ka tū te kōhanga reo hei wāhi haumaru mō ngā tamariki, i a rātou e ako ana.

Kia tutuki ai i te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā kawenga e pā ana ki ngā hanganga ture, me tahuri rātou ki te:

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

Te Kōhanga Reo o KapangaThis supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance ofgoverning body and management in relation to areas identified in the June 2012ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • Mana Atua
  • planning and evaluation
  • areas of non-compliance
  • any issues identified since the previous review.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Kapanga is located in the township of Coromandel. This is the only kōhanga reo in the area. The previous Education Review Office (ERO) report identified a number of issues that needed to be addressed in the areas of teaching and learning and policy implementation. The whānau is totally committed to the kaupapa of the kōhanga reo. The kaiwhakahaere is key to maintaining kōhanga reo operations.

The concerns raised in the previous ERO 2012 report were related to the organisation and management of the learning programme. Also noted were concerns that the teaching methods used to facilitate learning were inappropriate. Since then, the whānau has employed two new kaiako. They have brought many positive changes to the programme organisation and implementation.

Children enjoy a programme where they have more freedom to work alongside others as well as follow their own interests. Whānau must now consider planning and implementing a programme that meets the needs of the babies.

Whānau have established processes to manage the operations of their kōhanga reo. Previously ERO had concerns about policy implementation. Whānau now have documentation that aligns with policy requirements. Issues relating to planning and assessment have been resolved.

Programme evaluation remains an issue as do daily hazard checks. It is now time to consider processes to monitor the effectiveness of the learning programme and this particular aspect of health and safety for children.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Findings

Mana Atua

Background

The previous ERO review report identified the need for whānau to provide support to the kaiako with managing and organising the learning programme. It was also important that the kaiako facilitated learning that was appropriate for young children.

ERO will evaluate the extent to which the learning programme has responded to the issues raised in the previous report.

Areas of progress

The learning programme.Since the 2012 ERO report there have been improvements made to the learning programme. The whānau has employed two new kaiako who are working steadily toward providing a programme that is appropriate for all children.

The learning programme is now well managed and organised. Children are familiar with the routines of the day. Kaiako make good use of what resources are available even though resourcing remains scarce in some areas. Children have periods of sustained play where they are engaged in learning that is meaningful to them.

Children now experience a varied programme. Adult control remains an issue. However, this has been minimised. Kaiako are keen to improve their practice through participating in Whakapakari. Children enjoy a programme where they have opportunities to choose and initiate their own learning.

Areas for further improvement

Planning and implementing programmes for babies.There is still a need for the whānau to plan and implement a learning programme that best fits the needs of the babies. The babies are integrated in to the existing programme and at times wander aimlessly. It is now time for whānau to consider ways to organise a programme that caters specifically for the intellectual and physical needs of the babies.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

The previous ERO report expressed concerns about policy implementation. There were a number of areas that required attention. This was to ensure that key requirements were met to ensure that the kōhanga reo remained compliant. ERO will evaluate the extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau manages and monitors all areas of health and safety.

Areas of progress

Evacuation Drills.Kaimahi hold regular evacuation drills. Children are very familiar with this practice. Kaimahi have an action plan to extend emergency drills to other natural disasters such as earthquake and tsunami. Because the kōhanga reo is located high on a hill, tsunami is not recognised as a threat as with earthquakes.

Hazard Checks.The whānau has a system for carrying out three monthly hazard checks. These checks are linked to the ongoing monitoring of building maintenance. They are reported at whānau hui regularly.

Accident and incident register. There were concerns that there was no accident and incident register. This has now been implemented and provides clear information to whānau regarding any mishaps to children.

Nappy changing records.Nappy changing records are in place. Kaimahi ensure that records are kept up to date. Whānau are able to access these records readily.

Staff appraisal.Kaimahi appraisal has been established. Because both kaiako are new, their first lot of appraisals will be carried out in the middle of the year.

Programme planning and monitoring.Processes for programme planning and monitoring children’s progress are in place. Programme plans are very comprehensive and provide guidelines for the implementation of the learning programme. Children’s assessment clearly identifies what children are learning and overtime, monitor their progress.

Areas for further improvement

Daily hazard checks.Whānau need to identify the purpose of daily hazard checks as opposed to three monthly building checks. It is important that whānau consider a process where daily hazard checks are carried out. This should ensure that the kōhanga reo is a healthy and safe place for the children.

Programme evaluation.Programme evaluation requires attention. Kaimahi meet to discuss what has happened over the past weeks but there is no recorded documentation. It is important that discussions are recorded in order for any improvements to be clearly noted.

Areas of non-compliance

Background

Previously, ERO were concerned that policy procedures related to health and safety issues in the kōhanga reo required attention. Since then whānau have worked to ensure these areas were addressed.

Areas of progress

Since the 2012 ERO review:

  • regular evacuation drills are carried out
  • a system for eliminating potential hazards over a three month period has been implemented
  • whānau have established a process for recording accidents and incidents related to children at kōhanga reo
  • a kaimahi appraisal system has been developed.

Areas for further improvement

Daily hazardchecklist.It is important that whānau implement a daily hazard checklist. A check should be carried out every day before the children arrive. This should ensure that the kōhanga reo is a safe place for children to learn.

4 Actions Required for Compliance

In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements they need to:

  • establish a daily hazard check to be carried out every day before the children arrive.

[R24 (3) (ECE) Regulations 1998].

5 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau recommends:

  • the whānau works closely with the District Kaupapa to address the concerns outlined in this report.

6 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

General Information About Reviews

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.

Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.

Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
