Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ao Marama ki te tāone o Te Papaioea. He tino pai te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga, mā te whakawhanaungatanga. He atawhai, he manaaki, he mauritau hoki te āhua o ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi. Kei te wā ētahi wero, ā, me toro atu ki te tautoko o Te Pōari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ao Mārama i roto ngā tau e rua.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ao Mārama ki te tapa o te tāone o Te Papaioea, ā, kei taua wāhi tonu te nuinga o ngā tamariki me ngā whānau o te kōhanga reo e noho ana.

Ka noho pūmau te aronga a te whānau ki ngā whakawhanaketanga o te kōhanga reo mō meāke nei, hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te aroha me te manaaki ki tēnei kōhanga reo, ā, ka whakanuia te kanorau, me ngā āhuatanga motuhake ake.

I tāutu te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere, arā, ko te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki, te whakawhanaketanga i te reo Māori, te taiao ako, me ngā hononga ki waenga i te whānau. Ko te whakawhanaketanga o te whānau me te ārahitanga a ngā kaimahi ētahi atu wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere. Ko ētahi wāhanga hei whakawhanake, ko te whakamahere rautaki, te pūrongo, me te arotake whaiaro.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te ako a ngā tamariki, tō rātou whakawhanaketanga hoki mā te whakawhanaungatanga?

He tino pai te ako a ngā tamariki, tō rātou whakawhanaketanga anō hoki mā te whakawhanaungatanga.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e tino tautoko ana i a rātou ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. He tino tauira reo ngā kaimahi mō ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi rātou i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki e whai hua ana ki te hāpai i ngā tamariki. Ka kōrero ngā tamariki i te reo Māori me te reo Pākehā ki waenga i a rātou anō. Ka ako ngā tamariki i te reo Māori mā ngā huarahi e whai pūtake ana, ā, ka whakanuia wawetia ā rātou whakamātau i roto i te reo. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whakarongo me te whai wāhi atu ki ngā waiata Māori, ngā kanikani, me ngā mahi korikori. Ka taea e ētahi o ngā tamariki te taki ō rātou pepeha, ā rātou mihimihi hoki. E tīmata ana ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki ngā kaimahi, ā, ki ētahi o ngā mema hoki o te whānau i ia te wā.

Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te hōtaka akoranga i āta whakaritea ai. Ka ngana atu ngā tamariki, ā, he mātātoa tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki ngā taumahi ngahau e whakahihiri ana i a rātou. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te whiriwhiri i ā rātou ake taumahi. Ka whai wā, ka whai wāhi hoki ngā tamariki ki te whakaoti ngā taumahi i runga anō i tā rātou ake haere. Ka ākina rātou kia patapatai, kia whakariterite, me te tūhura haere i ngā tūmomo huarahi. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ngā mahinga me ngā tukanga o ia rātou, ā, ka whāia hoki e rātou. Ka tiaki ngā tamariki i ā rātou ake taputapu, ā, ka whakapai haere i ō rātou wāhi. He pai te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki hei ākonga tūmāia, hei ākonga pākiki hoki.

He atawhai, he manaaki, he mauritau hoki ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi. He māhorahora te pupū ake o te manaakitanga me te aroha ka kitea, ka whakaatuhia hoki e ngā tamariki. He pāroherohe tō rātou āhua ki te taha o ngā manuhiri. Ka whakamahi ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi i te reo katakata i te wā e tika ana, i a rātou e taunekeneke ana. Ka aro atu ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea o ia tamaiti. Ka kitea te pai o te noho o ngā tamariki hei tuakana, hei teina, ā, ka whakatinanahia e rātou te aroha me te tautoko o tētahi ki tētahi. Ka āta tautokona ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi hei whakatau i te tautohe. He mātātoa te tautoko o te whānau i ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi i roto i te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka whakaatuhia e ngā tamariki te koa me te ngākau nui ki te noho ki te kōhanga reo.

Kei te pai te kōtui haere o ngā pūkenga kori tinana o ngā tamariki, ā, he whai wāhitanga anō hoki mō rātou ki te whakapuaki ā-tinana i a rātou anō ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi. He rawaka ngā wāhi tākaro o waho. He pai te whakatutuki a ngā kaimahi i ngā matea ā-tinana, ā-whatumanawa hoki o ngā tamariki. He harikoa, he tūmāia, he mauritau hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore i te tino rerehua te taiao ako o ngā tamariki. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi me whakatika ake ngā pokapū taumahi, ā, me nui ake hoki i ngā tūmomo rauemi hei whakarato i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wero mā ngā tamariki. Me whakatutuki i aua whakarerekētanga, hei whakatairanga ake i te kounga o ngā wheako akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Me whakapaipai i te taiao, kia haumaru ai mō ngā tamariki. He maha rawa ngā mea ki te tomokanga, ngā papa, me te whare whakahaere tari, ā, he momo āhuatanga mōrearea tēnei mō ngā tamariki me te whānau. Mā te whakatutuki i aua take ka rerehua ake te āhua o te kōhanga reo, ā, ka tahuri ake ngā whānau hou ki te uru atu.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He mahere ā-tau māmā tā te whānau, hei ārahi i ā rātou kaupapa matua, tae atu ki te whakatutukitanga i te rōnaki tonutanga o te kōhanga reo.

Ka whakamahere ngātahi rātou i ngā hōtaka akoranga, ā, he aronga ki ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Nō nā tata tonu nei te whānau i whakatau i ngā whakaritenga matua e pā ana ki te whakarerekētanga o ngā tūnga kaimahi, ā, ka hāpai ake tēnei i te kounga o ngā putanga ako mā ngā tamariki. Kua tīmata ētahi mahi aromātai o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, e whakamahi ana ngā kaimahi i aua mōhiohio i roto i ā rātou mahi whakamahere. Ka tuhi ngā kaimahi i ā rātou whakawhitinga kōrero e pā ana ki te hōtaka, ā, e tīmata ana ki te arotahi ake ki te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whanonga. He kiko, he riterite hoki te āhua o ngā pūrongo ki te whānau.

E whai hua ana te whakahaeretanga o ngā pūtea, ā, ka āta aroturukitia. He mahere tā te whānau kia maha ake ai ngā rauemi ka whakaratohia mā ngā tamariki, mā te hōtaka akoranga hoki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

He whāiti te whakamahere rautaki. Nō nā tata tonu nei te whānau i te whakaoti tētahi angamahi hukihuki e mau nei i ngā kaupapa matua. Kāhore anō aua kaupapa matua kia whakatakotohia hei mahere rautaki, hei mahere ā-tau rānei, kia whai hua ai te whakatinanatanga o ngā whāinga matua me ngā wawata o te whānau.

Kāhore he kiko, kāhore rānei i te tino mārama ngā mahi aromatawai. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e tuhi kōrero ana i ia te wā ki ngā mōhiohio ka whakatakotohia e rātou ki ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki. Kāhore ngā aromatawai e āta tohu ana i te āhua o te ahu whakamua a ia tamaiti.

He iti noa te whai wāhi atu a te whānau. He rōpū matua o te whānau e whai wāhi mātātoa ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kāhore ngā tūranga o te whānau me ā rātou kawenga mahi e tino māramahia ana, ā, kāhore te whānau e āta whakaatu ana i te noho pūmau rawa atu ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo.

Me whakamahine ake i ētahi wāhanga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga a ngā kaimahi. E mōhio ana te whānau, he mea nui te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi, ā, me whakapau kaha rātou ki te whakatinana i aua tukanga. I tēnei wā, kāhore anō kia oti te katoa.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

E whakaae ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau, kia toro atu te whānau ki te tautoko o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ki te whakatutuki i ngā whakaritenga matua i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo hei whai ake.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ao Marama ki te tāone o Te Papaioea. He tino pai te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga, mā te whakawhanaungatanga. He atawhai, he manaaki, he mauritau hoki te āhua o ngā taunekeneke ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi. Kei te wā ētahi wero, ā, me toro atu ki te tautoko o Te Pōari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ao Mārama is situated on the outskirts of Palmerston North city and caters mainly for children and whānau who live in the immediate area.

There is a commitment by whānau to focus on the future developments of the kōhanga reo to benefit children. Children enjoy a loving and caring environment where diversity and difference is welcomed.

The previous ERO report identified areas of good performance with the children’s learning programme, te reo Māori development, learning environment and whanau relationships. Whānau development and kaimahi leadership were other areas of good performance. Areas for development included strategic planning and reporting and self review.

2 The Review Findings

How well are children learning and developing through the practice of whanaungatanga?

Children are learning and developing very well through the practice of whanaungatanga.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ao Mārama is located in Palmerston North city. Children are learning and developing very well through the practice of whanaungatanga. Children and kaimahi interactions are warm, caring and positive. The whānau faces some challenges and require support from TKRNT.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ao Mārama will be within two years.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are immersed in a supportive te reo Māori speaking environment. Kaimahi are positive language models for children. They use a range of effective strategies to support children. Children talk to each other in both te reo Māori and English. Children learn te reo Māori in meaningful ways and receive immediate and prompt acknowledgement for their language efforts. Children enjoy listening to and participating in waiata Māori, kanikani and korikori activities. Some children can recite their pepeha and mihimihi. Children are beginning to use te reo Māori with each other, kaimahi and some whānau members on a regular basis.

Children benefit from a well organised learning programme. Children are engaged in and actively participate in activities that are fun and interesting. Children are able to choose their own activities. Children are given time and space to complete activities at their own pace. They are encouraged to ask questions, negotiate and explore possibilities. Children know and can follow daily routines and proceedures. Children take responsibility for their personal belongings and they tidy up after themselves. Children are developing well as confident, curious learners.

Children and kaimahi interactions are warm, caring and positive. Children observe and demonstrate manaakitanga and aroha in open and spontaneous ways. They are relaxed with visitors. Children and kaimahi use humour appropriately when they interact. Kaimahi are attentive to individual children’s needs. Children show positive tuakana/teina interactions and display love and support for one another. Children are supported well by kaimahi to deal with conflict. Whānau actively support children and kaimahi during the daily programme. Children show joy and love being at kōhanga reo.

Children are developing good co-ordination skills and are given opportunities to express themselves physically in a range of activities. Outdoor play spaces are adequate. Kaimahi cater well for children’s physical and emotional needs. Children are happy, confident and comfortable in the kōhanga reo.

Key Next Steps

The children’s learning environment lacks appeal. Kaimahi acknowledge that activity stations need tidying up and additional resources need to be added to provide children with more variety and challenges. These modifications are vital in raising the quality of children’s learning experiences.

The environment needs cleaning to make sure it is safe for children. The entrance way, grounds and administration building are cluttered and a potential hazard to children and whānau. Addressing these issues will help make the kōhanga reo more appealing and attractive to prospective whānau.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau have a simple annual plan to guide their priorities which include addressing the ongoing sustainability of the kōhanga reo.

Programmes of learning are planned in a collaborative manner with a focus on children's needs and interests. Whānau have made key decisions recently regarding staffing changes that will better support quality learning outcomes for children. Some evaluation of children's learning has begun with kaimahi using this information in their planning. Kaimahi record their discussions about the programme and are beginning to focus more on children's learning and behaviour. Reports to whānau are informative and consistent.

Finances are managed effectively and carefully monitored. Whānau have a plan to ensure that more resources are provided to the children and the learning programme.

Key Next Steps

Strategic planning is limited. Whānau recently completed a draft framework with key priorities. These have yet to be completed as a strategic or annual plan to give effect to key goals and whānau aspirations.

Assessment practices lack detail and clarity. Kaimahi do not consistently annotate the information they include in children’s profiles. Assessments do not clearly show how each child is progressing.

Whānau participation and commitment is limited. There is a core group of whānau who take an active role in the management of the kōhanga reo. The roles and responsibilities of whānau are not fully understood and whānau do not show high levels of commitment to the kaupapa of Kōhanga Reo.

There are aspects of personnel management that require refinement. Whānau acknowledge kaimahi appraisals are important and they must take all reasonable steps to implement these processes, which are only partially completed.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the whānau agreed that they seek support from Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) to address the key next steps identified in this report.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ao Mārama is located in Palmerston North city. Children are learning and developing very well through the practice of whanaungatanga. Children and kaimahi interactions are warm, caring and positive. The whānau faces some challenges and require support from TKRNT.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Ao Mārama will be within two years.
