Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Pirirākau ki Te Puna, i Tauranga. Ka kitea te tino whanaungatanga, te aronga toi whenuatanga, me te tuakiri hoki o ngā tamariki. He māia, he tau hoki ngā tamariki i roto i tō rātou Pirirākautanga, ā, he pai hoki tō rātou whakawhanaketanga i roto i te reo Māori.

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri o Pirirākau, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Pirirākau ki Te Puna, i Tauranga, ā, nā ētahi kaumātua i whakatūngia ai, e toru tekau tau ki muri. Ka ngākau nui te whānau ki te noho pono ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo me te āta whakamātau i a rātou anō, i roto i ā rātou mahi katoa. Nō te hapū o Pirirākau te nuinga o ngā mema o te whānau. Ko ō rātou hononga ā-whakapapa e whakapūmau ana i te whanaungatanga me te whakaaro tahi. He pakari ngā hononga o te whānau ki te hapū, te iwi, me te hapori. He pakari anō hoki te tautoko me ngā hononga papai ki ngā ratonga me ngā kura o te hapori o Pirirākau. He aronga ā-whānau ki te waiora, ā, ko te tamaiti te pūtake. Ka whakamahi te whānau i ō rātou pūkenga, ō rātou mōhiotanga anō hoki hei hāpai i ngā putanga hua mō ngā tamariki.

Ka whakamana te whānau i te whakaaro kia pārekareka te ako a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki te taha o tō rātou whānau.

‘Ko te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo te tino take, pono ki te kaupapa.’

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te kite i te mōhio o ngā tamariki ki te whanaungatanga, te aronga toi whenuatanga, me te tuakiri?

Ka tino kitea te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki te whanaungatanga, te aronga toi whenuatanga, me te tuakiri.

Mana Atua me te Mana Whenua

He māia ngā tamariki, ā, he manawa whakahī tō rātou i runga i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae. Ka whakapūmautia tō rātou tuakiri i a rātou e taki ana i ā rātou mihimihi, ō rātou pepeha, ngā karakia hoki, ā, i a rātou e waiata ana i ngā waiata ā-iwi. Ko ngā tohu whenua me ngā tūpuna rongonui o Pirirākau, me ngā hononga ā-whānau hoki e ākona ane e ngā tamariki. Kei te tipu haere te tino mōhiotanga i roto i ngā tamariki mō ō rātou hononga ā-whakapapa, ā, nō hea hoki rātou.

He mārie, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Kua āta whakaritea ngā mahinga o ia rā, ā, kua tau hoki ēnei mā ngā tamariki. Ka āta whai atu ngā tamariki kōhungahunga i te whānau, ā, ka whai wheako rātou i te atawhai me te manaaki i roto i ā rātou taunekeneke ki ngā kaimahi.

Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i te hōhonutanga o ngā wheako reo Māori. Kōrero ai ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori ki ngā tamariki, puta noa i te rā. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo Māori e whakamahia ana ki ngā horopaki o nehe me ngā horopaki o nāianei hoki, i a rātou e noho ngātahi ana ki ngā kuia, ngā kaumātua, me te whānau. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi hihiri, rautaki hihiri anō hoki hei kapo atu, hei whakaongaonga hoki i te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. I te nuinga o te wā ka kōrero Māori ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakanuia ā rātou whakamātau. He pai te whakawhanaketanga haere o tō rātou reo Māori.

Ka whai wheako ngā tamariki i ngā hōtaka akoranga e whakaongaonga ana i a rātou. He whai wāhitanga mā rātou ki te waihanga, te hīraurau hopanga, me te tūhura hoki i ngā ariā e pā ana ki te pāngarau me te reo matatini. Haereere ai ngā tamariki ki ngā wāhi motuhake i ia te wā, pērā i te whare karakia, te marae, te urupā, me ngā huihuinga hoki o te hapū. Ka whai pānga nui ngā atua Māori me ngā kōrero tuku iho o te rohe, ki te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka aro nui ngā kaimahi ki ia tamaiti, ki te rōpū katoa anō hoki. Ka whiriwhiri ngā tamariki i ngā taumahi e hiahiatia ana e rātou, ā, e wātea ana hoki rātou ki te whai atu i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. He hihiri te āhua o ngā kaimahi, ā, ka whakahihiri, ka whakangahau anō hoki rātou i ngā akoranga mā ngā tamariki.

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao ako hāneanea e atawhai ana i a rātou. He pārekareka te wheako atu a ngā tamariki i te wāhi wāteatea nei, me ngā wāhanga i āta whakaritea ai ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi hei whiringa mā ngā tamariki. He tau ēnei wāhi ako, ā, he ngāwari mā ngā tamariki ki te hou atu. He whānui hoki ngā wāhi o waho, ā, kua āta waihangatia ai ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi hei whakaongaonga i te whakawhanaketanga ā-tinana o ngā tamariki me ō rātou pūkenga ki te hīraurau hopanga. Ka hāpai ngā wāhi ako i ngā matea ako e pā ana ki te katoa o te tamaiti, tae atu ki tōna whakawhanaketanga ā-tinana, ā-tangata, ā-whatumanawa, ā-hinengaro hoki. E tino hāpaitia ana ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga, ā, he pārekareka tā rātou noho ki te kōhanga reo.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga i āta whakamaheretia ai. He māmā ngā mahere e ārahi ana i te kaiako, ā, he mārama anō hoki ngā aratohu e pā ana ki te hōtaka. Ka arotake, ka whakapai ake hoki ngā kaimahi i ā rātou mahere i ia te wā, ā, ka whakarerekē haere i aua mahere i te wā e tika ana. He hōtaka e hāngai pū ana ki ngā tamariki e whā tau te pakeke, hei hāpai i tā rātou whakawhiti atu ki te kura.

Ka whai hua te aromātai hōtaka. Ka tohu ngā aromātai i te whai huatanga, te pūtake anō hoki o te hōtaka ki te hāpai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, me ngā āhuatanga hoki hei whakapai ake. Ka kitea te tino noho pūmau o ngā kaimahi ki te whakapai ake i ngā putanga ako mā ngā tamariki.

Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te mārama o te whakahaere a te whānau me te arotake whaiaro. Nō nā tata tonu nei te whānau i arotake ai i tā rātou tūtohinga, ā rātou kaupapa here hoki, ā, kua whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa here matua e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. E hāngai ana ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi a ngā kaimahi ki te rā. Kua whakaritea, kua whakatinanahia hoki te arohaehae kaimahi. Whiwhi ai te whānau i ngā pūrongo i ia te wā mō te hōtaka akoranga. Ka noho mōhio te whānau ki ngā nekehanga o te kōhanga reo i ia rā. Ka whakatutukihia e te whānau ngā take me ngā āwangawanga ka pupū ake, ā, ka aroturuki rātou, ka tuhi hoki rātou i te ahunga whakamua ki tā rātou mahere rautaki ā-tau. Ko ngā ngākau nuitanga me te waiora o ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā mahi whakamahere, whakariterite hoki a te whānau.

He kaha te manaaki, te aroha hoki o te whānau mō ā rātou tamariki. Ka noho ngākau nui te whānau ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. He pai tā rātou noho ngātahi, ā, he mātātoa tā rātou hāpai i ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e whakamana ana i te whakapau kaha o te whānau katoa.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kua tāutuhia e te whānau, ko tā rātou whakaritenga ka whai ako, ko te whai wāhi atu ki te ako ngaio, te whakawhanaketanga ngaio hoki ka whakatairanga ake i tō rātou māramatanga ki Te Whāriki, te mahi ngātahi me ngā tamariki kei raro i te rua o ngā tau, me ngā whakaritenga hoki e pā ana ki te aromatawai.

Me arotake, me whakatinana hoki te whānau i tā rātou pūnaha arohaehae, hei āta whakarite i tāna poipoi i ngā kaimahi katoa.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei:

  • kia whakapā atu ki ētahi atu whai wāhitanga ki te ako ngaio me te whakawhanake ngaio
  • kia whakapai ake i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aromatawai i ngā tamariki.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Pirirākau ki Te Puna, i Tauranga. Ka kitea te tino whanaungatanga, te aronga toi whenuatanga, me te tuakiri hoki o ngā tamariki. He māia, he tau hoki ngā tamariki i roto i tō rātou Pirirākautanga, ā, he pai hoki tō rātou whakawhanaketanga i roto i te reo Māori.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pirirākau is in Te Puna, Tauranga. Children display a strong sense of whānaungatanga, belonging and identity. Children are confident and comfortable in their Pirirākautanga and are developing well as emerging speakers of te reo Māori. When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pirirākau is in Te Puna, Tauranga and was established by kaumātua 30 years ago. Whānau are committed to being pono to the kaupapa of the kōhanga reo and seek to challenge themselves in all that they do. Most whānau members affiliate to their hapū of Pirirākau. Their links through whakapapa reinforce a strong sense of whanaungatanga and shared purpose. Whānau have positive relationships with hapū, iwi and the local community. There is strong support and positive relationships with Pirirākau community organisations and schools. There is a whānau-centred approach to wellbeing where the child is the focus. Whānau use their skills and knowledge to support positive outcomes for children.

Whānau acknowledge the right of children to enjoy learning te reo Māori alongside their whānau.

‘Ko te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo te tino take, pono ki te kaupapa’ Be true to the purpose of kōhanga reo

2 The Review Findings

How well do children display a sense of whānaungatanga, belonging and identity?

Children display a strong sense of whānaungatanga, belonging and identity.

Mana Atua and Mana Whenua

Children are confident and proud about where they are from. Their identity is reinforced when they recite their mihimihi, pepeha and karakia; and sing waiata-ā-iwi. Children learn about the Pirirākau landmarks, ancestors of significance and their whānau connections. Children are beginning to understand the importance about their whakapapa connections and where they come from.

Children are calm and settled in the kōhanga reo. The daily routines are well established and predictable for children. Toddlers follow everyone closely and experience warm and caring interactions with kaimahi.

Children receive rich te reo Māori language experiences. Kaimahi speak te reo Māori to children throughout the day. Children hear te reo Māori being used in traditional and contemporary forms when they are among kuia, kaumātua and whānau. Kaimahi use a range of exciting activities and strategies to captivate and stimulate children’s use of te reo Māori. Children respond mainly in te reo Māori and are praised for their efforts. They are developing well as emerging speakers of te reo Māori.

Children experience stimulating learning programmes. They have opportunities to create, problem solve and explore numeracy and literacy concepts. Children regularly go to places of significance including the whare karakia, marae, urupa and hapū events. The atua Māori and local legends are a normal part of the daily programme. Kaimahi are responsive and attuned to individual children as well as the whole group. Children choose the activities they like and are able to follow their own interests. Kaimahi have an enthusiastic manner and make learning interesting and fun for children.

Children learn in a warm and comfortable learning environment. Children enjoy and experience spacious and well organised areas with a range of activities for children to choose. These learning areas are tidy and accessible to children. The outdoor areas are extensive, well designed and include a range of resources to stimulate children’s physical development and problem solving skills. The learning areas support children’s holistic learning needs including physical, social, emotional and intellectual development. Children are well supported in their learning and enjoy being at kōhanga reo.

Planning and Evaluation

Children receive a variety of well planned learning experiences. Lead by the kaiako, planning is simple with clear guidelines for the programme. Kaimahi review and improve their plans regularly and make the changes when and where necessary. There is a specific programme for four year olds designed to support their transition to kura.

Programme evaluation is effective. Evaluations indicate how well and why the programme supports children’s learning and highlights areas for improvement. Kaimahi demonstrate a clear commitment to improving children’s learning outcomes.

Children benefit from clear whānau management and self review. Whānau have recently reviewed their charter and policies and have core health and safety policies in place. Kaimahi employment documents are up to date. Appraisals for kaimahi are in place and implemented. Whānau receive regular reports about the learning programme. The whānau are well informed about the day to day operations of the kōhanga reo. Whānau attend to emerging issues and concerns and they monitor and record progress on their annual strategic plan. Children’s interests and wellbeing are central to whānau planning and organisation.

There is a strong sense of care and love by whānau towards their children. Whānau are committed to the kaupapa of kōhanga reo. They get on well together and take an active role to support kōhanga reo operations. Children learn in an environment which values the collective efforts of whānau.

Key Next Steps

Whānau have identified that their next step is to participate in professional learning and development to enhance their understanding of Te Whāriki, working with the under two year olds and assessment practices.

Whānau need to review and implement their appraisal system to ensure it caters for all kaimahi.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • access further professional learning and development opportunities
  • improve children’s assessment practices.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pirirākau is in Te Puna, Tauranga. Children display a strong sense of whānaungatanga, belonging and identity. Children are confident and comfortable in their Pirirākautanga and are developing well as emerging speakers of te reo Māori. When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Pirirākau will be in three years.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori
