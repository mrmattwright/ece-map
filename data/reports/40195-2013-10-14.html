2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ko Mangakino Te Kōhanga Reo

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Mangakino ki te tāone iti, tuawhenua hoki o Mangakino. Kei te marae o Pouākani e tū ana. Nō te tau 1985 te kōhanga reo i tīmatahia ai. Nā te komiti o te marae te whare i koha ki te whānau. He taiohi tonu ngā mātua o te whānau o te kōhanga reo, ā, e ngākau nui ana, e tākare ana hoki rātou kia mātau ā rātou tamariki ki te reo Māori.

He pai te whakahaeretanga me te whakaritenga a ngā kaimahi i te hōtaka akoranga. Kua waia ngā tamariki ki ngā āhuatanga katoa o te hōtaka. He ngāwari noa te whakawhitiwhiti haere ki ngā tūmomo mahi. Ko ētahi āhuatanga o te hōtaka e hāpai ana i ngā tamariki ki te noho mārama ki te whanonga e tika ana i a rātou e whai wāhi atu ana ki ngā hui ōkawa ki te marae. He maha ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i ngā wā e tika ana. Ka whakatutuki te hōtaka i ngā matea o ngā pēpi. He pārekareka ki a rātou te taiao nei e whakawātea ana i ngā tamariki ki te kōkiri i ā rātou ake akoranga. He mātau te kaiako ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ko ngā rautaki whakaako e whakamahia ana e ngā kaiako e hāpai ana i ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori.

He pai ngā pūnaha ā te whānau ki te aroturuki i ngā whakahaeretanga o tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka whai wāhi nui te kaiwhakahaere ki te whakahaeretanga o ngā mahi tari ki te kōhanga reo. He pai ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakamahere me te aromatawai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki tuākana. He pai te noho mōhio o ngā whānau ki te ahunga whakamua o ā rātou tamariki.

Kua arotakengia, kua whakahoungia hoki te tūtohinga. He tino aronga ki te whakawhanaketanga o te katoa o te tamaiti i a ia i te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru e whakamōhio ana, e ārahi ana hoki i ngā whakaritenga. E tino mōhio ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki. Kua tāutuhia e te whānau ngā kaupapa matua mō te whakawhanaketanga o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei. Ka hāngai ēnei ki Te Ara Tūāpae. He pai te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo. Ko te whakatakoto haere i te pūtea e āta whakarite ana i te whakatutukitanga o ngā rauemi mō te hōtaka akoranga.

Me titiro ki ētahi wāhanga o te whakahaeretanga me ngā whakaritenga. Me whakarite ki te aromātai i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakatau anō hoki tēnei i te aroturuki i ia te wā i te kounga o te hōtaka. Kua tae ki te wā kia whai whakaaro ngā kaimahi ki ngā tukanga aromatawai e hāngai ana ki te reanga me te āheinga o ngā tamariki katoa. Inā koa, ko te whānuitanga o ngā matea o ngā pēpi me ngā tamariki. Me whakaoti te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi. Ka hāpai tēnei i ngā kaupapa matua o te whakawhanaketanga ngaio, ā, ka whai pānga matua ki te whakapai ake i ngā whakaritenga. Me whakapakari te arotake whaiaro. Mā tēnei, ka noho mōhio te whānau ki te ahunga whakamua i roto i ngā āhuatanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Mangakino kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Mangakino.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Tangata.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Tangata

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Mana Tangata te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E whakapono ana rātou mā te Mana Tangata ka tautokona ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā akoranga e whai pūtake ana ki a rātou. E whakapono ana hoki te whānau, ka hāpai te hōtaka akoranga i ngā tamariki ki te noho mārama ki tō rātou whai wāhitanga ki te whānau, te hapori, me te ao. Ka arotake te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te whānuitanga o te kōhanga reo ki te whakatairanga i ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka whakarato te whānau i tētahi hōtaka e whakarato ana i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whakapā atu ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga.

  • He pai te whakahaeretanga me te whakaritenga a ngā kaimahi i te hōtaka akoranga.
  • Kua waia ngā tamariki ki ngā āhuatanga katoa o te hōtaka. Nā tēnei, he māmā ngā whakawhitinga ki waenga i te hōtaka akoranga me ngā mahinga.
  • Nā ngā tikanga i te hōtaka pērā i te karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā mihimihi, me ngā pepeha, ka mārama, ka whai wāhi atu hoki ngā tamariki ki ngā kawa e whai pānga anō hoki ki ētahi atu horopaki Māori.
  • He maha ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. E āhei ana rātou ki te kōkiri i ngā akoranga e pā ana ki te hīraurau hopanga, te whakawero hoki, hei hāpai i te puāwaitanga o te reo matatini, te pāngarau, me te hangarau.
  • Nā te wāteatanga mai o ngā rauemi, nā te wāteatea hoki o te kōhanga reo, ka mahi auaha ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakaaro pohewa hoki rātou. Ka ngana ngā tamariki ki ngā mahi mō ngā wā roa. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i ngā wā e tika ana.
  • He pārekareka ki ngā pēpi tētahi taiao akoranga e whakatutuki ana i ō rātou matea ako. He ngāwari, he mārie hoki te rere o te hōtaka. Ka whai ngā kaimahi i ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā pēpi, i ngā wā katoa. He tika ngā rauemi, ā, ka whai pānga anō hoki ki ō rātou reanga. He pārekareka ki ngā pēpi tētahi taiao e whakawātea ana i a rātou ki te kōkiri i ā rātou ake akoranga.
  • He pakari ngā kaimahi ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā rautaki whakawhiti reo hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga reo o ngā tamariki. Ka mārama ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori, ā, he tika ā rātou aronga, ā rātou whakautu hoki ki ngā kōrero me ngā pātai ā ngā kaimahi.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai ki ngā kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mō ā rātou tamariki me te whānau. Ka hāngai hoki te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Kua whakaritea e te whānau ngā pūnaha hei aroturuki i te whakahaeretanga o tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka whai pānga nui te kaiwhakahaere ki te whakahaeretanga o ngā mahi tari ki te kōhanga reo.

He tika ngā tukanga ā ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahere me te aromatawai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He matawhānui ngā mahere, ā, ka whai kiko. Nā tēnei, he mārama ēnei hei aratohu i te whakatinanatanga o te hōtaka. Ko te pūrongo ki te whānau mō ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki ka āta tāutu i te ahunga whakamua mō ngā tamariki tuākana.

Kua arotakengia, kua whakahoungia hoki te tūtohinga. Ka āta whakaraupapa te hītori, te tauāki taketake, ngā tūmanako me ngā whāinga i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. He tino aronga ki te whakawhanaketanga o te katoa o te tamaiti, i a ia i te kōhanga reo.

Ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru, ka whakamōhio, ka ārahi hoki i ngā whakaritenga. Ki te kōhanga reo, ka ako, ka tupu hoki ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao haumaru, tau hoki mō rātou.

Kei ngā kaimahi katoa ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi me ngā kirimana mahi mā ia tangata i waitohungia ai, ā, kua arowhaingia hoki rātou e ngā pirihimana. Ka whakarite te whānau ki te whakarato i te tino tautoko ki ngā kaimahi.

Kua tāutuhia e te whānau ngā kaupapa matua mō te whanake tonutanga o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei. Ka hāngai ēnei ki Te Ara Tūāpae. Kua tāutu te whānau i ngā mahi e tika ana, kia tutukihia ai ā rātou whāinga. Ko te aronga rautaki e whakawhanake ana i tō rātou āheinga i roto i te reo Māori, ā, e whakapakari ana hoki i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo.

He pai te tūnga pūtea o te kōhanga reo. Ko te whakatakoto haere i te pūtea e āta whakarite ana i te whakatutukitanga o ngā rauemi mō te hōtaka akoranga.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Me titiro ki te aromātai i te hōtaka akoranga. He mea nui kia arotahi ngā hui a ngā kaimahi ki te whakapai i te hōtaka. Ko te tuhi haere i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ka hāpai i ngā kaimahi ki te tāutu me te aru haere i ngā whakapaitanga. Ka whakarite hoki tēnei i te aroturukitanga o te kounga o te hōtaka i ia te wā.

He mea nui kia hāngai ngā tukanga aromatawai ki ngā reanga me ngā āheinga o ngā tamariki katoa. Kāhore te tauira pūrongo o tēnei wā e whakatutuki ana i ngā matea o ngā pēpi, i te whānuitanga rānei o ngā matea tamariki. He mea nui, kia kapo ngā kaimahi i ngā akoranga ka whai pūtake ki ngā tamariki, mā te whakapakari i ngā mahi aromatawai.

Me whakaoti ngā arohaehae kaimahi. Ka āta whakarite tēnei i te hononga mārika o ngā kaupapa matua o te whakawhanaketanga ngaio ki te whakapai ake i ngā whakaritenga.

Me whakapakari te arotake whaiaro. Ahakoa ka tukua ngā pūrongo pūtea, pūrongo kaiako hoki ki ngā hui ā-whānau, kāhore i te rawaka. Me noho mōhio te whānau ki te ahunga whakamua o ngā āhuatanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga, tae atu ki te ahu whakamua, me ngā āhuatanga hoki hei whakapai ake tonu.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Mangakino, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko Mangakino Te Kōhanga Reo

Mangakino Te Kōhanga Reo is located in the small, rural township of Mangakino. It is situated on the Pouakani Marae. The kōhanga reo was established in 1985. The building was gifted to the whānau by the marae committee. The whānau of the kōhanga reo are young parents who are committed and keen for their children to be competent te reo Māori speakers.

The learning programme is well managed and organised by kaimahi. Children are familiar with all aspects of the programme. Transitions are smooth with little disruption. Aspects of the programme support children to understand appropriate behaviour when participating in formal events on the marae. There are many opportunities for children to follow their own interests. The kaimahi support children’s learning when required. The programme caters for the needs of babies. They enjoy an environment where children control their learning. The kaiako is a confident speaker of te reo Māori. The teaching strategies used by kaiako support children to use te reo Māori.

The whānau have good systems to monitor the operations of their kōhanga reo. The kaiwhakahaere plays a critical role in the administration of kōhanga reo business. There are good processes for planning and assessing older children’s learning. Whānau are well informed of their child’s progress.

The charter has been reviewed and updated. There is a clear focus on the holistic development of the child while at kōhanga reo. Health and safety policies and procedures inform and guide practice. Whānau and kaimahi are well aware of their roles and responsibilities. The whānau have identified priorities for the future development of the kōhanga reo. These align to Te Ara Tūāpae. The kōhanga is in a financially viable position. The budget forecast ensures that resourcing for the learning programme is well catered for.

There are some areas of operations and practice that require attention. Evaluating the learning programme needs to be put in place. This will also ensure that the quality of the programme is monitored regularly. It is time for kaimahi to consider assessment processes that are matched to the age and abilities of all children. In particular, babies and children with diverse needs. Kaimahi appraisals need to be completed. This will assist priorities for professional development and will link directly to improving practice. Self review needs to be strengthened. This will ensure whānau are well informed of progress in all aspects of operations.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Mangakino was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.  This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Te Kōhanga Reo o Mangakino.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Tangata.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning; and
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

4 Findings

Mana Tangata

Background

The whānau chose Mana Tangata as a focus for this review. They believe Mana Tangata is where children are supported to participate in learning that is meaningful to them. The whānau also believe that the learning programme assists children to understand where they fit in the whānau, community and the world. ERO will review the extent to which the kōhanga reo promotes children’s interests.

Areas of good performance

The whānau provide a programme where children have opportunities to engage in a range of learning experiences.

  • The learning programme is well managed and organised by kaimahi
  • Children are familiar with all aspects of the programme. This results in smooth transitions between the learning programme and routines.
  • The structured part of the programme such as karakia, hīmene, mihimihi, pepeha enables the children to understand and participate in kawa that is prominent in other Māori contexts.
  • There are many opportunities for children to follow their own interests. They are able to initiate learning where problem solving and challenge supports emergent literacy, mathematics and technology.
  • The availability of resources and space allows children to be creative and use their imagination. Children are engaged for sustained periods of time. The kaimahi support children’s learning when required.
  • The babies enjoy a learning environment where their learning needs are met. The programme is unhurried and calm. Kaimahi follow the babies interests at all times. Resources are appropriate and relevant to their ages. Babies enjoy an environment where they have control over their learning.
  • Kaimahi are confident speakers of te reo Māori. They use conversational strategies to support children’s language development. Children understand te reo Māori and respond appropriately to kaimahi suggestions and questions.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

The whānau have in place systems to monitor the operations of their kōhanga reo. The kaiwhakahaere plays a critical role in the administration of kōhanga reo business.

Kaimahi have appropriate processes for planning and assessing children’s learning. Planning is comprehensive and detailed. This provides clear guidelines for programme implementation. Reporting to whānau about children’s learning clearly identifies progress for the older children.

The charter has been reviewed and updated. The history, mission statement, goals and aims clearly outline the kaupapa of the kōhanga reo. There is a clear focus on the holistic development of the child while at kōhanga reo.

Health and safety policies and procedures inform and guide practice. The kōhanga reo provides a place where the children can learn and grow in an environment that is safe and secure.

All kaimahi have signed job descriptions, individual employment contracts and have been police vetted. The whānau ensure that they provide as much support as possible to kaimahi.

The whānau have identified priorities for the future development of the kōhanga reo. These align to Te Ara Tūāpae. Whānau have identified appropriate actions to achieve their goals. The strategic focus is growing their capability for te reo Māori and strengthening the kaupapa of the kōhanga reo.

The kōhanga is in a financially viable position. The budget forecast ensures that resourcing for the learning programme is well catered for.

Areas for development

Evaluating the learning programme needs attention. It is important that kaimahi hui focus on improving the programme. Recording discussions will enable kaimahi to identify and track improvements. This will also ensure that the quality of the programme is monitored regularly.

It is important that processes for assessment are matched to the age and abilities of all children. The current reporting template does not cater for babies or children with diverse needs. It is important that kaimahi capture the learning that is meaningful to children by strengthening assessment practice.

Kaimahi appraisals need to be completed. This will ensure that priorities for professional development will link directly to improving practice.

Self review needs to be strengthened. While reporting on finances and kaiako reports are submitted to whānau hui, this does not go far enough. Whānau need to be informed of progress in all aspects of operations including progress and where they need to make further improvements.

5 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of Te Kōhanga Reo o Mangakino completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist.  In these documents they haveattested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following itemsbecause they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

6 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  1. That whānau seek support to address the areas for improvement identified in this report. These include:
  • improving programme evaluation
  • strengthening assessment practices for babies and children with diverse needs
  • completing kaimahi appraisals
  • formally implementing self review processes.

7 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
