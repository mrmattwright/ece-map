1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā tētahi ō ngā Kaiwhakahaere Matua ā-Rohe o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei, kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga o Te Kōhanga Reo o Miropiko e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia e te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i te Pipiri 2011, me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

  • te hōtaka akoranga, te taiao, me ngā rauemi
  • te whakahaeretanga a te whānau me ngā kaimahi, te aromātai hōtaka me te aromatawai
  • ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru
  • ētahi atu tino take i tāutuhia ai whai muri i te arotake o mua.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ko ngā mokopuna manaakitia

Whāngaitia ki te ūkaipō ki te kupu kōrero

Atawhaitia i roto i ngā tikanga

Ka tupu, kia tū tangata i roto i tēnei ao hurihuri.

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Miropiko ki te hapori o Chartwell, ki Kirikiriroa. He tokomaha ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau kua whai wāhi ki ngā kapa haka o te rohe, o te motu hoki mō te roanga o te wā, ā, i reira whakahoahoa ai, whakataetae ai anō hoki. Ka whai pānga nui tēnei ki te hōtaka o ngā tamariki i ia rā.

I te tau 2011, i tāutu te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te maha o ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake i roto i te whakahaeretanga me te hōtaka o ia rā. Ko ētahi o ēnā i hāngai ai ki te aromatawai me te aromātai, ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga, te whakahaeretanga a ngā kaimahi me te whānau, te iti noa o ngā rauemi hei hāpai i ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, me te kaha hanganga rawa o ngā akoranga e tino kōkiri rawatia anō hoki e ngā pākeke.

Kua pai ake te hōtaka o ia, ā, he pai hoki te whakahaeretanga i tēnei. Ka rere pai ngā taumahi me ngā mahinga a ngā tamariki. He whānui ngā tūmomo taumahi, rauemi anō hoki, kia wātea ai ngā tamariki ki te whiriwhiri haere me te whai i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā āhuatanga katoa o te kapa haka, puta noa i te hōtaka. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te riterite o ngā taumahi o roto, o waho hoki. He pārekareka ki ngā pēpi te nui o te wā waiata, ngā tākaro ā-matimati, me ngā kēmu kōrerorero. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te riterite o te hōtaka e whakatutuki ana i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, ō rātou matea hoki.

Kua pai ake te whakahaeretanga i te kōhanga reo. Kua whakatutukihia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi ngā take whakahaere katoa i tāutuhia ai ki te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te tau 2011. Kua arotakengia, kua whakahoungia hoki te tūtohinga. Ka ū tonu ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaere tari, ki ngā taumata teitei. He nui te whai wāhi a te whānau, te tae ā-tinana atu, te whakapuaki whakaaro hoki ki ngā hui ā-whānau me ngā tūmomo kaupapa i te kōhanga reo. He mārama ngā pūrongo puta noa i te nuinga o ngā wāhanga whakahaere o te kōhanga reo, ā, he tino kiko anō hoki i roto. E aro ngātahi ana te whānau ki te tupu me te haere tonu i te whakapaitanga.

Ko ngā pūnaha me ngā whakaritenga katoa e pā ana ki te hauora, te haumaru, me te akuaku e tino māramahia ana, e whakatinanahia ana i ia te wā, ā, e pūrongotia ana ki te whānau. I nāianei, ka whakamōhiotia te whānau i ia te wā, mō te waiora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo.

E whakawhanake tonuhia ana e ngā kaimahi ngā whakaritenga aromatawai me te aromātai hōtaka.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Te hōtaka akoranga, te taiao, me ngā rauemi

He whakamārama

I te pūrongo arotake o Pipiri i te tau 2011, i tāutuhia ngā wāhanga o Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira hei whakapai ake. Kāhore i te rawaka ngā rauemi a ngā tamariki kia āhei ai rātou ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. He kaha rawa te hanganga o ngā akoranga, ā, e kōkiri rawatia ana hoki e ngā pākeke. Kāhore te ngākau nui o ngā tamariki i ū, ā, ka takeo haere rātou. Kāhore i te tika ngā taumahi ako mā ngā pēpi. Kāhore i te āta whakamahia te taiao akoranga.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Ngā ratonga rauemi. He whānui ngā tūmomo taumahi, rauemi hoki hei whiringa ā ngā tamariki, kia taea ai e rātou ō rātou ngākau nuitanga te whai. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā taumahi takirōpū iti, takitahi hoki. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te riterite o ngā taumahi o roto, o waho hoki. Ka tino hāpaitia te whakawhanaketanga o te katoa o te tamaiti. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā āhuatanga katoa o te kapa haka, puta noa i te hōtaka. He pārekareka ki ngā pēpi te nui o ngā waiata, ngā tākaro ā-matimati, me ngā kēmu kōrerorero. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā kori ā-tinana whakahauora, kia tahuri ai ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi ki ngā taumahi. He wāteatea, he muramura hoki te taiao akoranga. He tau, he harikoa hoki te āhua o ngā tamariki i te kōhanga reo.

Te hōtaka akoranga. He pai te whakahaeretanga o te hōtaka o ia rā. E tino mōhio ana ngā kaimahi ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki i ia rā. Nā tēnei, ka rere pai ngā taumahi me ngā mahinga o ia rā mā ngā tamariki. He pai te mahi ngātahi a ngā kaimahi ki te whakarato i tētahi hōtaka whakaongaonga mā ngā tamariki. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te riterite o tētahi hōtaka e poipoi ana i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga, ō rātou matea hoki.

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau me ngā kaimahi, te aromātai hōtaka me te aromatawai

He whakamārama

I te tau 2011, i tāutu te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te maha o ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake, tae atu ki te aromatawai me te aromātai, ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga, te whakahaeretanga a ngā kaimahi, me te whakahaeretanga hoki a te whānau.

Kua mahi ngātahi ngā kaimahi me te whānau o Miropiko ki te taha o te purapura me Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki tēnei rohe, ki te whakatutuki i aua wāhanga. He nui te ahunga whakamua, puta noa i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau. Kua tino pai ake te whakahaeretanga whānui o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakapau kaha te whānau ki te noho mārama ki te whakahaeretanga o tō rātou kōhanga reo. Kua whakatutukihia e rātou ngā take whakahaere katoa i tāutuhia ai ki te pūrongo o 2011. Kua arotakengia, kua whakahoungia hoki te tūtohinga. Kua whiwhi raihana anō te kōhanga reo, mai i te Tāhuhu o te Mātauranga, i raro i ngā ture e pā ana ki te Mātauranga Kōhungahunga o 2008. Ka ū ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaere tari ki te taumata teitei. He pai te whakaritenga o ngā tuhinga, ā, he māmā te whakapā atu ki ēnei i ngā wā e tika ana.

Ka whakamaheretia te tū auau o ngā hui ā-marama, puta noa i te tau. He nui te whai wāhi, te tae ā-tinana, te whakapuaki whakaaro anō hoki o te whānau. He mārama, he tino kiko hoki ki ngā pūrongo, puta noa i te nuinga o ngā wāhanga o te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Nā tēnei, he pai ngā mōhiohio e whakaratohia ana mā te whānau, mō te whakatau whakaritenga, me te whakamahere mō meāke nei. I nāianei, he aronga nui ki ngā matea o ngā tamariki, me te ako, te whakawhanaketanga hoki o te whānau. Ka aro ngātahi te whānau ki te tupu me te whakapai tonutanga.

Kua whakaritea tētahi mahere rautaki i nāianei. E hāpai ana tētahi mahere whakahaere ā-tau i te whakatinanatanga o ngā whāinga rautaki matua me ngā kaupapa matua a te whānau i ia tau. Kua pai ake hoki i nāianei ngā pūnaha i whakaritea ai mō te arotake, te pūrongo, me te aroturuki.

Te whakahaeretanga a ngā kaimahi. Kua arotakengia ngā pūnaha kaimahi, ngā tukanga me ngā whakaritenga i runga i te tūtohinga o te kōhanga reo. Whakatutukihia ai ngā arohaehae i ia tau. He tukanga pakari tēnei e aro nui ana ki te whakawhanaketanga o ngā kaimahi. I nāianei, e whakaatuhia ana ngā kaimahi i tō rātou tino māramatanga ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi anō hoki. Ka poipoi te ārahitanga, ā, ka tino whai hua. Ka mārama ngā kaimahi katoa ki ngā tūmanako e pā ana ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru

Ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga. Kua whakatutukihia e te whānau ngā take katoa i tāutuhia ai ki te pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. Ko te katoa o ngā pūnaha me ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te hauora, te haumaru, me te akuaku, e tino māramahia ana, he riterite te whakatinanatanga, ā, ka pūrongotia anō hoki ki te whānau. I nāianei, ka whakamōhiotia te whānau i ia te wā, mō te waiora, te noho haumaru hoki o ā rātou tamariki ki te taiao o te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te aromatawai me te aromātai i te hōtaka. E ai ki ngā kaimahi, whai muri i te arotake o mua, kei te whakamahine tonu, kei te whakawhanake tonu hoki rātou i te aromatawai me ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aromātai hōtaka.

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of Te Kōhanga Reo o Miropikogoverning body and management in relation to areas identified in the June 2011 ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • the learning programme, environment and resourcing
  • whānau and personnel management, programme evaluation and assessment
  • Health and Safety policies and procedures
  • any other significant issues identified since the previous review.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Ko ngā mokopuna manaakitia

Whāngaitia ki te ūkaipo ki te kupu kōrero

Atawhaitia i roto i ngā tikanga

Ka tupu, kia tū tangata i roto i ēnei ao hurihuri.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Miropiko is located in the Hamilton suburb of Chartwell. Several kaimahi and whānau members have a long association with local and national kapa haka teams both at social and competitive level. This plays a huge influence on the daily programme for children.

The 2011 Education Review report highlighted several areas for development in management and the daily programme. Some of these included assessment and evaluation, policies and procedures, personnel and whānau management, insufficient resources to enable children to follow their interests and learning was too structured and adult directed.

The daily programme has improved and is well managed. Activities and routines for children flow smoothly. Children have a variety of activities and resources to make choices and follow their interests. Children enjoy all aspects of kapa haka throughout the programme. Children participate in a balance of indoor and outdoor activities. Babies enjoy lots of singing, finger-play and talking games. Children benefit from a balanced programme that caters for their interests and needs.

Management of the kōhanga reo has improved. The whānau and kaimahi have addressed all management matters raised in the 2011 ERO report. The charter has been reviewed and updated. All areas of administration are maintained to a high standard. There are high levels of whānau participation, attendance and involvement at whānau hui and other kōhanga reo events. Reporting across most areas of the kōhanga reo operations is clear and detailed. The whānau is collectively focused on growth and continual improvement.

All health, safety and hygiene systems and practices are well understood, consistently implemented and reported to the whānau. The whānau are now regularly informed on about their children’s wellbeing and safety in the kōhanga environment.

Kaimahi are still developing assessment practices and programme evaluation.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Findings

The learning programme, environment and resourcing

Background

The June 2011 review report identified aspects of Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira that needed to be improved. Children had insufficient resources to enable them to follow their interests. Learning was too structured and adult directed. Children lost interest quickly and became bored. Learning activities for babies were unsuitable. The learning environment was not well used.

Areas of progress

Resource provisions. Children have a variety of activities and resources to make choices and follow their interests. Children enjoy both small group and individual activities. Children participate in a balance of indoor and outdoor activities. The holistic development of children is well supported. Children enjoy all aspects of kapa haka throughout the programme. Babies enjoy lots of singing, finger play and talking games. Kaimahi use gestures and animation to attract children to join in activities. The learning environment is spacious and bright. Children are settled and appear happy at kōhanga reo.

The learning programme. The daily programme is well managed. Kaimahi are fully aware of their daily roles and responsibilities. Consequently daily activities and routines for children flow smoothly. Kaimahi work well together to provide a stimulating programme for children. Children benefit from a balanced programme that caters for their interests and needs.

Whānau and personal management, programme evaluation and assessment

Background

The 2011 ERO report highlighted several areas for development including assessment and evaluation, policies and procedures, personnel management and whānau management.

The kaimahi and whānau of Miropiko have worked together with local purapura and Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust to address these areas. Significant progress has been made across all areas of operations.

Areas of progress

Whānau management. Overall management of the kōhanga reo has improved significantly. Whānau have worked hard to gain a shared understanding about the management of their kōhanga reo. They have addressed all management matters raised in the 2011 report. The charter has been reviewed and updated. The kōhanga reo has been relicensed by the Ministry of Education under the 2008 Early Childhood Education regulations. All areas of administration are maintained to a high standard. There is good organisation of documents and these are easily accessible and available when required.

Monthly hui throughout the year are planned and regular. There are high levels of whānau participation, attendance and involvement. Reporting across most areas of the kōhanga reo operations is clear and detailed. This provides whānau with good information for decision making and future planning. There is now a strong focus on children’s needs and whānau learning and development. The whānau is collectively focused on growth and continual improvement.

A strategic plan is now in place. An annual management plan supports each year’s implementation of key strategic goals and whānau priorities. There are now improved review, reporting and monitoring systems in place.

Personnel management. Personnel systems, procedures and practices have been reviewed in line with the kōhanga reo charter. Appraisals are carried out each year. The process is rigorous and strongly focused on kaimahi development. Kaimahi now demonstrate that they clearly understand their roles and responsibilities. Leadership is nurturing and highly effective. All kaimahi understand the expectations for children’s learning.

Health and Safety policies and procedures

Policies and procedures. The whānau have addressed all matters raised in the last ERO report. All health, safety and hygiene systems and practices are well understood, consistently implemented and reported to the whānau. The whānau are now regularly informed about their children’s wellbeing and safety in the kōhanga environment.

Areas for further improvement

Assessmentand programme evaluation. Since the last review, the kaimahi report that they are still refining and developing assessment and programme evaluation practices.

4 Recommendations

ERO and the kōhanga reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • the kaimahi and whānau continue to strengthen assessment and programme evaluation practices.

5 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
