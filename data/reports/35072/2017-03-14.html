Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhangā Reo o Miropiko ki Kirikiriroa. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. E whakawhanake ana rātou i ngā pūkengā ki te whakawhiti kōrero ki ētahi atu. Ka tūhura ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki tētahi taiao e āta hāpai ana i a rātou.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātaurangā i Te Kōhangā Reo o Miropiko i roto i ngā tau e toru. 

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhangā Reo o Miropiko ki te hapori o Chartwell, ki Kirikiriroa. He mahere rautaki tā te whānau, ā, e whakatakoto ana tēnei i ngā whāingā rautaki, hei hāpai i a rātou ki te āta whakarato i te mātaurangā o te koungā kairangi mō ā rātou tamariki. Nō nā tata tonu nei te kōhangā reo i whakahoungia ai, hei āta whakamahi ake i te whānuitangā o tōna wāhi noho.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e kōrero i te reo Māori?

E mārama ana te nuingā o ngā tamariki i te reo Māori, ā, e āta whanake ana tō rātou tūmāia ki te kōrero i te reo.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. Ka rongo, ka kōrero hoki rātou i te reo Māori i ngā wā horopaki ōkawa me ngā horopaki ōpaki hoki. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ngā kawa me ngā tikangā e pā ana ki ngā mihi whakatau. E āhei ana ngā tamariki tuākana ki te taki i ō rātou pepeha. E mōhio ana rātou ki ngā mōteatea me ngā waiata o tēnei hapori, ā, ka waiatatia. He pakari te nuingā o ngā tamariki ki te whakautu pātai mā te reo Māori. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i te whānuitangā o ngā tūmomo rautaki e hāpai ana i te whakawhanaketangā reo o ngā tamariki, tae atu ki te akiaki, te whakatauira, me te tāruarua. Kei te waia haere ngā pēpi ki te reo Māori. Ka taea e rātou ngā kōrero a ngā pākeke te tāwhai, ā, ka korikori, ka tohutohu hei whakawhiti kōrero ki ētahi atu. Ka mahi ngātahi ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki, ā, ka tāpiri atu i ngā kupu hou ki ngā kīangā e mōhio kētia ana. Ka rongo ētahi o ngā tamariki i te reo Māori ki ō rātou kāingā. E whanake ana ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

He pakari ngā tamariki ki te taunekeneke ki ētahi atu. He pai ā rātou taunekeneke me ō rātou honongā ki te taha o ō rātou hoa me ngā pākeke. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te noho ngātahi me te ako ngātahi. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i tētahi taiao e aro nui ana ki te awhi, te atawhai, me te manaaki. Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi pārekareka, tae atu ki te kori tinana me te waiata. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te ārahi i ngā kaupapa, tae atu ki ngā karakia. Ka whakamihi, ka whakanui hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā whakamātau me ngā whakatutukitangā a ngā tamariki. Ka whakanuia te whānaungātangā ki te kōhangā reo. E whakawhanake ana te arongā toi whenuatangā o ngā tamariki.

He pakari ngā pūmanawa ā-tinana o ngā tamariki. Ka hāpai te whānau i te noho haumaru o ngā tamariki ki te kōhangā reo. Ka whai wāhi atu ngā tamariki ki te whānuitangā o ngā tūmomo wero i a rātou e noho ana ki waho. Ka eke paihikara rātou, ā, ka tākaro hoki rātou ki te papa tākaro, ki te taha tonu o ō rātou hoa. E āta mātakina ana ngā tamariki e ngā kaimahi. He maha ā rātou whai wāhitangā ki te whakawero i ō rātou tinana, tae atu ki ngā hangāngā pikipiki, ngā hangāngā taupare, me ngā kēmu. Ka noho ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te kōhangā reo.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka whai kiko ngā mahere hōtaka. Ka āta kitea ngā wāhangā matua o ngā mahere. Ko ētahi o ēnei, ko ngā mahere mō te wā waengā, ngā wātaka o ia wiki, me te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka āta whakaraupapahia te kiko o te hōtaka, ngā rautaki e whakamahia ana hei whakatō i ngā akorangā, me ngā rārangi mahi a ngā kaimahi. Ka tino kitea ngā mahere ki te hōtaka akorangā o tēnei wā. E whai pāngā ana, e tika ana hoki ngā rautaki i tāutuhia ai ki ngā mahere, hei hāpai i ngā akorangā a ngā tamariki. E mōhio ana te whānau he aha ngā mahi e akohia ana e a rātou tamariki. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā ngā whai wāhitangā ako i āta whakamaheretia ai.

He pai te whakahaeretangā i te kōhangā reo. Ka tohungia ki te tūtohingā, ko te reo Māori me ōna tikangā, me te mātaurangā, te atawhai hoki o ngā tamariki te arongā matua. Arotakengia ai e te whānau tā rātou tūtohingā i ia te wā. Kua whakatakotongia ngā kaupapa here matua e pā ana ki te hauora, te haumaru, te waiora, me te atawhai, ā, e hāngāi ana hoki ēnei ki te wā. Ka āta whakarite te whānau i te whakatakotorangā o ngā mōhiohio e tika ana ki ngā tūmomo rehita. Kua whakaritea hoki ngā tuhingā e pā ana ki ngā tūrangā mahi a ngā kaimahi, ā, e hāngāi ana hoki ki te wā. Kua arohaehaengia ngā kaimahi katoa. He mahere rautaki tā te whānau i whakamōhiotia ai ki Te Ara Tūāpae. Kua whakamahere rātou i ā rātou whāingā me ā rātou kaupapa matua mō meāke nei. Ka tāutu te mahere mahi i ngā tāngāta ka kawe i ngā mahi matua o te tau 2017, i te āhua o aua tūmomo mahi, me te wā hoki ka whakatutukihia. Arotakengia ai te tūtohingā me ngā kaupapa here i ia te wā. E whai pūtake ana, e whai hua ana hoki te aromātai o roto, ā, e whakatutuki ana i ngā matea me ngā hiahia o te whānau me ngā tamariki. Ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā whakataungā katoa ki te kōhangā reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

I tāutuhia e ngā kaimahi ko te aromatawai i ngā akorangā a ngā tamariki, me te aromātai i te hōtaka, ētahi āhuatangā hei whakawhanake tonu. Ahakoa e āhei ana ngā kaimahi ki te whakapuaki i te pūtake o te aromatawai, kāhore he honongā ki waengā i tēnā e mōhiotia ana e rātou, me tēnā e whakatutukihia ana e rātou. E rua ā rātou huarahi aromatawai. Kāhore aua huarahi aromatawai e tāutu ana i tēnā e mōhiotia ana e ngā tamariki, i ā rātou akorangā, ā, i tā rātou ahu whakamua anō hoki hei ākongā. Ahakoa e mōhio ana ngā kaimahi mā te aromātai ka pupū ake ngā whakapaitangā, he uaua ki te aroturuki i ngā whakarerekētangā i whakatutukihia ai ki te hōtaka akorangā, me ngā pūtake i pērā ai ngā whakarerekētangā. Kāhore ngā tamariki e whiwhi ana i ngā hua ka puta ake i te pakaritangā ake o te aromatawai me te aromātai hōtaka.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātaurangā ngā pūnaha o te ratongā ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatangā i raro iho nei e whai pāngā nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritengā whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngāi rongoā; ko ngā whakaritengā akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikangā haerengā whakawaho);
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātaurangā; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatangā kaiako; me te tataurangā ki waengā i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritengā, tikangā hōneatangā e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikangā, ka whakatairangā ngā kōhangā reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitangā i ngā herengā ā-ture.

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātaurangā me te whānau o te kōhangā reo ngā taunakitangā e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • tautokona e te whānau te whakapakaritangā ake a ngā kaimahi i ngā whakaritengā e pā ana ki te aromatawai
  • whakaritea e ngā kaimahi tētahi tukangā mō te aromātai i te whai huatangā o te hōtaka akorangā.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Miropiko is in Hamilton. Children are familiar with te reo Māori. They are developing skills for communication with others. Children explore te reo Maori in a supportive environment.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Miropiko will be in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Miropiko is in Chartwell, Hamilton. Whānau have a strategic plan that includes strategic objectives to enable them to excel in the provision of high quality education for their children. The kōhanga reo has had recent renovations to make better use of space.

2 The Review Findings

To what extent do children speak te reo Māori?

Most children understand and are slowly developing confidence to speak te reo Maori

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are familiar with te reo Māori. They hear and speak te reo Māori in formal and informal settings. Children know the kawa and tikanga of mihi whakatau. The older children can recite their pepeha. They know and sing local mōteatea and waiata. Most children are confident to answer questions in te reo Māori. Kaimahi provide a range of strategies to support children's language development including prompting, role modelling, and repetition. Babies are becoming familiar with te reo Māori. They can mimic what adults say and use gestures to communicate with others. Kaimahi work directly with them adding new kupu to familiar phrases. Some children hear te reo Māori spoken at home. Children are developing speakers of te reo Māori.

Children are confident in their interactions with others. They have positive interactions and relationships with their peers and adults. Children enjoy each other’s company and learn from each other. Kaimahi provide an environment where awhi, atawhai and manaaki are a priority. Children participate in fun activities including kori tinana and waiata. Kaimahi support children to lead activities including karakia. Kaimahi praise and acknowledge children’s efforts and achievements. Whanaungatanga is valued at kōhanga reo. Children are developing a sense of belonging.

Children are confident in their physical abilities. Whānau support children to feel safe at kōhanga reo. Children experience a range of challenges while outdoors. They ride on bikes and play on the adventure play ground with their friends. Children are supervised well by kaimahi. They experience many physical opportunities and challenges including climbing frames, obstacle courses and games. Children enjoy their time at kōhanga reo. 

Planning and Evaluation

Programme planning is detailed. Essential elements of planning are obvious. These include medium term plans, weekly timetables and daily programme. Programme content, strategies used to embed learning and kaimahi rosters are all clearly outlined. Planning is easily identified in the current learning programme. The strategies identified in the plans are relevant and appropriate to support children’s learning. Whānau are well aware of what their children are learning. Children benefit from well- planned opportunities for learning.

The kōhanga reo is well managed. The charter identifies te reo Māori me ona tikanga and quality education and care are priorities for children. Whānau regularly review their charter. Core health and safety and wellbeing and care policies are current and in place. Whānau ensure that information required for various registers is recorded. Kaimahi employment documentation is current and in place. Kaimahi have all been appraised. Whanau have a strategic plan informed by Te Ara Tuapae.They have planned their goals and priorities for the future. The action plan identifies who, what, and when in relation to key actions and tasks undertaken for 2017. The charter and policies are reviewed regularly. Internal evaluation is both efficient and effective and meets the needs and interests of whānau and children. Children are the priority in all decisions made at kōhanga reo.

Key Next Steps

Kaimahi identified that assessment of children’s learning and programme evaluation remain areas for further development. While kaimahi are able to articulate the purpose of assessment, there are no links between what they know and what they are doing. They have two methods of assessment. Neither of these identify what children know, what they are learning and what progress they are making as learners. While kaimahi understand that evaluation leads to improvements it is difficult to monitor what changes have been made to the learning programme and the reasons behind such changes. Children do not benefit from robust assessment and programme evaluation.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the service’s systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)

  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)

  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)

  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendations

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • whānau to provide support to kaimahi to strengthen assessment practice
  • Kaimahi to establish a process for evaluating the effectiveness of the learning programme.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Miropiko is in Hamilton. Children are familiar with te reo Māori. They are developing skills for communication with others. Children explore te reo Maori in a supportive environment.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Miropiko will be in three years.
