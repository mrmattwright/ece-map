2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ko te kōhanga reo te poutokomanawa o te whare,

E pupū ake ai te mana, te wehi me te wairua o te Māori

E pā tata ana te kōhanga reo o Atutahi ki te kura tuatahi o Taumarunu. I whakatūwheratia te kōhanga i te marama o Hui-tanguru 2012. Ko ngā tamariki o te hapori o taua kāinga e taetae mai ana ki te kōhanga reo. Kei te āhua tamariki tonu ngā mema o te whānau. E kaha ana rātou ki te tautoko i tō rātou kōhanga reo me te mea nui hoki kua herea ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo me te mātauranga o ā rātou tamariki. Ko tā rātou Arotake Mātauranga tuatahi tēnei.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tana kaupapa arotahi mō tenei arotake mātauranga ko Mana Whenua. Ka whakatairangatia e ngā kaimahi kia tōtika ai te kaupapa o Mana Whenua mō te whānau me ngā tamariki. Mā te pepeha ngā tamariki e whakakoia ki ngā hononga whakapapa. Ka ākona rātou i ngā kaupapa toi whenua. E mōhio ana te whānau ki ō rātou tātai hono. E whakaute ana ngā tamariki i te whenua me tō rātou mōhio ki ngā kawenga tiaki whenua. He tino kaha te whānau ki te tautoko i a rātou tamariki kia tū māia, tū pakari hoki i roto i te ao Māori.

Kei te tipu tonu te māiatanga o ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori. Ka whakamahia ngā kaiako i ētahi rautaki whai tikanga hei whakaako i te reo Māori. Kei ngā taumata rerekē te matatau me te māiatanga o ngā kaiako ki te reo Māori. Ērā o ngā kuia e matatau ana ki te kōrero Māori, he rite tonu tā rātou haere ki te kōhanga reo. Ka āhei rātou ki te whakawhanake me te whakawhānui ake i ngā pūkenga reo o ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki.

E tōtika ana te whakaritenga me te kawenga o te hōtaka akoranga hei whakatutuki i ngā mātea akoranga o ngā tamariki katoa. He huarahi e whakatūwheratia mō ngā mahi ā-rōpū, ā, ka āhei ngā tamaiki ki te whai haere i wā rātou e kaingākautia ana. He rite tonu te aro a ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga e hāngai ana ki a rātou anō. E tōtika ana te tiaki i a rātou. He kaha hoki ngā mātua ki te tautoko i ngā akoranga o a rātou tamariki.

Ka whakahaeretia te whānau i te kōhanga kia hāngai pū ana ki wā rātou tamariki. Ka whakawhanake tonutia e te whānau ngā pūnaha kia pono ai te whakahaere i tō rātou kōhanga reo. Kua whakaraupapatia ngā kaupapa here mō te hauora me te waiora kia āhei ngā tamariki ki te ako i roto i tētahi taiao e tika ana. Ko te hauora me te waiora o ngā tamariki te tino arotahi mō te whānau.

Kua whanake paitia te mahere rautaki. E mārama ana te whānau ki ngā kaupapa matua o ēnei wā, ā muri ake nei hoki. Ko te arotake whaiaro e tautoko ana i ngā tirohanga a te whānau ka whakakaha ake i a rātou whakaritenga mahi katoa. E ārahi te mahere i te whakatinanatanga o te hōtaka. E ai ki ngā kōrero whakatau e pā ana ki ngā aro matawai kei te ako ngā tamariki, ā, kei te tipu haere.

Me tautoko te whānau i ngā kaimahi kia tutuki ai o rātou arohaehae.Mā tēnei ka āhei rātou ki te whakawhanake he huarahi pai hei ārahi i ngā mahi whakaako me te whiriwhiri i ngā taputapu e hiahia ana ki te tautoko i te reo Māori. Me whakakaha ake te arotake i te hōtaka. Mā tēnei ka taea te whānau te whiriwhiri he huarahi hei whakakaha ake i te hōtaka kia whai hua ai ngā tamariki.

Ngā Mahi ā Meake Nei

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kohanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i ïnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Atutahi kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te pupuritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Atutahi.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Whenua.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake matauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me te
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aro matawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Whenua

He whakamārama

I whiriwhiria te whānau ko Mana Whenua tana kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ko te whakawhanake mai i te āronga nui nō konei rātou, te tuakiri me te whakaute i te taiao ngā tino kaupapa o Mana Whenua.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Mana Whenua.Ka whakatairangatia te kaupapa e ngā kaimahi kia tōtika mō te whānau me ngā tamariki.

  • mā te pepeha ngā tamariki e whakakoia ki ngā hononga whakapapa
  • e mōhio ana te whānau ki tō rātou whakapapa, ngā wāriu me ngā whakapono
  • ka ākona ngā tamariki i ngā kaupapa toi whenua
  • mā roto i ngā kōrero tuku iho ka ākona ngā tamariki i te whakapapa

E whakaute ana ngā tamariki i te whenua me te tino mōhio ki tō rātou haepapatanga o te tiaki whenua. He tino kaha te whānau ki te tautoko i a rātou tamariki kia tū māia, tū pakari hoki i roto i te ao Māori.

Te reo Māori. Kua rūmakitia te reo Māori mō ngā tamariki i te roanga o te rā. He māia rātou ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori i roto i ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā mihi me ngā pepeha. Ka anga atu ngā tamariki ki ngā uru pounamu me ngā tono a te kaimahi. Mā te whānau anō rātou e poipoi.

Ka whakamahia ngā kaiako i ētahi rautaki whai tikanga hei whakaako i te reo Māori. Kei ngā taumata rerekē te mātau me te māia o ngā kaiako ki te reo Māori. Ērā o ngā kuia e matatau ana ki te kōrero Māori, he rite tonu tā rātou haere ki te kōhanga reo. Ka āhei rātou ki te whakawhanake me te whakawhānui ake i ngā pūkenga reo o ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki.

Hōtaka akoranga.Kua āta whakaritea te hōtaka ki ngā mātea akoranga me te whanaketanga o ngā tamariki. Hihiko rawa ngā kaimahi, ā, ka whakanuia ngā tutukitanga a ngā tamariki. He māia ngā tamariki, he mōhio hoki ki te wātaka, ngā kawenga o ia rā me ngā nekenekehanga. Ka whai wā rātou ki ngā putanga ā-roto mai, ā-waho mai hoki.

Ka whai wāhi ngā pēpi i roto i te wātaka me ngā korikori e hiahia ana. He kaha ngā mātua ki te tautoko i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Te ao whānui. He kaha te tautoko e whakaratoa ki ngā tamariki hei whakawhanake i tō rātou māia i roto i te ao whānui. Ka torotoro haere ki ngā wāhi whakamere e āhei ana rātou te kimi mātauranga hou e pā ana ki te ao whānui, me te pāheko hoki ki tāngata kē. Kei te tipu haere te mōhio me te mātauranga o ngā tamariki ki ngā tohu me ngā tino taonga i a rātou e torotoro haere ki wāhi kē. Ka whai whakaaro rātou ki te taiao koiri, wairua hoki me te pānga o ngā tini atua ki tō rātou oranga.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

E aro mātai ana a ERO i ngā wā katoa i te kounga o ngā whakamahere me ngā aro mātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo ki te tātari i te kaha o te whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakamahere, ki te whakahaere, ki te aro turuki hoki i te katoa o ngā wāhanga whakahaere o tō rātou kōhanga reo mō wā rātou tamariki me te whānau. Kei roto hoki i te whakamāherehere me te aromātai ko te whakamahere hōtaka me te aro turuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He tōtika ngā whakaritenga a te whānau hei whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. Kei reira katoa ngā pūnaha hauora me te waiora me ōnā tikanga katoa. Kua kitea katoatia ngā pukapuka e hāngai ana. Ka noho te kōhanga reo he wāhi e aro nui ana ki te hauora me te waiora mō ngā tamariki.

Te Tūtohinga. Ko tā te tauaki taketake me te kaupapa o Te Kōhanga Reo hei tautoko, hei whakamōhio atu ki te whānau te tikanga o Te Ara Tūāpae.

Ko ngā kaupapa here me ōna tikanga. Kua whakaraupapatia e te whānau ngā huihuinga kaupapa here i raro iho i ngā wāhanga o Ngā Pou e Whā. Ko ngā kaupapa here mō te hauora me te waiora e whakamārama ana, e ārahi ana i ngā mahi kia rite tonu ki tā te ture e āta tohu ai.

Ngā kaimahi katoa o te kōhanga reo. E whakaatu mai ana i roto i ngā mahi a te whānau ētahi wāhanga papai ki te whakahaere tika i ngā kaimahi katoa. Kua whakaritea ngā pūnaha hei tautoko i te mahi a ngā kaimahi. Kua oti katoa ngā tuhinga mō ngā whakaahuatanga mahi me ngā kirimana whiwhinga mahi.

Te whakahaeretanga i te pūtea. E tōtika ana te whakahaere a te whānau i ngā pūtea o te kōhanga reo. He tahua pūtea hei ārahi ki te hoko rauemi mātauranga kia whai hua ai ngā tamariki mai i te kounga o ēnei taputapu. He tūnga whai hua tō te kōhanga reo ki te taha pūtea.

Te whakamahere rautaki. E mārama pai ana te whānau ki ngā kaupapa matua ō nāianei me ērā kei tua i te awe māpara. Kua whakaritea ngā whāinga rautaki me ngā mahi i runga i ngā aratohu o Te Ara Tūāpae. Kua whakaraupapatia e te whānau ngā mahi mō ia whāinga e hāngai ana ki ngā mahi matua o ia tau me te pātai, ‘ko wai’, ‘he aha’, me ‘āwhea’ ēnei e whakatau. Mā te aro turuki me te tuku pūrongo mō te ahunga whakamua me ngā whakatutukitanga mai o ngā kaupapa matua o te tau e taea ai te kite i te wāriu a te wā ka arotake anō te whānau i ngā mahi kua oti.

Arotake whaiaro. Ka āta mahia te arotake whaiaro hei whakatau i te tū toiora mai o te kōhanga reo. Nā te tuku pūronga i ia te wā e taea ai te whakatau i ngā whakahaeretanga a te kōhanga reo. Kua tau kētia te arotake whaiaro i ia te wā, ā, kei te tipu tonutia te arotake whaiaro whakapuakitanga. Ka āta wānangahia te whānau i ngā kaupapa kōrero e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o ētahi wāhanga o te kōhanga reo, me te tuku pūrongo i ia te wā. Kua tīmata te whānau ki te aro turuki i tō rātou mahere rautaki me te tuku pūrongo hoki.

Te whakamāherehere. E tautoko ana ngā whakamāherehere a ngā kaimahi ki tō te akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka whakawhāititia ngā kaupapa raranga mahi ki ngā mātea o ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakama ēnei hei whakaraupapa i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whai mātauranga ngā tamariki me te māiatanga ki ngā akoranga e hāngai ana ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui hoki.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi. He moata kē te wāhanga o ngā arohaehae ki tōna otinga. Me tautoko te whānau i ngā kaimahi hei whakatutuki i o rātou arohaehae. Mā tēnei e āhei ai rātou ki ngā urupare tōtika hei ārahi i a rātou me ngā whiwhinga taputapu e tautoko ana i te hōtaka akoranga mō te reo Māori.

Te aro mātai i te hōtaka. Me whakakaha ake ngā kaimahi ki te aro mātai i te hōtaka. Ruarua noa iho ngā kōrero e whakaatu mai ana i te painga o te hōtaka akoranga me tōna pānga ki te akoranga a ngā tamariki. Me tiro arotahi ngā kaupapa kōrero e wānangahia e ngā kaimahi ki ngā mahi papai, ngā mahi hei whakapai ake me ngā whakarerekētanga hei hāpai i ngā tamariki.

Aromatawai. Kei te whanake tonu ngā mahi aromatawai. Ahakoa te whakamahi i ngā paki akoranga, kāore i te āta kite i te tino pūtake o ngā pukapuka pūkete nei. Me arotahi te kaimahi ki te whakamōhio atu ki ngā mātua ngā kōrero e hāngai ana ki te ahunga whakamua me ngā whakatutukitanga o a rātou tamariki.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Ko te kōhanga reo te poutokomanawa o te whare,

E pupu ake ai te mana, te wehi me te wairua o te Māori

Te kōhanga reo o Atutahi is located close to the Taumarunui primary school in Taumarunui. The kōhanga reo opened in February 2012. Children from the local community attend the kōhanga reo. Whānau members are relatively young. They support their kōhanga reo and in particular are committed to the kaupapa of the kōhanga reo and education of their children. This is their first Education Review.

The whānau chose Mana Whenua as the focus area for this education review. Kaimahi effectively promote the concept of Mana Whenua for whānau and children.Children affirm their whānau links through pepeha. They learn about the whenua. Whānau know their ancestral connections. Children respect the land and understand their duties and role to protect the land.Whānau are supporting their children to become confident and capable in te aō Māori.

Children’s te reo Māori competence is developing. Kaiako use some useful strategies to teach te reo Māori. Kaimahi are at varying stages of fluency and competency in te reo Māori. Kuia, who are fluent speakers of te reo Māori, are frequently at the kōhanga reo. They are able to develop and extend the language skills of kaimahi and children.

The learning programme is well organised and managed and caters appropriately for the needs of all children. There are opportunities for group work and for children to follow their individual interests. Children are frequently engaged in learning that is meaningful to them. They are well supervised. Parents also support their children’s learning.

The whānau manages the kōhanga reo in the best interests of their children. Thewhānau continues to develop systems to ensure the operations and management of their kōhanga reo is sound. The health and safety policies that are in place support children to learn in an appropriate environment. Whānau makechildren’s health and safety a priority.

The strategic plan is well developed. Whānau understand their current and future priorities. Self review supports the whānau to monitor and improve their operations and practice. Planning guides the implementation of the programme. Assessment information shows that children are learning and developing well.

The whānau should support kaimahi to complete their appraisals. This should enable them to develop a clear direction for teaching and to identify the resources needed to support learning. Programme evaluation needs strengthening. This should enable whānau to identify how to improve the programme to benefit children.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

4 Findings

Mana Whenua

Background

Whānau chose Mana Whenua as the focus for the review. Mana Whenua is about developing a strong sense of belonging, identity and respect for the environment.

Areas of good performance

Mana Whenua.Kaimahi promote this concept in positive and effective ways for whānau and children:

  • children affirm their whānau links through pepeha
  • whānau know their ancestral connections, values and beliefs
  • children learn about the whenua
  • children learn whakapapa through stories.

Children respect the land and understand their duties and role to protect the land.Whānau are supporting their children to become confident and capable in te aō Māori.

Te reo Māori. Children are immersed in te reo Māori throughout the day. They confidently use te reo Māori during karakia, hīmene, waiata, mihi and pepeha. Children respond to kaimahi questions and requests. They also learn from whānau.

Kaiako use some useful strategies to teach te reo Māori. Kaimahi are at varying stages of fluency and competency. Kuia, who are fluent speakers of te reo Māori, are frequently at the kōhanga reo. They are able to develop and extend the language skills of kaimahi and children.

Learning programme. The programme caters for children’s learning and development needs well. Kaimahi are enthusiastic and they celebrate children’s achievements. Children are confident and familiar with daily programme, routines and transitions. They have access to indoor and outdoor areas during the programme.

Babies are included in daily programme and have opportunities for free play. Parents support their children’s learning.

Wider world. Children are well supported to develop confidence in the wider world. They go to places of interest where they make new discoveries about the wider world and interact with many different people. Children are developing in their knowledge and understanding of symbols and artefacts when they visit different places. They are aware of the physical and spiritual worlds and the impact the different atua have on their daily lives.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau manage the kōhanga reo well. All health and safety policies and procedures are in place. All relevant documentation has been cited. The kōhanga reo is a place where children’s health and safety is a priority.

Charter.The mission statement and kaupapa of the Kōhanga Reo supports, informs the whānau and their interpretation of Te Ara Tūāpae.

Policies and procedures.Whānau have a set of policies categorised under the four sections of Ngā Pou e Whā. Health and safety policies inform procedures and guide practice to comply with appropriate regulations.

Personnel.Whānau practice reflects elements of good employer practice. Whānau have systems in place to support kaimahi in their work. Job descriptions and employment contracts are complete.

Financial.The whānau manage the kōhanga reo finances effectively. They budget for education resources to ensure that the children and the learning programme has high quality resources. The kōhanga reo is in a financially viable position.

Strategic Plan.Whānau understand their current and future priorities.Te Ara Tūāpae has informed the strategic objectives and actions. Whānau have listed the actions required for each objective and ‘who’, ‘what’, and ‘when’ in relation to keys tasks undertaken each year have been determined. Monitoring and reporting progress and successes against their annual priorities should add value when whānau review progress made.

Self review.Self review is used appropriately to determine the future direction of the kōhanga reo. Regular reporting appropriately informs decisions about kōhanga reo operations. Regular self review is established and emergent self review is developing. Whānau regularly discuss and report on aspects of the kōhanga reo operations. Whānau have begun to monitor and report on their strategic plan.

Planning.Kaimahi use planning appropriately to support children’s learning. Programme planning targets the needs of children and is used to organise the learning programme. Children gain knowledge and confidence learning about te aō Māori and the wider world.

Areas for development

Kaimahi appraisals:The appraisalsare in the early stages of completion. Whānau should support kaimahi to complete their appraisals. This should enable them to receive constructive feedback about a clear future direction and resources to support te reo Māori learning programme.

Programme evaluation. Kaimahi need to strengthen programme evaluation. There is very little information to indicate how effectively the learning programme impacts on children’s learning. Kaimahi hui discussions need to focus on what worked, what did not work and what changes need to happen to increase the impact of the programme on children.

Assessment.Assessment practices are still developing. While learning stories are used appropriately the purpose of profile books is not clear. Kaimahi should focus on informing parents about the progress of their child’s learning and achievement.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
