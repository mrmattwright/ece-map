Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangahaua ki te pūtahi o Whanganui. Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tō rātou tūmāia, nā runga i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki a rātou anō. Ka whakatinana ngā taunekeneke me ngā akoranga o ngā uaratanga o te kōhanga reo, pērā i te rangimārie, te whakapono, te aroha, te manaaki me te tiaki. Ka kōrero ngā tamariki i te reo me te mita o te rohe o Aotea.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangahaua i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Te Horopaki

Ko Aotea te waka
Ko Ruapehu te maunga
Ko Whanganui te awa
Ko Te Āti Haunui-ā-Pāpārangi te iwi.

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangahaua ki Whanganui. Kua ū pūmau te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, mai i tōna tīmatanga. Ko ngā kaimahi o tēnei wā he tamariki o mua o tēnei kōhanga reo, ā, kua hoki mai rātou ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou mātauranga, ō rātou wheako hoki. Ko te whanaungatanga me te manaakitanga kei te pūtahi o ngā mahi katoa ki tēnei kōhanga reo.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e āta whakaatu i tō rātou tūmāia?

Ka whakaatuhia e ngā tamariki tō rātou tūmāia ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka arohaina, ka atawhaitia hoki ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā uaratanga o te rangimārie, te whakapono, te aroha, te manaaki, me te tiaki. He pai ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. He hihiri te aro a ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea o te takitahi, me te takirōpū. He hāneanea, he tau hoki ngā tamariki.

He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te ārahi. Ka whakatakoto ngā kaimahi i ngā whāinga ako hei whakatairanga i te ārahitanga ki waenga i ngā tamariki. E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki ō rātou tūranga me ngā tūmanako ki te kōhanga reo. Ka ārahi ngā tamariki i ngā tikanga me ngā mahinga, tae atu hoki ki ngā karakia. Ka tautoko ngā tuākana i te whai wāhi a ō rātou tēina ki ngā taumahi. He tūmāia ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka ngana ngā tamariki ki te ako. He tino taiao ako te kōhanga reo. Kua tino whakaritea e ngā kaimahi ā rātou mahi, ā, ka whakarato i ngā whiringa ki ngā tamariki, puta noa i te rā. He whānui ngā tūmomo whai wāhitanga ako auaha. He pai ngā wāhi ako o roto, o waho hoki. Ka taea e ngā tamariki te whiriwhiri te mahi takitahi, te mahi takirōpū rānei. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā wheako akoranga mā ngā tamariki ki te hapori, ki tua atu hoki. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te noho ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te reo o Whanganui. Ko ētahi o ngā whāinga a te whānau ka hāngai ki te whakamahinga o te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki - tae atu ki ngā waiata me ngā haka hei hāpai i te ako reo. Ka whakatauira rātou i te reo o Whanganui puta noa i te rā. Ka whakamahi ngā tamariki i te reo o Whanganui.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

Ka whakamahia ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā matea o ngā tamariki hei whakamōhio i te āhua o ngā mahere. He pai ngā pūnaha i whakaritea ai hei ārahi i ngā mahere. Ka hāngai ngā mahere katoa ki Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Ka aromātai ngā kaimahi i te whai huatanga o te taiao ako ki te hāpai i ngā tūmomo matea ako katoa. Ka whakamahi rātou i aua mōhiohio ki te whakatinana haere i te hōtaka.

Ka aroturukitia, ka tuhia hoki te ahunga whakamua i ngā wā katoa. He mārama ngā tūmanako e pā ana ki ngā huarahi ka whāia e ngā kaimahi hei whakaemi i ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki ngā tamariki. Ka whakaemi ngā kaimahi i ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki te whakawhanaketanga o te katoa o ia tamaiti, me tōna mōhiotanga ki te reo Māori. Ka whakahāngaitia aua mōhiohio ki Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Ka pūrongotia, ka whakapuakihia hoki te ahu whakamua a ngā tamariki ki te whānau. Ka tino tautokona te ako a ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

Ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā mahere rautaki. Ka whakahāngaitia ngā whāinga o Te Ara Tūāpae ki ērā o te kōhanga reo. Ka arotahi te mahere rautaki ki te whakapai ake i te reo me ōna tikanga, te whai wāhi a te whānau, me te toro atu ki te hapori.

Ka whai hua ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro. Tū ai ngā hui a te whānau i ia marama, ā, ka whakatau rātou i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki ngā āhuatanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. I ngā wā katoa, ko ngā whakapaitanga ka whai hua ki ngā tamariki te aronga matua. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti whakaaro e pā ana ki te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga. Ko te aroturuki me te aromātai tētahi āhuatanga matua o ngā pūnaha ki te kōhanga reo. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te auau o ngā mahi arotake whaiaro.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

4  Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • ū tonu ki ngā whakaritenga papai e arotahi ana ki ngā tino putanga hua mō ngā tamariki.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangahaua ki te pūtahi o Whanganui. Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tō rātou tūmāia, nā runga i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki a rātou anō. Ka whakatinana ngā taunekeneke me ngā akoranga o ngā uaratanga o te kōhanga reo, pērā i te rangimārie, te whakapono, te aroha, te manaaki me te tiaki. Ka kōrero ngā tamariki i te reo me te mita o te rohe o Aotea.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangahaua is in central Whanganui. Children show they are confident in who they are. Interactions and learning are expressions of the kōhanga reo values of rangimārie, whakapono, aroha, manaaki and tiaki. Children speak te reo and mita of the Aotea tribal area.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangahaua will be in three years.

1 Context

Ko Aotea te waka
Ko Ruapehu te maunga
Ko Whanganui te awa
Ko Te Ati Haunui-ā-Pāpārangi te iwi.

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangahaua is in Whanganui. Whānau and kaimahi have been dedicated to the kaupapa of te kōhanga reo since it began. Current kaimahi are graduates of this kōhanga reo who have returned to share their knowledge and experience. Whanaungatanga and manaakitanga are at the heart of all that happens at kōhanga reo.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children show that they are confident?

Children show that they are confident at kōhanga reo.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are loved and cherished at the kōhanga reo. Children learn values such as rangimārie, whakapono, aroha, manaaki and tiaki. Relationships between kaimahi and children are positive. Kaimahi respond to individual and group needs with high levels of energy. Children are comfortable and settled.

Children enjoy leading. Kaimahi set learning objectives to promote leadership amongst children. Children are familiar with their roles and expectations at the kōhanga reo. Children lead tikanga and routines such as karakia. Tuakana support their teina to participate in activities. Children are confident at kōhanga reo.

Children are engaged in learning. The kōhanga reo is a positive learning environment. Kaimahi are highly organised and provide children with choices throughout the day. There are a range creative learning opportunities. There is a good use of indoor and outdoor learning spaces. Children can choose to work independently or in groups. Kaimahi provide learning experiences for children in the local and wider community. Children enjoy being at kōhanga reo.

Children are immersed in te reo o Whanganui. Whānau goals include increasing the use of te reo Māori. Kaimahi use a range of strategies including waiata and haka to support language learning. They model te reo o Whanganui throughout the day. Children use the Whanganui dialect.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s interests and needs are used to inform planning. There are good systems in place to guide planning. All planning is lined to Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Kaimahi evaluate how well the learning environment supports different learning needs. They use this information to inform the programme.

Children’s progress is consistently monitored and recorded. Expectations are clear for how kaimahi gather information about children. Kaimahi gather information about each child’s holistic development, knowledge of te reo Māori. This information is aligned to Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira. Children’s progress is reported and shared with whānau. Children are well supported to learn at kōhanga reo.

Children are the focus of strategic planning. The goals from Te Ara Tūāpae are aligned to the kōhanga reo goals. The strategic plan is focused on improving te reo me ōna tikanga, whānau participation and community engagement.

Self-review practices are useful. Whānau have regular monthly hui where they make decisions about all aspects of the kōhanga reo. Improvements that benefit children are always the priority. Kaimahi regularly meet to discuss the quality of the learning programme. Monitoring and evaluation is an integral part of the systems at the kōhanga reo. Children benefit from regular self-review.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • to maintain good practice that focuses on best outcomes for children.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangahaua is in central Whanganui. Children show they are confident in who they are. Interactions and learning are expressions of the kōhanga reo values of rangimārie, whakapono, aroha, manaaki and tiaki. Children speak te reo and mita of the Aotea tribal area. 

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rangahaua will be in three years.
