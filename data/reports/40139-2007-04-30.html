1. Te Aro Mātai A Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kei roto Te Köhanga Reo o Waihi i te nohonga käinga o Waihi kei te taha moana o te moana o Taupo. He ngäherehere kei te huri taiäwhio i tënei wähi. nä te iwi o te rohe i koha te papa whenua me te whare o te köhanga ki te whänau hei whakamahi mä rätou. E tu pätata ana te köhanga ki te marae o te iwi, ä, e tino tautokotia ana e te iwi o Tuwharetoa.

Ko Mana Atua te pütake o te hötaka akoranga e whakaratohia ana ki ngä mokopuna. He maha ngä huarahi e tuwhera ana ki ngä mokopuna ki te ako me te möhio ki ngä körero e pä ana ki o rätou tukunga iho mai i o rätou mätua/tüpuna. E titiro ana, ä, e whai wähi ana ngä mokopuna i ia wä, i ia wä, i roto i ngä mahi e heipü ana i runga i tö rätou marae, arä, i ngä pöwhiri ki ngä manuhiri e whakaeke ana, ä, me ëra atu tikanga e pä ana ki tënei. Torotoro haere ai rätou i roto i te taiao e pätata mai ana ki tö rätou köhanga, arä, te ngahere, te roto o Taupo-Nui-a-Tia me te moana hoki. E whakaako ana, ä, e akiaki ana ngä kaimahi i a rätou mokopuna ki te ako i ngä karakia me ngä waiata e motuhake ana ki tö rätou iwi me tö rätou rohe. E möhio ana ngä mokopuna he uri tuku iho rätou mai i o rätou mätua tüpuna.

E whakapakari ana ngä akoranga o mana whenua i ngä hononga o ngä mokopuna ki te iwi o tö rätou rohe, ki te marae, ä, ki te taiao mähorahora hoki e huri taiäwhio nei i tö rätou köhanga. Ë ngäkau whakapuke ana ngä mema o te whänau me ngä kaimahi ki ngä wheako akoranga e taea ana e rätou te whakarato ki a rätou mokopuna mai i te taiao e huri taiäwhio nei i tö rätou köhanga. E ako ana ngä mokopuna i ngä körero mö tö rätou köhanga, mö te taiao e äwhina nei i a rätou, ä, me ëra kei waho atu i tö rätou rohe mai i a rätou haerere i ia wä, i ia wä, ki ënei wähi. Ko ngä pakeke e whakarato ana i te ähurutanga ki a rätou mokopuna, mai i tä rätou whakaü märama i ngä kawa hei whai me ngä tumanakohanga mai i a rätou mokopuna i runga i ënei haerere a rätou. E möhio ana ngä mokopuna ki tëna, ki tëna, o a rätou wähi akoranga me ngä tumomo mahi tëra ka whakaritea e ngä kaimahi hei mahi i konei. E kitea ana te mäia me te mähorahora o ngä mokopuna i te taha o ngä tängata e tiaki ana i a rätou i roto i te taiao o tö rätou köhanga.

E kitea ana i roto i a Mana Tangata, te mana ake o te whänau, ngä kaiako me ngä kaimahi, ä, me ngä mokopuna hoki. He maha ngä tohungatanga me ngä pükenga o ngä mema o te whänau mö te häpai i a rätou i roto i tä rätou whakahaere i tö rätou köhanga. He röpu whäiti nei tö ngä mema o te whänau kua roa kë e whakarato ana i te pakaritanga o ngä möhio mö te iwi o te rohe me tö rätou köhanga. Äwhina mai ai te kaipupuri o te raihana i ngä whakahaere a tö rätou köhanga i ia wä, i ia wä. Nä te whakatöpu i ngä kaha o ngä mema o te whänau, ka häpaitia te haere tonu o te tu pakari o te köhanga o te whänau, ä, me a rätou mokopuna hoki.

Tëra tonu ëtahi wähanga hei whanaketanga mä te whänau me ngä kaimahi. Ko ëtahi o ënei, ko ngä whakamahere a te whänau, te arotake whaiaro me ngä whakamahere o ngä kaupapa hei whai ake. Me whakarato e ngä kaimahi ki roto i te hötaka akoranga te maha atu o ngä whiringa hei titirohanga mä te katoa o ngä mokopuna hei häpai i o rätou kaha ki te mahi takitahi, ki te mahi-a-röpu ränei, ä, ki te whakarato hoki i ngä mahi e tika ana hei mahi mä tëna, mä tëna, reanga o ngä mokopuna. Ka whakapakaritia ake hoki tënei, mä te aro mätai i ngä hötaka akoranga i ia wä, i ia wä.

Ngā Mahi Hei Mahi a Meake Nei

Ka hokimai anö a ERO ki te köhanga i roto i te rua tekau mä wha marama e tu mai nei, ki te aro mätai i te ahu whakamua a te whänau i roto i tä rätou whakautu ki ngä wähanga e äwangawangatia ana kua pupü ake nei i roto i tënei ripoata.

2. Ngā Mea Matua mō Te Arotake

Te Titiro Hāngai a Te Arotake

I mua atu i te arotake, i powhiritia te röpu whakahaere o Waihi Te Köhanga Reo ki te whai whakaaro ki ngä mea e matua ana ki a rätou mö te arotake, mä te whai i ngä aratohu me ngä rauemi i whakaratohia e ERO ki a rätou. I whakamahia hoki e ERO ngä tuhituhinga i whakaratohia e te köhanga ki a rätou, kia whai wähi ai rätou i roto i te hökai o tënei arotake.

I muri mai i ngä whakawhitiwhiti whakaaro i waenganui i ngä kaiarotake a ERO me te whänau, ka whakaritea e rätou ngä take matua hei arotakehanga. I titiro ënei whakawhitiwhiti whakaaro ki ngä körero i te pupuritia e te köhanga (täpiri atu ki ngä körero mö ngä arotake whaiaro), ä, me te whai wähi o ënei take ka arotakehia nei i runga i putanga e whai hua ana ki ngä tamariki o Waihi Te Köhanga Reo.

E titiro ana te katoa o ngä arotake a ERO i ngä köhanga, ki runga i ngä whakamahere me ngä aro mätai. E whai wähi nui ana i roto i ënei, ko te kounga o ngä whakahaere a te whänau, me te kounga o te mätauranga e whakaratohia ana ki ngä mokopuna.

Ko ngä wähanga i whakaritea e te whänau hei titirohanga mä rätou ko:

  • Mana Atua; me
  • Mana Whenua

I täpiritia atu e ERO tëtahi atu wähanga hei titirohanga mä te arotake, arä, ko:

  • Mana Tangata

E whakaatu ana i raro iho nei ngä mea i kitea e ERO i roto i ënei wähanga.

Ngā Mea e Matua ana Ki Te Köhanga

Mana Atua

He Whakamārama

I whakaritea e te whänau, ngä kaiako me ngä kaimahi o Te Köhanga Reo o Waihi ko Mana Atua te kaupapa hei titirohanga mä tënei arotake. E whakatuwhera ana ngä mahi e pä ana ki Mana Atua, i ngä huarahi ki ngä mokopuna ki te ako i ngä wäriu me ngä whakaponotanga o te iwi o Ngäti Tuwharetoa.

Ngā Wāhanga e Pai ana Ngā Whakahaere

Tähuhu whakapapa mai i ngä matua/tüpuna. E whakatuwheratia ana e te whänau te maha atu o ngä huarahi ki a rätou mokopuna ki te ako, ä, ki te möhio ki o rätou anö tukunga iho, mai i o rätou matua/tüpuna. E titiro ana, ä, e whai wähi ana ngä mokopuna i roto i ngä heipütanga pënei i ngä pöwhiri ki ngä manuhiri e whakaeke ana

ki runga i tö rätou marae, ä, me te whai wähi o ngä tikanga e pä ana ki ënei i runga i o rätou whanonga me a rätou mahi. Whai wähi ai rätou i roto i ngä mahi e heipü ana i runga i tö rätou marae i ia wä, i ia wä, ä, me tä rätou torotoro haere hoki i roto i te taiao e pätata mai ana ki tö rätou köhanga, te ngahere, te roto me te moana o Taupo-Nui-a-Tia. E whakaako ana, ä, e akiaki ana ngä kaiako me ngä kaimahi i a rätou mokopuna ki ngä karakia me ngä waiata e motuhake ana ki tö rätou rohe, ä, e möhio ana ngä mokopuna ki o rätou tukunga iho mai, i o rätou mätua/tüpuna.

Te möhio ki te taha wairua me te taha ahurea. E whakaratohia ana e te whänau o te köhanga he taiao e whakaähuru ana, ä, e whakaatu pakari mai ana i te ahureatanga, ngä wäriu me ngä whakaponotanga o te taha wairua. E whakaurua mai ana e ngä kaimahi ngä äkoranga mö ngä Atua i ngä wä e ako ana ngä mokopuna i ngä körero mö Rangi me Papa. E hono ana ënei whakaäkoranga ki ngä whakahaere a ngä mätua o mai räno, ä, ka whakatauiratia e ngä kaimahi i roto i tö rätou köhanga. Kei te ako ngä mokopuna i te whai tikanga o ngä Atua Mäori ki te iwi Mäori.

Te akiaki me te äwhina. E whakatauira mai ana ngä mema o te whänau, ngä kaiako me ngä kaimahi i ngä ähuatanga e pä ana ki te whakaute, te atawhai me te whai whakaaro mö tëtahi, mö tëtahi, ä, mö ëra atu mokopuna hoki. E häpaitia ana e rätou te pai o te whanaungatanga i waenganui i ngä tuakana me o rätou taina, ä, me te akiaki hoki a ngä kaimahi i a rätou mokopuna ki te mahi i ngä mahi e pai ana, ä, me te whakamana hoki i ngä angitü e taea ana e rätou. E kao ana ngä ähua o ngä mokopuna.

Te mäiatanga me te whai wähi i roto i a rätou mahi. E maha ana ngä wheako akoranga e whakawhiwhia ana ki ngä mokopuna, ä, e akiakitia ana rätou ki te whai wähi i roto i ënei. E whakaatu mai ana rätou i o rätou mäiatanga i roto i te taiao o tö rätou köhanga, ä, i roto hoki i te iwi käinga o tö rätou rohe.

Te ako i te reo Mäori. E rümakitia ana ngä mokopuna i roto i te taiao ko te reo Mäori anake te reo e körerotia ana. Whakauru mai ai ngä kaimahi i ngä kupu hou o te reo Mäori i roto i tëna, i tëna, kaupapa hou e whakaäkona ana, i ngä heipütanga hou e heipü ana i ia ra, ä, i roto hoki i ngä mahi e heipü ana i ia ra.Whakamahia ai e ngä kaimahi ngä rautaki pënei i ngä pikitia me te körerorero anö i ngä kupu, hei äwhina i ngä mokopuna ki te körero tötika i te reo Mäori. E akiakitia ana ngä mokopuna ki te körero i te reo Mäori i ngä wä katoa.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te anga tötika mö te mihimihi. Huihui ngätahi ai ngä mokopuna i roto i te röpu kotahi mö ngä mihimihi me ngä mahi mö te ata. He tere ngä mokopuna ki te höhä i ënei wä, ä, käore hoki o rätou ngäkau nuitanga e ü ki ngä mahi e whakaritea ana hei mahi mä rätou.

Te whakautu ki ngä hiahia a tëna, a tëna, a ngä mokopuna. Ahakoa e möhio ana ngä kaimahi ki ngä mäiatanga o tëna, o tëna, reanga o a rätou mokopuna, ä, me tä rätou whakarato i te whänuitanga atu o ngä wheako akoranga ki a rätou, e hiahia tonutia ana ëra atu wheako akoranga hei whakangäkau nui, hei whakaihiihi, ä, hei whakawhänui ake i o rätou kaha.

Te whakapakari ake i o rätou kaha ki te körero i te reo Mäori. Ahakoa e kaha ana ngä kaimahi ki te äwhina i ngä mokopuna ki te ako i te reo Mäori, käore kë rätou i te whakamahi i te whänuitanga atu o ngä rautaki mö te whakaako i te reo Mäori hei äwhina i a rätou i roto i tënei. He pai tonu mehemea e whai whakaaro ana rätou ki te körero kaha ake i te reo Mäori ki ngä mokopuna puta noa i te roanga ake o te ra.

Mana Whenua

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e te whänau o Te Köhanga Reo o Waihi, ko Mana Whenua anö tëtahi o ngä kaupapa hei titirohanga mä rätou. E whakapakari ana tënei taumata whakahirahira i ngä hononga i waenganui i te iwi, te marae me te taiao e huritaiäwhio nei i te köhanga. E kaha ana rätou ki te whakarei ake i te hötaka akoranga mä te whakaako i a rätou mokopuna ki ngä körero mïharo mö ngä tino wähi whai mana kei roto i tö rätou rohe.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana ngā whakahaere

Te möhio ki tö rätou taiao. E whakatuwheratia ana e te whänau me ngä kaimahi ngä huarahi ki a rätou mokopuna te whai wähi me te ako i ngä körero e pä ana ki te taiao e huri taiäwhio nei i tö rätou köhanga. E ako ana rätou i ngä körero mö tö rätou köhanga, i ngä körero mö tö rätou taiao, ä, mö te rohe o waho atu o tö rätou taiao, mai i a rätou haerere ki ënei wähi i ia wä, i ia wä. Ko ngä pakeke e whakaähuru ana i a rätou mai i ngä kawa me ngä tumanakohanga kua whakaritea mö ënei wä. E möhio ana ngä mokopuna ki ngä wähi akoranga, ä, me ngä tu-ähua mahi hoki e whakaratohia ana mö ënei wähanga. E möhio märama ana ngä mokopuna ki tö rätou taiao, ä, ki ngä tängata hoki e atawhai ana i a rätou.

Tö rätou mäia i roto i tö rätou taiao. E häpaitia ana te mäiatanga me te tu-tangatatanga i roto i ngä mokopuna, mai i te pai o tä rätou mahi ngätahi mä tä rätou täkaro ngätahi. Kua takoto ngä kawa whakahaere hei whai mö te kai, te haere ki te wharepaku me te moe. Kua pupü ake i roto i ngä mokopuna o te köhanga o rätou möhio mö to rätou ähurutanga i roto i te taiao o tö rätou köhanga mai i ënei kawa. E whakatauira mai ana ngä mokopuna mätaamua i ngä kawenga a te kaiärahi, mai i tä rätou ärahi me tä rätou äwhina i o rätou taina. E whakaatu mai ana rätou i tä rätou tiaki i te taiao mai i tä rätou whakawhäiti i a rätou taonga i muri mai i a rätou mahi täkaro, ä, me tä rätou whai i ngä kawa whakahaere kua whakaritea. E kitea ana te mähorahora me te ähuru o ngä mokopuna i roto i tö rätou köhanga.

Ngä türanga me ngä kawenga a te tangata whenua. E whakatüwheratia ana e ngä pöwhiri ki ngä manuhiri, ngä huarahi ki ngä mokopuna ki te äta titiro me te whai wähi i roto i ngä kawenga e pä ana ki te tangata whenua. E whakatauiratia mai ana ngä kawenga hei kawe mä te täne me te wahine ki a rätou, ä, e tuwhera ana ngä huarahi ki a rätou ki te noho i te taha o ënei tauira pai o te ao Mäori. E pöwhiri ana, e tautoko ana, ä, e mahi ngätahi ana rätou i te taha o ngä manuhiri, ä, e mäia ana ngä mokopuna mätaamua ki te körero me te mahi ngätahi i te taha o ngä manuhiri. E äta titiro ana rätou ki o rätou pakeke e mahi ana i a rätou mahi, ä, me tä rätou whakatauira mai i a rätou mahi. Täpiri atu ki tënei, e möhio ana, ä, e taea ana e ngä mokopuna te körero a rätou pepeha. E möhio ana ngä mokopuna ki ngä türanga me ngä kawa e pä ana ki te tangata whenua.

Te whanaungatanga. E tuwhera ana ngä huarahi ki ngä mokopuna i ia wä, i ia wä, ki te whai wähi atu ki ëra atu tängata, ä, e akiakitia ana rätou ki te whanake i te pai o te whanaungatanga i waenganui i a rätou ano, ä, i ëra atu tängata hoki. Mahi ngätahi ai ngä iwi käinga me ngä mema o ngä whänau i te taha o ngä mokopuna i runga i te marae me ëra atu o ngä mahi e heipü ana i roto i tö rätou rohe. Haere ai hoki ngä mätua me a rätou mokopuna ki ngä mea e heipü ana i roto i Tuwharetoa. E kitea ana te pai o te whanaungatanga i waenganui i ngä tuakana me o rätou taina, ä, me te äwhina me te häpai a tëtahi i tëtahi.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te maha o ngä mahi e whai whakaaro ana ki te rerekëtanga o ngä hiahia a ngä whanonga o tëna, o tëna, o ngä mokopuna. Ahakoa e pai ana te whakahaere a ngä kaimahi i te ihiihitanga o ngä whanonga o ëtahi o a rätou mokopuna, e tika ana kia whakawhänuitia atu, ä, kia whakaratohia e rätou te maha atu o ngä mahi akoranga kia whiwhi ai te katoa o ngä mokopuna i ngä mahi e hiahia ana rätou ki te mahi.

Mana Tangata

He whakamārama

I täpiritia atu e Te Tari Arotake Mätauranga a Mana Tangata hei wähanga hei titirohanga, arä, hei hopu i te mana ake o te whänau, i o rätou kaha me tä rätou tautoko i tënei köhanga me te iwi o te rohe.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana ngā whakahaere

Te möhio o tëna, o tëna, o ngä mokopuna ko wai rätou. E wäriutia ana te tu-tangatatanga o tëna, o tëna, o ngä mokopuna, ä, me ngä rerekëtanga o tëna, o tëna, o ngä mokopuna. E haria mai ana e tëna, e tëna, o ngä mokopuna me o rätou whänau, o rätou anö iwi, o rätou hononga-a-hapü, ä, mö o rätou pakaritanga ki tö rätou köhanga. E möhio ana te kaiako ki ana mokopuna, ä, me tana whakatuwhera i ngä huarahi ki a rätou ki te ako i ngä körero e pä ana ki a rätou, ä, ki te wäriu hoki i ngä rerekëtanga o tëna, o tëna, o rätou. E tautokotia ana e te katoa o ngä mokopuna ngä ähua motuhake me ngä rerekëtanga o ëra atu o rätou, ä, e möhio ana hoki ngä mokopuna ko wai rätou.

Te whanaungatanga. E pono ana, ä, e ähei ana ngä mätua ki te haeremai ki te äwhina i a rätou mokopuna i roto i tö rätou köhanga. E whai wähi mai ana hoki ngä kaumätua i roto i ngä mahi akoranga e whakaäkona ana ki a rätou mokopuna. E häpaitia ana te whanaungatanga i waenganui i ngä tuakana me o rätou taina, ä, me te kitea o te ngäkau nui a ngä mokopuna ki te äwhina i tëtahi, i tëtahi o rätou. E whakawhiwhia ana ngä mokopuna ki ngä wäriu e pä ana ki te iwi, ngä hapü me te whänau i roto i te taiao akoranga e häpai ana, ä, e atawhai ana i a rätou.

Te whanaketanga-a-tinana me te whanaketanga aronganui. E kaha ana ngä kaimahi ki te whakatutuki i ngä hiahia-a-tinana me ngä hiahia aronganui a a rätou mokopuna. Kua whakaüngia e rätou he kawa whakahaere motuhake mö te arataki i a rätou mokopuna i ngä wä e moe ana rätou, e kai ana, e haere ana ki te wharepaku, ä, e whakapakari-a-tinana ana. E whakaritea ana he wähanga o ia ra e taea ai e ngä mokopuna te mahi ngä mahi whakakori tinana. E whakamahia ana ngä karakia mö te häpai i te taha wairua o ngä mokopuna, ä, i te oranga aronganui o ngä mokopuna. E möhio ana ngä mokopuna ki ngä kawa whakahaere mö te tiaki i a rätou ano, ä, e puta ana ngä hua ki a rätou mai i tö rätou möhio ki ngä kawa whakahaere o tö rätou köhanga.

Ngä tohungatanga me te pono o te whänau. He maha ngä tohungatanga me ngä pükenga kei tëna, kei tëna mema o te whänau, hei äwhina i a rätou i roto i ngä whakahaere o tö rätou köhanga. He röpu motuhake kei roto i te whänau kua roa ke e whakarato ana i ngä körero märama mö te iwi o te rohe me te köhanga reo. Ko te kai pupuri hoki o te raihana o te köhanga e whakarato haere tonu ana i ngä äwhina ki te köhanga, ä, ko ia tëtahi o ngä mema o te whänau i timata i roto i te köhanga mai i töna whakatuwheratanga. Nä ënei pakaritanga o ngä mema o te whänau, ka whai wahi tënei i roto i te tu pakari a te köhanga i tënei wä.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Ngä whakamahere mö meake nei, me ëra hei whai ake. Käore anö kia tuhia e te whänau he mahere hei whakaatu mai i te huarahi hei whai mä tö rätou köhanga a meake nei. Ahakoa e märama ana rätou ki te kawenga kei runga i a rätou mö te whängai i o rätou möhio whakahaere ki ngä mema hou o te whänau, käore anö kia whakamaheretia e rätou te wä e heipü ai tënei.

Te ähei atu ki ngä tohungatanga o tëna, o tëna, mema o te whänau. Käore kë ngä mema o te whänau e whiwhi möhiotanga ana, i te whakarato haere tonu i ngä whakangungutanga ki ngä mema hou o te whänau e pä ana ki o rätou türanga me a rätou kawenga. E ngäkau nui ana ëtahi o ngä mätua e taiohi tonu ana, ki te ako ki te kawe i ënei kawenga.

Te ako takitahi i roto i te hötaka akoranga. E ruarua nei ngä wä e ähei ana ngä mokopuna ki te tohu i ngä mahi e hiahia ana rätou ki te mahi. Ahakoa e whakaratohia ana he wä mö rätou ki te ako ökawa, ä, ki te ako öpaki puta noa i te roanga ake o te ra, käore kë ënei i te whakamahia hei äwhina i ngä mokopuna ki te ako takitahi.

Te Whakamahere me Te Aro Mätai

He whakamārama

Kei roto i te katoa o ngä arotake a Te Tari Arotake Mätauranga, ko te whakamahere me te aro mätai. Ko ngä titirohanga e whakaatu mai ana e pëhea ana te whakamahere a te whänau mö ngä whakariteritenga me ngä whakahaere mö to rätou köhanga, ngä arotake whaiaro, te whakamahere o ngä hötaka akoranga, te aro mätai me te aro matawai.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana ngā whakahaere

Te whakamahere a ngä kaiako i a rätou hötaka akoranga. Ko ngä whakamahere o ngä hötaka akoranga e whakarato märama ana i ngä körero märama mö Te Whäriki, i ngä kaupapa kua whakaritea, te reo Mäori, ngä whäinga akoranga motuhake hei whai, ngä mahi hei mahi me ngä rauemi. E whakarato ana ënei körero ki ngä kaiako, i ngä mahi hei mahi i ia ra, ä, i ia wiki hoki. Kei roto i te whakatakotoranga o tënei, ko te wähanga mö te aro mätai i te hötaka akoranga, ä, me te whai whakaaro ki te hiahia ki te whakamahere i ngä mahi e tika ana mö tëna, mö tëna reanga o ngä mokopuna. E möhio märama ana ngä kaiako ki te kaupapa o te whakamahere tötika i a rätou hötaka akoranga.

Ngä whakahaere aro matawai. E möhio ana ngä kaiako ki te kaupapa o te aro matawai, ä, kei a rätou ëtahi köhanga motuhake e pä ana ki ngä mahi ka taea e a rätou mokopuna te mahi. Ko ëtahi o ngä rautaki i whakamahia e rätou mö te whakawhäiti i ënei körero, ko ngä tuhituhinga i tuhia ki roto i a rätou pukapuka, ngä körero mö ngä aronui me ngä tauira o ngä mahi kua mahia e ngä mokopuna. Tuaritia ai ënei körero i ia wä, i ia wä, i te taha o te whänau, ä, ka ripoatatia i roto i te ripoata o te mutunga o te tau. Körero opaki ai ngä kaimahi mö ngä hiahia a a rätou mokopuna. Ko te whakatakotoranga mö ngä ripoata, ko tëra e whai ana i ngä kaupapa motuhake.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Te arotake whaiaro a te whänau, te aro mätai me te titiro anö ki ngä mahi kua mahia. Käore kë te whänau i te whakamahi i ngä whakaaturanga o a rätou arotake whaiaro, aro mätai me a rätou titiro anö ki ngä mahi kua mahia e rätou, hei äwhina i a rätou i roto i a rätou whakamahere rautaki. Ahakoa he kaupapa tä rätou mö te whakaatu mai he aha ngä mea kei te heipü i roto i te köhanga, e tika ana kia whakamanatia e rätou tënei mä te arotake whaiaro i ia wä, i ia wä, i te katoa o ngä wähanga whakahaere o to rätou köhanga. Me titiro anö te whänau ki tä rätou tütohinga me tä rätou matakite mö tö rätou köhanga, hei wähanga tuatahi mö tä rätou kaupapa mö te arotake whaiaro.

Te whakamahere rautaki. Käore kë he whakamahere rautaki a te whänau hei ärahi i a rätou i roto i tä rätou whakahaere i tö rätou köhanga. I roto i ngä tau kua hipa, i whai rätou i tëtahi kaupapa märama mö te whakamahere mö te äwhina i a rätou ki te whakahaere i tö rätou köhanga. Käore kë a rätou körero arotake whaiaro hei whakamahi mä rätou hei pütake mö a rätou whakamahere rautaki.

Te aro mätai o ngä hötaka akoranga. E öpaki ana ngä whakahaere a te whänau mö te aro mätai i a rätou hötaka akoranga, ä, käore hoki i te whakarato i te katoa o ngä körero e tika ana mö te äwhina i te kaiako ki te whakapai ake i te kounga o ngä hötaka akoranga.

1. The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Waihi Te Köhanga Reo is situated in a small township near Lake Taupo in a natural, native bush setting. The land and building have been donated to the köhanga whänau for their use, by the local iwi. The köhanga is near the local marae and strongly supported by Ngäti Tuwharetoa.

Mana Atua forms the basis of the learning programme provided for children. Children have a range of opportunities to help them to become familiar with and learn about their individual, ancestral heritage. Children observe and participate in events including regular powhiri where tikanga influences their actions and behaviour. They regularly attend events at the local marae. They explore their immediate physical environment, the ngahere, the lake and the sea. Kaimahi teach and prompt tamariki to learn specific karakia and waiata. Children are aware of their ancestral heritage.

Mana Whenua learning reinforces links with the local iwi, marae and the natural environment surrounding the köhanga. Whänau and staff take pride in the experiences they are able to provide for children in the immediate environment. Children learn about the köhanga, the natural environment and the local and wider area though regular excursions. Adults provide a sense of security by establishing clear routines and expectations for children during these times. Children know the different learning spaces and the types of activity likely to be provided in these areas. Children are confident in their environment and are relaxed with the people who care for them.

Mana Tangata captures the individual uniqueness of the whänau, staff and tamariki. Whänau members have a range of expertise and skill to support them with whänau management activities. There is a long-standing, core group of whänau members who provide a strong foundation of knowledge about the local iwi and köhanga reo. The licensee provides regular support. The collective strengths of whänau members foster ongoing stability in the köhanga.

There are several areas for development for the whänau and staff. These include whänau planning, self review and succession planning. Within the programme of learning, kaimahi should provide greater options for all children to foster independence and more readily provide for the range of age differences. This should be further strengthened by regular programme evaluation.

Future Action 

ERO intends to return to the köhanga within 24 months to evaluate the progress made in response to the areas of concern identified in this report.

2. Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the management of Waihi Te Köhanga Reo was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO. ERO also used documentation provided by the köhanga reo to contribute to the scope of the review.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whänau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the köhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for tamariki at Waihi Te Köhanga Reo.

All ERO education reviews in köhanga reo focus on the planning and evaluation. Implicit in this is the quality of whänau management and the quality of education.

The whänau chose as its focus areas:

  • Mana Atua; and
  • Mana Whenua

ERO added an additional focus area:

  • Mana Tangata

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

Kōhanga Reo Priority

Mana Atua

Background

The whänau and staff of Waihi Te Köhanga Reo chose Mana Atua as a focus for review. Mana Atua activities provide an opportunity for children to learn about the values and beliefs of Ngäti Tuwharetoa.

Areas of good performance

Ancestral Heritage. The whänau provides a range of opportunities to support tamariki to become familiar with and learn about their individual ancestral heritage. Children observe and participate in events including powhiri where tikanga influences their actions and behaviour. They regularly attend events at the local marae. They explore their immediate physical environment, the ngahere, the lake and the sea. Kaimahi teach and prompt tamariki to learn specific karakia and waiata. Children are aware of their ancestral heritage.

Spiritual and cultural awareness. The köhanga provides a nurturing environment that reflects strong cultural and spiritual values and beliefs. Ngä Atua are introduced when learning about Rangi and Papa. These teachings are linked to traditional practices that are then modelled in the köhanga. Children are learning about the significance of the atua.

Encouragement and support. Whänau and staff model respect care and concern for each other and children. Tuakana teina relationships are fostered. Kaimahi encourage positive behaviour and affirm the successes of children. Children appear happy.

Confidence and participation. Children are exposed to a range of learning experiences where they are encouraged to participate. They display confidence within the köhanga environment and wider community.

Language learning. Children are immersed in an environment where te reo Mäori is the only language spoken. Kaimahi introduce new vocabulary with different kaupapa and use daily events and interactions to prompt children to use new language. Kaimahi use pictures and language repetition as strategies to develop the use of correct language. Children are encouraged to use te reo Mäori at all times.

Areas for improvement

Formal structure for mihi mihi. During the morning session and mihi mihi the different age groups of children gather as a whole group. Children are easily distracted and not able to sustain their interest in the whole session.

Response to individual needs. Although the staff are aware of the different age and ability levels of children and provide a range of learning experiences, further development is essential to provide an environment that engages, stimulates and extends all children at all times.

Language extension. Despite efforts to foster language learning opportunities, staff do not use a sufficiently broad range of language acquisition learning strategies. Further opportunities to extend language use throughout the course of the day are essential.

Mana Whenua

Background

The whänau of Waihi Te Köhanga Reo also chose Mana Whenua as a focus for review. This taumata whakahirahira reinforces the links with the iwi, marae and the natural environment surrounding the köhanga. Every opportunity is taken to enhance the programme by learning about significant places within the area.

Areas of good performance

Familiarity with the environment. Whänau and staff provide opportunities for children to experience and learn about the immediate environment. Children learn about the köhanga, the natural environment and the local and wider area through regular excursions. Adults provide a sense of security by establishing clear routines and expectations for children during these times. Children know the different learning spaces and the types of activity likely to be provided in these areas. Children are very familiar with their environment and the people who care for them.

Confidence in the environment. Interactions and opportunities for play and learning promote confidence and independence for children. There are established routines for eating, toileting and sleeping. These routines create a sense of security for children in the köhanga environment. Older children model leadership by guiding and supporting younger children. They show that they care for their environment by tidying after play and following simple rules. Children are confident and secure within the köhanga.

Tangata whenua roles and responsibilities. Powhiri provide children with opportunities to observe and participate as tangata whenua. Female and male roles are modelled to them and children are provided with opportunity to sit alongside male and female adult role models. They welcome, accept and interact with manuhiri. Older children are confident to talk with and question manuhiri. They observe adults and mimic the roles of manaaki. In addition children know and can recall their pepeha. Children know the roles and kawa associated with tangata whenua.

Relationships. Children have regular opportunities to relate with other people and are encouraged to develop effective relationships with their peers and others. Community and whänau members interact with children at the marae and during local events. The köhanga children and their parents attend Tuwharetoa events. Tuakana and teina interactions are positive and supportive. Children naturally care and support each other.

Areas for improvement

Range of activities to consider the different behavioural needs of children. Although kaimahi effectively manage exuberant behaviour , they should extend and provide a wider range of learning activities so that all children are actively engaged.

Mana Tangata

Background

The Education Review Office added Mana Tangata as a focus area to capture the whänau uniqueness, their efforts and support of this köhanga and the local iwi.

Areas of good performance

Individual identity. Individual identity is valued and difference accepted. Each child and his or her whänau bring specific iwi, hapü alliances and strengths to the köhanga. The kaiako know the children and provide ongoing opportunities for them to learn about themselves and value their individual differences. Collectively all children acknowledge the special attributes and differences of others. Children know their individual identity.

Whanaungatanga. Whänau members are committed and available to support children at köhanga. Kaumatua contribute to the type of learning experienced by children. Tuakana teina relationships are fostered with children eager to support each other. Children experience whänau, iwi and hapü values in a supportive and caring learning environment.

Emotional and physical development. Kaimahi actively look after the physical and emotional needs of all children. They have established specific routines to supervise children while sleeping, eating, toileting and exercising. There are daily opportunities for kori tinana. Karakia is used to foster spiritual and emotional well-being. Children know basic care routines and benefit from established practices.

Whänau expertise and commitment. Whänau members have a range of expertise and skills to support them with whänau management activities. There is a long-standing, core group of whänau members that provides a strong foundation of knowledge about the local iwi and köhanga reo. The licensee provides ongoing and regular support, as she has been a part of the köhanga since its inception. These collective strengths contribute to the stability of the köhanga.

Areas for improvement

Forward and succession planning. The köhanga whänau does not have a documented plan for the future direction of the köhanga reo. Although they are clear about transferring management knowledge to new members they have not planned for this to occur.

Access to whänau expertise. Experienced whänau members do not provide ongoing training to new whänau to support them with their roles and responsibilities. A younger and willing group of parents is keen to learn.

Independence in the learning programme. Children do not have sufficient opportunity for independent choice in the learning programme. Daily formal and less formal learning opportunities are provided for children but these are not well used to foster independence amongst children.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

The Education Review Office includes planning and evaluation in all köhanga reviews. Investigations reflect how the whänau plans to support köhanga operation and management, self review, programme planning, evaluation and assessment.

Areas of good performance

Kaiako programme planning. Programme planning provides clear direction about Te Whäriki, defined kaupapa, te reo, specific learning objectives, activities and resources. This information gives kaiako the detail to focus daily and weekly activities. The format used includes room for programme evaluation and takes into account the need to plan for children’s age differences. Kaiako have a clear understanding about the purpose of programme planning.

Assessment practices. Kaiako understand the purpose of assessment and have some specific information about what children are able to do. Different strategies to collect this information include notebook entries, unit comments and samples of work. This information is regularly shared with whänau and is then reported in an end of year report. Kaimahi take time to talk informally about children’s needs. The format for reporting follows defined specific kaupapa.

Areas for improvement

Whänau self review, evaluation and reflection. The whänau does not use self-review, reflection or evaluation to help with strategic planning. Although there is some timer given to what is happening in the köhanga it is important that it be formalised with regular self-review of all aspects of köhanga operation. The whänau should revisit the charter and whänau vision as a first step in the self-review process.

Strategic planning. The whänau does not have strategic planning to guide them with köhanga operations. They have in previous years followed a clear whänau planning format to support köhanga management. Self-review information is not available to use as a basis for strategic planning.

Programme evaluation. Programme evaluation is informal and does not give sufficient attention to help kaiako to improve the quality of the learning programme.

3. Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee and whänau of Waihi Te Köhanga Reo completed an ERO Köhanga Whänau Management Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration;
  • health, safety and welfare;
  • personnel management; and
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying, and abuse);
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices, accidents and medication, hygiene and routines, travel and excursion policies and procedures);
  • staff qualifications and organisation; and
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

ERO’s investigations did not identify any areas of concern.

4. Recommendations

ERO and the köhanga reo management agreed that they:

  1.  seek external support to address the areas for improvement identified in this report.

5. Future Action 

ERO intends to return to the köhanga within 24 months to evaluate the progress made in response to the areas of concern identified in this report.
