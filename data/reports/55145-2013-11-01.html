1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

E whakahaeretia ana te Arotake Tāpiri i runga i te whai whakaaro a tētahi o Ngā Kaiwhakahaere-a-Rohe o Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kia whakahaeretia tēnei.

E arotake ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānui me te whai hua o ngā mahi kua mahia e te whānau o te kōhanga, ki te whakatutuki i ngā take motuhake i pupü ake i roto i ta rātou arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, i ēra atu take rānei kua kitea mai i taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ī whakaritea tēnei arotake tāpiri i runga i te aro mātai i ngā whakahaere a te rōpu whakahaere o Te Kōhanga Reo o Waipukurau e pa ana ki ngā wāhanga i kitea i roto i te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te marama o Whiringa ā nuku o te tau 2012, i ēra atu take rānei kua pupü ake mai i taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mo ngā whakapuakitanga o tēnei arotake, ko te āta titiro ki:

  • Mana Tangata tae atu ana ki te hōtaka akoranga
  • Te whakamahere me te aromātai tae atu ana ki te arotake whaiaro, te whai wāhi o te whānau me te whakahaere pūtea
  • ēra atu tino take kua tāutu mai i te arotake o mua atu nei.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Kua takatika ētahi o ngā rerekētanga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Waipukurau i ngā marama tata nei. E harikoa ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki o rātou mahi whakatika i ngā take kua pupū ake i te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga o mua atu nei. Ko ētahi o ngā take ko te hōtaka akoranga, te whakarato rauemi, te taiao akoranga me ngā wāhi tākaro. I te āwangawanga hoki ki ngā mahi whakahaere pūtea, te whai wāhi me te ngākaunui o te whānau me te arotake whaiaro.

Kua whai awhina ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakatika i ngā take mai i Te Poari Matua o Te Kōhanga Reo me te purapura a rohe. Kua whakahōutia ētahi o ngā wahanga o te whare kōhanga me te taiao o waho kia noho hei wāhi maioha mo ngā tamariki. Ka hui ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau i ia wā me te purapura. Kua āwhina tēnei i ngā kaimahi ki te whakamahine i o rātou mahi whakamahere me te aromatawai me ngā rautaki whakaako. He mārama ināia nei te take me te ahunga o te whānau me ngā kaimahi.

He tino whakaihiihi te hōtaka o ngā tamariki o ia ra, ā, he pai te whakatutuki i ngā matea whānui o ngā tamariki. Kua whakapau pūtea te whānau ki te hoko rauemi me ngā taputapu. Ka taea e ngā tamariki te kōwhiri i o rātou ake mahi , te whai i o rātou ngākaunui me te tākaro ngātahi ki ngā rōpū nui me ngā rōpū iti. He whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te uru ki ngā mahi ā kaupapa, ngā tikanga me ngā mahi ko ngā pakeke kei te whakahaere. Kua whakaritea tētahi wāhi ako mō ngā tamariki kei raro i te rua tau. Kei tēnei wāhi ngā rauemi tōtika, rauemi whakamere hoki he māmā te tiki, ā, he ataahua te whakataha. He pai te tautoko i ngā tamariki, ka whiwhi hoki rātou i ngā wheako akoranga takatika.

He māia te kōrero a ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori i ia wā. Ka kōrero Māori ngā tamariki ki te whānui o ngā mahi ōkawa. He nohinohi te nuinga o ngā tamariki, ā, ka taea e rātou te whai i ngā tohutohu me te kōrero i ngā kianga māmā. Kei te noho rūmaki ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao reo Māori.

Ka kitea ināia nei te ngākaunui ake o te whānau ki a rātou tamariki me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. Ka tautoko mātātoa rātou i ngā mahi o te kōhanga reo me te hōtaka o ngā tamariki o ia rā. Ka tae atu te whānau ki ngā hui a whānau i ia marama ki reira whai wāhi ai ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero me ngā whakataunga kaupapa. Kei te mōhio rātou ki o rātou tūranga me o rātou kawenga. He marama ngā tukanga whakahaere pūtea. Kua pai ake ētahi āhuatanga o te arotake whaiaro. Kei te ngākaunui te whānau ki te tae atu ki ngā hui a purapura ki reira ngā kōrero e pā ana ki te arotake whaiaro.

Ngā Mahi ā Meake Nei

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Tangata

He whakamārama

I tāutu te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga o mua atu nei i ētahi āwangawanga e pā ana ki te hōtaka akoranga o ngā tamariki. I te kōpīpiri ngā rauemi hei kōwhiri mā ngā tamariki. Kāore i te rite ngā kaimahi ki te tuku i ngā hōtaka whakaihiihi, whakamere hoki ki ngā tamariki. E tika ana kia pai ake te taiao akoranga me ngā wāhi tākaro. Kāore i te tōtika ngā wāhi ako mō ngā tamariki raro i te rua tau.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Ngā wheako akoranga o ngā tamariki. He pai te hanga o te hōtaka o ia rā o ngā tamariki me te whakatutuki tika i ngā matea akoranga whānui o ngā tamariki. He māmā te whai wāhi atu o ngā tamariki ki te whānui o ngā rauemi me ngā mahi whakaihiihi. Kua whakapau pūtea te whānau ki te hoko rauemi me te hoko taputapu. Ka taea e ngā tamariki te kōwhiri i o rātou ake mahi , te whai i o rātou ngākaunui me te tākaro ngātahi ki ngā rōpū nui me ngā rōpū iti. He whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te uru ki ngā mahi ā kaupapa, ngā tikanga me ngā mahi ko ngā pakeke kei te whakahaere.

Kua whakaritea tētahi wāhi ako mō ngā tamariki kei raro i te rua tau. Kei tēnei wāhi ngā rauemi tōtika, rauemi whakamere hoki he māmā te tiki, ā, he ataahua te whakataha. He pai te tautoko i ngā tamariki, ka whiwhi hoki rātou i ngā wheako akoranga takatika.

Te taiao akoranga. He nui ngā whakarerekētanga ki ngā taiao akoranga kei rō whare me ērā kei waho. He wāhi maioha te wāhi i waho, he māhorahora, ā, he wāhi rata ana ki ngā tamariki. Ka whai wāhi ināia nei ngā tamariki ki ētahi hanganga tākaro me ngā rauemi. Ka whakamarumaru ngā uhi marumaru hou i ngā wāhi tākaro o waho hei ārai i te rā. Kua haumaru ake te kāuta me ngā wharepaku mā ngā tamariki o muri mai i ngā mahi nui ki te whakapai ake i ēnei wāhi. Ko ngā whāinga matua a meāke nei a te whānau ko te peita anō i te kōhanga reo me te whakatakoto whāriki hou ki te ruma tākaro o ngā tamariki. Kei te harikoa te whānau ki o rātou paetae me ngā tautoko kua whakaratoa e rātou ki te ako o a rātou tamariki.

Te whanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki.He māia ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Nā tēnei māiatanga o ngā kaimahi ka ākina ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori i ia wā. Ka kōrero Māori ngā tamariki ki te whānui o ngā mahi ōkawa. He nohinohi te nuinga o ngā tamariki, ā, ka taea e rātou te whai i ngā tohutohu me te kōrero i ngā kianga māmā. Kei te noho rūmaki ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao reo Māori.

Te whakamāherehere me te aromātai

He whakamārama

I whakarewa te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i ētahi āwangawanga e pā ana ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. He take e pā ana ki te whakahaere pūtea. He maha o ngā mema o te whānau kāore i te whai wāhi ki ngā mahi i te kōhanga reo. He mea whakakōroiroi ngā mahi whakatau kaupapa i te mea kāore i te rahi ngā mema o te whānau kia haere te hui. Me ako te whānau ki o rātou tūranga me o rātou kawenga.

Kāore i te mahi te arotake whaiaro. Kāore he huarahi kōtuitui, pūnahanaha rānei hei arotake i ngā mahi o te kōhanga reo.

Kei tēnei pūrongo ētahi atu whanaketanga kua pupū ake ki te kōhanga reo. Kei te hāngai ēnei te whakamahere kaupapa, te aromatawai, te whakamahere rautaki me ngā tautoko a Te Poari Matua o Te Kōhanga Reo me te purapura a rohe.

Ngā wāhanga kua pai ake

Te whai wāhi me te ngākaunui o te whānau. He mātātoa inaia nei te whānau ki ngā mahi o te kōhanga reo me te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka ate atu te whānau ki ngā hui i ia wā. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero me ngā whakataunga kaupapa. Ka kawe te whānau i ngā mahi awhina i ngā kaimahi me ngā tamariki. He mea nui ēnei tautoko ki ngā kaimahi me ngā painga ka puta ki te ako me te oranga o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamōhiotia te whānau ki o rātou tūranga me o rātou kawenga ki ngā hui, ngā mahi whakauru whānau hou me ngā hui a purapura. Nā te kaha ake o te whai wāhi o te whānau ki te kōhanga reo kua takatika ngā putanga ki ngā tamariki.

Te whakahaere pūtea. Ka whakahaere totika te whānau i o rātou pūtea. He tahua hei arahi i ngā whakapaunga pūtea. I te āwangawanga te kaitātari ki te pōturi o ngā utu GST. Kua pai ake ngā mahi utu nama i te wā e tika ana. He toenga pūtea o rātou. Ka noho ētahi o ēnei pūtea hei whakapai ake i te kōhanga reo. He pai te whānau ināia nei ki te whakapau o rātou pūtea.

Te whakamahere kaupapa. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i tētahi anga pūmau ki te whakamahere rātou i ngā kaupapa mō ngā tamariki. He pai te tuhi i ngā mahere mō te wā roa me te wā poto. Kei ngā mahere ngā aratohu whai hua mā ngā kaimahi ki te whakatinana rātou i ngā hōtaka mō ngā tamariki. Ka whakamārama ngā kaupapa i ngā ariā e pā ana ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui.

Ngā mahi aromatawai. Ka aroturuki, ka aromatawai māia ngā kaimahi i te ahunga whakamua o a rātou tamariki. Kua hangaia e rātou ngā kōpaki mahi ātaahua mā tēna, mā tēna o ngā tamariki. Ko ētahi o ngā mahi aromatawai ko te whai wāhi mai o te whānau, ngā mātakitaki, ngā aromatawai ā kaupapa, he rārangi arowhai me ngā tauira mahi a ngā tamariki. He māmā te whai wāhi o te whānau ki ēnei kōpaki e whakaatu ana i ngā wheako me ngā angitū o ngā tamariki.

Te tautoko a Te Poari Matua o Te Kōhanga Reo me te purapura.Nā te tautoko a Te Poari Matua me te purapura kua tino whanake ake te kōhanga reo. Kua puku mahi te kaiwhakahaere a rohe mai i Te Poari Matua me te kaupapa kaimahi ki te whakarite i ngā mahi whakatikatika, whakapai ake i te kōhanga reo. He mea tino nui te ako me te whakawhanake ngaiotanga mai i te purapura. Kua pai ake te mōhio me ngā mahi o ngā kaimahi e pā ana ki te aromatawai, te whakamahere kaupapa me ngā rautaki whakaako.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake anō

Te arotake whaiaro. Kei te puawai tonu te mōhio o te whānau ki ngā mahi arotake whaiaro. Ka tuku pūrongo rātou i ia wā e pā ana ki ngā mahi whakahaere pūtea, te maha o ngā tamariki i te kōhanga reo, ngā haerenga me te tiaki i te kōhanga reo. Kua tīmata te whānau te arotake i a rātou kaupapa here me te tūtohinga. E tahuri rātou ināia nei ki te tuku pūrongo i runga i ērā atu o ngā wāhi matua o te kōhanga reo. He takarepa te arotuuki me te tuku pūrongo ki te whānau e pā ana ki te tōtika o te kaupapa, te hauora me te haumaru, te reo Māori, te whakangungu kaimahi me te whai wāhi o te whānau.

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Waipukurau has undergone several positive changes over recent months. Whānau and kaimahi are proud of the actions they have taken to address the issues raised in the previous ERO report. Issues included the learning programme, resource provisions, learning environment and play spaces. There were further concerns with financial management, whānau participation and commitment and self review.

Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) and the local purapura have played a significant role in supporting kaimahi and whānau to bring about change. The kōhanga reo building has undergone major structural work and the outdoor environment is welcoming for children. Kaimahi and members of the whānau regularly meet with the purapura. This has helped kaimahi to refine their planning, assessment practices and teaching methods. Whānau and kaimahi now have a clear sense of purpose and direction.

The children’s daily programme is highly stimulating and caters well for their different learning needs. The whānau have spent a considerable amount of funding on resources and materials. Children are now able to choose their own activities, follow their interests and play cooperatively in large and small groups. There are opportunities for children to participate in kaupapa activities, tikanga and adult led activities. A specific learning space has been set aside for the under two year olds. This space has interesting and appropriate resources which are accessible to children and attractively presented. Children are well supported and receive positive learning experiences.

Kaimahi confidently speak te reo Māori. Kaimahi encourage children to use te reo Māori regularly. Children use te reo Māori in a range of formal settings. Most children are very young and are able to follow instructions and use simple phrases. Children are immersed in a rich te reo Māori environment.

Whānau now show a greater commitment to their children and the kaupapa of kōhanga reo. They actively support the operations of the kōhanga reo and the children’s daily programme. Whānau regularly attend monthly meetings where they share in discussions and decision making. They are aware of their roles and responsibilities. There are clear processes for financial management. Some aspects of self review have improved. Whānau have made a commitment to attend purapura meetings where there will be discussions about self review.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

2 Findings

Mana Tangata

Background

The previous ERO report identified concerns with the children’s learning programme. Children had limited resources to choose from. Kaimahi were not well prepared and organised in presenting children with stimulating and interesting programmes. The learning environment and play spaces needed improvement. Children under two years old were not provided with suitable learning spaces.

Areas of progress

Children’s learning experiences. The children’s daily programme is well structured and caters appropriately for the different learning needs of children. Children have easy access to a range of stimulating resources and activities. The whānau have spent a considerable amount of funding on resources and materials. Children are now able to choose their own activities, follow their interests and play cooperatively in large and small groups. There are opportunities for children to participate in kaupapa activities, small and large group play, tikanga and adult led activities.

A specific learning space has been set aside for the under two year olds. This space has various resources which are accessible to children and attractively presented. Children are well supported and enjoy positive learning experiences.

Learning environment.The indoor and external learning environments have been through major changes. The outdoor area is welcoming, spacious and has great appeal for children. Children now have access to various play structures and resources. New, colourful shade cloth which covers much of the key play areas provides protection from the sun. The kitchen and ablution areas are much safer for children following major repair work. Future priorities for whānau include repainting the kōhanga reo and the laying of new carpet in the children’s playroom. Whānau are proud of their achievements and the support they have provided to their children’s learning.

Children’s te reo Māori development. Kaimahi confidently speak te reo Māori. This level of confidence shown by kaimahi encourages children to regularly use te reo Māori. Children use te reo Māori in a range of formal settings. Most children are very young and can follow instructions and use simple phrases. Children are immersed in a rich te reo Māori environment.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

The previous ERO report raised concerns about the management of the kōhanga reo operations. There were some issues with financial management. A number of whānau members were not regularly involved in the life of the kōhanga reo. Decision making was often hindered because there were insufficient whānau members to form a quorum. Whānau needed to learn about their roles and responsibilities.

Self review did not occur. There was no coordinated or systematic approach to reviewing the kōhanga reo operations.

This report includes other developments that have occurred at the kōhanga reo. These relate to kaupapa planning, assessment, strategic planning and TKRNT and purapura support.

Areas of progress

Whānau participation and commitment.Whānau now take an active role in the kōhanga reo operations and the daily programme. Whānau regularly attend meetings. They are involved in discussions and share in decision making. Whanau carry out vital tasks to assist the kaimahi and children. Kaimahi value this support and the benefits this has on children’s learning and wellbeing. Whānau are made aware of their roles and responsibilities through meetings, the induction process and purapura hui. The increased involvement of whānau in the kōhanga reo has led to positive outcomes for children.

Financial management. Whānau manage their finances in an orderly manner. There is a budget to guide spending. The auditor raised concerns with the timeliness of goods and services tax (GST) payments. The whānau now make these payments on time. They have a good level of funding in reserve. Some of this money will go towards repair work at the kōhanga reo. Whānau are prudent with their spending.

Kaupapa planning. Kaimahi use a sound framework when they plan different kaupapa for children. Long and short term plans are well written. Plans include effective guidelines for kaimahi when they implement programmes for children. Kaupapa highlight concepts about te ao Māori and the wider world. Children have a strong sense of identity and belonging.

Assessment practices. Kaimahi confidently monitor and assess their children’s progress. They have developed attractive portfolio folders for each child. Assessment practices include whānau input, observations, kaupapa assessments, a checklist and children’s work samples. Whānau have easy access to these folders which document each child’s experiences and successes.

TKRNT and purapura support. TKRNT and purapura support has led to significant developments at the kōhanga reo. The District Manager from TKRNT and kaupapa kaimahi have been instrumental in organising the repair of the kōhanga reo building. Ongoing professional learning and development from the purapura has been vital. Kaimahi have improved their knowledge and practices in regards to assessment, kaupapa planning and teaching methods.

Areas for further improvement

Self review. The whānau are developing in their knowledge of self review. They regularly report on financial management practices, roll numbers, outings and property maintenance. Whānau have begun to review their policies and the charter. They should now report on other key areas of the kōhanga reo operations. There is limited monitoring and reporting to the whānau on the effectiveness of kaupapa, health and safety, te reo Māori, staff training and whānau participation.

3 Recommendations

ERO recommends that the kaimahi and whānau:

  • develop their knowledge of self review and report on the effectiveness of key areas of the kōhanga reo operations.

4 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

This has helped kaimahi to refine their planning, assessment practices and teaching methods. Whānau and kaimahi now have a clear sense of purpose and direction.

The children’s daily programme is highly stimulating and caters well for their different learning needs. The whānau have spent a considerable amount of funding on resources and materials. Children are now able to choose their own activities, follow their interests and play cooperatively in large and small groups. There are opportunities for children to participate in kaupapa activities, tikanga and adult led activities. A specific learning space has been set aside for the under two year olds. This space has interesting and appropriate resources which are accessible to children and attractively presented. Children are well supported and receive positive learning experiences.

Kaimahi confidently speak te reo Māori. Kaimahi encourage children to use te reo Māori regularly. Children use te reo Māori in a range of formal settings. Most children are very young and are able to follow instructions and use simple phrases. Children are immersed in a rich te reo Māori environment.

Whānau now show a greater commitment to their children and the kaupapa of kōhanga reo. They actively support the operations of the kōhanga reo and the children’s daily programme. Whānau regularly attend monthly meetings where they share in discussions and decision making. They are aware of their roles and responsibilities. There are clear processes for financial management. Some aspects of self review have improved. Whānau have made a commitment to attend purapura meetings where there will be discussions about self review.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

When ERO has reviewed a kōhanga reo we ask the whānau of the kōhanga reo to let us know how they intend to manage any difficulties set out in their report (copies of which are available from the kōhanga reo or ERO). We also encourage whānau to inform their community of any follow up action plan. You should talk to the whānau if you have any questions about this evaluation, the full ERO report or their future intentions.
