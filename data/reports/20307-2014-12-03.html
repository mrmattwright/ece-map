1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hau Kapua ki ngā papa o te kura tuatahi o Bayswater, ki te Raki Paewhenua, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau. Kua roa nei te kaiako me ngā kaiwhakahaere i whai wāhi ai ki te kōhanga reo nei. Ka arotahi te whānau ki te whakarato i te hōhonutanga o ngā whai wāhitanga ako ki ā rātou tamariki i roto i te ao Māori. E ai ki te whānau, mā te reo me ngā tikanga Māori, ka puāwai haere te mōhiotanga, te tū pakari hoki o ā rātou tamariki hei Māori.

Ka poipoia ngā tamariki e ō rātou whānau ki tētahi taiao e manaaki ana i a rātou. E whakawhanake tonu ana te whānau i ngā rautaki e tika ana hei hāpai i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i te tū pakari me te mōhiotanga ki a rātou anō i roto i te ao Māori.

Ko te kaha ngākau nuitanga o te kuia, ngā kaiwhakahaere, me te whānau ki te hāpai i te ako o ā rātou tamariki e whai pānga ana ki te eke angitu o ia tamaiti hei ākonga māia, hei ākonga pākiki, hei ākonga tākare hoki e kōkiri ana i a rātou ake akoranga. He māia, he tau hoki ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao ako. Ka whakaatu rātou i te noho mārama ki te whakahirahiratanga o te whanaungatanga.

Ko te hōtaka me ngā rautaki whānui e whakamahia ana, kāhore e āta whakatutuki ana, e āta aro ana hoki ki te rerekē haere o ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā matea o ngā tamariki katoa. Ko tētahi hōtaka e aro ake ana, e hāngai ake ana hoki ki te tamaiti, i runga anō i ngā akoranga o Te Whāriki ka whakapakari i ngā pūmanawa o ngā tamariki. Me mahi ngātahi te kaiako ki te whānau me ngā kaimahi katoa hei whakapai ake i te āhua o tā rātou whakahaere, tā rātou waihanga, me tā rātou whakapuaki i te hōtaka mā ngā tamariki.

Ko ngā wāhanga katoa e toe ana i roto i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, e āta whakamaheretia ana, e āta whakahaerehia ana, e āta arotakengia ana, ā, e āta pūrongotia ana hoki.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga reo (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whanaketanga o te tū pakari, te mōhiotanga hoki o ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō i roto i te ao Māori?

Ka poipoia ngā tamariki e tō rātou whānau ki tētahi taiao e manaaki ana i a rātou. E whakawhanake tonu ana te whānau i ngā rautaki e tika ana hei hāpai i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki i te tū pakari, te mōhiotanga anō hoki ki a rātou anō i roto i te ao Māori.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He maha ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te mārama haere ki te whanaungatanga. Ka ako te whānau ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki, ā, ka tautoko hoki i a rātou. Ka akiaki te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā hononga whakaute ki waenga i ngā tamariki. He tika te manaaki o ngā tuākana i ō u trātoeina. Ka whai pūtake, he whakaute hoki te whanaungatanga ki waenga i ngā tamariki. Ka tū pakari ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo.

He kaha hoki ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i ā rātou taonga tuku iho, i te reo hoki. He maha ngā wā puta noa i te rā, ka whiriwhiri ia tamaiti i ā rātou ake taumahi ako. Nā aua whai wāhitanga, ka whakatairanga ake, ka whakawhānui ake hoki ngā kaimahi i te mōhio haere o ngā tamariki ki a rātou i roto i te ao Māori, mā te whakamahi i te reo Māori.

He whānui ngā tūmomo rauemi e wātea ana ki ngā tamariki kia tūhura, kia ako hoki. Kua whakaritea e te whānau me ngā kaimahi ngā taiao e mau nei i te maha o ngā rauemi kia auaha ai te tūhura haere i te ao Māori. Kei te tino mōhio ngā tamariki ki ō rātou taiao, ā, kua waia hoki ki ngā rauemi. He maha ngā wā ka kitea tā rātou tino ngākau nui ki te whiriwhiri me te kōkiri i ā rātou ake akoranga.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake

Ahakoa he tōtika te aro a ngā tamariki ki te reo tohutohu, he iti noa te reo Māori e whakamahia ana e rātou. Me whakarato i ngā whai wāhitanga ake a ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori i roto i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, hei whakawhānui ake hoki i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki te reo.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whakamahere, te whakahaere, te arotake hoki a te whānau i ngā matea o ngā tamariki me ngā wawata o te whānau?

He rawaka te whakahaere, te aroturuki hoki o ngā matea o ngā tamariki me ngā wawata hoki ō te whānau.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo.E tino whakahaerehia ana Te Kōhanga Reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki, ā, i runga anō hoki i ngā wawata me ngā matea o te whānau. Ka tino hāngai pū ngā hui o ia marama ki te rerekē haere o ngā matea o ngā tamariki, te ako a te whānau, me te tupu o te kōhanga reo. Ka noho pūmau te kuia, te kaiwhakahaere tari, me ngā kaimahi ki ngā taumata teitei o te tuhi mauhanga, i roto i ngā tikanga herenga ā-ture katoa e pā ana ki ngā tamariki. He pakari te arotake me te pūrongo puta noa i ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. Ka noho mōhio te whānau ki te āhua o ngā mahi me te whakawhanaketanga o tō rātou kōhanga reo, tae atu ki ō rātou wawata, ō rātou matea hoki.

Te whai wāhi a te whānau.Ko te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki me ā rātou akoranga e tino hāpaitia ana nā te whai wāhi mātātoa a te whānau. Ka noho pūmau te whānau ki te hāpai i ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanake i ngā mōhiotanga e pā ana ki tō rātou tuakiri ahurea, te ao Māori, me tō rātou tū hei tangata whenua. Kei te whai atu ngā mema o te whānau i te maha o ngā tūmomo kaupapa whakangungu e hāpai ana i tā rātou ako i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo me te reo Māori.

He aronga rautaki.He aronga tā te mahere rautaki a te whānau. E whai wāhi atu ana te whānau i ō rātou wawata, ngā whāinga, me ngā kaupapa matua mō ngā tamariki. Kua āta whakaarohia te mahere rautaki me te mahere whakahaere, ā, ka aro nui kia tupu tonu.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

He nui rawa ngā wā kāore ngā tamariki e whakaatu ana te hiahia ki ngā mahi akoranga. Ko te nuinga o ēnei e mahia ana i ngā ata, ā, tae rā noa ki te poupoutanga o te rā. Kāore te hōtaka o te wā e aro ana ki ngā kaingākautanga o ngā tamariki, ngā matea me ngā akoranga e hiahiatia ana e rātou. Ko tētahi kaupapa matua, ko te arotake i te āhua o te whakahaere, te whakamahere, me te whakatinana i ngā akoranga o Te Whāriki mā ngā tamariki.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013,me tētahiRārangi Tātari Whaiaromā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, ā, e whakaae ana hoki te whānau, kia toro atu te kaiako ki te tino tautoko me te ārahi o ngā kaimahi i Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, hei whakatutuki, hei whakapai hoki i ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai ki tēnei pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, hei whakawhanake ake.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e rua.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

3 Hakihea 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Hau Kapua is located on the grounds of Bayswater Primary School, North Shore, Auckland. The kaiako and kaiwhakahāere have been members of the kōhanga reo for a long time. Whānau are focussed on providing their children with rich learning opportunities in te ao Māori. Whānau state that through te reo me ngā tīkanga Māori, their children’s knowledge and confidence about who they are as Māori is able to develop.

Children are nurtured by their whānau in a caring environment. Whānau are still developing appropriate strategies to support children to develop confidence and knowledge about who they are in te ao Māori.

The strong commitment by the kuia, kaiwhakahāere and the whānau to support their children’s learning contributes to each child’s successful development as confident, curious and keen independent learners. Children are confident and settled in their learning environment. They demonstrate a good understanding of the importance of whānaungatanga.

The programme and overall strategies that are used do not adequately cater for or respond well to the changing interests and needs of all children. A more child responsive and centred programme based on Te Whāriki learning should build on children’s strengths. The whānau must work together to review the way they manage, design and deliver the programme to children.

All other areas of kōhanga reo management are well planned, managed, reviewed and reported.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose ngā taumata whakahirahira as the focus for this review.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Background

How well are our children developing confidence and knowledge about who they are in te ao Māori?

Children are nurtured by their whānau in a caring environment. Whānau are still developing appropriate strategies to support children to develop confidence and knowledge about who they are in te ao Māori.

Areas of good performance

Children are confident in the kōhanga reo. There are many opportunities for children to develop an understanding of whanaungatanga. Whānau learn beside their children and support them. Whānau and kaimahi encourage respectful relationships among children. Tuakana appropriately care for their younger teina. Relationships between children are respectful and meaningful.

Children confidently explore their cultural heritage and language. There are many instances throughout the day where individuals can choose their own learning activities. These opportunities allow kaimahi to enrich and extend children’s developing knowledge of who they are in te ao Māori, through the use of te reo Māori.

Children have a wide range of resources for exploration and learning. The whānau and kaimahi have established well resourced environments for creative exploration of te ao Māori. Children know their environments well and are familiar with the resources. They often show a strong interest in selecting and managing their own learning.

Areas for development

While children respond appropriately to instructions they have limited conversations in te reo Māori. Children need more opportunities to use te reo Māori in conversations that extends their knowledge of the language.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How well are the needs and aspirations of children and whānau planned, managed and reviewed by the whānau?

Children and their whānau needs and aspirations are adequately managed and monitored.

Areas of good performance

Kōhanga reo management. The kōhanga reo is well managed in the interests of children and according to whānau aspirations and needs. Monthly hui are strongly centred on the continued needs of children, whānau learning and the growth of the kōhanga reo. The kuia and administrator and kaimahi maintain high standards of record keeping for all compliance requirements for children. Review and reporting across all areas of the kōhanga reo is robust. Whānau are well informed about the overall performance and development of their kōhanga reo including their aspirations and needs.

Whānau participation. Children’s development and learning is well supported through active whānau participation. Whānau are committed supporting children to develop knowledge about their cultural identity, the Māori world and their special place as tangata whenua. Whānau members are on various training courses to help them learn more about the kōhanga reo kaupapa and te reo Māori.

Strategic direction. The whānau has a focussed strategic plan. They have shared aspirations, goals and priorities for children. The strategic and management plans are well considered and strongly focus on continual growth.

Areas for development

There are many instances where children clearly show a disinterest in learning activities. Many activities last throughout the morning until lunchtime. The current programme does not adequately centre on children’s interests, needs or learning preferences. Review of the way in which Te Whāriki learning for children is managed, planned and implemented is a priority.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO LicensedKōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends and the whānau agree that the kaiako continue to work with the district Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust representatives to address and improve the areas identified for development in this ERO report.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in two years.

Lynda Pura Watson
