Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike ki Te Kuratini o Poike, ki Tauranga. Ko te tokoiti tonu ki te rārangi ingoa, ko te raihana hou hoki e pā ana ki te noho ki ngā whare o te kuratini, e whai pānga ana ki te āheinga o te kōhanga reo ki te whakapūmau i ngā whakahaeretanga mō te pae tawhiti. Kua toro atu te whānau ki te āwhina o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ki te whakatutuki haere i aua wero.

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike i roto ngā tau e rua.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike ki Te Kuratini o Poike, ki Tauranga. Ka ahu mai ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo i ngā ākonga e kuraina ana ki te kuratini. I tāutuhia ki te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i te Whiringa-ā-rangi o 2011, ngā āwangawanga i hāngai ai ki te iti noa o ngā tamariki e whakauru mai ana ki te kōhanga reo. He tino wero tēnei ki te kōhanga reo i nāianei, ā, ka pā hoki ki ngā whakaritenga, ngā akoranga, me ngā whakahaeretanga. E mahi ana ngā kaimahi o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te whānau o te kōhanga reo, ki te whakatutuki i aua take.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i te reo me ngā tikanga Māori ki tētahi taiao e āta poipoi ana i te whanaungatanga. Kua tino whai wāhi mai ngā mātua ki te hāpai i ngā whakahaerenga o te kōhanga reo me te tari i ia rā. Tokorua ngā kuia e whakarato ana i ngā akoranga o Te Whāriki mā ngā tamariki me te whānau.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu ana i te whanaungatanga ki te kōhanga reo?

Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki tō rātou whakaute mō rātou anō, ā, mō ētahi atu hoki ki tētahi taiao e whakapūmau ana i te reo me ngā tikanga Māori.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i te hōhonutanga o ngā wheako reo Māori. Kōrero Māori ai ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki, puta noa i te rā. Tokorua ngā kuia, ā, ko te reo Māori tō rāua reo taketake. Ka whakamahi rāua i ngā rautaki e whai hua ana ki te whakatairanga i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki te taha o ngā tamariki i a rātou e tūhura haere ana, e noho ā-whānau ana hoki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi whakahihiri me ngā rautaki hei kapo atu, hei whakaongaonga anō hoki i te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. Ka whakanuia ngā whakamātau a ngā tamariki. E āta whakawhanake ana te puāwai mai o tō rātou reo Māori.

Kua āta tūhonohono ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō, ki ngā pākeke hoki. Ka whai pānga matua te whanaungatanga ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He maha ngā wā ka hāpai, ka tautoko hoki tētahi i tētahi i roto i ngā taumahi me ngā mahinga. He kaha tā rātou whakawhitiwhiti haere i tō rātou wāhi, ā, kei te mārama hoki ki a rātou ngā tikanga ka whakatinanahia puta noa i te rā. Ka kitea te mōhio, te noho whakaute anō hoki o ngā tamariki ki ngā karakia e whai pānga ana ki tō rātou ao. Ka whakaongaongatia ngā tamariki ki te whakapuakitanga ā-whakaari nei o ngā kōrero tuku iho e pā ana ki ngā atua Māori. E ako ana ngā tamariki i te whakahirahiratanga o te whakapapa. Ka kitea te whakaute, te manaaki hoki o ngā tamariki mō rātou anō, ā, mō ētahi atu hoki.

Ka whai pūtake ngā wheako akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, e whakapūmau ana tēnei i tō rātou tū, tō rātou hononga anō hoki ki te kōhanga reo me te whānau. Ka ako rātou i ngā karakia e pā ana ki ngā tūmomo horopaki. Ia rā, ka whakapuakihia ngā mihimihi, ngā pepeha, ngā kōrerorero, me ngā waiata hei hāpai i te āta tūhonohono mārika o ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui. Ka ako ngā tamariki i te whai pānga o ngā tikanga Māori ki te whanaungatanga, tō rātou ahurea, me tō rātou reo.

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao akoranga e noho mahana ana, e noho hāneanea ana hoki. He wāteatea ngā wāhi i āta whakaritea ai, ā, e mau ana hoki i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi, rauemi hoki hei whiringa mā ngā tamariki. Kua whakarite ngā kuia me ngā mātua i ngā wāhi o roto hei poipoi i te tūhura takirōpū, takitahi hoki. Kei te tino mōhio ngā tamariki ki tō rātou wāhi. Ka hāpai ngā wāhi akoranga i ngā matea ako o te katoa o te tamaiti, tae atu ki te whakawhanaketanga ā-tinana, ā-tangata, ā-whatumanawa, ā-hinengaro hoki. E tino tautokona ana te ako a ngā tamariki, ā, ka noho ngākau nui rātou ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

E ai ki te whānau, ko te tokoiti tonu o ngā tamariki ki te rārangi ingoa me ngā herenga pūtea e pā tōraro ana ki ngā whai wāhitanga ki te whakatairanga ake i te ako a ngā tamariki. Ka hāngai hoki tēnei ki ngā haerenga me ētahi atu tūmomo kaupapa ki waho atu i te kōhanga reo. Kua toro atu te whānau ki te āwhina o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ki te whakatutuki wawe i ngā tino take e whai pānga ana ki tō rātou tupu me te rōnakitanga.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ko te atawhai i ngā tamariki, ko tā rātou ako hoki e pou here ana i te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo me tana tari. Ahakoa te tokoiti o ngā tamariki, kei reira hoki ngā mātua e noho pūmau mutunga kore ana ki te tautoko i te kōhanga reo. E arotahi ana te whānau ki ngā wāhanga matua o te whakahaeretanga e mau nei i te ako a ngā tamariki me te whānau ki te pūtake o ngā whakaritenga katoa. Ko te tautoko tonu, te tautoko mātātoa hoki o ō rātou kuia e tino hāpai hihiri nei i te whakapūmautanga o te whānau i te kaupapa o tō rātou kōhanga reo mō ngā tamariki. Ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā whakataunga.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore i te tino kaha te tūnga o te kōhanga reo ki te tupu, te rōnaki, me te whakahaere kia whai hua ai. He maha ngā āhuatanga o te whakahaeretanga kāhore anō kia whakatakotohia ngā tuhinga me ngā pūnaha e tika ana, hei whakatutuki i ngā herenga ā-ture me ngā paearu raihana. Tāpiri atu ki tēnā, he tino herenga ā-pūtea hoki nā runga i te tokoiti o ngā tamariki ki te rārangi ingoa me ngā tikanga pūtea o te raihana hou, ā, e noho āwangawanga ana te rōnakitanga me te tupu tonutanga o te kōhanga reo.

Kua tīmata te whānau ki te arotake me te whakahou ake i ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga ki te tūtohinga o te kōhanga reo. Kāhore anō ngā kaupapa here o te hauora me te haumaru kia arotakengia hei whakatutuki i ngā paearu raihana. He mea nui kia whakahou ake te whānau i ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga, hei whakatutuki i te Ture Mātauranga Kōhungahunga o te tau 2008.

Kāhore i te rawaka te whakahaere i ngā kaimahi. Kāhore anō kia oti ngā kirimana a ngā kaimahi, ā, kāhore hoki e mau ana i ngā mōhiohio matua e pā ana ki te tuku mahi. Kāhore te mahi arohaehae e whakatutukihia ana mā ngā kaimahi.

E tino taunaki ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia toro atu tonu te whānau ki te ārahitanga me ngā tohutohu āwhina a Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ki te whakatutuki wawe i ngā wero nei e whai pānga matua ana ki ngā whakaritenga, ngā whakahaeretanga, me te ako a ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Ngā mahi hei whakatutuki i ngā herenga ā-ture

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture, ā, e hāngai ana ēnei ki te hauora, te haumaru, me te whakahaeretanga. Hei whakatutuki i āna herenga, me whakapai ake te kōhanga reo i te āhua o āna mahi i roto i ngā wāhanga e whai ake nei, arā, me:

3.1 whakarite i te āta whakawhanaketanga o te katoa o ngā whakaritenga hauora, haumaru hoki, kia āhei ai ki te āta whakatinana i ngā tikanga hauora, tikanga haumaru katoa, tae atu ki te tuhinga o ngā aituā, ngā arowhai mōrearea, te arowhai a ngā pirihimana, te moe, me ngā akoako hōneatanga ki te taha o ngā tamariki inā he ahi, he rū whenua rānei
[R 46 (1)(2) Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; H&S taumata whakarite: whānui, Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

3.2 whakarite i te āta whakawhanaketanga, te āta whakatinanatanga anō hoki o te katoa o ngā taumata mahi e pā ana ki te kāwanatanga, te whakahaeretanga, me te whakahaere tari, tae atu anō hoki te arotake whaiaro me ngā tikanga whakahaere katoa e pā ana ki te tuku mahi me ngā kaimahi, hei painga mō ngā tamariki me ngā whakahaeretanga.
[R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; GMA 5-7 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • mahi te whānau ki te taha o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo ki te whakawhanake i tētahi mahere mahi ka whakapiki ake i te tatauranga tamariki, ka whakatairanga hoki i te kounga o te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo, ā, ka whakapūmau i te rōnakitanga o te kōhanga reo hei painga mō te mātauranga o ngā tamariki
  • tuku i tētahi tauira o te mahere mahi ki Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike ki Te Kuratini o Poike, ki Tauranga. Ko te tokoiti tonu ki te rārangi ingoa, ko te raihana hou hoki e pā ana ki te noho ki ngā whare o te kuratini, e whai pānga ana ki te āheinga o te kōhanga reo ki te whakapūmau i ngā whakahaeretanga mō te pae tawhiti. Kua toro atu te whānau ki te āwhina o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ki te whakatutuki haere i aua wero.

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike is at the Bay of Plenty Polytechnic Institute in Tauranga. The consistent low roll and new licence to occupy conditions from the polytechnic impacts on the ability of the kōhanga reo to sustain operations for the longer term. Members have sought the help of TKRNT to work through these challenges.

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike will be within two years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike is at the Bay of Plenty Polytechnic Institute in Tauranga. The kōhanga reo relies on the enrolment of children of students studying at the polytechnic. The last ERO report, November 2011, highlighted concerns about limited enrolments at the kōhanga reo. This has become a major challenge for the kōhanga reo, and now impacts on operations, learning and management. Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) kaimahi are working with the kōhanga reo whānau to respond to these matters.

Children learn te reo me ngā tikanga Māori in an environment that fosters whanaungatanga. Parent help has been significant to support daily operations and the administration of the kōhanga reo. Two local kuia provide Te Whāriki learning for tamariki and whānau.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children demonstrate whanaungatanga at kōhanga reo?

Children show respect for oneself and others in an environment that reinforces te reo me ngā tikanga Māori.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children receive rich te reo Māori language experiences. Kaimahi speak te reo Māori to children throughout the day. There are two kuia who are native speakers of te reo Māori. They use effective strategies to promote conversations with children during exploration and shared whānau time. Kaimahi use a range of exciting activities and strategies to captivate and stimulate children’s use of te reo Māori. Children are praised for their efforts. They are developing well as emerging speakers of te reo Māori.

Children have formed close relationships with other children and adults. Whanaungatanga is integral to children’s learning. They often help and support each other with activities and routines. They move with confidence in their surroundings and understand the expectations for tikanga during the day. Children show an awareness of and respect for karakia in their daily lives. They are stimulated by the dramatic expression of shared stories about atua Māori. Children are learning about the importance of whakapapa.Children show respect and care for themselves and others.

Children have meaningful learning experiences that reinforce their place and connection to kōhanga reo and whānau. They learn karakia for different situations. Daily mihimihi, pepeha, shared stories and waiata are all used to help children make meaningful connections to te ao Māori and the wider world. Children learn to associate tikanga Māori with whanaungatanga, their culture and language.

Children learn in a warm and comfortable learning environment. There are spacious and well organised areas with a range of activities and resources for children to choose. Kuia and parents have organised the indoor areas in ways that foster shared and independent exploration. Children know their environment well. The learning areas support children’s holistic learning needs including physical, social, emotional and intellectual development. Children are well supported in their learning and enjoy being at kōhanga reo.

Key Next Steps

Whānau state that the consistent low roll and financial constraints impact on opportunities to enhance the learning for children. This includes trips and other events outside the kōhanga reo. The whānau has have sought the assistance of TKRNT to work through matters of urgency that impact on their growth and viability.

Planning and Evaluation

Children’s care and learning underpins kōhanga reo management and administration. Despite the low roll, there are parents who have shown unwavering commitment to support the kōhanga reo. Whānau hui are focused on key areas of management that have children and whānau learning at the heart of all matters. The continued active support of their kuia is invaluable to whānau for preserving the kaupapa of their kōhanga reo for children. Children are a priority in decision making.

Key Next Steps

The kōhanga reo is not well placed for growth, sustainability and effective management. There are a number of management areas that do not have well developed documentation and systems in place that reflect the current regulations and licencing criteria. Also, significant financial constraints brought about by low enrolments and new licence financial conditions places the kōhanga reo in a vulnerable position for sustainability and growth.

The whānau are in the early stages of reviewing updating policies and procedures in the kōhanga reo tūtohinga. Health and safety policies have not been reviewed to fulfil the licensing criteria. It is important for whānau to update policies and procedures to comply with the Early Childhood Services Regulations 2008.

Personnel management is inadequate. Employment contracts for employees are incomplete and lack important employment information. There is no appraisal carried out for employees.

ERO strongly recommends the whānau continue to seek guidance and advice from TKRNT to work through these immediate challenges that impact directly on operations, management and learning for children.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Actions for compliance

ERO identified areas of non-compliance relating to health, safety and management. To meet requirements the kōhanga reo needs to improve its performance in the following areas:

3.1 ensure that all health and safety practice standards are well developed for implementation of all health and safety requirements, including the recording of accidents, hazard checks, police vetting, sleep, fire and earthquake evacuation drills with children.
[R46 (1)(2) Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; H&S practice standards: general, Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

3.2 ensure that all governance, management and administration practice standards are well developed and implemented, including internal review and all employment and personnel management requirements for children and operations.
[R47 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; GMA 5-7 practice standards: general, Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendations

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations:

  • whānau work with TKRNT to develop an action plan that will increase the roll, raise the quality of management by whānau of the kōhanga reo and secure the viability of the kōhanga reo in the interests of children’s education
  • submit a copy of the action plan to ERO.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike is at the Bay of Plenty Polytechnic Institute in Tauranga. The consistent low roll and new licence to occupy conditions from the polytechnic impacts on the ability of the kōhanga reo to sustain operations for the longer term. Members have sought the help of TKRNT to work through these challenges.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Poike will be within two years.
