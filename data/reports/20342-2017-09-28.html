1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Pihi o te Purapura Pai ki Birkdale, i Te Raki Paewhenua, i Tāmaki-makau-rau. E whakahaerehia ana mō tēnei wā, ki ētahi whare i Te Kura Tuatahi o Birkdale. E hangaia ana tētahi whare hou mō te kōhanga reo i tēnei wā, ā, ka oti ngā mahi hanga hei te mutunga o te tau 2017. He tokomaha ngā mema o te whānau kua roa nei e whai wāhi atu ana ki tēnei kōhanga reo. He tokomaha anō hoki ngā kaimahi i noho ai ki te kōhanga reo i a rātou e tamariki ana.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou māramatanga ki te whanaungatanga?

Ka kitea te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te whanaungatanga mā te pai o ō rātou hononga, ō rātou taunekeneke anō hoki ki ētahi atu.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E rongo ana ngā tamariki i te pai o te whanaungatanga. He tokomaha ngā whānau e tino mōhio ana ki a rātou anō. Ka āta atawhaitia, ka āta manaakitia hoki ngā whānau katoa. Ka whakamihi, ka whakanui hoki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki mō ā rātou mahi angitu. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te pukuhohe me te katakata puta noa i te rā. Kei te harikoakoa ngā kaimahi, ā, e poipoi ana hoki rātou i ngā hononga e āta whai pūtake ana. Ka ako ngā tamariki i te whakaute, te whakaaro nui, me te manaakitanga mō ētahi atu. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te mahi takirōpū me te mahi takitahi. E ākina ana te tū o ngā tamariki ki te ārahi i ngā wā karakia me ngā wā mihimihi o te ata. Kei te harikoa ngā tamariki.

E whakawhanake ana te ngākau titikaha o ngā tamariki i roto i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki pāhekoheko, whakaongaonga hoki hei whakawhanake i te reo Māori o ngā tamariki, tae atu ki ngā pātai whānui, te whakatauira, te whakapuakitanga o ngā kīwaha, ngā kēmu matimati, me ngā pakiwaitara. He maha ngā wā e whakamahi ana ngā tamariki tuākana i ngā rerenga kōrero hōhonu e whakawhānui ana i tō rātou mōhiotanga ki te reo, me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga reo. Whakawhiti kōrero ai ngā pākeke me ngā tamariki tuākana i ngā wā katoa. He tākare te whakahoki kōrero a ngā tuākana ki ngā pākeke mā te reo Māori. He māhorahora te tāwhai a ngā pēpi i ngā kupu me ngā mahinga. E tino tautokona ana te kōrero a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori.

E whai wāhi nui ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi hōtaka akoranga i āta whakaritea ai, ā, e whakahihiri ana, e whakaongaonga ana anō hoki i ō rātou whakaaro. Ka hāpai ngā pākeke i te tiaki o ngā tamariki i ō rātou matea ake me ō rātou ngākau nuitanga ake. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i te kawe a ngā tamariki i ā rātou ake akoranga. E āhei ana rātou ki te mahi takitahi, ki te taha rānei o ētahi atu. E wātea ana ki a rātou te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi o roto, o waho hoki. Kua āta whakaritea ngā mahinga. He ngāwari te whakawhiti a ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmomo wāhanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Ka āta whakarite ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te kōkiri i ā rātou akoranga, i runga anō i te āhua o ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. Ka whakawerohia te kori tinana me ngā whakaaro o ngā tamariki. He ākonga tūmāia ngā tamariki, ā, e āta mātakina ana hoki.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

E whai pūtake ana ngā mahere. Ka hāngai ngā mahere ā-wāhanga ki ngā kaupapa ako me ngā mahere ako, ā, e whakatakoto ana hoki i ngā whāinga ako mā ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā pūkete me ngā pūrongo ahu whakamua hei whakatakoto haere i te ako a ngā tamariki. Whakawhiti kōrero ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā mō te hōtaka akoranga, ā, ka tāutu anō hoki rātou i ngā whakapaitanga. E whai wāhi nui ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi hōtaka akoranga e poipoi ana i ō rātou matea.

He pai te whakahaeretanga o te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo. Tū ai ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, ā, he pai anō hoki te taetae atu. He auau ngā mahi ki te arotake i ngā kaupapa here. Kua whakaritea ngā kaupapa here matua me ngā tukanga matua e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. Tautokona ai e ngā mema o te whānau ngā huihuinga me ngā kaupapa mahi i te kōhanga reo. E hāpai ana te aromātai o roto i ngā whakapaitanga, puta noa i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga. He pai te kaha pūrongo atu ki te whānau. I tēnei wā e arotahi ana te whānau ki te whakatutukitanga o ngā mahi ki te whakatū i te whare hou o tō rātou kōhanga reo. E whakahaere ana te whānau i ngā whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Ka kitea te kawe tika a te whānau i te tū hei kaituku mahi. E hāngai ana ngā tuhinga kaimahi ki te wā. Ko aua tuhinga, ko ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi, ko ngā kirimana, ko te arowhai i ngā pirihimana, ā, ko ngā arohaehae hoki. Ko ngā putanga i te tukanga arohaehae a ngā kaimahi e whakamōhio ana i ngā whakataunga mō te whakawhanaketanga ngaio e tika ana. E whai hua ana ngā tamariki i ngā kaimahi e noho mārama ana ki ō rātou tūranga me ā rātou kawenga mahi.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Me whakamahine ake i te takotoranga mahere o tēnei wā, hei āta whakarite i ngā tino hononga mārama ki Te Whāriki.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo te taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakamāramahia ake e ngā kaimahi ngā tukanga whakamahere, kia āta puta ake ai Te Whāriki.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Pihi o Te Purapura Pai i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Pihi o te Purapura Pai is in Birkdale on the North Shore, Auckland. It is in temporary facilities on Birkdale Primary School. A new kōhanga reo building is currently being built and will be ready by the end of 2017. Many whānau are long standing members of this kōhanga reo. Several kaimahi are former tamariki at the kōhanga reo.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children display their understanding of whanaungatanga?

Children show they understand whanaungatanga through positive relationships and interactions with others.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children experience positive relationships. Many whānau know each other well. Everyone is made to feel welcome and comfortable. Kaimahi praise and acknowledge children for their successes. Children experience humour and laughter throughout the day. Kaimahi are positive and foster purposeful relationships. Children learn about respect, consideration and care for others. Kaimahi provide opportunities for children to work together or on their own. Children are encouraged to take on leadership roles during karakia and morning mihimihi. Children are happy.

Children are developing confidence in te reo Māori. Kaimahi use a range of interactive and stimulating strategies to develop children’s te reo Māori including open questioning, modelling, the use of kiwaha, finger plays and story telling. Older children often use complex sentences to extend language knowledge and development. Adults constantly have conversations with the older children. Tuākana eagerly respond to adults in te reo Māori. Babies happily mimic words and actions. Children are well supported to speak te reo Māori.

Children enjoy an organised learning programme that excites and stimulates their thinking. Adults support children to manage their own personal needs and interests. Kaimahi encourage children to take responsibility for learning. They are able to work on their own or with others. They have access to a range of resources both indoors and outdoors. Routines are well established. Children transition smoothly between aspects of the learning programme. Kaimahi ensure children have opportunities to initiate learning based on their interests. Children are challenged both physically and mentally. Children are well supervised, confident learners.

Planning and Evaluation

Planning is purposeful. Term plans align to kaupapa ako and mahere ako, and include learning goals for children. Kaimahi use profiles and progression reports to track children’s learning. Kaimahi regularly discuss the learning programme and identify improvements. Children enjoy a learning programme that caters to their needs.

Whānau manage the operations of their kōhanga reo well. Whānau hui are held regularly and well attended. Policy reviews are regular. Core health and safety policies and procedures are in place. Whānau members regularly support kōhanga reo events and activities. Internal evaluation supports improvement across all areas of operations. There is a good level of reporting back to whānau. The whānau is currently focussed on the completion of their new kōhanga reo building. The whānau manage the operations of the kōhanga reo in the best interest of their children.

Whānau show they are responsible employers. Personnel documentation is up to date. This includes job descriptions, employment contracts, police vetting and appraisals. The outcomes from the kaimahi appraisal process inform decisions about appropriate professional development. Children benefit when kaimahi are clear about their roles and responsibilities.

Key Next Steps

Further refinement of the current planning format to ensure the links to Te Whāriki are clear and explicit.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendation:

  • That kaimahi will clarify planning processes to make Te Whāriki more evident.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Pihi o Te Purapura Pai will be in three years.
