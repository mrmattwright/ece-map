1 Background

Introduction

A Supplementary Review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager, Review Services in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a centre has taken towards addressing issues specified in a previous education review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of the Whangarei Childcare Centre governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the March 2012 ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • improving the quality of governance and management practices
  • improving the quality of the programme
  • improving the indoor learning environment
  • any other issues relevant to the governance, management and operation of the Whangarei Childcare Centre that arise during the course of this review.

2 Evaluation Findings

Background

Whangarei Childcare Centre offers all day education and care for up to 45 children from three months to five years of age. The premises are organised into two main play spaces to cater for the needs of different age groups. The centre operates as a non-profit centre and is governed by an elected parent committee.

Since ERO’s 2012 review a new leadership structure has been introduced by the centre manager and governance committee. The 2012 ERO report identified that significant improvements were needed in governance and management, programme planning, and promoting success for Māori to enhance children’s learning outcomes. Further improvements were needed in the indoor learning environment.

Very good progress has been made since the 2012 ERO review. Managers and teachers responded well to the ERO report and have taken a number of steps to improve centre practices. The development of respectful relationships between adults has improved the emotional environment for children in the centre.

Areas of progress

The Governance Committee has established effective processes. It has reviewed and made changes to the Society Constitution and has clearly documented differences between governance and management responsibilities. The committee has made good use of external support to rebuild positive relationships between the community, committee members and staff. Long-term goals have been developed and are reflected in the annual business plan.

The centre manager is providing positive, professional leadership. She has managed the pace of change well in developing centre-wide teaching and learning practices. Multiple opportunities are now provided for lead teachers to improve their practice and to mentor other team members. A responsive and reflective performance appraisal process that promotes individual teacher responsibility and improved professional practice has been introduced.

The centre manager and teachers have made very good progress in improving programme planning and assessment systems. Planning is responsive to child-led and teacher-initiated interests. Individual records of children’s learning are attractive and show children’s participation in the programme. Teachers are building their understanding of ways to evaluate the impact of the programme and teaching practices on improved learning outcomes for children.

The indoor learning environment is visually attractive and stimulating for children. It is spacious and thoughtfully presented with defined learning areas. Children have many opportunities to revisit their learning through programme wall displays. A wide variety of resources is accessible to children.

Positive relationships have been re-established between the Governance Committee and the whānau group. Consultation with the whānau group has resulted in a centre pepeha, or motto, being developed. Children’s understanding of the pepeha promotes their connections with whānau, the centre and wider community. Children have a strong sense of well being and belonging.

Areas for further improvement

ERO and centre leaders agree that the key next steps for centre leaders are to:

  1. embed newly established self-review systems and performance management processes to promote ongoing improvement
  2. establish a shared understanding among teachers of effective programme planning, assessment and evaluation practices that result in improved learning outcomes for children.

3 Future Action

ERO is confident that the service is being managed in the interest of the children. Therefore ERO will review the service again as part of the regular review cycle.
