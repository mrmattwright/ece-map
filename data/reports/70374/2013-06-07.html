1 Evaluation of ABC St Martins

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

ABC St Martins is owned and administered by Kidicorp Education and Care Centres. The centre provides all day education and care for children from birth to school age in separate nursery and preschool areas. The centre is licensed for a maximum of 40 children. Teachers are proud of their small family-like environment.

The centre has had a stable teaching team for some time. A recent early childhood graduate began at the centre at the beginning of 2013. All teachers, except one in training, are qualified and registered early childhood teachers.

The centre suffered earthquake damage during the Canterbury earthquakes of 2010 and 2011, and was closed for three weeks. It is still awaiting repairs to its outside area. This is affecting the timing of planned redevelopment of the outdoor areas. Both indoor areas have been recently renovated.

Centre managers, staff and parents have recently reviewed the centre philosophy. It focuses on providing a respectful, welcoming, and inclusive environment where communication between whƒÅnau and staff is actively encouraged.

The Review Findings

Children are happy and settled at the centre. Teachers respond to their needs and interests. They provide good choices for children through the range of resources, and the way that they communicate with them. Children experience positive interactions with adults that successfully build their confidence and extend their social skills. They notice children, question and respond to them in ways that challenge and extend their learning.

In the nursery, children are well nurtured by warm and caring staff. There is a calm atmosphere as teachers respond sensitively to children‚Äôs actions. Babies and infants settle well. Routines are well communicated and respectful. Toddlers are confident and competent, and unafraid of approaching adults. Teachers acknowledge the need for interaction between siblings and provide opportunities for siblings in different parts of the centre to have time together.

Teachers are proactive in getting to know children and their families. They use a range of ways to include and involve them in the life of the centre. There are good links with home evident in the environment, including framed photographs of family members, and a display of a recent whƒÅnau afternoon tea event. The centre manager has recently held parent interviews and followed up with a written summary of the meeting. Parents are encouraged to contribute to the children‚Äôs profile books and do so through writing comments on learning stories, and providing photographs of family activities for children to revisit at the centre. They are able to participate in comprehensive surveys twice a year.

MƒÅori children are supported through the centre‚Äôs focus on actively involving families. There is informal discussion after enrolment about parents‚Äô aspirations for their children. Teachers provide some bicultural learning opportunities. One staff member is supporting others to develop in this area. This is a good model of tuakana teina as teachers learn with each other and alongside the children. Improving biculturalism has also been identified as one of the goals in the centre‚Äôs strategic plan.

Centre managers and staff are now involved in effective self review. A new process was used to undertake a comprehensive review of the centre‚Äôs philosophy. A strategic plan has now been developed with major reviews planned through to 2015.

Kidicorp and centre managers work together to provide clear direction for the teaching team. The Kidicorp management framework guides centre operations. Kidicorp managers are making good use of this framework to identify where improvements should occur. There are clear links between future planning, organisational self review, staff appraisal and professional development.

Key Next Steps

Kidicorp and centre managers have identified, and ERO agrees, that the next steps to improve learning and teaching include:

  • building on and extending the effective practices for the assessment of and planning for children‚Äôs learning
  • continue to support staff to use the new self-review process so that it is used for all reviews, spontaneous as well as formal reviews
  • strengthen bicultural learning and practices in meaningful ways to further support success for MƒÅori children as MƒÅori and to enrich the learning of all children in the heritage of Aotearoa New Zealand
  • continue to monitor the use of indoor and outdoor spaces to ensure the best use is made for children‚Äôs learning.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of ABC St Martins completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they select ‚Äòhave‚Äô or ‚Äòhave not‚Äô taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum

  • health and safety practices

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
