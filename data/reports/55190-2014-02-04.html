2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Awatea Hou ki Pā Harakeke, ki Heretaunga. Kei runga noa atu te āhua o ngā pūrongo o mua a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga mō tēnei kōhanga reo. Kei te whakarato tonu te whānau me ngā kaimahi i te kounga kairangi o ngā wheako akoranga ki ngā tamariki. Kua kī te rārangi ingoa o tēnei kōhanga reo, ā, 32 tamariki te rahi o tōna raihana. Haere ai te nuinga o ngā tamariki ki tēnei kōhanga reo i ia rā.

I whakaritea e te whānau me ngā kaimahi ko Mana Whenua te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā pepeha, ngā whakapapa, ngā pakiwaitara, ngā waiata, me ngā kōwaiwai pakitara ki te āta whakawhanake i te tuakiri, te tino aronga toi whenuatanga anō hoki o ngā tamariki. Ka akiaki rātou i ngā tamariki ki te ārahi me te whai wāhi atu mā te toro atu ki ngā tikanga. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ō rātou tūranga hei tangata whenua. He pakari te whakamahinga, te kōrerorero anō hoki o ngā tamariki tuākana i te reo Māori i ngā wā ōkawa. E tupu haere ana te noho pūmau o te whānau ki te whakapiki ake i tō rātou āheinga ki te kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga o tētahi taiao o te reo Māori.

He hanganga, he riterite hoki te hōtaka taumahi o ia rā, ā, kei te kounga kairangi hoki. Kua whakaritea e ngā kaimahi ngā pokapū akoranga muramura e mau ana i te maha o ngā rauemi, ā, e tū ana ēnei puta noa i te rūma tākaro matua. He whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga, te tākaro mō ngā wā roa, me te tūhura. Ko ētahi o ngā tino āhuatanga nui o te hōtaka o ia rā mā ngā tamariki, ko ngā haerenga, me te whakamahi i ngā īpapa me ngā rorohiko. Ka eke angitu ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou tākaro, ā rātou akoranga hoki.

He mārama, he tau hoki ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakamahere kaupapa, te aromātai hōtaka, me te aromatawai. Ko ngā kaupapa hihiri e whakawhanake ana i te mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui. Ko te arowhai auau ka hāpai i ngā kaimahi ki te whakatutuki i ngā whakarerekētanga pai mō ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā tūmomo rautaki ki te aromatawai me te aroturuki i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ka noho wātea ēnei mōhiohio ki ngā tamariki me te whānau i ia te wā.

He mātātoa te whai wāhi a te whānau ki te hōtaka o ia rā me ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. E mōhio ana te whānau ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga, me ngā kaupapa taumahi anō hoki ka whakatutukihia ki te kōhanga reo.

Ka tino whai hua ngā mahi a te whānau ki te whakahaere me te whakarite i te kōhanga reo. He pūkeke, he auaha hoki te whānau. He nui ō rātou tūmanako mō ā rātou tamariki. Ka kitea aua tūmanako nā te āta whakawhanaketanga o te mahere rautaki. E mōhio ana te whānau ki te whai pānga nui, te wāriu hoki o te arotake whaiaro, ā, ka whakamahi i aua mōhiohio hei whakapūmau, hei whakapai anō hoki i ā rātou whakaritenga.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Awatea Hou kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui atu o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o Awatea Hou.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Whenua.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamāherehere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ā te whānau
  • whakamāherehere hōtaka, te aromatawai me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Whenua

He whakamārama

I whakaritea e ngā kaimahi me te whānau ko Mana Whenua te kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Ko te tuakiri, te waiora, me te aronga toi whenuatanga o ngā tamariki e whai pānga matua ana ki ā rātou akoranga, ō rātou whakawhanaketanga anō hoki. Ko te taiao tūturu, māori hoki e whai pānga anō hoki hei āhuatanga tonu o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i ia te wā.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te tuakiri me te aronga toi whenuatanga. He pakari te aronga toi whenuatanga, te tuakiri anō hoki o ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā whakapapa, ngā pepeha, ngā kōwaiwai pakitara, me ngā waiata hei whakanui i ngā hononga ā-whānau, ā-hapū, ā-iwi hoki o ia tamaiti. Nā ēnei taumahi me ngā whakaritenga ka taea e ngā tamariki te kite ngā hononga ki waenga i ō rātou ake whakapapa me ngā kōrero tuku iho e pā ana ki a Papatūānuku rāua ko Ranginui. Kei te mōhio hoki ngā tamariki ki ngā tino pou whenua o tō rātou rohe. Ka tū pakari ngā tamariki ki mua i te minenga, i a rātou e whakapuaki ana i ngā mōhiotanga e whai pānga ana ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou whānau hoki.

Ngā tūranga hei tangata whenua. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te tū hei tangata whenua. Whai wāhi ai rātou i ia te wā ki te whakatau me te manaaki i ngā manuhiri. He mātātoa ētahi tamariki ki te ārahi me te whai wāhi ki te whaikōrero, te karanga, te hongi, me te harirū. Kua tau ngā tamariki ki ngā tikanga i roto i ngā kaupapa tuku iho.

Te taiao akoranga. Kua waihangatia e ngā kaimahi tētahi taiao akoranga muramura, whakahihiri hoki mō ā rātou tamariki. He maha ngā pokapū akoranga i whakatūngia ai puta noa i te kōhanga reo, ā, e mau ana ēnei i te whānui anō hoki o ngā tūmomo rauemi. Kua waihangatia e ngā kaimahi aua pokapū i runga anō i ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā matea whakawhanaketanga hoki o ngā tamariki. He whai wāhitanga mā ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i ō rātou āheinga, ō rātou pūkenga hoki i roto i te pāngarau, te reo matatini, te hangarau, te pūtaiao, te puoro, te kanikani, me te hauhake kai. He maha ngā āhuatanga ka whakawero i ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā kaha o ngā tamariki. Ia tau, hokona atu e te whānau te pūtea nui mō ngā tūmomo rauemi, haerenga anō hoki mā ngā tamariki. He whai wāhitanga anō hoki mō ngā tamariki ki te uru ki ngā tūmomo kaupapa, te kōkiri i ā rātou ake akoranga, te tākaro ki ngā rōpū iti, ngā rōpū nui hoki, me te whai wāhi ki ngā kaupapa ka kōkirihia e ngā pākeke.

Haereere ai ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmomo wāhi e whai pānga nui ana ki a rātou. Ka whakaongaonga aua haerenga i ngā tamariki, ā, ka hono ki ngā kaupapa ako. He whai wāhitanga mā rātou ki te taunekeneke ki ngā tāngata hou, te ako i ngā atua, me te whakahono atu ki te taiao māori, tūturu hoki.

He tino kaha te hononga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao akoranga, ki a rātou anō, ki ngā kaimahi hoki.

Te whakawhanaketanga o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te hōhonutanga o tētahi taiao o te reo Māori. He tino tauira reo ngā kaimahi e whai hua ana ki te kōrero i te reo Māori ki ngā tamariki puta noa i te rā. Ko ngā waiata tētahi tino rautaki ka whakamahia e ngā kaimahi hei whakawhanake i te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. Ka whakahihiri ngā kaimahi i te ako mō ngā tamariki. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero māmā, ā, e āhei ana hoki ki te whai i ngā tohutohu. He pakari ngā tamariki ki te kōrero me te whakamahi i te reo Māori i ngā hui ōkawa.

Te hangarau. Ka ākina ngā tamariki kia tūhura ngā tūmomo ariā mā te whakamahi i te hangarau. E noho wātea ana ngā rorohiko me ngā īpapa ki a rātou. Ka mahi ngātahi ngā tamariki i a rātou e whakamahi ana i aua rauemi. Ka hāpai wawe ngā tamariki pakari i ō rātou hoa i roto i ngā tūmomo kēmu. Ia rā, he akoranga tunu kai, he akoranga kāmura hoki e whakahaerehia ana mā ngā tamariki. He ākonga pakari ngā tamariki i a rātou e tūhura ana i ngā taumahi me ngā kēmu hōhonu ake e whakatairanga ana i te pāngarau, te reo matatini, me ngā tino pūkenga nekeneke.

Ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga. Ka whai hua ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi. He tino tauira ngā kaimahi i roto i ā rātou taunekeneke ki ngā tamariki, te whānau, ā, ki waenganui anō hoki i a rātou. Ka kite hoki i aua tūmomo whanonga ki te āhua o ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā aronga whakakoia hei ārahi, hei whakahaere hoki i ngā tamariki, ā, ka whakanuia ā rātou mahi angitu. He ākonga pakari ngā tamariki e mau nei i te tino aronga waiora.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

I ngā wā katoa ka aromātai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te kounga o te whakamahere me te aromātai i ngā kōhanga reo hei tātari i te kounga me te whānuitanga o te whānau ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere, me te aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaeretanga. Ka hāngai te whakamahere me te aromātai ki te whakamahere hōtaka me te aroturuki i te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whai wāhitanga, te ngākau nuitanga hoki o te whānau. He mātātoa te whai wāhi a te whānau ki te hōtaka o ngā tamariki i ia rā, me ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki ngā whakataunga e whai pānga ana ki ā rātou tamariki. Ka āwhina te whānau i te kōhanga reo, ā, ka maioha ngā kaimahi nā tā rātou whai wāhi. Ko te pakari o ngā pūnaha whakawhiti kōrero ka hāpai i te whānau ki te noho mōhio ki ngā mahi a ā rātou tamariki me ngā kaupapa hoki i te kōhanga reo. Nā te tupu haere o te pūmautanga o te whānau ki te whakamahi me te kōrero i te reo Māori, ka uru ētahi ki ngā akoranga o te reo Māori. Ko te nuinga o ngā mema o te whānau ka kōrero i te reo Māori i a rātou e noho ana ki te kōhanga reo. Ka tino tautoko te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Te whakamahere rautaki. He pūkeke, he auaha hoki te whānau, ā, he nui hoki ō rātou tūmanako mō ā rātou tamariki. He mahere rautaki tā te whānau e whakaraupapa ana i ngā mahi ki te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei. Ko ētahi o aua kaupapa, ko tētahi whare hou, ko te reo Māori, ko te waiora o ngā tamariki me ngā akoranga. Ka hono te mahere ki Te Ara Tūāpae me ngā whāinga rautaki a Ngāti Kahungunu. Ka ū te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakapūmau i ngā whai wāhitanga ako o te kounga kairangi.

Te arotake whaiaro. Ka mārama te whānau ki te whai huatanga me te pūtake o te arotake whaiaro. Ka whakamahi rātou i ngā arowhai hauora, haumaru hoki, ngā pūrongo o ia marama, me tētahi herenga ā-ture a Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo hei arotake i ā rātou whakaritenga i roto i aua wāhanga. Ka tino ngākau nui te whānau ki te whakahaere i ā rātou anō.

Ngā whakaritenga whakahaere. Ka whakahaere, ka whakarite hoki te whānau i te kōhanga reo mā te whai huatanga o ngā mahi. He pakari ngā pūnaha me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ko ngā tuhinga tae atu ki te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa here, me ngā tuhinga kaimahi e hāngai ana ki te rā.

Te whakamahere i ngā kaupapa. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i tētahi tino tauira whakamahere i a rātou e waihanga ana i ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga ā-kaupapa mā ngā tamariki. He matawhānui ngā mahere, ā, ka hono ki Te Whāriki. Ka whai hua te rumaki i ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao kaupapa Māori.

Te aromātai i te hōtaka. Ka noho matua te aromātai hōtaka ki te āwhina i ngā kaimahi ki te whakatutuki i ngā rerekētanga papai mō ngā tamariki. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki i roto i ā rātou akoranga nā te kaha o te āta arotake a ngā kaimahi i te taiao o te kōhanga reo, i ngā ngākau nuitanga o ia tamaiti, ā, i ā rātou whakaritenga whakaako anō hoki.

Te aromatawai. Kei te tino mōhio ngā kaimahi ki ngā āhuatanga e whai pānga ana ki ngā mahi aromatawai ka whai hua. Ko ētahi o aua whakaritenga, ko ngā kōrero urupare a te whānau me ngā kitenga. Nā runga i aua whakaritenga, e āhei ana ngā kaimahi ki te tāutu me te whakamahere anō hoki i ngā taumata akoranga a ia tamaiti.

Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā pukapuka mahi kāinga, hei whakapūmau ake i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, hei hāpai anō hoki i te ako a te whānau.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake

Ngā arohaehae. E mōhio ana te whānau, kāhore anō kia oti i a rātou te arohaehae i ngā kaimahi i ia tau. He tau tā rātou pūnaha arohaehae, ā, e tika ana kia whāia tēnei i ia tau hei hāpai i te whānau ki te tāutu i ngā matea ngaio o ngā kaimahi, me te whakarato anō hoki i te tautoko ngaio e tika ana.

5 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o Te Kōhanga Reo o Awatea Hou, tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro.  I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki: 

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Awatea Hou is located in Flaxmere, Hastings. The kōhanga reo has had an exceptional ERO reporting history. Whānau and kaimahi continue to provide children with high quality learning experiences. The kōhanga reo has a full roll and is licensed for 32 children. Most of these children attend kōhanga reo each day.

Whānau and kaimahi chose Mana Whenua as the focus area for the review. Kaimahi use pepeha, whakapapa, stories, waiata and murals to develop in children a strong sense of identity and belonging. They encourage children to lead and participate using tikanga. Children learn about their roles as tangata whenua. The older children confidently use and speak te reo Māori during formal events. Children are able to follow instructions and use simple phrases and commands. There is a growing commitment among whānau to increase their ability to speak te reo Māori. Children are immersed in a rich te reo Māori environment.

The daily programme of activities has structure, balance and is of high quality. Kaimahi have set up vibrant, well resourced learning centres throughout the main playroom. Children have opportunities to follow their interests, play for extended periods and explore. Outings, and the use of ipads and computers are a feature of the children’s daily programme. Children are successful in their play and learning.

There are clear, sound processes for kaupapa planning, programme evaluation and assessment. Interesting kaupapa develops children’s knowledge of te ao Māori and the wider world. Regular evaluation assists kaimahi to bring about positive change for children. Kaimahi use various methods to assess and monitor children’s learning and development. The information is regularly available to children and whānau.

Whānau are active participants in the children’s daily programme and the operations of the kōhanga reo. Whānau are aware of children’s learning and development and activities that occur in the kōhanga reo.

Whānau manage and organise the kōhanga reo in a highly effective manner. Whānau are determined, creative thinkers. They have high expectations for their children. The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau of Te Kōhanga Reo o Awatea Hou was invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.  This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at Te Kōhanga Reo o Awatea Hou.

4 Findings

Mana Whenua

Background

Kaimahi and whānau chose Mana Whenua as the focus area for the review. Children’s identity, wellbeing and sense of belonging are a critical part of their learning and development. The physical and natural environments are additional components that regularly feature in children’s learning.

Areas of good performance

Identity and belonging. Children have a strong sense of belonging and identity. Kaimahi use whakapapa, pepeha, murals, and waiata to highlight each child’s whānau, hapu and tribal links. These activities and practices enable children to see the connections between their own genealogy and that of the creation story of Papatuanuku and Ranginui. Children are familiar with their cultural landmarks. Children stand proudly before an audience when they share information that relates to themselves and whānau.

Tangata whenua roles. Children are confident in their roles as tangata whenua. They regularly participate in welcoming and hosting visitors. Some children actively lead and participate in whaikorero, karanga, hongi and hariru. Children are comfortable with the use of formalities in a traditional context.

Learning environment. Kaimahi have created a vibrant, exciting learning environment for their children. Several well resourced learning centres are set up throughout the kōhanga reo. Kaimahi have designed these centres around children’s interests and developmental needs. Children have opportunities to develop their capabilities and skills in numeracy, literacy, technology, science, music, dance and cultivation. There are several challenges to focus on the interests and strengths of children. Each year whānau spend a significant amount of funds on children’s resources and excursions. Children have opportunities to participate in kaupapa activities, independent learning, small and large group play and adult led activities.

Children often go on outings to places of interest. Children are stimulated by these outings that are linked to the learning kaupapa. They have opportunities to interact with new people, learn about ngā atua and make connections with the natural and physical environment.

Children have a strong sense of connectedness to their learning environment, each other and kaimahi.

Children’s te reo Māori development. Children are immersed in a rich te reo Māori environment. Kaimahi are effective language models who consistently speak te reo Māori to children throughout the day. Waiata is a highly effective strategy kaimahi use to develop children’s te reo Māori. Kaimahi make language learning exciting and interesting for children. They participate in simple conversations and are able to follow instructions. Children confidently speak and use te reo Māori during formal events.

Technology. Children are encouraged to explore different concepts through the use of technology. They have ready access to computers and ipads. Children work collaboratively when they use these resources. The more confident children readily assist their peers with the different games. Cooking lessons and carpentry activities are available for children on a daily basis. Children are confident learners as they explore complex activities and games which promote numeracy, literacy and fine motor skills.

Interactions and relationships. There are positive relationships exist between children and kaimahi. Kaimahi are positive role models in their interactions with children, whānau and each other. Children display similar behaviours in their interactions. Kaimahi use affirming approaches to guide and manage children, acknowledging their successes. Children are confident learners who have a strong sense of wellbeing.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which whānau plan, manage and monitor all areas of the operations. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and the monitoring of children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Whānau participation and commitment. Whānau are active participants in the children’s daily programme and the operations of the kōhanga reo. They share in decisions that affect their children. Whānau assist at the kōhanga reo and are appreciated by kaimahi. Sound communication systems help keep whānau aware of what is happening with their children and kōhanga reo activities. A growing commitment among whānau to use and speak te reo Māori has seen some whānau enrol in te reo Māori classes. The majority of whānau members speak te reo Māori when they are at kōhanga reo. Whānau are highly supportive of their kōhanga reo.

Strategic planning. Whānau are determined, creative thinkers who have high expectations for their children. Whānau have a strategic plan outlining their future kōhanga. Some of these include a new building, te reo Māori and children’s wellbeing and learning. The plan links to Te Ara Tuapae and Ngāti Kahungunu strategic goals. Whānau and kaimahi are committed to sustaining high quality learning opportunities.

Self review. Whānau understand the value and purpose of self review. They use health and safety checklists, monthly reports and a TKRNT compliance sheet to review their practices in these areas. There is a strong commitment by whānau to be self managing.

Management practices. Whānau manage and organise the kōhanga reo in a highly effective manner. They have robust systems and processes for the management of the kōhanga. Documents including the charter, policies and personnel records are up to date.

Kaupapa planning. Kaimahi use a sound planning model when they design different kaupapa learning experiences for children. Plans are comprehensive and link to Te Whariki. Children are successfully immersed in a kaupapa Māori environment.

Programme evaluation. Programme evaluation is vital in helping kaimahi bring about positive change for children. Children are advantaged in their learning because of the meticulous way in which kaimahi review the kōhanga reo environment, each child’s interests and their teaching practice.

Assessment. Kaimahi are well aware of what constitutes effective assessment practice. Some practices include whānau feedback and observations. Practices have enabled kaimahi to identify and plan for each child’s next learning steps.

Kaimahi use home work books to supplement children’s learning and to support whānau learning.

Areas for development

Appraisals. Whānau acknowledge they have not completed kaimahi appraisals each year. They have a sound appraisal system which should be followed annually to help whānau to identify kaimahi professional needs and provide appropriate professional support.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation – in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities – based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements – assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
