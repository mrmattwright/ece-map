1 Evaluation of Rising Stars Lynfield Early Childhood Centre

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Rising Stars, Lynfield, is one of two privately owned early childhood education centres in West Auckland. It is licensed to provide care and education for 54 children, including 12 under two years of age.

The centre is housed in a refurbished building that has undergone some improvements since the last ERO review in 2013. There are three main learning areas. One is for young infants and toddlers, one for the older toddlers and one for the pre-school children. The areas all connect to a large outdoor play area which has been recently extended. Older children are able to become involved in gardening, growing plants and practicing aspects of environmental sustainability.

Rising Stars has a philosophy which is inclusive and welcoming for children and their families. The centre vision focuses on developing children‚Äôs learning through their individual strengths and interests. Centre leaders have made some progress with recommendations from the previous ERO report.

ERO and centre leaders agreed that the centre‚Äôs next steps to improve educational outcomes for children are to:

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Rising Stars Lynfield Early Childhood Centre completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.
