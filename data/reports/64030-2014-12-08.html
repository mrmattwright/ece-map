1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ka whakahaeretia tētahi arotake tāpiri i runga o te whakataunga ā tētahi o ngā Kaiwhakahaere ā-Motu o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

E aromātai ana te arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga me te whai hua o ngā mahi ā te kōhanga reo ki te whakatutuki i ngā take i whakatakotohia ai i roto i te arotake mātauranga o mua atu i tēnei, me ētahi atu wāhanga rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake tāpiri i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whakatutukitanga a te rōpū kāwanatanga, whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, e pā ana ki ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai e te pūrongo a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i mua atu, me ngā take rānei kua tāutuhia whai muri o taua arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga e pā ana ki tēnei arotake, ko te titiro ki:

  • Mana Atua – te reo Māori me te hōtaka akoranga
  • te whakamahere me te aromātai – te whakamahere hōtaka, te aromātai me te aromatawai, te whakahaere i te tari, te whakamahere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro, me te whakahaeretanga o ngā kaimahi
  • ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture – te ū ki ngā tatauranga e tika ana ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi, me te whakatutuki i ngā akoako hōneatanga i ia te wā
  • ētahi atu take i tāutuhia ai whai muri i te arotake o mua.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kaahui ki Newtown ki Te Whanganui-a-Tara. E ngākau nui ana ngā mema o te whānau ki te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo me ā rātou tamariki. He pai ngā rauemi me te tiaki i ngā whare me ngā taonga ki te kōhanga reo. Ka arotahi te whānau ki ā rātou whāinga, ō rātou wawata hoki mō te ako a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori.

Ka aromātai tēnei arotake tāpiri i te whānuitanga o te tautoko i ngā tamariki i a rātou e ako ana. Ko te tino aronga a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, ko te hōtaka akoranga mō ngā tamariki kei raro i te rua o ngā tau, te whānuitanga o ngā whai wāhitanga ki te ako i te reo, me ētahi āhuatanga whakahaere. Ko te pātai aromātai whānui mō te arotake nei, e pēnei ana:

‘He pēhea te āta whakaongaonga i ngā tamariki, tā rātou tino whai wāhi mātātoa atu hoki hei ākonga?’

Ka whakaongaongatia ngā tamariki, ā, he mātātoa hoki tā rātou whai wāhi atu hei ākonga.

He taumahi ā ngā tamariki i raro i te rua o ngā tau, ā, e whakaongaonga ana ēnei i tō rātou whakawhanaketanga, e poipoi ana hoki i ō rātou matea, ō rātou ngākau nuitanga hoki. Ka noho pūmau te reo Māori hei aronga nui ki te whānau. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi e pā ana ki te whakawhanake reo. Ka kitea te kaha ake o tō rātou māia ki te whakapuaki i ō rātou mōhiotanga, ō rātou āheinga hoki i roto i te reo. He harikoa ngā tamariki, e whakaongaongatia ana rātou, ā, he tākare hoki rātou ki te tūhura i tō rātou taiao mā ngā huarahi auaha.

Ko te angitu o ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki e kōkiri ana i ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau. Kua pai ake te whānau i ngā wāhanga katoa o te whakahaere tari, me te whakahaere kōhanga. Ka arotahi tā rātou aronga rautaki ki te tupu tonu me te angitu tonu o ngā tamariki me te whānau. Ka whakahaerehia te arotake whaiaro e te whānau. Ka arotake hei aroturuki i te angitu, ā, hei tāutu hoki i ngā wāhanga hei whakapai ake.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Atua

He whakamārama

‘He pēhea te āta whakaongaonga i ngā tamariki, tā rātou tino whai wāhi mātātoa atu hoki hei ākonga?’

Ka whakaongaongatia ngā tamariki, ā, he mātātoa hoki tā rātou whai wāhi atu hei ākonga.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He harikoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki i raro i te rua tau, ki ngā akoranga. Kua whakapai ake ngā kaimahi i ngā hōtaka akoranga mō ngā tamariki i raro i te rua o ngā tau. He whānui ngā tūmomo kaupapa whakahihiri e tūhuratia ana e ngā tamariki. He pārekareka ki a rātou te noho tahi me ō rātou tuākana mō ngā karakia me ngā wā waiata. Kua waihangatia te hōtaka akoranga hei whakatutuki i ngā tino matea, ngā tino ngākau nuitanga hoki o ngā tamariki. He tau ngā tamariki, ā, e whakaongaongatia ana hoki ki tō rātou taiao ako.

He whānui ngā tūmomo whai wāhitanga reo, ngā wheako reo hoki a ngā tamariki. Ka rongo rātou i te reo Māori e whakamahia ana ki tō rātou taiao ako i ngā wā katoa. Ka whakatairanga ngā kaimahi i ngā whakawhitinga kōrero me ngā tamariki, hei whakawhānui i tā rātou whakamahinga, tō rātou māramatanga hoki ki te reo. Ka rongo i tēnā me tēnā, e whakamahi ana i te reo Māori ki waenga i a rātou anō. He tākare ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i tēnā e mōhio ana rātou, e whakamahia ana hoki ki te reo Māori.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka tautokona ngā kaimahi i roto i ā rātou mahi, kia whai hua ai ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Kua whakawhanake ake te whānau i ngā pūnaha, hei hāpai ake i ngā kaimahi. Ka whakatutukihia te arohaehae kaimahi. Kei te whakatutuki haere tonu te whānau i ētahi atu kaupapa matua o te whakahaere kaimahi, tae atu ki ngā aratohu e pā ana ki te whakahaere kaimahi.

Ko ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki e whakamōhio ana i ngā hōtaka akoranga. Kua whakapau kaha ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakatairanga ake i te kounga o te whakamahere, te aromātai, me te aromatawai. Kua whakaritea ngā mahere ako o Te Whāriki, ā, kua āta whakaarohia ēnei, he nui hoki te kiko o roto. Pūrongo atu ai ngā kaimahi ki te whānau i ia te wā mō te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whakamōhiotia aua pūrongo e te aromātai i te hōtaka i ia te wā. I nāianei, ko te pai ake o ngā mahi aromatawai, e whakarato ana i ngā mōhiohio papai anō hoki ki ngā mātua mō ngā akoranga a ā rātou tamariki.

Kua whakatūngia e te whānau tētahi kaiwhakahaere tari wā-kikī. Kua whakawātea tēnei i te kaiako ki te aro nui atu ki te hōtaka akoranga mō ngā tamariki. Ko te pai ake o te whakahaeretanga o te tari me te whakaritenga o ngā kaimahi, ka whai pānga anō hoki ki ngā akoranga me ngā whakahaeretanga mō ngā tamariki.

He aronga nui tā rātou whānau o te kōhanga reo ki te whakapai ake mō ā rātou tamariki. He pai te whakawhanake haere o te arotake whaiaro, puta noa i te nuinga o ngā wāhanga o te kōhanga reo. Ko te pūrongo atu i ia te wā ki ngā hui ā-whānau, e whakatakoto ana i te kiko o ngā mōhiohio ki āna mema, kia pai ake ai tā rātou aroturuki i te whānuitanga o te āhua o ngā mahi ki tō rātou kōhanga reo, mō ngā tamariki. Ināianei, kua whakaritea tētahi mahere rautaki hei whakatakoto ki te whānau te aronga i whakaritea ai.

4 Ngā wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ko te atawhai me te noho haumaru o ngā tamariki tētahi kaupapa matua. Kua whakatutukihia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi ngā wāhanga i tāutuhia ai ki te pūrongo o mua, nā runga i te kore tutuki i ngā herenga ā-ture. Ka noho pūmau ngā akoako hōneatanga o ngā tamariki i ia te wā. Ka āta aroturuki anō hoki i te tatauranga ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā pākeke, i runga anō i te tikanga o te raihana mō tō rātou kōhanga reo.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, ā, e whakaae ana hoki te whānau ki te:

  • ū tonu ki te arotake whaiaro a te whānau mō te aroturuki i te āhua o ngā mahi ki te kōhanga reo, me te whakatutukitanga o ngā whāinga ki te tūtohinga, ngā kaupapa rautaki matua anō hoki.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

1 Background

Introduction

A supplementary review is undertaken at the discretion of a National Manager in the Education Review Office (ERO).

A supplementary review evaluates the extent and effectiveness of actions a kōhanga reo has taken towards addressing issues specified in its previous ERO review and/or any additional areas identified since that review.

Terms of Reference

This supplementary review is based on an evaluation of the performance of the kōhanga reo governing body and management in relation to areas identified in the previous ERO report or issues identified since that review. The terms of reference for this review are to investigate:

  • Mana Atua – te reo Māori and the learning programme
  • Planning and evaluation - programme planning, evaluation and assessment, administration, strategic planning and self review, personnel management
  • areas of non-compliance - maintain ratio standards, and carry out regular evacuation drills
  • and any issues identified since the previous review.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kaahui is in Newtown, Wellington. Whānau members are committed to the kōhanga reo kaupapa and their children. The kōhanga reo premises and facilities are well resourced and maintained. Whānau are focused on their goals and aspirations for children’s learning of te reo Māori.

This supplementary review evaluates the extent to which children are supported in their learning. ERO’s particular interest was the learning programme for children under two years of age, the range of language learning opportunities, and some areas of management. The overarching evaluation question for this review was:

'To what extent are children stimulated and actively engaged as learners?'

Children are stimulated and actively engaged as learners.

Children under two years old have activities that stimulate their development and cater for their needs and interests. Te reo Māori remains a strong whānau focus. Children enjoy a range of language development activities. They show increased confidence to share with others what they know and can do with the language. Children are happy, stimulated and keen to explore their environment in creative ways.

Children’s success in learning drives whānau management. The whānau have made improvement across all areas of administration and management. Their strategic direction focuses on continual growth and success for children and whānau. Self review is managed by the whānau. Review is used to monitor success and identify areas for improvement.

3 Findings

Mana Atua

Background

To what extent are children stimulated and actively engaged as learners?

Children are stimulated and actively engaged as learners.

Areas of progress

Children under two years old are happily engaged in learning. Kaimahi have improved learning programmes for the under two’s. Children have a variety of interesting activities they explore. They enjoy participating with their tuakana for karakia and waiata sessions. The learning programme is designed to meet the particular needs and interests of children. Children are settled and stimulated in their learning environment.

Children have a variety of language learning opportunities and experiences. They hear the language used at all times in their leaning environment. Kaimahi promote conversations with children to extend their use and understanding of the language. Individuals are heard using te reo Māori with each other. Children are keen to share what they know and can do with te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of progress

Kaimahi are supported in their practice, so children benefit in their learning. Whānau have further developed systems to better support kaimahi. Appraisal is carried out. Whānau continue to work through other planned personnel management priorities, including personnel management guidelines. There is a focus on self improvement and professional growth.

Children’s needs and interests inform programmes of learning. Kaimahi and whānau have worked hard to raise the quality of planning, evaluation and assessment. Well considered and detailed Te Whāriki learning plans are in place. Kaimahi report regularly to whānau about the learning programme. These reports are informed by regular programme evaluation. Improved assessment practice now provides good information to parents about their children’s learning.

The whānau have addressed the need for a full time administrator who has been appointed. This has freed the kaiako to concentrate on the learning programme for children. Improved management of administration and organisation of kaimahi is having a positive impact on learning and operations for children.

The kōhanga reo whānau have a strong focus on improvement for their children. Self review is developing well across most areas of the kōhanga reo. Regular reporting at whānau hui provides detailed information to its members so they can better monitor the overall performance of their kōhanga reo for children. A strategic plan is now in place to give whānau planned direction.

4 Areas of non-compliance

Areas of progress

Children’s care and safety is a priority. Whānau and kaimahi have addressed the areas of non compliance in the last report. Regular emergency drills with children are maintained. Adult to child ratios in line with their kōhanga reo licence is closely monitored.

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends and the whānau agree to:

  • maintain whānau self review for monitoring kōhanga reo performance and success with charter objectives, and strategic priorities.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
