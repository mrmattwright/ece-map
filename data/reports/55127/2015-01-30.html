1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Marotiri ki te whanga o Tokomaru. He kōhanga reo noho marae tēnei, ā, e tū ana ki ngā papa whenua o te marae o Pākirikiri.

‘He pēhea rawa te māia a ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori?’

E whanake ana te māia, te mātau hoki o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

He harikoa ngā tamariki, ā, he tākare tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo taumahi ako, wheako ako hoki. E āhei rātou te tūhura takitahi me te tākaro ngātahi hoki. Kei te ako ngā tamariki ki ngā tino pou whenua ā-iwi me tō rātou maunga a Marotiri. Ka ako i ngā waiata, ā, ka tū hoki ki te waiata, te haka, me te mōteatea. Ka tūhura ngā tamariki i tō rātou taiao ake, tae atu ki te marae, te moana, me te awa iti. He hihiri ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki te hōtaka akoranga.

Ka whai wāhi ngā kaumātua ki te ao o te kōhanga reo. Ka whakatauira rātou i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te tino hononga ki waenga i te kōhanga reo me te marae. Ka ako, ka tākaro hoki ngā tamariki ki te marae, ā, ka mōhio hoki ki ngā wāhanga o te marae me āna kawenga.

Ka aro atu, ka whakamahi hoki ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki e whai hua ana ki te akiaki i ngā tamariki ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero. E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki kia whakawhanake ai tō rātou mōhiotanga ki te reo Māori, me te whakamahinga i te reo.

He pai te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. He pai te mahi ngātahi a te whānau me ngā kaimahi. Ka whakatau ngātahi rātou i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo me tana aronga whakamua. Ka whai hua te arotake whaiaro hei hāpai i te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakatau whakaritenga. Ka tautoko te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo i te kōkiri a ngā kaimahi i ā rātou mahi, ā, ka āta whakarite i ngā putanga hua mō ngā tamariki.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori me te whānau.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko āna kaupapa arotahi mō tēnei arotake, ko:

  • Mana Reo.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga Māori i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Reo

He whakamārama

‘He pēhea rawa te māia a ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori?’

E whanake ana te māia, te mātau hoki o ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka aro atu ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori, ka kōrero hoki rātou i te reo. Ka noho pūmau ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki tētahi taiao rumaki reo Māori puta noa i te rā. He pai te aro atu a ngā tamariki ki tēnei āhuatanga. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rautaki e whai hua ana ki te akiaki i te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā whakawhitinga kōrero. He pai te whakapau kaha a ngā tamariki ki te kōrero mō tō rātou kōhanga reo me ngā kaupapa mahi e whai pānga ana ki a rātou. He māia ki ngā tamariki te patapatai mō te ao e noho nei rātou. Ka kōrero rātou i te reo Māori i waenganui i a rātou anō me ngā pākeke hoki. Ka ako ngā tamariki ki kōrero i te reo ā-iwi o Ngāti Porou. Ka haere te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo whai wāhitanga whakangungu hei whakapiki ake, hei whakapai ake hoki i tō rātou āheinga ki roto i te reo Māori. E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake tonu i tō rātou āheinga i roto i te reo Māori.

Ka tūhura ngā tamariki i tō rātou taiao ake, tae atu ki te marae, te moana, me te awa iti. Ka ako ngā tamariki i te taiao māoriori, tūturu hoki mā ngā huarahi ngahau e whai pūtake ana. Ka ākina ngā tamariki ki te whiriwhiri i ā rātou taumahi ako. Ka ngākau nui rātou ki te tākaro ki te wai, ngā poraka hanganga nui, me ngā tūmomo taumahi, ā, ka whai hua ēnei ki te tahuri i ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga. Ka āta whakamahi ngā kaimahi i ngā rauemi māori hei whakatairanga anō hoki i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ka noho hihiri ngā tamariki ki te ako.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

He pai te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Tū ai ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, ā, he pai te tae ā-tinana atu a te whānau me ngā kaimahi. He pai te mahi ngātahi a te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo me tā rātou ahunga whakamua. Ka tuku pūrongo i ia te wā mō te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo pūtake. Ka noho mōhio ngā mema o te whānau ki ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo, tae atu ki te aronga ki te hōtaka akoranga mō ngā tamariki, te reo Māori me ngā kaupapa whakangungu, ngā pūtea, me ngā take tiaki whare i ia te wā. Ka whai hua te arotake whaiaro hei hāpai i te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga. Ka whakamahere nahanaha te whānau mō ngā tau kei te heke mai. Ka hāpai te mahere pakihi ā-tau i te whānau ki te whakaraupapa i ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake. Ka whakapūmau te whānau i tētahi pūnaha mō te arohaehae i ia tau, hei whakahaere, hei whakanui hoki i te āhua o ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi. Ka tautoko te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo i ngā kaimahi ki te āta whakatutuki i ā rātou mahi, me te whakanui i ngā putanga hua mō ngā tamariki.

Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā ngā hōtaka akoranga i āta whakamaheretia ai. Ka hāngai ngā kaupapa ako ki te taiao o te hapori, me tētahi aronga kaupapa Māori, tae atu ki te ako i ngā wāhanga o te marae. Ka whai atu ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga ako o te wā e pupū ake ana. Ka whakarerekē rātou i te hōtaka, nā runga i ngā tohu huarere, te taiao, me ngā kaupapa, ngā huihuinga anō hoki ki te marae. Ka tuhia ngā whāinga ako, ā, ka whakamahia kia arotahi ai ngā kaimahi ki te tautoko i te ako me te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. He ngāwari te kite i te hononga o ngā mahere hōtaka ki Te Whāriki. He mārama ki ngā kaimahi te aronga o ngā hōtaka akoranga, ā, ka āta arahina i roto i ngā mahi ka whāia e rātou, puta noa i te rā.

Ka whai hua ngā mahi aromatawai ki te whakatakoto haere i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki i roto i te roanga o te wā. He ātaahua te whakatakotoranga o ngā pūkete e mau nei i ngā whakaahua, ngā kōrerorero e pā ana ki ngā akoranga, me ngā mahi toi a ngā tamariki. Ka whakamōhio ngā pūkete i ngā mātua, mō te āhua o te ahunga whakamua a ā rātou tamariki me ō rātou whakawhanaketanga. He pai te whakamahinga a ngā kaimahi i ngā aromatawai, ki te whakaaro huritao, ā, ki te whakaatu hoki i ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki ngā mahi angitu a ngā tamariki.

Ka whai hua te aromātai hōtaka ki te hāpai i te whakawhanake tonutanga, te whakapai tonutanga a ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau i te hōtaka akoranga. Ka hui tahi, ka whakawhiti kōrero hoki ngā kaimahi me ngā mema o te whānau mō te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whai whakaaro rātou me pēhea te whakapai ake, hei whakahihiri, hei whakaongaonga anō hoki i te ako me te whakamahi a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. Ka whakarato ki ngā tamariki tētahi hōtaka akoranga o te kounga pai.

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Kāhore ngā mōhiohio i roto i ngā pūkete e hāngai ana ki ngā ngākau nuitanga ake o ia tamaiti. Ka hāngai ngā pūkete ki ngā taumahi e whakatutukihia ana e te katoa, ā, kāhore e whakatakotohia ana te rerekē haere o ō rātou ngākau nuitanga.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaeretanga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora, me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakaae Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau, kia whakamahine tonu te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aromatawai.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura-Watson Kaiurungi Whakatūruki Arotake Māori Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

30 Kohi-tātea 2015

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Marotiri is in Tokomaru Bay. This marae based kōhanga reo is located on the grounds of Pākirikiri Marae.

How well do children speak te reo Māori?

Children are developing as confident, competent speakers of te reo Māori.

Children are happy and eagerly participate in a good range of learning activities and experiences. They explore independently and play well together. Children learn about significant iwi landmarks including their maunga Marotiri. They learn and perform local waiata, haka and mōteatea. Children explore their local environment including the marae, the moana and small awa. Children are motivated to participate in the learning programme.

Kaumātua participate in the life of the kōhanga reo. They model te reo Māori and tikanga. Children benefit from the strong relationship between the kōhanga reo and the marae. Children learn and play on the marae and know about the parts and functions of the marae.

Children respond to and speak te reo Māori. Staff members use effective strategies to encourage children to engage in conversations. Children are well supported to develop their knowledge and use of te reo Māori.

The kōhanga reo is well managed in the interests of children. Whānau and staff members work well together. They collectively make decisions about kōhanga operations and the future direction. Self review is effective in supporting the whānau and staff members to make decisions. Kōhanga reo management supports staff members to do their jobs well and to ensure positive outcomes for children.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review.

The priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Reo.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out in the report findings.

3 Findings

Mana Reo

Background

How well do children use te reo Māori?

Children are developing as confident, competent users of te reo Māori.

Areas of good performance

Children respond to and use te reo Māori. Staff and whānau members maintain a te reo Māori immersion environment throughout the day. Children respond well to this. Staff use effective strategies to encourage children to engage in conversations. Children make good attempts to talk about their kōhanga reo and the things that they are involved in. Children are confident to ask questions about the world around them. They speak te reo Māori with each other and adults. Children are taught to use the dialect of Ngāti Porou. Whānau and staff members attend a range of training opportunities to increase and improve their te reo Māori capability. Children are well supported to continue to develop their capability in te reo Māori.

Children explore their local environment including the marae, the moana and small awa. Children learn about the natural and physical environment in fun and meaningful ways. Children are encouraged to choose their own learning activities. Water play, large building blocks, and activities are popular and effectively engage children in learning. Staff members use natural resources well to enhance children’s’ learning. Children are motivated to learn.

Planning and Evaluation

Areas of good performance

The kōhanga reo is well managed in the interests of children. Regular whānau meetings occur and are well attended by whānau and staff members. Whānau and staff members work well together to make decisions about kōhanga operations and their future direction. There is regular reporting for a variety of purposes. Whānau members are well informed about kōhanga operations including the focus of the learning programme for children, te reo Māori and training, finances and ongoing maintenance matters. Self review is effective in supporting the whānau and staff members to make decisions. The whānau plans strategically for the future. The annual business plan supports the whānau to prioritise development areas. The whānau maintains an annual system of appraisal to manage and acknowledge staff performance. Kōhanga reo management supports staff members to do their jobs effectively and promote positive outcomes for children.

Children benefit from well planned programmes of learning. Themes for learning reflect the local environment and a kaupapa Māori focus including learning about the parts of the local marae. Staff members maximise learning opportunities as they arise. They adjust the programme in response to the climate, the local environment and marae based events and occasions. Learning objectives are documented and are used to focus staff members on supporting children to learn and to use te reo Māori. Programme planning has clear links to Te Whāriki. Staff members are clear about the focus of learning programmes and are well guided about what they need to do during the day.

Assessment practices effectively record children’s learning over time. Profile books are attractively presented and include photos, comments about learning and children’s art works. Profiles inform parents about their children’s progress and development. Staff members use assessment well to reflect on and present information about children’s success.

Programme evaluation is effective in supporting staff and whānau members to continually develop and improve the learning programme. Staff and whānau members meet and discuss the quality of the learning programme. They consider how it can be improved to motivate and stimulate children to learn and use te reo Māori. Children are provided with a good quality learning programme.

Areas for development

Information that is included in profiles does not focus on children’s individual interests. Profiles reflect activities that all children are involved in rather than providing a record of their changing interests.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

5 Recommendations

ERO and the whānau agreed that the whānau and staff will continue to refine assessment practices.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.

Lynda Pura Watson

Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)
