1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Āwhina ki te taha o te marae o Te Āwhina, ki Motueka. I te tau 2016 te kōhanga reo i hūnuku ai, nā ngā whakawhanaketanga ki te marae. Koinei anake te kōhanga reo ki Motueka. Nō ngā hau e whā o te motu whānui o Aotearoa te nuinga o te whānau. E whakapūmau tonu ana te whānau o te kōhanga reo i tō rātou hononga ki te tangata whenua.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou tūmāia i ō rātou hononga ki ētahi atu?

E whakaatu ana ngā tamariki i tō rātou tūmāia i roto i ā rātou taunekeneke ki ētahi atu, ā, puta noa anō hoki i te katoa o ngā wheako ako.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E tautokona ana te ako a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki e hāpai ana ki te whakawhānui ake i te reo Māori e hopukina ana e ngā tamariki. Ko ētahi o aua rautaki, ko ngā karakia, ko ngā hīmene, ko ngā pepeha, ko ngā mihi, ko ngā waiata, ko ngā whakawhitinga kōrero, ko te pānui. Ka whakarongo pīkari ngā tamariki i ngā wā pānui pukapuka. He tika tā rātou aro atu ki ngā pātai me ngā tohutohu. Ka whakamahi ngā pēpi i te kupu kotahi, i ngā hanga o te kanohi, i te reo ā-tinana hei whakaō atu i te tīmatanga o tō rātou mārama ki ngā hanganga reo. Kei te mauminamina ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori mā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi rongo, rauemi ataata hoki. E whanake ana ngā pūkenga reo o ngā tamariki.

E whai wāhi atu ana ngā tamariki ki ngā hononga papai ki te taha o ō rātou hoa, ngā kaimahi, me te whānau. E whakapuaki ana te whānau me ngā kaimahi i tētahi taiao e mau nei i te whanaungatanga hei tūāpapa mō ngā akoranga. Ka whakatauira rātou i ngā uara o te aroha, te manaaki, me te tiaki. Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki aua uara i ā rātou taunekeneke ki ō rātou hoa. He wā anō ka āta noho tahi ngā kaimahi ki ngā pēpi, tētahi ki tētahi. Ka ngākau nui ngā pēpi ki aua wā e aro nuitia ana ki a rātou. Ka akiaki hoki ngā kaimahi i te hononga ki waenga i te tuakana me te teina. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki tuākana ki te hāpai, ki te ārahi, ki te whakauru anō hoki i ō rātou tēina ki ā rātou tākaro. Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te manaakitanga me te aroha.

E whai wāhi nui ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi wāhi e whakamana ana i ā rātou akoranga. Ka āta whakarite te whānau i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi e noho wātea ana ki ngā tamariki, hei hāpai i ā rātou mahi. Ka āta whakarite ngā kaimahi me te whānau kia ātaahua, kia mā, kia haumaru hoki ngā wāhi ako e āta tiakina ana. Ka whakamahere ngā kaimahi kia haere ngā tamariki ki ngā huihuinga ki te marae. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā tikanga me ngā kawa o te iwi o te rohe nei. Ka kawe atu ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki ngā tūmomo tino pou whenua me ngā wāhi whakahirahira o te takiwā. E hāpaitia ana te ako a ngā tamariki i ō rātou whakapapa, ā, ka pātere mai rātou i ō rātou pepeha ki ētahi atu. E whakawhanake ana te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou tū hei uri Māori ki te hapori.

He pakari ngā tamariki ki te tūhura me te whakatewhatewha i ō rātou taiao ako. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te hōtaka akoranga, ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki. Ka whakarato rātou i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te whai i ō rātou ake ngākau nuitanga. Ka aro pū, ka whai wāhi nui hoki ngā tamariki, i a rātou e kōkiri ana i ā rātou ake akoranga. Ka whai pānga nui te māra kai ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ko te whakatō kai, ko te tiaki hoki i te māra e hāpai ana i te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou tū hei kaitiaki. Ka ako hoki rātou i te moana, te ngahere, ngā awa, me ngā maunga o te takiwā nei. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i te mārama haere o ngā tamariki ki ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā atua Māori, me tā rātou whai pānga hoki ki te taiao māoriori. E whanake ana te ngākau titikaha o ngā tamariki hei ākonga.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kāhore ngā tamariki tuākana e āta tau ana i ngā wā ōkawa o te hōtaka akoranga. Kua tae ki te wā kia whakaarohia e te whānau ngā aronga ka hāpai i te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te whakahirahiratanga o ngā karakia me ngā pepeha, ā, ki te āhua tonu e tika ana mō rātou i aua wā.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka whakamahere i te hōtaka mā te mahi ngātahi me te whānau. Ka whakahāngai i ngā mahere ki Te Whāriki, ā, e tohu ana hoki i te matatau o ngā kaimahi i riro ai i a rātou mā te Tohu Whakapakari. Ka āta tāutuhia ki ngā tuhinga mahere, ko te kiko o te hōtaka, ngā huarahi whakapuaki, me ngā tini āhuatanga e pā ana ki ngā rauemi. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki i te wā e whai pūtake ana ngā mahere.

He pakari ngā mahi whakahaere a te whānau. He tautōhito te kaiwhakahaere o te tari i te kōhanga reo. Ka arotahi ia ki te whakamahere mō te tauatanga o tōna tūranga, kia pūmau tonu ai ngā pūnaha e whakahaere ana i te tari. Ka arotakengia ngā kaupapa here o te tūtohinga, ā, ka hāngai hoki ki te wā. Kua whakahāngaitia ēnei ki Ngā Pou e Whā. Ka arotake o roto i ia te wā, ā, ka aroturukitia, ka kōrerohia hoki aua arotake ki ngā hui ā-whānau. He mārama ngā pūrongo e pā ana ki ngā pūtea. Ka āta whakaarohia ngā rauemi whakaako. Tū ai ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā, ā, he pai anō hoki te whakatakotoranga o ngā meneti. Ko ngā pūrongo a te heamana, ngā kaiako, me te kaiwhakahaere tari e whakamōhio ana i te whānau ki tēnā e whakatutukihia ana. Ko tētahi kaupapa matua a te whānau, ko te whakapiki ake i te tatauranga o ngā tamariki ki te kōhanga reo. Ko te whai huatanga o ngā mahi whakahaere, ko te kounga hoki o te atawhai, e whai wāhi nui ana ki ngā tino painga mō ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Me whakapakari ake i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aromatawai me te aromātai. Kua tae ki te wā kia tautoko te whānau i ngā kaimahi ki te whakatakoto i ngā tino tukanga e aroturuki ai i te ako a ngā tamariki me te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Kua tāutuhia e te whānau ngā whāinga rautaki e hono atu ana ki Te Ara Tūāpae. He mea nui kia whai whakaaro te whānau i nāianei ki tētahi mahere mahi e āta whakarite ai i te whakatutukitanga o ngā kaupapa matua a te whānau. Kāhore anō kia whakatutukihia ngā akoako hōneatanga inā he ahi.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ētahi wāhanga kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture. Kia tutuki i te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā herenga ā-ture, me tahuri rātou ki te:

  • āta whakarite i te noho mōhio o ngā pakeke ki ngā akoako hōneatanga ohorere e tika ana, heoi, me whakatutuki haere i aua akoako ki te taha o ngā tamariki, ā, me whakatakoto ā-tuhi nei hoki i aua akoako.
    [R46 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; HS8 Paearu Raihana mō ngā Kōhanga Reo kei raro i te maru o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo 2008]

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau o te kōhanga reo ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakapakarihia ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te aromatawai me te aromātai, hei hāpai i te whakapai tonutanga o te hōtaka akoranga i ia te wā
  • whakawhanakehia tētahi mahere mahi e hāpai ai i te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā wawata i whakaraupapahia ai ki tā rātou mahere rautaki
  • whakatutukihia i ia te wā i ngā akoako hōneatanga inā he ahi, ā, kia whakatakoto ā-tuhi hoki i aua wā.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Āwhina i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Āwhina is located next to Te Āwhina marae in Motueka. In 2016, the kōhanga reo was relocated due to developments on the marae. This is the only kōhanga reo in Motueka. The majority of the whānau have tribal affiliations to many areas across New Zealand. The whānau of the kōhanga reo continue to strengthen their relationship with tangata whenua.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children show confidence in their relationships with others?

Children show confidence through their interactions with others and across all learning experiences.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are supported to learn te reo Māori. Kaimahi use a range of strategies to help extend children’s acquisition of te reo Māori. These strategies include karakia, hīmene, pepeha, mihi, waiata, conversations and reading. Children are active listeners during storybook time. They respond appropriately to questions and instructions. Babies use single words, facial expressions and body language to respond as they begin to recognise language structures. Children are receptive to te reo Māori through a range of audio and visual resources. Children’s language skills are developing.

Children experience positive relationships with their peers, kaimahi and whānau. Whānau and kaimahi offer an environment where whanaungatanga is the basis for learning. They model the values of aroha, manaaki and tiaki. The children display these values during interactions with their peers. Kaimahi provide meaningful one-to-one time for the younger babies. The babies enjoy the attention. Kaimahi encourage tuākana tēina relationships. The older children willingly support, guide and involve their tēina in their play. Children experience care and love.

Children enjoy an environment where their learning is valued. Whānau ensure there are a variety of resources available to support children’s work. Kaimahi and whānau ensure learning spaces are attractive, immaculate, well maintained and safe. Kaimahi plan for children to attend marae activities. Children learn about the tikanga and kawa of the local iwi. Kaimahi take children to visit significant landmarks and places of the area. Children are supported to learn their own whakapapa and share their pepeha readily with others. Children are developing an understanding of their place as Māori in the community.

Children confidently explore and investigate their learning environments. Kaimahi and whānau are actively engaged in the learning programme alongside their children. They provide opportunities for children to follow their own interests. Children are focused and engaged as they initiate their learning. Māra kai is an important part of children’s learning. Planting kai and caring for the māra help children to understand their role as kaitiaki. They also learn about the sea, forest and bush areas, rivers and mountains of the area. Kaimahi support children to understand the links between atua Māori and how they impact on the natural environment. Children are developing as confident learners.

Key Next Steps

During the formal part of the learning programme, the older children are not settled. It is time for whānau to consider approaches to help children understand the significance of karakia and pepeha and what is required of them during these times.

Planning and Evaluation

Programme planning is done in collaboration with whānau. Planning is aligned to Te Whāriki and reflects the experience provided through Tohu Whakapakari. Programme content, modes of delivery and implications for resources are clearly identified within the planning documents. Children benefit when planning is purposeful.

Whanau management practices are sound. The administrator of the kōhanga reo is experienced in her role. She is focussed on succession planning for her role to maintain the administration systems. Charter policies are reviewed and current. These are aligned to Ngā Pou e Whā. Internal review is ongoing, monitored and discussed at whānau hui. Financial reporting is transparent. Learning resources are well considered. Whānau hui are held regularly and the minutes are well recorded. Reporting by the chairperson, kaiako and administrator inform whānau of what is happening. A priority for the whānau is increasing the number of children attending the kōhanga reo. Effective management and quality of care contributes to positive outcomes for children.

Key Next Steps

Assessment and evaluation practices require strengthening. It is time for whānau to support kaimahi to establish clear processes to monitor children’s learning and the effectiveness of the learning programme. The whānau has identified strategic goals that are linked to Te Ara Tūāpae. It is important that whānau now consider an action plan to ensure that the whānau priorities are achieved. Emergency fire drills have not been carried out.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

ERO identified areas of non-compliance. In order for the kōhanga reo whānau to meet compliance requirements, they need to:

  • ensure adults are familiar with the relevant emergency drills, regularly carry these out with the children, and record these drills accordingly.
    [R46 Education (Early Childhood Services) Regulations 2008; HS8 Licensing Criteria for Kōhanga Reo affiliated with Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust 2008]

4 Recommendations

ERO and the Kōhanga Reo whānau developed the following recommendations that whānau:

  • strengthen assessment and evaluation practices to support ongoing improvements to the learning programme

  • develop an action plan to support the whānau to achieve aspirations in their strategic plan

  • regularly carry out fire drills and record when these take place.

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Āwhina will be in three years.
