1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whānau o Te Rākatō ki Māhia. He kōhanga reo noho marae tēnei e whakarato ana i te kounga kairangi o te mātauranga me te atawhai mō ngā tamariki. 

Haere ai te whānau ki te kōhanga reo i ia rā. Ka whai wāhi rātou ki ngā whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo mā ngā huarahi maha e whai pūtake ana. Ka whakatutuki haere te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā whakawhanaketanga ngaio, ka riro i a rātou ngā tohu mātauranga, ā, ka mahi ngātahi rātou ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki. 

He hihiri, he whakaongaonga hoki te taiao ako o te kōhanga reo e whakaratohia ana e te kaiako me ngā kaimahi. He tau, he hāneanea hoki ngā tamariki, ā, ka whai wāhi atu rātou. He atawhai, he manaaki hoki te āhua o ngā taunekeneke me ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā tamariki me ngā pākeke.  He pai te rere o te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka āwhina ngā pākeke ki te whakarite i ngā taumahi me ngā rauemi mā ngā tamariki, te whakahaere i ngā wā kai, me te hāpai hoki i ngā mahinga o ia rā.  Ka tautoko, ka akiaki hoki i ngā tamariki ki te tūhura, te tākaro, me te whai wāhi atu hoki ki ngā wheako me ngā whakawhitinga kōrero. 

Ko te reo Māori te reo matua ki ngā tamariki i te kōhanga reo. E tino tautokona ana rātou kia whakamahi i te reo Māori. Kōrero ai ngā kaimahi i te reo Māori, puta noa i te rā. Kua āta tuituia ngā tikanga Māori ki ngā whakahaeretanga o ia rā. Ka whakatauirahia ngā mihi whakatau me ngā karakia hoki mā ngā tamariki. Ka kawe ngā tamariki i ngā mahi ki te ārahi me te whakatutuki i ngā tikanga.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau.  I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga reo (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro) ā, me te whānui o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo. 

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko:

  • Mana Reo 

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga.  Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai. 

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Reo

He whakamārama

Ka pēhea te tautoko i ngā tamariki me te whānau ki te ako me te whakamahi i te reo Māori?

Ka tino tautokona ngā tamariki me ngā mema o te whānau ki te ako me te whakamahi i te reo Māori.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te hōtaka akoranga.  Ka whakaratohia ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo whai wāhitanga ki te whiriwhiri me te kōkiri i ā rātou ake akoranga. E āhei ana ngā tamariki ki te tūhura i te taiao o te kōhanga reo. He whānui ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga i roto i ngā akoranga a te takitahi me te noho ā-rōpū.  He pai te rere o ngā taumahi me ngā mahinga o ia rā. Ka tino whakarite ngā kaimahi ki te hāpai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki me te whai i ō rātou ngākau nuitanga. Kei te wātea ngā taputapu ki ngā tamariki. E mārama ana ngā kaimahi ki ō rātou tūranga me ngā wāhanga mahi mā ia tangata. He pai te whakahaeretanga o ngā rōpū. Ka whakapuakihia te hōtaka akoranga me ngā taumahi e ngā kaimahi, ā, he whitawhita, he whakaongaonga hoki rātou, ā, ka whakahihiri i te āhua o ngā mahi mā ngā tamariki. 

Ngā tikanga Māori. Ka whakauru te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki ngā kaupapa e whai pānga ana ki ngā tikanga Māori, pērā i te whakatau manuhiri. E tautokona ana ngā tamariki kia kawe i ngā karakia, i ngā wā waiata hoki. Ka whakanuia, ka whakamanahia hoki ngā mōhiotanga a ngā tamariki. Ka ako ngā tamariki ko wai rātou, nō hea rātou. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā ariā o te mana whenua, tae atu ki tō rātou noho mōhio ki ō rātou hononga ki ō rātou waka, ō rātou awa, me ō rātou marae hoki. Ka tino tautokona ngā tamariki kia ako i tō rātou tū hei tangata whenua. 

Te reo Māori. Ka tino tautokona ngā tamariki kia whanakehia tā rātou kōrero i te reo Māori. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā rautaki papai e pā ana ki te hopu reo. Ko ētahi o aua rautaki, ko te akiaki, te whakatauira, te whakaatu, te tātari, me te whakamahi hoki i ngā rauemi hei hāpai i te aroā me te māramatanga. Ka tautokona ngā tamariki kia tū pakari rātou, me te kōrero mō rātou anō. Ka āwhina ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te whakapuaki i ngā rerenga kōrero me te whakamahi i te reo Māori i te wā e āhei ana rātou. Ka whai wāhi ngā mātua me te whānau ki ngā whakangungu o te reo Māori, me te whakawhanaketanga i te reo Māori. Kua oti i te tokomaha o te whānau ngā akoranga me ngā tohu mātauranga e hāpai ana i ā rātou tamariki ki te ako me te whakamahi i te reo Māori. Ka ū pūmau ngā kaimahi ki tētahi taiao o te reo Māori, puta noa i te rā. 

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Te tika o te reo Māori. Kāhore anō ngā kaimahi me te whānau kia whakawhanake i tētahi pūnaha whakatau kounga hei āta whakarite i te tika o te reo Māori e whakaakona ana e rātou, e whakatauirahia ana hoki e rātou mā ngā tamariki. Me whai whakaaro rātou, me pēhea te whakamahi i te arohaehae hei hāpai i te whakapaitanga ake o te āheinga i roto i te reo Māori. 

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki?

He pai te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. 

Ngā wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Te whakahaeretanga a te whānau. He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau me ngā kaimahi i te kōhanga reo. Ka whakaaro huritao ngā kaimahi me te whānau, ā, ka arotahi kia whakapai tonu i ngā wā katoa. Ka whai wāhi te whānau ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga. He pai ngā pūrongo ki ngā hui ā-whānau mō ngā tino āhuatanga matua e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka whai hua, ka haere tonu hoki te arotake whaiaro. He tau te whakahaere i ngā pūtea, ā, he mārama hoki te aronga rautaki.  Ka whakarato ki ngā tamariki, he kōhanga reo ka āta whakahaerehia, ka āta whakarite ai hoki. 

Te aromatawai. He pai te tuhi me te whakatakoto haere a ngā kaimahi i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He ātaahua te whakaatu i ngā pūkete, ā, ka whai wāhi te reo a ngā mātua, ngā āhuatanga e ngākau nuitia ana e ngā tamariki me ērā kāhore i te pai ki a rātou, ō rātou pepeha, ngā pānui a te kaiako mō ngā huihuinga me ngā kaupapa i te kōhanga reo, ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki te hōtaka, ngā kitenga o te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki, ngā whakaahua me ngā kōrerorero, ngā mahi toi, me ngā whāinga ako hoki e tohu ana i ngā whakaaro huritao a ngā kaimahi. Ka whakaaro huritao ngā kaimahi ki te ahunga whakamua me te angitu a ngā tamariki, i runga i Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira.  He pai te noho mōhio o ngā mātua ki te ahunga whakamua me te whakawhanaketanga o ā rātou ake tamariki. 

Te aromātai hōtaka. Ka whai hua te aromātai hōtaka. Hui ai ngā kaimahi i ia te wā ki te whakawhiti kōrero e pā ana ki te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga. Whakatutukihia ai i ia te wā te arotake whaiaro o tēnā i whai hua ai, ngā āhuatanga hei whakapai ake, me ngā mahi hoki ka whai ake. Ka whakarerekē haere te whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo i runga i ngā mōhiohio o te kounga pai ka puta i te arotake whaiaro. 

Te whakamahere hōtaka.  He matawhānui te whakamahere i te hōtaka, ā, ka whai hua tēnei ki te ārahi i ngā kaimahi me tā rātou whakapuaki i te hōtaka akoranga.  Ka hono pai ngā kaupapa ako ki Te Whāriki o te Kōhanga Reo. Ka hāngai pū ngā mahere ki tēnā me tēnā o ngā rōpū reanga e toru, ā, e tino mōhiotia ana e ngā kaimahi me te whānau. Ka whakarato ki ngā tamariki he hōtaka akoranga i āta waihangatia, hei whakatutuki i ō rātou matea ako me ngā matea whanaketanga hoki. 

Te ako ngaio, te whakawhanaketanga ngaio. He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki te whakawhanaketanga ngaio me te whakangungu. He tokomaha ngā kaimahi, te whānau hoki e uru atu ana ki ngā whai wāhitanga ki te whakangungu i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo kaupapa.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Whānau o te Kōhanga me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro.  I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau;
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga;
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi;
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino);
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho);
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi; me
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia whai whakaaro ngā kaimahi me te whānau, me pēhea te whakawhanake i ngā tukanga kounga hei hāpai i te whakatairanga i te kounga o te reo Māori.  

6 Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Whānau o Te Rākatō is in Mahia. This marae based kōhanga reo provides high quality education and care for children. 

Whānau attend the kōhanga reo daily. They participate in kōhanga reo operations in numerous and meaningful ways. Whānau and staff attend professional development, gain qualifications and work collaboratively to manage the kōhanga reo in the best interests of their children.   

The kaiako and staff provide an exciting and stimulating learning environment. Children are settled, involved and comfortable. Interactions and relationships between children and adults are warm and caring. The daily programme flows smoothly. Adults help to prepare activities and resources for children, manage kai times and help with daily routines. Children are supported and encouraged to explore, play and engage in learning experiences and conversations.

Te reo Māori is the main language for children at the kōhanga reo. They are well supported to use te reo Māori. Staff speak te reo Māori throughout the day. Tikanga Māori is incorporated well into daily routines. Mihi, whakatau and karakia are modelled for children. Children take lead roles in helping to manage tikanga practices.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children atthe kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose as its focus area:

  • Mana Reo

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below. 

3 Findings

Mana Reo

Background

How well are children and whānau supported to learn and to use te reo Māori?

Children and whānau members are well supported to learn and to use te reo Māori.

Areas of good performance

Learning programme. Children are provided with numerous opportunities to select and direct their learning. Children are able to explore the kōhanga reo environment. A wide range of learning experiences occur in individual and group learning. Daily activities and routines run smoothly. Staff are well prepared to support children’s learning and follow their interests. Resources are accessible for children. Staff know their roles and responsibilities. Groups are well managed. The learning programme and activities are delivered by staff who are energetic, stimulating and create excitement for children.

Tikanga Māori. The whānau and staff involve children in tikanga such as welcoming visitors. Children are supported to take lead roles in karakia and during waiata sessions. Children’s knowledge is acknowledged and celebrated. Children learn about who they are and where they come from. Children learn about mana whenua concepts including knowing their connections to waka, awa and marae. Children are well supported to learn about their role as tangata whenua.

Te reo Māori. Children are well supported as developing speakers of te reo Māori. Staff use a wide range of effective language acquisition strategies and techniques. These strategies include prompting, modelling, demonstrating, pausing and using resources to support comprehension and understanding. Children are supported to stand confidently and talk about themselves. Staff help children to build sentences and to use te reo Māori when they can. Parents and whānau are involved in te reo Māori training and development. Many whānau have completed courses and qualifications to support their children to learn and to use te reo Māori. Staff maintain a te reo Māori environment throughout the day.

Areas for development

Accuracy of te reo Māori. The staff and whānau have yet to develop a quality assurance system to ensure that they are teaching and modelling te reo Māori accurately for children. Consideration should be given to how appraisal can be used to support improved capability in te reo Māori. 

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How well is the kōhanga reo managed in the interests of children?

The kōhanga reo is well managed in the interests of children.

Areas of good performance

Whānau management. The kōhanga reo is well managed by the whānau and staff. Staff and whānau are reflective practitioners who are focussed on continual improvement. Whānau participate in decision making. There is good reporting at whānau hui about key aspects of kōhanga operations. Self review is effective and ongoing. There is sound financial management and a clear strategic direction. Children are provided with a well managed and operated kōhanga reo.

Assessment. Staff document and record children’s learning well. Profile books are beautifully presented and include parent’s voice, children’s likes and dislikes, pepeha information, pānui from the kaiako about events and operations at kōhanga reo, information about the programme, observations of children’s involvement, annotated photos, art work, and learning goals with staff reflections. Staff reflect on children’s progress and success according to ngā taumata whakahirahira. Parents are well informed about their child’s progress and development.

Programme evaluation. Programme evaluation is effective. Staff members meet regularly to discuss the quality of the learning programme. Self review of what went well, what can be done better and what should happen next occurs regularly. Changes are made to kōhanga reo operations based on good quality self review information.

Programme planning.  There is comprehensive programme planning that effectively guides staff in the delivery of the learning programme. Kaupapa learning themes link well to Te Whāriki o te Kōhanga Reo. Plans are specific for each of the three age groups and well known by staff and the whānau. Children are provided with a well designed learning programme that meets their learning and development needs.

Professional learning and development. Whānau and staff are actively involved in professional development and training. Many staff and whānau participate in training opportunities for a variety of purposes.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO Kōhanga Whānau Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake. 

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends that the staff and whānau consider how quality control can be developed to help monitor the quality of te reo Māori.

6 When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
