1 Evaluation of St Clair Corner Early Learning Centre

How well placed is St Clair Corner Early Learning Centre to promote positive learning outcomes for children?

Not well placed

Requires further development

Well placed

Very well placed

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

St Clair Corner Early Learning Centre aims to provide a home away from home for children from two-to-five years of age. The centre provides a mixed-age programme for children with a strong focus on nature and the use of natural resources.

The centre licence has been recently changed so that it can now cater for fifty-four children.

The centre works closely with its sister centre, especially when transitioning children from one centre to the other. At the time of this review shared staff supported this movement. A unique feature of the centre is the high proportion of male teachers.

Centre leaders and teachers have made good progress in responding to the recommendations in the March 2011 ERO report. They have sought input from parents and teachers about what is going well in the centre and what can be improved. They have an ongoing focus on developing children‚Äôs communication and oral language skills and biculturalism. They have been developing the way information and communication technologies (ICT) are used to support learning and teaching.

This review was part of a cluster of two reviews in the St Clair Corner organisation.

The Review Findings

The Early Learning Centre provides a warm welcome to children and their parents and whƒÅnau. Children quickly settle on arrival and become comfortable with centre routines. This is achieved through the caring relationships established by the staff, and skilful use of tuakana/teina relationships (peer support). Children are given many opportunities to explore the various areas of the centre and engage in the range of experiences prepared by their teachers.

The centre environment and resources strongly reflect the natural world. These are purposefully provided to develop children‚Äôs curiosity, creativity and physical play and to encourage respect for the wider world and its resources. Teachers value relationships with parents and whƒÅnau. The centre has significantly developed the way it shows its value for te reo and tikanga MƒÅori and engages well with its MƒÅori whƒÅnau and wider community.

There is flexibility within the programme so that children‚Äôs changing interests can be readily responded to. Teachers put much time and effort into creating new resources and activities of interest to help engage the children in focused conversations and experiences for learning. They share information with each other about each child so that their planning is based on meaningful activities of interest. As a result, children develop a range of skills, including exploration and communication skills from the many well-planned choices of free activity and small group experiences.

Children‚Äôs strengths, interests, friendships and what learning is happening for them are quickly noticed and followed up on. This includes broadening their learning about Aotearoa/New Zealand, the world and people around them.

Teachers know the children well. ERO observed teachers skilfully engaging children in in-depth conversations that extend oral language and thinking skills. This includes ably using te reo MƒÅori and literacy and numeracy in children‚Äôs play and interactions.

Children are well supported as they move to and from the centre. Children in the process of moving to the centre from the Infants and Toddlers Centre are encouraged to develop confidence and independence skills. How well these skills are supported across the two centres may be a useful shared focus for review.

Teachers‚Äô increased use of eFolios has given them greater flexibility for managing their own work and increased the level of involvement of parents in their child‚Äôs learning.

The centre managers have placed a strong emphasis on developing the centre‚Äôs bicultural practices. They have carried out a very useful self review that has strengthened the centre‚Äôs relationship with MƒÅori whƒÅnau. A well-developed framework and systems support the centre‚Äôs operation and long-term planning.

Key Next Steps

A next step for the centre is to continue to develop how teachers plan for and assess each child‚Äôs learning. Teachers need to show continuity of learning for children in the many stories of learning written in children‚Äôs profiles and/or in teacher planning. Centre leaders and teachers need to show the difference teachers make to children‚Äôs learning, for example, by evaluating the effectiveness of the programmes and activities provided and the teaching and learning strategies used.

The next step for managers is to improve the focus across the centre for achieving the centre goals. This includes:

  • ensuring long term strategic planning has a stronger focus on children, particularly children‚Äôs learning and development
  • providing regular, ongoing written feedback to teachers and support to help meet the centre's goals and shared expectations for high quality
  • implementing a more rigorous review of centre policies.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of St Clair Corner Early Learning Centre completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist.

In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.
