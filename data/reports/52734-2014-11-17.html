1 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

He kōhanga reo noho marae Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūtiki Wharanui, ki Pūtiki i Whanganui.

E hāngai ana te taiao akoranga o ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori.Ko te Marae o Pūtiki te taiao e akoako ai ngā tamariki, i ia te wā, i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki tō rātou marae me ā rātou tikanga. E whanake ana ngā tamariki i tō rātou tuakiri ahurea me tō rātou tātaitanga tuku iho.

He pai te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki i tā rātou whakamahinga, tō rātou mōhiotanga anō hoki i te reo Māori. He pakari tā rātou whakamahinga i te reo Māori i ngā wā karakia, tae atu anō hoki ki ngā hīmene, ngā waiata, ngā mihi, ngā whakapapa, me ngā pepeha. Ka tino whakatauira te kaiako i te reo, ā, he tino matatau ia ki te reo Māori.

He pakari, he takitahi anō hoki ngā whakaaro o ngā tamariki. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā wheako hihiri e whai pūtake ana, hei hāpai i ngā tamariki ki te whakatau i ā rātou whakaritenga, te tūhura haere, me te hīraurau hopanga.

He kaha te whānau hei kaiwhakahaere, ā, he pai tā rātou whakahaere i te tari me tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka whakatutuki te hōtaka akoranga i ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Ka hāngai te arotake hōtaka ki te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Ka noho mōhio te whānau ki tēnā e akohia ana e ā rātou tamariki, me te āhua o tā rātou ahu whakamua.

Kāhore anō te whānau kia whakaoti ngā arohaehae mā ngā kaimahi, ā, kāhore anō hoki kia whakahou ake i tā rātou mahere rautaki.

2 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Arotahi o te Arotake

I mua o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, ā, kia whakamahia ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga.

Kātahi ka whakaritea ngā tino kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake, whai muri o te whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenga o te rōpū arotake o te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau. I arotahi tēnei whakawhitiwhitinga ki ngā kōrero i te puritia e te kōhanga reo (tāpiri atu ki ngā kōrero arotake whaiaro)ā, me te whānui o te pānga o ngā take mō te arotake ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo.

I whakaritea e te whānau ko tāna kaupapa arotahi ko Mana Aotūroa.

Ko te katoa o ngā arotake mātauranga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto o ngā kōhanga reo e arotahi ana ki te kounga o te mātauranga. Ki tā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o te:

  • arotake whaiaro a te whānau me te whakamahere rautaki
  • whakamahere hōtaka, te aromatawai, me te aromātai.

Ko ngā kitenga ā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Aotūroa

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whakawhanaketanga i te mōhio o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou tuakiri ahurea me ngā taonga tuku iho?

He pai te whakawhanaketanga i te mōhio o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou tuakiri ahurea me ngā taonga tuku iho.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

E hāngai ana te taiao akoranga o ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā wheako akoranga e whakatairanga ake ana i te tuakiri o ngā tamariki me tō rātou hononga ki a rātou anō. Whai wāhi ai ngā tamariki i ia te wā ki te ako i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki tō rātou marae me ngā tikanga. He ākonga mātātoa rātou ki ēnei horopaki. E whanake haere ana ngā mōhiotanga o ngā tamariki ki a rātou anō, ki tō rātou tūrangawaewae hoki.

He pai te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki i tā rātou whakamahinga, tō rātou mārama hoki i te reo Māori. He pakari tā rātou whakamahinga i te reo Māori i ngā wā karakia, tae atu ki ngā hīmene, ngā waiata, ngā mihi, ngā whakapapa me ngā pepeha. He tino tauira te kaiako o te reo, ā, he matatau ia ki te reo Māori. I ngā wā katoa, ka whakawhiti kōrero ia ki ngā tamariki hei whakawhānui ake i tō rātou reo. He mātātoa te tautoko o te whānau i te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori.

E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki kia pakari tā rātou kōkiri i ō rātou ake whakaaro. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā wheako hihiri e whai pūtake ana kia āhei ai ngā tamariki ki te whakatau i ngā whakaritenga, te tūhura haere, me te hīraurau hopanga. Ka kōkiri rātou i ngā wheako akoranga e akiaki ana i ngā tamariki ki te whakaaro auaha, kaikini hoki.

Ka whai hua te hāpai i ngā tamariki ki te whakawhanake i te mauritau o te waiora. Ko te waiora o ngā tamariki me te whānau tētahi kaupapa matua. Kei te tino mōhio ngā kaimahi ki ngā matea o ā rātou tamariki katoa. Aroturukihia ai e ngā kaimahi i ia te wā, te whai pānga o ā rātou whakaritenga ki te whakawhanaketanga i te katoa o te tamaiti. Ko ngā matea kai oranga o ngā tamariki me te whānau tētahi āhuatanga nui. Ka akiaki, ka whakanui hoki te whānau i te oranga kai ki te kōhanga reo.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

He pēhea te whai hua o ngā mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga reo hei hāpai i ngā wawata o ngā tamariki me te whānau mō te ako?

Ka tino hāpaitia ngā tamariki me te whānau kia tutukihia ai ō rātou wawata mā te whai huatanga o ngā mahi whakahaere ki te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Ka kitea te tino ngākau nuitanga o te whānau ki te hāpai i ngā akoranga a ā rātou tamariki. E hiahia ana te whānau kia tū pakari ā rātou tamariki mā te mōhio ki a rātou anō me tō rātou tūrangawaewae. He mātātoa tā rātou ako i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo, ngā tūmanako e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga, Te Whāriki, me te reo Māori. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te mātātoa o te whai wāhi atu a te whānau ki te kōhanga reo, me tā rātou whakarato i ngā wheako akoranga o te kounga kairangi.

Ka āta whakapuaki te tūtohinga i ngā wawata, ngā tūmanako, me ngā tino uaratanga o te whānau mō tō rātou kōhanga reo. Ka whakapūmau i te tino hononga o te whānau ki te marae o Pūtiki. Ko ētahi atu mōhiohio ki te tūtohinga, ko ngā whakamāramatanga e pā ana ki ngā whakaritenga papai, ngā tūranga o te whānau, me ngā kawenga mahi. E mōhio ana te whānau, he mea nui te mahi ngātahi.

He pai te māramatanga o te whānau ki te whakahirahiratanga o te arotake i ngā kaupapa here. Arotakengia ai aua kaupapa here i ia tau. He mārama, he matawhānui hoki ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga hei ārahi i ā rātou mahi whakahaere. Ko ētahi o ēnei, ko te hauora me te haumaru, ngā kaimahi katoa, me te whakahaeretanga i te whānau. Ka hāngai ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga ki ngā herenga ā-ture o te wā me te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo. Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao i āta whakaritea ai kia noho haumaru ai rātou.

Ka hāngai ngā whakaritenga a te whānau ki ngā mahi a te kaituku mahi pai. He pai ā rātou pūnaha hei hāpai i ngā kaimahi i roto i ā rātou mahi. Kua oti kētia ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi me ngā kirimana mahi.

He pakari te whakamahere rautaki. Kua oti i te whānau te mahere rautaki. Kua tino whakatutukihia e rātou ā rātou whāinga mō te hōtaka akoranga.

Ka whakamahi te whānau i ngā mōhiohio ka pupū ake i ā rātou mahi arotake whaiaro, hei tātari i te aronga whakamua o te kōhanga reo ā meāke nei. Ka whiwhi, ka whakatutuki haere hoki i ngā pūrongo ka whakapuakihia ki ngā hui i ia te wā. He matawhānui te arotake whaiaro i ia te wā, ā, he painga mō ngā tamariki. Ka whai wāhi atu te whānau ki te arotake whaiaro i ngā wā ka pupū ake ngā take me ngā kaupapa kāhore anō rātou kia whakarite ai. Ka aroturuki, ka pūrongo hoki rātou i te ahunga whakamua o tā rātou mahere rautaki. Ka whai hua nui te arotake whaiaro ki ngā tamariki.

Ka wheako ngā tamariki i ngā kaupapa i āta whakamaheretia ai me ngā wheako akoranga ka aromātaitia i ia te wā mō te whai huatanga o aua mahi. Ka whai kiko, ka whai pūtake hoki ngā mahere hōtaka. Ka tino ārahi, ka tino tautoko hoki te kaiako i ngā kaimahi katoa, i roto i te whakamaheretanga me te waihanga o te hōtaka akoranga. Ka poipoi te hōtaka i ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Kua āta tuhia te aromātai hōtaka, ā, e arotahi ana ki te whai huatanga o te hōtaka akoranga.

Ka whakamōhiotia te whānau ki tēnā e akohia ana e ā rātou tamariki. E ako ana ngā tamariki i tō rātou tuakiri me ngā taonga tuku iho. He pai te whakatakoto haere, te pūrongo hoki i aua mōhiohio.

Te wāhanga hei whakawhanake

Kāhore anō ngā arohaehae kia oti, hei aroturuki i te āhua o ngā mahi a ngā kaimahi, hei whakamōhio anō hoki i ngā matea e pā ana ki te whakawhanaketanga ngaio.

4 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

Te Tirohanga Whānui

I mua o te arotake, i whakakīia e te kaipupuri raihana, te whānau, me ngā kaimahi o te kōhanga reo tētahi Tauāki Kupu Tūturu a te Kōhanga Reo kua Raihanatia, o Pipiri 2013, me tētahi Rārangi Tātari Whaiaro mā te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. I roto i ēnei tuhinga i oati rātou, i whāia e rātou ngā huarahi whai take hei whakatutuki i ā rātou herenga ā-ture e pā ana ki:

  • ngā whakahaerenga ā te whānau
  • te hauora, te waiora me te whai oranga
  • ngā whakahaerenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakahaerenga pūtea me ngā whakahaerenga rawa.

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei, i te mea he nui te pānga o ēnei ki ngā hua ka puta ki ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki, ko te ārai i ngā mahi whakaweti, mahi tūkino)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te whakatau tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe, tiaki tamariki; ko ngā aituā me te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • ngā tohu mātauranga me ngā whakaritenga kaimahi
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

I tāutuhia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā wāhanga e whai ake nei kāhore e whakatutukihia ana e ai ki te ture:

4.1 me whakarite i te haere tonutanga o tētahi tukanga e pā ana ki te arohaehae me te tautoko ngaio i ngā kaimahi i ia te wā.

[R47 (1) (e) Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008]

5 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia tautoko te whānau i ngā kaimahi ki te whakaoti i te tukanga arohaehae, hei whakamōhio anō hoki i te whakariterite o te whakawhanaketanga ngaio.

Hei ā hea te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō a i te kōhanga reo?

Tērā ka whakahaeretia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri, i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū-ā-Motu)

17 Whiringa-ā-Rangi 2014

1 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Pūtiki Wharanui is marae-based and located in Pūtiki, Whanganui.

Children have a learning environment that reflects te ao Māori. Pūtiki Marae provides them with opportunities regularly to learn about their marae and customs. Children are developing their cultural identity and ancestral heritage.

Children are developing well in their use and knowledge of te reo Māori. They confidently use te reo Māori during karakia, hīmene, waiata, mihi, whakapapa and pepeha.The kaiako is a strong language model and has a high level of te reo Māori competency.

Children are confident and independent thinkers. Kaimahi and whānau provide exciting and meaningful experiences that support children to make decisions, explore, and problem solve.

Whānau are effective managers who administer their kōhanga reo well. The learning programme caters for children’s needs and interests. Programme review focuses on the effectiveness of the learning programme. Whānau are kept informed of what their children learn and how they are progressing.

Whānau have yet to complete appraisals for kaimahi and the update of their strategic plan.

2 Review Priorities

The Focus of the Review

Before the review, the whānau were invited to consider its priorities for review using guidelines and resources provided by ERO.

The detailed priorities for review were then determined following a discussion between the ERO review team and whānau. This discussion focused on existing information held by the kōhanga reo (including self-review information) and the extent to which potential issues for review contributed to positive outcomes for children at the kōhanga reo.

The whānau chose Mana Aotūroa as its focus area.

All ERO education reviews in kōhanga reo focus on the quality of education. For ERO this includes the quality of:

  • whānau self review and strategic planning
  • programme planning, assessment and evaluation.

ERO’s findings in these areas are set out below.

3 Findings

Mana Aotūroa

Background

How well are children developing knowledge of their cultural identity and ancestral heritage?

Children are developing well in their knowledge of their cultural identity and ancestral heritage.

Areas of good performance

Children have a learning environment that reflects te ao Māori. Kaimahi provide a wide range of learning experiences that enhance children’s sense of identity and connections to who they are. Children regularly have opportunities to learn about their marae and customs. They are active learners in these settings. Children are developing knowledge of who they are and where they come from.

Children are developing well in their use and understanding of te reo Māori. They confidently use te reo Māori during karakia, hīmene, waiata, mihi, whakapapa and pepeha.The kaiako is a strong language model and has a high level of te reo Māori competency. She engages in conversations with children to extend their language. Whānau actively support children’s te reo Māori use.

Children are developing as confident and independent thinkers. Kaimahi and whānau provide exciting and meaningful experiences that enable children to make decisions, explore and problem solve. They facilitate learning experiences that encourage children to be creative and critical thinkers.

Children are effectively supported to develop a positive sense of wellbeing. The wellbeing of children and whānau are major priorities. Kaimahi are fully aware of the needs of all their children. Kaimahi constantly monitor how their practices that impact on children’s holistic development. The nutritional needs of children and whānau are important. The whānau encourages and promotes healthy eating at the kōhanga reo.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

How successfully are children and whānau aspirations for learning supported through management of the kōhanga reo?

Children and whānau are successfully supported to realise their aspirations through effective whānau management while at the kōhanga reo.

Areas of good performance

Whānau demonstrate a strong commitment to support their children’s learning. Whānau want their children to be confident in who they are and where they come from. They actively learn about the kaupapa of the kōhanga reo, management expectations, Te Whāriki, and te reo Māori. Children benefit as their whānau take an active role in the kōhanga reo and provide high quality learning experiences.

The charter clearly articulates for whānau the aspirations, expectations and important values of their kōhanga reo. It reinforces the strong connection of whānau to Pūtiki Marae. Other information in the charter includes descriptions of good practice and whānau roles and responsibilities. Whānau recognise the importance of working together.

Whānau have a good understanding of the importance of policy review. These are reviewed every year. They have clear, comprehensive policies and procedures to guide their management practice. These include health and safety, personnel and whānau management. Policies and procedures reflect current regulations and the kōhanga reo philosophy. Children learn in an environment where all reasonable steps are taken to safeguard them.

Whānau practice reflects elements of good employer practice. They have good systems to support kaimahi in their work. Job descriptions and employment contracts are complete.

Strategic planning is strong. Whānau have completed the strategic plan. They have successfully achieved their goals for the learning programme.

Self review. Whānau use information from their self review activities to determine the future direction of the kōhanga reo. They receive and act on reports that are regularly shared at hui. Regular self review is thorough and benefits children. Whānau engage in self review when unplanned events and issues arise. They monitor and report on progress of their strategic plan. Self review benefits children in significant ways.

Children experience well planned kaupapa and learning experiences which are regularly evaluated for their effectiveness. Programme planning is detailed and useful. The kaiako provides strong leadership and support to all kaimahi in the planning and design of the learning programme. The programme caters for children’s needs and interests. Well documented programme evaluation focuses on the effectiveness of the learning programme.

Whānau are kept informed about what their children are learning. Children are learning about their identity and heritage. This information is well recorded and reported.

Area for development

Monitoring of kaimahi performance to inform professional development needs through appraisals are incomplete.

4 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

Overview

Before the review, the licensee, whānau and staff of the kōhanga reo completed an ERO LicensedKōhanga Reo Assurance Statement June 2013 and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they have attested that they had taken all reasonable steps to meet their legislative obligations related to:

  • administration
  • health, safety and welfare
  • personnel management
  • financial and property management.

During the review, ERO checked the following items because they have a potentially high impact on outcomes for children:

  • emotional safety (including behaviour management, prevention of bullying and abuse)
  • physical safety (including behaviour management, sleeping and supervision practices; accidents and medication; hygiene and routines; travel and excursion policies and procedures)
  • staff qualifications and organisation
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

ERO identified the following areas of non-compliance:

4.1 ensure that an ongoing process for the regular appraisal and professional support for staff is in place.[R47 (1) (e) Education (ECC) Regulations 2008]

5 Recommendations

ERO recommends that whānausupport kaimahi to complete the appraisal process to inform planning for professional development.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.
