1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Awa Kairangi ki ngā papa o te kura takawaenga o Avalon, ki Te Awakairangi. Kua āta whakaritea e te whānau ngā hononga papai ki te kura. Ka ahu mai ngā tamariki o te kōhanga reo i te hapori whānui, tae atu tonu ki a rātou e noho mai ana ki Wainuiōmata. E noho ngākau nui ana te whānau ki te āta hāpai i te whakaratonga a ngā kaimahi i te tino mātauranga ki ā rātou tamariki.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Aromātai

He pēhea rawa ngā tamariki e whakaatu i tō rātou harikoa, i ō rātou ngākau titikaha, i tō rātou tū takitahi anō hoki hei ākonga?

Ka kitea ki ngā tamariki te whanaketanga o ō rātou ngākau titikaha, me tō rātou tū takitahi hei ākonga, puta noa i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga me ā rātou taunekeneke ki ētahi atu.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira  

E tino tautokona ana te ako a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. He tino tauira ngā kaimahi o te reo Māori. Kei te āta māia haere ngā tamariki ki te whakamahi i te reo Māori, me te whakautu kōrero anō hoki mā te tautoko o ngā kaimahi. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi ka whanake ngā pūkenga reo o ngā tamariki mā te akiaki i a rātou ki te whai wāhi atu ki ngā taumahi whakangahau. Ka akiaki rātou i ngā tamariki ki te tū hei kaiārahi, me te tātaki i ngā waiata me ngā karakia. Ka hāngai hoki tēnei ki ngā mahi pērā i te waiata, te kanikani, te whakatauira, me tā rātou whiwhi anō hoki i te nui o ngā kōrero akiaki, kōrero whakamihimihi hoki. Ka whai wāhi atu hoki ngā pēpi ki te taha tonu o ō rātou tuākana, ā, he pārekareka ki a rātou te noho tahi. E whanake ana ngā tamariki ki te kōrero i te reo Māori.

E ako ana ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e āta poipoi ana i a rātou, ā, he mauritau, he harikoa hoki rātou. Ka whakapuakihia e ngā kaimahi, e te whānau hoki tētahi taiao ako o roto, o waho e atawhai ana, e noho muramura ana hoki mō ngā tamariki. Ka kitea ki ngā whakaaturanga i ngā pakitara, ko te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo wheako akoranga i whakatutukihia ai e ngā tamariki. Ka āta poipoia e ngā kaimahi ngā matea o ngā pēpi, ā, ka aro wawe atu rātou ki ngā pēpi i a rātou e hiamoe ana, e hiakai ana rānei, e tika ana rānei kia āta tautāwhitia. He whai wāhitanga mō ngā pēpi ki te tūhura i tō rātou wāhi e noho wāteatea ana ki a rātou. He pukumahi te āhua o tā rātou whai wāhi atu ki ngā rauemi me ngā taumahi, ā, e ākina ana hoki tā rātou tohatoha haere ki te taha o ētahi atu, me te atawhai o tētahi ki tētahi. Ko te mauritau me te manaaki e whakatinana ana i ngā hononga me ngā taunekeneke o te whānau, he tino tauira anō hoki mō ngā tamariki. He mauritau, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki hei ākonga.

He ākonga mātātoa ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e mau ana i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i ngā whai wāhitanga mō ngā tamariki ki te tūhura me te whakamātau i ngā rauemi, hei whakawhanake i tō rātou māramatanga ki te āhua o ngā whakamahinga o aua taonga. Ko ngā tamariki e mau ana i ngā matea tauwhāiti, ka whiwhi i te tautoko o waho i roto i te hōtaka o ia rā. Ka mahi ngātahi te whānau ki ngā ratonga o waho, hei āta whakarite i te whakatakotoranga o ngā āhuatanga ako e tika ana ki te whakatutuki i ngā matea ake o te tamaiti. Ka akiaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki ki te āta whakapuaki i ō rātou whakaaro mā ngā pātai papai me ngā whakautu i āta whakaarohia ai. Kei te tino mōhio ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki, ā, ka tautāwhi, ka tautoko hoki i a rātou i ngā wā e tika ana. Kua waia ngā tamariki ki ngā mahinga ako, ā, he pai ki a rātou te whakatutuki haere i aua mahinga. Ka whakaratohia ki ngā tamariki ngā whai wāhitanga ki te whai i ngā akoranga e ngākau nuitia ana e rātou. E whai pūtake ana te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki ā rātou akoranga.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai 

He pai ngā hanganga whakamahere a ngā kaimahi, hei whakatutuki i ngā matea o ngā tamariki. He hou ngā hanganga nei, ā, kei te waia haere ngā kaimahi ki tēnā e tūmanakohia ana mō rātou. Ka kitea ki ngā mahere ngā hononga ki Te Whāriki. Kei te kaiwhakahaere te kawenga ki te āta whakarite i te whakatakotoranga o ngā mahi whakamahere, mahi whakaako hoki e whai kounga ana. E whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i ngā painga ka ahu mai i ngā akoranga i āta whakamaheretia ai.

Kei ngā kaimahi tētahi tukanga ki te aromatawai i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Kua whakaae te whānau ki te whakamahi i tētahi hōtaka aromatawai ā-ipurangi o waho, mō te whakatakotoranga haere i ngā aromatawai e pā ana ki ngā tamariki. Kei te maioha te whānau ki aua aromatawai, me ngā whakaahua, ngā kōrero hoki ka puta i ia rā mā te whārangi Pukamata tūmataiti o te kōhanga reo. Kei te harikoa rātou mō te āhua o ngā pārongo e whiwhi ana rātou. He pai te whakamōhio i te whānau mō te ahu whakamua o ā rātou tamariki.

E whanake ana te aromātai hōtaka, ā, he wāhanga anō hoki tēnei o ngā tūmanako hou e whai pānga ana ki ngā mahere me te aromatawai. Kua whakamahia ngā mōhiohio o te aromātai o tērā tau, ki te whakapai ake i te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga o ngā tamariki.

Ko te atawhai me te waiora o ngā tamariki tētahi aronga matua o te whānau. He pai ngā tukanga me ngā pūnaha ā te whānau ki te kawe me te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. E tūmanakohia ana kia taetae ngā whānau ki ngā hui i ia te wā, ā, e whakatakoto ana ngā meneti o ngā hui i te pai o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero me te pakari anō hoki o ngā whakataunga. E hāngai ana ngā kaupapa here ki te wā, ā, kua hāpai tēnei i te ārahitanga o te whānau i ā rātou whakaritenga. E hāpai ana te tukanga arohaehae i te whakapaitanga ake o ngā kaimahi i ā rātou whakaritenga, ā, e tino whakahaerehia ana e te whānau. E tautoko ana te whānau i ngā whakangungu ngaio. E whiwhi ana ngā tamariki i ngā painga ka ahu mai i te kaha o ngā whakahaeretanga i te kōhanga reo.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Kua kōrerohia ngā whāinga o te pae tawhiti me te pae tata, engari kāhore anō kia whakaritea tētahi mahere ki te whakatutuki haere i aua whāinga. Ko tētahi mahere mahi e hāngai ana ki ngā whāinga, ki te roanga o te wā ki te whakatutuki haere i aua whāinga, ki ngā tāngata ka aroturuki i te mahere, ā, ki ngā pūtea hoki e tika ana ki te hāpai i aua whāinga, ka āta whakarite i te rōnakitanga pae tawhiti mō te whānau. Ka hāpai taua mahi i te paitanga ake o ngā putanga pae tawhiti mō ngā tamariki me te whānau.

3 Te Whakatau a te Whānau ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te aromātai, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti) 
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua. 

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture. 

4 Te Taunakitanga

I taunakihia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau kia:

  • whakaritea tētahi mahere ka āta whakatau i te rōnakitanga pae tawhiti o te kōhanga reo.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga aromātai anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka aromātai anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Awa Kairangi i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Awa Kairangi is located on the grounds of Avalon Intermediate School in Lower Hutt. The whānau has established positive working relationship with the school. Children from the wider community including Wainuiomata attend the kōhanga reo. The whānau is committed to ensuring kaimahi are supported to provide the best possible education for their children.

2 The Evaluation Findings

How well do children show they are happy, confident and independent learners?

Children show they are developing confidence and independence as learners across a range of learning experiences and in their interactions with others.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are well supported to learn te reo Maori. Kaimahi are good te reo Māori role models. Children are beginning to develop confidence to use and respond in te reo Māori with support from kaimahi. Kaimahi recognise that children’s language skills will develop by encouraging them to participate in fun activities. They encourage children to take leadership roles in leading waiata and karakia. These also include singing, dancing, modelling and receiving lots of encouragement and praise. Babies participate with their tuākana and enjoy the time together. Children are developing as speakers of te reo Māori.

Children learn in an environment where they are settled and happy.  Kaimahi and whānau present a warm, colourful indoor and outdoor learning environment. Wall displays show the broad range of learning experiences children participate in. Babies needs are well met by kaimahi who respond immediately when they are tired, hungry or need a reassuring cuddle. Babies have opportunities to explore their space and move freely. Resources and activities appear to keep them busy and they are encouraged to share with others and be kind. The positive and caring relationship and interactions whānau have are good models for children. Children are settled, happy learners.

Children are active learners in an environment that is well resourced. Kaimahi provide opportunities for the children to explore and experiment with resources to develop their understanding of how things work. Children with special needs receive extra external support in the daily programme. Whānau work collaboratively with external agencies to ensure that appropriate learning conditions are established to meet the specialist needs of this particular child.  Kaimahi encourage children to express themselves well through good questions and thoughtful answers. Kaimahi know the children very well and comfort and support them when needed. Children are familiar with the learning routines and happily complete tasks. Children are provided opportunities to pursue learning that is of interest to them. Children are purposefully engaged in their learning

Planning and Evaluation

Kaimahi have good planning structures to meet children’s needs. These are new and kaimahi are becoming familiar with what is expected of them. The links to Te Whāriki are evident in the planning. The manager is responsible for ensuring quality planning and teaching practices are in place. Children benefit from learning that is well planned.

Kaimahi have in place a process for assessing children’s learning. The whānau has agreed to adopt an external online assessment programme to record children’s assessments. Whānau appreciate these and the daily photos and kōrero on the closed Facebook page. They are happy with the information they receive. Whānau are well informed of their children’s progress

Programme evaluation is developing and is part of the new expectations for planning and assessment. Information from the previous year’s evaluation has been used to improve the quality of the learning programme for children.

The welfare and wellbeing of children is a priority for the whānau. The whānau has good processes and systems to manage and operate the kōhanga reo. Whānau are expected to attend regular hui and minutes of meetings record good discussions and sound decision making. Policies are current and have supported the whānau to guide their practice. The appraisal process supports kaimahi to improve their practice and is well managed by the whānau. Professional training is supported by the whānau. Children benefit from a kōhanga reo that is well managed.

Key Next Steps

Long and short term goals have been discussed, however there is no plan to realise these. An action plan based on what the goals are, what time it will take to achieve these, who will monitor the plan and what finances are needed to support these will ensure long term sustainability for the whānau. This will contribute to better, long term outcomes for children and the whānau.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the evaluation, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the whānau recommend that;

  • A plan be established to ensure the long term sustainability of the kōhanga reo. 

When is ERO likely to evaluate the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO evaluation of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Awa Kairangi will be in three years.
