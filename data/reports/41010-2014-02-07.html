2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

Ko Tawhiuau te maunga

Ko Rangitaiki te awa

Ko Mataatua te waka

Ko Tangiharuru te tangata

Ko Ngāti Manawa te iwi

Kei tua atu i te taone o Murupara Te Kōhanga Reo o Rangitahi, ā, kei muri i te marae o Rangitahi. I tapaina te marae me te kōhanga reo ki te ingoa o Rangitahi nā tētahi puna i whāngai i te iwi e noho ana ki te marae. He tino wāhi a Ngāti Manawatanga ki te marautanga me ngā mahi o te kōhanga reo. E ai ki te whānau ka noho rūmaki ngā mokopuna katoa ki ngā tikanga me te reo Māori, ngā waiata, ngā pūrākau me ngā mōteatea o te iwi. Ki tā te iwi he pērā te mana o Ngati Manawatanga ki tērā oTe WhārikimeTe Korowai.

I kōwhiri te whānau ko Mana Whenua hei wāhi arotahi mō tēnei arotake. E whakapono ana rātou ko te tikanga o te Mana Whenua ko te āhei kia noho ko te tuakiri o ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā mahi katoa o te kōhanga reo. Kei te kaha te whānau ki te aro ki te whakaora me te mau i tō rātou Ngāti Manawatanga.

He kaha te whakaatu o te hōtaka akoranga i ngā āhua me ngā mahi motuhake o te iwi. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi i te whānui o ngā mahi hei whakatairanga i te ako o ngā tamariki ki a Ngāti Manawa. Ka hāpaitia rātou ki te whakawhanake māramatanga ki o rātou hītori, o rātou tuakiri me tō rātou reo. He pai ki ngā tamariki te hōtaka whakatutuki pai i o rātou matea akoranga. He pai te whakarite i te hōtaka mō ngā pēpi, ā, e hāngai tika ana ki o rātou matea. He angitū te whakakotahi i te tiaki me te mātauranga.He rahi ngā rauemi kei te hōtaka akoranga. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki i tētahi hōtaka akoranga e whakarato ana i ngā wero ā hinengaro, ā tinana hoki.

E ngākaunui ana, e manawa popore ana te whānau ki te mātauranga me te oranga o ā rātou tamariki. He pai te tuhi me te amiki o ngā mahi whakamahere. He mārama ngā rauemi me ngā rautaki e pā ana ki te whakatinana i te hōtaka. Ka whai hua ngā tamariki i tētahi hōtaka e ōrite ana, ā, e mōhio pai ana e ngā tamariki. Ka whakamahi ngā kōpaki mahi me ngā pūrongo takitahi hei aromatawai i te ako o ngā tamariki. He pai te mōhio o ngā tamariki ki te ahunga whakamua o ā rātou tamariki.

Ka whakamārama te tūtohinga i te hītori o te kōhanga reo. Kua tāutu ngā moemoea me ngā wawata o te whānau mō a rātou tamariki. He tino wāhi a Ngāti Manawa ki te tūtohinga. He whānui ngā kaupapa here. Ka arotake ngā kaupapa here hauora me te haumaru i ia wā. He mea nui kia whakahaere te whānau i ngā putanga ohorere i ia wā.

Ka hāpai Te Ara Tūāpae i te mahere rautaki me ngā mahi. He tino whāinga nō te whānau ko te whakapakari ake i te kaha ki te reo Māori. He rite te tuku pūrongo ki ngā hui ā whānau i ia marama i runga i ngā mahi whakahaere i te kōhanga reo. He mārama ngā pūrongo a pūtea. He rahi ngā pūtea o te whānau hei whakahaere i tō rātou kōhanga reo. Me tahuri ngā kaimahi ināia nei ki te aroturuki i te kounga o te hōtaka akoranga.

Ngā Mahi ā Meake Nei

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kohanga hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Kaupapa Motuhake o te Arotake

Te Titiro Hāngai a te Arotake

I mua atu i te whakahaerenga o te arotake, i īnoitia te whānau o Te Kōhanga Reo o Rangitahi kia whai whakaaro ki ā rātou kaupapa motuhake mō te arotake mā te whai i ngā aratohu me ngā rauemi i whakaratohia e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ki a rātou.

Ī muri mai i ngā whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero i waenganui i te rōpū arotake o Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau, ka whakaritea e rātou ngā tino kaupapa motuhake hei arotakehanga. I titiro ēnei whakawhitiwhitinga kōrero ki runga i ngā kōrero i te pupuritia e te kōhanga reo (e whai wāhia anangā kōrero arotake whaiaro),ā, me te whānuitanga o ngā take pūmanawa hei arotakehanga i runga i ngā putanga e whai hua ana ki ngā mokopuna oTe Kōhanga Reo o Rangitahi.

Ko te kaupapa i whakaritea e te whānau hei titirohanga mā tēnei arotake ko:

  • Mana Whenua.

E titiro ana te katoa o ngā arotake a ERO i roto i ngā kōhanga, ki runga i te kounga o te mātauranga. E ai ki a ERO, kei roto i tēnei ko te kounga o:

  • ngā arotake whaiaro ā te whānau; me ngā whakamāhere rautaki; me
  • ngā whakamāhere o ngā hōtaka akoranga, ngā aro matawai me ngā aro mātai.

Ko ngā kitenga a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i roto i ēnei wāhanga, kei raro iho nei.

4 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Mana Whenua

He whakamārama

I kōwhiri te whānau ko Mana Whenua hei wāhi arotahi mō tēnei arotake. Kei te whakapono te whānau ko te tino ngako o Mana Whenua ko te āhei kia noho ko te tuakiri o ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā mahi katoa o te kōhanga reo. E arotahi ana te whānau ki te whakaora anō me te tiaki i a Ngāti Manawa. Ka arotake te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te whānui o te hāpai o te ako o ngā tamariki i tō rātou reo me tō rātou tuakiri.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Mana Whenua.Ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki tētahi hōtaka akoranga e whakaū ana i a rātou hei tino mema o te kōhanga reo me te hapori whānui o Murupara. Ka whakaū te hōtaka i te tuakiri o ngā tamariki mā ngā mahi e whai ake nei:

  • ka taki ngā tamariki i o rātou pepeha kātahi ka kaha ake o rātou hononga ki Ngāti Manawa
  • ka waiata ngā tamariki i ngā waiata o Ngāti Manawa
  • he mātātoa ngā tamariki me te whānau ki ngā tangihanga me ngā huihuinga ki te marae
  • ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te tuhura ki te taiao o Papatuanuku kei tua atu i te kōhanga reo
  • ka whakaako mōteatea te kuia ki ngā pakeke me ngā tamariki e pā ana ki ngā tipuna me ngā mahi o neherā
  • ka whakauru ngā kaimahi i ngā haerenga ki ngā wāhi tapu o te rohe ki te hōtaka akoranga
  • he matatau te nuinga o ngā kaimahi ki te reo Māori. He whakarae te reo o Ngāti Manawa, ā, ka whakatauiratia ki ngā tamariki
  • he kaha te whanaungatanga o te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki Te Wharekura o Tāwhiuau. He maheni te whakawhiti ki te kura. Ka haere te nuinga o ngā tamariki ki tēnei kura.
  • He māia ngā tuakana ki te kōrero i te reo Māori, ā, kei te whanake haere te tino mōhio o ngā tamariki ko wai rātou, nō hea hoki rātou.
  • Ngā whai wāhitanga ako.Kei te rata ngā tamariki ki te hōtaka whakatutuki i o rātou matea ako. Ko ētahi āhua takatika o te hōtaka nei kua pai te whakarite me te whakahaere ko ēnei e whai ake nei:
  • ka whai wāhi ngā tamariki ki te whānui o ngā akoranga. Nō reira ka taea e ngā tamariki te whai i o rātou ngākaunui, te hiraurau hopanga, te whai wāhi ki te ako me o rātou hoa me te whakatau wero whaiaro
  • he māhorahora te taiao ki rō whare me waho, ā, he māmā te whai wāhi o ngā tamariki ki ēnei wāhi
  • he tōtika te aro ki ngā matea o ngā tamariki. He angitū te kōmitimiti i te tiaki me te mātauranga. Ka whakatau ngā pēpi i te rere o te hōtaka. He rangimārie, he marino te ruma
  • ka whakarato ngā rauemi tika, ngā rauemi whānui.

Ka whai hua ngā tamariki i tētahi hōtaka akoranga ka whakarato i ngā wero ā hinengaro me ngā wero ā tinana.

Te Whakamāherehere me te Aromātai

He whakamārama

E aro mātai ana a ERO i ngā wā katoa, i te kounga o ngā whakamahere me ngā aro mātai i roto i ngā kōhanga reo ki te titiro ki te kaha o te whānau o te kōhanga ki te whakamahere, te whakahaere me te aro turuki i te katoa o ngā wāhanga whakahaere o te kōhanga mō aratou tamariki me te whānau. Kei roto i te wāhanga o te whakamahere me te aro mātai ko te whakamahere hōtaka me te aro turuki i te whanaketanga o ngā tamariki.

Ngā wāhanga e pai ana te whakahaere

Te whakamahere me te aromatawai.He pai te tuhi i ngā mahi whakamahere, ā, he āmiki ēnei. Kei reira tētahi mahere ka whakangohe e ai ki te pakeke me ngā āheinga o ngā tamariki. Ka tāutu ngā rauemi me ngā rautaki hei whakatinana. He mārama te tāutu i ngā kaupapa ako me ngā mahi o ia rā. Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i tētahi hōtaka e ōrite ana me te whakatutuki i o rātou matea.

Ka whakamahi tōtika ngā pūkete me ngā pūrongo a tēna, a tēna hei aromatawai i te ako o ngā tamariki. He pai te mōhio o te whānau ki te ahunga whakamua o a rātou tamariki.

Te tūtohinga.Ko Ngāti Manawatanga tētahi tino aronga o te tūtohinga. Ka whakamārama te tūtohinga i te hītori o te kōhanga reo. Ka tāutuhia te tirohanga roa me ngā wawata o te whānau mō a rātou tamariki.

Ngā kaupapa here.He whānui ngā kaupapa here. Ka arotake ngā kaupapa here hauora me te haumaru i ia wā. Kei reira katoa ngā rehita hei hāpai i ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga. Ka whakarato te whānau i tētahi taiao ko te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki te tino whāinga.

Te mahere rautaki.Ka kīnaki ngā whāinga rautaki o Te Ara Tūāpae i te mahere rautaki me ōna mahi. Ko te whakakaha ake i te reo Māori tētahi whāinga matua mō te whānau. Kei te mōhio rātou ki te hononga i waenganui i te kaupapa o te kōhanga reo me o rātou wawata mō te whakaoranga o Ngāti Manawatanga.

Te arotake whaiaro.He tōtika te whakamahi i te arotake whaiaro hei aroturuki i ngā mahi o te kōhanga reo me te hāpai i ngā whakapaitanga ki ngā mahi katoa. Ka tuku pūrongo ki ngā hui a whānau i ia marama e pā ana ki ngā āhuatanga o ngā whakahaere me ngā mahi o te kōhanga reo. Ka kōrerotia ngā take ka pupū ake me ngā wā ka tūtohu he arotake anō.

Ngā take a pūtea.He mārama ngā pūrongo a pūtea. Ka whakamāramatia ngā whakawhitinga katoa. He takatika te pūrongo whakahaere a te kaitātari. He tirohanga a tahua tā te kōhanga reo tae atu ana ki ngā tākoha ki te whiwhi rauemi mō te hōtaka akoranga. He pai te tūranga a pūtea o te kōhanga reo.

Ngā wahanga hei whakapai ake

Te aromātai i te hōtaka. Kāore ngā hui ā kaimahi me ngā pūrongo ā kaiako i te whakarato i ngā kōrero tika hei aroturuki i te tōika o te hōtaka. He mea nui kia arotahi te hui ā kaimahi ki te hōtaka akoranga. Me arotahi ngā whakawhitinga kōrero ki ngā mea i pai te haere, ngā mea kāore i pai te haere, me aha kē tatou kia pai ake ngā putanga mō ngā tamariki.

Ko Ngāti Manawa te iwi

Te Kōhanga Reo o Rangitahi is based on the outskirts of the township of Murupara and is located at the rear of Rangitahi Marae. The marae and kōhanga reo take their name from a puna which sustained whanau who lived on and around the marae. Ngāti Manawatanga is an integral part of the curriculum and operations. According to the whānau, this means that all mokopuna at the kōhanga reo will be immersed in tikanga and te reo Māori, waiata, pūrakau and mōteatea from their iwi. The whānau believe Ngāti Manawatanga to be of equal importance to that ofTe WhārikiandTe Korowai.

Whānau choose Mana Whenua as the focus for this review. They assert that Mana Whenua is about making sure children’s identity is at the centre of all kōhanga reo activities. The whānau is strongly focused on the revitalisation and maintenance of Ngāti Manawa.

The learning programme strongly reflects the unique features and practices of the iwi. Kaimahi provide a range of activities to promote children’s learning about Ngāti Manawa. They are supported to develop an understanding of their history, identity and language. Children enjoy a programme where their learning needs are well catered for. The programme for babies is organised and focussed on their needs. Care and education are successfully integrated. The learning programme is well resourced. Children benefit from a learning programme that provides intellectual and physical challenge.

The whānau are dedicated and passionate about the education and wellbeing of their children. Planning is well documented and very detailed. Resources and strategies for programme implementation are clear. Children benefit from a programme that is consistent and familiar. Profiles and individual reports are used to assess children’s learning. Whānau are well informed of their children’s progress.

The charter clearly outlines the history of the kōhanga reo. The vision and aspirations whānau have for their children are identified. Ngāti Manawa is a strong focus of the charter. There is a comprehensive set of policies. The health and safety policies are reviewed regularly. It is important that whānau carry out emergency evacuations on a regular basis.

Te Ara Tūāpae informs the strategic plan and actions. Growing te reo Māori capacity is clearly a priority for the whānau. There is regular reporting on aspects of the kōhanga reo operations and practices at monthly whānau hui. Financial reporting is transparent. The kōhanga reo is in a financially viable position. It is now time for kaimahi to monitor the quality of the learning programme.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

4 Findings

Mana Whenua

Background

Whānau choose Mana Whenua as the focus for this review. The whānau believe that Mana Whenua is about making sure the children’s identity is at the centre of all kōhanga reo activities. The whānau is strongly focused on the revitalisation and maintenance of Ngāti Manawa. ERO will review the extent to which children’s learning supports their language and identity.

Areas of good performance

Mana Whenua.The children experience a learning programme that affirms them as individuals as well as valued members of the kōhanga reo and the wider community of Murupara. The programme affirms children’s identity in the following ways:

  • children recite their pepeha which strengthens their links to Ngāti Manawa
  • children sing waiata that are linked to the local iwi
  • children and whānau are active participants in tangi and events on the marae
  • children are provided with opportunities to explore the natural environment outside of the kōhanga reo
  • the kuia teaches mōteatea to adults and children about past ancestors and events
  • kaimahi use visits to significant local landmarks and integrate these into the learning programme
  • most kaimahi are competent speakers of te reo Māori. Te reo o Ngāti Manawa is prominent and modelled for children
  • whānau and kaimahi have a strong relationship with Te Wharekura o Tāwhiuau. Transition to kura is much smooth. The majority of children attend the kura.

The older children are confident speakers of te reo Māori and all children are developing a strong sense of their identity.

Learning opportunities.Children enjoy a programme where their learning needs are well catered for. Positive aspects of the well organised and managed programme include:

  • access to a range of learning activities. This enables children to follow their own interests, problem solve, engage in learning with peers and set personal challenges
  • a spacious indoor and outdoor environment that is readily accessible to children
  • appropriate focus on the needs of the babies. Care and education are successfully integrated. The babies set the pace of the programme. The room is quiet and calm
  • provision of appropriate and varied resources.

Children benefit from a learning programme that provides intellectual and physical challenge.

Planning and Evaluation

Background

ERO always evaluates the quality of planning and evaluation in kōhanga reo to determine the quality and extent to which the kōhanga reo whānau plans, manages and monitors all areas on kōhanga reo operations for their children and whānau. Planning and evaluation includes programme planning and monitoring children’s development.

Areas of good performance

Planning and assessment.Planning is well documented and very detailed. There is a plan which is modified according to the age and abilities of children. Resources and strategies for implementation are identified. Learning topics and routines are clearly identified. Children receive a programme that is consistent and meets their needs.

Profiles and individual reports are used effectively to assess children’s learning. Whānau are well informed of their children’s progress.

Charter.Ngāti Manawatanga is a strong focus of the charter. The charter clearly outlines the history of the kōhanga reo. The vision and aspirations whānau have for their children are identified.

Policies.There is a comprehensive set of policies. The health and safety policies are reviewed regularly. All registers to support policy and procedures are in place. The whānau provide an environment where children’s health and safety are paramount.

Strategic Plan.Te Ara Tūāpae strategic objectives inform the strategic plan and actions. Increasing te reo Māori capacity is a priority for the whānau. They recognise the link between the kaupapa of kōhanga reo and their aspirations for the revitalisation of Ngāti Manawatanga.

Financial matters.Financial reporting is transparent. All transactions are accounted for. The auditor’s management letter is positive. There is a budget forecast which includes generous contributions toward resourcing of the learning programme. The kōhanga reo is in a financially viable position.

Discussions should focus on what worked well, what did not work, what could we need to do differently to improve outcomes for children.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
