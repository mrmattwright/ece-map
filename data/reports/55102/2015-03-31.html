Te Aromātai

Ko ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o E Tipu e Rea Taraia ki Ahuriri, e whakawhanake pai ana i tā rātou whakamahi, tā rātou ako hoki i te reo Māori. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki ngā tamariki, ngā wheako reo e whakaongaonga ana. Kua āta waihangatia te taiao ako kia whai pānga ki te ao Māori, me te whakahihiri hoki i ngā tamariki ki te ako. Tērā ka whakahaerehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri i roto i ngā tau e toru.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o E Tipu e Rea Taraia ki Maraenui, ki Ahuriri. E noho pūmau ana te whānau ki te whakatairanga i te tirohanga i whakatakotohia ai ki Te Korowai me Te Whāriki.

Ko E Tipu e Rea Taraia tētahi o ngā kōhanga reo me ngā ratonga mātauranga kōhungahunga e whitu e pā tata tonu ana ki te takiwā o Maraenui. He kura reo rua, he kura kaupapa Māori, he kura auraki anō hoki e pā tata tonu ana. He pai te hononga ki waenga i ngā kōhanga reo.

Mai i te pūrongo o mua a Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga, i te Hakihea o 2012, kua rerekē ētahi o ngā tūranga mahi. Nō nā tata tonu nei te kaiako, te kaiwhakahaere tari, me tētahi o ngā kaimahi i whakatūngia ai, waihoki, tokowhā ngā kaimahi katoa.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te ako me te whakamahi a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori?

He pai te whakawhanake haere o ngā tamariki i roto i tā rātou ako, tā rātou whakamahi anō hoki i te reo Māori.

Mana Reo

He pakari, he tino tākare hoki te kōrero a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori i ngā taumahi ako ōkawa. He pārekareka ki a rātou te taki, te waiata, me te whakatutuki haere hoki i ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, ngā mihimihi, ngā waiata, me te kori tinana. Ka tino ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki ngā taumahi e whai pānga ana ki te manawataki, te korikori, me te tāruarua. Ka whai, ka mārama hoki te nuinga o ngā tamariki i ngā pātai, ngā kōrero akiaki, ngā tohutohu, me ngā whakawhitinga kōrero. He pai ki ngā tamariki tuākana te ārahi, te hautū rānei i ngā mahinga me ngā taumahi ako. He āhuareka te ako reo ki ngā tamariki. Ka whakamanahia te ahurea, ngā taonga tuku iho hoki o ngā tamariki mā te reo Māori.

Ka rongo, ka ako, ka tūhura hoki ngā tamariki mā ngā huarahi hōhonu e whakaongaonga ana i a rātou. Tokotoru o ngā kaimahi e rima, e whakaoti ana i ngā wāhanga whakamutunga o te Tohu Whakapakari. Kua oti i te toenga o ngā kaimahi Te Ara Tuatahi, me Te Ara Tuarua. Haere ai ngā kaimahi katoa ki ngā kura reo, ngā wānanga ā-rohe, ngā wānanga hoki a te purapura i ia te wā, hei whakatairanga ake i tō rātou āheinga i roto i te reo Māori. Ka rumakina ngā tamariki ki te reo Māori me ngā tikanga e ngā kaimahi e ngākau nui ana ki tō rātou reo me te ao Māori.

Ka whiwhi ngā tamariki i ngā wheako i āta whakamaheretia ai, ā, e ahu mai ana ēnei i te ao Māori. Ka whakatauira ngā kaimahi i te awhi, te aroha, me te tiaki mā ā rātou taunekeneke ki ngā tamariki. Ka kitea hoki aua āhuatanga ki ngā taunekeneke a ngā tamariki ki waenga i a rātou anō. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki, te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi e whai pānga ana ki ngā tohu me ngā ariā Māori. He hanganga, he riterite hoki ki te hōtaka o ia rā, ā, e poipoi ana hoki i te whanake haere, te rerekē haere hoki o ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Ka puāwai ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e pou here ana i te katoa o ngā taunekeneke me ngā taumahi.

Ka tino tautokona te whakawhanaketanga reo Māori o ngā tamariki e te whānau. Ko tō te whānau wawata, kia kōrero ā rātou tamariki i te reo Māori i te marae kei tua i te awe māpara. Ka whakatinana te whānau i tēnā ka ākona e rātou i ngā akoranga, i ngā rārangi kupu, i ngā kīanga hoki, i a rātou e whakawhiti kōrero ana ki ngā tamariki. Ko te tae ā-tinana atu ki ngā akoranga o te reo Māori, tētahi kaupapa rautaki matua a te whānau. Ko te kaha pūmau o te whānau ki tēnei āhuatanga, ka tino hāpai mārika i te ako me te whakawhanaketanga reo a ngā tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Ahakoa te tokomaha o ngā tamariki e āhei ana ki te whakautu i ngā tohutohu, ngā pātai, me ngā kīanga mā te reo Māori, kāhore rātou e whakamahi ana i te reo Māori, puta noa i ā rātou taunekeneke katoa. Me tahuri ngā kaimahi i nāianei ki ngā huarahi ka whakawhānui i te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori, ki ngā wāhanga katoa o te hōtaka.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka whai hua ngā tamariki nā te pai o te whakahaere, te whakariterite hoki a te whānau. Kei te whānau tētahi anga kaupapa here e whai hua ana ki te ārahi i ā rātou mahi. He mārama ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Kei te arotake whaiaro ngā aronga ka pupū ake noa, me ērā i whakaritea ai, ā, ka whai pānga anō hoki ki ngā mahere mō meāke nei. He mārama ngā wawata o te whānau me ā rātou whāinga hoki, mō ā rātou tamariki. Ko te waiora o ngā tamariki me ngā painga hoki mō rātou, kei te pūtake o ngā mahi whakamahere a te whānau, me tā rātou whakatau i ngā whakaritenga.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Ka tino whai hua ngā tamariki nā te noho ngātahi o ngā mōhiotanga ngaio me te pai o ngā whakaritenga a ngā kaimahi. E mōhio ana te whānau, me whakarite ngā arohaehae hei hāpai i te tupu tonu o ngā whakaritenga ka whakatairanga i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Kāhore ngā whakatutukitanga me ngā wheako o ngā tamariki e aromatawaihia ana, e aromātaihia ana rānei i ia te wā. I te nuinga o te wā ka hāngai ngā aromatawai ki ngā tūmomo kaupapa, ā, kāhore e whai pānga nui ana ki ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā pūmanawa o ia tamaiti. He whāiti te titiro o ngā aromātai hōtaka. He iti noa te whakaaro huritao ki ngā tūmomo āhuatanga o te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki. Ko ngā whakapaitanga ki te aromatawai me te aromātai hōtaka, ka hāpai ake anō hoki i ngā putanga ako a ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

4 Te Taunakitanga

I whakawhanakehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei, arā, kia:

  • whakatinana ngā arohaehae a ngā kaimahi
  • whakapai ake te aromatawai i ngā tamariki me te aromātai i te hōtaka.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

Ko ngā tamariki o Te Kōhanga Reo o E Tipu e Rea Taraia ki Ahuriri, e whakawhanake pai ana i tā rātou whakamahi, tā rātou ako hoki i te reo Māori. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki ngā tamariki, ngā wheako reo e whakaongaonga ana. Kua āta waihangatia te taiao ako kia whai pānga ki te ao Māori, me te whakahihiri hoki i ngā tamariki ki te ako. Tērā ka whakahaerehia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te arotake whai muri i roto i ngā tau e toru.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Kōhanga Reo o E Tipu E Rea Taraiai roto i te toru o ngā tau.

Kaiurungi Whakaturuki Arotake Māori

31 Poutū-te-rangi, 2015

Evaluation

Children at Te Kōhanga Reo o E Tipu E Rea Taraia, Napier are developing well in their use and learning of te reo Māori. Kaimahi and whānau provide children with stimulating language experiences. The well designed learning environment reflects te ao Māori and motivate children to learn. ERO is likely to return to the kōhanga reo in three years.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o E Tipu E Rea Taraia is in Maraenui, Napier. The whānau are committed to promoting the philosophy outlined in Te Korowai and Te Whāriki.

E Tipu E Rea Taraia is one of seven kōhanga reo and early childhood services in the immediate area of Maraenui. There is a bilingual school, kura kaupapa Māori and mainstream school within close proximity. Relationships among the kōhanga reo are positive.

Since the previous ERO report of December 2012, there have been some staffing changes. The kaiako and administrator and one of the four kaimahi are recent appointments.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children learn and use te reo Māori?

Children are developing well in their learning and use of te reo Māori.

Mana Reo

Children speak te reo Māori with confidence and great enthusiasm during formal learning activities. They enjoy reciting, singing and participating in karakia, hīmene, mihimihi, waiata and kori tinana. Children particularly enjoy activities that incorporate rhythm, movement and repetition. Most children follow and understand questions, prompts, instructions and conversations. The older children like to lead or direct routines and learning activities. Language learning is a pleasant experience for children. Children are affirmed in their culture and heritage through te reo Maori.

Children hear, learn and explore the language in rich and stimulating ways. Three of the five kaimahi are in the final stages of completing the Whakapakari qualification. The other kaimahi have completed Te Ara Tuatahi and Tuarua. All kaimahi regularly attend kura reo, regional and purapura wānanga to enhance their te reo Māori capability. Children are immersed in te reo Māori and tīkanga by kaimahi who are passionate about their language and te ao Māori.

Children receive well planned experiences based on te ao Māori. Kaimahi model awhi, aroha and tiaki in their interactions with children. Children display similar behaviours with each other. Kaimahi provide children with a range of resources that reflect Māori symbols and concepts. The daily programme has structure, balance and caters for children’s developing and changing needs and interests. Children thrive in an environment where te reo Māori underpins all interactions and activities.

Children are well supported by whānau in their te reo Māori development. Whānau aspirations are that their children will be future speakers of te reo Māori on the marae. Whānau apply what they have learned from courses, word lists and phrases when they communicate with children. Attending te reo Māori courses is a strategic priority for whānau. This level of commitment by whānau will greatly advantage children in their learning and language development.

Key Next Steps

While several children are able to respond to instructions, questions and phrases in te reo Māori they do not use te reo Māori consistently in all of their interactions. Kaimahi should now look at how they can extend children’s use of te reo Māori in all areas of the programme.

Planning and Evaluation

Children benefit from effective whānau management and organisation. Whānau has a useful policy framework to guide their practice. There are clear processes for managing the kōhanga reo operations. Self review includes spontaneous and planned approaches and informs future planning. Whānau have clear aspirations and goals for their children. Children’s wellbeing and interests are central to whānau planning and decision-making.

Key Next Steps

Children benefit greatly from combined kaimahi professional knowledge and effective practice. Whānau recognise that appraisal needs to be put in place to support continued growth of practice to enhance children’s learning.

Children’s achievements and experiences do not always undergo thorough assessment or evaluation. Assessments tend to focus on different kaupapa and do not include each child’s interests or strengths. Programme evaluations are limited in scope. There is limited reflection on different aspects of the children’s learning programme. Improvements in assessment and programme evaluations will improve children’s learning outcomes.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

4 Recommendation

ERO and the whānau developed the following recommendations

  • implement kaimahi appraisals
  • improve children’s assessments and programme evaluations.

Conclusion

Children at Te Kōhanga Reo o E Tipu E Rea Taraia, Napier are developing well in their use and learning of te reo Māori. Kaimahi and whānau provide children with stimulating language experiences. The well designed learning environment reflects te ao Māori and motivate children to learn. ERO is likely to return to the kōhanga reo in three years.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o E Tipu E Rea Taraia will be in three years.

Lynda Pura-Watson

Deputy Chief Review Officer Māori
