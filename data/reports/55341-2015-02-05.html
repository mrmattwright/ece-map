1 Evaluation of Te Karaka Preschool

Te Karaka Preschool requires support to further develop aspects of:

  • governance and management
  • curriculum
  • the quality of the programme for infants and toddlers.

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

Te Karaka Preschool is a small, rural community-based service. It is governed by a voluntary committee, led by two teachers who share the supervisor‚Äôs role.

There is a close whƒÅnau connection with most families affiliating to Te Aitanga ƒÅ MƒÅhaki.

The centre was recently relicensed to increase the numbers of children aged up to two from five to eight.

The Preschool has a positive ERO reporting history. However, most areas identified for development in the February 2012 ERO report have yet to be sufficiently addressed.

The Review Findings

The centre is warm and welcoming. Positive, responsive and reciprocal relationships are formed with families and most are related. This facilitates a strong sense of belonging for both children and whƒÅnau.

The centre has a well-considered, consultatively developed philosophy. It provides an appropriate foundation for programme planning to reflect the community‚Äôs shared values and beliefs.

The children are active learners. They enjoy their time in the centre. They are enquiring and confident and often engage in sustained play. Teachers should provide an appropriate level of challenge to their learning, especially the older children. Children do not have regular access to a full range of learning areas, including arts, carpentry and dramatic play. The teaching of mathematics through play should be more deliberate. Centre routines, such as sleeping and eating, should also be reviewed.

Most of the children in the centre identify as MƒÅori. There is a strong commitment to meeting the principles of the Treaty of Waitangi although this is not documented in the philosophy. The parent committee should consider Ka Hikitia ‚Äì Accelerating Success 2013 - 2017 to support decisions about how the principles of the Treaty of Waitangi are enacted in this centre. Leaders have identified that the centre‚Äôs bicultural programme should be strengthened. They are participating in a professional development programme to improve this key aspect.

Children‚Äôs interests are the basis for planning. The next step is to make a closer link between the assessments documented in the observations and this planning. The learning journals are designed to meet whƒÅnau interest. Appropriate photos show the children‚Äôs participation in activities. Learning journals should be more consistently used and the children‚Äôs culture, language and identity recognised and celebrated within the learning stories.

The co-supervisors facilitate a team approach and demonstrate a collaborative working relationship with teachers and the community. They have responsibility for the ongoing centre reviews, which have been strengthened since ERO‚Äôs 2012 review. There is now a more formalised structure and some well-considered reviews have led to procedural changes and positive outcomes. However, this remains an area for further development.

Professional leadership should strengthen processes to grow professional teaching practice and facilitate consistency in implementing quality learning programmes. For example, the co-supervisors should review the implementation of the Positive Behaviour Guidelines to ensure all teachers have a clear understanding of this approach.

In 2012, ERO noted the centre appraisal system needed to be strengthened to facilitate a more consistent approach to teaching. Limited progress has been made. The co-supervisors should seek support to develop a suitable policy and procedures to guide the implementation of a robust process. Annual appraisal for all teachers should be implemented.

The recent change in the centre licence to enrol more children aged up to two years has prompted the co-supervisors to consider strategies to better cater for this age group. This is an area for development.

Key Next Steps

ERO and the co-supervisors have identified the following next steps:

  • professional leadership should be strengthened
  • the curriculum should be reviewed so children have regular access to all learning areas
  • the performance appraisal system needs to be developed, documented and robustly implemented to increase consistency of good teaching practice
  • increase the evaluation of aspects of the programme and how teachers‚Äô interactions contribute to children‚Äôs learning
  • the centre‚Äôs bicultural programme should be strengthened and consideration given to promoting MƒÅori success, as MƒÅori
  • further develop high quality practices for infants and toddlers.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Te Karaka Preschool completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum

  • premises and facilities

  • health and safety practices

  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)

  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)

  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)

  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

Actions for compliance

ERO identified areas of non-compliance relating to the health and safety of the children. To meet requirements the service needs to improve its performance in the following areas:

Development Plan Recommendation

ERO recommends that the service consult with the Ministry of Education and plan to address the key next steps and actions outlined in this report.

ERO‚Äôs Evaluation Framework

ERO‚Äôs overarching question for an early childhood education review is ‚ÄòHow well placed is this service to promote positive learning outcomes for children?‚Äô ERO focuses on the following factors as described in the bicultural framework NgƒÅ Pou Here:

ERO‚Äôs Overall Judgement and Next Review

The overall judgement that ERO makes and the timing of the next review will depend on how well placed a service is to promote positive learning outcomes for children. The categories are:

  • Well placed ‚Äì The next ERO review in three years
