Background

Creators @ Home - Waikato Rua is part of a national organisation that owns a range of early learning services. This network is based in Hamilton. It provides three models of support; ‚ÄòAt Home‚Äô, where educarers care for children in private homes, ‚ÄòAwhi', for caregivers of children up lifted from their families by Oranga Tamariki, and ‚ÄòAwhi WhƒÅnau', for whƒÅnau caring for the children of family members. The majority of children in this network are supported by the 'Awhi' model. The service receives standard funding from the Ministry of Education and is licensed for up to 80 children from birth to school age. The current roll of 29 includes 11 children of MƒÅori descent.

Educarers provide education and care programmes in homes. Visiting teachers provide support, educational oversight and leadership for educators. Regular playgroup sessions for educarers and children are hosted by visiting teachers. They are fully qualified and registered early childhood teachers.

The organisations philosophy aims for children to be provided with loving, respectful, exciting and inviting learning environments that foster their holistic development. Four Pillars guide service operations and practices they are, love and connection, nature play, the Reggio Emilia learning model and value-based learning.

This is the first review of this network, previously known as Footsteps. Creators Education Trust, a not for profit organisation purchased Footsteps in September 2015.

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.
