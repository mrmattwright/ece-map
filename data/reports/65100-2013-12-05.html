The Rabbit Patch Preschool ‚Äì Airport is well placed to promote positive learning outcomes for children.

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

The Rabbit Patch Preschool ‚Äì Airport Centre is located in the airport grounds and is one of three early childhood centres in Christchurch with the same owners. The three centres are strongly influenced by the philosophy of Reggio Emilia.

The Reggio Emilia philosophy makes significant use of the environment as a way of extending children‚Äôs learning. Teachers provide children with a balance of structured and free-choice learning experiences that are appropriate for their needs.

The centre provides for employees at Christchurch International Airport and from the wider community. The buildings are purpose built. The nursery, toddler and preschool areas each have their own separate, well-resourced indoor and outdoor learning environments.

The teachers are fully qualified. They have a commitment to make ongoing improvements to the programme and practices through self review. The teachers are learners alongside the children.

The Review Findings

The philosophy is an important part of how this service operates. The Reggio Emilia approach is highly evident in the set up of the environment and the way the programme is planned to build on children‚Äôs interests and wonderings.

Children, parents, whƒÅnau and families are warmly welcomed into the preschool. There are respectful, positive, and nurturing relationships among children and teachers, and parents and teachers.

The centre has developed strong links with its parent community. Teachers make frequent use of parent expertise in the programme. Parents contribute in many ways to the programme, fostering positive relationships and effective involvement in their child‚Äôs learning and development.

Babies and toddlers in the under-two area experience respectful, caring relationships with their teachers. They are well supported in their language development and are provided with a wide range of learning activities. Teachers notice what children are interested in and let them take the lead in their learning. They provide a wide range of practical activities to develop children‚Äôs confidence and social skills.

In all areas of the preschool, teachers support children‚Äôs learning in literacy and numeracy. Teachers respond to and follow children‚Äôs interests. They change learning activities to provide new, varied and stimulating experiences for children, particularly in science and group play. Children are well supported to develop positive social skills. Teachers integrate some te reo and tikanga MƒÅori into the learning programme.

Children play in well-planned, purposeful spaces that make good use of the natural environment and resources. Children benefit from playing with equipment that can be used in a variety of ways to foster their exploration and curiosity.

Journals about individual children‚Äôs learning show that teachers know children, their strengths, and their interests well. Teachers make good use of what they notice about children‚Äôs interests to identify how they will extend learning for each child and groups of children. Teachers meet regularly to discuss children‚Äôs learning. Managers provide teachers with clear guidelines for planning.

The centre has in place a range of useful practices to help teachers work together in identifying what is working well and what could be improved (self review). Teachers follow clear guidelines to help them record their self-review process.

The centre is well led and managed. A feature of the centre is the effective management systems and support for staff. Managers have well-developed long-term plans that focus on improvement over time. Managers have high expectations for what effective teaching and learning should look like. They provide regular, well-targeted and purposeful opportunities for teachers to extend their skills and knowledge.

The centre managers of the three Rabbit Patch Centres have regular opportunities to provide leadership and support to each other. They make good use of their strengths and skills to develop consistent practices and promote improvement. The manager of this centre role models effective practice within the programme.

Key Next Steps

Managers have made good use of self-review processes to identify appropriate areas for continued improvement. They have identified that they need to strengthen:

  • the teacher appraisal system to ensure their high expectations for teaching are met consistently. This is likely to help some teachers to increase the ways they help children think, share their ideas and problem solve
  • aspects of self review by including children‚Äôs perspectives more, increasing teachers' abilities to lead aspects of self review, and ensuring that the outcomes of self review are evaluated and revisited.

ERO has identified that other next steps include:

  • strengthening assessment by developing further ways to incorporate parents' perspectives on their children‚Äôs learning, more consistently show how children‚Äôs learning has progressed and deepened over time
  • promoting MƒÅori children‚Äôs cultural identities.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of The Rabbit Patch Preschool - Airport completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service‚Äôs systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.
