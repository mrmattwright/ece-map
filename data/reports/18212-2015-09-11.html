1 Te Horopaki

He kōhanga reo noho tuawhenua Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Tau e Toru, e tū ana ki te marae o Tau Henare, ki Pipiwai, e pā tata atu ana ki Whangārei. Nō te tau 1982 te kōhanga reo i whakatūwheratia ai, e toru tau ki muri i tōna whakaritenga.

Ko ngā wawata o te whānau mō ā rātou tamariki, ko te aronga toi whenuatanga, te mōhiotanga ki te reo me ngā tikanga Māori, me te whakahonohono ki ngā tāngata.

Ko te pūrongo o te arotake tāpiri o te tau 2009 i tāutu i te maha o ngā whakapaitanga ki te hōtaka akoranga, te tautoko i te reo Māori, me te whakahaeretanga a te whānau. I noho tonu i taua wā, ko te whakamahere rautaki me te arotake whaiaro ētahi wāhanga hei whakawhanake ake.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te kite i te whanaungatanga o ngā tamariki, me tō rātou mōhiotanga hoki ki te reo Māori?

Ka kitea te whanaungatanga o ngā tamariki, puta noa i te hōtaka akoranga. He pai tā rātou whakamahinga i te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Ka ako ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao e manaaki ana, e poipoi ana hoki i a rātou. He pai ā rātou taunekeneke ki waenga i a rātou anō, ki te taha hoki o ngā kaimahi. Ka kitea te atawhai me te whakaaro nui o ngā tuākana mō ngā tēina. Ka ako ngā tamariki i ngā āhuatanga e pā ana ki a rātou anō, ki ngā hononga hoki ki waenga i a rātou. He tau ngā tamariki.

Ka tūmāia ngā tamariki, ā, ka kawe ake rātou i ā rātou akoranga. He maha ā rātou whai wāhitanga ki te tūhura i ngā āhuatanga e ngākau nuitia ana e rātou. E noho wātea ana ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi e whakatairanga i te ao Māori. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki ngā tūmomo kaupapa kori tinana, pāhekoheko hoki. Ka kōrero rātou mō ā rātou mahi, ā, ka kōkiri hoki i ā rātou ake tākaro. He harikoa ngā tamariki.

Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te reo ake o tō rātou hapū me tō rātou iwi. Ka rongo, ka whakamahi hoki rātou i te reo Māori mā ngā horopaki ōkawa me ngā horopaki ōpaki. Ka taki rātou i ngā karakia, ngā mihimihi, me ngā pepeha i ia rā. Ka whakamahi ngā kaimahi i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rautaki e hāpai ana i te whakawhanaketanga reo. Ko ētahi o aua rautaki, ko te patapatai, te whakatauira, me te akiaki. Ka whakamahia hoki ngā waiata hei whakapūmau i ngā hanganga reo Māori. He tino aronga ki te reo Māori i te kāinga. Ka ākina ngā tamariki kia whakamahi rātou te reo Māori i ngā wā katoa.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

He mārama te tirohanga a te whānau mō te kōhanga reo. Ka hono tā rātou mahere rautaki ki Te Ara Tūāpae. He nui ō rātou wawata kia kōrero ā rātou tamariki i te reo Māori, ā, kia pakari hoki te whakawhanaungatanga. Ka tino whakapuaki te whānau i ngā hītori me ngā kaupapa matua o te kōhanga reo. Ka whai wāhi hoki aua āhuatanga ki te tūtohinga o te kōhanga reo.

He pai te whakahaeretanga a te whānau i te kōhanga reo. Tū ai ngā hui ā-whānau i ia te wā. He kaha te tae ā-tinana atu ki aua hui. He mārama te nuinga o ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga, ā, he māmā noa te whai haere. Ka whai wāhi atu te whānau ki ngā whakahaeretanga o ia rā, me ngā whakataunga hoki e arotahi pū ana ki ngā tamariki.

He matawhānui ngā mahi whakamahere, ā, he nui te kiko o roto. Ka tāutuhia ngā kaupapa ako, ā, ka whakaurua te kiko me ngā rauemi e whai pānga ana. Ka whakarato ngā mahere i ngā aratohu mārama mō te whakatinanatanga o te hōtaka. Ko ngā ngākau nuitanga me ngā matea o ngā tamariki te tūāpapa o ngā mahi whakamahere.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Ko te whakahaeretanga a ngā kaimahi tētahi āhuatanga hei whakapai ake. Kāhore he tuhinga tūranga mahi, kāhore hoki he kirimana ā ngā kaimahi. Kāhore he tukanga e pā ana ki te arohaehae. Ka tātari te arohaehae i ngā momo akoranga ngaio, whakawhanaketanga ngaio hoki e tika ana mā ngā kaimahi, hei hāpai a rātou ki te whakatairanga i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

Kāhore ngā whakaritenga arotake whaiaro me ngā putanga e pūrongohia ana mā te whānau. Me whakapakari ake te pūrongo atu a ngā kaiako ki ngā hui ā-whānau. I tēnei wā, he tuhinga ārahi noa te mahere rautaki, ā, me whakahāngai ake ki ngā wawata o te whānau nei. Kāhore ētahi o ngā kaupapa here o te hauora me te haumaru e hāngai ana ki te rā.

Me whakawhanake te aromatawai me te aromātai hōtaka. Kāhore ngā huarahi aromatawai o tēnei wā e hāngai ana ki ngā kaupapa ako, ki ngā akoranga rānei a ngā tamariki. Kāhore ngā kaimahi e tino noho mārama ana ki te pūtake o ngā pūkete a ngā tamariki. Me hāpai ake hei tāutu i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. Me whakapakari te aromātai hōtaka hei tātari i tēnā e whai hua ana, me tēnā hoki hei whakapai ake. Ko te whakapai ake i te aromatawai me ngā aromātai hōtaka ka whakapai ake anō hoki i ngā putanga ako a ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

I tāutuhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te wāhanga e whai ake nei, kāhore e tutuki ana e ai ki te ture:

  • me whakarite i ngā mahi whakahaere e tika ana mō ngā kaimahi, tae atu ki te whakatinanatanga o ngā arohaehae a ngā kaimahi.
    [R 47 Ture Mātauranga (Kōhungahunga) 2008; Paearu Raihana GMAS 7]

4 Te Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau i ngā taunakitanga e whai ake nei. Me tahuri te whānau ki te:

  • whakawhanake me te whakatinana i ngā tuhinga tūranga mahi me ngā kirimana mahi mā ngā kaimahi katoa
  • whakawhanake me te whakatinana i tētahi tukanga arohaehae mā ngā kaimahi
  • whakapai ake i ngā aromatawai a ngā tamariki me te aromātai i te hōtaka.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Tau e Toru ki te marae o Pipiwai, ki Whangārei. He pai te whanake haere o te whakamahinga a ngā tamariki i te reo Māori. Ka whakarato ngā kaimahi me te whānau i ngā wheako reo me ngā wheako akoranga e whakaongaonga ana i ngā tamariki. Me whakatika ake i ētahi āhuatanga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o ngā kaimahi.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Ka arotake anō Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Tau e Toru i roto i te toru o ngā tau.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Tau e Toru is a rural kōhanga reo located at the Tau Henare Marae in Pipiwai near Whangarei. The kōhanga reo opened in 1982 after three years of initial establishment.

Whānau aspirations for their children include having a sense of belonging, knowledge of te reo and tikanga Māori, and making connections to people.

The 2009 Supplementary review report identified a number of improvements to the learning programme, support for te reo Maori and whānau management. Strategic planning and self review remained as areas for further development.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children display whanaungatanga and knowledge of te reo Māori?

Children display whanaungatanga throughout the learning programme. They consistently use te reo Māori well.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children learn in a caring and nurturing environment. They interact well with each other and with kaimahi. Tuakana show care and concern for teina. Children learn about who they are and their connections to each other. Children are settled.

Children are confident and take responsibility for their learning. They have many opportunities to explore the things that interest them. Children have ready access to a variety of resources that promote te ao Māori. Children enjoy the regular physical and interactive activities. They talk about what they are doing and initiate their own play. Children are happy.

Children are exposed to language specific to their hapū and iwi. They hear and use te reo Māori in formal and informal ways. They recite karakia, mihimihi and pepeha daily. Kaimahi use a range of strategies to support language development. These include questioning, modelling and prompts. Waiata are used to reinforce te reo Māori structures. There is a strong focus on te reo Māori at home. Children are encouraged to consistently use te reo Māori.

Planning and Evaluation

Whānau have a clear vision for the kōhanga reo. Their strategic plan links to Te Ara Tūāpae. They have high aspirations for their children to be speakers of te reo Māori and have a strong sense of whanaungatanga. The whānau clearly articulates the histories and priorities of the kōhanga reo. These are also included in the kōhanga reo charter.

Whānau manage the kōhanga reo well. Whānau hui are held regularly. Attendance at these hui is high. Most policies and procedures are clear and easy to follow. Whānau contribute to day to day operations and decision making that is focused on children.

Planning is comprehensive and detailed. Kaupapa ako are identified and include content, and supporting resources. Planning provides clear guidelines for programme implementation. Children’s interests and needs are the foundation for planning.

Key Next Steps

Personnel management is an area for improvement. Kaimahi do not have job descriptions and employment contracts. There is no process for appraisal. Appraisal will determine what professional learning and development kaimahi need to support them to enhance children’s learning.

Self review practices and outcomes are not reported to whānau. Kaiako reporting to whānau hui needs strengthening. The strategic plan is currently a guidance document and needs to reflect aspirations of the current whānau. Some health and safety policies are out of date.

Assessment and programme evaluation requires development. Current assessment methods do not align to kaupapa ako or what children are learning. Kaimahi are unclear about the purpose of children’s profiles. Support is needed to identify children’s learning. Programme evaluation needs strengthening to determine what works well and what might be improved. Improvements in assessment and programme evaluations will improve children’s learning outcomes.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

  • Ensure suitable personnel management practices including staff appraisals are implemented.

[R47, Early Childhood Education and Care Centres 2008, Licensing Criteria, GMAS 7]

4 Recommendation

ERO and the whānau developed the following recommendations. The whānau needs to:

  • develop and implement job descriptions and employment contracts for all kaimahi
  • develop and implement an appraisal process for kaimahi
  • improve children’s assessments and programme evaluations.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Tau e Toru is at Pipiwai Marae in Whangarei. Children are developing well in their use of te reo Māori. Kaimahi and whānau provide children with stimulating language and learning experiences. Some areas of personnel management need to be addressed.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Ngā Tau e Toru will be in three years.
