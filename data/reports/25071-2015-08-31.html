Te Aromātai

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rāhuitanga ki Ōtara, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau ki te tonga. E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki kia ako ai rātou i tō rātou taiao, ā, kia whanake ai hoki rātou hei kaitiaki. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i te manaaki me te whanaungatanga i ia rā. He mahana, he atawhai hoki te kōhanga reo mō te katoa. He mātātoa te whakahaere a te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

1 Te Horopaki

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rāhuitanga ki Ōtara, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau ki te tonga. Kua roa nei te whakahaeretanga o tēnei kōhanga reo noho tāone. Kua roa nei hoki ngā kaumātua kia whai wāhi nui ki te kōhanga reo nei, a, kua āta whakatairanga hoki i te reo Māori me ōna tikanga. Kei te whakapūmau tonu, kei te whakatairanga tonu hoki te whānau i te reo, ngā mōhiotanga, me ngā tikanga ki ngā tamariki, arā, ko ngā mātauranga ērā i tuku iho ai i ō rātou kaumātua.

He aronga ki te ako mā te tūhura i te taiao māoriori, tūturu hoki, kia tīmata ai te whakawhanaketanga o ngā tamariki i tō rātou whakapapa ki te whenua, kia ako ai rātou i tō rātou hononga ki te whenua, ā, kia tūhura ai rātou i te kaitiakitanga.

2 Ngā Whakaaturanga o te Arotake

He pēhea rawa te hono o ngā tamariki ki tō rātou taiao?

Ka tino tautokona ngā tamariki kia ako ai rātou i tō rātou taiao, ā, kia whanake ai rātou hei kaitiaki.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

E mōhio ana ngā tamariki ki te taiao māoriori, tūturu hoki. Kua āta whakawhanakehia te wāhi o waho, ā, he nui hoki ngā rauemi i reira hei whakaongaonga i ngā tamariki. He wāhi whakahihiri, he wāhi tūhura, he wāhi pārekareka hoki mō ngā tamariki. Ka ngākau nui ngā tamariki ki te tiaki i ā rātou kararehe, te kohi haere i ngā hēki a ngā heihei, me te haereere i te hapori.

He mahira, he pākiki hoki ngā tamariki mō tō rātou taiao. Ka whai hua ngā kaimahi ki te whakahono i ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori. Ko te aronga ako i tēnei wā e hāngai ana ki ngā kōrero o nehe e pā ana ki te moa, ā, nā tēnei kua pupū ake anō hoki ngā whakaaro e pā ana ki ngā mokonui. Kua kapo atu taua kaupapa i ngā whakaaro pohewa o ngā tamariki, ā, kua whakawhānui ake ngā kaimahi i te kaupapa nei nā ngā haerenga ki te whare pupuri taonga, me te ako i te reo Māori, ngā waiata, me ngā kōrero tuku iho.

Ka noho mauminamina ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga hou. E mōhio ana rātou ki te tini o ngā tangi a ngā tūmomo manu, ā, ka ngākau nui ki ngā kēmu rotarota ā-kupu nei i waihangatia ai e ngā kaiako kia ngahau ai te ako i ngā manu. Ka whakahonohono rātou i ngā mea e ora ana ki tō rātou taiao, ā, ka kite i tō rātou maioha haere ki ngā taiao nei.

He mātātoa te whai wāhi atu a ngā tamariki ki ngā akoranga. He tākare tā rātou kapo atu i ngā whai wāhitanga ki te ārahi i ō rātou hoa. Ka whakapūmautia ngā tikanga Māori i ngā tamariki e ako ana. Kua āta whakaritea ngā mahinga. He pai te tautoko me te manaaki o ngā kaimahi. He harikoa ngā tamariki, ā, ka ngākau nui rātou ki te noho ki tō rātou kōhanga reo.

Ka ako ngā tamariki i te reo Māori me ngā tikanga mā ngā huarahi mahi e whai pūtake ana, ā, e whakaongaonga ana hoki. Ka whakatairanga ngā kaimahi i te reo mā ngā rotarota me te aronga nui ki ngā momo oro. He pārekareka ki ngā tamariki te tūhura haere i te reo mā ā rātou tākaro. E mōhio ana te whānau kei te pai te whanake haere o te reo Māori o ngā tamariki. Kei te maioha haere ngā kaimahi ki te tirohanga o ngā tamariki i a rātou e āta whakarongo ana ki ā rātou whakawhitinga kōrero. Ka whai wāhi nui ngā karakia, ngā hīmene, me ngā waiata ki ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki. He tau ngā tamariki ki tētahi taiao kōrero Māori.

Ka rongo ngā tamariki i te manaaki me te whanaungatanga puta noa i te rā. He mahana, he wāhi atawhai hoki te kōhanga reo mō te katoa. E mōhio ana te whānau ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi hoki, ā, he pakari tā rātou whakatutuki haere i aua āhuatanga. Ka ngākau nui rātou ki te noho ki te kōhanga reo, me te tautoko o tētahi ki tētahi, te ako ngātahi hoki ki te taha o ā rātou tamariki. Ka noho mā ngā wāhi o roto i ngā wā katoa, ā, he pai hoki te whakaritenga. Ka kitea te manaaki, te whakaaro nui hoki o ngā tamariki mō ētahi atu, mō tō rātou taiao hoki. He kaha tō rātou aronga toi whenuatanga.

Te Whakamahere me te Aromātai

Ka tino whakahaerehia te kōhanga reo e te whānau hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Ko ngā whakawhitinga kōrero i ngā hui ā-whānau e arotahi ana ki ngā mahi hei whakatutuki me ngā whāinga i whakatakotohia ai i mua. Pūrongo ai ki te whānau i ia te wā, ā, ka hāngai tēnei ki te āhua o ngā mahi me te ahunga whakamua i roto i te nuinga o ngā wāhanga o te kōhanga reo. Kua whakaritea ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga hei ārahi i te whānau ki te whakatutuki i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te hauora me te haumaru. Ka whai pānga ēnei ki te arowhai i ngā whare me ngā papa i ia te wā, hei āta whakarite i te haumaru o aua wāhi mō ngā tamariki.

Kua whakaritea tētahi mahere rautaki, ā, kua whakatakotohia anō hoki i konei ngā kaupapa matua e arotahi ana ki te tupu tonu me te whakapai tonu. He tino aronga tēnei mā te whānau. Kua āta whakawhanakehia, ka āta whakatinanahia hoki te arotake whaiaro hei aroturuki i ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. Ka arotake, ka pūrongo hoki i runga i ngā kaupapa rautaki matua ki ngā hui ā-whānau. Ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o ngā whakataunga katoa e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga.

He pai te whakamahere i te hōtaka akoranga a ngā tamariki, ā, ka whai pānga nui ki ngā matea me ngā ngākau nuitanga o ngā tamariki. Ka tāutu ngā mahere ā-kaupapa i te whānuitanga o ngā whai wāhitanga ako e poipoi ana i te māramatanga o ngā tamariki ki te ao Māori me te ao whānui. Ko tētahi aronga matua o ngā akoranga, ko te whakatairanga i te taiao tūturu, māoriori hoki. Ka ngākau nui, ka whai wāhi nui hoki ngā mātua ki te waihanga, te whakaritenga, me te whakapuakitanga o te hōtaka akoranga.

He pai te aroturuki i te ako a ngā tamariki me tō rātou whakawhanaketanga. Ka kitea ngā āhuatanga o tēnā e ākona ana e ngā tamariki, me te āhua hoki o tā rātou ako. He mōhiohio pēnei e pā ana ki te tamaiti, ā, ki ngā rōpū tamariki anō hoki. Kei roto ngā aromatawai katoa i te reo Māori. Kua whakaurua ngā whakaahua me ētahi kōrerorero a ngā kaimahi, ā, ka whakanui aua kōrero i ngā putanga ako mā ngā tamariki. He pai te whakamōhio i ngā mātua ki te ahunga whakamua a ā rātou tamariki.

Ngā Whakaritenga Matua ka Whai Ake

Ko tētahi wāhanga i tāutuhia ai e ngā kaimahi hei whakawhanake ake, ko te whakamahi i ngā mōhiohio aromatawai ki te whakamahere i ngā matea o te tamaiti, ā, ko te reo Māori tētahi tino wāhanga. E mōhio ana ngā kaimahi, me whakamahi aua mōhiohio hei whakamahere i ngā rautaki e tika ana ki te whakawhānui i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki.

3 Te Whakataunga a te Kōhanga Reo ki ngā Wāhanga Tautukunga

I te wā o te arotake, i whakamātauhia e Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga ngā pūnaha o te kōhanga reo ki te whakahaere i ngā āhuatanga i raro iho nei e whai pānga nui ana ki te waiora o ngā tamariki:

  • te haumaru whatumanawa (tāpiri atu ko te ārahi mauritau, ko te ārai tamariki)
  • te haumaru ā-tinana (tāpiri atu ko te mātakitaki tamariki; ko ngā whakaritenga whakamoe; ko ngā aituā; ko te whāngai rongoā; ko ngā whakaritenga akuaku; ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tikanga haerenga whakawaho)
  • te tū tika o ngā kaimahi (tae atu ki ngā taumata tohu mātauranga; te arowhai a ngā pirihimana; ngā rēhitatanga kaiako; me te tatauranga ki waenga i te kaiako me te tamaiti)
  • ngā whakaritenga, tikanga hōneatanga e pā ana ki te ahi me te rū whenua.

Ko te tikanga, ka whakatairanga ngā kōhanga reo katoa i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki, me te arotake anō hoki i ia te wā i tā rātou whakatutukitanga i ngā herenga ā-ture.

Te Whakarāpopototanga

E tū ana Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rāhuitanga ki Ōtara, ki Tāmaki-makau-rau ki te tonga. E tino tautokona ana ngā tamariki kia ako ai rātou i tō rātou taiao, ā, kia whanake ai hoki rātou hei kaitiaki. Ka wheako ngā tamariki i te manaaki me te whanaungatanga i ia rā. He mahana, he atawhai hoki te kōhanga reo mō te katoa. He mātātoa te whakahaere a te whānau i tō rātou kōhanga reo hei painga mō ā rātou tamariki.

Hei ā hea Te Tari Arotake Mātauranga arotake anō ai i te kōhanga reo?

Evaluation

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rāhuitanga is in Otara, South Auckland. Children are well supported to learn about their environment and to develop as kaitiaki. Children experience manaaki and whanaungatanga every day. The kōhanga reo is a warm and welcoming environment for all. The whānau actively manage their kohanga reo in the best interests of their children.

1 Context

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rāhuitanga is in Otara, South Auckland. This urban kōhanga reo has been established for many years. Kaumātua have given long service to the kōhanga reo and promoting te reo Māori me ōnā tikanga. Whānau continue to uphold and promote the language, knowledge and customs to children passed on by their kaumātua.

There is emphasis in learning through the exploration of the natural and physical environment so that children begin to develop an affinity to the land, learn about their connection to the whenua, and explore the role of kaitiaki.

2 The Review Findings

How well do children interact with their environment?

Children are well supported to learn about their environment and to develop as kaitiaki.

Ngā Taumata Whakahirahira

Children are aware of the natural and physical environment. The outdoor area is well developed and resourced to stimulate children. It is an exciting place for children to explore and enjoy. Children enjoy caring for their animals, gathering eggs from the hens and visits in to the local community.

Children are inquisitive and curious about their environment. Kaimahi successfully link children’s interests to te ao Māori. The current learning focus on the ancient stories of Moa has initiated an interest in dinosaurs. This kaupapa has captured children’s imagination and kaimahi have extended this into visits to the museum, specific te reo Māori learning, waiata and stories.

Children are open and receptive to new learning. They recognise a range of bird sounds and enjoy the rhythmic play on words the kaiako have created to make learning about bird’s fun. They make connections between living things and their environment, and show a growing appreciation for these worlds.

Children are actively engaged in learning. They eagerly accept opportunities to lead their peers. Tikanga Māori is reinforced for children when they are learning. Routines are well established. Kaimahi provide good support and care. Children are happy and enjoy being at their kōhanga reo.

Children learn te reo Māori and tikanga in a variety of meaningful and stimulating ways. Kaimahi promote the language through the use of rhymes and playing on the sounds. Children enjoy exploring language through their play. The whānau acknowledge children’s te reo Māori is developing well. Kaimahi are gaining an appreciation of children’s perspective when they listen carefully to their conversations. Karakia, hīmene and waiata are an important part of children’s learning. Children are comfortable in a Māori speaking environment.

Children experience manaaki and whanaungatanga every day. The kōhanga reo is a warm and welcoming environment for all. Whānau are aware of their various roles and responsibilities and carry these out with confidence. They enjoy being at kōhanga reo to support each other and learn beside their children. Indoor areas are kept clean and well organised. Children show care and concern with others and for their environment. They have a strong sense of belonging.

Planning and Evaluation

The kōhanga reo is effectively managed by whānau in the interests of children. Discussions at whānau hui focus on actions to achieve the goals previously set. Reporting to whānau is regular and is about the performance and progress of most areas of the kōhanga reo. Policies and procedures are in place to guide the whānau to carry out health and safety practices. These include regular checks of the premises and grounds to ensure the environment is safe for children.

A strategic plan is in place which includes key priorities focused on sustained growth and improvement. This provides good direction to whānau. Self review is well developed and implemented to monitor all areas of the kōhanga reo. Review and reporting against strategic priorities occur at whānau hui. Children are at the heart of all management decisions.

The learning programme for children is well planned and considers children’s needs and interests. Kaupapa planning identifies a range of learning opportunities that fosters children’s understanding of te ao Māori and the wider world. Emphasis in learning is on promoting the physical and natural environment. Experiences encourage children to appreciate and respect for all things, living and non living. There is a high level of interest and involvement by parents in the design, organisation and delivery of learning programme.

Children’s learning and development is monitored well. Elements of what and how children are learning are evident. This includes individual and group information. All assessments are in te reo Māori. Photographs are included with commentary from kaimahi that highlight learning outcomes for children. Parents are well informed about their children’s progress.

Key Next Steps

Using assessment information to plan for individual needs, particularly in te reo Māori, has been identified by kaimahi as an area for development. Kaimahi acknowledge that this information should be used to plan appropriate strategies for extending children’s learning.

3 Whānau Management Assurance on Compliance Areas

During the review, ERO looked at the kōhanga reo systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All kōhanga reo are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Conclusion

Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rāhuitanga is in Otara, South Auckland. Children are well supported to learn about their environment and to develop as kaitiaki. Children experience manaaki and whanaungatanga every day. The kōhanga reo is a warm and welcoming environment for all. The whānau actively manage their kohanga reo in the best interests of their children.

When is ERO likely to review the kōhanga reo again?

The next ERO review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Te Rāhuitanga will be in three years.
