1 He Whakamārama

He Kupu Whakataki

Ko te Arotake Motuhake tētahi arotake o ētahi o ngā tino āhuatanga mahi, ā, ka whakahaerehia nā runga i ētahi tino whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga.

Ka pūrongo pea ngā arotake motuhake i ētahi take i whakapuakihia ai e ētahi o waho.

I whakaritea tēnei pūrongo arotake motuhake i runga o ngā tikanga whakahaere paerewa kua whakamanatia e te Āpiha Kaiarotake Matua.

Ngā Whakataunga mō ngā Whakapuakitanga

Ko tēnei arotake motuhake i whakaritea i runga o te aromātaitanga o te whānau, te rōpū whakahaere hoki O Te Kōhanga Reo o Tararua ki Paraparaumu, i runga i ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga o tēnei arotake. Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga, ko te titiro ki:

  • Ngā kaupapa here, ngā tukanga, me ngā tuhinga e puritia ana
  • Ngā hononga me te whakatinanatanga o te marautanga
  • Ngā tukanga e pā ana ki ngā amuamu
  • ētahi atu tino take ka pupū ake i te wā o te arotake.

2 Te Aromātai a te Tari Arotake Mātauranga

I whakamanatia e te Āpiha Kaiarotake Matua tētahi Arotake Motuhake o Te Kōhanga Reo o Tararua ki Paraparaumu. I pupū ake tēnei arotake nā ngā mōhiohio i tukua ai e tētahi tangata o te hapori whānui mō te haumaru o ngā tamariki.

Ko ngā whakataunga mō ngā whakapuakitanga o tēnei Arotake Motuhake, ka hāngai ki ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga, ngā tuhinga e puritia ana mō ngā amuamu, te ārai i te tamaiti, te ārai tūkino, me te whakahaere i te whanonga. Ko ētahi atu take i hāngai ai ki ngā hononga, te hōtaka akoranga o ia rā, te mātakitaki me te manaaki i ngā tamariki, me ētahi atu tino take i pupū ake ai pea i te wā o te arotake. Ka whakaaro nui te whānau me ngā kaimahi ki ō rātou tūranga, ā rātou kawenga mahi anō hoki, ā, ka tino mahi ngātahi rātou ki te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i te wā o tēnei arotake.

I aro wawe mai ngā kaimahi o te tari ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, i te wā i tohu ai te Tari Arotake Mātauranga i tētahi Arotake Motuhake. I kōkiri Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo i tētahi hōparatanga o ngā take ki te taha o te whānau. Ka tino tautoko ā rātou kitenga i ngā kaimahi me te whānau, ā, i whakaratohia ēnei ki te Tari Arotake Mātauranga. He pūmau ngā whakaaro o te whānau, kia noho tonu te hauora, te haumaru, me te atawhai o ngā tamariki hei kaupapa matua ki a rātou.

He mārama, he matawhānui, ā, he tika hoki te whakamahinga o ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga hei ārahi i ngā mahi a te whānau, i roto i ngā take i hāngai ai ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. Ka tino whakamōhiotia te whānau, ā, e mōhio ana hoki rātou me pēhea te whakatutuki, te aro hoki ki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo take ka whai pānga ki ngā pou e whā i whakaraupapahia ai ki Te Korowai me Te Whāriki.

He aronga nui ki te waiora o ngā tamariki. Ka whakaraupapa ngā tuhinga katoa e pā ana ki ngā kaupapa here, ki ngā huarahi ka whāia e ngā kaimahi me te whānau, ki te hāpai i te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki. Kei roto i te kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te whanonga, ko ngā rautaki papai mō te ārahi i ngā tamariki. He mārama ngā tūmanako e pā ana ki te mātakitaki a ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa. He nui hoki te whakapūmautanga o ngā kaimahi me te whānau ki te whakarato i te atawhai o te kounga kairangi e aro ana ki ngā matea o ngā tamariki.

He mauritau ngā hononga, puta noa i te kōhanga reo. He nui te atawhai, te aroha hoki o ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki. He pai ngā taunekeneke ki waenganui i a rātou. Nā tēnei, he pai anō hoki te mahi ngātahi o ngā kaimahi hei rōpū. Ka whakanui ngā kaimahi i ngā mōhiotanga, ngā pūkenga, me ngā āheinga o tēnā me tēnā o rātou. He māia, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

He pai te whakaritenga o te hōtaka e whakaongaonga ana i a rātou, ā, ka whai hua ki te whakatutuki anō hoki i ngā tūmomo matea o ngā tamariki. Kua āta whakaritea ngā mahinga. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki mā ngā huarahi e whakakoia ana, e whai hua ana hoki, i a rātou e whakariterite haere ana i ngā tūmomo taumahi, e whai wāhi atu ana hoki ki ngā tūmomo kaupapa. E whakawhanake ana ngā tamariki hei ākonga pakari ka kōkiri i ā rātou ake akoranga.

Ko ngā tukanga mā te whānau mō ngā amuamu, ka āta whakaaro nui ki te noho tuwhera, te noho mārama hoki o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero. E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau, me whakapai ake i aua tukanga. Me pupuri te whānau i te whānuitanga ake o ngā kiko kōrero e pā ana ki ngā amuamu katoa. Me tūmataiti te pupuri i aua tuhinga.

Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

3 Ngā Whakaaturanga

Ngā kaupapa here, ngā tukanga, me te pupuri i ngā tuhinga

He mārama, he matawhānui anō hoki ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga, ā, he tika te whakamahinga a te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ēnei, hei ārahi i ngā whakaritenga e pā ana ki te whakahaeretanga o te kōhanga reo. E mōhio ana te whānau me pēhea te aro atu, te mahi hoki i roto i te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo kaupapa i te wā e tika ana. He aronga nui ki te waiora o ngā tamariki. Ka hāngai, ka whakanui hoki ngā tuhinga o ngā kaupapa here, i te whakahirahiratanga o te hauora me te haumaru o ngā tamariki. Ka whiwhi te whānau me ngā kaimahi i ngā mōhiohio e pā ana ki ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga i tā rātou whakaurutanga ki te kōhanga reo. He maha ngā wā ka kōrerohia aua tuhinga ki ngā hui ā-whānau, puta noa i te tau.

Ko ngā kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te ārai i te tamaiti, e āta tohu tika ana i te mana tangata me te waiora o ngā tamariki. He tino mārama ngā tukanga. Ko te kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te ārai tūkino tamaiti e whakamārama ana i ngā huarahi ka whāia e te whānau me ngā kaimahi, inā e whakapae ana rātou tērā pea he mahi tūkino. Ka hāngai hoki tēnei ki te toro atu ki te tautoko o waho i te wā e tika ana.

Ko te kaupapa here me ngā tukanga e pā ana ki te whakahaere whanonga, e mau hoki i ngā rautaki papai mō te ārahi, te tautoko hoki i ngā tamariki. Kei roto hoki i tēnei, ko te whakanui i te whanonga pai mā te pūnaha whakanui. Ka whakamahi tōtika ngā kaimahi i taua pūnaha mō te reanga o te tamaiti. He mārama ngā tūmanako e pā ana ki ngā mahi a te kaimahi, i a ia e mātakitaki ana i ngā tamariki. Ko te tikanga, ka mātakitaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa, me te whakarato i te atawhai o te kounga kairangi e aro nui ana ki ngā matea o ngā tamariki.

Ngā hononga, me te whakatinanatanga o te marautanga

Ngā hononga. He mauritau ngā hononga ki te kōhanga reo. He atawhai, he aroha hoki tō ngā kaimahi mō ngā tamariki. Kua waihangatia e rātou tētahi taiao e whakatau ana, e noho mauritau ana hoki, ā, ko ngā tamariki kei te pūtake o te katoa o ngā whakataunga me ngā taumahi. Kei te tino mōhio ngā kaimahi ki ngā tamariki, ā, ka aro ki ō rātou ake matea mā ngā huarahi e whai pūtake ana. He pakari, he harikoa hoki ngā tamariki ki te taiao o tō rātou kōhanga reo.

He mauritau ngā hononga ki waenga i ngā kaimahi. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i a rātou anō, ā, he pai tā rātou mahi ngātahi hei rōpū. Ka whakanui rātou i ngā mōhiotanga, ngā pūkenga, me ngā āheinga o tēnā me tēnā o rātou. Ka whai hua te kaiako ki te whakatauira i ngā taunekeneke papai ki ngā tamariki. Ka whakaatu ia i ngā huarahi ki te ārahi i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki, me te mahi ngātahi ki ngā tamariki nohinohi ake. Ka whakatauira te kaiako i te tino taumata o te reo Māori mō ngā tamariki me ngā kaimahi.

Te mātakitaki. Ka tino mātakitaki ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki i ngā wā katoa, hei ārai i tō rātou hauora, tō rātou noho haumaru, me tō rātou waiora. He tiwhikete whakaora whāwhai tā ia kaimahi, ā, e hāngai ana ēnei ki te rā. Ka aro wawe rātou ki ngā matea ā-tinana, ā-whatumanawa hoki o ngā tamariki i te wā e tika ana. He pai ngā tuhinga ka puritia mō ngā maiki me ngā aituā katoa. Ka tino whakapuaki ngā kaimahi i aua mōhiohio ki te whānau.

Te hōtaka akoranga. He pai te whakaritenga o te hōtaka e whakaongaonga ana, ā, e whai hua ana ki te whakatutuki i ngā matea o ia tamaiti, me ngā matea hoki o ngā rōpū tamariki. Kua āta whakaritea ngā mahinga. Kei te mārama ngā tamariki ki ngā mahinga me ngā taumahi puta noa te rā, ā, he ngāwari ki a rātou te whakawhitiwhiti haere ki ēnei. Kua āta whakamaheretia, kua āta whakaritea hoki nō mua noa atu ngā taumahi ako e hāpai ana i ngā akoranga a ngā tamariki mā te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo huarahi. E wātea ana ki ngā tamariki te whānuitanga o ngā tūmomo rauemi, taputapu tākaro hoki ki ngā wāhi i roto, i waho hoki. Ko ētahi o aua rauemi ko ngā taumahi reo matatini, te mahi māra, me te kaukau. Ka hāpai ngā kaimahi i ngā tamariki mā ngā huarahi e whakakoia ana, e whai hua ana hoki, i a rātou e whiriwhiri ana i ngā tūmomo taumahi. E whanake ana ngā tamariki hei ākonga māia e kōkiri ana i ā rātou ake akoranga.

Ngā tukanga inā he amuamu

Ko ngā tukanga mā te whānau mō ngā amuamu, ka āta whakaaro nui ki te noho tuwhera, te noho mārama hoki o ngā whakawhitinga kōrero. He mārama te mahere hei ārahi i te whānau, mō te wā e tika ana, mō ngā wāhi e tika ana hoki, ki te toro atu ki te tautoko o waho. E taunaki ana te mahere kia puritia ngā tuhinga e pā ana ki ngā āhuatanga katoa o te tukanga. E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga me te whānau, me whakapai ake i taua tukanga. Me pupuri te whānau i te whānuitanga ake o ngā kiko kōrero e pā ana ki ngā amuamu katoa. Me tūmataiti te pupuri i aua tuhinga.

Ētahi atu tino take matua

Ko te āta poipoi i te whānau tētahi āhuatanga matua ki tēnei kōhanga reo. He rangatahi tonu te tokomaha o te whānau, ā, kua whakawhanakehia te tino aronga toi whenuatanga, te hononga anō hoki ki te kōhanga reo me ngā kaimahi. Ka whiwhi rātou i te tautoko o ngā kaimahi, ā, ka tiaki rātou i ā rātou tamariki ki tua atu i ngā haora o te kōhanga reo i ngā wā e tika ana. He mātātoa te whai wāhi a te whānau ki te whakahaeretanga me te whakaritenga o te kōhanga reo. Ka tino whai hua anō hoki te tautoko o ngā kaumātua, o te purapura, me ngā kaimahi ā-rohe hoki o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo. Ka hāpai tēnei i te whānau ki te whakawhanake i tō rātou āheinga ki te whakahaere i te kōhanga reo.

4 Ngā Taunakitanga

E taunaki ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kia mahi ngātahi te whānau ki te tari ā-rohe o Te Poari Matua o Ngā Kōhanga Reo, ki te: 

  • whakatakoto tika, pupuri tika hoki i ngā kōnae tūmataiti e pā ana ki ngā amuamu me ngā tukanga whakatau
  • whakapiki ake i te āheinga o te whānau ki te whakahaere i ngā wāhanga katoa o te kōhanga reo. 

5 Whakaritenga Whaimuri

E whakaae ana te Tari Arotake Mātauranga kei te whakahaeretia te kōhanga reo hei painga mō ngā tamariki. Nō reira, ka arotakehia anō e te Tari Arotake Mātauranga te kōhanga reo hei wāhanga o te huringa arotake o te wā.

1 Background

Introduction

A Special Review is a review of particular areas of performance and is undertaken to specific terms of reference.

Special reviews may report on particular issues raised from external sources.

This special review report was prepared in accordance with standard procedures approved by the Chief Review Officer.

Terms of Reference

This special review is based on an evaluation of the performance of Te Kōhanga Reo o Tararua ki Paraparaumu whānau and management in relation to the terms of reference for this review. The terms of reference are:

  • Policies, procedures and records
  • Relationships and curriculum implementation
  • Complaints procedures
  • any other significant matters that arise during the review.

2 The Education Review Office (ERO) Evaluation

The Chief Review Officer authorised a Special Review of Te Kōhanga Reo o Tararua ki Paraparaumu. This is as a result of receiving information from a member of the public about the safety of children.

The terms of reference for this Special Review include policies with procedures, records of complaints, child protection and abuse prevention and behaviour management. Additional issues include relationships, the daily learning programme, the supervision and care of children and any other significant matters that may arise during the review. The whānau and kaimahi take their roles and responsibilities seriously and willingly cooperated with ERO during the review.

The district Te Kōhanga Reo National Trust (TKRNT) office staff responded immediately when ERO signalled the need for a Special Review. TKRNT initiated an investigation of the issues with the whānau. Their findings were highly supportive of kaimahi and whānau and were provided to ERO. Whānau remain adamant that the health, safety and care of children will continue to be priorities for them.

Policies with procedures are clear, comprehensive and used appropriately to guide whānau practice in matters relating to the kōhanga reo operations. Whānau are well informed and know how to act and respond across a range of circumstances pertaining to the four pou outlined in Te Korowai and Te Whariki, a Māori.

There is a strong focus on children’s wellbeing. All policy documents outline how kaimahi and whānau support children’s health and safety. The behaviour management policy and procedures include positive strategies for guiding children. There are clear expectations of kaimahi to supervise children at all times. There is a high level of commitment among kaimahi and whānau to provide high quality care that is responsive to the needs of children.

Relationships throughout the kōhanga reo are positive. Kaimahi are warm and loving toward children. They relate to each other in positive ways. This enables kaimahi to work well as a team. Kaimahi complement each other’s knowledge, skills and abilities. Children are confident and happy in their kōhanga reo surroundings.

The programme is well organised, stimulating and effectively caters for the different needs of children. Routines are well established. Kaimahi support children in an affirming and positive manner when they make choices about the various activities they participate in. Children are developing as confident and independent learners.

The complaints procedures for whānau emphasises “open and honest lines of communication”. ERO and the whānau agree there is a need to improve the procedures. Whānau should keep more detailed records of all complaints. These records should be stored in a confidential manner.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

3 Findings

Policies, Procedures and Records

Policies and procedures are clear, comprehensive and used appropriately by whānau and kaimahi to guide practice in matters relating to the kōhanga reo operations. Whānau know how to respond and act in a range of circumstances when required. There is a strong focus on children’s wellbeing. Policy documents acknowledge and emphasise the importance of children’s health and safety. Whānau and kaimahi receive information about policies with procedures at the time of induction into the kōhanga reo. These documents are often discussed at whānau hui throughout the year.

The policies with procedures for child protection appropriately recognise the rights and wellbeing of children. Procedures are explicit and clear. The child abuse prevention policy and procedure explains how whānau and kaimahi are to respond if they suspect child abuse. This includes seeking external support when necessary.

The behaviour management policy with procedures includes positive strategies for guiding and supporting children. These include acknowledging positive behaviour through a reward system. Kaimahi use this system in a way that is appropriate for the age of the child. There are clear expectations of how kaimahi should supervise children. Kaimahi are required to supervise children at all times and to provide high quality care that is responsive to the children’s needs.

Relationships and Curriculum Implementation

Relationships. Relationships within the kōhanga reo are positive. Kaimahi are warm and loving towards children. They have created a welcoming, friendly environment where children are at the forefront of all decisions and activities. Kaimahi know the children well and respond to their individual needs in purposeful ways. Children are confident and happy in their kōhanga reo surroundings.

Relationships between kaimahi are positive. Kaimahi are supportive of each other and work well as a team. They complement each other’s knowledge, skills and abilities. The kaiako effectively models positive interactions with children. She demonstrates how to guide children’s learning and how to work with their younger peers. The kaiako models high levels of te reo Māori for children and kaimahi.

Supervision. Kaimahi closely supervise children at all times to help safeguard their health, safety and wellbeing. All kaimahi have current first aid certificates. They readily respond to children’s physical and emotional needs when necessary. Good records are kept of all incidents and accidents. Kaimahi willingly share this information with whānau.

The learning programme. The programme is well organised, stimulating and effectively meets the individual and group needs of children. Routines are well established. Children understand and shift with ease between routines and activities that occur throughout the day. Learning activities are well planned and prepared in advance to support children’s learning in a range of ways. Children have access to various resources and play equipment in the indoor and outdoor areas. Some of these include literacy activities, gardening and swimming. Kaimahi support children in an affirming and positive manner when they make choices about different activities. Children are developing as confident and independent learners.

Procedures for Handling Complaints

The complaints procedures whānau use emphasises “open and honest lines of communication”. There is a clear diagram to guide whānau about when and where they should seek external support. The procedure recommends that records be kept of all aspects of the process. ERO and the whānau agree there is a need to improve the procedure. Whānau need to keep more detailed records of all complaints. These records should be stored in a confidential manner.

Any other significant matters

The positive way in which whānau are nurtured is a feature of the kōhanga reo. Many of the whānau are young and have developed a strong sense of belonging and connection to the kōhanga reo and kaimahi. They receive support from kaimahi who help care for their children outside of kōhanga reo hours when needed. Whānau take an active role in the management and operation of the kōhanga reo. They receive constructive support from kaumatua, the local purapura network and TKRNT district staff. This helps whānau to develop their capability to manage the kōhanga reo.

4 Recommendations

ERO recommends the whānau works closely with the regional office of TKRNT to:

  • accurately record and store confidential files of complaints and resolution processes
  • increase whānau capability to manage all aspects of the kōhanga reo.

5 Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

Lynda Pura Watson

National Manager Review Services Māori (Te Uepū ā-Motu)

Whānau are well informed and know how to act and respond across a range of circumstances pertaining to the four pou outlined in Te Korowai and Te Whariki, a Māori.

There is a strong focus on children’s wellbeing. All policy documents outline how kaimahi and whānau support children’s health and safety. The behaviour management policy and procedures include positive strategies for guiding children. There are clear expectations of kaimahi to supervise children at all times. There is a high level of commitment among kaimahi and whānau to provide high quality care that is responsive to the needs of children.

Relationships throughout the kōhanga reo are positive. Kaimahi are warm and loving toward children. They relate to each other in positive ways. This enables kaimahi to work well as a team. Kaimahi complement each other’s knowledge, skills and abilities. Children are confident and happy in their kōhanga reo surroundings.

The programme is well organised, stimulating and effectively caters for the different needs of children. Routines are well established. Kaimahi support children in an affirming and positive manner when they make choices about the various activities they participate in. Children are developing as confident and independent learners.

The complaints procedures for whānau emphasises “open and honest lines of communication”. ERO and the whānau agree there is a need to improve the procedures. Whānau should keep more detailed records of all complaints. These records should be stored in a confidential manner.

Future Action

ERO is confident that the kōhanga reo is being managed in the interests of the children. Therefore ERO will review the kōhanga reo again as part of the regular review cycle.

When ERO has reviewed a kōhanga reo we ask the whānau of the kōhanga reo to let us know how they intend to manage any difficulties set out in their report (copies of which are available from the kōhanga reo or ERO). We also encourage whānau to inform their community of any follow up action plan. You should talk to the whānau if you have any questions about this evaluation, the full ERO report or their future intentions.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT REVIEWS

About ERO

ERO is an independent, external evaluation agency that undertakes reviews of kura kaupapa Māori and kōhanga reo throughout New Zealand.

About ERO Reviews

ERO follows a set of standard procedures to conduct reviews. The purpose of each review is to:

  • improve quality of education for children in kōhanga reo; and
  • provide information to parents, communities and the Government.

Reviews are intended to focus on outcomes for tamariki and build on each kōhanga reo self review.

Review Focus

ERO’s framework for reviewing and reporting is based on four review strands.

  • Planning and Evaluation– in particular the quality of planning and evaluation. This area is most likely to support long term improvement. Implicit in this is the quality of whānau management and the quality of education.
  • Kōhanga Reo Priorities– based onTe Whāriki,thewhānau through their management and programme, influence positive outcomes for tamariki.
  • Compliance with Legal Requirements– assurance that this kōhanga reo has taken all reasonable steps to meet legal requirements.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of kōhanga reo performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for tamariki and useful to this kōhanga reo.

Review Recommendations

Most ERO reports include recommendations for improvement. A recommendation on a particular issue does not necessarily mean that a kōhanga reo is performing poorly in relation to that issue. There is no direct link between the number of recommendations in this report and the overall performance of this kōhanga reo.
